<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. The synthesis of silica-supported boron oxide catalysts involves preparing boron compounds, dispersing them on silica support, and forming boron oxide.
2. For the Oxidative Dehydrogenation of Propane (ODHP) reaction, 2-D dispersed active sites are developed through precise control of the dispersion process to achieve a high density of active sites.
3. Compared to linearly dispersed edge sites, 2-D dispersed active sites offer higher active site density leading to improved catalytic performance due to increased surface area and better reactant access.
4. The unique structural features have attracted significant research interest as they promise enhanced efficiency and selectivity in the ODHP reaction, which is essential for producing valuable chemicals from propane.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
