<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for the Oxidative Dehydrogenation of Propane (ODHP) reaction are developed through meticulous synthesis methods. These sites offer higher active site density compared to linearly dispersed edge sites, making them more effective for the ODHP reaction. The enhanced active site density contributes to improved catalytic performance and efficiency, which is why these unique structural features have garnered significant research interest.
