<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. The 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for the Oxidative Dehydrogenation of Propane (ODHP) reaction are developed through meticulous control of the catalyst's structure and composition. These sites provide a higher density of active sites compared to linearly dispersed edge sites, leading to enhanced catalytic performance due to more available sites for the reaction. The unique structural characteristics of these 2-D dispersed sites have garnered significant research interest because they potentially offer improved efficiency and selectivity in the ODHP reaction.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
