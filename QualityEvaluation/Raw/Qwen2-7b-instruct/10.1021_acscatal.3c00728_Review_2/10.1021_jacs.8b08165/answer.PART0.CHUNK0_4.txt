The article discusses the transformation of boron nitride catalysts under oxidative dehydrogenation conditions. It focuses on the development of 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts, which offer higher active site density than linearly dispersed edge sites. This unique structural feature leads to enhanced catalytic performance, attracting significant research interest in the field.
