<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for the Oxidative Dehydrogenation of Propane (ODHP) reaction are developed through a focus on creating a two-dimensional distribution pattern of active sites on the catalyst surface.
2. Compared to linearly dispersed edge sites, 2-D dispersed active sites provide a higher density of active sites, which significantly boosts the catalytic performance of the ODHP reaction.
3. The higher active site density in 2-D dispersed active sites leads to increased surface area, allowing for a more efficient use of the catalyst in the ODHP process.
4. These unique structural features have garnered considerable attention in the field due to their potential to enhance selectivity and stability in the ODHP process.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
