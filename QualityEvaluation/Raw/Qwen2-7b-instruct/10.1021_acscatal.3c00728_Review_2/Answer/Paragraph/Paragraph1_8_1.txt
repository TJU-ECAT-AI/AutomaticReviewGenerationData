        The development of 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for the Oxidative Dehydrogenation of Propane (ODHP) reaction involves creating a two-dimensional structure where the active sites are distributed throughout the material. These sites offer several advantages over linearly dispersed edge sites, including increased active site density which enhances catalytic performance through more direct contact with reactants. Moreover, the unique structural features of 2-D dispersed active sites attract significant research interest due to their potential for achieving high selectivity in the ODHP reaction, particularly in the production of propylene with minimal formation of CO2. The antioxidant ability of silicon boride catalysts ensures the stability of the reaction, contributing to their effectiveness in the ODHP process. The higher active site density of 2-D dispersed sites compared to linearly dispersed edge sites leads to improved catalytic performance due to increased surface area for reactant adsorption and product desorption. These features have garnered considerable research interest for their potential to enhance efficiency and selectivity in the ODHP process, which is crucial for the production of valuable chemical intermediates.
        
        Recent discoveries, such as the activity of hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN) as a metal-free catalyst for the ODH process of ethane and propane, have opened up new research directions in the selective cleavage of CH bonds in alkanes. This has expanded the fundamental understanding of industrially important ODH processes. Spectroscopic investigations suggest that borohydride (BOH) groups generated from boron oxide (BO) species under steam assistance could serve as catalytic activity sites. The BOH groups selectively break the CH bond while simultaneously shutting off the pathway of propylene overoxidation towards CO2. Studies by Su et al. propose that borohydride (BO(H)) formed at the edges of h-BN act as active sites for ethane ODH reactions. Furthermore, boron nitride can oxidize methane to valuable products, and carbon-doped BN catalysts show good activity in the ODH of ethylbenzene to styrene. The boron species is recognized as an indispensable element for high ODH activity in various boron-based catalysts. Silicon boride (SiB3-6), a metal-free material enriched in boron sites, exhibits good oxidation resistance at high temperatures, making it a potential catalyst for the ODH of light alkanes. Research on metal-free silicon boride may provide deeper insights into the catalytic origin of boron species and initiate the exploration of other new boron-based catalysts. Based on spectra and dynamics analysis, BOH groups generated from BO species under steam assistance are likely catalytic activity sites, supporting the catalytic origin of boron species from metal-free catalysts in initiating the oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes.

        最近的发现，如六方硼氮化物（h-BN）作为无金属催化剂在烷烃的ODH过程中的极强活性，开辟了在烷烃中选择性断裂CH键的新研究方向。这扩大了工业上重要的ODHP过程的基本理解。光谱学研究表明，从硼氧化物（BO）物种在蒸汽协助下生成的硼氢化物（BOH）组可能充当催化活性位点。BOH组选择性地断开CH键同时关闭丙烯过氧化向CO2的路径。苏等人提出，位于h-BN边缘的BO(H)形成是乙烷ODH反应的活性位点。此外，硼氮化物可以氧化甲烷产生有价值的产品，并且碳掺杂BN催化剂在乙苯到苯乙烯的ODH中显示出良好的活性。硼元素被识别为高ODHP活性不可或缺的元素。硅硼化物（SiB3-6），一种富含硼位点的无金属材料，在高温下表现出良好的氧化稳定性，使其成为轻烷烃ODH的潜在催化剂。对无金属硅硼化物的研究可能提供关于硼物种催化起源的更深入理解，并启动其他新硼基催化剂的探索。基于光谱和动力学分析，BOH组由BO物种在蒸汽协助下生成可能是催化活性位点，支持金属自由催化剂中硼物种的催化起源在轻烷烃的氧化脱氢作用的实验支持。
