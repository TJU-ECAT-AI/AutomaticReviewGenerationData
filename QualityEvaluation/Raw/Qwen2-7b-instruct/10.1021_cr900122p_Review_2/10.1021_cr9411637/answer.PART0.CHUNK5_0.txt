<EnglishVersionAnswer>

The composition, structure, and reaction conditions of metal-based catalysts, particularly supported vanadium oxides and metal-exchanged zeolites, significantly influence the mechanisms and efficiency of alkane transformations. Key factors include the nature of metal sites, acidity, and basicity. These catalysts enable improved yield, selectivity, and activity, as well as enhanced stability and longevity under harsh conditions. Fine-tuning these properties leads to optimized catalyst performance, making them cost-effective and environmentally friendly for industrial applications. Advancements in catalyst design have led to more efficient alkane transformation pathways, with synergistic effects between metal centers and the zeolite framework enhancing surface area and providing controlled acidity/basicity.

</EnglishVersionAnswer>
