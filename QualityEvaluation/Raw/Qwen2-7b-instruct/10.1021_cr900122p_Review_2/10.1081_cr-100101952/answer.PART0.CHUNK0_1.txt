The article discusses the conversion of ethane to aromatic hydrocarbons, covering historical advancements, current research, and future prospects. It delves into thermodynamic and kinetic considerations, catalyst selection, studies on reaction mechanisms, catalyst stability, and deactivation. The focus is on metal-based catalysts, including supported vanadium oxides and metal-exchanged zeolites, which play a crucial role in alkane transformations through processes such as oxidative dehydrogenation and zeolite-catalyzed reactions. The industrial significance of these catalysts stems from their ability to enhance the efficiency of alkane transformations, contributing to the production of valuable aromatic hydrocarbons. Factors influencing their performance include catalyst composition, structure, and reaction conditions.
