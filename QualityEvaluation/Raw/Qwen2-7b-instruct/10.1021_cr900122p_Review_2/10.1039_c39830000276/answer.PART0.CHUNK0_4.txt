<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The document discusses the facile C-H activation by lutetium-methyl and lutetium-hydride complexes. These complexes react at temperatures ranging from 20-50°C in hydrocarbon solvents with various reagents such as benzene, pyridine, ylide CH2PPh3, and tetramethylsilane, resulting in stable, isolatable products of C-H activation. Specifically, the hydride Lu(q5-C5Me5),H (2) can form through hydrogenolysis of its precursor (1) and undergo rapid H/D exchange with deuterated arenes, forming the deuteride Lu(q5-CSMe5),D. An important non-degenerate intermolecular C-H activation between the hydride (2) and benzene occurs, liberating hydrogen and forming the phenyl complex (3).
