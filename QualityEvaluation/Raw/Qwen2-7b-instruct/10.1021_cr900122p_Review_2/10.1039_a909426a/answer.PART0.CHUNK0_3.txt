[1] "Quantum-chemical calculations on the mechanism of ethane dehydrogenation catalysed by Zn-doped zeolites have been undertaken."
[2] "The results obtained indicate that the reaction proceeds via a mechanism starting from the alkyl rupture of the ethane CH bond and the zinc cation acts as an acceptor of the alkyl group."
[3] "The computed activation energies for these three steps Brnsted are 18.4, 53.4 and 20.5 kcal mol1, respectively."
[4] "Formation of dihydrogen from the Brnsted proton and hydrogen bound to Zn."
[5] "The activation energies of the three elementary steps involved in the alkyl route computed at the MP2/BS2/ B3LYP/BS1 level (the highest in this work) are 18.4, 53.4 and 20.5 kcal mol1, respectively."

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Quantum-chemical calculations were conducted on the mechanism of ethane dehydrogenation catalyzed by Zn-doped zeolites.
2. The zinc ion acts as an acceptor of the alkyl group during the ethane dehydrogenation process.
3. The computed activation energies for the three steps of the reaction are 18.4, 53.4, and 20.5 kcal mol1, respectively.
4. Dihydrogen is formed from the Brnsted proton and hydrogen bound to the zinc ion.
5. The activation energies for the three elementary steps in the alkyl route, computed at the highest level of calculation, are 18.4, 53.4, and 20.5 kcal mol1, respectively.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
