<EnglishVersionAnswer>

The composition, structure, and reaction conditions of metal-based catalysts, including supported vanadium oxides and metal-exchanged zeolites, significantly impact the mechanisms and efficiency of alkane transformations, particularly in processes like oxidative dehydrogenation and zeolite-catalyzed reactions. Key factors include the selection of metal, its oxidation state, and the support material, which together affect catalytic activity. For instance, supported vanadium oxides are effective due to their formation of strong acid sites that facilitate oxidative dehydrogenation. Metal-exchanged zeolites, characterized by high surface area and pore size distribution, enhance adsorption and diffusion of reactants, boosting transformation efficiency. Optimizing reaction conditions, such as temperature, pressure, and co-catalyst presence, further improves yield and product selectivity. These catalysts are industrially significant for converting abundant alkanes into valuable chemicals and fuels, addressing waste management and supporting sustainable energy production.

</EnglishVersionAnswer>
