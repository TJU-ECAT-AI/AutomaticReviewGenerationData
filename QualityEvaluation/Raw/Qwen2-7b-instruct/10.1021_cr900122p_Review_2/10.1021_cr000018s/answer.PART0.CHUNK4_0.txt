<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The composition, structure, and reaction conditions of metal-based catalysts, including supported vanadium oxides and metal-exchanged zeolites, significantly influence the mechanisms and efficiency of alkane transformations, particularly in processes such as oxidative dehydrogenation and zeolite-catalyzed reactions. Key factors contributing to their industrial significance include the catalysts' stability, selectivity, and adaptability to a variety of reaction conditions. Understanding these interactions enables the optimization of conversion pathways, resulting in improved yields and product quality.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
