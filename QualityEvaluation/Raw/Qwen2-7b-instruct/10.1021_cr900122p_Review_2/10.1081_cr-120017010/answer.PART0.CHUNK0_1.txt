The composition, structure, and reaction conditions of metal-based catalysts, including supported vanadium oxides and metal-exchanged zeolites, significantly influence the mechanisms and efficiency of alkane transformations. These catalysts are utilized in processes such as oxidative dehydrogenation and zeolite-catalyzed reactions, demonstrating their industrial significance due to enhanced selectivity, improved yield, and reduced energy consumption. They facilitate the conversion of hydrocarbons into valuable products, thereby making substantial contributions to the petrochemical industry.
