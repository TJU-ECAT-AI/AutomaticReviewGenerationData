<quotes>
[DFT study of CH4 activation by d0 Cl2LnZ (Z  H, CH3) complexes]
</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The DFT study examines the activation of CH4 by models (Cl2LnZ) of Cp*2LnZ, where Z is either H or CH3, across the entire lanthanide series. The calculations reveal that Cl2LnZ serves as a good model for Cp*2LnZ, accurately representing the coordination around the lanthanide center. The energetics of transforming X2LnH to CH4 and then to X2LnCH3 are closely similar for X = Cl and Cp, with differences attributed to the stronger electron donating ability of Cp. The formation of the lanthanide hydride complex is calculated to be exothermic, aligning with experimental evidence. Energy profiles of various reactions, including Cl2LnH to CH4, Cl2LnH* to CH4, and Cl2LnCH*3 to CH4, are calculated, with transition states for the first and third transformations being energetically accessible. The second reaction's transition state has a very high energy, indicating an unfeasible reaction.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

<quotes>
[Introduction]
</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The discovery of Cp*2LuCH3 activating CH4 marked a milestone in the search for new catalysts capable of activating inert bonds, particularly those in alkanes. Previous studies suggested that the activation of the CH bond occurs without a change in the metal center's oxidation state. DFT calculations on the exchange of hydrogen with Cp*2LnH have shown that the activation is energetically favorable, with the energy of activation depending on the lanthanide element's nature. However, it was noted that CH is harder to activate than HH due to the spherical nature of the 1s H orbital and the directional nature of the Me sp3 hydrid. These findings suggest that a σ-bond metathesis involving metalH and HH bonds might be more kinetically favorable than one involving MMe and CH bonds. Experimental results support this trend, indicating that reactions involving CH4 require heating, unlike those involving HH or Me.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

<quotes>
[Results and discussion]
</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The results indicate that Cl serves as an appropriate model for Cp in the context of the study. The computational details section discusses the use of large core relativistic effective core potentials (RECPs) optimized by the Stuttgart Dresden Group, along with augmented basis sets for the lanthanide and chlorine atoms. The calculations were carried out using the DFT level with the hybrid functional B3PW91, demonstrating that the coordination around the lanthanide center is similar for Cl2LnH and Cp2LnH. The LnH bond is slightly longer in Cl2LnH, reflecting the higher electron donating power of Cp. The study also compares the activation energy for H exchange with Cl2LnZ to that with Cp2LnZ, suggesting that Cl might not be an ideal model for Cp in cerium complexes. The authors conclude that Cl is suitable for modeling Cp in these systems, despite limitations in cerium complexes.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

结果表明，Cl可以作为Cp在本研究中的合适模型。计算细节部分讨论了Stuttgart Dresden组优化的大核心相对论有效核心势（RECPs），以及用于镧系和氯原子的增强基集。使用DFT水平和B3PW91杂化功能进行计算，显示了Cl2LnH和Cp2LnH中镧系中心的配位相似。LnH键在Cl2LnH中稍长，反映了Cp更强的电子捐赠能力。研究还比较了Cl2LnZ中的氢交换激活能与Cp2LnZ之间的激活能，提出Cl可能不是Ce复合物的理想模型。作者总结指出，尽管在Ce复合物中有局限性，但Cl适合用作这些系统的Cp模型。

The study considers the reactions where CH4 reacts with the lanthanide hydride, with a preference for the methyl group to approach the metal. The transition states for the α-CH3 approach are around 14 kcal mol−1 above the separated reactants, while the transition state for the β-CH3 approach is around 73 kcal mol−1 above the separated reactants. <quotes>
[Influence of the oxidation state]
</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The influence of the oxidation state on the activation of CH4 was explored through calculations for Ce(), Eu(), and Yb(). The study found that the oxidation state of the lanthanide metal does not vary during the σ-bond metathesis reaction. Only the most relevant systems were calculated for Ce(), Eu(), and Yb() to analyze the effect of the oxidation state on reactivity. The results showed that the oxidation state has a marginal influence on the activation energy, averaging around 13 kcal mol−1. However, the calculated activation energy for Ce() was significantly lower, possibly due to the use of Cl as a model for Cp. The study suggests that Ce() might be stable in the presence of H2 at low temperatures, as indicated by the calculations.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

<ChineseVersionAnswer>
镧系金属的氧化态对CH4激活的影响通过Ce()、Eu()和Yb()的计算进行了研究。研究发现，在σ-键互换反应过程中，镧系金属的氧化态保持不变。仅对Ce()、Eu()和Yb()的最相关系统进行了计算，以分析氧化态对反应性的影响。结果表明，氧化态对活化能的影响较小，平均约为13 kcal mol−1。然而，计算得到的Ce()的活化能显著较低，这可能是由于使用Cl作为Cp的模型导致的。研究表明，在低温条件下，Ce()可能在H2的存在下稳定，这是根据计算得出的结论。
