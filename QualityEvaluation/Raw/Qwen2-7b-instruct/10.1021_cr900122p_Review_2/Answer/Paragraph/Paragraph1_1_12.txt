        Metal-based catalysts, particularly supported vanadium oxides and metal-exchanged zeolites, play a pivotal role in alkane transformations through processes like oxidative dehydrogenation and zeolite-catalyzed reactions. The composition, structure, and reaction conditions of these catalysts significantly influence the mechanisms and efficiency of alkane transformations. Supported vanadium oxides excel in oxidative dehydrogenation due to their active sites, while metal-exchanged zeolites offer customizable acidity, enhancing product yield and selectivity. The redox properties of the feed, the number of surface vanadia sites, and the presence of additives like acidic and basic compounds all impact the efficiency of these catalysts. The use of a supported bipyrimidinylplatinum polyoxometalate hybrid compound exemplifies the effectiveness of these catalysts in converting methane to methanol and acetaldehyde, highlighting their industrial significance.

        The redox properties of the feed significantly affect the catalyst's surface oxidation state, profoundly impacting the nature of the products formed. For instance, the half-life of catalytic deuteration of propane is approximately 3 minutes, representing about 200 turnovers, showcasing the catalyst's efficiency. The stoichiometric reaction of the hydride with propane reveals that propane is removed and each sample is exposed to D₂₀ vapor, with the unique product in each case being propane-d, as determined by GC/MS. This study underscores the industrial relevance of metal-based catalysts in alkane transformation processes.

        In the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane over a supported vanadia catalyst, a hybrid density functional study of mechanisms on the (010) surface of V₂O₅ reveals that the highest energy on the potential energy surface is about 80 kcal/mol above the energy of the reactants, corresponding to the formation of an oxygen vacancy after water elimination. The barriers for propane adsorption and propene elimination are 4560 kcal/mol. Factors that may lower these barriers include the interaction of vanadium oxide clusters with a support material and a concerted reaction with O₂.

        The addition of steam to the hydrocarbon feed significantly enhances propane dehydrogenation over Ga/ZSM-5, leading to an increase in the steady-state concentration of hydroxylated reaction intermediates. This improvement is attributed to the more favorable conditions for hydrogen recombination in binuclear oxygen-bridged Ga₃⁺ complexes compared to mononuclear GaO⁺ ions. The presence of water facilitates the formation of reactive partly hydrolyzed gallium species, contributing to the enhanced catalytic activity. The nature of the supported species can vary significantly with the tungsten loading in W/HZSM-5 catalysts, which is crucial for the partial oxidation of methane to formaldehyde.

        Understanding the reactions between organometallic complexes and catalyst supports is fundamental for alkane transformation efficiency, particularly in oxidative dehydrogenation and zeolite-catalyzed processes. This knowledge enables the creation of well-defined surface species, crucial for mechanism and efficiency. Gallium in extra-framework positions accelerates alkene, diene, and cycloalkene dehydrogenation, and its dispersion and oxidation state within zeolite channels significantly influence alkane dehydrogenation and hydrocracking rates.

        The demand for maleic anhydride, used in manufacturing unsaturated polyester resins, agricultural chemicals, and pharmaceuticals, is set to grow over the next five years, driven by new applications. Supported vanadium oxides and metal-exchanged zeolites act as catalysts in oxidative dehydrogenation and zeolite-catalyzed processes, with orthophosphate (VOPO₄) phases transforming into (VO)₂P₂O₇ upon interaction with hydrocarbons. Enhanced Vv formation at the catalyst bed's end is attributed to a more oxidizing environment, and an increase in oxygen concentration boosts Vv formation and maleic anhydride production rate.

        In summary, metal-based catalysts significantly impact alkane transformations' mechanisms and efficiency in oxidative dehydrogenation and zeolite-catalyzed processes. Factors such as selectivity, stability, and robustness under harsh conditions are pivotal for their industrial significance. Supported vanadium oxides excel in oxidative dehydrogenation, while metal-exchanged zeolites enable controlled kinetics in zeolite-catalyzed reactions, making them indispensable in the petrochemical industry for processes like propane aromatization and maleic anhydride synthesis.

            金属基催化剂，尤其是支持的五氧化二钒和金属交换沸石，在烷烃转换过程中，特别是氧化脱氢和沸石催化反应中，发挥着关键作用。这些催化剂的组成、结构和反应条件显著影响了烷烃转换的机制和效率。支持的五氧化二钒在氧化脱氢方面表现出色，由于其活性位点，而金属交换沸石提供了可定制的酸性，提高了产品的产率和选择性。红氧化物性质的流体、表面五氧化二钒位点的数量以及添加剂如酸性和碱性化合物的影响都影响了这些催化剂的效率。使用支持的双吡啶基铂聚氧钼酸盐杂合化合物体现了这些催化剂将甲烷转化为甲醇和乙醛的有效性，突出了它们在烷烃转换过程中的工业重要性。

            饮料的红氧化物性质显著影响催化剂的表面氧化状态，极大地影响了产物的形成。例如，丙烷的催化脱氘化半衰期约为3分钟，代表约200个周转，展示了催化剂的效率。通过等摩尔混合物的丙烷和重水蒸气暴露后，每个样品的独特产物为丙烷-d，通过GC/MS确定。这一研究强调了金属基催化剂在烷烃转换过程中的工业相关性。

            在氧化脱氢过程中，向支持的范德华催化剂添加蒸汽显著增强了丙烷的脱氢，导致稳态浓度的羟基化反应中间体增加。这种改进归因于二核氧桥接Ga³⁺复合物中氢再组合的更有利条件，与单核GaO⁺离子相比。水分的存在促进了部分水解镓物种的形成，从而增加了催化活性。考虑的候选活性位点包括各种含镓的单核离子（Ga⁺、GaH₂⁺、GaH₂⁺、GaO⁺），实验观察到在473K时，氧化的Ga⁺物种在ZSM-5上的活性增加归因于gallyl（GaO⁺）离子的形成。随着水分含量的增加，主要产品仍然是丙烯，尽管丙烷、乙烯和甲烷的相对量略有增加。

            水的促进效果归因于形成活性部分水解镓物种。氢再组合的能量壁垒高达305kJmol⁻¹，这归因于与氧原子相连的氢原子的极低酸度。对于单核镓位点，催化循环由从[HGa³⁺(H)(OH)]⁺的H₂脱附形成GaO⁺离子启动，随后是烯烃脱附和氢再组合。这证明了镓基催化剂在烷烃转换过程中的效率和有效性。

            总之，金属基催化剂显著影响了氧化脱氢和沸石催化过程中烷烃转换的机制和效率。选择性、稳定性和在苛刻条件下保持稳定性等因素对它们的工业重要性至关重要。支持的五氧化二钒在氧化脱氢方面表现出色，而金属交换沸石则使裂解反应具有可控的速率，使其在石油化工行业对丙烷异构化和马来酸酐合成等过程至关重要。
