"Cyclohexene as a chemical probe of the low-temperature hydrogenation activity of Pt/Ni( ) bimetallic surfaces"
</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Cyclohexene serves as an ideal probe molecule for studying hydrogenation reactions because it undergoes multiple reaction pathways on various surfaces, providing insights into the influence of bimetallic catalysts on hydrogenation activity and selectivity. Its dehydrogenation, decomposition, and hydrogenation pathways offer a comprehensive view of how surface chemistry affects catalytic performance in industrial processes.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
