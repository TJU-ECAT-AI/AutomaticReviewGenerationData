        The spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in boron-based catalysts critically impact their performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions. Understanding the unique properties and reaction mechanisms of these sites, particularly 1-D edge active sites, is pivotal for designing more efficient catalysts suitable for industrial use. These insights contribute to optimizing catalyst design by focusing on site characteristics that enhance reactant interaction and transformation efficiency. For instance, studies have shown that BO oligomers or ring species in AlB2 can offer enhanced selectivity for propane oxidative dehydrogenation, while the spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in B-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in ODHP reactions (References \[1\], \[2\], \[3\]). The engineering of specific species in boron nitrous nanotubes further enhances olefin production, underscoring the importance of site dimensionality in catalyst efficiency (Reference \[4\]).

        A synthesis and characterization of silica-supported boron oxide catalysts for ODHP reveals that the spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites, especially 1-D edge sites, significantly affect catalytic performance. Understanding these sites' unique properties and reaction mechanisms is crucial for designing more efficient catalysts suitable for commercial applications (Reference \[5\]). Research on hexagonal boron nitride (hBN) as a catalyst for propane oxidative dehydrogenation emphasizes the role of the spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites, particularly 1-D edge sites, in enhancing catalyst efficiency, and this knowledge is pivotal in optimizing catalysts for commercial applications (Reference \[6\]).

        Studies have also investigated the role of vanadium oxide in alkane oxidative dehydrogenation, finding that the catalytic activity of vanadium oxide catalysts increases with V2O5 loading, balancing the activity of surface VOx species and their accessibility to reactants (Reference \[7\]). The use of combinatorial/parallel systems, including genetic algorithms and evolutionary catalyst selection approaches, has significantly advanced the preparation and study of catalytic systems, contributing to the discovery of new catalytic systems (Reference \[8\]). Moreover, it is noteworthy that most reported catalysts yield propylene below 30%, highlighting the importance of understanding the spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites, particularly 1-D edge sites, in enhancing catalyst efficiency (Reference \[9\]).

        Optimizing supported boron oxide catalysts on zirconia improves oxidative dehydrogenation of propane for ε-caprolactam synthesis. Key findings include exceptional catalytic efficiency, superior performance over zirconia alone, and an optimal 10% boron oxide load for maximum yield (Reference \[10\]). The dynamic self-dispersion of aggregated boron clusters into stable oligomeric boron species on MFI zeolite nanosheets under oxidative dehydrogenation of propane further highlights the impact of site configurations on catalytic efficiency (Reference \[11\]).

        In summary, the spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in B-based catalysts, notably 1-D edge active sites, significantly affect their performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions. Understanding these sites' unique properties and reaction mechanisms is crucial for designing more efficient catalysts suitable for commercial applications, as evidenced by the cited studies (References \[1\]-\[\[11\]). Future research endeavors might focus on exploring emerging fields and innovative methodologies, such as the role of specific boron species, site engineering techniques, and the integration of advanced computational methods to predict and optimize catalyst performance (Prospective Outlook).

        基于硼基催化剂中活性位点的空间分布和维度（1-D、2-D 和 3-D）对丙烷氧化脱氢反应（ODHP）性能的影响，深入理解这些位点的独特性质和反应机制，特别是 1-D 边缘活性位点，对于设计更高效的工业应用催化剂至关重要。这些见解有助于通过关注增强反应物相互作用和转化效率的位点特性来优化催化剂设计（参考文献 \[1\], \[2\], \[3\])。例如，研究显示，在 AlB2 中的 BO 多聚体或环物种可以为丙烷氧化脱氢提供更高的选择性，而硼基催化剂中活性位点的空间分布和维度显著影响其 ODHP 反应性能（参考文献 \[4\])。通过在硼酸盐纳米管中工程特定物种进一步增强烯烃生成，凸显了位点维度在催化剂效率中的重要性。

        对于二氧化硅支持的硼氧化物催化剂的合成和表征用于 ODHP，研究表明活性位点的空间分布和维度，尤其是 1-D 边缘位点，显著影响催化性能。了解这些位点的独特性质和反应机制对于设计适用于商业应用的更高效催化剂至关重要（参考文献 \[5\])。关于六方氮化硼（hBN）作为丙烷氧化脱氢催化剂的使用，强调了空间分布和维度在活性位点中增强催化剂效率的作用，并且这种知识直接指导了商业催化剂的开发（参考文献 \[6\])。

        在烷烃氧化脱氢中，对五氧化二钒的研究发现，随着 V2O5 负载量的增加，钒氧化物催化剂的活性提高，平衡表面 VOx 物种的活性及其对反应物的可接近性（参考文献 \[7\])。组合/并行系统（包括遗传算法和进化催化剂选择方法）的使用极大地提高了可以制备和研究的催化系统的数量，从而对新催化系统的发现做出了重大贡献（参考文献 \[8\])。值得注意的是，大多数报道的催化剂丙烯产量低于 30%，这突出了理解空间分布和维度对活性位点的影响以及特别是在 1-D 边缘位点中增强催化剂效率的重要性（参考文献 \[9\])。

        优化在锆酸盐上负载的硼氧化物催化剂可提高丙烷氧化脱氢的ε-己内酰胺合成。关键发现包括出色的催化效率、与单独的锆酸盐相比的优越性能以及在最大产率时的 10% 硼氧化物负载（参考文献 \[10\])。聚合硼簇在 MFI 粘土纳米片下的动态自分散形成稳定的硼多聚物物种进一步突出了位点配置对催化效率的影响（参考文献 \[11\])。

        总之，硼基催化剂中活性位点的空间分布和维度（特别是 1-D 边缘活性位点）显著影响其丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应性能。了解这些位点的独特性质和反应机制对于设计适用于商业应用的更高效催化剂至关重要，这在引用的文献（参考文献 \[1\]-\[\[11\]) 中得到了证明。未来的研究方向可能侧重于探索新兴领域和创新方法，如特定硼物种的作用、位点工程技术以及将先进的计算方法集成到预测和优化催化剂性能中（前瞻展望）。
