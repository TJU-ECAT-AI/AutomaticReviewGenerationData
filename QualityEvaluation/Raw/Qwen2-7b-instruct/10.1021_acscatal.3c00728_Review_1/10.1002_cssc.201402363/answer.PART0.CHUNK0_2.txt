Understanding the spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in boron-based catalysts, especially focusing on 1-D edge active sites, is fundamental to enhancing their performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions. These unique properties and reaction mechanisms allow for more efficient conversion and selectivity to propene, contributing to the development of advanced catalysts suitable for commercial use.
