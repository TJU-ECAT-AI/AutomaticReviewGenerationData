Coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts, a persistent challenge in propane dehydrogenation, involves understanding the mechanisms of its formation, addressing the associated challenges, and advancing catalyst design for enhanced performance and longevity.

Molecular dynamics simulation studies have elucidated the growth mechanism of coke on surfaces like Ni(111), highlighting the multifaceted nature of coke formation \([1]\). Prevention strategies, including modifying the catalyst surface chemistry to inhibit coke formation and developing novel catalyst designs with enhanced thermal stability, have been explored \([2]\).

        Incorporating materials with higher thermal stability into catalysts and employing cofeeding of hydrogen to remove coke precursors and weaken support acidity are among the preventive measures. Metal promoters, such as tin (Sn), directly influence the catalytic properties of Pt, balancing activity and stability \([4]\). However, determining the optimal type and loading of promoters remains an open question \([5]\).

        Regeneration techniques, which chemically remove deposited coke to restore catalyst activity, are crucial \([10]\). Lowering the regeneration temperature to reduce costs and stabilize activity \([11]\) is also a focus. On this foundation, three aspects are proposed for further reduction of coke's impact:

        1. **Optimizing Prevention Methods:** Enhancing the effectiveness of surface modification and promoter tuning to more precisely control coke formation.
        2. **Efficient Regeneration Techniques:** Developing more efficient and less energy-intensive methods to remove coke, aiming to prolong catalyst life without compromising activity.
        3. **Molecular-Level Models:** Creating detailed models of coke formation kinetics to better understand and predict coke behavior, facilitating the design of more resilient catalysts.

        This review highlights the progress made in understanding and mitigating coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts. Despite advances in catalyst design and prevention strategies, ongoing research is essential to refine these approaches and overcome remaining obstacles.         焦炭结构、组成和位置之间的复杂关系构成了管理焦炭沉积的主要挑战。分子动力学模拟研究已经阐明了在Ni(111)表面焦炭生长的机制，揭示了焦炭形成过程的多面性\([1]\)。预防策略包括修改催化剂表面化学以抑制焦炭形成和开发具有更高热稳定性的新型催化剂设计\([2]\)。
