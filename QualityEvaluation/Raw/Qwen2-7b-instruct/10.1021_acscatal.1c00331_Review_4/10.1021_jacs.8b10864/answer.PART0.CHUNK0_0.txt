<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Our understanding of coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts has recognized it as a significant challenge in the catalytic industry. Coke deposition can cause deactivation and poisoning of the catalyst, leading to a reduction in its efficiency and lifespan.
2. Various strategies have been developed to mitigate coke formation and enhance catalyst performance. These include prevention methods, innovative catalyst designs, and regeneration techniques.
3. Prevention methods involve utilizing promoters, modifying the catalyst structure, and adjusting operational conditions to reduce coke formation.
4. Novel catalyst designs aim to optimize surface properties and minimize the likelihood of coke deposition.
5. Regeneration techniques concentrate on removing deposited coke to restore catalyst activity.
6. Although progress has been made, there are still obstacles that necessitate further research, such as developing more effective regeneration methods and comprehending the fundamental mechanisms of coke deposition.
