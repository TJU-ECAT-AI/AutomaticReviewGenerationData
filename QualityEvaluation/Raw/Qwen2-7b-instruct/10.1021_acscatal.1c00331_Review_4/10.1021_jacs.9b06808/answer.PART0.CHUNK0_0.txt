<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Our understanding of coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts has significantly evolved over the last decade.
2. A major challenge is coke's negative impact on catalyst performance and longevity.
3. Strategies to address this include preventing coke formation, designing new catalyst structures, and implementing effective regeneration techniques.
4. Prevention methods involve modifying catalyst surface chemistry to prevent precursor adsorption to coke.
5. Novel catalyst designs may include additional metals or alloying elements to enhance stability against coke formation.
6. Regeneration techniques commonly involve thermal or chemical treatments to remove deposited coke.
7. Despite these advancements, challenges persist in maintaining high loading levels of active sites while minimizing coke deposition.
8. Further research is needed to optimize catalyst formulations and operating conditions for improved performance and durability.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
