<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The understanding of coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts has evolved to recognize its significant impact on catalyst activity and selectivity. Challenges associated with coke formation include decreased efficiency and productivity in industrial applications. Strategies to address this issue include prevention methods, which utilize chemical additives or adjust reaction conditions to inhibit coke formation. Novel catalyst designs aim to enhance stability and reduce the likelihood of coke deposition. Regeneration techniques focus on removing deposited coke to restore catalyst functionality. However, the effectiveness of these strategies varies depending on the specific catalyst system and reaction conditions. Remaining obstacles include a detailed understanding of coke deposition mechanisms and the development of universally effective prevention and regeneration methods.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
