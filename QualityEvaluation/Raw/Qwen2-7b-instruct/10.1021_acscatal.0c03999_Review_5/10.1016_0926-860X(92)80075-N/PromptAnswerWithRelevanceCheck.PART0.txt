<EnglishVersionAnswer>

Analyzing the properties that make zeolites promising support materials for CO2-ODHP catalysts, we see they possess unique features such as their porous structure, high surface area, and tunable acidity. Various methods, including dealumination and weakening, can be used to optimize catalytic performance. Dealumination reduces aluminum content in the zeolite framework, enhancing acidity. Weakening methods involve breaking the framework to increase surface area and acidity. Key factors in selecting and implementing these strategies include the type of zeolite, desired level of acidity, and impact on catalyst stability. Different types of zeolites, such as mesoporous and microporous, may require different approaches for optimal results. The effectiveness of these modifications depends on achieving a balance between acidity enhancement and maintaining structural integrity. Optimal conditions for these modifications must be carefully determined to avoid negative impacts on catalytic activity or selectivity.
