Analyzing the properties that make zeolites promising support materials for CO2-ODHP catalysts reveals their unique microporous structures, offering high surface areas and controlled pore sizes, which are essential for efficient adsorption and separation of CO2. Acidity modification techniques, such as dealumination and weakening, aim to optimize the catalytic performance of zeolite supports by enhancing acidity and creating new acid sites. Optimizing acidity is crucial for enhancing the performance of CO2-ODHP catalysts. The selection of strategies depends on the specific zeolite type, and considerations include the type of zeolite, desired acidity level, and potential impact on catalyst stability.
