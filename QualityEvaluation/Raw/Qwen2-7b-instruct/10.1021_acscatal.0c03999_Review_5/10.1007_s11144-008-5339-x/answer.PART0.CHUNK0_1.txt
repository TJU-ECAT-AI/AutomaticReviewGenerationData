<EnglishVersionAnswer>

The properties that make zeolites suitable for CO2-ODHP catalyst supports include their high surface area, pore structure, and acidic sites. Zeolites can be modified through dealumination and weakening methods to optimize catalytic performance. Dealumination involves removing aluminum ions from the zeolite framework, which can increase the number of acid sites available for the reaction. Weakening refers to reducing the overall strength of the zeolite structure, which can also enhance acidity. Key considerations in selecting and implementing these strategies include the type of zeolite, the desired level of acidity, and the specific requirements of the CO2-ODHP reaction process.

</EnglishVersionAnswer>
