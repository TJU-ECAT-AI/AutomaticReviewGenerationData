Zeolites can be optimized through acidity modification techniques such as dealumination and weakening methods. Dealumination reduces the amount of aluminum in the zeolite framework, which can lead to a more acidic environment that enhances the redox activity of iron species. Weakening methods involve altering the strength of the zeolite framework to control the acidity and stability of the catalyst. Key considerations in selecting and implementing these strategies include the type of zeolite used, the desired reaction conditions, and the balance between acidity and stability to achieve optimal catalytic performance.

Magnetite was considered an active phase of the catalyst in the dehydrogenation of isobutane in carbon dioxide, as it played a role in the redox cycle involving the reduction of magnetite with isobutane and the oxidation of iron with carbon dioxide. The amount of carbon deposited on the catalyst in carbon dioxide was lower than that in an argon atmosphere, suggesting that carbon dioxide helps to prevent carbon deposition on the catalyst.
