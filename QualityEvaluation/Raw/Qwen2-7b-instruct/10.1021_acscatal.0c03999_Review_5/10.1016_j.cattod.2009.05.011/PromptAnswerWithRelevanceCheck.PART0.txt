These materials can be modified to enhance their performance through techniques such as dealumination and weakening, which alter the acidity of the support.

Weakening, on the other hand, involves the alteration of the zeolite structure to weaken the acidity without completely removing it. This approach allows for a balance between maintaining catalytic activity and suppressing unwanted reactions. For instance, the introduction of weaker acid sites can enhance propene selectivity in the dehydrogenation of propane.

By facilitating the reverse water-gas shift reaction, CO2 contributes to the formation of the desired products while also suppressing the formation of coke, which is detrimental to catalyst longevity. How do these modifications contribute to optimizing catalytic performance, and what are the key considerations in selecting and implementing these strategies for different types of zeolites used as supports?

Zeolites are promising support materials for CO2-ODHP catalysts due to their unique properties. Specifically, they possess a well-defined pore structure that allows for efficient adsorption and desorption of reactants and products. Additionally, zeolites can be tailored to have specific acidity levels, which is crucial for the dehydrogenation of propane to propene in the presence of CO2. Acidic sites facilitate the dehydrogenation process, while the presence of basic sites can inhibit side reactions like cracking, oligomerization, and aromatization. 

To optimize catalytic performance, various acidity modification techniques are employed. Dealumination involves selectively removing aluminum species from the zeolite framework, which reduces the number of Brnsted acid sites. This helps to suppress side reactions, improving the selectivity for propene. Weakening methods, such as adjusting the Si/Al ratio, can also impact acidity. Increasing the Si/Al ratio typically leads to a decrease in both Brnsted and Lewis acid sites, which can enhance stability and selectivity. 

When selecting and implementing these strategies, key considerations include the type of zeolite used, the desired level of acidity, and the reaction conditions. For instance, HZSM-5 zeolites with high Si/Al ratios are found to be particularly effective in dehydrogenation reactions with CO2 due to their enhanced stability and propene selectivity. The FT-IR spectra of adsorbed pyridine on the catalysts provide insight into the nature of acid sites, allowing for the optimization of the acidity profile.

In summary, the use of zeolites as supports in CO2-ODHP catalysts leverages their ability to modulate acidity through dealumination and weakening methods. These strategies optimize catalytic performance by enhancing stability and selectivity. The selection and implementation of these techniques require careful consideration of zeolite type, acidity levels, and reaction conditions to achieve optimal results.

沸石是CO2-ODHP催化剂的有前景的支撑材料，由于它们独特的性质。具体来说，它们具有定义明确的孔结构，可以高效吸附和脱附反应物和产物。此外，可以通过特定的酸度水平来调整沸石，这对于在CO2存在下进行丙烷脱氢为丙烯至关重要。酸性位点促进了脱氢过程，而基团的存在则抑制了副反应，如裂解、多聚化和芳构化。

为了优化催化性能，采取了多种酸度调整技术。去铝化涉及从沸石框架中选择性去除铝物种，这减少了Brnsted酸位的数量。这有助于抑制副反应，提高丙烯的选择性。弱化方法，如调整Si/Al比，也可以影响酸度。增加Si/Al比通常导致Brnsted和Lewis酸位的数量减少，这可以增强稳定性和选择性。

在选择和实施这些策略时，关键考虑因素包括使用的沸石类型、所需酸度水平以及反应条件。例如，高Si/Al比例的HZSM-5沸石在CO2存在的脱氢反应中特别有效，因为它们的稳定性增强和丙烯选择性提高。对吸附在催化剂上的吡啶的FT-IR光谱提供了酸位性质的见解，允许优化酸度轮廓。

总之，将沸石用作CO2-ODHP催化剂的支撑材料利用了它们通过去铝化和弱化方法调节酸度的能力。这些策略通过增强稳定性和选择性来优化催化性能。在选择和实施这些技术时，需要仔细考虑沸石类型、酸度水平和反应条件，以实现最佳结果。
