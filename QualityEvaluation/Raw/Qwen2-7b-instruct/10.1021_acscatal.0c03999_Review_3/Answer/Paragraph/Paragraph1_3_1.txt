Metal oxide catalysts, particularly chromium (Cr)-based and vanadium oxide (VOx) catalysts, are prone to deactivation in oxidative dehydrogenation processes. The primary causes of deactivation include overoxidation, which is exacerbated by sintering and poisoning. Various regeneration methods, including reoxidation with air, carbon dioxide (CO2), or oxygen (O2), are effective but require different temperatures, with air reoxidation being less efficient due to its lower oxygen concentration. CO2 addition shows promise in mitigating overoxidation in VOx-catalyzed reactions.

        For instance, in reference [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3], it was found that regeneration in oxygen at 600°C for 5 minutes can completely restore the activity of CrOx/ZrO2 catalysts, and this recovery is repeatable after 4 cycles. However, CO2 reoxidation only allows a complete recovery in the second cycle, indicating its limited effectiveness in preventing further deactivation.

        In reference [10.1021/acscatal.6b00893], the use of oxygen in CO2ODHP helps remove coke, although it leads to a drastic decrease in propylene selectivity and C2C3 olefin selectivity. Moreover, the formation of inactive crystalline α-Cr2O3 at higher Cr loadings limits the activity and stability of the catalyst. To address this issue, high surface area CrSiO2 catalysts can be prepared through direct one-pot hydrothermal synthesis. Proper synthetic methods can also help overcome the problem of stability and activity loss.

        For VOx-based catalysts, overoxidation impedes achieving high propylene selectivity. CO2 addition in the feed gas helps reduce surface molecular oxygen species, neutralizing overoxidation sites. The preparation of VOx catalysts on mesoporous silica supports offers short-range, ordered mesopores and high dispersion of VOx species, which can improve the overall performance of the catalyst.

        In summary, the deactivation mechanisms of Cr-based and VOx catalysts in oxidative dehydrogenation processes are multifaceted, involving overoxidation, sintering, and poisoning. Various regeneration strategies, such as reoxidation with air, CO2, or O2, play crucial roles in restoring catalyst activity. CO2 addition, particularly when utilized in the feed gas, can mitigate overoxidation, leading to improved propylene selectivity and catalyst performance.

        根据参考文献[10.1016/j.cattod.2019.06.047]的深入分析，从[10.1021/ef501059k]和[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]的综合理解。金属氧化物催化剂，特别是铬(Cr)-基和钒氧化物(VOx)催化剂，在氧化脱氢过程中易发生失活。失活的主要原因包括过氧化，这在受到烧结和中毒的影响时会加剧。各种再生方法，包括空气、二氧化碳(CO2)或氧气(O2)的再氧化，虽然有效但需要不同的温度，其中空气再氧化由于其较低的氧浓度而效率较低。CO2添加显示出在VOx催化反应中缓解过氧化的潜力。

        比如，在参考文献[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]中，发现通过600°C下5分钟的氧气再生，可以完全恢复CrOx/ZrO2催化剂的活性，并且这种恢复在4个循环后是可重复的。然而，CO2再氧化仅允许在第二个循环中实现完全恢复，表明它在防止进一步失活方面的有效性有限。

        在参考文献[10.1021/acscatal.6b00893]中，向CO2ODHP中加入氧气有助于去除焦炭，尽管这伴随着丙烯选择性和C2C3烯烃选择性的大幅下降。此外，随着Cr负载量的增加，α-Cr2O3的形成会限制催化剂的活性和稳定性。为了解决这个问题，可以通过直接一锅法水热合成制备具有高表面积的CrSiO2催化剂。适当的合成方法也可以帮助克服催化剂稳定性和活性损失的问题。

        对于VOx基催化剂，过氧化阻碍了高丙烯选择性的实现。在气体流中添加CO2有助于减少表面分子氧物种，从而中和过氧化作用位点。在微孔硅支持上制备VOx催化剂提供了短程、有序微孔和VOx物种的高度分散，这些都能提高催化剂的整体性能。

        总之，Cr基和VOx催化剂在氧化脱氢过程中的失活机制是多方面的，涉及过氧化、烧结和中毒。各种再生策略，如空气、CO2或O2的再氧化，在恢复催化剂活性方面发挥着关键作用。CO2添加，尤其是当将其用于气体流中时，可以缓解过氧化，从而提高丙烯选择性和催化剂性能。

        整合理解和前瞻展望部分将深入探讨这些发现的含义，探索增强催化剂性能和稳定性的新兴技术和方法的可能性。未来研究可能侧重于优化催化剂的设计和合成、改进再生技术以及整合CO2利用，以实现更加可持续和高效的氧化脱氢过程。
