        Detailed analysis derived from the study referenced by \[10.1016/j.cattod.2019.06.047\] elucidates the primary causes of deactivation in metal oxide catalysts, specifically focusing on Cr-based and VOx catalysts, as overoxidation. The effectiveness of various regeneration methods, including reoxidation with air, CO2, or O2, varies significantly, as highlighted by \[10.1021/ef501059k\]. CO2 addition is noted as a potential strategy to mitigate overoxidation in VOx-catalyzed reactions, as discussed in \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3\].

        An in-depth exploration of the deactivation mechanisms in Cr-based catalysts, as detailed in \[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\], reveals that coke deposition and reduction of active sites are the leading causes. High temperatures, typically above 500°C, are required for effective reoxidation with air, as per \[10.1021/acscatal.5b01679\]. In contrast, CO2 reoxidation is less effective, only oxidizing to V2O4, indicating its inability to fully re-oxidize to V2O5 state, as reported in \[10.1021/acscatal.6b00893\]. Oxygen reoxidation, on the other hand, efficiently restores full activity but also necessitates high temperatures, as indicated in \[10.1016/j.cattod.2019.06.047\].

        To optimize the performance of these catalysts, the preparation of CrSiO2 catalysts with high surface area through direct one-pot hydrothermal synthesis, as described in \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3\], presents a viable solution. Controlling the Cr loading to maintain the unique pore structure, although sacrificing some activity, is crucial to overcoming the issue of inactive α-Cr2O3 formation at higher Cr loadings, as suggested in \[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\].

        For VOx-based catalysts, as analyzed in \[10.1021/acscatal.5b01679\] and \[10.1021/acscatal.6b00893\], overoxidation impedes achieving high C3H6 selectivity. CO2 addition in the feed gas is a promising approach to mitigate overoxidation, as it reduces surface molecular oxygen species by tuning adsorption-desorption equilibria on basic oxidizing sites, as explained in \[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\].

        The regeneration of active V sites in VOx catalysts differs, with V3+ being reoxidized through dissociative CO2 adsorption, while the regeneration in DHP follows the path of hydrogen migration, as outlined in \[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\].

        通过引用\[10.1016/j.cattod.2019.06.047\]的深入研究，可以了解到金属氧化物催化剂（特别是Cr基和VOx催化剂）失活的主要原因在于过氧化，而不同的再生方法，包括空气、二氧化碳或氧气的再氧化，其效果差异显著。\[10.1021/ef501059k\]中的文献揭示了CO2添加在VOx催化反应中缓解过氧化的可能性。

        在\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3\]中详细探讨了Cr基催化剂失活机制，表明焦炭沉积和活性位点减少是主要因素。通常需要高于500°C的高温进行有效的空气再氧化，如\[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]所述。相比之下，CO2再氧化的效果较差，仅能氧化到V2O4，这表明它无法完全还原至V2O5状态。氧气再氧化则能够高效地恢复全活性，但同样需要较高的温度，如\[10.1016/j.cattod.2019.06.047\]所述。

        为了优化这些催化剂的性能，通过直接一锅法合成制备具有高表面积的CrSiO2催化剂，以增强表面结构的独特性，从而克服更高Cr负载下α-Cr2O3形成导致的活性降低问题，如\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3\]所述。控制Cr负载以保持独特的孔结构，虽然牺牲了一定的活性，但对于解决催化剂稳定性问题至关重要。

        对于VOx基催化剂，如\[10.1021/acscatal.5b01679\]和\[10.1021/acscatal.6b00893\]所描述，过氧化阻碍了C3H6选择性的提高。通过将CO2作为气体加入，可以减少表面分子氧物种，通过调节基本氧化位点上的吸附-脱附平衡来缓解过氧化，如\[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]所述。

        VOx催化剂活性位点的再生过程存在差异，其中V3+通过解离CO2吸附进行氧化，而在DHP路径中氢迁移是再生途径之一，如\[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]所述。

        结合这些发现的整合理解表明，未来的研究方向可能集中在开发更耐受失活机制的催化剂，特别是在增强再生策略和优化催化剂制备技术方面的进步上。潜在的研究领域可能包括新型催化剂材料的发展、更有效再生协议的实施以及通过先进合成方法增强催化剂稳定性的策略。不仅将CO2作为原料使用，而且作为再生过程中辅助剂的应用，可促进氧化脱氢技术的重大进展，同时优化效率和可持续性。此外，利用机器学习算法预测催化剂性能和失活模式的能力，将有助于设计适应特定反应条件的更高效的催化剂系统。进一步探索不同催化剂组分之间的协同作用和发展多功能催化剂，也有可能进一步提升氧化脱氢过程中催化剂的整体性能和寿命。
