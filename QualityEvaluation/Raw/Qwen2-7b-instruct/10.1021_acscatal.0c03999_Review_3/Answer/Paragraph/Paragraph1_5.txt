The primary causes of metal oxide catalyst deactivation, focusing on Cr-based and VOx catalysts, include overoxidation. Various regeneration methods, such as reoxidation with air, CO2, or O2, exhibit differing effectiveness and temperature requirements. CO2 addition is noted as a method to mitigate overoxidation in VOx-catalyzed reactions.
        
        Coke deposition and reduction of active sites are major reasons for deactivation of Cr-based catalysts. Regeneration methods using air or CO2 at high temperatures (550-650°C) are effective for restoring most of the activity and selectivity. Oxygen at 600°C for 5 minutes can fully restore the activity of CrOx/ZrO2, with this recovery being repeatable after 4 cycles. Using CO2 as an oxidant only allows a complete recovery in the second cycle, failing to prevent further deactivation. Adding O2 in CO2ODHP helps remove coke but decreases C3H6 selectivity and C2C3 olefin selectivity. A complete recovery of active sites is challenging but can be achieved with well-dispersed Cr species. The formation of inactive α-Cr2O3 at higher Cr loadings limits activity and stability. To overcome this, CrSiO2 catalysts with high surface area can be prepared through direct one-pot hydrothermal synthesis. Controlling the Cr loading to maintain the unique pore structure sacrifices activity. Proper synthetic methods provide solutions to this issue. For VOx-based catalysts, overoxidation impedes achieving high C3H6 selectivity. CO2 addition in the feed gas helps reduce surface molecular oxygen species, neutralizing overoxidation sites. Four types of VOx species are present during the reaction: monovanadate, oligomeric vanadate, polyvanadate, and V2O5 crystallite. Alternative redox cycles, such as the V5+ V4+ cycle on an oxidized diamond-supported VOx catalyst, have been identified. Mesoporous silica supports are desirable for VOx catalysts, offering short-range, ordered mesopores and high dispersion of VOx species.
        
        CO2 addition is being considered for mitigating overoxidation in VOx-catalyzed reactions. Metal oxide catalyst deactivation in oxidative dehydrogenation processes, particularly for Cr-based and VOx catalysts, is mainly caused by sintering, poisoning, and overoxidation. Different regeneration methods like reoxidation with air, CO2, or O2 have been assessed. Air reoxidation is less effective compared to O2 reoxidation due to its lower oxygen concentration. CO2 reoxidation can help mitigate overoxidation in VOx-catalyzed reactions, maintaining the performance of the catalyst. Choosing proper synthetic methods to prepare catalysts provides solutions to overcome issues related to deactivation and regeneration.

        从参考文献\[10.1016/j.cattod.2019.06.047\]的深入分析出发，综合了来自\[10.1021/ef501059k\]和\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3\]的见解。金属氧化物催化剂失活的主要原因，特别是Cr基和VOx催化剂，是过氧化。不同的再生方法，如空气、CO2或氧气的再氧化，表现出不同的有效性和温度要求。CO2添加被确认为缓解VOx催化反应中过氧化的方法。

        Cr基催化剂失活的主要原因是焦炭沉积和活性位点的减少。使用空气或CO2在高温度（550-650°C）下的再生方法可以恢复大部分的活性和选择性。在600°C下用氧气处理5分钟可以完全恢复CrOx/ZrO2的活性，这种恢复在4个循环后可重复。使用CO2作为氧化剂仅允许第二次完全再生，无法阻止进一步的失活。在CO2ODHP中加入O2有助于去除焦炭，但降低了C3H6的选择性和C2C3烯烃的选择性。通过适当的合成方法制备具有良好分散性的Cr物种，可以实现活性位点的完全恢复。在较高Cr负载时形成不活跃的α-Cr2O3限制了活性并影响稳定性。为克服这个问题，可以通过直接一锅水热合成制备具有高表面积的CrSiO2催化剂。控制Cr负载以保持独特的孔结构牺牲了活性。合理的合成方法提供了克服此问题的解决方案。对于VOx基催化剂，过氧化阻碍了C3H6选择性的提高。在反应气体中添加CO2有助于减少表面分子氧物种，从而中和过氧化作用位点。反应中有四种类型的VOx物种存在：单钒酸盐、寡聚钒酸盐、多钒酸盐和V2O5晶粒。在氧化金刚石支持的VOx催化剂上的V5+ V4+循环已被识别为替代还原周期。对于VOx催化剂，介孔硅支持是理想的，提供短程、有序的介孔结构和VOx物种的高分散度。

        CO2添加被考虑用于缓解VOx催化反应中的过氧化。金属氧化物催化剂在氧化脱氢化过程中的主要失活原因包括烧结、中毒和过氧化。对空气、CO2或氧气的再生方法进行了评估。空气再氧化比氧气再氧化效率低，因为其氧气浓度较低。CO2再氧化有助于缓解VOx催化反应中的过氧化，维持催化剂性能。选择合适的合成方法来制备催化剂可以解决与失活和再生相关的问题。
