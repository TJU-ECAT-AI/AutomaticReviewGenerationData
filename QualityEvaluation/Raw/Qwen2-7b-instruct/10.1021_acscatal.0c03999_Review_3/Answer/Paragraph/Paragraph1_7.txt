        Metal oxide catalysts, particularly chromium (Cr)-based and vanadium oxide (VOx) catalysts, often suffer from deactivation in oxidative dehydrogenation processes. The primary causes of deactivation include overoxidation, sintering, and poisoning. Overoxidation is a significant factor, leading to the degradation of catalyst activity and selectivity.

        Various regeneration methods, including reoxidation with air, carbon dioxide (CO2), or oxygen (O2), have been explored to restore catalyst activity. Air reoxidation is effective but requires high temperatures, typically above 500°C. CO2 reoxidation is less effective, only oxidizing to V2O4, indicating the inability to fully re-oxidize to V2O5 state. Oxygen reoxidation is more efficient, restoring full activity but also demands high temperatures.

        CO2 addition is considered as a potential approach to mitigate overoxidation in VOx-catalyzed reactions. It assists in preserving VOx dispersion and improving propylene selectivity. CO2 addition in the feed gas of propane/oxygen mixtures enables a decrease in surface molecular oxygen species, neutralizing overoxidation sites.

        The formation of inactive crystalline α-Cr2O3 at higher Cr loadings limits activity and is detrimental to catalyst stability. To overcome this issue, researchers have prepared CrSiO2 catalysts through direct one-pot hydrothermal synthesis, maintaining the unique pore structure while sacrificing some activity. Proper synthetic methods offer solutions to this challenge.

        For VOx-based catalysts, overoxidation impedes achieving high C3H6 selectivity. CO2 addition in the feed gas helps reduce surface molecular oxygen species, neutralizing overoxidation sites. Four types of VOx species are present during the reaction: monovanadate, oligomeric vanadate, polyvanadate, and V2O5 crystallite. Alternative redox cycles, such as the V5+ V4+ cycle on an oxidized diamond-supported VOx catalyst, have been identified.

        Mesoporous silica supports are desirable for VOx catalysts, offering short-range, ordered mesopores and high dispersion of VOx species, enhancing the overall performance of the catalysts. Future research could focus on developing new synthetic methods, optimizing the support materials, and exploring novel redox cycles to further enhance catalyst performance and stability.

        Regeneration methods, such as reoxidation, require careful consideration of temperature requirements and the effectiveness of each method. CO2 addition can be a viable strategy for mitigating overoxidation, but its application depends on the specific reaction conditions and catalyst properties.

        特别是Cr基和VOx催化剂，在氧化脱氢过程中经常出现失活现象。主要失活原因包括过氧化、烧结和中毒。过氧化是导致催化剂活性和选择性降低的主要因素。

        复活方法，包括空气、二氧化碳或氧气的再氧化，已被探索用于恢复催化剂活性。空气再氧化有效，但需要高温（通常在500°C以上）。二氧化碳再氧化效果较差，仅能氧化到V2O4，表明无法完全重新氧化至V2O5状态。氧气再氧化效率更高，可以恢复全活性，但也需要高温。

        在VOx催化反应中加入二氧化碳被认为是缓解过氧化的潜在方法。它有助于保持VOx分散并提高丙烯选择性。向丙烷/氧气混合物中添加二氧化碳能够减少表面分子氧物种，中和过氧化位点。

        高Cr负载下形成的无活性α-Cr2O3限制了活性并损害了催化剂稳定性。通过直接一锅水热合成制备CrSiO2催化剂，可以维持独特的孔结构，同时牺牲一些活性。适当的合成方法为解决这一问题提供了解决方案。

        对于VOx基催化剂，过氧化阻碍了高C3H6选择性的实现。向气体中添加二氧化碳有助于减少表面分子氧物种，中和过氧化位点。反应中存在四种类型的VOx物种：单钒酸盐、寡聚钒酸盐、多聚钒酸盐和V2O5晶粒。识别了氧化金刚石支持的VOx催化剂上的V5+ V4+循环作为替代还原循环。

        硅基介孔支持材料对于VOx催化剂来说是理想的，提供短程、有序的介孔结构和高分散的VOx物种，从而增强催化剂的整体性能。未来的研究可能专注于开发更选择性和稳定的催化剂、优化再生过程，并探究不同添加剂的协同效应以改善催化剂性能。先进的材料科学、计算化学和过程工程技术的整合有可能引领氧化脱氢过程的重大突破，促进从烃类高效、可持续地生产有价值的化学品。
