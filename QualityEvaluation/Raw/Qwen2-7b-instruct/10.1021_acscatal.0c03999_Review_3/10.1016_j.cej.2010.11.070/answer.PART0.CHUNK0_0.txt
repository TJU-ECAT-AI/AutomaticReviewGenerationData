[1] "Metal oxide catalyst deactivation in oxidative dehydrogenation processes often involves overoxidation, especially for Cr-based and VOx catalysts."
[2] "Regeneration methods for metal oxide catalysts include reoxidation with air, CO2, or O2."

</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Overoxidation is a common cause of deactivation in Cr-based and VOx catalysts used in oxidative dehydrogenation processes. 
2. Regeneration methods for metal oxide catalysts typically involve reoxidation with air, CO2, or O2.
3. The effectiveness of these regeneration methods depends on the specific catalyst type, with air reoxidation usually being the most straightforward and effective approach.
4. Adding CO2 during the regeneration process can help reduce overoxidation in VOx-catalyzed reactions.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
