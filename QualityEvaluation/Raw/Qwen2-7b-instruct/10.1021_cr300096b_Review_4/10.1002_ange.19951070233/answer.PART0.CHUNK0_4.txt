<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The CO and H2 chemisorption techniques differ in their effectiveness for characterizing bimetallic catalysts. While electron, ion, and atom-based methods generally require vacuum conditions, photon-based techniques are applicable in situ. These methods can provide answers to various pertinent questions regarding the atomic structure of surfaces, their qualitative and quantitative chemical composition, the distribution of surface components, the electron structure of surfaces and adsorbed reactants, as well as their vibrational properties as indicators of chemical bonding behavior. Each technique offers unique insights into the complex interactions within bimetallic catalyst systems.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
