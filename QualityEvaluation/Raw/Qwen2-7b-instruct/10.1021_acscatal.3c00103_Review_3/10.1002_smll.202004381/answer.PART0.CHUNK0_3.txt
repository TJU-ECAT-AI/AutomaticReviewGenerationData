Answer: The hydroxyl anchoring method allows for the precise placement of active metal compounds within the crystal lattice structure of host materials, such as zeolites or metal-organic frameworks (MOFs). This is achieved through the coordination of hydroxy groups present on the surface of the host material with metal ions, creating a strong interaction that guides the metal compound's location and orientation within the crystal structure. This method enhances metal-support interactions by ensuring that the metal is optimally positioned for catalytic activity, leading to improved efficiency and selectivity in solid-gas reactions. Additionally, the controlled incorporation of metals through hydroxy anchoring can lead to increased stability of the catalyst, as the metal is securely embedded within the host material matrix, reducing the risk of leaching or deactivation over time.
