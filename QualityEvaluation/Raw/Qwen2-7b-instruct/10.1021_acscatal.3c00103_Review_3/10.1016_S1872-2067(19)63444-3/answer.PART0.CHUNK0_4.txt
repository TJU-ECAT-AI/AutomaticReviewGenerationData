Answer: The hydroxyl anchoring method facilitates the controlled incorporation of active metal compounds in crystal materials by creating strong interactions between the hydroxyl groups on the surface of the crystal and the metal ions. This results in enhanced metal-support interactions, which improve the activity, selectivity, and stability of heterogeneous catalysts in solid-gas reactions. The method allows for precise control over the location and orientation of the metal compounds on the crystal surface, leading to optimized catalytic performance.
