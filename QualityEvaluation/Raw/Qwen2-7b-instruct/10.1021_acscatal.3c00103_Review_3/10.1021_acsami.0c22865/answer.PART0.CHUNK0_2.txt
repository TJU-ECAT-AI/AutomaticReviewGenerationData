<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The hydroxyl anchoring method facilitates the controlled incorporation of active metal compounds in crystal materials by utilizing the hydroxyl groups on the surface of the support material as anchoring sites. This method leads to enhanced metal-support interactions due to the strong coordination of the metal ions with the hydroxyl groups, resulting in significant improvements in the activity, selectivity, and stability of heterogeneous catalysts in solid-gas reactions.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
