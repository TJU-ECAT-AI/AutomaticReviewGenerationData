<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The hydroxyl anchoring method facilitates the controlled incorporation of active metal compounds in crystal materials by offering a specific reactive site for metal deposition. This method strengthens metal-support interactions through hydrogen bonding, which leads to enhanced activity, selectivity, and stability in heterogeneous catalysts. By precisely positioning metals within the crystal structure, hydroxyl anchoring optimizes catalyst performance, thus having significant implications for the development of more efficient solid-gas reaction catalysts.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
