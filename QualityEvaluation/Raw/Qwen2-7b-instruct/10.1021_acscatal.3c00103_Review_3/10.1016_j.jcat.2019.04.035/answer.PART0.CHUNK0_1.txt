<EnglishVersionAnswer>

The hydroxyl anchoring method facilitates the controlled incorporation of active metal compounds in crystal materials by enabling a precise and selective attachment mechanism that creates strong covalent bonds between the metal species and the crystal surface. This enhances metal-support interactions, leading to improved activity, selectivity, and stability of heterogeneous catalysts in solid-gas reactions. By optimizing the distribution and bonding of active metals, this technique enables more efficient catalytic processes, resulting in higher yields and reduced side reactions.

</EnglishVersionAnswer>
