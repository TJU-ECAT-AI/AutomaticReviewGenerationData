The hydroxyl anchoring method facilitates the controlled incorporation of active metal compounds in crystal materials by providing a strong anchoring site for the metal compounds, thereby enhancing metal-support interactions. This method leads to improved activity, selectivity, and stability of heterogeneous catalysts in solid-gas reactions. Specifically, it allows for the formation of strong metal-support interactions, which are crucial for the efficient catalytic activity. The enhanced interactions ensure that the active metal species remain anchored to the support surface, preventing their dispersion and thus maintaining their catalytic effectiveness over multiple reaction cycles. The method also enables the precise control of metal loading, which can optimize the catalytic performance by balancing the availability of active sites with the support's structural integrity. Furthermore, the hydroxyl anchoring method can tailor the electronic properties of the catalyst surface, affecting the reactivity of the metal species and potentially leading to enhanced selectivity towards desired products in solid-gas reactions.
