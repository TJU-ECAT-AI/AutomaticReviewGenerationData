<quotes>
<p>Results from H2 chemisorption and H2-temperature-programmed reduction (H2-TPR) studies showed that a small amount of Pt addition could increase the chemisorption capacity and make the reduction of Co or Ni much easier, especially for Co-based catalysts.</p>
<p>The presence of Pt in the PtCo catalysts appears to promote the reduction of Co3O4 clusters to CoO, facilitating the reduction of cobalt oxides through hydrogen spillover.</p>
<p>Extended X-ray absorption fine structure (EXAFS) results confirmed the formation of PtCo and PtNi bimetallic bonds in the PtCo and PtNi bimetallic catalysts.</p>
<p>For the hydrogenation of cyclohexene, PtNi bimetallic catalysts showed higher hydrogenation activity than PtCo.</p>
<p>Overall, the PtCo and PtNi bimetallic catalysts showed higher hydrogenation activities than the corresponding monometallic catalysts and exhibited distinct hydrogenation activities for benzene and cyclohexene.</p>
</quotes>

1. The addition of a small amount of Pt to Co-based catalysts can increase chemisorption capacity and facilitate the reduction of Co or Ni, especially benefiting Co-based catalysts.
2. The presence of Pt in PtCo catalysts promotes the reduction of Co3O4 clusters to CoO through hydrogen spillover, enhancing the reduction of cobalt oxides.
3. The formation of PtCo and PtNi bimetallic bonds in the bimetallic catalysts was confirmed by EXAFS results.
4. In terms of cyclohexene hydrogenation, PtNi bimetallic catalysts demonstrated higher activity than PtCo.
5. The PtCo and PtNi bimetallic catalysts showed superior hydrogenation activities compared to monometallic catalysts, exhibiting distinct performances for benzene and cyclohexene hydrogenation.
