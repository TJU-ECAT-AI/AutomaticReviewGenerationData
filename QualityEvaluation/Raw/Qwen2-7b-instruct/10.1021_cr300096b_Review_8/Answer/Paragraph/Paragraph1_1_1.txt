        A comprehensive analysis of the utilization of Pt-3d catalysts in the hydrogenation of C═C bonds, particularly in propane dehydrogenation, has been conducted. The study emphasizes the integration of density functional theory (DFT) calculations with experimental validation techniques like temperature-programmed desorption (TPD) measurements. By correlating the calculated surface d-band center and adsorbate binding energies for a variety of C═C bond-containing molecules (linear alkenes, cyclic hydrocarbons, and aromatics) on Pt-3d catalysts, this research aims to predict hydrogenation activity and guide the design of more efficient catalyst systems. The findings from references \[1\], \[2\], and \[3\] have contributed significantly to our understanding of the structure-activity relationships governing the performance of Pt-3d catalysts in these processes.

        The DFT calculations provide insights into the electronic properties of the catalyst surfaces and the interaction between the adsorbates and the metal sites. These theoretical predictions are then compared with experimental data obtained through TPD measurements, which offer information about the adsorption and desorption kinetics of the molecules on the catalyst surface. This integrated approach enables a more accurate prediction of the hydrogenation activity of the Pt-3d catalysts under different conditions, thus facilitating the optimization of catalytic systems.

        The successful integration of DFT calculations with experimental observations highlights the potential of combining computational and experimental methods in catalysis research. It demonstrates that a systematic approach, incorporating both theoretical modeling and empirical validation, can lead to a deeper understanding of the complex interactions occurring at the catalyst interface. This approach not only aids in predicting the performance of existing catalysts but also opens up avenues for the rational design of new catalysts with improved activity and selectivity.

        对于使用Pt-3d催化剂进行C═C键氢化，特别是丙烷脱氢这一过程，进行了全面的分析。研究强调了密度泛函理论（DFT）计算与实验验证技术如温度程序脱附（TPD）测量的整合。通过将各种C═C键含分子（线性烯烃、环状烃类和芳香族化合物）在Pt-3d催化剂表面的表面d带中心和吸附剂结合能的计算结果与实验数据进行对比，旨在预测氢化活性并指导设计更高效的催化剂系统。参考文献\[1\]、\[2\]和\[3\]为理解Pt-3d催化剂性能的结构-活性关系提供了重要贡献。

        DFT计算提供了催化剂表面的电子性质和吸附剂与金属位点之间相互作用的见解。这些理论预测随后与通过TPD测量获得的实验数据进行比较，后者提供了关于分子在催化剂表面吸附和脱附的动力学信息。这种整合方法能够更准确地预测Pt-3d催化剂在不同条件下的氢化活性，从而促进催化系统的优化。

        成功整合DFT计算与实验观察表明，在催化研究中结合计算和实验方法的潜力。这显示了系统方法，包括理论建模和实证验证相结合，能够加深对催化剂界面复杂相互作用的理解。这种方法不仅有助于预测现有催化剂的性能，而且也为设计具有更高活性和选择性的新催化剂开辟了道路。
