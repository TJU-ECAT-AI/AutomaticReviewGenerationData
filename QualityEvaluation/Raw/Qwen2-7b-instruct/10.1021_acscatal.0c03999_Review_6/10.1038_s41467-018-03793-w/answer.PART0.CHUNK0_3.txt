1. The reducibility and support materials of Fe oxide-based catalysts play a crucial role in their performance in CO2-ODHP reactions. Ceria (CeO2) is a suitable support material because it has the ability to store and release oxygen, which might induce direct CO bond scission of CO2. This feature not only aids in the CO2-ODHP process but also provides available lattice oxygen for coke suppression, thereby improving the catalyst's efficiency and longevity.

2. The insights provided by the in situ X-ray absorption spectroscopy measurements highlight the dynamic changes in metal oxidation states under reaction conditions. These measurements, combined with density functional theory calculations, offer a comprehensive understanding of the reaction pathways, enabling researchers to optimize catalytic systems for CO2-ODHP processes effectively.

3. Fe3Ni catalysts demonstrate a promising selectivity for propylene production through the CO2-ODHP pathway, indicating their potential for efficient CO2 utilization and propylene generation. Conversely, Ni3Pt catalysts exhibit high selectivity for CO production via the DRP pathway, showcasing the versatility of bimetallic catalysts in different reaction mechanisms.

4. The comparative analysis of Fe3Ni and Ni3Pt catalysts reveals that Fe3Ni is more active for CO2-ODHP reactions, leading to the production of propylene, while Ni3Pt shows superior performance in DRP, producing CO. This distinction underscores the importance of catalyst composition in determining the specific reaction pathway and product distribution.

5. The DFT calculations predict that the bulk Fe3Ni(111) surface and the FeO/Ni (111) interface favor the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane to form propylene, supporting the CH bond scission pathway. On the other hand, the Pt-terminated Ni3Pt(111) surface is expected to promote the reforming reaction, favoring the cleavage of CC bonds to generate synthesis gas. These findings align with experimental observations, providing a theoretical foundation for the development of more efficient and selective catalytic systems for CO2-ODHP and DRP.

1. 铁氧化物基催化剂的可还原性和支持材料在二氧化碳氧化脱氢反应中起着关键作用。氧化铈（CeO2）是一种合适的支持材料，因为它能够存储和释放氧，可能促进CO2中的直接CO键断裂。这一特性不仅有助于二氧化碳氧化脱氢过程，还能提供用于抑制结焦的表面氧，从而提高催化剂的效率和寿命。

2. 在位X射线吸收光谱测量提供了有关金属在反应条件下氧化状态变化的洞察。这些测量与密度泛函理论计算相结合，为理解反应途径提供了全面的理解，从而帮助研究人员优化二氧化碳氧化脱氢过程中的催化系统。

3. Fe3Ni催化剂在二氧化碳氧化脱氢路径上显示出对丙烯的有前景的选择性，表明它们在有效利用二氧化碳和生成丙烯方面具有潜力。相反，Ni3Pt催化剂在重整路径上显示出高选择性，产生一氧化碳，展示了双金属催化剂在不同反应机制中的灵活性。

4. Fe3Ni和Ni3Pt催化剂的比较分析显示，Fe3Ni更适合二氧化碳氧化脱氢反应，导致丙烯的生产，而Ni3Pt在重整反应中表现出更好的性能，产生一氧化碳。这种差异强调了催化剂组成的的重要性，在确定特定反应途径和产品分布方面。

5. DFT计算预测，整块Fe3Ni(111)表面和FeO/Ni (111)界面有利于丙烷的氧化脱氢形成丙烯，支持CH键断裂途径。另一方面，Pt-终止的Ni3Pt(111)表面预计会促进重整反应，优先裂解CC键以生成合成气。这些发现与实验观察结果一致，为开发更高效、选择性的催化系统提供了理论基础，用于二氧化碳氧化脱氢和重整。
