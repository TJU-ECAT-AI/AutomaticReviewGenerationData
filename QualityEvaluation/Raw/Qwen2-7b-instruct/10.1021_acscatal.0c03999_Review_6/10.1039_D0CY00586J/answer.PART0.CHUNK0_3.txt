<quotes>
<p>The reducibility and support materials of Fe oxide-based catalysts influence their performance in CO2-ODHP reactions, providing insights into the proposed reaction mechanism and the optimization of catalytic systems for this process.</p>
<p>In this study, Fe-doped ceria materials were synthesized via a co-precipitation method. The calcination temperature was crucial for phase transformation and crystalline growth, as well as the removal of surface hydroxyl groups and possible carbon-based organics.</p>
<p>Fe-doped ceria materials with various Fe/Ce molar ratios were prepared and characterized by BET, Raman, and XRD. BET analysis revealed that calcination at 600°C was beneficial for higher specific surface area and the formation of Fe-Ce solid solution.</p>
<p>XRD analysis confirmed that all materials showed cerianite as the main crystalline phase upon calcination at 600°C. Raman spectroscopy was used to further analyze the structural implications of the Fe-doped ceria catalysts.</p>
<p>X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS) was employed to investigate the chemical state of the elements on the catalyst surface. Scanning transmission electron microscopy (STEM) was used to visualize the spatial distribution of the elements in the acquired area.</p>
<p>The catalytic performance of the Fe-doped ceria catalysts was evaluated in a fixed bed reactor for the CO2-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (CO2-ODHP). The outlet stream was analyzed for reactant conversion, selectivity, and yield to main products and by-products.</p>
</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The reducibility and support materials of Fe oxide-based catalysts significantly impact their performance in CO2-ODHP reactions, offering valuable insights into the reaction mechanism and strategies for optimizing catalytic systems. This study involved synthesizing Fe-doped ceria materials with various Fe/Ce ratios and evaluating their catalytic activity in a fixed bed reactor for CO2-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane. The materials were characterized using BET, Raman, XRD, XPS, and STEM techniques to understand their structural and chemical properties and to assess their performance in the CO2-ODHP process.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
