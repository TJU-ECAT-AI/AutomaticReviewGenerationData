The performance, reducibility, and reaction mechanism of Fe oxide-based catalysts in CO2-ODHP reactions are significantly influenced by the type of support materials they utilize. The reducibility of Fe oxides affects their reactivity towards CO2 and propane during the oxidation process, and support materials like carbon and metal oxides play a crucial role in stabilizing the catalyst structure and enhancing its activity. Insights from these studies suggest that optimizing the reducibility and carefully selecting the support materials could lead to more efficient catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions.

        The presence of CO2 promotes the dehydrogenation of propane, enhancing the yield of propylene and suppressing catalyst deactivation, especially on silica-supported catalysts. Pulse reaction studies further indicate that CO2 accelerates the dehydrogenation rate over Fe oxide catalysts, suggesting its beneficial role in the reaction mechanism. Higher reducibility in catalysts leads to better activity in these reactions, and the use of ceria as a support material enhances the stability and selectivity of the catalysts. Both aspects are crucial for optimizing catalytic systems for CO2-ODHP processes.

        The study elucidates how the reducibility and support materials of Fe oxide-based catalysts influence their performance in CO2-ODHP reactions. The use of tungsten oxide in the catalysts acts as a physical barrier, hindering the clustering of vanadium oxide species, which reduces the likelihood of non-selective oxygen formation and decreases carbon dioxide formation. Carbon dioxide, as an oxidant, can only partially re-oxidize the catalyst to V2O4, leading to higher propylene production and reduced carbon formation. The DFT-calculated Gibbs free energy profile indicates that propane dehydrogenation in the presence of CO2 can occur through both direct oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) and non-oxidative dehydrogenation followed by reverse water gas shift (RWGS) reaction. The activation of the secondary CAH bond of propane is identified as the rate-determining step in both pathways.

        碳氧化物的存在促进了丙烷的脱氢，提高了丙烯的产率并抑制了催化剂的失活，尤其是在二氧化硅支持的催化剂上更为显著。脉冲反应研究表明，CO2加速了Fe氧化物催化剂的脱氢速率，表明其在反应机制中的有益作用。更高的可还原性在催化剂中导致更好的活性，在这些反应中，使用铈作为支持材料可以增强催化剂的稳定性和选择性。这两个方面对于优化CO2-ODHP过程中的催化系统至关重要。

        研究阐明了Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中性能受可还原性和支持材料影响的方式。在催化剂中使用钨氧化物作为物理屏障，阻碍了钒氧化物种的聚集，降低了非选择性氧形成的可能性并减少了二氧化碳的形成。二氧化碳作为氧化剂只能部分将催化剂氧化为V2O4，导致更高产率的丙烯和较低的碳形成。基于第一性原理计算得到的吉布斯自由能图表明，二氧化碳存在下丙烷脱氢可以通过直接氧化脱氢（ODH）和非氧化脱氢后由逆水煤气变换（RWGS）反应组成的途径进行。丙烷次级CAH键的活化是两个途径中的决定性步骤。
