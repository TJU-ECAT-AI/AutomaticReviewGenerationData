        The performance, reducibility, and reaction mechanism of Fe oxide-based catalysts in CO2-ODHP reactions are significantly influenced by the type and reducibility of the support materials used. This insight offers a deeper understanding into the proposed reaction mechanism and guides the optimization of catalytic systems for CO2-ODHP processes. As highlighted in the studies \[10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4\], \[10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052\], \[10.1021/jp807864z\], \[10.1021/ja070908q\], \[10.1021/acscatal.7b03805\], \[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\], and \[10.1007/s11144-008-5339-x\], the reducibility of Fe oxides affects their reactivity towards CO2 and propane during the oxidation process, and support materials like carbon and metal oxides play a crucial role in stabilizing the catalyst structure and enhancing its activity. 

        Insights from these studies suggest that optimizing the reducibility and selecting appropriate support materials could lead to more efficient catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions. For instance, the presence of CO2 can promote the dehydrogenation of propane, enhancing the yield of propylene and suppressing catalyst deactivation, particularly on silica-supported catalysts \[10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4\]. This role of CO2 in the reaction mechanism is further corroborated by pulse reaction studies \[10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4\].

        The proposed reaction mechanism typically involves the formation of an active species on the catalyst surface, and the optimization of catalytic systems requires careful selection of both the reducibility and support materials \[10.1021/jp807864z\]. The use of ceria as a support material can enhance the stability and selectivity of the catalysts \[10.1021/acscatal.7b03805\].

        Future research might focus on exploring the synergistic effects of different support materials and their interactions with Fe oxide catalysts, aiming to further enhance the efficiency and selectivity of the catalytic systems for CO2-ODHP reactions.

        The integrative understanding and prospective outlook based on the provided information suggests a path for future research to explore the synergistic effects of different support materials and their interactions with Fe oxide catalysts, aiming to enhance the efficiency and selectivity of the catalytic systems for CO2-ODHP reactions. This could include the development of novel support materials and the optimization of existing ones to improve the overall performance of Fe oxide-based catalysts in CO2-ODHP reactions.

        铁氧化物基催化剂在二氧化碳氧化脱氢（CO2-ODHP）反应中的性能、可还原性和反应机制受支持材料类型和可还原性的影响显著。这些洞察提供了对提议反应机制的理解，并指导了CO2-ODHP过程催化系统的优化。根据文献 \[10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4\]、\[10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052\]、\[10.1021/jp807864z\]、\[10.1021/ja070908q\]、\[10.1021/acscatal.7b03805\] 和 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\] 的研究，铁氧化物的可还原性影响其对二氧化碳和丙烷的氧化过程的反应活性，而碳和金属氧化物等支持材料在稳定催化剂结构并增强其活性方面发挥着关键作用。

        这些研究的见解表明，优化可还原性和选择合适的支持材料可以导致更高效的CO2-ODHP反应催化剂。例如，二氧化碳可以通过促进丙烷的脱氢反应，提高丙烯的产率并抑制催化剂的失活，特别是在硅酸盐支持的催化剂上表现尤为明显 \[10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4\]。这种在反应机制中的二氧化碳作用通过脉冲反应实验进一步得到了证实 \[10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4\]。

        拟议的反应机制通常涉及在催化剂表面形成活性物种，而优化催化系统需要精心选择可还原性和支持材料 \[10.1021/jp807864z\]。使用铈作为支持材料可以增强催化剂的稳定性和选择性 \[10.1021/acscatal.7b03805\]。

        将来研究可能专注于探索不同支持材料的协同效应及其与铁氧化物催化剂的相互作用，旨在进一步提高CO2-ODHP反应中催化系统的效率和选择性。这包括开发新型支持材料和优化现有材料，以改善铁氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的整体性能。

        根据提供的信息，整合理解和前瞻视角建议了一条未来研究路径，旨在探索不同支持材料的协同效应及其与铁氧化物催化剂的相互作用，以增强CO2-ODHP反应中催化系统的效率和选择性。这可能包括发展新型支持材料并优化现有材料，以提高铁氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的整体性能。
