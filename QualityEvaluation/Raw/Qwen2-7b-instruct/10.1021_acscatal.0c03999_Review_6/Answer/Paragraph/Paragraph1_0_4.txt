    <English>
        The performance, reducibility, and reaction mechanism of Fe oxide-based catalysts in CO2-ODHP reactions have been extensively studied, with particular emphasis on the role of support materials and the relationship between catalyst properties and catalytic activity. The literature highlights that the reducibility and type of support material significantly impact the effectiveness of these catalysts in CO2-ODHP reactions. Reducibility influences the reactivity of Fe oxides towards CO2 and propane during the oxidation process, and various support materials, including carbon and metal oxides, stabilize the catalyst structure and enhance its activity.

        Insights from these studies suggest that optimizing the reducibility and carefully selecting the support materials could lead to more efficient catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions. The presence of CO2 promotes the dehydrogenation of propane, enhancing the yield of propylene and suppressing catalyst deactivation, especially when silica is used as a support. Pulse reaction studies further indicate that CO2 accelerates the dehydrogenation rate over Fe oxide catalysts, highlighting its beneficial role in the reaction mechanism.

        The DFT-calculated Gibbs free energy profile suggests that propane dehydrogenation in the presence of CO2 can occur through both direct oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) and non-oxidative dehydrogenation followed by reverse water gas shift (RWGS) reaction. The activation of the secondary CAH bond of propane is identified as the rate-determining step in both pathways. 

        The study elucidates how the reducibility and support materials of Fe oxide-based catalysts influence their performance in CO2-ODHP reactions. Higher reducibility in catalysts leads to better activity in these reactions, and the use of ceria as a support material enhances the stability and selectivity of the catalysts. Both aspects are crucial for optimizing catalytic systems for CO2-ODHP processes.

        Looking ahead, there is an opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies in the development of more efficient catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions. Future research might focus on designing catalysts with enhanced reducibility and novel support materials to further improve the efficiency and selectivity of CO2-ODHP processes.

    <Chinese>
        在CO2-ODHP反应中，铁氧化物基催化剂的性能、可还原性和反应机制已进行了深入研究，特别关注了支撑材料的作用以及催化剂性质与催化活性之间的关系。文献表明，铁氧化物的可还原性及其类型对CO2-ODHP反应的有效性有显著影响。可还原性影响了铁氧化物在氧化过程中对CO2和丙烷的反应活性，而碳和金属氧化物等不同类型的支撑材料则稳定了催化剂结构并提升了其活性。

        这些研究的见解表明，通过优化可还原性和精心选择支撑材料可以开发出更高效的CO2-ODHP反应催化剂。二氧化碳的存在促进了丙烷的脱氢，提高了丙烯的产率并抑制了催化剂的失活，特别是在使用硅酸盐作为支撑材料时。脉冲反应研究表明，二氧化碳加速了Fe氧化物催化剂的脱氢速率，这进一步强调了其在反应机制中的有益作用。

        根据DFT计算的吉布斯自由能图谱，可以看出在二氧化碳存在下，丙烷脱氢可以通过直接氧化脱氢（ODH）和非氧化脱氢后通过逆水煤气变换（RWGS）反应进行。丙烷次级CAH键的激活被识别为两个途径中的决定性步骤。

        文献揭示了Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的性能如何受到可还原性和支撑材料的影响。更高可还原性的催化剂在这些反应中表现出更好的活性，并且使用铈作为支撑材料可以增强催化剂的稳定性和选择性。这两个方面对于优化CO2-ODHP过程的催化系统至关重要。

        展望未来，有潜力探索新兴领域和创新方法来开发更高效的CO2-ODHP反应催化剂。未来研究可能集中在设计具有增强可还原性和新型支撑材料的催化剂，以进一步提高CO2-ODHP过程的效率和选择性。
