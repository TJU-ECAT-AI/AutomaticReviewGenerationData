<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Halogen-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane utilizes iodine (I2) to form propene. This process contrasts with oxyhalogenation, which involves oxygen and a halogen source, and the molten metal halide salt approach, where reactions take place in a molten lithium iodide (LiI) environment. Propane exhibits different reactivity compared to methane and ethane because of its larger molecular size and higher boiling point, affecting its interaction with halogen compounds.
