Gallium doping improves the catalytic performance of Pt catalysts in PDH, increasing propylene selectivity and enhancing catalytic stability. XRD results show that Ga31 cations are incorporated into the host lattice structure of ceria (CeO2), enhancing the redox property of the catalysts. The doping of Ga enhances the redox property of catalysts and modifies the catalytic properties of Pt nanoparticles. The addition of Ga forms PtGa alloys, which have a combination of geometric and electronic effects that contribute to decreasing the size of Pt ensembles, blocking low-coordinated defect active sites, and suppressing structure-sensitive side reactions like hydrogenolysis and deep dehydrogenation. The electronic effect of the promoter lowers the desorption barrier of alkenes and coke precursors, hindering their adsorption on metal sites and decreasing the likelihood of hydrogenolysis and coking.
