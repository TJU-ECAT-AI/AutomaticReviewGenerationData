            Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The study of hydrogen cofeeding in the propane dehydrogenation (PDH) process reveals a complex interplay between coke formation, catalyst stability, and reaction dynamics. By lowering energy barriers, hydrogen cofeeding influences coke accumulation patterns, acid site conversions, and mitigates graphitization, enhancing overall process efficiency and performance. The incorporation of hydrogen into the PDH process not only reduces the formation of coke, thereby improving catalyst stability, but also optimizes reaction pathways, leading to a more selective production of propylene. The reduction in energy barriers facilitates a more efficient conversion of propane, minimizing the accumulation of coke on the catalyst surface, and significantly decreases the deactivation rate of the catalyst. Moreover, hydrogen cofeeding enables a more selective reaction mechanism, favoring the dehydrogenation of light alkanes, which in turn reduces the formation of coke precursors, contributing to improved overall process efficiency. Additionally, hydrogen's role in mitigating the risk of graphitization further enhances catalyst performance, providing a promising avenue for optimizing the PDH process. Future research could delve deeper into the detailed mechanisms of hydrogen's impact on coke formation, catalyst stability, and reaction pathways, aiming to develop more efficient and sustainable PDH catalysts that can withstand longer operational cycles while maintaining high propylene selectivity and productivity.

            综合理解和前瞻性展望：对氢气共喂入丙烷脱氢（PDH）过程的研究揭示了焦炭形成、催化剂稳定性和反应动力学之间复杂而相互关联的关系。通过降低活化能，氢气共喂入影响焦炭积累模式、酸位转换，并抑制石墨化，从而提高整体工艺效率和性能。将氢气引入PDH过程中不仅减少了焦炭的形成，从而提高了催化剂稳定性，而且优化了反应路径，使丙烯的选择性生产成为可能。氢气的引入不仅减少了能量壁垒，促进了更高效的丙烷转化，减少了催化剂表面焦炭的累积，显著降低了催化剂的失活率，而且还使得反应机制更加选择性，优先促进轻烷烃的脱氢，进而减少焦炭前体的形成，从而提高整体过程效率。此外，氢气在减少石墨化风险方面的作用进一步提升了催化剂性能，为开发能够承受更长运行周期、保持高丙烯选择性和生产力的PDH催化剂提供了有前景的途径。未来研究可以深入探讨氢气对焦炭形成、催化剂稳定性和反应路径的影响机制，旨在发展更高效、可持续的PDH催化剂。
