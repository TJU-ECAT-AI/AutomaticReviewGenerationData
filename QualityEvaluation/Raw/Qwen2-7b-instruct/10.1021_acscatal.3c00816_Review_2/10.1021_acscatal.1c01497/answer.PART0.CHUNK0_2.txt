<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The primary synthesis methods for metal-zeolite catalysts in propane dehydrogenation reactions include co-precipitation, impregnation, and sol-gel techniques. Metal-zeolite catalysts with MFI and BEA topologies are preferred because they offer high surface area and pore size selectivity, crucial for efficient PDH reactions. Their structural features, including pore size and shape, facilitate the separation of reactants and products, boosting catalytic efficiency. Additionally, these zeolites demonstrate exceptional stability and resistance to sintering, making them suitable for industrial applications. Future advancements in PDH catalyst design might concentrate on enhancing metal dispersion within the zeolite structure to further increase activity and selectivity.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
