<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Metal-zeolite catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) primarily use two types of zeolites: MFI and BEA, due to their unique structural characteristics and properties.
2. Their effectiveness in PDH applications is attributed to the high surface area, well-defined pore structures, and the ability to control the size of the pores, which facilitates the optimal catalytic activity.
3. The dominance of MFI and BEA zeolites in PDH catalysts is influenced by factors such as the ease of synthesis, stability under reaction conditions, and the ability to tune the surface chemistry to enhance selectivity and efficiency.
4. Future developments in PDH catalyst design may focus on improving thermal stability, increasing activity and selectivity, and reducing the sintering of metals, while maintaining or enhancing the structural integrity of the zeolite framework.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
