<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The primary synthesis methods for metal-zeolite catalysts in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) involve hydrothermal synthesis and impregnation. Zeolites with MFI and BEA topologies dominate due to their unique physical properties that facilitate catalysis. Their structures allow for efficient adsorption and desorption of reactants, enhancing PDH efficiency. The incorporation of metals like tin (Sn) and cerium (Ce) into the zeolite framework modifies their electronic and geometric properties, improving catalyst performance. The Ce incorporation specifically reduces the catalyst's weak acidity, aiding in coke migration and enhancing resistance to coke formation. The optimal Ce content of 0.76 wt% in the support maximizes PDH activity and stability, achieving high propene selectivity.
