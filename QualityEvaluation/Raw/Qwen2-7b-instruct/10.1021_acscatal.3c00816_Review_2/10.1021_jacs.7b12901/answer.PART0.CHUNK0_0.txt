<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The primary synthesis methods for metal-zeolite catalysts used in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions involve the creation of materials with specific topologies, notably MFI and BEA structures. These zeolites dominate due to their unique properties, including high surface area, well-defined pore structures, and the ability to modulate acidity. The effectiveness of these materials is attributed to their ability to efficiently facilitate the dehydrogenation process, converting light alkanes into higher-value olefins. The implications for future developments in PDH catalyst design include the potential for enhanced selectivity, improved stability, and increased activity through modifications such as dopants, support systems, and the engineering of new zeolite frameworks.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
