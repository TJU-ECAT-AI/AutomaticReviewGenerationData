Zeolites with MFI and BEA topologies dominate in this application due to their unique structural characteristics, such as high surface area, large pore volumes, and well-defined frameworks. The presence of Al sites in the zeolite framework also plays a role in facilitating the interaction of metal species.          The implication for future developments in PDH catalyst design is that there is a need to optimize the synthesis methods to achieve a precise balance between the metal dispersion, the zeolite framework's acidity, and the presence of Al sites. Additionally, exploring the use of novel metal-zeolite combinations or developing new zeolite topologies with improved properties for metal dispersion might lead to more efficient and selective catalysts for PDH reactions. The key lies in understanding how these structural and compositional factors affect the catalytic performance and then tailoring the catalysts to meet the specific requirements of PDH processes.
