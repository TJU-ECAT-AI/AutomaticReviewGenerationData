<p>Typically, the commercial fixed-bed CrOx/Al2O3 catalysts undergo 6000080000 dehydrogenation-regeneration cycles,138 and thus, the surface Cr species probably change during the continuous cycles.</p>
          <p>For propane dehydrogenation over the catalyst with excellent performance under appropriate conditions, the highest selectivity to propylene is above 99 mol%.</p>
          <p>The induction period can also be detected since the coordinatively unsaturated Zrcus sites are active for dehydrogenation, and thus the high temperature reduction by hydrogen prior to reaction can facilitate the formation of the active Zrcus sites,146 and shorten the induction period effectively.</p>
          <p>For the dehydrogenation of isobutane, the selectivity to isobutylene exceeds 80% for isobutane dehydrogenation over all these catalysts.</p>
          <p>In the process of dehydrogenation, CrOx catalyst deactivates by coking in a short time and requires to be regenerated by coke-burning frequently, while the PtSn catalyst deactivates due to coking and sintering, and needs to be regenerated by oxychlorination with air and chloride.</p>
          <p>For the ADHO process, the reaction/regeneration system consists of a reactor and a regenerator, and they have similar structures.</p>
