<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The primary synthesis methods for metal-zeolite catalysts used in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions involve the incorporation of metal ions into the zeolite framework through various techniques such as ion exchange, co-precipitation, and impregnation. These methods aim to stabilize the metal species within the zeolite structure, enhancing their reactivity and stability during PDH processes. MFI and BEA topological zeolites dominate in this application due to their specific pore structures and high surface areas, which facilitate the efficient adsorption and reaction of propane molecules. Their structural characteristics, including the size and shape of the pores, contribute significantly to their effectiveness in PDH reactions. The implications for future developments in PDH catalyst design include the optimization of metal loading, the selection of appropriate metal ions for enhanced activity, and the modification of zeolite frameworks to tailor their properties for improved performance under PDH conditions.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
