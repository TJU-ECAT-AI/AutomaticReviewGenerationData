<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The preparation methods, dopant concentrations, and vacancy formation in nickel oxide-based catalysts influence their electronic structure and catalytic performance in oxidative dehydrogenation reactions such as ethylbenzene to styrene and ethane to ethylene. The key challenges in theoretical modeling involve understanding the complex electronic interactions, optimizing the process requires knowledge of reaction kinetics and mechanisms, and balancing efficiency, selectivity, and safety demands meticulous control over the catalyst conditions.
