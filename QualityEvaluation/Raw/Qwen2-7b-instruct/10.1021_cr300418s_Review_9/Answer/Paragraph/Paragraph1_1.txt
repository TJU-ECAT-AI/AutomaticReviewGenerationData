        The synthesis and application of doped nickel oxide materials in oxidative dehydrogenation reactions, particularly in the conversion of hydrocarbons to valuable chemicals, have been extensively studied. The preparation methods, dopant concentrations, and vacancy formation in these catalysts significantly influence their electronic structure and catalytic performance. As highlighted in the referenced studies \[10.1021/j100267a002\] and \[10.1016/j.cattod.2003.10.015\], the bond strength of the weakest C-H bond, the presence of radical-forming sites on the surface, and the propensity of radicals to desorb or undergo further surface reactions are pivotal factors that dictate the catalytic efficiency.

        Lead monoxide (PbO), for instance, exhibits unique transformation properties when subjected to specific conditions, converting to red lead (Pb2(II)Pb(IV)O), a state that affects its chemical behavior and surface properties \[10.1021/j100267a002\]. This transformation underscores the dynamic nature of oxide surfaces under reactive conditions and their potential impact on catalytic performance.

        The role of dopants, such as antimony (Sb), is particularly noteworthy. In the context of alumina-supported vanadium oxide catalysts, antimony acts as an effective co-component, enhancing both the activity and stability of the catalysts in CO2-ethylbenzene dehydrogenation (EBDH) reactions \[10.1016/j.cattod.2003.10.015\]. It not only boosts the initial styrene yield but also ensures stable catalytic performance over time. This effect can be attributed to antimony's ability to function as a spillover oxygen donor, influencing the remote control mechanism of the catalyst's active sites and preventing their deep reduction through re-oxidation \[10.1016/j.cattod.2003.10.015\].

        The dispersion of vanadium oxide on the support surface is another critical factor that contributes to the overall stability of the catalysts. Increased amounts of mobile lattice oxygen facilitate redox cycles, thereby enhancing the catalyst's efficiency in promoting the desired chemical transformations \[10.1016/j.cattod.2003.10.015\].

        在氧化脱氢反应（如乙苯转化为苯乙烯和乙烷转化为乙烯）中的丙烷脱氢催化剂的制备、性质及其催化应用方面，掺杂镍氧化物材料发挥着关键作用。这些材料的准备方法、掺杂浓度以及空位的形成对它们的电子结构和催化性能产生了深远影响。

        参考文献 \[10.1021/j100267a002\] 和 \[10.1016/j.cattod.2003.10.015\] 深入探讨了这一领域的研究。通过这些研究，我们可以了解到，最弱C-H键的键能、表面特定自由基生成点的存在以及自由基的脱附或进一步表面反应的倾向性是决定催化效率的关键因素。

        铅氧化物（PbO）在特定条件下表现出独特的转变特性，转化为红铅（Pb2(II)Pb(IV)O），这种状态改变了其化学行为和表面性质 \[10.1021/j100267a002\]。这种转变强调了氧化物表面在活性条件下的动态特性和其对催化性能的影响。

        掺杂元素，例如锑（Sb），扮演着重要角色。在铝酸盐支持的钒氧化物催化剂中，锑不仅作为有效共成分增强催化剂活性和稳定性，还能提高CO2-乙苯脱氢（EBDH）反应的活性和特别是在在线稳定性方面 \[10.1016/j.cattod.2003.10.015\]。它不仅提高了初始苯乙烯产率，还确保了长期稳定的催化性能。这一效果可归因于锑作为远程控制机制下主动位点的溢出氧供体功能，通过再氧化防止活性位点的深层还原 \[10.1016/j.cattod.2003.10.015\]。

        支持表面的钒氧化物分散是另一个关键因素，它促进了催化剂的整体稳定性。增加移动晶格氧的量促进原电池循环，从而增强催化剂促进所需化学转化的能力 \[10.1016/j.cattod.2003.10.015\]。
