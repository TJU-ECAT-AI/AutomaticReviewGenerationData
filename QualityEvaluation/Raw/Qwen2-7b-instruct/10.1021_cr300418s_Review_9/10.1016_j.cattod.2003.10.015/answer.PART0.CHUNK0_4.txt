Catalysis Today 87 (2003) 205212 Catalytic dehydrogenation of ethylbenzene with carbon dioxide: promotional effect of antimony in supported vanadiumantimony oxide catalysts
</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The document discusses the catalytic dehydrogenation of ethylbenzene with carbon dioxide, focusing on the promotion effect of antimony in supported vanadiumantimony oxide catalysts. The study involves the testing of alumina-supported vanadium oxide (VOx/Al2O3) and binary vanadiumantimony oxides (VSbOx/Al2O3) in the ethylbenzene dehydrogenation process with carbon dioxide. It is noted that the VSbOx/Al2O3 catalysts exhibit enhanced catalytic activity and especially on-stream stability compared to VOx/Al2O3 catalysts. Incorporation of antimony into VOx/Al2O3 increases the dispersion of active VOx species, enhances the redox properties of the systems, and forms a new mixed vanadium antimony oxide phase in the most catalytically efficient V0.43Sb0.57Ox/Al2O3 system.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
