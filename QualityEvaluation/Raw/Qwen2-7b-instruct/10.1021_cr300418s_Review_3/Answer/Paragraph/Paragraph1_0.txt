The coprecipitation method, a versatile technique for synthesizing doped ceria catalysts, was employed across these studies. The method typically involves the treatment of a solution containing salts of the host cation (cerium) and dopant (such as gadolinium, yttrium, lanthanum, samarium, ytterbium, praseodymium, niobium, tantalum, or zirconium) with chemicals that precipitate both cations simultaneously. The choice of salt for the dopant depends on its chemical nature, which dictates whether nitrate, chloride, or oxynitrate is used.

        The incorporation of dopants like niobium (Nb) into ceria (CeO2) through the coprecipitation method leads to the creation of novel catalysts with distinct properties. For instance, the two Nb-doped ceria samples in reference \[10.1021/cr300418s_3\] exhibited differing n-butane combustion rates, with one sample showing a 5% higher rate compared to the other. Similarly, the effective activation energies varied between the two samples, with the sample prepared from hydroxides having an activation energy of 85 kJ/mol, contrasting with the 105 kJ/mol observed for the other sample.

        The impact of different preparation techniques on the structural properties and catalytic performance of the resulting catalysts was a recurring theme across these studies. As illustrated in reference \[10.1134/s0023158408050121\], the pore sizes of mesoporous Ce1 xFexO2 mixed oxide catalysts, prepared via the citric acid sol-gel method, were typically around 250 nm, offering a balance between high surface area and moderate pore structure advantageous for the adsorption, diffusion, and reaction of organic molecules on the catalyst surfaces. The specific surface area of Ce1 xFexO2 mixed oxides decreased with increasing Fe content, indicating that a small amount of Fe doping could lead to a higher specific surface area and narrower pore size distribution.

        In reference \[10.1021/jp803775j\], Nb-doped ceria catalysts demonstrated enhanced catalytic performance relative to undoped ceria, highlighting the significance of the dopant type in influencing both the structure and catalytic efficiency. For example, Nb doping was shown to boost the activity of ceria catalysts in n-butane combustion reactions.

        Lastly, reference \[10.1021/cm800144q\] underscores the role of ultrasonic spray pyrolysis in the synthesis of doped ceria catalysts, noting that various preparation techniques significantly affect the structural properties and catalytic performance of the catalysts.
