The coprecipitation method for synthesizing doped ceria catalysts involves mixing aqueous solutions of cerium salts and dopant salts under alkaline conditions, followed by precipitation and calcination. Variations in the method might include the type of dopants used, such as niobium (Nb), and the specific conditions of pH, temperature, and time during coprecipitation. Different preparation techniques can significantly affect the structural properties and catalytic performance of the resulting catalysts. For instance, Nb-doped ceria catalysts were found to enhance the n-butane combustion rates and decrease activation energies compared to undoped ceria. This improvement is attributed to the introduction of Nb ions, which alter the oxygen vacancy density and surface structure, thereby improving the catalyst's activity and stability.
