<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Recent economic factors, notably the expansion of shale gas production, have significantly increased the availability and affordability of raw materials for olefin production, fueling advancements in dehydrogenation technologies. Key technological developments focus on enhancing catalyst efficiency, reactor design, and membrane separation techniques. Challenges include improving catalyst stability and membrane durability under harsh conditions. Optimizing reactor operations, selecting suitable sweep gases, and implementing sustainable practices are crucial for efficiency, sustainability, and industrial viability. Addressing these challenges and integrating renewable energy sources will contribute to greener manufacturing processes and environmental responsibility.
