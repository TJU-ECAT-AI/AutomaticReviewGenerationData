        Future challenges include addressing the limitations of palladium membranes, refining models to account for potential contamination issues, and exploring innovative methods for waste heat recovery. The ability of membrane reactors to operate under less severe conditions could reduce the extent of catalyst coking and the frequency of regeneration, contributing to more sustainable industrial practices.
