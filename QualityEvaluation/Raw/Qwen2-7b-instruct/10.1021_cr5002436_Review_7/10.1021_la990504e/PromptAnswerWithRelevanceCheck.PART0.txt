[1] "The dehydrogenation of alkanes may be performed in inert conditions. Up to the present supported chromia catalysts have been used for this purpose, 1-3 but this nonoxidative reaction presents some disadvantages. Indeed at the reaction temperature, usually above 800 K, undesired side reactions take place which are very difficult to control."
[2] "An alternative method for dehydrogenation is the reaction of alkane with oxygen, that is, to carry out the catalytic process under oxidative conditions (ODH). This provides great advantages over a nonoxidative process due to engineering and economic considerations."
[3] "Chromia-pillared zirconium phosphate materials show activity values for the ODH of propane similar to or higher than other catalysts reported in the literature, but working at lower temperatures."
[4] "The oxidation state of chromium after reaction was (III) in all cases. XPS spectra of spent chromia-pillared catalysts show a Cr 3p3/2 peak centered at 577.1 eV, close to the values reported for chromia (576.6-576.9 eV)."
