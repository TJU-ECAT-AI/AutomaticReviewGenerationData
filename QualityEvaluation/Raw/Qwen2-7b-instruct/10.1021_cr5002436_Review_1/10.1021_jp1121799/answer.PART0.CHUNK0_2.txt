1. Noble metal-based catalysts, especially Platinum, are extensively used in the nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins due to their high activity and selectivity.
2. Metal oxide-based catalysts, including CrOx, gallium, indium, vanadium, zinc, and molybdenum oxides, are also applied in this process, offering potentially lower costs and improved thermal stability compared to noble metals.
3. Recent advancements aim to improve the stability and selectivity of both types of catalysts.
4. Platinum-based catalysts are known for their exceptional performance in the nonoxidative dehydrogenation process.
5. Metal oxide catalysts are economically attractive alternatives, providing robustness against high temperatures.
6. These catalysts play a pivotal role in the petrochemical industry for the conversion of light olefins into valuable chemical intermediates.
