<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The noble metal-based catalysts, notably Platinum, and metal oxide-based catalysts, including CrOx, gallium, indium, vanadium, zinc, and molybdenum oxides, are widely used in the nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins. Recent advancements have introduced new catalysts with enhanced selectivity and stability. These catalysts are crucial components in the petrochemical industry, driving the transformation of hydrocarbons into useful olefins.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
