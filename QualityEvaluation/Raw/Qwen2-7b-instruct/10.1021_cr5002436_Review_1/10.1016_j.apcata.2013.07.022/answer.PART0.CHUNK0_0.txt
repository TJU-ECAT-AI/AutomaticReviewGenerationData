<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. The PtSn/-Al2O3 catalyst demonstrated high selectivity and stability in converting n-butane to n-butenes, outperforming noble metal-based catalysts in terms of activity and selectivity.
2. Metal oxide-based catalysts such as CrOx, gallium, indium, vanadium, zinc, and molybdenum oxides were utilized for dehydrogenation reactions, but they often lacked in selectivity and stability compared to noble metal catalysts.
3. Recent research has focused on enhancing the stability and selectivity of both noble metal and metal oxide catalysts through optimizing composition, support materials, and surface chemistry modifications.
4. These catalysts find applications in petrochemical processes and the production of various olefins, contributing to increased efficiency and reduced environmental impact.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
