<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Comparing noble metal-based catalysts, especially Platinum, and metal oxide-based catalysts, such as CrOx, gallium, indium, vanadium, zinc, and molybdenum oxides, in the nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins reveals distinct compositions and applications. Platinum catalysts are highly efficient, while CrOx catalysts are generally less selective and less active. Alternative options like gallium, indium, vanadium, zinc, and molybdenum oxides offer potential variations in properties. Recent advancements focus on enhancing selectivity and reducing energy usage. Noble metal-based catalysts benefit from superior stability and resistance to poisoning. Metal oxide-based catalysts may provide cost-effectiveness and broader accessibility.
