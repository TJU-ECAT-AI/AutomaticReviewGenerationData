Characterization and Catalytic Performance of PtSn Catalysts Supported on Al2O3 and Na-doped Al2O3 in n-butane Dehydrogenation Sergio R. de Miguel  Sonia A. Bocanegra

</quotes>
<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The document discusses the characterization and catalytic performance of PtSn catalysts supported on Al2O3 and Na-doped Al2O3 in n-butane dehydrogenation. The catalysts display important modifications in the metallic phase compared to Pt/Al2O3, including strong interactions between Pt and Sn, possibly resulting in alloy formation. The addition of Na to the bimetallic PtSn/Al2O3 catalysts completely eliminates hydrogenolytic ensembles, which could be due to a geometric modification of the metallic phase. The catalysts exhibit excellent performance in n-butane dehydrogenation, providing high conversions, selectivities to butenes higher than 95%, and lower deactivation capacity than bimetallic PtSn catalysts supported on undoped alumina.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
