<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Noble metal-based catalysts, particularly Platinum, are widely used in the nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins. Metal oxide-based catalysts, such as CrOx, gallium, indium, vanadium, zinc, and molybdenum oxides, are also employed. These catalysts have different compositions, with noble metals typically offering higher activity and selectivity. Industrial applications involve the production of ethene, propene, and other light alkenes. Recent advancements aim to enhance stability, reduce costs, and improve efficiency while minimizing environmental impact.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
