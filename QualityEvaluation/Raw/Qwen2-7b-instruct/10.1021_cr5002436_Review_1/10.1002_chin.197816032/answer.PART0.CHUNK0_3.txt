<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The sintering of supported metal catalysts, particularly focusing on platinum, iridium, and rhodium catalysts, was studied under reducing (H2) and oxidizing (O2) atmospheres with temperatures ranging from 250 to 800°C. The dispersion changes of these catalysts were observed. In oxygen, the thermal stability of the catalysts decreases in the sequence Rh > Pt > Ir, whereas in hydrogen, the sequence is Ir > Rh > Pt. An increase in dispersion was noted for platinum and iridium catalysts in oxygen. The relative stabilities were compared with qualitative predictions based on a sintering mechanism.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
