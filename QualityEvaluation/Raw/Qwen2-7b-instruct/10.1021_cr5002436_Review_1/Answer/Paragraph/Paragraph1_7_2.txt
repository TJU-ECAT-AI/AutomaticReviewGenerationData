        The noble metal-based catalysts, particularly platinum, are highly effective for non-oxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins, showing high selectivity and activity. Metal oxide-based catalysts, such as chromium oxide (CrOx), gallium, indium, vanadium, zinc, and molybdenum oxides, have also been utilized, although they generally exhibit lower stability and activity, especially under harsh conditions. Recent advancements in synthesis techniques have improved the performance of metal oxide catalysts. Industrial applications of these catalysts are widespread in petrochemical industries for producing ethylene and propylene. Noble metal-based catalysts offer higher efficiency and longer operational life, while metal oxide catalysts are cost-effective and use less toxic materials.
        
        Comparative analysis reveals that noble metal-based catalysts, like platinum, exhibit high selectivity and activity for the dehydrogenation process, making them suitable for various industrial applications. Metal oxide catalysts, on the other hand, are known for their robustness and resistance to poisoning under harsh reaction conditions. Recent developments in catalyst design aim to enhance stability, activity, and selectivity, leading to more efficient catalysts. In comparison, noble metal-based catalysts tend to offer higher efficiency and better selectivity, while metal oxide-based catalysts demonstrate greater stability and reduced susceptibility to deactivation.
        
        The demand for propylene worldwide is growing faster than ethylene, causing a shortfall of propylene supply from steam cracking. This highlights the importance of developing more efficient and selective catalysts for the dehydrogenation of light olefins.
        
        The role of tin in modifying the electronic and geometric properties of platinum is recognized, and its addition to platinum catalyst systems positively impacts stability and selectivity towards olefins. Zinc oxide modifies the dispersion and interaction between the metal and support in platinum-silver catalysts, leading to improved stability and selectivity. In zinc-doped platinum-silver catalysts, high selectivity and minimal activity loss are achieved in propane dehydrogenation due to the interaction between platinum and zinc.
        
        The study reported in this paper shows that the presence of zinc, whether taking part of the siliceous framework or deposited on the surface of well-defined MCM-41 spheres, improves the selectivity of platinum catalysts in the chemoselective hydrogenation of crotonaldehyde to crotyl alcohol. When zinc is integrated into the silica matrix of MCM-41 spheres, it generates structural centers that are more stable under reduction treatments and exhibit a higher polarizing/activation ability towards molecules containing a carbonyl double bond. This results in improved catalytic activity and selectivity for crotyl alcohol production compared to conventional PtZn/MCM-41 catalysts.
        
        Various characterization techniques have been applied to understand the active phases in platinum-silver catalysts, as their catalytic performance critically depends on the bimetallic platinum-silver phases. The formation and transition of platinum-silver alloy phases were directly observed during the reaction over both catalysts, and the different influences of the aluminum oxide and zinc aluminum oxide supports on the behavior of platinum-silver alloy phases were noted. Different platinum-silver alloy phases result in varying coke mobility across catalysts, ultimately affecting catalyst stability.
        
        In summary, noble metal-based catalysts, especially platinum, are highly effective for non-oxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins, offering high selectivity and activity. Metal oxide-based catalysts, though generally less active and stable, have seen improvements through recent developments. These catalysts find widespread application in petrochemical industries, and the choice between noble metal-based and metal oxide-based catalysts depends on factors such as stability, selectivity, and cost-effectiveness.

        特别是铂金的贵金属基催化剂在非氧化性脱氢化轻烯烃过程中表现出高效性和选择性。金属氧化物基催化剂，如铬酸盐（CrOx）、镓、铟、钒、锌和钼氧化物，也被广泛使用，但它们通常在恶劣条件下展现出较低的稳定性和活性。通过合成技术的进步，金属氧化物催化剂的性能得到了改善。这些催化剂在石油化工行业中广泛应用，用于生产乙烯和丙烯。贵金属基催化剂提供更高的效率和更长的操作寿命，而金属氧化物催化剂则因其成本效益和较低毒性材料的使用而具有优势。
        
        比较分析表明，特别是铂金的贵金属基催化剂在脱氢化过程中展现出高选择性和活性，适合各种工业应用。另一方面，金属氧化物催化剂因其对毒性的抵抗能力和在恶劣反应条件下的稳定性而受到重视。近年来，催化剂设计的目标在于提高稳定性和活性，从而创造出更高效的催化剂。相比之下，贵金属基催化剂倾向于提供更高的效率和更好的选择性，而金属氧化物基催化剂则显示出更大的稳定性和减少的失活倾向。
        
        全球对丙烯的需求正在以比乙烯更快的速度增长，导致丙烯供应从蒸汽裂解短缺。这强调了开发更高效和选择性催化剂以脱氢化轻烯烃的重要性。
        
        锡在修改铂金的电子和几何性质方面起着重要作用，并且其添加到铂金催化剂系统中可积极影响向烯烃的稳定性和选择性。锌氧化物通过修改金属和支撑之间的分散和相互作用，在铂金-银催化剂中提高了稳定性和选择性。在锌掺杂的铂金-银催化剂中，通过铂金和锌之间的相互作用，在丙烷脱氢化中实现了高选择性和最小的活性损失。
        
        研究报告指出，在二氧化硅骨架中参与或沉积在定义良好的MCM-41球表面的锌的存在可以改善铂催化剂在选择性氢化丙酮醛为丙烯醇中的选择性。当锌被整合到二氧化硅骨架的MCM-41中时，它产生了在还原处理下更加稳定的结构中心，并且能够激活包含羰基双键的分子，从而提高催化活性和选择性，与传统的PtZn/MCM-41催化剂相比，这种结果用于丙烯醇生产。
        
        应用各种表征技术来理解铂金-银催化剂中的活性相，因为它们的催化性能依赖于双金属铂金-银相。直接观察到了反应过程中的铂金-银合金相的形成和过渡，以及铝氧化物和锌铝氧化物支持对铂金-银合金相行为的不同影响。不同铂金-银合金相导致催化剂上的焦炭移动性存在差异，最终影响催化剂稳定性。
        
        总结起来，贵金属基催化剂，尤其是铂金，对于非氧化性脱氢化轻烯烃表现出高效性和选择性。金属氧化物基催化剂虽然在活性和稳定性方面一般较低，但通过近期的发展已经有所改善。这些催化剂在石油化工行业中得到广泛应用，贵金属基和金属氧化物基催化剂的选择取决于稳定性、选择性和成本效益等因素。
