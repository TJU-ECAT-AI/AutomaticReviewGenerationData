        Comparative analysis of noble metal-based and metal oxide-based catalysts for nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins highlights their distinct compositions, effectiveness, industrial applications, recent developments, and potential advantages. Noble metal-based catalysts, particularly platinum, are known for their high activity and selectivity, making them ideal for various industrial processes. Metal oxide-based catalysts, such as chromium oxide (CrOx), gallium, indium, vanadium, zinc, and molybdenum oxides, have also been extensively explored and utilized. However, these metal oxides often exhibit lower stability and activity, especially under harsh reaction conditions.

        Recent advancements in synthesis techniques have led to improvements in the performance of metal oxide catalysts, making them viable alternatives. Industrial applications of these catalysts are widespread, particularly in petrochemical industries for the production of ethylene, propylene, and butene from ethane, propane, and butane. Noble metal-based catalysts offer higher efficiency and longer operational life, providing a significant advantage in terms of productivity and sustainability. Meanwhile, metal oxide catalysts are cost-effective and utilize less toxic materials, which is beneficial for environmental considerations.

        Potential developments in the field focus on enhancing the stability, activity, and selectivity of these catalysts through modifications to noble metals or the introduction of co-catalysts. The promotion effect of metals like tin (Sn) in platinum-based catalysts largely depends on the preparation method. High-temperature treatments in air after reduction can negatively impact the catalytic properties of platinum-tin (PtSn) samples. CO and hydrogen adsorption on platinum-tin alloys is minimal, indicating limited reactivity under certain conditions.

        Zinc (Zn)-doped metal catalysts have demonstrated enhanced selectivity in chemoselective hydrogenation reactions, such as converting crotonaldehyde to crotyl alcohol, and in dehydrogenating iso-butane to iso-butene with high selectivity. The incorporation of Zn into the catalyst matrix improves the stability and polarizing/activation ability towards molecules containing carbonyl double bonds, leading to improved catalytic performance.

        In the context of propane dehydrogenation, PtSn catalysts supported by aluminum oxide (Al2O3) and magnesium-aluminum oxide (Mg-Al O) have been investigated. The addition of magnesium (Mg) to the supports has been found to inhibit side reactions, enhance mono-olefin selectivity and yield, and suppress dehydrogenation activity. The magnesium-to-aluminum molar ratio of 1/3 yields the optimal n-dodecene output. The deposition of coke on catalysts can increase the selectivity of the catalysts, and real-time TOF-MS monitoring technology offers a validated and efficient method for detecting products during high temperature reactions in situ.

        The role of tin in modifying the electronic and geometric properties of platinum is recognized, with its addition to platinum catalyst systems positively impacting stability and selectivity towards olefins. Zinc oxide (ZnO) modifies the dispersion and interaction between the metal and support in platinum-silver catalysts, leading to improved stability and selectivity. In zinc-doped platinum-silver catalysts, high selectivity and minimal activity loss are achieved in propane dehydrogenation due to the interaction between platinum and zinc.

        Various characterization techniques have been applied to understand the active phases in platinum-silver catalysts, as their catalytic performance critically depends on the bimetallic phases. The formation and transition of platinum-silver alloy phases were directly observed during reactions over both catalysts, with distinct influences from aluminum oxide and zinc aluminum oxide supports on these phases. Different platinum-silver alloy phases result in varying coke mobility across catalysts, ultimately affecting catalyst stability.

            比较分析了用于非氧化性脱氢化轻烯烃的贵金属基催化剂（特别是铂）和金属氧化物基催化剂（如CrOx、镓、铟、钒、锌和钼氧化物），强调了它们在组成、有效性、工业应用、近期发展和潜在优势方面的差异。贵金属，尤其是铂，以其高活性和选择性而闻名，适用于各种工业过程。金属氧化物催化剂，如铬酸盐（CrOx）、镓、铟、钒、锌和钼氧化物，也已广泛探索和使用。然而，在恶劣条件下，这些金属氧化物通常表现出较低的稳定性和活性。

            最近在合成技术上的进步已经提高了金属氧化物催化剂的性能，使其成为可行的选择。这些催化剂在石油化学工业中的应用广泛，特别是在从乙烷、丙烷和丁烷生产乙烯、丙烯和丁烯的过程中。贵金属基催化剂提供了更高的效率和更长的操作寿命，为提高生产力和可持续性提供了显著优势。与此同时，金属氧化物催化剂成本效益高，使用较少的有毒材料，这对环境考虑非常有利。

            该领域的潜在发展集中在通过修改贵金属或引入共催化剂来增强催化剂的稳定性和活性。锡（Sn）在铂基催化剂中的促进效果很大程度上取决于制备方法。高温空气处理后还原的铂锡（PtSn）样品的催化性质可能受到负面影响。铂锡合金（PtSn）合金上的二氧化碳（CO）和氢气（H2）吸附量有限，表明在某些条件下反应活性较低。

            含有锌（Zn）的金属催化剂在化学选择性氢化反应中显示出增强的选择性，例如将顺丁醛转化为顺丁醇，以及以高达100%的高选择性进行异丁烷脱氢。将锌整合到催化剂矩阵中可以提高稳定性并激活含有羰基双键分子的能力，从而提高催化性能。

            在丙烷脱氢的背景下，铂锡催化剂支持铝氧化物（Al2O3）和镁铝氧化物（Mg-Al O）进行了研究。添加镁（Mg）到支撑物中被发现可以抑制副反应，增强单烯选择性和产量，并抑制脱氢作用。镁与铝的摩尔比为1/3时可以获得最佳的n-十二碳烯输出。催化剂上沉积的焦炭可以增加催化剂的选择性，并且实时TOF-MS监测技术提供了一种验证和高效的方法，用于在原位检测高温反应过程中催化剂的产物。

            锡在修改铂的电子和几何属性方面的作用得到了认可，其添加到铂催化剂系统中对向烯烃的稳定性和选择性产生积极影响。锌氧化物（ZnO）通过修改金属和支撑之间的分散和相互作用，提高了铂银催化剂的稳定性和选择性。在锌掺杂的铂银催化剂中，通过铂与锌的相互作用实现了丙烷脱氢的高选择性和最小的活性损失。

            应用各种表征技术来理解铂银催化剂中的活性相，因为其催化性能依赖于双金属相。直接观察到了反应期间铂银合金相的形成和过渡，这两种催化剂的不同影响铝氧化物和锌铝氧化物支撑物在这些相的行为。不同的铂银合金相导致催化剂上焦炭的移动性不同，最终影响催化剂稳定性。
