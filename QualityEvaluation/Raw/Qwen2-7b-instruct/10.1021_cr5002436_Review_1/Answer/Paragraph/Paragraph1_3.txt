        Comparative analysis of noble metal-based and metal oxide-based catalysts for nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins: composition, effectiveness, and industrial applications

        Detailed analysis established from the study of reference [10.1021/jp1121799], synthesized comprehension stemming from references [10.1021/ie1024694] and [10.1016/j.apcata.2013.09.042]. Metal oxide-based catalysts, particularly chromium oxide (CrOx), gallium, indium, vanadium, zinc, and molybdenum oxides, have been explored alongside noble metal-based catalysts, notably platinum. Platinum-based catalysts are characterized by high activity and selectivity in dehydrogenation reactions, making them suitable for various industrial applications. Metal oxide catalysts, on the other hand, are known for their robustness and resistance to poisoning under harsh reaction conditions.

        Recent advancements in synthesis techniques have led to improved performance of metal oxide catalysts, particularly through the introduction of zinc aluminate supports due to their neutral characteristics, high thermal stability, and mechanical strength. The role of tin in modifying the electronic and geometric properties of platinum is recognized, positively impacting stability and selectivity towards olefins when added to platinum catalyst systems. Zinc oxide modifies the dispersion and interaction between the metal and support in platinum-silver catalysts, leading to improved stability and selectivity.

        In zinc-doped platinum-silver catalysts, high selectivity and minimal activity loss are achieved in propane dehydrogenation, due to the interaction between platinum and zinc. Various characterization techniques have been applied to understand the active phases in platinum-silver catalysts, as their catalytic performance critically depends on the bimetallic platinum-silver phases. The behavior of platinum-silver alloy phases is observed during reactions over both catalysts, with distinct influences from aluminum oxide and zinc aluminum oxide supports on these phases. Different platinum-silver alloy phases result in varying coke mobility across catalysts, ultimately affecting catalyst stability.

        Industrial applications of these catalysts are widespread in petrochemical industries, particularly for the production of ethylene and propylene. Potential advantages of noble metal-based catalysts include higher efficiency and longer operational life, while metal oxide catalysts offer cost-effectiveness and the possibility of using less toxic materials compared to noble metals.

        The comparative analysis highlights the distinct characteristics and applications of noble metal-based and metal oxide-based catalysts. While noble metals offer high activity and selectivity, metal oxides provide stability and lower costs. The field continues to focus on improving catalyst performance, often through the introduction of co-catalysts or modifications to noble metals.

        比较研究了贵金属基催化剂（特别是铂）和金属氧化物基催化剂（如铬酸盐、镓、铟、钒、锌和钼氧化物）在轻烯烃非氧化脱氢过程中的应用。详细分析来自参考文献[10.1021/jp1121799]，综合理解源自[10.1021/ie1024694]和[10.1016/j.apcata.2013.09.042]。金属氧化物催化剂，尤其是铬酸盐（CrOx）、镓、铟、钒、锌和钼氧化物，与贵金属催化剂（如铂）一起被探索。铂基催化剂以其在脱氢反应中的高活性和选择性而受到工业应用的青睐。金属氧化物催化剂因其对恶劣反应条件下的耐毒性和稳定性而广受赞誉。

        近年来，通过引入锌铝酸盐作为支撑材料，提高了金属氧化物催化剂的性能，这归功于其中性特性、高热稳定性和机械强度。锡在修改铂的电子和几何性质方面发挥着作用，当添加到铂催化剂系统中时，它能正面影响对烯烃的稳定性和选择性。锌氧化物改变了金属和支撑之间的分散和相互作用，在铂银催化剂中，这导致了改进的稳定性和选择性。

        在锌掺杂的铂银催化剂中，通过铂和锌之间的相互作用，在丙烷脱氢过程中实现了高选择性和最小的活性损失。各种表征技术已被应用于理解铂银催化剂中的活性相，因为它们的催化性能依赖于铂银双金属相的形成和稳定性。铂银合金相的行为在两种催化剂上被观察到，铝氧化物和锌铝氧化物支撑材料的不同影响这些相的行为。不同铂银合金相导致催化剂中的焦炭移动性的差异，最终影响催化剂的稳定性。

        这些催化剂在石油化工行业有广泛的应用，特别是在乙烯和丙烯的生产中。贵金属基催化剂的优势在于更高的效率和更长的操作寿命，而金属氧化物催化剂则提供成本效益和使用较少有毒物质的可能性，与贵金属相比。

        这种比较分析突出了贵金属基和金属氧化物基催化剂的显著特性和应用。虽然贵金属提供了高活性和选择性，但金属氧化物提供了稳定性和较低的成本。该领域继续专注于通过引入共催化剂或贵金属的修改来提高催化剂性能。这可能包括开发新的支撑材料、探索多种金属成分的协同效应，并增强对活性相形成和稳定性的理解。先进的表征技术的集成，如实时在线监测，将有助于优化催化剂性能。此外，探索具有特定烯烃产品高选择性的催化剂或那些能够在温和条件下运行的催化剂，可能使石油化学工业更加可持续和经济高效。
