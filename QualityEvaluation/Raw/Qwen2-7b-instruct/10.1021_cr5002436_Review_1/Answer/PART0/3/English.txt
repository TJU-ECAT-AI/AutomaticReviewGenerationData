Noble metal-based catalysts, especially platinum, have been extensively researched. Metal oxide-based catalysts like CrOx, gallium, indium, vanadium, zinc, and molybdenum oxides have also been explored. Noble metal catalysts are preferred due to their high efficiency and selectivity in nonoxidative dehydrogenation processes. Metal oxide catalysts provide alternative pathways for dehydrogenation, potentially offering cost-effective solutions. Recent advancements have targeted improving catalyst stability, reducing sintering, and enhancing deactivation rates. Industrial uses of noble metal catalysts are widespread in petrochemical industries for dehydrogenation reactions. Metal oxide catalysts are seen as cost-effective options for achieving high yields with lower operational costs. Noble metal catalysts excel in catalytic activity and selectivity, but they are often constrained by high production costs and deactivation rates. Metal oxide catalysts balance cost-effectiveness and performance, making them appealing for industrial applications.
