[1] "Comparing noble metal-based catalysts, particularly Platinum, and metal oxide-based catalysts, such as CrOx, gallium, indium, vanadium, zinc, and molybdenum oxides, in the nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins involves examining their compositions, effectiveness, industrial applications, recent developments, and potential advantages."
          [2] "Noble metal-based catalysts, like Platinum, are known for their high reactivity and selectivity in dehydrogenation processes. They are often used due to their ability to efficiently convert light olefins into lighter hydrocarbons."
          [3] "Metal oxide-based catalysts offer a more affordable alternative. They are often used to modulate the acidity and porosity of the catalyst, which can affect the dehydrogenation rates and product selectivity."
          [4] "By adding metals like Cr, Ga, In, V, Zn, or Mo to these oxides, the catalysts can be optimized for specific reactions."
          [5] "In the context of the dehydrogenation of n-dodecane, the addition of magnesium oxide to the supports showed several benefits. It helped in moderating the total acidity of the catalysts, reducing the formation of aromatics and enhancing the mono-olefin selectivity. This resulted in improved yields and catalyst stability, with the optimal n-dodecene yield achieved when the Mg/Al molar ratio was 1/3. The magnesium addition also suppressed deep dehydrogenation, leading to lower coke formation and improved selectivity."
          [6] "The use of on-line TOF-MS monitoring technology proved to be an effective method for analyzing products over catalysts in situ during high-temperature reactions. It offered rapid and continuous detection of changes in reaction products, which can significantly enhance the understanding and optimization of the dehydrogenation process."
