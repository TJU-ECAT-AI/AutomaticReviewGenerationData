<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Noble metal-based catalysts, especially platinum, are highly effective for the dehydrogenation of light olefins due to their high activity and selectivity. Metal oxide-based catalysts, including CrOx, gallium, indium, vanadium, zinc, and molybdenum oxides, are also utilized, offering distinct catalytic properties influenced by their composition and structure. Recent advancements aim to improve the stability, activity, and selectivity of these catalysts. They play a critical role in industrial petrochemical processes.
