To compare and contrast noble metal-based catalysts (primarily Platinum) and metal oxide-based catalysts (CrOx, gallium, indium, vanadium, zinc, and molybdenum oxides) in the nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins, we delve into the study's findings. The research employs first-principles calculations to elucidate the mechanisms involved. It highlights that both types of catalysts are extensively used in this process, each having distinct compositions and applications. The effectiveness of these catalysts is evaluated through their unique properties, recent advancements, and potential benefits. A critical comparison is drawn between noble metals and metal oxides, showcasing their roles and performances in the catalytic conversion of light olefins.
