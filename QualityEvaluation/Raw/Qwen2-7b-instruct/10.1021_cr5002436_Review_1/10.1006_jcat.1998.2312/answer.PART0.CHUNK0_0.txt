Comparing noble metal-based catalysts, particularly Platinum, and metal oxide-based catalysts in the nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins reveals distinct characteristics. Noble metals like Platinum are renowned for their high activity and stability, making them popular choices in industrial applications. Metal oxides, especially those containing transition metals, often display superior selectivity towards specific products. Recent advancements aim to enhance the performance of these catalysts by improving their activity, stability, reducing energy consumption, and boosting selectivity. The benefits of noble metal catalysts lie in their high activity and stability, whereas metal oxides excel in providing better selectivity. These catalysts find extensive use across industries, particularly in petrochemicals and fine chemicals manufacturing.
