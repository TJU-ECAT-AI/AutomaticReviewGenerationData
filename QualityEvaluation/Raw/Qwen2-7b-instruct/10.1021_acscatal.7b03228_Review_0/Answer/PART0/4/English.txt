Geometric and electronic effects in transition-metal alloys synergistically influence catalytic performance by optimizing electronic properties such as charge density distribution and energy level alignment, which are crucial for efficient formic acid decomposition. Innovative strategies that exploit this interplay involve designing alloy structures with tailored electronic states and geometries to achieve enhanced catalytic activity, surpassing the performance limitations of traditional monometals through improved selectivity and tunability.
