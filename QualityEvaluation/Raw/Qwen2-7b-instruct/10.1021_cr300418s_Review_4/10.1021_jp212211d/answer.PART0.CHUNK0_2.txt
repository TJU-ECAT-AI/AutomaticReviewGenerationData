<EnglishVersionAnswer>
To thoroughly characterize the incorporation and homogenous distribution of dopants in metal oxide catalysts, various methods such as X-ray diffraction (XRD), transmission electron microscopy (TEM), energy-dispersive X-ray spectroscopy (EDS), and Fourier-transform infrared spectroscopy (FTIR) are essential. These techniques collectively address the challenges by confirming the dopant's presence, elucidating structural modifications, and correlating these changes with enhanced catalytic activity. They also help in eliminating alternative explanations by providing concrete evidence of the dopant's impact on the catalyst's performance.
