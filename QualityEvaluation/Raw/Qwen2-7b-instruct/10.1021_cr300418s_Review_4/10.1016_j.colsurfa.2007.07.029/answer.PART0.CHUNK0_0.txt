<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. The solgel method is widely used for creating metallic catalysts due to its capability of uniformly distributing active metals across the support, thanks to the constituent elements being mixed at the atomic level.

2. Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy (FT-IR) was utilized for analyzing the spectra of the samples, using a Perkin-Elmer Paragon 1000 PC spectrometer.

3. Thermal analyses were conducted on the xerogels using a TA Instruments SDT-2960 thermal analyzer at a heating rate of 10°C/min in an air environment.

4. X-ray powder diffraction was performed using a Siemens D-5000 diffractometer equipped with Ni-filtered Cu K radiation within a 2θ range of 1090° to analyze the crystalline phases.

5. Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) images were obtained from a Carl Zeiss DSM950 microscope at a voltage of 30 kV, current of 50 mA, and working distance of 9 mm.

6. Nitrogen (N2) sorption isotherms at 77 K were measured through an Autosorb-1 volumetric instrument from Quantachrome Co.

7. The spatial time for the catalytic tests was set at 500 h−1.

8. The thermal analysis of the ZrO2 doped substrates showed a significant weight loss (20%) between 80 and 200°C.

9. Fresh and calcined ZrO2 sample FT-IR spectra were presented, showing distinctive features for each state.

10. The shapes of the nitrogen sorption hysteresis loops allowed for the categorization of the pore morphology of various Zr samples into two types.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
