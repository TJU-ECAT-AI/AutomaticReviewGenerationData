This material shows promise as a strong acid catalyst with size-selective properties due to its unique structural characteristics and chemical properties. The substitution of yttrium species into the gallium oxide framework stabilizes the hexagonal structure, enabling the synthesis of mesoporous gallium oxide. The observed activity trends and selectivity during both initial and steady-state stages of the CO2-ODHP reaction can be attributed to the specific surface properties, particularly acidity and basicity, and Lewis acid sites of the different gallium oxide polymorphs (α-, β-, γ-, and δ-).

English Answer:
The mechanisms proposed to explain the observed activity trends and selectivity during both initial and steady-state stages of the CO2-ODHP reaction involve the unique structural characteristics and chemical properties of the different gallium oxide polymorphs (α-, β-, γ-, and δ-). Specifically, the substitution of yttrium species into the gallium oxide framework stabilizes the hexagonal structure, enabling the synthesis of mesoporous gallium oxide. This stabilization leads to enhanced catalytic activity and selectivity due to the specific surface properties, particularly acidity and basicity, and Lewis acid sites. Additionally, the expansion of the unit cell parameter and decrease in crystallinity for the 9-hour reaction product compared to the 3-hour reaction product is attributed to the more extensive polymerization of the GaYO framework, which further contributes to the observed activity trends and selectivity.
