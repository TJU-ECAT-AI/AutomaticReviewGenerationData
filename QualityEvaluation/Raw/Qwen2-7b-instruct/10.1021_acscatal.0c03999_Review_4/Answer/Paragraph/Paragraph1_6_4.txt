        The structural characteristics, surface properties (particularly acidity and basicity), and Lewis acid sites of different gallium(III) oxide (Ga2O3) polymorphs (α-, β-, γ-, and δ-) significantly influence their catalytic performance in CO2-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation (ODHP) of propane (C3H8) reactions. β- and γ-Ga2O3 polymorphs outperform α- and δ-Ga2O3 in terms of C3H8 conversion and C3H6 selectivity. Surface acidity density decreases in the order of β > γ > δ > α, paralleling the surface basicity. The initial activity is dependent on the surface acid density, with β-Ga2O3 maximizing this activity. Tetrahedral gallium (Ga) cations in β- and γ-Ga2O3 structures create coordinatively unsaturated Ga3+ sites, functioning as Lewis acid sites for dehydrogenation reactions. Octahedral Ga3+ in α- and δ-Ga2O3 are less coordinated with weaker acidity, allowing for the potential reconstruction from octahedral to tetrahedral Ga cations, which generates surface Lewis acid sites. Surface basic sites are insufficient for CO2 adsorption and activation, leading to lower CO2 conversion compared to C3H8. The surface base site density correlates with initial activity, indicating its promotional effect in the beginning of the reaction. The weak reducibility of Ga2O3 and the presence of Ga+ and Ga3+ suggest that the reduction of Ga cations happens at the start of the reaction, benefiting dehydrogenation but only during the initial phase. At steady state, the reaction proceeds via heterolytic dissociation on Ga oxide without a redox cycle. Various methods have been developed to tune the textural properties of Ga2O3, such as the nanocasting technique using mesoporous carbon as a hard template and the urea-based hydrothermal method using polyethylene glycol as a soft template agent, enhancing its catalytic performance.

        The ideal support for Ga2O3-based catalysts is a zeolite with medium pore size and weak acidity. HZSM-48-supported catalysts exhibit higher selectivities to propene than HZSM-5-supported ones at similar propane conversion due to their weak acid strength. However, the stability of HZSM-48-supported catalysts is not as good as that of HZSM-5-supported catalysts, owing to their more weak acid sites and unidimensional pore structures. The activity decreases with the increase of the Si/Al ratio of the catalyst support, while the selectivity to propene shows a contrary trend. Ga2O3/HZSM-48 with a Si/Al ratio of 130 has the best propene yield of 22%.

        Different Ga2O3 polymorphs (α-, β-, γ-, and δ-) have distinct structural characteristics, surface properties, and Lewis acid sites that impact their catalytic performance in CO2-ODHP reactions. α-Ga2O3 demonstrates the highest activity due to its efficient promotion of the oxidative dehydrogenation process through specific Lewis acid sites. β-Ga2O3 exhibits better selectivity for the desired product because of its unique surface properties that favor the formation of the product over byproducts. γ-Ga2O3 is noted for its stability under reaction conditions, making it suitable for prolonged use in industrial applications. Lastly, δ-Ga2O3 shows moderate catalytic performance, influenced by its balance between acidity and basicity.

        The dehydrogenation reaction of propane in the presence of CO2 does not suppress coke deposition. Results from pulse reactions and dehydrogenation reactions with varying partial pressures of CO2 indicate that CO2 plays dual roles in the dehydrogenation reaction: a positive role by removing absorbed hydrogen on the catalyst surface through the reverse water-gas shift reaction, and a negative role by displacing propane adsorbed on the catalyst's basic sites.

        The mechanisms explaining the observed activity trends and selectivity involve specific interactions between the catalyst surface and the reactants during both initial and steady-state stages of the reaction. These interactions are crucial in determining the reaction kinetics and product selectivity, highlighting the importance of structural characteristics, surface properties, and Lewis acid sites in optimizing Ga2O3 polymorphs for CO2-ODHP catalysis.

        不同的镓(III)氧化物（Ga2O3）晶型（α-、β-、γ- 和 δ-）的结构特征、表面性质（特别是酸性和碱性），以及Lewis酸中心对二氧化碳辅助氧化脱氢（ODHP）丙烷（C3H8）反应的催化性能产生显著影响。β- 和 γ-Ga2O3 晶型在丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性方面优于 α- 和 δ-Ga2O3。表面酸密度按照 β > γ > δ > α 的顺序减少，与表面碱性一致。初始活性依赖于表面酸密度，β-Ga2O3 最大化了这一活性。β- 和 γ-Ga2O3 结构中的四面体镓（Ga）离子创建了未饱和的 Ga3+ 离子位点，作为脱氢反应中的Lewis酸中心。在 α- 和 δ-Ga2O3 中，八面体 Ga3+ 与较弱的酸性结合较少，允许从八面体到四面体 Ga 离子的潜在重建，从而在表面上生成 Lewis 酸中心。表面碱性位点不足以促进 CO2 吸附和活化，导致 CO2 转化率低于 C3H8。表面碱基位点密度与初始活性相关，表明其在反应初期具有促进效果。Ga2O3 的弱还原性及其存在 Ga+ 和 Ga3+ 表明，Ga 离子的还原发生在反应开始阶段，有利于脱氢，但仅在初始阶段有效。在稳态下，反应通过 Ga 氧化物上的异裂解进行，而无需氧化还原循环。已经开发了多种方法来调整 Ga2O3 的纹理性质，例如使用介孔碳作为硬模板的纳米成型技术和使用聚乙二醇作为软模板剂的尿素基水热方法，以增强其催化性能。

        理想的 Ga2O3 催化剂支持应为具有中等孔径大小和弱酸性的沸石。HZSM-48 支持的催化剂表现出高于 HZSM-5 支持的催化剂的丙烯选择性，在相似的丙烷转化率下，由于其较弱的酸强度。然而，HZSM-48 支持的催化剂稳定性不如 HZSM-5 支持的催化剂稳定，这是由于它们更多的弱酸位点和一维孔结构造成的。随着催化剂支持中 Si/Al 比的增加，活性降低，而丙烯选择性显示相反的趋势。Ga2O3/HZSM-48 以 130 的 Si/Al 比具有最佳的丙烯产率为 22%。

        α-、β-、γ- 和 δ- Ga2O3 拥有不同的结构特征、表面性质和 Lewis 酸中心，对 CO2-ODHP 反应的催化性能产生影响。α-Ga2O3 由于特定的 Lewis 酸中心促进了氧化脱氢过程，表现出最高的活性。β-Ga2O3 由于其独特的表面性质，在形成产品而不是副产品的过程中显示出更好的选择性。γ-Ga2O3 在反应条件下表现出稳定性，适合工业应用的长期使用。最后，δ-Ga2O3 显示出适度的催化性能，受其酸性和碱性之间的平衡影响。

        碳酸盐的存在下丙烷的脱氢反应不会抑制焦炭沉积。脉冲反应和不同 CO2 分压下的脱氢反应结果表明，CO2 在脱氢反应中扮演双重角色：通过逆水煤气变换反应去除催化剂表面吸附的氢，以及通过置换吸附在催化剂基本位点上的丙烷，发挥负作用。

        解释观察到的活性趋势和选择性的机制涉及催化剂表面与反应物之间在初始和稳态阶段的特定相互作用，这些相互作用对于确定反应动力学和产物选择性至关重要，突显了结构特征、表面性质和 Lewis 酸中心在优化 Ga2O3 晶型以实现 CO2-ODHP 催化作用的重要性。
