            Detailed analysis established from the study of reference [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4], [10.1016/j.catcom.2013.01.019], [10.1021/cm902015f], and others. The synthesis of knowledge from these sources highlights the pivotal role of structural characteristics, surface properties (with particular emphasis on acidity and basicity), and Lewis acid sites of Ga2O3 polymorphs (α-, β-, γ-, and δ-) in determining their catalytic performance in CO2-ODHP reactions. 

            For instance, β- and γ-Ga2O3 polymorphs outperform α- and δ-Ga2O3 in terms of C3H8 conversion and C3H6 selectivity (Table 5). Surface acidity density decreases in the order of β > γ > δ > α, paralleling the surface basicity. The initial activity is directly tied to the surface acid density, with β-Ga2O3 maximizing this activity. Tetrahedral Ga cations in β- and γ-Ga2O3 structures create coordinatively unsaturated Ga3+ sites, functioning as Lewis acid sites for dehydrogenation reactions. Octahedral Ga3+ in α- and δ-Ga2O3 are less coordinated with weaker acidity, enabling the potential reconstruction from octahedral to tetrahedral Ga cations, which generates surface Lewis acid sites. Surface basic sites are insufficient for CO2 adsorption and activation, leading to lower CO2 conversion compared to C3H8. The surface base site density correlates with initial activity, indicating its promotional effect in the beginning of the reaction. The weak reducibility of Ga2O3 and the presence of Ga+ and Ga3+ suggest that the reduction of Ga cations happens at the start of the reaction, benefiting dehydrogenation (DHP) but only during the initial phase. At steady state, the reaction proceeds via heterolytic dissociation on Ga oxide without a redox cycle. Various methods, such as nanocasting and urea-based hydrothermal processes, have been employed to tailor the textural properties of Ga2O3, enhancing its catalytic performance.

            The activity trends and selectivity during both initial and steady-state stages of the reaction are deeply influenced by these factors. Different Ga2O3 polymorphs exhibit distinct structural characteristics, surface properties, and Lewis acid sites that impact their catalytic performance in CO2-ODHP reactions. 

            In the context of CO2-ODHP reactions, these factors influence the reaction kinetics and product selectivity. High activity is observed in catalysts with abundant medium-strong acid sites on their surfaces, such as Ga2O3/TiO2, Ga2O3/Al2O3, and Ga2O3/ZrO2, compared to ineffective catalysts like Ga2O3/SiO2 and Ga2O3/MgO. Carbon dioxide (CO2) can enhance the dehydrogenation activity of Ga2O3/TiO2 but diminish those of Ga2O3/ZrO2 and Ga2O3/Al2O3. The dehydrogenation reaction likely proceeds through a heterolytic dissociation pathway rather than a redox mechanism.

            The ideal support should be a zeolite with medium pore size and weak acidity. HZSM-48 supported catalysts exhibit higher selectivities to propene than the HZSM-5 supported ones at similar propane conversion, due to their weak acid strength. Their stability is not as good as those of the latter, owing to their more weak acid sites and unidimensional pore structures. The activity decreases with the increase of Si/Al ratio of catalyst support while the selectivity to propene shows a contrary trend. Ga2O3/HZSM-48 with a Si/Al ratio of 130 has the best propene yield of 22%.

            The structural characteristics, surface properties (particularly acidity and basicity), and Lewis acid sites of different Ga2O3 polymorphs significantly impact their catalytic performance in CO2-ODHP reactions. α-, β-, γ-, and δ-Ga2O3 polymorphs display distinct reactivity patterns due to variations in their crystal structures, influencing the distribution and accessibility of Lewis acid sites. Higher acidity and basicity tend to enhance the initial activity of Ga2O3 polymorphs, facilitating faster reaction rates. However, this increased activity can lead to higher coke formation, which subsequently deactivates the catalysts. The selectivity towards propene is influenced by the balance between acidity and basicity, with more acidic supports favoring propene production but leading to shorter catalyst lifetimes due to coke formation. The stability of these catalysts is also affected by their pore geometries; catalysts with unidimensional pore structures tend to have lower stability due to more accessible surfaces for coke deposition.

            The dehydrogenation reaction of propane in the presence of CO2 does not suppress coke deposition, providing a clear pathway for continuous catalytic performance despite coke formation. The results from pulse reaction and dehydrogenation reaction with varying partial pressures of CO2 indicate that CO2 plays dual roles in the dehydrogenation reaction: a positive role by removing absorbed H2 on the catalyst surface through the reverse watergas shift reaction and a negative role by displacing propane adsorbed on the catalysts basic sites.

            In conclusion, the detailed analysis reveals that the structural characteristics, surface properties, particularly acidity and basicity, and Lewis acid sites of Ga2O3 polymorphs significantly impact their catalytic performance in CO2-ODHP reactions. These factors determine the reaction kinetics and product selectivity, influencing the initial activity, steady-state behavior, and overall efficiency of the catalytic process. The insights provided here lay the groundwork for further exploration into the optimization of Ga2O3 polymorphs for CO2-ODHP reactions, potentially leading to the development of more efficient and selective catalysts for industrial applications.

            比如，β- 和 γ-Ga2O3 在丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性方面显著优于 α- 和 δ-Ga2O3。表面酸密度按照β > γ > δ > α 的顺序递减，与表面碱性密度相吻合。初始活性直接依赖于表面酸密度，β-Ga2O3 最大化了这种活性。β- 和 γ-Ga2O3 结构中的四面体镓离子创造了未饱和的Ga3+位点，作为脱氢反应的Lewis酸位点。α- 和 δ-Ga2O3 中的八面体Ga3+由于较弱的酸性而与更少的酸性连接，允许从八面体到四面体的Ga离子重建，从而在表面上产生Lewis酸位点。表面碱性位点不足以吸附二氧化碳并激活，导致二氧化碳转化率低于丙烷。表面碱基位点密度与初始活性相关，表明其在反应初期具有促进作用。镓的弱可还原性和存在Ga+和Ga3+（XPS）表明，镓离子的还原发生在反应开始阶段，有利于脱氢（DHP），但仅在初始阶段有效。稳定状态时，反应通过镓氧化物上的异裂解进行，无需循环氧化还原过程。

            高活性在具有丰富中强酸位点的催化剂表面上观察到，例如Ga2O3/TiO2、Ga2O3/Al2O3 和Ga2O3/ZrO2，与无效催化剂Ga2O3/SiO2和Ga2O3/MgO相比。二氧化碳可以增强Ga2O3/TiO2的脱氢活性，同时减少Ga2O3/ZrO2和Ga2O3/Al2O3的活性。脱氢反应可能通过异裂解途径而不是氧化还原机制进行。

            理想的支撑材料应该是具有中等孔径和弱酸性的沸石。HZSM-48支持的催化剂在相似的丙烷转化率下表现出更高的丙烯选择性，由于它们的弱酸强度。然而，这些催化剂的稳定性不如后者，因为它们具有更多的弱酸位点和一维孔结构。随着催化剂支撑物Si/Al比的增加，活性降低，而丙烯选择性显示相反的趋势。Ga2O3/HZSM-48在Si/Al比为130时具有最佳的丙烯产率为22%。

            不同镓氧化物三氧化二铝（α-、β-、γ-、δ-）的结构特征、表面性质（特别是酸性和碱性），以及Lewis酸位点显著影响其在二氧化碳氧化脱氢反应中的催化性能。这些因素决定了反应动力学和产物选择性，影响了初始活性、稳态行为和整体催化过程的效率。这些见解为未来进一步探索镓氧化物三氧化二铝用于二氧化碳氧化脱氢反应提供了基础，有可能推动开发更高效和选择性更强的催化剂用于工业应用。

            总之，详细的研究揭示了镓氧化物三氧化二铝的结构特征、表面性质，特别是酸性和碱性，以及Lewis酸位点对其二氧化碳氧化脱氢反应催化性能的显著影响。这些因素决定了反应动力学和产物选择性，影响了初始活性、稳态行为和整个催化过程的有效性。这些提供的见解为镓氧化物三氧化二铝在二氧化碳氧化脱氢反应中的优化提供了理论基础，有可能引领开发更高效的催化剂应用于工业应用。
