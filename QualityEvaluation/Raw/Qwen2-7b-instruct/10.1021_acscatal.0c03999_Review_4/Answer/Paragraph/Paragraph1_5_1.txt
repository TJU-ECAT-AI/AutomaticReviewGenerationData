    <English>
        The structural characteristics, surface properties (particularly acidity and basicity), and Lewis acid sites of different gallium(II) oxide (Ga2O3) polymorphs (α-, β-, γ-, and δ-) play a pivotal role in determining their catalytic performance in CO2-oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions. Notably, β- and γ-Ga2O3 polymorphs outshine α- and δ-Ga2O3 in terms of converting propane (C3H8) to propylene (C3H6) with higher selectivity. Surface acidity density decreases in the order of β > γ > δ > α, paralleling the decrease in surface basicity. The initial catalytic activity is highly dependent on the surface acid density, with β-Ga2O3 exhibiting the highest activity. Tetrahedral gallium (Ga) cations in β- and γ-Ga2O3 structures provide coordinatively unsaturated Ga3+ sites, functioning as Lewis acid sites for the dehydrogenation process. In contrast, octahedral Ga3+ in α- and δ-Ga2O3 are less coordinated with weaker acidity, enabling the potential reconstruction from octahedral to tetrahedral Ga cations, which generates additional surface Lewis acid sites. Surfaces with too few basic sites hinder the adsorption and activation of CO2, leading to lower CO2 conversion compared to propane. The surface base site density correlates with initial activity, indicating its promotional effect in the onset of the reaction. The weak reducibility of Ga2O3 and the presence of Ga+ and Ga3+ suggest that the reduction of Ga cations occurs primarily at the beginning of the reaction, benefitting dehydrogenation (DHP) but only during the initial phase. At the steady state, the reaction proceeds via heterolytic dissociation on Ga oxide without undergoing a redox cycle. 

        Various methods have been developed to tailor the textural properties of Ga2O3, including the nanocasting technique using mesoporous carbon as a hard template (ref. 131) and the urea-based hydrothermal method utilizing polyethylene glycol as a soft template agent, to enhance its catalytic performance. 

        Looking ahead, an integrative understanding and prospective outlook on this field reveals potential avenues for future research. The advancements and findings discussed herein highlight the importance of further exploring the interplay between catalyst structure, surface properties, and reaction mechanisms in determining catalytic activity and selectivity. Future endeavors could delve deeper into the development of novel Ga2O3 polymorphs with optimized Lewis acid sites and surface functionalities, aiming to improve both initial activity and stability over multiple reaction cycles. Moreover, the integration of advanced characterization techniques with sophisticated computational models promises to offer new insights into the underlying reaction pathways and catalytic mechanisms, paving the way for the design of more efficient and sustainable catalytic systems. The exploration of synergistic effects between Ga2O3 and other metal oxides or catalyst modifiers could also contribute to enhancing the overall catalytic performance and broadening the scope of industrially relevant reactions facilitated by these materials.

    <Chinese>
        在二氧化碳氧化脱氢反应（CO2-ODHP）中，不同镓（II）氧化物（Ga2O3）多晶型（α-、β-、γ-、和δ-）的结构特性、表面性质（特别是酸性和碱性），以及Lewis酸中心对催化性能的影响至关重要。特别地，β-和γ-Ga2O3多晶型在将丙烷（C3H8）转化为丙烯（C3H6）时表现出更高的选择性和转化率，显著优于α-和δ-Ga2O3。表面酸密度按照β > γ > δ > α的顺序递减，同时表面碱性也呈现相同趋势。初始催化活性高度依赖于表面酸密度，β-Ga2O3表现出最高活性。β-和γ-Ga2O3结构中的四面体镓（Ga）离子提供了可协调的Ga3+位点作为Lewis酸中心参与脱氢过程。相比之下，α-和δ-Ga2O3中的八面体Ga3+与较弱的酸性更协调，可能允许从八面体向四面体Ga离子的重建，从而生成额外的表面Lewis酸中心。表面碱性位点数量不足会阻碍CO2的吸附和活化，导致CO2转化率低于丙烷。表面碱性位点密度与初始活性相关，表明其在反应开始阶段的促进作用。Ga2O3的弱还原性和存在Ga+和Ga3+表明Ga离子的还原主要发生在反应初期，有利于脱氢（DHP），但仅限于反应的初始阶段。稳定状态下，反应通过Ga氧化物上的异裂进行，而不经历氧化还原循环。

        已经开发了多种方法来调整Ga2O3的纹理性质，包括使用介孔碳作为硬模板的纳米铸造技术（参见131号引用）和基于尿素的水热法利用聚乙二醇作为软模板剂的方法，以增强其催化性能。

        展望未来，对这一领域的综合理解和前瞻性的视角揭示了未来研究的潜在途径。本节讨论的进展强调了深入探究催化剂结构、表面性质及其反应机制如何共同决定催化活性和选择性的必要性。未来的努力可以集中于设计具有优化Lewis酸中心和表面功能的新Ga2O3多晶型，以提高初始活性和多次反应周期的稳定性。此外，先进的表征技术和复杂计算模型的结合有望提供有关底层反应路径和催化机制的新见解，为设计更加高效和可持续的催化体系铺平道路。探索Ga2O3与其他金属氧化物或催化剂改性剂的协同效应也可能有助于增强这些材料的整体催化性能，并扩大这些材料在工业相关反应中的应用范围。
