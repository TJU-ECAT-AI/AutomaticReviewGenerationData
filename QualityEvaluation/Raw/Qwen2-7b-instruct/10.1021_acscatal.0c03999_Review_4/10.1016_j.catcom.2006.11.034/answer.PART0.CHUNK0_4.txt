<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. The catalysts were characterized using a series of advanced techniques including X-ray diffraction (XRD), Raman spectroscopy, ultraviolet-visible diffuse reflectance spectroscopy (UVVis DRS), X-ray photoelectron spectros- copy (XPS), scanning electron microscopy (SEM), energy dispersive X-ray (EDX), Fourier transform infrared spectroscopy (FTIR), temperature programmed desorption of NH3 and CO2 (NH3-TPD and CO2-TPD), and N2 adsorption methods.
2. The surface acidity of the GZ catalysts was quantified using the NH3-TPD technique, providing detailed data on the number of acid sites.
3. The nature of acid sites on the 15GZ catalyst was assessed through in situ FTIR spectra of pyridine adsorption.
4. The surface basicity of the GZ catalysts was determined by CO2-TPD, offering insights into the number of base sites.
5. A correlation was established between the catalytic activity for ethylbenzene dehydrogenation in the presence of CO2 and the surface acidity/basicity of the GZ catalysts.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
