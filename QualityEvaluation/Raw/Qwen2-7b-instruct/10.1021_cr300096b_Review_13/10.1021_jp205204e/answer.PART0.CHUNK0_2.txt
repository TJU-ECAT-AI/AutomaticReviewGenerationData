<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The thermodynamic stability of specific bimetallic structures plays a crucial role in hydrogenation reactions, influencing the activity and selectivity of catalysts. There is a strong correlation between the behavior of model surfaces and supported catalysts, which allows for insights into the catalyst's performance. This correlation has significant implications for designing more effective catalysts through first principles calculations on model surfaces, enabling the prediction and optimization of catalytic properties.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
