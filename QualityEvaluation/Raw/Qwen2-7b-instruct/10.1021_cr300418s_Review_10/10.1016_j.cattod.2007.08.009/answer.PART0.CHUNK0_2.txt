[1] "Recent STM, DFT and HAADF-STEM studies of sulfide-based hydrotreating catalysts: Insight into mechanistic, structural and particle size effects"
</quotes>
<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The document discusses recent studies using scanning tunnelling microscopy (STM), density functional theory (DFT), and high-angle annular dark-field scanning transmission electron microscopy (HAADF-STEM) to explore the structure, mechanism, and size effects of sulfide-based hydrotreating catalysts. It highlights insights into the detailed atomic structure of catalysts like MoS2, CoMoS, and NiMoS, and the development of brim states that play a role in catalytic properties. The studies reveal that the promoters Co and Ni change the morphology of the nanoparticles, and that size-dependent structures of MoS2 exhibit new properties.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
