Redox behavior of palladium at start-up in the Perovskite-type LaFePdOx automotive catalysts showing a self-regenerative function Mari Uenishia,*
</literature>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
This study investigates the redox properties of palladium species in Perovskite-type LaFePdOx catalysts used in automotive emissions control, particularly focusing on their behavior during start-up processes. Palladium species segregate from the B-site of the Perovskite matrix under a reductive atmosphere, forming a metallic alloy of PdCo. These segregated Pd0 species are re-oxidized at temperatures around 200-300°C, leading to a reversible redox reaction between surface Pd0 and cationic Pd in the matrix. This process contributes to the catalyst's superior start-up activity by providing active sites for catalytic reactions at low temperatures.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

这项研究探讨了在汽车排放控制中使用的Perovskite-type LaFePdOx催化剂中的钯物种的氧化还原性质，特别是关注其在启动过程中的行为。在还原气氛下，钯物种从Perovskite矩阵的B位点分离，形成PdCo的金属合金。这些分离出的Pd0物种在约200-300°C下被重新氧化，导致表面Pd0和矩阵中阳离子Pd之间的可逆氧化还原反应。这一过程通过提供低温条件下的活性中心，有助于催化剂的快速启动活动。
