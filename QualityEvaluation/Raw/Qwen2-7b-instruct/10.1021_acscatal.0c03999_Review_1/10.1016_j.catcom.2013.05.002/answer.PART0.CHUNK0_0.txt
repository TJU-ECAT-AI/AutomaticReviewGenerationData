The study focuses on hydrothermally prepared Cr2O3-ZrO2 as an innovative catalyst for the dehydrogenation of propane with CO2. The performance of these catalysts is influenced by the evolution of chromium species within supported Cr2O3 catalysts, which varies based on the loading and support properties.
