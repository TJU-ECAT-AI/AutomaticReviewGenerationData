How do various metal oxide catalysts compare in terms of activity and selectivity for CO2-ODHP, and what are the key factors influencing their catalytic performance, with particular emphasis on the evolution of chromium species in supported Cr2O3 catalysts as a function of loading and support properties?

Different metal oxides like Ga2O3, Fe2O3, and Cr2O3 show varying degrees of activity in CO2-ODHP, with Ga2O3 being the most active followed by Fe2O3 and Cr2O3. The performance of these catalysts is influenced by several factors including the dispersion of metal oxide species, particularly at lower oxidation states (Cr3+/Cr2+), and the textural properties of the support material. The evolution of chromium species in supported Cr2O3 catalysts is crucial for achieving high C3H6 yield in CO2-ODHP. The redox cycle is a significant factor in ensuring high C3H6 yield. The nature of the support material plays a critical role in affecting the dispersion and evolution of chromium species, thus determining the intrinsic activity of the catalyst.
