            <Main>
                    The oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) of propane is a pivotal industrial process for propylene production. Supported vanadium oxide catalysts have been extensively studied due to their high activity and selectivity, but supported chromium oxide (Cr2O3) also emerges as a viable alternative for CO2-ODHP, influenced by metal loading, support properties, and the evolution of metal species during the reaction. The performance of supported Cr2O3 catalysts is notably affected by the chromium loading and the characteristics of the support material. Chromium species within these catalysts evolve dynamically, playing a crucial role in catalytic activity and selectivity.
            </Main>
            <Conclusion>
                    The study of \[10.1021/cs5003417\] offers a detailed insight into the comparative analysis of metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP, emphasizing the importance of chromium species evolution in supported Cr2O3 catalysts. The work by \[10.1016/j.apcata.2018.03.020\], \[10.1016/j.molcata.2004.12.006\], and \[10.1021/cr5002436\] further enriches this understanding by exploring the impact of various metal oxides as supports for Cr2O3, highlighting the effect on catalytic performance. This review aims to provide a comprehensive overview of metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP, focusing on performance factors, species evolution, and structure-activity relationships.
            </Conclusion>
