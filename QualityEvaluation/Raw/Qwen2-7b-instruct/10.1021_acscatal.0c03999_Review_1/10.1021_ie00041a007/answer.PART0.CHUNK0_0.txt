<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. The 1.5% K-5.5% Cr-17% Mn-O/SiOn catalyst shows different properties in methane conversion with CO2, with CO prevailing in the reaction products.
2. The 1% La-13% Na-17% Mn-O/SiOz catalyst is selective for Cz-hydrocarbon formation in non-steady-state conditions.
3. The 5.5% Cr-17% Mn-O/SiOz catalyst is the most active and selective for producing CzH4.
4. Additional modification of the 5.5% Cr-17% Mn-O/SiOz catalyst with potassium increases its selectivity from 65.4% to 76.8% at 830 °C.
5. Manganese oxides play a crucial role in forming intermediate MnCO3, which decomposes through redox interaction with hydrocarbons.
6. The redox mechanism of manganese carbonate decomposition is essential in this context.
7. The rate of ethane dehydrogenation by carbon dioxide is governed by the equation involving rate constants of catalyst oxidation and reduction.
