1. The 1.5% K-5.5% Cr-17% Mn-O/SiOn catalyst shows that CO predominates in the products during methane conversion with CO2, conforming to reaction 8.
2. The 1% La-13% Na-17% Mn-O/SiOz catalyst was highly selective for Cz-hydrocarbon formation in the non-steady-state regime.
3. Among the studied supported manganese oxide systems, 17% MnO/SiO2 was the most effective catalyst.
4. The rate of catalyst reduction by ethane is nearly equal to that of catalyst reoxidation by CO2.
5. The scheme for ethane conversions includes C2Hs transforming into C2H4 through different sites (Mn and Cr sites).
6. The kinetic equation for the process resembles the Mars-Van Krevelen equation, differing from equations 12 and 19 for alkanes.
7. The rate of catalyst reduction by ethane is comparable to the rate of catalyst reoxidation by CO2.
8. The rate of CO formation during CO2 interaction with the reduced Cr-Mn-O/SiOz catalyst is notably lower than in steady-state conditions.
