<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Various transition metal oxide catalysts, such as those based on indium, gallium, vanadium, and chromium, have been reported for CO2-ODHP. Chromium oxides show particularly high activity in these reactions.

2. The nature of CO2 interaction varies significantly with different supports, including SiO2, Al2O3, ZrO2, and zeolites. 

3. For mixed Cr2O3AZrO2 catalysts, CO2 has a positive effect on the DHP reaction.

4. The synthesis strategy combines an acid-base pair pathway with evaporation-induced self-assembly.

5. The 7CrAZrO2 catalyst, with abundant dispersed oligomeric Cr6+ species and isolated Cr6+ species, demonstrated the highest catalytic activity with an initial conversion of 68% and yield of 40.5%.

6. The regenerative cycles of the xCrAZrO2 catalysts are represented in supplementary material Figure S3.

7. As the chromium species increased, the propylene selectivity initially rose but then decreased. Conversely, methane selectivity showed the opposite trend.

8. The reduction of surface chromium species is indicated by the Raman intensity of Cr@O bonds.

9. Oxygen vacancies facilitate the oxidation of Cr3+ species.

10. CO2 helps maintain Cr3+ species, inhibiting coke formation.

11. The highest TOF (C3H6) was observed on the 7CrAZrO2 catalyst.

12. Active sites under real reaction conditions and the redox reaction path involve CO2's role in facilitating propane combustion and light alkene polymerization.

13. Oxygen adsorption processes on oxygen vacancies are calculated for Cr species.

14. Acid sites on the catalyst surface are key factors in propane cracking.

15. The presence of CO2 is beneficial for coke elimination and inhibition.

</EnglishVersionAnswer>
