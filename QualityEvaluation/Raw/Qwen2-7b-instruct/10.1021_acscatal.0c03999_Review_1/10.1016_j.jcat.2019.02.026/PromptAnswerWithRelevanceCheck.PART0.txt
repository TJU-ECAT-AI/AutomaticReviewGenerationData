2. The synthesized materials feature a mesoporous structure and modified zirconia crystals with doped chromium species.

5. Under real reaction conditions, the active sites for propane dehydrogenation with CO2 are the coordinatively unsaturated oligomeric and isolated Cr3+ sites.

6. The active oxidative chromium species, which are the oligomeric and isolated Cr3+ species with activated oxygen from CO2 dissociation, play a crucial role in the reaction.

7. CO2 inhibits the reduction of Cr3+ species to Cr2+ species, preserving the active state of chromium species.

8. Methane and CO are primarily produced as byproducts from the reaction of propylene with CO2 on the active oxidative chromium sites.

9. The presence of CO2 primarily inhibits coke formation rather than eliminating it, due to the low rate of CO2 elimination of coke.

6. Under real reaction conditions, the coordinationally unsaturated oligomeric/isolated Cr3+ sites with oxygen vacancies and active oxidative chromium sites are the primary catalytically active sites.
