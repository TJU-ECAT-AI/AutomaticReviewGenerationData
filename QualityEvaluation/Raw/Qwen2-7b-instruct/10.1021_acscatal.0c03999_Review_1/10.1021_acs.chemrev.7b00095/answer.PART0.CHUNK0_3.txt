Answer: Based on the literature, the metal oxide catalysts' activity and selectivity for CO2-ODHP (oxidative decarboxylation of propionic acid to propene) vary significantly. The evolution of chromium species in supported Cr2O3 catalysts depends on the loading and support properties. Factors influencing the catalytic performance include the loading of chromium, the type of support material, and the conditions under which the catalyst operates. For instance, Rodriguez et al. studied adsorption of H2S on metal oxide surfaces, observing that chromium trioxide (Cr3O4) interacts strongly with H2S. DFT (Density Functional Theory) studies have been conducted on the reactions of H2S with various metal oxides, including zinc (Zn), iron (Fe), cerium (Ce), titanium (Ti), manganese (Mn), vanadium (V), copper (Cu), cobalt (Co), and magnesium (Mg). The size, shape, and surface area of the metal oxide particles, as well as the synthesis recipes, impact the catalyst's performance in terms of H2S breakthrough capacity. The studies highlight the importance of considering these factors to optimize the catalyst's efficiency and selectivity for CO2-ODHP reactions.
