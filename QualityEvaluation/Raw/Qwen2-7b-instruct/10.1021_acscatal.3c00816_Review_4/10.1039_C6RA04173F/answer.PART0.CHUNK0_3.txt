1. The demand for propene has led to increased interest in catalytic propane dehydrogenation. Higher temperatures can increase the equilibrium conversion by thermodynamics.
2. The optimal cerium content for the catalyst is 0.76 wt%, resulting in high activity and stability during propane dehydrogenation.
3. Ce-ZSM-5 zeolites were synthesized using a hydrothermal method.
4. The uniform distribution of Pt nanoparticles on the catalyst surface is achieved with a suitable cerium content in the support.
5. Increased interactions between tin species and the support lead to more tin species existing in their oxidized state.
6. The surface architecture consists of Pt nanoparticles decorated by SnOx species, serving as the main active sites for dehydrogenation reactions.
7. Ce-modified ZSM-5 zeolites strengthen the interactions between Pt active sites, promoters, and the support, stabilize Pt particles, and increase noble metal dispersion on the support.
8. The presence of cerium enhances the catalyst's ability to adsorb hydrogen on metallic Pt and at high temperatures.
9. The stabilization of Pt particles through increased surface interaction allows for better anchoring of active sites on tin oxide surfaces.
10. Changes in catalytic acidity and active metal character impact propene selectivity.
11. The substitution of cerium promotes the thermal stability of the zeolite against surface area loss.
12. The coke amount decreases with increasing cerium content in the support, following the order: PtSnNa/ZSM-5 > PtSnNa/Ce(0.35%)ZSM-5 > PtSnNa/Ce(0.76%)ZSM-5 > PtSnNa/Ce(1.15%)ZSM-5 > PtSnNa/Ce(1.58%)ZSM-5.
