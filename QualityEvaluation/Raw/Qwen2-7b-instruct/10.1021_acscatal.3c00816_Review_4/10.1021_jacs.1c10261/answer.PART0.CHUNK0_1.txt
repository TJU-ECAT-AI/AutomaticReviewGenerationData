1. The preparation methods, composition, and zeolite topology significantly influence the performance of various zeolite-based catalysts in propane dehydrogenation reactions. Key factors contributing to improved propylene selectivity, conversion rates, and catalyst stability include specific surface area, pore size distribution, and the presence of active metal sites.
2. BEA topologic and silicoaluminophosphate zeolites were employed in the study, showing enhanced activity and selectivity through the use of isolated Pt atoms within the SiOZnOH nests in dealuminated Zeolite Beta.
3. Comprehensive characterization techniques confirmed the structure and functionality of the catalysts, leading to high propylene yields under optimized conditions, which demonstrates their potential for industrial application.
