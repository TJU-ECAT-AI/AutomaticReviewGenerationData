1. The preparation methods, composition, and zeolite topology of BEA topologic and silicoaluminophosphate zeolites significantly impact their performance in propane dehydrogenation reactions. Key factors contributing to improved propylene selectivity, conversion rates, and catalyst stability include the precise arrangement of metal sites, the presence of acidic sites for dehydrogenation, and the topology of the zeolite structure, which influences diffusion pathways and accessibility of reactants and products. Metal loading and support properties also play crucial roles in determining the catalytic activity and stability.

2. In propane dehydrogenation, the performance of various zeolite-based catalysts is influenced by the preparation methods, composition, and zeolite topology. The BEA topologic zeolites and silicoaluminophosphate zeolites have been optimized for their pore structure and metal loading, leading to enhanced propylene selectivity and conversion rates. The topology of the zeolite helps in controlling the reaction pathways, affecting the efficiency of dehydrogenation and hydrogenolysis reactions.
