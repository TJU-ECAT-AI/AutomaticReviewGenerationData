The incorporation of zinc oxide (ZnO) into the Y zeolite, for instance, substantially enhances its activity, with a conversion rate reaching 1116% at 550°C upon preparation via 13 cycles of ZnO atomic layer deposition (ALD) (reference [10.1039/C5CP05043J]). This enhancement is paralleled by the superior performance of the nanolith catalytic system in oxidative dehydrogenation of cyclohexane, showcasing both efficiency and reduction in over-oxidation (reference [10.1021/acs.iecr.9b00032]). The ALD process generates high-density ZnO sites on the support, which boosts catalytic activity but may also lead to the formation of coke species and crystallization of catalytic phases, thus accelerating catalyst deactivation (references [10.1039/C5CP05043J], [10.1021/acs.iecr.9b00032]). The micropore size of the Y zeolite being significantly larger than that of DEZ allows for easier entry of precursor molecules into the micropores during the initial ALD cycles (reference [10.1039/C5CP05043J]). Post-stability tests reveal a color change from white to black in the catalyst samples, indicative of potential deactivation mechanisms (references [10.1039/C5CP05043J], [10.1021/acs.iecr.9b00032]).

        The BEA topological structure of the zeolite enhances catalytic activity, as evidenced by its role in propane dehydrogenation (reference [10.1021/jacs.2c03941]). Silicoaluminophosphate (SAPO) zeolites, on the other hand, demonstrate better stability and selectivity towards propylene compared to other types of zeolites (references [10.1021/acs.iecr.9b00032], [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]). The addition of phosphorus (P) to a nickel-phosphorus (NiP) catalyst improves its performance in isobutane dehydrogenation, highlighting the importance of composition in enhancing catalytic activity (references [10.1021/acs.iecr.9b00032], [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]). The use of cobalt (Co)-modified dealuminated Beta (SiBeta) zeolite, specifically in the form of CoSiBeta catalysts, showcases state-of-the-art propylene productivity and selectivity with propane conversion rates of about 72% and propylene selectivity exceeding 92% at 600°C (references [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]). This research not only provides a green and economically viable nonprecious dehydrogenation catalyst but also offers new insights into the active Co phase for propane dehydrogenation reactions (reference [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]).

        The high-temperature stability of the subnanometric Pt species in Pt@MCM-22, demonstrated through oxidation-reduction treatments at 650°C, is another critical factor influencing the performance of zeolite-based catalysts in propane dehydrogenation (reference [10.1038/nmat4757]). The formation of small Pt nanoparticles with sizes around 1nm after one cycle of treatment at this temperature indicates the exceptional stability of Pt@MCM-22, which is further validated by its higher activity in dehydrogenating propane compared to other catalysts (references [10.1038/nmat4757], [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]). The increasing world demand for propylene and the current low cost of propane derived from shale gas have renewed interest in propane dehydrogenation reactions (reference [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]). Direct dehydrogenation of propane to propylene is of great significance in the chemical industry, upgrading low-priced and abundant propane to value-added propylene (reference [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]).
        
        Understanding the impact of preparation methods, composition, and zeolite topology on the performance of zeolite-based catalysts in propane dehydrogenation reactions is crucial. This includes the role of BEA topology and silicoaluminophosphate zeolites in improving propylene selectivity, conversion rates, and catalyst stability (references [10.1021/jacs.2c03941], [10.1021/acs.iecr.9b00032], [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]). Indium species, especially in the form of [InH2]+, play a significant role in affecting catalytic activity, selectivity, and stability, influencing the overall efficiency of the catalysts in converting propane to propylene (references [10.1039/D1SC05866E], [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]). The high-temperature stability of Pt@MCM-22, as well as the stability and selectivity of CoSiBeta catalysts, are key factors contributing to the improved performance of these catalysts in propane dehydrogenation reactions (references [10.1038/nmat4757], [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]).

        The earth abundance of cobalt (Co) makes it a promising material for long-term utility in propane dehydrogenation reactions (reference [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]). Its reported catalytic activity for propane dehydrogenation, coupled with the ability to develop ultrasmall-sized Co particles within the dealuminated Beta (SiBeta) zeolite support, enhances its performance in catalyzing direct dehydrogenation of propane to propylene (reference [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]). The formation of highly dispersed CoOx species and their confinement in the T-sites (silanols) of the SiBeta support contribute to the catalyst's superior performance (reference [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]). The CoSiBeta catalysts with 0.5 wt% Co not only show state-of-the-art propylene productivity but also match the catalytic activity of commercial platinum (Pt) and chromium oxide (CrOx) catalysts, demonstrating their cost-effectiveness and high activity (reference [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]).

        Questions and discussions focusing on the impact of zeolite topology, preparation methods, and composition on the performance of zeolite-based catalysts in propane dehydrogenation reactions highlight the pivotal role of factors such as the BEA topology, silicoaluminophosphate zeolites, and the use of cobalt (Co) in enhancing propylene selectivity, conversion rates, and catalyst stability (references [10.1039/D1SC05866E], [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]). These studies collectively provide a comprehensive understanding of the factors influencing the performance of zeolite-based catalysts in propane dehydrogenation reactions, offering insights into the development of more efficient and sustainable catalysts for industrial applications.

        从[10.1039/C5CP05043J]的深入研究中获得了详细的分析。通过参考[10.1021/acs.iecr.9b00032]、[10.1021/jacs.2c03941]和[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]，我们综合理解了这些文献中的内容。例如，将锌氧化物（ZnO）融入Y沸石中显著提高了其活性，在550°C时通过13周期的ZnO原子层沉积（ALD）制备的Y沸石的转化率达到1116%（参考文献[10.1039/C5CP05043J]）。在氧化脱氢反应中，这种增强作用在环己烷的氧化脱氢中得以体现，既提高了效率，又减少了过氧化现象（参考文献[10.1021/acs.iecr.9b00032]）。ALD过程在支持体上生成高密度的ZnO位点，从而提升了催化活性，但这也可能导致焦炭物种的生成和催化相的结晶，进而加速催化剂的失效（参考文献[10.1039/C5CP05043J]、[10.1021/acs.iecr.9b00032]）。Y沸石的微孔尺寸显著大于DEZ，允许前驱分子在前三个ALD周期内轻松进入微孔（参考文献[10.1039/C5CP05043J]）。稳定性测试后，催化剂样品的颜色从白色变为黑色，这可能预示着潜在的失效机制（参考文献[10.1039/C5CP05043J]、[10.1021/acs.iecr.9b00032]）。

        BEA拓扑结构的沸石可以提高催化活性，这在丙烷脱氢反应中得到了体现（参考文献[10.1021/jacs.2c03941]）。相比之下，硅铝磷酸盐（SAPO）沸石在稳定性和丙烯选择性方面表现出色，与其它类型的沸石相比（参考文献[10.1021/acs.iecr.9b00032]、[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]）。向镍磷（NiP）催化剂中添加磷（P）显著提高了其异丁烷脱氢性能，强调了组成在提升催化活性中的重要性（参考文献[10.1021/acs.iecr.9b00032]、[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]）。改性为钴（Co）的去铝化贝塔（SiBeta）沸石，特别是以CoSiBeta催化剂的形式出现，展示了约72%的丙烷转化率和超过92%的丙烯选择性，且在600°C下具有极佳的产率（参考文献[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]）。这项研究不仅提供了一种绿色且经济高效的非贵金属脱氢催化剂，还为丙烷脱氢反应中的活性Co相提供了新的见解（参考文献[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]）。

        通过650°C下的氧化还原处理证明了Pt@MCM-22中亚纳米Pt物种的高稳定性（参考文献[10.1038/nmat4757]）。在这一过程中形成的大小约为1nm的小Pt纳米颗粒在经过一个周期的处理后存在于Pt@MCM-22中，这表明了Pt@MCM-22的出色稳定性，并通过在550°C下脱氢丙烷时表现出比其他催化剂更高的活性进行了验证（参考文献[10.1038/nmat4757]、[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]）。对丙烷脱氢反应的需求，以及来自页岩气的丙烷成本低廉，已经激发了人们对丙烷脱氢反应的兴趣（参考文献[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]）。直接将丙烷转化为丙烯对于化学工业而言具有重大意义，它能够将价格低廉、丰富的丙烷升级为附加值较高的丙烯（参考文献[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]）。

        理解准备方法、组成和沸石拓扑结构对丙烷脱氢反应中沸石基催化剂性能的影响至关重要。这包括BEA拓扑结构、硅铝磷酸盐沸石在提高丙烯选择性、转化率和催化剂稳定性方面的关键作用（参考文献[10.1021/jacs.2c03941]、[10.1021/acs.iecr.9b00032]、[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]）。尤其是In物种，特别是在形式为[InH2]+的情况下，对催化活性、选择性和稳定性有着显著影响，这直接影响了催化剂在将丙烷转化为丙烯过程中的效率（参考文献[10.1039/D1SC05866E]、[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]）。Pt@MCM-22的高温稳定性，以及CoSiBeta催化剂的稳定性和选择性，是这些催化剂在丙烷脱氢反应中表现优异的关键因素（参考文献[10.1038/nmat4757]、[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]）。

        钴（Co）的地球丰富性使其成为长期应用的有前景材料（参考文献[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]）。其在丙烷脱氢反应中的报告活性，结合在去铝化贝塔（SiBeta）沸石支撑中开发超小Co粒子的能力，增强了其催化直接脱氢丙烷为丙烯的能力（参考文献[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]）。高度分散的CoOx物种的存在及其被限制在SiBeta支持的T位点（硅醇）中，有助于催化剂的优异性能（参考文献[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]）。CoSiBeta催化剂的0.5 wt%不仅显示出最先进的丙烯生产力，而且与商用铂（Pt）和铬氧化物（CrOx）催化剂的活性相当，这表明它们在成本效益和高活性方面表现出色（参考文献[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]）。

        关注于沸石拓扑结构、准备方法和组成的影响力对丙烷脱氢反应中沸石基催化剂性能的理解问题和讨论，强调了BEA拓扑结构、硅铝磷酸盐沸石和钴（Co）在提高丙烯选择性、转化率和催化剂稳定性方面的关键作用（参考文献[10.1039/D1SC05866E]、[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]）。这些研究共同提供了全面理解影响丙烷脱氢反应中沸石基催化剂性能的因素，为促进该领域的高效可持续催化剂的发展提供了见解。
