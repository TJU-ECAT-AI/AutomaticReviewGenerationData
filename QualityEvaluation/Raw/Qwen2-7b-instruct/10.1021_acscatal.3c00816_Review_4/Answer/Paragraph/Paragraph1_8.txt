The increasing world demand for propylene and the current low cost of propane derived from shale gas have renewed interest in the propane dehydrogenation reaction (PDH), highlighting the importance of catalysts that efficiently convert propane to propylene. Zeolite-based catalysts, particularly those with BEA topology and silicoaluminophosphate (SAPO) zeolites, have shown significant promise due to their enhanced propylene selectivity, conversion rates, and stability. 

        Preparation methods, composition, and zeolite topology significantly influence the performance of these catalysts. For instance, the addition of phosphorus (P) to a nickel-phosphorus (NiP) catalyst improves its performance in isobutane dehydrogenation, while the BEA topological structure of the zeolite enhances catalytic activity in PDH. Silicoaluminophosphate zeolites demonstrate better stability and selectivity towards propylene compared to other types of zeolites. The ALD process for incorporating ZnO on the support leads to high-density ZnO sites, boosting catalytic activity but also posing risks for coke formation and crystallization of catalytic phases, which can accelerate catalyst deactivation. The micropore size of Y zeolite allows precursor molecules to easily enter the micropores during ALD cycles, facilitating the enhancement of catalytic properties.

        The use of cobalt (Co) in dealuminated Beta (SiBeta) zeolite, through the formation of ultrasmall Co particles confined within the zeolite's silanols, has proven highly efficient for catalyzing direct PDH to propylene. The CoOx species, easily reducible to metallic Co particles, are critical for the catalyst's activity, and their presence in the T-sites of the SiBeta support contributes to the catalyst's superior performance. The CoSiBeta catalysts with 0.5 wt% Co exhibit state-of-the-art propylene productivity and selectivity, matching that of commercial Pt and CrOx catalysts. The high-temperature stability of the subnanometric Pt species in Pt@MCM-22 has also been demonstrated, showcasing the potential for efficient dehydrogenation of short alkane molecules.

        However, challenges remain, including the need for further optimization of preparation methods, understanding of the precise role of zeolite topology, and the development of catalysts with enhanced stability and selectivity. The complex interplay between the structure of the zeolite, composition, and preparation techniques continues to be a focal area of research, aiming to improve the efficiency and longevity of PDH catalysts.

        准备方法、组成和沸石拓扑结构显著影响这些催化剂的性能。例如，向镍磷（NiP）催化剂中添加磷（P）可提高其在异丁烷脱氢化中的性能；而沸石的BEA拓扑结构则提高了丙烷脱氢的催化活性。与其它类型沸石相比，硅铝磷酸盐沸石在稳定性和丙烯选择性方面表现出更好的性能。等离子体原子层沉积（ALD）过程用于在支撑物上引入ZnO，导致高密度的ZnO位点，从而增强了催化活性，但也存在形成焦炭物种和催化相结晶的风险，这可能导致催化剂加速失效。Y沸石的微孔尺寸允许前体分子在ALD循环期间轻松进入微孔，从而促进了催化性质的增强。

        在dealuminated Beta（SiBeta）沸石中使用钴（Co），通过在二氧化硅支持物的T位点（硅醇）内形成超小钴颗粒，已证明对于直接将丙烷脱氢为丙烯非常有效。可还原的CoOx物种对于催化剂的活性至关重要，并且它们的存在有助于SiBeta支持的催化剂性能的优越性。CoSiBeta催化剂中0.5 wt％的Co显示出最先进的丙烯生产力和选择性，与商用Pt和CrOx催化剂匹配。650°C时，Pt@MCM-22中亚纳米级Pt物种的高稳定性也得到了证实，展示了高效脱氢短烷烃分子的能力。

        然而，挑战仍然存在，包括需要进一步优化准备方法、理解精确的结构作用和开发具有增强稳定性和选择性的催化剂。关于沸石基PDH催化剂结构、组成和准备技术的复杂相互作用仍然是研究的重点，旨在改进PDH催化剂的效率和寿命。
