Understanding of the impact of preparation methods, composition, and zeolite topology on the performance of zeolite-based catalysts in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions is pivotal. The addition of phosphorus (P) to a nickel-phosphorus (NiP) catalyst significantly improves its performance in isobutane dehydrogenation, while the BEA topological structure of the zeolite enhances catalytic activity in propane dehydrogenation. Silicoaluminophosphate (SAPO) zeolites show better stability and selectivity towards propylene compared to other types of zeolites. Improved propylene selectivity, conversion rates, and catalyst stability are attributed to specific features of the BEA and SAPO zeolites.

        The ALD process significantly enhances the activity of the Y zeolite, increasing its conversion rate to 1116% at 550°C when prepared through 13 cycles of ZnO ALD. The nanolith catalytic system outperforms conventional powders in oxidative dehydrogenation of cyclohexane, demonstrating both efficiency and reduction in over-oxidation. The ALD process generates high-density ZnO sites on the support, which boosts catalytic activity but also risks coke formation and crystallization of catalytic phases, leading to accelerated catalyst deactivation. The micropore size of Y zeolite is notably larger than that of DEZ, allowing precursor molecules to easily enter the micropores during the first three ALD cycles. Post-stability testing, the catalyst color transitions from white to black, indicating potential deactivation mechanisms.

        The high-temperature stability of the subnanometric Pt species in Pt@MCM-22 has been demonstrated through oxidation-reduction treatments at 650°C, resulting in the formation of small Pt nanoparticles with sizes around 1nm. These nanoparticles are found in Pt@MCM-22 after one cycle of treatment at this temperature. Individual Pt atoms and small Pt clusters within the MCM-22 crystallites aggregate to form larger Pt clusters or small Pt nanoparticles in the range of 1 to 2nm. Considering the exceptional stability of Pt@MCM-22, its catalytic activity for dehydrogenating propane has been tested. It is noted that the dehydrogenation of short alkane molecules is crucial for alkane activation and requires frequent regeneration cycles at high temperatures. At 550°C, Pt@MCM-22 demonstrates higher activity than the Pt/MCM-22-imp catalyst.

        Continuous global oil depletion provokes the urgent demand to find an alternative way to produce propylene. Direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) to propylene is thus of great significance in the chemical industry, upgrading low-priced and abundant propane to value-added propylene. Supported CrOx and Pt catalysts used in the PDH industry exhibit very high catalytic activity, but suffer from various problems such as high price, environmental hostility, and fast deactivation. Alternatively, several transition metals, void of shortcomings typical of CrOx and Pt catalysts, have been investigated for the PDH reaction. Unfortunately, they achieved much lower catalytic activity or propylene selectivity for PDH. The earth abundance of Co bodes well for long-term utility, and it has been reported to show a certain degree of catalytic activity for PDH. Dealuminated Beta (SiBeta) zeolite, particularly when modified with cobalt (Co), enables the creation of ultrasmall Co particles confined within the zeolite's silanols. This configuration facilitates high propane conversion and exceptional propylene selectivity. The CoOx species, which can easily be reduced to metallic Co particles, are crucial for the catalyst's activity. The presence of CoOx species in the T-sites of the SiBeta support contributes to the catalyst's superior performance. The CoSiBeta catalysts with 0.5 wt% Co exhibit state-of-the-art propylene productivity and selectivity, matching that of commercial Pt and CrOx catalysts. The small confined Co particles serve as the active sites, showing great catalytic activity with high product selectivity and excellent reusability.

        从参考文献[10.1039/C5CP05043J]中进行了深入分析。通过参考文献[10.1021/acs.iecr.9b00032]、[10.1021/jacs.2c03941]和[10.1039/D1SC05866E]合成了理解。在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中，通过制备方法、组成和沸石拓扑结构影响沸石基催化剂性能的理解至关重要。向镍磷（NiP）催化剂中添加磷（P）显著提高了其异丁烷脱氢性能。BEA拓扑结构的沸石增强了催化活性。硅铝磷酸盐（SAPO）沸石在稳定性方面优于其他类型的沸石，并且具有更好的丙烯选择性。

        ALD过程显著提高了Y沸石的活性，使其在550°C时通过13周期的ZnO ALD制备的转化率达到1116%。纳米石催化系统在氧化脱氢环己烷中的效率高于传统粉末催化剂，并且减少了过氧化。ALD过程在支持物上产生高密度ZnO位点，这增加了催化活性，但也可能促进焦炭物种的生成和催化相的结晶，导致催化剂加速失活。Y沸石的微孔尺寸明显大于DEZ，允许前体分子在前三个ALD循环期间容易进入微孔。稳定性测试后，催化剂的颜色从白色变为黑色，表明潜在的失活机制。

        关注沸石拓扑、制备方法和组成对PDH中沸石基催化剂性能的影响，特别是BEA拓扑和硅铝磷酸盐沸石的作用。讨论集中在以[InH2]+形式存在的铟物种如何影响催化剂的活性、选择性和稳定性。明显的是，因素如铟负载、支撑材料和沸石结构对催化剂在将丙烷转化为丙烯时的效率产生了影响。特定现象，如额外框架铟物种（In-CHA）、Si/Al比例以及催化剂随时间的稳定性，都对问题答案的相关性有所贡献。

        650°C下氧化还原处理证明了Pt@MCM-22中亚纳米级Pt物种的极高稳定性，形成了约1nm大小的Pt纳米颗粒。这些纳米颗粒在进行一次处理后存在于Pt@MCM-22中。MCM-22晶粒内单个Pt原子和少量原子的小Pt簇聚集形成较大的Pt簇或小Pt纳米颗粒，范围为1到2nm。考虑到Pt@MCM-22的非凡稳定性，对其脱氢丙烷的催化活性进行了测试。注意到短烷烃的脱氢对于烷烃激活以及需要在高温下频繁再生循环是至关重要的。在550°C时，Pt@MCM-22的活性高于Pt/MCM-22-imp催化剂。

        全球石油持续枯竭引发了寻找替代方式生产丙烯的迫切需求。直接将丙烷（PDH）转化为丙烯在化学工业中具有重大意义，升级价格低廉且丰富的丙烷至附加值高的丙烯。支持CrOx和Pt催化剂在PDH行业表现出极高的催化活性，但存在诸如高价、环境敌对性和快速失活等问题。作为替代方案，研究了几种免去CrOx和Pt催化剂常见缺点的过渡金属，用于PDH反应。不幸的是，它们的催化活性或丙烯选择性在PDH中均较低。钴的地球丰富性有利于长期应用，已报告其具有一定的PDH催化活性。通过去除Beta（SiBeta）沸石中的铝，尤其是与钴（Co）修饰的沸石，能够创造出超小的Co颗粒，局限在SiBeta支持的T位点（硅醇）。这种配置促进了高丙烷转化率和异常的丙烯选择性。CoOx物种可以轻松还原成金属Co颗粒，是催化剂活性的关键。SiBeta支持中CoOx物种的存在有助于催化剂的优越性能。CoSiBeta催化剂中0.5 wt%的Co展示了最先进的丙烯生产力和选择性，与商业Pt和CrOx催化剂相当。受限于小的Co颗粒的活性位点显示出极高的催化活性、高产物选择性和优秀的可重复使用性。
