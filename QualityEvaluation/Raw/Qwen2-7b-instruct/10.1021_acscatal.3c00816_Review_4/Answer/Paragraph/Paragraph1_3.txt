            Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The exploration of zeolite-based catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reveals the pivotal role of preparation methods, composition, and zeolite topology. By leveraging the ALD process, the introduction of phosphorus (P) to NiP catalysts, the utilization of BEA topology, and the employment of silicoaluminophosphate (SAPO) zeolites, researchers have significantly enhanced propylene selectivity, conversion rates, and catalyst stability. Notably, the incorporation of ZnO into the Y zeolite framework and the use of cobalt within dealuminated Beta zeolite have shown remarkable improvements in performance. The high-temperature stability of Pt@MCM-22 and the advanced understanding of indium speciation further highlight the complexity and importance of these factors in PDH reactions. The findings underscore the potential for developing more efficient, sustainable, and cost-effective catalysts for PDH, with a particular emphasis on the active phase of cobalt catalysts. Future research might delve deeper into optimizing the synthesis conditions, exploring novel zeolite structures, and refining the support materials to achieve even greater catalytic efficiency and longevity. The integration of machine learning algorithms and computational modeling could also provide new insights into predicting and enhancing catalyst performance based on the interplay between zeolite topology, composition, and preparation methods.
