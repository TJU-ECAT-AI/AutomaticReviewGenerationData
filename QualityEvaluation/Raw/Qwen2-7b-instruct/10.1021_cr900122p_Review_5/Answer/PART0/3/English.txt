Analyzing the comprehensive mechanism of alkane metathesis catalyzed by silica-supported tantalum hydrides involves examining both two-site and one-site schemes. Linear and cyclic alkane transformations are central processes, with alkenes and metallacyclobutanes acting as significant intermediates. These factors contribute to the reaction's selectivity and the catalyst's performance in different alkane conversions.

1. The comprehensive mechanism of alkane metathesis catalyzed by silica-supported tantalum hydrides is analyzed. This includes the examination of both two-site and one-site schemes. Processes involved in linear and cyclic alkane transformations are discussed, along with the formation and role of key intermediates like alkenes and metallacyclobutanes. These elements contribute to reaction selectivity and the performance of the catalyst in various alkane conversions.

Key intermediates such as alkenes and metallacyclobutanes are identified, with alkenes playing a crucial role in facilitating the formation of new bond configurations, and metallacyclobutanes being cyclic compounds containing a metal atom that are synthesized during the reaction.
