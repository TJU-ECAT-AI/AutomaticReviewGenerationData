<EnglishVersionAnswer>
An extensive mechanism of alkane metathesis catalyzed by silica-supported tantalum hydrides involves both two-site and one-site schemes, encompassing linear and cyclic alkane transformations. Key intermediates such as alkenes and metallacyclobutanes form during these processes, playing critical roles in reaction selectivity and catalyst performance in various alkane conversions. The silica-supported tantalum hydrides act as efficient catalysts due to their ability to facilitate the formation and manipulation of these intermediates through selective activation and deactivation pathways. This results in enhanced conversion rates and improved product distributions compared to traditional liquid or zeolitic acid catalysts.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
