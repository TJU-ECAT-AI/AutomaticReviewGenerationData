These mechanisms facilitate the transformation of both linear and cyclic alkanes through the formation of key intermediates such as alkenes and metallacyclobutanes. The processes involve the rearrangement of alkane chains and the formation of alkene intermediates, which then react to produce new alkane products. The selectivity of these reactions is determined by the stability and reactivity of the intermediates, which are influenced by factors such as catalyst structure, temperature, and pressure conditions.
