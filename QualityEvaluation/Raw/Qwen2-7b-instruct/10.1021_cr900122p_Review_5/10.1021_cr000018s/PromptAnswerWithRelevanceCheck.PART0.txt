Key intermediates in this process include alkenes and metallacyclobutanes.

During the two-site mechanism, an alkyl group is abstracted from one alkane, forming an alkyl radical. This results in the conversion of one linear alkane into a cyclic alkane.

In the one-site mechanism, a single alkyl group is abstracted from the alkane and recombined with the catalyst, forming a metallacyclobutane intermediate. This intermediate then undergoes rearrangement, leading to the formation of a new alkane and a new alkyl group attached to the catalyst. This process enables the conversion of cyclic alkanes back into linear alkanes.

The selectivity and performance of the catalyst in various alkane conversions are influenced by factors such as the specific structure of the catalyst, reaction conditions, and the nature of the alkane substrate.
