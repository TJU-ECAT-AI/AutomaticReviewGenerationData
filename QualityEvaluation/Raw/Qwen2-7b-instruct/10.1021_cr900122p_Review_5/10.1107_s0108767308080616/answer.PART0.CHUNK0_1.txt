<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Analyzing the comprehensive mechanism of alkane metathesis catalyzed by silica-supported tantalum hydrides involves understanding both the two-site and one-site schemes. In linear alkane transformations, the catalyst facilitates the efficient cleavage and recombination of alkyl groups. For cyclic alkane transformations, the formation of metallacyclobutanes as key intermediates is essential for achieving high yields of cyclic alkane products. The role of these intermediates, along with alkenes, significantly influences reaction selectivity. Catalyst performance in various alkane conversions is impacted by parameters such as temperature, pressure, and substrate concentration.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
