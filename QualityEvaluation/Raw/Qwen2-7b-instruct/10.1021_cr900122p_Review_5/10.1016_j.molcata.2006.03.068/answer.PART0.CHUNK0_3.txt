[2] "Both the transformations of pure alkanes: n-butane isomerization, C2C4 alkane aromatization and of alkanes in mixture with alkenes: isobutanebutene alkylation or with aromatic hydrocarbons: benzene alkylation with ethane or propane are considered."

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The review paper discusses significant advancements in understanding the transformation of C2C4 alkanes into more valuable products using acid and bifunctional zeolite catalysts. It covers the mechanisms of activation, reaction schemes, and the nature of active sites involved in the transformation of n-butane isomerization, C2C4 alkane aromatization, isobutanebutene alkylation, and benzene alkylation with ethane or propane. Zeolite catalysts play a crucial role in these transformations.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

<ChineseVersionAnswer>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The paper explores the disproportionation and isomerization processes of light alkanes over acid zeolite catalysts. It delves into the chemistry of alkylation reactions and showcases the demonstration and nature of a bifunctional aromatization scheme. Additionally, it examines the dehydrogenation steps involved in the transformation of propane over Ga/HMFI catalysts.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

It discusses the formation and role of critical intermediates such as alkenes and metallacyclobutanes, explaining how these factors influence the selectivity of reactions and the performance of catalysts in various alkane conversions.
