Analyzing the comprehensive mechanism of alkane metathesis catalyzed by silica-supported tantalum hydrides involves understanding both two-site and one-site schemes. In a two-site mechanism, the metathesis reaction occurs between two distinct sites on the catalyst surface, typically involving the migration of alkyl groups from one site to another. This process can lead to the formation of alkenes and, under certain conditions, metallacyclobutanes. 

In a one-site mechanism, the reaction occurs within a single site on the catalyst surface, often resulting in the formation of a single alkene product. This mechanism is simpler and can lead to higher conversion rates, but it may not always provide the same level of selectivity as the two-site mechanism.

Linear alkane transformations, facilitated by the catalyst, involve the breaking and reforming of carbon-carbon bonds to produce a new alkene product. This process is driven by the high reactivity of the metal hydride sites, allowing for the selective activation of C-H bonds.

Cyclic alkane transformations, on the other hand, involve the creation of a cyclobutane ring structure before the metathesis reaction occurs. This can lead to the formation of more complex products, depending on the specific conditions and catalyst used.

Key intermediates in the alkane metathesis process include alkenes, which are formed during the cleavage of alkane C-H bonds. Metallacyclobutanes, which are cyclic structures containing a metal atom, can also play a crucial role as intermediates in the reaction sequence. The stability and reactivity of these intermediates determine the selectivity and efficiency of the metathesis process.

The catalyst performance is influenced by factors such as the surface area and dispersion of the silica support, the concentration of tantalum hydrides, and the reaction conditions. The selectivity of the process depends on the ability of the catalyst to control the migration of alkyl groups and the formation of specific alkene products.

在单位点机制中，反应发生在催化剂表面上的一个特定位置内，通常会导致单一烯烃产品的形成。这种机制更简单，可能提供更高的转化率，但其选择性可能不如双位点机制高。

线性烷烃转换通过破坏碳-碳键并产生新的烯烃产品来实现。这个过程受到金属氢化物位点高活性的驱动，允许对C-H键的选择性活化。

环烷烃转换涉及在异构化反应发生之前形成环丁烷环结构。这取决于特定条件和催化剂的使用，可能导致更复杂的产物。

在烷烃异构化过程中起关键作用的中间体包括烯烃，它们在烷烃C-H键断裂时形成。金属环丁烷，包含金属原子的环状结构，也可以作为反应序列中的中间体发挥关键作用。这些中间体的稳定性和活性决定了异构化过程的选择性和效率。

催化剂性能受到二氧化硅支持物表面积和分散度、钽氢化物浓度以及反应条件等因素的影响。选择性取决于催化剂控制烷基团迁移和形成特定烯烃产品的能力。
