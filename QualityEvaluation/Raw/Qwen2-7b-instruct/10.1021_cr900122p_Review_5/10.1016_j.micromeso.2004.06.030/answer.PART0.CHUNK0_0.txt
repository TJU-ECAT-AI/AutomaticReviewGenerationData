In the context of alkane metathesis, the catalyst enables the rearrangement of alkane molecules through the breaking and reformation of carbon-carbon bonds. The two-site and one-site schemes refer to the different mechanisms by which the catalyst facilitates the metathesis reaction. In the two-site scheme, two alkyl groups are transferred simultaneously, whereas in the one-site scheme, only one alkyl group is transferred at a time.

During the transformation of linear and cyclic alkane structures, the formation of key intermediates, such as alkenes and metallacyclobutanes, plays a crucial role in determining the selectivity and performance of the catalyst. Alkenes are often produced as a result of alkane metathesis, while metallacyclobutanes can serve as intermediates in the formation of more complex hydrocarbon structures.
