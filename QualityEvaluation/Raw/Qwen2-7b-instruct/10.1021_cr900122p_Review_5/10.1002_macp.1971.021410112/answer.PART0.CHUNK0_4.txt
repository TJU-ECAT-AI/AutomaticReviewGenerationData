<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The comprehensive mechanism of alkane metathesis catalyzed by silica-supported tantalum hydrides involves the use of systems such as WOC14/Al(C2H5)Cl and WOCl4(Sn(C4H9)4). This results in the telomerization of cyclic olefins. At the ends of the telomer chains, two identical or different groups derived from the acyclic olefin are fixed through a non-consecutive reaction. The participation of pentyne-2 in the cross-reaction is random compared to its participation in the disproportion reaction. The formation of products occurs simultaneously with the formation of butyne and hexyne, as evidenced by the parallel evolution of the curves D = f(t) and C = f(t). The calculations performed on the pentyne-2 and cyclopentene and defined as follows: free pentyne + transformed pentyne introduced bilan pentyne-2 = free cyclopentene + combined cyclopentene bilan cyclopentene = introduced cyclopentene have been calculated using chlorobenzene as an internal standard. These values range between 0.9 and 1.2, which can be considered a confirmation of the validity of the measurements and hypotheses given the number of formed products.

</EnglishVersionAnswer>
