1. The use of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) has been explored as an alternative to traditional methods, particularly due to its potential for high propylene selectivity at relatively low temperatures. This makes N2O-ODHP an attractive option for practical implementation, especially considering the environmental benefits of using N2O over other oxidants.

2. Various catalysts have been employed in the ODHP process, including gallium oxide (Ga2O3) and its potassium-promoted versions (Ga2O3-K1, Ga2O3-K2). The effectiveness of these catalysts varies depending on their properties and the specific conditions under which they are used.

3. The critical challenges associated with catalyst deactivation and stability in N2O-ODHP include the formation of coke on the catalyst surface, which can significantly reduce the activity and efficiency of the process. Another challenge is the potential loss of catalytic activity when potassium is introduced to the Ga2O3 catalyst, likely due to the blocking of active sites for dehydrogenation.

4. Strategies to address these limitations might include optimizing the conditions for N2O usage, such as adjusting the flow rates, temperature, and pressure to maximize selectivity and minimize coke formation. Research could also focus on developing new catalyst materials or modifying existing ones to improve their stability and activity, possibly by enhancing their ability to resist coke formation or by finding ways to remove coke deposits effectively.

5. Potential research directions could involve exploring alternative oxidants besides N2O that might offer even better performance in terms of selectivity, stability, and cost-effectiveness. Additionally, studying the fundamental mechanisms of the ODHP process, such as the role of acidic sites on catalysts and the interactions between the oxidant, reactants, and catalyst surfaces, could provide insights into how to design more efficient catalysts and optimize operating parameters for N2O-ODHP.

1. 硝酸氧化（N2O）作为丙烷脱氢制丙烯（ODHP）过程中的氧化剂被探索，主要是由于其在较低温度下具有高丙烯选择性。这使得N2O-ODHP成为一种有吸引力的实用选择，尤其是在使用N2O而不是其他氧化剂时考虑环境因素时。

2. 在ODHP过程中使用了各种催化剂，包括镓氧化物（Ga2O3）及其钾促进版本（Ga2O3-K1、Ga2O3-K2）。这些催化剂的有效性根据它们的性质和所用条件而变化。

3. N2O-ODHP过程面临的关键挑战包括催化剂失活和稳定性问题，尤其是催化剂表面形成的焦炭，这可以显著降低活性和效率。另一个挑战是向Ga2O3催化剂中引入钾导致催化活性损失，这可能是因为阻塞了脱氢作用的活性位点。

4. 为解决这些问题，可能的策略包括优化N2O的使用条件，例如调整流速、温度和压力以最大化选择性和最小化焦炭形成。研究还可以集中在开发新材料或修改现有材料上，以提高稳定性和活性，可能通过增强抵抗焦炭形成的能力或找到有效去除焦炭沉积的方法。

5. 可能的研究方向包括探索除了N2O之外的其他氧化剂，它们可能在选择性、稳定性和成本效益方面提供更好的性能。此外，研究ODHP过程的基本机制，如催化剂上的酸性位点的作用以及氧化剂、反应物和催化剂表面之间的相互作用，可以提供设计更高效催化剂和优化操作参数的见解。
