Analyzing the use of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP), it's evident that N2O offers advantages in terms of propylene selectivity compared to other oxidants. Specifically, when N2O is used, overall activity tends to decrease while propene selectivity increases for the same degree of propane conversion. This is due to N2O's slower decomposition rate compared to oxygen (O2), which leads to a lower steady-state concentration of active lattice oxygen species involved in propane and propene activation. This lower concentration facilitates more selective oxidation rather than combustion reactions, enhancing propene formation. Moreover, replacing O2 with N2O decreases the initial rates of product formation across all temperatures, which aligns with the redox mechanism of ODP.

The process employs various catalysts, including VOx/-Al2O3 materials with different vanadium oxide loadings. These catalysts are characterized through methods like XRD, TPR, EPR, UVVIS-DRS, CPD, and electrical conductivity measurements to understand their properties and how they affect catalytic performance. The results indicate that the structure and distribution of vanadia species play a crucial role in determining the selectivity and activity of the catalysts in ODHP. 

Challenges associated with catalyst deactivation and stability are addressed by optimizing the concentration of active lattice oxygen species. Using N2O as an oxidant helps control the selective oxidation and combustion reactions, making the process more efficient. Strategies to further improve N2O-ODHP feasibility might involve refining catalyst design to maximize propene selectivity and stability, exploring the use of non-acidic supports, and enhancing the ability of catalysts to reoxidize reduced active sites.

To make N2O-ODHP a more practical option, researchers could focus on developing advanced catalysts that effectively utilize N2O without compromising selectivity or stability. This might involve studying the interaction of N2O with catalyst surfaces, optimizing vanadia loading and dispersion, and investigating the role of surface reduction in propene formation. Additionally, developing methods to efficiently regenerate deactivated catalysts could extend their operational life and enhance the overall efficiency of the process.

该过程使用不同钒氧化物负载量的VOx/-Al2O3催化剂。通过XRD、TPR、EPR、UVVIS-DRS、CPD和电导率测量等方法对这些催化剂进行表征，以了解它们的性质及其对催化性能的影响。结果表明，钒物种的结构和分布对于确定催化剂在ODHP中的选择性和活性至关重要。

催化剂失活和稳定性的挑战可通过优化活性氧物种的浓度来解决。使用N2O作为氧化剂有助于控制选择性氧化和燃烧反应，使过程更加有效。为了进一步提高N2O-ODHP的可行性，研究者可以集中精力开发能够充分利用N2O同时不影响选择性和稳定性的先进催化剂。这可能涉及研究N2O与催化剂表面的相互作用、优化钒氧化物的负载和分散以及探究表面还原在丙烯形成中的作用。

为了使N2O-ODHP成为更可行的选项，研究人员可以专注于开发能够有效利用N2O而不损害选择性和稳定性的高级催化剂。这可能包括研究N2O与催化剂表面的相互作用、优化钒氧化物的负载和分散以及探索表面还原在丙烯形成中的作用。此外，开发高效再生失活催化剂的方法可以延长其使用寿命并增强过程的整体效率。
