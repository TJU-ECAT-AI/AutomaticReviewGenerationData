In ODHP processes, various catalysts are employed to facilitate the reaction. These can include noble metals like platinum, palladium, or copper-based catalysts. Catalysts play a crucial role in enhancing the reaction rate and improving the efficiency of the process.

However, there are critical challenges associated with catalyst deactivation and stability. Catalysts can become deactivated over time due to poisoning by impurities in the feedstock or by sintering. This necessitates regular regeneration or replacement of the catalyst, which adds to the operational costs. Additionally, maintaining the stability of the catalyst under harsh reaction conditions can be difficult, affecting the overall process performance.
