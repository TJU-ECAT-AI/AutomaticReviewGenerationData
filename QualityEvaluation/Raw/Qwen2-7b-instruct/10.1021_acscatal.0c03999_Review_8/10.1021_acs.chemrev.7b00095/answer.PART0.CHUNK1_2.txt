<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Nitrous oxide (N2O) has been explored as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP), offering advantages over other oxidants in terms of propylene selectivity.
2. Catalysts used in N2O-ODHP typically include metal oxides like CuO and supported amines.
3. Challenges in this process include catalyst deactivation and maintaining stability over multiple cycles.
4. To address these issues, strategies could involve enhancing catalyst support robustness or adjusting catalyst composition.
5. Research avenues might explore new catalyst materials or refining operational conditions to boost propylene selectivity and catalyst durability.
