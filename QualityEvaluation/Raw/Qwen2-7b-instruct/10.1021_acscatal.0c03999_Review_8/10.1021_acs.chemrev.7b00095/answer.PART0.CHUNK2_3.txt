<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The use of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) offers high propylene selectivity. Catalysts like noble metals (Pt, Pd, Ru) and supported catalysts (CuO/Al2O3, NiO/γ-Al2O3, TiO2) have been utilized. Challenges involve catalyst deactivation and stability due to coke formation. Potential solutions include developing more stable catalyst supports, employing promoter metals, and optimizing reaction conditions to reduce coke formation.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
