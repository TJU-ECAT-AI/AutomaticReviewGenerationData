[1] "The oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) has emerged as a promising technology for the production of propylene, which is a key intermediate in the petrochemical industry."
[2] "Among the various oxidants used in ODHP, nitrous oxide (N2O) has attracted significant attention due to its potential environmental benefits and propylene selectivity."
[3] "Supported vanadium oxide catalysts have been widely investigated for their high activity and selectivity in the ODHP process."
[4] "However, the catalysts suffer from deactivation and stability issues, particularly when using N2O as the oxidant."
</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Nitrous oxide (N2O) has gained attention in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) due to its potential environmental benefits and higher propylene selectivity compared to other oxidants.
2. Supported vanadium oxide catalysts are commonly utilized in the ODHP process for their efficiency and selectivity in producing propylene.
3. The primary challenges associated with using N2O as an oxidant in the ODHP process are catalyst deactivation and stability issues.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
