<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Nitrous oxide (N2O) has been considered as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) due to its potential advantages.
2. Using N2O leads to higher propylene selectivity compared to other oxidants like oxygen.
3. Different types of catalysts, including well-defined supported vanadium oxide catalysts, are used in the ODHP process.
4. Catalyst deactivation and stability are critical challenges in the practical application of N2O-ODHP.
5. Approaches to overcome these limitations could include enhancing catalyst stability, optimizing reaction conditions, or investigating alternative oxidants.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
