<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. The use of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) process has been explored. This approach demonstrates high propylene selectivity. Multiple catalysts, such as CeO2 and NiO-modified CeO2 nanocrystals, have been utilized in this process. However, key challenges related to catalyst deactivation and stability include managing temperature and ensuring effective catalyst regeneration.

2. Strategies to overcome these obstacles might include refining reaction conditions, enhancing catalyst design, and implementing efficient regeneration techniques.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
