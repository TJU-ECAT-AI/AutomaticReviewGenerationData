<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Nitrous oxide (N2O) is advantageous in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) due to higher propylene selectivity and reduced production costs.
2. Catalysts used in this process are CeO2 and NiO-modified CeO2 nanocrystals.
3. Challenges faced include catalyst deactivation and stability issues.
4. To address these challenges, developing more durable catalyst materials and optimizing reaction conditions are proposed solutions.
5. Future research might explore alternative oxidants or catalyst modifications to improve selectivity and stability.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
