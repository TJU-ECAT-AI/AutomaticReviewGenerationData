<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. This study focuses on utilizing nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP). Compared to other oxidants, N2O provides advantages in enhancing propylene selectivity. Supported chromium oxide materials are among the catalysts used in this process. A major challenge is the deactivation and instability of the catalysts. Potential research directions to overcome these limitations might involve creating more stable catalysts, refining process conditions, and investigating synergistic effects with other oxidants.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
