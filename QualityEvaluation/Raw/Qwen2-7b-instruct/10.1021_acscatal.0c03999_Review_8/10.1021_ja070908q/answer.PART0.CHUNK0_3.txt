<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The use of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) was explored in the study. Compared to other oxidants like air and O2, N2O demonstrated higher propylene selectivity. Various catalysts, specifically Ce1-xZrxO2 solid solutions, were utilized in this process. Challenges related to catalyst deactivation and stability were acknowledged. Proposed strategies to mitigate these issues included the creation of more stable catalysts and the refinement of reaction conditions.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
