1. The use of sulfur dioxide (SO2) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) allows for the conversion of propane to propylene through an endothermic reaction, where the equilibrium constant (Kp) increases with temperature. This process is advantageous for achieving high propylene selectivity, particularly when employing a reduced palladium (Pd)/alumina (Al2O3) catalyst, which demonstrates superior activity and high selectivity to propylene at elevated temperatures. However, sulfiding the Pd/Al2O3 catalyst does not enhance its activity or selectivity, and the conventional chromium (Cr)-zirconia-alumina (Cr2O3-Al2O3-K2O) catalyst shows higher conversion rates but lower selectivity to propylene.

2. The choice of oxidant in ODHP is crucial for achieving desired propylene selectivity. Nitrous oxide (N2O) could potentially offer an alternative oxidant, as it might abstract hydrogen from the alkane, resulting in hydrogen sulfide and water formation, which aligns with the observed reaction outcomes. The relative low selectivity of the Pd/Al2O3 catalyst to propylene might stem from the subsequent oxidation of propylene by SO2, leading to the formation of water and carbon dioxide, thereby impacting the efficiency of propylene production.

3. Challenges associated with the oxidative dehydrogenation process, including catalyst deactivation and stability, are significant obstacles to practical implementation. Catalyst deactivation often occurs due to the deposition of coke on the catalyst surface, reducing its activity. Strategies to address these limitations might include the development of more stable catalyst materials, the design of improved reactor systems that facilitate efficient heat and mass transfer, and the implementation of effective regeneration techniques to restore catalyst activity. Research directions could focus on understanding the underlying reaction mechanisms, optimizing process conditions, and exploring alternative oxidants or catalyst systems that minimize coke formation and enhance selectivity to propylene.

1. 在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）过程中使用二氧化硫（SO2）作为氧化剂，允许通过一个吸热反应将丙烷转化为丙烯，其中平衡常数（Kp）随温度升高而增加。该过程在使用还原态钯（Pd）/氧化铝（Al2O3）催化剂时具有优势，表现出较高的活性和高丙烯选择性，尤其是在较高温度下。然而，硫化Pd/Al2O3催化剂并未提高其活性或选择性。常规的铬（Cr）-锆-氧化铝（Cr2O3-Al2O3-K2O）脱氢催化剂显示出更高的转化率但较低的丙烯选择性。

2. ODHP中选择氧化剂对于实现所需的丙烯选择性至关重要。氮氧化物（N2O）可能提供一种替代氧化剂，因为它可能会从烷烃中提取氢，导致形成氢硫酸和水，这与观察到的反应结果相吻合。Pd/Al2O3催化剂相对较低的丙烯选择性可能源自丙烯随后被SO2氧化为水和二氧化碳，从而影响丙烯生产效率。
