<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Analyzing the use of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP), it becomes evident that N2O holds significant promise due to its unique properties, balancing efficiency and environmental impact. Various catalysts, including precious metals like Pt, Pd, and Rh, as well as mixed metal oxides and solid acids, are employed to enhance the selectivity and efficiency of the process. However, challenges in catalyst deactivation and stability are major obstacles to practical implementation. Strategies to address these limitations involve developing more robust catalyst supports, designing catalysts with enhanced stability, understanding fundamental mechanisms, integrating the process into existing industrial infrastructure, and assessing economic viability.
