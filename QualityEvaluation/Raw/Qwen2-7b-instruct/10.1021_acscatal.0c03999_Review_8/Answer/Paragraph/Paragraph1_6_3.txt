        The use of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) offers several advantages, particularly in terms of propylene selectivity, compared to other oxidants such as oxygen (O2). This is highlighted in the study by [10.1016/j.cattod.2019.06.047], where alumina-supported vanadia catalysts were found to demonstrate superior propylene yield and propylene selectivity with N2O. The study by [10.1021/jp070747c] further emphasizes the benefits of N2O, noting its ability to offer higher propylene selectivity in the ODHP reaction over various catalyst materials such as vanadia-based catalysts, VOx/SiO2, VOx/MCM-41, and VOx/MCM-48. 

        [10.1016/j.jcat.2009.06.012] provides insights into the impact of N2O on the ODHP process, detailing how it influences the reduction degree of vanadium oxide (VOx) species, leading to enhanced propylene yield. The study by [10.1021/jp410750c] explores the effects of N2O on the ODHP reaction, demonstrating that replacing O2 with N2O can lead to a 25% increase in propylene selectivity at low propane conversion (110%) for vanadia-catalyzed reactions. 

        Challenges in the N2O-ODHP process, such as catalyst deactivation and stability, are recognized across various studies. [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00836-5] suggests strategies to maintain catalyst performance and address these limitations, making N2O-ODHP a viable industrial process. 

        To overcome these limitations, future research should focus on catalyst design improvements, operational condition optimization, and the exploration of novel oxidants or additives to enhance propylene selectivity without risking overoxidation or reaction runaway, as proposed in [10.1021/acscatal.8b00650].

        In summary, the use of N2O as an oxidant in ODHP presents a promising avenue for propylene production due to its enhanced selectivity compared to traditional oxidants. However, challenges in catalyst stability and deactivation require advanced research to make N2O-ODHP a more practical industrial option.

        硝酸氧化物（N2O）在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）过程中的应用提供了一系列优势，特别是与氧气（O2）等其他氧化剂相比，在丙烯选择性方面具有更高的优势。\[10.1016/j.cattod.2019.06.047\]的研究强调了使用铝氧载体氧化铁催化剂时N2O的优越性能，显示了更高的丙烯产率和丙烯选择性。\[10.1021/jp070747c\]进一步阐述了N2O的优势，指出与其他催化剂材料如VOx/SiO2、VOx/MCM-41和VOx/MCM-48相比，在ODHP反应中使用N2O可实现更高的丙烯选择性。

        \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.06.012\]探讨了N2O对ODHP过程的影响，详细说明了它如何影响还原度VOx物种，从而提高丙烯产量。\[10.1021/jp410750c\]研究了N2O对ODHP反应的影响，表明用N2O替换O2可以导致在低丙烷转化率（110%）下，vanadia催化反应中丙烯选择性增加25%。

        在ODHP过程中，催化剂失活和稳定性是普遍存在的挑战，如\[10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00836-5\]所述。通过维持催化剂性能并解决这些问题，可以使N2O-ODHP成为可行的工业选项。

        为了克服这些限制，未来的研究应重点放在开发专门针对N2O-ODHP的更耐用的催化剂材料上，优化操作条件以最大化丙烯选择性，并调查结合N2O与其他气体或添加剂的协同效应，以进一步提高过程的效率和可持续性。此外，引入计算模型和机器学习算法可以提供对反应机制的更深入理解，并预测催化剂性能，为更有针对性的研究方向铺平道路。

        总之，N2O作为ODHP氧化剂的应用为丙烯生产提供了有前景的途径，其选择性高于传统氧化剂。然而，催化剂稳定性和失活的问题需要高级研究来使N2O-ODHP成为更实际的工业选项。
