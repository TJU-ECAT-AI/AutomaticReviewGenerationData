        The use of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) has been widely studied, showcasing its advantages in terms of propylene selectivity over other oxidants. Catalysts such as vanadia, supported boron oxide, Fe-ZSM-5, and others have been employed in this process, each offering unique properties and challenges. Critical challenges associated with catalyst deactivation and stability have been identified, particularly in the context of N2O's ability to induce catalyst reduction and slow down oxidation rates.

        Strategies to address these limitations include optimizing the catalyst composition and operating conditions, developing more robust catalyst materials, and exploring alternative oxidants or catalyst modifications to enhance selectivity and stability. For instance, the redox dynamics of N2O, its unique interaction with hydrocarbons, and its ability to selectively donate oxygen atoms without causing extensive oxidation, contribute to its superior propylene selectivity. The presence of sulfur species, such as H2S, can also influence the ODHP reaction mechanisms and pathways, offering new insights and potential avenues for developing highly selective catalysts.

        The need for advanced research is evident in overcoming catalyst deactivation and stability challenges. Future efforts should focus on understanding the precise coordination structures of catalyst species, developing stable precursors, and refining catalyst design to ensure long-term activity and selectivity. Additionally, exploring alternative oxidants or catalyst modifications could lead to more efficient and sustainable ODHP processes, making N2O-ODHP a more practical option for industrial implementation.

        In summary, while N2O presents promising advantages in propylene selectivity and reaction efficiency, the challenges in catalyst stability and deactivation require targeted research to fully harness its potential. Advanced catalyst development and optimized operational parameters are essential steps toward realizing the practicality of N2O-ODHP technology.

        硝酸二氧化物（N2O）作为氧化脱氢反应中的丙烷（ODHP）氧化剂，其在丙烯选择性方面的优势与其它氧化剂相比，已经得到了广泛研究。使用的催化剂包括氧化钒、支持硼氧化物、Fe-ZSM-5等，每种催化剂都具有独特的特性和挑战。与N2O诱导催化剂还原和减缓氧化速率相关的关键挑战已被识别。

        应对这些限制的策略包括优化催化剂组成和操作条件、开发更稳定的催化剂材料以及探索替代氧化剂或催化剂改性以提高选择性和稳定性。例如，N2O的独特反应动力学，它能选择性地提供氧原子而不引起过度氧化，有助于其在丙烯选择性方面的优越性能。硫化物物种的存在，如H2S，可以影响ODHP反应机制和途径，为开发高度选择性的催化剂提供了新的见解和潜在路径。

        显然，尽管N2O在丙烯选择性和反应效率方面展现出有前景的优势，但催化剂稳定性和降解的挑战需要有针对性的研究才能充分利用其潜力。先进的催化剂开发和优化的操作参数是实现N2O-ODHP技术实用性的关键步骤。

        总结而言，虽然N2O在丙烯选择性和反应效率方面表现出有前景的优势，但催化剂稳定性和降解的挑战需要有针对性的研究来充分利用其潜力。先进的催化剂开发和优化的操作参数是实现N2O-ODHP技术实用性的关键步骤。

        未来研究方向可能包括：
        1. **增强催化剂稳定性**：通过引入新型金属-支撑相互作用或稳定元素开发具有改进稳定性的催化剂。
        2. **优化反应条件**：研究反应温度、压力和原料组分对催化剂性能和产物选择性的影响。
        3. **探索替代氧化剂**：调查可能与N2O协同作用或提供额外益处的其他氧化剂的使用，例如提高选择性或减少环境影响。
