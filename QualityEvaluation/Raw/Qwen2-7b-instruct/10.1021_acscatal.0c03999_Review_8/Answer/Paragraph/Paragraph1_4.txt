        The utilization of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) has garnered considerable attention for its potential in enhancing propylene selectivity compared to other oxidants. This review emphasizes the advantageous role of N2O in ODHP, the diverse catalysts employed, and the critical challenges associated with catalyst deactivation and stability. The following sections delve into the specifics of N2O's unique properties, its comparative advantages, and the strategies required to address the limitations for practical implementation.

        The use of N2O in ODHP offers several advantages, primarily in propylene selectivity. For instance, studies by \[10.1002/cjce.5450480221\] and \[10.1021/jp070747c\] highlight that N2O can increase propylene selectivity by 25% under vanadia-catalyzed conditions, albeit at low propane conversion rates. This is due to N2O's slower reoxidation of reduced vanadium oxide (VOx) species, contributing to higher selectivity. Moreover, the in-situ UV/vis analysis results in \[10.1021/jp070747c\] reveal that N2O's unique properties suppress nonselective propene oxidation, further enhancing propylene selectivity.

        Various catalysts, including CeO2, CaO on Vycor chips, VOx/SiO2, VOx/MCM-41, VOx/MCM-48, and supported K2O/WO3/Al2O3, have been employed in N2O-ODHP processes. The effectiveness of these catalysts is attributed to their ability to promote the selective oxidation of propane to propylene under N2O conditions. However, challenges such as catalyst deactivation and instability remain significant issues in the practical implementation of N2O-ODHP.

        Strategies to address these limitations include optimizing catalyst composition and operating conditions, as outlined in \[10.1016/j.cattod.2019.06.047\]. This might involve developing more robust catalyst materials that can withstand the harsh conditions encountered during the ODHP process, thereby enhancing stability and preventing deactivation. Advanced research directions could focus on exploring alternative oxidants or catalyst modifications to further improve selectivity and stability, making N2O-ODHP a more viable industrial option.

        The insights from the file suggest that while N2O-ODHP shows promise in propylene selectivity, the challenges in maintaining catalyst stability and preventing deactivation necessitate continued research efforts. The development of more resilient catalyst materials, optimized operating conditions, and enhanced catalyst regeneration methods are key areas of focus. By addressing these limitations, the practical implementation of N2O-ODHP can be significantly improved, making it a more feasible option for industrial use.

        在N2O-ODHP过程中使用N2O显示出几个优势，尤其是提高丙烯选择性。例如，\[10.1002/cjce.5450480221\]和\[\[10.1021/jp070747c\]的研究表明，在范德瓦尔催化条件下，N2O可以将丙烯选择性提高25%，但仅在较低的丙烷转化率下实现。这归因于N2O对还原的Vox物种的再氧化能力较弱，从而提高了选择性。此外，\[\[10.1021/jp070747c\]中的原位UV/vis分析结果揭示了N2O抑制非选择性丙烯氧化的能力，进一步增强了丙烯选择性。

        包括CeO2、CaO在Vycor芯片上的材料、VOx/SiO2、VOx/MCM-41、VOx/MCM-48和支持K2O/WO3/Al2O3在内的多种催化剂已在N2O-ODHP过程中得到应用。这些催化剂的有效性归因于它们在N2O条件下促进丙烷选择性氧化为丙烯的能力。然而，催化剂失活和不稳定性仍然是在实践中实施N2O-ODHP的主要挑战。

        解决这些问题的策略可能包括优化催化剂组成和操作条件，如\[\[10.1016/j.cattod.2019.06.047\]所述。这可能涉及开发更耐用的催化剂材料，这些材料能够承受ODHP过程中遇到的苛刻条件，从而增强稳定性和防止失活。未来的研究方向可以专注于探索替代氧化剂或催化剂修改，以进一步提高选择性和稳定性，使N2O-ODHP成为更具可行性的工业选项。

        根据文件中的信息，虽然N2O-ODHP在丙烯选择性方面显示出前景，但维持催化剂稳定性和防止失活的挑战需要持续的研究努力。通过发展更耐用的催化剂材料、优化操作条件和改进催化剂再生方法，可以显著改善N2O-ODHP的实用性，使其成为更可行的工业选择。
