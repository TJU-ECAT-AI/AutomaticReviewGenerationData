            The use of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) offers several advantages over other oxidants, primarily in terms of propylene selectivity. Key catalysts include chromium species, supported vanadium oxide catalysts, and metal oxides like Fe-ZSM-5. Challenges in the process include catalyst deactivation and stability, which limit its practical implementation. Strategies to address these limitations involve developing more robust catalyst materials, optimizing reaction conditions, and enhancing catalyst regeneration methods. Advanced research is needed to overcome these limitations, focusing on improving catalyst stability and selectivity to make N2O-ODHP a more practical option for industrial application.
