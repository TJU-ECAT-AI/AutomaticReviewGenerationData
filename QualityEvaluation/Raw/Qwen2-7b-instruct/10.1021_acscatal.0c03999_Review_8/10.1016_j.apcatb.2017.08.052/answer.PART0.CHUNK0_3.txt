<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Nitrous oxide (N2O) is used as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) due to its potential advantages over traditional oxidants like air or oxygen.
2. In the context of ODHP, N2O is considered an alternative oxidant because it can enhance the efficiency of the process for producing propylene.
3. Various catalysts are utilized in the ODHP process to improve its performance, and different materials are being studied for their catalytic properties and stability.
4. Practical implementation of N2O-ODHP processes has been hindered by issues related to catalyst deactivation and instability.
5. Potential solutions to overcome these limitations include the creation of more durable catalysts, fine-tuning reaction conditions, and exploring alternative oxidants.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
