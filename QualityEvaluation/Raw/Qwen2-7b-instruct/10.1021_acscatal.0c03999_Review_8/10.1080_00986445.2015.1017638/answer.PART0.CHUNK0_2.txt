<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The use of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) offers several advantages compared to other oxidants, particularly in enhancing propylene selectivity. Various catalysts, such as metal oxides like Cr, Ga, V, and Fe, are utilized in this process. Nevertheless, significant challenges persist, mainly related to catalyst deactivation and stability, which hinder practical implementation. Potential strategies to overcome these limitations include developing more active and selective catalysts, optimizing reaction conditions, and exploring the use of dual catalyst systems.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
