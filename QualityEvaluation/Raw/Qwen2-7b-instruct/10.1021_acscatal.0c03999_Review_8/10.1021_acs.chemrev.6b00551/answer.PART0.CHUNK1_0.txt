<EnglishVersionAnswer>

The Dow Chemicals Company envisioned a new fluidized-bed reactor technology for the direct conversion of ethane to VCM at low temperatures. This process uses a novel catalytic material, LaOCl/LaCl3, enabling the direct introduction of chlorine into the oxychlorination reactor, eliminating the chlorination step. The process is suitable for cofeeding ethane and ethylene, which can be recycled with HCl. Under conditions simulating a complete recycle of the reaction byproducts in a pilot plant, a VCM yield of 7981% and a productivity of 0.0500.055 g(VCM) h1 gcat1 were achieved.

Due to the exothermic nature of the process, fluidized-bed reactor technology was developed, enabling isothermal operation at a lower temperature. However, pilot plant reactor system tests showed inadequate gas-solid contact for some catalysts. Fluidization engineering, concerning reactor design and catalyst preparation, still poses a significant technical challenge for commercial-scale implementation of this process.
