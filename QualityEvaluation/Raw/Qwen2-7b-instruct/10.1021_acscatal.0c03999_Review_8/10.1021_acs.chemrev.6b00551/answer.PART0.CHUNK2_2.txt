The critical challenges associated with catalyst deactivation and stability in N2O-ODHP primarily stem from the formation of unwanted byproducts, such as carbon oxides and coke, which can accumulate on the catalyst surface over time, leading to a decline in activity and selectivity. Additionally, the reaction conditions, including the high temperature and the presence of nitrogen oxides, can lead to the formation of nitrogen compounds that can poison the catalyst.

Strategies to address these limitations and make N2O-ODHP a more feasible option for practical implementation include:
