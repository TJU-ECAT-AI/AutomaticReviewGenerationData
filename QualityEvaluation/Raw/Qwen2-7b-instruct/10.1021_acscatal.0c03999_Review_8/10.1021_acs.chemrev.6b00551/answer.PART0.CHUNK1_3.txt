<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1. Nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) has been considered due to its potential for higher propylene selectivity compared to other oxidants. Various catalysts such as noble metals (Pt, Pd, Rh) and transition metals (Ni, Co, Fe) have been used in this process. Challenges include catalyst deactivation and stability, which can be addressed through developing more durable catalysts, optimizing reaction conditions, and innovative reactor designs. Research directions aim to explore catalyst synergies, understand reaction mechanisms, and enhance deactivation understanding to improve ODHP efficiency and sustainability.

</EnglishVersionAnswer>
