<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Nitrous oxide (N2O) is considered as an alternative oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) due to its advantages in enhancing propylene selectivity compared to traditional oxidants like air or oxygen. 

2. Different types of catalysts, including noble metals such as platinum, palladium, and rhodium, and transition metal oxides like ceria (CeO2) and lanthanum oxide (LaOCl), have been utilized in the N2O-ODHP process.

3. A significant challenge in the N2O-ODHP process is the deactivation and instability of the catalysts. Factors such as high reaction temperatures, corrosive conditions, and the production of byproducts can cause catalyst degradation over time.

4. Potential solutions to overcome these limitations include the development of more durable catalyst formulations, the fine-tuning of reaction conditions, and the introduction of advanced catalyst regeneration methods. Future research could explore new catalyst materials that offer improved stability and selectivity, as well as the use of protective layers to shield catalysts from corrosive substances.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
