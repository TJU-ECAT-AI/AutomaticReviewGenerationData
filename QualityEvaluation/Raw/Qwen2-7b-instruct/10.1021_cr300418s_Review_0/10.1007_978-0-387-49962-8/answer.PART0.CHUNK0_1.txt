Substitutional cation doping in metal oxide catalysts modifies surface chemistry by altering the electronic structure and disrupting chemical bonding. This leads to enhancements in catalytic activity by creating more active sites and modifying the reactivity pathways. The resulting implications include improved efficiency and selectivity in industrially relevant reactions. The nature and location of active centers are influenced by the doping, potentially leading to localized regions of high activity. These modifications can lead to significant improvements in industrially relevant reactions, such as selective oxidation, hydrogenation, and synthesis gas production.
