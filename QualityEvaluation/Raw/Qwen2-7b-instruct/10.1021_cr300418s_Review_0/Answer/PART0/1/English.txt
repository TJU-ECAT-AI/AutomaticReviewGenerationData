Substitutional cation doping in metal oxide catalysts can modify surface chemistry and disrupt chemical bonding by altering the electronic structure of the surface. This modification can lead to the creation of new active sites, changing the nature and location of the active centers. The resulting implications for catalytic activity enhancement depend on the specific doping element and its ability to facilitate the desired chemical reactions. Potential improvements in industrially relevant reactions can be achieved by tailoring the surface chemistry to optimize the reactivity and selectivity of the catalyst. The exact nature of these improvements depends on the specific reaction conditions and the chemical properties of the dopant.
