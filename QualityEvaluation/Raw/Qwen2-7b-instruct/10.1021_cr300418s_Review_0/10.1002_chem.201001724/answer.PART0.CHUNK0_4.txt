<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1. Electron transfer plays a crucial role in determining reactivity in ultrathin oxide film catalysts. The flexibility of the oxide lattice in thin films allows for effective electron transfer, which initiates and drives catalytic processes.

2. The use of thin, well-ordered oxide films as support materials significantly impacts the complexity and reactivity of disperse metal catalysts. These films provide a platform that captures the complexity of real systems, allowing for detailed study and understanding of surface chemistry and structure-reactivity relationships.

3. The size and distribution of particles formed during wet impregnation affect the chemical reactivity of disperse metal catalysts by influencing factors such as particle size, surface area, and distribution. These characteristics determine the efficiency and selectivity of the catalyst.

4. Ultrathin oxide films on metal substrates contribute to the development of new catalytic concepts by offering a platform to explore the underlying principles of electron transfer and catalysis. This understanding enables the design of catalysts with tailored properties for specific reactions.

5. The flexibility of ultrathin oxide films, compared to bulk materials, allows for efficient electron transfer, which is essential for catalytic activity. This property opens new avenues for catalyst design, focusing on controlling electron transfer for specific molecules and inducing desired reactions.
