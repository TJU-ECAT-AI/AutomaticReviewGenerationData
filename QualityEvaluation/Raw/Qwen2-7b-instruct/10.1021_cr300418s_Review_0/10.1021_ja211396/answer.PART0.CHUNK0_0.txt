<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. The introduction of Mo dopants in CaO films through Auger spectroscopy confirms the presence of characteristic Mo transitions at 200 eV kinetic energy, indicating the successful doping process.
2. By inserting 4% of Mo into the CaO matrix, the particle shapes transform from three-dimensional to two-dimensional.
3. The addition of small amounts of Li to the Mo-doped CaO system significantly modifies the observed growth behavior.
4. The observed changes in the Au particle geometry on doped CaO films can be attributed to charge-transfer processes between the dopants and the ad-metal, influencing the surface chemistry and disrupting chemical bonding.
5. This study reveals that modifying the shape of metal ad-particles can be achieved through doping the oxide support, providing insights into the potential for enhancing catalytic activity and improving industrially relevant reactions.
