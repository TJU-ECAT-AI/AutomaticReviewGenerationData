<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The multidimensional nature of the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reaction mechanism over boron-based catalysts involves both surface and gas-phase radical reactions. This complexity enhances our understanding of the entire reaction network, highlighting the intricate interplay between surface and gas-phase processes. The synergistic effects of these mechanisms play a crucial role in optimizing catalyst performance, as they affect the efficiency and selectivity of the reaction. By considering the multidimensional aspects of the reaction, researchers can better design catalysts that exhibit superior performance and enhanced selectivity towards desired products.
