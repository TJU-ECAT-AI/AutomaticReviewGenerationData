                <p>The multidimensional nature of the oxidative dehydrogenation (ODHP) reaction mechanism over B-based catalysts, which includes both surface and gas-phase radical reactions, significantly contributes to our understanding of the overall reaction network. These mechanisms emphasize the importance of considering both surface chemistry and gas-phase dynamics in the catalytic system. The synergistic effects between surface reactions on boron nitride and gas-phase reactions play a crucial role in achieving high activity and selectivity during the ODHP process.</p>
                <p>This understanding enables insights into how different reaction pathways interact, leading to enhanced catalyst performance and facilitating design optimizations. The key aspects include:</p>
                <ul>
                    <li>The comprehensive approach allows for a more detailed understanding of the reaction network, integrating both surface and gas-phase reactions.</li>
                    <li>The synergistic effects of surface and gas-phase reactions have profound implications for catalyst performance and design optimization, leading to enhanced selectivity and activity.</li>
                    <li>The multidimensional nature of the reaction mechanism over B-based catalysts contributes to understanding the overall reaction network by highlighting the role of surface and gas-phase reactions in influencing the probability of cracking and selectivity.</li>
                    <li>Optimizing catalyst performance and design requires a thorough understanding of how surface and gas-phase reactions interact and influence each other.</li>
                </ul>

                <p>From the spatial dimension of reactant molecular motion, the reaction mechanism is divided into two parts: surface reaction mechanism and gas radical reaction mechanism. Mechanism studies about the hexagonal BN are explored initially. The radical reaction in the 3-D gas phase plays an important role in the selectivity of the ODHP reaction. On the basis of the research summary, reaction networks are plotted to correlate the surface and gas-phase radical reactions involved in the ODHP reactions, as shown in Figure 9. For different catalyst systems, the final catalytic site is considered as the tricoordination BOx(OH)3x species, though the initial B species before the ODHP reaction varies. The dehydrogenation of propane mainly occurs via the abstraction of hydrogen from propane by the reactive species of BO· and ·OOH radicals. The surface reaction rate affects the gas-phase free radical processes, and the gas-phase radical reaction also impacts the surface reaction. The mixed heterogeneous-homogeneous ODH mechanism proposes that the boron oxide/hydroxide phase on the surface of the catalyst acts as an initiator to form alkyl radicals, which then react in the gas phase. Achieving the synergy of the surface reaction and gas radical reaction is of considerable significance to enhance the understanding of the ODHP reaction mechanism and design a more effective B-based catalytic system.</p>

                    <li>ODHP反应机制在硼基催化剂上，结合表面和气体相自由基反应，显著地增强了我们对整体反应网络的理解。</li>
