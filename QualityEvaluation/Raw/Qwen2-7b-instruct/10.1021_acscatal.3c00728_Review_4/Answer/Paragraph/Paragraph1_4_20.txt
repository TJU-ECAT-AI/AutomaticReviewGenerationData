            <introduction>
                    The multidimensional nature of the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reaction mechanism over boron-based catalysts, encompassing both surface and gas-phase radical reactions, significantly contributes to our understanding of the overall reaction network. This understanding highlights the importance of considering both surface chemistry and gas-phase dynamics in the catalytic system, particularly in the context of achieving high activity and high propene selectivity during the ODHP process. The synergistic effects between surface and gas radical reactions play a crucial role in optimizing catalyst performance and design, leading to enhanced selectivity and activity. 

                    By integrating the surface reaction mechanism with the gas radical reaction mechanism, researchers gain insights into the complex interplay between surface and gas-phase processes, which govern the selective formation of propene and other products. This comprehensive view elucidates the pathways through which alkanes are dehydrogenated, providing a foundation for predicting and controlling product distribution. The interdependence of surface and gas-phase reactions underscores the importance of coupling these processes efficiently to achieve high activity and selectivity in the ODHP reaction. Key factors affecting the surface and radical reactions include reaction conditions, catalyst bed configuration, and the balance between surface and gas-phase reactions. Optimizing catalyst performance and design requires a thorough understanding of how these two-dimensional processes interact and influence each other.

                    The ODHP reaction mechanism over boron-based catalysts also contributes to understanding the role of surface and gas-phase reactions in influencing the probability of cracking, particularly in the context of improving propylene production in fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) units. Parameters such as site density and temperature have a significant impact on the cracking probability, and the very high reactivity of olefins with respect to corresponding paraffins under realistic FCC cracking conditions has been demonstrated. Moreover, the reactivity of olefins with six or fewer carbon atoms is considerably lower than that of longer chain olefins, which affects the overall reaction network.

                    The ODHP process can be optimized by tuning the olefin/paraffin ratio through the acidity of the zeolite component, offering flexibility in product customization. The use of a partially reduced oxide surface for carbon monoxide (CO) activation, combined with the confined acidic pores of zeolites for carbon-carbon (CC) coupling, enables a flexible approach to adjusting CO conversion and olefin selectivity simultaneously. This opens new avenues for the development of syngas-to-olefin technologies, potentially enabling the efficient utilization of coal- and biomass-derived syngas with varying hydrogen-to-carbon (H2/CO) ratios.

                    The ODHP catalyst's high selectivity and stability, as demonstrated in prolonged operation tests, showcase its potential for industrial applications. The ability of the catalyst to maintain high selectivity for propylene (C2) and low methane content (CH4) even under prolonged operation highlights its robustness and efficiency. The performance of the catalyst in converting ketene directly to light olefins through the mesoporous silica-alumina phosphorus oxide (MSAPO) component further underscores the importance of the catalyst's multifunctionality and the interplay between its components in achieving optimal reaction outcomes.

            <introduction>
                    氧化脱氢化反应（ODHP）机制在硼基催化剂上的多维性质，既包括表面和气体相自由基反应，对整体反应网络的理解做出了重大贡献。这一理解强调了考虑催化系统中表面化学和气体相动力学的重要性，特别是在实现高活性和高丙烯选择性期间。表面和气体自由基反应之间的协同效应在优化催化剂性能和设计方面起着关键作用，从而提高选择性和活性。

                    通过将表面反应机制与气体自由基反应机制整合在一起，研究人员获得了关于表面和气体相过程之间复杂相互作用的见解，这些过程决定了丙烯和其他产物的选择性形成。这种全面的观点揭示了烷烃脱氢化途径，为预测和控制产品分布提供了基础。表面和气体相反应之间的相互依存关系突出了高效耦合这两个过程以实现ODHP反应中的高活性和选择性的必要性。

                    ODHP机制还帮助理解了表面和气体相反应在影响裂解概率方面的角色，特别是在提高流化床催化裂化（FCC）单元中丙烯产量时。参数如位点密度和温度对裂解概率有显著影响，并且已经证明了烯烃相对于相应烷烃的极高反应性。此外，六碳原子或更少的烯烃的反应性显著低于更长链烯烃，这影响了整体反应网络。

                    通过调整酸性沸石成分来调节烯烃/烷烃比率，可以优化ODHP过程，提供产品定制的灵活性。利用部分还原的氧化物表面促进一氧化碳（CO）活化，结合沸石的受限酸性孔隙进行碳碳（CC）耦合，允许同时调整CO转化率和烯烃选择性。这为发展合成气到烯烃技术开辟了新途径，可能使煤炭和生物质来源的合成气利用更加有效。

                    在长时间运行测试中，ODHP催化剂的高选择性和稳定性展示了其工业应用的潜力。即使在长时间运行下仍能保持高丙烯（C2）选择性和低甲烷含量（CH4），凸显了其稳定性和效率。催化剂通过将酮直接转化为轻烯烃的能力进一步强调了其多功能性和组件之间的相互作用对于实现最佳反应结果的重要性。

                    总之，ODHP机制在硼基催化剂上的多维性质，不仅对整体反应网络的理解做出了重大贡献，而且强调了考虑催化系统中表面化学和气体相动力学的重要性，特别是在优化催化剂性能和设计方面，以提高选择性和活性。ODHP机制在理解裂解概率的作用方面也有所贡献，特别是在提高FCC单元中丙烯产量时。通过调整参数如位点密度和温度，以及优化烯烃/烷烃比率，可以定制ODHP过程，从而增强选择性和稳定性，展示出其在工业应用中的潜力。

                    ODHP催化剂的多功能性和组件之间的相互作用，例如部分还原的氧化物表面促进CO活化和沸石受限酸性孔隙进行CC耦合，提供了一个灵活的方法来同时调整CO转化率和烯烃选择性。这使得能够利用来自煤炭和生物质的合成气源，其氢气与碳（H2/CO）比不同。
            </introduction>

            <conclusion>
                    总结而言，ODHP机制在硼基催化剂上的多维性质，对整体反应网络的理解做出了重大贡献，强调了考虑催化系统中表面化学和气体相动力学的重要性，特别是为了提高选择性和活性而优化催化剂性能和设计。表面和气体自由基反应之间的协同效应在实现ODHP过程中的高活性和高丙烯选择性方面发挥着关键作用，为预测和控制产品分布提供了基础。

                    ODHP机制还帮助理解了表面和气体相反应在影响裂解概率方面的作用，特别是在提高FCC单元中丙烯产量时。通过调整参数如位点密度和温度，以及优化烯烃/烷烃比率，可以定制ODHP过程，从而增强选择性和稳定性，展示了其在工业应用中的潜力。

                    ODHP催化剂的多功能性和组件之间的相互作用，例如部分还原的氧化物表面促进CO活化和沸石受限酸性孔隙进行CC耦合，提供了一个灵活的方法来同时调整CO转化率和烯烃选择性。这使得能够高效利用煤炭和生物质来源的合成气，其H2/CO比不同。

                    综上所述，ODHP机制在硼基催化剂上的多维性质代表了催化领域的一个重大进展，为开发更高效和选择性的催化剂用于从合成气生产轻烯烃提供了有前景的途径。
