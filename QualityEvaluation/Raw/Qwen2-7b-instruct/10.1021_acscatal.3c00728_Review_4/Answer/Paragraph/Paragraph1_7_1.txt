        The multidimensional nature of the oxidative dehydrogenation (ODHP) reaction mechanism over boron-based catalysts, which includes both surface and gas-phase radical reactions, significantly contributes to our understanding of the overall reaction network. This understanding emphasizes the importance of considering both the surface chemistry and gas-phase dynamics in the catalytic system. The synergistic effects between the surface reaction and gas radical reaction play a crucial role in achieving high activity and high propene selectivity during the ODHP process. By integrating the surface reaction mechanism with the gas radical reaction mechanism, researchers gain insights into the complex interplay between surface and gas-phase processes, which govern the selective formation of propene and other products. This comprehensive view elucidates the pathways through which alkanes are dehydrogenated, providing a foundation for predicting and controlling product distribution. The interdependence of surface and gas-phase reactions underscores the importance of coupling these processes efficiently to realize high activity and selectivity in the ODHP reaction. Key factors affecting the surface and radical reactions include reaction conditions, catalyst bed configuration, and the balance between surface and gas-phase reactions. Optimizing catalyst performance and design requires a thorough understanding of how these two-dimensional processes interact and influence each other.

        The implications of these synergistic effects are profound for catalyst performance and design optimization. By integrating both surface and gas-phase reactions, the catalyst system can effectively manage competing pathways involved in the conversion of syngas to light olefins. This leads to higher selectivity for desired products, reduced formation of unwanted byproducts like methane, and improved stability over time. The use of a partially reduced oxide surface for CO activation, combined with the zeolite's confined acidic pores for CC coupling, enables a flexible approach to adjusting CO conversion and olefin selectivity simultaneously. This opens new avenues for the development of syngas-to-olefin technologies, potentially enabling the efficient utilization of coal- and biomass-derived syngas with varying H2/CO ratios.

        The composite catalyst's high selectivity and stability, as demonstrated in the 110-hour test, showcase its potential for industrial applications. The results indicate that the catalyst can maintain high C2C4 selectivity (94%) and low methane content (2%) even under prolonged operation, highlighting its robustness and efficiency. Additionally, the catalyst's performance in converting ketene directly to light olefins through the MSAPO component further underscores the importance of the catalyst's multifunctionality and the interplay between its components in achieving optimal reaction outcomes.

        To advance the field further, future research should focus on:
        - Exploring emerging fields and innovative methodologies based on the multidimensional nature of the ODHP reaction mechanism.
        - Investigating the effects of various parameters on catalyst performance, including reaction conditions, catalyst composition, and structure.
        - Designing catalysts that can efficiently convert syngas to light olefins under a wider range of feedstock compositions and operating conditions.
        - Understanding and optimizing the interactions between surface and gas-phase reactions to enhance selectivity and activity.
        - Developing new materials and catalyst designs that can improve the efficiency and selectivity of ODHP reactions for the production of valuable chemicals and fuels.
        - Integrating the knowledge gained from the multidimensional ODHP reaction mechanism into practical applications for the chemical industry, aiming to reduce energy consumption, increase yields, and minimize environmental impact.

        这些协同效应对于催化剂性能和设计优化具有深远的影响。通过整合表面和气相反应，催化剂系统可以有效地管理合成气向轻烯烃的转化过程中的竞争路径。这导致了对目标产品更高的选择性，减少了甲烷等副产品的生成，并提高了催化剂的稳定性。使用部分还原的氧化物表面进行CO活化，结合分子筛的受限酸性孔隙进行CC耦合，使调整CO转化率和烯烃选择性成为可能。这为开发合成气到烯烃技术开辟了新途径，有可能实现煤和生物质衍生合成气在不同H2/CO比例下的高效利用。

        复合催化剂的高选择性和稳定性，在110小时的测试中得到了证明，展示了其在工业应用的潜力。结果表明，即使在长时间操作下，催化剂仍能保持高达94%的C2C4选择性和2%的甲烷含量，凸显了其优异的稳定性和效率。此外，催化剂将酮烯直接转化为轻烯烃的能力进一步强调了催化剂组件之间的相互作用对于实现最佳反应结果的重要性。

        - 设计能够高效地将合成气转化为轻烯烃的催化剂，适用于更广泛的原料组成和操作条件。
