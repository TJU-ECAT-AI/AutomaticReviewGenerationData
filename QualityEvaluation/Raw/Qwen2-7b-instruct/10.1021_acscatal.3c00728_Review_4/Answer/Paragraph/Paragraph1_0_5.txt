                <h2>Integrative Understanding of the Multidimensional Nature of the ODHP Reaction Mechanism in B-based Catalysts</h2>
                <p>The multidimensional nature of the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reaction mechanism over B-based catalysts, involving both surface and gas-phase radical reactions, significantly contributes to our understanding of the overall reaction network. This understanding emphasizes the importance of considering both the surface chemistry and gas-phase dynamics in the catalytic system. The synergistic effects between the surface reaction and gas radical reaction play a crucial role in achieving high activity and high propene selectivity during the ODHP process. By integrating the surface reaction mechanism with the gas radical reaction mechanism, researchers gain insights into the complex interplay between surface and gas-phase processes, which govern the selective formation of propene and other products.</p>
                
                <p>Key factors affecting the surface and radical reactions include reaction conditions, catalyst bed configuration, and the balance between surface and gas-phase reactions. Optimizing catalyst performance and design requires a thorough understanding of how these two-dimensional processes interact and influence each other.</p>
                
                <p>Specifically, the highest propylene selectivity and propane conversion rates are often achieved with specific B-based catalysts, such as those containing chromium or vanadium, suggesting that optimizing the structure of the catalyst surface or the state of the metal species can lead to more efficient catalysts. Correlating the catalytic properties with the state of the metal, for example, chromium or vanadium, can provide insights into how to tailor catalysts for optimal performance in the ODHP reaction.</p>
                
                <h3>Implications for Catalyst Performance and Design Optimization</h3>
                <p>The implications of these synergistic effects are profound for catalyst performance and design optimization. By integrating both surface and gas-phase reactions, the catalyst system can effectively manage competing pathways involved in the conversion of propane to propene. This leads to higher selectivity for desired products, reduced formation of unwanted byproducts like methane, and improved stability over time. The use of a partially reduced oxide surface for CO activation, combined with the confined acidic pores of zeolites for CC coupling, enables a flexible approach to adjusting CO conversion and olefin selectivity simultaneously. This opens new avenues for the development of versatile syngas-to-olefin technologies, potentially enabling the efficient utilization of coal- and biomass-derived syngas with varying H2/CO ratios.</p>
                
                <p>Furthermore, the composite catalysts that demonstrate high selectivity and stability, as demonstrated in long-term tests, showcase their potential for industrial applications. Maintaining high C2C4 selectivity and low methane content even under prolonged operation highlights the robustness and efficiency of these catalysts. The ability to convert ketene directly to light olefins through the MSAPO component underscores the importance of the catalyst's multifunctionality and the interplay between its components in achieving optimal reaction outcomes.</p>
                
                <p>Overall, the multidimensional nature of the ODHP reaction mechanism over B-based catalysts provides a comprehensive framework for understanding and optimizing the performance of these catalysts. It emphasizes the need for a holistic approach that considers both surface and gas-phase reactions, leading to the development of catalysts that can efficiently convert propane to propene with high selectivity and stability.</p>

                <h2>基于B基催化剂的ODHP反应机制的多维性：整合表面和气相自由基反应以全面理解催化性能和协同效应</h2>
                <p>氧化脱氢化丙烷（ODHP）反应机制在B基催化剂上的多维特性，包括表面和气相自由基反应，对整体反应网络的理解做出了重大贡献。这种理解强调了在催化系统中同时考虑表面化学和气体动力学的重要性。表面反应与气体自由基反应之间的协同作用对于实现ODHP过程中的高活性和高丙烯选择性至关重要。通过将表面反应机制与气体自由基反应机制相结合，研究人员获得了表面与气体相间过程之间复杂相互作用的洞察，这些过程控制着丙烯和其他产品的选择性形成。</p>
                
                <p>影响表面和自由基反应的关键因素包括反应条件、催化剂床配置以及表面与气体相之间的平衡。优化催化剂性能和设计需要深入了解这两个维度过程如何相互作用并影响彼此。</p>
                
                <p>具体而言，通过特定的B基催化剂（如含铬或钒的催化剂），往往可以实现最高丙烯选择性和丙烷转化率，这表明优化催化剂表面结构或金属物种的状态可以导致更高效的催化剂。通过将催化剂的催化性质与金属状态（例如，铬或钒）相关联，可以提供如何根据ODHP反应调整催化剂性能的见解。</p>
                
                <h3>对催化剂性能和设计优化的影响</h3>
                <p>这些协同效应的深远意义在于催化剂性能和设计优化。通过整合表面和气体相反应，催化剂系统能够有效地管理丙烷转化为丙烯时的竞争路径。这导致了所需产品的更高选择性、减少了不希望的副产品（如甲烷）的形成，并且提高了催化剂的长期稳定性。使用部分还原的氧化物表面促进CO活化，结合多孔酸性沸石中的CC耦合，为调整CO转化和烯烃选择性提供了一种灵活的方法。这为开发具有多样性的新型合成气到烯烃技术开辟了新途径，有可能高效利用煤炭和生物质衍生的合成气。</p>
                
                <p>此外，复合催化剂在长时间测试中显示出高选择性和稳定性的能力，展示了其工业应用潜力。即使在长期运行过程中也能维持高C2C4选择性和低甲烷含量，突显了这些催化剂的稳健性和效率。通过MSAPO组件直接将酮转化为轻质烯烃，强调了催化剂组分之间的相互作用对于实现最优反应结果的重要性。</p>
                
                <p>总体而言，ODHP反应机制在B基催化剂上的多维特性提供了一个全面框架，用于理解和优化这些催化剂的性能。它强调了需要采取综合方法来同时考虑表面和气体相反应，从而开发出能够高效将丙烷转化为丙烯并保持高选择性和稳定性的催化剂。</p>
