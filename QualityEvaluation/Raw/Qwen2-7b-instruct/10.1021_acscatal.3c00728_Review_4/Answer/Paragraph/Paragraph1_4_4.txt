        <p>
            The multidimensional nature of the oxidative dehydrogenation (ODHP) reaction mechanism over boron-based catalysts, encompassing both surface and gas-phase radical reactions, significantly contributes to our understanding of the overall reaction network. This understanding emphasizes the importance of considering both the surface chemistry and gas-phase dynamics in the catalytic system. The synergistic effects between the surface reaction and gas radical reaction play a crucial role in achieving high activity and high propene selectivity during the ODHP process. By integrating the surface reaction mechanism with the gas radical reaction mechanism, researchers gain insights into the complex interplay between surface and gas-phase processes, which govern the selective formation of propene and other products. This comprehensive view elucidates the pathways through which alkanes are dehydrogenated, providing a foundation for predicting and controlling product distribution. The interdependence of surface and gas-phase reactions underscores the importance of coupling these processes efficiently to realize high activity and selectivity in the ODHP reaction. Key factors affecting the surface and radical reactions include reaction conditions, catalyst bed configuration, and the balance between surface and gas-phase reactions. Optimizing catalyst performance and design requires a thorough understanding of how these two-dimensional processes interact and influence each other.
        </p>
        <p>
            The implications of these synergistic effects are profound for catalyst performance and design optimization. By integrating both surface and gas-phase reactions, the catalyst system can effectively manage competing pathways involved in the conversion of syngas to light olefins. This leads to higher selectivity for desired products, reduced formation of unwanted byproducts like methane, and improved stability over time. The use of a partially reduced oxide surface for CO activation, combined with the zeolite's confined acidic pores for CC coupling, enables a flexible approach to adjusting CO conversion and olefin selectivity simultaneously. This opens new avenues for the development of syngas-to-olefin technologies, potentially enabling the efficient utilization of coal- and biomass-derived syngas with varying H2/CO ratios.
        </p>
        <p>
            The composite catalyst's high selectivity and stability, as demonstrated in the 110-hour test, showcase its potential for industrial applications. The results indicate that the catalyst can maintain high C2C4 selectivity (94%) and low methane content (2%) even under prolonged operation, highlighting its robustness and efficiency. Additionally, the catalyst's performance in converting ketene directly to light olefins through the MSAPO component further underscores the importance of the catalyst's multifunctionality and the interplay between its components in achieving optimal reaction outcomes.
        </p>

        <p>
            本研究探讨了硼基催化剂在氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应机制中的多维特性，包括表面和气相自由基反应，对整体反应网络的理解产生了显著贡献。这种理解强调了考虑催化体系中表面化学和气相动力学的重要性。表面反应与气体自由基反应之间的协同作用在实现ODHP过程中的高活性和高丙烯选择性中扮演着关键角色。通过将表面反应机制与气体自由基反应机制相结合，研究人员能够深入了解表面与气相过程之间的复杂相互作用，这些过程指导了丙烯和其他产品的选择性形成。这种全面的观点揭示了烷烃脱氢的途径，并为预测和控制产物分布提供了基础。表面与气相反应之间的依赖关系强调了在实现ODHP反应的高活性和选择性时有效耦合这两个过程的重要性。影响表面和自由基反应的关键因素包括反应条件、催化剂床配置以及表面与气相反应之间的平衡。优化催化剂性能和设计需要深入理解这两个维度过程之间的交互作用及其对彼此的影响。
        </p>
        <p>
            这些协同效应对于催化剂性能和设计优化具有深远的影响。通过整合表面和气相反应，催化剂系统可以有效地管理合成气向轻烯烃转化过程中的竞争路径。这导致了目标产品选择性的提高，减少了甲烷等副产品的形成，并且提高了催化剂的长期稳定性。通过部分还原氧化物表面促进CO活化，结合具有限制性酸孔的沸石对CC耦合的使用，可以灵活调整CO转化率和烯烃选择性。这开辟了发展合成气到烯烃技术的新途径，可能使煤炭和生物质衍生的合成气中不同H2/CO比例的有效利用成为可能。
        </p>
        <p>
            该复合催化剂在110小时测试中的高选择性和稳定性表明其在工业应用方面的潜力。结果表明，即使在长时间运行下，催化剂也能保持高达94%的C2C4选择性和仅2%的甲烷含量，这凸显了其强大的稳定性和效率。此外，催化剂通过MSAPO组件直接将酮烯转化为轻烯烃的能力进一步强调了催化剂的多功能性和其组成部分之间交互作用在实现最优反应结果中的重要性。
        </p>
