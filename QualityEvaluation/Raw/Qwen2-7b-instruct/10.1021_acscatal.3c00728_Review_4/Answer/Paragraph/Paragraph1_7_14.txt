This understanding emphasizes the importance of considering both the surface chemistry and gas-phase dynamics in the catalytic system. The synergistic effects between the surface reaction and gas radical reaction play a crucial role in achieving high activity and high propene selectivity during the ODHP process. By integrating the surface reaction mechanism with the gas radical reaction mechanism, researchers gain insights into the complex interplay between surface and gas-phase processes, which govern the selective formation of propene and other products. This comprehensive view elucidates the pathways through which alkanes are dehydrogenated, providing a foundation for predicting and controlling product distribution. The interdependence of surface and gas-phase reactions underscores the importance of coupling these processes efficiently to realize high activity and selectivity in the ODHP reaction. Key factors affecting the surface and radical reactions include reaction conditions, catalyst bed configuration, and the balance between surface and gas-phase reactions. Optimizing catalyst performance and design requires a thorough understanding of how these two-dimensional processes interact and influence each other.
                            The implications of these synergistic effects are profound for catalyst performance and design optimization. By integrating both surface and gas-phase reactions, the catalyst system can effectively manage competing pathways involved in the conversion of syngas to light olefins. This leads to higher selectivity for desired products, reduced formation of unwanted byproducts like methane, and improved stability over time. The use of a partially reduced oxide surface for CO activation, combined with the zeolite's confined acidic pores for CC coupling, enables a flexible approach to adjusting CO conversion and olefin selectivity simultaneously. This opens new avenues for the development of syngas-to-olefin technologies, potentially enabling the efficient utilization of coal- and biomass-derived syngas with varying H2/CO ratios. The composite catalyst's high selectivity and stability, as demonstrated in the 110-hour test, showcase its potential for industrial applications.                             B基催化剂上ODHP反应机制的多维特性，包括表面和气相自由基反应，显著增强了我们对整体反应网络的理解。这种理解强调了在催化系统中考虑表面化学和气体相动力学的重要性。表面反应与气相自由基反应之间的协同作用在实现ODHP过程中的高活性和高丙烯选择性中发挥着关键作用。通过将表面反应机制与气相自由基反应机制集成，研究人员获得了关于表面与气相过程之间复杂相互作用的洞察，这些过程控制着丙烯和其他产物的选择性形成。全面的观点揭示了烷烃脱氢的途径，为预测和控制产品分布提供了基础。表面与气相反应之间的依赖性强调了高效耦合这些过程以实现ODHP反应中的高活性和选择性的必要性。影响表面和自由基反应的因素包括反应条件、催化剂床配置以及表面与气相反应之间的平衡。优化催化剂性能和设计需要彻底理解这两个维度过程如何交互和影响彼此。
