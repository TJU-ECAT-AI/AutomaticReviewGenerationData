                    Detailed analysis established from the study of reference [10.1126/sciadv.aav8063]. Synthesized comprehension stemming from references [10.1126/sciadv.aav8063], [10.1021/acscatal.9b02284], and [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].
                    The multidimensional nature of the oxidative dehydrogenation (ODHP) reaction mechanism over boron-based catalysts, incorporating both surface and gas-phase radical reactions, contributes significantly to our understanding of the overall reaction network. This understanding emphasizes the importance of considering both the surface chemistry and gas-phase dynamics in the catalytic system. The synergistic effects between the surface reaction and gas radical reaction play a crucial role in achieving high activity and high propene selectivity during the ODHP process. By integrating the surface reaction mechanism with the gas radical reaction mechanism, researchers gain insights into the complex interplay between surface and gas-phase processes, which govern the selective formation of propene and other products. This comprehensive view elucidates the pathways through which alkanes are dehydrogenated, providing a foundation for predicting and controlling product distribution. The interdependence of surface and gas-phase reactions underscores the importance of coupling these processes efficiently to realize high activity and selectivity in the ODHP reaction. Key factors affecting the surface and radical reactions include reaction conditions, catalyst bed configuration, and the balance between surface and gas-phase reactions. Optimizing catalyst performance and design requires a thorough understanding of how these two-dimensional processes interact and influence each other.

                    Increasing process severity can significantly increase propylene production in fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) units. With this option, naphtha-range olefins may be transformed into liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) olefins, while at the same time, reducing the olefin content of FCC naphtha. Parameters such as site density or temperature have an influence on the cracking probability. The very high reactivity of olefins with respect to the corresponding paraffins under realistic FCC cracking conditions has been demonstrated. Moreover, it was observed that the reactivity of olefins with six or less carbon atoms was considerably lower than the reactivity of longer chain olefins. As shown in Figure 3, it is clear that at moderate temperature (723 and 823 K, transported bed), the hydrogen-transfer ability of the Y zeolite is the governing reaction. The major operational drawback of this operation would be the substantial increase in dry gas yield resulting from thermal cracking.

                    The process involves a bifunctional catalyst that achieves higher conversions and avoids deactivation. The partially reduced oxide surface (ZnCrOx) activates CO and H2, and carbon-carbon (CC) coupling is subsequently manipulated within the confined acidic pores of zeolites. This C2=C4= selectivity is higher than the best value reported for Fischer-Tropsch to olefins (FTTO) (61%) (4) and exceeds the maximum predicted for C2-C4 hydrocarbons according to the Aspen Statistical Fundamental (ASF) distribution in typical Fischer-Tropsch synthesis (FTS), as depicted in Figure 1B. We observed an even higher selectivity of C2=C4= (80%), with overall C2C4 selectivity reaching 94% and only 2% CH4 over the well-mixed composite catalyst (Mode 4). By forming ketene, the reaction pathway of surface polymerization of CHx is blocked, hence circumventing the ASF limits of FTS. The olefin/paraffin ratio increased with the decreasing NH3 desorption temperature and reached 4.7 for a MSAPO sample with NH3 desorption peak at 350C (Figure 3C).

                    The multiple promotional effects of vanadium oxide on boron nitride for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane suggest a bold new hypothesis that combines catalytic activity measurements with quantum chemical calculations. Understanding the unique antioxidant property of boron-based catalysts during oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes implies that the interaction between the surface reactions on boron nitride and the gas-phase reactions plays a crucial role in the efficiency and selectivity of the catalytic process. By optimizing these interactions, one can enhance the performance of the catalyst, leading to more effective propane conversion and improved product yield. The unique antioxidant property during the process is highlighted, contributing to the understanding of the overall reaction network and implications for catalyst performance and design optimization.

                    A systematic analysis was performed of the physicochemical properties of amorphous silicas (Acros, Degussa, KSKG) and chromium oxide/silica catalysts prepared from them with various chromium loadings. In the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane with carbon dioxide, the highest propylene selectivity (95%) was observed for the 5% CrOx/SiO2 (Degussa) sample, and the highest propane conversion (53%) was reported on the 5% CrOx/SiO2 (Acros) catalyst. The structure of chromium oxide particles on the support surface was studied by UV-Vis spectroscopy, revealing correlations between the catalytic properties and the state of chromium.

                    [10.1126/sciadv.aav8063]中的深入研究建立了关于氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应机制在硼基催化剂上的多维性质的理解，包括表面和气体相自由基反应，这显著增加了对整体反应网络的了解。这种理解强调了在催化系统中考虑表面化学和气相动力学的重要性。表面反应与气体自由基反应之间的协同效应在实现高活性和高丙烯选择性方面起着关键作用。通过将表面反应机制与气体自由基反应机制相结合，研究人员获得了表面对气相过程之间复杂交互作用的洞察，这些过程控制着丙烯和其他产品的选择性形成。全面的观点阐明了烷烃脱氢化途径，为预测和控制产品分布提供了基础。表面和气相反应之间的相互依赖性强调了有效耦合这些过程以实现高活性和选择性的ODHP反应的重要性。

                    提高工艺强度可以显著增加流化床催化裂化（FCC）单元中的丙烯产量。通过这种方法，芳烃范围内的烯烃可能转化为液化石油气（LPG）烯烃，同时减少FCC石脑油中的烯烃含量。温度、床层配置等参数会影响裂解概率。在现实的FCC裂解条件下，烯烃与对应的烷烃的高反应性已被证实。此外，观察到六碳或更少碳原子的烯烃的反应性显著低于较长链烯烃的反应性。如图3所示，在适度温度（723和823 K，运输床）下，Y沸石的氢转移能力是主导反应。该操作的主要操作难题是由于热裂解导致的干气产量显著增加。

                    过程涉及一个双功能催化剂，实现了更高的转化率并避免了失活。部分还原的氧化物表面（ZnCrOx）激活CO和H2，并在限制酸性孔的沸石内进行碳-碳（CC）耦合。这种C2=C4=选择性高于Fischer-Tropsch到烯烃（FTTO）的最佳值（61%）（4），超过典型Fischer-Tropsch合成（FTS）中C2-C4烃根据Aspen统计基本分布的最大预测值，如图1B所示。我们观察到C2=C4=的更高选择性（80%），整体C2C4选择性达到94%，仅2%CH4存在于混合复合催化剂（模式4）中。通过形成酮烯，表面聚合物化过程中的CHx路径被阻断，从而绕过了FTS的ASF限制。随着NH3脱附温度的降低，烯烃/烷烃比增加至4.7，对于具有NH3脱附峰值的MSAPO样品为350C（图3C）。

                    硼氮化合物对丙烷的氧化脱氢过程中的多重促进效果，结合了催化活性测量与量子化学计算，提出了一个大胆的新假设。理解硼基催化剂在烷烃氧化脱氢过程中的独特抗氧化性能表明，表面反应在硼氮上与气体相反应之间的相互作用在催化效率和选择性方面起着关键作用。通过优化这些相互作用，可以提高催化剂的性能，从而实现更有效的丙烷转换和提高产品产率。在过程中突出的独特抗氧化性能，对整体反应网络的理解和催化剂性能与设计优化的影响具有重要意义。

                    对无定形二氧化硅（Acros、Degussa、KSKG）和不同铬负载的铬氧化物/二氧化硅催化剂的物理化学性质进行了系统分析。在丙烷与二氧化碳的氧化脱氢过程中，5% CrOx/SiO2（Degussa）样品显示出95%的最高丙烯选择性，而5% CrOx/SiO2（Acros）催化剂则报告了53%的最高丙烷转化率。通过UV-Vis光谱研究了支持表面的铬氧化物颗粒结构，揭示了催化性质与铬状态之间的相关性。
