Answer: Bimetallic catalysts have undergone substantial evolution since their commercial introduction, thanks to advancements in materials science that allow for the fine-tuning of heterometallic nanoparticle characteristics. Their distinctive electronic and chemical attributes are instrumental in narrowing the 'materials gap' and 'pressure gap' between theoretical models and industrially applicable supported catalysts, both in foundational research and real-world applications.
