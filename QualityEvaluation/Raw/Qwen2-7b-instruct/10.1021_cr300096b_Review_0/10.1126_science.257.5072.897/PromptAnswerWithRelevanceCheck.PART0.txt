The evolution of bimetallic catalysts in heterogeneous catalysis since their commercial introduction has been significant, particularly in addressing the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts. Their unique electronic and chemical properties enable them to significantly alter the chemical properties of metals and improve catalytic efficiency. Specifically, the formation of a surface metal-metal bond facilitates the transfer of electron density from one metal to another, with the electron density flowing towards the metal with a larger fraction of empty states in its valence band. This behavior contrasts with bulk alloys, where the nature of the metal-metal bond is more influenced by the structural geometry of the system. By studying the electronic and chemical properties of these bimetallic systems, researchers can better understand and optimize their performance in various applications, effectively bridging the aforementioned gaps.

Charge transfer is a critical component in these bonds, especially when involving dissimilar elements.
