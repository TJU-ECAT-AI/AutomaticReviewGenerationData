How have bimetallic catalysts evolved in heterogeneous catalysis since their commercial introduction, and how do their unique electronic and chemical properties contribute to bridging the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts in both fundamental research and practical applications?

The evolution of bimetallic catalysts has been driven by advancements in materials science and understanding of their unique electronic and chemical properties. These properties contribute to bridging the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts. In both fundamental research and practical applications, bimetallic catalysts have proven to offer superior performance compared to their monometallic counterparts. This is due to the synergistic effects of the two metals in the bimetallic system, which can lead to improved stability, selectivity, and activity. The electronic and chemical properties of bimetallic catalysts allow for a better match between the catalyst and the reaction conditions, leading to enhanced catalytic performance. The ability of bimetallic catalysts to modulate the adsorption and activation of reactants through the interaction between the two metals is a key factor in their effectiveness.
