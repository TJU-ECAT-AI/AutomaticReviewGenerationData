<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Bimetallic catalysts have evolved significantly since their commercial introduction, effectively bridging the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts. Their unique electronic and chemical properties play a crucial role in this evolution, enabling more efficient and selective catalytic processes. These catalysts have been pivotal in understanding complex metal interactions, leading to the development of highly active and stable catalysts for various industrial applications. They offer the potential to overcome limitations associated with traditional homogeneous and heterogeneous catalysts, enhancing the performance of chemical reactions across multiple industries. From model surfaces to industrially relevant supported catalysts, bimetallic systems demonstrate their versatility and adaptability, contributing significantly to both fundamental research and practical applications.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
