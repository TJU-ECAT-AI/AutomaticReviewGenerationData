        The evolution of bimetallic catalysts in heterogeneous catalysis since their commercial introduction has significantly advanced our understanding of their unique electronic and chemical properties, which effectively bridge the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts. This evolution is pivotal for both fundamental research and practical applications. Studies such as those by Hammer et al. (DOI: 10.1103/physrevlett.76.2141) have illuminated the importance of modifications to chemisorption and catalytic properties, correlating these changes with alterations in the structural and electronic properties of bimetallic systems. Representative studies, like those by Yu et al. (DOI: 10.1021/ja208786f), have explored the use of glycolaldehyde as a probe molecule for biomass derivatives, focusing on reactions involving monolayer Ni surfaces.

        Near-surface alloys (NSAs), defined as alloys with a solute metal present near the surface of a host metal in concentrations different from the bulk, have emerged as a promising area of research (DOI: 10.1038/nmat1223). NSAs, when subjected to density functional theory calculations, can introduce new classes of alloys that exhibit superior catalytic behavior for hydrogen-related reactions. These alloys bind atomic hydrogen (H) as weakly as noble metals (Cu, Au) while dissociating H2 more easily, making them potential low-temperature, highly selective catalysts for pharmaceutical production and robust fuel-cell anodes.

        The work by Chen et al. (DOI: 10.1016/s0039-6028(99)00570-1) on morphologically unstable bimetallic surfaces highlights the significance of studying faceting of metals induced by ultrathin metallic films. This research has contributed to the foundational understanding of the structural and electronic properties of bimetallic systems.

        The evolution of bimetallic catalysts has been driven by their unique electronic and chemical properties, which bridge the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between theoretical model surfaces and real-world industrial catalysts (DOI: 10.1021/ar50109a003). For instance, near-monodisperse NiCu bimetallic nanocrystals (DOI: 10.1021/jp805788x) have been developed, offering enhanced catalytic activity for hydrogen generation through their controlled synthesis and variable compositions.

        The synthesis of a novel AuAg alloy catalyst (DOI: 10.1021/jp051530q) supported on mesoporous aluminosilicate AuAg@MCM, which is highly active for low-temperature CO oxidation, demonstrates the impact of bimetallic catalysts in practical applications. The activity of this catalyst is significantly influenced by hydrogen pretreatment conditions, with the AuAg alloy undergoing phase segregation during calcination and restoration via reduction treatment. DFT calculations reveal a stronger synergistic effect in the coadsorption of CO and O2 on neighboring sites on the AuAg nanoparticle compared to either Au or Ag individually, explaining the observed catalytic synergetic effect.

        The selective hydrogenation of the CO bond in acrolein through the architecture of bimetallic surface structures showcases the significant evolution of bimetallic catalysts in heterogeneous catalysis, primarily due to their unique electronic and chemical properties (DOI: 10.1021/ja070264). These advancements have bridged the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts, contributing to advancements in both fundamental research and practical applications.

        In summary, the evolution of bimetallic catalysts has been profoundly influenced by their distinctive electronic and chemical properties, enabling them to effectively address the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" in heterogeneous catalysis. These catalysts offer substantial value in both basic research and practical applications, particularly in the areas of pharmaceutical production, fuel cell technology, and energy production and storage.

        自商用化以来，双金属催化剂在异相催化领域的进化显著推进了我们对它们独特电子和化学性质的理解，这些特性有效地解决了“材料间隙”和“压力间隙”之间的鸿沟，即模型表面与工业相关支持催化剂之间。这一进化对于基础研究和实际应用至关重要。Hammer等人的研究（DOI：10.1103/physrevlett.76.2141）揭示了修改化学吸附和催化性质与双金属系统结构和电子性质变化之间的关系的重要性。

        近表面合金（NSAs），定义为溶质金属在主金属表面附近以不同浓度存在的合金，是一个有前景的研究领域（DOI：10.1038/nmat1223）。通过密度泛函理论计算，这些合金可以引入一类新型的、对氢相关反应表现出优越催化行为的合金。这些合金将氢原子（H）结合得如同贵金属（Cu，Au）一样弱，同时更易于分解H2，使其成为潜在的低温度、高度选择性的催化剂，用于制药生产以及坚固的燃料电池阳极。

        陈等人（DOI：10.1016/s0039-6028(99)00570-1）对形态不稳定的双金属表面的研究强调了由超薄金属薄膜诱导的金属表面刻面化的重要性。这项研究为双金属系统的结构和电子性质的基础理解做出了贡献。

        双金属催化剂的进化是由其独特的电子和化学性质驱动的，这些性质解决了理论模型表面与现实世界工业催化剂之间的“材料间隙”和“压力间隙”（DOI：10.1021/ar50109a003）。例如，近单分散NiCu双金属纳米晶（DOI：10.1021/jp805788x）的开发，通过其可控合成和可变组成，提供了氢生成的增强催化活性。

        在Mesoporous Aluminosilicate AuAg@MCM上合成的新型AuAg合金催化剂（DOI：10.1021/jp051530q），其活性非常依赖于氢预处理条件，在煅烧过程中AuAg合金经历相分离，并且在还原处理后完全去除Br-并恢复AuAg合金结构。第一性原理计算显示，AuAg纳米粒子上CO和O2的协同吸附在相邻位点比仅在Au或Ag上更强，解释了观察到的协同催化效应。

        通过双金属表面结构中CO键的选择性氢化展示了双金属催化剂在异相催化领域的显著进化，主要归功于它们独特的电子和化学性质（DOI：10.1021/ja070264）。这些进步已经解决了“材料间隙”和“压力间隙”，为模型表面与工业相关支持催化剂之间架起了桥梁，推动了基础研究和实际应用的发展。

        总之，双金属催化剂的进化受到了其独特电子和化学性质的深远影响，使它们能够有效地解决异相催化中的“材料间隙”和“压力间隙”。这些催化剂在基础研究和实际应用中提供了巨大的价值，特别是在制药生产、燃料电池技术以及能源生产和存储领域。
