        The evolution of bimetallic catalysts in heterogeneous catalysis since their commercial introduction has been marked by significant advancements in understanding their unique electronic and chemical properties. These properties have enabled researchers to bridge the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts. Many bimetallic catalysts, such as those featuring near-surface alloys (NSAs), have evolved to confer novel catalytic properties to surfaces, including the ability to bind atomic hydrogen (H) as weakly as noble metals while dissociating H2 more easily. This unique set of properties allows these alloys to serve as low-temperature, highly selective catalysts for pharmaceutical production and robust fuel-cell anodes. The research focuses on modifying the chemisorption and catalytic properties of the overlayers, which has led to distinct structural and electronic characteristics in bimetallic systems that contribute to their enhanced performance in both fundamental research and practical applications. For instance, the synthesis of near-monodisperse NiCu bimetallic nanocrystals has advanced catalytic activity for hydrogen generation, while the AuAg alloy catalyst supported on mesoporous aluminosilicate AuAg@MCM, which is very active for low-temperature CO oxidation, demonstrates the impact of bimetallic catalysts in practical applications. The strong synergism in the coadsorption of CO and O2 on the AuAg nanoparticle can explain the observed synergetic effect in catalysis, highlighting the importance of bimetallic catalysts in achieving high catalytic efficiency. The evolution of bimetallic catalysts has been significantly influenced by their distinctive electronic and chemical properties, effectively addressing the 'materials gap' and 'pressure gap' between theoretical model surfaces and real-world industrial catalysts. By altering surface reactivity and boosting catalytic performance, bimetallic catalysts offer substantial value in both basic research and practical applications. The advancements in bimetallic catalysts have been crucial for both fundamental research and practical applications, as they have facilitated progress in various fields, including pharmaceutical production, energy production, and storage technologies.
