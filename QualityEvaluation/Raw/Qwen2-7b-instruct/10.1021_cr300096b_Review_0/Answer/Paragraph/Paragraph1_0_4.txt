        The evolution, unique properties, and applications of bimetallic catalysts in heterogeneous catalysis have significantly advanced since their commercial introduction, serving as a bridge between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts. This evolution has been driven by their distinctive electronic and chemical properties, which enable them to effectively address the 'materials gap' and 'pressure gap' between theoretical model surfaces and real-world industrial catalysts.

        Key advancements include:
        - The utilization of near-surface alloys (NSAs) which confer novel catalytic properties by having a solute metal present near the surface of a host metal in concentrations different from the bulk. These NSAs bind atomic hydrogen (H) as weakly as noble metals (Cu, Au) while dissociating H2 more easily, making them suitable for low-temperature, highly selective catalysts for pharmaceutical production and robust fuel-cell anodes.
        - The application of density functional theory calculations to introduce new classes of alloys with superior catalytic behavior for hydrogen-related reactions, offering predictive power for catalysis.
        - The synthesis of near-monodisperse NiCu bimetallic nanocrystals, which exhibit variable compositions and controlled synthesis, enhancing their catalytic activity for hydrogen generation. These materials effectively bridge the gap between theoretical model surfaces and industrially applicable catalysts.
        - The development of novel catalysts like the AuAg alloy supported on mesoporous aluminosilicate AuAg@MCM, which are very active for low-temperature CO oxidation. The catalyst's activity is highly dependent on hydrogen pretreatment conditions, undergoing phase segregation during calcination and restoration upon reduction treatment.
        - The exploration of selective hydrogenation of the CO bond in acrolein through the architecture of bimetallic surface structures, highlighting the significant role of bimetallic catalysts in heterogeneous catalysis.

        These advancements are crucial for both fundamental research and practical applications, as they allow for the modification of surface reactivity and enhancement of catalytic efficiency, making bimetallic catalysts invaluable tools in the field of catalysis.

        关键进展包括：
        - 利用近表面合金（NSAs），通过在主金属表面附近存在溶剂金属，并且浓度不同于基体，来赋予表面新的催化特性。这些NSAs以与贵金属（Cu、Au）相似的方式结合原子氢（H），同时更容易分解H2，使其成为制药生产中的低温度、高选择性催化剂以及坚固的燃料电池阳极的理想选择。
        - 使用密度泛函理论计算引入具有优越氢相关反应催化行为的新类合金，为催化提供预测能力。
        - 合成近单分散NiCu双金属纳米晶，显示了可变组成和合成控制，增强了氢生成过程中的催化活性。这些材料有效地弥合了理论模型表面与工业适用催化剂之间的差距。
        - 开发新型催化剂，如AuAg合金，通过一锅法制备并支持在介孔铝硅酸盐AuAg@MCM上，非常活跃的低温CO氧化。催化剂的活性高度依赖于氢预处理条件，在煅烧过程中经历相分离，并在还原处理后恢复。
        - 探索丙烯醛中CO键的定向氢化通过双金属表面结构的架构，突出双金属催化剂在异相催化中的重要作用。

        这些进步对于基础研究和实际应用都至关重要，因为它们允许调整表面反应性并提高催化效率，使双金属催化剂成为催化领域的宝贵工具。
