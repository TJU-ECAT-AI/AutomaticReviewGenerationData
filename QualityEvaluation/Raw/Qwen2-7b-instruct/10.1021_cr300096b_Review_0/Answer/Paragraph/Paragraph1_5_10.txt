        The evolution of bimetallic catalysts in heterogeneous catalysis since their commercial introduction has significantly advanced, incorporating unique electronic and chemical properties that bridge the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts. This evolution is crucial for both fundamental research and practical applications, particularly in areas like propane dehydrogenation.

        Studies have focused on near-surface alloys (NSAs), which can confer novel catalytic properties to surfaces. NSAs consist of a solute metal present near the surface of a host metal in concentrations differing from the bulk. Density functional theory calculations have introduced new classes of these alloys that exhibit superior catalytic behavior for hydrogen-related reactions. Some NSAs bind atomic hydrogen (H) as weakly as noble metals (Cu, Au) while dissociating H2 more easily, making them suitable for applications like pharmaceutical production and robust fuel-cell anodes. The unique set of properties of these alloys allows them to serve as low-temperature, highly selective catalysts.

        Representative studies, such as those involving glycolaldehyde as a probe molecule for biomass derivatives, have explored reactions of COH and CO functional groups on monolayer Ni surfaces. This work highlights the importance of modifying the chemisorption and catalytic properties of the overlayers, leading to distinct structural and electronic characteristics in bimetallic systems. 

        The synthesis of near-monodisperse NiCu bimetallic nanocrystals has also advanced the field, with variable compositions and controlled synthesis enhancing catalytic activity for hydrogen generation. These materials enable the bridging of the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between theoretical model surfaces and industrially applicable catalysts, facilitating progress in both fundamental research and practical applications.

        For instance, the development of a novel AuAg alloy catalyst supported on mesoporous aluminosilicate AuAg@MCM has exhibited exceptional activity in low-temperature CO oxidation, demonstrating the importance of hydrogen pretreatment conditions. Post-calcination phase segregation of the AuAg alloy and enrichment of the catalyst surface with AgBr were observed. Subsequent reduction treatment restored the AuAg alloy structure, highlighting the synergy in the coadsorption of CO and O2 on neighboring sites compared to either Au or Ag individually, which explains the observed catalytic synergetic effect.

        The selective hydrogenation of the CO bond in acrolein through the architecture of bimetallic surface structures further exemplifies the significant evolution of bimetallic catalysts in heterogeneous catalysis. This research underscores the pivotal role of unique electronic and chemical properties in bridging the "materials gap" and "pressure gap", making bimetallic catalysts invaluable in both fundamental research and practical applications.

        Future directions might focus on exploring emerging fields and innovative methodologies, taking into account the advancements and findings discussed in the literature. Potential pathways include the exploitation of emerging fields, innovative research approaches, and the development of bimetallic catalysts with tailored electronic and chemical properties for enhanced catalytic performance.

        In summary, the evolution of bimetallic catalysts in heterogeneous catalysis since their commercial introduction has been marked by significant advancements in understanding their unique electronic and chemical properties. These properties enable them to bridge the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts, contributing to advancements in both fundamental research and practical applications.

Future research endeavors might focus on the development of bimetallic catalysts with tailored electronic and chemical properties for enhanced catalytic performance.         自商业引入以来，双金属催化剂在非均相催化领域的演变取得了显著进展，融入了独特的电子和化学性质，成功地跨越了“材料差距”和“压力差距”，将模型表面与工业相关的支持催化剂联系起来。这种发展对于基础研究和应用都至关重要。

        研究重点放在了近表面合金（NSAs）上，这些合金可以为表面提供新颖的催化特性。NSAs由一种溶质金属组成，其位于主金属表面附近，在浓度上不同于主体。密度泛函理论计算引入了新型的这些合金类，它们在氢相关反应中表现出优越的催化行为。一些NSAs能够以与铜、金等贵金属相似的弱方式吸附原子氢（H），同时更易于分解H2，这使得它们适用于制药生产以及耐用燃料电池阳极等应用。这些合金的独特性质使它们能够作为低温、高选择性催化剂用于药物制造和燃料电池阳极。

        例如，关于使用甘油醛作为生物衍生物探针分子的研究，探讨了COH和CO功能团在单层Ni表面上的反应。这项工作强调了通过修改覆盖层的化学吸附和催化性质来调整表面结构和电子特性的关键作用，从而在双金属系统中展现出独特的特性和结构。

        近来，开发出近单分散NiCu双金属纳米晶，具有可变组成和可控合成，显著提高了氢生成过程中的催化活性。这些材料使“材料差距”和“压力差距”之间得到跨越，对理论模型表面和工业适用催化剂的进展提供了重要支持，促进了基础研究和应用的进展。

        比如，报道了一种在一种一锅合成程序下制备的新型AuAg合金催化剂，它被支持在介孔铝硅酸盐AuAg@MCM上，该催化剂在低温CO氧化中表现出非常高的活性。催化剂的活性高度依赖于氢预处理条件。在煅烧过程中，AuAg合金经历了相分离，表面富集了AgBr。随后的还原处理去除了Br-并恢复了AuAg合金结构。DFT计算揭示了AuAg合金在相邻位点上CO和O2共吸附的协同性更强，这与仅Au或Ag相比，解释了观察到的催化协同效应。

        在丙烯醛的CO键选择性氢化通过双金属表面结构的架构中，进一步说明了双金属催化剂在非均相催化领域显著进化的情况。这项研究强调了独特电子和化学性质在“材料差距”和“压力差距”中扮演的关键角色，使双金属催化剂成为基础研究和应用中不可或缺的工具。

        未来的方向可能包括探索新兴领域和创新方法，考虑到文献中讨论的进展。潜在路径可能涉及新表面架构的开发、合成方法的优化以及机器学习算法的应用，以预测和增强催化活性。这些领域的发展有可能带来更高效、选择性和可持续的催化剂的创造，用于各种工业过程，包括但不限于制药生产、能源转换和环境修复。

        总结，自商业引入以来，双金属催化剂在非均相催化领域的演变标志着对独特电子和化学性质的深入理解。这些性质使它们能够跨越“材料差距”和“压力差距”与模型表面和工业相关的支持催化剂之间的界限，推动了基础研究和应用的进展。
