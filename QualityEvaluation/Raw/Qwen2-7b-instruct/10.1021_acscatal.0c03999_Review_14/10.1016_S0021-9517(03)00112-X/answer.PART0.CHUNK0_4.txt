<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. The use of lithium-promoted magnesia catalysts offers an alternative method for producing propene and ethene through the oxidative conversion of lower alkanes.
2. Propane oxidative dehydrogenation and cracking yields up to 50% of olefin (propene and ethene).
3. Activation of alkanes through a CH bond splitting is the rate-determining step for all products in the reaction.
4. The presence of gas-phase oxygen plays a crucial role in propane conversion.
5. Carbon dioxide significantly reduces the activity of the catalyst for all products.
6. The reaction trends of ethane, propane, and butane indicate that hydrogen abstraction from the alkane acts as the rate-determining step.
7. Future research should focus on enhancing C-H bond activation, catalyst regenerability, and optimizing reaction systems for better oxidative dehydrogenation processes.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
