<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Recent studies have shown that nanocrystalline MgO can serve as an effective support for nickel-based catalysts in the reverse water gas shift reaction for CO2 mitigation.
2. Transition metal/metal oxides and main group element metal oxides have been assessed in their performance during oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP).
3. Challenges faced in this field include the corrosiveness of materials and the inherent characteristics of the reactions.
4. Key areas for future investigation involve enhancing the activation of C-H bonds, improving the recyclability of catalysts, and refining the reaction systems to optimize the ODHP processes.
