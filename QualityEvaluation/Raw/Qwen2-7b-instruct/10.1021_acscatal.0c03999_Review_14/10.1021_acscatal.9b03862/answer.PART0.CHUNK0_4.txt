1. The oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) has become a promising process for the production of valuable products like propylene and hydrogen. Various catalyst systems, including transition metal/metal oxides and main group element metal oxides, have been investigated for selective dehydrogenation of propane. Challenges include corrosiveness and inherent reaction properties that impact catalyst stability and activity. Future research focuses on improving C-H bond activation, enhancing catalyst regenerability, and optimizing reaction systems.
