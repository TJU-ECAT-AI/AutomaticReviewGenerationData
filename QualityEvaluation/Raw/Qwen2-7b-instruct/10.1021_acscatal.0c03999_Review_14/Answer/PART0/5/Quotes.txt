[1] "Silica or alumina supported chromium oxide was industrially used for the productions of propene and isobutene through the dehydrogenation of the corresponding alkanes."
          [2] "Recently, a SBA-15 supported chromium oxide by the impregnation method has been applied to the dehydrogenation and the oxidative dehydrogenation of C3H8 (1) and showed good catalytic performances."
          [3] "Effects of metal species were tested by using Cr, Ga, Ni, V, Fe, Mn, and Co on MCM-41- DHT (Si/M=50), among which Cr-MCM-41 showed the highest activity, followed by Ga, Ni, V, Fe, Mn, and Co for the CO2 dehydrogenation of C3H8 (Fig. 1)."
          [4] "Cr-MCM-41 with Si/Cr=50 showed a yield above 15 % with a high selectivity above 90 % of propene."
          [5] "When the Cr loading was changed on MCM-41-DHT, pore diameter was constant at 2.7 nm up to the Si/Cr ratio of 25, even though both pore volume and surface area decreased with increasing the Cr content (Table 1)."
          [6] "Cr-MCM-41 was prepared by the direct hydrothermal (DHT) synthesis method. The synthesized materials were characterized by XRD, N2 adsorption, diffuse reflectance UV-Vis, XANES, and EXAFS spectroscopic measurements."
          [7] "Cr-MCM-41 showed a low surface area compared to Cr-MCM-41 and no detectable pore volume."
          [8] "We have reported that only one kind of monochromate species was observed on Cr-MCM-41 prepared by the DHT method, whereas several types of chromate species possibly including both monochromates and polychromates existed on Cr-MCM-41 prepared by the TIE method by UV Raman spectroscopy."
          [9] "According to the Cri-edge XANES spectra for the Cr-MCM-41-DHT sample with Si/Cr ratio of 50 (Fig. 5, a & b), a preedge peak clearly observed before the reaction became very weak, and the edge position shifted to lower energy, suggesting that the chromium has been changed from Cr6+ in tetrahedral coordination to Cr3+ in octahedral coordination after the reaction."
          [10] "The results of TPR for the Cr-MCM-41-DHT (Si/Cr=50) sample are shown in Fig. 7. A peak observed between 350600 C (a) is probably due to the reduction of Cr6+,14 and disappeared in the second TPR (b)."
