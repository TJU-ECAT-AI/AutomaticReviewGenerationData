            Recent advances in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) using soft oxidants have significantly propelled the field, particularly focusing on catalyst types, especially those featuring transition metals and main group elements. A critical aspect highlighted is the substantial influence of nickel particle size on the kinetic parameters of CO2 hydrogenation, leading to divergent pathways for CO and CH4 formation. The study emphasizes that smaller nickel particles at lower loadings promote CO production, whereas higher loadings result in a preference for CH4 formation. The proposed active sites, corresponding to kink, corner, or step positions on the catalyst surface, play a pivotal role in the reaction mechanism. Two distinct reaction pathways—consecutive and parallel—are noted, each influenced by the size of the Ni clusters and particles, respectively. The formate species, existing in a monodentate configuration, is intricately involved in CO2 hydrogenation across different Ni/SiO2 catalysts, irrespective of the nickel loading and particle size.

            The use of alternative oxidants, notably sulfur vapor (S2), for ODHP holds promise in suppressing the facile overoxidation to COx that typically occurs with oxygen. For a series of metal sulfide catalysts, a strong correlation is observed between sulfur binding energy and activation energy for propylene formation, underscoring the significance of metal-sulfur bond strength in SODHP activity. The wide variation in propylene selectivity among catalysts such as S-ZrO2 and PdS underscores the importance of catalyst selection. Steam-activated iron-zeolites (FeZSM-5, Fe-silicalite, Fe-beta) are highly efficient catalysts for ODHP using N2O, achieving initial propene yields in the range of 20-25% at 723-773 K, with a similar degree of propane conversion and propene selectivity. The use of iron phosphate (FePO4), europium oxychloride (EuOCl), and nickel oxide modified ceria (NiO-CeO2) as catalysts for propane oxychlorination have reached propylene yields of 15%, 40%, and 55% respectively, at high (80-98%) selectivity. Chromium phosphate (CrPO) emerges as a promising solution due to its unique electronic properties and enhanced selectivity.

            Fe-zeolites, particularly FeZSM-5 and FeBEA, have gained significant interest due to their excellent catalytic performance in various environmental applications and selective oxidation for chemical production. These materials demonstrate remarkable resistance against deactivation while exhibiting rapid and complete deactivation in other catalysts. They showcase significantly higher initial propene yields compared to commercial H-ZSM-5 and H-BEA catalysts, despite having 30-40 times less iron content. The higher activity and lower rate of deactivation of ex-H-ZSM-5 compared to ex-H-BEA draws parallels with the results obtained over ex-FeZSM-5 and ex-FeBEA. The essential role of iron in the reaction can be clearly concluded from the significantly higher C3H8 and N2O conversions over ex-FeZSM-5 and ex-FeBEA, as compared to ex-H-ZSM-5 and ex-H-BEA.

            The use of supported alkali metal chloride melts as new groups of catalysts for ODHP applications offers minimized metal cation surface concentration, providing a dynamic surface that minimizes unsaturation of cations and anions, thereby minimizing interaction with highly reactive products. The order of the catalysts with respect to selectivity to olefins is Li-K-MD > Li-Na-MD > Li-MD > K-MD > Na-MD. The increase in temperature leads to an increase in selectivity to olefin, while the formation of COx decreases in parallel.

            Challenges persist in this field, notably corrosion and inherent reaction properties, which limit the industrial application of ODHP methods due to the necessity of using high temperatures to increase the olefin equilibrium concentration. The oxidative dehydrogenation by molecular chlorine was studied using a series of ruthenium-titanium dioxide catalysts, revealing the highest catalytic activity at temperatures between 150 and 450°C, with a propylene selectivity reaching 95% at 50% propane conversion and complete conversion of chlorine at 400°C. However, the yield of propylene even over the best catalysts known to date does not exceed 35%, which is insufficient for economic feasibility. The total enthalpy of the reaction is 60.34 kJ/mol, facilitating waste heat utilization.

            Future research directions in ODHP are centered around improving C-H bond activation, enhancing catalyst regenerability, and renovating reaction systems to optimize processes. Strategies for improving these aspects are anticipated to address the persistent challenges in the field, leading to more efficient and economically viable ODHP technologies.

            Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements and findings discussed in the file, there lies an opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies. Future research endeavors might focus on developing novel catalysts with enhanced stability and activity, optimizing reaction conditions for better efficiency, and integrating advanced reaction engineering principles to streamline the ODHP process. The exploration of alternative oxidants, such as sulfur vapor and supported alkali metal chloride melts, offers promising avenues for enhancing selectivity and reducing by-product formation. Additionally, the development of more robust catalyst supports and the design of catalyst architectures that facilitate improved mass transfer and reaction kinetics are crucial for advancing ODHP technology. The integration of advanced computational methods for catalyst design and the application of machine learning algorithms to predict catalyst performance can also significantly contribute to the optimization of ODHP processes. Future work should also aim to reduce energy consumption and environmental impact by developing more sustainable and eco-friendly catalysts and processes.

            近年来，使用软氧化剂进行丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）取得了显著进展，特别集中在催化剂类型上，尤其是过渡金属和主族元素金属氧化物。镍颗粒大小对CO2氢化动力学参数的影响显著，导致了CO和CH4形成的不同途径。研究表明，较小的镍颗粒在较低负载下促进CO生产，而较高负载则倾向于CH4形成。在催化剂表面对应于棱线、角点或台阶位置的活性位点在反应机制中发挥关键作用。存在两种不同的反应途径——连续和并行——它们分别受小Ni簇和大Ni颗粒的影响。在Ni/SiO2催化剂上，以单齿配置存在的形式酸物种在CO2氢化中起着至关重要的作用，无论镍负载和颗粒大小如何。

            使用替代氧化剂，特别是硫蒸汽（S2），作为ODHP的方法具有抑制通常使用氧气时发生的COx易过氧化的优点。对于一系列金属硫化物催化剂，硫结合能与丙烯形成激活能量之间存在强相关性，突出了金属-硫键强度在SODHP活性中的重要性。丙烯选择性在S-ZrO2和PdS等催化剂之间存在广泛变化，表明催化剂选择的重要性。使用铁沸石（FeZSM-5、Fe硅酸盐、Fe贝塔）作为使用N2O的ODHP催化剂，实现了20-25%的初始丙烯产率，在723-773 K的温度范围内，与丙烷转化和丙烯选择性相似度很高。

            铁磷酸（FePO4）、铕氧氯化物（EuOCl）和镍氧化铈（NiO-CeO2）作为丙烷氧氯化过程的催化剂，已达到15%、40%和55%的丙烯产率，分别在80-98%的选择性下。由于其独特的电子特性和增强的选型性，铬磷酸（CrPO）被证明是一种有前景的解决方案。Fe-沸石，特别是FeZSM-5和FeBEA，由于在各种环境应用和选择性氧化化学生产方面的出色催化性能而受到关注。这些材料显示出对去活的显著抵抗力，同时表现出其他催化剂的快速和完全去活。与商业H-ZSM-5和H-BEA催化剂相比，它们的初始丙烯产率显著更高，尽管铁含量减少了30-40倍。FeZSM-5的活动性和去活速率较低与FeBEA相比，与FeZSM-5和FeBEA的实验结果相吻合。C3H8和N2O的转换可以清楚地从FeZSM-5和FeBEA的显著更高C3H8和N2O转换得出结论。

            支持的碱金属氯化熔体作为ODHP应用的新组催化剂，提供了最小化的金属阳离子表面浓度，提供了一个动态表面，可最小化阳离子和阴离子的不饱和性，从而最小化与高度活性产品的相互作用。催化剂的顺序与烯烃选择性的顺序是Li-K-MD > Li-Na-MD > Li-MD > K-MD > Na-MD。随着温度的升高，烯烃的选择性增加，同时COx的形成减少。

            在此领域中仍然存在挑战，特别是腐蚀和固有的反应性质，限制了ODHP方法的工业应用，因为需要使用高温来增加烯烃平衡浓度。通过分子氯进行的氧化脱氢研究揭示了一系列钌-钛氧化物催化剂在150至450°C之间的最高催化活性，400°C时丙烯选择性达到95%，丙烷转化为50%，氯完全转化为95%。然而，即使是最具知名度的催化剂，丙烯的最大产率也不超过35%，这不足以满足经济可行性。反应的总焓为60.34 kJ/mol，有利于废热利用。

            优化C-H键活化、提高催化剂再生能力和革新反应系统以优化过程是ODHP未来研究方向的核心。预期这些策略将解决该领域存在的持续挑战，并推动更高效和经济可行的ODHP技术的发展。
