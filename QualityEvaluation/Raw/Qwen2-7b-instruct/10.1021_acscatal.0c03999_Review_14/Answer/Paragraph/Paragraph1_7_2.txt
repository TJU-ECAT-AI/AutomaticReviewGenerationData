        Recent advancements in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) using soft oxidants have been pivotal in enhancing the efficiency and selectivity of the process, particularly with the utilization of transition metal/metal oxide and main group element metal oxide catalysts. Notable examples include the effectiveness of iron-zeolites like FeZSM-5 and FeBEA, which exhibit high initial propene yields despite having significantly less iron content than commercial catalysts. The reverse water gas shift reaction with nanocrystalline MgO as a favorable support for nickel-based catalysts is another promising approach, especially in the context of CO2 mitigation.

        However, the persistent challenge of catalyst deactivation remains a hurdle, particularly due to coke formation, which impacts propene yield. Iron's pivotal role in the reaction is evident, with FeZSM-5 and FeBEA showing higher activity and initial propene yields compared to commercial catalysts. The higher deactivation resistance of steam-activated FeZSM-5 in ODHP with N2O is intriguing, possibly attributed to differences in iron constitution and porous structure.

        The use of alternative oxidants, such as sulfur vapor, has shown promise in suppressing overoxidation, offering a novel route to propylene production. Metal phosphates, especially chromium phosphate (CrPO), stand out for their exceptional stability and high selectivity, rivaling the best catalysts reported. The relationship between propane conversion and product selectivity was also explored, highlighting the importance of catalyst selection in achieving optimal results.

        In the realm of sulfur vapor, the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane was studied, demonstrating that sulfur vapor can act as an effective oxidant, potentially leading to a new, promising route to propylene production. The selectivity toward propylene reached high levels, but the yield remains a challenge, with the best-known yield not exceeding 35%, which is insufficient for economic feasibility.

        Insights from recent studies on nickel particle size also emphasize its impact on CO2 hydrogenation kinetics, influencing the formation of CO and CH4 and reaction selectivity. The sites corresponding to kink, corner, or step positions on the Ni/SiO2 surface are proposed as primary active sites for CO2 hydrogenation.

        The continuous propene production via a cyclically-operated process, utilizing FeZSM-5, has been demonstrated, achieving >20% propene yields over 2000 minutes with two parallel fixed-bed reactors, showcasing advancements in catalyst efficiency and cyclic operation strategies.

        The reduction-oxidation cycle between chromium hexa-oxo-tetrahedra (Cr6+C>4) and chromium tri-oxy-octahedra (Cr3+Og) during the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane over Cr-MCM-41 is crucial, with both oxygen and carbon dioxide (CO2) capable of regenerating the Cr6+C>4 tetrahedra from the reduced Cr3+Og octahedra.

        Supported alkali metal chloride melts have emerged as a new group of catalysts for ODHP, offering minimized surface concentration of metal cations and a dynamic surface that reduces interactions with reactive products, with Li-K-MD > Li-Na-MD > Li-MD > K-MD > Na-MD ranking in selectivity towards olefins.

        Future research directions are geared towards improving C-H bond activation, enhancing catalyst regenerability, and optimizing reaction systems to maximize ODHP processes' efficiency and selectivity. Strategies include developing open structures, creating mesopores through desilication, and exploring more open pore architectures to mitigate deactivation by coke and facilitate better reactant and product transport.

        * * *

        Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements and findings discussed, there is a clear opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies. Future research endeavors might focus on developing catalysts with enhanced stability and regenerability, optimizing the reaction conditions for sulfur vapor oxidative dehydrogenation, and refining the use of supported alkali metal chloride melts. There is also potential for exploring the synergistic effects of combining multiple catalyst types or introducing novel metal compositions to further improve the selectivity and yield of propylene production. Additionally, investigating the underlying mechanisms of deactivation and catalyst rejuvenation could lead to breakthroughs in prolonging catalyst life and enhancing process efficiency.

    <Chinese>
        最近在使用软氧化剂进行丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）过程中取得的进步，在提高过程效率和选择性方面发挥了关键作用，尤其是利用过渡金属/金属氧化物和主族元素金属氧化物作为催化剂。例如，铁沸石如FeZSM-5和FeBEA表现出显著的催化性能，能够实现高达25%的丙烯产率，尽管它们的铁含量仅为商业催化剂的30-40倍。纳米晶镁氧化物作为镍基催化剂的支持物在二氧化碳减排方面的潜力也是一个有前景的方法。

        然而，催化剂失活这一持续的挑战仍然是一个障碍，尤其是在焦炭形成影响丙烯产率的情况下。铁在反应中的核心作用是显而易见的，FeZSM-5和FeBEA在活性和初始丙烯产率方面均优于商业催化剂，尽管它们的铁含量较低。蒸汽激活的FeZSM-5在使用N2O进行ODHP时展现出较高的失活抵抗力，其原因可能与催化剂中铁的构成差异以及原始沸石框架中铝的存在有关。

        使用替代氧化剂，如硫蒸汽，显示出抑制过度氧化的潜力，并可能为丙烯生产开辟一条新的有前途的途径。金属磷酸盐，特别是铬磷酸盐（CrPO），因其极高的稳定性和高选择性而突出，其性能可与迄今为止报告的最佳催化剂相媲美。在连续丙烯生产过程中使用FeZSM-5已经得到证明，通过两个并行的固定床反应器实现了>20%的丙烯产率，持续了2000分钟，这展示了催化剂效率和循环操作策略的改进。

        在硫蒸汽中，对丙烷的氧化脱氢研究表明，硫蒸汽可以作为一种有效的氧化剂，潜在地导致丙烯生产的新型有前途的途径。达到高丙烯选择性的水平，但产量仍然是一个挑战，最高已知的产量不超过35%，这不足以满足经济可行性。

        近期关于镍颗粒大小的研究强调了它在二氧化碳氢化动力学上的影响，影响了二氧化碳和甲烷的形成以及反应选择性。在Ni/SiO2表面的尖端、角落或台阶位置被认为是CO2氢化的主要活性位点。

        支持的碱金属氯化物熔体已经发展成为ODHP的新组催化剂，提供了一个金属阳离子表面浓度最小化的平台，并具有减少与反应性产物相互作用的动态表面，Li-K-MD > Li-Na-MD > Li-MD > K-MD > Na-MD在烯烃选择性方面排名。

        将来研究的方向旨在提高C-H键活化、增强催化剂再生能力和优化反应系统以最大化ODHP过程的效率和选择性。策略包括开发开放结构、通过脱硅创建介孔孔道，并探索更多开放孔架构来减轻焦炭的失活并促进更好的反应物和产物传输。

        整合理解和前瞻性展望：考虑到讨论的进展和发现，存在探索新兴领域和创新方法的机会。未来的研究重点可能集中在开发稳定性更强、再生能力更高的催化剂上，优化硫蒸汽氧化脱氢的条件，以及改进支持的碱金属氯化物熔体的使用。还有潜力通过结合多种催化剂类型或引入新型金属组成来进一步提高丙烯产率的选择性和产量。此外，调查失活和催化剂恢复的底层机制也可能导致延长催化剂寿命并提高工艺效率的突破。
