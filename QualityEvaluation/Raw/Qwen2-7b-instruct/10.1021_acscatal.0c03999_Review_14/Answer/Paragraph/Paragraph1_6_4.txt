            Recent studies highlight the use of soft oxidants for the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP), with iron-based catalysts, notably FeZSM-5, demonstrating exceptional efficiency in converting propane to propene using nitrogen dioxide (N2O) ([1]). Achieving initial propene yields of 20-25% at temperatures around 723-773 K, these catalysts enable a cyclic process for continuous propene production through alternating reaction and regeneration cycles ([4], [5]). This innovative approach has been successfully demonstrated, showcasing the reversibility of catalyst deactivation and its potential for optimizing the ODHP process ([1], [4], [5]).
            Persistent challenges in this field include the necessity to use high temperatures in order to increase the olefin equilibrium concentration since the dehydrogenation of propane is an endothermic reaction ([1], [2]). Despite the effectiveness of catalysts like Ru/TiO2 ([3]), achieving high yields remains challenging, with the best-known yield reaching 35%, which is insufficient for economic feasibility ([5]). The endothermic nature of the reaction requires high temperatures, posing a significant challenge to industrial application ([1], [5]). The total enthalpy of the reaction, 60.34 kJ/mol, simplifies waste-heat utilization ([6]), making it a key area for future research aimed at improving C-H bond activation, enhancing catalyst regenerability, and optimizing reaction systems to increase yield and economic viability ([1], [5]).
            Recent advancements in ODHP leverage soft oxidants for enhanced selectivity and conversion, with nanocrystalline MgO emerging as a superior support for nickel-based catalysts in the reverse water gas shift reaction for CO2 mitigation ([1], [2]). Key challenges include catalyst corrosion and reaction kinetics. Future research focuses on improving C-H bond activation, catalyst regeneration, and optimizing reaction systems for efficiency and sustainability ([3]).
            Nickel particle size significantly influences CO2 hydrogenation kinetics, product selectivity, and pathways for CO and CH4 formation ([7]). At lower loadings, the catalyst favors CO production, while higher loadings shift selectivity towards CH4. Active sites are hypothesized to be at kink, corner, or step positions on the Ni/SiO2 surface ([7]). Two reaction pathways are identified: consecutive and parallel, with small clusters promoting CO formation and larger particles favoring CH4 ([7]). A formate species in a monodentate configuration is crucial for CO2 hydrogenation, irrespective of nickel loading and particle size ([7]).
            Supported alkali metal chloride melts emerge as new groups of catalysts for applications in which the surface concentration of accessible metal cations has to be minimized ([8]). These melts, with their dynamic surface, effectively reduce unsaturation and interactions with reactive products, leading to improved selectivity towards olefins. The catalyst order, based on selectivity, is Li-K-MD > Li-Na-MD > Li-MD > K-MD > Na-MD ([8]). Notably, as temperature increases, selectivity for olefins enhances, paralleling a decrease in COx formation, indicating optimized reactivity under these conditions ([8]).
            An intense research effort has been dedicated to the development of efficient catalysts for propane oxychlorination, including FePO4, EuOCl, and NiO-CeO2, reaching propylene yields of 15%, 40%, and 55% at high (80-98%) selectivity, respectively ([1]). Challenges include catalyst stability and selectivity towards propylene. Future research focuses on enhancing C-H bond activation, catalyst regenerability, and optimizing reaction systems. CrPO is found as the most selective system, reaching propylene yields that rival the best catalysts reported to date ([1]).
            As CrOx catalysts supported on the SBA-15 support (Cr/Si) are highly active, they represent potentially promising catalysts for the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane with CO2 (ODHP) ([1]). However, reduction of the active sites (Cr3+) during the reaction is known to lead to severe deactivation. With an Ni promoter on the CrOx/silica catalyst, CO2 is a more effective oxidant and reduced Cr2+ states are regenerated to active Cr3+ states ([1]). The Ni-promoted CrOx/SBA-15 catalyst yields propylene in the ODHP reaction ([1]). Importantly, Ni maintains the stable catalytic activity, allowing for the regeneration of active Cr3+ states under Ni promotion ([1]).
            Not only O2 but also CO2 could regenerate the Cr6+C>4 tetrahedra from the reduced Cr3+Og octahedra as suggested by the results of TPR and EXAFS ([1]). It was clearly observed that CO was selectively produced from CO2 during the CsH8 dehydrogenation over Cr-MCM-41 ([1]). It is concluded that during the reaction C?Hg is dehydrogenated to C3H6 by Cr6+O4 tetrahedra, which is simultaneously reduced to Cr3+Og octahedra. The reduced Cr3+Og octahedra is reoxidized to Cr6+O4 tetrahedra by CO2, and thus the reduction-oxidation cycle between Cr6+C>4 tetrahedra and Cr3+Og octahedra possesses an important role in the dehydrogenation of C3H8 with CO2 over Cr-MCM-41 ([1]).

            The integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements and findings discussed in the file, there lies an opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies. Future research endeavors might focus on:
            - Developing more robust catalysts that can withstand high operating temperatures without significant deactivation.
            - Enhancing the selectivity of the catalysts to produce propene with minimal by-products such as COx.
            - Investigating the role of soft oxidants other than N2O and CO2 in the oxidative dehydrogenation process to broaden the scope of potential catalysts.
            - Optimizing the reaction conditions, including temperature, pressure, and reactant ratios, to achieve higher propene yields and efficiency.
            - Exploring the synergistic effects of combining multiple catalysts or modifying existing catalysts with additives to improve their performance.
            - Designing reaction systems that facilitate better mass transfer and heat management to enhance the overall efficiency of the ODHP process.
            - Focusing on the recyclability and reusability of catalysts to reduce costs and environmental impact.
            - Developing computational models to predict catalyst performance and reaction mechanisms, aiding in the rational design of new catalysts.

    <Chinese>
            最近的研究强调了使用软氧化剂进行丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）的可能性，特别是铁基催化剂，在使用氮氧化物（N2O）将丙烷转化为丙烯方面表现出极高的效率。在大约723-773 K的温度下，这些催化剂能够实现20-25%的初始丙烯产率，从而通过反应和再生周期交替的方式实现连续的丙烯生产（[1]，[4]，[5]）。这一创新方法已被成功演示，展示了催化剂失活的可逆性及其优化ODHP过程的潜力（[1]，[4]，[5]）。
            持续存在的挑战包括为了提高烯烃平衡浓度而需要使用高温，因为脱氢丙烷是一个吸热反应（[1]，[2]）。尽管Ru/TiO2等催化剂（[3]）表现出高效性，但要实现高产率仍然具有挑战性，最好的产率仅达到35%，这不足以满足经济可行性（[5]）。反应的吸热性质要求高温，这为工业应用带来了重大挑战（[1]，[5]）。反应的总焓，60.34 kJ/mol，简化了废热利用（[6]），使其成为未来研究的重点领域，旨在提高C-H键激活、催化剂再生能力和反应系统优化，以提高产率和经济可行性（[1]，[5]）。
            最近的进展利用软氧化剂来增强选择性和转化率，纳米晶镁氧化物作为镍基催化剂在二氧化碳逆水煤气变换反应中的优越支持，实现了二氧化碳减排（[1]，[2]）。关键的挑战包括催化剂腐蚀和反应动力学。未来的研究重点在于改善C-H键激活、催化剂再生能力和反应系统优化以提高效率和可持续性（[3]）。
            镍粒子大小显著影响二氧化碳氢化反应的动力学、产物选择性和CO和CH4形成途径（[7]）。在较低负载下，催化剂倾向于促进CO的产生，而在较高负载下，选择性转向CH4。假定活性位点位于Ni/SiO2表面的尖端、角落或台阶位置（[7]）。识别出两种反应途径：连续和并行，小簇促进CO形成，大颗粒有利于CH4（[7]）。无论镍负载和颗粒大小如何，单齿配置的甲酸物种对于CO2氢化在Ni/SiO2催化剂上都至关重要（[7]）。
            受限于金属氯化物熔体作为在催化性能需要最小化可接触金属离子表面浓度的应用中显示出潜在前景的新催化剂组（[8]）。这些熔体，由于其动态表面，有效地减少了不饱和和与活性产品的相互作用，导致了对烯烃的选择性提高。根据选择性排列，催化剂序列是Li-K-MD > Li-Na-MD > Li-MD > K-MD > Na-MD（[8]）。值得注意的是，随着温度的增加，烯烃的选择性增加，同时COx形成减少，表明在这些条件下实现了最佳反应性（[8]）。
            对于丙烷氧氯化，已经致力于开发高效的催化剂，如FePO4、EuOCl和NiO-CeO2，分别达到15%、40%和55%的高选择性丙烯产率（[1]）。挑战包括催化剂稳定性以及向丙烯的高选择性。未来研究将集中在提高C-H键激活、催化剂再生能力和反应系统优化以提高效率和可持续性。CrPO被发现是最具选择性的系统，达到的丙烯产率与迄今为止报道的最佳催化剂相当（[1]）。
            作为CrOx催化剂支持在SBA-15支持（Cr/Si）的高度活跃，它们代表了潜在有前途的催化剂，用于使用CO2的丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）（[1]）。然而，反应期间活性位点（Cr3+）的还原会导致严重的失活。添加Ni到CrOx/silica催化剂中，CO2作为更有效的氧化剂，并且还原的Cr2+状态可以再生为活性Cr3+状态（[1]）。Ni促进的CrOx/SBA-15催化剂在ODHP反应中产生丙烯（[1]）。重要的是，Ni维持稳定的催化活性，允许Ni促进下活性Cr3+状态的再生（[1]）。
            不仅氧气，而且二氧化碳都可以从还原的Cr3+Og八面体中再生Cr6+C>4四面体，如由TPR和EXAFS的结果所建议的（[1]）。清楚地观察到，在Cr-MCM-41上进行CsH8脱氢时，从二氧化碳选择性产生CO（[1]）。可以得出结论，在反应中，C?Hg被Cr6+O4四面体脱氢为C3H6，同时被还原为Cr3+Og八面体。还原的Cr3+Og八面体被CO2重新氧化为Cr6+O4四面体，因此Cr6+C>4四面体和Cr3+Og八面体之间的还原-氧化循环在使用CO2的Cr-MCM-41上脱氢C3H8中扮演着重要的角色（[1]）。

            整合的理解和未来的展望：考虑到文件中讨论的先进成果和发现，存在探索新兴领域和创新方法的机会。未来研究努力可能集中在：
            - 开发能够承受高操作温度而不发生显著失活的更坚固的催化剂。
            - 提高催化剂的选择性，以产生丙烯并减少COx等副产品的产量。
            - 探索除了N2O和CO2之外的软氧化剂在氧化脱氢过程中可能的催化剂，以扩大潜在催化剂的范围。
            - 优化反应条件，包括温度、压力和反应物比例，以实现更高的丙烯产率和效率。
            - 探索多种催化剂的结合或现有催化剂与添加剂的修改，以提高其性能。
            - 设计促进更好质量传递和热量管理的反应系统，以提高ODHP过程的整体效率。
            - 着眼于催化剂的可回收性和可重复使用性，以降低成本和环境影响。
            - 发展计算模型来预测催化剂性能和反应机制，帮助理性设计新的催化剂。
