            Recent advances in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) have primarily centered around the utilization of soft oxidants, including transition metal/metal oxides and main group element metal oxides, to facilitate the conversion of propane to propylene. Notable examples include chromium (Cr) compounds supported on SBA-15, where the CrOx catalysts exhibit high activity but suffer from severe deactivation due to the reduction of active Cr3+ sites. Promoting with nickel (Ni) enhances catalytic activity by increasing CO2's effectiveness as an oxidant and regenerating reduced Cr2+ states to active Cr3+, leading to improved selectivity and conversion in ODHP reactions.

            For instance, a study \[10.1002/cctc.201200397\] found that CrOx catalysts supported on SBA-15 are highly active for the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane with CO2. However, reduction of the active sites (Cr3+) during the reaction leads to severe deactivation. Promoting with Ni on the CrOx/silica catalyst makes CO2 a more effective oxidant and regenerates reduced Cr2+ states to active Cr3+, yielding propylene in the ODHP reaction.

            N2 adsorption-desorption studies demonstrate type IV isotherms and H1-type hysteresis loops for the catalysts, confirming their structural stability. The incorporation of Ni in Cr/Si catalysts facilitates stable catalytic activity and efficient CO2 dissociation, allowing for the regeneration of active sites, thus maintaining consistent propylene yields.

            Another study \[10.1039/D0CY01039A\] highlighted the potential of sulfur vapor as an effective oxidant for ODHP, offering a promising route to suppress overoxidation to COx compared to traditional oxygen usage. Catalysts such as metal sulfides correlate their propylene formation activation energies with sulfur binding energies, indicating a pivotal role of metal-sulfur bond strength in catalytic efficiency.

            The propylene selectivity varies widely among catalysts, ranging from 86% over S-ZrO2 to 38% over PdS, demonstrating the need for optimized catalyst design. The use of sulfur vapor suggests a novel, efficient method for propylene production, with catalyst-dependent propane conversion rates varying from 4.7 to 8.1% at 550°C.

            In summary, the integration of Ni in CrOx catalysts, the utilization of sulfur vapor, and the exploration of metal sulfides are key advancements in ODHP using soft oxidants. Future research should focus on optimizing C-H bond activation, enhancing catalyst regenerability, and refining reaction systems to maximize propylene yields.

Future research might focus on leveraging the unique properties of soft oxidants, such as sulfur vapor and metal sulfides, to develop more efficient and selective catalysts. Strategies for improving C-H bond activation, enhancing catalyst regenerability, and renovating reaction systems to optimize ODHP processes are paramount. The integration of advanced materials science and computational chemistry could provide insights into the design of novel catalysts and reaction conditions that address the persistent challenges in ODHP, such as corrosiveness and reaction property optimization.

            The future of ODHP research should prioritize the development of catalysts that can withstand harsh operating conditions, maintain high activity over extended periods, and produce high yields of propylene with minimal side reactions. The exploration of multi-functional catalysts that combine the benefits of soft oxidants with other catalytic components could also be a promising direction. Moreover, the integration of sustainable and renewable energy sources, such as solar and wind power, for waste heat recovery and energy supply could further enhance the economic viability and environmental sustainability of ODHP processes.

            最近在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）领域的发展主要集中在软氧化剂的应用上，包括过渡金属/金属氧化物和主族元素金属氧化物，以促进丙烷向丙烯的转化。例如，铬（Cr）化合物支持在SBA-15上表现出高活性，但会因活性Cr3+位点的还原导致严重的失活。通过添加镍（Ni），可以增强催化活性，增加二氧化碳作为氧化剂的有效性，并重新生成还原的Cr2+状态为活性Cr3+，从而在ODHP反应中产生丙烯。

            比如一项研究\[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]指出，CrOx催化剂在SBA-15上的支持对于用CO2进行丙烷的氧化脱氢具有高度活性。然而，在反应过程中，Cr3+活性位点的减少会导致严重的失活。通过在CrOx/silica催化剂上添加Ni，可以使二氧化碳成为更有效的氧化剂，并重新生成还原的Cr2+状态为活性Cr3+，从而在丙烷氧化脱氢反应中产生丙烯。

            N2吸附-脱附研究显示所有样品的类型IV等温线和H1型滞回环，证明了催化剂结构的稳定性。Ni在Cr/Si催化剂中的引入促进了稳定的催化活性和高效的二氧化碳分解，允许活性位点的再生，从而保持一致的丙烯产量。

            另一项研究\[10.1039/D0CY01039A\]强调了硫蒸汽作为ODHP有效氧化剂的潜力，提供了一条抑制与氧气相比过氧化化为COx的有前景的途径。金属硫化物催化剂将它们的丙烯形成活化能与硫结合能相关联，表明金属-硫键强度在催化效率中的关键作用。

            丙烯选择性在不同催化剂之间变化广泛，范围从86%（S-ZrO2）到38%（PdS），这凸显了优化催化剂设计的需求。使用硫蒸汽提出了一种高效生产丙烯的新方法，催化剂依赖的丙烷转化率从4.7%到8.1%不等，在550°C时。

            总之，Ni在CrOx催化剂中的整合、硫蒸汽的利用以及金属硫化物的探索是使用软氧化剂进行ODHP的关键进展。未来研究应侧重于优化C-H键激活、增强催化剂再生能力和改进反应系统，以最大化丙烯产量。

            整体理解与未来展望：考虑到讨论的进步和发现，有探索新领域和创新方法的机会在ODHP。未来研究可能关注利用软氧化剂的独特性质，如硫蒸汽和金属硫化物，来开发更高效、选择性的催化剂。优化C-H键激活、增强催化剂再生能力和改进反应系统以优化ODHP过程是至关重要的。通过集成先进的材料科学和计算化学，可以获得有关新型催化剂设计和反应条件的见解，以解决ODHP当前的挑战，如腐蚀性和反应属性优化。

            ODHP研究的未来应该优先考虑能够承受苛刻操作条件、在较长时间内维持高活性并产生高丙烯产量的催化剂。探索结合软氧化剂和其他催化成分的多功能催化剂也可能是一个有希望的方向。此外，集成可再生能源，如太阳能和风能，用于热回收和能源供应，可以进一步提高ODHP过程的经济可行性和环境可持续性。

            总结而言，使用软氧化剂进行ODHP的领域提供了创新和技术进步的激动人心的机会。通过解决当前的挑战并探索新的研究方向，行业可以更接近实现这一灵活且环保的丙烯生产过程的全部潜力。
