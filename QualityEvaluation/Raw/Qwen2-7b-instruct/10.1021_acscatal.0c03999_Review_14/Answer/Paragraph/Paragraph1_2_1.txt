            Recent advancements in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) utilizing soft oxidants have significantly expanded the catalytic landscape for producing propylene from propane. Transition metal/metal oxide catalysts, notably chromium (Cr) and iron (Fe)-based systems, have shown remarkable activity, especially when supported on materials like SBA-15 or zeolites. Chromium catalysts, such as those supported on SBA-15 (Cr/Si), exhibit high activity due to their active Cr3+ sites, but face the challenge of site reduction leading to severe deactivation. The introduction of nickel (Ni) as a promoter enhances the effectiveness of CO2 as an oxidant, regenerating reduced Cr2+ states back to active Cr3+, thus maintaining the catalyst's activity. This strategy enables stable propylene production, highlighting the importance of C-H bond activation, catalyst regenerability, and reaction system optimization in the ODHP process.

            In parallel, supported alkali metal chloride melts have emerged as innovative catalysts for ODHP, offering a dynamic surface that minimizes the saturation of cations and anions, reducing interactions with reactive products. These catalysts demonstrate a descending order of selectivity for olefins, with Li-K-MD showing the highest selectivity followed by Li-Na-MD, Li-MD, K-MD, and Na-MD. As temperature increases, the selectivity towards olefins improves, paralleling a decrease in COx formation, indicating reduced re-adsorption of alkenes under these conditions.

            Fe-zeolites, including FeZSM-5 and FeBEA, have garnered significant interest due to their excellent catalytic performance in various environmental applications and selective oxidation for chemical production. FeZSM-5 exhibits remarkable resistance against deactivation, while FeBEA shows a very rapid and complete deactivation. Despite having 30-40 times less iron content than commercial H-ZSM-5 and H-BEA catalysts, FeZSM-5 and FeBEA display significantly higher initial propene yields. The higher activity and lower rate of deactivation of ex-H-ZSM-5 (170 ppm Fe) compared to ex-H-BEA (210 ppm Fe) suggests that iron's role in the reaction is crucial. The presence of mesopores in ex-FeZSM-5, due to dealumination upon steam treatment, offers a distinct advantage over ex-Fe-silicalite, further contributing to its enhanced performance.

            The challenges in ODHP, particularly corrosion and deactivation, require ongoing research to improve C-H bond activation, catalyst regenerability, and refine reaction systems. Strategies to address these challenges involve enhancing open structures, developing mesopores, and exploring more open architectures to mitigate coke blocking and improve reactant and product transport. These efforts aim to optimize ODHP processes and make them more industrially feasible.

            Nanocrystalline MgO has shown promise as a support for nickel-based catalysts in the reverse water gas shift reaction for CO2 mitigation, offering a pathway for sustainable and efficient CO2 utilization in ODHP. The reverse water gas shift reaction is a promising method for CO2 mitigation, demonstrating the potential for soft oxidants in addressing environmental concerns while advancing ODHP technology.

            In summary, recent studies emphasize the efficacy of catalysts like transition metal/metal oxide and main group element metal oxides in achieving high propene yields through ODHP using soft oxidants. Challenges in corrosion and optimized C-H bond activation necessitate future research focusing on enhancing catalyst regenerability and tailoring reaction systems for improved efficiency in ODHP processes.

            Looking ahead, the future of ODHP with soft oxidants holds significant promise, with research directed towards refining catalyst design, optimizing reaction conditions, and developing strategies for efficient waste heat utilization. Emerging trends include the exploration of novel catalyst materials, the enhancement of reaction mechanisms, and the integration of soft oxidants in sustainable and environmentally friendly processes. This forward-looking perspective underscores the importance of continued innovation in catalysis for the advancement of ODHP technologies.

                    近年来，在使用软氧化剂进行丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）以生产丙烯方面取得了显著进展。过渡金属/金属氧化物催化剂，尤其是铬（Cr）和铁（Fe）基系统，显示出极高的活性，特别是在使用SBA-15或沸石等材料支持时。Cr催化剂，如Cr/Si支持的SBA-15，由于其活性Cr3+位点而表现出高活性，但面临位点还原导致严重失活的挑战。通过引入镍（Ni）作为促进剂增强CO2的有效性，可再生还原后的Cr2+状态回至活性Cr3+状态，从而维持催化剂的活性。这一策略使得稳定丙烯的产生成为可能，突出了在ODHP过程中激活C-H键、催化剂再生能力和反应系统优化的重要性。

                    同时，支持的碱金属氯化物熔体已作为ODHP的创新催化剂出现，提供了一个动态表面，最小化了阳离子和阴离子的饱和度，减少了与反应性产物的相互作用。这些催化剂的降序选择性显示，Li-K-MD具有最高的选择性，随后是Li-Na-MD、Li-MD、K-MD和Na-MD。随着温度的升高，选择性向烯烃增加，同时COx形成减少，表明在这些条件下减少了对烯烃的再吸附。

                    Fe-沸石，包括FeZSM-5和FeBEA，因其在各种环境应用和选择性氧化化学生产中的出色催化性能而受到关注。FeZSM-5表现出对抗失活的卓越能力，而FeBEA则显示出非常快速且完全的失活。尽管FeZSM-5和FeBEA的铁含量比商用H-ZSM-5和H-BEA催化剂低30-40倍，但它们仍能显示出更高的初始丙烯产量。ex-H-ZSM-5（Fe含量为170 ppm）与ex-H-BEA（Fe含量为210 ppm）相比，更高的活性和更低的失活速率表明铁在反应中的关键作用。ex-FeZSM-5中的介孔结构，由于蒸汽处理引起的铝的去除，提供了独特的优势，进一步提高了其性能。

                    ODHP面临的挑战，尤其是腐蚀和失活，需要持续研究以提高C-H键激活、催化剂再生能力和反应系统优化。针对这些挑战的策略包括增强开放结构、开发介孔结构以及探索更开放的架构来缓解焦炭堵塞并改善反应物和产物的传输。这些努力旨在优化ODHP过程并使其更具工业化可行性。

                    碳酸镁纳米晶体已被证明是镍基催化剂的支持物，在逆水煤气变换反应中用于CO2减排，展示了在ODHP中利用软氧化剂以实现可持续和高效CO2利用的潜力。逆水煤气变换反应是一种有前景的CO2减排方法，展示了软氧化剂在解决环境问题的同时推进ODHP技术的潜力。

                    总之，近期研究表明，使用过渡金属/金属氧化物和主族元素金属氧化物催化剂通过软氧化剂实现高丙烯产量的ODHP。腐蚀和优化C-H键激活的挑战要求未来的研究重点放在提高催化剂再生能力和定制反应系统上，以提高ODHP过程的效率。

                    展望未来，ODHP与软氧化剂相结合的技术前景广阔，研究方向集中在改进催化剂设计、优化反应条件以及开发高效的废热利用策略。新兴趋势包括探索新型催化剂材料、优化反应机制以及整合软氧化剂以实现可持续和环保的过程。这种前瞻性的视角强调了催化创新在推动ODHP技术发展方面的重要性。

                    在此背景下，ODHP的研究不仅关注当前的挑战和解决方案，还着眼于未来的机遇和潜在的应用领域，旨在构建一个更加绿色、高效和可持续的化学工业体系。这将为全球能源转型和环境保护做出重要贡献，同时推动化学工艺的革新和发展。
