        Recent advances in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) have significantly expanded the potential for sustainable chemical production, with soft oxidants such as nitrogen dioxide (N2O), molecular chlorine (Cl2), and sulfur vapor (S2) gaining prominence. The utilization of iron-based catalysts, notably iron-zeolites (FeZSM-5 and FeBEA), showcases their exceptional efficiency in converting propane to propene. These catalysts demonstrate high initial propene yields and remarkable resistance against deactivation, surpassing conventional catalysts in terms of both selectivity and conversion.

        Nanocrystalline magnesium oxide (MgO) has emerged as a favorable support for nickel-based catalysts in the reverse water gas shift reaction, a promising method for carbon dioxide (CO2) mitigation. This highlights the potential for enhanced catalytic activity and selectivity towards desired products.

        Challenges persist in the form of catalyst corrosion and reaction kinetics, which limit the efficiency and scalability of ODHP processes. Future research efforts are geared towards improving C-H bond activation, enhancing catalyst regenerability, and optimizing reaction systems to address these limitations.

        Strategies for catalyst improvement include the refinement of catalyst structure, such as the creation of mesopores through desilication, which can mitigate deactivation and optimize ODHP processes. The formate species in a monodentate configuration plays a crucial role in CO2 hydrogenation across different nickel-silica catalysts, underscoring its importance in the development of efficient catalysts.

        An integrative understanding and prospective outlook reveals that while significant progress has been made, there is a need for innovative methodologies and advanced catalyst designs. The exploration of emerging fields such as metal phosphates and alkali metal chloride melts offers new avenues for enhancing selectivity and reducing the formation of undesired byproducts like COx.

        Future research might focus on developing strategies for more effective C-H bond activation, improving catalyst regenerability, and optimizing reaction systems to maximize propene yields and economic feasibility. By addressing these challenges, the field of ODHP stands poised to contribute significantly to the sustainable production of valuable chemicals from renewable resources.

        This comprehensive review underscores the importance of continued scientific inquiry and technological innovation in catalysis, aiming to unlock the full potential of ODHP as a sustainable and economically viable chemical synthesis pathway.

        最近在氧化脱氢丙烷（ODHP）方面的进展极大地扩展了可持续化学生产的可能性，软氧化剂如氮氧化物（N2O）、分子氯（Cl2）和硫蒸汽（S2）引起了广泛关注。铁基催化剂，特别是铁沸石（FeZSM-5和FeBEA），显示出其在将丙烷转化为丙烯方面的高效能，并且具有抵抗失活的显著优势，超越了传统的催化剂在选择性和转化率方面的表现。

        纳米晶镁氧化物（MgO）作为镍基催化剂的支持材料，在反向水煤气变换反应中显示出优良的催化活性和选择性，这为二氧化碳（CO2）减排提供了有前景的方法。这表明了提高催化活性和目标产物选择性的潜力。

        存在的挑战包括催化剂腐蚀和反应动力学，这些限制了ODHP过程的效率和可扩展性。未来研究工作致力于改善C-H键激活、增强催化剂再生能力和优化反应系统以解决这些问题。

        催化剂改进策略包括通过去硅化创建介孔结构，这可以减少失活并优化ODHP过程。在不同镍硅催化剂中，形成物种在单齿配置下的重要性凸显，这强调了在开发高效催化剂时对这一成分的重视。

        整合理解和前瞻性展望揭示，尽管已经取得了重大进展，但仍有需要创新方法和先进催化剂设计的新领域。金属磷酸盐和碱金属氯化物熔体等新兴领域的探索为增强选择性和减少不期望副产品的生成提供了新途径。

        未来研究可能集中于开发更有效和选择性的催化剂、增强失活过程的可逆性以及优化反应条件，以最大化丙烯产量和经济可行性。通过解决这些挑战，ODHP领域有望在可持续生产和有价值的化学品生产方面发挥重要作用。

        这个全面的综述强调了继续科学探索和技术创新在催化领域的重要性，旨在利用ODHP的全部潜力作为可持续和经济上可行的化学合成途径。
