<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Recent advances in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) have focused on the development of catalyst systems that include transition metals, metal oxides, and main group elements. These systems demonstrate differing performances in terms of selectivity and conversion. Persistent challenges in the field revolve around the corrosiveness of the reaction medium and the inherent difficulties in activating C-H bonds. Areas of future research and development are centered around enhancing C-H bond activation, improving catalyst regenerability, and refining reaction systems to optimize the ODHP processes.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
