1. Recent advancements in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) using soft oxidants involve studying various catalyst systems, including transition metals and metal oxides, focusing on selectivity and conversion efficiency.

2. Transition metal/metal oxide catalysts have received significant attention, with emphasis on the impact of oxygen vacancies and metal loading on reaction dynamics.

3. Main group element metal oxides, like antimony oxide and phosphorus oxide, have also shown potential due to their distinctive electronic properties and ability to adjust surface acidity.

4. Challenges persist in ODHP, including the corrosiveness of the reaction environment, the inherent exothermic nature of the process, and difficulty in achieving high product selectivity.

5. Key areas for future research and development in ODHP include enhancing C-H bond activation, improving catalyst recyclability, and refining reaction systems to optimize selectivity and yield.

6. Strategies to address these challenges involve developing new catalyst materials with customized electronic structures, incorporating co-catalysts to improve selectivity, and exploring alternative reaction conditions or media to alleviate corrosion.
