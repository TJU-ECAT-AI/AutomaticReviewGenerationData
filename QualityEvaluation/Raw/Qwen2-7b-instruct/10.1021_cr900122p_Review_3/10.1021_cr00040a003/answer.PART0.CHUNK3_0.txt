The preparation, structure, and catalytic properties of silica-supported zirconium hydrides involve the use of zirconium compounds, such as zirconocene, supported on silica. The structure of these materials typically consists of zirconium atoms embedded within a silica matrix, which can be synthesized through various methods including sol-gel techniques or impregnation. Silica supports offer good thermal stability and high surface area, which are advantageous for heterogeneous catalysis. 

Thorium hydrides supported on alumina exhibit a similar concept where thorium-based compounds are immobilized onto an alumina support. Alumina provides a stable framework for the thorium hydride species, facilitating their use in catalytic applications. The structure of these catalysts often depends on the method of synthesis and the specific properties of the alumina used.

In terms of catalytic properties, both silica-supported zirconium hydrides and alumina-supported thorium hydrides have been utilized in C-H bond activation reactions, particularly in H/D exchange and alkane metathesis. They show distinct capabilities in these processes due to the different reactivities of the metals involved. For instance, zirconium hydrides tend to promote σ-bond metathesis, whereas thorium hydrides might be more effective in hydrogenolysis and chain walking reactions.

Regarding reaction rates and selectivity, studies indicate that the choice of support (silica vs. alumina) can influence these parameters. For example, silica supports may offer better selectivity in certain reactions due to their higher surface area, while alumina supports could enhance stability and resistance to deactivation over time.

However, detailed mechanisms for these reactions, such as the role of specific surface sites, the coordination of metal centers, and the dynamics of substrate binding and product release, require further investigation based on experimental data and theoretical calculations.

二氧化硅支持的锆氢化物的制备、结构和催化性质涉及使用如锆二烷基化合物（如锆二烷基）在二氧化硅上的负载。这些材料的结构通常包括嵌入在二氧化硅网络中的锆原子，通过溶胶-凝胶技术或浸渍法等方法合成。二氧化硅支持材料提供了良好的热稳定性与高比表面积，这对于均相催化反应非常有利。

氧化铝支持的钍氢化物也采用类似的概念，将钍基化合物固定在氧化铝载体上。氧化铝提供了一个稳定的基础，使钍氢化物物种得以应用。这些催化剂的结构往往取决于合成方法和特定的氧化铝特性。

至于催化性能，在C-H键活化反应中，特别是H/D交换和烷烃裂合，这两种材料被用作催化剂。由于金属的活性差异，它们在这些过程中表现出不同的能力。例如，锆氢化物倾向于促进σ键的裂合，而钍氢化物可能更适合氢解和链行走反应。

关于反应速率和选择性，研究表明，支持物的选择（二氧化硅vs. 氧化铝）会影响这些参数。例如，二氧化硅支持物可能在某些反应中提供更好的选择性，因为其更高的比表面积，而氧化铝支持物则可能增强稳定性并减少失活时间。

然而，这些反应的具体机制，如表面特定位点的作用、金属中心的配位、底物结合和产物释放的动力学，需要基于实验数据和理论计算的进一步研究。
