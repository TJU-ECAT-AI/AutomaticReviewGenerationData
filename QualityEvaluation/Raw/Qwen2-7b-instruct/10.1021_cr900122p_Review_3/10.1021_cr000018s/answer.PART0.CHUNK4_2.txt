<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1. The mechanism of C-H bond activation involves the creation of a transition state facilitated by a catalyst, which allows for the breaking of the C-H bond.

2. Silica-supported zirconium hydrides are effective catalysts in C-H bond activation due to their high surface area and durability.

3. Alumina-supported thorium hydrides are utilized in alkane metathesis reactions, which involve rearranging carbon-carbon bonds within hydrocarbons.

4. In H/D exchange reactions, catalysts enable the substitution of hydrogen atoms with deuterium atoms, resulting in isotopically labeled products.

5. Selectivity in these reactions is determined by factors such as catalyst type, reaction conditions, and the presence of alternative pathways.

6. In alkane metathesis, the key role of σ-bond metathesis involves one alkane being transformed into another through the rearrangement of its carbon-carbon bonds.

7. Hydrogenolysis plays a significant part in C-H bond activation, involving the cleavage of the C-H bond by hydrogen gas to form alkanes.

8. Chain walking describes the movement of a catalyst along the carbon chain during a series of C-H bond activation steps, enhancing the efficiency of the process.

9. The speed of these reactions is influenced by temperature, catalyst concentration, and the characteristics of the substrate being activated.

10. The catalytic properties of silica-supported zirconium hydrides and alumina-supported thorium hydrides have been thoroughly examined for their capability to promote C-H bond activation and alkane metathesis.
