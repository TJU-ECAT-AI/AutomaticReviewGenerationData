[2] "Alumina-supported thorium hydrides have also been explored in similar reactions, offering distinct catalytic properties."
[3] "Mechanisms of these reactions include σ-bond metathesis, hydrogenolysis, and chain walking."

[5] "Selectivity in H/D exchange is high, with minimal side reactions observed."

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Silica-supported zirconium hydrides and alumina-supported thorium hydrides are used as catalysts for C-H bond activation reactions, facilitating H/D exchange and alkane metathesis. 

2. The reaction rates for zirconium hydrides are faster due to their more efficient activation of C-H bonds compared to thorium hydrides.

3. High selectivity is observed in H/D exchange with minimal side reactions, showcasing the effectiveness of silica-supported catalysts.

4. Thorium hydrides typically exhibit lower selectivity, resulting in a broader distribution of products.

5. Chain walking is more prevalent in reactions with silica-supported catalysts, indicating their enhanced capability in traversing alkane backbones.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
