<EnglishVersionAnswer>
In the context of hydrogen production, carbon formation is a significant challenge. The process involves several pathways that lead to carbon buildup on the catalyst surface. These pathways are influenced by reaction kinetics, process conditions, and the design of the reformer. To mitigate carbon formation, commercial practices often employ excess steam-to-hydrocarbon ratios, typically at a 3:1 ratio, to prevent the rapid accumulation of carbon that can poison the catalyst and cause fouling. Carbon buildup not only affects the efficiency of the hydrogen production process but also blocks active sites on the catalyst, necessitating its inhibition to ensure optimal performance.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
