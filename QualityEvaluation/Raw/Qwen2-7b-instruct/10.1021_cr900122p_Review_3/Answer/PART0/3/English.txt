The silica-alumina-supported tungsten-based catalyst was prepared using a combination of sol-gel and impregnation methods. It has a high surface area and a mesoporous structure. The catalytic properties of silica-supported zirconium hydrides were compared with alumina-supported thorium hydrides. In H/D exchange reactions, the silica-supported catalyst demonstrated higher reactivity. For alkane metathesis, the alumina-supported catalyst showed better selectivity. The mechanism involves σ-bond metathesis, hydrogenolysis, and chain walking. Reaction rates were faster for the silica-supported catalyst, while selectivity was higher for the alumina-supported catalyst.
