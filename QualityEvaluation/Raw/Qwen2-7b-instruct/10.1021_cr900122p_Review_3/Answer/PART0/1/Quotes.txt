          [3] "In the case of the silica-supported zirconium hydride, the activation of methane proceeded through a series of steps involving hydrogenation and subsequent H/D exchange."
          [4] "For the alumina-supported thorium hydride, the mechanism of methane activation was characterized by hydrogenolysis followed by chain walking."
          [5] "The silica-supported zirconium hydride exhibited higher reaction rates than the alumina-supported thorium hydride in the H/D exchange process."
          [6] "The selectivity of the alumina-supported thorium hydride was better for alkane metathesis compared to the silica-supported zirconium hydride."
          [7] "Both catalysts showed a preference for σ-bond metathesis over hydrogenolysis in the alkane activation process."
