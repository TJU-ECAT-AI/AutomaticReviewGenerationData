Examination of their mechanisms in H/D exchange and alkane metathesis, including reaction rates, selectivity, and the roles of σ-bond metathesis, hydrogenolysis, and chain walking. The study shows that silica-supported zirconium hydrides are more reactive in H/D exchange and alkane metathesis, while alumina-supported thorium hydrides exhibit enhanced stability and selectivity. The mechanisms involve alkyl radical species generation, quick adsorption state reactions, reactions near the catalyst surface, and transfer into the gas phase. Reaction rates, selectivity, and the contributions of each mechanism vary significantly between the two catalyst types.
