"An Alkylidyne Analogue of Tebbe's Reagent: Trapping Reactions of a Titanium Neopentylidyne"
</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The literature discusses the preparation, structure, and catalytic properties of a Lewis acid stabilized titanium alkylidyne, akin to Tebbe's reagent. In contrast to incomplete 1,2-addition of Al(CH3)3 across the TiC linkage, milder electrophiles, such as B(OCH3)3, break apart to form an unprecedented titanium alkylidene incorporating the B(OCH3)2 moiety.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
