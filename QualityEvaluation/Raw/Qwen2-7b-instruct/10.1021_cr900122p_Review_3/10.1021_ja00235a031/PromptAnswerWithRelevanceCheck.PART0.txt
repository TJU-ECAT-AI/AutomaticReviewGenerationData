The silica-supported zirconium hydrides and alumina-supported thorium hydrides have distinct roles in catalyzing different chemical reactions. Silica-supported zirconium hydrides are primarily used for H/D exchange reactions, where they activate C-H bonds and facilitate the exchange of hydrogen isotopes. On the other hand, alumina-supported thorium hydrides exhibit catalytic properties for alkane metathesis reactions, undergoing processes such as hydrogenolysis and chain walking to effect alkane rearrangements. 

Reaction rates and selectivities differ between the two systems. Silica-supported zirconium hydrides display faster H/D exchange rates, whereas alumina-supported thorium hydrides have slower alkane metathesis kinetics. Both systems utilize distinct mechanisms for C-H bond activation, incorporating elements such as σ-bond metathesis, hydrogenolysis, and chain walking. The catalytic properties of these hydrides depend on the type of hydride and the support material, with silica aiding in H/D exchange and alumina facilitating alkane metathesis. 

In summary, silica-supported zirconium hydrides are specialized for isotope exchange, while alumina-supported thorium hydrides are tailored for alkane rearrangement, showcasing different mechanisms and applications in C-H bond activation chemistry.
