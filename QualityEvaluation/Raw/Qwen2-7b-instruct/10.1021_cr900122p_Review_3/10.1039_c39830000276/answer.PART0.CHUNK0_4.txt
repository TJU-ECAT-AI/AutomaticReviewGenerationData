Answer: The literature describes the preparation, structure, and catalytic properties of lutetium-based complexes in C-H bond activation reactions. The alkyls and hydrides, while stable and isolatable, show a remarkable level of reactivity. The hydride (2) is readily formed by hydrogenolysis of (1). The activation of sp2 and sp3 C-H bonds occurs through both intra- and intermolecular reactions. The formation of products is thermodynamically disfavored, but can be forced by specific methods. Complex (4) is the only doubly metallated isomer formed due to steric reasons. Coordination of Lewis bases leads to further activation of the ligand. Pyridine coordination results in initial adduct formation followed by metallation at the 2-position of pyridine. Addition of CH2PPh3 leads to the formation of metallacycles. The hydride (2) acts as a catalyst for H/D exchange with tetramethylsilane. There are precedents for intramolecular activation of silyl-methyl C-H groups in uranium chemistry, and intermolecular activation of tetramethylsilane is common. The products of these C-H activation reactions are interesting complexes themselves, often not easily prepared by other routes.
