The catalytic hydrogenation of C═O bonds, especially with bimetallic catalysts like Pt-Ni systems, plays a crucial role in various industrial and research applications. These include fine chemical synthesis and cellulosic biomass conversion. Industrial applications may involve the production of pharmaceuticals, fragrances, and other chemicals. In research, these catalysts are pivotal for understanding and developing more efficient conversion processes from renewable sources, such as converting sugars and lignocellulose into valuable chemicals and biofuels. Methodologies employed in studying, optimizing, and enhancing the selectivity and efficiency of this process include theoretical modeling, such as density functional theory (DFT) calculations, to predict the behavior of mixed metal ensembles. Experimental approaches include the synthesis and characterization of bimetallic catalysts, testing their activity and selectivity in hydrogenation reactions, and monitoring the product distribution through chromatography and spectroscopic techniques. Additionally, the influence of reaction conditions, such as temperature, pressure, and the presence of promoters like ammonia, is systematically investigated to improve process efficiency.
