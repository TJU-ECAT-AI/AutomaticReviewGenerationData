These methodologies reveal the interplay between theoretical calculations and experimental studies, optimizing the selectivity and efficiency of the process.

Moreover, studies of the hydrogenation of propanal, a potential product of the hydrogenation of acrolein, offer insights into selectively hydrogenating α,β-unsaturated aldehyde CO bonds, aiding in the development of efficient catalysts for the hydrogenation of unsaturated aldehydes and the catalytic processing of cellulose.

This highlights the need for further exploration of catalyst formulations that can adapt to varying conditions, enhancing their performance in diverse applications. The introduction of tin notably increases both the reaction rate and the selectivity of crotylalcohol (SCO), demonstrating its potential in accelerating hydrogenation reactions and improving product selectivity.
