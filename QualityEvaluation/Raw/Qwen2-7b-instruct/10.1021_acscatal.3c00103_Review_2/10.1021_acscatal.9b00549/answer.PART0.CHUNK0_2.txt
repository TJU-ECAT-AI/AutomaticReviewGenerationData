<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Recent strategies in the development of supported metal catalysts for direct dehydrogenation processes involve the use of intermetallic alloys, core-shell structures, and surface modification techniques. These approaches aim to enhance catalyst stability, efficiency, and selectivity, addressing key challenges in industrial applications. Intermetallic alloys offer improved stability and activity due to unique properties like altered electronic configurations. Core-shell structures enable distinct active and inert components, mitigating deactivation risks. Surface modifications like protective layers or functional groups alter surface chemistry, reducing susceptibility to deactivation mechanisms such as coke formation or hydrogenolysis. These advancements collectively lead to more efficient and durable catalysts for industries including petrochemicals, refining, and renewable energy production.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
