<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Key strategies and approaches that have been recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes include the development of metal-support interactions, creation of highly dispersed active sites, and incorporation of additives. These advancements address the challenges in catalyst design, fabrication, and performance optimization for industrial applications by utilizing novel synthesis methods and incorporating multifunctional materials. Metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) serve as promising precursors for catalysts, enhancing both stability and selectivity. In-situ and operando characterization techniques aid in understanding the catalyst behavior under real conditions, enabling optimization of the catalyst design process.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
