<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Recent strategies and approaches to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes include the utilization of metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) as precursors for the synthesis of tailored catalysts. These MOF-derived catalysts offer enhanced structural control and porosity, crucial for optimizing catalytic performance. Another approach involves modifying the catalyst's surface chemistry through functionalization with specific ligands or co-catalysts to improve selectivity, stability, and activity by tuning the interaction between the active sites and reaction substrates. Additionally, the development of advanced fabrication methods, such as solvothermal synthesis and template-assisted growth, enables the creation of catalysts with superior geometric and electronic properties, collectively addressing the challenges in catalyst design, fabrication, and performance optimization to make the catalysts more suitable for industrial applications.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
