<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The key strategies and approaches that have been recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes involve the use of novel materials and confinement techniques. In the case of propane dehydrogenation, subsurface-regulated PtGa nanoparticles confined within silicalite-1 have been introduced. This innovative approach tackles the challenges in catalyst design, fabrication, and performance optimization for industrial applications by providing enhanced stability and activity. These advancements aim to overcome limitations such as high energy consumption, low selectivity, and rapid deactivation typically encountered in traditional dehydrogenation processes.
