<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The key strategies and approaches that have been recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes involve designing catalysts that balance high activity with long-term stability under harsh conditions. This includes confining metal nanoparticles within highly porous materials like silicalite-1, which provides a stable environment that mitigates issues such as sintering and aggregation. This study specifically introduces a novel approach by utilizing PtGa nanoparticles confined within silicalite-1, demonstrating enhanced stability compared to conventional Pt-based catalysts, making it a promising candidate for industrial applications in propane dehydrogenation.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
