<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The key strategies and approaches recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes include designing catalyst supports with tailored surface chemistry and morphology to improve interaction with the reaction medium, using noble metals like palladium and platinum for their high reactivity and selectivity, incorporating promoters to stabilize the catalyst structure and prevent sintering, and employing advanced synthesis methods such as atomic layer deposition and sol-gel processes for precise control over the catalyst's composition and microstructure. These advancements aim to improve efficiency, selectivity, and durability in industrial applications.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
