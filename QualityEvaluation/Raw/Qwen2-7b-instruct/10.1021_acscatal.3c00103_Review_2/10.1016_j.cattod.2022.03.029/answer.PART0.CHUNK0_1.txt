[1] "Recent studies have focused on developing new materials and modifying existing ones to improve the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts."
[2] "These advancements include the use of metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) and metal-organic polyhedra (MOPs) as supports for metal catalysts."
[3] "Another strategy involves the incorporation of heteroatoms into the catalyst structure to enhance its resistance to poisoning and promote better dehydrogenation efficiency."
[4] "Designing catalysts with tailored surface properties, such as creating specific active sites or modifying the surface morphology, has also proven effective."
[5] "Optimization of the catalyst synthesis conditions, including temperature, pressure, and the presence of additives, can significantly impact the catalytic activity and stability."

<EnglishVersionAnswer>

Key strategies and approaches that have been recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes include the use of metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) and metal-organic polyhedra (MOPs) as supports, incorporating heteroatoms into the catalyst structure, designing catalysts with tailored surface properties, and optimizing the catalyst synthesis conditions.
