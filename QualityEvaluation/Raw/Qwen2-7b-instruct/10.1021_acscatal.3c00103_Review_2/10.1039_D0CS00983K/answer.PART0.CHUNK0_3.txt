<EnglishVersionAnswer>

Recent advancements in direct dehydrogenation processes have focused on enhancing the stability of supported metal catalysts. Key strategies include developing highly coking and sintering-resistant platinum (Pt) catalysts, optimizing the long-term stability of new environmentally friendly catalysts, and exploring in situ characterization techniques for a deeper understanding of reaction mechanisms and active sites. These advancements aim to address challenges in catalyst design, fabrication, and performance optimization for industrial applications.
