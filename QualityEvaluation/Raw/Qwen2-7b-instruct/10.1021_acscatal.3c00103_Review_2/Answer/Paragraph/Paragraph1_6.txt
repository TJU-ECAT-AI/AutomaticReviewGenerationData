        Recent advancements in enhancing the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes involve developing advanced materials for non-oxidative propane dehydrogenation. Key focus is on synthesizing highly active, selective, and durable catalysts that address the challenge of reaction-induced loss of active sites by incorporating Silicalite-1 as a support material. This leads to improved thermal and chemical stability, enhancing the ZnOx/Silicalite-1 catalysts' activity and durability. Support material selection is crucial for designing efficient, long-lasting catalysts for direct dehydrogenation processes, as highlighted in recent studies.

        Strategies and approaches to minimize platinum usage while improving catalytic stability have been developed, focusing on optimizing metal dispersion, maximizing surface atoms for increased specific activity at low temperatures, and employing computational methods for structure prediction. Incorporating rare earth elements (REEs) alongside platinum (Pt) into the zeolite structure significantly increases the catalytic lifetime, contrary to initial expectations. The addition of lanthanum (La) and yttrium (Y) results in a more than tenfold increase in catalytic lifetime, and the formation of Pt3La and Pt3Y intermetallic nanoparticles on the degallated zeolite brings striking enhancements in all aspects of the PDH catalytic performance.

        The specific activity for hydrogenation of cyclopropane at 0°C on platinum catalysts was found to be independent of platinum content on two different forms of alumina, independent of the nature of the support with alumina or silica gel, and almost the same for highly dispersed samples in which nearly every platinum atom is a surface atom as for a platinum foil. However, this approach introduces a susceptibility to oxygen poisoning, challenging catalyst design and performance optimization for industrial use.

        Promoting propane dehydrogenation with CO2 over the PtFe bimetallic catalyst by eliminating the non-selective Fe(0) phase addresses challenges in catalyst design, fabrication, and performance optimization for industrial applications.

        最近，在直接脱氢过程中提高支持金属催化剂催化稳定性的进展涉及开发非氧化性丙烷脱氢的先进材料。重点是合成高度活性、选择性和耐用的催化剂，通过将Silicalite-1作为支撑材料来解决反应诱导活性位点损失的问题。这提高了热稳定性和化学稳定性，从而增强了ZnOx/Silicalite-1催化剂的活性和耐久性。支持材料的选择对于设计高效、持久的直接脱氢过程催化剂至关重要，这是近期研究强调的。

        减少铂使用量的同时提高催化剂稳定性的策略已经发展起来，这些策略包括优化金属分散度，最大化表面原子以在低温下提高特定活动性，并采用计算方法进行结构预测。通过将镧（La）和钇（Y）等稀土元素与铂（Pt）一起引入沸石结构，催化寿命显著增加了十倍以上，与最初的预期相反。添加镧（La）和钇（Y）导致了Pt3La和Pt3Y互金属纳米颗粒在去镓化沸石上的形成，带来了PDH催化性能在各个方面显著的增强。

        在0°C下，对环丙烷进行氢化所需的铂催化剂活性不受两种不同形式的氧化铝中的铂含量影响，也不受支撑材料为氧化铝或硅胶的影响，并且几乎相同于几乎每个铂原子都是表面原子的情况下的铂箔。然而，这种方法引入了对氧气中毒的敏感性，这挑战了工业应用中催化剂设计、制造和性能优化的挑战。

        通过消除非选择性Fe(0)相来促进丙烷脱氢的PtFe双金属催化剂，使用CO2进行了推广，这解决了催化剂设计、制造和性能优化方面的挑战，适用于工业应用。

        总结并展望：考虑到文件中讨论的先进成果和发现，存在探索新兴领域和创新方法的机会。未来研究努力可能集中在开发更高级的计算模型来预测催化剂在各种条件下的行为，探索多种金属在一个催化剂中协同作用的作用，以及改进合成技术以更好地控制催化剂的结构和性质。使用如金属有机框架（MOFs）和介孔二氧化硅上的金属纳米颗粒等先进材料，以及铂、钯等贵金属，为直接脱氢过程中的支持金属催化剂提供了有前景的途径来提高稳定性和效率。此外，环境条件，如氢气、蒸汽和软氧化剂CO2的存在，如何减轻焦炭形成，可以进一步调查以优化催化剂在实际工业应用中的性能和稳定性。
