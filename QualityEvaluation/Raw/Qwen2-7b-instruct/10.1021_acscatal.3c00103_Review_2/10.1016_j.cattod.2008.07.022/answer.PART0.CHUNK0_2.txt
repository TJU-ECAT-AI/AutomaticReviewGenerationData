Key strategies and approaches that have been recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes include:
1. Utilizing surface modifications such as heteroatom incorporation or defect site creation to improve catalyst-reactant interactions.
2. Developing novel support materials with tailored properties like enhanced surface area, improved thermal stability, and better metal-support compatibility.
3. Employing advanced synthesis methods for precise control over active site distribution and catalyst morphology.
4. Understanding kinetic and thermodynamic factors to predict catalyst behavior under various conditions and tailor catalyst properties accordingly.
5. Considering catalyst morphology's impact on mass transport and reaction kinetics to enhance overall performance.
6. Utilizing computational methods like DFT simulations for rational catalyst design based on atomic-level understanding.
7. Investigating catalyst deactivation mechanisms to remove poisons, prevent unwanted reactions, and mitigate the formation of deactivating species.
8. Optimizing catalyst regeneration protocols to restore activity after use, extending operational life and reducing replacement needs.
