<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The key strategies and approaches that have been recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes include the incorporation of protective ligands and the use of alloy catalysts. These methods help to shield the active metal sites from poisoning agents and harsh environments, thereby extending the operational life of the catalyst. Additionally, the design and fabrication of catalyst supports with tailored surface properties, along with advanced characterization techniques for understanding catalyst behavior during reactions, have also been crucial in performance optimization.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
