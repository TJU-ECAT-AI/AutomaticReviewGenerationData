The key strategies and approaches that have been recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes involve modifying the structure of the catalyst particles, specifically focusing on the arrangement and type of atoms within the particle. In the case of the 2 nm Pt3Mn nanoparticles studied, the researchers investigated the role of subsurface atoms in affecting the catalytic and adsorptive properties. By altering the composition of these subsurface atoms, they observed significant changes in the nanoparticle's properties, which could potentially lead to improved catalytic stability. These advancements aim to address the challenges in catalyst design, fabrication, and performance optimization for industrial applications by providing insights into how subtle structural modifications can significantly impact catalytic efficiency.
