The key strategies and approaches that have been recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes involve exploring the synergistic effect between different components, such as ZrO2 and CrOx. These advancements focus on optimizing the interaction between support materials and active metals through uniform dispersion of metal particles on the support surface. Performance optimization is achieved by tuning reaction conditions to maximize product yield while minimizing byproducts, thereby addressing the challenge of catalyst deactivation and improving overall efficiency in industrial applications.
