<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The key strategies and approaches recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes include modifying the support material to improve electron transfer and coordination properties around the metal center, utilizing alloying or doping techniques to create a more stable active site, and employing structural engineering of the catalyst, such as through the use of mesoporous supports, to increase both stability and selectivity. These advancements collectively aim to improve durability, reduce sintering, and enhance reaction kinetics, addressing the challenges in catalyst design, fabrication, and performance optimization for industrial applications.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
