Key strategies and approaches to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts include developing new catalyst designs with nanostructured supports, improving the dispersion of active metal species on the support surface, modifying the surface chemistry of the catalyst to reduce deactivation pathways, and creating self-supporting catalysts that eliminate the need for additional support materials.
2. Performance optimization for these catalysts involves tuning reaction conditions, such as temperature and pressure, to maximize the selectivity and yield of desired products.
