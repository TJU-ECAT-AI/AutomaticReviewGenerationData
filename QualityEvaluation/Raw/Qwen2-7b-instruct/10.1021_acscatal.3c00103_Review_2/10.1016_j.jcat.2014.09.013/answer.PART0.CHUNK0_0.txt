<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Key strategies and approaches that have been recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes include:
1. Utilizing support materials with high surface areas and porosities to improve metal particle dispersion and facilitate mass transfer.
2. Incorporating additives or modifiers into the catalyst composition to alter its electronic structure and enhance activity and stability.
3. Developing new metal-nanoparticle shapes and sizes to optimize active surface area and reduce agglomeration.
4. Employing advanced synthesis methods such as sol-gel, microwave-assisted, and plasma-based techniques to control the morphology and composition of catalyst precursors.
5. Integrating catalysts with appropriate support systems, like carbon fibers or zeolites, to improve thermal conductivity and stability under reaction conditions.
6. Leveraging computational methods to predict catalytic behavior, aiding in the selection and modification of catalysts for specific applications.
These advancements address challenges in catalyst design, fabrication, and performance optimization for industrial applications by improving stability, enhancing activity, controlling morphology, optimizing support interactions, and guiding material selection through predictive modeling.

增强直接脱氢过程中支持型金属催化剂催化稳定性的关键策略和方法包括：
1. 使用具有高比表面积和孔隙率的支持材料来改善金属颗粒的分散并促进质量传递。
2. 将添加剂或改性剂加入催化剂组成中，改变其电子结构，从而增强活性和稳定性。
3. 开发新的金属纳米粒子形状和尺寸，以优化活性表面面积并减少聚集。
4. 应用高级合成方法，如溶胶-凝胶、微波辅助和等离子体技术，控制催化剂前体的形态和组成。
5. 将催化剂与适当的支撑系统（如碳纤维或沸石）结合使用，以改善在反应条件下的热导率和稳定性。
6. 利用计算方法预测催化行为，指导特定应用中的材料选择和修改。
这些进步通过提高稳定性、增强活性、控制形态、优化支撑相互作用以及通过预测模型指导材料选择，解决了催化剂设计、制造和性能优化过程中的挑战，适用于工业应用。
