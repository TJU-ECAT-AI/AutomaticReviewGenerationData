<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The key strategies and approaches that have been recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes include modifying the support material to improve the dispersion of active metal sites and increasing the surface area. Researchers have also explored the use of alloy catalysts, which combine multiple metals to create a synergistic effect. Incorporating additives or modifiers to the catalyst system can also stabilize the active sites and reduce sintering. These strategies collectively aim to address the challenges in catalyst design, fabrication, and performance optimization for industrial applications.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
