          [3] "The use of noble metals, particularly palladium and platinum, has been emphasized due to their high activity and selectivity in dehydrogenation reactions."

      <English>
          Key strategies and approaches to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes involve improving both the intrinsic properties of the catalyst and the support material. Strategies include modifying the metal surface chemistry through treatments like thermal annealing or chemical modification to resist deactivation. Using noble metals like palladium and platinum due to their high activity and selectivity in dehydrogenation reactions, incorporating support materials with specific functionalities, optimizing reaction conditions (temperature, pressure, and co-catalyst presence), and exploring composite catalysts combining different metals or metal oxides to achieve synergistic effects and improve stability play crucial roles in maximizing catalytic efficiency and longevity.
