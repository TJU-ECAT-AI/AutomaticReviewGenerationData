<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Key strategies and approaches to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes involve improving the intrinsic properties of the catalyst and the support material through modifications such as thermal annealing, chemical modification, and the use of noble metals like palladium and platinum. Incorporating support materials with specific functionalities, optimizing reaction conditions, and utilizing composite catalysts are also crucial. These advancements aim to maximize catalytic efficiency and longevity by addressing the challenges in catalyst design, fabrication, and performance optimization for industrial applications.
