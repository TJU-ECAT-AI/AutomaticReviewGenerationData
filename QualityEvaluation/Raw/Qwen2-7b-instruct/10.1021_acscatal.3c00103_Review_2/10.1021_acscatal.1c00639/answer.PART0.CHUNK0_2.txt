<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The key strategies and approaches that have been recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes involve understanding the mechanisms behind the segregation of tin (Sn) atoms on platinum (Pt)-based catalysts. These strategies focus on forming Pt-Sn alloy nanoparticles, which are crucial for enhancing the stability of the catalysts. These advancements aim to address the challenges in catalyst design, fabrication, and performance optimization for industrial applications. The role of Sn segregation in improving the activity and stability of Pt-based catalysts has been extensively investigated.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
