<EnglishVersionAnswer>
To enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes, recent strategies and approaches have been developed focusing on several key areas. Firstly, researchers are modifying the support material to improve stability, recognizing the critical role of the support in maintaining the integrity of the catalyst structure. Secondly, optimizing the catalyst preparation process to precisely control the size and distribution of active sites ensures efficient utilization of the metal components. This approach aims to mitigate common issues such as sintering and activity loss over time, which are major challenges in catalyst design, fabrication, and performance optimization for industrial applications. Additionally, tailoring the electronic properties of the catalyst surface plays a crucial role in enhancing its reactivity and selectivity, ultimately contributing to more efficient and stable dehydrogenation processes.
