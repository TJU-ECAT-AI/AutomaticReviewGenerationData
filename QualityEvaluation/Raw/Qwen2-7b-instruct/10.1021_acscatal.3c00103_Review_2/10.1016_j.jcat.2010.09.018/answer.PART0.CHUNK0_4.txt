Key strategies and approaches that have been recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes include:

1. **Promotion with a Second Metal**: Using a second metal, such as tin (Sn), to promote the catalyst. Sn modifies the electronic properties of the Pt nanoparticles, affecting propane activation mechanisms and enhancing stability. 

2. **In-Situ Characterization Techniques**: Employing advanced in-situ characterization techniques like UVVis, Raman spectroscopy, and X-ray absorption spectroscopy to monitor the formation and evolution of coke deposits during the dehydrogenation process. These tools help correlate activity, stability, and selectivity with the formation of coke and the nature of the catalyst's electronic and structural properties.

3. **Modeling and Simulation**: Conducting numerical simulations to determine the coordination numbers and predict the particle morphology/bimetal distribution from experimental EXAFS analysis. This aids in understanding the structure-electronic-performance relationships and the dynamics of PtSn alloy formation.

4. **Understanding Coke Formation**: Investigating the nature and dynamics of coke formation on the catalyst surface. This includes analyzing the coke's structure and evolution during the reaction and regeneration phases, which is crucial for improving catalyst stability.

5. **Proposing Working Models**: Developing comprehensive models to explain the short- and long-term deactivation mechanisms of PtSn dehydrogenation catalysts. These models integrate insights from the above strategies to optimize catalyst design and fabrication for better performance under industrial conditions.

These advancements address the challenges in catalyst design, fabrication, and performance optimization by providing deeper understanding of the complex interactions between metals, coke formation, and catalyst activity, thus paving the way for the development of more stable and efficient dehydrogenation catalysts.

在直接脱氢过程中增强支持型金属催化剂稳定性的关键策略和方法包括：

1. **第二种金属的促进**：使用第二种金属，如锡（Sn），来促进催化剂。Sn通过修改Pt纳米粒子的电子性质，影响丙烷激活机制并增强稳定性。

2. **原位表征技术的应用**：采用先进的原位表征技术，如UVVis、拉曼光谱学和X射线吸收光谱学，来监测脱氢过程中的焦炭形成过程。这些工具有助于将活性、稳定性与催化剂表面焦炭的形成及其性质之间的相关性联系起来。

3. **建模与模拟**：进行数值模拟以确定第一二层配位数，并从实验XAFS分析中预测颗粒形态/双金属分布。这有助于理解结构-电子性能关系以及PtSn合金形成动力学。

4. **理解焦炭形成**：研究催化剂表面焦炭形成的状态和动态特性。这包括分析焦炭的结构及其在反应和再生阶段的演变，这对于提高催化剂稳定性至关重要。

5. **提出工作模型**：开发全面模型来解释PtSn脱氢催化剂的短期和长期失活机制。这些模型综合了上述策略的见解，以优化催化剂设计和制造，从而实现更稳定的工业条件下的性能。

这些进步通过提供对金属之间相互作用、焦炭形成与催化剂活性之间复杂关系的深入理解，为开发更稳定和高效的脱氢催化剂铺平了道路，从而解决了催化剂设计、制造和性能优化方面的挑战。
