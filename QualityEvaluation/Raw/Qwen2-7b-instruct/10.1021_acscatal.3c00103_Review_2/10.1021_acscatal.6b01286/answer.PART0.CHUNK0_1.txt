The key strategies and approaches that have been recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes involve understanding and controlling the structural evolution of metal species on the catalyst surface. These advancements aim to optimize the catalyst's performance by improving its activity and selectivity towards desired products like propylene. They address challenges in catalyst design, fabrication, and performance optimization for industrial applications through meticulous control over the catalyst's composition, structure, and reaction environment, ensuring better stability and efficiency under practical conditions.
