The key strategies and approaches that have been recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes involve the synthesis of novel catalyst materials, modification of existing ones, and the employment of first-principles calculations to predict reaction pathways and identify optimal catalyst structures. These strategies also encompass the incorporation of dopants, the use of alloy catalysts, and the engineering of support materials to enhance stability.
