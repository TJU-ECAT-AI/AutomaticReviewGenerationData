<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Key strategies and approaches that have been recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes include:
1) Enhancing the interaction between the metal and the support material.
2) Optimizing the composition of the catalyst.
3) Improving the surface properties of the catalyst.
These advancements address challenges such as:
1) Preventing deactivation due to sintering.
2) Overcoming poisoning by impurities.
3) Increasing selectivity.
First-principles calculations have been utilized to study the mechanisms of propane dehydrogenation over PtSn catalysts, providing valuable insights for understanding and predicting their behavior under different conditions.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
