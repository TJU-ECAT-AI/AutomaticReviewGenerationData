The key strategies and approaches that have been recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes include the synthesis of catalysts with improved stability, selectivity, and activity. These advancements often involve the use of alloying elements like tin (Sn) to modify the surface properties of metals such as platinum (Pt). Theoretical calculations, specifically first-principles calculations, have been utilized to gain insights into the electronic structure and reactivity of these catalysts.
