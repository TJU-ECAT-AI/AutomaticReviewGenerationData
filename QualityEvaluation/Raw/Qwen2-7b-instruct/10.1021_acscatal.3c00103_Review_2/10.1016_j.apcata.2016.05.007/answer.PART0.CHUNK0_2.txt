<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The key strategies and approaches that have been recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes include the development of new support materials, modification of metal surface properties, and the incorporation of dual-metal systems. These advancements aim to increase the catalyst's resistance to sintering and deactivation by utilizing porous supports like carbon or metal-organic frameworks (MOFs), employing surface modification techniques that deposit noble metals, and combining multiple metals in dual-metal systems to boost catalytic stability and reduce the risk of deactivation during high-temperature operations.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
