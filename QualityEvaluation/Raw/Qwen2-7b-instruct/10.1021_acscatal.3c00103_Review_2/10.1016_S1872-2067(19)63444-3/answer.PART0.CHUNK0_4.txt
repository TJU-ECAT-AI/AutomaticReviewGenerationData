Key strategies and approaches that have been recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes include the use of novel support materials, modification of metal surface properties, and the introduction of dual-metal systems. These strategies aim to improve catalyst performance through surface modification techniques, optimizing particle size and morphology, and refining fabrication methods. Performance optimization involves tailoring the catalyst composition, support material, and reaction conditions to achieve desired outcomes for specific industrial applications.
