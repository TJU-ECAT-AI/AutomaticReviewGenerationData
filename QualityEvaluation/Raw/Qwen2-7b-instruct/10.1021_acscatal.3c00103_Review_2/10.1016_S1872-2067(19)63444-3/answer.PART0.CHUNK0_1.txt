Key strategies and approaches recently developed to enhance the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes include the use of metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) as supports due to their high surface area and tunable pore structures, surface modifications such as doping with specific elements or functional groups to improve electron distribution and reduce deactivation pathways, mechanical activation and precise control over particle size for enhanced reactivity and durability, and innovations in fabrication techniques like spray-drying, plasma treatment, and sol-gel methods.
