Recent advancements in enhancing the stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes involve the use of transition metals like Zr, Co, V, and Zn as independent catalysts, rather than just promoters for Pt catalysts. 2. Unsatisfactory catalytic performance and rapid deactivation of Zn catalysts hinder their development in PDH reactions, necessitating improvements.

7. The small-sized PtZn particles (either alloys or mixed oxides) and high level of framework Zn species were identified on the 10Zn0.1Pt/HZ catalyst, facilitating its high catalytic activity at 525°C.

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Recent advancements in enhancing the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes focus on transitioning from conventional CrOx and Pt-based catalysts to utilizing transition metals like Zr, Co, V, and Zn independently or in bimetallic combinations. This addresses the challenges of toxicity, cost, and deactivation by improving selectivity, stability, and efficiency.

2. To overcome the limitations of Zn catalysts, researchers have explored the use of Pt-based catalysts with small particle sizes, which exhibit higher activity than bulk catalysts. The preparation of bimetallic PtZn catalysts through various promoters has proven to be an effective strategy to disperse and prevent sintering of small-sized Pt species, enhancing the catalyst's performance.

3. Gong's group introduced a novel approach by employing ZnO supported on Al2O3 in conjunction with a trace amount of Pt as the catalyst in PDH reactions, challenging the traditional belief that Pt was the active site and Zn merely a promoter. This demonstrates the potential of Lewis acidic Zn species in catalysis, providing a new avenue for designing stable dehydrogenation catalysts.

4. The study highlights the importance of achieving a balance between metal and acid functionalities in catalyst design. The ZnPt/HZ catalyst with a specific Zn/Pt ratio (1000 ppm Pt, 10 wt% Zn) exhibited superior catalytic performance at 525°C compared to single-metal catalysts and catalysts with different Zn/Pt ratios, showcasing the effectiveness of combining Pt and Zn in a synergistic manner for enhanced activity and stability in PDH reactions.
