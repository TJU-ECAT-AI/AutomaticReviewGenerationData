[1] "Catalyst support pore structures significantly influence coke formation, accommodation, and overall catalyst performance."
          [2] "Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions are sensitive to the pore size, connectivity, uniformity, and hierarchical arrangements of catalyst support."
          [3] "Coke formation is more likely in catalyst supports with larger pore sizes and lower connectivity."
          [4] "Hierarchical pore structures can enhance catalyst performance by providing better accommodation for reactants and products."
          [5] "Uniformity in pore size contributes to more consistent catalytic activity across the support surface."
          [6] "The implications for designing optimal and effective catalysts include considering the specific pore structure characteristics of the support material."

[1] "In this study, different oxidation patterns of coke over 3% Pt-Al2O3 and 3% Pt-SBA-15 catalysts were observed but the same catalytic performance was obtained in the dehydrogenation of propane."
          [2] "Coke can be removed by gasification with O2, H2O, CO2, and H2."
          [3] "The structure of the coke formed over the catalysts was the same."
          [4] "The oxidation temperatures of the first and second peaks in the TPO profiles of 3% Pt-Al2O3 were 465 and 513 C (at the shoulder), respectively, whereas the oxidation temperature in the TPO profiles of 3% Pt-SBA-15 was 556 C."
          [5] "From the XRD patterns and TPO profiles of the liberated cokes, we suggest that the cokes on both catalysts have the similar structure and are composed of very weakly crystallized turbostratic carbon."
          [6] "The oxidation temperature of coke before removing the supports should be equal to or lower than that of the liberated coke if the contact between Pt and coke was intimate."
          [7] "On 3% Pt-Al2O3, coke was located primarily on both the metal-support boundary and the support, while coke was located mainly on the support in the case of 3% Pt-SBA-15."
          [8] "The oxidation of coke located near metallic sites was catalyzed, resulting in an oxidation peak at a lower temperature."
          [9] "However, the oxidation of coke located far from metallic sites was controlled by oxygen mobility which results from the nature of the supports."
          [10] "The mobility of activated oxygen species on the surface of Al2O3 was much higher than that of SiO2."
