<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques, such as XRD, XANES, EXAFS, and ADF-STEM with EELS, synergistically contribute to our understanding of the structural properties, oxidation states, spatial distribution, and morphological characteristics of active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts. These techniques provide insights into the catalysts' behavior under different conditions, revealing key differences between the two catalyst types that impact their catalytic activity. The findings have significant implications for optimizing the composition and structure of catalysts to improve propylene production from propane dehydrogenation with CO2.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
