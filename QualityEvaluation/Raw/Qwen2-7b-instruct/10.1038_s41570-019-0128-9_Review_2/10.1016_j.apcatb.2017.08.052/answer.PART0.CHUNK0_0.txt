<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques, such as XRD, XANES, EXAFS, and ADF-STEM with EELS, contribute to our understanding of the structural properties, oxidation states, spatial distribution, and morphological characteristics of active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts by providing a comprehensive view of their behavior. These methods have shown that the active sites in both catalysts display different structural features that significantly impact their performance in converting propane to propylene. The combination of in situ and ex situ techniques allows for a detailed examination of catalyst behavior under various conditions, offering insights into the underlying mechanisms.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
