<quotes>
[1] "Propane adsorbed weakly and reversibly on supported Mo2C below 273 K."
[2] "A stronger interaction occurred above 300 K, when vibrations of adsorbed propylene and propylidyne were observed."
[3] "Mo2C dispersed on silica is an effective dehydrogenating catalyst for propane: the highest selectivity to propylene is 50% at a conversion of 25% at 873 K."
[4] "The reaction pathway of propane is completely different over Mo2C/ZSM-5. In this case the dominant reaction is the aromatization of propane."
</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Propane binds to Mo2C catalysts in a weak and reversible manner at temperatures below 273 K. The interaction strengthens above 300 K, leading to the observation of adsorbed propylene and propylidyne vibrations.
2. When dispersed on silica, Mo2C acts as an efficient dehydrogenating catalyst for propane, achieving the highest propylene selectivity of 50% at a 25% conversion rate at 873 K.
3. The process of propane reacting over Mo2C/ZSM-5 differs significantly from previous observations. Instead of dehydrogenation, the main reaction is the aromatization of propane.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

<ChineseVersionAnswer>
1. 在低于273 K的温度下，丙烷以弱且可逆的方式吸附在Mo2C催化剂上。当温度高于300 K时，观察到吸附的丙烯和丙基炔振动，说明相互作用增强。
2. 将Mo2C分散在硅酸盐上，可以作为有效的脱氢催化剂用于丙烷，最高丙烯选择性达到50%，在873 K时丙烷转化率为25%。
3. 在Mo2C/ZSM-5上的丙烷反应过程与之前的观察结果完全不同。主要反应是丙烷的芳香化。
</ChineseVersionAnswer>
