<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The three possible reforming processes for the conversion of methane to syngas are:
1. Steam reforming: CH4 + H2O → CO + 3H2, with a ΔH of -206 kJ/mol at 298 K.
2. Dry reforming (CO2 reforming): CH4 + CO2 → 2CO + 2H2, with a ΔH of -247 kJ/mol at 298 K.
3. Partial oxidation of methane to syngas: CH4 + 0.5O2 → CO + 2H2, with a ΔH of -36 kJ/mol at 298 K.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

In the oxidative coupling of methane (OCM) reaction, the conversion of methane into ethane, ethylene, and water is described by:
2CH4 + 0.5O2 → CH3CH3 + H2O, with a ΔH of -42.4 kJ/mol at 298 K (Equation 5)
2CH4 + O2 → CH2CH2 + 2H2O, with a ΔH of -67.4 kJ/mol at 298 K (Equation 6).

These equations represent the direct conversion of methane into ethane and ethylene.
