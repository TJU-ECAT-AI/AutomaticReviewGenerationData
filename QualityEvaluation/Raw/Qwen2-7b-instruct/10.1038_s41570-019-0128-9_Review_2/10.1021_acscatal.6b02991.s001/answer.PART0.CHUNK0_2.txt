<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques, including XRD, XANES, EXAFS, and ADF-STEM with EELS, synergistically contribute to our understanding of the structural properties, oxidation states, spatial distribution, and morphological characteristics of active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts by providing detailed insights into the catalyst's behavior under operational conditions. These methodologies reveal the crystalline structure of the support material, CeO2, and elucidate the local coordination environment of the metal species. Comparative studies highlight distinct structural features and electronic configurations that impact the catalysts' performance in specific reactions. Key differences in the catalysts' morphology, such as the size and shape of the metal particles, influence their reactivity and stability. Tailored synthesis methods could lead to the development of more efficient catalysts with improved activity and longevity.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
