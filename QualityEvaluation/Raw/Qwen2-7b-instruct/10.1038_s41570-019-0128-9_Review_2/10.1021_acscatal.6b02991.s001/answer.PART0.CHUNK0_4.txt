<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques, such as XRD, XANES, EXAFS, and ADF-STEM with EELS, work together to provide insights into the structural properties, oxidation states, spatial distribution, and morphological characteristics of active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts. These techniques allow for a comprehensive understanding of the catalysts' microstructure, which is essential for optimizing their performance. The synergy between these methods enables researchers to correlate the catalysts' morphology with their activity and stability, leading to improved catalyst design. Comparative studies reveal differences in active site characteristics between the two types of catalysts, which have implications for their performance.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
