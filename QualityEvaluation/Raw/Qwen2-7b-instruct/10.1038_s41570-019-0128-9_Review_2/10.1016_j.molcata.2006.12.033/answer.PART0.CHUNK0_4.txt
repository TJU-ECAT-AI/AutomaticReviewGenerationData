<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The study aimed to enhance the ethylene selectivity during the dehydrogenation of ethane with carbon dioxide on a Cr2O3/ZrO2 nano-composite catalyst by incorporating Fe, Ni, Mn, and Co oxides. The surface properties of the catalyst, including the distribution of chromium species, oxygen species, and the acid-base characteristics, collectively influenced the catalytic activity in the conversion of ethane to ethylene using carbon dioxide as an oxidant. The catalysts' performance was ranked based on ethylene yield at 650°C, with Fe5-Cr10/Zr exhibiting the highest activity, followed by Mn5-Cr10/Zr, Co5-Cr10/Zr, and Ni5-Cr10/Zr.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
