<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques provided insights into the structural properties, oxidation states, spatial distribution, and morphological characteristics of active sites in the modified Cr2O3/ZrO2 nano-composite catalysts. These techniques revealed that the nature of the modifier influenced the catalyst's textural and structural properties, distribution of chromium species, oxygen species, and acid/base properties on the catalyst surface. These factors collectively determined the catalytic activity of the modified Cr2O3/ZrO2 nano-composite catalysts in the dehydrogenation of ethane with carbon dioxide. Specifically, the order of catalyst activity at 650°C was Fe5-Cr10/Zr > Mn5-Cr10/Zr > Co5-Cr10/Zr > Ni5-Cr10/Zr, highlighting the impact of the modifier on catalytic performance.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
