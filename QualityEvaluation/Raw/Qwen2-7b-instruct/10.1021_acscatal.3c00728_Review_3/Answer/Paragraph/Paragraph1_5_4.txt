The intrinsic activity of these isolated Cr species is higher than those observed on γ-Al2O3 (1 wt% Cr). For xCrSBA-15, isolated Cr(III) sites with coordination number greater than four are more active, selective and stable than Cr sites on the surface of crystalline Cr2O3. Contrarily, for xCrAl2O3, oligomeric Cr species are more active and selective than the isolated Cr sites. It is reasonable to say that active DHP sites are generated during the reaction, the origin of which are Cr(VI) species in coordination reflected by the pre-edge peak in XANES. Analyzing B-based materials featuring 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks reveals enhancements in catalytic performance through increased surface area and improved reactant access.
