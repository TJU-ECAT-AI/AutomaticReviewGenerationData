        The synthesis and characterization of B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks have been extensively studied, revealing their enhanced catalytic performance, particularly in the oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) of propane. These materials, characterized by increased surface area and efficient mass transfer, demonstrate superior selectivity for propylene formation. The incorporation of boron active components into these structures is achieved through various methods, including impregnation, solvothermal synthesis, and sonochemical methods. The effectiveness of these methods varies based on the specific catalytic application and the desired product properties.

        For instance, Xiao Jiang et al. successfully synthesized B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks, which significantly boost catalytic efficiency (DOI: 10.1021/jacsau.1c00542). Similarly, Lu et al. noted the importance of boron incorporation for catalytic enhancement, with the 3-D dispersed active sites significantly improving catalytic activity (DOI: 10.1016/0926-860X(95)00273-1).

        The presence of BOH groups on h-BN and boron nitrous nanotubes surfaces enhances catalytic performance in ODH reactions, acting as active sites. This is further supported by the structural analysis of the BNOH catalyst, which reveals numerous accessible edges and lattice relaxations/distortions at the edges, contributing to enhanced catalytic activity (DOI: 10.1039/D0CS01174F).

        The synthesis and characterization of silica-supported boron oxide catalysts for the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane have been studied, demonstrating effective incorporation methods that vary in effectiveness for different catalytic applications (DOI: 10.1021/acs.jpcc.9b07429).

        The coordination environment plays a crucial role in determining the activity and stability of atomically dispersed Pt catalysts, with vapor-phase deposition techniques effectively integrating boron while maintaining the desired 3-D dispersion (DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.9b04558).

        The presence of BOH groups was also reported in the study of ODH of propane on h-BN and boron nitrous nanotubes through the characterization of materials in their fresh and spent forms by means of advanced experimental techniques, further highlighting their catalytic advantages (DOI: 10.1021/jacs.2c12136).

        The influence of reactor parameters on the boron nitride-catalyzed oxidative dehydrogenation of propane also underscores the importance of understanding the structural underpinnings of B-based materials for optimized catalytic processes (DOI: 10.1021/acs.oprd.8b00301).

        These studies collectively demonstrate the pivotal role of boron-based materials in catalysis, particularly in enhancing the efficiency of ODH reactions through their unique structural characteristics and incorporation methods.

        B基于材料在具有三维分散活性位点的多孔框架中的合成与表征揭示了其催化性能的显著提升，尤其是在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODH）反应中的应用。这些材料通过增加表面积和改善质量传输特性，显示出对丙烯的高选择性形成能力。将硼活性成分引入这些结构是通过浸渍、溶剂热合成和超声化学方法等实现的。这些方法的有效性取决于特定催化应用及其最终产品的期望属性。

        小乔等人的研究成功合成了具有三维分散活性位点的多孔框架中的B基于材料，并证明了它们的催化效率显著提高（DOI：10.1021/jacsau.1c00542）。同样，卢等人强调了硼掺杂对于催化增强的重要性，三维分散的活性位点显著提高了催化活性（DOI：10.1016/0926-860X(95)00273-1）。

        在h-BN和硼氮化物纳米管表面存在的BOH基团增强了ODH反应中的催化性能，作为活性位点。这一结论进一步得到了结构分析BNOH催化剂的支持，该催化剂揭示了边缘上大量的可访问边缘和边缘上的晶格松弛和变形，从而促进了增强的催化活性（DOI：10.1039/D0CS01174F）。

        硅基支持的硼氧化物催化剂用于丙烷氧化脱氢的研究也表明了有效掺杂方法的有效性，这些方法在不同催化应用中的有效性各不相同（DOI：10.1021/acs.jpcc.9b07429）。

        前提环境在确定原子分散铂催化剂的活动性和稳定性方面起着关键作用，气相沉积技术有效地整合硼同时保持所需三维分散（DOI：10.1021/acscatal.9b04558）。

        在硼氮化物催化丙烷氧化脱氢的反应参数影响研究中也强调了理解B基于材料结构基础对于优化催化过程的重要性（DOI：10.1021/acs.oprd.8b00301）。

        这些研究共同证明了硼基材料在催化领域的关键作用，尤其是通过其独特的结构特性和掺杂方法来提高ODH反应效率。
