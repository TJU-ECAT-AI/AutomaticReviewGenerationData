        The structural characteristics of B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks significantly enhance catalytic performance, particularly in propane dehydrogenation (ODHP). These materials, as exemplified in studies referenced by DOI 10.1126/science.abe7935 and DOI 10.1021/jacs.2c12136, exhibit unique properties such as high surface area, efficient mass transfer, and optimal reactivity, which are crucial for catalytic applications. The incorporation of B active components into these structures through methods like impregnation, ion exchange, and covalent attachment varies in effectiveness for different applications, as discussed in DOI 10.1021/acscatal.2c01622.

        For instance, the BS-1 catalyst, synthesized via a solvent-free crystallization process with tetrapropylammonium (TPA+) hydroxide at 180°C, showcases a Si/B ratio of 62 after calcination, revealing its potential for enhanced catalytic activity. Its active sites, distributed throughout a porous framework, enable efficient mass transfer and reaction kinetics, leading to increased propane conversion efficiency, as observed at temperatures of 540°C and 560°C, with conversions of 23.8% and 41.4%, respectively. Furthermore, the BS-1 catalyst maintains its high performance after equivalent treatment, as demonstrated in a continuous ODHP test, producing up to 10 vol% water in the gas stream.

        The 11B MAS NMR spectroscopy technique has been instrumental in elucidating the spatial proximity between boron and hydrogen species within the BS-1 catalyst, providing insights into its catalytic mechanism. This study highlights the importance of boron's structural characteristics in enhancing catalytic performance and offers a foundation for future research in this field.

        Other studies, including those referenced by DOI 10.1021/jacs.2c12136 and DOI 10.1021/acscatal.2c01622, have investigated the synthesis of antiexfoliating h-BNIn2O3 catalysts and the role of boron nitrous nanotubes in increasing olefins for propane oxidative dehydrogenation. These catalysts, with their 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks, demonstrate enhanced catalytic performance and the critical role of boron incorporation methods in optimizing their effectiveness across various applications.

        The structural characteristics of B-based materials, as described in DOI 10.1016/0926-860X(95)00273-1 and DOI 10.1021/jacs.1c13563, significantly contribute to their catalytic advantages, with the 3-D dispersed active sites playing a pivotal role in improving catalytic activity. Different methods for incorporating B active components, including impregnation, ion exchange, and covalent attachment, have been explored, each demonstrating varying effectiveness across different catalytic applications.

        Additional studies, such as those referenced by DOI 10.1039/D0CS01174F, have focused on the presence of BOH groups on h-BN and BNNT surfaces, which act as active sites in ODH reactions. The inertness of intact boron nitrides becomes active through in situ surface functionalization, highlighting the importance of boron's structural characteristics in catalytic performance.

        Carbon nanotubes, as discussed in DOI 10.1002/anie.201507119, have shown improved performance in converting light alkanes, achieving higher yields compared to traditional catalysts, further emphasizing the significance of 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks for B-based materials.

        In summary, the structural characteristics of B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks enhance catalytic performance through increased surface area, optimized mass transfer, and optimal reactivity. Incorporation methods vary, differing in effectiveness across different catalytic applications. Key insights come from studies highlighting the materials' unique structure, demonstrating superior efficiency in catalytic reactions, and the importance of selecting appropriate methods for specific applications.

        Looking ahead, the findings from these studies suggest several potential pathways and pioneering research opportunities. Future research might focus on exploring the dynamic self-dispersion of boron clusters on MFI zeolite nanosheets, engineering boron species for improved olefin production, and refining boron incorporation methods to suit different chemical processes. The integrative understanding of these findings paves the way for a forward-thinking outlook, emphasizing the potential for breakthroughs in the field of catalysis and the development of more efficient and sustainable chemical processes.

    <Chinese>
        B基于材料在多维分散活性位点的孔框架结构中展现出独特的结构特性，显著增强了催化性能，尤其是在丙烷脱氢（ODHP）反应中的应用。这些材料，如通过DOI 10.1126/science.abe7935和DOI 10.1021/jacs.2c12136研究中的实例所展示的，具有高表面面积、优化的质量传输特性和最佳反应性等独特属性，对催化应用至关重要。不同方法（如浸渍、离子交换和共价结合）用于将硼活性成分引入这些结构中，在不同应用中的有效性各不相同，正如DOI 10.1021/acscatal.2c01622研究中所述。

        例如，BS-1催化剂，通过溶剂自由结晶过程使用四丙基铵（TPA+）氢氧化物在180°C下进行结晶后，显示了62的Si/B比例，在煅烧去除有机模板后，揭示了其增强催化活性的潜力。其分散在整个孔框架中的活性位点能够有效促进质量传输和反应动力学，导致在540°C和560°C时的丙烷转换效率提高，分别为23.8%和41.4%。此外，BS-1催化剂在等效处理后保持其高性能，在连续ODHP测试中，气体流中产生高达10 vol%的水。

        11B MAS核磁共振光谱技术在解析BS-1催化剂中硼和氢物种之间的空间接近度方面发挥了关键作用，为理解其催化机制提供了洞察。这项研究强调了硼的结构特征在催化性能提升中的重要性，并为这一领域的未来研究奠定了基础。

        其他研究，如通过DOI 10.1021/jacs.2c12136和DOI 10.1021/acscatal.2c01622研究，探讨了抗剥离h-BNIn2O3催化剂的合成以及硼硝酸纳米管在增加丙烷氧化脱氢制乙烯中的作用。这些催化剂，具有多维分散活性位点的孔框架结构，展示了增强的催化性能，并强调了将硼活性成分有效集成到结构中的方法对于不同催化应用的有效性差异。

        进一步的研究，如DOI 10.1039/D0CS01174F中所述，强调了h-BN和BNNT表面上存在的BOH组分作为ODH反应中的活性位点的重要性。原本惰性的完整硼氮化物通过原位表面功能化变得活跃，突显了硼结构特征在催化性能中的重要作用。

        碳纳米管，如在DOI 10.1002/anie.201507119中所述，显示出在转化轻烷烃方面的改进性能，与传统催化剂相比，实现了更高的产率，进一步强调了孔框架中多维分散活性位点对B基于材料的重要意义。

        总之，B基于材料在多维分散活性位点的孔框架结构中通过增加表面面积、优化质量传输和最佳反应性来增强催化性能。不同的集成方法各具特色，适用于不同的催化应用。来自这些研究的关键见解表明，材料的独特结构在催化反应中表现出优越的效率，并强调了根据特定应用选择适当集成方法的重要性。

        未来展望，这些发现提出了几个潜在路径和开创性的研究机会。未来研究可能集中在探索MFI沸石纳米片上动态自分散的硼簇、工程硼物种以提高烯烃产量，以及细化硼集成方法以适应不同化学过程。整合这些发现提供了一个前瞻性视角，强调催化领域突破的可能性和更高效、可持续化学过程的发展前景。
