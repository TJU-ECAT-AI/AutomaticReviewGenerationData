Analyzing the structural characteristics of B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks involves understanding how these features enhance catalytic performance. The presence of these sites allows for a more efficient distribution of the active components across the material's surface and volume, increasing the likelihood of contact with reactants. This leads to improved activity and selectivity in various catalytic processes. The incorporation of B active components into these structures is typically achieved through various methods, including impregnation, sol-gel processes, or covalent attachment to the framework. Each method has its advantages and limitations depending on the specific application. For instance, impregnation might offer better control over the loading of B species but could lead to aggregation and loss of activity. In contrast, covalent attachment ensures more stable dispersion but might require complex synthesis procedures. The effectiveness of these methods varies significantly based on factors such as the type of B species, the structure of the porous framework, and the catalytic conditions.

The proposed oxygen-terminated armchair BN edge site as an active site for the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reaction is a novel finding. This site is characterized by its one-dimensional nature, which contributes to the observed favorable olefin selectivity. Unlike traditional 0D single-site catalysts that can create highly reactive propyl radicals, leading to overoxidation, the 1D nature of the armchair edge avoids the formation of such radicals. Additionally, the suppression of overoxidation is attributed to the stabilization of adsorbed species, which prevents the diffusion of reactants and products that could lead to unwanted side reactions. The radical rebound mechanism, involving the abstraction of hydrogen atoms and subsequent radical interactions, further elucidates the pathway of the ODHP reaction on the BN surface. This mechanism not only explains the high olefin selectivity but also suggests a potential for efficient water desorption and regeneration of the active site, contributing to the overall catalytic cycle.

In conclusion, the structural characteristics of B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks significantly enhance catalytic performance by optimizing the distribution of active components, facilitating efficient reactant access, and promoting desired reaction pathways. 分析硼基材料中三维分散活性位点在多孔框架中的结构特征，对于理解这些特征如何增强催化性能至关重要。这些位点的存在允许活性成分在整个材料表面和体积上的更有效分布，从而增加与反应物接触的可能性，进而提高各种催化过程的活性和选择性。将硼活性成分整合到这些结构中的方法通常包括浸渍、溶胶-凝胶过程或共价连接等。每种方法都有其优势和局限性，取决于特定的应用。例如，浸渍可能提供更好的活性成分负载控制，但可能导致聚集和活性丧失。相比之下，共价连接确保了更稳定的分散，但可能需要复杂的合成程序。这些方法的有效性根据硼物种类型、多孔框架结构和催化条件等因素显著不同。

作为氧化脱氢反应中丙烷生成丙烯（ODHP）反应的活性位点的提议氧终止的臂状BN边缘是一个新颖的发现。该位点的特点是其一维性质，这有助于观察到有利的烯烃选择性。与传统0D单个位点催化剂可以创建高度反应性的丙基自由基，导致过氧化，臂状边缘的1D性质避免了形成此类自由基。此外，抑制过氧化归因于吸附物种的稳定化，防止扩散反应物和产物，可能导致不必要的副反应。自由基反弹机制，涉及氢原子的抽象和随后的自由基相互作用，进一步阐明了BN表面上ODHP反应的途径。这一机制不仅解释了高烯烃选择性，而且暗示了水高效脱附和活性位点再生的潜力，对整个催化循环具有重要意义。

综上所述，硼基材料中三维分散活性位点在多孔框架中的结构特征通过优化活性成分的分布、促进有效反应物访问并促进所需反应路径来显著增强催化性能。将硼活性成分整合到这些结构中的方法通过适应特定催化应用的要求而有所不同。提出的臂状BN边缘上的活性位点为ODHP反应提供了一个独特解决方案，显示了高烯烃选择性和有前景的催化活性。自由基反弹机制提供了关于反应途径的见解，并且暗示了水高效脱附的潜力，突显了硼基材料在可持续和高效催化技术发展中开发的潜力。
