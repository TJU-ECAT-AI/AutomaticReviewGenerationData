[1] "Supported Boron Oxide Catalysts for Selective and Low-Temperature Oxidative Dehydrogenation of Propane."
[2] "The development of efficient catalysts for selective and low-temperature oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) of propane has been a significant challenge in the chemical industry."
[3] "In this study, we report on the synthesis and characterization of a series of boron oxide catalysts supported on various metal oxides, including γ-Al2O3, SiO2, and TiO2."
[4] "The catalysts were characterized by X-ray diffraction (XRD), nitrogen adsorption-desorption, and temperature-programmed reduction (TPR) to investigate their structural properties."
[5] "The results showed that the 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks significantly enhance the catalytic performance of the B-based materials."
[6] "The incorporation of B active components into these structures was achieved through various methods, including impregnation, sol-gel, and co-precipitation."
[7] "The effectiveness of these methods varied depending on the specific catalytic application, with some methods being more suitable for certain types of reactions than others."
</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Analyzing the structural characteristics of B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks reveals that these features significantly enhance catalytic performance for selective and low-temperature oxidative dehydrogenation of propane. The incorporation of B active components into these structures was achieved through impregnation, sol-gel, and co-precipitation methods, with varying effectiveness for different catalytic applications.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
