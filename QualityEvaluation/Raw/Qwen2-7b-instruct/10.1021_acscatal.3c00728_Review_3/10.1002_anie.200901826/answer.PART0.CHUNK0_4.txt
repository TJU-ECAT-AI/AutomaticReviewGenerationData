The literature highlights the use of carbon nanotubes (CNTs) modified by boron oxide as catalysts for the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane. The boron oxide modifies the surface of CNTs, enabling a feasible investigation of the reaction mechanism and providing a sustainable technology for alkane conversion. The enhanced selectivity towards propene formation is attributed to the blocking or covering of combustion sites on the surface, which suppresses the formation of non-selective oxygen species. The method of incorporating boron into the CNT structure involves impregnation with aqueous solutions of boron compounds, leading to a dispersion of boron oxide particles throughout the CNT framework. This approach results in 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks, enhancing the catalytic performance by providing a larger surface area for reactions and facilitating the diffusion of reactants and products. The effectiveness of this method is demonstrated through comparisons with various metal-based catalysts, showing that boron-modified CNTs offer a sustainable metal-free strategy for propene synthesis with high selectivity and stability.

Critical evaluation of the method involves assessing the stability and reactivity of the boron oxide-modified CNTs under different reaction conditions. The literature shows that the samples maintain stable catalytic performance after a few hours, indicating good stability. The method is adaptable for kinetic and mechanistic investigations of complex redox reactions, providing insights into the nature of the oxidative dehydrogenation reactions. The effectiveness of the method for different catalytic applications depends on factors such as the type of alkane substrate, the desired product, and the reaction conditions. For instance, the use of ethylbenzene as a substrate in carbon-catalyzed oxidative dehydrogenation reactions is highlighted, demonstrating the versatility of the method in producing high yields of styrene. The method's relative effectiveness compared to traditional metal oxide catalysts is evident in the enhanced selectivity and stability exhibited by boron-modified CNTs, suggesting potential for wider adoption in industrial alkane conversion processes.

文献强调了通过硼氧化物改性的碳纳米管（CNT）作为丙烷氧化脱氢反应的催化剂。硼氧化物修改了CNT表面，使得对反应机制的可行研究成为可能，并提供了可持续的烷烃转换技术。硼氧化物增强丙烯形成选择性的原因归结为对表面燃烧点的覆盖或阻塞，这抑制了非选择性氧物种的生成。将硼引入CNT结构的方法是通过用水溶液浸渍硼化合物，导致硼氧化物颗粒在整个CNT骨架中分散。这种方法产生了多维分散在孔框架中的活性位点，通过提供更大的反应表面和促进反应物和产物的扩散来增强催化性能。文献通过与各种金属基催化剂的比较，展示了硼改性CNT的可持续无金属策略在高选择性和稳定性方面具有优势。

对于方法的批判性评价涉及评估硼氧化物修饰CNT在不同反应条件下的稳定性和反应性。文献显示样品在几个小时后仍保持稳定的催化性能，表明良好的稳定性。该方法适应于复杂氧化还原反应的动态和机制研究，提供了深入了解氧化脱氢反应性质的见解。该方法在不同催化应用中的相对有效性取决于多种因素，如烷烃底物的类型、所需产物以及反应条件。例如，文献中提到使用乙苯作为底物在碳催化氧化脱氢反应中的应用，表明了该方法在生产高产率的苯乙烯方面的灵活性。与传统金属氧化物催化剂相比，该方法的相对有效性体现在硼改性CNT的增强选择性和稳定性上，暗示其在工业烷烃转化过程中的广泛应用潜力。
