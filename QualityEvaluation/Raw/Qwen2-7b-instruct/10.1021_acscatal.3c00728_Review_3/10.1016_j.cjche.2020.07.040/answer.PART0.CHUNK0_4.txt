The document analyzes the structural characteristics of B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks, emphasizing how these features enhance catalytic performance through efficient dispersion and firm attachment of the catalytically active B2O3 layer on the modified SiO2@HC support, and improved mass transfer facilitated by the honeycomb structure. The incorporation of B active components is achieved through a two-step wash-coating method, which is found to be effective for various catalytic applications. The monolithic structure ensures a uniform flow distribution, optimally utilizes the active component, shortens diffusion paths, and restrains overoxidation. This results in high selectivity towards olefins and negligible production of CO2 under high space velocities, leading to a high propylene productivity. The document critically evaluates the methods used for incorporating B active components into these structures, highlighting their relative effectiveness for different catalytic applications, such as oxidative dehydrogenation of propane. The findings suggest that the B2O3/SiO2@HC monolithic catalyst can be potentially recognized for industrial application of ODHP, overcoming issues related to high pressure drop and poor mass transfer in particulate boron-based catalysts.
