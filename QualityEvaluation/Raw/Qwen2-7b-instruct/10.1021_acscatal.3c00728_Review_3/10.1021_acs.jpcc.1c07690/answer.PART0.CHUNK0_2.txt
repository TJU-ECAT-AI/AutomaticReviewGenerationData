<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. The study utilized density functional theory (DFT) calculations to explore the interaction between on- and off-surface processes during the B2O3-catalyzed oxidative dehydrogenation of propane. It was found that B-based materials with three-dimensional dispersed active sites in porous frameworks exhibit enhanced catalytic performance due to an increase in surface area and improved accessibility to reactants.
2. The methods for integrating B active components into these structures included chemical vapor deposition and impregnation techniques. These approaches are effective for different catalytic applications depending on the specific requirements and properties sought in the final product.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
