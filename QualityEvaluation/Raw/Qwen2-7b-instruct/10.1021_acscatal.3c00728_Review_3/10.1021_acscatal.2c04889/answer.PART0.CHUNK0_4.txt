<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Analyzing the structural characteristics of B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks reveals that these features significantly enhance catalytic performance. The incorporation of boron (B) active components into these structures leverages the unique electronic and geometric properties of B. Various methods, including solvothermal synthesis, sonochemical synthesis, and microwave-assisted synthesis, have been utilized to integrate B active components into the materials. The effectiveness of these methods for different catalytic applications depends on factors such as reaction conditions, material stability, and the desired product selectivity.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
