<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Analyzing the structural characteristics of B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks, these features significantly enhance catalytic performance. The ease of access to reactants and products, improved diffusion, and optimized utilization of active sites contribute to the effectiveness of these materials. Different methods such as impregnation, co-precipitation, and ion exchange are utilized to incorporate B active components into these structures. The choice of method depends on the specific catalytic application, with varying degrees of effectiveness observed.
