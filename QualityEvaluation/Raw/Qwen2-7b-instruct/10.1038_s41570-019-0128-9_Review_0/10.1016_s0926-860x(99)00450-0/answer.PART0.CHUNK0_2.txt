Various support materials such as SiO2, ZrO2, Al2O3, and TiO2 affect the activity, selectivity, stability, and mechanistic pathways of chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions. SiO2-supported catalysts show the highest activity, followed by ZrO2, Al2O3, and TiO2. Increasing chromium loading up to 8 wt.% optimizes the activity. The nature of the support impacts the redox properties and chromium species, influencing the catalyst's behavior.

2. The key challenges in optimizing the overall performance of these catalysts involve balancing activity, selectivity, and stability. The trade-offs might include selecting the right support material that enhances the redox properties and chromium species, ensuring efficient CO2 activation and conversion, and maintaining the catalyst's stability over multiple reactions.

3. The stability of the catalysts is crucial for long-term efficiency in industrial applications. The trade-off here involves finding a balance between high activity and long-term stability. For instance, while SiO2-supported catalysts might offer high conversion and selectivity, they might experience faster deactivation compared to others. Optimizing the catalyst design could involve modifying the support material or the chromium loading to improve stability without compromising activity and selectivity.
