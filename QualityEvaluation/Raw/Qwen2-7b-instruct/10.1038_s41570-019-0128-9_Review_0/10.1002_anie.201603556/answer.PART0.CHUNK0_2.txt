The literature provided discusses the fabrication and properties of Fe-based catalysts modulated with Zn and Na, focusing on their activity and selectivity for syngas-derived alkenes. The catalysts are synthesized via a coprecipitation/washing method, where Zn acts as a structural promoter by altering the size of iron species, and Na modifies the electronic structure, enhancing CO activation and suppressing the hydrogenation of double bonds, thus increasing the selectivity for alkenes, especially those with five or more carbon atoms.
