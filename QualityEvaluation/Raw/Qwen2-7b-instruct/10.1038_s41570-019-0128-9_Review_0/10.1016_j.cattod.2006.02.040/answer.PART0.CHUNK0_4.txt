Various support materials and reaction conditions significantly impact the activity, selectivity, stability, and mechanistic pathways of chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions. Key challenges and trade-offs include balancing activity, selectivity, stability, and cost-effectiveness. Optimizing performance requires selecting suitable support materials and fine-tuning reaction conditions, considering factors such as CO2 partial pressure and the potential for competitive reactions. Despite progress, further research aims to improve selectivity and stability without compromising activity, addressing ongoing challenges in this field.
