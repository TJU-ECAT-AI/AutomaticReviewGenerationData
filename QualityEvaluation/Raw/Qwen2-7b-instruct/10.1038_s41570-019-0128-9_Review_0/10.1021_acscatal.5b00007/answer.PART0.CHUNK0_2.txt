<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Various support materials like SiO2, MCM-41, H-ZSM-5, Al2O3, ZrO2, and CeO2, along with CO2 partial pressure, significantly impact the activity, selectivity, stability, and mechanistic pathways of chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions. Optimizing the overall performance of these catalysts involves balancing activity, selectivity, and stability. A comprehensive understanding of how different factors affect catalytic performance is crucial for advancing the commercialization of CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation processes.
