<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The various support materials such as SiO2, MCM-41, H-ZSM-5, Al2O3, ZrO2, and CeO2, along with the reaction conditions including CO2 partial pressure, significantly influence the activity, selectivity, stability, and mechanistic pathways of chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions. Optimizing their overall performance presents key challenges, primarily balancing high activity and selectivity with long-term stability.
