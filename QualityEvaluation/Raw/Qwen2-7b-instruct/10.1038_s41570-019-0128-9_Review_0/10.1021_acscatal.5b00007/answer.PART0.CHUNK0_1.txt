<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. In CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation, the selection of support materials like SiO2, MCM-41, H-ZSM-5, Al2O3, ZrO2, and CeO2 greatly affects the activity, selectivity, stability, and mechanistic pathways of chromium oxide catalysts.
2. CO2 partial pressure plays a significant role in influencing the catalytic performance, impacting the reaction rate and product distribution.
3. Optimizing the overall performance of these catalysts presents key challenges, including balancing high activity and selectivity while ensuring stability over repeated reactions.
4. There are often trade-offs between enhancing one property, such as activity or selectivity, and potentially sacrificing another, like stability or cost.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
