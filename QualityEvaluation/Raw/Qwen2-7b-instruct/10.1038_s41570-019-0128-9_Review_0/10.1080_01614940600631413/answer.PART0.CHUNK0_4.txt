Factors like CO2 partial pressure also play a crucial role. Higher CO2 partial pressures might enhance the catalytic activity by providing more reactive oxygen species, but excessive pressure could lead to catalyst deactivation or poisoning. Trade-offs exist between maximizing activity and selectivity, with some supports favoring one property over the other. For instance, supports that enhance activity might reduce selectivity, and vice versa. Optimizing the overall performance of chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions requires balancing these factors to achieve the desired balance between activity, selectivity, and stability.

The key challenges in optimizing the performance of chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions include dealing with catalyst deactivation, enhancing selectivity towards desired products, and improving stability against CO2 poisoning.
