<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Various support materials including SiO2, MCM-41, H-ZSM-5, Al2O3, ZrO2, and CeO2 have an impact on the activity, selectivity, stability, and mechanistic pathways of chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions. The partial pressure of CO2 plays a critical role in influencing the performance of these catalysts. Key challenges in optimizing their overall performance involve achieving a balance between high activity and selectivity while ensuring long-term stability.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
