<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The use of various support materials including SiO2, MCM-41, H-ZSM-5, Al2O3, ZrO2, and CeO2 and the variation of CO2 partial pressure have been studied in the context of the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane with CO2. The choice of these materials and the CO2 pressure significantly affect the activity, selectivity, stability, and mechanistic pathways of chromium oxide catalysts. Key challenges and trade-offs in optimizing their performance involve balancing activity and selectivity, enhancing stability under high CO2 pressures, and understanding the reaction mechanisms.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
