<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. The presence of vanadium oxide significantly increases the activity of the catalyst, and the support material plays a crucial role in maintaining stable catalytic activity.
2. The redox property of the catalyst has a substantial impact on its activity in the oxidative dehydrogenation of n-butane with CO2.
3. The CeO2ZrO2 mixed oxide support facilitates the Ce4+/Ce3+ redox chemistry and oxygen mobility, making it a well-known material for catalytic reactions.
4. The redox cycle of the catalyst, particularly the redox cycle of cerium sites near vanadium sites, is essential for the catalytic activity of vanadia-containing catalysts.
5. The key active site in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane over ceria-supported vanadia catalysts is the VOV or V OCe bridging oxygen.
6. The catalysts with acidic and basic surface characters have different 1-butene/2-butene ratios, with the former having a ratio of 1 and the latter having a ratio less than 1.
