1. Various support materials such as SiO2, MCM-41, H-ZSM-5, Al2O3, ZrO2, and CeO2, along with reaction conditions including CO2 partial pressure, influence the activity, selectivity, stability, and mechanistic pathways of chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions. Challenges and trade-offs in optimizing overall performance often involve balancing these factors against each other to achieve desired outcomes.

2. The oxidative dehydrogenation of ethane by oxygen provides an alternative to thermal cracking of ethane. This process exhibits advantages over the oxidative dehydrogenation with oxygen, as demonstrated by various researchers.

3. The Cr/TS-1 catalyst with a nSi/nTi ratio of 30 effectively dehydrogenates ethane in the presence of carbon dioxide at 650°C, achieving a 47.4% ethylene yield with a high selectivity of 90%.

4. The study also investigates the catalytic stability and regeneration performance of the Cr/TS-1 catalyst.

5. The order of catalyst activity is as follows: Cr/TS-1(150) > Cr/TS-1(60) > Cr/TS-1(30) > Cr/TS-1(15).

6. Among these catalysts, the Cr/TS-1(30) catalyst demonstrates a high ethylene yield of 47.4% with a selectivity of 90%, but it quickly deactivates during the reaction.

7. The stability and regeneration capabilities of the Cr/TS-1(30) catalyst are explored in the study.
