<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The various support materials such as SiO2, MCM-41, H-ZSM-5, Al2O3, ZrO2, and CeO2, along with different reaction conditions including CO2 partial pressure, play significant roles in influencing the activity, selectivity, stability, and mechanistic pathways of chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions. Challenges include optimizing the overall performance, balancing the trade-offs between these factors, and addressing specific issues encountered during the process. The mini-review summarizes the challenges faced and innovative catalytic technologies developed to tackle these problems.
