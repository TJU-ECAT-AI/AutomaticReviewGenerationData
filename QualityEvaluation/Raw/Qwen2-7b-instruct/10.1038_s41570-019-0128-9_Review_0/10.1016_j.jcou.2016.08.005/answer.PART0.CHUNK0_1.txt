<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The utilization of CO2 as a soft oxidant for industrial production of alkenes is an emerging technology that aims to reduce energy consumption compared to traditional direct dehydrogenation methods of alkanes. This approach is more environmentally friendly and economically beneficial for polymer and fuel industries. However, challenges in the process, such as the activation of inert CO2, have led to the development of specific catalytic technologies. These technologies aim to optimize the performance of catalysts used in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions, considering factors such as support materials, reaction conditions, and the redox properties of the catalysts. The review summarizes the challenges faced and the advancements made in this field.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
