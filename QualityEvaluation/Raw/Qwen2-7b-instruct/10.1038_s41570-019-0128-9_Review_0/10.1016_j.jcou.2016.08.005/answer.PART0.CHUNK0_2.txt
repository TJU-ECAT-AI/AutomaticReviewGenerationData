<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Various support materials such as SiO2, MCM-41, H-ZSM-5, Al2O3, ZrO2, and CeO2 and reaction conditions including CO2 partial pressure significantly impact the activity, selectivity, stability, and mechanistic pathways of chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions. Key challenges involve optimizing these factors for better performance, balancing activity with selectivity and stability, and adapting the catalysts to efficiently utilize CO2 as a soft oxidant. The mini-review summarizes the encountered challenges and the innovative catalytic technologies developed to tackle these issues.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
