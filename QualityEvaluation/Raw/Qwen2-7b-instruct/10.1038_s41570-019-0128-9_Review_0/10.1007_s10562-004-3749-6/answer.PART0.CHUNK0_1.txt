<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. The nature of support materials significantly impacts the catalytic activity of chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions. Specifically, silica-supported catalysts exhibit a promoting effect of CO2 on propene yield, while alumina-supported catalysts show an inhibitory effect. 
2. The introduction of CO2 to the feed decreases the selectivity and yield of propene over catalysts.
3. CO2 does not promote dehydrogenation of propane over alumina-supported chromium oxide catalysts.
4. Steam affects the yield and selectivity of propene over both silica and alumina-supported catalysts, with a reversible effect noted for alumina-supported catalysts.
5. An increase in chromium loading enhances the promoting effect of CO2 on propene yield over silica-supported catalysts, whereas it diminishes the poisoning effect of CO2 on propene yield over alumina-supported catalysts.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
