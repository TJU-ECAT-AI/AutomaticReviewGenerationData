<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The nature of the support material significantly affects the catalytic activity of chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions. Specifically, silica (SiO2) supports tend to promote the dehydrogenation of propane when CO2 is present, enhancing propene yield. Conversely, aluminum oxide (Al2O3) supports often exhibit an inhibitory effect, reducing both the yield and selectivity of propene when CO2 is introduced. The ratio of propene yield in hydrogen (YHe) to propene yield in CO2 (YCO2) increases with the amount of chromium loaded onto the SiO2 support, while it decreases with the same increase in loading on the Al2O3 support. This suggests that the acidity/basicity of the support plays a crucial role in determining the overall catalytic performance in CO2 dehydrogenation reactions.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
