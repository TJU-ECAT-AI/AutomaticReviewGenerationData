<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The oxidative coupling of methane (OCM) reaction involves the direct conversion of methane into ethane, ethylene, and water in the presence of oxygen and a suitable catalyst. In this process, methane is first oxidized, forming methyl radicals, which then couple to form ethane. In a subsequent step, ethane undergoes dehydrogenation to produce ethylene. The reaction equations demonstrate the thermodynamic values for these conversions.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
