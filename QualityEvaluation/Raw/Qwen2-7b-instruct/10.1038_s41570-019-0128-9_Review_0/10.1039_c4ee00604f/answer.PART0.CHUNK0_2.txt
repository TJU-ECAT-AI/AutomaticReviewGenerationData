<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The oxidative coupling of methane (OCM) reaction involves the conversion of methane into ethane, ethylene, and water in the presence of oxygen and a suitable catalyst. The first equation represents the conversion of methane into ethane and water with a yield of 42.4 kJ mol−1. The second equation depicts the conversion of methane into ethylene and water with a higher yield of 67.4 kJ mol−1.
