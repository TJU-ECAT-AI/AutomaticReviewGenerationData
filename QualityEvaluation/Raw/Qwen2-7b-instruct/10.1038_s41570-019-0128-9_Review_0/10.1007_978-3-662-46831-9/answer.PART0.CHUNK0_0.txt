Various support materials such as SiO2, MCM-41, H-ZSM-5, Al2O3, ZrO2, and CeO2, and reaction conditions including CO2 partial pressure, can significantly impact the activity, selectivity, stability, and mechanistic pathways of chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions. These factors can influence how efficiently the catalysts convert CO2 into desired products. Key challenges in optimizing the overall performance often involve balancing these parameters to achieve high conversion rates while maintaining product selectivity and catalyst stability. Trade-offs might include choosing a support material that enhances selectivity at the cost of reduced activity, or adjusting the reaction conditions to improve both activity and selectivity but potentially compromising stability.
