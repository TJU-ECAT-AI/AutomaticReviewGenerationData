The reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) involves three sequential steps: dissociative adsorption, β-H extraction, and desorption. Dissociative adsorption involves the breaking of a bond in propane upon its adsorption onto a catalyst surface, forming a reactive intermediate. The β-H extraction step removes a hydrogen atom from this intermediate, leading to the formation of propylene. The desorption step releases the propylene product from the catalyst surface. The ongoing debate in the field revolves around which of these steps—dissociative adsorption or β-H extraction—is the rate-controlling step in the PDH process. This uncertainty impacts our understanding of the PDH process by highlighting the complexity of the reaction mechanism and the need for further research to elucidate the kinetic details of each step. The lack of consensus on the rate-controlling step also underscores the importance of developing strategies that can effectively address the limitations posed by the current understanding of the PDH mechanism.
