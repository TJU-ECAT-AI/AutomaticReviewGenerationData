Answer: The reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) involves three key steps: dissociative adsorption, β-H extraction, and desorption. This mechanism suggests that the first step, dissociative adsorption, where propane molecules break apart upon contact with the catalyst surface, is followed by β-H extraction, the removal of a hydrogen atom from one of the newly formed carbons, and finally, desorption, the release of the resulting products from the surface. The ongoing debate centers on whether the dissociative adsorption process or the β-H extraction is the rate-controlling step in PDH. This uncertainty impacts our understanding of the PDH process by complicating the identification of the primary rate-limiting step, which is crucial for optimizing catalyst performance and enhancing the efficiency of the dehydrogenation reaction. The stabilization of adsorbed species on the small clusters of Pt/Sn within the zeolite micropores plays a significant role in increasing the reaction rates over Pt/Sn/KL and Pt/Sn/K/SiO2 catalysts, suggesting that these conditions favor a more efficient and faster dehydrogenation process.
