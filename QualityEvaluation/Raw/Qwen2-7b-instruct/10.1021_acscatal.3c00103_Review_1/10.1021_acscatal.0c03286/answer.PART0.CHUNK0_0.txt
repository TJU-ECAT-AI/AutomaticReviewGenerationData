The reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) involves three steps: dissociative adsorption, β-H extraction, and desorption. The exact order of these steps and which one acts as the rate-controlling step is a subject of debate in the field. The uncertainty surrounding this debate impacts our understanding of the PDH process because it affects how the reaction dynamics are modeled and optimized for industrial applications. Specifically, if dissociative adsorption is the rate-controlling step, then enhancing the adsorption efficiency could lead to increased catalytic activity. Conversely, if β-H extraction is the rate-limiting step, improving the catalyst's ability to extract hydrogen from the adsorbed propane molecules would be crucial for achieving higher selectivity towards propylene. The precise mechanism would influence the design of catalysts and the operational conditions to maximize the yield of propylene and minimize the formation of unwanted byproducts.
