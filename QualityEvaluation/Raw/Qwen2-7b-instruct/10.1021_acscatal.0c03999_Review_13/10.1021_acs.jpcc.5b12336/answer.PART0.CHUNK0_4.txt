How does the use of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling compare to traditional oxygen-based systems in terms of thermodynamics, reaction mechanisms (including surface vs. gas-phase reactions), catalytic performance, and alkene selectivity, and what are the key challenges and opportunities for future improvements in this approach?

Thermodynamically, sulfur offers a more favorable activation energy for certain reactions compared to traditional oxygen-based systems. Sulfur-mediated processes can exhibit both surface and gas-phase reactions, showcasing versatile mechanisms. Catalytic performance varies based on the specific catalyst used, and alkene selectivity can be influenced by the choice of sulfur source and reaction conditions. Key challenges include the stability and reactivity of sulfur compounds under reaction conditions. Opportunities for future improvements involve optimizing catalyst design and reaction conditions to enhance efficiency and selectivity.
