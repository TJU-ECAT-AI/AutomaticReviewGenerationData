<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces, synthesized by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates, involves techniques like scanning tunneling microscopy (STM), low energy electron diffraction (LEED), and Auger electron spectroscopy (AES). This process is crucial for structural modeling because it provides insights into the precise atomic arrangement on the surface. Accurate structural information is essential for DFT calculations to predict electronic and chemical behaviors. Understanding these properties comprehensively is vital for advancements in surface science and catalysis research.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

<ChineseVersionAnswer>
对通过在单晶Pt（111）或多晶Pt基板上沉积Ni单层合成的Ni/Pt双金属表面的表征涉及扫描隧道显微镜（STM）、低能电子衍射（LEED）和Auger电子能谱（AES）等技术。这一过程对于结构建模至关重要，因为它提供了表面精确原子排列的见解，这影响了表面的化学和催化性质。准确的结构信息对于基于密度泛函理论（DFT）的计算预测电子和化学行为是必不可少的。全面理解这些性质对于表面科学和催化研究的进步至关重要。
</ChineseVersionAnswer>
