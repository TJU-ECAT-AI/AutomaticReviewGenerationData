The experiments were carried out using synchrotron radiation at the SuperAco storage ring, with the beamline SA73b featuring a TGM7 monochromator. The experiments took place in a stainless-steel UHV chamber, equipped with a hemispherical analyzer for photoelectron spectroscopy (PES), a cylindrical mirror analyzer (CMA) for Auger electron spectroscopy (AES), and LEED apparatus. Clean Cu(100) single-crystal surfaces were obtained through cycles of argon-ion sputtering followed by annealing at 850 K. The Pt 4f core-level photoemission line was studied with 150 eV photons polarized in the p direction. Up to 2 ML of Pt deposition revealed a bulk-like peak (B*) at 71.0 ± 0.03 eV and a surface-like peak (S*) at 70.6 ± 0.03 eV. Annealing caused the intensities of B and S to decrease and the peaks to shift by 150 meV towards lower binding energy. After annealing, the LEED patterns became sharp c(2 x 2) with low background intensities.
