Comprehensive characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces involves depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates. This process is crucial for structural modeling, DFT calculations, and understanding the electronic and chemical properties of these bimetallic surfaces. It utilizes synchrotron radiation, specifically the SA73b beamline with a TGM7 monochromator, for experiments conducted in a stainless-steel ultra-high vacuum (UHV) chamber equipped with a hemispherical analyzer for photoelectron spectroscopy (PES), a cylindrical mirror analyzer (CMA) for Auger electron spectroscopy (AES), and LEED apparatus. Clean Cu(100) single-crystal surfaces are obtained through cycles of argon-ion sputtering followed by annealing at 850 K. The Pt 4f core-level photoemission line is studied using 150 eV photons polarized in the p direction. Up to 2 ML deposition reveals an extended Pt-Cu surface alloy, characterized by a bulk-like peak at 71.0 ± 0.03 eV and a surface-like peak at 70.6 ± 0.03 eV. Annealing causes the intensities of these peaks to decrease and their positions to shift by 150 meV to B* and S* positions, respectively. After annealing, the LEED patterns become sharp c(2 x 2) with low background intensities. The characterization also includes the formation of a highly ordered nanoscale surface alloy through Cr-induced Pt(111) reconstruction, which is essential for advanced surface science and catalysis research. The process allows for precise control over the surface morphology, influencing the material's properties. Particular merits of these systems include good high-temperature stability and tunable chemical selectivity. The adsorption of ethylene molecules on these surfaces is influenced by the substrate's topography, impacting the stability and reactivity of adsorbates. The theoretical study of small Pt aggregates adsorbed on Ni(111) further aids in understanding the interaction and properties at the interface between the two metals. Atomic scale investigations of the Co/Pt(111) interface structure and magnetic properties provide insights into the electronic and chemical properties of bimetallic surfaces. The characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces also involves studying the formation of an inert La-Pt(111) surface alloy through the synthesis of a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates, which is crucial for structural modeling, DFT calculations, and our comprehensive understanding of the electronic and chemical properties. The stress relief via island formation of an isotropically strained bimetallic surface layer contributes to the mesoscopic morphology of the Ag/Pt (111) surface alloy. At 870 K, the required operating temperature, all three types of chemical species are decomposed to yield the same type of graphitic deposit, an essential requirement for quantitative applications. These systems exhibit particular merits, including good high-temperature stability and tunable chemical selectivity. The physical properties of thin transition metal films supported on Pt(hkl) and Au(hkl) have been studied in detail to establish a link between surface properties and electrocatalytic activity. The morphology and stability of Pd films on Pt(1 1 1) were examined through in-situ surface X-ray scattering (SXS) and Fourier transform infrared (FTIR) measurements, revealing that electrochemically deposited films are pseudomorphic. The weakening of metal-adsorbate bonds in Ni/Pt(1 1 1) alloy systems is attributed to the modification of the electronic structure of the surface Pt atoms by subsurface Ni atoms. The adsorption of CO on Ce/Pt (111) was studied using LEED, XPS, and temperature-programmed desorption, providing further insights into the electronic and chemical properties of bimetallic surfaces. In summary, the characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces is pivotal for advanced surface science and catalysis research, enabling detailed analysis of their structure and behavior under various conditions.

        来源于参考文献\[1\]的深入分析。来自参考文献\[2\], \[3\], 和\[4\]的综合理解。Ni/Pt双金属表面的全面表征涉及在Pt(111)单晶或聚晶Pt基底上沉积Ni单层。这一过程对于结构建模、DFT计算以及理解这些双金属表面的电子和化学性质至关重要。利用同步辐射，特别是SA73b束线配合TGM7色散器，进行实验，在不锈钢超高真空（UHV）室中配备有用于光电子能谱（PES）的半球形分析仪、用于阿勒尔电子能谱（AES）的圆柱镜分析器以及LEED装置。通过氩离子溅射循环并随后在850 K下退火获得清洁的Cu(100)单晶表面。使用150 eV的p极化光子研究了Pt 4f核心级光发射线。在至2 ML的沉积过程中，揭示了一个扩展的Pt-Cu表面合金，特征为一个类似于纯相的峰（B*）位于71.0 ± 0.03 eV，另一个类似于表面相的峰（S*）位于70.6 ± 0.03 eV。退火导致这两个峰值的强度降低，并分别向B*和S*位置移动了150 meV。退火后，LEED图案变为清晰的c(2 x 2)，背景强度低。此外，通过Cr诱导的Pt(111)重构形成了一种高度有序的纳米尺度表面合金，这对于先进的表面科学和催化研究至关重要。该过程允许对表面形态进行精确控制，影响材料的性质。这些系统具有特定的优点，包括高温稳定性良好和可调化学选择性。通过详细研究薄过渡金属薄膜在Pt(hkl)和Au(hkl)上的支持，建立了表面性质与电催化剂活性之间的联系。例如，对Pt(1 1 1)表面上的Pd薄膜的形态和稳定性进行了利用原位表面X射线散射（SXS）和傅里叶变换红外（FTIR）测量的检查，表明电化学沉积的薄膜是拟形变的，其原子占据Pt基底上的通量对齐点以继续ABC堆叠。Ni/Pt(1 1 1)合金体系中的金属-吸附剂键的减弱归因于表面Pt原子的电子结构由亚表面Ni原子的修改。Ce/Pt (111)上的CO吸附通过LEED、XPS和温度程序脱附进行了研究，进一步提供了关于双金属表面的电子和化学性质的见解。总结起来，Ni/Pt双金属表面的表征对于高级表面科学和催化研究至关重要，它使我们能够深入了解其结构及其在不同条件下的行为。
