        The characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces involves depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates. This process is crucial for structural modeling, DFT calculations, and understanding the electronic and chemical properties of these bimetallic surfaces. It employs synchrotron radiation, specifically the SA73b beamline with a TGM7 monochromator, for experiments conducted in a stainless-steel ultra-high vacuum (UHV) chamber equipped with a hemispherical analyzer for photoelectron spectroscopy (PES), a cylindrical mirror analyzer (CMA) for Auger electron spectroscopy (AES), and LEED apparatus. Clean Cu(100) single-crystal surfaces are obtained through cycles of argon-ion sputtering followed by annealing at 850 K. The Pt 4f core-level photoemission line, with a bulk binding energy of 7 1.15 eV, is studied using 150 eV photons polarized in the p direction. The growth process up to 2 ML reveals the formation of an extended Pt-Cu surface alloy, characterized by a bulk-like peak (B*) at 71.0 ± 0.03 eV and a surface-like peak (S*) at 70.6 ± 0.03 eV. The relative variation of the intensity is +10%. Annealing affects the alloy structure, causing the intensities of B and S to decrease and the peaks to shift by 150 meV towards lower binding energy to the B* and S* positions, respectively. After annealing, the LEED patterns become sharp c(2 × 2) with low background intensities.

        The characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces also encompasses the formation of a highly ordered nanoscale surface alloy through Cr-induced Pt(111) reconstruction (reference \[10.1103/physrevb.57.r4285\]), the formation of a specific surface alloy by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates (references \[10.1103/physrevb.59.15935\], \[10.1143/jjap.44.l31\]), and the stress relief via island formation of an isotropically strained bimetallic surface layer (reference \[10.1103/physrevb.54.13476\]). These processes are pivotal for structural modeling, DFT calculations, and our comprehensive understanding of the electronic and chemical properties of these bimetallic surfaces in the context of advanced surface science and catalysis research.

It also investigates the microscopic work function distributions and the long-range effect of work function reduction, which provides insights into the electronic structure and reactivity of the bimetallic surfaces (reference \[10.1143/jjap.44.l31\]).

        尼/铂双金属表面的表征涉及在铂(111)单晶或多晶铂基底上沉积一层镍单层。这一过程对于结构建模、DFT计算以及全面理解这些双金属表面的电子和化学性质至关重要，在高级表面科学和催化研究领域具有重要性。它使用同步辐射，特别是SA73b光束线配以TGM7单色器，进行在不锈钢超高真空（UHV）室中进行的实验，该室配备有用于光电发射光谱（PES）的球面分析仪、用于阿贝尔电子能谱（AES）的圆柱镜分析仪以及LEED装置。通过氩离子溅射循环和在850 K下退火制备清洁的Cu(100)单晶表面。铂4f核心能级光发射线，其本体结合能为7 1.15 eV，使用150 eV的p偏振光进行研究。生长过程至2 ML揭示了Pt-Cu表面合金的形成，特征是本体类似峰（B*）位于71.0 ± 0.03 eV处和表面类似峰（S*）位于70.6 ± 0.03 eV处。相对强度的变化为+10%。退火影响合金结构，导致B和S的强度降低，并使峰值向较低结合能量移动至B*和S*位置，分别移动150 meV。退火后，LEED图案变为清晰的c(2 × 2)模式，背景强度低。

        双金属表面的表征还涵盖了通过Cr诱导的Pt(111)重构形成高度有序的纳米尺度表面合金（参考文献\[10.1103/physrevb.57.r4285\]），在Pt(111)单晶或多晶铂基底上沉积一层镍单层形成特定的表面合金（参考文献\[10.1103/physrevb.59.15935\]、\[10.1143/jjap.44.l31\]），以及通过岛形成缓解各向同性拉伸的双金属表面层的应力（参考文献\[10.1103/physrevb.54.13476\]）。这些过程对于结构建模、DFT计算以及全面理解这些双金属表面的电子和化学性质在高级表面科学和催化研究领域中至关重要。

        表征进一步探讨了基底拓扑对键合和反应性的影响，如乙稀分子在这些表面上的吸附（参考文献\[10.1016/s0039-6028(00)00978-x\]），以及基底拓扑对吸附剂稳定性和反应性的影响（参考文献\[10.1016/j.susc.2003.09.007\]）。它还研究了微区工作函数分布和工作函数减小的远程效应，这为理解这些表面的电子结构和活性提供了见解，对于设计更高效和选择性的催化剂和其他材料具有重要意义。
