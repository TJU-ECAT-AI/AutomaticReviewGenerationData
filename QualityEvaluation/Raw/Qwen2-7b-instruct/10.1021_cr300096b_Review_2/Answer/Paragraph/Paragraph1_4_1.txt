        The synthesis of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces involves depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates. This methodology is fundamental for structural modeling, Density Functional Theory (DFT) calculations, and our thorough understanding of the electronic and chemical properties of these bimetallic surfaces, particularly within the domain of advanced surface science and catalysis research. It entails the utilization of synchrotron radiation, specifically the SA73b beamline with a TGM7 monochromator, for experiments conducted in a stainless-steel ultra-high vacuum (UHV) chamber equipped with a hemispherical analyzer for photoelectron spectroscopy (PES), a cylindrical mirror analyzer (CMA) for Auger electron spectroscopy (AES), and LEED apparatus. Clean Cu(100) single-crystal surfaces are acquired through cycles of argon-ion sputtering (600 eV, 8 μA cm−) followed by annealing at 850 K. The Pt 4f core-level photoemission line, with a bulk binding energy of 7 1.15 eV, is studied using 150 eV photons polarized in the p direction. Up to 2 ML, the formation reveals an extended Pt-Cu surface alloy, characterized by a bulk-like peak (B*) at 71.0 ± 0.03 eV and a surface-like peak (S*) at 70.6 ± 0.03 eV. The relative variation of the intensity is +10%. Annealing impacts the alloy structure, causing the intensities of B and S to decrease and the peaks to shift by 150 meV towards lower binding energy to the B* and S* positions, respectively. Post-annealing, the LEED patterns transform into sharp c(2 × 2) with low background intensities.

        The formation of a highly ordered nanoscale surface alloy through Cr-induced Pt(111) reconstruction, as detailed in \[10.1103/physrevb.57.r4285\], is pivotal for understanding the electronic and chemical properties of these bimetallic surfaces. The process of depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates, as outlined in \[10.1103/physrevb.59.15935\], facilitates the creation of a model surface suitable for DFT calculations and structural modeling, offering insights into the effects of the substrate's topography on bonding and reactivity.

        The synthesis of a specific surface alloy through the formation of a Pt overlayer, as explored in \[10.1021/jp0615407\], enables detailed analysis of the surface structure and its implications on the electronic and chemical properties. The influence of the substrate's topography on bonding and reactivity, as evidenced in \[10.1016/j.elecom.2003.07.001\], underscores the importance of the synthesis method in understanding the behavior of bimetallic surfaces in advanced surface science and catalysis research.

        The comprehensive understanding of the electronic and chemical properties of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces is achieved through a combination of experimental techniques and theoretical calculations. The synthesis process, as described in \[10.1016/j.susc.2003.09.007\], allows for the detailed examination of the surface structure and the effects of the Pt overlayer on the electronic states of the surface atoms, which are influenced by the presence of subsurface Ni atoms.         Ni/Pt 双金属表面的合成涉及在 Pt(111) 单晶或聚晶 Pt 基板上沉积 Ni 单层。这一方法对于结构建模、密度泛函理论（DFT）计算以及全面理解这些双金属表面的电子和化学性质至关重要，特别是在先进的表面科学和催化研究领域。这需要使用同步辐射，特别是 SA73b 光束线搭配 TGM7 谱仪，用于在不锈钢超高真空（UHV）室中进行实验，该室内配备了用于光电子能谱（PES）的球面分光器、用于阿贝尔电子能谱（AES）的圆柱镜分光器以及 LEED 装置。通过氩离子溅射（600 eV，8 μA cm−）获得清洁的 Cu(100) 单晶表面，并随后在 850 K 下进行退火。使用 150 eV 的 p 极化光子研究 Pt 4f 核级光发射线，其基态结合能为 7 1.15 eV。在前 2 ML 的形成过程中，揭示了一个扩展的 Pt-Cu 表面合金，由一个类似于基态的峰（B*）表示，其能量为 71.0 ± 0.03 eV，以及一个表面类似峰（S*），其能量为 70.6 ± 0.03 eV。相对强度的变化为 +10%。退火影响合金结构，导致 B 和 S 的强度降低，并使峰值分别向较低的结合能移动至 B* 和 S* 位置，移动量为 150 meV。退火后，LEED 图案转变为清晰的 c(2 × 2)，并伴有低背景强度。

        通过 Cr 引发的 Pt(111) 重构形成的高度有序纳米尺度表面合金，如在 \[10.1103/physrevb.57.r4285\] 中详细描述的那样，对于理解这些双金属表面的电子和化学性质至关重要。在 \[10.1103/physrevb.59.15935\] 中概述的 Ni 单层在 Pt(111) 单晶或聚晶 Pt 基板上的沉积过程，允许进行结构分析和模型构建，提供有关基板表面拓扑对键合和反应性的影响的见解。

        通过组合实验技术和理论计算实现对 Ni/Pt 双金属表面电子和化学性质的全面理解。在 \[10.1021/jp0615407\] 描述的合成过程允许对表面结构进行详细检查，并揭示了 Pt 过层对表面原子电子状态的影响，这些状态受到亚表面 Ni 原子的存在所影响。这导致了对表面化学和活性的更深入理解，这对于催化剂和其他材料的设计和优化至关重要。

        因此，Ni/Pt 双金属表面的特征取决于合成方法，不仅决定了表面结构，而且决定了电子和化学性质。这种理解对于推动表面科学和催化研究领域的发展至关重要，使我们能够开发出性能更优的催化剂和其他具有定制属性的材料。
