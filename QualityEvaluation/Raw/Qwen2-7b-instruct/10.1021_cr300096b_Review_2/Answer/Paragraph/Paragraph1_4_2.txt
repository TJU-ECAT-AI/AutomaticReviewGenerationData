        The characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces involves depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates. This process is crucial for structural modeling, DFT calculations, and understanding the electronic and chemical properties of these bimetallic surfaces. It utilizes synchrotron radiation, specifically the SA73b beamline with a TGM7 monochromator, for experiments conducted in a stainless-steel ultra-high vacuum (UHV) chamber equipped with a hemispherical analyzer for photoelectron spectroscopy (PES), a cylindrical mirror analyzer (CMA) for Auger electron spectroscopy (AES), and LEED apparatus. Clean Cu(100) single-crystal surfaces are obtained through cycles of argon-ion sputtering (600 eV, 8 μA cm−) followed by annealing at 850 K. The Pt 4f core-level photoemission line, with a bulk binding energy of 7 1.15 eV, is studied using 150 eV photons polarized in the p direction. The growth process up to 2 ML reveals the formation of an extended Pt-Cu surface alloy, characterized by a bulk-like peak (B*) at 71.0 ± 0.03 eV and a surface-like peak (S*) at 70.6 ± 0.03 eV. The relative variation of the intensity is +10%. Annealing affects the alloy structure, causing the intensities of B and S to decrease and the peaks to shift by 150 meV towards lower binding energy to the B* and S* positions, respectively. After annealing, the LEED patterns become sharp c(2 × 2) with low background intensities.

        Upcoming research directions could involve exploring the role of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces in more complex catalytic processes, such as the conversion of hydrocarbons to higher value chemicals. The development of new computational methods to accurately predict the electronic structure and reactivity of these surfaces could also significantly advance our understanding and predictive capabilities. Additionally, the study of dynamic changes in the surface structure during catalytic reactions, including the effects of different reactants and conditions, could lead to the discovery of novel catalysts with improved performance.

        尼/铂双金属表面的表征涉及在铂（111）单晶或多晶铂基底上沉积一层镍单层。这一过程对于结构建模、DFT计算以及理解这些双金属表面的电子和化学性质至关重要。利用同步辐射，具体是SA73b光束线搭配TGM7单色器，进行实验，在不锈钢超高真空（UHV）室中进行，该系统配备了用于光电谱分析的球面分析仪、用于阿勒尔电子谱分析的圆柱镜分析仪（CMA）和LEED装置。通过重复氩离子溅射（600 eV，8 μA cm−）和在850 K下退火来制备清洁的Cu（100）单晶表面。Pt 4f核心能级光发射线（块体结合能为7 1.15 eV），使用150 eV的光子进行p极化研究。生长过程至2 ML揭示了扩展的Pt-Cu表面合金形成，由一个类似块状的峰值（标记为B*）在71.0 ± 0.03 eV处和一个类似表面的峰值（标记为S*）在70.6 ± 0.03 eV处组成。强度的变化为+10%。退火影响合金结构，导致B和S的强度降低，并使峰向较低的结合能移动至B*和S*位置，分别移动150 meV。退火后，LEED图案变为尖锐的c(2 × 2)，背景强度低。

        探索方向可能包括研究Ni/铂双金属表面在更复杂催化过程中的作用，如将碳氢化合物转化为更高价值化学品。开发新的计算方法以准确预测这些表面的电子结构和活性也将极大地促进我们的理解和预测能力。此外，研究催化反应过程中表面结构的动态变化，包括不同反应物和条件的影响，可以发现性能改进的新催化剂。
