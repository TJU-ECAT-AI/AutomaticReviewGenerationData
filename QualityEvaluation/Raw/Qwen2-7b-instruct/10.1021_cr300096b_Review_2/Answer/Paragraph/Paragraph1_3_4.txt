        The characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces involves depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates. This process is crucial for structural modeling, DFT calculations, and understanding the electronic and chemical properties of these bimetallic surfaces. It employs synchrotron radiation, specifically the SA73b beamline with a TGM7 monochromator, for experiments conducted in a stainless-steel ultra-high vacuum (UHV) chamber equipped with various spectroscopic techniques including PES, AES, and LEED. The Pt 4f core-level photoemission line is studied using 150 eV photons. Up to 2 ML deposition reveals the formation of an extended Pt-Cu surface alloy, characterized by distinct peaks corresponding to bulk and surface-like configurations. Annealing affects the alloy structure, altering the intensities and shifting the peaks. After annealing, the LEED patterns become sharper, indicating a more ordered surface.

        The effects of annealing are twofold: it decreases the intensities of the bulk and surface-like peaks, suggesting diffusion of Pt into the Cu substrate, and shifts the peaks by 150 meV towards lower binding energy, indicative of changes in the electronic structure. The process also influences the reactivity of the surface, as evidenced by the adsorption of ethylene molecules being affected by the substrate's topography.

        Comprehensive characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces is pivotal for understanding the interplay between structure, electronic states, and chemical reactivity, which is fundamental to advanced surface science and catalysis research. It allows for the design and optimization of catalysts by elucidating the underlying mechanisms that govern their performance.

        Ni/Pt双金属表面的表征涉及在Pt(111)单晶或多晶Pt基片上沉积Ni单层。这一过程对于结构建模、DFT计算以及理解这些双金属表面的电子和化学性质至关重要。使用同步辐射，特别是SA73b束线配以TGM7色散器，在不锈钢超高真空（UHV）室中进行实验，配备包括PES、AES和LEED在内的各种光谱技术。研究了Pt 4f核心能级发射线，使用150 eV的光子进行测量。在2 ML沉积过程中，形成了扩展的Pt-Cu表面合金，具有代表性的峰分别对应于块状和表面像特征。退火影响合金结构，降低峰的强度并使峰向较低结合能位移150 meV。退火后，LEED图案变得更加锐利，表明表面更加有序。

        退火的影响是两方面的：它减少了块状和表面像峰的强度，表明Pt扩散到Cu基体中，并且峰向较低结合能位移150 meV，表明电子结构的变化。该过程还影响表面反应性，因为乙烯分子的吸附受基体表面拓扑结构的影响。

        对Ni/Pt双金属表面的全面表征对于理解结构、电子状态和化学活性之间的相互作用至关重要，这是高级表面科学和催化研究的基础。它允许通过揭示控制其性能的机制来设计和优化催化剂。
