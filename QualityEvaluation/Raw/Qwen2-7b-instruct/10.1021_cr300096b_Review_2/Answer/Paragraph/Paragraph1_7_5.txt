This process employs synchrotron radiation, specifically the SA73b beamline with a TGM7 monochromator, for experiments conducted in a stainless-steel ultra-high vacuum (UHV) chamber equipped with various spectroscopic tools like PES, AES, and LEED apparatus. The formation of highly ordered nanoscale surface alloys through Cr-induced Pt(111) reconstruction and the examination of Pt 4f core-level photoemission lines provide insights into the electronic structure of the surfaces. The influence of annealing on the alloy structure, including changes in surface peak positions and intensities, further elucidates the electronic and chemical properties. The characterization also encompasses the study of CO adsorption on inert La-Pt(111) surfaces, highlighting the importance of understanding surface dynamics and reactivity.

Techniques like AES, LEED, LEIS, XPS, and STM are employed to determine the surface composition and structure, revealing the effects of subsurface Ni atoms on the electronic structure of the surface Pt atoms, leading to the observation of weaker metal-adsorbate bonds in Ni/Pt(111) alloy systems.

        在Pt(111)单晶或多晶Pt基底上沉积Ni单层的过程是高级表面科学和催化研究的基础，它允许对表面结构和电子状态进行详细分析。这种理解对于预测这些表面在不同条件下的行为以及优化它们的使用至关重要。采用AES、LEED、LEIS、XPS和STM等技术来确定表面组成和结构，揭示了表面Pt原子电子结构受表面下Ni原子影响的现象，导致Ni/Pt(111)合金系统中金属-吸附剂键变弱的观察。

        综合考虑后，可以清楚地看出，对Ni/Pt双金属表面的详细表征及其通过多种光谱学方法进行的表征提供了有关其电子和化学性质的宝贵见解。这些知识对于推动表面科学和催化研究的发展至关重要，指导高效催化剂的设计和开发。这为特定催化反应提供了显著改进的可能性。
