        The characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces, synthesized by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates, involves conducting the formation of a specific surface alloy. This process is crucial for structural modeling, Density Functional Theory (DFT) calculations, and our comprehensive understanding of the electronic and chemical properties of these bimetallic surfaces in the context of advanced surface science and catalysis research. It utilizes synchrotron radiation, specifically the SA73b beamline with a TGM7 monochromator, for experiments conducted in a stainless-steel ultra-high vacuum (UHV) chamber equipped with a hemispherical analyzer for photoelectron spectroscopy (PES), a cylindrical mirror analyzer (CMA) for Auger electron spectroscopy (AES), and LEED apparatus. Clean Cu(100) single-crystal surfaces are obtained through cycles of argon-ion sputtering followed by annealing at 850 K. The Pt 4f core-level photoemission line is studied using 150 eV photons polarized in the p direction. Up to 2 ML deposition reveals an extended Pt-Cu surface alloy characterized by a bulk-like peak (B*) and a surface-like peak (S*). Annealing affects the alloy structure, causing intensities to decrease and peaks to shift towards lower binding energy. After annealing, the LEED patterns become sharp c(2x2) with low background intensities. This process allows for the detailed analysis of structure, electronic states, and reactivity, essential for advanced surface science and catalysis research.

        The synthesis and characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces through depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystals or polycrystalline Pt substrates enable us to gain insights into the electronic and chemical properties of these surfaces. This includes understanding the impact of the substrate's topography on bonding and reactivity, as well as the effects of the interface between Ni and Pt on the surface chemistry. The use of various spectroscopic techniques like AES, LEED, LEIS, XPS, and STM aids in determining the surface composition and structure, revealing the influence of subsurface Ni atoms on the electronic states of the surface Pt atoms. The weakening of metal-adsorbate bonds in Ni/Pt(111) alloy systems can be attributed to the modification of the electronic structure of the surface Pt atoms by these subsurface Ni atoms. The adsorption of ethylene molecules on these surfaces is influenced by the substrate's topography, impacting the stability and reactivity of adsorbates.

        通过在单晶铂(111)或多晶铂基板上沉积一层镍单层来合成和表征Ni/Pt双金属表面，使我们能够深入了解这些双金属表面的电子和化学性质。这包括了解基底拓扑结构对键合和反应性的影响，以及Ni和Pt界面对表面化学的影响。光电电子能谱、LEED、LEIS、XPS和STM等各种光谱技术的使用有助于确定表面组成和结构，揭示表面Pt原子电子状态受到表面下Ni原子的影响。Ni/Pt(111)合金系统中金属-吸附剂键强度的减弱可归因于表面Pt原子由这些表面下Ni原子引起的电子结构的修改。乙烯分子在这些表面上的吸附受到基底拓扑结构的影响，从而影响吸附物的稳定性和活性。
