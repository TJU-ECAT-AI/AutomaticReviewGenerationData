        The characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces, synthesized by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates, is a foundational aspect of advanced surface science and catalysis research. This process is pivotal for structural modeling, Density Functional Theory (DFT) calculations, and understanding the electronic and chemical properties of these surfaces. The utilization of synchrotron radiation, specifically the SA73b beamline with a TGM7 monochromator, enables experiments in a stainless-steel ultra-high vacuum (UHV) chamber equipped with advanced spectroscopic tools like photoelectron spectroscopy (PES), Auger electron spectroscopy (AES), and LEED. Clean Cu(100) single-crystal surfaces are obtained through cycles of argon-ion sputtering and annealing, providing a pristine base for Pt deposition. The Pt 4f core-level photoemission line is studied with 150 eV photons polarized in the p direction, revealing the formation of an extended Pt-Cu surface alloy with characteristic peaks at 71.0 ± 0.03 eV and 70.6 ± 0.03 eV. Annealing processes affect the alloy structure, leading to shifts in the peak positions and changes in intensity, which are indicative of Pt diffusion into the substrate and modifications to the electronic structure. The formation of a highly ordered nanoscale surface alloy through Cr-induced Pt(111) reconstruction further highlights the significance of this process in achieving a comprehensive understanding of the surfaces' properties.

        The characterization process encompasses a variety of techniques including atomic scale investigations, surface spectroscopy, and in-situ measurements, providing insights into the interface structure, magnetic properties, and the nature of chemical reactivity. Stress relief via island formation of an isotropically strained bimetallic surface layer also plays a crucial role in modulating the mesoscopic morphology of the surface alloy. At elevated temperatures, these surfaces demonstrate good high-temperature stability and tunable chemical selectivity, making them essential for quantitative applications.

        表征过程包括各种技术，如原子尺度调查、表面光谱学和原位测量，为界面结构、磁性属性以及化学反应性的本质提供深入了解。通过岛形成应力释放的各向同性变形双金属表面层的形态也对表面合金的微结构起着关键作用。在较高温度下，这些表面展现出良好的高温稳定性和可调的化学选择性，使其成为定量应用的重要组成部分。
