<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces, synthesized by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates, is conducted through the use of low-energy electron diffraction (LEED) and Auger electron spectroscopy (AES). These techniques allow for the detailed examination of the initial growth and the alloy formation of Ni ultrathin films on the Pt(111) surface. The process is crucial for structural modeling, DFT calculations, and the comprehensive understanding of the electronic and chemical properties of these bimetallic surfaces in the context of advanced surface science and catalysis research. The characterization provides insights into the various structural phases observed at different coverage levels, including pseudo-(1×1) structures, coherent epitaxy, incoherent epitaxy, and (2×2) superstructures. Additionally, the study reveals the starting temperatures for the formation of NiPt alloys, which vary depending on the Ni coverage on the Pt(111) surface.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

对Ni/Pt双金属表面的表征，通过在Pt(111)单晶或多晶Pt基底上沉积Ni单层来合成，是通过使用低能电子衍射（LEED）和俄歇电子能谱（AES）进行的。这些技术允许对Ni超薄薄膜在Pt(111)表面上的初始生长和合金形成进行详细的检查。这一过程对于结构建模、DFT计算以及对先进表面科学和催化研究背景下这些双金属表面的电子和化学性质的全面理解至关重要。该表征提供了在不同覆盖度水平下观察到的各种结构相的信息，包括伪-(1×1)结构、共轭外延、非共轭外延和(2×2)超结构。此外，研究揭示了NiPt合金开始形成的温度，这些温度根据Pt(111)表面上的Ni覆盖度而变化。
