<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces is conducted through X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS), high-resolution transmission electron microscopy (HRTEM), and density functional theory (DFT) calculations. This process is crucial for structural modeling as it enables researchers to understand the precise atomic arrangement on the surface, impacting the electronic structure and reactivity of the bimetallic system. DFT calculations are employed to predict the electronic properties of the bimetallic surfaces, offering insights into bonding and energy levels. This detailed characterization and theoretical modeling significantly enhance our understanding of the electronic and chemical properties of these bimetallic surfaces within advanced surface science and catalysis research.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

<ChineseVersionAnswer>
对Ni/Pt双金属表面的表征采用X射线光电子能谱(XPS)、高分辨透射电子显微镜(HRTEM)以及密度泛函理论(DFT)计算。这一过程对于结构建模至关重要，因为它使研究者能够理解表面精确的原子排列情况，这影响了双金属体系的电子结构和反应性。DFT计算被用来预测双金属表面的电子性质，提供了有关键合和能量水平的见解。这种详细表征和理论建模显著增强了我们对先进表面科学和催化研究中这些双金属表面的电子和化学性质的理解。
