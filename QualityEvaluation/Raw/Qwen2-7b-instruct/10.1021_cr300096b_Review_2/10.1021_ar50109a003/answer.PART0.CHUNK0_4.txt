<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces, synthesized by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates, typically employs scanning tunneling microscopy (STM), high-resolution x-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (HRXPS), and low-energy electron diffraction (LEED). This process is essential for structural modeling, DFT calculations, and gaining insight into the electronic and chemical properties of these surfaces in the field of advanced surface science and catalysis research.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

<ChineseVersionAnswer>
对通过在Pt(111)单晶或多晶Pt基板上沉积Ni单层合成的Ni/Pt双金属表面进行表征，通常采用扫描隧道显微镜（STM）、高分辨率x射线光电子能谱（HRXPS）和低能电子衍射（LEED）技术。这一过程对于结构建模、DFT计算以及深入理解这些双金属表面的电子和化学性质至关重要，在高级表面科学和催化研究领域中具有重要意义。
</ChineseVersionAnswer>
