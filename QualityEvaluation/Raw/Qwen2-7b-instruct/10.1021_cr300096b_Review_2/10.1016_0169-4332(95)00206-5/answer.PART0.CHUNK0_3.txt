<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces involves depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates. This process is crucial for structural modeling, DFT calculations, and our comprehensive understanding of the electronic and chemical properties of these bimetallic surfaces in the context of advanced surface science and catalysis research. Specifically, the use of Auger electron spectroscopy (AES) and low-energy electron diffraction (LEED) allows us to observe the growth mechanisms of Ni on both reconstructed (1 × 2) and unreconstructed (1 × 1) surfaces of the Pt(110) substrate at low temperatures. The results indicate that the growth of Ni on the Pt(110)-(1 × 1) surface follows the Volmer-Weber (VW) mode, while the Pt(110)-(1 × 2) surface exhibits layer growth followed by aggregation into 3D islands, indicative of the Stranski-Krastanov (SK) mode.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

<ChineseVersionAnswer>
对Ni/Pt双金属表面的表征涉及在Pt(111)单晶或聚晶Pt基底上沉积Ni单层。这一过程对于结构建模、DFT计算以及我们对这些双金属表面在先进表面科学和催化研究背景下电子和化学性质的全面理解至关重要。具体来说，通过使用AES和LEED，我们可以观察到Ni在Pt(110)基底上两种不同重构状态（1 × 2）和未重构状态（1 × 1）下的生长机制，特别是在低温下。结果表明，Ni在Pt(110)-(1 × 1)表面上的生长遵循Volmer-Weber（VW）模式，而Pt(110)-(1 × 2)表面则表现为层状生长后聚集形成三维岛屿，这符合Stranski-Krastanov（SK）模式。
</ChineseVersionAnswer>
