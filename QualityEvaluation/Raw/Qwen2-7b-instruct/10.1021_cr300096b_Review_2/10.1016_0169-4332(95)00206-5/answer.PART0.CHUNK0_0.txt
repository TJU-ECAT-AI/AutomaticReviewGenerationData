<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces involves depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates. This process is crucial for structural modeling, DFT calculations, and our comprehensive understanding of the electronic and chemical properties of these bimetallic surfaces in the context of advanced surface science and catalysis research. The Auger uptake curve analysis reveals the growth mode on the surface. For the Pt(110)-(1 × 1) surface, the exponential-type growth indicates a Volmer-Weber mode, suggesting a monolayer-by-monolayer growth. Conversely, the premonolayer break with an initial linear growth on the Pt(110)-(1 × 2) surface points to a Stranski-Krastanov mode, implying layer growth followed by 3D island formation. These findings contribute to a deeper insight into the growth mechanisms and properties of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
