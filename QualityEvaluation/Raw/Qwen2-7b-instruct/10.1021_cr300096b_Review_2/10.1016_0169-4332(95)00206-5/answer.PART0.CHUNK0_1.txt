<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces involves depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates. This process is crucial for structural modeling, DFT calculations, and gaining a comprehensive understanding of the electronic and chemical properties of these surfaces in advanced surface science and catalysis research. The growth mechanisms on the surfaces are determined through Auger electron spectroscopy (AES) and low-energy electron diffraction (LEED). The AES uptake curves and LEED spot intensity profiles reveal that the growth of Ni on the Pt(110)-(1 × 1) surface follows the Volmer-Weber (VW) mode, whereas the Pt(110)-(1 × 2) surface exhibits the Stranski-Krastanov (SK) mode of growth.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
