<EnglishVersionAnswer>
To characterize Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces synthesized by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates, the researchers monitor the surface compositions using Ni(849 eV)/Pt(241 eV) and Co(777 eV)/Pt(241 eV) Auger ratios. These ratios are adjusted for variations in electron inelastic mean free paths, atomic radii, and Auger sensitivity factors. Based on these adjustments, the researchers estimate that the onset of a monolayer at 600 K corresponds to Auger ratios of Ni(849 eV)/Pt(241 eV) 1.0 and Co(777 eV)/Pt(241 eV) 2.0. Therefore, the freshly deposited Ni/Pt or Co/Pt surfaces at 300 K are referred to as NiPtPt(111) or CoPtPt(111) surface structures, while the 600 K surfaces are designated as PtNiPt(111) or PtCoPt(111) subsurface structures.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
