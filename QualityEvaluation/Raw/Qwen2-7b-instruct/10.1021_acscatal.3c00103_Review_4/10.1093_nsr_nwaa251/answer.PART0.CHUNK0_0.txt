<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The physicochemical properties of catalysts, including chemical qualities (such as acid strength and stability) and morphological characteristics, significantly influence their performance in dehydrogenation reactions. Modification strategies to optimize these properties for enhanced catalytic efficiency and selectivity involve fine-tuning the catalyst's surface area, pore structure, and acidity. For instance, increasing the surface area can provide more active sites for reactions, while optimizing the pore structure ensures efficient mass transfer and access to reaction sites. Additionally, enhancing the acid strength can improve the dehydrogenation selectivity, leading to more efficient production of desired products. These strategies aim to maximize the catalyst's activity and reduce side reactions, thereby improving both efficiency and selectivity in dehydrogenation processes.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
