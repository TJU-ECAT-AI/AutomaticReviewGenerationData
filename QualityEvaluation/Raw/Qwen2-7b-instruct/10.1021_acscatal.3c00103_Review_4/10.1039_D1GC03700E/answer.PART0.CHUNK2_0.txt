<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. The physicochemical properties of catalysts, including their chemical qualities and morphological characteristics, significantly influence their performance in dehydrogenation reactions. These properties affect both the efficiency and selectivity of the catalysts.

2. Support modification strategies can optimize these properties to enhance catalytic efficiency and selectivity. For example, using supports with weak and moderate surface acidity can reduce the need for precious metals, leading to lower costs.

3. Promoting elements such as boron, gallium, copper, and nickel can be added to catalysts to achieve better performance. The use of noble and non-noble based intermetallic alloys and mixed metal oxides can also serve as viable alternatives to traditional catalysts.

4. Optimizing the structure of the catalyst, such as the size and shape of the particles, can improve its performance. For oxidative dehydrogenation of propane, catalysts with controlled redox and acid-base properties can minimize competing reactions.

5. Abundant elements like boron, zinc, vanadium, or zirconium can offer economic and environmental benefits in the design of catalysts for dehydrogenation reactions.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
