1. The physicochemical properties of catalysts, including their chemical qualities such as acid strength and stability, and morphological characteristics, significantly influence their performance in dehydrogenation reactions. Specifically, the presence of coordinately unsaturated Al3+ sites on the γ-Al2O3 surface can serve as anchor sites for atomically dispersed Pt and Ru sites, enhancing the catalyst's performance. For instance, a PtSn/Al2O3 sheet catalyst demonstrated excellent performance in PDH reactions at high space velocities. Additionally, eliminating hydroxyl groups from Al2O3 can increase the activity of coordinately unsaturated Al3+ sites, which are crucial for non-oxidative dehydrogenation reactions. Furthermore, γ-Al2O3 nanosheets with specific coordination numbers of Al3+ sites can effectively disperse and stabilize bimetallic nanoclusters, improving the catalytic efficiency and selectivity.

2. The structure of the catalyst plays a pivotal role in its performance. As illustrated in Figure 1, the CoAl2O3HT catalyst exhibits a sheet-like morphology, consisting of nanosheets with a thickness of less than 6 nm, devoid of nanoparticles. In contrast, the Co/Al2O3-IMP catalyst displays individual nanoparticles dispersed on the Al2O3 sheets. These morphological differences suggest that the structure of the catalyst influences its activity and selectivity in PDH reactions.

3. Detailed characterizations, including UVVisDR spectra, XRD patterns, TGA, and XPS, are essential for understanding the physicochemical properties of catalysts. These techniques provide insights into the catalyst's composition, phase structure, and surface chemistry, enabling researchers to optimize the catalyst for enhanced catalytic efficiency and selectivity in dehydrogenation reactions.

4. The effect of C3H6 desorption behavior in the Co-catalyzed PDH reaction is a critical factor affecting the catalyst's performance. Understanding and optimizing this behavior can lead to improved catalytic activity and stability in PDH processes.
