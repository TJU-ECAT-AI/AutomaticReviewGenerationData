<p>Along with the decreasing fossil oil reserves and development on utilization of nature gas and shale gas, propane dehydrogenation (PDH) seems to be a promising alternative to produce propylene.</p>
<p>The main problems of Pt-based catalyst are its low selectivity to propylene and fast deactivation caused by coke formation.</p>
<p>The most active site for the first dehydrogenation step is a bridge-bound surface O atom.</p>
<p>Once the H is abstracted from hydrocarbon, rather stable surface hydroxyl groups are formed.</p>
<p>The direct removal of the H, either as H2 or H2O, to regenerate the O site of the catalyst is difficult and will eventually decrease the activity of the catalyst.</p>
<p>The initial dehydrogenation barrier of ethane on such a Zn site is lower than the barrier at Zn of a conventional ion-exchange site.</p>
<p>This is related to the stronger Lewis acidity in the former case caused by the indirect charge-compensation.</p>
<p>[GaH]2+ is more stable in a six-membered ring structure than in an eight-membered ring.</p>
<p>Calculations suggested that the optimal AlAl distance to be 453 pm corresponding to a minimum overall barrier, and a simple structure-to-activity correlation based on the Sabatier principle was proposed.</p>
<p>Guo et al. reported a direct, nonoxidative process for the conversion of methane to ethylene, aromatics and hydrogen that is catalyzed by a single Fe atom embedded in a silica matrix.</p>
<p>In this catalyst, the Zn2+ center is coordinated with three O centers of the SiO2 surface.</p>
<p>The formation of graphene islands on Ni is a structural dependent process, which requires step sites on the Ni surface.</p>
<p>Note that the formation of the graphene island is essentially independent from the dehydrogenation steps, whereas the transportation of the C atoms and the growth of the graphene islands are the crucial molecular processes.</p>
<p>The adsorption of C atoms on the closed packed surface of a NiAu surface alloy is thermodynamically less favoured, by 23 kJ mol1 or more, than the analogous process on a pure Ni(111) surface.</p>
<p>A similar strategy, by blocking the crucial sites, has been proposed to prevent the formation of a carbidic phase.</p>

1. Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) is considered a promising method for producing propylene due to the depletion of fossil oil reserves and advancements in utilizing natural gas and shale gas.

2. Platinum-based catalysts face challenges in selectivity towards propylene and rapid deactivation due to coke formation.

3. The initial dehydrogenation step on a bridging surface oxygen atom is the most active site for propane dehydrogenation.

4. After abstraction of hydrogen from hydrocarbons, stable surface hydroxyl groups are formed.

5. Regeneration of the catalyst's active site through direct removal of hydrogen as H2 or H2O is difficult and reduces the catalyst's activity.

6. The dehydrogenation barrier for ethane on a zinc site that is indirectly compensated for charge is lower than on a conventional ion-exchange site.

7. The stronger Lewis acidity in the former case is due to indirect charge compensation.

8. [GaH]2+ is more stable in a six-membered ring structure than in an eight-membered ring.

9. The optimal Al-Al distance for a minimum overall barrier is calculated to be 453 pm, supporting a simple structure-to-activity correlation based on the Sabatier principle.

10. Guo et al. reported a process that directly converts methane to ethylene, aromatics, and hydrogen, catalyzed by a single iron atom embedded in a silica matrix.

11. In the Zn/ZSM-5 system, the Zn2+ center coordinates with three O centers of the SiO2 surface.

12. The formation of graphene islands on nickel is a structural-dependent process that necessitates step sites on the nickel surface.

13. The growth of graphene islands involves the transportation of carbon atoms and their subsequent aggregation on the surface.

14. Adsorption of carbon atoms on a nickel-aurum surface alloy is less favorable thermodynamically than on pure nickel(111).

15. Strategies involving blocking key sites are proposed to prevent the formation of a carbidic phase.
