1. The physicochemical properties of catalysts, including chemical qualities (such as acid strength and stability) and morphological characteristics, play a crucial role in influencing their performance in dehydrogenation reactions. Chemical qualities determine the reaction rates and selectivities, while morphological characteristics affect the accessibility of reactants to active sites and the diffusion of products. Modification strategies such as adjusting the concentration of alkali metal ions, dealinguminating the catalyst, or using an alcoholic solution can alter these properties, thus optimizing catalytic efficiency and selectivity.
