To understand how the physicochemical properties of catalysts, including chemical qualities and morphological characteristics, influence their performance in dehydrogenation reactions, we need to consider the role of diffusion phenomena. These properties impact the movement of reactants and products within the catalyst structure, affecting reaction rates and selectivity. For instance, the presence of transport barriers or the nature of the diffusion path (microscopic or macroscopic) can dictate the efficiency of the catalytic process. Techniques like PFG NMR, which can provide insights into both microscopic and macroscopic diffusion processes, offer valuable tools for studying these properties. By analyzing the diffusion coefficients and patterns, researchers can identify optimal modifications to catalysts that enhance their performance in dehydrogenation reactions. This might involve adjusting the acidity, stability, or morphology of the catalyst to improve its selectivity and activity.
