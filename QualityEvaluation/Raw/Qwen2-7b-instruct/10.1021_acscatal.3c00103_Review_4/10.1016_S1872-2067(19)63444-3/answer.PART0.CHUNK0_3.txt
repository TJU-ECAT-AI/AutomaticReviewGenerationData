<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The physicochemical properties of catalysts, including their chemical qualities like acid strength and stability, and morphological characteristics such as particle size, shape, and surface area, greatly influence their performance in dehydrogenation reactions. To optimize these properties for enhanced catalytic efficiency and selectivity, various modification strategies can be employed. These strategies might include altering the catalyst's composition, modifying its surface chemistry, or adjusting its structure.
