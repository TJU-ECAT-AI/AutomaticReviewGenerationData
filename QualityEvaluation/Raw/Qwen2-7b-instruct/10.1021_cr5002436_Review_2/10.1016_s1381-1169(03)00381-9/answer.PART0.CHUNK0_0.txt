<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Chromium catalysts have evolved significantly since their discovery in 1933, with advancements in understanding their structure, active sites, support materials, promoters, and reaction mechanisms contributing to their significance in dehydrogenation processes. Key industrial applications include ethylene polymerization and dehydrogenation reactions of hydrocarbons to olefins. Research focuses on optimizing catalyst properties for enhanced activity and selectivity.

2. O2 and CO chemisorption are validated methods for quantifying Cr3+ and Cr2+ sites, respectively, which are crucial active species in dehydrogenation and ethylene polymerization reactions.

3. The distribution of chromium species across the catalyst, influenced by its composition, precursor, and support, correlates with the catalytic performance in dehydrogenation and polymerization.

4. The activity in cyclohexane dehydrogenation is influenced by the dispersion of amorphous Cr3+ species.

5. In ethylene polymerization, the activity is related to the quantity of Cr2+ and Cr2+ B species.

6. The selectivity of O2 and CO chemisorption in characterizing Cr2+ and amorphous Cr3+ sites, respectively, has been confirmed.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
