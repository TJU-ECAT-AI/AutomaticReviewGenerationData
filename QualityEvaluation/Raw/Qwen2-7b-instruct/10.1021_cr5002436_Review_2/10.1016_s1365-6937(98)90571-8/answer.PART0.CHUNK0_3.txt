<EnglishVersionAnswer>
ABB will handle the engineering, procurement, and construction of the power plant, including the power plant control, distribution systems, and other plant equipment. Work on the new power plant is scheduled to start this year, with commercial operation planned for the summer of 2000. The contract for this project, worth over US$350 million, was initiated by American National Power Inc., a subsidiary of National Power plc UK. Duke/Fluor Daniel, a joint venture between Duke Energy Corp and Fluor Daniel, offers engineering, procurement, construction, operations, and maintenance services for fossil-fueled power plants globally. The GTi Dakar power plant in Senegal, developed and sponsored by GE Capital Structured Finance Group, will use energy-efficient power generation equipment provided by NUovo Pignone, a subsidiary of General Electric Power Systems.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
