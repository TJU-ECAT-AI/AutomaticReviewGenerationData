            The evolution, industrial significance, and multifaceted research of chromium oxide-based catalysts in dehydrogenation processes since their discovery in 1933 have been a subject of extensive study. These catalysts, particularly chromium oxide catalysts like vanadium oxide and chromium on silica-alumina, have found extensive commercial applications in oxidation processes, including the selective oxidation of o-xylene, ammoxidation of alkyl aromatics, and the catalytic reduction of NOx. The higher surface density of vanadia species enhances catalytic activity, minimizing unwanted side reactions, and the unique properties of vanadia catalysts are attributed to their weak acidic character compared to other transition metal oxides.

            Chromium oxide-based catalysts have also been pivotal in the polymerization of ethylene, propylene, and the dehydrogenation of alkanes, such as propane, to produce high-purity propene used for polymer materials. The catalysts' structure, active sites, support materials, and reaction mechanisms are key areas of research. Studies have focused on understanding the formation and nature of chromium species on the catalyst surface, identifying the valence state of the species responsible for catalysis, and the redox behavior of chromium on different supports.

            Since 1933, chromium oxide-based catalysts have been extensively studied in alkane dehydrogenation, an industrially important process for the production of high-purity propene. Research has emphasized the optimization of catalyst performance through modifications to support materials, such as increasing θ-alumina through higher pretreatment temperatures, which positively impacts dehydrogenation activity. UV-vis spectroscopy has been instrumental in monitoring catalyst behavior under reaction conditions, providing insights into changes in coordination environment and oxidation state.

            The catalysts' evolution includes the development of new series of chromium oxide-based catalysts containing 0.77 wt.% of Cr, prepared by impregnation of conventional amorphous silicas and mesoporous siliceous sieves with cubic and hexagonal pore structures. The influence of Cr loading and support properties on the surface composition and Cr dispersion has been investigated using various physicochemical characterization techniques. The catalytic performance of chromium oxide supported on different siliceous supports has been presented, leading to the conclusion that chromium species enable alternative oxidative pathways for propene formation.

            Challenges like catalyst deactivation and regeneration have been addressed in commercial reactor designs, taking different approaches to manage these issues through oxidation and periodic regeneration. Detailed catalyst surface analysis via techniques like X-ray diffraction, UV-visible spectroscopy, and EPR has provided insights into catalyst dynamics.

            Chromium oxide catalysts have been pivotal in dehydrogenation processes, notably in petrochemical production of polypropylene, acrylic acid, and propylene oxide. Research emphasizes the importance of surface composition, Cr dispersion, and chromium-silica interaction in enhancing catalytic activity. The presence of crystalline α-Cr2O3 on the surface of silica has a negative influence on catalytic activity, as not all of the Cr atoms are accessible to reactant molecules. The high dispersion of active sites has been shown to optimize catalytic performance.

            The vast majority of catalysts suggested for FTO contain iron, which is less expensive, has a lower activity, and displays lower methane selectivity at high temperatures necessary to drive alpha to lower values compared to cobalt-based catalysts. Researchers have enhanced catalyst performance through Fe-based bimetallic catalysts. The design strategy used by Kang et al. involves using a bifunctional catalyst where the FT active metal transforms syngas into a primary straight hydrocarbon chain that undergoes cracking on the acid zeolite to produce hydrocarbons of limited chain length.

            Iron-based catalysts, particularly those on aluminum oxide support, have been pivotal in dehydrogenating paraffins since 1933, producing propene and isobutene. Their efficacy hinges on redox-active Cr3+ sites, which enable the reduction of Cr6+ under conditions like hydrogen, methane, or n-butane exposure at 580°C. At lower loadings, Cr6+ anchors on alumina, while higher loadings feature stabilized Cr3+ species, crucial for the catalyst's performance.

            Most characterization studies on supported chromium oxide catalysts describe the catalyst structure under conditions often far away from real catalytic ones. The dehydrogenation activity is proportional with the amount of in situ measured surface Cr, and maximum for a 7.5 wt.% CrAl2O3 catalyst operating at 500°C in 2% isobutane in N2. Active Cr-sites are more effective than Cr2q-sites in alkane dehydrogenation, underscoring the catalyst's nuanced performance.

            Supported chromium oxide catalysts are crucial in the chemical industry for the production of several important commodity chemicals. Cr/SiO2 catalysts are known as the Phillips catalyst for the production of high-density polyethylene (HDPE) and linear low density polyethylene (LLDPE), and Cr/Al2O3 catalysts are used for the dehydrogenation of butane to butene. The industrial importance of chromium oxide catalysts has attracted a great deal of interest in industrial and academic research toward the elucidation of the molecular structure of supported chromium oxides.

            Selective oxidative dehydrogenation of propane over V-Mg-O catalysts was studied, revealing that at 500-540°C, changing the propane/O2 ratio from 1:2 to 1:1 decreases propane conversion by 10% but increases propene formation selectivity by 10%, highlighting the significance of chromium oxide-based catalysts in dehydrogenation processes since their discovery in 1933.

            Since the pioneering work of Frey and Huppke, the superior properties of supported chromium oxide catalysts in alkane dehydrogenation are well-known, and Cr2O3/Al2O3 catalysts are widely used as the commercial catalyst of choice. Recent research focuses on identifying active sites, understanding the impact of preparation and treatment on catalyst properties, and elucidating dehydrogenation mechanisms. The main result concerning Cr2O3/Al2O3 catalysts until the late 1960s are summarized in review papers by Poole and Mciver, and by Kearby. All the newer studies primarily focus on identifying the active sites, influence of preparation and treatment conditions on chromium oxide speciation, and on the catalytic activity/selecitivity, and elucidation of the dehydrogenation mechanism.

            The catalytic dehydrogenation of propane under oxidative and nonoxidative conditions has been investigated in a flow apparatus. Under nonoxidative conditions at 823 K, the initial activity varies between 1.56 μmol of propene·g-1·s-1 for the sample Ga/Cr 70/30 and 2.62 μmol of propene·g-1·s-1 for the sample Ga/Cr 50/50. Under oxidative conditions, the catalytic activity at 673 K, after 5 h of reaction, varies between 0.45 and 1.22 μmol of propene·g-1·s-1. The system was found to be highly active and selective in the dehydrogenation of propane at 723–823 K. Cr(III) species are the active sites with a possible role of the oxide ions adjacent to Cr(III).

                    自1933年发现以来，铬氧化物基催化剂在脱氢过程中的演变、工业重要性和多方面的研究已经进行了广泛的研究。这些催化剂，尤其是铬氧化物催化剂，如钒氧化物和铬在硅藻土上的氧化物，已经在氧化过程中找到了广泛的商业应用，包括选择性氧化对甲苯、烷基芳香族化合物的氨氧化以及NOx的催化还原。范德华物种的更高表面密度增强了催化活性，最大限度地减少了暴露的氧化物支持的副反应，并且vanadia催化剂的独特性质归因于与其他过渡金属氧化物相比其较弱的酸性特征。

                    自1933年以来，铬氧化物基催化剂在烷烃脱氢过程中的作用至关重要，这一过程是生产高纯度丙烯用于聚合物材料的重要途径。研究强调了催化剂结构、活性位点、支持材料和反应机制的关键领域。已经专注于理解催化剂表面的形成和性质，确定负责催化作用的物种的电荷状态，以及不同支持物的红氧化行为。

                    自1933年以来，铬氧化物基催化剂在烷烃脱氢过程中的演变包括开发包含0.77 wt.%的Cr的新系列铬氧化物基催化剂，通过浸渍常规无定形硅和具有立方和六角孔结构的微孔硅质筛子。已经调查了Cr负载和支持物属性对表面组成和Cr分散的影响，使用各种物理化学表征技术。在不同的硅质支持上呈现的催化剂性能表明，铬物种能够启用丙烷脱氢产生丙烯的替代氧化途径。

                    面临挑战，如催化剂失活和再生，已经被商业反应器设计以不同方式管理，通过氧化和定期再生。详细催化剂表面分析通过技术如X射线衍射、UV-可见光谱和EPR提供了有关催化剂动态性的见解。

                    铬氧化物催化剂在聚丙烯、丙烯酸和丙烯氧化合物的石油化工生产中发挥了至关重要的作用。研究强调了表面组成、Cr分散性和铬-硅相互作用对于增强催化活性的重要性。在表面上存在的结晶α-Cr2O3对催化活性有负面影响，因为不是所有Cr原子都对反应分子可访问。

                    大多数催化剂建议用于FTO含有铁，这比钴基催化剂更便宜，活动性较低，Fe FischerTropsch产品的烯烃含量较高，因为铁对二级氢化反应的反应性较低，并且在驱动α至较低值时显示出较低的甲烷选择性。研究人员通过使用Fe基双金属催化剂提高了催化剂性能。Kang等人的设计策略涉及使用双功能催化剂，其中FT活性金属将合成气转化为一个主要的直链碳氢化合物链，该链在酸沸石上裂解生成有限链长的碳氢化合物。

                    铁基催化剂，特别是那些在氧化铝支撑上的，自1933年以来，在脱氢帕拉芬中发挥了关键作用，产生了丙烯和异丁烯。它们的效能取决于还原活性的Cr3+位点，这些位点能够在氢、甲烷或n-丁烷暴露下的580°C条件下将Cr6+还原。在较低负载下，Cr6+锚定在氧化铝上，而在较高负载下，稳定了Cr3+物种，对于催化剂的性能至关重要。

                    支持的铬氧化物催化剂在化学工业中对几种重要的商品化学品的生产至关重要。Cr/SiO2催化剂作为生产高密度聚乙烯（HDPE）和线性低密度聚乙烯（LLDPE）的菲利普斯催化剂，而Cr/Al2O3催化剂用于异丁烷到异丁烯的脱氢。铬氧化物催化剂的工业重要性吸引了工业和学术研究对支持的铬氧化物分子结构的深入了解。
