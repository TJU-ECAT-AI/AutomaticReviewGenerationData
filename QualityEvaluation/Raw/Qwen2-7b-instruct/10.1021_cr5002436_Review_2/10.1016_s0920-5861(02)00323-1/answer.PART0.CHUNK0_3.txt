Since their discovery in 1933, chromium oxide-based catalysts have evolved significantly. Key industrial applications and research focus areas include understanding the structure of the catalyst, identifying active sites, selecting appropriate support materials, incorporating promoters to enhance performance, and elucidating reaction mechanisms. These advancements have been pivotal in the dehydrogenation processes, enabling more efficient and selective conversions of hydrocarbons to valuable chemical products.
