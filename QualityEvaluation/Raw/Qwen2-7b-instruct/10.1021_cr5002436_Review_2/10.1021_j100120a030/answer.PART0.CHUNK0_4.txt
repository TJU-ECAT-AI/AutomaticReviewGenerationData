<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Since their discovery in 1933, chromium oxide-based catalysts have evolved significantly, particularly in the context of ethylene polymerization, where they play a crucial role in the Phillips particle-form process for producing high-density polyethylene. They have also been adapted for the production of linear low-density polyethylene and for the copolymerization of ethylene and butene-1. Additionally, these catalysts are utilized in the polymerization of propylene and in processes such as dehydrogenation of alkanes, dehydrocyclization, and catalytic reforming.

2. The key industrial applications of chromium oxide-based catalysts include the production of high-density and linear low-density polyethylene through ethylene polymerization, the copolymerization of ethylene and butene-1, the polymerization of propylene, and the dehydrogenation of alkanes, dehydrocyclization, and catalytic reforming.

3. Research focus areas related to these catalysts involve understanding the structure, active sites, support materials, promoters, and reaction mechanisms. The structure of the catalysts, including the coordination geometry of the chromium species on the surface, is of particular interest. Active sites on the catalyst surface are critical for the efficiency of the reactions they catalyze. The type of support material, such as silica, alumina, or silica-alumina, affects the properties of the catalyst and its performance. Promoters, if used, influence the reactivity and selectivity of the catalyst. Reaction mechanisms, including how the catalysts facilitate the specific chemical transformations, are also extensively studied.

4. The redox behavior of chromium on alumina (Al203), sulfur dioxide (SO2), and silica-alumina (Si02.A1203) is a significant focus in research. This involves understanding how the oxidation states of chromium change under different conditions and the implications of these changes for the catalytic activity of the materials.

5. The evolution of chromium oxide-based catalysts has been driven by advancements in understanding the fundamental chemistry and physics underlying their operation. These advancements have led to improvements in catalyst design, optimization of operating conditions, and the development of new applications. The field continues to evolve with ongoing research aimed at enhancing catalyst performance, efficiency, and selectivity.

1. 自1933年发现以来，铬氧化物催化剂经历了显著的发展，特别是在乙烯聚合领域发挥了关键作用，这是菲利普斯粒子形过程的基础，用于生产高密度聚乙烯。它们也被扩展用于生产线性低密度聚乙烯以及乙烯和丁烯-1的共聚。此外，这些催化剂还用于丙烯聚合、烷烃脱氢、环化反应和催化重整等过程。

2. 铬氧化物催化剂的主要工业应用包括通过乙烯聚合生产高密度和线性低密度聚乙烯、乙烯和丁烯-1的共聚、丙烯聚合以及烷烃脱氢、环化反应和催化重整。

3. 关于这些催化剂的研究焦点涉及理解其结构、活性位点、支撑材料、促进剂和反应机制。催化剂的结构，特别是表面铬物种的配位几何结构，是特别感兴趣的。催化剂表面的活性位点对于它们催化特定化学转化的效率至关重要。所使用的支撑材料类型（如二氧化硅、氧化铝或二氧化硅-氧化铝）影响催化剂的特性和性能。如果使用了促进剂，则会影响催化剂的反应活性和选择性。研究反应机制，即催化剂如何促进特定化学转换，也是广泛研究的主题。

4. 研究铬在氧化铝（Al203）、二氧化硫（SO2）和二氧化硅-氧化铝（Si02.A1203）上的还原行为是一个重要焦点。这涉及到在不同条件下铬的氧化状态的变化及其对催化剂活性的影响。

5. 铬氧化物催化剂的发展驱动因素在于对催化操作背后的基底化学和物理学的深入理解。这些进展导致了催化剂设计的改进、操作条件的优化以及新应用的发展。随着持续研究旨在提高催化剂性能、效率和选择性的领域继续发展。
