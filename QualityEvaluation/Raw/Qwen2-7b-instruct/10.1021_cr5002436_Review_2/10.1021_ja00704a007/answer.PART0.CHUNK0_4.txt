<p>The evolution of chromium oxide-based catalysts since their discovery in 1933 has been marked by advancements in understanding their structure, active sites, support materials, promoters, and reaction mechanisms. The key industrial applications include petrochemical processes such as dehydrogenation, isomerization, and reforming, where chromium oxide catalysts enable efficient conversion of hydrocarbons.</p>
<p>Research focus areas have centered on improving catalyst stability, selectivity, and activity, often through the optimization of support materials, promoter addition, and pretreatment conditions. The aim is to develop catalysts that can withstand harsh reaction conditions, operate over a wide temperature range, and maintain high efficiency over extended periods.</p>
</quotes>

Key industrial applications include petrochemical processes such as dehydrogenation, isomerization, and reforming, where these catalysts enable efficient conversion of hydrocarbons.
2. Research focus areas have been on improving catalyst stability, selectivity, and activity, often through the optimization of support materials, promoter addition, and pretreatment conditions. The goal is to develop catalysts that can withstand harsh reaction conditions, operate over a wide temperature range, and maintain high efficiency over extended periods.
