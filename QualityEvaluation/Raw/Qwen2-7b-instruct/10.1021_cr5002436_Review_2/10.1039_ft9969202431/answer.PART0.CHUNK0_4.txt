Answer: The evolution of chromium oxide-based catalysts since their discovery in 1933 includes their widespread recognition for catalytic performance, extensive study, particularly of the industrially used Phillips catalyst (Cr/SiO,), and the development of advanced characterization techniques like electron paramagnetic resonance (EPR) to understand the structure, active sites, support materials, promoters, and reaction mechanisms. The focus areas include the coordination environments of chromium ions (Cr3+ and Cr5+) on various supports, the identification of specific types of Cr3+ and Cr5+ species, and the role of chromium in dehydrogenation processes through understanding the interaction between chromium and the support materials.
