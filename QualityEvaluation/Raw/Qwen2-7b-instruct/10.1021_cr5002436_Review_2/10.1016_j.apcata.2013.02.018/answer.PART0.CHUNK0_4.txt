The evolution of chromium oxide-based catalysts since their discovery in 1933 has been marked by significant advancements in their industrial applications and research focus areas. These include:

1. **Key Industrial Applications**: Chromium oxide catalysts play a crucial role in the production of isobutene, which is essential for synthesizing MTBE (methyl tert-butyl ether) and ETBE (ethyl tert-butyl ether), both widely used as octane number boosters in unleaded gasoline. They are also pivotal in the dehydrogenation processes for paraffins, occupying over half of the global commercial catalyst market for this purpose.

2. **Research Focus Areas**:
   - **Structure**: The nature of surface chromium in these catalysts significantly influences their catalytic activity and selectivity.
   - **Active Sites**: The dispersion of chromium species over the support material impacts the catalyst's performance, particularly in dehydrogenation reactions.
   - **Support Materials**: Various preparation methods, including incipient wetness impregnation, atomic layer epitaxy (ALE), co-precipitation, and evaporation-induced self-assembly (EISA) strategies, have been developed to enhance the catalyst's properties.
   - **Promoters**: The addition of alkali metals, such as potassium, is being studied for their ability to improve the catalyst's selectivity and resistance to deactivation.
   - **Reaction Mechanisms**: Research is ongoing to understand the detailed mechanisms involved in dehydrogenation processes, aiming to optimize the catalysts' efficiency and longevity.

3. **Synthesis and Characterization**: The use of metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) like MIL-101 offers a novel approach to synthesize chromium oxide catalysts with desired properties, such as large specific surface areas and high pore volumes, enhancing their catalytic performance in dehydrogenation reactions.

4. **Stability and Regeneration**: Studies focus on evaluating the catalyst's stability and regenerative capacity, which is critical for maintaining optimal performance over multiple cycles.

These advancements reflect the ongoing efforts to refine and optimize chromium oxide catalysts for more efficient and sustainable industrial applications, particularly in the realm of petrochemical processing.

自1933年发现以来，铬氧化物催化剂的发展经历了显著的进步，其工业应用和研究重点领域包括：

1. **关键工业应用**：铬氧化物催化剂在MTBE（甲基叔丁基醚）和ETBE（乙基叔丁基醚）合成中的重要作用是汽油无铅化的主要添加剂，用于提高辛烷值。它们还被广泛应用于烷烃脱氢过程，占全球商业催化剂市场的一半以上。

2. **研究焦点领域**：
   - **结构**：表面铬的性质对催化剂的催化活性和选择性有重要影响。
   - **活性位点**：铬物种在支撑材料上的分散程度直接影响催化剂的性能，特别是脱氢反应。
   - **支撑材料**：包括浸渍法、原子层外延（ALE）、共沉淀和蒸发诱导自组装（EISA）等方法在内的各种制备技术的发展，旨在增强催化剂的性质。
   - **促进剂**：研究钾等碱金属的添加如何改善催化剂的选择性和抗失活能力。
   - **反应机理**：正在进行深入研究以理解脱氢过程的详细机制，以优化催化剂的效率和寿命。

3. **合成与表征**：使用金属有机框架（MOFs）如MIL-101提供了一种新的合成铬氧化物催化剂的方法，这些催化剂具有所需的特性，如大的比表面积和高孔体积，从而增强其在脱氢反应中的催化性能。

4. **稳定性和再生**：研究集中在评估催化剂的稳定性和再生能力，这对于维持多次循环的最佳性能至关重要。

这些进步反映了持续努力改进和优化铬氧化物催化剂，以实现更高效和可持续的工业应用，特别是在石油化学加工领域的应用。
