1. Various methods for preparing PtCo nanoparticles were used by altering the initial composition of the precursor solution.
2. The reduction reaction takes place in a confined area within the microemulsions.
3. Preparations of PtCo nanoparticles were conducted by modifying the initial composition of the precursor solution.
4. For all PtCo compositions, a narrow size distribution with an average of approximately 2 nanometers was noted.
5. Uniformity in both size and composition of the PtCo nanoparticles was achieved.
6. Among the electrodes with different Pt:Co ratios, the 1:0.5 ratio demonstrated the best electrocatalytic performance.
7. An optimal ratio of Pt:Co exists, ensuring a balanced activity for the dehydrogenation step involving platinum and the subsequent steps involving cobalt.
8. The relationship between the catalytic properties of PtCo nanoparticles and their electronic properties was explored by plotting steady-state currents at certain potentials against the Pt:Co ratio.
