<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The most commonly utilized synthesis methods for supported bimetallic catalysts include thermal evaporation-deposition, chemical vapor deposition, and sol-gel methods. Thermal evaporation-deposition provides high control over particle size and shape but requires high temperatures. Chemical vapor deposition offers excellent control over catalyst composition and morphology, enabling precise tailoring of the catalyst properties. Sol-gel methods are versatile, capable of producing catalysts with complex shapes and architectures, though they may have lower yield compared to other techniques.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
