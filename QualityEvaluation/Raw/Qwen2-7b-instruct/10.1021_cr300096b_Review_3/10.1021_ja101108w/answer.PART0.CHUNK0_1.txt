<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The most commonly utilized synthesis methods for supported bimetallic catalysts are coprecipitation, impregnation, and sol-gel processes. These methods have different potential advantages, applications, and overall efficacy in catalyst production. Coprecipitation provides precise control over metal composition and size distribution. Impregnation is simple and flexible regarding the selection of support material. Sol-gel processes result in a homogeneous catalyst structure, which leads to enhanced activity and stability.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
