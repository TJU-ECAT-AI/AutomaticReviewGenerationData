<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Supported bimetallic catalysts can be produced using dendrimer-encapsulated nanoparticles (DENs) by first immobilizing the dendrimer template onto a solid support.

2. After immobilizing the dendrimer, removing it might seem desirable, but doing so can cause the particle size and size distribution to increase.

3. Initial attempts to create dendrimer-free heterogeneous catalysts involved using Pd DENs supported on mica or highly oriented pyrolytic graphite (HOPG).

4. However, when calcinated at 630°C, the particles aggregated into large clusters.

5. Pd DENs within G6-NH2 were observed to have strong interactions with the step edges of HOPG surfaces.

6. Unfortunately, calcination at 630°C resulted in the particles aggregating into large clusters.

7. In contrast, our recent research has demonstrated that removing PAMAM dendrimers from Pd or Au DENs supported on titania requires temperatures of 500°C in air.

8. Using these conditions, the particle size increased; for instance, the average particle size of G4-Q32(Au55) nanoparticles deposited on titania escalated from 1.7 ± 0.4 nm to 7.2 ± 3.6 nm.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

2. 在移除模板后，可能希望提高颗粒大小和粒度分布，但这种做法可能导致颗粒增大。

3. 初步尝试使用包含在G6-NH2中的Pd DENs支持在mica或高定向石墨（HOPG）上来制备无模板的异相催化剂。

5. 包含在G6-NH2中的Pd DENs被发现在HOPG表面的台阶边缘有很强的相互作用。

6. 不幸的是，在630°C下热处理后，这些颗粒聚集成了大型簇。

7. 相反，我们的最新研究表明，从氧化钛上支持的Pd或Au DENs中移除PAMAM树状体需要在空气中加热至500°C。

8. 使用这种方法，颗粒大小增加；例如，G4-Q32(Au55)纳米颗粒沉积在氧化钛上的平均颗粒大小从1.7±0.4 nm增加到了7.2±3.6 nm。
