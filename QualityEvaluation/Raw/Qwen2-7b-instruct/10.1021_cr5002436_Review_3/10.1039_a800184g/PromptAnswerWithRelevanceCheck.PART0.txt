Variables such as carrier type, support surface area, and metal loading significantly affect the nature and behavior of vanadium species within these catalysts. Metal loading determines the concentration of vanadium species, which can influence the overall catalytic activity and the formation of desired products. These factors collectively contribute to the optimization of vanadium oxide-based catalysts for more efficient and environmentally friendly paraffin dehydrogenation.
