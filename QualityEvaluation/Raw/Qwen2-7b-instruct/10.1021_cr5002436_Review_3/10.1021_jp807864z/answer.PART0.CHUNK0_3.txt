<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Vanadium oxide-based catalysts have been explored as alternatives to platinum (Pt) and chromium oxide (CrOx) for paraffin dehydrogenation due to their potential for being more cost-effective, environmentally friendly, and industrially scalable.
2. Research in this field has advanced substantially since the 1980s, concentrating on elucidating the detailed mechanisms of dehydrogenation processes and enhancing catalyst characteristics.
3. Factors like the carrier type, support surface area, and metal loading significantly affect the properties and performance of vanadium species in these catalysts.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
