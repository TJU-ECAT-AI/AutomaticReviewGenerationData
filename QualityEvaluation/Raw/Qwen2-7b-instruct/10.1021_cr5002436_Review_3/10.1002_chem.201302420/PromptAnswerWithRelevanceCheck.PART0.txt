Research in this area has evolved significantly since the 1980s, with advancements in understanding the role of vanadium species and the impact of various factors on catalyst performance. Variables such as carrier type, support surface area, and metal loading influence the nature and behavior of vanadium in these catalysts. The introduction of vanadium doping, for instance, has been shown to stabilize the redox properties of molybdenum carbides and enhance their catalytic activity for dehydrogenation reactions.
