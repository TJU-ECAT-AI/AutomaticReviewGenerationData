1. Catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) serves as a method for producing propene. Important factors to consider include reaction conditions, catalyst types, and industrial applications. 

2. Coke formation on platinum-based catalysts can significantly affect the efficiency of the PDH process.

3. To mitigate coke formation and enhance catalyst regeneration, strategies such as using catalysts with higher thermal stability, implementing periodic catalyst replacement, and optimizing operating conditions can be implemented.
