<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) functions as a method for producing propylene by converting propane into propylene through a series of chemical reactions. Key considerations regarding reaction conditions involve temperature, pressure, and the presence of a suitable catalyst. Catalyst types play a crucial role in the efficiency of PDH processes, with Ga2O3-based catalysts showing contrasting performance compared to others. Industrial applications of PDH are widespread in petrochemical industries for propylene production.

2. Coke formation on Pt-based catalysts during propane dehydrogenation negatively affects process efficiency due to reduced surface area available for reactions and potential blockage of catalyst pores. Strategies to mitigate coke formation and improve catalyst regeneration include optimizing operating conditions, such as temperature and pressure, implementing advanced catalyst design to enhance stability and resistance to coking, and employing periodic catalyst regeneration techniques.
