<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Propane dehydrogenation to produce propene is an endothermic and thermodynamically limited reaction requiring high temperatures above 550°C to achieve a propane conversion of over 50% at atmospheric pressure. This process is motivated by the use of propene as a feedstock for the production of valuable chemicals, including polymers, oxygenates, and other chemical intermediates.

2. Multiple installations for the selective production of propene are being constructed or planned.

3. Noble metals and metal oxides display catalytic activity and selectivity for CH bond activation in propane dehydrogenation. However, severe catalyst deactivation occurs due to coke deposition on the surface of active materials and sintering of active phases under high temperatures.

4. Various transition metal oxides, such as GaOx, VOx, FeOx, InOx, ZrOx, and ZnOx, have been reported as active for CH bond activation in propane.

5. Tungsten oxide is of significant interest due to its applications in heterogeneous catalysis, photochemistry, and electrochemistry.

6. Oxygen vacancies and multivalent oxidation states in tungsten oxides contribute to unique catalytic properties.

7. Bulk and supported tungsten oxides are shown to be active in dehydrogenation, hydrogenation, and isomerization of hydrocarbons.

8. Activating tungsten oxide through hydrogen pretreatment or co-feeding of hydrogen enhances its catalytic activity for propane dehydrogenation.

9. Partially reduced tungsten oxide (WO3x) exhibits superior catalytic activity and selectivity compared to Cr2O3 and Ga2O3.

10. Partially reduced tungsten oxide obtained through 2.5 hours of hydrogen pretreatment has significantly higher initial activity than air-pretreated Cr2O3 and Ga2O3.

11. The catalytic activity of tungsten oxide catalysts largely depends on hydrogen reduction conditions.

12. After hydrogen pretreatment, partially reduced tungsten oxide displays superior catalytic activity and selectivity compared to other highly active metal oxides, such as Cr2O3 and Ga2O3.

13. Fully oxidized bulk tungsten oxide (WO3) is inactive under propane feed conditions.

14. Coke formation on platinum-based catalysts reduces process efficiency.

15. Strategies to mitigate coke formation and improve catalyst regeneration include pretreatment with oxidizing agents and regular regeneration under high temperature conditions.

1. 烯烃脱氢将丙烷转化为丙烯是一个吸热且热力学受限的反应，需要高于550°C的高温，在大气压下实现丙烷转化率超过50%。这个过程受到丙烯作为生产聚合物、氧化物和其他化学中间体原料的推动。

2. 多个用于选择性生产丙烯的装置正在建设或规划中。

3. 稀有金属和金属氧化物在丙烷脱氢反应中显示了催化活性和选择性。然而，在高温条件下，由于焦炭沉积在活性材料表面和活性催化剂相的结块，催化剂的活性会严重降低。

4. 包括镓氧化物、钒氧化物、铁氧化物、铟氧化物、锆氧化物和锌氧化物在内的各种过渡金属氧化物已被报告为丙烷脱氢反应中的活性物质。

5. 钨氧化物因其在异质催化、光化学和电化学方面的应用而受到广泛关注。

6. 氧化物中的氧空位和多价氧化态导致独特的催化特性。

7. 整体和支撑的钨氧化物被证明是脱氢、氢化和烃类异构化的活性催化剂。

8. 通过氢预处理或氢气共给激活钨氧化物增强了其丙烷脱氢催化活性。

9. 部分还原的钨氧化物（WO3x）在活性和选择性方面优于Cr2O3和Ga2O3。

10. 经过2.5小时氢预处理获得的部分还原钨氧化物具有比空气预处理的Cr2O3和Ga2O3更高的初始活性。

11. 钨氧化物催化剂的活性很大程度上取决于氢还原条件。

12. 在氢预处理后，部分还原的钨氧化物显示出与其它高度活性金属氧化物相比的优越催化活性和选择性。

13. 完全氧化的钨氧化物（WO3）在丙烷供给条件下是不活跃的。

14. 铂基催化剂上的焦炭形成降低了过程效率。

15. 为了减少焦炭形成并改善催化剂再生，可以采取的策略包括使用氧化剂进行预处理，并在高温条件下定期再生。
