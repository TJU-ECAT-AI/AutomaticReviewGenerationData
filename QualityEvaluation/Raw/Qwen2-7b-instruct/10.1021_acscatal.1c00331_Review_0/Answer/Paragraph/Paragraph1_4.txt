            Propyne is a crucial building block for the production of a wide array of chemicals, driving the demand for efficient and sustainable methods to produce propene from propane. The catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) has emerged as a prominent approach, characterized by its high selectivity and efficiency. However, coke formation on Pt-based catalysts poses a significant challenge, impacting process efficiency and necessitating strategies to mitigate its formation and improve catalyst regeneration.

            The key considerations in PDH include reaction conditions, catalyst types, and industrial applications. Optimizing these factors ensures the effective conversion of propane into propene, while the selection of suitable catalysts plays a pivotal role in enhancing reaction kinetics and product distribution. Pt-based catalysts are renowned for their high activity and selectivity, yet they are susceptible to rapid deactivation due to coke formation. Strategies to mitigate coke formation involve the use of supports with low Brnsted acidity, addition of promoters, and control of reaction conditions.

            coke formation on Pt-based catalysts can significantly impact process efficiency by reducing the surface area available for reactions, thus necessitating frequent regeneration. This highlights the importance of developing catalysts that minimize side reactions and promote efficient regeneration protocols. Earth-abundant metals, such as iron, form effective in situ carbide phases, offering a sustainable alternative to costly and hazardous catalysts like Pt and Cr. Moreover, the use of carbon dioxide as an oxidant in PDH reactions has shown promise in enhancing propene selectivity and improving overall efficiency.

            The promoting effect of carbon dioxide on the reaction has been observed on various catalysts, including silica-supported Cr2O3, rare earth vanadates, and β-Ga2O3. These catalysts demonstrate increased olefin yield in the presence of CO2, with β-Ga2O3 showing particularly high catalytic activity at higher temperatures. The enhanced activity of β-Ga2O3 is likely associated with an abundance of surface medium-strong acid sites related to the coordinatively unsaturated Ga3+ cations and the conjugated effect of proton and oxide. Increasing the reaction temperature facilitates the activation of CO2 over β-Ga2O3, making it an attractive option for improving propene production.

            In summary, the catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane for propene production is a promising method, but the challenges posed by coke formation on Pt-based catalysts require ongoing research to develop more efficient and sustainable catalysts. Strategies focusing on catalyst design, operational conditions, and advanced regeneration techniques are crucial for overcoming these challenges and achieving efficient propene production.

            丙烯是生产一系列化学品的关键原料，因此高效和可持续的方法对于从丙烷生产丙烯至关重要。催化直接脱氢丙烷（PDH）作为一种突出的方法，以其高选择性和效率而闻名。然而，铂基催化剂上的焦炭形成构成了一个重大挑战，影响了过程效率，并需要实施策略来减轻焦炭的形成并提高催化剂再生性。

            PDH的关键考虑因素包括反应条件、催化剂类型和工业应用。通过优化这些因素，可以实现丙烷的有效转化成丙烯，而选择合适的催化剂在增强反应动力学和产品分布方面发挥着核心作用。铂基催化剂因其高活性和选择性而受到赞誉，但它们由于焦炭形成而容易迅速失活。减轻焦炭形成的策略包括使用低Brnsted酸性的支持材料、添加促进剂和控制反应条件。

            在铂基催化剂上形成的焦炭显著影响了过程效率，减少了用于反应的表面面积，从而需要频繁地进行催化剂再生。这突显了开发能够减少副反应并促进高效再生技术的催化剂的重要性。铁等地球丰富的金属形成了有效的原位碳化物相，提供了成本效益更高且环境友好的替代品，以替代昂贵或有害的催化剂如铂和铬。此外，在PDH反应中利用二氧化碳作为氧化剂已显示出增加丙烯选择性和改善整体效率的潜力。

            二氧化碳对各种催化剂（包括二氧化硅支持的Cr2O3、稀土钒酸盐和β-Ga2O3）表现出促进效果，这些催化剂在二氧化碳存在下显示出了更高的烯烃产率，尤其是β-Ga2O3在较高温度下显示出特别高的催化活性。β-Ga2O3的增强活性可能与丰富的表面中强酸位点有关，这些位点与配位不饱和的Ga3+离子和质子与氧的耦合作用相关。增加反应温度有助于在β-Ga2O3上激活二氧化碳，使其成为提高丙烯生产的有吸引力的选择。

            总之，催化直接脱氢丙烷用于丙烯生产是一种有前景的方法，但铂基催化剂上焦炭形成的挑战需要持续研究来开发更有效和可持续的催化剂。专注于催化剂设计、操作条件和先进的再生技术的策略对于克服这些挑战和实现高效的丙烯生产至关重要。
