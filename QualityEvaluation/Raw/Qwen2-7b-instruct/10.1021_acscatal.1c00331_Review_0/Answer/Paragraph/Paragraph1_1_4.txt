    <English>
        A comprehensive review of the catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) for propene production reveals intricate processes that are pivotal for the global chemical industry. The method relies on the thermal conversion of propane into propene and hydrogen, where reaction conditions, catalyst types, and industrial applications are critical factors for optimizing efficiency. Notably, Pt-based catalysts, while highly active and selective, are susceptible to coke formation, a phenomenon that significantly impacts process efficiency. Strategies to mitigate coke formation, such as the use of supports with low Brnsted acidity, incorporating promoters, and controlling reaction conditions, are essential for maintaining catalyst activity and longevity.

        The integration of carbon dioxide (CO2) in PDH processes further enhances the selectivity of propene production, offering environmental and economic benefits. CO2, acting as an oxidant, increases the olefin yield and promotes reactions on catalysts like silica-supported Cr2O3, rare earth vanadates, and β-Ga2O3, thereby reducing the need for fossil fuels and potentially mitigating climate change impacts.

        Looking ahead, the field presents exciting opportunities for innovation and advancement. The development of new catalysts, such as single-atom catalysts, and the exploration of alternative catalyst materials could revolutionize PDH processes, enabling more efficient and sustainable propene production. Moreover, the understanding of coke formation mechanisms on Pt-based catalysts opens avenues for improved catalyst design and operation strategies, which are crucial for enhancing overall process efficiency and reducing environmental impact.

        The future of PDH technology is likely to be characterized by a focus on reducing costs, increasing selectivity, and minimizing environmental footprint. Innovations in catalyst design, process optimization, and the integration of sustainable practices will be paramount in achieving these goals. As the chemical industry continues to evolve, PDH remains a cornerstone in the production of propene, with the potential to adapt and thrive in a more sustainable and technologically advanced landscape.

    <Chinese>
        对于通过丙烷直接脱氢（PDH）生产丙烯的方法进行全面审查，揭示了这一过程对于全球化工行业的关键性。这种方法依赖于丙烷转化为丙烯和氢气的热转化，其中反应条件、催化剂类型和工业应用是优化效率的关键因素。值得注意的是，基于铂（Pt）的催化剂虽然活性高且选择性好，但易受到焦炭形成的影响，这一现象显著影响了过程效率。为了维持催化剂活性和寿命，减少焦炭形成策略，如使用低Brnsted酸性的支持物、添加促进剂以及控制反应条件，至关重要。

        在PDH过程中整合二氧化碳（CO2）进一步提高了丙烯选择性生产的能力，提供了环境和经济效益的双重优势。CO2作为氧化剂作用，增加了烯烃产率，并在如硅基负载的Cr2O3、稀土钒酸盐和β-Ga2O3等催化剂上促进反应，从而减少了对化石燃料的需求，有可能减轻气候变化的影响。

        看向未来，该领域提供了激动人心的机会进行创新和发展。新型催化剂的发展，如单原子催化剂，以及替代催化剂材料的探索，将彻底改变PDH过程，使更高效和可持续的丙烯生产成为可能。此外，对焦炭形成机制在Pt基催化剂上的理解为改进催化剂设计和操作策略提供了途径，这对于提高整体过程效率和减少环境影响至关重要。

        PDH技术的未来很可能被成本降低、选择性提高和环境足迹最小化所定义。在催化剂设计、过程优化和融入可持续实践方面的创新将是实现这些目标的关键。随着化学工业继续发展，PDH仍然是丙烯生产的基石，拥有适应并繁荣在一个更加可持续和技术创新领域的潜力。
