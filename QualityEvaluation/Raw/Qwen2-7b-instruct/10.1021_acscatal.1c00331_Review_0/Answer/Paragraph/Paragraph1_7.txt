            The catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) efficiently converts propane to propene through optimized conditions and transition metal catalysts, notably platinum (Pt). Critical factors include temperature, pressure, and reactant concentration for maximum propene yield. Coke formation on Pt-based catalysts diminishes efficiency, necessitating periodic regeneration. Mitigation strategies involve tuning operational parameters, enhancing catalyst stability through promoters, and developing more effective catalyst materials.

            The demand for propylene is increasingly being met by propane dehydrogenation (PDH) due to its high efficiency. Noble Pt is widely used as an active component in PDH due to its superior activity. To address the problem of unsatisfactory propylene selectivity, Sn is typically added as a promoter for monometallic Pt catalysts to enhance propylene selectivity in the industry process. However, alloying Pt with Sn significantly lowers the reaction rate of PDH. Coke formation is an insuperable issue that gradually deactivates the catalyst.

            PDH is a fully known structure-sensitive reaction. The catalytic activity on Pt nanoparticles increases when decreasing the particle size in the range of 19 nm, while the propylene selectivity behaves in an opposite manner. Smaller Pt nanoparticles dominated by Pt(211) facets reveal a lower dehydrogenation energy barrier and are more reactive for propane conversion, but large Pt(111) facets exhibit a higher energy barrier for CH bond activation and a weaker binding strength of propylene. Single-atom catalysts are providing new approaches for designing desired propane dehydrogenation catalysts with both high activity and selectivity.

            The Al2O3-supported atomically dispersed Pt catalyst is a novel catalyst with a high atom efficiency and excellent catalytic dehydrogenation ability, selectivity, and stability. Coke formation on Pt-based catalysts impacts process efficiency by leading to deactivation and necessitating frequent regeneration of the catalyst.

            Understanding the mechanism of coke formation is of great significance. On one hand, based on the mechanism of coke formation, one can optimize the reaction conditions to reduce the frequency of dehydrogenation and regeneration cycle by controlling the rate of coke formation so as to increase the propylene productivity. Strategies to mitigate coke formation include the design of catalysts that minimize side reactions and the development of methods for catalyst regeneration. 

            The process of catalytic dehydrogenation of alkanes into their corresponding alkenes is of increasing importance because of the growing demand for alkenes. High temperatures favor thermal cracking reactions to coke and light alkanes, leading to a decrease in product yield and an increase in catalyst deactivation. Carbon dioxide, a significant greenhouse gas, promotes reactions like methane reforming and oxidative coupling, increasing olefin yield on catalysts such as silica-supported Cr2O3, rare earth vanadates, and β-Ga2O3. The use of CO2 enhances β-Ga2O3's catalytic activity, attributed to its rich surface of medium-strong acid sites, boosting olefin production.

            Increasing the reaction temperature facilitates the activation of CO2 over β-Ga2O3. Furthermore, increasing the reaction temperature facilitates the activation of CO2 over β-Ga2O3. The promoting effect of CO2 on β-Ga2O3 catalyst is more evident above 550°C, making it an effective solution to mitigate coke formation and improve catalyst regeneration.

The integration of emerging technologies and materials promises to further enhance efficiency and sustainability in the PDH process.

            丙烷脱氢（PDH）是满足丙烯需求的关键方法，由于其高效率而受到青睐。Pt是广泛使用的活性成分，因其优越的活性而在PDH中使用。为了解决丙烯选择性不高的问题，通常添加锡（Sn）作为单质Pt催化剂的促进剂来提高丙烯选择性。然而，将Pt与Sn合金会显著降低PDH的反应速率。焦炭形成是一个不可克服的问题，逐渐使催化剂失活。

            PDH是一种结构敏感的反应。当纳米颗粒尺寸在19 nm范围内减少时，Pt的催化活性增加，而丙烯选择性则表现出相反的行为。由Pt(211)晶面主导的小Pt纳米颗粒具有较低的脱氢能垒，对丙烷转化更为活跃，但由Pt(111)晶面主导的大纳米颗粒具有更高的CH键活化能垒和较弱的丙烯结合强度。单原子催化剂为设计具有高活性和选择性的PDH催化剂提供了新的途径。

            铝酸盐支持的原子分散Pt催化剂是一种新颖的催化剂，具有高原子效率和出色的脱氢能力、选择性和稳定性。丙烷脱氢（PDH）是将丙烷转化为丙烯的关键方法，通过断裂丙烷中的C-H键产生丙烯和氢气作为产品。PDH的关键考虑因素包括优化的反应条件、催化剂类型的筛选和工业应用。在Pt基催化剂上形成的焦炭影响了过程效率，导致催化剂失活并需要频繁再生。

            理解焦炭形成机制极为重要。一方面，基于焦炭形成机制，可以通过控制焦炭形成速率来优化反应条件，从而减少脱氢和再生周期的频率，以提高丙烯产量。缓解焦炭形成策略包括设计减少副反应的催化剂和开发催化剂再生方法。

            丙烷脱氢（PDH）的过程是越来越重要的方法，因为它满足了对烯烃的不断增长的需求。高温有利于热裂解反应到焦炭和轻烷烃，导致产品收率下降和催化剂降解。二氧化碳作为主要温室气体，在甲烷重整和氧化耦合等部分氧化反应中被利用。在二氧化硅支持的Cr2O3、稀土钒酸盐和β-Ga2O3等催化剂上，二氧化碳增加了烯烃的产率。二氧化碳的使用增强了β-Ga2O3的催化活性，归因于其丰富的表面中强酸位点，促进了烯烃生产。

            提高反应温度有利于β-Ga2O3上的二氧化碳激活。β-Ga2O3的高催化活性可能与Ga3+离子的协调不饱和相关的表面中强酸位点和质子与氧的共轭效应有关。进一步地，提高反应温度也有利于β-Ga2O3上的二氧化碳激活。二氧化碳对β-Ga2O3催化剂的促进作用在550°C以上更为明显，这使其成为缓解焦炭形成和提高催化剂再生的有效解决方案。
