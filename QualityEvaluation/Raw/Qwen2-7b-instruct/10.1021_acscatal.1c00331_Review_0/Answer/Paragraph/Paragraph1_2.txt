        The catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) functions as a method for propene production, where propane molecules undergo a thermal decomposition to form propene and hydrogen. Key considerations regarding reaction conditions include temperature, pressure, and the presence of a suitable catalyst. Catalysts play a critical role in the efficiency of the PDH process, with noble metals like platinum (Pt) being widely used due to their superior activity. However, coke formation on Pt-based catalysts impacts process efficiency by reducing the surface area available for reactions. Strategies to mitigate coke formation and improve catalyst regeneration involve optimizing operating conditions, such as temperature and pressure, and using catalyst supports. Earth-abundant metals, such as iron, form in situ carbide phases that exhibit good activity and high selectivity for PDH, offering cost-effective and eco-friendly alternatives to traditional Pt or Cr catalysts. The promoting effect of carbon dioxide (CO2) on the reaction has been observed on catalysts like silica-supported Cr2O3, rare earth vanadates, and β-Ga2O3, boosting olefin yield. The high catalytic activity of β-Ga2O3 is likely associated with an abundance of surface medium-strong acid sites related to coordinatively unsaturated Ga3+ cations and the conjugated effect of protons and oxides, which facilitates the activation of CO2 and enhances olefin yield.

        coke formation on the metal is weakly dependent on the propylene and hydrogen pressures but increases concurrently with the propane pressure. Coke formation on the support increases concurrently with the propane and propylene pressures and decreases with the hydrogen pressure. The high reaction temperature favours thermal cracking reactions to coke and light alkanes, leading to a decrease in product yield and an increase in catalyst deactivation. Carbon dioxide is one of the major greenhouse gases, making its use not only economically but also ecologically attractive for catalyst promotion. The promoting effect of CO2 on β-Ga2O3 catalyst is more evident above 550°C, facilitating the activation of CO2 and enhancing olefin yield.

        The demand for propylene is increasingly being met by propane dehydrogenation (PDH) due to its high efficiency. To address the problem of unsatisfactory propylene selectivity, tin (Sn) is typically added as a promoter for monometallic Pt catalysts to enhance propylene selectivity in the industry process. However, alloying Pt with Sn significantly lowers the reaction rate of PDH. Strategies to mitigate coke formation and improve catalyst regeneration include using supports with low Brnsted acidity, adding promoters, and controlling reaction conditions.

        coke formation on Pt-based catalysts can significantly impact process efficiency by leading to deactivation and necessitating frequent regeneration of the catalyst. The critical requirements in PDH are to minimize noble Pt usage for cost reduction, maintain high propylene selectivity at high levels of activity, and increase anticoking ability. The catalytic activity on Pt nanoparticles increases when decreasing the particle size in the range of 19 nm, while the propylene selectivity behaves in an opposite manner. Smaller Pt nanoparticles dominated by Pt(211) faces reveal a lower dehydrogenation energy barrier and are more reactive for propane conversion, but large Pt nanoparticles dominated by Pt(111) faces exhibit a higher energy barrier for CH bond activation and a weaker binding strength of propylene. Single-atom catalysts are providing new approaches for designing desired propane dehydrogenation catalysts with both high activity and selectivity.

        PDH（丙烷直接脱氢）作为丙烯生产的有效方法，通过热分解过程将丙烷转化为丙烯和氢气。关键因素包括反应条件（如温度、压力和反应物浓度），催化剂的类型以及工业应用。催化剂在PDH过程中扮演着至关重要的角色，铂（Pt）等贵金属因其优越的活性而被广泛使用。然而，Pt基催化剂上的焦炭形成会降低过程效率，因为它减少了可用于反应的催化剂表面积。策略包括优化操作条件（如温度和压力），使用催化剂支持物来缓解焦炭形成并改善催化剂再生。铁等地球丰富金属形成的原位碳化物相表现出良好的活性和高选择性，为PDH提供了成本效益高且环境友好的替代传统Pt或Cr催化剂的选择。二氧化碳（CO2）在催化剂上如二氧化硅支持的Cr2O3、稀土钒酸盐和β-Ga2O3中显示出促进作用，提高了烯烃产率。β-Ga2O3的高催化活性可能与富含表面中强酸位点有关，这些位点与配合态Ga3+离子相关，并且具有质子和氧化物的耦合作用，这促进了CO2的激活并增强了烯烃产率。

        焦炭形成在金属上对丙烯和氢的压力依赖性较弱，但随丙烷压力增加而增加。焦炭形成在支持物上随丙烷和丙烯压力增加而增加，随氢气压力减少而降低。高温有利于丙烷裂解生成焦炭和轻烷烃，导致产品收率下降和催化剂失活。二氧化碳是主要的温室气体之一，其在催化剂促进方面不仅经济而且生态友好。β-Ga2O3催化剂上CO2的促进作用在550°C以上更为明显，有助于CO2的激活并提高烯烃产率。

        丙烷的日益增长的需求促使丙烯生产转向PDH，由于其高效性。铂是PDH过程中广泛使用的活性成分，因其优越的活性。为了解决丙烯选择性不理想的问题，通常添加锡（Sn）作为单金属Pt催化剂的促进剂，以提高丙烯选择性。然而，Pt与Sn的合金显著降低了PDH的反应速率。缓解焦炭形成并提高催化剂再生的策略包括使用低Brnsted酸性支持物、添加促进剂和控制反应条件。

        Pt基催化剂上的焦炭形成会显著影响过程效率，导致催化剂失活并需要频繁的催化剂再生。在PDH中，降低成本、保持高丙烯选择性和高活性水平，同时增强抗焦能力是关键要求。当纳米颗粒尺寸减小至约19nm时，Pt纳米颗粒的催化活性增加，而丙烯选择性则表现出相反的行为。由Pt(211)面主导的小Pt纳米颗粒揭示了较低的脱氢能垒，更易于丙烷转换，而由Pt(111)面主导的大Pt纳米颗粒则表现出较高的CH键活化能垒和较弱的丙烯结合强度。单原子催化剂提供了一种设计具有高活性和选择性的PDH催化剂的新途径。

        PDH是一种结构敏感反应。Al2O3支持的原子分散Pt催化剂是一种新型催化剂，具有高的原子效率和出色的催化脱氢能力、选择性和稳定性。这表明了高级催化剂设计用于增强PDH过程性能的潜力。
