<p>The demand for propylene is increasingly being met by propane dehydrogenation (PDH) due to its high efficiency.</p>
         <p>Noble Pt is widely used as an active component in PDH due to its superior activity.</p>
         <p>To address the problem of unsatisfactory propylene selectivity, Sn is typically added as a promoter for monometallic Pt catalysts to enhance propylene selectivity in the industry process.</p>
         <p>However, alloying Pt with Sn significantly lowers the reaction rate of PDH.</p>
         <p>Formation of coke is an insuperable issue that gradually deactivates the catalyst.</p>
         <p>The critical requirements in PDH are to minimize noble Pt usage for cost reduction, maintain high propylene selectivity at high levels of activity, and increase anticoking ability.</p>
         <p>PDH is a fully known structure-sensitive reaction.</p>
         <p>The catalytic activity on Pt nanoparticles increases when decreasing the particle size in the range of 19 nm, while the propylene selectivity behaves in an opposite manner.</p>
         <p>Smaller Pt nanoparticles dominated by Pt(211) faces reveal a lower dehydrogenation energy barrier and are more reactive for propane conversion, but large Pt nanoparticles dominated by Pt(111) faces exhibit a higher energy barrier for CH bond activation and a weaker binding strength of propylene.</p>
         <p>Single-atom catalysts are providing new approaches for designing desired propane dehydrogenation catalysts with both high activity and selectivity.</p>
         <p>The Al2O3-supported atomically dispersed Pt catalyst is a novel catalyst with a high atom efficiency and excellent catalytic dehydrogenation ability, selectivity, and stability.</p>

         <p>Noble Pt is widely used as an active component in PDH due to its superior activity.</p>
         <p>To address the problem of unsatisfactory propylene selectivity, Sn is typically added as a promoter for monometallic Pt catalysts to enhance propylene selectivity in the industry process.</p>
         <p>However, alloying Pt with Sn significantly lowers the reaction rate of PDH.</p>
         <p>Formation of coke is an insuperable issue that gradually deactivates the catalyst.</p>
         <p>The critical requirements in PDH are to minimize noble Pt usage for cost reduction, maintain high propylene selectivity at high levels of activity, and increase anticoking ability.</p>
         <p>PDH is a fully known structure-sensitive reaction.</p>
         <p>The catalytic activity on Pt nanoparticles increases when decreasing the particle size in the range of 19 nm, while the propylene selectivity behaves in an opposite manner.</p>
         <p>Smaller Pt nanoparticles dominated by Pt(211) faces reveal a lower dehydrogenation energy barrier and are more reactive for propane conversion, but large Pt nanoparticles dominated by Pt(111) faces exhibit a higher energy barrier for CH bond activation and a weaker binding strength of propylene.</p>
