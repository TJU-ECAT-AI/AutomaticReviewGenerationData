        The in-depth exploration of thermodynamic preferences and reaction conditions reveals their profound influence on the stability, surface segregation, and structural configuration of bimetallic catalysts, particularly in Pt-based systems such as Pt-3d alloys. These factors are pivotal in determining the catalytic properties and performance across various applications.

        A comprehensive study by Ruban, Skriver, and Nrskov (DOI: 10.1103/physrevb.59.15990) highlights the role of surface segregation energies in transition-metal alloys, underscoring the importance of thermodynamic conditions in shaping the stability and configuration of bimetallic catalysts. Further theoretical insights (DOI: 10.1021/jp2000859) suggest that the local electronic effects of oxygen-related molecules on a PtPd cluster model significantly affect the stability, surface segregation, and structural configuration of bimetallic catalysts, with Pt-based systems experiencing profound implications for their catalytic properties.

        The work on ternary alloys (DOI: 10.1149/1.3409559) indicates that the strong Pt surface segregation tendency leads to the formation of a Pt monolayer on the surfaces, influencing the binding strengths of oxygen and hydroxyl ions, thereby enhancing the activity of the oxygen reduction reaction (ORR) compared to bimetallic alloy surfaces. This, coupled with improved electrochemical stability, showcases the enhanced performance of Pt-based alloy catalysts under varying reaction conditions.

        Studies employing density functional theory (DFT) (DOI: 10.1063/1.2900962) predict the stability of subsurface 3d metals in Pt surfaces and the effect of crystal planes on ORR activity, emphasizing the critical role of surface configurations in catalytic efficiency. The dynamics of surface segregation patterns (DOI: 10.1002/cphc.201001087) and the behavior of PtNi bimetallic catalysts under redox treatments (DOI: 10.1021/jp206517) further illustrate the intricate relationship between reaction conditions and catalytic performance.

        The exposure of nitrogen dioxide (NO2) (DOI: 10.1016/j.susc.2004.03.030) to Pt-based catalysts leads to the formation of an N2O4 dimer, which dissociates to facilitate the release of oxygen and other intermediates, impacting the catalytic activity of Pt-3d alloys. This process underscores the dynamic nature of surface configurations and their response to environmental stimuli.

        Research on the surface segregation of Re 3 Pt polycrystalline alloys (DOI: 10.1063/1.2987704) aims to enhance the stability, surface segregation, and structural configuration of bimetallic catalysts, specifically in Pt-based systems, indicating potential for improved catalytic properties.

        Oscillations in surface structure and reactivity of PtNi bimetallic catalysts (DOI: 10.1021/jp206517) reveal the influence of thermodynamic preferences and reaction conditions on catalyst performance, highlighting the need for careful control of these factors to optimize catalytic outcomes.

        The integration of these findings into a comprehensive review not only consolidates our understanding of the fundamental mechanisms governing the behavior of bimetallic catalysts but also paves the way for future advancements in catalytic technology. As we delve deeper into the realm of catalysis, the ability to precisely control thermodynamic preferences and reaction conditions promises to unlock new avenues for optimizing catalyst performance, expanding the scope of applications from clean energy production to chemical synthesis.

        ※※※

        The integrative understanding and prospective outlook highlight the transformative potential of bimetallic catalysts in catalysis, particularly in Pt-based systems. With the advent of advanced materials science and computational tools, there is a growing opportunity to tailor catalyst properties for specific applications, addressing global challenges such as renewable energy generation and sustainable chemical production. The future of catalysis is poised to be marked by the development of highly selective and efficient catalysts, driven by the principles elucidated in the current review. Potential areas for innovation include the design of catalysts with enhanced stability against corrosion, the creation of catalysts that can operate under mild conditions to reduce energy consumption, and the development of catalysts that can selectively catalyze complex reactions, thus increasing the yield of desired products. Moreover, the integration of machine learning algorithms with experimental data could revolutionize the predictive modeling of catalyst performance, enabling rapid optimization cycles and the discovery of novel catalyst architectures. As we look ahead, the horizon of catalytic chemistry is expanding, offering unprecedented possibilities for technological advancement and environmental sustainability.

        对热力学偏好和反应条件的深入研究揭示了它们对双金属催化剂稳定性、表面分离和结构配置的深远影响，特别是在Pt基系统如Pt-3d合金中的作用。这些因素在决定催化剂的催化性质和性能方面至关重要，适用于各种应用。

        Ruban、Skriver和Nrskov（DOI：10.1103/physrevb.59.15990）的研究强调了过渡金属合金中表面分离能的重要性，突出了热力学条件在塑造双金属催化剂稳定性和配置方面的关键作用。进一步的理论研究（DOI：10.1021/jp2000859）表明，氧相关分子在PtPd簇模型上的局部电子效应显著影响双金属催化剂的稳定性、表面分离和结构配置，尤其是对基于Pt的系统来说，其催化性质和性能具有深远的影响。

        关于三元合金的工作（DOI：10.1149/1.3409559）表明，强烈的Pt表面分离倾向导致合金表面形成一层Pt单层，影响氧和羟基离子的结合强度，从而增强氧气还原反应（ORR）的活性，与二元合金表面相比。此外，改善的电化学稳定性展示了基于Pt的合金催化剂在不同反应条件下的优越性能。

        使用密度泛函理论（DFT）的研究（DOI：10.1063/1.2900962）预测了亚表面3d金属在Pt表面的稳定性以及晶面对ORR活性的影响，强调了表面配置在催化效率中的关键作用。关于表面分离模式（DOI：10.1002/cphc.201001087）和PtNi双金属催化剂在氧化还原处理下的行为（DOI：10.1021/jp206517）进一步说明了反应条件对催化剂性能的动态影响。

        硝酸二甲酯（NO2）（DOI：10.1016/j.susc.2004.03.030）暴露于Pt基催化剂上导致N2O4二聚体的形成，该二聚体完全分解以释放氧气和其他中间体，影响Pt-3d合金的催化活性。这一过程凸显了表面配置的动态特性和对环境刺激的响应。

        研究Re 3 Pt多晶体合金表面分离（DOI：10.1063/1.2987704）旨在提高双金属催化剂的稳定性、表面分离和结构配置，特别针对基于Pt的系统，表明可能为增强催化性质提供了改进空间。

        PtNi双金属催化剂表面结构和反应性的振荡（DOI：10.1021/jp206517）揭示了热力学偏好和反应条件对催化剂性能的影响，强调了精确控制这些因素以优化催化结果的重要性。

        将这些发现整合到全面的综述中不仅巩固了我们对双金属催化剂基本机制的理解，而且为未来的进步开辟了道路。随着材料科学和计算工具的先进发展，有潜力根据当前综述的原理来定制催化剂属性，以满足特定应用的需求，解决全球挑战，如可再生能源生产和可持续化学合成。催化化学的未来正朝着高度选择性和高效的催化剂方向发展，这为技术进步和环境保护提供了前所未有的可能性。

        ※※※

        整合的理解和前景展望强调了基于Pt的系统中的双金属催化剂在催化领域的变革潜力。借助先进的材料科学和计算工具，有潜力设计出具有增强稳定性的催化剂，以抵抗腐蚀，并在温和条件下操作以减少能耗，同时开发能够选择性催化复杂反应的催化剂，从而提高所需产物的产率。此外，将机器学习算法与实验数据相结合有可能彻底改变催化剂性能的预测建模，加速优化周期并发现新型催化剂架构。展望未来，催化化学的视野正在扩大，提供了一个前所未有的机遇，推动了技术进步和环境可持续性的发展。
