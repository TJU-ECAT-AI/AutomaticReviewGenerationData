        The work by Ruban, Skriver, and Nrskov \([1]\) delves into the surface segregation energies in transition-metal alloys, elucidating how thermodynamic forces and reaction conditions govern the stability, surface segregation, and structural organization of bimetallic catalysts, notably in Pt-based systems like Pt-3d alloys. This study underscores the pivotal role of thermodynamic stability, surface segregation patterns, and structural configurations in influencing the catalyst's performance.

        Theoretical studies by other authors \([2], [3]\) focus on the local electronic alloy effects of oxygen-related molecules on a PtPd cluster model. They reveal that thermodynamic preferences and reaction conditions significantly alter the stability, surface segregation, and structural arrangement of bimetallic catalysts, especially in Pt-based systems such as Pt-3d alloys. These findings have profound implications for enhancing the catalytic properties and performance of these materials across various applications.

        Research on ternary alloys emphasizes the strong Pt surface segregation tendency leading to the formation of a Pt monolayer (ML) on their surfaces, which impacts the binding strength of oxygen and hydroxyl ions, thereby affecting the oxygen reduction reaction (ORR) activity and electrochemical stability in comparison to bimetallic alloy surfaces. This highlights the importance of surface segregation in determining the catalytic properties of Pt-based systems.

        Theoretical predictions by other authors \([4], [5]\) predict the stability of subsurface 3d metals in Pt(111) and Pt(100) in oxygen environments and the effects of crystal planes on ORR activity and subsurface stability. This further illuminates the intricate relationship between thermodynamic conditions, structural features, and catalytic performance in Pt-based systems.

        Density functional theory (DFT) calculations by other researchers \([6], [7]\) demonstrate the conversion of an ethylene molecule confined between two metal surfaces into a radical anion monomer, showcasing the enhanced reactivity for controlled polymerization and specific chemical reactions. The effect of electron donor surfaces on the confinement effect is explored, underscoring its utility in applications requiring precise control over polymerization and specific chemical reactions.

        Studies by other authors \([8], [9]\) investigate the energetic interpretation of resulting structures in terms of interaction energies and segregation energies, providing insights into the stability and configuration of surface alloys. These investigations suggest that surface alloys, when confined to the surface layer, exhibit tendencies for Pt and Pd to remain in the outermost layer, influenced by kinetic effects such as a lower barrier for exchange compared to bulk diffusion. The stability of these alloys and the tendency for segregation are studied through various experimental techniques, offering a deeper understanding of the underlying mechanisms.

        The exposure of NO2 to produce a saturation monolayer coverage on a (3×3)R30 Sn/Pt(1 1 1) surface alloy at 120 K leads to the formation of an NN bonded N2O4 dimer in an upright, tilted geometry. This dimer undergoes complete dissociation between 220 and 300 K, releasing surface oxygen and coadsorbed NO. Further dissociation of adsorbed NO2 at higher temperatures results in coadsorbed NO and O, and the desorption of oxygen as O2 in distinct states. The Sn oxidation process is monitored through shifts in the Sn AES peaks, indicating the role of NO2 in altering the surface configuration and catalytic properties of the Pt-3d alloy.

        Ruban等人的研究 \([1]\) 和其他研究人员对PtPd簇模型的理论探讨 \([2], [3]\) 显示了热力学偏好和反应条件对双金属催化剂稳定性、表面分离和结构配置的显著影响，特别是在以Pt为基础的系统如Pt-3d合金中的作用。这些因素在决定这些材料在各种应用中的催化性质和性能方面起着关键作用。

        Ruban、Skriver和Nrskov的工作 \([1]\) 深入探讨了过渡金属合金表面分离能，阐明了热力学力量和反应条件如何控制双金属催化剂的稳定性、表面分离和结构组织，特别是对以Pt为基础的系统如Pt-3d合金的作用。这一研究强调了热力学稳定性、表面分离模式和结构配置在决定Pt基系统催化性能方面的重要性。

        其他作者关于局部电子合金效应的研究 \([2], [3]\) 集中在氧相关分子对PtPd簇模型的影响上。它们揭示了热力学偏好和反应条件如何改变双金属催化剂的稳定性、表面分离和结构排列，尤其是在以Pt为基础的系统如Pt-3d合金中的作用。这些发现对于增强这些材料在各种应用中的催化性质和性能具有深远的影响。

        对于三元合金的研究强调了强烈的Pt表面分离倾向，导致在它们的表面上形成一个Pt单层（ML），这影响了氧气和羟基离子的结合强度，从而影响了氧气还原反应（ORR）活性和电化学稳定性与相应的双金属合金表面相比。这突出了表面分离在确定Pt基系统催化性质中的重要性。

        其他作者关于密度泛函理论（DFT）计算的研究 \([4], [5]\) 展示了限制在两个金属表面之间的乙烯分子转化为自由基阴离子单体的过程，展示了用于控制聚合物化和特定化学反应的增强反应性的能力。电子捐赠表面对其已知的限制效果的影响也得到了探讨，突显了其在需要精确控制聚合物化和特定化学反应的应用中的实用性。

        其他作者的研究 \([6], [7]\) 探索了结果结构的能动解释，涉及相互作用能量和分离能量，提供了关于表面合金稳定性和配置的见解。这些调查表明，当限制到表面层时，表面合金表现出倾向于Pt和Pd停留在外层的倾向，受到动力学效应的影响，例如相对于宏观扩散，顶层之间交换的较低壁垒。这些合金的稳定性以及分离趋势通过各种实验技术进行了研究，为了解潜在机制提供了更深入的理解。

        NO2暴露导致在120 K时在(3×3)R30 Sn/Pt(1 1 1)表面合金上形成一个NN键合的N2O4二聚体，呈直立倾斜几何形状。这种二聚体在220至300 K之间完全分解，释放出表面氧和共吸附的NO。进一步在较高温度下吸附的NO2的分解产生共吸附的NO和O，并且吸附的氧以三种清晰的脱附状态释放。Sn氧化过程通过Sn AES峰的变化来监测，表明NO2在改变表面配置和催化性质中所起的作用。

        通过光电子谱研究Re 3 Pt多晶合金的表面分离 \([10]\)，旨在改善双金属催化剂的稳定性、表面分离和结构配置，特别是在以Pt为基础的系统如Pt-3d合金中的作用。这项工作对于增强这些材料在各种应用中的催化性质和性能具有重要的意义。

        PtNi双金属催化剂的表面结构和反应性随变温下的还原处理而振荡 \([11]\) 表明热力学偏好和反应条件对双金属催化剂的稳定性、表面分离和结构配置有显著影响，特别是在以Pt为基础的系统中的作用。这些因素是催化剂活性和选择性的关键决定因素，影响其在各种应用中的性能。

        总结起来，热力学偏好和反应条件对双金属催化剂，尤其是以Pt为基础的系统如Pt-3d合金的稳定性、表面分离和结构配置施加了深远的影响。这些因素在决定这些材料的催化性质和性能方面起着关键作用，使它们在多个工业领域不可或缺。
