        Detailed analysis established from the study by Ruban, Skriver, and Nrskov \[1\], focusing on surface segregation energies in transition-metal alloys. This work elucidates how thermodynamic preferences and reaction conditions influence the stability, surface segregation, and structural configuration of bimetallic catalysts, particularly in Pt-based systems like Pt-3d alloys. The theoretical study by Skriver and coworkers \[2\] and the examination of local electronic alloy effects on OOH, OH, and O adsorption on a PtPd cluster model \[3\] further underscore the profound impact of thermodynamic conditions on the properties of these catalysts. In particular, for Pt-based systems such as Pt-3d alloys, these factors significantly determine their catalytic properties and performance in various applications \[4\].

        Surface alloys, confined to the surface layer, demonstrate a strong tendency for Pt surface segregation, leading to the formation of a Pt monolayer (ML) on ternary alloy surfaces \[5\]. This segregation affects the binding strengths of oxygen and hydroxyl ions, enhancing the activity for the oxygen reduction reaction (ORR) compared to bimetallic alloy surfaces \[6\]. Moreover, these conditions result in improved electrochemical stability, reducing the dissolution of Pt atoms from the alloy surface \[7\]. The thermodynamic stability of Pt-based systems is influenced by the composition, interatomic interactions, and energy barriers for alloy formation \[8\]. Surface segregation patterns are determined by kinetic processes such as nucleation and growth, controlling the distribution of metals on the surface \[9\]. The size, shape, and orientation of nanoparticles can be manipulated to tune structural configurations, impacting reactivity and selectivity \[10\].

        In another study, DFT calculations reveal changes in geometric and electronic properties of an ethylene molecule confined between two metal surfaces and its conversion into a radical anion monomer, crucial for polymerization \[11\]. This process is enabled by electron transfer between metal surfaces when they are in close proximity, typically within 0.6 to 0.4 nm \[12\]. For Pt-based systems, thermodynamic preferences and reaction conditions significantly influence stability, surface segregation, and structural configuration, affecting the efficiency of oxygen reduction reaction activity \[13\].

        Surface alloys have been extensively characterized structurally, including the 2D distribution of surface atoms, and their properties relevant to (electro-)catalysis have been thoroughly investigated \[14\]. The tendency for surface segregation and stability of Pt-based surface alloys were studied by depositing a Ru layer on top of equilibrated surface alloys and then annealing \[15\]. The floating of buried metals, akin to phenomena seen in surface ant-mediated growth or metal deposition on various single crystal surfaces, further supports the understanding of surface segregation \[16\]. Comparisons with previous mechanistic predictions highlight the island growth behavior during the initial deposition step, which is consistent with earlier reports \[17\]. Quantitative analysis of STM images after surface alloy reformation yields specific compositions (Pt0.66Ru0.34/Ru(0001) and Pd0.44Ru0.56/Ru(0001)) with a high degree of accuracy \[18\]. The slight variations in stability can be attributed to the different contents of guest metals (44% Pd vs. 70% Pt) \[19\].

        NO2 exposure leads to the formation of an upright, NN bonded N2O4 dimer adsorbed in an upright, tilted geometry on the surface, undergoing complete dissociation between 220 and 300 K \[20\]. This structure releases surface oxygen and coadsorbed NO, facilitating the oxygen reduction reaction and influencing the catalytic properties of Pt-3d alloys \[21\]. The study of surface segregation of Re 3 Pt polycrystalline alloy with photoelectron spectroscopy \[22\] aims to improve the stability, surface segregation, and structural configuration of bimetallic catalysts, specifically in Pt-based systems like Pt-3d alloys \[23\]. Oscillations of surface structure and reactivity of PtNi bimetallic catalysts with redox treatments at variable temperatures \[24\] highlight the dynamic nature of these catalysts under changing conditions, influenced by thermodynamic preferences and reaction conditions \[25\].

        Thermodynamic preferences and reaction conditions significantly impact the stability, surface segregation, and structural configuration of bimetallic catalysts, particularly in Pt-based systems like Pt-3d alloys. The stability of these catalysts is determined by the interaction energies between components and segregation energies. Surface alloys, confined to the surface layer, are associated with the tendency of guest metals like Pt and Pd to remain in the outermost layer, influenced by kinetic effects such as a lower barrier for exchange between the top two layers compared to bulk diffusion \[26\]. This stability is crucial for maintaining the structure of surface alloys \[27\].

        The study by Skriver and coworkers \[28\] focuses on the energetic interpretation of resulting structures, emphasizing the importance of knowing whether they are thermodynamically stable or metastable configurations \[29\]. Surface alloys have been extensively characterized structurally, and their properties relevant to (electro-)catalysis have been thoroughly investigated \[30\].         The study by Skriver and coworkers \[36\] investigates the effect of an electron donor surface on the confinement effect on reactivity, providing insights into controlled polymerization and specific chemical reactions \[37\]. Their findings demonstrate a theoretical demonstration that the ethylene molecule under electronic and geometric confinement is converted into a radical anion and initiates the polymerization chain without the need of an initiator \[38\]. The reactive environment is caused by electron transfer from the two surfaces when they are separated by sufficiently short distances in the order of 0.60.4 nm \[39\].

These factors significantly influence the catalytic properties and performance in various applications, setting the foundation for further exploration into the optimization of catalyst design and application.

        来自Ruban、Skriver和Nrskov的研究 \[1\] 深入探讨了过渡金属合金表面分离能的影响，揭示了热力学偏好和反应条件如何影响双金属催化剂的稳定性、表面分离和结构配置，特别是在Pt基系统如Pt-3d合金中的作用。Skriver等人的理论研究 \[2\] 和对OH、OH和O在PtPd簇模型上吸附的局部电子合金效应的考察 \[3\] 进一步强调了热力学条件对这些催化剂性质的深远影响。特别是对于基于Pt的系统如Pt-3d合金，这些因素显著决定了它们在各种应用中的催化性质和性能 \[4\]。

        表面合金，限定在表面层，显示出强烈的Pt表面分离倾向，在三元合金表面上形成了一层Pt单层（ML） \[5\]。这种分离影响氧和羟基离子的结合强度，相对于相应的双金属合金表面，这增加了氧气还原反应（ORR）的活性 \[6\]。此外，这些条件导致了增强的电化学稳定性，减少了Pt原子从合金表面的溶解 \[7\]。Pt基系统中的热力学稳定性受到组成、原子间相互作用和合金形成能量壁垒的影响 \[8\]。表面分离模式由核化和生长等动力学过程决定，控制着金属在表面的分布 \[9\]。通过调整纳米颗粒的大小、形状和取向可以调整结构配置，从而影响其活性和选择性 \[10\]。

        在另一项研究中，密度泛函理论（DFT）计算揭示了一个乙烯分子在两个金属表面之间的几何和电子性质的变化，并将其转化为一个可以作为聚合物链前体的自由基阴离子单体 \[11\]。这个过程在金属表面足够接近时（大约0.6到0.4nm的距离）由电子转移所启用 \[12\]。对于基于Pt的系统，热力学偏好和反应条件显著影响稳定性、表面分离和结构配置，直接影响氧气还原反应的效率 \[13\]。

        表面合金已经被广泛地从结构上进行了表征，包括表面原子的二维分布，并且它们与（电）催化相关的性质已经得到了彻底的研究 \[14\]。表面合金的分离趋势和稳定性研究通过在平衡表面合金上沉积一层12ML的Ru层然后进行退火来开展 \[15\]。埋藏金属的漂浮现象，类似于表面抗介导生长或在各种单晶表面上的金属沉积现象，进一步支持了表面分离的理解 \[16\]。与先前的机制预测的比较强调了初始沉积步骤期间岛屿生长行为的一致性，这与之前的报告一致 \[17\]。STM图像表面合金重组后的定量评估提供了特定的组成（Pt0.66Ru0.34/Ru(0001)和Pd0.44Ru0.56/Ru(0001)）的高精度 \[18\]。稳定性的小差异可归因于客体金属（44%Pd vs. 70%Pt）的含量不同 \[19\]。

        NO2暴露导致在120K下在（3×3）R30 Sn/Pt(1 1 1)表面合金上形成一个NN键合的N2O4二聚体，以直立倾斜的几何形状吸附在表面上，该结构在220至300K之间完全分解，释放出表面氧和共吸附的NO \[20\]。这一结构通过氧气还原反应影响了Pt-3d合金的催化性质，并且导致特定表面构型的形成和释放的氧气，在氧化Sn的过程中发挥关键作用 \[21\]。Skriver等人的研究 \[22\] 集中于表面分离的表面合金用光电子谱法的研究，旨在改善稳定性、表面分离和结构配置，特别针对基于Pt的系统如Pt-3d合金 \[23\]。通过红ox处理在不同温度下的PtNi双金属催化剂的表面结构和活性的振荡 \[24\] 强调了这些催化剂在变化条件下的动态特性，受热力学偏好和反应条件的影响 \[25\]。

        热力学偏好和反应条件显著影响双金属催化剂的稳定性、表面分离和结构配置，特别是在基于Pt的系统如Pt-3d合金中的作用。这些催化剂的稳定性是由组件之间的相互作用能和分离能决定的。限定在表面层的表面合金与客体金属如Pt和Pd倾向于留在外层的倾向相关联，受到如交换层顶两层的低壁垒相比块扩散这样的动力学效应的影响 \[26\]。这种稳定性对于保持表面合金的结构至关重要 \[27\]。

        Skriver等人的研究 \[28\] 强调了知道它们是否是热力学稳定或可能不明确配置的重要性，对于结果结构的能量解释 \[29\]。表面合金已经被广泛地从结构上进行了表征，并且它们与（电）催化相关的性质已经得到了彻底的研究 \[30\]。表面合金的分离趋势和稳定性研究通过在平衡表面合金上沉积一层12ML的Ru层然后进行退火来开展 \[31\]。埋藏金属的漂浮现象，类似于表面抗介导生长或在各种单晶表面上的金属沉积现象，进一步支持了表面分离的理解 \[32\]。与先前的机制预测的比较强调了初始沉积步骤期间岛屿生长行为的一致性，这与之前的报告一致 \[33\]。STM图像表面合金重组后的定量评估提供了特定的组成（Pt0.66Ru0.34/Ru(0001)和Pd0.44Ru0.56/Ru(0001)）的高精度 \[34\]。稳定性的小差异可归因于客体金属（44%Pd vs. 70%Pt）的含量不同 \[35\]。

        Skriver等人的研究 \[36\] 探讨了电子供体表面在已知的限制效应下的反应性，为受控聚合和特定化学反应提供了有价值的见解 \[37\]。他们的发现提供了一个理论证明，即在电子和几何限制下，乙烯分子被转换成一个自由基阴离子并启动聚合链，无需引发剂 \[38\]。反应环境是由两个表面在它们被分开到足够短的距离（大约0.60.4nm）时发生的电子转移造成的 \[39\]。

        热力学偏好和反应条件显著影响双金属催化剂的稳定性、表面分离和结构配置，特别是在基于Pt的系统如Pt-3d合金中的作用。这些催化剂的稳定性是由组件之间的相互作用能和分离能决定的。限定在表面层的表面合金与客体金属如Pt和Pd倾向于留在外层的倾向相关联，受到如交换层顶两层的低壁垒相比块扩散这样的动力学效应的影响 \[40\]。这种稳定性对于保持表面合金的结构至关重要 \[41\]。

        总结，Skriver等人的研究 \[42\] 强调了热力学偏好和反应条件在双金属催化剂的稳定性、表面分离和结构配置中的关键作用，特别是在基于Pt的系统如Pt-3d合金中的作用。这些因素显著影响了各种应用中的催化性质和性能，为进一步探索催化剂设计和应用优化奠定了基础。
