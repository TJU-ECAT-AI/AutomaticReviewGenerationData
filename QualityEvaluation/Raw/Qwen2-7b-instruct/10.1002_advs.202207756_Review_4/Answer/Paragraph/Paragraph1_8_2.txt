        Detailed analysis of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions on gallium-based catalysts reveals the alkyl mechanism, which is characterized by several key steps from initial propane activation to product formation. The activation of CH groups is proposed as the rate-determining step (RDS) in these reactions, highlighting its critical role in influencing the overall reaction kinetics. This mechanism contrasts with the carbenium mechanism, offering a more straightforward understanding of the PDH process. The alkyl mechanism's applicability varies across different Ga species structures, impacting the catalytic conversion's selectivity and efficiency.

        The initial step involves the activation of propane, followed by the insertion of a gallium atom into the C-H bond, leading to the formation of an alkyl group. This process is considered the RDS due to its high energy barrier and sequential nature. The subsequent step involves alkyl migration, and finally, the formation of products such as propylene, ethylene, and methane through the reaction with oxygen or other reactants.

        Compared to the carbenium mechanism, the alkyl mechanism provides a simpler framework for understanding the reaction dynamics, particularly in terms of the activation process and the subsequent steps leading to product formation. The activation of the CH group is pivotal in the alkyl mechanism, acting as the bottleneck in the reaction sequence. The stability and reactivity of the alkyl radical, along with the subsequent steps, are significantly influenced by the activation of the CH group.

        Different Ga species structures affect the mechanism's applicability by altering the specific interactions and pathways involved in activation and product release. Gallium's electronic properties, including its ability to donate electrons to the alkyl radical, play a crucial role in stabilizing the alkyl radical intermediate and facilitating its reactivity towards other molecules. The coordination environment and surface defects of the Ga species can further influence the reactivity and selectivity of the PDH reaction.

        The computational modeling of the nature and role of Ga species in light alkane dehydrogenation catalyzed by Ga/H-MFI provides insights into the key steps of the alkyl mechanism. It elucidates the differences between the alkyl and carbenium mechanisms, underscoring the importance of the activation step in determining the reaction pathway. The results suggest that while the dehydrogenation reactions are inhibited by increasing hydrogen partial pressure, hydrogenolysis activities exhibit a maximum, indicating a competitive interaction between hydrogen and propane for the active sites.

        The findings indicate that hydrogenolysis activities reach a maximum when hydrogen competes with propane for the active sites, demonstrating the complex interplay between reactant concentration and catalytic performance. The results also highlight the potential of gallium-based catalysts in efficiently converting propane to valuable hydrocarbons, with the alkyl mechanism serving as a fundamental basis for optimizing catalytic conditions and enhancing product yields.

        对于基于镓的催化剂上的丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应，深入分析揭示了烷基机制，该机制由几个关键步骤组成，从丙烷的初始活化到产物形成。CH组的活化被提议为PDH反应中的速率决定步骤（RDS），突显其对整体反应动力学的决定性作用。与卡宾离子机制相比，烷基机制提供了更直观的理解途径，特别是在激活过程和后续步骤引导产物形成方面。

        初始步骤涉及丙烷的活化，随后是镓原子插入C-H键，形成烷基团。这一过程被认为是RDS，由于其高能量壁垒和序列性质。随后的步骤包括烷基迁移，最终通过与氧气或其他反应物的反应形成产物，如丙烯、乙烯和甲烷。

        相比之下，烷基机制相对于卡宾离子机制提供了一个更简单的框架来理解反应动态，特别是在激活过程及其后续步骤引导产物形成方面。CH组的活化在烷基机制中至关重要，是反应序列中的瓶颈。烷基自由基的稳定性和反应性，以及后续步骤，受到CH组活化的显著影响。

        不同的Ga物种结构通过改变激活和产物释放的具体相互作用和路径影响机制的应用。镓的电子特性，特别是其向烷基自由基捐赠电子的能力，在稳定烷基自由基中间体并促进其与其他分子的反应性方面起着关键作用。Ga物种的配位环境和表面缺陷可以进一步影响反应活性和选择性。

        Ga/H-MFI催化下的轻烷烃脱氢计算模型提供了关于烷基机制关键步骤的见解。它阐明了烷基机制与卡宾离子机制之间的差异，并强调了激活步骤在确定反应途径中的重要性。结果表明，随着氢分压的增加，脱氢反应受到抑制，而氢解反应活动达到最大，表明氢和丙烷之间存在竞争活性位点。

        结果显示，当氢与丙烷竞争活性位点时，氢解反应活动达到最大，强调了反应物浓度与催化性能之间复杂相互作用的重要性。这些发现表明，基于镓的催化剂在有效将丙烷转化为有价值的碳氢化合物方面具有潜力，而烷基机制构成了优化催化条件和提高产品产率的基础。
