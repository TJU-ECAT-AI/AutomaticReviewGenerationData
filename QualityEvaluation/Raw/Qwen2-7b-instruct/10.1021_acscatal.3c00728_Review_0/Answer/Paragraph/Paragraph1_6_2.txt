        Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) has become a preferred method for propene production due to its economic and environmental advantages. The method offers a more cost-effective way to produce propene compared to traditional processes, such as catalytic cracking. It also reduces the carbon footprint associated with propene production, making it a greener solution. As the global demand for propene continues to grow, the adoption of PDH is expected to increase, facilitating sustainable industrial growth and meeting the rising needs for this versatile olefin.

        Beyond its economic benefits, PDH has environmental advantages. For instance, the use of hexagonal boron nitride (hBN) and boron nitride nanotubes (BNNTs) as catalysts offers higher selectivity to propylene compared to previous catalysts, thus reducing the production of unwanted byproducts. This not only improves the efficiency of the process but also minimizes environmental impact.

        The process of PDH involves converting propane into propene in a more energy-efficient manner. The exothermic nature of the overall reactions results in minimal energy demand, making it an attractive alternative to traditional methods. Moreover, the availability of water as a product helps to avoid thermodynamic limitations, which is a common issue in catalytic cracking reactions.

        To optimize PDH, the focus has been on the development of catalysts that can efficiently convert propane into propene with minimal waste and energy consumption. This includes the use of composite catalysts that contain an oxide (ZnCrOx) and a mesoporous SAPO zeolite (MSAPO). These catalysts exhibit a typical spinel structure and a hierarchical pore texture, respectively, allowing for better separation of CO activation and CC coupling, thereby enhancing the selectivity for desired products.

        The economic and environmental benefits of PDH are further emphasized by its ability to achieve high selectivity for propylene. This is only favorable at low propane conversions due to lower propane reactivity when compared to propylene. The use of CaO can improve the superficial area and acidity of the resulting catalyst, maximizing propane adsorption and propylene desorption while minimizing propylene and propane combustion.

        The integration of PDH with advanced catalyst designs and optimization strategies ensures a reliable supply of propene while minimizing environmental impact. This approach aligns with the growing demands for propene across various applications, from packaging to automotive and pharmaceutical industries, and supports sustainable industrial growth.

        The future of PDH is expected to involve continued innovation in catalyst development and process optimization. This will likely include the exploration of new catalyst materials, the enhancement of reaction conditions, and the integration of PDH with other chemical processes to create more efficient and sustainable production systems. The aim is to further reduce costs, improve selectivity, and expand the scope of applications for propene, ensuring its continued significance in the chemical industry.

        碳氢化合物在化学工业中的重要性已经显著提升，这主要归因于丙烯需求的增长及其广泛的应用范围，推动了丙烷脱氢（PDH）作为首选生产方法的采用。PDH提供了比传统方法如催化裂化更经济的方式生产丙烯，并且减少了与丙烯生产相关的碳足迹，使之成为一个更环保的解决方案。考虑到全球对丙烯需求的持续增长，预计PDH的采用将增加，促进可持续工业增长并满足丙烯日益增长的需求。

        除了经济效益，PDH还有环境优势。例如，使用六方硼氮化物（hBN）和硼氮化物纳米管（BNNTs）作为催化剂可以与以往的催化剂相比提供更高的丙烯选择性，从而减少不希望产生的副产品。这不仅提高了过程的效率，而且还减少了对环境的影响。

        PDH的过程涉及以更节能的方式将丙烷转化为丙烯。整体反应的放热性质导致了极低的能量需求，使其成为传统方法的有吸引力的替代品。此外，来自产品的水有助于避免催化裂解反应中观察到的热力学限制。

        为了优化PDH，重点放在开发能够高效地将丙烷转化为丙烯并同时最小化浪费和能量消耗的催化剂上。这包括使用包含氧化物（ZnCrOx）和多孔SAPO沸石（MSAPO）的复合催化剂。这些催化剂具有典型的立方结构和层次孔隙结构，分别允许更好的CO激活和CC耦合分离，从而增强所需产品的选择性。

        PDH的经济和环境优势进一步体现在其能够实现高丙烯选择性。这种只有在较低的丙烷转化率下才有效，因为丙烷的反应性相对于丙烯较低。使用CaO可以改善最终催化剂的表面积和酸性，最大化丙烷吸附和丙烯脱附，同时减少丙烯和丙烷燃烧。

        将PDH与先进的催化剂设计和优化策略相结合，确保了丙烯的可靠供应并最小化了环境影响。这种方法符合各种应用领域对丙烯的需求增长，支持可持续工业发展。

        PDH的未来预计将涉及催化剂材料的新探索、反应条件的改进以及PDH与其他化学过程的集成，以创建更高效和可持续的生产系统。目标是进一步降低成本、提高选择性和扩大丙烯的应用范围，确保其在化学工业中的持续重要性。
