<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Propene's significance in the chemical industry has evolved to become increasingly crucial, particularly as the demand for propylene has surged. Its growing importance has led to the adoption of propane dehydrogenation as a preferred production method. This approach offers several economic and environmental advantages in meeting the expanding needs for propylene across various applications. Propane dehydrogenation enables the direct conversion of propane to propylene, a versatile olefin, without the need for additional refining steps. This not only enhances efficiency but also reduces environmental impact by minimizing energy consumption and waste generation compared to traditional methods. Economically, it provides a cost-effective way to produce propylene, a key component in the synthesis of numerous chemicals and plastics, thereby supporting the growth of industries relying on these products.
