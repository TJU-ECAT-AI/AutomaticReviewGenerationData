<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. The demand for olefins, particularly propylene, is anticipated to rise significantly in the upcoming years.
2. Commonly employed methods for producing olefins include steam cracking, Fluid Catalytic Cracking (FCC), and dehydrogenation (DH).
3. These conventional methods for olefin production possess drawbacks, including being endothermic and leading to coke formation.
4. An appealing alternative to these methods is the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODP) using molecular oxygen, which allows for lower reaction temperatures and enhanced prevention of surface coke formation.
5. Propene, or propylene, is a widely utilized olefin in various industrial applications.
6. The economic and environmental benefits of propane dehydrogenation encompass reduced reaction temperatures, effective coke inhibition, and its feasibility for commercial implementation.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
