Tandem catalysis at the nanoscale integrates multiple reaction steps in chemical engineering processes, aiming to enhance energy efficiency and process optimization. This method enables precise control over reactive intermediates, allowing for the sequential execution of reactions like oxidative dehydrogenation of propane to propylene and selective hydrogen combustion. The In2O3-Pt/Al2O3 catalyst demonstrates this by achieving high per-pass yields that surpass the equilibrium conversion of non-oxidative PDH, showcasing the potential for improved chemical processing. Further advancements are expected through optimizing process conditions and independently adjusting the functionality and location of dehydrogenation and selective hydrogen combustion sites.

The concept of tandem catalysis at the nanoscale addresses the challenges of integrating multiple reaction steps in chemical engineering processes by utilizing the unique properties of small metal clusters within protected channels of shape-selective zeolites. This allows for high turnover rates and stability in dehydrogenation reactions, particularly for alkanes like C3H8 and C2H6. By staging the introduction of O2, selective H2 removal can be achieved, enhancing alkene yields beyond equilibrium. This method reduces process complexity and costs compared to traditional multi-stage systems, optimizing energy efficiency and process integration.
