        Detailed analysis has been conducted on tandem catalysis at the nanoscale, a technique that integrates multiple reaction steps in chemical engineering processes, aiming to enhance energy efficiency and process optimization (DOI: 10.1126/science.abd4441). This method enables precise control over reactive intermediates, facilitating sequential execution of reactions such as oxidative dehydrogenation of propane to propylene and selective hydrogen combustion. The In2O3-Pt/Al2O3 catalyst exemplifies this approach by achieving high per-pass yields that exceed the equilibrium conversion of non-oxidative PDH, highlighting the potential for improved chemical processing. Further advancements are anticipated through refining process conditions and independently adjusting the functionality and location of dehydrogenation and selective hydrogen combustion sites (DOI: 10.1126/science.abd4441).

        Additionally, the utilization of small metal clusters within protected channels of shape-selective zeolites in tandem catalysis addresses the challenge of integrating multiple reaction steps, leading to high turnover rates and stability in dehydrogenation reactions, particularly for alkanes like C3H8 and C2H6 (DOI: 10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011). Staging the introduction of oxygen facilitates selective hydrogen removal, enhancing alkene yields beyond equilibrium, which significantly decreases process complexity and costs compared to traditional multi-stage systems (DOI: 10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011).

        The integrative understanding and prospective outlook suggest that the exploration of emerging fields and innovative methodologies can further harness the potential of tandem catalysis. Future research endeavors might focus on refining catalyst design, optimizing operating conditions, and exploring the synergies between dehydrogenation and selective hydrogen combustion sites to achieve higher yields and selectivities (DOI: 10.1126/science.abd4441).

        此外，利用形状选择性沸石内保护通道中的小型金属簇在串联催化中解决多步反应集成挑战，导致烷烃如C3H8和C2H6的高转化率和稳定性，同时通过分阶段引入氧气实现选择性氢去除，增强了高于平衡的烯烃产率，显著降低了与传统多级系统相比的过程复杂性和成本（DOI: 10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011）。
