    <English>
        The concept of tandem catalysis at the nanoscale integrates multiple reaction steps in chemical engineering processes, aiming to enhance energy efficiency and process optimization. This method enables precise control over reactive intermediates, allowing for the sequential execution of reactions like oxidative dehydrogenation of propane to propylene and selective hydrogen combustion. The In2O3-Pt/Al2O3 catalyst demonstrates this by achieving high per-pass yields that surpass the equilibrium conversion of non-oxidative PDH, showcasing the potential for improved chemical processing. Further advancements are expected through optimizing process conditions and independently adjusting the functionality and location of dehydrogenation and selective hydrogen combustion sites. This approach significantly decreases process complexity and costs compared to traditional multi-stage systems, optimizing energy efficiency and process integration. Moreover, the staged introduction of oxygen facilitates selective hydrogen combustion, enhancing alkene yields beyond equilibrium. 

        The utilization of small metal clusters within protected channels of shape-selective zeolites like Na-[Fe]ZSM5 ensures high turnover rates and stability in dehydrogenation reactions, particularly for alkanes like C3H8 and C2H6. This method, coupled with staged oxygen introduction, leads to the selective removal of hydrogen, thereby increasing alkene yields beyond equilibrium. It markedly decreases process complexity and costs, offering a promising avenue for the optimization of energy efficiency and process integration in chemical engineering.

        Looking ahead, the field is poised for further exploration into the optimization of process conditions and the independent tuning of dehydrogenation and selective hydrogen combustion sites. The integration of these advancements could lead to more efficient and cost-effective methods for the coupling of catalytic propane dehydrogenation with selective H2 combustion, setting the stage for transformative improvements in energy efficiency and process optimization across various industrial applications.

    <Chinese>
        在纳米尺度上进行串联催化整合了化学工程过程中的多个反应步骤，旨在提高能效并优化工艺流程。这种方法能够精确控制反应中间体，允许氧化脱氢反应（如丙烷转化为丙烯）和选择性氢燃烧的有序执行。In2O3-Pt/Al2O3催化剂展示了这一潜力，通过超越非氧化PDH平衡转化率实现高一次通过产率，从而展示出改进化学处理的潜力。通过优化工艺条件和独立调整脱氢和选择性氢燃烧站点的功能性和位置，预期将取得进一步进展。这种策略显著降低了与传统多级系统相比的工艺复杂度和成本，并优化了能源效率和流程集成。

        利用小金属簇在形状选择性沸石（如Na-[Fe]ZSM5）的保护通道内确保了脱氢反应的高周转率和稳定性，特别是对烷烃如C3H8和C2H6的脱氢。结合分步氧气引入的方法使选择性氢去除成为可能，从而增加了超过平衡的烯烃产率。它显著降低了过程复杂度和成本，为在各种工业应用中优化能源效率和流程集成开辟了有前景的途径。

        展望未来，研究方向集中在进一步优化工艺条件和独立调整脱氢和选择性氢燃烧站点。这些进步的整合可能导致更高效、成本效益更高的方法来耦合催化丙烷脱氢与选择性H2燃烧，为在化学工程领域实现变革性改进铺平道路。
