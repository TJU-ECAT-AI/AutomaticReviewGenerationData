Answer: The literature focuses on the mechanism of coke formation on Pt or modified Pt catalysts during propane dehydrogenation. It highlights the significance of understanding this mechanism, as it enables optimizing reaction conditions to minimize the frequency of dehydrogenation and regeneration cycles, thereby enhancing propylene productivity. Moreover, knowledge of the mechanism aids in the rational design of catalysts that can reduce coke formation rates while preserving the catalyst's activity and selectivity. The study emphasizes the scarcity of research on how different gas compositions influence coke formation during propane dehydrogenation.
