<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism explains the catalytic dehydrogenation process, involving four primary stages: reactant adsorption, activation, deactivation, and desorption. The debate over which step acts as the rate-limiting factor persists due to the intricate nature of the process. Despite decades of research, scientists continue to find interest in this mechanism, seeking to enhance understanding and efficiency through further exploration of each step.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
