The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for catalytic dehydrogenation reactions involves four main steps: activation of the substrate by adsorption on the catalyst surface, formation of an intermediate species, decomposition of the intermediate to release the product, and desorption of the product. The ongoing debate regarding which step is considered rate-limiting has been a topic of scientific interest and controversy for nearly a century.
