<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for catalytic dehydrogenation reactions involves four key steps: adsorption of the reactant, activation of the catalyst, decomposition of the reactant, and desorption of the product. There is an ongoing debate regarding which of these steps is rate-limiting, with differing views among researchers. This mechanism remains a topic of scientific interest and controversy because of its importance in elucidating catalytic processes and its potential for improvement in industrial applications, even after nearly a century of investigation.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
