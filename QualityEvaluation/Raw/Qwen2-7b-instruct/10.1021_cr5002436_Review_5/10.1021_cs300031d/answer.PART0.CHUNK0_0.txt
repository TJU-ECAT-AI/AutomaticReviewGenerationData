Answer: The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for catalytic dehydrogenation reactions involves four main steps: adsorption of the reactant on the catalyst surface, activation of the bond, reaction of the activated species, and desorption of the product. The ongoing debate regarding which step is considered rate-limiting is due to the complexity of the catalytic process and the influence of various factors such as temperature, pressure, and catalyst properties. Despite nearly a century of study, this mechanism remains a topic of scientific interest because it provides insights into the fundamental aspects of catalysis and can guide the design of more efficient catalysts for industrial applications.
