<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for catalytic dehydrogenation reactions involves four main steps: adsorption of propane, dehydrogenation, desorption of propene, and regeneration of the catalyst. The debate around which step is rate-limiting continues, and this mechanism remains a topic of scientific interest due to its importance in understanding catalytic processes and potential enhancements in industrial applications.
