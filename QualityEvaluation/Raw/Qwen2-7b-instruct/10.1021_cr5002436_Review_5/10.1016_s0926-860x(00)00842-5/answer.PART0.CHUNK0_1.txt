<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1. The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for catalytic dehydrogenation reactions involves four main steps: adsorption of the molecule onto the catalyst surface, activation of the molecule by breaking of a bond, diffusion of the activated molecule across the surface of the catalyst, and desorption of the dehydrogenated product from the catalyst surface.
2. The debate over which step in the Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism is rate-limiting is ongoing.
3. Despite nearly a century of study, the Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for catalytic dehydrogenation reactions remains a topic of scientific interest and controversy.

</EnglishVersionAnswer>
