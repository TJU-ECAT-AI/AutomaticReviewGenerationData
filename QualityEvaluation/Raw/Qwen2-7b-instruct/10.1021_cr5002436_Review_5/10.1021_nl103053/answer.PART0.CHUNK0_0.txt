<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for catalytic dehydrogenation reactions, which has been a subject of study for nearly a century, involves four primary steps: activation, deprotonation, rearrangement, and desorption. There is ongoing scientific debate about which of these steps acts as the rate-limiting factor. This mechanism continues to attract attention because it is fundamental to the effectiveness of catalytic processes. Insights into the mechanism could pave the way for advancements in catalyst design for practical applications in industry.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
