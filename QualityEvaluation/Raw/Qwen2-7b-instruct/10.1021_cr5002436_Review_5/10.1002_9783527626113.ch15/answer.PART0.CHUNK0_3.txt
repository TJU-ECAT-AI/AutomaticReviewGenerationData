The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for catalytic dehydrogenation reactions involves four main steps:

1. Adsorption of the alkane molecule onto the catalyst surface.
2. Activation of the alkane molecule by breaking the C-H bonds through interaction with the catalyst.
3. Formation of the alkene product by rearranging the activated alkane molecule.
4. Desorption of the alkene product from the catalyst surface.

Despite nearly a century of study, the debate continues regarding which step is considered rate-limiting. Some researchers argue that the adsorption step controls the overall reaction rate, while others believe that the activation step is the rate-limiting factor. The complexity of the interactions between the alkane molecules, the catalyst surface, and the activation energy required for bond breaking contribute to the ongoing discussion.

The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism remains a topic of scientific interest and controversy due to the intricate nature of the reactions involved. Researchers continue to explore the detailed mechanisms, the role of catalyst properties, and the influence of reaction conditions on the efficiency of dehydrogenation processes. The understanding of this mechanism helps in optimizing industrial processes and developing more efficient catalysts for dehydrogenation reactions.

The ongoing debate and scientific interest in the Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism highlight the challenges and complexities in understanding catalytic dehydrogenation reactions. 2. 通过与催化剂的相互作用，激活烷烃分子并断裂C-H键。
3. 形成烯烃产物，通过重组激活的烷烃分子。
4. 从催化剂表面释放烯烃产物。
