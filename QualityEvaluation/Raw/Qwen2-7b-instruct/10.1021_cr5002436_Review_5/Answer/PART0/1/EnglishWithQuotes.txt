It helps researchers understand how modifying catalyst components, such as tin and potassium, can influence reaction pathways and enhance selectivity.\n\n          The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism is of ongoing scientific interest due to its application in catalytic conversion processes. By studying the rates and mechanisms of dehydrogenation, scientists can optimize catalyst design for industrial applications, such as producing oxygenates for reformulated gasoline. The mechanism explains how the addition of tin to platinum catalysts can suppress hydrogenolysis and isomerization reactions, thereby improving dehydrogenation selectivity. Additionally, the inclusion of potassium further reduces the size of surface platinum ensembles, suppressing isomerization and hydrogenolysis, and enhancing catalyst stability. The Pt/Sn/K-L-zeolite catalysts, characterized by their high dehydrogenation activity, selectivity, and resistance to deactivation, exemplify the practical application of this mechanism in achieving efficient and stable catalytic conversion processes.
