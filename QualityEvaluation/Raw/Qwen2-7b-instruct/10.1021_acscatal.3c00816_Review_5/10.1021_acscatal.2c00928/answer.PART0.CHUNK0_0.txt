Answer: Hierarchical zeolites combine micropores and mesopores/macropores to enhance accessibility for PDH reactions by providing a larger surface area and more pathways for reactants and products to diffuse through. This allows for more efficient interactions within the zeolite framework, leading to enhanced catalytic activity. The main types of these materials typically include modified versions of natural zeolites (like SiO2-Al2O3) and synthetic zeolites tailored for specific applications. Synthesis methods often involve hydrothermal processes, ion exchange, and template-directed assembly, which enable precise control over pore size and topology. Compared to traditional zeolites, hierarchical zeolites offer improved selectivity, stability, and activity due to their optimized pore architecture and enhanced accessibility properties.
