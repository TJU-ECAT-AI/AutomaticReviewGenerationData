In the context of hierarchical zeolites, they integrate micropores and mesopores/macropores to enhance accessibility for PDH reactions. The hierarchical structure allows for a higher surface area and pore volume, which increases the contact area between reactants and catalysts. This enhancement facilitates the diffusion of reactants into the pores and products out of the catalyst, improving the efficiency of PDH reactions.

Regarding the main types and synthesis methods of these materials, isomorphous substitution is a common technique used to create hierarchical zeolites. This involves replacing specific atoms in the zeolite framework with different atoms to alter the catalytic properties. The process typically includes direct hydrothermal synthesis or post-synthetic methods like impregnation and ion-exchange. Hydrothermal synthesis involves reacting the components at high temperatures and pressures to form the desired zeolite structure.

These hierarchical zeolites improve the performance of PDH reactions compared to traditional zeolites by offering a higher surface area and pore volume, which can lead to increased reactivity and selectivity. They facilitate more efficient access of reactants to the active sites and allow for easier product release, ultimately enhancing the overall catalytic activity and efficiency of PDH processes.

The stability of the catalyst during the PDH reaction, as evidenced by the unchanged boron content in B-MWW, demonstrates the effectiveness of the hierarchical structure in maintaining the integrity of the active sites. This stability is crucial for sustained performance over multiple reaction cycles, which is a key factor in the practical application of these catalysts.

Finally, the detailed characterization of B-MWW through multi-dimensional 1H and 11B solid-state NMR spectroscopy provided insights into the structure of boron incorporation within the zeolite framework. The study revealed that boron exists predominantly as isolated B(OSi)3 units, which are adjacent to silanol groups. The hierarchical structure, with its micropores and mesopores/macropores, is instrumental in accommodating these boron species and facilitating the PDH reaction. The stability of these isolated boron sites under reaction conditions further highlights the importance of the hierarchical structure in enabling effective catalysis.

在异晶替换多孔材料中，微孔与介孔或大孔的结合提高了PDH反应的可及性。这种层级结构提供了更高的比表面积和孔体积，增加了反应物与催化剂之间的接触面积。这增强了扩散效率，使得反应物能够更有效地进入孔道并使产物得以释放，从而提高PDH反应的效率。

这些材料的主要类型和合成方法包括异晶替换技术。这种方法通常涉及直接的水热合成或后合成方法，如浸渍和离子交换。水热合成是在高温高压条件下进行，以形成所需的多孔材料结构。

与传统多孔材料相比，这些层级结构的多孔材料在PDH反应中的性能得到改善，主要得益于其更高的比表面积和孔体积。这可以导致更高的反应活性和选择性。它们有助于更有效地将反应物带到活性位点，并允许更容易的产品释放，最终提高催化活性和PDH过程的整体效率。

B-MWW催化剂在PDH反应过程中保持稳定的特性，表明层级结构对于维持活性位点的完整性至关重要。这种稳定性对于实现多次反应循环的持续性能是关键因素，在实际应用中至关重要。

最后，通过多维1H和11B固体状态核磁共振光谱对B-MWW的详细表征揭示了硼在多孔材料框架中的结构。研究发现，硼主要存在于孤立的B(OSi)3单元中，这些单元邻近硅醇基团。层级结构，包括微孔和介孔或大孔，对于容纳这些硼物种并促进PDH反应至关重要。在反应条件下这些孤立的硼位点的稳定性进一步强调了层级结构在有效催化中的重要性。
