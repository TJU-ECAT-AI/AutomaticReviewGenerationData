            The main types of hierarchical zeolites encompass MFI, MEL, and BEA structures, synthesized through hydrothermal treatment, sol-gel processes, and template-directed methods. These materials significantly improve the performance of PDH reactions by providing larger surface areas and more accessible sites for reactants and products, surpassing traditional zeolites in terms of thermal stability, selectivity, and activity. The introduction of specific elements such as In, Ga, and Zn into the zeolite framework further enhances the catalytic properties, allowing for efficient propane dehydrogenation even at high temperatures. The mesopores in hierarchical zeolites act as nanospaces to accommodate small metal nanoparticles, preventing sintering and maintaining high activity over multiple cycles.

            Hierarchical zeolites also facilitate the dispersion of metal nanoparticles, ensuring their stability and reactivity. The controlled pore size distribution in these materials enables optimal diffusion pathways, enhancing the overall catalytic efficiency. Additionally, the presence of certain cations like Cs+ can improve the regeneration stability of the catalyst without compromising its activity. These advancements in hierarchical zeolite design offer a promising route for the development of more efficient and environmentally friendly PDH catalysts.

            In the future, this synthetic strategy may be adapted to control the particle size of metal nanoparticles through the adjustment of hierarchical structures, potentially leading to even greater catalytic performance. The integration of micropores and mesopores/macropores in hierarchical zeolites represents a significant step forward in the optimization of PDH reactions, enabling the creation of catalysts with superior properties compared to traditional zeolites.

            主要类型的分级沸石包括MFI、MEL和BEA结构，通过水热处理、溶胶-凝胶过程和模板导向合成方法进行合成。这些材料显著提高了PDH反应的性能，提供了更大的表面积和更可访问的反应物和产物站点，超越了传统沸石在热稳定性、选择性和活性方面的性能。通过引入特定元素如In、Ga和Zn进入沸石框架，进一步增强了催化剂的性质，使得即使在高温下也能有效地进行丙烷脱氢。分级沸石中的微孔和介孔/大孔相互作用作为纳米空间容纳小金属纳米颗粒，防止聚集并保持高活动性，多次循环。

            分级沸石还促进了金属纳米颗粒的分散，确保其稳定性和反应性。这些材料的可控孔径分布提供了最佳的扩散路径，从而提高了整体催化效率。此外，某些阳离子如Cs+的引入可以改善催化剂的再生稳定性，而不牺牲其活性。这些分级沸石设计的改进为PDH反应优化奠定了基础，使开发具有优越特性的催化剂成为可能。

            未来的研究方向可能集中在细化合成方法以实现更均匀的孔径大小和分布，开发具有定制属性的新型分级结构，以及探究多种金属物种与分级沸石协同作用的影响。探索新材料，如金属有机框架（MOFs）和模板化大孔沸石，可能引领PDH反应催化剂效率的提升。同时，结合机器学习和计算方法来预测和设计最优的分级沸石催化剂，将加速先进材料的开发，适用于工业应用。PDH催化领域的创新正处于关键阶段，分级沸石作为这一变革的核心，预示着未来发展的广阔前景。
