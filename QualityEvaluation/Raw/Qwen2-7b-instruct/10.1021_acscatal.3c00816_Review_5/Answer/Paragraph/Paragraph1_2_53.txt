        Hierarchical zeolites, integrating micropores and mesopores/macropores, significantly enhance the accessibility for PDH reactions, thereby improving their performance compared to traditional zeolites. These materials, which include types such as MFI, MEL, BEA, MCM-41, SBA-15, SBA-16, ZSM-5, MFI, MEL, BEA, TS-1, ZSM-5, Beta, and dealuminated β zeolites, are synthesized through various methods like hydrothermal treatment, sol-gel processes, template-directed assembly, and template-assisted synthesis. They offer enhanced surface area, controlled pore size distribution, and increased stability, leading to superior performance in PDH reactions.

        The hierarchical zeolites' structure allows for optimized catalytic performance in PDH processes by providing larger accessible surface areas and better diffusion pathways for reactants and products. For instance, the introduction of Cs+ ions into the zeolite matrix can improve the regeneration stability of the catalyst, maintaining its activity after multiple cycles without affecting its performance. Additionally, the formation of small metal clusters within the mesopores restrains their movement and prevents severe sintering at high temperatures, ensuring the stability and efficiency of the catalyst.

        The synthesis of hierarchical zeolites often involves the incorporation of organic templates or sacrificial materials to create mesopores and macropores. This method results in materials that surpass traditional zeolites in terms of stability, selectivity, and activity in PDH reactions. The unique pore structure of these materials enables them to provide more active sites and better diffusion channels, contributing to their enhanced catalytic properties.

        In the future, the synthetic strategies for hierarchical zeolites may pave the way for the designed synthesis of small metal nanoparticles with controllable particle sizes through adjustments in hierarchical structures. This advancement could lead to the development of more efficient and durable catalysts for PDH reactions, expanding the potential applications in the field of catalysis and chemical engineering.

        结合微孔和介孔/大孔结构，分级沸石显著增强了PDH反应的可接近性，从而提高了它们的性能，与传统沸石相比。这些材料包括MFI、MEL、BEA、MCM-41、SBA-15、SBA-16、ZSM-5、MFI、MEL、BEA、TS-1、ZSM-5、β等类型，通过水热处理、溶胶-凝胶过程、模板导向组装和模板辅助合成等方法合成。它们提供了增强的表面积、控制的孔径分布和增加的稳定性，导致PDH反应中的优越性能。

        分级沸石的结构允许在PDH过程中优化催化性能，提供更大的可访问表面区域和更好的反应物和产物扩散路径。例如，向沸石矩阵中引入Cs+离子可以提高催化剂的再生稳定性，在多个循环后保持其活性，而不影响其性能。此外，将金属簇限制在介孔内可以防止它们在高温下发生严重的烧结，确保催化剂的稳定性和效率。

        分级沸石的合成通常涉及有机模板或牺牲材料的引入以创建介孔和宏观孔。这种方法产生的材料在稳定性、选择性和PDH反应活性方面超过了传统沸石。这些材料的独特孔结构使它们能够提供更多的活性位点和更有效的扩散通道，从而贡献于其增强的催化性质。
