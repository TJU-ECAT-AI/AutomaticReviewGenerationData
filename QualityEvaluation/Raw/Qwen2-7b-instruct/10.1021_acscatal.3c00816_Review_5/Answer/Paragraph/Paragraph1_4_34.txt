        Hierarchical zeolites, combining micropores and mesopores/macropores, significantly enhance the accessibility for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, integrating improved surface area and controlled pore size distribution for better catalyst distribution and reactivity. Main types of hierarchical zeolites include MFI, MEL, BEA, ZSM-5, MCM-41, SBA-15, SBA-16, and others, synthesized through hydrothermal treatment, sol-gel processes, and template-directed methods. These materials offer superior performance in PDH reactions compared to traditional zeolites due to their unique pore structure, allowing for enhanced surface area and improved diffusion pathways for reactants and products.

        The presence of specific elements such as In, Ga, Zn, and others in hierarchical zeolites further enhances their catalytic properties, leading to higher activity and selectivity in PDH reactions. The introduction of cesium ions into the zeolite framework also improves the regeneration stability of the catalyst without compromising its performance, making it a promising option for PDH applications.

        The future of hierarchical zeolites in PDH reactions is promising, with ongoing research focusing on the development of novel synthesis methods, optimization of pore structures, and the integration of new metals and elements to further enhance their catalytic performance. The ability of hierarchical zeolites to prevent metal sintering, maintain high activity across multiple cycles, and offer a positive environment for metal nanoparticles dispersion makes them a crucial component in advancing PDH technologies.
