            <p>These hierarchical zeolites, integrating micropores and mesopores/macropores, significantly enhance the accessibility for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions. The materials are synthesized through various methods such as dealumination, sol-gel processes, hydrothermal treatments, and template-directed assembly. They are primarily categorized into types such as MFI, MEL, ZSM-5, dealuminated β zeolites, and others, depending on their pore size distribution and structure. The improved pore structure and increased surface area of these materials lead to enhanced performance in PDH reactions compared to traditional zeolites. For instance, dealuminated β zeolites can be synthesized through a two-step post-synthesis method involving dealumination and conventional impregnation procedures, which results in the creation of a large amount of small ZnO nanoclusters, acting as active phases in PDH reactions. These materials not only provide a sustainable solution but also reveal insights into the active cobalt (Co) phase for PDH reactions, demonstrating superior performance compared to conventional methods.</p>
            <p>Moreover, the introduction of metals such as zinc (Zn), gallium (Ga), and chromium (Cr) into the zeolite framework, either through direct incorporation or reaction with support OH groups, enables the in situ formation of metal oxide species (e.g., ZnOx, Ga+-Chabazite zeolite) that serve as active sites for PDH reactions. These metal-containing hierarchical zeolites can provide higher activity and selectivity in PDH reactions due to their unique pore structures and controlled metal speciation. Additionally, the use of Cs+ ions can improve the regeneration stability of the catalyst, allowing for unchanged catalytic activity even after four continuous cycles.</p>
            <p>These studies suggest that hierarchical zeolites represent a promising avenue for enhancing the performance of PDH reactions. Their unique pore architectures and synthesis methods allow for the optimization of accessibility, surface area, and pore size distribution, leading to improved catalytic activity and selectivity. Future research endeavors might focus on exploring the full potential of hierarchical zeolites, investigating the role of different metals and their interactions within the zeolite framework, and developing more environmentally friendly and economically viable synthesis methods to produce these materials on an industrial scale.</p>

            <p>这些分级沸石，结合了微孔和介孔/大孔，显著提高了丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应的可接近性。材料通过各种方法合成，如去铝化、凝胶法、水热处理和模板导向组装。它们主要分为MFI、MEL、ZSM-5等类型，根据其孔径分布和结构进行分类。这些材料改进的孔结构和增加的表面积导致PDH反应性能优于传统沸石。例如，去铝化β沸石可以通过两步后合成方法（去铝化和常规浸渍程序）合成，从而产生大量小锌氧化物纳米簇，作为PDH反应中的活性阶段。这些材料不仅提供了可持续解决方案，而且揭示了PDH反应中活性钴（Co）相的见解，与传统方法相比表现出优越的性能。</p>
            <p>此外，金属如锌（Zn）、镓（Ga）和铬（Cr）的引入沸石框架中，无论是直接掺入还是与支持OH基团反应，都可以促进金属氧化物物种（例如ZnOx、Ga+-Chabazite沸石）的原位形成，作为PDH反应的活性中心。这些含金属的分级沸石由于其独特的孔结构和金属物种的精确控制，可以在PDH反应中提供更高的活性和选择性。另外，引入铯（Cs）离子可以改善催化剂的再生稳定性，允许在连续四个周期后保持不变的催化活性。</p>
            <p>这些研究表明，分级沸石为提高PDH反应性能提供了一条有前景的道路。它们独特的孔结构和合成方法允许优化可接近性、表面积和孔径分布，从而提高催化活性和选择性。未来的研究可能专注于探索分级沸石的全部潜力，研究不同金属及其在沸石框架内的作用，并开发更环保和经济可行的方法来大规模生产这些材料。</p>
