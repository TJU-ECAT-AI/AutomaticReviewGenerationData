        Hierarchical zeolites, designed to integrate micropores and mesopores/macropores, significantly enhance the accessibility for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions. They are synthesized through various methods including dealumination, hydrothermal treatment, sol-gel processes, and template-directed assembly. These materials, such as dealuminated β zeolites, mesoporous silica materials, metal-organic frameworks (MOFs), and hierarchical-like heteroatomic Fe-Silicalite-1, exhibit improved performance over traditional zeolites due to their enhanced surface area and controlled pore size distribution. Specifically, the removal of aluminum atoms from the zeolite structure in dealuminated β zeolites creates a large amount of small ZnO nanoclusters, which act as active phases for PDH reactions, reducing side reactions and enhancing propylene selectivity. Additionally, mesoporous silica materials and MOFs provide a positive environment for the dispersion and formation of small ZnO nanoclusters, which act as Lewis acid sites (LAS) for catalyzing PDH reactions. The LAS derived from the interaction between Zn species and silanol groups in the zeolite framework are responsible for the enhanced catalytic activity. The dealuminated β zeolite, therefore, serves as an ideal support for obtaining well-dispersed small ZnO nanoclusters with catalytic activity for PDH reactions.

        In comparison to traditional zeolites, hierarchical zeolites show improved performance in PDH reactions due to their unique pore structure and surface area enhancement. The enhanced surface area facilitates better reagent access and product release, thereby improving the overall efficiency of PDH reactions. Moreover, the absence of strong acid sites in the dealuminated β zeolite reduces side reactions, further enhancing propylene selectivity.

        Looking ahead, hierarchical zeolites offer a promising avenue for enhancing PDH reaction performance. The development of advanced synthesis methods and the exploration of new materials could lead to the creation of even more efficient catalysts. The ability to tailor the pore structure and surface chemistry of hierarchical zeolites could result in catalysts with unparalleled performance for PDH reactions. Future research efforts might focus on the optimization of synthesis parameters, the discovery of novel materials, and the integration of hierarchical zeolites with other catalytic systems to further enhance PDH reaction performance. The potential for hierarchical zeolites to revolutionize PDH processes and contribute to the advancement of chemical engineering and catalysis science is vast and exciting.

        高级孔型（或称层次孔型）的沸石结合了微孔和介孔/大孔，显著提高了丙烷脱氢反应（PDH）的可接近性。它们通过多种方法合成，包括去铝化、水热处理、溶胶凝胶过程和模板导向生长。这些材料，如去铝化β沸石、介孔二氧化硅材料、金属有机框架（MOFs）、以及类Fe-Silicalite-1高级异原子材料，比传统沸石表现出更优的性能，归功于其增强的表面积和控制的孔径分布。具体而言，从β沸石中去除铝原子产生了大量小ZnO纳米簇，作为PDH反应中的活性部分，减少了副反应并增强了丙烯选择性。此外，介孔二氧化硅材料和MOFs为小ZnO纳米簇的分散和形成提供了有利环境，这些纳米簇作为路易斯酸位（LAS）催化PDH反应。由Zn物种与沸石骨架中的硅醇基团相互作用产生的LAS对增强催化活性负责。因此，去铝化β沸石作为提供良好分散的小ZnO纳米簇的支撑体，对PDH反应具有催化活性。

        相对于传统沸石，高级孔型沸石在PDH反应中展现出改进的性能，这得益于它们独特的孔结构和表面积增强。增强的表面积促进了反应物和产物的更好访问和释放，从而提高了PDH反应的整体效率。此外，去铝化β沸石中强酸位的缺失减少了副反应，进一步提高了丙烯选择性。

        未来展望，高级孔型沸石为提高PDH反应性能开辟了有前景的途径。先进的合成方法的发展和新材料的探索可能带来更高效的催化剂的创造。通过定制高级孔型沸石的孔结构和表面化学特性，可以创造出性能卓越的PDH反应催化剂。未来研究工作可能集中在优化合成参数、发现新型材料以及将高级孔型沸石与其它催化系统集成，以进一步提高PDH反应性能。高级孔型沸石在PDH过程革命和化学工程及催化科学领域的潜在贡献是巨大的且令人兴奋的。
