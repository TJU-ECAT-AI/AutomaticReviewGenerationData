        The main types of hierarchical zeolites include MFI, MEL, and BEA structures, synthesized via template-directed assembly or sol-gel processes. These materials improve the performance of PDH reactions compared to traditional zeolites due to enhanced surface area and controlled pore size distribution. The introduction of specific elements, such as Zn, Ga, or Co, further enhances the catalytic properties of these materials.

        For benchmarking purposes, the developed ZnOsilicalite-1 and an analogue of commercial KCrOx/Al2O3 were tested in the same setup under industrially relevant conditions at close propane conversion over about 400h on propane stream. This showcases the improved performance of hierarchical zeolites in PDH reactions, especially when compared to traditional zeolites.

        The commercial PDH technologies utilizing Cr-containing or Pt-containing catalysts suffer from the toxicity of Cr(VI) compounds or the need to use ecologically harmful chlorine for catalyst regeneration. Hierarchical zeolites offer a promising alternative, providing a larger surface area and increased porosity, which improves the diffusion of reactants and products.

        The design of these materials utilizes robust reactor/catalyst regenerator technology, allowing the process to be scaled as needed. This scalability, combined with the improved catalytic performance, makes hierarchical zeolites a valuable tool for PDH reactions.

        The strong interactions between CeO2 and metal particles, where Pt atoms can be trapped in the form of Pt ions, further improves the performance of these catalysts. This phenomenon contributes to the stability and efficiency of the PDH process.

        In the future, this synthetic strategy may be a promising route for the designed synthesis of small metal nanoparticles with controllable particle size through adjustment of the hierarchical structures. This could lead to even more tailored and efficient catalysts for PDH reactions.

        The combination of micropores and mesopores/macropores in hierarchical zeolites enhances accessibility for PDH reactions, providing a larger surface area for reactant-catalyst contact. This structure allows for optimized catalytic performance in PDH processes.

        The development of hierarchical zeolites has opened up new possibilities for the design and synthesis of materials with superior catalytic properties. The integration of advanced synthesis methods, such as hydrothermal treatment, sol-gel processes, and template-directed assembly, enables the creation of materials with tailored pore structures and surface areas, optimizing the PDH reaction performance.

        These materials can take advantage of both the shape selectivity of micropores and the transport efficiency of the mesoporous materials. The mesopores in hierarchical supports act as nanospaces to accommodate small metal nanoparticles, greatly restraining the free migration of metal species and preventing severe sintering at high temperatures.

        The introduction of Cs+ ions into the zeolite can improve the regeneration stability, keeping the catalytic activity unchanged after four continuous cycles. This feature makes hierarchical zeolites suitable for durable PDH processes.

        In summary, hierarchical zeolites provide a versatile platform for enhancing the accessibility and efficiency of PDH reactions. Their unique pore architecture, combined with the ability to tailor their surface area and pore size distribution, offers significant advantages over traditional zeolites in terms of catalytic performance and environmental impact.

        - Designing hierarchical zeolites with improved thermal stability and resistance to sintering to ensure long-term performance in industrial applications.
        - Expanding the scope of hierarchical zeolites beyond propane dehydrogenation to other catalytic processes where enhanced accessibility and controlled pore structure are beneficial.

        混合型沸石的主要类型包括MFI、MEL和BEA结构，通过模板导向组装或水溶胶-凝胶过程合成。这些材料在PDH反应中的性能优于传统沸石，由于其增强的表面积和可控的孔径分布。

        对比商业PDH技术利用Cr含有的或Pt含有的催化剂，Cr(VI)化合物的毒性或使用对环境有害的氯进行催化剂再生是一个挑战。混合型沸石提供了替代方案，提供更大的表面积和更多的可访问位点，从而改善了PDH反应的扩散率。

        设计这些材料利用了稳健的反应器/催化剂再生技术，允许过程根据需要进行缩放。这种可扩展性结合了改进的催化性能，使混合型沸石成为PDH反应的重要工具。

        铈O2与金属粒子之间的强相互作用，其中Pt原子可以被锁定为Pt离子，进一步提高了这些催化剂的性能。这种现象对于PDH过程的稳定性和效率做出了贡献。

        将来，这个合成策略可能是一个有前景的途径，用于设计合成具有可控颗粒尺寸的小金属纳米颗粒的新型混合型结构。这可以导致更高效且定制化的催化剂用于PDH反应。

        混合型沸石通过集成微孔和介孔/大孔，增强了PDH反应的可访问性，提供了反应物-催化剂接触的大表面积。这种结构允许PDH过程的优化催化性能。

        这些材料能够利用微孔的形状选择性和介孔/大孔的运输效率。混合支持的介孔作为纳米空间容纳小金属纳米颗粒，大大限制了金属物种的自由迁移，并防止在高温下发生严重的结块。

        将Cs+离子引入沸石可以提高再生稳定性，在四个连续周期后保持催化活性不变。这种特性使混合型沸石适合持久的PDH过程。

        总之，混合型沸石为增强PDH反应的可访问性和效率提供了一个灵活的平台。它们独特的孔隙结构，结合了定制的表面积和孔径分布，相对于传统沸石在催化性能和环境影响方面提供了显著优势。

        整体理解与未来展望揭示了探索新兴领域和创新方法开发混合型沸石用于PDH反应的机遇。未来的研究努力可能集中在：
        
        - 开发新型合成策略以形成可调孔大小和形状的混合型结构。
        - 探索将混合型沸石与其他材料结合以创建具有增强催化特性的复合催化剂的可能性。
        - 研究不同金属离子及其与混合型沸石框架的相互作用，以优化催化活性和选择性。
        - 设计具有增强热稳定性和抗结块性的混合型沸石，以确保工业应用中的长期性能。
        - 扩展混合型沸石的应用范围，超越丙烷脱氢反应，以其他催化过程，其中增强的可访问性和控制孔隙结构是有益的。
        - 解决与混合型沸石基催化剂的再生和循环相关的挑战，以减少废物并提高可持续性。

        这些研究方向有望推动催化科学和材料科学领域的创新，为石油化学行业提供更多高效和环保的过程。
