            The integration of micropores and mesopores/macropores in hierarchical zeolites has been shown to significantly enhance the accessibility for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions. This structural feature allows for the optimization of reactant-catalyst contact, thereby improving the overall performance of PDH reactions. The main types of hierarchical zeolites include MFI, MEL, BEA, and ZSM-5 structures, among others. Synthesis methods for these materials often involve hydrothermal processes, sol-gel processes, and template-directed assembly. The enhanced surface area and controlled pore size distribution provided by hierarchical zeolites contribute to their superior performance compared to traditional zeolites in PDH reactions.

            For instance, the development of ZnOx-supported catalysts, as highlighted in reference \[10.1038/s41586-021-03923-3\], showcases the innovative use of materials in PDH catalysis. The in-situ formation of supported ZnOx species through the reaction of support OH groups with Zn atoms generated from ZnO upon reductive treatment above 550°C exemplifies the strategic design of hierarchical zeolites for enhanced catalytic performance.

            Additionally, the use of CeO2 and its interactions with metal particles, particularly Pt, as discussed in reference \[10.1039/D1SC05866E\], demonstrates the synergistic effects that can be achieved with hierarchical zeolites in PDH reactions. The ability of CeO2 to trap Pt atoms in the form of ions enhances the catalytic activity and stability of the system.

            The hierarchical zeolites also provide a platform for the incorporation of metal nanoparticles, as seen in the work of reference \[10.1002/adma.201803966\]. The mesopores in these materials act as nanospaces to accommodate small metal nanoparticles, which helps in preventing severe sintering at high temperatures and maintaining the integrity of the active sites.

            The design and synthesis of hierarchical zeolites have opened up new avenues for the development of PDH catalysts with improved performance. The ability to tailor the pore structure and surface area of these materials allows for better control over the catalytic activity and selectivity in PDH reactions.

            Looking towards the future, the field of hierarchical zeolites presents several opportunities for innovation. The development of new synthesis strategies, the exploration of novel metal loading techniques, and the optimization of hierarchical structures could lead to the creation of even more efficient PDH catalysts. Furthermore, the integration of hierarchical zeolites with other materials, such as metal oxides or organic components, might provide additional functionalities, such as enhanced stability, selectivity, and environmental compatibility.

            In summary, hierarchical zeolites represent a promising direction in the design of PDH catalysts. Their unique structure, synthesis methods, and improved performance characteristics offer significant advantages over traditional zeolites. As research continues to advance, the potential for hierarchical zeolites to revolutionize PDH catalysis and contribute to the broader field of chemical engineering is immense.

            结合微孔和介孔/大孔的整合在分级沸石中的作用，显著提高了丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应的可访问性。这种结构特征允许优化反应物-催化剂接触，从而提高PDH反应的整体性能。主要的分级沸石类型包括MFI、MEL、BEA和ZSM-5结构等。这些材料的合成方法通常涉及热液过程、溶胶凝胶过程和模板导向组装。通过提供更大的表面积和精确控制的孔隙尺寸分布，分级沸石为PDH反应提供了优于传统沸石的性能。

            比如，在参考文献 \[\[10.1038/s41586-021-03923-3\]\] 中所展示的ZnOx支持催化剂的发展，展示了在PDH催化作用中材料创新应用的一个例子。在550°C以上通过还原处理生成的Zn原子与支持OH基团之间的反应形成支持的ZnOx物种，体现了在分级沸石设计中增强催化性能的可能性。

            此外，参考文献 \[\[10.1039/D1SC05866E\]\] 中提到的CeO2与金属粒子（尤其是Pt）的相互作用，展示了分级沸石在PDH反应中协同效应的实现。CeO2能够捕获Pt原子形成离子，从而增强系统的催化活性和稳定性。

            分级沸石还为金属纳米颗粒的集成提供了平台，参考文献 \[\[10.1002/adma.201803966\]\] 中的论述就展示了这一点。这些材料的介孔作为纳米空间容纳小型金属纳米颗粒，有助于防止高温下的严重结焦并维持活性位点的完整性。

            分级沸石的设计和合成开辟了PDH催化剂发展的新途径。它们独特的结构、合成方法以及改进的性能特性为PDH反应提供了显著优势。随着研究的不断深入，分级沸石在PDH催化领域的革命性潜力和对更广泛化学工程领域的贡献是巨大的。

            总之，分级沸石代表了PDH催化剂设计的一个有前景的方向。它们独特的结构、合成方法以及增强的性能特性提供了与传统沸石相比的明显优势。随着未来研究的推进，分级沸石有可能彻底改变PDH催化，并对化学工程领域产生深远影响。
