These characteristics lead to superior performance in PDH reactions compared to traditional zeolites, as demonstrated across numerous studies.

        These materials, especially those incorporating metals like cobalt, zinc, and tin, show improved catalytic properties and stability, allowing for efficient propane dehydrogenation while minimizing side reactions. The presence of mesopores acts as a nanospace for metal nanoparticles, restraining their migration and preventing sintering, thus enhancing the catalytic activity.

        In the future, this synthetic strategy may be adapted to synthesize small metal nanoparticles with controllable particle sizes through the adjustment of hierarchical structures, potentially leading to more efficient and selective catalysts for PDH reactions.
