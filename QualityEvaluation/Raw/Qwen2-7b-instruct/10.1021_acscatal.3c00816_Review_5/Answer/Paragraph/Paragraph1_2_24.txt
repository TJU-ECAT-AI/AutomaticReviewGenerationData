        The hierarchical zeolites, integrating micropores and mesopores/macropores, significantly enhance PDH reaction accessibility. These materials, categorized into different types such as MFI, MEL, BEA, ZSM-5, MCM-41, SBA-15, SBA-16, and dealuminated zeolite beta, are synthesized via hydrothermal treatment, sol-gel processes, template-directed assembly, and other methods. Their improved pore structures and surface areas lead to enhanced performance in PDH reactions compared to traditional zeolites. The synthesis methods and the types of hierarchical zeolites vary, including those made from solid raw materials, dealuminated zeolites, and metal-organic frameworks. These materials show higher activity and stability in PDH reactions, demonstrating the effectiveness of hierarchical zeolites in catalysis.

        In terms of their application, hierarchical zeolites are used in various PDH technologies, including those utilizing Cr-containing or Pt-containing catalysts. However, these traditional catalysts face challenges such as the toxicity of Cr(VI) compounds or the requirement for environmentally harmful chlorine for catalyst regeneration. The development of ZnOx species in hierarchical zeolites offers a sustainable alternative, as they are in-situ formed through the reaction of support OH groups with Zn atoms generated from ZnO upon reductive treatment. This not only enhances the performance of PDH reactions but also promotes the use of eco-friendly materials.

        The future prospects for hierarchical zeolites in PDH catalysis are promising. The integration of hierarchical zeolites into reactor/catalyst regenerator technology enables scalable processes, which is crucial for industrial applications. Moreover, the robustness of these materials against sintering and their ability to maintain high activity over multiple cycles make them particularly valuable. The use of strong interactions between CeO2 and metal particles, trapping Pt in the form of ions, further enhances their catalytic performance.

        The review emphasizes the importance of hierarchical zeolites in PDH reactions, highlighting their structural and synthesis diversity, as well as their potential for improving the efficiency and sustainability of PDH technologies. The future research directions might focus on exploring novel synthesis methods, designing hierarchical structures for optimized catalytic properties, and developing more eco-friendly catalysts that can withstand harsh industrial conditions while maintaining high activity and selectivity.

        The integrative understanding and prospective outlook suggest that hierarchical zeolites hold significant promise for advancing PDH catalysis. With ongoing research, these materials could pave the way for more efficient and sustainable propane dehydrogenation processes, contributing to the broader field of catalysis and chemical engineering.

        级联型沸石通过结合微孔和介孔/大孔来显著提高PDH反应的可接近性。这些材料根据类型分为MFI、MEL、BEA、ZSM-5、MCM-41、SBA-15、SBA-16以及去铝沸石β等，通过热处理、溶胶-凝胶过程、模板导向合成和其他方法进行合成。它们改进的孔结构和表面积导致PDH反应性能超过传统沸石。合成方法和级联型沸石的类型各不相同，包括从固体原始材料合成的沸石、去铝沸石和金属有机框架。这些材料在PDH反应中表现出更高的活性和稳定性，证明了级联型沸石在催化中的有效性。

        在应用方面，级联型沸石用于各种PDH技术，包括利用Cr或Pt催化剂的技术。然而，这些传统催化剂面临着Cr(VI)化合物毒性或需要使用有害的氯气再生催化剂的挑战。在级联型沸石中开发的ZnOx物种提供了一种可持续的替代方案，因为它们是在通过还原处理产生Zn原子时，支持OH基团与Zn元素反应形成的。这不仅提高了PDH反应的性能，而且促进了环保材料的应用。

        未来对级联型沸石在PDH催化方面的前景非常乐观。将级联型沸石整合到反应器/催化剂再生技术中，可以实现可扩展的过程，这对工业应用至关重要。此外，这些材料的抗结块性和维持高活性的能力超过多个周期，使它们特别有价值。通过强CeO2和金属颗粒之间的相互作用，将Pt保持为离子形式，进一步增强了它们的催化性能。

        该综述强调了级联型沸石在PDH反应中的重要性，突出了其结构和合成多样性和促进PDH技术更高效和可持续发展的潜力。随着持续研究，这些材料有可能为提高PDH过程效率和选择性的方法铺平道路，并为更广泛的催化和化学工程领域开辟新途径。
