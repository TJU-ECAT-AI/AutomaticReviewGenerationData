            The commercial PDH technologies currently utilize catalysts containing Cr or Pt, which suffer from the toxicity of Cr(VI) compounds or the necessity for environmentally harmful chlorine for catalyst regeneration [10.1038/s41586-021-03923-3]. Supported ZnOx species are in situ formed through a reaction of support OH groups with Zn atoms generated from ZnO upon reductive treatment above 550°C, showcasing the innovative use of materials in PDH catalysis [10.1038/s41586-021-03923-3]. For benchmarking purposes, the developed ZnOsilicalite-1 and an analogue of commercial KCrOx/Al2O3 were tested under industrially relevant conditions at close propane conversion over about 400 hours on a propane stream [10.1038/s41586-021-03923-3].
            The combination of micropores and mesopores/macropores in hierarchical zeolites enhances accessibility for PDH reactions, as seen in the development of single-crystal house-of-cards-like ZSM-5 [10.1021/acs.chemmater.8b04970]. These materials can be synthesized using hydrothermal synthesis or template-directed assembly, offering improved pore structures and increased surface area, which surpasses traditional zeolites in PDH reaction performance [10.1021/acs.chemmater.8b04970].
            The presence of [InH2]+ as the active site for ethane dehydrogenation was confirmed by ex situ FTIR spectroscopy and DFT calculations [10.1039/D1SC05866E]. Stability at 600°C remains uncertain, but the lower H+ concentration in reduced In-CHA samples compared to H-CHA suggests that In+ species rather than InHx were formed at 550°C [10.1039/D1SC05866E]. The experimentally determined ratio of formed water to consumed hydrogen during the entire process was 1.1, indicating that the main oxidized species was [InO]+, as supported by XRD that revealed no In2O3 diffraction peaks detected on H2-O2 treated In-CHA(11, 1.0) (Fig. S25) [10.1039/D1SC05866E]. In-CHA shows better stability than In2O3, 10In/SiO2, and 10In/Al2O3, with no significant decrease of the C3H6 yield over 14 hours (Fig. 9a) [10.1039/D1SC05866E].
            In summary, hierarchical zeolites, featuring their unique combination of micropores and mesopores/macropores, significantly enhance the accessibility and efficiency of PDH reactions. These materials, characterized by their advanced synthesis methods and tailored pore structures, outperform traditional zeolites in terms of catalytic performance and stability. The integration of novel metallic species and the optimization of pore architectures provide a promising avenue for the development of highly selective and durable catalysts for PDH reactions.

            商业化的PDH技术主要依赖含Cr或Pt的催化剂，但Cr(VI)化合物的毒性或使用有害氯气进行催化剂再生等问题限制了其应用。\[10.1038/s41586-021-03923-3\]中提到，通过还原处理在550°C以上形成的Zn原子与支持OH基团反应生成ZnOx物种，实现了材料在PDH催化中的创新应用。为了对比测试，开发了ZnOsilicalite-1和商业KCrOx/Al2O3的等效物，并在工业相关条件下进行了测试，结果显示在大约400小时的连续丙烷流中，转化率保持稳定。\[10.1038/s41586-021-03923-3\]
            在ZSM-5单晶房屋结构形式下，微孔和介孔/大孔的结合显著提高了PDH反应的可访问性，如\[10.1021/acs.chemmater.8b04970\]中所描述的。这些材料可以通过水热合成或模板导向组装合成，提供改进的孔结构和更大的表面积，这使得它们在PDH反应性能上超越了传统沸石。\[10.1021/acs.chemmater.8b04970\]
            通过电化学红外光谱和密度泛函理论计算确认了\[InH2]+\作为乙烷脱氢的活性位点\[10.1039/D1SC05866E\]。尽管在600°C下的稳定性仍有待确定，但550°C下减少的In-CHA样品中的较低H+浓度表明形成了In+物种而非InHx\[10.1039/D1SC05866E\]。在整个过程中形成水与消耗氢的比例为1.1，表明主要氧化物种是\[InO]+\，这得到了X射线衍射的支持，检测不到在H2-O2处理下In-CHA(11, 1.0)的In2O3衍射峰\[10.1039/D1SC05866E\]。In-CHA比In2O3、10In/SiO2和10In/Al2O3表现出更好的稳定性，丙烷的产率在14小时内没有明显下降\[10.1039/D1SC05866E\]。
            总之，通过结合微孔和介孔/大孔的特性，层级沸石显著提高了PDH反应的可访问性和效率。这些材料，以其先进的合成方法和定制的孔结构，优于传统的沸石，在催化性能和稳定性方面表现出色。金属纳米粒子与层级沸石的集成可能创造多功能催化剂，能够同时执行多种反应，提高整体能源效率。此外，研究层级沸石在不同反应条件下的性能，包括不同的原料组成和环境，可以解锁在化学合成和能源转换领域的新型应用。可持续和环保的合成路线的利用，以及探索层级沸石在PDH过程之外的其他工业过程的应用潜力，代表了一个有前景的方向，旨在利用这些材料的独特性质实现更广泛的社会影响和环境可持续性。
