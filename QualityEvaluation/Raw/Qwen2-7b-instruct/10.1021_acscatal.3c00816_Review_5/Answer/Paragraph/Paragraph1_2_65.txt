        The hierarchical structure of zeolites, characterized by the integration of micropores and mesopores/macropores, significantly enhances the accessibility of PDH reactions by optimizing the surface area and pore size distribution. This structural characteristic allows for a more efficient interaction between the catalyst and reactants, thereby improving the overall performance of PDH reactions compared to traditional zeolites. The synthesis of these materials often involves hydrothermal processes, sol-gel methods, and template-directed assembly, among others, which facilitate the creation of complex pore systems.

        The main types of hierarchical zeolites include MFI, MEL, and BEA structures, with some studies also highlighting the importance of dealuminated zeolite beta, mesoporous silica materials, metal-organic frameworks (MOFs), and others. These materials show improved performance in PDH reactions due to their enhanced pore structures and increased surface area, which facilitates better diffusion of reactants and products.

        An integrative understanding and prospective outlook reveals the potential for exploring new fields and innovative methodologies in the realm of hierarchical zeolites. Future research might focus on the development of new synthesis routes, the exploration of novel hierarchical structures, and the integration of different materials to create hybrid catalysts that can further enhance the performance of PDH reactions. Additionally, there is a growing interest in leveraging the unique properties of hierarchical zeolites for other catalytic processes, expanding their potential applications beyond PDH reactions.

        The advancements in hierarchical zeolites suggest a promising direction for the design of more efficient and selective catalysts, capable of addressing the challenges associated with traditional zeolite-based catalysts in terms of stability, selectivity, and activity. The field is poised for significant developments, driven by the continued optimization of synthesis methods and the discovery of new materials with tailored properties for specific catalytic tasks.

        高级结构的沸石，通过结合微孔和介孔/大孔，显著提高了PDH反应的可接近性，优化了表面积和孔径分布。这种结构特征允许催化剂和反应物之间更有效的相互作用，从而提高PDH反应的整体性能，超过传统的沸石。这些材料的合成通常涉及热处理、溶胶-凝胶法和模板导向组装等方法，这些方法有助于创建复杂的孔系统。
