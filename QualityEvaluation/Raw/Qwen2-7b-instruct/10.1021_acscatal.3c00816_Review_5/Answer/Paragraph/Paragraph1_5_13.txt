            The integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The exploration of hierarchical zeolites has unveiled a plethora of opportunities for innovation in the field of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysts. The integration of micropores and mesopores/macropores within these materials not only enhances the accessibility for PDH reactions but also optimizes the catalytic performance through improved surface area, controlled pore size distribution, and the creation of specialized active sites. This approach not only offers a sustainable solution by utilizing eco-friendly synthesis methods but also provides a deeper insight into the active phases involved in PDH reactions, surpassing the limitations of traditional zeolites. Future research could focus on the development of novel hierarchical zeolite architectures, the refinement of synthesis techniques to achieve even greater control over pore structures, and the exploration of synergies with other materials to create hybrid catalysts with enhanced performance. The potential for advancing the sustainability and efficiency of PDH processes through hierarchical zeolites holds significant promise for the petrochemical industry, paving the way for more environmentally friendly and economically viable production methods.
