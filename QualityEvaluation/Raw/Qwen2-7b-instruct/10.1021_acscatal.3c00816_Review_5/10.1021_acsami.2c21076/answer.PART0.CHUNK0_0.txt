<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Answer: Hierarchical zeolites combine micropores and mesopores/macropores to enhance accessibility for PDH reactions by providing a larger surface area for the reaction, thus increasing the contact between propane molecules and active sites. The main types of these materials include MFI, MOR, and ZSM-5 zeolites. They can be synthesized through hydrothermal synthesis, sol-gel method, or template-directed synthesis. Compared to traditional zeolites, hierarchical zeolites show improved performance in PDH reactions due to their enhanced pore structure, which facilitates better diffusion of reactants and products.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
