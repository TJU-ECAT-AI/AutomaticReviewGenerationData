<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Hierarchical zeolites combine micropores and mesopores/macropores to enhance accessibility for PDH reactions by creating a highly porous structure that allows for efficient diffusion of reactants and products.
2. Main types of hierarchical zeolites include those with ordered mesoporous structures and those with a combination of microporous and macroporous features. Synthesis methods typically involve template-directed assembly, sol-gel processes, and ion-exchange techniques.
3. These materials improve the performance of PDH reactions compared to traditional zeolites due to their enhanced surface area, improved pore connectivity, and better control over pore size distribution, which leads to increased catalytic activity and selectivity.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
