<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Hierarchical zeolites combine micropores and mesopores/macropores to enhance accessibility for PDH reactions. The main types of hierarchical zeolites include MFI, MEL, and BEA structures. Synthesis methods typically involve hydrothermal treatment, sol-gel processes, or ion-exchange techniques. These materials improve PDH reactions by providing a larger surface area and more accessible sites for reaction. Compared to traditional zeolites, hierarchical zeolites show enhanced selectivity and activity due to their unique pore structure.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
