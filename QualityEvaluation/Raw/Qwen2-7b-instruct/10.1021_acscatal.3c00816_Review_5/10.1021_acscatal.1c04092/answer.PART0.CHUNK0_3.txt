<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Hierarchical zeolites integrate micropores and mesopores/macropores to enhance the accessibility for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions.
2. The main types of these materials encompass mesoporous-silica-based zeolites, metal-organic frameworks (MOFs), and covalent organic frameworks (COFs).
3. Synthesis methods for these materials typically involve hydrothermal synthesis, sol-gel processes, and template-directed assembly.
4. These materials improve PDH reaction performance by offering more active sites, better thermal stability, and enhanced selectivity.
5. Compared to traditional zeolites, they demonstrate higher activity, improved selectivity, and better resistance to sintering.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
