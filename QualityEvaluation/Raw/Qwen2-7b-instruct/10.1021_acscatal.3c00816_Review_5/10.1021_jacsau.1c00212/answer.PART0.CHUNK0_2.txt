1. Hierarchical zeolites combine micropores and mesopores/macropores to enhance accessibility for PDH reactions through their unique pore structure. This allows for better control over reactant diffusion and product expulsion, improving the efficiency of the PDH process.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

The main types of hierarchical zeolites include those with a combination of micropores and mesopores/macropores, which are synthesized using methods such as template-directed synthesis, sol-gel processes, and microwave-assisted synthesis. These materials improve the performance of PDH reactions compared to traditional zeolites by providing enhanced surface area and controlled pore sizes.
