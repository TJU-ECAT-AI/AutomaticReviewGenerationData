The unintentional use of doped oxide catalysts throughout history has been largely attributed to the presence of impurities in these catalysts. These impurities have contributed to the discovery of catalysts with enhanced activity and selectivity. This historical context emphasizes the critical role of impurities and additives in determining catalyst performance and suggests opportunities for developing innovative catalytic materials.
