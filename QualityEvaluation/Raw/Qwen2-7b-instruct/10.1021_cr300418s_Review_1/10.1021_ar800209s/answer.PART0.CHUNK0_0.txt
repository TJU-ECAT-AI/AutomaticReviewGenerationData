<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Detailed EXAFS studies confirmed the substitution of Pd, Cu, Pt, and Rh ions in the CeO2 lattice.
2. The presence of Pt X-ray emission in Figure 2a,c confirmed the substitution of Pt ions in Ce0.85Ti0.15O2.
3. The valence band of TiO2 and CeO2 contains the O(2p) band spread over 39 eV, with the empty Ce(4f) level located at 2 eV below the EF.
4. Ionic catalysts generally exhibit higher catalytic rates, up to 20 to 30 times faster, than metal impregnated catalysts of the same mass.
5. Ce0.98Pd0.02O2δ demonstrated high rates of NO and N2O reduction by CO, surpassing the performance of Pt, Pd, and Rh metals on oxide supports.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
