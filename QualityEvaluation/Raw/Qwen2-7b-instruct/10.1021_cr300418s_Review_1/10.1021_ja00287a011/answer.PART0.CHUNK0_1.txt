Impurities and additives, particularly lithium dopants in magnesium oxide, have played a crucial role in the development of catalysts. Lithium dopants create [Li'O-] centers, enhancing the catalyst's activity for forming methyl radicals. This discovery builds upon historical perspectives where impurities have often unexpectedly enhanced catalyst performance. The stability and regeneration of these [Li'O-] centers under specific conditions, as well as their similar behavior to the production of methyl radicals, highlight the importance of defect sites in catalysts. This historical context and modern findings suggest that understanding and manipulating defect sites could lead to more efficient and versatile catalyst designs.
