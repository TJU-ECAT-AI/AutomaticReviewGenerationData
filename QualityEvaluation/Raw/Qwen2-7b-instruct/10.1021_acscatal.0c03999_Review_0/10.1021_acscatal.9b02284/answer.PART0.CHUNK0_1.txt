<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Current propylene production methods often lead to high energy consumption and CO2 emissions due to the steam cracking of naphtha or ethane. Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) provides an alternative that is more energy-efficient and environmentally friendly.
2. The main challenge in using oxygen as the oxidant in ODHP is its reactivity and potential for rapid decomposition.
3. Supported boron oxide catalysts have been suggested to address the challenges related to oxygen usage and to enable selective and low-temperature ODHP of propane, potentially reducing energy consumption and enhancing process efficiency.
4. The specific challenges of using oxygen and optimizing supported boron oxide catalysts are still subjects of ongoing research.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
