The primary challenges in current propylene production methods include high energy consumption, environmental impact, and the risk of producing low-value products. Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) addresses these issues by potentially offering a more energy-efficient process with lower environmental impact. However, selecting the right oxidant is crucial, and using oxygen presents specific challenges such as the need to manage the oxygen concentration to avoid overheating and ensure a stable reaction. The use of oxygen can lead to the formation of low-value products like methane and ethane, which might not be ideal for propylene production. To overcome these limitations, further research is needed to optimize the ODHP process, including the development of more selective catalysts and the implementation of advanced process control strategies.
