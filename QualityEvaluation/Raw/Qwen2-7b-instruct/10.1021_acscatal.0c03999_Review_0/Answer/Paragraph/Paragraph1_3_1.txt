        The challenges in current propylene production methods are primarily centered around high energy consumption, high capital costs, and low selectivity to propylene, as detailed in studies referenced by DOI: 10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052, 10.1002/cctc.201200913, and 10.1021/cr5002436. Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP), as outlined in DOI: 10.1016/j.apcatb.2018.03.044, and 10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3, offers a potential solution to these issues by providing a more energy-efficient and cost-effective method for propylene production. The use of oxygen as an oxidant in ODHP is advantageous due to its abundance and low cost, as stated in DOI: 10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052. However, the selection of an appropriate oxidant, particularly pure oxygen, can lead to the formation of unwanted byproducts such as CO and CO2. Carbon dioxide (CO2) can be used as an oxidant in ODHP, which may offer certain advantages, especially in enhancing propylene selectivity, as noted in DOI: 10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3.

        ODHP faces its own set of advantages and limitations, particularly concerning oxidant selection. Supported K2O/WO3/Al2O3 catalysts are studied for their potential in improving the performance of ODHP processes, as indicated in DOI: 10.1016/j.apcatb.2018.03.044. However, non-oxygen-based systems may offer better performance in terms of avoiding coke deposit formation and maintaining catalyst activity and selectivity.

        The process efficiency of ODHP is significantly influenced by factors such as temperature, pressure, and catalyst properties, as emphasized in DOI: 10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052. The potential of ODHP to reduce energy consumption and CO2 emissions contrasts with the high energy consumption and environmental impact of conventional methods, as described in DOI: 10.1002/cctc.201200913. The use of carbon dioxide as a soft oxidant mitigates issues related to process flammability and poor product selectivity due to over-oxidation, as discussed in DOI: 10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3.

        In the realm of propane dehydrogenation, there is a shift towards utilizing more economical feedstocks, such as shale gas, and generating pure olefins without the need for costly FCC or steam cracking processes, as highlighted in DOI: 10.1021/cr5002436. This shift is partly driven by the availability of relatively cheap natural gas liquids, specifically ethane, leading to the construction of ethane crackers in the US and the dismantling or conversion of naphtha crackers.

        当前丙烯生产方法的主要挑战包括高能消耗、高昂的资本成本以及丙烯选择性低，这些在参考文献\[10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052\]、\[10.1002/cctc.201200913\]及\[10.1021/cr5002436\]中得到了详细阐述。氧化丙烷脱氢（ODHP），在参考文献\[10.1016/j.apcatb.2018.03.044\]及\[10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3\]中被提出作为解决这些问题的一种有潜力的方法，提供了一种更节能且成本效益更高的丙烯生产方式。氧气作为ODHP的氧化剂因其丰富性和低廉的成本而具有优势，正如在\[10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052\]中所述。然而，纯氧作为氧化剂的选择可能导致不希望的副产物如CO和CO2的生成。二氧化碳（CO2）可以作为ODHP的氧化剂使用，这可能在提高丙烯选择性方面带来一些优势，如在\[10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3\]中所述。

        ODHP有自己的优点和局限性，特别是在氧化剂选择方面。支持的K2O/WO3/Al2O3催化剂被研究用于提高ODHP过程的性能，如在\[10.1016/j.apcatb.2018.03.044\]中指出。然而，非氧气基系统可能在避免焦炭沉积形成和保持催化剂活性及选择性方面提供更好的性能。

        ODHP的过程效率受到温度、压力和催化剂性质等众多因素的影响，这一点在\[10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052\]中得到了强调。与传统方法相比，ODHP通过减少能源消耗和CO2排放来降低环境影响，从而与高能耗和环境影响大的传统方法形成了对比。碳二作为软氧化剂能够缓解工艺可燃性和产品选择性差的问题，这是由于过氧化导致的，这一问题在\[10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3\]中得到了讨论。

        在丙烷脱氢领域，正朝着利用更经济的原料，如页岩气，并生成纯烯烃而不必依赖昂贵的FCC或蒸汽裂化过程的方向发展，这在\[10.1021/cr5002436\]中被强调。这种转变部分归因于相对便宜的天然气液体的可用性，特别是乙烷，导致在美国建立乙烷裂解器并拆卸或改造石脑油裂解器。
