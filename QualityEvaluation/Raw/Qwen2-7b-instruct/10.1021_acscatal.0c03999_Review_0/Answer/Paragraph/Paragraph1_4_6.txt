        Challenges in propylene production methods predominantly stem from high energy consumption, high capital costs, and low selectivity to propylene, as noted in [10.1021/cr5002436]. Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP), as presented in [10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052] and [10.1016/j.apcatb.2018.03.044], offers a promising alternative that reduces energy consumption and CO2 emissions. However, the selection of an appropriate oxidant is crucial, with pure oxygen leading to the formation of unwanted byproducts like CO and CO2, as observed in [10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052]. Carbon dioxide (CO2), as highlighted in [10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3], can be employed as an oxidant in ODHP, potentially enhancing propylene selectivity, albeit with its own set of challenges concerning process selectivity and efficiency.

        The use of oxygen as an oxidant in ODHP benefits from its abundance and low cost, as detailed in [10.1016/j.apcatb.2018.03.044]. Non-oxygen-based systems might offer advantages in terms of avoiding coke deposit formation and maintaining catalyst activity and selectivity, as suggested in [10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052]. Supported K2O/WO3/Al2O3 catalysts are studied for their potential in improving ODHP processes, as reported in [10.1016/j.apcatb.2018.03.044].

        The challenges in current propylene production methods, as outlined in [10.1021/cr5002436], include high energy consumption, low selectivity towards specific olefins, and diminishing petroleum reserves with increasing oil prices. ODHP, as discussed in [10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052], addresses these issues by utilizing a more economical feedstock, shale gas, and generating pure olefins without the need for costly FCC or steam cracking processes.

        In [10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3], the authors delve into the intricacies of ODHP, emphasizing the importance of a balanced approach in selecting an oxidant that ensures high product yield and good propylene selectivity, despite the tendency of formed alkenes towards further oxidation.

        The use of carbon dioxide as a soft oxidant, as proposed in [10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3], not only mitigates the flammability issues associated with pure oxygen but also shifts the equilibrium to favor propylene production, enhancing the dehydrogenation process through the coupling of propane dehydrogenation and the reverse water-gas shift reaction.

        当前丙烯生产方法的主要挑战主要来自高能耗、高资本成本和丙烯选择性低的问题，如在[10.1021/cr5002436]中所述。氧化脱氢丙烷（ODHP），如在[10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052]和[10.1016/j.apcatb.2018.03.044]中提出，提供了减少能耗和二氧化碳排放的有前景的替代方案。然而，选择合适的氧化剂至关重要，纯氧可能导致形成有害副产品的CO和CO2，如在[10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052]中观察到。作为[10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3]指出，二氧化碳可以作为ODHP的氧化剂使用，有可能增强丙烯选择性，尽管存在过程选择性和效率方面的挑战。

        在[10.1016/j.apcatb.2018.03.044]中提到，氧气作为氧化剂在ODHP中的应用受益于其丰富和低成本。非氧气基系统可能在避免焦炭沉积形成和维持催化剂活性和选择性方面提供优势，如在[10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052]中所述。研究了支持的K2O/WO3/Al2O3催化剂，以提高ODHP过程的潜力，如在[10.1016/j.apcatb.2018.03.044]中报告。

        如[10.1021/cr5002436]所述，当前丙烯生产方法面临的挑战包括高能耗、特定烯烃选择性低以及石油储量逐渐减少与油价上涨。ODHP，如在[10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052]中阐述，通过利用更经济的原料，页岩气，并且无需昂贵的FCC或蒸汽裂解过程生成纯净的烯烃，来解决这些问题。

        在[10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3]中，作者深入探讨了ODHP，强调了在确保高产品收率和良好丙烯选择性的同时，优化非氧气基氧化剂使用的平衡的重要性，尽管形成烯烃倾向于进一步氧化。

        如在[10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3]中提出，使用二氧化碳作为软氧化剂不仅解决了纯氧相关的可燃性问题，而且通过将丙烷脱氢和逆水煤气变换反应耦合，将平衡转向有利于丙烯生产，从而增强脱氢过程。
