        Challenges in propylene production primarily revolve around high energy consumption, high capital costs, and low selectivity to propylene. Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) emerges as an attractive alternative by offering a more energy-efficient and cost-effective method for propylene production. However, the selection of an appropriate oxidant, particularly pure oxygen, poses significant challenges, leading to the formation of unwanted byproducts such as CO and CO2. Carbon dioxide (CO2), on the other hand, can be used as an oxidant in ODHP, offering potential advantages in enhancing propylene selectivity. The efficiency of the ODHP process is influenced by factors such as temperature, pressure, and catalyst properties.

        The use of carbon dioxide as a soft oxidant can mitigate some of the issues encountered with oxygen usage. It not only provides a safer and more readily available alternative but also shifts the equilibrium to the product side, promoting dehydrogenation through the coupling of simple dehydrogenation of propane and the reverse water-gas shift reaction. However, designing a better catalytic system that achieves high product yield and good propylene selectivity remains a challenge due to the tendency of formed alkenes towards further oxidation.

        Supported K2O/WO3/Al2O3 catalysts are explored for their potential in improving the performance of ODHP processes. These catalysts, with their activity determined by the nature of chromium species on the support surface, are particularly effective in ODHP processes. The TUD-1 material, characterized by its unique three-dimensional mesoporous network structure, offers improved catalytic activity by providing high substrate accessibility and facilitating the formation of a titanium-enriched phase on its surface. Microwave synthesis techniques enhance the incorporation of hetero-atoms, increase hydrophobicity, and improve thermal stability.

        Ethane, as a relatively inexpensive natural gas liquid, has led to a shift from traditional naphtha-based steam cracking to ethane cracking in the United States, with ethane crackers being established and naphtha crackers either dismantled or repurposed. This shift reflects the growing importance of utilizing more economical feedstocks for propylene production.

        The oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) provides a favorable alternative to conventional methods for propylene production, offering solutions to reduce energy consumption and CO2 emissions. However, the development of efficient and selective catalysts remains a critical area for future research to overcome the limitations associated with oxidant selection and process optimization.

        当前丙烯生产的主要挑战包括高能消耗、高资本成本以及丙烯选择性低。氧化脱氢丙烷(ODHP)作为一种有吸引力的替代方法，通过提供更节能和成本效益更高的丙烯生产途径来解决这些问题。然而，在选择合适的氧化剂，特别是纯氧气，存在显著的挑战，导致形成如一氧化碳和二氧化碳等不需要的副产品。碳二酸盐(CO2)可以在ODHP中作为氧化剂使用，提供增强丙烯选择性的潜力。ODHP过程的效率受到温度、压力和催化剂性质等因素的影响。

        可以通过安全、温和且易于获取的二氧化碳作为软氧化剂来解决氧气使用带来的问题。它不仅提供了比氧气更安全、更易获取的替代方案，而且能够将平衡向产物侧移动，通过简单脱氢丙烷和逆水煤气变换反应耦合促进脱氢。然而，设计具有高产品产率和良好丙烯选择性的更好催化剂系统仍然是一个挑战，原因是生成的烯烃倾向于进一步氧化。

        支持的K2O/WO3/Al2O3催化剂被探索用于提高ODHP过程性能的潜力。这些催化剂的活性由支持表面上的铬物种的性质决定，特别适用于ODHP过程。TUD-1材料，以其独特的三维介孔网络结构为特征，提供改进的催化活性，通过提供高基质可接近性和促进钛富集相在表面上的形成，提高了催化活性。微波合成技术增强了杂原子的掺入、增加了疏水性，并提高了热稳定性。

        乙烷作为相对便宜的天然气液体，导致美国从基于石脑油的蒸汽裂解转向乙烷裂解，乙烷裂解器被建立，而石脑油裂解器要么被拆除，要么重新利用。这一转变反映了利用更经济的原料进行丙烯生产的日益重要性。

        丙烷氧化脱氢(ODHP)提供了与传统方法相比减少能量消耗和二氧化碳排放的有利替代方法。然而，开发高效和选择性更强的催化剂，能够在较低温度下运行，降低能耗并提高丙烯选择性，仍然是未来研究的关键领域，以克服氧化剂选择和过程优化方面的局限性。
