Promoters, such as potassium, calcium, manganese, and tin, have been shown to address the limitations of CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness. Specifically, they facilitate the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane with CO2, allowing for the efficient use of underutilized reactants to produce propylene or syngas. 

            Bimetallic systems, combining chromium and nickel, have been optimized to balance reaction pathways, with Fe3Ni favoring the CO2-ODHP pathway for propylene production and Ni3Pt being active for dry reforming with high CO selectivity. The use of promoters offers several advantages over alternative strategies like oxygen co-feeding, including improved CO2 activation, enhanced oxidizing ability, and selective and stable catalytic performance without high chromium toxicity and coking issues. 

            Promoters like potassium notably reduce carbon deposition, maintaining high stability in catalytic activity, unlike calcium, manganese, and tin, which decrease activity and increase carbon deposition. Calcium improves Ni diffusion but promotes carbon deposition, deactivating the catalyst. Manganese reduces carbon deposition slightly but decreases activity. Potassium's location on nickel surface prevents carbon nucleation, enhancing catalytic stability.

            铬基催化剂系统中促进剂在提高CO2活化和氧化能力方面的作用已被广泛研究。例如，钾、钙、锰和锡等促进剂被证明能够解决CO2弱氧化潜能和惰性的限制。具体来说，它们能够促进丙烷的氧化脱氢反应，并利用未充分利用的反应物生产丙烯或合成气。双金属体系，如铬和镍的组合，通过平衡反应途径优化了催化性能，Fe3Ni有利于丙烷氧化脱氢产生丙烯的途径，而Ni3Pt则在干重整过程中具有高CO选择性。
