        The oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes, particularly propane, involves the activation of C-H bonds, which can be facilitated by both traditional oxidants like oxygen and alternative oxidants such as carbon dioxide (CO2). This process is pivotal for the selective conversion of alkanes into valuable products like propene. The mechanisms of C-H bond activation and catalyst regeneration in oxidative dehydrogenation differ significantly based on the type of catalyst used, whether it is redox-active or redox-inactive.

        Redox-active metal oxide catalysts, exemplified by vanadia (V2O5) and vanadyl oxide (VOx/SiO2), are effective in activating CO2 for the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane. These catalysts participate in a redox cycle where they are oxidized by the alkane and then reduced by a traditional oxidant, leading to the regeneration of the active surface. This process highlights the synergy between the redox properties of these catalysts and the oxidative environment provided by the oxidant.

        On the other hand, redox-inactive catalysts, such as ceria (CeO2), may require additional conditions or additives for effective activation of CO2. These catalysts activate C-H bonds through a heterolytic cleavage mechanism, utilizing the gas phase oxidant rather than the surface. This approach contrasts with the redox-active catalysts, demonstrating the versatility in how C-H bonds can be activated for oxidative dehydrogenation.

        Transition metal clusters also play a role in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane, although their specific mechanisms and effectiveness may vary. These clusters offer localized and efficient redox sites, contributing to the overall efficiency of the dehydrogenation process.

        Traditional oxidants and CO2 are central to the oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes, influencing the energy barriers for CO2 dissociation. The activation energy for CO2 dissociation, as determined experimentally, often aligns with theoretical predictions, providing insights into the energetics of the reaction.

        CO2, as an alternative oxidant, offers several advantages. It can mitigate deep oxidation of alkanes and alkynes, reduce coke formation, and facilitate propane conversion by participating in the reverse water-gas shift reaction (RWGS). This property makes CO2 a valuable alternative oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane, offering environmental benefits alongside economic advantages.

        Iron-doped ceria (Fe-CeO2) has been reported as a catalyst that shows enhanced stability and promising catalytic performance for the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane using CO2 as a soft oxidant. This system demonstrates that ceria, despite being redox-inactive, can be effectively utilized in oxidative dehydrogenation processes when combined with appropriate dopants and synthesis conditions. The specific synthesis parameters, including calcination temperature, influence the catalyst's performance, affecting its activity and stability.

        The synthesis of Fe-CeO2 involves a co-precipitation method followed by calcination at various temperatures. Optimal synthesis conditions, such as calcination at 600°C, lead to a higher specific surface area and the formation of a Fe-Ce solid solution rather than two separated cerianite-hematite phases. This optimal condition is crucial for achieving a stable and active surface, thereby enhancing the catalyst's performance in oxidative dehydrogenation.

        Overall, the use of CO2 as an oxidant in oxidative dehydrogenation opens new avenues for sustainable and efficient alkane conversion processes, with the potential to improve both the environmental impact and the economic viability of such transformations. The integration of CO2 into oxidative dehydrogenation systems, alongside the strategic selection and optimization of catalysts, represents a promising direction for advancing this field.

        Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes, particularly with the utilization of CO2 as an alternative oxidant, suggest a growing interest in developing more sustainable and efficient conversion processes. The role of metal oxide catalysts, especially redox-active catalysts like V2O5 and VOx/SiO2, in the activation of CO2 for propane dehydrogenation highlights the importance of redox chemistry in catalysis. The discovery of enhanced stability and catalytic performance in iron-doped ceria (Fe-CeO2) systems underscores the potential of ceria-based catalysts in oxidative dehydrogenation when tailored with dopants and optimized synthesis conditions.

        Future research directions might focus on:
        - Expanding the scope of alternative oxidants beyond CO2, exploring the use of other gases or liquid phase oxidants that could be more abundant or less reactive.
        - Developing catalysts that can efficiently activate multiple types of alkanes, potentially reducing the complexity and cost of industrial processes.
        - Enhancing the understanding of reaction mechanisms at the molecular level, aiming to design catalysts with improved selectivity and activity.
        - Investigating the integration of oxidative dehydrogenation with other chemical processes, such as hydrogen production or CO2 capture and utilization, to create circular economy models.
        - Exploring the use of nanomaterials and hierarchical structures to further optimize catalyst performance and stability, potentially enabling higher throughput and lower energy consumption in industrial settings.
        
        The future of oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes holds promise in both environmental sustainability and technological innovation, with ongoing research poised to uncover new catalysts and processes that could revolutionize the petrochemical industry.

        烷烃的氧化脱氢过程，尤其是丙烷的氧化脱氢，涉及到C-H键的活化，这可以通过传统氧化剂如氧气或替代氧化剂如二氧化碳（CO2）来促进。这一过程对于选择性转化烷烃为有价值的产物如丙烯至关重要。氧化脱氢过程中C-H键活化和催化剂再生的机制在活性催化剂是顺氧化或逆氧化时存在显著差异。

        顺氧化金属氧化物催化剂，例如五氧化二钒（V2O5）和氧钒硅氧烷（VOx/SiO2），在二氧化碳（CO2）活化下对丙烷的氧化脱氢表现出有效性。这些催化剂参与一个顺氧化循环，在此过程中它们被烷烃氧化，并随后由传统氧化剂还原，从而实现活性表面的再生。这一过程展示了这些催化剂的顺氧化性质与其所提供的氧化环境之间的协同作用。

        相比之下，逆氧化催化剂，如铈（CeO2），可能需要额外的条件或添加剂才能有效地激活CO2。这些催化剂通过异裂断裂机制激活C-H键，利用气体相氧化剂而不是表面。这种途径与顺氧化催化剂不同，表明了活化C-H键进行氧化脱氢的不同方式。

        过渡金属簇也参与丙烷的氧化脱氢，尽管其特定机制和有效性可能有所不同。这些簇提供局部且高效的还原位点，有助于提高脱氢过程的整体效率。

        传统氧化剂和CO2是烷烃氧化脱氢的核心，影响着CO2分解的能量壁垒。实验确定的CO2分解激活能量通常与理论预测相符，提供了反应动力学的见解。

        CO2作为替代氧化剂，具有多种优势。它能够减轻烷烃和烯烃的深氧化，减少焦炭形成，并通过参与逆水煤气变换反应（RWGS）促进丙烷转换。这种特性使CO2成为烷烃氧化脱氢中的有价值替代氧化剂，同时提供环境效益和经济效益。

        铁掺杂铈（Fe-CeO2）已被报告为显示增强稳定性和有前景催化性能的催化剂，用于使用CO2作为软氧化剂的丙烷氧化脱氢。这个系统表明，尽管铈是逆氧化的，但通过适当的掺杂物和优化合成条件，铈基催化剂在氧化脱氢过程中可以发挥作用。

        Fe-CeO2的合成涉及共沉淀法，随后在不同温度下煅烧。最佳合成条件，例如在600°C下煅烧，导致更高的比表面积和Fe-Ce固溶体的形成，而不是分离的铈亚铁-赤铁矿相。这一最佳条件对于实现稳定的活性表面至关重要，从而提高了催化剂在氧化脱氢中的性能。

        总之，将CO2作为氧化剂应用于氧化脱氢开辟了可持续和高效转化过程的新途径，有可能改善环境影响和经济可行性。CO2在氧化脱氢系统中的集成，结合对催化剂的精心选择和优化，代表了推动该领域发展的有前景的方向。

        整合理解和前瞻性展望：氧化脱氢烷烃的发展，特别是以CO2作为替代氧化剂的应用，表明了对开发更可持续和高效转化过程的兴趣日益增长。顺氧化金属氧化物催化剂，尤其是五氧化二钒（V2O5）和氧钒硅氧烷（VOx/SiO2）在CO2活化下对丙烷脱氢的有效性强调了氧化化学在催化中的重要性。铁掺杂铈（Fe-CeO2）系统的发现，显示了在适当掺杂和优化合成条件下，铈基催化剂在氧化脱氢中的潜在性能提升。

        未来研究方向可能包括：
        - 探索超出CO2范围的其他气体或液相氧化剂的使用，这些氧化剂可能更加丰富或反应性较低。
        - 开发能够有效活化多种烷烃的催化剂，可能降低工业流程的复杂性和成本。
        - 加强对反应机理的分子水平理解，旨在设计具有更好选择性和活性的催化剂。
        - 探讨氧化脱氢与其他化学过程（如氢生产或CO2捕获和利用）的集成，以创建循环经济模型。
        - 探索纳米材料和分级结构的使用，以进一步优化催化剂性能和稳定性，可能使工业设置中的处理能力提高，能耗降低。

        烷烃氧化脱氢的未来充满了环境可持续性和技术创新的希望，正在进行的研究有望揭示新的催化剂和过程，有可能彻底改变石油化学工业。
