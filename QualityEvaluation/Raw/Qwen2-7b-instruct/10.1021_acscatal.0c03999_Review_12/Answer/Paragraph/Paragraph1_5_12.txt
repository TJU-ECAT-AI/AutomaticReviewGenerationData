        Comparative analysis of C-H bond activation, catalyst regeneration, and oxidant utilization in oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes, focusing on redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts and the role of CO2 as an alternative oxidant. This review delves into the mechanisms of C-H bond activation and catalyst regeneration in the oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes, with a spotlight on the differences between redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts, specifically V2O5, VOx/SiO2, CeO2, and transition metal clusters. It also scrutinizes the utilization of CO2 as an alternative oxidant and its impact on the energy barriers for CO2 dissociation. The effectiveness of different catalysts in activating CO2 for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane is assessed, with insights into the proposed mechanisms and the energy barriers involved. 

        The document highlights the potential of CO2 as an alternative oxidant in oxidative dehydrogenation, which can avoid deep oxidation of alkanes/alkenes, reduce coke formation, and favor propane conversion through the participation of the produced hydrogen in the reverse water-gas shift reaction (RWGS). The role of redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts in this process is discussed, emphasizing their distinct mechanisms and effectiveness in activating CO2 for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane. 

        The literature further explores the use of iron-doped ceria (Fe-CeO2) as a catalyst for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane with CO2, demonstrating enhanced stability and promising catalytic performance compared to traditional redox-active catalysts. The synthesis parameters, including calcination temperature, are critical in achieving a stable and active surface for the reaction, as demonstrated by the formation of a Fe-Ce solid solution at 600°C.

        The integrative understanding and prospective outlook section suggests future research directions and innovative methodologies in the field. It advocates for exploring emerging areas and pioneering approaches based on the detailed insights provided in the document. Potential avenues for future exploration might include refining catalyst designs, optimizing synthesis methods, and investigating the synergistic effects of combining different types of catalysts to enhance the efficiency of CO2 activation and propane conversion.

        The comprehensive review underscores the importance of CO2 utilization as an alternative oxidant in oxidative dehydrogenation processes, highlighting its potential benefits and the ongoing challenges in catalyst development. It encourages researchers to consider the unique properties of metal oxide catalysts, especially those that can effectively activate CO2, in the quest for sustainable and efficient chemical transformations.

        文章强调了二氧化碳作为替代氧化剂在氧化脱氢过程中的潜力，它能够避免深氧化烷烃/烯烃、减少焦炭形成，并通过氢气参与逆水煤气变换反应促进丙烷转化。活性氧化物催化剂（活性与非活性）在这一过程中扮演的角色被详细分析，指出它们在激活二氧化碳进行烷烃脱氢方面的独特机制和有效性。

        文献还展示了铁掺杂的氧化铈（Fe-CeO2）作为一种催化剂，在二氧化碳下进行丙烷氧化脱氢的潜在应用，显示了与传统活性氧化物催化剂相比的增强稳定性和有前景的催化性能。合成参数，特别是煅烧温度，对于实现反应所需的稳定且活跃的表面至关重要，这由600°C时形成的Fe-Ce固溶体所证实。

        该全面综述强调了利用二氧化碳作为氧化剂在化学转换过程中的重要性，突出了其潜在优势和催化剂开发面临的持续挑战。它鼓励研究人员考虑具有有效激活二氧化碳的独特性质的金属氧化物催化剂，以追求可持续和高效的化学转化。
