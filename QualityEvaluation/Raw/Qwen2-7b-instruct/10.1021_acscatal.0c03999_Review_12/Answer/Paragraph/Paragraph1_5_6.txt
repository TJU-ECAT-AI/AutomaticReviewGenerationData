        Comparative analysis of C-H bond activation mechanisms, catalyst regeneration processes, and oxidant utilization in the oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes, with a focus on redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts, CO2 as an alternative oxidant, and the effectiveness of catalysts such as V2O5, VOx/SiO2, CeO2, and transition metal clusters in activating CO2 for the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane. The study emphasizes the role of traditional oxidants and CO2 in influencing the energy barriers for CO2 dissociation. 

        Redox-active metal oxide catalysts, such as vanadia (V2O5) and vanadyl oxide (VOx/SiO2), effectively utilize oxygen as a strong oxidant to facilitate the breaking of C-H bonds in alkanes, while redox-inactive catalysts, like ceria (CeO2), might require additional conditions or additives for effective CO2 activation. Transition metal clusters also contribute to the process, though their specific mechanisms and effectiveness may vary.

        The oxidative dehydrogenation of propane typically involves the activation of C-H bonds, where traditional oxidants like oxygen play a critical role in the redox reactions. Redox-active catalysts, such as vanadia and vanadyl oxide, involve a redox cycle where the catalyst is oxidized by the alkane and then reduced by a traditional oxidant, leading to the regeneration of the active surface. Redox-inactive catalysts, like ceria, activate C-H bonds through heterolytic cleavage, utilizing the gas phase oxidant rather than the surface.

        The effectiveness of different catalysts in activating CO2 for oxidative dehydrogenation depends on their ability to selectively activate the C-H bonds in propane without requiring an external oxidant like O2. Ceria shows promise due to its unique properties that allow for the efficient activation of CO2 and the promotion of alkane dehydrogenation. CO2 acts as a soft oxidant in oxidative dehydrogenation, helping to mitigate deep oxidation of alkanes and alkynes, reducing coke formation, and facilitating propane conversion by participating in the reverse water-gas shift reaction (RWGS).

        The use of CO2 as an alternative oxidant offers several advantages, including avoiding deep oxidation of alkanes and alkynes, reducing coke formation, and favoring propane conversion through the participation of produced hydrogen in the RWGS. This makes CO2 a valuable alternative oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane.

        The study highlights the use of iron-doped ceria (Fe-CeO2) as a catalyst for the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane with CO2. This system demonstrates enhanced stability and promising catalytic performance compared to traditional redox-active catalysts. The catalyst's performance is attributed to the specific synthesis parameters, including calcination temperature, which affects the formation of a solid solution between iron and ceria, thereby enhancing the catalyst's activity and stability. The synthesis of Fe-doped ceria catalysts involves a co-precipitation method followed by calcination at various temperatures, with calcination at 600°C being particularly advantageous due to the higher specific surface area and formation of a Fe-Ce solid solution, rather than two separated cerianite-hematite phases.

        Future research endeavors might focus on exploring emerging fields and innovative methodologies, such as optimizing catalyst synthesis conditions, improving CO2 activation efficiency, and developing more selective and stable catalysts for the oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes, considering the proposed mechanisms and energy barriers for CO2 dissociation.

        比较分析了在烷烃氧化脱氢过程中C-H键活化机制、催化剂再生过程以及不同氧化剂（如氧气、二氧化碳）的利用情况，重点探讨了活性氧金属氧化物催化剂（如V2O5、VOx/SiO2）与非活性氧金属氧化物催化剂（如CeO2）在促进CO2氧化脱氢中的作用，以及V2O5、VOx/SiO2、CeO2、过渡金属簇等催化剂在丙烷氧化脱氢中的CO2激活效果。

        活性氧金属氧化物催化剂有效利用氧气作为强氧化剂来促进烷烃中C-H键的断裂，而非活性氧金属氧化物催化剂可能需要额外条件或添加剂以有效激活CO2。过渡金属簇也参与此过程，但其具体机制和有效性可能有所不同。

        烷烃的氧化脱氢通常涉及C-H键的活化，其中传统氧化剂如氧气在红ox反应中起关键作用。活性氧催化剂如V2O5和VOx/SiO2通过氧化烷烃并由传统氧化剂还原，形成活性表面来参与这一过程。而非活性催化剂如CeO2则通过异裂键解离来激活C-H键，利用气相氧化剂而非表面。

        各种催化剂在激活CO2进行氧化脱氢时的有效性取决于它们对丙烷中C-H键的选择性激活能力，无需外部氧化剂如O2。CeO2因其独特的性质，在高效激活CO2和促进烷烃脱氢方面表现出潜力。

        CO2作为替代氧化剂提供了多方面优势，包括避免深氧化烷烃和烷炔、减少焦炭生成、通过参与逆水煤气变换反应（RWGS）促进丙烷转换。这使得CO2成为烷烃氧化脱氢过程中的有价值的替代氧化剂。

        CO2的使用作为替代氧化剂具有潜在优势，能有效避免深氧化烷烃和烷炔、减少焦炭生成、促进丙烷转换通过产生的氢参与逆水煤气变换反应。这使得CO2成为烷烃氧化脱氢过程中的有价值替代氧化剂。

        该研究强调了铁掺杂的铈氧化物（Fe-CeO2）在CO2条件下用于丙烷氧化脱氢的催化剂应用。该系统展示了与传统活性氧催化剂相比的增强稳定性和有前景的催化性能。催化剂性能归因于特定合成参数的影响，包括煅烧温度，这影响铁与铈之间的固溶体形成，从而增强催化剂的活性和稳定性。Fe-CeO2催化剂的合成涉及共沉淀法后不同温度的煅烧，其中600°C的煅烧尤为有利，因为可以产生更高表面积和Fe-Ce固溶体，而不是分离的cerianite-hematite相。

        未来研究可能关注探索新兴领域和创新方法，如优化催化剂合成条件、提高CO2激活效率、开发更选择性和稳定的催化剂，考虑提出CO2分解能垒的机制和能量壁垒。
