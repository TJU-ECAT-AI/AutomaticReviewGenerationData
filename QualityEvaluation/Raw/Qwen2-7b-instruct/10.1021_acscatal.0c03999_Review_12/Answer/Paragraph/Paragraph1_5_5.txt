                The comparative analysis of mechanisms involved in the oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes, focusing on the activation of C-H bonds and catalyst regeneration, reveals significant differences based on the catalyst's redox activity. Redox-active metal oxide catalysts, exemplified by vanadia (V2O5) and vanadyl oxide (VOx/SiO2), are proficient in utilizing oxygen as a strong oxidant to break C-H bonds through a redox cycle. These catalysts are oxidized by the alkane and subsequently reduced by a traditional oxidant, leading to their regeneration and facilitating the activation of C-H bonds. On the other hand, redox-inactive catalysts, such as ceria (CeO2), may necessitate additional conditions or additives for effective activation of CO2. Transition metal clusters also contribute to the process, albeit with varying mechanisms and effectiveness.

Redox-active catalysts like V2O5 and VOx/SiO2, alongside redox-inactive catalysts like CeO2, demonstrate differing abilities in activating CO2 for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane, with ceria showing particular promise due to its unique properties.

This system demonstrates superior performance compared to traditional redox-active catalysts, attributing its effectiveness to the specific synthesis parameters, notably calcination temperature, which influences the formation of a solid solution between iron and ceria, thus enhancing the catalyst's activity and stability.

                Density Functional Theory (DFT) calculations provide excellent agreement with experimental data, validating the proposed reaction mechanisms for both redox-active and redox-inactive catalysts. The effectiveness of different catalysts in activating CO2 for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane is contingent upon their ability to selectively activate C-H bonds in propane without requiring an external oxidant like oxygen. Traditional oxidants and CO2 play pivotal roles in influencing the energy barriers for CO2 dissociation during the oxidative dehydrogenation process.

This optimal synthesis condition is crucial for the catalyst's performance in oxidative dehydrogenation, ensuring a stable and active surface for the reaction.

                In summary, the study of redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts in oxidative dehydrogenation highlights the importance of catalyst type, oxidant choice, and synthesis parameters in achieving efficient activation of C-H bonds and effective CO2 activation for propane dehydrogenation.

                氧化脱氢反应中C-H键激活和催化剂再生机制的比较分析揭示了基于催化剂的还原活性的显著差异。具有还原活性的金属氧化物催化剂，如V2O5和VOx/SiO2，能够有效地利用氧气作为强氧化剂来断裂C-H键，并通过一个还原周期进行催化循环。这些催化剂在被烷烃氧化后由传统氧化剂还原，从而再生并促进C-H键的激活。相比之下，无还原活性的催化剂，如铈氧化物（CeO2），可能需要额外条件或添加剂才能有效激活CO2。

                将CO2用作氧化脱氢反应中的替代氧化剂具有多重优势，包括避免深氧化烷烃、减少焦炭形成以及通过参与逆水煤气变换反应促进丙烷转化。具有还原活性的催化剂（如V2O5和VOx/SiO2）和无还原活性的催化剂（如CeO2）在丙烷氧化脱氢过程中对CO2的激活表现出不同的能力，其中铈氧化物显示出特别的潜力，这归功于其独特的特性，使其能够高效地激活CO2并促进烷烃脱氢。

                铁掺杂铈氧化物（Fe-CeO2）被报道在使用CO2进行丙烷氧化脱氢时展现出增强的稳定性和有前景的催化性能。该系统与传统还原活性催化剂相比表现出更优性能，其效果归因于铁与铈之间合成参数的影响，特别是通过控制特定温度的煅烧过程，形成铁-铈固溶体而不是分离的铈亚铁和铁亚氧化物相，从而增强了催化剂的活性和稳定性。

                密度泛函理论（DFT）计算与实验数据高度吻合，验证了所提出的反应机制的有效性。不同催化剂在丙烷氧化脱氢过程中激活CO2的能力取决于它们选择性激活C-H键的能力，而无需外部氧化剂如氧气。传统氧化剂和CO2在影响CO2解离能垒方面发挥着关键作用。
