        Comparative analysis reveals the distinct mechanisms of C-H bond activation and catalyst regeneration in oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes, contingent upon whether the catalyst is redox-active or redox-inactive. Redox-active metal oxide catalysts, notably V2O5 and VOx/SiO2, are proficient in activating CO2 for the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane, whereas redox-inactive catalysts like CeO2 may necessitate supplementary conditions or additives for effective CO2 activation. Transition metal clusters also contribute to the process, albeit with variable mechanisms and efficacy. Traditional oxidants and CO2 are pivotal in influencing the energy barriers for CO2 dissociation during the oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes.

        The utilization of CO2 as an alternative oxidant, instead of oxygen, holds potential to avoid deep oxidation of alkanes and alkynes, reduce coke formation, and favor propane conversion by virtue of the hydrogen produced participating in the reverse water-gas shift reaction (RWGS). However, Cr-based catalysts often suffer from severe and rapid deactivation within a short timeframe, irrespective of sample preparation, reaction conditions, and reactor design. Iron-doped ceria (Fe-CeO2) showcases enhanced stability and promising catalytic performance for propane oxidative dehydrogenation using CO2 as a soft oxidant. 

        The synthesis of Fe-doped ceria catalysts involves a co-precipitation method followed by calcination at specific temperatures, with 600°C being advantageous for higher specific surface area and the formation of a Fe-Ce solid solution, thus enhancing the catalyst's activity and stability.

        The effectiveness of different catalysts in activating CO2 for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane varies, with ceria demonstrating promise due to its unique properties enabling efficient CO2 activation and alkane dehydrogenation. The role of CO2 as a soft oxidant in oxidative dehydrogenation is significant, mitigating deep oxidation, reducing coke formation, and facilitating propane conversion through participation in the RWGS.

        A series of studies highlight the comparative advantages and disadvantages of redox-active and redox-inactive catalysts in oxidative dehydrogenation, emphasizing the pivotal role of CO2 as an alternative oxidant and the importance of catalyst synthesis parameters for optimal performance.

        比较分析显示，在烷烃氧化脱氢过程中，根据催化剂是否为氧化还原活性或非氧化还原活性，C-H键活化和催化剂再生的机制存在显著差异。氧化还原活性金属氧化物催化剂，如V2O5和VOx/SiO2，能够有效地利用氧气作为强氧化剂来促进C-H键的断裂，而氧化还原非活性催化剂，如CeO2，可能需要额外条件或添加剂来有效激活CO2。过渡金属簇也参与这一过程，但其机制和有效性可能有所不同。传统的氧化剂和CO2在影响CO2解离能垒方面起着关键作用。

        将CO2用作替代氧化剂（而不是氧气），有可能避免深氧化烷烃和烯烃、减少焦炭形成，并通过产生的氢参与逆水煤气变换反应（RWGS）促进丙烷的转化。然而，Cr基催化剂往往在短时间内经历严重的快速失活，无论样品制备、反应条件还是反应器设计如何。铁掺杂的铈氧化物（Fe-CeO2）展示了使用CO2作为软氧化剂进行丙烷氧化脱氢的增强稳定性和有前景的催化性能。

        Fe-CeO2催化剂的合成涉及共沉淀法，随后在不同温度下进行煅烧，其中600°C特别有利，可获得更高的比表面积和Fe-Ce固溶体的形成，从而增强催化剂的活性和稳定性。

        各种催化剂在激活CO2以进行丙烷氧化脱氢中的有效性各不相同，其中铈显示出潜力，因为它独特的特性允许高效地激活CO2并促进烷烃脱氢。CO2作为软氧化剂在氧化脱氢中的作用至关重要，可以缓解深氧化，减少焦炭形成，并通过参与RWGS促进丙烷转化。

        多项研究表明，对比红氧化还原活性和非氧化还原活性催化剂在氧化脱氢过程中的优缺点，强调了CO2作为替代氧化剂的关键作用以及催化剂合成参数对于最佳性能的重要性。

        从整合理解和前瞻性展望的角度来看，这个领域的潜在方向包括：
        - 开发能够有效利用CO2作为氧化剂而不需额外添加剂或条件的催化剂。
        - 探索结合不同类型的催化剂（红氧化还原活性和非氧化还原活性）以提高性能的协同效应。
        - 优化金属氧化物催化剂的合成，以定制其性质以更好地激活CO2和烷烃脱氢。
        - 探讨催化膜反应器集成的可能性，以实现更高效的丙烷脱氢过程。
        - 提高催化剂的稳定性和选择性以提高产品纯度和产率。
