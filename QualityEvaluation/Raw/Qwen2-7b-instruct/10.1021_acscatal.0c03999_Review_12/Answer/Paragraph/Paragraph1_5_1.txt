        A comprehensive review of the mechanisms underlying the oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes, focusing on the activation of C-H bonds, catalyst regeneration, and oxidant utilization, particularly in the context of redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts and the role of CO2 as an alternative oxidant. This analysis encompasses a range of studies that highlight the effectiveness of catalysts such as V2O5, VOx/SiO2, CeO2, and transition metal clusters in activating CO2 for the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane. The review elucidates the differences in mechanisms and energy barriers associated with redox-active and redox-inactive catalysts, emphasizing the pivotal role of CO2 in mitigating deep oxidation, reducing coke formation, and facilitating propane conversion through its participation in the reverse water-gas shift reaction.

        The study of oxygen and nitrous oxide as oxidants in ethane oxidative dehydrogenation over silica-titania-supported molybdenum catalysts underscores the importance of the oxidant's effect on both the catalyst structure and the kinetics of the ethane oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) reaction. The role of CO2 as a soft oxidant is further explored, demonstrating its potential benefits in avoiding deep oxidation, reducing coke formation, and promoting propane conversion.

        The utilization of CO2 as an alternative oxidant is discussed in the context of its potential to replace oxygen, offering advantages such as avoiding deep oxidation, reducing coke formation, and favoring propane conversion through its involvement in the reverse water-gas shift reaction. However, challenges include the severe and rapid deactivation of Cr-based catalysts within a short time, regardless of sample preparation, reaction conditions, and reactor design. The introduction of iron-doped ceria (Fe-CeO2) as a catalyst for propane oxidative dehydrogenation using CO2 showcases enhanced stability and promising catalytic performance, attributed to specific synthesis parameters that optimize the catalyst's activity and stability.

        This review provides an integrative understanding of the complex interplay between catalysts, oxidants, and reaction mechanisms in oxidative dehydrogenation processes, highlighting the potential of CO2 as a viable alternative oxidant and the ongoing challenges in catalyst development and optimization. The future direction of research in this field includes exploring emerging catalysts and innovative methodologies to enhance the efficiency and sustainability of oxidative dehydrogenation processes.
        
        * * *
        
        Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Building upon the insights presented in this review, there is an opportunity to explore new frontiers in oxidative dehydrogenation technology. Future research could focus on the development of novel catalyst materials and the integration of advanced computational methods to predict and optimize catalyst performance. The incorporation of renewable sources of energy and the exploration of alternative oxidants, beyond CO2, might lead to more sustainable and efficient dehydrogenation processes. Additionally, the design of catalysts that can operate under mild conditions or with lower energy requirements could significantly enhance the economic viability of these processes. The integration of these advancements with existing technologies could pave the way for more environmentally friendly and economically sustainable solutions in petrochemical processing and renewable energy applications.

        氧化脱氢反应中烷烃C-H键活化、催化剂再生以及氧化剂利用的机制综述，特别关注红氧化活性和非氧化性金属氧化物催化剂及其在丙烷氧化脱氢过程中CO2作为替代氧化剂的应用。本综述涵盖了对催化剂如V2O5、VOx/SiO2、CeO2和过渡金属簇的有效性分析，强调了CO2在丙烷氧化脱氢中的作用。

        对使用氧气和硝酸盐作为氧化剂在硅酸盐-钛酸盐支持的钼催化剂上的乙烷氧化脱氢研究，揭示了氧化剂对催化剂结构和乙烷氧化脱氢（ODH）反应动力学的影响。探讨了CO2作为软氧化剂的角色，表明其在避免深氧化、减少焦炭形成以及通过参与逆水煤气变换反应促进丙烷转化方面的潜力。

        在考虑CO2作为潜在替代氧化剂时，讨论了其作为氧的替代品的潜在优势，包括避免深氧化、减少焦炭形成以及通过其参与逆水煤气变换反应促进丙烷转化。然而，Cr基催化剂在较短时间内的严重且快速失活是一个挑战，无论样品制备、反应条件还是反应器设计如何。铁掺杂的铈氧化物（Fe-CeO2）作为丙烷氧化脱氢过程中使用CO2的催化剂的引入展示了增强的稳定性及有前景的催化性能，这归因于合成参数的优化，这些参数提高了催化剂的活性和稳定性。

        本综述提供了催化剂、氧化剂和反应机制之间复杂相互作用的全面理解，突出了CO2作为可行替代氧化剂的潜力以及催化剂开发和优化的持续挑战。未来的研究方向包括探索新型催化剂材料和集成高级计算方法以预测和优化催化剂性能的可能性。探索替代能源源和寻找除了CO2之外的其他氧化剂，可能有助于实现更可持续和高效的脱氢过程。此外，设计在温和条件下或较低能量需求下运行的催化剂，可以显著提高这些过程的经济可行性。将这些进步与现有技术相结合，可能为更环保、经济上可行的解决方案铺平道路，在石化加工和可再生能源应用领域开辟新的前景。
