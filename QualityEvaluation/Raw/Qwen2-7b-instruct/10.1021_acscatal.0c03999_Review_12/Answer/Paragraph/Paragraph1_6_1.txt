        The in-depth analysis of oxidative dehydrogenation mechanisms for propane, focusing on the activation of C-H bonds, catalyst regeneration, and oxidant utilization, reveals distinct differences between redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts. Redox-active catalysts, exemplified by vanadia (V2O5) and vanadyl oxide (VOx/SiO2), effectively harness oxygen as a potent oxidant to facilitate the cleavage of C-H bonds, while redox-inactive catalysts like ceria (CeO2) require additional conditions or additives for efficient CO2 activation. Transition metal clusters also contribute to the process, though their specific mechanisms and efficacy vary.

        The use of CO2 as an alternative oxidant holds promise for avoiding deep oxidation of alkanes, reducing coke formation, and enhancing propane conversion through its participation in the reverse water-gas shift reaction. Notably, iron-doped ceria (Fe-CeO2) demonstrates enhanced stability and catalytic performance for propane oxidative dehydrogenation using CO2, outperforming traditional redox-active catalysts. This system benefits from calcination at 600°C, which yields a higher specific surface area and a Fe-Ce solid solution, improving the catalyst's activity and stability.

        Comparative studies across multiple publications highlight the effectiveness of catalysts such as V2O5, VOx/SiO2, CeO2, and transition metal clusters in activating CO2 for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane. These catalysts influence the energy barriers for CO2 dissociation, with ceria showing particular promise due to its unique properties that enable efficient CO2 activation and alkane dehydrogenation.

        The integration of these insights points to future directions in optimizing catalysts for oxidative dehydrogenation processes. The focus should be on enhancing catalyst stability, improving CO2 activation, and exploring the synergistic effects of metal clusters with oxide supports. The development of novel catalysts and the refinement of existing ones could lead to more efficient and environmentally friendly methods for alkane conversion, particularly in the context of sustainable hydrogen production from CO2.

        对丙烷氧化脱氢作用机制的深入分析，特别关注C-H键激活、催化剂再生和氧化剂利用，揭示了活性氧化物催化剂（活性与非活性）之间的显著差异。活性氧化物催化剂，如五氧化二钒(V2O5)和五氧化二钒/二氧化硅(VOx/SiO2)，有效利用氧气作为强氧化剂来促进C-H键的断裂，而非活性催化剂如氧化铈(CeO2)则需要额外条件或添加剂以实现高效的二氧化碳激活。过渡金属簇也参与这一过程，尽管其特定机制和有效性各不相同。

        将二氧化碳作为替代氧化剂的应用具有避免深氧化烷烃、减少焦炭形成以及通过参与逆水煤气变换反应促进丙烷转化的潜力。值得注意的是，铁掺杂氧化铈(Fe-CeO2)在使用二氧化碳进行丙烷氧化脱氢过程中表现出增强的稳定性和催化性能，优于传统的活性氧化物催化剂。这一系统得益于在600°C下进行的煅烧，这提高了特定的表面积并形成了Fe-Ce固溶体，从而改善了催化剂的活性和稳定性。

        多篇出版物的比较研究强调了V2O5、VOx/SiO2、CeO2、过渡金属簇等催化剂在二氧化碳激活下丙烷氧化脱氢的有效性。这些催化剂影响二氧化碳分解的能量壁垒，其中铈显示出特别的潜力，因为它独特的特性允许高效地激活二氧化碳并促进烷烃脱氢。

        这些见解的整合表明了优化氧化脱氢过程催化剂的未来方向。重点应放在提高催化剂稳定性、改进二氧化碳激活以及探索金属簇与氧化物支撑材料协同效应上。开发新型催化剂和精炼现有催化剂可能引领更高效、更环保的烷烃转换方法，特别是在二氧化碳制氢的背景下。
