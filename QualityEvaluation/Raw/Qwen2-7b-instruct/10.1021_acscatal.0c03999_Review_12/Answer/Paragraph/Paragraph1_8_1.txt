        The oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes, particularly propane, has been extensively studied using a variety of catalysts, focusing on the mechanisms of C-H bond activation, catalyst regeneration, and the utilization of both traditional oxidants and CO2. This analysis delves into the comparative study of redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts, notably V2O5, VOx/SiO2, CeO2, and transition metal clusters, in the context of oxidative dehydrogenation.

        Redox-active catalysts like V2O5 and VOx/SiO2 are adept at facilitating the activation of C-H bonds and regeneration of the active surface through a redox cycle, typically involving the alkane as the oxidant and a traditional oxidant. Conversely, redox-inactive catalysts such as CeO2 might necessitate additional conditions or additives for effective CO2 activation. Transition metal clusters also play a pivotal role, though their specific mechanisms and effectiveness can vary.

        CO2 acts as a central component in oxidative dehydrogenation processes, impacting the energy barriers for its dissociation. It serves as a potential alternative oxidant, offering benefits such as avoiding deep oxidation of alkanes, reducing coke formation, and facilitating propane conversion through participation in the reverse water-gas shift reaction (RWGS).

        The use of CO2 as a soft oxidant in oxidative dehydrogenation presents several advantages. It mitigates the deep oxidation of alkanes and alkynes, reduces the formation of coke, and enhances propane conversion by leveraging the produced hydrogen. A notable example is the use of iron-doped ceria (Fe-CeO2) as a catalyst for oxidative dehydrogenation with CO2, which exhibits enhanced stability and promising catalytic performance compared to traditional redox-active catalysts. The performance of Fe-CeO2 is attributed to the synthesis parameters, including calcination temperature, which affects the formation of a Fe-Ce solid solution, thereby enhancing the catalyst's activity and stability.

        The literature encompasses a wide array of catalysts and their performance in activating CO2 for oxidative dehydrogenation. The effectiveness of these catalysts is contingent upon their ability to selectively activate C-H bonds in propane without requiring an external oxidant like oxygen. Ceria, for instance, demonstrates promise due to its unique properties that enable efficient CO2 activation and alkane dehydrogenation.

        The study of the mechanisms of C-H bond activation and catalyst regeneration in oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes reveals significant differences between redox-active and redox-inactive catalysts. Redox-active catalysts like V2O5 and VOx/SiO2 are instrumental in the redox cycle, where the catalyst is oxidized by the alkane and subsequently reduced by a traditional oxidant, leading to the regeneration of the active surface. Redox-inactive catalysts, such as ceria, activate C-H bonds through heterolytic cleavage, utilizing the gas phase oxidant instead of the surface.

        In conclusion, the integration of CO2 as an alternative oxidant in oxidative dehydrogenation processes presents a promising avenue for enhancing efficiency and sustainability. The utilization of redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts, along with transition metal clusters, showcases the versatility and adaptability in the field of oxidative dehydrogenation. The detailed exploration of these mechanisms and catalysts offers insights into the optimization of processes for the selective conversion of alkanes, paving the way for advancements in chemical synthesis and environmental sustainability.

        烷烃的氧化脱氢作用，特别在丙烷上，通过多种催化剂进行了深入研究，主要关注C-H键激活、催化剂再生机制以及传统氧化剂和CO2的利用。本分析对比了红氧化活性金属氧化物催化剂（如V2O5、VOx/SiO2）和红氧化不活性催化剂（如CeO2）在氧化脱氢作用中的应用。

        红氧化活性催化剂如V2O5和VOx/SiO2能够通过红氧化循环促进C-H键的激活和催化剂表面的再生，通常涉及烷烃作为氧化剂和传统氧化剂。相反，红氧化不活性催化剂如CeO2可能需要额外条件或添加剂来有效激活CO2。过渡金属簇也发挥关键作用，尽管它们的具体机制和有效性可能有所不同。

        CO2作为氧化脱氢作用的核心成分，影响其分解的能量壁垒，并作为一种潜在的替代氧化剂，提供避免深氧化烷烃、减少焦炭形成以及通过参与逆水煤气变换反应（RWGS）促进丙烷转换的优点。

        将CO2作为软氧化剂应用于氧化脱氢作用带来了一系列优势。它有助于减轻烷烃和烯烃的深氧化，减少焦炭的形成，并通过利用产生的氢气促进丙烷转换。一个显著的例子是使用铁掺杂的铈氧化物（Fe-CeO2）作为使用CO2的氧化脱氢催化剂，该系统相比传统红氧化活性催化剂展现出增强的稳定性和有前景的催化性能。催化剂的性能归因于合成参数，包括煅烧温度，这影响了铁-铈固溶体的形成，从而增强了催化剂的活性和稳定性。

        关于不同催化剂在激活CO2进行氧化脱氢作用的有效性研究表明，这些催化剂的能力取决于它们是否能选择性地激活丙烷中的C-H键而无需外部氧化剂如氧气。铈表现出潜力，由于其独特的性质，能够有效地激活CO2并促进烷烃脱氢。

        氧化脱氢作用机制和催化剂再生过程的深入研究揭示了红氧化活性和红氧化不活性催化剂之间的重要差异。红氧化活性催化剂如V2O5和VOx/SiO2通过红氧化循环起作用，其中催化剂被烷烃氧化，随后由传统氧化剂还原，从而实现活性表面的再生。红氧化不活性催化剂，如铈，通过异裂性裂解激活C-H键，利用气体相氧化剂而不是表面。

        总结而言，将CO2作为氧化剂的应用为提高效率和可持续性提供了有前景的机会。红氧化活性和红氧化不活性金属氧化物催化剂，以及过渡金属簇的利用展示了氧化脱氢作用领域的多样性和适应性。对这些机制和催化剂的详细探索提供了优化烷烃选择性转化过程的见解，为化学合成和环境保护开辟了新的方向。
