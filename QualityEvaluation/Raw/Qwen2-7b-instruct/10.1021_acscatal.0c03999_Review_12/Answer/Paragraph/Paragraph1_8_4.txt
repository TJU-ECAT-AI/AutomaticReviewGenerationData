        Comparative analysis of the mechanisms of C-H bond activation, catalyst regeneration, and oxidant utilization in the oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes, focusing on the role of redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts with CO2 as an alternative oxidant, has been extensively explored. The literature reveals that redox-active catalysts such as vanadia (V2O5) and vanadyl oxide (VOx/SiO2) effectively utilize oxygen as a strong oxidant to facilitate the breaking of C-H bonds, whereas redox-inactive catalysts like ceria (CeO2) may require additional conditions or additives for CO2 activation. Transition metal clusters also play a role in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane, though their specific mechanisms and effectiveness may vary.

        Traditional oxidants and CO2 are central to the oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes, significantly influencing the energy barriers for CO2 dissociation. The study of Ni-Mo/Al2O3 catalysts indicates that CO2 and nitrous oxide (N2O) as oxidants result in activation energies of 161 and 112 kJ mol-1, respectively, closely aligned with theoretical predictions.         The use of CO2 as an alternative oxidant offers several advantages, including avoiding deep oxidation of alkanes, reducing coke formation, and facilitating propane conversion through participation in the reverse water-gas shift reaction. This makes CO2 a valuable alternative in oxidative dehydrogenation processes.

        传统氧化剂和二氧化碳是烷烃氧化脱氢过程的核心，显著影响二氧化碳的分解能垒。Ni-Mo/Al2O3催化剂的研究表明，使用二氧化碳和硝酸盐氧化剂（N2O）分别得到的激活能量为161和112 kJ mol-1，与理论预测值高度一致。最丰富的产物丁二烯，在使用N2O作为氧化剂时表现出高达0.89 gbutadiene. gcat -1 h-1的生产力，这强调了二氧化碳在该过程中的有效性。
