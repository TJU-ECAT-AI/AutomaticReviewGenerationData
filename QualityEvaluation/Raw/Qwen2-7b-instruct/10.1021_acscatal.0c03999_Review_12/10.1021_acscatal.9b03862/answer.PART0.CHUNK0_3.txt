Answer: In the context of oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes, redox-active metal oxides such as V2O5 and VOx/SiO2 facilitate the activation of C-H bonds and catalyst regeneration, whereas redox-inactive catalysts like CeO2 and transition metal clusters might not efficiently activate CO2 for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane due to their distinct mechanisms and energy barriers for CO2 dissociation. Traditional oxidants and CO2 play crucial roles in these processes, influencing the effectiveness of different catalysts in CO2 activation and alkane dehydrogenation.
