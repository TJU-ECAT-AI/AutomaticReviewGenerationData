The document discusses the behavior of NO2 in the selective catalytic reduction of nitric oxides with ammonia on the surface of a V2O5 catalyst. It identifies two main reaction routes for NO2: direct reoxidation of V4+OH to V5+O and reoxidation through the production of HNO3. In the direct reoxidation, NO2 adsorbs on the cluster, captures a hydrogen from V4+OH, and desorbs as V5+O and cis-HNO2. The reoxidation through HNO3 production involves NO2 reacting with H2O or NH3 to form HNO3, which then reoxidizes V4+OH to V5+O. The reoxidation pathway through HNO3 is favored at lower temperatures due to a lower activation energy. Both cis-HNO2 and trans-HNO2 can react with NH3 to produce H2O and N2 or react with themselves to produce H2O, NO, and NO2. The document provides detailed energy profiles and identifies the rate-determining steps for each reaction route.
