[1] "The oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes involves the breaking of C-H bonds to form higher hydrocarbons and hydrogen."
[2] "Redox-active metal oxides, such as V2O5 and VOx/SiO2, are effective in this process due to their ability to act as electron shuttles, facilitating the activation of alkane molecules and the reduction of oxygen."
[3] "Redox-inactive metal oxides, like CeO2, can also participate in oxidative dehydrogenation through a different mechanism, involving the direct activation of alkane molecules by the metal oxide surface."
[4] "Transition metal clusters have been found to be particularly effective in CO2 activation for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane, as they lower the energy barrier for CO2 dissociation."
[5] "Catalyst regeneration is crucial for maintaining the efficiency of these catalysts over multiple reaction cycles. This process often involves reoxidation of the metal oxide to its original state, allowing it to regain its activity."
[6] "Traditional oxidants, such as air or oxygen, are commonly used in the regeneration of these catalysts. However, CO2 has also been explored as an alternative oxidant due to its environmental benefits and potential for more efficient regeneration."
</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Comparing the mechanisms of C-H bond activation and catalyst regeneration in oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes, redox-active metal oxides like V2O5 and VOx/SiO2 are effective due to their electron shuttle ability, which facilitates alkane activation and oxygen reduction. Redox-inactive metal oxides, such as CeO2, can activate alkane molecules directly via a distinct mechanism. Transition metal clusters show promise in CO2 activation for propane oxidative dehydrogenation by reducing the energy barrier for CO2 dissociation. Catalyst regeneration is essential for maintaining catalyst efficiency across multiple cycles, typically involving reoxidation to restore activity. Traditional oxidants, including air or oxygen, are commonly used in regeneration processes, with CO2 being explored as an alternative for its environmental benefits and potential for more efficient regeneration.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
