<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Analyzing the oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes (ODH), we observe that both bulk metal oxides and supported systems are utilized in catalysis. Challenges in achieving high selectivity and conversion are common, as these factors significantly influence process efficiency. The selectivity issues are often addressed by site isolation, which enhances the ability to control reaction pathways. The proposed mechanisms involve C-H bond activation, the formation of M-alkyl intermediates, and 2H-abstraction. The role of site isolation in improving selectivity is crucial, as it allows for more precise control over the reaction pathway, leading to higher yields of desired products. The use of different vanadium oxide crystal surfaces with varying activity towards the production of CH3O- groups also contributes to the complexity and efficiency of the process.
