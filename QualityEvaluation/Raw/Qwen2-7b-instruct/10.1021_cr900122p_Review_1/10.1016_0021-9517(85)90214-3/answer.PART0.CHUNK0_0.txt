Analyzing the oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes (ODH), the interplay between various catalysts such as bulk metal oxides and supported systems, challenges in achieving high selectivity and conversion, and the proposed reaction mechanisms such as C-H bond activation, M-alkyl intermediates, and 2H-abstraction plays a critical role in process efficiency. The use of V205-Si02 catalysts demonstrates a lower and narrower working range compared to molybdenum oxide catalysts, offering advantages in terms of lower operating temperatures for CH30H formation. However, these catalysts exhibit an unfavorable product selectivity towards CH30H, indicating a trade-off between temperature and selectivity. The kinetic data suggest that the reaction mechanisms on V205-Si02 catalysts involve parallel pathways for the formation of CH30H, HCHO, and CO, rather than sequential oxidation processes.
