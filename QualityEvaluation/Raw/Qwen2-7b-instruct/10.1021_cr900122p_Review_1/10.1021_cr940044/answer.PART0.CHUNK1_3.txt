<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1. Chromium exists as Cr5+, Cr3+, and Cr2+ under reducing conditions, with varying coordination and polymerization levels depending on the support type and composition.

2. The chemical behavior of chromium on amorphous surfaces or molecular sieve surfaces is quite similar.

3. Supported chromium ions exhibit high reactivity towards various molecules, including alcohols, ethylene, carbon monoxide, and water.

4. The redox behavior of chromium is dependent on the support type and composition, and it tends to be more easily reduced on chemically softer supports.

5. The chemistry on amorphous surfaces or molecular sieve surfaces is comparable, indicating potential similarities in reaction pathways.

6. Challenges in achieving high selectivity and conversion in oxidative dehydrogenation include identifying the precise nature of active sites, their isolation, and understanding the mechanism of C-H bond activation.

7. Site isolation is crucial in addressing selectivity issues, particularly in supported catalysts, as spatial confinement can lead to distinct reactivity patterns.

8. Mechanisms such as C-H bond activation, formation of M-alkyl intermediates, and 2H-abstraction contribute to the oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes.

9. Collectively, these factors influence the efficiency of the process by affecting reaction kinetics, product distribution, and catalyst longevity.
