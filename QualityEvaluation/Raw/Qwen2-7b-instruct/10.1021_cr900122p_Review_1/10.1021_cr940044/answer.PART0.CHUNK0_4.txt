1. The oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes (ODH) is a challenging process that requires high selectivity and conversion.
2. Various catalysts, including bulk metal oxides and supported systems, have been explored for ODH.
3. Achieving high selectivity and conversion in ODH is difficult due to the complexity of the reactions involved.
4. Proposed reaction mechanisms involve C-H bond activation, M-alkyl intermediates, and 2H-abstraction.
5. The interplay between these factors collectively influences the process efficiency.
6. Site isolation plays a crucial role in addressing selectivity issues in ODH.
7. Catalyst design and optimization can improve the efficiency of ODH processes.
