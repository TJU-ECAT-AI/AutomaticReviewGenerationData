            Recent reports confirm nitrous oxide (N20) as an efficient oxidant in ethane dehydrogenation over supported molybdenum oxides. Key insights from the literature suggest that the surface density of vanadium oxide (VOx) affects propane combustion rates less, causing a decrease in the propene production rate relative to propane dehydrogenation (k2/k1) with increased surface density. The high k3/k1 ratio on VOx-based catalysts results in significant yield losses. As the Lewis acidity of the cations increases, the difference in activation energies between propane ODH and propene combustion also increases. Catalysts with high redox properties exhibit enhanced catalytic activity, and less acidic metal oxides lead to reduced ΔE1 ΔE3 values and a smaller k3/k1 ratio, thus boosting propene yield. Site isolation strategies help in addressing selectivity issues by optimizing the reaction pathways.
