        The comprehensive analysis of oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes (ODH) involves an intricate interplay between various catalysts, including bulk metal oxides and supported systems. The key challenges in achieving high selectivity and conversion are deeply rooted in the mechanisms of C-H bond activation, M-alkyl intermediates, and 2H-abstraction. Site isolation plays a pivotal role in addressing selectivity issues by confining the reaction pathways.

        La-based catalysts, exemplified by LaOCl, LaCl3, and La phases with an intermediate extent of chlorination, are highly active for methane conversion to methyl chloride in the presence of hydrogen chloride and oxygen, demonstrating remarkable stability with no observed deactivation after three weeks of testing. These catalysts, previously known for their role in hydrocarbon chlorination, challenge traditional beliefs regarding the necessity of a reducible metal for catalytic sites.

        For propane dehydrogenation, the high selectivity towards CH3Cl, achieved at methane conversions below 12 mol % with only trace amounts of CHCl3 detected and zero CCl4 formation, underscores the effectiveness of these catalysts. The proposed mechanism involves the surface chlorine species being activated by oxygen, leading to enhanced selectivity and process efficiency.

        In the realm of metal molybdates, particularly Nickel and Cobalt-based metal molybdates, they stand out for their high activity in propane activation at relatively low temperatures, offering an alternative to vanadium-based catalysts. These catalysts are promising for the direct conversion of propane to acrylic acid or acrylonitrile, with the possibility of incorporating a second component consisting of olefin-converting, multicomponent mixed metal molybdates to enhance both activity and selectivity.

        Supported vanadium oxide catalysts are integral to molecular structural characterization and reactivity properties, further enriching our understanding of ODH mechanisms. High molecular stability, while complicating methane conversion, supports the development of useful chemicals. Insights from recent studies underscore the importance of these mechanisms in enhancing yield and selectivity.

        The oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes, particularly methane, faces significant challenges due to the high activation barriers for C-H bond cleavage. Catalysts play a crucial role in overcoming these obstacles, with site isolation being a key strategy for enhancing selectivity. The formation of alkyl radicals through surface 0- ions over molybdenum/silica catalysts highlights a catalytic cycle essential for understanding and optimizing process efficiency.

        Despite advancements, achieving high selectivity and conversion remains elusive, with mechanistic problems proving particularly challenging. The role of site isolation in addressing selectivity issues and the ongoing efforts to develop tunable, selective hydrocarbon functionalization are crucial areas of focus.

        Phosphorus modification of PV0.0 catalysts significantly enhances selectivity in the oxidation of butane, particularly at low conversions, demonstrating the effectiveness of phosphorus modification in optimizing the ODH process. Raman spectroscopy elucidates the formation of VOPO4 compounds, providing empirical evidence for the improvement in product selectivity.

        Nitrous oxide (N20) emerges as an efficient oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of ethane over supported molybdenum oxides, demonstrating its effectiveness in this context. Understanding the role of oxygen ions (O-) in methane oxidation sheds light on the versatility of N20 in catalytic processes.

        The synthesis of CuTh oxide catalysts via calcination of copper and thorium hydroxides opens new avenues for selective hydrogenation and dehydrogenation reactions. The catalytic oxidative dehydrogenation of ethane or propane, closely correlated with the presence of copper in thoria, reveals the critical impact of copper content on catalyst efficiency and product selectivity.

        Challenges in the oxidation of alkene products faster than starting alkane products necessitate the development of processes that can handle high temperatures, often predominant in homogeneous gas phase reactions. The conversion of propene into acrolein and acrylonitrile using bismuthmolybdate catalysts exemplifies well-known processes, highlighting the role of CH bond activation involving H-abstraction to generate stabilized propenyl radicals.

        Bulk metal halides, notably LaCl3, catalyze several processes involving CH bond activation, including the selective transformation of methane into chloromethane and the hydrogen-chlorine exchange between chlorinated hydrocarbons. The proposed mechanism involves the activation of a CrOS bond, forming a Cr-alkyl intermediate that decomposes via β-H transfer, leading to the formation of the alkene and a CrIIIH surface species.

        The consensus emerging from the study of catalysts and reaction mechanisms points towards the probable active sites corresponding to an isolated Cr3+ in a pseudo-octahedral environment. Despite the complexity of the surface state of the catalysts, the role of site isolation in controlling reaction pathways and enhancing selectivity becomes increasingly evident.

    <Chinese>
        氧化脱氢化烷烃（ODH）的深入分析涉及各种催化剂的相互作用，包括多相金属氧化物和支撑系统，以实现高选择性和转化率。面临的挑战主要在于C-H键活化、甲基中间体生成等机制，导致副产品的形成。关键反应机制包括C-H键活化、M-甲基中间体和2H摘除。隔离活性位点有助于解决选择性问题，通过局部控制反应路径。

        具有镧元素的催化剂，如镧氧化氯、镧氯化物和镧的中间氯化物形式，在氢氯酸和氧气存在下对甲烷转换为甲基氯具有活性，显示出极高的稳定性：在测试后的三周内未检测到任何失活。这些催化剂，以前被认为是用于烃类氯化的催化剂，挑战了传统观点，即需要可还原的金属来形成催化位点。

        对于丙烷脱氢化，CH3Cl的选择性特别高，当甲烷转化率低于12摩尔％时，仅检测到微量的CHCl3，从未检测到CCl4。提出的机制涉及表面氯物种被氧气激活，这增强了选择性和过程效率。

        在钼基金属氧化物中，特别是镍和钴基钼氧化物，它们在相对较低温度下表现出高活性，对丙烷进行丙烷激活，为使用范德华催化剂提供了替代方案。这些催化剂有潜力直接将丙烷转化为丙烯酸或丙烯腈，同时考虑引入一种包含用于在气相反应中转换烯烃的多组分混合钼氧化物的第二成分，以增强活性和选择性。

        支撑的钒氧化物催化剂在分子结构表征和反应性质方面起着重要作用，进一步丰富了我们对ODH机制的理解。高分子稳定性虽然使甲烷转化变得复杂，但支持了有用化学品的发展。近期研究强调了这些机制在提高产率和选择性方面的关键作用。

        虽然取得了进展，但实现高选择性和转化率仍然是一个挑战，机制问题尤其具有挑战性。解决选择性问题的关键是通过隔离技术控制反应路径，并且正在努力开发可调的、选择性的碳氢化合物功能化。

        磷酸修饰的PV0.0催化剂在丁烷氧化中显著提高了选择性，尤其是在低转化率时，这表明磷酸修饰在优化ODH过程中非常有效。拉曼光谱揭示了VOPO4化合物的形成，为产品选择性的改善提供了实证证据。

        硝酸氧化氮（N20）作为支持的钼氧化物中的高效氧化剂，在乙烷的氧化脱氢化中表现出效率，证明了其在此上下文中的有效性。理解氧离子（O-）在甲烷氧化中的作用为N20在催化过程中的多功能性提供了洞察。

        铜钍氧化物催化剂通过铜和钍氢氧化物的煅烧合成，为选择性氢化和脱氢化反应开辟了新途径。在823 K的条件下对乙烷或丙烷进行氧化脱氢化，在铜的存在下，乙二醇的产生选择性与铜在钍氧化物中的含量成正比，表明铜含量对催化剂效率和产物选择性的影响至关重要。

        面临的问题之一是烷烃产物的氧化通常比初始烷烃更快，这要求开发能够处理较高温度的过程，其中气相反应往往占主导地位。使用铋钼酸盐催化剂将丙烯转化为丙烯醛和丙烯腈是广为人知的过程，突出显示了CH键活化涉及H摘除形成稳定丙烯基自由基这一关键步骤。

        大量金属卤化物，尤其是LaCl3，催化涉及CH键活化的多种过程，例如选择性地将甲烷转化为氯甲烷以及氯化碳氢化合物之间的氢氯交换。提出的机制涉及CH键在CrOS键上的活化，从而形成Cr-甲基中间体，随后通过β-H转移分解，形成烯烃和CrIIIH表面物种。

        当前形成的共识表明，可能的活性位点对应于孤立的Cr3+在一个伪八面体环境中。尽管催化剂表面状态的复杂性，控制反应路径和提高产物纯度的关键作用越来越明显。

        评论部分阐明了催化剂在ODH中的核心作用，包括多相金属氧化物和支撑系统的有效性的关键。实现高选择性和转化率面临的挑战通过理解反应机制和优化催化剂参数得到解决。隔离活性位点有助于防止不需要的副反应并提高产品纯度。

        将来的展望着重于探索新兴领域和创新方法，旨在利用催化剂设计和反应机制的进步。未来的研究重点可能集中在开发具有增强活性和选择性的催化剂，优化反应条件，并扩展可以有效转化的烷烃衍生物的范围。对于能够处理高转化率同时保持特定产品高选择性的催化剂的开发，仍然是该领域的一个重要前沿。
