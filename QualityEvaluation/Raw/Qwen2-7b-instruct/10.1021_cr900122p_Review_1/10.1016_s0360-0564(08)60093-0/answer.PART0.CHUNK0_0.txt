<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1. The oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes is a demanding process due to the high activation barriers for C-H bond cleavage.
2. Catalysts play a vital role in oxidative dehydrogenation, especially in achieving high selectivity and conversion.
3. Bulk metal oxides and supported systems have been extensively investigated for oxidative dehydrogenation, each having distinct advantages and constraints.
4. Factors such as catalyst loading, support material, and reaction conditions significantly affect the efficiency of oxidative dehydrogenation.
5. Proposed mechanisms for oxidative dehydrogenation include C-H bond activation, the formation of metal-alkyl intermediates, and 2H-abstraction.
6. Site isolation is crucial in addressing selectivity issues by restricting the reaction pathways.
7. High selectivity and conversion in oxidative dehydrogenation necessitate meticulous optimization of the catalyst and reaction parameters.

</EnglishVersionAnswer>
