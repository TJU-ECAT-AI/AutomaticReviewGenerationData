<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Analyzing the oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes (ODH), we see that it is a complex process that relies on various catalysts, including bulk metal oxides and supported systems, to achieve high selectivity and conversion. Achieving these goals presents several challenges, which are addressed through mechanisms such as C-H bond activation, M-alkyl intermediates, and 2H-abstraction. Site isolation techniques play a crucial role in enhancing selectivity within the ODH process.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
