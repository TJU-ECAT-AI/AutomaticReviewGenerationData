<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. This article discusses the development of Lewis acids from traditional homogeneous catalysts to greener homogeneous and heterogeneous catalysts, focusing on their application in selective organic reactions, particularly in aqueous media. It highlights the importance of these catalysts in enhancing reaction efficiency while minimizing environmental impact.

2. The study by Paramita Koley et al. investigates the high chemoselectivity achieved in the oxidative dehydration of glycerol to acrylic acid using a bifunctional metal-organic framework-polyoxometalate composite catalyst. The researchers aim to elucidate the underlying mechanisms and conditions that enable such high selectivity in the conversion process.
