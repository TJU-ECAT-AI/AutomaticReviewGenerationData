<EnglishVersionAnswer>

Analyzing the oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) process, we find that achieving high conversion and selectivity is challenging. Various catalysts, encompassing bulk metal oxides and supported systems, have been employed in this context. Site isolation is highlighted as a pivotal factor in tackling selectivity issues. Additionally, catalyst deactivation poses a considerable hurdle for commercializing the process. Innovations have been made to enhance catalyst stability, notably through hydrothermal and acid treatments.
