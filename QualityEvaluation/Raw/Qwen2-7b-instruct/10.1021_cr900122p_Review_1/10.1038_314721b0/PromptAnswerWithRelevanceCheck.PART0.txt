The challenge in achieving high selectivity and conversion in oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) of alkanes lies in the complex interplay between various catalysts, including bulk metal oxides and supported systems. Challenges include the difficulty in activating C-H bonds, forming M-alkyl intermediates, and efficiently removing hydrogen atoms through 2H-abstraction. These factors collectively influence process efficiency by affecting the rate and direction of chemical reactions.

3. Site isolation plays a crucial role in addressing selectivity issues in ODH processes. By isolating active sites on the catalyst surface, the risk of unwanted side reactions is reduced, thereby improving the selectivity towards desired products.
