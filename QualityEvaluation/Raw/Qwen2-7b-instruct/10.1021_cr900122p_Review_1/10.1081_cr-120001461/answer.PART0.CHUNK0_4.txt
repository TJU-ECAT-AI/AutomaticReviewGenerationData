<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes involves the use of various catalysts, both in bulk metal oxides and supported systems. Achieving high selectivity and conversion presents challenges due to the complex interplay between catalyst composition, operating conditions, oxygen reactivity, and the nature of the oxygen species involved. Site isolation plays a crucial role in addressing selectivity issues. Reaction mechanisms typically involve C-H bond activation, the formation of M-alkyl intermediates, and 2H-abstraction. These factors collectively influence process efficiency by affecting catalytic activity and selectivity. The reviewed work highlights the importance of understanding these mechanisms to improve catalyst performance and optimize reaction conditions for more efficient and selective alkane dehydrogenation.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
