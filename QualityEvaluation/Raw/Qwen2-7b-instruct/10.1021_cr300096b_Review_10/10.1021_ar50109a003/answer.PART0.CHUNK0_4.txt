Analyzing the significance of selective dehydrogenation reactions in chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage, we find that they are pivotal for various industrial applications. Reactor studies and UHV studies have greatly enhanced our comprehension of these processes, especially concerning C-H and N-H bond dehydrogenation over supported catalysts. These studies involve exploring essential research methodologies, characterization techniques, and reaction conditions, which in turn facilitates the optimization of catalytic processes in this domain.
