Reactor studies and UHV studies both play critical roles in understanding these processes.

UHV studies, on the other hand, offer a more detailed mechanistic insight by examining the surface chemistry of catalysts at the atomic level. In conclusion, the synergy between reactor studies and UHV studies provides a comprehensive understanding of selective dehydrogenation reactions, enabling the development of advanced catalysts that enhance chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage capabilities.
