Analyzing selective dehydrogenation reactions is significant for chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage. These reactions involve the breaking of C-H and N-H bonds, which are critical steps in various industrial processes. Studies utilizing reactor and UHV techniques have contributed greatly to our understanding of these processes. Key research methods and characterization techniques, such as density functional theory, microkinetic modeling, and experiments under ultra-high vacuum conditions, have been employed. Reaction conditions, including temperature, pressure, and catalyst properties, are typically investigated. These studies help in optimizing catalytic processes, leading to more efficient and environmentally friendly methods in these industries.
