<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The significance of selective dehydrogenation reactions in chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage is analyzed through a combination of computational and experimental methods. Density functional theory and microkinetic modeling are used for theoretical predictions, while experiments are conducted under ultra-high vacuum conditions on supported catalysts. The results highlight the importance of understanding the stability of catalysts and the conditions that optimize their performance in selective dehydrogenation reactions. These studies inform the optimization of catalytic processes in this field.
