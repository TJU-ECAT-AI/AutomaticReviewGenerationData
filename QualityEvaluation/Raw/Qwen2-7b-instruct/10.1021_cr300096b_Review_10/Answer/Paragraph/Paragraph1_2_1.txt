        The significance, applications, and research methodologies of selective dehydrogenation reactions in chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage, with emphasis on C-H and N-H bond dehydrogenation over supported catalysts, are thoroughly analyzed through reactor and Ultra-High Vacuum (UHV) studies. This review delves into the intricate relationship between chemical transformations and catalytic processes, highlighting how these reactions facilitate the conversion of hydrocarbons and other hydrophilic compounds into valuable fuels and chemicals, significantly impacting the energy sector.

        Reactor studies, which involve the examination of reaction kinetics, product yields, and selectivities under varied operational conditions, such as temperature, pressure, and feedstock composition, provide insights into the effectiveness of different catalysts and their ability to optimize desired reactions while minimizing unwanted side reactions. UHV studies, on the other hand, offer a more microscopic perspective by characterizing the surface properties of catalysts, including the coordination environment around metal centers, bond strengths, and adsorbate coverage. Techniques such as X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS) and extended X-ray absorption fine structure (EXAFS) spectroscopy are instrumental in revealing the electronic states and local structures of catalysts, providing critical information on active sites and their dynamics during the dehydrogenation process.

        Characterization techniques like temperature-programmed reduction (TPR) and adsorption microcalorimetry are employed to analyze catalyst behavior, while careful control of reaction conditions optimizes catalytic processes. These studies provide valuable insights into enhancing catalyst performance and selectivity, contributing to the development of more efficient and sustainable processes for chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage.

        Research on the nature of PtSn systems demonstrates the complexity involved in understanding the interactions between platinum and tin, particularly how tin modifies selectivity by inhibiting cracking reactions. The electronic environment of platinum atoms changes upon the presence of tin, and the EXAFS profiles of PtSn samples exhibit differences, suggesting the formation of a PtSn alloy. The coexistence of an unalloyed metallic Pt phase can be confirmed by analyzing the quantity of Pt and Sn atoms in the catalysts.

        Studies on bimetallic NiPt nanocatalysts for the selective decomposition of hydrazine in aqueous solutions highlight the importance of these catalysts in chemical hydrogen storage. Key research methods involve reactor studies and UHV studies, with characterization techniques including X-ray diffraction (XRD), transmission electron microscopy (TEM), and energy-dispersive X-ray spectroscopy (EDS). Reaction conditions are typically investigated for temperature, pressure, and solvent presence, informing the optimization of catalytic processes for enhanced efficiency and selectivity.

        The addition of rhenium to catalysts leads to greater stability and improved selectivity, although its role in platinum-rhenium catalysts remains a subject of debate. Thermogravimetric analysis indicates possible reduction mechanisms that form bimetallic PtRe particles. The catalyst properties are often attributed to PtRe alloy formation and its interaction with sulfur, with direct evidence of alloy formation through physical characterization techniques being challenging due to low metal loading and dispersion.

        Bimetallic interaction in PtRe catalysts is highly dependent on pretreatment conditions, with the highest degree of interaction observed when the catalyst is not dehydrated before reduction. High-temperature N2 drying before reduction causes sintering of platinum particles and reduces bimetallic interaction.

        Aging tests with sulfur-free ethanol-gasoline blends under varying space velocities and catalyst loadings reveal that under high space velocity conditions, the catalyst is unstable regardless of sulfur presence. The addition of 5 ppm sulfur leads to significant deactivation after 22 hours, making hydrogen production industrially unfeasible. Air treatment can regenerate the catalyst to initial activity, but deactivation occurs more quickly following regeneration. A preemptive regeneration process extends the stable activity period for sulfur-containing fuels.

        XPS studies indicate a shift in precious metal oxidation state when the catalyst has been on stream continuously versus with periodic air treatments. This research underscores the critical role of catalyst studies, particularly those employing UHV techniques, in advancing our understanding of selective dehydrogenation reactions and catalytic processes. It also emphasizes the importance of optimizing catalyst performance and selectivity for various applications, contributing to the development of more efficient and environmentally friendly processes in chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage.

There is an opportunity to delve deeper into the mechanisms of dehydrogenation reactions, assess the efficacy of different catalysts and conditions, and tailor processes for specific applications, fostering the development of sustainable and efficient technologies in the energy sector.

        By pursuing these avenues, researchers can push the boundaries of catalytic technology, leading to breakthroughs in chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage, ultimately contributing to a more sustainable and energy-efficient future.

        选择性脱氢反应在化学生产、燃料合成和氢存储中的重要性和应用，特别是在支持催化剂上进行的C-H和N-H键脱氢，通过反应器和超高真空（UHV）研究进行了深入分析。本综述探讨了化学转换和催化过程之间的复杂关系，强调了这些反应如何使碳水化合物和其他亲水化合物转化为有价值的燃料和化学品，显著影响能源行业。

        反应器研究涉及对不同操作条件（如温度、压力和原料组成）下反应动力学、产物产率和选择性的考察，为不同催化剂的有效性提供了见解，并指导优化所需反应同时最小化副反应。另一方面，UHV研究提供了一个更微观的视角，通过表征催化剂的表面性质，包括金属中心的配位环境、键强度和吸附剂覆盖度，来揭示催化剂的电子状态和局部结构。X射线光电子能谱（XPS）和扩展X射线吸收精细结构（EXAFS）光谱技术是揭示活性位点及其在脱氢过程中动态变化的关键信息的工具。

        表征技术如温度程序还原（TPR）和吸附微热量计法被用来分析催化剂行为，而仔细控制反应条件优化催化过程。这些研究提供了提高催化剂性能和选择性的宝贵见解，促进了化学生产、燃料合成和氢存储领域的更高效和可持续工艺的发展。

        对PtSn系统的性质研究表明，铂和锡之间的作用非常复杂，仍然存在争议。锡显著改变了铂原子的电子环境，并且PtSn样品的EXAFS轮廓显示差异，这表明合金的形成。可以确认无合金化的纯铂相的存在，通过分析Pt和Sn原子的数量在PtSn催化剂中。

        对NiPt纳米催化剂的研究表明，这些催化剂对于在室温下将肼在水中分解为氢气至关重要，这是化学氢存储的关键。关键的研究方法包括反应器研究和UHV研究，以及X射线衍射（XRD）、透射电子显微镜（TEM）和能量色散X射线光谱（EDS）等表征技术的应用。通常调查的反应条件包括温度、压力和溶剂的存在，这些信息有助于优化催化过程以提高效率和选择性。

        添加铼到催化剂中可增强其稳定性和选择性，尽管它在铂铼催化剂中的作用仍存在争议。热重分析表明可能的还原机制形成了铂铼（PtRe）金属颗粒。催化剂的性质通常归因于铂铼合金的形成及其与硫的相互作用，直接通过物理表征技术（如红外和XPS）获得合金形成的证据具有挑战性，因为铂铼重整催化剂中的金属负载量低且分散。

        PtRe催化剂中二金属相互作用的高度依赖于预处理条件，没有脱水前进行还原时，观察到最高程度的PtRe相互作用。在高温度氮气干燥前进行还原会导致铂粒子烧结并降低二金属相互作用。

        在硫自由乙醇/汽油混合物的加速老化测试中，在不同的空间速度和催化剂装载条件下，发现即使在高空间速度条件下，催化剂在有或无硫的情况下也不稳定。添加5 ppm硫导致22小时后测量的失活，使得工业氢生产的可行性无法实现。空气处理可以再生催化剂至初始活性，但再生后失活速度更快。预先进行再生的过程延长了含硫燃料的稳定活性期。

        XPS研究表明，当催化剂连续运行时与周期性空气处理相比，会发生贵金属氧化态的变化。这项研究强调了催化剂研究，特别是那些使用UHV技术的研究，对理解和优化选择性脱氢反应及催化过程的重要性。它还强调了针对特定应用优化催化剂性能和选择性的必要性，从而推动化学生产、燃料合成和氢存储领域的技术创新。

        展望未来，未来的研究努力可能集中在探索新兴领域和创新方法上。未来研究可以集中在：
        
        - 研究表现增强稳定性、选择性的新型催化剂组成和材料。
        - 开发优化热和质量传递的先进反应器设计，提高过程效率。
        - 扩展UHV研究，实时探查表面化学和动力学，提供动态催化过程的见解。
        - 加强计算建模能力，准确预测催化剂行为和反应途径。
        - 关注催化过程的环境影响和可持续性方面，寻求更加绿色和环保的解决方案。
        - 集成机器学习和人工智能技术，自动化过程优化和催化系统预测维护。
        - 探索将可再生能源与催化过程结合，生产可持续的燃料和化学品。
        - 解决实验室规模发现有效放大到工业规模操作的挑战。

        通过追求这些途径，研究人员可以在化学生产、燃料合成和氢存储领域推动技术突破，最终为更加可持续和高效的未来做出贡献。
