        Selective dehydrogenation reactions play a pivotal role in chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage. Catalyst studies, particularly those employing ultra-high vacuum (UHV) techniques, significantly enhance our understanding of these processes. Research often involves examining the dehydrogenation of C-H and N-H bonds over supported catalysts. Techniques such as temperature-programmed reduction (TPR) and adsorption microcalorimetry are utilized to analyze catalyst behavior. Reaction conditions are carefully controlled to optimize catalytic processes, leading to insights into catalyst performance and selectivity improvements. UHV studies enable detailed analysis of surface reactions, contributing to catalyst design and improvement. Understanding the mechanisms of dehydrogenation reactions is crucial for optimizing catalytic processes, and by evaluating the effectiveness of different catalysts and conditions, researchers can tailor processes for specific applications.

        An accurate nature of PtSn systems is very complicated and still a matter of debate. The addition of tin notably modifies this selectivity by inhibiting cracking reactions. The presence of tin changes the electronic environment of platinum atoms, affecting the reactivity of the catalyst. EXAFS profiles of PtSn samples exhibit differences, indicating the complexity of the system. The coexistence of an unalloyed metallic Pt phase can be confirmed considering the quantity of Pt and Sn atoms in the catalyst. There is a diminution in the reaction rate as the Sn(0)/Pt ratio increases. Catalytic, XPS, and EXAFS results show that bimetallic PtSn catalysts can undergo sequential reaction-regeneration cycles while maintaining initial activity levels and showing minimal changes in the catalytic surface.

        Bimetallic NiPt nanocatalysts are effective for selective decomposition of hydrazine in aqueous solution at room temperature for chemical hydrogen storage. These catalysts demonstrate selective dehydrogenation reactions over supported catalysts. Key research methods involve reactor studies and UHV studies. Characterization techniques include X-ray diffraction (XRD), transmission electron microscopy (TEM), and energy-dispersive X-ray spectroscopy (EDS). Reaction conditions typically investigated include temperature, pressure, and the presence of a solvent. These studies inform the optimization of catalytic processes in chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage.

        It has been demonstrated that the addition of rhenium leads to a catalyst with greater stability and improved selectivity. However, the structure and exact role of rhenium in platinum-rhenium catalysts remain controversial. Rhenium enhances the hydrogenolysis activity over bimetallic catalysts compared to monometallic counterparts and physical mixtures. TPR studies suggest possible reduction mechanisms leading to the formation of bimetallic PtRe particles. The catalyst's properties are often attributed to PtRe alloy formation and its interaction with sulfur. Bimetallic interaction in PtRe catalysts depends significantly on pretreatment conditions, with the highest degree of interaction observed when the catalyst is not dehydrated prior to reduction.

        Aging tests with sulfur-free E85 under various conditions show that under high space velocity, the catalyst is unstable regardless of sulfur presence. The addition of 5 ppm sulfur leads to significant deactivation after 22 hours, making hydrogen production industrially unfeasible. Air treatment can regenerate the catalyst to initial activity, but deactivation occurs faster following regeneration. A preemptive regeneration process extends the stable activity period for sulfur-containing fuels. XPS studies reveal a shift in precious metal oxidation state during continuous operation versus periodic air treatments.

        Reactor studies and UHV studies provide insights into optimizing catalytic processes in chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage.         选择性脱氢反应在化学生产、燃料合成和氢存储中起着关键作用。超真空(UHV)技术下的催化剂研究显著增强了我们对这些过程的理解。研究通常涉及检查支持催化剂上的C-H和N-H键的脱氢。温度程序还原(TPR)和吸附微热量计等技术被用来分析催化剂的行为。通过仔细控制反应条件优化催化过程，可以提供有关催化剂性能和选择性的见解。UHV研究允许对表面反应进行详细分析，促进催化剂设计和改进。理解脱氢反应机制对于优化催化过程至关重要，通过评估不同催化剂和条件的有效性，研究人员可以根据特定应用定制流程。

        PtSn系统的准确性质非常复杂，仍然是争论的焦点。锡的添加明显地改变了这种选择性，抑制了裂解反应。锡的存在改变了铂原子的电子环境，影响了催化剂的活性。PtSn样品的EXAFS轮廓显示差异，表明系统复杂性。可以确认存在未合金化的纯铂相，考虑到Pt和Sn原子的数量。随着Sn(0)/Pt比的增加，反应速率减少。催化、XPS和EXAFS结果表明，双金属PtSn催化剂可以进行一系列的反应再生周期，同时保持初始活动水平，并且催化表面几乎没有变化。

        NiPt纳米双金属催化剂有效用于室温下水溶液中过氧化氢的可控分解，用于化学氢存储。这些催化剂在支持催化剂上表现出选择性脱氢反应。关键的研究方法包括反应器研究和UHV研究。表征技术包括X射线衍射(XRD)、透射电子显微镜(TEM)和能量色散X射线光谱(EDS)。通常研究的反应条件包括温度、压力和溶剂的存在。这些研究为化学生产、燃料合成和氢存储中的催化过程的优化提供了信息。

        已经证明，添加铼可提高催化剂的稳定性和选择性。然而，铼在铂铼催化剂中的结构和确切作用仍然存在争议。铼提高了双金属催化剂相对于单金属和物理混合物的氢解活性。TPR研究表明，可能的还原机制导致形成双金属PtRe颗粒。催化剂的性质通常归因于PtRe合金形成及其与硫的相互作用。PtRe催化剂的双金属相互作用高度依赖于预处理条件，在催化剂未干燥之前进行还原时，可以获得最高的PtRe相互作用。

        进行了硫自由E85的衰老测试，在不同的条件下进行。在高空间速度下，催化剂在有或没有硫的情况下不稳定，无论速度如何。添加5 ppm硫导致22小时后测量的失活，使得工业氢生产的可行性降低。空气处理可以将催化剂恢复到初始活性，但失活在再生后发生得更快。预处理再生过程延长了含硫燃料的稳定活动期。XPS研究表明，在连续操作与周期性空气处理之间，贵金属氧化状态发生了转变。

        反应器研究和UHV研究为优化化学生产、燃料合成和氢存储中的催化过程提供了见解。这些研究帮助我们了解脱氢反应机制，并改进催化剂设计，以高效地将碳氢化合物和其他亲水化合物转化为有价值的燃料和化学品，对能源领域做出了重大贡献。
