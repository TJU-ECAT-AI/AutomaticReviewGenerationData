<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The growing demand for propylene has driven the development of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as an alternative production method. PDH directly converts propane into propylene, offering an efficient alternative to traditional cracking processes. The use of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts has been a significant advancement in PDH technology, improving selectivity and yield. The debate around the active species responsible for the catalytic activity of these catalysts has led to a better understanding of the role of Ga3+ ions and their interactions within the zeolite framework. Recent research has contributed to improvements in catalyst design, enhancing performance and efficiency in PDH processes.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
