1. The increased availability of natural gas has shifted the feedstocks used for ethylene production in the U.S., impacting propylene and butadiene markets.
2. Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) processes are gaining attention due to abundant propane supplies from shale gas.
3. PDH processes often utilize Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for propane-to-propylene conversion.
4. Recent research has focused on elucidating the active gallium species on Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts involved in propylene formation.
5. Studies propose that both Ga(III) and Ga(V) species contribute to propylene production, but the precise mechanism is under debate.
6. Advancements in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalyst preparation have improved activity and selectivity towards propylene.
7. Research continues to explore the nature of active gallium species and optimize their activity on the catalyst surface.
