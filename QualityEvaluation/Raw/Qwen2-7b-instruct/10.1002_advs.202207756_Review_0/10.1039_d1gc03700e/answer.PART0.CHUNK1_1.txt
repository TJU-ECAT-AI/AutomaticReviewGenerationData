<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Coordinatively unsaturated metal cations with neighboring oxygen vacancies serve as adsorption sites for light alkanes, where surface oxygen sites abstract hydrogen from the adsorbed alkane.
2. ZrO2 exhibits activity in its own right for direct dehydrogenation of propane, highlighting the significance of crystallite size and phase composition in influencing activity.
3. The early work emphasized the role of the VO bond in creating effective oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODH-O2) catalysts.
4. Isolated vanadium species are more selective than polymeric species featuring VOV bridging bonds.
5. Despite the large surface area of SiO2, the low reactivity of silanol groups towards vanadium precursors results in a relatively low loading of isolated vanadium sites.
6. In situ infrared analysis revealed that the presence of OH groups facilitates the interaction of molecular oxygen with the surface.
7. The presence of BOx species has been identified as essential for catalytic activity in certain systems.
8. The as-prepared boron nitride catalysts already contained a substantial proportion of hydroxyl groups, underscoring the importance of hydroxylated boron in catalyzing oxidative dehydrogenation of propane.
9. Activation of oxygen is recognized as the rate-limiting step for boron-based catalysts in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane.
