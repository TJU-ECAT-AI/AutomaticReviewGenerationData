The increasing demand for propylene has driven the development of new production techniques, with a particular emphasis on propane dehydrogenation (PDH). This process has become a focal point for research due to its potential for efficient propylene production. The understanding of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH has advanced significantly, with an ongoing discussion around the active gallium species playing a crucial role in the catalytic activity. Recent studies have contributed to our current knowledge by elucidating the specific functions of gallium species within these catalysts.
