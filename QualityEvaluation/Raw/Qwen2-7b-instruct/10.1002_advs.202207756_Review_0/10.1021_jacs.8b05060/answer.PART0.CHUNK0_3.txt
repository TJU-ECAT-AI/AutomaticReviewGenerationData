<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The growing demand for propylene has driven the development of new production methods, particularly propane dehydrogenation (PDH). This has sparked interest in exploring non-noble-metal catalysts, such as Ga/H-ZSM-5, for their cost-effectiveness and sustainability. The debate over the active Ga species on these catalysts continues, with studies aiming to clarify their role in the dehydrogenation process. Recent research has provided valuable insights into the nature and activity of Ga species on these catalysts.
