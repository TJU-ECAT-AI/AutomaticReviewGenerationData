                    <English>Driven by the escalating demand for propylene, the petrochemical industry has embraced new production methods, notably propane dehydrogenation (PDH), aiming to replace costly platinum-based catalysts and mitigate environmental issues associated with chromium. PDH encompasses non-oxidative and oxidative processes, with the Catofin and Oleflex methods employing distinct catalysts: CrOx/Al2O3 and Pt-based, respectively. The development of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, with their ongoing scientific debate surrounding the active Ga species, has significantly advanced propylene production efficiency and selectivity. Research on the tandem and cooperative catalysis models suggests that these could revolutionize chemical production by optimizing reaction networks and driving transformations forward. The focus on material structure and reaction performance at the molecular level highlights the importance of catalysts in PDH processes. Recent studies, including the utilization of encapsulated metal promoters and mesoporous materials, aim to address future challenges in designing nanocatalysts with well-defined active sites and employing in situ/operando techniques. This review underscores the dynamic shift in propylene production technology, emphasizing the complex and innovative nature of PDH and the Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalyst system. </English>
