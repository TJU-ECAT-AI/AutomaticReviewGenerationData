                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolving landscape of propylene production has seen a significant shift towards more efficient and selective methods, notably propane dehydrogenation (PDH). The development and understanding of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH have been pivotal, particularly in the elucidation of active gallium species and their role in enhancing catalytic activity and selectivity. Recent research findings have provided valuable insights into the structure and function of these catalysts, contributing to the ongoing debate on the active Ga species. Looking ahead, there is an opportunity to explore the integration of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts with other innovative methodologies, such as the use of Ga-based catalysts in tandem with noble metals or the incorporation of Ga into more sustainable support materials. This could lead to the development of even more efficient PDH processes, capable of meeting the growing demand for propylene while minimizing environmental impact.
