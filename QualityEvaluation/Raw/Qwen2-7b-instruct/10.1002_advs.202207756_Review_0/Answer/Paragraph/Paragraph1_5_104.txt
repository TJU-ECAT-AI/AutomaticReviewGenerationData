            Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The growing demand for propylene has fundamentally reshaped the landscape of petrochemical production, driving the development of more efficient and environmentally friendly methods. Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) stands as a pivotal technique in this shift, with the understanding of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH undergoing significant evolution. This evolution includes the ongoing debate around the nature and role of active gallium species, which has been illuminated by recent research findings. These studies have not only advanced our knowledge of catalyst structure and function but also opened avenues for the design of more efficient PDH processes. Looking ahead, there is a compelling opportunity to explore the integration of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts with other emerging technologies, such as those utilizing metal-free materials or novel metal combinations. The potential for synergies between these catalysts and next-generation PDH methods holds promise for further enhancing propylene production efficiency and sustainability.
