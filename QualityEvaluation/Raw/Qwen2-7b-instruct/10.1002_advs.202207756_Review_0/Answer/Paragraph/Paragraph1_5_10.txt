    <English>
        <p>Driven by the escalating demand for propylene, the petrochemical industry has embraced new production methods, notably propane dehydrogenation (PDH), which has seen significant advancements in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts. These catalysts have evolved, addressing debates on active Ga species, with recent research findings contributing to our current knowledge. The growing interest in PDH has also highlighted the importance of understanding the nature of active sites on VOx/Al2O3 catalysts and the role of gallium species in enhancing PDH efficiency. This evolution reflects the industry's response to the high cost of platinum and environmental concerns, leading to the development of more sustainable and cost-effective alternatives.</p>
        <p>The Catofin process, employing fixed-bed reactors with CrOx/Al2O3 catalysts, and the Oleflex process, utilizing fluidized bed reactors with Pt-based catalysts, are two principal industrial processes for PDH. The Catofin process boasts a high chromium oxide content and alkaline metal promoters, while Oleflex relies on Pt-based catalysts for its efficiency. This shift towards more sustainable PDH methods underscores the industry's commitment to innovation in propylene production.</p>
        <p>As the demand for propylene continues to rise, new catalysts like those involving group IIIA metal oxides, particularly gallium, have gained attention. These catalysts, when supported on silica, have shown promising results in terms of propane conversion, surpassing previous mixed In2O3Al2O3 and -Ga2O3 catalysts on both a catalyst mass and surface area basis. This research has deepened our understanding of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts and their potential in PDH, offering insights into the mechanisms involved in the process and the role of gallium species.</p>
        <p>The introduction of tandem and cooperative catalysis has opened up new avenues for chemical production, aiming to minimize separations, couple reactions, and control selectivity in reaction networks. While challenges remain in precisely controlling reactive intermediates, these approaches have the potential to revolutionize chemical production, potentially including PDH and oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP).</p>
        <p>Innovative strategies, such as modifying catalyst supports and introducing additives, have been proposed to strengthen the catalytic performance and extend the robust stability of Pt/Cr-based catalysts, which suffer from fast deactivation and inferior stability due to active species sintering and coke deposition. This comprehensive review provides a brief overview of the development of PDH and PDH catalysts, systematically summarizes the advanced research on supported noble and non-noble metals, metal-free materials, and material design, and emphasizes advanced synthetic strategies based on novel design of PDH catalysts with improved dehydrogenation activity and stability. Future challenges and directions for PDH catalysts are also provided to facilitate their broader industrial application.</p>
        <p>With the increasing demand for propylene, the petrochemical industry has embarked on a journey of innovation, embracing new production methods like propane dehydrogenation (PDH) and exploring Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts. These advancements have been pivotal in addressing the ongoing debate on active Ga species and have contributed to a better understanding of these catalysts, leading to the development of more efficient PDH processes. The future of PDH catalysts looks promising, with a focus on further enhancing efficiency, selectivity, and sustainability.</p>
        <p>※※※</p>
        <p>Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements and findings discussed in the file, there lies an opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies. Future research endeavors might focus on developing more sustainable catalysts that utilize renewable feedstocks, optimizing the design of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts to achieve higher selectivity and activity, and integrating PDH with other chemical processes to create more efficient, integrated production systems. The integration of machine learning and artificial intelligence could also play a pivotal role in predicting catalyst performance and guiding the development of new catalyst designs. Additionally, the exploration of biocatalysts and their potential synergies with inorganic catalysts could open new avenues for propylene production, leveraging the natural selectivity and efficiency of biological systems.</p>

    <Chinese>
        <p>随着丙烯需求的增长，石油化学工业已经采纳了新的生产方法，尤其是丙烷脱氢（PDH），在Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂方面取得了显著进展。这些催化剂的演变解决了关于活性Ga物种的争论，并且最近的研究结果丰富了我们对这些催化剂的理解。对PDH的兴趣也强调了理解VOx/Al2O3催化剂上活性位点性质以及Ga物种在提高PDH效率中的作用的重要性。这种演变反映了行业应对铂高昂的成本和环境问题的承诺，推动了更可持续和成本效益更高的替代品的发展。</p>
        <p>两种主要的工业PDH过程是采用固定床反应器带有高铬氧化物/Al2O3催化剂的Catofin过程，以及采用流化床反应器带有铂基催化剂的Oleflex过程。Catofin过程具有高铬氧化物含量和碱金属促进剂，而Oleflex依赖于铂基催化剂以实现其效率。这一转向更加可持续的PDH方法体现了行业在丙烯生产方面的创新承诺。</p>
        <p>随着丙烯需求的持续增长，涉及第IIIA族金属氧化物的新催化剂，特别是镓，已经引起了关注。这些催化剂，当支持在硅酸盐上时，在丙烷转化方面显示出有希望的结果，超过了之前混合的In2O3Al2O3和-Ga2O3催化剂在催化剂质量和表面积基础上的表现。这项研究深化了我们对Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂及其在PDH中的潜力的理解，提供了有关过程机制和Ga物种作用的见解。</p>
        <p>串联和协同催化已为化学品生产开辟了新途径，旨在最小化分离、连接反应并控制反应网络中的选择性。尽管在精确控制中间活性化合物方面仍存在挑战，但这些方法有可能彻底改变化学品生产，可能包括PDH和丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）。</p>
        <p>为了增强催化性能并延长Pt/Cr基催化剂的稳定性和耐久性，提出了一些策略，例如修改催化剂支持和引入添加剂，这些催化剂由于活性物种聚集和焦炭沉积导致快速失活和较差稳定性。这篇综述提供了一个PDH和PDH催化剂发展的简要概述，系统地总结了支持的贵金属和非贵金属、无金属材料和材料设计的高级研究，以及基于新型设计的PDH催化剂合成策略，提高了脱氢活性和稳定性。还提供了PDH催化剂未来挑战和方向的概述，以促进它们在工业应用中的进一步发展。</p>
        <p>随着丙烯需求的增长，石油化学工业踏上了一条创新之路，采纳了新的生产方法，如丙烷脱氢（PDH）和探索Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂。这些进步在解决关于活性Ga物种的争论和加深我们对这些催化剂的理解方面发挥了关键作用，导致了更有效PDH过程的发展。PDH催化剂的未来前景令人鼓舞，重点是进一步提高效率、选择性和可持续性。</p>
        <p>※※※</p>
        <p>整合理解和未来展望：考虑到文件中讨论的进步和发现，存在探索新兴领域和创新方法的机会。未来研究努力可能集中在开发利用可再生原料的更可持续的催化剂、优化Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂的设计以实现更高选择性和活性，并将PDH与其他化学过程集成以创建更高效、集成的生产系统。机器学习和人工智能的整合也可能在预测催化剂性能和指导新催化剂设计的开发中发挥核心作用。此外，生物催化剂及其与无机催化剂的潜在协同作用的探索也可能开启丙烯生产的全新途径，利用生物系统的自然选择性和效率。</p>
