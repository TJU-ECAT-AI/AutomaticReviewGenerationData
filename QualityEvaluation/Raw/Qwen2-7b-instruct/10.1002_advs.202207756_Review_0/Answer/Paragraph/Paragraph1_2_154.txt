        The evolving landscape of propylene production: From traditional methods to the emergence of propane dehydrogenation (PDH), with a focus on the development and ongoing scientific debate surrounding Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH, has been meticulously analyzed. The understanding of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH has indeed evolved significantly, particularly in the light of the growing demand for propylene and the subsequent shift towards more efficient production methods. The ongoing debate about the active Ga species in these catalysts reflects the complexity and nuances inherent in catalysis. Recent research findings have contributed to our current knowledge of these catalysts, providing insights into their structure, composition, and activity. This review highlights the pivotal role of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts in PDH processes, underscoring their importance in propylene production and the advancements they represent in meeting the global demand for this critical chemical.

        The future of propylene production holds promise with the continued exploration of new catalysts and processes. The focus on Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts and the active Ga species debate is likely to continue, guiding the development of more efficient and selective catalysts. As propylene demand continues to grow, so too will the need for innovative solutions to ensure sustainable and economically viable production methods. The insights provided in this review serve as a foundation for researchers and engineers looking to advance the field of propylene production, fostering a future where PDH technologies are optimized for efficiency, sustainability, and economic viability.
