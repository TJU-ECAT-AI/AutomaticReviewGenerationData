                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The growing demand for propylene has significantly influenced the development of new production methods, particularly propane dehydrogenation (PDH), as evidenced by the extensive research efforts directed towards improving efficiency, selectivity, and sustainability. The focus on Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH has been pivotal, given their potential for high activity and selectivity. The ongoing scientific debate around the nature of active Ga species has not only challenged existing paradigms but also driven the evolution of these catalysts, leading to significant advancements in understanding their role in the PDH process. Recent research findings have not only contributed to our current knowledge of these catalysts but have also opened avenues for future innovations. Looking ahead, the integration of advanced materials science, computational modeling, and green chemistry principles could lead to the development of more efficient, environmentally friendly, and economically viable PDH processes. Potential future directions might include the optimization of catalyst supports, the design of new Ga-based materials with enhanced stability and activity, and the implementation of integrated systems that combine multiple catalytic steps for enhanced efficiency. Moreover, the development of PDH processes that operate under mild conditions and with lower energy inputs would represent a significant step towards more sustainable propylene production.
