                The evolving landscape of propylene production: From traditional methods to the emergence of propane dehydrogenation (PDH), with a focus on the development and ongoing scientific debate surrounding Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, has significantly impacted the chemical industry's efficiency and sustainability. The growing demand for propylene has driven the development of new production methods, particularly PDH, which offers a cleaner alternative to conventional processes. Understanding of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH has evolved over time, particularly regarding the nature of active gallium (Ga) species, which has been a central topic in recent research. This evolution has enhanced propylene production efficiency and selectivity, making Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts pivotal in PDH processes. Tandem and cooperative catalysis, promising improvements in chemical production, tackle challenges in controlling reactive intermediates. As a result, PDH provides a sustainable path for propylene production from propane, offering a cleaner alternative to traditional methods. The ongoing debate about active Ga species in these catalysts highlights the complexity of catalyst function, with recent studies contributing to our understanding of these catalysts' role in PDH. This has led to significant advancements in catalyst design and tuning, particularly in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH. A particular focus has been on promoters like Cu, Fe, Mn, Co, Sn, Ga, In, Zn, and supports like Al2O3, SiO2, and zeolites, which regulate the shape of nanostructured particles and metals-support interaction. Research, including the utilization of encapsulated metal promoters and mesoporous materials, aims to address future challenges in designing nanocatalysts with well-defined active sites and employing in situ/operando techniques. The text emphasizes the importance of rational design for nanocatalysts and the ongoing exploration of cost-effective transition metals in PDH, underscoring the dynamic shift in propylene production methods.

            <p>
                石油气制丙烯生产方式的发展演变：从传统方法到丙烷脱氢（PDH）的出现，特别是在Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂发展及其活性镓（Ga）物种的持续科学辩论方面，对化学工业的效率和可持续性产生了重大影响。随着对丙烯需求的增长，新的生产方法得到了开发，尤其是PDH，它提供了一种比传统过程更清洁的丙烯生产替代方案。对于Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂在PDH中的理解随着时间的推移而演变，特别是关于活性镓物种的性质，这是最近研究的重点。这一演变提高了丙烯生产效率和选择性，使Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂成为PDH过程中的关键因素。组合催化和协同催化，有望改进化学生产，解决控制反应中间体的挑战。因此，PDH为从丙烷生产丙烯提供了一条可持续途径，提供了一种比传统方法更清洁的替代方案。关于这些催化剂中活性Ga物种的持续辩论凸显了催化剂功能的复杂性，最近的研究贡献了我们对这些催化剂在PDH中作用的理解。这导致了催化剂设计和调谐的重大进展，特别是在Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂用于PDH时。特别关注的是铜（Cu）、铁（Fe）、锰（Mn）、钴（Co）、锡（Sn）、镓（Ga）、铟（In）、锌（Zn）等促进剂和支持物如Al2O3、SiO2和沸石，它们调节纳米结构颗粒和金属-支持相互作用的形状。研究包括封装金属促进剂和使用介孔材料，旨在解决设计具有明确活性位点的纳米催化剂和应用原位/操作技术的未来挑战。文本强调了纳米催化剂的理性设计的重要性以及PDH中成本效益良好的过渡金属的持续探索，突出了丙烯生产方式的动态转变。
