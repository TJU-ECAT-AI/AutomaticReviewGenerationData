The growing demand for propylene has driven the development of more efficient and selective processes, particularly propane dehydrogenation (PDH). The understanding of gallium (Ga) in H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH has evolved through ongoing research aimed at optimizing the selectivity and activity of these catalysts. The debate about the active Ga species and recent research findings have contributed to our current knowledge of these catalysts, highlighting the significance of specific configurations and interactions of Ga atoms within the zeolite framework for enhancing PDH performance.
