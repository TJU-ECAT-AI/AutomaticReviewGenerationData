<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The growing demand for propylene has influenced the development of new production methods, particularly propane dehydrogenation (PDH), as an efficient way to produce propylene. In PDH, Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts have been studied, with ongoing debates about the active Ga species involved. Recent research suggests that isolated Ga2+ species and Ga3+ species play crucial roles in the reaction mechanism. Studies on the redox properties of Ga species have revealed the formation of radical intermediates, contributing to a better understanding of the PDH reaction on Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts. The incorporation of sulfate groups has been shown to enhance catalyst activity by stabilizing Ga species and improving the acidity of the catalyst.

</EnglishVersionAnswer>
