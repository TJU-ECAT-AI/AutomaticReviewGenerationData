<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1. Light olefins, such as propylene and ethylene, are crucial components in the petrochemical industry, serving as intermediates for the production of various products including plastics, synthetic fibers, solvents, and lubricants. Their synthesis relies on methods like steam cracking, catalytic reforming, and alkylation, which demand meticulous control over temperature and pressure to maximize yield and selectivity.

2. Temperature significantly affects these synthesis processes. Increased temperatures typically speed up reaction rates, but they can also boost the formation of unwanted non-olefinic byproducts. Pressure impacts the vapor-liquid equilibrium, influencing the distribution of the produced products.

3. The length of the alkane chain impacts the selectivity of the process. Longer chains often necessitate harsher conditions for effective breakdown, affecting the overall energy usage and economic viability of the process.
