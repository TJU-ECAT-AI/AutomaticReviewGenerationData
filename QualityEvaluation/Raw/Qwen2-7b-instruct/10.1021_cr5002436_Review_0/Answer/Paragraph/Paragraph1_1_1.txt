            <English>Light olefins, such as propylene and ethylene, are pivotal to the chemical industry due to their extensive applications and growing market trends. These hydrocarbons are fundamental in the production of polyolefins, including polyethylene and polypropylene, as well as in the creation of styrene, ethylene dichloride, acrylonitrile, and isopropanol. Key production methods include steam cracking, catalytic cracking, and dehydrogenation. Thermodynamic challenges, notably concerning temperature, pressure, and alkane chain length effects, are inherent in these processes. Olefin/paraffin separation, a cornerstone of the petrochemical industry, confronts difficulties due to the high energy demands of traditional methods such as cryogenic distillation. The implementation of membrane separation processes offers an energy-efficient alternative. The development of polymer-inorganic nano composite materials aims to enhance the physical properties of polymer membranes, concurrently improving selectivity and permeability. This innovation has the potential to significantly augment the utilization of membrane technology in the petrochemical industry, addressing the limitations posed by insufficient gas flux in conventional separation methods.</English>
