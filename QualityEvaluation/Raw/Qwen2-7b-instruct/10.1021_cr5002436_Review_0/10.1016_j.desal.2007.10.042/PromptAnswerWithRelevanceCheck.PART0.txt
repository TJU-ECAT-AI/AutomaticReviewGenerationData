<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Light olefins, including propylene and ethylene, play a crucial role in the chemical industry due to their extensive applications and market trends. They are key components in the synthesis of polyolefins, which are widely used in various sectors such as packaging, construction, automotive, and electronics. Ethylene is also essential for the production of polyethylene, while propylene is vital for polypropylene and other derivatives. Market trends show a steady demand for these olefins, driven by the growth in global population, urbanization, and the rise in consumer spending on goods requiring packaging and disposable products.

2. The key production methods for olefins involve steam cracking, catalytic cracking, and dehydrogenation of paraffins. Steam cracking converts heavier hydrocarbons into lighter olefins under high temperatures and pressures. Catalytic cracking involves breaking down larger hydrocarbons into smaller olefins using catalysts. Dehydrogenation transforms paraffins into olefins, typically requiring high temperatures and sometimes hydrogen as a co-feedstock.

3. Thermodynamic challenges associated with olefin synthesis include controlling temperature, pressure, and alkane chain length to optimize yield and selectivity. Higher temperatures increase the reaction rates but may lead to side reactions, while lower temperatures reduce yields. Pressure plays a critical role in maintaining the optimal reaction conditions, especially in steam cracking processes. The chain length of alkane feedstocks significantly affects the composition of olefins produced, necessitating precise control to achieve desired product profiles.

4. Membrane separation offers a promising alternative to traditional distillation methods for olefin/paraffin separation due to its energy efficiency, simplicity, and low environmental impact. Polymer-inorganic nano composite membranes are particularly attractive because they enhance both permeability and selectivity, addressing the trade-off between these properties. Research continues to develop materials with improved physical properties and higher separation efficiencies for olefin/paraffin separation.

5. The use of polymeric membranes for olefin/paraffin separation has been extensively explored by various researchers. These studies aim to optimize membrane design, select appropriate polymer materials, and evaluate the performance of membranes in separating olefins from paraffins under different operating conditions.

6. Polymer-inorganic nano composite materials have emerged as a solution to the limitations of traditional polymeric membranes in gas separation. These materials combine the flexibility and adaptability of polymers with the high surface area and stability of inorganic nanoparticles, leading to enhanced permeability and selectivity in membrane gas separations.
