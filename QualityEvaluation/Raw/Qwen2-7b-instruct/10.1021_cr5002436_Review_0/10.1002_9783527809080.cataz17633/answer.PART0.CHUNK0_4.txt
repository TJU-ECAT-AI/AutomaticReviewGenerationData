<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Light olefins, such as propylene and ethylene, play a crucial role in the chemical industry due to their extensive applications and market trends. They are widely used in the production of plastics, solvents, and various other chemical products. Their demand has been growing steadily over the years, driven by advancements in technology and increasing consumer needs.
2. The synthesis of light olefins involves several key production methods, including steam cracking, catalytic reforming, and gas-to-liquids (GTL) processes. Thermodynamic challenges associated with their synthesis are mainly related to temperature, pressure, and alkane chain length effects. Optimal conditions for these processes require careful control of parameters to ensure efficient conversion rates and product yields. For instance, high temperatures promote cracking reactions, while moderate pressures are beneficial for maintaining stability and selectivity in the desired olefin products.
