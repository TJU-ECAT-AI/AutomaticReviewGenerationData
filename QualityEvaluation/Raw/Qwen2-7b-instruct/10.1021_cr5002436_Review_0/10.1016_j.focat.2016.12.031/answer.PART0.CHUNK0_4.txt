<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. SABIC is constructing a pilot facility in Sittard-Geleen, Netherlands, for producing polypropylene along with catalysts, copolymers, and homopolymers by late March 2017.

2. Global Bioenergies has finished building a demonstration facility in Leuna, Germany, specialized in producing high-purity isobutene from renewable resources.

3. Jacobs Engineering is conducting a feasibility study for Borealis to establish a new propane dehydrogenation (PDH) unit at Borealis' existing production site in Kallo, Belgium.

4. SBE Chemical Partners, a joint venture between Stonebridge Energy Partners and Evonik Industries, plans to build a butene-1 plant in Baytown, Texas, USA, with a capacity of 100,000 tons per year, scheduled to open in 2018.

5. Haldor Topsoe will provide technology and engineering services for a new ammonia plant with a production capacity of 250 tons per day, being built by Grannus LLC in Kern County, California, USA.

6. Shchekinoazot and Haldor Topsoe have agreed on executing engineering projects.
