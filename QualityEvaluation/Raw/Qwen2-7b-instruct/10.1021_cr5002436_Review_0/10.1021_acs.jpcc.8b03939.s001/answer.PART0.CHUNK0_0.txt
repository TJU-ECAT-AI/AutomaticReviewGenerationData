The light olefins, such as ethylene and propylene, are vital components in the chemical industry because of their wide range of uses and high market demand. Their production often necessitates intricate thermodynamic operations, which must be meticulously managed with respect to temperature and pressure levels. These factors, particularly temperature and pressure, profoundly affect the efficiency and product purity in olefin synthesis, especially concerning the length of the alkane chains.
