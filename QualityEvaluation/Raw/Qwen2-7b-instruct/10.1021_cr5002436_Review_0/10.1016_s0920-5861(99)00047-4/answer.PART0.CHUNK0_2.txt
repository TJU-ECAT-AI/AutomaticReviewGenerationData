<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Light olefins, such as propylene and ethylene, are crucial in the chemical industry due to their applications in polymer synthesis, as well as in the production of various chemical intermediates. Their market trends are closely tied to global demand for plastics, packaging materials, and fuel additives. The synthesis of these olefins involves endothermic alkane dehydrogenation reactions, which are temperature-sensitive and require high temperatures and low pressures to achieve significant yields of alkenes. Longer alkane chains tend to be dehydrogenated at lower temperatures compared to lighter alkanes, illustrating the thermodynamic constraints of these processes.

2. The production of propene and isobutene through alkane dehydrogenation has significant industrial impact. The CATO-FIN1 process, for instance, is a prominent method for producing these olefins globally, with capacities reaching up to 600,000 and 2,600,000 metric tons per year for propene and isobutene, respectively. The increasing demand for isobutene in the synthesis of MTBE, a gasoline additive, indicates the growing importance of this process.

3. During the 1970s to 1980s, the focus was on catalytic dehydrogenation of n-butane to produce butene and butadiene, which served as precursors for synthetic rubber manufacturing. The transition towards the dehydrogenation of propane and isobutane reflects changes in market demands and technological advancements in the chemical industry.

4. It is estimated that 25% of the global MTBE production stems from the catalytic dehydrogenation of isobutane. This number is anticipated to rise in the future, given the constraints on traditional sources of isobutene, such as steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC), and the growing demand for isobutene in the MTBE market.

5. Alkane dehydrogenation processes are characterized by their endothermic nature, requiring elevated temperatures and low pressures to efficiently convert alkanes into alkenes. The selectivity and activity of the catalysts play a pivotal role in achieving desired product yields and minimizing thermal cracking reactions.

5. 脱氢烷烃过程具有吸热特性，需要高温度和低压力来有效地将烷烃转化为烯烃。催化剂的活性和选择性在实现所需产品产率并减少热裂解反应方面起着关键作用。
