<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Light olefins, including propylene and ethylene, are essential raw materials in the chemical industry.
2. They are primarily utilized in the manufacturing of plastics, synthetic rubber, and solvents.
3. The worldwide market for light olefins is anticipated to expand due to rising demand for plastic goods.
4. Manufacturing techniques include steam cracking of hydrocarbons and the oxyalkylation of alkanes.
5. Temperature and pressure have significant impacts on the production of light olefins.
6. Longer alkane chains necessitate higher temperatures and pressures for cracking.
7. Nonetheless, high temperatures can result in thermal breakdown and the creation of unwanted byproducts.
8. Pressure is critical in regulating the yield and purity of olefins.
9. Enhancing catalyst effectiveness can improve the selectivity of desired light olefins.
10. Ongoing research aims to develop more moderate conditions and selective processes.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
