<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Light olefins, including propylene and ethylene, are essential raw materials in the chemical industry because of their broad applications, such as plastics, solvents, and synthetic fibers. They are also utilized in pharmaceutical production and other chemical industries.
   
2. The primary methods for producing light olefins involve steam cracking of naphtha and other hydrocarbons, along with catalytic processes like the Ziegler-Natta process and metathesis reactions.
   
3. Temperature significantly impacts olefin production, necessitating high temperatures to break down long carbon chains into shorter olefins. The optimal temperature varies depending on the process but usually falls within the range of 700°C to 900°C.
   
4. Pressure plays a crucial role as well, as increased pressure enhances reaction rates and improves olefin yields, although it requires additional energy to maintain, influencing the balance between efficiency and cost.
   
5. The length of the alkane chain influences the selectivity and purity of the resulting olefins. Longer alkanes demand higher temperatures and pressures to efficiently produce the desired olefins.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
