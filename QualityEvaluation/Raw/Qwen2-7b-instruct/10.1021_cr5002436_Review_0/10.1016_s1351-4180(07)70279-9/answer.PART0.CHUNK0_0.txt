3. Researchers from the University of Witwatersand in South Africa will lead the construction of a new Fischer-Tropsch plant in Shaanxi, China, by April 2007. This pilot plant, costing R75 million ($10.4 million), will convert carbon monoxide and hydrogen into liquid hydrocarbons. The plant is being financed by the Chinese company Golden Nest Technology Group, which anticipates it will be approximately 30% more efficient than traditional technologies.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
