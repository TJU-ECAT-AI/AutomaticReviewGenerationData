[2] "The oxygenates may be derived from renewable biomass and offer advantages such as low toxicity, low reactivity, and compatibility with the current infrastructure for transportation and storage."
[3] "Recently, aqueous phase reforming of oxygenates, such as ethylene glycol, has been studied on various transition metals."
[4] "The reforming activity of ethylene glycol and ethanol can be correlated with the electronic properties of the corresponding surfaces as characterized by their d-band centers."

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Selective oxygenate reforming contributes to sustainable energy and chemical production by utilizing renewable resources and generating hydrogen for fuel cells. Its key advantages over traditional methods include:

1. **Environmental Impact**: Oxygenates derived from biomass are less toxic and reactive, leading to a potentially lower environmental impact compared to fossil fuels.
2. **Product Yield**: The reforming processes yield hydrogen efficiently, optimizing the conversion of oxygenates to fuel.
3. **Infrastructure Compatibility**: The compatibility of oxygenates with existing infrastructure for transportation and storage facilitates a smoother transition towards sustainable energy sources.

The d-band center of surfaces plays a crucial role in determining the activity of reforming reactions, offering insights into how to rationally design catalysts for enhanced efficiency.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
