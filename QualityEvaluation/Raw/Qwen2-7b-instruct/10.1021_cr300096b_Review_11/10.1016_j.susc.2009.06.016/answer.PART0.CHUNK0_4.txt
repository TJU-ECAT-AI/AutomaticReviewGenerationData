[1] "Reactions of methanol and ethylene glycol on Ni/Pt: Bridging the materials gap between single crystal and polycrystalline bimetallic surfaces"
</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
This research explores the catalytic decompositions of methanol and ethylene glycol on polycrystalline Ni/Pt surfaces to evaluate if the favorable chemistry observed on single crystal Ni/Pt(111) can be extended to more commercially relevant polycrystalline surfaces. The goal is to bridge the materials gap between single crystal and polycrystalline bimetallic surfaces, focusing on the reforming of oxygenates for hydrogen production.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

<questions>
How does the process of selective oxygenate reforming contribute to sustainable energy and chemical production, and what are its key advantages in terms of product yield, environmental impact, and practical implementation compared to traditional methods?
</questions>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Selective oxygenate reforming processes can contribute to sustainable energy and chemical production by efficiently converting biomass-derived oxygenates into hydrogen and carbon monoxide, providing a renewable route to a hydrogen economy. Key advantages include higher product yields, reduced environmental impact due to the use of renewable feedstocks, and practical implementation potential through the use of less expensive catalysts like Ni-based catalysts in place of precious metals like Pt. Compared to traditional methods, selective oxygenate reforming offers improved efficiency and sustainability, especially when using multi-metallic catalysts that can replace bulk atoms with less costly substitutes and enhance activity and selectivity for desired products.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

<ChineseVersionAnswer>
选择性氧化物重整过程可以通过高效地将生物质衍生的氧化物转化为氢气和一氧化碳，为可持续能源和化学生产提供途径，从而建立一个基于氢的经济体系。关键优势包括更高的产品产量、由于使用可再生原料而降低的环境影响、以及通过使用镍基催化剂（而非贵金属如铂）来实现的可行实施潜力。与传统方法相比，选择性氧化物重整提供了改进的效率和可持续性，特别是当使用可以替代大量原子并增强所需产品活性和选择性的多元金属催化剂时。
</ChineseVersionAnswer>
