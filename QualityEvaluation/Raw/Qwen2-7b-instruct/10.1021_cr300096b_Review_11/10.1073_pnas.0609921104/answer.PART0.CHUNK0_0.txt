<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Selective oxygenate reforming (SOR) contributes to sustainable energy and chemical production by converting oxygenates like alcohols and ethers into valuable products through catalytic reactions. Its key advantages over traditional methods include higher product yields, reduced environmental impact, and enhanced practical implementation.

2. SOR provides higher product yields, lower carbon footprints, and improved selectivity and efficiency, making it economically viable for producing high-quality fuels and chemicals.
