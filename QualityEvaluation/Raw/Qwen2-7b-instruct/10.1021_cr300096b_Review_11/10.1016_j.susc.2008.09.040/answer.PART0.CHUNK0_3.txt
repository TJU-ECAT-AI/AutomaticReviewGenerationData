<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Selective oxygenate reforming contributes to sustainable energy and chemical production by offering an alternative method to produce hydrogen, which can reduce the environmental impact of fossil fuel consumption.

2. Ethanol and ethylene glycol are considered potential feedstocks for hydrogen production, and they can also serve as models for larger polyols derived from biomass sources.

3. Other 3d-PtPt(1 1 1) bimetallic surfaces have been predicted to potentially act as catalysts for the reforming of oxygenates, suggesting a broad range of materials that could be used for hydrogen production.

4. The current paper focuses on detailing the decomposition pathways of ethanol and ethylene glycol on Ni/Pt(1 1 1) bimetallic surfaces, providing insights into their catalytic properties.
