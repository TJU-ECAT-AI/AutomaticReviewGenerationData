<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The Soviet Russian State by Robert G. Wesson offers a textbook-like approach to Soviet politics, providing balanced coverage of various aspects with commendable depth. However, the critique suggests that the book may unintentionally highlight Soviet hypocrisy, potentially leading some student readers to perceive it as having an "anti-Soviet bias." The review also points out that the book leaves readers with fragmented insights into Soviet society and lacks coherent guidelines for independent thinking and research.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
