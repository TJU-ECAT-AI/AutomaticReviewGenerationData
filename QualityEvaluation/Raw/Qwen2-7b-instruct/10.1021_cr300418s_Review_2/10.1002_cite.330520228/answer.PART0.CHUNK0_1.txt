<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The document discusses the use of quantum chemical calculations and experimental results regarding gas adsorption on metal, semiconductor, oxide, and zeolite surfaces from the perspective of an experimental chemist. It focuses on the surface states of limited solid bodies characterized by their own electron distributions and energies. These molecular methods are given preference over solid-state methods, assuming that the interaction between the molecular species and a small section of the solid body is decisive for adsorption. Finding appropriate models for complex adsorption phenomena might be as important as choosing the approximation levels of the computational methods to be applied.
