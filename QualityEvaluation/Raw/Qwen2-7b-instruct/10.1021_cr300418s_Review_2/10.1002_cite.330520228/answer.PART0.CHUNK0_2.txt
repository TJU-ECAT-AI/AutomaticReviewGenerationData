<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The book aims to present quantum chemical calculations and results on the adsorption of gases on metal, semiconductor, oxide, and zeolite surfaces from the perspective of experimental chemists. It discusses Tamm and Shockley surface states of limited solid bodies characterized by their own electron distributions and energies. These molecular methods share the assumption that the interaction between the molecular species and a small section of the solid body is crucial for adsorption. In describing complex adsorption phenomena, finding suitable models might be as important as choosing the approximation levels of the computational methods applied.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
