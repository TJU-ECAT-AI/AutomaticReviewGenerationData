What are the functions of Ga and Zn cations during propane dehydrocyclodimerization on H-ZSM5?

</questions>

<quotes>
"Ga and Zn species increase the rates of propane chemical conversion, recombinative hydrogen desorption, and deuterium incorporation from D 2 into reaction products. Disposal of hydrogen formed in C-H bond activation steps occurs by transfer of H-atoms to unsaturated species to form alkanes or to Ga and Zn species, which catalyze the recombinative desorption of H-atoms to form dihydrogen (H2). The sequential release of several H-atoms during a propane dehydrocyclodimerization turnover limits the rate and selectivity of this reaction on H-ZSM5."
</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
During propane dehydrocyclodimerization on H-ZSM5, Ga and Zn cations play key roles in accelerating the rates of propane chemical conversion, facilitating recombinative hydrogen desorption, and enhancing the incorporation of deuterium from D2 into products. The hydrogen generated from C-H bond activation is disposed of by transferring H-atoms to unsaturated species to form alkanes or to Ga and Zn species, which catalyze the recombinative desorption of H-atoms to form dihydrogen (H2). The sequential release of multiple H-atoms during a single dehydrocyclodimerization cycle restricts the rate and selectivity of this reaction on H-ZSM5.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

<questions>

How do Ga and Zn species affect the formation of aromatics and the selectivity of the reaction?

</questions>

<quotes>
"The sequential release of several H-atoms during a propane dehydrocyclodimerization turnover limits the rate and selectivity of this reaction on H-ZSM5. Isotopic studies show that C-H activation steps are fast during steady-state propane dehydrocyclodimerization on H-ZSM5, Ga/H-ZSM5, and Zn/H-ZSM5. Ga and Zn species increase the rates of propane chemical conversion, recombinative hydrogen desorption, and deuterium incorporation from D 2 into reaction products."
</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The release of several hydrogen atoms in sequence during a single propane dehydrocyclodimerization cycle restricts the rate and selectivity of the reaction on H-ZSM5. Isotope studies demonstrate that C-H activation stages are rapid during steady-state propane dehydrocyclodimerization on H-ZSM5, Ga/H-ZSM5, and Zn/H-ZSM5. The introduction of Ga and Zn species enhances the rates of propane chemical transformation, hydrogen atom recombination desorption, and the incorporation of deuterium from D2 into reaction products.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
