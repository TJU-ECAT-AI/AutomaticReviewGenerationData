The Ga/Al ratio, Si/Al ratio, and framework aluminum pair density in Ga-modified zeolite catalysts influence the formation and nature of active gallium species, including gallium hydrides. Gallium hydrides are formed due to the reduced Ga species, which lower hydrogen surface fugacities and allow for the catalytic removal of hydrogen by recombinative desorption. This leads to an increase in propane conversion rates and aromatics selectivity when Ga is introduced into non-framework positions in H-ZSM5. The structure-activity correlations revealed by these relationships suggest that the presence of Ga species in specific locations enhances the dehydrogenation process by facilitating hydrogen removal, thus preventing concurrent cracking and allowing acid sites to turnover effectively.
