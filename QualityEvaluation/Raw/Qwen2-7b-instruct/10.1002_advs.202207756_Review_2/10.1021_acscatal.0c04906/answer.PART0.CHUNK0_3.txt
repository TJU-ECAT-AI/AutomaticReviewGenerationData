Answer: The variability in intraporous Ga species structure and distribution in H-ZSM-5 zeolites, influenced by factors such as Ga loading and Si/Al ratio, challenges the assumption of uniform PDH activity because these variations lead to non-uniform catalytic performance. The non-monotonic relationship between Ga/Al ratio and catalytic performance implies that there might be an optimal range for the ratio where the catalyst's efficiency peaks. This suggests that the active sites are not homogeneously distributed or structured throughout the catalyst, which complicates the understanding of the true nature of the active sites. To optimize Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, researchers need to carefully control the synthesis conditions to achieve a more uniform distribution of Ga species and possibly find the optimal Ga/Al ratio that maximizes the catalyst's performance.
