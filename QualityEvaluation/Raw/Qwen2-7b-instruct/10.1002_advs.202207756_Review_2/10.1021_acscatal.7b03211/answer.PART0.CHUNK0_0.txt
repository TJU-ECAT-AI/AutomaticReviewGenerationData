Answer: The variability in intraporous Ga species structure and distribution in H-ZSM-5 zeolites, influenced by factors such as Ga loading and Si/Al ratio, challenges the assumption of uniform PDH activity because it shows that the actual active sites within the catalyst are not uniformly distributed or structured. This implies that the presence of Ga in different forms and locations within the zeolite can significantly impact its catalytic performance in methanol-to-aromatics conversion. The observed non-monotonic relationship between Ga/Al ratio and catalytic performance suggests that there might be an optimal range of Ga concentration for achieving maximum efficiency, beyond which additional Ga may not necessarily enhance performance. This has important implications for the design and optimization of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, necessitating a more nuanced understanding of how Ga species structure and distribution affect their activity and selectivity.
