Answer: The variability in intraporous Ga species structure and distribution in H-ZSM-5 zeolites, influenced by factors such as Ga loading and Si/Al ratio, challenges the assumption of uniform PDH activity because these factors lead to a non-uniform distribution of active sites within the zeolite framework. This non-uniformity results in a complex interplay between the Brnsted and Lewis acid functionalities, which is not adequately captured by simple models assuming homogeneity. The observed non-monotonic relationship between Ga/Al ratio and catalytic performance implies that there is an optimal ratio where the synergy between Brnsted and Lewis acidity is maximized, leading to enhanced conversion efficiency. This insight suggests that traditional approaches to catalyst optimization may not fully capture the complexity of the active site environment and that a more nuanced understanding of the structural effects on activity is required. To optimize Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, researchers should focus on fine-tuning the Ga loading and Si/Al ratio to achieve the most favorable distribution of active sites that promotes the synergistic effect between Brnsted and Lewis acidities.
