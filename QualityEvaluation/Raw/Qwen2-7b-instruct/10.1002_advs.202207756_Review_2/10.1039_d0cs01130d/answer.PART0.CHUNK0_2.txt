The variability in intraporous Ga species structure and distribution in H-ZSM-5 zeolites, influenced by factors such as Ga loading and Si/Al ratio, challenge the assumption of uniform PDH activity because the non-uniform distribution implies that the active sites may not be evenly spread throughout the zeolite. This can lead to inconsistencies in catalytic performance across different regions of the catalyst. The observed non-monotonic relationship between Ga/Al ratio and catalytic performance suggests that there might be an optimal ratio where the catalyst performs best. This is due to the complex interplay between the metal loading, the support properties, and the resulting structure of the active sites. Optimizing Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts would require a deep understanding of how these factors affect the creation and distribution of active sites, which can then be tailored to achieve the best catalytic outcomes.
