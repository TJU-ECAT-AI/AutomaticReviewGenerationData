Applied Catalysis A: General 242 (2003) 187203 The active role of CO2 at low temperature in oxidation processes:the case of the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane on NiMoO4 catalysts
F. Dury, E.M. Gaigneaux,1, P. Ruiz
Unit de catalyse et chimie des matriaux diviss, Universit catholique, Croix du Sud 2/17, B-1348 Louvain-la-Neuve, Belgium Received 29 May 2002; received in revised form 23 July 2002; accepted 10 September 2002 
</literature>
</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
In the study of CO2-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane on NiMoO4 catalysts, it was found that CO2 behaves as a strong oxidant with respect to the catalyst. CO2 helps to maintain the catalytic surface in a high oxidation state under conditions for which NiMoO4 undergoes a reduction. CO2 clearly promotes the non-selectivity of the catalyst and can inhibit deactivation. Under particular conditions, it can improve propene formation. CO2 is able to oxidize propane to propene. We suggest that CO2 dissociates during the reaction to CO and an active oxygen species, which is responsible for the oxidizing effect of carbon dioxide. This work opens promising perspectives in using CO2 to tune dynamically the oxidation state of working oxide surfaces in their most active and selective configurations for oxidation processes.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

在对NiMoO4催化剂上丙烷氧化脱氢作用于二氧化碳的研究中，发现二氧化碳相对于催化剂表现为强氧化剂。二氧化碳有助于在镍钼氧化物还原条件下保持催化表面处于高氧化状态。二氧化碳明显促进催化剂的非选择性，并且可以抑制催化剂失活。在特定条件下，它可以提高丙烯的形成。二氧化碳能够将丙烷氧化为丙烯。我们推测，二氧化碳在反应过程中分解为CO和活性氧物种，这是二氧化碳产生氧化效应的原因。这项工作为动态调整工作氧化表面的氧化状态以用于氧化过程提供了有前景的视角，使其处于最活跃和选择性的配置。
