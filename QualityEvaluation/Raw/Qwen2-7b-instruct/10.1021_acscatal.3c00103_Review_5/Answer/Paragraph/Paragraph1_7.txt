Metal oxides like gallium oxide (Ga2O3), molybdenum oxide (MoOx), and vanadium oxide (Vox) are explored as alternatives to traditional catalysts like platinum (Pt) and chromium oxide (CrOx), which suffer from high costs, environmental concerns, and rapid deactivation due to coke formation. Strategies focus on controlling coke formation, enhancing catalyst stability, and improving selectivity.

            Opportunities arise from reducing propylene production's dependence on crude oil, leveraging abundant natural gas resources. Shale gas production's economic advantage over crude oil fuels interest in alternative methods such as oxidative dehydrogenation and methanol-to-olefins (MTO) processes. Advances in catalyst design, reaction engineering, and process control are anticipated to lead to more efficient and sustainable PDH technologies.
