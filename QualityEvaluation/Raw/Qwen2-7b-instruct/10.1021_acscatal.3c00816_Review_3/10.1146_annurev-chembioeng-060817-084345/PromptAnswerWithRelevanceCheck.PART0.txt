<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The ultrafast zeolite crystallization strategy developed by Yu et al. addresses the time-scale mismatch challenges in direct synthesis procedures for metal encapsulation by accelerating the formation of the zeolite structure. This allows for the rapid creation of a stable matrix for metal clusters, such as PtSn, which can be encapsulated within the zeolite. This results in improved structure integrity and enhanced catalytic performance due to optimized interaction between the metal clusters and the zeolite surface. Specifically, in the context of propane conversion, the encapsulated PtSn clusters demonstrate higher activity and selectivity towards desired products compared to unsupported PtSn catalysts, owing to the protective and stabilizing properties of the zeolite matrix. The encapsulation also mitigates sintering and agglomeration issues commonly encountered with unsupported metal catalysts, leading to better long-term stability and efficiency in the conversion process.
