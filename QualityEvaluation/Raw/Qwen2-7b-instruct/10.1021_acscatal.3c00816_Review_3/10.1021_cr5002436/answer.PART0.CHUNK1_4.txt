<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The ultrafast zeolite crystallization strategy developed by Yu et al. efficiently addresses the time-scale mismatch challenges in direct synthesis procedures for metal encapsulation, enabling the rapid formation of zeolite frameworks that facilitate the concurrent encapsulation of metal species. This strategy ensures the formation of well-defined PtSn clusters that are evenly dispersed throughout the zeolite structure, optimizing their accessibility and catalytic performance for propane conversion. The enhanced dispersion and uniform distribution of these clusters within the zeolite matrix result in improved surface area utilization, boosting the catalytic activity and selectivity towards desired products. The rapid crystallization process minimizes the risk of metal particle growth, maintaining the individual catalytic sites and preventing deactivation. Overall, this strategy represents a significant advancement in the direct synthesis of metal encapsulated zeolites, offering a powerful tool for enhancing catalyst performance in applications like propane conversion.
