<li>The ultrafast zeolite crystallization strategy developed by Yu et al. addresses the time-scale mismatch challenges in direct synthesis procedures for metal encapsulation by allowing for the rapid formation of zeolite frameworks. This method facilitates the encapsulation of metals like Pt and Sn within the zeolite structure without the need for prolonged synthesis times.</li>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The ultrafast zeolite crystallization strategy developed by Yu et al. efficiently encapsulates metals such as Pt and Sn within zeolite structures, addressing time-scale mismatch challenges in direct synthesis procedures. This method allows for the rapid formation of zeolite frameworks, facilitating the encapsulation of metals. In the context of propane conversion, the formation of PtSn clusters within ZSM-5 zeolites results in high dispersion, leading to improved catalytic performance. The clusters display high stability and activity in the PDH reaction due to the confinement effect of the zeolite pores.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
