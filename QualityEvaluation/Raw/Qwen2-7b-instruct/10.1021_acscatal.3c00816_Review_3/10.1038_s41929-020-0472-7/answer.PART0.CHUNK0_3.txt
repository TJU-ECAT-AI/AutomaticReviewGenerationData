<quotes>
<p>Structural modulation and direct measurement of subnanometric bimetallic PtSn clusters confined in zeolites</p>
</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
This research demonstrates how the catalytic properties of subnanometric platinum clusters (0.50.6nm) confined in the sinusoidal 10R channels of purely siliceous MFI zeolite can be modulated upon introduction of partially reduced tin species that interact with the noble metal at the metal/support interface. The platinumtin clusters are stable in H2 over an extended period of time (>6h), even at high temperatures (for example, 600C), which is determined by only a few additional tin atoms added to the platinum clusters. The structural features of platinumtin clusters, which are not immediately visible by conventional characterization techniques but can be established after combination of in situ extended X-ray absorption fine structure, high-angle annular dark-field scanning transmission electron microscopy and CO infrared data, are key to providing a one-order of magnitude lower deactivation rate in the propane dehydrogenation reaction while maintaining high intrinsic (initial) catalytic activity.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

<ChineseVersionAnswer>
这项研究展示了如何通过在金属/载体界面处引入部分还原的锡物种与贵金属相互作用，在二氧化硅纯MFI沸石的正弦通道中限制的亚纳米尺寸铂金簇（0.50.6nm）的结构可以被调节。这些铂锡簇在氢气气氛下长达数小时（例如，600°C）时稳定，即使只有少量的锡原子添加到铂簇中。通过结合原位扩展X射线吸收精细结构、高角度环形暗场扫描透射电子显微镜和CO红外数据，可以确定铂锡簇的结构特征，这些特征不立即通过常规表征技术可见，但可以在组合使用后建立。这些结构特征对于提供在丙烷脱氢反应中降低一个数量级的失活率同时保持高内在（初始）催化活性至关重要。
