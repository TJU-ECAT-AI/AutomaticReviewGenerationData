<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Bimetallic catalysts, particularly those based on Pt, significantly influence the mechanisms and efficiencies of CO oxidation, water gas shift reaction, and methane conversions in reactor studies. For instance, Pt3Ni(111) is identified as an optimum catalyst for CO preferential oxidation in excess of hydrogen. The reversible control of surface structures on bimetallic surfaces like Fe/Pt(111) demonstrates the potential for tuning the surface activity. The PtNi system showcases the interface confinement effect that enhances CO oxidation. 

2. In the water gas shift reaction, the addition of metals like Re, Co, and Au to Pt-based bimetallic catalysts enhances their activity. This highlights the importance of alloyed metals and dispersion in the activity of the WGS reaction.

3. PtPd catalysts outperform monometallic Pd catalysts in the catalytic combustion of CH4, indicating the synergy between metals in bimetallic systems.

4. PtNi catalysts supported on MgAl2O4 exhibit self-activation and self-regeneration characteristics, suggesting they are effective for processes requiring continuous self-renewal.

5. The principles, synthesis, and characterization techniques of Pt-based bimetallic electrocatalysts are similar to those of bimetallic catalysts for chemical reactions, offering a foundation for understanding their performance.

6. Trimetallic catalysts have demonstrated better performance than both monometallic and bimetallic systems, indicating the potential benefits of incorporating additional metals in catalytic applications.

2. 在水煤气变换反应中，添加如Re、Co和Au等合金金属到Pt基双金属催化剂中可以增强其活性。这突显了合金金属和分散度在水煤气变换反应活性中的重要性。

5. Pt基双金属电催化剂的基本原理、合成方法和表征技术与化学反应中的双金属催化剂相似，为理解其性能提供了一个基础。

6. 三金属催化剂已经显示出比单金属和双金属系统更好的性能，暗示了在催化应用中引入额外金属的潜在益处。
