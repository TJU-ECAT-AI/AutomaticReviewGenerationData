        The review of catalytic reactions utilizing bimetallic catalysts, particularly focusing on CO oxidation, water gas shift reaction, and methane conversions, reveals several key insights. From the studies referenced by DOI numbers \[10.1021/ja101108w\], \[10.1038/nmat2359\], and \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.05.020\], we understand that the preference of CO oxidation over Pt-based bimetallic catalysts is significantly influenced by the composition of the metal core and shell. This factor plays a crucial role in the reactivity of these catalysts across various chemical transformations, including the water gas shift reaction and methane conversions. These catalysts exhibit enhanced efficiency in these processes, largely attributed to their unique properties.

These investigations could illuminate the underlying mechanisms and optimize the design of more effective catalysts for industrial applications.
