To understand how bimetallic catalysts, particularly Pt-based ones, influence the mechanisms and efficiencies of CO oxidation, water gas shift reaction, and methane conversions in reactor studies, consider the following insights:

1. The addition of Re to Pt catalysts on CeO2-ZrO2 mixed oxides enhances the water gas shift (WGS) rate compared to pure Pt catalysts. This enhancement is observed even at similar Pt weight loadings.

2. The exposed Pt surface area plays a crucial role in the WGS rate. Catalysts with higher Pt dispersion, such as Pt-Re, exhibit a higher WGS rate per unit area of exposed Pt. This suggests that the synergy between Pt and Re might extend beyond mere Pt dispersion improvements.

3. The stability of Pt-Re catalysts is superior to that of Pt-only catalysts when exposed to air at high temperatures, which is a potential drawback in fuel processing applications. This stability indicates that Re might stabilize Pt against sintering, contributing to better long-term performance.

4. The research focuses on developing WGS catalysts that address the drawbacks of using conventional Cu-Zn and Fe-Cr oxide catalysts in fuel processors for automotive and distributed power generation. The enhanced WGS activity of Pt-Re catalysts holds promise for more efficient hydrogen production in these applications.
