Steam treatment enhances the dispersion of iron species, promoting better access to the active sites and potentially increasing the reaction rates. Solid-state ion exchange can modify the acidity of the zeolite framework, influencing the selectivity towards desired products. Liquid exchange might alter the hydration state of the zeolite, affecting the solvation dynamics of reactants and products.

The specific effects on C3H8 conversion, N2O conversion, C3H6 selectivity, and C3H6 yield depend on the details of the synthesis method used. Liquid exchange might affect the solvation of reactants and products, impacting the reaction pathways and yields.

Overall, the choice of synthesis method can significantly impact the catalytic performance of iron-containing ZSM-5 zeolites in the ODHP reaction, affecting the efficiency of N2O-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane. Optimizing the synthesis conditions to achieve the best balance among C3H8 conversion, N2O conversion, C3H6 selectivity, and C3H6 yield is crucial for enhancing the performance of these catalysts in the process.
