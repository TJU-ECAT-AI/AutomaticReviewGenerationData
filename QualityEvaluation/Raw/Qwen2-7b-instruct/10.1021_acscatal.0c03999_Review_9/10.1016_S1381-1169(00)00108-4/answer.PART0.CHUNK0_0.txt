<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. In the presence of N2O on HZSM-5, the reactivity of hydrocarbons correlates with the strength of C-H bond. Methane is capable of alkylating the aromatic ring of benzene, producing toluene and xylenes.

2. The conversion of methane, ethane, propane, benzene, hydrogen, and their mixtures in the presence of N2O on HZSM-5 results in the formation of toluene, xylenes, cresols, and diphenylmethane without significant deep oxidation products like CO, CO2.

3. When 13CH4, C6H6, N2O, and He are fed into the system, the mass spectra of toluene, xylenes, and diphenylmethane indicate the presence of only one 13C atom in these molecules.

4. The mass-spectroscopy or 13C NMR data do not show any carbon label insertion into the aromatic ring of phenol during the conversion process.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
