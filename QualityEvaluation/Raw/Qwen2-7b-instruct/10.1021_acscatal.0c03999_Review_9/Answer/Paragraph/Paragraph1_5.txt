The influence of various synthesis methods, such as wet ion-exchange (WIE), chemical vapor deposition/sublimation, and ex-framework method, on the catalytic performance of iron-containing ZSM-5 zeolites in the N2O-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reaction has been comprehensively explored. The effectiveness of these catalysts is evaluated through key performance metrics including C3H8 conversion, N2O conversion, C3H6 selectivity, and C3H6 yield under diverse reaction conditions. Fe-ZSM-5 prepared via solid-state exchange using ferrocene as the iron precursor demonstrates superior performance for ODHP with N2O, achieving a maximal initial propylene yield of 30% at a high turnover frequency of 0.037 s−1. Active sites identified as FeOAl species enable the formation of stable mono-oxygen species through chemisorption of nitrous oxide, facilitating the reaction with gaseous propane to produce propylene with high selectivity. Remarkably, Fe-ZSM-5 maintains a stable propane conversion rate of approximately 13 mmol gcat−1 h−1 and a propylene production rate of about 6 mmol gcat−1 h−1 for over 40 hours with a N2O/C3H8 ratio of 1:2. Moreover, steam-activated Fe-zeolites, particularly FeZSM-5, show high activity and selectivity in the ODHP reaction, achieving propylene yields of up to 25% at 723 K and space velocities of 400,000 ml/h/g-cat. The presence of extraframework iron species, generated during steam treatment, is crucial for producing active catalysts. FeZSM-5 exhibits remarkable resistance against deactivation, contrasting with FeBEA which experiences rapid and complete deactivation. The initial propene yield from FeZSM-5 and FeBEA is notably higher than from commercial H-ZSM-5 and H-BEA, which have 30-40 times lower iron content. However, their residual activities after 400 minutes of operation are similar. The nature and location of the active extraframework iron species play a critical role in the deactivation behavior, and ex-FeZSM-5 shows significantly higher activity in direct N2O decomposition compared to ex-Fe-silicalite.

            基于\[10.1016/j.apcatb.2004.02.013\]参考文献的深度分析。从\[10.1016/j.micromeso.2014.07.032\]和\[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005\]的参考文献中获得的综合理解。各种合成方法，如湿离子交换（WIE）、化学气相沉积/升华和离框架法，对含铁ZSM-5沸石在N2O辅助氧化脱氢丙烷（ODHP）反应中的催化性能产生了全面的影响。这些催化剂的有效性通过关键性能指标，包括C3H8转化率、N2O转化率、C3H6选择性和C3H6产率，在不同的反应条件下进行了评估。通过固体状态交换使用二茂铁作为铁前体制备的Fe-ZSM-5催化剂在与N2O进行ODHP反应时表现出优越的性能，实现了最大初始丙烯产率为30%，在0.037 s−1的高周转频率下。识别出的FeOAl物种通过硝酸盐的化学吸附作用形成稳定的单氧物种，进而促进与气态丙烷的反应，产生高选择性的丙烯。值得注意的是，Fe-ZSM-5能够在N2O/C3H8比例为1:2的情况下，维持稳定丙烷转化率约为13 mmol gcat−1 h−1和丙烯生产率约为6 mmol gcat−1 h−1，持续时间超过40小时。此外，蒸汽活化Fe-沸石，特别是FeZSM-5，显示出在ODHP反应中高活性和选择性，实现最高丙烯产率达到25%，在723 K温度下和400,000 ml/h/g-cat的空间速度下。在蒸汽处理过程中生成的额外框架铁物种的存在对于产生活性催化剂至关重要。FeZSM-5表现出显著的抗失活能力，与经历快速和完全失活的FeBEA形成对比。FeZSM-5和FeBEA的初始丙烯产率明显高于商业H-ZSM-5和H-BEA，后者铁含量仅为前者的30-40倍。然而，它们在400分钟后流经后的残余活性相似。活性额外框架铁物种的性质和位置在失活行为中起着至关重要的作用，并且ex-FeZSM-5在直接N2O分解中显示出与ex-Fe-silicalite相比的显著更高活性。
