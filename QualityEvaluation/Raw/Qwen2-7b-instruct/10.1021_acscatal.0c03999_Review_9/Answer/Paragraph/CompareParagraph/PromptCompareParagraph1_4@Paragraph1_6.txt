The topology of the zeolite significantly affects both the activity and stability of the catalyst under realistic conditions. Over-exchange, aiming for an Fe/Al ratio of 1, which can be accomplished through chemical vapor deposition (CVD), is not a critical factor for attaining high activity in N2O decomposition. Moreover, WIE yields a catalyst where less than 50% of the exchange sites are occupied by Fe.

            Various synthesis methods, including steam treatment, solid-state ion exchange, and liquid exchange, influence the catalytic performance of iron-containing ZSM-5 zeolites in the ODHP reaction differently. Fe-ZSM-5, prepared via solid-state exchange using ferrocene as the iron precursor, demonstrates superior performance for ODHP with N2O. After severe calcination, Fe-ZSM-5 exhibits a maximal initial propylene yield of 30% at a high turnover frequency of 0.037 s−1. Active sites identified as FeOAl species enable the formation of stable mono-oxygen species through chemisorption of nitrous oxide, which then react with gaseous propane to produce propylene with high selectivity. The nature and location of the active extraframework iron species play a crucial role in the deactivation behavior. Additionally, ex-FeZSM-5 shows significantly higher activity in direct N2O decomposition compared to ex-Fe-silicalite.
