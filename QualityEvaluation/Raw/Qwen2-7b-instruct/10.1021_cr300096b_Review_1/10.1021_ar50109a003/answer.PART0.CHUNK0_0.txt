<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Physical vapor deposition (PVD), especially thermal evaporation, is crucial for creating bimetallic surfaces in UHV settings. The process typically involves a UHV chamber with a crucible containing the source material, a substrate, and a vacuum pump system. Critical parameters include the source material selection, substrate temperature, and evaporation rate. Ensuring high-purity metal layers necessitates meticulous control over deposition rate and environmental cleanliness. Thermal evaporation enables detailed surface science studies by allowing precise layer creation, essential for understanding interactions and catalytic activities. Key factors for achieving optimal results include choosing suitable substrate materials, optimizing the deposition environment, and utilizing advanced analytical tools for post-deposition characterization.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
