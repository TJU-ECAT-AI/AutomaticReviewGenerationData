Among the various deposition methods, physical vapor deposition (PVD) is most compatible with the UHV environment necessary for surface science studies. PVD methods utilize line-of-sight deposition to produce a metal overlayer on the target substrate, with common techniques including magnetron sputtering, pulsed laser deposition, e-beam sputtering, and thermal evaporation. The evaporation filament is typically housed in a stainless steel cylinder with a small opening at the front face to direct metal deposition onto the substrate, preventing evaporation onto other components in the UHV system. The pressure in the UHV system should be kept at 5 × 10^-10 Torr or lower during evaporation to prevent background impurities from accumulating on the bimetallic surface. AES and XPS are often used to quantify the elemental compositions of bimetallic surfaces. In LEIS experiments, the surface is bombarded with positive ions of an inert element at relatively low energy to detect the topmost layer's composition. LEED determines the overlayer structure, while STM assesses the structure and morphology of the metal overlayer. The AES quantifies the physical vapor deposition and thermal behavior of metals like Fe and Cu on Pt(111), revealing distinct deposition characteristics. After deposition, surfaces are heated to high temperatures, and AES scans are performed to assess changes in surface composition. AES ratios for Fe-Pt-Pt(111) and Cu-Pt-Pt(111) surfaces are calculated to be 1.7 and 1.8, respectively, consistent with experimental results.

        Surface cleanliness is paramount in experiments, as impurities can lead to misleading results, especially in LEED experiments, which are sensitive to surface cleanliness. Precise data can only be obtained from initially well-defined surfaces.

        In summary, PVD, particularly thermal evaporation, facilitates the creation of bimetallic surfaces in UHV environments for surface science studies. Key considerations for optimal results include precise control over process parameters, ensuring high-purity metal deposition, and maintaining surface cleanliness. Achieving these factors allows for the detailed characterization of surface structures and properties, essential for advancing our understanding in materials science and catalysis.

        总之，尤其是热蒸发，使我们能够在UHV环境中为表面科学研究创建双金属表面。实现最佳结果的关键考虑因素包括精确控制工艺参数、确保高纯度金属沉积以及维持表面清洁度。这些因素的实现允许对表面结构和性质进行详细表征，这对于材料科学和催化领域的进步至关重要。
