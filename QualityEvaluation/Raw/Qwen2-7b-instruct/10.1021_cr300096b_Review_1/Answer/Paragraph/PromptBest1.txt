This approach is compatible with the stringent conditions required for surface science studies. It involves depositing a metal overlayer on the target substrate through line-of-sight deposition, utilizing common techniques such as magnetron sputtering, pulsed laser deposition, e-beam sputtering, and physical evaporation via a tungsten or tantalum filament. The metal source typically comprises a thin tungsten or tantalum wire wrapped with a smaller wire of the desired metal. The evaporation filament is housed in a stainless steel cylinder with a small opening to direct the metal deposition onto the substrate and prevent evaporation onto other components in the UHV system. To ensure high purity, the UHV system pressure during evaporation should be kept at 5 × 10^-10 Torr or lower.
