<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism operates in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions by utilizing the energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) to facilitate the reaction. ΔEv determines the catalytic activity and oxidizing ability of the oxide surface. A lower ΔEv typically indicates higher catalytic activity and oxidizing power, as it facilitates the formation of active sites for oxidation reactions.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
