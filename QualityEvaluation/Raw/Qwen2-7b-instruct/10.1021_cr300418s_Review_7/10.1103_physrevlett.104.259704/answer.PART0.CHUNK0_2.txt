Regarding the Mars-van Krevelen mechanism, the energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) plays a crucial role in determining both the catalytic activity and the oxidizing ability of the oxide surface. This is because ΔEv influences how easily oxygen vacancies can be created on the surface of the oxide catalyst. When ΔEv is low, it becomes easier for oxygen vacancies to form, which can enhance the surface's catalytic activity and oxidizing power. Conversely, a higher ΔEv may hinder the formation of oxygen vacancies, potentially reducing the catalyst's efficiency. Therefore, understanding and controlling ΔEv is essential for optimizing the performance of oxide catalysts in oxidation reactions.
