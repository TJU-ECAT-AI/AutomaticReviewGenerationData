<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Answer: The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism operates in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions through the formation of oxygen vacancies on the surface of the oxide catalyst. The energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) plays a significant role in determining both the catalytic activity and the oxidizing ability of the oxide surface. A lower ΔEv typically leads to higher catalytic activity and stronger oxidizing power due to increased surface reactivity.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
