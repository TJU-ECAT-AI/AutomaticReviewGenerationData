<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism operates in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions by facilitating the creation of oxygen vacancies (Ovac) on the surface of the oxide catalyst. The energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) is critical because it influences both the catalytic activity and the oxidizing ability of the oxide surface. Lower ΔEv values suggest easier formation of oxygen vacancies, potentially enhancing the surface's catalytic efficiency and oxidizing power.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
