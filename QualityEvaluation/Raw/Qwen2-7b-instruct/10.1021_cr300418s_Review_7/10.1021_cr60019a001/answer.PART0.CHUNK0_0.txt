<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism operates in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions through the formation of oxygen vacancies on the oxide surface. The energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) plays a significant role in determining both the catalytic activity and the oxidizing ability of the oxide surface. This is because the presence of oxygen vacancies acts as active sites for the adsorption and oxidation of reactants, thereby facilitating the reaction process.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
