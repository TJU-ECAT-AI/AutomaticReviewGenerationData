<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism operates in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions by utilizing oxygen vacancies on the surface of the oxide catalyst. Oxygen atoms from the reactants or the catalyst itself migrate to these vacancies to form oxygen molecules, which then participate in the oxidation process.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

2. The energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) plays a significant role in determining both the catalytic activity and the oxidizing ability of the oxide surface. A lower ΔEv indicates that more oxygen vacancies can be easily formed, potentially enhancing the surface's reactivity and catalytic efficiency.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
