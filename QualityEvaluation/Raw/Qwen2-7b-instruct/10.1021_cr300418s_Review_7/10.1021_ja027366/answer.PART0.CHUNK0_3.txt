<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Answer: The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism operates in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions through a series of steps involving the formation and migration of oxygen vacancies on the surface of the oxide catalyst. The energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) plays a crucial role in determining both the catalytic activity and the oxidizing ability of the oxide surface. Higher ΔEv typically leads to a more stable oxygen vacancy, which can enhance the surface reactivity and thus increase the catalytic activity and oxidizing power of the catalyst.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
