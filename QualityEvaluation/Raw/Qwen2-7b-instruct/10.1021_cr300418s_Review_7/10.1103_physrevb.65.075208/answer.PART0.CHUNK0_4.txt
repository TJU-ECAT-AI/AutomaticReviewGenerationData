<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism operates in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions by utilizing the energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) to drive the reaction. ΔEv signifies the energy needed to create an oxygen vacancy on the oxide surface. This energy barrier plays a crucial role in determining the catalytic activity and oxidizing ability of the oxide surface. A lower ΔEv typically indicates higher catalytic activity and oxidizing power, as it facilitates the creation of reactive oxygen vacancies that can effectively participate in oxidation reactions.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
