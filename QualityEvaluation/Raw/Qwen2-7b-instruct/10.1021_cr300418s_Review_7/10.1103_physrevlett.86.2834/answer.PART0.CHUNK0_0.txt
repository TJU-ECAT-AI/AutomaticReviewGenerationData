<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Density functional theory (DFT) is a standard tool in physics and chemistry for parameter-free total-energy calculations for molecules and solids, offering a considerable improvement in accuracy over the Hartree-Fock (HF) approximation while being more computationally efficient. However, the range of validity of DFT approximations is difficult to estimate, and it is important to clarify the limitations of the theory and gain inspiration for improved density functionals by a careful study of the rare instances where the method fails.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
