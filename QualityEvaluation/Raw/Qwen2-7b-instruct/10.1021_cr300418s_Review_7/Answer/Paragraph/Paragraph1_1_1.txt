Oxygen vacancy formation energy (ΔEv) determines the reactivity of the oxide surface. Doping with overvalent Mo species promotes metal island growth, stabilized by electron transfer. Codoping with Li halts this through trapping excess electrons, affecting ΔEv. This mechanism elucidates how modifying oxide supports can influence metal particle formation and catalytic performance.
