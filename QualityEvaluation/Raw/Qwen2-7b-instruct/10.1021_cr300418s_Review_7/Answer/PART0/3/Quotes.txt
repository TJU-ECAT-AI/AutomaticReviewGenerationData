[1] "We find that Cr2+ forms on bridging oxygen ions and a 5-fold coordinated surface Ti atom is reduced to Ti3+."
          [2] "Upon adsorption of Cr atoms, the relative intensity of the Ti4+ peak is reduced and the intensity in the Ti3+ shoulder increases."
          [3] "The computed spin magnetization gives a 3d4 electronic configuration on Cr, with the four 3d electrons unpaired, so that upon adsorption Cr is oxidised to Cr2+ , with the 4s electron and a 3d electron transferred to the surface."
          [4] "By exposing this structure to oxygen we re-oxidise all of the Ti to Ti4 + through a strongly preferred adsorption of oxygen only on the Cr species."
          [5] "The adsorption of oxygen results in an energy gain of 1.88 eV when adsorbed at Cr."
