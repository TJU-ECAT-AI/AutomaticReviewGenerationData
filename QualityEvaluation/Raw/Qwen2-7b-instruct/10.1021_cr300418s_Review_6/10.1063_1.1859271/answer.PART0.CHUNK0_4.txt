Computational methods, particularly density functional theory (DFT) and cluster models, significantly enhance our ability to predict, analyze, and understand the catalytic activity of doped oxide catalysts in alkane activation. These methods allow for the prediction of the behavior of size-selected clusters on specific surfaces, providing insights into their electronic structure.
