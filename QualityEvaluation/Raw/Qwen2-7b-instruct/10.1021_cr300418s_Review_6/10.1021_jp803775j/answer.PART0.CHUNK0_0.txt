Computational methods, particularly density functional theory (DFT) and cluster models, enhance our ability to predict, analyze, and understand the catalytic activity of doped oxide catalysts in alkane activation by exploring their electronic structure and catalytic properties. These methods allow for the prediction of the catalytic activity, identification of promising materials, and understanding of the mechanism of water splitting. Key considerations include the accuracy of the theoretical calculations, the efficiency of computational resources, and the ability to explore new catalytic materials.
