<EnglishVersionAnswer>

Computational methods, particularly density functional theory (DFT) and cluster models, significantly enhance our ability to predict, analyze, and understand the catalytic activity of doped oxide catalysts in alkane activation. DFT provides a reliable theoretical framework for understanding the electronic structure and chemical behavior of these catalysts, while cluster models are useful for simulating the local environment around active sites and predicting the adsorption behavior of alkane molecules. Key considerations in utilizing DFT and cluster models include the choice of exchange-correlation functional, the size of computational cells, and the accuracy of basis sets. Challenges include high computational costs and the need for expert knowledge in interpreting the results. Potential benefits include the ability to screen large libraries of catalysts, optimize existing catalysts, and gain insights into the mechanisms of catalytic reactions.

</EnglishVersionAnswer>
