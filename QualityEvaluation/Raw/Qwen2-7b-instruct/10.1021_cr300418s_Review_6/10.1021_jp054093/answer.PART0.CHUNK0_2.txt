Computational methods, specifically density functional theory (DFT) and cluster models, significantly enhance our ability to predict, analyze, and understand the catalytic activity of doped oxide catalysts in alkane activation. These approaches offer key benefits such as high accuracy in modeling chemical reactions, efficiency in computational resources, and the exploration of novel catalytic materials. The challenges include the complexity of accurately representing multi-scale phenomena and the computational demands of large-scale simulations.
