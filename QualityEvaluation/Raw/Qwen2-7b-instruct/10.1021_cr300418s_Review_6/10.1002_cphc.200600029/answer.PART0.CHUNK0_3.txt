<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Computational methods, such as density functional theory (DFT) and cluster models, significantly improve our prediction, analysis, and understanding of the catalytic activity of doped oxide catalysts in alkane activation.
2. The presence of a dopant like an F center in magnesium oxide (MgO) leads to the charging of gold clusters, which activates oxygen and carbon monoxide molecules adsorbed on the Au8 cluster.
3. The charging of the Au8 cluster can be experimentally observed through a red shift in the CO stretching frequency of adsorbed CO.
4. The ability of the gold cluster to dynamically adapt its structure is crucial for its catalytic activity, as it allows the reaction to proceed along the most favorable energy path.
5. The promotional effect of water is explained by the formation of a hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) complex, which facilitates proton sharing and activates the oxygen molecule.
6. Activation of the OO bond by water further facilitates the reaction with CO, enhancing the catalytic activity of the system.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
