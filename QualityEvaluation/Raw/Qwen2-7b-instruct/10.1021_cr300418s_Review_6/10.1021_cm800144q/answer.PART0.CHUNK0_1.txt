<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Computational methods, such as density functional theory (DFT) and cluster models, enhance our understanding of doped oxide catalysts' activity in alkane activation by enabling precise predictions and analyses of their electronic structures and reactivity. Key factors in utilizing these methods are accuracy, efficiency, and the capability to simulate complex systems. However, challenges arise due to the high computational costs of advanced calculations and the necessity for precise input parameters. The benefits of these computational tools include the discovery of active sites, the improvement of catalyst design, and the prediction of reaction pathways.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
