<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Computational methods, particularly density functional theory (DFT) and cluster models, significantly enhance our ability to predict, analyze, and understand the catalytic activity of doped oxide catalysts in alkane activation by providing a powerful framework for calculating electronic properties and simulating local environments and interactions. This integration offers insights into structure-reactivity relationships, allowing for the exploration of new materials and optimization of existing ones.

2. The key considerations when utilizing these computational methods involve ensuring the accuracy of theoretical predictions against experimental data, while acknowledging the challenges of high computational costs and the complexity of modeling real catalyst surfaces. Potential benefits include advancements in material science, leading to more efficient and effective catalysts for alkane activation.
