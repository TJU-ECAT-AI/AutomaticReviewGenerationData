<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Computational methods such as density functional theory (DFT) and cluster models enhance our understanding of the catalytic activity of doped oxide catalysts in alkane activation by predicting and analyzing electronic structure, reactivity pathways, and energetics. Key considerations involve the selection of functional, cluster size, and solvent model. Challenges include computational expense and accurately simulating surface chemistry. Benefits include the ability to screen materials, optimize parameters, and understand fundamental mechanisms. Combining computational predictions with experimental data provides comprehensive insights into catalyst performance.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
