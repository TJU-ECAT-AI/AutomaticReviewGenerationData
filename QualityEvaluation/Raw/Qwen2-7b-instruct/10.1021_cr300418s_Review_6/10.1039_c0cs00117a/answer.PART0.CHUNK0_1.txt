<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Alkali-metal cations M+ serve as ideal probing sites for a pure electrostatic interaction. When CO binds to these sites, the perturbation of its stretching frequency is directly proportional to the local electric field strength (E), following the Stark effect. This linear relationship facilitates the understanding of how the local electric field affects the CO molecule's vibrational properties, providing insights into the interaction strength between the metal site and the CO ligand.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

The interaction between CO and metal cations can involve various contributions depending on the nature of the metal-cation sites. (a) The electric field generated by a positive charge, such as an alkaline metal cation, causes the CO molecule to polarize, which depends on the local electric field of the site. This polarization is influenced by the cation's charge density, primarily, and is further modulated by neighboring anions. This process explains how the CO molecule's vibrational properties are affected by the surrounding environment at the metal site.
