<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Density functional theory (DFT) enables precise predictions of electronic structures and properties of materials, including catalysts. Cluster models provide insights into the local environment around active sites, thereby enhancing the understanding of catalytic mechanisms.
2. Key considerations when employing computational methods involve selecting the appropriate functional within DFT, determining the cluster size, and accounting for dispersion forces.
3. Challenges associated with these approaches include high computational costs and difficulties in extrapolating results to larger systems.
4. Potential advantages encompass rapid screening of novel catalyst designs and exploration of complex reactions under diverse conditions.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
