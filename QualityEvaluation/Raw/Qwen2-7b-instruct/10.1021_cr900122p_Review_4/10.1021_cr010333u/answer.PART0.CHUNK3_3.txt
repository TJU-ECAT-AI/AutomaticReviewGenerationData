<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1. The deactivation of supported alkene polymerization and metathesis catalysts often involves the formation of π-allyl surface complexes and β-H transfer reactions. 
2. Lanthanide/actinide-based catalysts typically undergo deactivation through a process involving the formation of a π-allyl complex on the surface followed by β-H transfer. 
3. Silica-supported Re-based catalysts also experience deactivation through the formation of a π-allyl complex, but the β-H transfer step may occur differently due to the different nature of the support material.
4. The key steps in the deactivation process involve the formation of a π-allyl surface complex, which can then lead to β-H transfer, leading to catalyst deactivation.
