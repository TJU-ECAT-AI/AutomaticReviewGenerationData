Analyzing the role of intramolecular C-H bond activation in the deactivation of supported alkene polymerization and metathesis catalysts involves understanding the mechanisms specific to lanthanide/actinide-based and silica-supported Re-based catalysts. The processes include the formation of π-allyl surface complexes and β-H transfer, which are crucial for the performance and longevity of these catalysts.

For lanthanide/actinide-based catalysts, the key steps involve the activation of intramolecular C-H bonds leading to the formation of π-allyl surface complexes. This process is initiated by the interaction of the catalyst with the alkene substrate, leading to the stabilization of the π-allyl complex. The β-H transfer step then facilitates the deactivation pathway, releasing the product and regenerating the catalyst. This mechanism allows for efficient alkene polymerization, but the stability of the catalyst can be compromised by the deactivation pathways.

In the case of silica-supported Re-based catalysts, the activation of intramolecular C-H bonds plays a significant role in metathesis reactions. The catalyst interacts with the alkene substrate, forming surface complexes that can undergo β-H transfer. This step is essential for the formation of new alkene products through metathesis. However, the repeated activation and deactivation cycles can lead to deactivation pathways that negatively impact the catalyst's performance and longevity. The potential consequences of these deactivation pathways include reduced catalytic activity, decreased selectivity, and the formation of byproducts, ultimately affecting the efficiency and effectiveness of the catalyst in alkene polymerization and metathesis reactions.

These mechanisms highlight the importance of understanding the specific pathways involved in the deactivation of supported alkene polymerization and metathesis catalysts. By elucidating the key steps, including the formation of π-allyl surface complexes and β-H transfer, researchers can optimize catalyst design and development, enhancing the performance and longevity of these catalysts in industrial applications.

对于镧系/锕系基催化剂，关键步骤涉及激活介分子C-H键导致π-亚基表面复合物的形成。此过程由催化剂与烯烃底物的相互作用启动，导致π-亚基复合物的稳定化。随后的β-H转移步骤是失活途径的关键，释放产物并再生催化剂。这一机制允许高效的烯烃聚合，但催化剂稳定性可能因失活途径而受损。

对于硅支持的铼基催化剂，在金属环化反应中，激活介分子C-H键起着重要作用。催化剂与烯烃底物相互作用，形成表面复合物，可以进行β-H转移。这一步骤是新烯烃产品形成的关键。然而，反复的激活和失活循环可能导致失活途径，从而对催化剂性能和寿命产生负面影响。这些失活路径的潜在后果包括催化活性降低、选择性下降以及副产品的形成，最终影响催化剂在烯烃聚合和金属环化反应中的效率和效果。

这些机制强调了理解支持型烯烃聚合和金属环化催化剂失活特定途径的重要性。通过阐明关键步骤，包括π-亚基表面复合物的形成和β-H转移，研究人员可以优化催化剂设计和发展，增强这些催化剂的性能和寿命，满足工业应用需求。
