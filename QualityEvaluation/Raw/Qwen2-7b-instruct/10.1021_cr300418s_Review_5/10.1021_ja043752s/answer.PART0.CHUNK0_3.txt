<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Raman spectroscopy contributes significantly to the comprehensive characterization and understanding of doped catalysts. It aids in elucidating the composition, structure, in situ behavior, and reactivity of these materials. By correlating spectral features with catalyst properties, such as reducibility, Raman spectroscopy provides insights into how dopants influence the electronic structure and bonding environment of catalysts. This technique is particularly useful for studying catalysts like Ce-doped CoCr2O4 and alkali-doped vanadia on titania, revealing how these dopants enhance catalytic activity and modify the catalyst's behavior under reaction conditions.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
