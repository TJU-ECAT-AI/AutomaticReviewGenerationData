<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Raman spectroscopy, when used in conjunction with other analytical methods, contributes significantly to the comprehensive characterization and understanding of doped catalysts. It offers insights into the catalysts' composition, structure, in situ behavior, and reactivity. Specifically, Raman spectroscopy aids in analyzing the spectral features associated with the reducibility of catalysts, as demonstrated in studies on Ce-doped CoCr2O4 and alkali-doped vanadia on titania.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
