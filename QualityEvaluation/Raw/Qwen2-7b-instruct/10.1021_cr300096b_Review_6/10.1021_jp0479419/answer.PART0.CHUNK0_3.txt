<p>These reactions on the Co/Pt(111) surfaces are further compared to our earlier studies of the Ni/Pt(111) surfaces. The novel activity of the 1 ML Co/Pt(111) surface is explained by the modification of the Pt d-band center by the subsurface Co atoms.</p>
<p>In addition, several studies of the structural properties of CoPt single-crystal alloys have shown similar properties to that of NiPt single-crystal alloys. 10,11 Therefore, the CoPt system is a good system to compare with the Ni/Pt(111) system.</p>
<p>These results show that Co diffuses into Pt(111) at low temperatures.</p>
<p>The calculated segregation energies of Co in Pt(111) indicate that Co atoms are more stable in the bulk of Pt(111) than on the surface.</p>
<p>These calculations clearly show that incorporating subsurface Co or Ni into a Pt(111) substrate changes the electronic properties of the surface Pt d-band and the adsorption properties of hydrogen.</p>
<p>On Co/Pt(111) surfaces with approximately 1 ML Co coverage, cyclohexene self-hydrogenates to form cyclohexane at 221 K.</p>
<p>The novel activities of the 1 ML Co/Pt(111) surfaces can be attributed to the presence of subsurface Co atoms, which modify the electronic properties of the Pt(111) surface.</p>
