[1] "This research employs techniques such as HREELS and AES to verify and quantify the oxygen-induced surface segregation of subsurface 3d atoms, particularly Ni and Co, in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems."
          [2] "The measured segregation kinetics and activation barriers are compared with DFT predictions for these systems."

[1] "The significance of the study lies in its investigation of the effects of oxide support and support surface area on the catalytic activity of Pt/Ni bimetallic catalysts supported on zirconia with low and high surface areas."
          [2] "Characterization results from a previous study on γ-Al2O3-supported Pt/Ni bimetallic catalysts suggest that the bimetallic catalyst nanoparticles exhibit a structure with Pt residing on the top-most surface, which closely resembles the Pt-terminated model surface identified in surface science studies."
          [3] "In the presence of adsorbed atomic hydrogen, the PtNiPt(1 1 1) surface is thermodynamically stable, whereas in the presence of adsorbed oxygen, Ni segregates to the surface to form the NiPtPt(1 1 1) structure."
          [4] "The thermodynamic stability of these Pt/Ni surface configurations is also of interest because both computational and experimental studies have shown that the Ni monolayer can migrate in the presence of adsorbates."
          [5] "The activation barrier for the transition between the Pt- and Ni-terminated configurations is relatively low, 15 kcal/mol, implying that the transformation can take place at temperatures relevant to calcination, reduction, or reaction during synthesis and testing of supported catalysts."
