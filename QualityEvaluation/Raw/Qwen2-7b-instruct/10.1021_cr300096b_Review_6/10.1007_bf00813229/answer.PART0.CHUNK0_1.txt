<p>The interaction of sulfur with Pt(111), Zn/Pt(111) and Cu/Pt(111) has been examined using X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS), X-ray excited Auger electron spectroscopy (XAES), and thermal desorption mass spectroscopy (TDS).</p>
<p>At temperatures between 300 and 600 K, the exposure of Pt(111) to S2 gas produces a chemisorbed layer of sulfurwithout the formation of bulk sulfides.</p>
<p>Exposure of S2 to a Zn/Pt(111) alloy, at room temperature, results in a breakdown of the alloy and formation of a zinc-sulfide film on Pt(111).</p>
<p>Further S2 exposure at 550 K sulfidizes the remaining metallic zincwithout affecting platinum.</p>
<p>For the Cu/Pt(111) surface alloy, on the other hand, exposure to S22 at 550 K leads to sulfidation of the platinum.</p>
<p>Platinum can effectively compete for sulfur atoms bonded to copper but not for those bonded to zinc.</p>
<p>The reaction of S2 gas with Cu/Pt(111) surfaces produces copper sulfides that promote the sulfidation of Pt by providing surface sites for the dissociation of S2, and by favoring the diffusion of S into the bulk of the system.</p>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The study utilized X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS), X-ray excited Auger electron spectroscopy (XAES), and thermal desorption mass spectroscopy (TDS) to investigate the interaction of sulfur with various surfaces, including Pt(111), Zn/Pt(111), and Cu/Pt(111). The research showed that sulfur exposure to Pt(111) resulted in a chemisorbed layer without forming bulk sulfides. With Zn/Pt(111), sulfur led to the breakdown of the alloy and formation of a zinc-sulfide film. On the Cu/Pt(111) surface, sulfur promoted the sulfidation of platinum, highlighting the ability of platinum to compete for sulfur atoms bound to copper but not to zinc. Copper sulfides produced by the reaction of S2 gas with Cu/Pt(111) surfaces facilitated the sulfidation of platinum by providing sites for sulfur dissociation and enhancing sulfur diffusion into the bulk system.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

研究利用X射线光电子能谱（XPS）、X射线激发阿格电子能谱（XAES）和热脱附质谱（TDS）等技术，对硫与Pt(111)、Zn/Pt(111)和Cu/Pt(111)表面的相互作用进行了考察。研究表明，硫暴露于Pt(111)表面形成吸附层，未生成硫化物。对于Zn/Pt(111)，硫导致合金分解并形成锌硫化物薄膜在Pt(111)上。在Cu/Pt(111)表面，硫促进了铂的硫化。这表明铂能够与铜结合的硫原子竞争，但不能与锌结合的硫原子竞争。Cu/Pt(111)表面上S2气体反应产生的铜硫化物为铂的硫化提供了表面位点，并促进了硫的扩散进入体系内部。
