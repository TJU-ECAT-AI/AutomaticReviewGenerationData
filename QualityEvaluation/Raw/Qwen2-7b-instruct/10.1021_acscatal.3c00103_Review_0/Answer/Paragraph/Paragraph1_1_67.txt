            <div>Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) techniques address the growing industrial demand for propylene and limitations in traditional methods by efficiently converting propane into propylene. Innovations in catalyst design, such as the use of late transition metals, main-group metals, and rare-earth metal oxides, enhance stability and recyclability. In oxidative PDH, alloying can weaken propylene binding energy, while in nonoxidative PDH, embedding reactive Rh atoms alters dehydrogenation pathways. These strategies address catalyst deactivation challenges, improving efficiency and flexibility in propylene production.</div>
            <div>Notably, single-atom-doped Ga2O3 catalysts represent a significant advancement in the field of propane dehydrogenation. By leveraging the Lewis acid-base interactions, these catalysts offer improved performance and selectivity towards propylene production. This approach aims to overcome deactivation issues and enhance stability and recyclability in both oxidative and nonoxidative PDH processes. The rational design of these catalysts optimizes the electronic structure and coordination environment for enhanced catalytic activity, contributing significantly to the global energy landscape.</div>
            <div>Moreover, the utilization of metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) derived catalysts for nonoxidative PDH processes showcases promise. These catalysts demonstrate enhanced activity, selectivity, and stability compared to conventional catalysts, addressing the challenges posed by traditional PDH processes. The development of these catalysts aims to overcome deactivation issues, improve stability, and increase recyclability, making PDH a sustainable solution for propylene production.</div>
            <div>Additionally, the study of catalyst design strategies for propane dehydrogenation includes the exploration of sintering rates and mechanisms through multiscale simulation. Researchers have gained insights into how factors such as temperature, particle size, and support material properties influence the sintering rate, informing the development of innovative catalyst design strategies to maintain high activity levels while minimizing risks of agglomeration and deactivation.</div>
            <div>Lastly, the exploitation of low-cost and green catalyst systems with good stability for PDH is necessary and urgent. Innovations in catalyst design, especially utilizing ZnO catalysts supported on high-silica HZSM-5 zeolites, aim to enhance stability and recyclability, overcoming deactivation issues. The ease of preparation, low cost, and environmental friendliness of these catalysts represent a promising avenue for PDH, aligning with efforts to develop green catalyst systems.</div>

Additionally, there is a need for further understanding of the fundamental mechanisms governing the activity and stability of PDH catalysts, which could lead to the design of catalysts with tailored properties for specific applications. These developments would not only improve the efficiency and sustainability of PDH processes but also contribute to the broader goal of reducing dependence on fossil fuels and mitigating environmental impacts.</div>

            <div>在传统的生产方法面临的挑战以及工业对丙烯日益增长的需求背景下，丙烷脱氢（PDH）技术通过高效地将丙烷转化为丙烯，有效地解决了这一问题。在催化剂设计上的创新，如使用晚期过渡金属、主族金属和稀土金属氧化物，提高了稳定性和可回收性。在氧化PDH过程中，合金化可以削弱丙烯的结合能；而在非氧化PDH过程中，嵌入活性Rh原子改变了脱氢途径。这些策略解决了催化剂失活的问题，提高了丙烯生产的效率和灵活性。</div>
            <div>特别值得一提的是，单原子掺杂的Ga2O3催化剂在PDH领域代表了显著的进步。通过利用Lewis酸-碱相互作用，这些催化剂提供了提高性能和丙烯选择性的可能性。这种方法旨在克服失活问题，增强稳定性和可回收性，在氧化和非氧化PDH过程中都具有重要作用。这种催化剂的设计理解决定了电子结构和配位环境的优化，从而提高了催化活性，对全球能源格局产生了重大贡献。</div>
            <div>此外，金属有机框架（MOF）衍生的催化剂在非氧化PDH过程中的应用展示了巨大的潜力。与传统催化剂相比，这些催化剂表现出增强的活性、选择性和稳定性，有效解决了传统PDH过程面临的问题。发展这些催化剂旨在克服失活问题，提高稳定性和增加可回收性，使PDH成为丙烯生产的可持续解决方案。</div>
            <div>研究还探讨了通过多尺度模拟来研究催化剂设计策略的成核率和机制。研究人员获得了温度、颗粒尺寸和支持材料性质等如何影响成核率的见解，为开发能够维持高活性同时最小化聚集和失活风险的创新催化剂设计策略提供了信息。</div>
            <div>最后，低成本和绿色的催化剂系统以良好的稳定性用于PDH是必要的且迫切需要的。在高硅HZSM-5沸石上支持的ZnO催化剂的创新，旨在增强稳定性和可回收性，克服失活问题。这些催化剂的易于制备、低成本和环保特性代表了PDH的一个有前景的方向，符合发展绿色催化剂系统的努力。</div>

            <div>综合理解与未来展望：考虑到文件中讨论的先进成果和发现，我们有机会探索新兴领域和创新方法。未来研究的努力可能集中在开发具有特定丙烯衍生物选择性的催化剂、探索多种金属成分的协同效应以及研究利用可再生能源为PDH过程供电的可能性。此外，还需要进一步了解PDH催化剂的基本机制，这可能导致设计具有定制属性以满足特定应用的催化剂。这些发展不仅会提高PDH过程的效率和可持续性，而且还会促进更广泛的目标——减少对化石燃料的依赖并减轻环境影响。</div>
