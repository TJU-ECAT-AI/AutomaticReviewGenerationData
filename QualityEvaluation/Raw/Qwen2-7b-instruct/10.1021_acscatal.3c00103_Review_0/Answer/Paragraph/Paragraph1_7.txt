        The evolution of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as a promising alternative for propylene production has been significantly shaped by the challenges posed by increasing industrial demand and the limitations of traditional production methods. This review focuses on the advancements in catalyst design strategies that address the critical issue of catalyst deactivation, enhance stability, and improve recyclability in both oxidative and non-oxidative PDH processes. It draws upon several key studies, each contributing to a deeper understanding of PDH techniques and the innovative approaches they employ.

        The first reference \[10.1021/acs.langmuir.1c01628\] delves into the study of the sintering rate and mechanism of supported Pt nanoparticles, highlighting the influence of temperature, particle size, and support material properties on this process. This research underscores the need for innovative catalyst design strategies that can maintain high activity levels while mitigating the risk of agglomeration and deactivation, thereby extending the lifetime of the catalyst.

        Reference \[10.1021/cs5003417\] explores how PDH techniques address the challenge of increasing industrial demand for propylene by offering an efficient alternative to traditional production methods. It emphasizes the development of catalyst design strategies, particularly those involving well-defined supported vanadium oxide catalysts, which aim to overcome deactivation, enhance stability, and improve recyclability in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane.

        The study \[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508\] introduces the use of ZnO catalysts supported on high-silica HZSM-5 zeolites as a promising solution for PDH. This approach demonstrates the superiority of these catalysts in terms of catalytic performance and reaction stability, offering a sustainable and cost-effective solution for industrial propylene production.

        Another reference \[10.1039/C5SC01227A\] showcases the potential of propane dehydrogenation as an alternative for propylene production, focusing on the development of innovative catalyst design strategies. This includes the use of promoters such as late transition metals, main-group metals (Sn, Ga), alkali-metal oxides, and rare-earth metal oxides to enhance stability and recyclability in PDH processes.

        The paper \[10.1021/acscatal.0c05009\] discusses the increasing industrial demand for propylene and the limitations of traditional production methods, leading to the development of innovative catalyst design strategies. It highlights the importance of PDH techniques in addressing these challenges by providing an alternative method for propylene production that overcomes deactivation issues, enhances stability, and improves recyclability.

        Reference \[10.1021/an...23\] emphasizes the role of supported vanadium catalysts in PDH processes, particularly focusing on the importance of hydroxyl groups on the catalyst surface for enhancing stability and reducing coke formation. This study employs surface structure manipulation and density functional theory (DFT) calculations to optimize catalyst design and advance PDH processes.

        Lastly, the study \[10.1016/j.catcom.2009.09.011\] investigates the activation of carbon dioxide in the dehydrogenation process, demonstrating that catalyst properties, such as medium-strength basicity and acidity, are crucial for the activation of both propane and carbon dioxide. This highlights the importance of catalyst optimization in achieving efficient PDH processes.

        The integrative understanding and prospective outlook suggest that the advancements discussed in these studies pave the way for future research directions. They underscore the potential for exploring emerging fields and innovative methodologies, such as the development of novel catalyst materials, enhanced reaction conditions, and advanced process control strategies. Future efforts might focus on integrating these strategies to create more efficient, sustainable, and economically viable PDH processes, contributing to the evolving global energy landscape.

    <Chinese>
        在工业对丙烯日益增长的需求以及传统生产方法限制的背景下，丙烷脱氢（PDH）技术作为丙烯生产的一种有前景的替代方案，通过高效地将丙烷转化为丙烯，显著推动了这一领域的进步。本文着重探讨了针对催化剂失活、增强稳定性和提高可回收性而发展出的创新催化剂设计策略在氧化性和非氧化性PDH过程中的应用。我们引用了多个关键研究，旨在深入理解PDH技术及其创新方法，并揭示这些方法如何解决挑战并促进可持续性。

        参考文献 \[10.1021/acs.langmuir.1c01628\] 针对支持型铂纳米粒子的烧结速率和机制进行了深入研究，指出温度、颗粒大小和支持材料性质对这一过程的影响。这项研究强调了在保持高活性的同时避免团聚和失活风险、延长催化剂寿命的创新催化剂设计策略的重要性。

        文献 \[10.1021/cs5003417\] 探讨了PDH技术如何通过提供一种高效的丙烯生产替代方法来应对工业需求的增长，以及通过使用结构明确的二氧化钒基催化剂来克服失活、增强稳定性和提高可回收性在氧化性丙烷脱氢过程中。这表明了在提高效率和减少环境影响方面，PDH技术具有巨大的潜力。

        研究 \[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508\] 引入了在高硅铝酸盐H-ZSM-5沸石上支持的锌氧化物催化剂作为PDH的有前景解决方案。这种方法证明了这些催化剂在催化性能和反应稳定性方面的优势，为工业规模的丙烯生产提供了可持续且经济有效的解决方案。

        另一项研究 \[10.1039/C5SC01227A\] 展示了PDH作为一种替代途径的潜力，重点是通过晚期过渡金属、主族金属（Sn、Ga）、碱金属氧化物和稀土金属氧化物的使用来增强稳定性和可回收性，从而提高PDH过程的效率。

        论文 \[10.1021/acscatal.0c05009\] 分析了工业对丙烯的持续需求以及传统生产方法的局限性，导致了创新催化剂设计策略的发展。它强调了PDH技术如何通过提供一种能够克服失活问题、增强稳定性和提高可回收性的丙烯生产替代方法，以满足这些挑战。

        参考文献 \[10.1021/an...23\] 强调了在PDH过程中支持型钒催化剂的作用，特别是表面结合的羟基群在增强稳定性和减少焦炭形成方面的关键作用。通过表面结构的调整和密度泛函理论（DFT）计算，优化了催化剂设计，促进了PDH过程的发展。

        最后，研究 \[10.1016/j.catcom.2009.09.011\] 探讨了二氧化碳在脱氢过程中的激活，指出催化剂特性，如中等强度的基本性和酸性，对于激活丙烷和二氧化碳至关重要。这凸显了在实现高效PDH过程时催化剂优化的重要性。

        结合这些研究的综合理解与未来展望表明，这些进展为未来的研究方向铺平了道路。它们强调了探索新兴领域和创新方法的可能性，例如开发新型催化剂材料、改进反应条件和高级过程控制策略。未来的工作可能集中在整合这些策略，创建更高效、可持续且经济可行的PDH过程，以适应不断演变的全球能源格局。
