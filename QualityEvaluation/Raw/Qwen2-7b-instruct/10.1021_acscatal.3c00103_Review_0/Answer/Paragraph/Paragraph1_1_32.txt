For instance, the use of late transition metals, main-group metals, and rare-earth metal oxides enhances the catalytic properties of PDH catalysts. In oxidative PDH, alloying can weaken the propylene binding energy, while in non-oxidative PDH, embedding reactive Rh atoms alters the dehydrogenation pathways. These strategies not only improve the efficiency and flexibility of propylene production but also contribute to the sustainability of global energy landscapes.
