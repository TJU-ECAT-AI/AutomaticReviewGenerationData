            The evolution of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as a promising alternative for propylene production is critically examined in the context of increasing industrial demand and changing global energy landscapes. This review emphasizes the pivotal role of innovative catalyst design strategies in addressing the challenges of deactivation, enhancing stability, and improving recyclability, especially in both oxidative and nonoxidative PDH processes. 

            Innovative catalyst design strategies, such as the utilization of late transition metals, main-group metals, and rare-earth metal oxides, significantly enhance the stability and recyclability of PDH catalysts. In oxidative PDH, alloying can weaken the propylene binding energy, leading to improved efficiency and selectivity. Conversely, in nonoxidative PDH, the embedding of reactive Rh atoms alters dehydrogenation pathways, thereby improving the overall process. These strategies collectively address the catalyst deactivation issues, improving the efficiency and flexibility of propylene production.

            The rational design of single-atom-doped Ga2O3 catalysts represents a significant advancement, offering improved performance and selectivity towards propylene production through optimized electronic structure and coordination environment. The use of MOFs-derived iron catalysts provides another avenue for more efficient and environmentally friendly PDH processes, demonstrating promise in overcoming deactivation issues, enhancing stability, and increasing recyclability.

            In the realm of CO2 utilization, PDH techniques show potential in converting CO2 to CO and O2, highlighting the versatility of these processes in addressing both industrial demands and environmental concerns. The development of catalysts that can activate CO2 at specific temperatures, while simultaneously undergoing the dehydrogenation process, is crucial for the sustainable production of propylene.

            The incorporation of hydroxyl groups on the surface of catalysts, as exemplified by the VOx/Al2O3 catalyst, plays a fundamental role in the catalytic performance of PDH reactions. These hydroxyl groups are produced under H2 pre-reduction, affecting the concentration of VOH species, which in turn influences the catalytic activity and resistance to coke formation. The evolution of these groups under H2 treatment and their role in the catalytic performance underscores the importance of surface chemistry in PDH.

            The utilization of ZnO catalysts supported on high-silica HZSM-5 zeolites represents a promising low-cost, environmentally-friendly, and easily-prepared solution for PDH. This catalyst system exhibits great catalytic activity and reaction stability, offering a new direction for designing highly-efficient transition metal catalysts for PDH reactions.

            Lastly, the exploration of sintering rate and mechanism of supported Pt nanoparticles through multiscale simulation provides insights into the complex interactions between nanoparticles and their support material, enabling the development of strategies to overcome deactivation issues and enhance stability. This understanding is crucial for maintaining high activity levels while minimizing the risk of agglomeration and deactivation, extending the lifetime of the catalyst.

            The integrative understanding and prospective outlook section suggests that future research might focus on exploring emerging fields and innovative methodologies, such as the integration of AI-driven catalyst design, the utilization of renewable energy sources in PDH processes, and the development of multifunctional catalysts that combine PDH and CO2 utilization. These advancements could lead to more sustainable, efficient, and environmentally friendly propylene production methods, positioning PDH as a key player in the global energy landscape.

            根据参考文献\[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]进行了深入分析，并整合了来自\[10.1021/acscatal.6b00893\], \[10.1021/acscatal.0c05454\], \[10.1016/j.catcom.2009.09.011\], \[10.1021/acscatal.0c05009\], \[10.1021/anie.201800123\], \[10.1002/anie.201800123\], \[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508\], \[10.1021/acs.langmuir.1c01628\]和\[10.1021/cs5003417\]的内容。

            在不断增长的工业需求和全球能源格局变化的背景下，丙烷脱氢（PDH）作为一种有前景的丙烯生产替代方案，得到了深入探讨。本文重点强调了创新催化剂设计策略在解决催化剂失活、提高稳定性和增强可回收性方面的关键作用，特别是在氧化性和非氧化性PDH过程中。

            利用晚期过渡金属、主族金属和稀土金属氧化物等创新催化剂设计策略显著提高了PDH催化剂的稳定性和可回收性。在氧化性PDH中，合金化可以削弱丙烯结合能，从而提高效率和选择性。相反，在非氧化性PDH中，嵌入活性Rh原子改变了脱氢途径，从而改善了整体过程。这些策略共同解决了催化剂失活问题，提高了丙烯生产的效率和灵活性。

            合理设计单原子掺杂的Ga2O3催化剂代表了一个显著的进步，通过优化电子结构和配位环境，提供改进的性能和丙烯生产选择性。MOFs衍生的铁催化剂提供了更高效、环保的PDH过程的另一个途径，显示了克服失活问题、提高稳定性和增加可回收性的潜力。

            在利用CO2方面，PDH技术显示出将CO2转化为CO和O2的潜力，突显了这些过程在满足工业需求和环境保护方面的灵活性。能够激活CO2在特定温度下同时进行脱氢过程的催化剂对于可持续的丙烯生产方法至关重要。

            表面含有羟基的催化剂，如VOx/Al2O3催化剂，对PDH反应的催化性能起着决定性的作用。这些羟基在H2预还原下产生，影响VOH物种的浓度，进而影响催化活性和焦炭形成抗性。这些组分在H2处理下的演化及其对催化性能的影响强调了表面化学在PDH中的重要性。

            利用高硅HZSM-5沸石支持的ZnO催化剂代表了一种有前景的低成本、环境友好且易于制备的PDH解决方案。这种催化剂系统表现出出色的催化活性和反应稳定性，为设计高效过渡金属催化剂开辟了新方向。

            最后，通过多尺度模拟探索支持Pt纳米颗粒的烧结速率和机制提供了对纳米颗粒与其支撑材料之间复杂相互作用的理解，这为克服失活问题、提高稳定性和延长催化剂寿命的发展策略提供了关键信息。

            集成理解和前瞻展望部分建议未来研究可能关注探索新兴领域和创新方法，如AI驱动的催化剂设计、利用可再生能源的PDH过程以及结合PDH和CO2利用的多功能催化剂。这些进展可能导致更加可持续、高效和环境友好的丙烯生产方法，使PDH成为全球能源格局的关键参与者。
