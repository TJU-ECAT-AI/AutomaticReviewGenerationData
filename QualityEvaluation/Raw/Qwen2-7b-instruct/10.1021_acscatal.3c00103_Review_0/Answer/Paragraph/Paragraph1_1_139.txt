        Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) techniques address the escalating industrial demand for propylene and the constraints of traditional production methods by offering an alternative route for propylene synthesis. These methods tackle the issues of catalyst deactivation, enhance stability, and improve recyclability in both oxidative and nonoxidative PDH processes. Innovations in catalyst design, such as the incorporation of late transition metals, main-group metals, and rare-earth metal oxides, have been pivotal in achieving these goals.

        In oxidative PDH, alloying can weaken the binding energy of propylene, while in nonoxidative PDH, embedding reactive rhodium atoms can alter the dehydrogenation pathways. These strategies effectively address the challenges posed by catalyst deactivation, thereby improving the efficiency and flexibility of propylene production.

        The rational design of single-atom-doped Ga2O3 catalysts represents a significant advancement in the field of propane dehydrogenation. This approach aims to overcome deactivation issues and enhance stability and recyclability in both oxidative and nonoxidative PDH processes by leveraging Lewis acid-base interactions. By optimizing the electronic structure and coordination environment of the catalysts, these strategies offer improved performance and selectivity toward propylene production.

        The development of MOF-derived iron catalysts for nonoxidative PDH is another notable innovation. These catalysts demonstrate enhanced activity, selectivity, and stability compared to conventional catalysts, making them a promising solution for sustainable propylene production. The development of these catalysts aims to address the challenges posed by traditional PDH processes, such as high energy consumption and environmental concerns, while also improving stability and recyclability.

        The utilization of vanadium-based catalysts, especially those supported on aluminum oxide, has also shown promise in nonoxidative PDH. The surface hydroxyl groups on these catalysts significantly contribute to their stability and coke reduction capabilities, enhancing the efficiency of the PDH process. Research has focused on understanding the role of these hydroxyl groups and their evolution under reaction conditions, which has led to the optimization of catalyst design for improved catalytic performance.

        Lastly, the exploration of cobalt-based catalysts for PDH has emerged as a promising direction, particularly those featuring isolated active sites. These catalysts leverage the earth abundance of cobalt and offer a sustainable solution for propylene production, addressing the limitations associated with hazardous chromium and costly platinum in traditional catalysts.

        The evolution of propane dehydrogenation as a promising alternative for propylene production is a testament to the ongoing advancements in catalyst design and development. These innovations not only tackle the challenges of increasing industrial demand and changing global energy landscapes but also pave the way for more sustainable and efficient propylene production methods.

        管道脱氢（PDH）技术通过提供一种替代路线来解决丙烯工业需求的快速增长和传统生产方法的限制。这些方法通过处理催化剂失活、提高稳定性和提高可回收性，在氧化和非氧化PDH过程中克服了挑战。在催化剂设计方面的创新，如引入晚期过渡金属、主族金属和稀土金属氧化物，对于实现这些目标至关重要。

        在氧化PDH中，合金可以削弱丙烯的结合能，而在非氧化PDH中，嵌入反应性铑原子可以改变脱氢路径。这些策略有效地解决了催化剂失活带来的挑战，从而提高了丙烯生产的效率和灵活性。

        单原子掺杂Ga2O3催化剂的理性设计代表了管道脱氢领域的一个重要进展。这种方法旨在通过Lewis酸-碱相互作用克服失活问题，并增强在氧化和非氧化PDH过程中的稳定性和可回收性。通过优化催化剂的电子结构和配位环境，这些策略提供了更高的性能和选择性，以产生丙烯。

        MOF衍生的铁催化剂用于非氧化PDH是另一个值得关注的创新。这些催化剂在活性、选择性和稳定性方面均优于传统催化剂，为可持续丙烯生产提供了有前景的解决方案。这些催化剂的发展旨在解决传统PDH过程的挑战，如高能耗和环境问题，同时提高稳定性和可回收性。

        利用范德姆催化剂，尤其是那些支持在铝氧化物上的催化剂，在非氧化PDH中显示出希望。这些催化剂表面的羟基对他们的稳定性和焦炭减少能力有显著贡献，增强了PDH过程的效率。研究已经集中于理解这些羟基的作用及其在反应条件下的演变，这导致了对优化催化剂设计以提高催化性能的优化。

        最后，钴基催化剂在PDH中的应用已成为一个有前景的方向，特别是那些具有孤立活性位点的催化剂。这些催化剂利用钴的地球丰富性，提供了一种可持续的丙烯生产方式，解决了传统催化剂中危险的铬和昂贵的铂的局限性。

        PDH作为丙烯生产有前景的替代方案的演变证明了催化剂设计和开发持续进步的重要性。这些创新不仅解决了增加工业需求和全球能源格局变化的挑战，而且还为更高效和可持续的丙烯生产方法铺平了道路。
