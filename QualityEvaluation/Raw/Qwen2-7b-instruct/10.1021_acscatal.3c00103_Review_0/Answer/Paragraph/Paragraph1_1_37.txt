        Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) techniques address the increasing industrial demand for propylene and limitations of traditional methods by offering a promising alternative for propylene production. Innovations in catalyst design strategies tackle deactivation issues, enhance stability, and improve recyclability in both oxidative and nonoxidative PDH processes. Catalysts like late transition metals, main-group metals, and rare-earth metal oxides are developed to meet these needs. In oxidative PDH, alloying weakens the propylene binding energy, while in nonoxidative PDH, embedding reactive Rh atoms alters the dehydrogenation pathway. The rational design of single-atom-doped Ga2O3 catalysts leverages Lewis acid-base interactions to improve performance and selectivity towards propylene production. Additionally, MOF-derived iron catalysts offer enhanced activity, selectivity, and stability compared to conventional catalysts, contributing significantly to sustainable propylene production.

        The development of these catalysts aims to overcome deactivation issues, improve stability, and increase recyclability, addressing the challenges posed by traditional PDH processes and contributing to the sustainable production of propylene. This evolution in PDH techniques represents a promising alternative in the context of the evolving energy landscape.

        在面对工业对丙烯日益增长的需求和传统生产方法的局限性时，丙烷脱氢（PDH）技术提供了一个有前景的替代方案，用于丙烯的生产。在催化剂设计策略上的创新解决了催化剂失活问题，增强了稳定性和可回收性，并在氧化性和非氧化性PDH过程中提高了性能。诸如晚期过渡金属、主族金属和稀土金属氧化物之类的催化剂被开发以满足这些需求。在氧化PDH中，合金化减弱了丙烯的结合能，在非氧化PDH中，嵌入活性Rh原子改变了脱氢途径。单原子掺杂Ga2O3催化剂通过Lewis酸碱相互作用提高了性能和丙烯选择性。此外，MOF衍生的铁催化剂与传统催化剂相比，表现出更高的活性、选择性和稳定性，对可持续丙烯生产做出了重大贡献。

        这些催化剂的发展旨在克服失活问题，提高稳定性和增加可回收性，从而解决传统PDH过程面临的挑战，并为丙烯的可持续生产做出贡献。PDH技术的这一演变代表了在不断变化的能量景观背景下的一个有前景的替代方案。
