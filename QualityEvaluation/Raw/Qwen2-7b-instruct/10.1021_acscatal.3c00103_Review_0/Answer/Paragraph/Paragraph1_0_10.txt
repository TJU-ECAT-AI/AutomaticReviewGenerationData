        Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) techniques address growing industrial propylene demand and traditional production limitations by efficiently converting propane to propylene, tackling deactivation issues, enhancing stability, and improving recyclability through innovative catalyst design in both oxidative and nonoxidative processes. This evolution in PDH aims to meet the challenges posed by increased industrial needs and evolving global energy landscapes, as highlighted in recent advancements and discussions.

        The growing demand for propylene and the increasing reserve of propane from shale-gas deposits are drawing attention to propane dehydrogenation (PDH). Commercial PDH processes operate at temperatures above 500°C and are generally based on the Catofin process with Cr/Al2O3 catalyst and the Oleflex process with PtSn/Al2O3 catalyst. However, the severe toxicity of Cr and the high price of Pt greatly restrict the further development of the PDH industry, and the demand for new catalysts is increasingly urgent. Cobalt-based catalysts have been shown to be promising for PDH, and the earth abundance of element Co bodes well for long-term utility.

        Innovations in catalyst design, particularly the use of ZnO catalysts supported on high-silica HZSM-5 zeolites, aim to mitigate deactivation challenges and enhance stability, making PDH a promising solution. These catalysts, highlighted for their superior selectivity and stability, represent a low-cost, green approach to propylene production, offering environmental benefits and cost-effectiveness.

        Along with the decreasing fossil oil reserves and development on utilization of natural gas and shale gas, propane dehydrogenation (PDH) seems to be a promising alternative to produce propylene. The main problems of Pt-based catalysts are their low selectivity to propylene and fast deactivation caused by coke formation. Recent studies highlight the potential of MOF-derived iron catalysts in nonoxidative PDH processes, offering enhanced activity, selectivity, and stability compared to conventional catalysts.

        Rational design of single-atom-doped Ga2O3 catalysts represents a significant advancement in the field of propane dehydrogenation. This approach aims to overcome the deactivation issues and enhance the stability and recyclability of catalysts in both oxidative and nonoxidative PDH processes by leveraging the Lewis acid-base interactions.

        炔烃脱氢化（PDH）技术通过高效地将丙烷转化为丙烯，解决工业需求增长和传统生产方法的限制，解决了丙烯生产中的挑战，并通过创新催化剂设计在氧化性和非氧化性PDH过程中应对了活性损耗问题、增强了稳定性和提高了可回收性。这一PDH的演变旨在满足工业需求增加和全球能源格局变化的挑战，正如最近的进展和讨论所强调的那样。

        随着对丙烯日益增长的需求和从天然气和页岩气储备中增加的丙烷储备，丙烷脱氢化（PDH）似乎是一个有前景的丙烯生产替代方案。商业PDH过程通常在500°C以上的温度下进行，基于Cr/Al2O3催化剂的Catofin过程和基于PtSn/Al2O3催化剂的Oleflex过程。然而，Cr的严重毒性以及Pt的高昂价格极大地限制了PDH行业的进一步发展，对于新型催化剂的需求日益迫切。钴基催化剂已被证明是PDH的有前景选择，且Co元素的地球丰富性预示着其长期实用性。

        通过ZnO催化剂支持在高硅HZSM-5沸石上的创新催化剂设计，旨在缓解活性损耗问题、增强稳定性和提高可回收性，使PDH成为有前途的解决方案。这些催化剂因其优越的选择性和稳定性而被强调，代表了一种低成本、绿色的丙烯生产途径，提供了环境效益和成本效益。

        伴随着化石油资源的减少和天然气及页岩气利用的发展，PDH似乎成为了生产丙烯的一个有前景的替代方案。Pt基催化剂的主要问题是其丙烯选择性低以及由于焦炭形成导致的快速失活。近期研究表明，MOF衍生铁催化剂在非氧化性PDH过程中具有潜在优势，与传统催化剂相比，它们提供增强的活性、选择性和稳定性。

        合理设计单原子掺杂Ga2O3催化剂代表了脱氢化领域的重大进步。这种方法旨在通过Lewis酸碱相互作用克服活性损耗问题，增强催化剂在氧化性和非氧化性PDH过程中的稳定性和可回收性。
