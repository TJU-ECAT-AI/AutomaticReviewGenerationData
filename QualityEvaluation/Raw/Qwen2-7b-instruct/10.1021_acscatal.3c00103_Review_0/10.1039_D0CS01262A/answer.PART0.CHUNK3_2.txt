<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) techniques address the challenges of increasing industrial demand for propylene by providing a more efficient and sustainable method of producing propylene compared to traditional methods. Traditional methods often involve high energy consumption and low selectivity, leading to the formation of unwanted byproducts. PDH, on the other hand, selectively converts propane to propylene through controlled dehydrogenation reactions. To overcome deactivation issues, enhance stability, and improve recyclability, innovative catalyst design strategies focus on optimizing the active sites for propylene formation. These strategies include:

1. **Adjusting Cluster Size and Valence State**: Small clusters with a low ratio of terminal VO sites to other oxygen sites increase propylene selectivity, while maintaining sufficient activity. Polymeric VOx with a high VO ratio can lower propylene selectivity but may improve activity. The balance between activity and selectivity is crucial for catalyst design.

2. **Utilizing Active Sites**: Terminal VO sites are key for acetone formation and further combustion to CO2. These sites also play a role in propylene formation. The presence of lattice oxygen on metal oxides is essential for CH bond activation, leading to the formation of desired propylene and undesired COx species.

3. **Incorporating Metal Additives**: Alkali metal additives can improve propylene selectivity by modifying the acid-base properties of the catalyst. Potassium, for example, inhibits consecutive propylene and parallel propane combustion, enhancing propylene selectivity.

4. **Exploring Other Catalysts**: Beyond vanadium-based catalysts, chromium, gallium, molybdenum, and mixed metal oxides are studied for their ability to perform redox reactions without structural collapse. These catalysts allow for the activation of CH bonds without significant loss of structure.

5. **Single Atom Oxide Hybrids**: Single-atom catalysts (SACs) offer unique properties due to their defined local structures, allowing for precise control over the catalytic activity and selectivity. Strategies like SMSI (Strong Metal-Support Interaction) are employed to keep single atoms from aggregating and to anchor them effectively on supports.

6. **Kinetic Analysis and Microkinetic Modeling**: Computational approaches using density functional theory (DFT) combined with descriptor-based scaling relationships aid in understanding the pathways of CH bond activation, olefin desorption, and deep CH bond activation. These models help in designing catalysts that optimize the trade-offs between activity, selectivity, and stability.

7. **Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence**: Advanced computational tools are being explored to accelerate the development of catalysts by predicting their performance and optimizing the catalyst composition and structure.

By employing these strategies, PDH techniques aim to address the challenges of industrial demand for propylene while improving efficiency, reducing environmental impact, and enhancing the recyclability of catalysts.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

在工业对丙烯需求增加和传统生产方法限制的背景下，丙烷脱氢（PDH）技术通过提供与传统方法相比更高效、更可持续的丙烯生产方法来应对这些挑战。传统方法往往消耗大量能源且选择性低，导致形成不必要的副产品。PDH通过受控的脱氢反应选择性地将丙烷转化为丙烯。为了克服失活问题、提高稳定性和增强可回收性，创新的催化剂设计策略集中在优化形成丙烯的活性位点上。这些策略包括：

1. **调整簇大小和价态状态**：较小的簇，其终端VO点与其他氧点的比例较低，可以增加丙烯的选择性，同时保持足够的活性。聚合的VOx具有较高的VO比率，可能会降低丙烯的选择性，但可能提高活性。活动性和选择性的平衡对于催化剂设计至关重要。

2. **利用活性位点**：终端VO位点在乙酸酮的形成以及进一步燃烧至二氧化碳中起关键作用。金属氧化物表面的晶格氧对于CH键激活至关重要，导致形成所需的丙烯和不希望的COx物种。

3. **整合金属添加剂**：碱金属添加剂可以通过修改催化剂的酸碱性质来改善丙烯的选择性。例如，钾可以抑制连续的丙烯和平行的丙烷燃烧，从而提高丙烯的选择性，并导致初期选择性达到95%。

4. **探索其他催化剂**：除了基于钒的催化剂外，铬、镓、钼和混合金属氧化物也正在研究用于丙烷脱氢，这些催化剂允许在没有结构崩溃的情况下发生还原反应。这些催化剂允许对CH键进行激活，而不会显著损失结构。

5. **单原子氧化物杂合体**：单原子催化剂（SACs）因其定义明确的局部结构而具有独特性质，允许对催化活性和选择性进行精确控制。例如，通过SMSI（强金属-支持相互作用）策略可以防止单原子聚集并有效锚定它们在支撑物上。

6. **动力学分析和微观动力学建模**：使用密度泛函理论（DFT）结合描述符基尺度关系的计算方法有助于理解CH键激活、烯烃脱附和深层CH键激活的途径。这些模型有助于设计优化活动性、选择性和稳定性的催化剂。

7. **机器学习和人工智能**：先进的计算工具正在被探索以加速催化剂的发展，预测其性能并优化催化剂组成和结构。

通过采用这些策略，PDH技术旨在解决工业对丙烯的需求挑战，同时提高效率、减少环境影响，并增强催化剂的可回收性。
