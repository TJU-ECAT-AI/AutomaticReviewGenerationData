<EnglishVersionAnswer>
To address the challenges of increasing industrial demand for propylene and limitations in traditional production methods, propane dehydrogenation (PDH) techniques utilize innovative catalyst design strategies. These strategies aim to overcome deactivation issues, enhance stability, and improve recyclability in both oxidative and nonoxidative PDH processes. The use of advanced catalysts, such as supported V2O5 catalysts, allows for high selectivity in the production of olefins like propylene. The key lies in the reactivity of oxygen and the interaction between the catalyst's metal oxide bonds and hydrocarbons to form oxygen vacancies and regenerate the catalyst. This MVK mechanism ensures efficient use of oxygen while maintaining the stability and recyclability of the catalysts, contributing to sustainable and efficient PDH processes.
