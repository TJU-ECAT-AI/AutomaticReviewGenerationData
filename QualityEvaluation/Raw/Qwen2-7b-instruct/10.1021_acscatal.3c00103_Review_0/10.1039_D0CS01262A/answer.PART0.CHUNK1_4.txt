To address the challenges of increasing industrial demand for propylene and limitations of traditional production methods, propane dehydrogenation (PDH) techniques utilize innovative catalyst design strategies. These strategies aim to overcome deactivation issues, enhance stability, and improve recyclability in both oxidative and nonoxidative PDH processes. For instance, researchers are developing catalysts that can selectively convert propane to propylene without unwanted side reactions, optimizing the catalytic activity and reducing the energy consumption. Innovative approaches include the use of supported metal oxides, metal organic frameworks (MOFs), and other advanced materials that offer enhanced surface area, improved thermal stability, and better selectivity. Additionally, catalysts are designed with specific properties to facilitate the activation of CH bonds in propane, leading to the formation of propylene through selective dehydrogenation reactions. These advancements contribute to more efficient and environmentally friendly PDH processes, supporting the growth of the petrochemical industry and ensuring a sustainable supply of propylene.

The bonding strength difference between paraffins and olefins plays a crucial role in determining the selectivity of olefin production. Ethane, due to its weaker CH bonds compared to ethylene, serves as the optimal feedstock for achieving high selectivity to olefins in the oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) process. The analysis at the molecular level reveals that the weakest CH bond in ethane has a bond energy of 4.35 eV, while the weakest CH bond in ethylene has an energy of 4.60 eV. This difference ensures that ethane can be selectively converted to ethylene under ODH conditions without undergoing over-oxidation to form CO and CO2. In contrast, propane and propylene have weaker CH bonds, which makes them more reactive towards oxygen, leading to a less favorable selectivity towards olefin production. The distinction between ethane and ethylene lies in their ability to maintain stability against oxygen, enabling ethane to serve as a superior feedstock for the production of olefins through ODH processes.

为应对工业对丙烯需求的增长以及传统生产方法的限制，丙烷脱氢（PDH）技术通过创新催化剂设计策略来解决这些问题。这些策略旨在克服失活问题、提高稳定性和增强可回收性，适用于氧化性和非氧化性PDH过程。例如，研究人员正在开发能够选择性地将丙烷转化为丙烯而不发生副反应的催化剂，优化催化活性并降低能耗。创新的方法包括使用支持金属氧化物、金属有机框架（MOFs）和其他先进材料，它们提供增强的比表面积、改进的热稳定性以及更好的选择性。此外，催化剂设计具有特定属性以促进丙烷中CH键的激活，从而通过选择性脱氢反应形成丙烯。这些进步有助于更高效的PDH过程，支持石化行业的增长，并确保丙烯的可持续供应。

分子水平的分析表明，在氧化性脱氢（ODH）过程中，由于其较弱的CH键，乙烷是实现高烯烃选择性的最佳原料。分析揭示了乙烷中最弱的CH键的键能为4.35 eV，而乙烯中最弱的CH键的键能为4.60 eV。这种差异确保了在ODH条件下，乙烷可以被选择性地转化为乙烯，而不会发生过氧化反应形成CO和CO2。相比之下，丙烷和丙烯的CH键较弱，这使得它们更容易与氧气反应，导致不利于向烯烃生产的选择性。乙烷与乙烯之间的区别在于它们抵抗氧气的能力，这使乙烷成为生产烯烃的更优原料，通过ODH过程。
