The primary hurdle lies in the accurate identification and quantification of Ga ions, compounded by the rapid exchange of Ga species with the surrounding environment and the intricacies involved in identifying stable Ga forms within the zeolite matrix. In situ or operando characterization techniques are indispensable in addressing these challenges, offering real-time insights into the catalyst's structure and reactivity dynamics during PDH reactions. These methodologies significantly advance our understanding of structure-activity relationships in these catalytic systems by providing critical information on the dynamic behavior of Ga, essential for optimizing catalytic activity.

        The pivotal role of in situ/operando characterization techniques becomes evident when considering the manifold challenges in elucidating Ga speciation. Techniques such as X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS), inductively coupled plasma optical emission spectrometry (ICP-OES), and nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) spectroscopy enable the real-time monitoring of Ga species and their changes over time, thereby facilitating a deeper understanding of Ga behavior in PDH catalysis. For instance, XPS offers high-resolution analysis of surface composition, while ICP-OES provides quantitative data on Ga concentration, and NMR spectroscopy elucidates the molecular environment of Ga ions. These methods collectively contribute to a more nuanced understanding of Ga speciation dynamics and its impact on catalytic performance.

        原位或操作中表征技术的关键作用体现在解决Ga物种阐明问题时变得显而易见。例如，X射线光电子能谱（XPS）、电感耦合等离子体光发射光谱（ICP-OES）和核磁共振（NMR）光谱法使研究人员能够实时监控Ga物种及其随时间的变化，从而更深入地了解Ga行为对于PDH催化的重要性。XPS提供了表面组成的高度分辨率分析，而ICP-OES提供了Ga浓度的定量数据，NMR光谱则揭示了Ga离子的分子环境。这些方法共同促进了对Ga物种动态特性的更细致理解及其对催化性能影响的深刻认识。
