1. The utilization of CO2 as a soft oxidant in catalytic reactions for CO2 abatement offers several advantages, including alleviating thermodynamic equilibrium limitations, minimizing hot spots on catalyst surfaces, reducing nonselective sites, accelerating reaction rates, enhancing selectivity, and suppressing total oxidation products.
2. The conversion of CO2 through catalytic reactions is considered one of the most promising methods for CO2 abatement.
3. CO2 has a significant positive impact on the global carbon balance when utilized as a source or non-traditional oxidant.
4. CO2 is being increasingly studied as an alternative oxidant or oxygen source in various industrial processes.
5. CO2 consumption positively affects the global carbon balance.
6. Unsaturated hydrocarbons, including alkenes, are crucial building blocks in chemical industries.
7. n-Butane, a paraffin, is less extensively studied in oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) processes utilizing CO2 as an oxidant.
8. Vanadium oxide-based materials are frequently employed as catalysts in ODH reactions.
9. Mixed oxide nanocomposites demonstrate enhanced ODH activity compared to their individual components due to improved acid-base bifunctionality.
10. The performance of vanadium oxide catalysts is influenced by the acid-base properties of their supporting materials.
