<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The study focuses on the activation and dissociation of carbon dioxide on ceria (110) surfaces using density functional theory (DFT). Computational methods were employed to investigate oxygen vacancy formation, CO2 adsorption on both stoichiometric and reduced ceria (110), electronic analysis of ceria as a catalyst for CO2 activation, and CO2 dissociation on reduced ceria (110). The conclusions summarize the research outcomes.
