        Comparative analysis of FexNiy/CeO2 and vanadium oxide catalysts in CO2-ODH reactions: Performance, selectivity, and the influence of supports and composition on reaction mechanisms and product formation

        Detailed analysis established from the study of reference [10.1080/01614949608006464]. Synthesized comprehension stemming from references [10.1021/la903836v], [10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052], and [10.1002/anie.201603556]. The study reveals that FexNiy/CeO2 catalysts, notably Fe3Ni1/CeO2, demonstrate enhanced activity and selectivity in CO2-ODH reactions compared to vanadium oxide catalysts. The performance of these catalysts is significantly influenced by the support materials, composition, and structural factors. Fe3Ni1/CeO2 catalysts exhibit superior performance due to an optimized structure and composition, yet challenges in scaling up production and cost-effectiveness remain.

        The role of structural defects, such as oxygen vacancies, enhances the catalytic properties of these catalysts. Potential for improvement lies in optimizing the catalyst composition and synthesis methods. By refining the catalyst composition and structure, there is an opportunity to address scalability and cost-effectiveness challenges.

        The study also highlights the effects of modifying iron catalysts with zinc and sodium. Zinc acts as a structural promoter, while sodium ions alter the electronic structure of the catalyst, facilitating CO activation and promoting the desorption of products. The introduction of sodium ions can significantly increase the conversion of CO and the selectivity for alkenes, especially those with five or more carbon atoms. The reaction mechanisms involved in the synthesis of alkenes through FTS processes are explored, with the role of electronic structure modifications in controlling product distribution emphasized.

        The literature discusses the exchange of oxygen between CO2 and CeO2, focusing on the role of Pt in the process. C18O2 is used in an advanced TAP reactor to analyze the exchange mechanism. Between 200 and 300°C, only one oxygen atom of the C18O2 molecule is exchanged, while above 300°C, C16O2 is formed. The effect of Pt is not evident at high temperatures but accelerates the exchange rate between 200 and 400°C, favoring the exchange of the second oxygen atom of the C18O2 molecule. In the presence of Pt, the exchange of the second oxygen atom can occur with C18O16O in the gas phase or with C18O16O-like surface species, leading to the formation of C16O2.

        结构缺陷，如氧空位，增强了这些催化剂的催化性质。改善催化剂组成的潜力在于优化合成方法。通过调整催化剂组成和结构，有可能解决规模化生产和成本效益问题。

        文献讨论了氧在CO2和CeO2之间的交换，重点是铂在这一过程中的作用。使用C18O2在高级TAP反应器中分析交换机制。在200至300°C之间，仅有一个氧原子的C18O2分子被交换，而在高于300°C时，形成C16O2。铂在高温下对效果不明显，但在200至400°C之间加速了交换速率，主要促进了C18O2分子第二个氧原子的交换。在铂的存在下，第二个氧原子可以与C18O16O在气体相或C18O16O类似表面物种中发生交换，最终形成C16O2。

        铁催化剂的改性，特别是通过锌和钠，具有显著影响。锌作为结构促进剂，而钠离子改变了催化剂的电子结构，促进了CO激活和产物的脱附。引入钠离子可以显著增加CO的转化率和五碳以上烷烯的选择性。通过FTS过程合成烷烯的反应机制被探索，强调了电子结构修改在控制产物分布中的作用。
