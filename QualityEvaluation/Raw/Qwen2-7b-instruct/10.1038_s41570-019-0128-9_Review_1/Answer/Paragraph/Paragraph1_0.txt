        Comparative analysis of FexNiy/CeO2 and vanadium oxide catalysts in CO2-ODH reactions: Performance, selectivity, and the influence of supports and composition on reaction mechanisms and product formation

        Detailed analysis established from the study of reference [10.1021/la903836v], synthesized comprehension stemming from references [10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052] and [10.1002/anie.201603556]. The literature highlights that FexNiy/CeO2 catalysts, notably Fe3Ni1/CeO2, demonstrate improved activity and selectivity in CO2-ODH reactions compared to vanadium oxide catalysts, influenced significantly by support materials, composition, and structural factors. The study of reference [10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052] confirms that Fe3Ni1/CeO2 catalysts exhibit superior performance due to their optimized structure and composition, despite facing challenges in scaling up production and cost-effectiveness. Moreover, the work in reference [10.1002/anie.201603556] reveals that sodium ions, when introduced to iron catalysts, can significantly increase CO conversion and the selectivity for alkenes, especially those with five or more carbon atoms.

        The study in reference [10.1016/j.apcata.2008.03.013] delves deeper into the role of Pt in the oxygen exchange process between CO2 and CeO2, demonstrating that its participation depends on temperature. At lower temperatures (200-400°C), Pt accelerates the oxygen exchange rate, which is crucial for understanding the complex multicomponent FTS catalytic systems and developing highly selective FTS processes. This research underscores the importance of considering the electronic structure modifications in catalysts to control product distribution.

        Looking ahead, there is an opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies. Future research might focus on optimizing catalyst composition and synthesis techniques to overcome scalability and cost-effectiveness challenges. By refining the catalyst structure and composition, it may be possible to enhance the performance of FexNiy/CeO2 and vanadium oxide catalysts in CO2-ODH reactions, paving the way for more efficient and sustainable industrial processes.

        The integrative understanding and prospective outlook suggest that advancements in catalyst design and synthesis could lead to significant improvements in CO2-ODH reactions. Potential avenues include the development of novel catalyst materials, the implementation of advanced synthesis methods, and the exploration of synergistic interactions between catalyst components. These efforts could not only improve the efficiency of CO2 utilization but also contribute to the broader goal of reducing greenhouse gas emissions and fostering a more sustainable chemical industry.

        FexNiy/CeO2与vanadium氧化物催化剂在CO2-ODH反应中的比较分析：性能、选择性及其对反应机制和产物形成的影响，支持材料、组成因素的作用

        参考文献[10.1021/la903836v]提供了详细的分析，参考文献[10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052]和[10.1002/anie.201603556]则为综合理解提供了基础。文献表明，FexNiy/CeO2催化剂，尤其是Fe3Ni1/CeO2，在CO2-ODH反应中表现出增强的活性和选择性，这些特性显著受到支撑材料、组成因素和结构因素的影响。根据参考文献[10.1016/j.apcatb.2017.08.052]的结论，Fe3Ni1/CeO2催化剂由于其优化的结构和组成而表现出优越的性能，尽管面临生产规模扩大和成本效益的挑战。此外，参考文献[10.1002/anie.201603556]揭示了当向铁催化剂引入钠离子时，可以显著提高CO转换率和五碳以上烯烃的选择性。

        参考文献[10.1016/j.apcata.2008.03.013]深入探讨了Pt在CO2和CeO2之间氧交换过程中的作用，证明其参与取决于温度。在较低温度（200-400°C）下，Pt加速氧交换速率，对于理解复杂的多组分FTS催化系统以及开发高度选择性的FTS过程至关重要。这项研究强调了在催化剂中考虑电子结构修改的重要性，以控制产物分布。

        展望未来，有潜力探索新的研究领域和创新方法。未来的研究可能专注于优化催化剂组成和合成技术，以克服规模化生产和成本效益的挑战。通过精细调整催化剂结构和组成，有可能提高FexNiy/CeO2和vanadium氧化物催化剂在CO2-ODH反应中的性能，从而为更高效、可持续的工业过程铺平道路。

        整合的理解和前瞻性的展望表明，催化剂设计和合成的进步可能带来CO2-ODH反应的重大改进。潜在的途径包括开发新型催化剂材料、实施先进的合成方法以及探索催化剂成分之间的协同作用。这些努力不仅能够提高CO2的利用效率，还能为减少温室气体排放和促进更可持续的化学工业做出贡献。
