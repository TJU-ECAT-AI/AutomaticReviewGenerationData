Analyzing the performance of Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts, particularly MCM-41 and SBA-15 supported VOx and CrOx, in Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions involves understanding their structural properties and catalytic behavior compared to traditional metal oxide supports. These catalysts are characterized by their ordered mesoporous structure which provides a large surface area and uniform pore size, leading to improved selectivity and activity in PDH reactions.

The lattice oxygen species present on these catalysts play a crucial role in the PDH process, facilitating the oxidation of propane molecules to propylene. The presence of lattice oxygen allows for a more efficient use of oxygen in the reaction, reducing the amount required and improving energy efficiency.

H2 pretreatment of the catalysts before the PDH process enhances their activity and stability. This treatment helps to remove impurities and activate the surface of the catalyst, creating more active sites for the reaction. It also improves the dispersion of the metal component, leading to better reaction kinetics.

The advantages of using MCM-41 and SBA-15 supported VOx and CrOx catalysts in PDH reactions include their high selectivity towards propylene production, their ability to operate at lower temperatures compared to traditional metal oxide catalysts, and their enhanced resistance to coking. These catalysts are less prone to deactivation due to coke formation, which is a major issue in PDH reactions.

However, these catalysts also have limitations. They might require higher costs for synthesis and preparation compared to traditional metal oxide catalysts. Additionally, the complex nature of their structure may lead to challenges in scaling up the production process for industrial applications.

Overall, the use of MCM-41 and SBA-15 supported VOx and CrOx catalysts in PDH reactions offers several benefits in terms of improved efficiency, selectivity, and operational stability. These advantages make them promising candidates for enhancing the PDH process, particularly in the context of sustainable chemical production aiming to reduce the environmental impact and improve energy efficiency.
