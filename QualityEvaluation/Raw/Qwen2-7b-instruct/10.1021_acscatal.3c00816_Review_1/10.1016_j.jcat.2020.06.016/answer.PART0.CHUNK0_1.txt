<quotes>
Metal-ordered mesoporous silica (OMS) catalysts, including MCM-41 and SBA-15, supported with VOx and CrOx, have been investigated for their performance in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions.
Structural properties and catalytic behavior of these materials are compared to traditional metal oxide supports, highlighting advantages, limitations, and reaction mechanisms.
Lattice oxygen species and hydrogen (H2) pretreatment significantly influence catalyst performance and deactivation in PDH reactions.
</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The performance of Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts, specifically MCM-41 and SBA-15 supported with VOx and CrOx, has been analyzed in Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions. These materials are contrasted with conventional metal oxide supports regarding their structural characteristics and catalytic activities. The study emphasizes the impact of lattice oxygen species and hydrogen (H2) pretreatment on catalyst efficiency and deactivation during PDH reactions.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
