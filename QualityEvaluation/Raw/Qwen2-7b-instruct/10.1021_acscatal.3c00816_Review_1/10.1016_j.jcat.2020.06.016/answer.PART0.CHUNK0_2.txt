1. Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts, specifically MCM-41 and SBA-15 supported with VOx and CrOx, were studied for their performance in Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions.
2. The structural characteristics and catalytic activity of these materials were contrasted with conventional metal oxide supports.
3. The efficiency of the OMS catalysts was affected by elements like lattice oxygen species and H2 pretreatment.
4. The PDH reaction pathways were examined in relation to the catalysts' operational dynamics.
