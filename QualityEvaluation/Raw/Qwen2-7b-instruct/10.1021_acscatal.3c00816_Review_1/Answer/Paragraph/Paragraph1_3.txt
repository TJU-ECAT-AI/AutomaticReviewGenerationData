        Detailed analysis established from the study of reference \[10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005\]. Synthesized comprehension stemming from references \[10.1021/acscatal.1c05921\] and \[10.1021/acsami.0c22759\]. The potential of highly dispersed VOx species supported on mesoporous silica (MCM-41) for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) in the absence of gas-phase O2 has been explored, with a particular emphasis on long-term stability. Compared to non-oxidative dehydrogenation, the oxidative (ODH) route offers thermodynamic advantages, enabling higher conversion at lower temperatures. Catalyst reduction was found to be crucial for olefin production and on-stream stability, as demonstrated through tests with both oxidized and reduced catalysts. In situ UV/Vis analysis in conjunction with thermal programmed oxidation (TPO) of used catalysts has provided insights into factors governing catalyst deactivation within a single PDH cycle and over multiple cycles. An additional benefit of VOx/MCM-41 is its ability to achieve comparable activity and on-stream stability without requiring reductive activation, unlike PtSn/Al2O3.

The integration of advanced materials science and computational methods could offer new insights into optimizing catalyst design. Additionally, exploring the synergistic effects of combining different metals or metal oxides on the surface of OMS supports may lead to the discovery of novel catalysts with superior performance in PDH reactions. The role of lattice oxygen species and hydrogen pretreatment in influencing catalyst activity and deactivation should also be further investigated to optimize reaction conditions and enhance catalyst efficiency.
