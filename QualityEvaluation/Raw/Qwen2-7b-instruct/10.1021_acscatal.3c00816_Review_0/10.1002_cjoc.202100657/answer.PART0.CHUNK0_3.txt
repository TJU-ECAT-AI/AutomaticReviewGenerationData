<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The key advantages of propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology for propylene production are its ability to address challenges related to feedstock availability, process efficiency, and product selectivity. PDH technology specifically addresses these challenges in the following ways:

1. **Feedstock Availability**: PDH utilizes propane as a feedstock, which is abundant and readily available globally. This makes the technology more flexible and less dependent on the fluctuating prices and supply of traditional crude oil sources.

2. **Process Efficiency**: The PDH process is more energy-efficient compared to conventional methods like steam cracking of oil and coal to olefins, and methanol to olefins. It consumes less energy and generates fewer byproducts, making it a more environmentally friendly option.

3. **Product Selectivity**: PDH technology produces propylene directly from propane, with high selectivity for propylene over other olefins. This ensures a high-quality product with minimal waste, enhancing the economic viability of the process.

4. **Economic Competitiveness**: With the shale gas revolution, the price of ethane has significantly dropped. This makes the production of ethene from ethane economically competitive, thereby increasing the attractiveness of PDH technology over traditional methods that rely on more expensive feedstocks.

1. **原料供应**：PDH使用丙烷作为原料，这种原料全球丰富且易于获取。这使得技术更加灵活，对传统原油来源的价格波动和供应依赖性较小。

2. **过程效率**：与传统的油裂解、煤制烯烃和甲醇制烯烃方法相比，PDH过程更节能。它消耗的能量较少，并产生较少的副产品，使其从环境角度来看更为环保。

3. **产品选择性**：PDH技术直接将丙烷转化为丙烯，具有高选择性，几乎不生成其他烯烃。这确保了高质量的产品，减少了浪费，提高了过程的经济可行性。

4. **经济竞争力**：随着页岩气革命的发展，乙烷的价格显著下降。这使得从乙烷生产乙烯的经济性变得有竞争力，从而增加了PDH技术相对于依赖更昂贵原料的传统方法的吸引力。
