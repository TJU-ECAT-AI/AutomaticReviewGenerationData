<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology stands out for its economic viability and efficiency, offering a shorter route for propylene production compared to conventional methods. It addresses the challenges of feedstock availability, process efficiency, and product selectivity by providing an alternative to the traditional steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking processes that rely heavily on dwindling petroleum resources.

2. PDH technology utilizes alternative substrates like methanol-to-olefin (MTO), metathesis of ethylene and butylene, and propane dehydrogenation for propylene production. These methods are designed to meet the escalating demand for propylene while conserving scarce oil resources.

3. PDH is characterized by its high-endothermic and equilibrium-limited nature, necessitating a high operating temperature above 550°C to activate the catalytic process effectively.

4. Traditional catalysts often suffer from metal aggregation during thermal treatments, leading to a decrease in propylene selectivity and efficiency.

5. Incorporating mesoporous systems into microporous zeolite networks enhances metal dispersion through the creation of numerous defect sites, improving catalyst performance.

6. The use of a hierarchical nanosheet structure in the catalyst design offers enhanced metal-support interaction, shape-selectivity, and prevents metal aggregation, contributing to high propylene selectivity.

7. The hierarchical structure of the catalyst reduces diffusion path lengths, thereby suppressing side reactions like oligomerization, which are common in conventional catalysts.

8. The performance of the PDH catalyst is assessed based on the interplay between metal-support interaction and acidity, optimizing the catalytic process.

9. The study focuses on a highly selective PDH catalyst based on monometallic nanoparticles supported on weakly acidic siliceous MFI-type zeolite with a hierarchical nanosheet structure.

10. There is a significant challenge in developing alternative PDH catalysts that balance minimizing acidity and increasing the surface interaction between support and metal species to achieve optimal performance.

1. PDH技术以其经济性和高效性脱颖而出，提供了与传统方法相比更短的丙烯生产途径。它通过提供一种替代石油资源日益枯竭的传统蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解过程的方法，解决了原料可用性、工艺效率和产品选择性等挑战。

2. PDH技术利用甲醇到烯烃（MTO）、乙烯和丁二烯的裂合以及丙烷脱氢等替代原料进行丙烯生产。这些方法旨在满足对丙烯的快速增长需求，同时节省稀缺的石油资源。

3. PDH具有高度吸热和平衡限制的特性，需要在550°C以上的高温下激活催化过程。

4. 传统的催化剂在热处理过程中经常出现金属聚集现象，导致丙烯选择性降低和效率下降。

5. 将介孔系统引入微孔沸石网络中，通过增加大量缺陷点显著提高金属分散度，从而改善催化剂性能。

6. 催化剂设计中的层级纳米片结构提供了增强的金属-支持相互作用、形状选择性和防止金属团聚，从而提高了丙烯选择性。

7. 层级结构的催化剂缩短了扩散路径长度，从而抑制了常规催化剂中常见的副反应，如裂解。

8. 催化剂的性能评估基于金属-支持相互作用和酸度的交互作用，优化催化过程。

9. 研究关注的是基于弱酸性硅铝酸盐MFI型沸石的层级纳米片结构上支持的单金属纳米颗粒的高选择性PDH催化剂。

10. 在开发平衡减少酸度并增加支持和金属物种表面相互作用的替代PDH催化剂以实现最佳性能方面，仍存在重大挑战。
