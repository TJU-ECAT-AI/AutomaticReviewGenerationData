<EnglishVersionAnswer>
In light of the growing global demand for propylene, the key advantages of propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology that make it the most promising method for propylene production include:

1. Efficiency and lower energy consumption compared to traditional production methods, as PDH directly converts propane into propylene.
2. Flexibility in utilizing a broader range of feedstocks, including natural gas, coal, and biomass, which enhances the ability to meet diverse market demands.
3. Reduced operational complexity and cost due to fewer purification steps required for the feedstock.
4. High product selectivity, ensuring the production of propylene with minimal byproducts, which maximizes profitability and environmental sustainability.
