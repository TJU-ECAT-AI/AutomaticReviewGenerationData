1. Propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology is considered the most promising method for propylene production due to its ability to directly convert propane into propylene and hydrogen. It addresses the challenges faced by conventional production methods by providing a more efficient process, utilizing cheaper and plentiful feedstocks from shale gas condensates, and offering higher product selectivity. This technology specifically addresses the issues of feedstock availability, process efficiency, and product selectivity.

2. Increasing reaction temperatures and decreasing partial pressures enhance the conversion of propane in PDH processes, as demonstrated by thermodynamic analysis. This indicates that higher temperatures and lower pressures contribute to the production of more propylene.

3. The Catofin process utilizes chromium oxide (CrOx) catalysts due to their low cost and high catalytic Lewis acid activity. However, these catalysts have high toxicity and environmental impacts, causing quick deactivation and requiring frequent regeneration.

4. There is a high demand for alternative metal oxide catalysts with lower chromium content to replace CrOx-based catalysts, aiming to develop more stable and environmentally friendly alternatives.

5. The lack of comprehensive summaries on newly developed dehydrogenation processes, catalyst design strategies, and the chemistry involved in propane dehydrogenation reactions necessitates a thorough review of the advancements in these areas.

6. The review emphasizes the need for innovative strategies to enhance the stability of platinum (Pt)-based and chromium oxide (CrOx)-based catalysts, as well as the search for alternative catalysts, to overcome the economic and environmental drawbacks associated with current technologies.

7. The Oleflex process employs a moving bed reactor and utilizes a fully recyclable platinum-based alumina catalyst, resulting in low production costs, high returns on investment, and minimal environmental impact.

8. Recent advancements in catalyst design have led to the development of more efficient catalysts with decreased platinum loading and increased propylene selectivity, as exemplified by the DeH catalyst series.

9. The economical and environmental issues associated with platinum and chromium oxide catalysts highlight the need for ongoing research and development to optimize PDH technologies and catalysts.

10. The Oleflex process converts propane into propylene through catalytic dehydrogenation under moving bed reactors, showcasing its commercial application since 1990.

11. The Catofin process incorporates counter-current flow technology to optimize the direction of reactive materials, enabling higher product yields with reduced raw material usage and cost savings.

12. The FBD-4 process is based on fluidized bed dehydrogenation, featuring a reaction-regeneration system operated in a fluidized bed configuration.

13. The PDH process draws from Linde's mature steam reforming technology for its front end, while its back end relies on ethylene technology for product separation.

14. The STAR catalyst, composed of platinum (Pt) and tin (Sn) on zinc aluminate (ZnCa), exhibits excellent dehydrogenation performance and high selectivity at a balanced conversion rate.

15. The ADHO technology offers a streamlined process without the need for pretreatment steps, making it suitable for direct feedstock utilization.

16. The FCDh technology demonstrates high propylene selectivity of approximately 93% at a 45% propane conversion rate, utilizing a K-promoted platinum (Pt) and gallium (Ga) catalyst on aluminum oxide (Al2O3).

17. The K-PRO technology combines the KBR Catalytic Olefins Technology (K-COT) with a novel high selectivity dehydrogenation catalyst, free of precious metals and chromium, indicating its potential for industrial application.

1. 烯丙直接脱氢（PDH）技术被认为是丙烯生产最有前景的方法，因为它可以直接将丙烷转化为丙烯和氢气。它通过提供更高效的工艺、利用来自页岩气凝析液的便宜且充足的原料，并提供更高的产品选择性来解决传统生产方法面临的挑战。这项技术特别解决了原料可用性、工艺效率和产品选择性的问题。

2. 提高反应温度和降低部分压力可以增加丙烷在PDH过程中的转化率，如热力学分析所示。这表明更高的温度和更低的压力有助于产生更多的丙烯。

3. 在Catofin过程中使用铬氧化物（CrOx）催化剂，因为它们的成本低和催化Lewis酸活性高。然而，这些催化剂具有高度的毒性和环境影响，导致快速失活和频繁再生。

4. 对于替代金属氧化物催化剂的需求很高，其铬含量较低，以取代CrOx基催化剂，旨在开发更稳定和环保的替代品。

5. 缺乏对新开发的脱氢过程、催化剂设计策略和丙烷脱氢反应化学的新总结，需要对这些领域近年来的进展进行彻底审查。

6. 需要创新策略来增强铂（Pt）基和铬氧化物（CrOx）基催化剂的稳定性，以及寻找替代催化剂，以克服当前技术的经济和环境问题。

7. Oleflex过程采用移动床反应器并利用完全可回收的铂基氧化铝催化剂，实现低成本、高投资回报率和低环境影响。

8. 最近的催化剂设计进展导致铂加载量减少和丙烯选择性提高的催化剂的发展，例如DeH催化剂系列。

9. 对铂和铬氧化物催化剂的经济和环境问题的考虑强调了持续研究和开发以优化PDH技术和催化剂的必要性。

10. Oleflex过程通过移动床反应器中的催化脱氢实现了丙烷到丙烯的转换，自1990年以来就已商业化应用。

11. Catofin过程采用了逆流流动技术，改变反应材料的方向，使得较少的原材料能够产生更多的产品，从而降低成本和运营成本。

12. FBD-4过程基于流化床脱氢，其核心技术是运行在流化床配置中的反应-再生系统。

13. PDH过程从林德成熟的蒸汽重整技术获取前端，而其后端则依赖于乙烯技术进行产品分离。

14. STAR催化剂由铂（Pt）和锡（Sn）在锌铝酸盐（ZnCa）上组成，表现出出色的脱氢性能和接近平衡转化率时的高选择性。

15. ADHO技术无需预处理步骤即可实现直接原料利用，简化了流程。

16. FCDh技术显示了大约93%的丙烯选择性，在45%的丙烷转化率下，使用了促进的铂（Pt）和镓（Ga）催化剂在铝氧化物（Al2O3）上的。

17. K-PRO技术结合了KBR催化烯烃技术（K-COT）与无贵金属和铬的新型高选择性脱氢催化剂，表明其有工业应用的潜力。
