<quotes>
<p>The demand for propylene is rapidly growing globally.</p>
<p>Conventional production methods for propylene face challenges in terms of feedstock availability, process efficiency, and product selectivity.</p>
<p>Propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology addresses these challenges effectively.</p>
<p>PDH offers several key advantages:</p>
<p>It provides a more efficient and direct route to produce propylene.</p>
<p>It ensures higher product selectivity, yielding propene as the primary product.</p>
<p>PDH reduces the dependency on traditional feedstocks, offering flexibility in sourcing raw materials.</p>
<p>It improves process efficiency by avoiding thermodynamic equilibrium limitations and reducing carbon deposition.</p>
<p>The use of PDH allows for the utilization of lower quality propane feedstocks, enhancing resource utilization.</p>
</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology is the most promising method for propylene production due to its ability to efficiently and directly convert propane into propylene. It specifically addresses the challenges faced by conventional production methods in terms of feedstock availability, process efficiency, and product selectivity. PDH offers several key advantages, including higher product selectivity, improved process efficiency, reduced carbon deposition, and the utilization of lower quality propane feedstocks. This technology enhances resource utilization and flexibility in sourcing raw materials, making it a superior choice for propylene production.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
