<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology offers several key advantages over conventional production methods, including addressing challenges related to feedstock availability, process efficiency, and product selectivity.
2. Conventional methods often face difficulties in securing a stable supply of suitable feedstocks, maintaining high process efficiencies, and achieving high product selectivity, leading to the formation of unwanted byproducts.
3. PDH technology utilizes propane as a feedstock, which is abundant and widely available, ensuring a stable source for propylene production.
4. By enhancing process efficiency, PDH processes reduce energy consumption and improve yield, making them more economically viable.
5. The use of selective catalysts, such as nickel on alumina, enables high product selectivity in PDH, minimizing byproduct formation and increasing the purity of propylene.
6. This leads to a more environmentally friendly process, characterized by lower emissions compared to traditional methods.
