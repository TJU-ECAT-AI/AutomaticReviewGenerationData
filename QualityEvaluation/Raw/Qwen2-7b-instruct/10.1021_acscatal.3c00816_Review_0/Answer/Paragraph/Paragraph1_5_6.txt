            The emergence of propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) as a promising and advantageous technology to address the challenges of meeting the growing global demand for propylene production, in the context of limitations faced by conventional methods, is a topic of significant interest. PDH technology specifically addresses the challenges of conventional production methods in terms of feedstock availability, process efficiency, and product selectivity. It offers several key advantages, including feedstock flexibility, high product selectivity, and efficient energy utilization.

            In the realm of feedstock availability, PDH utilizes propane, an abundant and widely accessible feedstock, bypassing the need for more expensive and sometimes scarce feedstocks like naphtha or heavy oil. This not only ensures a stable supply but also reduces the overall cost of production. Process efficiency is another area where PDH shines, demonstrating higher yield and lower energy consumption compared to traditional methods. This efficiency translates into lower operational costs and a reduced environmental footprint. Regarding product selectivity, PDH shows superior performance in producing propylene with minimal byproducts, aligning closely with market demands for high-purity propylene.

            The technological advancements in PDH catalysts, such as those featuring PtZn localized at Ti sites on TS-1 zeolite, Ga-MFI catalysts, and atomically dispersed Co2+ sites in silicalite-1 zeolite frameworks, have significantly enhanced the performance of PDH processes. These catalysts exhibit high activity, selectivity, and durability, contributing to the overall efficiency and economic viability of PDH technology.

            Looking ahead, the future of PDH technology is promising, with ongoing research focusing on the optimization of catalysts, the development of new feedstocks, and the improvement of process conditions. This includes the exploration of emerging fields and innovative methodologies, such as the use of renewable feedstocks and the enhancement of catalytic activity through advanced material designs.

                    在满足全球日益增长的丙烯需求背景下，直接丙烷脱氢（PDH）技术作为解决传统生产方法所面临挑战的有前景且优势明显的技术，引起了广泛关注。PDH技术针对传统生产方法在原料可用性、过程效率和产品选择性方面的问题提供了具体解决方案。

                    PDH技术提供了几个关键优势，包括原料灵活性、高产品选择性和高效的能源利用。在原料可用性方面，PDH利用丙烷作为主要原料，这是一种丰富且易于获取的资源，可以确保稳定的供应，并降低生产成本。在过程效率方面，PDH显示了与传统方法相比更高的产出率和更低的能量消耗，这不仅降低了运营成本，也减少了对环境的影响。在产品选择性方面，PDH表现出色，在产生丙烯的同时产生较少的副产品，符合市场对高纯度丙烯的需求。

                    在PDH催化剂领域的技术创新，如PtZn局部化在TS-1沸石上的Ti位点、Ga-MFI催化剂以及原子分散的Co2+位点在硅酸盐-1沸石框架中的应用，显著提高了PDH过程的性能。这些催化剂具有高活性、选择性和稳定性，为PDH技术的整体效率和经济可行性做出了贡献。

                    未来，PDH技术的发展前景广阔，研究重点可能包括集成可再生能源以驱动PDH过程、开发更可持续的催化剂以及优化工艺参数以进一步提高效率并减少环境影响。探索这些途径有可能导致更加可持续、高效和经济上可行的丙烯生产解决方案，为石化行业的未来开辟新的篇章。
