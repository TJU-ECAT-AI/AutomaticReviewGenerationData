            The emergence of propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) as a promising and advantageous technology to address the challenges of meeting the growing global demand for propylene production, in the context of limitations faced by conventional methods, is a pivotal shift in the petrochemical industry. By leveraging propane as a primary feedstock, PDH ensures a stable and abundant resource supply, bypassing the constraints faced by traditional methods that often rely on scarce and expensive naphtha or heavy oil. This not only enhances feedstock availability but also aligns with the growing global demand for propylene, particularly in the context of the expanding shale gas industry.

            PDH technology improves process efficiency by employing a streamlined conversion process that minimizes energy consumption and operational costs. This is achieved through optimized reaction conditions and catalyst systems designed to maximize propylene yield while maintaining high product selectivity. The process efficiency of PDH is further enhanced by its ability to directly convert propane to propylene without the need for intermediate products, which is a stark contrast to conventional methods that often require multiple steps and intermediate treatments.

            Product selectivity is another key advantage of PDH technology. It allows for the precise control of the dehydrogenation reaction, leading to a higher purity of propylene output with minimal byproducts. This high product selectivity not only meets the stringent quality requirements of various downstream applications but also reduces waste and environmental impact.
