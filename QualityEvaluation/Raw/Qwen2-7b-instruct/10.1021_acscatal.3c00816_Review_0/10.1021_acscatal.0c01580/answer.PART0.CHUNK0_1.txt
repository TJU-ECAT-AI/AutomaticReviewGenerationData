<EnglishVersionAnswer>
In light of the growing global demand for propylene, nonoxidative propane dehydrogenation (PDH) technology stands out as the most promising method for propylene production. It addresses the challenges faced by conventional production methods in several ways. Firstly, PDH technology boasts high yield and low energy consumption, making it more efficient. Secondly, it offers better feedstock flexibility, as it can utilize a wide range of hydrocarbons as feedstocks, unlike conventional methods that often rely on specific resources. Thirdly, PDH exhibits higher product selectivity, which enhances the quality and purity of the propylene produced. Lastly, PDH has a lower environmental impact compared to traditional methods, making it a more sustainable choice.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
