1. The key advantages of propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology in addressing the challenges of conventional propylene production methods include:
   - **Feedstock Availability**: PDH utilizes abundant propane resources, particularly from shale gas exploitation, making it a sustainable and cost-effective feedstock option compared to traditional naphtha or heavy oil sources.
   - **Process Efficiency**: PDH processes are designed to efficiently convert propane to propylene, often with higher yields and better energy efficiency than conventional methods.
   - **Product Selectivity**: PDH enables the production of high-quality propylene with controlled selectivity, reducing byproduct formation and enhancing overall product value.

2. The PDH process specifically addresses the challenges of conventional production methods in terms of:
   - **Feedstock Availability**: By leveraging propane from unconventional sources, PDH diversifies the feedstock base, mitigating risks associated with volatile crude oil markets and ensuring a steady supply of raw materials.
   - **Process Efficiency**: Advanced catalytic systems and optimized process conditions in PDH enhance conversion rates and yield, leading to more efficient use of resources and lower environmental impacts.
   - **Product Selectivity**: Tailored catalysts and reaction conditions in PDH allow for precise control over the selectivity towards propylene, optimizing product quality and market fit, especially in scenarios where high-purity propylene is required for specific applications.
