<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The key advantages of propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology are its ability to address the challenges of conventional production methods in terms of feedstock availability, process efficiency, and product selectivity. Conventional methods often face difficulties in accessing high-quality propane feedstocks due to their high cost and scarcity. In contrast, PDH technology can utilize a wider range of feedstocks, including lower-grade propane, reducing reliance on expensive raw materials. Moreover, PDH technology demonstrates higher conversion rates and yields of propylene compared to traditional methods, resulting in increased productivity and reduced energy consumption. Additionally, PDH offers enhanced selectivity towards propylene, minimizing the formation of unwanted byproducts such as carbon monoxide and hydrogen, which improves economic viability and environmental performance.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
