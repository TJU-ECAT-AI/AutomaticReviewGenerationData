1. Propane dehydrogenation is the most promising method for propylene production due to its ability to address challenges related to feedstock availability, process efficiency, and product selectivity. The method allows for direct conversion of propane to propylene, addressing issues with conventional production methods that often rely on less readily available feedstocks or suffer from lower efficiency and selectivity.
2. The key advantages of propane direct dehydrogenation technology include the utilization of abundant propane feedstock, enhanced process efficiency, and improved product selectivity. This method ensures a steady supply of propylene, a critical chemical used in numerous industries, by converting a readily available resource into a valuable product efficiently.
3. Supported bi-component Pt-Sn catalysts are widely used in various chemical reactions due to their high activities and environmentally friendly characteristics. Their application in propane dehydrogenation highlights their versatility and effectiveness in petrochemical processes.
4. The PtSnNa/SUZ-4 catalyst exhibits superior performance in propane dehydrogenation compared to the PtSnNa/ZSM-5 catalyst. The primary reason for this is the better dispersion of platinum (Pt) particles on the surface of the SUZ-4 zeolite, which prevents deactivation by carbon deposition, thereby maintaining higher catalytic activity and stability.
