In light of the growing global demand for propylene, propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology offers several key advantages over conventional production methods. PDH technology specifically addresses challenges related to feedstock availability, process efficiency, and product selectivity. The high demand for propylene is met by PDH's ability to efficiently convert propane into propylene through an endothermic process, requiring a relatively high temperature to maximize yield. This contrasts with conventional methods, which might struggle with feedstock availability, particularly in regions where propane is scarce or expensive.

PDH technology utilizes supported platinum catalysts, which are highly reactive and efficient in converting propane into propylene. The use of new supports with distinct structures, such as ZSM-5 zeolite, enhances the performance of these catalysts. ZSM-5 zeolite, with its unique three-dimensional channels and adjustable Si-Al ratio, serves as an effective carrier for the catalyst, improving the reaction pathway and selectivity for propylene production.

Metal promoters, notably zinc (Zn), play a significant role in enhancing the catalytic properties of PDH catalysts. Zn can improve the selectivity of the reaction, particularly for the dehydrogenation of light alkanes, by neutralizing the acidity of the support material. The strong interactions between Zn and Pt catalysts lead to the formation of PtZn alloys, which modify the electronic properties of the Pt atoms, potentially increasing the activity and stability of the catalyst.

In the context of the study, the incorporation of zinc into the ZSM-5 zeolite support via hydrothermal synthesis creates an environment that facilitates the formation of strong interactions between the support and the platinum catalyst. This results in a catalyst that exhibits superior activity and stability, as evidenced by the PtNa/Zn(1.0%)-ZSM-5 sample, which showed the highest initial activity and the lowest deactivation parameter after 10 hours of reaction.

Overall, PDH technology, with its strategic use of advanced catalyst supports and metal promoters, provides an efficient and selective method for propylene production, addressing critical challenges in the industry and meeting the escalating demand for propylene worldwide.
