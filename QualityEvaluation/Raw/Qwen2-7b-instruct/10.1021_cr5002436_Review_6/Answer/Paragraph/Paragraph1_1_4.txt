        The methodology for comparing catalyst performance in nonoxidative dehydrogenation of C2-C4 paraffins, as detailed in the referenced studies \[1\], \[2\], and \[3\], focuses primarily on three key parameters: olefin yield, stability, and space velocity. This systematic approach enables a clear and standardized assessment of catalyst efficiency in transforming paraffins into olefins, thereby addressing the complex challenge of evaluating diverse catalysts across various conditions. By concentrating on these specific criteria, the methodology simplifies the evaluation process, enhancing its manageability and cost-effectiveness. However, this focus may inadvertently exclude less quantifiable factors that also significantly influence catalyst performance, such as the catalyst's selectivity, activity over time, and operational robustness under fluctuating conditions.

        The comparative analysis grounded in these performance indicators supports the optimization of petrochemical processes, leading to increased yields and reduced costs, especially in regions characterized by low-permeability reservoirs, like those found in Eastern Siberia. This methodology underscores the importance of leveraging quantitative measures to streamline decision-making in catalyst selection, ensuring that the most efficient catalysts are identified for specific reactions.

        Looking ahead, future research endeavors might explore avenues to integrate additional, less quantifiable factors into the assessment framework. Such efforts could enhance the predictive capabilities of the methodology, allowing for a more holistic evaluation of catalyst performance. Potential enhancements could include incorporating aspects like catalyst lifetime, reaction selectivity, and environmental impact into the analysis. These improvements would facilitate a more nuanced understanding of catalyst behavior, enabling researchers and industry professionals to make informed decisions that optimize both economic and environmental outcomes.

        The prospect of refining the methodology to encompass a wider array of performance indicators, while maintaining its core strengths in efficiency and cost-effectiveness, opens up exciting possibilities for advancing the field of catalysis in nonoxidative dehydrogenation. Future work might also focus on developing more sophisticated computational models that can simulate catalyst behavior under diverse conditions, providing insights that complement experimental data and guiding the design of novel catalysts tailored to specific applications.

        The overarching goal remains to strike a balance between simplicity and comprehensiveness in catalyst evaluation, ensuring that the methodology remains a powerful tool for driving innovation and efficiency in the petrochemical industry, particularly in challenging environments such as those encountered in Eastern Siberia.

        在\[\ref{1}\]、\[\ref{2}\]和\[\ref{3}\]引用的研究中，比较C2-C4烷烃非氧化脱氢催化剂性能的方法主要聚焦于三个关键指标：烯烃产率、稳定性以及空间速度。这种方法提供了清晰且标准化的评估手段，帮助我们了解不同条件下催化剂在将烷烃转化为烯烃时的效率，有效解决了评价多样催化剂的复杂挑战。通过专注于这些特定标准，该方法简化了评估流程，使其更易于管理和成本效益高。然而，这种聚焦可能忽视了影响催化剂性能的其他不可量化因素，如催化剂的选择性、时间内的活性以及在波动条件下运行的稳健性。

        基于这些性能指标的比较分析支持优化石油化工过程，从而实现更高产量和更低成本，尤其是在像西伯利亚东部这样的低渗透性油藏区域。这种方法凸显了利用定量措施来简化催化剂选择决策的重要性，确保识别出最适合特定反应的最高效催化剂。

        展望未来，未来研究可能探索将更多、不可量化的因素纳入评估框架的途径。这些改进将增强方法的预测能力，使对催化剂性能的评估更加全面。潜在的增强可以包括整合催化剂寿命、反应选择性、以及环境影响等要素。这些改进将促进对催化剂行为的更深入理解，指导研究人员和行业专业人员做出既能优化经济又能兼顾环境结果的决策。

        未来的重点可能是平衡简单性和全面性，在催化剂评估中保持其强大的工具地位，确保该方法能够推动创新和效率的提升，特别是在西伯利亚东部这样的挑战环境中。
