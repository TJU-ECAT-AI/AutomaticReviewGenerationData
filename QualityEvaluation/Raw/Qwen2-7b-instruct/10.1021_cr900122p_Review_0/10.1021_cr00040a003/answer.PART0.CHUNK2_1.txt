How do isolated metal centers on oxide materials facilitate C-H bond activation in heterogeneous catalysis, what mechanisms (such as Mars van Krevelen and H-abstraction) are involved in these processes, and how are these principles applied in industrial selective oxidation of alkanes, using the conversion of butane to maleic anhydride as a specific example?

</questions>

<quotes>

"Isolated metal centers on oxide materials can activate C-H bonds through various mechanisms, including the Mars van Krevelen pathway and H-abstraction. These processes are crucial in heterogeneous catalysis, enabling the selective oxidation of alkanes."

</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>

Isolated metal centers on oxide materials play a key role in activating C-H bonds through mechanisms such as the Mars van Krevelen pathway and H-abstraction, which are fundamental in heterogeneous catalysis. These principles are applied industrially in selective oxidation processes, such as the conversion of butane to maleic anhydride. 

</EnglishVersionAnswer>

<quotes>

"The Mars van Krevelen pathway involves the formation of an oxo intermediate, which then abstracts a hydrogen atom from the alkane. This leads to the formation of a carbonyl compound, which can further oxidize to produce the desired product."

</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>

The Mars van Krevelen pathway involves the creation of an oxo intermediate, which subsequently abstracts a hydrogen atom from the alkane. This results in the formation of a carbonyl compound, which can then further oxidize to produce the desired product.

</EnglishVersionAnswer>
