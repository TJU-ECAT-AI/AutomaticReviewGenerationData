<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The study indicates that different types of aluminas exhibit varying activities in the isomerization of 1-butene and hydrogen exchanges between ethylene/cyclopropane and deuterium. Alumina V, prepared from aluminium sulfate with urea, shows high activity in 1-butene isomerization. On the other hand, alumina III or II, prepared from aluminium nitrate with ammonia or urea, demonstrates the highest activities for hydrogen exchanges with ethylene or cyclopropane, respectively.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
