The literature discusses the chemisorption of molecular hydrogen on zinc oxide, which facilitates C-H bond activation. The Mars van Krevelen mechanism and H-abstraction play significant roles in these processes. These principles are applied in industrial selective oxidation of alkanes. For instance, the conversion of butane to maleic anhydride exemplifies the use of these catalytic processes in industry.
