<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The article provides a molecular-level discussion of the nature of the active sites in metal catalysis. It introduces fundamental concepts like Brnsted-Evans-Polanyi relations and volcano curves, and uses them to establish a partitioning between electronic and geometrical effects. This partitioning is used to define the concept of degree of structure sensitivity, which is useful for analyzing the structure sensitivity of catalytic reactions. The article covers the extensive use of metal surfaces as catalysts in various industries, focusing on the role of nanoparticles to maximize surface area.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
