<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The isolated metal centers on oxide materials play a crucial role in facilitating C-H bond activation in heterogeneous catalysis. The Mars van Krevelen mechanism and H-abstraction are key processes involved in these activations. These principles are applied industrially in the selective oxidation of alkanes. A specific application is the conversion of butane to maleic anhydride, where the catalyst's ability to activate and selectively oxidize the alkane is demonstrated.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
