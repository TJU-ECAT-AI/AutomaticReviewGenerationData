2. Liquid acid alkylation catalysts have properties that enable them to adsorb and diffuse hydrocarbons effectively.

3. Zeolitic alkylation catalysts are characterized by the presence of Brnsted acid sites, Lewis acid sites with extra-framework aluminum, a specific silicon/aluminum ratio, metal ions in ion-exchange positions, and various types of zeolite structures.

4. In the conversion of butane to maleic anhydride, isolated metal centers on oxide materials are pivotal in activating C-H bonds through mechanisms such as Mars van Krevelen and H-abstraction.
