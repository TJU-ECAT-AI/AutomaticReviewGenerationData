The performance of various catalysts is extensively compared in the literature, focusing on achieving high yields of olefins while maintaining selectivity across different conversion levels. Catalysts based on Group IA and IIA metals, transition metal oxides, and rare earth oxides show promising results, with some systems achieving yields as high as 34% to ethylene. However, the use of chlorine for enhancing selectivity and yield is limited by corrosion concerns in equipment.
