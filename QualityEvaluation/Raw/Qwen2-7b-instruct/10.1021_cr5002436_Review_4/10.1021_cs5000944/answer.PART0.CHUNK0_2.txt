1. Carbon-based materials such as coke deposits, carbon nanofibers, and hybrid nanocarbons have shown unexpected catalytic activity in alkane dehydrogenation reactions. This activity is due to their distinctive electronic structure, high surface area, and adjustable surface chemistry.
2. Temperature and surface chemistry significantly impact the catalytic performance of these materials.
3. The potential for developing new and efficient carbon-based catalysts is enhanced by the unique properties of these materials and the capability to modify their surface chemistry.
