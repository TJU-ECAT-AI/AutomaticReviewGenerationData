      <English>
          The selective dehydrogenation of isobutane to isobutene over metal sulfide catalysts has been widely studied. Various carbon-based materials, including coke deposits, carbon nanofibers, and hybrid nanocarbons, have demonstrated unexpected catalytic activity in alkane dehydrogenation reactions. The enhanced activity of these materials is attributed to the formation of active sites on their surfaces. Temperature is critical in determining the reaction rate and product selectivity in these reactions. Surface chemistry, encompassing the presence of functional groups and the degree of graphitization, significantly affects the catalytic performance. This phenomenon promises significant development opportunities for novel and efficient carbon-based catalysts, particularly due to their potential for high activity at a lower cost.
      </English>
