<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Various carbon-based materials, including coke deposits, carbon nanofibers, and hybrid nanocarbons, exhibit unexpected catalytic activity in alkane dehydrogenation reactions. The mechanism proposed for their enhanced activity involves the formation of active sites on their surfaces.
2. Temperature and surface chemistry, specifically the presence of functional groups and the degree of graphitization, play crucial roles in determining the reaction rate and product selectivity in alkane dehydrogenation reactions over these materials.
3. This phenomenon suggests great promise for developing novel and efficient carbon-based catalysts due to their potential for high activity and lower cost compared to traditional catalysts.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
