<EnglishVersionAnswer>
Various carbon-based materials, including coke deposits, carbon nanofibers, and hybrid nanocarbons, exhibit unexpected catalytic activity in alkane dehydrogenation reactions due to their unique surface chemistry and electronic structure, which facilitate the adsorption and activation of alkane molecules. Temperature and surface area are critical influencing factors that enhance the catalytic performance of these materials. This phenomenon has significant implications for developing more efficient carbon-based catalysts, potentially improving the efficiency and selectivity of alkane dehydrogenation processes.
