Various carbon-based materials, including coke deposits, carbon nanofibers, and hybrid nanocarbons, showed unexpected catalytic activity in alkane dehydrogenation reactions due to the formation of active sites on their surfaces. These active sites enable the facilitation of the dehydrogenation process. Influencing factors like temperature and surface chemistry significantly impact the efficiency of these materials as catalysts. This discovery holds great promise for developing novel and efficient carbon-based catalysts, potentially enhancing chemical processes involving alkane dehydrogenation.
