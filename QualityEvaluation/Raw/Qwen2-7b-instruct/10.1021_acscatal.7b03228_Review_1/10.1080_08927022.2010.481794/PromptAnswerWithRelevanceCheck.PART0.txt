Single-atom alloys, particularly Pd atoms embedded in Cu surfaces, enhance catalytic performance in selective hydrogenation reactions through several key mechanisms. Firstly, the d-band characteristics of Pd atoms play a crucial role in determining the catalytic reactivity. By modifying the d-band characteristics through alloying, the electronic structure of the surface is altered, influencing the bond formation and breaking capabilities of the metal surface. Specifically, the Pd atoms in the Cu matrix can create a more favorable environment for hydrogen adsorption and activation, leading to enhanced selectivity in hydrogenation reactions.

Designing successful single-atom alloys (SAAs) for hydrogenation reactions requires careful consideration of multiple factors:
