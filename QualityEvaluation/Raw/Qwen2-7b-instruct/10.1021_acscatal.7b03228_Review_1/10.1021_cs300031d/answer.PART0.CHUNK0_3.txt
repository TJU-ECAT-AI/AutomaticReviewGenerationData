They achieve this through their unique atomic structure and electronic properties, which enable them to break scaling rules and improve selectivity across different catalyst systems.

2. The key factors for designing successful single-atom alloy (SAA) catalysts involve comprehending the conditions under which the reactions occur, the properties of the metals used, and how they surpass traditional catalysts in terms of performance and efficiency.
