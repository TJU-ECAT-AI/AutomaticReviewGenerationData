<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. Single-atom alloy (SAA) catalysts, especially those featuring Pd atoms embedded in Cu surfaces, exhibit outstanding catalytic performance in selective hydrogenation reactions. They surpass conventional catalysts by maintaining high activity under challenging conditions and breaking scaling rules. Success in designing these catalysts hinges on the meticulous control of metal properties, including the size and spatial arrangement of the metal atoms on the surface. Their capacity to enhance selectivity across diverse systems is linked to their distinctive electronic configuration and the presence of numerous active sites per unit cell.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
