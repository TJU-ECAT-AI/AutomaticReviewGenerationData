<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The CeO2TiO2 mixed oxide demonstrates thermal stability, transitioning from poorly crystalline to well-crystallized phases with increasing calcination temperature. Impregnation of vanadia enhances the crystallization of both CeO2 and TiO2 oxides. XRD patterns show a mixture of CeO2 and TiO2 anatase phases, while Raman spectra confirm the presence of both CeO2 and TiO2 anatase phases. Broad bands in the Raman spectra suggest the presence of polyvanadate species at lower calcination temperatures, indicating vanadium oxide is mostly in the V(V) oxidation state. XPS results confirm that elements in the CeO2TiO2 and V2O5/CeO2TiO2 samples are in their highest oxidation states at 773 K, with indications of Ce(III) ions present at all temperatures.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
