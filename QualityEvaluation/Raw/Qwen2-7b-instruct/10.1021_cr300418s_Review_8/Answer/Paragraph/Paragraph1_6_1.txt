        The in-depth analysis presented in the file reveals the complex effects of dopants on the catalytic properties of metal oxides, specifically focusing on the role of same-valence dopants (SVDs) and high-valence dopants (HVDs) in influencing the catalytic performance of different metal oxides. This review delves into the findings from computational and experimental studies that have elucidated the mechanisms behind how dopants, such as Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs, Os, Rh, Ir, Ni, Pd, Pt, Cu, Ag, Au, and Cd, alter the electronic structure and reactivity of metal oxides, notably affecting the energy of oxygen vacancy formation (OVFE). These dopants, particularly Au and Ag, are shown to significantly reduce OVFE, enhancing the reactivity of the oxide towards gases like CO, which reacts more readily with oxygen atoms near the dopant.

        Factors such as Lewis acid-base interactions and the nature of the host oxide (reducible vs. irreducible) play critical roles in determining dopant behavior. However, formulating general predictive rules for dopant behavior remains challenging due to the complexity and variability of these factors. The model used in the studies acknowledges limitations, particularly in its relevance to the flat facets of highly dispersed catalysts, which have a complex surface morphology.

        Insights gained from these studies have been instrumental in understanding how dopants can be strategically used to enhance the catalytic properties of metal oxides. For instance, the promotion of catalytic activity in Pt/CeO2 catalysts for CO oxidation is attributed to CeO2's oxygen storage capacity, higher reducibility of Pt in the presence of CeO2, and the prevention of Pt sintering. The presence of Pt ions as active sites in the catalyst further underscores the effectiveness of this combination.

        The review also emphasizes the importance of understanding the specific dopant's effect on the oxide's electronic structure and the energetics of oxygen-vacancy formation to optimize catalytic applications. Challenges persist in correlating dopant properties with their catalytic effects, necessitating a deeper exploration of the underlying mechanisms.

        Looking ahead, the field presents exciting opportunities for innovation and advancement. Future research efforts might focus on leveraging the knowledge gained to develop more efficient and selective catalysts, possibly through the design of novel metal oxide compositions and structures. Emerging fields, such as the integration of machine learning algorithms for predicting dopant behavior, could offer new avenues for accelerating the discovery process and tailoring catalysts for specific applications.

        In summary, the review highlights the multifaceted impact of dopants on the catalytic properties of metal oxides, underscoring the importance of continued research to unlock the full potential of these materials in catalysis. The future direction of the field is promising, with the potential to significantly advance the design and application of catalysts for a wide range of industrial processes.

        文件中的深入分析揭示了掺杂剂对金属氧化物催化性能的复杂影响，特别是同价掺杂剂（SVD）和高价掺杂剂（HVD）如何改变不同金属氧化物的催化性能。本综述探讨了从计算和实验研究中获得的见解，这些研究阐明了Li、Na、K、Rb、Cs、Os、Rh、Ir、Ni、Pd、Pt、Cu、Ag、Au和Cd等掺杂剂如何通过改变金属氧化物的电子结构和活性来影响氧空位形成能（OVFE）。特别是Au和Ag被证明能够显著降低OVFE，从而增强氧化物对气体如CO的反应性，CO更易于与靠近掺杂剂的氧原子反应。

        李-碱性酸基相互作用和宿主氧化物（可还原性或不可还原性）的性质是决定掺杂剂行为的关键因素。然而，由于这些因素的复杂性和变化性，制定通用的掺杂剂行为预测规则仍然具有挑战性。所使用模型的局限性承认了对于高度分散催化剂表面的复杂形态，其结果的适用性仅限于平滑晶面。

        这些研究获得的洞察表明，如何有策略地利用掺杂剂来增强金属氧化物的催化性能。例如，在Pt/CeO2催化剂中，CO氧化的促进作用归因于CeO2的氧存储能力、Pt在CeO2存在的更高可还原性以及防止Pt颗粒聚集。Pt离子作为催化剂的活性中心进一步强调了这种组合的有效性。

        该综述还强调了理解特定掺杂剂对氧化物电子结构及其氧空位形成能的热力学效应的重要性，以优化催化应用。在与掺杂剂属性相关联以预测其催化效果方面仍存在挑战，需要进一步探索潜在机制。

        展望未来，该领域提供了激动人心的机会和进步的可能性。未来的研究工作可能集中在利用已获得的知识来开发更高效、选择性更强的催化剂，这可能涉及设计新型金属氧化物组成和结构。新兴领域，如将机器学习算法应用于预测掺杂剂行为，可能为加速发现过程和针对特定应用定制催化剂提供新的途径。

        总之，该综述强调了掺杂剂对金属氧化物催化性能的多面影响，突出了继续研究以完全挖掘这些材料潜力的重要性。未来的方向充满希望，有可能显著推进催化剂设计和应用的发展，适用于各种工业过程。
