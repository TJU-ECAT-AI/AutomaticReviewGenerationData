        The complex effects of dopants on the catalytic properties of metal oxides, especially in the realm of propane dehydrogenation, are multifaceted. Same-valence dopants tend to decrease the energy of oxygen vacancy formation (OVFE), often favoring surface locations over the bulk, with notable exceptions like Au and Ag, which exhibit substantial reductions in OVFE comparable to alkali metal dopants. On the other hand, high-valence dopants that increase OVFE preferentially substitute Zn atoms in the bulk of the host oxide, except for certain divalent dopants like Ca, Sr, Ba, La, Ce, Re, Fe, Ru, Co, Sn, and Pb, which substitute Zn atoms at the surface.

        Despite efforts to correlate the magnitude of OVFE with properties such as ionization energy, electronegativity, chemical hardness, and atomic radii, no clear correlations have emerged. However, it has been rationalized that dopants at the surface form fewer bonds with oxygen, and if the dopant-oxygen bond is weaker than the ZnO bond, less binding energy is lost when the dopant is at the surface.

        The model employed in these studies acknowledges two weak points: highly dispersed catalysts exhibit a complex surface morphology, and the calculations are pertinent only to the flat facets of such oxides. This highlights the challenge in predicting dopant behavior universally across different catalyst geometries.

            关于同价掺杂剂在催化性能方面的影响，它们倾向于降低氧空位形成能（OVFE），通常偏好表面位置而非内部，其中金（Au）和银（Ag）尤为突出，它们导致的OVFE降低与碱金属掺杂剂相当，显著。相比之下，高价掺杂剂增加OVFE时，倾向于用Zn原子替换宿主氧化物中的原子，除非是某些二价掺杂剂如Ca、Sr、Ba、La、Ce、Re、Fe、Ru、Co、Sn和Pb，这些掺杂剂更倾向于在表面替换Zn原子。

            尽管尝试将OVFE的大小与其他掺杂剂属性（如电离能、电负性、化学硬度和离子半径）相关联的努力未能产生明确的关联，但可以合理地解释，表面的掺杂剂与氧形成的键较少，如果掺杂剂-氧键比ZnO键弱，则当掺杂剂处于表面时，失去的结合能量较少。

            模型在两个弱点上：高度分散的催化剂具有复杂的表面形态，且计算仅适用于此类氧化物的平滑面。这突显出在预测掺杂剂行为时存在普遍性的挑战，强调了需要针对表面化学和催化剂结构细节的精细方法来理解并优化催化应用。

            该部分提出了未来研究的方向，强调了发现能够增强金属氧化物催化效率的新掺杂剂的可能性，特别是对于丙烷脱氢的应用。未来研究可能聚焦于基于表面化学的高级计算模型的开发，以更准确地预测掺杂剂行为。

            它建议通过理论预测和实验验证的结合，探索新兴材料和优化现有材料，以实现更高效的催化活性。通过利用不同宿主系统中不同掺杂剂的研究洞见，研究人员可以定制具有增强选择性和活性的催化剂，以适应丙烷脱氢。
