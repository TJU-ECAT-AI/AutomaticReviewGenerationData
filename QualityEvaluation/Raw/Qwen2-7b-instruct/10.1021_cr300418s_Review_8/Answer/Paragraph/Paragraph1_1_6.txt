        The complex effects of dopants on the catalytic properties of metal oxides, specifically in the context of propane dehydrogenation catalysts, have been extensively studied. This review section aims to provide a comprehensive analysis of the influence of both same-valence dopants (SVDs) and high-valence dopants (HVDs) on the catalytic performance of various metal oxides, drawing insights from computational and experimental studies. The key findings highlight the role of dopants in altering the electronic structure and reactivity of metal oxides, affecting their ability to catalyze reactions such as CO oxidation and methane oxidative coupling.

        <ul>
            <li><strong>SVDs and HVDs Influence Catalytic Properties:</strong> SVDs and HVDs can significantly alter the energy of oxygen vacancy formation (OVFE), influencing the catalytic properties of metal oxides. For instance, gold (Au) and silver (Ag) act as substantial OVFE reducers, comparable to alkali metals. High-valence dopants that increase OVFE tend to substitute zinc (Zn) atoms in the bulk of the host oxide, except for certain elements like calcium (Ca), strontium (Sr), barium (Ba), lanthanum (La), cerium (Ce), iron (Fe), cobalt (Co), tin (Sn), and lead (Pb), which prefer to substitute a Zn atom at the surface.</li>
            <li><strong>Lewis Acid-Base Interactions and Oxide Nature:</strong> The nature of the host oxide plays a crucial role in determining dopant behavior. For example, in reducible oxides like TiO2 and CeO2, the removal of an oxygen atom leads to a reduction of Ti or Ce from a formal charge of 4+ to 3+, facilitating electron localization on the cation post-oxygen removal. The irreducibility of lanthanum oxide (La2O3) means that chemical events at the surface do not induce changes in La atom valence, impacting dopant location and behavior.</li>
            <li><strong>Challenges in Formulating Predictive Rules:</strong> Despite advancements in understanding the effects of dopants on catalytic properties, formulating general predictive rules remains challenging due to the complexity and variability of factors such as dopant properties, host oxide characteristics, and reaction conditions.</li>
        </ul>
        
        <p>These findings underscore the intricate relationship between dopants, metal oxides, and catalytic performance, offering insights into optimizing catalyst design and performance. However, the field continues to face challenges in predicting dopant behavior accurately and universally across different metal oxides and catalytic applications.</p>
        
        <p><strong>Prospective Outlook:</strong> Looking ahead, the integration of advanced computational methods and experimental techniques holds promise for addressing the remaining challenges. Future research may focus on developing more sophisticated models that account for the dynamic nature of catalyst surfaces, the role of dopant clustering, and the impact of reaction conditions on dopant behavior. Additionally, exploring the synergistic effects of multiple dopants and the optimization of dopant loading could lead to the discovery of novel catalysts with superior catalytic properties for propane dehydrogenation and other important chemical processes.</p>
        
        <p>The study of propane dehydrogenation catalysts thus opens up new avenues for innovation in catalysis, emphasizing the importance of understanding the complex interplay between dopants, metal oxides, and catalytic activity. This knowledge can be leveraged to design more efficient and selective catalysts, contributing to advancements in the petrochemical industry and beyond.</p>

        同价掺杂剂（SVDs）和高价掺杂剂（HVDs）对金属氧化物的催化性能产生了复杂的影响，特别是在丙烷脱氢催化剂的研究中。本综述部分旨在全面分析同价和高价掺杂剂对不同金属氧化物催化性能的影响，并从计算和实验研究中汲取洞见。主要发现强调了掺杂剂在改变金属氧化物的电子结构和反应活性方面的作用，影响其催化CO氧化和甲烷氧化耦合等反应的能力。

        <ul>
            <li><strong>同价和高价掺杂剂的影响：</strong> 同价掺杂剂和高价掺杂剂可以显著改变氧空位形成能（OVFE），从而影响金属氧化物的催化性能。例如，金（Au）和银（Ag）作为重要的OVFE降低剂，其效果与碱金属类似。增加的价态掺杂剂倾向于在宿主氧化物的主体中替换锌（Zn）原子，除非是某些元素如钙（Ca）、锶（Sr）、钡（Ba）、镧（La）、铈（Ce）、铁（Fe）、钴（Co）、锡（Sn）和铅（Pb），这些元素更倾向于在表面替换一个Zn原子。</li>
            <li><strong>Lewis酸碱相互作用和氧化物性质：</strong> 宿主氧化物的性质对掺杂剂行为起着关键作用。例如，在可还原氧化物如TiO2和CeO2中，氧原子的去除导致钛或铈的电荷从4+降低至3+，促进电子在去氧后定位在阳离子上。镧氧化物（La2O3）的不可还原性意味着化学事件在表面不会诱导镧原子电荷的变化，影响掺杂剂的位置和行为。</li>
            <li><strong>预测规则的挑战：</strong> 尽管在理解掺杂剂对催化性能的影响方面取得了进步，但制定普遍的预测规则仍面临挑战，这主要是由于因素的复杂性和变化性，如掺杂剂属性、宿主氧化物特性以及反应条件。</li>
        </ul>
        
        <p>这些发现凸显了掺杂剂、金属氧化物和催化活性之间的复杂关系，提供了优化催化剂设计和性能的见解。然而，该领域继续面临准确且普遍预测掺杂剂行为的挑战。</p>
        
        <p><strong>未来展望：</strong> 展望未来，先进的计算方法和实验技术的整合有望解决剩余的挑战。未来研究可能侧重于开发更高级的模型，以考虑催化剂表面的动态性质、掺杂剂聚集的影响以及反应条件对掺杂剂行为的影响。此外，探索多种掺杂剂的协同效应和最佳掺杂量的优化可能引领新型高效和选择性催化剂的发现，从而为丙烷脱氢和其他重要化学过程提供支持。

        因此，对丙烷脱氢催化剂的研究开辟了催化领域的创新途径，强调了理解掺杂剂、金属氧化物和催化活性之间复杂相互作用的重要性。这种知识可以用于设计更高效的催化剂，推动石油化工行业乃至更广泛的科学领域的发展。</p>
