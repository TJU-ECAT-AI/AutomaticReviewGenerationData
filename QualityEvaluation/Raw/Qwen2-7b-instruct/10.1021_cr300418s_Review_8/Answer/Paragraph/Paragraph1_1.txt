        The complex effects of dopants on the catalytic properties of metal oxides, particularly in the context of propane dehydrogenation catalysts, have been comprehensively explored. Same-valence dopants (SVDs) and high-valence dopants (HVDs) influence the electronic structure and reactivity of metal oxides by weakening the bond between oxygen atoms in the surface layer and the oxide, thereby facilitating oxygen vacancy formation. This alteration enhances the reactivity of the oxide towards gases like CO, which reacts more readily with oxygen atoms near the dopant. HVDs, in particular, tend to adsorb oxygen from the gas phase and activate it, leading to improved catalytic activity against reactions such as CO oxidation.

        Studies on the doping of zinc oxide (ZnO) have shown that dopants like gold (Au) and silver (Ag) cause a substantial reduction in oxygen vacancy formation energy (OVFE), comparable to that caused by alkali metals. High-valence dopants that increase OVFE generally prefer to substitute zinc atoms in the bulk of the host oxide, except for certain divalent elements like calcium (Ca), strontium (Sr), barium (Ba), lanthanum (La), cerium (Ce), ruthenium (Ru), iron (Fe), cobalt (Co), tin (Sn), and lead (Pb), which prefer to substitute a Zn atom at the surface. Factors such as Lewis acid-base interactions and the nature of the host oxide (reducible vs. irreducible) play crucial roles in determining dopant behavior, but formulating general predictive rules remains challenging due to the complexity and variability of these factors.

        Insights gained from computational and experimental studies suggest that dopants that decrease OVFE prefer to be located at the surface of ZnO rather than in the bulk, as they form fewer bonds with oxygen than dopants in the bulk. If the dopant-oxygen bond is weaker than the ZnO bond, less binding energy is lost when the dopant is at the surface, thus facilitating oxygen vacancy formation.

        Challenges persist in formulating general predictive rules for dopant behavior, as the interaction between dopants and the host oxide is influenced by numerous factors including the dopant's ionization energy, electronegativity, chemical hardness, and atomic radii. However, some rationalization is possible based on the fact that a dopant at the surface forms fewer bonds with oxygen than a dopant in the bulk, and the enthalpy of the formation of the dopant's oxide plays a role in determining the location of the dopant within the oxide.

        在丙烷脱氢催化剂的背景下，同价掺杂剂（SVD）和高价掺杂剂（HVD）通过减弱表面层中的氧原子与氧化物之间的键合，促进了氧空位的形成，从而影响了金属氧化物的电子结构和反应性。这一改变增强了氧化物对气体如CO的反应性，特别是当CO更易于与靠近掺杂剂的氧原子反应时。特别是高价掺杂剂倾向于从气相吸附氧并激活它，从而提高了催化活性，例如在CO氧化反应中。

        对于锌氧化物（ZnO）的研究表明，掺杂剂如金（Au）和银（Ag）可以显著降低氧空位形成能（OVFE），与碱金属相似。增加OVFE的高价掺杂剂通常倾向于在宿主氧化物的主体中替换锌原子，除非是某些二价元素如钙（Ca）、锶（Sr）、钡（Ba）、镧（La）、铈（Ce）、钌（Ru）、铁（Fe）、钴（Co）、锡（Sn）和铅（Pb），这些元素倾向于在表面上替换一个Zn原子。Lewis酸-碱相互作用以及宿主氧化物的还原性和不可还原性等因素在确定掺杂剂行为方面起着关键作用，但构建通用的预测规则仍然具有挑战性，这归因于这些因素的复杂性和变异性。

        计算和实验研究的见解表明，降低OVFE的掺杂剂倾向于位于ZnO的表面而不是内部，因为它们与氧化物内的掺杂剂形成的键较少。如果掺杂剂与氧的键强弱于ZnO的键，则表面的掺杂剂损失的结合能量较少，从而促进氧空位的形成。

        存在的挑战在于构建通用的预测规则来描述掺杂剂的行为，因为掺杂剂与宿主氧化物之间的相互作用受到包括掺杂剂的电离能、电负性、化学硬度和原子半径在内的多种因素的影响。然而，基于事实，表面的掺杂剂形成的键少于内部的掺杂剂，氧化物形成焓也参与了确定掺杂剂在氧化物中的位置。
