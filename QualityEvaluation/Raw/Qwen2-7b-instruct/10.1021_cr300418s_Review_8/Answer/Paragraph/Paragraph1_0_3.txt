        The complex effects of dopants on the catalytic properties of metal oxides, specifically focusing on same-valence dopants (SVDs) and high-valence dopants (HVDs), have been extensively explored. The studies highlight the critical role of dopants in modifying the electronic structure and reactivity of metal oxides, thereby influencing their catalytic performance. Factors such as Lewis acid-base interactions and the nature of the host oxide (reducible vs. irreducible) play crucial roles in determining the behavior of dopants.

        Among SVDs, Au and Ag notably cause a substantial lowering of the energy of oxygen vacancy formation (OVFE), comparable to that caused by alkali metals. High-valence dopants that increase OVFE generally prefer to substitute zinc (Zn) atoms in the bulk of the host oxide, except for certain exceptions like Ca, Sr, Ba, La, Ce, Re, Fe, Ru, Co, Sn, and Pb, which prefer to substitute a Zn atom at the surface. The challenge in formulating general predictive rules for dopant behavior stems from the complexity and variability of these factors.

        The model used in these studies has two weak points: the relevance of calculations for highly dispersed catalysts, which have a large number of steps, kinks, mesas, and other morphological features on their surface, and the fact that the calculations are relevant only for the flat facets of such oxides.

        Insights gained from these studies suggest that dopants that decrease OVFE tend to be located at the surface of zinc oxide (ZnO) rather than in the bulk, due to the formation of fewer bonds with oxygen. This leads to less binding energy loss when the dopant is at the surface, especially if the dopant-oxygen bond is weaker than the ZnO bond.

        The model used here has two weak points: highly dispersed catalysts have a large number of steps, kinks, mesas, and other morphological features on their surface, and the calculations performed here are relevant only for the flat facets of such oxides. Addressing these limitations could enhance the predictive power of models for dopant behavior.

        The integrative understanding and prospective outlook suggest that future research might focus on exploring the effects of dopants in more complex oxide systems, understanding the role of dopant distribution on catalyst morphology, and developing more accurate models that can predict dopant behavior across a range of oxide systems. Potential future directions include investigating the synergistic effects of multiple dopants, the impact of dopant clustering, and the influence of dopant concentration on catalyst performance.

        In summary, the studies provide valuable insights into the complex interplay between dopants and metal oxides, highlighting the importance of dopant location, electronic structure, and the nature of the host oxide in catalytic performance. Addressing the remaining challenges will require interdisciplinary efforts combining computational modeling, experimental techniques, and theoretical frameworks to create more robust and predictive models for dopant behavior in metal oxides.

        对于金属氧化物中掺杂剂（同价掺杂剂SVDs和高价掺杂剂HVDs）对催化性能的复杂影响进行了广泛探索。研究表明，掺杂剂通过修改金属氧化物的电子结构和反应性，进而影响它们的催化性能。Lewis酸碱相互作用以及氧化物的性质（可还原性vs不可还原性）是决定掺杂剂行为的关键因素。

        在SVD中，Au和Ag显著降低了氧空位形成能（OVFE），与碱金属相当。高电荷掺杂剂增加的OVFE通常倾向于在主氧化物中取代锌（Zn）原子，除了某些例外如Ca、Sr、Ba、La、Ce、Re、Fe、Ru、Co、Sn和Pb，这些更倾向于在表面取代一个Zn原子。对于预测掺杂剂行为而言，挑战在于这些因素的复杂性和多样性。

        使用的模型有两个弱点：高度分散的催化剂具有大量台阶、凸起、山脉和其他表面特征，而进行的计算仅适用于这些氧化物的平坦晶面。

        从这些研究中获得的见解表明，降低OVFE的掺杂剂倾向于位于锌氧化物（ZnO）的表面而不是内部，由于形成了较少的氧键，导致当掺杂剂位于表面时，绑定能量损失较少。特别是如果掺杂剂-氧键比ZnO键弱，则情况如此。

        模型使用的两个弱点是：高度分散的催化剂具有大量的台阶、凸起、山脉和其他表面特征，而进行的计算仅适用于这些氧化物的平坦晶面。解决这些限制将增强模型预测掺杂剂行为的能力。

        集成理解和前瞻性展望表明，未来的研究可能集中在探索更多复杂氧化物系统的影响、理解掺杂剂分布对催化剂形态的作用，并开发能够预测不同氧化物系统中掺杂剂行为的更准确模型。潜在的未来方向包括研究多种掺杂剂的协同效应、掺杂剂团聚的影响以及掺杂剂浓度对催化性能的影响。

        总之，这些研究提供了关于掺杂剂与金属氧化物之间复杂相互作用的重要见解，强调了掺杂剂位置、电子结构和氧化物性质在催化性能中的重要性。解决剩余的挑战需要跨学科的努力，结合计算建模、实验技术以及理论框架，以创建更稳健且预测性强的模型，用于描述金属氧化物中的掺杂剂行为。
