        The comprehensive review on the complex effects of dopants on the catalytic properties of metal oxides, specifically focusing on same-valence dopants (SVDs) and high-valence dopants (HVDs), is derived from an in-depth analysis of various scientific references. This includes studies on the electronic structure and reactivity of cerium (Ce) and zirconium (Zr)-doped titanium dioxide (TiO2) using density functional theory approaches, which shed light on how dopants influence the catalytic properties of metal oxides, taking into account factors such as Lewis acid-base interactions and the nature of the host oxide (reducible vs. irreducible).

        The review elucidates the role of dopants in weakening the bond between oxygen atoms in the surface layer and the oxide, facilitating oxygen vacancy formation, and enhancing the reactivity of the oxide towards various gases, particularly carbon monoxide (CO). High-valence dopants (HVDs) are shown to facilitate the Marsvan Krevelen mechanism for oxidation, suggesting an improvement in catalytic activity. Doping can lead to a substantial reduction in the energy of oxygen-vacancy formation, thereby enhancing the catalytic efficiency of metal oxides.

        However, formulating general predictive rules for dopant behavior remains challenging due to the complexity and variability of factors involved. The model used in studies has limitations, especially when applied to highly dispersed catalysts with numerous surface features, as calculations are typically relevant for flat facets of such oxides.

        Insights from these studies suggest that dopants preferentially substitute Zn atoms in the bulk for most HVDs, except for certain divalent and trivalent dopants that substitute at the surface. The behavior of dopants at the surface versus in the bulk is influenced by their ability to form bonds with oxygen and the strength of these bonds relative to the ZnO bond.

        The review also discusses the importance of understanding the specific dopant's effect on the oxide's electronic structure and the energetics of oxygen-vacancy formation to optimize catalytic applications. This knowledge is crucial for developing more efficient catalysts, particularly in processes involving propane dehydrogenation.

        Looking ahead, future research efforts might focus on exploring emerging fields and innovative methodologies to address the challenges in formulating general predictive rules for dopant behavior. Potential areas include the development of new computational models that can accurately predict dopant effects on catalytic properties, as well as the discovery of novel dopants that could further enhance the catalytic performance of metal oxides.

        In summary, the review underscores the intricate interplay between dopant type, oxide properties, and catalytic efficiency, highlighting the need for a multidisciplinary approach to unravel the complex mechanisms underlying dopant effects. It also points towards promising avenues for future research aimed at advancing the design and optimization of catalysts for various industrial applications, including those related to propane dehydrogenation.

    <Chinese>
        对于金属氧化物中掺杂剂对催化性能的复杂影响进行了全面综述，特别关注同价掺杂剂（SVDs）和高价掺杂剂（HVDs）的作用。这一综述基于对多种科学参考文献的深入分析，包括利用密度泛函理论方法研究铈（Ce）和锆（Zr）-掺杂的钛酸盐（TiO2）的电子结构和反应性，揭示了掺杂剂如何影响金属氧化物的催化性能，考虑了Lewis酸碱相互作用以及作为可还原或不可还原氧化物的宿主氧化物的性质。

        该综述说明了掺杂剂在削弱表面层中氧原子与氧化物之间的键合、促进氧空位形成以及增强氧化物对各种气体（尤其是碳氧化合物）的反应性的作用。对于高价掺杂剂（HVDs），它们被发现在氧化过程中促进了Marsvan Krevelen机制，表明了催化活性的改善。掺杂可以显著降低氧空位形成能，从而提高金属氧化物的催化效率。

        然而，由于涉及因素的复杂性和变化性，预测掺杂行为的一般规则仍具有挑战性。所使用模型的局限性尤其体现在高度分散的催化剂上，这些催化剂表面具有众多特征，计算通常仅适用于此类氧化物的平滑面。

        该综述还指出，大多数HVD倾向于用Zn原子取代氧化物的主体，除了某些二价和三价掺杂剂，这些掺杂剂在表面上取代。表面与主体之间的掺杂剂行为受其与氧形成的键合强度及其相对于ZnO键的强度的影响。

        此外，综述强调了理解特定掺杂剂对氧化物电子结构和氧空位形成能的效应的重要性，以优化催化应用。这种知识对于开发更高效的催化剂至关重要，尤其是在涉及丙烷脱氢等工业应用的过程中。

        展望未来，未来研究工作可能集中在探索新兴领域和创新方法来解决预测掺杂行为一般规则的挑战。潜在的领域包括开发能够准确预测掺杂剂对催化性能影响的新计算模型，以及发现能够进一步提高金属氧化物催化性能的新型掺杂剂。

        总之，综述强调了掺杂类型、氧化物性质与催化效率之间错综复杂的相互作用，突出了多学科方法在揭示掺杂剂效应机制中的重要性。它也指出了未来研究的前景，旨在通过设计和优化催化剂来推进各种工业应用的发展，特别是与丙烷脱氢相关的过程。
