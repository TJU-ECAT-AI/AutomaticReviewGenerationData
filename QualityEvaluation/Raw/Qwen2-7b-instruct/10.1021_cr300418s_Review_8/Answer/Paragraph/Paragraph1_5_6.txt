        The in-depth analysis and comprehensive review on the complex effects of dopants on the catalytic properties of metal oxides, specifically focusing on propane dehydrogenation catalysts, reveals a nuanced understanding of how same-valence dopants (SVDs) and high-valence dopants (HVDs) impact the performance of these catalysts. The review delves into the mechanisms underlying the influence of dopants, including their role in modifying the electronic structure, altering the energetics of oxygen vacancy formation, and affecting the Lewis acid-base interactions within the host oxide. Insights gained from both computational and experimental studies underscore the pivotal role of dopants in enhancing the reactivity and selectivity of metal oxides in catalytic processes.

        The review emphasizes that SVDs typically tend to locate at the surface of the host oxide rather than in the bulk, and among them, gold (Au) and silver (Ag) notably reduce the energy of oxygen vacancy formation (OVFE) to a degree comparable to alkali metal dopants. Conversely, HVDs that increase OVFE preferentially substitute zinc (Zn) atoms in the bulk of the host oxide, except for certain divalent dopants such as calcium (Ca), strontium (Sr), barium (Ba), lanthanum (La), cerium (Ce), ruthenium (Ru), iron (Fe), cobalt (Co), tin (Sn), and lead (Pb), which exhibit a preference for substitution at the surface. Despite efforts to correlate the magnitude of OVFE with properties such as ionization energy, electronegativity, chemical hardness, and atomic radii, successful correlations have proven elusive.

        A key factor influencing the location of dopants is the strength of the bond between the dopant and oxygen, with dopants that decrease OVFE forming fewer bonds with oxygen than those in the bulk. This allows them to be more stable at the surface, where the dopant-oxygen bond is weaker than the bond within the ZnO lattice. However, formulating general predictive rules for dopant behavior remains challenging due to the intricate interplay of factors such as the Lewis acid-base interactions and the nature of the host oxide (reducible vs. irreducible).

        The model used in the studies has identified two weak points: first, the relevance of the calculations to the flat facets of highly dispersed catalysts, given the presence of complex morphologies on their surfaces, and second, the limitations in accounting for the dynamic surface conditions during the actual catalytic process.

        The review also highlights the role of ceria (CeO2) in enhancing the catalytic properties of different metal oxides, particularly in the context of Pt/CeO2 catalysts for CO oxidation. Ceria is attributed to its oxygen storage capacity (OSC), enhanced reducibility of Pt in the presence of ceria, higher dispersion of Pt over ceria compared to other supports like Al2O3, and the prevention of sintering of Pt metal particles. Quantitative evidence from studies demonstrates that ceria significantly improves the CO conversion rate over Pt/CeO2 catalysts, showcasing its potential in enhancing the efficiency of catalysts.

        The review concludes with an integrative understanding and prospective outlook, emphasizing the importance of leveraging these insights to develop more efficient and selective catalysts for propane dehydrogenation and other catalytic processes. It advocates for future research to focus on exploring emerging fields and innovative methodologies, potentially addressing the challenges in formulating general predictive rules and optimizing catalyst performance through a deeper understanding of dopant behavior and their interaction with the host oxide. This includes investigating the effects of dopants under dynamic reaction conditions, understanding the role of surface morphology, and developing computational models that can accurately predict dopant behavior and catalytic performance.

        对于金属氧化物中掺杂同价（SVD）和高价（HVD）掺杂剂对催化性能的影响进行了深入的分析和全面的综述，特别是在丙烷脱氢催化剂的背景下，揭示了掺杂剂在催化过程中的复杂作用机制。综述强调了同价掺杂剂通常倾向于位于主氧化物表面而非内部，并且其中金（Au）和银（Ag）显著降低了氧空位形成能（OVFE），其程度与碱金属掺杂剂相当。相比之下，增加OVFE的高价掺杂剂倾向于在主氧化物内部替换锌（Zn）原子，除非是某些二价掺杂剂如钙（Ca）、锶（Sr）、钡（Ba）、镧（La）、铈（Ce）、钌（Ru）、铁（Fe）、钴（Co）、锡（Sn）和铅（Pb），这些掺杂剂更倾向于在表面进行替换。

        尽管尝试将OVFE的大小与离子化能、电负性、化学硬度和电离或离子半径等属性相关联，但成功的相关性一直难以建立。影响掺杂剂位置的关键因素是掺杂剂与氧之间的键强度，降低OVFE的掺杂剂与氧形成的键少于在主体内的键，允许它们在表面稳定，而表面的掺杂剂-氧键比ZnO晶格内的键弱。然而，由于动态表面条件的影响，以及在实际催化过程中模型使用的局限性，制定一般性的掺杂剂行为预测规则仍然具有挑战性。

        模型已识别出两个弱点：首先，对高度分散催化剂的平滑面的计算结果的适用性，考虑到表面存在复杂的形态；其次，缺乏准确预测催化剂性能的计算模型，尤其是在考虑动态反应条件时。

        综述还强调了铈（CeO2）在增强不同金属氧化物催化性能中的作用，特别是Pt/CeO2催化剂在CO氧化中的应用。铈被认为具有氧气存储能力（OSC）、Pt在铈存在的增强还原性、铈上Pt的更高分散度与Al2O3相比以及防止Pt金属颗粒聚集，这显著提高了1% Pt/CeO2催化剂的CO转化率，展示了铈在提高催化剂效率方面的潜力。

        综述以整合理解和前瞻展望结束，强调利用这些见解开发更高效、选择性更强的催化剂对于丙烷脱氢和其他催化过程的重要性。它提倡未来的研究应聚焦于探索新兴领域和创新方法，可能解决制定通用预测规则的挑战并优化催化剂性能，通过更深入地了解掺杂剂行为及其与主氧化物的相互作用。这包括调查在动态反应条件下掺杂剂的作用，理解表面形态的作用，以及开发能够准确预测掺杂剂行为和催化性能的计算模型。
