        The complex effects of dopants on the catalytic properties of metal oxides, specifically focusing on same-valence dopants (SVDs) and high-valence dopants (HVDs), have been extensively explored. SVDs and HVDs impact the catalytic properties of metal oxides by weakening the bond between oxygen atoms in the surface layer and the oxide, thus facilitating oxygen vacancy formation. This enhancement increases the reactivity of the oxide towards various gases, notably carbon monoxide (CO), which reacts more readily with oxygen atoms near the dopant. HVDs, in particular, are adept at adsorbing oxygen from the gas phase and activating it, thereby boosting the catalytic activity against reactions such as CO oxidation. The energy of oxygen vacancy formation is influenced by the choice of dopant, revealing a correlation between the energy of oxygen vacancy formation on a doped oxide and the enthalpy of the dopant's oxide formation. This relationship provides valuable insights into the dopant's effect on the oxide's catalytic performance.

        For instance, doping lanthanum oxide (La2O3) with copper (Cu), zinc (Zn), magnesium (Mg), iron (Fe), or aluminum (Al) leads to a substantial reduction in the energy of oxygen vacancy formation, aligning with the propensity rule outlined earlier. This finding underscores the enhanced catalytic efficiency of the modified oxide under these conditions. The study of cerium (Ce) and zirconium (Zr)-doped titanium dioxide (TiO2) further elucidates the role of dopants in altering the electronic structure and reactivity of metal oxides, particularly in the context of Lewis acid-base interactions and the nature of the host oxide (reducible vs. irreducible).

        However, formulating general predictive rules for dopant behavior remains challenging due to the intricate nature of factors such as the Lewis acid-base interactions and the specific characteristics of the host oxide. The model employed in the study has two notable limitations: the relevance of calculations to highly dispersed catalysts is limited to flat facets of such oxides, and the model does not account for the complex morphological features present on the surface of highly dispersed catalysts.

        The promoting action of ceria (CeO2) on the catalytic properties of different metal oxides, specifically in the context of platinum (Pt)/ceria (CeO2) catalysts for CO oxidation, is attributed to its oxygen storage capacity (OSC), enhanced reducibility of Pt in the presence of ceria, higher dispersion of Pt over ceria compared to other supports like alumina (Al2O3), and the prevention of sintering of Pt metal particles. Quantitative evidence from Figure 1 demonstrates that the CO conversion rate over 1% Pt/CeO2 is significantly higher (85 times) than that over pure Pt metal particles at 200°C. XRD analysis confirms the presence of 0.08% Pt metal and indicates that if any Pt oxides were formed, they would have been detected. The catalyst preparation involves a single-step solution combustion technique, yielding 1% Pt/CeO2 nanocrystallites. Platinum is primarily stabilized in a +2 oxidation state on nanosized ceria in a Ce1-xPtxO2-δ-like phase, making the Pt ions the active sites for CO oxidation in the Pt/CeO2 catalyst.

        对于同价掺杂剂（SVDs）和高价掺杂剂（HVDs）对金属氧化物催化性质的复杂影响进行了广泛研究。这些掺杂剂通过削弱表面层中氧原子与氧化物之间的键合，促进氧空位形成，从而增强氧化物的催化活性。这一增强作用特别体现在气体如CO与氧气原子反应的活性提高上，其中CO更容易与靠近掺杂剂的氧原子反应。对于活性较高的掺杂剂，它们倾向于从气相吸附氧气并激活其活性，从而增强对CO氧化等反应的催化活性。掺杂剂对氧化物催化性能的影响受到选择掺杂剂的影响，观察到掺杂氧化物上的氧空位形成能与其掺杂剂氧化物形成的焓之间存在相关性。这一关系为理解掺杂剂对氧化物电子结构和氧空位形成能的作用提供了宝贵的见解。例如，掺杂镧氧化物（La2O3）时，使用铜（Cu）、锌（Zn）、镁（Mg）、铁（Fe）或铝（Al），可以显著降低氧空位形成能，符合上述倾向规则，从而增强改性氧化物的催化效率。

        对铈（Ce）和锆（Zr）掺杂的钛酸盐（TiO2）的研究进一步阐明了掺杂剂在改变金属氧化物的电子结构和活性中的作用，特别是在Lewis酸碱相互作用和宿主氧化物（可还原与不可还原）特性的背景下。

        然而，由于因素如Lewis酸碱相互作用和宿主氧化物的特性（可还原与不可还原）的复杂性和变异性，构建一般预测规则以描述掺杂行为仍然具有挑战性。所使用的模型有两个显著的局限性：高度分散催化剂表面具有大量的台阶、尖峰、台地和其他形态特征，而在此进行的计算仅适用于此类氧化物的平坦面；模型未能充分考虑实际催化剂的复杂形态。 

        铈氧化物（CeO2）在不同金属氧化物（尤其是铂（Pt）/铈氧化物（CeO2）催化剂用于CO氧化）中的促进作用归因于其氧存储容量（OSC）、铂在铈氧化物存在的增强可还原性、铂在铈氧化物上相对于其他支持（如氧化铝（Al2O3））的更高分散度以及防止铂金属颗粒团聚。定量证据来自图1，显示了1% Pt/CeO2对CO的转化率比纯铂金属颗粒在200°C时高出85倍。X射线衍射分析确认了0.08%的铂金属的存在，并表明如果任何铂氧化物形成，它们都将被检测出来。催化剂的制备采用单步溶液燃烧技术，产生1% Pt/CeO2纳米晶粒。铂主要稳定在纳米尺寸的铈中，形成Ce1-xPtxO2-δ型相，使铂离子成为Pt/CeO2催化剂中CO氧化的活性位点。
