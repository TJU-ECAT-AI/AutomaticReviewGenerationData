        The review section on the complex effects of dopants on the catalytic properties of metal oxides, focusing on the analysis of same-valence dopants (SVDs) and high-valence dopants (HVDs) in various host systems, reveals a nuanced picture of dopant behavior and its implications for catalytic performance. The studies highlight the influence of dopants on oxygen vacancy formation energy (OVFE) and their spatial distribution within the oxide lattice, shedding light on the role of dopants in modifying the electronic structure and reactivity of metal oxides.

        Insights gained from both computational and experimental studies indicate that dopants can significantly alter the catalytic properties of metal oxides by influencing factors such as Lewis acid-base interactions and the nature of the host oxide (reducible vs. irreducible). However, formulating general predictive rules for dopant behavior remains challenging due to the complexity and variability of these factors.

        The review emphasizes the importance of understanding the specific dopant's effect on the oxide's electronic structure and the energetics of oxygen-vacancy formation to optimize catalytic applications. It discusses the impact of dopants on the reactivity of metal oxides towards various gases, such as carbon monoxide (CO), and the role of dopants in facilitating mechanisms like the Marsvan Krevelen mechanism for oxidation.

        Key findings include:
        - SVDs and HVDs can weaken the bond between oxygen atoms in the surface layer and the oxide, facilitating oxygen vacancy formation and enhancing the reactivity of the oxide towards gases like CO.
        - Gold (Au) and silver (Ag) cause substantial reductions in OVFE, comparable to that caused by alkali dopants, suggesting a unique role in improving the catalytic efficiency of metal oxides.
        - HVDs that increase OVFE tend to substitute zinc (Zn) atoms in the bulk of the host oxide, except for certain divalent and trivalent dopants that prefer to substitute Zn atoms at the surface.
        - The model used in studies acknowledges limitations, such as the relevance of calculations to flat facets of dispersed catalysts and the complexity in correlating dopant properties with OVFE.

        The review also underscores the importance of considering the host oxide's reducibility and the dopant's valence state in predicting dopant behavior. The interplay between the dopant's electronic properties, its spatial distribution within the oxide lattice, and its impact on the oxide's catalytic performance presents a rich area for further exploration.

        Looking ahead, the review suggests a forward-thinking outlook, emphasizing the need for innovative methodologies and interdisciplinary approaches to address the challenges in formulating general predictive rules for dopant behavior. Potential avenues for future research might include:
        - Investigating the role of dopants in the electronic structure of reducible and irreducible oxides under varying conditions.
        - Developing more accurate models that account for the complex morphology of dispersed catalysts and their surface features.
        - Exploring the synergistic effects of multiple dopants and their interactions within the same or different metal oxides.
        - Enhancing computational methods to predict dopant behavior more accurately and efficiently.

        This comprehensive analysis provides a solid foundation for researchers aiming to optimize the catalytic properties of metal oxides through strategic dopant selection and placement, paving the way for advancements in the development of more efficient and selective catalysts for various industrial processes.

        关于同价掺杂剂（SVD）和高电荷掺杂剂（HVD）在不同金属氧化物中的催化性能复杂影响的综述揭示了掺杂剂行为及其对催化性能影响的微妙图景。研究表明，掺杂剂可以通过影响因素如Lewis酸碱相互作用和宿主氧化物的可还原性或不可还原性，显著改变金属氧化物的催化性质。

        从计算和实验研究中获得的见解表明，掺杂剂可以通过调节金属氧化物的电子结构和反应活性来显著改变其催化性能。然而，由于这些因素的复杂性和变异性，形成通用的掺杂剂行为预测规则仍然具有挑战性。

        该综述强调了理解特定掺杂剂对氧化物电子结构和氧空位形成能的效应的重要性，以优化催化应用。它讨论了掺杂剂如何影响金属氧化物对各种气体（如一氧化碳（CO））的反应性，并阐述了掺杂剂在促进氧化机制如Marsvan Krevelen机制中的作用。

        主要发现包括：
        - 同价和高价掺杂剂能够减弱氧化物表面层中氧原子之间的键合，促进氧空位的形成，并增强氧化物对气体如CO的反应性。
        - 黄金（Au）和银（Ag）可以造成显著的氧空位形成能量（OVFE）降低，类似于碱金属掺杂剂，表明它们在提高金属氧化物催化效率的独特作用。
        - 高价掺杂剂增加的OVFE倾向于用锌（Zn）原子替换宿主氧化物的基体，除非某些二价和三价掺杂剂更倾向于在表面上替换Zn原子。
        - 在使用模型时，考虑到分散催化剂的复杂形态及其表面特征的局限性以及在预测掺杂剂行为时离子化能、电负性、化学硬度和原子半径等属性之间的相关性不成功。

        该综述还强调了考虑宿主氧化物的可还原性和掺杂剂的电荷状态在预测掺杂剂行为中的重要性。金属氧化物内掺杂剂电子性质、空间分布及其对氧化物催化性能的影响提供了一个丰富的研究领域。

        展望未来，综述提出了前瞻性的视角，强调需要创新的方法和跨学科的方法来解决形成通用掺杂剂行为预测规则的挑战。可能的研究方向包括：
        - 探索在不同条件下可还原和不可还原氧化物中掺杂剂在电子结构中的作用。
        - 开发更准确的模型，考虑到分散催化剂复杂的形态及其表面特征。
        - 探索单一或不同金属氧化物中多个掺杂剂的协同作用及其相互作用。
        - 提升计算方法，以更准确、更高效地预测掺杂剂的行为。

        这个全面的分析为寻求通过战略选择和定位掺杂剂来优化金属氧化物催化性能的研究者提供了坚实的基础，开辟了开发更高效、选择性更强催化剂的新途径，为各种工业过程的进步铺平道路。
