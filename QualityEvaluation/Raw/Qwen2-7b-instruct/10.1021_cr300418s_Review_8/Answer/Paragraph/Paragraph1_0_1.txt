Insights gleaned from both computational and experimental studies suggest that SVDs, such as gold (Au) and silver (Ag), can significantly reduce the energy of oxygen vacancy formation (OVFE) and preferentially locate at the surface of zinc oxide (ZnO) rather than in the bulk, thereby enhancing its catalytic efficiency. In contrast, HVDs, which tend to increase OVFE, typically substitute zinc (Zn) atoms in the bulk of the host oxide, except for certain exceptions like calcium (Ca), strontium (Sr), barium (Ba), lanthanum (La), cerium (Ce), and others, which prefer to substitute a Zn atom at the surface. However, computational approaches like density functional theory (DFT) provide valuable insights into how dopants influence the electronic structure and reactivity of metal oxides, contributing to the development of more efficient catalysts.
