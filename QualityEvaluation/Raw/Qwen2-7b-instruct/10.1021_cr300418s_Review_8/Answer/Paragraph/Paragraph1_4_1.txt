        The in-depth analysis of dopant behavior in metal oxides, particularly focusing on same-valence dopants (SVDs) and high-valence dopants (HVDs), reveals their profound influence on catalytic properties. Studies indicate that SVDs, like Au and Ag, tend to lower the energy of oxygen vacancy formation (OVFE) and preferentially locate at the surface of zinc oxide (ZnO) rather than in the bulk, whereas HVDs that increase OVFE generally substitute Zn atoms in the bulk of the host oxide, except for certain divalent and trivalent dopants like Ca, Sr, Ba, La, Ce, Re, Fe, Ru, Co, Sn, and Pb, which substitute a Zn atom at the surface. Efforts to correlate the magnitude of OVFE with properties such as ionization energy, electronegativity, chemical hardness, and atomic radii have proven unsuccessful. However, it is rationalized that dopants at the surface form fewer bonds with oxygen than those in the bulk, and if the dopant-oxygen bond is weaker than the ZnO bond, less binding energy is lost when the dopant is at the surface.

        Computational and experimental studies have illuminated the role of Lewis acid-base interactions and the nature of the host oxide (reducible vs. irreducible) in determining dopant behavior, yet formulating general predictive rules remains challenging due to the complexity and variability of these factors. The model used in these studies, while insightful, has two notable limitations: highly dispersed catalysts have a large number of steps, kinks, mesas, and other morphological features on their surface, and the calculations are relevant only for the flat facets of such oxides.

        An integrative understanding and prospective outlook on the field reveals opportunities for exploring emerging areas and innovative methodologies. Future research endeavors might focus on elucidating the underlying mechanisms of dopant behavior across a wider range of catalysts, leveraging the advancements in first-principles calculations to predict and optimize catalytic performance. Potential future directions include the development of strategies to enhance the stability and selectivity of catalysts through strategic dopant selection and the exploration of novel oxide-host systems that may offer unique catalytic properties. The field is poised for growth, with the potential to revolutionize catalysis through a deeper understanding of dopant effects on metal oxide catalysts.

    <Chinese>
        对金属氧化物掺杂行为的深入分析表明，同价掺杂剂（SVD）和高价掺杂剂（HVD）对催化性能有深远的影响。研究表明，SVD如Au和Ag倾向于降低氧空位形成能（OVFE），并偏好在锌氧化物（ZnO）表面而不是内部；而增加OVFE的HVD通常在主氧化物的基体中替代Zn原子，除非是某些二价和三价掺杂剂如Ca、Sr、Ba、La、Ce、Re、Fe、Ru、Co、Sn和Pb，这些掺杂剂在表面上替代一个Zn原子。尝试将OVFE的大小与离子化能、电负性、化学硬度和原子半径等属性相关联的努力未获成功。然而，可以合理解释的是，表面的掺杂剂与氧形成的键少于内部的掺杂剂，如果掺杂剂-氧键比ZnO键弱，则当掺杂剂位于表面时损失的结合能较少。

        计算和实验研究揭示了Lewis酸碱相互作用和主氧化物的性质（可还原性vs不可还原性）在决定掺杂剂行为中的关键作用，但制定通用的预测规则仍然具有挑战性，因为这些因素的复杂性和变异性。所使用的模型虽然有启发意义，但也存在两个主要局限：高度分散的催化剂在其表面上有大量台阶、凸起、台地和其他形态特征，而进行的计算仅适用于这类氧化物的平滑面。

        对该领域的综合理解和未来展望揭示了探索新领域和创新方法的机会。未来的研究可能集中在通过选择性掺杂优化催化剂性能，并利用第一原理计算来预测催化性能。潜在的未来方向包括开发策略以增强催化剂的稳定性和选择性，通过精心选择掺杂剂，以及探索可能提供独特催化性质的新氧化物-载体系统。该领域正处于发展期，有可能通过更深入地了解金属氧化物催化剂的掺杂效应来彻底改变催化技术。
