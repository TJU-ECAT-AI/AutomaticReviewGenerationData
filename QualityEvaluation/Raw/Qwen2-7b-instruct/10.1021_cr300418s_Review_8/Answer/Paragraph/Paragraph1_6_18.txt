            <p>
                The complex effects of dopants on the catalytic properties of metal oxides, particularly in the context of propane dehydrogenation catalysts, are illuminated through the examination of same-valence dopants (SVDs) and high-valence dopants (HVDs). This review delves into the intricate ways in which these dopants alter the electronic structure and reactivity of metal oxides, notably affecting the energy of oxygen vacancy formation (OVFE) and influencing the catalytic performance in various applications. Insights are drawn from both computational and experimental studies, highlighting the pivotal role of dopants in enhancing or diminishing the efficiency of catalytic processes.

                <p>
                    The study of dopants in metal oxides reveals that dopants that decrease the OVFE tend to localize at the surface of the oxide rather than in the bulk, exemplified by gold (Au) and silver (Ag) which cause a substantial reduction in OVFE, akin to alkali metal dopants. High-valence dopants that increase OVFE, conversely, prefer to substitute the host oxide’s cation in the bulk, except for certain divalent and trivalent dopants that favor substitution at the surface. This surface-bulk preference is rationalized based on the bond strength between the dopant and oxygen, where weaker bonds at the surface result in less binding energy loss when the dopant is placed there.
                </p>

                <p>
                    The overarching challenge in formulating general predictive rules for dopant behavior lies in the complexity and variability of factors such as Lewis acid-base interactions and the nature of the host oxide (reducible vs. irreducible). These factors play crucial roles in determining how dopants will behave within the oxide lattice. Despite advancements in computational and experimental techniques, there remains a gap in accurately correlating dopant properties with their catalytic effects, necessitating further exploration into the nuanced interplay between dopant chemistry and catalytic performance.
                </p>

                <p>
                    The model employed in these studies has two weak points: it is limited in its applicability to highly dispersed catalysts with complex morphologies and does not account for the effects on flat facets of oxides. This limitation underscores the need for more sophisticated models that can capture the full spectrum of dopant behavior across different oxide surfaces and morphologies.
                </p>

            <p>
                本综述基于参考文献<doi:10.1021/jp071671n>进行了深入的分析，并综合了来自<doi:10.1021/jp203112>和<doi:10.1063/1.3515424>的见解。金属氧化物中掺杂对催化性能的影响，特别是在丙烷脱氢催化剂中的作用，通过研究同价掺杂剂（SVD）和高电荷掺杂剂（HVD）的复杂效应而揭示。这些分析展示了掺杂剂如何通过改变电子结构和活性来影响金属氧化物的氧空位形成能（OVFE），从而影响各种应用中的催化性能。

                <p>
                    对于降低OVFE的掺杂剂倾向于在氧化物表面而非内部聚集，如金（Au）和银（Ag）等，它们显著降低了OVFE，类似于碱金属掺杂剂。增加OVFE的高电荷掺杂剂通常倾向于取代主氧化物的离子在内部，除了某些二价和三价掺杂剂，它们更倾向于在表面取代。这种表面与内部的选择性是由掺杂剂与氧之间的键强度决定的，表面较弱的键意味着当掺杂剂位于表面时损失较少的结合能。
                </p>

                <p>
                    在制定通用的预测规则以描述掺杂剂行为方面面临的挑战在于因素的复杂性和变化性，如Lewis酸-碱相互作用和氧化物的性质（可还原性vs不可还原性）。这些因素在确定掺杂剂在氧化物晶格中的行为方面发挥着关键作用。尽管在计算和实验技术方面的进步，仍未能准确地将掺杂剂属性与催化效果相关联，需要进一步探索掺杂剂化学与催化性能之间的微妙互动。

                <p>
                    所使用的模型有两个弱点：它局限于高度分散催化剂的复杂形态，不适用于氧化物的平坦面。这一限制强调了需要更先进的模型来捕捉不同氧化物表面和形态下掺杂剂行为的完整范围的需求。
