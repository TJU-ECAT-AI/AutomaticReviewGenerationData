<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The study of how same-valence dopants (SVDs) and high-valence dopants (HVDs) influence the catalytic properties of different metal oxides has revealed that these dopants can significantly alter the electronic structure, affecting the catalytic activity of metal oxides. For example, HVDs can increase the bandgap, which may be advantageous for photocatalytic water splitting, while SVDs might enhance the surface reactivity due to their interaction with the host oxide. However, formulating general predictive rules for dopant behavior remains challenging, with factors such as Lewis acid-base interactions and the nature of the host oxide playing crucial roles. In reducible oxides, dopants can facilitate electron transfer, whereas in irreducible oxides, they might hinder it.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

<ChineseVersionAnswer>
对不同金属氧化物中同价掺杂剂（SVD）和高价掺杂剂（HVD）的影响研究揭示，这些掺杂剂可以显著改变电子结构，从而影响金属氧化物的催化性能。例如，HVD可能会增加带隙，这对光催化水分解可能有利，而SVD可能会通过与宿主氧化物的相互作用增强表面活性。然而，对于掺杂行为的通用预测规则的形成仍然具有挑战性，其中Lewis酸碱相互作用和宿主氧化物的性质起着关键作用。在可还原氧化物中，掺杂剂可以促进电子转移，而在不可还原氧化物中，它们可能会阻碍电子转移。
</ChineseVersionAnswer>
