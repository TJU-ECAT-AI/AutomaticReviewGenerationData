Wesson's fixation on the distribution of power, particularly the party's monopoly, in his books on the Soviet Russian State and The Soviet State: An Aging Revolution, provides a framework that highlights discrepancies between political reality and mythology. This emphasis could potentially limit the understanding of how the political system operates beyond just power dynamics.
