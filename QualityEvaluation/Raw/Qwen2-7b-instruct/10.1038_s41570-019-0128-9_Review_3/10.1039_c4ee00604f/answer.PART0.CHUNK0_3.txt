<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The reforming processes for converting methane to syngas involve three methods: steam reforming, CO2 reforming (dry reforming), and partial oxidation of methane to syngas. Steam reforming and CO2 reforming have low conversion rates and produce CO2 and H2 rather than CO and H2 at low temperatures. Partial oxidation of methane to syngas is exothermic with an optimal CO:H2 ratio of 1:2 in the reaction product, but requires high temperatures of around 1100-1200 K to obtain CO and H2.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
These equations represent the three possible reforming processes for converting methane to syngas: steam reforming, CO2 reforming (dry reforming), and partial oxidation of methane to syngas. Steam reforming and CO2 reforming are hardly favored at low temperatures (<450 K) due to thermodynamic equilibrium, leading to the production of CO2 and H2 instead of CO and H2. High pressure conditions do not favor the formation of CO and H2 either. Partial oxidation of methane to syngas is exothermic and can readily result in more than 90% methane conversion and 90% selectivity to CO and H2 at high temperatures (1100-1300 K). However, high temperature reactions increase operational costs.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The thermocatalytic decomposition (TCD) of methane involves the conversion of the methane molecule into pure hydrogen and solid carbon with high selectivity. This process only produces solid carbon and hydrogen, offering a simple gas product separation process and the possibility to produce high-quality hydrogen. Different types of carbon, including amorphous carbons, carbon nanofibers, carbon nanotubes, and layered carbon materials, can be produced depending on the operating conditions.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>

<ChineseVersionAnswer>
甲烷的热催化分解（TCD）涉及将甲烷分子转化为纯氢和固体碳，具有高选择性。此过程仅产生固体碳和氢，提供简单的气体产品分离过程以及生产高质量氢气的可能性。根据操作条件，可以产生不同的碳类型，包括无定形碳、碳纳米纤维、碳纳米管和层状碳材料。
</ChineseVersionAnswer>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>
In the oxidative coupling of methane (OCM) reaction, methane is directly converted into ethane, ethylene, and water in the presence of oxygen and a suitable catalyst. Ethylene, a crucial building block in the petrochemical industry, can be further converted to propylene or polymerized to polyethylene. Ethane can be easily converted to ethylene through oxidative dehydrogenation. Despite extensive efforts, only a few catalysts can achieve a C2+ yield higher than 25% and selectivity to C2+ greater than 80%, which are essential for industrial applications.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
