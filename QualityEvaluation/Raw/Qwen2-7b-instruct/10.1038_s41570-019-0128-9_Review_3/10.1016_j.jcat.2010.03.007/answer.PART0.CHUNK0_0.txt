<EnglishVersionAnswer>
The study introduces the utilization of InAl mixed oxide as a novel catalyst for the dehydrogenation of propane into propylene. The researchers have observed that the formation and preservation of metallic indium species at suitable surface concentrations are crucial for achieving efficient propane dehydrogenation. The catalysts under investigation are derived from mixed oxides containing group III elements (Al, Ga, and In), with a focus on the InAl system. This work advances the exploration of mixed oxides in the field of propane dehydrogenation, aiming to develop catalysts that exhibit both improved stability and activity compared to existing materials.
</EnglishVersionAnswer>
