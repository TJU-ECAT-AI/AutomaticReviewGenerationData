<EnglishVersionAnswer>
1. The In2O3Al2O3 mixed oxide catalysts showed significant modifications in surface properties and bulk dispersion of the In2O3 phase, leading to increased reducibility of the InAlO composite.
2. The formation of surface metallic indium species during the reaction is essential for the dehydrogenation performance of the In2O3Al2O3 catalyst.
3. Propylene production is facilitated by both the direct process and the two-step pathway involving simple dehydrogenation followed by the reverse water gas shift reaction.
4. The presence of CO2 enhances the yield of propylene by facilitating simple dehydrogenation coupled with the reverse water gas shift reaction.
5. The creation and maintenance of optimal surface concentrations of catalytically active metallic indium species are crucial for efficient propane dehydrogenation.
