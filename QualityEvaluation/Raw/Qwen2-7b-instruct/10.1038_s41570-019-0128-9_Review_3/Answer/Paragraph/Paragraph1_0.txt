        The comparative analysis of the bimetallic catalysts Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 in the context of CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane, as detailed in the referenced studies \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\] and \[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w\], illuminates their distinct performances, mechanisms, and characteristics. The synthesis of these catalysts involves a blend of a precious metal (Pt or Pd) with a non-precious metal (Fe, Co, or Ni), supported on CeO2, showcasing a versatile approach to enhancing catalytic activity and selectivity \[101\].

        Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 emerged as promising catalysts for CO2-ODH and DRP of propane, respectively, as per the steady-state flow reactions \[102\]. These catalysts exhibit high selectivity and activity, which can be assessed by plotting CO2 conversion against CO selectivity. Unlike traditional oxidation reactions with oxygen leading to CO2 production due to over-oxidation, the CO2-ODH reaction results in a net reduction of CO2, as CO2 is consumed rather than produced \[101\].

        In situ XANES measurements revealed that the Ni3Pt catalyst comprises metallic Pt, while both Fe3Ni and Ni3Pt catalysts consist of metallic Ni under reaction conditions. The binding configurations of all intermediates on Fe3Ni(111) and Pt-terminated Ni3Pt(111) surfaces are similar, with all intermediates binding more strongly to the Fe3Ni(111) surface \[101\].

        Thermogravimetric analysis and in situ XRD measurements indicated that Fe3Ni1/CeO2 does not readily coke or experience surface metal agglomeration under reaction conditions, highlighting its stability \[101\]. The apparent activation energies for the CO2-ODH reaction over Fe3Ni were found to be 115 kJ mol−1, while those for DRP over Ni3Pt were 119 kJ mol−1 \[102\].

        The DFT calculations on bulk-terminated-Fe3Ni(111) and Pt-terminated-Ni3Pt(111) surfaces further elucidated the potential reaction pathways, revealing that the CC bond cleavage pathway is preferred on Ni3Pt(111), whereas bulk-terminated-Fe3Ni(111) favors the CH bond cleavage pathway \[102\].

        A notable synergy between the Fe3Ni catalysts increases the propylene yield compared to monometals, underscoring the benefits of bimetallic systems in enhancing catalytic performance \[102\]. The Fe3Ni catalyst maintains stability with minimal mass loss, indicating a low risk of deactivation due to coking \[102\].

        Considering the advancements and findings discussed in the referenced studies, there lies an opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies in the realm of environmentally benign catalysis for global alkene production and CO2 emissions reduction. Future research endeavors might focus on refining catalyst design, optimizing reaction conditions, and expanding the scope of applications to encompass a broader range of feedstocks and products.

        对比研究了在丙烷CO2-ODH和DRP反应中Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2双金属催化剂的性能、机制和特性，参考文献 \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\] 和 \[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w\] 揭示了它们在不同方面的表现。这些催化剂由贵金属（Pt或Pd）与非贵金属（Fe、Co或Ni）混合组成，并且均支持在CeO2上，这展示了增强催化活性和选择性的多功能性方法 \[101\]。

        Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2被识别为CO2-ODH和丙烷DRP的有前景的催化剂，分别对应于参考文献 \[102\] 中的稳态流反应。这些催化剂具有高选择性和活性，可以通过绘制CO2转化率与CO选择性之间的关系来评估。与传统使用氧气进行氧化导致产生CO2的过氧化反应不同，CO2-ODH反应导致CO2净减少，因为CO2被消耗而不是产生 \[101\]。

        在线原位XANES测量表明，Ni3Pt催化剂包含金属态Pt，而Fe3Ni和Ni3Pt催化剂均包含金属态Ni，在反应条件下。所有中间体在Fe3Ni(111)和Pt-终止Ni3Pt(111)表面上的结合方式相似，所有中间体更强烈地结合到Fe3Ni(111)表面 \[101\]。

        根据热重分析和在线原位XRD测量，Fe3Ni1/CeO2在反应条件下不易结焦或发生表面金属聚集，表明其稳定性 \[101\]。CO2-ODH反应在Fe3Ni上的显着活化能为115 kJ mol−1，而在Ni3Pt上的活化能为119 kJ mol−1 \[102\]。

        DFT计算对bulk-终止Fe3Ni(111)和Pt-终止Ni3Pt(111)表面进一步揭示了潜在的反应途径，结果显示，Ni3Pt(111)上的CC键裂解路径优选，而bulk-终止Fe3Ni(111)倾向于CH键裂解路径 \[102\]。

        Fe3Ni催化剂之间观察到的协同作用提高了与单金属相比的丙烯产量，凸显了双金属系统在提高催化性能方面的优势 \[102\]。Fe3Ni催化剂保持稳定性，仅损失不到原始质量的一半，表明由于结焦导致的主要失活途径的可能性较低 \[102\]。
