        The comparative analysis of the performance, mechanisms, and characteristics of the bimetallic catalysts Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane, as explored through experimental and theoretical (DFT) studies, offers valuable insights into their potential for environmentally benign catalysis and their role in global alkene production. The detailed examination of these catalysts reveals their distinct advantages in terms of selectivity, stability, and reaction mechanisms.

        The experimental results highlight the unique synergistic effects between Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2, where Fe3Ni1/CeO2 demonstrates high selectivity for propylene production via the CO2-ODHP pathway, while Ni3Pt1/CeO2 is highly active for DRP with high selectivity towards CO. This indicates that the bimetallic composition significantly influences the catalyst's ability to generate desired products efficiently. Moreover, the theoretical studies through DFT calculations provide deeper understanding of the reaction pathways involved in the oxidative cleavage of CH and CC bonds, predicting that the CC bond cleavage pathway is preferred on Ni3Pt(111), while bulk-terminated-Fe3Ni(111) favors the CH bond cleavage pathway. These findings contribute to the understanding of the underlying mechanisms governing the CO2-ODH and DRP processes.

        The stability and selectivity of these catalysts are assessed by plotting CO2 conversion against CO selectivity, revealing that Fe3Ni1/CeO2 maintains stability with minimal mass loss, suggesting that coking is not the primary deactivation pathway. This is significant for practical applications as it ensures a longer operational life for the catalysts. The in situ XANES measurements further confirm the metallic states of the catalysts under reaction conditions, supporting the notion of efficient electron transfer and reactivity.

        The results obtained from this comparative study underscore the importance of bimetallic catalysts in achieving a balance between selectivity and stability, which is crucial for the effective use of CO2 in the synthesis of alkenes. The findings not only contribute to the field of catalysis but also pave the way for future research aimed at optimizing catalyst design for enhanced performance and environmental sustainability.

        对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2两种双金属催化剂在丙烷CO2-ODH和DRP反应中的性能、机制和特性进行比较分析，通过实验和理论（DFT）研究揭示了它们在环境友好催化和全球烯烃生产中的潜力。详细的研究表明了这些催化剂在选择性、稳定性和反应机制方面的独特优势。

        实验结果强调了Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2之间的独特协同效应：Fe3Ni1/CeO2通过CO2-ODHP途径高效生成丙烯，而Ni3Pt1/CeO2在DRP过程中对CO具有高选择性。这表明双金属组成显著影响催化剂产生所需产物的效率。此外，通过DFT计算理论研究提供了对CH和CC键氧化裂解反应路径的更深入理解，预测CC键裂解路径在Ni3Pt(111)上更为有利，而bulk-terminated-Fe3Ni(111)倾向于CH键裂解路径。这些发现有助于理解CO2-ODH和DRP过程的基本机制。

        这些催化剂的选择性和稳定性通过将CO2转化率与CO选择性进行绘制来评估，显示Fe3Ni1/CeO2仅损失不到原质量的一半，这表明焦化不是主要失活途径。这对于实际应用至关重要，因为它确保了催化剂的长期使用寿命。在反应条件下进行的in situ XANES测量进一步确认了催化剂的金属状态，支持了有效电子转移和活性的概念。

        根据本研究的比较结果，双金属催化剂平衡选择性和稳定性的重要性被强调，这是有效使用CO2合成烯烃的关键。这些发现不仅为催化领域做出了贡献，也为未来研究提供了优化催化剂设计以提高性能和环境可持续性的方向。
