        Comparative analysis of bimetallic catalysts' performance, mechanisms, and characteristics in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane has been extensively studied, as evidenced by the references \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\] and \[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w\]. These studies have identified Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 as promising catalysts for CO2-ODH and DRP of propane, respectively. The catalysts' selectivity and activity are assessed by plotting CO2 conversion against CO selectivity, highlighting their efficiency in these reactions.

        The CO2-ODH reaction, unlike oxidations with O2, leads to a net reduction of CO2 as CO2 is consumed rather than produced. This unique characteristic is attributed to the selective nature of these catalysts in favoring the reduction of CO2 to alkenes. The in situ XANES measurements reveal distinct binding configurations on the surfaces of Fe3Ni(111) and Pt-terminated Ni3Pt(111), with all intermediates binding more strongly to the Fe3Ni(111) surface. Moreover, both Fe3Ni and Ni3Pt consist of metallic Ni under reaction conditions, indicating their stability and potential for long-term use.

        The activation barriers for CO2-ODHP and DRP over Fe3Ni and Ni3Pt are reported to be 115 kJ mol−1 and 119 kJ mol−1, respectively, suggesting that Fe3Ni may offer a slight advantage in energy efficiency. Thermogravimetric analysis and in situ XRD measurements confirm that Fe3Ni1/CeO2 does not readily coke or experience surface metal agglomeration under reaction conditions, indicating its robust stability.

        Density functional theory (DFT) calculations provide insights into the potential reaction pathways for the oxidative cleavage of propane, predicting that the CC bond cleavage pathway is preferred on Ni3Pt(111), while bulk-terminated-Fe3Ni(111) favors the CH bond cleavage pathway. These findings contribute to a deeper understanding of the reaction mechanisms and suggest that the choice of catalyst can significantly influence the product distribution.

        The synergistic effect between Fe3Ni and Ni3Pt is evident in the increased propylene yield observed over Fe3Ni, compared to monometals. This highlights the importance of bimetallic systems in enhancing the catalytic activity and selectivity for alkene production.

        In conclusion, Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 demonstrate promising performance in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane, offering a sustainable approach to alkene production and CO2 emissions reduction. The synergistic effects, stability, and unique reactivity profiles of these catalysts underscore their potential for environmentally benign catalysis in the global alkene industry.

        对于丙烷在二氧化碳氧化脱氢（CO2-ODH）和脱反应（DRP）过程中的双金属催化剂性能、机制和特性进行了深入比较分析，参考文献 \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\] 和 \[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w\] 显示了Fe3Ni1/CeO2 和Ni3Pt1/CeO2 分别作为CO2-ODH 和DRP 丙烷反应的理想催化剂。催化剂的选择性和活性通过CO2 转化率与CO选择性之间的关系进行评估，这凸显了这些催化剂在这些反应中的效率。

        与使用O2 的氧化相比，CO2-ODH 反应导致CO2 净减少，因为CO2 被消耗而不是产生。这种独特的特性归因于这些催化剂在选择性地将CO2 转化为烯烃方面的高效性。原位XANES 测量揭示了Fe3Ni(111) 和Pt-终止的Ni3Pt(111) 表面的明显不同绑定配置，所有中间体在Fe3Ni(111) 表面的结合更为强烈。此外，在反应条件下，Fe3Ni 和Ni3Pt 都由金属Ni 组成，表明它们的稳定性以及可能的长期应用潜力。

        Fe3Ni 和Ni3Pt 上的CO2-ODHP 和DRP 活化能分别为115 kJ mol−1 和119 kJ mol−1，表明Fe3Ni 在能量效率上可能略有优势。热重分析和原位XRD 测量证实了Fe3Ni1/CeO2 不容易焦化或表面金属团聚，在反应条件下具有很强的稳定性。

        密度泛函理论（DFT）计算提供了关于丙烷氧化裂解潜在途径的见解，预测CC 键裂解途径在Ni3Pt(111) 上更受青睐，而Fe3Ni(111) 批处理倾向于CH 键裂解途径。这些发现加深了对反应机制的理解，并表明催化剂的选择可以显著影响产物分布。

        Fe3Ni1/CeO2 和Ni3Pt1/CeO2 的协同作用体现在Fe3Ni 上观察到的丙烯产率增加，相比于单金属。这强调了在醇类生产中和CO2 排放减少方面利用这些催化剂的可持续性的潜力。

        总之，Fe3Ni1/CeO2 和Ni3Pt1/CeO2 在CO2-ODH 和DRP 丙烷反应中表现出有前景的性能，提供了一种可持续的方法来生产醇类和减少CO2 排放。这些催化剂的协同效应、稳定性和独特的反应性特征突显了它们在醇类生产和CO2 排放减少领域的潜在环境友好型催化应用。
