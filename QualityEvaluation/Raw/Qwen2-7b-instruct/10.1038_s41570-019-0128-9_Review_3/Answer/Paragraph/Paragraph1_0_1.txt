    <English>
        The comparative analysis of bimetallic catalysts Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane reveals a detailed understanding of their composition, selectivity, stability, and reaction mechanisms, as well as their synergistic effects and potential for environmentally benign catalysis. This review focuses on the insights gained from experimental and theoretical (DFT) studies, highlighting the advancements in global alkene production and CO2 emissions reduction.

        The catalysts were synthesized from bimetallic systems that comprised a precious metal (Pt or Pd) and a non-precious metal (Fe, Co or Ni), or combinations of non-precious metals, all supported on CeO2 (ref.101). Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 emerged as promising catalysts for CO2-ODH and DRP of propane, respectively, based on steady-state flow reactions (102). The selectivity and activity of the catalysts were assessed by plotting CO2 conversion against CO selectivity, indicating their efficiency in these processes.

        Unlike oxidations with O2, which produce CO2 due to over-oxidation, the CO2-ODH reaction results in a net reduction of CO2 because CO2 is consumed instead of being produced. The in situ XANES measurements indicated that Ni3Pt/CeO2 featured metallic Pt, while both Fe3Ni/CeO2 and Ni3Pt/CeO2 included metallic Ni under reaction conditions. This finding supports the potential for these catalysts to maintain structural integrity during the reactions.

        DFT calculations on bulk-terminated-Fe3Ni(111) and Pt-terminated-Ni3Pt(111) surfaces provided insights into the potential reaction pathways for the oxidative CH and CC bond cleavage of propane, revealing that the CC bond cleavage pathway is preferred on Ni3Pt(111), while bulk-terminated-Fe3Ni(111) favors the CH bond cleavage pathway. These results offer a deeper understanding of the mechanism behind these catalytic processes.

        Thermogravimetric analysis and in situ XRD measurements showed that Fe3Ni1/CeO2 does not readily coke or experience surface metal agglomeration under reaction conditions, indicating its stability and potential for long-term use. The apparent activation energies for CO2-ODHP and DRP over Fe3Ni and Ni3Pt were 115 kJ mol−1 and 119 kJ mol−1, respectively, highlighting the energy efficiency of these processes.

        The Fe3Ni catalyst demonstrated a synergistic effect, increasing propylene yield compared to monometals. This suggests that the combination of Fe and Ni in the bimetallic system leads to enhanced catalytic activity and selectivity, which could have significant implications for global alkene production and CO2 emissions reduction.

        The TGA results indicated that the Fe3Ni catalyst lost only a negligible amount of its original mass, making it unlikely that the main deactivation pathway is due to coking. This stability is crucial for maintaining high yields and efficiency in industrial applications.

        Overall, the experimental and theoretical studies provide a comprehensive understanding of the performance, mechanisms, and characteristics of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane. These insights suggest that these catalysts hold great promise for the development of environmentally benign catalysis, contributing to sustainable alkene production and reducing CO2 emissions.

        Looking ahead, future research might focus on exploring the synergistic effects of different bimetallic systems, investigating the role of CeO2 support in enhancing catalytic properties, and optimizing reaction conditions to further improve the efficiency of CO2-ODH and DRP processes. Additionally, the integration of advanced materials and novel synthesis methods could lead to the discovery of even more efficient and sustainable catalysts for alkene production and CO2 utilization.

        The integrative understanding and prospective outlook of this work underscores the importance of continued investigation into bimetallic catalysts for CO2-ODH and DRP reactions. By leveraging the synergistic effects observed in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2, researchers can develop new catalysts that not only enhance global alkene production but also significantly reduce CO2 emissions, thereby addressing critical environmental challenges.

    <Chinese>
        对于CO2-ODH和DRP反应中丙烷使用Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2双金属催化剂的比较分析揭示了它们在组成、选择性、稳定性和反应机制方面的详细理解，以及协同效应和环境友好催化潜力。本综述重点介绍了实验和理论（DFT）研究带来的见解，突出了全球烯烃生产与二氧化碳减排的进展。

        合成的催化剂包括由贵金属（Pt或Pd）和非贵金属（Fe、Co或Ni）组成的二元系统，或者非贵金属的组合，全部支持在CeO2上（ref.101）。Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2分别作为CO2-ODH和DRP的有希望的催化剂，基于稳态流动反应（102）。通过将CO2转化率与CO选择性进行绘制，评估了催化剂的选择性和活性，表明这些过程的效率。

        与使用O2的氧化反应不同，由于CO2被消耗而不是产生，CO2-ODH反应导致CO2净减少。原位XANES测量表明Ni3Pt/CeO2含有金属Pt，而Fe3Ni/CeO2和Ni3Pt/CeO2都包含金属Ni，在反应条件下。这支持这些催化剂在反应条件下保持结构完整性的潜力。

        DFT计算在Fe3Ni(111)的体终止表面和Ni3Pt(111)的Pt终止表面上提供了关于丙烷氧化CH和CC键断裂潜在反应路径的深入理解，揭示了CC键断裂途径在Ni3Pt(111)上更优选，而体终止Fe3Ni(111)倾向于CH键断裂途径。这些结果为理解这些催化过程的机制提供了更深层次的理解。

        热重分析和原位XRD测量显示Fe3Ni1/CeO2在反应条件下不会迅速结焦或经历表面金属聚集，表明其稳定性，暗示其长期应用的可能性。CO2-ODHP和DRP在Fe3Ni和Ni3Pt上的显着激活能分别为115 kJ mol−1和119 kJ mol−1，强调了这些过程的能量效率。

        Fe3Ni催化剂表现出协同效应，提高了丙烯产量，相比单金属具有增强的催化活性和选择性。这表明Fe和Ni在二元系统中的结合可以提高催化剂性能和选择性，对全球烯烃生产和二氧化碳减排具有重要意义。

        TGA结果显示Fe3Ni催化剂仅失去原始质量的一小部分，这意味着主要的失活途径不太可能是结焦。这种稳定性对于维持高产率和工业应用的效率至关重要。

        总体而言，实验和理论研究表明Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2在CO2-ODH和DRP反应中丙烷的性能、机制和特性。这些洞察表明，这些催化剂在发展环境友好的催化作用方面拥有巨大潜力，有助于可持续烯烃生产和减少二氧化碳排放。

        展望未来，未来的研究可能集中在探索不同的二元系统协同效应、调查CeO2支持如何增强催化性能，以及优化反应条件以进一步提高CO2-ODH和DRP过程的效率。此外，采用先进的材料和新颖的合成方法可能引领发现更多高效且可持续的催化剂用于烯烃生产与二氧化碳利用。

        本工作的整合理解和前瞻展望凸显了继续研究CO2-ODH和DRP反应中双金属催化剂的重要性。通过利用Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2观察到的协同效应，研究人员可以开发出不仅增强全球烯烃生产，而且显著降低二氧化碳排放的新催化剂，从而应对关键的环境挑战。
