        Comparative analysis of bimetallic catalysts' performance, mechanisms, and characteristics in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane: An experimental and theoretical study focusing on Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2

        Detailed analysis established from the study of reference \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\]. Synthesized comprehension stemming from references \[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w\] and \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\].

        The comparative study underscores the distinct roles of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane. Both catalysts demonstrate remarkable selectivity and activity in their respective pathways, with Fe3Ni1/CeO2 favoring CO2-ODH and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 being active for DRP. A synergistic effect emerges when comparing Fe3Ni1/CeO2 to Ni and Fe monometals, indicating enhanced propylene yield attributed to the bimetallic structure.

        The experimental and theoretical (DFT) studies reveal that the activation barriers for CO2-ODH and DRP are 115 kJ mol−1 and 119 kJ mol−1, respectively, over Fe3Ni and Ni3Pt. These values suggest that the Fe3Ni system exhibits lower activation energy, making it more efficient in the CO2-ODH process. Moreover, the Fe3Ni catalyst maintains stability with minimal mass loss, which could be attributed to its resistance against coking under reaction conditions, a critical factor for long-term operation.

        The in situ XANES measurements provide insights into the surface chemistry of these catalysts, highlighting the metallic states of Ni and Pt in Ni3Pt/CeO2 and Fe3Ni/CeO2, respectively. This information is crucial for understanding the catalytic activity and selectivity of these materials.

        Density functional theory (DFT) calculations offer a deeper understanding of the reaction mechanisms, revealing that the CC bond cleavage pathway is preferred on Ni3Pt(111), while bulk-terminated-Fe3Ni(111) favors the CH bond cleavage pathway. This distinction in reaction mechanisms underscores the importance of surface structure in determining the efficiency of CO2 conversion and alkene production.

        In summary, the study provides a comprehensive evaluation of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 as promising catalysts for CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane. Their unique properties, including selectivity, stability, and activity, make them attractive candidates for enhancing global alkene production and reducing CO2 emissions.

        这项比较研究表明，在丙烷CO2-ODH和DRP反应中，Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2两种催化剂在各自途径中的独特作用。这两种催化剂分别在CO2-ODH和DRP过程中显示出显著的选择性和活性。Fe3Ni1/CeO2表现出协同效应，其丙烯产量相对于单金属Ni和Fe有显著提高，这归因于双金属结构的增强效果。

        实验和理论（DFT）研究揭示了CO2-ODH和DRP的活化能分别为115 kJ mol−1和119 kJ mol−1，分别在Fe3Ni和Ni3Pt上。这些值表明Fe3Ni系统具有较低的活化能，使其在CO2-ODH过程中更有效。此外，Fe3Ni催化剂仅损失原质量的不到一半，这可能归因于其在反应条件下对结焦的抵抗，这是确保长期运行的关键因素。

        在位XANES测量提供了这些催化剂表面化学的见解，指出了Ni和Pt在Ni3Pt/CeO2和Fe3Ni/CeO2中的金属状态。这些信息对于理解这些材料的催化活性和选择性至关重要。

        密度泛函理论（DFT）计算提供了反应机制的深入了解，揭示了Ni3Pt(111)上的CC键断裂途径被优先考虑，而Fe3Ni(111)表面则倾向于CH键断裂途径。这种反应机制的区别强调了表面结构在确定CO2转换效率和烷烃生产效率中的重要性。

        总之，这项研究对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2作为CO2-ODH和DRP反应中丙烷的有前景催化剂进行了全面评估。它们的独特性质，包括选择性、稳定性和活性，使它们成为促进全球烷烃生产和减少CO2排放的有吸引力的候选者。
