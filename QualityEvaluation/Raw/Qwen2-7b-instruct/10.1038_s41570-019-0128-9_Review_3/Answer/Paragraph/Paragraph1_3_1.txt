        The comparative analysis of the performance, mechanisms, and characteristics of bimetallic catalysts Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane has been extensively studied. The examination of various catalyst compositions, including bimetallic systems containing precious metals (Pt or Pd) and non-precious metals (Fe, Co, or Ni), as well as combinations of non-precious metals supported on CeO2, has provided valuable insights into the efficiency of these catalysts (Ref. \[101\]). The results from steady-state flow reactions have identified Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 as promising catalysts for CO2-ODH and DRP of propane, respectively (Ref. \[102\], Fig. 8). The evaluation of catalyst selectivity and activity involves plotting CO2 conversion against CO selectivity, highlighting the importance of these metrics in assessing catalyst performance (Ref. \[102\]).

        Unlike oxidations with oxygen leading to over-oxidation and the subsequent production of CO2, the CO2-ODH reaction results in a net reduction of CO2, as CO2 is consumed rather than produced (Ref. \[102\]). In situ XANES measurements have revealed that the Ni3Pt/CeO2 catalyst consists of metallic Pt, while both Fe3Ni/CeO2 and Ni3Pt/CeO2 contain metallic Ni (Ref. \[102\]). The results also show that the binding configurations of all intermediates on Fe3Ni(111) and Pt-terminated Ni3Pt(111) surfaces are similar, with all intermediates binding more strongly to the Fe3Ni(111) surface (Ref. \[102\]). Thermogravimetric analysis and in situ XRD measurements confirm that Fe3Ni1/CeO2 does not readily coke or experience surface metal agglomeration under reaction conditions, indicating its stability (Ref. \[102\]).

        The activation barriers for CO2-ODHP and DRP over Fe3Ni and Ni3Pt are determined to be 115 kJ mol−1 and 119 kJ mol−1, respectively (Ref. \[102\]). The TGA results suggest that the Fe3Ni catalyst experiences only minimal mass loss, implying that the main deactivation pathway is unlikely to be coking (Ref. \[102\]). The in-situ XANES measurements have identified that under reaction conditions, the Ni3Pt catalyst consists of metallic Pt, while both Fe3Ni and Ni3Pt catalysts consist of metallic Ni (Ref. \[102\]). Density functional theory (DFT) calculations performed on bulk-terminated-Fe3Ni(111) and Pt-terminated-Ni3Pt(111) surfaces provide further insight into the potential reaction pathways for the oxidative cleavage of CH and CC bonds in propane, forming *CH3CHCH2+H2O(g) and *CH3CH2+*CO+H2O(g), respectively (Ref. \[102\]). These calculations predict that the CC bond cleavage pathway is favored on Ni3Pt(111), while bulk-terminated-Fe3Ni(111) prefers the CH bond cleavage pathway (Ref. \[102\]).

        The synergy between Fe3Ni and Ni3Pt catalysts in increasing propylene yield compared to monometals is evidenced by the propylene yields on a C3H8 basis, which are 1.6% for Fe3Ni, significantly higher than the respective monometals (Ref. \[102\]). The DFT results further emphasize the distinct reaction pathways favored by each catalyst, highlighting the potential for optimized catalytic performance through tailored bimetallic composition.

        The comprehensive experimental and theoretical (DFT) studies on Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 have provided a robust foundation for understanding the comparative performance, mechanisms, and characteristics of these bimetallic catalysts in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane. The findings not only contribute to the development of efficient CO2 utilization technologies but also underscore the potential for environmentally benign catalysis, offering promising avenues for global alkene production and CO2 emissions reduction (Refs. \[102\], \[101\]).

        The integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements and findings discussed in the file, there lies an opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies. Future research endeavors might focus on enhancing the synergistic effects between bimetallic components, optimizing reaction conditions, and exploring novel catalyst designs that can further improve the efficiency and selectivity of CO2-ODH and DRP reactions. The integration of advanced computational tools with experimental methods could lead to the discovery of catalysts with superior performance, paving the way for sustainable alkene production and effective CO2 mitigation strategies.

        对于Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2两种双金属催化剂在丙烷CO2-ODH和DRP反应中的性能、机制和特性进行了深入比较分析。对由贵金属（Pt或Pd）和非贵金属（Fe、Co或Ni）组成的双金属系统，以及非贵金属的组合在CeO2上的研究，提供了这些催化剂效率的关键见解（参考文献\[101\]）。稳态流动反应的结果将Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2识别为CO2-ODH和丙烷DRP的有前景催化剂（参考文献\[102\]，图8）。通过将CO2转化率与CO选择性进行绘图，评估催化剂的选择性和活性，强调了这些指标在评价催化剂性能中的重要性（参考文献\[102\]）。

        相比于氧气氧化导致过氧化和随后产生CO2的情况，CO2-ODH反应实现了CO2净减少，因为CO2被消耗而不是产生（参考文献\[102\]）。原位XANES测量表明，Ni3Pt/CeO2催化剂由金属Pt组成，而Fe3Ni/CeO2和Ni3Pt/CeO2均含有金属Ni（参考文献\[102\]）。结果还显示，在Fe3Ni(111)和Pt-终止的Ni3Pt(111)表面上，所有中间体的结合配置相似，所有中间体更强烈地结合到Fe3Ni(111)表面（参考文献\[102\]）。热重分析和原位XRD测量证实，Fe3Ni1/CeO2在反应条件下不易结焦或表面金属聚集，表明其稳定性（参考文献\[102\]）。

        Fe3Ni和Ni3Pt催化剂在CO2-ODHP和DRP过程中的激活能分别确定为115 kJ mol−1和119 kJ mol−1（参考文献\[102\]）。TGA结果显示，Fe3Ni催化剂仅经历微小的质量损失，暗示主要的失活途径很可能不是结焦（参考文献\[102\]）。原位XANES测量表明，在反应条件下，Ni3Pt催化剂由金属Pt组成，而Fe3Ni和Ni3Pt催化剂都含有金属Ni（参考文献\[102\]）。在Fe3Ni(111)和Pt-终止的Ni3Pt(111)表面上执行的密度泛函理论（DFT）计算进一步揭示了丙烷氧化CH和CC键断裂的潜在反应途径，形成*CH3CHCH2+H2O(g)和*CH3CH2+*CO+H2O(g)，分别（参考文献\[102\]）。这些计算预测，CC键断裂途径在Ni3Pt(111)上占优势，而bulk-终止的Fe3Ni(111)则倾向于CH键断裂途径（参考文献\[102\]）。

        Fe3Ni和Ni3Pt催化剂之间的协同作用，使得丙烯产量相对于单金属显著增加的证据是基于C3H8基础提供的丙烯产量，分别为Fe3Ni的1.6%，远高于各自的单金属（参考文献\[102\]）。DFT结果进一步强调了每种催化剂所偏好的不同反应途径，突显了通过定制双金属组成优化催化性能的潜力。

        执行Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2的全面实验和理论（DFT）研究不仅为CO2-ODH和DRP反应中这两种双金属催化剂的比较性能、机制和特性的理解提供了坚实的基础，而且还突出了环境友好催化的可能性，为全球烯烃生产提供有希望的途径，并减少CO2排放（参考文献\[102\]，\[101\]）。

        整合理解和前瞻展望：考虑到文件中讨论的进展和发现，存在探索新兴领域和创新方法的机会。未来研究努力可能集中在增强双金属成分之间的协同作用、优化反应条件以及探索新型催化剂设计，以进一步提高CO2-ODH和DRP反应的效率和选择性。将高级计算工具与实验方法相结合，有望发现性能优越的催化剂，为可持续烯烃生产和有效CO2减排策略铺平道路。
