        Comparative analysis of bimetallic catalysts’ performance, mechanisms, and characteristics in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane: An experimental and theoretical study focusing on Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 reveals their distinct roles and synergistic effects. The study by \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\] highlights that Fe3Ni1/CeO2 exhibits promising selectivity for propylene via the CO2-ODH pathway, while Ni3Pt1/CeO2 is active for DRP with high selectivity towards CO. The Fe3Ni catalyst demonstrates a synergistic effect, significantly increasing propylene yield compared to monometals. The activation barriers for CO2-ODH and DRP over Fe3Ni and Ni3Pt are found to be 115 kJ mol−1 and 119 kJ mol−1, respectively. 

        Thermogravimetric analysis and in situ XRD measurements confirm that Fe3Ni1/CeO2 maintains stability under reaction conditions, showing minimal mass loss, thus indicating that coking is not the primary deactivation pathway. The in-situ XANES measurements reveal that Ni3Pt/CeO2 consists of metallic Pt, while both Fe3Ni and Ni3Pt catalysts include metallic Ni under reaction conditions.

        Density functional theory (DFT) calculations provide insights into the potential reaction pathways for the oxidative CH and CC bond cleavage of propane, predicting that the CC bond cleavage pathway is favored on Ni3Pt(111), while bulk-terminated-Fe3Ni(111) favors the CH bond cleavage pathway. These findings underscore the unique capabilities of these bimetallic catalysts in CO2 utilization, highlighting their potential for environmentally benign catalysis in the context of global alkene production and CO2 emissions reduction.

        The experimental and theoretical studies collectively demonstrate the efficiency of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane, offering a robust platform for the development of sustainable catalysts in chemical engineering. The synergistic effects observed between the bimetallic systems suggest avenues for further optimization and integration into industrial processes aimed at reducing CO2 emissions and enhancing alkene production.

        The future research endeavors might focus on exploring the broader scope of bimetallic catalyst design, leveraging the insights gained from the current studies to create catalysts with even higher selectivity, stability, and activity for CO2 conversion. The potential for innovative methodologies and emerging fields, grounded in the detailed understanding of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2, opens new horizons for environmental remediation and the production of valuable chemicals from renewable resources.
        
        The comparative analysis of these bimetallic catalysts showcases the importance of synergistic effects in catalytic systems, particularly in the context of CO2 utilization. By integrating experimental and theoretical approaches, we can better understand the underlying mechanisms governing the performance of these catalysts, paving the way for the development of more efficient and environmentally friendly technologies for CO2 conversion and alkene production.

        在针对丙烷在二氧化碳氧化脱氢（CO2-ODH）和脱水反应（DRP）中的性能、机制和特性进行的比较分析中，以Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2为焦点的实验和理论研究表明了它们在不同反应路径中的独特作用及其协同效应。来自\[[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]\]的研究指出，Fe3Ni1/CeO2通过CO2-ODH途径显示出对丙烯选择性高的特点，而Ni3Pt1/CeO2则在DRP中表现出高选择性向CO的活性。

        Fe3Ni1/CeO2催化剂显示出了协同效应，显著提高了丙烯的产率，相较于单金属而言。CO2-ODH和DRP的活化能分别在Fe3Ni和Ni3Pt上被测定为115 kJ mol−1和119 kJ mol−1。热重分析和原位X射线衍射测量表明，在反应条件下，Fe3Ni1/CeO2催化剂仅损失不到其原始质量的一半，因此推测主要的失活途径很可能不是焦碳化。

        在原位X射线吸收光谱（XANES）测量中发现，Ni3Pt/CeO2由金属态铂组成，而Fe3Ni和Ni3Pt催化剂在反应条件下均包含金属态Ni。

        密度泛函理论（DFT）计算揭示了丙烷氧化脱氢和脱水可能的反应途径，预测CC键断裂途径在Ni3Pt(111)上占优势，而Fe3Ni(111)表面则倾向于CH键断裂途径。这些发现强调了这些双金属催化剂在CO2利用中的高效性，凸显了它们在减少CO2排放和提高烯烃生产方面环境友好型催化体系的发展潜力。

        这些实验和理论研究共同证明了Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2在CO2-ODH和DRP反应中的有效性，为化学工程领域提供了坚实的平台。双金属系统中观察到的协同效应提示着进一步优化和整合催化剂的可能性，以实现更高选择性、稳定性和活性的CO2转化。基于当前研究的详细理解，这一领域的未来发展充满了机遇，包括探索更广泛的双金属催化剂设计，并利用这些见解开发更高效、环境友好的技术来处理CO2并生产有价值的化学品。

        双金属催化剂的比较分析突显了协同效应在催化系统中的重要性，尤其是在CO2利用的背景下。通过结合实验和理论方法，我们可以更好地理解这些催化剂性能背后的作用机制，为开发更高效、更环保的技术提供道路，旨在促进CO2转化和烯烃生产。
        
        在这一领域的未来研究中，应着重于探索更广泛的双金属催化剂设计，并利用当前研究的见解来创建具有更高选择性、稳定性和活性的催化剂，用于CO2转化。由此产生的新视野将为环境修复和利用可再生资源生产有价值化学品提供新的途径。
