        Detailed analysis has been conducted on the comparative study of the performance, mechanisms, and characteristics of bimetallic catalysts Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane, as reported in reference \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\]. The examination includes their selectivity, stability, and activity, evaluated through both experimental and theoretical (DFT) methods. Insights into their synergistic effects and potential for environmentally benign catalysis in the context of global alkene production and CO2 emissions reduction have been drawn.

        The catalysts were part of bimetallic systems consisting of a precious metal (Pt or Pd) and a non-precious metal (Fe, Co, or Ni), or combinations of non-precious metals, all supported on CeO2 (reference 101). The steady-state flow reactions identified Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 as promising catalysts for CO2-ODH and DRP of propane, respectively (Figure 8). The selectivity and activity of the catalysts were assessed by plotting CO2 conversion against CO selectivity. Unlike oxidations with O2, which result in CO2 due to over-oxidation, the CO2-ODH reaction leads to a net reduction of CO2, as CO2 is consumed rather than produced.

        In situ XANES measurements showed that Ni3Pt/CeO2 contains metallic Pt, while both Fe3Ni/CeO2 and Ni3Pt/CeO2 consist of metallic Ni under reaction conditions. The results also highlighted that the binding configurations of all intermediates on Fe3Ni(111) and Pt-terminated Ni3Pt(111) surfaces are similar, with all intermediates binding more strongly to the Fe3Ni(111) surface. Thermogravimetric analysis and in situ XRD measurements confirmed that Fe3Ni1/CeO2 does not readily coke or experience surface metal agglomeration under reaction conditions.

        The apparent activation energies for CO2-ODHP and DRP were measured in the temperature range of 803–843 K, revealing an activation barrier of 115 kJ mol−1 over Fe3Ni for propane CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation, and 119 kJ mol−1 over Ni3Pt for reforming. The TGA results indicated that the Fe3Ni catalyst loses less than half a percent of its original mass, suggesting that the main deactivation pathway is not due to coking. Density functional theory (DFT) calculations on the surfaces of bulk-terminated-Fe3Ni(111) and Pt-terminated-Ni3Pt(111) provided insights into the potential reaction pathways for the oxidative cleavage of propane's CH and CC bonds, forming *CH3CHCH2+H2O(g) and *CH3CH2+*CO+H2O(g), respectively. These calculations predicted that the CC bond cleavage pathway is favored on Ni3Pt(111), while bulk-terminated-Fe3Ni(111) favors the CH bond cleavage pathway.

        The comparative analysis reveals that Fe3Ni1/CeO2 exhibits promising selectivity toward propylene via the CO2-ODHP pathway, whereas Ni3Pt1/CeO2 is active for the DRP with high selectivity toward CO. The Fe3Ni catalyst demonstrates a synergistic effect, increasing propylene yield compared to monometals. The activation barriers for CO2-ODH and DRP are 115 kJ mol−1 and 119 kJ mol−1, respectively, over Fe3Ni and Ni3Pt. The Fe3Ni catalyst maintains stability with minimal mass loss, suggesting that deactivation is unlikely due to coking. Both Fe3Ni and Ni3Pt consist of metallic Ni under reaction conditions. DFT calculations predict that the CC bond cleavage pathway is favored on Ni3Pt(111), while bulk-terminated-Fe3Ni(111) favors the CH bond cleavage pathway.

        The findings underscore the potential of these bimetallic catalysts in the efficient conversion of CO2 to valuable chemicals and fuels, offering a sustainable approach to mitigate CO2 emissions and contribute to global alkene production. Future research might explore the integration of these catalysts in industrial processes, optimize their performance, and investigate the impact of operational parameters on their activity and selectivity.

        根据\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\]中的深入研究，对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2两种双金属催化剂在丙烷CO2-ODH和DRP反应中的性能、机制和特性进行了比较分析。这些催化剂的评估包括选择性、稳定性和活性的实验和理论（DFT）方法。这些研究揭示了它们在减少二氧化碳排放和全球烯烃生产方面的潜在环境友好型催化潜力。

        考察的催化剂由贵金属（Pt或Pd）和非贵金属（Fe、Co或Ni），或者非贵金属的组合组成，并且都支持在CeO2上（参考101）。稳态流反应识别出Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2分别作为CO2-ODH和DRP丙烷反应的有前景催化剂。催化剂的选择性和活性通过CO2转化率与CO选择性的图示进行评估。与使用O2的氧化作用产生CO2不同的是，CO2-ODH反应导致CO2净减少，因为CO2被消耗而不是产生。

        在线XANES测量表明，Ni3Pt/CeO2包含金属Pt，而Fe3Ni/CeO2和Ni3Pt/CeO2在反应条件下均包含金属Ni。结果还显示，在Fe3Ni(111)和Pt-终止Ni3Pt(111)表面的所有中间体的结合配置相似，所有中间体更强烈地结合在Fe3Ni(111)表面上。热重分析和原位XRD测量证实，Fe3Ni1/CeO2在反应条件下不会迅速结焦或经历表面金属聚集。

        在803–843 K的温度范围内测量的显着活化能为CO2-ODHP和DRP提供了活性屏障：Fe3Ni在115 kJ mol−1下对丙烷CO2氧化脱氢化具有活性，而Ni3Pt在119 kJ mol−1下对重整具有活性。TGA结果表明，Fe3Ni催化剂仅损失不到其原始质量的半个百分点，这表明主要的失活途径不太可能是结焦。Fe3Ni和Ni3Pt在反应条件下均由金属Ni组成。密度泛函理论（DFT）计算在bulk-terminated-Fe3Ni(111)和Pt-终止Ni3Pt(111)表面上提供有关丙烷CH和CC键断裂可能路径的信息，形成*CH3CHCH2+H2O(g)和*CH3CH2+*CO+H2O(g)。这些计算预测CC键断裂路径在Ni3Pt(111)上占优势，而bulk-terminated-Fe3Ni(111)则倾向于CH键断裂路径。

        比较分析表明，Fe3Ni1/CeO2表现出通过CO2-ODHP途径对丙烯的有希望的选择性，而Ni3Pt1/CeO2在DRP中对CO具有高选择性。Fe3Ni催化剂展示了协同效应，增加了丙烯产量与单金属相比。CO2-ODH和DRP的激活壁垒分别为115 kJ mol−1和119 kJ mol−1，分别位于Fe3Ni和Ni3Pt上。Fe3Ni催化剂保持稳定性，仅损失不到其原始质量的半个百分点，这表明由于结焦导致的失活不太可能是主要途径。Fe3Ni和Ni3Pt在反应条件下均由金属Ni组成。DFT计算预测CC键断裂路径在Ni3Pt(111)上占优势，而bulk-terminated-Fe3Ni(111)则倾向于CH键断裂路径。

        这些发现强调了这些双金属催化剂在有效转换CO2为有价值化学品和燃料方面的潜力，为减少二氧化碳排放和促进全球烯烃生产提供了一种可持续解决方案。未来的研究可能关注改进这些双金属催化剂的合成和表征技术以提高效率和选择性。此外，集成催化系统的发展，将CO2-ODH和DRP结合在一起，可以实现CO2同时转化为多种产品，优化资源利用和环境效益。此外，探索替代支撑材料和混合金属氧化物催化剂的开发，可以扩展催化库，可能引领CO2再利用和烯烃生产的新型途径。此研究的结果为推动绿色化学和催化领域的进展奠定了基础，贡献了可持续解决方案的开发。
