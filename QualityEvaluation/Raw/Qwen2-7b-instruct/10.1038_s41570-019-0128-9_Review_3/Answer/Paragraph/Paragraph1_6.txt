        The comparative analysis of bimetallic catalysts Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane has been extensively studied, providing insights into their unique performance, mechanisms, and characteristics. This review delves into the experimental and theoretical (DFT) aspects, focusing on their selectivity, stability, and synergistic effects.

        The detailed analysis established from the study of reference \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\] highlights the distinct roles of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions, respectively. The synthesized comprehension stemming from references \[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w\] further elucidates the underlying mechanisms and performance metrics of these catalysts.

        Fe3Ni1/CeO2 is noted for its promising selectivity towards propylene production via the CO2-ODHP pathway, while Ni3Pt1/CeO2 exhibits high selectivity towards CO in DRP. The synergistic effect between Fe3Ni and Ni3Pt catalysts increases propylene yield compared to their respective monometal counterparts. This synergy is substantiated by the higher propylene yield observed on Fe3Ni (1.6% C3H6 yield) compared to Ni (0.4%) and Fe (0.2%).

        The activation barriers for CO2-ODHP and DRP over Fe3Ni and Ni3Pt are found to be 115 kJ mol−1 and 119 kJ mol−1, respectively, indicating the energetic requirements for these reactions. Thermogravimetric analysis reveals that the Fe3Ni catalyst maintains stability with minimal mass loss, suggesting that deactivation is unlikely due to coking.

        In situ XANES measurements indicate that Ni3Pt/CeO2 consists of metallic Pt, while both Fe3Ni/CeO2 and Ni3Pt/CeO2 include metallic Ni. This suggests that the metallic state of Ni in these catalysts plays a crucial role in their reactivity.

        Density functional theory (DFT) calculations provide deeper insights into the potential reaction pathways for the oxidative cleavage of propane bonds. It is found that the CC bond cleavage pathway is preferred on Ni3Pt(111), while bulk-terminated-Fe3Ni(111) favors the CH bond cleavage pathway. This prediction aligns with the observed selectivities of the catalysts in the CO2-ODH and DRP reactions.

        These findings underscore the potential of bimetallic catalysts in achieving environmentally benign catalysis for global alkene production and CO2 emissions reduction. The insights gathered from experimental and theoretical studies offer a foundation for future research endeavors aimed at exploring emerging fields and innovative methodologies.

        In conclusion, the comparative analysis of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane showcases the promise of bimetallic catalysts in sustainable chemical synthesis.         参考文献\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\]中的详细分析表明了Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2在CO2-ODH和DRP反应中的独特作用。来自参考文献\[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w\]的综合理解进一步阐明了这些催化剂的内在机制和性能指标。

        Fe3Ni1/CeO2表现出通过CO2-ODHP途径选择性地生产丙烯的能力，而Ni3Pt1/CeO2在DRP中对CO具有高选择性。Fe3Ni和Ni3Pt催化剂之间的协同作用增加了丙烯产量，相比各自的单金属催化剂，Fe3Ni的丙烯产率更高（1.6% C3H6产率），而Ni为0.4%，Fe为0.2%。

        CO2-ODHP和DRP在Fe3Ni和Ni3Pt上的激活势垒分别为115 kJ mol−1和119 kJ mol−1，表明这些反应的能量需求。热重分析显示，Fe3Ni催化剂仅损失不到其原始质量的一半，因此，主要的失活途径不太可能是焦化。

        在线原位XANES测量表明，Ni3Pt/CeO2由金属态Pt组成，而Fe3Ni/CeO2和Ni3Pt/CeO2均包含金属态Ni。这表明在反应条件下Ni的金属状态对于这些催化剂的活性起着关键作用。
