Enhancing catalytic performance through substitutional cation doping in metal oxide catalysts has emerged as a promising strategy for modifying surface chemistry and disrupting chemical bonding, with significant implications for active centers and industrially relevant reactions. Doping can alter metal particle size and morphology, which are critical for catalytic performance, as demonstrated by the enhanced activity of flat metal deposits in reactions such as CO oxidation and propanol oxidation \[10.1021/ja211396\]. The particle perimeter enables simultaneous adsorbate interaction with metal and oxide, explaining the shape-dependence in catalysis. Mo dopants in CaO films lead to 2D Au island growth via electron transfer, stabilizing metal-oxide adhesion, while Li doping blocks this charge exchange, reducing electrons for Au, forcing 3D growth \[10.1021/ja211396\]. These findings reveal how doping can shape metal ad-particles and highlight the internal charge compensation effect of codoping.

Doping can also transform metal oxide surfaces into Lewis acids or bases, enhancing halogen dissociation and catalytic activity. La-doped CeO2 acts as a Lewis acid, strengthening halogen binding to oxygen sites, while Ta-doped ceria, as a Lewis base, intensifies halogen binding to cerium sites \[10.1021/jp211693v\]. These changes improve catalytic activity and alter active centers, potentially boosting industrial reactions.

Moreover, substitutional cation doping creates an electron deficit when lower-valence cations replace host cations, enhancing the oxide's electron-accepting ability and making it a stronger acid \[10.1021/jp301341\]. This increases the oxide's electron acceptance ability, leading to stronger base binding on doped surfaces compared to undoped ones. In reducible oxides, cations can reduce in the presence of a base, while in irreducible oxides, the base fills the hole created by the dopant. These modifications can alter active centers and improve industrial reactions.

The disruption of surface chemical bonding by substitutional cation doping potentially enhances catalytic activity, with active centers forming near the dopant or involving the dopant itself \[10.1021/cr300418s_0\]. This approach could lead to improvements in industrially relevant reactions by favorably altering the oxide's catalytic properties.

通过金属氧化物催化剂中的替代性阳离子掺杂来增强催化性能，已成为一种有前景的策略，用于修改表面化学和破坏化学键，对活性中心和工业相关反应具有重大影响。掺杂可以改变金属粒子的大小和形态，这对催化性能至关重要，正如在CO氧化和丙醇氧化等反应中，扁平金属沉积物的增强活性所展示的那样\[10.1021/ja211396\]。粒子周界使吸附物能够同时与金属和氧化物相互作用，解释了催化作用的形状依赖性。Mo掺杂剂在CaO薄膜中导致通过电子转移的2D Au岛生长，稳定了金属-氧化物的粘附，而Li掺杂阻止了这种电荷交换，减少了Au的电子，迫使3D生长\[10.1021/ja211396\]。这些发现揭示了掺杂如何塑造金属吸附粒子，并突出了共掺杂的内部电荷补偿效应。

掺杂还可以将金属氧化物表面转化为Lewis酸或碱，增强卤素解离和催化活性。La掺杂的CeO2作为Lewis酸，加强了卤素与氧位点的结合，而Ta掺杂的铈作为Lewis碱，增强了卤素与铈位点的结合\[10.1021/jp211693v\]。这些变化提高了催化活性，改变了活性中心，可能促进了工业反应。

此外，替代性阳离子掺杂在较低价态的阳离子取代宿主阳离子时，会产生电子缺陷，增强了氧化物的电子接受能力，使其成为更强的酸\[10.1021/jp301341\]。这增加了氧化物的电子接受能力，导致与未掺杂表面相比，更强的碱在掺杂表面上的结合。在可还原氧化物中，阳离子可以在碱的存在下还原，而在不可还原氧化物中，碱总是填充由掺杂剂产生的空穴。这些修改可以改变活性中心，改善工业反应。

替代性阳离子掺杂对表面化学键的破坏可能增强催化活性，活性中心可能在掺杂剂附近形成，或涉及掺杂剂本身\[10.1021/cr300418s_0\]。这种方法可以通过有利地改变氧化物的催化性质，导致工业相关反应的改进。
