                The strategic introduction of substitutional cations into metal oxide catalysts significantly modifies surface chemistry and disrupts chemical bonding, leading to enhanced catalytic activity \[10.1021/ja211396\]. This is particularly evident in the context of propane dehydrogenation, where the performance of metal particles is closely tied to their interactions with the oxide support. Flat metal deposits, such as bilayer Au rafts on titania, Au/FeOx catalysts, and Pt on alumina, have demonstrated superior reactivity in reactions including CO oxidation and propanol oxidation \[10.1021/ja211396\]. The particle perimeter facilitates simultaneous adsorbate interaction with both the metal and the oxide, elucidating the shape-dependence of catalytic activity. Doping CaO films with Mo or Li has been shown to alter Au particle geometry through charge-transfer processes \[10.1021/ja211396\]. Mo impurities in CaO adopt a +2 charge state, transferring an electron to Au, stabilizing 2D metal islands. Conversely, Li, acting as a charge acceptor, creates holes in O orbitals, reducing electron availability for Au, thereby forcing 3D growth \[10.1021/ja211396\]. This study underscores the potential of doping to control metal ad-particle shape, with implications for catalytic activity enhancement.

                Substitutional cation doping also transforms metal oxides into Lewis acids or bases, enhancing halogen dissociation \[10.1021/jp211693v\]. La-doped CeO2 acts as a Lewis acid, strengthening halogen binding to oxygen sites, while Ta-doped ceria, as a Lewis base, intensifies halogen binding to cerium sites \[10.1021/jp211693v\]. These modifications in surface chemistry can significantly boost catalytic activity and alter active centers, potentially improving industrial reactions \[10.1021/jp211693v\].

                The creation of an electron deficit by lower-valence cations replacing the host oxide's cation turns surfaces into Lewis acids, enhancing the oxide's electron-accepting ability \[10.1021/jp301341\]. This phenomenon is observed in both reducible and irreducible oxides, with bases binding more strongly to doped surfaces \[10.1021/jp301341\]. In reducible oxides, cations can be reduced in the presence of a base, introducing complexity in charge exchange \[10.1021/jp301341\]. In irreducible oxides, bases always fill the hole created by the low-valence dopant \[10.1021/jp301341\]. These modifications in surface chemistry can enhance catalytic activity and alter active centers, potentially improving industrial reactions \[10.1021/jp301341\].

                Active centers may form near dopants or involve the dopants themselves, potentially improving industrially relevant reactions \[10.1021/cr300418s_0\]. This approach aims to favorably alter the oxide's catalytic properties, as evidenced by experiments and calculations \[10.1021/cr300418s_0\].

                通过在金属氧化物催化剂中引入替代性阳离子，显著地改变了表面化学并破坏了化学键，从而提高了催化活性\[10.1021/ja211396\]。特别是在丙烷脱氢的背景下，金属颗粒的性能与其与氧化物载体的相互作用密切相关。像双层Au在二氧化钛上的筏状结构、Au/FeOx催化剂和Pt在氧化铝上的扁平金属沉积物，在包括CO氧化和丙醇氧化的反应中表现出超凡的反应性\[10.1021/ja211396\]。粒子周界促进了吸附物与金属和氧化物的同时相互作用，解释了催化活性的形状依赖性。通过电荷转移过程，Mo或Li对CaO薄膜的掺杂已被证明可以改变Au粒子的几何形状\[10.1021/ja211396\]。CaO中的Mo杂质采用+2电荷状态，向Au转移一个电子，稳定了二维金属岛。相反，作为电荷接受者的Li，在O轨道中产生空穴，减少了Au的电子可用性，从而迫使三维生长\[10.1021/ja211396\]。这项研究强调了掺杂控制金属吸附粒子形状的潜力，对催化活性增强有影响。

                替代性阳离子掺杂也将金属氧化物转化为Lewis酸或碱，增强了卤素的解离\[10.1021/jp211693v\]。La掺杂的CeO2作为Lewis酸，加强了卤素与氧位点的结合，而Ta掺杂的铈作为Lewis碱，增强了卤素与铈位点的结合\[10.1021/jp211693v\]。这些表面化学的改变可以显著提高催化活性并改变活性中心，可能改善工业反应\[10.1021/jp211693v\]。

                通过较低价态的阳离子取代宿主氧化物的阳离子，产生电子缺陷，使表面成为Lewis酸，增强了氧化物的电子接受能力\[10.1021/jp301341\]。这一现象在可还原和不可还原的氧化物中都有观察到，碱在掺杂表面上的结合更强烈\[10.1021/jp301341\]。在可还原的氧化物中，阳离子可以在碱的存在下被还原，引入了电荷交换的复杂性\[10.1021/jp301341\]。在不可还原的氧化物中，碱总是填充由低价态掺杂剂产生的空穴\[10.1021/jp301341\]。这些表面化学的改变可以提高催化活性并改变活性中心，可能改善工业反应\[10.1021/jp301341\]。

                活性中心可能在掺杂剂附近形成，或涉及掺杂剂本身，可能改善工业相关的反应\[10.1021/cr300418s_0\]。这种方法旨在有利地改变氧化物的催化性能，如实验和计算所证明\[10.1021/cr300418s_0\]。
