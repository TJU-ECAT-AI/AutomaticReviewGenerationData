                The strategic incorporation of substitutional cations into metal oxide catalysts significantly modifies surface chemistry and disrupts chemical bonding, leading to enhanced catalytic performance \[10.1021/ja211396\]. This is achieved by controlling the equilibrium shape of metal ad-particles, as demonstrated by the changes in Au particle geometry on doped CaO films \[10.1021/ja211396\]. Mo dopants in CaO adopt a +2 charge state, transferring an electron to Au, resulting in the stabilization of 2D metal islands. Conversely, Li dopants create holes in adjacent O ions, reducing electron transfer to Au and forcing a 3D growth regime \[10.1021/ja211396\]. These modifications in surface chemistry and chemical bonding can lead to the formation of active centers near the dopant or involving the dopant itself, potentially improving industrially relevant reactions \[10.1021/cr300418s_0\].

                Substitutional cation doping also transforms metal oxides into Lewis acids or bases, enhancing catalytic activity. La-doped CeO2 acts as a Lewis acid, strengthening halogen binding to oxygen sites, while Ta-doped ceria becomes a Lewis base, intensifying halogen binding to cerium sites \[10.1021/jp211693v\]. These changes in surface chemistry improve catalytic activity and redefine active centers, potentially boosting industrially relevant reactions \[10.1021/jp211693v\].

                By replacing a host oxide's cation with a lower-valence dopant, an electron deficit is created, turning the surface into a Lewis acid. This substitution increases the oxide's electron acceptance ability, making it a stronger acid \[10.1021/jp301341\]. Consequently, bases bind more strongly to doped surfaces than to undoped ones, affecting catalytic performance and active center characteristics \[10.1021/jp301341\].

                These findings underscore the potential of substitutional cation doping to enhance the catalytic performance of metal oxide catalysts through the modification of surface chemistry and the disruption of chemical bonding, leading to the formation of active centers near the dopant or involving the dopant itself \[10.1021/cr300418s_0\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The insights gained from the study of substitutional cation doping in metal oxide catalysts reveal a promising avenue for the enhancement of catalytic performance in industrially relevant reactions. By tailoring the surface chemistry and controlling the chemical bonding through strategic doping, it becomes possible to engineer active centers that are more efficient and selective. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel doping strategies that can further refine the control over metal ad-particle shapes and the nature of active centers. Additionally, the exploration of codoping effects and the internal charge compensation mechanisms could lead to the discovery of new catalysts with enhanced stability and activity. The integration of computational modeling with experimental studies could also provide deeper insights into the complex interplay between surface chemistry modifications and catalytic activity, guiding the design of next-generation catalysts for propane dehydrogenation and other industrially significant reactions.

                通过在金属氧化物催化剂中战略性地掺入替代性阳离子，显著地改变了表面化学并破坏了化学键，从而提高了催化性能\[10.1021/ja211396\]。通过控制金属吸附粒子的平衡形状，如在掺杂CaO薄膜上Au粒子几何形状的变化，实现了这一点\[10.1021/ja211396\]。CaO中的Mo掺杂物采用+2电荷状态，向Au转移一个电子，导致2D金属岛的稳定。相反，Li掺杂物在相邻O离子中产生空穴，减少向Au的电子转移，迫使3D生长机制\[10.1021/ja211396\]。这些表面化学和化学键的修改可以导致在掺杂物附近或涉及掺杂物本身的活性中心的形成，可能改善工业相关的反应\[10.1021/cr300418s_0\]。

                替代性阳离子掺杂还将金属氧化物转化为Lewis酸或碱，增强催化活性。La掺杂的CeO2作为Lewis酸，加强了卤素与氧位点的结合，而Ta掺杂的铈则成为Lewis碱，增强了卤素与铈位点的结合\[10.1021/jp211693v\]。这些表面化学的变化提高了催化活性，重新定义了活性中心，可能增强工业相关的反应\[10.1021/jp211693v\]。

                通过用低价掺杂物取代宿主氧化物的阳离子，产生了电子缺陷，使表面变成了Lewis酸。这种替代增加了氧化物的电子接受能力，使其成为更强的酸\[10.1021/jp301341\]。因此，碱在掺杂表面上的结合比在未掺杂表面上更强烈，影响催化性能和活性中心特性\[10.1021/jp301341\]。

                这些发现强调了替代性阳离子掺杂通过修改表面化学和破坏化学键，导致在掺杂物附近或涉及掺杂物本身的活性中心的形成，从而增强金属氧化物催化剂的催化性能的潜力\[10.1021/cr300418s_0\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: 从金属氧化物催化剂中替代性阳离子掺杂的研究中获得的见解揭示了提高工业相关反应催化性能的有希望的途径。通过战略性的掺杂来调整表面化学和控制化学键，有可能设计出更有效和选择性的活性中心。未来的研究工作可能集中在开发新的掺杂策略，以进一步细化对金属吸附粒子形状和活性中心性质的控制。此外，探索共掺杂效应和内部电荷补偿机制可能导致发现具有增强稳定性和活性的新催化剂。将计算建模与实验研究相结合，也可能为表面化学修改与催化活性之间的复杂相互作用提供更深入的见解，指导下一代催化剂的设计，用于丙烷脱氢和其他工业上重要的反应。
