                The enhancement of catalytic performance in metal oxide catalysts through substitutional cation doping is a topic of significant interest, particularly in the context of propane dehydrogenation [10.1021/ja211396]. Doping modifies surface chemistry and disrupts chemical bonding, impacting catalytic activity by altering metal particle size, morphology, and interactions with oxide supports. Flat metal deposits, such as bilayer Au rafts on titania and Au/FeOx, exhibit enhanced activity in reactions like low-temperature CO oxidation and propanol oxidation over Pt on alumina [10.1021/ja211396]. The particle perimeter enables simultaneous interaction of adsorbates with the metal and oxide support, explaining the shape-dependence of metal particles in catalysis. Doping can control the equilibrium shape of metal ad-particles, as demonstrated by changes in Au particle geometry on doped CaO films. Mo impurities in CaO adopt a +2 charge state, transferring an electron to Au, resulting in 2D metal islands. Li doping, in contrast, creates holes in adjacent O ions, reducing electron availability for Au and forcing a 3D growth regime [10.1021/ja211396].

                Substitutional cation doping also transforms metal oxides into Lewis acids or bases, enhancing catalytic activity. La-doped CeO2 acts as a Lewis acid, strengthening halogen binding to oxygen sites, while Ta-doped ceria, a Lewis base, enhances halogen binding to cerium sites [10.1021/jp211693v]. These changes in surface chemistry can boost catalytic activity and alter active centers, potentially improving industrially relevant reactions.

                By replacing host cations with lower-valence dopants, an electron deficit is created, making the surface a stronger acid. This increases the oxide's electron-accepting ability, leading bases to bind more strongly to doped surfaces [10.1021/jp301341]. In reducible oxides, cations can be reduced in the presence of a base, creating competition for the electron donated by the base. In irreducible oxides, the base always fills the hole created by the dopant. These modifications can lead to enhanced catalytic activity and altered active centers, potentially improving industrial reactions.

                Active centers may form near dopants or at the dopants themselves, potentially improving industrially relevant reactions [10.1021/cr300418s_0].

                通过替代性阳离子掺杂来增强金属氧化物催化剂的催化性能是一个备受关注的话题，特别是在丙烷脱氢的背景下 [10.1021/ja211396]。掺杂改变了表面化学，破坏了化学键，通过改变金属颗粒的大小、形态以及与氧化物载体的相互作用来影响催化活性。例如，双层Au筏在二氧化钛上的沉积和Au/FeOx在低温度CO氧化和Pt在氧化铝上的丙醇氧化反应中表现出增强的活性 [10.1021/ja211396]。金属颗粒的周边使吸附物能够同时与金属和氧化物载体相互作用，解释了金属颗粒在催化作用中的形状依赖性。掺杂可以控制金属吸附颗粒的平衡形状，如在掺杂CaO薄膜上Au颗粒几何形状的变化所示。CaO中的Mo杂质采用+2价态，向Au转移一个电子，形成2D金属岛。相比之下，Li掺杂在相邻的O离子中产生空穴，减少了Au的电子可用性，迫使3D生长 [10.1021/ja211396]。

                替代性阳离子掺杂还将金属氧化物转化为路易斯酸或碱，增强催化活性。La掺杂的CeO2作为路易斯酸，加强了卤素与氧位点的结合，而Ta掺杂的铈作为路易斯碱，增强了卤素与铈位点的结合 [10.1021/jp211693v]。这些表面化学的变化可以增强催化活性，改变活性中心，可能改善工业相关的反应。

                通过用低价态掺杂剂取代宿主氧化物的阳离子，产生了电子缺陷，使表面成为更强的酸。这增加了氧化物接受电子的能力，导致碱在掺杂表面上的结合力更强 [10.1021/jp301341]。在可还原氧化物中，阳离子可以在碱的存在下被还原，为电子捐赠的碱创造了竞争。在不可还原氧化物中，碱总是填充由掺杂剂产生的空穴。这些表面化学和化学键的修改可以增强催化活性，改变活性中心，可能改善工业反应。

                活性中心可能在掺杂剂附近形成，或涉及掺杂剂本身，可能改善工业相关的反应 [10.1021/cr300418s_0]。
