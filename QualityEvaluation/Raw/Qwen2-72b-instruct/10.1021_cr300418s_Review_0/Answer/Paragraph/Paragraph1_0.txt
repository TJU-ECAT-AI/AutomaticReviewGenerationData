                    The strategic application of substitutional cation doping in metal oxide catalysts has been shown to significantly modify surface chemistry and disrupt chemical bonding, thereby enhancing catalytic performance \[10.1021/ja211396\]. This modification is particularly critical in the context of propane dehydrogenation, where the nature and location of active centers play a pivotal role in determining the efficiency of the catalytic process. Doping with specific cations can alter the metal particle size and morphology, leading to improved catalytic activity \[10.1021/ja211396\]. For instance, flat metal deposits such as bilayer Au rafts on titania, Au/FeOx, and Pt on alumina have demonstrated enhanced reactivity in relevant reactions \[10.1021/ja211396\]. The particle perimeter's interaction with the oxide support and metal is a key factor in catalytic performance, as it enables simultaneous interaction of adsorbates with both the metal and the oxide support \[10.1021/ja211396\]. Doping CaO films with Mo or Li can change Au particle geometry; Mo promotes 2D growth due to electron transfer to Au, stabilizing anionic gold with strong metal-oxide adhesion, whereas Li doping blocks this charge exchange, forcing Au to adopt 3D growth by reducing available electrons \[10.1021/ja211396\]. These findings underscore the potential of doping to tailor metal ad-particle shapes, with implications for catalytic activity enhancement and active center modification.

                    Substitutional cation doping can also transform metal oxide surfaces into Lewis acids or bases, influencing the dissociation of halogens and enhancing catalytic activity \[10.1021/jp211693v\]. La-doped CeO2 acts as a Lewis acid, enhancing halogen binding to oxygen sites, while Ta-doped ceria becomes a Lewis base, strengthening halogen binding to cerium sites \[10.1021/jp211693v\]. These changes in chemical bonding can boost catalytic activity and alter active centers, potentially improving industrial reactions \[10.1021/jp211693v\].

                    Furthermore, substitutional cation doping in metal oxides creates an electron deficit, turning surfaces into Lewis acids by enhancing the oxide's electron-accepting ability \[10.1021/jp301341\]. This modification strengthens the binding of Lewis bases to doped surfaces compared to undoped ones, observed in both reducible and irreducible oxides \[10.1021/jp301341\]. These changes in surface chemistry and chemical bonding can significantly enhance catalytic activity, alter active centers, and potentially improve industrial reactions \[10.1021/jp301341\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The insights gained from the study of substitutional cation doping in metal oxide catalysts offer a promising avenue for the enhancement of catalytic performance in propane dehydrogenation. The ability to tailor metal particle shapes and modify surface chemistry through doping presents a strategic approach to optimize active centers and catalytic activity. Future research efforts might focus on the development of novel doping strategies that can simultaneously address multiple aspects of catalytic performance, such as increasing the stability of active centers, improving selectivity, and enhancing the efficiency of heat management in catalytic processes. Additionally, the exploration of codoping effects and the internal charge compensation mechanisms could lead to the discovery of new catalyst designs that overcome the limitations of conventional doping methods. The integration of advanced computational methods with experimental techniques could further accelerate the discovery of optimal doping conditions for specific catalytic applications, including propane dehydrogenation.

                    通过在金属氧化物催化剂中进行替代性阳离子掺杂的战略应用，已经证明可以显著改变表面化学并破坏化学键，从而增强催化性能\[10.1021/ja211396\]。在丙烷脱氢的背景下，这一点尤为重要，其中活性中心的性质和位置在决定催化过程效率方面起着决定性作用。通过特定阳离子的掺杂可以改变金属颗粒的大小和形态，从而提高催化活性\[10.1021/ja211396\]。例如，像二氧化钛上的双层Au筏、Au/FeOx和Pt/氧化铝这样的扁平金属沉积物在相关反应中表现出增强的反应性\[10.1021/ja211396\]。金属与氧化物支撑物之间的粒子周界的相互作用是催化性能的关键因素，因为它使吸附物能够同时与金属和氧化物支撑物相互作用\[10.1021/ja211396\]。通过Mo或Li掺杂CaO薄膜可以改变Au颗粒的几何形状；Mo通过向Au转移电子促进二维生长，稳定带负电的金，与金属-氧化物粘附力强，而Li掺杂阻止这种电荷交换，通过减少可用电荷迫使Au采用三维生长\[10.1021/ja211396\]。这些发现强调了掺杂在调整金属吸附颗粒形状方面的潜力，对催化活性增强和活性中心修改有影响。

                    替代性阳离子掺杂还可以将金属氧化物表面转化为Lewis酸或碱，影响卤素的解离并增强催化活性\[10.1021/jp211693v\]。La掺杂的CeO2作为Lewis酸，增强卤素与氧位点的结合，而Ta掺杂的铈则成为Lewis碱，加强卤素与铈位点的结合\[10.1021/jp211693v\]。这些化学键的变化可以增强催化活性，改变活性中心，可能改善工业反应\[10.1021/jp211693v\]。

                    此外，金属氧化物中的替代性阳离子掺杂通过在表面产生电子缺陷，将表面转化为Lewis酸，增强氧化物的电子接受能力\[10.1021/jp301341\]。这种修改加强了与掺杂表面相比，Lewis碱与掺杂表面的结合，这在可还原和不可还原氧化物中都有观察到\[10.1021/jp301341\]。这些表面化学和化学键的变化可以显著增强催化活性，改变活性中心，可能改善工业反应\[10.1021/jp301341\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 从金属氧化物催化剂中替代性阳离子掺杂的研究中获得的见解为丙烷脱氢催化性能的增强提供了一条有希望的途径。通过掺杂调整金属颗粒形状和修改表面化学的能力为优化活性中心和催化活性提供了一种战略方法。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发新的掺杂策略，可以同时解决催化性能的多个方面，如增加活性中心的稳定性，提高选择性，以及提高催化过程中热管理的效率。此外，探索共掺杂效应和内部电荷补偿机制可能导致发现新的催化剂设计，克服传统掺杂方法的局限性。将先进的计算方法与实验技术相结合，可以进一步加速特定催化应用（包括丙烷脱氢）的最优掺杂条件的发现。
