                The strategic incorporation of substitutional cations into metal oxide catalysts significantly modifies surface chemistry and disrupts chemical bonding, leading to enhanced catalytic performance \[10.1021/ja211396\]. This process impacts catalytic activity by altering metal particle size, morphology, and interactions with oxide supports. Flat metal deposits, such as bilayer Au rafts on titania, Au/FeOx catalysts, and Pt on alumina for propanol oxidation, exhibit higher activity due to the role of the particle perimeter in enabling adsorbate interaction with both the metal and the oxide support \[10.1021/ja211396\]. Doping with Mo or Li can control the equilibrium shape of metal ad-particles on oxide supports, with Mo leading to 2D metal island growth due to electron transfer from Mo to Au, stabilizing the islands \[10.1021/ja211396\]. Li doping, in contrast, blocks this charge exchange by creating holes in O 2p orbitals, reducing the number of electrons available for Au, forcing Au to adopt its original 3D growth \[10.1021/ja211396\]. These findings highlight how shaping metal ad-particles via doping can optimize catalytic performance, with implications for active centers and reaction enhancements \[10.1021/ja211396\].
                Substitutional cation doping in metal oxides, like La-doped CeO2 and Ta-doped ceria, modifies surface chemistry to act as Lewis acids or bases, enhancing halogen dissociation and catalytic activity \[10.1021/jp211693v\]. La-doped CeO2 strengthens halogen binding to oxygen sites, while Ta-doped ceria enhances halogen-cerium site bonding, both improving industrially relevant reactions by altering active centers \[10.1021/jp211693v\].
                Substitutional cation doping in metal oxides transforms surfaces into Lewis acids by creating an electron deficit when a lower-valence cation replaces the host oxide's cation \[10.1021/jp301341\]. This substitution increases the oxide's electron acceptance ability, making it a stronger acid \[10.1021/jp301341\]. Consequently, bases bind more strongly to doped surfaces than to undoped ones \[10.1021/jp301341\]. In reducible oxides, cations can be reduced in the presence of a base, introducing complexity to the charge exchange mechanism \[10.1021/jp301341\]. In contrast, bases in irreducible oxides always fill the hole created by the low-valence dopant \[10.1021/jp301341\]. These surface chemistry modifications can enhance catalytic activity and alter active centers, potentially improving industrial reactions \[10.1021/jp301341\].
                Substitutional cation doping in metal oxide catalysts disrupts surface chemical bonding, aiming to enhance catalytic activity \[10.1021/cr300418s_0\]. The active centers could be oxygen atoms near the dopant or the dopant itself, potentially improving industrially relevant reactions \[10.1021/cr300418s_0\].

                通过在金属氧化物催化剂中战略性地掺入替代性阳离子，显著地改变了表面化学并破坏了化学键，从而提高了催化性能\[10.1021/ja211396\]。这一过程通过改变金属颗粒的大小、形态以及与氧化物载体的相互作用，影响了催化活性。扁平金属沉积物，如在二氧化钛上的双层Au筏、Au/FeOx催化剂以及在氧化铝上的Pt用于丙醇氧化，由于粒子周界在使吸附物与金属和氧化物载体相互作用中所起的作用，表现出更高的活性\[10.1021/ja211396\]。通过Mo或Li的掺杂可以控制金属吸附粒子在氧化物载体上的平衡形状，Mo导致2D金属岛的生长，这是由于从Mo到Au的电子转移，稳定了岛屿\[10.1021/ja211396\]。相比之下，Li掺杂通过在O 2p轨道中产生空穴，阻止了这种电荷交换，减少了Au可用的电子数量，迫使Au采用其原始的3D生长\[10.1021/ja211396\]。这些发现突出了通过掺杂塑形金属吸附粒子以优化催化性能的潜力，对活性中心和反应增强具有影响\[10.1021/ja211396\]。
                在金属氧化物中，如La掺杂的CeO2和Ta掺杂的铈，通过替代性阳离子掺杂，将表面化学转化为路易斯酸或碱，增强了卤素的解离和催化活性\[10.1021/jp211693v\]。La掺杂的CeO2加强了卤素与氧位点的结合，而Ta掺杂的铈增强了卤素-铈位点的结合，通过改变活性中心，都改善了工业相关的反应\[10.1021/jp211693v\]。
                通过替代性阳离子掺杂，金属氧化物转变为路易斯酸，当较低价的阳离子取代氧化物的阳离子时，产生电子缺陷\[10.1021/jp301341\]。这种替代增加了氧化物的电子接受能力，使其成为更强的酸\[10.1021/jp301341\]。因此，碱在掺杂表面上的结合比在未掺杂表面上更强\[10.1021/jp301341\]。在可还原氧化物中，阳离子可以在碱的存在下被还原，为电荷交换机制引入复杂性\[10.1021/jp301341\]。相比之下，不可还原氧化物中的碱总是填充由低价掺杂剂产生的空穴\[10.1021/jp301341\]。这些表面化学的修改可以增强催化活性并改变活性中心，可能改善工业反应\[10.1021/jp301341\]。
                通过替代性阳离子掺杂，金属氧化物催化剂破坏了表面化学键，旨在提高催化活性\[10.1021/cr300418s_0\]。活性中心可能是靠近掺杂剂的氧原子或掺杂剂本身，可能改善工业相关的反应\[10.1021/cr300418s_0\]。
