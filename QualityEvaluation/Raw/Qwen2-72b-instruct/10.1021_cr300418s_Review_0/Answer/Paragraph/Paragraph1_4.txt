    The enhancement of catalytic performance in metal oxide catalysts through substitutional cation doping is a multifaceted process that modifies surface chemistry and disrupts chemical bonding, leading to significant implications for active centers and catalytic activity \[10.1021/ja211396\]. Metal particle size and morphology, influenced by interactions with the oxide support, are pivotal for catalysis. Flat metal deposits, such as bilayer Au on titania, Au/FeOx, and Pt on alumina, exhibit enhanced activity in reactions like CO oxidation and propanol oxidation \[10.1021/ja211396\]. The particle perimeter enables simultaneous adsorbate interaction with metal and oxide, elucidating the shape-dependence in catalysis \[10.1021/ja211396\]. Doping can tailor metal deposit shapes, overcoming limitations on conventional bulk oxides \[10.1021/ja211396\]. For instance, Mo doping in CaO films leads to 2D Au island growth due to electron transfer from Mo to Au, stabilizing the anionic gold with strong metal-oxide adhesion \[10.1021/ja211396\]. Conversely, Li doping, creating charge acceptors, blocks this electron transfer, forcing Au to adopt its original 3D growth \[10.1021/ja211396\]. This study reveals how oxide support doping can control metal ad-particle shapes, with implications for catalytic activity and active center locations \[10.1021/ja211396\].
    
    Substitutional cation doping modifies surface chemistry, enhancing catalytic activity by turning metal oxides into Lewis acids or bases \[10.1021/jp211693v\]. La-doped CeO2 acts as a Lewis acid, strengthening halogen binding to the oxygen site \[10.1021/jp211693v\]. Conversely, Ta-doped ceria becomes a Lewis base, intensifying halogen binding to the cerium site \[10.1021/jp211693v\]. These changes improve catalytic performance and alter active centers, potentially boosting industrially relevant reactions \[10.1021/jp211693v\].
    
    The transformation of metal oxide surfaces into Lewis acids by substitutional cation doping, creating an electron deficit when a lower-valence cation replaces the host oxide's cation, enhances the oxide's electron acceptance ability \[10.1021/jp301341\]. Consequently, bases bind more strongly to doped oxide surfaces than to undoped ones \[10.1021/jp301341\]. These modifications in surface chemistry can significantly enhance catalytic activity and alter the nature and location of active centers, potentially improving industrially relevant reactions \[10.1021/jp301341\].
    
    Substitutional cation doping in metal oxide catalysts modifies surface chemistry by disrupting chemical bonding, aiming to enhance catalytic activity \[10.1021/cr300418s_0\]. Active centers could be oxygen atoms near the dopant or the dopant itself, potentially improving industrially relevant reactions \[10.1021/cr300418s_0\].

    通过替代性阳离子掺杂增强金属氧化物催化剂的催化性能是一个多方面的过程，它通过改变表面化学和破坏化学键合，对活性中心和催化活性产生重大影响\[10.1021/ja211396\]。金属颗粒的大小和形态，受氧化物载体相互作用的影响，对于催化作用至关重要。平坦的金属沉积物，如双层Au在二氧化钛上，Au/FeOx，以及Pt在氧化铝上，在CO氧化和丙醇氧化等反应中表现出增强的活性\[10.1021/ja211396\]。颗粒的周界使吸附物能够同时与金属和氧化物相互作用，解释了催化作用的形状依赖性\[10.1021/ja211396\]。掺杂可以定制金属沉积物的形状，克服了传统大块氧化物的限制\[10.1021/ja211396\]。例如，在CaO薄膜中Mo掺杂导致2D Au岛的生长，由于Mo向Au的电子转移，稳定了带负电的金，与金属-氧化物的强粘附\[10.1021/ja211396\]。相反，Li掺杂，作为电荷接受者，阻止了这种电子转移，迫使Au采用其原始的3D生长\[10.1021/ja211396\]。这项研究揭示了氧化物载体掺杂如何控制金属吸附粒子的形状，对催化活性和活性中心位置有影响\[10.1021/ja211396\]。
    
    替代性阳离子掺杂通过将金属氧化物转化为Lewis酸或碱来改变表面化学，从而增强催化活性\[10.1021/jp211693v\]。La掺杂的CeO2作为Lewis酸，加强了卤素与氧位点的结合\[10.1021/jp211693v\]。相反，Ta掺杂的铈成为Lewis碱，增强了卤素与铈位点的结合\[10.1021/jp211693v\]。这些变化提高了催化性能，改变了活性中心，可能促进了工业相关反应\[10.1021/jp211693v\]。
    
    通过替代性阳离子掺杂将金属氧化物表面转化为Lewis酸，当低价阳离子取代氧化物的阳离子时，产生电子缺陷，增强了氧化物的电子接受能力\[10.1021/jp301341\]。因此，碱在掺杂氧化物表面的结合比在未掺杂的表面更强\[10.1021/jp301341\]。这些表面化学的修改可以显著增强催化活性，改变活性中心的性质和位置，可能改善工业相关反应\[10.1021/jp301341\]。
    
    金属氧化物催化剂中替代性阳离子掺杂通过破坏化学键合来改变表面化学，旨在增强催化活性\[10.1021/cr300418s_0\]。活性中心可能是靠近掺杂剂的氧原子或掺杂剂本身，可能改善工业相关反应\[10.1021/cr300418s_0\]。
