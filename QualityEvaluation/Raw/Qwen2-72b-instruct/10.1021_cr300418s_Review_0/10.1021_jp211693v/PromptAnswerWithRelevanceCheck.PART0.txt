<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1.Substitutional cation doping in metal oxide catalysts can modify the surface chemistry by turning the surface into a Lewis acid or base, which strongly influences the dissociation of halogens. For instance, La-doped CeO2 becomes a Lewis acid, causing halogen atoms to bind more strongly to the oxygen site as a base. Conversely, Ta-doped ceria becomes a Lewis base, leading halogen atoms to bind more strongly to the cerium site as an acid. These changes in bonding can enhance catalytic activity, alter the nature and location of active centers, and potentially improve industrially relevant reactions.

</EnglishVersionAnswer>
