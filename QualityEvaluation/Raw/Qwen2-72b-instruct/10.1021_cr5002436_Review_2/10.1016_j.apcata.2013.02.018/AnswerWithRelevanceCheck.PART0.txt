      <English>
          The literature does not provide a historical overview of chromium oxide-based catalysts since their discovery in 1933. However, it does focus on the synthesis and performance of mesoporous Cr2O3/Al2O3 catalysts derived from a metal-organic framework (MIL-101) for isobutane dehydrogenation. The key aspects highlighted include the synthesis/preparation method, the effect/influence of chromium loading, the role/addition of potassium as a promoter, and the calcination temperature on the catalytic activity and selectivity. The research emphasizes the importance of surface chromia species, particularly Cr3+, as the active site for isobutene production and the dehydrogenation reaction, and the role of potassium in enhancing isobutene selectivity, stability, and catalyst regenerative ability. The catalysts are evaluated for their ability to produce isobutene, a vital component for the synthesis of MTBE and ETBE, which are used as octane number boosters in unleaded gasoline. The research demonstrates the significance of these catalysts in dehydrogenation processes by achieving high isobutene selectivity and stability over multiple dehydrogenation-regeneration cycles.
