1.The literature does not provide a historical overview of chromium oxide-based catalysts since their discovery in 1933. However, it does focus on the synthesis and performance of mesoporous Cr2O3/Al2O3 catalysts derived from a metal-organic framework (MIL-101) for isobutane dehydrogenation. The key aspects highlighted include the synthesis method, the effect of chromium loading, the role of potassium as a promoter, and the stability and regenerative ability of the catalyst. The research emphasizes the importance of surface chromia species, particularly Cr3+, in catalytic activity and selectivity. The catalysts are evaluated for their ability to produce isobutene, a vital component for the synthesis of MTBE and ETBE, which are used as octane number boosters in unleaded gasoline.
