1.The literature does not provide a historical overview of chromium oxide-based catalysts since their discovery in 1933. However, it does focus on the synthesis and performance of mesoporous Cr2O3/Al2O3 catalysts derived from a metal-organic framework (MIL-101) for isobutane dehydrogenation. The key aspects highlighted include the preparation method, the influence of chromium loading, the addition of potassium as a promoter, and the calcination temperature on the catalytic activity and selectivity. The research demonstrates the significance of these catalysts in dehydrogenation processes by achieving high isobutene selectivity and stability over multiple dehydrogenation-regeneration cycles.
