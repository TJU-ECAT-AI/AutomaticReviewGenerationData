1.The literature does not provide a historical overview of chromium oxide-based catalysts since their discovery in 1933. However, it does focus on the synthesis and performance of mesoporous Cr2O3/Al2O3 catalysts derived from a metal-organic framework (MIL-101) for isobutane dehydrogenation. The key aspects highlighted include the preparation method, the effect of chromium loading, the addition of potassium as a promoter, and the calcination temperature on the catalytic activity and selectivity. The research emphasizes the importance of surface chromia species, particularly Cr3+, as the active site for the dehydrogenation reaction, and the role of potassium in enhancing isobutene selectivity and catalyst stability.
