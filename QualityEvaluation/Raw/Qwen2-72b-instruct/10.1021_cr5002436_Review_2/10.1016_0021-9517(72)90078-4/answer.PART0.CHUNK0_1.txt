1.The literature does not provide a historical overview of chromium oxide-based catalysts since their discovery in 1933, nor does it detail the evolution of these catalysts in industrial applications. The focus of the literature is on the study of the activity of true Cr2O3-Al2O3 solid solutions in dehydrogenation processes, specifically the dehydrogenation of isobutane to isobutene. The research highlights the intrinsic activities and stability of different solid solutions, the role of chromium ions in catalytic activity and deactivation, and the potential benefits of substituting phases with a low content of chromia for richer phases in usual dehydrogenation catalysts. The literature does not cover the broader industrial applications and research focus areas related to chromium oxide-based catalysts, such as structure, active sites, support materials, promoters, and reaction mechanisms in dehydrogenation processes.
