      <English>
          The literature does not provide a historical overview of chromium oxide-based catalysts since their discovery in 1933. However, it does focus on the preparation and characterization of CrOx/Al2O3 catalysts for the dehydrogenation of i-butane. The key research areas include the use of Atomic Layer Epitaxy (ALE) and impregnation techniques for catalyst preparation, the analysis of chromium oxidation states (Cr6+ and Cr3+), the dispersion of CrOx on the Al2O3 support, and the activity of the catalysts in dehydrogenation reactions. Studies also examine the decay of dehydrogenation activity in successive prereduction-reaction-regeneration cycles, attributing it to a decrease in the number of redox Cr3+ sites, and investigate the role of redox Cr3+ and exposed non-redox Cr3+ sites in the dehydrogenation process, as well as the impact of CrOx ensemble size on catalyst activity.
