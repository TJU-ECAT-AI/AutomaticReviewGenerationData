            <English> 
                    Chromium oxide-based catalysts, pivotal since their discovery in 1933, have significantly evolved in dehydrogenation processes, particularly for propane to propene conversion, with CO2 as a key oxidant \[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\]. Research emphasizes the importance of high dispersion for enhanced activity, facilitated by mesoporous SBA-1 supports providing large surface areas. Promoters and redox mechanisms, influenced by chromium loading and preparation conditions, are vital. CO2 acts as an oxidant in oxidative dehydrogenation, and the concentration of redox Cr species is critical for activity. The evolution of these catalysts has been marked by a focus on structure, active sites, support materials, and reaction mechanisms \[10.1007/s10562-008-9599-x\]. Preparation methods, including impregnation, coprecipitation, and sol-gel techniques, aim to achieve high dispersion of chromia species on supports. Sol-gel preparation is versatile, leading to materials with high surface area and highly dispersed metal ions, while mesoporous molecular sieves combined with chromia-doped gel inside their pores create catalysts with unique properties. The industrial significance of chromium oxide-based catalysts is underscored by their role in processes like catalytic oxidation, ethylene polymerization, chlorinated carbon removal, and propane dehydrogenation \[10.1007/s10562-008-9599-x\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of chromium oxide-based catalysts since 1933 has been a testament to their adaptability and significance in dehydrogenation processes. The multifaceted research focusing on structure, active sites, support materials, promoters, and reaction mechanisms has led to significant advancements in catalyst performance. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel preparation methods that enhance the dispersion of chromium species and optimize the redox properties of the catalysts. The exploration of alternative oxidants, such as CO2, presents an opportunity to improve the efficiency and selectivity of dehydrogenation processes. Additionally, the integration of in situ spectroscopy and online activity/selectivity measurements could provide deeper insights into the catalytic action under real conditions, paving the way for the design of more efficient and stable catalysts. The potential of carbon nanomaterials as active catalysts in oxidative dehydrogenation is also an emerging field that warrants further investigation. The goal is to understand the role of carbon deposits in alkane dehydrogenation reactions and to explore their potential as active catalyst materials. The future of chromium oxide-based catalysts in dehydrogenation processes is promising, with ongoing research aimed at improving their efficiency, selectivity, and stability.

                    自1933年发现以来，基于铬氧化物的催化剂在脱氢过程中，特别是在丙烷转化为丙烯的过程中，具有关键作用，二氧化碳作为关键氧化剂\[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\]。研究强调了高分散性对于增强活性的重要性，通过SBA-1介孔支持物提供大表面积得以实现。促进剂和氧化还原机制，受铬负载和制备条件的影响，至关重要。二氧化碳在氧化脱氢中充当氧化剂，而氧化还原Cr物种的浓度对于活性至关重要。这些催化剂的演变以结构、活性位点、支持材料和反应机制为重点\[10.1007/s10562-008-9599-x\]。制备方法，包括浸渍、共沉淀和溶胶-凝胶技术，旨在在支持物上实现高分散的铬物种。溶胶-凝胶制备具有通用性，可产生具有高表面积和高度分散金属离子的材料，而与介孔分子筛结合的铬掺杂凝胶在其孔内形成具有独特性质的催化剂。基于铬氧化物的催化剂在催化氧化、乙烯聚合、氯化碳去除和丙烷脱氢等过程中的工业重要性不容忽视\[10.1007/s10562-008-9599-x\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 自1933年以来，基于铬氧化物的催化剂的演变证明了它们在脱氢过程中的适应性和重要性。专注于结构、活性位点、支持材料、促进剂和反应机制的多方面研究，已经导致催化剂性能的显著进步。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发增强铬物种分散性和优化催化剂氧化还原性质的新制备方法上。探索替代氧化剂，如二氧化碳，为提高脱氢过程的效率和选择性提供了机会。此外，将原位光谱学和在线活性/选择性测量的整合可以为在实际条件下催化作用提供更深入的见解，为设计更高效和稳定的催化剂铺平道路。碳纳米材料作为氧化脱氢中活性催化剂的潜力也是一个新兴领域，值得进一步研究。目标是理解碳沉积物在烷烃脱氢反应中的作用，并探索它们作为活性催化剂材料的潜力。基于铬氧化物的催化剂在脱氢过程中的未来充满希望，正在进行的研究旨在提高其效率、选择性和稳定性。
