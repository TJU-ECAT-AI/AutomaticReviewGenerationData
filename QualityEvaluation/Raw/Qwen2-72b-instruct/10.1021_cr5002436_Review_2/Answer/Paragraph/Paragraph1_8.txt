                    The evolution of chromium oxide-based catalysts since their discovery in 1933 has been pivotal in industrial dehydrogenation processes, particularly for propane to propene conversion \[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\]. Research has emphasized the importance of high dispersion of chromium oxide species for enhanced activity, facilitated by mesoporous supports like SBA-1 \[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\]. The presence of CO2 acts as an oxidant in oxidative dehydrogenation, influenced by chromium loading, preparation conditions, precursor compound, and support nature \[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\]. Redox Cr species (Cr6+, Cr5+, Cr3+) are crucial for catalytic performance, highlighting the significance of these catalysts in dehydrogenation processes \[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\].

                    Chromium oxide-based catalysts have evolved significantly since 1933, playing a crucial role in the chemical industry for the production of commodity chemicals \[10.1021/jp960543\]. In situ Raman spectroscopy has advanced our understanding of surface chromium oxide species on various supports, crucial for methanol oxidation and butane dehydrogenation/oxidation \[10.1021/jp960543\]. The molecular structure of supported chromium oxides, influenced by pretreatment, Cr loading, and support type, is essential for industrial applications like the production of HDPE and LLDPE with Cr/SiO2 (Phillips catalyst) and butane dehydrogenation to butene with Cr/Al2O3 \[10.1021/jp960543\]. Raman spectroscopy is a powerful tool for studying supported metal oxide catalysts, revealing different molecular states and redox behavior, which is strongly support-dependent and critical for catalytic action in redox reactions \[10.1021/jp960543\].

                    Since 1933, chromium oxide-based catalysts have evolved significantly in dehydrogenation processes, with a focus on understanding active sites, support materials, and reaction mechanisms \[10.1016/0021-9517(72)90078-4\]. Studies on isobutane dehydrogenation over chromia-alumina solid solutions have revealed the importance of chromium ions in various oxidation states as active sites and the impact of solid solution structure on catalytic performance \[10.1016/0021-9517(72)90078-4\]. Deactivation is attributed to carbonaceous material formation on the catalyst surface \[10.1016/0021-9517(72)90078-4\]. A key finding suggests that substituting phases with low chromia content for richer ones could enhance the activity and stability of dehydrogenation catalysts \[10.1016/0021-9517(72)90078-4\].

                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of chromium oxide-based catalysts since 1933 has been marked by significant advancements in understanding their structure, active sites, and reaction mechanisms. The role of high dispersion of chromium oxide species, facilitated by mesoporous supports, and the influence of CO2 as an oxidant in oxidative dehydrogenation have been pivotal in enhancing catalytic activity. The use of in situ Raman spectroscopy has deepened our understanding of surface chromium oxide species on various supports, revealing the molecular structure's dependence on pretreatment, Cr loading, and support type. The redox behavior of supported chromium oxides is support-ligand specific, impacting their catalytic action in redox reactions. Future research might focus on developing catalysts with tailored support properties to enhance dispersion and control the oxidation state of chromium species, aiming for higher activity and stability. The exploration of alternative oxidants, such as CO2, could lead to more sustainable and efficient dehydrogenation processes. Additionally, the use of advanced characterization techniques could provide deeper insights into the dynamic behavior of chromium oxide species during dehydrogenation, guiding the design of next-generation catalysts.

                    自1933年发现以来，基于铬氧化物的催化剂在工业脱氢过程中，特别是丙烷转化为丙烯的转化中，发挥了关键作用\[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\]。研究强调了高分散的铬氧化物物种对于增强活性的重要性，这得益于SBA-1等介孔支撑材料的使用\[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\]。CO2的存在作为氧化脱氢过程中的氧化剂，受铬负载量、制备条件、前体化合物和支撑材料性质的影响\[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\]。Cr6+、Cr5+、Cr3+等氧化还原Cr物种对于催化性能至关重要，突显了这些催化剂在脱氢过程中的重要性\[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\]。

                    自1933年以来，基于铬氧化物的催化剂在化学工业中生产商品化学品方面发生了显著演变\[10.1021/jp960543\]。原位拉曼光谱学在我们对各种支撑材料上表面铬氧化物物种的理解上取得了进展，这对于甲醇氧化和丁烷脱氢/氧化至关重要\[10.1021/jp960543\]。受预处理、Cr负载量和支撑类型影响的支撑铬氧化物的分子结构对于工业应用至关重要，例如使用Cr/SiO2（菲利普斯催化剂）生产HDPE和LLDPE，以及使用Cr/Al2O3进行丁烷脱氢至丁烯\[10.1021/jp960543\]。拉曼光谱学是研究支撑金属氧化物催化剂的强大工具，揭示了不同的分子状态和氧化还原行为，这在很大程度上依赖于特定的支撑配体，并对氧化还原反应中的催化作用至关重要\[10.1021/jp960543\]。

                    自1933年以来，基于铬氧化物的催化剂在脱氢过程中发生了显著演变，重点在于理解活性位点、支撑材料和反应机制\[10.1016/0021-9517(72)90078-4\]。关于异丁烷在铬-氧化铝固溶体上的脱氢的研究揭示了不同氧化态的铬离子作为活性位点的重要性，以及固溶体结构对催化性能的影响\[10.1016/0021-9517(72)90078-4\]。催化剂表面碳质材料的形成导致了失活\[10.1016/0021-9517(72)90078-4\]。一项关键发现表明，用低铬含量的相替代富铬相可以增强脱氢催化剂的活性和稳定性\[10.1016/0021-9517(72)90078-4\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望：自1933年以来，基于铬氧化物的催化剂的演变以对它们的结构、活性位点和反应机制的显著进展为标志。高分散的铬氧化物物种的作用，得益于介孔支撑材料的使用，以及CO2作为氧化脱氢过程中的氧化剂的影响，对于增强催化活性至关重要。使用原位拉曼光谱学加深了我们对各种支撑材料上表面铬氧化物物种的理解，揭示了分子结构对预处理、Cr负载量和支撑类型的依赖性。支撑铬氧化物的氧化还原行为是特定支撑配体的，影响它们在氧化还原反应中的催化作用。未来的研究可能集中在开发具有定制支撑性质的催化剂，以增强分散性和控制铬物种的氧化态，目标是提高活性和稳定性。探索替代氧化剂，如CO2，可能导致更可持续和高效的脱氢过程。此外，使用先进的表征技术可以提供对脱氢过程中铬氧化物物种的动态行为的更深入见解，指导下一代催化剂的设计。
