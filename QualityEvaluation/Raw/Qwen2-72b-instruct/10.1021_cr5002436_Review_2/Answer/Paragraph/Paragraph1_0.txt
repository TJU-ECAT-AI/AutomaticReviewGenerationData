                    The evolution of chromium oxide-based catalysts since their discovery in 1933 has been pivotal in industrial dehydrogenation processes, particularly for propane to propene conversion \[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\]. High dispersion of chromium oxide species on mesoporous SBA-1 supports has enhanced catalytic activity, with CO2 acting as an oxidant in oxidative dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\]. Research has emphasized the role of chromium ions in various oxidation states as active sites, influenced by chromium loading, preparation conditions, precursor compounds, and support nature \[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\]. The presence of CO2 promotes dehydrogenation by consuming hydrogen, shifting the equilibrium towards propene production \[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\]. 

                    Chromium oxide-based catalysts have been crucial in the chemical industry for producing commodity chemicals since 1933 \[10.1021/jp960543\]. Supported chromium oxide catalysts, studied using in situ Raman spectroscopy, have revealed the evolution of surface species and their reactivity in methanol oxidation and butane dehydrogenation/oxidation \[10.1021/jp960543\]. The molecular structure of these catalysts, influenced by pretreatment, Cr loading, and support type, is critical for industrial applications like the production of HDPE and LLDPE using the Phillips catalyst (Cr/SiO2) and the dehydrogenation of butane to butene using Cr/Al2O3 \[10.1021/jp960543\]. Raman spectroscopy is a powerful tool for studying the redox behavior of supported chromium oxides, which is strongly dependent on the specific support ligand, impacting the catalyst's performance in redox reactions \[10.1021/jp960543\].

                    Since 1933, research on chromium oxide-based catalysts for dehydrogenation processes has focused on active sites, dehydration temperature effects, chromium oxidation states, and support materials \[10.1016/0021-9517(85)90297-0\]. Coordinatively unsaturated Cr(II) ions, with low nuclearity at low loadings, are identified as active sites for ethane dehydrogenation, highlighting their significance in catalytic activity \[10.1016/0021-9517(85)90297-0\].

                    Since 1933, chromium oxide-based catalysts have evolved significantly in dehydrogenation processes, with a focus on CrOx/Al2O3 catalysts \[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5\]. Key research areas include ALE and impregnation techniques for preparation, analysis of Cr6+ and Cr3+ oxidation states, CrOx dispersion on Al2O3, and the role of redox Cr3+ sites in activity \[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5\]. Studies reveal that dehydrogenation activity decays due to a decrease in redox Cr3+ sites, highlighting the importance of these sites and CrOx ensemble size in catalyst performance \[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5\].

                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of chromium oxide-based catalysts since 1933 has underscored their significance in dehydrogenation processes, particularly for propane to propene conversion. The multifaceted research has focused on structure, active sites, support materials, promoters, and reaction mechanisms, with CO2 emerging as a promising oxidant. Future research endeavors might focus on developing catalysts with enhanced stability and activity, exploring the role of novel support materials, and optimizing preparation methods to achieve higher dispersion of chromium species. The potential of using CO2 as a mild oxidant in dehydrogenation reactions opens new avenues for sustainable catalytic processes, which could be a key area for future exploration. Additionally, the use of in situ spectroscopy and online activity/selectivity measurements under real catalytic conditions could provide deeper insights into the catalytic action of chromium oxide-based catalysts, guiding the design of more efficient catalysts for industrial applications.

                    自1933年发现以来，基于铬氧化物的催化剂在工业脱氢过程中，特别是丙烷转化为丙烯的转化中，发挥了关键作用\[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\]。在SBA-1介孔支持物上高分散的铬氧化物物种增强了催化活性，其中CO2作为氧化脱氢的氧化剂\[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\]。研究强调了不同氧化态的铬离子作为活性位点的作用，受铬负载量、制备条件、前体化合物和支撑性质的影响\[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\]。CO2的存在通过消耗氢气，推动平衡向丙烯生成的方向移动，促进了脱氢\[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\]。

                    自1933年以来，基于铬氧化物的催化剂在生产商品化学品中至关重要\[10.1021/jp960543\]。使用原位拉曼光谱研究的支撑铬氧化物催化剂揭示了表面物种的演变及其在甲醇氧化和丁烷脱氢/氧化中的反应性\[10.1021/jp960543\]。这些催化剂的分子结构，受预处理、Cr负载量和支撑类型的影响，对于工业应用至关重要，如使用菲利普斯催化剂(Cr/SiO2)生产HDPE和LLDPE，以及使用Cr/Al2O3将丁烷脱氢为丁烯\[10.1021/jp960543\]。拉曼光谱是研究支撑金属氧化物催化剂的强大工具，其红ox行为强烈依赖于特定的支撑配体，影响催化剂在红ox反应中的性能\[10.1021/jp960543\]。

                    自1933年以来，对于脱氢过程的基于铬氧化物的催化剂的研究集中在活性位点、脱水温度效应、铬氧化态和支撑材料\[10.1016/0021-9517(85)90297-0\]。低核度、低负载量的配位不饱和Cr(II)离子被识别为乙烷脱氢的活性位点，突出了它们在催化活性中的重要性\[10.1016/0021-9517(85)90297-0\]。

                    自1933年以来，基于铬氧化物的催化剂在脱氢过程中显著发展，重点是CrOx/Al2O3催化剂\[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5\]。关键研究领域包括用于制备的ALE和浸渍技术，Cr6+和Cr3+氧化态的分析，CrOx在Al2O3上的分散，以及红ox Cr3+位点在活性中的作用\[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5\]。研究表明，脱氢活性的衰减是由于红ox Cr3+位点的减少，突出了这些位点和CrOx集合体大小在催化剂性能中的重要性\[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5\]。

                    综合理解与未来展望：自1933年以来，基于铬氧化物的催化剂的演变强调了它们在脱氢过程中，特别是丙烷转化为丙烯转化中的重要性。多方面的研究集中在结构、活性位点、支撑材料、促进剂和反应机制上，CO2作为有前途的氧化剂出现。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发具有增强稳定性和活性的催化剂，探索新型支撑材料的作用，以及优化制备方法以实现铬物种的更高分散。使用CO2作为温和氧化剂在脱氢反应中的潜力开辟了可持续催化过程的新途径，这可能是未来探索的关键领域。此外，在真实催化条件下使用原位光谱学和在线活性/选择性测量，可以为基于铬氧化物的催化剂的催化作用提供更深入的见解，指导更高效催化剂的设计，用于工业应用。
