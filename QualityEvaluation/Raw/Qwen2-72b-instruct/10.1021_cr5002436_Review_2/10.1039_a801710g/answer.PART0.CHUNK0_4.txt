1.The literature does not provide a historical overview of chromium oxide-based catalysts since their discovery in 1933. However, it highlights their significance in industrial processes such as the production of high density polyethylene (HDPE) and the Houdry CATOFIN and CATADIENE processes for isobutane and propylene production. The focus of research on these catalysts includes understanding their molecular structure and its relation to catalytic performance, particularly in dehydrogenation reactions. The study investigates the role of different chromium oxidation states (Cr6, Cr3, and Cr2) in n-butane dehydrogenation catalyzed by supported chromium oxide catalysts.
