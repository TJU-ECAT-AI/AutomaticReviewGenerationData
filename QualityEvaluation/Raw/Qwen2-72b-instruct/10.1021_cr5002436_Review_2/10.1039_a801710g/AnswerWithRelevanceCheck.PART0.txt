      <English>
          Chromium oxide-based catalysts have evolved significantly since their discovery in 1933, playing a crucial role in industrial processes such as the production of high density polyethylene (HDPE) and the Houdry CATOFIN and CATADIENE processes for the production of isobutane and propylene. Research focuses on understanding the molecular structure of chromium oxides, the nature of active sites, the role of support materials, the effects of promoters, and the mechanisms of dehydrogenation reactions, particularly in the context of n-butane dehydrogenation catalyzed by supported chromium oxide catalysts. Recent studies have aimed to understand the catalytic action under conditions as close as possible to real catalytic conditions through in situ spectroscopic studies combined with online activity/selectivity measurements.
