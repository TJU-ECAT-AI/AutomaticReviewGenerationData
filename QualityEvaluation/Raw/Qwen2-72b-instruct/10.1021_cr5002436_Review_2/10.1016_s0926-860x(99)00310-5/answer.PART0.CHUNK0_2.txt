1.The literature does not provide a historical overview of chromium oxide-based catalysts since their discovery in 1933. However, it does focus on the preparation and characterization of CrOx/Al2O3 catalysts for the dehydrogenation of i-butane. The key research areas include the use of atomic layer epitaxy (ALE) and impregnation techniques for catalyst preparation, the analysis of chromium oxidation states (Cr6+ and Cr3+), and the investigation of the role of redox Cr3+ and exposed non-redox Cr3+ sites in the dehydrogenation reaction. The study also examines the stability and activity of the catalysts in successive prereduction-reaction-regeneration cycles.
