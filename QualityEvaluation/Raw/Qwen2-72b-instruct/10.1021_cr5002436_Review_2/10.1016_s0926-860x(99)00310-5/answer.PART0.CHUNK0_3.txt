1.The literature does not provide a historical overview of chromium oxide-based catalysts since their discovery in 1933. However, it does focus on the preparation and characterization of CrOx/Al2O3 catalysts for the dehydrogenation of i-butane. The key research areas include the use of atomic layer epitaxy (ALE) and impregnation techniques for catalyst preparation, the analysis of chromium oxidation states (Cr6+ and Cr3+), and the evaluation of catalyst activity and stability in dehydrogenation reactions. The study also investigates the role of redox Cr3+ and exposed non-redox Cr3+ sites in the dehydrogenation process, and the impact of CrOx ensemble size on catalyst activity.
