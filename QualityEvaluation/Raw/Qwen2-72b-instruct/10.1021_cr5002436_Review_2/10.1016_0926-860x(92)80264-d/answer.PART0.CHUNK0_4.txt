1.The literature does not provide a historical overview of chromium oxide-based catalysts since their discovery in 1933. It focuses on the study of propane dehydrogenation on chromia/zirconia catalysts, investigating parameters influencing activity, deactivation, regeneration, and the nature of active sites. The key research areas include the influence of pretreatment, conversion and selectivity, deactivation and reversibility, surface hydration, dependence of activity on chromium content, apparent activation energy, influence of potassium addition, and the effect of reactant composition on reaction rates and deactivation coefficients. The study highlights the role of mononuclear Cr species and adjacent O2- ions in the active site for dehydrogenation.
