1.The literature does not provide a historical overview of chromium oxide-based catalysts since their discovery in 1933. However, it highlights the significance of chromia-based catalysts in various industrial processes such as catalytic oxidation of organic compounds, polymerization of ethylene into polyethylene, chlorinated carbon removal, and non-oxidative dehydrogenation of propane to produce propylene. The focus areas of research include the structure of supported chromium oxides, the nature of active sites, and the mechanisms of catalytic reactions. The oxidation state of active chromium species in non-oxidative dehydrogenation processes is a topic of ongoing debate, with some studies favoring Cr2+ species and others advocating the importance of Cr3+ sites or both.
