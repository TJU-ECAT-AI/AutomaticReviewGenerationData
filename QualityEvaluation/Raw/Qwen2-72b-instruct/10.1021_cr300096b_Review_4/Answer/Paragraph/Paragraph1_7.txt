                Comparative analysis of CO and H2 chemisorption techniques for characterizing active metal sites and metal dispersion in bimetallic catalysts reveals insights into their methodologies, applications, and limitations \[10.1021/cr300096b_4\]. CO chemisorption is a prevalent method for determining the number of active metal sites, achieved by measuring CO uptake using specialized instruments like the AMI-200ip. The process involves loading a known quantity of calcined catalyst into a quartz tube, reducing it with a hydrogen-containing gas mixture at a specific temperature, cooling it to room temperature, and then pulsing CO over the catalyst. The effluent CO is measured using a thermal conductivity detector (TCD). To compare catalytic activity among different catalysts, reaction rates must be normalized by the CO uptake values. CO chemisorption can also estimate metal dispersion assuming a 1:1 M/CO stoichiometry, although this is an oversimplification. H2 chemisorption is also used to determine active metal sites, but caution is needed as hydrogen can desorb from some bimetallic surfaces at room temperature \[10.1021/cr300096b_4\]. The literature does not provide a direct comparison between CO and H2 chemisorption techniques for characterizing bimetallic catalysts. However, it does mention the use of CO chemisorption to determine metal dispersion and CO uptake, which provides a quantitative comparison of the number of active sites among the two series of catalysts. The dispersion values might not be very meaningful due to the uncertainty in stoichiometry and surface compositions of the bimetallic catalysts, but they are listed to provide a relative ranking of metal dispersion \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.01.019\]. CO chemisorption is used to estimate the exposed metal fraction (dispersion) of different catalysts to avoid complications that may arise with hydrogen chemisorption, such as spillover. CO uptakes increase with the exposed metal fraction, but direct quantification of dispersion is not possible from these values alone due to varying CO/metal stoichiometry from Pt to Pd and even for the same metal with varying particle size. On platinum catalysts, the CO/Pt(surf) ratio is unity for a wide range of dispersions. Losses in metal dispersion can be related to increased color inhomogeneities observed after calcinations of the fluorinated catalysts, which is consistent with EXAFS data and cluster sizes observed by TEM. The CO uptake values can only be used as a semiquantitative measure of differences within one metal system. The impregnation method also affects metal dispersion, with the F-Pt-Pd/Al2O3 sample prepared by aqueous solution resulting in higher exposed metal fraction than that prepared using ethylene glycol \[10.1016/j.apcata.2003.11.032\].

                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Taking into consideration the advancements and findings discussed in the file, there lies an opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies. Future research endeavors might focus on refining the CO/metal stoichiometry for a more accurate quantification of metal dispersion across different bimetallic catalysts. Additionally, the development of new instruments that can mitigate the complications arising from hydrogen chemisorption, such as spillover, could lead to a more comprehensive characterization of bimetallic catalysts. The exploration of alternative impregnation methods and their effects on metal dispersion could also be a fruitful area of investigation. Moreover, the integration of CO and H2 chemisorption data with other advanced characterization techniques, such as EXAFS and TEM, could provide a more holistic understanding of the active metal sites and metal dispersion in bimetallic catalysts.

                通过比较分析CO和H2化学吸附技术在表征双金属催化剂的活性金属位点和金属分散度方面的效果，我们深入了解了它们的方法论、应用和局限性\[10.1021/cr300096b_4\]。CO化学吸附是一种常见的方法，用于确定双金属催化剂中的活性金属位点数量，通过使用如AMI-200ip等专用仪器测量CO的吸收量来实现。该过程包括将已知量的煅烧催化剂装入石英管中，用含氢气体混合物在特定温度下还原，冷却至室温，然后向催化剂脉冲CO。使用热导检测器（TCD）测量流出的CO。为了比较不同催化剂之间的催化活性，反应速率必须通过每个催化剂上的CO吸收值进行归一化。CO化学吸附还可以通过假设1:1的M/CO化学计量比来估计金属分散度，尽管这是一个过度简化的假设。H2化学吸附也被用来确定活性金属位点，但需要注意的是，氢气在室温下会从某些双金属表面解吸\[10.1021/cr300096b_4\]。文献没有直接比较CO和H2化学吸附技术在表征双金属催化剂方面的效果。然而，它确实提到了使用CO化学吸附来确定金属分散度和CO吸收量，这为两系列催化剂之间活性位点数量的定量比较提供了依据。由于双金属催化剂化学计量比和表面组成的不确定性，分散度值可能并不十分有意义，但它们被列出以提供金属分散度的相对排名\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.01.019\]。为了避开氢气化学吸附可能带来的并发症，如溢流，我们使用CO化学吸附来估计不同催化剂的暴露金属分数（分散度）。CO吸收量随着暴露金属分数的增加而增加，但仅凭这些值无法直接量化分散度，因为从Pt到Pd的CO/金属化学计量比会变化，甚至对于同一金属，随着颗粒尺寸的变化，化学计量比也会变化。在铂催化剂上，CO/Pt(surf)的比值在广泛的分散度范围内为1。金属分散度的损失可与氟化催化剂煅烧后观察到的颜色不均匀性增加相关，这与EXAFS数据和通过TEM观察到的聚集体大小一致。CO吸收值只能用作同一金属系统内差异的半定量度量。浸渍方法也影响金属分散度，其中通过水溶液制备的F-Pt-Pd/Al2O3样品的暴露金属分数高于使用乙二醇制备的F-Pt-Pd/Al2O3样品\[10.1016/j.apcata.2003.11.032\]。
