                Comparative analysis of CO and H2 chemisorption techniques for characterizing active metal sites and metal dispersion in bimetallic catalysts reveals distinct methodologies and applications, as well as limitations that must be considered for accurate data interpretation and normalization of reaction rates \[10.1021/cr300096b_4\]. CO chemisorption is a prevalent method for quantifying the number of active metal sites, utilizing specialized instruments such as the AMI-200ip to measure CO uptake \[10.1021/cr300096b_4\]. The process involves loading a known quantity of calcined catalyst into a quartz tube, reducing it with a hydrogen-containing gas mixture at a specific temperature, cooling it to room temperature, and then pulsing CO over the catalyst. The effluent CO is measured using a thermal conductivity detector (TCD). To compare catalytic activity among different catalysts, reaction rates must be normalized by the CO uptake values \[10.1021/cr300096b_4\]. CO chemisorption can also estimate metal dispersion assuming a 1:1 M/CO stoichiometry, although this is an oversimplification \[10.1021/cr300096b_4\]. H2 chemisorption is also used to determine active metal sites, but caution is needed as hydrogen can desorb from some bimetallic surfaces at room temperature \[10.1021/cr300096b_4\]. The literature does not provide a direct comparison between CO and H2 chemisorption techniques for characterizing bimetallic catalysts \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.01.019\]. However, it does mention the use of CO chemisorption to determine metal dispersion and CO uptake, which provides a quantitative comparison of the number of active sites among the two series of catalysts \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.01.019\]. The dispersion values might not be very meaningful due to the uncertainty in stoichiometry and surface compositions of the bimetallic catalysts, but they are listed to provide a relative ranking of metal dispersion \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.01.019\]. The amount of adsorbed CO is used as a quantitative comparison of the number of active sites among the catalysts \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.01.019\]. CO chemisorption is used to estimate the exposed metal fraction (dispersion) of different catalysts to avoid complications that may arise with hydrogen chemisorption, such as spillover \[10.1016/j.apcata.2003.11.032\]. CO uptakes increase with the exposed metal fraction, but direct quantification of dispersion is not possible from these values alone due to varying CO/metal stoichiometry from Pt to Pd and even for the same metal with varying particle size \[10.1016/j.apcata.2003.11.032\]. On platinum catalysts, the CO/Pt(surf) ratio is unity for a wide range of dispersions \[10.1016/j.apcata.2003.11.032\]. Losses in metal dispersion can be related to increased color inhomogeneities observed after calcinations of the fluorinated catalysts, which is consistent with EXAFS data and cluster sizes observed by TEM \[10.1016/j.apcata.2003.11.032\]. The CO uptake values can only be used as a semiquantitative measure of differences within one metal system \[10.1016/j.apcata.2003.11.032\]. The impregnation method also affects metal dispersion, with the F-Pt-Pd/Al2O3 sample prepared by aqueous solution resulting in higher exposed metal fraction than that prepared using ethylene glycol \[10.1016/j.apcata.2003.11.032\].

                通过比较分析CO和H2化学吸附技术在表征双金属催化剂的活性金属位点和金属分散度方面的应用，揭示了不同的方法学和应用，以及必须考虑的限制，以确保数据解释的准确性和反应速率的标准化\[10.1021/cr300096b_4\]。CO化学吸附是一种普遍的方法，用于量化活性金属位点的数量，利用如AMI-200ip等专业仪器测量CO的吸收\[10.1021/cr300096b_4\]。该过程包括将已煅烧的催化剂的已知量装入石英管中，用含氢气体混合物在特定温度下还原，冷却至室温，然后将CO脉冲通过催化剂。使用热导检测器（TCD）测量流出的CO\[10.1021/cr300096b_4\]。为了比较不同催化剂之间的催化活性，反应速率必须通过每个催化剂上的CO吸收值进行标准化\[10.1021/cr300096b_4\]。CO化学吸附还可以通过假设1:1的M/CO化学计量比来估计金属分散度，尽管这是一个过度简化的假设\[10.1021/cr300096b_4\]。H2化学吸附也被用来确定活性金属位点，但需要注意的是，氢气在室温下可以从某些双金属表面解吸\[10.1021/cr300096b_4\]。文献没有直接比较CO和H2化学吸附技术在表征双金属催化剂方面的应用\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.01.019\]。然而，它确实提到了使用CO化学吸附来确定金属分散度和CO吸收，这为两系列催化剂之间活性位点数量的定量比较提供了依据\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.01.019\]。由于双金属催化剂的化学计量比和表面组成的不确定性，分散度值可能没有太多意义，但它们被列出以提供金属分散度的相对排名\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.01.019\]。吸附的CO量被用作催化剂之间活性位点数量的定量比较\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.01.019\]。为了避开氢气化学吸附可能带来的并发症（例如溢流），使用CO化学吸附来估计不同催化剂的暴露金属分数（分散度）\[10.1016/j.apcata.2003.11.032\]。CO吸收量随着暴露金属分数的增加而增加，但由于从Pt到Pd的CO/金属化学计量比的变化，以及对于相同金属，随着颗粒尺寸的变化，仅从这些值本身无法直接量化分散度\[10.1016/j.apcata.2003.11.032\]。在铂催化剂上，CO/Pt(surf)的比率在广泛的分散度范围内为1\[10.1016/j.apcata.2003.11.032\]。金属分散度的损失与氟化催化剂煅烧后观察到的颜色不均匀性的增加有关，这与EXAFS数据和通过TEM观察到的聚集体大小一致\[10.1016/j.apcata.2003.11.032\]。CO吸收值只能用作同一金属系统内差异的半定量度量\[10.1016/j.apcata.2003.11.032\]。浸渍方法也影响金属分散度，其中通过水溶液制备的F-Pt-Pd/Al2O3样品的暴露金属分数高于使用乙二醇制备的F-Pt-Pd/Al2O3(E)\[10.1016/j.apcata.2003.11.032\]。
