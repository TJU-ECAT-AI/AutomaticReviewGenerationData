                    The pivotal role of isolated metal centers on oxide materials in facilitating C-H bond activation in heterogeneous catalysis is well-established \[10.1021/cr010333u\]. These centers act as Lewis acid sites, stabilizing transition states for C-H bond activation. The Mars van Krevelen mechanism, involving redox cycling and hydrogen abstraction by lattice oxygen, is a key process \[10.1021/cr010333u\]. H-abstraction, which involves direct hydrogen atom transfer from C-H bonds to the metal center, is another mechanism \[10.1021/cr010333u\]. These principles are applied in the industrial selective oxidation of alkanes, exemplified by the conversion of butane to maleic anhydride \[10.1021/cr010333u\]. The selective oxidation of butane to maleic anhydride is a 14-electron process, involving the formation of butenes, butadiene, and 1,4-dihydrofuran \[10.1021/cr900122p_0\]. The rate-determining step is the activation of the CH bond in butane, typically via hydrogen abstraction on VIVO species or by activated O2 coordinated to VIV centers \[10.1021/cr900122p_0\]. The conversion of butane to maleic anhydride is a well-studied process, providing insights into paraffin activation \[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]. The catalyst's morphology, P/V ratio, and activation conditions are critical factors \[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]. The P/V ratio higher than stoichiometric stabilizes the catalyst, preventing oxidation \[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]. The reaction pattern, starting with paraffin activation, is rate-determined by the first step \[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]. The unique properties of the catalyst, including the morphology of (VO)HP04.0.5H20 crystallites and the excess phosphorus adsorbed on its surface, contribute to its high selectivity \[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]. The activation process, involving either oxygen-free atmosphere activation or calcination in air, influences the catalyst's behavior \[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]. The equilibrated catalyst, with stable V4+, is no longer reoxidizable at high temperatures \[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]. The P/V ratio affects the catalyst's reducibility and reoxidizability, with a slight excess of phosphorus providing optimal activity and selectivity \[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]. The selective oxidation of n-butane to maleic anhydride is a well-studied process, providing insights into paraffin activation \[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]. The catalyst's ability to selectively activate n-butane is attributed to its morphology, P/V ratio, and activation conditions \[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]. The mechanism involves oxidative dehydrogenation, allylic oxidation, and electrophilic oxygen insertion, facilitated by the catalyst's polyfunctional nature \[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\].

                    隔离金属中心在氧化物材料上促进异相催化中C-H键活化的关键作用已得到充分证实\[10.1021/cr010333u\]。这些中心作为路易斯酸位点，稳定C-H键活化的过渡态。Mars van Krevelen机制，涉及氧化还原循环和晶格氧的氢抽象，是一个关键过程\[10.1021/cr010333u\]。H-抽象，涉及直接从C-H键向金属中心转移氢原子，是另一种机制\[10.1021/cr010333u\]。这些原则应用于工业选择性氧化烷烃，以丁烷转化为马来酸酐为例\[10.1021/cr010333u\]。丁烷选择性氧化为马来酸酐是一个14电子过程，涉及丁烯、丁二烯和1,4-二氢呋喃的形成\[10.1021/cr900122p_0\]。速率决定步骤是丁烷中CH键的活化，通常通过VIVO物种或与VIV中心配位的激活O2的氢抽象来实现\[10.1021/cr900122p_0\]。丁烷转化为马来酸酐是一个经过深入研究的过程，为石蜡活化提供了见解\[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]。催化剂的形态、P/V比和活化条件是关键因素\[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]。P/V比高于化学计量比稳定催化剂，防止氧化\[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]。反应模式从石蜡活化开始，由第一步决定速率\[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]。催化剂的独特性质，包括(VO)HP04.0.5H20结晶体的形态和表面吸附的过量磷，促成了其高选择性\[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]。活化过程，涉及无氧气氛活化或在空气中煅烧，影响催化剂的行为\[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]。平衡催化剂，具有稳定的V4+，在高温下不再可再氧化\[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]。P/V比影响催化剂的还原性和再氧化性，轻微过量的磷提供最佳活性和选择性\[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]。n-丁烷选择性氧化为马来酸酐是一个经过深入研究的过程，为石蜡活化提供了见解\[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]。催化剂选择性活化n-丁烷的能力归因于其形态、P/V比和活化条件\[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]。该机制涉及氧化脱氢、烯丙基氧化和亲电氧插入，由催化剂的多功能性质促进\[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]。
