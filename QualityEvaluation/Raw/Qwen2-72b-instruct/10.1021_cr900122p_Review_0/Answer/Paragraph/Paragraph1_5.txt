                The pivotal role of isolated metal centers on oxide materials in facilitating C-H bond activation in heterogeneous catalysis is underscored by their function as Lewis acid sites, enabling the Mars van Krevelen and H-abstraction mechanisms \[10.1021/cr010333u\]. These mechanisms are critical for the selective oxidation of alkanes, as exemplified by the conversion of butane to maleic anhydride, where the metal-oxide catalysts with isolated metal centers selectively activate C-H bonds, promoting desired oxidation reactions while minimizing over-oxidation to CO2 \[10.1021/cr010333u\]. The Mars van Krevelen mechanism involves redox cycling and hydrogen abstraction by lattice oxygen, forming metal-oxo intermediates, while H-abstraction involves direct hydrogen atom transfer to the metal center \[10.1021/cr010333u\]. The rate-determining step in the selective oxidation of butane to maleic anhydride is the activation of the C-H bond, facilitated by mechanisms such as concerted hydrogen abstraction on VIVO species or H-abstraction by activated O2 coordinated to VIV centers \[10.1021/cr900122p_0\]. This process is commercially viable, achieving high selectivities up to 97% at 65% conversion \[10.1021/cr900122p_0\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The detailed exploration of isolated metal centers on oxide materials in heterogeneous catalysis, particularly in the selective oxidation of alkanes, reveals a promising landscape for future research. The Mars van Krevelen and H-abstraction mechanisms, elucidated through the conversion of butane to maleic anhydride, offer a foundation for the development of novel catalysts with enhanced selectivity and efficiency. Future directions might focus on the synthesis of catalysts with tailored properties, such as optimized P/V ratios and controlled morphology, to improve the activation of C-H bonds and the selectivity towards desired products. Additionally, the investigation of alternative alkane oxidation pathways, beyond the well-studied butane to maleic anhydride process, could lead to the discovery of new catalytic systems for the conversion of other alkanes into valuable oxygenates. The integration of computational methods with experimental studies could further enhance our understanding of the complex reaction mechanisms involved in C-H bond activation, guiding the rational design of catalysts for selective alkane oxidation.

                隔离金属中心在氧化物材料上在异相催化中促进C-H键活化的关键作用在于它们作为路易斯酸位点的功能，使Mars van Krevelen和H-抽象机制得以实现\[10.1021/cr010333u\]。这些机制对于选择性氧化烷烃至关重要，以丁烷转化为马来酸酐为例，其中隔离金属中心的金属氧化物催化剂选择性地激活C-H键，促进所需氧化反应，同时最小化氧化至CO2\[10.1021/cr010333u\]。Mars van Krevelen机制涉及氧化还原循环和晶格氧的氢抽象，形成金属-氧中间体，而H-抽象涉及直接将氢原子转移到金属中心\[10.1021/cr010333u\]。丁烷选择性氧化为马来酸酐的速率决定步骤是C-H键的活化，通过VIVO物种的协同氢抽象或VIV中心协调的激活O2的H-抽象机制促进\[10.1021/cr900122p_0\]。这一过程在商业上可行，最高选择性可达97%，转化率为65%\[10.1021/cr900122p_0\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: 对氧化物材料上隔离金属中心在异相催化中的详细探索，特别是在选择性氧化烷烃方面，揭示了未来研究的前景。通过丁烷转化为马来酸酐阐明的Mars van Krevelen和H-抽象机制，为开发具有增强选择性和效率的新型催化剂提供了基础。未来的研究方向可能集中在合成具有定制性质的催化剂，如优化的P/V比率和受控形态，以改善C-H键的活化和对所需产品的选择性。此外，除了研究透彻的丁烷转化为马来酸酐过程之外，探索替代的烷烃氧化途径，可能会发现用于将其他烷烃转化为有价值的含氧化合物的新催化系统。将计算方法与实验研究相结合，可以进一步加深我们对涉及C-H键活化复杂反应机制的理解，指导选择性烷烃氧化催化剂的合理设计。
