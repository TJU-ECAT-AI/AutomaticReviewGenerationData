The pivotal role of isolated metal centers on oxide materials in facilitating C-H bond activation in heterogeneous catalysis is well-established \[10.1021/cr010333u\]. These centers act as Lewis acid sites, enabling the Mars van Krevelen mechanism and H-abstraction, both critical for selective alkane oxidation. The Mars van Krevelen mechanism involves redox cycling and hydrogen abstraction by lattice oxygen, forming metal-oxo intermediates, while H-abstraction involves direct hydrogen atom transfer from C-H bonds to the metal center \[10.1021/cr010333u\]. These principles are applied in the industrial selective oxidation of alkanes, exemplified by the conversion of butane to maleic anhydride, using supported metal oxide catalysts with isolated metal centers that selectively activate C-H bonds and promote desired oxidation reactions while minimizing over-oxidation to CO2 \[10.1021/cr010333u\].

The conversion of butane to maleic anhydride is a well-studied industrial process, involving a 14-electron oxidation process with key stages: butene formation, then butadiene, and finally 1,4-dihydrofuran to maleic anhydride \[10.1021/cr900122p_0\]. The rate-determining step is butane's C-H bond activation, typically via concerted abstraction on VIVO species or H-abstraction by O2 coordinated to VIV centers \[10.1021/cr900122p_0\]. This process, achieving high selectivities (up to 97% at 65% conversion), is commercially utilized, demonstrating the effectiveness of isolated metal centers in alkane oxidation \[10.1021/cr900122p_0\].

V-P-based catalysts, like vanadylpyrophosphate (VO)2P2O7, are pivotal in C-H bond activation, enabling selective alkane oxidation \[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]. The catalytic behavior of these catalysts depends on the method of preparation, P/V ratio, presence of additives, and activation procedures. The morphology of (VO)HP04.0.5H20 crystallites, influenced by factors such as the type of aliphatic alcohol and temperature of reduction of V5+, affects the exposure of the (001) plane and the extent of reduction of V5+ \[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]. A P/V ratio higher than stoichiometric stabilizes the catalyst in reactants' atmosphere and during calcination \[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]. The reaction pattern for butane oxidation to maleic anhydride involves oxidative dehydrogenation, allylic oxidation, and electrophilic oxygen insertion, with the rate-determining step being the activation of paraffins \[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]. The catalyst's ability to activate butane selectively is attributed to its unique properties, including morphology, P/V ratio, and activation conditions \[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\].
