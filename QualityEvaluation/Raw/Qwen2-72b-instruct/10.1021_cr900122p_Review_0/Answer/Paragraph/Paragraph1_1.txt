                    The pivotal role of isolated metal centers on oxide materials in facilitating C-H bond activation in heterogeneous catalysis is underscored by their function as Lewis acid sites, enabling the Mars van Krevelen mechanism and H-abstraction \[10.1021/cr010333u\]. These mechanisms, involving redox cycling and hydrogen abstraction by lattice oxygen, form metal-oxo intermediates and directly remove hydrogen atoms from C-H bonds, respectively. The principles are applied in the industrial selective oxidation of alkanes, exemplified by the conversion of butane to maleic anhydride, using supported metal oxide catalysts with isolated metal centers to selectively activate C-H bonds and promote desired oxidation reactions while minimizing over-oxidation to CO2 \[10.1021/cr010333u\].

                    Vanadium phosphate (VPO) catalysts, with isolated metal centers, enable selective C-H bond activation in heterogeneous catalysis, crucial for the direct conversion of alkanes to oxygenates \[10.1021/cr900122p_0\]. The Mars van Krevelen mechanism, involving alkane oxidation by the metal oxide and solid reoxidation by molecular oxygen, is central to this process. Butane's conversion to maleic anhydride, achieving up to 97% selectivity, exemplifies this mechanism. Butane undergoes a 14-electron oxidation, forming butenes, then butadiene, and finally 1,4-dihydrofuran, which is converted to maleic anhydride \[10.1021/cr900122p_0\].

                    The selective oxidation of n-butane to maleic anhydride, a well-studied industrial process, involves the activation of paraffins with high selectivity by vanadylpyrophosphate catalysts \[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]. The catalytic behavior of V-P-based catalysts, used in this process, is influenced by the method of preparation, P/V ratio, additives, and high-temperature activation and conditioning. The morphology of (VO)HP04.0.5H20 crystallites, affected by the type of aliphatic alcohol and temperature of V5+ reduction, impacts the exposure of the (001) plane and the extent of V5+ reduction \[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\].

                    隔离金属中心在氧化物材料上促进C-H键活化在异相催化中的关键作用，通过它们作为路易斯酸位点的功能得以强调，使Mars van Krevelen机制和H-抽象得以实现\[10.1021/cr010333u\]。这些机制涉及通过晶格氧的氧化还原循环和氢原子的抽象形成金属-氧中间体，以及直接从C-H键中去除氢原子。这些原则应用于工业选择性氧化烷烃，以丁烷转化为马来酸酐为例，使用具有隔离金属中心的金属氧化物催化剂选择性地活化C-H键，促进所需的氧化反应，同时最小化氧化为CO2\[10.1021/cr010333u\]。

                    磷酸钒(VPO)催化剂，具有隔离金属中心，使异相催化中选择性C-H键活化成为可能，对于直接将烷烃转化为含氧化合物至关重要\[10.1021/cr900122p_0\]。Mars van Krevelen机制，涉及金属氧化物氧化烷烃和分子氧的固体再氧化，是这一过程的核心。丁烷转化为马来酸酐，达到高达97%的选择性，是这一机制的例证。丁烷经历14电子氧化，形成丁烯，然后是丁二烯，最后是1,4-二氢呋喃，转化为马来酸酐\[10.1021/cr900122p_0\]。

                    选择性氧化n-丁烷为马来酸酐，一个经过深入研究的工业过程，涉及通过高选择性的钒氧磷酸盐催化剂激活石蜡\[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]。用于这一过程的V-P基催化剂的催化行为受制备方法、P/V比、添加剂和高温激活和条件化的影响。(VO)HP04.0.5H20晶粒的形态，受烷醇类型和V5+还原温度的影响，影响(001)面的暴露和V5+还原的程度\[10.1039/9781847553232-00246\]。
