1.Isolated metal centers on oxide materials can facilitate C-H bond activation in heterogeneous catalysis through mechanisms such as the Mars van Krevelen mechanism and H-abstraction. In the Mars van Krevelen mechanism, the metal center oxidizes the substrate, and the oxide lattice provides oxygen atoms for the reaction. In H-abstraction, the metal center directly abstracts a hydrogen atom from the substrate. These principles are applied in industrial selective oxidation of alkanes, such as the conversion of butane to maleic anhydride, where the metal center selectively oxidizes the alkane to the desired product while minimizing over-oxidation.
