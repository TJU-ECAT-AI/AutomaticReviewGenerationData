1.Isolated metal centers on oxide materials can facilitate C-H bond activation in heterogeneous catalysis through mechanisms such as the Mars van Krevelen mechanism and H-abstraction. In the Mars van Krevelen mechanism, a metal oxide catalyst is reduced by the substrate, forming a metal-carbon bond that can activate the C-H bond. H-abstraction involves the direct removal of a hydrogen atom from the C-H bond by the metal center. These principles are applied in industrial selective oxidation of alkanes, such as the conversion of butane to maleic anhydride, where a metal oxide catalyst selectively oxidizes the alkane to the desired product while minimizing over-oxidation.
