Answer: Isolated metal centers on oxide materials facilitate C-H bond activation in heterogeneous catalysis by acting as Lewis acid sites that can stabilize the transition state of C-H bond activation reactions. The Mars van Krevelen mechanism involves the abstraction of a hydrogen atom from an organic molecule by a metal oxide surface, forming a metal-hydrogen bond and a carbon-centered radical. The H-abstraction mechanism involves the direct abstraction of a hydrogen atom from an organic molecule by a metal center, forming a metal-hydrogen bond and a carbon-centered radical. These principles are applied in the industrial selective oxidation of alkanes, such as the conversion of butane to maleic anhydride, by using metal oxide catalysts that contain isolated metal centers that can activate C-H bonds and facilitate the selective oxidation of alkanes to more valuable products.
