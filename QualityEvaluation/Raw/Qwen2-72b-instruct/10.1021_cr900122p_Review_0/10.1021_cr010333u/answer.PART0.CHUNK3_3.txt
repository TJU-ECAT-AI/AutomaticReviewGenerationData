1.Isolated metal centers on oxide materials can facilitate C-H bond activation in heterogeneous catalysis by acting as Lewis acid sites that stabilize the transition state for C-H bond activation. The Mars van Krevelen mechanism involves the abstraction of a hydrogen atom from an organic substrate by a metal oxide lattice oxygen, forming a metal-oxo intermediate. The H-abstraction mechanism involves direct hydrogen atom abstraction by the metal center. These principles are applied in the industrial selective oxidation of alkanes, such as the conversion of butane to maleic anhydride, by using supported metal oxide catalysts with isolated metal centers that can activate C-H bonds selectively.
