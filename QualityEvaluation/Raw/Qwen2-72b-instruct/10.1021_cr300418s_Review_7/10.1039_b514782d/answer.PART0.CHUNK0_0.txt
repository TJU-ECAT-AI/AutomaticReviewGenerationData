1.The literature does not provide a direct explanation of how the Mars-van Krevelen mechanism operates in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions or the significance of the energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) in determining both the catalytic activity and the oxidizing ability of the oxide surface. However, it does discuss the role of ceria surfaces in the catalytic conversion of CO, NO2, and NO to CO2, NO, and N2, and the importance of understanding the properties of ceria surfaces for developing and optimizing new ceria-containing catalysts. The study found that the sensitivity of the energetics of the catalytic reactions to the nature of the surfaces is determined by the surface vacancy formation energy, which is not related to the stability of the pure surface. This suggests that the energy required for oxygen vacancy formation plays a crucial role in determining the catalytic activity and oxidizing ability of ceria surfaces.
