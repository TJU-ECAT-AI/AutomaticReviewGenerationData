1.The literature does not provide a direct explanation of how the Mars-van Krevelen mechanism operates in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions or the significance of the energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) in determining both the catalytic activity and the oxidizing ability of the oxide surface. However, it does discuss the role of ceria surfaces in catalytic reactions, particularly the reduction of ceria surfaces providing oxygen atoms for the oxidation of carbon monoxide to CO2, and the re-oxidation of a reduced ceria surface by extracting oxygen from NO2, which dissociates to NO. The NO formed can dissociate on a reduced ceria surface with the release of N2. The study also finds that the surface sensitivity of the catalytic reactions is determined by the surface vacancy formation energy, indicating that the energy required for oxygen vacancy formation plays a crucial role in the catalytic activity and oxidizing ability of the ceria surface.
