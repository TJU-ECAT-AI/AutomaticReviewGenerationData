1.The literature does not provide a direct explanation of how the Mars-van Krevelen mechanism operates in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions or the significance of the energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) in determining both the catalytic activity and the oxidizing ability of the oxide surface. However, it does discuss the role of ceria surfaces in catalytic reactions, particularly the reduction of ceria surfaces providing oxygen atoms for the oxidation of carbon monoxide to CO2, and the re-oxidation of a reduced ceria surface by extracting oxygen from NO2, which dissociates to NO. The energy for the conversion of CO to CO2, NO2 to NO, and NO to N2 over ceria surfaces is computed, and the surface sensitivity is discussed on the basis of the vacancy formation energies. The stability of the vacancy is driven by atomic relaxations, and the vacancy formation energies are not related to the stability of the pure surface.
