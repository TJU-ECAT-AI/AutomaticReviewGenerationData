1.The literature does not provide a direct explanation of how the Mars-van Krevelen mechanism operates in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions or the significance of the energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) in determining both the catalytic activity and the oxidizing ability of the oxide surface. However, it does discuss the role of ceria surfaces in catalytic reactions, particularly the conversion of CO, NO2, and NO to CO2, NO, and N2, and the sensitivity of these reactions to the nature of the ceria surface. The formation of oxygen vacancies on ceria surfaces is crucial for the catalytic cycle, as it provides oxygen atoms for the oxidation of CO to CO2 and the reduction of NO2 and NO to NO and N2. The surface sensitivity of the reactions is determined by the vacancy formation energies, which are not directly related to the stability of the pure ceria surfaces. The (110) surface is found to be the most suitable for studying these catalytic reactions due to its favorable vacancy formation energy.
