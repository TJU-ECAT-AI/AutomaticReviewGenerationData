          The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism involves surface oxygens from ceria participating in the PROX reaction, which proceeds via the mechanism involving the release and re-oxidation of oxygen from the ceria support. The oxygen vacancy formation energy is an important probe of the reducibility of the oxide, and a lower energy required for oxygen vacancy formation indicates a more easily reducible oxide, which can enhance catalytic activity and the oxidizing ability of the oxide surface. The energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) is an important probe of the reducibility of the oxide, and lower ΔEv values indicate easier reduction and higher catalytic activity and oxidizing ability of the oxide surface. The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism involves surface oxygens from ceria participating in the PROX reaction, which proceeds via the mechanism involving the removal and re-addition of oxygen from the oxide lattice. When the oxide is reduced, it can release oxygen to oxidize reactants, and then be re-oxidized by oxygen from the atmosphere or other oxidants, regenerating the original oxide state. The lower the ΔEv, the easier it is for the oxide to release oxygen, and the higher its catalytic activity and oxidizing ability.
