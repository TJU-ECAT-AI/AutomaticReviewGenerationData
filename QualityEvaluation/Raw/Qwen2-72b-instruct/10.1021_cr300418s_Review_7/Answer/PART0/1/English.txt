The energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) is assumed to be connected to the surface oxygen's ability to act as an oxidizing agent. A smaller ΔEv indicates a better oxidant surface. However, a very small ΔEv does not necessarily mean the surface is a good oxidation catalyst. In designing an oxidation catalyst, it is important to follow a moderation principle, making ΔEv small but not too small, to ensure the surface can be reoxidized by the gaseous oxidant.
