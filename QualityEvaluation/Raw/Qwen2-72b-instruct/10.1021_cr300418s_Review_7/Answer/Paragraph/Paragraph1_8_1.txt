Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions, with its intricate dynamics and the critical role of oxygen vacancy formation energy (ΔEv), presents a fertile ground for future research. The modulation of ΔEv through doping with specific elements offers a promising avenue for enhancing catalytic activity and surface oxidizing ability. The exploration of novel dopants and their effects on ΔEv could lead to the development of more efficient catalysts for various oxidation reactions. Additionally, the optimization of ΔEv to achieve a balance between oxygen removal and reinsertion is crucial for maintaining catalytic function.
