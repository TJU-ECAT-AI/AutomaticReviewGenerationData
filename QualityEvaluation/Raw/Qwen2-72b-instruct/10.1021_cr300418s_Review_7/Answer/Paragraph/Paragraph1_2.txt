<English>
The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions is a pivotal process that involves the interaction of reactants with surface oxygen, leading to the creation of oxygen vacancies [10.1021/jp071671n]. This mechanism is characterized by the reduced oxide's reoxidation by gas-phase oxygen, with the energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) playing a critical role in determining both the catalytic activity and the oxidizing ability of the oxide surface [10.1140/epjb_e2010-00295-x]. ΔEv measures the lattice oxygen's participation, impacting the catalytic activity and surface oxidizing ability [10.1021/jp071671n]. Lower ΔEv values indicate easier oxide reduction, enhancing catalytic activity and surface oxidizing ability [10.1140/epjb_e2010-00295-x]. Dopants like Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs, Os, Rh, Ir, Ni, Pd, Pt, Cu, Ag, Au, and Cd decrease ΔEv, enhancing catalytic activity [10.1021/jp071671n], whereas Mg, Ca, Sr, Ba, Sc, Y, La, Ti, Zr, Hf, Ce, V, Nb, Ta, Cr, Mo, W, Mn, Re, Fe, Ru, Co, Al, Ga, Sn, and Pb increase ΔEv, potentially reducing catalytic efficiency [10.1021/jp071671n].

ΔEv is a key descriptor of catalytic activity, as it indicates the oxide's oxidizing power [10.1021/jp073424]. The energy for oxygen vacancy formation reflects the oxide's catalytic activity and surface oxidizing ability [10.1021/jp073424]. Dopants lower ΔEv, enhancing the oxidizing ability of doped oxides compared to undoped ones [10.1021/jp802296g]. ΔEv's predictive value for surface oxygen's chemical reactivity is highlighted in the literature [10.1021/jp103604b].

In the context of propane dehydrogenation catalysts, the Mars-van Krevelen mechanism's dynamics, the role of oxygen vacancies, and the critical influence of ΔEv on catalytic activity and surface oxidizing ability are essential for understanding the rate of CO oxidation [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.06.008]. Lower ΔEv values on {110} and {100} surfaces facilitate CO reaction more easily, impacting CO oxidation rates [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.06.008]. The energy required for oxygen vacancy formation is crucial for catalytic activity and surface oxidizing ability [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.06.008].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism's dynamics, the role of oxygen vacancies, and the critical influence of ΔEv on catalytic activity and surface oxidizing ability provide a comprehensive framework for the design of efficient propane dehydrogenation catalysts. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel dopants that can further decrease ΔEv, enhancing catalytic activity while maintaining the stability of the oxide surface. The exploration of advanced computational methods, such as density functional theory (DFT), can provide deeper insights into the electronic structure of doped oxides and the mechanisms of oxygen vacancy formation. Moreover, the investigation of the interplay between ΔEv and other surface properties, such as the electronic structure and the nature of the dopant-oxide interface, can lead to the design of catalysts with optimized performance for propane dehydrogenation. The development of in situ spectroscopic techniques for monitoring the dynamic changes in the oxide surface during catalytic reactions can also contribute to a better understanding of the Mars-van Krevelen mechanism and its role in propane dehydrogenation.

Mars-van Krevelen机制在氧化物催化的氧化反应中是一个关键过程，涉及反应物与表面氧的相互作用，导致氧空位的形成 [10.1021/jp071671n]。该机制的特点是还原氧化物通过气相氧的再氧化，氧空位形成所需能量（ΔEv）在决定催化活性和氧化物表面的氧化能力方面起着关键作用 [10.1140/epjb_e2010-00295-x]。ΔEv衡量晶格氧的参与，影响催化活性和表面氧化能力 [10.1021/jp071671n]。较低的ΔEv值表明氧化物更容易还原，增强催化活性和表面氧化能力 [10.1140/epjb_e2010-00295-x]。Li、Na、K、Rb、Cs、Os、Rh、Ir、Ni、Pd、Pt、Cu、Ag、Au和Cd等掺杂剂降低ΔEv，增强催化活性 [10.1021/jp071671n]，而Mg、Ca、Sr、Ba、Sc、Y、La、Ti、Zr、Hf、Ce、V、Nb、Ta、Cr、Mo、W、Mn、Re、Fe、Ru、Co、Al、Ga、Sn和Pb等掺杂剂增加ΔEv，可能降低催化效率 [10.1021/jp071671n]。

ΔEv是催化活性的关键描述符，因为它指示氧化物的氧化能力 [10.1021/jp073424]。氧空位形成能量反映了氧化物的催化活性和表面氧化能力 [10.1021/jp073424]。掺杂剂降低ΔEv，增强掺杂氧化物的氧化能力，与未掺杂氧化物相比 [10.1021/jp802296g]。ΔEv对表面氧化学反应性的预测价值在文献中被突出 [10.1021/jp103604b]。

在丙烷脱氢催化剂的背景下，Mars-van Krevelen机制的动力学、氧空位的作用以及ΔEv对催化活性和表面氧化能力的决定性影响对于理解CO氧化速率至关重要 [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.06.008]。{110}和{100}表面上较低的ΔEv值使CO反应更容易，影响CO氧化速率 [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.06.008]。氧空位形成所需能量对于催化活性和表面氧化能力至关重要 [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.06.008]。

综合理解与未来展望：Mars-van Krevelen机制的动力学、氧空位的作用以及ΔEv对催化活性和表面氧化能力的决定性影响为设计高效的丙烷脱氢催化剂提供了全面的框架。未来的研究工作可能集中在开发新型掺杂剂，进一步降低ΔEv，增强催化活性，同时保持氧化物表面的稳定性。探索先进的计算方法，如密度泛函理论（DFT），可以提供掺杂氧化物的电子结构和氧空位形成机制的更深入见解。此外，研究ΔEv与其他表面性质（如电子结构和掺杂剂-氧化物界面性质）之间的相互作用，可以导致设计具有优化性能的丙烷脱氢催化剂。开发原位光谱技术，用于监测催化反应过程中氧化物表面的动态变化，也可以有助于更好地理解Mars-van Krevelen机制及其在丙烷脱氢中的作用。
