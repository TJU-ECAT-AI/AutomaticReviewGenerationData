The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions is a pivotal process in catalysis, particularly in the context of propane dehydrogenation [10.1021/jp071671n]. This mechanism involves reactants interacting with surface oxygen atoms, leading to the creation of oxygen vacancies [10.1021/jp071671n]. The reduced oxide is subsequently reoxidized by gas-phase oxygen, completing the catalytic cycle [10.1021/jp071671n]. Central to this mechanism is the energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv), which measures the participation of lattice oxygen and significantly influences catalytic activity and surface oxidizing ability [10.1021/jp071671n]. ΔEv is a critical descriptor, with lower values indicating easier reduction and enhanced catalytic activity [10.1140/epjb_e2010-00295-x]. Metal dopants, such as Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs, Os, Rh, Ir, Ni, Pd, Pt, Cu, Ag, Au, and Cd, decrease ΔEv, enhancing catalytic activity at the surface [10.1021/jp071671n]. Conversely, dopants like Mg, Ca, Sr, Ba, Sc, Y, La, Ti, Zr, Hf, Ce, V, Nb, Ta, Cr, Mo, W, Mn, Re, Fe, Ru, Co, Al, Ga, Sn, and Pb increase ΔEv, potentially reducing catalytic efficiency [10.1021/jp071671n].

The dynamics of the Mars-van Krevelen mechanism are further elucidated by the role of oxygen vacancies in facilitating the reaction's progress [10.1021/jp073424]. The energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) is a key factor, with lower ΔEv values indicating easier reduction and enhanced catalytic activity [10.1021/jp073424]. When the oxide is reduced, it can oxidize reactants and then be re-oxidized, regenerating its original state [10.1021/jp073424]. The lower the ΔEv, the higher the catalytic activity and oxidizing ability [10.1021/jp073424].

In the context of ceria surfaces, the Mars-van Krevelen mechanism is characterized by the alternate reduction and oxidation of the surface, with oxygen vacancy formation as a critical step [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.06.008]. Oxygen vacancies are replenished by gas-phase oxygen, and the energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) on {110} and {100} surfaces is lower, facilitating CO reaction [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.06.008]. This energy's significance lies in its impact on catalytic activity and surface oxidizing ability, influencing CO oxidation rates [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.06.008].

The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism in oxide-catalyzed reactions is influenced by the energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv), which is critical for catalytic activity and surface oxidizing ability [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.05.005]. A lower ΔEv indicates a more reactive surface, as seen in doped ceria where Pt dopants weaken oxygen bonds, making oxygen vacancy formation easier [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.05.005]. This results in a more reactive oxide surface, enhancing catalytic activity for oxidation reactions, particularly in methane activation [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.05.005].

In oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions, the Mars-van Krevelen mechanism uses surface oxygen atoms to oxidize reactants [10.1021/jp103604b]. Doping with specific elements enhances the reactivity of these oxygen atoms, reducing the energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) [10.1021/jp103604b]. This reduction in ΔEv indicates improved oxidizing ability [10.1021/jp103604b]. For example, in doped oxides, CO reacts more readily with oxygen near the dopant, facilitating the mechanism [10.1021/jp103604b]. Dopants with higher valence than the replaced cation adsorb oxygen from the gas phase, enhancing its oxidizing capacity [10.1021/jp103604b].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism, central to oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions, highlights the critical role of oxygen vacancy formation energy (ΔEv) in determining catalytic activity and surface oxidizing ability. The modulation of ΔEv through doping with specific elements offers a promising avenue for enhancing catalytic performance in propane dehydrogenation and other oxidation reactions. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel dopants that can significantly lower ΔEv while maintaining the balance required for efficient oxygen vacancy replenishment. Additionally, the exploration of advanced computational methods, such as density functional theory, could provide deeper insights into the electronic structure of doped oxides and guide the rational design of catalysts with optimized ΔEv values. The integration of experimental and computational approaches will be crucial for advancing our understanding of the Mars-van Krevelen mechanism and for the development of more efficient and selective catalysts for propane dehydrogenation and other important chemical transformations.

Mars-van Krevelen机制在氧化物催化的氧化反应中是关键过程，特别是在丙烷脱氢的背景下[10.1021/jp071671n]。该机制涉及反应物与表面氧原子的相互作用，导致氧空位的形成[10.1021/jp071671n]。随后，还原的氧化物通过气相氧重新氧化，完成催化循环[10.1021/jp071671n]。该机制的核心是氧空位形成所需的能量(ΔEv)，它衡量了晶格氧的参与，并显著影响催化活性和表面氧化能力[10.1021/jp071671n]。ΔEv是一个关键的描述符，较低的值表明更容易还原和增强的催化活性[10.1140/epjb_e2010-00295-x]。金属掺杂剂，如Li、Na、K、Rb、Cs、Os、Rh、Ir、Ni、Pd、Pt、Cu、Ag、Au和Cd，降低ΔEv，增强表面的催化活性[10.1021/jp071671n]。相反，如Mg、Ca、Sr、Ba、Sc、Y、La、Ti、Zr、Hf、Ce、V、Nb、Ta、Cr、Mo、W、Mn、Re、Fe、Ru、Co、Al、Ga、Sn和Pb等掺杂剂增加ΔEv，可能降低催化效率[10.1021/jp071671n]。

Mars-van Krevelen机制的动力学通过氧空位在促进反应进展中的作用进一步阐明[10.1021/jp073424]。氧空位形成所需的能量(ΔEv)是一个关键因素，较低的ΔEv值表明更容易还原和增强的催化活性[10.1021/jp073424]。当氧化物被还原时，它可以氧化反应物，然后重新氧化，恢复其原始状态[10.1021/jp073424]。ΔEv越低，催化活性和氧化能力越高[10.1021/jp073424]。

在铈表面的背景下，Mars-van Krevelen机制的特点是表面的交替还原和氧化，氧空位的形成是一个关键步骤[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.06.008]。氧空位由气相氧补充，{110}和{100}表面上氧空位形成所需的能量(ΔEv)较低，促进CO反应[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.06.008]。这种能量的重要性在于它对催化活性和表面氧化能力的影响，影响CO氧化速率[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.06.008]。

在氧化物催化的反应中，Mars-van Krevelen机制受到氧空位形成所需能量(ΔEv)的影响，这对催化活性和表面氧化能力至关重要[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.05.005]。较低的ΔEv表明表面更具有反应性，如在掺杂铈中所见，Pt掺杂剂削弱氧键，使氧空位形成更容易[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.05.005]。这导致更具有反应性的氧化物表面，增强氧化反应的催化活性，特别是在甲烷活化中[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.05.005]。

在氧化物催化的氧化反应中，Mars-van Krevelen机制使用表面氧原子来氧化反应物[10.1021/jp103604b]。通过特定元素的掺杂增强这些氧原子的反应性，降低氧空位形成所需的能量(ΔEv)[10.1021/jp103604b]。ΔEv的这种降低表明氧化能力提高[10.1021/jp103604b]。例如，在掺杂氧化物中，CO在掺杂剂附近更易于与氧反应，促进机制[10.1021/jp103604b]。比被取代的阳离子具有更高价态的掺杂剂从气相吸附氧，增强其氧化能力[10.1021/jp103604b]。
