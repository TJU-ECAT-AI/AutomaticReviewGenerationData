The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism, pivotal in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions, involves the interaction of reactants with surface oxygen, leading to the creation of oxygen vacancies [\[10.1021/jp071671n\]]. The reduced oxide is subsequently reoxidized by gas-phase oxygen, a process that is critical for the mechanism's cyclic nature. Oxygen vacancy formation energy (ΔEv) is a key descriptor, measuring the participation of lattice oxygen and significantly impacting both catalytic activity and surface oxidizing ability [\[10.1021/jp071671n\]]. The substitution of Zn atoms in ZnO(1010) with various dopants can alter ΔEv, with some dopants decreasing it and enhancing surface properties, while others increase ΔEv, potentially reducing catalytic efficiency [\[10.1021/jp071671n\]].

In ceria-catalyzed oxidation reactions, the Mars-van Krevelen mechanism utilizes ceria surface oxygens in the PROX reaction, involving the removal and re-oxidation of oxygen [\[10.1140/epjb\_e2010-00295-x\]]. A lower ΔEv indicates easier oxide reduction, enhancing catalytic activity and surface oxidizing ability [\[10.1140/epjb\_e2010-00295-x\]]. When the oxide is reduced, it releases oxygen to oxidize reactants and can be re-oxidized, regenerating its original state [\[10.1140/epjb\_e2010-00295-x\]]. The lower the ΔEv, the higher the oxide's catalytic activity and oxidizing ability [\[10.1140/epjb\_e2010-00295-x\]].

The dynamics of the Mars-van Krevelen mechanism and the role of ΔEv significantly influence CO oxidation rates [\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.06.008\]]. Lower ΔEv on {110} and {100} surfaces facilitates CO reaction, enhancing catalytic activity and surface oxidizing ability [\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.06.008\]].

ΔEv is a critical indicator of the oxide's oxidizing ability and catalytic activity [\[10.1021/jp073424\]]. In doped oxides, ΔEv's variability makes it a key descriptor of catalytic activity [\[10.1021/jp802296g\]]. Dopants lower ΔEv, enhancing the oxidizing ability of oxides compared to undoped ones [\[10.1021/jp802296g\]]. In ceria, Pt dopants weaken the oxygen-surface bond, facilitating oxygen vacancy formation and reducing ΔEv [\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.05.005\]]. This enhances the oxide's reactivity and catalytic activity for methane activation and oxidation [\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.05.005\]].
