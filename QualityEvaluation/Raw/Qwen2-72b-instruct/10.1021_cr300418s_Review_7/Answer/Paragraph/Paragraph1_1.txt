    The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions is a pivotal process that involves reactants interacting with surface oxygen, creating oxygen vacancies, and the subsequent reoxidation of the oxide by gas-phase oxygen \[10.1021/jp071671n\]. The energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) is a critical parameter that measures lattice oxygen's participation in this mechanism, significantly impacting both catalytic activity and surface oxidizing ability \[10.1021/jp071671n\]. Lower ΔEv values indicate easier oxide reduction, enhancing catalytic activity and surface oxidizing ability \[10.1140/epjb_e2010-00295-x\]. The role of ΔEv in determining the catalytic activity and surface oxidizing ability is directly linked to the rate of oxygen vacancy formation, density, and easier oxygen release, boosting surface reactivity and oxidizing ability \[10.1039/c1jm11238d\].

    Metal dopants in ZnO(1010) surface, such as Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs, Os, Rh, Ir, Ni, Pd, Pt, Cu, Ag, Au, and Cd, decrease ΔEv, enhancing catalytic activity and oxidizing ability by preferentially locating at the surface \[10.1021/jp071671n\]. Conversely, Mg, Ca, Sr, Ba, Sc, Y, La, Ti, Zr, Hf, Ce, V, Nb, Ta, Cr, Mo, W, Mn, Re, Fe, Ru, Co, Al, Ga, Sn, and Pb increase ΔEv, potentially reducing catalytic efficiency, with some preferring surface substitution \[10.1021/jp071671n\]. In ceria doped with Pt, the presence of Pt dopants weakens the bond between oxygen atoms and the surface, facilitating oxygen vacancy formation and enhancing catalytic activity for methane activation and oxidation reactions \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.05.005\].

    Zr dopants in ceria surfaces lower ΔEv, enhancing lattice oxygen mobility and catalytic activity \[10.1007/s10562-010-0446-5\]. This reduction in ΔEv facilitates the Ce4+ to Ce3+ reduction, assisting oxygen release for the oxidation process \[10.1007/s10562-010-0446-5\]. Doping with elements that lower the energy of oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) enhances the oxide's oxidizing ability, as oxygen atoms near dopants react more readily with reactants like CO \[10.1021/jp103604b\]. Dopants with higher valence adsorb O2, improving its oxidizing capacity \[10.1021/jp103604b\].

    在ZnO(1010)表面，如Li、Na、K、Rb、Cs、Os、Rh、Ir、Ni、Pd、Pt、Cu、Ag、Au和Cd等金属掺杂剂会降低ΔEv，通过优先定位在表面增强催化活性和氧化能力\[10.1021/jp071671n\]。相反，Mg、Ca、Sr、Ba、Sc、Y、La、Ti、Zr、Hf、Ce、V、Nb、Ta、Cr、Mo、W、Mn、Re、Fe、Ru、Co、Al、Ga、Sn和Pb等会增加ΔEv，可能降低催化效率，其中一些更倾向于表面替代\[10.1021/jp071671n\]。在Pt掺杂的CeO2中，Pt掺杂剂削弱了氧原子与表面之间的键，促进了氧空位的形成，增强了甲烷活化和氧化反应的催化活性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.05.005\]。

    Zr掺杂剂在CeO2表面降低了ΔEv，增强了晶格氧的流动性，提高了催化活性\[10.1007/s10562-010-0446-5\]。这种ΔEv的降低促进了Ce4+到Ce3+的还原，协助了氧化过程中的氧释放\[10.1007/s10562-010-0446-5\]。通过降低氧空位形成能（ΔEv）的元素掺杂增强了氧化物的氧化能力，因为靠近掺杂剂的氧原子更容易与像CO这样的反应物反应\[10.1021/jp103604b\]。高化合价的掺杂剂吸附O2，提高了其氧化能力\[10.1021/jp103604b\]。

    在Mars-van Krevelen机制中，氧化物表面通过向还原剂捐赠氧原子而充当氧化剂。氧空位形成所需能量（ΔEv）是关键的，因为它决定了氧化物的氧化能力和催化活性\[10.1021/jp301341\]。较低的ΔEv使氧太容易被移除，阻碍了通过气相氧对表面的再氧化，这是催化循环的关键\[10.1021/jp301341\]。因此，在催化剂设计中，适度原则适用：掺杂剂应促进氧的移除，但不应过度\[10.1021/jp301341\]。低化合价的掺杂剂增强了氧化剂的能力，但保持平衡以确保再氧化是关键\[10.1021/jp301341\]。

    在AuxTi1xO2中，由于放热反应，CO氧化在热力学上是有利的。吸附后，CO与一个氧分子反应，释放能量和CO2，而一个氧原子填充空位，恢复表面\[10.1007/s10562-005-0014-6\]。掺杂削弱了表面氧键，增强了氧化能力，使CO氧化在掺杂表面上更加有利\[10.1007/s10562-005-0014-6\]。一个好的催化剂平衡了氧化物的还原和氧化速率，确保了氧空位的快速补充\[10.1007/s10562-005-0014-6\]。
