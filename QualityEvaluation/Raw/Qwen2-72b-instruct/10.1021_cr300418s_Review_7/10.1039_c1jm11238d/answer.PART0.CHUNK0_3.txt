1.The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism operates in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions by utilizing the oxygen vacancies on the oxide surface to facilitate the transfer of oxygen to the reactant molecules. The energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) is significant in determining both the catalytic activity and the oxidizing ability of the oxide surface. A lower ΔEv indicates a higher density of oxygen vacancies, which enhances the reactivity of the surface and its ability to oxidize reactants. This is particularly relevant for ceria-based materials, where the presence of oxygen vacancies is crucial for their catalytic performance in reactions such as CO oxidation and NOx reduction.

1.火星-范克雷维尔机制在氧化物催化的氧化反应中通过利用氧化物表面的氧空位来促进氧向反应分子的转移。氧空位形成所需的能量(ΔEv)在确定氧化物表面的催化活性和氧化能力方面具有重要意义。较低的ΔEv表明氧空位的密度较高，这增强了表面的反应性和氧化反应物的能力。这对铈基材料尤其相关，其中氧空位的存在对于它们在CO氧化和NOx还原等反应中的催化性能至关重要。
