1.The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism operates in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions by involving the redox properties of the oxide surface. The energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) is significant in determining both the catalytic activity and the oxidizing ability of the oxide surface. Lower ΔEv values facilitate the formation of oxygen vacancies, which enhances the oxidative power of the oxide, making it more effective in catalyzing oxidation reactions.
