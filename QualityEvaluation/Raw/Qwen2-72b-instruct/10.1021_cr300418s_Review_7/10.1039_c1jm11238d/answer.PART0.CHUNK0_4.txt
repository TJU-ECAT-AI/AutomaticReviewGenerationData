1.The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism operates in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions by utilizing the oxygen vacancies on the oxide surface to facilitate the transfer of oxygen from the oxide to the reactant molecules. The energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) is significant in determining both the catalytic activity and the oxidizing ability of the oxide surface. A lower ΔEv indicates that the oxide surface can more easily release oxygen, which enhances its catalytic activity for oxidation reactions and increases its oxidizing ability.
