<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1.The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism involves surface oxygens from ceria participating in the PROX reaction, which proceeds via the mechanism involving the release and re-oxidation of oxygen from the ceria support. The oxygen vacancy formation energy is an important probe of the reducibility of the oxide, and a lower energy required for oxygen vacancy formation indicates a more easily reducible oxide, which can enhance catalytic activity and the oxidizing ability of the oxide surface.

</EnglishVersionAnswer>
