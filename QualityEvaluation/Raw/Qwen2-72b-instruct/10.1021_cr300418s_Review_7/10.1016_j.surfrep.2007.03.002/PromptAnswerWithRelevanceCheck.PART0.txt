The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism operates in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions by utilizing lattice oxygen from the oxide surface to oxidize the reactant. The energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) is significant as it determines the ease with which lattice oxygen can be released, impacting both the catalytic activity and the oxidizing ability of the oxide surface. A lower ΔEv indicates a more active catalyst and a higher oxidizing ability due to the easier formation of oxygen vacancies.

</EnglishVersionAnswer>
