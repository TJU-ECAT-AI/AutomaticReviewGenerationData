<EnglishVersionAnswer>

The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism operates in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions by utilizing lattice oxygen from the oxide surface to oxidize reactants, with the subsequent reduction of the oxide by oxygen vacancy formation. The energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) is significant in determining both the catalytic activity and the oxidizing ability of the oxide surface, as lower ΔEv values indicate easier oxygen vacancy formation, which can enhance the catalytic performance and the ability of the oxide to act as an oxidizing agent.

</EnglishVersionAnswer>

<ChineseVersionAnswer>

Mars-van Krevelen机制通过利用氧化物表面的晶格氧来氧化反应物，在氧化物催化的氧化反应中起作用，随后通过氧空位的形成使氧化物还原。氧空位形成所需能量（ΔEv）在确定氧化物表面的催化活性和氧化能力方面具有重要意义，因为较低的ΔEv值表明氧空位形成更容易，这可以增强催化性能和氧化物作为氧化剂的能力。
