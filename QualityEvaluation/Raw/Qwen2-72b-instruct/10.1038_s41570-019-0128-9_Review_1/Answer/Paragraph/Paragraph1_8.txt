                Comparative analysis of FexNiy/CeO2 and vanadium oxide catalysts in CO2-ODH reactions: Performance, selectivity, and the influence of supports and composition on reaction mechanisms and product formation \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1\]. Vanadium oxide catalysts, such as V2O5, exhibit activity and selectivity that are significantly influenced by the nature of the support, which impacts reducibility, acid-base properties, and dispersion. Supported VOx catalysts are more selective than bulk V2O5 due to the stabilization of active VOx surface structures by support interactions \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1\]. DFT calculations and isotopic labeling experiments suggest that both one-step and two-step pathways are possible for CO2-ODH over V-based catalysts \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1\]. The Mars van Krevelen mechanism is common in V-based catalysts, and increased participation of oxygen atoms from the support, such as CeO2, improves alkene selectivity \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1\]. CeO2 inclusion enhances oxygen mobility and vacancy concentration compared to pure ZrO2 \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1\]. Mixed-oxide supports have better activity due to greater acid-base bifunctionality \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1\]. Acidic supports interact weakly with V2O5, leading to poorly dispersed crystallites \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1\]. Polymeric V species result in low alkene yield, highlighting dispersion's role in controlling selectivity \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1\]. DFT studies show that the optimal size of V2O5 on supports like SiO2 or CeO2 differs \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1\]. Dimers have a kinetic preference for the rate-determining H-abstraction step \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1\]. V2O5 dispersed in mesoporous SiO2 nanospheres can achieve 90% propylene selectivity, but conversion decreases from 60% to 20% within one cycle \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1\]. This performance is maintained for eight reaction-regeneration cycles \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1\]. The development of V2O5 catalysts is promising, but acidic sites promoting alkene cracking and health concerns regarding V2O5 dust remain challenges \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1\]. Fe3Ni1/CeO2 catalysts exhibit high C2H4 selectivity (>75%) in CO2-ODH reactions, while Fe1Ni3/CeO2 has high CO selectivity (>98%) through the dry reforming (DR) pathway \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1\]. The selectivities remain stable at comparable C2H6 conversions, indicating intrinsic kinetic differences \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1\]. C2H4 is the primary product over Fe3Ni1/CeO2, suggesting its potential as a CO2-ODH catalyst \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1\]. Ni/CeO2 is an excellent reforming catalyst, whereas Fe/CeO2 is less effective \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1\]. The high selectivity of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 for CO2-ODH is attributed to distinct active sites involving Fe-Ni interaction \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1\].
