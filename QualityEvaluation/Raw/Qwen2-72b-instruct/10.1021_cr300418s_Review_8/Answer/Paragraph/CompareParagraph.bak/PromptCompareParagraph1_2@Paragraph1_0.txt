SVDs have been shown to favorably affect the properties of the host oxide in various catalytic applications \[10.1021/cr300418s\_8\]. Challenges persist in formulating general predictive rules for dopant behavior due to the complexity of interactions between dopants and host oxides \[10.1021/cr300418s\_8\]. Challenges persist in formulating general predictive rules for dopant behavior due to the complexity of interactions between dopants and host oxides. The behavior of flexible-valence dopants (FVDs) is particularly difficult to predict, especially in reducible host oxides. More computational and experimental work is needed to clarify the behavior of these systems \[10.1021/cr300418s\_8\].

While significant progress has been made in understanding the effects of dopants on the catalytic properties of metal oxides, challenges remain in formulating general predictive rules for dopant behavior. Future research endeavors might focus on developing more sophisticated computational models that can accurately predict the behavior of dopants in various host systems, considering factors such as Lewis acid-base interactions and the reducibility of the host oxide. Additionally, experimental studies could be designed to systematically investigate the effects of dopants on the catalytic properties of metal oxides under different reaction conditions, providing a comprehensive understanding of dopant-host interactions. The development of new dopant materials with tailored properties could also be explored, aiming to enhance the catalytic activity and selectivity of metal oxides in various applications.
