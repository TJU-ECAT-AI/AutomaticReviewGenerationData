1.The literature does not provide specific details on how the diverse properties of niobium oxides, including their various stable forms and oxidation states, influence their behavior as dopants in NiO catalysts for ethane oxidative dehydrogenation. However, it does mention that doping NiO with Nb leads to the formation of a NiNb solid solution, which effectively converts NiO from a total oxidation catalyst to an efficient ODH catalytic material. The optimum incorporation of Nb ions in the NiO lattice was achieved for a Nb/Ni ratio of 0.176 (Ni0.85Nb0.15 catalyst), resulting in high activity at low temperature and high ethene selectivity at high conversion levels. Niobium affects the selectivity to olefin by modifying the oxygen species on the catalytic surface. The introduction of Nb induces a significant increase in the activation energy for oxygen exchange up to 175.6 kJ/mol, reflecting more strongly bonded oxygen species on Nb/NiO compared with pure NiO. Doping with Nb reduces or eliminates the very active nonselective Ospecies on the surface of NiO by reducing the active sites for rapid oxygen dissociation on the surface. This leads to the formation of less labile selective lattice O2species, responsible for the high ODH activity recorded over the NiNbO mixed oxide.
