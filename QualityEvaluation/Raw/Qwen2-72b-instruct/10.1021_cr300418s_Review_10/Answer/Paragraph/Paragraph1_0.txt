                    The diverse properties and oxidation states of niobium oxides significantly influence their behavior as dopants in NiO catalysts for ethane oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. At low Nb content (3-7%), the apparent activation energy and intrinsic rate of ethane conversion decrease, enhancing selectivity to ethylene \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. Beyond 15% Nb, the inactive NiNb2O6 phase forms, reducing the active phase content \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. The optimal 15% Nb content boosts yields by decreasing active phase particle size and preventing sintering \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. Over time, all catalysts show a monotonous decrease in activity due to the formation of NiNb2O6 and reduced oxygen over-stoichiometry, leading to a lack of active Ospecies \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. Attempts to regenerate the catalysts have been unsuccessful \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. Niobium oxides enhance NiO catalysts' redox properties and selectivity for ethane ODH \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]. At Nb content >5 at.%, NbNiO composites are less active but more selective due to the Nb-rich phase covering unselective active oxygen species of NiO \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]. However, high Nb loadings lead to significant catalyst deactivation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]. The most efficient ODH catalysts contain 34 at.% Nb, combining high activity, selectivity, and stability \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]. The preparation method's impact on NbNiO nanocomposites suggests potential for further improvement in NiO-catalyzed ethane ODH \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]. Challenges arise in reconciling theoretical predictions with experimental observations due to the complex interplay between niobium oxides' properties and catalyst behavior \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements and findings discussed, there is a clear opportunity to explore the optimization of niobium-doped NiO catalysts for ethane ODH. Future research endeavors might focus on developing novel preparation methods to enhance the stability and activity of these catalysts, particularly in addressing the formation of inactive NiNb2O6 phases. Additionally, the development of computational models that accurately predict the behavior of niobium oxides in NiO catalysts could significantly aid in the design of more efficient catalysts. The exploration of alternative dopants that can synergistically interact with niobium to further improve selectivity and activity is also a promising direction. Moreover, the investigation of the role of surface morphology and defect structures in the catalytic performance of niobium-doped NiO catalysts could provide insights into the mechanisms underlying the observed catalytic behavior.

                    钽氧化物的多样性质和氧化态显著影响其在NiO催化剂中作为乙烷氧化脱氢(ODH)掺杂剂的行为\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]。在低Nb含量(3-7%)时，乙烷转化的表观活化能和内在速率降低，提高了对乙烯的选择性\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]。超过15%的Nb，形成不活跃的NiNb2O6相，减少了活性相含量\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]。15%的Nb含量通过减少活性相颗粒尺寸和防止烧结来提高产量\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]。随着时间的推移，所有催化剂的活性都单调下降，这是由于NiNb2O6的形成和氧过量减少，导致缺乏活性O物种\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]。尝试再生催化剂均未成功\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]。钽氧化物增强了NiO催化剂的氧化还原性质和对乙烷ODH的选择性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]。在Nb含量>5 at.%时，NbNiO复合材料的活性较低但选择性较高，因为Nb富集相覆盖了NiO的非选择性活性氧物种\[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]。然而，高Nb负载导致显著的催化剂失活\[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]。最有效的ODH催化剂含有34 at.%的Nb，结合了高活性、选择性和稳定性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]。NbNiO纳米复合材料的制备方法对其性能的影响表明NiO催化的乙烷ODH有进一步改进的潜力\[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]。由于钽氧化物的性质和催化剂行为之间的复杂相互作用，理论预测与实验观察之间的协调存在挑战\[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]。

                    综合理解与未来展望: 考虑到讨论的进展和发现，有明显的机会探索铌掺杂NiO催化剂的优化，用于乙烷ODH。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发新的制备方法，以增强这些催化剂的稳定性和活性，特别是在解决不活跃的NiNb2O6相的形成问题上。此外，开发能够准确预测铌氧化物在NiO催化剂中行为的计算模型，可以显著帮助设计更有效的催化剂。探索可以与铌协同作用以进一步提高选择性和活性的替代掺杂剂也是一个有希望的方向。此外，研究表面形态和缺陷结构在铌掺杂NiO催化剂催化性能中的作用，可以为观察到的催化行为背后的机制提供见解。
