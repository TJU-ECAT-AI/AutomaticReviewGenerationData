                The diverse properties and oxidation states of niobium oxides significantly influence their behavior as dopants in NiO catalysts for ethane oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH), as established in reference \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. At low Nb content (3-7%), the apparent activation energy and intrinsic rate of ethane conversion decrease, enhancing ethylene selectivity. The optimal Ni0.85Nb0.15O compound maximizes yields, as observed in reference \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. However, beyond 15% Nb, the inactive NiNb2O6 phase forms, reducing catalytic activity. Over time, all catalysts deactivate due to the formation of NiNb2O6 and reduced oxygen over-stoichiometry, leading to a lack of Oactive species, as reported in reference \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. The surface interaction between NiO and amorphous Nb2O5, influenced by the niobium precursor, is crucial for catalytic efficiency, as highlighted in reference \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. The thermal decomposition of oxalate ions further impacts catalytic properties, as detailed in reference \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. Catalyst regeneration attempts have been unsuccessful, as noted in reference \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The intricate interplay between niobium oxides' properties and NiO catalysts' behavior for ethane ODH presents a fertile ground for future research. The challenges in reconciling theoretical predictions with experimental observations, as discussed in reference \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\], underscore the need for advanced characterization techniques and computational models that can accurately predict the behavior of these complex systems. Future studies might focus on developing catalyst regeneration strategies, optimizing the preparation methods to enhance the stability and activity of NiO catalysts, and exploring the role of other dopants in conjunction with niobium to further improve ethane ODH performance. The development of in situ spectroscopic methods to monitor the structural changes in niobium-doped NiO catalysts under reaction conditions could provide insights into the deactivation mechanisms and guide the design of more robust catalysts. Additionally, the exploration of niobium oxides in other catalytic systems for hydrocarbon conversion could broaden the application scope of these materials.

                根据\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]参考文献，铌氧化物的多样性质和氧化态显著影响其在NiO催化剂中作为乙烷氧化脱氢（ODH）的掺杂剂的行为。在低Nb含量（3-7%）时，乙烷转化的表观活化能和内在速率降低，提高了乙烯选择性。如\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]中所述，最优的Ni0.85Nb0.15O化合物可最大化产率。然而，超过15%的Nb，会形成不活跃的NiNb2O6相，降低催化活性。随着时间的推移，所有催化剂由于NiNb2O6的形成和NiO氧过量的减少，导致O活性物种的缺乏，而逐渐失活，如\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]中所述。NiO和无定形Nb2O5之间的表面相互作用，受铌前体的影响，对催化效率至关重要，如\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]中所述。草酸盐离子的热分解进一步影响催化性能，如\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]中所述。催化剂再生尝试未能成功，如\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]中所述。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: 铌氧化物的性质与NiO催化剂在乙烷ODH中的行为之间的复杂相互作用为未来的研究提供了肥沃的土壤。如\[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]中讨论的，理论预测与实验观察之间的协调挑战，突出了需要先进的表征技术和计算模型，以准确预测这些复杂系统的性能。未来的研究可能集中在开发催化剂再生策略，优化制备方法以增强NiO催化剂的稳定性和活性，以及探索其他掺杂剂与铌结合，以进一步提高乙烷ODH性能。开发原位光谱方法，以监测反应条件下铌掺杂NiO催化剂的结构变化，可以提供对失活机制的见解，并指导更坚固催化剂的设计。此外，探索铌氧化物在其他用于碳氢化合物转化的催化系统中的作用，可以拓宽这些材料的应用范围。
