    Hierarchical zeolites, by integrating micropores with mesopores/macropores, significantly enhance the accessibility and metal dispersion in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions \[10.3390/catal9020174\]. These materials, characterized by their enhanced structures, offer superior catalytic efficiency in reactions involving bulky molecules compared to conventional zeolites. Synthesis methods often involve the use of structure directing agents and high-temperature crystallization processes \[10.3390/catal9020174\]. The combination of intrinsic micropores with mesoporous/macroporous networks in hierarchical zeolites improves micropore accessibility for PDH reactions without compromising confinement effects \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. These zeolites are synthesized via desilication, self-assembly, bifunctional surfactants, and cotemplates like TPAOH and TBPOH, which result in higher surface areas, weaker acidity, and improved stability, thereby boosting propane conversion and propylene selectivity \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. The hierarchical structure facilitates intermetallic compound formation, enhancing PDH performance and stability \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. Hydrothermal-synthesized hierarchical zeolites, with higher silanol concentration, provide superior catalytic lifetime \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. Enhanced metal-support interactions lead to higher dispersion and electron density over metal sites, further improving PDH performance \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\].
    
    Synthesis methods for hierarchical zeolites include in situ approaches, which fabricate microporous and mesoporous products with or without secondary hard or soft templates, and postsynthetic approaches, which modify as-synthesized zeolites to introduce a hierarchical structure \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. The hard templating route uses sacrificial templates to create additional porosity, while the soft templating route employs polymers and surfactants to tailor mesopores \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. The indirect templating route involves partial zeolite crystallization or deposition onto a mesoporous skeleton \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. Hierarchical zeolites improve PDH reaction performance by maximizing the utilization of zeolite materials, minimizing energy, time, and raw material consumption \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\].
    
    A novel composite material, TS-1@SBA-16, combines TS-1 zeolite with mesoporous SBA-16 silica, enhancing accessibility for PDH reactions \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]. The 10% TS-1 content catalyst showed peak PDH activity, achieving higher propane conversion than TS-1 or SBA-16-supported catalysts \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]. Its superior performance is attributed to the synergistic effect of the hierarchical porous structure, suitable acid acidity, and Ti species' electronic effect \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\].
    
    ※※※
    
    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in hierarchical zeolites for PDH reactions have opened up new avenues for improving catalytic efficiency and selectivity. The synergistic effects of hierarchical porous structures, suitable acid acidity, and electronic effects of metal species have been pivotal in enhancing PDH performance. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel synthesis methods that can further tailor the hierarchical structure to optimize the balance between micropore confinement and mesopore/macropore accessibility. Additionally, exploring the use of advanced characterization techniques to gain deeper insights into the metal-support interactions and the role of silanol concentration in hydrothermal-synthesized hierarchical zeolites could lead to the design of more efficient and stable catalysts. The integration of computational modeling with experimental studies could also provide a comprehensive understanding of the structure-performance relationships in hierarchical zeolites, guiding the rational design of next-generation PDH catalysts.

<Chinese>
    层状沸石通过结合微孔和介孔/大孔，显著提高了丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中的可及性和金属分散度\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。这些材料，以其增强的结构为特征，与传统沸石相比，在涉及大分子的反应中提供了优越的催化效率。合成方法通常涉及结构导向剂的使用和高温结晶过程\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。层状沸石中固有微孔与介孔/大孔网络的结合，在不牺牲限制效应的情况下，提高了PDH反应的微孔可及性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。这些沸石通过脱硅、自组装、双功能表面活性剂和TPAOH、TBPOH等共模板剂合成，结果是更高的表面积、较弱的酸性和改善的稳定性，从而提高了丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。层状结构促进了金属间化合物的形成，增强了PDH性能和稳定性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。水热合成的层状沸石，具有更高的硅醇浓度，提供了优越的催化寿命\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。增强的金属-载体相互作用导致金属位点上更高的分散度和电子密度，进一步提高了PDH性能\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。
    
    层状沸石的合成方法包括原位方法，该方法使用或不使用二次硬模板或软模板制造微孔和介孔产品，以及后合成方法，该方法修改现成的沸石以引入层状结构\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。硬模板路线使用牺牲模板来创建额外的孔隙度，而软模板路线使用聚合物和表面活性剂来定制介孔\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。间接模板路线涉及部分沸石结晶或沉积在介孔骨架上\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。层状沸石通过最大化沸石材料的利用，最小化能源、时间和原材料消耗，提高了PDH反应性能\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。
    
    一种新型复合材料TS-1@SBA-16，将TS-1沸石与介孔SBA-16二氧化硅结合，提高了PDH反应的可及性\[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]。10% TS-1含量的催化剂显示出峰值PDH活性，实现了比TS-1或SBA-16支撑的催化剂更高的丙烷转化率\[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]。其优越性能归因于层状多孔结构、合适的酸酸度和Ti物种电子效应的协同作用\[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]。
    
    ※※※
    
    综合理解与未来展望: 层状沸石在PDH反应中的进展为提高催化效率和选择性开辟了新的途径。层状多孔结构、合适的酸酸度和金属物种电子效应的协同作用对增强PDH性能至关重要。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发新的合成方法，进一步定制层状结构，以优化微孔限制和介孔/大孔可及性之间的平衡。此外，探索使用先进的表征技术，以深入了解金属-载体相互作用和水热合成层状沸石中硅醇浓度的作用，可能导致设计更高效和稳定的催化剂。将计算建模与实验研究相结合，也可能提供对层状沸石结构-性能关系的全面理解，指导下一代PDH催化剂的合理设计。
