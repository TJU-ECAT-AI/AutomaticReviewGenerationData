Hierarchical zeolites, by integrating micropores with mesopores/macropores, significantly enhance the accessibility and efficiency of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions \[10.3390/catal9020174\]. This structural amalgamation facilitates improved metal species dispersion through increased defect sites, thereby boosting catalytic efficiency, especially in reactions involving bulky molecules \[10.3390/catal9020174\]. The synthesis of these materials typically involves the use of structure directing agents and high-temperature crystallization processes \[10.3390/catal9020174\].

The enhanced accessibility of hierarchical zeolites for PDH reactions is achieved without compromising the confinement effects that are crucial for catalytic activity \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. Synthesized through methods such as desilication, self-assembly, and the use of bifunctional surfactants or cotemplates like TPAOH and TBPOH, these zeolites offer higher surface areas, weaker acidity, and improved stability \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. The hierarchical structure facilitates the formation of intermetallic compounds, boosts silanol concentration, and strengthens metal-support interactions, leading to higher propane conversion, propylene selectivity, and overall PDH performance \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\].

Synthesis methods for hierarchical zeolites include in situ and postsynthetic approaches, involving hard and soft templating routes \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. Hard templating employs sacrificial templates to create additional porosity, while soft templating uses polymers and surfactants to tailor mesopores \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. Indirect templating synthesizes hierarchical zeolites by partial crystallization or deposition onto mesoporous skeletons \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. These materials improve PDH reaction performance through enhanced mass transport properties, higher activity, selectivity, and cycle time \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\].

A composite material, TS-1@SBA-16, combining micropores and mesopores for enhanced PDH reaction accessibility, was synthesized by enwrapping TS-1 zeolite with mesoporous SBA-16 silica \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]. The 10% TS-1 content catalyst showed the highest PDH activity, achieving 54.5% initial propane conversion, outperforming TS-1 or SBA-16-supported catalysts \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]. The superior performance of Pt-SnOx/TS-1@SBA-16 is attributed to its hierarchical porous structure, suitable acid acidity, and the electronic effect of Ti species \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\].
