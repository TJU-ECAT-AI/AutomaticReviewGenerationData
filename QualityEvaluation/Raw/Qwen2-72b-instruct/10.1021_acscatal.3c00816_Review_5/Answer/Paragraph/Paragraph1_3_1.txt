<English>
Hierarchical zeolites, by virtue of their integrated micropore and meso/macropore structures, significantly enhance the accessibility and efficiency of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions \[10.3390/catal9020174\]. These materials, characterized by their hierarchical architectures, outperform conventional zeolites in reactions involving bulky molecules, primarily due to improved metal dispersion and catalytic efficiency \[10.3390/catal9020174\]. The synthesis of hierarchical zeolites often employs structure directing agents and high-temperature crystallization processes, which are critical for the creation of desired pore structures \[10.3390/catal9020174\].

The integration of micropores with meso/macropores in hierarchical zeolites facilitates enhanced accessibility without compromising the confinement effects that are beneficial for PDH reactions \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. Synthesis methods, including desilication, self-assembly, the use of bifunctional surfactants, and cotemplating with TPAOH and TBPOH, result in materials with higher surface areas, weaker acidity, and improved stability \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. These characteristics contribute to higher propane conversion and propylene selectivity, as well as the formation of intermetallic compounds, which are crucial for enhanced PDH performance and stability \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\].

Hierarchical zeolites, by merging micropores with meso- and/or macropores, offer unimpeded transport paths and improved micropore accessibility, without sacrificing intrinsic catalytic activity \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. Synthesis approaches include in situ methods, which create microporous and mesoporous products with or without secondary hard or soft templates, and postsynthetic methods, which introduce a hierarchical structure post-treatment \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. These materials improve PDH reaction performance through enhanced mass transport properties, increased activity and selectivity, and reduced cycle time \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\].

TS-1@SBA-16, a composite material combining TS-1 zeolite with mesoporous SBA-16 silica, was synthesized to enhance PDH reaction accessibility \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]. This hierarchical structure, featuring micropores and mesopores, led to a catalyst with 10% TS-1 content showing the highest PDH activity, with notably higher initial propane conversion rates than over TS-1 or SBA-16-supported catalysts \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]. The superior catalytic performance is attributed to the synergistic effect of the hierarchical porous structure, suitable acid acidity, and the electronic effect of Ti species \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in hierarchical zeolite synthesis and their applications in PDH reactions have opened up new avenues for catalytic efficiency and selectivity. Future research should focus on the development of novel synthesis methods that can precisely control the hierarchical pore structure, aiming to optimize the balance between micropore confinement effects and meso/macropore accessibility. Additionally, the exploration of dual-function templates, such as multivalent surfactants, could offer a pathway to synthesize hierarchical zeolites with controlled mesoporosity and crystallinity, potentially leading to enhanced catalytic performance in PDH and other reactions. The integration of computational modeling with experimental studies could also provide deeper insights into the structure-performance relationships of hierarchical zeolites, guiding the design of next-generation catalysts for PDH and related processes.

<Chinese>
层次状沸石，通过其集成的微孔和中/大孔结构，显著提高了丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应的可及性和效率\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。这些材料，以其层次结构为特征，在涉及大分子的反应中，由于金属分散和催化效率的提高，超越了传统沸石\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。层次状沸石的合成通常采用结构导向剂和高温结晶过程，对创建所需的孔结构至关重要\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。

微孔与中/大孔在层次状沸石中的结合，在不牺牲对PDH反应有益的限制效应的情况下，促进了增强的可及性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。合成方法，包括脱硅、自组装、使用双功能表面活性剂以及与TPAOH和TBPOH的共模板，产生具有更高表面积、较弱酸性和改进稳定性的材料\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。这些特性有助于提高丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性，以及间金属化合物的形成，这对增强PDH性能和稳定性至关重要\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。

层次状沸石，通过合并微孔与中/大孔，提供了无障碍的传输路径和改进的微孔可及性，而不牺牲内在的催化活性\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。合成方法包括原位方法，创建微孔和中孔产品，有或没有二次硬或软模板，以及后合成方法，后处理引入层次结构\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。这些材料通过增强的质量传输特性、增加的活性和选择性以及减少的循环时间，改善了PDH反应性能\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。

TS-1@SBA-16，一种结合TS-1沸石与中孔SBA-16二氧化硅的复合材料，被合成以增强PDH反应的可及性\[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]。这种层次结构，具有微孔和中孔，导致10% TS-1含量的催化剂显示出最高的PDH活性，与TS-1或SBA-16支持的催化剂相比，初始丙烷转化率显著提高\[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]。优越的催化性能归因于层次多孔结构、合适的酸酸度和Ti物种的电子效应的协同作用\[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望: 层次状沸石合成及其在PDH反应中的应用的进展为催化效率和选择性开辟了新的途径。未来的研究应集中在开发新型合成方法上，这些方法可以精确控制层次孔结构，旨在优化微孔限制效应与中/大孔可及性之间的平衡。此外，双功能模板（如多价表面活性剂）的探索可能为合成具有受控中孔性和结晶度的层次状沸石提供一条途径，这可能带来PDH和其他反应中增强的催化性能。将计算建模与实验研究相结合，也可能为层次状沸石的结构-性能关系提供更深入的见解，指导下一代PDH及相关过程催化剂的设计。
