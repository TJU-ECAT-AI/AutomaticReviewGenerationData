        Hierarchical zeolites, by merging micropores with meso/macropores, significantly enhance the accessibility and efficiency of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions \[10.3390/catal9020174\]. This structural amalgamation not only improves metal dispersion and reactant accessibility but also prevents metal aggregation, thereby boosting catalytic efficiency compared to traditional zeolites \[10.3390/catal9020174\]. The synthesis of these materials involves the use of structure directing agents and high-temperature crystallization processes \[10.3390/catal9020174\]. 

        The integration of micropores with meso/macropores in hierarchical zeolites enhances PDH reactions without compromising the confinement effects that are crucial for catalytic activity \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. Synthesis methods include desilication, self-assembly, and the use of bifunctional surfactants, as well as cotemplating with tetrapropylammonium hydroxide (TPAOH) and tetrabutylphosphonium hydroxide (TBPOH) \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. These materials offer higher surface areas, weaker acidity, and improved stability, which collectively boost propane conversion and propylene selectivity \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. The hierarchical structure facilitates the formation of intermetallic compounds, contributing to enhanced PDH performance and stability \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. Hydrothermal-synthesized hierarchical zeolites have higher silanol concentrations, leading to superior catalytic lifetime \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. Enhanced metal-support interactions in hierarchical S-1 zeolites result in higher dispersion and electron density over metal sites, improving PDH performance, as evidenced by higher turnover frequencies (TOF) values \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\].

        The synthesis of hierarchical zeolites can be achieved through in situ fabrication of microporous and mesoporous products with or without secondary hard or soft templates, and postsynthetic modification of as-synthesized zeolites to introduce a hierarchical structure \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. The hard templating route uses sacrificial templates to create additional porosity, while the soft templating route employs silylated polymers, hydrophilic cationic polymers, and surfactants to generate mesopores \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. The indirect templating route involves partial zeolite crystallization of a mesoporous material or deposition onto a mesoporous skeleton \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. These synthesis methods significantly improve the utilization effectiveness of zeolite materials, minimizing energy, time, and raw materials consumption \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\].

        A novel composite material, TS-1@SBA-16, combines TS-1 zeolite crystals with mesoporous SBA-16 silica to enhance PDH reaction accessibility \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]. The 10% TS-1 content catalyst achieved a 54.5% initial propane conversion, outperforming TS-1 or SBA-16-supported catalysts \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]. Pt-SnOx/TS-1@SBA-16's superior catalytic performance is attributed to its hierarchical porous structure, suitable acid acidity, and Ti species' electronic effect \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\].

        ※※※
        Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in hierarchical zeolite synthesis and their application in PDH reactions have opened up new avenues for catalytic efficiency and selectivity. The future of PDH catalyst development might focus on the optimization of synthesis methods to achieve more precise control over pore size and distribution, aiming to further enhance metal dispersion and accessibility. Additionally, the exploration of novel structure directing agents and templating strategies could lead to the creation of hierarchical zeolites with tailored properties for specific catalytic applications. The integration of computational methods with experimental approaches could also provide deeper insights into the structure-performance relationships of hierarchical zeolites, guiding the design of next-generation PDH catalysts.

<Chinese>
        层状沸石通过将微孔与介孔/大孔结合，显著提高了丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应的可及性和效率\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。这种结构融合不仅提高了金属分散度和反应物的可及性，还防止了金属聚集，从而与传统沸石相比，提高了催化效率\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。这些材料的合成涉及结构导向剂的使用和高温结晶过程\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。

        层状沸石通过整合微孔与介孔/大孔，增强了PDH反应，同时没有损害对催化活性至关重要的限制效应\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。合成方法包括脱硅、自组装和使用双功能表面活性剂，以及与四丙基铵氢氧化物（TPAOH）和四丁基膦氢氧化物（TBPOH）的共模板\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。这些材料提供了更高的表面积、较弱的酸性和改进的稳定性，这些共同提高了丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。层状结构促进了金属间化合物的形成，对增强PDH性能和稳定性有贡献\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。水热合成的层状沸石具有更高的硅醇浓度，导致更优的催化寿命\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。层状S-1沸石中增强的金属-载体相互作用导致金属位点上更高的分散度和电子密度，提高了PDH性能，如更高的周转频率（TOF）值所示\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。

        层状沸石的合成可以通过原位制备微孔和介孔产品实现，有或没有二级硬或软模板，以及对合成沸石进行后合成改性以引入层状结构\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。硬模板路线使用牺牲模板来创造额外的孔隙度，而软模板路线使用硅化聚合物、亲水性阳离子聚合物和表面活性剂来生成介孔\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。间接模板路线涉及介孔材料的部分沸石结晶或沉积到介孔骨架上\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。这些合成方法显著提高了沸石材料的利用效率，减少了能源、时间和原材料的消耗\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。

        一种新型复合材料，TS-1@SBA-16，通过将TS-1沸石晶体与介孔SBA-16二氧化硅结合，增强了PDH反应的可及性\[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]。10% TS-1含量的催化剂实现了54.5%的初始丙烷转化率，超过了TS-1或SBA-16支持的催化剂\[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]。Pt-SnOx/TS-1@SBA-16的卓越催化性能归因于其层状多孔结构、适宜的酸酸度和Ti物种的电子效应\[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]。

        ※※※
        综合理解与未来展望: 层状沸石合成的进步及其在PDH反应中的应用为催化效率和选择性开辟了新的途径。PDH催化剂开发的未来可能集中在优化合成方法，以实现对孔径和分布的更精确控制，旨在进一步提高金属分散度和可及性。此外，探索新型结构导向剂和模板策略可能导向具有定制属性的层状沸石的创建，适用于特定的催化应用。将计算方法与实验方法的整合也可能为层状沸石的结构-性能关系提供更深入的见解，指导下一代PDH催化剂的设计。
