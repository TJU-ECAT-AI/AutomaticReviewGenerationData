        Hierarchical zeolites, by integrating mesopores/macropores with micropores, significantly enhance the accessibility and efficiency of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions \[10.3390/catal9020174\]. These materials, characterized by their hierarchical structures, outperform conventional zeolites in reactions involving bulky molecules due to improved metal dispersion and catalytic efficiency \[10.3390/catal9020174\]. The synthesis methods typically involve the use of structure directing agents and high-temperature crystallization processes \[10.3390/catal9020174\]. This design boosts accessibility, reduces metal aggregation, and increases the dispersion of active metal species, which is crucial for enhancing the performance of PDH reactions \[10.3390/catal9020174\].

        The main types of hierarchical zeolites for PDH reactions include mesoporous zeolites synthesized via desilication/self-assembly, 2D/layered zeolites created using bifunctional surfactants, and pillared zeolites utilizing TPAOH/TBPOH cotemplates \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. These materials boost PDH performance by offering higher surface areas, weaker acidity, and improved stability, achieving higher propane conversion and propylene selectivity \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. The enhanced PDH performance and stability are attributed to the formation of intermetallic compounds and higher silanol concentration in hydrothermal-synthesized hierarchical zeolites \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. Enhanced metal-support interactions lead to higher dispersion and electron density over metal sites, improving PDH performance \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\].

        Synthesis methods for hierarchical zeolites include in situ and postsynthetic approaches \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. In situ fabrication involves the use of hard or soft templates during synthesis, while postsynthetic methods involve post-treatment of as-synthesized zeolites \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. These materials improve PDH reaction performance by enhancing mass transport properties, activity, selectivity, and cycle time \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. The additional meso- and/or macroporous system significantly improves the utilization effectiveness of zeolite materials, resulting in minimum energy, time, and raw materials consumption \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\].

        A composite material, TS-1@SBA-16, combining TS-1 zeolite with mesoporous SBA-16 silica, was synthesized to enhance PDH reaction accessibility \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]. The 10% TS-1 content catalyst showed peak PDH activity, achieving 54.5% initial propane conversion, outperforming TS-1 or SBA-16-supported catalysts \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]. The superior performance of Pt-SnOx/TS-1@SBA-16 is attributed to its hierarchical porous structure, suitable acid acidity, and Ti species' electronic effect \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\].

        ※※※
        Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Hierarchical zeolites, with their unique combination of micropores and meso-/macropores, have revolutionized the PDH reaction landscape by enhancing accessibility and catalytic efficiency. The synthesis methods, including in situ and postsynthetic approaches, have opened up new avenues for tailoring zeolite structures to meet specific catalytic demands. The future of PDH catalyst development could focus on the optimization of synthesis conditions to achieve more precise control over the hierarchical structure, aiming for enhanced stability and selectivity. Moreover, the exploration of novel structure directing agents and the development of advanced templating strategies could lead to the creation of hierarchical zeolites with unprecedented properties, potentially unlocking new catalytic applications beyond PDH. The integration of computational methods to predict and design hierarchical zeolites with tailored properties could also be a promising direction for future research.

        层状沸石通过将介孔/大孔与微孔结合，显著提高了丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应的可及性和效率\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。这些材料，以其层次结构为特征，在涉及大分子的反应中，由于金属分散和催化效率的提高，优于传统沸石\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。合成方法通常涉及结构导向剂的使用和高温结晶过程\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。这种设计提高了可及性，减少了金属聚集，并增加了活性金属物种的分散，这对提高PDH反应的性能至关重要\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。

        PDH反应的主要类型层状沸石包括通过脱硅/自组装合成的介孔沸石，使用双功能表面活性剂创建的二维/层状沸石，以及使用TPAOH/TBPOH共模板的柱状沸石\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。这些材料通过提供更高的表面积、较弱的酸性和改进的稳定性，提高了PDH性能，实现了更高的丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。增强的PDH性能和稳定性归因于在水热合成的层次沸石中形成的金属间化合物和更高的硅醇浓度\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。增强的金属-支撑相互作用导致金属位点上更高的分散和电子密度，提高了PDH性能\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。

        层状沸石的合成方法包括原位和后合成方法\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。原位制造涉及在合成过程中使用硬或软模板，而后合成方法涉及对合成沸石的后处理\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。这些材料通过增强质量传输特性、活性、选择性和循环时间，提高了PDH反应性能\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。额外的介孔和/或大孔系统显著提高了沸石材料的利用效率，导致能源、时间和原材料消耗最小化\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。

        一种复合材料，TS-1@SBA-16，结合了TS-1沸石和介孔SBA-16二氧化硅，被合成以增强PDH反应的可及性\[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]。10% TS-1含量的催化剂显示了峰值PDH活性，实现了54.5%的初始丙烷转化率，优于TS-1或SBA-16支持的催化剂\[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]。Pt-SnOx/TS-1@SBA-16的优越性能归因于其层次多孔结构、合适的酸酸度和Ti物种的电子效应\[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]。

        ※※※
        综合理解与未来展望: 层状沸石，以其独特的微孔和介/大孔的结合，通过提高可及性和催化效率，彻底改变了PDH反应领域。包括原位和后合成方法在内的合成方法为定制沸石结构以满足特定的催化需求开辟了新的途径。PDH催化剂开发的未来可能集中在优化合成条件，以实现对层次结构的更精确控制，目标是增强稳定性和选择性。此外，新型结构导向剂的探索和先进模板策略的发展，可能会导致具有前所未有的特性的层次沸石的创建，可能解锁超出PDH的新催化应用。将计算方法整合到预测和设计具有定制特性的层次沸石中，也可能成为未来研究的有希望的方向。
