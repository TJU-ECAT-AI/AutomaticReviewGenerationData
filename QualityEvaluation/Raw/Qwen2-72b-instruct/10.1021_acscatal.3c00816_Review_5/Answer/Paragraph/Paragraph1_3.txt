Hierarchical zeolites, characterized by the integration of mesopores/macropores with micropores, significantly enhance the accessibility and catalytic efficiency in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions \[10.3390/catal9020174\]. This unique structure prevents metal aggregation, boosts the dispersion of active metal species, and improves the selectivity to propylene \[10.3390/catal9020174\]. Synthesis methods often involve the use of structure directing agents and high-temperature crystallization processes \[10.3390/catal9020174\]. These materials outperform conventional zeolites in reactions involving bulky molecules, as evidenced by their superior catalytic efficiency in PDH \[10.3390/catal9020174\].

The integration of intrinsic micropores with mesoporous/macroporous networks in hierarchical zeolites enhances micropore accessibility for PDH reactions without compromising confinement effects \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. Synthesized via desilication, self-assembly, bifunctional surfactants, or cotemplates, these materials offer higher surface areas, weaker acidity, and improved stability for PDH reactions \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. The hierarchical structure facilitates the formation of intermetallic compounds, contributing to higher propane conversion and propylene selectivity \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. Hydrothermal-synthesized hierarchical zeolites have higher silanol concentrations, leading to superior catalytic lifetime \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]. Enhanced metal-support interactions increase dispersion and electron density over metal sites, improving PDH performance \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\].

The synthesis of hierarchical zeolites can be achieved through in situ and postsynthetic approaches \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. In situ methods involve the fabrication of microporous and mesoporous products with or without secondary hard or soft templates, while postsynthetic methods concern the post-treatment of as-synthesized zeolites \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. Hard templating uses sacrificial templates to create additional porosity, while soft templating employs polymers and surfactants to tailor mesopores \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. Indirect templating synthesizes hierarchical zeolites by partial crystallization of mesoporous materials or deposition onto mesoporous skeletons \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. These materials improve PDH reaction performance by enhancing mass transport properties, increasing activity and selectivity, and reducing cycle time \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\].

A notable example is the TS-1@SBA-16 composite material, synthesized by enwrapping TS-1 zeolite crystals with mesoporous SBA-16 silica \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]. This hierarchical structure, featuring micropores and mesopores, led to a catalyst with 10% TS-1 content showing the highest PDH activity \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]. The initial propane conversion was notably higher than over TS-1 or SBA-16-supported catalysts \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]. The superior catalytic performance is attributed to the synergistic effect of the hierarchical porous structure, suitable acid acidity, and electronic effect of Ti species \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in hierarchical zeolites for PDH reactions, as detailed in the file, open up new avenues for exploring emerging fields and innovative methodologies. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of dual-function templates, such as multivalent surfactants, to effectively eliminate phase separation and allow for the synthesis of hierarchical zeolites with controlled mesoporosity and crystallinity \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. Additionally, the optimization of synthesis methods, including hard and soft templating, could lead to the creation of hierarchical zeolites with tailored properties for enhanced PDH performance \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. The exploration of novel hierarchical structures, such as those with micro-mesoporous, micro-macroporous, and micro-meso-macroporous configurations, could offer significant advantages over traditional zeolites in catalytic reactions \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]. Moreover, the investigation of the synergistic effects between hierarchical porous structures, acid acidity, and electronic effects of metal species could provide insights into the design of more efficient PDH catalysts \[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\].

层次状沸石,通过将介孔/大孔与微孔结合,显著提高了丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应的可及性和催化效率\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。这种独特的结构防止了金属聚集,提高了活性金属物种的分散,并改善了对丙烯的选择性\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。合成方法通常涉及结构导向剂的使用和高温结晶过程\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。这些材料在涉及大分子的反应中优于传统沸石,如PDH反应中表现出的优越催化效率所证明\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。

层次状沸石通过将内在微孔与介孔/大孔网络结合,在不牺牲限域效应的情况下增强了PDH反应的微孔可及性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。通过脱硅、自组装、双功能表面活性剂或共模板剂合成,这些材料提供了更高的表面积、较弱的酸性和PDH反应的改进稳定性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。层次结构促进了金属间化合物的形成,有助于提高丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。水热合成的层次状沸石具有更高的硅醇浓度,导致更优越的催化寿命\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。增强的金属-载体相互作用增加了金属位点上的分散和电子密度,改善了PDH性能\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5\]。

层次状沸石的合成可以通过原位和后合成方法实现\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。原位方法涉及使用或不使用二次硬模板或软模板的微孔和介孔产品的制造,而后合成方法涉及对合成沸石的后处理\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。硬模板使用牺牲模板来创建额外的孔隙度,而软模板使用聚合物和表面活性剂来定制介孔\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。间接模板通过介孔材料的部分结晶或在介孔骨架上的沉积来合成层次状沸石\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。这些材料通过增强质量传递特性、提高活性和选择性以及减少循环时间来改善PDH反应性能\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016\]。

一个值得注意的例子是TS-1@SBA-16复合材料,通过将TS-1沸石晶体与介孔SBA-16二氧化硅包裹合成\[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]。这种具有微孔和介孔的层次结构导致了10% TS-1含量的催化剂显示出最高的PDH活性\[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]。初始丙烷转化率明显高于TS-1或SBA-16支持的催化剂\[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]。优越的催化性能归因于层次多孔结构、合适的酸酸度和Ti物种的电子效应的协同效应\[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7\]。
