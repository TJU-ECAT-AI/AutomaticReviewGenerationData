The catalytic hydrogenation of C═O bonds, particularly using bimetallic catalysts such as Pt-Ni systems, is pivotal in converting biomass derivatives into renewable and sustainable fuels and chemicals \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]. Bimetallic catalysts offer unique properties, enhancing hydrogenation, dehydrogenation, and reforming reactions. Studies reveal that bimetallic structures significantly impact catalytic performance, with Pt/Ni catalysts showing higher hydrogenation activity than monometallic counterparts \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]. The enhanced activity is attributed to Pt-terminated bimetallic structures, where Ni atoms diffuse beneath the surface while Pt segregates to the top, a process supported by density functional theory (DFT) calculations \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]. This interplay between theoretical predictions and experimental findings optimizes the selectivity and efficiency of C═O bond hydrogenation in industrial and research applications \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\].

Selective hydrogenation of α,β-unsaturated aldehydes to unsaturated alcohols, facilitated by the synergistic effects of bimetallic catalysts, is of growing interest \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.10.003\]. Optimization and understanding of this process employ methodologies such as temperature-programmed desorption (TPD), high-resolution electron energy loss spectroscopy (HREELS), and DFT modeling, which help in comprehending hydrogenation pathways, binding energy, and adsorption geometry on bimetallic surfaces \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.10.003\]. These methodologies are vital for understanding hydrogenation pathways and the activation of functional groups, leading to more efficient catalysts \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.10.003\].

Bimetallic catalysts, like Pt-Ni systems, significantly enhance hydrogenation activities, offering improved selectivity and activity in processes such as maleic anhydride hydrogenation, crucial for chemical, pharmaceutical, and food industries \[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]. The addition of a small amount of Pt to Ni/Al2O3 boosts hydrogenation efficiency without compromising selectivity \[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]. Supported PtNi catalysts exhibit superior activity in maleic anhydride hydrogenation \[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]. Optimization strategies include X-ray diffraction (XRD) and H2 temperature-programmed reduction (TPR) for catalyst characterization and flow reactor studies for activity evaluation, with hydrogen dissociation on nickel controlling the process \[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\].

The catalytic hydrogenation of C=O bonds using bimetallic Pt-Ni catalysts is significant in fine chemical synthesis, exemplified by the 100% conversion of maleic acid to succinic acid at 40°C \[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\]. HYAl2O3-supported PtNi catalysts outperform monometallic and other bimetallic catalysts, highlighting the role of bimetallic formation \[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\]. Zeolites are excellent supports for succinic anhydride production from maleic anhydride hydrogenation, linking to cellulosic biomass conversion \[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\]. Optimization strategies include the preparation of supported Ni and NiPt catalysts, investigation of support and reaction condition effects, and catalyst characterization using X-ray diffraction and temperature-programmed reduction \[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\].

Bimetallic catalysts, like NiPt systems, are pivotal in C═O bond hydrogenation, enhancing industrial and research applications like fine chemical synthesis and cellulosic biomass conversion \[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]. Their unique properties offer superior performance in hydrogenation reactions, particularly in the selective hydrogenation of CO bonds in α,β-unsaturated aldehydes \[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]. NiPt catalysts exhibit significantly higher CO hydrogenation activity than monometallic counterparts, as demonstrated in studies using Fourier transform infrared spectroscopy (FTIR) and gas chromatography (GC) \[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]. This enhanced activity suggests a resemblance to Pt-terminated configurations predicted by theoretical calculations, establishing a direct link between model surfaces and supported catalysts, which is crucial for designing efficient hydrogenation bimetallic catalysts \[10.1039/c1cy00066g\].

催化C═O键氢化，特别是使用Pt-Ni等双金属催化剂，对于将生物质衍生物转化为可再生和可持续的燃料和化学品至关重要\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]。双金属催化剂提供了独特的性质，增强了氢化、脱氢和重整反应。研究显示，特定的双金属结构，如Pt/Ni催化剂，显著提高了催化性能，与单金属催化剂相比，氢化活性更高\[10.16/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]。增强的活性归因于Pt终止的双金属结构，其中Ni原子扩散到表面以下，而Pt则分层到顶部，这一过程得到了密度泛函理论(DFT)计算的支持\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]。理论预测与实验发现之间的这种相互作用优化了工业和研究应用中C═O键氢化的选择性和效率\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]。

α,β-不饱和醛选择性氢化为不饱和醇，得益于双金属催化剂的协同效应，越来越受到关注\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.10.003\]。优化和理解这一过程的方法包括温度程序脱附(TPD)、高分辨率电子能量损失光谱(HREELS)和DFT建模，这些方法有助于理解双金属表面上的氢化途径、结合能和吸附几何\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.10.003\]。这些方法对于理解氢化途径和功能基团的激活至关重要，从而导致更有效的催化剂\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.10.003\]。

像Pt-Ni系统这样的双金属催化剂显著增强了氢化活性，为化学、制药和食品工业中诸如马来酸酐氢化等过程提供了改进的选择性和活性\[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]。向Ni/Al2O3中添加少量Pt可以提高氢化效率，而不牺牲选择性\[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]。支持的PtNi催化剂在马来酸酐氢化中表现出更高的活性\[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]。优化策略包括X射线衍射(XRD)和H2温度程序还原(TPR)进行催化剂表征，以及流动反应器研究进行活性评估，其中氢在镍上的解离控制着过程\[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]。

使用双金属Pt-Ni催化剂催化C=O键的氢化在精细化学合成中具有重要意义，例如在40°C下将马来酸100%转化为琥珀酸\[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\]。HYAl2O3支持的PtNi催化剂优于单金属和其他双金属催化剂，突出了双金属形成的作用\[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\]。沸石作为马来酸酐氢化生产琥珀酸酐的优良载体，与纤维素生物质转化相关\[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\]。优化策略包括制备支持的Ni和NiPt催化剂，研究载体和反应条件对活性、选择性和稳定性的影响，以及使用X射线衍射和温度程序还原进行催化剂表征\[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\]。

像NiPt系统这样的双金属催化剂在C═O键氢化中至关重要，增强了精细化学合成和纤维素生物质转化等工业和研究应用\[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]。它们独特的性质在α,β-不饱和醛的CO键选择性氢化中提供了优越的性能\[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]。NiPt催化剂的CO氢化活性明显高于单金属催化剂，这在使用傅里叶变换红外光谱(FTIR)和气相色谱(GC)的研究中得到了证明\[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]。这种增强的活性表明与理论计算中预测的Pt终止配置相似，建立了模型表面与支持催化剂之间的直接联系，这对于设计高效的氢化双金属催化剂至关重要\[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]。
