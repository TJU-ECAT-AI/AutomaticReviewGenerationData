                    The catalytic hydrogenation of C═O bonds, particularly using bimetallic catalysts such as Pt-Ni systems, is pivotal in industrial and research applications, notably in converting biomass derivatives into renewable, sustainable, and environmentally friendly fuels and chemicals \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]. This process is crucial for the selective hydrogenation of CO bonds in α,β-unsaturated aldehydes for fine chemical production and as an initial step in cellulosic biomass conversion \[10.1002/cctc.201000268\]. Bimetallic catalysts offer unique properties, enhancing hydrogenation, dehydrogenation, and reforming reactions \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]. Studies reveal that bimetallic structures significantly impact catalytic performance, with Pt/Ni catalysts showing superior hydrogenation activity compared to monometallic counterparts \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]. Theoretical calculations and experimental evidence suggest that Pt atoms segregate to the surface while Ni atoms diffuse beneath, optimizing hydrogenation pathways \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]. This interplay between theoretical predictions and experimental findings is crucial for optimizing bimetallic catalysts in biomass conversion processes \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\].

                    Optimization strategies include DFT calculations for binding energies, UHV studies for low-temperature hydrogenation, and reactor evaluations \[10.1021/cr300096b_9\]. These methodologies have proven effective in predicting bimetallic catalysts with enhanced CO hydrogenation activity \[10.1021/cr300096b_9\]. The process has been applied to various compounds, including propanal, acetaldehyde, acetone, acrolein, maleic anhydride, acetic acid, and N-methylpyrrolidin-2-one, demonstrating its versatility and industrial significance \[10.1021/cr300096b_9\]. Supported PtNi catalysts show the highest activity in maleic anhydride hydrogenation, a key intermediate for various industries \[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]. Methodologies for studying and optimizing the process include XRD and H2 TPR for catalyst characterization, and flow reactor studies for catalytic activity evaluation \[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]. The process is controlled by H2 dissociation on nickel, which can be improved by adding a small amount of Pt without losing selectivity \[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\].

                    催化氢化C═O键，特别是使用Pt-Ni等双金属催化剂，是工业和研究应用中的关键环节，特别是在将生物质衍生物转化为可再生、可持续和环境友好的燃料和化学品方面\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]。这一过程对于α,β-不饱和醛中CO键的精细化学品生产中的选择性氢化以及作为纤维素生物质转化的初始步骤至关重要\[10.1002/cctc.201000268\]。双金属催化剂提供了独特的性质，增强了氢化、脱氢和重整反应\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]。研究表明，双金属结构对催化性能有显著影响，Pt/Ni催化剂的氢化活性优于单金属催化剂\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]。理论计算和实验证据表明，Pt原子向表面迁移，而Ni原子向内部扩散，优化了氢化途径\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]。理论预测与实验发现之间的这种相互作用对于优化生物质转化过程中的双金属催化剂至关重要\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]。

                    优化策略包括DFT计算结合能、UHV研究低温氢化和反应器评估\[10.1021/cr300096b_9\]。这些方法已被证明能有效预测具有增强CO氢化活性的双金属催化剂\[10.1021/cr300096b_9\]。这一过程已应用于多种化合物，包括丙醛、乙醛、丙酮、丙烯醛、马来酸酐、乙酸和N-甲基吡咯烷-2-酮，展示了其多样性和工业重要性\[10.1021/cr300096b_9\]。PtNi催化剂在马来酸酐氢化中表现出最高活性，是各种行业的关键中间体\[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]。研究和优化过程的方法包括XRD和H2 TPR进行催化剂表征，以及流动反应器研究进行催化活性评估\[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]。该过程受镍上H2解离的控制，通过添加少量Pt可以提高活性，而不会牺牲选择性\[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]。
