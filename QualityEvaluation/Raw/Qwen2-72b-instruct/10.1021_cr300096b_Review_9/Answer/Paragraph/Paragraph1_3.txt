                    The catalytic hydrogenation of C═O bonds, particularly using bimetallic catalysts like Pt-Ni systems, is pivotal in industrial and research applications, notably in fine chemical synthesis and cellulosic biomass conversion \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]. These catalysts offer unique properties, enhancing hydrogenation, dehydrogenation, and reforming reactions. Studies reveal that bimetallic structures significantly impact catalytic performance, with Pt/Ni catalysts showing higher hydrogenation activity than monometallic counterparts \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]. Theoretical and experimental research on well-defined surfaces have demonstrated that specific bimetallic configurations, such as Pt-terminated structures, are more active for hydrogenation and dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]. Moreover, subsurface structures like PtNiPt(1 1 1) enable novel low-temperature hydrogenation pathways \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]. These findings align with density functional theory (DFT) predictions, indicating that Pt atoms segregate to the surface while Ni atoms diffuse beneath, thermodynamically favored in hydrogen environments and vacuum \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]. The catalytic hydrogenation of C=O bonds using bimetallic PtNi catalysts is significant for fine chemical synthesis and cellulosic biomass conversion \[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\]. PtNi catalysts, particularly supported on HYAl2O3, efficiently convert maleic acid to succinic acid at 100% conversion at 40°C, demonstrating their effectiveness \[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\]. The interplay between theoretical calculations and experimental studies enhances the selectivity and efficiency of this process, with methodologies including catalyst preparation, investigation of support and reaction condition effects, and catalyst characterization using X-ray diffraction and temperature-programmed reduction \[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\]. Bimetallic catalysts, notably NiPt systems, are pivotal in C═O bond hydrogenation, enhancing industrial and research applications \[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]. Their unique properties make them effective for organic synthesis, specifically in the hydrogenation of propanal to 1-propanol \[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]. This process is crucial for selectively hydrogenating CO bonds in α,β-unsaturated aldehydes, a key step in cellulosic biomass conversion \[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]. Studies reveal that NiPt/γ-Al2O3 catalysts significantly outperform monometallic counterparts, suggesting a structure akin to theoretical Pt-terminated configurations \[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]. This work bridges model surfaces and supported catalysts, offering an efficient design strategy for hydrogenation catalysts \[10.1039/c1cy00066g\].

                    催化氢化C═O键，特别是使用Pt-Ni等双金属催化剂，对于工业和研究应用至关重要，特别是在精细化学品合成和纤维素生物质转化中\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]。这些催化剂提供了独特的性质，增强了氢化、脱氢和重整反应。研究表明，双金属结构对催化性能有显著影响，Pt/Ni催化剂的氢化活性高于单金属催化剂\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]。在定义明确的表面上进行的理论和实验研究证明，特定的双金属配置，如Pt-终止结构，对于氢化和脱氢更活跃\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]。此外，像PtNiPt(1 1 1)这样的亚表面结构可以实现新颖的低温氢化途径\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]。这些发现与密度泛函理论（DFT）预测一致，表明Pt原子在氢环境和真空下向表面分层，而Ni原子向内扩散，热力学上是有利的\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023\]。使用双金属PtNi催化剂催化C=O键的氢化对于精细化学品合成和纤维素生物质转化具有重要意义\[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\]。特别是，HYAl2O3支持的PtNi催化剂在40°C下将马来酸高效转化为琥珀酸，转化率为100%，证明了其有效性\[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\]。理论计算与实验研究之间的相互作用提高了这一过程的选择性和效率，方法包括催化剂制备、支持和反应条件影响的调查，以及使用X射线衍射和温度程序还原的催化剂表征\[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\]。值得注意的是，NiPt系统等双金属催化剂在C═O键氢化中起着关键作用，增强了工业和研究应用\[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]。它们的独特性质使它们在有机合成中有效，特别是在将丙醛氢化为1-丙醇中\[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]。这一过程对于选择性氢化α,β-不饱和醛中的CO键至关重要，这是纤维素生物质转化的关键步骤\[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]。研究表明，NiPt/γ-Al2O3催化剂的性能显著优于单金属催化剂，表明其结构类似于理论上的Pt-终止配置\[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]。这项工作在模型表面和支撑催化剂之间建立了直接联系，为氢化催化剂提供了高效的设计策略\[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]。
