                The pivotal role of bimetallic catalysts, particularly Pt-Ni systems, in the selective hydrogenation of C═O bonds is underscored by their unique properties, distinct from monometallic catalysts \[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]. These catalysts significantly enhance hydrogenation activities, offering superior performance in maleic anhydride hydrogenation, a key intermediate in various industries \[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]. The addition of a small amount of Pt to Ni/Al2O3 improves activity and selectivity, as evidenced by the supported PtNi catalysts' high performance in maleic anhydride hydrogenation \[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]. Methodologies including XRD, H2 TPR, and flow reactor studies are employed to optimize and enhance the process, revealing that H2 dissociation on nickel controls the activity, which can be further improved by Pt addition without compromising selectivity \[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]. The catalytic hydrogenation of C═O bonds using PtNi bimetallic catalysts is significant in fine chemical synthesis, exemplified by the 100% conversion of maleic acid to succinic acid at 40 C \[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\]. HYAl2O3-supported PtNi catalysts outperform monometallic and other bimetallic catalysts due to bimetallic formation, showing higher hydrogenation activities \[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\]. Optimization strategies include the preparation of supported Ni and NiPt catalysts, studying the effects of supports and reaction conditions on activity, selectivity, and stability, and catalyst characterization using X-ray diffraction and temperature-programmed reduction \[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\]. Bimetallic catalysts, notably NiPt systems, are pivotal in industrial and research applications for C═O bond hydrogenation, offering unique properties for organic synthesis and cellulosic biomass conversion \[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]. This process is crucial for selective hydrogenation in α,β-unsaturated aldehydes and the initial step in biomass catalytic conversion \[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]. NiPt catalysts exhibit significantly higher CO hydrogenation activity than monometallic counterparts, suggesting a structure similar to theoretical Pt-terminated configurations \[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]. This work establishes a direct link between theoretical predictions and experimental outcomes, offering an efficient approach to design hydrogenation catalysts \[10.1039/c1cy00066g\].

                特别是Pt-Ni系统等双金属催化剂在C═O键选择性氢化中的关键作用，由其独特的性质所彰显，这些性质与单金属催化剂截然不同\[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]。这些催化剂显著增强了氢化活性，为马来酸酐氢化等关键工业中间体提供了卓越的性能\[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]。向Ni/Al2O3中添加少量Pt可以改善活性和选择性，这一点在支持的PtNi催化剂在马来酸酐氢化中的高表现中得到了证实\[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]。包括XRD、H2 TPR和流动反应器研究在内的方法被用于优化和增强过程，揭示了镍上的H2解离控制着活性，可以通过添加Pt来进一步提高活性，而不会牺牲选择性\[10.1016/j.cej.2011.09.023\]。使用PtNi双金属催化剂的C═O键催化氢化在精细化学品合成中具有重要意义，例如在40°C下将马来酸100%转化为琥珀酸\[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\]。HYAl2O3支持的PtNi催化剂由于双金属形成而优于单金属和其他双金属催化剂，显示出更高的氢化活性\[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\]。优化策略包括制备支持的Ni和NiPt催化剂，研究支持和反应条件对活性、选择性和稳定性的影响，以及使用X射线衍射和程序升温还原进行催化剂表征\[10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7\]。特别是NiPt系统等双金属催化剂在C═O键氢化中对于工业和研究应用至关重要，为有机合成和纤维素生物质转化提供了独特的性质\[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]。这一过程对于α,β-不饱和醛的选择性氢化和生物质催化转化的初始步骤至关重要\[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]。NiPt催化剂的CO氢化活性显著高于单金属对应物，表明其结构类似于理论上的Pt-终止配置\[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]。这项工作在理论预测和实验结果之间建立了直接联系，为设计氢化催化剂提供了一种有效的方法\[10.1039/c1cy00066g\]。
