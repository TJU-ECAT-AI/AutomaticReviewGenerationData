1.The literature does not provide information on the primary synthesis methods for metal-zeolite catalysts used in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, nor does it explain why MFI and BEA topological zeolites dominate in this application. However, it does mention that the pore geometry of the catalyst supports significantly influences the dehydrogenation of propane (DHP) activity, selectivity to propylene, and the degree of coke formation. Pt-ZSM-5, a MFI type zeolite, presents the highest propane conversion, followed by Pt-Beta (BEA type) and Pt-SBA-15. The literature suggests that three-dimensional microporous materials like Pt-ZSM-5 and Pt-Beta are better catalytic supports for DHP than the mesoporous SBA-15 due to their intrinsic nature that may induce optimum catalytic properties of Pt sites.
