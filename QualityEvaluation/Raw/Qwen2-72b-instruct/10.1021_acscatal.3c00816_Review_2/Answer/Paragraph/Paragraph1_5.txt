                    The synthesis of metal-zeolite catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, particularly those featuring MFI and BEA topological zeolites, has been extensively studied \[10.1016/j.cej.2015.01.008\]. MFI zeolites, such as ZSM-5, are favored due to their three-dimensional channel structure and adjustable Si-Al ratio, which enhance their suitability as catalyst carriers. Their superior coke accommodation capacity over γ-Al2O3 supports is attributed to their large surface area and channel characteristics. Metal promoters, like zinc, improve reaction selectivity by neutralizing acidity, with the method of addition impacting the support and metallic phase properties. Zinc incorporation into ZSM-5 creates new active centers, altering catalytic behavior compared to conventional preparation techniques. BEA topological zeolites, such as Sn-Beta, are also pivotal in PDH reactions due to their single-site Sn atoms facilitating dehydrogenation while Brnsted acids promote cracking. A high Lewis-to-Brnsted acid (L/B) ratio is advantageous for PDH, and the Na-Sn-Beta-30 catalyst, with the highest Lewis acidity and lowest B/L ratio, excels in PDH \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]. A mesoporous MFI zeolite, synthesized using a multi-ammonium surfactant, has gained attention for its unique structure featuring ultrathin frameworks and interconnected mesopores, enhancing catalytic performance by facilitating reactant and product diffusion and increasing metal nanoparticle dispersion \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. Cerium substitution in ZSM-5 zeolites enhances PDH catalyst performance by affecting morphologies and textural properties, improving metallic particle dispersion, and increasing hydrogen adsorption at low temperatures \[10.1039/C6RA04173F\]. MFI and BEA zeolites dominate in PDH reactions due to their pore geometry, which significantly influences activity, selectivity, and coke formation \[10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031\]. Silicalite-1 (S-1) zeolite, with an MFI topology, serves as a support for ZnO in PDH catalysts, enhancing dispersion and stability of ZnO species via interaction with silanol nests \[10.1002/cctc.202101069\]. Metal-zeolite catalysts for PDH use postsynthesis (ion exchange, impregnation) and direct synthesis methods. MFI and BEA zeolites lead in PDH due to easy synthesis, adjustable acidity, and moderate pore size, crucial for effectiveness and future PDH catalyst design \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_2\]. ZnO supported on high-silica HZSM-5 (MFI topology) excels in PDH due to its high specific surface area, large pore volume, well-defined structure, and sparse Al sites, promoting uniform Zn distribution \[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508\]. MFI and BEA zeolites dominate in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) due to their high thermal and hydrothermal stability, large surface area, and well-defined pore structures that accommodate metal species and provide accessible active sites \[10.1039/D0CS00814A\]. Zeolites, due to their high thermal stability, mechanical strength, and ability to maintain dispersion of active metal particles, are favored as supports for PDH catalysts. MFI and BEA zeolites, with their high surface area, uniform pore size distribution, and limited acidity, stabilize small metal particles, improving catalyst stability and selectivity \[10.1021/cr5002436\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in metal-zeolite catalyst design for PDH reactions, particularly focusing on MFI and BEA topological zeolites, have revealed the critical role of structural characteristics, synthesis methods, and metal-support interactions. Future research endeavors might focus on optimizing metal loading and distribution, developing synthesis methods for hierarchical pore structures, and adjusting Si/Al ratios to prevent coke deposits and improve catalytic stability. The effectiveness of MFI and BEA zeolites in PDH is attributed to their structural properties, such as uniform pore sizes and shape selectivity, which inhibit the formation of coke and other side reactions. Potential developments include optimizing zeolite structures and incorporating other active metals for improved selectivity and stability. The exploration of emerging fields, such as the use of pre-transition metals as co-catalysts, despite synthetic challenges, offers promising avenues for innovative methodologies in PDH catalyst design.

                    金属-沸石催化剂在丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应中的合成,特别是那些具有MFI和BEA拓扑结构的沸石,已经得到了广泛的研究\[10.1016/j.cej.2015.01.008\]。MFI沸石,如ZSM-5,因其三维通道结构和可调的Si-Al比例而受到青睐,这增强了它们作为催化剂载体的适用性。它们在γ-Al2O3支撑材料上的优越的焦炭容纳能力归因于它们的高表面积和通道特性。金属促进剂,如锌,通过中和酸性提高反应选择性,添加方法影响支撑材料和金属相的性质。锌在ZSM-5中的掺入创建了新的活性中心,与传统制备技术相比,改变了催化行为。BEA拓扑结构的沸石,如Sn-Beta,在PDH反应中也至关重要,因为它们的单位点Sn原子促进了脱氢,而Brnsted酸促进了裂解。高Lewis/Brnsted酸(L/B)比率对PDH有利,Na-Sn-Beta-30催化剂,具有最高的Lewis酸性和最低的B/L比率,在PDH中表现出色\[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]。使用多铵表面活性剂合成的介孔MFI沸石因其独特的结构而受到关注,该结构具有超薄框架和相互连接的介孔,通过促进反应物和产物的扩散以及增加金属纳米粒子的分散度,增强了催化性能\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。在ZSM-5沸石中掺入铈增强了PDH催化剂的性能,影响了形态和纹理特性,改善了金属粒子的分散度,并在低温下增加了氢吸附\[10.1039/C6RA04173F\]。MFI和BEA沸石由于其孔几何形状在PDH反应中占主导地位,这显著影响了活性、选择性和焦炭形成\[10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031\]。具有MFI拓扑结构的硅酸铝-1(S-1)沸石作为PDH催化剂中ZnO的支撑材料,通过与硅醇巢的相互作用增强了ZnO物种的分散度和稳定性\[10.1002/cctc.202101069\]。金属-沸石PDH催化剂使用后合成(离子交换、浸渍)和直接合成方法。MFI和BEA沸石在PDH中领先,因为它们易于合成,酸度可调,孔径适中,对有效性和未来PDH催化剂设计至关重要\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_2\]。支持在高硅HZSM-5(MFI拓扑结构)上的ZnO在PDH中表现出色,因为其高比表面积、大孔体积、明确定义的结构和稀疏的Al位点,促进了均匀的Zn分布\[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508\]。MFI和BEA沸石在丙烷脱氢(PDH)中占主导地位,因为它们具有高热和水热稳定性、大表面积和明确定义的孔结构,可以容纳金属物种并提供可访问的活性位点\[10.1039/D0CS00814A\]。沸石因其高热稳定性、机械强度和保持活性金属粒子分散的能力而被选作PDH催化剂的支撑材料。MFI和BEA沸石,由于其高表面积、均匀的孔径分布和有限的酸度,稳定了小金属粒子,提高了催化剂的稳定性和选择性\[10.1021/cr5002436\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望:在PDH反应中,特别是在MFI和BEA拓扑结构的沸石中,金属-沸石催化剂设计的进展揭示了结构特性、合成方法和金属-支撑相互作用的关键作用。未来的研究努力可能集中在优化金属负载和分布、开发合成方法以形成分级孔结构,以及调整Si/Al比率以防止焦炭沉积并提高催化稳定性。MFI和BEA沸石在PDH中的有效性归因于它们的结构特性,如均匀的孔径和形状选择性,这抑制了焦炭和其他副反应的形成。潜在的发展包括优化沸石结构和掺入其他活性金属以提高选择性和稳定性。探索新兴领域,如使用预过渡金属作为共催化剂,尽管存在合成挑战,为PDH催化剂设计的创新方法提供了有希望的途径。
