            <English> 
                    The synthesis of metal-zeolite catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, particularly focusing on MFI and BEA topological zeolites, reveals their dominance due to facile synthesis procedures, tunable acidity, and moderate pore size \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_2\]. MFI zeolites, such as ZSM-5, are favored for their three-dimensional channel structure and adjustable Si-Al ratio, which enhance their suitability as catalyst carriers and their superior coke accommodation capacity compared to γ-Al2O3-supported catalysts \[10.1016/j.cej.2015.01.008\]. Incorporating metal promoters, like zinc, improves reaction selectivity by neutralizing acidity and creating new active centers \[10.1016/j.cej.2015.01.008\]. Sn-Beta zeolites, featuring single-site Sn atoms in the BEA framework, are crucial for PDH reactions due to their Lewis acid sites, which facilitate dehydrogenation, and a high L/B ratio that benefits PDH \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]. The synthesis involves dealumination and Sn incorporation, with the efficiency of dealumination impacting Sn incorporation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]. A mesoporous MFI zeolite, synthesized using a multi-ammonium surfactant, has gained attention for its unique structure featuring ultrathin frameworks and interconnected mesopores, which enhance catalytic performance by facilitating reactant and product diffusion and increasing metal nanoparticle dispersion \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in metal-zeolite catalysts for PDH reactions, particularly focusing on MFI and BEA topological zeolites, offer a fertile ground for exploring innovative methodologies and emerging fields. Future research endeavors might focus on optimizing the synthesis methods to achieve higher dispersion of active metal species, developing hierarchical pore structures for improved mass transfer, and adjusting Si/Al ratios to prevent coke deposits. The exploration of pre-transition metals as co-catalysts, despite synthetic challenges, presents an opportunity to expand the potential scope of PDH industrial catalysts \[10.1016/j.ces.2023.118450\]. Additionally, the utilization of zeolites with abundant silanol nests and suitable topology, like MFI and BEA, to disperse and stabilize active metal species could enhance catalytic performance \[10.1002/cctc.202101069\]. Tailoring zeolite properties, such as increased thermal stability and reduced acidity, could lead to more stable and selective PDH catalysts in the future \[10.1021/cr5002436\].

                    金属-沸石催化剂在丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应中的合成，特别是关注MFI和BEA拓扑沸石，揭示了它们的主导地位，因为它们具有易于合成的程序、可调的酸度和适中的孔径\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_2\]。MFI沸石，如ZSM-5，因其三维通道结构和可调的Si-Al比而受到青睐，这增强了它们作为催化剂载体的适用性，以及与γ-Al2O3支持的催化剂相比，它们具有优越的焦炭容纳能力\[10.1016/j.cej.2015.01.008\]。加入金属促进剂，如锌，通过中和酸度提高反应选择性并创建新的活性中心\[10.1016/j.cej.2015.01.008\]。BEA框架中具有单个Sn原子的Sn-Beta沸石对于PDH反应至关重要，因为它们的Lewis酸位点促进了脱氢，并且高L/B比对PDH有益\[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]。合成涉及脱铝和Sn掺入，脱铝的效率影响Sn的掺入\[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]。使用多铵表面活性剂合成的介孔MFI沸石因其独特的结构而受到关注，该结构具有超薄框架和相互连接的介孔，通过促进反应物和产物的扩散以及增加金属纳米粒子的分散度来增强催化性能\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 在PDH反应中，特别是在MFI和BEA拓扑沸石上的金属-沸石催化剂的进展，为探索创新方法和新兴领域提供了肥沃的土壤。未来的研究努力可能集中在优化合成方法以实现活性金属物种的更高分散，开发分层孔结构以改善质量传递，以及调整Si/Al比以防止焦炭沉积。尽管存在合成挑战，但探索预过渡金属作为助催化剂为扩大PDH工业催化剂的潜在范围提供了机会\[10.1016/j.ces.2023.118450\]。此外，利用具有丰富硅醇巢和合适拓扑结构的沸石，如MFI和BEA，来分散和稳定活性金属物种，可以提高催化性能\[10.1002/cctc.202101069\]。调整沸石性质，如提高热稳定性并降低酸度，可以导致未来更稳定和选择性的PDH催化剂\[10.1021/cr5002436\]。
