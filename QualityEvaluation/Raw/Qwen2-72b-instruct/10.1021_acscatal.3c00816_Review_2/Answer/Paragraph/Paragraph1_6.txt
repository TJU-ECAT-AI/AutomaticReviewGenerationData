                    The synthesis methods for metal-zeolite catalysts in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions are diverse, encompassing postsynthesis techniques like ion exchange and impregnation, as well as direct synthesis where metal sites are introduced during zeolite crystallization \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_2\]. Among these, MFI and BEA topological zeolites dominate due to their facile synthesis procedure, tunable acidity, and moderate pore size, which are crucial for PDH catalyst effectiveness and future developments \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_2\]. ZSM-5 zeolite, with its MFI topology, is favored in PDH due to its three-dimensional channel structure and adjustable Si-Al ratio, making it an effective support for platinum catalysts \[10.1016/j.cej.2015.01.008\]. Its superior coke accommodation capacity over γ-Al2O3-supported catalysts, attributed to its large surface area and channel characteristics, is a key advantage \[10.1016/j.cej.2015.01.008\]. The addition of metal promoters, such as zinc, improves reaction selectivity by neutralizing acidity, with the method of addition impacting the final properties \[10.1016/j.cej.2015.01.008\]. Sn-Beta zeolites, featuring single-site Sn atoms in the BEA framework, are key in catalytic biomass conversion and PDH \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]. The Lewis acid sites from single-site Sn facilitate PDH, with a high Lewis-to-Brnsted acid (L/B) ratio favoring dehydrogenation over cracking \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]. Sn-Beta synthesis involves dealumination of H-Beta followed by Sn incorporation, with dealumination efficiency impacting Sn atom incorporation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]. High HNO3 concentration aids Al removal, creating lattice defects for Sn incorporation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]. Sn-Beta retains its crystalline structure and pore architecture, with Sn species tetrahedrally coordinated, reducing the zeolite's reducibility and creating Lewis acid sites \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]. Na-Sn-Beta-30, with the highest Lewis acidity and lowest B/L ratio, excels in PDH \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]. Direct dehydrogenation pathways on open sites are kinetically favorable \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]. A mesoporous MFI zeolite synthesized using a multi-ammonium surfactant as a structure-directing agent has gained attention for its hierarchical micro-/mesoporous structure, comprising ultrathin zeolite frameworks and interconnected mesopores \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. This structure boosts catalytic performance by facilitating reactant and product diffusion and enhancing metal nanoparticle dispersion \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. The use of this zeolite as a support enables the discovery of various alloy catalysts, expanding catalytic applications \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\].

                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements and findings discussed in the file suggest that the future of PDH catalyst design will focus on optimizing synthesis methods for metal-zeolite catalysts, particularly for MFI and BEA topological zeolites. Innovations in postsynthesis techniques and direct synthesis methods will aim to enhance the tunable acidity and moderate pore size of these zeolites, crucial for PDH catalyst effectiveness. The development of hierarchical pore structures for improved mass transfer and the adjustment of Si/Al ratios to prevent coke deposits will be key areas of research \[10.1039/D0CS00814A\]. The incorporation of other active metals, in addition to Pt and Sn, will be explored to improve selectivity and stability \[10.1039/D0CS00814A\]. The use of cerium-modified ZSM-5 zeolites as supports for bimetallic PtSn catalysts will be further investigated for improved reaction performance \[10.1039/C6RA04173F\]. The optimization of metal-zeolite interactions, leveraging supports with abundant silanol nests and suitable topology, will be a priority for enhanced catalytic performance \[10.1002/cctc.202101069\]. Tailoring zeolite properties, such as increased thermal stability and reduced acidity, will lead to more stable and selective PDH catalysts in the future \[10.1021/cr5002436\]. The synthesis of mesoporous MFI zeolites using multi-ammonium surfactants will be expanded to discover various alloy catalysts, including REE-based and transition metal-based alloys, for broader catalytic applications \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\].

                    金属-沸石催化剂在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中的合成方法多样，包括后合成技术如离子交换和浸渍，以及直接合成，其中金属位点在沸石结晶过程中引入 \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_2\]。其中，MFI和BEA拓扑结构的沸石由于其简便的合成程序、可调的酸性和适中的孔径，在PDH催化剂的有效性和未来发展中起着关键作用 \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_2\]。ZSM-5沸石，具有MFI拓扑结构，因其三维通道结构和可调的Si-Al比，在PDH中受到青睐，使其成为铂催化剂的有效载体 \[10.1016/j.cej.2015.01.008\]。它在γ-Al2O3支撑的催化剂上的优越的焦炭容纳能力，归因于其大表面积和通道特性，是一个关键优势 \[10.1016/j.cej.2015.01.008\]。添加金属促进剂，如锌，通过中和酸性提高反应选择性，添加方法影响最终性质 \[10.1016/j.cej.2015.01.008\]。Sn-Beta沸石，其BEA框架中具有单个Sn原子，在催化生物质转化和PDH中起关键作用 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]。来自单个Sn的Lewis酸位点促进PDH，高Lewis-to-Brnsted酸（L/B）比率有利于脱氢而不是裂解 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]。Sn-Beta的合成涉及H-Beta的脱铝化，随后是Sn的掺入，脱铝化效率影响Sn原子的掺入 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]。高浓度的HNO3有助于Al的去除，为Sn的掺入创造晶格缺陷 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]。Sn-Beta保留其结晶结构和孔结构，Sn物种四面体配位，降低沸石的还原性，创建Lewis酸位点 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]。Na-Sn-Beta-30，具有最高的Lewis酸性和最低的B/L比，在PDH中表现出色 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]。在开放位点上的直接脱氢途径在动力学上是有利的 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]。使用多铵表面活性剂作为结构导向剂合成的介孔MFI沸石因其分层的微/介孔结构而受到关注，包括超薄沸石框架和相互连接的介孔 \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。这种结构通过促进反应物和产物的扩散以及增强金属纳米粒子的分散性来提高催化性能 \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。使用这种沸石作为支撑体，可以发现各种合金催化剂，扩展催化应用 \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。

                    综合理解与未来展望：文件中讨论的进展和发现表明，PDH催化剂设计的未来将集中在优化金属-沸石催化剂的合成方法，特别是MFI和BEA拓扑结构的沸石。后合成技术和直接合成方法的创新将旨在增强这些沸石的可调酸性和适中的孔径，这对PDH催化剂的有效性至关重要。开发分层孔结构以改善质量传递和调整Si/Al比以防止焦炭沉积将是研究的关键领域 \[10.1039/D0CS00814A\]。除了Pt和Sn之外，探索其他活性金属的掺入，以提高选择性和稳定性，将是研究的重点 \[10.1039/D0CS00814A\]。使用铈改性的ZSM-5沸石作为双金属PtSn催化剂的支撑体，以改善反应性能，将进行进一步的研究 \[10.1039/C6RA04173F\]。优化金属-沸石的相互作用，利用具有丰富硅醇巢和合适拓扑结构的支撑体，将是增强催化性能的优先事项 \[10.1002/cctc.202101069\]。调整沸石性质，如增加热稳定性和降低酸性，将导致未来更稳定和选择性的PDH催化剂 \[10.1021/cr5002436\]。使用多铵表面活性剂合成介孔MFI沸石将被扩展到发现各种合金催化剂，包括REE基和过渡金属基合金，以实现更广泛的催化应用 \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。
