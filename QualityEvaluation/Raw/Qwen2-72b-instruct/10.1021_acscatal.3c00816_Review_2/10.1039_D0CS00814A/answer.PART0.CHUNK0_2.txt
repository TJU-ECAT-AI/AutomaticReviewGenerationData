However, it does mention that zeolites with adjustable Si/Al ratios and relatively large surface areas can hinder the formation of large hydrocarbon molecules, thus preventing the so-called coke deposits and improving the catalytic stability. Additionally, Pt can be embedded in zeolites with special three-dimensional channels such as Pt@KA (LTA-type zeolite) and Pt@ZSM-5 (MFI-type zeolite) with some unique physical properties. The effectiveness of these zeolites in PDH reactions is attributed to their structural characteristics, such as high surface area, pore size, and shape selectivity, which facilitate the adsorption and activation of propane while inhibiting the formation of coke and other side reactions. The implications for future developments in PDH catalyst design include the potential for further optimization of zeolite structures and the incorporation of other active metals to improve selectivity and stability.
