1.The literature does not provide a detailed analysis of the primary synthesis methods for metal-zeolite catalysts used in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, nor does it explain why MFI and BEA topological zeolites dominate in this application. However, it does mention that ZnO supported on high-silica HZSM-5 (an MFI zeolite) is an efficient catalyst for PDH due to its high specific surface area, large pore volume, well-defined structure, and sparse Al sites, which cause Zn species to distribute well throughout the support. This is directly related to the catalytic activity, while ZnO supported on low-silica HZSM-5 is prone to aggregate into bulk ZnO on the zeolite external surface. The well-adjusted acidity of high-silica HZSM-5 can significantly improve the propylene selectivity and dehydrogenation stability by promoting the propylene desorption and minimizing side reactions. The implications for future developments in PDH catalyst design are that high-silica MFI zeolites with well-defined structures and sparse Al sites may be promising supports for metal-zeolite catalysts in PDH reactions.
