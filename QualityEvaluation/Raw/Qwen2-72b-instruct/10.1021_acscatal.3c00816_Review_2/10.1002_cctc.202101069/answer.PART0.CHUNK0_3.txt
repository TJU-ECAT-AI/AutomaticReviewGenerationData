1.The literature does not provide a detailed analysis of the primary synthesis methods for metal-zeolite catalysts used in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, nor does it explain why MFI and BEA topological zeolites dominate in this application. However, it does mention that silicalite-1 (S-1) zeolite, which has an MFI topology, is used as a support for ZnO in the preparation of a PDH catalyst. The S-1 zeolite is chosen because it has a high silica content and lacks strong acidity, which can lead to undesirable side reactions. The dispersed ZnO species on the S-1 support are stabilized through interaction with silanol nests, leading to excellent PDH catalytic performance. The high activity and stability of the catalyst can be attributed to the dispersed ZnO species. The implications for future developments in PDH catalyst design are that supports with high silica content and abundant silanol groups may be effective for dispersing and stabilizing active metal species, leading to improved PDH catalytic performance.
