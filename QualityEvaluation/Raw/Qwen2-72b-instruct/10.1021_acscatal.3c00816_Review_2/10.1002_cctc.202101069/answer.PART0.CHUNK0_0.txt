1.The literature does not provide a detailed analysis of the primary synthesis methods for metal-zeolite catalysts used in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, nor does it explain why MFI and BEA topological zeolites dominate in this application. However, it does mention that silicalite-1 (S-1) zeolite, which has an MFI topology, is used as a support for ZnO in the catalyst studied. The S-1 zeolite is chosen for its topology without strong acidity, which is beneficial for the PDH reaction. The effectiveness of the catalyst is attributed to the dispersed ZnO species due to the interaction between silanol nests of the S-1 support and ZnO species. This interaction suppresses the aggregation of ZnO species under reaction conditions, leading to high activity and stability. The implications for future developments in PDH catalyst design could include the use of other MFI or BEA topological zeolites as supports for metal oxides, with a focus on maximizing the dispersion and stability of the active species.
