1.The literature does not provide a detailed analysis of the primary synthesis methods for metal-zeolite catalysts used in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, nor does it explain why MFI and BEA topological zeolites dominate in this application. However, it does mention that silicalite-1 (S-1) zeolite, which has an MFI topology, is used as a support for ZnO in the preparation of a PDH catalyst. The S-1 zeolite is chosen because it has the same MFI topology without strong acidity, which is beneficial for the PDH reaction. The dispersed ZnO species on the S-1 support are stabilized via interaction with silanol nests, which suppresses aggregation of ZnO species under reaction conditions. This results in an excellent catalytic activity and stability for the PDH reaction. The implication for future developments in PDH catalyst design is that supports with abundant silanol nests and suitable topology, such as MFI and BEA, may be effective for dispersing and stabilizing active metal species, leading to improved PDH catalytic performance.
