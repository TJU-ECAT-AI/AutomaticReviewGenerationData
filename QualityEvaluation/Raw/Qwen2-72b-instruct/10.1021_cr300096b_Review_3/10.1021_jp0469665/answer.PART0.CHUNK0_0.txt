However, it does describe a method for preparing bimetallic dendrimer-encapsulated nanoparticles (DENs) that can be immobilized on supports. These bimetallic DENs can be synthesized using a co-complexation route to form alloy-type bimetallics or a sequential dendrimer-templating approach to create core/shell structures. The literature highlights the potential advantages of these methods, including the ability to control the size and composition of the nanoparticles, the stability of the DENs, and their catalytic activity. The applications of these bimetallic DENs include catalysis, particularly for hydrogenation and carbon-coupling reactions. The efficacy of these methods in catalyst production is demonstrated by the successful synthesis of bimetallic DENs with enhanced catalytic properties compared to their monometallic counterparts.
