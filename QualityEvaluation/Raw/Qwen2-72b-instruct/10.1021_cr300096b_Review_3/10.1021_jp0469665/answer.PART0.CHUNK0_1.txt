1.The literature does not provide a direct comparison of synthesis methods for supported bimetallic catalysts. However, it does describe a method for preparing supported bimetallic catalysts by incorporating dendrimer-encapsulated nanoparticles (DENs) into sol-gel matrices and then calcining the composites to remove the dendrimer. This approach minimizes particle growth because individual nanoparticles are isolated within the sol-gel framework. The literature also mentions that the dendrimer has a dual templating role: it defines the size and monodispersity of both the nanoparticles and the pore structure in the sol-gel framework. After calcination, the size and chemical composition of individual particles are largely retained. Supported bimetallic nanoparticles can also be prepared via this sol-gel route, and even after calcining to remove the dendrimer, the size and chemical composition of individual particles are largely retained.
