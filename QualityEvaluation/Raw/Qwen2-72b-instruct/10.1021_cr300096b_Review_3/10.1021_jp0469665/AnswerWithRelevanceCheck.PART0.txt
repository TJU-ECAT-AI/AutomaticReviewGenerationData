          The literature describes methods for preparing bimetallic dendrimer-encapsulated nanoparticles (DENs) that can be immobilized on supports using a co-complexation route to form alloy-type bimetallics or a sequential dendrimer-templating approach to create core/shell structures. These methods offer advantages such as control over the size and composition of the nanoparticles, stability of the DENs, and enhanced catalytic activity, particularly for hydrogenation and carbon-coupling reactions. Another method involves incorporating DENs into sol-gel matrices and calcining the composites to remove the dendrimer, minimizing particle growth and retaining the size and chemical composition of individual particles. The literature also discusses synthesizing bimetallic DENs through co-complexation of two different metal ions or through redox displacement reactions, highlighting their stability and catalytic activity. However, the literature does not provide a direct comparison of synthesis methods for supported bimetallic catalysts in terms of potential advantages, applications, and overall efficacy in catalyst production.
