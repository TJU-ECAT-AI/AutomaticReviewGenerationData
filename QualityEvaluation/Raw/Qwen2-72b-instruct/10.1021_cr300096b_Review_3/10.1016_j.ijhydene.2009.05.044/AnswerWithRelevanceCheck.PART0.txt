          The literature does not provide a direct comparison of synthesis methods for supported bimetallic catalysts. However, it does mention several methods that have been reported in the literature, including precipitation, deposition-precipitation, co-precipitation, sol-gel, sonochemical, and incipient wetness impregnation. Among these, incipient wetness impregnation is the most frequently used method. The literature highlights the use of organometallic cluster-derived nanoparticles as a method that offers excellent control of bimetallic composition and smaller particle sizes, which can lead to more stable and active catalysts for hydrogenation reactions. The use of organometallic cluster precursors is highlighted as offering advantages in terms of control over particle size and bimetallic composition, leading to more active and selective catalysts. This method is demonstrated to be useful in the design of efficient ethanol steam reforming catalysts.
