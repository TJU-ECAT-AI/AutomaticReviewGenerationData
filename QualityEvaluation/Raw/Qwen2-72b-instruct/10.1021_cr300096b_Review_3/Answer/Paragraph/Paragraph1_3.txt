                    The ammonia method for synthesizing supported bimetallic catalysts, particularly on silica and synthetic kenyaite, outperforms classical impregnation by achieving finer dispersion of Co3O4, enhancing catalytic activity in benzene combustion \[10.1007/s10853-006-0894-x\]. This method leads to more active monometallic Co samples, and the addition of Pt boosts activity further due to the synergy between cobalt oxide and Pt. The ammonia method's superiority in benzene oxidation is attributed to the finer dispersion of easily reducible Co3O4. Microemulsion techniques offer precise control over nanoparticle size and composition, an advantage over other methods for consistent catalyst production \[10.1016/s0022-0728(04)00328-6\]. A novel technique using adsorbate-induced surface segregation addresses challenges in synthesis methods such as active surface area loss and incomplete noble-metal shells, offering a significant advancement in catalyst production \[10.1002/anie.200806209\]. Co-complexation for alloy-type bimetallics and sequential dendrimer-templating for core/shell structures offer control over nanoparticle size and composition, stability, and enhanced catalytic activity, particularly for hydrogenation and carbon-coupling reactions \[10.1021/jp0469665\]. Organometallic cluster-derived nanoparticles offer superior control over bimetallic composition and particle size, leading to more stable and active catalysts, particularly for hydrogenation reactions \[10.1016/j.ijhydene.2009.05.044\]. Reverse micelle synthesis, using microemulsions, produces uniform nanoparticles by reducing metal precursors, enhancing size control and understanding of catalyst activity \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.12.021\]. Impregnation, while widely used, may not ensure close metal contact, crucial for catalytic performance, whereas reductive deposition precipitation (RDP) promotes closer metal interaction through redox chemistry, showing performance advantages \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.02.003\]. The one-step synthesis method using reducing agents like NH3BH3, ODA, BT, and ascorbic acid is simpler and more efficient, suitable for synthesizing bimetallic nanoparticles with diverse compositions and morphologies \[10.1039/c1jm12020d\]. Common synthesis methods for supported bimetallic catalysts include wet impregnation, favored for ease of preparation; slurry synthesis, suitable for high metal loadings; organometallic cluster precursors, enhancing uniformity; reductive deposition precipitation, useful for highly coordinated bimetallics; electroless deposition, enabling targeted metal placement; colloidal synthesis, offering precise control over particle size and composition; core-shell synthesis, creating transition metal core catalysts; reverse micelle synthesis, producing nanoparticles with narrow size distribution; dendrimer support synthesis, yielding monodisperse particles; microwave-assisted polyol reduction (MAPR), allowing rapid uniform heating; and laser vaporization, synthesizing uniform catalysts \[10.1021/cr300096b_3\].

                    氨水法合成支撑双金属催化剂，特别是在硅胶和合成肯尼亚石上，与传统浸渍法相比，实现了Co3O4更细的分散，提高了苯燃烧的催化活性\[10.1007/s10853-006-0894-x\]。这种方法导致更活跃的单金属Co样品，而Pt的添加由于钴氧化物和Pt之间的协同作用进一步提高了活性。氨水法在苯氧化中的优越性归因于易还原的Co3O4更细的分散。微乳液技术提供了对纳米粒子大小和组成的精确控制，这是其他方法无法比拟的优势，对于一致的催化剂生产至关重要\[10.1016/s0022-0728(04)00328-6\]。一种使用吸附诱导表面分层的新技术解决了合成方法中的挑战，如活性表面积损失和不完全的贵金属壳形成，为催化剂生产提供了显著的进展\[10.1002/anie.200806209\]。共复合物法用于合金型双金属，而顺序树状分子模板法用于核心/壳结构，提供了对纳米粒子大小和组成的控制，稳定性，以及增强的催化活性，特别是对于氢化和碳耦合反应\[10.1021/jp0469665\]。有机金属簇衍生的纳米粒子提供了对双金属组成和粒子大小的优越控制，导致更稳定和更活跃的催化剂，特别是对于氢化反应\[10.1016/j.ijhydene.2009.05.044\]。反胶束合成，使用微乳液，通过还原金属前体产生均匀的纳米粒子，增强了尺寸控制和对催化剂活性的理解\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.12.021\]。浸渍法，虽然广泛使用，可能无法确保对催化性能至关重要的紧密金属接触，而还原沉积沉淀（RDP）通过氧化还原化学促进更紧密的金属相互作用，显示出性能优势\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.02.003\]。使用像NH3BH3，ODA，BT和抗坏血酸这样的还原剂的一步合成法更简单，更有效，适用于合成具有各种组成和形态的双金属纳米粒子\[10.1039/c1jm12020d\]。支撑双金属催化剂的常见合成方法包括湿浸渍，因其制备的简便性而受到青睐；浆料合成，适用于高金属负载；有机金属簇前体，增强均匀性；还原沉积沉淀，对高度协调的双金属有用；无电沉积，允许有针对性的金属放置；胶体合成，提供对粒子大小和组成的精确控制；核心-壳合成，创建过渡金属核心催化剂；反胶束合成，产生具有窄尺寸分布的纳米粒子；树状分子支持合成，产生单分散粒子；微波辅助聚醇还原（MAPR），允许快速均匀加热；以及激光蒸发，合成均匀催化剂\[10.1021/cr300096b_3\]。
