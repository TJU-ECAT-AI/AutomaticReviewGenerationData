                The preparation, properties, and catalytic applications of doped nickel oxide materials in oxidative dehydrogenation reactions, particularly propane dehydrogenation, are significantly influenced by dopant concentrations and vacancy formation \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. Niobium-doped nickel oxide catalysts exhibit enhanced performance in ethane oxidative dehydrogenation, achieving optimal conversion (33%) and selectivity (78%) to ethylene at 15% Nb content \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. The efficiency is attributed to surface interaction between NiO and amorphous Nb2O5, reducing NiO oxygen over-stoichiometry and preventing sintering \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. Above 15% Nb, an inactive NiNb2O6 phase forms, leading to long-term deactivation \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. Citric acid-based preparation methods for NiO and NbNiO nanocomposites impact electronic structure and catalytic performance in ethane oxidative dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]. The Nb-rich phase inhibits NiO reducibility, affecting redox properties \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]. Optimal activity, selectivity, and stability are achieved at 34 at.% Nb content, balancing coverage of active oxygen species and catalyst deactivation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]. Low-temperature treatment of pure NiO creates a non-stoichiometric phase with cationic deficiency \[10.1016/j.apcata.2007.02.030\]. Upon Nb doping, two phases emerge: a NiO phase with Nb cations forming a NiNb solid solution, and a distorted Nb-rich phase, a precursor for NiNb2O6 \[10.1016/j.apcata.2007.02.030\]. The interaction between niobium ions and structural defects in NiO enhances ethylene selectivity in ethane ODH \[10.1016/j.apcata.2007.02.030\]. Dopant concentrations and vacancy formation in nickel oxide catalysts significantly impact their electronic structure and catalytic performance in oxidative dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]. Different dopant valences modify the lattice constant, influencing the formation of electrophilic oxygen species crucial for ethane conversion \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]. Lower valence dopants increase non-stoichiometric oxygen, favoring ethylene production, while higher valence dopants enhance initial selectivity to the desired product \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]. The linear relationship between oxygen exchange and ethane consumption confirms the active role of oxygen species in ethane activation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]. Theoretical modeling challenges include understanding oxygen species reactivity, optimizing dopant concentration for desired performance, and ensuring catalyst safety \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]. Balancing efficiency, selectivity, and safety is key in these catalytic systems \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in the preparation and application of doped nickel oxide catalysts for oxidative dehydrogenation reactions, particularly propane dehydrogenation, have opened new avenues for improving catalytic performance. The role of dopants, such as niobium, in enhancing selectivity and conversion rates by altering the electronic structure and oxygen species dynamics is a promising area for further exploration \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. Future research might focus on developing more efficient preparation methods that optimize the balance between active oxygen species coverage and catalyst deactivation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]. The investigation of the effects of different dopant valences on the lattice constant and the formation of electrophilic oxygen species could lead to the design of catalysts with tailored electronic properties for enhanced ethylene production \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]. Addressing the challenges in theoretical modeling, such as understanding the complex reaction mechanisms and predicting dopant and vacancy effects on electronic structure and reactivity, is crucial for process optimization and ensuring safety in high-temperature and pressure operations \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]. The development of innovative methodologies for catalyst regeneration and the exploration of new dopants with unique electronic properties could further enhance the efficiency and selectivity of nickel oxide-based catalysts in oxidative dehydrogenation reactions.

                镍氧化物材料的掺杂、性质及其在氧化脱氢反应，特别是丙烷脱氢反应中的催化应用，受到掺杂浓度和空位形成的影响\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]。铌掺杂的镍氧化物催化剂在乙烷氧化脱氢反应中表现出增强的性能，当铌含量为15%时，乙烷向乙烯的转化率达到33%，选择性达到78%\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]。效率归因于NiO与无定形Nb2O5之间的表面相互作用，减少了NiO的过量氧，防止了烧结\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]。超过15%的铌，形成不活跃的NiNb2O6相，导致长期失活\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]。基于柠檬酸的制备方法对NiO和NbNiO纳米复合材料的电子结构和催化性能在乙烷氧化脱氢反应中产生影响\[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]。铌富集相抑制了NiO的还原性，影响了氧化还原性质\[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]。在34 at.%的铌含量下，实现了最佳的活性、选择性和稳定性，平衡了活性氧物种的覆盖和催化剂失活\[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]。纯NiO的低温处理产生了一个具有阳离子缺陷的非化学计量相\[10.1016/j.apcata.2007.02.030\]。铌掺杂后，形成了两个相：一个NiO相，其中Nb阳离子形成NiNb固溶体，以及一个扭曲的铌富集相，是NiNb2O6的前体\[10.1016/j.apcata.2007.02.030\]。铌离子与NiO中结构缺陷的相互作用增强了乙烷ODH中对乙烯的选择性\[10.1016/j.apcata.2007.02.030\]。镍氧化物催化剂中的掺杂浓度和空位形成对其电子结构和氧化脱氢反应中的催化性能有显著影响\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]。不同的掺杂价态改变了晶格常数，影响了对乙烷转化至关重要的电亲性氧物种的形成\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]。低价态掺杂剂增加了非化学计量氧，有利于乙烯的生产，而高价态掺杂剂增强了对所需产品的初始选择性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]。氧交换与乙烷消耗之间的线性关系确认了氧物种在乙烷活化中的积极作用\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]。理论建模的挑战包括理解氧物种的反应性，优化掺杂浓度以获得所需性能，以及确保催化剂安全\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]。在这些催化系统中，平衡效率、选择性和安全性是关键\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]。

                综合理解与未来展望: 镍氧化物催化剂的制备和应用在氧化脱氢反应，特别是丙烷脱氢反应中的进展，为提高催化性能开辟了新的途径。铌等掺杂剂在通过改变电子结构和氧物种动力学来提高选择性和转化率方面的作用是一个有前景的进一步探索领域\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]。未来的研究可能集中在开发更有效的制备方法，以优化活性氧物种覆盖与催化剂失活之间的平衡\[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]。研究不同掺杂价态对晶格常数和电亲性氧物种形成的影响，可以设计出具有定制电子性质的催化剂，以增强乙烯的生产\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]。解决理论建模的挑战，如理解复杂的反应机制和预测掺杂剂和空位对电子结构和反应性的影响，对于过程优化和确保在高温和高压操作中的安全性至关重要\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]。催化剂再生的创新方法的发展和具有独特电子性质的新掺杂剂的探索，可以进一步提高镍氧化物基催化剂在氧化脱氢反应中的效率和选择性。
