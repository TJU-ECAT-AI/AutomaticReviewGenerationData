The preparation, properties, and catalytic applications of doped nickel oxide materials in oxidative dehydrogenation reactions, with emphasis on the effects of dopants and vacancies on electronic structure, theoretical modeling challenges, and process optimization considerations, have been extensively studied \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. NiNbO catalysts with varying Nb contents have been optimized for ethane oxidative dehydrogenation, achieving 33% conversion and 78% selectivity to ethylene at 15% Nb content \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. The efficiency is attributed to surface interaction between NiO and amorphous Nb2O5, and the reduction of NiO oxygen over-stoichiometry due to oxalate ions \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. Citric acid-assisted preparation of Nb-doped NiO nanocomposites impacts their electronic structure and catalytic efficiency in ethane oxidative dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]. A niobium-rich mixed oxide layer inhibits NiO reducibility, affecting redox properties \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]. Optimal activity and selectivity are achieved at 34 at.% Nb, balancing high activity with stability \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]. Low-temperature treatment of pure NiO creates a non-stoichiometric phase with cationic deficiency \[10.1016/j.apcata.2007.02.030\]. Upon Nb doping, two phases emerge: a NiO phase with Nb cations forming a NiNb solid solution, and a distorted Nb-rich phase, a precursor for NiNb2O6 \[10.1016/j.apcata.2007.02.030\]. The interaction between niobium ions and structural defects in NiO enhances selectivity to ethylene in ethane oxidative dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.apcata.2007.02.030\]. Dopant concentrations and vacancy formation in nickel oxide catalysts alter their electronic structure, impacting catalytic performance in oxidative dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]. Lower valence dopants increase non-stoichiometric oxygen, enhancing ethylene selectivity, while higher valence dopants reduce it \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]. The valence of dopants modifies the lattice constant and the lability of oxygen species \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]. A linear relationship exists between oxygen exchange rates and ethane consumption, confirming the role of oxygen species in ethane activation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]. The selectivity to ethylene increases with higher dopant valence \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]. Dopant concentrations and preparation methods in nickel oxide catalysts significantly influence their electronic structure and catalytic efficiency in oxidative dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1021/cr300418s_9\]. Doping with Fe or Zr boosts surface area, enhancing catalytic activity \[10.1021/cr300418s_9\]. Low-valence dopants facilitate oxide reduction, improving performance \[10.1021/cr300418s_9\]. V doping increases surface area compared to undoped TiO2 \[10.1021/cr300418s_9\]. Zr-doped NiO exhibits enhanced selectivity to ethylene, reduced reducibility, and stronger surface oxygen binding, aligning with theoretical predictions \[10.1021/cr300418s_9\]. Niobium-doped nickel oxide catalysts exhibit enhanced performance in oxidative dehydrogenation reactions, such as ethane to ethylene conversion, due to altered electronic structures \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.003\]. Niobium doping reduces activation energy and changes the oxygen replenishment mechanism, leading to selective ethane conversion \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.003\]. Nickel oxide-based catalysts, prepared by incorporating niobium through the evaporation method, exhibit enhanced catalytic performance in oxidative dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]. The Nb/Ni ratio, varying from 0 to 0.67, significantly influences the electronic structure and catalytic activity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]. Niobium incorporation leads to a contraction of the NiO lattice, affecting the lattice constant and electronic properties \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]. High Nb loading reaches an upper limit for Nb5+ cation dissolution, impacting the electronic structure \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]. The incorporation of niobium inhibits crystallization, resulting in smaller grain sizes, but at high Nb loading, segregation of NiO and Nb2O5 phases occurs \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]. The introduction of niobium significantly increases the total acidity, generating new acid sites of weak and moderate strength, and enhances the reducibility of the catalysts \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]. A 10% introduction of niobium leads to a 300% increase in acidity compared to pure NiO \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]. The Ni0.85Nb0.15 catalyst shows maximum solubility of niobium cations, confirming the acidity is related to NiONb bond formation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]. Niobium acts as an electron donor, reducing the p+ hole concentration in the NiO acceptor \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]. The NiNbO mixed oxide catalysts exhibit high activity in ethane oxidative dehydrogenation, with a 100% increase in per weight reactivity for a Nb/Ni ratio of 0.111 compared to pure NiO \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]. Incorporating niobium increases ethene selectivity from 20% for NiO to 80% for the Ni0.9Nb0.1 catalyst \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]. The developed NiNbO catalysts for ethane ODH combine high activity at low temperatures and high ethene selectivity at high conversion levels, with stable catalytic performance \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\].

at.% Nb时，实现了最佳的活性和选择性，平衡了高活性与稳定性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]。低温处理纯NiO产生了一个具有阳离子缺陷的非化学计量相\[10.1016/j.apcata.2007.02.030\]。铌掺杂后，形成了两个相：一个NiO相，其中Nb阳离子形成NiNb固溶体，以及一个扭曲的Nb富集相，是NiNb2O6的前体\[10.1016/j.apcata.2007.02.030\]。铌离子与NiO中的结构缺陷之间的相互作用提高了乙烷氧化脱氢中对乙烯的选择性\[10.1016/j.apcata.2007.02.030\]。镍氧化物催化剂中的掺杂浓度和空位形成改变了它们的电子结构，影响了氧化脱氢反应中的催化性能\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]。低价掺杂剂增加了非化学计量氧，提高了乙烯的选择性，而高价掺杂剂则减少了它\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]。掺杂剂的价态修改了晶格常数和氧物种的活性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]。氧交换率与乙烷消耗之间存在线性关系，确认了氧物种在乙烷活化中的作用\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]。乙烯的选择性随着掺杂剂价态的增加而增加\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]。镍氧化物催化剂中的掺杂浓度和制备方法对其在氧化脱氢反应中的电子结构和催化效率有显著影响\[10.1021/cr300418s_9\]。Fe或Zr的掺杂提高了表面积，增强了催化活性\[10.1021/cr300418s_9\]。低价掺杂剂促进了氧化物的还原，提高了性能\[10.1021/cr300418s_9\]。V掺杂与未掺杂TiO2相比，增加了表面积\[10.1021/cr300418s_9\]。Zr掺杂的NiO表现出对乙烯的增强选择性，降低了氧化物的还原性，并更强烈地结合了表面氧，与理论预测一致\[10.1021/cr300418s_9\]。铌掺杂的镍氧化物催化剂由于电子结构的改变，在氧化脱氢反应中，如乙烷转化为乙烯，表现出增强的性能\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.003\]。铌掺杂降低了活化能，改变了氧补给机制，导致了乙烷的有选择性转化\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.003\]。通过蒸发方法掺入铌的镍氧化物基催化剂在氧化脱氢反应中表现出增强的催化性能\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]。从0到0.67变化的Nb/Ni比显著影响了电子结构和催化活性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]。铌的掺入导致NiO晶格的收缩，影响了晶格常数和电子性质\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]。高铌负载达到了Nb5+阳离子溶解的上限，影响了电子结构\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]。铌的掺入抑制了结晶，导致了更小的晶粒尺寸，但在高铌负载下，NiO和Nb2O5相的分离发生\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]。铌的引入显著增加了总酸度，产生了弱和中等强度的新酸位点，并增强了催化剂的还原性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]。10%的铌引入导致与纯NiO相比，酸度增加了300%\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]。Ni0.85Nb0.15催化剂显示了铌阳离子的最大溶解度，确认了酸度与NiONb键的形成有关\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]。铌作为电子供体，减少了NiO受体中的p+空穴浓度\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]。NiNbO混合氧化物催化剂在乙烷氧化脱氢中表现出高活性，对于Nb/Ni比为0.111，与纯NiO相比，每重量活性增加了100%\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]。掺入铌将乙烯的选择性从NiO的20%提高到Ni0.9Nb0.1催化剂的80%\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]。开发的NiNbO催化剂结合了在低温下的高活性和在高转化率下的高乙烯选择性，具有稳定的催化性能\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]。
