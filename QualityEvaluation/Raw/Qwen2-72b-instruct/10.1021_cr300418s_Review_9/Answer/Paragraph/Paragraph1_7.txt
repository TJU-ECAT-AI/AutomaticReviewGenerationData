The preparation, properties, and catalytic applications of doped nickel oxide materials in oxidative dehydrogenation reactions, with emphasis on the effects of dopants and vacancies on electronic structure, theoretical modeling challenges, and process optimization considerations, have been extensively studied \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. NiNbO catalysts, with varying Nb contents, have been optimized for ethane oxidative dehydrogenation, achieving 33% conversion and 78% selectivity to ethylene at 15% Nb content \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. The efficiency is linked to the surface interaction between NiO and amorphous Nb2O5, reducing NiO oxygen over-stoichiometry. Optimal activity at 15% Nb is attributed to reduced particle size, preventing sintering. Above this threshold, an inactive NiNb2O6 phase forms \[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]. Citric acid-based preparation of Nb-doped NiO nanocomposites affects their electronic structure and catalytic performance in ethane oxidative dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]. The Nb-rich phase inhibits NiO reducibility, impacting redox properties. Optimal activity and selectivity are achieved at 34 at.% Nb, balancing high activity with stability \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]. High Nb loadings lead to catalyst deactivation, highlighting the importance of the preparation method in optimizing structural and catalytic properties for improved oxidative dehydrogenation performance \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]. Low-temperature treatment of pure NiO creates a non-stoichiometric phase with cationic deficiency \[10.1016/j.apcata.2007.02.030\]. Upon Nb doping, two phases emerge: a NiO phase with Nb cations forming a NiNb solid solution, and a distorted Nb-rich phase, precursor to NiNb2O6 \[10.1016/j.apcata.2007.02.030\]. The interaction between niobium ions and structural defects in NiO correlates with high ethylene selectivity in ethane oxidative dehydrogenation, as vacancies generate strong oxidizing oxygen species, leading to ethane's total oxidation to CO2 \[10.1016/j.apcata.2007.02.030\].

Dopant concentrations and vacancy formation in nickel oxide catalysts significantly impact their electronic structure and catalytic performance in oxidative dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]. Lower valence dopants increase non-stoichiometric oxygen, enhancing ethylene selectivity, while higher valence dopants reduce it, favoring COx formation. The valence of dopants modifies the lattice constant, influencing the formation of electrophilic oxygen species, which are crucial for ethane activation. High valence dopants create more strongly bonded oxygen species, impacting their lability. A linear relationship exists between oxygen exchange rates and ethane consumption, confirming the active role of oxygen species in ethane conversion. The inverse trend between non-stoichiometric oxygen and ethane selectivity with increasing dopant valence supports the role of oxygen species in selectivity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\].

Dopant concentrations and preparation methods in nickel oxide-based catalysts significantly affect their electronic structure and catalytic performance in oxidative dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1021/cr300418s_9\]. Systematic studies are scarce, with few cases of a single host oxide being doped with various elements and tested under diverse conditions. Doping with Fe or Zr boosts surface area, enhancing catalytic activity. Low-valence dopants (LVDs) facilitate oxide reduction, potentially improving performance. Doping with V increases surface area compared to undoped TiO2. Ni-doped ceria shows superior conversion for ethylbenzene to styrene, while Zr-doped NiO improves selectivity to ethylene, makes the oxide less reducible, and binds surface oxygen more strongly, aligning with theoretical predictions \[10.1021/cr300418s_9\].

Niobium-doped nickel oxide catalysts exhibit enhanced performance in oxidative dehydrogenation reactions, such as ethane to ethylene, due to reduced activation energy and altered oxygen species \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.003\]. Niobium doping decreases electrophilic active oxygen, favoring selective ethane conversion. The key challenge lies in understanding complex reaction mechanisms, predicting dopant effects on electronic structure, optimizing conditions for yield and selectivity, and ensuring safe operation. Niobium fills cationic vacancies, reducing active sites for oxygen dissociation, and promotes diffusion, leading to nucleophilic oxygen species selective for ethylene production \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.003\].

Nickel oxide-based catalysts prepared by the evaporation method, with varying Nb/Ni ratios, exhibit altered electronic structures and catalytic performance in oxidative dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]. Incorporating niobium (Nb) into nickel oxide (NiO) causes lattice contraction, evidenced by a decrease in lattice constant up to a Nb/Ni ratio of 0.176. This impacts the electronic structure and catalytic activity. High Nb loading reaches an upper limit for Nb5+ dissolution in the NiO lattice. NiO crystallizes in a cubic rock salt structure, and niobium incorporation modifies this, affecting electronic properties and catalytic activity. Niobium inhibits crystallization, leading to smaller grain sizes, but at high Nb loading, larger crystals form due to phase segregation. The introduction of Nb significantly broadens the reduction profiles, indicating catalyst modifications. Niobium causes a 300% increase in total acidity compared to pure NiO, generating new acid sites that influence catalytic performance. The incorporation of 10% Nb leads to an almost 300% increase in acidity, demonstrating its pronounced effect. The Ni0.85Nb0.15 catalyst shows maximum solubility of niobium cations, confirming acidity is related to NiONb bond formation. XPS determines surface composition, providing insights into electronic structure and catalytic activity. Electrical conductivity measurements offer information on structural defects and oxidizing species, crucial for understanding catalytic performance. Incorporating Nb5+ in the p-type NiO lattice suggests n-doping, modifying electronic structure and catalytic activity. Niobium oxide is an n-type semiconductor with nonstoichiometry, impacting electronic properties and catalytic performance. Incorporating Nb reduces the p+ concentration, affecting excess oxygen accommodation and catalytic performance. The activity of NiNbO catalysts in ethane oxidative dehydrogenation shows enhanced reactivity with Nb addition. A good ODH catalyst effectively converts ethane to ethylene at low temperatures with high selectivity, influenced by preparation methods and dopant concentrations. Incorporating Nb leads to an 80% ethene selectivity increase from 20% for NiO to 80% for the lowest Nb content catalyst, demonstrating its impact on selectivity. Developed NiNbO catalysts for ethane ODH combine high activity at low temperatures and high ethene selectivity at high conversion levels, with stable catalytic performance. Niobium acts as an electron donor, reducing the p+ hole concentration, affecting electronic structure and catalytic performance. The elimination of nonstoichiometric oxygen due to Nb doping is likely responsible for high ethene selectivity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\].

NiNbO催化剂,通过改变Nb含量,已被优化用于乙烷氧化脱氢,在15% Nb含量时达到33%的乙烷转化率和78%的乙烯选择性\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]。效率与NiO和无定形Nb2O5之间的表面相互作用有关,减少了NiO的过量氧。15% Nb时的最优活性归因于减小的活性相粒径,防止了烧结。超过这一阈值,形成不活跃的NiNb2O6相\[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.004\]。基于柠檬酸的制备方法影响Nb掺杂NiO纳米复合材料的电子结构和催化性能在乙烷氧化脱氢中的表现\[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]。Nb富集相抑制了NiO的还原性,影响了氧化还原性质。在34 at.% Nb时,实现了最优的活性和选择性,平衡了高活性与稳定性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]。高Nb负载导致催化剂失活,突出了制备方法在优化结构和催化性能以改善氧化脱氢性能方面的重要性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.10.005\]。低温处理纯NiO产生了一个具有阳离子缺陷的非化学计量相\[10.1016/j.apcata.2007.02.030\]。Nb掺杂形成了两个相:一个NiO相,其中Nb阳离子形成NiNb固溶体,以及一个扭曲的Nb富集相,是NiNb2O6的前体\[10.1016/j.apcata.2007.02.030\]。铌离子与NiO中的结构缺陷之间的相互作用与乙烷氧化脱氢中高乙烯选择性相关,因为空位产生强烈的氧化氧物种,导致乙烷完全氧化为CO2\[10.1016/j.apcata.2007.02.030\]。

镍氧化物催化剂中的掺杂浓度和空位形成对其在氧化脱氢反应中的电子结构和催化性能有显著影响\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]。低价掺杂剂增加非化学计量氧,增强乙烯选择性,而高价掺杂剂减少它,有利于COx的形成。掺杂剂的价态修改了晶格常数,影响了电荷性氧物种的形成,这对乙烷的活化至关重要。高价掺杂剂产生更强烈结合的氧物种,影响它们的活性。氧交换率和乙烷消耗之间存在线性关系,确认了氧物种在乙烷转化中的积极作用。随着掺杂剂价态的增加,非化学计量氧与乙烷选择性之间的逆趋势支持了氧物种在选择性中的作用\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.12.004\]。

镍氧化物基催化剂中的掺杂浓度和制备方法对其在氧化脱氢反应中的电子结构和催化性能有显著影响\[10.1021/cr300418s_9\]。系统研究很少,很少有单一的氧化物被各种元素掺杂并在不同的条件下进行测试。用Fe或Zr掺杂会增加每克的表面积,增强催化活性。低价掺杂剂(LVDs)促进氧化物还原,可能改善性能。用V掺杂与未掺杂TiO2相比增加了表面积。Ni掺杂的CeO2显示出乙基苯转化为苯乙烯的优越转化率,而Zr掺杂的NiO提高了对乙烯的选择性,使氧化物的还原性降低,并更强烈地结合表面氧,与理论预测一致\[10.1021/cr300418s_9\]。

铌掺杂的镍氧化物催化剂由于降低了活化能和改变了氧物种,在氧化脱氢反应中表现出增强的性能,如乙烷到乙烯\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.003\]。铌掺杂降低了电荷性活性氧,有利于选择性乙烷转化。关键挑战在于理解复杂的反应机制,预测掺杂剂对电子结构的影响,优化产量和选择性的条件,并确保安全操作。铌填充了阳离子空位,减少了氧解离的活性位点,并促进了扩散,导致选择性用于乙烯生产的亲核氧物种\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.003\]。

通过蒸发方法制备的镍氧化物基催化剂,具有不同的Nb/Ni比,在氧化脱氢反应中表现出改变的电子结构和催化性能\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]。将铌(Nb)掺入镍氧化物(NiO)中导致晶格收缩,晶格常数减少至Nb/Ni比为0.176。这影响了电子结构和催化活性。高Nb负载达到了Nb5+在NiO晶格中溶解的上限。NiO以立方岩盐结构结晶,铌的掺入修改了这一点,影响了电子性质和催化活性。铌抑制了结晶,导致更小的晶粒尺寸,但在高Nb负载下,由于相分离,形成了更大的晶体。铌的引入显著拓宽了还原曲线,表明催化剂的修改。与纯NiO相比,铌使总酸性增加了300%,产生了影响催化性能的新酸位。10% Nb的掺入导致酸性几乎增加了300%,展示了其显著的影响。Ni0.85Nb0.15催化剂显示了铌离子的最大溶解度,证实了酸性与NiONb键的形成有关。XPS确定了表面组成,提供了对电子结构和催化活性的见解。电导率测量提供了关于结构缺陷和氧化物种的信息,对理解催化性能至关重要。将Nb5+掺入p型NiO晶格表明了n掺杂,修改了电子结构和催化活性。铌氧化物是一种具有非化学计量的n型半导体,影响了电子性质和催化性能。掺入Nb减少了p+浓度,影响了过量氧的容纳和催化性能。在乙烷氧化脱氢中,即使NiO中少量的Nb也显著提高了反应性和乙烯选择性。一个好的ODH催化剂有效地在低温下将乙烷转化为乙烯,具有高选择性,受制备方法和掺杂浓度的影响。掺入Nb将乙烯选择性从NiO的20%增加到最低Nb含量催化剂的80%,展示了其对选择性的影响。开发的NiNbO催化剂结合了在低温下的高活性和在高转化率下的高乙烯选择性,具有稳定的催化性能。铌作为电子供体,减少了p+空穴浓度,影响了电子结构和催化性能。由于Nb掺杂消除了非化学计量氧,这可能是高乙烯选择性的原因\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.002\]。
