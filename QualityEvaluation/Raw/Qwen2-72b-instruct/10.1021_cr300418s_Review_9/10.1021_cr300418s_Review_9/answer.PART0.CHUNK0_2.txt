<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1.The preparation methods, dopant concentrations, and vacancy formation in nickel oxide-based catalysts significantly influence their electronic structure and catalytic performance in oxidative dehydrogenation reactions. Doping with Fe or V increases the surface area per gram, which can enhance catalytic activity. The presence of a low-valence dopant (LVD) facilitates the reduction of the oxide, making it a better oxidant. The performance of Ni-doped ceria catalysts can be optimized by adjusting the Ni content and the method of preparation. The method of preparation can significantly affect the catalytic performance, as demonstrated by the difference in conversion between Ce0.9Ni0.1O2 prepared by oxalate-gel coprecipitation and the template-assisted method. The presence of an LVD, such as Al, is expected to make the surface a better oxidant. Doping with Zr increases the specific area of the catalyst and improves the selectivity to ethylene, making the oxide less reducible and binding surface oxygen more strongly to the oxide surface. Doping with a small amount of Zr increases the temperature at which hydrogen consumption by the oxide begins.
2.Key challenges in theoretical modeling include accurately predicting the effects of dopants on the electronic structure and catalytic activity of nickel oxide-based catalysts. Process optimization requires a thorough understanding of the reaction mechanisms and the influence of dopant concentration and preparation method on catalyst performance. Balancing efficiency, selectivity, and safety in these catalytic systems involves optimizing reaction conditions to maximize conversion and selectivity while minimizing the risk of explosion and fire, especially when using oxygen as an oxidant.
