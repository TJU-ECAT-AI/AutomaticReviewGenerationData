                Carbon-based materials, including coke deposits, carbon nanofibers (CNFs), and hybrid nanocarbons, exhibit unexpected catalytic activity in alkane dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1021/cr5002436_4\]. The VOx/Al2O3 catalyst, when completely encapsulated by coke deposits, remains active in butane dehydrogenation, suggesting that the carbon species on the catalyst surface may be catalyzing the reaction \[10.1021/cr5002436_4\]. The highest activity is observed at 700 degrees Celsius, where the carbon deposits have a very ordered structure \[10.1021/cr5002436_4\]. CNFs, tested under similar conditions, also prove to be catalytically active \[10.1021/cr5002436_4\]. Radicals formed on the surface of coke are believed to facilitate the dehydrogenation reaction, although at high temperatures, noncatalytic conversion of propane is also relatively high \[10.1021/cr5002436_4\]. Hybrid nanocarbons, with a diamond core and a graphitic shell, show activity in propane dehydrogenation, attributed to the presence of ketone groups or structural defects in the graphitic material \[10.1021/cr5002436_4\]. These findings suggest that carbon-based materials can be developed as novel and efficient catalysts for alkane dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1021/cr5002436_4\].

                Carbonaceous materials, including coke deposits and carbon nanofibers, exhibit unexpected catalytic activity in alkane dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]. This phenomenon is confirmed through a comparison of the activity of coked VOx/Al2O3 and carbon nanofibres \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]. The nature of the carbonaceous material deposited on VOx/Al2O3 during n-butane dehydrogenation shows a strong dependence on reaction temperature \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]. At high reaction temperatures, much more extensive and highly ordered coke is deposited \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]. The deposited carbon can be considered conducting in nature and as such highly ordered \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]. The initial carbon deposits are most likely formed through the catalytic action of VOx units breaking down butane molecules \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]. Despite the VOx sites being rendered inaccessible to n-butane molecules, the catalyst remains highly active, even after 180 min on stream \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]. The yield of C4 hydrocarbons reaches its greatest value at this time \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]. The deposited coke can be characterised as curved graphene-like sheets \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]. The activity of CNFs towards n-butane DH is similar to that of coked VOx/Al2O3 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]. Neither the CNFs nor the coked VOx/Al2O3 exhibit deactivation as a function of increasing exposure to n-butane \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]. The dehydrogenation of n-butane can be added to the list of reactions in which carbonaceous materials can provide high activity and selectivity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\].

                Nitrogen-doped carbon nanotubes (CNTs) exhibit enhanced catalytic activity in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODH) \[10.1039/c3cc41500g\]. The graphitic nitrogen in the CNTs plays a key role in this enhancement by speeding up the activation of oxygen and decreasing the overall activation energy of the reaction \[10.1039/c3cc41500g\]. The doped CNT catalysts with suitable content of graphitic nitrogen are stable and more active for the ODH of propane \[10.1039/c3cc41500g\]. The graphitic nitrogen reduces the overall activation energy and increases the reaction rate \[10.1039/c3cc41500g\]. The improved surface coverage of active sites for propane conversion is believed to be the reason for the enhanced ODH activity \[10.1039/c3cc41500g\]. The doped nitrogen atoms may also facilitate propene to desorb from the surface due to the repulsion between the electron-rich graphitic surface and unsaturated C=C bonds of propene \[10.1039/c3cc41500g\]. A linear relationship between activation energy and the atomic abundance of graphitic N can be fitted to quantitatively describe the role of graphitic N in decreasing the activation energy of ODH \[10.1039/c3cc41500g\]. This concept can be applied to other reactions such as selective oxidation, electrochemical oxygen reduction, peroxidation, etc. \[10.1039/c3cc41500g\].

                Ordered mesoporous carbons, without any metal, have been shown to be robust catalysts for the direct dehydrogenation of propane to propylene, without the need for auxiliary steam \[10.1039/c1cc12806j\]. They exhibit high activity, selectivity, and long-term stability, outperforming nanostructured carbons \[10.1039/c1cc12806j\]. The presence of surface carbonyl groups is crucial for the dehydrogenation activity \[10.1039/c1cc12806j\]. The large and well-ordered porosity of mesoporous carbon catalysts is beneficial for mass transport in catalytic reactions, leading to long-term stability \[10.1039/c1cc12806j\]. The stable propylene yield of over 38% and the steady propylene selectivity of about 88% over 100 hours of reaction are promising for industrial applications, especially considering the absence of auxiliary steam and the resulting energy savings \[10.1039/c1cc12806j\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The unexpected catalytic activity of carbon-based materials in alkane dehydrogenation reactions, as highlighted by the studies discussed, opens up new avenues for the development of efficient and novel catalysts. The findings suggest that the surface chemistry and structure of carbon-based materials, such as the presence of radicals, ketone groups, structural defects, and graphitic nitrogen, play a crucial role in facilitating dehydrogenation reactions. The temperature dependence of catalytic activity and the stability of these materials under prolonged exposure to reactants are also significant factors to consider in catalyst design. Future research efforts could focus on the optimization of carbon-based materials for specific dehydrogenation reactions, the exploration of new carbon-based materials with tailored surface properties, and the integration of these materials with traditional metal-based catalysts to enhance overall catalytic performance. Additionally, the development of methods to control the deposition and structure of carbon deposits could lead to the creation of more efficient and stable catalysts. The potential of carbon-based materials to reduce energy consumption and increase selectivity in dehydrogenation reactions could have significant implications for industrial processes, particularly in the production of propylene and other valuable hydrocarbons.

                碳基材料，包括焦炭沉积物、碳纳米纤维（CNFs）和混合纳米碳，表现出在烷烃脱氢反应中的意外催化活性\[10.1021/cr5002436_4\]。当VOx/Al2O3催化剂完全被焦炭沉积物包裹时，它在丁烷脱氢中仍然保持活性，这表明催化剂表面的碳物种可能正在催化反应\[10.1021/cr5002436_4\]。在700摄氏度时观察到最高的活性，此时碳沉积物具有非常有序的结构\[10.1021/cr5002436_4\]。在类似条件下测试的CNFs也被证明具有催化活性\[10.1021/cr5002436_4\]。在焦炭表面形成的自由基被认为促进了脱氢反应，尽管在高温下，丙烷的非催化转化也相对较高\[10.1021/cr5002436_4\]。具有金刚石核心和石墨壳的混合纳米碳在丙烷脱氢中表现出活性，这归因于石墨材料中酮基团或结构缺陷的存在\[10.1021/cr5002436_4\]。这些发现表明，碳基材料可以被开发为新型和高效的烷烃脱氢反应催化剂\[10.1021/cr5002436_4\]。

                碳质材料，包括焦炭沉积物和碳纳米纤维，在烷烃脱氢反应中表现出意外的催化活性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]。通过比较coked VOx/Al2O3和碳纳米纤维的活性，这一现象得到了证实\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]。在n-丁烷脱氢过程中沉积在VOx/Al2O3上的碳质材料的性质强烈依赖于反应温度\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]。在高反应温度下，例如973 K，沉积了更多且高度有序的焦炭\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]。沉积的碳可以被认为是导电性质的，因此高度有序\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]。最初的碳沉积物很可能通过VOx单元分解丁烷分子的催化作用形成\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]。尽管VOx位点对n-丁烷分子变得不可接近，催化剂在180分钟后仍然保持高度活性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]。此时C4烃的产量达到最大值\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]。沉积的焦炭可以被描述为弯曲的石墨状片\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]。CNFs对n-丁烷DH的活性与coked VOx/Al2O3相似\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]。无论是CNFs还是coked VOx/Al2O3，在增加暴露于n-丁烷的情况下都不会出现失活\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]。n-丁烷的脱氢可以添加到碳质材料可以提供高活性和选择性的反应列表中\[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.11.016\]。

                氮掺杂的碳纳米管（CNTs）在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODH）中表现出增强的催化活性\[10.1039/c3cc41500g\]。CNTs中的石墨氮在这一增强中起着关键作用，通过加速氧气的激活和降低反应的总活化能\[10.1039/c3cc41500g\]。具有合适石墨氮含量的掺杂CNT催化剂稳定且对丙烷的ODH更活跃\[10.1039/c3cc41500g\]。石墨氮降低了总活化能并增加了反应速率\[10.1039/c3cc41500g\]。丙烷转化的活性位点表面覆盖率的提高被认为是增强ODH活性的原因\[10.1039/c3cc41500g\]。掺杂的N原子也可能由于电子丰富的石墨表面和丙烯不饱和C=C键之间的排斥作用，促进丙烯从表面脱附\[10.1039/c3cc41500g\]。活化能与石墨N的原子丰度之间的线性关系可以被拟合，以定量描述石墨N在降低ODH活化能中的作用\[10.1039/c3cc41500g\]。这一概念可以应用于其他反应，如选择性氧化、电化学氧气还原、过氧化等\[10.1039/c3cc41500g\]。

                无金属的有序介孔碳被证明是直接将丙烷脱氢为丙烯的稳健催化剂，无需任何辅助蒸汽\[10.1039/c1cc12806j\]。它们表现出高活性、选择性和长期稳定性，优于纳米结构碳\[10.1039/c1cc12806j\]。表面羰基的存在对于脱氢活性至关重要\[10.1039/c1cc12806j\]。有序介孔碳催化剂的大而有序的孔隙度有利于催化反应中的质量传递，导致长期稳定性\[10.1039/c1cc12806j\]。超过38%的稳定丙烯产量和大约88%的稳定丙烯选择性在100小时的反应中是工业应用的有希望的指标，特别是考虑到辅助蒸汽的缺失和随后的能源节省\[10.1039/c1cc12806j\]。

                综合理解与未来展望：碳基材料在烷烃脱氢反应中的意外催化活性，如所讨论的研究所强调的，为开发高效和新型催化剂开辟了新的途径。这些发现表明，碳基材料的表面化学和结构，如自由基、酮基团、结构缺陷和石墨氮的存在，在促进脱氢反应中起着关键作用。催化活性的温度依赖性和这些材料在长时间暴露于反应物下的稳定性也是催化剂设计中需要考虑的重要因素。未来的研究工作可以集中在优化碳基材料以适应特定的脱氢反应，探索具有定制表面特性的新型碳基材料，以及将这些材料与传统的金属基催化剂结合以增强整体催化性能。此外，开发控制碳沉积物的沉积和结构的方法可能会导致更有效和稳定的催化剂的创建。碳基材料在减少能源消耗和提高脱氢反应选择性方面的潜力可能对工业过程有重大影响，特别是在丙烯和其他有价值的烃类的生产中。
