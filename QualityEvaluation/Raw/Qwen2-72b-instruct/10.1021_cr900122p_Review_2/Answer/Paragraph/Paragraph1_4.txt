    Metal-based catalysts, particularly vanadium oxides and metal-exchanged zeolites, play a pivotal role in alkane transformations, including oxidative dehydrogenation and zeolite-catalyzed reactions \[10.1021/cr00083a003\]. The efficiency of these catalysts in alkane transformations is influenced by their composition, structure, and reaction conditions \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. The loading of V2O5 and the size of VOx domains are critical factors that boost activity and selectivity in alkane transformations \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Selectivity peaks at intermediate loadings, indicating an optimal balance between activity and selectivity \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Surface reduction of vanadium oxides increases propylene selectivity, with N2O promoting a reduced and selective surface \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Among the supports, V2O5-ZrO2 shows the highest reactivity \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Additives such as P, K, and Ca modify the properties of metal-based catalysts, impacting their performance \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Alkali dopants, while reducing activity, increase propylene selectivity \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. VOx-SBA-15 is efficient with isolated sites, enhancing its catalytic performance \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Mo-based catalysts peak at 4.5 Mo/nm2, with alkali dopants inhibiting MoOx reduction, slowing CH activation but boosting propylene selectivity \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\].
