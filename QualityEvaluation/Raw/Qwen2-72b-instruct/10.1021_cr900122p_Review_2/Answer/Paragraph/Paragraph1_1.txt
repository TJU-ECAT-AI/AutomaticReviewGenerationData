    Metal-based catalysts, particularly vanadium oxides, play a pivotal role in alkane transformations, including oxidative dehydrogenation and zeolite-catalyzed processes \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. The efficiency of these catalysts in oxidative dehydrogenation is influenced by the size of VOx domains, surface reduction, and the type of support \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Selectivity peaks at intermediate loadings, and surface reduction boosts propylene selectivity \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. The presence of N2O enhances reduction and selectivity \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Silica support lowers propane combustion \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Additives modify acid-base and redox properties, impacting activity \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Alkali dopants reduce activity but increase selectivity \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. The surface density and domain size of Mo affect oxidative dehydrogenation activity \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Alkali alters MoOx structure, enhancing selectivity \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\].
