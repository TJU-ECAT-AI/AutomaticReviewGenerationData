1.The literature does not provide specific information on how the composition, structure, and reaction conditions of metal-based catalysts influence the mechanisms and efficiency of alkane transformations. However, it does discuss the use of zeolites as catalysts for selective oxidation reactions, particularly the oxidative hydroxylation of aromatics using dinitrogen monoxide. The best results were obtained for the oxidation of benzene to phenol over Fe-containing ZSM-5 zeolites, which proceeds with nearly 100% selectivity at 25-30% benzene conversion. The efficiency of these catalysts is influenced by the nature of catalytic activity of zeolites, the role of iron, the chemical composition of the zeolite matrix, and the specific features of dinitrogen monoxide as an oxidant. Further progress in this promising direction, the hydroxylation of aromatics, can provide new efficient technologies in organic synthesis.
