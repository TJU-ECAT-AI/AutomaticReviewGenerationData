1.The literature does not provide specific information on how the composition, structure, and reaction conditions of metal-based catalysts, including supported vanadium oxides and metal-exchanged zeolites, influence the mechanisms and efficiency of alkane transformations, particularly in processes such as oxidative dehydrogenation and zeolite-catalyzed reactions. However, it does discuss the synthesis, structure, and catalytic properties of various gallium-substituted zeolites and mesoporous silicates, which can be used in alkane transformations. The efficiency and selectivity of these catalysts can be influenced by factors such as the type of zeolite structure, the Si/Me ratio, the distribution of gallium in the framework, the presence of other metal ions, the reaction temperature, and the presence of water or other polar molecules. For example, the literature mentions that the aromatization of alkanes over gallium-substituted ZSM-5 catalysts can be influenced by the location of gallium species at/near cation positions in the windows, channels, and cavities of the zeolite lattice, which can give a high degree of dispersion and an enhanced stability of dispersed species. The literature also discusses the influence of thermal or hydrothermal treatments on the distribution of gallium in gallosilicate zeolite structures, which can affect the stability of framework gallium and the catalytic properties of the zeolite.
