1.The literature does not provide specific information on how the composition, structure, and reaction conditions of metal-based catalysts, including supported vanadium oxides and metal-exchanged zeolites, influence the mechanisms and efficiency of alkane transformations, particularly in processes such as oxidative dehydrogenation and zeolite-catalyzed reactions, and what factors contribute to their industrial significance. However, it does discuss the theoretical study of methane adsorption on Zn-exchanged zeolites using density functional theory (DFT). The study found that the Zn(II) cation is most exposed to probe molecules when sitting on the 4T ring of zeolites, and that methane prefers to adsorb physically in a 3-fold configuration on Zn(II) at both 4T and 5T rings. The [ZnOZn]2 site appears to be one of the active forms of the Zn cation on zeolites with a low Si/Al ratio. This site is more thermodynamically stable than both Zn(II) on 5T and (ZnO)4. In the absence of Brnsted sites, does not decompose into Zn(II) or [ZnOZn]2. Owing to its size, this oxide cluster may only be stable in special positions inside the pores.
