      <English>
          The literature does not provide a comprehensive analysis of the oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes (ODH), including the interplay between various catalysts, challenges in achieving high selectivity and conversion, proposed reaction mechanisms, and the role of site isolation in addressing selectivity issues. However, it does discuss the mechanism of CH activation by oxidative addition as a possible route to catalytic dehydrogenation over Cr(III)silica surface sites. The mechanism involves a formal double oxidation of chromium and a minimum energy crossing point (MECP) between the quartet spin potential energy surface of Cr(III) and the doublet spin potential energy surface of Cr(V). The ethylhydridochromium(V) complex formed is unstable with respect to the back-reaction, and β-hydrogen transfer must take place in the extension of the CH activation step to complete the catalytic cycle. The computed activation energy for this mechanism is high, around 220 kJ mol-1, making CH activation by oxidative addition an unlikely mechanism for dehydrogenation in this system. Site isolation is not explicitly discussed, but the literature does compare the reactivity of different Cr(III)silica surface sites, including those with two and three oxygen bridges to the silica surface.
