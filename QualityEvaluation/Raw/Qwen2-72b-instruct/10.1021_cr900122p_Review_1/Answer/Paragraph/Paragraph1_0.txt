The oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes (ODH) is a critical process for the conversion of alkanes to valuable olefins, with significant economic implications due to the vast reserves of alkanes [\[10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00053-x\]]. The efficiency of ODH is influenced by various catalysts, including bulk metal oxides and supported systems, with the nature of the alkane dictating catalyst choice [\[10.1080/01614940600631413\]]. Challenges in achieving high selectivity and conversion are addressed through understanding reaction mechanisms such as C-H bond activation, M-alkyl intermediates, and 2H-abstraction [\[10.1016/s0920-5861(96)00103-4\]]. Active sites, often molecular 'inorganic ensembles' of metallic oxide atoms, significantly impact ODH selectivity and conversion rates [\[10.1016/s0920-5861(96)00103-4\]]. Site isolation stabilizes specific sites that favor desired reactions over side reactions [\[10.1016/s0920-5861(96)00103-4\]]. Monomeric species like MoO42- or VO43- exhibit acidic features leading to total oxidation, whereas low-size polymeric species show better selectivity for partial oxidation reactions [\[10.1016/s0920-5861(96)00103-4\]].

Vanadium oxide catalysts' performance in propane ODH is optimized with intermediate-sized VOx domains, balancing activity and accessibility [\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]]. Selectivity to olefins peaks at intermediate loadings, correlating with a monolayer structure [\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]]. COx formation in ethane ODH is attributed to adsorbed oxygen species [\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]]. The reduction of vanadium during steady-state ODH is lower than stoichiometric reduction [\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]]. Both isolated and polymeric vanadium species are active, with polymerized VO4 species more extensively reduced, leading to higher selectivity to propylene with greater surface reduction [\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]]. The support significantly impacts vanadium oxide reactivity, with silica-supported catalysts being more selective [\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]]. Acid and redox sites in VCoAPO-18 are crucial for high ethylene selectivity in ethane ODH [\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]]. Alkali additives influence MoOx domain structure and catalytic properties, affecting CH bond activation and selectivity to propylene [\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]].

ODH of alkanes faces the challenge of faster alkene oxidation than alkane conversion, necessitating low conversions for high selectivity [\[10.1021/cr900122p_1\]]. Bulk metal oxides like V2O5 offer low yields, whereas supported systems provide higher activity and selectivity [\[10.1021/cr900122p_1\]]. Among various systems, MOP, VMgOx, VSiZrOx, and CrSiMO show high selectivities [\[10.1021/cr900122p_1\]]. CH bond activation is the rate-determining step, leading to proposed Malkyl intermediates that decompose to 1-alkenes via β-H abstraction [\[10.1021/cr900122p_1\]]. An alternative 2H-abstraction mechanism is also proposed [\[10.1021/cr900122p_1\]]. Site isolation is crucial for selectivity between dehydrogenation and oxygenated product formation [\[10.1021/cr900122p_1\]].

ODH of alkanes, notably ethane to ethene and propane to propylene, is promising [\[10.1021/cr9800208\]]. Mo16V4Nb2 catalysts excel over other mixed-metal oxides in activity and selectivity [\[10.1021/cr9800208\]]. Niobium oxide has high selectivity for propane conversion, albeit with low conversion rates [\[10.1021/cr9800208\]]. Adding V, Cr, and Mo boosts activity without harming selectivity [\[10.1021/cr9800208\]]. In VNbO catalysts, niobium's resistance to reduction enhances vanadium sites' selectivity [\[10.1021/cr9800208\]].

Lithium-promoted magnesium oxide catalyzes methane's partial oxidation to ethane and ethylene with high selectivity and conversion [\[10.1021/ja00304a008\]]. The mechanism involves methyl radical formation through hydrogen abstraction by [Li'O-] centers, followed by ethane coupling and further oxidation to ethylene [\[10.1021/ja00304a008\]]. Increasing temperature up to a certain point enhances C2 product selectivity and methane conversion [\[10.1021/ja00304a008\]]. Lower surface areas favor high C2 selectivities by limiting CO and CO2 formation [\[10.1021/ja00304a008\]].

The oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes mechanism, involving CH activation by oxidative addition over Cr(III)silica surface sites, leads to an unstable ethylhydridochromium(V) complex [\[10.1002/poc.990\]]. The high activation energy suggests this pathway is unlikely for dehydrogenation [\[10.1002/poc.990\]]. Site isolation on varying Cr(III)silica surface sites is crucial for addressing selectivity issues [\[10.1002/poc.990\]].

In ODH of propane over ZrO2-supported MoOx catalysts, a Mars-van Krevelen mechanism is proposed, involving irreversible CH bond activation by lattice oxygens, leading to propene desorption [\[10.1021/jp002100x\]]. Reduced Mo centers reoxidize via O2 dissociative chemisorption, with reversible OH recombination steps forming water, aiding the redox cycle [\[10.1021/jp002100x\]]. Site isolation prevents undesired reactions, enhancing selectivity and process efficiency [\[10.1021/jp002100x\]].

CuTh oxide catalysts with varying Cu/Th ratios significantly influence the oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes [\[10.1007/bf02070119\]]. Increased copper concentration in thoria boosts catalytic activity and selectivity for ethylene and propene, peaking at specific ratios before declining [\[10.1007/bf02070119\]]. Higher copper content enhances ethane conversion but may inhibit ethylene formation due to CuO crystallites [\[10.1007/bf02070119\]]. Selectivity in ethylene correlates with copper(II) sites, and for propane, activity is linked to Cu 2+ ions on the surface, with selectivity in propene attributed to substitutional Cu 2+ ions [\[10.1007/bf02070119\]]. Site isolation optimizes selectivity by controlling active site distribution [\[10.1007/bf02070119\]].

In ODH of alkanes, top catalysts yield 20% methane conversion with 80% ethane/ethylene selectivity [\[10.1002/anie.199509701\]]. Ethane production from OXD is double that from methane oxidative coupling [\[10.1002/anie.199509701\]]. A Li/MgO catalyst with chloride ions achieves 77% C2H4 selectivity at 75% C2H6 conversion [\[10.1002/anie.199509701\]]. C2H6's weaker C-H bond accelerates reaction rates, underlining C-H bond strength's role in ODH efficiency [\[10.1002/anie.199509701\]].

In ODH of alkanes, high-valence d0 transition metal oxides are critical, with MO bond strength optimization enhancing efficiency [\[10.1021/jp709651n\]]. Site isolation, preventing secondary reactions, boosts selectivity by avoiding unwanted products [\[10.1021/jp709651n\]].

In ODH of alkanes, surface radicals influence reaction pathways and selectivity [\[10.1016/s0360-0564(08)60093-0\]]. Supported catalyst systems and site isolation control radical formation, enhancing selectivity by limiting side reactions and affecting active site distribution [\[10.1016/s0360-0564(08)60093-0\]]. Site isolation prevents radical interactions, improving selectivity, and supported systems manage reactive site availability for intermediates, optimizing the process [\[10.1016/s0360-0564(08)60093-0\]].

烷烃的氧化脱氢（ODH）是将烷烃转化为有价值烯烃的关键过程，由于烷烃储量巨大，具有重要的经济意义\[10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00053-x\]。ODH的效率受各种催化剂影响，包括块体金属氧化物和支撑系统，烷烃的性质决定了催化剂的选择\[10.1080/01614940600631413\]。在实现高选择性和转化率方面面临的挑战，通过理解反应机制如C-H键活化、M-烷基中间体和2H-抽氢得以解决\[10.1016/s0920-5861(96)00103-4\]。活性位点，通常是金属氧化原子的“无机分子集合”，对ODH的选择性和转化率有重大影响\[10.1016/s0920-5861(96)00103-4\]。位点隔离稳定了特定位点，有利于期望的反应而非副反应\[10.1016/s0920-5861(96)00103-4\]。单体物种如MoO42-或VO43-表现出酸性特征，导致完全氧化，而低尺寸的聚合物种显示出更好的部分氧化反应选择性\[10.1016/s0920-5861(96)00103-4\]。

钒氧化物催化剂在丙烷ODH中的性能通过中间大小的VOx域优化，平衡了活性和可及性\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。烯烃的选择性在中间负载时达到峰值，与单层结构相关\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。乙烷ODH中COx的形成归因于吸附的氧物种\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。钒在稳态ODH期间的还原低于化学计量还原\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。孤立和聚合的钒物种都是活性的，聚合的VO4物种更广泛地还原，导致表面还原性增加时丙烯选择性更高\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。支撑对钒氧化物反应性有重大影响，硅支撑的催化剂更具选择性\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。VCoAPO-18中的酸性和氧化还原位点对于乙烷ODH中乙烯的高选择性至关重要\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。碱金属添加剂影响MoOx域结构和催化性能，影响C-H键活化和丙烯选择性\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。

烷烃的ODH面临着比烷烃转化更快的烯烃氧化的挑战，需要低转化率以获得高选择性\[10.1021/cr900122p_1\]。像V2O5这样的块体金属氧化物提供低产量，而支撑系统提供更高的活性和选择性\[10.1021/cr900122p_1\]。在各种系统中，MOP、VMgOx、VSiZrOx和CrSiMO显示出高选择性\[10.1021/cr900122p_1\]。C-H键活化是决定速率的步骤，导致提出的M-烷基中间体分解为1-烯烃通过β-H抽氢\[10.1021/cr900122p_1\]。还提出了2H-抽氢机制\[10.1021/cr900122p_1\]。位点隔离对于脱氢和氧化产物形成之间的选择性至关重要\[10.1021/cr900122p_1\]。

烷烃的ODH，特别是将乙烷转化为乙烯和将丙烷转化为丙烯，很有前景\[10.1021/cr9800208\]。Mo16V4Nb2催化剂在活性和选择性方面超过其他混合金属氧化物\[10.1021/cr9800208\]。铌氧化物对丙烷转化具有高选择性，尽管转化率低\[10.1021/cr9800208\]。添加V、Cr和Mo提高了活性，而不会损害选择性\[10.1021/cr9800208\]。在VNbO催化剂中，铌对还原的抵抗力增强了钒位点的选择性\[10.1021/cr9800208\]。

锂促进的氧化镁催化甲烷的部分氧化为乙烷和乙烯，具有高选择性和转化率\[10.1021/ja00304a008\]。机制涉及通过[Li'O-]中心的氢抽离形成甲基自由基，随后是乙烷偶联和进一步氧化为乙烯\[10.1021/ja00304a008\]。在一定温度范围内提高温度会增强C2产品选择性和甲烷转化\[10.1021/ja00304a008\]。较低的表面积有利于高C2选择性，通过限制CO和CO2的形成\[10.1021/ja00304a008\]。

烷烃的ODH机制，涉及通过Cr(III)硅表面位点的氧化加成对CH的活化，导致不稳定的乙基氢化铬(V)复合物\[10.1002/poc.990\]。高活化能表明该途径不太可能用于脱氢\[10.1002/poc.990\]。在不同Cr(III)硅表面位点上的位点隔离对于解决选择性问题至关重要\[10.1002/poc.990\]。

在ZrO2支撑的MoOx催化剂上丙烷的ODH中，提出了Mars-van Krevelen机制，涉及晶格氧对C-H键的不可逆活化，导致丙烯的脱附\[10.1021/jp002100x\]。还原的Mo中心通过O2的解离化学吸附重新氧化，可逆的OH重组步骤形成水，有助于氧化还原循环\[10.1021/jp002100x\]。位点隔离防止了不需要的反应，提高了选择性和过程效率\[10.1021/jp002100x\]。

CuTh氧化物催化剂中Cu/Th比率的差异对烷烃的氧化脱氢有重大影响\[10.1007/bf02070119\]。钍中铜浓度的增加提高了催化活性和乙烯和丙烯的选择性，在特定比率前达到峰值\[10.1007/bf02070119\]。较高的铜含量提高了乙烷转化，但由于CuO晶体可能抑制乙烯形成\[10.1007/bf02070119\]。乙烯的选择性与铜(II)位点相关，而丙烷的活性与表面Cu 2+离子相关，丙烯的选择性归因于替代Cu 2+离子\[10.1007/bf02070119\]。位点隔离通过控制活性位点分布优化了选择性\[10.1007/bf02070119\]。

在烷烃的ODH中，顶级催化剂产生20%的甲烷转化率和80%的乙烷/乙烯选择性\[10.1002/anie.199509701\]。从OXD产生的乙烷产量是甲烷氧化耦合的两倍\[10.1002/anie.199509701\]。含氯离子的Li/MgO催化剂在75%的C2H6转化下实现了77%的C2H4选择性\[10.1002/anie.199509701\]。C2H6较弱的C-H键加速了反应速率，突出了C-H键强度在ODH效率中的作用\[10.1002/anie.199509701\]。

在烷烃的ODH中，高价d0过渡金属氧化物是关键的，MO键强度的优化提高了效率\[10.1021/jp709651n\]。位点隔离，防止了副反应，通过避免不需要的产品提高了选择性\[10.1021/jp709651n\]。

在烷烃的ODH中，表面自由基影响反应途径和选择性\[10.1016/s0360-0564(08)60093-0\]。支撑催化剂系统和位点隔离控制自由基的形成，通过限制副反应和影响活性位点分布，提高了选择性\[10.1016/s0360-0564(08)60093-0\]。位点隔离防止了自由基的相互作用，提高了选择性，支撑系统管理了中间体的反应位点的可用性，优化了过程\[10.1016/s0360-0564(08)60093-0\]。
