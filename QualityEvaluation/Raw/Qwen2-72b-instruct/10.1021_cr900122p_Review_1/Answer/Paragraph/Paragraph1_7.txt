                    The oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) of alkanes is a complex process influenced by catalyst characteristics, including metal cation agglomeration, acid-base properties, valence state, and electronic properties \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Supported metal oxides, particularly vanadium oxide, exhibit increased activity with higher V2O5 loading, but optimal performance is achieved with intermediate-sized VOx domains \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Selectivity to olefins peaks at intermediate loadings, correlating with a monolayer structure \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. COx formation in ethane ODH is attributed to adsorbed oxygen species \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. The reduction of vanadium during steady-state ODH is lower than stoichiometric reduction, affecting the number of catalytically significant reduced centers \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Both isolated and polymeric vanadium species are active, with polymerized VO4 species being more extensively reduced \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Higher surface reduction leads to higher selectivity to propylene \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. The use of N2O instead of O2 results in a more reduced and selective surface \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. The support significantly influences the reactivity of vanadium oxide in alkane ODH, with silica-supported catalysts being more selective than alumina or titania \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. The formation of binary dispersed structures can significantly affect the reduction and catalytic properties of active VOx domains \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Selectivity to propylene can be correlated with work function values, and secondary surface metal oxide additives can enhance propane activation to propylene \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Incorporating high vanadium contents in hosting structures can sometimes lead to structural instability \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. The presence of both acid and redox sites in VCoAPO-18 is crucial for high selectivity to ethylene in ethane ODH \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Supported molybdenum oxide catalysts are generally less active than vanadia-based catalysts, with propane ODH rates increasing with Mo surface density up to a certain point \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. The size of MoOx domains affects the Mo-O strength, with stronger bonds showing lower ODH activity \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. The activation of ethane CH bonds forms alkoxides that decompose to ethylene or are oxidized to oxygenates \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Alkali additives affect the structure of MoOx domains, influencing their electronic and catalytic properties, and can inhibit the reduction of MoOx, affecting the CH bond activation step and leading to higher selectivity to propylene \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\].

                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes is a process of significant industrial interest, driven by the need for efficient conversion of alkanes to more valuable olefins. The role of catalysts, particularly supported metal oxides, is pivotal in this process. The detailed analysis of vanadium and molybdenum-based catalysts reveals the importance of controlling the size and electronic properties of active sites for optimal performance. The use of N2O as an oxidant offers a promising direction for enhancing selectivity. The influence of support materials on the reactivity of vanadium oxide highlights the potential of silica-supported catalysts for improved selectivity. The correlation between work function values and selectivity to propylene suggests a path for the rational design of catalysts. The dynamic nature of oxidation catalysts under reaction conditions, influenced by the Mars and van Krevelen mechanism and oxide lattice mobility, is essential for understanding their functioning. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel catalysts with tailored electronic properties and the optimization of reaction conditions for enhanced selectivity and conversion. The exploration of new support materials and the design of catalysts with controlled active site distribution could lead to significant advancements in the field. The investigation of the role of surface radicals and the control of radical formation through catalyst design could also open new avenues for process optimization. The potential of site isolation in addressing selectivity issues and the optimization of active site structures for selective hydrocarbon oxidation are promising areas for future research.

                    烷烃的氧化脱氢（ODH）是一个复杂的过程，受催化剂特性的影响，包括金属阳离子聚集、酸碱性质、价态和电子性质\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。特别是钒氧化物的支撑金属氧化物在V2O5负载量增加时表现出更高的活性，但最佳性能在中间大小的VOx域实现\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。烯烃的选择性在中间负载量时达到峰值，与单层结构相关\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。乙烷ODH中COx的形成归因于吸附的氧物种\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。在稳态ODH期间，钒的还原低于化学计量还原，影响催化显著还原中心的数量\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。孤立和聚合的钒物种都是活性的，聚合的VO4物种被更广泛地还原\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。更高的表面还原导致更高的丙烯选择性\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。使用N2O代替O2会导致更还原和选择性的表面\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。支撑材料对钒氧化物在烷烃ODH中的反应性有显著影响，硅支撑的催化剂比氧化铝或二氧化钛更具有选择性\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。二元分散结构的形成可以显著影响活性VOx域的还原和催化性质\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。丙烯的选择性可以与功函数值相关联，二次表面金属氧化物添加剂可以增强丙烷到丙烯的活化\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。在宿主结构中掺入高钒含量有时会导致结构不稳定\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。VCoAPO-18中酸性和还原位点的存在对于乙烷ODH中乙烯的高选择性至关重要\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。支撑的钼氧化物催化剂通常不如基于钒的催化剂活性高，丙烷ODH速率随着Mo表面密度的增加而增加，直到达到某个点\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。MoOx域的大小影响Mo-O强度，强度较大的键显示出较低的ODH活性\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。乙烷CH键的活化形成分解为乙烯或氧化为氧酸的烷氧基\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。碱金属添加剂影响MoOx域的结构，影响其电子和催化性质，并能抑制MoOx的还原，影响CH键活化步骤，导致更高的丙烯选择性\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。

                    综合理解与未来展望：烷烃的氧化脱氢是一个具有重要工业兴趣的过程，由将烷烃高效转化为更有价值的烯烃的需求驱动。特别是支撑金属氧化物的催化剂在这一过程中起着关键作用。对基于钒和钼的催化剂的详细分析揭示了控制活性位点的大小和电子性质以实现最佳性能的重要性。使用N2O作为氧化剂为提高选择性提供了一个有希望的方向。支撑材料对钒氧化物反应性的影响突出了硅支撑催化剂在提高选择性方面的潜力。功函数值与丙烯选择性的相关性为催化剂的理性设计提供了一条途径。在反应条件下，氧化催化剂的动态性质，受Mars和van Krevelen机制和氧化物晶格流动性的影响，对于理解其功能至关重要。未来的研究工作可能集中在开发具有定制电子性质的新型催化剂和优化反应条件以增强选择性和转化率上。探索新的支撑材料和设计具有受控活性位点分布的催化剂可能会在该领域取得重大进展。通过催化剂设计控制自由基形成和表面自由基作用的探索也可能为过程优化开辟新的途径。在解决选择性问题和优化活性位点结构以进行选择性烃氧化方面，位点隔离的潜力和活性位点结构的优化是未来研究的有希望领域。
