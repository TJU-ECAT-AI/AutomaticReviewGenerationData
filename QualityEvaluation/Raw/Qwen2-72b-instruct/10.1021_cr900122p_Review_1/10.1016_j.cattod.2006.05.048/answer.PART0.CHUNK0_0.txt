It does not discuss the challenges in achieving high selectivity and conversion, nor does it propose reaction mechanisms such as C-H bond activation, M-alkyl intermediates, and 2H-abstraction. The literature focuses on the activation of methane over various transition metal oxides using cluster model calculations, finding that H abstraction is feasible on most oxides and that when the M O bond possesses a high polarity, (2 + 2) becomes an alternative pathway. The interplay between the MO bond polarity and the M O p bond strength may counterbalance the reactivity and the selectivity, hence V and Mo oxides are the most active and selective catalysts experimentally observed.
