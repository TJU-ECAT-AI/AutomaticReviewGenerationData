<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1.The oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes (ODH) is a complex process due to the strong C-H bonds in alkanes and the requirement for high selectivity to desired products.
2.Bulk metal oxides and supported metal oxide catalysts have been widely investigated for ODH reactions.
3.High selectivity and conversion in ODH reactions necessitate precise control of the catalyst structure and reaction conditions.
4.Proposed mechanisms for ODH include C-H bond activation, M-alkyl intermediates, and 2H-abstraction.
5.The efficiency of the ODH process is determined by the interplay between catalyst structure, reaction conditions, and proposed reaction mechanisms.
6.Site isolation is crucial in addressing selectivity issues by preventing side reactions and promoting desired reaction pathways.
