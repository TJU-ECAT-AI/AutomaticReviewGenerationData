          Metal molybdates, particularly NiMoO4 and Ni0.5Co0.5MoO4, show promise for the oxydehydrogenation of propane to propylene. The molybdenum content significantly influences catalytic activity and product yields. Binary NiCo molybdates offer a stable catalytic system for further optimization. The addition of redox elements like V, Fe, Ce, and Cr enhances activity, with Cr showing potential for retaining high selectivity at enhanced conversions. The NiCo molybdate system is an alternative to V-based catalysts for light paraffin activation and holds promise for direct conversion of propane to acrylic acid or acrylonitrile in a single reactor.
