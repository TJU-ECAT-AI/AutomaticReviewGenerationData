                The development of 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for the Oxidative Dehydrogenation of Propane (ODHP) reaction has been achieved through the strategic doping of active boron oxide or boron hydroxyl units onto the surface of various hosting materials \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2\]. Additionally, the exposed surface of bulk boride materials can also function as 2-D dispersed active sites, offering a significant advantage over linearly dispersed edge sites in terms of active site density, which can be substantially increased \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2\]. This unique structural feature has attracted extensive research interest due to its potential to enhance catalytic performance in the ODHP reaction \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in developing 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for the ODHP reaction have opened up new avenues for enhancing catalytic performance. The unique structural features of these catalysts, which allow for a higher density of active sites compared to linearly dispersed edge sites, have significant implications for the efficiency and selectivity of the ODHP reaction. Future research endeavors might focus on further optimizing the synthesis methods to achieve even higher active site densities, exploring the use of novel hosting materials that can support the 2-D dispersed active sites more effectively, and investigating the potential of these catalysts in other hydrocarbon dehydrogenation reactions. Additionally, the development of advanced characterization techniques to better understand the nature of the 2-D dispersed active sites and their role in catalysis could provide insights that lead to the design of more efficient catalysts for a wide range of industrial processes.
