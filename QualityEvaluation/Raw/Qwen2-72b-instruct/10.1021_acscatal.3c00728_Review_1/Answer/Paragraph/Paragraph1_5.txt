The spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in B-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. These sites are categorized into 1-D, 2-D, and 3-D configurations, with 1-D sites being linearly dispersed on the edges of h-BN sheets or BNNTs. 1-D sites, particularly those found on BNNTs, exhibit higher propene selectivity than conventional metal oxide catalysts, with over a 10-fold higher mass-specific propane consumption rate \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. The high reactivity, selectivity, and stability of BN materials enhance propene productivity, making them commercially viable for ODHP \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. The reaction mechanism involves hydrogen abstraction from propane, forming a propyl intermediate and then propene \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. Among 1-D edge active sites, the zigzag B-terminated edge is more active \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. Strategies to improve 1-D BN edge sites include preoxygen modification to shorten the induction period, increasing edge density by reducing BN nanosheet size, and aggregating active components into 1-D shapes \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. Stability can be enhanced by designing composite catalysts like h-BNxIn2O3, which strengthens interlayer forces and reduces oxygen affinity \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\].

Isolated boron species in B-based catalysts dynamically transform into aggregated ones, enhancing catalytic activity for ODHP \[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]. Aggregated BOx species, particularly BOH groups hydrogen bonded with adjacent SiOH groups, are identified as high-efficiency active centers for catalyzing ODHP at low temperatures \[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]. Defect boron sites and aggregated boron species are crucial for CH bond activation of propane \[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]. Uniformly anchoring boron species to a hydroxyl-rich MFI-type zeolite matrix may construct highly active boron centers \[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\].

Defective trigonal boron species (B[3]a and B[3]b) are crucial in propane oxidative dehydrogenation, highlighting the significance of understanding 1-D edge active sites \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021\]. This understanding can contribute to the design of more efficient B-based catalysts for commercial ODHP applications \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021\].

In B-based catalysts for ODHP, 1-D edge active sites on CNFs, particularly quinone or dicarbonyl groups, are crucial due to their high density of defects accessible to reactants \[10.1002/cssc.201402363\]. Doping with B or P enhances selectivity to propene, influenced by the type of heteroatom and precursor \[10.1002/cssc.201402363\]. Understanding these sites' unique properties and reaction mechanisms can lead to more efficient catalyst design for commercial applications \[10.1002/cssc.201402363\].

Edge B atoms in B-based catalysts play a critical role in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) by activating O2 and forming BO(H) active sites, particularly at 1-D edges \[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]. These sites can abstract hydrogen from propane with O2 assistance, producing propylene \[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]. Understanding these edge active sites could lead to more efficient catalyst design for commercial ODHP applications \[10.1002/cctc.201700725\].

Edge-hydroxylated boron nitride, a metal-free catalyst, exhibits superior selectivity for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane to propylene with minimal CO2 formation \[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]. The BNO. site, generated through hydrogen abstraction by molecular oxygen, selectively breaks the CH bond, avoiding propylene oxidation \[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]. Understanding these 1-D edge active sites aids in designing more efficient catalysts for commercial applications \[10.1002/cctc.201700004\].

In B-based catalysts for ODHP, 1-D nanomaterials like h-BN/SiO2 nanofibers are pivotal due to their ability to facilitate substance transmission and expose active sites \[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]. Prepared by electrospinning, these nanofibers allow for adjustable properties, ensuring good interaction between nanoparticles and support, enhancing activity, selectivity, and stability \[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]. The h-BN/SiO2 nanofiber catalyst, with a fibrous structure and uniform h-BN dispersion, exhibits high activity, selectivity, and stability for ODHP, producing propylene efficiently \[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]. Increasing h-BN loading and reaction temperature boosts propane conversion, indicating diffusion isn't limiting \[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]. The 1-D structure facilitates active site exposure, increasing conversion \[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]. Under high space velocity, the catalyst's productivity of olefins is remarkably higher than bulk h-BN and most boron-based catalysts, due to enhanced mass transfer and exposed active sites \[10.1002/cctc.202100476\].

Boron oxide modified carbon nanotubes (CNTs) exhibit enhanced selectivity for propene in propane oxidative dehydrogenation (ODHP) \[10.1002/anie.200901826\]. The modification blocks combustion sites, suppressing carbon framework combustion at high temperatures and increasing activation energy for O2 conversion by ~40 kJ/mol \[10.1002/anie.200901826\]. Even at low boron oxide loadings (0.003 wt%), propene selectivity rises from 17% to 28%, while the overall reaction rate decreases, improving propene production by up to 18% \[10.1002/anie.200901826\]. The maximum propene productivity is achieved at 0.01 wt% B2O3 loading \[10.1002/anie.200901826\]. Understanding 1-D edge active sites could aid in designing more efficient catalysts for commercial use \[10.1002/anie.200901826\].

In B-based catalysts for ODHP, 1-D nanofibers with open pore structures prevent diffusion limitation and ensure high catalytic activity at high WHSV \[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]. The uniform dispersion and stability of boron species on the fiber framework contribute to high performance \[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]. Understanding 1-D edge active sites is crucial for designing more efficient catalysts for commercial applications \[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\].

In B-based catalysts for ODHP, boron-oxygen coordination is critical, with active-site density and electronic interactions affecting reactivity \[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]. Surface-stabilized BOx sites form on all tested boride catalysts, with crystal structure influencing BOx species formation and active-site density \[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]. Layered borides may present different boron surface concentrations than tetragonal borides, impacting BOx species formation \[10.1002/cctc.201701140\].

In B-based catalysts for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP), 1-D edge active sites, like the oxygen-terminated armchair edge of BN (>BOON<), enhance performance by stabilizing the propyl radical and suppressing overoxidation of adsorbed species \[10.1126/science.aaf7885\]. This unique property improves selectivity to olefins and prevents facile overoxidation of propene to COx, contributing to the design of more efficient catalysts for commercial applications \[10.1126/science.aaf7885\].

In B-based catalysts for ODHP, the spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites, notably 1-D edge sites, significantly impact performance \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]. A hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN) wash-coated cordierite monolithic catalyst demonstrates high selectivity and productivity due to optimized mass and heat transfer \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]. The h-BN layer's thickness and uniform dispersion are crucial, indicating the importance of active site distribution \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]. Efficient B-based catalyst design for commercial ODHP should focus on optimizing mass and heat transfer, utilizing active sites effectively, and minimizing deep oxidation secondary reactions \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\].

在B基催化剂中，活性位点的空间分布和维度对丙烷氧化脱氢(ODHP)反应的性能有显著影响\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。这些位点被分为1-D、2-D和3-D配置，其中1-D位点线性分布在h-BN片或BNNT的边缘\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。特别是BNNT上的1-D位点，其丙烯选择性高于传统金属氧化物催化剂，丙烷消耗率超过10倍\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。BN材料的高反应性、选择性和稳定性提高了丙烯生产力，使其在商业上适用于ODHP\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。反应机制涉及从丙烷中抽氢，形成丙基中间体，然后生成丙烯\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。在1-D边缘活性位点中，锯齿状B-终止边缘更活跃\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。改善1-D BN边缘位点的策略包括预氧改性以缩短诱导期，通过减小BN纳米片尺寸增加边缘密度，以及将活性成分聚集为1-D形状\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。通过设计如h-BNxIn2O3的复合催化剂，可以增强1-D分散活性位点的稳定性，这种催化剂加强了层间力并降低了BN键对氧的亲和力\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。

B基催化剂中的孤立硼物种动态转化为聚集态，增强了ODHP的催化活性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]。聚集的BOx物种，特别是与相邻SiOH基团氢键结合的BOH基团，被识别为在低温下催化ODHP的高效率活性中心\[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]。缺陷硼位点和聚集的硼物种对于丙烷的CH键活化至关重要\[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]。将硼物种均匀锚定到富含羟基的MFI型沸石基质中可能构建出高活性的硼中心\[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]。

缺陷三价硼物种(B[3]a和B[3]b)在丙烷氧化脱氢中起关键作用，强调了理解1-D边缘活性位点的重要性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021\]。这种理解可以为设计更高效的B基催化剂用于商业ODHP应用做出贡献\[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021\]。

在B基催化剂中，CNF上的1-D边缘活性位点，特别是醌或二羰基基团，由于其高密度的缺陷对反应物可及，至关重要\[10.1002/cssc.201402363\]。用B或P掺杂可以增强对丙烯的选择性，受杂原子类型和前体的影响\[10.1002/cssc.201402363\]。理解这些位点的独特性质和反应机制可以导致更有效的催化剂设计用于商业应用\[10.1002/cssc.201402363\]。

B基催化剂中的边缘B原子在丙烷氧化脱氢(ODHP)中通过激活O2和形成BO(H)活性位点，特别是在1-D边缘，起着关键作用\[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]。这些位点可以在O2的帮助下从丙烷中抽氢，产生丙烯\[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]。理解这些边缘活性位点可以引导更有效的催化剂设计用于商业ODHP应用\[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]。

边缘羟基化硼氮化物，一种无金属催化剂，对丙烷氧化脱氢至丙烯具有优越的选择性，CO2生成量最小\[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]。通过分子氧抽氢生成的BNO.位点选择性地断裂CH键，避免丙烯氧化\[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]。理解这些1-D边缘活性位点有助于设计更高效的催化剂用于商业应用\[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]。

在B基催化剂中，1-D纳米材料如h-BN/SiO2纳米纤维由于其促进物质传输和暴露活性位点的能力而至关重要\[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]。通过电纺丝制备的这些纳米纤维允许可调性质，确保纳米粒子与支撑物之间的良好相互作用，增强活性、选择性和稳定性\[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]。h-BN/SiO2纳米纤维催化剂，具有纤维结构和均匀的h-BN分散，对ODHP表现出高活性、选择性和稳定性，高效产生丙烯\[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]。增加h-BN负载和反应温度会提高丙烷转化率，表明扩散不是限制因素\[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]。1-D结构促进了活性位点的暴露，增加了转化率\[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]。在高空间速度下，催化剂的烯烃生产力显著高于块状h-BN和大多数硼基催化剂，这是由于增强的质量传递和活性位点的暴露\[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]。

硼氧化物改性的碳纳米管(CNTs)在丙烷氧化脱氢(ODHP)中对丙烯表现出增强的选择性\[10.1002/anie.200901826\]。这种改性阻止了燃烧位点，抑制了高温下碳框架的燃烧，增加了O2转化的活化能约40 kJ/mol\[10.1002/anie.200901826\]。即使在低硼氧化物负载(0.003 wt%)下，丙烯选择性从17%增加到28%，而总反应速率降低，丙烯产量提高高达18%\[10.1002/anie.200901826\]。丙烯最大生产力在0.01 wt% B2O3负载下实现\[10.1002/anie.200901826\]。理解1-D边缘活性位点可能有助于设计更高效的催化剂用于商业应用\[10.1002/anie.200901826\]。

在B基催化剂中，1-D纳米纤维具有开放的孔结构，防止了扩散限制，确保了在高WHSV下的高催化活性\[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]。硼物种在纤维框架上的均匀分散和稳定性对高性能有贡献\[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]。理解1-D边缘活性位点对于设计更高效的催化剂用于商业应用至关重要\[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]。

在B基催化剂中，硼-氧配位是关键，活性位点密度和电子相互作用影响反应性\[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]。表面稳定的BOx位点在所有测试的硼化物催化剂上形成，晶体结构影响BOx物种的形成和活性位点密度\[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]。层状硼化物可能具有与四方硼化物不同的硼表面浓度，影响BOx物种的形成\[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]。

在B基催化剂中，1-D边缘活性位点，如BN的氧终止扶手椅边缘(>BOON<)，通过稳定丙基自由基和抑制吸附物种的过度氧化，增强性能\[10.1126/science.aaf7885\]。这种独特的性质提高了对烯烃的选择性，防止了丙烯容易过度氧化为COx，为设计更高效的催化剂用于商业应用做出了贡献\[10.1126/science.aaf7885\]。

在B基催化剂中，活性位点的空间分布和维度，特别是1-D边缘位点，对性能有显著影响\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]。六方硼氮化物(h-BN)涂层的堇青石整体催化剂由于优化的质量和热量传递而表现出高选择性和生产力\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]。h-BN层的厚度和均匀分散至关重要，表明活性位点分布的重要性\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]。有效的B基催化剂设计用于商业ODHP应侧重于优化质量和热量传递，有效利用活性位点，以及最小化深度氧化的二次反应\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]。
