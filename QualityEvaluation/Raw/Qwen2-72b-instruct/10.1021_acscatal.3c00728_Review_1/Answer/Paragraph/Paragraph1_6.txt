                    The spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in B-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. 1-D active sites, particularly those on the edges of 2-D h-BN sheets or BNNTs, exhibit higher propene selectivity than conventional metal oxide catalysts \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. BNNTs demonstrate a notably higher mass-specific propane consumption rate than h-BN \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. The reaction mechanism involves hydrogen abstraction from propane, forming a surface-stabilized propyl intermediate and subsequent radical rebound to produce propene \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. Strategies to improve 1-D BN edge active sites include preoxygen modification to reduce the induction period, increasing edge density by reducing BN nanosheet size, and aggregating active components into 1-D shapes \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. Composite catalysts, like h-BNxIn2O3, enhance the stability of 1-D dispersed active sites \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in understanding the unique properties and reaction mechanisms of 1-D edge active sites in B-based catalysts for ODHP reactions open up new avenues for catalyst design. Future research should focus on the precise control of spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites, particularly the optimization of 1-D edge sites, to enhance selectivity and productivity. Innovations in catalyst preparation methods, such as electrospinning for the synthesis of 1-D nanofibers, could lead to the development of catalysts with superior mass and heat transfer properties. Additionally, the exploration of novel boron-based materials with enhanced stability and reactivity, as well as the integration of these materials into composite catalysts, could significantly improve the efficiency of ODHP processes for commercial applications.

                    B基催化剂中活性位点的空间分布和维度对丙烷氧化脱氢(ODHP)反应的性能有显著影响\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。1-D活性位点，特别是2-D h-BN片层或BNNT边缘的位点，显示出比传统金属氧化物催化剂更高的丙烯选择性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。BNNTs的丙烷消耗率比h-BN高\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。反应机制涉及从丙烷中抽氢，形成表面稳定的丙基中间体，随后通过自由基反弹产生丙烯\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。改善1-D BN边缘活性位点的策略包括预氧改性以缩短诱导期，通过减小BN纳米片尺寸增加边缘密度，以及将活性成分聚集为1-D形状\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。复合催化剂，如h-BNxIn2O3，增强了1-D分散活性位点的稳定性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 对B基催化剂中1-D边缘活性位点的独特性质和反应机制的深入理解，为催化剂设计开辟了新的途径。未来的研究应集中在活性位点空间分布和维度的精确控制上，特别是1-D边缘位点的优化，以提高选择性和生产力。催化剂制备方法的创新，如用于合成1-D纳米纤维的静电纺丝，可能促进具有优越质量和热传递性能的催化剂的开发。此外，探索具有增强稳定性和反应性的新型硼基材料，以及将这些材料整合到复合催化剂中，可能显著提高ODHP过程的效率，适用于商业应用。
