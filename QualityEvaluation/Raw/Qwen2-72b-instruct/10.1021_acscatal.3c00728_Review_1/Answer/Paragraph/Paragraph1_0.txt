The spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in B-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. These catalysts feature active sites classified as 1-D, 2-D, and 3-D, with 1-D sites on the edge of 2-D h-BN sheets or BNNTs showing higher propene selectivity than conventional metal oxides \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. BNNTs outperform h-BN in mass-specific propane consumption, enhancing propene productivity due to their high reactivity and selectivity \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. The reaction mechanism involves hydrogen abstraction from propane, forming a propyl intermediate and propene \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. DFT studies reveal the zigzag B-terminated edge as the most active \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. Challenges include an induction period, low edge density, and stability issues, which can be addressed by preoxygen modification, reducing BN nanosheet size, and aggregating active components into 1-D shapes \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. Composite catalysts like h-BNxIn2O3 enhance stability through the SMOSI effect \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\].

Isolated boron species in B-based catalysts for ODHP transform into aggregated ones during the reaction, enhancing catalytic activity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]. Aggregated BOx species, particularly those with BOH groups hydrogen bonded to adjacent SiOH groups, are identified as high-efficiency active centers for ODHP at low temperatures \[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]. Defect boron sites are crucial for catalyzing ODHP, with aggregated species more efficient in CH bond activation of propane than isolated species \[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]. Uniformly anchoring boron species to a hydroxyl-rich MFI-type zeolite matrix may construct highly active boron centers \[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\].

Defective trigonal boron species (B[3]a and B[3]b) are crucial for propane oxidative dehydrogenation activity in B-based catalysts \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021\]. Understanding the unique properties and mechanisms of these sites, particularly 1-D edge active sites, could enhance catalyst design for commercial ODHP applications \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021\].

In B-based catalysts for ODHP, CNFs' basal plane edges with a high density of defects act as active sites, where quinone or dicarbonyl groups are selective for propene, unlike unselective carboxylic acids \[10.1002/cssc.201402363\]. Doping with B or P enhances propene selectivity at isoconversion, influenced by the heteroatom type and precursor \[10.1002/cssc.201402363\]. Understanding 1-D edge active sites' unique properties and reaction mechanisms is crucial for designing efficient catalysts \[10.1002/cssc.201402363\].

Edge B atoms in boron nitride nanosheets play a critical role in the oxidative dehydrogenation of ethane, forming BO(H) active sites at the edges \[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]. These 1-D edge active sites activate O2, abstract hydrogen from ethane, and produce ethylene \[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]. Understanding these unique properties and mechanisms could lead to the design of more efficient B-based catalysts for ODHP reactions \[10.1002/cctc.201700725\].

Edge-hydroxylated boron nitride, a metal-free catalyst, exhibits superior selectivity for propane oxidative dehydrogenation to propylene with minimal CO2 formation \[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]. The active BNO. site, formed by hydrogen abstraction from BOH groups by molecular oxygen, selectively breaks the CH bond, avoiding propylene oxidation \[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]. Understanding 1-D edge active sites' unique properties and reaction mechanisms is essential for designing more efficient B-based catalysts for commercial ODHP applications \[10.1002/cctc.201700004\].

In B-based catalysts for ODHP, 1-D nanomaterials like h-BN/SiO2 nanofibers are advantageous due to their ability to facilitate substance transmission and expose active sites \[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]. Prepared by electrospinning, these nanofibers exhibit uniform h-BN dispersion on a SiO2 support, enhancing activity, selectivity, and stability \[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]. The one-dimensional structure facilitates active site exposure, increasing propane conversion \[10.1002/cctc.202100476\], and the catalyst shows high productivity of propylene under high space velocity conditions \[10.1002/cctc.202100476\].

Boron oxide-modified carbon nanotubes (CNTs) enhance propene selectivity in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP), demonstrating the protective effect against O2 activation, even at low loadings \[10.1002/anie.200901826\]. This modification increases propene selectivity from 17% to 28% while decreasing the overall reaction rate, improving propene production by up to 18% \[10.1002/anie.200901826\]. The maximum propene productivity is achieved at a B2O3 loading of 0.01wt% \[10.1002/anie.200901826\]. Understanding 1-D edge active sites' unique properties and reaction mechanisms can contribute to designing more efficient B-based catalysts for commercial ODHP applications \[10.1002/anie.200901826\].

In B-based catalysts for ODHP, 1-D nanofibers with open pore structures prevent diffusion limitation and ensure high catalytic activity at high WHSV \[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]. The uniform dispersion and stability of boron species on the fiber framework contribute to high performance \[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]. Understanding 1-D edge active sites can lead to more efficient catalyst design for commercial applications \[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\].

In B-based catalysts for ODHP, boron-oxygen coordination is critical, affecting active-site density and electronic interactions \[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]. Surface-stabilized BOx sites form on all tested boride catalysts, with crystal structure influencing BOx species formation and active-site density \[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]. Layered and tetragonal borides differ in boron surface concentration, impacting BOx species formation \[10.1002/cctc.201701140\].

In B-based catalysts for ODHP, the 1-D nature of the oxygen-terminated armchair edge of BN (>BOON<) as an active site prevents the creation of highly reactive propyl radicals and suppresses overoxidation of adsorbed species \[10.1126/science.aaf7885\]. This unique property enhances selectivity to olefins and mitigates the facile overoxidation of propene to COx, contributing to the design of more efficient catalysts for commercial applications \[10.1126/science.aaf7885\].

For B-based catalysts in ODHP, the h-BN wash-coated cordierite monolithic catalyst demonstrates high selectivity and productivity, with a thin h-BN layer optimizing mass and heat transfer \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]. The thickness and uniform dispersion of the h-BN layer are crucial, indicating the importance of the spatial distribution of active sites \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]. Efficient catalyst design for commercial ODHP should focus on optimizing mass and heat transfer, utilizing active sites, and suppressing secondary reactions of deep oxidation \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\].

B基催化剂中活性位点的空间分布和维度对丙烷氧化脱氢(ODHP)反应性能有显著影响\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。这些催化剂的活性位点被分类为1-D、2-D和3-D，其中1-D位点位于2-D h-BN片或BNNT的边缘，显示出比传统金属氧化物更高的丙烯选择性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。BNNTs在质量特异性丙烷消耗方面优于h-BN，由于其高反应性和选择性，增强了丙烯生产力\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。反应机制涉及从丙烷中抽氢，形成丙基中间体和丙烯\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。DFT研究表明，锯齿状B-终止边缘是最活跃的\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。挑战包括诱导期、低边缘密度和稳定性问题，这些问题可以通过预氧改性、减少BN纳米片尺寸和将活性成分聚集为1-D形状来解决\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。复合催化剂如h-BNxIn2O3通过SMOSI效应增强稳定性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。

在B基催化剂中，用于ODHP的孤立硼物种在反应过程中转变为聚集态，增强了催化活性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]。聚集的BOx物种，特别是那些BOH基团与相邻SiOH基团氢键合的物种，被识别为在低温下高效催化ODHP的活性中心\[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]。缺陷硼位点对于催化ODHP至关重要，聚集物种在丙烷CH键活化方面比孤立物种更有效\[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]。将硼物种均匀锚定到羟基丰富的MFI型沸石基质中可能构建高度活性的硼中心\[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]。

缺陷三价硼物种(B[3]a和B[3]b)对于B基催化剂中丙烷氧化脱氢活性至关重要\[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021\]。理解这些位点的独特性质和机制，特别是1-D边缘活性位点，可以增强商业ODHP应用的催化剂设计\[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021\]。

在B基催化剂中，CNFs的基面边缘具有高密度的缺陷，充当活性位点，其中醌或二羰基基团对丙烯具有选择性，而羧酸则不具备选择性\[10.1002/cssc.201402363\]。B或P的掺杂在等转化率下增强了丙烯选择性，受杂原子类型和前体的影响\[10.1002/cssc.201402363\]。理解1-D边缘活性位点的独特性质和反应机制对于设计高效催化剂至关重要\[10.1002/cssc.201402363\]。

硼氮化物纳米片边缘的B原子在乙烷氧化脱氢中起关键作用，形成边缘的BO(H)活性位点\[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]。这些1-D边缘活性位点激活O2，从乙烷中抽氢，产生乙烯\[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]。理解这些独特性质和机制可能引导设计更高效的B基催化剂用于ODHP反应\[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]。

边缘羟基化的硼氮化物，一种无金属催化剂，对丙烷氧化脱氢至丙烯表现出优越的选择性，CO2形成最少\[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]。由分子氧从BOH基团中抽氢形成的活性BNO.位点，选择性地断裂CH键，避免丙烯氧化\[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]。理解1-D边缘活性位点的独特性质和反应机制对于设计更高效的B基催化剂用于商业ODHP应用至关重要\[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]。

在B基催化剂中，1-D纳米材料如h-BN/SiO2纳米纤维由于其促进物质传输和暴露活性位点的能力而具有优势\[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]。通过电纺丝制备的这些纳米纤维在SiO2支撑上展现出均匀的h-BN分散，增强了活性、选择性和稳定性\[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]。一维结构促进了活性位点的暴露，增加了丙烷转化率\[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]，催化剂在高空间速度条件下表现出高丙烯生产力\[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]。

硼氧化物改性的碳纳米管(CNTs)在丙烷氧化脱氢(ODHP)中增强了丙烯选择性，即使在低负载下也展示了对O2活化的保护作用\[10.1002/anie.200901826\]。这种改性将丙烯选择性从17%提高到28%，同时降低了总反应速率，将丙烯产量提高了最多18%\[10.1002/anie.200901826\]。丙烯最大生产力在0.01wt% B2O3负载下实现\[10.1002/anie.200901826\]。理解1-D边缘活性位点的独特性质和反应机制可以为设计更高效的B基催化剂用于商业ODHP应用做出贡献\[10.1002/anie.200901826\]。

在B基催化剂中，1-D纳米纤维具有开放的孔结构，防止扩散限制，确保在高WHSV下具有高催化活性\[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]。硼物种在纤维框架上的均匀分散和稳定性对高性能有贡献\[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]。理解1-D边缘活性位点可以引导更高效催化剂的设计用于商业应用\[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]。

在B基催化剂中，硼-氧配位是关键，影响活性位点密度和电子相互作用\[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]。表面稳定的BOx位点在所有测试的硼化物催化剂上形成，晶体结构影响BOx物种的形成和活性位点密度\[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]。层状和四方硼化物在硼表面浓度上有所不同，影响BOx物种的形成\[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]。

在B基催化剂中，BN (>BOON<)的氧终止扶手椅边缘的1-D性质作为活性位点，防止了高反应性丙基自由基的产生，抑制了吸附物种的过度氧化\[10.1126/science.aaf7885\]。这种独特性质增强了对烯烃的选择性，缓解了丙烯向COx的容易过度氧化，为商业应用的更高效催化剂设计做出了贡献\[10.1126/science.aaf7885\]。

对于B基催化剂在ODHP中，h-BN洗涂的堇青石整体催化剂表现出高选择性和生产力，薄的h-BN层优化了质量和热传递\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]。h-BN层的厚度和均匀分散至关重要，表明活性位点空间分布的重要性\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]。商业ODHP高效催化剂设计应侧重于优化质量和热传递，利用活性位点，抑制深度氧化的副反应\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]。
