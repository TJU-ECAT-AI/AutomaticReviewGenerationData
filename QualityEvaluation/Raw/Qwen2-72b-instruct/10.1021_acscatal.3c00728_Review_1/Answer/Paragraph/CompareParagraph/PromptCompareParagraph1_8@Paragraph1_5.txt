The spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in B-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728\_1\]. The high reactivity, selectivity, and stability of BN materials enhance propene productivity, making them commercially viable for ODHP \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728\_1\]. The reaction mechanism involves hydrogen abstraction from propane, forming a propyl intermediate and then propene \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728\_1\]. Among 1-D edge active sites, the zigzag B-terminated edge is more active \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728\_1\]. Strategies to improve 1-D BN edge sites include preoxygen modification to shorten the induction period, increasing edge density by reducing BN nanosheet size, and aggregating active components into 1-D shapes \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728\_1\]. Stability can be enhanced by designing composite catalysts like h-BNxIn2O3, which strengthens interlayer forces and reduces oxygen affinity \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728\_1\].
