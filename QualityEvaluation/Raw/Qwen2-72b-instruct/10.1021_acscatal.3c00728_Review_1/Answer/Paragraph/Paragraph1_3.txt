The spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in B-based catalysts, particularly 1-D edge sites, significantly influence their performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. 1-D active sites, found on the edges of 2-D h-BN sheets and BNNTs, exhibit higher selectivity for propene than conventional metal oxide catalysts, with BNNTs showing notably higher mass-specific propane consumption rates \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. BN materials' high reactivity, selectivity, and stability enhance propene productivity, making them commercially viable for ODHP \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. The reaction mechanism involves hydrogen abstraction from propane, forming a surface-stabilized propyl intermediate and subsequent radical rebound to produce propene \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. Among 1-D edge active sites, the zigzag B-terminated edge is more active \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. Strategies to improve 1-D BN edge active sites include preoxygen modification to shorten the induction period, increasing edge density by reducing BN nanosheet size, and aggregating active components into 1-D shapes \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. Enhancing the stability of 1-D dispersed active sites through composite catalyst design, such as h-BNxIn2O3, can extend catalytic stability \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\].

Isolated boron species in B-based catalysts dynamically transform into aggregated ones, enhancing catalytic activity for ODHP \[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]. Aggregated BOx species, particularly those with BOH groups hydrogen-bonded to adjacent SiOH groups, are identified as high-efficiency active centers for ODHP at low temperatures \[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]. Defect boron sites and aggregated boron species are crucial for catalyzing the ODHP reaction, with aggregated species more efficient in CH bond activation of propane than isolated species \[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]. Uniformly anchoring boron species to a hydroxyl-rich MFI-type zeolite matrix may construct highly active boron centers \[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\].

Defective trigonal boron species (B[3]a and B[3]b) are crucial for propane oxidative dehydrogenation activity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021\]. Understanding the unique properties and mechanisms of these 1-D edge active sites can significantly contribute to the design of more efficient B-based catalysts for commercial ODHP applications \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021\].

In B-based catalysts for ODHP, 1-D edge active sites on CNFs, particularly quinone or dicarbonyl groups, are critical due to their high density of defects accessible to reactants \[10.1002/cssc.201402363\]. Doping with B or P enhances selectivity to propene, influenced by the type of heteroatom and precursor \[10.1002/cssc.201402363\]. Understanding these sites' unique properties and reaction mechanisms is key to designing more efficient catalysts \[10.1002/cssc.201402363\].

Edge B atoms in B-based catalysts play a critical role in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) by activating O2 and forming BO(H) active sites, particularly at 1-D edges \[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]. These sites abstract hydrogen from propane with O2 assistance, producing propylene \[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]. Understanding these unique properties and mechanisms of 1-D edge active sites is essential for designing more efficient catalysts for commercial ODHP applications \[10.1002/cctc.201700725\].

Edge-hydroxylated boron nitride, a metal-free catalyst, demonstrates superior selectivity for propane oxidative dehydrogenation to propylene with minimal CO2 formation \[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]. The active BNO. site, generated by hydrogen abstraction from BOH groups by molecular oxygen, selectively breaks the CH bond, avoiding propylene oxidation \[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]. Understanding these 1-D edge active sites aids in designing more efficient catalysts for commercial ODHP applications \[10.1002/cctc.201700004\].

The 1-D h-BN/SiO2 nanofiber catalyst, prepared by electrospinning, significantly enhances active site exposure in ODHP reactions, leading to higher propane conversion and propylene productivity \[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]. The catalyst's performance improves with increased h-BN loading and reaction temperature, indicating efficient mass transfer and active site utilization \[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]. Its productivity of olefins under high space velocity conditions is notably higher than most boron-based catalysts, highlighting the benefits of its 1-D structure in facilitating substance transmission and active site participation \[10.1002/cctc.202100476\].

Boron oxide modified CNTs exhibit enhanced propene selectivity in ODHP, with borate and phosphate blocking combustion sites, suppressing carbon framework combustion \[10.1002/anie.200901826\]. This protective effect raises O2 activation energy by ~40 kJ/mol, demonstrating that a low 0.003 wt% loading of boron oxide significantly impacts catalytic activity \[10.1002/anie.200901826\]. Propene selectivity rises from 17% to 28%, while the reaction rate decreases, improving propene production by up to 18% \[10.1002/anie.200901826\]. Maximum propene productivity is achieved at 0.01 wt% B2O3 loading \[10.1002/anie.200901826\]. Understanding 1-D edge active sites could lead to more efficient catalyst design for commercial use \[10.1002/anie.200901826\].

In B-based catalysts for ODHP, 1-D nanofibers with open pore structures prevent diffusion limitation and ensure high catalytic activity at high WHSV \[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]. The uniform dispersion and stability of boron species on the fiber framework contribute to high performance \[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]. Understanding the unique properties and reaction mechanisms of 1-D edge active sites can lead to the design of more efficient catalysts for commercial applications \[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\].

In B-based catalysts for ODHP, boron-oxygen coordination is critical, affecting active-site density and electronic interactions \[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]. Surface-stabilized BOx sites form on all tested boride catalysts, with crystal structure influencing BOx species formation and active-site density \[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]. Layered borides may have different boron surface concentrations than tetragonal borides, impacting BOx species formation \[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]. Understanding these factors, especially for 1-D edge active sites, is key to designing efficient catalysts for commercial applications \[10.1002/cctc.201701140\].

In B-based catalysts, the 1-D oxygen-terminated armchair edge of BN (>BOON<) as an active site prevents the formation of highly reactive propyl radicals and suppresses overoxidation of adsorbed species, typical issues with 0D and 2D catalysts \[10.1126/science.aaf7885\]. This unique property enhances selectivity to olefins and mitigates the facile overoxidation of propene to COx, crucial for designing efficient catalysts for commercial ODHP applications \[10.1126/science.aaf7885\].

In B-based catalysts for ODHP, the spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites, particularly 1-D edge sites, significantly impact performance \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]. A h-BN wash-coated cordierite monolithic catalyst demonstrates high efficiency due to optimized mass and heat transfer, influenced by the h-BN layer's thickness and uniform dispersion \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]. Controlling the spatial distribution of active sites could enhance catalyst design for commercial ODHP applications, focusing on mass and heat transfer optimization, active site utilization, and suppression of deep oxidation secondary reactions \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\].

空间分布和维度性在B基催化剂中，特别是1-D边缘位点，对丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应的性能有显著影响\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。1-D活性位点，位于2-D h-BN片和BNNTs的边缘，显示出比传统金属氧化物催化剂更高的丙烯选择性，BNNTs表现出显著更高的丙烷消耗率\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。BN材料的高反应性、选择性和稳定性提高了丙烯生产力，使其在商业ODHP中具有可行性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。反应机制涉及从丙烷中抽氢，形成表面稳定的丙基中间体，随后通过自由基反弹产生丙烯\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。在1-D边缘活性位点中，锯齿状B-终止边缘更活跃\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。改进1-D BN边缘活性位点的策略包括预氧改性以缩短诱导期，通过减小BN纳米片尺寸增加边缘密度，以及将活性成分聚集为1-D形状\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。通过复合催化剂设计，如h-BNxIn2O3，增强1-D分散活性位点的稳定性，可以延长催化稳定性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。

B基催化剂中的孤立硼物种动态转化为聚集态，增强了ODHP的催化活性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]。聚集的BOx物种，特别是那些具有与相邻SiOH基团氢键结合的BOH基团，被识别为低温度下ODHP的高效率活性中心\[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]。缺陷硼位点和聚集的硼物种对于催化ODHP反应至关重要，聚集物种在丙烷CH键活化方面比孤立物种更有效\[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]。将硼物种均匀锚定到富含羟基的MFI型沸石基质中可能构建出高活性的硼中心\[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]。

缺陷三角硼物种（B[3]a和B[3]b）对于丙烷氧化脱氢活性至关重要\[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021\]。理解这些1-D边缘活性位点的独特性质和机制可以显著贡献于设计更高效的B基催化剂，用于商业ODHP应用\[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021\]。

在B基催化剂中用于ODHP的1-D边缘活性位点，特别是在CNFs上，特别是醌或二羰基基团，由于其高密度的缺陷可被反应物访问，因此至关重要\[10.1002/cssc.201402363\]。用B或P掺杂可以增强丙烯的选择性，受杂原子类型和前体的影响\[10.1002/cssc.201402363\]。理解这些位点的独特性质和反应机制是设计更高效催化剂的关键\[10.1002/cssc.201402363\]。

B基催化剂中的边缘B原子在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）中起关键作用，通过激活O2和形成BO(H)活性位点，特别是在1-D边缘\[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]。这些位点在O2的帮助下从丙烷中抽氢，产生丙烯\[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]。理解这些1-D边缘活性位点的独特性质和机制对于设计更高效的催化剂用于商业ODHP应用至关重要\[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]。

边缘羟基化硼氮化物，一种无金属催化剂，对丙烷氧化脱氢至丙烯表现出优越的选择性，CO2形成最少\[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]。由分子氧从BOH基团中抽氢生成的活性BNO.位点选择性地断裂CH键，避免丙烯氧化\[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]。理解这些1-D边缘活性位点有助于设计更高效的催化剂用于商业ODHP应用\[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]。

通过电纺丝制备的1-D h-BN/SiO2纳米纤维催化剂在ODHP反应中显著增强了活性位点的暴露，导致更高的丙烷转化率和丙烯生产力\[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]。随着h-BN负载量和反应温度的增加，催化剂的性能提高，表明了有效的质量传递和活性位点的利用\[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]。在高空间速度条件下，其烯烃的生产力明显高于大多数硼基催化剂，突出了其1-D结构在促进物质传输和活性位点参与方面的优势\[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]。

硼氧化物改性的CNTs在ODHP中表现出增强的丙烯选择性，硼酸盐和磷酸盐阻塞燃烧位点，抑制碳框架燃烧\[10.1002/anie.200901826\]。这种保护作用使O2活化能提高了约40 kJ/mol，证明了低至0.003 wt%的硼氧化物负载量对催化活性有显著影响\[10.1002/anie.200901826\]。丙烯选择性从17%上升到28%，而反应速率下降，丙烯产量提高了最多18%\[10.1002/anie.200901826\]。在0.01 wt% B2O3负载下，丙烯生产力达到最大\[10.1002/anie.200901826\]。理解1-D边缘活性位点可以引导更高效催化剂的设计用于商业使用\[10.1002/anie.200901826\]。

在B基催化剂中用于ODHP的1-D纳米纤维，具有开放的孔结构，防止了扩散限制，确保了高WHSV下的高催化活性\[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]。硼物种在纤维框架上的均匀分散和稳定性对高性能有贡献\[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]。理解1-D边缘活性位点的独特性质和反应机制可以引导设计更高效的催化剂用于商业应用\[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]。

在B基催化剂中用于ODHP的硼-氧配位是关键，影响活性位点密度和电子相互作用\[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]。表面稳定的BOx位点在所有测试的硼化物催化剂上形成，晶体结构影响BOx物种的形成和活性位点密度\[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]。层状硼化物可能具有与四方硼化物不同的硼表面浓度，影响BOx物种的形成\[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]。理解这些因素，特别是对于1-D边缘活性位点，是设计高效催化剂用于商业应用的关键\[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]。

在B基催化剂中，BN的1-D氧终止扶手椅边缘（>BOON<）作为活性位点，防止了高度反应性丙基自由基的形成，并抑制了吸附物种的过度氧化，这是0D和2D催化剂的典型问题\[10.1126/science.aaf7885\]。这种独特的性质提高了对烯烃的选择性，并缓解了丙烯到COx的容易过度氧化，对于设计高效的催化剂用于商业ODHP应用至关重要\[10.1126/science.aaf7885\]。

在B基催化剂中用于ODHP的，特别是1-D边缘位点的空间分布和维度性对性能有显著影响\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]。h-BN洗涂的堇青石整体催化剂由于优化的质量和热量传递，表现出高效率，受h-BN层厚度和均匀分散的影响\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]。控制活性位点的空间分布可以增强催化剂设计用于商业ODHP应用，专注于质量传递和热量传递优化，活性位点的利用，以及深度氧化副反应的抑制\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]。
