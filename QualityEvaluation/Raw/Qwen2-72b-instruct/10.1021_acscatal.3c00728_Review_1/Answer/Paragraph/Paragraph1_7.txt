    The spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in B-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. These sites are categorized into 1-D, 2-D, and 3-D configurations, with 1-D sites linearly dispersed on the edges of 2-D h-BN sheets or BNNTs. The 1-D sites exhibit higher propene selectivity than conventional metal oxide catalysts, particularly BNNTs, which show higher mass-specific propane consumption rates \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. The reaction mechanism involves hydrogen abstraction from propane, forming a surface-stabilized propyl intermediate and subsequent radical rebound to produce propene \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. Strategies to enhance 1-D BN edge active sites include preoxygen modification to reduce the induction period, increasing edge density by reducing BN nanosheet size, and aggregating active components into 1-D shapes \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]. Composite catalysts, like h-BNxIn2O3, improve stability by strengthening interlayer forces and weakening BN bond affinity to oxygen \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\].

    Defective trigonal boron species (B[3]a and B[3]b) are crucial for propane oxidative dehydrogenation activity in B-based catalysts \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021\]. Understanding 1-D edge active sites' unique properties and reaction mechanisms can significantly contribute to designing more efficient catalysts for commercial ODHP applications, despite a lack of literature on spatial distribution and dimensionality effects \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021\].

    Aggregated boron species are more efficient in CH bond activation of propane than isolated species \[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]. Uniformly anchoring boron species to a hydroxyl-rich zeolite matrix in oligomeric form may construct highly active boron centers \[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\].

    Edge B atoms in B-based catalysts play a critical role in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) by activating O2 and forming BO(H) active sites, particularly at 1-D edges \[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]. These sites facilitate hydrogen abstraction from propane with O2 assistance, leading to ethylene production \[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]. Understanding these 1-D edge active sites' unique properties and reaction mechanisms is essential for designing more efficient B-based catalysts for commercial ODHP applications \[10.1002/cctc.201700725\].

    Edge-hydroxylated boron nitride, a metal-free catalyst, exhibits superior selectivity for propane oxidative dehydrogenation to propylene with minimal CO2 formation \[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]. The active BNO. site, formed by hydrogen abstraction from BOH groups by molecular oxygen, selectively breaks the CH bond, avoiding propylene oxidation \[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]. Understanding these 1-D edge active sites enhances catalyst design for commercial ODHP applications \[10.1002/cctc.201700004\].

    The h-BN/SiO2 nanofiber catalyst, with its one-dimensional structure, facilitates active site exposure, enhancing activity and selectivity \[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]. This is evidenced by the increased propane conversion with higher h-BN loading and reaction temperature, indicating that conversion is not diffusion-limited \[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]. The catalyst's high productivity of propylene and olefins under high space velocity conditions is attributed to enhanced mass transfer, allowing for easy exposure of active sites \[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]. This performance surpasses that of bulk h-BN and most boron-based catalysts reported for ODHP \[10.1002/cctc.202100476\].

    Boron oxide modified CNTs exhibit high selectivity for propene in ODHP reactions, with borate and phosphate blocking combustion sites, suppressing carbon framework combustion \[10.1002/anie.200901826\]. This protective effect raises O2 activation energy by 40 kJ/mol, demonstrating that a small fraction of surface functionalities (0.003 wt% B2O3) are catalytically active \[10.1002/anie.200901826\]. Propene selectivity increases from 17% to 28%, and productivity peaks at 0.01 wt% B2O3 loading, improving propene production by 18% \[10.1002/anie.200901826\]. Understanding 1-D edge active sites can enhance catalyst design for commercial applications \[10.1002/anie.200901826\].

    In B-based catalysts for ODHP, 1-D nanofibers with open pore structures prevent diffusion limitations and ensure high catalytic activity at high WHSV \[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]. The uniform dispersion and stability of boron species on the fiber framework contribute to high performance \[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]. Understanding 1-D edge active sites can lead to the design of more efficient catalysts for commercial applications \[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\].

    In B-based catalysts for ODHP, boron-oxygen coordination is critical, with active-site density and electronic interactions affecting reactivity \[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]. Surface-stabilized BOx sites form on all tested boride catalysts, with crystal structure influencing BOx species formation and active-site density \[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]. Layered and tetragonal borides have differing boron surface concentrations, impacting BOx species formation \[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]. Understanding these properties, especially of 1-D edge active sites, aids in designing more efficient catalysts \[10.1002/cctc.201701140\].

    In B-based catalysts for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP), the 1-D nature of the oxygen-terminated armchair edge of BN (>BOON<) as an active site prevents the creation of highly reactive propyl radicals and suppresses overoxidation, typical issues with 0D and 2D catalysts \[10.1126/science.aaf7885\]. This unique property enhances selectivity to olefins and mitigates the facile overoxidation of propene to COx, contributing to the design of more efficient catalysts for commercial applications \[10.1126/science.aaf7885\].

    The performance of B-based catalysts in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) is influenced by the spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites, with 1-D edge sites being crucial \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]. A hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN) wash-coated cordierite monolithic catalyst demonstrates high selectivity and productivity in ODHP due to optimized mass and heat transfer \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]. The thickness and uniform dispersion of the h-BN layer play a significant role in the catalyst's performance, highlighting the importance of spatial distribution of active sites \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]. Optimizing these factors can lead to more efficient catalysts for commercial ODHP applications \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\].

    B基催化剂中活性位点的空间分布和维度对丙烷氧化脱氢(ODHP)反应性能有显著影响\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。这些位点被分类为1-D，2-D和3-D配置，其中1-D位点线性分布在2-D h-BN片层或BNNT的边缘。1-D位点比传统金属氧化物催化剂表现出更高的丙烯选择性，特别是BNNT，其显示出更高的丙烷消耗率\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。反应机制涉及从丙烷中抽氢，形成表面稳定的丙基中间体，随后通过自由基反弹产生丙烯\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。增强1-D BN边缘活性位点的策略包括预氧改性以缩短诱导期，通过减小BN纳米片尺寸增加边缘密度，以及将活性成分聚集为1-D形状\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。复合催化剂如h-BNxIn2O3通过增强层间力和减弱BN键对氧的亲和力来提高稳定性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1\]。

    缺陷三角硼物种(B[3]a和B[3]b)对B基催化剂中的丙烷氧化脱氢活性至关重要\[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021\]。理解1-D边缘活性位点的独特性质和反应机制可以显著贡献于设计更高效的催化剂用于商业ODHP应用，尽管关于空间分布和维度效应的文献缺乏\[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021\]。

    聚集的硼物种在CH键活化丙烷方面比孤立物种更有效\[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]。将硼物种均匀锚定到富羟基沸石基质中，以寡聚形式可能构建高活性硼中心\[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]。

    B基催化剂中的边缘B原子在丙烷氧化脱氢(ODHP)中通过激活O2和形成BO(H)活性位点，特别是在1-D边缘，起着关键作用\[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]。这些位点通过O2辅助从丙烷中抽氢，导致乙烯的产生\[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]。理解这些1-D边缘活性位点的独特性质和反应机制对于设计更高效的B基催化剂用于商业ODHP应用至关重要\[10.1002/cctc.201700725\]。

    边缘羟基化硼氮化物，一种无金属催化剂，对丙烷氧化脱氢至丙烯表现出优越的选择性，CO2形成最小\[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]。由分子氧从BOH基团中抽氢形成的活性BNO.位点选择性地断裂CH键，避免丙烯氧化\[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]。理解这些1-D边缘活性位点增强了催化剂设计用于商业ODHP应用\[10.1002/cctc.201700004\]。

    h-BN/SiO2纳米纤维催化剂，由于其一维结构，促进了活性位点的暴露，增强了活性和选择性\[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]。这从随着h-BN负载和反应温度的增加，丙烷转化率的增加中得到证实，表明转化不是扩散受限的\[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]。催化剂在高空间速度条件下对丙烯和烯烃的高生产力归因于增强的质量传递，允许活性位点容易暴露\[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]。这种性能超越了块状h-BN和大多数报道的用于ODHP的硼基催化剂\[10.1002/cctc.202100476\]。

    硼氧化物改性的CNTs在ODHP反应中对丙烯表现出高选择性，其中硼酸盐和磷酸盐阻断了燃烧位点，抑制了碳框架的燃烧\[10.1002/anie.200901826\]。这种保护作用将O2活化能提高了40 kJ/mol，证明了表面功能(0.003 wt% B2O3)的一小部分是催化活性的\[10.1002/anie.200901826\]。丙烯选择性从17%增加到28%，生产力在0.01 wt% B2O3负载时达到峰值，丙烯产量提高了18%\[10.1002/anie.200901826\]。理解1-D边缘活性位点可以增强催化剂设计用于商业应用\[10.1002/anie.200901826\]。

    在B基催化剂中用于ODHP，1-D纳米纤维具有开放的孔结构，防止了扩散限制，并确保了高WHSV下的高催化活性\[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]。硼物种在纤维框架上的均匀分散和稳定性对高性能有贡献\[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]。理解1-D边缘活性位点可以导致设计更高效的催化剂用于商业应用\[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]。

    在B基催化剂中用于ODHP，硼氧配位是关键的，活性位点密度和电子相互作用影响反应性\[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]。表面稳定的BOx位点在所有测试的硼化物催化剂上形成，晶体结构影响BOx物种的形成和活性位点密度\[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]。层状和四方硼化物具有不同的硼表面浓度，影响BOx物种的形成\[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]。理解这些性质，特别是1-D边缘活性位点，有助于设计更高效的催化剂\[10.1002/cctc.201701140\]。

    在B基催化剂中用于丙烷氧化脱氢(ODHP)，BN (>BOON<)的氧终止扶手椅边缘的1-D性质作为活性位点防止了高度反应性的丙基自由基的产生，并抑制了0D和2D催化剂常见的过度氧化\[10.1126/science.aaf7885\]。这种独特的性质提高了对烯烃的选择性，并缓解了丙烯到COx的容易过度氧化，对设计更高效的催化剂用于商业应用有贡献\[10.1126/science.aaf7885\]。

    B基催化剂在丙烷氧化脱氢(ODHP)中的性能受活性位点的空间分布和维度影响，1-D边缘位点是关键\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]。六方硼氮化物(h-BN)涂覆的堇青石整体催化剂由于优化的质量和热量传递，在ODHP中表现出高选择性和生产力\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]。h-BN层的厚度和均匀分散在催化剂性能中起着重要作用，突出了活性位点空间分布的重要性\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]。优化这些因素可以导致更高效的催化剂用于商业ODHP应用\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028\]。
