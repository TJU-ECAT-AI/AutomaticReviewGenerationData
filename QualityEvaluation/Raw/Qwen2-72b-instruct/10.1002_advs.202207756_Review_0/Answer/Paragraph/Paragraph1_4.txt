    The escalating demand for propylene, forecasted to grow at a rate of 2-3% annually by 2035, has catalyzed the development of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as a pivotal production method [\[10.1039/d0cs00814a\]]. PDH, alongside methanol-to-olefins (MTO) and Fischer-Tropsch processes, addresses the limitations of traditional methods. Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, central to PDH, have seen evolving understanding, with active Ga species being a subject of debate. Recent research underscores the [GaH]2+ ion paired with a basic framework oxygen as a critical active site, significantly more active than isolated Ga+ [\[10.1039/d0cs00814a\]]. Studies confirm the Lewis acid site provided by coordinatively unsaturated Ga3+ in Ga2O3 as essential [\[10.1039/d0cs00814a\]]. Trace Pt modification of GaOx has shown efficiency in PDH, facilitating hydrogen desorption and gallium species recovery [\[10.1039/d0cs00814a\]]. These findings enrich our knowledge of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts in PDH.

    Rising propylene demand has driven the expansion of PDH technologies [\[10.1039/c6ra27204e\]]. The debate over active Ga species in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts persists, with recent studies emphasizing the significance of Lewis(Ga+)-Brnsted acid pairs for enhancing CH bond activation and facilitating hydrogen elimination in propane dehydrogenation [\[10.1039/c6ra27204e\]]. Among samples, 5Ga2O3/ZSM-5 exhibits peak catalytic activity with optimal gallium dispersion, while 5Ga2O3/SBA-15 shows the highest stability [\[10.1039/c6ra27204e\]]. Ongoing research continues to explore the impact of supports on Ga2O3 dispersion and gallium state [\[10.1039/c6ra27204e\]].

    Increased propylene demand has driven the adoption of propane dehydrogenation (PDH), a key production method [\[10.1039/d2nr02208g\]]. The understanding of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH has advanced, with a focus on the active Ga species debate [\[10.1039/d2nr02208g\]]. Recent studies pinpointed [GaH]2+ and a basic framework oxygen of the Brnsted-acid site as critical for PDH reactions, enriching our knowledge of these catalysts [\[10.1039/d2nr02208g\]].

    Growing propylene demand has driven the development of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) methods [\[10.1006/jcat.1993.1079\]]. Ga species in H-ZSM5 zeolites catalyze dehydrogenation, promoting H2 formation and inhibiting cracking [\[10.1006/jcat.1993.1079\]]. Fresh catalysts initially contain Ga+3 species that reduce to monomeric hydride species under reaction conditions, which release H2 during propane dehydrocyclodimerization [\[10.1006/jcat.1993.1079\]]. Reduced Ga species reoxidize to Ga+3 at room temperature in H2, indicating active forms exist only under reaction conditions [\[10.1006/jcat.1993.1079\]].

    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements and findings discussed in the file, there lies an opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel catalysts that can further enhance the efficiency and selectivity of PDH processes. The ongoing debate about active Ga species in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts highlights the need for more in-depth studies using advanced characterization techniques to definitively identify the active sites. Additionally, the role of supports in affecting gallium species dispersion and gallium state remains a key area of investigation, with acidity affecting catalytic performance. The impact of CO2 in enhancing olefin yields and the effect of promoters like potassium in reducing coke formation and increasing selectivity are also promising areas for further exploration. The reducibility of Ga2O3 and the influence of hydrogen and steam on catalyst activity, as well as the role of metal promoters like Pt in assisting hydrogen desorption and active site regeneration, should be further investigated. These directions could potentially lead to the development of more efficient and sustainable PDH processes, addressing the growing demand for propylene in the chemical industry.

    预计到2035年，丙烯需求将以每年2-3%的速度增长，这推动了丙烷脱氢（PDH）作为关键生产方法的发展 [\[10.1039/d0cs00814a\]]. PDH与甲醇制烯烃（MTO）和费托合成过程一起，解决了传统方法的局限性。Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂是PDH的核心，对活性Ga物种的理解不断演变，活性Ga物种是辩论的主题。最近的研究强调了[GaH]2+离子与基本框架氧配对作为关键活性位点，其活性显著高于孤立的Ga+ [\[10.1039/d0cs00814a\]]. 研究证实，Ga2O3中未配位的Ga3+提供的Lewis酸位点是必不可少的 [\[10.1039/d0cs00814a\]]. 微量Pt改性的GaOx在PDH中显示出效率，促进了氢气的解吸和镓物种的恢复 [\[10.1039/d0cs00814a\]]. 这些发现丰富了我们对PDH中Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂的理解。

    丙烯需求的增加推动了PDH技术的发展 [\[10.1039/c6ra27204e\]]. 关于Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂中活性Ga物种的辩论持续存在，最近的研究强调了Lewis(Ga+)-Brnsted酸对在增强CH键活化和促进氢气消除中的重要性 [\[10.1039/c6ra27204e\]]. 在样品中，5Ga2O3/ZSM-5表现出峰值催化活性和最佳的镓分散，而5Ga2O3/SBA-15显示出最高的稳定性 [\[10.1039/c6ra27204e\]]. 持续的研究继续探索支撑物对Ga2O3分散和镓状态的影响 [\[10.1039/c6ra27204e\]].

    丙烯需求的增加推动了丙烷脱氢（PDH）作为关键生产方法的采用 [\[10.1039/d2nr02208g\]]. 对于PDH，Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂的理解已经发展，重点是活性Ga物种的辩论 [\[10.1039/d2nr02208g\]]. 最近的研究确定了[GaH]2+和Brnsted酸位点的基本框架氧作为PDH反应的关键，丰富了我们对这些催化剂的理解 [\[10.1039/d2nr02208g\]].

    丙烯需求的增长推动了丙烷脱氢（PDH）方法的发展 [\[10.1006/jcat.1993.1079\]]. H-ZSM5沸石中的Ga物种催化脱氢，促进H2的形成并抑制裂化 [\[10.1006/jcat.1993.1079\]]. 新鲜催化剂最初含有Ga+3物种，在反应条件下减少到单体氢化物物种，这些物种在丙烷脱氢环二聚化过程中释放H2 [\[10.1006/jcat.1993.1079\]]. 减少的Ga物种在室温下在H2中重新氧化为Ga+3，表明活性形式仅在反应条件下存在 [\[10.1006/jcat.1993.1079\]].
