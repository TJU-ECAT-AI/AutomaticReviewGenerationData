The escalating demand for propylene, forecasted to reach 191 megatons by 2030\[10.1002/advs.202207756_0\], has catalyzed the adoption of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as a key production method\[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012\]. Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, pivotal in PDH, have been in use since the 1970s for hydrocarbon processing\[10.1002/advs.202207756_0\]. The identity of the active Ga species in these catalysts remains a subject of debate, with recent research pinpointing [GaH]2+ and a basic framework oxygen as active sites\[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012\]. The debate on active Ga species persists, with recent studies highlighting the significance of Lewis(Ga+)-Brnsted acid pairs in propane dehydrogenation, where enhanced Lewis acidity facilitates CH bond activation and Brnsted acidity reduces the barrier for hydrogen elimination\[10.1039/d0cs00983k\]. Ga species in H-ZSM5 zeolites catalyze dehydrogenation, promoting H2 formation and inhibiting cracking\[10.1006/jcat.1993.1079\]. Under reaction conditions, Ga+3 species reduce to monomeric hydride forms, which release H2 during propane dehydrocyclodimerization\[10.1006/jcat.1993.1079\]. Reduced Ga species reoxidize to Ga+3 at room temperature in H2, indicating that active Ga forms are reaction-specific\[10.1006/jcat.1993.1079\].

The understanding of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH has evolved, with a debate on active Ga species\[10.1021/acscatal.1c01497\]. The debate over active Ga species in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts persists, with recent studies suggesting coordinatively unsaturated tetrahedral Ga3+ cations as key dehydrogenation sites\[10.1021/cr5002436\].

The active Ga species in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH remains a contentious issue, with various Ga forms proposed\[10.1002/advs.202207756_0\]. Recent research using FTIR spectroscopy and PDH kinetics has revealed that Ga speciation and PDH performance are influenced by Ga/Al ratios and Al distributions in zeolites\[10.1002/advs.202207756_0\]. The active Ga species in PDH in Ga/H-ZSM-5 with moderate tetrahedral aluminum content are identified as Lewis-Brnsted acid pairs, which are more active than isolated Ga+ due to increased Lewis acidity upon protonation, promoting heterolytic CH bond activation\[10.1039/d0cs00814a\]. This understanding, along with the role of Ga3+ as a Lewis acid site, contributes to the ongoing scientific discourse on Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts\[10.1039/d0cs00814a\].
