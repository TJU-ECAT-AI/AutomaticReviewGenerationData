        <English> 
                The escalating demand for propylene, forecasted to grow 2-3% annually by 2035, has catalyzed the development of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as a key production method [\[10.1039/d0cs00814a\]]. PDH, alongside methanol-to-olefins (MTO) and Fischer-Tropsch-to-olefins processes, addresses the limitations of traditional methods. Central to PDH are Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, whose active Ga species have been a subject of scientific debate. Recent studies highlight [GaH]2+ ions paired with a basic framework oxygen as the active sites, offering lower activation barriers for propane dehydrogenation [\[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012\]]. Ga2O3's active site is identified as the Lewis acid site provided by coordinatively unsaturated Ga3+ [\[10.1039/d0cs00814a\]]. Trace Pt-modified GaOx has been reported as an efficient catalyst, facilitating hydrogen desorption and recovery of gallium hydride species [\[10.1039/d0cs00814a\]]. DFT calculations reveal that Zrcus cations in ZrO2 lower activation barriers for H2 and C3H6 formation, contributing to our evolving understanding of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts in PDH [\[10.1039/d0cs00814a\]].

                ※※※

                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in PDH technology and the evolving understanding of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts have opened new avenues for research and development. Future efforts might focus on the optimization of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for enhanced efficiency and selectivity, exploring the role of metal promoters like Pt in facilitating hydrogen desorption and active site regeneration. The development of novel catalysts with tailored active sites, such as [GaH]2+ and Lewis acid sites, could lead to more efficient PDH processes. Additionally, the investigation of alternative supports for Ga2O3 species and the impact of support on gallium species dispersion and state could provide insights into improving catalytic performance. The use of computational methods, such as DFT calculations, to predict the behavior of catalysts under reaction conditions could also guide the design of next-generation PDH catalysts. The exploration of renewable feedstocks for PDH, aligning with green chemistry principles, could further enhance the sustainability of propylene production.

        <Chinese> 
                预计到2035年，丙烯需求将以每年2-3%的速度增长，这推动了丙烷脱氢（PDH）作为关键生产方法的发展 [\[10.1039/d0cs00814a\]]. PDH与甲醇制烯烃（MTO）和费托合成制烯烃过程并驾齐驱，解决了传统方法的局限性。PDH的核心是Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂，其活性Ga物种一直是科学辩论的焦点。最近的研究强调了[GaH]2+离子与基本框架氧配对作为活性位点，为丙烷脱氢提供了更低的活化能垒 [\[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012\]]. Ga2O3的活性位点被确定为由配位不饱和Ga3+提供的路易斯酸位点 [\[10.1039/d0cs00814a\]]. 痕量Pt改性的GaOx被报道为一种有效的催化剂，有助于氢解吸和镓氢化物物种的回收 [\[10.1039/d0cs00814a\]]. 密度泛函理论（DFT）计算揭示了ZrO2中的Zrcus阳离子降低了H2和C3H6形成的活化能垒，为我们在PDH中对Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂的理解提供了新的进展 [\[10.1039/d0cs00814a\]].

                ※※※

                综合理解与未来展望：PDH技术的进步和Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂理解的演变开辟了研究和开发的新途径。未来的努力可能集中在优化Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂以提高效率和选择性，探索金属促进剂如Pt在促进氢解吸和活性位点再生中的作用。开发具有定制活性位点（如[GaH]2+和路易斯酸位点）的新型催化剂可能会导致更有效的PDH过程。此外，对Ga2O3物种的替代载体以及载体对镓物种分散和状态影响的调查可能为提高催化性能提供见解。使用计算方法，如DFT计算，来预测催化剂在反应条件下的行为，也可以指导下一代PDH催化剂的设计。探索符合绿色化学原则的可再生原料用于PDH，可以进一步提高丙烯生产的可持续性。
