                    The industrial importance of light olefins, notably ethylene and propylene, is underscored by their vast production volumes and applications in the chemical industry \[10.1021/cs4003436\]. These olefins serve as key building blocks for a wide range of products, from packaging materials to synthetic textiles. Ethylene, the largest-volume petrochemical, is primarily used in plastics production, including polyethylene, polystyrene, polyethylene terephthalate (PET), and polyvinyl chloride (PVC), which are essential in packaging, textiles, and construction \[10.1021/cs4003436\]. Propylene, with an even more diverse range of derivatives, has seen significant demand growth due to the increased consumption of polypropylene \[10.1021/cs4003436\]. Its applications extend to propylene oxide, cumene, acrylonitrile, isopropyl alcohol, and other industrially important chemicals \[10.1021/cs4003436\].

                    The primary production methods for light olefins include steam cracking, fluid-catalytic-cracking (FCC), and catalytic dehydrogenation \[10.1002/9783527610044.hetcat0162\]. Steam cracking is the dominant method, capable of producing over 1 million tons of ethylene annually \[10.1002/9783527610044.hetcat0162\]. Catalytic cracking, particularly FCC, is another source, especially for propylene \[10.1002/9783527610044.hetcat0162\]. The share of catalytic dehydrogenation of propane in propylene production is expected to rise due to thermodynamic limits in naphtha crackers and the low propylene output from ethane-based steam crackers \[10.1002/9783527610044.hetcat0162\]. Propylene's growth rate is projected to outpace ethylene's \[10.1002/9783527610044.hetcat0162\].

                    The synthesis of light olefins faces thermodynamic challenges, requiring high temperatures and low pressures for high conversions \[10.1021/cr5002436_0\]. Dehydrogenation of C2-C4 paraffins requires 550-750°C for 50% conversion at 1 bar \[10.1021/cr5002436_0\]. Energy for dehydrogenation decreases with longer alkane chains, necessitating more energy for light paraffins \[10.1021/cr5002436_0\].

                    Propylene production through catalytic dehydrogenation of propane has seen steady growth, influenced by economic conditions and propane availability \[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x\]. Ethylene production via catalytic dehydrogenation over platinum catalysts is highly selective but faces challenges with ethane due to severe operating conditions \[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x\]. Higher temperatures are required for dehydrogenating light paraffins compared to heavy paraffins, with propane needing at least 580 degrees Celsius for 40% conversion, while dodecane requires only 450 degrees Celsius \[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x\]. The equilibrium constant for paraffin dehydrogenation increases with carbon number \[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x\].

                    Catalytic dehydrogenation is a critical technology for producing light olefins, especially propylene, facing thermodynamic equilibrium limitations due to high reaction enthalpy \[10.1007/s11244-012-9912-1\]. This necessitates high-temperature (>600°C) and low-pressure (<30 psig) operations for economic conversion \[10.1007/s11244-012-9912-1\]. Catalyst design must balance chemical and mass transport properties, often using shaped particles and pore architecture modifications to enhance activity and selectivity \[10.1007/s11244-012-9912-1\]. The Lummus/UOP SMART process innovatively burns hydrogen liberated by dehydrogenation, directly reheating the reaction mixture, reducing steam consumption, and increasing styrene monomer production, thus improving single-pass ethylbenzene conversion with high selectivity \[10.1007/s11244-012-9912-1\].

                    轻烯烃，尤其是乙烯和丙烯，在化学工业中具有重要的工业价值，这体现在它们庞大的生产量和广泛的应用上\[10.1021/cs4003436\]。这些烯烃作为关键的构建块，用于从包装材料到合成纺织品的广泛产品。乙烯，作为最大量的石化产品，主要用于塑料生产，包括聚乙烯、聚苯乙烯、聚对苯二甲酸乙二醇酯（PET）和聚氯乙烯（PVC），这些材料在包装、纺织和建筑中至关重要\[10.1021/cs4003436\]。丙烯，拥有更广泛的衍生物，由于聚丙烯消费量的增加，其需求显著增长\[10.1021/cs4003436\]。其应用范围包括丙烯氧化物、异丙苯、丙烯腈、异丙醇和其他重要的工业化学品\[10.1021/cs4003436\]。

                    轻烯烃的主要生产方法包括蒸汽裂解、流化催化裂化（FCC）和催化脱氢\[10.1002/9783527610044.hetcat0162\]。蒸汽裂解是主要方法，每年可生产超过100万吨的乙烯\[10.1002/9783527610044.hetcat0162\]。催化裂化，尤其是FCC，是另一种来源，特别是对于丙烯\[10.1002/9783527610044.hetcat0162\]。预计丙烯生产中丙烷催化脱氢的比例将上升，这是由于在石脑油裂解器中存在热力学限制，以及来自基于乙烷的蒸汽裂解器的丙烯产量较低\[10.1002/9783527610044.hetcat0162\]。丙烯的增长率预计会超过乙烯\[10.1002/9783527610044.hetcat0162\]。

                    轻烯烃的合成面临热力学挑战，需要高温和低压以实现高转化率\[10.1021/cr5002436_0\]。C2-C4烷烃的脱氢需要在1巴下达到50%转化率时的温度为550-750°C\[10.1021/cr5002436_0\]。脱氢所需的能量随着烷烃链的增长而减少，对于轻烷烃来说需要更多的能量\[10.1021/cr5002436_0\]。

                    通过丙烷催化脱氢生产丙烯已经稳步增长，受到经济条件和丙烷可用性的影响\[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x\]。通过铂催化剂催化脱氢生产乙烯具有高度选择性，但在使用乙烷时面临严峻的操作条件\[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x\]。与重烷烃相比，脱氢轻烷烃需要更高的温度，丙烷在40%转化率时需要至少580摄氏度，而十二烷只需450摄氏度\[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x\]。烷烃脱氢的平衡常数随着碳数的增加而增加\[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x\]。

                    催化脱氢是生产轻烯烃，尤其是丙烯的关键技术，面临高反应焓引起的热力学平衡限制\[10.1007/s11244-012-9912-1\]。这需要高温（>600°C）和低压（<30 psig）的操作以实现经济转化\[10.1007/s11244-012-9912-1\]。催化剂设计必须平衡化学和质量传递特性，通常使用形状粒子和孔结构修改以增强活性和选择性\[10.1007/s11244-012-9912-1\]。Lummus/UOP SMART工艺创新性地燃烧脱氢过程中释放的氢气，直接加热反应混合物，减少蒸汽消耗，并增加苯乙烯单体的生产，从而提高单程乙苯转化率并具有高选择性\[10.1007/s11244-012-9912-1\]。
