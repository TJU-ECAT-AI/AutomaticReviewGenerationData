                    The chemical industry anticipates a significant rise in demand for light olefins, notably ethylene and propylene, as highlighted in reference \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. These are primarily produced through steam cracking, fluid-catalytic-cracking, and catalytic dehydrogenation. Direct conversion of alkanes to olefins, particularly ethane and propane to commodity chemical intermediates, could revolutionize the industry \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. The economic viability of alkane-based processes hinges on the catalyst's efficiency for alkane conversion and selectivity to desired products, addressing thermodynamic challenges related to temperature, pressure, and alkane chain length \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. 

                    Light olefins, ethylene and propylene, are the top petrochemical intermediates, as elucidated in reference \[10.1007/s11244-012-9917-9\]. Ethylene is mainly produced by thermal cracking of ethane, LPG, and heavier feedstocks. Propylene, a byproduct of LPG cracking, has seen increased demand, leading to propane dehydrogenation units. Isobutane dehydrogenation produces isobutylene, converted to MTBE or isooctane. Dehydrogenation of light paraffins, like propane, requires higher temperatures than heavy paraffins; for instance, propane needs at least 580 C for 40% conversion, compared to 450 C for dodecane \[10.1007/s11244-012-9917-9\].

                    Light olefins, including propylene and ethylene, are vital in the chemical industry for producing plastics, detergents, and synthetic fibers, as detailed in reference \[10.1126/science.1226840\]. Steam cracking, using ethane as feed, yields high amounts of ethylene and propylene but also produces a mix of olefins, hydrocarbons, and hydrogen. Steam reforming, though costly, requires high pressures (15-40 atm) and temperatures (700-1000°C) and faces heat transfer and coke management challenges. Overall, these processes have efficiencies of 75% or less. Process chemistry, such as free-radical routes and catalytic partial oxidation, converts heavier alkanes in natural gas into valuable light olefins \[10.1126/science.1226840\].

                    化学工业预计对轻烯烃，特别是乙烯和丙烯的需求将显著增加，如\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]中所述。这些主要通过蒸汽裂解、流化催化裂化和催化脱氢生产。直接将烷烃转化为烯烃，特别是将乙烷和丙烷转化为商品化学中间体，可能彻底改变行业\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。基于烷烃的工艺的经济可行性取决于催化剂对烷烃转化的效率和对所需产品的选择性，解决与温度、压力和烷烃链长度相关的热力学挑战\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。

                    轻烯烃，乙烯和丙烯，是顶级石化中间体，如\[10.1007/s11244-012-9917-9\]中所述。乙烯主要通过热裂解乙烷、LPG和较重的原料生产。丙烯，作为LPG裂解的副产品，需求增加，导致丙烷脱氢装置的出现。异丁烷脱氢产生异丁烯，转化为MTBE或异辛烷。轻质石蜡，如丙烷的脱氢，需要比重石蜡更高的温度；例如，丙烷至少需要580 C才能达到40%的转化率，而十二烷只需要450 C\[10.1007/s11244-012-9917-9\]。

                    包括丙烯和乙烯在内的轻烯烃对化学工业至关重要，用于生产塑料、洗涤剂和合成纤维，如\[10.1126/science.1226840\]中详述。使用乙烷作为原料的蒸汽裂解产生大量乙烯和丙烯，但也产生一系列烯烃、碳氢化合物和氢气。虽然成本高昂，但蒸汽重整需要高压（15-40 atm）和高温（700-1000°C），并面临传热和焦炭管理挑战。总体而言，这些过程的效率在75%或以下。过程化学，如自由基途径和催化部分氧化，将天然气中的较重烷烃转化为有价值的轻烯烃\[10.1126/science.1226840\]。
