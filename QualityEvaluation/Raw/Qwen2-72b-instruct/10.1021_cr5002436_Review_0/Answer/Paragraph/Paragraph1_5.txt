                    The industrial importance of light olefins, particularly ethylene and propylene, is underscored by their vast production volumes and diverse applications in the chemical industry \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. These olefins are predominantly produced through steam cracking, fluid-catalytic-cracking, and catalytic dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. The economic viability of alkane-based chemical processes is determined by the catalyst's activity for conversion and selectivity to desired products \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Direct conversion of alkanes to olefins could revolutionize the chemical industry \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Thermodynamic challenges, including temperature, pressure, and alkane chain length effects, are pivotal in the synthesis of light olefins \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\].

                    Propylene production via catalytic dehydrogenation of propane has seen steady growth and is expected to expand further under favorable economic conditions \[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x\]. Ethylene production through catalytic dehydrogenation over platinum catalysts is selective but extending this to ethane requires harsher conditions, leading to catalyst coking or costly vacuum operations \[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x\]. Higher temperatures are needed for dehydrogenation of light paraffins compared to heavy paraffins, with propane requiring at least 580 degrees Celsius for 40% conversion versus 450 degrees Celsius for dodecane \[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x\].

                    Catalytic dehydrogenation is a critical technology for producing light olefins, particularly propylene, involving high reaction enthalpy and thermodynamic equilibrium limitations \[10.1007/s11244-012-9912-1\]. This necessitates high temperatures (>600°C) and lower pressures (<30 psig) for economic conversion \[10.1007/s11244-012-9912-1\]. Catalyst design must balance chemical and mass transport properties for high activity and selectivity, addressing intraparticle pore diffusional limitations \[10.1007/s11244-012-9912-1\]. Platinum modifiers selectively weaken platinum-olefin interaction without affecting platinum-paraffin interaction, enhancing selectivity and stability \[10.1007/s11244-012-9912-1\].

                    轻烯烃，尤其是乙烯和丙烯，在化学工业中的工业重要性由其庞大的生产量和广泛的应用所凸显\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。这些烯烃主要通过蒸汽裂解、流化催化裂化和催化脱氢生产\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。基于烷烃的化学过程的经济可行性取决于催化剂的转化活性和对所需产品的选择性\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。直接将烷烃转化为烯烃可能彻底改变化学工业\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。轻烯烃合成的热力学挑战，包括温度、压力和烷烃链长的影响，是至关重要的\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。

                    通过催化脱氢生产丙烯的丙烷催化脱氢过程已经稳步增长，并且在有利的经济条件下预计将进一步扩大\[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x\]。通过铂催化剂催化脱氢生产乙烯是选择性的，但将此扩展到乙烷需要更苛刻的条件，导致催化剂积碳或昂贵的真空操作\[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x\]。轻质石蜡的脱氢需要比重质石蜡更高的温度，丙烷至少需要580摄氏度才能达到40%的转化率，而十二烷只需要450摄氏度\[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x\]。

                    催化脱氢是生产轻烯烃，特别是丙烯的关键技术，涉及高反应焓和热力学平衡限制\[10.1007/s11244-012-9912-1\]。这需要高温（>600°C）和较低的压力（<30 psig）以实现经济转换\[10.1007/s11244-012-9912-1\]。催化剂设计必须平衡化学和质量传递特性，以实现高活性和选择性，解决颗粒内部孔扩散限制\[10.1007/s11244-012-9912-1\]。铂修饰剂有选择性地削弱铂-烯烃相互作用，而不影响铂-石蜡相互作用，从而增强选择性和稳定性\[10.1007/s11244-012-9912-1\]。
