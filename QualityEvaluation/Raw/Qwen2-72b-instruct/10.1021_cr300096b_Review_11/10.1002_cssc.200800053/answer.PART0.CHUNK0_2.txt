<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1.The process of selective oxygenate reforming, such as the conversion of glycerol into H2 and CO, contributes to sustainable energy and chemical production by utilizing biomass-derived feedstocks that are carbon-neutral and renewable. This method offers several key advantages over traditional methods: it increases the energy supply without raising CO2 emissions, produces H2 for use in fuel cells, and can generate H2/CO mixtures for Fischer-Tropsch synthesis, yielding liquid alkanes compatible with existing infrastructure. The use of smaller oxygenates as models for reforming larger, biomass-derived oxygenates validates the approach and facilitates the development of bimetallic catalysts with enhanced reforming activity.
