                The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism, a cornerstone in catalytic dehydrogenation on metal surfaces, encompasses four pivotal steps: adsorption, hydrogen dissociation, hydrogen migration, and desorption \[10.1021/jp909163z\]. The identity of the rate-limiting step remains a subject of debate, influenced by factors such as the type of metal, the nature of the hydrocarbon, and the reaction conditions \[10.1021/jp909163z\]. Despite nearly a century of extensive study, the mechanism's relevance persists in scientific discourse due to its foundational role in understanding hydrocarbon dehydrogenation, a critical process in the petrochemical industry \[10.1021/jp909163z\].

                The mechanism's application to ethane dehydrogenation over Cr/Al2O3 exemplifies its four steps: end-on, dissociative adsorption of ethane on a Cr3+O active site, followed by ethene and hydrogen desorption \[10.1007/s10562-005-6521-7\]. Ethane adsorption is suggested as the rate-determining step, supported by transient kinetic data \[10.1007/s10562-005-6521-7\]. The absence of isotopic scrambling indicates that coke deposits are not involved in the cycle \[10.1007/s10562-005-6521-7\]. The ongoing debate over the rate-limiting step underscores the mechanism's enduring scientific interest and controversy \[10.1007/s10562-005-6521-7\].

                Studies focusing on ethane, propane, and butane dehydrogenation over Pt and Pt-Sn alloy catalysts reveal the cleavage of the first CH bond upon alkane adsorption as the rate-determining step \[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]. Selectivity towards alkene production is attributed to higher barriers for the third hydrogen atom cleavage and low alkene binding energies \[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]. An adsorbed H atom facilitates alkene desorption over further dehydrogenation \[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]. Substituting a Sn atom in the cluster to form Pt3Sn lowers the activation barrier for the rate-limiting step, enhancing catalyst activity \[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]. The ongoing debate on the mechanism's rate-limiting step and the balance between alkene production and coke precursor formation remain topics of scientific interest and controversy \[10.1039/c3cp53796j\].

                Horiuti-Polanyi机制是金属表面催化脱氢反应的核心，包括四个关键步骤：吸附、氢解离、氢迁移和解吸\[10.1021/jp909163z\]。速率限制步骤的身份仍然是一个争议的话题，受到金属类型、烃类性质和反应条件等因素的影响\[10.1021/jp909163z\]。尽管近一个世纪的广泛研究，该机制在科学讨论中的相关性仍然存在，因为它在理解石化工业中关键的烃类脱氢过程方面起着基础性作用\[10.1021/jp909163z\]。

                该机制在Cr/Al2O3上对乙烷脱氢的应用，通过四个步骤来体现：Cr3+O活性位点上的端部、解离性吸附乙烷，随后是乙烯和氢的解吸\[10.1007/s10562-005-6521-7\]。乙烷吸附被建议为速率决定步骤，由瞬态动力学数据支持\[10.1007/s10562-005-6521-7\]。同位素混乱的缺失表明焦炭沉积物不参与循环\[10.1007/s10562-005-6521-7\]。关于速率限制步骤的持续辩论凸显了该机制在科学文献中持久的兴趣和争议\[10.1007/s10562-005-6521-7\]。

                专注于Pt和Pt-Sn合金催化剂上乙烷、丙烷和丁烷脱氢的研究揭示了烷烃吸附时第一个CH键的裂解是速率决定步骤\[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]。选择性地生产烯烃归因于第三个氢原子裂解的较高障碍和低烯烃结合能\[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]。吸附的H原子促进了烯烃的解吸，而不是进一步的脱氢\[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]。在簇中用Sn原子取代形成Pt3Sn降低了速率限制步骤的活化势垒，提高了催化剂的活性\[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]。关于机制的速率限制步骤和烯烃生产与焦炭前体形成之间的平衡的持续辩论仍然是科学兴趣和争议的话题\[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]。
