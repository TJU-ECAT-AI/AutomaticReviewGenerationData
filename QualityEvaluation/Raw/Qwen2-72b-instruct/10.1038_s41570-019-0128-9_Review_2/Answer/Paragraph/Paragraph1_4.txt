        <English> 
                Advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques, including XRD, XANES, EXAFS, and ADF-STEM with EELS, have been utilized to elucidate the structural properties, oxidation states, spatial distribution, and morphological characteristics of active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts for CO2-assisted dehydrogenation of ethane \[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]. XRD experiments revealed the structural information of the catalysts under reaction conditions, showing the formation of Ni-rich NiFe alloy with fcc structure. XANES spectra determined the valence state of Ni and Fe, indicating metallic Ni and Fe with features between FeO and Fe3O4. EXAFS fitting results revealed the coordination numbers and bond lengths of Ni and Fe, suggesting the formation of metallic NiFe alloy particles with diameters of 2.5 to 4.0 nm. ADF-STEM with EELS analysis confirmed the formation of amorphous FeOx thin layers on CeO2, with a 2-nm layer observed in Ni1Fe3/CeO2 but absent in Ni3Fe1/CeO2. These findings highlight the differences in the structural properties and spatial distribution of active sites between the two catalysts, which have implications for their performance and design \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive characterization of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced analytical techniques has provided insights into the active sites responsible for catalytic activity in CO2-assisted dehydrogenation of ethane. Future research might focus on the development of novel catalysts with optimized Ni/Fe ratios for enhanced performance. The exploration of the role of CeO2 in promoting the formation of FeOx layers and its impact on catalytic activity could also be a promising direction. Additionally, the use of in situ techniques for real-time monitoring of catalyst behavior under reaction conditions could further enhance our understanding of the catalytic mechanisms involved.

                利用包括XRD、XANES、EXAFS和ADF-STEM与EELS在内的先进原位和非原位分析技术，对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂的结构特性、氧化态、空间分布和形态特征进行了研究，以了解其在CO2辅助乙烷脱氢中的作用 \[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]。XRD实验揭示了催化剂在反应条件下的结构信息，显示形成了Ni丰富的NiFe合金，具有fcc结构。XANES光谱确定了Ni和Fe的价态，表明Ni为金属态，Fe的特征介于FeO和Fe3O4之间。EXAFS拟合结果揭示了Ni和Fe的配位数和键长，表明形成了直径为2.5至4.0 nm的金属NiFe合金颗粒。ADF-STEM与EELS分析证实了CeO2上形成了2 nm厚的无定形FeOx薄层，而Ni3Fe1/CeO2中未观察到这种层。这些发现突出了两种催化剂之间活性位点的结构特性和空间分布的差异，对它们的性能和设计具有重要意义 \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2\].
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: 利用先进分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂的全面表征为了解CO2辅助乙烷脱氢中负责催化活性的活性位点提供了见解。未来的研究可能集中在开发具有优化Ni/Fe比的新催化剂，以提高性能。探索CeO2在促进FeOx层形成及其对催化活性影响方面的作用也可能是一个有前景的方向。此外，使用原位技术实时监测催化剂在反应条件下的行为，可以进一步增强我们对涉及的催化机制的理解。
