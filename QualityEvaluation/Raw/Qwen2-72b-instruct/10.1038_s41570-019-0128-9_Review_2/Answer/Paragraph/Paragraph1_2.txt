        <English> 
                The comprehensive characterization of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques has provided significant insights into their structural, chemical, and morphological properties at atomic and nanoscale levels \[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]. XRD experiments revealed the structural information of the catalysts under reaction conditions, showing the formation of Ni-rich NiFe alloy with fcc structure. XANES spectra determined the valence state of Ni and Fe, indicating metallic Ni and Fe with near-edge features between FeO and Fe3O4. EXAFS fitting results revealed the coordination numbers and bond lengths of Ni and Fe, suggesting the formation of metallic NiFe alloy particles with diameters of 2.5 to 4.0 nm. ADF-STEM with EELS analysis confirmed the formation of amorphous FeOx thin layers on CeO2, with a 2-nm layer observed in Ni1Fe3/CeO2 but absent in Ni3Fe1/CeO2. These findings highlight the differences in the structural properties and spatial distribution of active sites between the two catalysts, which have implications for their performance and design in CO2-assisted dehydrogenation of ethane \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The synergistic use of advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques has significantly advanced our understanding of the active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts. Future research should focus on correlating the observed structural and chemical properties with catalytic performance, particularly in the context of CO2-assisted dehydrogenation of ethane. The development of new catalysts with tailored active sites, informed by these detailed characterizations, could lead to enhanced activity and selectivity. Moreover, the exploration of novel in situ techniques that can provide real-time information on catalyst behavior under reaction conditions is warranted. This could include the use of in situ X-ray tomography and in situ Raman spectroscopy, which could offer additional insights into the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and composition during reaction. The integration of machine learning algorithms for data analysis could also revolutionize the interpretation of complex in situ data, enabling the prediction of catalyst performance based on structural and chemical properties.

                利用先进的原位和非原位分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂进行了全面的表征，为了解它们在原子和纳米尺度上的结构、化学和形态学性质提供了重要见解\[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]。XRD实验揭示了催化剂在反应条件下的结构信息，显示形成了Ni丰富的NiFe合金，具有fcc结构。XANES光谱确定了Ni和Fe的价态，表明Ni为金属态，Fe的近边特征介于FeO和Fe3O4之间。EXAFS拟合结果揭示了Ni和Fe的配位数和键长，表明形成了直径为2.5至4.0 nm的金属NiFe合金颗粒。ADF-STEM与EELS分析证实了CeO2上形成了无定形FeOx薄层，其中在Ni1Fe3/CeO2中观察到了2-nm的层，而在Ni3Fe1/CeO2中则不存在。这些发现突出了两种催化剂之间活性位点的结构特性和空间分布的差异，这对它们在CO2辅助乙烷脱氢中的性能和设计具有重要意义\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: 利用先进的原位和非原位分析技术的协同作用，我们对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂的活性位点有了显著的了解。未来的研究应侧重于将观察到的结构和化学性质与催化性能相关联，特别是在CO2辅助乙烷脱氢的背景下。基于这些详细表征信息开发具有定制活性位点的新催化剂，可能会导致活性和选择性的增强。此外，探索能够在反应条件下提供实时信息的新型原位技术是值得的。这可能包括使用原位X射线断层扫描和原位拉曼光谱，这些技术可以提供催化剂结构和组成在反应过程中动态变化的额外见解。将机器学习算法整合到数据分析中也可能彻底改变复杂原位数据的解释，使我们能够根据结构和化学性质预测催化剂性能。
