                The comprehensive characterization of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques has provided profound insights into their structural, chemical, and morphological properties at atomic and nanoscale levels \[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]. XRD experiments revealed the structural information of the catalysts under reaction conditions, showing the formation of Ni-rich NiFe alloy with fcc structure. XANES spectra determined the valence state of Ni and Fe, indicating metallic Ni and Fe with near-edge features between FeO and Fe3O4. EXAFS fitting results revealed the coordination numbers and bond lengths of Ni and Fe, suggesting the formation of metallic NiFe alloy particles with diameters of 2.5 to 4.0 nm. ADF-STEM with EELS analysis confirmed the formation of amorphous FeOx thin layers on CeO2, with a 2-nm layer observed in Ni1Fe3/CeO2 but absent in Ni3Fe1/CeO2 \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2\]. These findings highlight the differences in the structural properties and spatial distribution of active sites between the two catalysts, which have implications for their performance and design in CO2-assisted dehydrogenation of ethane.
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The synergistic use of advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques has unveiled the intricate details of active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts, providing a solid foundation for the rational design of catalysts for propane dehydrogenation. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel catalysts with optimized Ni/Fe ratios and enhanced stability, leveraging the insights gained from the detailed characterization of active sites. The exploration of new synthesis methods that can precisely control the formation of FeOx layers on CeO2 could lead to catalysts with improved activity and selectivity. Moreover, the integration of machine learning algorithms with experimental data could accelerate the discovery of catalysts with tailored properties for specific reactions, such as propane dehydrogenation. The application of in situ spectroscopic techniques to monitor the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and composition under reaction conditions could further enhance our understanding of the catalytic mechanisms and guide the design of more efficient catalysts.

                利用先进的原位和非原位分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂进行了全面的表征，提供了它们在原子和纳米尺度上的结构、化学和形态学性质的深刻见解\[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]。XRD实验揭示了催化剂在反应条件下的结构信息，显示了富Ni的NiFe合金的fcc结构的形成。XANES光谱确定了Ni和Fe的价态，表明Ni为金属态，Fe的近边特征介于FeO和Fe3O4之间。EXAFS拟合结果揭示了Ni和Fe的配位数和键长，表明形成了直径为2.5至4.0 nm的金属NiFe合金颗粒。ADF-STEM与EELS分析证实了CeO2上形成了无定形FeOx薄层，其中在Ni1Fe3/CeO2中观察到了2 nm的层，而在Ni3Fe1/CeO2中则不存在\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2\]。这些发现突出了两种催化剂之间活性位点的结构特性和空间分布的差异，对它们在CO2辅助乙烷脱氢中的性能和设计具有重要意义。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: 利用先进的原位和非原位分析技术的协同作用，揭示了Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂中活性位点的复杂细节，为丙烷脱氢催化剂的合理设计奠定了坚实的基础。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发具有优化的Ni/Fe比和增强稳定性的新型催化剂上，利用从活性位点的详细表征中获得的见解。探索能够精确控制CeO2上FeOx层形成的新型合成方法，可能会导致活性和选择性提高的催化剂。此外，将机器学习算法与实验数据相结合，可以加速具有特定反应（如丙烷脱氢）定制特性的催化剂的发现。在反应条件下使用原位光谱技术监测催化剂结构和组成的动态变化，可以进一步增强我们对催化机制的理解，并指导更高效催化剂的设计。
