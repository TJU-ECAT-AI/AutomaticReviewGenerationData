        <English> 
                Advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques, including XRD, XANES, EXAFS, and ADF-STEM with EELS, have been employed to elucidate the structural properties, oxidation states, spatial distribution, and morphological characteristics of active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts for propane dehydrogenation \[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]. XRD experiments revealed the structural information of the catalysts under reaction conditions, showing the formation of Ni-rich NiFe alloy with fcc structure. XANES spectra were collected to determine the valence state of Ni and Fe, indicating that the oxidation state of Ni was metallic Ni, while Fe exhibited similar near-edge features between FeO and Fe3O4 \[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]. EXAFS fitting results revealed the coordination numbers and bond lengths of Ni and Fe, suggesting the formation of metallic NiFe alloy particles with diameters of 2.5 to 4.0 nm \[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]. ADF-STEM with EELS analysis confirmed the formation of amorphous FeOx thin layers on CeO2, with a 2-nm layer observed in Ni1Fe3/CeO2 but absent in Ni3Fe1/CeO2 \[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]. These findings highlight the differences in the structural properties and spatial distribution of active sites between the two catalysts, which have implications for their performance and design in CO2-assisted dehydrogenation of ethane \[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\].

                ※※※

                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive characterization of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced analytical techniques has provided insights into the active sites responsible for catalytic activity in propane dehydrogenation. The presence of a thin FeOx layer on CeO2 in Fe3Ni1/CeO2, as opposed to its absence in Fe1Ni3/CeO2, suggests a significant role in catalytic performance. Future research might focus on the precise control of the FeOx layer thickness and its interaction with CeO2 to optimize catalytic activity. Moreover, the exploration of other transition metal ratios in NiFe/CeO2 catalysts could lead to the discovery of more efficient catalysts for propane dehydrogenation. The use of in situ techniques for real-time monitoring of catalyst behavior under reaction conditions is also a promising direction for future studies.

                利用包括XRD、XANES、EXAFS和ADF-STEM与EELS在内的先进原位和非原位分析技术，对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂的结构特性、氧化态、空间分布和形态特征进行了深入研究，以用于丙烷脱氢\[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]。XRD实验揭示了催化剂在反应条件下的结构信息，显示形成了Ni丰富的NiFe合金，具有fcc结构。XANES光谱用于确定Ni和Fe的价态，表明Ni的氧化态为金属Ni，而Fe的近边特征介于FeO和Fe3O4之间\[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]。EXAFS拟合结果揭示了Ni和Fe的配位数和键长，表明形成了直径为2.5至4.0 nm的金属NiFe合金颗粒\[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]。ADF-STEM与EELS分析证实了CeO2上形成了无定形FeOx薄层，其中在Ni1Fe3/CeO2中观察到2 nm的层，而在Ni3Fe1/CeO2中则不存在\[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]。这些发现突出了两种催化剂之间活性位点的结构特性和空间分布的差异，对它们在CO2辅助乙烷脱氢中的性能和设计具有重要意义\[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]。

                ※※※

                综合理解与未来展望: 利用先进分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂的全面表征为丙烷脱氢中催化活性位点提供了深入理解。Fe3Ni1/CeO2中CeO2上存在薄FeOx层，而在Fe1Ni3/CeO2中不存在，这表明其在催化性能中起着重要作用。未来的研究可能集中在精确控制FeOx层的厚度及其与CeO2的相互作用，以优化催化活性。此外，探索NiFe/CeO2催化剂中其他过渡金属比例可能会发现更有效的丙烷脱氢催化剂。在反应条件下实时监测催化剂行为的原位技术的使用也是未来研究的一个有希望的方向。
