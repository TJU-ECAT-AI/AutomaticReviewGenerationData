        <English> 
                The comprehensive characterization of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques, including XRD, XANES, EXAFS, and ADF-STEM with EELS, has provided profound insights into their structural, chemical, and morphological properties at atomic and nanoscale levels \[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]. XRD experiments revealed the structural information of the catalysts under reaction conditions, showing the formation of Ni-rich NiFe alloy with fcc structure. XANES spectra determined the valence state of Ni and Fe, indicating metallic Ni and Fe with near-edge features between FeO and Fe3O4. EXAFS fitting results revealed the coordination numbers and bond lengths of Ni and Fe, suggesting the formation of metallic NiFe alloy particles with diameters of 2.5 to 4.0 nm. ADF-STEM with EELS analysis confirmed the formation of amorphous FeOx thin layers on CeO2, with a 2-nm layer observed in Ni1Fe3/CeO2 but absent in Ni3Fe1/CeO2. These findings highlight the differences in the structural properties and spatial distribution of active sites between the two catalysts, which have implications for their performance and design in CO2-assisted dehydrogenation of ethane \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The synergistic use of advanced analytical techniques has elucidated the complex interplay between the structural, chemical, and morphological properties of active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts. These findings underscore the critical role of the Ni/Fe ratio in determining the formation of FeOx layers on CeO2, which significantly impacts the catalytic activity and selectivity in CO2-assisted dehydrogenation of ethane. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel catalysts with tailored active sites for enhanced performance in propane dehydrogenation. This could involve the exploration of alternative support materials and the optimization of metal ratios to achieve a desired balance between structural stability and catalytic activity. Additionally, the integration of machine learning algorithms with experimental data could facilitate the prediction of catalyst performance based on their structural and chemical properties, paving the way for a more rational design of catalysts for propane dehydrogenation.

                综合理解与未来展望: 先进分析技术的协同应用阐明了Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂中活性位点的结构、化学和形态学特性之间的复杂相互作用。这些发现凸显了Ni/Fe比在决定CeO2上FeOx层的形成中所起的关键作用，这极大地影响了CO2辅助乙烷脱氢中的催化活性和选择性。未来的研究工作可能集中在开发具有定制活性位点的新型催化剂，以增强丙烷脱氢的性能。这可能涉及探索替代的载体材料和优化金属比例，以实现结构稳定性和催化活性之间的理想平衡。此外，将机器学习算法与实验数据相结合，可以促进基于催化剂的结构和化学性质预测其性能，为丙烷脱氢催化剂的更合理设计铺平道路。
