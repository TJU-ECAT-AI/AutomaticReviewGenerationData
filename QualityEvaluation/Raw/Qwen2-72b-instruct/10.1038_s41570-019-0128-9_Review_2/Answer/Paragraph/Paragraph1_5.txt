            <English> 
                    The comprehensive characterization of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques, including XRD, XANES, EXAFS, and ADF-STEM with EELS, has provided detailed insights into their structural, chemical, and morphological properties at atomic and nanoscale levels \[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]. XRD experiments revealed the structural information of the catalysts under reaction conditions, showing the formation of Ni-rich NiFe alloy with fcc structure. XANES spectra determined the valence state of Ni and Fe, indicating metallic Ni and Fe with near-edge features between FeO and Fe3O4. EXAFS fitting results revealed the coordination numbers and bond lengths of Ni and Fe, suggesting the formation of metallic NiFe alloy particles with diameters of 2.5 to 4.0 nm. ADF-STEM with EELS analysis confirmed the formation of amorphous FeOx thin layers on CeO2, with a 2-nm layer observed in Ni1Fe3/CeO2 but absent in Ni3Fe1/CeO2 \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The synergistic use of advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques has unveiled the intricate details of active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts, providing a foundation for the design of more efficient propane dehydrogenation catalysts. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel catalysts with tailored active sites for enhanced selectivity and stability. The exploration of the dynamic behavior of active sites under reaction conditions, facilitated by in situ techniques, could offer insights into the catalytic mechanisms and guide the rational design of catalysts. Moreover, the integration of computational methods with experimental data could provide a deeper understanding of the structure-performance relationships, paving the way for the discovery of new catalysts with optimized properties.

                    利用包括XRD、XANES、EXAFS和ADF-STEM与EELS在内的先进原位和非原位分析技术，对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂进行了全面的表征，提供了它们在原子和纳米尺度上的结构、化学和形态学性质的详细信息\[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]。XRD实验揭示了催化剂在反应条件下的结构信息，显示形成了Ni丰富的NiFe合金，具有fcc结构。XANES光谱确定了Ni和Fe的价态，表明Ni为金属态，Fe的近边特征介于FeO和Fe3O4之间。EXAFS拟合结果揭示了Ni和Fe的配位数和键长，表明形成了直径为2.5至4.0 nm的金属NiFe合金颗粒。ADF-STEM与EELS分析证实了CeO2上形成了无定形FeOx薄层，其中在Ni1Fe3/CeO2中观察到了2 nm的层，而在Ni3Fe1/CeO2中则不存在\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 先进的原位和非原位分析技术的协同应用揭示了Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂中活性位点的复杂细节，为设计更高效的丙烷脱氢催化剂奠定了基础。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发具有定制活性位点的新型催化剂，以增强选择性和稳定性。通过原位技术探索活性位点在反应条件下的动态行为，可以提供对催化机制的见解，并指导催化剂的理性设计。此外，将计算方法与实验数据相结合，可以更深入地理解结构-性能关系，为发现具有优化性质的新催化剂开辟道路。
