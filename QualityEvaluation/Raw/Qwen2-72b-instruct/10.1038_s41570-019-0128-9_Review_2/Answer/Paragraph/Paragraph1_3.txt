            <English> 
                    The comprehensive characterization of active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques has provided significant insights into their structural, chemical, and morphological properties at atomic and nanoscale levels \[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]. XRD experiments revealed the structural information of the catalysts under reaction conditions, showing the formation of Ni-rich NiFe alloy with fcc structure. XANES spectra determined the valence state of Ni and Fe, indicating metallic Ni and Fe with near-edge features between FeO and Fe3O4. EXAFS fitting results revealed the coordination numbers and bond lengths of Ni and Fe, suggesting the formation of metallic NiFe alloy particles with diameters of 2.5 to 4.0 nm. ADF-STEM with EELS analysis confirmed the formation of amorphous FeOx thin layers on CeO2, with a 2-nm layer observed in Ni1Fe3/CeO2 but absent in Ni3Fe1/CeO2. These findings highlight the differences in the structural properties and spatial distribution of active sites between the two catalysts, which have implications for their performance and design in CO2-assisted dehydrogenation of ethane \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The synergistic use of advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques has significantly advanced our understanding of the active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts. These techniques have revealed the formation of Ni-rich NiFe alloy with fcc structure, the oxidation states of Ni and Fe, and the presence of amorphous FeOx thin layers on CeO2. The differences in the structural properties and spatial distribution of active sites between the two catalysts have implications for their performance and design in CO2-assisted dehydrogenation of ethane. Future research might focus on exploring the role of these active sites in the catalytic activity and stability, as well as the development of new catalysts with optimized active sites for enhanced performance. The integration of in situ and ex situ techniques will continue to play a crucial role in the design and optimization of catalysts for various chemical reactions.

                    利用先进的原位和非原位分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂的活性位点进行了全面表征，这些技术提供了它们在原子和纳米尺度上的结构、化学和形态学性质的重要见解\[10.1073/pnas.1806950115\]。XRD实验揭示了催化剂在反应条件下的结构信息，显示形成了Ni丰富的NiFe合金，具有fcc结构。XANES光谱确定了Ni和Fe的价态，表明Ni为金属态，Fe的近边特征介于FeO和Fe3O4之间。EXAFS拟合结果揭示了Ni和Fe的配位数和键长，表明形成了直径为2.5至4.0 nm的金属NiFe合金颗粒。ADF-STEM与EELS分析证实了CeO2上形成了无定形FeOx薄层，其中在Ni1Fe3/CeO2中观察到了2 nm的层，而在Ni3Fe1/CeO2中则不存在。这些发现突出了两种催化剂之间活性位点的结构特性和空间分布的差异，这对它们在CO2辅助乙烷脱氢中的性能和设计具有重要意义\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 先进的原位和非原位分析技术的协同使用显著提高了我们对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂活性位点的理解。这些技术揭示了Ni丰富的NiFe合金的形成，具有fcc结构，Ni和Fe的氧化态，以及CeO2上无定形FeOx薄层的存在。两种催化剂之间活性位点的结构特性和空间分布的差异对它们在CO2辅助乙烷脱氢中的性能和设计具有重要意义。未来的研究可能会关注这些活性位点在催化活性和稳定性中的作用，以及开发具有优化活性位点的新催化剂，以提高性能。原位和非原位技术的结合将继续在各种化学反应的催化剂设计和优化中发挥关键作用。
