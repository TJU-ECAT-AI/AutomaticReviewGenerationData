<English>
The elucidation of Ga speciation in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts under propane dehydrogenation (PDH) conditions presents significant challenges, primarily due to the dynamic and complex nature of the catalytic environment \[10.1039/c6cy00698a\]. The high operating temperatures and reducing environment make it difficult to detect short-lived reaction intermediates, complicating the understanding of the active species and their role in the reaction mechanism \[10.1002/advs.202207756_1\]. Moreover, the exact role of gallium in the dehydrogenation reactions remains unclear, owing to the wide variety of possible structures, including bulk Ga2O3 aggregates on the external zeolite surface, small gallium oxide aggregates occluded in the zeolite micropore space, and reduced Ga species such as Ga2O and cationic Ga species in oxidized or reduced form \[10.1007/s10562-004-3753-x\].

In situ or operando characterization techniques, such as X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS) and in situ FTIR spectroscopy, are crucial for overcoming these challenges \[10.1002/advs.202207756_1\]. These techniques provide real-time information about the structural and electronic properties of the active Ga species during catalysis, allowing for the monitoring of changes in the local structure and electronic environment of Ga under reaction conditions \[10.1039/c6cy00698a\]. X-ray absorption spectroscopy at the Ga K-edge, for instance, contributes to overcoming the challenges by providing real-time information on the physical and chemical state of Ga under reaction conditions \[10.1006/jcat.1993.1079\]. This approach helps establish the chemical form of catalytic sites and avoids misleading conclusions that can arise from pre- and postreaction characterization, which may not accurately represent the active state of the catalyst.

In situ transmission Fourier Transform infrared (FTIR) spectroscopy has been employed to systematically investigate Ga speciation in Ga/H-ZSM-5 with varying Si/Al ratios and Ga/Al ratios, providing direct experimental evidence for the existence of two types of Ga species on reduced Ga/H-ZSM-5, which may have different catalytic activities in alkane dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.11.004\]. The use of water as a probe molecule further supports the conclusion that isolated Ga+ and Ga+H+ pair sites have distinct properties, with isolated Ga+ being easily oxidized by water at 150 C, while Ga+H+ pair sites are inactive under the same conditions.

In situ X-ray absorption techniques are extremely useful in detecting dispersed and isolated species, providing quantitative correlations between dispersion, acidity, and reducibility of the oxide form of GaZSM5 catalysts of varying composition \[10.1021/jp111635\]. These techniques allow for the detection of dispersed and even isolated species, providing insights into the structure-activity relationships in these catalytic systems. However, the data analysis has been generally limited to qualitative interpretation of the XANES (qualitative correlations between edge shifts and oxidation state of gallium atoms) and EXAFS spectra (qualitative conclusions about the dispersion of gallium species). There is a need for a more quantitative correlation between the dispersion of gallium species and their state of oxidation.

In situ or operando characterization techniques are crucial for advancing our understanding of structure-activity relationships in these catalytic systems by allowing real-time monitoring of the catalyst under reaction conditions \[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.07.021\]. They contribute to overcoming the challenges by providing real-time information about the catalyst's structure and the active species under reaction conditions, thus advancing our understanding of structure-activity relationships in these catalytic systems \[10.1016/s0167-2991(09)60939-3\].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in in situ and operando characterization techniques have significantly contributed to our understanding of Ga speciation in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts under PDH conditions. However, there is still a need for more sophisticated and quantitative methods to correlate the dispersion of gallium species with their state of oxidation. Future research efforts might focus on developing advanced in situ and operando techniques that can provide a more detailed understanding of the dynamic changes in the chemical state of Ga, particularly the reduction and reoxidation of Ga+3 species, and the formation of monomeric hydride species coordinated to basic oxygens within zeolite channels. This could involve the integration of multiple characterization techniques, such as XAS, FTIR, and possibly advanced computational methods, to provide a comprehensive picture of the catalytic process. Additionally, there is a need for more systematic studies that can systematically investigate Ga speciation in Ga/H-ZSM-5 with varying Si/Al ratios and Ga/Al ratios, providing insights into the effect of these parameters on the catalytic activity and selectivity. The development of such methodologies will not only advance our understanding of the structure-activity relationships in these catalytic systems but also guide the design of more efficient and selective catalysts for PDH and other related reactions.

<Chinese>
在Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂下对丙烷脱氢（PDH）条件下Ga的物种化（speciation）进行阐明，面临着显著的挑战，主要由于催化环境的动态和复杂性质\[10.1039/c6cy00698a\]。高温和还原环境使得检测短寿命反应中间体变得困难，这使得理解活性物种及其在反应机制中的作用变得复杂\[10.1002/advs.202207756_1\]。此外，Ga在脱氢反应中的确切作用仍然不清楚，这归因于可能结构的广泛多样性，包括外部沸石表面上的Ga2O3团聚体，沸石微孔空间中被封闭的小Ga氧化物团聚体，以及还原的Ga物种，如Ga2O和氧化或还原形式的Ga阳离子物种\[10.1007/s10562-004-3753-x\]。

原位或操作条件下（operando）的表征技术，如X射线吸收光谱（XAS）和原位FTIR光谱，对于克服这些挑战至关重要\[10.1002/advs.202207756_1\]。这些技术提供了关于催化过程中活性Ga物种的结构和电子性质的实时信息，允许监测Ga在反应条件下的局部结构和电子环境的变化\[10.1039/c6cy00698a\]。例如，Ga K边的X射线吸收光谱有助于克服挑战，通过提供Ga在反应条件下的物理和化学状态的实时信息\[10.1006/jcat.1993.1079\]。这种方法有助于建立催化位点的化学形式，并避免了可能源于预反应和后反应表征的误导性结论，这些结论可能无法准确代表催化剂的活性状态。

原位传输傅里叶变换红外（FTIR）光谱已被用于系统地研究具有不同Si/Al比和Ga/Al比的Ga/H-ZSM-5中的Ga物种化，提供了关于还原Ga/H-ZSM-5上存在两种类型的Ga物种的直接实验证据，这些物种在烷烃脱氢反应中可能具有不同的催化活性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.11.004\]。水作为探针分子的使用进一步支持了孤立Ga+和Ga+H+对位具有不同性质的结论，其中孤立Ga+在150°C下容易被水氧化，而Ga+H+对位在相同条件下不活跃。

原位X射线吸收技术在检测分散和孤立的物种方面极其有用，提供了分散性、酸性和GaZSM5催化剂不同组成氧化物形式的还原性之间的定量相关性\[10.1021/jp111635\]。这些技术允许检测分散和甚至孤立的物种，为这些催化系统中的结构-活性关系提供了见解。然而，数据分析通常仅限于XANES（Ga原子氧化态与边缘位移之间的定性相关性）和EXAFS光谱（关于Ga物种分散性的定性结论）的定性解释。需要更定量的相关性来关联Ga物种的分散性和它们的氧化状态。

原位或操作条件下（operando）的表征技术对于通过允许在反应条件下实时监测催化剂来推进我们对这些催化系统中结构-活性关系的理解至关重要\[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.07.021\]。它们通过提供关于催化剂结构和反应条件下活性物种的实时信息，从而推进我们对这些催化系统中结构-活性关系的理解，有助于克服挑战\[10.1016/s0167-2991(09)60939-3\]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：原位和操作条件下（operando）表征技术的进步对我们在PDH条件下对Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂中Ga的物种化的理解做出了重大贡献。然而，仍然需要更复杂和定量的方法来关联Ga物种的分散性和它们的氧化状态。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发先进的原位和操作条件下（operando）技术，可以提供对Ga的化学状态的动态变化的更深入理解，特别是Ga+3物种的还原和再氧化，以及在沸石通道内与基本氧协调的单体氢化物物种的形成。这可能涉及XAS、FTIR和可能的高级计算方法等多种表征技术的集成，以提供催化过程的全面图景。此外，需要进行更系统的研究，可以系统地研究具有不同Si/Al比和Ga/Al比的Ga/H-ZSM-5中的Ga物种化，为这些参数对催化活性和选择性的影响提供见解。这些方法的发展不仅将推进我们对这些催化系统中结构-活性关系的理解，还将指导更高效和选择性PDH催化剂以及相关反应的设计。
