1.The primary challenges in elucidating Ga speciation in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts under propane dehydrogenation (PDH) conditions are not explicitly stated in the literature. However, the literature does mention that the exact role of gallium in the mechanism of dehydrogenation reactions has not been totally elucidated yet, owing to the wide variety of possible structures including bulk Ga2O3 aggregates on the external zeolite surface, small gallium oxide aggregates occluded in the zeolite micropore space, reduced Ga species such as Ga2O and cationic Ga species in oxidized or reduced form. In situ or operando characterization techniques contribute to overcoming these challenges by providing insights into the structural changes of Ga species under reaction conditions, which is crucial for advancing our understanding of structure-activity relationships in these catalytic systems.
