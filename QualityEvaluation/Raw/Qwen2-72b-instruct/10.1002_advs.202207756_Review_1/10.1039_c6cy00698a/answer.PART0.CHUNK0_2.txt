1.The primary challenges in elucidating Ga speciation in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts under propane dehydrogenation (PDH) conditions include the complex and dynamic nature of the catalytic environment, which can lead to changes in the oxidation state and coordination environment of Ga under reaction conditions. In situ or operando characterization techniques, such as X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS), can contribute to overcoming these challenges by providing real-time information on the structural and electronic properties of the active Ga species during catalysis. These techniques allow for the monitoring of changes in the local structure and electronic environment of Ga under reaction conditions, which is crucial for understanding the structure-activity relationships in these catalytic systems.
