      <English>
          Non-oxidative propane dehydrogenation (PDH) is an attractive reaction for both industrial and scientific reasons, allowing direct large-scale production of propene and fundamental analysis of C-H activation. The main challenges include achieving high activity, selectivity, and on-stream stability of environment-friendly and cost-efficient catalysts without non-noble metals. The approach involves encapsulating NPs into a nitrogen-doped carbon (NC) layer in situ grown from zeolitic imidazolate framework-8 on a Silicalite-1 support. The NC layer controls the size of ZnO NPs and hinders their loss at high temperatures. The designed catalysts exhibit high activity, selectivity, and on-stream stability in PDH, achieving propene selectivity of about 90% at 44.4% propane conversion at 600C after nearly 6 h on stream.
