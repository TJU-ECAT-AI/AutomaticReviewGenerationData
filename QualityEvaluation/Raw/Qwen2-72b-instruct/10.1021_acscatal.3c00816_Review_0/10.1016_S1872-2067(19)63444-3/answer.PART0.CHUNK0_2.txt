1.The growing global demand for propylene has led to increased attention on propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology as an alternative method for propylene production. PDH offers several key advantages over conventional production methods, particularly in terms of feedstock availability, process efficiency, and product selectivity. Unlike traditional methods that rely on steam cracking of naphtha or fluid catalytic cracking of heavier oil, PDH can directly convert propane, a readily available and abundant feedstock, into propylene. This makes PDH a more sustainable and cost-effective option, especially in regions with abundant shale gas resources. Moreover, PDH technology typically exhibits higher process efficiency and product selectivity, as it can directly target the conversion of propane to propylene with minimal production of by-products. This results in a cleaner, more targeted production process that can better meet the increasing demand for propylene while minimizing environmental impact and maximizing economic benefits.
