      <English>
          The literature highlights that propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology is an economical and environmentally friendly route for propylene production compared to traditional thermal or catalytic cracking of crude-oil-derived naphtha, which requires large amounts of energy and generates enormous CO2 emissions. PDH using an alumina-supported Pt catalyst with a Sn promoter can achieve high conversion of propane into propylene with excellent selectivity and stability, minimizing coke formation and sintering processes, which are major causes of catalyst deactivation in conventional methods.
