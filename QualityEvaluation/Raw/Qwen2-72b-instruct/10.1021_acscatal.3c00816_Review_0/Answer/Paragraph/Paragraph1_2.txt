<English>
The emergence of propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) as a promising and advantageous technology to address the challenges of meeting the growing global demand for propylene production, in the context of limitations faced by conventional methods, is substantiated by a plethora of studies. PDH, using propane, meets the rising propylene demand by leveraging the abundance and cost-effectiveness of shale gas alkanes for a competitive production process \[10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081\]. This technology boosts propylene yields but faces challenges at high temperatures, causing coking and particle sintering \[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012\]. Despite these challenges, PDH boosts propylene production, leveraging the abundance of propane for high selectivity and efficiency, addressing the scarcity of fossil oil \[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5\]. It offers a direct, economical route for propylene production, tackling demand and resource issues, albeit with endothermic process challenges and side reactions that necessitate efficient, stable catalysts \[10.3390/catal9020174\]. PDH, using cleaner gas, leads in propylene production, addressing global demand. New plants highlight growth, while CrOx and Pt catalysts have flaws: health risks, cost, and deactivation. Eco-friendly ZrO2, with Ru/Rh, boosts propane dehydrogenation \[10.1038/s41467-018-06174-5\]. PDH's high efficiency, selectivity, and feedstock flexibility make it ideal for surging propylene demand, outshining conventional methods \[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\].
※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements and findings discussed in the file highlight the potential of PDH technology to revolutionize propylene production. The use of shale gas alkanes as a feedstock offers a sustainable and cost-effective alternative to conventional methods, addressing the challenges of feedstock availability and process efficiency. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of more efficient and stable catalysts to mitigate the effects of high temperatures, coking, and particle sintering. Additionally, the exploration of eco-friendly catalysts, such as ZrO2 with Ru/Rh, presents a promising avenue for reducing health risks and costs associated with conventional catalysts. The integration of PDH technology with other processes, such as hydrogen production, could further enhance its economic and environmental benefits. Moreover, the optimization of reaction conditions and the design of modular plants could improve the flexibility and scalability of PDH technology, making it more adaptable to different market demands and feedstock sources.

<Chinese>
丙烷直接脱氢(PDH)技术作为应对全球丙烯需求增长的有前景且具优势的技术,在传统方法面临的局限性背景下,得到了大量研究的证实。PDH利用丙烷,通过利用页岩气烷烃的丰富性和成本效益,满足了丙烯需求的增长,为具有竞争力的生产过程提供了支持\[10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081\]。该技术虽然提高了丙烯产量,但在高温下面临结焦和颗粒烧结的挑战\[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012\]。尽管存在这些挑战,PDH通过利用丙烷的丰富性,提高了选择性和效率,解决了化石油稀缺的问题\[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5\]。它提供了一条直接、经济的丙烯生产路线,应对需求和资源问题,尽管存在吸热过程的挑战和副反应,这需要高效、稳定的催化剂\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。PDH利用更清洁的气体,在丙烯生产中处于领先地位,满足全球需求。新工厂的建设凸显了增长,而CrOx和Pt催化剂存在健康风险、成本和失活的缺点。ZrO2与Ru/Rh结合的环保催化剂增强了丙烷脱氢\[10.1038/s41467-018-06174-5\]。PDH的高效率、选择性和原料灵活性使其成为丙烯需求激增的理想选择,超越了传统方法\[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\]。
※※※
综合理解与未来展望: 文件中讨论的进展和发现突显了PDH技术在丙烯生产领域可能带来的革命性变化。利用页岩气烷烃作为原料提供了一种可持续且成本效益高的替代方案,解决了原料可用性和过程效率的挑战。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发更高效和稳定的催化剂,以减轻高温、结焦和颗粒烧结的影响。此外,探索环保催化剂,如ZrO2与Ru/Rh的结合,为减少与传统催化剂相关的健康风险和成本提供了一条有前景的途径。将PDH技术与其它过程,如氢气生产,的整合可以进一步增强其经济和环境效益。此外,反应条件的优化和模块化工厂的设计可以提高PDH技术的灵活性和可扩展性,使其更适应不同的市场需求和原料来源。
