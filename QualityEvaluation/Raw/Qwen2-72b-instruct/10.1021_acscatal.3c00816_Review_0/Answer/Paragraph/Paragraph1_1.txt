            Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The emergence of propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) as a promising technology for propylene production has been driven by the growing global demand for propylene and the limitations of conventional production methods. PDH technology, by leveraging the abundant and cost-effective availability of light alkanes, particularly propane, offers a single-feedstock efficiency that surpasses conventional methods. This is achieved through the use of advanced catalysts, such as Pt- and CrOx, which significantly enhance propylene yields at high temperatures, thereby improving process efficiency and selectivity.

            The use of cleaner gas sources in PDH has grown in propylene production, addressing environmental concerns. Eco-friendly ZrO2-based catalysts, enhanced with Ru/Rh nanoparticles, have replaced costly and health-risk CrOx/Pt catalysts, improving non-oxidative dehydrogenation. PDH boosts propylene production, enhancing feedstock use, efficiency, and selectivity, outshining conventional methods. The use of shale gas in PDH boosts propylene output with high efficiency and selectivity, modular design, and lower costs, outperforming conventional methods via advanced catalysts.

            Future research endeavors might focus on the development of new catalysts that can further enhance the efficiency and selectivity of PDH processes. This could include the exploration of catalysts that can operate at lower temperatures to reduce energy consumption and minimize the formation of unwanted byproducts. Additionally, the development of catalysts that can effectively activate CO2 could lead to more sustainable and environmentally friendly propylene production methods. The integration of PDH technology with renewable energy sources could also be a promising avenue for future research, potentially leading to a more sustainable and carbon-neutral propylene production industry.

            综合理解与未来展望：丙烷直接脱氢（PDH）技术作为丙烯生产有前途的方法的出现，是由全球丙烯需求的增长和传统生产方法的局限性推动的。PDH技术通过利用轻烷烃，特别是丙烷的丰富和成本效益，提供了超越传统方法的单一原料效率。这是通过使用先进的催化剂，如Pt-和CrOx，实现的，这些催化剂在高温下显著提高了丙烯产量，从而提高了过程效率和选择性。
