<English> 
                    The emergence of propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) as a promising and advantageous technology to address the challenges of meeting the growing global demand for propylene production, in the context of limitations faced by conventional methods, is substantiated by a plethora of studies. PDH, using propane, meets rising propylene demand with high efficiency, selectivity, and lower costs, leveraging abundant shale gas \[10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081\]. This technology boosts propylene yields but faces high-temperature challenges, limiting C3H6 selectivity, which are addressed by the use of Pt- and CrOx catalysts \[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012\]. PDH boosts propylene production, leveraging abundant propane with high selectivity, economically, avoiding cracking and coke issues \[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5\]. It economically converts propane, non-petroleum-dependent, addressing challenges of high temperatures, side reactions, affecting selectivity, and stability, with efficient catalysts being key \[10.3390/catal9020174\]. PDH, using cleaner gas, boosts propylene output, tackling feedstock limits, seen in new plants. CrOx, Pt catalysts have issues; new ZrO2-Ru/Rh NP catalysts improve efficiency, selectivity \[10.1038/s41467-018-06174-5\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in PDH technology, as illuminated by the studies, offer a promising pathway to meet the escalating global demand for propylene. The use of abundant shale gas as a feedstock, coupled with the development of efficient and selective catalysts, such as ZrO2-Ru/Rh NP, presents a sustainable and cost-effective solution. Future research endeavors might focus on the optimization of catalysts to further enhance selectivity and stability under high-temperature conditions, the exploration of alternative feedstocks, and the integration of PDH processes with renewable energy sources to reduce the carbon footprint. Additionally, the development of novel catalysts that can operate at lower temperatures could significantly improve the energy efficiency of PDH processes, making them even more competitive in the global propylene market.

                    丙烷直接脱氢（PDH）技术的兴起，作为一项有前景且具优势的技术，旨在解决全球丙烯需求增长的挑战，同时克服传统方法的局限性，这一点得到了大量研究的证实。PDH使用丙烷，通过高效率、选择性和较低成本，利用丰富的页岩气，满足日益增长的丙烯需求\[10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081\]。该技术虽然能提高丙烯产量，但面临高温挑战，限制了C3H6的选择性，这一问题通过使用Pt-和CrOx催化剂得到解决\[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012\]。PDH通过高选择性、经济地利用丰富的丙烷，避免了裂解和焦炭问题，从而提高了丙烯产量\[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5\]。它经济地将丙烷转化为丙烯，不依赖石油，解决了高温、副反应影响选择性和稳定性的问题，高效催化剂是关键\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。PDH使用更清洁的气体，提高了丙烯产量，解决了原料限制问题，在新建工厂中可见。CrOx和Pt催化剂存在问题；新的ZrO2-Ru/Rh NP催化剂提高了效率和选择性\[10.1038/s41467-018-06174-5\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 研究所揭示的PDH技术的进步，为满足全球丙烯需求的激增提供了一条有前景的路径。使用丰富的页岩气作为原料，结合高效、选择性催化剂（如ZrO2-Ru/Rh NP）的开发，提供了一个可持续且成本效益高的解决方案。未来的研究可能集中在催化剂的优化，以进一步提高在高温条件下的选择性和稳定性，探索替代原料，以及将PDH过程与可再生能源源集成，以减少碳足迹。此外，开发能在较低温度下运行的新型催化剂，可以显著提高PDH过程的能源效率，使其在全球丙烯市场中更具竞争力。
