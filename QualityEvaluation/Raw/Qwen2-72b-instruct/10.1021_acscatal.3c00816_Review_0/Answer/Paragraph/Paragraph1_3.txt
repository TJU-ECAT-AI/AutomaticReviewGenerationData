            <English> 
                    Propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) emerges as a promising technology to address the growing global demand for propylene production, leveraging the abundance and cost-effectiveness of shale gas \[10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081\]. PDH offers several advantages over conventional production methods, including a single feed/product stream, wide availability of raw materials, and lower costs \[10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081\]. The use of Pt/CrOx catalysts in PDH boosts propylene yield, tackling the challenges of high temperatures and enhancing selectivity for global demand \[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012\]. PDH converts propane directly to propylene with high selectivity, enhancing efficiency and eliminating intermediate steps, thereby addressing the limitations of conventional methods \[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5\]. This direct, economical method of propylene production is particularly advantageous in the face of petroleum depletion, as it ensures a steady supply of propylene \[10.3390/catal9020174\]. However, PDH faces challenges in selectivity and catalyst stability at high temperatures, necessitating the development of efficient catalysts for industrial use \[10.3390/catal9020174\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in PDH technology, particularly the use of efficient and stable catalysts, present an opportunity to explore innovative methodologies for propylene production. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of eco-friendly catalysts, such as ZrO2-based and Ru/Rh NP-promoted catalysts \[10.1038/s41467-018-06174-5\], which offer eco-friendly, cost-efficient dehydrogenation, overcoming the limitations of conventional CrOx/Pt-based catalysts. The exploration of active site isolation techniques to enhance alkene selectivity and catalyst stability \[10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008\] could also be a promising direction. Additionally, the optimization of reaction conditions, such as temperature and pressure, to enhance conversion and reduce costs \[10.1039/D0CS00983K\] is crucial for the commercial success of PDH technology. The development of catalysts that can activate CO2 at lower temperatures \[10.1039/D1GC03700E\] could also open new avenues for propylene production, addressing environmental concerns and enhancing the sustainability of the process.

                    丙烷直接脱氢（PDH）技术作为满足全球丙烯需求增长的有前途的技术，利用了页岩气的丰富性和成本效益\[10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081\]。PDH与传统生产方法相比，具有单一的进料/产品流，广泛的原材料可用性，以及更低的成本\[10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081\]。在PDH中使用Pt/CrOx催化剂可以提高丙烯产量，应对高温挑战，增强全球需求的丙烯选择性\[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012\]。PDH直接将丙烷转化为丙烯，具有高选择性，提高了效率，消除了中间步骤，从而解决了传统方法的局限性\[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5\]。这种直接、经济的丙烯生产方法在面对石油枯竭时尤其有利，因为它确保了丙烯的稳定供应\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。然而，PDH在高温下的选择性和催化剂稳定性方面面临挑战，需要开发高效的催化剂以供工业使用\[10.3390/catal9020174\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: PDH技术的进展，特别是高效稳定催化剂的使用，为探索丙烯生产的创新方法提供了机会。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发环保催化剂，如ZrO2基和Ru/Rh NP促进的催化剂\[10.1038/s41467-018-06174-5\]，这些催化剂提供环保、成本高效的脱氢，克服了传统CrOx/Pt基催化剂的局限性。探索活性位点隔离技术以增强烯烃选择性和催化剂稳定性\[10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008\]也可能是一个有希望的方向。此外，优化反应条件，如温度和压力，以提高转化率和降低成本\[10.1039/D0CS00983K\]对于PDH技术的商业成功至关重要。开发能够在较低温度下活化CO2的催化剂\[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]也可能为丙烯生产开辟新的途径，解决环境问题，提高过程的可持续性。
