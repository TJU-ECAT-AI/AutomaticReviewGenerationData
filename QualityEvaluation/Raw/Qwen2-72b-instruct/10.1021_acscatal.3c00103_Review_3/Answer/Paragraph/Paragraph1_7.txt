                    The hydroxyl anchoring method, a precise molecular approach, facilitates the controlled incorporation of active metal compounds in crystal materials by providing a subtle ambiance for grafting metal sites or anchoring metal compounds on hydroxyl groups \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_3\]. This method allows for the controllable incorporation of active compounds on the support, either by grafting or confining them on isolated hydroxyls or hydroxyl nests. The surface organometallic chemistry (SOMC) method, which utilizes the organic groups on the support, can graft metal active sites on the support by forming strong covalent MO bonds. By selectively reacting hydroxyl groups on tailored supports with specific thermolytic molecular precursors (TMPs) under high temperatures, highly dispersed metal oxide species can be generated. The SOMC approach can achieve the precise adjustment of metal-supported catalysts at an atomic level, leading to the construction of strong metal-support interactions and the improvement of activity, selectivity, and stability of heterogeneous catalysts in solid-gas reactions.

                    The atomic layer deposition (ALD) technique, another method discussed in the literature, allows for the controlled incorporation of active metal compounds in crystal materials by depositing ZnO onto the internal and external surfaces of ZSM-5 and Y zeolites \[10.1039/C5CP05043J\]. This method facilitates the precise control over the dispersion and form of active species, enhancing metal-support interactions. The implications for enhancing the activity, selectivity, and stability of heterogeneous catalysts in solid-gas reactions are demonstrated by the improved catalytic performance of the ALD ZnO modified zeolites in propane conversion, with higher activities and selectivities to desired products compared to the unmodified zeolites.

                    The SOMC approach, which exploits the formation of covalent bonds between molecular species and supports through grafting, allows for the generation of uniform supported, isolated species and supported metallic nanoparticles with a narrow particle size distribution, enhancing metal-support interactions \[10.1039/D0CS01424A\]. The implications for heterogeneous catalysts include improved activity, selectivity, and stability in solid-gas reactions due to the precise molecular control and explicit study of the interplay between structure and activity.

                    The hydroxyl anchoring method, alongside the SOMC and ALD techniques, provides a controllable approach to fabricating well-tuned catalytic materials with improved performance in propane dehydrogenation reactions.

                    氢氧根锚定方法，一种精确的分子方法，通过在氢氧基团上提供微妙的环境来促进活性金属化合物在晶体材料中的可控掺入\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_3\]。该方法允许在支撑上可控地掺入活性化合物，无论是通过在孤立的氢氧基或氢氧巢上嫁接还是限制它们。表面有机金属化学（SOMC）方法，利用支撑上的有机基团，可以通过形成强共价MO键在支撑上嫁接金属活性位点。通过在高温下选择性地使定制支撑上的氢氧基团与特定的热解分子前体（TMPs）反应，可以生成高度分散的金属氧化物物种。SOMC方法可以在原子水平上精确调整金属支撑的催化剂，导致强金属-支撑相互作用的构建和固体-气体反应中异相催化剂的活性、选择性和稳定性的提高。

                    文献中讨论的另一种方法，原子层沉积（ALD）技术，允许通过在ZSM-5和Y沸石的内部和外部表面沉积ZnO来控制性地掺入活性金属化合物\[10.1039/C5CP05043J\]。该方法通过精确控制活性物种的分散和形式，增强金属-支撑相互作用。增强固体-气体反应中异相催化剂的活性、选择性和稳定性的含义体现在ALD改性的ZnO沸石在丙烷转化中的改进催化性能，与未改性的沸石相比，具有更高的活性和选择性。

                    SOMC方法，通过嫁接利用分子物种和支撑之间的共价键的形成，允许生成均匀的支撑、孤立的物种和具有窄粒径分布的支撑金属纳米粒子，增强金属-支撑相互作用\[10.1039/D0CS01424A\]。异相催化剂的含义包括由于精确的分子控制和结构与活性之间相互作用的明确研究，固体-气体反应中活性、选择性和稳定性的提高。

                    氢氧根锚定方法，连同SOMC和ALD技术，为丙烷脱氢反应中性能改进的催化材料的制备提供了一种可控的方法。
