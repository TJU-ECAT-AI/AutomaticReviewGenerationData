                    The hydroxyl anchoring method, as elucidated in reference \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_3\], facilitates the controlled incorporation of active metal compounds in crystal materials by providing a subtle ambiance for grafting metal sites or anchoring metal compounds on hydroxyl groups. This approach allows for the controllable incorporation of active compounds on the support, either by grafting or confining them on isolated hydroxyls or hydroxyl nests. The surface organometallic chemistry (SOMC) method, which utilizes the organic groups on the support, can graft metal active sites on the support by forming strong covalent MO bonds. By selectively reacting hydroxyl groups on tailored supports with specific thermolytic molecular precursors (TMPs) under high temperatures, highly dispersed metal oxide species can be generated. The SOMC approach can achieve the precise adjustment of metal-supported catalysts at an atomic level, leading to the construction of strong metal-support interactions and the improvement of activity, selectivity, and stability of heterogeneous catalysts in solid-gas reactions. The implications for enhancing metal-support interactions and improving the activity, selectivity, and stability of heterogeneous catalysts in solid-gas reactions are significant, as demonstrated by the improved catalytic performance of the ALD ZnO modified zeolites in propane conversion, with higher activities and selectivities to desired products compared to the unmodified zeolites \[10.1039/C5CP05043J\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The hydroxyl anchoring method, as a controllable approach to fabricating well-tuned catalytic materials, opens up new avenues for the development of advanced heterogeneous catalysts. By enabling the precise control over the dispersion and form of active species, this method can lead to optimized catalytic properties, particularly in propane dehydrogenation reactions. Future research endeavors might focus on exploring the synergistic effects of different metal-support interactions and the role of hydroxyl groups in enhancing the catalytic performance of materials. Additionally, the integration of the hydroxyl anchoring method with other advanced synthesis techniques, such as atomic layer deposition (ALD), could further enhance the control over the size and distribution of metal clusters, leading to improved catalytic performance. The exploration of novel crystal materials with abundant hydroxyl groups for anchoring metal compounds could also be a promising direction, potentially leading to the discovery of new catalysts with enhanced activity, selectivity, and stability.

                    氢氧根锚定方法，如参考文献\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_3\]所述，通过在氢氧根上提供微妙的环境来锚定金属位点或固定金属化合物，从而实现活性金属化合物在晶体材料中的可控掺入。该方法允许通过在孤立的氢氧根或氢氧根巢中嫁接或限制活性化合物来在载体上可控地掺入活性化合物。表面有机金属化学（SOMC）方法，利用载体上的有机基团，可以在载体上通过形成强共价MO键来嫁接金属活性位点。通过在高温下选择性地使定制载体上的氢氧根与特定的热解分子前体（TMPs）反应，可以生成高度分散的金属氧化物物种。SOMC方法可以在原子水平上精确调整金属支撑的催化剂，从而构建强大的金属-支撑相互作用，提高固体-气体反应中异质催化剂的活性、选择性和稳定性。增强金属-支撑相互作用和提高固体-气体反应中异质催化剂的活性、选择性和稳定性的意义重大，如通过改进的催化性能所证明的那样，ALD ZnO改性沸石在丙烷转化中的活性和选择性高于未改性的沸石\[10.1039/C5CP05043J\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 作为制备调谐良好的催化材料的可控方法，氢氧根锚定方法为开发先进的异质催化剂开辟了新的途径。通过使活性物种的分散和形式的精确控制，该方法可以导致优化的催化性能，特别是在丙烷脱氢反应中。未来的研究工作可能集中在探索不同金属-支撑相互作用的协同效应以及氢氧根在增强材料催化性能中的作用。此外，将氢氧根锚定方法与原子层沉积（ALD）等其他先进合成技术相结合，可以进一步增强对金属簇的大小和分布的控制，从而提高催化性能。探索具有丰富氢氧根的新型晶体材料以固定金属化合物也可能是一个有希望的方向，可能发现具有增强活性、选择性和稳定性的新型催化剂。
