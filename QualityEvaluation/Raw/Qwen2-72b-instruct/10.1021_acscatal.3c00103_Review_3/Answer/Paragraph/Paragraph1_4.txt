                    The hydroxyl anchoring method, as elucidated in reference \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_3\], facilitates the controlled incorporation of active metal compounds in crystal materials by providing a subtle ambiance for grafting metal sites or anchoring metal compounds on hydroxyl groups. This approach allows for the controllable incorporation of active compounds on the support, either by grafting or confining them on isolated hydroxyls or hydroxyl nests. The surface organometallic chemistry (SOMC) method, which utilizes the organic groups on the support, can graft metal active sites on the support by forming strong covalent MO bonds. By selectively reacting hydroxyl groups on tailored supports with specific thermolytic molecular precursors (TMPs) under high temperatures, highly dispersed metal oxide species can be generated. The SOMC approach can achieve the precise adjustment of metal-supported catalysts at an atomic level, leading to the construction of strong metal-support interactions and the improvement of activity, selectivity, and stability of heterogeneous catalysts in solid-gas reactions. The implications for enhancing metal-support interactions and improving the activity, selectivity, and stability of heterogeneous catalysts in solid-gas reactions are significant, as demonstrated by the improved catalytic performance of ALD ZnO modified zeolites in propane conversion, with higher activities and selectivities to desired products compared to the unmodified zeolites \[10.1039/C5CP05043J\].

This could lead to the development of novel catalysts with improved activity, selectivity, and stability in solid-gas reactions. Additionally, the exploration of new crystal materials with abundant hydroxyl groups could provide a broader range of supports for anchoring metal compounds, potentially expanding the scope of catalytic applications. The hydroxyl anchoring method, combined with advanced characterization techniques, could also shed light on the fundamental mechanisms of metal-support interactions, paving the way for the rational design of catalysts tailored for specific reactions.

                    羟基锚定方法，如参考文献\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_3\]所述，通过在羟基上提供微妙的环境来锚定金属位点或固定金属化合物，从而实现活性金属化合物在晶体材料中的可控掺入。该方法允许通过在孤立的羟基或羟基巢上进行嫁接或限制来在支撑上可控地掺入活性化合物。表面有机金属化学（SOMC）方法利用支撑上的有机基团，通过形成强共价MO键将金属活性位点嫁接到支撑上。通过在高温下选择性地使定制支撑上的羟基与特定的热解分子前体（TMPs）反应，可以生成高度分散的金属氧化物物种。SOMC方法可以在原子水平上精确调整金属支撑催化剂，从而构建强金属-支撑相互作用，提高固体-气体反应中异质催化剂的活性、选择性和稳定性。增强金属-支撑相互作用和提高固体-气体反应中异质催化剂的活性、选择性和稳定性的影响是显著的，如ALD ZnO改性沸石在丙烷转化中表现出的催化性能的提高，与未改性沸石相比，活性和选择性更高\[10.1039/C5CP05043J\]。
