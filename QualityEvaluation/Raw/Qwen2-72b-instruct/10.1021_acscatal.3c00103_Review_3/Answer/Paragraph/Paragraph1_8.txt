                    The hydroxyl anchoring method facilitates the controlled incorporation of active metal compounds in crystal materials, providing a subtle ambiance for grafting metal sites or anchoring metal compounds on hydroxyl groups \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_3\]. This approach allows for the controllable incorporation of active compounds on the support, either by grafting or confining them on isolated hydroxyls or hydroxyl nests. The surface organometallic chemistry (SOMC) method, which utilizes the organic groups on the support, can graft metal active sites on the support by forming strong covalent MO bonds. By selectively reacting hydroxyl groups on tailored supports with specific thermolytic molecular precursors (TMPs) under high temperatures, highly dispersed metal oxide species can be generated. The SOMC approach can achieve the precise adjustment of metal-supported catalysts at an atomic level, leading to the construction of strong metal-support interactions and the improvement of activity, selectivity, and stability of heterogeneous catalysts in solid-gas reactions.

                    The atomic layer deposition (ALD) technique used in the study allows for the controlled incorporation of active metal compounds in crystal materials by depositing ZnO onto the internal and external surfaces of ZSM-5 and Y zeolites \[10.1039/C5CP05043J\]. This method facilitates the precise control over the dispersion and form of active species, enhancing metal-support interactions. The implications for enhancing the activity, selectivity, and stability of heterogeneous catalysts in solid-gas reactions are demonstrated by the improved catalytic performance of the ALD ZnO modified zeolites in propane conversion, with higher activities and selectivities to desired products compared to the unmodified zeolites.

                    The SOMC approach, which exploits the formation of covalent bonds between molecular species and supports through grafting, allows for the generation of uniform supported, isolated species and supported metallic nanoparticles with a narrow particle size distribution, enhancing metal-support interactions \[10.1039/D0CS01424A\]. The implications for heterogeneous catalysts include improved activity, selectivity, and stability in solid-gas reactions due to the precise molecular control and explicit study of the interplay between structure and activity.

                    The hydroxyl anchoring method, through the use of SOMC and ALD techniques, facilitates the controlled incorporation of active metal compounds in crystal materials, leading to improved activity, selectivity, and stability of heterogeneous catalysts in solid-gas reactions, particularly in propane dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1039/C9SC05599A\].

                    氢氧根锚定方法促进了活性金属化合物在晶体材料中的可控掺入，为在氢氧基团上嫁接金属位点或锚定金属化合物提供了一种微妙的环境\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_3\]。这种方法允许在支撑上可控地掺入活性化合物，无论是通过嫁接还是在孤立的氢氧基团或氢氧巢穴上限制它们。表面有机金属化学（SOMC）方法，利用支撑上的有机基团，可以在支撑上通过形成强共价MO键嫁接金属活性位点。通过在高温下选择性地使定制支撑上的氢氧基团与特定的热解分子前体（TMPs）反应，可以生成高度分散的金属氧化物物种。SOMC方法可以在原子水平上精确调整金属支撑的催化剂，导致强金属支撑相互作用的构建和固体气体反应中异质催化剂的活性、选择性和稳定性的提高。

                    研究中使用的原子层沉积（ALD）技术允许通过在ZSM-5和Y沸石的内部和外部表面沉积ZnO来在晶体材料中可控地掺入活性金属化合物\[10.1039/C5CP05043J\]。这种方法促进了对活性物种的分散和形式的精确控制，增强了金属支撑相互作用。通过ALD ZnO改性沸石在丙烷转化中的催化性能的提高，证明了增强固体气体反应中异质催化剂的活性、选择性和稳定性的意义，与未改性的沸石相比，活性和选择性更高。

                    SOMC方法，通过利用分子物种和支撑之间的共价键的形成通过嫁接，允许生成均匀的支撑、孤立的物种和具有窄粒度分布的支撑金属纳米粒子，增强了金属支撑相互作用\[10.1039/D0CS01424A\]。对异质催化剂的影响包括由于精确的分子控制和结构与活性之间相互作用的明确研究，固体气体反应中的活性、选择性和稳定性得到改善。

                    通过使用SOMC和ALD技术，氢氧根锚定方法促进了活性金属化合物在晶体材料中的可控掺入，导致固体气体反应中异质催化剂的活性、选择性和稳定性提高，特别是在丙烷脱氢反应中\[10.1039/C9SC05599A\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望：通过SOMC和ALD技术展示的氢氧根锚定方法的进展为开发调谐良好的催化材料提供了一条有希望的途径。未来的研究工作可能集中在探索具有丰富氢氧基团的新型晶体材料，这些材料可以为锚定金属化合物提供微妙的环境。此外，通过ALD实现的对活性物种的分散和形式的精确控制可以进一步优化，以增强金属支撑相互作用，导致固体气体反应中异质催化剂的活性、选择性和稳定性的提高。开发新的热解分子前体（TMPs）和研究它们与定制支撑上的氢氧基团的反应性，以生成高度分散的金属氧化物物种，也是可以追求的。SOMC方法可以进一步完善，以在原子水平上精确调整金属支撑的催化剂，导致强金属支撑相互作用的构建和催化性能的提高。探索新的晶体材料和优化氢氧根锚定方法有可能导致更有效和选择性的催化剂的开发，用于丙烷脱氢和其他固体气体反应。
