1.Bimetallic catalysts have evolved significantly in heterogeneous catalysis since their commercial introduction, contributing to bridging the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts. Their unique electronic and chemical properties have been harnessed in both fundamental research and practical applications, leading to advancements in catalytic efficiency and selectivity. The development of bimetallic catalysts has allowed for the creation of materials with tailored properties that can perform under industrial conditions, making them crucial for various chemical processes.
