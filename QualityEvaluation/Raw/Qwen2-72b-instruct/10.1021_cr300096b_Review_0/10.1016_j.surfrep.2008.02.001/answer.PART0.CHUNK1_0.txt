1.Bimetallic catalysts have evolved significantly in heterogeneous catalysis since their commercial introduction, bridging the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts. Their unique electronic and chemical properties contribute to this by allowing for the rational design of catalysts with tailored reactivities. The formation of bimetallic surfaces modifies the electronic properties of the parent metals, shifting the surface d-band center with respect to the Fermi level. This shift affects the binding energy of both atomic and molecular adsorbates on bimetallic surfaces, providing the possibility to control the activity of these surfaces for various reactions. By understanding the correlation between the d-band center and reactivity, bimetallic catalysts can be designed to optimize performance for specific reactions, bridging the gap between fundamental research and practical applications.
