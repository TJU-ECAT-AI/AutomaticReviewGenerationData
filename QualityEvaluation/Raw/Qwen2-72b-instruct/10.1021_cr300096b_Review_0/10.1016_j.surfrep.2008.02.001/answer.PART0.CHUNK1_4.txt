1.Bimetallic catalysts have evolved significantly in heterogeneous catalysis since their commercial introduction, bridging the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts. Their unique electronic and chemical properties contribute to this bridging by allowing for the rational design of catalysts with enhanced activity, selectivity, and stability under reaction conditions. The formation of bimetallic surfaces modifies the electronic properties of the parent metals, shifting the surface d-band center with respect to the Fermi level. This shift affects the binding energy of both atomic and molecular adsorbates on bimetallic surfaces, providing the possibility to control the activity of these surfaces for various reactions. By understanding the correlation between the d-band center and the reactivity of bimetallic surfaces, researchers can predict and design bimetallic catalysts that are potentially better than the parent metals for specific reactions, thus advancing both fundamental research and practical applications in heterogeneous catalysis.
