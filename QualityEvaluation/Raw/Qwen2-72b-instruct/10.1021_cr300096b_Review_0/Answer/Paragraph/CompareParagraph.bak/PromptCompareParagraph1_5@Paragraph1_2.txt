This understanding has been applied to the design of catalysts for hydrogenation, reforming, and other reactions, offering a systematic approach to identify potential bimetallic combinations and surface structures that are potentially better than the parent metals \[10.1021/ar50109a003\]. The use of bimetallic catalysts in both fundamental research and practical applications has led to significant advancements in the field of heterogeneous catalysis, enabling the development of more efficient and sustainable chemical processes \[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001\].
