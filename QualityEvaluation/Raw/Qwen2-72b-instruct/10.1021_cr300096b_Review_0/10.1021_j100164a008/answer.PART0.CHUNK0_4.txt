1.Bimetallic catalysts have evolved significantly in heterogeneous catalysis since their commercial introduction, contributing to bridging the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts. Their unique electronic and chemical properties have been extensively studied, leading to advancements in both fundamental research and practical applications. The literature highlights the use of bimetallic catalysts in various reactions, including hydrogenation, oxidation, and hydrocarbon conversion, demonstrating their versatility and effectiveness. The development of bimetallic catalysts has also led to the exploration of new synthesis methods and characterization techniques, enabling a deeper understanding of their structure-activity relationships. Overall, bimetallic catalysts have played a crucial role in advancing the field of heterogeneous catalysis and continue to be a subject of intense research.
