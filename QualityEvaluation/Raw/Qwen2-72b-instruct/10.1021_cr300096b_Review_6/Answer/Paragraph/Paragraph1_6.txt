                The experimental verification and quantification of oxygen-induced surface segregation of subsurface 3d atoms (Ni and Co) in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems have been extensively studied using HREELS and AES techniques \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The AES ratio, normalized based on the formation of the clean Pt-Ni-Pt(111) surface, showed an increase with oxygen exposure, indicating subsurface Ni atoms' segregation to the surface due to the strong interaction with atomic oxygen adsorbate \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. This finding aligns with DFT predictions, confirming the thermodynamic preference of the surface Ni-Pt-Pt(111) configuration with adsorbed oxygen \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The measured segregation kinetics and activation barriers for Ni and Co were compared with DFT predictions, revealing that the activation barrier for subsurface Ni segregation in oxygen was 15 kcal/mol, compared to 7 kcal/mol for Co \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The activation barriers for diffusion are expected to decrease for other 3d/Pt(111) bimetallic surfaces based on the shift in the d-band center \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in experimental techniques such as HREELS and AES have provided a deeper understanding of the oxygen-induced surface segregation of subsurface 3d atoms in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems. These findings not only validate DFT predictions but also offer insights into the stability and ORR activity of Pt3d bimetallic cathode electrocatalysts. Future research might focus on exploring the impact of surface segregation on the catalytic activity and stability of propane dehydrogenation catalysts, potentially leading to the development of more efficient and stable catalysts. Additionally, the investigation of other 3d/Pt(111) bimetallic surfaces could provide a broader perspective on the role of subsurface atoms in catalytic processes.

                实验验证和量化氧气诱导的表面分层现象，特别是3d原子（Ni和Co）在Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中的分层现象，已经通过HREELS和AES技术进行了广泛研究\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。基于清洁Pt-Ni-Pt(111)表面形成的AES比率，随着氧气暴露的增加，表明了由于与吸附氧原子的强烈相互作用，次表面Ni原子向表面的分层\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。这一发现与DFT预测一致，确认了吸附氧气时表面Ni-Pt-Pt(111)配置的热力学偏好\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。Ni和Co的测量分层动力学和活化势垒与DFT预测进行了比较，揭示了氧气中次表面Ni分层的活化势垒为15 kcal/mol，而Co为7 kcal/mol\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。根据d带中心的移动，其他3d/Pt(111)双金属表面的扩散活化势垒预计会降低\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: HREELS和AES等实验技术的进步为氧气诱导的表面分层现象，特别是在Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中3d原子的分层现象，提供了更深入的理解。这些发现不仅验证了DFT预测，还为Pt3d双金属阴极电催化剂的稳定性和ORR活性提供了见解。未来的研究可能会关注表面分层对丙烷脱氢催化剂的催化活性和稳定性的影响，这可能导向更高效和稳定的催化剂的开发。此外，对其他3d/Pt(111)双金属表面的探究可能为次表面原子在催化过程中的作用提供更广阔的视角。
