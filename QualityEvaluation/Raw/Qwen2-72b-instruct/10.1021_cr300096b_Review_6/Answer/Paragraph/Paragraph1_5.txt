        <English> 
                The experimental verification and quantification of oxygen-induced surface segregation of subsurface 3d atoms (Ni and Co) in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems have been extensively studied using HREELS and AES \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The AES ratio, normalized based on ratios measured during the formation of the clean Pt-Ni-Pt(111) surface, showed an increase with oxygen exposure to the Pt-Ni-Pt(111) surface, indicating that subsurface Ni atoms started to segregate to the surface due to the strong interaction of the atomic oxygen adsorbate with surface Ni atoms \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. This confirms the DFT prediction that the surface Ni-Pt-Pt(111) configuration is thermodynamically preferred with adsorbed oxygen \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The measured segregation kinetics and activation barriers for Ni and Co were compared with DFT predictions, showing that the activation barrier for the segregation of subsurface Ni to the surface in oxygen was 15 kcal/mol, compared to 7 kcal/mol for Co \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The activation barriers for diffusion are expected to further decrease for other 3d/Pt(111) bimetallic surfaces based on the shift in the d-band center \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in experimental techniques such as HREELS and AES have provided a deeper understanding of the oxygen-induced surface segregation of subsurface 3d atoms in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems. The alignment of experimental results with DFT predictions highlights the predictive power of computational methods in catalysis. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of new bimetallic systems with tailored d-band centers to optimize the activation barriers for diffusion and segregation, potentially leading to enhanced catalytic performance in propane dehydrogenation and other reactions. The exploration of novel experimental methods that can provide real-time monitoring of surface segregation under reaction conditions could also provide valuable insights into the dynamic behavior of bimetallic catalysts.

        <Chinese> 
                通过使用HREELS和AES，对Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中氧诱导的次表面3d原子（Ni和Co）的表面分层进行了实验验证和量化 \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。基于在清洁Pt-Ni-Pt(111)表面形成过程中测量的比率进行归一化的AES比率表明，随着Pt-Ni-Pt(111)表面暴露于氧气，次表面Ni原子开始向表面分层，这是由于表面Ni原子与原子氧吸附物之间的强相互作用 \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。这证实了DFT预测，即吸附氧气时，表面Ni-Pt-Pt(111)配置在热力学上是优选的 \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。测量的Ni和Co的分层动力学和活化势垒与DFT预测进行了比较，显示在氧气中次表面Ni向表面分层的活化势垒为15 kcal/mol，而Co为7 kcal/mol \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。根据d带中心的移动，其他3d/Pt(111)双金属表面的扩散活化势垒预计将进一步降低 \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: HREELS和AES等实验技术的进步为Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中氧诱导的次表面3d原子表面分层提供了更深入的理解。实验结果与DFT预测的一致性突出了计算方法在催化领域的预测能力。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发具有定制d带中心的新双金属系统，以优化扩散和分层的活化势垒，这可能促进丙烷脱氢和其他反应的催化性能。探索能够在反应条件下实时监测表面分层的新型实验方法也可能为双金属催化剂的动态行为提供有价值的见解。
