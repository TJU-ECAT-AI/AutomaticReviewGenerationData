                The utilization of high-resolution electron energy loss spectroscopy (HREELS) and Auger electron spectroscopy (AES) has provided a robust framework for the experimental verification and quantification of oxygen-induced surface segregation of subsurface 3d atoms, specifically Ni and Co, in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The AES ratio, normalized based on the formation of the clean Pt-Ni-Pt(111) surface, revealed an increase with oxygen exposure, indicating the segregation of subsurface Ni atoms to the surface due to the strong interaction with atomic oxygen adsorbate \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. This finding corroborates the density functional theory (DFT) prediction that the surface Ni-Pt-Pt(111) configuration is thermodynamically preferred in the presence of adsorbed oxygen \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The measured segregation kinetics and activation barriers for Ni and Co were compared with DFT predictions, revealing that the activation barrier for subsurface Ni segregation to the surface in oxygen was 15 kcal/mol, whereas for Co it was 7 kcal/mol \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The activation barriers for diffusion are anticipated to decrease further for other 3d/Pt(111) bimetallic surfaces based on the shift in the d-band center \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The alignment of experimental findings with DFT predictions for oxygen-induced surface segregation in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems opens avenues for the exploration of novel catalysts with tailored surface properties. Future research might focus on the development of bimetallic systems with optimized d-band centers to enhance catalytic activity and stability. The understanding of segregation kinetics and activation barriers can guide the design of catalysts for propane dehydrogenation, aiming to mitigate the deleterious effects of oxygen on surface structure and reactivity. Moreover, the integration of advanced spectroscopic techniques with computational methods could provide deeper insights into the dynamic surface processes under reaction conditions, paving the way for the rational design of next-generation catalysts.

                高分辨率电子能量损失光谱（HREELS）和俄歇电子能谱（AES）的使用为实验验证和量化Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中，特别是在Ni和Co的次表面3d原子的氧气诱导表面分层提供了坚实的基础\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。基于清洁Pt-Ni-Pt(111)表面形成的AES比率，随着氧气暴露的增加，揭示了次表面Ni原子向表面的分层，这是由于与吸附氧原子的强烈相互作用\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。这一发现证实了密度泛函理论（DFT）的预测，即在吸附氧气存在下，表面Ni-Pt-Pt(111)配置在热力学上是优选的\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。Ni和Co的测量分层动力学和活化能与DFT预测进行了比较，显示氧气中次表面Ni向表面的分层的活化能为15 kcal/mol，而Co为7 kcal/mol\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。根据d带中心的移动，其他3d/Pt(111)双金属表面的扩散活化能预计会进一步降低\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中实验发现与DFT预测的氧气诱导表面分层的一致性，为探索具有定制表面特性的新型催化剂开辟了道路。未来的研究可能会集中在开发具有优化d带中心的双金属系统，以增强催化活性和稳定性。对分层动力学和活化能的理解可以指导丙烷脱氢催化剂的设计，旨在减轻氧气对表面结构和反应性不利影响。此外，高级光谱技术与计算方法的结合可以为反应条件下动态表面过程提供更深入的见解，为下一代催化剂的理性设计铺平道路。
