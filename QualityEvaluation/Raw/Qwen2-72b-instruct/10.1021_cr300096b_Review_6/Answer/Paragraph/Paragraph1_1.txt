        <English> 
                The experimental verification and quantification of oxygen-induced surface segregation of subsurface 3d atoms (Ni and Co) in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems have been extensively studied using HREELS and AES techniques \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The AES ratio, normalized based on the formation of the clean Pt-Ni-Pt(111) surface, revealed an increase with oxygen exposure, indicating subsurface Ni atoms' segregation to the surface due to the strong interaction with atomic oxygen adsorbate \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. This finding aligns with DFT predictions, confirming the thermodynamic preference of the surface Ni-Pt-Pt(111) configuration with adsorbed oxygen \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The measured segregation kinetics and activation barriers for Ni and Co were compared with DFT predictions, showing that the activation barrier for subsurface Ni to the surface in oxygen was 15 kcal/mol, compared to 7 kcal/mol for Co \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The activation barriers for diffusion are expected to further decrease for other 3d/Pt(111) bimetallic surfaces based on the shift in the d-band center \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in experimental techniques such as HREELS and AES have provided a deeper understanding of the oxygen-induced surface segregation of subsurface 3d atoms in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems. These findings not only confirm the DFT predictions but also open up new avenues for the design of more efficient propane dehydrogenation catalysts. Future research might focus on the development of novel bimetallic systems that can mitigate the negative effects of oxygen-induced segregation, potentially leading to enhanced stability and catalytic activity. Moreover, the exploration of the d-band center's role in diffusion barriers could guide the synthesis of catalysts with optimized electronic structures, tailored for specific reaction conditions. The integration of experimental data with theoretical predictions, as demonstrated in these studies, will be crucial for the rational design of next-generation catalysts.

                实验验证和量化氧气诱导的表面分层现象，特别是3d原子（Ni和Co）在Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中的分层现象，已经通过HREELS和AES技术进行了广泛研究\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。基于清洁Pt-Ni-Pt(111)表面形成的AES比率，随着氧气暴露的增加，揭示了由于与吸附氧原子的强相互作用，次表面Ni原子向表面的分层\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。这一发现与DFT预测一致，确认了吸附氧气时表面Ni-Pt-Pt(111)配置的热力学偏好\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。测量的Ni和Co的分层动力学和活化能与DFT预测进行了比较，显示氧气中次表面Ni到表面的活化能为15 kcal/mol，而Co为7 kcal/mol\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。根据d带中心的移动，其他3d/Pt(111)双金属表面的扩散活化能预计将进一步降低\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: HREELS和AES等实验技术的进步为Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中氧气诱导的次表面3d原子表面分层现象提供了更深入的理解。这些发现不仅证实了DFT预测，还为设计更高效的丙烷脱氢催化剂开辟了新的途径。未来的研究可能会集中在开发能够缓解氧气诱导分层负面影响的新型双金属系统，这可能带来增强的稳定性和催化活性。此外，探索d带中心在扩散障碍中的作用可以指导催化剂的合成，优化电子结构，以适应特定的反应条件。正如这些研究中所展示的，实验数据与理论预测的结合对于下一代催化剂的理性设计至关重要。
