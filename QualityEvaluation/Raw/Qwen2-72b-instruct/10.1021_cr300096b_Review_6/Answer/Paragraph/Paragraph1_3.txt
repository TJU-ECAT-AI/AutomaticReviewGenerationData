        <English> 
                The experimental verification and quantification of oxygen-induced surface segregation of subsurface 3d atoms (Ni and Co) in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems have been extensively studied using HREELS and AES techniques \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The AES ratio, normalized based on the formation of the clean Pt-Ni-Pt(111) surface, showed an increase with oxygen exposure, indicating subsurface Ni atoms segregating to the surface due to strong atomic oxygen adsorbate interaction \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. This confirms the DFT prediction that the surface Ni-Pt-Pt(111) configuration is thermodynamically preferred with adsorbed oxygen \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The activation barrier for the segregation of subsurface Ni to the surface in oxygen was determined to be 15 kcal/mol, compared to 7 kcal/mol for Co \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The activation barriers for diffusion are expected to further decrease for other 3d/Pt(111) bimetallic surfaces based on the shift in the d-band center \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in experimental techniques such as HREELS and AES have provided a deeper understanding of the oxygen-induced surface segregation of subsurface 3d atoms in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems. These findings not only confirm the DFT predictions but also open up new avenues for the design of more efficient propane dehydrogenation catalysts. Future research might focus on the development of novel bimetallic systems that can mitigate the negative effects of oxygen-induced segregation, potentially leading to catalysts with enhanced stability and activity. Additionally, the exploration of the role of the d-band center in the activation barriers for diffusion could provide insights into the optimization of bimetallic surfaces for improved catalytic performance.

                实验验证和量化氧气诱导的表面分层现象，特别是3d原子（Ni和Co）在Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中的分层，已经通过HREELS和AES技术进行了广泛的研究\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。基于清洁Pt-Ni-Pt(111)表面形成的AES比率，随着氧气暴露的增加，表明由于强原子氧吸附物的相互作用，次表面Ni原子开始向表面分层\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。这证实了DFT预测，即吸附氧气时，表面Ni-Pt-Pt(111)配置在热力学上是优选的\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。氧气中次表面Ni向表面分层的活化能被确定为15 kcal/mol，而Co为7 kcal/mol\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。根据d带中心的偏移，其他3d/Pt(111)双金属表面的扩散活化能预计将进一步降低\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: HREELS和AES等实验技术的进步为氧气诱导的表面分层现象，特别是在Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中次表面3d原子的分层，提供了更深入的理解。这些发现不仅证实了DFT预测，还为设计更高效的丙烷脱氢催化剂开辟了新的途径。未来的研究可能会集中在开发能够缓解氧气诱导分层负面影响的新型双金属系统，这可能导向具有增强稳定性和活性的催化剂。此外，探索d带中心在扩散活化能中的作用可能为优化双金属表面以提高催化性能提供见解。
