The utilization of high-resolution electron energy loss spectroscopy (HREELS) and Auger electron spectroscopy (AES) has been pivotal in verifying and quantifying the oxygen-induced surface segregation of subsurface 3d atoms (Ni and Co) in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The AES ratio, normalized based on ratios measured during the formation of the clean Pt-Ni-Pt(111) surface, showed an increase with oxygen exposure to the Pt-Ni-Pt(111) surface, indicating that subsurface Ni atoms started to segregate to the surface due to the strong interaction of the atomic oxygen adsorbate with surface Ni atoms. This finding corroborates the DFT prediction that the surface Ni-Pt-Pt(111) configuration is thermodynamically preferred with adsorbed oxygen \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The measured segregation kinetics and activation barriers for Ni and Co were compared with DFT predictions, showing that the activation barrier for the segregation of subsurface Ni to the surface in oxygen was 15 kcal/mol, compared to 7 kcal/mol for Co \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The activation barriers for diffusion are expected to further decrease for other 3d/Pt(111) bimetallic surfaces based on the shift in the d-band center \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. These experimental findings align with the predictions made by DFT modeling, confirming the instability of the PtNiPt(111) and PtCoPt(111) surface structures in the presence of adsorbed oxygen \[10.1021/jp061697z\]. The measured activation barriers for the segregation of subsurface Ni and Co atoms were determined to be 15 ± 2 and 7 ± 1 kcal/mol, respectively, which are consistent with the DFT predictions suggesting a general trend in the stability and oxygen reduction reaction (ORR) activity of Pt3d bimetallic cathode electrocatalysts \[10.1021/jp061697z\].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in experimental techniques such as HREELS and AES, coupled with DFT predictions, have significantly enhanced our understanding of oxygen-induced surface segregation in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems. These methodologies have not only verified the segregation kinetics and activation barriers for Ni and Co but also provided insights into the broader implications for other 3d/Pt(111) bimetallic surfaces. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel bimetallic systems that can mitigate the negative effects of oxygen-induced segregation, potentially leading to enhanced stability and catalytic activity in propane dehydrogenation processes.
