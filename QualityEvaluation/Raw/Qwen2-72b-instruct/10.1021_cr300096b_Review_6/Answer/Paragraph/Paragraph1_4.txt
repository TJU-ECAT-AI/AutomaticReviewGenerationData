                The experimental verification and quantification of oxygen-induced surface segregation of subsurface 3d atoms (Ni and Co) in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems have been extensively studied using HREELS and AES techniques \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The AES ratio, normalized based on the formation of the clean Pt-Ni-Pt(111) surface, revealed an increase with oxygen exposure, indicating subsurface Ni atoms' segregation to the surface due to the strong interaction with atomic oxygen adsorbate \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. This finding aligns with DFT predictions, confirming the thermodynamic preference of the surface Ni-Pt-Pt(111) configuration with adsorbed oxygen \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The measured segregation kinetics and activation barriers for Ni and Co were compared with DFT predictions, revealing that the activation barrier for subsurface Ni to the surface in oxygen was 15 kcal/mol, contrasting with 7 kcal/mol for Co \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The activation barriers for diffusion are anticipated to further decrease for other 3d/Pt(111) bimetallic surfaces based on the shift in the d-band center \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in experimental techniques and DFT predictions for understanding the oxygen-induced surface segregation of subsurface 3d atoms in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems open avenues for exploring the dynamic behavior of other bimetallic surfaces. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel bimetallic catalysts with optimized d-band centers to enhance the stability and activity for oxygen reduction reactions. Additionally, the integration of machine learning algorithms with DFT calculations could expedite the discovery of new catalysts with tailored properties for various electrochemical applications.

                实验验证和量化氧气诱导的表面分层现象，特别是3d原子（Ni和Co）在Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中的分层，已经通过HREELS和AES技术进行了广泛研究\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。基于清洁Pt-Ni-Pt(111)表面形成的AES比率表明，随着氧气暴露，表面Ni原子开始分层到表面，这是由于与吸附的原子氧吸附物的强烈相互作用\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。这一发现与DFT预测一致，确认了吸附氧气时表面Ni-Pt-Pt(111)配置的热力学偏好\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。测量的Ni和Co的分层动力学和活化能与DFT预测进行了比较，显示氧气中Ni从次表面到表面的活化能为15 kcal/mol，而Co为7 kcal/mol\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。基于d带中心的移动，其他3d/Pt(111)双金属表面的扩散活化能预计将进一步降低\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: 在实验技术和DFT预测方面取得的进展，用于理解Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中氧气诱导的3d原子表面分层现象，为探索其他双金属表面的动态行为开辟了道路。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发具有优化d带中心的新型双金属催化剂，以增强氧气还原反应的稳定性和活性。此外，将机器学习算法与DFT计算相结合，可以加速具有定制属性的新催化剂的发现，用于各种电化学应用。
