                The experimental verification and quantification of oxygen-induced surface segregation of subsurface 3d atoms (Ni and Co) in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems have been extensively studied using HREELS and AES techniques \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The AES ratio, normalized based on the formation of the clean Pt-Ni-Pt(111) surface, revealed an increase with oxygen exposure, indicating subsurface Ni atoms' segregation to the surface due to the strong interaction with atomic oxygen adsorbate \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. This finding corroborates DFT predictions, confirming the thermodynamic preference of the surface Ni-Pt-Pt(111) configuration with adsorbed oxygen \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The measured segregation kinetics and activation barriers for Ni and Co were compared with DFT predictions, revealing that the activation barrier for subsurface Ni segregation in oxygen was 15 kcal/mol, whereas for Co, it was 7 kcal/mol \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]. The activation barriers for diffusion are anticipated to decrease further for other 3d/Pt(111) bimetallic surfaces based on the d-band center shift \[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The insights gained from the experimental and theoretical studies on oxygen-induced surface segregation in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems open avenues for the design of advanced propane dehydrogenation catalysts. Future research might focus on the development of novel bimetallic systems that can mitigate the negative effects of oxygen-induced segregation, potentially by engineering the d-band center to optimize the activation barriers for diffusion. Moreover, the exploration of alternative experimental techniques that can provide real-time monitoring of surface segregation under reaction conditions could offer deeper insights into the dynamic behavior of these catalysts. The integration of machine learning algorithms with DFT calculations could also accelerate the discovery of new catalysts with tailored properties for enhanced stability and activity in propane dehydrogenation.

                实验验证和量化氧气诱导的表面分层现象，特别是3d原子（Ni和Co）在Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中的分层，已通过HREELS和AES技术进行了广泛研究\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。基于清洁Pt-Ni-Pt(111)表面形成的AES比率显示，随着氧气暴露的增加，表明由于与吸附氧原子的强烈相互作用，次表面Ni原子向表面分层\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。这一发现与DFT预测相吻合，确认了吸附氧气时表面Ni-Pt-Pt(111)配置的热力学偏好\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。Ni和Co的测量分层动力学和活化能与DFT预测进行了比较，揭示了氧气中次表面Ni分层的活化能为15 kcal/mol，而Co的活化能为7 kcal/mol\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。基于d带中心的位移，其他3d/Pt(111)双金属表面的扩散活化能预计将进一步降低\[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: 从实验和理论研究中获得的关于Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中氧气诱导的表面分层的见解为设计先进的丙烷脱氢催化剂开辟了道路。未来的研究可能集中在开发新型双金属系统，以减轻氧气诱导的分层的负面影响，可能通过工程设计d带中心来优化扩散的活化能。此外，探索能够在反应条件下实时监测表面分层的替代实验技术，可能为这些催化剂的动态行为提供更深入的见解。将机器学习算法与DFT计算相结合，也可能加速发现具有定制性能的新催化剂，以增强丙烷脱氢中的稳定性和活性。
