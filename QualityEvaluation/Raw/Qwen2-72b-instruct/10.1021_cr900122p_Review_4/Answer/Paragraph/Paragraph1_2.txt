                    The deactivation of supported alkene polymerization catalysts, particularly those based on lanthanides and actinides, is intricately linked to the activation of the allylic CH bond, a process that supersedes propene insertion and culminates in the formation of inactive π-allyl surface complexes \[10.1021/cr900122p_4\]. This mechanism elucidates a critical pathway for catalyst deactivation, where the allylic CH bond activation diverts the reaction pathway from productive polymerization to the generation of inactive surface species. Similarly, silica-supported Re-based alkene metathesis catalysts undergo deactivation through the activation of the CH bond in the β-position of metallacyclobutane intermediates, involving a β-H transfer process \[10.1021/cr900122p_4\]. The allyl hydride intermediate resulting from this process can react with ethene, forming a highly reactive species that has dual potential: either to regenerate the catalyst through α-H abstraction between two adjacent alkyl ligands or to lead to degrafting through β-H abstraction by the siloxy ligand \[10.1021/cr900122p_4\]. These deactivation pathways significantly impact the performance and longevity of the catalysts, highlighting the importance of understanding and controlling intramolecular C-H bond activation in the context of supported alkene polymerization and metathesis catalysts.
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The insights into the mechanisms of intramolecular C-H bond activation in the deactivation of supported alkene polymerization and metathesis catalysts underscore the need for advanced catalyst design strategies that can mitigate these deactivation pathways. Future research endeavors might focus on developing novel catalyst systems that are resistant to intramolecular C-H bond activation, thereby enhancing catalyst longevity and performance. This could involve the engineering of catalyst supports with tailored functionalities that prevent the formation of inactive π-allyl surface complexes and the stabilization of reactive intermediates to avoid β-H transfer processes. Additionally, the exploration of alternative activation modes that bypass the allylic CH bond activation could lead to the development of more robust catalysts for alkene polymerization and metathesis reactions.

                    基于镧系和锕系元素的支撑烯烃聚合催化剂的失活与烯丙基CH键的激活紧密相关，这一过程优先于丙烯插入，最终形成不活跃的π-烯丙基表面复合物\[10.1021/cr900122p_4\]。这一机制揭示了催化剂失活的关键途径，其中烯丙基CH键的激活将反应路径从有效的聚合转向生成不活跃的表面物种。同样，硅胶支撑的Re基烯烃复分解催化剂通过在金属环丁烷中间体的β位激活CH键而失活，涉及β-H转移过程\[10.1021/cr900122p_4\]。由此产生的烯丙基氢化物中间体可以与乙烯反应，形成具有双重潜力的高反应性物种：要么通过两个相邻烷基配体之间的α-H抽提再生催化剂，要么通过硅氧配体的β-H抽提导致脱植。这些失活途径对催化剂的性能和寿命有重大影响，强调了在支撑烯烃聚合和复分解催化剂的背景下理解和控制分子内C-H键激活的重要性。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 对支撑烯烃聚合和复分解催化剂中分子内C-H键激活机制的洞察强调了需要先进的催化剂设计策略，以减轻这些失活途径。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发对分子内C-H键激活具有抵抗力的新型催化剂系统，从而提高催化剂的寿命和性能。这可能涉及催化剂支撑体的工程设计，具有定制的功能，以防止不活跃的π-烯丙基表面复合物的形成，并稳定反应中间体，以避免β-H转移过程。此外，探索绕过烯丙基CH键激活的替代激活模式，可能导致更坚固的催化剂用于烯烃聚合和复分解反应的开发。
