                    The deactivation of supported alkene polymerization catalysts, particularly those based on lanthanides and actinides, is intricately linked to the activation of the allylic CH bond, rather than propene insertion, culminating in the formation of inactive π-allyl surface complexes \[10.1021/cr900122p_4\]. This pathway diverts from the conventional catalytic cycle, leading to a reduction in catalyst activity and longevity. Similarly, silica-supported Re-based alkene metathesis catalysts undergo deactivation through the activation of the CH bond in the β-position of metallacyclobutane intermediates, a process involving β-H transfer \[10.1021/cr900122p_4\]. The allyl hydride intermediate resulting from this process can react with ethene, forming a highly reactive species. This species has a dual role: it can either regenerate the catalyst through α-H abstraction between two adjacent alkyl ligands or lead to degrafting through β-H abstraction by the siloxy ligand \[10.1021/cr900122p_4\]. These deactivation pathways significantly impact the performance and longevity of the catalysts, highlighting the critical role of intramolecular C-H bond activation in the deactivation of supported alkene polymerization and metathesis catalysts.
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The detailed analysis of intramolecular C-H bond activation in the deactivation of supported alkene polymerization and metathesis catalysts reveals the complexity of these processes and their profound impact on catalyst performance. Future research endeavors might focus on developing strategies to mitigate the effects of these deactivation pathways, such as modifying the catalyst structure to prevent intramolecular C-H bond activation or designing new catalysts with enhanced stability against β-H transfer processes. Additionally, the exploration of alternative deactivation mechanisms that do not involve C-H bond activation could lead to the development of more robust and efficient catalysts for alkene polymerization and metathesis reactions.

                    基于镧系和锕系元素的支撑烯烃聚合催化剂的失活与烯丙基CH键的激活紧密相关，而不是丙烯插入，最终形成不活跃的π-烯丙基表面复合物\[10.1021/cr900122p_4\]。这一途径偏离了常规的催化循环，导致催化剂活性和寿命的降低。同样，硅胶支撑的Re基烯烃复分解催化剂通过在金属环丁烷中间体的β位激活CH键而失活，这一过程涉及β-H转移\[10.1021/cr900122p_4\]。这一过程产生的烯丙基氢化物中间体可以与乙烯反应，形成高度反应性的物种。这一物种具有双重作用：它可以通过两个相邻烷基配体之间的α-H抽提来再生催化剂，或者通过硅氧配体的β-H抽提导致脱接\[10.1021/cr900122p_4\]。这些失活途径对催化剂的性能和寿命有重大影响，突出了分子内C-H键激活在支撑烯烃聚合和复分解催化剂失活中的关键作用。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 对支撑烯烃聚合和复分解催化剂中分子内C-H键激活的失活的详细分析揭示了这些过程的复杂性及其对催化剂性能的深远影响。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发策略以减轻这些失活途径的影响，例如修改催化剂结构以防止分子内C-H键激活，或设计新的催化剂，以增强对β-H转移过程的稳定性。此外，探索不涉及C-H键激活的替代失活机制可能导致开发更坚固和更有效的催化剂用于烯烃聚合和复分解反应。
