The Fe3Ni catalyst demonstrates a synergistic effect, with a higher propylene yield compared to the respective monometallic catalysts. Density functional theory (DFT) calculations indicate that the CC bond cleavage pathway is favored on the Ni3Pt(111) surface, while the CH bond cleavage pathway is preferred on the bulk-terminated Fe3Ni(111) surface. Experimental results align with DFT predictions, as Fe3Ni is selective for propylene production and Ni3Pt shows good activity and selectivity for CO. The Fe3Ni catalyst is stable, with less than half a percent of its original mass lost, indicating that the main deactivation pathway is unlikely to be due to coking. The Ni3Pt catalyst loses about 8% of its original mass but does not illustrate signs of sintering.
