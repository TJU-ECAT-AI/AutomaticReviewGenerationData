                Comparative analysis of bimetallic catalysts' performance, mechanisms, and characteristics in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane: An experimental and theoretical study focusing on Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\]. Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 are promising for CO2-ODH and DRP with propane, respectively \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\]. Fe3Ni1/CeO2 shows 58% C3H6 selectivity and stability, while Ni3Pt1/CeO2 is active for DRP with 96% CO selectivity \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\]. Synergy between Fe and Ni in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 boosts C3H6 yield \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\]. Ni3Pt1/CeO2 features metallic Pt, and Fe3Ni1/CeO2 includes metallic Ni and oxidized Fe \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\]. DFT studies indicate Fe3Ni(111) favors CH scission over CC scission, unlike Pt-terminated Ni3Pt(111) \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\]. Fe3Ni(111) binds oxygen strongly, promoting selective CH scission and C3H6 formation, whereas Pt-terminated Ni3Pt(111) binds oxygen moderately, enabling rapid formation of oxygenated intermediates in reforming \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\].
