                Comparative analysis of bimetallic catalysts' performance, mechanisms, and characteristics in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane: An experimental and theoretical study focusing on Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\]. Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 are promising catalysts for CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane, respectively \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\]. Fe3Ni1/CeO2 exhibits 58% C3H6 selectivity with good stability, while Ni3Pt1/CeO2 is highly active for DRP with 96% CO selectivity \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\]. Fe3Ni1/CeO2's C3H6 yield surpasses monometallic catalysts, indicating synergy \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\]. Ni3Pt1/CeO2 features metallic Pt, Fe3Ni1/CeO2 includes metallic Ni and oxidized Fe \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\]. DFT studies suggest Fe3Ni(111) favors CH scission, unlike Pt-terminated Ni3Pt(111) \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\]. Fe3Ni(111) binds oxygen strongly, facilitating selective CH scission and C3H6 formation, whereas Pt-terminated Ni3Pt(111) binds oxygen moderately, allowing rapid formation of oxygenated intermediates \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3\].
