1.The primary challenges in current propylene production methods, such as steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking, include high energy consumption, low selectivity towards propylene, and reliance on dwindling petroleum reserves. Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) addresses these issues by offering a more energy-efficient process with higher selectivity for propylene production. However, ODHP presents its own set of advantages and limitations, particularly in the context of oxidant selection. Using oxygen as an oxidant can lead to overoxidation and the formation of undesired carbon oxides, while using molecular oxygen can result in better selectivity but requires additional steps for oxygen separation. The specific challenges associated with using oxygen include the need for precise temperature control to prevent hotspots and the potential for combustion of the desirable dehydrogenation products.
