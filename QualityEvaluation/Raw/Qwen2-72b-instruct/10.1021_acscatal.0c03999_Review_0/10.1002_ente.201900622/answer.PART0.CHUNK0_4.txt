<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1.The primary challenges in current propylene production methods, such as steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking, are their high energy consumption and the requirement for large-scale centralized plants, which limits the use of natural gas reserves in remote areas due to high shipping costs. Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) addresses these issues by offering a potentially more energy-efficient and decentralized process for propylene production. However, ODHP presents its own set of advantages and limitations, particularly in the context of oxidant selection. Using oxygen as an oxidant can lead to side cracking and combustion pathways, resulting in lower yields of propylene. The weaker CH and CC bonds in propane compared to ethane make selectivity control a greater challenge in ODHP.
