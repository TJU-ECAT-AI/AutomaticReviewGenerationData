                    Challenges in propylene production and the potential of oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) as an alternative method: advantages, limitations, and the role of oxidants [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]. Propylene production via steam cracking and dehydrogenation struggles to meet growing demand, especially in regions favoring ethylene [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]. Dehydrogenation's thermodynamic limits and coking issues hinder efficiency [10.1021/jp410750c]. ODHP, an exothermal process, enhances energy efficiency over endothermal methods [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]. However, using oxygen in ODHP boosts energy losses and fixed costs due to CO2 production, affecting economic and environmental aspects [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]. Catalyst performance, including activity and selectivity, is pivotal for the economic feasibility of alkane-based chemical processes [10.1021/jp410750c].

Future research endeavors might focus on developing catalysts with enhanced selectivity and activity, particularly in the context of ODHP using CO2 as an oxidant [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. This could potentially mitigate the over-oxidation issues associated with oxygen use and offer a more sustainable and efficient route to propylene production. Additionally, the exploration of alternative oxidants, such as N2O, which improves propene selectivity and reduces catalyst deactivation [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010], could provide new avenues for process optimization. The development of reactor designs that can better manage the exothermic nature of ODHP and prevent overheating is also a critical area for future investigation [10.1002/ente.201900622]. Moreover, the integration of ODHP with other processes, such as CO2 utilization and syngas production [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018], could offer synergistic benefits and enhance the overall efficiency of propylene production. Lastly, the investigation of novel catalysts that can withstand the harsh conditions of ODHP and maintain high activity and selectivity over extended periods is essential for the commercial viability of this process [10.1021/cr5002436].
