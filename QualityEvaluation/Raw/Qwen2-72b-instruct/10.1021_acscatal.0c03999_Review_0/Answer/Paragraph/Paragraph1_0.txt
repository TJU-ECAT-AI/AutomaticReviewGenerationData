                    The escalating demand for propylene, particularly in Western Europe, and the limitations of current production methods like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC), which are constrained by thermodynamics and catalyst deactivation, underscore the need for innovative technologies\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) presents an exothermal, energy-efficient alternative to endothermal dehydrogenation, addressing the challenges of current methods\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. However, using oxygen as an oxidant in ODHP increases energy losses and fixed costs due to CO2 production\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. The effectiveness of the catalyst is crucial for economic viability, and successful technologies will integrate innovative process designs with catalyst performance\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\].

                    Current propylene production faces high energy consumption, greenhouse gas emissions, and co-product dilution\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]. ODHP offers a cleaner, more energy-efficient route\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]. Oxygen boosts propylene yields but risks deep oxidation and requires energy-intensive separation\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]. N2O improves propene selectivity, reducing deep oxidation risk, though it may lower activity and has higher costs and environmental concerns\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]. The oxidant choice significantly impacts reaction pathway, product selectivity, and catalyst stability\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\].

                    Propylene production faces challenges including side reactions, thermodynamic constraints, high energy needs, and catalytic deactivation due to coke\[10.1021/jp410750c\]. ODHP, using an oxidant, addresses these by potentially avoiding endothermic issues and coke formation\[10.1021/jp410750c\]. However, oxygen as an oxidant risks overoxidation to CO and CO2 and reaction instability\[10.1021/jp410750c\]. Catalysts, particularly vanadium-based and mixed-metal oxides, are explored to control oxidation and enhance ODHP efficiency\[10.1021/jp410750c\], though none have achieved yields comparable to current industrial methods\[10.1021/jp410750c\].

                    Propylene production, critical for various chemicals, faces high costs and sustainability issues with current non-oxidative methods\[10.1039/C5CY00998G\]. Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) offers higher energy efficiency and process simplicity, addressing these challenges\[10.1039/C5CY00998G\]. However, ODHP's use of oxygen can lead to over-oxidation, limiting selectivity to olefins\[10.1039/C5CY00998G\]. The use of CO2 as a mild oxidant is proposed to mitigate this issue, potentially turning the process exothermic and reducing catalyst coking\[10.1039/C5CY00998G\].

                    西欧地区对丙烯需求的激增，以及当前生产方法如蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂化（FCC）受到热力学和催化剂失活的限制，凸显了对创新技术的需求\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）作为一种放热、能效高的替代方案，解决了当前方法的挑战，与吸热脱氢相比\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。然而，在ODHP中使用氧气作为氧化剂会因CO2的产生而增加能源损失和固定成本\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。催化剂的有效性对于经济可行性至关重要，成功的技术将创新的工艺设计与催化剂性能相结合\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。

                    当前丙烯生产面临高能耗、温室气体排放和副产品稀释的问题\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]。ODHP提供了一条更清洁、更节能的路线\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]。氧气可以提高丙烯产量，但存在深度氧化的风险，需要耗能的分离\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]。N2O提高了丙烯选择性，降低了深度氧化的风险，尽管它可能降低活性并具有更高的成本和环境问题\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]。氧化剂的选择对反应路径、产品选择性和催化剂稳定性有重大影响\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]。

                    丙烯生产面临侧反应、热力学约束、高能量需求和由于焦炭导致的催化失活的挑战\[10.1021/jp410750c\]。使用氧化剂的ODHP通过潜在地避免吸热问题和焦炭形成来解决这些问题\[10.1021/jp410750c\]。然而，氧气作为氧化剂存在CO和CO2过度氧化和反应不稳定的风险\[10.1021/jp410750c\]。特别是基于钒和混合金属氧化物的催化剂被研究用于控制氧化和提高ODHP效率\[10.1021/jp410750c\]，尽管没有一种催化剂的产量可以与当前工业方法相媲美\[10.1021/jp410750c\]。

                    对于各种化学品至关重要的丙烯生产，当前的非氧化方法面临着高成本和可持续性问题\[10.1039/C5CY00998G\]。丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）提供了更高的能效和更简单的工艺，解决了这些挑战\[10.1039/C5CY00998G\]。然而，ODHP使用氧气会导致过度氧化，限制了对烯烃的选择性\[10.1039/C5CY00998G\]。提议使用CO2作为温和氧化剂来缓解这一问题，有可能使过程放热并减少催化剂焦化\[10.1039/C5CY00998G\]。
