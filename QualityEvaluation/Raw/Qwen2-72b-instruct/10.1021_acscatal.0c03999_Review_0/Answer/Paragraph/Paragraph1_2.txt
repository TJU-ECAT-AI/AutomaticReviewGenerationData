                    The escalating demand for propylene, particularly in Western Europe, and the constraints of current production methods, such as coking and thermodynamic limitations, underscore the necessity for alternative approaches [\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]]. Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP), an exothermal process, presents an improved energy efficiency over endothermal dehydrogenation. However, the use of oxygen as an oxidant in ODHP can escalate energy losses and fixed costs due to CO2 production [\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]]. Current propylene production methods are energy-intensive and emit greenhouse gases, whereas ODHP offers a cleaner, more efficient route with higher yields [\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]]. Oxygen boosts ODHP yields but risks deep oxidation and necessitates costly separation [\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]]. Propylene production's equilibrium limitations are addressed by ODHP, which uses SO2 as an oxidant to enhance selectivity and propane conversion at high temperatures [\[10.1016/0021-9517(77)90228-7\]]. Rising propylene demand outpaces current production, necessitating efficient methods. DDP's limitations, including high temperatures for significant yields, are mitigated by ODHP. Using CO2 as a mild oxidant in ODHP reduces operating temperatures by oxidizing hydrogen and releasing heat, enhancing propane conversion by consuming H2 [\[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w\]].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The exploration of ODHP as an alternative to current propylene production methods reveals its potential to address energy efficiency, environmental impact, and thermodynamic constraints. The use of CO2 as a mild oxidant in ODHP not only reduces energy consumption and emissions but also enhances the process's selectivity and efficiency. Future research endeavors might focus on developing catalysts that can further suppress over-oxidation and improve propylene selectivity, while also exploring the integration of ODHP with renewable energy sources to further reduce its carbon footprint. Additionally, the optimization of reactor designs and operating conditions could lead to more efficient and economically viable ODHP processes.

                    西欧等地对丙烯需求的激增，以及当前生产方法如焦化和热力学限制的局限性，凸显了寻求替代方案的必要性 [\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]]. 丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）作为一种放热过程，相较于吸热脱氢，展现出更高的能效 [\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]]. 然而，ODHP中使用氧气作为氧化剂会因二氧化碳的生成而增加能量损失和固定成本 [\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]]. 当前的丙烯生产方法能耗高且排放温室气体，而ODHP提供了一条更清洁、更高效且产量更高的路径 [\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]]. 氧气虽能提升ODHP的产量，但存在深度氧化的风险，且需要昂贵的分离过程 [\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]]. 丙烯生产中的平衡限制通过ODHP得到解决，该过程使用二氧化硫作为氧化剂，提高了在高温下的选择性和丙烷转化率 [\[10.1016/0021-9517(77)90228-7\]]. 丙烯需求的上升速度超过了当前的生产能力，迫切需要高效的方法。直接脱氢（DDP）的局限性，包括需要高温才能获得显著的产量，被ODHP所缓解。在ODHP中使用二氧化碳作为温和氧化剂，通过氧化氢并释放热量，降低了操作温度，通过消耗H2增强了丙烷的转化率 [\[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w\]].
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 探索ODHP作为当前丙烯生产方法的替代方案，揭示了其在能效、环境影响和热力学限制方面的潜力。在ODHP中使用二氧化碳作为温和氧化剂，不仅减少了能源消耗和排放，还提高了过程的选择性和效率。未来的研究工作可能集中在开发能进一步抑制过度氧化、提高丙烯选择性的催化剂，同时探索ODHP与可再生能源的整合，以进一步减少其碳足迹。此外，优化反应器设计和操作条件可能带来更高效、经济上更可行的ODHP过程。
