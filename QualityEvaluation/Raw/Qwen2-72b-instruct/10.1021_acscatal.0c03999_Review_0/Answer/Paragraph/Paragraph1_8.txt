                    Challenges in propylene production, such as high energy consumption and low selectivity, are addressed by oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP), offering a more energy-efficient and selective process \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]. However, using oxygen as an oxidant in ODHP can cause overoxidation, necessitating precise temperature control to prevent catalyst deactivation and coke formation \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]. Oxygen's abundance and low cost are advantageous, but its use requires careful management. Suitable catalysts and reactor designs are critical for ODHP's success \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\].

                    ODHP presents an exothermal, direct route to propylene, improving energy efficiency over endothermal dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. However, using oxygen in ODHP increases energy losses and fixed costs due to CO2 production \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Catalyst performance and innovative process designs are crucial for ODHP's commercial success \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\].

                    ODHP offers a cleaner, more efficient alternative to current propylene production methods, which are energy-intensive and emit greenhouse gases \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]. Oxygen boosts propylene yields but risks deep oxidation and requires costly oxygen separation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]. Nitrous oxide (N2O) enhances propene selectivity and reduces deep oxidation risk, albeit with lower activity and higher costs \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]. The oxidant choice significantly impacts the reaction pathway, product selectivity, and catalyst stability \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\].

                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The challenges in propylene production, including high energy consumption, low selectivity, and environmental concerns, highlight the potential of ODHP as an alternative method. ODHP's exothermal nature and improved energy efficiency offer a promising route to propylene production. However, the use of oxygen as an oxidant presents limitations, including overoxidation and increased costs due to CO2 production. The choice of oxidant is critical, with N2O offering enhanced selectivity but at higher costs. Future research should focus on developing catalysts that can suppress overoxidation and improve selectivity, as well as exploring alternative oxidants that offer higher efficiency and lower environmental impact. Innovative reactor designs and process optimization are also essential for ODHP's commercial success.

                    丙烯生产面临的挑战，如高能耗和低选择性，通过丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）得到解决，它提供了一个更节能和选择性的过程\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]。然而，在ODHP中使用氧气作为氧化剂会导致过度氧化，需要精确的温度控制以防止催化剂失活和焦炭形成\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]。氧气的丰富和低成本是有利的，但其使用需要谨慎管理。合适的催化剂和反应器设计对于ODHP的成功至关重要\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]。

                    ODHP提供了一条直接的、放热的丙烯生产途径，与吸热脱氢相比，提高了能源效率\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。然而，在ODHP中使用氧气会增加能源损失和固定成本，因为CO2的产生\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。催化剂性能和创新的工艺设计对于ODHP的商业成功至关重要\[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]。

                    ODHP为当前的丙烯生产方法提供了一个更清洁、更有效的替代方案，这些方法能耗高且排放温室气体\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]。氧气提高了丙烯产量，但存在深度氧化的风险，并且需要昂贵的氧气分离\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]。一氧化二氮（N2O）增强了丙烯的选择性，降低了深度氧化的风险，尽管活性较低，成本更高\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]。氧化剂的选择对反应途径、产品选择性和催化剂稳定性有重大影响\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]。

                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望：丙烯生产面临的挑战，包括高能耗、低选择性和环境问题，凸显了ODHP作为一种替代方法的潜力。ODHP的放热性质和提高的能源效率为丙烯生产提供了一条有希望的途径。然而，使用氧气作为氧化剂存在局限性，包括过度氧化和因CO2产生而增加的成本。氧化剂的选择至关重要，N2O提供了增强的选择性，但成本更高。未来的研究应集中在开发能够抑制过度氧化和提高选择性的催化剂，以及探索提供更高效率和更低环境影响的替代氧化剂。创新的反应器设计和工艺优化对于ODHP的商业成功也是必不可少的。
