                    Challenges in propylene production and the potential of oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) as an alternative method: advantages, limitations, and the role of oxidants. Propylene production, via steam cracking and FCC, struggles with growing demand and issues like thermodynamic constraints and catalyst deactivation \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. ODHP offers an alternative with improved energy efficiency as an exothermal process, directly converting propane to propylene \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. However, using oxygen in ODHP increases energy losses and costs due to CO2 production \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Innovative catalysts and process designs are vital for enhancing performance and efficiency in ODHP, addressing the need for new technologies to produce specific olefins without the disadvantages of traditional methods \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements and findings discussed in the file, there lies an opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel catalysts that can mitigate the over-oxidation issues in ODHP, particularly when using oxygen as an oxidant. The exploration of alternative oxidants, such as CO2, which can reduce the risk of over-oxidation and potentially turn the process exothermic, is also a promising direction \[10.1039/C5CY00998G\]. Moreover, the design of efficient reactor configurations that can optimize the use of oxygen or other oxidants while minimizing side reactions and enhancing selectivity to propylene is crucial. The integration of ODHP with other processes, such as the reverse water-gas shift reaction, could also be a fruitful area of investigation, as it could provide a means to consume the CO2 produced and improve the overall efficiency of the process. Additionally, the development of advanced control strategies to manage the exothermic nature of ODHP and prevent overheating is essential for the safe and efficient operation of the process. Lastly, the economic and environmental impacts of ODHP, including the cost of oxygen separation and the potential for CO2 utilization, should be thoroughly evaluated to ensure the commercial viability of this technology.
