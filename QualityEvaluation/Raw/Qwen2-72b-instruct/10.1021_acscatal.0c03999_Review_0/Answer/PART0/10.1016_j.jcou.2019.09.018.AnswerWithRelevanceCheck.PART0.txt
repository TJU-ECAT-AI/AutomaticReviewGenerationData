      <English>
          The demand for propene is growing, and current sources such as steam cracking and catalytic cracking may not meet this demand without negatively impacting the production of other valuable co-products. Selective processes dedicated solely to propene synthesis, such as propane dehydrogenation, may gain significance. Oxidative dehydrogenation processes, including ODHP, can offer higher per-pass conversion of propane and yield of propene compared to non-oxidative dehydrogenation. The use of CO2 in the process can be an attractive way for chemical CO2 utilisation, producing hydrogen and carbon monoxide (syngas) as by-products. However, the literature does not delve into the specific challenges associated with using oxygen as an oxidant in ODHP.
