1.The primary challenges in current propylene production methods, such as steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking, include high energy consumption, complex separation processes, and the production of unwanted byproducts. Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) addresses these issues by offering a more energy-efficient process with a simpler product separation. However, ODHP presents its own set of advantages and limitations, particularly in the context of oxidant selection. The use of oxygen as an oxidant in ODHP can lead to deep oxidation reactions, resulting in the formation of carbon dioxide and water, which reduces the selectivity to propylene. Oxygen also poses safety concerns due to its reactivity with hydrocarbons, necessitating the use of diluents and careful control of reaction conditions to prevent runaway reactions and ensure process safety.
