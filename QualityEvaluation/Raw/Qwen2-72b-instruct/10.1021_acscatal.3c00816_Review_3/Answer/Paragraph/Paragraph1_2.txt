                The ultrafast zeolite crystallization strategy developed by Yu et al. \[10.1002/anie.202007044\] addresses the time-scale mismatch challenges in direct synthesis procedures for metal encapsulation by expediting the crystallization of zeolites to synchronize with the rapid formation of nanoclusters. This strategy leads to the ultrafast in situ encapsulation of metal nanoclusters into zeolites within just a few minutes. The effects on the formation, structure, and catalytic performance of PtSn clusters within ZSM-5 zeolites are significant. The resultant Pt/Sn-ZSM-5 exhibits excellent stability and activity in the dehydrogenation of propane to propene, with exceptional selectivity and stability even after multiple cycles of reaction and regeneration at high temperatures. The role of Sn in modulating the interaction between the Pt precursor and the zeolite precursor affects the size and dispersion of the Pt nanoclusters, leading to improved catalytic performance in propane conversion.

                The ultrafast zeolite crystallization strategy allows the in situ encapsulation of PtSn clusters with approximately 1 nm size inside high-silica ZSM-5 zeolites through a rapid one-pot synthesis procedure \[10.1039/D0CS01260B\]. This results in a Pt/Sn-ZSM-5 catalyst with superior catalytic stability, as evidenced by its propane conversion remaining at 45% after 24 hours in the PDH reaction at 600 degrees Celsius.

                Yu et al. developed an ultrafast zeolite crystallization strategy by combining precursor age, seed additives, and a fast-heating tubular reactor \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_3\]. This allows complete crystallization of ZSM-5 zeolites within 5 minutes at 170 C, or 2 minutes at 190 C. This ultrafast crystallization rate enables the full encapsulation of PtSn clusters within ZSM-5 zeolites. The Pt loadings in the silica matrix increase with increasing zeolite crystallinity, indicating the formation of Pt nanoclusters. The Sn species transform into amorphous SnO2 initially and then into Td Sn species within the zeolite framework, which improves Pt dispersion. By fixing the Pt loading at 0.32 wt % and increasing Sn loadings from 0 to 1.2 wt %, the size of PtSn clusters decreases from 4 nm to 1 nm. The optimized PtSn-ZSM-5 catalyst shows an initial propane conversion of 65%, significantly higher than that of impregnated Pt/Sn-ZSM-5.

The development of new protective agents for the stabilization of metal nanoclusters during zeolite synthesis could also be a promising direction. Moreover, the application of this strategy to other metal-zeolite systems and reactions could broaden its impact in the field of catalysis.

                尤等人开发的超快速沸石结晶策略\[10.1002/anie.202007044\]通过加速沸石的结晶以与纳米团簇的快速形成同步，解决了直接合成金属封装过程中的时间尺度不匹配挑战。这一策略实现了金属纳米团簇在几分钟内快速原位封装到沸石中。对ZSM-5沸石内PtSn团簇的形成、结构和催化性能的影响是显著的。所得的Pt/Sn-ZSM-5在丙烷脱氢至丙烯的反应中表现出优异的稳定性和活性，具有出色的选择性和稳定性，即使在高温下多次反应和再生后仍能保持。Sn在调节Pt前体与沸石前体之间的相互作用中所起的作用影响了Pt纳米团簇的大小和分散，从而提高了丙烷转化的催化性能。

                超快速沸石结晶策略允许大约1纳米大小的PtSn团簇通过快速一锅合成程序原位封装在高硅ZSM-5沸石内\[10.1039/D0CS01260B\]。这导致Pt/Sn-ZSM-5催化剂具有优越的催化稳定性，其丙烷转化率在600摄氏度的PDH反应中24小时后仍保持在45%。

                尤等人通过结合前体年龄、种子添加剂和快速加热管式反应器开发了超快速沸石结晶策略\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_3\]。这使得ZSM-5沸石在170°C下5分钟内或190°C下2分钟内完全结晶。这种超快速结晶速率使PtSn团簇完全封装在ZSM-5沸石内。随着沸石结晶度的增加，硅酸盐基质中的Pt负载量增加，表明Pt纳米团簇的形成。Sn物种最初完全接枝到硅酸盐基质中，随后转化为沸石框架内的无定形SnO2，然后转化为Td Sn物种，有助于提高Pt的分散性。通过将Pt负载固定在0.32 wt %，并将Sn负载从0增加到1.2 wt %，PtSn团簇的尺寸从4 nm减小到1 nm。优化的PtSn-ZSM-5催化剂的初始丙烷转化率高达65%，显著高于浸渍的Pt/Sn-ZSM-5。
