                The ultrafast zeolite crystallization strategy developed by Yu et al. \[10.1002/anie.202007044\] addresses the time-scale mismatch challenges in direct synthesis procedures for metal encapsulation by expediting the crystallization of zeolites to synchronize with the rapid formation of nanoclusters. This strategy leads to the ultrafast in situ encapsulation of metal nanoclusters into zeolites within just a few minutes \[10.1002/anie.202007044\]. The effects on the formation, structure, and catalytic performance of PtSn clusters within ZSM-5 zeolites are significant. The resultant Pt/Sn-ZSM-5 exhibits excellent stability and activity in the dehydrogenation of propane to propene, with exceptional selectivity and stability even after multiple cycles of reaction and regeneration at high temperatures \[10.1002/anie.202007044\]. The role of Sn in modulating the interaction between the Pt precursor and the zeolite precursor affects the size and dispersion of the Pt nanoclusters, leading to improved catalytic performance in propane conversion \[10.1002/anie.202007044\]. The Pt/Sn-ZSM-5 catalyst, synthesized through this ultrafast encapsulation strategy, shows superior catalytic stability, as evidenced by its propane conversion remaining at 45% after 24 hours in the PDH reaction at 600 degrees Celsius \[10.1039/D0CS01260B\]. The optimized PtSn-ZSM-5 catalyst exhibits an initial propane conversion of 65%, significantly higher than that of impregnated Pt/Sn-ZSM-5 \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_3\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in ultrafast zeolite crystallization strategies for metal encapsulation, as demonstrated by Yu et al., open up new avenues for the development of highly efficient and stable catalysts for propane dehydrogenation. The ability to synchronize the rapid formation of nanoclusters with zeolite crystallization not only enhances the catalytic performance but also ensures superior stability under high-temperature conditions. Future research endeavors might focus on exploring the impact of different metal combinations and zeolite structures on the catalytic activity and selectivity. Additionally, the investigation of the role of seed additives and precursor age in the ultrafast crystallization process could lead to further optimization of the encapsulation strategy. The development of novel synthesis procedures that can maintain the ultrafast encapsulation while reducing the reaction temperature could also be a promising direction, potentially expanding the applicability of this strategy to a broader range of catalytic systems.

                由Yu等人开发的超快速沸石结晶策略\[10.1002/anie.202007044\]通过加速沸石的结晶以与纳米团簇的快速形成同步，解决了直接合成金属封装中的时间尺度不匹配问题。这一策略实现了金属纳米团簇在几分钟内快速原位封装到沸石中\[10.1002/anie.202007044\]。PtSn团簇在ZSM-5沸石内的形成、结构和催化性能的影响显著。由此产生的Pt/Sn-ZSM-5在丙烷脱氢至丙烯中表现出优异的稳定性和活性，具有出色的选择性和稳定性，即使在高温下多次反应和再生后也是如此\[10.1002/anie.202007044\]。Sn在调节Pt前体与沸石前体之间的相互作用中所起的作用影响了Pt纳米团簇的大小和分散，从而提高了丙烷转化的催化性能\[10.1002/anie.202007044\]。通过这种超快速封装策略合成的Pt/Sn-ZSM-5催化剂在600摄氏度下24小时PDH反应后，丙烷转化率保持在45%，显示出卓越的催化稳定性\[10.1039/D0CS01260B\]。优化的PtSn-ZSM-5催化剂的初始丙烷转化率高达65%，显著高于浸渍的Pt/Sn-ZSM-5\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_3\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: Yu等人展示的超快速沸石结晶策略在金属封装中的进展为开发高效稳定的丙烷脱氢催化剂开辟了新的途径。将纳米团簇的快速形成与沸石结晶同步的能力不仅提高了催化性能，还确保了在高温条件下的卓越稳定性。未来的研究努力可能集中在探索不同金属组合和沸石结构对催化活性和选择性的影响。此外，研究种子添加剂和前体年龄在超快速结晶过程中的作用可能会导致封装策略的进一步优化。开发在保持超快速封装的同时降低反应温度的新合成程序也可能是一个有希望的方向，可能扩大这一策略在更广泛催化系统中的应用范围。
