                The ultrafast zeolite crystallization strategy developed by Yu et al. \[10.1002/anie.202007044\] addresses the time-scale mismatch challenges in direct synthesis procedures for metal encapsulation by expediting the crystallization of zeolites to synchronize with the rapid formation of nanoclusters. This strategy leads to the ultrafast in situ encapsulation of metal nanoclusters into zeolites within just a few minutes \[10.1002/anie.202007044\]. The effects on the formation, structure, and catalytic performance of PtSn clusters within ZSM-5 zeolites are significant. The resultant Pt/Sn-ZSM-5 exhibits excellent stability and activity in the dehydrogenation of propane to propene, with exceptional selectivity and stability even after multiple cycles of reaction and regeneration at high temperatures \[10.1002/anie.202007044\]. The role of Sn in modulating the interaction between the Pt precursor and the zeolite precursor affects the size and dispersion of the Pt nanoclusters, leading to improved catalytic performance in propane conversion \[10.1002/anie.202007044\]. The Pt/Sn-ZSM-5 catalyst, synthesized through this ultrafast encapsulation strategy, shows superior catalytic stability, as evidenced by its propane conversion remaining at 45% after 24 hours in the PDH reaction at 600 degrees Celsius \[10.1039/D0CS01260B\]. The optimized PtSn-ZSM-5 catalyst exhibits an initial propane conversion of 65%, significantly higher than that of impregnated Pt/Sn-ZSM-5 \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_3\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The ultrafast zeolite crystallization strategy developed by Yu et al. represents a significant advancement in the field of catalysis, particularly for propane dehydrogenation. This strategy not only addresses the time-scale mismatch challenges but also enhances the catalytic performance of PtSn clusters within ZSM-5 zeolites. Future research endeavors might focus on exploring the potential of this strategy in the encapsulation of other metal clusters and in different zeolite structures. Additionally, the investigation of the role of Sn in modulating the interaction between the metal precursor and the zeolite precursor could lead to the development of new catalysts with improved selectivity and stability. The optimization of synthesis conditions, such as precursor age, seed additives, and reaction temperature, could further enhance the encapsulation efficiency and catalytic performance. Moreover, the study of the long-term stability and regeneration of these catalysts under industrial conditions could provide insights into their practical applications.

                由Yu等人开发的超快速沸石结晶策略\[10.1002/anie.202007044\]通过加速沸石的结晶以与纳米团簇的快速形成同步，解决了直接合成金属封装的时间尺度不匹配问题。这一策略实现了金属纳米团簇在几分钟内快速原位封装到沸石中\[10.1002/anie.202007044\]。对ZSM-5沸石内PtSn团簇的形成、结构和催化性能的影响是显著的。所得的Pt/Sn-ZSM-5在丙烷脱氢到丙烯中表现出优异的稳定性和活性，具有出色的选择性和稳定性，即使在高温下多次反应和再生后也是如此\[10.1002/anie.202007044\]。Sn在调节Pt前驱体与沸石前驱体之间的相互作用，影响Pt纳米团簇的大小和分散，从而提高丙烷转化的催化性能方面的作用\[10.1002/anie.202007044\]。通过这种超快速封装策略合成的Pt/Sn-ZSM-5催化剂在PDH反应中24小时后丙烷转化率保持在45%，显示出优越的催化稳定性\[10.1039/D0CS01260B\]。优化的PtSn-ZSM-5催化剂的初始丙烷转化率高达65%，显著高于浸渍的Pt/Sn-ZSM-5\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_3\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: Yu等人开发的超快速沸石结晶策略在催化领域，特别是在丙烷脱氢方面，代表了显著的进步。这一策略不仅解决了时间尺度不匹配的挑战，还增强了ZSM-5沸石内PtSn团簇的催化性能。未来的研究努力可能集中在探索这一策略在封装其他金属团簇和不同沸石结构中的潜力。此外，研究Sn在调节金属前驱体与沸石前驱体之间相互作用中的作用，可能导致开发具有改进选择性和稳定性的新催化剂。优化合成条件，如前驱体年龄、种子添加剂和反应温度，可以进一步提高封装效率和催化性能。此外，研究这些催化剂在工业条件下的长期稳定性和再生性，可以为它们的实际应用提供见解。
