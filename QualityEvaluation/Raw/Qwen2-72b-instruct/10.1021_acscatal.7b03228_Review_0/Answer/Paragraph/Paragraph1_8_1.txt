The interplay of geometric and electronic effects in transition-metal alloys significantly enhances catalytic performance, selectivity, and reactivity compared to monometallic catalysts \[10.1016/j.jcat.2014.07.016\]. These effects synergistically influence catalytic activity by modifying the local electronic structure and chemical reactivity \[10.1007/s11244-010-9448-1\]. Alloying impacts electronic properties, facilitating charge transfer between elements due to orbital energy differences and altering metal bonding hybridization from changed bond distances \[10.1016/j.jcat.2014.07.016\]. Geometric effects involve bonding configurations, while electronic effects concern adsorbate-metal surface interactions \[10.1021/acscatal.7b03228_0\]. Perturbation of the d-band through charge transfer and hybridization affects adsorption behavior, with charge transfer stabilizing d-orbitals and impacting d-orbital density \[10.1021/acscatal.7b03228_0\].

In PtSn-based catalysts, Sn addition modifies the platinum active phase and support, suppressing hydrogenolysis and isomerization, minimizing metal sintering, neutralizing support acidity, and facilitating coke species diffusion \[10.1021/cr5002436\]. The formation of PtSn alloy or partial covering of platinum by tin creates smaller ensembles, curbing coking. Electronic modification of Pt by Sn weakens ethylidyne adsorption, inhibiting coke formation. An optimum Pt3Sn ratio balances geometric and electronic effects, resulting in high activity and selectivity for dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1021/cr5002436\].

Innovative strategies to exploit this interplay include high-throughput screening for optimal alloy compositions, theoretical calculations to predict alloying effects, and new synthesis methods for precise control of catalyst structure \[10.1021/cr5002436\]. These strategies can optimize the balance between geometric and electronic effects, leading to more efficient, selective, and tunable catalytic systems that outperform traditional monometallic catalysts \[10.1021/ar50109a003\].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The synergistic effects of geometric and electronic properties in transition-metal alloys offer a promising avenue for enhancing catalytic performance, selectivity, and reactivity. By understanding and controlling these properties, it is possible to design superior catalytic systems that outperform monometallic catalysts. Future research efforts might focus on developing advanced computational tools for predicting alloying effects, refining synthesis methods for precise control of catalyst structure, and exploring the potential of intermetallic compounds, single-atom alloys, and coadsorbed structural modifiers \[10.1021/acscatal.7b03228_0\]. These advancements could lead to the development of more efficient, selective, and tunable catalytic systems for various applications, including propane dehydrogenation.

过渡金属合金中几何和电子效应的相互作用显著提高了催化性能、选择性和反应性，与单金属催化剂相比具有优势 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2014.07.016\]。这些效应协同影响催化活性，通过改变局部电子结构和化学反应性 \[10.1007/s11244-010-9448-1\]。合金化影响电子性质，通过轨道能量差异促进元素之间的电荷转移，并由于键距变化改变金属键合杂化 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2014.07.016\]。几何效应涉及键合配置，而电子效应涉及吸附物-金属表面相互作用 \[10.1021/acscatal.7b03228_0\]。通过电荷转移和杂化扰动d带影响吸附行为，电荷转移稳定d轨道，影响d轨道密度 \[10.1021/acscatal.7b03228_0\]。

在PtSn基催化剂中，Sn的添加修改了铂活性相和支持物，抑制了氢解和异构化，最小化了金属烧结，中和了支持物酸性，并促进了焦炭物种的扩散 \[10.1021/cr5002436\]。PtSn合金的形成或铂部分被锡覆盖创建了较小的集合体，抑制了焦化。Sn对Pt的电子修饰削弱了乙基化物吸附，抑制了焦炭形成。Pt3Sn的最优比例平衡了几何和电子效应，为脱氢反应带来了高活性和选择性 \[10.1021/cr5002436\]。

为了利用这种相互作用，创新策略包括对最优合金组成的高通量筛选、预测合金效应的理论计算以及精确控制催化剂结构的新合成方法 \[10.1021/cr5002436\]。这些策略可以优化几何和电子效应之间的平衡，导致比传统单金属催化剂更高效、更选择性和更可调的催化系统 \[10.1021/ar50109a003\]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：过渡金属合金中几何和电子性质的协同效应为提高催化性能、选择性和反应性提供了一条有希望的途径。通过理解和控制这些性质，有可能设计出优于单金属催化剂的优越催化系统。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发预测合金效应的先进计算工具、改进催化剂结构精确控制的合成方法以及探索金属间化合物、单原子合金和共吸附结构修饰剂的潜力 \[10.1021/acscatal.7b03228_0\]。这些进展可能导致开发更高效、更选择性和更可调的催化系统，用于各种应用，包括丙烷脱氢。
