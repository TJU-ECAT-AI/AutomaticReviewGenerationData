The interplay between geometric and electronic effects in transition-metal alloys significantly enhances catalytic performance, selectivity, and reactivity compared to monometallic catalysts \[10.1016/j.jcat.2014.07.016\]. These effects synergistically influence the catalytic activity by altering the bonding configurations and adsorbate interaction strength \[10.1021/acscatal.7b03228_0\]. D-band perturbation, comprising charge transfer and hybridization, plays a crucial role in adsorption behavior \[10.1007/s11244-006-0005-x\]. The relative dominance of geometric and electronic effects in controlling selectivity is often unclear, but understanding their interplay can guide the design of superior catalysts \[10.1016/j.jcat.2014.07.016\].

Innovative strategies to exploit this interplay include intermetallic compounds, single-atom alloys, and coadsorbed structural modifiers \[10.1021/acscatal.7b03228_0\]. These approaches offer pathways to tailor reaction selectivity and tune reactivity, potentially leading to more efficient, selective, and tunable catalytic systems \[10.1007/s11244-006-0005-x\]. For instance, alloying Sn with Pt(111) opens a new hydrogenation pathway, selectively producing butene from coadsorbed H and 1,3-butadiene \[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.12.006\]. This highlights the role of surface properties in adsorption and reaction, suggesting that controlling the composition and structure of bimetallic catalysts could optimize their performance for specific reactions \[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.12.006\].

PtSn-based transition-metal alloys demonstrate enhanced catalytic performance for dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1021/cr5002436\]. Sn addition modifies the platinum active phase and support, suppressing undesired reactions, minimizing sintering, and facilitating coke diffusion \[10.1021/cr5002436\]. Electronic modification of Pt by Sn weakens ethylidyne adsorption, inhibiting coke formation \[10.1021/cr5002436\]. An optimal Pt3Sn ratio balances these effects for high activity and selectivity \[10.1021/cr5002436\]. Strategies to exploit this interplay include high-throughput screening for optimal compositions, theoretical calculations to predict alloying effects, and precise synthesis methods for alloy catalysts \[10.1021/cr5002436\].

过渡金属合金中几何和电子效应的相互作用显著提高了催化性能、选择性和反应性，与单金属催化剂相比具有优势 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2014.07.016\]。这些效应通过改变键合配置和吸附物相互作用强度，协同影响催化活性 \[10.1021/acscatal.7b03228_0\]。d带扰动，包括电荷转移和杂化，对吸附行为起着关键作用 \[10.1007/s11244-006-0005-x\]。几何和电子效应在控制选择性中的相对主导地位通常不清楚，但理解它们的相互作用可以指导设计更优秀的催化剂 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2014.07.016\]。

为了利用这种相互作用，创新策略包括金属间化合物、单原子合金和共吸附结构修饰剂 \[10.1021/acscatal.7b03228_0\]。这些方法提供了途径来定制反应选择性和调节反应性，可能导向更高效、更选择性和更可调的催化系统 \[10.1007/s11244-006-0005-x\]。例如，将Sn与Pt(111)合金化，开辟了一条新的氢化途径，从共吸附的H和1,3-丁二烯中选择性地产生丁烯 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.12.006\]。这突出了表面性质在吸附和反应中的作用，表明通过控制双金属催化剂的组成和结构，可以优化其在特定反应中的性能 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.12.006\]。

基于PtSn的过渡金属合金展示了在脱氢反应中增强的催化性能 \[10.1021/cr5002436\]。Sn的添加修改了铂活性相和支持物，抑制了不希望的反应，最小化了烧结，并促进了焦炭扩散 \[10.1021/cr5002436\]。Pt的电子改性通过Sn减弱了乙炔吸附，抑制了焦炭形成 \[10.1021/cr5002436\]。Pt3Sn的最优比例平衡了这些效应，实现了高活性和选择性 \[10.1021/cr5002436\]。利用这种相互作用的策略包括对最优组成的高通量筛选、预测合金效应的理论计算，以及合金催化剂的精确合成方法 \[10.1021/cr5002436\]。
