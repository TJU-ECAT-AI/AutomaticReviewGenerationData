      <English>
          The document does not provide a direct comparison of various metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP or a detailed analysis of the factors influencing their catalytic performance. However, it does discuss the preparation and characterization of Cr-MCM-41 catalysts using two different methods: direct hydrothermal synthesis (DHT) and template-ion exchange (TIE). The study indicates that the coordination structure and dispersion of chromium species on the support can significantly affect the catalytic properties. For instance, monochromate species mainly exist on Cr-MCM-41 prepared by DHT, while several types of chromate species, including both monochromates and polychromates, coexist on Cr-MCM-41 prepared by TIE. The two kinds of samples exhibit similar catalytic properties in the dehydrogenation of propane with carbon dioxide, with a selectivity to propylene higher than 90%. The presence of carbon dioxide enhances propane conversion. During the dehydrogenation reactions, the chromate species on both types of samples are reduced to aggregated Cr(III) with octahedral coordination. In contrast, the selectivity to formaldehyde in the partial oxidation of methane with oxygen is remarkably higher over the sample prepared by DHT than that by TIE. The structure of the chromate species is kept during the oxidation of methane, and the high dispersion of monochromate species probably accounts for the higher selectivity over the sample prepared by DHT.
