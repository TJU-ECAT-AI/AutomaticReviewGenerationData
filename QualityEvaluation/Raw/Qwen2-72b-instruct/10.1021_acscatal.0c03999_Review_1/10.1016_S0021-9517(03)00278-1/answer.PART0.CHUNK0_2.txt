However, it does discuss the preparation and characterization of Cr-MCM-41 catalysts using two different methods: direct hydrothermal synthesis (DHT) and template-ion exchange (TIE). The study suggests that the structure of the chromate species on the catalysts affects their catalytic properties. In the dehydrogenation of propane with carbon dioxide, both types of samples exhibit similar catalytic properties with high selectivity to propylene. In the partial oxidation of methane with oxygen, the selectivity to formaldehyde is higher over the sample prepared by the DHT method compared to the TIE method. The high dispersion of monochromate species on the DHT sample is suggested to account for the higher selectivity.
