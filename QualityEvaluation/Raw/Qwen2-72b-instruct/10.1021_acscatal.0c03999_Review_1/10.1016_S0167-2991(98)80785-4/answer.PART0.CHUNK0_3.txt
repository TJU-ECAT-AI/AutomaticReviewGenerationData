1.The literature does not provide a direct comparison of various metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP or detail the evolution of chromium species in supported Cr2O3 catalysts as a function of loading and support properties. However, it does mention that CO2 has promoting effects on the dehydrogenation of propane over a silica-supported Cr2O3 catalyst, enhancing the yield of C3H6 and suppressing catalyst deactivation. The partially oxidized Cr2O3 supported on SiO2 is active for the dehydrogenation of C3H8, and in the presence of CO2, the surface of Cr2O3/SiO2 might be maintained partially oxidized during the reaction.
