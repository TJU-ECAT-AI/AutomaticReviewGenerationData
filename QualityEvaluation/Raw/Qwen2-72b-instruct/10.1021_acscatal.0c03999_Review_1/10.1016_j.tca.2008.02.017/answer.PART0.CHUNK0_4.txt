1.The literature does not provide a direct comparison of various metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP or discuss the evolution of chromium species in supported Cr2O3 catalysts as a function of loading and support properties. However, it does mention that the catalytic activity and selectivity of CrOx-containing active carbons for propane dehydrogenation in the presence of carbon dioxide strongly depend on the Cr loading, with the highest propane conversions achieved for the sample with a Cr content close to 5 wt.%. An increase in the Cr loading results in the formation of crystalline -Cr2O3 phase, a partial blocking of micropores, and a decrease in catalytic activity. The active carbon-based catalyst undergoes fast deactivation due to the gradual decrease in the surface concentration of carbonyl- and carboxyl-like surface species and the formation of inactive coke on the catalyst surface during the propane dehydrogenation process.
