1.The document does not provide a direct comparison of various metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP or a detailed analysis of the evolution of chromium species in supported Cr2O3 catalysts as a function of loading and support properties. However, it does discuss the effect of Ru loading on the activity and selectivity of a SiO2-supported chromium oxide catalyst for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODP) with CO2. The C3H8 conversion and C3H6 production rate showed a volcano-shaped activity trend with respect to Ru loading, with an optimal loading amount of 1 wt% Ru, resulting in a two-fold enhancement of C3H6 production rate compared with the unmodified silica supported chromium oxide catalyst. Small amounts of Ru promote the activation of C3H8 and CO2 and facilitate the equilibrium shift of direct dehydrogenation by removing the adsorbed H2 on the catalyst surface through the reverse water-gas shift reaction. However, large amounts of Ru lead to a low C3H8 conversion and poor selectivity to C3H6 due to the increased adsorption of CO2 and high activity for the reforming reaction of C3H6 and CO2.
