                    Comprehensive evaluation of metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP: From initial screening to in-depth analysis of chromium-based catalysts, focusing on performance factors, species evolution, and structure-activity relationships. The study of reference \[10.1002/cctc.201200397\] established that CrOx/silica excels in CO2-ODHP, with Cr3+ boosting activity. Ni regenerates Cr3+ from Cr2+, enhancing stability and selectivity. Species evolve with loading and support properties, with Ni sustaining Cr3+. The reference \[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\] further elucidates that CrOx excels in CO2-ODHP, with CO2 boosting propene via CrOx. Low Cr loads favor redox Cr6+, while high loads introduce Cr3+ and Cr2O3. The reference \[10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017\] highlights that Cr2O3 at 5 wt.% Cr optimizes CO2-ODHP; loads higher than 5 wt.% cause crystallization, blocking pores. Active carbon support, enriched with oxygen, is key, and CO2 stabilizes oxygen, preventing deactivation. Chromium species evolve, impacting performance. The reference \[10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070\] reveals that in CO2-ODHP, Cr oxide catalysts up to 6.0 wt% Cr boost activity via chromate species, maximizing propene yield at 69%; loads above 6.0 wt% Cr yield inactive -Cr2O3, plus Brnsted sites, enhancing coke, hindering performance.
