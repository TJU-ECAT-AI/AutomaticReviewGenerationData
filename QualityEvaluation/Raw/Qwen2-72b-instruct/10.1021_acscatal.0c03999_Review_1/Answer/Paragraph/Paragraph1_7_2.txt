Comprehensive evaluation of metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP: From initial screening to in-depth analysis of chromium-based catalysts, focusing on performance factors, species evolution, and structure-activity relationships.

The comprehensive evaluation of metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP has revealed the pivotal role of chromium species, particularly Cr3+, in enhancing activity and selectivity \[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]. Chromium oxides supported on silica have been found to excel in CO2-ODHP, with the Cr3+ state being vital for catalytic performance \[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]. Nickel has been shown to boost the regeneration of Cr3+, thereby enhancing the stability and selectivity of Cr2O3 catalysts \[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]. The evolution of chromium species in supported Cr2O3 catalysts as a function of loading and support properties has been a critical area of investigation, with Cr3+ species, loading, and support properties playing a key role in activity and selectivity \[10.1007/s10562-006-0077-z\]. Chromium species evolve with loading and support, impacting the catalytic performance in CO2-ODHP \[10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017\]. The activity of Cr2O3 catalysts peaks at 5 wt.%, with higher loads forming crystalline Cr2O3, blocking pores \[10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017\]. Active carbon support enriched with oxygen groups has been found to aid propane dehydrogenation, with CO2 stabilizing surface groups \[10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017\]. The evolution of chromium species, influenced by loading and support, has a significant impact on the performance of Cr2O3 catalysts in CO2-ODHP \[10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017\].

Cr2O3 catalysts peak at 6.0 wt% Cr, yielding 69% propene via chromate species; higher loadings form inactive -Cr2O3 and increase coke-producing Brnsted sites \[10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070\]. The activity of Cr2O3 catalysts is linked to chromate species, and high Cr boosts Brnsted sites, aiding coke formation \[10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070\]. The optimal dispersion of Cr at 6.0 wt% is key to the high activity of Cr2O3 catalysts \[10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070\].

CO2 boosts propene yield in Cr2O3-catalyzed CO2-ODHP, key to activity; species evolution, loading, and support properties impact performance \[10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4\]. The role of CO2 in stabilizing the surface of Cr2O3 catalysts and influencing species evolution has been highlighted, with CO2 playing a critical role in sustaining activity and selectivity \[10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4\].

b-Ga2O3-supported Cr2O3 excels in CO2-ODHP; Cr3+ species, loading, and support properties are key to activity, selectivity, and coke resistance \[10.1007/s10562-006-0077-z\].
