The comprehensive evaluation of metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP, from initial screening to in-depth analysis of chromium-based catalysts, reveals a multifaceted landscape of activity and selectivity \[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]. CrOx/silica emerges as a leading catalyst, with Cr3+ playing a pivotal role in the reaction mechanism \[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]. The addition of Ni boosts the regeneration of Cr3+, enhancing the stability and selectivity of the catalyst \[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]. Chromium species evolve with loading and support properties, significantly impacting the catalytic performance \[10.1002/cctc.201200397\].

Cr and Ga oxides also excel in CO2-ODHP, with CO2 boosting the yield of propene \[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\]. The redox properties of Cr (Cr6+, Cr3+) are crucial, influenced by loading, preparation, precursor, and support \[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\]. On SiO2-supported catalysts, low Cr loadings favor Cr6+, while high loadings introduce Cr3+ and Cr2O3 \[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040\].
