      <English>
          The literature does not provide a direct comparison of various metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP, nor does it detail the evolution of chromium species in supported Cr2O3 catalysts as a function of loading and support properties. However, it does discuss the effect of support type (SiO2 vs Al2O3) on the catalytic performance of CrOx-based catalysts in the dehydrogenation of propane with CO2. Over CrOx/SiO2, CO2 enhances propene yield, while over CrOx/Al2O3, the yield and selectivity of propene in the presence of CO2 decrease. The acidity/basicity of the oxide support is suggested to play a significant role in the catalytic properties of the supported chromium catalysts. The nature of the support strongly influences the catalytic performance. The promoting effect of CO2 on propene yield was observed on the CrOx/SiO2 catalyst, while on the CrOx/Al2O3 catalyst, carbon dioxide did not promote dehydrogenation. It seems that CO2 and/or the steam formed during the dehydrogenation with CO2 change the acidity/basicity of the CrOx/Al2O3 catalyst. The chromium loading level also influences the effect of CO2, with the ratio of yield in He to yield in CO2 (YHe/YCO2) increasing over CrOx/SiO2 with higher chromium loading, and decreasing over CrOx/Al2O3 with higher loading.
