      <English>
          The literature does not provide a direct comparison of various metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP. However, it does state that b-Ga2O3 supported chromium oxide materials are active and selective for dehydrogenation of propane. The high catalytic activity is attributed to the dehydrogenating properties of both gallium and chromium oxides. Chromium oxide deposition on b-Ga2O3 enhances resistance to coke deposition, possibly due to decreased surface acidity. The effect of chromium loading and support properties on the evolution of chromium species is not explicitly discussed. The evolution of chromium species in supported Cr2O3 catalysts as a function of loading and support properties is not discussed in detail, but it is suggested that deposition of chromium oxide generates surface Cr3+ species highly active in the dehydrogenation process.
