<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1.The primary causes of metal oxide catalyst deactivation, focusing on Cr-based and VOx catalysts, in oxidative dehydrogenation processes are not explicitly stated in the literature. However, it is mentioned that the presence of WOx hinders the clustering of VOx species during the reaction, preventing the formation of non-selective oxygen, which reduces the probability of CO2 formation and thus total oxidation of propane. Reoxidation with CO2 is less effective than with O2, as it can only oxidize to V2O4, not V2O5. CO2 addition can mitigate overoxidation in VOx-catalyzed reactions by suppressing the formation of surface carbon and enhancing the removal of coke.
