The deactivation mechanisms of metal oxide catalysts, particularly Cr-based and VOx catalysts, in oxidative dehydrogenation processes are multifaceted and complex \[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]. CrOx/silica catalysts, for instance, suffer from rapid deactivation due to coke deposition and the reduction of CrOx, which converts the coordinatively unsaturated Cr3+ state into the inactive Cr2+ state \[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]. The deactivation of VOx/SiO2 catalysts is accelerated with increasing VOx content above the theoretical monolayer coverage, leading to a decrease in catalytic activity and selectivity to propylene at the expense of propane cracking reactions \[10.1002/ejic.201701049\]. The effectiveness of regeneration methods, such as reoxidation with air, CO2, or O2, varies in terms of their ability to recover the activity and selectivity of the catalysts \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3\]. Reoxidation with air or CO2 at high temperatures (550-650 degrees Celsius) is effective for Cr-based catalysts, but achieving a complete recovery of active sites requires proper preparation methods to ensure well-dispersed Cr species \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3\]. For VOx catalysts, CO2 addition in the feed gas of C3H8/O2 can mitigate overoxidation by decreasing surface molecular oxygen species and partially reoxidizing VOx catalysts, converting V2O3 to V2O4, but not fully to V2O5 \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3\]. The addition of CO2 can also enhance the selectivity towards propylene production by suppressing the formation of surface carbon \[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\].

The potential of CO2 addition for mitigating overoxidation in VOx-catalyzed reactions is a promising strategy \[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]. CO2 can re-oxidize partially reduced metal oxides, suppress the formation of surface carbon, and enhance the selectivity towards propylene production \[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]. The presence of WOx hinders the clustering of VOx species during the reaction, preventing the formation of non-selective oxygen, which reduces the probability of CO2 formation and thus total oxidation of propane \[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]. Reoxidation with CO2 is less effective than with O2 in fully reoxidizing the catalyst to its V2O5 state, as it can only oxidize to V2O4 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The literature provides a comprehensive understanding of the deactivation mechanisms and regeneration strategies for Cr-based and VOx catalysts in oxidative dehydrogenation processes. The potential of CO2 addition for mitigating overoxidation in VOx-catalyzed reactions is a promising strategy that warrants further investigation. Future research efforts might focus on developing novel catalyst preparation methods that ensure well-dispersed Cr species and enhance the stability of VOx catalysts. The exploration of alternative regeneration methods that can fully reoxidize VOx catalysts to their V2O5 state without compromising catalytic performance is also a critical area for future research. The use of CO2 as a co-feed in oxidative dehydrogenation processes could potentially lead to the development of more sustainable and environmentally friendly catalytic systems. The optimization of reaction conditions, such as temperature and pressure, to minimize coke formation and maximize propylene selectivity is another important aspect that requires further investigation. The integration of advanced characterization techniques, such as in situ spectroscopy and microscopy, could provide valuable insights into the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and composition during oxidative dehydrogenation processes. The development of predictive models based on density functional theory (DFT) calculations could also aid in the rational design of new catalysts with improved performance and stability.

金属氧化物催化剂，特别是Cr基和VOx催化剂，在氧化脱氢过程中的失活机制是多方面的和复杂的\[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]。例如，CrOx/二氧化硅催化剂由于焦炭沉积和CrOx的还原而迅速失活，这将配位不饱和的Cr3+状态转化为不活跃的Cr2+状态\[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]。VOx/SiO2催化剂的失活随着VOx含量超过理论单层覆盖度而加速，导致催化活性和选择性向丙烯的降低，以牺牲丙烷裂解反应为代价\[10.1002/ejic.201701049\]。再生方法，如用空气、CO2或O2进行再氧化，其恢复催化剂活性和选择性的效果各不相同\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3\]。对于Cr基催化剂，高温(550-650摄氏度)下的空气或CO2再氧化是有效的，但要完全恢复活性位点，需要适当的制备方法来确保Cr物种的均匀分散\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3\]。对于VOx催化剂，C3H8/O2进料气体中CO2的添加可以通过减少表面分子氧物种来缓解过度氧化，并将VOx催化剂部分再氧化，将V2O3转化为V2O4，但不能完全转化为V2O5\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3\]。CO2的添加也可以通过抑制表面碳的形成来增强对丙烯生产的选择性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]。

CO2添加在缓解VOx催化反应中的过度氧化方面的潜力是一个有前景的策略\[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]。CO2可以再氧化部分还原的金属氧化物，抑制表面碳的形成，并增强对丙烯生产的选择性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]。WOx的存在可以阻止反应过程中VOx物种的聚集，防止非选择性氧的形成，从而降低CO2的形成概率，从而降低丙烷的总氧化\[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]。与O2相比，CO2的再氧化在将催化剂完全再氧化至其V2O5状态方面效果较差，因为它只能氧化至V2O4\[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]。

综合理解与未来展望：文献提供了Cr基和VOx催化剂在氧化脱氢过程中的失活机制和再生策略的全面理解。CO2添加在缓解VOx催化反应中的过度氧化方面的潜力是一个值得进一步研究的有希望的策略。未来的研究工作可能集中在开发确保Cr物种均匀分散并增强VOx催化剂稳定性的新型催化剂制备方法上。探索不会损害催化性能的、可以将VOx催化剂完全再氧化至其V2O5状态的替代再生方法，也是未来研究的关键领域。CO2作为氧化脱氢过程中的共进料的使用，可能最终导致更可持续和环境友好的催化系统的开发。优化反应条件，如温度和压力，以最小化焦炭的形成并最大化丙烯的选择性，是另一个需要进一步研究的重要方面。先进的表征技术，如原位光谱学和显微镜的集成，可以为氧化脱氢过程中催化剂结构和组成动态变化提供有价值的见解。基于密度泛函理论(DFT)计算的预测模型的发展也可以帮助合理设计具有改进性能和稳定性的新型催化剂。
