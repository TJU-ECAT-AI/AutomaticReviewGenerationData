<English>
The deactivation mechanisms of metal oxide catalysts, specifically Cr-based and VOx, in oxidative dehydrogenation processes are multifaceted and complex. CrOx/silica catalysts, for instance, suffer from rapid deactivation due to coke deposition and the reduction of CrOx, which converts the coordinatively unsaturated Cr3+ state into the inactive Cr2+ state \[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]. This deactivation is exacerbated by the abstraction of hydrogen from the allylic position of higher alkenes, leading to the formation of coke precursors \[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2\]. For VOx catalysts, deactivation is observed with increasing VOx content above the theoretical monolayer coverage, resulting in decreased catalytic activity and selectivity to propylene \[10.1002/ejic.201701049\]. The VOx/SiO2 catalysts are particularly susceptible to deactivation at higher reaction temperatures, with propane conversion decreasing by about 50% after 1 h under catalytic reaction conditions \[10.1002/ejic.201701049\].

Regeneration strategies for these catalysts include reoxidation with air, CO2, or O2. Reoxidation with air or CO2 at high temperatures (550-650 degrees Celsius) is effective in recovering most of the activity and selectivity of Cr-based catalysts \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3\]. However, achieving a complete recovery of active sites is challenging and requires proper preparation methods to ensure well-dispersed Cr species \[10.1021/cr5002436\]. For VOx catalysts, the addition of CO2 in the feed gas of C3H8/O2 can mitigate overoxidation by decreasing surface molecular oxygen species \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3\]. CO2 can partially reoxidize VOx catalysts, converting V2O3 to V2O4, but cannot fully reoxidize them to V2O5 \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3\]. The presence of WOx hinders the clustering of VOx species during the reaction, preventing the formation of non-selective oxygen, which reduces the probability of CO2 formation and thus total oxidation of propane \[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\].

The addition of CO2 can mitigate overoxidation in VOx-catalyzed reactions by suppressing the formation of surface carbon and allowing the reaction to proceed via direct oxidative dehydrogenation and non-oxidative dehydrogenation followed by the reverse water gas shift reaction \[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]. However, the effect of CO2 depends strongly on the catalyst support. If the basic sites are too strong, CO2 irreversibly adsorbs and poisons these sites, increasing the acidity of the support, which in turn increases coke formation and lowers selectivity \[10.1021/cr5002436\]. If these basic sites are absent, no beneficial effects of CO2 are observed \[10.1021/cr5002436\].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The deactivation mechanisms of Cr-based and VOx catalysts in oxidative dehydrogenation processes are complex, involving coke deposition, reduction of active sites, and overoxidation. Regeneration strategies, particularly the use of CO2, offer promising avenues for mitigating these issues. However, the effectiveness of regeneration methods is influenced by factors such as temperature requirements and the impact on catalytic performance. The potential of CO2 addition for mitigating overoxidation in VOx-catalyzed reactions is a promising area for further exploration. Future research might focus on developing catalysts with enhanced stability against deactivation, optimizing regeneration methods for specific catalyst types, and exploring the synergistic effects of CO2 addition on catalytic performance. The use of advanced characterization techniques and computational methods could provide deeper insights into the deactivation mechanisms and guide the design of more robust catalysts for oxidative dehydrogenation processes.

<Chinese>
金属氧化物催化剂，特别是Cr基和VOx催化剂，在氧化脱氢过程中的失活机制是多方面的且复杂。例如，CrOx/二氧化硅催化剂由于焦炭沉积和CrOx的还原而迅速失活，这将配位不饱和的Cr3+状态转化为不活跃的Cr2+状态\[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]。这种失活因从更高烯烃的烯丙位抽象氢，导致焦炭前体的形成而加剧\[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2\]。对于VOx催化剂，当VOx含量超过理论单层覆盖时，观察到失活，导致催化活性和丙烯选择性下降\[10.1002/ejic.201701049\]。VOx/SiO2催化剂在较高反应温度下特别容易失活，丙烷转化率在催化反应条件下1小时后下降约50%\[10.1002/ejic.201701049\]。

这些催化剂的再生策略包括使用空气、CO2或O2进行再氧化。在高温(550-650摄氏度)下使用空气或CO2进行再氧化对于恢复Cr基催化剂的大部分活性和选择性是有效的\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3\]。然而，完全恢复活性位点具有挑战性，需要适当制备方法以确保Cr物种的均匀分散\[10.1021/cr5002436\]。对于VOx催化剂，在C3H8/O2进料气体中添加CO2可以通过减少表面分子氧物种来缓解过度氧化\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3\]。CO2可以部分再氧化VOx催化剂，将V2O3转化为V2O4，但不能完全再氧化至V2O5\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3\]。WOx的存在阻碍了反应过程中VOx物种的聚集，防止了非选择性氧的形成，从而降低了CO2的形成概率和丙烷的总氧化\[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]。

CO2的添加可以通过抑制表面碳的形成和允许反应通过直接氧化脱氢和非氧化脱氢后跟逆水煤气变换反应进行，来缓解VOx催化反应中的过度氧化\[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]。然而，CO2的效果强烈依赖于催化剂载体。如果碱性位点太强，CO2不可逆地吸附并毒化这些位点，增加了载体的酸性，反过来增加了焦炭的形成并降低了选择性\[10.1021/cr5002436\]。如果这些碱性位点不存在，CO2的有益效果不会被观察到\[10.1021/cr5002436\]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：Cr基和VOx催化剂在氧化脱氢过程中的失活机制是复杂的，涉及焦炭沉积、活性位点的还原和过度氧化。再生策略，特别是CO2的使用，为缓解这些问题提供了有希望的途径。然而，再生方法的有效性受到温度要求和对催化性能影响等因素的影响。在VOx催化反应中添加CO2以缓解过度氧化的潜力是一个值得进一步探索的有希望领域。未来的研究可能集中在开发对失活具有增强稳定性的催化剂，针对特定催化剂类型优化再生方法，以及探索CO2添加对催化性能的协同效应。使用先进的表征技术和计算方法可以为失活机制提供更深入的见解，并指导设计氧化脱氢过程的更坚固催化剂。
