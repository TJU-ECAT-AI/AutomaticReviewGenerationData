<English>
    The catalytic prowess of B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks for propane dehydrogenation is a burgeoning field, marked by intricate structural characteristics and catalytic advantages. These materials, characterized by their high specific surface areas and porous structures, provide numerous sites for active B species, regulating radical reaction behavior and facilitating mass transport during reactions \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3\]. The pore structure of these materials plays a pivotal role in enhancing catalytic performance, as it facilitates the diffusion of reactants and products, thereby improving the accessibility of active sites \[10.1016/j.cattod.2023.114048\].

    Aggregated boron species, particularly those hydrogen-bonded to silanols, are more active than isolated units for propane oxidative dehydrogenation (ODHP) \[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]. These aggregated BOx species with BOH groups hydrogen-bonded to SiOH groups serve as the active center for efficient ODHP catalysis. Isolated boron in zeolite frameworks, without oligomers, exhibits high activity and selectivity for ODHP, with superior durability due to the BOSiOx linkage hindering full hydrolysis and leaching in humid conditions \[10.1126/science.abe7935\]. The dynamic nature of boron sites under reaction conditions complicates the identification of active sites, necessitating the synthesis of materials with controlled boron oxide speciation to understand active site formation \[10.1002/anie.201914696\].

    The incorporation of B active components into these structures is achieved through various methods, each with its relative effectiveness for different catalytic applications. For instance, the two-step wash-coat method, where SiO2 acts as a media layer between cordierite substrate and boron oxide, facilitates the incorporation of B active components \[10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040\]. Electrospinning synthesis ensures uniform dispersion and stability of boron species on open silica fiber frameworks, enhancing catalytic performance \[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]. The synthesis of embryonic borosilicate zeolites, with abundant defective sites and large porosity, catalyzes propane oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) \[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031\].

    The effectiveness of incorporating B active components is influenced by the pore structure, B2O3 loading, and propane partial pressure, affecting the apparent propane reaction order, which varies from 1 to 3 \[10.1016/j.cattod.2023.114048\]. This suggests that specific pore structures and B2O3 loading are critical for optimizing catalytic activity and selectivity in different applications.

    ※※※
    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in the field of B-based materials for propane dehydrogenation catalysis have opened up new avenues for exploration. The intricate relationship between the structural characteristics of these materials and their catalytic performance highlights the potential for developing novel synthesis methods that can precisely control the dispersion and coordination of boron species. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of advanced characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic nature of boron sites under reaction conditions, which could lead to the design of more efficient and selective catalysts. Moreover, the exploitation of nonmetal catalysts like B2O3, with their low cost and eco-friendly properties, is a growing trend that could redefine the landscape of catalysis in hydrocarbon conversions. The synthesis of materials with controlled, uniform boron oxide speciation is necessary to understand active site formation and could pave the way for the creation of next-generation catalysts with enhanced stability and activity under high-temperature conditions.

<Chinese>
    含硼材料在丙烷脱氢催化领域的应用正迅速发展，其3-D分散活性位点在多孔框架中的结构特征和催化优势成为研究焦点。这些材料以其高比表面积和多孔结构为特征，为活性硼物种提供了大量位点，调节了自由基反应行为，促进了反应过程中的质量传递\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3\]。这些材料的孔结构在增强催化性能中扮演着关键角色，它促进了反应物和产物的扩散，从而提高了活性位点的可及性\[10.1016/j.cattod.2023.114048\]。

    聚集的硼物种，特别是与硅醇氢键结合的物种，在丙烷氧化脱氢(ODHP)中比孤立单元更活跃\[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]。这些聚集的BOx物种，带有与SiOH基团氢键结合的BOH基团，作为高效ODHP催化作用的活性中心。在没有寡聚物的沸石框架中孤立的硼表现出高活性和选择性，由于BOSiOx键在潮湿条件下阻碍了完全水解和浸出，确保了优异的耐用性\[10.1126/science.abe7935\]。反应条件下硼位点的动态性质使得活性位点的识别变得复杂，需要合成具有受控硼氧化物规格化的材料来理解活性位点的形成\[10.1002/anie.201914696\]。

    将B活性组分引入这些结构的方法多种多样，每种方法在不同的催化应用中都有其相对有效性。例如，两步洗涤-涂覆方法中，SiO2作为介于cordierite基底和硼氧化物之间的介质层，促进了B活性组分的引入\[10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040\]。电纺丝合成确保了硼物种在开放的二氧化硅纤维框架上的均匀分散和稳定性，增强了催化性能\[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3\]。合成的胚胎硼硅酸盐沸石，具有丰富的缺陷位点和大孔隙率，催化丙烷氧化脱氢(ODH)\[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031\]。

    引入B活性组分的有效性受孔结构、B2O3负载量和丙烷分压的影响，影响了表观丙烷反应级数，该级数在1到3之间变化\[10.1016/j.cattod.2023.114048\]。这表明特定的孔结构和B2O3负载量对于在不同应用中优化催化活性和选择性至关重要。

    ※※※
    综合理解与未来展望：在丙烷脱氢催化领域，含硼材料的应用进展开辟了新的探索途径。这些材料的结构特征与其催化性能之间的复杂关系凸显了开发新型合成方法的潜力，这些方法可以精确控制硼物种的分散和配位。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发先进的表征技术上，以更好地理解反应条件下硼位点的动态性质，这可能导致设计更高效、更选择性的催化剂。此外，像B2O3这样的非金属催化剂的利用，由于其低成本和环保特性，正成为一种日益增长的趋势，这可能重新定义在烃类转化中催化作用的格局。合成具有受控、均匀硼氧化物规格化的材料对于理解活性位点的形成是必要的，这可能为创建下一代催化剂铺平道路，这些催化剂在高温条件下具有增强的稳定性和活性。
