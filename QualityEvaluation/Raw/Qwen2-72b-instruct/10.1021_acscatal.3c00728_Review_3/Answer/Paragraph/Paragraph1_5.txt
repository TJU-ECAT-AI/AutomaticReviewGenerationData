    The structural characteristics of B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks significantly enhance catalytic performance in propane dehydrogenation [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017]. Aggregated boron species, particularly BOH groups hydrogen-bonded to SiOH groups, are more effective in propane CH bond activation and catalyze oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) at low temperatures [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017]. Lower crystallinity in boron-containing MFI-type zeolites enriches defect boron sites, enhancing catalytic activity and olefin productivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017]. Isolated boron in zeolite frameworks, without oligomers, shows high activity and selectivity for ODHP, with superior durability due to the BOSiOx linkage that hinders full hydrolysis and boron leaching in humid conditions [10.1126/science.abe7935]. The framework's confinement effect and the presence of hydroxyl groups are crucial for catalysis, facilitating the formation of B[OHO(H)Si]2 groups, which can reverse into SiOB linkages [10.1126/science.abe7935]. This feature provides necessary active species while preventing full hydrolysis and dissolution, enhancing durability [10.1126/science.abe7935]. The importance of hydroxyl groups for the reaction is confirmed by isotope-labeled experiments [10.1126/science.abe7935]. The zeolite microporous structure's confinement effect contributes to reaction activity, as evidenced by higher energy barriers in models without a zeolite framework [10.1126/science.abe7935]. B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites feature porous structures and high specific surface areas, offering numerous sites for B species and regulating radical reaction behavior [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]. Loading B components on 3-D porous surfaces or doping them into the framework enhances catalytic performance [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]. Examples include h-BN/cordierite monoliths with a 3-D straight-channel structure improving mass transport and catalytic efficiency [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]. SBA-15 zeolite's 3-D mesoporous structure anchors boron oxide active sites, boosting propene selectivity at low temperatures [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]. B2O3/SiO2@cordierite honeycomb catalysts achieve high B loading and excellent olefin selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]. Zeolites with B species doped into their frameworks exhibit high activity and selectivity for propane oxidative dehydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]. 3-D ordered macroporous boron phosphate, synthesized via a hard template method, has rich pores and a high surface area, enhancing molecular diffusion and heat transfer [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]. 3-D h-BN bowl foam, with a large surface area and numerous mesopores, shows excellent activity for propane oxidative dehydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3].

    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks for propane dehydrogenation catalysis open up new avenues for exploration. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel synthesis methods that can precisely control the dispersion and spatial confinement of boron sites, aiming to optimize catalytic activity and selectivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017, 10.1126/science.abe7935]. The exploitation of nonmetal catalysts, like B2O3, is a growing trend due to their low cost and eco-friendly properties [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028]. The synthesis of B-based materials with bowl-like structures, offering large surface areas and porous frameworks, could be further optimized for enhanced catalytic performance in environmental remediation and energy storage applications [10.1016/j.apsusc.2023.156743]. The investigation of the dynamic nature of boron sites under reaction conditions and the identification of active sites in B-based materials could lead to the design of more efficient catalysts for propane dehydrogenation [10.1002/anie.201914696]. The exploration of innovative incorporation methods for B active components, considering the specific pore structure and B2O3 loading, could significantly affect catalytic activity and selectivity for different applications [10.1016/j.cattod.2023.114048]. The development of B-based materials with enhanced mass transport and increased accessibility of active sites, such as ordered macroporous boron phosphate (BPO4), could lead to improved catalytic efficiency under high space velocity conditions [10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3]. The exploitation of B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks for various catalytic applications, including h-BN, boron carbide, boron oxide, and boron phosphate, could pave the way for the design of more efficient and selective catalysts [10.1039/D0CS01174F].

    B基材料在多孔框架中具有3-D分散活性位点的结构特性在丙烷脱氢中显著提高了催化性能 [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017]。特别是氢键合到SiOH基团的BOH基团的聚集硼物种在丙烷CH键活化中更有效，并在低温下催化丙烷氧化脱氢(ODHP) [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017]。在含硼MFI型沸石中，较低的结晶度丰富了缺陷硼位点，提高了催化活性和烯烃产量 [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017]。在没有寡聚物的沸石框架中孤立的硼显示出高活性和选择性用于ODHP，由于BOSiOx键在潮湿条件下防止完全水解和硼的浸出，具有优越的耐用性 [10.1126/science.abe7935]。框架的限制效应和羟基的存在对于催化至关重要，促进形成可逆为SiOB键的B[OHO(H)Si]2基团 [10.1126/science.abe7935]。这一特性在防止完全水解和溶解的同时提供了必要的活性物种，从而增强了耐用性 [10.1126/science.abe7935]。通过同位素标记实验确认了羟基对于反应的重要性 [10.1126/science.abe7935]。沸石微孔结构的限制效应通过没有沸石框架的模型中更高的能量壁垒证明了对反应活性的贡献 [10.1126/science.abe7935]。B基材料具有3-D分散活性位点，具有多孔结构和高比表面积，为B物种提供了大量位点并调节了自由基反应行为 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]。在3-D多孔表面上加载B组分或将其掺入框架中增强了催化性能 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]。例如，具有3-D直通道结构的h-BN/堇青石单体改善了质量传递和催化效率 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]。SBA-15沸石的3-D介孔结构锚定了硼氧化物活性位点，在低温下提高了丙烯选择性 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]。B2O3/SiO2@堇青石蜂窝催化剂实现了高B负载和优异的烯烃选择性 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]。在框架中掺入B物种的沸石在丙烷氧化脱氢中表现出高活性和选择性 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]。通过硬模板方法合成的3-D有序大孔硼酸盐磷酸盐具有丰富的孔隙和高比表面积，增强了分子扩散和热传递 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]。具有大比表面积和大量介孔的3-D h-BN碗泡沫在丙烷氧化脱氢中表现出优异的活性 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]。

    综合理解与未来展望：B基材料在多孔框架中具有3-D分散活性位点的丙烷脱氢催化作用的进展开辟了新的探索途径。未来的研究工作可能集中在开发可以精确控制硼位点的分散和空间限制的新合成方法，旨在优化催化活性和选择性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017, 10.1126/science.abe7935]。利用像B2O3这样的非金属催化剂的趋势正在增长，因为它们成本低且环保 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028]。具有大表面积和多孔框架的B基材料的碗状结构的合成可以进一步优化，以增强在环境修复和能源存储应用中的催化性能 [10.1016/j.apsusc.2023.156743]。对反应条件下硼位点的动态性质的调查以及B基材料中活性位点的识别可能导致设计更有效的丙烷脱氢催化剂 [10.1002/anie.201914696]。考虑特定孔结构和B2O3负载的B活性组分的创新掺入方法的探索可能对不同应用的催化活性和选择性产生重大影响 [10.1016/j.cattod.2023.114048]。具有增强质量传递和活性位点可及性增加的B基材料的开发，例如有序大孔硼酸盐磷酸盐(BPO4)，可能导致在高空间速度条件下提高催化效率 [10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3]。具有3-D分散活性位点的多孔框架的B基材料在各种催化应用中的开发，包括h-BN、硼化物、硼氧化物和硼酸盐磷酸盐，可能为设计更高效和选择性的催化剂铺平道路 [10.1039/D0CS01174F]。
