                    The structural characteristics of B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks significantly enhance catalytic performance in propane dehydrogenation applications \[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]. Aggregated boron species, particularly defect sites hydrogen-bonded to silanols, are more active than isolated units for propane oxidative dehydrogenation (ODHP) \[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]. Low crystallinity in boron-containing MFI-type zeolites enriches these defect sites, enhancing catalytic activity and olefin productivity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]. Isolated boron in zeolite frameworks without oligomers shows high activity and selectivity for ODHP, with superior durability due to the BOSiOx linkage \[10.1126/science.abe7935\]. The 2D 11B1H HETCOR spectrum reveals spatial proximities between B and H species, while FTIR identifies framework boron species \[10.1126/science.abe7935\]. NMR and IR results suggest hydrolysis forms B[OHO(H)Si]2 groups, boosting catalysis, with hydroxyl groups crucial for the reaction \[10.1126/science.abe7935\]. The confinement effect of the zeolite microporous structure enhances reaction activity \[10.1126/science.abe7935\]. B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites exhibit porous structures and high specific surface areas, providing numerous sites for B species and regulating radical reaction behavior \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3\]. Loading B components on surfaces or doping them into porous frameworks creates effective 3-D B active sites \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3\]. Examples include h-BN/cordierite monoliths with a 3-D straight-channel structure for gas-phase radical reactions, achieving high propane conversion and propene selectivity \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3\]. SBA-15 zeolite's 3-D mesoporous structure anchors boron oxide active sites, boosting low-temperature activity and efficiency in catalyzing light alkanes \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3\]. B2O3/SiO2@cordierite honeycomb ceramic catalysts allow high B loading, improving olefin selectivity and minimizing CO2 production \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3\]. Zeolite frameworks doped with B species create 3-D dispersion, enhancing activity and selectivity for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3\]. 3-D ordered macroporous boron phosphate, synthesized using a hard template method, has rich pores and high surface area, improving molecular diffusion and heat transfer \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3\]. 3-D h-BN bowl foam, with a large surface area and mesopores, shows excellent activity for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in B-based materials for propane dehydrogenation catalysis, grounded in the structural characteristics and catalytic advantages of 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks, open up new avenues for research and innovation. Future directions might focus on the development of novel synthesis methods that enhance the dispersion and stability of B active sites, leading to improved catalytic performance and durability. Additionally, the exploration of hybrid materials combining B-based components with other metal or non-metal elements could offer synergistic effects, further boosting catalytic efficiency. The optimization of pore structures and loading methods for B components will be crucial for tailoring catalysts to specific applications, such as the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane. Moreover, the investigation of the dynamic behavior of boron sites under reaction conditions could provide insights into the active site formation and stability, guiding the design of more effective catalysts. Lastly, the integration of computational modeling and experimental studies could accelerate the discovery of new B-based catalysts with enhanced catalytic properties, paving the way for more sustainable and efficient propane dehydrogenation processes.

                    具有3-D分散活性位点的B基材料的结构特性在丙烷脱氢应用中显著提高了催化性能\[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]。特别是与硅醇氢键结合的缺陷硼物种在丙烷氧化脱氢(ODHP)中比孤立单元更活跃\[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]。含硼MFI型沸石的低结晶度丰富了这些缺陷位点，提高了催化活性和烯烃产量\[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017\]。无寡聚物的沸石框架中的孤立硼表现出高活性和选择性，由于BOSiOx连接而具有优异的耐用性\[10.1126/science.abe7935\]。2D 11B1H HETCOR光谱揭示了B和H物种之间的空间邻近性，而FTIR识别了框架硼物种\[10.1126/science.abe7935\]。NMR和IR结果表明，水解形成B[OHO(H)Si]2组，提高了催化作用，而羟基对反应至关重要\[10.1126/science.abe7935\]。沸石微孔结构的限制效应增强了反应活性\[10.1126/science.abe7935\]。具有3-D分散活性位点的B基材料表现出多孔结构和高比表面积，为B物种提供了大量位点，并调节了自由基反应行为\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3\]。在表面加载B组分或将其掺入多孔框架中创建了有效的3-D B活性位点\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3\]。例如，h-BN/堇青石单体具有3-D直通道结构，用于气相自由基反应，实现了高丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3\]。SBA-15沸石的3-D介孔结构锚定了氧化硼活性位点，提高了催化轻烃的低温活性和效率\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3\]。B2O3/SiO2@堇青石蜂窝陶瓷催化剂允许高B负载，提高了烯烃选择性并最小化了CO2生产\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3\]。掺杂B物种的沸石框架创建了3-D分散，增强了丙烷氧化脱氢的活性和选择性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3\]。使用硬模板方法合成的3-D有序大孔硼酸盐具有丰富的孔隙和高表面积，改善了分子扩散和热传递\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3\]。3-D h-BN碗泡沫具有大表面积和介孔，对丙烷氧化脱氢表现出优异的活性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望：基于3-D分散活性位点在多孔框架中的结构特性和催化优势的B基材料在丙烷脱氢催化方面的进步，为研究和创新开辟了新的途径。未来的研究方向可能集中在开发增强B活性位点分散性和稳定性的新型合成方法，从而提高催化性能和耐用性。此外，探索结合B基组分与其他金属或非金属元素的杂化材料可能提供协同效应，进一步提高催化效率。优化孔结构和B组分的加载方法对于针对特定应用(如丙烷氧化脱氢)定制催化剂至关重要。此外，研究硼位点在反应条件下的动态行为可能提供有关活性位点形成和稳定性的见解，指导更有效催化剂的设计。最后，计算建模与实验研究的结合可以加速具有增强催化性能的新B基催化剂的发现，为更可持续和高效的丙烷脱氢过程铺平道路。
