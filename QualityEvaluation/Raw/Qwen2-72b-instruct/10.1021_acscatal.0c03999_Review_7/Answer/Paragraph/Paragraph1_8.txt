        <English> 
                Fe oxide-based catalysts have emerged as promising candidates for CO2-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (CO2-ODHP) due to their ability to catalyze the oxidative dehydrogenation of hydrocarbons, as evidenced in reference \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7\]. Among the various catalysts tested, Fe2O3 supported on activated carbon (Fe2O3/AC) has demonstrated superior performance in terms of C3H8 conversion and C3H6 yield, with comparable C3H6 selectivity to bare Fe2O3 \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7\]. The enhanced reducibility of Fe2O3 on AC, as confirmed by H2-TPR results, is a pivotal factor in its high catalytic performance \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7\]. This is in stark contrast to Fe2O3 supported on γ-Al2O3 and hydrotalcite-derived FeMg catalysts, where the strong interaction between Fe oxide and the support significantly hinders reducibility \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7\]. The redox cycle of Fe oxide, involving the oxidation of C3H8 to C3H6 on lattice oxygen from Fe2O3 or Fe3O4 and the subsequent reoxidation of reduced Fe oxide by CO2, is proposed to play a crucial role in CO2-ODHP \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The findings from the study \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7\] underscore the pivotal role of support materials in enhancing the reducibility and catalytic activity of Fe oxide-based catalysts for CO2-ODHP. The superior performance of Fe2O3/AC, attributed to its enhanced reducibility, opens up new avenues for exploring the influence of different support materials on the redox properties of Fe oxide catalysts. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel support materials that can further improve the reducibility and stability of Fe oxide catalysts, potentially leading to higher catalytic activity and selectivity in CO2-ODHP. Additionally, the elucidation of the detailed reaction mechanism involving the redox cycle of Fe oxide could pave the way for the design of more efficient catalysts for CO2-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation reactions.

                基于\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7\]的参考文献，Fe氧化物基催化剂因其催化氧化脱氢烃类的能力，如乙苯和异丁烷，而成为CO2辅助丙烷氧化脱氢（CO2-ODHP）的潜在候选者。在测试的催化剂中，Fe2O3/AC（Fe2O3负载于活性炭上）在C3H8转化率和C3H6产率方面表现出色，与裸Fe2O3的C3H6选择性相当\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7\]。H2-TPR结果证实，Fe2O3在AC上的增强还原性是其高催化性能的关键因素\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7\]。这与Fe2O3负载于γ-Al2O3和水滑石衍生的FeMg催化剂形成鲜明对比，后者由于Fe氧化物与载体之间的强相互作用，还原性受到显著抑制\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7\]。Fe氧化物的氧化还原循环，涉及C3H8在Fe2O3或Fe3O4的晶格氧上的氧化以及随后由CO2还原的Fe氧化物的再氧化，被提议在CO2-ODHP中起着关键作用\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: 研究\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7\]的结果突出了载体材料在提高Fe氧化物基催化剂的还原性和催化活性中的关键作用，特别是在CO2-ODHP中。Fe2O3/AC的卓越性能，归因于其增强的还原性，为探索不同载体材料对Fe氧化物催化剂的氧化还原性质的影响开辟了新的途径。未来的研究工作可能集中在开发新型载体材料，以进一步提高Fe氧化物催化剂的还原性和稳定性，可能导致CO2-ODHP中更高的催化活性和选择性。此外，阐明涉及Fe氧化物氧化还原循环的详细反应机制，可能为设计更有效的CO2辅助氧化脱氢反应催化剂铺平道路。
