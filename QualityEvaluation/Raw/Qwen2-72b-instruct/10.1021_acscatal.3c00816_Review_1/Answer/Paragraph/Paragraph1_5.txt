                    The application, structure, and performance of Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts in Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions have been extensively studied \[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014\], \[10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005\], \[10.1039/D2NR02208G\], \[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022\], \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1\]. MCM-41 and SBA-15 supported VOx and CrOx catalysts exhibit superior performance compared to traditional metal oxide supports due to their unique structural properties \[10.1039/D2NR02208G\]. Surface acidity influences coke formation, with higher acidity leading to increased coke \[10.1039/D2NR02208G\]. Surface OH on Al2O3 affects VOx species and interaction, crucial for catalytic behavior \[10.1039/D2NR02208G\]. The SMSI effect between support and active center is vital for high conversion and selectivity \[10.1039/D2NR02208G\]. Transition metals improve Pt atom electronic structure, enhancing catalytic performance \[10.1039/D2NR02208G\]. Zn anchoring enhances electron interaction and dispersion \[10.1039/D2NR02208G\]. Sulfur addition promotes C3H6 desorption and anti-coking \[10.1039/D2NR02208G\]. Na-doping regulates Brnsted sites and Cr components, decreasing propane adsorption and CH bond activation \[10.1039/D2NR02208G\]. The 5%Cr/Z-5(260) catalyst demonstrates high propane conversion and propylene selectivity, showcasing the potential of OMS catalysts in PDH reactions \[10.1039/D2NR02208G\]. VOx/MCM-41 catalysts exhibit good onstream stability and high selectivity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022\]. Isolated VOx species on MCM-41 show higher intrinsic activity than oligomerized counterparts, impacting catalyst performance \[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022\]. The literature lacks direct comparison with traditional metal oxides and specific discussion on lattice oxygen species and H2 pretreatment effects on catalyst performance and deactivation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022\]. OMS, synthesized via self-assembly, supports PDH reactions, offering low acidity, high surface area, and ordered pores, enhancing reactive site dispersion and coking resistance \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1\]. VOx and CrOx on MCM-41/SBA-15 show high propylene productivity but rapid deactivation \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1\]. Lattice oxygen species participate in PDH, detectable as CO, eliminated by H2 pretreatment \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1\]. As-prepared VOx/MCM-41 and CrOx/MCM-41 outperform prereduced versions in propylene productivity but have lower stability, with oxidized species more active than reduced ones \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1\]. VOx/MCM-41 exhibits superior catalytic activity and selectivity over CrOx/MCM-41 \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1\].
