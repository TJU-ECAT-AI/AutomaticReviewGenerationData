Answer: The literature does not provide a detailed comparison of the mechanisms of C-H bond activation and catalyst regeneration in oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes for redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts. It does not discuss the role of traditional oxidants and CO2 in this context, nor does it evaluate the effectiveness of different catalysts such as V2O5, VOx/SiO2, CeO2, and transition metal clusters in activating CO2 for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane. The literature focuses on the dehydrogenation of light alkanes over oxidized diamond-supported catalysts in the presence of carbon dioxide, observing that CO2 can promote the dehydrogenation of propane and butanes over Cr2O3 and V2O5 catalysts by maintaining a higher oxidation state of the metal species.
