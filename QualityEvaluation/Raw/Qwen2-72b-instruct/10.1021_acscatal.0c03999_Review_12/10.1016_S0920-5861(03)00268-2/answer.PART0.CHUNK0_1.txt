1.The literature does not provide a direct comparison of mechanisms for C-H bond activation and catalyst regeneration in oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes for redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts. However, it does discuss the role of CO2 in maintaining a higher oxidation state of chromium and vanadium species on oxidized diamond-supported Cr2O3 and V2O5 catalysts during the dehydrogenation of lower alkanes. The effectiveness of different catalysts in activating CO2 for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane is not directly addressed, but the oxidized diamond-supported V2O5 catalyst is noted to have high catalytic activities for the dehydrogenation of C3 and C4 alkanes.
