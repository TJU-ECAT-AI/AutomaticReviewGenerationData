However, it does discuss the role of different oxidants, including traditional oxidants like O2 and soft oxidants like CO2 and N2O. The literature suggests that CO2 and N2O limit the formation of electrophilic oxygen species on the catalyst surface, which can promote the oxidation of alkenes to carbon oxides, thereby increasing the selectivity towards butadiene. The literature also indicates that the use of soft oxidants like CO2 and N2O facilitates easier migration of lattice oxygen from bulk to surface to complete the oxidation process.
