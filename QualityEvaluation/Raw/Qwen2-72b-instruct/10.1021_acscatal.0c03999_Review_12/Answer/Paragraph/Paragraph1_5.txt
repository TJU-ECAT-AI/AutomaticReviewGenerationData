                    In oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes, CO2 acts as an oxidant, regenerating redox-active metal oxide catalysts like Cr2O3 and V2O5 by maintaining their higher oxidation states \[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2\]. Redox-active catalysts such as V2O5 and VOx/SiO2 employ the Mars-van Krevelen mechanism, donating oxygen to the alkane to form alkenes and water, with CO2 regenerating the catalyst by refilling lattice oxygen vacancies \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9\]. Redox-inactive catalysts, such as Ga2O3/MxOy, activate CO2 through heterolytic dissociation into CO and *O, which dehydrogenates alkanes, but can lead to cracking and catalyst deactivation \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9\]. Effective CO2 activation for propane dehydrogenation is observed in V2O5, VOx/SiO2, CeO2, and transition metal clusters, with mechanisms involving CO2 activation by the catalyst to form CO and *O \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9\]. Energy barriers for CO2 dissociation vary, with redox-active catalysts typically having lower barriers \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The literature highlights the effectiveness of redox-active metal oxide catalysts, particularly V2O5 and VOx/SiO2, in oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes using CO2 as an oxidant. These catalysts maintain higher oxidation states and regenerate through CO2 reduction, making them promising for industrial applications. Redox-inactive catalysts, while less effective, offer alternative pathways for CO2 activation. Future research might focus on enhancing the CO2 activation efficiency of redox-inactive catalysts, exploring the role of transition metal clusters in CO2 dissociation, and developing bifunctional catalysts that can simultaneously activate CO2 and dehydrogenate alkanes. Additionally, understanding the detailed mechanisms of CO2 dissociation on various catalysts could lead to the design of more efficient catalysts for oxidative dehydrogenation processes.

                    在烷烃氧化脱氢中，CO2作为氧化剂，通过维持Cr2O3和V2O5等还原活性金属氧化物催化剂的高氧化态来再生催化剂\[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2\]。还原活性催化剂如V2O5和VOx/SiO2采用Mars-van Krevelen机制，向烷烃供氧形成烯烃和水，CO2通过填充晶格氧空位来再生催化剂\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9\]。还原非活性催化剂，如Ga2O3/MxOy，通过异裂解离CO2为CO和*O，脱氢烷烃，但可能导致裂解和催化剂失活\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9\]。V2O5，VOx/SiO2，CeO2和过渡金属簇在丙烷脱氢中对CO2的有效激活，机制涉及催化剂激活CO2形成CO和*O\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9\]。CO2解离的能量势垒因催化剂而异，还原活性催化剂通常具有较低的势垒\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 文献强调了还原活性金属氧化物催化剂，特别是V2O5和VOx/SiO2，在使用CO2作为氧化剂的烷烃氧化脱氢中的有效性。这些催化剂维持高氧化态并通过CO2还原再生，使其在工业应用中具有前景。还原非活性催化剂，虽然效果较差，但提供了CO2激活的替代途径。未来的研究可能集中在提高还原非活性催化剂的CO2激活效率，探索过渡金属簇在CO2解离中的作用，以及开发能够同时激活CO2和脱氢烷烃的双功能催化剂。此外，理解各种催化剂上CO2解离的详细机制可能有助于设计更有效的氧化脱氢过程催化剂。
