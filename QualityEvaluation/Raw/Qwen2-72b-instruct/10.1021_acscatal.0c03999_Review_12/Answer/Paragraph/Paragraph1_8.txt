<English>
The oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes, particularly propane, is a critical process for the production of light olefins, with CO2 emerging as an alternative and milder oxidant compared to traditional oxidants like O2 \[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2\]. Redox-active metal oxide catalysts, such as V2O5 and VOx/SiO2, employ the Mars-van Krevelen (MvK) mechanism for C-H bond activation, donating oxygen to the alkane to form alkenes and water, with CO2 regenerating the catalyst by refilling lattice oxygen vacancies \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9\]. Redox-inactive catalysts, like Ga2O3/MxOy, activate CO2 through heterolytic dissociation into CO and *O, which dehydrogenates alkanes but can lead to cracking and coke formation \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9\]. CO2, as a milder oxidant, avoids over-oxidation, making it preferable to O2 \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9\].

V2O5, VOx/SiO2, CeO2, and transition metal clusters are effective for CO2 activation due to their redox properties and oxygen mobility, facilitating CO2 dissociation with varying energy barriers \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9\]. Among these, V2O5 exhibits high catalytic activities for dehydrogenation of C3 and C4 alkanes, highlighting its effectiveness in CO2 activation mechanisms \[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2\]. The CO2-ODH reaction necessitates a bifunctional catalyst that dissociates CO2 into CO and *O, with *O dehydrogenating the alkane \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9\].

In redox-active oxides, the MvK mechanism forms hydroxide and abstracts hydrogen, leaving an oxygen vacancy that requires an oxidant for regeneration \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12\]. Redox-inactive oxides directly reduce the oxidant without forming a vacancy \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12\]. CO2 as an oxidant can either dissociate directly or be hydrogenated before CO formation \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12\]. V2O5 has a high barrier for CO2 oxidation, VOx/SiO2 has a lower but still significant barrier, while CeO2 facilitates easier CO2 dissociation due to oxygen vacancies \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12\].

DFT calculations confirm that CO2-assisted propane dehydrogenation can proceed via direct oxidative dehydrogenation and non-oxidative dehydrogenation followed by the reverse water gas shift (RWGS) reaction \[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]. The activation of the secondary CAH bond of propane is the rate-limiting step, while the re-oxidation of the catalyst with CO2 occurs on a faster timescale \[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]. CO2 suppresses surface carbon formation, enhancing catalyst stability and activity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\].

Redox-active metal oxides like V2O5 and VOx/SiO2 activate C-H bonds through redox cycles, with CO2 partially oxidizing the catalyst from V2O3 to V2O4, participating in propane's conversion to propylene \[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]. Redox-inactive catalysts, such as CeO2 and transition metal clusters, have less effective CO2 activation due to higher energy barriers for CO2 dissociation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The role of CO2 as an oxidant in oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes, particularly over redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts, offers a promising avenue for the sustainable production of light olefins. The effectiveness of catalysts like V2O5, VOx/SiO2, CeO2, and transition metal clusters in activating CO2 for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane is contingent upon their redox properties and oxygen mobility, with varying energy barriers for CO2 dissociation. The bifunctional nature of the catalyst, capable of dissociating CO2 into CO and *O, is crucial for the dehydrogenation of alkanes. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel catalysts with enhanced redox properties and oxygen mobility, aiming to lower the energy barriers for CO2 dissociation. Additionally, the integration of CO2 activation with the RWGS reaction could potentially improve equilibrium conversion toward propylene production, offering a more sustainable and efficient route for light olefin synthesis.

<Chinese>
在烷烃的氧化脱氢反应中，尤其是丙烷，CO2作为一种温和的氧化剂，与传统的氧化剂如O2相比，显示出其独特的优势\[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2\]。红氧化金属氧化物催化剂，如V2O5和VOx/SiO2，通过Mars-van Krevelen（MvK）机制激活C-H键，向烷烃供氧形成烯烃和水，CO2通过填充晶格氧空位来再生催化剂\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9\]。非红氧化催化剂，如Ga2O3/MxOy，通过异裂CO2生成CO和*O，脱氢烷烃，但可能导致裂解和焦炭形成\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9\]。CO2作为一种温和的氧化剂，避免了过度氧化，优于O2\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9\]。

V2O5，VOx/SiO2，CeO2和过渡金属簇由于其红氧化性质和氧流动性，对CO2激活有效，促进CO2的解离，具有不同的能量障碍\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9\]。其中，V2O5对C3和C4烷烃的脱氢表现出高催化活性，突显了其在CO2激活机制中的有效性\[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2\]。CO2-ODH反应需要一种双功能催化剂，将CO2解离为CO和*O，其中*O脱氢烷烃\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9\]。

在红氧化氧化物中，MvK机制形成羟基并抽氢，留下一个需要氧化剂再生的氧空位\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12\]。非红氧化氧化物直接还原氧化剂而不形成空位\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12\]。CO2作为氧化剂，可以直接解离或在CO形成前被氢化\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12\]。V2O5对CO2氧化有高障碍，VOx/SiO2有较低但仍然显著的障碍，而CeO2由于氧空位而促进CO2更容易的解离\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12\]。

DFT计算证实，CO2辅助的丙烷脱氢可以通过直接氧化脱氢和非氧化脱氢后跟逆水煤气变换（RWGS）反应进行\[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]。丙烷的二级CAH键的激活是速率限制步骤，而催化剂与CO2的再氧化发生在更快的时间尺度上\[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]。CO2抑制表面碳的形成，增强催化剂的稳定性和活性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]。

红氧化金属氧化物如V2O5和VOx/SiO2通过红氧化循环激活C-H键，CO2部分氧化催化剂从V2O3到V2O4，参与丙烷转化为丙烯\[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]。非红氧化催化剂，如CeO2和过渡金属簇，由于CO2解离的高能量障碍，对CO2激活效果较差\[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015\]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：CO2作为氧化剂在烷烃氧化脱氢反应中的作用，特别是在红氧化和非红氧化金属氧化物催化剂上，为轻烯烃的可持续生产提供了一个有希望的途径。V2O5，VOx/SiO2，CeO2和过渡金属簇等催化剂在激活CO2进行丙烷氧化脱氢方面的有效性取决于它们的红氧化性质和氧流动性，具有不同的CO2解离能量障碍。催化剂的双功能性质，能够将CO2解离为CO和*O，对于烷烃的脱氢至关重要。未来的研究工作可能集中在开发具有增强红氧化性质和氧流动性的新型催化剂，旨在降低CO2解离的能量障碍。此外，将CO2激活与RWGS反应的整合可能潜在地提高向丙烯生产的平衡转化，为轻烯烃合成提供更可持续和有效的途径。
