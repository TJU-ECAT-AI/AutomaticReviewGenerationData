        <English> 
                The utilization of promoters in chromium-based catalytic systems for propane dehydrogenation is pivotal in addressing the inherent limitations of CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness \[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]. These promoters enhance the oxidizing ability of CO2, which is essential for the reoxidation of reduced CrOx species. Alternative strategies, such as O2 co-feeding, can decrease the deactivation rate of CrOx/silica catalysts, but they significantly decrease the selectivity of the reaction for C3H6 \[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]. Promoters, particularly Ni-based catalysts, offer a distinct advantage by activating CO2 under harsh reaction conditions, lowering the activation energy for CO2 dissociation, and generating activated oxygen (O*ads) that can reoxidize reduced CrOx species without compromising the selectivity and stability of the catalytic performance \[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]. This strategy overcomes the limitations of CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness, offering an alternative to O2 co-feeding which can result in a drop in selectivity \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in promoter technology for chromium-based catalytic systems have opened up new avenues for enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability. Future research should focus on the development of novel promoters that can further lower the activation energy for CO2 dissociation while maintaining high selectivity and stability. Additionally, the exploration of synergistic effects between different promoter elements could lead to the design of more efficient and selective catalysts for propane dehydrogenation. The integration of computational methods, such as ab initio calculations, with experimental studies will be crucial in understanding the fundamental mechanisms of CO2 activation and the role of promoters in reoxidation processes. This multidisciplinary approach will facilitate the discovery of innovative catalysts that can operate under harsh conditions, thereby pushing the boundaries of catalytic performance in propane dehydrogenation.

                在丙烷脱氢反应中，铬基催化体系中促进剂的应用对于解决CO2氧化潜力弱和惰性的问题至关重要\[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]。这些促进剂增强了CO2的氧化能力，这对于还原CrOx物种的再氧化至关重要。替代策略，如O2共喂，可以降低CrOx/二氧化硅催化剂的失活速率，但会显著降低C3H6反应的选择性\[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]。特别是基于Ni的催化剂作为促进剂，提供了一个独特的优势，即在恶劣的反应条件下激活CO2，降低CO2解离的活化能，并生成可再氧化还原CrOx物种的活化氧（O*ads），同时不损害催化性能的选择性和稳定性\[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]。这一策略克服了CO2氧化潜力弱和惰性的限制，为O2共喂提供了一个替代方案，后者可能导致选择性下降\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: 铬基催化体系中促进剂技术的进步为增强CO2激活和氧化能力开辟了新的途径。未来的研究应集中在开发新型促进剂上，这些促进剂可以进一步降低CO2解离的活化能，同时保持高选择性和稳定性。此外，探索不同促进剂元素之间的协同效应，可能会导致设计出更高效、更选择性的丙烷脱氢催化剂。将计算方法，如ab initio计算，与实验研究相结合，对于理解CO2激活的基本机制和促进剂在再氧化过程中的作用至关重要。这种跨学科的方法将促进创新催化剂的发现，这些催化剂可以在恶劣条件下运行，从而推动丙烷脱氢催化性能的边界。
