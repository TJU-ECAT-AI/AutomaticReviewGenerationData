        <English> 
                The utilization of promoters in chromium-based catalytic systems for propane dehydrogenation is pivotal in addressing the inherent limitations of CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness \[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]. These promoters significantly enhance the oxidizing ability of CO2, which is essential for the reoxidation of reduced CrOx species. Alternative strategies, such as the co-feeding of O2, can decrease the deactivation rate of CrOx/silica catalysts, but they come with the drawback of significantly decreasing the selectivity of the reaction for C3H6 \[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]. In contrast, promoters, particularly Ni-based catalysts, offer the advantage of activating CO2 under harsh reaction conditions, lowering the activation energy for CO2 dissociation, and generating activated oxygen (O*ads) that can reoxidize reduced CrOx species without compromising the selectivity and stability of the catalytic performance \[10.1002/cctc.201200397\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The introduction of promoters, especially Ni-based catalysts, in chromium-based catalytic systems for propane dehydrogenation presents a promising avenue for enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability. This strategy not only overcomes the limitations of CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness but also maintains stable selectivity for propylene. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel promoters that can further improve the catalytic performance under even more challenging conditions, such as higher temperatures and pressures. Additionally, the exploration of synergistic effects between different promoter elements could lead to the design of more efficient and selective catalysts for propane dehydrogenation. The integration of computational methods, such as ab initio calculations, could provide deeper insights into the mechanisms of CO2 activation and the role of promoters in the reoxidation of CrOx species, guiding the rational design of catalysts.

                在丙烷脱氢反应中，铬基催化体系中使用促进剂对于解决CO2氧化潜力弱和惰性的固有限制至关重要\[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]。这些促进剂显著增强了CO2的氧化能力，这对于还原CrOx物种的再氧化至关重要。替代策略，如O2的共喂入，可以降低CrOx/二氧化硅催化剂的失活速率，但会显著降低C3H6反应的选择性\[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]。相比之下，促进剂，特别是Ni基催化剂，具有在苛刻反应条件下激活CO2、降低CO2解离的活化能、生成可再氧化还原CrOx物种的活化氧（O*ads）而不损害催化性能的选择性和稳定性等优点\[10.1002/cctc.201200397\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: 在丙烷脱氢反应中，铬基催化体系中引入促进剂，尤其是Ni基催化剂，为增强CO2的活化和氧化能力提供了一条有希望的途径。这一策略不仅克服了CO2氧化潜力弱和惰性的限制，而且保持了对丙烯的稳定选择性。未来的研究工作可能集中在开发新型促进剂上，这些促进剂可以在更具有挑战性的条件下，如更高的温度和压力下，进一步提高催化性能。此外，探索不同促进剂元素之间的协同效应，可以引导设计更高效、更选择性的丙烷脱氢催化剂。将计算方法，如ab initio计算，与实验研究相结合，可以提供对CO2活化机制和促进剂在CrOx物种再氧化中的作用的更深入理解，指导催化剂的合理设计。
