                    The recent surge in shale gas production has significantly impacted the economics of natural gas, making it more competitive than crude oil for olefin production \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. This economic shift has bolstered the competitiveness of alternative olefin production methods, such as dehydrogenation and MTO, relative to traditional methods like fluid catalytic cracking and steam cracking. Advanced CrOx/Al2O3 (Catofin) and PtSn/Al2O3 (Oleflex) catalysts have been developed to a point where dehydrogenation reactions can run continuously for several years \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. However, current dehydrogenation technologies still require improvement in noble metal loading, catalyst stability, and energy efficiency \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Alternative catalysts, while promising, deactivate too quickly due to coke deposition and the inability to fully recover activity through regeneration \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Oxidative dehydrogenation, which avoids thermodynamic limitations of nonoxidative methods, requires finding the right catalyst and oxidizing agent \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. The development of an effective oxidative dehydrogenation system hinges on identifying the optimal combination of catalyst material and oxidizing agent \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Cofeeding CO2 may lead to additional coke deposition due to the reverse watergas shift reaction \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Solid oxygen carriers offer an alternative solution for selectively oxidizing hydrogen in the SHC reaction \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Membranes can be introduced to the reactor system to remove hydrogen and shift the equilibrium toward olefin production \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Catalyst deactivation, exacerbated by coke deposition when hydrogen partial pressure drops, represents a significant challenge \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. The interplay between catalyst deactivation and regeneration is crucial in industrial settings \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Operando spectroscopy is anticipated to provide more sophisticated process control in the near future \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. UVvis spectroscopy can monitor long-term irreversible catalyst deactivation \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. The demand for olefins is expected to remain high due to their widespread use as feedstock in the chemical industry \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Dozens of new PDH projects have been announced in recent years, utilizing advanced CrOx/Al2O3 or PtSn/Al2O3 catalysts that allow continuous operation for several years \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. The reactions face challenges such as limited equilibrium conversion and side reactions at high temperatures \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Alternative catalysts like GaOx, MoOx, and VOx have been explored but have not yet become practical alternatives due to rapid deactivation and irrecoverable activity after regeneration \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Further research is needed to address these issues \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\].
