Solid oxygen carriers, such as CeO2, SbO4, In2O3, WO3, PbOx, Tl2O3, and BiO3, offer an alternative solution for selectively oxidizing hydrogen in the SHC reaction \[10.1021/cr5002436\_7\]. Membranes can be introduced to the reactor system to remove hydrogen and shift the equilibrium toward olefin production \[10.1021/cr5002436\_7\]. Catalyst deactivation, exacerbated by coke deposition when hydrogen partial pressure drops, represents a significant challenge in industrial settings \[10.1021/cr5002436\_7\]. Operando spectroscopy is anticipated to provide more sophisticated process control in the near future, while UVvis spectroscopy can monitor long-term irreversible catalyst deactivation \[10.1021/cr5002436\_7\]. The demand for olefins is expected to remain high due to their widespread use as feedstock in the chemical industry, with dozens of new PDH projects announced in recent years \[10.1021/cr5002436\_7\].
