                The recent surge in shale gas production has significantly impacted the economics of natural gas, making it more competitive than crude oil for olefin production \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. This economic shift has bolstered the competitiveness of alternative olefin production methods, such as dehydrogenation and MTO, relative to traditional methods like fluid catalytic cracking and steam cracking. Advanced CrOx/Al2O3 (Catofin) and PtSn/Al2O3 (Oleflex) catalysts have been developed to a point where dehydrogenation reactions can be sustained for several years \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. However, current dehydrogenation technologies still require improvements in noble metal loading, catalyst stability, and energy efficiency \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Alternative catalysts, while promising, deactivate too quickly due to coke deposition and the inability to fully recover activity through regeneration \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Oxidative dehydrogenation, which avoids thermodynamic limitations of nonoxidative methods, requires the development of suitable catalysts and oxidizing agents \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Cofeeding CO2 may lead to additional coke deposition due to the reverse watergas shift reaction \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Solid oxygen carriers offer an alternative solution for selectively oxidizing hydrogen in the SHC reaction \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Membranes can be introduced to the reactor system to remove hydrogen and shift the equilibrium toward olefin production \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Catalyst deactivation, exacerbated by coke deposition when hydrogen partial pressure drops, is a significant challenge \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. The interplay between catalyst deactivation and regeneration is crucial in industrial settings \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Operando spectroscopy is anticipated to provide more sophisticated process control in the near future \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. UVvis spectroscopy can monitor long-term irreversible catalyst deactivation \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. The demand for olefins is expected to remain high due to their widespread use as feedstock in the chemical industry \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Dozens of new PDH projects have been announced in recent years, utilizing advanced CrOx/Al2O3 or PtSn/Al2O3 catalysts that allow continuous operation for several years \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. The reactions face challenges such as limited equilibrium conversion and side reactions at high temperatures \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Alternative catalysts like GaOx, MoOx, and VOx have been explored but have not yet become practical alternatives due to rapid deactivation and irrecoverable activity after regeneration \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Further research is needed to address these issues \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in dehydrogenation technologies, particularly the development of CrOx/Al2O3 and PtSn/Al2O3 catalysts, have significantly enhanced the industrial viability of olefin production. However, the limitations in catalyst stability, noble metal loading, and energy efficiency necessitate further research. The exploration of oxidative dehydrogenation as an alternative pathway, which requires the development of suitable catalysts and oxidizing agents, presents a promising direction. Addressing the challenges of catalyst deactivation and regeneration, particularly in the context of coke deposition, is crucial for improving process efficiency. The integration of advanced spectroscopic techniques for process control and monitoring long-term catalyst deactivation is anticipated to lead to more sophisticated process management. The high demand for olefins, driven by their widespread use in the chemical industry, underscores the need for continued innovation in dehydrogenation technologies. Future research efforts should focus on developing alternative catalysts that can overcome the limitations of current systems, such as rapid deactivation and irrecoverable activity after regeneration. The development of more efficient and sustainable dehydrogenation processes will be pivotal in meeting the growing demand for olefins while addressing environmental concerns.

                最近页岩气产量的激增显著影响了天然气的经济性，使其在烯烃生产中比原油更具竞争力\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。这一经济转变增强了替代烯烃生产方法，如脱氢和MTO，相对于传统方法如流化催化裂化和蒸汽裂解的竞争力。先进的CrOx/Al2O3（Catofin）和PtSn/Al2O3（Oleflex）催化剂已经发展到脱氢反应可以持续数年的程度\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。然而，当前的脱氢技术仍然需要在贵金属负载、催化剂稳定性和能源效率方面进行改进\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。虽然替代催化剂有前景，但它们由于焦炭沉积和无法通过再生完全恢复活性而过快失活\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。氧化脱氢避免了非氧化方法的热力学限制，但需要开发合适的催化剂和氧化剂\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。CO2的共进料可能会由于逆水煤气变换反应导致额外的焦炭沉积\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。固体氧载体为选择性氧化SHC反应中产生的氢提供了一种替代解决方案\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。可以在反应器系统中引入膜来去除氢气，使平衡向烯烃生产偏移\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。当氢气分压下降时，催化剂失活，尤其是焦炭沉积，是一个重大挑战\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。催化剂失活和再生之间的相互作用在工业环境中至关重要\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。原位光谱学有望在不久的将来提供更复杂的过程控制\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。UVvis光谱可以监测催化剂长期不可逆失活\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。由于它们在化工行业作为原料的广泛使用，对烯烃的需求预计在未来可预见的时间内保持高位\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。近年来宣布了数十个新的PDH项目，利用先进的CrOx/Al2O3或PtSn/Al2O3催化剂，可实现数年的连续运行\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。反应面临有限的平衡转化率和高温下副反应盛行的挑战\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。虽然已经探索了如GaOx、MoOx和VOx等替代催化剂，但由于快速失活和再生后不可恢复的活性，它们尚未成为实用的替代品\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。需要进一步的研究来解决这些问题\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: 脱氢技术的进步，特别是CrOx/Al2O3和PtSn/Al2O3催化剂的发展，显著提高了烯烃生产的工业可行性。然而，催化剂稳定性、贵金属负载和能源效率的局限性需要进一步的研究。氧化脱氢作为一种替代途径的探索，需要开发合适的催化剂和氧化剂，提供了一个有希望的方向。解决催化剂失活和再生的挑战，特别是在焦炭沉积的背景下，对于提高过程效率至关重要。将先进的光谱技术整合到过程控制中，监测长期催化剂失活，预计将导致更复杂的过程管理。烯烃的高需求，由其在化工行业的广泛使用驱动，凸显了在脱氢技术中持续创新的必要性。未来的研究工作应集中在开发能够克服当前系统局限性的替代催化剂，如快速失活和再生后不可恢复的活性。开发更高效、更可持续的脱氢过程对于满足不断增长的烯烃需求，同时解决环境问题至关重要。
