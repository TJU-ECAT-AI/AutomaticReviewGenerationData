                    The recent surge in shale gas production has significantly altered the economic landscape for olefin production, making natural gas a more economical feedstock compared to crude oil \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. This economic shift has bolstered the competitiveness of alternative olefin production methods, such as dehydrogenation and MTO, relative to traditional methods like fluid catalytic cracking and steam cracking. Advanced CrOx/Al2O3 (Catofin) and PtSn/Al2O3 (Oleflex) catalysts have been pivotal in this transition, enabling dehydrogenation reactions to run continuously for several years \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. However, current dehydrogenation technologies still present room for improvement, necessitating further advances to lower noble metal loading, increase catalyst stability, and enhance energy efficiency \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Alternative catalysts, while receiving significant attention, deactivate too quickly due to coke deposition and the inability to fully recover activity through regeneration \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Oxidative dehydrogenation, an emerging approach, circumvents the thermodynamic limitations of nonoxidative methods but requires the identification of suitable catalysts and oxidizing agents \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. The development of an effective oxidative dehydrogenation system hinges on finding the optimal combination of catalyst material and oxidizing agent \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Cofeeding CO2, while potentially beneficial, may lead to additional coke deposition due to the reverse watergas shift reaction \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Solid oxygen carriers, such as CeO2, SbO4, In2O3, WO3, PbOx, Tl2O3, and BiO3, offer an alternative solution for selectively oxidizing hydrogen in the SHC reaction \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Membranes can be introduced to the reactor system to remove hydrogen and shift the equilibrium toward olefin production \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Catalyst deactivation, exacerbated by coke deposition when hydrogen partial pressure drops, represents a significant challenge \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. The interplay between catalyst deactivation and regeneration is critical in industrial settings \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Operando spectroscopy is anticipated to provide more sophisticated process control in the near future \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\], while UVvis spectroscopy can monitor long-term irreversible catalyst deactivation \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. The demand for olefins, driven by their widespread use as feedstock in the chemical industry, is expected to remain high \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Dozens of new PDH projects have been announced in recent years, utilizing advanced CrOx/Al2O3 or PtSn/Al2O3 catalysts that allow continuous operation for several years \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. The reactions, however, face challenges such as limited equilibrium conversion and side reactions at high temperatures \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Alternative catalysts like GaOx, MoOx, and VOx have been explored but have not yet become practical alternatives due to rapid deactivation and irrecoverable activity after regeneration \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]. Further research is needed to address these issues \[10.1021/cr5002436_7\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in dehydrogenation technologies, particularly the development of CrOx/Al2O3 and PtSn/Al2O3 catalysts, have significantly enhanced the industrial viability of olefin production. However, the challenges of noble metal loading, catalyst stability, and energy efficiency remain critical areas for improvement. The exploration of oxidative dehydrogenation as an alternative method, while promising, necessitates the identification of suitable catalysts and oxidizing agents. The use of solid oxygen carriers and membranes in reactor systems presents innovative solutions for hydrogen removal and equilibrium shifting. The interplay between catalyst deactivation and regeneration, as well as the monitoring of long-term catalyst deactivation through UVvis spectroscopy, are crucial for process optimization. The high demand for olefins, driven by their role as feedstock in the chemical industry, underscores the need for continuous innovation in dehydrogenation technologies. Future research efforts should focus on developing alternative catalysts with improved stability and activity recovery, as well as on the integration of advanced spectroscopic techniques for real-time process monitoring. The development of more efficient and sustainable dehydrogenation processes will not only address current limitations but also pave the way for the next generation of olefin production technologies.

                    近年来，页岩气产量的激增显著改变了烯烃生产的经济格局，使天然气成为比原油更具经济性的原料\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。这一经济转变增强了替代烯烃生产方法，如脱氢和MTO，相对于传统方法如流化催化裂化和蒸汽裂解的竞争力。先进的CrOx/Al2O3（Catofin）和PtSn/Al2O3（Oleflex）催化剂在这一转型中发挥了关键作用，使脱氢反应能够连续运行数年\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。然而，当前的脱氢技术仍有改进空间，需要进一步发展以降低贵金属负载，提高催化剂稳定性，并提高脱氢过程的能源效率\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。尽管替代催化剂受到了广泛关注，但它们由于焦炭沉积和无法完全通过再生恢复活性而过快失活\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。氧化脱氢，作为一种新兴方法，避免了非氧化脱氢的热力学限制，但需要找到合适的催化剂和氧化剂\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。开发有效的氧化脱氢系统取决于找到催化剂材料和氧化剂的最佳组合\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。虽然共喂二氧化碳可能有益，但可能会因逆水煤气变换反应导致额外的焦炭沉积\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。固体氧载体，如CeO2，SbO4，In2O3，WO3，PbOx，Tl2O3和BiO3，为选择性氧化SHC反应中产生的氢气提供了替代解决方案\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。可以在反应器系统中引入膜以去除氢气并使平衡向烯烃生产方向移动\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。当氢气分压下降时，催化剂失活，尤其是焦炭沉积，构成了重大挑战\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。催化剂失活与再生之间的相互作用在工业环境中至关重要\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。预计原位光谱学将在不久的将来提供更复杂的工艺控制\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]，而UVvis光谱学可以监测长期不可逆的催化剂失活\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。由于它们在化工行业作为原料的广泛应用，对烯烃的需求预计将在可预见的未来保持高位\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。近年来，宣布了数十个新的PDH项目，利用先进的CrOx/Al2O3或PtSn/Al2O3催化剂，使连续运行数年成为可能\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。然而，这些反应面临着有限的平衡转化率和高温下副反应的挑战\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。尽管已经探索了如GaOx，MoOx和VOx等替代催化剂，但由于快速失活和再生后不可恢复的活性，它们尚未成为实用的替代品\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。需要进一步的研究来解决这些问题\[10.1021/cr5002436_7\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 脱氢技术的进步，特别是CrOx/Al2O3和PtSn/Al2O3催化剂的发展，显著提高了烯烃生产的工业可行性。然而，贵金属负载、催化剂稳定性和能源效率的挑战仍然是需要改进的关键领域。氧化脱氢作为一种替代方法的探索，虽然前景光明，但需要确定合适的催化剂和氧化剂。在反应器系统中使用固体氧载体和膜为氢气去除和平衡转移提供了创新解决方案。催化剂失活与再生之间的相互作用以及通过UVvis光谱学监测长期催化剂失活对于工艺优化至关重要。由它们在化工行业作为原料的角色驱动的对烯烃的高需求，凸显了在脱氢技术中持续创新的必要性。未来的研究工作应集中在开发具有改进稳定性和活性恢复的替代催化剂，以及将先进的光谱技术集成到实时过程监控中。更高效、更可持续的脱氢过程的发展不仅将解决当前的局限性，还将为下一代烯烃生产技术铺平道路。
