Current dehydrogenation technologies need improvement in noble metal loading, catalyst stability, and energy efficiency. Oxidative dehydrogenation avoids thermodynamic limitations of nonoxidative methods, but requires finding the right catalyst and oxidizing agent. Solid oxygen carriers offer an alternative solution for selectively oxidizing hydrogen in the SHC reaction. Catalyst deactivation is a challenge as coke deposition increases when hydrogen partial pressure drops.
