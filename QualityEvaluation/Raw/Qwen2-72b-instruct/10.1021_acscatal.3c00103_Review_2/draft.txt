1. Recent advancements in strategies for enhancing catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes: Addressing fabrication challenges and optimizing performance

Recent advancements in strategies for enhancing the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes have significantly addressed fabrication challenges and optimized performance for industrial applications \[10.1039/C9CY00237E\], \[10.1016/j.jechem.2021.06.002\], \[10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129\]. Catalysts such as 10Zn0.1Pt/HZ, featuring 1000ppm Pt and 10%Zn on HZSM-5, have demonstrated stable active species and strong carbon resistance, optimizing dehydrogenation stability \[10.1039/C9CY00237E\]. The anchoring of Pt-Sn clusters at Al3+penta sites on Al2O3 has been shown to prevent Pt-Sn cluster agglomeration, boosting stability in dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.jechem.2021.06.002\]. Ultrafine Ga-Pt nanocatalysts in silicalite-1 have also been reported to boost propene selectivity and formation rate, with zeolite restriction enhancing stability and maintaining activity \[10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129\].

Strategies for enhancing catalytic stability in dehydrogenation have also involved the use of Pt alloy nanoparticles on mesoporous zeolites, which have been shown to boost activity and selectivity \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. Silanol nests enable rare-earth diffusion onto Pt, forming stable, active intermetallics such as PtREE/zeolite, which achieves 40% initial propane conversion and retains 8% after 30 days \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. The oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) process, which combines dehydrogenation and oxidation, has also been explored to reduce energy needs \[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016\]. Catalysts like Pt/Na[Fe]-ZSM-5 have been found to enhance stability and selectivity in this process \[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016\].

Innovative double-decoration of PtGa with Pb and Ca has been shown to boost stability in propane dehydrogenation, addressing fabrication and performance challenges \[10.1002/anie.202107210\]. Strategies for optimizing Pt dispersion, alloying, and carrier selection have also been reported to prevent migration and sintering, enhancing stability \[10.1039/D0CS00983K\]. Recent strategies for enhancing catalytic stability in supported metal catalysts for direct dehydrogenation have focused on controlling particle size, tailoring bimetallics, stabilizing through strong interactions, encapsulating in zeolites, maintaining texture, and using single-site precursors \[10.1039/D0CS01260B\].

Support modification has been shown to boost Pt-based PDH catalysts' stability, enhancing activity and selectivity \[10.1039/C9CY01907C\]. The addition of In to Pt/Sn-SBA-15 has been found to enhance redox, antisintering, and coke resistance, boosting propylene selectivity and stability \[10.1021/acs.iecr.9b01413\]. Recent strategies have also involved the use of promoters in Pt-based catalysts, such as Sn, Ga, In, Zn, Cu, Co, and REEs, to suppress side reactions, inhibit coke, and optimize performance \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_2\]. Pt/Cu clusters on h-BN nanosheets have been reported to optimize propane dehydrogenation performance, addressing fabrication challenges and optimizing performance \[10.1039/C9CY02313E\].

Bimetallic Pt catalysts have been shown to boost PDH via enhanced dispersion and electron transfer \[10.3390/catal9090767\]. Support modifications with ZnO, La2O3, TiO2, and single Pt atom sites have been reported to improve propane conversion \[10.3390/catal9090767\]. Hierarchical structures have been found to offer stable, accessible sites for PDH \[10.3390/catal9090767\]. Hydrotalcite-like compounds' 3D oxide nanosheet array has been reported to boost PDH performance \[10.3390/catal9090767\].

Recent catalyst design for direct dehydrogenation has used silica-supported Pt1Sn1 nanoparticles, operating at thermodynamic limits with high propylene selectivity, without the need for hydrogen dilution \[10.1126/science.abg7894\]. This strategy prevents Pt-Sn segregation, maintains 97% initial activity, and >99% selectivity after regeneration \[10.1126/science.abg7894\]. Larger size and higher Sn/Pt ratio in PtxSn100x/Mg(Al)O have been shown to boost activity and reduce carbon, solving fabrication challenges and optimizing performance \[10.1016/j.jcat.2013.11.017\]. CoSiBeta, with ultrasmall Co in dealuminated Beta zeolite, has been reported to boost propane conversion and propylene selectivity, tackling fabrication challenges and performance optimization \[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037\]. Optimized 0.5 wt% Cu on Pt/Al2O3 has been shown to boost propylene selectivity to 90.8% and yield to 36.5%, addressing fabrication challenges and optimizing performance in propane dehydrogenation \[10.1039/C4NR02143F\].
