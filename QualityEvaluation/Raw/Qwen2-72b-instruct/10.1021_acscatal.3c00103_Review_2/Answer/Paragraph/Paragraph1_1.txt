                    Recent advancements in strategies for enhancing the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes have significantly addressed fabrication challenges and optimized performance, particularly in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) \[10.1039/C9CY00237E\]. The utilization of 10Zn0.1Pt/HZ, featuring Pt-Zn on HZSM-5, has been instrumental in boosting stable dehydrogenation through active Zn-PtZn centers that resist carbon deposition and optimize performance \[10.1039/C9CY00237E\]. Additionally, Pt-Sn/Al3+penta has been shown to enhance stability and regenerability, with high Al3+penta preventing Pt-Sn agglomeration \[10.1016/j.jechem.2021.06.002\]. Ultrafine Ga-Pt/S-1 zeolite nanocatalysts have also emerged as a promising strategy, boosting PDH product desorption, selectivity, and regeneration stability, effectively addressing fabrication challenges and optimizing performance \[10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129\]. 
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in strategies for enhancing the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes, particularly in PDH, have opened new avenues for research and development. Future directions might focus on the exploration of novel alloy compositions and the optimization of metal-support interactions to further enhance stability and regenerability. The integration of advanced characterization techniques could provide deeper insights into the active sites and reaction mechanisms, guiding the rational design of next-generation catalysts. Moreover, the development of scalable synthesis methods and the optimization of process conditions could bridge the gap between laboratory-scale successes and industrial implementation, ensuring the practical applicability of these advancements.
