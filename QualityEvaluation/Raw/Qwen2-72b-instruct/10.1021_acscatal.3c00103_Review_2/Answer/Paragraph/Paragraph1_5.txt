                    Recent advancements in strategies for enhancing the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes have significantly addressed fabrication challenges and optimized performance for industrial applications \[10.1039/C9CY00237E\], \[10.1016/j.jechem.2021.06.002\], \[10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129\]. Catalysts such as 10Zn0.1Pt/HS HZSM-5 have been shown to boost propylene yield and stability through the presence of Lewis acidic sites and tiny PtZn clusters that resist carbon deposition \[10.1039/C9CY00237E\]. Pt-Sn/Al3+penta sites have been found to enhance stability and prevent agglomeration, optimizing dehydrogenation performance \[10.1016/j.jechem.2021.06.002\]. GaPt@S-1 nanocatalysts have also been reported to boost propene selectivity and address fabrication challenges while optimizing performance in dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The recent advancements in enhancing the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts for direct dehydrogenation processes have opened new avenues for research and development. Future directions might focus on the exploration of novel bimetallic and multimetallic catalysts that can further improve selectivity and stability under harsh industrial conditions. Additionally, the development of advanced synthesis techniques that can precisely control the size, shape, and distribution of metal nanoparticles on supports could lead to the creation of more efficient and durable catalysts. The integration of computational methods with experimental approaches could also provide deeper insights into the catalytic mechanisms and guide the rational design of catalysts. Moreover, the investigation of alternative supports with enhanced thermal stability and mechanical strength could contribute to the development of catalysts that can withstand the high temperatures and pressures encountered in industrial dehydrogenation processes.

                    最近在直接脱氢过程中增强支撑金属催化剂催化稳定性的策略方面取得了显著进展，这些进展显著解决了制造挑战，并为工业应用优化了性能\[10.1039/C9CY00237E\], \[10.1016/j.jechem.2021.06.002\], \[10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129\]。例如，10Zn0.1Pt/HS HZSM-5催化剂通过存在Lewis酸性位点和微小的PtZn簇，能够抵抗碳沉积，从而提高了丙烯产量和稳定性\[10.1039/C9CY00237E\]。Pt-Sn/Al3+penta位点被发现可以增强稳定性，防止聚结，从而优化脱氢性能\[10.1016/j.jechem.2021.06.002\]。GaPt@S-1纳米催化剂也被报道能够提高丙烯选择性，同时解决制造挑战并优化脱氢性能\[10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129\]。

                    综合理解与未来展望: 最近在增强支撑金属催化剂催化稳定性方面取得的进展为直接脱氢过程开辟了新的研究和发展途径。未来的研究方向可能集中在探索新型双金属和多金属催化剂，这些催化剂可以在恶劣的工业条件下进一步提高选择性和稳定性。此外，开发先进的合成技术，可以精确控制支撑上金属纳米粒子的大小、形状和分布，可能会导致更高效和耐用的催化剂的产生。将计算方法与实验方法相结合，也可能为催化机制提供更深入的见解，并指导催化剂的理性设计。此外，对具有增强热稳定性和机械强度的替代支撑的研究，也可能有助于开发能够承受工业脱氢过程中遇到的高温和高压的催化剂。
