                    Recent advancements in strategies for enhancing the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes have significantly addressed fabrication challenges and optimized performance for industrial applications \[10.1039/C9CY00237E\], \[10.1016/j.jechem.2021.06.002\], \[10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129\]. Catalysts such as 10Zn0.1Pt/HZ, with 1000ppm Pt and 10% Zn on HZSM-5, have achieved 46% propylene yield with exceptional stability without regeneration, thanks to the cooperative work of Zn and Pt ensuring active species stability and carbon resistance \[10.1039/C9CY00237E\]. Pt-Sn/Al2O3 with Al3+penta sites has been shown to boost stability and regenerability in dehydrogenation-regeneration cycles, preventing agglomeration \[10.1016/j.jechem.2021.06.002\]. Ultrafine Ga-Pt nanocatalysts in silicalite-1 have enhanced propene selectivity, addressing fabrication and performance challenges \[10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The recent advancements in enhancing the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts for direct dehydrogenation processes have opened new avenues for research and development. The use of bimetallic catalysts, such as Pt-Sn/Al2O3 and Ga-Pt/S-1, has shown promise in improving stability and selectivity \[10.1016/j.jechem.2021.06.002\], \[10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129\]. The development of 10Zn0.1Pt/HZ has challenged conventional fabrication norms, offering a new perspective on catalyst design \[10.1039/C9CY00237E\]. Future research might focus on the exploration of novel bimetallic combinations and the optimization of support materials to further enhance stability and performance. The integration of computational methods for predicting catalyst behavior could also be a fruitful area for investigation, potentially leading to the design of more efficient and stable catalysts for industrial dehydrogenation processes.
