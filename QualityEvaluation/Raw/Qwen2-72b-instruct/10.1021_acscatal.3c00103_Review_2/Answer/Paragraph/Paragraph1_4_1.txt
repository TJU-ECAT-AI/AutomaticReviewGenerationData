                    Recent advancements in strategies for enhancing the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes have significantly addressed fabrication challenges and optimized performance for industrial applications \[10.1039/C9CY00237E\]. Catalysts such as 10Zn0.1Pt/HZ, featuring PtZn and Zn's Lewis acidic sites, have demonstrated improved stability and propylene yield \[10.1039/C9CY00237E\]. Pt-Sn/Al3+penta boosts dehydrogenation stability by preventing agglomeration \[10.1016/j.jechem.2021.06.002\]. GaPt@S-1 nanocatalysts, with confined GaPt alloy in silicalite-1, have enhanced product desorption and propene selectivity, ensuring stability and addressing fabrication and performance optimization \[10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129\]. Recent strategies use Pt alloy nanoparticles on mesoporous zeolites with silanol nests for enhanced catalytic stability in dehydrogenation, significantly improving PDH performance \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. ODH, combining dehydrogenation and oxidation for lower temperature operations, and zeolite-supported catalysts, like Pt/Na[Fe]-ZSM-5, have exhibited high stability for dehydrogenation of light alkanes due to weaker Brnsted sites minimizing side reactions \[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016\].

                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in enhancing the catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts for direct dehydrogenation processes have opened new avenues for research and development. Future directions might focus on the integration of multifunctional catalysts that combine dehydrogenation and oxidation for energy-efficient processes, as seen in ODH \[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016\]. The exploration of novel support materials, such as mesoporous zeolites with silanol nests \[10.1038/s41586-2020-2671-4\], and the development of bimetallic catalysts, like GaPt@S-1 nanocatalysts \[10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129\], could lead to catalysts with enhanced stability and selectivity. Moreover, the optimization of Pt alloy nanoparticles on mesoporous zeolites \[10.1038/s41586-2020-2671-4\] and the use of Pt/Na[Fe]-ZSM-5 for dehydrogenation of light alkanes \[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016\] suggest potential for the design of catalysts that can operate under milder conditions, reducing energy consumption and environmental impact.
