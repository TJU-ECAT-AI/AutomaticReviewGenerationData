1.The multidimensional nature of the ODHP reaction mechanism over B-based catalysts, which encompasses both surface and gas-phase radical reactions, contributes to a more comprehensive understanding of the overall reaction network. The synergistic effects of these reactions have implications for catalyst performance and design optimization, as they suggest that the stabilization of the propyl radical by the nitroxyl radical site may largely contribute to the observed high olefin selectivity. The one-dimensional (1D) nature of the edge avoids the creation of a highly reactive propyl radical (typical of 0D single-site catalysts) and also suppresses the overoxidation of the adsorbed species (typical of 2D surface catalysts). This understanding can guide the design of more efficient and selective catalysts for ODHP and similar reactions.
