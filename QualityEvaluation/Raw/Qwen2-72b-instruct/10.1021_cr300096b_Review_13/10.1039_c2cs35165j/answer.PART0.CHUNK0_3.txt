The d-band center correlates well with adsorbate binding energy and thus catalytic and electrocatalytic activity. The literature also discusses the stability of metal overlayers on different substrates, with carbides being more stable supports for admetals than Pt. This stability is important for maintaining the desired bimetallic structure under reaction conditions. The implications of this correlation for designing more effective catalysts using first principles on model surfaces are that understanding the electronic and structural properties of bimetallic surfaces can guide the design of catalysts with desired catalytic properties. The stability of the bimetallic structure under reaction conditions is also crucial for maintaining the catalytic activity over time.
