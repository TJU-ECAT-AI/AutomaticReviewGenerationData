                The effectiveness and performance of iron-containing ZSM-5 zeolites as catalysts in N2O-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions: Impact of synthesis methods on catalytic performance and evaluation through key performance metrics. Steam treatment of iron-containing ZSM-5 zeolites results in a C3H8 conversion of 48%, N2O conversion of 96%, and C3H6 selectivity of 45% at 450 degrees Celsius \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_9\]. Solid-state ion exchange or liquid exchange methods of synthesizing Fe-ZSM-5 also show good catalytic performance, with C3H8 conversion ranging from 20% to 60%, C3H6 selectivity from 25% to 70%, and C3H6 yield from 14% to 30% in the temperature range of 377 to 527 degrees Celsius \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_9\]. The highest propane conversion (51.7%) and selectivity to propene (39.7%) and to the sum of aromatics and oxygenates (11.5%, mainly C1C3 alcohols) was achieved when a mixture of oxygen and nitrous oxide was used as the oxidant over the steamed FeH-ZSM-5 \[10.1016/j.apcata.2003.12.020\]. Fe-ZSM-5 zeolites prepared with the solid-state method have comparable activity and better stability towards coking compared to Fe-ZSM-5 zeolites prepared by liquid ion exchange and hydrothermal synthesis methods \[10.1016/j.apcata.2012.06.038\]. Steaming of Fe-ZSM-5 zeolites results in a low concentration of Brønsted sites and bare Fe(II) ions, instead, Td-coordinated Fe(III) ions in the FeAAlASi extra-framework species and Fe-oxide-like particles become predominant, which is crucial for high activity in N2O decomposition \[10.1016/j.jcat.2012.12.010\]. Steam-activated Fe-zeolites (MFI and BEA) are efficient catalysts for the N2O-mediated oxidative dehydrogenation of propane, achieving initial propylene yields up to 25% at 723 K \[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005\].

The integration of computational methods with experimental studies could provide a deeper understanding of the catalytic mechanisms involved in the ODHP reaction.
