The effectiveness and performance of iron-containing ZSM-5 zeolites as catalysts in N2O-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions have been extensively studied \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_9\]. Steam treatment of these zeolites results in a C3H8 conversion of 48%, N2O conversion of 96%, and C3H6 selectivity of 45% at 450 degrees Celsius. Solid-state ion exchange or liquid exchange methods of synthesizing Fe-ZSM-5 also show good catalytic performance, with C3H8 conversion ranging from 20% to 60%, C3H6 selectivity from 25% to 70%, and C3H6 yield from 14% to 30% in the temperature range of 377 to 527 degrees Celsius \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_9\]. The study of propane oxidation using oxygen, nitrous oxide, or a mixture of both over ZSM-5 zeolites with low iron concentrations (30-1200 ppm) treated under various conditions at 450 C revealed that the highest propane conversion (51.7%) and selectivity to propene (39.7%) was achieved when a mixture of oxygen and nitrous oxide was used as the oxidant over the steamed FeH-ZSM-5 \[10.1016/j.apcata.2003.12.020\]. The catalytic activity was attributed to extra-framework Fe-oxo complexes generated by hydrolysis of framework SiOFe bonds followed by extraction of iron to extra-framework positions. Fe-zeolites with the highest Fe content steamed in the flow of water vapor at 600 C showed the highest catalytic activity. In addition to propene, other valuable products such as ethene, methanol, ethanol, and acetone were formed \[10.1016/j.apcata.2003.12.020\]. The selectivity and yield of propene were highest over Fe-ZSM-5 with low Al contents and reduced acidity. The initial propene yield over Fe-ZSM-5 was significantly higher than that of FeSiO2 due to the presence of weak and/or medium acid sites together with oligonuclear iron species and iron oxides on the ZSM-5, which enhance the N2O activation \[10.1016/j.apcata.2012.06.038\]. Fe-ZSM-5 zeolites prepared with the solid-state method have comparable activity and better stability towards coking compared to Fe-ZSM-5 zeolites prepared by liquid ion exchange and hydrothermal synthesis methods \[10.1016/j.apcata.2012.06.038\].

Steam treatment, solid-state ion exchange, and liquid exchange methods have been shown to yield zeolites with varying catalytic activities and stabilities. Future research might focus on optimizing the synthesis conditions to achieve higher selectivity and yield of propene, as well as improving the stability of the catalysts under prolonged reaction times. Additionally, the investigation of the role of other metal ions in the zeolite framework and their interaction with iron could provide insights into the mechanism of the ODHP reaction and guide the design of new catalysts.
