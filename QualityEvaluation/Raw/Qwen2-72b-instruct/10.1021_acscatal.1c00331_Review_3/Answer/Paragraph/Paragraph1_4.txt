                    The comprehensive influence of catalyst support pore structure characteristics on coke formation, accommodation, and overall catalyst performance in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions is multifaceted and critical. In PDH reactions, the hierarchical micro-/mesoporous structure of siliceous MFI zeolites significantly enhances catalytic performance by facilitating reactant and product diffusion and providing high metal nanoparticle dispersion \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. Traditionally, PtSn bimetallic catalysts on porous alumina have been used, but Pt alone deactivates rapidly due to coke deposition, reducing selectivity to propylene \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. Incorporating La and Y with Pt into a mesoporous zeolite improves catalyst dispersion and catalytic lifetime by over tenfold \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. The Pt3La and Pt3Y nanoparticles on degallated zeolite (PtLa/mz-deGa) exhibit enhanced PDH performance, including activity, selectivity, and durability, with high initial propane conversion and slow deactivation \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. Surface silanol nests play a critical role in enabling atomistic alloying of La and Y into Pt nanoparticles, contributing to the high PDH catalytic performance \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\].

                    The pore network structure of catalyst supports significantly influences coke formation and accommodation, affecting overall catalyst performance in PDH reactions \[10.1002/aic.16410\]. Coke distribution is non-uniform, influenced by pore structure and reaction conditions. Some large pores become inaccessible when surrounded by plugged pores, complicating the coking process. This leads to sudden changes in macroscopic properties like effective diffusivity and reaction rates. Coke deactivation occurs through covering active sites, increasing diffusion resistance, and blocking pores. The pore network's capacity to contain coke is crucial. During reactions, coke narrows and plugs pores, altering the pore network structure. A percolation phenomenon occurs when the fraction of open pores falls below a critical level, causing significant activity loss due to inaccessible active sites and increased diffusion resistance \[10.1002/aic.16410\].

                    Encapsulating subnanometer PtZn clusters within S-1 zeolite enhances stability and propylene selectivity in PDH reactions, with hydrogen generation rates from formic acid decomposition and catalyst stability after calcination significantly improved \[10.1002/anie.202003349\]. The addition of Cs+ ions boosts regeneration stability without affecting catalytic activity, highlighting the critical role of pore structure in PDH catalyst performance \[10.1002/anie.202003349\]. These findings underscore the importance of pore size, connectivity, uniformity, and hierarchical arrangements in designing optimal catalysts for PDH reactions \[10.1002/anie.202003349\].

                    In PDH reactions, non-acidic supports minimize coke formation by reducing cracking and polymerization reactions \[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]. Adding tin to platinum catalysts shifts coke from the metal to the support, enhancing coke tolerance and performance \[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]. This insight suggests that modifying pore structures can optimize catalysts for better coke accommodation and efficiency \[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\].

                    Pore size significantly impacts coke formation and accommodation, affecting catalyst performance in PDH reactions \[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\]. Smaller pore sizes lead to lower coke yields, as seen in the comparison: 3% Pt-Al2O3 (19.0) > 3% Pt-Al2O3-1 (9.6) and 3% Pt-SBA-15 (9.7) > 3% Pt-SiO2 (5.5) \[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\]. This relationship is crucial for designing optimal catalysts, as it highlights the role of pore structure in managing coke formation and enhancing overall performance \[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The insights gained from the study of catalyst support pore structure characteristics in PDH reactions underscore the pivotal role of pore structure in managing coke formation and accommodation, thereby influencing overall catalyst performance. The hierarchical micro-/mesoporous structure of siliceous MFI zeolites, the pore network's capacity to contain coke, and the encapsulation of subnanometer metal clusters within zeolites are key factors in enhancing PDH catalytic performance. The addition of elements like La, Y, and Cs+ ions, and the modification of pore structures to optimize coke accommodation, offer promising strategies for designing more efficient and durable catalysts. Future research endeavors might focus on developing catalysts with tailored pore structures that can further minimize coke formation while maximizing selectivity and stability. Additionally, exploring the role of surface silanol nests in enabling atomistic alloying and the impact of hierarchical pore modifications on acidity and coke accommodation could lead to breakthroughs in PDH catalyst design. The potential for integrating multiple strategies, such as non-acidic supports and the addition of tin to platinum catalysts, to enhance coke tolerance and performance also warrants further investigation. Ultimately, these advancements could pave the way for the development of next-generation PDH catalysts with improved efficiency and longevity.

                    催化剂载体孔结构特性对丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应中焦炭形成、容纳和整体催化剂性能的全面影响是多方面的且至关重要。在PDH反应中，硅质MFI沸石的分层微/介孔结构通过促进反应物和产物扩散以及提供高金属纳米粒子分散度，显著提高了催化性能\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。传统上，多金属PtSn催化剂在多孔氧化铝上使用，但单独的Pt由于焦炭沉积而迅速失活，降低了对丙烯的选择性\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。将La和Y与Pt结合到介孔沸石中，可将催化剂分散度和催化寿命提高十倍以上\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。在脱镓沸石上形成的Pt3La和Pt3Y纳米粒子(PtLa/mz-deGa)表现出增强的PDH性能，包括活性、选择性和耐久性，具有高初始丙烷转化率和缓慢失活\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。表面硅醇巢在使La和Y原子化合金化到Pt纳米粒子中起着关键作用，对高PDH催化性能的贡献\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。

                    催化剂载体的孔网络结构对PDH反应中的焦炭形成和容纳有显著影响，影响整体催化剂性能\[10.1002/aic.16410\]。焦炭分布不均匀，受孔结构和反应条件的影响。当被堵塞的孔包围时，一些大孔变得无法进入，使焦化过程复杂化。这导致宏观性质如有效扩散率和反应速率的突然变化。焦炭失活是通过覆盖活性位点、增加扩散阻力和堵塞孔隙发生的。孔网络容纳焦炭的能力至关重要。在反应过程中，焦炭会缩小并堵塞孔隙，改变孔网络结构。当开放孔的分数低于临界水平时，会发生渗透现象，由于无法进入的活性位点和增加的扩散阻力，导致显著的活性损失\[10.1002/aic.16410\]。

                    在S-1沸石中封装亚纳米PtZn簇增强了PDH反应中的稳定性和丙烯选择性，从甲酸分解产生的氢气生成率和煅烧后催化剂稳定性显著提高\[10.1002/anie.202003349\]。添加Cs+离子在不影响催化活性的情况下提高了再生稳定性，突出了孔结构在PDH催化剂性能中的关键作用\[10.1002/anie.202003349\]。这些发现强调了孔尺寸、连通性、均匀性和分层排列在设计PDH反应的最优催化剂中的重要性\[10.1002/anie.202003349\]。

                    在PDH反应中，非酸性载体通过减少裂解和聚合反应来最小化焦炭形成\[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]。向铂催化剂中添加锡将焦炭从金属转移到载体，增强了焦炭耐受性和性能\[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]。这一见解表明，修改孔结构可以优化催化剂，以更好地容纳焦炭和提高效率\[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]。

                    孔尺寸对PDH反应中的焦炭形成和容纳有重大影响，影响催化剂性能\[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\]。较小的孔尺寸导致较低的焦炭产量，如比较所示：3% Pt-Al2O3 (19.0) > 3% Pt-Al2O3-1 (9.6)和3% Pt-SBA-15 (9.7) > 3% Pt-SiO2 (5.5)\[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\]。这种关系对于设计最优催化剂至关重要，因为它突出了孔结构在管理焦炭形成和增强整体性能中的作用\[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 从PDH反应中催化剂载体孔结构特性的研究中获得的见解突出了孔结构在管理焦炭形成和容纳，从而影响整体催化剂性能中的核心作用。硅质MFI沸石的分层微/介孔结构、孔网络容纳焦炭的能力以及在沸石中封装亚纳米金属簇是增强PDH催化性能的关键因素。添加如La、Y和Cs+离子以及修改孔结构以优化焦炭容纳，为设计更高效和更耐用的催化剂提供了有希望的策略。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发具有定制孔结构的催化剂，以进一步最小化焦炭形成，同时最大化选择性和稳定性。此外，探索表面硅醇巢在使原子化合金化和分层孔修改对酸性和焦炭容纳的影响的作用，可能会在PDH催化剂设计方面取得突破。将非酸性载体和向铂催化剂中添加锡以增强焦炭耐受性和性能的多种策略的整合，也值得进一步研究。最终，这些进展可能为开发具有改进效率和寿命的下一代PDH催化剂铺平道路。
