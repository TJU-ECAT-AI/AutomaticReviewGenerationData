                    The comprehensive influence of catalyst support pore structure characteristics on coke formation, accommodation, and overall catalyst performance in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions is multifaceted and critical \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. Hierarchical micro-/mesoporous structures in siliceous MFI zeolites significantly enhance catalytic performance by facilitating diffusion and providing high metal nanoparticle dispersion \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. Traditionally, PtSn bimetallic catalysts on alumina have been used in PDH, but Pt alone rapidly deactivates due to coke deposition, reducing selectivity to propylene \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. Incorporating La and Y into a mesoporous zeolite support improves Pt dispersion, forming Pt3La and Pt3Y nanoparticles that exhibit enhanced PDH catalytic performance, including activity, selectivity, and durability \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. The pore network structure's characteristics significantly influence coke formation and accommodation, affecting catalyst performance \[10.1002/aic.16410\]. Coke distribution is non-uniform, influenced by pore structure and reaction conditions, leading to sudden changes in macroscopic properties like effective diffusivity and reaction rates \[10.1002/aic.16410\]. Encapsulating PtZn clusters within S-1 zeolite enhances stability and propylene selectivity, with pore structure playing a critical role \[10.1002/anie.202003349\]. Incorporating Cs+ ions maintains catalytic activity through multiple cycles, underscoring the importance of pore structure in PDH catalysts and its implications for optimal design \[10.1002/anie.202003349\]. Non-acidic supports reduce coke formation by minimizing cracking and polymerization reactions \[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]. Adding tin to platinum catalysts enhances coke tolerance and performance by transferring coke from the metal to the support \[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]. Pore size significantly impacts coke formation and yield, with smaller pores leading to lower coke amounts \[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\]. The pore geometry of catalyst supports significantly influences PDH activity, selectivity, and coke formation \[10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031\]. Hierarchical pore modifications in ZSM-5 reduce acidity, inhibiting coke formation and enhancing coke accommodation \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_3\]. Hierarchical-pore structures in ZQ zeolite significantly improve coke accommodation and catalyst performance in PDH reactions compared to ZSM-5 \[10.1002/jctb.4686\]. The pore structure of MFI zeolite supports the stability and robustness of subnanometric PtSn clusters, even at high temperatures, suggesting its role in accommodating structural changes that influence coke formation and catalyst performance \[10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The insights gained from the study of catalyst support pore structure characteristics in PDH reactions underscore the pivotal role of pore size, connectivity, uniformity, and hierarchical arrangements in influencing coke formation, accommodation, and overall catalyst performance. These findings suggest that optimal catalyst design should focus on engineering hierarchical pore structures that balance high metal dispersion, facilitate diffusion, and minimize coke formation while maximizing selectivity and stability. Future research endeavors might concentrate on developing advanced characterization techniques to better understand the atomic-level interactions within these complex pore structures. Additionally, exploring the synthesis of novel catalyst supports with tailored pore architectures could lead to the discovery of materials with enhanced coke accommodation and resistance, thereby improving the longevity and efficiency of PDH catalysts. The integration of computational modeling with experimental studies could also provide a deeper understanding of the relationships between pore structure and catalytic performance, guiding the rational design of next-generation PDH catalysts.

                    催化剂载体孔结构特征对丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应中焦炭形成、容纳和整体催化剂性能的全面影响是多方面的且至关重要\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。硅质MFI沸石中层次微/介孔结构显著增强催化性能，通过促进扩散和提供高金属纳米粒子分散度\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。传统上，PtSn双金属催化剂在PDH中使用氧化铝，但单独的Pt由于焦炭沉积而迅速失活，降低了对丙烯的选择性\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。将La和Y掺入介孔沸石载体中改善了Pt分散，形成Pt3La和Pt3Y纳米粒子，展现出增强的PDH催化性能，包括活性、选择性和耐用性\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。催化剂载体孔网络结构的特性显著影响焦炭形成和容纳，影响催化剂性能\[10.1002/aic.16410\]。焦炭分布不均匀，受孔结构和反应条件影响，导致宏观性质如有效扩散率和反应速率的突然变化\[10.1002/aic.16410\]。将PtZn簇封装在S-1沸石中增强了稳定性和丙烯选择性，孔结构起着关键作用\[10.1002/anie.202003349\]。掺入Cs+离子通过多个循环维持催化活性，突出了孔结构在PDH催化剂中的重要性及其对最优设计的启示\[10.1002/anie.202003349\]。非酸性载体通过最小化裂解和聚合反应减少焦炭形成\[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]。向铂催化剂中添加锡通过将焦炭从金属转移到载体上，增强焦炭耐受性和性能\[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]。孔尺寸显著影响焦炭形成和产量，小孔导致较低的焦炭量\[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\]。催化剂载体孔几何结构显著影响PDH活性、选择性和焦炭形成\[10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031\]。ZSM-5的层次孔修改减少了酸性，抑制了焦炭形成并增强了焦炭容纳\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_3\]。ZQ沸石的层次孔结构在PDH反应中显著改善了焦炭容纳和催化剂性能，与ZSM-5相比\[10.1002/jctb.4686\]。MFI沸石载体的孔结构支持了即使在高温下也稳定的亚纳米PtSn簇的稳定性和稳健性，表明其在适应结构变化、影响焦炭形成和催化剂性能中的作用\[10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 从PDH反应中催化剂载体孔结构特征的研究中获得的见解强调了孔尺寸、连通性、均匀性和层次排列在影响焦炭形成、容纳和整体催化剂性能中的关键作用。这些发现表明，最优催化剂设计应侧重于工程层次孔结构，以平衡高金属分散度、促进扩散、最小化焦炭形成，同时最大化选择性和稳定性。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发先进的表征技术，以更好地理解这些复杂孔结构中的原子级相互作用。此外，探索合成具有定制孔结构的新型催化剂载体，可能会导致发现具有增强焦炭容纳和抵抗性的材料，从而提高PDH催化剂的寿命和效率。将计算建模与实验研究相结合，也可能提供对孔结构与催化性能之间关系的更深入理解，指导下一代PDH催化剂的理性设计。
