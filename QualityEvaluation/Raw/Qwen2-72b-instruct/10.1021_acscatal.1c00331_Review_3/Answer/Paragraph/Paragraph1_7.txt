                    The comprehensive influence of catalyst support pore structure characteristics on coke formation, accommodation, and overall catalyst performance in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions is multifaceted and critical. Hierarchical micro-/mesoporous structures in siliceous MFI zeolites significantly enhance catalytic performance by facilitating diffusion and providing high metal nanoparticle dispersion \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. These structures improve durability and selectivity compared to traditional PtSn bimetallic catalysts on alumina, where Pt alone shows high initial activity but deactivates rapidly due to coke deposition, reducing selectivity to propylene. Incorporating La and Y with Pt into the mesoporous zeolite support enhances dispersion and catalytic lifetime, with Pt3La and Pt3Y nanoparticles showing enhanced PDH performance, including activity, selectivity, and durability \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. The pore network structure significantly influences coke formation and accommodation, affecting overall catalyst performance \[10.1002/aic.16410\]. Encapsulating PtZn clusters within S-1 zeolite enhances stability and propylene selectivity, highlighting the critical role of pore structure in PDH catalyst performance \[10.1002/anie.202003349\]. Non-acidic supports minimize coke formation by reducing cracking and polymerization reactions, while adding tin to platinum catalysts enhances coke tolerance and performance by transferring coke from the metal to the support \[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]. Pore size significantly impacts coke yield, with smaller pore sizes leading to lower coke amounts \[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\]. The pore geometry of catalyst supports significantly influences PDH activity, selectivity, and coke formation \[10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The detailed analysis of the influence of catalyst support pore structure on PDH reactions reveals the pivotal role of pore characteristics in managing coke formation, accommodation, and overall catalyst performance. The hierarchical pore structure of zeolites, particularly MFI zeolites, offers significant advantages in enhancing catalytic performance and durability. The encapsulation of metal clusters within zeolite pores and the modification of pore structures to reduce acidity and enhance surface properties have shown promising results in improving coke accommodation and catalytic stability. The transfer of coke from metal to support, facilitated by hierarchical pore structures, unexpectedly boosts performance. These findings suggest that future research should focus on the development of catalysts with optimized pore structures that can better accommodate cokes while minimizing their formation. The exploration of non-acidic supports and the addition of elements like tin to modify electronic properties and enhance coke tolerance presents a promising avenue for designing more effective catalysts. The role of pore size, connectivity, uniformity, and hierarchical arrangements in PDH catalysts should be further investigated to derive optimal catalytic properties. Advanced characterization techniques can provide deeper insights into the structural features of active species and their interactions with pore structures, guiding the design of catalysts with enhanced stability and selectivity. The integration of these strategies could lead to the development of next-generation PDH catalysts with improved performance and longevity.

                    催化剂载体孔结构特性对丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应中焦炭形成、容纳和整体催化剂性能的全面影响是多方面的且至关重要。硅质MFI沸石的分级微/介孔结构通过促进扩散和提供高金属纳米粒子分散度显著增强催化性能\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。这些结构与传统的PtSn双金属催化剂相比，提高了耐用性和选择性，后者在氧化铝上，Pt单独存在时虽然初始活性高，但由于焦炭沉积而迅速失活，降低了对丙烯的选择性。将La和Y与Pt结合到介孔沸石载体中，增强了分散性和催化寿命，Pt3La和Pt3Y纳米粒子表现出增强的PDH性能，包括活性、选择性和耐用性\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。孔网络结构对焦炭形成和容纳有重大影响，影响整体催化剂性能\[10.1002/aic.16410\]。将PtZn簇封装在S-1沸石中增强了稳定性和丙烯选择性，突显了孔结构在PDH催化剂性能中的关键作用\[10.1002/anie.202003349\]。非酸性载体通过减少裂解和聚合反应来最小化焦炭形成，而向铂催化剂中添加锡通过将焦炭从金属转移到载体上，增强了焦炭耐受性和性能\[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]。孔尺寸对焦炭产量有显著影响，较小的孔尺寸导致较低的焦炭量\[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\]。催化剂载体的孔几何结构对PDH活性、选择性和焦炭形成有重大影响\[10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 对催化剂载体孔结构对PDH反应影响的详细分析揭示了孔特性在管理焦炭形成、容纳和整体催化剂性能中的核心作用。特别是MFI沸石的分级孔结构，在增强催化性能和耐用性方面提供了显著优势。将金属簇封装在沸石孔中以及通过减少酸性和增强表面性质来修改孔结构，在提高焦炭容纳能力和催化稳定性方面显示出有希望的结果。通过分级孔结构促进的从金属到载体的焦炭转移，意外地提高了性能。这些发现表明，未来的研究应集中在开发具有优化孔结构的催化剂，这些催化剂可以更好地容纳焦炭，同时最小化其形成。探索非酸性载体和添加如锡等元素以修改电子性质和增强焦炭耐受性，为设计更有效的催化剂提供了一条有希望的途径。应进一步研究PDH催化剂中孔尺寸、连通性、均匀性和分级排列的作用，以推导出最佳的催化性质。先进的表征技术可以提供对活性物种的结构特征及其与孔结构相互作用的更深层次的见解，指导具有增强稳定性和选择性的催化剂的设计。这些策略的整合可能导致下一代PDH催化剂的开发，具有改进的性能和更长的寿命。
