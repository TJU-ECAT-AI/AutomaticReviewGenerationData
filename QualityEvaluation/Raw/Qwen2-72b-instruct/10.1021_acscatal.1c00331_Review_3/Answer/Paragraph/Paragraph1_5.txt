                    The comprehensive influence of catalyst support pore structure characteristics on coke formation, accommodation, and overall catalyst performance in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions has been extensively studied \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. Hierarchical micro-/mesoporous structures in zeolite catalyst supports significantly enhance catalytic performance by facilitating reactant and product diffusion and providing high metal nanoparticle dispersion. Traditional PtSn catalysts on alumina deactivate rapidly due to coke deposition, reducing selectivity to propylene. Incorporating La and Y into a mesoporous zeolite support improves Pt dispersion, resulting in Pt3La and Pt3Y nanoparticles that exhibit enhanced PDH performance, including activity, selectivity, and durability \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. These nanoparticles, formed throughout the zeolite support, show slow deactivation, retaining high conversion rates over extended periods. The pore network structure of catalyst supports significantly influences coke formation and accommodation, affecting overall catalyst performance \[10.1002/aic.16410\]. Coke distribution is non-uniform, influenced by pore structure and reaction conditions. Large pores can become inaccessible when surrounded by plugged pores, complicating the coking process. This leads to sudden changes in macroscopic properties like effective diffusivity and reaction rates. Coke deactivation occurs through covering active sites, increasing diffusion resistance, and blocking pores, rendering active sites inaccessible. Encapsulating PtZn clusters within S-1 zeolite enhances stability and propylene selectivity, highlighting the critical role of pore structure in catalyst performance \[10.1002/anie.202003349\]. The introduction of Cs+ ions further improves regeneration stability, underscoring the importance of pore characteristics in optimal catalyst design. Non-acidic supports reduce coke formation by minimizing cracking and polymerization reactions, while the addition of tin to platinum catalysts enhances coke tolerance and performance by transferring coke from the metal to the support \[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]. These findings have implications for designing effective catalysts with improved coke accommodation and overall performance.
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The insights gained from the comprehensive analysis of catalyst support pore structure characteristics in PDH reactions open up new avenues for catalyst design and optimization. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel hierarchical pore structures that can further enhance coke accommodation and resistance to deactivation. The exploration of advanced characterization techniques could provide deeper insights into the structural changes of active species within the pore structures, guiding the engineering of optimal pore geometries. Additionally, the integration of computational modeling and machine learning algorithms could accelerate the discovery of new catalysts with tailored pore structures for enhanced PDH performance. The optimization of pore size, connectivity, uniformity, and hierarchical arrangements will remain critical for the design of next-generation PDH catalysts that can achieve higher activity, selectivity, and durability under industrial conditions.

                    催化剂载体孔结构特征对丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应中焦炭形成、容纳和整体催化剂性能的全面影响已经得到深入研究\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。在PDH反应中，沸石催化剂载体的分级微/介孔结构通过促进反应物和产物扩散以及提供高金属纳米粒子分散度，显著增强催化性能。传统的PtSn催化剂在氧化铝上由于焦炭沉积而迅速失活，降低了对丙烯的选择性。将La和Y掺入介孔沸石载体中可以改善Pt的分散性，形成Pt3La和Pt3Y纳米粒子，这些粒子表现出增强的PDH性能，包括活性、选择性和耐久性\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。这些纳米粒子在整个沸石载体中形成，显示出缓慢失活，长时间保持高转化率。催化剂载体的孔网络结构对焦炭形成和容纳有重大影响，影响整体催化剂性能\[10.1002/aic.16410\]。焦炭分布不均匀，受孔结构和反应条件的影响。当被堵塞的孔包围时，大孔可能变得无法进入，使焦化过程复杂化。这导致宏观性质如有效扩散率和反应速率的突然变化。焦炭失活是通过覆盖活性位点、增加扩散阻力和堵塞孔隙，使活性位点无法进入。将PtZn簇封装在S-1沸石中增强了稳定性和丙烯选择性，突出了孔结构在催化剂性能中的关键作用\[10.1002/anie.202003349\]。Cs+离子的引入进一步提高了再生稳定性，突出了孔特性在最佳催化剂设计中的重要性。非酸性载体通过最小化裂解和聚合反应来减少焦炭形成，而将锡添加到铂催化剂中通过将焦炭从金属转移到载体来增强焦炭耐受性和性能\[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]。这些发现对设计具有改进的焦炭容纳能力和整体性能的有效催化剂具有启示。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 从PDH反应中催化剂载体孔结构特征的全面分析中获得的见解为催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的途径。未来的研究工作可能集中在开发新型分级孔结构，以进一步增强焦炭容纳能力和抵抗失活的能力。先进表征技术的探索可以提供对活性物种在孔结构内结构变化的更深入理解，指导最佳孔几何形状的工程设计。此外，计算建模和机器学习算法的集成可以加速具有定制孔结构的新型催化剂的发现，以增强PDH性能。孔尺寸、连通性、均匀性和分级排列的优化对于设计下一代PDH催化剂仍然至关重要，这些催化剂可以在工业条件下实现更高的活性、选择性和耐久性。
