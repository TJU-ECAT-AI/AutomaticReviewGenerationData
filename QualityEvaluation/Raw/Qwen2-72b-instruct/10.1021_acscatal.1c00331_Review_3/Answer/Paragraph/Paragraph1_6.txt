                    The comprehensive influence of catalyst support pore structure characteristics on coke formation, accommodation, and overall catalyst performance in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions is multifaceted and critical. Hierarchical micro-/mesoporous structures, such as those found in siliceous MFI zeolites, significantly enhance catalytic performance by facilitating diffusion and providing high metal nanoparticle dispersion \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. These structures improve durability and selectivity, as evidenced by the formation of Pt3La and Pt3Y nanoparticles, which exhibit enhanced PDH catalytic performance, including activity, selectivity, and durability. The addition of La and Y to the mesoporous zeolite increases the catalytic lifetime more than tenfold, and the PtLa/mz-deGa catalyst shows a high initial propane conversion of 40% and slow deactivation, retaining 8% conversion after 30 days of reaction \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. The pore network structure of catalyst supports significantly influences coke formation and accommodation, affecting overall catalyst performance. Coke distribution is non-uniform, influenced by pore structure and reaction conditions \[10.1002/aic.16410\]. Large pores can become inaccessible when surrounded by plugged pores, complicating the coking process. This leads to sudden changes in macroscopic properties like diffusivity and reaction rates. Coke deactivation occurs through covering active sites, increasing diffusion resistance, and blocking pores, impacting catalyst activity. A percolation phenomenon occurs when the fraction of open pores falls below a critical level, further reducing activity \[10.1002/aic.16410\]. Encapsulating PtZn clusters within S-1 zeolite enhances hydrogen generation rates and improves catalyst stability under industrial conditions, highlighting the critical role of pore structure in PDH catalyst performance \[10.1002/anie.202003349\]. The introduction of Cs+ ions into the zeolite maintains catalytic activity through multiple cycles, underscoring the importance of pore size, connectivity, uniformity, and hierarchical arrangements in designing effective PDH catalysts \[10.1002/anie.202003349\]. Non-acidic supports minimize coke formation by reducing cracking and polymerization reactions, unlike acidic supports. Adding tin to platinum catalysts facilitates coke transfer from metal to support, enhancing coke tolerance and performance \[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]. Smaller pore sizes lead to reduced coke yield, as evidenced by the comparison of different catalysts: 3% Pt-SiO2 exhibits the least coke formation compared to other catalysts like 3% Pt-Al2O3 and 3% Pt-SBA-15 \[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\]. This relationship is crucial for designing effective catalysts with controlled pore structures to minimize coke formation and enhance PDH performance.

                    催化剂载体孔结构特征对丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应中焦炭形成、容纳和整体催化剂性能的全面影响是多方面的且至关重要。具有层次微/介孔结构的硅质MFI沸石，如那些在硅质MFI沸石中发现的，通过促进扩散和提供高金属纳米粒子分散度，显著增强催化性能\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。这些结构提高了耐用性和选择性，如通过形成Pt3La和Pt3Y纳米粒子所证明的，这些纳米粒子表现出增强的PDH催化性能，包括活性、选择性和耐用性。向介孔沸石中添加La和Y使催化寿命增加超过十倍，而PtLa/mz-deGa催化剂显示出40%的初始丙烷转化率和缓慢失活，反应30天后仍保持8%的转化率\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。催化剂载体的孔网络结构对焦炭形成和容纳有重大影响，影响整体催化剂性能。焦炭分布是非均匀的，受孔结构和反应条件的影响\[10.1002/aic.16410\]。当被堵塞的孔包围时，大孔可能变得无法进入，使焦化过程复杂化。这导致宏观性质如扩散性和反应速率的突然变化。焦炭失活通过覆盖活性位点、增加扩散阻力和堵塞孔隙影响催化剂活性。当开放孔的分数低于临界水平时，会发生渗透现象，进一步降低活性\[10.1002/aic.16410\]。将PtZn簇封装在S-1沸石中增强了工业条件下的氢气生成率和催化剂稳定性，突显了孔结构在PDH催化剂性能中的关键作用\[10.1002/anie.202003349\]。将Cs+离子引入沸石中通过多个循环保持催化活性，强调了在设计有效的PDH催化剂时考虑孔大小、连通性、均匀性和层次排列的重要性\[10.1002/anie.202003349\]。非酸性载体通过减少裂解和聚合反应来最小化焦炭形成，与酸性载体不同。向铂催化剂中添加锡促进焦炭从金属向载体的转移，增强焦炭耐受性和性能\[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]。较小的孔径导致焦炭产量减少，如通过不同催化剂的比较所证明：3% Pt-SiO2与其他催化剂如3% Pt-Al2O3和3% Pt-SBA-15相比，表现出最少的焦炭形成\[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\]。这种关系对于设计具有受控孔结构的有效催化剂以最小化焦炭形成并增强PDH性能至关重要。
