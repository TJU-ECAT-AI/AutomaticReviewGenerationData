                    The comprehensive influence of catalyst support pore structure characteristics on coke formation, accommodation, and overall catalyst performance in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions is multifaceted and critical. In PDH reactions, the hierarchical micro-/mesoporous structure of zeolite catalyst supports significantly boosts catalytic performance by facilitating reactant and product diffusion and providing high metal nanoparticle dispersion \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. This is a marked improvement over traditional PtSn bimetallic catalysts on porous alumina, which suffer from rapid deactivation due to coke deposition, leading to a loss of selectivity to propylene \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. Incorporating La and Y with Pt into the mesoporous zeolite enhances the dispersion of Pt nanoparticles, leading to a tenfold increase in catalytic lifetime \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. The formation of Pt3La and Pt3Y nanoparticles on the degallated zeolite significantly improves PDH catalytic performance, including activity, selectivity, and durability \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. The critical role of surface silanol nests in enabling atomistic alloying of La and Y into Pt nanoparticles contributes to the high PDH catalytic performance \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. The pore network structure of catalyst supports significantly influences coke formation and accommodation, affecting overall catalyst performance \[10.1002/aic.16410\]. Coke distribution is non-uniform, influenced by pore structure and reaction conditions. Large pores can become inaccessible when surrounded by plugged pores, complicating the coking process. This leads to sudden changes in macroscopic properties like effective diffusivity and reaction rates. Coke deactivation occurs through covering active sites, increasing diffusion resistance, and blocking pores, rendering active sites inaccessible. The pore network's capacity to contain coke is crucial. During reactions, coke narrows and plugs pores, altering the pore network structure. When the fraction of open pores drops below a critical threshold, a percolation phenomenon occurs, causing significant activity loss due to increased diffusion resistance and inaccessible active sites \[10.1002/aic.16410\]. In PDH reactions, the pore structure of catalyst supports significantly influences coke formation, accommodation, and overall performance. Encapsulating subnanometer PtZn clusters within S-1 zeolite enhances stability and propylene selectivity \[10.1002/anie.202003349\]. The confinement of metal species boosts hydrogen generation and improves catalyst stability. Incorporating Cs+ ions into the zeolite maintains catalytic activity after multiple cycles, highlighting the critical role of pore structure in PDH catalysts and its implications for optimal design \[10.1002/anie.202003349\]. Non-acidic supports in catalysts reduce coke formation by minimizing cracking and polymerization reactions \[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]. Adding tin to platinum catalysts enhances coke tolerance and performance by transferring coke from the metal to the support \[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]. These insights suggest strategies for designing effective catalysts in PDH reactions \[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]. In PDH reactions, pore size significantly influences coke formation and yield, with smaller pores leading to lower coke amounts \[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\]. The impact of acidity on coke formation is less clear, as pore size appears to limit coke accumulation \[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\].

                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The intricate relationship between catalyst support pore structure characteristics and their impact on coke formation, accommodation, and overall catalyst performance in PDH reactions underscores the need for a multidisciplinary approach to catalyst design. The hierarchical micro-/mesoporous structure of zeolite catalyst supports, which facilitates reactant and product diffusion and provides high metal nanoparticle dispersion, is a key factor in enhancing catalytic performance \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. The role of surface silanol nests in enabling atomistic alloying of La and Y into Pt nanoparticles, contributing to high PDH catalytic performance, highlights the importance of understanding the atomic-level interactions within catalysts \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. The non-uniform distribution of coke, influenced by pore structure and reaction conditions, and the percolation phenomenon that occurs when the fraction of open pores drops below a critical threshold, leading to significant activity loss, emphasize the need for advanced characterization techniques to monitor pore structure changes in real-time \[10.1002/aic.16410\]. The encapsulation of subnanometer PtZn clusters within S-1 zeolite, which enhances stability and propylene selectivity, and the role of Cs+ ions in maintaining catalytic activity after multiple cycles, suggest that the design of PDH catalysts should consider the synergistic effects of pore size, connectivity, uniformity, and hierarchical arrangements \[10.1002/anie.202003349\]. The reduction of coke formation by non-acidic supports and the enhancement of coke tolerance and performance by transferring coke from the metal to the support, facilitated by the addition of tin to platinum catalysts, point to the potential of modifying electronic properties for improved catalytic efficiency \[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]. The significant influence of pore size on coke yield, with smaller pores leading to lower coke amounts, indicates that pore size optimization is a critical parameter for designing effective PDH catalysts \[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\]. Future research endeavors might focus on developing advanced characterization techniques for real-time monitoring of pore structure changes, exploring the synergistic effects of pore size, connectivity, uniformity, and hierarchical arrangements on PDH catalyst performance, and modifying electronic properties for improved coke tolerance and catalytic efficiency. Additionally, the development of non-acidic supports and the optimization of pore size for minimizing coke formation and maximizing selectivity and stability are promising avenues for the design of optimal PDH catalysts.

                    催化剂载体孔结构特性对丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应中焦炭形成、容纳和整体催化剂性能的全面影响是多方面的且至关重要。在PDH反应中，沸石催化剂载体的分层微/介孔结构通过促进反应物和产物扩散以及提供高金属纳米粒子分散度，显著提高了催化性能\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。这与传统的在多孔氧化铝上的PtSn双金属催化剂相比，是一个显著的改进，后者由于焦炭沉积而迅速失活，导致丙烯选择性降低\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。将La和Y与Pt结合到介孔沸石中，增强了Pt纳米粒子的分散，导致催化寿命提高了十倍\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。在脱镓沸石上形成的Pt3La和Pt3Y纳米粒子显著提高了PDH催化性能，包括活性、选择性和耐久性\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。表面硅醇巢在使La和Y原子化合金化到Pt纳米粒子中发挥关键作用，对高PDH催化性能的贡献\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。催化剂载体的孔网络结构对焦炭形成和容纳有重大影响，影响整体催化剂性能\[10.1002/aic.16410\]。焦炭分布不均匀，受孔结构和反应条件的影响。当被堵塞的孔包围时，大孔可能变得无法进入，使焦化过程复杂化。这导致宏观性质如有效扩散率和反应速率的突然变化。焦炭失活通过覆盖活性位点、增加扩散阻力和堵塞孔，使活性位点无法进入。孔网络容纳焦炭的能力至关重要。在反应过程中，焦炭会缩小并堵塞孔，改变孔网络结构。当开放孔的分数低于临界阈值时，会发生渗透现象，由于扩散阻力增加和活性位点无法进入，导致显著的活性损失\[10.1002/aic.16410\]。在PDH反应中，催化剂载体的孔结构对焦炭形成、容纳和整体性能有显著影响。将亚纳米PtZn簇封装在S-1沸石中增强了稳定性和丙烯选择性\[10.1002/anie.202003349\]。金属物种的限制提高了氢气生成率并改善了催化剂稳定性。将Cs+离子引入沸石中，在多次循环后维持催化活性，突出了孔结构在PDH催化剂中的关键作用及其对最优设计的影响\[10.1002/anie.202003349\]。催化剂中的非酸性支持通过最小化裂解和聚合反应来减少焦炭形成\[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]。在铂催化剂中添加锡通过将焦炭从金属转移到支持物来增强焦炭耐受性和性能\[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]。这些见解为设计有效的PDH催化剂提供了策略\[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]。在PDH反应中，孔径对焦炭形成和产量有重大影响，小孔径导致较低的焦炭量\[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\]。孔径对焦炭形成的影响不太清楚，因为孔径似乎限制了焦炭积累\[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 催化剂载体孔结构特性及其对PDH反应中焦炭形成、容纳和整体催化剂性能的影响的复杂关系强调了催化剂设计需要跨学科方法。沸石催化剂载体的分层微/介孔结构，通过促进反应物和产物扩散以及提供高金属纳米粒子分散度，是提高催化性能的关键因素\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。表面硅醇巢在使La和Y原子化合金化到Pt纳米粒子中，对高PDH催化性能的贡献，突出了理解催化剂内原子级相互作用的重要性\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。受孔结构和反应条件影响的焦炭非均匀分布，以及当开放孔的分数低于临界阈值时发生的渗透现象，导致显著的活性损失，强调了需要先进的表征技术来实时监测孔结构变化\[10.1002/aic.16410\]。将亚纳米PtZn簇封装在S-1沸石中，增强了稳定性和丙烯选择性，以及Cs+离子在多次循环后维持催化活性的作用，表明PDH催化剂的设计应考虑孔径、连通性、均匀性和分层排列的协同效应\[10.1002/anie.202003349\]。非酸性支持减少焦炭形成和通过将焦炭从金属转移到支持物来增强焦炭耐受性和性能，通过在铂催化剂中添加锡，指出了通过修改电子性质来提高催化效率的潜力\[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]。孔径对焦炭产量有重大影响，小孔径导致较低的焦炭量，表明孔径优化是设计有效PDH催化剂的关键参数\[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\]。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发先进的表征技术以实时监测孔结构变化，探索孔径、连通性、均匀性和分层排列对PDH催化剂性能的协同效应，以及通过修改电子性质来提高焦炭耐受性和催化效率。此外，开发非酸性支持和优化孔径以最小化焦炭形成和最大化选择性和稳定性是设计最优PDH催化剂的有希望的途径。
