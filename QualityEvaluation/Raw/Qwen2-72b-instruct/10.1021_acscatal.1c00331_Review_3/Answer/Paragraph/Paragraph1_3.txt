                    The comprehensive influence of catalyst support pore structure characteristics on coke formation, accommodation, and overall catalyst performance in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions is multifaceted and critical. Hierarchical micro-/mesoporous structures in siliceous MFI zeolites significantly enhance catalytic performance by facilitating diffusion and providing high metal nanoparticle dispersion \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. Traditionally, PtSn bimetallic catalysts on porous alumina have been used, but Pt alone rapidly deactivates due to coke deposition, reducing selectivity to propylene. Incorporating La and Y with Pt into a mesoporous zeolite support improves dispersion and catalytic lifetime, with Pt3La and Pt3Y nanoparticles showing enhanced PDH performance, including activity, selectivity, and durability \[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]. The pore network structure significantly influences coke formation and accommodation, affecting catalyst performance. Coke distribution is non-uniform, influenced by pore structure and reaction conditions, leading to sudden changes in macroscopic properties like effective diffusivity and reaction rates \[10.1002/aic.16410\]. Encapsulating subnanometer PtZn clusters within S-1 zeolite enhances stability and propylene selectivity, highlighting the critical role of pore structure in PDH catalyst performance \[10.1002/anie.202003349\]. Non-acidic supports minimize coke formation by reducing cracking and polymerization reactions compared to acidic supports. Adding tin to platinum catalysts enhances coke tolerance and performance by transferring coke from the metal to the support \[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]. Pore size significantly impacts coke formation and accommodation, affecting overall catalyst performance. Smaller pore sizes lead to reduced coke yields, as evidenced by the comparison of different catalysts \[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\]. The pore geometry of catalyst supports, including size and structure, significantly influences activity, selectivity, and coke formation. Micro-porous Pt-ZSM-5 exhibits the highest propane conversion, followed by Pt-Beta and meso-porous Pt-SBA-15, indicating activity decreases with pore size \[10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031\]. Catalyst support pore structure significantly influences coke formation and PDH performance. Hierarchical pore modifications in ZSM-5 reduce acidity, inhibiting coke formation and enhancing coke accommodation \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_3\]. Hierarchical-pore structures in ZQ zeolite significantly enhance coke accommodation and catalyst performance in PDH reactions compared to ZSM-5 \[10.1002/jctb.4686\]. The pore structure of MFI zeolite supports PtSn clusters, accommodating structural changes that influence coke formation and catalyst performance \[10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7\].

                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements and findings discussed in the file highlight the pivotal role of catalyst support pore structure in PDH reactions. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel hierarchical pore structures that optimize the balance between diffusion, metal dispersion, and coke accommodation. The exploration of non-acidic supports and the incorporation of elements like tin and lanthanides could lead to the design of catalysts with enhanced coke tolerance and stability. The integration of advanced characterization techniques to understand the structural features of subnanometric clusters and their impact on catalyst performance will be crucial. Moreover, the investigation of pore geometry and its influence on side reactions and coke precursor formation could provide insights into the design of catalysts with improved selectivity and stability. The development of catalysts with tailored pore structures that can accommodate structural changes in active species while minimizing coke formation and maximizing selectivity and stability will be a key area of focus. The potential of emerging fields such as machine learning and computational modeling in predicting and optimizing catalyst performance based on pore structure characteristics holds promise for the future of PDH catalyst design.

                    催化剂载体孔结构特性对丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应中焦炭形成、容纳和整体催化剂性能的全面影响是多方面的且至关重要。硅质MFI沸石的分级微/介孔结构通过促进扩散和提供高金属纳米粒子分散度显著增强催化性能\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。传统上，多金属PtSn催化剂在多孔氧化铝上使用，但单独的Pt由于焦炭沉积而迅速失活，降低了对丙烯的选择性。将La和Y与Pt结合到介孔沸石载体中改善了分散性和催化寿命，Pt3La和Pt3Y纳米粒子表现出增强的PDH性能，包括活性、选择性和耐久性\[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4\]。孔网络结构对焦炭形成和容纳有重大影响，影响催化剂性能。焦炭分布受孔结构和反应条件影响而不均匀，导致宏观性质如有效扩散率和反应速率的突然变化\[10.1002/aic.16410\]。在S-1沸石中封装亚纳米PtZn簇增强了稳定性和丙烯选择性，突显了孔结构在PDH催化剂性能中的关键作用\[10.1002/anie.202003349\]。非酸性载体通过减少裂解和聚合反应来最小化焦炭形成，与酸性载体相比。向铂催化剂中添加锡通过将焦炭从金属转移到载体上，增强了焦炭耐受性和性能\[10.1016/j.jiec.2010.10.011\]。孔径对焦炭形成和容纳有显著影响，影响整体催化剂性能。较小的孔径导致较低的焦炭产量，如不同催化剂的比较所示\[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4\]。催化剂载体的孔几何结构，包括尺寸和结构，对活性、选择性和焦炭形成有显著影响。微孔Pt-ZSM-5表现出最高的丙烷转化率，其次是Pt-Beta和介孔Pt-SBA-15，表明活性随孔径增大而降低\[10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031\]。催化剂载体孔结构对PDH性能和焦炭形成有重大影响。ZSM-5的分级孔修改降低了酸性，抑制了金属上的焦炭形成并增强了焦炭容量\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_3\]。ZQ沸石的分级孔结构与ZSM-5相比，在PDH反应中显著增强了焦炭容纳和催化剂性能\[10.1002/jctb.4686\]。MFI沸石的孔结构支持PtSn簇，适应结构变化，影响焦炭形成和催化剂性能\[10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7\]。

                    综合理解与未来展望: 文件中讨论的进展和发现突出了催化剂载体孔结构在PDH反应中的核心作用。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发优化扩散、金属分散和焦炭容纳之间平衡的新型分级孔结构。探索非酸性载体和掺入锡和镧系元素可能导向设计具有增强焦炭耐受性和稳定性的催化剂。将先进的表征技术整合以理解亚纳米簇的结构特征及其对催化剂性能的影响将是至关重要的。此外，研究孔几何结构及其对副反应和焦炭前体形成的影响可能为设计具有改进选择性和稳定性的催化剂提供见解。开发具有定制孔结构的催化剂，能够适应活性物种的结构变化，同时最小化焦炭形成，最大化选择性和稳定性，将是关注的关键领域。新兴领域如机器学习和计算建模在根据孔结构特性预测和优化催化剂性能方面的潜力，为PDH催化剂设计的未来带来了希望。
