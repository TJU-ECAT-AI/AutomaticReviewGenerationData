                    The comprehensive effects and mechanisms of hydrogen cofeeding on coke formation, catalyst stability, and reaction dynamics in the propane dehydrogenation (PDH) process have been elucidated through extensive research \[10.1002/anie.201701115\], \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\], \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. Hydrogen cofeeding suppresses coke formation, akin to water vapor's effect, by maintaining a reducing atmosphere that prevents coke precursor formation \[10.1002/anie.201701115\]. This mechanism enhances catalyst stability and efficiency by reducing deactivation and regeneration needs, potentially prolonging catalyst life and enhancing process performance. Hydrogen cofeeding boosts alkene formation by facilitating hydrogen-assisted dehydrogenation of surface alkyl species, with little impact on alkene selectivity, which remains high \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. It significantly reduces coke formation on catalysts by hydrogenating coke precursor species. The presence of Ga in the catalyst further suppresses coke buildup. Hydrogen cofeeding enhances catalyst performance, akin to Sn addition, boosting propane conversion and propene selectivity \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. It alters coke deposits, fostering smaller, less defective graphite crystallites. Competitive adsorption of hydrogen on Pt-based catalysts curbs coke formation, reducing carbon species surface coverage, and thus polymerization. Increased hydrogen concentration boosts conversion and selectivity on Pt-Sn/Al2O3 catalysts, with optimal performance at 75% propane. Hydrogen's addition either increases graphitic lattice defects or decreases crystallite size, correlating with decreased coke deposition. Graphitic coke predominates in high hydrogen concentrations, covering the catalyst support, while disordered coke forms in its absence, affecting Pt nanoparticles. Hydrogen maintains Pt surfaces free of disordered coke, improving catalyst stability and efficiency.
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The insights gained from the comprehensive effects of hydrogen cofeeding on the PDH process suggest a promising direction for future research. By focusing on the intricate interplay between hydrogen cofeeding, coke formation, and catalyst stability, there is potential to develop more efficient and stable catalysts for PDH processes. The optimization of hydrogen-to-propane ratios could lead to enhanced selectivity and conversion rates, while minimizing coke formation. Moreover, the exploration of novel catalyst compositions, such as the incorporation of Ga or Sn, could further suppress coke buildup and improve catalyst performance. Future studies might also investigate the impact of hydrogen cofeeding on the reaction mechanisms at a molecular level, aiming to understand the precise role of hydrogen in altering energy barriers and coke accumulation patterns. This could pave the way for the design of advanced catalysts with tailored properties for PDH and other dehydrogenation reactions, potentially revolutionizing the chemical industry by improving process efficiency and reducing environmental impact.

                    通过广泛的研究，深入阐明了氢气共进料对丙烷脱氢(PDH)过程中焦炭形成、催化剂稳定性和反应动力学的全面影响和机制\[10.1002/anie.201701115\], \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\], \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。氢气共进料通过维持还原气氛来抑制焦炭形成，类似于水蒸气的作用，防止焦炭前体的形成\[10.1002/anie.201701115\]。这种机制通过减少失活和再生需求，提高催化剂稳定性和效率，潜在地延长催化剂寿命，增强过程性能。氢气共进料通过促进表面烷基物种的氢辅助脱氢来增强烯烃的形成，对烯烃选择性的影响很小，烯烃选择性保持较高\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。它通过氢化焦炭前体物种显著减少催化剂上的焦炭形成。催化剂中Ga的存在进一步抑制焦炭积累。氢气共进料增强催化剂性能，类似于Sn的添加，提高丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。它改变焦炭沉积，形成更小、缺陷更少的石墨结晶体。氢在Pt基催化剂上的竞争性吸附抑制焦炭形成，减少碳物种的表面覆盖，从而减少聚合。增加的氢浓度在Pt-Sn/Al2O3催化剂上提高转化率和选择性，最佳性能在75%丙烷时。氢的添加要么增加石墨晶格缺陷，要么减少结晶体尺寸，与焦炭沉积的减少相关。高氢浓度下，石墨化焦炭占主导，覆盖催化剂载体，而在其缺失时，无序焦炭形成，影响Pt纳米颗粒。氢保持Pt表面无无序焦炭，提高催化剂稳定性和效率。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 从氢气共进料对PDH过程的全面影响中获得的见解为未来的研究指明了有希望的方向。通过关注氢气共进料、焦炭形成和催化剂稳定性之间的复杂相互作用，有可能开发出更高效、更稳定的PDH过程催化剂。优化氢气与丙烷的比例可能会提高选择性和转化率，同时最小化焦炭形成。此外，探索新型催化剂组成，如Ga或Sn的掺入，可以进一步抑制焦炭积累，提高催化剂性能。未来的研究可能还会调查氢气共进料对分子水平反应机制的影响，旨在理解氢在改变能垒和焦炭积累模式中的确切作用。这可能为设计具有定制属性的先进催化剂铺平道路，用于PDH和其他脱氢反应，通过提高过程效率和减少环境影响，可能彻底改变化工行业。
