                    The comprehensive effects and mechanisms of hydrogen cofeeding on coke formation, catalyst stability, and reaction dynamics in the propane dehydrogenation (PDH) process are elucidated from the study of reference \[10.1002/anie.201701115\]. Hydrogen cofeeding, akin to water vapor in steam reforming, suppresses coke formation by maintaining a reducing atmosphere that prevents coke precursor formation, enhancing catalyst stability and reducing deactivation and regeneration needs \[10.1002/anie.201701115\]. This mechanism is crucial for sustaining catalytic activity and improving process efficiency \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]. Hydrogen cofeeding also enhances alkene formation by accelerating hydrogen-assisted dehydrogenation, with minimal impact on alkene selectivity, which remains near 100% \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. It significantly reduces coke formation by hydrogenating coke precursor species, and the presence of Ga in the catalyst further suppresses coke \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. The hydrogen-to-propane ratio affects propene selectivity more than ethene, yet propene selectivity remains above 95.5% \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. Hydrogen cofeeding enhances catalyst performance, akin to Sn addition, boosting propane conversion and propene selectivity \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. It transforms coke deposits, promoting smaller, less defective graphite crystallites, and suppresses coke formation on Pt-based catalysts by competitive adsorption \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. Hydrogen cofeeding raises the energy barrier for the third, undesired cleavage step more than the first two, potentially increasing alkene yield \[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]. This selective reaction enhancement by hydrogen boosts PDH efficiency and performance \[10.1039/c3cp53796j\].

The development of advanced catalysts that can harness the benefits of hydrogen cofeeding more effectively, while minimizing coke formation and maximizing alkene yield, represents a promising direction for future research.                     从\[10.1002/anie.20170115\]的参考文献中，我们深入了解了氢气共馈在丙烷脱氢(PDH)过程中对焦炭形成、催化剂稳定性和反应动力学的全面影响和机制。氢气共馈类似于蒸汽重整中的水蒸气，通过维持还原气氛来抑制焦炭形成，防止焦炭前体的形成，从而增强催化剂的稳定性和减少失活和再生需求\[10.1002/anie.201701115\]。这一机制对于维持催化活性和提高过程效率至关重要\[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]。氢气共馈还通过加速氢辅助脱氢来增强烯烃的形成，对烯烃选择性的影响最小，烯烃选择性保持在接近100%\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。它通过氢化焦炭前体物种显著减少焦炭形成，而催化剂中Ga的存在进一步抑制焦炭\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。氢气与丙烷的比例对丙烯选择性的影响大于乙烯，但丙烯选择性仍保持在95.5%以上\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。氢气共馈增强催化剂性能，类似于Sn的添加，提高丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。它改变焦炭沉积物，促进形成更小、缺陷更少的石墨结晶体，并通过竞争性吸附抑制Pt基催化剂上的焦炭形成\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。氢气共馈将第三步，即不希望发生的裂解步骤的能量障碍提高得比前两步更多，可能增加烯烃产量\[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]。这种选择性反应增强通过氢气提高了PDH的效率和性能\[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]。

                    综合理解与未来展望: 文件中的见解突出了氢气共馈在PDH中的关键作用，强调了它在焦炭形成、催化剂稳定性和反应动力学方面的多方面影响。未来的研究工作可能集中在优化氢气共馈策略，以进一步增强催化剂的寿命和过程效率。探索氢气共馈与其他添加剂，如Sn和Ga的协同效应，可以为提高PDH性能提供新的途径。此外，研究氢气对能量障碍和酸位点转化的详细影响机制，可以提供对反应动力学的更深入理解。开发能够更有效地利用氢气共馈的好处的先进催化剂，同时最小化焦炭形成并最大化烯烃产量，代表了未来研究的一个有希望的方向。将原位光谱技术集成到监测催化剂结构和焦炭形成在反应条件下的动态变化中，可以提供实时数据，增强我们对PDH过程的理解，并指导更有效催化剂的设计。
