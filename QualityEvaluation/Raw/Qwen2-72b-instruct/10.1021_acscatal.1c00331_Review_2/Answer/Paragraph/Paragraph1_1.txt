                    The comprehensive effects and mechanisms of hydrogen cofeeding on coke formation, catalyst stability, and reaction dynamics in the propane dehydrogenation (PDH) process have been elucidated through extensive research \[10.1002/anie.201701115\], \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\], \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\], \[10.1039/c3cp53796j\], \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\], \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\], \[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]. Hydrogen cofeeding suppresses coke formation by maintaining a reducing atmosphere that prevents the formation of coke precursors, akin to the effect of water vapor \[10.1002/anie.201701115\]. This mechanism enhances catalyst stability and efficiency by reducing deactivation and regeneration needs, potentially prolonging catalyst life and improving overall process performance. Hydrogen cofeeding also enhances alkene formation by accelerating hydrogen-assisted dehydrogenation of surface alkyl species, with minimal impact on alkene selectivity, which remains near 100% \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. It significantly reduces coke formation on catalysts by hydrogenating coke precursor species. The presence of Ga in the catalyst further suppresses coke formation. The hydrogen-to-propane ratio impacts propene formation differently than ethene, with propene selectivity remaining above 95.5% even at high ratios. Hydrogen cofeeding transforms coke deposits, fostering smaller, less defective graphite crystallites \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. By competitively adsorbing on the catalyst surface, hydrogen curbs coke formation, reducing carbon species' surface coverage, and decreasing polymerization likelihood. This increases stability, conversion, and selectivity on Pt-Sn/Al2O3 catalysts, with optimal performance at 75% propane concentration. Hydrogen cofeeding reduces coke formation and enhances alkene desorption, improving reaction selectivity \[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]. It raises the energy barrier for the third, undesired cleavage step more than the first two, favoring alkene formation. Hydrogen cofeeding suppresses coke formation, maintaining catalyst activity and preventing deactivation \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]. It prevents coke precursor formation, crucial for catalytic activity, and can remove reversible coke through hydrogenation, enhancing process efficiency and performance. Hydrogen cofeeding reduces coke formation and enhances catalyst stability by eliminating coke precursors \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\]. It raises the energy barrier for propylene dehydrogenation, preventing deep reactions. For PtSn/Al2O3 catalysts, hydrogen decreases coke on the support without affecting metal coke levels. Hydrogen cofeeding reduces coke formation on the support, with a minor effect on the metal \[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]. It suppresses coke precursor formation, decreasing catalyst deactivation. Hydrogen decreases the total coke amount, notably reducing Peak II, associated with support-formed coke, and promotes softer coke formation.
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The literature on hydrogen cofeeding in PDH reveals a multifaceted impact on coke formation, catalyst stability, and reaction dynamics. The suppression of coke formation by hydrogen cofeeding, akin to water vapor's effect, and its role in maintaining a reducing atmosphere that prevents coke precursor formation, significantly enhances catalyst stability and efficiency. The transformation of coke deposits into smaller, less defective graphite crystallites, the reduction of coke on the support, and the promotion of softer coke formation by hydrogen cofeeding are critical for improving catalyst longevity and process efficiency. The enhancement of alkene formation and selectivity by hydrogen cofeeding, particularly through the acceleration of hydrogen-assisted dehydrogenation and the raising of energy barriers for undesired cleavage steps, suggests that small hydrogen additions can boost process efficiency and performance. Future research endeavors might focus on optimizing the hydrogen-to-propane ratio for maximum propene selectivity and minimizing coke formation. Additionally, the exploration of novel catalyst compositions, such as the inclusion of Ga, could further suppress coke formation and enhance catalyst stability. The investigation of hydrogen cofeeding's effect on energy barriers, coke accumulation patterns, acid site conversions, and graphitization could provide deeper insights into the reaction mechanisms and guide the design of more efficient PDH processes.

                    通过广泛的研究，氢气共进料对丙烷脱氢(PDH)过程中焦炭形成、催化剂稳定性和反应动力学的全面影响和机制已被阐明\[10.1002/anie.201701115\], \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\], \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\], \[10.1039/c3cp53796j\], \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\], \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\], \[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]。氢气共进料通过维持还原气氛，防止焦炭前体的形成，抑制焦炭形成，类似于水蒸气的作用\[10.1002/anie.201701115\]。这种机制通过减少失活和再生需求，增强催化剂稳定性和效率，潜在地延长催化剂寿命，改善整体过程性能。氢气共进料还通过加速表面烷基物种的氢辅助脱氢，增强烯烃的形成，对烯烃选择性的影响最小，烯烃选择性保持在近100%\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。它通过氢化焦炭前体物种，显著减少催化剂上的焦炭形成。催化剂中镓的存在进一步抑制焦炭形成。氢气与丙烷的比例对丙烯形成的影响与乙烯不同，即使在高比例下，丙烯选择性仍保持在95.5%以上。氢气共进料转变焦炭沉积物，促进形成较小、缺陷较少的石墨结晶体\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。通过在催化剂表面的竞争性吸附，氢气抑制焦炭形成，减少碳物种的表面覆盖，降低聚合可能性。这在Pt-Sn/Al2O3催化剂上增加了稳定性、转化率和选择性，75%丙烷浓度时性能最佳。氢气共进料减少焦炭形成，增强烯烃的解吸，提高反应选择性\[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]。它将第三、不希望的裂解步骤的能量屏障提高得比前两个步骤多，有利于烯烃的形成。氢气共进料抑制焦炭形成，维持催化剂活性，防止失活\[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]。它防止焦炭前体的形成，对催化活性至关重要，并能通过氢化去除可逆焦炭，增强过程效率和性能。氢气共进料通过消除焦炭前体，减少焦炭形成，增强催化剂稳定性\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\]。它提高了丙烯脱氢的能量屏障，防止深度反应。对于PtSn/Al2O3催化剂，氢气减少了支撑上的焦炭，而对金属焦炭水平影响不大。氢气共进料减少了支撑上的焦炭形成，对金属的影响较小\[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]。它抑制焦炭前体的形成，减少催化剂失活。氢气减少了总焦炭量，特别是减少了与支撑形成的焦炭相关的峰值II，并促进形成较软的焦炭。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 关于PDH中氢气共进料的文献揭示了其对焦炭形成、催化剂稳定性和反应动力学的多方面影响。氢气共进料抑制焦炭形成，类似于水蒸气的作用，以及其在维持还原气氛、防止焦炭前体形成方面的作用，显著增强了催化剂稳定性和效率。氢气共进料将焦炭沉积物转变为较小、缺陷较少的石墨结晶体，减少支撑上的焦炭，促进形成较软的焦炭，对于提高催化剂寿命和过程效率至关重要。氢气共进料通过加速氢辅助脱氢和提高不希望的裂解步骤的能量屏障，增强烯烃的形成和选择性，表明小量的氢气添加可以提高过程效率和性能。未来的研究工作可能集中在优化氢气与丙烷的比例，以实现最大丙烯选择性和最小化焦炭形成。此外，探索新型催化剂组成，如加入镓，可以进一步抑制焦炭形成，增强催化剂稳定性。研究氢气共进料对能量屏障、焦炭积累模式、酸位点转化和石墨化的影响，可以为反应机制提供更深入的见解，并指导设计更有效的PDH过程。
