                    The comprehensive effects and mechanisms of hydrogen cofeeding on coke formation, catalyst stability, and reaction dynamics in the propane dehydrogenation (PDH) process have been elucidated through extensive research \[10.1002/anie.201701115\]. Hydrogen cofeeding, akin to water vapor, suppresses coke formation by maintaining a reducing atmosphere, thereby enhancing catalyst stability and efficiency \[10.1002/anie.201701115\]. It prevents the formation of coke precursors, significantly reducing coke accumulation and improving process performance through decreased catalyst deactivation and regeneration needs \[10.1002/anie.201701115\]. This leads to better catalyst longevity and overall process efficiency \[10.1002/anie.201701115\].

                    Hydrogen cofeeding boosts alkene formation by enhancing hydrogen-assisted dehydrogenation, with little effect on alkene selectivity, which remains near 100% \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. It significantly reduces coke formation on catalysts by hydrogenating coke precursor species \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. The presence of Ga in the catalyst further suppresses coke formation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. Hydrogen cofeeding affects propene formation similarly to ethene, but optimal ratios differ \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. Propene selectivity remains above 95.5% even at high hydrogen-to-propane ratios \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. Coke accumulation is greater for ethane than propane dehydrogenation, attributed to the easier hydrogenation of propyli-dyne compared to ethylidyne \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\].

                    Hydrogen cofeeding in PDH enhances catalyst performance, akin to Sn addition, boosting propane conversion and propene selectivity \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. It alters coke deposits, promoting smaller, less defective graphite crystallites \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. Competitive adsorption by hydrogen reduces surface coverage of carbon species, decreasing coke formation \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. Catalyst stability, conversion, and selectivity improve, especially at 75% propane concentration \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. Increased hydrogen concentration correlates with reduced coke deposition, as observed by Raman spectroscopy \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. More graphitic coke forms in high hydrogen concentrations, covering the support, while disordered coke is prevalent without hydrogen, affecting Pt nanoparticles \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. Hydrogen maintains Pt surfaces free of disordered coke, enhancing active sites for dehydrogenation \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. These effects lead to improved conversion, selectivity, and catalyst stability, with potential for longer lifetimes and reduced deactivation \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\].

                    Hydrogen cofeeding in PDH reduces coke formation, facilitates alkene desorption, and raises the energy barrier for the third cleavage step, enhancing selectivity towards alkene formation \[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]. This improves overall process efficiency and performance \[10.1039/c3cp53796j\].

                    Hydrogen cofeeding in PDH suppresses coke formation, enhancing catalyst stability and efficiency \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]. It prevents coke precursor formation, maintaining activity but doesn't remove existing coke \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]. Hydrogen removes reversible coke via hydrogenation, crucial for sustaining process performance \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\].

                    Hydrogen cofeeding in PDH reduces coke formation and enhances catalyst stability by eliminating coke precursors \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\]. It increases the energy barrier for propylene dehydrogenation, preventing deep reactions \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\]. For PtSn/Al2O3 catalysts, hydrogen decreases coke on the support, with minimal effect on metal coke \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\]. Hydrogen weakens support acidity, converting Lewis to Brnsted acid sites, reducing coking \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\]. Graphitization of coke correlates with hydrogen pressure, making coke removal harder due to higher combustion temperatures \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\]. These effects improve process efficiency and performance, extending catalyst lifetimes and potentially increasing propylene yields \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\].

                    Hydrogen cofeeding in PDH decreases coke formation on the support, with a minor effect on the metal \[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]. It suppresses coke precursor formation, reducing catalyst deactivation \[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]. Hydrogen reduces the total coke amount, especially on the support, forming softer coke at lower temperatures \[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]. The reaction order for coking on the support is first-order with propylene, 0.7-order with hydrogen, and 1.4-order with propane, indicating distinct mechanisms \[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]. Hydrogen weakens support acidity, converting Brnsted to Lewis acid sites, reducing hard coke formation \[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]. It has a lesser impact on soft coke on the metal \[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]. Hydrogen influences reaction mechanisms, suppressing coke precursors and lowering polymer molecular weight during propylene polymerization \[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\].

                    通过广泛的研究，氢气共进料对丙烷脱氢(PDH)过程中焦炭形成、催化剂稳定性和反应动力学的全面影响和机制已被阐明\[10.1002/anie.201701115\]。氢气共进料类似于水蒸气，通过维持还原气氛抑制焦炭形成，从而增强催化剂稳定性和效率\[10.1002/anie.201701115\]。它防止了焦炭前体的形成，显著减少了焦炭积累，通过减少催化剂失活和再生需求改善了过程性能\[10.1002/anie.201701115\]。这导致了更好的催化剂寿命和整体过程效率\[10.1002/anie.201701115\]。

                    氢气共进料通过增强氢辅助脱氢，促进烯烃形成，对烯烃选择性影响较小，烯烃选择性保持在接近100%\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。它通过氢化焦炭前体物种显著减少了催化剂上的焦炭形成\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。催化剂中镓的存在进一步抑制了焦炭形成\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。氢气共进料对丙烯形成的影响类似于乙烯，但最佳比例不同\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。丙烯选择性即使在高氢气对丙烷比例下也保持在95.5%以上\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。丙烷脱氢的焦炭积累大于乙烷脱氢，归因于丙基丁烯比乙基丁烯更容易氢化\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。

                    氢气共进料在PDH中增强催化剂性能，类似于Sn添加，提高了丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。它改变了焦炭沉积，促进了较小、缺陷较少的石墨结晶体的形成\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。氢气的竞争力吸附减少了碳物种的表面覆盖，减少了焦炭形成\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。催化剂稳定性、转化率和选择性提高，特别是在75%丙烷浓度下\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。氢气浓度增加与焦炭沉积减少相关，如拉曼光谱所示\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。在高氢气浓度下形成更多的石墨化焦炭，覆盖了支撑，而在没有氢气的情况下，无序焦炭在Pt纳米粒子上普遍存在\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。氢气保持Pt表面无无序焦炭，增强了脱氢的活性位点\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。这些效应导致转化率、选择性和催化剂稳定性的提高，具有更长的寿命和减少的失活潜力\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。

                    氢气共进料在PDH中减少了焦炭形成，促进了烯烃脱附，并提高了第三裂解步骤的能量势垒，增强了对烯烃形成的选择性\[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]。这改善了整体过程效率和性能\[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]。

                    氢气共进料在PDH中抑制了焦炭形成，增强了催化剂稳定性和效率\[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]。它防止了焦炭前体的形成，维持了活性，但不能去除已形成的焦炭\[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]。氢气通过氢化去除了可逆焦炭，对维持过程性能至关重要\[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]。

                    氢气共进料在PDH中通过消除焦炭前体减少了焦炭形成，增强了催化剂稳定性\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\]。它增加了丙烯脱氢的能量势垒，防止了深度反应\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\]。对于PtSn/Al2O3催化剂，氢气减少了支撑上的焦炭，对金属焦炭的影响较小\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\]。氢气减弱了支撑酸性，将Lewis酸位转化为Brnsted酸位，减少了焦化\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\]。焦炭的石墨化与氢气压力相关，由于更高的燃烧温度，使焦炭去除更加困难\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\]。这些效应改善了过程效率和性能，延长了催化剂寿命，可能增加了丙烯产量\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\]。

                    氢气共进料在PDH中减少了支撑上的焦炭形成，对金属的影响较小\[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]。它抑制了焦炭前体的形成，减少了催化剂失活\[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]。氢气减少了总焦炭量，特别是在支撑上，形成了较低温度下的较软焦炭\[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]。支撑上焦炭的反应级数为丙烯的一级，氢气的0.7级，丙烷的1.4级，表明了不同的机制\[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]。氢气减弱了支撑酸性，将Brnsted酸位转化为Lewis酸位，减少了硬焦炭的形成\[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]。它对金属上的软焦炭影响较小\[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]。氢气影响反应机制，抑制了焦炭前体，降低了丙烯聚合期间的聚合物分子量\[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]。
