                    Hydrogen cofeeding in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) significantly suppresses coke formation, akin to water vapor's effect at high temperatures, by maintaining a reducing atmosphere that prevents coke precursor formation \[10.1002/anie.201701115\]. This enhances catalyst stability and efficiency by reducing deactivation and regeneration needs, potentially improving overall process performance. Hydrogen cofeeding enhances alkene formation by accelerating hydrogen-assisted dehydrogenation of surface alkyl species, with little effect on alkene selectivity, which remains near 100% \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. It significantly reduces coke formation on catalysts by hydrogenating coke precursor species. The presence of Ga in the catalyst further suppresses coke formation. As alkene concentration approaches equilibrium, the rate of alkene formation decreases with increasing H2/alkane ratio. The lower coke formation during propane dehydrogenation compared to ethane dehydrogenation is attributed to the greater ease of hydrogenation of propyli-dyne compared to ethylidyne. Hydrogen cofeeding enhances catalyst performance, akin to adding Sn, boosting propane conversion and propene selectivity \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. It alters coke deposits, promoting smaller, less defective graphite crystallites. By competitively adsorbing on the catalyst surface, hydrogen suppresses coke formation, reducing carbon species' surface coverage, which lessens polymerization and coke formation. This increases catalyst stability, conversion, and selectivity on Pt-Sn/Al2O3 catalysts, with optimal performance at 75% propane concentration. Hydrogen cofeeding reduces coke formation, enhances alkene desorption, and raises the energy barrier for undesired cleavage steps, improving selectivity towards alkene formation \[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]. This effect, observed in the presence of adsorbed hydrogen atoms, suggests that small hydrogen additions can significantly boost process efficiency and performance by favoring desired reactions over coke-producing pathways. Hydrogen cofeeding in PDH suppresses coke formation, maintaining catalyst activity and preventing deactivation \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]. It prevents the formation of coke precursors, crucial for sustaining catalytic activity. Hydrogen can remove reversible coke through hydrogenation, enhancing process efficiency. However, it does not remove already formed coke, highlighting its preventive rather than remedial role.

                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive effects of hydrogen cofeeding on coke formation, catalyst stability, and reaction dynamics in the propane dehydrogenation (PDH) process reveal a multifaceted mechanism that significantly enhances process efficiency and performance. Hydrogen cofeeding's ability to suppress coke formation, akin to water vapor's effect, by maintaining a reducing atmosphere and preventing coke precursor formation, is a critical factor in improving catalyst stability and efficiency. The literature suggests that hydrogen cofeeding can significantly reduce coke formation on catalysts by hydrogenating coke precursor species, thereby enhancing alkene formation while maintaining high alkene selectivity. The presence of Ga in the catalyst further suppresses coke formation, and the optimal hydrogen-to-propane ratio can influence propene selectivity. The alteration of coke deposits into smaller, less defective graphite crystallites by hydrogen cofeeding enhances catalyst stability, conversion, and selectivity, particularly on Pt-Sn/Al2O3 catalysts. The competitive adsorption of hydrogen on the catalyst surface reduces carbon species' surface coverage, decreasing polymerization and coke formation. The raising of energy barriers for undesired cleavage steps by hydrogen cofeeding improves selectivity towards alkene formation, suggesting that small hydrogen additions can significantly boost process efficiency and performance. Hydrogen cofeeding's role in maintaining catalyst activity and preventing deactivation by preventing the formation of coke precursors is crucial for sustaining catalytic activity. The preventive rather than remedial role of hydrogen cofeeding in coke management highlights the importance of early intervention in coke formation. Future research endeavors might focus on optimizing hydrogen cofeeding strategies to further enhance catalyst stability and efficiency, exploring the synergistic effects of hydrogen cofeeding with other additives like Sn and Ga, and investigating the impact of hydrogen cofeeding on the energy barriers, coke accumulation patterns, acid site conversions, and graphitization in PDH processes. The development of advanced catalysts that can effectively utilize hydrogen cofeeding to suppress coke formation and enhance alkene selectivity while maintaining high conversion rates and stability will be a key area of investigation. Additionally, the integration of hydrogen cofeeding with other process optimization techniques, such as temperature and pressure control, could lead to significant improvements in PDH process efficiency and performance.

                    在丙烷脱氢(PDH)过程中，氢气共进料显著抑制焦炭形成，类似于高温下水蒸气的作用，通过维持还原气氛防止焦炭前体的形成\[10.1002/anie.201701115\]。这通过减少失活和再生需求提高了催化剂稳定性和效率，从而可能改善整体过程性能。氢气共进料通过加速表面烷基物种的氢辅助脱氢来增强烯烃的形成，对烯烃选择性影响很小，烯烃选择性保持在接近100%\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。它通过氢化焦炭前体物种显著减少了催化剂上的焦炭形成。催化剂中镓的存在进一步抑制了焦炭形成。当烯烃浓度接近平衡时，随着H2/烷烃比率的增加，烯烃形成速率降低。丙烷脱氢过程中焦炭形成量低于乙烷脱氢，归因于丙基-乙炔比乙基-乙炔更容易氢化。氢气共进料增强了催化剂性能，类似于添加Sn，提高了丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。它改变了焦炭沉积物，促进了更小、缺陷更少的石墨结晶体的形成。通过在催化剂表面的竞争性吸附，氢气抑制了焦炭形成，减少了碳物种的表面覆盖，减少了聚合和焦炭形成。这在Pt-Sn/Al2O3催化剂上增加了催化剂稳定性、转化率和选择性，最佳性能在75%丙烷浓度下。氢气共进料减少了焦炭形成，增强了烯烃的解吸，提高了不希望的裂解步骤的能量屏障，改善了对烯烃形成的选择性\[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]。在吸附氢原子存在下观察到的这种效应表明，小量的氢气添加可以显著提高过程效率和性能，通过有利于所需反应而不是产生焦炭的途径。氢气共进料在PDH中抑制焦炭形成，维持催化剂活性并防止失活\[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]。它防止了焦炭前体的形成，这是维持催化活性的关键。氢气可以通过氢化去除可逆焦炭，从而增强过程效率。然而，它不能去除已经形成的焦炭，突出了其预防而非补救的作用。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 氢气共进料对丙烷脱氢(PDH)过程中焦炭形成、催化剂稳定性和反应动力学的全面影响揭示了一个多方面的机制，显著提高了过程效率和性能。氢气共进料能够抑制焦炭形成，类似于水蒸气的作用，通过维持还原气氛和防止焦炭前体的形成，是提高催化剂稳定性和效率的关键因素。文献表明，氢气共进料可以显著减少催化剂上的焦炭形成，通过氢化焦炭前体物种，从而在保持高烯烃选择性的同时增强烯烃的形成。催化剂中镓的存在进一步抑制了焦炭形成，而最佳的氢/丙烷比率可以影响丙烯选择性。氢气共进料将焦炭沉积物改变为更小、缺陷更少的石墨结晶体，增强了催化剂稳定性、转化率和选择性，特别是在Pt-Sn/Al2O3催化剂上。氢气在催化剂表面的竞争性吸附减少了碳物种的表面覆盖，减少了聚合和焦炭形成。氢气共进料提高不希望的裂解步骤的能量屏障，改善了对烯烃形成的选性，表明小量的氢气添加可以显著提高过程效率和性能。氢气共进料在维持催化剂活性和防止失活方面的作用，通过防止焦炭前体的形成，对于维持催化活性至关重要。氢气共进料在焦炭管理中的预防而非补救作用突出了在焦炭形成早期干预的重要性。未来的研究努力可能集中在优化氢气共进料策略，以进一步增强催化剂稳定性和效率，探索氢气共进料与Sn和Ga等其他添加剂的协同效应，以及调查氢气共进料对PDH过程中能量屏障、焦炭积累模式、酸位点转化和石墨化的影响。开发能够有效利用氢气共进料抑制焦炭形成并增强烯烃选择性，同时保持高转化率和稳定性的先进催化剂将是调查的关键领域。此外，将氢气共进料与温度和压力控制等其他过程优化技术相结合，可能会显著提高PDH过程的效率和性能。
