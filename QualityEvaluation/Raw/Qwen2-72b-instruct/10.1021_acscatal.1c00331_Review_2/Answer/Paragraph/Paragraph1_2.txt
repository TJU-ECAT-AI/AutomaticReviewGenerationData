            <English> 
                    The comprehensive effects and mechanisms of hydrogen cofeeding on coke formation, catalyst stability, and reaction dynamics in the propane dehydrogenation (PDH) process have been elucidated through extensive research \[10.1002/anie.201701115\], \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\], \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\], \[10.1039/c3cp53796j\], \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\], \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\], \[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]. Hydrogen cofeeding suppresses coke formation, akin to water vapor's effect at high temperatures, thereby improving catalyst stability and reducing regeneration needs \[10.1002/anie.201701115\]. It enhances alkene formation by accelerating hydrogen-assisted dehydrogenation of surface alkyl species, with little effect on alkene selectivity, which remains near 100% \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. Hydrogen cofeeding transforms coke deposits, fostering smaller, less defective graphite crystallites, and suppresses coke formation on Pt-based catalysts by competitive adsorption \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. It raises the energy barrier for the third, undesired cleavage step more than for the first two, improving selectivity towards alkene formation \[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]. Hydrogen cofeeding maintains catalyst activity and prevents deactivation by preventing coke precursor formation \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]. It decreases coke on the support while keeping metal coke stable, weakens support acidity, and correlates positively with coke graphitization \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\]. Hydrogen cofeeding reduces coke formation on the support, with a minor effect on the metal, by suppressing coke precursor formation and reducing catalyst deactivation \[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The insights gained from the comprehensive effects of hydrogen cofeeding on the PDH process suggest a promising direction for future research. The ability of hydrogen cofeeding to suppress coke formation, enhance alkene selectivity, and maintain catalyst stability opens avenues for developing more efficient and sustainable PDH processes. Future research might focus on optimizing hydrogen cofeeding conditions to further enhance process efficiency and performance. Additionally, exploring the synergistic effects of hydrogen cofeeding with other process variables, such as temperature and pressure, could lead to the development of novel PDH catalysts and processes with improved selectivity and stability. The role of hydrogen in altering coke deposition patterns and reaction mechanisms also warrants further investigation, potentially leading to the design of catalysts with tailored properties for enhanced PDH performance.

                    通过广泛的研究，氢气共喂入对丙烷脱氢(PDH)过程中焦炭形成、催化剂稳定性和反应动力学的全面影响和机制得到了阐明\[10.1002/anie.201701115\], \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\], \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\], \[10.1039/c3cp53796j\], \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\], \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\], \[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]。氢气共喂入抑制焦炭形成，类似于高温下水蒸气的作用，从而提高催化剂稳定性和减少再生需求\[10.1002/anie.201701115\]。它通过加速表面烷基物种的氢辅助脱氢来增强烯烃的形成，对烯烃选择性的影响很小，烯烃选择性保持在接近100%\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。氢气共喂入改变焦炭沉积物，促进形成较小、缺陷较少的石墨结晶体，并通过竞争吸附抑制Pt基催化剂上的焦炭形成\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。它提高了第三步，不希望的裂解步骤的能量障碍，超过前两步，改善了向烯烃形成的选择性\[10.1039/c3cp53796j\]。氢气共喂入维持催化剂活性和防止失活，通过防止焦炭前体的形成\[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]。它减少了支撑上的焦炭，同时保持金属焦炭稳定，削弱了支撑酸性，并与焦炭石墨化呈正相关\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2\]。氢气共喂入减少了支撑上的焦炭形成，对金属的影响较小，通过抑制焦炭前体的形成和减少催化剂失活\[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 从氢气共喂入对PDH过程的全面影响中获得的见解为未来的研究指明了一个有希望的方向。氢气共喂入抑制焦炭形成、增强烯烃选择性和维持催化剂稳定性的能力为开发更高效、更可持续的PDH过程开辟了途径。未来的研究可能集中在优化氢气共喂入条件，以进一步提高过程效率和性能。此外，探索氢气共喂入与其他过程变量，如温度和压力的协同效应，可能导致开发具有改进选择性和稳定性的新型PDH催化剂和过程。氢气在改变焦炭沉积模式和反应机制中的作用也值得进一步研究，可能导向设计具有增强PDH性能的定制特性的催化剂。
