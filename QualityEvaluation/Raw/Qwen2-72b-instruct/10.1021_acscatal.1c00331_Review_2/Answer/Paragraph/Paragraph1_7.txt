            <English> 
                    The comprehensive effects and mechanisms of hydrogen cofeeding on coke formation, catalyst stability, and reaction dynamics in the propane dehydrogenation (PDH) process are elucidated through a synthesis of insights from references \[10.1002/anie.201701115\], \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\], and \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. Hydrogen cofeeding suppresses coke formation by maintaining a reducing atmosphere that prevents coke precursor formation, akin to water vapor's effect, as detailed in \[10.1002/anie.201701115\]. This improves catalyst stability and efficiency by reducing deactivation and regeneration needs, potentially prolonging catalyst life and enhancing process performance. It boosts alkene formation by enhancing hydrogen-assisted dehydrogenation, with little effect on alkene selectivity, which remains near 100%, as reported in \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. It significantly reduces coke formation by hydrogenating coke precursor species. The presence of Ga in the catalyst further suppresses coke formation. Hydrogen cofeeding enhances catalyst performance, akin to Sn addition, boosting propane conversion and propene selectivity, as described in \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. It alters coke deposits, promoting smaller, less defective graphite crystallites. Competitive adsorption of hydrogen on Pt-based catalysts suppresses coke formation, reducing carbon species surface coverage, and decreasing coke polymerization likelihood. Hydrogen boosts catalyst stability, conversion, and selectivity on Pt-Sn/Al2O3, with optimal performance at 75% propane concentration.
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The insights from the literature on hydrogen cofeeding in PDH suggest a multifaceted approach to enhancing process efficiency and catalyst longevity. Future research might focus on the development of novel catalyst formulations that synergistically combine the benefits of hydrogen cofeeding with other stabilizing elements, such as Sn and Ga, to further suppress coke formation and improve selectivity. Additionally, the exploration of hydrogen cofeeding in conjunction with advanced reactor designs could optimize the distribution of hydrogen and propane, potentially leading to more efficient and sustainable PDH processes. The investigation of the fundamental mechanisms underlying hydrogen's impact on energy barriers, coke accumulation patterns, acid site conversions, and graphitization could also provide new avenues for catalyst design and process optimization. Moreover, the development of in-situ monitoring techniques for real-time coke formation and catalyst activity could offer deeper insights into the dynamic interplay between hydrogen cofeeding and PDH reaction dynamics, guiding the design of more robust and efficient processes.

            <Chinese> 
                    通过综合参考文献\[10.1002/anie.201701115\], \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\], 和\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]的见解，全面阐述了氢气共进料对丙烷脱氢(PDH)过程中焦炭形成、催化剂稳定性和反应动力学的综合影响和机制。氢气共进料通过维持还原气氛来抑制焦炭形成，防止焦炭前体的形成，类似于水蒸气的作用，如\[10.1002/anie.201701115\]中所述。这通过减少失活和再生需求来提高催化剂稳定性和效率，可能延长催化剂寿命并增强过程性能。它通过增强氢辅助脱氢来促进烯烃的形成，对烯烃选择性的影响很小，烯烃选择性保持在接近100%，如\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]中所述。它通过氢化焦炭前体物种显著减少焦炭形成。催化剂中镓的存在进一步抑制焦炭形成。氢气共进料增强催化剂性能，类似于锡的添加，提高丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性，如\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]中所述。它改变焦炭沉积物，促进形成更小、缺陷更少的石墨结晶体。氢在基于铂的催化剂上的竞争性吸附抑制焦炭形成，减少碳物种的表面覆盖，降低焦炭聚合的可能性。氢提高Pt-Sn/Al2O3催化剂的稳定性、转化率和选择性，在75%丙烷浓度下性能最佳。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 文献中关于PDH中氢气共进料的见解表明，提高过程效率和催化剂寿命需要多方面的方法。未来的研究可能集中在开发新型催化剂配方上，这些配方将氢气共进料的好处与其它稳定元素(如Sn和Ga)协同结合，以进一步抑制焦炭形成并提高选择性。此外，探索氢气共进料与先进反应器设计的结合，可以优化氢气和丙烷的分布，可能带来更高效和可持续的PDH过程。研究氢气对能量障碍、焦炭积累模式、酸位点转化和石墨化影响的基本机制，也可能为催化剂设计和过程优化提供新的途径。此外，开发用于实时监测焦炭形成和催化剂活性的原位监测技术，可以为氢气共进料和PDH反应动力学之间的动态相互作用提供更深入的见解，指导设计更坚固和高效的过程。
