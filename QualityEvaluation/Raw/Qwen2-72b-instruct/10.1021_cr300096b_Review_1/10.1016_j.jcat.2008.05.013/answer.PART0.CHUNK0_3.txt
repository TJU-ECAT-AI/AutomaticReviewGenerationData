1.The physical vapor deposition (PVD) technique, specifically thermal evaporation, is a method used to create bimetallic surfaces in ultra-high vacuum (UHV) environments. The experimental setup involves a two-level stainless steel UHV chamber equipped with an Auger electron spectrometer (AES) for surface characterization and a quadrupole mass spectrometer (MS) for temperature-programmed desorption (TPD) experiments. A Pt(111) single-crystal disk is spot-welded to tantalum posts and can be resistively heated or cooled. The surface is cleaned by Ne+ sputter cycles and oxygen dosing, followed by modification with a 3d metal using physical vapor deposition. The 3d metal is deposited by wrapping a wire around a tungsten filament, which is then resistively heated to vaporize the metal and deposit it onto the Pt(111) surface. The quantity of metal deposited is determined by the 3d (LMM)/Pt (241 eV) AES peak-to-peak ratio. The deposition rate is controlled to achieve a monolayer coverage. This method facilitates surface science studies by allowing the creation of well-defined bimetallic surfaces with controlled composition and structure. Key considerations for achieving optimal results include maintaining a high vacuum, controlling the deposition rate, and accurately monitoring the surface composition using AES.

1.物理气相沉积（PVD）技术，特别是热蒸发，是在超高真空（UHV）环境中创建双金属表面的方法。实验装置涉及一个两级不锈钢UHV室，配备有Auger电子光谱仪（AES）用于表面表征和四极质量光谱仪（MS）用于温度编程脱附（TPD）实验。Pt(111)单晶圆盘被点焊到钽柱上，可以进行电阻加热或冷却。表面通过Ne+溅射循环和氧气剂量进行清洁，然后使用物理气相沉积进行3d金属的改性。3d金属通过将金属丝缠绕在钨丝上，然后电阻加热以蒸发金属并将其沉积到Pt(111)表面上来沉积。沉积的金属量由3d (LMM)/Pt (241 eV) AES峰峰比确定。控制沉积速率以实现单层覆盖。该方法通过允许创建具有受控组成和结构的明确定义的双金属表面来促进表面科学研究。为了获得最佳结果，关键考虑因素包括保持高真空，控制沉积速率，并使用AES准确监测表面组成。
