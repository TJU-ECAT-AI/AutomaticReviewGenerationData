1.The physical vapor deposition (PVD) technique, specifically thermal evaporation, is a method used to create bimetallic surfaces in ultra-high vacuum (UHV) environments. The experimental setup typically involves a UHV chamber equipped with an Auger electron spectrometer (AES) for surface characterization and a quadrupole mass spectrometer (MS) for temperature-programmed desorption (TPD) experiments. A single-crystal substrate, such as Pt(111), is resistively heated and modified by depositing a 3d metal using physical vapor deposition. The quantity of metal deposited is determined by the 3d (LMM)/Pt (241 eV) AES peak-to-peak ratio. The deposition rate and temperature are critical process parameters, with deposition at room temperature resulting in a surface monolayer of the 3d metal and deposition at elevated temperatures leading to the formation of a surface alloy enriched in Pt. This method facilitates surface science studies by allowing the creation of well-defined bimetallic surfaces for the investigation of their catalytic properties. To ensure the deposition of high-purity metal overlayers, the gases used for dosing must be of high purity and the UHV chamber must have a base pressure of . The surfaces are cleaned by several Ne+ sputter cycles and oxygen dosing before modification. The AES quantification of the physical vapor deposition and thermal behavior of the deposited metals is essential for determining the coverage and stability of the bimetallic surfaces.

1.物理气相沉积（PVD）技术，特别是热蒸发，是在超高真空（UHV）环境中创建双金属表面的方法。实验设置通常涉及配备有Auger电子光谱仪（AES）进行表面表征和四极质量光谱仪（MS）进行温度编程脱附（TPD）实验的UHV室。使用单晶基底，如Pt(111)，通过电阻加热并使用物理气相沉积来修改3d金属。沉积金属的数量由3d (LMM)/Pt (241 eV) AES峰到峰比确定。沉积速率和温度是关键的工艺参数，室温沉积导致3d金属的表面单层，而高温沉积导致形成Pt富集的表面合金。该方法通过允许创建明确定义的双金属表面来促进表面科学研究，用于研究其催化性质。为了确保沉积高纯度金属覆盖层，用于剂量的气体必须具有高纯度，UHV室必须具有基本压力。在修改之前，表面通过多次Ne+溅射循环和氧气剂量进行清洁。AES量化物理气相沉积和沉积金属的热行为对于确定双金属表面的覆盖和稳定性至关重要。
