1.The physical vapor deposition (PVD) technique, specifically thermal evaporation, is a method used to create bimetallic surfaces in ultra-high vacuum (UHV) environments. The experimental setup involves a two-level stainless steel UHV chamber equipped with an Auger electron spectrometer (AES) for surface characterization and a quadrupole mass spectrometer (MS) for temperature-programmed desorption (TPD) experiments. A Pt(111) single-crystal disk is spot-welded to tantalum posts and can be resistively heated or cooled. The surface is cleaned by Ne+ sputter cycles and oxygen dosing, followed by modification with a 3d metal using physical vapor deposition. A 0.1 mm diameter wire of the desired 3d metal is wrapped around a 0.5 mm diameter tungsten wire, wound into a filament, and enclosed in a stainless steel shield. The filament is resistively heated to vaporize the 3d metal, which is then deposited onto the Pt(111) surface. The quantity of metal deposited is determined by the 3d (LMM)/Pt (241 eV) AES peak-to-peak ratio. The deposition rate is controlled to achieve a monolayer coverage. The thermal behavior of the deposited metal is monitored by AES scans after heating the surface to determine changes in surface composition. This method facilitates surface science studies by allowing the creation of well-defined bimetallic surfaces with controlled composition and structure. Key considerations for achieving optimal results include maintaining a high vacuum, controlling the deposition rate, and accurately monitoring the surface composition.

1.物理气相沉积（PVD）技术，特别是热蒸发，是在超高真空（UHV）环境中创建双金属表面的方法。实验装置包括一个两级不锈钢UHV室，配备有Auger电子光谱仪（AES）用于表面表征，以及四极质量光谱仪（MS）用于温度编程脱附（TPD）实验。Pt(111)单晶圆盘被点焊到钽柱上，可以进行电阻加热或冷却。表面通过Ne+溅射循环和氧气剂量进行清洁，然后使用物理气相沉积进行3d金属的改性。将所需3d金属的0.1毫米直径的线缠绕在0.5毫米直径的钨线上，卷成灯丝，并封闭在不锈钢护套中。通过电阻加热灯丝使3d金属气化，然后沉积到Pt(111)表面上。通过3d (LMM)/Pt (241 eV) AES峰峰比确定沉积的金属量。控制沉积速率以实现单层覆盖。通过AES扫描后加热表面来监测沉积金属的热行为，以确定表面组成的变化。该方法通过允许创建具有受控组成和结构的明确定义的双金属表面，促进了表面科学的研究。为了获得最佳结果，关键考虑因素包括保持高真空，控制沉积速率，以及准确监测表面组成。
