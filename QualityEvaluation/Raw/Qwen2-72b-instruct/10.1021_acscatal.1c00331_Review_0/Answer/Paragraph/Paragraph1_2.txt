            <English> 
                    The catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) is a pivotal process for propene production, with Pt-based catalysts playing a central role \[10.1016/j.catcom.2007.08.015\]. However, the efficiency of these catalysts is significantly impacted by coke formation, which not only reduces activity but also alters selectivity \[10.1039/d0cp04669h\]. The size of Pt nanoparticles is critical, with smaller particles (3nm) exhibiting higher activity but lower C3H6 selectivity and higher coking rates compared to larger particles (21nm) \[10.1016/j.catcom.2007.08.015\]. Research efforts are directed towards enhancing the dehydrogenation rate while minimizing cracking and coking \[10.1016/j.catcom.2007.08.015\]. Strategies to mitigate coke formation include the use of Pt alloys, transition metals, and optimized supports \[10.1039/d0cs00983k\]. Phosphate and regeneration techniques have also been shown to mitigate coking \[10.1039/d0cp04669h\]. The addition of Cu to Pt catalysts has been found to suppress coke formation, enhancing stability and selectivity \[10.1039/c4nr02143f\]. Moreover, the use of single-atom Pt catalysts on Cu nanoparticles and doped graphene has been proposed to mitigate coking issues, optimizing metal use, activity, and stability \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03286\].

                    ※※※

                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in PDH catalysis, particularly the strategies to mitigate coke formation on Pt-based catalysts, have opened new avenues for research. Future directions might focus on the development of novel catalysts with enhanced coking resistance and improved selectivity. The exploration of single-atom catalysts and the use of doped graphene for Pt stabilization present promising paths. Additionally, the optimization of reaction conditions, such as the introduction of H2 or steam, could further prolong catalyst regeneration time. The integration of advanced characterization techniques and computational methods could provide deeper insights into the deactivation mechanisms, guiding the design of more efficient and stable catalysts for PDH processes.

                    直接脱氢丙烷（PDH）催化过程是生产丙烯的关键，其中基于Pt的催化剂扮演着核心角色\[10.1016/j.catcom.2007.08.015\]。然而，这些催化剂的效率受到焦炭形成的重大影响，这不仅降低了活性，还改变了选择性\[10.1039/d0cp04669h\]。Pt纳米颗粒的大小至关重要，较小的颗粒（3nm）表现出更高的活性，但C3H6选择性较低，焦炭形成率也更高，与较大的颗粒（21nm）相比\[10.1016/j.catcom.2007.08.015\]。研究工作正朝着提高脱氢速率同时最小化裂解和焦炭形成的方向发展\[10.1016/j.catcom.2007.08.015\]。缓解焦炭形成的策略包括使用Pt合金、过渡金属和优化的载体\[10.1039/d0cs00983k\]。磷酸盐和再生技术也被证明可以缓解焦化\[10.1039/d0cp04669h\]。在Pt催化剂中添加Cu被发现可以抑制焦炭形成，增强稳定性和选择性\[10.1039/c4nr02143f\]。此外，提出了在Cu纳米颗粒和掺杂石墨烯上使用单原子Pt催化剂来缓解焦炭问题，优化金属使用、活性和稳定性\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03286\]。

                    ※※※

                    综合理解与未来展望：PDH催化领域，特别是缓解基于Pt催化剂上的焦炭形成策略的进展，为研究开辟了新的方向。未来的研究方向可能集中在开发具有增强抗焦炭性和改进选择性的新型催化剂。探索单原子催化剂和使用掺杂石墨烯稳定Pt提供了有希望的路径。此外，优化反应条件，如引入H2或蒸汽，可以进一步延长催化剂再生时间。将先进的表征技术和计算方法的整合可以提供对失活机制的更深入理解，指导设计更高效和稳定的PDH过程催化剂。
