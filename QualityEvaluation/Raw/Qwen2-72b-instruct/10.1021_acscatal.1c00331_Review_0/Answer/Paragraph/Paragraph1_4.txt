Comprehensive analysis of catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) for propene production: Process characteristics, catalyst performance, and the critical challenge of coke formation on Pt-based catalysts.

The catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) is a pivotal process for propene production, with a significant focus on optimizing process conditions, catalyst types, and addressing the critical challenge of coke formation on Pt-based catalysts \[10.1016/j.catcom.2007.08.015\]. PDH yields propene, with key factors including temperature and pressure \[10.1039/d0cp04669h\]. Pt and Cr catalysts face the issue of coke formation, impacting efficiency \[10.1039/d0cs01262a\]. Phosphate and coking-resistant catalysts mitigate coke formation, enhancing regeneration \[10.1039/d0cp04669h\]. The process follows the reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism, with propyne aiding CC bond break \[10.1039/d0cs01262a\]. ZrO2 boosts propene production, mitigating coke on Pt-catalysts via in situ regeneration \[10.1038/s41467-018-06174-5\]. PtSn catalysts with water vapor show low coke formation (<1wt%) at 680°C, impacting conversion \[10.1002/anie.201701115\]. Strategies to mitigate coke formation include optimizing H2, H2O, hydrocarbon pressure, space time, linear velocity, and improving catalyst composition \[10.1039/d0cs00983k\]. Pt/Cu single atom alloys boost propene yield, mitigate coke formation, and regeneration restores activity \[10.1038/s41467-018-06967-8\]. PtZn4 in Pt1M1 IMA SACs boosts selectivity and stability, preventing coke formation \[10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008\]. PtGaPb/SiO2 catalysts achieve high propene selectivity and stability at high temperatures, minimizing coke \[10.1038/s41467-020-16693-9\].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements and findings discussed in the file, there lies an opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel catalysts that can further mitigate coke formation while maintaining high selectivity and activity. The exploration of single-atom catalysts and intermetallic alloys, as demonstrated by PtZn4 and PtGaPb/SiO2, presents a promising direction for enhancing PDH efficiency \[10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008, 10.1038/s41467-020-16693-9\]. Additionally, the optimization of reaction conditions, such as pressure and temperature, in conjunction with the use of water vapor and hydrogen, could lead to more efficient and sustainable PDH processes \[10.1002/anie.201701115, 10.1039/d0cs00983k\]. The integration of advanced computational methods, such as density functional theory (DFT), could provide deeper insights into the mechanisms of coke formation and guide the design of more effective catalysts \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_0\]. Moreover, the development of in situ regeneration techniques, as exemplified by the use of ZrO2 for in situ regeneration \[10.1038/s41467-018-06174-5\], could significantly enhance the longevity and efficiency of PDH catalysts. These directions hold the potential to revolutionize the PDH process, leading to more sustainable and economically viable propene production.

催化直接脱氢丙烷（PDH）是丙烯生产的关键过程，重点关注优化过程条件、催化剂类型和解决Pt基催化剂上关键的焦炭形成挑战\[10.1016/j.catcom.2007.08.015\]。PDH产生丙烯，关键因素包括温度和压力\[10.1039/d0cp04669h\]。Pt和Cr催化剂面临焦炭形成问题，影响效率\[10.1039/d0cs01262a\]。磷酸盐和抗焦炭催化剂减轻焦炭形成，增强再生\[10.1039/d0cp04669h\]。该过程遵循逆Horiuti-Polanyi机制，丙炔有助于CC键断裂\[10.1039/d0cs01262a\]。ZrO2增强丙烯生产，通过原位再生减轻Pt催化剂上的焦炭\[10.1038/s41467-018-06174-5\]。在680°C下，PtSn催化剂与水蒸气显示出低焦炭形成（<1wt%），影响转化率\[10.1002/anie.201701115\]。减轻焦炭形成的策略包括优化H2、H2O、烃压力、空间时间、线性速度和改进催化剂组成\[10.1039/d0cs00983k\]。Pt/Cu单原子合金增强丙烯产量，减轻焦炭形成，再生恢复活性\[10.1038/s41467-018-06967-8\]。PtZn4在Pt1M1 IMA SACs中增强选择性和稳定性，防止焦炭形成\[10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008\]。PtGaPb/SiO2催化剂在高温下实现高丙烯选择性和稳定性，最小化焦炭\[10.1038/s41467-020-16693-9\]。

综合理解与未来展望：考虑到文件中讨论的先进成果和发现，我们有机会探索新兴领域和创新方法。未来的研究努力可能会集中在开发可以进一步减轻焦炭形成同时保持高选择性和活性的新型催化剂。PtZn4和PtGaPb/SiO2展示的单原子催化剂和金属间合金的探索为增强PDH效率提供了有希望的方向\[10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008, 10.1038/s41467-020-16693-9\]。此外，结合使用水蒸气和氢气，优化压力和温度等反应条件，可以导致更有效和可持续的PDH过程\[10.1002/anie.201701115, 10.1039/d0cs00983k\]。先进的计算方法，如密度泛函理论（DFT）的集成，可以为焦炭形成机制提供更深入的见解，并指导更有效催化剂的设计\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_0\]。此外，如ZrO2用于原位再生所示的原位再生技术的发展\[10.1038/s41467-018-06174-5\]，可以显著增强PDH催化剂的寿命和效率。这些方向具有革命化PDH过程的潜力，导致更可持续和经济上可行的丙烯生产。
