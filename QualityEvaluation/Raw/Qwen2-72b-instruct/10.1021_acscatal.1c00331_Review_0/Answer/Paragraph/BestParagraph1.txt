The catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) is a pivotal process for propene production, with a significant focus on optimizing process conditions, catalyst types, and addressing the critical challenge of coke formation on Pt-based catalysts \[10.1016/j.catcom.2007.08.015\]. PDH yields propene, with key factors including temperature and pressure \[10.1039/d0cp04669h\]. Pt and Cr catalysts face the issue of coke formation, impacting efficiency \[10.1039/d0cs01262a\]. Phosphate and coking-resistant catalysts mitigate coke formation, enhancing regeneration \[10.1039/d0cp04669h\]. The process follows the reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism, with propyne aiding CC bond break \[10.1039/d0cs01262a\]. ZrO2 boosts propene production, mitigating coke on Pt-catalysts via in situ regeneration \[10.1038/s41467-018-06174-5\]. PtSn catalysts with water vapor show low coke formation (<1wt%) at 680°C, impacting conversion \[10.1002/anie.201701115\]. Strategies to mitigate coke formation include optimizing H2, H2O, hydrocarbon pressure, space time, linear velocity, and improving catalyst composition \[10.1039/d0cs00983k\]. Pt/Cu single atom alloys boost propene yield, mitigate coke formation, and regeneration restores activity \[10.1038/s41467-018-06967-8\]. PtZn4 in Pt1M1 IMA SACs boosts selectivity and stability, preventing coke formation \[10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008\]. PtGaPb/SiO2 catalysts achieve high propene selectivity and stability at high temperatures, minimizing coke \[10.1038/s41467-020-16693-9\].
