<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1.The catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) is a key industrial reaction for the production of propylene. It is normally catalyzed by PtM alloys such as PtSn, PtZn, PtGa, PtIn, PtGe, and PtCu. The reaction conditions typically involve atmospheric pressure, temperatures around 520°C, and a propane-to-nitrogen ratio of 1:1. The selectivity and conversion rate of propane to propylene are crucial considerations. Coke formation on Pt-based catalysts can lead to deactivation and reduced efficiency. Strategies to mitigate coke formation include using single atom alloys (SAAs) like Pt/Cu, which can maintain high propylene selectivity and stability even after 120 hours on stream. The Pt/Cu SAA catalyst shows excellent stability and anti-coke capability, with a high formation rate of propylene and low deactivation rate.
