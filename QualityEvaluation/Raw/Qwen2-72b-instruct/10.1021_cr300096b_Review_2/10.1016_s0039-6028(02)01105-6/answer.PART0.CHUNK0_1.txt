1.The document does not provide information on the characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces, synthesized by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates. The document focuses on the characterization of Fe/Pt bimetallic surfaces synthesized by depositing Fe onto a Pt(111) single crystal at 350 K in ultrahigh vacuum. The characterization was conducted using X-ray electron spectroscopy, low energy electron diffraction (LEED), and low energy ion scattering. This process is crucial for understanding the alloying that occurs upon deposition, the diffusion of Fe into the near-surface region upon heating, and the formation of a PtFe alloy surface layer. The thermal stability of these Fe films and the concentration of Fe in the topmost layer were characterized by X-ray electron spectroscopy (XPS) and low energy ion scattering (LEIS). The (2×2) LEED pattern observed after annealing indicates the formation of an ordered PtFe alloy at the surface containing 0.5-ML Fe. This procedure produces Fe-containing alloy surfaces that are more thermally stable against Pt surface segregation than expected from the behavior of Pt80Fe20(1 1 1) bulk crystals, and significant amounts of Fe are stabilized in the surface layer until temperatures as high as 950 K.
