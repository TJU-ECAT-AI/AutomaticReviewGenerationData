          The literature does not provide information on the characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces synthesized by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates. However, it does discuss the study of ultrathin metal overlayers on W(111) by high-resolution photoelectron spectroscopy using synchrotron radiation. The bimetallic systems are prepared by adsorbing approximately one monolayer of K, Co, Ni, Pd, Pt, Cu, Ag and Au on W(111) at 100 K. The W 4f7/2 core levels from the W(111) substrate are measured. The characterization of these bimetallic surfaces is crucial for understanding the electronic and chemical properties of the interfaces, which is important for surface science and catalysis research. The process is crucial for understanding the electronic properties of the bimetallic systems, as it allows for the identification of the interfacial W 4f7/2 binding energies and the correlation between these energies and the heats of adsorption for metal films on W(111). It is found that the core-level W 4f7/2 binding energy shifts originating from W atoms at the interface correlate well with the heats of adsorption for metal films on W(111). This process is crucial for understanding the electronic and chemical properties of these bimetallic surfaces in the context of advanced surface science and catalysis research.
