                Comprehensive characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces, synthesized by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) or polycrystalline Pt, is crucial for structural modeling and DFT calculations, enhancing our understanding of their electronic and chemical properties \[10.1021/jp061697z\]. At 300 K, reactivity is similar to Ni surfaces, but after annealing to 600 K, unique surface activity emerges, unlike Ni, Co, or Pt(111), due to Ni diffusion into the subsurface region, creating PtNiPt(111) structures \[10.1021/jp061697z\]. This process is essential for advanced surface science and catalysis research, providing insights into electronic and chemical properties \[10.1021/cr300096b_2\]. Techniques like LEIS, AES, XPS, and STM confirm the Ni monolayer presence and Ni diffusion into the subsurface after annealing, suggesting a PtNiPt(111) structure \[10.1021/cr300096b_2\]. This detailed characterization is essential for modeling and computational studies, providing insights into structural and electronic properties of Ni/Pt surfaces, critical for catalysis and other surface science applications \[10.1016/s0039-6028(01)00696-3\].
