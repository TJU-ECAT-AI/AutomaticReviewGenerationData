<English>
The comprehensive characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces, synthesized by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) or polycrystalline Pt substrates, is pivotal for structural modeling, DFT calculations, and understanding their electronic and chemical properties in advanced surface science and catalysis research \[10.1021/jp061697z\]. This process elucidates the effects of subsurface Ni on surface properties, as seen in the cyclohexene reaction pathways, which demonstrate the role of subsurface Ni in low-temperature hydrogenation activity \[10.1016/j.susc.2005.03.046\]. It also aids in determining surface structure, electronic properties, and reactivity, crucial for predicting and controlling catalytic activity and selectivity in various reactions \[10.1016/j.susc.2004.03.032\]. Understanding the PtNi system fundamentally, particularly in hydrocarbon hydrogenation and dehydrogenation, is a key outcome of this characterization \[10.1016/s0039-6028(01)00696-3\].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The detailed characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces, synthesized by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) or polycrystalline Pt substrates, has provided significant insights into their electronic and chemical properties, crucial for advanced surface science and catalysis research. The process of subsurface Ni diffusion and its impact on surface properties, as observed in the cyclohexene reaction pathways, highlights the role of subsurface Ni in low-temperature hydrogenation activity. This understanding is pivotal for predicting and controlling catalytic activity and selectivity in various reactions, particularly in hydrocarbon hydrogenation and dehydrogenation.

Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel characterization techniques that can provide real-time monitoring of surface changes during catalytic reactions. This could include advanced in-situ spectroscopic methods and high-resolution electron microscopy. Additionally, the integration of machine learning algorithms with DFT calculations could enhance the predictive capabilities of computational models, allowing for the rational design of bimetallic catalysts with improved activity and selectivity. The exploration of other bimetallic systems, beyond Ni/Pt, could also offer new insights into the effects of subsurface metal diffusion on surface properties and catalytic activity. This could potentially lead to the discovery of new catalysts with enhanced performance for various chemical reactions.

Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces, synthesized by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) or polycrystalline Pt substrates, 的全面表征对于结构建模、DFT计算以及理解它们在高级表面科学和催化研究中的电子和化学性质至关重要\[10.1021/jp061697z\]。这一过程揭示了次表面Ni对表面性质的影响，如在环己烯反应途径中所见，展示了次表面Ni在低温氢化活性中的作用\[10.1016/j.susc.2005.03.046\]。它还有助于确定表面结构、电子性质和反应性，对于预测和控制各种反应中的催化活性和选择性至关重要\[10.1016/j.susc.2004.03.032\]。从根本上理解PtNi系统，特别是在碳氢化合物氢化和脱氢中的作用，是这一表征的关键成果\[10.1016/s0039-6028(01)00696-3\]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：通过在Pt(111)或多晶Pt基底上沉积Ni单层合成的Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces的详细表征，为它们的电子和化学性质提供了重要见解，这对于高级表面科学和催化研究至关重要。次表面Ni扩散的过程及其对表面性质的影响，如在环己烯反应途径中所观察到的，突出了次表面Ni在低温氢化活性中的作用。这种理解对于预测和控制各种反应中的催化活性和选择性至关重要，特别是在碳氢化合物氢化和脱氢中。

未来的研究工作可能集中在开发新型表征技术，可以实时监测催化反应期间的表面变化。这可能包括先进的原位光谱方法和高分辨率电子显微镜。此外，将机器学习算法与DFT计算相结合，可以增强计算模型的预测能力，允许合理设计具有改进活性和选择性的bimetallic催化剂。探索Ni/Pt以外的其他bimetallic系统，也可能为次表面金属扩散对表面性质和催化活性的影响提供新的见解。这可能最终导致发现具有增强性能的各种化学反应的新催化剂。
