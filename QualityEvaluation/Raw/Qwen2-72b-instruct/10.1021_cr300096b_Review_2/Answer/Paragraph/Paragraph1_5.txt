        <English> 
                The comprehensive characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces, synthesized by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) or polycrystalline Pt substrates, is pivotal for structural modeling, DFT calculations, and understanding their electronic and chemical properties \[10.1021/jp061697z\]. This process reveals unique surface activity at 600 K, differing from 300 K synthesis, where reactivity is similar to Ni surfaces. The 600 K surfaces exhibit activity distinct from Ni, Co, or Pt(111), attributed to Ni diffusion into the subsurface region, forming PtNiPt(111) structures \[10.1021/jp061697z\]. Structural modeling and DFT calculations elucidate the impact of subsurface Ni on surface properties, as seen in the cyclohexene reaction pathways on Pt/Ni(111) surfaces, revealing subsurface Ni's role in low-temperature hydrogenation activity \[10.1016/j.susc.2004.03.032\]. This process also aids in predicting and controlling catalytic activity and selectivity in various chemical reactions, offering insights into the fundamental effects of the PtNi system in hydrocarbon catalysis \[10.1016/j.susc.2004.03.032\].

                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The detailed analysis of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces, synthesized by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt substrates, is essential for structural modeling, DFT calculations, and understanding their electronic and chemical properties \[10.1021/j100089a040\]. This process elucidates the impact of subsurface Ni on surface properties, as seen in the cyclohexene reaction pathways on Pt/Ni(111) surfaces, revealing subsurface Ni's role in low-temperature hydrogenation activity \[10.1016/j.susc.2004.03.032\]. The comprehensive characterization of these surfaces is pivotal for advanced surface science and catalysis research, enabling a comprehensive grasp of bimetallic surface properties \[10.1021/j100089a040\]. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of advanced characterization techniques that can provide real-time monitoring of surface reactions and the dynamic behavior of bimetallic surfaces under catalytic conditions. This could potentially lead to the design of more efficient and selective catalysts for various chemical reactions, including hydrocarbon hydrogenation and dehydrogenation.
