1.The characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces, synthesized by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates, is crucial for structural modeling, DFT calculations, and our comprehensive understanding of the electronic and chemical properties of these bimetallic surfaces in the context of advanced surface science and catalysis research. This process allows us to determine the precise atomic structure of the bimetallic surfaces, which is essential for accurate DFT calculations. The structural information obtained through characterization techniques such as Auger electron spectroscopy (AES), temperature-programmed desorption (TPD), and high-resolution electron energy loss spectroscopy (HREELS) provides the necessary input for DFT modeling. This modeling, in turn, helps us understand the electronic properties of the surfaces, such as the d-band center, which is crucial for predicting the adsorption energies of molecules like hydrogen and ethylene. The combination of experimental characterization and theoretical calculations enables a deeper understanding of the catalytic properties of bimetallic surfaces, facilitating the rational design of catalysts with improved activity and selectivity.

1.通过在Pt(111)单晶或多晶Pt基底上沉积Ni单层来合成Ni/Pt双金属表面的表征对于结构建模、DFT计算以及我们对这些双金属表面在高级表面科学和催化研究背景下的电子和化学性质的全面理解至关重要。这一过程使我们能够确定双金属表面的精确原子结构，这对于准确的DFT计算至关重要。通过Auger电子光谱（AES）、温度程序脱附（TPD）和高分辨率电子能量损失光谱（HREELS）等表征技术获得的结构信息为DFT建模提供了必要的输入。反过来，这种建模帮助我们理解表面的电子性质，如d带中心，这对于预测像氢和乙烯这样的分子的吸附能至关重要。实验表征和理论计算的结合使我们能够更深入地理解双金属表面的催化性质，从而促进具有改进的活性和选择性的催化剂的合理设计。
