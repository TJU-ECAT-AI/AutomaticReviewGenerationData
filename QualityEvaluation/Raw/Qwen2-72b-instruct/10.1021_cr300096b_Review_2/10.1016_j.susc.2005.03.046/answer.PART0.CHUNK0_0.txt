<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1.The characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces, synthesized by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates, is conducted through a combination of experimental techniques and theoretical calculations. This process is crucial for structural modeling, DFT calculations, and our comprehensive understanding of the electronic and chemical properties of these bimetallic surfaces in the context of advanced surface science and catalysis research because it allows for the validation of theoretical predictions with experimental data. By comparing the results from DFT modeling with surface science experiments, researchers can gain insights into the unique chemical properties of bimetallic surfaces, such as the Ni/Pt system, which displays novel catalytic behavior due to the interaction between the subsurface Ni and the surface Pt atoms. This interaction leads to a shift in the d-band center of the surface Pt atoms, resulting in a reduction in the adsorption energy of hydrogen and olefins compared to pure Pt or Ni surfaces. The combination of DFT and experimental techniques provides a powerful tool for the rational design of bimetallic catalysts with improved activity and selectivity for various chemical reactions, including the low-temperature hydrogenation of olefins.

1.通过在Pt(111)单晶或多晶Pt基底上沉积Ni单层来合成Ni/Pt双金属表面的表征是通过实验技术和理论计算的组合进行的。这一过程对于结构建模、DFT计算以及我们在高级表面科学和催化研究背景下对这些双金属表面的电子和化学性质的全面理解至关重要，因为它允许将理论预测与实验数据进行验证。通过比较DFT建模的结果与表面科学实验的结果，研究人员可以深入了解双金属表面的独特化学性质，例如Ni/Pt系统，由于表面Ni和表面Pt原子之间的相互作用，显示出新颖的催化行为。这种相互作用导致表面Pt原子的d带中心的偏移，导致氢和烯烃的吸附能与纯Pt或Ni表面相比降低。DFT和实验技术的结合为合理设计具有改进的活性和选择性的双金属催化剂提供了强大的工具，用于各种化学反应，包括烯烃的低温氢化。
