1.The characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces, synthesized by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates, is conducted through a series of advanced surface science techniques and theoretical calculations. This process is crucial for structural modeling, DFT calculations, and our comprehensive understanding of the electronic and chemical properties of these bimetallic surfaces in the context of advanced surface science and catalysis research. The detailed structural information obtained from surface science techniques allows for accurate modeling of the bimetallic surfaces, which is essential for DFT calculations. These calculations provide insights into the electronic structure and bonding characteristics of the bimetallic surfaces, which are critical for understanding their catalytic properties. The combination of experimental data and theoretical calculations enables a comprehensive understanding of the electronic and chemical properties of the bimetallic surfaces, which is essential for the rational design of advanced catalysts.
