1.The literature does not provide information on the significance of selective dehydrogenation reactions in chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage, nor does it discuss the contributions of reactor studies and UHV studies to our understanding of these processes. The literature focuses on the dehydrogenation of cyclohexane to benzene over Ni supported on activated carbon cloth (ACC) catalysts under spray-pulsed mode reactor conditions. The maximum rate of hydrogen evolution using monometallic Ni/ACC catalysts was 8.5 mmol g-1cat min-1 for a Ni loading of 20% by weight. The addition of a small amount of Pt (0.5 wt%) to the Ni-based catalysts resulted in a synergistic effect, enhancing hydrogen production by approximately 1.5 times compared to the 20 wt% Ni only catalyst. The 20 wt% Ni0.5 wt% Pt bimetallic catalyst exhibited approximately 60 times higher hydrogen production rates compared to the 0.5 wt% monometallic Pt catalyst. The selectivity towards dehydrogenation was observed to be enhanced by the addition of Pt into Ni/ACC catalysts.
