The literature focuses on the preparation and characterization of SiO2-supported PtFe catalysts and their catalytic properties in various reactions, including the oxidation of CO, the dehydrogenation of cyclohexane, and the selective hydrogenation of citral. The key research methods include high-resolution transmission electron microscopy (HRTEM), Fourier transform infrared (FTIR) spectroscopy, and extended X-ray absorption fine structure (EXAFS) spectroscopy. The reaction conditions investigated include temperatures ranging from 25 to 300 degrees Celsius and pressures ranging from atmospheric to 7 MPa. The studies inform the optimization of catalytic processes by providing insights into the structure-reactivity relationships of the bimetallic PtFe system.
