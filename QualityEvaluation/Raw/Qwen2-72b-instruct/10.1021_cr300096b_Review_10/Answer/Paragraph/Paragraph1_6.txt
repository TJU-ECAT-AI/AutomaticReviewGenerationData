                The significance of selective dehydrogenation reactions in chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage is underscored by their pivotal role in generating valuable chemicals, fuels, and a method for hydrogen storage with higher volumetric energy density than hydrogen gas \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. Reactor studies and UHV studies significantly contribute to our understanding of these processes by providing insights into the performance of supported catalysts under reaction conditions \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. These studies typically investigate synthesis methods, characterization techniques, and dehydrogenation temperatures for C-H and N-H bond dehydrogenation \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The characterization techniques include N2 adsorption, TEM, XRD, TPR, TPO, H2 chemisorption, XPS, AES, TPD, and CO chemisorption \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The reaction conditions are varied to understand the effects of temperature, pressure, and reactant composition on catalytic activity and selectivity \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. UHV studies, in particular, allow for the examination of surface reactions at the atomic level, which is essential for understanding the fundamental mechanisms of dehydrogenation \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The volcano relationship between nitrogen-binding energy and ammonia conversion, as well as the comparison of ammonia decomposition activity on different surfaces, highlight the importance of surface chemistry in dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The use of hydrazine monohydrate as a hydrogen-storage material and the selective dehydrogenation of hydrazine over PtNi nanocatalysts demonstrate the potential of dehydrogenation for hydrogen storage applications \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The dehydration of alkanes for polymer, ether, and gasoline production, and the use of dehydrogenation for hydrogen storage, emphasize the versatility of dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The enhancement of dehydrogenation activity and selectivity through the modification of Pt catalysts with other metals, such as Au, Ni, Sn, and Bi, illustrates the role of bimetallic interactions in improving catalytic performance \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The synergistic effect of bimetallic catalysts on dehydrogenation, as observed in the enhanced hydrogen production on PtNi/AC, underscores the importance of alloying in dehydrogenation catalyst design \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The DFT calculations and experimental studies of alkenes on Sn/Pt alloys demonstrate the selectivity enhancement through the presence of surface Sn \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The decrease in deactivation rate due to the lower binding energy of unsaturated hydrocarbons on bimetallic particles and the improvement of catalytic activity through pretreatment of PtSn catalysts with H2 and O2 highlight the strategies for enhancing catalyst stability and activity \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The promotion of dehydrogenation reactions by other metals, such as Re, Pd, In, and Fe, when alloyed with Pt, and the utilization of template synthesis procedures for the formulation of high-activity catalysts, such as PtRe/γ-Al2O3 for naphtha dehydrogenation, showcase the innovation in catalyst design \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The enhanced stability of bimetallic catalysts, such as PtNi and PtPd, for diesel dehydrogenation, and the improved sulfur tolerance and overall catalytic performance due to strong metal-metal and metal-support interactions, demonstrate the potential of bimetallic catalysts for fuel reforming applications \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The superiority of PtRh supported on ZrO2 over monometallic catalysts for gasoline dehydrogenation, in terms of activity, selectivity, and deactivation rate, highlights the importance of support selection in dehydrogenation catalyst design \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\].

                选择性脱氢反应在化学生产、燃料合成和氢气储存中的重要性，通过其在生成有价值的化学品、燃料以及提供比氢气更高的体积能量密度的氢气储存方法中的核心作用得以凸显\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。反应器研究和UHV研究对理解这些过程做出了重大贡献，通过提供在反应条件下支持催化剂性能的见解\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。这些研究通常调查合成方法、表征技术以及C-H和N-H键脱氢的脱氢温度\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。表征技术包括N2吸附、TEM、XRD、TPR、TPO、H2化学吸附、XPS、AES、TPD和CO化学吸附\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。反应条件的变化是为了理解温度、压力和反应物组成对催化活性和选择性的影响\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。特别是，UHV研究允许在原子水平上检查表面反应，这对于理解脱氢反应的基本机制至关重要\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。氮结合能与氨转化之间的火山关系，以及在不同表面上比较氨分解活性，突出了表面化学在脱氢反应中的重要性\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。水合肼作为氢气储存材料的使用，以及在PtNi纳米催化剂上选择性脱氢的水合肼，展示了脱氢在氢气储存应用中的潜力\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。烷烃的脱水用于聚合物、醚和汽油的生产，以及脱氢用于氢气储存，强调了脱氢反应的多功能性\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。通过Au、Ni、Sn和Bi等金属对Pt催化剂的改性，增强脱氢活性和选择性，说明了双金属相互作用在提高催化性能中的作用\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。在PtNi/AC上观察到的双金属催化剂对脱氢的协同效应，通过增强的氢气生产，突出了合金在脱氢催化剂设计中的重要性\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。Sn/Pt合金上烯烃的DFT计算和实验研究展示了通过表面Sn的存在增强选择性\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。由于在双金属颗粒上不饱和烃的结合能降低，导致失活速率降低，以及通过H2和O2预处理PtSn催化剂来提高催化活性，突出了增强催化剂稳定性和活性的策略\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。当与Pt合金化时，Re、Pd、In和Fe等金属促进脱氢反应，以及用于萘脱氢的PtRe/γ-Al2O3等高活性催化剂的模板合成程序的使用，展示了催化剂设计的创新\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。对于柴油脱氢，PtNi和PtPd等双金属催化剂的增强稳定性，以及由于强烈的金属-金属和金属-载体相互作用导致的硫耐受性和整体催化性能的提高，展示了双金属催化剂在燃料重整应用中的潜力\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。在汽油脱氢方面，PtRh在ZrO2上的优越性，就活性、选择性和失活速率而言，超过了单金属催化剂，突出了载体选择在脱氢催化剂设计中的重要性\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。

                综合理解与未来展望：考虑到讨论的进展和发现，有机会探索脱氢催化剂设计的新兴领域和创新方法。未来的研究工作可能集中在开发新型双金属和多金属催化剂，以增强脱氢活性和选择性。利用原位和操作条件下光谱等先进表征技术，可以对催化剂在反应条件下的动态行为提供更深入的见解。探索具有定制电子和几何特性的新型载体材料，可以进一步增强脱氢催化剂的稳定性和活性。将DFT计算等计算方法与实验研究相结合，可以指导具有优化电子和几何特性的催化剂的合理设计。在非热等离子体条件下研究脱氢反应，可以为具有改进效率和选择性的氢气生产提供新的途径。利用生物质衍生原料开发可持续和可再生的脱氢过程，可以为向更可持续的化学工业转型做出贡献。优化用于氢气储存应用的脱氢催化剂，如水合肼的选择性脱氢，可以为高效氢气储存系统的开发铺平道路。探索从可再生资源，如木质素，生产增值化学品的脱氢反应，可以为生物质的增值和化石燃料依赖性的降低做出贡献。研究将CO2转化为有价值的化学品和燃料的脱氢反应，可以为温室气体排放的缓解提供新的机会。开发用于汽油和柴油燃料重整的脱氢催化剂，可以为向更可持续的交通部门转型做出贡献。优化用于从可再生资源，如水电解，生产氢气的脱氢催化剂，可以为可持续氢经济的发展做出贡献。探索从生物质和塑料废物等废物流中生产氢气的脱氢反应，可以为废物的增值和环境污染的减少做出贡献。
