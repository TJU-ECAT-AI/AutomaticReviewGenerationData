                The significance of selective dehydrogenation reactions in chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage is underscored by their pivotal role in generating valuable chemicals, fuels, and a method for hydrogen storage with higher volumetric energy density than hydrogen gas \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. Reactor studies and UHV studies significantly contribute to our understanding of these processes by providing insights into the performance of supported catalysts under reaction conditions. These studies typically investigate synthesis methods, characterization techniques, and dehydrogenation temperatures for C-H and N-H bond dehydrogenation \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The characterization techniques include N2 adsorption, TEM, XRD, TPR, TPO, H2 chemisorption, XPS, AES, TPD, and CO chemisorption \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The reaction conditions are varied to understand the effects of temperature, pressure, and reactant composition on catalytic activity and selectivity \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. UHV studies, in particular, allow for the examination of surface reactions at the atomic level, which is essential for understanding the fundamental mechanisms of dehydrogenation \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The volcano relationship between nitrogen-binding energy and ammonia conversion, as well as the comparison of ammonia decomposition activity on different surfaces, highlight the importance of surface chemistry in dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The use of hydrazine monohydrate as a hydrogen-storage material and the selective dehydrogenation of hydrazine over PtNi nanocatalysts demonstrate the potential of dehydrogenation for hydrogen storage applications \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The dehydration of alkanes for polymer, ether, and gasoline production, and the use of dehydrogenation for hydrogen storage, emphasize the versatility of dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The enhancement of dehydrogenation activity and selectivity through the modification of Pt catalysts with other metals, such as Au, Ni, Sn, and Bi, illustrates the role of bimetallic interactions in improving catalytic performance \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The synergistic effect of bimetallic catalysts on dehydrogenation, as observed in the enhanced hydrogen production on PtNi/AC, underscores the importance of alloying in dehydrogenation catalyst design \[10.1007/s10562-005-8009-x\]. The DFT calculations and experimental studies of alkenes on Sn/Pt alloys demonstrate the selectivity enhancement through the presence of surface Sn \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The decrease in deactivation rate due to the lower binding energy of unsaturated hydrocarbons on bimetallic particles and the improvement of catalytic activity through pretreatment of PtSn catalysts with H2 and O2 highlight the strategies for enhancing catalyst stability and activity \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The promotion of dehydrogenation reactions by other metals, such as Re, Pd, In, and Fe, when alloyed with Pt, and the utilization of template synthesis procedures for the formulation of high-activity catalysts, such as PtRe/γ-Al2O3 for naphtha dehydrogenation, showcase the innovation in catalyst design \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The enhanced stability of bimetallic catalysts, such as PtNi and PtPd, for diesel dehydrogenation, and the improved sulfur tolerance and overall catalytic performance due to strong metal-metal and metal-support interactions, demonstrate the potential of bimetallic catalysts for fuel reforming applications \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The superiority of PtRh supported on ZrO2 over monometallic catalysts for gasoline dehydrogenation, in terms of activity, selectivity, and deactivation rate, highlights the importance of support selection in dehydrogenation catalyst design \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\].

                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in selective dehydrogenation reactions, particularly focusing on C-H and N-H bond dehydrogenation over supported catalysts, as investigated through reactor and UHV studies, open up new avenues for innovation in chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel bimetallic catalysts with enhanced stability and activity, the optimization of reaction conditions for improved selectivity, and the exploration of alternative supports for dehydrogenation catalysts. The integration of advanced characterization techniques, such as in-situ spectroscopy and microscopy, could provide deeper insights into the dynamic processes occurring on catalyst surfaces during dehydrogenation reactions. Moreover, the application of machine learning algorithms to predict catalyst performance based on structural and electronic properties could accelerate the discovery of efficient dehydrogenation catalysts. The potential of dehydrogenation reactions for hydrogen storage applications, especially in the context of renewable energy systems, warrants further investigation into the design of materials with high hydrogen storage capacity and fast dehydrogenation kinetics. The role of dehydrogenation in fuel reforming processes, particularly in the context of declining fossil fuel reserves and stricter environmental regulations, highlights the need for the development of catalysts with improved sulfur tolerance and overall catalytic performance. The utilization of template synthesis procedures for the formulation of high-activity catalysts, such as PtRe/γ-Al2O3 for naphtha dehydrogenation, showcases the potential of innovative synthesis methods in enhancing catalytic performance. The exploration of alternative dehydrogenation pathways, such as the use of non-noble metals and the development of electrocatalytic dehydrogenation systems, could lead to more sustainable and cost-effective processes for chemical production and hydrogen storage.

                选择性脱氢反应在化学生产、燃料合成和氢气存储中的重要性在于它们在生成有价值的化学品、燃料以及提供比氢气更高的体积能量密度的氢气存储方法方面起着关键作用\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。反应器研究和UHV研究对理解这些过程做出了重大贡献，通过提供在反应条件下支持催化剂性能的见解。这些研究通常调查C-H和N-H键脱氢的合成方法、表征技术以及脱氢温度\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。表征技术包括N2吸附、TEM、XRD、TPR、TPO、H2化学吸附、XPS、AES、TPD和CO化学吸附\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。通过改变反应条件，如温度、压力和反应物组成，以了解对催化活性和选择性的影响\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。特别是，UHV研究允许在原子水平上检查表面反应，这对于理解脱氢反应的基本机制至关重要\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。氮结合能与氨转化之间的火山关系，以及在不同表面上比较氨分解活性，突出了表面化学在脱氢反应中的重要性\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。使用水合肼作为氢气存储材料，以及在PtNi纳米催化剂上选择性脱氢水合肼，展示了脱氢反应在氢气存储应用中的潜力\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。烷烃的脱水用于聚合物、醚和汽油生产，以及脱氢用于氢气存储，强调了脱氢反应的多功能性\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。通过用其他金属如Au、Ni、Sn和Bi修改Pt催化剂来增强脱氢活性和选择性，说明了双金属相互作用在提高催化性能中的作用\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。在PtNi/AC上观察到的双金属催化剂对脱氢的协同效应，强调了合金化在脱氢催化剂设计中的重要性\[10.1007/s10562-005-8009-x\]。Sn/Pt合金上烯烃的DFT计算和实验研究展示了通过表面Sn的存在增强选择性\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。由于在双金属颗粒上未饱和烃的结合能降低，导致失活率降低，以及通过用H2和O2预处理PtSn催化剂来提高催化活性，突出了增强催化剂稳定性和活性的策略\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。当与Pt合金化时，其他金属如Re、Pd、In和Fe促进脱氢反应，以及用于萘脱氢的PtRe/γ-Al2O3等高活性催化剂的模板合成程序的利用，展示了催化剂设计的创新\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。对于柴油脱氢，双金属催化剂如PtNi和PtPd的增强稳定性，以及由于强烈的金属-金属和金属-载体相互作用导致的硫耐受性和整体催化性能的提高，展示了双金属催化剂在燃料重整应用中的潜力\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。在汽油脱氢方面，PtRh在ZrO2上的优越性，就活性、选择性和失活率而言，超过单金属催化剂，突出了载体选择在脱氢催化剂设计中的重要性\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望：通过反应器和UHV研究，特别是在C-H和N-H键脱氢的支撑催化剂上，选择性脱氢反应的进展为化学生产、燃料合成和氢气存储的创新开辟了新的途径。未来的研究工作可能集中在开发具有增强稳定性和活性的新型双金属催化剂、优化反应条件以提高选择性，以及探索脱氢催化剂的替代载体。将先进的表征技术，如原位光谱学和显微镜，与动态过程中催化剂表面发生的脱氢反应相结合，可以提供更深入的见解。此外，基于结构和电子性质预测催化剂性能的机器学习算法的应用可以加速高效脱氢催化剂的发现。脱氢反应在氢气存储应用中的潜力，特别是在可再生能源系统背景下，值得进一步研究材料设计，具有高氢气存储容量和快速脱氢动力学。脱氢在燃料重整过程中的作用，特别是在化石燃料储备下降和更严格的环境法规背景下，突出了开发具有改进硫耐受性和整体催化性能的催化剂的必要性。用于萘脱氢的PtRe/γ-Al2O3等高活性催化剂的模板合成程序的利用，展示了创新合成方法在增强催化性能方面的潜力。探索替代脱氢途径，如使用非贵金属和开发电催化脱氢系统，可以导致更可持续和成本效益更高的化学生产和氢气存储过程。
