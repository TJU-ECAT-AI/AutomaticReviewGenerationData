                Selective dehydrogenation reactions are pivotal in chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage, offering a method for hydrogen storage with higher volumetric energy density than hydrogen gas \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. Reactor studies and UHV studies significantly contribute to our understanding of these processes by providing insights into the performance of supported catalysts under reaction conditions \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. These studies typically investigate synthesis methods, characterization techniques, and dehydrogenation temperatures for C-H and N-H bond dehydrogenation. The characterization techniques include N2 adsorption, TEM, XRD, TPR, TPO, H2 chemisorption, XPS, AES, TPD, and CO chemisorption \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The reaction conditions are varied to understand the effects of temperature, pressure, and reactant composition on catalytic activity and selectivity \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. UHV studies allow for the examination of surface reactions at the atomic level, essential for understanding the fundamental mechanisms of dehydrogenation \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The volcano relationship between nitrogen-binding energy and ammonia conversion, as well as the comparison of ammonia decomposition activity on different surfaces, highlight the importance of surface chemistry in dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The use of hydrazine monohydrate as a hydrogen-storage material and the selective dehydrogenation of hydrazine over PtNi nanocatalysts demonstrate the potential of dehydrogenation for hydrogen storage applications \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The dehydration of alkanes for polymer, ether, and gasoline production, and the use of dehydrogenation for hydrogen storage, emphasize the versatility of dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The enhancement of dehydrogenation activity and selectivity through the modification of Pt catalysts with other metals, such as Au, Ni, Sn, and Bi, illustrates the role of bimetallic interactions in improving catalytic performance \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The synergistic effect of bimetallic catalysts on dehydrogenation, as observed in the enhanced hydrogen production on PtNi/AC, underscores the importance of alloying in dehydrogenation catalyst design \[10.1007/s10562-005-8009-x\]. The DFT calculations and experimental studies of alkenes on Sn/Pt alloys demonstrate the selectivity enhancement through the presence of surface Sn \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The decrease in deactivation rate due to the lower binding energy of unsaturated hydrocarbons on bimetallic particles and the improvement of catalytic activity through pretreatment of PtSn catalysts with H2 and O2 highlight strategies for enhancing catalyst stability and activity \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The promotion of dehydrogenation reactions by other metals, such as Re, Pd, In, and Fe, when alloyed with Pt, and the utilization of template synthesis procedures for the formulation of high-activity catalysts, such as PtRe/γ-Al2O3 for naphtha dehydrogenation, showcase innovation in catalyst design \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The enhanced stability of bimetallic catalysts, such as PtNi and PtPd, for diesel dehydrogenation, and the improved sulfur tolerance and overall catalytic performance due to strong metal-metal and metal-support interactions, demonstrate the potential of bimetallic catalysts for fuel reforming applications \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The superiority of PtRh supported on ZrO2 over monometallic catalysts for gasoline dehydrogenation, in terms of activity, selectivity, and deactivation rate, highlights the importance of support selection in dehydrogenation catalyst design \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\].

                选择性脱氢反应在化学生产、燃料合成和氢气储存中起着关键作用，提供了一种比氢气具有更高体积能量密度的氢气储存方法 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。反应器研究和UHV研究对这些过程的理解做出了重大贡献，通过提供在反应条件下支持催化剂性能的见解 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。这些研究通常调查合成方法、表征技术以及C-H和N-H键脱氢的脱氢温度。表征技术包括N2吸附、TEM、XRD、TPR、TPO、H2化学吸附、XPS、AES、TPD和CO化学吸附 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。反应条件的变化是为了理解温度、压力和反应物组成对催化活性和选择性的影响 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。UHV研究允许在原子水平上检查表面反应，这对于理解脱氢反应的基本机制至关重要 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。氮结合能与氨转化之间的火山关系，以及在不同表面上的氨分解活性的比较，突出了表面化学在脱氢反应中的重要性 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。水合肼作为氢储存材料的使用和在PtNi纳米催化剂上选择性脱氢的水合肼，展示了脱氢在氢储存应用中的潜力 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。烷烃的脱水用于聚合物、醚和汽油的生产，以及脱氢用于氢气储存，强调了脱氢反应的多功能性 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。通过其他金属如Au、Ni、Sn和Bi对Pt催化剂的改性，提高脱氢活性和选择性，说明了二元金属相互作用在提高催化性能中的作用 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。在PtNi/AC上观察到的二元金属催化剂对脱氢的协同效应，强调了合金化在脱氢催化剂设计中的重要性 \[10.1007/s10562-005-8009-x\]。Sn/Pt合金上烯烃的DFT计算和实验研究展示了通过表面Sn的存在增强选择性的方法 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。由于二元金属颗粒上不饱和烃的结合能降低，导致失活速率降低，以及通过H2和O2预处理PtSn催化剂来提高催化活性，突出了增强催化剂稳定性和活性的策略 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。当与Pt合金化时，其他金属如Re、Pd、In和Fe促进脱氢反应，以及用于萘脱氢的PtRe/γ-Al2O3等高活性催化剂的模板合成程序的使用，展示了催化剂设计的创新 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。对于柴油脱氢，PtNi和PtPd等二元金属催化剂的增强稳定性，以及由于强烈的金属-金属和金属-载体相互作用导致的硫耐受性和整体催化性能的提高，展示了二元金属催化剂在燃料重整应用中的潜力 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。在汽油脱氢方面，PtRh在ZrO2上的优越性超过了单金属催化剂，在活性、选择性和失活速率方面，突出了载体选择在脱氢催化剂设计中的重要性 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。

                综合理解与未来展望：考虑到讨论的进展和发现，有机会探索新兴领域和创新方法。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发具有增强选择性和稳定性的新型二元金属催化剂用于脱氢反应。利用先进的表征技术，如原位光谱学和显微镜，可以提供催化剂在反应条件下动态行为的更深入见解。替代载体的探索和合成方法的优化，包括模板合成和原子层沉积，可能导致更高效和稳定催化剂的设计。脱氢反应用于从可再生资源如生物质生产增值化学品和燃料，可能有助于可持续化学生产。脱氢反应与氢储存和燃料重整等其他过程的整合，可能导致用于能量储存和转换的集成系统的开发。机器学习和人工智能在催化剂设计中的应用可以加速具有改进性能的新催化剂的发现。可扩展和成本效益高的脱氢过程的发展，可能有助于脱氢技术在化学生产、燃料合成和氢气储存的商业化。
