                Selective dehydrogenation reactions are pivotal in chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage, as they facilitate the generation of valuable chemicals and fuels and offer a method for hydrogen storage with higher volumetric energy density than hydrogen gas \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. Reactor studies and UHV studies significantly contribute to our understanding of these processes by providing insights into the performance of supported catalysts under reaction conditions. These studies typically investigate the synthesis methods, characterization techniques, and dehydrogenation temperatures for C-H and N-H bond dehydrogenation \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The characterization techniques include N2 adsorption, TEM, XRD, TPR, TPO, H2 chemisorption, XPS, AES, TPD, and CO chemisorption \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The reaction conditions are varied to understand the effects of temperature, pressure, and reactant composition on the catalytic activity and selectivity \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. UHV studies, in particular, allow for the examination of surface reactions at the atomic level, which is essential for understanding the fundamental mechanisms of dehydrogenation \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The volcano relationship between nitrogen-binding energy and ammonia conversion, as well as the comparison of ammonia decomposition activity on different surfaces, highlight the importance of surface chemistry in dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The use of hydrazine monohydrate as a hydrogen-storage material and the selective dehydrogenation of hydrazine over PtNi nanocatalysts demonstrate the potential of dehydrogenation for hydrogen storage applications \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The dehydration of alkanes for polymer, ether, and gasoline production, and the use of dehydrogenation for hydrogen storage, emphasize the versatility of dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The enhancement of dehydrogenation activity and selectivity through the modification of Pt catalysts with other metals, such as Au, Ni, Sn, and Bi, illustrates the role of bimetallic interactions in improving catalytic performance \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The synergistic effect of bimetallic catalysts on dehydrogenation, as observed in the enhanced hydrogen production on PtNi/AC, underscores the importance of alloying in dehydrogenation catalyst design \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The DFT calculations and experimental studies of alkenes on Sn/Pt alloys demonstrate the selectivity enhancement through the presence of surface Sn \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The decrease in deactivation rate due to the lower binding energy of unsaturated hydrocarbons on bimetallic particles and the improvement of catalytic activity through pretreatment of PtSn catalysts with H2 and O2 highlight the strategies for enhancing catalyst stability and activity \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The promotion of dehydrogenation reactions by other metals, such as Re, Pd, In, and Fe, when alloyed with Pt, and the utilization of template synthesis procedures for the formulation of high-activity catalysts, such as PtRe/γ-Al2O3 for naphtha dehydrogenation, showcase the innovation in catalyst design \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The enhanced stability of bimetallic catalysts, such as PtNi and PtPd, for diesel dehydrogenation, and the improved sulfur tolerance and overall catalytic performance due to strong metal-metal and metal-support interactions, demonstrate the potential of bimetallic catalysts for fuel reforming applications \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The superiority of PtRh supported on ZrO2 over monometallic catalysts for gasoline dehydrogenation, in terms of activity, selectivity, and deactivation rate, highlights the importance of support selection in dehydrogenation catalyst design \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\].

                选择性脱氢反应在化学生产、燃料合成和氢气储存中起着至关重要的作用，因为它们促进了有价值的化学品和燃料的生成，并提供了一种比氢气具有更高体积能量密度的氢气储存方法 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。反应器研究和UHV研究对这些过程的理解做出了重大贡献，通过提供在反应条件下支持催化剂性能的见解。这些研究通常调查C-H和N-H键脱氢的合成方法、表征技术以及脱氢温度 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。表征技术包括N2吸附、TEM、XRD、TPR、TPO、H2化学吸附、XPS、AES、TPD和CO化学吸附 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。反应条件的变化是为了理解温度、压力和反应物组成对催化活性和选择性的影响 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。特别是，UHV研究允许在原子水平上检查表面反应，这对于理解脱氢反应的基本机制至关重要 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。氮结合能与氨转化之间的火山关系，以及在不同表面上氨分解活性的比较，突出了表面化学在脱氢反应中的重要性 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。水合肼作为氢储存材料的使用，以及在PtNi纳米催化剂上选择性脱氢的水合肼，展示了脱氢在氢储存应用中的潜力 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。烷烃的脱水用于聚合物、醚和汽油的生产，以及脱氢用于氢气储存，强调了脱氢反应的多功能性 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。通过Au、Ni、Sn和Bi等金属对Pt催化剂的改性，提高脱氢活性和选择性，说明了双金属相互作用在提高催化性能中的作用 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。在PtNi/AC上观察到的双金属催化剂对脱氢的协同效应，强调了合金化在脱氢催化剂设计中的重要性 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。Sn/Pt合金上烯烃的DFT计算和实验研究展示了通过表面Sn的存在提高选择性 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。由于在双金属颗粒上未饱和烃的结合能降低，导致失活速率降低，以及通过H2和O2预处理PtSn催化剂提高催化活性，突出了提高催化剂稳定性和活性的策略 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。当与Pt合金化时，Re、Pd、In和Fe等金属促进脱氢反应，以及用于萘脱氢的PtRe/γ-Al2O3等高活性催化剂的模板合成程序的利用，展示了催化剂设计的创新 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。PtNi和PtPd等双金属催化剂在柴油脱氢中的增强稳定性，以及由于强烈的金属-金属和金属-载体相互作用导致的硫耐受性和整体催化性能的提高，展示了双金属催化剂在燃料重整应用中的潜力 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。在汽油脱氢方面，PtRh在ZrO2上的优越性，就活性、选择性和失活率而言，超过单金属催化剂，突出了载体选择在脱氢催化剂设计中的重要性 \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。
