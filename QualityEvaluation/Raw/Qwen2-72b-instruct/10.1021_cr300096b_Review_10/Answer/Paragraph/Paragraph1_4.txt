                The significance of selective dehydrogenation reactions in chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage is underscored by their pivotal role in generating valuable chemicals, fuels, and a more efficient hydrogen storage medium compared to gaseous hydrogen \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. Reactor studies and UHV studies significantly contribute to our understanding of these processes by elucidating the performance of supported catalysts under reaction conditions. These studies typically investigate synthesis methods, characterization techniques, and dehydrogenation temperatures for C-H and N-H bond dehydrogenation \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. Characterization techniques such as N2 adsorption, TEM, XRD, TPR, TPO, H2 chemisorption, XPS, AES, TPD, and CO chemisorption are employed to gain insights into the catalysts' electronic and structural properties \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The reaction conditions are varied to understand the effects of temperature, pressure, and reactant composition on catalytic activity and selectivity \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. UHV studies, in particular, allow for the examination of surface reactions at the atomic level, which is essential for understanding the fundamental mechanisms of dehydrogenation \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The volcano relationship between nitrogen-binding energy and ammonia conversion, as well as the comparison of ammonia decomposition activity on different surfaces, highlight the importance of surface chemistry in dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The use of hydrazine monohydrate as a hydrogen-storage material and the selective dehydrogenation of hydrazine over PtNi nanocatalysts demonstrate the potential of dehydrogenation for hydrogen storage applications \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The dehydration of alkanes for polymer, ether, and gasoline production, and the use of dehydrogenation for hydrogen storage, emphasize the versatility of dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The enhancement of dehydrogenation activity and selectivity through the modification of Pt catalysts with other metals, such as Au, Ni, Sn, and Bi, illustrates the role of bimetallic interactions in improving catalytic performance \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The synergistic effect of bimetallic catalysts on dehydrogenation, as observed in the enhanced hydrogen production on PtNi/AC, underscores the importance of alloying in dehydrogenation catalyst design \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The DFT calculations and experimental studies of alkenes on Sn/Pt alloys demonstrate the selectivity enhancement through the presence of surface Sn \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The decrease in deactivation rate due to the lower binding energy of unsaturated hydrocarbons on bimetallic particles and the improvement of catalytic activity through pretreatment of PtSn catalysts with H2 and O2 highlight strategies for enhancing catalyst stability and activity \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The promotion of dehydrogenation reactions by other metals, such as Re, Pd, In, and Fe, when alloyed with Pt, and the utilization of template synthesis procedures for the formulation of high-activity catalysts, such as PtRe/γ-Al2O3 for naphtha dehydrogenation, showcase the innovation in catalyst design \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The enhanced stability of bimetallic catalysts, such as PtNi and PtPd, for diesel dehydrogenation, and the improved sulfur tolerance and overall catalytic performance due to strong metal-metal and metal-support interactions, demonstrate the potential of bimetallic catalysts for fuel reforming applications \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]. The superiority of PtRh supported on ZrO2 over monometallic catalysts for gasoline dehydrogenation, in terms of activity, selectivity, and deactivation rate, highlights the importance of support selection in dehydrogenation catalyst design \[10.1021/cr300096b_10\].

                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in selective dehydrogenation reactions, particularly focusing on C-H and N-H bond dehydrogenation over supported catalysts, as investigated through reactor and UHV studies, have opened new avenues for innovation in chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage. The synergistic effects observed in bimetallic catalysts, such as PtNi and PtPd, and the enhanced stability and activity achieved through the modification of Pt catalysts with other metals, suggest a promising future for the design of more efficient and selective dehydrogenation catalysts. The utilization of advanced characterization techniques and the exploration of novel synthesis methods, such as template synthesis, offer opportunities to further enhance catalyst performance and stability. The potential of dehydrogenation reactions for hydrogen storage applications, as demonstrated by the use of hydrazine monohydrate, highlights the need for continued research in this area to develop more efficient and practical hydrogen storage materials. The importance of support selection in dehydrogenation catalyst design, as evidenced by the superiority of PtRh supported on ZrO2, suggests that future studies should focus on the development of novel supports that can further improve catalytic performance. The exploration of new metals and alloys for dehydrogenation reactions, as well as the optimization of reaction conditions, such as temperature and pressure, will be crucial for the advancement of dehydrogenation processes in chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage.

                选择性脱氢反应在化学生产、燃料合成和氢气存储中的重要性，由其在生成有价值的化学品、燃料和比氢气更高效的氢气存储介质中的核心作用所凸显\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。反应器研究和UHV研究对理解这些过程做出了重大贡献，通过阐明在反应条件下支撑催化剂的性能。这些研究通常调查合成方法、表征技术以及C-H和N-H键脱氢的脱氢温度\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。N2吸附、TEM、XRD、TPR、TPO、H2化学吸附、XPS、AES、TPD和CO化学吸附等表征技术被用于深入了解催化剂的电子和结构特性\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。通过改变反应条件，如温度、压力和反应物组成，以理解对催化活性和选择性的影响\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。特别是，UHV研究允许在原子水平上检查表面反应，这对于理解脱氢反应的基本机制至关重要\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。氮结合能与氨转化之间的火山关系，以及在不同表面上的氨分解活性的比较，突出了表面化学在脱氢反应中的重要性\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。水合肼作为氢气存储材料的使用，以及在PtNi纳米催化剂上对水合肼的有选择性脱氢，展示了脱氢反应在氢气存储应用中的潜力\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。烷烃的脱水用于聚合物、醚和汽油生产，以及脱氢用于氢气存储，强调了脱氢反应的多功能性\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。通过用其他金属，如Au、Ni、Sn和Bi，修改Pt催化剂，以增强脱氢活性和选择性，说明了双金属相互作用在提高催化性能中的作用\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。在PtNi/AC上观察到的双金属催化剂对脱氢的协同效应，强调了合金化在脱氢催化剂设计中的重要性\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。Sn/Pt合金上烯烃的DFT计算和实验研究展示了通过表面Sn的存在增强选择性\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。由于在双金属颗粒上未饱和烃的结合能降低，导致失活速率降低，以及通过H2和O2预处理PtSn催化剂来提高催化活性，突出了增强催化剂稳定性和活性的策略\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。当与Pt合金化时，其他金属，如Re、Pd、In和Fe，以及用于萘脱氢的PtRe/γ-Al2O3等高活性催化剂的模板合成程序的利用，展示了催化剂设计的创新\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。PtNi和PtPd等双金属催化剂在柴油脱氢中的增强稳定性和由于强金属-金属和金属-载体相互作用而提高的硫耐受性和整体催化性能，展示了双金属催化剂在燃料重整应用中的潜力\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。在汽油脱氢方面，PtRh在ZrO2上的优越性，就活性、选择性和失活率而言，突出了支撑选择在脱氢催化剂设计中的重要性\[10.1021/cr300096b_10\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望：通过反应器和UHV研究，特别是在支撑催化剂上聚焦于C-H和N-H键脱氢的选择性脱氢反应的进展，为化学生产、燃料合成和氢气存储的创新开辟了新的途径。在PtNi和PtPd等双金属催化剂中观察到的协同效应，以及通过其他金属修改Pt催化剂以增强稳定性和活性，预示着设计更高效和选择性脱氢催化剂的光明未来。利用先进的表征技术和探索新的合成方法，如模板合成，为进一步增强催化剂性能和稳定性提供了机会。水合肼作为氢气存储材料的使用，以及在氢气存储应用中脱氢反应的潜力，凸显了在这一领域进行持续研究以开发更高效和实用的氢气存储材料的必要性。PtRh在ZrO2上的支撑选择在脱氢催化剂设计中的重要性，表明未来的研究应集中在开发可以进一步提高催化性能的新支撑上。探索新的金属和合金用于脱氢反应，以及优化温度和压力等反应条件，对于脱氢过程在化学生产、燃料合成和氢气存储中的进步至关重要。
