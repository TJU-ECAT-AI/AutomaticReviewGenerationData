                    The concept of tandem catalysis at the nanoscale is pivotal in addressing the challenges of integrating multiple reaction steps in chemical engineering processes, as it provides precise spatiotemporal control over reactive intermediates \[10.1126/science.abd4441\]. This is exemplified by the In2O3-Pt/Al2O3 catalyst, which couples catalytic propane dehydrogenation with selective H2 combustion, translating macroscale engineering strategies into a single nanoscale tandem catalyst particle for propane to propylene conversion \[10.1126/science.abd4441\]. The proximity and hierarchy of catalytic sites within the tandem catalyst are crucial for regulating the transport of intermediates, facilitating the on-purpose production of propylene and breaking the thermodynamic limitation of nonoxidative catalytic reactions by instantly removing H2 through selective combustion \[10.1126/science.abh0424\]. The porous overcoating structure of the In2O3-Pt/Al2O3 catalyst regulates the transport of hydrogen species, promoting selective H2 combustion, which in turn drives the catalytic propane dehydrogenation forward \[10.1126/science.abh0424\]. This tandem catalysis has implications for energy efficiency and process optimization in the chemical industry, potentially filling the supply-demand gap in the alkene market \[10.1126/science.abh0424\]. The In2O3-Pt/Al2O3 catalyst allows for the coupling of two reaction steps in a single catalytic system, potentially improving energy efficiency and process optimization by minimizing the need for intermediate separation steps and reducing the overall reaction temperature \[10.1021/cr5002436\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in tandem catalysis at the nanoscale, as exemplified by the In2O3-Pt/Al2O3 catalyst, open up new avenues for the development of high-performance, multifunctional nanostructured catalysts for multiple-step chemical reactions. Future research endeavors might focus on the design of catalysts with enhanced spatiotemporal control over reactive intermediates, leading to more efficient and selective catalytic performance. The integration of advanced characterization techniques and computational modeling could provide deeper insights into the catalytic mechanisms and guide the rational design of tandem catalysts. Moreover, the exploration of tandem catalysis for other grand challenge reactions could lead to breakthroughs in energy efficiency and process optimization in chemical engineering. The development of scalable synthesis methods for tandem catalysts could also pave the way for their industrial application, potentially revolutionizing the production of high-demand chemicals.

                    纳米尺度串联催化概念在解决化学工程过程中整合多个反应步骤的挑战中起着关键作用，因为它提供了对反应中间体的精确时空控制\[10.1126/science.abd4441\]。这通过In2O3-Pt/Al2O3催化剂得到例证，该催化剂将催化丙烷脱氢与选择性H2燃烧相结合，将宏观工程策略转化为单一纳米尺度串联催化剂粒子，用于丙烷到丙烯的转化\[10.1126/science.abd4441\]。串联催化剂中催化位点的邻近性和层次性对于调节中间体的传输至关重要，通过即时去除H2实现丙烯的定向生产，打破了非氧化催化反应的热力学限制\[10.1126/science.abh0424\]。In2O3-Pt/Al2O3催化剂的多孔涂层结构调节氢物种的传输，促进选择性H2燃烧，进而推动催化丙烷脱氢向前发展\[10.1126/science.abh0424\]。这种串联催化对化学工业中的能源效率和过程优化具有影响，可能填补烯烃市场供需缺口\[10.1126/science.abh0424\]。In2O3-Pt/Al2O3催化剂允许在单一催化系统中耦合两个反应步骤，通过最小化中间分离步骤的需要和降低总体反应温度，可能提高能源效率和过程优化\[10.1021/cr5002436\]。

                    综合理解与未来展望：以In2O3-Pt/Al2O3催化剂为例的纳米尺度串联催化进展为开发高性能、多功能纳米结构催化剂用于多步化学反应开辟了新途径。未来的研究努力可能集中在设计具有增强时空控制反应中间体能力的催化剂上，从而实现更高效、更选择性的催化性能。先进表征技术和计算建模的集成可以为催化机制提供更深入的见解，并指导串联催化剂的理性设计。此外，探索串联催化在其他重大挑战反应中的应用可能导致化学工程中能源效率和过程优化的突破。串联催化剂的可扩展合成方法的发展也可能为它们的工业应用铺平道路，可能彻底改变高需求化学品的生产。
