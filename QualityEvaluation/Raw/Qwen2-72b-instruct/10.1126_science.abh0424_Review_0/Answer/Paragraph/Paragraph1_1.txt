                    The concept of tandem catalysis at the nanoscale, as elucidated in reference \[10.1126/science.abd4441\], addresses the challenges of integrating multiple reaction steps in chemical engineering processes by providing precise spatiotemporal control over reactive intermediates. This is exemplified by the In2O3-Pt/Al2O3 catalyst, which couples catalytic propane dehydrogenation with selective H2 combustion, translating macroscale engineering strategies into a single nanoscale tandem catalyst particle for propane to propylene conversion. The proximity and hierarchy of catalytic sites within the tandem catalyst are crucial for regulating the transport of intermediates, leading to rapid and stable oxidative dehydrogenation of propane at high per-pass yields exceeding the PDH equilibrium. The nanoscale overcoating geometry of tandem catalysis offers opportunities for highly selective catalytic performance in grand challenge reactions, potentially improving energy efficiency and process optimization in chemical engineering.

                    The literature, as referenced in \[10.1016/j.joule.2018.10.027\], further elucidates that tandem catalysis can address the challenges of integrating multiple reaction steps by bringing together different catalytic sites in close proximity, allowing for efficient transfer of intermediates between the sites. This can lead to improved energy efficiency and process optimization by reducing the need for intermediate separation steps and enabling the coupling of reactions that are thermodynamically favorable together but not individually. The In2O3-Pt/Al2O3 catalyst for coupling catalytic propane dehydrogenation with selective H2 combustion is an example of how tandem catalysis can be used to optimize processes by coupling endothermic and exothermic reactions to improve overall energy efficiency.

                    The concept of tandem catalysis at the nanoscale, as discussed in reference \[10.1021/cr5002436\], involves the use of bimetallic catalysts and the combination of different catalytic functionalities on a single support. This approach can lead to more efficient use of reactants and energy, as well as better control over product selectivity and yield. The In2O3-Pt/Al2O3 catalyst combines the dehydrogenation activity of In2O3 with the hydrogen combustion activity of Pt, potentially improving energy efficiency and process optimization by coupling two reaction steps on a single catalyst. The synergistic effect of bimetallic catalysts, such as PtSn, can be seen as a form of tandem catalysis, where the two metals work together to enhance the catalytic activity and selectivity. The use of such catalysts can lead to higher energy efficiency and process optimization by reducing the formation of unwanted byproducts and increasing the yield of the desired olefins.

                    纳米尺度串联催化概念，如参考文献\[10.1126/science.abd4441\]所述，通过提供对反应中间体的精确时空控制，解决了化学工程过程中整合多个反应步骤的挑战。这在In2O3-Pt/Al2O3催化剂中得到了例证，该催化剂将催化丙烷脱氢与选择性H2燃烧相结合，将宏观工程策略转化为单一纳米尺度串联催化剂粒子，用于丙烷到丙烯的转化。串联催化剂中催化位点的邻近性和层次性对于调节中间体的传输至关重要，从而实现了超过PDH平衡的高每程产率的丙烷快速稳定氧化脱氢。纳米尺度过涂层几何结构的串联催化为大挑战反应提供了高度选择性的催化性能机会，可能在化学工程中提高能源效率和过程优化。

                    文献，如参考文献\[10.1016/j.joule.2018.10.027\]进一步阐明，串联催化可以通过将不同的催化位点紧密邻近，允许中间体在位点间高效转移，来解决整合多个反应步骤的挑战。这可以减少中间分离步骤的需要，从而提高能源效率和过程优化，同时使热力学上一起有利但单独不利的反应耦合。In2O3-Pt/Al2O3催化剂用于耦合催化丙烷脱氢与选择性H2燃烧，是串联催化如何通过耦合吸热和放热反应来提高整体能源效率以优化过程的一个例子。

                    参考文献\[10.1021/cr5002436\]中讨论的纳米尺度串联催化概念，涉及双金属催化剂的使用和在单一载体上结合不同的催化功能。这种方法可以更有效地利用反应物和能源，以及更好地控制产品选择性和产量。In2O3-Pt/Al2O3催化剂将In2O3的脱氢活性与Pt的氢燃烧活性相结合，通过在单一催化剂上耦合两个反应步骤，可能提高能源效率和过程优化。双金属催化剂，如PtSn的协同效应，可以被视为一种串联催化形式，其中两种金属协同工作以增强催化活性和选择性。使用此类催化剂可以减少不需要的副产品的形成，提高所需烯烃的产量，从而提高能源效率和过程优化。
