This approach can lead to more efficient use of reactants and energy, as well as better control over product selectivity and yield. The In2O3-Pt/Al2O3 catalyst combines the dehydrogenation activity of In2O3 with the hydrogen combustion activity of Pt, potentially improving energy efficiency and process optimization by coupling two reaction steps on a single catalyst. The synergistic effect of bimetallic catalysts, such as PtSn, can be seen as a form of tandem catalysis, where the two metals work together to enhance the catalytic activity and selectivity. The use of such catalysts can lead to higher energy efficiency and process optimization by reducing the formation of unwanted byproducts and increasing the yield of the desired olefins. The Pt and Sn components of the PtSn/Al2O3 catalyst work in tandem to optimize the dehydrogenation of light alkanes, improving selectivity and stability of the catalyst, thus potentially increasing the yield of desired products and reducing the need for catalyst regeneration. The In2O3-Pt/Al2O3 catalyst allows for the coupling of two reaction steps in a single catalytic system, potentially improving energy efficiency and process optimization by minimizing the need for intermediate separation steps and reducing the overall reaction temperature. The use of bimetallic catalysts and the combination of different catalytic functionalities on a single support can simplify the overall process, potentially leading to higher energy efficiency and better process optimization. The synergistic effect of the two metals in bimetallic catalysts can lead to higher selectivity and stability, reducing the need for regeneration and thus saving energy. The literature highlights the importance of careful catalyst design to minimize side reactions and maximize the desired product yield, which is a key goal of tandem catalysis.
