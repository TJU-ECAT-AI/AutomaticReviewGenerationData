            <English> 
                    The utilization of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) significantly enhances propylene selectivity compared to traditional oxidants, such as molecular oxygen (O2) \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]. This enhancement is attributed to the slower reoxidation of vanadia (VOx) species, which reduces the density of active oxygen sites and curbs the conversion of propene to carbon oxides (COx) \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]. Moreover, N2O facilitates the reduction of the catalyst, thereby promoting a selective oxidation pathway that favors propylene production over combustion \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025\]. However, the use of N2O in ODHP is not without challenges. Catalyst deactivation, primarily due to coke deposition, is a significant hurdle that limits the practicality of this process \[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035\]. This deactivation is exacerbated by the agglomeration of VOx species, which increases the oxidation of propene to COx, leading to a drop in propylene selectivity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]. Furthermore, the stability of the catalyst under N2O conditions is a critical issue that requires advanced research for the practical implementation of N2O-ODHP \[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010\].

                    ※※※

                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in utilizing N2O as an oxidant in ODHP have illuminated a promising pathway for enhancing propylene selectivity. However, the challenges of catalyst deactivation and stability, particularly in the context of coke formation and VOx agglomeration, demand innovative solutions. Future research endeavors might focus on developing coke-resistant catalysts, optimizing regeneration techniques, and understanding the mechanisms of coke formation and VOx agglomeration. Additionally, the exploration of alternative oxidants that can synergize with N2O to improve selectivity and stability could be a fruitful area of investigation. Advanced in situ studies, such as real-time monitoring of coke deposition and catalyst regeneration, are essential for gaining deeper insights into the deactivation processes. Moreover, the development of cost-effective and selective catalysts for the conversion of ammonia (NH3) to N2O could provide a sustainable source of N2O for ODHP, further enhancing the practicality of this process.

            <Chinese> 
                    利用一氧化二氮（N2O）作为氧化剂在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）中，与传统氧化剂如氧气（O2）相比，显著提高了丙烯的选择性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]。这种提升归因于五氧化二钒（VOx）物种的缓慢再氧化，减少了活性氧位点的密度，抑制了丙烯转化为碳氧化物（COx）\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]。此外，N2O促进了催化剂的还原，从而促进了有利于丙烯生产的有选择性的氧化途径，而非燃烧\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025\]。然而，N2O在ODHP中的使用并非没有挑战。催化剂失活，主要是由于焦炭沉积，是限制该过程实用性的重大障碍\[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035\]。这种失活因VOx物种的聚集而加剧，增加了丙烯氧化为COx，导致丙烯选择性下降\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]。此外，催化剂在N2O条件下的稳定性是一个关键问题，需要先进的研究来实现N2O-ODHP的实用实施\[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010\]。

                    ※※※

                    综合理解与未来展望：利用N2O作为ODHP氧化剂的进展揭示了一条提高丙烯选择性的有希望的途径。然而，催化剂失活和稳定性方面的挑战，特别是在焦炭形成和VOx聚集的背景下，需要创新的解决方案。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发抗焦炭催化剂、优化再生技术以及理解焦炭形成和VOx聚集的机制上。此外，探索可以与N2O协同作用以提高选择性和稳定性的替代氧化剂可能是一个富有成效的研究领域。先进的原位研究，如焦炭沉积和催化剂再生的实时监测，对于深入了解失活过程至关重要。此外，开发成本效益高、选择性高的催化剂，用于将氨（NH3）转化为N2O，可以为ODHP提供可持续的N2O来源，进一步增强该过程的实用性。
