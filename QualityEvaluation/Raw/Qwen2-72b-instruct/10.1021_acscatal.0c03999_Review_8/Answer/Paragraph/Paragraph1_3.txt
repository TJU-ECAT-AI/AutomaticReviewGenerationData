            <English> 
                    The utilization of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) has been extensively studied, demonstrating its superiority in propylene selectivity over conventional oxidants like oxygen (O2) \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]. N2O's role in enhancing propylene selectivity is attributed to its ability to slow down the reoxidation of vanadium oxides (VOx), thereby reducing the number of active oxygen sites and curtailing the over-oxidation of propene to COx \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]. This characteristic of N2O is particularly advantageous over iron-zeolites and vanadium oxides, where it significantly boosts propylene selectivity compared to O2 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]. However, the use of N2O in ODHP is not without challenges. Catalyst deactivation, primarily due to coke formation, is a significant hurdle that limits the practical implementation of N2O-ODHP \[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035\]. Advanced research is required to address these limitations, focusing on the development of stable catalysts that can withstand deactivation and maintain high propylene selectivity over extended periods \[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010\]. Moreover, the understanding of oxygen species and their role in selectivity and activity is crucial for the optimization of catalysts and the practicality of N2O-ODHP \[10.1021/jp070747c\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The use of N2O in ODHP presents a promising avenue for enhancing propylene selectivity while mitigating the over-oxidation of propene to COx. However, the challenges associated with catalyst deactivation and stability necessitate innovative research strategies. Future research should focus on the development of advanced catalysts that can resist coke formation and maintain high activity and selectivity. This could involve the design of novel catalyst structures and the incorporation of promoters that can modulate the reactivity of N2O and prevent catalyst deactivation. Additionally, the exploration of alternative regeneration methods that can restore catalyst activity without compromising selectivity is crucial. Advanced characterization techniques, such as in situ spectroscopy, should be employed to gain deeper insights into the mechanisms of catalyst deactivation and the role of oxygen species in selectivity. Moreover, the optimization of reaction conditions, including the concentration of N2O and the presence of O2, is essential for maximizing propylene yield while minimizing deactivation. The development of cost-effective and sustainable methods for N2O production, such as the conversion of ammonia (NH3) to N2O, could also significantly enhance the practicality of N2O-ODHP. Overall, the integration of these research directions has the potential to overcome the limitations of N2O-ODHP and pave the way for its industrial implementation.

                    硝酸氧化物（N2O）作为氧化脱氢丙烷（ODHP）中的氧化剂的使用已经得到了广泛的研究，显示出其在丙烯选择性方面优于传统的氧化剂如氧气（O2）\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]。N2O在提高丙烯选择性方面的作用归因于其能够减缓钒氧化物（VOx）的再氧化，从而减少活性氧位点的数量，抑制丙烯过度氧化为COx \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]。N2O的这一特性在铁沸石和钒氧化物上尤其有利，与O2相比，它显著提高了丙烯选择性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]。然而，N2O在ODHP中的使用并非没有挑战。催化剂失活，主要是由于焦炭形成，是一个显著的障碍，限制了N2O-ODHP的实际应用\[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035\]。需要进行高级研究来解决这些限制，重点是开发能够抵抗失活并保持高丙烯选择性的稳定催化剂\[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010\]。此外，理解氧物种及其在选择性和活性中的作用对于催化剂的优化和N2O-ODHP的实用性至关重要\[10.1021/jp070747c\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望：N2O在ODHP中的使用为提高丙烯选择性同时抑制丙烯过度氧化为COx提供了一条有希望的途径。然而，与催化剂失活和稳定性相关的挑战需要创新的研究策略。未来的研究应集中在开发能够抵抗焦炭形成并保持高活性和选择性的先进催化剂上。这可能涉及设计新型催化剂结构和加入促进剂，可以调节N2O的反应性和防止催化剂失活。此外，探索可以恢复催化剂活性而不损害选择性的替代再生方法至关重要。应使用先进的表征技术，如原位光谱学，以获得对催化剂失活机制和氧物种在选择性中作用的更深入理解。此外，反应条件的优化，包括N2O的浓度和O2的存在，对于最大化丙烯产量同时最小化失活至关重要。成本有效和可持续的N2O生产方法的发展，如氨（NH3）转化为N2O，也可能显著增强N2O-ODHP的实用性。总体而言，这些研究方向的整合有可能克服N2O-ODHP的限制，并为其实现工业应用铺平道路。
