        <English> 
                The utilization of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) significantly enhances propylene selectivity compared to traditional oxidants like O2 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]. This is attributed to N2O's ability to curb O2 adsorption, enhance catalyst reduction, and control the oxidation states of active sites, thereby limiting the formation of COx and propene over-oxidation \[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2\]. N2O's slower reoxidation of VOx species, compared to O2, reduces the density of active lattice oxygen, favoring propene over COx formation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025\]. However, catalyst deactivation and stability remain critical challenges, often due to coke deposition, which hinders the performance of iron-zeolite catalysts \[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035\]. Advanced in situ studies, such as FTIR spectroscopy, are crucial for real-time monitoring of carbon deposits and surface changes, aiding in the understanding of hydrocarbon conversion \[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010\]. Research gaps exist in the selective oxidation with N2O over iron zeolites and the reusability of catalysts post-regeneration \[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010\]. Steam activation has been shown to mitigate deactivation by reducing the formation of coke \[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020\]. Strategies to optimize conditions, enhance stability, and inhibit oxygen adsorption on catalysts like NiMoO4 are essential for practical implementation \[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The use of N2O in ODHP presents a promising avenue for propylene production, offering higher selectivity and reduced COx formation. However, the challenges of catalyst deactivation and stability, particularly due to coke deposition, necessitate innovative research directions. Advanced in situ techniques, such as FTIR and EPR spectroscopy, offer insights into the mechanisms of deactivation and the nature of oxygen species, which are critical for catalyst design and optimization. The development of coke-resistant catalysts and efficient regeneration methods, possibly involving steam activation, could significantly enhance the practicality of N2O-ODHP. Moreover, exploring the synergistic effects of N2O with other promoters and optimizing the N2O:O2 ratio could lead to more stable and selective ODHP processes. Future research should also focus on the development of cost-effective N2O sources and the integration of N2O production from ammonia decomposition, which could potentially address the economic and environmental concerns associated with N2O use. Additionally, the exploration of new catalyst structures and the stabilization of vanadium species could mitigate the issues of catalyst deactivation and enhance the overall efficiency of the N2O-ODHP process.

        <Chinese> 
                利用一氧化二氮（N2O）作为氧化剂在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）中，与传统氧化剂如O2相比，显著提高了丙烯的选择性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]。这归因于N2O能够抑制O2吸附，增强催化剂还原，并控制活性位点的氧化状态，从而限制COx和丙烯过度氧化的形成\[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2\]。与O2相比，N2O对VOx物种的较慢再氧化减少了活性晶格氧的密度，有利于丙烯而非COx的形成\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025\]。然而，催化剂失活和稳定性仍然是关键挑战，通常由于焦炭沉积，这阻碍了铁沸石催化剂的性能\[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035\]。原位高级研究，如FTIR光谱，对于实时监测碳沉积和表面变化至关重要，有助于理解碳氢化合物的转化\[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010\]。在铁沸石上使用N2O进行选择性氧化和催化剂再生后的可重用性方面存在研究空白\[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010\]。蒸汽活化已被证明可以减少失活，通过减少焦炭的形成\[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020\]。为了实际应用，优化条件、增强稳定性和抑制催化剂如NiMoO4上的氧气吸附是必不可少的\[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望：N2O在ODHP中的应用为丙烯生产提供了一条有前景的途径，提供了更高的选择性和减少的COx形成。然而，由于焦炭沉积导致的催化剂失活和稳定性挑战，需要创新的研究方向。高级原位技术，如FTIR和EPR光谱，提供了对失活机制和氧物种性质的见解，这对催化剂设计和优化至关重要。开发抗焦炭催化剂和有效的再生方法，可能涉及蒸汽活化，可以显著增强N2O-ODHP的实用性。此外，探索N2O与其他促进剂的协同效应和优化N2O:O2比率可能导致更稳定和选择性的ODHP过程。未来的研究还应集中在开发经济有效的N2O来源和从氨分解中整合N2O生产上，这可能解决与N2O使用相关的经济和环境问题。此外，探索新的催化剂结构和稳定钒物种可以缓解催化剂失活问题，提高N2O-ODHP过程的整体效率。
