            <English> 
                    The utilization of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) has been extensively studied, demonstrating its superiority in propylene selectivity over traditional oxidants like O2 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]. N2O's unique properties, such as its ability to slow VOx reoxidation, limit COx formation, and promote selective oxidation, have been pivotal in enhancing propylene yield \[10.1016/j.cattod.2005.11.028\]. However, catalyst deactivation, primarily due to coke formation, remains a significant challenge \[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005\]. Iron-zeolites, particularly Fe-ZSM-5, have shown promise in ODHP with N2O, achieving high propylene yields \[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035\]. Yet, the stability and reusability of these catalysts under N2O conditions require further investigation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010\]. Advanced research is essential to address these limitations and make N2O-ODHP a more feasible option for practical implementation \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_8\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The use of N2O in ODHP presents a promising avenue for propylene production, offering higher selectivity and reduced COx formation compared to conventional oxidants. However, the challenge of catalyst deactivation, particularly through coke formation, necessitates innovative strategies for catalyst regeneration and stability enhancement. Future research should focus on developing catalysts with improved coke resistance and exploring methods for efficient regeneration under N2O conditions. Additionally, the optimization of N2O concentration and the integration of in situ regeneration techniques could significantly improve the practicality of N2O-ODHP. Advanced catalyst design, incorporating insights from mechanistic studies and utilizing advanced characterization techniques, is crucial for achieving stable and selective N2O-ODHP processes.

                    硝酸氧化物（N2O）作为氧化脱氢丙烷（ODHP）的氧化剂已被广泛研究，显示其在丙烯选择性上优于传统氧化剂如O2 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010\]。N2O的独特性质，如减缓VOx再氧化、限制COx形成和促进选择性氧化，对于提高丙烯产量至关重要 \[10.1016/j.cattod.2005.11.028\]。然而，催化剂失活，主要是由于焦炭形成，仍然是一个重大挑战 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005\]。铁-沸石，特别是Fe-ZSM-5，在N2O下的ODHP中显示出潜力，实现了高丙烯产量 \[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035\]。然而，这些催化剂在N2O条件下的稳定性和可重复使用性需要进一步研究 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010\]。高级研究对于解决这些限制并使N2O-ODHP成为更可行的实践选择至关重要 \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_8\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: N2O在ODHP中的应用为丙烯生产提供了一条有希望的途径，与传统氧化剂相比，它提供了更高的选择性和减少的COx形成。然而，催化剂失活的挑战，特别是通过焦炭形成，需要创新的催化剂再生和稳定性增强策略。未来的研究应集中在开发具有改进焦炭抗性的催化剂和探索N2O条件下高效再生的方法上。此外，N2O浓度的优化和原位再生技术的集成可以显著提高N2O-ODHP的实用性。先进的催化剂设计，结合机理研究的见解和利用先进的表征技术，对于实现稳定和选择性的N2O-ODHP过程至关重要。
