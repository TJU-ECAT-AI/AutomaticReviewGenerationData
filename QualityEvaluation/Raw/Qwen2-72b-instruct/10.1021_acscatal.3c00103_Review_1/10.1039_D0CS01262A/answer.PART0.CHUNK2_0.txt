<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1.The reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) involves three steps: (1) dissociative adsorption of propane on the metal surface, (2) β-H extraction from the adsorbed propane to form a surface-bound propyl species, and (3) desorption of propylene from the surface. There is ongoing debate regarding whether the dissociative adsorption process or the β-H extraction is the rate-controlling step in this reaction. Some studies suggest that the first CH bond activation, which is part of the β-H extraction step, is the rate-determining step (RDS) on certain metal surfaces like Pt(111). However, recent studies propose that over Pt(211), a non-reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism dominates, where the first CH bond activation is the RDS. This uncertainty impacts our understanding of the PDH process by complicating the identification of the true RDS and thus the design of more efficient catalysts. Without a clear consensus on the RDS, it is challenging to develop catalysts that can selectively enhance the desired reaction pathway while suppressing side reactions.

1.丙烷脱氢（PDH）的反向Horiuti-Polanyi机制涉及三个步骤：（1）丙烷在金属表面的解离吸附，（2）从吸附的丙烷中提取β-H，形成表面结合的丙基物种，（3）从表面解吸丙烯。关于解离吸附过程还是β-H提取是该反应的速率控制步骤，目前存在争议。一些研究表明，在某些金属表面，如Pt(111)，首次CH键活化，作为β-H提取步骤的一部分，是速率决定步骤（RDS）。然而，最近的研究提出，在Pt(211)上，一个非反向Horiuti-Polanyi机制占主导，其中首次CH键活化是RDS。这种不确定性通过使RDS的识别复杂化，从而影响我们对PDH过程的理解，从而影响更有效催化剂的设计。没有关于RDS的明确共识，很难开发出能够选择性增强所需反应途径同时抑制副反应的催化剂。
