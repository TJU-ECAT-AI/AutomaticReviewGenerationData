1.The literature does not provide detailed information on the reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for propane dehydrogenation (PDH), nor does it discuss the ongoing debate regarding whether the dissociative adsorption process or the β-H extraction is the rate-controlling step in this reaction. However, it does suggest that propane dehydrogenation over platinum and over the alloys occurs via the same reaction mechanism, which involves dissociative chemisorption of propane on a single platinum atom, followed by the conversion of the propyl radical into π-bonded propene via β-hydrogen elimination, and the last step, desorption of π-bonded propene. The difference in negative reaction order, with respect to hydrogen, between platinum and its diluted alloys reflects a lower steady-state θH on Pt atoms surrounded by Au atoms vis--vis Pt atoms on a Pt surface.
