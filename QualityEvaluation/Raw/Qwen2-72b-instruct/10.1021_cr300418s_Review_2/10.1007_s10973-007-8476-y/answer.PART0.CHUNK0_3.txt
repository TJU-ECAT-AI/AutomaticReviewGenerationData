1.The literature does not provide information to directly compare and contrast the solid-state synthesis method and impregnation method for preparing doped oxide materials. However, it can be inferred that both methods aim to achieve high dispersion and uniform composition at the atomic scale. Solid-state synthesis involves reactions at moderate to low temperatures to prevent unwanted atomic restructuring, while impregnation involves depositing active precursors onto a support. Dopant loading and calcination temperature can influence the structural characteristics and catalytic performance by affecting the dispersion and stability of the doped materials. X-ray diffraction (XRD) can be used to confirm successful doping and characterize the resulting materials by identifying the crystalline phases and lattice parameters. For example, Sr0.9La0.1Ti1–xTaxO3 and copper-doped ceria catalysts for CO oxidation can be analyzed using XRD to confirm the incorporation of dopants and assess their impact on the crystal structure.
