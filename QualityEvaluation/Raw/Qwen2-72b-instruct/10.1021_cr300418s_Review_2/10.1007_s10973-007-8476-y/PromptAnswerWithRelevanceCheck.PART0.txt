1.The literature does not provide information to directly compare and contrast the solid-state synthesis method and impregnation method for preparing doped oxide materials. However, it can be inferred that both methods aim to achieve high dispersion and uniformity of the dopant in the oxide matrix. Solid-state synthesis typically involves mixing the dopant and oxide precursors in the solid phase, followed by high-temperature calcination to promote diffusion and reaction. Impregnation involves depositing the dopant onto the oxide support from a solution, often followed by drying and calcination. Dopant loading and calcination temperature can significantly influence the structural characteristics and catalytic performance. Higher dopant loadings may lead to agglomeration and reduced dispersion, while higher calcination temperatures can promote sintering and reduce surface area. X-ray diffraction (XRD) can be used to confirm successful doping by detecting shifts in peak positions or the appearance of new peaks corresponding to the dopant or doped phases. For example, in Sr0.9La0.1Ti1–xTaxO3 and copper-doped ceria catalysts for CO oxidation, XRD can reveal the incorporation of La or Cu into the lattice, indicating successful doping.
