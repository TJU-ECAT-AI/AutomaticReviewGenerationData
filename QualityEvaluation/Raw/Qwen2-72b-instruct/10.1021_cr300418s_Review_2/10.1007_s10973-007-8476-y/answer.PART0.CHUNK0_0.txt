1.The literature does not provide information to directly compare and contrast the solid-state synthesis method and impregnation method for preparing doped oxide materials. However, it can be inferred that the solid-state synthesis method involves starting from a homogeneous solid precursor and transforming it to the desired catalyst at low temperatures, below the Tammann or Httig temperatures, to prevent segregation. This method is advantageous for obtaining highly dispersed solids with controlled texture. On the other hand, impregnation methods are not detailed in the literature, but generally involve depositing a dopant onto a pre-formed oxide support, which can be less effective in achieving uniform dispersion and may require higher calcination temperatures. X-ray diffraction (XRD) can be used to confirm successful doping and characterize the resulting materials by identifying the presence of dopant phases and changes in lattice parameters. For example, Sr0.9La0.1Ti1–xTaxO3 and copper-doped ceria catalysts for CO oxidation can be analyzed using XRD to confirm the incorporation of La and Cu into the lattice, respectively.
