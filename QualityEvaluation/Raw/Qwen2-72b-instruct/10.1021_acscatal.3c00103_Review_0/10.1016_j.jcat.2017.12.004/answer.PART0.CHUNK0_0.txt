<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1.In the context of increasing industrial demand for propylene and the limitations of traditional production methods, propane dehydrogenation (PDH) techniques address these challenges by providing an alternative method for propylene production. PDH processes, both oxidative and non-oxidative, are being developed to overcome deactivation issues, enhance stability, and improve recyclability of catalysts. Innovative catalyst design strategies, such as the use of V-based catalysts supported on mesoporous silica solids, are being explored to achieve these goals. The study of VOX-SiO2 catalysts prepared at different gelation and calcination temperatures demonstrates the potential for optimizing catalyst performance and stability in PDH reactions.
