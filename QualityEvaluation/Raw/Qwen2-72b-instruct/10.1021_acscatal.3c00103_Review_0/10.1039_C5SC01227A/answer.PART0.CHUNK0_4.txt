1.In the context of increasing industrial demand for propylene and the limitations of traditional production methods, propane dehydrogenation (PDH) techniques address these challenges by providing an alternative process to produce propylene from propane, which is abundant and cheap. PDH techniques can be catalyzed by supported Pt or CrOx catalysts. However, the catalyst deactivation due to coke formation remains a challenging issue. Strategies to overcome deactivation issues include the use of additional promoters, such as late transition metals, main-group metals including Sn and Ga, alkali-metal oxides and rare-earth metal oxides, which can increase the robustness against coking. Moreover, the development of single atom catalysts, such as Fe embedded in a silica matrix, can suppress deep dehydrogenation and coke formation, and enhance the selectivity towards propylene. In both oxidative and nonoxidative PDH processes, the design of catalysts with optimal metal-support interactions, controlled particle size and shape, and the use of alloying elements can improve the stability and recyclability of the catalysts.

1.在工业对丙烯需求增加和传统生产方法局限性的背景下，丙烷脱氢(PDH)技术通过提供从丰富且廉价的丙烷生产丙烯的替代过程来应对这些挑战。PDH技术可以由负载的Pt或CrOx催化剂催化。然而，由于焦炭形成导致的催化剂失活仍然是一个具有挑战性的问题。克服失活问题的策略包括使用额外的助剂，如晚期过渡金属、主族金属(包括Sn和Ga)、碱金属氧化物和稀土金属氧化物，这些助剂可以提高对焦炭形成的抗性。此外，单原子催化剂的发展，如嵌入二氧化硅基质中的Fe，可以抑制深度脱氢和焦炭形成，提高丙烯的选择性。在氧化和非氧化PDH过程中，设计具有最佳金属-支撑相互作用、受控粒径和形状以及使用合金元素的催化剂可以提高催化剂的稳定性和可回收性。
