                    The evolution of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as a promising alternative for propylene production has been significantly influenced by the development of innovative catalyst design strategies aimed at addressing catalyst deactivation challenges \[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]. These strategies have been pivotal in enhancing stability and recyclability, particularly in the context of increasing industrial demand and changing global energy landscapes. Catalysts based on vanadium oxides (VOx) have shown remarkable potential, with VOx/Al2O3 optimized through co-assembly directed hydrothermal aging (CADH) to yield V3+ species, which are crucial for high propene selectivity \[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]. Phosphorus (P) modification has been effective in curbing VOx polymerization, while magnesium (Mg) modification has improved anti-coking properties, leading to a 1927% increase in propene yield \[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]. The use of vanadium on silica (V/SiO2) has also excelled, demonstrating high propene yield and stable selectivity \[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]. Vanadium-doped mesoporous silica nanoparticles (V-DMSNs) have achieved 18.1% propane conversion, 91% propene selectivity, and 22 molC3H6 kg1cat h1 space-time yield (STY) \[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]. VOx supported on zirconia (VOx/ZrO2) has shown five times higher activity than VOx/Al2O3, with a STY of 9.2 molC3H6 kg1cat h1 \[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]. Preparation methods and support types have been found to be crucial for the performance of these catalysts \[10.1039/D1GC03700E\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in PDH catalyst design, particularly the use of vanadium-based catalysts, have opened up new avenues for enhancing propylene production while addressing the challenges of catalyst deactivation and stability. Future research efforts might focus on the development of novel catalysts that can further improve the selectivity and activity of PDH processes, potentially exploring the use of single-atom catalysts and alloying strategies to minimize coke formation and enhance recyclability. Additionally, the integration of PDH processes with renewable energy sources could offer a sustainable pathway for propylene production, aligning with the global shift towards cleaner and more efficient energy systems.

                    丙烷脱氢(PDH)作为丙烯生产的一种有前景的替代方案,其发展受到创新催化剂设计策略的显著影响,这些策略旨在解决催化剂失活挑战\[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]。这些策略在增强稳定性和可回收性方面发挥了关键作用,特别是在工业需求增加和全球能源格局变化的背景下。基于钒氧化物(VOx)的催化剂显示出巨大潜力,通过共组装定向水热老化(CADH)优化的VOx/Al2O3产生V3+物种,对高丙烯选择性至关重要\[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]。磷(P)改性有效抑制了VOx聚合,而镁(Mg)改性改善了抗焦性能,导致丙烯产量提高了1927%\[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]。使用钒在二氧化硅(V/SiO2)上也表现出色,显示出高丙烯产量和稳定的丙烯选择性\[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]。钒掺杂的介孔二氧化硅纳米颗粒(V-DMSNs)实现了18.1%的丙烷转化率,91%的丙烯选择性,以及22 molC3H6 kg1cat h1的空间时间产率(STY)\[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]。VOx在氧化锆(VOx/ZrO2)上的支撑显示出比VOx/Al2O3高五倍的活性,STY为9.2 molC3H6 kg1cat h1\[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]。制备方法和支持类型被发现对这些催化剂的性能至关重要\[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: PDH催化剂设计的进展,特别是使用基于钒的催化剂,为提高丙烯生产同时解决催化剂失活和稳定性挑战开辟了新的途径。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发新型催化剂上,以进一步提高PDH过程的选择性和活性,可能探索使用单原子催化剂和合金策略来最小化焦炭形成并增强可回收性。此外,将PDH过程与可再生能源来源的整合可能为丙烯生产提供一条可持续的途径,与全球向更清洁、更高效能源系统的转变相一致。
