                    Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) techniques have emerged as a promising alternative for propylene production, addressing the limitations of traditional methods and the increasing industrial demand for propylene \[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]. Catalyst deactivation, a significant challenge in PDH processes, has been tackled through innovative design strategies aimed at enhancing stability and recyclability \[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]. The synthesis of Al2O3 via CADH (controlled atmosphere deposition and heat treatment) optimizes the distribution of V3+ species, leading to enhanced activity \[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]. P-modified VOx/Al2O3 retains high activity, while Mg-modified VOx/Al2O3 exhibits anti-coking properties, achieving 19-27% propene yields \[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]. V/SiO2 yields 59.5% propene with a space-time yield (STY) of 7.9 molC3H6 kg-1cat h-1 \[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]. V-DMSNs (vanadium-doped mesoporous silica nanoparticles) achieve 18.1% propane conversion and 91% propene selectivity, with an STY of 22 molC3H6 kg-1cat h-1 \[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]. VOx/ZrO2 outperforms VOx/Al2O3, achieving an STY of 9.2 molC3H6 kg-1cat h-1 \[10.1039/D1GC03700E\]. A catalyst with 8 wt% V loading hits an STY of 24 molC3H6 kg-1cat h-1 \[10.1039/D1GC03700E\].
