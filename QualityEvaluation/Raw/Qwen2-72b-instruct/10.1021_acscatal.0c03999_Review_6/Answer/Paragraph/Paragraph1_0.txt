                The performance of Fe oxide-based catalysts in CO2-ODHP reactions is significantly influenced by their reducibility and the nature of the support materials \[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\]. Active carbon has been identified as the most suitable support material for the dehydrogenation of ethylbenzene to styrene in the presence of carbon dioxide, with iron oxide showing the best activity and slower deactivation compared to chromium and copper oxides \[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\]. The type of active carbon used and the iron content significantly impact the catalytic behavior, with the optimum amount of iron being 5 wt.% \[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\]. Added alkaline metal oxides, such as potassium, enhance the activity of iron oxide supported on active carbon, with the best alkali metal/Fe atomic ratio being 0.1 \[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\]. The formation of carbon deposits or gasification of the support after catalytic tests can be attributed to the reaction mechanism and the optimization of catalytic systems for the CO2-ODHP process \[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\]. The reducibility of Fe2O3/AC is improved, and its reduction proceeds through Fe2O3 to Fe3O4, FeO, and finally Fe \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6\]. However, the reducibility of Fe2O3/γ-Al2O3 and hydrotalcite-derived FeMg catalysts is hindered due to the strong interaction between Fe oxide and the support \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6\]. The enhanced reducibility of Fe2O3 on AC contributes to the high catalytic performance among the tested catalysts, and the redox cycle of Fe oxide is likely crucial in CO2ODHP \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6\]. Ceria (CeO2) is a good choice of oxide support because it has the ability to store/release oxygen and thus may induce direct CO bond scission of CO2, while also providing available lattice oxygen for coke suppression \[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w\]. The Fe3Ni catalyst, which consists of oxidized Fe and metallic Ni, shows promising selectivity toward propylene via the CO2-ODHP pathway \[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w\]. The combination of Fe and Ni on a ceria support can enhance the catalytic activity for CO2-ODHP by promoting the oxidative CH bond cleavage of propane, leading to enhanced performance in CO2-ODHP reactions \[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w\]. The effects of bimetallic interactions on the catalytic reduction of CO2 by propane have been discussed, with Pt-based bimetallic catalysts outperforming Pd-based catalysts in terms of conversion and stability \[10.1002/aic.16670\]. The selectivity of FexNiy catalysts toward CO2-ODHP can be modified by changing the Ni content in the bimetallic composition, with increasing the Ni atomic ratio above 1 altering the selectivity to favor the dry reforming pathway rather than CO2-ODHP \[10.1002/aic.16670\]. CeO2 is described as a readily reducible oxide with the ability to reversibly convert between Ce4+ and Ce3+, which is critical for oxygen exchange properties that are important for CO2 activation \[10.1002/aic.16670\]. The Fe3Ni1 catalyst, which consists of metallic Ni and oxidized Fe under reaction conditions, is found to favor oxidative C-H bond cleavage via the CO2-ODHP pathway \[10.1002/aic.16670\].

                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The findings from the studies discussed in this review suggest that the reducibility and support materials of Fe oxide-based catalysts play a significant role in their performance in CO2-ODHP reactions. Active carbon has been identified as the most suitable support material for the dehydrogenation of ethylbenzene to styrene in the presence of carbon dioxide, with iron oxide showing the best activity and slower deactivation compared to chromium and copper oxides. The type of active carbon used and the iron content significantly impact the catalytic behavior, with the optimum amount of iron being 5 wt.%. Added alkaline metal oxides, such as potassium, enhance the activity of iron oxide supported on active carbon, with the best alkali metal/Fe atomic ratio being 0.1. The formation of carbon deposits or gasification of the support after catalytic tests can be attributed to the reaction mechanism and the optimization of catalytic systems for the CO2-ODHP process. The reducibility of Fe2O3/AC is improved, and its reduction proceeds through Fe2O3 to Fe3O4, FeO, and finally Fe. However, the reducibility of Fe2O3/γ-Al2O3 and hydrotalcite-derived FeMg catalysts is hindered due to the strong interaction between Fe oxide and the support. The enhanced reducibility of Fe2O3 on AC contributes to the high catalytic performance among the tested catalysts, and the redox cycle of Fe oxide is likely crucial in CO2ODHP. Ceria (CeO2) is a good choice of oxide support because it has the ability to store/release oxygen and thus may induce direct CO bond scission of CO2, while also providing available lattice oxygen for coke suppression. The Fe3Ni catalyst, which consists of oxidized Fe and metallic Ni, shows promising selectivity toward propylene via the CO2-ODHP pathway. The combination of Fe and Ni on a ceria support can enhance the catalytic activity for CO2-ODHP by promoting the oxidative CH bond cleavage of propane, leading to enhanced performance in CO2-ODHP reactions. The effects of bimetallic interactions on the catalytic reduction of CO2 by propane have been discussed, with Pt-based bimetallic catalysts outperforming Pd-based catalysts in terms of conversion and stability. The selectivity of FexNiy catalysts toward CO2-ODHP can be modified by changing the Ni content in the bimetallic composition, with increasing the Ni atomic ratio above 1 altering the selectivity to favor the dry reforming pathway rather than CO2-ODHP. CeO2 is described as a readily reducible oxide with the ability to reversibly convert between Ce4+ and Ce3+, which is critical for oxygen exchange properties that are important for CO2 activation. The Fe3Ni1 catalyst, which consists of metallic Ni and oxidized Fe under reaction conditions, is found to favor oxidative C-H bond cleavage via the CO2-ODHP pathway. Future research efforts could focus on developing new support materials that enhance the reducibility and stability of Fe oxide-based catalysts, as well as optimizing the bimetallic composition to improve selectivity toward CO2-ODHP. Additionally, further investigation into the reaction mechanism and the role of the redox cycle of Fe oxide in CO2-ODHP could provide insights into the optimization of catalytic systems for this process.

                Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的性能受到其还原性和支撑材料性质的显著影响\[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\]。活性碳被确定为在二氧化碳存在下将乙苯脱氢至苯乙烯的最合适的支撑材料，铁氧化物表现出最佳的活性和较慢的失活，与铬和铜氧化物相比\[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\]。所用活性碳的类型和铁含量对催化行为有重大影响，铁的最佳含量为5 wt.%\[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\]。添加的碱金属氧化物，如钾，提高了铁氧化物在活性碳上的活性，最佳的碱金属/Fe原子比为0.1\[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\]。催化测试后形成碳沉积物或支撑材料的气化，可归因于反应机制和CO2-ODHP过程催化系统的优化\[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\]。Fe2O3/AC的还原性得到改善，其还原过程从Fe2O3到Fe3O4，FeO，最后到Fe\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6\]。然而，Fe2O3/γ-Al2O3和水滑石衍生的FeMg催化剂的还原性受到Fe氧化物与支撑材料之间强烈相互作用的阻碍\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6\]。Fe2O3在AC上的增强还原性对测试催化剂的高催化性能有贡献，Fe氧化物的氧化还原循环在CO2ODHP中可能是关键的\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6\]。CeO2是一种良好的氧化物支撑材料，因为它具有储存/释放氧气的能力，从而可能诱导CO2的直接CO键断裂，同时提供可用于焦炭抑制的晶格氧\[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w\]。Fe3Ni催化剂，由氧化Fe和金属Ni组成，通过CO2-ODHP途径对丙烯显示出有希望的选择性\[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w\]。Fe和Ni在CeO2支撑材料上的组合可以通过促进丙烷的氧化性CH键断裂，增强CO2-ODHP的催化活性，从而提高CO2-ODHP反应的性能\[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w\]。已经讨论了双金属相互作用对丙烷催化还原CO2的影响，Pt基双金属催化剂在转化率和稳定性方面优于Pd基催化剂\[10.1002/aic.16670\]。FexNiy催化剂对CO2-ODHP的选择性可以通过改变双金属组成中的Ni含量来修改，增加Ni原子比超过1会改变选择性，使其有利于干重整途径，而不是CO2-ODHP\[10.1002/aic.16670\]。CeO2被描述为一种可逆地在Ce4+和Ce3+之间转换的可还原氧化物，这对CO2激活中重要的氧交换性质至关重要\[10.1002/aic.16670\]。在反应条件下，由金属Ni和氧化Fe组成的Fe3Ni1催化剂被发现有利于通过CO2-ODHP途径进行氧化性C-H键断裂\[10.1002/aic.16670\]。

                综合理解与未来展望：从本综述中讨论的研究中得出的发现表明，Fe氧化物基催化剂的还原性和支撑材料在它们的CO2-ODHP反应性能中起着重要作用。活性碳被确定为在二氧化碳存在下将乙苯脱氢至苯乙烯的最合适的支撑材料，铁氧化物表现出最佳的活性和较慢的失活，与铬和铜氧化物相比。所用活性碳的类型和铁含量对催化行为有重大影响，铁的最佳含量为5 wt.%。添加的碱金属氧化物，如钾，提高了铁氧化物在活性碳上的活性，最佳的碱金属/Fe原子比为0.1。催化测试后形成碳沉积物或支撑材料的气化，可归因于反应机制和CO2-ODHP过程催化系统的优化。Fe2O3/AC的还原性得到改善，其还原过程从Fe2O3到Fe3O4，FeO，最后到Fe。然而，Fe2O3/γ-Al2O3和水滑石衍生的FeMg催化剂的还原性受到Fe氧化物与支撑材料之间强烈相互作用的阻碍。Fe2O3在AC上的增强还原性对测试催化剂的高催化性能有贡献，Fe氧化物的氧化还原循环在CO2ODHP中可能是关键的。CeO2是一种良好的氧化物支撑材料，因为它具有储存/释放氧气的能力，从而可能诱导CO2的直接CO键断裂，同时提供可用于焦炭抑制的晶格氧。Fe3Ni催化剂，由氧化Fe和金属Ni组成，通过CO2-ODHP途径对丙烯显示出有希望的选择性。Fe和Ni在CeO2支撑材料上的组合可以通过促进丙烷的氧化性CH键断裂，增强CO2-ODHP的催化活性，从而提高CO2-ODHP反应的性能。已经讨论了双金属相互作用对丙烷催化还原CO2的影响，Pt基双金属催化剂在转化率和稳定性方面优于Pd基催化剂。FexNiy催化剂对CO2-ODHP的选择性可以通过改变双金属组成中的Ni含量来修改，增加Ni原子比超过1会改变选择性，使其有利于干重整途径，而不是CO2-ODHP。CeO2被描述为一种可逆地在Ce4+和Ce3+之间转换的可还原氧化物，这对CO2激活中重要的氧交换性质至关重要。在反应条件下，由金属Ni和氧化Fe组成的Fe3Ni1催化剂被发现有利于通过CO2-ODHP途径进行氧化性C-H键断裂。未来的研究工作可以集中在开发新的支撑材料，以增强Fe氧化物基催化剂的还原性和稳定性，以及优化双金属组成以提高CO2-ODHP的选择性。此外，对反应机制和Fe氧化物在CO2-ODHP中的氧化还原循环的作用的进一步调查，可以为优化该过程的催化系统提供见解。
