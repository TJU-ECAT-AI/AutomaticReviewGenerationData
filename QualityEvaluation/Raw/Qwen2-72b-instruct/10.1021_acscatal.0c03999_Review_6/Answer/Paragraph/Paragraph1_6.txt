                    The performance of Fe oxide-based catalysts in CO2-ODHP reactions is significantly influenced by their reducibility and the choice of support materials \[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\]. Active carbon has been identified as the most suitable support material for the dehydrogenation of ethylbenzene to styrene in the presence of carbon dioxide, with iron oxide showing superior activity and slower deactivation compared to chromium and copper oxides \[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\]. The type of active carbon used and the iron content significantly impact the catalytic behavior, with the optimum amount of iron being 5 wt.% \[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\]. The addition of alkaline metal oxides, particularly potassium, enhances the activity of iron oxide supported on active carbon, with the best alkali metal/Fe atomic ratio being 0.1 \[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\]. The formation of carbon deposits or gasification of the support after catalytic tests can be attributed to the reaction mechanism and the optimization of catalytic systems for the CO2-ODHP process \[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\].

                    The reducibility of Fe2O3/AC is notably improved, with its reduction proceeding through Fe2O3 to Fe3O4, FeO, and finally Fe \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6\]. However, the reducibility of Fe2O3/γ-Al2O3 and hydrotalcite-derived FeMg catalysts is hindered due to the strong interaction between Fe oxide and the support \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6\]. For instance, when Fe2O3 is loaded on γ-Al2O3, a spinel phase FeAl2O3 is formed, which is difficult to reduce; Fe3+ cations are reduced to Fe2+ ions that are strongly stabilized in the MgO matrix \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6\]. The enhanced reducibility of Fe2O3 on AC contributes to the high catalytic performance among the tested catalysts, and the redox cycle of Fe oxide is likely crucial in CO2ODHP \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6\].

                    Ceria (CeO2) is a good choice of oxide support for Fe oxide-based catalysts in CO2-ODHP reactions due to its ability to store and release oxygen, which may induce direct CO bond scission of CO2, while also providing available lattice oxygen for coke suppression \[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w\]. The Fe3Ni catalyst, consisting of oxidized Fe and metallic Ni, shows promising selectivity toward propylene via the CO2-ODHP pathway, suggesting that the combination of Fe and Ni on a ceria support can enhance the catalytic activity for CO2-ODHP by promoting the oxidative CH bond cleavage of propane \[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w\].

                    The effects of bimetallic interactions on the catalytic reduction of CO2 by propane have been investigated, revealing that Pt-based bimetallic catalysts outperform Pd-based catalysts in terms of conversion and stability \[10.1002/aic.16670\]. The selectivity of FexNiy catalysts toward CO2-ODHP can be modified by changing the Ni content in the bimetallic composition, with increasing the Ni atomic ratio above 1 altering the selectivity to favor the dry reforming pathway rather than CO2-ODHP \[10.1002/aic.16670\]. CeO2, as a support material, may influence the performance of Fe oxide-based catalysts in CO2-ODHP reactions by affecting the reducibility and stability of the catalysts, as well as the availability of oxygen for CO2 activation \[10.1002/aic.16670\]. The Fe3Ni1 catalyst, consisting of metallic Ni and oxidized Fe under reaction conditions, is found to favor oxidative C-H bond cleavage via the CO2-ODHP pathway \[10.1002/aic.16670\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The findings from these studies underscore the critical role of reducibility and support materials in the performance of Fe oxide-based catalysts in CO2-ODHP reactions. Active carbon and ceria (CeO2) emerge as promising support materials due to their ability to enhance the catalytic activity and stability of Fe oxide catalysts. The redox cycle of Fe oxide and the availability of oxygen for CO2 activation are key factors in the catalytic performance. Future research efforts should focus on optimizing the interaction between Fe oxide and support materials, exploring the effects of bimetallic interactions, and understanding the mechanisms involved in CO2-ODHP reactions. The development of novel catalysts with enhanced reducibility and stability, as well as the exploration of alternative support materials, could lead to significant improvements in the efficiency and selectivity of CO2-ODHP processes.

                    Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的性能受到其还原性和支撑材料选择的显著影响\[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\]。活性碳被确认为在二氧化碳存在下将乙苯脱氢为苯乙烯的最合适的支撑材料，铁氧化物表现出比铬和铜氧化物更优的活性和更慢的失活速率\[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\]。所用活性碳的类型和铁含量对催化行为有重大影响，铁的最佳含量为5 wt.%\[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\]。添加碱金属氧化物，特别是钾，提高了铁氧化物在活性碳上的活性，最佳的碱金属/Fe原子比为0.1\[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\]。催化测试后形成碳沉积物或支撑材料的气化可归因于反应机制和CO2-ODHP过程催化系统的优化\[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7\]。

                    Fe2O3/AC的还原性显著提高，其还原过程从Fe2O3到Fe3O4，FeO，最后到Fe\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6\]。然而，Fe2O3/γ-Al2O3和水滑石衍生的FeMg催化剂的还原性受到Fe氧化物与支撑材料之间强相互作用的阻碍\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6\]。例如，当Fe2O3加载在γ-Al2O3上时，形成难以还原的尖晶石相FeAl2O3；Fe3+离子被还原为在MgO基质中强烈稳定的Fe2+离子\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6\]。Fe2O3在AC上的增强还原性对测试催化剂的高催化性能有贡献，Fe氧化物的氧化还原循环在CO2ODHP中可能是关键\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6\]。

                    由于CeO2（铈氧化物）能够储存和释放氧气，这可能诱导CO2的直接CO键断裂，同时提供可用于焦炭抑制的晶格氧，因此它是Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的良好选择\[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w\]。Fe3Ni催化剂，由氧化Fe和金属Ni组成，通过CO2-ODHP途径对丙烯显示出有希望的选择性，表明Fe和Ni在铈氧化物上的组合可以通过促进丙烷的氧化CH键断裂来增强CO2-ODHP的催化活性\[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w\]。

                    对丙烷催化还原CO2的双金属相互作用的影响进行了研究，发现基于Pt的双金属催化剂在转化率和稳定性方面优于基于Pd的催化剂\[10.1002/aic.16670\]。通过改变双金属组成中的Ni含量，FexNiy催化剂对CO2-ODHP的选择性可以被修改，Ni原子比超过1时选择性倾向于干重整途径而不是CO2-ODHP\[10.1002/aic.16670\]。作为支撑材料，CeO2可能通过影响Fe氧化物基催化剂的还原性和稳定性，以及CO2活化所需的氧的可用性，影响Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的性能\[10.1002/aic.16670\]。在反应条件下由金属Ni和氧化Fe组成的Fe3Ni1催化剂被发现有利于通过CO2-ODHP途径的氧化C-H键断裂\[10.1002/aic.16670\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望：这些研究的发现强调了还原性和支撑材料在Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的性能中的关键作用。活性碳和铈氧化物（CeO2）作为有前途的支撑材料脱颖而出，因为它们能够增强Fe氧化物催化剂的催化活性和稳定性。Fe氧化物的氧化还原循环和CO2活化所需的氧的可用性是催化性能的关键因素。未来的研究工作应集中在优化Fe氧化物和支撑材料之间的相互作用，探索双金属相互作用的影响，以及理解CO2-ODHP反应中涉及的机制。开发具有增强还原性和稳定性的新型催化剂，以及探索替代支撑材料，可能会导致CO2-ODHP过程的效率和选择性的显著提高。
