1.The literature does not provide information on comprehensive characterization methods and analytical approaches for verifying the successful incorporation and homogeneous distribution of dopants in metal oxide catalysts. However, it does mention High Sensitivity-Low Energy Ion Scattering (HS-LEIS) as a technique that provides quantitative compositional information about the outermost atomic layer of a material, which is crucial in the fundamental study of catalysis and the optimization of industrial catalysts. HS-LEIS compares favorably to XPS (ESCA) and SIMS due to its specificity for the outermost atomic layer and straightforward quantification. The technique determines the mass of surface atoms by measuring the energy from ions that are scattered from the surface, enabling the identification of elements with masses in the range from 12 to 60 amu using 4He+ ions. For heavier elements, 20Ne+ ions can be used to improve mass resolution. HS-LEIS is orders of magnitude more sensitive than conventional LEIS, allowing for Static LEIS where good spectra are obtained while less than 0.5% of a monolayer is sputtered from the sample surface. Quantification is straightforward, as HS-LEIS peaks are proportional to the amount of scattering surface atoms, and peak areas can be expressed as fractions of the peak area for a pure element, yielding surface fractions for unknown samples.
