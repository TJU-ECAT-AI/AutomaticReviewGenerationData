1.The literature does not provide information on comprehensive characterization methods and analytical approaches for verifying the successful incorporation and homogeneous distribution of dopants in metal oxide catalysts. However, it does mention High Sensitivity-Low Energy Ion Scattering (HS-LEIS) as a technique that provides quantitative compositional information about the outermost atomic layer of a material, which is crucial in the fundamental study of catalysis and the optimization of industrial catalysts. HS-LEIS compares favorably to XPS (ESCA) and SIMS in terms of information depth and quantification of results. The technique determines the mass of surface atoms by measuring the energy from ions that are scattered from the surface, which can help in identifying the presence of dopants and their distribution on the catalyst surface. The sensitivity and ease of quantification of HS-LEIS make it a useful tool for studying the relationship between dopant presence, structural changes, and enhanced catalytic performance.
