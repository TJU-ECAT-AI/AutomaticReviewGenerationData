1.The literature does not provide information on comprehensive characterization methods and analytical approaches for verifying the successful incorporation and homogeneous distribution of dopants in metal oxide catalysts. However, it does mention High Sensitivity-Low Energy Ion Scattering (HS-LEIS) as a technique that can analyze the chemical composition of the outermost atomic layer of a material, which is crucial in the fundamental study of catalysis and the optimization of industrial catalysts. HS-LEIS compares favorably to XPS (ESCA) and SIMS in terms of information depth and quantification of results. The technique determines the mass of surface atoms by measuring the energy from ions that are scattered from the surface, providing sensitive analysis for the outermost atomic layer where catalytic processes take place. HS-LEIS can also provide information about layers immediately beneath the surface, which is relevant for the dispersion of the active catalytic component on the surface of the catalyst. The availability of information about the surface composition can help in establishing a clear relationship between dopant presence, structural changes, and enhanced catalytic performance.
