                    Comprehensive characterization methods for verifying dopant incorporation, distribution, and effects on the structure and catalytic performance of doped metal oxide catalysts include synchrotron X-ray diffraction (XRD) \[10.1021/jp103958u\], X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS) \[10.1021/jp103958u\], Raman spectroscopy \[10.1021/jp103958u\], and density functional theory (DFT) calculations \[10.1021/jp103958u\]. XAS confirmed Ni in a +2 oxidation state, while EXAFS indicated a distorted NiO shell. DFT suggested modest Ce charge redistribution, and Raman spectroscopy revealed shifts in ceria peak frequency, corroborating XRD and EXAFS data. Catalytic testing with H2 and in situ XRD, XANES, and DRIFT during the WGS reaction provided insights into structural changes and dopant effects. The solubility limit for Ni was found to be closer to 10% \[10.1021/jp103958u\]. High Sensitivity-Low Energy Ion Scattering (HS-LEIS) is a sensitive technique for analyzing the outermost atomic layer of materials, crucial for studying dopant incorporation in metal oxide catalysts \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.10.012\]. It compares favorably to XPS and SIMS due to its specificity and straightforward quantification. HS-LEIS determines surface atom mass by measuring scattered ion energy, identifying elements with masses 12-60 amu using 4He+ ions, with improved resolution for heavier atoms using 20Ne+ ions. Its sensitivity allows Static LEIS, obtaining spectra while sputtering less than 0.5% of a monolayer. Quantification is simple, with peak areas proportional to surface atom fractions. HS-LEIS can analyze layers beneath the surface, aiding in understanding dopant dispersion and its impact on catalytic performance. Its sensitivity and quantification capabilities make it a powerful tool for establishing the relationship between dopant presence, structural changes, and enhanced catalytic activity \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.10.012\].

                    验证掺杂剂在金属氧化物催化剂中掺入、分布及其对结构和催化性能影响的全面表征方法包括同步加速器X射线衍射(XRD) \[10.1021/jp103958u\]、X射线吸收光谱(XAS) \[10.1021/jp103958u\]、拉曼光谱 \[10.1021/jp103958u\]和密度泛函理论(DFT)计算 \[10.1021/jp103958u\]。XAS确认Ni处于+2氧化态，而EXAFS表明NiO壳层发生畸变。DFT建议Ce的电荷重新分布适度，而拉曼光谱揭示了CeO2峰频率的偏移，证实了XRD和EXAFS数据。使用H2进行的催化测试和在WGS反应中进行的原位XRD、XANES和DRIFT提供了对结构变化和掺杂剂效应的见解。Ni的溶解度极限接近10% \[10.1021/jp103958u\]。高灵敏度低能离子散射(HS-LEIS)是一种敏感的技术，用于分析材料最外层原子层，对于研究金属氧化物催化剂中的掺杂剂掺入至关重要 \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.10.012\]。它与XPS和SIMS相比具有优势，因为它的特异性和直接量化。HS-LEIS通过测量散射离子能量确定表面原子质量，使用4He+离子识别12-60 amu质量范围内的元素，使用20Ne+离子提高对较重原子的分辨率。其灵敏度允许静态LEIS，在剥离不到0.5%单层的情况下获得光谱。量化简单，峰面积与表面原子分数成正比。HS-LEIS可以分析表面以下的层，有助于理解掺杂剂分散及其对催化性能的影响。其灵敏度和量化能力使其成为建立掺杂剂存在、结构变化和增强催化活性之间关系的强大工具 \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.10.012\].
