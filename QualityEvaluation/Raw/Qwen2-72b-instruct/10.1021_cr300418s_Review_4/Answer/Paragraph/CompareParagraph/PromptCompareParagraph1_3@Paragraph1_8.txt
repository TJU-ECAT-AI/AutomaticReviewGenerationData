These techniques confirmed Ni in a +2 oxidation state, indicated a distorted NiO first shell bond, suggested modest charge redistribution for Ce, and revealed frequency shifts and broadening for the F2g ceria peak, corroborating XRD and EXAFS data. Chemical reactivity tests using H2 and in situ XRD, XANES, and DRIFT data during the WGS reaction provided insights into catalytic performance. The solubility limit for Ni was closer to 10%, as indicated by similar shifts in samples containing 10% and 20% Ni \[10.1021/jp103958u\]. High Sensitivity-Low Energy Ion Scattering (HS-LEIS) is a pivotal technique for characterizing doped metal oxide catalysts, offering quantitative surface composition data \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.10.012\]. It excels in specificity for the outermost atomic layer and straightforward quantification, surpassing XPS and SIMS in sensitivity and information depth. HS-LEIS identifies elements with masses 12-60 amu using 4He+ ions, with enhanced mass resolution for heavier atoms using 20Ne+ ions. Its sensitivity allows Static LEIS, minimizing sample surface sputtering. Quantification is achieved by correlating peak areas to surface atom fractions, with calibration plots for precise quantification. HS-LEIS's sensitivity and analysis of the outermost layer, where catalytic processes occur, are essential for understanding dopant effects on structure and catalytic performance \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.10.012\].
