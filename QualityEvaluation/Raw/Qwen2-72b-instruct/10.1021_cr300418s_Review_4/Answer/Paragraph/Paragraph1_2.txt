                    Comprehensive characterization methods, including synchrotron-based X-ray diffraction (XRD) \[10.1021/jp103958u\], X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS) \[10.1021/jp103958u\], Raman spectroscopy \[10.1021/jp103958u\], and density functional theory (DFT) calculations \[10.1021/jp103958u\], were employed to verify dopant incorporation and distribution in metal oxide catalysts. XAS confirmed Ni in a +2 oxidation state, while EXAFS indicated a distorted NiO first shell bond. DFT suggested modest charge redistribution for Ce. Raman spectroscopy revealed a frequency shift and broadening for the ceria peak, corroborating XRD and EXAFS data. Chemical reactivity tests using H2 and in situ XRD, XANES, and DRIFT data during the WGS reaction provided insights into catalytic performance. The solubility limit for Ni was found to be closer to 10% \[10.1021/jp103958u\].

                    High Sensitivity-Low Energy Ion Scattering (HS-LEIS) \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.10.012\] is a sensitive technique for analyzing the outermost atomic layer of materials, crucial for studying dopant incorporation and distribution in metal oxide catalysts. It measures the energy of scattered ions to determine surface atom masses, offering advantages over XPS and SIMS with its specificity and straightforward quantification. HS-LEIS uses 4He+ ions for elements with masses 12-60 amu and 20Ne+ for heavier atoms, achieving ppm detection limits and good mass resolution. Its sensitivity allows Static LEIS, minimizing surface disruption. Quantification is achieved by comparing peak areas to pure element references, facilitating the study of dopant effects on catalytic performance and structural changes.

                    Crucial methods for verifying dopant incorporation and distribution in metal oxide catalysts include XRD \[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.03.061\], Raman spectroscopy, BET, SEM, XPS, CH4 TPR-MS, and O2-TPD. These techniques elucidate the relationship between dopants, structural changes, and enhanced catalytic performance, as demonstrated by cerium doping causing structural deformation, increasing crystal defects and oxygen vacancies, and thus improving oxygen mobility and catalytic efficiency.

                    采用包括基于同步加速器的X射线衍射(XRD) \[10.1021/jp103958u\]、X射线吸收光谱(XAS) \[10.1021/jp103958u\]、拉曼光谱 \[10.1021/jp103958u\]和密度泛函理论(DFT)计算 \[10.1021/jp103958u\]在内的综合表征方法，验证了金属氧化物催化剂中掺杂剂的掺入和分布。XAS确认Ni处于+2氧化态，而EXAFS表明NiO第一壳层的扭曲。DFT建议Ce的电荷重新分配适度。拉曼光谱揭示了铈峰的频率偏移和展宽，证实了XRD和EXAFS数据。使用H2的化学反应性测试和WGS反应期间的原位XRD、XANES和DRIFT数据提供了催化性能的见解。Ni的溶解度极限接近10% \[10.1021/jp103958u\]。

                    高灵敏度-低能离子散射(HS-LEIS) \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.10.012\]是一种敏感的技术，用于分析材料最外层原子层，对于研究金属氧化物催化剂中掺杂剂的掺入和分布至关重要。它通过测量散射离子的能量来确定表面原子质量，与XPS和SIMS相比，具有其特异性和直接量化的优势。HS-LEIS使用4He+离子来识别质量为12-60 amu的元素，使用20Ne+离子来识别更重的原子，实现ppm检测极限和良好的质量分辨率。其灵敏度允许静态LEIS，最小化表面扰动。通过将峰面积与纯元素参考进行比较来实现量化，促进对掺杂剂对催化性能和结构变化影响的研究。

                    验证金属氧化物催化剂中掺杂剂掺入和分布的关键方法包括XRD \[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.03.061\]、拉曼光谱、BET、SEM、XPS、CH4 TPR-MS和O2-TPD。这些技术阐明了掺杂剂、结构变化和增强催化性能之间的关系，如铈掺杂导致结构变形，增加晶体缺陷和氧空位，从而提高氧流动性和催化效率。
