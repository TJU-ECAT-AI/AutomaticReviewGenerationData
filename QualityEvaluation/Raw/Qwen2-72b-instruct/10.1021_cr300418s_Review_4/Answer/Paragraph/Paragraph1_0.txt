                    Comprehensive characterization methods, including synchrotron-based X-ray diffraction (XRD) \[10.1021/jp103958u\], X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS) \[10.1021/jp103958u\], Raman spectroscopy \[10.1021/jp103958u\], and density functional theory (DFT) calculations \[10.1021/jp103958u\], are essential for verifying dopant incorporation and distribution in metal oxide catalysts. XAS confirmed Ni in a +2 oxidation state, while EXAFS indicated a distorted NiO first shell bond. DFT calculations suggested modest charge redistribution for Ce. Raman spectroscopy showed a frequency shift and broadening for the ceria peak, corroborating XRD and EXAFS data. Chemical reactivity tests using H2 and in situ time-resolved XRD, XANES, and DRIFT data during the WGS reaction provided insights into catalytic performance. The solubility limit for Ni was found to be closer to 10% \[10.1021/jp103958u\]. High Sensitivity-Low Energy Ion Scattering (HS-LEIS) is a sensitive technique for analyzing the outermost atomic layer of materials, crucial for studying dopant incorporation and distribution in metal oxide catalysts \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.10.012\]. It measures the energy from ions scattered from the surface to determine the mass of surface atoms, identifying elements with masses from 12 to 60 amu using 4He+ ions, with improved resolution for heavier elements using 20Ne+ ions. HS-LEIS is orders of magnitude more sensitive than conventional LEIS, allowing Static LEIS with minimal surface sputtering. Quantification is straightforward, with peak areas proportional to surface atom fractions. HS-LEIS compares favorably to XPS and SIMS for information depth and quantification, providing sensitive analysis for the outermost atomic layer where catalytic processes occur. It also offers insights into layers beneath the surface, relevant for the dispersion of the active catalytic component. The technique's sensitivity and quantification capabilities make it a valuable tool for establishing the relationship between dopant presence, structural changes, and enhanced catalytic performance \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.10.012\]. Crucial characterization methods for verifying dopant incorporation and distribution in metal oxide catalysts include XRD, Raman spectroscopy, BET, SEM, XPS, CH4 TPR-MS, and O2-TPD \[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.03.061\]. These techniques elucidate the relationship between dopants, structural changes, and enhanced catalytic performance. For instance, cerium doping causes structural deformation, increasing crystal defects and oxygen vacancy concentration, which facilitates oxygen mobility and boosts catalytic activity.

                    全面的表征方法，包括基于同步加速器的X射线衍射(XRD) \[10.1021/jp103958u\]，X射线吸收光谱(XAS) \[10.1021/jp103958u\]，拉曼光谱 \[10.1021/jp103958u\]，以及密度泛函理论(DFT)计算 \[10.1021/jp103958u\]，对于验证金属氧化物催化剂中掺杂剂的掺入和分布至关重要。XAS确认Ni处于+2氧化态，而扩展X射线吸收精细结构(EXAFS)表明NiO第一壳层的扭曲。DFT计算表明Ce的电荷重新分布适度。拉曼光谱显示了铈峰的频率偏移和展宽，这与XRD和EXAFS数据相吻合。使用H2的化学反应性测试和在WGS反应中进行的原位时间分辨XRD，XANES和DRIFT数据提供了对催化性能的见解。Ni的溶解度极限接近10% \[10.1021/jp103958u\]。高灵敏度低能离子散射(HS-LEIS)是一种敏感的技术，用于分析材料最外层原子层，对于研究金属氧化物催化剂中掺杂剂的掺入和分布至关重要 \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.10.012\]。它测量从表面散射的离子的能量，以确定表面原子的质量，使用4He+离子识别12至60 amu质量的元素，使用20Ne+离子为较重元素提供改进的分辨率。HS-LEIS的灵敏度比常规LEIS高几个数量级，允许静态LEIS，同时表面溅射最小。量化是直接的，峰面积与表面原子分数成正比。HS-LEIS与XPS和SIMS相比，在信息深度和量化方面表现良好，为催化过程发生的最外层原子层提供了敏感的分析。它还为活性催化组分的分散提供了对表面以下层的见解。该技术的灵敏度和量化能力使其成为建立掺杂剂存在，结构变化和增强催化性能之间关系的宝贵工具 \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.10.012\]。验证金属氧化物催化剂中掺杂剂掺入和分布的关键表征方法包括XRD，拉曼光谱，BET，SEM，XPS，CH4 TPR-MS和O2-TPD \[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.03.061\]。这些技术阐明了掺杂剂，结构变化和增强催化性能之间的关系。例如，铈掺杂导致结构变形，增加晶体缺陷和氧空位浓度，这促进了氧的流动性并提高了催化活性。
