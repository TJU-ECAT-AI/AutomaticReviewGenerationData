                    Comparative analysis of noble metal-based and metal oxide-based catalysts for nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins reveals that Pt-based catalysts excel due to their unique bond activation properties, leading to commercial use, notably in the Pacol process (1968) and UOP's Oleflex for butadiene and propylene production \[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]. Metal oxide catalysts, particularly CrOx, have industrial applications and show promise, with gallium, indium, vanadium, zinc, and molybdenum oxides also demonstrating success \[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]. A novel Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O catalyst demonstrates high activity and selectivity for ethene and propene formation from ethane and propane, respectively, in nonoxidative dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. Its enhanced performance is attributed to PtGa alloys, which inhibit product alkene adsorption and suppress undesired products like methane. Ga addition also reduces coke formation, leading to lower deactivation and higher stability. Optimizing the Ga/Pt ratio achieves peak activity and selectivity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. In the nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins, gallium-modified Pt/Al2O3 catalysts show enhanced selectivity to propylene, reduced deactivation, and decreased carbon formation compared to pure Pt/Al2O3 \[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00058-7\]. Gallium's effect is minimal on the acidity function but modifies the metallic phase structure, improving performance in propane dehydrogenation \[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00058-7\]. A novel Pt/Mg(In)(Al)O catalyst demonstrates high effectiveness for ethane and propane dehydrogenation, achieving maximum activity at a bulk In/Pt ratio of 0.48, with nearly 100% selectivity to alkene \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008\]. The alloying of Pt with In reduces coke deposition, enhancing stability and selectivity, making it a promising candidate for industrial light olefin production \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008\].

The optimization of the Ga/Pt and In/Pt ratios in Pt-based catalysts for peak activity and selectivity suggests a promising direction for catalyst design. Moreover, the investigation of the role of trace sulfur in suppressing hydrogenolysis and coke formation could lead to the development of sulfur-tolerant catalysts for enhanced stability and performance. The exploration of new metal oxide compositions and the integration of noble metals with metal oxides could also open new avenues for improving the activity and stability of catalysts under high-temperature conditions.

                    对于非氧化脱氢轻烯烃的催化过程，基于贵金属和金属氧化物的催化剂的比较分析显示，铂基催化剂因其独特的键激活特性而表现出色，这导致了其在商业应用中的广泛使用，特别是在1968年的Pacol工艺和UOP的Oleflex工艺中用于丁二烯和丙烯的生产\[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]。金属氧化物催化剂，尤其是CrOx，具有工业应用，并显示出潜力，而镓、铟、钒、锌和钼氧化物也表现出成功\[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]。一种新型的Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O催化剂在非氧化脱氢过程中对乙烷和丙烷分别形成乙烯和丙烯表现出高活性和选择性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。其增强的性能归因于PtGa合金，它们抑制了产物烯烃的吸附并抑制了不需要的产物如甲烷的生成。镓的添加也减少了焦炭的形成，导致较低的失活和更高的稳定性。优化Ga/Pt比率可以实现峰值活性和选择性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。在非氧化脱氢轻烯烃中，镓改性的Pt/Al2O3催化剂显示出对丙烯的增强选择性，降低了失活和碳形成，与纯Pt/Al2O3相比\[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00058-7\]。镓对酸性功能的影响最小，但改变了金属相结构，提高了丙烷脱氢的性能\[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00058-7\]。一种新型的Pt/Mg(In)(Al)O催化剂对乙烷和丙烷脱氢表现出高效率，当In/Pt的体积比为0.48时，达到最大活性，对烯烃的选择性接近100%\[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008\]。铂与铟的合金化减少了焦炭的沉积，提高了稳定性和选择性，使其成为工业轻烯烃生产有前景的候选催化剂\[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008\]。
