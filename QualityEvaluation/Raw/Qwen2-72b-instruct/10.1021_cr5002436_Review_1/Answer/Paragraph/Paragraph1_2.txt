                Comparative analysis of noble metal-based and metal oxide-based catalysts for nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins: composition, effectiveness, and industrial applications. Platinum (Pt)-based catalysts, due to their unique activation properties and low CC cleavage activity, excel in nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins, making Pt the only noble metal in commercial use \[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]. Historically, the Pacol process, utilizing Pt on alumina, was the first industrial alkane dehydrogenation technology \[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]. Metal oxide-based catalysts, notably CrOx, have industrial applications, with gallium, indium, vanadium, zinc, and molybdenum oxides showing promising results \[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]. A novel Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O catalyst demonstrates high activity and selectivity for ethene and propene formation from ethane and propane, respectively, in nonoxidative dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. The catalyst's performance is attributed to PtGa alloys, which dilute Pt ensembles and inhibit product alkene adsorption, suppressing undesired products like methane. Ga addition also reduces coke formation, enhancing stability \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. Recent developments focus on optimizing the Ga/Pt ratio for peak activity and selectivity, with selectivity reaching nearly 100% at Ga/Pt = 5.4 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. In the nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins, gallium-modified Pt/Al2O3 catalysts exhibit enhanced selectivity to propylene, reduced deactivation, and carbon formation compared to pure Pt/Al2O3 \[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00058-7\]. Gallium's effect on the acidity function is minimal but significantly alters the metallic phase structure, improving performance in propane dehydrogenation \[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00058-7\]. This suggests that metal oxide-based catalysts, when combined with noble metals, offer advantages in activity and stability for dehydrogenation processes, making them potentially more effective for industrial applications in light olefin production \[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00058-7\].

                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Taking into consideration the advancements and findings discussed in the file, there lies an opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of bimetallic catalysts that combine the advantages of noble metals and metal oxides, optimizing the synergy between the two for enhanced activity and stability. The exploration of novel supports that can further suppress coke formation and improve catalyst longevity is also a promising direction. Additionally, the integration of continuous catalyst regeneration (CCR) technology with metal oxide-based catalysts could lead to significant breakthroughs in maintaining stable activity over extended periods. The potential for lower cost and improved stability under high-temperature conditions offered by metal oxide-based catalysts, when combined with the high activity and selectivity of noble metal-based catalysts, presents a compelling avenue for future research in the nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins.

                对比分析了用于轻烯烃非氧化脱氢的贵金属基和金属氧化物基催化剂：组成、效果和工业应用。由于其独特的活化性能和低CC断裂活性，铂（Pt）基催化剂在轻烯烃的非氧化脱氢中表现出色，使Pt成为商业上唯一使用的贵金属\[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]。历史上，Pacol工艺，使用Pt在氧化铝上，是第一个工业烷烃脱氢技术\[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]。金属氧化物基催化剂，特别是CrOx，在工业上有应用，而镓、铟、钒、锌和钼氧化物显示出有希望的结果\[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]。一种新型的Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O催化剂在非氧化脱氢中表现出从乙烷和丙烷中形成乙烯和丙烯的高活性和选择性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。催化剂的性能归因于PtGa合金，它们稀释了Pt集合体并抑制了产物烯烃的吸附，抑制了不需要的产品如甲烷的生成。Ga的添加也减少了焦炭的形成，提高了稳定性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。最近的发展集中在优化Ga/Pt比以达到峰值活性和选择性，选择性在Ga/Pt = 5.4时接近100%\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。在轻烯烃的非氧化脱氢中，镓改性的Pt/Al2O3催化剂与纯Pt/Al2O3相比，显示出对丙烯的增强选择性，降低了失活和碳形成\[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00058-7\]。镓对酸性功能的影响最小，但显著改变了金属相结构，改善了丙烷脱氢的性能\[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00058-7\]。这表明，当与贵金属结合时，金属氧化物基催化剂在脱氢过程中提供了在活性和稳定性方面的优势，使它们在轻烯烃生产中的工业应用中可能更有效\[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00058-7\]。
