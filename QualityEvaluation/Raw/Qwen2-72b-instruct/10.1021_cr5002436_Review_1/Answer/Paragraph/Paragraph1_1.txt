                    The comparative analysis of noble metal-based and metal oxide-based catalysts for nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins, particularly propane, reveals distinct advantages and applications for each type \[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]. Noble metal-based catalysts, notably Platinum (Pt), excel in commercial use due to their unique ability to activate paraffinic CH bonds without cleaving CC bonds \[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]. The Pacol process, using Pt on alumina, was the first industrial alkane dehydrogenation technology in 1968, and UOP's Oleflex process, an advancement, produces butadiene and propylene \[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]. Metal oxides, especially CrOx, have industrial applications, with promising results from gallium, indium, vanadium, zinc, and molybdenum oxides \[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]. A novel Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O catalyst demonstrates high activity and selectivity for ethene and propene formation from ethane and propane, respectively, in nonoxidative dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. The PtGa alloys formed inhibit product alkene adsorption, suppressing undesired products like methane, while Ga addition suppresses coke formation, enhancing stability \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. Recent developments focus on optimizing the Ga/Pt ratio for peak activity and selectivity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. A Pt-based catalyst supported on Mg(Ga)(Al)O mixed oxide shows strong dependence on Ga content, with peak activity at Ga/Pt = 1.45.4 and nearly 100% selectivity at Ga/Pt = 5.4 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. Hydrogen addition to the feed increases alkene formation and enhances hydrogen-assisted dehydrogenation, but beyond the peak, it contributes to alkene hydrogenation, reducing the net dehydrogenation rate \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. Hydrogen addition has little effect on alkene selectivity, which remains near 100% \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. The presence of Ga suppresses coke formation, with less coke formed during propane dehydrogenation than ethane dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\].

                    对于非氧化脱氢轻烯烃，特别是丙烷，的催化过程，对贵金属基和金属氧化物基催化剂的比较分析揭示了各自独特的优势和应用领域\[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]。贵金属基催化剂，尤其是铂（Pt），因其独特的能力而商业上表现出色，这种能力在于激活石蜡CH键而不裂解CC键\[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]。1968年，采用Pt/Al2O3的Pacol工艺标志着工业烷烃脱氢技术的首次应用，而UOP的Oleflex工艺，作为其进步，生产丁二烯和丙烯\[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]。金属氧化物，特别是CrOx，具有工业应用，从镓、铟、钒、锌和钼氧化物中获得了有希望的结果\[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]。一种新型的Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O催化剂在非氧化脱氢中对乙烷和丙烷分别形成乙烯和丙烯显示出高活性和选择性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。形成的PtGa合金抑制了产物烯烃的吸附，抑制了不需要的产物如甲烷的生成，而镓的添加抑制了焦炭的形成，增强了稳定性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。最近的发展集中在优化Ga/Pt比率以达到峰值活性和选择性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。基于Mg(Ga)(Al)O混合氧化物的Pt基催化剂对Ga含量表现出强烈的依赖性，Ga/Pt = 1.45.4时活性达到峰值，Ga/Pt = 5.4时选择性接近100%\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。向进料中添加氢气增加了烯烃的形成并增强了氢辅助脱氢，但超过峰值，它会导致烯烃氢化，降低净脱氢速率\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。氢气添加对烯烃选择性的影响很小，选择性保持在接近100%\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。镓的存在抑制了焦炭的形成，在丙烷脱氢中形成的焦炭比在乙烷脱氢中少\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。
