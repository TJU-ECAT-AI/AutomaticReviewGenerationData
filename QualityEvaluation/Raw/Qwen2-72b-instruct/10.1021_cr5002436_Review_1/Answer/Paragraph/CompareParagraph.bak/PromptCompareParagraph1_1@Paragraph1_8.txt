Comparative analysis of noble metal-based and metal oxide-based catalysts for nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins reveals that Pt is the sole noble metal catalyst used commercially due to its superior activation of paraffinic CH bonds and low CC cleavage activity \[10.1021/cr5002436\_1\]. The Pacol process (1968) and UOP's Oleflex process, which produces butadiene and propylene, exemplify its industrial application \[10.1021/cr5002436\_1\]. Metal oxide catalysts, notably CrOx, have industrial use and show promise, with gallium, indium, vanadium, zinc, and molybdenum oxides also demonstrating success \[10.1021/cr5002436\_1\]. A novel Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O catalyst demonstrates high activity and selectivity for ethene and propene formation from ethane and propane, respectively, in nonoxidative dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. The PtGa alloys within the catalyst enhance performance by diluting Pt ensembles and inhibiting product alkene adsorption, suppressing undesired products like methane. Ga addition also reduces coke formation, improving stability. Recent developments focus on optimizing the Ga/Pt ratio for peak activity and selectivity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. The catalyst's high activity, selectivity, and stability are advantageous for industrial light olefin production. Hydrogen addition to the feed boosts alkene formation due to enhanced hydrogen-assisted dehydrogenation, with selectivity remaining near 100% \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. Gallium-modified Pt/Al2O3 catalysts exhibit enhanced selectivity to propylene, reduced catalyst deactivation, and decreased carbon formation compared to pure Pt/Al2O3 \[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00058-7\]. Gallium's effect on the metallic phase structure improves performance in propane dehydrogenation, suggesting advantages in industrial applications for light olefin production \[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00058-7\].
