                    Comparative analysis of noble metal-based and metal oxide-based catalysts for nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins reveals that Pt, due to its superior activation of paraffinic CH bonds and low CC cleavage activity, is the sole noble metal in commercial use \[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]. Historically, the Pacol process (1968) and UOP's Oleflex process utilized Pt for alkane dehydrogenation and butadiene/propylene production, respectively. Metal oxide-based catalysts, notably CrOx, have industrial applications and show promise, with gallium, indium, vanadium, zinc, and molybdenum oxides also demonstrating success \[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]. A novel Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O catalyst demonstrates high activity and selectivity for the nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins, forming ethene and propene from ethane and propane \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. The catalyst's effectiveness is attributed to PtGa alloys, which inhibit product alkene adsorption and suppress undesired methane formation. Ga addition also reduces coke formation, enhancing stability. Optimal Ga/Pt ratios achieve peak activity and selectivity, crucial for industrial light olefin production. The catalyst's high performance, selectivity, and stability, along with reduced coke formation, highlight its potential advantages over metal oxide-based catalysts in the field \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]. Gallium-modified Pt/Al2O3 catalysts exhibit enhanced selectivity to propylene, reduced catalyst deactivation, and decreased carbon formation \[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00058-7\]. Gallium's impact on the metallic phase structure, particularly after high-temperature reduction, leads to blocking and dilution effects on platinum sites, improving selectivity and stability. This suggests that combining metal oxide-based catalysts with noble metals can offer advantages in terms of activity and stability for industrial dehydrogenation processes \[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00058-7\].

The optimization of catalyst composition, particularly the Ga/Pt ratio in Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O catalysts, could lead to further improvements in activity and selectivity for industrial light olefin production. Moreover, the investigation of novel supports and the role of trace elements, such as sulfur, in enhancing catalyst stability and suppressing coke formation could open new avenues for catalyst design and process optimization. The potential for continuous catalyst regeneration (CCR) technology to maintain stable activity for years without shutdowns, as demonstrated with noble metal catalysts, could also be explored for metal oxide-based catalysts, enhancing their industrial applicability and economic viability.

                    比较分析表明，由于铂（Pt）对石蜡CH键的优异活化性能和较低的CC断裂活性，它是唯一在商业上使用的贵金属 \[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]。历史上，Pacol工艺（1968年）和UOP的Oleflex工艺分别利用Pt进行烷烃脱氢和丁二烯/丙烯生产。金属氧化物基催化剂，尤其是CrOx，具有工业应用并显示出前景，而镓、铟、钒、锌和钼氧化物也表现出成功 \[10.1021/cr5002436_1\]。一种新型的Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O催化剂在非氧化脱氢轻烯烃方面表现出高活性和选择性，能从乙烷和丙烷中形成乙烯和丙烯 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。该催化剂的效率归因于PtGa合金，它们抑制了产物烯烃的吸附并抑制了不需要的甲烷形成。镓的添加还减少了焦炭的形成，提高了稳定性。最佳的Ga/Pt比例实现了峰值活性和选择性，这对工业轻烯烃生产至关重要。该催化剂的高性能、选择性和稳定性，以及减少的焦炭形成，突显了它在该领域相对于金属氧化物基催化剂的潜在优势 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016\]。镓改性的Pt/Al2O3催化剂表现出对丙烯的增强选择性，催化剂失活减少，碳形成减少 \[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00058-7\]。镓对金属相结构的影响，特别是在高温还原后，导致铂位点的阻塞和稀释效应，提高了选择性和稳定性。这表明将金属氧化物基催化剂与贵金属结合可以提供在工业脱氢过程中的活性和稳定性方面的优势 \[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00058-7\]。
