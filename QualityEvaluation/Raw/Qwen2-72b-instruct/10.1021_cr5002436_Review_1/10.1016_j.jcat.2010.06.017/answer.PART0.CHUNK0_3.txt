The focus of the literature is on the synthesis and characterization of a new catalyst, Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O, for alkane dehydrogenation. It discusses the preparation of Ga-promoted Pt particles for the dehydrogenation of light alkanes to alkenes, the role of the modifying element Ga introduced by transference from the support, a calcined Mg(Ga)(Al)O hydrotalcite-like material, and the formation of PtGa alloy particles dependent on reduction temperature. The literature does not delve into the effectiveness, industrial applications, recent developments, or potential advantages of metal oxide-based catalysts in the field of nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins.
