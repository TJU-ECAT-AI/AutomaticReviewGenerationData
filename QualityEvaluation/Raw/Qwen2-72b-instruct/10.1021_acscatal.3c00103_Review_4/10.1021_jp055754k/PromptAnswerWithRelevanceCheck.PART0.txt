1.The literature does not provide specific information on how physicochemical properties of catalysts influence their performance in dehydrogenation reactions or on support modification strategies to optimize these properties. However, it does discuss the influence of support acidity on the electronic properties of supported metals, and how the presence of metals affects the acidic properties of supports. The study shows that the electronic properties of Pt are influenced by the acidity of the support, and the alteration of Pt properties increases with increasing acidity of the support. At the same time, the presence of platinum affects the acidic properties of the supports by reducing the strength of the acid sites. This is observed directly as the changes in desorption of pyridine from the acid sites and indirectly as the suppression of cracking reactions during the ring opening of decalin on the Pt-modified catalysts.
