<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1.The physicochemical properties of catalysts, including chemical qualities and morphological characteristics, significantly influence their performance in dehydrogenation reactions. Al2O3, due to its structural advantages and stability, is commonly used as a support in heterogeneous catalysis. The coordinately unsaturated Al3+ sites on the (100) facets of commercial γ-Al2O3 can serve as anchor sites for atomically dispersed Pt and Ru sites. The specific interaction between Ga2O3 and Al2O3 allows the tetrahedral Ga3+ cations to highly disperse in the spinel-type γ-Ga2O3-Al2O3 solid solution, enhancing activity and stability in PDH reactions. The γ-Al2O3 nanosheet, containing 27% penta-coordinated Al3+ sites, can disperse and stabilize bimetallic PtSn nanoclusters due to strong interactions between metals and support. The diversity and complexity of Al2O3 structure, such as surface acidity at low-coordinated Al sites, determine different properties in propane adsorption, activation, and coke deposition in PDH reactions. Support modification strategies can optimize these properties for enhanced catalytic efficiency and selectivity.
