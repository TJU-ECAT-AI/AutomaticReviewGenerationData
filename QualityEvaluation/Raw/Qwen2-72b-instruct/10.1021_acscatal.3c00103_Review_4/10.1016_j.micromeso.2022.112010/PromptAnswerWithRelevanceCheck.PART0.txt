Washing the catalysts after calcination further increases their activity and stability due to the redispersion of water-soluble chlorinated Pt compounds. High external surface acid site concentrations, specific Na/Pt atomic ratios, calcination temperatures, and the order of component introduction all contribute to improved Pt dispersion and catalyst stability.
