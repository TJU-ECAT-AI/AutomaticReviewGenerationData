The influence of catalyst physicochemical properties on dehydrogenation reactions is profound, with chemical qualities and morphological characteristics playing pivotal roles in determining catalytic efficiency and selectivity \[10.1016/j.cej.2020.126656\]. The addition of calcium to alumina (Al2O3) reduces acid centers and promotes alloy formation, enhancing propylene selectivity and stability \[10.1016/j.cej.2020.126656\]. This strategic support modification results in a lower deactivation rate (0.05 h^-1 vs. 0.22 h^-1) and higher residual activity (0.82 vs. 0.63) at 600°C, indicating improved resistance against coke formation \[10.1016/j.cej.2020.126656\]. The presence of Lewis acid sites from AlIII in Al2O3 significantly influences dehydrogenation reactions, with γ-Al2O3 demonstrating higher activity in propane dehydrogenation due to a higher density of Lewis acid sites \[10.1039/D0CY00779J\]. The γ-Al2O3-200 catalyst, characterized by a high level of strong acid sites, achieves the highest yield and is regenerable, making it a sustainable choice for industrial applications \[10.1039/D0CY00779J\].

Strategic support modification, such as the use of specific surfactants and silica sources, optimizes zeolite properties for enhanced catalytic efficiency and selectivity in dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\]. Pt3Y alloy nanoparticles, formed on zeolites with silanol nests, exhibit high catalytic durability, propane conversion, and propylene selectivity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\]. These nanoparticles show fully reversible hydrogen chemisorption at room temperature, unlike monometallic Pt \[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\]. The inverse relationship between propane conversion and metal loading amount, with increased metal dispersion at low loadings, is also observed \[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\].

CrOx supported on high-silica HZSM-5 (Cr/Z-5(260)) exhibits high activity, selectivity, and stability for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) \[10.1016/j.jechem.2019.12.010\]. Acidic sites, particularly Brnsted acids in HZSM-5, are pivotal for catalytic performance, enhancing propane adsorption and CH bond activation \[10.1016/j.jechem.2019.12.010\]. Cr species combined with Brnsted acid sites in Cr/HZSM-5 are responsible for high catalytic efficiency \[10.1016/j.jechem.2019.12.010\]. The high surface area and well-defined pores of HZSM-5 provide an optimal environment for Cr dispersion, ensuring stability and activity \[10.1016/j.jechem.2019.12.010\].

Support modification strategies, including the use of γ-Al2O3 nanosheets and Ga2O3-Al2O3 interaction, optimize catalytic efficiency and selectivity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.026\]. Al2O3, a prevalent support, anchors atomically dispersed Pt and Ru sites, while the Ga2O3-Al2O3 interaction disperses Ga3+ cations, enhancing PDH reaction activity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.026\]. γ-Al2O3 nanosheets with penta-coordinated Al3+ sites stabilize bimetallic PtSn nanoclusters, improving PDH efficiency \[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.026\]. Hydroxyl groups-eliminated Al2O3 activates non-oxidative dehydrogenation of isobutane, while surface acidity at low-coordinated Al sites affects propane adsorption and coke deposition \[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.026\].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements and findings discussed in the file highlight the critical role of catalyst physicochemical properties in dehydrogenation reactions. These modifications enhance resistance against deactivation, improve selectivity, and promote stability.

Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel support materials with tailored acid properties and morphologies to further optimize catalytic performance. The exploration of new bimetallic and multimetallic catalysts, as well as the investigation of the electronic and geometric effects of promoter elements, could lead to the development of ultra-stable catalytic systems for dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1002/anie.202107210\].

The future of dehydrogenation catalysis lies in the development of novel support materials, the exploration of innovative methodologies, and the optimization of metal-support interactions for enhanced stability and selectivity.

催化剂的理化性质对脱氢反应的影响深远，化学品质和形态特征在决定催化效率和选择性方面扮演着关键角色\[10.1016/j.cej.2020.126656\]。向氧化铝(Al2O3)中添加钙可以减少酸中心并促进合金形成，提高丙烯选择性和稳定性\[10.1016/j.cej.2020.126656\]。这种战略性的载体改性导致更低的失活速率(0.05 h^-1 vs. 0.22 h^-1)和更高的残余活性(0.82 vs. 0.63)在600°C下，表明对焦炭形成的抵抗能力增强\[10.1016/j.cej.2020.126656\]。来自Al2O3中的AlIII的路易斯酸位点对脱氢反应有显著影响，γ-Al2O3由于具有更高密度的路易斯酸位点而在丙烷脱氢中表现出更高的活性\[10.1039/D0CY00779J\]。γ-Al2O3-200催化剂，以高水平的强酸位点为特征，实现了最高的产率并且是可再生的，使其成为工业应用的可持续选择\[10.1039/D0CY00779J\]。

通过使用特定的表面活性剂和硅源，战略性的载体改性优化了沸石的性质，以增强脱氢反应的催化效率和选择性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\]。在具有硅醇巢的沸石上形成的Pt3Y合金纳米颗粒表现出高催化耐久性、丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\]。这些纳米颗粒在室温下显示出完全可逆的氢化学吸附，与单金属Pt不同\[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\]。丙烷转化率与金属负载量之间的反比关系，低负载量时金属分散度增加，也被观察到\[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\]。

在高硅H-ZSM-5上支撑的CrOx(Cr/Z-5(260))在丙烷脱氢(PDH)中表现出高活性、选择性和稳定性\[10.1016/j.jechem.2019.12.010\]。酸性位点，特别是H-ZSM-5中的布伦斯特酸，对于催化性能至关重要，增强丙烷吸附和CH键活化\[10.1016/j.jechem.2019.12.010\]。Cr/HZSM-5中的Cr物种与布伦斯特酸位点结合对高催化效率负责\[10.1016/j.jechem.2019.12.010\]。H-ZSM-5的高表面积和明确定义的孔隙为Cr分散提供了最佳环境，确保了稳定性和活性\[10.1016/j.jechem.2019.12.010\]。

通过使用γ-Al2O3纳米片和Ga2O3-Al2O3相互作用，载体改性策略优化了催化效率和选择性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.026\]。Al2O3，一种普遍的载体，锚定了原子分散的Pt和Ru位点，而Ga2O3-Al2O3相互作用分散了Ga3+离子，增强了PDH反应活性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.026\]。具有五配位Al3+位点的γ-Al2O3纳米片稳定了双金属PtSn纳米簇，提高了PDH效率\[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.026\]。去除了羟基基团的Al2O3激活了异丁烷的非氧化脱氢，而低配位Al位点的表面酸性影响了丙烷吸附和焦炭沉积\[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.026\]。

综合理解与未来展望：文件中讨论的进展和发现突出了催化剂理化性质在脱氢反应中的关键作用。通过向Al2O3中添加钙、使用特定的表面活性剂和硅源以及Ga2O3和Al2O3之间的相互作用，战略性的载体改性优化了催化效率和选择性\[10.1016/j.cej.2020.126656, 10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011, 10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.026\]。这些改性增强了对失活的抵抗，改善了选择性，并促进了稳定性。

未来的研究工作可能集中在开发具有定制酸性和形态的新型载体材料，以进一步优化催化性能。探索新兴领域，如在较低温度下使用多孔铜-铝框架材料进行丙烷脱氢，表明了创新方法的潜力\[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016\]。此外，研究氧化还原性质在实现较低温度下丙烷脱氢的作用表明了一个有希望的研究方向\[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016\]。
