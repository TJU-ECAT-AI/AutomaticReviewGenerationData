                    The physicochemical properties of catalysts, including chemical qualities like acid strength and morphological characteristics, significantly influence dehydrogenation reactions, as evidenced by studies \[10.1016/j.cej.2020.126656\], \[10.1039/D0CY00779J\], and \[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\]. The addition of Ca to Al2O3 decreases acid centers and increases alloy formation, enhancing propylene selectivity and stability \[10.1016/j.cej.2020.126656\]. γ-Al2O3, with more Lewis acid sites than θ-Al2O3, exhibits higher activity in propane dehydrogenation \[10.1039/D0CY00779J\]. The synthesis method using a diammonium-type bromide surfactant and sodium-free silica source creates silanol nests in zeolites, facilitating the formation of Pt3Y alloy nanoparticles, which exhibit high catalytic durability, propane conversion, and propylene selectivity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\]. These findings highlight the importance of strategic support modification for optimizing catalytic efficiency and selectivity in dehydrogenation reactions.
