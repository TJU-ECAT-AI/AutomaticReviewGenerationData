The influence of catalyst physicochemical properties on dehydrogenation reactions, particularly propane dehydrogenation (PDH), is profound, with chemical qualities and morphological characteristics playing pivotal roles in catalytic efficiency and selectivity \[10.1016/j.cej.2020.126656\]. The addition of Ca to Al2O3 decreases acid centers and enhances alloy formation, boosting propylene selectivity to 98.5% and stability \[10.1016/j.cej.2020.126656\]. This modification results in lower deactivation rates (0.05 h^-1) and higher residual activity (0.82) at 600 C, compared to non-promoted catalysts (0.22 h^-1 and 0.63), indicating improved resistance against coke-induced deactivation \[10.1016/j.cej.2020.126656\]. The slightly higher activation energy for Ca-promoted catalysts, due to enhanced GeOx reduction and Pt-Ge alloy formation, initially reduces activity but optimizes steady-state performance in terms of both activity and selectivity \[10.1016/j.cej.2020.126656\].

Lewis acid sites, originating from unsaturated AlIII in Al2O3, are crucial for dehydrogenation reactions, particularly in propane conversion \[10.1039/D0CY00779J\]. γ-Al2O3, with a higher density of Lewis acid sites than θ-Al2O3, exhibits superior activity in PDH \[10.1039/D0CY00779J\]. The γ-Al2O3-200 catalyst, characterized by a high yield and regenerability, is sustainable for industrial PDH \[10.1039/D0CY00779J\]. Support modification strategies can optimize these properties, enhancing catalytic efficiency and selectivity \[10.1039/D0CY00779J\].

Strategic support modification, such as using specific surfactants and silica sources, optimizes zeolite properties for enhanced catalytic efficiency and selectivity in dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\]. Using a diammonium-type bromide surfactant and sodium-free silica source creates silanol nests in zeolites, confirmed by spectroscopy \[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\]. These nests facilitate the formation of Pt3Y alloy nanoparticles, which exhibit high catalytic durability, propane conversion, and propylene selectivity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\]. Pt3Y shows fully reversible hydrogen chemisorption at room temperature, unlike monometallic Pt \[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\]. Catalysts on silanol nest-containing zeolites have an activation period to reach full conversion, with propane conversion exceeding equilibrium values initially \[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\].

CrOx on high-silica HZSM-5 (Cr/Z-5(260)), synthesized by wet impregnation, exhibits high activity, selectivity, and stability for PDH \[10.1016/j.jechem.2019.12.010\]. Cr species and Brnsted acid sites are pivotal in dehydrogenation reactions, enhancing propane adsorption and CH bond activation \[10.1016/j.jechem.2019.12.010\]. Zeolite supports, particularly HZSM-5, offer a unique environment for catalysis, with Brnsted acid sites enhancing propane adsorption and activation \[10.1016/j.jechem.2019.12.010\]. Cr loading on HZSM-5 does not affect its crystallite structure, while acidity properties are crucial for catalytic performance \[10.1016/j.jechem.2019.12.010\]. High surface area and well-defined pores of HZSM-5 enhance Cr species dispersion and stability \[10.1016/j.jechem.2019.12.010\]. Strategic support modification is necessary to achieve high selectivity and stability in PDH reactions \[10.1016/j.jechem.2019.12.010\].

The double modification of PtGa nanoparticles with Pb for geometric blocking of Pt3 sites and Ca for electronic enrichment of Pt1 sites creates an ultra-stable catalytic system \[10.1002/anie.202107210\]. In dehydrogenation reactions, support acidity significantly impacts the electronic properties of supported metals like Pt, enhancing catalytic efficiency and selectivity \[10.1021/jp055754k\]. The interaction between Ga2O3 and Al2O3 allows for the dispersion of Ga3+ cations, improving PDH reaction activity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.026\]. γ-Al2O3 nanosheets, with penta-coordinated Al3+ sites, stabilize bimetallic PtSn nanoclusters, leading to superior PDH performance \[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.026\]. Surface acidity at low-coordinated Al sites on Al2O3 influences propane adsorption and activation, affecting coke deposition \[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.026\]. 源自Al2O3中未饱和AlIII的Lewis酸位点对脱氢反应至关重要,特别是在丙烷转化中\[10.1039/D0CY00779J\]。γ-Al2O3,比θ-Al2O3具有更高密度的Lewis酸位点,在PDH中表现出更优的活性\[10.1039/D0CY00779J\]。γ-Al2O3-200催化剂,以高产率和可再生性为特征,适合工业PDH\[10.1039/D0CY00779J\]。支持改性策略可以优化这些性质,增强催化效率和选择性\[10.1039/D0CY00779J\]。

通过使用特定的表面活性剂和硅源的战略支持改性,优化了沸石性质,以增强脱氢反应中的催化效率和选择性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\]。使用二铵型溴化物表面活性剂和无钠硅源创建了沸石中的硅醇巢,通过光谱学得到证实\[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\]。这些巢促进了Pt3Y合金纳米粒子的形成,表现出高催化耐久性、丙烷转化和丙烯选择性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\]。Pt3Y在室温下显示出完全可逆的氢化学吸附,与单金属Pt不同\[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\]。含有硅醇巢的沸石上的催化剂有一个激活期来达到完全转化,最初丙烷转化超过了平衡值\[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011\]。
