This modification reduces deactivation rates and increases residual activity, indicating better resistance to coke formation. Consequently, the activation energy for the propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reaction rises, promoting Pt-Ge alloy formation and improving steady-state activity and selectivity. Support modification, such as Ca addition, optimizes catalytic efficiency and selectivity.

                    The Lewis acid sites from unsaturated AlIII in Al2O3 are crucial for dehydrogenation reactions, as highlighted in reference \[10.1039/D0CY00779J\]. γ-Al2O3 exhibits more Lewis acid sites than θ-Al2O3, leading to higher activity in propane dehydrogenation. The γ-Al2O3-200 catalyst, with the highest yield, is regenerable, making it sustainable for industrial use.

PtY(0.5)/MZ-sf-Br and PtY(0.5)/MZ-sf-OH outperform PtY(0.5)/MZ-dg in durability.
