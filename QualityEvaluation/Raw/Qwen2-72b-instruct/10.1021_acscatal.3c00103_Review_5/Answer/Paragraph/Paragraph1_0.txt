                    The challenges in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) for industrial propylene production are multifaceted, encompassing high-temperature stability, propylene selectivity, and managing thermodynamic limitations and side reactions \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]. High temperatures, essential for propane conversion, can lead to catalyst deactivation through sintering and coke deposition. Researchers are addressing these issues by developing high-performance catalysts using strategies such as promoter introduction, encapsulation, hydroxyl anchoring, and support modifications \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]. Commercial PDH catalysts, predominantly Cr- or Pt-based, face challenges with low propylene selectivity and rapid deactivation due to coke formation \[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]. Studies reveal that Pt(211) surfaces exhibit the highest dehydrogenation activity among Pt surfaces, and stepped surfaces like Pt(221) show enhanced activity for dehydrogenation \[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]. Theoretical studies aim to optimize catalysts for specific processes, but limitations exist in modeling complex catalytic systems, requiring in situ characterization and controlled synthesis to maximize active and selective sites \[10.1039/C5SC01227A\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in PDH catalyst development, particularly in addressing high-temperature stability, propylene selectivity, and thermodynamic limitations, have opened new avenues for industrial propylene production. The ongoing research in promoter effects, encapsulation techniques, and support modifications holds promise for enhancing catalyst stability and resistance to sintering and coke deposition. The development of stepped surfaces like Pt(221) and the exploration of alternative catalysts such as h-BN and BNNTs for high olefin selectivity are significant steps forward \[10.1039/D0CS00814A\]. Future research might focus on the scale-up of well-defined catalysts, developing thermodynamically stable single atom catalysts, and improving the understanding of active sites and mechanisms under reaction conditions through advanced operando/in situ characterization techniques. Theoretical calculations can assist in explaining experimental results, but the challenge lies in matching the complexities of practical catalysts with DFT models. Developing efficient strategies for improving catalyst stability and finding alternative catalysts that meet industrial standards remain grand challenges in the field of PDH for propylene production.

                    丙烷脱氢(PDH)用于工业丙烯生产面临的挑战是多方面的，包括高温稳定性、丙烯选择性以及管理和热力学限制和副反应 \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]。高温对丙烷转化至关重要，但可能导致催化剂通过烧结和焦炭沉积而失活。研究人员通过开发高性能催化剂，采用诸如引入促进剂、封装、羟基锚定和支撑修改等策略来解决这些问题 \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]。商业PDH催化剂主要是Cr或Pt基，面临丙烯选择性低和因焦炭形成而快速失活的挑战 \[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]。研究表明，Pt(211)表面在Pt表面中脱氢活性最高，而像Pt(221)这样的阶梯表面显示出增强的脱氢活性 \[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]。理论研究旨在为特定过程优化催化剂，但在模拟复杂的催化系统方面存在局限性，需要原位表征和受控合成来最大化活性和选择性位点 \[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: PDH催化剂开发的进步，特别是在解决高温稳定性、丙烯选择性和热力学限制方面，为工业丙烯生产开辟了新的途径。关于促进剂效应、封装技术和支撑修改的持续研究为增强催化剂稳定性和抵抗烧结和焦炭沉积提供了希望。开发像Pt(221)这样的阶梯表面以及探索如h-BN和BNNTs等替代催化剂以实现高烯烃选择性是重要的进展 \[10.1039/D0CS00814A\]。未来的研究可能集中在扩大定义明确的催化剂的规模，开发热力学稳定的单原子催化剂，以及通过先进的原位/在位表征技术改进对反应条件下活性位点和机制的理解。理论计算可以帮助解释实验结果，但挑战在于将实际催化剂的复杂性与DFT模型相匹配。开发提高催化剂稳定性的有效策略以及寻找符合工业标准的替代催化剂仍然是PDH用于丙烯生产领域中的重大挑战。
