                    Challenges in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) for industrial propylene production are multifaceted, encompassing high-temperature stability, propylene selectivity, and resistance to side reactions, all within the constraints of thermodynamics \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]. High temperatures, essential for propane conversion, can lead to catalyst deactivation through sintering and coke deposition \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]. Researchers are developing strategies such as promoter introduction, encapsulation, hydroxyl anchoring, and support modifications to enhance catalyst stability and resistance \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]. However, excessive metal promoters can make catalysts uneconomical \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\], and encapsulation techniques require proper ligands, adding complexity \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]. Diffusion restriction in supports with large outer surfaces is hindered by the use of rare templates \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]. Hierarchical zeolites as PDH supports offer a solution to diffusion limitations \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\].

                    Commercial PDH catalysts, particularly Pt-based, face challenges with low propylene selectivity and rapid deactivation due to coke formation \[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]. Studies reveal that Pt(211) surfaces exhibit the highest dehydrogenation activity among Pt surfaces \[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]. Thermodynamic corrections are crucial for accurate selectivity calculations, as they significantly reduce free energy barriers for desorption steps \[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]. CHC accumulation during conversion, indicated by a higher dissociation barrier than the dehydrogenation barrier, poses a risk for coke formation, aligning with experimental observations \[10.1039/C5SC01227A\].

                    PDH offers an alternative to petrochemical routes for propylene production, leveraging abundant shale gas \[10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058\]. Challenges include high energy consumption, waste production, and the need for enhanced Pt-based catalysts \[10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058\]. Researchers aim to develop catalysts with high-temperature stability, propylene selectivity, and resistance to side reactions \[10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058\]. Pt-based catalysts require improvement for better utilization and cost reduction \[10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058\]. Agglomeration of metal species and catalytic deactivation are critical issues for industrial applications \[10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058\].

                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The challenges in PDH for industrial propylene production, including high-temperature stability, propylene selectivity, and resistance to side reactions, necessitate innovative strategies and advanced catalyst development. The ongoing research in developing high-performance catalysts for PDH, with a focus on addressing high-temperature stability, propylene selectivity, and thermodynamic limitations, is promising. Future directions might include the exploration of novel catalyst supports, such as hierarchical zeolites, to mitigate diffusion limitations and enhance catalyst performance. Additionally, the development of advanced encapsulation techniques with optimized ligands could offer a solution to the complex issue of catalyst stability and resistance to sintering and coke formation. Theoretical studies and computational methods, such as DFT calculations, can play a pivotal role in understanding the active sites and mechanisms under reaction conditions, guiding the design of more efficient and selective catalysts. The scale-up of dehydrogenation catalysts with well-defined structures, particularly single atom catalysts, remains a grand challenge, but novel synthesis methods and stabilization strategies offer opportunities for improving their stability and performance under PDH conditions.

                    丙烷脱氢(PDH)用于工业丙烯生产面临的挑战是多方面的，包括高温稳定性、丙烯选择性以及对副反应的抵抗能力，所有这些都在热力学的限制下\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]。高温，对于丙烷转化至关重要，但可能导致催化剂通过烧结和焦炭沉积而失活\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]。研究人员正在开发策略，如引入促进剂、封装、羟基锚定和载体改性，以增强催化剂的稳定性和抵抗能力\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]。然而，过多的金属促进剂会使催化剂变得不经济\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]，封装技术需要合适的配体，增加了复杂性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]。具有大外表面的载体中的扩散限制受到使用稀有模板的阻碍\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]。作为PDH载体的分级沸石提供了解决扩散限制的方案\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]。

                    商业PDH催化剂，尤其是铂基催化剂，面临着丙烯选择性低和由于焦炭形成而快速失活的挑战\[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]。研究显示，Pt(211)表面在铂表面中表现出最高的脱氢活性\[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]。热力学修正对于准确的选择性计算至关重要，因为它们显著降低了脱附步骤的自由能障碍\[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]。在转化过程中，CHC的积累，由高于脱氢障碍的解离障碍指示，对焦炭形成构成风险，与实验观察一致\[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]。

                    PDH为丙烯生产提供了一种替代石化路线，利用了丰富的页岩气\[10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058\]。挑战包括高能耗、废物产生以及需要增强的铂基催化剂\[10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058\]。研究人员旨在开发具有高温稳定性、丙烯选择性和抵抗副反应的催化剂\[10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058\]。铂基催化剂需要改进，以提高利用效率和降低成本\[10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058\]。金属物种的聚集和催化失活是工业应用中的关键问题\[10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058\]。

                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: PDH用于工业丙烯生产面临的挑战，包括高温稳定性、丙烯选择性以及对副反应的抵抗能力，需要创新策略和先进催化剂的开发。在开发高性能PDH催化剂方面，专注于解决高温稳定性、丙烯选择性和热力学限制的持续研究是充满希望的。未来方向可能包括探索新型催化剂载体，如分级沸石，以缓解扩散限制并增强催化剂性能。此外，开发先进的封装技术与优化的配体可以为催化剂稳定性和抵抗烧结和焦炭形成这一复杂问题提供解决方案。理论研究和计算方法，如DFT计算，在理解反应条件下的活性位点和机制方面可以发挥关键作用，指导更高效、更选择性催化剂的设计。具有明确定义结构的脱氢催化剂，特别是单原子催化剂的放大，仍然是一个重大挑战，但新型合成方法和稳定策略为改善其在PDH条件下的稳定性和性能提供了机会。
