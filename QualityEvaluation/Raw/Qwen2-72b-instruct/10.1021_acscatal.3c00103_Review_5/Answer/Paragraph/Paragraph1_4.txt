                    The challenges in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) for industrial propylene production are multifaceted, encompassing the inertness of lower alkanes, high-temperature stability, propylene selectivity, and managing thermodynamic limitations \[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016\]. High temperatures, essential for propane conversion, can lead to side reactions and catalyst deactivation due to sintering and coke deposition \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]. Researchers address these issues by introducing promoters, encapsulation, hydroxyl anchoring, and support modifications to enhance catalyst stability and resistance \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]. However, excessive metal promoters can make catalysts uneconomical, and hydroxyl anchoring and encapsulation techniques can be complex and expensive \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]. Utilizing hierarchical zeolites as supports can alleviate diffusion limitations, but rare templates hinder industrial application \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]. Commercial PDH catalysts, predominantly Cr- and Pt-based, face challenges with low propylene selectivity and rapid deactivation due to coke formation \[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]. Studies reveal that Pt(211) surfaces exhibit the highest dehydrogenation activity among Pt surfaces \[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]. Thermodynamic corrections for desorption barriers significantly influence propylene selectivity, not accounted for in DFT calculations \[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]. CHC accumulation during conversion, indicated by a higher dissociation barrier than the dehydrogenation barrier, poses a risk for coke formation \[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]. Theoretical studies aim to optimize catalysts for specific processes, but limitations exist in modeling complex catalytic systems \[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]. In situ characterization and controlled synthesis are crucial for identifying and maximizing active and selective sites in real catalysts \[10.1039/C5SC01227A\].

Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel catalysts that can address thermodynamic limitations while maintaining high selectivity and stability. The exploration of alternative catalysts, such as VOx- and GaOx-based materials, presents an opportunity to diversify the catalyst portfolio and potentially overcome the limitations of current Pt-based systems. Developing thermodynamically stable single atom catalysts is a high priority, with novel methods for scale-up production offering promising avenues for progress. The integration of in situ characterization techniques and theoretical calculations will be crucial for understanding the dynamic changes of active sites under reaction conditions and for optimizing catalyst performance. The development of efficient and cost-effective encapsulation and hydroxyl anchoring techniques could lead to more stable and economically viable catalysts. The potential of oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) for its favorable thermodynamics, despite challenges in inhibiting overoxidation, suggests a promising direction for future research. The identification of active sites and mechanisms under reaction conditions remains a significant challenge, necessitating the continued development of operando/in situ characterization techniques. Addressing these challenges will require a multidisciplinary approach, combining insights from materials science, chemical engineering, and computational chemistry.

                    丙烷脱氢（PDH）用于工业丙烯生产面临的挑战是多方面的，包括低碳烷烃的惰性、高温稳定性、丙烯选择性以及管理热力学限制\[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016\]。高温，对于丙烷转化至关重要，可能导致副反应和催化剂失活，由于烧结和焦炭沉积\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]。研究人员通过引入促进剂、封装、羟基锚定和支持修改来解决这些问题，以增强催化剂的稳定性和抗性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]。然而，过量的金属促进剂会使催化剂不经济，而羟基锚定和封装技术可能复杂且昂贵\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]。使用分级沸石作为支持物可以减轻扩散限制，但稀有模板阻碍了工业应用\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5\]。商业PDH催化剂，主要是Cr和Pt基，面临着低丙烯选择性和由于焦炭形成而快速失活的挑战\[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]。研究表明，Pt(211)表面在Pt表面中表现出最高的脱氢活性\[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]。脱附屏障的热力学修正对丙烯选择性有重大影响，这在DFT计算中没有考虑\[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]。转化过程中CHC的积累，由高于脱氢屏障的解离屏障指示，对焦炭形成构成风险\[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]。理论研究旨在为特定过程优化催化剂，但在模拟复杂的催化系统方面存在局限性\[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]。原位表征和受控合成对于识别和最大化真实催化剂中活性和选择性位点至关重要\[10.1039/C5SC01227A\]。

                    综合理解与未来展望: 文件中讨论的进展和发现强调了催化剂开发在克服丙烷脱氢用于工业丙烯生产挑战中的关键作用。追求高温稳定性、丙烯选择性和对副反应的抵抗力是正在进行的研究的核心。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发新型催化剂，这些催化剂可以解决热力学限制，同时保持高选择性和稳定性。探索VOx和GaOx基材料等替代催化剂为催化剂组合的多样化提供了机会，有可能克服当前Pt基系统的局限性。开发热力学稳定的单原子催化剂是当务之急，新型的规模化生产方法为进展提供了有希望的途径。高效和经济的封装和羟基锚定技术的开发可能会导致更稳定和经济上可行的催化剂。氧化脱氢（ODH）因其有利的热力学特性，尽管存在抑制过度氧化的挑战，表明了未来研究的有希望的方向。将放热反应与PDH耦合，探索太阳能光催化和电催化等环境温度催化过程，可以降低能耗，提高丙烯生产的效率。在反应条件下识别活性位点和机制仍然是一个重大挑战，需要继续开发原位表征技术。具有明确定义结构的脱氢催化剂的规模化提出了重大挑战，特别是对于在PDH条件下遭受烧结和重构的单原子催化剂。解决这些挑战将需要多学科的方法，结合材料科学、化学工程和计算化学的见解。
