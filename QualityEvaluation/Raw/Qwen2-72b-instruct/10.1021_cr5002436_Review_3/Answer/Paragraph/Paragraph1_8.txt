                The exploration of vanadium oxide-based catalysts for paraffin dehydrogenation, driven by their rich chemistry and potential as alternatives to Pt and CrOx, has evolved significantly since the 1980s \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. Techniques like Raman spectroscopy, using multiple excitation wavelengths, have provided detailed structural information of vanadium oxide species \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. The nature of vanadium species is influenced by variables such as carrier type, support surface area, and metal loading \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. Isolated VOx species dominate at low surface densities, polyvanadates coexist with monovanadates at intermediate densities, and V2O5 forms at high densities \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. The weight loss at the reduction step increases with vanadia loading, and the rate of catalyst re-oxidation is greater for higher vanadium loading catalysts \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. Supported VOx catalysts show good catalytic performance in both oxidation and reduction reactions, being among the most active and selective for dehydrogenation and oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. The initial dehydrogenation activity increases with surface VOx density, but decreases significantly with prolonged reaction time for samples with densities higher than 1.2 V/nm2 due to surface coke formation \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. Catalysts with surface VOx density no higher than 1.2 V/nm2 maintain nearly unchanged dehydrogenation activity \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. At higher butane pressures, catalysts with higher V/nm2 densities show enhanced activity \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. The structure of VOx species can be restored by oxidation of coke deposits up to 873 K, but only high-loaded samples are fully restored according to UV-Vis DRS \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. 51V MAS NMR confirms the re-oxidation of reduced vanadium species \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. After regeneration, the surface area of an 8%V catalyst was reduced by 50%, indicating transformations in the nature of the support \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\].
