The development and characterization of vanadium oxide-based catalysts as alternatives for paraffin dehydrogenation have been a focal point of research since the 1980s, driven by the complex chemistry and potential of these catalysts as alternatives to Pt and CrOx \[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]. VOx/MCM-41 catalysts, featuring isolated VOx species, have demonstrated stable catalytic performance in dehydrogenation (DH) and regeneration cycles, outperforming industrially relevant CrOx/MCM-41 and PtSn/Al2O3 \[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]. The stability of these catalysts is attributed to the VOx species' resistance to agglomeration. Research has shown that the highest spacetime yield of propene is only 25% lower than that of PtSn/Al2O3 under similar conditions \[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]. Deactivation is linked to carbon deposits covering VOx sites, but these deposits can also participate in propane dehydrogenation with lower activity \[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]. Acidic support properties are crucial for generating active carbon species \[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]. Catalysts based on Siral supports with 10 and 1 wt.% SiO2 achieve high propene yields, approaching PtSn/Al2O3 values \[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]. Most catalysts can restore initial activity after 10 DH/oxidative regeneration cycles \[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]. The optimal DH cycle duration is 6 hours, as carbon deposits initially form on non-VOx sites, leading to deactivation when VOx sites are also covered \[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]. Catalysts' tendency to form coke increases with surface acidity \[10.1039/c3cy01083j\].

The structure of supported vanadia catalysts under redox conditions is crucial for catalytic performance, and research since the 1980s has revealed that the nature of vanadium species is influenced by factors such as carrier type, support surface area, and metal loading \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. Techniques like Raman spectroscopy, using multiple excitation wavelengths, have provided new insights into the structure of supported metal oxides \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. The weight loss during reduction increases with vanadia loading, and higher loading catalysts re-oxidize faster \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. Supported VOx catalysts are highly active for dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. The initial dehydrogenation activity increases with surface VOx density, but prolonged reaction time leads to activity decrease due to coke formation on catalysts with densities above 1.2 V/nm2 \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. Catalysts with lower densities maintain stable activity \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. At higher pressures, catalysts with higher densities show enhanced activity \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. Raman spectroscopy indicates that VOx species can be restored by oxidation of coke deposits up to 873 K, but only high-loaded samples are fully restored as indicated by UV-Vis DRS \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. After regeneration, the surface area of an 8%V catalyst was reduced by 50%, suggesting transformations in the support nature \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\].

在氧化还原条件下，支撑的钒氧化物催化剂的结构对于催化性能至关重要，自1980年代以来的研究揭示了钒物种的性质受载体类型、支持物表面积和金属负载等因素的影响\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。使用多种激发波长的拉曼光谱等技术为支撑金属氧化物的结构提供了新的见解\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。还原过程中重量损失随钒氧化物负载的增加而增加，高负载催化剂的再氧化速率更快\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。支撑VOx催化剂在脱氢反应中高度活跃\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。初始脱氢活性随表面VOx密度的增加而增加，但反应时间的延长导致密度高于1.2 V/nm2的催化剂活性下降，这是由于焦炭形成的缘故\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。密度较低的催化剂保持稳定的活性\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。在较高压力下，密度较高的催化剂显示出增强的活性\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。拉曼光谱表明，VOx物种可以通过氧化焦炭沉积物恢复至873 K，但只有高负载样品根据UV-Vis DRS完全恢复\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。再生后，8%V催化剂的表面积减少了50%，表明支持物性质发生了变化\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。
