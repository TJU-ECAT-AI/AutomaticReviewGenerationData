The development and characterization of vanadium oxide-based catalysts as alternatives for paraffin dehydrogenation: from historical context to species behavior.

Research since the 1980s has highlighted vanadium oxide-based catalysts, notably VOx/MCM-41 with dispersed VOx species, for their stable dehydrogenation (DH) performance, outperforming PtSn/Al2O3 and CrOx/MCM-41 \[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]. This stability is attributed to VOx species' resistance to agglomeration. Studies have focused on VOx-based catalysts using Al2O3SiO2, Al2O3, and SiO2 supports, achieving propene yields close to industrial PtSn/Al2O3 standards \[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]. Deactivation is linked to carbon deposits on VOx sites, with deposits on support sites also participating in propane dehydrogenation but with lower activity. Acidic support properties are crucial for generating active carbon species \[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]. Catalysts based on Siral supports with 10 and 1 wt.% SiO2 showed high propene yields, approaching PtSn/Al2O3 values and outperforming VOx/MCM-41 \[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]. Most catalysts effectively restored initial activity after DH/oxidative regeneration cycles, with the optimal DH cycle duration being 6 hours for Siral-based catalysts \[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]. Catalysts' tendency to form coke increases with surface acidity \[10.1039/c3cy01083j\].

The exploration of vanadium oxide-based catalysts as alternatives to Pt and CrOx for paraffin dehydrogenation has been driven by their rich chemistry, evolving since the 1980s \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. Techniques like TPR and Raman spectroscopy have revealed the structure and behavior of vanadium species, showing that isolated VOx species dominate at low surface densities, polyvanadates coexist at intermediate densities, and V2O5 forms at high densities \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. The weight loss during reduction increases with vanadia loading, and higher loadings result in faster re-oxidation \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. Supported VOx catalysts exhibit good catalytic performance in dehydrogenation reactions, with activity initially increasing as a function of surface VOx density \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. However, prolonged reaction time leads to significant activity decrease for samples with densities above 1.2 V/nm2 due to coke formation and site blocking \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. Catalysts with densities no higher than 1.2 V/nm2 maintain stable activity \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. At higher pressures, catalysts with higher densities show enhanced activity \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. The structure of VOx species can be restored by oxidation of coke deposits up to 873 K, but only high-loaded samples are fully restored, as indicated by UV-Vis DRS \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. 51V MAS NMR confirms the re-oxidation of reduced vanadium species \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. After regeneration, the surface area of an 8%V catalyst was reduced by 50%, indicating transformations in the support's nature \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\].

Exploration of vanadium oxide catalysts for paraffin dehydrogenation, driven by the need to address catalyst deactivation issues, has evolved since the 1980s \[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.07.015\]. Research has focused on understanding how carrier type, support surface area, and metal loading affect vanadium species, which can exist as isolated or polymeric forms \[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.07.015\]. These species' nature influences catalytic activity and selectivity, particularly for carbon deposition and dehydrogenation reactions, with redox properties playing a crucial role \[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.07.015\]. The average oxidation state during operation is determined by the rates of reduction and reoxidation, highlighting the importance of these variables in catalyst performance \[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.07.015\].

Driven by the limitations of Pt and CrOx catalysts, research on vanadium oxide-based alternatives for paraffin dehydrogenation has evolved since the 1980s \[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.05.017\]. Carrier type, support surface area, and metal loading significantly impact the nature of vanadium species, affecting catalytic performance \[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.05.017\]. Carrier type influences the formation of monomeric, dimeric vanadates, VOx chains, vanadia overlayers, or V2O5 crystallites \[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.05.017\]. Support surface area and metal loading affect dispersion and redox properties, crucial for activity and selectivity \[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.05.017\].

Exploration of vanadium oxide-based catalysts for paraffin dehydrogenation, spurred by Pt's high cost and Cr's environmental concerns, began in the 1980s focusing on vanadium-magnesium mixed oxides \[10.1021/cr5002436_3\]. Vanadium species' nature is influenced by carrier type, support surface area, metal loading, and oxidation state \[10.1021/cr5002436_3\]. Below monolayer coverage, isolated species form; above, V2O5 crystallites dominate \[10.1021/cr5002436_3\]. Surface area-to-metal loading ratio dictates species type, with monomeric species below 1.2 V/nm2, polymeric between 1.2 and 4.4 V/nm2, and crystalline V2O5 at higher loadings \[10.1021/cr5002436_3\]. Hydroxyl groups on the support surface enhance dispersion, while acid sites hinder performance by facilitating cracking and coke formation \[10.1021/cr5002436_3\].

Rising demand for light olefins and production capacity constraints have spurred interest in vanadium oxide (VOx) catalysts for paraffin dehydrogenation, particularly for propane and butane, since the 1980s \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.005\]. These catalysts are active and selective, but their structure and deactivation mechanisms under dehydrogenation conditions are still under investigation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.005\]. Research has focused on surface VOx structure and coke formation, revealing that carrier type, support surface area, and metal loading impact vanadium species \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.005\]. The distribution of VOx species, including monovanadate, polyvanadate, and V2O5, is influenced by surface VOx density \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.005\].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Taking into consideration the advancements and findings discussed in the file, there lies an opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel catalysts with enhanced stability and activity, leveraging the insights gained on the behavior of vanadium species under various conditions. The exploration of new carrier types and support materials that can optimize the dispersion and redox properties of vanadium species could lead to breakthroughs in catalytic performance. Additionally, the investigation of advanced regeneration techniques that can effectively remove carbon deposits without compromising the catalyst's structure could significantly enhance the longevity and efficiency of these catalysts. The integration of computational methods with experimental approaches could provide a deeper understanding of the structure-activity relationships in vanadium oxide-based catalysts, guiding the design of next-generation catalysts for paraffin dehydrogenation.

范氧化物基催化剂在石蜡脱氢中的发展与表征：从历史背景到物种行为。

自1980年代以来的研究强调了范氧化物基催化剂，特别是VOx/MCM-41中分散的VOx物种，因其稳定的脱氢（DH）性能而脱颖而出，优于PtSn/Al2O3和CrOx/MCM-41\[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]。这种稳定性归因于VOx物种对聚结的抵抗力。研究集中在使用Al2O3SiO2，Al2O3和SiO2支撑的VOx基催化剂上，丙烯产量接近工业PtSn/Al2O3标准\[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]。失活与VOx位点上的碳沉积有关，支撑位点上的沉积物也参与了丙烷脱氢，但活性较低。支撑的酸性性质对于生成活性碳物种至关重要\[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]。基于Siral支撑的催化剂，含有10和1 wt.% SiO2，显示出高丙烯产量，接近PtSn/Al2O3值，优于VOx/MCM-41\[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]。大多数催化剂在DH/氧化再生循环后有效恢复初始活性，对于基于Siral的催化剂，最佳DH循环持续时间为6小时\[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]。催化剂形成焦炭的倾向随着表面酸度的增加而增加\[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]。

将范氧化物基催化剂作为Pt和CrOx的替代品用于石蜡脱氢的探索，由其丰富的化学性质驱动，自1980年代以来不断发展\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。像TPR和拉曼光谱这样的技术揭示了范氧化物物种的结构和行为，表明在低表面密度下，孤立的VOx物种占主导，在中间密度下，多范氧化物共存，在高密度下，形成V2O5\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。还原过程中的重量损失随着范氧化物负载的增加而增加，较高负载的催化剂重新氧化的速率更快\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。支撑VOx催化剂在脱氢反应中表现出良好的催化性能，初始随着表面VOx密度的增加而增加\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。然而，对于密度高于1.2 V/nm2的样品，由于焦炭形成和位点堵塞，长时间反应导致显著的活性下降\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。密度不超过1.2 V/nm2的催化剂保持稳定的活性\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。在较高压力下，密度较高的催化剂显示出增强的活性\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。VOx物种的结构可以通过氧化焦炭沉积物至873 K来恢复，但只有高负载样品完全恢复，如UV-Vis DRS所示\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。51V MAS NMR确认了还原的钒物种的再氧化\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。再生后，8%V催化剂的表面积减少了50%，表明支撑性质发生了变化\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。

由于需要解决催化剂失活问题，对范氧化物催化剂用于石蜡脱氢的探索自1980年代以来不断发展\[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.07.015\]。研究集中在理解载体类型、支撑表面积和金属负载如何影响范氧化物物种，这些物种可以存在为孤立或聚合形式\[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.07.015\]。这些物种的性质影响催化活性和选择性，特别是对于碳沉积和脱氢反应，氧化还原性质起着关键作用\[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.07.015\]。操作期间的平均氧化态由还原和再氧化的速率决定，突出了这些变量在催化剂性能中的重要性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.07.015\]。

由于Pt和CrOx催化剂的局限性，对范氧化物基替代品用于石蜡脱氢的研究自1980年代以来不断发展\[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.05.017\]。载体类型、支撑表面积和金属负载对范氧化物物种的性质有重大影响，影响催化性能\[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.05.017\]。载体类型影响单体、二聚体范氧化物、VOx链、范氧化物覆盖层或V2O5晶体的形成\[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.05.017\]。支撑表面积和金属负载影响分散和氧化还原性质，对活性和选择性至关重要\[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.05.017\]。

由于Pt的高成本和Cr的环境问题，对范氧化物基催化剂用于石蜡脱氢的探索始于1980年代，专注于范氧化物-镁混合氧化物\[10.1021/cr5002436_3\]。范氧化物物种的性质受载体类型、支撑表面积、金属负载和氧化态的影响\[10.1021/cr5002436_3\]。低于单层覆盖，形成孤立物种；高于此，V2O5晶体占主导\[10.1021/cr5002436_3\]。表面面积与金属负载比决定了物种类型，低于1.2 V/nm2的单体物种，1.2和4.4 V/nm2之间的聚合物，以及更高负载下的结晶V2O5\[10.1021/cr5002436_3\]。支撑表面上的羟基增强分散，而酸位阻碍性能，通过促进裂解和焦炭形成\[10.1021/cr5002436_3\]。

由于对轻烯烃的需求增加和生产能力限制，自1980年代以来，对范氧化物（VOx）催化剂用于石蜡脱氢的兴趣激增，特别是对于丙烷和丁烷\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.005\]。这些催化剂具有活性和选择性，但它们在脱氢条件下的结构和失活机制仍在研究中\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.005\]。研究集中在表面VOx结构和焦炭形成上，揭示载体类型、支撑表面积和金属负载影响范氧化物物种\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.005\]。VOx物种的分布，包括单范氧化物、多范氧化物和V2O5，受表面VOx密度的影响\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.005\]。
