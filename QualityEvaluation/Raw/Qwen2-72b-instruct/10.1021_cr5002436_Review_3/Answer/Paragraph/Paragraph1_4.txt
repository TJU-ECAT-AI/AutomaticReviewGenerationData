The development and characterization of vanadium oxide-based catalysts as alternatives for paraffin dehydrogenation: from historical context to species behavior.

The exploration of vanadium oxide (VOx)-based catalysts for paraffin dehydrogenation, as alternatives to Pt and CrOx, has been driven by their complex chemistry and potential to replace conventional catalysts \[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]. Research since the 1980s has revealed that the structure of supported vanadia catalysts under redox conditions is crucial for catalytic performance \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. Techniques like Raman spectroscopy, using multiple excitation wavelengths, have provided new insights into the structure of vanadium species \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. The nature of vanadium species is influenced by variables such as carrier type, support surface area, and metal loading \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. Isolated VOx species dominate at low surface densities, polyvanadates coexist with monovanadates at intermediate densities, and V2O5 forms at high densities \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. The weight loss during reduction increases with vanadia loading, and higher vanadium loading catalysts re-oxidize faster \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. Supported VOx catalysts exhibit good performance in dehydrogenation reactions, with activity influenced by surface VOx density \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. At densities higher than 1.2 V/nm2, activity decreases over time due to coke formation, while lower densities maintain stable activity \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. Enhanced activity is observed at higher butane pressures for catalysts with higher V/nm2 densities \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. Regeneration via oxidation can restore the structure of VOx species up to 873 K, with high-loaded samples showing full restoration \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. The transformation of the support is observed after regeneration, affecting the catalyst's surface area \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Taking into consideration the advancements and findings discussed in the file, there lies an opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies. Moreover, the exploration of mixed oxides as supports could lead to the discovery of new catalysts with improved stability and activity. The optimization of reaction conditions, such as temperature and pressure, could also be a fruitful area of investigation to maximize the selectivity and yield of dehydrogenation reactions. Lastly, the integration of computational methods with experimental data could offer a more comprehensive understanding of the structure-activity relationships in VOx-based catalysts, guiding the rational design of next-generation catalysts for paraffin dehydrogenation.

自1980年代以来，对钒氧化物（VOx）基催化剂在石蜡脱氢中的探索，作为Pt和CrOx的替代品，受到了其复杂化学性质和替代传统催化剂潜力的驱动\[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]。研究揭示了在氧化还原条件下，支撑钒氧化物催化剂的结构对催化性能至关重要\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。使用多种激发波长的拉曼光谱等技术为钒物种的结构提供了新的见解\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。钒物种的性质受载体类型、支撑表面面积和金属负载量等因素的影响\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。在低表面密度下，孤立的VOx物种占主导，而在中间密度下，多钒酸盐与单钒酸盐共存，而在高密度下形成V2O5\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。还原过程中的重量损失随钒氧化物负载量的增加而增加，而高钒负载催化剂的再氧化速度更快\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。支撑VOx催化剂在脱氢反应中表现出良好的性能，其活性受表面VOx密度的影响\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。在密度高于1.2 V/nm2时，由于焦炭的形成，活性随时间的推移而降低，而较低的密度则保持稳定的活性\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。在较高丁烷压力下，高V/nm2密度的催化剂显示出增强的活性\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。通过氧化再生可以恢复VOx物种的结构，直到873 K，高负载样品显示出完全恢复\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。再生后观察到支撑物的转变，影响催化剂的表面积\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。
