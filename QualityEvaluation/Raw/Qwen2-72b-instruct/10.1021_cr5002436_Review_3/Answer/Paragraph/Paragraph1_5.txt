                    The exploration of vanadium oxide-based catalysts for paraffin dehydrogenation, driven by their complex chemistry and catalytic performance, has evolved significantly since the 1980s \[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]. Techniques like Raman spectroscopy, employing multiple excitation wavelengths, have provided deeper insights into the structure of supported vanadia catalysts \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. The nature of vanadium species is influenced by carrier type, support surface area, and metal loading, with isolated VOx species dominating at low surface densities, polyvanadates coexisting with monovanadates at intermediate densities, and V2O5 forming at high densities \[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.07.015\]. The weight loss at the reduction step increases with vanadia loading, and higher vanadium loading catalysts re-oxidize faster \[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.05.017\]. Supported VOx catalysts are highly active and selective for dehydrogenation and oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes \[10.1021/cr5002436_3\]. The initial dehydrogenation activity increases with surface VOx density, but activity decreases significantly for samples with densities higher than 1.2 V/nm2 due to coke formation and site blocking \[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.005\]. Catalysts with surface VOx density no higher than 1.2 V/nm2 maintain stable activity \[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]. At higher butane pressures, catalysts with higher densities show enhanced activity \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. The structure of VOx species can be restored by oxidation of coke deposits up to 873 K, but only high-loaded samples are fully restored \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]. After regeneration, the surface area of an 8%V catalyst was reduced by 50%, indicating transformations in the support nature \[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in understanding the behavior and structure of vanadium species in VOx-based catalysts have opened new avenues for optimizing paraffin dehydrogenation processes. Future research might focus on developing novel supports with tailored surface properties to enhance dispersion and stability of VOx species, aiming for a surface area-to-metal loading ratio that favors monomeric species below 1.2 V/nm2. The exploration of advanced characterization techniques, such as in situ Raman spectroscopy and 51V MAS NMR, could provide deeper insights into the dynamic behavior of vanadium species under reaction conditions. Additionally, the development of strategies to mitigate coke formation and enhance catalyst regeneration efficiency could lead to more sustainable and economically viable dehydrogenation processes.

                    自1980年代以来，由于钒氧化物催化剂的复杂化学性质和催化性能，对石蜡脱氢中钒氧化物基催化剂的探索已显著发展\[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]。采用多种激发波长的拉曼光谱技术为支持的钒氧化物催化剂结构提供了更深入的见解\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。钒物种的性质受载体类型、支撑表面面积和金属负载的影响，低表面密度下以孤立的VOx物种为主，中等密度下聚钒酸盐与单钒酸盐共存，高密度下形成V2O5\[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.07.015\]。还原步骤中的重量损失随钒氧化物负载的增加而增加，高钒负载催化剂的再氧化速度更快\[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.05.017\]。支持的VOx催化剂在烷烃脱氢和氧化脱氢中表现出高活性和选择性\[10.1021/cr5002436_3\]。表面VOx密度的初始脱氢活性增加，但密度高于1.2 V/nm2的样品由于焦炭形成和位点堵塞，活性显著下降\[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.11.005\]。表面VOx密度不超过1.2 V/nm2的催化剂保持稳定活性\[10.1039/c3cy01083j\]。在较高丁烷压力下，高密度催化剂表现出增强的活性\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。VOx物种的结构可以通过氧化焦炭沉积物至873 K来恢复，但只有高负载样品完全恢复\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。再生后，8%V催化剂的表面积减少了50%，表明支撑性质发生了变化\[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 对VOx基催化剂中钒物种行为和结构理解的进展为优化石蜡脱氢过程开辟了新的途径。未来的研究可能集中在开发具有定制表面特性的新型支撑，以增强VOx物种的分散性和稳定性，目标是表面面积与金属负载比，有利于低于1.2 V/nm2的单体物种。探索先进的表征技术，如原位拉曼光谱和51V MAS NMR，可以提供对反应条件下钒物种动态行为的更深入理解。此外，开发减少焦炭形成和提高催化剂再生效率的策略，可以导致更可持续和经济上可行的脱氢过程。
