How does the composition of coke deposits in catalytic systems, particularly in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, vary across different reaction conditions and catalyst types, and what analytical techniques have been employed to elucidate the complex nature and diversity of coke constituents in these systems?

<quotes>

[1] "The exact constituents of coke are not well resolved because of the complex process of the coke formation. Most studies broadly attributed coke as the deposition of undesirable organic compounds."
[2] "A recent study with gas chromatography combined with mass spectrometry (GC-MS) and thermogravimetric (TG) analysis pin down the detailed components of coke under PDH on PtSn catalysts. It was suggested that the coke mainly consists of three kind of species: aliphatics, aromatics, and pregraphite."
[3] "The species could be atomic carbon or aromatics on Pt/Al2O3 and C3 species on PtSn/Al2O3 catalyst, respectively."
[4] "The composition of cokes varies with reaction condition and catalytic system without a universal definition."

</quotes>

<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1.The exact composition of coke is not well resolved due to the complex process of coke formation. Most studies generally attribute coke to the deposition of undesirable organic compounds. A recent study using gas chromatography combined with mass spectrometry (GC-MS) and thermogravimetric (TG) analysis has identified the detailed components of coke under propane dehydrogenation (PDH) on PtSn catalysts, suggesting that coke mainly consists of three types of species: aliphatics, aromatics, and pregraphite. The species of coke deposition can vary on different catalysts, such as atomic carbon or aromatics on Pt/Al2O3 and C3 species on PtSn/Al2O3 catalysts. The composition of cokes varies with reaction conditions and catalytic systems, lacking a universal definition.

</EnglishVersionAnswer>
