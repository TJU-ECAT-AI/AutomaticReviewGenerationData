                The exact composition of coke deposits in catalytic systems, particularly in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, is a complex and multifaceted topic that has been extensively studied \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1\]. Recent investigations using gas chromatography combined with mass spectrometry (GC-MS) and thermogravimetric (TG) analysis have revealed that coke primarily comprises three distinct species: aliphatics, aromatics, and pregraphite \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1\]. These species can vary depending on the catalyst type, with atomic carbon or aromatics observed on Pt/Al2O3 and C3 species on PtSn/Al2O3 catalysts \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1\]. The composition of coke is influenced by reaction conditions and catalytic systems, lacking a universal definition \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1\].

                Operando Raman spectroscopy has been instrumental in elucidating the nature of coke deposits in PDH reactions \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. Hydrogen has been shown to alter the nature of coke deposits, leading to a more ordered system with fewer defects, smaller graphitic crystallites, and an increased D1/G ratio \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. The amount of amorphous coke decreases with increasing hydrogen content, and catalyst cycling affects the Raman band positions, widths, and D1/G ratio \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. A correlation exists between the type of coke formed and selectivity, with increased propane dehydrogenation conversion related to decreased coke deposition \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. Lower propane concentrations result in higher selectivity and less coke of a more graphitic nature, suggesting a link between disordered coke deposits and side reactions like cracking \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. TGA experiments confirm that hydrogen reduces coke formation and increases the combustion temperature of coke, consistent with a more graphitic nature \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\].

                Three types of coke have been identified in PDH reactions: coke on and in the vicinity of the metal, coke on the carrier, and graphitic coke on the carrier \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]. The formation of the first two types increases with temperature and partial pressure of propene, while hydrogen suppresses their formation \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]. Graphitic coke increases with temperature and propane partial pressure \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]. A model suggests that only a minor part of the coke deactivates the catalyst, with this deactivating coke formed in parallel with other types \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]. Graphitic coke on the carrier forms through a separate mechanism \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]. Hydrogen prevents coke formation and deactivation but cannot remove already formed coke \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\].

                Temperature programmed oxidation (TPO) has been used to characterize coke deposited on the catalyst during PDH, identifying different types of coke such as coke on the metal, coke on the support, and graphitic coke on the support \[10.1016/s0167-2991(99)80483-2\]. The amounts of these types of coke depend on the conditions, and a model has been proposed where a minor part of the coke deactivates the catalyst \[10.1016/s0167-2991(99)80483-2\]. The document focuses on the study of PDH over alumina-supported Pt-Sn catalysts, determining the kinetics of coke formation and the deactivation functions for the coking and dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1016/s0167-2991(99)80483-2\].

                The coke formed on the metal is softer (containing more hydrogen) than that formed on the support, with the rate of coke formation on the metal weakly dependent on propylene and hydrogen pressures but increasing with propane pressure \[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]. The rate of coke formation on the support increases with propane and propylene pressures and decreases with hydrogen pressure \[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]. The coke on the metal has aliphatic hydrocarbon characteristics, containing more hydrogen than that on the support, which has an aromatic characteristic \[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]. Analytical techniques such as thermal gravimetric analysis, IR spectroscopy, Raman spectroscopy, and elemental analysis have been employed to elucidate the complex nature and diversity of coke constituents in these systems \[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\].

                丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应中催化系统中焦炭沉积物的确切组成是一个复杂且多维的话题，已经进行了广泛的研究\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1\]。最近使用气相色谱结合质谱(GC-MS)和热重(TG)分析的研究揭示，焦炭主要由三种不同的物种组成：脂肪族、芳香族和预石墨\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1\]。这些物种会根据催化剂类型而变化，例如在Pt/Al2O3上观察到原子碳或芳香族，在PtSn/Al2O3催化剂上观察到C3物种\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1\]。焦炭的组成受反应条件和催化系统的影响，缺乏普遍的定义\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1\]。

                操作拉曼光谱在阐明PDH反应中焦炭沉积物的性质方面发挥了关键作用\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。氢气被发现可以改变焦炭沉积物的性质，导致一个更有序的系统，具有更少的缺陷，更小的石墨化晶粒，以及D1/G比的增加\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。随着氢气含量的增加，无定形焦炭的量减少，催化剂循环影响拉曼带的位置、宽度和D1/G比\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。焦炭类型与选择性之间存在相关性，丙烷脱氢转化率的增加与焦炭沉积的减少有关\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。较低的丙烷浓度导致选择性的显著增加，检测到较少的更石墨性质的焦炭，这表明无定形焦炭沉积物与裂解等副反应之间存在联系\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。TGA实验确认，氢气减少了焦炭的形成，并提高了焦炭的燃烧温度，与更石墨性质一致\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。

                在PDH反应中已经确定了三种类型的焦炭：金属上和金属附近的焦炭、载体上的焦炭、以及载体上的石墨化焦炭\[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]。前两种类型的焦炭的形成随着温度和丙烯的部分压力的增加而增加，而氢气抑制了它们的形成\[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]。石墨化焦炭随着温度和丙烷的部分压力的增加而增加\[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]。一个模型表明，只有焦炭的一小部分会失活催化剂，这种失活的焦炭与其它类型的焦炭并行形成\[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]。载体上的石墨化焦炭通过一个单独的机制形成\[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]。氢气可以防止焦炭的形成和失活，但不能去除已经形成的焦炭\[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]。

                温度编程氧化(TPO)已被用于表征PDH期间沉积在催化剂上的焦炭，识别不同类型的焦炭，如金属上的焦炭、支撑上的焦炭，以及支撑上的石墨化焦炭\[10.1016/s0167-2991(99)80483-2\]。这些类型的焦炭的量取决于条件，并且已经提出了一个模型，其中焦炭的一小部分会失活催化剂\[10.1016/s0167-2991(99)80483-2\]。该文件专注于研究在氧化铝支持的Pt-Sn催化剂上PDH，确定焦炭形成和失活功能的焦化和脱氢反应的速率\[10.1016/s0167-2991(99)80483-2\]。

                金属上的焦炭比支撑上的焦炭更软(含有更多的氢)，金属上的焦炭形成速率对丙烯和氢气压力的依赖性较弱，但随着丙烷压力的增加而增加\[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]。支撑上的焦炭形成速率随着丙烷和丙烯压力的增加而增加，随着氢气压力的降低而降低\[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]。金属上的焦炭具有脂肪族烃的特性，比支撑上的焦炭含有更多的氢，而支撑上的焦炭具有芳香族的特性\[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]。热重分析、红外光谱、拉曼光谱和元素分析等分析技术已被用于阐明这些系统中焦炭成分的复杂性和多样性\[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]。
