                The intricate nature of coke deposits in catalytic systems, particularly in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, has been elucidated through a plethora of studies, revealing the variability in coke composition across different reaction conditions and catalyst types \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1\]. GC-MS and TG analysis have identified coke as primarily comprising aliphatics, aromatics, and pregraphite, with the specific species of coke deposition varying on different catalysts, such as atomic carbon or aromatics on Pt/Al2O3 and C3 species on PtSn/Al2O3 catalysts \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1\]. Operando Raman spectroscopy has further illuminated the nature of coke deposits, demonstrating that hydrogen alters the nature of the coke, resulting in a more ordered system with fewer defects, smaller graphitic crystallites, and an increased D1/G ratio \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. The amount of amorphous coke decreases with increasing hydrogen content, and experiments with lower propane concentrations show a strong increase in selectivity, with less coke of a more graphitic nature detected, suggesting a link between disordered coke deposits and side reactions like cracking \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. TGA experiments confirm that less coke is formed in the presence of hydrogen, and that this coke combusts at higher temperatures, consistent with a more graphitic nature \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. Three types of coke have been identified: coke on and in the vicinity of the metal, coke on the carrier, and graphitic coke on the carrier \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]. The formation of the first two types of coke increases with temperature and partial pressure of propene, while hydrogen suppresses their formation. The amount of graphitic coke increases with temperature and propane partial pressure \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]. A model suggests that only a minor part of the coke deactivates the catalyst, and this deactivating coke is formed in parallel with the other coke types \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]. The graphitic coke on the carrier is formed through a separate mechanism \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]. Analytical techniques such as thermal gravimetric analysis, IR spectroscopy, Raman spectroscopy, and elemental analysis have been employed to elucidate the complex nature and diversity of coke constituents in these systems \[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\].

                催化系统中，尤其是在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中，焦炭沉积的复杂性质已通过大量研究得到阐明，揭示了在不同反应条件和催化剂类型下焦炭组成的差异性\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1\]。GC-MS和TG分析已确定焦炭主要由脂肪族、芳香族和预石墨组成，不同催化剂上的焦炭沉积物种存在差异，例如Pt/Al2O3上的原子碳或芳香族，以及PtSn/Al2O3催化剂上的C3物种\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1\]。原位拉曼光谱进一步揭示了焦炭沉积的性质，表明氢气可以改变焦炭的性质，导致更有序的系统、更少的缺陷、更小的石墨结晶体和更高的D1/G比\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。随着氢气含量的增加，无定形焦炭的量减少，而在较低的丙烷浓度下进行的实验显示选择性显著增加，检测到的焦炭更少且性质更石墨化，这表明无序焦炭沉积与裂解等副反应之间存在联系\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。TGA实验确认，在氢气存在下形成的焦炭较少，且在较高温度下燃烧，与更石墨化的性质一致\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。已识别出三种类型的焦炭：金属及其附近区域的焦炭、载体上的焦炭，以及载体上的石墨化焦炭\[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]。前两种类型的焦炭的形成随温度和丙烯分压的增加而增加，而氢气抑制了它们的形成。石墨化焦炭的量随温度和丙烷分压的增加而增加\[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]。一个模型表明，只有少量的焦炭会失活催化剂，而这种失活焦炭与其它类型的焦炭并行形成\[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]。载体上的石墨化焦炭通过单独的机制形成\[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]。热重分析、红外光谱、拉曼光谱和元素分析等分析技术已被用于阐明这些系统中焦炭成分的复杂性和多样性\[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]。
