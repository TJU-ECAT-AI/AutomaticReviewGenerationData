                The exact composition of coke deposits in catalytic systems, particularly in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, remains elusive due to the complex nature of coke formation \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1\]. Recent studies using gas chromatography combined with mass spectrometry (GC-MS) and thermogravimetric (TG) analysis have identified the detailed components of coke under PDH on PtSn catalysts, revealing that coke primarily consists of aliphatics, aromatics, and pregraphite \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1\]. The species of coke deposition can vary on different catalysts, such as atomic carbon or aromatics on Pt/Al2O3 and C3 species on PtSn/Al2O3 catalysts \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1\]. Operando Raman spectroscopy has been employed to study the nature of coke deposits in PDH reactions, showing that hydrogen alters the nature of the coke deposits, resulting in a more ordered system with fewer defects, smaller graphitic crystallites, and an increased D1/G ratio \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. The amount of amorphous coke decreases with increasing hydrogen content, and hydrogen is effective in preventing coke formation and deactivation \[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]. Three types of coke have been identified: coke on and in the vicinity of the metal, coke on the carrier, and graphitic coke on the carrier \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]. The formation of the first two types of coke increases with temperature and partial pressure of propene, while hydrogen suppresses their formation. The amount of graphitic coke increases with temperature and propane partial pressure \[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]. Temperature programmed oxidation (TPO) has been used to characterize the coke deposited on the catalyst during PDH, identifying different types of coke such as coke on the metal, coke on the support, and graphitic coke on the support \[10.1016/s0167-2991(99)80483-2\]. The rate of coke formation on the metal is weakly dependent on the propylene and hydrogen pressures but increases concurrently with the propane pressure. The rate of coke formation on the support increases concurrently with the propane and propylene pressures and decreases with the hydrogen pressure \[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The complexity of coke formation in PDH reactions necessitates the development of advanced analytical techniques and modeling approaches to better understand the composition and variability of coke deposits. Future research should focus on the integration of in situ and operando spectroscopic methods with advanced computational simulations to elucidate the detailed mechanisms of coke formation and its impact on catalyst deactivation. The exploration of novel catalyst designs and reaction conditions that minimize coke formation while maximizing selectivity and activity is also a promising direction. The use of machine learning algorithms to predict coke formation based on catalyst composition and reaction conditions could further enhance our understanding and control of PDH processes.

                丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中催化系统中焦炭沉积物的确切组成由于焦炭形成的复杂性而难以确定\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1\]。最近的研究使用气相色谱结合质谱（GC-MS）和热重分析（TG）确定了PDH在PtSn催化剂下焦炭的详细成分，揭示焦炭主要由脂肪族、芳香族和预石墨组成\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1\]。焦炭沉积物的种类在不同的催化剂上可以变化，例如Pt/Al2O3上的原子碳或芳香族和PtSn/Al2O3催化剂上的C3物种\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1\]。原位拉曼光谱已被用于研究PDH反应中焦炭沉积物的性质，显示氢气改变了焦炭沉积物的性质，导致更有序的系统，缺陷更少，石墨结晶体更小，D1/G比增加\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。随着氢气含量的增加，无定形焦炭的量减少，氢气在防止焦炭形成和失活方面有效\[10.1039/c3cp50646k\]。已确定三种类型的焦炭：金属上和金属附近的焦炭，载体上的焦炭，以及载体上的石墨焦炭\[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]。前两种类型焦炭的形成随着温度和丙烯分压的增加而增加，而氢气抑制了它们的形成。石墨焦炭的量随着温度和丙烷分压的增加而增加\[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361\]。温度程序氧化（TPO）已被用于表征PDH期间沉积在催化剂上的焦炭，识别不同类型的焦炭，如金属上的焦炭，支撑上的焦炭，以及支撑上的石墨焦炭\[10.1016/s0167-2991(99)80483-2\]。金属上的焦炭形成速率对丙烯和氢气压力的依赖性较弱，但与丙烷压力同时增加。支撑上的焦炭形成速率与丙烷和丙烯压力同时增加，而与氢气压力减少\[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望：PDH反应中焦炭形成的复杂性要求开发先进的分析技术和建模方法，以更好地理解焦炭沉积物的组成和变化。未来的研究应侧重于将原位和原位光谱方法与先进的计算模拟相结合，以阐明焦炭形成和其对催化剂失活影响的详细机制。探索最小化焦炭形成同时最大化选择性和活性的新型催化剂设计和反应条件也是一个有前景的方向。根据催化剂组成和反应条件预测焦炭形成的机器学习算法的使用可以进一步增强我们对PDH过程的理解和控制。
