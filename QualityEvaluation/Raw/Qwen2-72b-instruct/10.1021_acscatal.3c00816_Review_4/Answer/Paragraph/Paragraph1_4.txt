The performance of zeolite-based catalysts in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions is significantly influenced by preparation methods, composition, and topology \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]. Zn ion-exchange followed by Pt impregnation boosts Pt dispersion and reduces Zn loss, enhancing propylene selectivity and conversion \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]. Optimized PtZn-Beta catalysts achieve high propane conversion and propylene selectivity, nearing thermodynamic equilibrium \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]. Dealumination to create Si-Beta zeolites with abundant silanol nests improves metal dispersion and stabilization, boosting PDH performance \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]. Zn/Si-Beta catalysts outperform Zn/Al-Beta in propane conversion and propylene selectivity \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]. Co/Si-Beta catalysts exhibit higher propylene formation TOF \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]. Na+ doping of Sn-Beta catalysts weakens Bronsted acidity, improving propylene selectivity without affecting Lewis acidity \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]. Direct PDH mechanism on -Sn-(OSi)4 sites is energetically favorable \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]. Encapsulated Pt species within Si-Beta channels due to strong PtSn interactions lead to high propane conversion, propylene selectivity, and excellent stability \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]. PtSn/SAPO-34 shows higher propylene selectivity and yield than PtSn/ZSM-5 due to better coke-resistance and inert Bronsted acid sites in side pores \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\].

An improved post-synthesis strategy for PDH catalysts involves dealumination of H-beta and metal impregnation, leading to Sn incorporation into Si-beta's tetrahedral coordination states \[10.1039/C9CY01907C\]. This promotes high dispersion of Pt nanoclusters due to strong Pt-Sn(IV) interaction \[10.1039/C9CY01907C\]. Pt/Sn-Si-beta catalysts show high propene formation rates and selectivity, with stable dehydrogenation and regeneration due to PtSn interactions and Sn confinement, maintaining catalytic activity over multiple cycles \[10.1039/C9CY01907C\].

Encapsulating subnanometric clusters in zeolite pores boosts stability and catalytic applications \[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022\]. Framework-Sn in Beta zeolite anchors Pt precursors, forming Pt@Sn-Beta with enhanced stability in propane dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022\]. Zeolite pore restrictions and Sn's electronic effect inhibit coke formation and Pt cluster sintering, maintaining high propane conversion and propene selectivity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022\].

In PDH reactions, CoOx species confined in SiBeta zeolite's T-sites (silanols) form ultrasmall Co particles under H2 or reactant gas, enhancing catalytic efficiency for PDH to propylene \[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037\]. The CoSiBeta catalyst with 0.5 wt% Co shows state-of-the-art propylene productivity, achieving high propane conversion and propylene selectivity, outperforming other Co catalysts and matching industrial standards \[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037\]. Co loading impacts catalytic performance, with initial propane conversion increasing and maximal conversion reaching around 53, 59, 80, and 81% for 0.5/1/3/10CoSiBeta catalysts, respectively \[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037\]. Propylene selectivities are high and stable for 0.5/1CoSiBeta, stabilizing at about 98% \[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037\]. The small metallic Co particles generated by the reduction of confined CoOx are the active sites, showing great catalytic activity with high product selectivity and ideal reusability \[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037\].

In PDH reactions, PtSnNa/SUZ-4 outperforms PtSnNa/ZSM-5 due to a higher external Pt quantity, minimizing deactivation by carbon deposition \[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62750-5\]. The strong acid sites of SUZ-4 prevent Pt precursor impregnation into the zeolite, while ZSM-5's weak acidity allows more precursor entry, leading to dispersed Pt clusters \[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62750-5\]. Sn oxides on PtSnNa/ZSM-5 are easier to reduce, decreasing catalytic activity \[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62750-5\]. Key factors for improved performance include Pt distribution, zeolite acidity, and Pt-Sn-support interactions \[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62750-5\].

基于\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]参考文献的深度分析。从\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]的参考文献中获得的综合理解。Zn离子交换后Pt浸渍提高了Pt分散度并减少了Zn损失，增强了丙烯选择性和转化率\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]。优化的PtZn-Beta催化剂实现了高丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性，接近热力学平衡\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]。脱铝化创建了含有丰富硅醇巢的Si-Beta沸石，改善了金属分散度和稳定化，提高了PDH性能\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]。Zn/Si-Beta催化剂在丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性方面优于Zn/Al-Beta\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]。Co/Si-Beta催化剂表现出更高的丙烯形成TOF\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]。Sn-Beta催化剂的Na+掺杂削弱了Bronsted酸性，提高了丙烯选择性而不影响Lewis酸性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]。直接PDH机制在-Sn-(OSi)4位点上是能量有利的\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]。由于强PtSn相互作用，封装在Si-Beta通道内的Pt物种导致高丙烷转化率、丙烯选择性和优异的稳定性\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]。PtSn/SAPO-34的丙烯选择性和产量高于PtSn/ZSM-5，由于更好的焦炭抗性和侧孔中惰性Bronsted酸位点\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]。

改进的后合成策略涉及H-beta的脱铝化和金属浸渍，导致Sn被纳入Si-beta的四面体配位状态\[10.1039/C9CY01907C\]。这促进了由于强Pt-Sn(IV)相互作用而高度分散的Pt纳米簇\[10.1039/C9CY01907C\]。Pt/Sn-Si-beta催化剂显示出高丙烯形成率和选择性，由于PtSn相互作用和Sn限制，稳定脱氢和再生，维持催化活性超过多个周期\[10.1039/C9CY01907C\]。

封装亚纳米簇在沸石孔中提高了稳定性和催化应用\[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022\]。Beta沸石中的框架Sn锚定了Pt前体，形成了增强丙烷脱氢稳定性的Pt@Sn-Beta\[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022\]。沸石孔限制和Sn的电子效应抑制了焦炭形成和Pt簇烧结，维持了高丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022\]。

在PDH反应中，CoOx物种在SiBeta沸石的T位点（硅醇）下形成超小Co颗粒，在H2或反应气体下，增强了PDH至丙烯的催化效率\[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037\]。0.5 wt% Co的CoSiBeta催化剂展示了最先进的丙烯生产力，实现了高丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性，优于其他Co催化剂并匹配工业标准\[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037\]。Co负载影响催化性能，初始丙烷转化率增加，最大转化率分别达到约53、59、80和81%的0.5/1/3/10CoSiBeta催化剂\[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037\]。丙烯选择性对于0.5/1CoSiBeta是高且稳定的，稳定在约98%\[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037\]。由受限CoOx还原产生的小金属Co颗粒是活性位点，显示出高催化活性和高产品选择性以及理想的可重用性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037\]。

在PDH反应中，PtSnNa/SUZ-4由于更高的外部Pt量，优于PtSnNa/ZSM-5，最小化了由碳沉积引起的失活\[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62750-5\]。SUZ-4的强酸位阻止了Pt前体浸渍到沸石中，而ZSM-5的弱酸性允许更多的前体进入，导致分散的Pt簇\[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62750-5\]。PtSnNa/ZSM-5上的Sn氧化物更容易还原，降低了催化活性\[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62750-5\]。改进性能的关键因素包括Pt分布、沸石酸性和Pt-Sn-支持相互作用\[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62750-5\]。
