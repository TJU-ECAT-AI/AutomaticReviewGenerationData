            <English> 
                    The performance of zeolite-based catalysts in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions is significantly influenced by preparation methods, composition, and topology, as highlighted in studies \[10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008\], \[10.1002/cctc.201801708\], and \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]. PtSn/SAPO-34 catalysts, prepared by sequential impregnation, achieved 94% propylene selectivity and >97% total olefins selectivity, surpassing PtSn/ZSM-5 due to SAPO-34's larger surface area, shape selectivity, and suitable acidity \[10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008\]. Dealuminated β zeolite-supported ZnO nanoclusters served as an efficient PDH catalyst, achieving high propane conversion and propylene selectivity, with dealumination reducing strong acid sites and minimizing side reactions \[10.1002/cctc.201801708\]. Sn-Beta zeolite, with single-site Sn in the BEA framework, excelled in PDH, achieving high selectivity and stability, with the Na-Sn-Beta-30 catalyst achieving 40% propane conversion and 92% propylene selectivity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in zeolite-based catalysts for PDH reactions, as detailed in the studies \[10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008\], \[10.1002/cctc.201801708\], and \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\], offer a fertile ground for future research. The optimization of preparation methods, such as sequential impregnation and dealumination, can lead to the development of catalysts with enhanced selectivity, conversion rates, and stability. The exploration of novel zeolite topologies, like SAPO-34 and Sn-Beta, with specific acidity and shape selectivity, presents opportunities for improving PDH efficiency. Future studies might focus on the synergistic effects of metal doping and zeolite topology, aiming to create catalysts that can operate under harsh conditions with minimal deactivation. Additionally, the investigation of the electronic effects of metal-support interactions, as seen in PtSn/SAPO-34 and Sn-Beta, could provide insights into the design of catalysts with tailored properties for PDH and other dehydrogenation reactions.

                    丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应中，基于沸石的催化剂的性能受到制备方法、组成和拓扑结构的显著影响，如研究\[10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008\]、\[10.1002/cctc.201801708\]和\[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]所示。通过顺序浸渍制备的PtSn/SAPO-34催化剂实现了94%的丙烯选择性和>97%的总烯烃选择性，由于SAPO-34具有更大的表面积、形状选择性和适宜的酸性，其性能超过了PtSn/ZSM-5\[10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008\]。脱铝β沸石支持的ZnO纳米簇作为高效的PDH催化剂，实现了高丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性，脱铝减少了强酸位点，最小化了副反应\[10.1002/cctc.201801708\]。BEA框架中具有单位点Sn的Sn-Beta沸石在PDH中表现出色，实现了高选择性和稳定性，Na-Sn-Beta-30催化剂实现了40%的丙烷转化率和92%的丙烯选择性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]。

                    综合理解与未来展望: 如研究\[10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008\]、\[10.1002/cctc.201801708\]和\[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]中详述的PDH反应中基于沸石的催化剂的进展，为未来的研究提供了肥沃的土壤。制备方法的优化，如顺序浸渍和脱铝，可以导致具有增强选择性、转化率和稳定性的催化剂的开发。探索新型沸石拓扑结构，如SAPO-34和Sn-Beta，具有特定的酸性和形状选择性，为提高PDH效率提供了机会。未来的研究可能集中在金属掺杂和沸石拓扑结构的协同效应上，旨在创建能够在恶劣条件下以最小失活运行的催化剂。此外，金属-支持相互作用的电子效应的调查，如在PtSn/SAPO-34和Sn-Beta中所见，可能为设计具有定制性质的催化剂以用于PDH和其他脱氢反应提供见解。
