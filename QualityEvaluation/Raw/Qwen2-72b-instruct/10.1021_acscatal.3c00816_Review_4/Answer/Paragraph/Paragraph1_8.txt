<English>
The performance of zeolite-based catalysts in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions is significantly influenced by preparation methods, composition, and topology. PtSn/SAPO-34 catalysts, prepared by sequential impregnation, achieved 94% propylene selectivity and >97% total olefins selectivity, outperforming PtSn/ZSM-5 due to their larger surface area, shape selectivity, and suitable acidity \[10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008\]. Dealuminated β zeolite, with anchored ZnO nanoclusters, serves as a high-performance PDH catalyst, achieving high initial propane conversion and superior propylene selectivity, with excellent stability and reusability \[10.1002/cctc.201801708\]. Sn-Beta zeolite, with single-site Sn in the BEA framework, excels in PDH, achieving high selectivity, activity, and stability \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]. Atomic layer deposition (ALD) of ZnO onto ZSM-5 and Y zeolites enhances catalytic activity for propane conversion in PDH reactions, improving propylene selectivity and conversion rates \[10.1039/C5CP05043J\]. The location of Pt clusters on X-zeolite significantly affects PDH catalyst performance \[10.1039/D1CY00738F\]. PtZn-containing zeolites Beta and mordenite with nSi/nAl ratios >8.7 and 2.5 wt.% zinc content showed the highest and most stable propane conversion levels, achieving thermodynamic equilibrium for propane dehydrogenation to propene \[10.1016/j.apcata.2006.03.028\]. Preparation methods, composition, and topology significantly impact zeolite-based catalysts in PDH \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]. The performance of PtSn-based catalysts in PDH is influenced by interactions between Pt, Sn, and the support \[10.1021/ie901465s\]. An improved post-synthesis strategy for PDH catalysts involves dealumination of H-beta and metal impregnation \[10.1039/C9CY01907C\]. VOx catalysts on dealuminated Beta zeolite (SiBeta) exhibit varying catalytic activities depending on V loading \[10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63444-3\]. Encapsulating subnanometric clusters in zeolite pores boosts stability and catalytic applications \[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022\]. In PDH reactions, CoSiBeta catalysts, particularly those with 0.5 wt% Co, exhibit superior propylene productivity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037\]. PtSnNa/SUZ-4 outperforms PtSnNa/ZSM-5 due to a higher external Pt quantity, minimizing deactivation by carbon deposition \[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62750-5\].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in zeolite-based catalysts for PDH reactions, as discussed in the file, offer a rich landscape for future research. The optimization of preparation methods, such as sequential impregnation and ALD, and the exploration of novel topologies like SAPO-34 and dealuminated β zeolite, have significantly improved propylene selectivity and conversion rates. The role of metal species, particularly Pt, Sn, and Zn, in enhancing catalytic activity and stability has been elucidated. Future research might focus on the development of multifunctional catalysts that combine the advantages of different metal species and zeolite topologies. The integration of advanced characterization techniques, such as in-situ spectroscopy and microscopy, could provide deeper insights into the active sites and reaction mechanisms. Moreover, the design of catalysts with enhanced coke-resistance and improved stability under harsh PDH conditions remains a critical challenge. The exploration of novel synthesis strategies, such as the use of ionic liquids and green solvents, could offer sustainable alternatives for catalyst preparation. The optimization of reaction conditions, including temperature, pressure, and reactant ratios, could further enhance the efficiency and selectivity of PDH processes. The development of catalysts that can operate under milder conditions could reduce energy consumption and environmental impact. The integration of catalytic PDH with other processes, such as hydrogen production and carbon capture, could lead to more sustainable and integrated chemical production systems.

基于\[10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008\]的参考文献，PtSn/SAPO-34催化剂通过顺序浸渍制备，实现了94%的丙烯选择性和>97%的总烯烃选择性，优于PtSn/ZSM-5，归因于其较大的表面积、形状选择性和适宜的酸性。基于\[10.1002/cctc.201801708\]的参考文献，脱铝β沸石，带有锚定的ZnO纳米簇，作为高性能PDH催化剂，实现了高初始丙烷转化率和优越的丙烯选择性，具有优异的稳定性和可重复使用性。基于\[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019\]的参考文献，Sn-Beta沸石，BEA框架中的单位点Sn，在PDH中表现出高选择性、活性和稳定性。基于\[10.1039/C5CP05043J\]的参考文献，ZnO通过原子层沉积（ALD）到ZSM-5和Y沸石上，增强了PDH反应中丙烷转化的催化活性，提高了丙烯选择性和转化率。基于\[10.1039/D1CY00738F\]的参考文献，X-沸石上Pt簇的位置对PDH催化剂性能有显著影响。基于\[10.1016/j.apcata.2006.03.028\]的参考文献，含PtZn的Beta和mordenite沸石，nSi/nAl比率>8.7和2.5 wt.%锌含量，显示出最高和最稳定的丙烷转化水平，实现了丙烷脱氢到丙烯的热力学平衡。基于\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4\]的参考文献，制备方法、组成和拓扑结构对PDH中的沸石基催化剂有重大影响。基于\[10.1021/ie901465s\]的参考文献，PtSn基催化剂在PDH中的性能受Pt、Sn和载体之间相互作用的影响。基于\[10.1039/C9CY01907C\]的参考文献，PDH催化剂的改进后合成策略涉及H-beta的脱铝和金属浸渍。基于\[10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63444-3\]的参考文献，VOx催化剂在脱铝Beta沸石（SiBeta）上，根据V负载的不同，表现出不同的催化活性。基于\[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022\]的参考文献，将亚纳米簇封装在沸石孔中，提高了稳定性和催化应用。基于\[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037\]的参考文献，在PDH反应中，CoSiBeta催化剂，特别是含有0.5 wt% Co的催化剂，表现出优越的丙烯生产力。基于\[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62750-5\]的参考文献，PtSnNa/SUZ-4优于PtSnNa/ZSM-5，因为外部Pt数量较高，减少了由碳沉积引起的失活。

综合理解与未来展望：文件中讨论的PDH反应中沸石基催化剂的进展，为未来的研究提供了丰富的景观。制备方法的优化，如顺序浸渍和ALD，以及新型拓扑结构的探索，如SAPO-34和脱铝β沸石，显著提高了丙烯选择性和转化率。金属物种，特别是Pt、Sn和Zn，在增强催化活性和稳定性方面的作用已被阐明。未来的研究可能集中在开发结合不同金属物种和沸石拓扑结构优势的多功能催化剂上。先进的表征技术，如原位光谱学和显微镜，的集成可以提供对活性位点和反应机制的更深入理解。此外，设计具有增强的焦炭抗性和在恶劣PDH条件下改善稳定性的催化剂仍然是一个关键挑战。新型合成策略，如使用离子液体和绿色溶剂，的探索可以为催化剂制备提供可持续的替代方案。反应条件的优化，包括温度、压力和反应物比例，可以进一步提高PDH过程的效率和选择性。在较温和条件下运行的催化剂的开发可以降低能源消耗和环境影响。PDH催化与氢气生产和碳捕获等其他过程的集成可以导致更可持续和集成的化学生产系统。
