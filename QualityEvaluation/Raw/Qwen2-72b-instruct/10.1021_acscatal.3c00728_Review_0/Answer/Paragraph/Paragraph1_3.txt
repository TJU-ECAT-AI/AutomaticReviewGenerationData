                    The evolving significance of propene in the chemical industry is driven by its demand outpacing that of ethylene, particularly in Western Europe \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. This trend propels the adoption of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as a preferred production method, addressing the misalignment between current olefin production and forecasted demand. Successful PDH technologies combine catalyst performance with innovative designs, offering improved energy efficiency and process simplicity \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. However, achieving better olefin yields is crucial for cost-effectiveness. The higher exothermicity of PDH reactions leads to increased energy losses and fixed costs, especially considering CO2 production \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Despite these challenges, PDH remains an economically viable and environmentally friendly option for meeting the growing demand for propene in various applications \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\].
