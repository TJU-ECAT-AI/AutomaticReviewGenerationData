                    Propene's significance in the chemical industry has grown due to its demand outpacing that of ethylene, particularly in Western Europe \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. This mismatch between current olefin production and market needs drives the adoption of propane dehydrogenation (PDH), offering improved energy efficiency and process simplicity \[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009\]. Propene, the second most important olefin, is critical for producing high-value-added chemicals and polymers \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_0\]. Its demand has surged with the chemical industry's expansion and increased consumption of these products \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_0\]. Propane dehydrogenation is favored for propene production due to its economic viability and environmental benefits over other methods \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_0\]. Propene's role as a key feedstock for polymers, oxygenates, and derivatives has grown, driving demand \[10.1039/C8CC04604B\]. Propane dehydrogenation, a more direct and efficient production method, is increasingly preferred over traditional processes \[10.1039/C8CC04604B\]. Economically, it offers higher yields and lower costs, while environmentally, it reduces carbon emissions and energy consumption, making it a sustainable choice for meeting the growing demand for propene \[10.1039/C8CC04604B\].

                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of propene's significance in the chemical industry, driven by increasing demand and diverse applications, highlights the pivotal role of propane dehydrogenation as a preferred production method. The economic and environmental advantages of this approach, including improved energy efficiency and reduced carbon emissions, position it as a sustainable solution for meeting the growing needs for propene. Future research endeavors might focus on enhancing catalyst performance and process design to achieve higher olefin yields, addressing the challenge of overoxidation, and developing materials for better process control. The discovery of innovative catalysts and the optimization of reactor designs could significantly impact the industry, given the high demand for propene and the energy-intensive nature of its production. Moreover, the exploration of alternative feedstocks and the integration of renewable energy sources in the PDH process could further enhance its sustainability and economic viability.
