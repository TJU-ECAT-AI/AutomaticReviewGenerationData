Its increasing demand, particularly in the production of polypropylene, has driven the adoption of propane dehydrogenation as a preferred production method. This approach offers economic and environmental advantages by providing a more direct route to propene production, bypassing the traditional and energy-intensive cracking of petroleum-derived hydrocarbons.
