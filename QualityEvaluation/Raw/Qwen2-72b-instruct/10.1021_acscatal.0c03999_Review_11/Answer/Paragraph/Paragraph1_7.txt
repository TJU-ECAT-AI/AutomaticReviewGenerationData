                Halogen-assisted methods for selective propane dehydrogenation have been extensively studied, offering a range of approaches to convert propane (C3H8) to propene (C3H6) with high selectivity \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11\]. These methods include dehydrogenation with molecular halogens, oxidative dehydrogenation in the presence of halogen and oxygen, propane oxyhalogenation using halides and oxygen, and molten metal halide salts assisted oxidative dehydrogenation \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11\]. The catalytic transformation of propane in these processes is distinct from other alkanes like methane and ethane due to the specific reactivity of each alkane \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11\]. Iodine-mediated dehydrogenation, alkali metal chloride-catalyzed oxidative dehydrogenation, metal iodide-catalyzed oxidative dehydrogenation, and bihalogen-aided dehydrogenation are among the methods for selectively dehydrogenating propane to propene \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]. Iodine-mediated dehydrogenation involves the substitution of molecular iodine with hydrocarbons, but iodine is poorly reactive toward saturated hydrocarbons \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]. A free radical mechanism was proposed for the stoichiometric iodination, consisting of successive hydrogen abstraction reactions involving both atomic and molecular iodine \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]. Molten salts (metal chlorides and iodides) catalyzed oxidative dehydrogenation uses supported alkali metal chloride catalysts, which have been demonstrated to be one of the most promising candidates for the oxidative dehydrogenation of light aliphatic alkanes (C2C4) to olefins \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]. Oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes can also be catalyzed by iodide-based molten salts \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]. An interesting oxidative dehydrogenation process for various hydrocarbons, based on metal iodides, was developed by Shell \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]. Propane dehydrogenation to propylene has been extensively explored as an alternative approach to supplement the current propylene production based on steam cracking, fluidic catalytic cracking of naphtha and other hydrocarbons, and SAPO-34 catalyzed methanol to mixed olefins \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]. Iodine-mediated dehydrogenation generally gives higher propylene yields compared to the conventional dehydrogenation routes \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]. Recently, Stucky et al. developed a low-temperature route for propane dehydrogenation by using bihalogens (Br2 and I2) as the mediators \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]. A high yield of C3H6+C3H7X (X = Br and I) up to 80% was attained at moderate reaction temperatures below 800 K \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]. The interplay between Br2 and I2 was believed to be crucial, combining the high reactivity of bromine and high selectivity of iodine \[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in halogen-assisted methods for selective propane dehydrogenation have opened up new avenues for the production of propene. The unique role of halogens in these processes, particularly the interplay between different halogens, offers a promising direction for future research. The development of low-temperature routes using bihalogens as mediators, such as the work by Stucky et al., highlights the potential for more efficient and environmentally friendly processes. The functions of metal iodides in the dehydrogenation, while not well-understood, present an opportunity for further investigation. The exploration of novel catalysts and reaction conditions that can enhance the selectivity and yield of propene while minimizing byproducts and energy consumption is a critical area for future research. The integration of computational methods with experimental studies could provide deeper insights into the mechanisms of these reactions and guide the design of more effective catalysts. The potential for combining halogen-assisted methods with other processes, such as steam cracking or catalytic cracking, to improve overall efficiency and product distribution is also worth exploring. The development of sustainable and economically viable processes for propane dehydrogenation is crucial for meeting the growing demand for propene in the chemical industry.

                卤素辅助的丙烷选择性脱氢方法已被广泛研究，提供了一系列将丙烷（C3H8）转化为丙烯（C3H6）的高选择性途径\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11\]。这些方法包括使用分子卤素的脱氢、在卤素和氧气存在下的氧化脱氢、使用卤化物和氧气的丙烷氧卤化，以及熔融金属卤化盐辅助的氧化脱氢\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11\]。在这些过程中，丙烷的催化转化与甲烷和乙烷等其他烷烃明显不同，这是由于每种烷烃的特定反应性\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11\]。选择性将丙烷脱氢至丙烯的方法包括碘介导的脱氢、碱金属氯化物催化的氧化脱氢、金属碘化物催化的氧化脱氢和双卤素辅助的脱氢\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]。碘介导的脱氢涉及分子碘与烃类的置换，但碘对饱和烃类的反应性较差\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]。提出了一个自由基机制来描述计量碘化，它涉及原子和分子碘参与的连续氢原子抽提反应\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]。熔融盐（金属氯化物和碘化物）催化的氧化脱氢使用了支持的碱金属氯化物催化剂，这些催化剂已被证明是将轻质脂肪族烷烃（C2C4）氧化脱氢为烯烃的最有希望的候选者之一\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]。烷烃的氧化脱氢也可以通过碘化物基熔融盐催化\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]。壳牌公司开发了一种基于金属碘化物的烷烃氧化脱氢的有趣过程\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]。丙烷脱氢至丙烯已被广泛探索，作为一种补充当前基于蒸汽裂解、流化催化裂解石脑油和其他烃类以及SAPO-34催化甲醇至混合烯烃的丙烯生产的替代途径\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]。碘介导的脱氢通常比传统脱氢路线产生更高的丙烯产量\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]。最近，Stucky等人开发了一种使用双卤素（Br2和I2）作为介质的丙烷脱氢的低温路线\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]。在800 K以下的温和反应温度下，C3H6+C3H7X（X = Br和I）的高产率高达80%\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]。Br2和I2之间的相互作用被认为是关键，结合了溴的高反应性和碘的高选择性\[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望：卤素辅助的丙烷选择性脱氢方法的进展为丙烯的生产开辟了新的途径。卤素在这些过程中的独特作用，特别是不同卤素之间的相互作用，为未来的研究提供了一个有希望的方向。Stucky等人开发的使用双卤素作为介质的低温路线，突显了更高效和环境友好的过程的潜力。金属碘化物在脱氢中的功能，虽然尚未得到充分理解，为更深入的研究提供了机会。探索能够提高丙烯选择性和产量，同时最小化副产品和能源消耗的新催化剂和反应条件是未来研究的关键领域。将计算方法与实验研究相结合，可以为这些反应的机制提供更深入的见解，并指导更有效催化剂的设计。将卤素辅助方法与其他过程（如蒸汽裂解或催化裂解）结合，以提高整体效率和产品分布，也值得探索。开发可持续和经济可行的丙烷脱氢过程，对于满足化学工业对丙烯日益增长的需求至关重要。
