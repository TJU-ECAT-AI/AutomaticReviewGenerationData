1.The literature does not provide a detailed comparison of various halogen-assisted methods for selectively dehydrogenating propane to propene. It focuses on the dual-halogen process using bromine and iodine together as oxidants in propane oxidative dehydrogenation. This process achieves a single-pass C3H6+C3H7X yield above 80% at moderate reaction temperatures (<800K). The literature does not discuss dehydrogenation with halogens, oxidative dehydrogenation, oxyhalogenation, or molten metal halide salt approaches in detail. It also does not analyze why propane's reactivity in these processes differs significantly from that of other alkanes such as methane and ethane.
