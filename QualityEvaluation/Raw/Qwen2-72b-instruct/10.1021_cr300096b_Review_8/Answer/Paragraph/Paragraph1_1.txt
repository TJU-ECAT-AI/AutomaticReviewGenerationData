                    DFT calculations elucidate that the Pt-terminated surfaces of bimetallic Pt-3d catalysts exhibit weaker bonding with hydrogen and hydrocarbon adsorbates compared to monometallic Pt surfaces, leading to optimized binding energies that are accountable for higher hydrogenation rates \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. This observation is corroborated by experimental findings from TPD measurements, which have demonstrated that bimetallic PtNi and PtCo catalysts possess higher hydrogenation rates for benzene and 1,4-cyclohexadiene than the monometallic Pt catalyst \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. The enhancement in hydrogenation rates can be attributed to the presence of Pt-terminated configurations in the bimetallic catalysts \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. DFT calculations of surface d-band center and adsorbate binding energies for various C═C bond-containing molecules (including linear alkenes, cyclic hydrocarbons, and aromatics) on Pt-3d catalysts are in agreement with experimental observations from techniques such as TPD measurements \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. This integrated theoretical and experimental approach can be utilized to predict hydrogenation activity and inform the design of more efficient Pt-3d catalytic systems \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. Goda et al. performed DFT calculations of 1-butene and 1-hexene on 3d/Pt(111) surfaces and found that the subsurface Pt3dPt(111) structure binds more weakly to 1-butene and 1-hexene than on Pt(111) and 3dPtPt(111) surfaces, similar to the trend observed for cyclohexene \[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]. Murillo et al. verified these predictions through TPD measurements of low-temperature hydrogenation of 1-butene and 1-hexene on PtNiPt(111) \[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]. These results suggest that DFT calculations of surface d-band center and adsorbate binding energy can serve as descriptors for catalysts with good hydrogenation activity \[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]. Similar calculations have been conducted for 1,3-butadiene, 1,3-cyclohexadiene, 1,4-cyclohexadiene, and benzene on 3d/Pt(111) surfaces \[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]. Based on binding energies and subsurface Pt3dPt(111) structures, supported Pt3d catalysts are predicted to exhibit high hydrogenation activity for these molecules, a prediction that has been validated in reactor studies \[10.1021/cr300096b_8\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The integration of DFT calculations and experimental techniques such as TPD measurements has provided a comprehensive framework for understanding the hydrogenation activity of Pt-3d catalysts. This approach has not only enabled the prediction of hydrogenation activity but also informed the design of more efficient catalytic systems. Future research endeavors might focus on expanding the scope of DFT calculations to include a wider range of C═C bond-containing molecules and exploring the effects of different bimetallic compositions on hydrogenation activity. Additionally, the development of novel experimental techniques that can provide more detailed insights into the catalytic mechanisms at play could further enhance our understanding of these systems. The integration of machine learning algorithms with DFT calculations could also offer a promising avenue for predicting the hydrogenation activity of catalysts with unprecedented accuracy and efficiency.

                    密度泛函理论(DFT)计算表明，双金属Pt-3d催化剂的Pt终止表面与氢和烃类吸附物的结合力较单金属Pt表面弱，导致优化的结合能，这可归因于更高的氢化速率\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。这一观察结果得到了热脱附光谱(TPD)测量的实验发现的证实，这些发现表明，双金属PtNi和PtCo催化剂对苯和1,4-环己二烯的氢化速率高于单金属Pt催化剂\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。氢化速率的提高可归因于双金属催化剂中存在Pt终止的配置\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。对于各种含C═C键的分子（包括线性烯烃、环状烃和芳香烃）在Pt-3d催化剂上的表面d带中心和吸附物结合能的DFT计算与热脱附光谱(TPD)测量等技术的实验观察结果相一致\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。这种理论与实验相结合的方法可用于预测氢化活性，并指导更高效Pt-3d催化系统的开发\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。Goda等人对3d/Pt(111)表面上的1-丁烯和1-己烯进行了DFT计算，发现与Pt(111)和3dPtPt(111)表面相比，亚表面Pt3dPt(111)结构与1-丁烯和1-己烯的结合力较弱，与对环己烯观察到的趋势相似\[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]。Murillo等人通过在PtNiPt(111)上对1-丁烯和1-己烯的低温氢化的TPD测量验证了这些预测\[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]。这些结果表明，DFT计算的表面d带中心和吸附物结合能可以作为具有良好氢化活性催化剂的描述符\[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]。对3d/Pt(111)表面上的1,3-丁二烯、1,3-环己二烯、1,4-环己二烯和苯进行了类似的计算\[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]。根据结合能和亚表面Pt3dPt(111)结构，支持的Pt3d催化剂被预测对这些分子表现出高氢化活性，这一预测已在反应器研究中得到验证\[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望：DFT计算与热脱附光谱(TPD)测量等实验技术的结合为理解Pt-3d催化剂的氢化活性提供了一个全面的框架。这种方法不仅使氢化活性的预测成为可能，还指导了更高效催化系统的开发。未来的研究工作可能侧重于扩大DFT计算的范围，包括更广泛的含C═C键的分子，并探索不同双金属组成的氢化活性的影响。此外，开发能够提供催化机制更详细见解的新实验技术，可以进一步增强我们对这些系统的理解。将机器学习算法与DFT计算相结合，也可能为以前所未有的准确性和效率预测催化剂的氢化活性提供一个有希望的途径。
