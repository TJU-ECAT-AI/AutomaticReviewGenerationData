                    DFT calculations elucidate that the Pt-terminated surfaces of bimetallic Pt-3d catalysts exhibit weaker bonding with hydrogen and hydrocarbon adsorbates compared to monometallic Pt surfaces, leading to optimized binding energies that are accountable for higher hydrogenation rates \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. This observation is corroborated by experimental findings from TPD measurements, which demonstrate that bimetallic PtNi and PtCo catalysts manifest superior hydrogenation rates for benzene and 1,4-cyclohexadiene over the monometallic Pt catalyst \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. The enhancement in hydrogenation rates can be attributed to the presence of Pt-terminated configurations in the bimetallic catalysts \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. DFT calculations of surface d-band center and adsorbate binding energies for a variety of C═C bond-containing molecules, encompassing linear alkenes, cyclic hydrocarbons, and aromatics, on Pt-3d catalysts, align with experimental observations from TPD measurements \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. This integrated theoretical and experimental approach can be harnessed to forecast hydrogenation activity and guide the design of more efficient Pt-3d catalytic systems \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. Goda et al. performed DFT calculations for 1-butene and 1-hexene on 3d/Pt(111) surfaces, revealing that the subsurface Pt3dPt(111) structure binds these molecules more weakly than Pt(111) and 3dPtPt(111) surfaces, mirroring the trend observed for cyclohexene \[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]. Murillo et al. substantiated these predictions through TPD measurements of facile low-temperature hydrogenation of 1-butene and 1-hexene on PtNiPt(111) \[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]. These results underscore the potential of DFT calculations of surface d-band center and adsorbate binding energy as descriptors for catalysts with commendable hydrogenation activity \[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]. Similar DFT calculations have been executed for the binding energies of 1,3-butadiene, 1,3-cyclohexadiene, 1,4-cyclohexadiene, and benzene on 3d/Pt(111) surfaces \[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]. Based on binding energies and subsurface Pt3dPt(111) structures, supported Pt3d catalysts are anticipated to display high hydrogenation activity for these molecules, a prediction that has been substantiated in reactor studies \[10.1021/cr300096b_8\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The amalgamation of DFT calculations and experimental techniques, such as TPD measurements, has proven instrumental in elucidating the structure-activity relationships of Pt-3d catalysts for hydrogenation reactions. This integrated approach not only facilitates the prediction of hydrogenation activity but also informs the design of more efficient catalytic systems. Future research endeavors might concentrate on the exploration of novel bimetallic configurations and the optimization of subsurface structures to further enhance the catalytic performance. Additionally, the development of advanced computational methodologies and the refinement of experimental techniques could provide deeper insights into the catalytic mechanisms and enable the design of catalysts with tailored properties for specific hydrogenation reactions. The integration of machine learning algorithms with DFT calculations could potentially accelerate the discovery of new catalysts by predicting the hydrogenation activity of a wide range of materials with high accuracy.

                    密度泛函理论(DFT)计算揭示，双金属Pt-3d催化剂的Pt终止表面与氢和碳氢化合物吸附物的结合力较单金属Pt表面弱，导致优化的结合能，这是更高氢化率的原因\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。这一观察结果得到了热脱附光谱(TPD)测量的实验发现的证实，这些发现表明，双金属PtNi和PtCo催化剂在苯和1,4-环己二烯的氢化率上优于单金属Pt催化剂\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。氢化率的提高可归因于双金属催化剂中存在Pt终止的配置\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。DFT计算了Pt-3d催化剂表面d带中心和吸附物结合能，涉及各种含C═C键的分子，包括线性烯烃、环状烃和芳香烃，这些计算与TPD测量的实验观察结果相吻合\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。这种理论和实验的综合方法可用于预测氢化活性，并指导更高效Pt-3d催化系统的开发\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。Goda等人对3d/Pt(111)表面上的1-丁烯和1-己烯进行了DFT计算，发现与Pt(111)和3dPtPt(111)表面相比，亚表面Pt3dPt(111)结构与这些分子的结合力较弱，这与环己烯观察到的趋势相呼应\[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]。Murillo等人通过PtNiPt(111)上1-丁烯和1-己烯的TPD测量，证实了低温度下氢化反应的容易性，验证了这些预测\[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]。这些结果强调了DFT计算的表面d带中心和吸附物结合能作为具有良好氢化活性催化剂的描述符的潜力\[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]。类似的DFT计算已对3d/Pt(111)表面上的1,3-丁二烯、1,3-环己二烯、1,4-环己二烯和苯的结合能进行了执行\[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]。根据结合能和亚表面Pt3dPt(111)结构，支持的Pt3d催化剂预计对这些分子表现出高氢化活性，这一预测已在反应器研究中得到证实\[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: DFT计算与热脱附光谱(TPD)等实验技术的结合，在阐明Pt-3d催化剂的结构-活性关系方面发挥了关键作用。这种综合方法不仅有助于预测氢化活性，还指导了更高效催化系统的开发。未来的研究工作可能集中在探索新的双金属配置和优化亚表面结构上，以进一步提高催化性能。此外，开发先进的计算方法和改进实验技术可能为催化机制提供更深入的见解，并使设计具有特定氢化反应定制特性的催化剂成为可能。将机器学习算法与DFT计算相结合，可能通过以高精度预测广泛材料的氢化活性，加速新型催化剂的发现。
