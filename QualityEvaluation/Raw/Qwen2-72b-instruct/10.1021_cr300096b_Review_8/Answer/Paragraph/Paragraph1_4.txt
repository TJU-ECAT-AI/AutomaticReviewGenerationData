                    DFT calculations elucidate that the Pt-terminated surfaces of bimetallic Pt-3d catalysts exhibit weaker bonding with hydrogen and hydrocarbon adsorbates compared to monometallic Pt surfaces, leading to optimized binding energies that are accountable for higher hydrogenation rates \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. This observation aligns with experimental findings from TPD measurements, demonstrating that bimetallic PtNi and PtCo catalysts possess higher hydrogenation rates for benzene and 1,4-cyclohexadiene than the monometallic Pt catalyst \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. The enhancement in hydrogenation rates can be attributed to the presence of Pt-terminated configurations in the bimetallic catalysts \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. DFT calculations of surface d-band center and adsorbate binding energies for various C═C bond-containing molecules (including linear alkenes, cyclic hydrocarbons, and aromatics) on Pt-3d catalysts correlate with experimental observations from TPD measurements \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. This integrated theoretical and experimental approach can be utilized to predict hydrogenation activity and inform the design of more efficient Pt-3d catalytic systems \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\].

                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The integration of DFT calculations with experimental techniques such as TPD measurements has provided a robust framework for understanding the hydrogenation activity of Pt-3d catalysts. This approach has not only validated the predictive power of DFT calculations but also highlighted the importance of subsurface structures in influencing the binding energies of adsorbates. Future research endeavors might focus on expanding the scope of DFT calculations to include a wider range of C═C bond-containing molecules and exploring the effects of different subsurface structures on the catalytic activity. Additionally, the development of advanced experimental techniques that can provide more detailed insights into the catalytic processes at the atomic level could further enhance our understanding of the structure-activity relationships in Pt-3d catalysts. The integration of machine learning algorithms with DFT calculations could also offer a promising avenue for predicting the catalytic activity of novel Pt-3d catalysts, potentially accelerating the discovery of more efficient catalytic systems.

                    密度泛函理论(DFT)计算表明，双金属Pt-3d催化剂的Pt终止表面与氢和碳氢化合物吸附物的结合力较单金属Pt表面弱，导致优化的结合能，这可归因于更高的氢化速率\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。这一观察结果与热脱附光谱(TPD)测量的实验发现一致，表明PtNi和PtCo双金属催化剂对苯和1,4-环己二烯的氢化速率高于单金属Pt催化剂\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。氢化速率的提高可归因于双金属催化剂中Pt终止配置的存在\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。对于各种含C═C键的分子（包括线性烯烃、环状烃和芳香烃）在Pt-3d催化剂上的表面d带中心和吸附物结合能的DFT计算与TPD测量的实验观察结果相关联\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。这种理论与实验相结合的方法可用于预测氢化活性并指导更高效Pt-3d催化系统的开发\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: DFT计算与TPD测量等实验技术的结合为理解Pt-3d催化剂的氢化活性提供了一个强大的框架。这种方法不仅验证了DFT计算的预测能力，还强调了亚表面结构在影响吸附物结合能方面的重要性。未来的研究努力可能集中在扩大DFT计算的范围，包括更广泛的含C═C键的分子，并探索不同亚表面结构对催化活性的影响。此外，开发能提供原子水平催化过程更详细见解的先进实验技术，可以进一步增强我们对Pt-3d催化剂结构-活性关系的理解。将机器学习算法与DFT计算相结合，也可能为预测新型Pt-3d催化剂的催化活性提供一个有希望的途径，可能加速更高效催化系统的发现。
