                    DFT calculations elucidate that the Pt-terminated surfaces of bimetallic Pt-3d catalysts exhibit weaker bonding to hydrogen and hydrocarbon adsorbates compared to monometallic Pt surfaces, leading to optimized binding energies that are accountable for higher hydrogenation rates \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. This observation is corroborated by experimental findings from techniques such as TPD measurements, which have demonstrated that bimetallic PtNi and PtCo catalysts possess higher hydrogenation rates for benzene and 1,4-cyclohexadiene than the monometallic Pt catalyst \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. The enhancement in hydrogenation rates can be attributed to the presence of Pt-terminated configurations in the bimetallic catalysts \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. DFT calculations of surface d-band center and adsorbate binding energies for various C═C bond-containing molecules (including linear alkenes, cyclic hydrocarbons, and aromatics) on Pt-3d catalysts are in agreement with experimental observations from techniques such as TPD measurements \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. This integrated theoretical and experimental approach can be utilized to predict hydrogenation activity and inform the design of more efficient Pt-3d catalytic systems \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\].

                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in DFT calculations and their successful correlation with experimental observations from TPD measurements underscore the potential for an integrated theoretical and experimental approach in the design of efficient Pt-3d catalytic systems. Future research endeavors might focus on expanding the scope of DFT calculations to encompass a broader range of C═C bond-containing molecules and catalyst configurations, thereby enhancing the predictive power of these calculations. Additionally, the development of novel experimental techniques that can provide more detailed insights into the catalytic processes at the molecular level could further refine our understanding of the structure-activity relationships in Pt-3d catalysts. The integration of machine learning algorithms with DFT calculations and experimental data could also revolutionize the field by enabling the rapid screening of catalyst candidates and the prediction of their performance under various reaction conditions. This multidisciplinary approach holds the promise of accelerating the discovery of new and more efficient catalytic systems for hydrogenation reactions.

                    密度泛函理论(DFT)计算表明，双金属Pt-3d催化剂的Pt终止表面与氢和烃吸附物的结合力比单金属Pt表面弱，导致优化的结合能，这可归因于更高的氢化速率\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。这一观察结果得到了来自温度程序脱附(TPD)等技术的实验发现的支持，这些发现表明，双金属PtNi和PtCo催化剂对苯和1,4-环己二烯的氢化速率高于单金属Pt催化剂\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。氢化速率的提高可归因于双金属催化剂中Pt终止配置的存在\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。对于各种含C═C键的分子（包括线性烯烃、环状烃和芳香烃）在Pt-3d催化剂上的表面d带中心和吸附物结合能的DFT计算与来自TPD等技术的实验观察结果相一致\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。这种理论与实验相结合的方法可用于预测氢化活性并指导更高效Pt-3d催化系统的开发\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: DFT计算的进步及其与TPD测量的实验观察结果的成功相关性，凸显了理论与实验相结合的方法在设计高效Pt-3d催化系统方面的潜力。未来的研究努力可能集中在扩大DFT计算的范围，以涵盖更广泛的含C═C键的分子和催化剂配置，从而增强这些计算的预测能力。此外，开发能够提供更详细分子水平催化过程见解的新实验技术，可以进一步细化我们对Pt-3d催化剂结构-活性关系的理解。将机器学习算法与DFT计算和实验数据相结合，也可能彻底改变这一领域，通过使催化剂候选者的快速筛选和在不同反应条件下的性能预测成为可能。这种跨学科的方法有望加速新且更高效催化系统的发现，用于氢化反应。
