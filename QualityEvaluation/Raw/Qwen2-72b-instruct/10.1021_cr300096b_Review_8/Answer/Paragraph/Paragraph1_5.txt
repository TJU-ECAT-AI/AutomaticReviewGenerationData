                    DFT calculations elucidate that bimetallic Pt-3d catalysts, with Pt-terminated surfaces, exhibit weaker bonding to hydrogen and hydrocarbon adsorbates compared to monometallic Pt surfaces, leading to optimized binding energies that are accountable for enhanced hydrogenation rates \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. This observation is corroborated by experimental techniques such as TPD measurements, which have demonstrated that bimetallic PtNi and PtCo catalysts possess higher hydrogenation rates for benzene and 1,4-cyclohexadiene than the monometallic Pt catalyst \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. The enhancement in hydrogenation rates can be attributed to the presence of Pt-terminated catalyst configurations in the bimetallic catalysts \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. DFT calculations of surface d-band center and adsorbate binding energies for various C═C bond-containing molecules (including linear alkenes, cyclic hydrocarbons, and aromatics) on Pt-3d catalysts correlate with experimental observations from techniques such as TPD measurements \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. This integrated theoretical and experimental approach can be utilized to predict hydrogenation activity and inform the design of more efficient Pt-3d catalytic systems \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\].

                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The integration of DFT calculations and experimental validations, such as TPD measurements, has provided a robust framework for understanding the hydrogenation activity of Pt-3d catalysts. This approach has not only elucidated the structure-activity relationships but also facilitated the prediction of hydrogenation activity and the design of more efficient catalytic systems. Future research endeavors might focus on expanding the scope of DFT calculations to encompass a broader range of C═C bond-containing molecules and exploring the potential of other bimetallic catalysts. Additionally, the development of advanced experimental techniques that can provide more detailed insights into the catalytic processes at the molecular level could further enhance our understanding and guide the design of next-generation catalysts. The integration of machine learning algorithms with DFT calculations could also offer a promising avenue for predicting catalyst performance based on structural and electronic properties, potentially revolutionizing the field of catalysis.

                    密度泛函理论（DFT）计算表明，具有Pt终止表面的双金属Pt-3d催化剂对氢和碳氢化合物吸附物的结合力较单金属Pt表面弱，导致优化的结合能，这是增强氢化速率的原因\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。这一观察结果得到了实验技术如TPD测量的证实，这些测量显示，PtNi和PtCo双金属催化剂对苯和1,4-环己二烯的氢化速率高于单金属Pt催化剂\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。氢化速率的提高可归因于双金属催化剂中Pt终止催化剂配置的存在\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。Pt-3d催化剂上各种含C═C键分子（包括线性烯烃、环状烃和芳香烃）的表面d带中心和吸附物结合能的DFT计算与TPD测量等实验观察结果相关联\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。这种理论与实验相结合的方法可用于预测氢化活性并指导更高效Pt-3d催化系统的开发\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望：DFT计算与TPD测量等实验验证的结合，为理解Pt-3d催化剂的氢化活性提供了一个坚实的基础。这种方法不仅阐明了结构-活性关系，还促进了氢化活性的预测和更高效催化系统的开发。未来的研究工作可能侧重于扩大DFT计算的范围，以涵盖更广泛的含C═C键分子，并探索其他双金属催化剂的潜力。此外，开发能够提供催化过程分子层面更详细见解的先进实验技术，可以进一步加深我们的理解并指导下一代催化剂的设计。将机器学习算法与DFT计算相结合，也可能为根据结构和电子性质预测催化剂性能提供一个有希望的途径，这可能彻底改变催化领域。
