                    DFT calculations elucidate that bimetallic Pt-3d catalysts, with Pt-terminated surfaces, exhibit weaker bonding to hydrogen and hydrocarbon adsorbates compared to monometallic Pt surfaces, leading to optimized binding energies that correlate with higher hydrogenation rates \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. This observation is corroborated by experimental techniques such as TPD measurements, which have demonstrated that bimetallic PtNi and PtCo catalysts outperform monometallic Pt in hydrogenation rates for benzene and 1,4-cyclohexadiene \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. The enhancement in hydrogenation rates is attributed to the Pt-terminated configurations in bimetallic catalysts \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. DFT calculations of surface d-band center and adsorbate binding energies for various C═C bond-containing molecules on Pt-3d catalysts align with experimental observations from TPD measurements \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. This integrated theoretical and experimental approach can predict hydrogenation activity and guide the design of more efficient Pt-3d catalytic systems \[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]. Goda et al. performed DFT calculations for 1-butene and 1-hexene on 3d/Pt(111) surfaces, revealing that the subsurface Pt3dPt(111) structure binds these molecules more weakly than Pt(111) and 3dPtPt(111) surfaces, akin to the trend observed for cyclohexene \[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]. Murillo et al. validated these predictions through TPD measurements of low-temperature hydrogenation of 1-butene and 1-hexene on PtNiPt(111) \[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]. These findings suggest that DFT calculations of surface d-band center and adsorbate binding energy can serve as descriptors for catalysts with good hydrogenation activity \[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]. Similar calculations have been conducted for 1,3-butadiene, 1,3-cyclohexadiene, 1,4-cyclohexadiene, and benzene on 3d/Pt(111) surfaces \[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]. Based on binding energies and subsurface Pt3dPt(111) structures, supported Pt3d catalysts are predicted to exhibit high hydrogenation activity for these molecules, a prediction that has been validated in reactor studies \[10.1021/cr300096b_8\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in DFT calculations and their successful correlation with experimental observations from TPD measurements underscore the potential for an integrated theoretical and experimental approach in predicting hydrogenation activity and designing more efficient Pt-3d catalytic systems. Future research endeavors might focus on expanding the scope of DFT calculations to encompass a broader range of C═C bond-containing molecules and exploring the effects of different bimetallic compositions on catalyst performance. Additionally, the development of novel experimental techniques that can provide more detailed insights into the catalytic mechanisms at the molecular level could further enhance our understanding and predictive capabilities. The integration of machine learning algorithms with DFT calculations could also offer a promising avenue for accelerating the discovery of new catalysts with optimized properties. These advancements could significantly contribute to the development of more efficient and sustainable catalytic systems for hydrogenation reactions.

                    密度泛函理论（DFT）计算表明，双金属Pt-3d催化剂的Pt终止表面与氢和碳氢化合物吸附物的结合力较单金属Pt表面弱，导致优化的结合能与更高的氢化率相关联\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。这一观察结果得到了实验技术如温度程序脱附（TPD）测量的证实，这些测量显示双金属PtNi和PtCo催化剂在苯和1,4-环己二烯的氢化率上优于单金属Pt\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。氢化率的提高归因于双金属催化剂中的Pt终止配置\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。对于Pt-3d催化剂上各种含C═C键分子的表面d带中心和吸附物结合能的DFT计算与TPD测量的实验观察相一致\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。这种理论与实验的综合方法可以预测氢化活性并指导更高效Pt-3d催化系统的开发\[10.1039/c2gc16544a\]。Goda等人对3d/Pt(111)表面上的1-丁烯和1-己烯进行了DFT计算，发现与Pt(111)和3dPtPt(111)表面相比，亚表面Pt3dPt(111)结构与这些分子的结合力更弱，与环己烯观察到的趋势相似\[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]。Murillo等人通过在PtNiPt(111)上对1-丁烯和1-己烯的低温氢化的TPD测量验证了这些预测\[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]。这些发现表明，表面d带中心和吸附物结合能的DFT计算可以作为具有良好氢化活性催化剂的描述符\[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]。类似计算已对3d/Pt(111)表面上的1,3-丁二烯、1,3-环己二烯、1,4-环己二烯和苯进行\[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]。根据结合能和亚表面Pt3dPt(111)结构，支持的Pt3d催化剂被预测对这些分子表现出高氢化活性，这一预测已在反应器研究中得到验证\[10.1021/cr300096b_8\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望：DFT计算的进步及其与TPD测量的实验观察的成功相关性凸显了理论与实验综合方法在预测氢化活性和设计更高效Pt-3d催化系统方面的潜力。未来的研究努力可能集中在扩大DFT计算的范围，以涵盖更广泛的含C═C键分子，并探索不同双金属组成对催化剂性能的影响。此外，开发能够提供分子水平催化机制更详细见解的新实验技术可以进一步增强我们的理解和预测能力。将机器学习算法与DFT计算相结合，也可能为加速发现具有优化性质的新催化剂提供一个有希望的途径。这些进展可以为氢化反应开发更高效和可持续的催化系统做出重大贡献。
