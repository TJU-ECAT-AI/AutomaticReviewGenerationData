                The systematic study of B-based catalysts for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP), encompassing the multi-dimensional design of active sites and the analysis of surface and gas-phase reaction mechanisms, has significantly deepened our understanding of catalytic processes \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_5\]. This comprehensive analysis has elucidated the catalytic mechanisms on potential active sites of tricoordination B species with varying dimensional geometric configurations, thereby providing effective guidance for the development of more efficient ODHP catalysts in the future \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_5\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in the systematic study of B-based catalysts for ODHP have opened up new avenues for exploring the intricate relationships between catalyst structure, reaction mechanisms, and catalytic efficiency. Future research endeavors might focus on the integration of computational modeling with experimental techniques to predict and design catalysts with enhanced selectivity and activity. Moreover, the exploration of novel B-containing materials with tailored active sites could lead to breakthroughs in the catalytic conversion of propane to propylene. The development of in situ characterization methods to monitor the dynamic changes in catalyst structure during ODHP reactions could also provide insights into the real-time catalytic processes, guiding the optimization of reaction conditions for improved catalyst performance.
