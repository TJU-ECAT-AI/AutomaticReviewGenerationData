<EnglishVersionAnswer>

1.The literature does not provide information on how various support materials such as MCM-41, H-ZSM-5, ZrO2, and CeO2, influence the activity, selectivity, stability, and mechanistic pathways of chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions. It only discusses the effect of SiO2 and Al2O3 as supports. Over the CrOx/SiO2 catalyst, CO2 enhances the propene yield, whereas over the CrOx/Al2O3 catalyst, the yield and selectivity of propene in the presence of CO2 strongly decrease. The effect of steam on catalytic performance of the tested catalysts was also discussed. The promoting effect of CO2 on propene yield was observed on the CrOx/SiO2 catalyst, while on the CrOx/Al2O3 catalyst, carbon dioxide did not promote dehydrogenation. It seems that CO2 and/or the steam formed during the dehydrogenation with CO2 change the acidity/basicity of the CrOx/Al2O3 catalyst.

</EnglishVersionAnswer>
