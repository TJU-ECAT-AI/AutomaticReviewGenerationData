                    The performance, stability, and mechanistic behavior of chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation (CO2-ODH) reactions are significantly influenced by support materials and reaction conditions, as highlighted in reference \[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]. Chromium oxide catalysts supported on SiO2 and Al2O3 exhibit good performance in CO2-ODH reactions, with CO2 promoting Cr-O/SiO2 and negatively affecting Cr-O/Al2O3 \[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]. The synthesis gas composition can be controlled by adjusting the CO2:C3H8 ratio \[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]. CrOx/γ-Al2O3 and CrOx/ZrO2 show high activity, with CrOx/γ-Al2O3 favoring direct dehydrogenation of ethane and CrOx/ZrO2 promoting selective oxidative dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]. CrOx/ZrO2 faces rapid deactivation at high temperatures due to sintering or inactive chromium species formation \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]. CrOx/CeO2, while less active, is the most stable due to the formation of α-Cr2O3, which is inactive in direct dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]. Its stability is attributed to the removal of carbon deposits by CO2 due to the support's basic sites \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]. CO2 enhances the yield of propylene and suppresses deactivation when using a SiO2-supported Cr2O3 catalyst \[10.1246/cl.1996.973\]. CrOx catalysts show peak activity and selectivity on SiO2, MCM-41, and H-ZSM-5, yet inactive polychromates favor undesired products \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]. CrOx/γ-Al2O3 and CrOx/ZrO2 are most active but follow distinct pathways: the former uses DDE and RWGS, risking deactivation from coke, while the latter performs CO2-ODH in one step, deactivating through sintering or Cr speciation changes \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]. CrOx/CeO2 is less active but more stable due to coke consumption \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]. CO2 partial pressure boosts CrOx/SiO2's activity and C3H6 selectivity, whereas CrOx/γ-Al2O3's activity drops \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]. CrVI centers activate CO2, and an 8wt% Cr content maximizes CrVI:CrIII ratios and alkane conversion \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]. Excessive Cr content leads to agglomeration and inactive polychromates \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]. CrVI=O species rapidly dehydrogenate alkanes, but CrIII reoxidation lags, causing deactivation and coking \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The literature reveals that support materials and reaction conditions significantly influence the performance of chromium oxide catalysts in CO2-ODH reactions. SiO2 and Al2O3 supports have distinct effects on the activity and selectivity of chromium oxide catalysts, with CO2 playing a critical role in promoting or inhibiting the reaction \[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1, 10.1246/cl.1996.973\]. The stability and activity of CrOx catalysts are also influenced by the nature of the support, with CrOx/CeO2 showing the highest stability due to its ability to remove carbon deposits \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]. The control of Cr speciation and the prevention of sintering are key to maintaining high activity and selectivity \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]. Future research should focus on developing catalysts with enhanced stability and selectivity, optimizing the Cr speciation and distribution, and understanding the role of CO2 in the reaction mechanism. The exploration of novel support materials and the optimization of reaction conditions, such as CO2 partial pressure, could lead to the development of more efficient and stable chromium oxide catalysts for CO2-ODH reactions.

                    根据参考文献\[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]，CO2氧化脱氢反应(CO2-ODH)中，铬氧化物催化剂的性能、稳定性和机理行为受到支撑材料和反应条件的显著影响。SiO2和Al2O3支撑的铬氧化物催化剂在CO2-ODH反应中表现出良好的性能，CO2促进Cr-O/SiO2，而对Cr-O/Al2O3产生负面影响\[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]。通过调整CO2:C3H8比例可以控制合成气组成\[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]。CrOx/γ-Al2O3和CrOx/ZrO2表现出高活性，CrOx/γ-Al2O3有利于直接脱氢，CrOx/ZrO2促进选择性氧化脱氢\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]。CrOx/ZrO2在高温下因烧结或非活性铬物种的形成而迅速失活\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]。CrOx/CeO2虽然活性较低，但稳定性最高，由于α-Cr2O3的形成，它在直接脱氢中不活跃\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]。其稳定性归因于CO2由于支撑材料的碱性位点而去除碳沉积物\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]。使用SiO2支撑的Cr2O3催化剂时，CO2增强丙烯产量并抑制催化剂失活\[10.1246/cl.1996.973\]。CrOx催化剂在SiO2、MCM-41和H-ZSM-5上表现出峰值活性和选择性，但非活性多铬酸盐有利于不希望的产品\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。CrOx/γ-Al2O3和CrOx/ZrO2是最活跃的，但遵循不同的途径：前者使用DDE和RWGS，存在从焦炭失活的风险，而后者通过一步CO2-ODH，通过烧结或Cr物种变化失活\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。CrOx/CeO2活性较低，但稳定性更高，由于焦炭消耗\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。CO2分压增强CrOx/SiO2的活性和C3H6选择性，而CrOx/γ-Al2O3的活性下降\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。CrVI中心激活CO2，8wt% Cr含量最大化CrVI:CrIII比率和烷烃转化\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。过量的Cr含量导致聚结和非活性多铬酸盐的形成\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。CrVI=O物种迅速脱氢烷烃，但CrIII再氧化滞后，导致失活和焦化\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。

                    综合理解与未来展望: 文献显示，支撑材料和反应条件对CO2-ODH反应中铬氧化物催化剂的性能有重大影响。SiO2和Al2O3支撑对铬氧化物催化剂的活性和选择性有不同影响，CO2在促进或抑制反应中起关键作用\[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1, 10.1246/cl.1996.973\]。CrOx催化剂的稳定性和活性也受到支撑材料性质的影响，CrOx/CeO2由于其去除碳沉积物的能力而显示出最高的稳定性\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]。控制Cr物种和防止烧结是维持高活性和选择性的关键\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。未来的研究应集中在开发具有增强稳定性和选择性的催化剂，优化Cr物种和分布，以及理解CO2在反应机制中的作用。探索新型支撑材料和优化反应条件，如CO2分压，可能导致开发更有效和稳定的铬氧化物催化剂用于CO2-ODH反应。
