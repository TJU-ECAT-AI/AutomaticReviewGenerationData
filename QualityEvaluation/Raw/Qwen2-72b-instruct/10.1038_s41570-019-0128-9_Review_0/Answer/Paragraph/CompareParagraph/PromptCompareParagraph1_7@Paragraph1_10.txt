By adjusting the CO2:C3H8 ratio, the synthesis gas composition can be controlled, presenting a key challenge in achieving high conversion and selectivity while maintaining stability and minimizing side reactions \[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]. CrOx/γ-Al2O3 and CrOx/ZrO2 are highly active, with CrOx/γ-Al2O3 favoring direct dehydrogenation of ethane and CrOx/ZrO2 promoting selective oxidative dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]. CrOx/ZrO2 can deactivate rapidly at high temperatures due to sintering or the formation of inactive chromium species \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]. CrOx/CeO2, while less active, is the most stable due to the formation of α-Cr2O3, which is inactive in direct dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]. Its stability and low activity for ethylene formation contribute to the removal of carbon deposits by CO2 \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]. The side reactions for CrOx/CeO2 and CrOx/CexZr(1-x)O2 are similar to CrOx/ZrO2, involving non-selective oxidation or dry-reforming reactions \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\].

The performance, stability, and mechanistic behavior of chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation (CO2-ODH) reactions are significantly influenced by the nature of support materials and reaction conditions. Support materials, such as silica, alumina, and titania, play a crucial role in dispersing the chromium oxide, enhancing its surface area, and providing additional active sites, which can significantly affect the catalytic activity and selectivity. Moreover, the choice of support can influence the stability of the catalyst by preventing sintering and promoting thermal stability. Reaction conditions, including temperature, pressure, and gas composition, also have a profound impact on the catalytic performance. Higher temperatures generally enhance the reaction rate but can also lead to catalyst deactivation due to sintering or volatilization of active components. The presence of oxygen is essential for the oxidative dehydrogenation process, but its concentration must be carefully controlled to avoid over-oxidation of the desired products. Understanding the interplay between support materials, reaction conditions, and the mechanistic behavior of chromium oxide catalysts is crucial for optimizing CO2-ODH reactions and achieving high yields of valuable products.
