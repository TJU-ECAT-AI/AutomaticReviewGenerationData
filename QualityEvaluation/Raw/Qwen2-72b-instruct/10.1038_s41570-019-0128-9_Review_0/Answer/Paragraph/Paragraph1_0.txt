                    The performance, stability, and mechanistic behavior of chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation (CO2-ODH) reactions are significantly influenced by support materials and reaction conditions \[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]. Chromium oxide catalysts supported on SiO2 and Al2O3 exhibit good performance in CO2-ODH reactions, with CO2 having a promoting effect on Cr-O/SiO2 and a negative effect on Cr-O/Al2O3, impacting propene yield \[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]. The synthesis gas composition can be controlled by adjusting the CO2:C3H8 molar ratio in the feed \[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]. Key challenges include achieving high conversion and selectivity while maintaining stability and preventing side reactions \[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\].

                    CrOx/γ-Al2O3 and CrOx/ZrO2 show high activity in CO2-ODH reactions, with CrOx/γ-Al2O3 favoring direct dehydrogenation of ethane and CrOx/ZrO2 promoting selective oxidative dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]. CrOx/ZrO2 can deactivate rapidly at high temperatures due to sintering or inactive chromium species formation \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]. CrOx/CeO2, while less active, is the most stable due to the formation of α-Cr2O3, which is inactive in direct dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]. Its stability is attributed to low ethylene formation activity and CO2 removal of carbon deposits \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]. The side reactions for CrOx/CeO2 and CrOx/CexZr(1-x)O2 are similar to CrOx/ZrO2, involving non-selective oxidation or dry-reforming reactions \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\].

                    In CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions, CO2 promotes the activity of SiO2-supported Cr2O3 catalysts, enhancing propylene yield and suppressing deactivation \[10.1246/cl.1996.973\]. This effect is specific to SiO2-supported catalysts, as observed in propane dehydrogenation \[10.1246/cl.1996.973\]. No data is available on other support materials or CO2 partial pressure effects \[10.1246/cl.1996.973\].

                    Chromium oxide catalysts on SiO2, MCM-41, and H-ZSM-5 show high activity and selectivity, though excess Cr forms inactive polychromates \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]. CrOx/γ-Al2O3 and CrOx/ZrO2 are most active but operate differently, with the former prone to deactivation by coke and the latter by sintering \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]. CrOx/CeO2 is less active but more stable due to coke consumption \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]. CO2 partial pressure boosts CrOx/SiO2's activity and C3H6 selectivity, whereas CrOx/γ-Al2O3's activity declines \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]. CrVI centers in MCM-41 activate CO2, and an 8wt% Cr content maximizes CrVI:CrIII ratios, enhancing alkane conversion \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]. Excessive Cr content leads to agglomeration and inactive polychromates \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]. CrVI=O species rapidly dehydrogenate alkanes, but CrIII reoxidation is slower, causing deactivation and coking in highly dispersed CrOx catalysts \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\].

                    In CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions, CrOx/SiO2 catalysts show enhanced propene yield with CO2, whereas CrOx/Al2O3 catalysts exhibit decreased yield and selectivity \[10.1007/s10562-004-3749-6\]. Steam's effect on catalytic performance was noted, with CO2 promoting dehydrogenation over SiO2 but not over Al2O3, suggesting changes in acidity/basicity of the Al2O3-supported catalyst \[10.1007/s10562-004-3749-6\].

                    In CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions, Cr/SiO2 catalyst activity increases with higher CO2 partial pressure, whereas Cr/Al2O3 activity decreases with even a small amount of CO2, due to CO2 adsorption inhibiting C3H8 adsorption \[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.10.028\]. The redox cycle between Cr(III)O6 and Cr(VI)O4 is crucial for dehydrogenation over both supports \[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.10.028\]. CO2 can oxidize Cr(III) to Cr(VI) species, enabling oxidative dehydrogenation of propane over Cr/SiO2 in addition to simple dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.10.028\].

                    Support materials significantly impact CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions by providing high surface area, immobilization, and porosity, which aid in overcoming diffusion issues and maintaining highly dispersed active sites \[10.1039/c2ee22409g\]. Activated carbon-supported catalysts exhibit the highest conversion due to their high surface area and dispersion of metal oxide \[10.1039/c2ee22409g\]. Oxidized diamond-supported metal oxides show varied catalytic activities despite similar surface areas \[10.1039/c2ee22409g\]. The support's acidic or basic properties modify the active site's behavior, influencing the valence electron distribution within the metal particle \[10.1039/c2ee22409g\]. Supported Cr2O3 catalysts have better performance due to lower reoxidation rates compared to pure chromia, with higher Cr6+O bond energy in supported catalysts \[10.1039/c2ee22409g\]. In supported chromium systems, CrOx units are isolated, limiting oxygen transfer and preventing reoxidation, thus enhancing catalytic performance \[10.1039/c2ee22409g\]. The phase and dispersion of the metal oxide, support type, support nature, changes in MOM bond strengths, and oxygen transfer ease are critical factors affecting redox catalysts' performance \[10.1039/c2ee22409g\]. Key challenges include maintaining high surface area, dispersion, and preventing reoxidation while maximizing activity and selectivity \[10.1039/c2ee22409g\].

                    在CO2氧化脱氢(CO2-ODH)反应中，支撑材料和反应条件对铬氧化物催化剂的性能、稳定性和机制行为有显著影响\[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]。在SiO2和Al2O3支撑的铬氧化物催化剂在CO2-ODH反应中表现出良好的性能，CO2对Cr-O/SiO2有促进作用，而对Cr-O/Al2O3有负面影响，影响丙烯产量\[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]。通过调整进料中CO2:C3H8的摩尔比可以控制合成气的组成\[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]。主要挑战包括在保持稳定性和防止副反应的同时实现高转化率和选择性\[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]。

                    CrOx/γ-Al2O3和CrOx/ZrO2在CO2-ODH反应中显示出高活性，CrOx/γ-Al2O3有利于直接脱氢，而CrOx/ZrO2促进选择性氧化脱氢\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]。CrOx/ZrO2在高温下可能迅速失活，由于烧结或非活性铬物种的形成\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]。CrOx/CeO2虽然活性较低，但最稳定，由于形成α-Cr2O3，它在直接脱氢中不活跃\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]。其稳定性归因于低乙烯形成活性和CO2去除碳沉积物\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]。CrOx/CeO2和CrOx/CexZr(1-x)O2的副反应与CrOx/ZrO2相似，涉及非选择性氧化或干重整反应\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]。

                    在CO2氧化脱氢反应中，CO2促进了SiO2支撑的Cr2O3催化剂的活性，提高了丙烯产量，抑制了失活\[10.1246/cl.1996.973\]。这种效果在SiO2支撑的催化剂中是特定的，如在丙烷脱氢中观察到\[10.1246/cl.1996.973\]。没有关于其他支撑材料或CO2分压效应的数据\[10.1246/cl.1996.973\]。

                    在SiO2、MCM-41和H-ZSM-5上的铬氧化物催化剂显示出高活性和选择性，尽管过量的Cr形成非活性多铬酸盐\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。CrOx/γ-Al2O3和CrOx/ZrO2是最活跃的，但操作方式不同，前者容易因焦炭而失活，后者因烧结而失活\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。CrOx/CeO2活性较低，但稳定性较高，由于焦炭消耗\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。CO2分压提高了CrOx/SiO2的活性和C3H6选择性，而CrOx/γ-Al2O3的活性下降\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。MCM-41中的CrVI中心激活CO2，8wt%的Cr含量最大化CrVI:CrIII比率，增强烷烃转化\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。过量的Cr含量导致聚结和非活性多铬酸盐的形成\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。CrVI=O物种迅速脱氢烷烃，但CrIII再氧化较慢，导致高度分散的CrOx催化剂失活和焦化\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。

                    在CO2氧化脱氢反应中，CrOx/SiO2催化剂在CO2存在下显示出增强的丙烯产量，而CrOx/Al2O3催化剂显示出产量和选择性的下降\[10.1007/s10562-004-3749-6\]。蒸汽对催化性能的影响被注意到，CO2促进SiO2上的脱氢，但不促进Al2O3上的脱氢，表明Al2O3支撑催化剂的酸碱性发生变化\[10.1007/s10562-004-3749-6\]。

                    在CO2氧化脱氢反应中，Cr/SiO2催化剂活性随着CO2分压的增加而增加，而Cr/Al2O3催化剂活性即使在少量CO2下也会下降，由于CO2吸附抑制C3H8吸附\[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.10.028\]。Cr(III)O6和Cr(VI)O4之间的氧化还原循环对于两个支撑上的脱氢至关重要\[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.10.028\]。CO2可以将Cr(III)氧化为Cr(VI)物种，使Cr/SiO2上的丙烷氧化脱氢成为可能，除了简单的脱氢\[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.10.028\]。

                    支撑材料对CO2氧化脱氢反应有重大影响，通过提供高表面积、固定化和孔隙度，有助于克服扩散问题，保持高度分散的活性位点\[10.1039/c2ee22409g\]。活性炭支撑的催化剂由于其高表面积和金属氧化物的分散而表现出最高的转化率\[10.1039/c2ee22409g\]。氧化金刚石支撑的金属氧化物尽管表面积相似，但显示出不同的催化活性\[10.1039/c2ee22409g\]。支撑的酸性或碱性性质修改活性位点的行为，影响金属颗粒内的价电子分布\[10.1039/c2ee22409g\]。支撑的Cr2O3催化剂由于与纯铬酸盐相比具有较低的再氧化率和支撑催化剂中较高的Cr6+O键能而具有更好的性能\[10.1039/c2ee22409g\]。在支撑的铬系统中，CrOx单元被隔离，限制了氧的转移，防止了再氧化，从而增强了催化性能\[10.1039/c2ee22409g\]。金属氧化物的相和分散度、支撑类型、支撑性质、MOM键强度的变化和氧转移的容易性是影响氧化还原催化剂性能的关键因素\[10.1039/c2ee22409g\]。主要挑战包括在最大化活性和选择性的同时保持高表面积、分散度和防止再氧化\[10.1039/c2ee22409g\]。
