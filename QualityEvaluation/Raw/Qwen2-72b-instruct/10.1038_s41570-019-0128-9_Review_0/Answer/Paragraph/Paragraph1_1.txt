                    The influence of support materials and reaction conditions on the performance, stability, and mechanistic behavior of chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation (CO2-ODH) reactions is a critical area of investigation \[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]. Chromium oxide catalysts supported on SiO2 and Al2O3 exhibit good performance in CO2-ODH reactions, with CO2 having a promoting effect on Cr-O/SiO2 and a negative effect on Cr-O/Al2O3 for propene yield \[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]. The synthesis gas composition can be controlled by adjusting the CO2:C3H8 molar ratio \[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]. Key challenges include achieving high conversion and selectivity while maintaining stability and preventing side reactions \[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]. CrOx/γ-Al2O3 and CrOx/ZrO2 show high activity, with CrOx/γ-Al2O3 favoring direct dehydrogenation and CrOx/ZrO2 selective oxidative dehydrogenation \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]. CrOx/ZrO2 deactivates rapidly at high temperatures due to sintering or inactive chromium species formation \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]. CrOx/CeO2, while less active, is the most stable due to the formation of α-Cr2O3, with selective oxidative dehydrogenation being limited by surface re-oxidation difficulties \[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]. CO2 enhances the yield of propylene and suppresses deactivation of the Cr2O3/SiO2 catalyst \[10.1246/cl.1996.973\]. CrOx/SiO2's activity and C3H6 selectivity increase with CO2 partial pressure, unlike CrOx/γ-Al2O3, which decreases \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]. CO2 adsorption on γ-Al2O3 competes with C3H8 adsorption and affects CrOx/γ-Al2O3's acid-base properties \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]. CrVI centers in MCM-41 activate CO2, and an 8wt% Cr content maximizes CrVI:CrIII ratios and alkane conversion \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]. Excessive Cr content leads to agglomeration into α-Cr2O3 and increased inactive polychromate species \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]. CrVI=O species rapidly dehydrogenate alkanes, but CrIII reoxidation is slower, causing deactivation and coking in highly dispersed CrOx catalysts \[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\].

Additionally, the optimization of reaction conditions, such as CO2 partial pressure, could lead to more efficient and selective CO2-ODH processes.                     支撑材料和反应条件对二氧化碳氧化脱氢（CO2-ODH）反应中铬氧化物催化剂的性能、稳定性和机理行为的影响是研究的关键领域\[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]。在SiO2和Al2O3支撑的铬氧化物催化剂在CO2-ODH反应中表现出良好的性能，CO2对Cr-O/SiO2有促进作用，而对Cr-O/Al2O3的丙烯产量有负面影响\[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]。通过调整CO2:C3H8的摩尔比可以控制合成气的组成\[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]。主要挑战包括在保持稳定性和防止副反应的同时实现高转化率和选择性\[10.2478/v10026-012-0106-1\]。CrOx/γ-Al2O3和CrOx/ZrO2表现出高活性，CrOx/γ-Al2O3倾向于直接脱氢，而CrOx/ZrO2促进选择性氧化脱氢\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]。CrOx/ZrO2在高温下迅速失活，原因是烧结或形成不活跃的铬物种\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]。CrOx/CeO2虽然活性较低，但稳定性最高，由于形成α-Cr2O3，选择性氧化脱氢受到表面再氧化困难的限制\[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.04.047\]。CO2增强了Cr2O3/SiO2催化剂的丙烯产量并抑制了失活\[10.1246/cl.1996.973\]。CrOx/SiO2的活性和C3H6选择性随着CO2分压的增加而增加，与CrOx/γ-Al2O3的活性下降形成对比\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。CO2在γ-Al2O3上的吸附与C3H8的吸附竞争，并影响CrOx/γ-Al2O3的酸碱性质\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。MCM-41中的CrVI中心激活CO2，8wt%的Cr含量最大化CrVI:CrIII的比例和烷烃转化\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。过量的Cr含量导致α-Cr2O3的聚集和不活跃的多铬酸盐物种的增加\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。CrVI=O物种迅速脱氢烷烃，但CrIII的再氧化较慢，导致高度分散的CrOx催化剂的失活和焦炭形成\[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_0\]。
