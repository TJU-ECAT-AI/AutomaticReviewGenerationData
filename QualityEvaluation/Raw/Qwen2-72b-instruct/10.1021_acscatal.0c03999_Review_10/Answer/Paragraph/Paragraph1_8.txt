                The potential and challenges of sulfur-based alternative oxidants in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) for propylene production have been elucidated through a critical examination of their effectiveness, associated problems, and future directions for catalyst design optimization \[10.1016/S0167-2991(08)60185-8\]. Sulfur-based oxidants, including H2S, S2, and SO2, have demonstrated the ability to enhance propylene yield in comparison to conventional oxidants. The effectiveness of SO2 in the ODHP process is evidenced by the formation of oxidative condensation products (OCP) on the catalyst surface, which selectively accelerate the interaction of propane with SO2 \[10.1016/S0167-2991(08)60185-8\]. However, the use of SO2 results in low propylene yield due to the formation of COx products over bulk metal oxide catalysts \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]. The use of a soft oxidant like S2 can mitigate overoxidation of propane over ZrO2 \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]. A significant challenge in designing efficient catalysts for S2ODHP is the formation of byproducts such as CS2 \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]. The role of sulfur as a promoter or inhibitor is well established in hydrogenation reactions, and sulfur compounds like S2 and H2S have been used as cocatalysts to reduce overoxidation during oxidative dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]. Shorter residence times are required to suppress the cracking reaction, and sulfur in the diatomic state may abstract hydrogen from an alkane \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]. The presence of S2 improves C3H6 yield and selectivity, but the conversion is barely affected by the catalysts, indicating the dominance of gas-phase reactions \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]. Sulfur-based byproducts like CS2 are observed in the reactor effluent \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]. The apparent activation barrier for propylene formation decreases with lower metal-sulfur bond strength, indicating a more active sulfur species \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]. CH activation proceeds by a surface sulfur species, and propylene and H2S formation leads to sulfur vacancy, which is replenished by gas phase S2 \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The exploration of sulfur-based oxidants in the ODHP process has revealed promising avenues for enhancing propylene yield. The use of SO2, despite its limitations, has shown potential in forming OCP that selectively accelerate propane interaction. The introduction of soft oxidants like S2 has been beneficial in reducing overoxidation. However, the formation of byproducts such as CS2 presents a significant challenge in catalyst design. Future research should focus on developing a structure-activity relationship to rationally design catalysts that can optimize the overall process. The role of sulfur compounds as cocatalysts in reducing overoxidation during oxidative dehydrogenation reactions should be further explored. Shorter residence times and the use of sulfur in the diatomic state may offer strategies to suppress cracking reactions. The observation of sulfur-based byproducts in the reactor effluent suggests the need for catalysts that can minimize their formation. The decrease in the activation barrier for propylene formation with lower metal-sulfur bond strength indicates the potential for more active sulfur species. The replenishment of sulfur vacancies by gas phase S2 offers a dynamic aspect to the reaction mechanism. Future directions should aim to optimize catalyst design, investigate the role of different sulfur-based oxidants, and develop strategies to minimize byproduct formation. The development of catalysts that can efficiently utilize sulfur-based oxidants while minimizing byproduct formation could lead to significant advancements in the ODHP process for propylene production.

                通过深入分析，硫基替代氧化剂在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）制丙烯过程中的潜力和挑战得到了揭示，重点考察了它们的有效性、相关问题以及催化剂设计优化的未来方向\[10.1016/S0167-2991(08)60185-8\]。硫基氧化剂，包括H2S、S2和SO2，已证明能够提高丙烯产量，与传统氧化剂相比具有优势。SO2在ODHP过程中的有效性通过催化剂表面形成氧化缩合产物（OCP）得以体现，这些产物选择性地加速了丙烷与SO2的相互作用\[10.1016/S0167-2991(08)60185-8\]。然而，使用SO2会导致丙烯产量较低，原因是大量金属氧化物催化剂上形成COx产物\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]。使用软氧化剂如S2可以减少ZrO2上丙烷的过度氧化\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]。设计高效S2ODHP催化剂的一个重大挑战是CS2等副产品的形成\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]。硫作为促进剂或抑制剂在氢化反应中的作用已被广泛研究，硫化合物如S2和H2S已被用作共催化剂，以减少氧化脱氢反应中的过度氧化\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]。需要更短的停留时间来抑制裂解反应，而二原子态的硫可能从烷烃中抽提氢原子\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]。S2的存在提高了C3H6的产量和选择性，但催化剂几乎不影响转化率，这表明气相反应占主导\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]。在反应器流出物中观察到CS2等硫基副产品\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]。丙烯形成的表观活化能随着金属硫键强度的降低而降低，表明硫物种更加活跃\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]。CH活化通过表面硫物种进行，丙烯和H2S的形成导致硫空位，而气相S2补充了这些硫空位\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]。

                综合理解与未来展望：硫基氧化剂在ODHP过程中的探索揭示了提高丙烯产量的有希望的途径。尽管存在局限性，SO2的使用在形成选择性加速丙烷相互作用的OCP方面显示出潜力。引入软氧化剂如S2在减少过度氧化方面有益。然而，CS2等副产品的形成对催化剂设计构成了重大挑战。未来的研究应侧重于开发结构-活性关系，以理性设计能够优化整个过程的催化剂。硫化合物作为共催化剂在减少氧化脱氢反应中过度氧化的作用应进一步探索。更短的停留时间和二原子态硫的使用可能提供抑制裂解反应的策略。在反应器流出物中观察到硫基副产品表明需要开发能够最小化其形成的催化剂。金属硫键强度降低时丙烯形成活化能的降低表明了更活跃的硫物种的潜力。气相S2补充硫空位为反应机制提供了动态方面。未来方向应旨在优化催化剂设计，研究不同硫基氧化剂的作用，并开发最小化副产品形成的策略。开发能够有效利用硫基氧化剂同时最小化副产品形成的催化剂，可能在丙烯生产中的ODHP过程方面取得重大进展。
