                The potential and challenges of sulfur-based alternative oxidants in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) for propylene production have been elucidated in recent studies \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]. Sulfur-based oxidants, such as H2S, S2, and SO2, have demonstrated the ability to enhance propylene yield compared to conventional oxidants. SO2, in particular, has been shown to be effective in the selective oxidation of propane to propylene, with the formation of oxidative condensation products (OCP) on the catalyst surface accelerating the interaction of propane with SO2 \[10.1016/S0167-2991(08)60185-8\]. However, the use of SO2 as an oxidant results in low propylene yield due to the formation of COx products over bulk metal oxide catalysts. The use of a soft oxidant like S2 can reduce the overoxidation of propane over ZrO2 \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]. A major challenge in designing efficient catalysts for S2ODHP is the formation of byproducts such as CS2. Future research should focus on developing a structure-activity relationship to rationally design catalysts in this field \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]. The role of sulfur as a promoter or inhibitor is well studied in hydrogenation reactions, and sulfur compounds like S2 and H2S have been used as cocatalysts to reduce overoxidation during oxidative dehydrogenation reactions. Shorter residence times are required to suppress the cracking reaction, and sulfur in the diatomic state may abstract hydrogen from an alkane \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]. The presence of S2 improves C3H6 yield and selectivity, but the conversion is barely affected by the catalysts, indicating the dominance of gas-phase reactions \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]. Sulfur-based byproducts like CS2 are observed in the reactor effluent. The apparent activation barrier for propylene formation decreases with lower metal-sulfur bond strength, indicating a more active sulfur species. CH activation proceeds by a surface sulfur species, and propylene and H2S formation leads to sulfur vacancy, which is replenished by gas phase S2 \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The use of sulfur-based oxidants in ODHP presents a promising avenue for improving propylene yield. However, the challenges associated with each oxidant, such as byproduct formation and overoxidation, must be addressed. Future research should focus on developing catalysts that can optimize the overall process, particularly in the context of S2ODHP, where the formation of byproducts like CS2 poses a significant challenge. Developing a structure-activity relationship will be crucial for rational catalyst design in this field. Moreover, the role of sulfur in promoting or inhibiting reactions during ODHP should be further explored, as sulfur compounds like S2 and H2S have shown potential in reducing overoxidation. Shorter residence times and the use of sulfur in the diatomic state may also contribute to suppressing the cracking reaction and improving C3H6 yield and selectivity. The observation of sulfur-based byproducts in the reactor effluent suggests the need for catalysts that can minimize their formation. The decrease in the apparent activation barrier for propylene formation with lower metal-sulfur bond strength indicates the potential for more active sulfur species, which could be harnessed for enhancing propylene yield. CH activation by a surface sulfur species and the replenishment of sulfur vacancies by gas phase S2 highlight the dynamic nature of sulfur in ODHP. Future research efforts should aim to optimize these processes for more efficient propylene production.

                硫基替代氧化剂在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）制丙烯生产中的潜力和挑战已在最近的研究中得到阐明\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]。硫基氧化剂，如H2S、S2和SO2，已显示出与传统氧化剂相比提高丙烯产量的能力。特别是，SO2已被证明在丙烷选择性氧化为丙烯中有效，通过在催化剂表面形成氧化缩合产物（OCP）加速丙烷与SO2的相互作用\[10.1016/S0167-2991(08)60185-8\]。然而，使用SO2作为氧化剂导致丙烯产量低，因为在其上形成COx产物。使用像S2这样的软氧化剂可以减少ZrO2上的丙烷过度氧化\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]。设计高效S2ODHP催化剂的主要挑战是形成如CS2的副产品。未来的研究应集中在开发结构-活性关系，以理性设计该领域的催化剂\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]。硫作为促进剂或抑制剂的作用在氢化反应中得到了广泛研究，硫化合物如S2和H2S已被用作减少氧化脱氢反应中过度氧化的助催化剂。需要更短的停留时间来抑制裂解反应，而二原子状态的硫可能从烷烃中提取氢\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]。S2的存在提高了C3H6的产量和选择性，但催化剂几乎不影响转化率，表明气相反应的主导地位\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]。观察到如CS2的硫基副产品在反应器流出物中。丙烯形成的表观活化能随着金属-硫键强度的降低而降低，表明更活跃的硫物种。CH活化通过表面硫物种进行，丙烯和H2S的形成导致硫空位，这由气相S2补充\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望：在ODHP中使用硫基氧化剂为提高丙烯产量提供了一条有希望的途径。然而，必须解决与每个氧化剂相关的挑战，如副产品形成和过度氧化。未来的研究应集中在开发可以优化整个过程的催化剂，特别是在S2ODHP的背景下，形成如CS2的副产品构成重大挑战。在该领域开发结构-活性关系对于理性催化剂设计至关重要。此外，应进一步探索硫在ODHP期间促进或抑制反应的作用，因为硫化合物如S2和H2S在减少过度氧化方面显示出潜力。更短的停留时间和使用二原子状态的硫也可能有助于抑制裂解反应并提高C3H6的产量和选择性。在反应器流出物中观察到硫基副产品表明需要能够最小化其形成的催化剂。随着金属-硫键强度降低，丙烯形成表观活化能的降低表明了更活跃的硫物种的潜力，这可以用于增强丙烯产量。通过表面硫物种进行的CH活化和通过气相S2补充硫空位突出了硫在ODHP中的动态性质。未来的研究工作应旨在优化这些过程，以实现更有效的丙烯生产。
