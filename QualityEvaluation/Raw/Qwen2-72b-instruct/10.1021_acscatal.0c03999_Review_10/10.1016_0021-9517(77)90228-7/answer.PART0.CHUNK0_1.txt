1.The literature does not provide a comparison of different sulfur-based oxidants such as H2S, S2, and SO2 with conventional oxidants in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) to propylene. It specifically discusses the use of SO2 in the dehydrogenation of propane over a Pd/Al2O3 catalyst. The addition of SO2 to the feed increases the conversion of propane, however, the selectivities to propylene are lower than those obtained with pure propane feed. The reduced Pd/Al2O3 catalyst shows moderate activity and high selectivity to propylene at high temperatures. Sulfiding the Pd/Al2O3 catalyst did not improve activity or selectivity. The literature does not discuss the challenges associated with each oxidant or the key areas for future research to improve catalyst design and optimize the overall process.
