1.The document does not provide a comparison of different sulfur-based oxidants (such as H2S, S2, and SO2) with conventional oxidants in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) to propylene. It focuses on the use of SO2 in the dehydrogenation of propane over a Pd/A1203 catalyst. The addition of SO2 to the feed increases the conversion of propane, but the selectivities to propylene are lower than those obtained with pure propane feed. The reduced Pd/A1203 catalyst shows moderate activity and high selectivity to propylene at high temperatures, while the sulfided Pd/A1203 catalyst shows poor activity although its selectivity is fairly high. Sulfiding Pd/A1203 did not improve activity or selectivity over the reduced catalyst. The oxidative dehydrogenation using SO2 offers an alternative to overcome the equilibrium limitation of the reaction, and Pd/A1203 offers some hope in this respect. However, the relatively low selectivity of Pd/A1203 to propylene could result from propylene being oxidized by SO2 to water and carbon dioxide.
