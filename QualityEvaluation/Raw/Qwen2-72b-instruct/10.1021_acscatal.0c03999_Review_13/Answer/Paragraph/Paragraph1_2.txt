        <English> 
                Comparative analysis of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling, as explored by Zhu et al. \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13\], reveals a lower thermodynamic driving force for overoxidation compared to traditional oxygen-based systems. The thermodynamic well for sulfur oxidation to CS2 is considerably higher in energy at 3.45 eV, contrasting with 13.41 eV for O2 oxidation of methane to CO2. This indicates a potential for improved selectivity towards alkene products. An ethylene selectivity of up to 18% can be achieved on a PdS catalyst at 1050 C, albeit high temperatures are required due to the thermodynamics of the reaction and the relatively high CH activation barriers on sulfide catalysts. These barriers, ranging from 1.91 eV on MoS2 to 2.68 eV for TiS2, highlight the need for catalysts with enhanced hydrogen abstraction capabilities. The performance of the current catalyst is relatively modest and far from practical consideration, yet future studies can identify catalysts with higher conversion rates by descriptor-based theoretical screening. The MS bond energy is observed to be a good descriptor for both CH activation and CC coupling reaction barriers.
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The exploration of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling presents a promising avenue for improving alkene selectivity and reducing overoxidation. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of novel sulfide catalysts with optimized MS bond energies, aiming to lower the CH activation barriers and enhance hydrogen abstraction capabilities. Descriptor-based theoretical screening could play a pivotal role in identifying catalysts with higher conversion rates and improved selectivity. Moreover, the investigation of reaction mechanisms at the molecular level could provide insights into the design of more efficient catalysts. The potential for sulfur-based systems to offer a lower thermodynamic driving force for overoxidation suggests a fertile ground for innovation in alkane conversion processes.

                硫作为甲烷氧化偶联中替代氧化剂的比较分析，由Zhu等人探索 \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13\]，揭示了与传统氧基系统相比，过氧化的热力学驱动力较低。硫氧化至CS2的热力学井的能量显著较高，为3.45 eV，而氧气氧化甲烷至CO2的能量为13.41 eV。这表明了提高烯烃产品选择性的潜力。在1050 C下，PdS催化剂上可实现高达18%的乙烯选择性，尽管需要高温，因为反应的热力学和硫化物催化剂上相对较高的CH活化能垒。这些能垒，从MoS2上的1.91 eV到TiS2上的2.68 eV，突显了对增强氢抽象能力的催化剂的需求。当前催化剂的性能相对温和，远未达到实用考虑，但未来的研究可以通过基于描述符的理论筛选来识别具有更高转化率的催化剂。MS键能被观察到是CH活化和CC偶联反应能垒的良好描述符。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: 硫作为甲烷氧化偶联中替代氧化剂的探索为提高烯烃选择性和减少过氧化提供了一条有希望的途径。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发具有优化MS键能的新型硫化物催化剂上，旨在降低CH活化能垒，增强氢抽象能力。基于描述符的理论筛选可能在识别具有更高转化率和改进选择性的催化剂方面发挥关键作用。此外，对分子水平的反应机制的调查可能为设计更有效的催化剂提供见解。硫基系统可能提供较低的过氧化热力学驱动力的潜力表明，在烷烃转化过程中创新的肥沃土壤。
