                The exploration of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling, as detailed by Zhu et al. \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13\], presents a less active oxidant compared to traditional oxygen-based systems. The thermodynamic well for sulfur oxidation to CS2 is notably higher in energy at 3.45 eV, contrasting with 13.41 eV for O2 oxidation of methane to CO2. This discrepancy indicates a lower thermodynamic driving force for overoxidation when utilizing sulfur. An ethylene selectivity of up to 18% can be achieved on a PdS catalyst at 1050 C. However, the process necessitates high temperatures due to the thermodynamics of the reaction and the relatively high CH activation barriers on sulfide catalysts, which are inferior hydrogen abstractors compared to metal oxides. These barriers span from 1.91 eV on MoS2 to 2.68 eV for TiS2. The current performance of this catalyst is relatively modest and far from practical consideration, yet future studies can identify catalysts with higher conversion rates through descriptor-based theoretical screening. The MS bond energy is observed to be a good descriptor for both CH activation and CC coupling reaction barriers.
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The insights gained from the study by Zhu et al. \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13\] on sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling offer a promising direction for future research. The lower thermodynamic driving force for overoxidation with sulfur compared to oxygen suggests a potential for improved selectivity in alkene production. The identification of the MS bond energy as a descriptor for CH activation and CC coupling reaction barriers opens avenues for the design of more efficient catalysts. Future research might focus on the development of novel sulfide catalysts with optimized MS bond energies, aiming to lower the CH activation barriers while maintaining high alkene selectivity. Additionally, the exploration of sulfur-based systems could benefit from advancements in computational chemistry, enabling the prediction and design of catalysts with enhanced performance.

                综合理解与未来展望: Zhu等人\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13\]关于硫作为甲烷氧化偶联中替代氧化剂的研究见解为未来的研究提供了一个有希望的方向。与氧气相比，硫的过氧化热力学驱动力较低，这表明在烯烃生产中可能有改进的选择性。MS键能作为CH活化和CC偶联反应能垒的描述符的识别，为设计更有效的催化剂开辟了途径。未来的研究可能集中在开发具有优化MS键能的新型硫化物催化剂上，旨在降低CH活化能垒，同时保持高烯烃选择性。此外，基于硫的系统的研究可以从计算化学的进步中获益，使预测和设计性能增强的催化剂成为可能。
