        <English> 
                The exploration of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling, as detailed by Zhu et al. \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13\], presents a less active oxidant compared to traditional oxygen-based systems. The thermodynamic well for sulfur oxidation to CS2 is notably higher in energy at 3.45 eV, contrasting with 13.41 eV for O2 oxidation of methane to CO2. This discrepancy suggests a lower thermodynamic driving force for overoxidation when employing sulfur. An ethylene selectivity of up to 18% can be attained on a PdS catalyst at 1050 C. However, the process necessitates high temperatures due to the thermodynamics of the reaction and the relatively high CH activation barriers on sulfide catalysts, which are inferior hydrogen abstractors compared to metal oxides. These barriers span from 1.91 eV on MoS2 to 2.68 eV for TiS2. The current catalyst performance is relatively modest and distant from practical application, yet future studies might identify catalysts with enhanced conversion rates through descriptor-based theoretical screening. The MS bond energy is identified as a promising descriptor for both CH activation and CC coupling reaction barriers.
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The investigation into sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling, while currently in its nascent stages, holds potential for innovation in catalytic performance and selectivity. Future research should focus on developing catalysts that can lower the activation barriers for CH bond activation, particularly on sulfide catalysts, to improve hydrogen abstraction efficiency. Descriptor-based theoretical screening could be a pivotal tool in identifying novel catalysts with higher conversion rates and selectivity. Moreover, the exploration of MS bond energy as a descriptor for CH activation and CC coupling reaction barriers opens avenues for the design of more efficient catalysts. The optimization of reaction conditions, such as temperature and pressure, to mitigate the high energy requirements for sulfur-based oxidation is also a critical area for future study. The development of sulfur-based oxidative coupling processes could lead to more sustainable and economically viable routes for alkene production, potentially revolutionizing the chemical industry.

                硫作为甲烷氧化偶联中替代氧化剂的探索，由Zhu等人详细阐述\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13\]，与传统的基于氧气的系统相比，它是一种较不活跃的氧化剂。硫氧化至CS2的热力学势能显著高于13.41 eV的氧气氧化甲烷至CO2，达到3.45 eV。这种差异表明，使用硫时，过氧化的热力学驱动力较低。在1050 C的PdS催化剂上，可以达到高达18%的乙烯选择性。然而，由于反应的热力学特性和硫化物催化剂上相对较高的CH活化势垒，该过程需要高温，硫化物催化剂的氢抽提能力较金属氧化物差。这些势垒从MoS2上的1.91 eV到TiS2上的2.68 eV不等。目前的催化剂性能相对温和，距离实际应用尚远，但未来的研究可能通过基于描述符的理论筛选识别出具有更高转化率的催化剂。MS键能被确认为CH活化和CC偶联反应势垒的有希望的描述符。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: 硫作为甲烷氧化偶联中替代氧化剂的调查，虽然目前处于起步阶段，但为催化性能和选择性的创新提供了潜力。未来的研究应集中在开发能够降低硫化物催化剂上CH键活化势垒的催化剂上，以提高氢抽提效率。基于描述符的理论筛选可能是识别具有更高转化率和选择性的新型催化剂的关键工具。此外，MS键能作为CH活化和CC偶联反应势垒的描述符的探索为设计更有效的催化剂开辟了途径。优化反应条件，如温度和压力，以减轻硫基氧化的高能量需求，也是未来研究的关键领域。硫基氧化偶联过程的发展可能导致更可持续和经济上可行的烯烃生产路线，可能彻底改变化工行业。
