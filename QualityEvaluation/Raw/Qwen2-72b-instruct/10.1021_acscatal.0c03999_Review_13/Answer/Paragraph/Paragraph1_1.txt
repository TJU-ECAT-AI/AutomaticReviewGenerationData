                The exploration of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling, as detailed in reference \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13\], presents a less active oxidant compared to traditional oxygen-based systems. The thermodynamic well for sulfur oxidation to CS2 is notably higher in energy at 3.45 eV, contrasting with 13.41 eV for O2 oxidation of methane to CO2. This discrepancy suggests a lower thermodynamic driving force for overoxidation with sulfur. An ethylene selectivity of up to 18% can be achieved on a PdS catalyst at 1050 C. However, the process necessitates high temperatures due to the thermodynamics of the reaction and the relatively high CH activation barriers on sulfide catalysts, which are inferior hydrogen abstractors compared to metal oxides. These barriers span from 1.91 eV on MoS2 to 2.68 eV for TiS2. The current catalyst performance is relatively modest and far from practical consideration, yet future studies can identify catalysts with higher conversion rates through descriptor-based theoretical screening. The MS bond energy is observed to be a good descriptor for both CH activation and CC coupling reaction barriers \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The insights gained from the study of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling offer a promising avenue for the development of novel catalytic systems. By focusing on the MS bond energy as a descriptor for CH activation and CC coupling, future research can potentially identify catalysts with enhanced selectivity and conversion rates. The lower thermodynamic driving force for overoxidation with sulfur compared to oxygen suggests a reduced propensity for unwanted side reactions, which could lead to more efficient and selective alkene production. The challenges associated with high activation barriers and the need for high temperatures provide opportunities for innovation in catalyst design, aiming to lower the energy requirements and improve the practicality of sulfur-based oxidative coupling processes. Future directions might include the exploration of advanced materials with optimized MS bond energies, the development of new reaction mechanisms that circumvent high-temperature limitations, and the integration of computational methods for the rational design of catalysts with improved performance.

                硫作为甲烷氧化偶联中替代氧化剂的探索，如参考文献\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13\]所详述，提供了一种与传统氧基系统相比活性较低的氧化剂。硫氧化至CS2的热力学井的能量显著高于13.41 eV的O2氧化甲烷至CO2，达到3.45 eV。这种差异表明硫氧化时过氧化的热力学驱动力较低。在1050 C下，PdS催化剂上可实现高达18%的乙烯选择性。然而，由于反应的热力学和硫化物催化剂上相对较高的CH活化能垒，该过程需要高温，硫化物催化剂作为氢抽象剂的性能较差，与金属氧化物相比。这些能垒从MoS2上的1.91 eV到TiS2上的2.68 eV不等。目前催化剂的性能相对温和，远未达到实用考虑，但未来的研究可以通过基于描述符的理论筛选识别出转化率更高的催化剂。MS键能被观察到是CH活化和CC偶联反应能垒的良好描述符\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13\].
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: 从硫作为甲烷氧化偶联中替代氧化剂的研究中获得的见解为新型催化系统的开发提供了一条有希望的途径。通过关注MS键能作为CH活化和CC偶联的描述符，未来的研究可能识别出具有增强选择性和转化率的催化剂。与氧相比，硫氧化时过氧化的热力学驱动力较低，这表明了对不希望的副反应的倾向性降低，这可能导致更有效和选择性的烯烃生产。与高活化能垒和高温需求相关的挑战为催化剂设计的创新提供了机会，旨在降低能量需求并提高硫基氧化偶联过程的实用性。未来的研究方向可能包括探索具有优化MS键能的先进材料，开发绕过高温限制的新反应机制，以及将计算方法整合到催化剂的合理设计中，以提高性能。
