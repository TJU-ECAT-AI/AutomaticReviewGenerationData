        <English> 
                Comparative analysis of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling, as elucidated by Zhu et al. \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13\], reveals a lower thermodynamic driving force for overoxidation compared to traditional oxygen-based systems. The thermodynamic well for sulfur oxidation to CS2 is considerably higher in energy at 3.45 eV, contrasting with 13.41 eV for O2 oxidation of methane to CO2. This indicates a potential for enhanced selectivity towards alkene products. An ethylene selectivity of up to 18% can be achieved on a PdS catalyst at 1050 C, albeit high temperatures are required due to the thermodynamics of the reaction and the relatively high CH activation barriers on sulfide catalysts, which are poor hydrogen abstractors compared to metal oxides. These barriers range from 1.91 eV on MoS2 to 2.68 eV for TiS2. The performance of this catalyst is relatively modest and far from practical consideration, but future studies can identify catalysts with higher conversion rates by descriptor-based theoretical screening. The MS bond energy is observed to be a good descriptor for both CH activation and CC coupling reaction barriers.
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The exploration of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling offers a promising avenue for enhancing alkene selectivity while mitigating overoxidation. Future research endeavors might focus on developing novel sulfide catalysts with optimized MS bond energies to improve CH activation and CC coupling. Descriptor-based theoretical screening could guide the design of catalysts with lower CH activation barriers, potentially leading to higher conversion rates at lower temperatures. Moreover, the investigation of reaction mechanisms at the molecular level could provide insights into the role of sulfur in alkane conversion, paving the way for the development of more efficient and selective catalytic systems.

                通过Zhu等人\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13\]的研究，对硫作为甲烷氧化偶联中替代氧化剂的比较分析表明，与传统的氧基系统相比，硫氧化具有较低的过氧化热力学驱动力。硫氧化至CS2的热力学井的能量显著较高，为3.45 eV，而氧气氧化甲烷至CO2的能量为13.41 eV。这表明了增强烯烃产品选择性的潜力。在1050 C下，PdS催化剂上可实现高达18%的乙烯选择性，尽管需要高温，这是由于反应的热力学和硫化物催化剂上相对较高的CH活化能垒，与金属氧化物相比，硫化物催化剂是较差的氢抽象剂。这些能垒在MoS2上为1.91 eV，TiS2上为2.68 eV。该催化剂的性能相对温和，远未达到实用考虑，但未来的研究可以通过基于描述符的理论筛选识别出具有更高转化率的催化剂。MS键能被观察到是CH活化和CC偶联反应能垒的良好描述符。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: 硫作为甲烷氧化偶联中替代氧化剂的探索为增强烯烃选择性同时减轻过氧化提供了一条有希望的途径。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发具有优化MS键能的新型硫化物催化剂，以改善CH活化和CC偶联。基于描述符的理论筛选可以指导设计具有较低CH活化能垒的催化剂，可能在较低温度下实现更高的转化率。此外，对分子水平的反应机制的调查可能提供关于硫在烷烃转化中的作用的见解，为开发更高效和选择性的催化系统铺平道路。
