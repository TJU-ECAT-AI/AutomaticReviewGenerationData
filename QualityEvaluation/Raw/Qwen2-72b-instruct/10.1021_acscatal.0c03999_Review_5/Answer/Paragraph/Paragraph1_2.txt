            <English> 
                    Zeolites, such as HZSM-5 with a high Si/Al ratio, are recognized as promising support materials for CO2-ODHP catalysts due to their high catalytic activity and stability in the dehydrogenation of propane to propene in the presence of CO2 \[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1\]. The modification of acidity through dealumination and weakening methods is crucial for optimizing catalytic performance, as it reduces the amount of strong acid sites that can cause catalyst deactivation through side reactions like oligomerization and cracking \[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1\]. Zeolites' high surface area, pore structure, and tunable acidity make them ideal for CO2-ODHP catalysts, as the pore structure provides a confined space for the reaction, enhancing selectivity and efficiency \[10.1039/C5CS00626K\]. Dealumination, which involves removing aluminum from the zeolite framework, can increase acidity and catalytic activity but must be balanced against potential structural instability and surface area reduction \[10.1039/C5CS00626K\]. Weakening methods, such as steam treatment or acid leaching, can reduce acidity, improving selectivity \[10.1039/C5CS00626K\]. The balance between the number of acid sites and their strength is critical for optimizing catalytic performance \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5\]. Increasing the Si/Al ratio can weaken acidity, and introducing phosphorus into HZSM-5 can displace strong Brnsted acid sites with weaker ones, improving hydrothermal stability \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5\]. However, reducing the number of acid sites on HZSM-5 is more effective for boosting C3H8 dehydrogenation to C3H6 than simply weakening the acidity \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The optimization of zeolite-supported catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions involves a delicate balance between acidity, stability, and selectivity. Future research should focus on developing novel dealumination and weakening methods that can precisely control the acidity of zeolites without compromising their structural integrity or surface area. The exploration of new zeolite structures with tailored pore sizes and acidity could lead to enhanced catalytic performance. Additionally, the integration of advanced characterization techniques to monitor changes in acidity and surface properties during dealumination and weakening processes could provide insights into the optimal conditions for catalyst preparation. The development of multifunctional catalysts that combine the properties of different zeolites or incorporate additional metal species could also be a promising direction for improving selectivity and yield in CO2-ODHP reactions.

                    诸如高Si/Al比的HZSM-5等沸石被公认为CO2-ODHP催化剂的有前途的支撑材料，因为它们在CO2存在下具有高催化活性和稳定性，用于丙烷脱氢至丙烯\[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1\]。通过脱铝和弱化方法修改酸性对于优化催化性能至关重要，因为它减少了可能导致催化剂失活的强酸位点的数量，例如通过聚合作用和裂解等副反应\[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1\]。沸石的高表面积、孔结构和可调酸性使其成为CO2-ODHP催化剂的理想选择，因为孔结构为反应提供了受限的空间，提高了选择性和效率\[10.1039/C5CS00626K\]。脱铝，涉及从沸石框架中去除铝，可以增加酸性和催化活性，但必须与潜在的结构不稳定性和表面积减少相平衡\[10.1039/C5CS00626K\]。弱化方法，如蒸汽处理或酸浸，可以降低酸性，提高选择性\[10.1039/C5CS00626K\]。酸位点的数量和强度之间的平衡对于优化催化性能至关重要\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5\]。增加Si/Al比可以削弱酸性，将磷引入HZSM-5可以将强Brnsted酸位点替换为较弱的酸位点，改善水热稳定性\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5\]。然而，减少HZSM-5上的酸位点数量对于促进C3H8脱氢至C3H6比仅仅削弱酸性更有效\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 优化用于CO2-ODHP反应的沸石支撑催化剂涉及酸性、稳定性和选择性之间的微妙平衡。未来的研究应集中在开发新的脱铝和弱化方法，这些方法可以精确控制沸石的酸性，而不损害其结构完整性和表面积。探索具有定制孔径和酸性的新型沸石结构可能会导致增强的催化性能。此外，整合先进的表征技术来监测脱铝和弱化过程中酸性和表面性质的变化，可以为催化剂制备的最优条件提供见解。开发结合不同沸石特性的多功能催化剂或加入额外的金属物种，也可能是提高CO2-ODHP反应选择性和产量的有希望的方向。
