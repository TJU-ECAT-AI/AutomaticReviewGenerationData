        <English> 
                Zeolites, such as HZSM-5 with a high Si/Al ratio, are recognized as promising support materials for CO2-ODHP catalysts due to their high catalytic activity and stability in dehydrogenation of propane to propene in the presence of CO2 \[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1\]. The properties that make zeolites suitable for CO2-ODHP catalysts include their microporosity with a large surface area, tunable acidity, and excellent thermostability \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5\]. Dealumination and weakening methods are used to modify the acidity of zeolites, which can contribute to optimizing catalytic performance by reducing the amount of strong acid sites that cause catalyst deactivation through side reactions like oligomerization and cracking \[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1\]. The intrinsic properties of the supports, their interactions with gallium oxide, and the balance between enhancing catalyst activity and preventing catalyst deactivation are key considerations in selecting and implementing these strategies for different types of zeolites used as supports \[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in understanding the role of zeolites as support materials in CO2-ODHP catalysts and the strategies for modifying their acidity present a fertile ground for future research. There is an opportunity to explore the synergistic effects of combining different acidity modification techniques, such as dealumination and phosphorus incorporation, to achieve optimal catalytic performance. Future research endeavors might focus on developing a comprehensive model that predicts the catalytic activity and selectivity based on the zeolite's acidity, pore structure, and surface area. This could lead to the design of novel zeolite supports with tailored properties for specific catalytic applications, potentially revolutionizing the field of propane dehydrogenation and other related reactions.

                HZSM-5等具有高Si/Al比的沸石被公认为是CO2-ODHP催化剂的有前途的支撑材料，因为它们在CO2存在下将丙烷脱氢为丙烯时具有高催化活性和稳定性\[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1\]。使沸石适合用作CO2-ODHP催化剂的性质包括其具有大表面积的微孔性、可调酸性和优异的热稳定性\[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5\]。脱铝和弱化方法用于修改沸石的酸性，这有助于通过减少导致催化剂失活的强酸位点的数量来优化催化性能\[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1\]。在为不同类型的沸石作为支撑材料选择和实施这些策略时，需要考虑支撑材料的内在性质、它们与氧化镓的相互作用以及增强催化剂活性和防止催化剂失活之间的平衡\[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望：在理解沸石作为CO2-ODHP催化剂支撑材料的作用以及修改其酸性的策略方面的进展为未来的研究提供了肥沃的土壤。有机会探索结合不同的酸性修改技术，如脱铝和磷掺入，以实现最佳催化性能的协同效应。未来的研究工作可能集中在开发一个全面的模型，根据沸石的酸性、孔结构和表面积预测催化活性和选择性。这可能导致设计具有针对特定催化应用的定制特性的新型沸石支撑，可能彻底改变丙烷脱氢和其他相关反应的领域。
