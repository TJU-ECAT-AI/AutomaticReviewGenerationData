        <English> 
                Zeolites, such as HZSM-5 with a high Si/Al ratio, are recognized as promising support materials for CO2-ODHP catalysts due to their high catalytic activity and stability in the dehydrogenation of propane to propene in the presence of CO2 \[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1\]. The modification of acidity through dealumination and weakening methods is crucial for optimizing catalytic performance, as it reduces the amount of strong acid sites that can cause catalyst deactivation through side reactions like oligomerization and cracking \[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1\]. Zeolites' high surface area, pore structure, and tunable acidity make them suitable for CO2-ODHP catalysts, with pore structure enhancing selectivity and efficiency of the catalytic process \[10.1039/C5CS00626K\]. Dealumination, which removes aluminum from the zeolite framework, can increase acidity and catalytic activity but may lead to structural instability and decreased surface area if excessive \[10.1039/C5CS00626K\]. Weakening methods, such as steam treatment or acid leaching, can reduce acidity, improving selectivity \[10.1039/C5CS00626K\]. The balance between acidity and stability, compatibility with active metal species, and effect on selectivity and yield of desired products are key considerations in selecting and implementing acidity modification strategies \[10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011\]. For instance, ZnO/HZSM-5 catalysts with a Si/Al ratio of 160 exhibit optimal performance for propane dehydrogenation to propene in the presence of CO2, achieving a propane conversion of 41.5% and a propene yield of 25.8% at steady state \[10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in modifying zeolite acidity for CO2-ODHP catalysts have opened up new avenues for enhancing catalytic performance. Future research might focus on developing novel dealumination techniques that maintain structural stability while reducing strong acid sites. Additionally, exploring the synergistic effects of different acidity modification methods could lead to more efficient catalysts. The integration of computational modeling with experimental studies could provide deeper insights into the optimal acidity for different zeolite types and reaction conditions. Moreover, the development of multifunctional zeolite supports that simultaneously address selectivity, stability, and activity could revolutionize CO2-ODHP catalysis. The exploration of alternative metal species and their interactions with modified zeolite supports could also be a promising direction for enhancing catalytic outcomes.

                诸如高Si/Al比的HZSM-5等沸石，由于其在CO2存在下对丙烷脱氢至丙烯的高催化活性和稳定性，被公认为CO2-ODHP催化剂的有前途的支撑材料\[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1\]。通过脱铝和弱化方法修改酸性对于优化催化性能至关重要，因为它减少了可能导致催化剂失活的强酸位点的数量\[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1\]。沸石的高表面积、孔结构和可调酸性使其适合于CO2-ODHP催化剂，孔结构增强了催化过程的选择性和效率\[10.1039/C5CS00626K\]。脱铝，即从沸石框架中去除铝，可以增加酸性和催化活性，但如果过度则可能导致结构不稳定和表面积减少\[10.1039/C5CS00626K\]。弱化方法，如蒸汽处理或酸浸，可以降低酸性，提高选择性\[10.1039/C5CS00626K\]。选择和实施酸性修改策略时，考虑酸性和稳定性之间的平衡、与活性金属物种的兼容性以及对所需产品选择性和产量的影响是关键\[10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011\]。例如，Si/Al比为160的ZnO/HZSM-5催化剂在CO2存在下对丙烷脱氢至丙烯表现出最佳性能，达到41.5%的丙烷转化率和25.8%的丙烯产率\[10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: 在CO2-ODHP催化剂中修改沸石酸性的进展为提高催化性能开辟了新的途径。未来的研究可能集中在开发维持结构稳定性的同时减少强酸位点的新型脱铝技术。此外，探索不同酸性修改方法的协同效应可能导致更有效的催化剂。将计算建模与实验研究相结合，可以为不同沸石类型和反应条件下的最佳酸性提供更深入的见解。此外，开发同时解决选择性、稳定性和活性的多功能沸石支撑材料可能彻底改变CO2-ODHP催化。探索替代金属物种及其与改性沸石支撑材料的相互作用，也可能是一个增强催化结果的有希望的方向。
