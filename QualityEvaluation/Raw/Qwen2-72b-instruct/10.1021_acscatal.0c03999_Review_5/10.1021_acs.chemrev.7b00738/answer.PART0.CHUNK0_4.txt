How do these modifications contribute to optimizing catalytic performance, and what are the key considerations in selecting and implementing these strategies for different types of zeolites used as supports?

The provided literature does not contain relevant quotes to answer this question directly. However, zeolites are promising support materials for CO2-ODHP catalysts due to their high surface area, pore structure, and acidity, which can be tailored through various modification techniques. Dealumination and weakening methods can adjust the acidity and stability of zeolites, which is crucial for optimizing catalytic performance. Key considerations in selecting and implementing these strategies include the type of zeolite, the desired acidity level, and the compatibility with the reaction conditions. Dealumination can be achieved through thermal treatment, steaming, or hydrothermal aging, while weakening methods involve the introduction of bulky organic molecules or the use of fluoride ions to reduce the acidity. The choice of modification technique should be based on the specific requirements of the catalytic process and the properties of the zeolite support.
