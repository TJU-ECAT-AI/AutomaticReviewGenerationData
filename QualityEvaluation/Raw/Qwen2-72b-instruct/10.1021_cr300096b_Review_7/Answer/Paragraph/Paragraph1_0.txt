                Cyclohexene's multifaceted role as a probe molecule for hydrogenation reactions is underscored by its sensitivity to the surface termination of Pt or Ni in bimetallic catalysts \[10.1016/j.apcata.2008.01.024\]. Its multiple reaction pathways, particularly the disproportionation pathway, offer insights into the influence of bimetallic catalysts on hydrogenation activity and selectivity \[10.1016/j.apcata.2008.01.024\]. The unique hydrogenation pathway observed on the PtNiPt(1 1 1) subsurface structure, not present on either parent metal surfaces, reveals the enhanced catalytic properties of bimetallic systems compared to monometallic materials \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.03.007\]. The weaker bonding of atomic hydrogen and cyclohexene on this surface leads to a low-temperature hydrogenation pathway, demonstrating the enhanced catalytic properties of bimetallic systems \[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.03.007\]. Comparative studies of cyclohexene's reaction pathways provide opportunities to determine how the formation of bimetallic bonds affects hydrogenation activity and selectivity in industrial processes \[10.1021/cr300096b_7\]. Cyclohexene's ability to undergo self-hydrogenation and hydrogenation on bimetallic catalysts like Ni/Pt(111) and PtNiPt(111), which are not observed on parent metals, confirms its role as an ideal probe molecule \[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001\]. The correlation of self-hydrogenation activity with the surface d-band center of MBS structures is further confirmed by experimental results, providing insights into the influence of bimetallic catalysts on hydrogenation activity and selectivity in industrial processes \[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001\]. Cyclohexene's multiple reaction pathways, including hydrogenation, dehydrogenation, and decomposition, allow researchers to understand the catalytic hydrogenation and dehydrogenation of hydrocarbons \[10.1016/j.susc.2004.03.032\]. The hydrogenation of cyclohexene was used as a probe reaction to demonstrate the enhanced hydrogenation activity toward the CC bond on the bimetallic catalysts, consistent with predictions from surface science studies \[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.08.016\].

                作为研究氢化反应的探针分子，环己烯在双金属催化剂表面终止的Pt或Ni敏感性方面扮演着重要角色\[10.1016/j.apcata.2008.01.024\]。其多种反应途径，特别是歧化途径，提供了对双金属催化剂在氢化活性和选择性影响的见解\[10.1016/j.apcata.2008.01.024\]。在PtNiPt(1 1 1)亚表面结构上观察到的独特氢化途径，不在任一母金属表面上出现，揭示了双金属系统与单金属材料相比的增强催化性能\[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.03.007\]。原子氢和环己烯在该表面上的较弱结合导致低温氢化途径，展示了双金属系统的增强催化性能\[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.03.007\]。环己烯反应途径的比较研究提供了确定双金属键形成如何影响工业过程中氢化活性和选择性的机会\[10.1021/cr300096b_7\]。环己烯在像Ni/Pt(111)和PtNiPt(111)这样的双金属催化剂上进行自氢化和氢化的能力，这些在母金属上没有观察到，确认了其作为理想探针分子的角色\[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001\]。自氢化活性与MBS结构表面d带中心的关联进一步通过实验结果得到证实，为双金属催化剂在工业过程中的氢化活性和选择性影响提供了见解\[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001\]。环己烯的多种反应途径，包括氢化、脱氢和分解，使研究人员能够理解烃类的催化氢化和脱氢\[10.1016/j.susc.2004.03.032\]。环己烯的氢化被用作探针反应，以证明双金属催化剂上CC键的增强氢化活性，与表面科学研究的预测一致\[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.08.016\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望: 利用环己烯作为研究氢化反应的探针分子，已经为双金属催化剂对氢化活性和选择性的影响提供了重要见解。在PtNiPt(1 1 1)亚表面结构上观察到的独特氢化途径，不在任一母金属表面上出现，揭示了双金属系统与单金属材料相比的增强催化性能。原子氢和环己烯在该表面上的较弱结合导致低温氢化途径，展示了双金属系统的增强催化性能。自氢化活性与MBS结构表面d带中心的关联进一步通过实验结果得到证实，为双金属催化剂在工业过程中的氢化活性和选择性影响提供了见解。未来的研究努力可能集中在指导新型双金属催化材料的合理设计上，用于工业过程中的氢化反应，由在PtNiPt(1 1 1)亚表面结构上观察到的独特氢化途径和自氢化活性与MBS结构表面d带中心的关联引导。环己烯在双金属表面上的氢化研究可以指导工业氢化过程催化材料的合理设计。
