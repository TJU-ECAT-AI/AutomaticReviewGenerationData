1.The literature does not provide a direct comparison between silica-supported zirconium hydrides and alumina-supported thorium hydrides. However, it does detail the preparation, structure, and catalytic properties of silica-supported zirconium hydrides in C-H bond activation reactions. These catalysts are prepared by contacting molecular tetraalkyl complexes of zirconium with a partially dehydroxylated silica surface. The structure of the supported zirconium materials is consistent with a tris(siloxy)/mono(hydrido) structure. The catalysts are highly active in H/D exchange reactions, with H/D exchange between hydrogen and deuterium being rapid even at liquid nitrogen temperatures. The activation energy for H2/D2 exchange is estimated to be about 2 kcal/mol. The H/D exchange rates between methane and hydrogen support the view that these reactions proceed via σ-bond metathesis, with a low energy of activation and a high negative entropy of activation. The literature does not provide information on the selectivity of the catalysts or the roles of hydrogenolysis and chain walking in these processes.
