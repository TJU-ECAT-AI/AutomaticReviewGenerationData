1.The literature does not provide a direct comparison between silica-supported zirconium hydrides and alumina-supported thorium hydrides in C-H bond activation reactions. However, it does detail the catalytic properties of alumina-supported thorium hydrides, specifically Th(allyl),/DA, in C-H bond activation reactions. The catalyst is highly active for alkane C-H + C-D exchange reactions, with turnover frequencies comparable to or exceeding those of conventional group 9 heterogeneous alkane activation catalysts. The selectivity of C-H functionalization does not parallel C-H bond dissociation energies, with primary > secondary > tertiary and sterically less hindered > sterically more hindered. The mechanism involves Th-C bond formation and HD elimination via a four-center, heterolytic a-bond metathesis pathway, followed by deuterolysis. Cycloalkane skeletal isomerization occurs via a β-H elimination and readdition of the Th+-H to the opposite face of the double bond. The roles of σ-bond metathesis, hydrogenolysis, and chain walking in these processes are not explicitly discussed in the literature.
