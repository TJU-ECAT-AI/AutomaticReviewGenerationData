                    The comprehensive application of Raman spectroscopy in characterizing, understanding, and correlating the composition, structure, and reactivity of doped catalysts under various conditions, including in situ measurements and in conjunction with other analytical techniques, has been extensively explored in recent studies \[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.03.061\], \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.04.049\], \[10.1039/c0cs00117a\], \[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\], and \[10.1021/cr300418s_5\]. Raman spectroscopy, alongside XRD, SEM, XPS, CH4 TPR-MS, and O2-TPD, provides insights into the complex nature of doped catalysts, such as Ce-doped CoCr2O4 and alkali-doped vanadia on titania, by elucidating their composition, structure, and reactivity \[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.03.061\]. For instance, cerium doping in CoCr2O4 was found to deform the spinel structure, redistribute metal ions with different oxidation states, increase crystal defects, and oxygen vacancy concentration, which facilitates oxygen mobility and enhances catalytic performance \[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.03.061\]. Similarly, the addition of alkali promoters to vanadia/titania catalysts induces a red shift of the vibration frequency related to the V O bond, indicating a weakening of the V O double bond, which can affect the activity of the material if the vanadyl group is involved in the reaction \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.04.049\]. Moreover, the hydrogenation process is more favorable for undoped systems, and the alkali-doped systems show a decrease of the first hydrogen adsorption energy in the order undoped > Li > Na > K \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.04.049\]. The presence of the Li ion decreases the reducibility of dispersed vanadium oxide species compared to the undoped system, and the reduction becomes more difficult with the period of the alkali atom in the periodic table \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.04.049\]. Raman spectroscopy can also reveal information about the reducibility of the catalysts, which is crucial for understanding their catalytic properties \[10.1039/c0cs00117a\]. The spectral features obtained from Raman spectroscopy can be correlated with the catalyst properties, such as the degree of reduction, which is an important factor in determining the catalytic activity \[10.1039/c0cs00117a\]. By correlating the Raman spectral features with the catalyst's activity and selectivity, researchers can gain a deeper understanding of the structure-activity relationships and optimize the catalyst design for improved performance \[10.1039/c0cs00117a\]. The literature emphasizes the importance of using multiple spectroscopic techniques, including Raman spectroscopy, to gain a comprehensive understanding of catalyst structure and activity \[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]. Raman spectroscopy is highlighted for its ability to provide molecular level insights into the structure of catalytic active sites and associated surface intermediates under reaction conditions \[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]. Operando spectroscopy, which combines in situ spectroscopy with simultaneous activity measurements under catalytic reaction conditions, is also emphasized \[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]. Raman spectroscopy is suitable for studying supported oxides due to the minimal interference from the gas phase and its ability to function over a wide range of temperatures and pressures \[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]. The use of several spectroscopic techniques can provide complementary information, contributing to a more detailed understanding of the electronic and molecular structures of catalytic active sites and surface reaction intermediates \[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]. In situ measurements of Raman spectra under reaction conditions were combined with catalyst reactivity characterizations to evaluate the effect of alkali doping on the reducibility of vanadia supported on titania, revealing a correlation between the reducibility of vanadia and the Raman spectrum of the vanadyl group \[10.1021/cr300418s_5\].

                    Raman光谱法在各种条件下，包括原位测量和与其他分析技术结合，全面应用于表征、理解和关联掺杂催化剂的组成、结构和反应性，这一领域在最近的研究中得到了广泛探索\[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.03.061\], \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.04.049\], \[10.1039/c0cs00117a\], \[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\], 和\[10.1021/cr300418s_5\]。Raman光谱法，与XRD、SEM、XPS、CH4 TPR-MS和O2-TPD一起，通过阐明Ce掺杂CoCr2O4和TiO2上掺杂钒酸盐等复杂催化剂的组成、结构和反应性，提供了深入的见解\[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.03.061\]。例如，Ce在CoCr2O4中的掺杂被发现会变形尖晶石结构，重新分配具有不同氧化态的金属离子，增加晶体缺陷和氧空位浓度，这促进了氧的流动性并增强了催化性能\[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.03.061\]。类似地，向TiO2上的钒酸盐催化剂中添加碱金属促进剂会诱导与V O键相关的振动频率的红移，表明V O双键的减弱，如果钒氧基团参与反应，这可能会影响材料的活性\[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.04.049\]。此外，氢化过程对未掺杂系统更为有利，而掺杂系统显示出第一氢吸附能的降低，顺序为未掺杂 > Li > Na > K\[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.04.049\]。Li离子的存在降低了分散的钒氧化物物种的还原性，与未掺杂系统相比，随着碱金属原子在周期表中的周期增加，还原变得更加困难\[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.04.049\]。Raman光谱法还可以揭示有关催化剂还原性的信息，这对于理解它们的催化性质至关重要\[10.1039/c0cs00117a\]。从Raman光谱法获得的光谱特征可以与催化剂性质相关联，如还原程度，这是决定催化活性的重要因素\[10.1039/c0cs00117a\]。通过将Raman光谱特征与催化剂的活性和选择性相关联，研究人员可以更深入地理解结构-活性关系，并优化催化剂设计以提高性能\[10.1039/c0cs00117a\]。文献强调了使用包括Raman光谱法在内的多种光谱技术来全面理解催化剂结构和活性的重要性\[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]。Raman光谱法因其在反应条件下提供催化活性位点和相关表面中间体的分子级见解的能力而受到关注\[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]。原位光谱与催化反应条件下同时的活性测量相结合的原位光谱学也被强调\[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]。由于气体相的干扰最小，Raman光谱法适用于研究氧化物支撑材料，并且能够在广泛的温度和压力范围内工作\[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]。使用几种光谱技术可以提供补充信息，有助于更详细地理解催化活性位点和表面反应中间体的电子和分子结构\[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]。原位条件下反应条件下的Raman光谱测量与催化剂反应性表征相结合，评估了TiO2上钒酸盐的碱金属掺杂对还原性的影响，揭示了钒酸盐的还原性与钒氧基团的Raman光谱之间的相关性\[10.1021/cr300418s_5\]。
