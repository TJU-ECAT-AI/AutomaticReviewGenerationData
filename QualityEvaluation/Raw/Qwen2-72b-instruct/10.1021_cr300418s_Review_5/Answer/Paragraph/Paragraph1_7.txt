                    The comprehensive application of Raman spectroscopy in characterizing, understanding, and correlating the composition, structure, and reactivity of doped catalysts under various conditions, including in situ measurements and in conjunction with other analytical techniques, has been extensively documented \[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.03.061\], \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.04.049\], \[10.1039/c0cs00117a\], \[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\], \[10.1021/cr300418s_5\]. Raman spectroscopy, alongside XRD, SEM, XPS, CH4 TPR-MS, and O2-TPD, contributes to the comprehensive characterization and understanding of doped catalysts by providing insights into their composition, structure, in situ behavior, and reactivity \[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.03.061\]. It helps to understand the relationship between spectral features and catalyst properties, such as reducibility. For instance, in the study of Ce-doped CoCr2O4, Raman spectroscopy along with other techniques revealed that cerium doping deforms the spinel structure, causes redistribution of metal ions with different oxidation states, increases crystal defects and oxygen vacancy concentration, which facilitates oxygen mobility and enhances the catalytic performance of the doped catalysts \[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.03.061\]. The addition of alkali promoters to vanadia/titania catalysts induces a red shift of the vibration frequency related to the V O bond, indicating a weakening of the V O double bond \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.04.049\]. This weakening can affect the activity of the material if the vanadyl group is involved in the reaction. Moreover, the hydrogenation process is more favorable for undoped systems, and the alkali-doped systems show a decrease of the first hydrogen adsorption energy in the order undoped > Li > Na > K \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.04.049\]. The presence of the Li ion decreases the reducibility of dispersed vanadium oxide species compared to the undoped system, and the reduction becomes more difficult with the period of the alkali atom in the periodic table \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.04.049\]. Raman spectroscopy can reveal information about the reducibility of the catalysts, which is crucial for understanding their catalytic properties \[10.1039/c0cs00117a\]. The spectral features obtained from Raman spectroscopy can be correlated with the catalyst properties, such as the degree of reduction, which is an important factor in determining the catalytic activity \[10.1039/c0cs00117a\]. Raman spectroscopy can also help in understanding the relationship between spectral features and catalyst properties, such as reducibility, and can identify the presence of specific dopants and their impact on the catalyst's electronic and vibrational properties, which in turn affect the catalyst's performance \[10.1039/c0cs00117a\]. By correlating the Raman spectral features with the catalyst's activity and selectivity, researchers can gain a deeper understanding of the structure-activity relationships and optimize the catalyst design for improved performance \[10.1039/c0cs00117a\]. The literature emphasizes the importance of using multiple spectroscopic techniques, including Raman spectroscopy, to gain a comprehensive understanding of catalyst structure and activity \[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]. Raman spectroscopy is highlighted for its ability to provide molecular level insights into the structure of catalytic active sites and associated surface intermediates under reaction conditions \[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]. The combined use of several complementary techniques, including Raman, UVvis-DRS, IR, and others, can afford more detailed electronic and molecular structural insights into the nature of surface catalytic active sites, surface reaction intermediates, and their adsorption sites on the catalyst \[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]. This combination can help establish links between catalyst structure and activity/selectivity during reaction conditions \[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]. Operando spectroscopy, which combines in situ spectroscopy with simultaneous activity measurements under catalytic reaction conditions, is also emphasized \[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]. Raman spectroscopy is suitable for studying supported oxides due to the minimal interference from the gas phase and its ability to function over a wide range of temperatures and pressures \[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]. The use of several spectroscopic techniques can provide complementary information, contributing to a more detailed understanding of the electronic and molecular structures of catalytic active sites and surface reaction intermediates \[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]. In studies of Ce-doped CoCr2O4 catalyst for methane oxidation, Cr was substituted by Ce from 2% to 100%, and the material was characterized by Raman, XRD, and XPS \[10.1021/cr300418s_5\]. XRD suggested that a single phase solid solution was produced up to 10% substitution \[10.1021/cr300418s_5\]. Raman spectra were used to identify vibrations associated with pure phase materials and with the doped chromite \[10.1021/cr300418s_5\]. In another study, in situ Raman spectroscopy was combined with catalyst reactivity characterizations to evaluate the effect of alkali doping on the reducibility of vanadia supported on titania, revealing a correlation between the reducibility of vanadia and the Raman spectrum of the vanadyl group \[10.1021/cr300418s_5\].

                    Raman光谱学在各种条件下，包括原位测量和与其他分析技术结合，全面应用于表征、理解和关联掺杂催化剂的组成、结构和反应性，已有大量文献报道\[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.03.061\], \[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.04.049\], \[10.1039/c0cs00117a\], \[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\], \[10.1021/cr300418s_5\]。Raman光谱学与XRD、SEM、XPS、CH4 TPR-MS和O2-TPD等技术一起，为掺杂催化剂的全面表征和理解提供了深入的见解，包括它们的组成、结构、原位行为和反应性\[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.03.061\]。它有助于理解光谱特征与催化剂性质之间的关系，如还原性。例如，在Ce掺杂CoCr2O4的研究中，Raman光谱学和其他技术揭示了铈掺杂会变形尖晶石结构，导致具有不同氧化态的金属离子重新分布，增加晶体缺陷和氧空位浓度，从而促进氧的流动性，增强掺杂催化剂的催化性能\[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.03.061\]。向V2O5/TiO2催化剂中添加碱金属促进剂会导致与V O键相关的振动频率的红移，表明V O双键的减弱\[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.04.049\]。如果Vadyl基团参与反应，这种减弱可能会影响材料的活性。此外，氢化过程对未掺杂系统更有利，而掺杂系统显示出第一氢吸附能的降低，顺序为未掺杂 > Li > Na > K\[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.04.049\]。Li离子的存在降低了分散的V2O5物种的还原性，与未掺杂系统相比，随着碱金属原子在周期表中的周期增加，还原变得更加困难\[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.04.049\]。Raman光谱学可以揭示有关催化剂还原性的信息，这对于理解它们的催化性质至关重要\[10.1039/c0cs00117a\]。从Raman光谱学获得的光谱特征可以与催化剂性质相关联，如还原程度，这是决定催化活性的重要因素\[10.1039/c0cs00117a\]。Raman光谱学还可以帮助理解光谱特征与催化剂性质之间的关系，如还原性，并能识别特定掺杂剂及其对催化剂电子和振动性质的影响，进而影响催化剂的性能\[10.1039/c0cs00117a\]。通过将Raman光谱特征与催化剂的活性和选择性相关联，研究人员可以更深入地理解结构-活性关系，并优化催化剂设计以提高性能\[10.1039/c0cs00117a\]。文献强调了使用包括Raman光谱学在内的多种光谱技术来全面理解催化剂结构和活性的重要性\[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]。Raman光谱学因其在反应条件下提供催化活性位点和相关表面中间体的分子级见解的能力而受到关注\[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]。Raman、UVvis-DRS、IR等几种互补技术的联合使用可以提供更详细的电子和分子结构见解，了解表面催化活性位点、表面反应中间体及其在催化剂上的吸附位点的性质\[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]。这种组合有助于在反应条件下建立催化剂结构与活性/选择性之间的联系\[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]。原位光谱与催化反应条件下的同时活性测量相结合的原位光谱学也受到强调\[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]。由于气体相的干扰最小，Raman光谱学适用于研究支撑氧化物，并且能够在广泛的温度和压力范围内工作\[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]。几种光谱技术的使用可以提供补充信息，有助于更详细地理解催化活性位点和表面反应中间体的电子和分子结构\[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.017\]。在Ce掺杂CoCr2O4催化剂用于甲烷氧化的研究中，Cr被Ce从2%到100%取代，材料通过Raman、XRD和XPS进行表征\[10.1021/cr300418s_5\]。XRD表明，最多可以产生10%的单相固溶体\[10.1021/cr300418s_5\]。Raman光谱用于识别与纯相材料和掺杂尖晶石相关的振动\[10.1021/cr300418s_5\]。在另一项研究中，原位Raman光谱与催化剂反应性表征相结合，以评估碱金属掺杂对支撑在TiO2上的V2O5还原性的影响，揭示了V2O5的还原性与Vadyl基团的Raman光谱之间的相关性\[10.1021/cr300418s_5\]。
