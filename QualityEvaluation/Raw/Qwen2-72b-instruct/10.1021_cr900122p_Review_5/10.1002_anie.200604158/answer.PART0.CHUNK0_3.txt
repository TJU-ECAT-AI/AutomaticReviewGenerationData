1.The literature does not provide a comprehensive mechanism of alkane metathesis catalyzed by silica-supported tantalum hydrides. It does mention that silica-supported tantalum hydrides catalyze the conversion of alkanes into their direct lower and higher homologues through a reaction named alkane metathesis. This process involves carbene and metallacyclobutane intermediates, which explains the selective formation of the directly lower and higher linear homologues of the starting alkane through the transfer of one carbon atom at a time, thereby preventing the formation of long-chain alkanes.
