                    Comprehensive mechanistic investigation of alkane metathesis processes catalyzed by silica-supported tantalum hydrides: From linear to cyclic alkanes and the role of reaction intermediates \[10.1007/s11244-006-0108-4\], \[10.1351/pac-con-08-07-18\], \[10.1021/cr900122\], \[10.1039/b823226a\], \[10.1126/science.276.5309.99\], \[10.1002/anie.200602171\], \[10.1021/cr900122p_5\], \[10.1002/chem.200390162\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The detailed analysis of alkane metathesis catalyzed by silica-supported tantalum hydrides reveals a complex interplay of reaction intermediates and mechanisms, from linear to cyclic alkanes. The role of carbene hydrides, olefin hydrides, and metallacyclobutanes in governing product selectivity and catalyst performance is pivotal. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of ancillary ligands that can enhance catalyst reactivity and selectivity without hindering performance. Additionally, the exploration of alternative metal hydrides and their comparative study with tantalum could offer insights into the mechanistic differences and potentially lead to the discovery of more efficient catalysts. The selective transfer of two carbons, as observed in the transformation of 2-methylpropane to 2,3-dimethylbutane, presents a novel avenue for alkane metathesis, suggesting the potential for innovative catalytic systems capable of more complex carbon rearrangements.
