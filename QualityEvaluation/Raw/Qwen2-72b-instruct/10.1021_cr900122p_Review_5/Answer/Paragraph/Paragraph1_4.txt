                    Comprehensive mechanistic investigation of alkane metathesis processes catalyzed by silica-supported tantalum hydrides: From linear to cyclic alkanes and the role of reaction intermediates \[10.1007/s11244-006-0108-4\]. The catalytic activity of silica-supported tantalum hydrides in alkane metathesis, converting alkanes into their lower and higher homologs via a pi-bond metathesis mechanism involving metallacarbene and alkene intermediates, is exemplified by propane's transformation into ethane and butane \[10.1351/pac-con-08-07-18\]. The two-site mechanism involves tantalum hydrides as intermediates, generating alkenes via β-H transfer and evolving toward Ta-alkylidene hydrides via α-H transfer. Alkenes undergo homologation, leading to different alkenes and alkylidene species, with subsequent hydrogenation and hydrogenolysis liberating lower and higher alkane homologues \[10.1021/cr900122\]. The one-site mechanism is based on tantalum alkyl alkylidene intermediates, with alkene formation and conversion within the metal center's coordination sphere. Selectivity in higher homologues, mainly n + 1 linear homologues, is consistent with alkene metathesis as the key homologation process. γ-H activation after cycloalkyl derivative formation leads to a metallacyclobutane intermediate, which undergoes cycloreversion and subsequent steps to yield lower cyclic and acyclic alkane homologues \[10.1021/cr900122p_5\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in understanding the comprehensive mechanism of alkane metathesis catalyzed by silica-supported tantalum hydrides open up new avenues for the optimization of oil resources through the conversion of alkanes into lower and higher homologues at low temperatures. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of ancillary ligands that enhance catalyst performance without hindering selectivity, aiming to minimize steric interactions in metalla-cyclobutane intermediates. The exploration of novel catalysts based on metal hydrides, alkyl alkylidene, or alkylidyne surface complexes could offer insights into the role of the metal center's coordination sphere in alkene formation and conversion. Moreover, the investigation of the selective transfer of two carbons, as observed in the transformation of 2-methylpropane to 2,3-dimethylbutane and 2-methylpropene to 2,3-dimethylbutene, could lead to the discovery of innovative mechanisms for alkane conversions. The mechanistic differences between Group 4 metal hydrides and silica-supported tantalum hydrides in ring contraction processes suggest the potential for developing catalysts with tailored ring size dependence. The understanding of the role of intermediates, such as alkenes and metallacyclobutanes, in reaction selectivity and catalyst performance could pave the way for the design of more efficient and selective alkane metathesis catalysts.

                    硅胶支持的钽氢化物催化烷基化反应的全面机理研究：从线性到环状烷烃以及反应中间体的作用\[10.1007/s11244-006-0108-4\]。硅胶支持的钽氢化物在烷基化反应中的催化活性，通过π-键化机制涉及金属卡宾和烯烃中间体，将烷烃转化为其较低和较高同系物，以丙烷转化为乙烷和丁烷为例\[10.1351/pac-con-08-07-18\]。双位点机制涉及钽氢化物作为中间体，通过β-H转移生成烯烃，并通过α-H转移向Ta-烷基化氢化物发展。烯烃经历同系化，导致不同的烯烃和烷基化物种，随后的氢化和氢解释放较低和较高的烷烃同系物\[10.1021/cr900122\]。单位点机制基于钽烷基烷基化中间体，其中烯烃在金属中心的配位球内形成和转化。在较高同系物中，主要是n + 1线性同系物的选择性与烯烃同系化作为关键同系化过程一致。γ-H激活后环烷基衍生物的形成导致一个金属环丁烷中间体，它经历环反转和后续步骤，产生较低的环状和非环状烷烃同系物\[10.1021/cr900122p_5\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望：对硅胶支持的钽氢化物催化烷基化反应的全面机制理解的进展为通过在低温下将烷烃转化为较低和较高同系物来优化石油资源开辟了新的途径。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发增强催化剂性能而不损害选择性的辅助配体上，目标是减少金属环丁烷中间体中的空间相互作用。基于金属氢化物、烷基烷基化或烷基化表面复合物的新型催化剂的探索可能为金属中心的配位球在烯烃形成和转化中的作用提供见解。此外，对两个碳的有选择性转移的调查，如在2-甲基丙烷转化为2,3-二甲基丁烷和2-甲基丙烯转化为2,3-二甲基丁烯中观察到的，可能导致发现烷烃转化的创新机制。第4组金属氢化物和硅胶支持的钽氢化物在环收缩过程中的机理差异表明了开发具有定制环大小依赖性的催化剂的潜力。对中间体，如烯烃和金属环丁烷，在反应选择性和催化剂性能中的作用的理解，可能为设计更有效和选择性的烷基化催化剂铺平道路。
