1.The literature does not provide a comprehensive mechanism for alkane metathesis catalyzed by silica-supported tantalum hydrides. However, it does mention that the reaction likely involves the activation of a CH bond by the tantalum hydride, leading to the formation of a surface tantalum alkyl complex. From this complex, a carbene hydride and an olefin hydride species can result from alpha and beta hydrogen abstractions. The interaction of the carbene and the olefin can lead to a metallacyclobutane, which decomposes to afford a new carbene and a new olefin. The hydrogenation of these species produces new alkanes. The selectivity of products is governed by the [1,2] and [1,3] interactions of substituents in the metallacyclobutane intermediates. Tungsten-based catalysts give a narrower distribution of products and a higher amount of linear products compared to tantalum-based catalysts.
