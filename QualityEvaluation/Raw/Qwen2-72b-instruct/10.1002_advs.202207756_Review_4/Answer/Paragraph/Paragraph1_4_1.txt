The alkyl mechanism in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions on gallium-based catalysts is a complex process involving the activation of propane by a metal cation, leading to the formation of an alkyl gallium intermediate, followed by β-elimination and the desorption of propylene and hydrogen \[10.1002/advs.202207756_4\]. This mechanism applies to various Ga species structures, including Ga+, [GaH]+, and Ga2O22+, with the CH activation step and alkyl metal intermediate formation being the rate-determining step (RDS) \[10.1002/advs.202207756_4\]. Unlike the carbenium mechanism, where Ga species facilitate hydrogen recombination, the alkyl mechanism activates the CH bond in propane \[10.1002/advs.202207756_4\].

The initial step in the alkyl mechanism involves the oxidative addition of an alkane molecule to the Ga+ cation via an indirect heterolytic mechanism \[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]. This mechanism is preferred over the carbenium mechanism, which forms a carbenium ion and a hydride ion. The activation energy for the alkyl mechanism (193 kJ/mol) is higher than for GaH2+ species (158 kJ/mol), but the overall activation barrier for the alkyl mechanism (233 kJ/mol) is lower than for the carbenium mechanism \[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]. The CH activation step is proposed as the RDS due to the strong C-H bond breakage \[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\].

The alkyl mechanism applies to different Ga species structures, with Ga+ being the most favorable, followed by GaH2+ and GaH+2 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]. The reactivity of GaH+2 ions is strongly dependent on the distance between stabilizing aluminum-occupied oxygen tetrahedra \[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]. When Al atoms are closely located, GaH+2 ions are the most stable but exhibit lower activity for CH bond cleavage \[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\].

In the alkyl mechanism, propane activation by gallium species forms alkyl-gallium intermediates that decompose to produce alkene molecules and regenerate the active site \[10.1016/j.apcata.2012.05.036\]. This mechanism contrasts with the carbenium mechanism, where the CH activation step is proposed as the RDS \[10.1016/j.apcata.2012.05.036\]. Different Ga species structures, including isolated Ga(I) species and dihydridogallium ions, play roles depending on reaction conditions \[10.1016/j.apcata.2012.05.036\]. Hydrogen addition suppresses aromatization reactions and promotes methane and ethane formation, competing with propane for active sites \[10.1016/j.apcata.2012.05.036\].

In PDH reactions on gallium-based catalysts, the alkyl mechanism involves propane activation through hydride abstraction or proton addition, differing from the carbenium mechanism which forms carbonium ions directly \[10.1016/s0920-5861(96)00028-4\]. Ga and Zn enhance propene formation by facilitating H-atom removal from acid sites, preventing cracking and catalyzing H-atom desorption as H2 \[10.1016/s0920-5861(96)00028-4\]. This H-atom disposal is the RDS for sequential dehydrogenation reactions \[10.1016/s0920-5861(96)00028-4\]. The mechanism applies to various Ga species structures, as Ga's positive effect on alkane aromatization is observed with different introduction techniques \[10.1016/s0920-5861(96)00028-4\].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The alkyl mechanism in PDH reactions on gallium-based catalysts presents a promising avenue for enhancing propene production through the selective activation of propane. The detailed understanding of the mechanism's key steps, from initial propane activation to product formation, and its comparison with the carbenium mechanism, highlights the importance of the CH activation step as the RDS. This knowledge can guide the design of more efficient gallium-based catalysts for PDH reactions.

Future research efforts might focus on the development of novel gallium species structures that optimize the CH activation step, aiming to lower the activation barrier and increase the reaction rate. Additionally, the exploration of alternative metal cations and their interaction with propane could lead to the discovery of new mechanisms that surpass the limitations of the alkyl and carbenium mechanisms. The integration of theoretical studies with experimental data will be crucial for a comprehensive understanding of the PDH reaction mechanism on gallium-based catalysts, paving the way for the design of advanced catalysts with enhanced selectivity and activity.

在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中，基于镓的催化剂上的烷基机制是一个复杂的过程，涉及通过金属阳离子激活丙烷，形成烷基镓中间体，随后β-消除和丙烯和氢气的解吸\[10.1002/advs.202207756_4\]。该机制适用于各种Ga物种结构，包括Ga+、[GaH]+和Ga2O22+，其中CH激活步骤和烷基金属中间体的形成是决定速率的步骤（RDS）\[10.1002/advs.202207756_4\]。与通过镓物种促进氢重组的卡宾机制不同，烷基机制激活丙烷中的CH键\[10.1002/advs.202207756_4\]。

烷基机制的初始步骤涉及通过间接杂裂机制将烷烃分子氧化加合到Ga+阳离子\[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]。该机制优于形成卡宾离子和氢化物离子的卡宾机制。烷基机制的活化能（193 kJ/mol）高于GaH2+物种（158 kJ/mol），但烷基机制的总体活化势垒（233 kJ/mol）低于卡宾机制\[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]。由于强烈的C-H键断裂，CH激活步骤被提议为RDS\[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]。

烷基机制适用于不同的Ga物种结构，其中Ga+是最有利的，其次是GaH2+和GaH+2\[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]。GaH+2离子的反应性强烈依赖于稳定铝占据的氧四面体之间的距离\[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]。当Al原子紧密排列时，GaH+2离子是最稳定的，但对CH键断裂的活性较低\[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]。

在烷基机制中，通过镓物种激活丙烷形成烷基镓中间体，这些中间体分解产生烯烃分子并再生活性位点\[10.1016/j.apcata.2012.05.036\]。这与CH激活步骤被提议为RDS的卡宾机制形成对比\[10.1016/j.apcata.2012.05.036\]。根据反应条件，不同的Ga物种结构，包括孤立的Ga(I)物种和二氢化镓离子，发挥作用\[10.1016/j.apcata.2012.05.036\]。氢添加抑制芳构化反应，促进甲烷和乙烷的形成，与丙烷竞争活性位点\[10.1016/j.apcata.2012.05.036\]。

在基于镓的催化剂上的PDH反应中，烷基机制涉及通过氢化物抽提或质子添加激活丙烷，与直接形成碳离子的卡宾机制不同\[10.1016/s0920-5861(96)00028-4\]。Ga和Zn通过促进从酸位点去除H-原子来增强丙烯的形成，防止裂解并催化H-原子作为H2的解吸\[10.1016/s0920-5861(96)00028-4\]。这是连续脱氢反应的RDS\[10.1016/s0920-5861(96)00028-4\]。该机制适用于各种Ga物种结构，因为通过不同的引入技术观察到Ga对烷烃芳构化的积极影响\[10.1016/s0920-5861(96)00028-4\]。

综合理解与未来展望：基于镓的催化剂上的PDH反应中的烷基机制为通过选择性激活丙烷增强丙烯生产提供了有希望的途径。从初始丙烷激活到产品形成的关键步骤的详细理解，以及与卡宾机制的比较，突出了CH激活步骤作为RDS的重要性。这些知识可以指导设计更有效的镓基催化剂用于PDH反应。

未来的研究努力可能集中在开发优化CH激活步骤的新型镓物种结构，旨在降低活化势垒并提高反应速率。此外，探索替代金属阳离子及其与丙烷的相互作用可能会发现超越烷基和卡宾机制局限性的新机制。理论研究与实验数据的整合对于全面理解基于镓的催化剂上的PDH反应机制至关重要，为设计具有增强选择性和活性的先进催化剂铺平道路。
