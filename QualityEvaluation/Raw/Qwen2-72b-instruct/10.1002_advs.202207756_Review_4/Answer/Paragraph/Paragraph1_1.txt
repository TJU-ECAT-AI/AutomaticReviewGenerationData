                    The alkyl mechanism in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions on gallium-based catalysts involves the activation of propane by a metal cation to form an alkyl gallium intermediate, followed by β-elimination and desorption of propylene and hydrogen \[10.1002/advs.202207756_4\]. This mechanism applies to various Ga species structures, with the CH activation step and alkyl metal intermediate formation proposed as the rate-determining step (RDS) \[10.1002/advs.202207756_4\]. Unlike the carbenium mechanism, where Ga species facilitate hydrogen recombination, the alkyl mechanism activates the CH bond in propane \[10.1002/advs.202207756_4\]. The initial CH activation step proceeds via oxidative addition of an alkane molecule to the Ga+ cation, involving the basic oxygen atoms of the zeolite lattice \[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]. The activation energy for this process is 193 kJ/mol, significantly higher than the 158 kJ/mol reported for GaH2+ species, yet the overall activation barrier for the alkyl mechanism is lower than for the carbenium mechanism \[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]. Among Ga species, Ga+ is the most favorable, followed by GaH2+ and GaH+2, with the reactivity of GaH+2 ions strongly dependent on the distance between stabilizing aluminum-occupied oxygen tetrahedra \[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The alkyl mechanism in PDH reactions on gallium-based catalysts presents a promising pathway for the efficient conversion of propane to propylene. The identification of the CH activation step as the RDS highlights the need for catalyst design strategies that can lower this barrier. Future research efforts might focus on the development of novel gallium-based catalysts with optimized Ga species structures, particularly those that can stabilize the alkyl gallium intermediate and facilitate its decomposition to propylene and hydrogen. Additionally, the exploration of alternative reaction pathways, such as the one-step H2 and C2H4 desorption pathway from gallium alkyl intermediates, could offer new insights into enhancing the overall efficiency of PDH reactions. The understanding of the role of GaH+2 ions and their reactivity dependence on the distance between stabilizing aluminum-occupied oxygen tetrahedra opens avenues for the design of catalysts with controlled Ga species distribution, aiming to maximize the catalytic activity and selectivity for propylene production.

                    在基于镓的催化剂上的丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中，烷基机制涉及金属阳离子激活丙烷形成烷基镓中间体，随后β-消除和丙烯和氢的解吸\[10.1002/advs.202207756_4\]。该机制适用于各种Ga物种结构，其中CH激活步骤和烷基金属中间体的形成被提议为速率决定步骤（RDS）\[10.1002/advs.202207756_4\]。与碳正离子机制不同，其中Ga物种促进氢重组，烷基机制激活丙烷中的CH键\[10.1002/advs.202207756_4\]。初始CH激活步骤通过氧化加成烷烃分子至Ga+阳离子进行，涉及沸石晶格的基本氧原子\[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]。该过程的活化能为193 kJ/mol，显著高于GaH2+物种报告的158 kJ/mol，但烷基机制的总体活化能低于碳正离子机制\[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]。在Ga物种中，Ga+是最有利的，其次是GaH2+和GaH+2，GaH+2离子的反应性强烈依赖于稳定铝占据氧四面体之间的距离\[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望：基于镓的催化剂上的PDH反应中的烷基机制为丙烷高效转化为丙烯提供了一条有前景的途径。将CH激活步骤识别为RDS突显了需要催化剂设计策略来降低这一障碍。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发新型镓基催化剂，具有优化的Ga物种结构，特别是那些能够稳定烷基镓中间体并促进其分解为丙烯和氢的催化剂。此外，探索替代反应途径，如从镓烷基中间体的一步H2和C2H4解吸途径，可能为提高PDH反应的整体效率提供新的见解。理解GaH+2离子的作用及其反应性依赖于稳定铝占据氧四面体之间的距离，为设计具有受控Ga物种分布的催化剂开辟了途径，旨在最大化催化活性和丙烯生产的选择性。
