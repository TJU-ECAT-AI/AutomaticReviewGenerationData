                The alkyl mechanism in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions on gallium-based catalysts involves propane activation by a metal cation to form an alkyl gallium intermediate, followed by β-elimination and desorption of propylene and hydrogen \[10.1002/advs.202207756_4\]. This mechanism applies to various Ga species structures, including Ga+, [GaH]+, and Ga2O22+. The CH activation step and alkyl metal intermediate formation are proposed as the rate-determining steps (RDS) \[10.1002/advs.202207756_4\]. Unlike the carbenium mechanism, where Ga species facilitate hydrogen recombination, the alkyl mechanism activates the CH bond in propane \[10.1002/advs.202207756_4\]. The CH activation step is the RDS due to the strong C-H bond breaking \[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]. The alkyl mechanism is more favorable than the carbenium mechanism, which involves the formation of a carbenium ion and a hydride ion, with an activation energy of 104 kJ/mol \[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]. Ga+ is the most favorable Ga species, followed by GaH2+ and GaH+2, whose reactivity is strongly dependent on the distance between stabilizing aluminum-occupied oxygen tetrahedra \[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\].
                ※※※
                Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The alkyl mechanism in PDH reactions on gallium-based catalysts offers a unique pathway for the selective conversion of propane to propylene. The mechanism's applicability to various Ga species structures and its lower activation barrier compared to the carbenium mechanism highlight its potential for industrial applications. Future research might focus on optimizing the Ga species structures to enhance the reactivity and selectivity of the alkyl mechanism. Additionally, exploring the role of stabilizing aluminum-occupied oxygen tetrahedra in the reactivity of GaH+2 ions could lead to the development of more efficient gallium-based catalysts. The understanding of the RDS in the alkyl mechanism could also guide the design of novel catalysts for PDH reactions, aiming to lower the activation energy for the CH activation step.

                在基于镓的催化剂上的丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中，烷基机制涉及金属阳离子激活丙烷形成烷基镓中间体，随后β-消除和丙烯和氢的解吸\[10.1002/advs.202207756_4\]。该机制适用于各种Ga物种结构，包括Ga+，[GaH]+和Ga2O22+。CH活化步骤和烷基金属中间体的形成被提议为速率决定步骤（RDS）\[10.1002/advs.202207756_4\]。与Ga物种促进氢重组的碳正离子机制不同，烷基机制激活丙烷中的CH键\[10.1002/advs.202207756_4\]。CH活化步骤是RDS，因为需要断裂强C-H键\[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]。烷基机制比涉及形成碳正离子和氢化物离子的碳正离子机制更有利，后者具有104 kJ/mol的活化能\[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]。Ga+是最有利的Ga物种，其次是GaH2+和GaH+2，其反应性强烈依赖于稳定铝占据氧四面体之间的距离\[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.011\]。
                ※※※
                综合理解与未来展望：基于镓的催化剂上的PDH反应中的烷基机制为丙烷选择性转化为丙烯提供了一条独特的途径。该机制适用于各种Ga物种结构，与碳正离子机制相比具有较低的活化能，突显了其在工业应用中的潜力。未来的研究可能集中在优化Ga物种结构以增强烷基机制的反应性和选择性。此外，探索稳定铝占据氧四面体在GaH+2离子反应性中的作用可能导致更有效的镓基催化剂的开发。对烷基机制中RDS的理解也可能指导PDH反应新型催化剂的设计，旨在降低CH活化步骤的活化能。
