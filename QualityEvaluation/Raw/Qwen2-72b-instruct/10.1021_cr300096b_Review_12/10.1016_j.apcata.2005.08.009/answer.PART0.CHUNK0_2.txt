1.The literature does not provide specific information on how bimetallic catalysts, particularly Pt-based ones, influence the mechanisms and efficiencies of CO oxidation, water gas shift reaction, and methane conversions in reactor studies. However, it does mention that the addition of Re to both Pt/TiO2 and Pd/TiO2 catalysts increases WGS activity. The effect of Re-addition is more remarkable in Pd/TiO2 than in Pt/TiO2, and is rather dependent upon the reducing temperature in the case of Pt/TiO2. The formation of bimetallic clusters between Pt and Re or Pd and Re is confirmed, which may be responsible for the acceleration of H2 formation rate. Different types of CO adsorption occur by addition of Re, which are observed in the IR spectra taken after WGS reaction. WGS reaction proceeds via the formation of the surface-formate anion over Re-added Pt and Pd catalysts, and the formation step of formats is the rate-determining step of WGS reaction. Future UHV studies could potentially provide insights in correlating these findings with model surfaces and supported catalysts, considering the current limitations in UHV studies on Pt-based bimetallic surfaces.
