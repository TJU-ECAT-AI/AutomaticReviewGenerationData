However, it does mention that PtPd/CeO2 catalysts exhibit high activity for selective CO oxidation in the presence of hydrogen. The positive effect of water vapor and the negative effect of CO2 on the catalytic performance are also discussed. Future UHV studies could potentially provide insights into correlating these findings with model surfaces and supported catalysts, but the current limitations in UHV studies on Pt-based bimetallic surfaces are not addressed in the literature. Additionally, bimetallic PdPt catalysts are less sensitive to water in the process steam compared with monometallic Pd catalysts. The effects of various factors such as support, ratio of PtPd, O2 concentration, water vapor concentration, CO2 concentration, and the combination of H2O and CO2 in the feed stream on the catalytic performance of PtPd/CeO2 catalysts for selective CO oxidation are also discussed. The addition of water vapor to the feed stream enhances the CO oxidation activity, while CO2 concentration reduces conversion and selectivity. Future UHV studies could potentially correlate these findings with model surfaces and supported catalysts, providing insights into the mechanisms and efficiencies of these reactions on bimetallic surfaces.
