        The study reveals that adjusting the Pt/Ni ratio in bimetallic PtNi catalysts impacts surface properties and catalytic behavior in methane reforming with CO2 \[10.1007/s11144-007-5139-8\]. Incorporating a small amount of Pt (0.3-0.5 wt.%) into Ni catalysts decreases NiO size, facilitates its reduction, and ensures a uniform distribution of nickel particles, as evidenced by TPR, FTIR, and XPS data. Pt-Fe nanoparticles on silica spheres exhibit high activity and stability in CO oxidation under realistic conditions, maintaining 100% CO conversion and selectivity at room temperature, attributed to CUF sites on Pt(111) surfaces \[10.1126/science.1188267\]. Future UHV studies could correlate these findings with model surfaces and supported catalysts, offering insights into CO oxidation and other reactions on bimetallic catalysts. Bimetallic catalysts, notably Pt-based ones, significantly impact CO oxidation, water gas shift reaction, and methane conversions. The PtSnOx system exhibits high activity in CO oxidation due to a synergistic bifunctional mechanism where Pt offers CO adsorption sites and SnOx facilitates oxygen's dissociative adsorption \[10.1016/j.apcatb.2011.06.014\]. PtSn/AC-N catalysts demonstrate high CO conversion and selectivity, particularly at lower temperatures under hydrogen-rich conditions. Future UHV studies could correlate these findings with model surfaces and supported catalysts, offering potential insights.
