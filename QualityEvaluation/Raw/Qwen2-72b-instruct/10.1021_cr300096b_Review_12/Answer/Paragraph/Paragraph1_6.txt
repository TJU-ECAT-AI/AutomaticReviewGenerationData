                    The study reveals that adjusting the Pt/Ni ratio in bimetallic PtNi catalysts for methane reforming with CO2 impacts surface properties and catalytic behavior \[10.1007/s11144-007-5139-8\]. Incorporating a small amount of Pt (0.3-0.5 wt.%) into Ni catalysts decreases NiO size, facilitates its reduction, and ensures a uniform nickel distribution, as evidenced by TPR, FTIR, and XPS data. Pt-Fe nanoparticles on silica spheres exhibit high activity and stability in CO oxidation under realistic conditions, including water and CO2, typical of PEMFC environments \[10.1126/science.1188267\]. These catalysts maintain 100% CO conversion and selectivity at room temperature and remain active at lower temperatures. The high reactivity is attributed to coordinatively unsaturated ferrous (CUF) sites at the peripheries of FeO nanoislands on Pt(111) surfaces. Bimetallic catalysts, notably Pt-based ones, play a crucial role in CO oxidation, water gas shift reaction, and methane conversions \[10.1016/j.apcatb.2011.06.014\]. The PtSnOx system catalyzes CO oxidation at moderate temperatures through a synergistic bifunctional mechanism, where Pt offers CO adsorption sites and SnOx facilitates oxygen adsorption. PtSn/AC-N catalysts exhibit high CO conversion and selectivity, especially at lower temperatures, under hydrogen-rich conditions. Bimetallic catalysts, notably AuPt/CeO2, exhibit high activity in CO oxidation, achieving 78% conversion at 250°C in the water gas shift reaction \[10.1016/j.cattod.2010.04.005\]. Platinum's introduction facilitates the formation of partly oxidized gold species and aids in ceria reduction to Ce3+ species, creating oxygen vacancies and reducing ceria size. Bimetallic catalysts, notably PtCu and PdCu on ceria, exhibit superior oxygen-enhanced water gas shift (OWGS) activity compared to monometallic counterparts \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.10.014\]. Strong metal-metal interactions, as evidenced by TPR and OSC measurements, make these catalysts more oxidation-resistant. EXAFS spectra confirm the formation of Pt-Cu and Pd-Cu alloys, with Cu facilitating CO2 desorption and reducing surface carbonate on ceria. Bimetallic catalysts, especially those with Pt-skin surfaces and 3d transition metals, outperform pure Pt in CO oxidation, water gas shift, and methane conversions \[10.1002/cphc.200900053\]. Their reversible structural modulation, controlled by oxidation and reduction cycles, tunes surface CO adsorption strength and oxidation ability. Bimetallic catalysts, notably Pt-based ones, play a crucial role in industrial processes like fuel cells and catalytic reforming \[10.1016/j.apsusc.2009.03.086\]. Their reversible structural changes under redox conditions may impact performance in CO oxidation, water gas shift reaction, and methane conversions. Fe-Pt bimetallic catalysts exhibit high selectivity for CO oxidation at low temperatures, attributed to the electronic structure modification of surface Pt by subsurface Fe \[10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1\]. This influences adsorption and reaction characteristics, making Pt-skin structures active in hydrogenation of unsaturated hydrocarbons and oxygen reduction reactions.

Future research endeavors might focus on the development of advanced bimetallic catalysts for CO oxidation, water gas shift reaction, and methane conversions, with an emphasis on understanding the synergistic effects between metals and the role of subsurface elements. The potential for UHV studies to bridge the gap between model surfaces and supported catalysts, despite current limitations, suggests a promising direction for future research. The design of high-performance, low-cost catalysts could benefit from a deeper understanding of the electronic and structural properties of bimetallic surfaces under reaction conditions. Moreover, the exploration of novel bimetallic combinations and the optimization of preparation methods, such as sequential impregnation, could lead to catalysts with enhanced stability and activity. The integration of UHV studies with reactor findings could provide insights into the detailed mechanisms of bimetallic catalysts and guide the design of more efficient catalysts for industrial processes.

                    基于\[10.1007/s11144-007-5139-8\]的研究，调整Pt/Ni比率在PtNi双金属催化剂中对甲烷与CO2重整的影响，表明表面性质和催化行为的变化。将少量Pt（0.3-0.5 wt.%）加入Ni催化剂中，可以减小NiO的尺寸，促进其还原，并确保镍颗粒的均匀分布，这通过TPR，FTIR和XPS数据得到证实。Pt-Fe纳米粒子在二氧化硅球上在包括水和CO2在内的实际条件下表现出高活性和稳定性，这些条件类似于PEMFC环境\[10.1126/science.1188267\]。这些催化剂在室温下保持100％的CO转化率和选择性，并在较低温度下保持活性。高反应性归因于Pt(111)表面上FeO纳米岛边缘的配位不饱和亚铁（CUF）位点。值得注意的是，基于Pt的双金属催化剂在CO氧化，水煤气变换反应和甲烷转化中起着至关重要的作用\[10.1016/j.apcatb.2011.06.014\]。PtSnOx系统通过协同双功能机制在中等温度下催化CO氧化，其中Pt提供CO吸附位点，而SnOx促进氧气吸附。PtSn/AC-N催化剂在氢气丰富的条件下表现出高CO转化率和选择性，特别是在较低温度下。值得注意的是，AuPt/CeO2双金属催化剂在水煤气变换反应中表现出高活性，CO氧化在250°C时达到78％的转化率\[10.1016/j.cattod.2010.04.005\]。铂的引入促进了部分氧化金物种的形成，并有助于CeO2还原为Ce3+物种，从而产生氧空位并减小CeO2的尺寸。值得注意的是，PtCu和PdCu在CeO2上的双金属催化剂与单金属催化剂相比表现出优越的氧气增强水煤气变换（OWGS）活性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.10.014\]。TPR和OSC测量证实的强金属-金属相互作用使这些催化剂更耐氧化。EXAFS光谱证实了Pt-Cu和Pd-Cu合金的形成，其中Cu促进了CO2的解吸并减少了CeO2表面上的碳酸盐。值得注意的是，具有Pt皮肤表面和3d过渡金属的双金属催化剂在CO氧化，水煤气变换和甲烷转化中优于纯Pt\[10.1002/cphc.200900053\]。它们的可逆结构调制，通过氧化和还原循环控制，调节表面CO吸附强度和氧化能力。值得注意的是，基于Pt的双金属催化剂在诸如燃料电池和催化重整等工业过程中起着至关重要的作用\[10.1016/j.apsusc.2009.03.086\]。它们在氧化还原条件下的可逆结构变化可能会影响CO氧化，水煤气变换反应和甲烷转化的性能。Fe-Pt双金属催化剂在低温下对CO氧化表现出高选择性，归因于表面Pt的电子结构由次表面Fe的修改\[10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1\]。这影响了吸附和反应特性，使Pt皮肤结构在不饱和烃的氢化和氧还原反应中活性。
