These findings have significant implications for future ultra-high vacuum (UHV) research, which can provide atomic-level insights into the active sites and reaction pathways on model surfaces and supported catalysts. By bridging the gap between fundamental surface science and applied catalysis, UHV studies on bimetallic systems promise to guide the design of more efficient and selective catalysts for these critical reactions.
