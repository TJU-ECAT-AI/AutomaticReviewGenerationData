The comprehensive overview of significant catalytic reactions utilizing bimetallic catalysts, with a focus on reactor studies and their implications for future UHV research on model surfaces and supported catalysts, reveals the pivotal role of Pt-based bimetallic catalysts in CO oxidation, water gas shift reaction, and methane conversions \[10.1007/s11144-007-5139-8\]. A minor addition of Pt (0.3-0.5 wt.%) to Ni catalysts for methane reforming with CO2 reduces NiO size, facilitates its reduction, and ensures a uniform distribution of nickel particles, as evidenced by TPR, FTIR, and XPS data \[10.1007/s11144-007-5139-8\]. Pt-Fe nanoparticles supported on silica spheres exhibit high activity and stability in CO oxidation under realistic conditions, including water and CO2, typical of PEMFC environments \[10.1126/science.1188267\]. These catalysts maintain 100% CO conversion and selectivity at room temperature, with high reactivity attributed to CUF sites at the peripheries of FeO nanoislands on Pt(111) surfaces \[10.1126/science.1188267\]. PtSnOx systems exhibit high activity in CO oxidation due to a synergistic bifunctional mechanism where Pt adsorbs CO and SnOx facilitates oxygen adsorption \[10.1016/j.apcatb.2011.06.014\]. PtSn/AC-N catalysts show high CO conversion and selectivity, especially at lower temperatures, under hydrogen-rich conditions \[10.1016/j.apcatb.2011.06.014\]. AuPt/CeO2 exhibits high activity, achieving 78% CO conversion at 250°C in the water gas shift reaction \[10.1016/j.cattod.2010.04.005\]. Platinum's introduction facilitates the formation of partly oxidized gold species through interatomic charge transfer, aiding ceria reduction to Ce3+ species and oxygen vacancy creation \[10.1016/j.cattod.2010.04.005\]. Bimetallic catalysts, notably PtCu and PdCu supported on ceria, show higher activity in oxygen-enhanced water gas shift (OWGS) than monometallic counterparts \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.10.014\]. Strong metal-metal interactions, as evidenced by TPR and OSC measurements, make these catalysts more oxidation-resistant \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.10.014\]. EXAFS spectra reveal Cu forming alloys with Pt and Pd, and the unique structure of bimetallic particles, with highly dispersed Pt or Pd in Cu, is key to their performance \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.10.014\]. The presence of Cu in PdCu/CeO2 facilitates CO2 desorption and reduces surface carbonate on CeO2 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.10.014\]. Bimetallic catalysts, especially those with Pt-skin surfaces and 3d transition metals, outperform pure Pt in CO oxidation, water gas shift, and methane conversions \[10.1002/cphc.200900053\]. Their reversible structural modulation, controlled by oxidation and reduction cycles, tunes surface CO adsorption strength and oxidation ability \[10.1002/cphc.200900053\]. Bimetallic catalysts, notably those based on Pt, are crucial in industrial processes like fuel cells and catalytic reforming \[10.1016/j.apsusc.2009.03.086\]. Their performance in reactions such as CO oxidation, water gas shift, and methane conversions can be influenced by reversible structural changes under different redox conditions \[10.1016/j.apsusc.2009.03.086\]. Fe-Pt bimetallic catalysts exhibit high selectivity for CO oxidation at low temperatures (<100°C) due to the subsurface Fe modifying the surface Pt's electronic structure, affecting adsorption and reaction characteristics \[10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1\]. Future UHV studies could correlate these findings with model surfaces and supported catalysts by examining Fe-Pt structure stability under various conditions, aiding in the design of high-performance, low-cost catalysts \[10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1\]. Pt-skin structures with subsurface Fe show higher activity than pure Pt in oxygen reduction reactions, highlighting the importance of controlling temperature and gaseous environment to modulate Fe-Pt structures \[10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1\].

全面概述了利用双金属催化剂的重要催化反应，重点关注反应器研究及其对未来UHV研究在模型表面和支撑催化剂上的影响，揭示了基于Pt的双金属催化剂在CO氧化、水煤气变换反应和甲烷转化中的关键作用\[10.1007/s11144-007-5139-8\]。在甲烷与CO2重整中，向Ni催化剂中添加少量Pt (0.3-0.5 wt.%)可减少NiO尺寸，促进其还原，并确保镍颗粒均匀分布，这由TPR、FTIR和XPS数据证实\[10.1007/s11144-007-5139-8\]。在SiO2支撑的Pt-Fe纳米颗粒在包括水和CO2在内的实际条件下，在PEMFC环境中表现出CO氧化的高活性和稳定性\[10.1126/science.1188267\]。这些催化剂在室温下保持100%的CO转化率和选择性，高反应性归因于Pt(111)表面上FeO纳米岛边缘的CUF位点\[10.1126/science.1188267\]。PtSnOx系统在CO氧化中表现出高活性，这是由于Pt吸附CO和SnOx促进氧气吸附的协同双功能机制\[10.1016/j.apcatb.2011.06.014\]。PtSn/AC-N催化剂在氢气丰富的条件下表现出高CO转化率和选择性，特别是在较低温度下\[10.1016/j.apcatb.2011.06.014\]。AuPt/CeO2表现出高活性，在水煤气变换反应中在250°C下达到78%的CO转化率\[10.1016/j.cattod.2010.04.005\]。铂的引入通过原子间电荷转移促进部分氧化的金物种的形成，帮助CeO2还原为Ce3+物种并创造氧空位\[10.1016/j.cattod.2010.04.005\]。在CeO2上支撑的双金属催化剂，尤其是PtCu和PdCu，在氧气增强的水煤气变换（OWGS）中比单金属催化剂表现出更高的活性\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.10.014\]。TPR和OSC测量证实的强金属-金属相互作用使这些催化剂更抗氧化\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.10.014\]。EXAFS光谱显示Cu与Pt和Pd形成合金，以及具有高度分散的Pt或Pd在Cu中的双金属颗粒的独特结构是其性能的关键\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.10.014\]。PdCu/CeO2中的Cu促进CO2解吸并减少CeO2上的表面碳酸盐\[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.10.014\]。具有Pt皮肤表面和3d过渡金属的双金属催化剂，尤其是那些优于纯Pt的催化剂，在CO氧化、水煤气变换和甲烷转化中表现出色\[10.1002/cphc.200900053\]。它们的可逆结构调制，由氧化和还原循环控制，调节表面CO吸附强度和氧化能力\[10.1002/cphc.200900053\]。基于Pt的双金属催化剂在工业过程如燃料电池和催化重整中至关重要\[10.1016/j.apsusc.2009.03.086\]。它们在CO氧化、水煤气变换和甲烷转化等反应中的性能可能受到不同氧化还原条件下可逆结构变化的影响\[10.1016/j.apsusc.2009.03.086\]。Fe-Pt双金属催化剂在低温(<100°C)下对CO氧化表现出高选择性，这是由于次表面Fe修改表面Pt的电子结构，影响吸附和反应特性\[10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1\]。未来的UHV研究可以通过在各种条件下检查Fe-Pt结构的稳定性，与模型表面和支撑催化剂相关联，帮助设计高性能、低成本的催化剂\[10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1\]。具有次表面Fe的Pt皮肤结构在氧还原反应中的活性高于纯Pt，强调了控制温度和气体环境以调节Fe-Pt结构的重要性\[10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1\]。
