                    Incorporating a small amount of Pt (0.3-0.5 wt.%) into Ni catalysts for methane reforming with CO2 reduces NiO particle size, eases its reduction, and ensures a more uniform distribution of nickel metal particles, as evidenced by TPR, FTIR, and XPS data \[10.1007/s11144-007-5139-8\]. Pt-Fe nanoparticles on silica spheres exhibit high activity and stability in CO oxidation under realistic conditions, maintaining 100% CO conversion and selectivity at room temperature, with sustained performance at lower temperatures \[10.1126/science.1188267\]. Bimetallic catalysts, notably Pt-based ones, significantly impact CO oxidation, with the PtSnOx system exhibiting high activity due to a synergistic bifunctional mechanism where Pt adsorbs CO and SnOx facilitates oxygen adsorption \[10.1016/j.apcatb.2011.06.014\]. Bimetallic catalysts, notably AuPt/CeO2, exhibit high activity in CO oxidation and the water gas shift reaction, achieving 78% CO conversion at 250°C \[10.1016/j.cattod.2010.04.005\]. Ceria-supported bimetallic catalysts, such as PtCu and PdCu, exhibit higher oxygen-enhanced water gas shift (OWGS) activity than monometallic catalysts due to the strong interaction between Pt and Cu or Pd and Cu, making them less pyrophoric \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.10.014\]. Bimetallic catalysts, especially those with Pt-skin surfaces and 3d transition metals, outperform pure Pt in CO oxidation, water gas shift, and methane conversions \[10.1002/cphc.200900053\]. Bimetallic catalysts, notably those based on Pt, play a significant role in industrial processes like fuel cells and catalytic reforming \[10.1016/j.apsusc.2009.03.086\]. Fe-Pt bimetallic catalysts demonstrate high selectivity for CO oxidation at low temperatures, attributed to the electronic structure modification of surface Pt by subsurface Fe, influencing adsorption and reaction characteristics \[10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1\]. Pt-based bimetallic catalysts enhance CO oxidation, water gas shift, and methane conversions. In CO oxidation, PtFe and PtNi catalysts show increased activity due to interface confinement and specific surface structures. Water gas shift benefits from Pt alloys, with dispersion and metal nature affecting activity. Methane conversions to CO and H2 are improved by PtCo and PtNi, facilitated by increased reducibility and chain growth \[10.1021/cr300096b_12\]. Co-Pt bimetallic catalysts outperform their monometallic counterparts in selective CO oxidation in hydrogen mixtures, significantly reducing CO concentration. This enhanced activity is attributed to the formation of a Co-Pt solid solution on the support surface \[10.1134/s0023158407020127\]. Bimetallic PtCo catalysts supported on YSZ are highly efficient for PROX reactions in hydrogen-rich gas streams, even with low Pt content (0.5wt%) at temperatures below 423K \[10.1002/anie.200603144\]. Bimetallic PtFe catalysts on SiO2 support influence CO oxidation, dehydrogenation of cyclohexane, and selective hydrogenation of citral \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.05.013\]. Adding Pt to Ni catalysts in oxidative steam reforming of methane enhances catalytic performance and bed temperature uniformity \[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.10.004\]. Pt-based bimetallic catalysts, notably PtSn alloys, enhance CO oxidation by facilitating oxygen adsorption at lower temperatures compared to monometallic Pt \[10.1016/s0920-5861(00)00435-1\]. Bimetallic Pt-based catalysts, notably PtPd/CeO2, exhibit high activity for selective CO oxidation, with water vapor enhancing activity and CO2 reducing conversion and selectivity \[10.1016/j.ijhydene.2006.05.002\]. Bimetallic catalysts, especially Pt-based ones, influence CO oxidation, WGS, and methane conversions. WGS, crucial for hydrogen production, benefits from advanced LTS catalysts for CO-free hydrogen for PEM fuel cells \[10.1016/j.apcata.2005.08.009\]. Bimetallic catalysts, particularly Pt and Ru, enhance methane partial oxidation's stability and activity, increasing selectivity for CO and H2 production \[10.1016/j.apcata.2008.06.044\].

                    将少量的Pt (0.3-0.5 wt.%)加入到Ni催化剂中用于与CO2的甲烷重整，可以减少NiO颗粒的尺寸，使其更容易还原，并确保镍金属颗粒更均匀的分布，这由TPR，FTIR和XPS数据证实 \[10.1007/s11144-007-5139-8\]。在实际条件下，Pt-Fe纳米颗粒在二氧化硅球上表现出高活性和稳定性，维持100%的CO转化率和室温下的选择性，且在较低温度下保持高性能 \[10.1126/science.1188267\]。值得注意的是，基于Pt的双金属催化剂对CO氧化有显著影响，其中PtSnOx系统由于Pt吸附CO和SnOx促进氧气吸附的协同双功能机制而表现出高活性 \[10.1016/j.apcatb.2011.06.014\]。值得注意的是，AuPt/CeO2双金属催化剂在CO氧化和水煤气变换反应中表现出高活性，250°C时CO转化率达到78% \[10.1016/j.cattod.2010.04.005\]。诸如PtCu和PdCu的氧化铈支持的双金属催化剂由于Pt和Cu或Pd和Cu之间的强相互作用，表现出比单金属催化剂更高的氧气增强水煤气变换（OWGS）活性，使它们的热爆性降低 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.10.014\]。特别是那些具有Pt皮肤表面和3d过渡金属的双金属催化剂在CO氧化，水煤气变换和甲烷转化方面优于纯Pt \[10.1002/cphc.200900053\]。基于Pt的双金属催化剂在诸如燃料电池和催化重整的工业过程中起着重要作用 \[10.1016/j.apsusc.2009.03.086\]。Fe-Pt双金属催化剂在低温下对CO氧化表现出高选择性，归因于表面Pt的电子结构由次表面Fe的修改，影响吸附和反应特性 \[10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1\]。Pt基双金属催化剂增强CO氧化，水煤气变换和甲烷转化。在CO氧化中，PtFe和PtNi催化剂由于界面限制和特定表面结构而表现出增加的活性。水煤气变换得益于Pt合金，分散性和金属性质影响活性。甲烷转化为CO和H2通过PtCo和PtNi的增加的还原性和链增长而得到改善 \[10.1021/cr300096b_12\]。Co-Pt双金属催化剂在氢混合物中的选择性CO氧化中优于其单金属对应物，显著降低CO浓度。这种增强的活性归因于在支撑表面上形成Co-Pt固溶体粒子 \[10.1134/s0023158407020127\]。在YSZ上支持的PtCo双金属催化剂在氢气丰富的气体流中对PROX反应具有高效率，即使在423K以下的温度下，Pt含量低至0.5wt% \[10.1002/anie.200603144\]。在二氧化硅上支持的PtFe双金属催化剂影响CO氧化，环己烷脱氢和香叶醇选择性氢化 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.05.013\]。在氧化蒸汽重整甲烷中添加Pt可以增强催化性能和床层温度均匀性 \[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.10.004\]。值得注意的是，PtSn合金等基于Pt的双金属催化剂通过在较低温度下促进氧气吸附，从而增强CO氧化，与单金属Pt相比 \[10.1016/s0920-5861(00)00435-1\]。值得注意的是，基于Pt的双金属催化剂，如PtPd/CeO2，对选择性CO氧化表现出高活性，水蒸气增强活性，CO2降低转化率和选择性 \[10.1016/j.ijhydene.2006.05.002\]。特别是Pt和Ru的双金属催化剂增强甲烷部分氧化的稳定性和活性，增加CO和H2生产的可选择性 \[10.1016/j.apcata.2008.06.044\]。
