          The literature does not provide specific details on how bimetallic catalysts, particularly Pt-based ones, influence the mechanisms and efficiencies of CO oxidation, water gas shift reaction, and methane conversions in reactor studies. However, it does mention that Pt-based bimetallic catalysts with Pt-skin surfaces and 3d transition metals at the subsurface region exhibit superior catalytic performance in many reactions compared to pure Pt surfaces. The reversible structural modulation of the bimetallic catalysts regulated by cycled oxidation and reduction treatments shows tunability in the surface CO adsorption strength and CO oxidation ability. Future UHV studies could potentially correlate these findings with model surfaces and supported catalysts, providing insights into the mechanisms and efficiencies of reactions involving CO oxidation, water gas shift, and methane conversions. The current limitations in UHV studies on Pt-based bimetallic surfaces might include the inability to fully replicate the complex redox conditions and gas environments present in reactor studies.
