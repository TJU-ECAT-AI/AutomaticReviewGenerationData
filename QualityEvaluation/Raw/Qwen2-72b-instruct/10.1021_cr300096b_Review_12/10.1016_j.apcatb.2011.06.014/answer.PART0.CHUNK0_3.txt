However, it does mention that the PtSnOx system has shown to catalyze CO oxidation at moderate temperatures where neither platinum nor tin dioxide alone has stable activity. The high activity of the PtSnOx system is attributed to a synergistic bifunctional mechanism where Pt provides adsorption sites for CO, while oxygen adsorbs dissociatively on SnOx. The literature also discusses the preparation and testing of PtSn/AC-N catalysts for preferential CO oxidation under hydrogen-rich feed conditions, finding that the catalyst has high CO conversion levels and selectivity, especially at lower temperatures. Future UHV studies could potentially provide insights into correlating these findings with model surfaces and supported catalysts, but the current limitations in UHV studies on Pt-based bimetallic surfaces are not discussed in the literature.
