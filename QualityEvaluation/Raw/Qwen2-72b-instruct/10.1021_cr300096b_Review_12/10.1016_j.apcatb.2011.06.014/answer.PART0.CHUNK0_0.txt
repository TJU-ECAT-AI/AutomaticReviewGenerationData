1.The literature does not provide specific information on how bimetallic catalysts, particularly Pt-based ones, influence the mechanisms and efficiencies of CO oxidation, water gas shift reaction, and methane conversions in reactor studies. However, it does mention that the PtSnOx system has shown to catalyze CO oxidation at moderate temperatures, where neither platinum nor tin dioxide alone has stable activity. The high activity of the PtSnOx system is attributed to a synergistic bifunctional mechanism in which Pt provides adsorption sites for CO, while oxygen adsorbs dissociatively on SnOx. The literature also notes that the active phase on PtSn catalysts prepared on nitric acid oxidized activated carbon (AC-N) is probably the Pt3Sn alloy, which leads to enhanced CO oxidation ability. The literature does not discuss the limitations of UHV studies on Pt-based bimetallic surfaces or the potential insights that future UHV studies could provide in correlating findings with model surfaces and supported catalysts.
