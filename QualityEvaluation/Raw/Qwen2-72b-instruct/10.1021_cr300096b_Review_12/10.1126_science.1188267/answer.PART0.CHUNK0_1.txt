1.The literature does not provide specific information on how bimetallic catalysts, particularly Pt-based ones, influence the mechanisms and efficiencies of CO oxidation, water gas shift reaction, and methane conversions in reactor studies. However, it does discuss the preparation of Pt-Fe nanoparticles supported on nanosized silica spheres, which show high activity and stability in CO oxidation under realistic conditions, including the presence of water and CO2 typical of PEMFC working conditions. The catalysts maintain almost 100% CO conversion and selectivity at room temperature and remain highly active even at lower temperatures. The literature does not address the limitations of UHV studies on Pt-based bimetallic surfaces or potential insights from future UHV studies correlating findings with model surfaces and supported catalysts.
