1.The literature does not provide specific information on how bimetallic catalysts, particularly Pt-based ones, influence the mechanisms and efficiencies of CO oxidation, water gas shift reaction, and methane conversions in reactor studies. However, it does discuss the preparation of Pt-Fe nanoparticles supported on nanosized silica spheres, which show high activity and stability in CO oxidation under realistic conditions, including the presence of water and CO2 typical of PEMFC working conditions. The catalysts maintain almost 100% CO conversion and selectivity at room temperature and remain highly active even at lower temperatures. The high reactivity is attributed to the presence of coordinatively unsaturated ferrous (CUF) sites at the peripheries of FeO nanoislands on Pt(111) surfaces. Future UHV studies could potentially correlate these findings with model surfaces and supported catalysts, providing insights into the mechanisms of CO oxidation and other reactions on bimetallic catalysts.
