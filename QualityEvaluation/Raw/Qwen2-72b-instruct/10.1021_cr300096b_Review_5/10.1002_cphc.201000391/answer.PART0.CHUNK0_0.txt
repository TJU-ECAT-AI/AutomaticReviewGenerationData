However, it does discuss the effects of annealing temperature on the structural and chemical properties of bimetallic PtRu/Ru(0001) surfaces, which can be related to the broader question. The study shows that annealing leads to changes in the surface morphology from Pt islands on the Ru substrate to a PtRu/Ru(0001) surface alloy, which significantly alters the adsorption properties of the surface. This can be attributed to changes in the composition of the adsorption ensembles and electronic interactions between the metallic constituents upon alloy formation. These changes have implications for the catalytic properties of the surface, as they can affect the activity and selectivity of the catalyst.
