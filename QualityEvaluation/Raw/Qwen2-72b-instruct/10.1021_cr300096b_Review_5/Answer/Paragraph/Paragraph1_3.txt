                    The thermodynamic stability, structural dynamics, and catalytic implications of bimetallic surface structures under varying reaction conditions, with emphasis on Pt-based alloy systems, have been extensively studied \[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]. Pt3dPt subsurface structures are stable in vacuum and with hydrogen, but oxygen induces 3d atom segregation to surface structures. Experimental results confirm these predictions, showing higher activation barriers for oxygen-induced segregation in subsurface configurations. PtNiPt is most resistant, while PtTiPt is most susceptible. Δεd is used to screen alloys for stability in various environments. A BrnstedEvansPolanyi relationship correlates stability with kinetic barriers. Empirical equations predict stability trends in different conditions. Binding energy differences highlight the need to understand stability in reacting environments. Sabatier's principle suggests optimal binding properties in subsurface structures may halt reactions on surface structures and vice versa. DFT and experiments show subsurface configurations are preferred in vacuum and with hydrogen, and surface configurations with oxygen. Linear trends in thermodynamic stability extend beyond Pt3d alloys. Switching admetal/host components shifts segregation driving potential. All bimetallic systems have preferred configurations in each environment, with subsurface favored in vacuum and hydrogen, and surface favored with oxygen.
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive analysis of thermodynamic stability, structural dynamics, and catalytic implications of bimetallic surface structures under varying reaction conditions, particularly in Pt-based alloy systems, has revealed a rich landscape of phenomena and mechanisms. The stability of Pt3dPt subsurface structures in vacuum and hydrogen, and their susceptibility to oxygen-induced segregation, underscores the critical role of environmental conditions in determining surface configurations. The use of Δεd for alloy screening, the BrnstedEvansPolanyi relationship correlating stability with kinetic barriers, and empirical equations predicting stability trends in different conditions, all contribute to a deeper understanding of bimetallic catalyst behavior. Sabatier's principle, suggesting optimal binding properties in subsurface structures may halt reactions on surface structures and vice versa, offers a new perspective on the interplay between stability and catalytic activity. The linear trends in thermodynamic stability extending beyond Pt3d alloys and the impact of switching admetal/host components on segregation driving potential highlight the universality and complexity of these phenomena. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of advanced computational models to predict the stability and catalytic properties of bimetallic catalysts under a wider range of reaction conditions. Additionally, the exploration of novel bimetallic systems with tailored surface structures could lead to the discovery of catalysts with enhanced performance for specific applications, such as propane dehydrogenation. The integration of experimental and computational approaches will be crucial for advancing the field and unlocking the full potential of bimetallic catalysts in various industrial processes.

                    在不同反应条件下，双金属表面结构的热力学稳定性、结构动力学及其催化含义，特别是强调Pt基合金系统，已经进行了广泛的研究\[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]。Pt3dPt亚表面结构在真空和氢气中是稳定的，但氧气会诱导3d原子向表面结构的分层。实验结果证实了这些预测，显示出在亚表面配置中，氧气诱导的分层具有更高的活化势垒。PtNiPt是最抗性的，而PtTiPt是最敏感的。使用Δεd筛选在各种环境中稳定的合金。BrnstedEvansPolanyi关系将稳定性与动力学势垒相关联。经验方程预测了不同条件下的稳定性趋势。结合能差异突出了理解在反应环境中稳定性的重要性。萨巴蒂尔原理表明，亚表面结构中的最优结合性质可能在表面结构上停止反应，反之亦然。DFT和实验表明，在真空和氢气中，亚表面配置是首选的，而在氧气中，表面配置是首选的。热力学稳定性的线性趋势超出了Pt3d合金。切换吸附金属/基体组件会改变分层驱动势。所有双金属系统在每个环境中都有首选的配置，亚表面在真空和氢气中是首选的，而表面在氧气中是首选的。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望：对在不同反应条件下，特别是在Pt基合金系统中，双金属表面结构的热力学稳定性、结构动力学及其催化含义的全面分析，揭示了现象和机制的丰富景观。Pt3dPt亚表面结构在真空和氢气中的稳定性，以及它们对氧气诱导分层的敏感性，突出了环境条件在确定表面配置中的关键作用。使用Δεd进行合金筛选，BrnstedEvansPolanyi关系将稳定性与动力学势垒相关联，以及预测不同条件下稳定性趋势的经验方程，都为双金属催化剂行为的更深入理解做出了贡献。萨巴蒂尔原理，建议亚表面结构中的最优结合性质可能在表面结构上停止反应，反之亦然，为稳定性和催化活性之间的相互作用提供了新的视角。热力学稳定性线性趋势超出Pt3d合金，以及切换吸附金属/基体组件对分层驱动势的影响，突出了这些现象的普遍性和复杂性。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发先进的计算模型，以预测在更广泛的反应条件下，双金属催化剂的稳定性和催化性质。此外，探索具有定制表面结构的新型双金属系统，可能会导致发现具有增强性能的催化剂，用于特定应用，如丙烷脱氢。实验和计算方法的整合对于推进该领域和解锁双金属催化剂在各种工业过程中的全部潜力至关重要。
