            <English> 
                    The thermodynamic stability and structural dynamics of bimetallic surface structures under varying reaction conditions, particularly in Pt-based alloy systems, have been thoroughly investigated \[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]. These studies reveal that Pt3dPt subsurface structures are stable in vacuum and with hydrogen, but oxygen induces 3d atom surface segregation, verified experimentally. PtNiPt resists oxygen-induced segregation most, while PtTiPt is most susceptible. Δεd correlates with thermodynamic stability and kinetic barriers, predicting stability trends in various conditions. Sabatier's principle suggests subsurface structures with optimal binding may halt reactions on surface structures, and vice versa. DFT and experiments confirm subsurface configurations are preferred in vacuum and with hydrogen, while surface configurations are favored with oxygen. Activation barriers for oxygen-induced segregation vary among Pt3dPt structures, with Pt3dPt(100) having higher barriers than Pt3dPt(111) \[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive analysis of thermodynamic stability, structural dynamics, and catalytic implications of bimetallic surface structures under varying reaction conditions, with emphasis on Pt-based alloy systems, reveals a rich landscape of opportunities for future research. The insights gained from understanding the role of thermodynamics, reaction conditions, and crystal plane orientation in the stability and catalytic performance of Pt-based bimetallic catalysts can be leveraged to design novel catalysts with enhanced stability and activity. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of advanced computational methods to predict the behavior of bimetallic catalysts under various reaction conditions, the synthesis of new Pt-based alloys with tailored surface structures for specific catalytic applications, and the exploration of the effects of surface segregation on the catalytic properties of bimetallic catalysts in real-world catalytic processes. The integration of experimental and computational approaches will be crucial for advancing our understanding of the complex interplay between thermodynamics, reaction conditions, and surface structure in bimetallic catalysts.

                    在变化的反应条件下，特别是Pt基合金系统中，对双金属表面结构的热力学稳定性和结构动态进行了深入研究\[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]。这些研究揭示了Pt3dPt亚表面结构在真空和氢气中是稳定的，但氧气会诱导3d原子表面分层，这一现象已经通过实验验证。PtNiPt对氧气诱导的分层抵抗最强，而PtTiPt最易受到影响。Δεd与热力学稳定性和动力学障碍相关，预测了在各种条件下的稳定性趋势。萨巴蒂尔原理表明，具有最佳结合的亚表面结构可能阻止表面结构上的反应，反之亦然。DFT和实验确认，在真空和氢气中，亚表面配置是首选的，而在氧气中，表面配置更受欢迎。氧气诱导的分层的活化障碍在Pt3dPt结构中各不相同，Pt3dPt(100)的障碍高于Pt3dPt(111)\[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望: 对在变化的反应条件下，特别是Pt基合金系统中，双金属表面结构的热力学稳定性、结构动态和催化含义的全面分析揭示了未来研究的丰富机遇。从理解热力学、反应条件和晶面取向在Pt基双金属催化剂的稳定性和催化性能中的作用中获得的见解，可以用来设计具有增强稳定性和活性的新型催化剂。未来的研究工作可能集中在开发先进的计算方法来预测双金属催化剂在各种反应条件下的行为，合成具有定制表面结构的新型Pt基合金，用于特定的催化应用，以及探索表面分层对双金属催化剂在实际催化过程中的催化性质的影响。实验和计算方法的结合对于推进我们对双金属催化剂中热力学、反应条件和表面结构之间的复杂相互作用的理解至关重要。
