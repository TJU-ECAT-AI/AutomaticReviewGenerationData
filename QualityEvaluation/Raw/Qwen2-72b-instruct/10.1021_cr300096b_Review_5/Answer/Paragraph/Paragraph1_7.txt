                The thermodynamic stability and structural dynamics of bimetallic surface structures in Pt-based alloy systems, particularly in propane dehydrogenation catalysts, are significantly influenced by varying reaction conditions. Pt3dPt subsurface structures exhibit stability in vacuum and with hydrogen, but segregate with oxygen, as predicted by DFT and verified experimentally \[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]. PtNiPt shows the highest resistance to oxygen-induced segregation, while PtTiPt is most susceptible. Stability trends correlate with d-band center differences, predicting preferred configurations in various environments. Oxygen binding energy differences highlight the need for understanding stability in reacting environments. Sabatier's principle suggests optimal binding properties in subsurface structures may halt reactions on surface structures and vice versa. Empirical equations predict thermodynamic stability in different conditions, and linear trends extend beyond Pt3d alloys. Segregation behavior is consistent across single crystal and polycrystalline substrates, bridging the materials gap. In PtPd nanoparticles, Pd segregates to the outer layers, forming Pd-enriched shells, while Pt concentration increases towards the bulk, creating Pt-enriched cores \[10.1088/0953-8984_23_47_475301\]. Notably, Pd atoms prefer (100) facets over (111) facets on the nanoparticle shell, which has implications for catalytic properties and performance in various applications. Under specific reaction conditions, Fe dissolution in PtFe alloys leads to the formation of a protective Pt skin layer with (111)-oriented facets, as observed by in situ electrochemical STM \[10.1039/b107037a\]. This layer, formed through increased Fe dissolution and Pt rearrangement, shields the PtFe alloy from corrosion, enhancing its catalytic properties in fuel cells, as indicated by the disappearance of the Fe2+/Fe3+ redox peak. Alloying Pt with Fe boosts electrocatalytic activity, peaking at 50% Fe content, 25 times higher than pure Pt, due to the 5d-vacancy of the surface, crucial for high-performance cathode electrocatalysts in polymer electrolyte fuel cells \[10.1016/s0022-0728(98)00361-1\]. Thermodynamic preferences and reaction conditions significantly influence the stability, surface segregation, and structural configuration of bimetallic catalysts, particularly in Pt-based systems. Pt, a crucial cathode electrocatalyst in PEM fuel cells, faces efficiency limitations due to slow ORR kinetics. Pt-based alloys, featuring Pt and transition metals, exhibit enhanced ORR activity attributed to surface segregation, which forms a pure Pt-skin on the outer layer, boosting stability. Au demonstrates strong surface segregation in PtAu alloys, unlike Co in PtCo alloys, where Au's segregation is thermodynamically favorable, whereas Co's is both thermodynamically unfavorable and kinetically prohibitive. Surface segregation notably increases ORR activity on Pt-skin surfaces, quadrupling the reaction rate compared to pure Pt \[10.1021/ct8004062\]. These findings underscore the critical role of surface segregation in the catalytic performance of Pt-based alloys.

Lastly, the study of the dynamic behavior of bimetallic catalysts under real reaction conditions, using in situ spectroscopic techniques, could offer a deeper understanding of the mechanisms underlying catalytic activity and stability.

                Pt基合金系统中，特别是在丙烷脱氢催化剂中，双金属表面结构的热力学稳定性和结构动力学受到变化反应条件的显著影响。Pt3dPt亚表面结构在真空和氢气中表现出稳定性，但在氧气中发生分层，这一预测由DFT理论和实验验证\[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]。PtNiPt对氧气诱导的分层表现出最高的抵抗力，而PtTiPt最易受到影响。稳定性趋势与d带中心差异相关，预测在不同环境中的优选配置。氧气结合能差异强调了理解反应环境中稳定性的重要性。萨巴蒂尔原理表明，亚表面结构中的最优结合性质可能在表面结构上停止反应，反之亦然。经验方程预测了不同条件下的热力学稳定性，线性趋势超出了Pt3d合金。分层行为在单晶和多晶基底上保持一致，弥合了材料间的差距。在PtPd纳米粒子中，Pd分层到外层，形成Pd富集壳层，而Pt浓度向内核增加，形成Pt富集核心\[10.1088/0953-8984_23_47_475301\]。值得注意的是，Pd原子在纳米粒子壳层上更倾向于(100)面而不是(111)面，这对催化性质和各种应用的性能有影响。在特定反应条件下，PtFe合金中的Fe溶解导致形成具有(111)取向面的保护性Pt皮肤层，通过原位电化学STM观察\[10.1039/b107037a\]。通过增加Fe溶解和Pt重组形成的这一层，保护PtFe合金免受腐蚀，通过Fe2+/Fe3+氧化还原峰的消失，增强其在燃料电池中的催化性质。将Pt与Fe合金化，电催化活性在Fe含量达到50%时达到峰值，比纯Pt高25倍，这是由于表面的5d空位，对于聚合物电解质燃料电池中的高性能阴极电催化剂至关重要\[10.1016/s0022-0728(98)00361-1\]。热力学偏好和反应条件对双金属催化剂的稳定性、表面分层和结构配置有显著影响，特别是在Pt基系统中。Pt是PEM燃料电池中至关重要的阴极电催化剂，由于ORR动力学缓慢而面临效率限制。Pt基合金，以Pt和过渡金属为特征，表现出增强的ORR活性，归因于表面分层，它在最外层形成纯Pt皮肤，增强稳定性。Au在PtAu合金中表现出强烈的表面分层，与PtCo合金中的Co不同，Au的分层在热力学上是有利的，而Co的分层在热力学上是不利的，且在动力学上是禁止的。表面分层显著增加了Pt皮肤表面上的ORR活性，与纯Pt相比，反应速率提高了四倍\[10.1021/ct8004062\]。这些发现强调了表面分层在Pt基合金催化性能中的关键作用。
