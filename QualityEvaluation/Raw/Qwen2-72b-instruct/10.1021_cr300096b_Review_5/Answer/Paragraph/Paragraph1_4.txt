    The thermodynamic stability, structural dynamics, and catalytic implications of bimetallic surface structures under varying reaction conditions, with emphasis on Pt-based alloy systems, have been extensively studied \[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]. Pt3dPt subsurface structures are stable in vacuum and with hydrogen, but oxygen induces 3d atom segregation to the surface, verified experimentally \[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]. PtNiPt resists oxygen-induced segregation most, while PtTiPt is most susceptible \[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]. Stability trends predict subsurface configurations favored in vacuum and hydrogen, and surface configurations in oxygen \[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]. Sabatier's principle suggests optimal binding properties for subsurface structures may halt reactions on surface structures and vice versa \[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]. DFT and experimental results on single crystals apply to polycrystalline substrates, highlighting the importance of understanding stability in reacting environments \[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]. Activation barriers for oxygen-induced segregation vary by crystal facet \[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]. Linear trends in stability correlate with d-band center differences, applicable beyond Pt3d alloys \[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\].

    Surface segregation in Pt-3d bimetallic catalysts significantly enhances catalytic activities, particularly in oxygen reduction reactions (ORR), outperforming pure Pt by four times with Pt-skin structures \[10.1016/j.susc.2008.11.036\]. Adsorbates under operating conditions alter segregation trends and surface composition, critically affecting catalytic properties \[10.1016/j.susc.2008.11.036\]. Studying segregation under adsorbates is paramount for understanding the catalytic performance of Pt-based alloys \[10.1016/j.susc.2008.11.036\].

    Thermodynamic preferences and reaction conditions significantly impact the stability, surface segregation, and structure of bimetallic catalysts, particularly in Pt-based systems \[10.1021/ct8004062\]. Pt's sluggish kinetics for the oxygen reduction reaction (ORR) in PEM fuel cells limits efficiency, prompting the exploration of Pt alloys with other transition metals for enhanced ORR activity \[10.1021/ct8004062\]. Surface segregation, where a pure Pt-skin forms on the outermost layer, is crucial for enhanced stability \[10.1021/ct8004062\]. Au shows strong segregation in PtAu alloys, thermodynamically favoring surface enrichment, unlike Co in PtCo alloys \[10.1021/ct8004062\]. Au's exothermic segregation process contrasts with Co's endothermic process, making continuous Co segregation unlikely and both thermodynamically and kinetically prohibitive \[10.1021/ct8004062\]. Surface segregation's role is evident in the four times higher ORR rate on Pt-skin surfaces compared to pure Pt \[10.1021/ct8004062\].

    In Pt-based bimetallic catalysts, thermodynamic preferences and reaction conditions determine stability, surface segregation, and structural configuration \[10.1021/jp8046888\]. 3d metals enhance Pt surface segregation on Pt3Ni (111) surfaces, whereas Ag, Au, and Pd suppress it \[10.1021/jp8046888\]. Segregation is influenced by surface energy, strain, and heat of solution, depending on the doped element \[10.1021/jp8046888\]. Stronger Pt segregation occurs when Ni is replaced by transition metals, while Pt segregation is prevented when Ni is replaced by Ag, Au, and Pd \[10.1021/jp8046888\].

    在不同反应条件下，双金属表面结构的热力学稳定性、结构动力学及其催化含义，特别是对基于Pt的合金系统，已经进行了广泛研究\[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]。在真空和氢气中，Pt3dPt亚表面结构稳定，但氧气会诱导3d原子向表面迁移，这一现象已经通过实验验证\[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]。PtNiPt对氧气诱导的迁移具有最强的抵抗力，而PtTiPt最易受到影响\[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]。稳定性趋势预测，在真空和氢气中，亚表面配置更受青睐，而在氧气中，表面配置更占优势\[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]。萨巴蒂尔原理表明，亚表面结构的最优结合特性可能会阻止表面结构上的反应，反之亦然\[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]。单晶上的DFT和实验结果适用于多晶基底，强调了在反应环境中理解稳定性的必要性\[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]。氧气诱导的迁移的活化势垒因晶面而异\[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]。稳定性的线性趋势与d带中心的差异相关，这一规律超出了Pt3d合金的范畴\[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]。

    在Pt-3d双金属催化剂中，表面迁移显著增强了催化活性，特别是在氧还原反应(ORR)中，Pt皮肤结构的性能是纯Pt的四倍\[10.1016/j.susc.2008.11.036\]。在操作条件下，吸附物会改变迁移趋势和表面组成，对催化性质产生关键影响\[10.1016/j.susc.2008.11.036\]。在吸附物下研究迁移至关重要，以理解基于Pt的合金的催化性能\[10.1016/j.susc.2008.11.036\]。

    热力学偏好和反应条件对双金属催化剂的稳定性、表面迁移和结构，特别是在基于Pt的系统中，有显著影响\[10.1021/ct8004062\]。在PEM燃料电池中，Pt对氧还原反应(ORR)的缓慢动力学限制了效率，促使人们探索与其它过渡金属合金化的Pt，以增强ORR活性\[10.1021/ct8004062\]。在最外层形成纯Pt皮肤对于增强稳定性至关重要\[10.1021/ct8004062\]。Au在PtAu合金中表现出强烈的迁移，热力学上倾向于表面富集，而Co在PtCo合金中则不然\[10.1021/ct8004062\]。Au的放热迁移过程与Co的吸热过程形成对比，使得连续的Co迁移不太可能，而且在热力学和动力学上都是不利的\[10.1021/ct8004062\]。在Pt皮肤表面上的ORR速率比纯Pt高四倍，这清楚地表明了表面迁移的作用\[10.1021/ct8004062\]。

    在基于Pt的双金属催化剂中，热力学偏好和反应条件决定了稳定性、表面迁移和结构配置\[10.1021/jp8046888\]。在Pt3Ni(111)表面上，3d金属增强了Pt的表面迁移，而Ag、Au和Pd则抑制了这一过程\[10.1021/jp8046888\]。迁移受表面能、应变和溶解热的影响，这取决于掺杂的元素\[10.1021/jp8046888\]。当Ni被过渡金属取代时，Pt的迁移更强，而当Ni被Ag、Au和Pd取代时，Pt的迁移被阻止\[10.1021/jp8046888\]。
