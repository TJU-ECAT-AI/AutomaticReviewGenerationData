                    The thermodynamic stability, structural dynamics, and catalytic implications of bimetallic surface structures under varying reaction conditions, with emphasis on Pt-based alloy systems, have been extensively studied \[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]. Pt3dPt subsurface structures are stable in vacuum and with hydrogen, but segregate with oxygen, verified experimentally. PtNiPt resists oxygen-induced segregation most, while PtTiPt is most susceptible. Δεd and BEP relationships predict preferred configurations in different environments, impacting catalytic properties by potentially binding intermediates too strongly or weakly \[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]. In PtPd nanoparticles, Pd segregates to the outer layers, creating a gradient Pt concentration, with Pd-enriched shells and Pt-enriched cores. Pd atoms prefer (100) facets over (111) facets on the nanoparticle shell, affecting catalytic properties and performance in various applications \[10.1088/0953-8984_23_47_475301\]. Under specific reaction conditions, Fe dissolution in a PtFe alloy leads to the formation of a protective Pt skin layer with (111)-oriented facets, as observed by in situ electrochemical STM. This layer shields the PtFe alloy from corrosion, enhancing its stability and catalytic properties in fuel cell applications \[10.1039/b107037a\]. Alloying Pt with Fe enhances electrocatalytic activity, peaking at 50% Fe content, 25 times higher than pure Pt, due to the 5d-vacancy on the surface \[10.1016/s0022-0728(98)00361-1\]. Thermodynamic preferences and reaction conditions significantly influence the stability, surface segregation, and structure of bimetallic catalysts, particularly Pt-based alloys. Pt's sluggish ORR kinetics limit efficiency, prompting research into Pt alloys for enhanced activity. Surface segregation, forming a pure Pt-skin, boosts stability and catalytic performance. PtAu exhibits favorable Au segregation, unlike PtCo, where Co migration to the surface is inhibited. Stamenkovic et al. observed that Pt-skin surfaces quadruple ORR activity. Modern techniques have elucidated segregation trends in Pt alloys, revealing critical surface defects and rearrangement processes \[10.1021/ct8004062\]. In Pt-based bimetallic catalysts, particularly Pt-3d alloys, 3d metals significantly boost surface stability and catalytic properties by modifying adsorption characteristics. This enhancement, attributed to the protective role of 3d subsurface atoms, improves oxygen reduction reaction rates and electrochemical stability. Surface segregation facilitates the removal of adsorbed species, increasing the ORR rate, and the stability of Pt-skin surfaces is associated with a positive potential shift, delaying Pt atom dissolution \[10.1021/jp8046888\].
                    ※※※
                    Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive review of thermodynamic stability, structural dynamics, and catalytic implications of bimetallic surface structures under varying reaction conditions, with a focus on Pt-based alloy systems, reveals a rich landscape of phenomena and mechanisms that govern the behavior of these catalysts. The interplay between thermodynamics, reaction conditions, and surface segregation is a critical determinant of catalytic performance, with implications for the design of more efficient and selective catalysts. Future research endeavors might focus on the development of advanced computational methods to predict and optimize the stability and catalytic properties of bimetallic catalysts under various reaction conditions. Additionally, the exploration of novel bimetallic systems beyond Pt-based alloys could lead to the discovery of new catalysts with enhanced performance in a wide range of applications. The integration of experimental and computational approaches will be essential for advancing our understanding of the complex interfacial processes that occur at bimetallic surfaces and for guiding the rational design of next-generation catalysts.

                    在不同反应条件下，对双金属表面结构的热力学稳定性、结构动力学和催化影响进行了广泛研究，重点是基于Pt的合金系统\[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]。Pt3dPt亚表面结构在真空和氢气中稳定，但在氧气中会分层，实验已证实。PtNiPt对氧气诱导的分层的抵抗力最强，而PtTiPt最易受到影响。Δεd和BEP关系预测了在不同环境中的首选配置，通过可能将中间体结合得太强或太弱来影响催化性能\[10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2\]。在PtPd纳米粒子中，Pd分层到外层，形成Pt浓度梯度，Pd富集的壳层和Pt富集的核心。Pd原子在纳米粒子壳层上更喜欢(100)面而不是(111)面，影响了各种应用中的催化特性和性能\[10.1088/0953-8984_23_47_475301\]。在特定反应条件下，PtFe合金中的Fe溶解导致形成具有(111)取向面的保护性Pt皮肤层，通过原位电化学STM观察到。这一层保护PtFe合金免受腐蚀，增强其在燃料电池应用中的稳定性和催化性能\[10.1039/b107037a\]。将Pt与Fe合金化可增强电催化活性，在50%Fe含量时达到峰值，比纯Pt高25倍，这是由于表面的5d空位\[10.1016/s0022-0728(98)00361-1\]。热力学偏好和反应条件对双金属催化剂的稳定性、表面分层和结构有重大影响，特别是Pt基合金。Pt的缓慢ORR动力学限制了效率，促使研究Pt合金以增强活性。表面分层，形成纯Pt皮肤，提高了稳定性和催化性能。PtAu表现出有利的Au分层，而PtCo中Co向表面的迁移受到抑制。Stamenkovic等人观察到，Pt皮肤表面的ORR活性是纯Pt的四倍。现代技术阐明了Pt合金中的分层趋势，揭示了关键的表面缺陷和重组过程\[10.1021/ct8004062\]。在基于Pt的双金属催化剂中，特别是Pt-3d合金，3d金属通过改变吸附特性显著提高了表面稳定性和催化性能。这种增强归因于3d亚表面原子的保护作用，提高了氧还原反应速率和电化学稳定性。表面分层促进了吸附物种的去除，提高了ORR速率，而Pt皮肤表面的稳定性与正电位偏移相关，延缓了Pt原子的溶解\[10.1021/jp8046888\]。
                    ※※※
                    综合理解与未来展望：对基于Pt的合金系统下不同反应条件下双金属表面结构的热力学稳定性、结构动力学和催化影响的全面回顾揭示了决定这些催化剂行为的现象和机制的丰富景观。热力学、反应条件和表面分层之间的相互作用是催化性能的关键决定因素，对设计更高效、更选择性的催化剂有影响。未来的研究工作可能集中在开发先进的计算方法，以预测和优化在各种反应条件下双金属催化剂的稳定性和催化性能。此外，探索Pt基合金以外的新型双金属系统可能会发现具有增强性能的新催化剂，适用于广泛的应用。实验和计算方法的整合对于推进我们对发生在双金属表面的复杂界面过程的理解至关重要，并为指导下一代催化剂的理性设计提供指导。
