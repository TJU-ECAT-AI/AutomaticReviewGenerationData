Dramatic Improvements of Well-Defined Silica Supported Mo-Based Olefin Metathesis Catalysts by Tuning the N-Containing Ligands
Frédéric BlancRomain BerthoudAlain SalamehJean-Marie BassetChristophe CopéretRojendra SinghRichard R. Schrock

Abstract
Grafting [Mo(⋮NAr)(CHCMe2R 1)(NR2)2] on SiO2-(700) gives [(⋮SiO)Mo(⋮NAr)(CHCMe2R1)(NR2)] as a major surface species along with [(⋮SiO)Mo(NAr)(CH2CMe2R1)(NR2)2] according to mass balance analysis, IR and NMR spectroscopies. The replacement of one amido group by a siloxy group from the surface of silica dramatically increased the activity of the Mo complex in olefin metathesis, and tuning the imido ligands allows the reactivity to be increased by at least one order of magnitude so that an overall turnover of 231 000 can be achieved in the metathesis of propene. Additionally, ethyl oleate can also be converted into a thermodynamic equilibrated mixture in 8 h with only 0.02% of catalyst.

Despite the recent advances in the design of olefin metathesis catalysts, optimal systems combining high activity, selectivity, stability, and functional group tolerance still need to be discovered, whether based on d0 transition metals (Mo, W, Re) or Ru, homogeneous 1,2 or heterogeneous catalysts. 3-8 In the specific case of d0 systems, we have recently shown that asymmetry at the metal center and site isolation through grafting on rigid oxide supports can allow an increase in the performances of these systems: 9-12 today well-defined silica supported heterogeneous catalysts out-perform their homogeneous equivalents (rates and TON). The current best catalysts are based on the amido complexes 1 [(⋮SiO)Mo(⋮NR1)(CHCMe2R2)(NR2)]: 12,13 the pyrrolyl derivatives (NR2pyrrole) are very attractive, because they can be readily accessible in two steps from the triflate derivative [Mo(⋮NR1)(CHCMe2R2)(OTf)2(dme)] (synthesis of [Mo(⋮NR1)-(CHCMe2R2)(NR2)2] followed by grafting), 14 but they undergo a fast deactivation. On the contrary, the diphenylamido systems are more stable catalysts, but these systems require a longer synthetic route. 15 We have therefore investigated the use of 2,5-dimethylpyrrolyl derivatives [Mo(⋮NR1)(CHCMe2R2)(NR2)2] (2a−c, R2N = 2,5-dimethylpyrrolyl), also readily available from the triflate derivatives, 16 as an access to the well-defined silica supported system 1a−c, [(⋮SiO)Mo(⋮NR1)(CHCMe2R2)(NR2)]. Here, we show (1) the dramatic effect of this 2,5-dimethylpyrrolyl ligand on the structures, the stability and the activity of the resulting surface species compared to the parent pyrrolyl system, and (2) the improvement (activity and stability) of this new generation of catalysts by tuning the imido ligand.
First, grafting of 2a-Ph (NR2 = 2,5-dimethylpyrrolyl, R1 = 2,6-i-Pr2C6H3, R2 = Ph) on SiO2-(700) gives the corresponding monosiloxy complex 1a-Ph as the major species (ca. 80%) along with 3a-Ph (ca. 20%) according to mass balance analysis, IR, and NMR studies (Scheme 1, 3a-Ph = 3-R2 with R1 = 2,6-i-Pr2C6H3 and R2 = Ph) in contrast to the diphenylamido and the parent pyrrolyl systems, which give a single surface species 1. Upon grafting, the surface silanols, observed at 3747 cm-1 in the IR spectrum of SiO2-(700), are consumed while ν(C−H) and δ(C−H) vibrations of aromatic and alkyl ligands appeared in the 3100−2700 and 1600−1350 cm-1 regions, respectively (Figure S1). The appearance of a broad band centered at 3620 cm-1 is consistent with the presence of remaining silanols in interactions, probably with the ligands of the grafted Mo complex. Conversely, 0.8 equiv of 2,5-dimethylpyrrole are formed during grafting, and the elemental analyses on the resulting solid for Mo (1.30 ± 0.05 wt %), C (5.1 ± 0.1 wt %) and N (0.4 ± 0.1 wt %) are consistent with the consumption of 55% of the surface silanols (0.14 mmol Mo·g-1 for 0.26 mmol SiOH·g-1 of SiO2-(700)) and the presence of an average of 31 ± 2 carbons and 2.2 ± 0.5 nitrogens per grafted Mo by comparison with 28 C/Mo and 2 N/Mo expected for 1a-Ph. Furthermore, the 1H MAS solid-state NMR spectrum and especially the constant time (CT) 1H MAS NMR spectrum (Figure S2) display the following resonances:  12.2 (CHCMe2Ph), 7.1 (Csp2-H), 5.5 (pyrrolyl-Csp2-H), 3.3 (CHMe2), 1.8 (unreacted SiOH), 1.3 (CHCMe2Ph, pyrrolyl-Csp3-H) and 1.0 (CHMe2) ppm in full agreement with the presence of 1a-Ph as a major surface species. The 13C CP MAS spectrum displays the expected signals for 1a-Ph at the exception of the carbenic carbon (Figure S3; Table S1). However, an extra signal is observed at 69 ppm in agreement with the presence of another surface species. In fact, when the 13C labeled neopentylidene complex, 2a-Me [Mo(⋮NR1)(*CHCMe3)(NR2)2] (R2 = Me), 99% 13C labeled on the α-carbon to Mo (Figures S4−S6; Table S1), is grafted on SiO2-(700) in place of 2a-Ph, two isotropic signals at 69 and 285 ppm, corresponding to the 13C labeled carbons, appear in the 13C NMR spectrum. These signals have distinct correlation peaks (1H/13C) at (12.2/285) and (2.3/69) ppm in the 2D 1H-13C HETCOR NMR spectrum, consistent with the presence of two surface species:  one having one neopentyl ligand and the other one having kept the neopentylidene ligand. All the data are consistent with the major species being 1a-Me, and the minor species 3a-Me (Scheme 1). Heating this mixture of surface species (1a/3a) in toluene at 80 °C for 4 h does not show the formation of 2,5-dimethylpyrrole, and the 13C NMR spectrum remains unchanged, which is consistent with the high stability of 3a. This also probably shows that 1a is probably formed by the direct electrophilic cleavage of the Mo−N bond by the surface silanols, while 3a results from the competing addition of the surface silanol to the Mo−C double bond of 2a.

Second, contacting [2a-Ph/SiO2-(700)] with propene in a flow reactor (ca. 400 mL·mn-1; 4800 mol of propene.mol of Mo-1.min-1) gives selectively ethene and 2-butenes with a very fast initial rate (5.2 s-1), and over 1500 min, 101 000 TON have been achieved {Figure 1, TON = f(t)}. While the initial rate is similar to this obtained for the parent pyrrolyl system [(⋮SiO)Mo(⋮NR1)-(CHCMe2Ph)(NR2)] (NR2 = pyrrolyl, R1 = 2,6-di-i-PrC6H3) 1d-Ph (TOF = 6.2 s-1, TON = 62 000), the stability of the catalyst has been improved (slower deactivation).12 This is especially true if one corrects the rates and the TON for the actual number of active sites in [2a-Ph/SiO2-(700)] (ca. 80%, TOFcorr. and TONcorr. of 6.5 s-1 and 126 000, respectively). The difference between these two systems is even more pronounced in the self-metathesis of ethyl oleate (EO)used as received without any purification:  the equilibrated mixture (1:1:2 ratio of 9-octadecene, the diester, and EO) is obtained within 3 h in the presence of 0.05 mol % of [2a-Ph/SiO2-(700)] with an initial rate of 0.05 s-1, while only 10% conversion could be achieved with 1d-Ph after 8 h of reaction (initial rate, 0.04 s-1; Figure S7).12 Third, while changing the substituents on the imido ligand, from a 2,6-i-Pr2C6H3 in 2a-Ph to an 1-adamantyl in 2b-Ph or a 2-CF3-C6H4 in 2c-Ph, provides a homologous mixture of surface complexes 1b-Ph/3b-Ph and 1c-Ph/3c-Ph (see Supporting Information, Figures S8−14), this significantly increases the catalytic performances of the catalysts (higher initial rates and TON). The adamantyl imido system is the most stable system for propene metathesis and only slowly deactivates, allowing 231 000 TON to be achieved in 1500 min {Figure 1, TON = f(t)}. Noteworthy, the order of efficiency is different in the self-metathesis of EO:  with 0.05 mol % of catalyst, the reaction mixture is equilibrated in 1 h and 30 min for [2b-Ph/SiO2-(700)] and [2c-Ph/SiO2-(700)], respectively, which is much faster than this obtained for [2a-Ph/SiO2-(700)] (3 h). Conversely, the initial turnover frequencies increase to 0.5 [2b-Ph/SiO2-(700)] and 1.2 [2c-Ph/SiO2-(700)] from 0.05 s-1 for [2a-Ph/SiO2-(700)]. Note that these performances are far better than those obtained for [(RF6O)2Mo(⋮NAr)(CHCMe2Ph)] (4) and 1d-Ph (Figure S7). In fact, EO is still equilibrated with only 0.02% of [2c-Ph/SiO2-(700)] in 8 h.

In conclusion, the silica supported 2,5-dimethylpyrrolyl family of silica supported Mo imido alkylidene complexes constitutes the most efficient d0 olefin metathesis developed so far, which can be prepared in two steps from the readily available triflate derivatives. We have shown in this preliminary account (1) that having methyl substituents on the pyrrolyl ligand in position 2 and 5 improves the stability of the system and (2) that the activity of the catalyst can be increased by tuning the imido substituents:  2-CF3-C6H4 (1c-Ph) is best for EO (cat. loading as low as 0.02%) and Ad (1b-Ph) for propene metathesis (TON = 231 000 in 1500 min), respectively. While we are currently looking for optimal imido ligands, we believe that these supported systems have already reached catalytic performances compatible with their use in fine chemical synthesis, and we are currently further exploring the scope of these systems.
