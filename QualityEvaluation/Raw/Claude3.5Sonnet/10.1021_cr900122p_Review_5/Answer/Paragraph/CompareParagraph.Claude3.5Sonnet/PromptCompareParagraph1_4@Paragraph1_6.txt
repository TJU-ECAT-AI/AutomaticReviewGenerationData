Key steps in the mechanism include:

   a) Carbene hydrides through α-H transfer

3. Olefin Metathesis: The key carbon-carbon bond breaking and forming steps correspond to those of olefin metathesis, involving [2+2] cycloaddition and cycloreversion processes [10.1002/anie.200602155].

4. Product Formation: The final steps involve hydrogenation of newly formed olefins to produce alkane products [10.1039/b823226a].

One-site mechanism: All steps occur within the coordination sphere of a single metal center, based on tantalum alkyl alkylidene intermediates [10.1021/cr900122p\_5].

Two-site mechanism: One site generates an alkene via β-H transfer, while another site forms a Ta-alkylidene hydride via α-H transfer [10.1021/cr900122p\_5].
