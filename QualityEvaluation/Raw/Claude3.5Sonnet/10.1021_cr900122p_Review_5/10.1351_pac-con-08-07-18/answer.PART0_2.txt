The mechanism of alkane metathesis catalyzed by silica-supported tantalum hydrides involves several key processes:

1. Alkane activation: The tantalum hydride species activates the C-H bond of the alkane, likely through a σ-bond metathesis mechanism.

2. Formation of intermediates: This activation leads to the formation of tantalum alkyl species and metallacarbenes.

3. Alkene formation: β-hydrogen elimination from the tantalum alkyl species produces alkenes as intermediates.

4. π-bond metathesis: The key C-C bond formation/cleavage step occurs through π-bond metathesis between the metallacarbene and the alkene intermediates. This step is crucial for redistributing carbon atoms.

5. Metallacyclobutane formation: The π-bond metathesis likely proceeds through metallacyclobutane intermediates, similar to alkene metathesis mechanisms.

6. Product formation: The metallacyclobutane intermediates then break down to form new alkanes and regenerate the tantalum carbene species.

This mechanism can operate through both one-site and two-site pathways:

- One-site mechanism: All steps occur on a single tantalum center.
- Two-site mechanism: Alkane activation and alkene formation occur on one site, while carbene formation and metathesis occur on another.

For linear alkanes, this process results in the formation of lower and higher homologs. For example, propane is converted into a mixture of mainly ethane and butane. The selectivity is influenced by the relative rates of alkane activation, alkene formation, and metathesis steps.

Cyclic alkanes can undergo similar transformations, potentially leading to ring opening or redistribution of carbon atoms in the ring.

The catalyst's performance is affected by factors such as:
- The nature of the silica support, which provides site isolation
- The reactivity of the tantalum hydride species
- The stability of the various intermediates

The system also shows interesting reactivity in related processes:
- Cross-metathesis of methane with higher alkanes, likely involving similar intermediates
- Non-oxidative coupling of methane, which involves C-H activation on tantalum carbene or carbyne intermediates, though this process is less efficient due to its endothermic nature.

Overall, the silica-supported tantalum hydride system demonstrates remarkable activity in alkane transformation, enabled by its ability to form and stabilize highly reactive intermediates through site isolation on the silica surface.

由二氧化硅支持的钽氢化物催化的烷烃复分解机理涉及几个关键过程：

1. 烷烃活化：钽氢化物物种通过σ键复分解机制活化烷烃的C-H键。

2. 中间体形成：这种活化导致钽烷基物种和金属卡宾的形成。

3. 烯烃形成：钽烷基物种的β-氢消除产生烯烃作为中间体。

4. π键复分解：关键的C-C键形成/断裂步骤通过金属卡宾和烯烃中间体之间的π键复分解发生。这一步骤对重新分配碳原子至关重要。

5. 金属环丁烷形成：π键复分解可能通过金属环丁烷中间体进行，类似于烯烃复分解机制。

6. 产物形成：金属环丁烷中间体随后分解形成新的烷烃并再生钽卡宾物种。

这种机制可以通过单中心和双中心途径进行：

- 单中心机制：所有步骤都在单个钽中心上发生。
- 双中心机制：烷烃活化和烯烃形成在一个位点发生，而卡宾形成和复分解在另一个位点发生。

对于直链烷烃，这个过程导致形成较低和较高的同系物。例如，丙烷被转化为主要由乙烷和丁烷组成的混合物。选择性受烷烃活化、烯烃形成和复分解步骤的相对速率影响。

环烷烃可以经历类似的转化，可能导致开环或环中碳原子的重新分配。

催化剂的性能受以下因素影响：
- 二氧化硅支持的性质，提供位点隔离
- 钽氢化物物种的反应性
- 各种中间体的稳定性

该系统还在相关过程中表现出有趣的反应性：
- 甲烷与高级烷烃的交叉复分解，可能涉及类似的中间体
- 甲烷的非氧化偶联，涉及钽卡宾或卡拜中间体上的C-H活化，尽管由于其吸热性质，这个过程效率较低。

总的来说，二氧化硅支持的钽氢化物系统在烷烃转化中表现出显著的活性，这得益于其在二氧化硅表面通过位点隔离形成和稳定高反应性中间体的能力。
