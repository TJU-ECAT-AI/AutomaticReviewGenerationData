      <English>
          The comprehensive mechanism of alkane metathesis catalyzed by silica-supported tantalum hydrides involves several key processes:

1. Alkane activation: The Ta-H species activates C-H bonds in alkanes to form Ta-alkyl intermediates.

2. Formation of intermediates: This activation leads to the formation of metallacarbene and alkene intermediates.

3. π-bond metathesis: The key C-C bond formation/cleavage step occurs through π-bond metathesis between the metallacarbene and the alkene intermediates. This likely proceeds through metallacyclobutane intermediates.

4. Product formation: The metallacyclobutane intermediates break down to form new alkanes and regenerate the tantalum carbene species.

The mechanism can operate through both one-site and two-site pathways:

- One-site mechanism: All steps occur on a single tantalum center.
- Two-site mechanism: Alkane activation and alkene formation occur on one site, while carbene formation and metathesis occur on another.

For linear alkanes, this process results in the formation of lower and higher homologs. For example, propane is converted into a mixture of mainly ethane and butane. 

Cyclic alkanes can undergo similar transformations, potentially leading to ring opening or redistribution of carbon atoms in the ring.

The selectivity and performance of the catalyst are influenced by factors such as:
- The nature of the silica support, which provides site isolation
- The reactivity of the tantalum hydride species
- The stability of the various intermediates
- The relative rates of alkane activation, alkene formation, and metathesis steps

Related processes include:
- Cross-metathesis of methane with higher alkanes, likely involving similar intermediates
- Non-oxidative coupling of methane, which involves C-H activation on tantalum carbene or carbyne intermediates, though this process is less efficient due to its endothermic nature.

The unique ability of supported Ta-H species to perform both C-H activation and olefin metathesis allows for the direct transformation of unactivated alkanes under relatively mild conditions (e.g., 150°C).

      <Chinese>
          由二氧化硅支持的钽氢化物催化的烷烃复分解的全面机理涉及几个关键过程：

1. 烷烃活化：Ta-H物种活化烷烃中的C-H键，形成Ta-烷基中间体。

2. 中间体形成：这种活化导致金属卡宾和烯烃中间体的形成。

3. π键复分解：关键的C-C键形成/断裂步骤通过金属卡宾和烯烃中间体之间的π键复分解发生。这可能通过金属环丁烷中间体进行。

4. 产物形成：金属环丁烷中间体分解形成新的烷烃并再生钽卡宾物种。

该机制可以通过单中心和双中心途径进行：

- 单中心机制：所有步骤都在单个钽中心上发生。
- 双中心机制：烷烃活化和烯烃形成在一个位点发生，而卡宾形成和复分解在另一个位点发生。

对于直链烷烃，这个过程导致形成较低和较高的同系物。例如，丙烷被转化为主要由乙烷和丁烷组成的混合物。

环烷烃可以经历类似的转化，可能导致开环或环中碳原子的重新分配。

催化剂的选择性和性能受以下因素影响：
- 二氧化硅支持的性质，提供位点隔离
- 钽氢化物物种的反应性
- 各种中间体的稳定性
- 烷烃活化、烯烃形成和复分解步骤的相对速率

相关过程包括：
- 甲烷与高级烷烃的交叉复分解，可能涉及类似的中间体
- 甲烷的非氧化偶联，涉及钽卡宾或卡拜中间体上的C-H活化，尽管由于其吸热性质，这个过程效率较低。

支持的Ta-H物种独特的能力，即同时进行C-H活化和烯烃复分解，允许在相对温和的条件下（如150°C）直接转化未活化的烷烃。
