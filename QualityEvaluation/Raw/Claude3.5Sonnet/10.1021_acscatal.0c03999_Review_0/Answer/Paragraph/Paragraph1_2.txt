The primary challenges in current propylene production methods include:

1. Insufficient supply to meet growing demand, particularly from traditional methods like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].
2. High energy intensity, with steam cracking being one of the most energy-demanding processes in the chemical industry [10.1002/ente.201900622].
3. Low selectivity and yields, especially for propylene production in steam cracking and FCC processes [10.1002/cctc.201200913].
4. Thermodynamic limitations in direct dehydrogenation, requiring high temperatures and leading to rapid catalyst deactivation due to coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].

Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) addresses these issues by:

1. Offering a more targeted approach to propylene production [10.1039/D0CY00586J].
2. Providing an exothermic process, potentially reducing energy requirements [10.1021/cr5002436].
3. Overcoming thermodynamic limitations, allowing for higher conversion rates [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].
4. Reducing coke formation and improving catalyst stability [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

However, ODHP presents its own set of advantages and limitations, particularly in the context of oxidant selection:

Advantages:
1. Lower reaction temperatures compared to traditional dehydrogenation [10.1021/ef0340716].
2. Higher per-pass conversion of propane [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].
3. Potential for CO2 utilization when used as an oxidant [10.1039/C5FD00062A].

Limitations:
1. Selectivity issues, especially when using oxygen as an oxidant, due to over-oxidation of propylene to carbon oxides [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010].
2. Safety concerns related to the use of oxygen in hydrocarbon mixtures [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].
3. Challenges in catalyst design to balance activity and selectivity [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

The specific challenges associated with using oxygen as an oxidant include:
1. High reactivity leading to uncontrolled combustion and low product yields [10.1002/cjce.5450480221].
2. Difficulty in controlling the reaction to prevent over-oxidation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].
3. Formation of highly reactive oxygen species on the catalyst surface, making selectivity control challenging [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010].

To address these issues, alternative oxidants such as CO2 and N2O are being explored:
1. CO2 as a milder oxidant can suppress deep oxidation and reduce coke formation [10.1039/D0CY00586J].
2. N2O has shown higher selectivity towards propene compared to oxygen [10.1021/jp809733s].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of ODHP technology represents a significant shift in propylene production strategies, addressing many of the shortcomings of traditional methods. The key to its success lies in finding the right balance between reactivity and selectivity, which heavily depends on oxidant choice and catalyst design. Future research efforts may focus on:

1. Advanced catalyst engineering: Developing multi-functional catalysts that can activate propane selectively while suppressing over-oxidation, possibly incorporating novel materials like hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN) [10.1002/anie.202002440].

2. Innovative reactor designs: Exploring membrane reactors or cyclic-operation reactors to better control reactant distribution and improve safety [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].

3. Hybrid processes: Combining ODHP with other technologies, such as membrane separation or in-situ product removal, to overcome equilibrium limitations and enhance selectivity.

4. Sustainable oxidants: Further investigation into CO2 utilization as an oxidant, potentially coupling ODHP with CO2 capture technologies to address both propylene production and greenhouse gas mitigation.

5. Process intensification: Developing more efficient heat management systems to harness the exothermic nature of ODHP while maintaining optimal reaction conditions.

These advancements could lead to more economically viable and environmentally friendly propylene production methods, potentially revolutionizing the petrochemical industry and contributing to a more sustainable chemical manufacturing landscape.

<Chinese>
目前丙烯生产方法的主要挑战包括：

1. 无法满足日益增长的需求，特别是传统的蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解（FCC）方法 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。
2. 高能耗，其中蒸汽裂解是化工行业最耗能的工艺之一 [10.1002/ente.201900622]。
3. 选择性和产率低，尤其是在蒸汽裂解和FCC工艺中的丙烯生产 [10.1002/cctc.201200913]。
4. 直接脱氢的热力学限制，需要高温并导致催化剂因结焦而快速失活 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。

丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）通过以下方式解决这些问题：

1. 提供更有针对性的丙烯生产方法 [10.1039/D0CY00586J]。
2. 提供放热过程，可能降低能源需求 [10.1021/cr5002436]。
3. 克服热力学限制，允许更高的转化率 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。
4. 减少结焦形成并提高催化剂稳定性 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

然而，ODHP在氧化剂选择方面也存在自身的优势和限制：

优势：
1. 与传统脱氢相比，反应温度更低 [10.1021/ef0340716]。
2. 丙烷单程转化率更高 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。
3. 当使用CO2作为氧化剂时，具有CO2利用的潜力 [10.1039/C5FD00062A]。

限制：
1. 选择性问题，特别是使用氧气作为氧化剂时，由于丙烯过度氧化为碳氧化物 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010]。
2. 在碳氢化合物混合物中使用氧气的安全问题 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。
3. 催化剂设计挑战，需要平衡活性和选择性 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

使用氧气作为氧化剂的具体挑战包括：
1. 高反应性导致不受控制的燃烧和低产品产率 [10.1002/cjce.5450480221]。
2. 难以控制反应以防止过度氧化 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。
3. 在催化剂表面形成高度活性的氧物种，使选择性控制变得具有挑战性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010]。

为解决这些问题，正在探索CO2和N2O等替代氧化剂：
1. CO2作为较温和的氧化剂可以抑制深度氧化并减少结焦形成 [10.1039/D0CY00586J]。
2. N2O与氧气相比表现出更高的丙烯选择性 [10.1021/jp809733s]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：ODHP技术的发展代表了丙烯生产策略的重大转变，解决了传统方法的许多缺点。其成功的关键在于找到反应性和选择性之间的适当平衡，这在很大程度上取决于氧化剂的选择和催化剂的设计。未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 先进的催化剂工程：开发可以选择性地活化丙烷同时抑制过度氧化的多功能催化剂，可能引入诸如六方氮化硼（h-BN）等新型材料 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。

2. 创新的反应器设计：探索膜反应器或循环操作反应器，以更好地控制反应物分布并提高安全性 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。

3. 混合工艺：将ODHP与其他技术（如膜分离或原位产品去除）相结合，以克服平衡限制并提高选择性。

4. 可持续氧化剂：进一步研究CO2作为氧化剂的利用，可能将ODHP与CO2捕获技术相结合，同时解决丙烯生产和温室气体减排问题。

5. 过程强化：开发更高效的热管理系统，以利用ODHP的放热性质，同时维持最佳反应条件。

这些进展可能导致更具经济可行性和环境友好性的丙烯生产方法，有潜力革新石化行业，并为更可持续的化学品制造格局做出贡献。
