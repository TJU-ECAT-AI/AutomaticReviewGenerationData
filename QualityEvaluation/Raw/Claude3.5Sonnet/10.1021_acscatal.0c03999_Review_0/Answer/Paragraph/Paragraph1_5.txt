<English>
The primary challenges in current propylene production methods include:

1. Insufficient supply to meet growing demand, particularly from traditional methods like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].
2. High energy intensity, with steam cracking being one of the most energy-demanding processes in the chemical industry [10.1002/ente.201900622].
3. Low selectivity and yields, especially for propylene production in steam cracking and FCC processes [10.1002/cctc.201200913].
4. Thermodynamic limitations in direct dehydrogenation, requiring high temperatures (around 1000K) for significant conversion [10.1002/cctc.201200913].
5. Rapid catalyst deactivation due to coke formation in traditional dehydrogenation processes [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].

Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) addresses these issues by:

1. Offering a more targeted approach to propylene production [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].
2. Providing an exothermic reaction, potentially reducing energy requirements [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].
3. Overcoming thermodynamic limitations, allowing for higher conversion rates [10.1021/cr5002436].
4. Inhibiting coke formation, thus reducing catalyst deactivation [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

However, ODHP also presents its own set of advantages and limitations, particularly in the context of oxidant selection:

Advantages:
1. Lower reaction temperatures compared to traditional dehydrogenation [10.1021/cr5002436].
2. Potential for higher conversion rates and yields [10.1021/cr5002436].
3. Reduced or eliminated coke deposition when using O2 as an oxidant [10.1021/cr5002436].

Limitations:
1. Over-oxidation of propylene to carbon oxides, reducing selectivity [10.1002/anie.202002440].
2. Safety concerns due to the flammability of propane-oxygen mixtures [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].
3. Difficulty in controlling selectivity at high conversion rates [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].

The specific challenges associated with using oxygen as an oxidant include:
1. Tendency to over-oxidize propylene, leading to the formation of CO and CO2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].
2. Potential for reaction runaway due to the highly exothermic nature of the process [10.1021/jp410750c].
3. Need for careful process control to maintain safe operating conditions [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].

To address these challenges, alternative oxidants such as CO2 and N2O have been explored:

1. CO2 as an oxidant:
   - Milder oxidation conditions, potentially improving selectivity [10.1039/c2ee22409g].
   - Reduces the risk of over-oxidation compared to oxygen [10.1039/c2ee22409g].
   - Allows for CO2 utilization, addressing environmental concerns [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

2. N2O as an oxidant:
   - Higher selectivity towards propene compared to air [10.1021/jp809733s].
   - Slower reoxidation of reduced catalyst species, suppressing consecutive oxidation [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of ODHP technology represents a significant step towards addressing the challenges in propylene production. While it offers promising advantages in terms of energy efficiency and overcoming thermodynamic limitations, the key to its success lies in balancing reactivity and selectivity. Future research efforts should focus on:

1. Advanced catalyst design: Developing catalysts that can selectively activate propane while suppressing over-oxidation reactions. This may involve exploring novel materials or multi-component systems that can effectively manage the oxidation process.

2. Innovative reactor designs: Investigating membrane reactors or cyclic-operation reactors to better control the distribution of reactants and products, potentially improving selectivity and safety.

3. Hybrid oxidant systems: Exploring the use of mixed oxidants or sequential oxidation processes that combine the benefits of different oxidants (e.g., O2 for high activity and CO2 for improved selectivity) while mitigating their individual drawbacks.

4. Process intensification: Developing integrated systems that combine ODHP with downstream separation and purification processes to improve overall efficiency and reduce capital costs.

5. In-situ characterization techniques: Advancing our understanding of the reaction mechanisms and catalyst behavior under operating conditions to guide further improvements in catalyst and process design.

By addressing these areas, ODHP technology has the potential to revolutionize propylene production, offering a more sustainable and efficient alternative to traditional methods while meeting the growing global demand for this crucial chemical feedstock.

<Chinese>
目前丙烯生产方法的主要挑战包括：

1. 传统方法如蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解（FCC）无法满足不断增长的需求 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。
2. 高能耗，其中蒸汽裂解是化工行业最耗能的工艺之一 [10.1002/ente.201900622]。
3. 低选择性和产率，尤其是在蒸汽裂解和FCC工艺中的丙烯生产 [10.1002/cctc.201200913]。
4. 直接脱氢的热力学限制，需要高温（约1000K）才能达到显著转化率 [10.1002/cctc.201200913]。
5. 传统脱氢工艺中因积碳导致的催化剂快速失活 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。

丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）通过以下方式解决了这些问题：

1. 提供更有针对性的丙烯生产方法 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。
2. 提供放热反应，可能降低能耗 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。
3. 克服热力学限制，实现更高的转化率 [10.1021/cr5002436]。
4. 抑制积碳形成，从而减少催化剂失活 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

然而，ODHP也带来了自身的优势和局限性，特别是在氧化剂选择方面：

优势：
1. 与传统脱氢相比，反应温度更低 [10.1021/cr5002436]。
2. 有潜力实现更高的转化率和产率 [10.1021/cr5002436]。
3. 使用O2作为氧化剂时，可以减少或消除积碳沉积 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

局限性：
1. 丙烯过度氧化为碳氧化物，降低选择性 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。
2. 由于丙烷-氧气混合物的可燃性，存在安全隐患 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。
3. 在高转化率下难以控制选择性 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。

使用氧气作为氧化剂的特定挑战包括：
1. 倾向于过度氧化丙烯，导致CO和CO2的形成 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。
2. 由于反应的高度放热性，可能导致反应失控 [10.1021/jp410750c]。
3. 需要仔细控制工艺以维持安全的操作条件 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。

为了解决这些挑战，研究人员探索了CO2和N2O等替代氧化剂：

1. CO2作为氧化剂：
   - 氧化条件更温和，可能提高选择性 [10.1039/c2ee22409g]。
   - 与氧气相比，降低了过度氧化的风险 [10.1039/c2ee22409g]。
   - 允许CO2利用，解决环境问题 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

2. N2O作为氧化剂：
   - 与空气相比，对丙烯的选择性更高 [10.1021/jp809733s]。
   - 还原态催化剂物种的再氧化速度较慢，抑制连续氧化 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：ODHP技术的发展代表着解决丙烯生产挑战的重要一步。虽然它在能源效率和克服热力学限制方面提供了令人期待的优势，但其成功的关键在于平衡反应活性和选择性。未来的研究努力应该集中在：

1. 先进催化剂设计：开发能选择性激活丙烷同时抑制过度氧化反应的催化剂。这可能涉及探索新型材料或多组分系统，以有效管理氧化过程。

2. 创新反应器设计：研究膜反应器或循环操作反应器，以更好地控制反应物和产物的分布，可能提高选择性和安全性。

3. 混合氧化剂系统：探索混合氧化剂或序列氧化过程的使用，结合不同氧化剂的优点（例如，O2的高活性和CO2的改进选择性），同时减轻它们各自的缺点。

4. 工艺强化：开发将ODHP与下游分离和纯化过程相结合的集成系统，以提高整体效率并降低资本成本。

5. 原位表征技术：提高我们对操作条件下反应机理和催化剂行为的理解，以指导催化剂和工艺设计的进一步改进。

通过解决这些领域的问题，ODHP技术有潜力彻底改变丙烯生产，为传统方法提供更可持续和高效的替代方案，同时满足这一关键化学原料不断增长的全球需求。
