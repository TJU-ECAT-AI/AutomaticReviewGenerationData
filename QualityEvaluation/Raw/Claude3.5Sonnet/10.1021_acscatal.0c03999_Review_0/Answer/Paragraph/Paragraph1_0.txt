<English>
The primary challenges in current propylene production methods include:

1. Insufficient supply to meet growing demand, especially from conventional methods like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].
2. High energy requirements, with steam cracking being one of the most energy-intensive processes in the chemical industry [10.1002/ente.201900622].
3. Low selectivity towards propylene in traditional processes, which often produce more ethylene [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009].
4. Thermodynamic limitations in direct dehydrogenation, requiring high temperatures and leading to coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].

Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) addresses these issues by:

1. Offering a more targeted approach to propylene production [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].
2. Providing an exothermic process, potentially reducing energy requirements [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].
3. Overcoming thermodynamic limitations, allowing for higher conversion rates [10.1021/cr5002436].
4. Reducing coke formation and the need for frequent catalyst regeneration [10.1002/ente.201900622].

However, ODHP presents its own set of advantages and limitations, particularly in the context of oxidant selection:

Advantages:
1. Higher conversion rates and potentially lower operating temperatures [10.1021/cr5002436].
2. Improved catalyst stability due to reduced coking [10.1126/science.aaf7885].
3. Potential for significant energy savings, estimated around 45% compared to conventional methods [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

Limitations:
1. Selectivity issues, especially when using oxygen as an oxidant, due to over-oxidation of propylene to COx [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].
2. Safety concerns related to handling mixtures of hydrocarbons and oxygen [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].
3. Challenges in catalyst design to balance activity and selectivity [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

The choice of oxidant plays a crucial role in ODHP. Oxygen, while providing high reactivity, faces challenges such as:
1. Over-oxidation of propylene, leading to low selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].
2. Safety concerns due to the risk of explosive mixtures [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].
3. Difficulty in controlling the reaction to prevent complete oxidation [10.1002/anie.202002440].

To address these issues, alternative oxidants like CO2 and N2O have been explored:
1. CO2 as a mild oxidant can suppress deep oxidation and reduce coke formation [10.1039/D0CY00586J].
2. N2O has shown higher selectivity towards propene compared to air, albeit with lower conversion rates [10.1021/jp809733s].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 

The development of ODHP represents a significant shift in propylene production strategies, aiming to address the limitations of conventional methods while meeting the growing demand for this crucial petrochemical feedstock. The key to unlocking the full potential of ODHP lies in finding the right balance between reactivity and selectivity, which is heavily influenced by oxidant choice and catalyst design.

Future research efforts may focus on:
1. Development of novel catalysts that can effectively activate propane while suppressing over-oxidation, potentially exploring multi-functional or hierarchical catalyst structures.
2. Innovative reactor designs, such as membrane reactors or cyclic-operation reactors, to better control the distribution of reactants and improve selectivity.
3. Exploration of hybrid processes that combine the advantages of different oxidants or integrate ODHP with other technologies like membrane separation for in-situ product removal.
4. Advanced process control strategies utilizing machine learning and artificial intelligence to optimize reaction conditions in real-time, maximizing yield and selectivity.
5. Life cycle assessments and techno-economic analyses to evaluate the overall sustainability and economic viability of ODHP compared to conventional methods, considering factors such as CO2 utilization and energy efficiency.

By addressing these challenges and opportunities, ODHP has the potential to revolutionize propylene production, offering a more sustainable and efficient route to meet the growing global demand for this essential chemical building block.

<Chinese>
目前丙烯生产方法的主要挑战包括：

1. 无法满足日益增长的需求，特别是传统的蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解（FCC）方法 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。
2. 高能耗需求，其中蒸汽裂解是化工行业中能耗最高的工艺之一 [10.1002/ente.201900622]。
3. 传统工艺对丙烯的选择性低，通常生产更多的乙烯 [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。
4. 直接脱氢的热力学限制，需要高温并导致积碳形成 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。

丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）通过以下方式解决这些问题：

1. 提供更有针对性的丙烯生产方法 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。
2. 提供放热过程，有可能降低能耗 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。
3. 克服热力学限制，实现更高的转化率 [10.1021/cr5002436]。
4. 减少积碳形成和频繁催化剂再生的需求 [10.1002/ente.201900622]。

然而，ODHP也存在自身的优势和局限性，特别是在氧化剂选择方面：

优势：
1. 更高的转化率和潜在的较低操作温度 [10.1021/cr5002436]。
2. 由于减少积碳而提高催化剂稳定性 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。
3. 与传统方法相比，能源节省潜力显著，估计可达45% [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

局限性：
1. 选择性问题，特别是使用氧气作为氧化剂时，由于丙烯过度氧化生成COx [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。
2. 处理烃类和氧气混合物相关的安全问题 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。
3. 催化剂设计面临平衡活性和选择性的挑战 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

氧化剂的选择在ODHP中起着关键作用。氧气虽然提供高反应性，但面临以下挑战：
1. 丙烯过度氧化，导致低选择性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。
2. 由于爆炸性混合物的风险而产生安全问题 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。
3. 难以控制反应以防止完全氧化 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。

为解决这些问题，已探索了CO2和N2O等替代氧化剂：
1. CO2作为温和氧化剂可以抑制深度氧化并减少积碳形成 [10.1039/D0CY00586J]。
2. N2O相比空气显示出对丙烯更高的选择性，但转化率较低 [10.1021/jp809733s]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：

ODHP的发展代表了丙烯生产策略的重大转变，旨在解决传统方法的局限性，同时满足这一关键石化原料日益增长的需求。释放ODHP全部潜力的关键在于找到反应性和选择性之间的正确平衡，这在很大程度上受氧化剂选择和催化剂设计的影响。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：
1. 开发新型催化剂，能够有效活化丙烷同时抑制过度氧化，可能探索多功能或分层催化剂结构。
2. 创新反应器设计，如膜反应器或循环操作反应器，以更好地控制反应物分布并提高选择性。
3. 探索混合工艺，结合不同氧化剂的优势或将ODHP与其他技术（如用于原位产品分离的膜分离）集成。
4. 利用机器学习和人工智能的先进过程控制策略，实时优化反应条件，最大化产率和选择性。
5. 生命周期评估和技术经济分析，评估ODHP相比传统方法的整体可持续性和经济可行性，考虑CO2利用和能源效率等因素。

通过解决这些挑战和机遇，ODHP有潜力革新丙烯生产，为满足这一重要化学建筑块不断增长的全球需求提供更可持续、更高效的途径。
