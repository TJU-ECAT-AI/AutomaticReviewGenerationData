<English>
The primary challenges in current propylene production methods include:

1. Insufficient supply to meet growing demand: Traditional methods like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) are unable to keep up with the increasing demand for propylene [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

2. Low selectivity and yield: Current processes often produce propylene as a by-product, resulting in low selectivity and yields [10.1002/cctc.201200913].

3. High energy consumption: Steam cracking, in particular, is highly energy-intensive, estimated to be the most energy-demanding process in the chemical industry [10.1002/ente.201900622].

4. Coke formation and catalyst deactivation: Traditional dehydrogenation processes suffer from rapid catalyst deactivation due to coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].

Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) addresses these issues by:

1. Providing a dedicated process for propylene production, potentially increasing supply [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

2. Offering improved thermodynamics: ODHP is exothermic, allowing for lower reaction temperatures and overcoming equilibrium limitations [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].

3. Reducing energy requirements: The exothermic nature of ODHP can lead to energy savings of up to 45% compared to conventional methods [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

4. Minimizing coke formation: The presence of oxygen helps inhibit coke deposition on catalyst surfaces [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

However, ODHP also presents its own set of advantages and limitations, particularly in the context of oxidant selection:

Advantages:
1. Higher conversion rates and potentially improved selectivity [10.1002/cctc.201200913].
2. Lower operating temperatures, reducing energy costs [10.1021/cr5002436].
3. Potential for CO2 utilization when using CO2 as an oxidant, addressing environmental concerns [10.1039/D0CY00586J].

Limitations:
1. Over-oxidation: When using oxygen, there's a risk of propylene being further oxidized to CO and CO2, reducing selectivity [10.1002/anie.202002440].
2. Safety concerns: Mixtures of hydrocarbons and oxygen can be explosive at certain compositions [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].
3. Catalyst development challenges: Finding catalysts that can selectively activate propane while suppressing complete oxidation remains difficult [10.1002/ente.201900622].

The choice of oxidant significantly impacts the process:

Oxygen as oxidant:
- Advantages: High reactivity, elimination of coke formation, lower reaction temperatures [10.1021/cr5002436].
- Challenges: Risk of over-oxidation, safety concerns, difficulty in controlling selectivity [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].

CO2 as oxidant:
- Advantages: Milder oxidation conditions, potential for improved selectivity, CO2 utilization [10.1039/D0CY00586J].
- Challenges: Lower oxidizing power, potential for reverse water-gas shift reaction, need for more active catalysts [10.1021/cr5002436].

Alternative oxidants like N2O have also been explored, offering potential improvements in selectivity but with lower conversion rates [10.1016/j.micromeso.2014.07.032].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of ODHP technology represents a significant shift in propylene production strategies. By addressing the limitations of traditional methods, ODHP offers a promising path towards more efficient and sustainable propylene production. However, the challenges associated with oxidant selection and catalyst design underscore the need for continued research and innovation.

Future research efforts may focus on:
1. Development of novel catalysts that can effectively balance reactivity and selectivity, particularly for CO2-based ODHP.
2. Exploration of hybrid processes combining different oxidants or reaction stages to optimize both conversion and selectivity.
3. Integration of ODHP processes with existing refinery operations to improve overall efficiency and reduce capital costs.
4. Investigation of membrane reactor technologies to control reactant distribution and enhance product selectivity.
5. Computational modeling and machine learning approaches to accelerate catalyst discovery and process optimization.

The successful commercialization of ODHP technology could significantly impact the propylene market, potentially alleviating supply shortages and reducing the carbon footprint of propylene production. However, this will require overcoming technical challenges and demonstrating economic viability on an industrial scale.

<Chinese>
目前丙烯生产方法的主要挑战包括：

1. 供应不足，无法满足日益增长的需求：传统方法如蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解（FCC）无法跟上丙烯需求的增长速度[10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

2. 选择性和产率低：当前工艺通常将丙烯作为副产品生产，导致选择性和产率低[10.1002/cctc.201200913]。

3. 能耗高：特别是蒸汽裂解，被认为是化工行业能耗最高的工艺[10.1002/ente.201900622]。

4. 积碳和催化剂失活：传统脱氢工艺因积碳导致催化剂快速失活[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。

丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）通过以下方式解决这些问题：

1. 提供专门的丙烯生产工艺，潜在地增加供应[10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

2. 改善热力学条件：ODHP是放热反应，允许在较低的反应温度下进行，克服了平衡限制[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。

3. 降低能耗：ODHP的放热特性可以使能耗比传统方法降低45%[10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

4. 最小化积碳：氧气的存在有助于抑制催化剂表面的积碳[10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

然而，ODHP也有自身的优势和局限性，特别是在氧化剂选择方面：

优势：
1. 更高的转化率和潜在的更好选择性[10.1002/cctc.201200913]。
2. 较低的操作温度，降低能源成本[10.1021/cr5002436]。
3. 使用CO2作为氧化剂时，有潜力利用CO2，解决环境问题[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。

局限性：
1. 过度氧化：使用氧气时，丙烯有被进一步氧化成CO和CO2的风险，降低选择性[10.1002/anie.202002440]。
2. 安全问题：碳氢化合物和氧气的混合物在某些成分下可能具有爆炸性[10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。
3. 催化剂开发挑战：找到能选择性活化丙烷同时抑制完全氧化的催化剂仍然困难[10.1002/ente.201900622]。

氧化剂的选择对工艺有显著影响：

氧气作为氧化剂：
- 优势：反应活性高，消除积碳，反应温度较低[10.1021/cr5002436]。
- 挑战：过度氧化风险，安全问题，选择性控制困难[10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。

CO2作为氧化剂：
- 优势：氧化条件较温和，有潜力改善选择性，CO2利用[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。
- 挑战：氧化能力较弱，可能发生逆水气变换反应，需要更活性的催化剂[10.1021/cr5002436]。

还探索了N2O等替代氧化剂，可能提高选择性但转化率较低[10.1016/j.micromeso.2014.07.032]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：ODHP技术的发展代表了丙烯生产策略的重大转变。通过解决传统方法的局限性，ODHP为更高效、更可持续的丙烯生产提供了一条有前途的道路。然而，与氧化剂选择和催化剂设计相关的挑战凸显了继续研究和创新的需求。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：
1. 开发能有效平衡反应性和选择性的新型催化剂，特别是用于基于CO2的ODHP。
2. 探索结合不同氧化剂或反应阶段的混合工艺，以优化转化率和选择性。
3. 将ODHP工艺与现有炼油厂操作集成，以提高整体效率并降低资本成本。
4. 研究膜反应器技术，以控制反应物分布并提高产品选择性。
5. 利用计算机模拟和机器学习方法加速催化剂发现和工艺优化。

ODHP技术的成功商业化可能对丙烯市场产生重大影响，潜在地缓解供应短缺并减少丙烯生产的碳足迹。然而，这将需要克服技术挑战并在工业规模上证明经济可行性。
