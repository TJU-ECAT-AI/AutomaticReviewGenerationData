<English>
The primary challenges in current propylene production methods include:

1. Insufficient supply to meet growing demand: Traditional methods like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) are unable to keep up with the increasing demand for propylene [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

2. Low selectivity and yields: Current processes produce propylene as a by-product, resulting in low selectivity and yields [10.1002/cctc.201200913].

3. High energy consumption: Steam cracking is estimated to be the most energy-demanding process in the chemical industry [10.1002/ente.201900622].

4. Thermodynamic limitations: Direct dehydrogenation of propane (DHP) is limited by thermodynamics, requiring high temperatures for significant conversion [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

5. Catalyst deactivation: Rapid coking leads to frequent catalyst regeneration in traditional methods [10.1134/S0023158418060010].

Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) addresses these issues by:

1. Providing an alternative, on-purpose production method to meet demand [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

2. Offering improved reaction efficiency due to favorable thermodynamics (exothermic, lower reaction temperatures) [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

3. Enhancing catalyst stability by preventing coke formation [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

4. Potentially reducing energy consumption by up to 45% compared to conventional methods [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

5. Overcoming thermodynamic limitations of traditional dehydrogenation [10.1021/cr5002436].

However, ODHP presents its own set of advantages and limitations, particularly in the context of oxidant selection:

Advantages:
1. Higher conversion rates and potentially improved selectivity [10.1021/cr5002436].
2. Lower energy requirements due to exothermic nature [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].
3. Potential for CO2 utilization when using CO2 as an oxidant [10.1039/D0CY00586J].

Limitations:
1. Over-oxidation of propylene to carbon oxides, reducing selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].
2. Safety concerns due to the use of oxygen at high temperatures [10.1039/c2ee22409g].
3. Balancing reactivity and selectivity remains challenging [10.1039/D0CY00586J].

The specific challenges associated with using oxygen as an oxidant include:
1. Formation of explosive mixtures with propane, requiring careful process control [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].
2. Difficulty in controlling selectivity due to oxygen's high reactivity [10.1021/jp410750c].
3. Heat management issues due to the highly exothermic nature of the reaction [10.1002/cjce.5450520615].

To address these challenges, alternative oxidants like CO2 and N2O are being explored:
1. CO2 as a mild oxidant can suppress deep oxidation and reduce coke formation [10.1039/D0CY00586J].
2. N2O shows higher selectivity towards propene compared to air, albeit with lower conversion rates [10.1021/jp809733s].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of ODHP technologies represents a significant shift in propylene production strategies, addressing many of the limitations of traditional methods. The key to unlocking the full potential of ODHP lies in optimizing the balance between reactivity and selectivity through careful oxidant selection and catalyst design.

Future research efforts might focus on:
1. Developing novel catalysts that can selectively activate propane while suppressing over-oxidation, potentially exploring hybrid materials or advanced nanostructures.
2. Investigating alternative oxidants or oxidant mixtures that combine the benefits of different species (e.g., oxygen's reactivity with CO2's selectivity).
3. Exploring innovative reactor designs, such as membrane reactors or cyclic-operation systems, to better control reactant distribution and heat management.
4. Integrating ODHP processes with CO2 capture and utilization technologies to create more sustainable and economically viable production routes.
5. Advancing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand reaction mechanisms and catalyst behavior under operating conditions, enabling more targeted improvements.

These research directions could lead to breakthroughs in propylene production, potentially revolutionizing the petrochemical industry by offering more efficient, selective, and environmentally friendly processes.

<Chinese>
目前丙烯生产方法的主要挑战包括：

1. 供应不足以满足不断增长的需求：传统方法如蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解（FCC）无法跟上丙烯需求的增长速度[10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

2. 选择性和产率低：现有工艺将丙烯作为副产品生产，导致选择性和产率低[10.1002/cctc.201200913]。

3. 能耗高：蒸汽裂解被认为是化工行业中能耗最高的工艺[10.1002/ente.201900622]。

4. 热力学限制：丙烷直接脱氢（DHP）受热力学限制，需要高温才能实现显著转化[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

5. 催化剂失活：传统方法中快速积碳导致需要频繁再生催化剂[10.1134/S0023158418060010]。

丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）通过以下方式解决了这些问题：

1. 提供了一种替代性的、专门的生产方法来满足需求[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

2. 由于热力学有利（放热，反应温度较低），提供了改进的反应效率[10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

3. 通过防止积碳提高了催化剂稳定性[10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

4. 与传统方法相比，可能减少高达45%的能耗[10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

5. 克服了传统脱氢的热力学限制[10.1021/cr5002436]。

然而，ODHP也存在自身的优势和局限性，特别是在氧化剂选择方面：

优势：
1. 更高的转化率和可能改善的选择性[10.1021/cr5002436]。
2. 由于放热性质，能耗较低[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。
3. 使用CO2作为氧化剂时，可能实现CO2利用[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。

局限性：
1. 丙烯过度氧化为碳氧化物，降低选择性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。
2. 在高温下使用氧气存在安全隐患[10.1039/c2ee22409g]。
3. 平衡反应性和选择性仍然具有挑战性[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。

使用氧气作为氧化剂的具体挑战包括：
1. 与丙烷形成爆炸性混合物，需要仔细控制工艺[10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。
2. 由于氧气的高反应性，难以控制选择性[10.1021/jp410750c]。
3. 由于反应的强放热性质，存在热管理问题[10.1002/cjce.5450520615]。

为了解决这些挑战，正在探索使用CO2和N2O等替代氧化剂：
1. CO2作为温和的氧化剂可以抑制深度氧化并减少积碳[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。
2. 与空气相比，N2O对丙烯的选择性更高，但转化率较低[10.1021/jp809733s]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：ODHP技术的发展代表了丙烯生产策略的重大转变，解决了传统方法的许多限制。充分发挥ODHP潜力的关键在于通过谨慎的氧化剂选择和催化剂设计来优化反应性和选择性之间的平衡。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：
1. 开发新型催化剂，可以选择性地活化丙烷同时抑制过度氧化，可能探索混合材料或先进纳米结构。
2. 研究替代氧化剂或氧化剂混合物，结合不同物种的优势（例如，氧气的反应性和CO2的选择性）。
3. 探索创新的反应器设计，如膜反应器或循环操作系统，以更好地控制反应物分布和热管理。
4. 将ODHP工艺与CO2捕获和利用技术相结合，创造更可持续和经济可行的生产路线。
5. 推进原位表征技术，以更好地理解操作条件下的反应机理和催化剂行为，实现更有针对性的改进。

这些研究方向可能会带来丙烯生产的突破，通过提供更高效、更具选择性和更环保的工艺，潜在地革新石化工业。
