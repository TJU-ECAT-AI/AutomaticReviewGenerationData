<English>
The primary challenges in current propylene production methods include:

1. Insufficient supply from traditional cracking processes to meet growing demand [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80992-X].
2. High energy consumption in processes like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking of naphtha [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551].
3. Limited selectivity and yield of propylene in conventional methods [10.1002/cctc.201200913].
4. Thermodynamic limitations and high energy requirements of direct dehydrogenation of propane (DDP) [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].
5. Catalyst deactivation due to coking in traditional dehydrogenation processes [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].

Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) addresses these issues by:

1. Providing an alternative, on-purpose production method to meet increasing demand [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].
2. Offering improved reaction efficiency due to favorable thermodynamics (exothermic reaction, lower temperatures) [10.126/science.aaf7885].
3. Enhancing catalyst stability by preventing coke formation [10.126/science.aaf7885].
4. Potentially reducing energy consumption by up to 45% compared to conventional methods [10.126/science.aaf7885].
5. Overcoming thermodynamic limitations of non-oxidative dehydrogenation [10.1021/cr5002436].

However, ODHP also presents its own set of advantages and limitations, particularly in the context of oxidant selection:

Advantages:
1. Higher conversion rates and yields compared to traditional dehydrogenation methods [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].
2. Potential for lower operating temperatures, especially when using CO2 as an oxidant [10.1016/S0926-860X(03)00368-5].
3. Opportunity for CO2 utilization, addressing environmental concerns [10.1039/D0CY00586J].

Limitations:
1. Over-oxidation of propylene to carbon oxides (COx), resulting in low selectivity when using oxygen as an oxidant [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].
2. Balancing reactivity and selectivity remains challenging, especially at high conversions [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].
3. Safety concerns due to the potential formation of explosive mixtures when using oxygen [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].

The specific challenges associated with using oxygen as an oxidant include:
1. Higher risk of deep oxidation, leading to the formation of COx instead of propylene [10.1002/ente.201900622].
2. Difficulty in controlling the reaction to prevent over-oxidation [10.1002/cjce.5450480221].
3. Formation of highly reactive oxygen species on the catalyst surface, making it challenging to control selectivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010].

To address these issues, alternative oxidants such as CO2 and N2O have been explored:

1. CO2 as an oxidant:
   - Suppresses deep oxidation, leading to higher selectivity for propylene [10.1021/ef0340716].
   - Reduces catalyst deactivation and increases energy savings [10.1021/ef0340716].
   - However, it may result in lower activity compared to oxygen [10.1039/D0CY00586J].

2. N2O as an oxidant:
   - Offers higher selectivity towards propene compared to air [10.1021/jp809733s].
   - However, it results in much lower propane conversion compared to air [10.1021/jp809733s].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of ODHP technology represents a significant step towards addressing the growing demand for propylene while potentially offering environmental and economic benefits. The key to realizing the full potential of ODHP lies in optimizing the balance between reactivity and selectivity. Future research efforts should focus on:

1. Advanced catalyst design: Developing novel catalysts that can selectively activate propane while suppressing over-oxidation, possibly through the use of multi-functional or hierarchical structures.

2. Innovative reactor designs: Exploring membrane reactors or cyclic-operation reactors to better control reactant distribution and improve overall process efficiency.

3. Hybrid oxidant systems: Investigating the use of mixed oxidants (e.g., CO2 + O2) or sequential oxidant introduction to combine the benefits of different oxidants while mitigating their individual drawbacks.

4. In-situ characterization techniques: Developing and applying advanced spectroscopic methods to gain deeper insights into the reaction mechanisms and catalyst behavior under realistic operating conditions.

5. Process intensification: Integrating ODHP with other processes, such as CO2 capture or syngas production, to create more sustainable and economically viable industrial systems.

By addressing these challenges and opportunities, ODHP has the potential to revolutionize propylene production, offering a more sustainable and efficient alternative to traditional methods.

<Chinese>
当前丙烯生产方法的主要挑战包括：

1. 传统裂解工艺无法满足日益增长的需求 [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80992-X]。
2. 蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解等工艺能耗高 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551]。
3. 常规方法的丙烯选择性和产率有限 [10.1002/cctc.201200913]。
4. 丙烷直接脱氢（DDP）存在热力学限制和高能耗问题 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。
5. 传统脱氢工艺中催化剂因结焦而失活 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。

丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）通过以下方式解决这些问题：

1. 提供一种替代性的定向生产方法来满足不断增长的需求 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。
2. 由于热力学有利（放热反应，较低温度），反应效率得到提高 [10.126/science.aaf7885]。
3. 通过防止结焦提高催化剂稳定性 [10.126/science.aaf7885]。
4. 与传统方法相比，可能降低高达45%的能耗 [10.126/science.aaf7885]。
5. 克服了非氧化脱氢的热力学限制 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

然而，ODHP也存在自身的优势和局限性，特别是在氧化剂选择方面：

优势：
1. 与传统脱氢方法相比，具有更高的转化率和产率 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。
2. 可能实现更低的操作温度，尤其是使用CO2作为氧化剂时 [10.1016/S0926-860X(03)00368-5]。
3. 为CO2利用提供机会，解决环境问题 [10.1039/D0CY00586J]。

局限性：
1. 使用氧气作为氧化剂时，丙烯过度氧化为碳氧化物（COx），导致选择性低 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。
2. 平衡反应性和选择性仍然具有挑战性，特别是在高转化率下 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。
3. 使用氧气时可能形成爆炸性混合物，存在安全隐患 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。

使用氧气作为氧化剂的具体挑战包括：
1. 深度氧化的风险更高，导致形成COx而非丙烯 [10.1002/ente.201900622]。
2. 难以控制反应以防止过度氧化 [10.1002/cjce.5450480221]。
3. 在催化剂表面形成高活性氧物种，使选择性控制变得困难 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010]。

为解决这些问题，研究人员探索了CO2和N2O等替代氧化剂：

1. CO2作为氧化剂：
   - 抑制深度氧化，提高丙烯选择性 [10.1021/ef0340716]。
   - 减少催化剂失活并提高能源效率 [10.1021/ef0340716]。
   - 然而，与氧气相比，其活性可能较低 [10.1039/D0CY00586J]。

2. N2O作为氧化剂：
   - 与空气相比，提供更高的丙烯选择性 [10.1021/jp809733s]。
   - 但与空气相比，丙烷转化率大大降低 [10.1021/jp809733s]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：ODHP技术的发展代表着满足日益增长的丙烯需求的重要一步，同时可能带来环境和经济效益。充分发挥ODHP潜力的关键在于优化反应性和选择性之间的平衡。未来研究工作应重点关注：

1. 先进催化剂设计：开发可选择性活化丙烷同时抑制过度氧化的新型催化剂，可能通过使用多功能或分级结构实现。

2. 创新反应器设计：探索膜反应器或循环操作反应器，以更好地控制反应物分布并提高整体工艺效率。

3. 混合氧化剂系统：研究混合氧化剂（如CO2 + O2）的使用或顺序引入氧化剂，以结合不同氧化剂的优势，同时减轻各自的缺点。

4. 原位表征技术：开发并应用先进的光谱方法，在实际操作条件下深入了解反应机理和催化剂行为。

5. 工艺强化：将ODHP与其他工艺（如CO2捕获或合成气生产）集成，创建更可持续和经济可行的工业系统。

通过解决这些挑战和机遇，ODHP有潜力革新丙烯生产，为传统方法提供更可持续和高效的替代方案。
