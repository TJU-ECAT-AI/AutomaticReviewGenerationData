<English>
The primary challenges in current propylene production methods include:

1. Insufficient supply to meet growing demand, particularly from traditional methods like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

2. High energy intensity, with steam cracking being one of the most energy-demanding processes in the chemical industry [10.1002/ente.201900622].

3. Low selectivity towards propylene in conventional processes, with steam cracking producing more ethylene than propylene [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009].

4. Thermodynamic limitations and high temperatures required for direct dehydrogenation of propane (DHP) [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

5. Rapid catalyst deactivation due to coke formation in DHP [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].

Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) addresses these issues by:

1. Offering a more targeted approach to propylene production [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

2. Providing an exothermic process, potentially reducing energy requirements [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].

3. Overcoming thermodynamic limitations of conventional dehydrogenation [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].

4. Allowing operation at lower temperatures, which can improve selectivity [10.1016/S0926-860X(03)00368-5].

5. Reducing or eliminating coke formation on catalysts [10.1021/cr5002436].

However, ODHP also presents its own set of advantages and limitations, particularly in the context of oxidant selection:

Advantages:
1. Higher conversion rates compared to non-oxidative methods [10.1021/cr5002436].
2. Potential for improved energy efficiency [10.1126/science.aaf7885].
3. Ability to use CO2 as an oxidant, addressing environmental concerns [10.1039/D0CY00586J].

Limitations:
1. Risk of over-oxidation when using oxygen, leading to the formation of CO and CO2 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010].
2. Challenges in balancing reactivity and selectivity [10.1002/ente.201900622].
3. Safety concerns when using oxygen due to potential explosive mixtures [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].

The specific challenges associated with using oxygen as an oxidant include:
1. Difficulty in controlling selectivity due to its high reactivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010].
2. Formation of highly reactive oxygen species on catalyst surfaces [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010].
3. Need for careful process control to avoid runaway reactions [10.1002/anie.202002440].

To address these issues, alternative oxidants like CO2 and N2O are being explored, offering milder oxidation conditions and potentially improved selectivity [10.1039/C5FD00062A] [10.1016/j.micromeso.2014.07.032].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of ODHP technology represents a significant shift in propylene production strategies, addressing many of the limitations of conventional methods. The key to realizing the full potential of ODHP lies in optimizing the balance between reactivity and selectivity, which is heavily influenced by oxidant choice and catalyst design.

Future research directions may focus on:
1. Development of novel catalysts that can effectively activate propane while suppressing over-oxidation, particularly when using oxygen as an oxidant.
2. Exploration of hybrid systems combining different oxidants (e.g., O2 and CO2) to leverage their respective advantages.
3. Investigation of advanced reactor designs, such as membrane reactors or cyclic-operation reactors, to better control reactant distribution and improve overall process efficiency.
4. Integration of ODHP processes with CO2 capture and utilization technologies to create more sustainable and environmentally friendly production routes.
5. Computational modeling and machine learning approaches to accelerate catalyst discovery and optimization for ODHP reactions.

These advancements could potentially lead to more efficient, selective, and sustainable propylene production methods, addressing the growing demand while minimizing environmental impact.

<Chinese>
当前丙烯生产方法的主要挑战包括：

1. 供应不足以满足不断增长的需求，特别是从蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解（FCC）等传统方法 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

2. 能源强度高，蒸汽裂解是化工行业能耗最高的过程之一 [10.1002/ente.201900622]。

3. 常规工艺对丙烯的选择性低，蒸汽裂解产生的乙烯多于丙烯 [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。

4. 丙烷直接脱氢（DHP）存在热力学限制，需要高温 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

5. DHP中由于积碳导致催化剂快速失活 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。

丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）通过以下方式解决这些问题：

1. 提供更有针对性的丙烯生产方法 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

2. 提供放热过程，可能降低能源需求 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。

3. 克服传统脱氢的热力学限制 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。

4. 允许在较低温度下操作，可以提高选择性 [10.1016/S0926-860X(03)00368-5]。

5. 减少或消除催化剂上的积碳 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

然而，ODHP也带来了自身的优势和局限性，特别是在氧化剂选择方面：

优势：
1. 与非氧化方法相比，转化率更高 [10.1021/cr5002436]。
2. 有潜力提高能源效率 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。
3. 能够使用CO2作为氧化剂，解决环境问题 [10.1039/D0CY00586J]。

局限性：
1. 使用氧气时存在过度氧化的风险，导致CO和CO2的形成 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010]。
2. 平衡反应性和选择性的挑战 [10.1002/ente.201900622]。
3. 使用氧气时由于潜在的爆炸性混合物而存在安全隐患 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。

使用氧气作为氧化剂的具体挑战包括：
1. 由于其高反应性，难以控制选择性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010]。
2. 在催化剂表面形成高度活性的氧物种 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010]。
3. 需要仔细控制过程以避免失控反应 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。

为解决这些问题，正在探索CO2和N2O等替代氧化剂，提供更温和的氧化条件和可能提高的选择性 [10.1039/C5FD00062A] [10.1016/j.micromeso.2014.07.032]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：ODHP技术的发展代表了丙烯生产策略的重大转变，解决了传统方法的许多局限性。实现ODHP全部潜力的关键在于优化反应性和选择性之间的平衡，这在很大程度上受氧化剂选择和催化剂设计的影响。

未来的研究方向可能集中在：
1. 开发新型催化剂，能够有效活化丙烷的同时抑制过度氧化，特别是在使用氧气作为氧化剂时。
2. 探索结合不同氧化剂（如O2和CO2）的混合系统，以利用它们各自的优势。
3. 研究先进的反应器设计，如膜反应器或循环操作反应器，以更好地控制反应物分布并提高整体过程效率。
4. 将ODHP过程与CO2捕获和利用技术相结合，创造更可持续和环境友好的生产路线。
5. 应用计算机模拟和机器学习方法，加速ODHP反应的催化剂发现和优化。

这些进展可能会导致更高效、更有选择性和更可持续的丙烯生产方法，在满足不断增长的需求的同时最大限度地减少环境影响。
