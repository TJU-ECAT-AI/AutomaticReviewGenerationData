<English>
The primary challenges in current propylene production methods include:

1. Insufficient supply to meet growing demand: Traditional methods like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) are unable to keep up with the rapidly increasing demand for propylene [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

2. High energy consumption: Current processes, especially steam cracking, are highly energy-intensive, with steam cracking being the most energy-demanding process in the chemical industry [10.1002/ente.201900622].

3. Low selectivity and yields: Conventional methods produce propylene as a by-product, resulting in low selectivity and yields [10.1002/cctc.201200913].

4. Thermodynamic limitations: Direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) faces thermodynamic constraints, requiring high temperatures for significant propylene yields [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

5. Catalyst deactivation: Coking is a major issue in dehydrogenation processes, leading to rapid catalyst deactivation [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009].

Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) addresses these issues by:

1. Providing an alternative, on-purpose production method to meet demand [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

2. Offering improved energy efficiency through exothermic reactions [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009].

3. Potentially increasing propylene selectivity and yields [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010].

4. Overcoming thermodynamic limitations of conventional dehydrogenation [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].

5. Reducing catalyst deactivation by inhibiting coke formation [10.1021/jp410750c].

However, ODHP also presents its own set of advantages and limitations, particularly in the context of oxidant selection:

Advantages:
1. Lower reaction temperatures compared to thermal cracking processes [10.1021/cr5002436].
2. Higher conversion rates and yields compared to traditional dehydrogenation methods [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].
3. Potential for CO2 utilization when using CO2 as an oxidant [10.1039/D0CY00586J].

Limitations:
1. Over-oxidation of propylene to carbon oxides, reducing selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].
2. Safety concerns due to the use of oxygen at high temperatures [10.1039/c2ee22409g].
3. Balancing reactivity and selectivity remains challenging [10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3].

The selection of oxidants plays a crucial role in ODHP:

Oxygen as oxidant:
- Advantages: Higher propane conversion, lower reaction temperatures, elimination of coke formation [10.1021/jp809733s].
- Challenges: Risk of over-oxidation, safety concerns due to explosive mixtures, difficulty in controlling selectivity [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].

CO2 as oxidant:
- Advantages: Milder oxidation conditions, improved selectivity, CO2 utilization [10.1039/C5FD00062A].
- Challenges: Lower oxidizing power, potential for reverse water-gas shift reaction, need for more active catalysts [10.1021/cr5002436].

Alternative oxidants like N2O have also been explored, offering improved selectivity but lower conversion rates compared to oxygen [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of ODHP technology represents a significant shift in propylene production, addressing many of the limitations of conventional methods. The key to realizing its full potential lies in optimizing the balance between reactivity and selectivity. Future research should focus on:

1. Advanced catalyst design: Developing multi-functional catalysts that can activate propane selectively while suppressing over-oxidation reactions.

2. Novel reactor configurations: Exploring membrane reactors or cyclic-operation reactors to better control reactant distribution and improve selectivity.

3. Hybrid processes: Investigating the combination of different oxidants or the integration of ODHP with other processes to maximize efficiency and selectivity.

4. In-situ characterization techniques: Developing methods to study the reaction mechanisms and catalyst behavior under real operating conditions to guide process optimization.

5. Process intensification: Exploring microreactor technology or structured catalysts to enhance heat and mass transfer, potentially improving selectivity and yield.

6. Artificial intelligence and machine learning: Utilizing these tools for catalyst discovery, process optimization, and predictive modeling of complex reaction networks.

By addressing these areas, ODHP technology could potentially revolutionize propylene production, offering a more sustainable and efficient alternative to current methods while meeting the growing global demand for this crucial chemical feedstock.

<Chinese>
目前丙烯生产方法的主要挑战包括：

1. 供应不足以满足日益增长的需求：传统方法如蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解（FCC）无法跟上丙烯需求的快速增长 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

2. 高能耗：当前工艺，尤其是蒸汽裂解，能耗很高，蒸汽裂解是化工行业能耗最高的工艺 [10.1002/ente.201900622]。

3. 选择性和产率低：传统方法将丙烯作为副产品生产，导致选择性和产率低 [10.1002/cctc.201200913]。

4. 热力学限制：丙烷直接脱氢（PDH）面临热力学限制，需要高温才能获得显著的丙烯产率 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

5. 催化剂失活：结焦是脱氢过程中的一个主要问题，导致催化剂快速失活 [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。

丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）通过以下方式解决这些问题：

1. 提供一种满足需求的替代性定向生产方法 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

2. 通过放热反应提高能源效率 [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。

3. 潜在提高丙烯选择性和产率 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010]。

4. 克服传统脱氢的热力学限制 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。

5. 通过抑制结焦减少催化剂失活 [10.1021/jp410750c]。

然而，ODHP也存在自身的优势和局限性，特别是在氧化剂选择方面：

优势：
1. 与热裂解相比，反应温度更低 [10.1021/cr5002436]。
2. 与传统脱氢方法相比，转化率和产率更高 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。
3. 使用CO2作为氧化剂时，具有CO2利用的潜力 [10.1039/D0CY00586J]。

局限性：
1. 丙烯过度氧化生成碳氧化物，降低选择性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。
2. 在高温下使用氧气存在安全隐患 [10.1039/c2ee22409g]。
3. 平衡反应性和选择性仍然具有挑战性 [10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3]。

氧化剂的选择在ODHP中起着关键作用：

氧气作为氧化剂：
- 优势：丙烷转化率高，反应温度低，消除结焦形成 [10.1021/jp809733s]。
- 挑战：过度氧化风险，易形成爆炸性混合物引起安全隐患，选择性控制困难 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。

CO2作为氧化剂：
- 优势：氧化条件温和，选择性提高，CO2利用 [10.1039/C5FD00062A]。
- 挑战：氧化能力较弱，可能发生逆水煤气反应，需要更活跃的催化剂 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

还探索了N2O等替代氧化剂，与氧气相比，它们提供了更好的选择性，但转化率较低 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：ODHP技术的发展代表了丙烯生产的重大转变，解决了传统方法的许多局限性。实现其全部潜力的关键在于优化反应性和选择性之间的平衡。未来研究应关注：

1. 先进催化剂设计：开发多功能催化剂，可以选择性地活化丙烷，同时抑制过度氧化反应。

2. 新型反应器配置：探索膜反应器或循环操作反应器，以更好地控制反应物分布并提高选择性。

3. 混合工艺：研究不同氧化剂的组合或ODHP与其他工艺的集成，以最大化效率和选择性。

4. 原位表征技术：开发在实际操作条件下研究反应机理和催化剂行为的方法，以指导工艺优化。

5. 过程强化：探索微反应器技术或结构化催化剂，以增强传热和传质，可能提高选择性和产率。

6. 人工智能和机器学习：利用这些工具进行催化剂发现、工艺优化和复杂反应网络的预测建模。

通过解决这些领域的问题，ODHP技术可能彻底革新丙烯生产，为当前方法提供更可持续和高效的替代方案，同时满足这一关键化学原料不断增长的全球需求。
