<English>
The primary challenges in current propylene production methods include:

1. Insufficient supply to meet growing demand: Traditional methods like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) are unable to keep up with the increasing demand for propylene [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

2. High energy consumption: Steam cracking is estimated to be the most energy-demanding process in the chemical industry, consuming around 3 × 10^18 J per year globally [10.1002/ente.201900622].

3. Low selectivity and yield: Current methods produce propylene as a by-product, resulting in low selectivity and yields [10.1002/cctc.201200913].

4. Thermodynamic limitations: Direct dehydrogenation of propane (DHP) is endothermic and requires high temperatures, leading to thermodynamic constraints [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].

5. Catalyst deactivation: Rapid coke formation on catalysts in DHP processes leads to frequent regeneration needs [10.1134/S0023158418060010].

Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) addresses these issues by:

1. Providing an alternative, targeted production method for propylene [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

2. Offering improved energy efficiency through exothermic reactions [10.1021/cr5002436].

3. Potentially increasing propylene yield and selectivity [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

4. Overcoming thermodynamic limitations of traditional dehydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].

5. Reducing coke formation and improving catalyst stability [10.1134/S0023158418060010].

However, ODHP also presents its own set of advantages and limitations, particularly in the context of oxidant selection:

Advantages:
1. Lower reaction temperatures compared to traditional methods [10.1021/cr5002436].
2. Higher conversion rates and yields [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].
3. Potential for CO2 utilization as an oxidant, addressing environmental concerns [10.1039/c2ee22409g].

Limitations:
1. Over-oxidation of propylene to carbon oxides, reducing selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].
2. Safety concerns due to the use of oxygen at high temperatures [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].
3. Challenges in catalyst development for optimal performance [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

The specific challenges associated with using oxygen as an oxidant include:
1. Difficulty in controlling selectivity due to oxygen's high reactivity [10.1002/anie.202002440].
2. Risk of forming explosive mixtures with hydrocarbons [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].
3. Need for efficient heat management due to the highly exothermic nature of the reaction [10.1021/ef0340716].

To address these issues, alternative oxidants such as CO2 and N2O are being explored. CO2, as a milder oxidant, offers potential advantages in selectivity and safety, but may result in lower activity compared to oxygen [10.1039/D0CY00586J]. N2O has shown promise in improving selectivity while reducing over-oxidation [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of ODHP technology represents a significant step towards addressing the growing demand for propylene while potentially improving energy efficiency and reducing environmental impact. The key to realizing the full potential of ODHP lies in overcoming the selectivity-conversion trade-off, particularly when using oxygen as an oxidant.

Future research efforts might focus on:

1. Novel catalyst designs that can effectively activate propane while suppressing over-oxidation pathways.
2. Hybrid approaches combining different oxidants or reaction steps to optimize both conversion and selectivity.
3. Advanced reactor designs, such as membrane reactors or cyclic-operation systems, to better control reactant distribution and product formation.
4. Integration of ODHP processes with CO2 capture and utilization technologies to create more sustainable production methods.
5. Development of in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the reaction mechanisms and catalyst behavior under operating conditions.

These advancements could potentially lead to more efficient, selective, and environmentally friendly propylene production processes, helping to bridge the gap between supply and demand while reducing the industry's carbon footprint.

<Chinese>
目前丙烯生产方法的主要挑战包括：

1. 供应不足以满足日益增长的需求：传统方法如蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解(FCC)无法跟上丙烯需求的增长 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

2. 高能耗：蒸汽裂解被认为是化工行业中最耗能的过程，全球每年消耗约3×10^18焦耳 [10.1002/ente.201900622]。

3. 低选择性和产率：当前方法将丙烯作为副产品生产，导致选择性和产率较低 [10.1002/cctc.201200913]。

4. 热力学限制：丙烷直接脱氢(DHP)是吸热反应，需要高温，导致热力学限制 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。

5. 催化剂失活：DHP过程中催化剂上快速形成焦炭，导致需要频繁再生 [10.1134/S0023158418060010]。

丙烷氧化脱氢(ODHP)通过以下方式解决这些问题：

1. 提供一种针对性的丙烯生产替代方法 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

2. 通过放热反应提高能源效率 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

3. 潜在提高丙烯产率和选择性 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

4. 克服传统脱氢的热力学限制 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。

5. 减少焦炭形成并提高催化剂稳定性 [10.1134/S0023158418060010]。

然而，ODHP也带来了自身的优势和局限性，特别是在氧化剂选择方面：

优势：
1. 与传统方法相比，反应温度较低 [10.1021/cr5002436]。
2. 更高的转化率和产率 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。
3. 潜在利用CO2作为氧化剂，解决环境问题 [10.1039/c2ee22409g]。

局限性：
1. 丙烯过度氧化为碳氧化物，降低选择性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。
2. 在高温下使用氧气带来安全隐患 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。
3. 开发最佳性能催化剂面临挑战 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

使用氧气作为氧化剂的具体挑战包括：
1. 由于氧气的高反应性，难以控制选择性 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。
2. 与烃类形成爆炸性混合物的风险 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。
3. 由于反应高度放热，需要有效的热管理 [10.1021/ef0340716]。

为解决这些问题，正在探索CO2和N2O等替代氧化剂。CO2作为一种较温和的氧化剂，在选择性和安全性方面具有潜在优势，但活性可能低于氧气 [10.1039/D0CY00586J]。N2O在提高选择性和减少过度氧化方面显示出潜力 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：ODHP技术的发展代表着满足日益增长的丙烯需求的重要一步，同时有潜力提高能源效率并减少环境影响。实现ODHP全部潜力的关键在于克服选择性-转化率之间的权衡，特别是在使用氧气作为氧化剂时。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 开发新型催化剂设计，能够有效活化丙烷同时抑制过度氧化途径。
2. 结合不同氧化剂或反应步骤的混合方法，以优化转化率和选择性。
3. 先进的反应器设计，如膜反应器或循环操作系统，以更好地控制反应物分布和产物形成。
4. 将ODHP过程与CO2捕获和利用技术相结合，创造更可持续的生产方法。
5. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解操作条件下的反应机理和催化剂行为。

这些进展可能会导致更高效、更具选择性和更环保的丙烯生产工艺，有助于弥合供需差距，同时减少行业的碳足迹。
