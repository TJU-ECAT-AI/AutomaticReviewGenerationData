The primary challenges in current propylene production methods include:

1. Insufficient supply to meet growing demand: Traditional methods like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) are unable to keep up with the increasing demand for propylene [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

2. Low selectivity and yields: Current processes produce propylene as a by-product, resulting in low selectivity and yields [10.1002/cctc.201200913].

3. High energy consumption: Steam cracking is estimated to be the most energy-demanding process in the chemical industry [10.1002/ente.201900622].

4. Thermodynamic limitations: Direct dehydrogenation of propane faces equilibrium constraints, requiring high temperatures for significant yields [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

5. Catalyst deactivation: Coking is a major issue in dehydrogenation, leading to rapid catalyst deactivation [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009].

Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) addresses these issues by:

1. Providing an alternative, on-purpose production method to meet demand [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

2. Offering improved reaction efficiency due to favorable thermodynamics (exothermic, lower reaction temperatures) [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

3. Enhancing catalyst stability by preventing coke formation [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

4. Overcoming thermodynamic limitations of conventional dehydrogenation [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].

5. Potentially reducing energy consumption by up to 45% compared to conventional methods [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

However, ODHP also presents its own set of challenges, particularly in the context of oxidant selection:

1. Overoxidation: When using oxygen as an oxidant, there is a risk of over-oxidation of propylene to carbon oxides (COx), resulting in low selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].

2. Safety concerns: The use of oxygen can lead to process flammability issues [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].

3. Selectivity control: Balancing reactivity and selectivity to maximize propylene production while minimizing complete oxidation to CO and CO2 is challenging [10.1002/cjce.5450480221].

4. Catalyst development: Finding catalysts that can selectively activate propane while suppressing complete oxidation pathways remains difficult [10.1002/ente.201900622].

To address these challenges, alternative oxidants such as CO2 and N2O have been explored:

1. CO2 as an oxidant:
   - Advantages: Suppresses deep oxidation, reduces coke formation, and allows for CO2 utilization [10.1039/D0CY00586J].
   - Limitations: Lower oxidizing power compared to oxygen, potentially resulting in lower conversion rates [10.039/C5FD00062A].

2. N2O as an oxidant:
   - Advantages: Higher selectivity towards propene compared to air [10.1021/jp809733s].
   - Limitations: Much lower propane conversion compared to air [10.1021/jp809733s].

The selection of the most appropriate oxidant depends on balancing factors such as reactivity, selectivity, safety, and environmental considerations. Ongoing research focuses on developing more effective catalysts, optimizing reaction conditions, and exploring novel reactor designs to overcome the current limitations of ODHP.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of ODHP technology represents a significant shift in propylene production strategies, addressing many of the limitations of traditional methods. The key to realizing the full potential of ODHP lies in finding the right balance between reactivity and selectivity, which is heavily influenced by oxidant choice and catalyst design.

Future research efforts may focus on:

1. Advanced catalyst design: Developing multi-functional catalysts that can effectively activate propane while controlling the oxidation process to maximize propylene yield.

2. Hybrid oxidant systems: Exploring combinations of oxidants (e.g., O2 + CO2) to leverage the advantages of each while mitigating their individual limitations.

3. Innovative reactor designs: Investigating membrane reactors or cyclic-operation reactors to better control reactant distribution and improve overall process efficiency.

4. In-situ catalyst regeneration techniques: Developing methods to continuously regenerate catalysts during the reaction, reducing downtime and improving long-term stability.

5. Process intensification: Integrating ODHP with other processes, such as CO2 capture or syngas production, to create more efficient and sustainable production systems.

6. Computational modeling and machine learning: Utilizing advanced computational techniques to predict catalyst performance and optimize reaction conditions.

These research directions could potentially lead to breakthroughs in ODHP technology, making it a viable and competitive alternative to traditional propylene production methods while addressing environmental concerns and meeting the growing global demand for this crucial chemical feedstock.

<Chinese>
目前丙烯生产方法的主要挑战包括：

1. 供应不足以满足日益增长的需求：传统方法如蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解（FCC）无法跟上丙烯需求的增长速度 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

2. 低选择性和产率：现有工艺将丙烯作为副产品生产，导致选择性和产率低 [10.1002/cctc.201200913]。

3. 高能耗：蒸汽裂解被认为是化工行业能耗最高的工艺 [10.1002/ente.201900622]。

4. 热力学限制：丙烷直接脱氢面临平衡限制，需要高温才能获得显著产率 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

5. 催化剂失活：结焦是脱氢过程中的一个主要问题，导致催化剂快速失活 [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。

丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）通过以下方式解决这些问题：

1. 提供一种替代性的、专门的生产方法来满足需求 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

2. 由于热力学有利（放热，较低反应温度），提供了改进的反应效率 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

3. 通过防止焦炭形成来增强催化剂稳定性 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

4. 克服传统脱氢的热力学限制 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。

5. 与传统方法相比，可能减少高达45%的能耗 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

然而，ODHP也面临自身的一系列挑战，特别是在氧化剂选择方面：

1. 过度氧化：使用氧气作为氧化剂时，存在丙烯过度氧化为碳氧化物（COx）的风险，导致选择性低 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。

2. 安全问题：使用氧气可能导致工艺易燃性问题 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。

3. 选择性控制：平衡反应性和选择性以最大化丙烯产量，同时最小化完全氧化为CO和CO2的难度很大 [10.1002/cjce.5450480221]。

4. 催化剂开发：找到能选择性激活丙烷同时抑制完全氧化途径的催化剂仍然很困难 [10.1002/ente.201900622]。

为解决这些挑战，研究人员探索了CO2和N2O等替代氧化剂：

1. CO2作为氧化剂：
   - 优势：抑制深度氧化，减少焦炭形成，并允许CO2利用 [10.1039/D0CY00586J]。
   - 限制：与氧气相比氧化能力较弱，可能导致较低的转化率 [10.039/C5FD00062A]。

2. N2O作为氧化剂：
   - 优势：与空气相比，对丙烯的选择性更高 [10.1021/jp809733s]。
   - 限制：与空气相比，丙烷转化率要低得多 [10.1021/jp809733s]。

选择最合适的氧化剂取决于平衡反应性、选择性、安全性和环境因素等多个方面。持续的研究重点是开发更有效的催化剂，优化反应条件，探索新型反应器设计，以克服ODHP当前的局限性。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：ODHP技术的发展代表了丙烯生产策略的重大转变，解决了传统方法的许多局限性。充分发挥ODHP潜力的关键在于找到反应性和选择性之间的正确平衡，这在很大程度上受氧化剂选择和催化剂设计的影响。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 高级催化剂设计：开发多功能催化剂，能有效激活丙烷的同时控制氧化过程，以最大化丙烯产率。

2. 混合氧化剂系统：探索氧化剂组合（如O2 + CO2），以利用各自的优势，同时减轻其各自的局限性。

3. 创新反应器设计：研究膜反应器或周期操作反应器，以更好地控制反应物分布并提高整体工艺效率。

4. 原位催化剂再生技术：开发反应过程中持续再生催化剂的方法，减少停机时间并提高长期稳定性。

5. 工艺强化：将ODHP与其他工艺（如CO2捕获或合成气生产）集成，创建更高效和可持续的生产系统。

6. 计算机模拟和机器学习：利用先进的计算技术预测催化剂性能并优化反应条件。

这些研究方向可能会带来ODHP技术的突破，使其成为传统丙烯生产方法的可行和有竞争力的替代方案，同时解决环境问题并满足这一关键化学原料不断增长的全球需求。
