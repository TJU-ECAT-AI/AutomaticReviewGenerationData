The primary challenges in current propylene production methods include:

1. Insufficient supply to meet growing demand, particularly from traditional methods like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80992-X].
2. High energy consumption, with steam cracking being one of the most energy-intensive processes in the chemical industry [10.1002/ente.201900622].
3. Low selectivity towards propylene, especially in FCC and steam cracking processes [10.1002/cctc.201200913].
4. Thermodynamic limitations in direct dehydrogenation of propane, requiring high temperatures (around 1000K) [10.1002/cctc.201200913].
5. Rapid catalyst deactivation due to coke formation in dehydrogenation processes [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].

Oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) addresses these issues by:

1. Offering a more targeted approach to propylene production [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].
2. Providing an exothermic process, potentially reducing energy requirements [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0].
3. Overcoming thermodynamic limitations of traditional dehydrogenation [10.1021/cr5002436].
4. Potentially reducing coke formation and improving catalyst stability [10.126/science.aaf7885].

However, ODHP presents its own set of advantages and limitations, particularly in the context of oxidant selection:

Advantages:
1. Higher conversion rates compared to non-oxidative methods [10.1021/cr5002436].
2. Lower reaction temperatures, especially when using oxygen as an oxidant [10.1021/cr5002436].
3. Potential for improved catalyst stability and reduced coking [10.126/science.aaf7885].

Limitations:
1. Over-oxidation of propylene to carbon oxides, leading to low selectivity [10.1002/anie.202002440].
2. Safety concerns due to the flammability of propane-oxygen mixtures [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].
3. Difficulty in controlling selectivity, especially at high conversions [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].

The specific challenges associated with using oxygen as an oxidant include:
1. High reactivity leading to undesired deep oxidation products [10.1021/jp410750c].
2. Safety issues related to handling explosive mixtures [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].
3. Difficulty in balancing conversion and selectivity [10.1080/17415990412331327909].

To address these issues, alternative oxidants such as CO2 and N2O have been explored:

1. CO2 as an oxidant:
   - Advantages: Milder oxidation, potentially higher selectivity, and CO2 utilization [10.1039/C5FD00062A].
   - Limitations: Lower oxidizing power, potential for reverse water-gas shift reaction [10.1021/ef0340716].

2. N2O as an oxidant:
   - Advantages: Higher selectivity towards propylene compared to oxygen [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010].
   - Limitations: Lower conversion rates compared to oxygen [10.1021/jp809733s].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 

Considering the advancements and challenges in ODHP, future research efforts should focus on:

1. Developing novel catalysts that can selectively activate C-H bonds in propane while suppressing over-oxidation of propylene. This may involve exploring multi-component catalysts or advanced materials like hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN) [10.1002/anie.202002440].

2. Investigating hybrid approaches that combine the benefits of different oxidants. For example, using a mixture of O2 and CO2 or exploring the potential of membrane reactors to control oxidant distribution [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].

3. Exploring cyclic operation or chemical looping concepts to separate the oxidation and reduction steps, potentially improving selectivity and safety [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X].

4. Integrating ODHP processes with CO2 capture and utilization technologies to address both propylene production and environmental concerns [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

5. Developing advanced reactor designs and process control strategies to optimize heat management and reaction conditions for improved selectivity and yield.

These research directions could lead to more efficient and sustainable propylene production methods, addressing the growing demand while minimizing environmental impact and improving process economics.

<Chinese>
目前丙烯生产方法的主要挑战包括：

1. 传统方法如蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解（FCC）无法满足不断增长的需求 [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80992-X]。
2. 高能耗，其中蒸汽裂解是化工行业能耗最高的工艺之一 [10.1002/ente.201900622]。
3. 丙烯选择性低，尤其是在FCC和蒸汽裂解工艺中 [10.1002/cctc.201200913]。
4. 丙烷直接脱氢的热力学限制，需要高温（约1000K）[10.1002/cctc.201200913]。
5. 脱氢过程中由于积碳导致催化剂快速失活 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。

丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）通过以下方式解决这些问题：

1. 提供更有针对性的丙烯生产方法 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。
2. 提供放热过程，有潜力降低能耗 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_0]。
3. 克服传统脱氢的热力学限制 [10.1021/cr5002436]。
4. 潜在减少积碳，提高催化剂稳定性 [10.126/science.aaf7885]。

然而，ODHP也有其自身的优势和局限性，特别是在氧化剂选择方面：

优势：
1. 与非氧化方法相比，具有更高的转化率 [10.1021/cr5002436]。
2. 反应温度较低，尤其是使用氧气作为氧化剂时 [10.1021/cr5002436]。
3. 有潜力提高催化剂稳定性并减少积碳 [10.126/science.aaf7885]。

局限性：
1. 丙烯过度氧化为碳氧化物，导致选择性低 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。
2. 由于丙烷-氧气混合物的易燃性而产生安全隐患 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。
3. 难以控制选择性，尤其是在高转化率下 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。

使用氧气作为氧化剂的具体挑战包括：
1. 高反应性导致不需要的深度氧化产物 [10.1021/jp410750c]。
2. 处理爆炸性混合物相关的安全问题 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。
3. 难以平衡转化率和选择性 [10.1080/17415990412331327909]。

为解决这些问题，已探索了CO2和N2O等替代氧化剂：

1. CO2作为氧化剂：
   - 优势：温和氧化，潜在更高的选择性，以及CO2利用 [10.1039/C5FD00062A]。
   - 局限性：氧化能力较低，可能发生逆水气变换反应 [10.1021/ef0340716]。

2. N2O作为氧化剂：
   - 优势：与氧气相比，对丙烯的选择性更高 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010]。
   - 局限性：与氧气相比，转化率较低 [10.1021/jp809733s]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：

考虑到ODHP的进展和挑战，未来的研究工作应该集中在：

1. 开发能够选择性活化丙烷C-H键同时抑制丙烯过度氧化的新型催化剂。这可能涉及探索多组分催化剂或六方氮化硼（h-BN）等先进材料 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。

2. 研究结合不同氧化剂优势的混合方法。例如，使用O2和CO2的混合物，或探索膜反应器控制氧化剂分布的潜力 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。

3. 探索循环操作或化学链概念，将氧化和还原步骤分离，有可能提高选择性和安全性 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00816-X]。

4. 将ODHP工艺与CO2捕获和利用技术结合，同时解决丙烯生产和环境问题 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

5. 开发先进的反应器设计和过程控制策略，优化热管理和反应条件，以提高选择性和产率。

这些研究方向可能导致更高效和可持续的丙烯生产方法，在满足不断增长的需求的同时，最大限度地减少环境影响并改善工艺经济性。
