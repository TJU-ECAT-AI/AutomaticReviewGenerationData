<English>
The composition of coke deposits in catalytic systems, particularly in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, varies significantly across different reaction conditions and catalyst types. This comprehensive review synthesizes findings from multiple studies to elucidate the complex nature and diversity of coke constituents in these systems.

Composition and Location of Coke Deposits:
1. Types of Coke:
   - Coke on the metal surface
   - Coke on the catalyst support
   - Coke in the vicinity of the metal
   - Graphitic coke on the support
   [10.1016/s0167-2991(99)80483-2]

2. Chemical Composition:
   - Aliphatics, aromatics, and pregraphite species on PtSn catalysts
   - Atomic carbon or aromatics on Pt/Al2O3
   - C3 species on PtSn/Al2O3
   [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1]

3. Structural Characteristics:
   - Extended graphene sheets on small Pt nanoparticles (1.5-2 nm)
   - Encapsulating graphitic layers on larger Pt nanoparticles (5-10 nm)
   [10.1016/j.jcat.2016.10.023]

Variability Across Reaction Conditions and Catalyst Types:
1. Temperature Effects:
   - Higher temperatures increase coke formation and shift TPO peak temperatures upwards
   [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]

2. Pressure Effects:
   - Higher propane and propylene partial pressures increase coke formation
   - Hydrogen pressure suppresses coke formation
   [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]

3. Catalyst Composition:
   - Ga-containing catalysts show reduced coke deposition compared to Sn-containing catalysts
   - Bimetallic catalysts (e.g., PtSn) generally show better resistance to coke formation than monometallic catalysts
   [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]

4. Support Effects:
   - Different supports (e.g., alumina, silica, zeolites) influence coke composition and distribution
   [10.1016/s0926-860x(97)00338-4]

Analytical Techniques:
1. Temperature Programmed Oxidation (TPO):
   - Primary technique for distinguishing between coke types based on oxidation temperatures
   - Reveals information about coke location (metal vs. support) and nature
   [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]

2. Raman Spectroscopy:
   - Provides information on the degree of graphitization and disorder in coke deposits
   - D and G bands at 1320 cm-1 and 1584 cm-1 respectively are typical for coke deposits
   [10.1039/c3cp50646k]

3. Thermogravimetric Analysis (TGA):
   - Quantifies the amount of coke and determines combustion temperatures
   [10.1039/c3cp50646k]

4. Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM):
   - Visualizes coke morphology and distribution on catalyst surfaces
   [10.1016/j.jcat.2016.10.023]

5. Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS):
   - Identifies specific components in coke deposits
   [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1]

6. Fourier Transform Infrared (FTIR) Spectroscopy:
   - Identifies functional groups in coke deposits
   [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]

7. Elemental Analysis:
   - Determines H/C ratios in coke deposits
   [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The complex nature of coke formation in PDH systems presents both challenges and opportunities for future research. The variability in coke composition across different catalysts and reaction conditions suggests that tailored approaches to catalyst design and process optimization could significantly improve PDH performance. Future research directions might include:

1. In-situ characterization techniques: Developing methods for real-time monitoring of coke formation and composition during PDH reactions could provide invaluable insights into the dynamics of coke evolution.

2. Machine learning approaches: Utilizing big data and machine learning algorithms to predict coke formation based on catalyst properties and reaction conditions could accelerate catalyst design and process optimization.

3. Novel catalyst architectures: Exploring hierarchical pore structures or core-shell designs that can mitigate coke formation while maintaining high catalytic activity.

4. Green regeneration methods: Investigating environmentally friendly techniques for catalyst regeneration that can effectively remove coke without damaging the catalyst structure.

5. Mechanism elucidation: Further studies on the molecular-level mechanisms of coke formation and how they relate to catalyst deactivation could lead to breakthrough strategies for extending catalyst lifetimes.

By addressing these research areas, future advancements in PDH technology could lead to more efficient, sustainable, and economically viable processes for propylene production.

<Chinese>
催化体系中焦炭沉积物的组成，特别是在丙烷脱氢 (PDH) 反应中，在不同的反应条件和催化剂类型下有显著变化。本综述综合了多项研究的结果，阐明了这些系统中焦炭成分的复杂性质和多样性。

焦炭沉积物的组成和位置：
1. 焦炭类型：
   - 金属表面上的焦炭
   - 催化剂载体上的焦炭
   - 金属附近的焦炭
   - 载体上的石墨化焦炭
   [10.1016/s0167-2991(99)80483-2]

2. 化学组成：
   - PtSn 催化剂上的脂肪族、芳香族和前石墨物种
   - Pt/Al2O3 上的原子碳或芳香族
   - PtSn/Al2O3 上的 C3 物种
   [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1]

3. 结构特征：
   - 小型 Pt 纳米粒子（1.5-2 nm）上的延伸石墨烯片
   - 大型 Pt 纳米粒子（5-10 nm）上的包覆石墨层
   [10.1016/j.jcat.2016.10.023]

反应条件和催化剂类型的变化：
1. 温度效应：
   - 较高温度增加焦炭形成并使 TPO 峰温度向上移动
   [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]

2. 压力效应：
   - 较高的丙烷和丙烯分压增加焦炭形成
   - 氢气压力抑制焦炭形成
   [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]

3. 催化剂组成：
   - 含 Ga 催化剂比含 Sn 催化剂显示更少的焦炭沉积
   - 双金属催化剂（如 PtSn）通常比单金属催化剂对焦炭形成有更好的抗性
   [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]

4. 载体效应：
   - 不同载体（如氧化铝、二氧化硅、沸石）影响焦炭组成和分布
   [10.1016/s0926-860x(97)00338-4]

分析技术：
1. 程序升温氧化（TPO）：
   - 基于氧化温度区分焦炭类型的主要技术
   - 揭示焦炭位置（金属vs载体）和性质的信息
   [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]

2. 拉曼光谱：
   - 提供焦炭沉积物石墨化程度和无序度的信息
   - D 带和 G 带分别位于 1320 cm-1 和 1584 cm-1，是焦炭沉积物的典型特征
   [10.1039/c3cp50646k]

3. 热重分析（TGA）：
   - 量化焦炭量并确定燃烧温度
   [10.1039/c3cp50646k]

4. 透射电子显微镜（TEM）：
   - 可视化催化剂表面上焦炭的形态和分布
   [10.1016/j.jcat.2016.10.023]

5. 气相色谱-质谱联用（GC-MS）：
   - 识别焦炭沉积物中的特定成分
   [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1]

6. 傅里叶变换红外（FTIR）光谱：
   - 识别焦炭沉积物中的官能团
   [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]

7. 元素分析：
   - 确定焦炭沉积物中的 H/C 比
   [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：PDH 系统中焦炭形成的复杂性为未来研究既带来挑战也带来机遇。不同催化剂和反应条件下焦炭组成的变化表明，定制化的催化剂设计和工艺优化方法可能显著提高 PDH 性能。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 原位表征技术：开发在 PDH 反应过程中实时监测焦炭形成和组成的方法，可以为焦炭演变动力学提供宝贵的见解。

2. 机器学习方法：利用大数据和机器学习算法，基于催化剂性质和反应条件预测焦炭形成，可以加速催化剂设计和工艺优化。

3. 新型催化剂结构：探索可以减少焦炭形成同时保持高催化活性的分层孔结构或核壳设计。

4. 绿色再生方法：研究环境友好的催化剂再生技术，可以有效去除焦炭而不损坏催化剂结构。

5. 机理阐明：进一步研究焦炭形成的分子层面机理及其与催化剂失活的关系，可能导致延长催化剂寿命的突破性策略。

通过解决这些研究领域，PDH 技术的未来进展可能导致更高效、更可持续和更具经济可行性的丙烯生产工艺。
