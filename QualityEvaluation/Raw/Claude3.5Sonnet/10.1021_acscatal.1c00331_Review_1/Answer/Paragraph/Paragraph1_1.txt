<English>
The composition of coke deposits in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions varies significantly across different reaction conditions and catalyst types. This comprehensive review synthesizes findings from multiple studies to elucidate the complex nature and diversity of coke constituents in these systems.

Coke Composition and Location:
1. Multiple types of coke have been identified:
   a. Coke on the metal surface
   b. Coke on the catalyst support
   c. Coke in the vicinity of the metal
   d. Graphitic coke on the support
   [10.1016/s0167-2991(99)80483-2]

2. Coke composition varies based on location:
   a. Coke on metal: Softer, contains more hydrogen (higher H/C ratio)
   b. Coke on support: Harder, contains less hydrogen (lower H/C ratio)
   [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]

3. On PtSn catalysts, coke mainly consists of three species: aliphatics, aromatics, and pregraphite
   [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1]

Factors Influencing Coke Formation:
1. Catalyst composition:
   a. Ga-containing catalysts show reduced coke deposition compared to Sn-containing catalysts
   b. Bimetallic catalysts (e.g., PtSn) generally show better resistance to coke formation than monometallic catalysts
   [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]

2. Catalyst particle size:
   a. On small Pt nanoparticles (1.5-2 nm): Extended graphene sheets form and shift onto the support
   b. On larger Pt nanoparticles (5-10 nm): Graphene forms encapsulating graphitic layers
   [10.1016/j.jcat.2016.10.023]

3. Reaction conditions:
   a. Temperature: Higher temperatures increase coke formation
   b. Partial pressures: Higher propene partial pressure increases coke formation
   c. Hydrogen presence: Suppresses coke formation but cannot remove already formed coke
   [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]

4. Reactant type:
   a. Propene generally produces more coke deposits than propane
   b. Propane dehydrogenation results in lower coke formation compared to ethane dehydrogenation
   [10.1163/156856798x00122] [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]

Analytical Techniques:
Several analytical techniques have been employed to elucidate the nature and diversity of coke constituents:

1. Temperature Programmed Oxidation (TPO): The most commonly used technique, providing information on coke location and oxidation temperatures
   [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]

2. Raman Spectroscopy: Used for structural characterization and determination of graphene disorder
   [10.1039/c3cp50646k]

3. Thermogravimetric Analysis (TGA): Quantifies coke amount and determines combustion temperatures
   [10.1039/c3cp50646k]

4. Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM): Provides morphological analysis and visualization of coke deposits
   [10.1016/j.jcat.2016.10.023]

5. Elemental Analysis: Determines H/C ratios of coke deposits
   [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]

6. Fourier Transform Infrared (FTIR) Spectroscopy: Identifies functional groups in coke deposits
   [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]

7. Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS): Used to identify detailed components of coke
   [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1]

8. Density Functional Theory (DFT) calculations: Investigate pathways for coke production
   [10.1002/adsu.202000092]

These techniques provide complementary information, allowing researchers to build a comprehensive understanding of coke formation, composition, and behavior in PDH systems.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The complex nature of coke formation in PDH reactions presents both challenges and opportunities for future research. While significant progress has been made in understanding coke composition and formation mechanisms, several areas warrant further investigation:

1. In-situ characterization: Developing advanced in-situ techniques to study coke formation in real-time under reaction conditions could provide deeper insights into the dynamic processes of coke evolution.

2. Molecular-level understanding: Combining experimental techniques with advanced computational modeling could help elucidate the exact molecular structures of coke species and their formation pathways.

3. Novel catalyst designs: Leveraging the understanding of coke formation mechanisms, researchers could design novel catalysts with enhanced coke resistance, potentially incorporating advanced materials such as 2D materials or metal-organic frameworks.

4. Machine learning approaches: Utilizing machine learning algorithms to analyze large datasets from multiple analytical techniques could reveal hidden patterns and correlations in coke formation, potentially leading to predictive models for catalyst performance and longevity.

5. Sustainable regeneration methods: Developing more efficient and environmentally friendly catalyst regeneration techniques based on the detailed understanding of coke composition and structure.

These future directions could lead to significant advancements in PDH catalyst design and process optimization, ultimately improving the efficiency and sustainability of propylene production.

<Chinese>
在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中，积碳沉积物的组成在不同反应条件和催化剂类型下存在显著差异。本综述综合了多项研究的结果，阐明了这些系统中积碳成分的复杂性和多样性。

积碳组成和位置：
1. 已识别出多种类型的积碳：
   a. 金属表面上的积碳
   b. 催化剂载体上的积碳
   c. 金属附近的积碳
   d. 载体上的石墨状积碳
   [10.1016/s0167-2991(99)80483-2]

2. 积碳组成因位置而异：
   a. 金属上的积碳：较软，含氢量较高（H/C比较高）
   b. 载体上的积碳：较硬，含氢量较低（H/C比较低）
   [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]

3. 在PtSn催化剂上，积碳主要由三种物质组成：脂肪族化合物、芳香族化合物和前石墨
   [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1]

影响积碳形成的因素：
1. 催化剂组成：
   a. 含Ga的催化剂比含Sn的催化剂显示出较少的积碳沉积
   b. 双金属催化剂（如PtSn）通常比单金属催化剂表现出更好的抗积碳能力
   [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]

2. 催化剂粒子大小：
   a. 在小的Pt纳米粒子（1.5-2 nm）上：形成延伸的石墨烯片并转移到载体上
   b. 在较大的Pt纳米粒子（5-10 nm）上：石墨烯形成包覆的石墨层
   [10.1016/j.jcat.2016.10.023]

3. 反应条件：
   a. 温度：更高的温度增加积碳形成
   b. 分压：更高的丙烯分压增加积碳形成
   c. 氢气存在：抑制积碳形成，但无法去除已形成的积碳
   [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]

4. 反应物类型：
   a. 丙烯通常比丙烷产生更多的积碳沉积物
   b. 与乙烷脱氢相比，丙烷脱氢产生较少的积碳
   [10.1163/156856798x00122] [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]

分析技术：
已使用多种分析技术来阐明积碳成分的性质和多样性：

1. 程序升温氧化（TPO）：最常用的技术，提供关于积碳位置和氧化温度的信息
   [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]

2. 拉曼光谱：用于结构表征和确定石墨烯无序度
   [10.1039/c3cp50646k]

3. 热重分析（TGA）：量化积碳量并确定燃烧温度
   [10.1039/c3cp50646k]

4. 透射电子显微镜（TEM）：提供积碳沉积物的形态分析和可视化
   [10.1016/j.jcat.2016.10.023]

5. 元素分析：确定积碳沉积物的H/C比
   [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]

6. 傅里叶变换红外（FTIR）光谱：识别积碳沉积物中的官能团
   [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]

7. 气相色谱-质谱（GC-MS）：用于识别积碳的详细组分
   [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1]

8. 密度泛函理论（DFT）计算：研究积碳生成的途径
   [10.1002/adsu.202000092]

这些技术提供了互补的信息，使研究人员能够全面理解PDH系统中积碳的形成、组成和行为。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：PDH反应中积碳形成的复杂性为未来研究提供了挑战和机遇。尽管在理解积碳组成和形成机制方面已取得重大进展，但仍有几个领域值得进一步研究：

1. 原位表征：开发先进的原位技术，在反应条件下实时研究积碳形成，可以提供对积碳演变动态过程的更深入见解。

2. 分子水平理解：结合实验技术和先进的计算模拟，有助于阐明积碳物种的确切分子结构及其形成途径。

3. 新型催化剂设计：利用对积碳形成机制的理解，研究人员可以设计具有增强抗积碳能力的新型催化剂，可能包括利用二维材料或金属有机框架等先进材料。

4. 机器学习方法：利用机器学习算法分析来自多种分析技术的大型数据集，可能揭示积碳形成中隐藏的模式和相关性，潜在地导致催化剂性能和寿命的预测模型。

5. 可持续再生方法：基于对积碳组成和结构的详细理解，开发更高效、更环保的催化剂再生技术。

这些未来方向可能导致PDH催化剂设计和工艺优化的重大进展，最终提高丙烯生产的效率和可持续性。
