<English>
The composition of coke deposits in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions varies significantly across different reaction conditions and catalyst types. This comprehensive review synthesizes findings from multiple studies to elucidate the complex nature and diversity of coke constituents in PDH systems.

Coke Composition and Location:
1. Types of coke:
   - Coke on the metal surface
   - Coke on the catalyst support
   - Coke in the vicinity of the metal
   - Graphitic coke on the support
   [10.1016/s0167-2991(99)80483-2]

2. Composition:
   - Aliphatics, aromatics, and pregraphite species on PtSn catalysts
   - Atomic carbon or aromatics on Pt/Al2O3
   - C3 species on PtSn/Al2O3
   [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1]

3. Structure:
   - Extended graphene sheets on small Pt nanoparticles (1.5-2 nm)
   - Encapsulating graphitic layers on larger Pt nanoparticles (5-10 nm)
   [10.1016/j.jcat.2016.10.023]

Factors Influencing Coke Formation:
1. Catalyst composition:
   - Ga-containing catalysts show reduced coke deposition compared to Sn-containing catalysts
   - PtSn catalysts exhibit different coke distribution compared to monometallic Pt catalysts
   [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]

2. Reaction conditions:
   - Temperature: Higher temperatures increase coke formation
   - Partial pressures: Higher propene partial pressure increases coke formation
   - Hydrogen presence: Suppresses coke formation but cannot remove existing coke
   [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]

3. Reactant type:
   - Propene generally produces more coke than propane
   - Propane dehydrogenation results in lower coke formation compared to ethane dehydrogenation
   [10.1163/156856798x00122] [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]

Analytical Techniques:
1. Temperature Programmed Oxidation (TPO): Primary technique for distinguishing coke types based on oxidation temperatures
   [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]

2. Raman Spectroscopy: Reveals structural information about coke deposits, particularly graphene-like structures
   [10.1039/c3cp50646k]

3. Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM): Provides morphological analysis of coke deposits
   [10.1016/j.jcat.2016.10.023]

4. Thermogravimetric Analysis (TGA): Quantifies coke amount and combustion behavior
   [10.1039/c3cp50646k]

5. Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS): Identifies specific components of coke deposits
   [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1]

6. Fourier Transform Infrared (FTIR) Spectroscopy: Identifies functional groups in coke deposits
   [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]

7. Elemental Analysis: Determines H/C ratios in coke deposits
   [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]

These techniques collectively provide a comprehensive understanding of coke composition, structure, and formation mechanisms under various reaction conditions and catalyst types in PDH systems.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive analysis of coke formation in PDH reactions reveals the complex interplay between catalyst properties, reaction conditions, and coke characteristics. Future research directions may focus on developing in situ characterization techniques to monitor coke formation in real-time, potentially using advanced spectroscopic methods or synchrotron-based techniques. This could provide deeper insights into the dynamic processes of coke formation and evolution during PDH reactions.

Additionally, the development of novel catalyst designs that can mitigate coke formation while maintaining high catalytic activity is a promising avenue for future work. This might involve exploring new catalyst compositions, support materials, or nanostructured catalysts that can alter the coke formation pathways or facilitate easier coke removal.

Computational modeling and machine learning approaches could also be employed to predict coke formation behaviors under various conditions, potentially leading to optimized reaction parameters and catalyst designs for minimal coking. Lastly, investigating the potential for valorization of coke deposits, such as their use in carbon materials or as precursors for other valuable products, could transform this challenge into an opportunity for additional value creation in PDH processes.

<Chinese>
在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中，积碳沉积物的组成在不同的反应条件和催化剂类型下有显著变化。本综述综合了多项研究的发现，阐明了PDH系统中积碳成分的复杂性质和多样性。

积碳组成和位置：
1. 积碳类型：
   - 金属表面上的积碳
   - 催化剂载体上的积碳
   - 金属附近的积碳
   - 载体上的石墨化积碳
   [10.1016/s0167-2991(99)80483-2]

2. 组成：
   - PtSn催化剂上的脂肪族、芳香族和类石墨物种
   - Pt/Al2O3上的原子碳或芳香族
   - PtSn/Al2O3上的C3物种
   [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1]

3. 结构：
   - 小型Pt纳米粒子（1.5-2 nm）上的延伸石墨烯片
   - 大型Pt纳米粒子（5-10 nm）上的包覆石墨层
   [10.1016/j.jcat.2016.10.023]

影响积碳形成的因素：
1. 催化剂组成：
   - 含Ga的催化剂比含Sn的催化剂显示出较少的积碳沉积
   - PtSn催化剂与单金属Pt催化剂相比展现出不同的积碳分布
   [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]

2. 反应条件：
   - 温度：更高的温度增加积碳形成
   - 分压：更高的丙烯分压增加积碳形成
   - 氢气存在：抑制积碳形成但不能去除现有积碳
   [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]

3. 反应物类型：
   - 丙烯通常比丙烷产生更多积碳
   - 与乙烷脱氢相比，丙烷脱氢导致较少的积碳形成
   [10.1163/156856798x00122] [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]

分析技术：
1. 程序升温氧化（TPO）：根据氧化温度区分积碳类型的主要技术
   [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]

2. 拉曼光谱：揭示积碳沉积物的结构信息，特别是类石墨烯结构
   [10.1039/c3cp50646k]

3. 透射电子显微镜（TEM）：提供积碳沉积物的形态分析
   [10.1016/j.jcat.2016.10.023]

4. 热重分析（TGA）：量化积碳量和燃烧行为
   [10.1039/c3cp50646k]

5. 气相色谱-质谱联用（GC-MS）：识别积碳沉积物的特定组分
   [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_1]

6. 傅里叶变换红外（FTIR）光谱：识别积碳沉积物中的官能团
   [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]

7. 元素分析：确定积碳沉积物中的H/C比
   [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]

这些技术共同为理解PDH系统中在各种反应条件和催化剂类型下的积碳组成、结构和形成机制提供了全面的认识。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：对PDH反应中积碳形成的全面分析揭示了催化剂性质、反应条件和积碳特性之间的复杂相互作用。未来研究方向可能集中在开发原位表征技术，以实时监测积碳形成，可能使用先进的光谱方法或同步加速器基础技术。这可以为PDH反应中积碳形成和演变的动态过程提供更深入的见解。

此外，开发能够减少积碳形成同时保持高催化活性的新型催化剂设计是未来工作的一个有前景的方向。这可能涉及探索新的催化剂组成、载体材料或纳米结构催化剂，这些可以改变积碳形成路径或促进更容易的积碳去除。

计算模型和机器学习方法也可以用于预测各种条件下的积碳形成行为，可能导致优化的反应参数和催化剂设计，以最小化积碳。最后，研究积碳沉积物的潜在增值利用，如在碳材料中的应用或作为其他有价值产品的前体，可能将这一挑战转化为PDH过程中额外价值创造的机会。
