1. Bulk composition analysis: X-ray fluorescence (XRF) can determine the overall chemical composition and dopant concentration in the bulk material.

2. Surface composition analysis: Low-energy ion scattering (LEIS) provides information on the outermost surface composition, which is crucial for catalysis. This can reveal surface segregation or enrichment of dopants.

5. Impurity detection: XRF can also identify trace impurities that may affect catalytic performance.

4. Perform in-situ or operando characterization to observe changes under reaction conditions.

1. Carefully control for impurities and their potential effects.
2. Use model catalysts with well-defined structures when possible.
3. Conduct systematic studies varying dopant concentration and preparation methods.
4. Employ complementary characterization techniques to cross-validate results.

This multi-technique approach allows for a comprehensive understanding of the catalyst structure and composition, enabling researchers to draw more reliable conclusions about the role of dopants in enhancing catalytic performance.
