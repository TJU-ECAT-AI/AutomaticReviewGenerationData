The literature does not directly address comprehensive characterization methods for verifying dopant incorporation in metal oxide catalysts. However, it does discuss High Sensitivity-Low Energy Ion Scattering (HS-LEIS) as a powerful analytical technique for surface analysis of catalysts. 

HS-LEIS offers several advantages for characterizing catalyst surfaces:

1. It provides quantitative compositional information specifically about the outermost atomic layer, where catalytic reactions occur.

2. It has high sensitivity, with detection limits in the ppm range.

3. It offers good mass resolution, especially for lighter elements.

4. Quantification of results is straightforward, as peak areas are proportional to the surface fraction of atoms.

5. It can analyze multiple elements by using different primary ions (He, Ne, Ar).

While HS-LEIS alone may not provide a comprehensive characterization of dopant incorporation, it could be a valuable part of a multi-technique approach. Its surface specificity and quantitative nature make it well-suited for analyzing the distribution of dopants on catalyst surfaces.
