The literature does not directly address comprehensive characterization methods for verifying dopant incorporation in metal oxide catalysts. However, it does discuss High Sensitivity-Low Energy Ion Scattering (HS-LEIS) as a powerful analytical technique for surface analysis of catalysts. HS-LEIS provides quantitative compositional information about the outermost atomic layer of a material, which is crucial for studying catalytic processes. 

HS-LEIS offers several advantages for catalyst characterization:

1. Surface specificity: It analyzes only the outermost atomic layer, where catalytic reactions occur.

2. High sensitivity: Detection limits are in the ppm range.

3. Good mass resolution: Allows identification of different elements on the surface.

4. Quantification: Straightforward quantification of surface composition.

5. Minimal damage: Uses low ion doses, causing negligible damage to samples.

While HS-LEIS doesn't directly address dopant distribution or structural changes, it could be valuable as part of a comprehensive characterization approach. By providing detailed information about surface composition, HS-LEIS could help verify the presence of dopants on the catalyst surface and potentially provide insights into their distribution. However, additional techniques would be needed to fully characterize dopant incorporation, structural changes, and catalytic performance relationships.
