1. X-ray Diffraction (XRD): Used to confirm the absence of separate rhodium metal or oxide phases and to refine the structure, showing Rh substitution in Ce sites.

4. Extended X-ray Absorption Fine Structure (EXAFS) spectroscopy: Provides detailed information about the local structure around Rh ions, including coordination numbers and bond distances.

5. Rietveld refinement: Confirms the absence of separate rhodium phases and helps in structural analysis.

6. H2 uptake studies: Indicates the reduction of Ce4+ to Ce3+, providing information on redox properties.

These techniques address the challenges in establishing a clear relationship between dopant presence, structural changes, and enhanced catalytic performance by:

1. Ruling out the presence of separate Rh phases, confirming its incorporation into the CeO2 structure.
2. Providing evidence of Rh substitution in Ce sites and its oxidation state.
3. Showing the local environment around Rh ions, including oxide vacancies.
4. Demonstrating the enhanced concentration of Rh in surface layers, which is crucial for catalytic activity.
5. Revealing redox properties that are important for catalytic performance.

By combining these techniques, researchers can build a comprehensive picture of the catalyst structure and composition, ruling out alternative explanations for enhanced catalytic performance such as the presence of separate Rh phases or surface-deposited Rh particles.
