<English>
The concept of tandem catalysis at the nanoscale has emerged as a powerful approach to address the challenges of integrating multiple reaction steps in chemical engineering processes. This innovative strategy aims to improve efficiency and reduce costs by combining sequential reactions within a single catalyst system. A prime example of this approach is the novel nanoscale porous overcoating catalyst for coupling catalytic propane dehydrogenation with selective H2 combustion.

Tandem catalysis introduces a new class of nanocrystal catalysts with multiple metal-metal oxide interfaces designed for sequential reactions [10.1038/nchem.1018]. This approach allows for the integration of different catalytic functions within a single nanostructured system, potentially reducing the number of separate process units required and minimizing intermediate product separation and purification steps.

The principles of tandem catalysis at the nanoscale involve:

1. Rational design of catalyst structures: Nanocrystal bilayers with multiple built-in metal-metal oxide interfaces are assembled to facilitate sequential reactions [10.1038/nchem.1018].

2. Spatial organization of catalytic sites: Core/shell structures are designed where core and shell components independently catalyze different reactions, enhancing reactant migration and improving selectivity [10.1002/anie.200703335].

3. Coordination of reactive intermediate transport: The key to manipulating tandem catalysis is coordinating the transport of reactive intermediates among catalytic sites [10.1126/science.abh0424].

4. Overcoming thermodynamic limitations: Selective combustion of byproducts (e.g., H2) can drive reactions forward, breaking thermodynamic constraints of nonoxidative catalytic reactions [10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011].

The application of tandem catalysis in chemical engineering processes is exemplified by the In2O3-Pt/Al2O3 catalyst for coupling catalytic propane dehydrogenation with selective H2 combustion. This catalyst features a porous overcoating structure that regulates the transport of hydrogen species and promotes selective H2 combustion, consequently driving catalytic propane dehydrogenation forward [10.1126/science.abh0424].

The benefits of this approach include:

1. Improved yields: The tandem catalyst outperforms state-of-the-art oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) catalysts and exceeds the thermodynamic equilibrium conversion for non-oxidative propane dehydrogenation (PDH) [10.1126/science.abd4441].

2. Enhanced process efficiency: By combining multiple reaction steps in a single catalyst particle, this approach simplifies reactor designs and reduces the need for separate unit operations [10.1126/science.abd4441].

3. Increased stability: The overcoat structure stabilizes metal nanoparticles, potentially extending catalyst lifetime and reducing the need for frequent regeneration or replacement [10.1126/science.abd4441].

4. Process simplification: The use of a single reactor and catalyst composition significantly decreases process complexity and costs compared to multi-stage systems [10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011].

5. Scalability: The approach can be implemented in large-scale reactors using multiple injectors or ceramic membranes [10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of nanoscale tandem catalysts represents a significant advancement in chemical engineering, offering new possibilities for process intensification and sustainability. Future research in this field may focus on:

1. Expanding the application of tandem catalysis to other industrially relevant processes, such as CO2 conversion or biomass valorization.

2. Developing more sophisticated catalyst designs that can accommodate a greater number of sequential reactions or handle more complex reaction networks.

3. Investigating the use of advanced materials, such as metal-organic frameworks or 2D materials, as supports or components in tandem catalysts.

4. Integrating tandem catalysis with other emerging technologies, such as microfluidics or artificial intelligence, for optimized reactor design and process control.

5. Exploring the potential of tandem catalysis in addressing challenges in energy storage and conversion, particularly in the context of renewable energy systems.

These future directions could lead to even more efficient and sustainable chemical processes, contributing to the ongoing transformation of the chemical industry towards greater environmental responsibility and resource efficiency.

纳米尺度上的串联催化概念已成为解决化学工程过程中整合多个反应步骤挑战的有力方法。这种创新策略旨在通过将顺序反应结合在单一催化剂系统中来提高效率并降低成本。这种方法的一个典型例子是用于耦合催化丙烷脱氢与选择性H2燃烧的新型纳米多孔包覆催化剂。

串联催化引入了一种新型的纳米晶催化剂，具有多个金属-金属氧化物界面，专为顺序反应而设计[10.1038/nchem.1018]。这种方法允许在单一纳米结构系统中整合不同的催化功能，潜在地减少所需的单独工艺单元数量，并最小化中间产物分离和纯化步骤。

纳米尺度串联催化的原理包括：

1. 催化剂结构的合理设计：组装具有多个内置金属-金属氧化物界面的纳米晶双层，以促进顺序反应[10.1038/nchem.1018]。

2. 催化位点的空间组织：设计核/壳结构，其中核和壳组件独立催化不同的反应，增强反应物迁移并提高选择性[10.1002/anie.200703335]。

3. 协调反应中间体的传输：操纵串联催化的关键是协调反应中间体在催化位点之间的传输[10.1126/science.abh0424]。

4. 克服热力学限制：选择性燃烧副产品（如H2）可以推动反应向前进行，突破非氧化催化反应的热力学限制[10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011]。

串联催化在化学工程过程中的应用以In2O3-Pt/Al2O3催化剂为例，该催化剂用于耦合催化丙烷脱氢与选择性H2燃烧。这种催化剂具有多孔包覆结构，可调节氢物种的传输并促进选择性H2燃烧，从而推动催化丙烷脱氢反应向前进行[10.1126/science.abh0424]。

这种方法的优势包括：

1. 提高产率：串联催化剂的性能优于最先进的氧化脱氢丙烷（ODHP）催化剂，并超过了非氧化丙烷脱氢（PDH）的热力学平衡转化率[10.1126/science.abd4441]。

2. 提高工艺效率：通过在单个催化剂颗粒中结合多个反应步骤，这种方法简化了反应器设计，并减少了对单独工艺单元的需求[10.1126/science.abd4441]。

3. 增加稳定性：包覆结构稳定了金属纳米粒子，可能延长催化剂寿命并减少频繁再生或更换的需求[10.1126/science.abd4441]。

4. 工艺简化：使用单一反应器和催化剂组成显著降低了工艺复杂性和成本，相比多阶段系统[10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011]。

5. 可扩展性：这种方法可以在大规模反应器中实施，使用多个注入器或陶瓷膜[10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011]。
