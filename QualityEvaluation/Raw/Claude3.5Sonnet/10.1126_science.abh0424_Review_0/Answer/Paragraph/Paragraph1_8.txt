<English>
The introduction of tandem catalysis at the nanoscale represents a significant advancement in addressing the challenges of integrating multiple reaction steps in chemical engineering processes. This approach involves the design of nanocrystal catalysts with multiple metal-metal oxide interfaces capable of performing sequential reactions within a single catalyst system [10.1038/nchem.1018]. The concept of tandem catalysis promises to improve chemical processing by coupling multiple reactions, although precise spatiotemporal control over reactive intermediates remains a challenge [10.1126/science.abd4441].

One of the key principles of tandem catalysis is the coordination of reactive intermediate transport among catalytic sites [10.1126/science.abh0424]. This is achieved through careful catalyst design, which plays a crucial role in mediating multiple reactions under the same conditions. The development of core/shell structures and porous overcoatings has been instrumental in enhancing reactant migration, improving selectivity, and creating confined reaction environments [10.1002/anie.200703335].

The application of tandem catalysis is exemplified by the novel In2O3-Pt/Al2O3 catalyst for coupling catalytic propane dehydrogenation with selective H2 combustion [10.1126/science.abd4441]. This catalyst demonstrates how nanoscale design can overcome limitations of traditional catalysts by allowing for rapid and stable oxidative dehydrogenation of propane, achieving high per-pass yields that exceed the thermodynamic equilibrium of conventional propane dehydrogenation.

The potential for improving efficiency and reducing costs through tandem catalysis is significant. By combining multiple reaction steps in a single catalyst, this approach can reduce the number of separate process units required, minimize intermediate product separation and purification steps, and improve overall process efficiency [10.1038/nchem.1018]. The instant removal of H2 through selective combustion drives the catalytic dehydrogenation forward, breaking the thermodynamic limitations of nonoxidative catalytic reactions [10.1126/science.abh0424].

Furthermore, the use of staged O2 introduction in tandem catalysis processes allows for the selective combustion of H2 products, substantially removing thermodynamic constraints. This approach requires only one reactor and one catalytic composition, significantly decreasing process complexity and cost [10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in nanoscale tandem catalysis present exciting opportunities for future research and development in chemical engineering. The ability to design and control reactions at the nanoscale opens up possibilities for creating highly efficient and selective processes that mimic biological systems. Future research may focus on expanding the application of tandem catalysis to other industrially relevant reactions, such as CO2 conversion or biomass valorization.

Additionally, the development of in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamics of reactive intermediates in these complex systems could lead to even more precise control over reaction pathways. The integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning in catalyst design could accelerate the discovery of novel tandem catalysts with optimized performance.

Furthermore, the principles of nanoscale tandem catalysis could be extended to develop "smart" catalytic systems that can adapt to changing reaction conditions or feedstock compositions, potentially revolutionizing process control in chemical manufacturing. As we continue to push the boundaries of nanoscale engineering, the future of tandem catalysis holds great promise for creating more sustainable, efficient, and economical chemical processes.

纳米尺度上的串联催化的引入代表了化学工程过程中整合多个反应步骤的挑战方面的重大进展。这种方法涉及设计具有多个金属-金属氧化物界面的纳米晶体催化剂，能够在单一催化剂系统内执行顺序反应[10.1038/nchem.1018]。串联催化的概念通过耦合多个反应来改善化学处理，尽管对反应中间体的精确时空控制仍然是一个挑战[10.1126/science.abd4441]。

串联催化的关键原则之一是协调催化位点之间的反应中间体传输[10.1126/science.abh0424]。这是通过精心设计催化剂来实现的，在相同条件下调节多个反应起着至关重要的作用。核壳结构和多孔过覆盖层的开发在增强反应物迁移、改善选择性和创造受限反应环境方面发挥了重要作用[10.1002/anie.200703335]。

串联催化的应用由新型In2O3-Pt/Al2O3催化剂为例，它将催化丙烷脱氢与选择性H2燃烧耦合[10.1126/science.abd4441]。这种催化剂展示了纳米尺度设计如何克服传统催化剂的局限性，实现快速稳定的丙烷氧化脱氢，达到超过常规丙烷脱氢热力学平衡的高单程产率。

通过串联催化提高效率和降低成本的潜力是巨大的。通过在单一催化剂中结合多个反应步骤，这种方法可以减少所需的单独处理单元数量，最小化中间产物分离和纯化步骤，并提高整体过程效率[10.1038/nchem.1018]。通过选择性燃烧立即去除H2推动催化脱氢向前进行，打破非氧化催化反应的热力学限制[10.1126/science.abh0424]。

此外，在串联催化过程中使用分级O2引入允许选择性燃烧H2产物，大大消除了热力学限制。这种方法只需要一个反应器和一种催化剂组成，显著降低了过程复杂性和成本[10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011]。

综合理解与未来展望：纳米尺度串联催化的进展为化学工程的未来研究和开发提供了令人兴奋的机会。在纳米尺度上设计和控制反应的能力为创造高效和选择性的过程开辟了可能性，这些过程可以模仿生物系统。未来的研究可能集中在将串联催化应用扩展到其他工业相关反应，如CO2转化或生物质增值。

此外，开发原位表征技术以更好地理解这些复杂系统中反应中间体的动态可能导致对反应途径更精确的控制。在催化剂设计中集成人工智能和机器学习可能加速发现具有优化性能的新型串联催化剂。

此外，纳米尺度串联催化的原理可以扩展到开发可以适应变化的反应条件或原料组成的"智能"催化系统，可能彻底改变化学制造中的过程控制。随着我们继续推动纳米尺度工程的边界，串联催化的未来在创造更可持续、更高效和更经济的化学过程方面具有巨大的前景。
