<English>
Tandem catalysis at the nanoscale represents a significant advancement in addressing the challenges of integrating multiple reaction steps in chemical engineering processes. This innovative approach involves the design of nanostructured catalysts that can perform sequential reactions within a single environment, offering considerable potential for improving process efficiency and reducing costs.

The introduction of nanocrystal tandem catalysts with multiple metal-metal oxide interfaces for sequential reactions marks a new frontier in catalysis [10.1038/nchem.1018]. These catalysts integrate different catalytic functions within a single nanostructured system, as exemplified by the CeO2-Pt-SiO2 catalyst that combines methanol decomposition and ethylene hydroformylation [10.1038/nchem.1018]. This approach allows for the rational design and assembly of nanocrystal bilayers with specific interfaces for each reaction step, enabling fine-tuning of catalytic properties and potentially higher selectivity and yield of desired products [10.1038/nchem.1018].

The principles of tandem catalysis have been further developed through the introduction of core/shell bifunctional catalysts. These catalysts enhance reactant migration and improve selectivity by creating a confined reaction environment that provides spatially confined effects and shape selectivity [10.1002/anie.200703335]. The core and shell components independently catalyze different reactions, increasing the collision probability between intermediates and active sites, which leads to higher selectivity in consecutive reactions [10.1002/anie.200703335].

A particularly promising application of tandem catalysis is demonstrated by the In2O3-Pt/Al2O3 catalyst for coupling catalytic propane dehydrogenation with selective H2 combustion. This nanoscale design overcomes limitations of traditional catalysts by allowing for rapid and stable oxidative dehydrogenation of propane, achieving high per-pass yields that exceed the thermodynamic equilibrium of conventional propane dehydrogenation [10.1126/science.abd4441]. The porous overcoating structure of this catalyst regulates the transport of hydrogen species and promotes selective H2 combustion, consequently driving catalytic propane dehydrogenation forward [10.1126/science.abh0424].

The implications for energy efficiency and process optimization are significant. By combining multiple reaction steps in a single catalyst, this approach can potentially reduce the number of separate process units required, minimize intermediate product separation and purification steps, and improve overall process efficiency [10.1038/nchem.1018]. The instant removal of H2 through selective combustion pushes the catalytic dehydrogenation process forward, breaking the thermodynamic limitations of nonoxidative catalytic reactions [10.1126/science.abh0424]. This results in higher product yields from a given amount of feedstock, reduced separation and recycling costs due to higher single-pass conversions, and lower capital and operating costs from using a simpler, single-stage process [10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011].

Furthermore, the staged introduction of O2 co-reactants during alkane dehydrogenation leads to selective combustion of H2 products and substantial removal of thermodynamic constraints [10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011]. This approach requires only one reactor and one catalytic composition, significantly decreasing process complexity and cost [10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of nanoscale tandem catalysts represents a paradigm shift in chemical engineering, offering a powerful approach to designing high-performance, multifunctional catalysts. This technology has the potential to revolutionize various industrial processes by enabling more efficient, integrated, and sustainable chemical transformations.

Looking forward, research efforts may focus on expanding the application of tandem catalysis to a broader range of chemical processes, particularly in the production of high-value chemicals and sustainable energy solutions. The biomimetic approach of direct transfer of reactive intermediates between catalyst functions opens up possibilities for developing highly efficient and selective catalytic processes inspired by biological systems.

Future developments may also involve the integration of advanced materials science and nanotechnology to create even more sophisticated catalyst designs. This could include the development of self-assembling nanostructures or stimuli-responsive catalysts that can adapt to changing reaction conditions in real-time.

Additionally, the combination of tandem catalysis with other emerging technologies, such as artificial intelligence for catalyst design and optimization, or the use of renewable energy sources to drive catalytic processes, could lead to breakthroughs in sustainable chemical manufacturing. As we continue to push the boundaries of nanoscale engineering, the potential for tandem catalysis to address global challenges in energy efficiency and environmental sustainability becomes increasingly promising.

<Chinese>
纳米尺度上的串联催化代表了化学工程过程中整合多个反应步骤挑战的重大进展。这种创新方法涉及设计可以在单一环境中执行顺序反应的纳米结构催化剂，为提高过程效率和降低成本提供了巨大潜力。

引入具有多个金属-金属氧化物界面的纳米晶串联催化剂用于顺序反应，标志着催化领域的新前沿[10.1038/nchem.1018]。这些催化剂在单一纳米结构系统中整合了不同的催化功能，以CeO2-Pt-SiO2催化剂为例，该催化剂结合了甲醇分解和乙烯氢甲酰化[10.1038/nchem.1018]。这种方法允许理性设计和组装具有特定界面的纳米晶双层，以实现每个反应步骤的催化特性的微调，从而可能提高目标产品的选择性和产率[10.1038/nchem.1018]。

通过引入核壳双功能催化剂，串联催化的原理得到了进一步发展。这些催化剂通过创造一个限制性反应环境来增强反应物迁移并提高选择性，提供空间限制效应和形状选择性[10.1002/anie.200703335]。核心和壳层组件独立催化不同的反应，增加了中间体和活性位点之间的碰撞概率，从而在连续反应中实现更高的选择性[10.1002/anie.200703335]。

串联催化的一个特别有前景的应用是由In2O3-Pt/Al2O3催化剂实现的，用于耦合催化丙烷脱氢与选择性H2燃烧。这种纳米尺度设计克服了传统催化剂的限制，实现了丙烷的快速和稳定的氧化脱氢，获得了超过常规丙烷脱氢热力学平衡的高单程产率[10.1126/science.abd4441]。该催化剂的多孔过覆盖结构调节氢物种的传输，促进选择性H2燃烧，从而推动催化丙烷脱氢向前发展[10.1126/science.abh0424]。

对能源效率和过程优化的影响是显著的。通过在单一催化剂中结合多个反应步骤，这种方法可能减少所需的单独过程单元数量，最小化中间产品分离和纯化步骤，并提高整体过程效率[10.1038/nchem.1018]。通过选择性燃烧瞬时去除H2推动催化脱氢过程向前发展，打破了非氧化催化反应的热力学限制[10.1126/science.abh0424]。这导致了从给定数量的原料中获得更高的产品产率，由于更高的单程转化率而降低了分离和回收成本，以及使用更简单的单级过程而降低了资本和运营成本[10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011]。

此外，在烷烃脱氢过程中分阶段引入O2共反应物导致H2产物的选择性燃烧和热力学约束的实质性消除[10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011]。这种方法仅需要一个反应器和一种催化剂组成，显著降低了过程复杂性和成本[10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011]。

综合理解与未来展望：纳米尺度串联催化剂的发展代表了化学工程的范式转变，为设计高性能、多功能催化剂提供了强大的方法。这项技术有潜力通过实现更高效、更集成和更可持续的化学转化来革新各种工业过程。

展望未来，研究努力可能集中在将串联催化的应用扩展到更广泛的化学过程，特别是在高价值化学品和可持续能源解决方案的生产方面。反应中间体在催化功能之间直接转移的仿生方法为开发受生物系统启发的高效和选择性催化过程开辟了可能性。

未来的发展可能还涉及先进材料科学和纳米技术的整合，以创造更复杂的催化剂设计。这可能包括开发自组装纳米结构或能够实时适应变化反应条件的刺激响应催化剂。

此外，将串联催化与其他新兴技术相结合，如用于催化剂设计和优化的人工智能，或使用可再生能源来驱动催化过程，可能会在可持续化学制造方面取得突破。随着我们继续推动纳米尺度工程的边界，串联催化在解决能源效率和环境可持续性全球挑战方面的潜力变得越来越有希望。
