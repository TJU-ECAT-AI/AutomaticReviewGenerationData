<English>
The introduction, principles, and application of tandem catalysis in chemical engineering processes, with a focus on improving efficiency and reducing costs, have been exemplified by a novel nanoscale porous overcoating catalyst for coupling catalytic propane dehydrogenation with selective H2 combustion. This innovative approach addresses several challenges in integrating multiple reaction steps and offers significant potential for process optimization and energy efficiency.

Tandem catalysis at the nanoscale involves the coupling of multiple reactions within a single condition, presenting an effective scheme for emerging chemical industries [10.1126/science.abh0424]. The key principle behind this approach is the coordination of reactive intermediate transport among different catalytic sites, allowing for precise control over reaction sequences and selectivity [10.1126/science.abh0424]. This is achieved through the careful design of catalysts that can mediate multiple reactions under the same conditions, even when reactants, intermediates, and products coexist [10.1126/science.abh0424].

The In2O3-Pt/Al2O3 catalyst exemplifies this concept by coupling catalytic propane dehydrogenation with selective H2 combustion. This nanoscale design features a porous overcoating structure that regulates the transport of hydrogen species and promotes selective H2 combustion, consequently driving the catalytic propane dehydrogenation forward [10.1126/science.abh0424]. The instant removal of H2 through selective combustion forces the catalytic dehydrogenation to proceed, breaking the thermodynamic limitations of conventional nonoxidative catalytic reactions [10.1126/science.abh0424].

The application of tandem catalysis at the nanoscale offers several advantages for chemical engineering processes:

1. Improved yields: The tandem catalyst outperforms state-of-the-art oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) catalysts and exceeds the thermodynamic equilibrium conversion for non-oxidative propane dehydrogenation (PDH) [10.1126/science.abd4441].

2. Process simplification: The approach requires only one reactor and one catalytic composition, significantly decreasing process complexity and cost [10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011].

3. Enhanced selectivity: The design allows for high selectivity in consecutive reactions by improving the collision probability between intermediates and active sites [10.1002/anie.200703335].

4. Removal of thermodynamic constraints: Selective combustion of H2 products enables higher alkene yields than equilibrium would normally permit [10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011].

5. Scalability: The approach can be implemented in large-scale reactors using multiple injectors or ceramic membranes [10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011].

The potential implications for energy efficiency and process optimization are significant. By combining multiple reaction steps in a single catalyst, this approach can reduce the number of separate process units required, minimize intermediate product separation and purification steps, and improve overall process efficiency [10.1038/nchem.1018]. The ability to rationally design and assemble nanostructured catalysts with specific interfaces for each reaction step allows for fine-tuning of catalytic properties and potentially higher selectivity and yield of desired products [10.1038/nchem.1018].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in nanoscale tandem catalysis represent a paradigm shift in chemical engineering, offering unprecedented control over complex reaction networks. This approach mimics the efficiency of biological systems, where multiple reactions occur in close proximity within cellular environments. Future research may focus on:

1. Expanding the range of reactions that can be coupled in tandem catalysts, potentially leading to the development of "one-pot" synthesis methods for complex molecules.

2. Incorporating artificial intelligence and machine learning to optimize catalyst design and predict novel combinations of catalytic functions.

3. Exploring the integration of tandem catalysis with other emerging technologies, such as flow chemistry and additive manufacturing, to create highly efficient and flexible chemical production systems.

4. Investigating the potential of tandem catalysis in addressing global challenges, such as CO2 utilization, renewable energy storage, and sustainable chemical production.

5. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamics of reactive intermediates within nanoscale tandem catalysts, leading to more precise control over reaction pathways.

These future directions could revolutionize chemical manufacturing, leading to more sustainable, energy-efficient, and economically viable processes across various industries.

串联催化在化学工程过程中的引入、原理和应用，重点关注提高效率和降低成本，以一种新型纳米多孔包覆催化剂为例，该催化剂用于耦合催化丙烷脱氢与选择性氢气燃烧。这种创新方法解决了整合多个反应步骤的几个挑战，并为过程优化和能源效率提供了重要潜力。

纳米尺度的串联催化涉及在单一条件下耦合多个反应，为新兴化学工业提供了一个有效的方案[10.1126/science.abh0424]。这种方法的关键原理是协调不同催化位点之间的反应中间体传输，从而精确控制反应序列和选择性[10.1126/science.abh0424]。这是通过精心设计能够在相同条件下调节多个反应的催化剂来实现的，即使在反应物、中间体和产物共存的情况下也能实现[10.1126/science.abh0424]。

In2O3-Pt/Al2O3催化剂例证了这一概念，它将催化丙烷脱氢与选择性氢气燃烧耦合在一起。这种纳米尺度设计具有多孔包覆结构，可调节氢物种的传输并促进选择性氢气燃烧，从而推动催化丙烷脱氢向前进行[10.1126/science.abh0424]。通过选择性燃烧立即去除氢气，促使催化脱氢反应向前进行，打破了传统非氧化催化反应的热力学限制[10.1126/science.abh0424]。

纳米尺度串联催化的应用为化学工程过程提供了几个优势：

1. 提高产率：串联催化剂的性能优于最先进的氧化脱氢丙烷（ODHP）催化剂，并超过了非氧化丙烷脱氢（PDH）的热力学平衡转化率[10.1126/science.abd4441]。

2. 过程简化：该方法仅需一个反应器和一种催化剂组成，显著降低了过程复杂性和成本[10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011]。

3. 提高选择性：该设计通过提高中间体和活性位点之间的碰撞概率，实现了连续反应的高选择性[10.1002/anie.200703335]。

4. 消除热力学限制：选择性燃烧氢气产物使得烯烃产率高于平衡状态下的正常水平[10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011]。

5. 可扩展性：该方法可以在大规模反应器中使用多个喷射器或陶瓷膜来实现[10.1016/j.jcat.2003.12.011]。

对能源效率和过程优化的潜在影响是显著的。通过在单一催化剂中结合多个反应步骤，这种方法可以减少所需的单独过程单元数量，最小化中间产物分离和纯化步骤，并提高整体过程效率[10.1038/nchem.1018]。能够理性设计和组装具有特定界面的纳米结构催化剂，用于每个反应步骤，允许对催化性能进行微调，并可能提高所需产品的选择性和产率[10.1038/nchem.1018]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：纳米尺度串联催化的进展代表了化学工程的一个范式转变，为复杂反应网络提供了前所未有的控制能力。这种方法模仿了生物系统的效率，在细胞环境中多个反应在近距离发生。未来的研究可能集中在：

1. 扩大可以在串联催化剂中耦合的反应范围，可能导致复杂分子的"一锅法"合成方法的开发。

2. 结合人工智能和机器学习来优化催化剂设计并预测新的催化功能组合。

3. 探索串联催化与其他新兴技术的整合，如流动化学和增材制造，以创建高效灵活的化学生产系统。

4. 研究串联催化在应对全球挑战方面的潜力，如二氧化碳利用、可再生能源存储和可持续化学品生产。

5. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解纳米尺度串联催化剂中反应中间体的动态，从而更精确地控制反应路径。

这些未来方向可能彻底改变化学制造，在各种行业中实现更可持续、更节能和更具经济可行性的过程。
