<English>
Experimental verification and quantification of oxygen-induced surface segregation of subsurface 3d atoms (Ni and Co) in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems have been achieved through the combined use of High-Resolution Electron Energy Loss Spectroscopy (HREELS) and Auger Electron Spectroscopy (AES). These techniques provide complementary information on the segregation process, its kinetics, and activation barriers.

HREELS has been utilized to monitor the segregation of Ni and Co in Pt-3d-Pt(111) subsurface configurations when exposed to oxygen [10.1021/cr300096b_6]. The onset of characteristic ν(NiO) and ν(CoO) vibrational modes in the HREEL spectra confirms the segregation of subsurface Ni and Co after exposing the subsurface Pt-3d-Pt(111) configuration to oxygen at different temperatures [10.1021/cr300096b_6]. This spectroscopic evidence provides a qualitative verification of the segregation process.

AES measurements have been employed to quantify the segregation kinetics [10.1021/cr300096b_6]. The AES intensity ratios of Co/Pt or Ni/Pt were used to monitor the interchange between the subsurface and surface structures after exposure to oxygen [10.1021/cr300096b_6]. For instance, the normalized Ni/Pt AES ratio increases with oxygen exposure to the Pt-Ni-Pt(111) surface, indicating that subsurface Ni atoms start to segregate to the surface due to the strong interaction of the atomic oxygen adsorbate with surface Ni atoms [10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7].

The activation barriers for oxygen-induced segregation have been estimated using Arrhenius equation analysis of AES data [10.1021/cr300096b_6]. For the segregation of subsurface Ni to the surface in oxygen, the activation barrier was found to be 15 ± 2 kcal/mol, while for Co it was 7 ± 1 kcal/mol [10.1021/jp061697z]. These experimental results align well with DFT predictions, which suggest that Ni should have a higher activation barrier for segregation compared to Co due to its smaller driving potential for segregation [10.1021/cr300096b_6].

The experimental findings are consistent with DFT calculations showing that in the presence of adsorbed oxygen, the Pt-3d-Pt subsurface structure is no longer thermodynamically stable [10.1021/jp061697z]. Instead, the stable surface structure is the one with 3d metals on the topmost surface (3d-Pt-Pt), leading to a thermodynamic driving force for the segregation of subsurface 3d atoms [10.1021/jp061697z].

The measured activation barriers follow the trend predicted by DFT calculations, decreasing as the d-band center shifts closer to the Fermi level for different 3d metals [10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7]. DFT results also suggest that the activation barrier for the segregation of other subsurface 3d metals (Ti, V, Cr, Mn, Fe) should be even lower than that for Co or Ni [10.1021/jp061697z].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The combined use of experimental techniques (HREELS and AES) and theoretical predictions (DFT) has provided a comprehensive understanding of oxygen-induced surface segregation in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems. This integrated approach has revealed not only the occurrence of segregation but also its kinetics and energetics. Future research directions could focus on:

1. Expanding the study to other 3d metals (Ti, V, Cr, Mn, Fe) to verify the DFT predictions of even lower activation barriers for these elements.

2. Investigating the influence of different adsorbates (beyond oxygen) on the segregation process, considering the dependence of stability on adsorbate coverage and electronegativity.

3. Employing in-situ techniques like EXAFS to study the segregation process under realistic reaction conditions, as suggested in the literature [10.1021/jp0548927].

4. Exploring the reversibility of the segregation process and its implications for catalyst stability and regeneration.

5. Developing strategies to control and manipulate the segregation process for tailoring catalyst properties in specific applications.

These future directions could lead to the design of more efficient and stable bimetallic catalysts with tunable surface properties for various catalytic processes.

<Chinese>
通过高分辨电子能量损失谱（HREELS）和俄歇电子能谱（AES）的综合使用，实现了对Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中氧诱导的亚表面3d原子（Ni和Co）表面偏析的实验验证和定量分析。这些技术为偏析过程、其动力学和活化能障提供了互补信息。

HREELS被用来监测Pt-3d-Pt(111)亚表面构型中暴露于氧气时Ni和Co的偏析[10.1021/cr300096b_6]。在HREEL谱中出现特征性的ν(NiO)和ν(CoO)振动模式，证实了在不同温度下将亚表面Pt-3d-Pt(111)构型暴露于氧气后，亚表面Ni和Co的偏析[10.1021/cr300096b_6]。这种光谱证据为偏析过程提供了定性验证。

AES测量被用来量化偏析动力学[10.1021/cr300096b_6]。Co/Pt或Ni/Pt的AES强度比被用来监测暴露于氧气后亚表面和表面结构之间的交换[10.1021/cr300096b_6]。例如，Pt-Ni-Pt(111)表面暴露于氧气时，归一化的Ni/Pt AES比率增加，表明由于原子氧吸附剂与表面Ni原子的强相互作用，亚表面Ni原子开始偏析到表面[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7]。

氧诱导偏析的活化能障通过对AES数据进行阿伦尼乌斯方程分析来估算[10.1021/cr300096b_6]。对于亚表面Ni向氧气中表面的偏析，活化能障被发现为15 ± 2 kcal/mol，而对于Co则为7 ± 1 kcal/mol [10.1021/jp061697z]。这些实验结果与DFT预测很好地吻合，DFT预测表明由于Ni的偏析驱动势较小，其偏析的活化能障应该比Co高[10.1021/cr300096b_6]。

实验结果与DFT计算一致，显示在吸附氧的存在下，Pt-3d-Pt亚表面结构不再热力学稳定[10.1021/jp061697z]。相反，稳定的表面结构是3d金属位于最上层表面（3d-Pt-Pt），这导致了亚表面3d原子偏析的热力学驱动力[10.1021/jp061697z]。

测得的活化能障遵循DFT计算预测的趋势，即随着不同3d金属的d带中心向费米能级靠近，活化能障降低[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7]。DFT结果还表明，其他亚表面3d金属（Ti，V，Cr，Mn，Fe）的偏析活化能障应该比Co或Ni更低[10.1021/jp061697z]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：实验技术（HREELS和AES）和理论预测（DFT）的结合使用，为Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中氧诱导的表面偏析提供了全面的理解。这种综合方法不仅揭示了偏析的发生，还揭示了其动力学和能量学。未来的研究方向可以集中在：

1. 扩展研究到其他3d金属（Ti，V，Cr，Mn，Fe），以验证DFT对这些元素更低活化能障的预测。

2. 考虑到稳定性对吸附剂覆盖率和电负性的依赖性，研究不同吸附剂（除氧气外）对偏析过程的影响。

3. 如文献所建议，采用原位技术如EXAFS来研究实际反应条件下的偏析过程[10.1021/jp0548927]。

4. 探索偏析过程的可逆性及其对催化剂稳定性和再生的影响。

5. 开发控制和操纵偏析过程的策略，以针对特定应用调整催化剂特性。

这些未来方向可能导致设计出更高效、更稳定的双金属催化剂，具有可调的表面特性，适用于各种催化过程。
