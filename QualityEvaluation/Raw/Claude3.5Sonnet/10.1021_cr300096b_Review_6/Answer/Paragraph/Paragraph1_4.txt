Experimental verification and quantification of oxygen-induced surface segregation of subsurface 3d atoms (Ni and Co) in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems have been conducted using various techniques, primarily High-Resolution Electron Energy Loss Spectroscopy (HREELS) and Auger Electron Spectroscopy (AES). These methods provide complementary information on the segregation process and its kinetics.

HREELS has been utilized to monitor the segregation of Ni and Co in Pt-3d-Pt(111) subsurface configurations when exposed to oxygen [10.1021/cr300096b_6]. The technique identifies characteristic ν(NiO) and ν(CoO) vibrational modes, which confirm the segregation of subsurface Ni and Co after exposing the subsurface Pt-3d-Pt(111) configuration to oxygen at different temperatures [10.1021/cr300096b_6]. This method provides a qualitative verification of the segregation process.

AES measurements have been employed to quantify the segregation kinetics [10.1021/cr300096b_6]. The AES intensity ratios of Co/Pt or Ni/Pt are used to monitor the interchange between the subsurface and surface structures after exposure to oxygen [10.1021/cr300096b_6]. For instance, the normalized Ni/Pt AES ratio increases with oxygen exposure to the Pt-Ni-Pt(111) surface, indicating that subsurface Ni atoms start to segregate to the surface due to the strong interaction of atomic oxygen adsorbates with surface Ni atoms [10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7].

The segregation kinetics are measured by tracking the change in AES ratio over time during oxygen exposure [10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7]. Activation barriers for segregation are estimated experimentally based on the time required for half of the subsurface 3d atoms to segregate to the surface [10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7]. Using an Arrhenius equation to analyze the AES data, the activation barriers for oxygen-induced segregation of Co and Ni are estimated to be 7 ± 1 and 15 ± 2 kcal/mol, respectively [10.1021/cr300096b_6].

These experimental results align well with DFT predictions. DFT calculations show that:

1. The 3d-Pt-Pt(111) surface structures are more stable than Pt-3d-Pt(111) structures in the presence of oxygen, consistent with the observed segregation [10.1021/jp061697z].
2. Oxygen binding is stronger on 3d-Pt-Pt(111) surfaces compared to Pt, which agrees with the experimental observation of segregation driven by oxygen adsorption [10.1021/jp061697z].
3. The activation barriers for other 3d metals (Ti, V, Cr, Mn, Fe) are predicted to be even lower than those for Co or Ni [10.1021/jp061697z].

The lower activation barrier for Co segregation compared to Ni matches the DFT-predicted trend of easier segregation for earlier 3d transition metals [10.1021/cr300096b_6]. This trend is attributed to the shift of the d-band center closer to the Fermi level for different 3d metals [10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7].

However, experiments reveal some nuances not captured by DFT, such as the coexistence of outward segregation and inward diffusion processes, particularly for Co [10.1021/jp061697z]. This highlights the complementary nature of experimental and theoretical approaches in understanding these complex systems.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The combination of experimental techniques (HREELS and AES) with DFT calculations has provided a comprehensive understanding of oxygen-induced surface segregation in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems. This approach has revealed not only the occurrence of segregation but also quantified its kinetics and energetics. 

Future research directions could include:

1. Expanding the study to other 3d metals (Ti, V, Cr, Mn, Fe) to verify the DFT predictions of even lower activation barriers for these elements.
2. Investigating the impact of different adsorbates (e.g., nitrogen, carbon monoxide) on segregation behavior to understand the role of adsorbate electronegativity and coverage.
3. Conducting in-situ studies under realistic catalytic conditions using techniques like EXAFS to bridge the gap between model systems and practical applications.
4. Developing more sophisticated theoretical models that can account for the complex interplay between segregation and diffusion processes observed experimentally.
5. Exploring the potential of tailored bimetallic catalysts with controlled surface compositions for specific catalytic reactions, leveraging the understanding of adsorbate-induced segregation.

These future endeavors could lead to the design of more efficient and stable bimetallic catalysts for various applications, including propane dehydrogenation and other industrially relevant processes.

<Chinese>
实验验证和量化Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中亚表面3d原子（Ni和Co）的氧诱导表面偏析主要使用了高分辨电子能量损失谱（HREELS）和俄歇电子能谱（AES）等技术。这些方法为偏析过程及其动力学提供了互补信息。

HREELS被用于监测Pt-3d-Pt(111)亚表面构型中Ni和Co在暴露于氧气时的偏析[10.1021/cr300096b_6]。该技术识别特征性的ν(NiO)和ν(CoO)振动模式，确认了亚表面Pt-3d-Pt(111)构型在不同温度下暴露于氧气后亚表面Ni和Co的偏析[10.1021/cr300096b_6]。这种方法提供了偏析过程的定性验证。

AES测量被用于量化偏析动力学[10.1021/cr300096b_6]。Co/Pt或Ni/Pt的AES强度比被用于监测氧气暴露后亚表面和表面结构之间的交换[10.1021/cr300096b_6]。例如，Pt-Ni-Pt(111)表面暴露于氧气时，归一化的Ni/Pt AES比率增加，表明由于原子氧吸附剂与表面Ni原子的强相互作用，亚表面Ni原子开始偏析到表面[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7]。

偏析动力学通过在氧气暴露期间追踪AES比率随时间的变化来测量[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7]。偏析的活化能障根据一半亚表面3d原子偏析到表面所需的时间进行实验估计[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7]。使用阿伦尼乌斯方程分析AES数据，Co和Ni的氧诱导偏析活化能障分别估计为7 ± 1和15 ± 2 kcal/mol [10.1021/cr300096b_6]。

这些实验结果与DFT预测很好地吻合。DFT计算表明：

1. 在氧气存在的情况下，3d-Pt-Pt(111)表面结构比Pt-3d-Pt(111)结构更稳定，这与观察到的偏析一致[10.1021/jp061697z]。
2. 与Pt相比，氧在3d-Pt-Pt(111)表面的结合更强，这与实验观察到的氧吸附驱动偏析一致[10.1021/jp061697z]。
3. 预测其他3d金属（Ti，V，Cr，Mn，Fe）的活化能障甚至比Co或Ni更低[10.1021/jp061697z]。

Co偏析的活化能障比Ni低，这与DFT预测的早期3d过渡金属更易偏析的趋势相符[10.1021/cr300096b_6]。这一趋势归因于不同3d金属的d带中心向费米能级靠近[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7]。

然而，实验揭示了一些DFT未能捕捉到的细微差别，如外向偏析和内向扩散过程的共存，特别是对于Co [10.1021/jp061697z]。这突出了实验和理论方法在理解这些复杂系统中的互补性。

※※※

综合理解和未来展望：实验技术（HREELS和AES）与DFT计算的结合为理解Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中的氧诱导表面偏析提供了全面的认识。这种方法不仅揭示了偏析的发生，还量化了其动力学和能量学。

未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 扩展研究到其他3d金属（Ti，V，Cr，Mn，Fe），以验证DFT对这些元素更低活化能障的预测。
2. 研究不同吸附剂（如氮气、一氧化碳）对偏析行为的影响，以理解吸附剂电负性和覆盖率的作用。
3. 使用EXAFS等技术在实际催化条件下进行原位研究，以弥合模型系统和实际应用之间的差距。
4. 开发更复杂的理论模型，能够解释实验观察到的偏析和扩散过程之间的复杂相互作用。
5. 利用对吸附剂诱导偏析的理解，探索针对特定催化反应的定制双金属催化剂，控制表面组成。

这些未来的努力可能导致更高效、更稳定的双金属催化剂的设计，用于各种应用，包括丙烷脱氢和其他工业相关过程。
