<English>
Experimental verification and quantification of oxygen-induced surface segregation of subsurface 3d atoms (Ni and Co) in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems have been achieved through the complementary use of High-Resolution Electron Energy Loss Spectroscopy (HREELS) and Auger Electron Spectroscopy (AES). These techniques provide valuable insights into the segregation process, its kinetics, and activation barriers, which can be compared with Density Functional Theory (DFT) predictions.

HREELS has been utilized to monitor the segregation of Ni and Co in Pt-3d-Pt(111) subsurface configurations when exposed to oxygen [10.1021/cr300096b_6]. The onset of characteristic ν(NiO) and ν(CoO) vibrational modes confirms the segregation of subsurface Ni and Co after exposing the subsurface Pt-3d-Pt(111) configuration to oxygen at different temperatures [10.1021/cr300096b_6]. This technique provides a qualitative verification of the segregation process.

AES measurements have been employed to quantify the segregation kinetics [10.1021/cr300096b_6]. The AES intensity ratios of Co/Pt or Ni/Pt are used to monitor the interchange between the subsurface and surface structures after exposure to oxygen [10.1021/cr300096b_6]. The normalized Ni/Pt or Co/Pt AES ratio increases with oxygen exposure, indicating segregation of subsurface 3d atoms to the surface [10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7]. 

The segregation kinetics are measured by tracking the change in AES ratio over time during oxygen exposure [10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7]. Activation barriers for segregation are estimated experimentally based on the time required for half of the subsurface 3d atoms to segregate to the surface [10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7]. Using an Arrhenius equation to analyze the AES data, the activation barriers for oxygen-induced segregation of Co and Ni are estimated to be 7 ± 1 and 15 ± 2 kcal/mol, respectively [10.1021/cr300096b_6].

These experimental results align well with DFT predictions. DFT calculations show that:

1. The 3d-Pt-Pt surface structures are more stable than Pt-3d-Pt structures in the presence of oxygen, consistent with the observed segregation [10.1021/jp061697z].
2. Oxygen binding is stronger on 3d-Pt-Pt surfaces compared to Pt, which agrees with the experimental observation of segregation driven by oxygen adsorption [10.1021/jp061697z].
3. The activation barriers for other 3d metals (Ti, V, Cr, Mn, Fe) are predicted to be even lower than those for Co or Ni [10.1021/jp061697z].

The lower activation barrier for Co segregation compared to Ni matches the DFT-predicted trend of easier segregation for earlier 3d transition metals [10.1021/cr300096b_6]. This trend is attributed to the shift of the d-band center closer to the Fermi level for different 3d metals [10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7].

However, experiments revealed some nuances not captured by DFT, such as the coexistence of outward segregation and inward diffusion processes, particularly for Co [10.1021/jp061697z]. This highlights the complementary nature of experimental and theoretical approaches in understanding these complex systems.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The combination of experimental techniques (HREELS and AES) with DFT calculations has provided a comprehensive understanding of oxygen-induced surface segregation in Pt-3d-Pt(111) bimetallic systems. This approach has successfully verified and quantified the segregation process, revealing important insights into the kinetics and thermodynamics of these systems.

Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Expanding the range of 3d metals studied to validate the DFT predictions for Ti, V, Cr, Mn, and Fe, which are expected to have even lower activation barriers for segregation.

2. Investigating the effects of different adsorbates, beyond oxygen and nitrogen, to further understand the role of adsorbate electronegativity and coverage on segregation behavior.

3. Developing in-situ experimental techniques, such as EXAFS studies under reaction conditions, to reveal whether the diffusion of subsurface 3d metals occurs during actual catalytic processes like reforming [10.1021/jp0548927].

4. Exploring the potential of these bimetallic systems in catalytic applications, leveraging the unique surface properties that result from controlled segregation.

5. Investigating the long-term stability and reversibility of the segregation process under varying conditions, which is crucial for practical applications.

6. Developing more sophisticated theoretical models that can capture the nuances observed experimentally, such as the coexistence of outward segregation and inward diffusion processes.

These future directions could lead to the design of more efficient and stable bimetallic catalysts with tunable surface properties, potentially revolutionizing various catalytic processes in the chemical industry.

<Chinese>
通过高分辨电子能量损失谱（HREELS）和俄歇电子能谱（AES）的互补使用，实验验证和量化了Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中亚表面3d原子（Ni和Co）的氧诱导表面偏析。这些技术为偏析过程、其动力学和活化能障提供了宝贵的见解，可与密度泛函理论（DFT）预测进行比较。

HREELS被用于监测Pt-3d-Pt(111)亚表面构型中Ni和Co在暴露于氧气时的偏析[10.1021/cr300096b_6]。特征ν(NiO)和ν(CoO)振动模式的出现证实了在不同温度下将亚表面Pt-3d-Pt(111)构型暴露于氧气后，亚表面Ni和Co的偏析[10.1021/cr300096b_6]。这种技术提供了偏析过程的定性验证。

AES测量被用于量化偏析动力学[10.1021/cr300096b_6]。Co/Pt或Ni/Pt的AES强度比被用于监测暴露于氧气后亚表面和表面结构之间的交换[10.1021/cr300096b_6]。归一化的Ni/Pt或Co/Pt AES比随氧气暴露增加而增加，表明亚表面3d原子向表面偏析[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7]。

偏析动力学通过在氧气暴露期间跟踪AES比随时间的变化来测量[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7]。偏析的活化能障基于一半亚表面3d原子偏析到表面所需的时间进行实验估计[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7]。使用阿伦尼乌斯方程分析AES数据，估计Co和Ni的氧诱导偏析活化能障分别为7±1和15±2 kcal/mol [10.1021/cr300096b_6]。

这些实验结果与DFT预测很好地吻合。DFT计算表明：

1. 在氧气存在下，3d-Pt-Pt表面结构比Pt-3d-Pt结构更稳定，这与观察到的偏析一致[10.1021/jp061697z]。
2. 与Pt相比，氧在3d-Pt-Pt表面上的结合更强，这与实验观察到的由氧吸附驱动的偏析一致[10.1021/jp061697z]。
3. 预测其他3d金属（Ti，V，Cr，Mn，Fe）的活化能障甚至比Co或Ni更低[10.1021/jp061697z]。

Co偏析的较低活化能障与Ni相比，符合DFT预测的前期3d过渡金属更容易偏析的趋势[10.1021/cr300096b_6]。这种趋势归因于不同3d金属的d带中心向费米能级的移动[10.1007/s11244-008-9119-7]。

然而，实验揭示了DFT未捕捉到的一些细微差别，如向外偏析和向内扩散过程的共存，特别是对于Co [10.1021/jp061697z]。这突显了实验和理论方法在理解这些复杂系统时的互补性质。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：实验技术（HREELS和AES）与DFT计算的结合为理解Pt-3d-Pt(111)双金属系统中的氧诱导表面偏析提供了全面的认识。这种方法成功地验证和量化了偏析过程，揭示了这些系统动力学和热力学的重要见解。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 扩大研究的3d金属范围，以验证DFT对Ti、V、Cr、Mn和Fe的预测，这些金属预计具有更低的偏析活化能障。

2. 研究除氧和氮以外的不同吸附质的影响，以进一步理解吸附质电负性和覆盖度对偏析行为的作用。

3. 开发原位实验技术，如反应条件下的EXAFS研究，以揭示在实际催化过程（如重整）中是否发生亚表面3d金属的扩散[10.1021/jp0548927]。

4. 探索这些双金属系统在催化应用中的潜力，利用受控偏析产生的独特表面性质。

5. 研究在不同条件下偏析过程的长期稳定性和可逆性，这对实际应用至关重要。

6. 开发更复杂的理论模型，能够捕捉到实验观察到的细微差别，如向外偏析和向内扩散过程的共存。

这些未来方向可能导致设计更高效和稳定的双金属催化剂，具有可调的表面性质，潜在地革新化学工业中的各种催化过程。
