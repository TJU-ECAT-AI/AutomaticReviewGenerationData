<English>
The use of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) offers significant advantages in terms of propylene selectivity compared to other oxidants, particularly oxygen (O2). This review analyzes the advantages, challenges, and potential research directions for N2O-ODHP.

Advantages of N2O as an oxidant:

1. Higher propylene selectivity: N2O consistently provides higher propene selectivity compared to O2 across various catalysts [10.1021/jp070747c]. For example, propene selectivity can increase from 55% with O2 to 75% with N2O for 0.5% VOx/γ-Al2O3 catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_8].

2. Reduced over-oxidation: N2O inhibits direct propane oxidation and consecutive oxidation of propene to COx, leading to improved selectivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025].

3. Lower oxidation state of catalyst: N2O promotes a more reduced state of catalytic sites, which is associated with higher propylene selectivity [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2].

4. Lower reaction temperatures: N2O-ODHP can be carried out at 643-773 K, significantly lower than the 823-923 K required for mixed metal oxide catalysts with O2 [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020].

5. Dual benefits: The reaction allows for the functionalization of propane, a cheap and abundant feedstock, while also utilizing N2O, an environmentally harmful gas [10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035].

Catalysts employed in N2O-ODHP:

1. Iron-based zeolites: Steam-activated Fe-ZSM-5 has shown the most promising results, with initial propene yields up to 25% [10.1021/ie0493863].

2. Vanadium-based catalysts: Supported on materials like MCM-41, SiO2, and γ-Al2O3 [10.1016/j.cattod.2005.11.028].

3. Molybdenum-based catalysts: Such as NiMoO4 [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005].

Critical challenges:

1. Catalyst deactivation: Rapid deactivation due to coke formation is a major drawback [10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010].

2. Lower overall activity: N2O results in lower catalytic activity compared to O2 due to its weaker oxidizing potential [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025].

3. Conversion-selectivity trade-off: While N2O improves selectivity, it also decreases overall conversion [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.09.043].

4. Incomplete regeneration: Regeneration of catalysts used in N2O-mediated ODHP is not fully reversible [10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010].

Strategies to address limitations:

1. Catalyst design optimization: Develop catalysts with improved resistance to coke formation and better stability in reduced states [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005].

2. Reaction conditions optimization: Fine-tune temperature, pressure, and feed composition to minimize coke formation while maintaining high propylene yields [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020].

3. Hybrid oxidant systems: Investigate the use of N2O in combination with other oxidants to balance selectivity and activity [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2].

4. Advanced characterization and mechanistic studies: Utilize techniques like TEOM for simultaneous measurement of reaction and coking to better understand deactivation mechanisms [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005].

5. Process engineering improvements: Develop reactor designs that allow for better control of the oxidation state of the catalyst throughout the reaction [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005].

6. Economic and environmental assessment: Evaluate the overall feasibility of N2O-ODHP, considering factors such as N2O production, handling, and potential environmental impacts [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The use of N2O as an oxidant in ODHP presents a promising avenue for improving propylene selectivity while potentially mitigating N2O emissions. The unique properties of N2O, particularly its ability to maintain catalysts in a more reduced state, offer significant advantages over traditional oxidants. However, the challenges of catalyst deactivation and lower overall activity must be addressed to make this process commercially viable.

Future research efforts should focus on developing more stable and active catalysts specifically designed for N2O-ODHP. This may involve exploring novel support materials, bimetallic catalysts, or hierarchical pore structures to balance selectivity, activity, and stability. Additionally, the integration of N2O-ODHP with other industrial processes that produce N2O as a by-product could create synergies and improve overall process economics.

Advanced in-situ characterization techniques and computational modeling could provide deeper insights into the reaction mechanisms and catalyst behavior under N2O-ODHP conditions. This knowledge could guide the rational design of next-generation catalysts and process optimizations.

Furthermore, the development of efficient N2O production and purification methods tailored for ODHP applications could enhance the economic feasibility of this process. Exploring the potential of N2O-ODHP in the broader context of sustainable chemical production and greenhouse gas mitigation could open up new opportunities for industrial implementation.

<Chinese>
使用一氧化二氮（N2O）作为丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）的氧化剂相比其他氧化剂，特别是氧气（O2），在丙烯选择性方面具有显著优势。本综述分析了N2O-ODHP的优势、挑战和潜在研究方向。

N2O作为氧化剂的优势：

1. 更高的丙烯选择性：与O2相比，N2O在各种催化剂上一致地提供更高的丙烯选择性[10.1021/jp070747c]。例如，对于0.5% VOx/γ-Al2O3催化剂，丙烯选择性可以从使用O2时的55%提高到使用N2O时的75%[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_8]。

2. 减少过度氧化：N2O抑制丙烷的直接氧化和丙烯连续氧化为COx的过程，从而提高选择性[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025]。

3. 催化剂的较低氧化态：N2O促进催化位点处于更还原的状态，这与更高的丙烯选择性相关[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2]。

4. 较低的反应温度：N2O-ODHP可以在643-773 K进行，显著低于使用O2的混合金属氧化物催化剂所需的823-923 K [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020]。

5. 双重效益：该反应允许对丙烷（一种廉价且丰富的原料）进行官能化，同时利用N2O（一种环境有害气体）[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035]。

N2O-ODHP中使用的催化剂：

1. 铁基沸石：蒸汽活化的Fe-ZSM-5显示出最有希望的结果，初始丙烯产率高达25%[10.1021/ie0493863]。

2. 钒基催化剂：负载在MCM-41、SiO2和γ-Al2O3等材料上[10.1016/j.cattod.2005.11.028]。

3. 钼基催化剂：如NiMoO4 [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005]。

关键挑战：

1. 催化剂失活：由于焦炭形成导致的快速失活是一个主要缺点[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010]。

2. 整体活性较低：由于N2O的氧化能力较弱，与O2相比，其导致的催化活性较低[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025]。

3. 转化率-选择性权衡：虽然N2O提高了选择性，但也降低了整体转化率[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.09.043]。

4. 不完全再生：N2O介导的ODHP中使用的催化剂再生不是完全可逆的[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010]。

解决限制的策略：

1. 催化剂设计优化：开发具有改进的抗焦炭形成能力和在还原状态下更好稳定性的催化剂[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]。

2. 反应条件优化：微调温度、压力和进料组成，以最小化焦炭形成，同时保持高丙烯产率[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020]。

3. 混合氧化剂系统：研究将N2O与其他氧化剂结合使用，以平衡选择性和活性[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2]。

4. 先进表征和机理研究：利用TEOM等技术同时测量反应和焦炭形成，以更好地理解失活机理[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]。

5. 工艺工程改进：开发允许在整个反应过程中更好地控制催化剂氧化态的反应器设计[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005]。

6. 经济和环境评估：评估N2O-ODHP的整体可行性，考虑N2O生产、处理和潜在环境影响等因素[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：在ODHP中使用N2O作为氧化剂为提高丙烯选择性同时潜在减少N2O排放提供了一个有希望的途径。N2O的独特性质，特别是其保持催化剂处于更还原状态的能力，相对于传统氧化剂提供了显著优势。然而，催化剂失活和整体活性较低的挑战必须得到解决，才能使这一过程在商业上可行。

未来的研究努力应该集中在开发专门为N2O-ODHP设计的更稳定和活性的催化剂上。这可能涉及探索新型载体材料、双金属催化剂或分级孔结构，以平衡选择性、活性和稳定性。此外，将N2O-ODHP与其他产生N2O作为副产品的工业过程整合可能创造协同效应并改善整体工艺经济性。

先进的原位表征技术和计算建模可以提供对N2O-ODHP条件下反应机理和催化剂行为的更深入洞察。这些知识可以指导下一代催化剂的理性设计和工艺优化。

此外，开发针对ODHP应用的高效N2O生产和纯化方法可以提高这一过程的经济可行性。在更广泛的可持续化学品生产和温室气体减排背景下探索N2O-ODHP的潜力，可能为工业实施开辟新的机会。
