<English>
The use of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) offers significant advantages in terms of propylene selectivity compared to other oxidants, particularly oxygen (O2). The key advantages of using N2O include:

1. Higher propylene selectivity: N2O consistently provides higher propene selectivity compared to O2 at similar propane conversion levels across various catalysts [10.1021/jp070747c]. This improvement in selectivity is attributed to N2O's ability to inhibit non-selective sites on the catalyst surface [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.09.043].

2. Reduced over-oxidation: N2O leads to a significant decrease in the formation of carbon oxides (CO and CO2) compared to O2, both from direct propane oxidation and consecutive propylene oxidation [10.1016/j.apcata.2006.11.021].

3. Lower oxidation state of catalyst: N2O promotes a more reduced state of catalytic sites, particularly for molybdenum and vanadium-based catalysts. This reduced state is associated with higher selectivity [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005] [10.1016/j.apcata.2006.11.021].

4. Improved performance at lower temperatures: N2O-mediated ODHP can operate at substantially lower temperatures (643-773 K) compared to mixed metal oxide catalysts with O2 (823-923 K) [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020].

Various catalysts have been employed in N2O-ODHP, including:

1. Iron-based zeolites: Steam-activated Fe-ZSM-5 has shown the most promising results, with initial propene yields up to 25% [10.1021/ie0493863].
2. Vanadium-based catalysts: Supported on materials such as SiO2, MCM-41, and MCM-48 [10.1021/jp070747c].
3. Molybdenum-based catalysts: Including NiMoO4 and NiMoO4 + α-Sb2O4 mixtures [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005].

Critical challenges associated with catalyst deactivation and stability in N2O-ODHP include:

1. Coke formation: The primary cause of catalyst deactivation, leading to a decrease in propylene yield over time [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005].
2. Rapid deactivation: Particularly pronounced for certain catalyst types, such as BEA framework zeolites [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005].
3. Incomplete regeneration: The regeneration process of iron-containing zeolites is not fully reversible, leading to faster deactivation in subsequent cycles [10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010].
4. Lower overall activity: While N2O improves selectivity, it generally results in lower catalytic activity compared to O2 [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025].

To address these limitations and make N2O-ODHP more feasible for practical implementation, several strategies and research directions can be proposed:

1. Catalyst design optimization: Develop catalysts with improved resistance to coke formation and better stability in reduced states [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025].
2. Mesopore introduction: Explore methods like desilication to create mesopores in zeolites, enhancing transport properties and active site accessibility [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005].
3. Regeneration strategies: Optimize regeneration processes to efficiently remove coke and restore catalyst activity without altering the active site distribution [10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010].
4. Hybrid oxidant systems: Investigate the use of N2O in combination with other oxidants to achieve an optimal balance of activity and selectivity [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2].
5. Process engineering: Develop reactor designs that allow for better control of the oxidation state of the catalyst throughout the reaction [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005].
6. Mechanistic studies: Conduct more in-depth research on the reaction mechanisms and the role of different oxygen species in the ODHP process [10.1016/j.cattod.2005.11.028].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The use of N2O as an oxidant in ODHP represents a promising avenue for improving propylene selectivity while potentially addressing environmental concerns related to N2O emissions. The unique properties of N2O, particularly its ability to maintain catalysts in a more reduced state, offer a pathway to overcome the selectivity limitations of traditional O2-based processes. However, the challenges of catalyst deactivation and lower overall activity need to be addressed to make N2O-ODHP commercially viable.

Future research efforts might focus on developing novel catalyst designs that can better resist coke formation while maintaining high selectivity. This could involve exploring new support materials, bimetallic catalysts, or advanced preparation techniques to optimize the distribution and stability of active sites. Additionally, innovative reactor designs, such as membrane reactors or cyclic operation modes, could help balance the trade-offs between selectivity and activity.

The integration of N2O-ODHP with other industrial processes, particularly those that produce N2O as a by-product, could create synergies that improve the overall economic and environmental profile of propylene production. Furthermore, the development of more efficient N2O production methods could make this process more attractive from a cost perspective.

As the chemical industry continues to seek more sustainable and selective processes, N2O-ODHP has the potential to play a significant role in the future of propylene production, provided that the current challenges can be overcome through focused research and development efforts.

<Chinese>
在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）中使用一氧化二氮（N2O）作为氧化剂相比其他氧化剂，特别是氧气（O2），在丙烯选择性方面具有显著优势。使用N2O的主要优势包括：

1. 更高的丙烯选择性：在相似的丙烷转化水平下，N2O在各种催化剂上始终提供比O2更高的丙烯选择性[10.1021/jp070747c]。这种选择性的提高归因于N2O抑制催化剂表面非选择性位点的能力[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.09.043]。

2. 减少过度氧化：与O2相比，N2O显著减少了碳氧化物（CO和CO2）的形成，无论是来自直接丙烷氧化还是连续丙烯氧化[10.1016/j.apcata.2006.11.021]。

3. 催化剂的低氧化态：N2O促进催化活性位点处于更还原的状态，特别是对于钼和钒基催化剂。这种还原状态与更高的选择性相关[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005] [10.1016/j.apcata.2006.11.021]。

4. 在较低温度下表现更好：N2O介导的ODHP可以在显著较低的温度（643-773 K）下操作，相比之下，使用O2的混合金属氧化物催化剂需要823-923 K的温度[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020]。

N2O-ODHP中使用的各种催化剂包括：

1. 铁基沸石：蒸汽活化的Fe-ZSM-5显示了最有前景的结果，初始丙烯产率高达25%[10.1021/ie0493863]。
2. 钒基催化剂：负载在SiO2、MCM-41和MCM-48等材料上[10.1021/jp070747c]。
3. 钼基催化剂：包括NiMoO4和NiMoO4 + α-Sb2O4混合物[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005]。

N2O-ODHP中与催化剂失活和稳定性相关的关键挑战包括：

1. 积碳形成：催化剂失活的主要原因，导致丙烯产率随时间降低[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]。
2. 快速失活：某些类型的催化剂尤其明显，如BEA框架沸石[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]。
3. 不完全再生：含铁沸石的再生过程不完全可逆，导致后续循环中失活更快[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010]。
4. 整体活性较低：虽然N2O改善了选择性，但通常导致与O2相比催化活性较低[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025]。

为解决这些限制并使N2O-ODHP更适合实际应用，可以提出几种策略和研究方向：

1. 催化剂设计优化：开发具有改进的抗积碳形成能力和在还原态下更好稳定性的催化剂[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025]。
2. 介孔引入：探索如脱硅等方法在沸石中创造介孔，增强传输性能和活性位点可及性[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]。
3. 再生策略：优化再生过程，有效去除积碳并恢复催化剂活性，同时不改变活性位点分布[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010]。
4. 混合氧化剂系统：研究将N2O与其他氧化剂结合使用，以达到活性和选择性的最佳平衡[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2]。
5. 过程工程：开发能够在整个反应过程中更好地控制催化剂氧化态的反应器设计[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005]。
6. 机理研究：对ODHP过程中的反应机理和不同氧物种的作用进行更深入的研究[10.1016/j.cattod.2005.11.028]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：在ODHP中使用N2O作为氧化剂代表了一种提高丙烯选择性的有前景的方法，同时潜在地解决了与N2O排放相关的环境问题。N2O的独特性质，特别是其保持催化剂处于更还原状态的能力，为克服传统O2基过程的选择性限制提供了一条途径。然而，催化剂失活和整体活性较低的挑战需要解决，以使N2O-ODHP在商业上可行。

未来的研究努力可能集中在开发能更好地抵抗积碳形成同时保持高选择性的新型催化剂设计。这可能涉及探索新的载体材料、双金属催化剂或先进的制备技术，以优化活性位点的分布和稳定性。此外，创新的反应器设计，如膜反应器或循环操作模式，可能有助于平衡选择性和活性之间的权衡。

将N2O-ODHP与其他产生N2O作为副产品的工业过程整合，可能创造协同效应，改善丙烯生产的整体经济和环境概况。此外，开发更高效的N2O生产方法可能从成本角度使这一过程更具吸引力。

随着化工行业继续寻求更可持续和选择性的工艺，N2O-ODHP有潜力在未来的丙烯生产中发挥重要作用，前提是通过集中的研究和开发努力克服当前的挑战。
