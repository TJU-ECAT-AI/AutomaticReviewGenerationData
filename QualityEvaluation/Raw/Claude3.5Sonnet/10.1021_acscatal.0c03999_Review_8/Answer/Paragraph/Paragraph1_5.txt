<English>
The use of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) offers significant advantages in terms of propylene selectivity compared to other oxidants, particularly oxygen (O2). These advantages include:

1. Higher propylene selectivity: N2O consistently provides higher propene selectivity compared to O2 at similar propane conversion levels across various catalysts [10.1021/jp070747c]. For example, propene selectivity can reach 80% with N2O compared to only 50% with O2 at approximately 10% propane conversion [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010].

2. Reduced over-oxidation: N2O inhibits direct propane oxidation and consecutive oxidation of propene to COx, leading to improved selectivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025]. This is attributed to N2O's lower ability to reoxidize reduced catalyst species, particularly vanadium oxide (VOx) and molybdenum species [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005].

3. Lower operation temperature: N2O-ODHP can be carried out at 643-773 K, which is significantly lower than the 823-923 K required for mixed metal oxide catalysts with other oxidants [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020].

4. Improved catalytic performance: Some catalysts that perform poorly with O2 become selective when using N2O as the oxidant [10.1016/j.apcata.2006.11.021].

Various catalysts have been employed in N2O-ODHP, including:

1. Iron-based zeolites: Steam-activated Fe-ZSM-5 has shown initial propene yields up to 25% at 723-773 K [10.1021/ie0493863].
2. Vanadium-based catalysts: VOx supported on various materials such as SiO2, MCM-41, and MCM-48 [10.1021/jp070747c].
3. Molybdenum-based catalysts: NiMoO4 and NiMoO4 + α-Sb2O4 mixtures [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005].

However, critical challenges associated with catalyst deactivation and stability in N2O-ODHP include:

1. Coke formation: The main cause of catalyst deactivation, leading to a decrease in propylene yield and propane conversion over time [10.1016/j.micromeso.2014.07.032].
2. Reduction of active species: The limited ability of N2O to reoxidize reduced catalyst species can lead to catalyst deactivation [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.028].
3. Lower overall activity: While N2O improves selectivity, it also decreases overall catalytic activity compared to O2 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00836-5].

To address these limitations and make N2O-ODHP more feasible for practical implementation, several strategies and research directions can be pursued:

1. Catalyst design optimization: Develop catalysts with improved resistance to coke formation and better stability in reduced states [10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010].
2. Reaction conditions optimization: Fine-tune temperature, pressure, and feed composition to minimize coke formation while maintaining high propylene yields [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020].
3. Regeneration strategies: Explore efficient methods for in-situ or periodic catalyst regeneration to remove coke deposits and restore catalyst activity [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005].
4. Hybrid oxidant systems: Investigate the use of N2O in combination with other oxidants to achieve an optimal balance of activity and selectivity [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.09.043].
5. Process engineering: Develop reactor designs that allow for better control of the oxidation state of the catalyst throughout the reaction [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2].
6. Mechanistic studies: Conduct more in-depth research on the reaction mechanisms and catalyst surface interactions to better understand and control the factors affecting catalyst performance [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_8].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The use of N2O as an oxidant in ODHP represents a promising avenue for improving propylene selectivity and yields. The unique properties of N2O, such as its ability to maintain catalysts in a more reduced state and suppress over-oxidation, offer significant advantages over traditional oxidants like O2. However, the challenges of catalyst deactivation and lower overall activity need to be addressed to make N2O-ODHP commercially viable.

Future research efforts should focus on developing novel catalyst formulations that can maintain high selectivity while improving stability and activity. This may involve exploring bimetallic systems, optimizing support materials, or investigating new synthesis methods to control the dispersion and oxidation state of active species. Additionally, advanced in-situ characterization techniques could provide valuable insights into the dynamic changes occurring on the catalyst surface during the reaction, guiding the design of more effective catalysts.

From a process engineering perspective, innovative reactor designs that allow for cyclic operation or continuous regeneration could help overcome the limitations of catalyst deactivation. Furthermore, integrating N2O-ODHP with other industrial processes that produce N2O as a by-product could create synergies and improve the overall economics and sustainability of the process.

As research in this field progresses, it is crucial to conduct comprehensive techno-economic and life cycle analyses to evaluate the feasibility of N2O-ODHP compared to conventional propylene production methods. This holistic approach will help identify key areas for improvement and guide future research directions, ultimately paving the way for the practical implementation of N2O-ODHP technology in the chemical industry.

<Chinese>
在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）中使用一氧化二氮（N2O）作为氧化剂相比其他氧化剂（特别是氧气O2）在丙烯选择性方面具有显著优势。这些优势包括：

1. 更高的丙烯选择性：在相似的丙烷转化水平下，N2O在各种催化剂上始终提供比O2更高的丙烯选择性[10.1021/jp070747c]。例如，在约10%的丙烷转化率下，使用N2O时丙烯选择性可达80%，而使用O2时仅为50%[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010]。

2. 减少过度氧化：N2O抑制直接丙烷氧化和丙烯连续氧化生成COx，从而提高选择性[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025]。这归因于N2O重新氧化还原态催化剂物种（特别是钒氧化物（VOx）和钼物种）的能力较弱[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005]。

3. 较低的操作温度：N2O-ODHP可在643-773 K下进行，显著低于使用其他氧化剂的混合金属氧化物催化剂所需的823-923 K[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020]。

4. 改善的催化性能：一些在O2中表现不佳的催化剂在使用N2O作为氧化剂时变得具有选择性[10.1016/j.apcata.2006.11.021]。

N2O-ODHP中使用了各种催化剂，包括：

1. 铁基沸石：蒸汽活化的Fe-ZSM-5在723-773 K下显示出高达25%的初始丙烯产率[10.1021/ie0493863]。
2. 钒基催化剂：负载在SiO2、MCM-41和MCM-48等各种材料上的VOx[10.1021/jp070747c]。
3. 钼基催化剂：NiMoO4和NiMoO4 + α-Sb2O4混合物[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005]。

然而，N2O-ODHP中与催化剂失活和稳定性相关的关键挑战包括：

1. 积碳形成：催化剂失活的主要原因，导致丙烯产率和丙烷转化率随时间降低[10.1016/j.micromeso.2014.07.032]。
2. 活性物种还原：N2O重新氧化还原态催化剂物种的能力有限，可能导致催化剂失活[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.028]。
3. 整体活性降低：虽然N2O提高了选择性，但与O2相比，它也降低了整体催化活性[10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00836-5]。

为了解决这些限制并使N2O-ODHP更适合实际应用，可以采取以下几种策略和研究方向：

1. 催化剂设计优化：开发具有更好抗积碳形成能力和在还原态下更好稳定性的催化剂[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010]。
2. 反应条件优化：微调温度、压力和进料组成，以最小化积碳形成，同时保持高丙烯产率[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020]。
3. 再生策略：探索高效的原位或周期性催化剂再生方法，以去除积碳沉积物并恢复催化剂活性[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]。
4. 混合氧化剂系统：研究将N2O与其他氧化剂结合使用，以达到活性和选择性的最佳平衡[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.09.043]。
5. 工艺工程：开发能够更好地控制反应过程中催化剂氧化态的反应器设计[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2]。
6. 机理研究：对反应机理和催化剂表面相互作用进行更深入的研究，以更好地理解和控制影响催化剂性能的因素[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_8]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：在ODHP中使用N2O作为氧化剂代表了提高丙烯选择性和产率的一个有前景的方向。N2O的独特性质，如保持催化剂处于更还原的状态和抑制过度氧化的能力，相较于传统的氧化剂如O2提供了显著的优势。然而，催化剂失活和整体活性降低的挑战需要得到解决，以使N2O-ODHP具有商业可行性。

未来的研究努力应集中在开发能够保持高选择性的同时提高稳定性和活性的新型催化剂配方上。这可能涉及探索双金属系统、优化载体材料，或研究新的合成方法来控制活性物种的分散和氧化状态。此外，先进的原位表征技术可以提供关于反应过程中催化剂表面动态变化的宝贵见解，指导更有效的催化剂设计。

从工艺工程的角度来看，允许周期性操作或连续再生的创新反应器设计可能有助于克服催化剂失活的限制。此外，将N2O-ODHP与其他产生N2O作为副产品的工业过程整合可能会创造协同效应，提高整个过程的经济性和可持续性。

随着该领域研究的进展，至关重要的是要进行全面的技术经济和生命周期分析，以评估N2O-ODHP与传统丙烯生产方法相比的可行性。这种整体方法将有助于确定关键改进领域并指导未来的研究方向，最终为N2O-ODHP技术在化工行业的实际应用铺平道路。
