<English>
The use of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) offers significant advantages in terms of propylene selectivity compared to other oxidants, particularly oxygen (O2). This review analyzes the advantages, challenges, and potential strategies for improving N2O-ODHP for practical implementation.

Advantages of N2O as an oxidant in ODHP:

1. Higher propylene selectivity: N2O consistently provides higher propene selectivity compared to O2 across various catalysts, including iron-containing zeolites, vanadium-based catalysts, and molybdenum-based catalysts [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010]. For example, propylene selectivity can reach 80% with N2O compared to only 50% with O2 at similar propane conversion levels [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010].

2. Lower operation temperature: N2O-ODHP can be carried out at 643-773 K, which is significantly lower than the 823-923 K required for mixed metal oxide catalysts with O2 [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020].

3. Reduced over-oxidation: N2O inhibits direct propane oxidation and consecutive oxidation of propene to COx, leading to improved selectivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025].

4. Modulation of catalyst oxidation state: N2O promotes a more reduced state of catalytic sites, which is associated with higher selectivity [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2].

5. Dual benefits: The reaction allows for the functionalization of propane, a cheap and abundant feedstock, while also utilizing N2O, an environmentally harmful gas [10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035].

Catalysts employed in N2O-ODHP:

1. Iron-based zeolites: Steam-activated Fe-ZSM-5 has shown the most promising results, with initial propene yields up to 25% [10.1021/ie0493863].

2. Vanadium-based catalysts: VOx supported on various materials such as SiO2, MCM-41, and γ-Al2O3 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00836-5].

3. Molybdenum-based catalysts: NiMoO4 and MoO3/SiO2 have shown good performance [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2].

Critical challenges associated with N2O-ODHP:

1. Catalyst deactivation: Rapid deactivation due to coke formation is a major drawback, particularly for iron zeolites [10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010].

2. Lower overall activity: The use of N2O results in lower catalytic activity compared to O2 due to its weaker oxidizing potential [10.1021/jp070747c].

3. Balancing selectivity and conversion: While N2O improves selectivity, it often leads to decreased propane conversion [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.09.043].

4. Catalyst stability: Maintaining the optimal reduced state of the catalyst without over-reduction is challenging [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005].

Strategies to address limitations and improve N2O-ODHP feasibility:

1. Catalyst design optimization: Develop catalysts with improved resistance to coke formation and better stability in reduced states [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005].

2. Process engineering: Explore reactor designs and operating conditions that maximize the benefits of N2O while minimizing its drawbacks [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00836-5].

3. Hybrid oxidant systems: Investigate the use of N2O in combination with other oxidants to balance selectivity and activity [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.09.043].

4. Regeneration techniques: Develop efficient methods for in-situ or periodic catalyst regeneration [10.1021/ie0493863].

5. Mechanistic studies: Conduct more in-depth research on the reaction mechanisms and catalyst surface interactions [10.1016/j.apcata.2006.11.021].

6. Economic and environmental assessment: Evaluate the overall feasibility of N2O-ODHP, considering factors such as N2O production, handling, and potential environmental impacts [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_8].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The use of N2O as an oxidant in ODHP presents a promising approach to enhance propylene selectivity and yield while potentially mitigating N2O emissions. The unique properties of N2O, such as its ability to maintain catalysts in a more reduced state and suppress over-oxidation, offer significant advantages over conventional oxidants. However, the challenges of catalyst deactivation and lower overall activity need to be addressed for practical implementation.

Future research efforts should focus on developing novel catalyst designs that can withstand the reducing environment while maintaining high activity and selectivity. This may involve exploring bimetallic catalysts or advanced support materials that can stabilize active sites and prevent coke formation. Additionally, innovative reactor designs, such as membrane reactors or cyclic operation modes, could help optimize the use of N2O and improve overall process efficiency.

The integration of N2O-ODHP with other industrial processes that produce N2O as a byproduct could create synergies and improve the economic viability of the technology. Furthermore, as environmental regulations become stricter, the dual benefit of propylene production and N2O utilization may become increasingly attractive.

Ultimately, the success of N2O-ODHP will depend on overcoming the current limitations through targeted research and development efforts, as well as a comprehensive assessment of its economic and environmental impacts in comparison to conventional propylene production methods.

<Chinese>
在丙烷的氧化脱氢(ODHP)中使用一氧化二氮(N2O)作为氧化剂与其他氧化剂相比，特别是与氧气(O2)相比，在丙烯选择性方面具有显著优势。本综述分析了N2O-ODHP的优势、挑战以及提高其实际应用可行性的潜在策略。

N2O作为ODHP氧化剂的优势：

1. 更高的丙烯选择性：与O2相比，N2O在各种催化剂上持续提供更高的丙烯选择性，包括含铁沸石、钒基催化剂和钼基催化剂[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010]。例如，在相似的丙烷转化水平下，使用N2O可达到80%的丙烯选择性，而使用O2仅为50%[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010]。

2. 较低的操作温度：N2O-ODHP可在643-773 K下进行，这显著低于使用O2的混合金属氧化物催化剂所需的823-923 K[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020]。

3. 减少过度氧化：N2O抑制丙烷的直接氧化和丙烯连续氧化为COx，从而提高选择性[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025]。

4. 调节催化剂氧化态：N2O促进催化活性位点处于更还原的状态，这与更高的选择性相关[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2]。

5. 双重效益：该反应既可以利用廉价且丰富的丙烷原料，又可以利用环境有害气体N2O [10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035]。

N2O-ODHP中使用的催化剂：

1. 铁基沸石：蒸汽活化的Fe-ZSM-5显示出最有希望的结果，初始丙烯产率高达25%[10.1021/ie0493863]。

2. 钒基催化剂：VOx负载在各种材料上，如SiO2、MCM-41和γ-Al2O3 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00836-5]。

3. 钼基催化剂：NiMoO4和MoO3/SiO2表现良好[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2]。

N2O-ODHP相关的关键挑战：

1. 催化剂失活：由于焦炭形成导致的快速失活是一个主要缺点，特别是对于铁沸石[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010]。

2. 整体活性较低：由于N2O的氧化能力较弱，与O2相比，使用N2O会导致较低的催化活性[10.1021/jp070747c]。

3. 平衡选择性和转化率：虽然N2O改善了选择性，但通常会导致丙烷转化率降低[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.09.043]。

4. 催化剂稳定性：在不过度还原的情况下维持催化剂的最佳还原状态具有挑战性[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005]。

解决限制并提高N2O-ODHP可行性的策略：

1. 催化剂设计优化：开发具有更好抗焦炭形成能力和还原状态稳定性的催化剂[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]。

2. 工艺工程：探索能够最大化N2O优势同时最小化其缺点的反应器设计和操作条件[10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00836-5]。

3. 混合氧化剂系统：研究将N2O与其他氧化剂结合使用，以平衡选择性和活性[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.09.043]。

4. 再生技术：开发高效的原位或周期性催化剂再生方法[10.1021/ie0493863]。

5. 机理研究：对反应机理和催化剂表面相互作用进行更深入的研究[10.1016/j.apcata.2006.11.021]。

6. 经济和环境评估：评估N2O-ODHP的整体可行性，考虑N2O的生产、处理和潜在环境影响等因素[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_8]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：在ODHP中使用N2O作为氧化剂是一种有前景的方法，可以提高丙烯的选择性和产率，同时潜在地减少N2O排放。N2O的独特性质，如其能够使催化剂保持在更还原的状态并抑制过度氧化，相比于传统氧化剂具有显著优势。然而，催化剂失活和整体活性较低的挑战需要得到解决，以实现实际应用。

未来的研究工作应集中在开发能够在还原环境下保持高活性和选择性的新型催化剂设计。这可能涉及探索双金属催化剂或先进的载体材料，以稳定活性位点并防止焦炭形成。此外，创新的反应器设计，如膜反应器或周期性操作模式，可能有助于优化N2O的使用并提高整体过程效率。

将N2O-ODHP与其他产生N2O副产品的工业过程整合可能创造协同效应，提高该技术的经济可行性。此外，随着环境法规变得更加严格，丙烯生产和N2O利用的双重效益可能变得越来越具有吸引力。

最终，N2O-ODHP的成功将取决于通过有针对性的研究和开发努力来克服当前的限制，以及与传统丙烯生产方法相比，对其经济和环境影响进行全面评估。
