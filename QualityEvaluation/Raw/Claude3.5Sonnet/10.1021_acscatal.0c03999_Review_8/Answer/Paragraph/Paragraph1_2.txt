<English>
The use of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) offers significant advantages in terms of propylene selectivity compared to other oxidants, particularly oxygen (O2). N2O demonstrates superior performance across various catalysts, including iron-containing zeolites, vanadium-based catalysts, and molybdenum-based catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_8].

Key advantages of using N2O in ODHP include:

1. Higher propylene selectivity: N2O consistently provides higher propene selectivity compared to O2 at similar propane conversion levels. For example, using N2O instead of O2 as an oxidant for 0.5% VOx/γ-Al2O3 can increase propylene selectivity from 55% to 75% [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_8].

2. Reduced over-oxidation: N2O inhibits direct propane oxidation and consecutive oxidation of propene to COx, leading to improved selectivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025].

3. Lower operation temperature: N2O-ODHP can be carried out at 643-773 K, which is significantly lower than the 823-923 K required for mixed metal oxide catalysts with other oxidants [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020].

4. Catalyst reduction: N2O promotes a more reduced state of catalytic sites, leading to higher selectivity for propylene. This is due to N2O inhibiting O2 adsorption, limiting the formation of non-selective oxygen species [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2].

Various catalysts have been employed in N2O-ODHP, including:

1. Steam-activated Fe-zeolites (e.g., Fe-ZSM-5), which show remarkable performance with initial propene yields up to 25% [10.1021/ie0493863].

2. Vanadium-based catalysts supported on silica-based materials (e.g., VOx/SiO2, VOx/MCM-41, VOx/MCM-48) [10.1021/jp070747c].

3. Molybdenum-based catalysts, such as NiMoO4 [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005].

However, N2O-ODHP faces critical challenges related to catalyst deactivation and stability:

1. Coke formation: The main cause of catalyst deactivation, leading to a decrease in propylene yield and propane conversion over time [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020].

2. Lower catalytic activity: The use of N2O results in lower overall catalytic activity compared to O2 due to its weaker oxidizing potential for re-oxidizing reduced surface sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025].

3. Incomplete regeneration: Regeneration of the catalyst is often not fully reversible, leading to decreased performance in subsequent cycles [10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010].

To address these limitations and make N2O-ODHP more feasible for practical implementation, several strategies and research directions can be pursued:

1. Catalyst design optimization: Develop catalysts with improved resistance to coke formation and better stability in reduced states [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005].

2. Process engineering: Explore reactor designs and operating conditions that can maximize the benefits of N2O while minimizing its drawbacks [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.09.043].

3. Regeneration strategies: Investigate efficient methods for in-situ or periodic catalyst regeneration to remove coke deposits and restore catalyst activity [10.1021/ie0493863].

4. Hybrid oxidant systems: Study the use of N2O in combination with other oxidants to achieve an optimal balance of activity and selectivity [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2].

5. Mechanistic studies: Conduct more in-depth research on the reaction mechanisms and the role of different oxygen species in the ODHP process [10.1016/j.cattod.2005.11.028].

6. Economic and environmental assessment: Evaluate the overall feasibility of N2O-ODHP, considering factors such as N2O production, handling, and potential environmental impacts [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The use of N2O in ODHP represents a promising avenue for improving propylene selectivity and yield while potentially addressing environmental concerns related to N2O emissions. The unique properties of N2O as a mild oxidant offer opportunities to design more selective catalytic processes. However, the challenges of catalyst deactivation and lower overall activity compared to O2-based systems need to be overcome.

Future research efforts might focus on developing novel catalyst formulations that can maintain high selectivity while improving stability and activity. This could involve exploring bimetallic or multi-component catalysts, or investigating advanced support materials that can better stabilize active sites. Additionally, the integration of N2O-ODHP with other industrial processes that produce N2O as a by-product could create synergies and improve overall process economics.

Another promising direction is the development of intelligent reactor designs that can dynamically adjust oxidant compositions or implement cyclic regeneration strategies to maintain optimal catalyst performance. This could be coupled with advanced in-situ characterization techniques to monitor catalyst state in real-time and inform process control decisions.

Ultimately, the success of N2O-ODHP will depend on demonstrating its economic viability and environmental benefits compared to conventional propylene production methods. This will require comprehensive life cycle assessments and techno-economic analyses to guide future research and development efforts.

<Chinese>
使用一氧化二氮（N2O）作为丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）的氧化剂相比其他氧化剂，特别是氧气（O2），在丙烯选择性方面具有显著优势。N2O在各种催化剂上都表现出卓越的性能，包括含铁沸石、钒基催化剂和钼基催化剂 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_8]。

使用N2O进行ODHP的主要优势包括：

1. 更高的丙烯选择性：在相似的丙烷转化水平下，N2O始终提供比O2更高的丙烯选择性。例如，使用N2O代替O2作为0.5% VOx/γ-Al2O3的氧化剂可以将丙烯选择性从55%提高到75% [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_8]。

2. 减少过度氧化：N2O抑制直接丙烷氧化和丙烯连续氧化成COx的过程，从而提高选择性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025]。

3. 较低的操作温度：N2O-ODHP可以在643-773 K的温度下进行，这显著低于使用其他氧化剂的混合金属氧化物催化剂所需的823-923 K温度 [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020]。

4. 催化剂还原：N2O促进催化活性位点处于更还原的状态，从而提高丙烯选择性。这是由于N2O抑制了O2的吸附，限制了非选择性氧物种的形成 [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2]。

N2O-ODHP中使用了各种催化剂，包括：

1. 蒸汽活化的Fe-沸石（如Fe-ZSM-5），表现出卓越的性能，初始丙烯产率高达25% [10.1021/ie0493863]。

2. 以硅基材料为载体的钒基催化剂（如VOx/SiO2、VOx/MCM-41、VOx/MCM-48）[10.1021/jp070747c]。

3. 钼基催化剂，如NiMoO4 [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005]。

然而，N2O-ODHP面临着与催化剂失活和稳定性相关的关键挑战：

1. 积炭形成：这是催化剂失活的主要原因，导致丙烯产率和丙烷转化率随时间降低 [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020]。

2. 较低的催化活性：由于N2O对还原表面位点的再氧化能力较弱，使用N2O会导致整体催化活性低于使用O2时的活性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025]。

3. 不完全再生：催化剂的再生通常不是完全可逆的，导致后续循环中性能下降 [10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010]。

为了解决这些限制并使N2O-ODHP更适合实际应用，可以采取以下几种策略和研究方向：

1. 催化剂设计优化：开发具有更强抗积炭能力和在还原状态下更好稳定性的催化剂 [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]。

2. 工艺工程：探索能够最大化N2O优势同时最小化其缺点的反应器设计和操作条件 [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.09.043]。

3. 再生策略：研究高效的原位或周期性催化剂再生方法，以去除积炭沉积物并恢复催化剂活性 [10.1021/ie0493863]。

4. 混合氧化剂系统：研究将N2O与其他氧化剂结合使用，以实现活性和选择性的最佳平衡 [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2]。

5. 机理研究：对ODHP过程中的反应机理和不同氧物种的作用进行更深入的研究 [10.1016/j.cattod.2005.11.028]。

6. 经济和环境评估：评估N2O-ODHP的整体可行性，考虑N2O的生产、处理和潜在环境影响等因素 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：在ODHP中使用N2O代表了提高丙烯选择性和产率的一个有前景的方向，同时有可能解决与N2O排放相关的环境问题。N2O作为温和氧化剂的独特性质为设计更具选择性的催化过程提供了机会。然而，催化剂失活和与O2系统相比整体活性较低的挑战需要克服。

未来的研究努力可能集中在开发能够保持高选择性的同时改善稳定性和活性的新型催化剂配方。这可能涉及探索双金属或多组分催化剂，或研究可以更好地稳定活性位点的先进载体材料。此外，将N2O-ODHP与其他产生N2O副产物的工业过程整合可能创造协同效应并改善整体过程经济性。

另一个有前景的方向是开发智能反应器设计，可以动态调整氧化剂组成或实施循环再生策略以维持最佳催化剂性能。这可以与先进的原位表征技术相结合，实时监测催化剂状态并为过程控制决策提供信息。

最终，N2O-ODHP的成功将取决于证明其相对于传统丙烯生产方法的经济可行性和环境效益。这将需要全面的生命周期评估和技术经济分析来指导未来的研究和开发工作。
