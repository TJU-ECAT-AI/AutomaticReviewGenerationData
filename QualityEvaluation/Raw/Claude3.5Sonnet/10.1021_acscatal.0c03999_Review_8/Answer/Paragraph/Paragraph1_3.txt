<English>
The use of nitrous oxide (N2O) as an oxidant in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) offers significant advantages in terms of propylene selectivity compared to other oxidants, particularly oxygen (O2). N2O demonstrates superior performance across various catalysts, including iron-containing zeolites, vanadium-based catalysts, and molybdenum-based catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_8].

Key advantages of using N2O include:

1. Higher propylene selectivity: N2O consistently provides higher propene selectivity compared to O2 at similar propane conversion levels. For example, propylene selectivity can increase from 55% with O2 to 75% with N2O for 0.5% VOx/γ-Al2O3 catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_8].

2. Lower oxidation state of active sites: N2O promotes a more reduced state of catalytic sites, leading to higher selectivity for propylene. This is due to N2O's lower ability to reoxidize reduced metal species compared to O2 [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2].

3. Reduced over-oxidation: N2O inhibits direct propane oxidation and consecutive oxidation of propene to COx, resulting in improved selectivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025].

4. Lower reaction temperatures: N2O-ODHP can be carried out at 643-773 K, which is significantly lower than the 823-923 K required for mixed metal oxide catalysts with O2 [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020].

Various catalysts have been employed in N2O-ODHP, including:

1. Iron zeolites: Steam-activated Fe-ZSM-5 has shown the most promising results, with initial propene yields up to 25% [10.1021/ie0493863].
2. Vanadium-based catalysts: VOx supported on SiO2, MCM-41, and MCM-48 [10.1021/jp070747c].
3. Molybdenum-based catalysts: NiMoO4 and NiMoO4 + α-Sb2O4 mixtures [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005].

However, critical challenges associated with catalyst deactivation and stability include:

1. Coke formation: The primary cause of catalyst deactivation, leading to a decrease in propylene yield over time [10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010].
2. Rapid deactivation: Particularly problematic for some catalysts, such as Fe-BEA zeolites [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005].
3. Incomplete regeneration: The regeneration process of iron-containing zeolites is not fully reversible, leading to faster deactivation in subsequent cycles [10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010].
4. Lower overall activity: While N2O improves selectivity, it also decreases overall catalytic activity compared to O2 [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025].

To address these limitations and make N2O-ODHP more feasible for practical implementation, several strategies and research directions can be pursued:

1. Catalyst design optimization: Develop catalysts with improved resistance to coke formation and better stability in reduced states [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005].
2. Mesopore introduction: Explore methods like desilication to create mesopores in zeolites, enhancing transport properties and active site accessibility [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005].
3. Regeneration strategies: Optimize regeneration processes to efficiently remove coke and restore catalyst activity without altering the active site distribution [10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010].
4. Hybrid oxidant systems: Investigate the use of N2O in combination with other oxidants to achieve an optimal balance of activity and selectivity [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2].
5. Process engineering: Develop reactor designs that allow for better control of the oxidation state of the catalyst throughout the reaction [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005].
6. Mechanistic studies: Conduct more in-depth research on the reaction mechanisms and the role of different oxygen species in the ODHP process [10.1016/j.apcata.2006.11.021].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The use of N2O as an oxidant in ODHP presents a promising avenue for improving propylene selectivity and yield. The unique properties of N2O, such as its ability to maintain catalysts in a more reduced state and suppress over-oxidation, offer significant advantages over traditional oxidants like O2. However, the challenges of catalyst deactivation and lower overall activity need to be addressed for practical implementation.

Future research efforts should focus on developing novel catalyst formulations that can maintain high selectivity while improving stability and activity. This may involve exploring bimetallic systems, hierarchical pore structures, or advanced support materials. Additionally, the integration of N2O-ODHP with other industrial processes, such as N2O abatement technologies, could provide synergistic benefits and improve overall process economics.

The development of in-situ characterization techniques and advanced modeling approaches could provide deeper insights into the reaction mechanisms and catalyst behavior under N2O-ODHP conditions. This knowledge could guide the rational design of next-generation catalysts and processes.

Furthermore, the environmental impact of N2O use in large-scale ODHP processes should be carefully evaluated, considering both the potential for N2O mitigation and the overall life cycle assessment of the technology. As the chemical industry moves towards more sustainable practices, N2O-ODHP could play a significant role in utilizing a potent greenhouse gas for valuable chemical production.

<Chinese>
使用一氧化二氮（N2O）作为丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）的氧化剂在丙烯选择性方面比其他氧化剂（尤其是氧气（O2））具有显著优势。N2O在各种催化剂上都表现出优异的性能，包括含铁沸石、钒基催化剂和钼基催化剂 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_8]。

使用N2O的主要优势包括：

1. 更高的丙烯选择性：在相似的丙烷转化水平下，N2O始终提供比O2更高的丙烯选择性。例如，对于0.5% VOx/γ-Al2O3催化剂，丙烯选择性可以从使用O2时的55%提高到使用N2O时的75% [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_8]。

2. 活性位点的较低氧化态：N2O促进催化位点处于更还原的状态，从而导致更高的丙烯选择性。这是由于N2O相比O2对还原金属物种的再氧化能力较弱 [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2]。

3. 降低过度氧化：N2O抑制丙烷的直接氧化和丙烯连续氧化为COx，从而提高选择性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025]。

4. 较低的反应温度：N2O-ODHP可以在643-773 K下进行，这显著低于使用O2的混合金属氧化物催化剂所需的823-923 K [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.020]。

N2O-ODHP中使用的各种催化剂包括：

1. 铁沸石：蒸汽活化的Fe-ZSM-5显示出最有前景的结果，初始丙烯收率高达25% [10.1021/ie0493863]。
2. 钒基催化剂：负载在SiO2、MCM-41和MCM-48上的VOx [10.1021/jp070747c]。
3. 钼基催化剂：NiMoO4和NiMoO4 + α-Sb2O4混合物 [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005]。

然而，与催化剂失活和稳定性相关的关键挑战包括：

1. 积碳形成：这是催化剂失活的主要原因，导致丙烯产率随时间降低 [10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010]。
2. 快速失活：对某些催化剂特别成问题，如Fe-BEA沸石 [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]。
3. 不完全再生：含铁沸石的再生过程不完全可逆，导致随后循环中更快的失活 [10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010]。
4. 较低的整体活性：虽然N2O提高了选择性，但与O2相比，它也降低了整体催化活性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025]。

为了解决这些限制并使N2O-ODHP更适合实际应用，可以采取几种策略和研究方向：

1. 催化剂设计优化：开发具有改进的抗积碳形成能力和在还原态下更好稳定性的催化剂 [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]。
2. 引入介孔：探索去硅化等方法在沸石中创建介孔，增强传输性能和活性位点可及性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]。
3. 再生策略：优化再生过程，有效去除积碳并恢复催化剂活性，同时不改变活性位点分布 [10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010]。
4. 混合氧化剂系统：研究将N2O与其他氧化剂结合使用，以达到活性和选择性的最佳平衡 [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2]。
5. 工艺工程：开发能够在整个反应过程中更好地控制催化剂氧化状态的反应器设计 [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.03.005]。
6. 机理研究：对ODHP过程中的反应机理和不同氧物种的作用进行更深入的研究 [10.1016/j.apcata.2006.11.021]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：在ODHP中使用N2O作为氧化剂为提高丙烯选择性和收率提供了一个有前途的途径。N2O的独特性质，如能够使催化剂保持在更还原的状态并抑制过度氧化，相比传统的O2氧化剂提供了显著优势。然而，催化剂失活和整体活性较低的挑战需要解决，以实现实际应用。

未来的研究努力应集中在开发能够保持高选择性同时提高稳定性和活性的新型催化剂配方。这可能涉及探索双金属系统、分级孔结构或先进的载体材料。此外，将N2O-ODHP与其他工业过程（如N2O减排技术）集成可能提供协同效益并提高整体工艺经济性。

开发原位表征技术和先进建模方法可以为N2O-ODHP条件下的反应机理和催化剂行为提供更深入的见解。这些知识可以指导下一代催化剂和工艺的理性设计。

此外，应仔细评估大规模ODHP过程中使用N2O的环境影响，考虑N2O减排的潜力和技术的整体生命周期评估。随着化学工业向更可持续的实践发展，N2O-ODHP可能在利用强效温室气体生产有价值化学品方面发挥重要作用。
