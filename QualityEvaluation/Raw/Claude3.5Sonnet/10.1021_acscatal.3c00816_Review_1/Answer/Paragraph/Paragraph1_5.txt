<English>
Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts, particularly MCM-41 and SBA-15 supported VOx and CrOx, demonstrate promising performance in Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions compared to traditional metal oxide supports. These catalysts offer several advantages due to their unique structural properties and catalytic behavior.

Structural properties:
1. High specific surface area: OMS materials provide larger surface areas for active site dispersion, potentially increasing catalyst efficiency [10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081].
2. Ordered mesoporous structure: MCM-41 and SBA-15 have well-defined pore structures, allowing for better diffusion of reactants and products [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5].
3. Enhanced metal dispersion: The uniform pore structure leads to better dispersion of VOx and CrOx species, increasing the number of active sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.05.024].
4. Thermal stability: OMS materials exhibit good thermal stability, crucial for high-temperature PDH reactions [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.04.018].

Catalytic behavior:
1. VOx/MCM-41 catalysts show superior performance with propane productivity of 0.2 kgC3H6·kgcat-1·h-1 and propylene selectivity of 90% [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1].
2. CrOx/MCM-41 catalysts demonstrate good activity, with 10Cr/MCM-41 achieving 40% propane conversion and 80% propene selectivity after 1 hour [10.1002/cctc.201801598].
3. Both VOx and CrOx supported on MCM-41 and SBA-15 show considerable propylene productivity but suffer from fast deactivation [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1].

Advantages over traditional supports:
1. Better control over metal dispersion and pore structure [10.1039/D2NR02208G].
2. Improved optimization of active site accessibility [10.1016/j.cej.2019.122423].
3. Enhanced reactant/product diffusion [10.1016/j.apcata.2016.12.026].

Reaction mechanisms:
The PDH reaction over VOx/OMS catalysts involves C-H bond activation in propane, followed by propylene desorption. The activity and selectivity are influenced by the nature of V-O bonds on the surface [10.1039/D0CS00814A]. For CrOx catalysts, a redox cycle between Cr(VI) and Cr(III) species is proposed to be responsible for the dehydrogenation activity [10.1002/cctc.201801598].

Factors influencing catalyst performance and deactivation:
1. Lattice oxygen species: Play a crucial role in the redox cycle and overall catalytic activity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1].
2. H2 pretreatment: Can enhance metal-support interactions, improving stability and anti-coking properties [10.1016/j.micromeso.2021.111187].
3. Metal loading: Optimal loading is crucial for maintaining catalyst activity and stability [10.1002/cctc.201801598].
4. Degree of polymerization of metal oxide species: Isolated species generally show higher activity and resistance to deactivation [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022].

Challenges and limitations:
1. Coke formation leading to catalyst deactivation [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005].
2. Potential pore blockage due to coke deposition [10.1039/D2NR02208G].
3. Possible metal leaching under harsh reaction conditions [10.1039/D2NR02208G].

In conclusion, while OMS-supported VOx and CrOx catalysts show great potential for PDH reactions, careful optimization of catalyst composition, preparation methods, and reaction conditions is crucial to maximize their advantages and overcome limitations.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts for Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions represents a significant advancement in catalyst design. By leveraging the unique structural properties of materials like MCM-41 and SBA-15, researchers have created catalysts that outperform traditional supports in many aspects. However, the field still faces challenges, particularly in terms of catalyst stability and deactivation.

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Advanced synthesis techniques: Developing new methods to further enhance metal dispersion and control the formation of active sites within the OMS structure.

2. In-situ characterization: Utilizing advanced spectroscopic techniques to better understand the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and active sites during PDH reactions.

3. Multifunctional catalysts: Exploring the incorporation of multiple active components or promoters within OMS supports to create synergistic effects that enhance activity, selectivity, and stability.

4. Hierarchical pore structures: Investigating the potential of combining micro-, meso-, and macropores in OMS materials to optimize reactant diffusion and product desorption.

5. Novel regeneration strategies: Developing more efficient methods for catalyst regeneration that can extend catalyst lifetime and reduce operational costs.

6. Computational modeling: Employing advanced simulation techniques to predict catalyst behavior and guide the design of next-generation PDH catalysts.

7. Sustainable catalyst production: Exploring eco-friendly synthesis routes and investigating the use of abundant, non-precious metals as active components in OMS-supported catalysts.

By addressing these research areas, the scientific community can work towards developing more efficient, stable, and sustainable catalysts for propane dehydrogenation, potentially revolutionizing the production of propylene and other valuable chemical intermediates.

<Chinese>
金属有序介孔硅（OMS）催化剂，特别是MCM-41和SBA-15负载的VOx和CrOx，在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中表现出比传统金属氧化物载体更好的性能。这些催化剂由于其独特的结构特性和催化行为而具有多项优势。

结构特性：
1. 高比表面积：OMS材料为活性位点分散提供了更大的表面积，可能增加催化剂效率[10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081]。
2. 有序介孔结构：MCM-41和SBA-15具有明确定义的孔结构，有利于反应物和产物的扩散[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5]。
3. 增强金属分散：均匀的孔结构导致VOx和CrOx物种更好的分散，增加活性位点数量[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.05.024]。
4. 热稳定性：OMS材料表现出良好的热稳定性，这对高温PDH反应至关重要[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.04.018]。

催化行为：
1. VOx/MCM-41催化剂表现出优异的性能，丙烷生产率为0.2 kgC3H6·kgcat-1·h-1，丙烯选择性为90%[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。
2. CrOx/MCM-41催化剂表现出良好的活性，10Cr/MCM-41在1小时后实现40%的丙烷转化率和80%的丙烯选择性[10.1002/cctc.201801598]。
3. MCM-41和SBA-15负载的VOx和CrOx都表现出可观的丙烯生产率，但存在快速失活的问题[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。

相对于传统载体的优势：
1. 更好地控制金属分散和孔结构[10.1039/D2NR02208G]。
2. 改善活性位点可及性的优化[10.1016/j.cej.2019.122423]。
3. 增强反应物/产物扩散[10.1016/j.apcata.2016.12.026]。

反应机理：
VOx/OMS催化剂上的PDH反应涉及丙烷C-H键的活化，随后是丙烯的脱附。活性和选择性受表面V-O键性质的影响[10.1039/D0CS00814A]。对于CrOx催化剂，Cr(VI)和Cr(III)物种之间的氧化还原循环被认为是脱氢活性的原因[10.1002/cctc.201801598]。

影响催化剂性能和失活的因素：
1. 晶格氧物种：在氧化还原循环和整体催化活性中起关键作用[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。
2. H2预处理：可以增强金属-载体相互作用，改善稳定性和抗积碳性能[10.1016/j.micromeso.2021.111187]。
3. 金属负载量：最佳负载量对维持催化剂活性和稳定性至关重要[10.1002/cctc.201801598]。
4. 金属氧化物物种的聚合度：孤立物种通常表现出更高的活性和抗失活能力[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022]。

挑战和局限性：
1. 积碳导致催化剂失活[10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005]。
2. 由于积碳沉积可能导致孔道堵塞[10.1039/D2NR02208G]。
3. 在严苛反应条件下可能发生金属浸出[10.1039/D2NR02208G]。

总之，虽然OMS负载的VOx和CrOx催化剂在PDH反应中表现出巨大潜力，但仍需谨慎优化催化剂组成、制备方法和反应条件，以最大限度地发挥其优势并克服局限性。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：金属有序介孔硅（OMS）催化剂在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中的发展代表了催化剂设计的重大进步。通过利用MCM-41和SBA-15等材料的独特结构特性，研究人员创造了在多个方面优于传统载体的催化剂。然而，该领域仍面临挑战，特别是在催化剂稳定性和失活方面。

未来研究方向可能集中在：

1. 先进合成技术：开发新方法以进一步增强金属分散和控制OMS结构内活性位点的形成。

2. 原位表征：利用先进的光谱技术更好地理解PDH反应过程中催化剂结构和活性位点的动态变化。

3. 多功能催化剂：探索在OMS载体中结合多种活性组分或助剂，创造协同效应以增强活性、选择性和稳定性。

4. 分级孔结构：研究在OMS材料中结合微孔、介孔和大孔的潜力，以优化反应物扩散和产物脱附。

5. 新型再生策略：开发更有效的催化剂再生方法，延长催化剂寿命并降低运营成本。

6. 计算机模拟：采用先进的模拟技术预测催化剂行为，指导下一代PDH催化剂的设计。

7. 可持续催化剂生产：探索环保的合成路线，研究使用丰富、非贵金属作为OMS负载催化剂的活性组分。

通过解决这些研究领域，科学界可以致力于开发更高效、稳定和可持续的丙烷脱氢催化剂，可能彻底改变丙烯和其他有价值化学中间体的生产。
