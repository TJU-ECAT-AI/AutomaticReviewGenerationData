<English>
Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts, particularly MCM-41 and SBA-15 supported VOx and CrOx, demonstrate promising performance in Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions compared to traditional metal oxide supports. These catalysts offer several advantages due to their unique structural properties and catalytic behavior.

Structural properties:
1. High surface area: OMS materials provide larger surface areas (e.g., MCM-41 with ~780-900 m2/g) for active site dispersion, potentially increasing catalyst efficiency [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005].
2. Ordered mesoporous structure: The well-defined pore structure of MCM-41 and SBA-15 allows for better diffusion of reactants and products, potentially improving catalytic activity [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5].
3. Enhanced metal dispersion: The uniform pore structure leads to better dispersion of VOx and CrOx species, increasing the number of active sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.05.024].
4. Thermal stability: OMS materials exhibit good thermal stability, crucial for high-temperature PDH reactions [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.04.018].

Catalytic behavior:
1. Higher activity and selectivity: VOx/MCM-41 catalysts show good on-stream stability and high selectivity, performing similarly to industrial CrOx/Al2O3 and Pt-Sn/Al2O3 catalysts [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022].
2. Superior performance: VOx/MCM-41 exhibits better catalytic activity than CrOx/MCM-41, achieving a propane productivity of 0.2 kgC3H6·kgcat-1·h-1 and a propylene selectivity of 90% [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1].
3. Active sites: V4+ and V3+ species are identified as catalytically active sites for VOx catalysts, while Cr(III) sites with coordination number greater than four are more active for CrOx catalysts [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014].

Reaction mechanisms:
The PDH reaction over VOx/OMS and CrOx/OMS catalysts involves:
1. Activation of C-H bonds in propane by lattice oxygen species [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1].
2. Desorption of propylene from the catalyst surface.
3. Regeneration of reduced metal sites through gas-phase oxygen or lattice oxygen migration.

Factors influencing performance and deactivation:
1. Metal loading and dispersion: Optimal metal loading is crucial for maintaining catalyst activity and stability [10.1002/cctc.201801598].
2. Lattice oxygen species: Play a vital role in the redox cycle and overall catalytic activity [10.1021/acs.iecr.9b01413].
3. H2 pretreatment: Can enhance metal-support interactions, improving stability and anti-coking properties [10.1016/j.micromeso.2021.111187].
4. Surface acidity: Higher acidity leads to increased coking tendency [10.1039/D2NR02208G].

Limitations and challenges:
1. Fast deactivation: Both VOx and CrOx supported on MCM-41 and SBA-15 suffer from rapid deactivation [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1].
2. Coke formation: The rate of coke formation increases with the degree of polymerization of VOx species [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022].
3. Balancing activity and stability: Oxidized species are more active but less stable than reduced species [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The development of Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts for Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions represents a significant advancement in heterogeneous catalysis. The unique structural properties of OMS materials, combined with carefully tuned metal species, offer a promising platform for designing highly efficient and stable PDH catalysts.

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Advanced synthesis techniques: Developing methods to precisely control metal dispersion, oxidation state, and metal-support interactions within OMS structures.

2. In-situ characterization: Employing cutting-edge spectroscopic techniques to study the dynamic behavior of active sites under reaction conditions, providing deeper insights into reaction mechanisms and deactivation processes.

3. Multifunctional catalysts: Exploring the incorporation of multiple active components or promoters within OMS frameworks to enhance activity, selectivity, and stability simultaneously.

4. Regeneration strategies: Investigating novel approaches for efficient catalyst regeneration, potentially including in-situ regeneration techniques to prolong catalyst lifetime.

5. Computational modeling: Utilizing advanced computational methods to predict optimal catalyst compositions and structures, guiding experimental design and accelerating catalyst development.

6. Scale-up and industrial implementation: Addressing challenges related to large-scale synthesis of OMS catalysts and their integration into existing industrial processes.

By addressing these areas, researchers can work towards developing next-generation PDH catalysts that overcome current limitations and pave the way for more efficient and sustainable propylene production.

<Chinese>
金属有序介孔二氧化硅（OMS）催化剂，特别是MCM-41和SBA-15负载的VOx和CrOx，在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中表现出比传统金属氧化物载体更有前景的性能。这些催化剂由于其独特的结构特性和催化行为而具有多种优势。

结构特性：
1. 高比表面积：OMS材料提供更大的表面积（例如，MCM-41约780-900 m2/g）用于活性位点分散，可能增加催化剂效率[10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005]。
2. 有序介孔结构：MCM-41和SBA-15的明确孔结构允许反应物和产物更好的扩散，可能提高催化活性[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5]。
3. 增强金属分散：均匀的孔结构导致VOx和CrOx物种更好的分散，增加活性位点的数量[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.05.024]。
4. 热稳定性：OMS材料表现出良好的热稳定性，这对于高温PDH反应至关重要[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.04.018]。

催化行为：
1. 更高的活性和选择性：VOx/MCM-41催化剂表现出良好的在线稳定性和高选择性，性能类似于工业用CrOx/Al2O3和Pt-Sn/Al2O3催化剂[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022]。
2. 优越性能：VOx/MCM-41展示出比CrOx/MCM-41更好的催化活性，实现0.2 kgC3H6·kgcat-1·h-1的丙烷生产率和90%的丙烯选择性[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。
3. 活性位点：对于VOx催化剂，V4+和V3+物种被识别为催化活性位点，而对于CrOx催化剂，配位数大于四的Cr(III)位点更活跃[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014]。

反应机理：
VOx/OMS和CrOx/OMS催化剂上的PDH反应涉及：
1. 通过晶格氧物种活化丙烷中的C-H键[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。
2. 从催化剂表面脱附丙烯。
3. 通过气相氧或晶格氧迁移再生还原的金属位点。

影响性能和失活的因素：
1. 金属负载量和分散度：最佳金属负载量对于维持催化剂活性和稳定性至关重要[10.1002/cctc.201801598]。
2. 晶格氧物种：在氧化还原循环和整体催化活性中起关键作用[10.1021/acs.iecr.9b01413]。
3. H2预处理：可以增强金属-载体相互作用，提高稳定性和抗积碳性能[10.1016/j.micromeso.2021.111187]。
4. 表面酸度：较高的酸度导致积碳倾向增加[10.1039/D2NR02208G]。

局限性和挑战：
1. 快速失活：负载在MCM-41和SBA-15上的VOx和CrOx都遭受快速失活[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。
2. 积碳形成：VOx物种聚合度的增加导致积碳形成速率增加[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022]。
3. 平衡活性和稳定性：氧化物种比还原物种更活跃但稳定性较差[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。

综合理解与未来展望:
金属有序介孔二氧化硅（OMS）催化剂在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中的发展代表了多相催化领域的重大进步。OMS材料的独特结构特性，结合精心调控的金属物种，为设计高效稳定的PDH催化剂提供了一个有前景的平台。

未来研究方向可能集中在：

1. 先进合成技术：开发精确控制OMS结构中金属分散、氧化态和金属-载体相互作用的方法。

2. 原位表征：采用先进的光谱技术研究反应条件下活性位点的动态行为，深入了解反应机理和失活过程。

3. 多功能催化剂：探索在OMS框架中结合多种活性组分或助剂，同时提高活性、选择性和稳定性。

4. 再生策略：研究高效催化剂再生的新方法，可能包括原位再生技术以延长催化剂寿命。

5. 计算机建模：利用先进的计算方法预测最佳催化剂组成和结构，指导实验设计并加速催化剂开发。

6. 规模放大和工业实施：解决OMS催化剂大规模合成及其整合到现有工业过程中的挑战。

通过解决这些领域的问题，研究人员可以致力于开发克服当前局限性的下一代PDH催化剂，为更高效、更可持续的丙烯生产铺平道路。
