<English>
Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts, particularly MCM-41 and SBA-15 supported VOx and CrOx, demonstrate promising performance in Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions compared to traditional metal oxide supports. These catalysts offer several advantages due to their unique structural properties and catalytic behavior.

Structural Properties:
1. High surface area: OMS materials provide larger surface areas (e.g., MCM-41 with 780-900 m2/g) for better dispersion of active sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005].
2. Ordered mesoporous structure: The well-defined pore structure of MCM-41 and SBA-15 allows for better diffusion of reactants and products [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5].
3. Enhanced metal dispersion: The uniform pore structure leads to better dispersion of VOx and CrOx species, increasing the number of active sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.05.024].
4. Thermal stability: OMS materials exhibit good thermal stability, crucial for high-temperature PDH reactions [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.04.018].

Catalytic Behavior:
1. Higher activity: VOx/MCM-41 catalysts show superior activity compared to bulk V2O5 or traditional supports, with propane productivity of 0.2 kgC3H6·kgcat-1·h-1 and propylene selectivity of 90% [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1].
2. Improved selectivity: VOx/MCM-41 catalysts demonstrate high selectivity (around 90%) for propene formation [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022].
3. Enhanced stability: OMS-supported catalysts, especially VOx/MCM-41, show better resistance to deactivation compared to traditional supports [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005].

Reaction Mechanisms:
The PDH reaction over VOx/OMS catalysts involves:
1. C-H bond activation in propane by V-O species.
2. Propylene desorption from the catalyst surface.
3. Participation of lattice oxygen species in the initial stages of the reaction [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1].

For CrOx/OMS catalysts:
1. Active Cr sites are generated on-site during the reaction.
2. Cr typically exists in the (III) oxidation state during the reaction.
3. Isolated Cr(VI) species are immediately reduced to Cr(III) upon interaction with propane [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014].

Factors Influencing Performance:
1. Metal dispersion: Isolated VOx and CrOx species show higher activity and resistance to deactivation [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022].
2. Oxidation state: Oxidized VOx and CrOx species are generally more active than reduced ones [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1].
3. Support acidity: Lower acidity of OMS supports contributes to better anticoking capacity [10.1016/j.cej.2019.122423].
4. H2 pretreatment: Can enhance metal-support interactions and improve stability [10.1039/D2NR02208G].

Limitations and Challenges:
1. Fast deactivation: Despite their advantages, OMS-supported VOx and CrOx catalysts still suffer from relatively rapid deactivation [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1].
2. Coke formation: The main cause of deactivation, with the rate increasing with the degree of polymerization of VOx species [10.1039/D0CS00814A].
3. Potential pore blockage: Coke formation can lead to pore blocking in the mesoporous structure [10.1039/D2NR02208G].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The application of Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts in Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions represents a significant advancement in catalyst design. The unique structural properties of OMS materials, combined with their ability to stabilize and disperse active metal species, offer a promising platform for developing highly efficient and stable PDH catalysts.

Future research directions may focus on:
1. Optimizing metal loading and dispersion techniques to maximize the formation of isolated, highly active metal species while minimizing aggregation.
2. Developing novel synthesis methods to create hierarchical pore structures that combine the advantages of different OMS materials (e.g., combining the thick walls of SBA-15 with the high surface area of MCM-41).
3. Exploring bimetallic or multi-component catalysts supported on OMS to synergistically enhance activity, selectivity, and stability.
4. Investigating the use of advanced characterization techniques, such as in-situ XAS and operando spectroscopy, to gain deeper insights into the dynamic behavior of active sites during PDH reactions.
5. Developing strategies to mitigate coke formation and enhance catalyst regeneration, possibly through the incorporation of promoters or the design of self-cleaning catalyst surfaces.
6. Exploring the potential of OMS-supported catalysts in related reactions, such as oxidative dehydrogenation or dehydrogenation of higher alkanes, to broaden their application scope.

By addressing these challenges and opportunities, researchers can work towards developing next-generation PDH catalysts that combine high activity, selectivity, and long-term stability, potentially revolutionizing the production of propylene and other light olefins in the chemical industry.

<Chinese>
金属有序介孔二氧化硅（OMS）催化剂，特别是MCM-41和SBA-15负载的VOx和CrOx，在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中表现出比传统金属氧化物载体更好的性能。这些催化剂由于其独特的结构特性和催化行为而具有几个优点。

结构特性：
1. 高比表面积：OMS材料提供更大的表面积（如MCM-41为780-900 m2/g），可以更好地分散活性位点 [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005]。
2. 有序介孔结构：MCM-41和SBA-15的明确孔道结构允许反应物和产物更好地扩散 [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5]。
3. 增强金属分散：均匀的孔结构导致VOx和CrOx物种更好的分散，增加了活性位点的数量 [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.05.024]。
4. 热稳定性：OMS材料表现出良好的热稳定性，这对于高温PDH反应至关重要 [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.04.018]。

催化行为：
1. 更高的活性：VOx/MCM-41催化剂显示出比体相V2O5或传统载体更高的活性，丙烷生产率为0.2 kgC3H6·kgcat-1·h-1，丙烯选择性为90% [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。
2. 改善的选择性：VOx/MCM-41催化剂对丙烯形成表现出高选择性（约90%）[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022]。
3. 增强的稳定性：OMS负载的催化剂，特别是VOx/MCM-41，与传统载体相比表现出更好的抗失活能力 [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005]。

反应机理：
VOx/OMS催化剂上的PDH反应涉及：
1. V-O物种活化丙烷中的C-H键。
2. 丙烯从催化剂表面脱附。
3. 晶格氧物种参与反应的初始阶段 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。

对于CrOx/OMS催化剂：
1. 活性Cr位点在反应过程中现场生成。
2. 反应过程中Cr通常以(III)价态存在。
3. 孤立的Cr(VI)物种在与丙烷相互作用时立即还原为Cr(III) [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014]。

影响性能的因素：
1. 金属分散度：孤立的VOx和CrOx物种表现出更高的活性和抗失活能力 [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022]。
2. 氧化态：氧化态的VOx和CrOx物种通常比还原态的更活跃 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。
3. 载体酸度：OMS载体较低的酸度有助于提高抗积碳能力 [10.1016/j.cej.2019.122423]。
4. H2预处理：可以增强金属-载体相互作用并提高稳定性 [10.1039/D2NR02208G]。

限制和挑战：
1. 快速失活：尽管具有优势，OMS负载的VOx和CrOx催化剂仍然遭受相对快速的失活 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。
2. 积碳形成：失活的主要原因，其速率随VOx物种聚合度的增加而增加 [10.1039/D0CS00814A]。
3. 可能的孔道堵塞：积碳形成可能导致介孔结构中的孔道堵塞 [10.1039/D2NR02208G]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：金属有序介孔二氧化硅（OMS）催化剂在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中的应用代表了催化剂设计的重大进步。OMS材料独特的结构特性，结合其稳定和分散活性金属物种的能力，为开发高效稳定的PDH催化剂提供了一个有前景的平台。

未来研究方向可能集中在：
1. 优化金属负载和分散技术，以最大化形成孤立的高活性金属物种，同时最小化聚集。
2. 开发新型合成方法，创造兼具不同OMS材料优势的分级孔结构（例如，结合SBA-15的厚壁和MCM-41的高比表面积）。
3. 探索OMS负载的双金属或多组分催化剂，以协同增强活性、选择性和稳定性。
4. 研究使用先进的表征技术，如原位XAS和操作中光谱，以深入了解PDH反应过程中活性位点的动态行为。
5. 开发减少积碳形成和增强催化剂再生的策略，可能通过引入助剂或设计自清洁催化剂表面。
6. 探索OMS负载催化剂在相关反应中的潜力，如氧化脱氢或高级烷烃脱氢，以扩大其应用范围。

通过解决这些挑战和机遇，研究人员可以致力于开发下一代PDH催化剂，结合高活性、选择性和长期稳定性，有可能革新化学工业中丙烯和其他轻烯烃的生产。
