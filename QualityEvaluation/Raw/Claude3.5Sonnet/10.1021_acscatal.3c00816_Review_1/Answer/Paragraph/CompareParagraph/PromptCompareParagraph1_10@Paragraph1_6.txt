Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts have gained significant attention in the field of Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions due to their unique structural properties and enhanced catalytic performance. Among the various OMS materials, MCM-41 and SBA-15 have emerged as prominent support structures for active metal species such as vanadium oxide (VOx) and chromium oxide (CrOx). These catalysts offer several advantages, including high surface area, uniform pore distribution, and improved metal dispersion, which contribute to their superior activity and selectivity in PDH reactions. The reaction mechanism typically involves the adsorption of propane on the catalyst surface, followed by C-H bond activation and subsequent desorption of propylene. VOx-based catalysts generally exhibit higher initial activity, while CrOx catalysts often demonstrate better stability and resistance to deactivation. However, challenges persist, such as coke formation, sintering of metal particles, and maintaining long-term stability under industrial conditions. Comparative analysis of these catalysts reveals that the choice between MCM-41 and SBA-15 supports, as well as the selection of VOx or CrOx active species, depends on specific process requirements and desired performance characteristics. Ongoing research focuses on optimizing catalyst composition, improving regeneration strategies, and developing novel synthesis methods to enhance the overall efficiency and longevity of OMS catalysts in PDH applications.

Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts, particularly MCM-41 and SBA-15 supported VOx and CrOx, have shown promising performance in Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions compared to traditional metal oxide supports. These catalysts offer several advantages due to their unique structural properties and catalytic behavior.

Structural properties:
1. High surface area: OMS materials provide larger surface areas (e.g., MCM-41 with ~780-900 m2/g) for active site dispersion, increasing catalyst efficiency [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005].
2. Ordered pore structure: The well-defined mesoporous structure of MCM-41 and SBA-15 allows for better diffusion of reactants and products, improving catalytic activity [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5].
3. Enhanced metal dispersion: The uniform pore structure leads to better dispersion of VOx and CrOx species, increasing the number of active sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.05.024].
4. Thermal stability: OMS materials exhibit good thermal stability, crucial for high-temperature PDH reactions [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.04.018].

Catalytic behavior:
1. VOx/MCM-41 catalysts show superior performance compared to CrOx/MCM-41, achieving propane productivity of 0.2 kgC3H6·kgcat-1·h-1 and propylene selectivity of 90% [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\_1].
2. Isolated VOx species are most active and resistant to deactivation, with Lewis-acidic V3+ and V4+ identified as active sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022].
3. For CrOx/SBA-15, isolated Cr(III) sites with coordination number >4 are more active, selective, and stable than surface Cr sites on Cr2O3 crystals [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014].

Reaction mechanisms:
The PDH reaction over VOx/OMS and CrOx/OMS catalysts involves:
1. C-H bond activation in propane, influenced by the nature of V-O or Cr-O bonds.
2. Propylene desorption, affected by the metal-support interaction.
3. Participation of lattice oxygen species in the initial stages of the reaction [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\_1].

Advantages over traditional supports:
1. Better control over metal dispersion and pore structure.
2. Improved optimization of active site accessibility.
3. Enhanced reactant/product diffusion.
4. Lower acidity, contributing to better anticoking capacity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\_1].

Limitations and challenges:
1. Fast deactivation, particularly for CrOx catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\_1].
2. Potential pore blockage due to coke formation.
3. Possible metal leaching under harsh reaction conditions.
4. Need for careful optimization of metal loading and preparation methods.

Factors influencing performance and deactivation:
1. Metal loading: Optimal loading is crucial for maintaining high dispersion and activity [10.1002/cctc.201801598].
2. Oxidation state: Oxidized species are generally more active but less stable than reduced ones [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\_1].
3. H2 pretreatment: Can enhance metal-support interactions, improving stability and anti-coking properties [10.1039/D2NR02208G].
4. Lattice oxygen: Plays a crucial role in the redox cycle and overall catalytic activity [10.1021/acs.iecr.9b01413].
5. Support acidity: Higher acidity leads to increased coking tendency [10.1039/D2NR02208G].

In conclusion, while OMS-supported VOx and CrOx catalysts show great potential for PDH reactions, careful optimization of catalyst composition, preparation methods, and reaction conditions is crucial to maximize their advantages and overcome limitations.
