Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts, particularly MCM-41 and SBA-15 supported VOx and CrOx, demonstrate promising performance in Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions compared to traditional metal oxide supports. These catalysts offer several advantages due to their unique structural properties and catalytic behavior.

Structural Properties:
1. High surface area: OMS materials provide larger surface areas (e.g., MCM-41 with 780-900 m2/g) for better dispersion of active sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005].
2. Ordered mesoporous structure: The well-defined pore structure of MCM-41 and SBA-15 allows for better diffusion of reactants and products [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5].
3. Enhanced metal dispersion: The uniform pore structure leads to better dispersion of VOx and CrOx species, increasing the number of active sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.05.024].
4. Thermal stability: OMS materials exhibit good thermal stability, crucial for high-temperature PDH reactions [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.04.018].

Catalytic Behavior:
1. Higher activity: VOx/MCM-41 catalysts show superior activity compared to bulk V2O5 or traditional supports, with propane productivity of 0.2 kgC3H6·kgcat-1·h-1 and propylene selectivity of 90% [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\_1].
2. Improved selectivity: VOx/MCM-41 catalysts demonstrate high selectivity (around 90%) for propene formation [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022].
3. Enhanced stability: OMS-supported catalysts, especially VOx/MCM-41, show better resistance to deactivation compared to traditional supports [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005].

Reaction Mechanisms:
The PDH reaction over VOx/OMS catalysts involves:
1. C-H bond activation in propane by V-O species.
2. Propylene desorption from the catalyst surface.
3. Participation of lattice oxygen species in the initial stages of the reaction [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\_1].

For CrOx/OMS catalysts:
1. Active Cr sites are generated on-site during the reaction.
2. Cr typically exists in the (III) oxidation state during the reaction.
3. Isolated Cr(VI) species are immediately reduced to Cr(III) upon interaction with propane [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014].

Factors Influencing Performance:
1. Metal dispersion: Isolated VOx and CrOx species show higher activity and resistance to deactivation [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022].
2. Oxidation state: Oxidized VOx and CrOx species are generally more active than reduced ones [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\_1].
3. Support acidity: Lower acidity of OMS supports contributes to better anticoking capacity [10.1016/j.cej.2019.122423].
4. H2 pretreatment: Can enhance metal-support interactions and improve stability [10.1039/D2NR02208G].

Limitations and Challenges:
1. Fast deactivation: Despite their advantages, OMS-supported VOx and CrOx catalysts still suffer from relatively rapid deactivation [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\_1].
2. Coke formation: The main cause of deactivation, with the rate increasing with the degree of polymerization of VOx species [10.1039/D0CS00814A].
3. Potential pore blockage: Coke formation can lead to pore blocking in the mesoporous structure [10.1039/D2NR02208G].

Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts have gained significant attention in the field of Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions due to their unique structural properties and enhanced catalytic performance. Among the various OMS materials, MCM-41 and SBA-15 have emerged as prominent support structures for active metal species such as vanadium oxide (VOx) and chromium oxide (CrOx). These catalysts offer several advantages, including high surface area, uniform pore distribution, and improved metal dispersion, which contribute to their superior activity and selectivity in PDH reactions. The reaction mechanism typically involves the adsorption of propane on the catalyst surface, followed by C-H bond activation and subsequent desorption of propylene. VOx-based catalysts generally exhibit higher initial activity, while CrOx catalysts often demonstrate better stability and resistance to deactivation. However, challenges persist, such as coke formation, sintering of metal particles, and maintaining long-term stability under industrial conditions. Comparative analysis of these catalysts reveals that the choice between MCM-41 and SBA-15 supports, as well as the selection of VOx or CrOx active species, depends on specific process requirements and desired performance characteristics. Ongoing research focuses on optimizing catalyst composition, improving regeneration strategies, and developing novel synthesis methods to enhance the overall efficiency and longevity of OMS catalysts in PDH applications.
