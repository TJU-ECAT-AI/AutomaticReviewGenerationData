<English>
Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts, particularly MCM-41 and SBA-15 supported VOx and CrOx, demonstrate promising performance in Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions compared to traditional metal oxide supports. These catalysts offer several advantages due to their unique structural properties and catalytic behavior.

Structural properties:
1. High surface area: OMS materials provide a larger surface area for active site dispersion, potentially increasing catalyst efficiency [10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081].
2. Ordered pore structure: The well-defined mesoporous structure of MCM-41 and SBA-15 allows for better diffusion of reactants and products, potentially improving catalytic activity [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5].
3. Enhanced metal dispersion: The uniform pore structure of OMS supports can lead to better dispersion of VOx and CrOx species, potentially increasing the number of active sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.05.024].
4. Thermal stability: OMS materials generally exhibit good thermal stability, which is crucial for PDH reactions that occur at high temperatures [10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081].

Catalytic behavior:
1. Higher activity and selectivity: VOx/MCM-41 catalysts exhibit good on-stream stability and high selectivity, performing similarly to industrial CrOx/Al2O3 and Pt-Sn/Al2O3 catalysts [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022].
2. Active sites: For VOx catalysts, V4+ and V3+ species are identified as the catalytically active sites, with Lewis acidity increasing as the number of V-O-V bonds decreases [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022]. For CrOx catalysts, isolated Cr(III) sites with coordination number greater than four are more active, selective, and stable than Cr sites on the surface of crystalline Cr2O3 [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014].
3. Resistance to deactivation: Isolated VOx species show higher resistance to deactivation compared to aggregated species [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022].

Reaction mechanisms:
The PDH reaction over VOx/OMS catalysts involves the activation of C-H bonds in propane, followed by the desorption of propylene. The activity and selectivity of these catalysts are influenced by the nature of the V-O bonds present on the surface [10.039/D0CS00814A]. For CrOx catalysts, active Cr sites are generated on-site during the reaction, with Cr typically in the (III) oxidation state [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014].

Factors influencing performance:
1. Metal loading and dispersion: Optimal metal loading is crucial for maintaining catalyst activity and stability [10.1002/cctc.201801598].
2. Lattice oxygen species: Play a crucial role in the redox cycle and overall catalytic activity [10.021/acscatal.3c00816_1].
3. H2 pretreatment: Can enhance metal-support interactions, improving stability and anti-coking properties [10.039/D2NR02208G].
4. Support acidity: Lower acidity of OMS supports contributes to better anticoking capacity [10.021/acscatal.3c00816_1].

Limitations and challenges:
1. Deactivation: The main causes are sintering of active phases and coke deposition [10.021/acs.iecr.9b01413].
2. Balance between activity and stability: Oxidized species are more active but less stable than reduced species [10.021/acscatal.3c00816_1].

In conclusion, MCM-41 and SBA-15 supported VOx and CrOx catalysts offer significant advantages in PDH reactions due to their structural properties and catalytic behavior. However, optimizing catalyst composition, preparation methods, and reaction conditions is crucial to maximize their advantages and overcome limitations.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts for Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions represents a significant advancement in catalyst design. The unique structural properties of these materials, combined with the catalytic activity of supported VOx and CrOx species, offer a promising avenue for improving PDH processes.

Future research efforts might focus on:

1. Tailoring OMS structures: Developing novel OMS materials with optimized pore sizes, wall thicknesses, and surface properties to further enhance metal dispersion and catalyst stability.

2. Advanced characterization techniques: Utilizing in-situ and operando spectroscopic methods to gain deeper insights into the dynamic behavior of active sites during PDH reactions.

3. Multifunctional catalysts: Exploring the incorporation of additional promoters or co-catalysts within the OMS framework to create synergistic effects that enhance activity, selectivity, and stability.

4. Computational modeling: Employing advanced computational techniques to predict and design optimal catalyst compositions and structures for PDH reactions.

5. Sustainable catalyst production: Investigating eco-friendly synthesis methods for OMS catalysts to reduce the environmental impact of catalyst production.

6. Process intensification: Developing innovative reactor designs that capitalize on the unique properties of OMS catalysts to improve overall process efficiency and economics.

7. Regeneration strategies: Exploring novel approaches for in-situ catalyst regeneration to extend catalyst lifetime and reduce operational costs.

By addressing these areas, researchers can potentially overcome the current limitations of OMS-supported catalysts and pave the way for more efficient and sustainable PDH processes in the future.

<Chinese>
金属有序介孔二氧化硅（OMS）催化剂，特别是MCM-41和SBA-15负载的VOx和CrOx，在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中表现出比传统金属氧化物载体更优异的性能。这些催化剂由于其独特的结构特性和催化行为，具有几个优点。

结构特性：
1. 高比表面积：OMS材料为活性位点的分散提供了更大的表面积，可能增加催化剂效率 [10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081]。
2. 有序孔结构：MCM-41和SBA-15的良好定义的介孔结构允许反应物和产物更好地扩散，可能提高催化活性 [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5]。
3. 增强的金属分散性：OMS载体的均匀孔结构可以导致VOx和CrOx物种更好的分散，可能增加活性位点的数量 [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.05.024]。
4. 热稳定性：OMS材料通常表现出良好的热稳定性，这对于在高温下进行的PDH反应至关重要 [10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081]。

催化行为：
1. 更高的活性和选择性：VOx/MCM-41催化剂表现出良好的在线稳定性和高选择性，性能与工业用CrOx/Al2O3和Pt-Sn/Al2O3催化剂相似 [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022]。
2. 活性位点：对于VOx催化剂，V4+和V3+物种被识别为催化活性位点，随着V-O-V键数的减少，Lewis酸性增加 [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022]。对于CrOx催化剂，配位数大于四的孤立Cr(III)位点比晶态Cr2O3表面上的Cr位点更活跃、选择性更高且更稳定 [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014]。
3. 抗失活能力：孤立的VOx物种比聚集的物种表现出更高的抗失活能力 [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022]。

反应机理：
VOx/OMS催化剂上的PDH反应涉及丙烷C-H键的活化，随后是丙烯的脱附。这些催化剂的活性和选择性受表面存在的V-O键性质的影响 [10.039/D0CS00814A]。对于CrOx催化剂，活性Cr位点在反应过程中原位生成，Cr通常处于(III)氧化态 [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014]。

影响性能的因素：
1. 金属负载量和分散度：最佳金属负载量对维持催化剂活性和稳定性至关重要 [10.1002/cctc.201801598]。
2. 晶格氧物种：在氧化还原循环和整体催化活性中起关键作用 [10.021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。
3. H2预处理：可以增强金属-载体相互作用，提高稳定性和抗积碳性能 [10.039/D2NR02208G]。
4. 载体酸度：OMS载体的较低酸度有助于提高抗积碳能力 [10.021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。

局限性和挑战：
1. 失活：主要原因是活性相的烧结和积碳沉积 [10.021/acs.iecr.9b01413]。
2. 活性和稳定性之间的平衡：氧化态物种活性更高但稳定性较低，而还原态物种则相反 [10.021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。

结论：MCM-41和SBA-15负载的VOx和CrOx催化剂由于其结构特性和催化行为，在PDH反应中提供了显著的优势。然而，优化催化剂组成、制备方法和反应条件对于最大化其优势和克服局限性至关重要。

综合理解与未来展望：金属有序介孔二氧化硅（OMS）催化剂在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中的开发代表了催化剂设计的重大进步。这些材料独特的结构特性，结合负载的VOx和CrOx物种的催化活性，为改进PDH过程提供了一个充满希望的途径。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 定制OMS结构：开发具有优化孔径、壁厚和表面特性的新型OMS材料，以进一步增强金属分散性和催化剂稳定性。

2. 先进表征技术：利用原位和操作中的光谱方法，深入了解PDH反应过程中活性位点的动态行为。

3. 多功能催化剂：探索在OMS框架内引入额外的促进剂或共催化剂，创造协同效应以增强活性、选择性和稳定性。

4. 计算机模拟：采用先进的计算技术预测和设计PDH反应的最佳催化剂组成和结构。

5. 可持续催化剂生产：研究OMS催化剂的环保合成方法，以减少催化剂生产对环境的影响。

6. 过程强化：开发利用OMS催化剂独特性质的创新反应器设计，以提高整体过程效率和经济性。

7. 再生策略：探索原位催化剂再生的新方法，以延长催化剂寿命并降低运营成本。

通过解决这些领域的问题，研究人员可能克服OMS负载催化剂的当前限制，并为未来更高效、更可持续的PDH过程铺平道路。
