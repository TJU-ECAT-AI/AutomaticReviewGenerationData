<English>
Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts, particularly MCM-41 and SBA-15 supported VOx and CrOx, demonstrate promising performance in Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions compared to traditional metal oxide supports. These catalysts offer several advantages due to their unique structural properties and catalytic behavior.

Structural properties:
1. High specific surface area (e.g., MCM-41 with ~780-900 m2/g) [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005]
2. Ordered mesoporous structure with uniform pore size [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5]
3. Better thermal stability compared to traditional supports [10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081]
4. Low acidity, which contributes to improved anticoking capacity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]

Catalytic behavior:
1. Enhanced dispersion of active metal species (VOx and CrOx) [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.05.024]
2. Higher activity and selectivity compared to bulk V2O5 or traditional supports [10.1039/D0CS00814A]
3. Improved stability and regenerability, especially for VOx/MCM-41 [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005]

Advantages:
1. Better control over metal dispersion and pore structure [10.1039/D2NR02208G]
2. Improved optimization of active site accessibility [10.1016/j.cej.2019.122423]
3. Enhanced reactant/product diffusion [10.1016/j.cej.2019.122423]
4. Reduced coke formation compared to traditional supports [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005]

Reaction mechanisms:
The PDH reaction over VOx and CrOx supported on OMS catalysts involves:
1. Activation of C-H bonds in propane by metal oxide species [10.1039/D0CS00814A]
2. Participation of lattice oxygen in the initial stages of the reaction [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]
3. Redox cycle between oxidized and reduced metal species (e.g., Cr(VI) and Cr(III)) [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014]
4. Desorption of propylene from the catalyst surface [10.1039/D0CS00814A]

Challenges and limitations:
1. Fast deactivation due to coke formation and sintering of active metal species [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]
2. Potential pore blockage in mesoporous structures [10.1039/D2NR02208G]
3. Formation of less active metal oxide particles at higher loadings (e.g., Cr2O3 on SBA-15) [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014]

Influence of lattice oxygen species and H2 pretreatment:
1. Lattice oxygen participates in the PDH reaction, contributing to initial high activity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]
2. H2 pretreatment can improve catalyst stability by modifying metal-support interactions [10.1039/D2NR02208G]
3. Prereduced catalysts show lower initial activity but improved stability compared to as-prepared (oxidized) catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]

In conclusion, MCM-41 and SBA-15 supported VOx and CrOx catalysts offer significant advantages in PDH reactions due to their structural properties and enhanced catalytic behavior. However, optimizing metal loading, pretreatment conditions, and reaction parameters is crucial to balance high activity with long-term stability and minimize deactivation.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts for Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions represents a significant advancement in catalysis research. The unique properties of these materials, such as high surface area, ordered pore structure, and improved metal dispersion, offer a platform for designing more efficient and stable catalysts.

Looking forward, several research directions could further enhance the performance of OMS-supported catalysts in PDH reactions:

1. Tailored pore structures: Developing OMS materials with optimized pore sizes and interconnectivity could improve mass transfer and further enhance catalyst stability. Novel synthesis methods, such as the dendritic mesoporous silica nanoparticles (DMSNs) approach, show promise in this area [10.1016/j.cej.2019.122423].

2. Advanced metal incorporation techniques: Exploring new methods for incorporating active metal species into OMS supports, such as atomic layer deposition or advanced grafting techniques, could lead to more uniform and stable catalysts with higher metal dispersion.

3. Multifunctional catalysts: Combining VOx or CrOx with other promoters or active species within the OMS framework could create synergistic effects, potentially improving activity, selectivity, and stability simultaneously.

4. In-situ characterization studies: Utilizing advanced spectroscopic techniques to study the dynamic behavior of OMS-supported catalysts under reaction conditions could provide deeper insights into the reaction mechanisms and deactivation processes.

5. Computational modeling: Employing advanced computational methods to predict and optimize catalyst performance could accelerate the development of next-generation PDH catalysts.

6. Sustainable catalyst design: Investigating the use of abundant, non-toxic metals and environmentally friendly synthesis methods for OMS catalysts could address growing concerns about sustainability in chemical processes.

By pursuing these research directions, it may be possible to develop OMS-supported catalysts that not only outperform current industrial standards but also address the long-standing challenges of catalyst stability and longevity in PDH reactions.

<Chinese>
金属有序介孔硅（OMS）催化剂，特别是MCM-41和SBA-15负载的VOx和CrOx，在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中表现出比传统金属氧化物载体更有前景的性能。这些催化剂由于其独特的结构特性和催化行为而具有几个优势。

结构特性：
1. 高比表面积（例如，MCM-41约780-900 m2/g）[10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005]
2. 具有均匀孔径的有序介孔结构 [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5]
3. 与传统载体相比具有更好的热稳定性 [10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081]
4. 低酸度，有助于提高抗积炭能力 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]

催化行为：
1. 活性金属物种（VOx和CrOx）的分散度提高 [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.05.024]
2. 与体相V2O5或传统载体相比，具有更高的活性和选择性 [10.1039/D0CS00814A]
3. 改善了稳定性和再生能力，特别是对于VOx/MCM-41 [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005]

优势：
1. 更好地控制金属分散和孔结构 [10.1039/D2NR02208G]
2. 改善了活性位点的可及性优化 [10.1016/j.cej.2019.122423]
3. 提高了反应物和产物的扩散 [10.1016/j.cej.2019.122423]
4. 与传统载体相比，积炭形成减少 [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005]

反应机理：
OMS载体上VOx和CrOx催化的PDH反应涉及：
1. 金属氧化物物种活化丙烷中的C-H键 [10.1039/D0CS00814A]
2. 晶格氧参与反应的初始阶段 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]
3. 氧化态和还原态金属物种之间的氧化还原循环（例如，Cr(VI)和Cr(III)）[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014]
4. 丙烯从催化剂表面脱附 [10.1039/D0CS00814A]

挑战和局限性：
1. 由于积炭形成和活性金属物种烧结导致的快速失活 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]
2. 介孔结构中可能发生孔道堵塞 [10.1039/D2NR02208G]
3. 在高负载量时形成活性较低的金属氧化物颗粒（例如，SBA-15上的Cr2O3）[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014]

晶格氧物种和H2预处理的影响：
1. 晶格氧参与PDH反应，贡献于初始高活性 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]
2. H2预处理可以通过改变金属-载体相互作用来提高催化剂稳定性 [10.1039/D2NR02208G]
3. 预还原的催化剂与未处理（氧化态）催化剂相比，显示出较低的初始活性但改善的稳定性 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]

总之，MCM-41和SBA-15负载的VOx和CrOx催化剂由于其结构特性和增强的催化行为，在PDH反应中提供了显著的优势。然而，优化金属负载量、预处理条件和反应参数对于平衡高活性和长期稳定性以及最小化失活至关重要。

综合理解和前景展望：金属有序介孔硅（OMS）催化剂在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中的发展代表了催化研究的重大进步。这些材料的独特性质，如高比表面积、有序孔结构和改善的金属分散性，为设计更高效和稳定的催化剂提供了平台。

展望未来，几个研究方向可能进一步提高OMS负载催化剂在PDH反应中的性能：

1. 定制孔结构：开发具有优化孔径和互连性的OMS材料可以改善传质并进一步提高催化剂稳定性。新型合成方法，如树枝状介孔二氧化硅纳米粒子（DMSNs）方法，在这一领域显示出前景 [10.1016/j.cej.2019.122423]。

2. 先进的金属掺入技术：探索将活性金属物种掺入OMS载体的新方法，如原子层沉积或先进的接枝技术，可能会导致更均匀和稳定的催化剂，具有更高的金属分散度。

3. 多功能催化剂：在OMS框架内将VOx或CrOx与其他助剂或活性物种结合，可能会产生协同效应，潜在地同时提高活性、选择性和稳定性。

4. 原位表征研究：利用先进的光谱技术研究OMS负载催化剂在反应条件下的动态行为，可以深入了解反应机理和失活过程。

5. 计算机模拟：采用先进的计算方法预测和优化催化剂性能，可以加速下一代PDH催化剂的开发。

6. 可持续催化剂设计：研究使用丰富、无毒金属和环境友好的OMS催化剂合成方法，可以解决化学过程中日益增长的可持续性问题。

通过追求这些研究方向，可能开发出不仅优于当前工业标准，而且解决PDH反应中催化剂稳定性和寿命长期存在的挑战的OMS负载催化剂。
