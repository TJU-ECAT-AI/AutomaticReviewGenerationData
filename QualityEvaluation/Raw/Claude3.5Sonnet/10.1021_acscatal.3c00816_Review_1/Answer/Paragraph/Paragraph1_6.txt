<English>
Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts, particularly MCM-41 and SBA-15 supported VOx and CrOx, have shown promising performance in Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions compared to traditional metal oxide supports. These catalysts offer several advantages due to their unique structural properties and catalytic behavior.

Structural properties:
1. High surface area: OMS materials provide larger surface areas (e.g., MCM-41 with ~780-900 m2/g) for active site dispersion, increasing catalyst efficiency [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005].
2. Ordered pore structure: The well-defined mesoporous structure of MCM-41 and SBA-15 allows for better diffusion of reactants and products, improving catalytic activity [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5].
3. Enhanced metal dispersion: The uniform pore structure leads to better dispersion of VOx and CrOx species, increasing the number of active sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.05.024].
4. Thermal stability: OMS materials exhibit good thermal stability, crucial for high-temperature PDH reactions [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.04.018].

Catalytic behavior:
1. VOx/MCM-41 catalysts show superior performance compared to CrOx/MCM-41, achieving propane productivity of 0.2 kgC3H6·kgcat-1·h-1 and propylene selectivity of 90% [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1].
2. Isolated VOx species are most active and resistant to deactivation, with Lewis-acidic V3+ and V4+ identified as active sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022].
3. For CrOx/SBA-15, isolated Cr(III) sites with coordination number >4 are more active, selective, and stable than surface Cr sites on Cr2O3 crystals [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014].

Reaction mechanisms:
The PDH reaction over VOx/OMS and CrOx/OMS catalysts involves:
1. C-H bond activation in propane, influenced by the nature of V-O or Cr-O bonds.
2. Propylene desorption, affected by the metal-support interaction.
3. Participation of lattice oxygen species in the initial stages of the reaction [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1].

Advantages over traditional supports:
1. Better control over metal dispersion and pore structure.
2. Improved optimization of active site accessibility.
3. Enhanced reactant/product diffusion.
4. Lower acidity, contributing to better anticoking capacity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1].

Limitations and challenges:
1. Fast deactivation, particularly for CrOx catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1].
2. Potential pore blockage due to coke formation.
3. Possible metal leaching under harsh reaction conditions.
4. Need for careful optimization of metal loading and preparation methods.

Factors influencing performance and deactivation:
1. Metal loading: Optimal loading is crucial for maintaining high dispersion and activity [10.1002/cctc.201801598].
2. Oxidation state: Oxidized species are generally more active but less stable than reduced ones [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1].
3. H2 pretreatment: Can enhance metal-support interactions, improving stability and anti-coking properties [10.1039/D2NR02208G].
4. Lattice oxygen: Plays a crucial role in the redox cycle and overall catalytic activity [10.1021/acs.iecr.9b01413].
5. Support acidity: Higher acidity leads to increased coking tendency [10.1039/D2NR02208G].

In conclusion, while OMS-supported VOx and CrOx catalysts show great potential for PDH reactions, careful optimization of catalyst composition, preparation methods, and reaction conditions is crucial to maximize their advantages and overcome limitations.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 

The development of Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts for Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions represents a significant advancement in heterogeneous catalysis. The unique structural properties of OMS materials, combined with the catalytic activity of supported VOx and CrOx species, offer a promising path towards more efficient and stable PDH processes.

Looking forward, several research directions emerge:

1. Hybrid catalyst designs: Combining the advantages of different active species (e.g., VOx-CrOx or VOx-PtSn) on OMS supports could lead to synergistic effects, potentially improving both activity and stability.

2. Advanced characterization techniques: In-situ and operando spectroscopic methods could provide deeper insights into the dynamic behavior of active sites during PDH reactions, guiding rational catalyst design.

3. Tailored pore structures: Developing OMS materials with hierarchical pore systems or optimized pore geometries could enhance mass transfer and further improve catalyst performance.

4. Novel preparation methods: Exploring atomic layer deposition or other advanced synthesis techniques may allow for more precise control over active site distribution and metal-support interactions.

5. Multifunctional catalysts: Incorporating additional functionalities into OMS-supported catalysts, such as CO2 capture capabilities, could lead to more sustainable PDH processes.

6. Theoretical modeling: Computational studies focusing on the interplay between OMS structure, active site distribution, and reaction mechanisms could guide the design of next-generation PDH catalysts.

7. Process intensification: Integrating OMS-supported catalysts into novel reactor designs or membrane systems could lead to more efficient PDH processes with reduced energy consumption.

These research directions aim to address current limitations while leveraging the unique advantages of OMS-supported catalysts, potentially revolutionizing propylene production through more efficient and sustainable PDH processes.

<Chinese>
金属有序介孔二氧化硅（OMS）催化剂，特别是MCM-41和SBA-15负载的VOx和CrOx，在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中表现出比传统金属氧化物载体更优异的性能。这些催化剂由于其独特的结构特性和催化行为，具有多项优势。

结构特性：
1. 高比表面积：OMS材料提供更大的表面积（如MCM-41约780-900 m2/g）用于活性位分散，提高催化剂效率[10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005]。
2. 有序孔结构：MCM-41和SBA-15的良好定义的介孔结构允许反应物和产物更好的扩散，提高催化活性[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5]。
3. 增强金属分散：均匀的孔结构导致VOx和CrOx物种的更好分散，增加活性位点数量[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.05.024]。
4. 热稳定性：OMS材料表现出良好的热稳定性，这对高温PDH反应至关重要[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.04.018]。

催化行为：
1. VOx/MCM-41催化剂表现优于CrOx/MCM-41，丙烷生产率达到0.2 kgC3H6·kgcat-1·h-1，丙烯选择性达90%[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。
2. 孤立的VOx物种最活跃且抗失活，Lewis酸性V3+和V4+被确定为活性位点[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022]。
3. 对于CrOx/SBA-15，配位数>4的孤立Cr(III)位点比Cr2O3晶体表面的Cr位点更活跃、选择性更高、更稳定[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014]。

反应机理：
VOx/OMS和CrOx/OMS催化剂上的PDH反应涉及：
1. 丙烷C-H键的活化，受V-O或Cr-O键性质的影响。
2. 丙烯脱附，受金属-载体相互作用的影响。
3. 晶格氧物种在反应初期的参与[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。

相较于传统载体的优势：
1. 更好地控制金属分散和孔结构。
2. 改善活性位点可及性的优化。
3. 增强反应物/产物扩散。
4. 较低的酸度，有助于更好的抗积碳能力[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。

局限性和挑战：
1. 快速失活，特别是CrOx催化剂[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。
2. 由于积碳形成可能导致孔道堵塞。
3. 在苛刻反应条件下可能出现金属浸出。
4. 需要仔细优化金属负载量和制备方法。

影响性能和失活的因素：
1. 金属负载量：最佳负载量对维持高分散度和活性至关重要[10.1002/cctc.201801598]。
2. 氧化态：氧化态物种通常比还原态更活跃但稳定性较差[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。
3. H2预处理：可以增强金属-载体相互作用，提高稳定性和抗积碳性能[10.1039/D2NR02208G]。
4. 晶格氧：在氧化还原循环和整体催化活性中起关键作用[10.1021/acs.iecr.9b01413]。
5. 载体酸度：较高的酸度导致更容易积碳[10.1039/D2NR02208G]。

总之，虽然OMS负载的VOx和CrOx催化剂在PDH反应中表现出巨大潜力，但仍需仔细优化催化剂组成、制备方法和反应条件，以最大化其优势并克服局限性。

综合理解与未来展望：

金属有序介孔二氧化硅（OMS）催化剂在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中的发展代表了多相催化领域的重大进步。OMS材料的独特结构特性，结合负载的VOx和CrOx物种的催化活性，为更高效、更稳定的PDH过程提供了一条有前途的道路。

展望未来，几个研究方向浮现出来：

1. 混合催化剂设计：在OMS载体上结合不同活性物种（如VOx-CrOx或VOx-PtSn）的优势可能会产生协同效应，有望同时提高活性和稳定性。

2. 先进表征技术：原位和操作条件下的光谱方法可以提供PDH反应过程中活性位点动态行为的深入见解，指导合理的催化剂设计。

3. 定制孔结构：开发具有分层孔系统或优化孔几何结构的OMS材料可以增强质量传递，进一步提高催化剂性能。

4. 新型制备方法：探索原子层沉积或其他先进合成技术可能允许更精确地控制活性位点分布和金属-载体相互作用。

5. 多功能催化剂：将额外功能（如CO2捕获能力）整合到OMS负载催化剂中，可能导致更可持续的PDH过程。

6. 理论建模：聚焦于OMS结构、活性位点分布和反应机理之间相互作用的计算研究可以指导下一代PDH催化剂的设计。

7. 过程强化：将OMS负载催化剂集成到新型反应器设计或膜系统中可能导致更高效的PDH过程，减少能源消耗。

这些研究方向旨在解决当前的局限性，同时利用OMS负载催化剂的独特优势，有可能通过更高效、更可持续的PDH过程彻底改变丙烯生产。
