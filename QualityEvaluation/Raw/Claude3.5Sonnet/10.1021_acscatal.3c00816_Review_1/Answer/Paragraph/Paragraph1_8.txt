<English>
Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts, particularly MCM-41 and SBA-15 supported VOx and CrOx, have shown promising performance in Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions compared to traditional metal oxide supports. These catalysts offer several advantages due to their unique structural properties and catalytic behavior.

Structural properties:
1. High surface area: OMS materials provide a larger surface area for active site dispersion, potentially increasing catalyst efficiency [10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081].
2. Ordered pore structure: The well-defined mesoporous structure of MCM-41 and SBA-15 allows for better diffusion of reactants and products, potentially improving catalytic activity [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5].
3. Enhanced metal dispersion: The uniform pore structure of OMS supports can lead to better dispersion of VOx and CrOx species, potentially increasing the number of active sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.05.024].
4. Thermal stability: OMS materials generally exhibit good thermal stability, which is crucial for PDH reactions that occur at high temperatures [10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081].

Catalytic behavior:
1. VOx/MCM-41 catalysts show superior performance compared to CrOx/MCM-41, achieving a propane productivity of 0.2 kgC3H6·kgcat-1·h-1 and a propylene selectivity of 90% [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1].
2. Isolated VOx species are most active and resistant against deactivation [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022].
3. Lewis-acidic V3+ and V4+ were identified as active sites for dehydrogenation reactions [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022].
4. For CrOx/SBA-15, isolated Cr(III) sites with coordination number greater than four are more active, selective, and stable than Cr sites on the surface of crystalline Cr2O3 [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014].

Reaction mechanisms:
1. The reaction mechanism involves the participation of lattice oxygen species from as-prepared CrOx and VOx in the PDH reaction [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1].
2. The degree of dispersion of V species on the catalyst surface mainly determines the catalytic activity towards propene production [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.05.024].
3. For VOx-based catalysts, different V-O bonds exhibit varying C-H activation and propylene desorption energy barriers [10.1039/D0CS00814A].

Advantages:
1. Higher activity and selectivity compared to traditional supports [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022].
2. Improved stability and regenerability, especially for VOx/MCM-41 [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005].
3. Reduced coke formation, leading to more stable performance over longer periods [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005].

Limitations and challenges:
1. Fast deactivation, especially for CrOx/MCM-41 catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1].
2. Formation of less active Cr2O3 particles at higher loadings on SBA-15 [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014].
3. Potential pore blockage due to coke formation [10.1039/D2NR02208G].

Factors influencing performance and deactivation:
1. Metal loading and dispersion: Optimal metal loading is crucial for maintaining catalyst activity [10.1002/cctc.201801598].
2. Redox properties: The redox cycle between active metal species plays a role in catalytic activity [10.1002/cctc.201801598].
3. H2 pretreatment: Can enhance metal-support interactions, improving stability and anti-coking properties [10.1039/D2NR02208G].
4. Lattice oxygen capacity: Plays an important role in determining the initial conversion of propane and the final yield of propylene [10.1021/acs.iecr.9b01413].

In conclusion, while OMS-supported VOx and CrOx catalysts show great potential for PDH reactions, careful optimization of catalyst composition, preparation methods, and reaction conditions is crucial to maximize their advantages and overcome limitations.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of Metal-Ordered Mesoporous Silica (OMS) catalysts for Propane Dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions represents a significant advancement in catalysis research. The unique structural properties of OMS materials, combined with the catalytic activity of supported VOx and CrOx species, offer a promising avenue for improving PDH processes.

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Tailoring OMS structures: Developing novel OMS materials with optimized pore sizes, wall thicknesses, and surface properties to further enhance metal dispersion and stability.

2. Advanced characterization techniques: Utilizing in-situ and operando spectroscopic methods to gain deeper insights into the dynamic behavior of active sites during PDH reactions.

3. Multifunctional catalysts: Exploring the incorporation of additional promoters or co-catalysts to further improve activity, selectivity, and stability.

4. Catalyst regeneration strategies: Developing more efficient regeneration methods to prolong catalyst lifetime and reduce operational costs.

5. Theoretical modeling: Employing advanced computational techniques to predict catalyst behavior and guide rational catalyst design.

6. Scale-up and industrial implementation: Addressing challenges related to large-scale production and long-term stability of OMS-supported catalysts in industrial settings.

By addressing these areas, researchers can work towards developing next-generation PDH catalysts that overcome current limitations and potentially revolutionize propylene production processes.

<Chinese>
金属有序介孔二氧化硅（OMS）催化剂，特别是MCM-41和SBA-15负载的VOx和CrOx，在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中显示出比传统金属氧化物载体更优异的性能。这些催化剂由于其独特的结构特性和催化行为而具有多项优势。

结构特性：
1. 高比表面积：OMS材料为活性位点分散提供了更大的表面积，可能会提高催化剂效率 [10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081]。
2. 有序孔道结构：MCM-41和SBA-15的定义良好的介孔结构可以促进反应物和产物的扩散，可能会提高催化活性 [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5]。
3. 增强金属分散：OMS载体的均匀孔道结构可以导致VOx和CrOx物种更好的分散，可能会增加活性位点的数量 [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.05.024]。
4. 热稳定性：OMS材料通常表现出良好的热稳定性，这对于在高温下进行的PDH反应至关重要 [10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081]。

催化行为：
1. VOx/MCM-41催化剂显示出优于CrOx/MCM-41的性能，丙烷生产率达到0.2 kgC3H6·kgcat-1·h-1，丙烯选择性为90% [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。
2. 孤立的VOx物种最为活跃且抗失活能力最强 [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022]。
3. 路易斯酸性V3+和V4+被确定为脱氢反应的活性位点 [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022]。
4. 对于CrOx/SBA-15，配位数大于四的孤立Cr(III)位点比晶态Cr2O3表面的Cr位点更活跃、选择性更高且更稳定 [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014]。

反应机理：
1. 反应机理涉及新制备的CrOx和VOx中的晶格氧物种参与PDH反应 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。
2. 催化剂表面V物种的分散程度主要决定了对丙烯生成的催化活性 [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.05.024]。
3. 对于基于VOx的催化剂，不同的V-O键表现出不同的C-H活化和丙烯脱附能垒 [10.1039/D0CS00814A]。

优势：
1. 与传统载体相比，具有更高的活性和选择性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.022]。
2. 改善了稳定性和再生能力，特别是VOx/MCM-41 [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005]。
3. 降低了积碳形成，导致更长时间内性能更稳定 [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.06.005]。

局限性和挑战：
1. 快速失活，特别是CrOx/MCM-41催化剂 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_1]。
2. 在SBA-15上高负载时形成活性较低的Cr2O3颗粒 [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014]。
3. 由于积碳形成可能导致孔道堵塞 [10.1039/D2NR02208G]。

影响性能和失活的因素：
1. 金属负载量和分散度：最佳金属负载量对维持催化剂活性至关重要 [10.1002/cctc.201801598]。
2. 氧化还原性质：活性金属物种之间的氧化还原循环在催化活性中起作用 [10.1002/cctc.201801598]。
3. H2预处理：可以增强金属-载体相互作用，改善稳定性和抗积碳性能 [10.1039/D2NR02208G]。
4. 晶格氧容量：在决定丙烷初始转化率和丙烯最终产率方面起重要作用 [10.1021/acs.iecr.9b01413]。

总之，虽然基于OMS的VOx和CrOx催化剂在PDH反应中显示出巨大潜力，但仍需要仔细优化催化剂组成、制备方法和反应条件，以最大化其优势并克服局限性。

综合理解与未来展望：金属有序介孔二氧化硅（OMS）催化剂在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中的开发代表了催化研究的重大进展。OMS材料的独特结构特性，结合支持的VOx和CrOx物种的催化活性，为改进PDH工艺提供了一个有前景的途径。

未来研究方向可能集中在：

1. 定制OMS结构：开发具有优化孔径、壁厚和表面性质的新型OMS材料，以进一步增强金属分散和稳定性。

2. 先进表征技术：利用原位和操作中的光谱方法，深入了解PDH反应过程中活性位点的动态行为。

3. 多功能催化剂：探索额外促进剂或共催化剂的引入，以进一步提高活性、选择性和稳定性。

4. 催化剂再生策略：开发更高效的再生方法，延长催化剂寿命并降低运营成本。

5. 理论建模：采用先进的计算技术预测催化剂行为并指导理性催化剂设计。

6. 规模扩大和工业实施：解决与OMS支持催化剂在工业环境中大规模生产和长期稳定性相关的挑战。

通过解决这些领域，研究人员可以努力开发克服当前限制的下一代PDH催化剂，并可能彻底改变丙烯生产工艺。
