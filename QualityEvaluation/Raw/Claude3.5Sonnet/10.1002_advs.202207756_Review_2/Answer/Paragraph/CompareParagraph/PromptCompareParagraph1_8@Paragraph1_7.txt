Recent studies have demonstrated that the variability in intraporous Ga species structure and distribution in H-ZSM-5 zeolites, influenced by factors such as Ga loading and Si/Al ratio, significantly challenges the assumption of uniform PDH activity [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.11.004]. This variability is manifested in the formation of two distinct types of Ga species: Ga+H+ pair sites and isolated Ga+ sites, each with different properties and reactivities [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.11.004].

The distribution of these Ga species is heavily influenced by the Si/Al ratio of the zeolite support. Lower Si/Al ratios lead to higher densities of framework Al pairs, which in turn affect Ga speciation [10.1002/advs.202207756\_2]. This relationship between zeolite composition and Ga speciation has profound implications for catalytic performance, as different Ga species exhibit varying levels of activity in PDH reactions.

A non-monotonic relationship between Ga/Al ratio and catalytic performance has been observed, with PDH rates increasing as the Ga/Al ratio rises from 0 to 0.5, before declining with further addition of Ga [10.1002/advs.202207756\_2]. This trend suggests that a single type of active Ga species is unlikely to dominate PDH activity across all Ga loadings, contrary to previous assumptions.

Furthermore, Ga-normalized PDH rates have been shown to decrease with increasing Ga loading, indicating that highly active Ga species are introduced at low loadings, while higher loadings lead to the formation of less active or inactive Ga species [10.1002/advs.202207756\_2]. This observation underscores the importance of carefully controlling Ga loading to optimize catalyst performance.

The complexity of Ga speciation in H-ZSM-5 zeolites is further evidenced by studies using advanced characterization techniques such as 71Ga QCPMG NMR. These studies have identified two main types of Ga species: highly dispersed oxides and cationic Ga species, with their relative content varying depending on preparation methods [10.1002/cctc.202000650].

The impact of different Ga species on catalytic activity extends beyond PDH reactions. For instance, cationic Ga species like GaO+ have been shown to promote methane activation, with the local environment of the active Ga species influencing reaction pathways [10.1002/cctc.202000650].
