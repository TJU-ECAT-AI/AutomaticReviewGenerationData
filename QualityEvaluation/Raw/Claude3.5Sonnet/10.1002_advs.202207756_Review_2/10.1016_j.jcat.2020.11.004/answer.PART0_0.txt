The variability in intraporous Ga species structure and distribution in H-ZSM-5 zeolites, influenced by factors such as Ga loading and Si/Al ratio, significantly challenges the assumption of uniform PDH activity. The study reveals that Ga speciation varies with BAS density in the zeolite, which is directly related to the Si/Al ratio. Two distinct types of Ga species are identified: Ga+H+ pair sites formed by exchanging paired BAS sites, and isolated Ga+ species formed by exchanging isolated BAS.

The non-monotonic relationship between Ga/Al ratio and catalytic performance can be explained by the different properties and reactivities of these two Ga species. As the Ga loading increases, the formation of Ga+H+ pair sites is initially favored, but at higher loadings, isolated Ga+ species become more prevalent. This shift in speciation likely contributes to the observed non-linear relationship between Ga loading and catalytic activity.

These findings have significant implications for understanding the true nature of active sites and optimizing Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts. The different reactivities of Ga+H+ pair sites and isolated Ga+ species suggest that they may have different catalytic properties in the PDH reaction. This challenges the conventional view of a single type of active site and highlights the need for a more nuanced approach to catalyst design and optimization.

To optimize Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, it is crucial to control not only the overall Ga loading but also the distribution of Ga species between paired and isolated sites. This may involve carefully tuning the Si/Al ratio of the zeolite support and developing preparation methods that favor the formation of the more active Ga species. Future research should focus on elucidating the specific catalytic roles of Ga+H+ pair sites and isolated Ga+ species in the PDH reaction to guide the rational design of more efficient catalysts.
