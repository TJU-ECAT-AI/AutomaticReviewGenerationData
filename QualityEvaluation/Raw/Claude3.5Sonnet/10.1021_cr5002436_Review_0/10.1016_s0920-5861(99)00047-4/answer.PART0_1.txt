<EnglishVersionAnswer>

Light olefins like propylene and ethylene are important feedstocks for the chemical industry. Propylene is used to produce polypropylene, while ethylene is a key building block for many chemicals and plastics. Isobutene, another light olefin, is used to make fuel additives like MTBE and ETBE.

The main production method for light olefins discussed is alkane dehydrogenation. This process faces several thermodynamic challenges:

1. It is endothermic, requiring high temperatures.
2. Low pressures are needed for significant yields.
3. Lighter alkanes (e.g. ethane) require higher temperatures than longer chain alkanes for dehydrogenation.
4. Conversion is limited by thermodynamic equilibrium - for example, ethane dehydrogenation to ethylene only reaches 50% conversion at 725°C under atmospheric pressure.

These factors make the industrial production of light olefins via alkane dehydrogenation technically challenging and energy-intensive. The high temperatures also promote unwanted side reactions like cracking and coking. Despite these challenges, alkane dehydrogenation remains an important route for light olefin production, especially as demand grows for chemicals like polypropylene.

轻质烯烃如丙烯和乙烯是化学工业的重要原料。丙烯用于生产聚丙烯，而乙烯是许多化学品和塑料的关键构建块。异丁烯，另一种轻质烯烃，用于制造MTBE和ETBE等燃料添加剂。

文中讨论的轻质烯烃主要生产方法是烷烃脱氢。这个过程面临几个热力学挑战：

1. 它是吸热反应，需要高温。
2. 需要低压才能获得显著产率。
3. 较轻的烷烃（如乙烷）比较长链的烷烃需要更高的脱氢温度。
4. 转化率受热力学平衡限制 - 例如，在大气压下乙烷脱氢生成乙烯的转化率在725°C时只能达到50%。

这些因素使得通过烷烃脱氢进行轻质烯烃的工业生产在技术上具有挑战性且能源密集。高温还会促进裂解和结焦等不良副反应。尽管存在这些挑战，随着聚丙烯等化学品需求的增长，烷烃脱氢仍然是轻质烯烃生产的重要途径。
