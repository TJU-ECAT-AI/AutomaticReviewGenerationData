Light olefins like ethylene and propylene are crucial to the chemical industry as they are large-tonnage, high-value petrochemicals. The UOP/Hydro MTO (Methanol-to-Olefins) process is a key production method, utilizing a SAPO-34 catalyst to achieve up to 80% yield of ethylene and propylene from methanol.

The synthesis of these light olefins faces several thermodynamic challenges. Temperature is a critical factor, with higher temperatures favoring ethylene production over propylene, but potentially reducing overall light olefin selectivity. Pressure also plays a role, with lower methanol partial pressures generally resulting in higher overall yields.
