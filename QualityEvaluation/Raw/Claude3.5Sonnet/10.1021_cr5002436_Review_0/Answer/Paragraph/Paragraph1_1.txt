<English>
Light olefins, particularly ethylene and propylene, are crucial feedstocks in the chemical industry, with significant market demand and diverse applications. These compounds serve as building blocks for a wide range of products including polymers (e.g., polyethylene, polypropylene), oxygenates (e.g., ethylene glycol, acetone), and important chemical intermediates (e.g., ethylbenzene) [10.1021/cr5002436_0].

Market trends indicate a steady increase in demand for light olefins, with global production capacity doubling over the past 15 years. The demand for propylene, in particular, has been growing faster than that of ethylene, especially in Western Europe [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]. This trend has led to increased interest in on-purpose propylene production methods.

The main production methods for light olefins include:

1. Steam cracking of hydrocarbons (primary method)
2. Fluid catalytic cracking (FCC)
3. Catalytic dehydrogenation of alkanes (growing in importance, especially for propylene)
4. Methanol-to-Olefins (MTO) technology (emerging method)

The synthesis of light olefins, particularly through dehydrogenation, faces significant thermodynamic challenges:

1. Endothermic reactions: Dehydrogenation reactions are highly endothermic (e.g., propane to propene, ΔH298° = +110 kJ mol−1), requiring substantial energy input [10.1002/9783527626113.ch15].

2. Temperature effects: High temperatures (550-750°C) are typically required to achieve economically viable conversions. However, higher temperatures also promote unwanted side reactions, coke formation, and catalyst deactivation [10.1021/cr5002436_0].

3. Pressure effects: Lower pressures generally favor olefin formation. Commercial processes often operate at subatmospheric pressures (0.2-0.5 bar) to shift the equilibrium towards products [10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.103].

4. Alkane chain length effects: The equilibrium constant for paraffin dehydrogenation increases significantly with carbon number. This means longer chain alkanes are easier to dehydrogenate, achieving higher conversions at lower temperatures. For example, at 1 atm pressure and 40% conversion, propane dehydrogenation requires at least 580°C, while dodecane only needs 450°C [10.1007/s11244-012-9917-9].

5. Catalyst considerations: Catalyst design is crucial to overcome thermodynamic limitations and improve selectivity. For instance, chromia-based catalysts have been used for decades, while newer technologies like UOP's Oleflex and CATOFIN processes employ specialized catalysts to enhance performance [10.1016/s1351-4180(12)70255-6].

To address these challenges, researchers are exploring alternative production methods such as oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) with oxygen or CO2 as oxidants. These processes aim to overcome thermodynamic limitations by making the reaction exothermic and avoiding carbon formation [10.1039/c2ee22409g].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The production of light olefins represents a critical intersection of thermodynamics, catalysis, and process engineering in the chemical industry. As demand continues to grow, particularly for propylene, there is a pressing need for more efficient and sustainable production methods.

Future research efforts may focus on:

1. Advanced catalyst design: Developing catalysts that can operate at lower temperatures while maintaining high selectivity and resistance to coking. This could involve exploring novel materials or fine-tuning existing catalysts through techniques like nanostructuring or promoter addition.

2. Process intensification: Integrating reaction and separation steps to overcome equilibrium limitations and improve energy efficiency. Membrane reactors or reactive distillation could be promising avenues for exploration.

3. Alternative feedstocks: With the shift towards lighter feedstocks in some regions due to shale gas availability, there's an opportunity to develop processes that can efficiently produce a balanced portfolio of olefins from various feedstocks.

4. CO2 utilization: Further development of CO2-mediated oxidative dehydrogenation could address both the thermodynamic challenges of olefin production and contribute to CO2 mitigation efforts.

5. Modelling and simulation: Advanced computational methods could help optimize reactor designs and operating conditions, potentially identifying novel process configurations that better manage the thermodynamic constraints.

As the industry evolves, a holistic approach considering not only the thermodynamics of olefin production but also its environmental impact and integration with other chemical processes will be crucial for sustainable growth and innovation in this vital sector of the chemical industry.

<Chinese>
轻质烯烃，尤其是乙烯和丙烯，是化工行业的重要原料，具有显著的市场需求和广泛的应用。这些化合物作为多种产品的基础构件，包括聚合物（如聚乙烯、聚丙烯）、含氧化合物（如乙二醇、丙酮）和重要的化学中间体（如乙苯）[10.1021/cr5002436_0]。

市场趋势显示轻质烯烃的需求稳步增长，过去15年全球产能翻了一番。特别是丙烯的需求增长速度快于乙烯，尤其是在西欧地区[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。这一趋势导致人们对定向丙烯生产方法的兴趣日益增加。

轻质烯烃的主要生产方法包括：

1. 烃类蒸汽裂解（主要方法）
2. 流化催化裂解（FCC）
3. 烷烃催化脱氢（日益重要，特别是对于丙烯）
4. 甲醇制烯烃（MTO）技术（新兴方法）

轻质烯烃的合成，特别是通过脱氢方式，面临着重大的热力学挑战：

1. 吸热反应：脱氢反应高度吸热（例如，丙烷制丙烯，ΔH298° = +110 kJ mol−1），需要大量能量输入[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15]。

2. 温度效应：通常需要高温（550-750°C）才能实现经济可行的转化率。然而，更高的温度也会促进不需要的副反应、积碳形成和催化剂失活[10.1021/cr5002436_0]。

3. 压力效应：较低的压力通常有利于烯烃的形成。商业过程通常在亚大气压（0.2-0.5巴）下操作，以将平衡向产物方向移动[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.103]。

4. 烷烃链长效应：随着碳数的增加，烷烃脱氢的平衡常数显著增加。这意味着较长链的烷烃更容易脱氢，在较低温度下也能达到更高的转化率。例如，在1个大气压和40%转化率下，丙烷脱氢需要至少580°C，而十二烷仅需450°C[10.1007/s11244-012-9917-9]。

5. 催化剂考虑：催化剂设计对于克服热力学限制和提高选择性至关重要。例如，铬基催化剂已使用数十年，而UOP的Oleflex和CATOFIN等新技术采用专门的催化剂来提高性能[10.1016/s1351-4180(12)70255-6]。

为了应对这些挑战，研究人员正在探索替代生产方法，如以氧气或CO2为氧化剂的氧化脱氢（ODH）。这些过程旨在通过使反应成为放热反应并避免碳的形成来克服热力学限制[10.1039/c2ee22409g]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：轻质烯烃的生产代表了化工行业中热力学、催化和过程工程的关键交叉点。随着需求持续增长，特别是丙烯，对更高效和可持续生产方法的需求日益迫切。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 先进催化剂设计：开发能在较低温度下运行，同时保持高选择性和抗积碳性能的催化剂。这可能涉及探索新材料或通过纳米结构化或添加助剂等技术来微调现有催化剂。

2. 过程强化：整合反应和分离步骤，以克服平衡限制并提高能源效率。膜反应器或反应精馏可能是值得探索的有前景方向。

3. 替代原料：由于页岩气的可用性，某些地区正转向使用较轻的原料，这为开发能够从各种原料高效生产平衡烯烃组合的工艺提供了机会。

4. CO2利用：进一步发展CO2介导的氧化脱氢可以同时解决烯烃生产的热力学挑战并为CO2减排作出贡献。

5. 模型和模拟：先进的计算方法可以帮助优化反应器设计和操作条件，可能识别出能更好地管理热力学约束的新型工艺配置。

随着行业的发展，不仅要考虑烯烃生产的热力学，还要考虑其环境影响以及与其他化学工艺的整合，这种全面的方法对于化工行业这一重要领域的可持续增长和创新至关重要。
