<English>
Light olefins, particularly ethylene and propylene, are crucial feedstocks in the chemical industry, with significant market demand and diverse applications. These compounds serve as building blocks for a wide range of products including polymers (e.g., polyethylene, polypropylene), oxygenates (e.g., ethylene glycol, acetone), and important chemical intermediates (e.g., ethylbenzene, propionaldehyde) [10.1021/cr5002436_0].

Market trends indicate a steady increase in demand for light olefins, with global production capacity doubling over the past 15 years. In 2003, worldwide production reached 100 million tonnes for ethylene and 56 million tonnes for propylene [10.1002/9783527610044.hetcat0162]. The demand for propylene, in particular, has been growing faster than that of ethylene, especially in Western Europe [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009].

The main production methods for light olefins include:

1. Steam cracking of hydrocarbons (primary method)
2. Fluid catalytic cracking (FCC)
3. Catalytic dehydrogenation of alkanes (growing in importance)
4. Methanol-to-Olefins (MTO) technology (emerging method)

Each of these methods faces significant thermodynamic challenges in the synthesis of light olefins:

1. Temperature effects: Higher temperatures are generally required to achieve economically viable conversion levels. For example, propane dehydrogenation requires temperatures of 550-750°C to obtain alkane conversions >50% at 1 bar [10.1021/cr5002436_0]. However, higher temperatures also accelerate unwanted side reactions, coke formation, and catalyst deactivation [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

2. Pressure effects: Lower pressures are typically favored for dehydrogenation reactions to shift the equilibrium towards products. Commercial processes often operate at subatmospheric pressures (0.3-0.5 atm) to achieve higher per-pass conversions [10.1002/9783527626113.ch15].

3. Alkane chain length effects: The equilibrium constant for paraffin dehydrogenation increases significantly with carbon number. This means that lighter alkanes (e.g., ethane) require higher temperatures for dehydrogenation compared to heavier alkanes. For instance, at 1 atm pressure and 40% conversion, propane dehydrogenation needs at least 580°C, while dodecane only requires 450°C [10.1007/s11244-012-9917-9].

4. Endothermic nature: Dehydrogenation reactions are highly endothermic (e.g., ΔH298° = +110 kJ mol−1 for propane to propene), requiring substantial energy input [10.1002/9783527626113.ch15].

5. Equilibrium limitations: Conversions are thermodynamically limited, especially at lower temperatures, necessitating careful reactor and process design to achieve economically viable yields [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009].

To address these challenges, researchers and industry professionals are exploring various strategies:

1. Development of more effective catalysts with high activity and selectivity [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009].
2. Use of CO2 as an oxidant in oxidative dehydrogenation to make the reaction exothermic and avoid carbon formation [10.1021/ef0340716].
3. Optimization of process conditions, including the use of lower pressures and diluents to increase conversions [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4].
4. Exploration of alternative production methods like methanol-to-olefins (MTO) technology, which allows for greater flexibility in adjusting product ratios [10.1016/s0167-2991(04)80018-1].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The production of light olefins, particularly ethylene and propylene, remains a critical area of research and development in the chemical industry. The growing demand for these feedstocks, coupled with the thermodynamic challenges associated with their synthesis, necessitates innovative approaches to improve efficiency and sustainability.

Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Advanced catalyst design: Developing novel catalysts that can operate at lower temperatures while maintaining high selectivity and resistance to coking. This could involve exploring new materials, such as metal-organic frameworks or single-atom catalysts, which offer precise control over active sites.

2. Process intensification: Integrating reaction and separation processes to overcome equilibrium limitations and improve overall efficiency. Membrane reactors or reactive distillation could be promising avenues for this approach.

3. Alternative feedstocks: Investigating the use of renewable resources, such as biomass or CO2, as feedstocks for light olefin production. This could help address sustainability concerns and reduce dependence on fossil fuels.

4. Artificial intelligence and machine learning: Employing these tools to optimize process conditions, predict catalyst performance, and accelerate the discovery of new materials and reaction pathways.

5. Modular and flexible production systems: Developing adaptable processes that can quickly respond to changing market demands and feedstock availability, potentially incorporating multiple production routes within a single facility.

By addressing these challenges and exploring new opportunities, the chemical industry can continue to meet the growing demand for light olefins while improving sustainability and economic viability.

<Chinese>
轻烯烃，特别是乙烯和丙烯，是化学工业中至关重要的原料，具有巨大的市场需求和广泛的应用。这些化合物作为多种产品的基础构建块，包括聚合物（如聚乙烯、聚丙烯）、含氧化合物（如乙二醇、丙酮）和重要的化学中间体（如乙苯、丙醛）[10.1021/cr5002436_0]。

市场趋势显示轻烯烃的需求稳步增长，过去15年全球产能翻了一番。2003年，全球乙烯产量达到1亿吨，丙烯产量达到5600万吨[10.1002/9783527610044.hetcat0162]。特别是丙烯的需求增长速度快于乙烯，尤其是在西欧地区[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。

轻烯烃的主要生产方法包括：

1. 烃类蒸汽裂解（主要方法）
2. 流化催化裂解（FCC）
3. 烷烃催化脱氢（重要性日益增加）
4. 甲醇制烯烃（MTO）技术（新兴方法）

这些方法在轻烯烃合成中都面临着重大的热力学挑战：

1. 温度效应：通常需要较高的温度才能达到经济可行的转化水平。例如，丙烷脱氢需要550-750°C的温度才能在1巴压力下获得>50%的烷烃转化率[10.1021/cr5002436_0]。然而，更高的温度也会加速不必要的副反应、积碳形成和催化剂失活[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

2. 压力效应：脱氢反应通常倾向于较低的压力，以将平衡转移到产物一侧。商业过程通常在亚大气压（0.3-0.5 atm）下操作，以获得更高的单程转化率[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15]。

3. 烷烃链长效应：随着碳数的增加，烷烃脱氢的平衡常数显著增加。这意味着较轻的烷烃（如乙烷）与较重的烷烃相比需要更高的温度进行脱氢。例如，在1 atm压力和40%转化率下，丙烷脱氢需要至少580°C，而十二烷只需要450°C[10.1007/s11244-012-9917-9]。

4. 吸热性质：脱氢反应是高度吸热的（例如，丙烷到丙烯的ΔH298° = +110 kJ mol−1），需要大量的能量输入[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15]。

5. 平衡限制：转化率受热力学限制，特别是在较低温度下，需要精心设计反应器和工艺以达到经济可行的产率[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。

为应对这些挑战，研究人员和行业专业人士正在探索各种策略：

1. 开发具有高活性和选择性的更有效催化剂[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。
2. 在氧化脱氢中使用CO2作为氧化剂，使反应成为放热反应并避免碳的形成[10.1021/ef0340716]。
3. 优化工艺条件，包括使用较低压力和稀释剂以提高转化率[10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4]。
4. 探索替代生产方法，如甲醇制烯烃（MTO）技术，该技术允许更灵活地调整产品比例[10.1016/s0167-2991(04)80018-1]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：轻烯烃，特别是乙烯和丙烯的生产仍然是化学工业研究和开发的关键领域。这些原料不断增长的需求，加上其合成过程中面临的热力学挑战，需要创新方法来提高效率和可持续性。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 先进催化剂设计：开发能在较低温度下操作，同时保持高选择性和抗积碳能力的新型催化剂。这可能涉及探索新材料，如金属有机框架或单原子催化剂，它们能够精确控制活性位点。

2. 工艺强化：整合反应和分离过程，以克服平衡限制并提高整体效率。膜反应器或反应精馏可能是这种方法的有前途的途径。

3. 替代原料：研究使用可再生资源，如生物质或CO2，作为轻烯烃生产的原料。这可以帮助解决可持续性问题，减少对化石燃料的依赖。

4. 人工智能和机器学习：利用这些工具优化工艺条件，预测催化剂性能，并加速新材料和反应途径的发现。

5. 模块化和灵活的生产系统：开发可以快速响应不断变化的市场需求和原料可用性的适应性工艺，可能在单个设施内整合多种生产路线。

通过解决这些挑战并探索新的机会，化学工业可以继续满足轻烯烃不断增长的需求，同时提高可持续性和经济可行性。
