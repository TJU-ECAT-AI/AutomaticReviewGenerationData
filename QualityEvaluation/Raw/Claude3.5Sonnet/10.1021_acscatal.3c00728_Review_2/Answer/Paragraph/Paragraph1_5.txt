<English>
The development of 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for the Oxidative Dehydrogenation of Propane (ODHP) reaction has attracted significant research interest due to their unique structural features and potential for improved catalytic performance. These active sites can be developed through two main methods: (1) doping active boron oxide or boron hydroxyl units onto the surface of various hosting materials, and (2) utilizing the exposed surface of bulk boride materials [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2].

One of the key advantages of 2-D dispersed active sites over linearly dispersed edge sites is the significantly improved density of active sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]. This increased density can potentially lead to enhanced catalytic activity and efficiency in ODHP reactions. The ability to create a higher concentration of active sites across the catalyst surface has implications for improved catalytic performance and has thus drawn extensive interest from researchers in the field [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2].

Recent studies have challenged previous assumptions about edge sites being the primary active sites for boron nitride catalysts. Instead, research suggests the formation of surface-stabilized BOx active sites on various boron-containing materials during the ODHP reaction [10.1002/cctc.201701140]. This shift in understanding from edge-site catalysis to surface-wide BOx active sites has opened up new possibilities for catalyst design and optimization in the field of oxidative dehydrogenation reactions.

The crystal structure of boron-based materials has been found to influence the formation of BOx species, with layered borides potentially presenting different boron surface concentrations compared to tetragonal borides [10.1002/cctc.201701140]. This structural variation could affect the density and distribution of active sites, although specific advantages in terms of catalytic performance are not fully detailed in the current literature.

In the case of hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN), which has shown promising results in selective oxidation of propane to propylene with minimal CO2 formation, the high crystallinity of commercial h-BN leads to a limited number of active sites [10.1002/anie.202106713]. To address this limitation and improve catalytic performance, researchers have explored various surface modification techniques, such as plasma treatment.

Plasma treatment, particularly with N2, has been shown to create three-boron center (TBC) N-defects on the h-BN surface, which act as favorable sites for generating active species [10.1002/anie.202106713]. These defects play a crucial role in facilitating propane conversion, leading to remarkable catalytic performance (26.0% propane conversion with 89.4% selectivity toward olefins at 520°C) [10.1002/anie.202106713]. The positive correlation between reaction rates and the intensity of TBC N-defects suggests that aggregated boron sites are more favorable for becoming active sites, implying that creating a higher density of these defects on the h-BN surface can potentially improve catalytic performance [10.1002/anie.202106713].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for ODHP reactions represents a significant advancement in the field of heterogeneous catalysis. The ability to create a higher density of active sites across catalyst surfaces offers the potential for improved catalytic performance and efficiency. Future research directions may focus on optimizing the methods for creating these 2-D dispersed active sites, such as exploring novel doping techniques or developing new hosting materials that can support a higher concentration of active boron species.

Additionally, the shift in understanding from edge-site catalysis to surface-wide BOx active sites opens up new avenues for catalyst design. Future studies could investigate the relationship between the crystal structure of boron-based materials and the formation of BOx species, aiming to develop catalysts with optimal surface concentrations of active sites. The use of advanced characterization techniques to elucidate the exact nature and distribution of these active sites could provide valuable insights for rational catalyst design.

Furthermore, the success of plasma treatment in creating active defects on h-BN surfaces suggests that similar techniques could be applied to other boron-based materials. Future research could explore a wider range of plasma treatments or other surface modification methods to engineer catalysts with tailored active site distributions and enhanced catalytic performance. The development of scalable methods for creating these high-performance catalysts will be crucial for their potential industrial application in ODHP processes.

<Chinese>
在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应中，硼基催化剂中二维分散活性位点的开发因其独特的结构特征和提高催化性能的潜力而引起了研究人员的极大兴趣。这些活性位点可以通过两种主要方法开发：（1）将活性硼氧化物或硼羟基单元掺杂到各种宿主材料的表面上，以及（2）利用体相硼化物材料的暴露表面 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。

二维分散活性位点相对于线性分散边缘位点的一个主要优势是活性位点密度显著提高 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。这种增加的密度可能导致ODHP反应中催化活性和效率的提高。在催化剂表面创造更高浓度的活性位点的能力对提高催化性能有重要影响，因此引起了该领域研究人员的广泛兴趣 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。

最近的研究挑战了之前关于边缘位点是硼氮化物催化剂主要活性位点的假设。相反，研究表明在ODHP反应过程中，各种含硼材料表面形成了稳定的BOx活性位点 [10.1002/cctc.201701140]。这种从边缘位点催化到表面广泛BOx活性位点的认识转变为氧化脱氢反应领域的催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的可能性。

研究发现，硼基材料的晶体结构会影响BOx物种的形成，层状硼化物可能比四方硼化物呈现不同的硼表面浓度 [10.1002/cctc.201701140]。这种结构变化可能影响活性位点的密度和分布，尽管目前文献中没有详细说明具体的催化性能优势。

对于六方氮化硼（h-BN），它在丙烷选择性氧化为丙烯且CO2生成量极少方面显示了有希望的结果，但商业h-BN的高结晶度导致活性位点数量有限 [10.1002/anie.202106713]。为了解决这一限制并提高催化性能，研究人员探索了各种表面改性技术，如等离子体处理。

等离子体处理，特别是N2等离子体处理，已被证明可以在h-BN表面创造三硼中心（TBC）N缺陷，这些缺陷作为生成活性物种的有利位点 [10.1002/anie.202106713]。这些缺陷在促进丙烷转化方面起着关键作用，导致了显著的催化性能（在520°C时，丙烷转化率为26.0%，烯烃选择性为89.4%）[10.1002/anie.202106713]。反应速率与TBC N缺陷强度之间的正相关关系表明，聚集的硼位点更有利于成为活性位点，这意味着在h-BN表面创造更高密度的这些缺陷可能会提高催化性能 [10.1002/anie.202106713]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：在ODHP反应中开发硼基催化剂的二维分散活性位点代表了非均相催化领域的重大进展。在催化剂表面创造更高密度的活性位点的能力为提高催化性能和效率提供了潜力。未来的研究方向可能集中在优化创建这些二维分散活性位点的方法上，例如探索新的掺杂技术或开发可以支持更高浓度活性硼物种的新型宿主材料。

此外，从边缘位点催化到表面广泛BOx活性位点的认识转变为催化剂设计开辟了新的途径。未来的研究可以调查硼基材料的晶体结构与BOx物种形成之间的关系，旨在开发具有最佳表面活性位点浓度的催化剂。使用先进的表征技术来阐明这些活性位点的确切性质和分布可以为理性催化剂设计提供有价值的见解。

此外，等离子体处理在h-BN表面创造活性缺陷的成功表明，类似的技术可以应用于其他硼基材料。未来的研究可以探索更广泛的等离子体处理或其他表面改性方法，以设计具有定制活性位点分布和增强催化性能的催化剂。开发这些高性能催化剂的可扩展方法对于它们在ODHP过程中的潜在工业应用至关重要。
