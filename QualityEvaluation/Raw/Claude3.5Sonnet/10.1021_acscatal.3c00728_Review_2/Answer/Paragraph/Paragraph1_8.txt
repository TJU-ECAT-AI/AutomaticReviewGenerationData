The development of 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for the Oxidative Dehydrogenation of Propane (ODHP) reaction has emerged as a promising approach to enhance catalytic performance. These active sites can be developed through two primary methods: (1) doping active boron oxide or boron hydroxyl units onto the surface of various hosting materials, and (2) utilizing the exposed surface of bulk boride materials [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]. This approach offers significant advantages over linearly dispersed edge sites, particularly in terms of active site density, which can be substantially improved [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2].

The increased density of active sites in 2-D dispersed configurations potentially leads to enhanced catalytic performance. The unique structural features of these 2-D active sites, including their improved density and distribution across surfaces, have attracted considerable research interest in the field of ODHP reactions [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2].

Recent studies have challenged previous assumptions about edge sites being the primary active sites for boron nitride catalysts. Instead, research suggests that surface-stabilized BOx active sites form on various boron-containing materials during the ODHP reaction [10.1002/cctc.201701140]. This shift in understanding from edge-site catalysis to surface-wide BOx active sites has opened up new possibilities for catalyst design and optimization in the field of oxidative dehydrogenation reactions.

The crystal structure of boron-based materials may influence the formation of BOx species, with layered borides potentially presenting different boron surface concentrations compared to tetragonal borides [10.1002/cctc.201701140]. This structural difference could affect active site density and, consequently, catalytic performance.

In parallel research, efforts have been made to increase the density of active sites on hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN) surfaces through plasma treatment, particularly using N2 plasma [10.1002/anie.202106713]. This approach creates three-boron center (TBC) N-defects on the h-BN surface, which act as favorable sites for generating active species. These defects play a crucial role in facilitating propane conversion, leading to remarkable catalytic performance (26.0% propane conversion with 89.4% selectivity toward olefins at 520°C) [10.1002/anie.202106713].

The research shows a positive correlation between reaction rates and the intensity of TBC N-defects, suggesting that aggregated boron sites are more favorable for becoming active sites [10.1002/anie.202106713]. This implies that creating a higher density of these defects on the h-BN surface can potentially improve catalytic performance, aligning with the concept of 2-D dispersed active sites.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for ODHP reactions represents a significant advancement in the field of heterogeneous catalysis. The shift from edge-site catalysis to surface-wide active sites opens up new avenues for catalyst design and optimization. Future research efforts may focus on:

1. Developing novel methods to create and control the density of 2-D dispersed active sites on various boron-based materials.
2. Investigating the synergistic effects between the boron active sites and the hosting materials to further enhance catalytic performance.
3. Exploring the potential of combining plasma treatment techniques with 2-D dispersed active site development to create highly efficient ODHP catalysts.
4. Conducting in-depth mechanistic studies to better understand the formation and behavior of BOx species under reaction conditions, which could lead to more rational catalyst design.
5. Extending the concept of 2-D dispersed active sites to other catalytic systems beyond ODHP, potentially revolutionizing various industrial processes.

These research directions could lead to the development of more efficient and selective catalysts for ODHP and other related reactions, ultimately contributing to more sustainable chemical production processes.

<Chinese>
在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应中，开发基于硼的催化剂中的二维分散活性位点已成为提高催化性能的一种有前景的方法。这些活性位点可以通过两种主要方法开发：（1）在各种宿主材料的表面掺杂活性硼氧化物或硼羟基单元，以及（2）利用体相硼化物材料的暴露表面[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。与线性分散的边缘位点相比，这种方法在活性位点密度方面具有显著优势，可以大幅提高[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。

二维分散构型中活性位点密度的增加可能导致催化性能的提升。这些二维活性位点的独特结构特征，包括它们改善的密度和在表面上的分布，在ODHP反应领域引起了相当大的研究兴趣[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。

最近的研究对边缘位点是氮化硼催化剂主要活性位点的先前假设提出了挑战。相反，研究表明在ODHP反应过程中，各种含硼材料上形成了表面稳定的BOx活性位点[10.1002/cctc.201701140]。这种从边缘位点催化到表面广泛BOx活性位点的理解转变为氧化脱氢反应领域的催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的可能性。

硼基材料的晶体结构可能会影响BOx物种的形成，层状硼化物可能比四方硼化物呈现不同的硼表面浓度[10.1002/cctc.201701140]。这种结构差异可能影响活性位点密度，进而影响催化性能。

在平行研究中，通过等离子体处理（特别是使用N2等离子体）来增加六方氮化硼（h-BN）表面上活性位点的密度[10.1002/anie.202106713]。这种方法在h-BN表面创造了三硼中心（TBC）N缺陷，这些缺陷作为生成活性物种的有利位点。这些缺陷在促进丙烷转化方面起着关键作用，导致了显著的催化性能（在520°C下，丙烷转化率为26.0%，烯烃选择性为89.4%）[10.1002/anie.202106713]。

研究表明反应速率与TBC N缺陷强度之间存在正相关，这表明聚集的硼位点更有利于成为活性位点[10.1002/anie.202106713]。这意味着在h-BN表面创造更高密度的这些缺陷可能会提高催化性能，这与二维分散活性位点的概念一致。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：在ODHP反应中开发基于硼的催化剂的二维分散活性位点代表了多相催化领域的重大进展。从边缘位点催化到表面广泛活性位点的转变为催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的途径。未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 开发新方法来创造和控制各种硼基材料上二维分散活性位点的密度。
2. 研究硼活性位点与宿主材料之间的协同效应，以进一步提高催化性能。
3. 探索结合等离子体处理技术与二维分散活性位点开发的潜力，以创造高效的ODHP催化剂。
4. 进行深入的机理研究，以更好地理解BOx物种在反应条件下的形成和行为，这可能导致更合理的催化剂设计。
5. 将二维分散活性位点的概念扩展到ODHP以外的其他催化系统，可能彻底改变各种工业过程。

这些研究方向可能导致ODHP和其他相关反应的更高效和选择性催化剂的开发，最终为更可持续的化学生产过程做出贡献。
