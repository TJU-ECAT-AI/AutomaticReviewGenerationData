<English>
The development of 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for the Oxidative Dehydrogenation of Propane (ODHP) reaction has garnered significant attention in recent years due to their unique structural features and potential advantages over traditional linearly dispersed edge sites. These 2-D active sites can be created through two primary methods: (1) doping active boron oxide or boron hydroxyl units onto the surface of various hosting materials, and (2) utilizing the exposed surface of bulk boride materials [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2].

One of the key advantages of 2-D dispersed active sites is the substantial improvement in active site density compared to linearly dispersed edge sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]. This increased density of active sites can potentially lead to enhanced catalytic performance, making these materials particularly attractive for ODHP reactions.

Recent studies have challenged previous assumptions about the role of edge sites in boron-based catalysts. Instead of edge sites being the primary active sites, research now suggests that surface-stabilized BOx active sites form on various boron-containing materials during the ODHP reaction [10.1002/cctc.201701140]. This shift in understanding has opened up new possibilities for catalyst design and optimization in the field of oxidative dehydrogenation reactions.

The crystal structure of boron-containing materials has been found to influence the formation of BOx species. Layered borides (e.g., NiB, TiB2, hBN) may present different boron surface concentrations compared to tetragonal borides (e.g., WB, Co2B), potentially affecting the formation of BOx species during ODH [10.1002/cctc.201701140]. This structural diversity offers opportunities for tailoring catalysts with optimized active site densities and distributions.

In the case of hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN), which has shown promising results in selectively oxidizing propane to propylene with minimal CO2 formation, the high crystallinity of commercial h-BN leads to a limited number of active sites [10.1002/anie.202106713]. To address this limitation and improve catalytic performance, researchers have explored various surface modification techniques, such as plasma treatment.

Plasma treatment, particularly with N2, has been shown to create three-boron center (TBC) N-defects on the h-BN surface, which act as favorable sites for generating active species [10.1002/anie.202106713]. These defects play a crucial role in facilitating propane conversion, leading to remarkable catalytic performance (26.0% propane conversion with 89.4% selectivity toward olefins at 520°C) [10.1002/anie.202106713].

The research has demonstrated a positive correlation between reaction rates and the intensity of TBC N-defects, suggesting that aggregated boron sites are more favorable for becoming active sites [10.1002/anie.202106713]. This finding implies that creating a higher density of these defects on the h-BN surface can potentially improve catalytic performance, aligning with the advantages observed in 2-D dispersed active sites.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for ODHP reactions represents a significant advancement in the field of heterogeneous catalysis. The shift from edge-site catalysis to surface-wide BOx active sites has opened up new avenues for catalyst design and optimization. Future research efforts may focus on:

1. Exploring novel methods to create and control the density of 2-D dispersed active sites on various boron-containing materials.
2. Investigating the synergistic effects between the boron active sites and different hosting materials to further enhance catalytic performance.
3. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the formation and evolution of BOx species under reaction conditions.
4. Optimizing plasma treatment parameters and exploring other surface modification techniques to create tailored defect structures in boron-based catalysts.
5. Scaling up the production of high-performance boron-based catalysts with 2-D dispersed active sites for potential industrial applications in ODHP processes.

These research directions could lead to the development of more efficient and selective catalysts for ODHP reactions, potentially revolutionizing the production of valuable olefins from alkanes.

<Chinese>
在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应中，硼基催化剂中二维分散活性位点的开发近年来因其独特的结构特征和相对于传统线性分散边缘位点的潜在优势而引起了广泛关注。这些二维活性位点可以通过两种主要方法创建：（1）在各种宿主材料表面掺杂活性硼氧化物或硼羟基单元，以及（2）利用体相硼化物材料的暴露表面[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。

二维分散活性位点的一个关键优势是与线性分散边缘位点相比，活性位点密度显著提高[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。这种增加的活性位点密度可能导致催化性能的提升，使这些材料在ODHP反应中特别具有吸引力。

最近的研究对硼基催化剂中边缘位点作用的先前假设提出了质疑。研究现在表明，在ODHP反应过程中，各种含硼材料上形成了表面稳定的BOx活性位点，而不是边缘位点作为主要活性位点[10.1002/cctc.201701140]。这种理解的转变为氧化脱氢反应领域的催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的可能性。

研究发现，含硼材料的晶体结构会影响BOx物种的形成。层状硼化物（如NiB、TiB2、hBN）可能比四方硼化物（如WB、Co2B）呈现不同的硼表面浓度，这可能影响ODH过程中BOx物种的形成[10.1002/cctc.201701140]。这种结构多样性为定制具有优化活性位点密度和分布的催化剂提供了机会。

在六方氮化硼（h-BN）的情况下，它在选择性氧化丙烷生成丙烯时几乎不产生CO2，显示出了令人鼓舞的结果。然而，商业h-BN的高结晶度导致活性位点数量有限[10.1002/anie.202106713]。为了解决这一限制并提高催化性能，研究人员探索了各种表面改性技术，如等离子体处理。

特别是使用N2的等离子体处理已被证明可以在h-BN表面创建三硼中心（TBC）N缺陷，这些缺陷作为生成活性物种的有利位点[10.1002/anie.202106713]。这些缺陷在促进丙烷转化中起着关键作用，导致了显著的催化性能（在520°C下，丙烷转化率为26.0%，烯烃选择性为89.4%）[10.1002/anie.202106713]。

研究表明，反应速率与TBC N缺陷强度之间存在正相关关系，这表明聚集的硼位点更有利于成为活性位点[10.1002/anie.202106713]。这一发现意味着在h-BN表面创建更高密度的这些缺陷可能会提高催化性能，这与二维分散活性位点观察到的优势相一致。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：在ODHP反应中开发硼基催化剂的二维分散活性位点代表了多相催化领域的重大进展。从边缘位点催化到表面广泛BOx活性位点的转变为催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的途径。未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 探索在各种含硼材料上创建和控制二维分散活性位点密度的新方法。
2. 研究硼活性位点与不同宿主材料之间的协同效应，以进一步提高催化性能。
3. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解反应条件下BOx物种的形成和演变。
4. 优化等离子体处理参数并探索其他表面改性技术，以在硼基催化剂中创建定制的缺陷结构。
5. 扩大具有二维分散活性位点的高性能硼基催化剂的生产规模，以应用于ODHP过程的潜在工业应用。

这些研究方向可能导致ODHP反应更高效和选择性催化剂的开发，从而可能彻底改变从烷烃生产有价值烯烃的方式。
