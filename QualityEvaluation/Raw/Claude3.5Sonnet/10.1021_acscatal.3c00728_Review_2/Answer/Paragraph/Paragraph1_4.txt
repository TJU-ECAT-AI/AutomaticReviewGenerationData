<English>
The development of 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for the Oxidative Dehydrogenation of Propane (ODHP) reaction has attracted significant research interest due to their unique structural features and potential for improved catalytic performance. These active sites can be developed through two primary methods: (1) doping active boron oxide or boron hydroxyl units onto the surface of various hosting materials, and (2) utilizing the exposed surface of bulk boride materials [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2].

Compared to linearly dispersed edge sites, 2-D dispersed active sites offer a substantial advantage in terms of active site density. This increased density can potentially lead to enhanced catalytic performance, making these materials particularly attractive for ODHP reactions [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2].

Recent studies have challenged previous assumptions about edge sites being the primary active sites for boron nitride catalysts. Instead, research suggests that surface-stabilized BOx active sites form on various boron-containing materials during the ODHP reaction. This observation has led to a paradigm shift in understanding the catalytic mechanism, from edge-site catalysis to surface-wide BOx active sites in boron-based ODHP catalysts [10.1002/cctc.201701140].

The crystal structure of boron-containing materials has been found to influence the formation of BOx species. Layered borides (e.g., NiB, TiB2, hBN) may present different boron surface concentrations compared to tetragonal borides (e.g., WB, Co2B), potentially affecting the formation of BOx species during ODH [10.1002/cctc.201701140].

One notable breakthrough in this field was the discovery that hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN) can selectively oxidize propane to propylene with minimal CO2 formation. However, the high crystallinity of commercial h-BN leads to a limited number of active sites, necessitating methods to increase their density and improve catalytic performance [10.1002/anie.202106713].

To address this limitation, researchers have explored various surface modification techniques, such as plasma treatment. N2 plasma treatment, in particular, has shown promising results in creating three-boron center (TBC) N-defects on the h-BN surface. These defects act as favorable sites for generating active species and play a crucial role in facilitating propane conversion. For instance, N2-treated BN exhibited remarkable activity, achieving 26.0% propane conversion with 89.4% selectivity toward olefins at 520°C [10.1002/anie.202106713].

The research has also revealed a positive correlation between reaction rates and the intensity of g1 defects (TBC N-defects), suggesting that aggregated boron sites are more favorable for becoming active sites. This finding implies that creating a higher density of these defects on the h-BN surface can potentially improve catalytic performance [10.1002/anie.202106713].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for ODHP reactions represents a significant advancement in the field of heterogeneous catalysis. The shift from edge-site catalysis to surface-wide BOx active sites opens up new possibilities for catalyst design and optimization.

Future research efforts may focus on:

1. Developing novel methods to further increase the density of active sites on boron-based materials, potentially through advanced doping techniques or innovative surface modification approaches.

2. Investigating the relationship between crystal structure and BOx species formation to optimize catalyst performance for specific ODHP reactions.

3. Exploring the potential of hybrid materials that combine the advantages of 2-D dispersed active sites with other catalytic systems to achieve even higher selectivity and conversion rates.

4. Utilizing advanced characterization techniques to gain deeper insights into the formation and evolution of active sites under reaction conditions, enabling more precise control over catalyst properties.

5. Scaling up the production of high-performance boron-based catalysts with 2-D dispersed active sites for industrial applications, addressing challenges related to stability, regeneration, and cost-effectiveness.

These research directions could potentially lead to the development of more efficient and sustainable processes for the production of valuable olefins from alkanes, contributing to the advancement of the chemical industry and reducing its environmental impact.

在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应中，硼基催化剂中二维分散活性位点的开发因其独特的结构特征和提高催化性能的潜力而引起了研究人员的极大兴趣。这些活性位点可以通过两种主要方法开发：（1）将活性硼氧化物或硼羟基单元掺杂到各种宿主材料的表面上，以及（2）利用体相硼化物材料的暴露表面 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。

与线性分散的边缘位点相比，二维分散活性位点在活性位点密度方面具有显著优势。这种密度的增加可能导致催化性能的提高，使这些材料在ODHP反应中特别具有吸引力 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。

最近的研究挑战了之前关于边缘位点是硼氮化物催化剂主要活性位点的假设。相反，研究表明，在ODHP反应过程中，各种含硼材料上形成了表面稳定的BOx活性位点。这一观察结果导致了对催化机制理解的范式转变，从边缘位点催化转向硼基ODHP催化剂的表面全域BOx活性位点 [10.1002/cctc.201701140]。

研究发现，含硼材料的晶体结构会影响BOx物种的形成。层状硼化物（如NiB、TiB2、hBN）可能呈现出与四方硼化物（如WB、Co2B）不同的硼表面浓度，这可能会影响ODH过程中BOx物种的形成 [10.1002/cctc.201701140]。

该领域的一个显著突破是发现六方氮化硼（h-BN）可以选择性地将丙烷氧化为丙烯，同时仅产生少量的CO2。然而，商业h-BN的高结晶度导致活性位点数量有限，需要采取方法来增加其密度并提高催化性能 [10.1002/anie.202106713]。

为了解决这一限制，研究人员探索了各种表面改性技术，如等离子体处理。特别是N2等离子体处理在h-BN表面创建三硼中心（TBC）N缺陷方面显示出了promising的结果。这些缺陷充当了生成活性物种的有利位点，在促进丙烷转化中起着关键作用。例如，经N2处理的BN表现出显著的活性，在520°C下实现了26.0%的丙烷转化率，对烯烃的选择性为89.4% [10.1002/anie.202106713]。

研究还揭示了反应速率与g1缺陷（TBC N缺陷）强度之间的正相关关系，表明聚集的硼位点更有利于成为活性位点。这一发现意味着在h-BN表面创造更高密度的这些缺陷可能会提高催化性能 [10.1002/anie.202106713]。

综合理解与未来展望：在ODHP反应中开发硼基催化剂的二维分散活性位点代表了多相催化领域的重大进展。从边缘位点催化到表面全域BOx活性位点的转变为催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的可能性。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 开发新方法以进一步增加硼基材料上活性位点的密度，可能通过先进的掺杂技术或创新的表面改性方法。

2. 研究晶体结构与BOx物种形成之间的关系，以优化特定ODHP反应的催化剂性能。

3. 探索结合二维分散活性位点优势与其他催化系统的混合材料的潜力，以实现更高的选择性和转化率。

4. 利用先进的表征技术深入了解反应条件下活性位点的形成和演变，从而更精确地控制催化剂性能。

5. 扩大具有二维分散活性位点的高性能硼基催化剂的产业化生产，解决与稳定性、再生和成本效益相关的挑战。

这些研究方向可能会导致开发出更高效、更可持续的烷烃生产有价值烯烃的工艺，为化学工业的进步做出贡献，并减少其环境影响。
