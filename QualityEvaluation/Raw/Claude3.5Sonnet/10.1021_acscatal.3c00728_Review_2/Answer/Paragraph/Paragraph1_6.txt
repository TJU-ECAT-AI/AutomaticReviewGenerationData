<English>
The development of 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for the Oxidative Dehydrogenation of Propane (ODHP) reaction has attracted significant research interest due to their unique structural features and potential for improved catalytic performance. These active sites can be developed through two primary methods: (1) doping active boron oxide or boron hydroxyl units onto the surface of various hosting materials, and (2) utilizing the exposed surface of bulk boride materials [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]. 

Compared to linearly dispersed edge sites, 2-D dispersed active sites offer a substantial advantage in terms of active site density. This increased density can potentially lead to enhanced catalytic performance, making these materials particularly attractive for ODHP reactions [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]. The improved density and distribution of active sites across surfaces have sparked considerable research interest in the field.

Recent studies have challenged previous assumptions about edge sites being the primary active sites for boron nitride catalysts. Instead, research suggests that surface-stabilized BOx active sites form on various boron-containing materials during the ODHP reaction [10.1002/cctc.201701140]. This shift in understanding from edge-site catalysis to surface-wide BOx active sites has opened up new possibilities for catalyst design and optimization in the field of oxidative dehydrogenation reactions.

The crystal structure of boron-based materials may influence the formation of BOx species, with layered borides potentially presenting different boron surface concentrations compared to tetragonal borides [10.1002/cctc.201701140]. This structural diversity could affect active site density and distribution, although specific advantages in terms of catalytic performance are not yet fully elucidated.

In the case of hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN), which has shown promise in selectively oxidizing propane to propylene with minimal CO2 formation, the high crystallinity of commercial h-BN leads to a limited number of active sites [10.1002/anie.202106713]. To address this limitation and improve catalytic performance, researchers have explored various surface modification techniques, such as plasma treatment.

Plasma treatment, particularly with N2, has been shown to create three-boron center (TBC) N-defects on the h-BN surface, which act as favorable sites for generating active species [10.1002/anie.202106713]. These defects play a crucial role in facilitating propane conversion, leading to remarkable catalytic performance (26.0% propane conversion with 89.4% selectivity toward olefins at 520°C). The positive correlation between reaction rates and the intensity of TBC N-defects suggests that aggregated boron sites are more favorable for becoming active sites, implying that creating a higher density of these defects on the h-BN surface can potentially improve catalytic performance.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for ODHP reactions represents a significant advancement in the field of heterogeneous catalysis. The ability to create high-density active sites across surfaces, rather than relying solely on edge sites, opens up new avenues for catalyst design and optimization. Future research efforts may focus on:

1. Developing novel methods for creating and controlling the distribution of 2-D dispersed active sites on various boron-based materials.
2. Investigating the synergistic effects between the boron active sites and the hosting materials to further enhance catalytic performance.
3. Exploring the potential of 2-D dispersed active sites in other catalytic reactions beyond ODHP, such as other dehydrogenation or oxidation reactions.
4. Utilizing advanced characterization techniques to gain deeper insights into the nature and behavior of these active sites under reaction conditions.
5. Developing structure-activity relationships to guide the rational design of next-generation boron-based catalysts with optimized 2-D dispersed active sites.

These research directions could lead to the development of highly efficient and selective catalysts for ODHP and related reactions, potentially revolutionizing the production of valuable chemical intermediates in the petrochemical industry.

<Chinese>
在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应中，基于硼的催化剂中二维分散活性位点的开发因其独特的结构特征和提高催化性能的潜力而引起了广泛的研究兴趣。这些活性位点可以通过两种主要方法开发：（1）在各种宿主材料表面掺杂活性硼氧化物或硼羟基单元，以及（2）利用体相硼化物材料的暴露表面 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。

与线性分散的边缘位点相比，二维分散活性位点在活性位点密度方面具有显著优势。这种增加的密度可能导致催化性能的提高，使这些材料在ODHP反应中特别具有吸引力 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。活性位点在表面上改进的密度和分布引起了该领域相当大的研究兴趣。

最近的研究挑战了之前关于边缘位点是氮化硼催化剂主要活性位点的假设。相反，研究表明，在ODHP反应过程中，各种含硼材料上形成了表面稳定的BOx活性位点 [10.1002/cctc.201701140]。这种从边缘位点催化到表面宽泛BOx活性位点的理解转变为氧化脱氢反应领域的催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的可能性。

基于硼的材料的晶体结构可能影响BOx物种的形成，层状硼化物可能比四方硼化物呈现不同的硼表面浓度 [10.1002/cctc.201701140]。这种结构多样性可能影响活性位点的密度和分布，尽管在催化性能方面的具体优势尚未完全阐明。

对于六方氮化硼（h-BN），它在选择性氧化丙烷生成丙烯同时最小化CO2生成方面显示出潜力，商业h-BN的高结晶度导致活性位点数量有限 [10.1002/anie.202106713]。为了解决这一限制并提高催化性能，研究人员探索了各种表面改性技术，如等离子体处理。

特别是使用N2的等离子体处理已被证明可在h-BN表面创建三硼中心（TBC）N缺陷，这些缺陷作为产生活性物种的有利位点 [10.1002/anie.202106713]。这些缺陷在促进丙烷转化中起着至关重要的作用，导致了显著的催化性能（在520°C下，丙烷转化率为26.0%，烯烃选择性为89.4%）。反应速率与TBC N缺陷强度之间的正相关关系表明，聚集的硼位点更有利于成为活性位点，这意味着在h-BN表面创建更高密度的这些缺陷可能会提高催化性能。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：在ODHP反应中开发基于硼的催化剂的二维分散活性位点代表了多相催化领域的重大进展。能够在表面上创建高密度活性位点，而不仅仅依赖于边缘位点，为催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的途径。未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 开发新方法来创建和控制各种基于硼的材料上二维分散活性位点的分布。
2. 研究硼活性位点与宿主材料之间的协同效应，以进一步提高催化性能。
3. 探索二维分散活性位点在ODHP以外的其他催化反应中的潜力，如其他脱氢或氧化反应。
4. 利用先进的表征技术深入了解这些活性位点在反应条件下的性质和行为。
5. 建立结构-活性关系，指导下一代具有优化二维分散活性位点的基于硼的催化剂的理性设计。

这些研究方向可能导致ODHP和相关反应的高效和选择性催化剂的开发，潜在地革新了石化工业中有价值化学中间体的生产。
