<English>
The development of 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for the Oxidative Dehydrogenation of Propane (ODHP) reaction has garnered significant attention in recent years due to their unique structural features and potential for enhanced catalytic performance. These active sites can be developed through two primary methods: (1) doping active boron oxide or boron hydroxyl units onto the surface of various hosting materials, and (2) utilizing the exposed surface of bulk boride materials [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]. This approach offers several advantages over traditional linearly dispersed edge sites, particularly in terms of active site density.

One of the key advantages of 2-D dispersed active sites is the significant improvement in active site density compared to linearly dispersed edge sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]. This increased density of active sites can potentially lead to enhanced catalytic performance, making these materials particularly attractive for ODHP reactions. The unique structural features of these 2-D active sites, including their improved density and distribution across surfaces, have attracted considerable research interest in the field [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2].

Recent studies have challenged previous assumptions about edge sites being the primary active sites for boron nitride catalysts in ODHP reactions. Instead, research suggests that surface-stabilized BOx active sites form on various boron-containing materials during the ODHP reaction [10.1002/cctc.201701140]. This discovery has led to a paradigm shift in understanding the catalytic mechanism, moving from edge-site catalysis to surface-wide BOx active sites in boron-based ODHP catalysts.

The crystal structure of boron-containing materials has been found to influence the formation of BOx species. Layered borides (e.g., NiB, TiB2, hBN) may present different boron surface concentrations compared to tetragonal borides (e.g., WB, Co2B), potentially affecting the formation of BOx species during ODH [10.1002/cctc.201701140]. This observation opens up new possibilities for catalyst design and optimization in the field of oxidative dehydrogenation reactions.

In the case of hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN), which has shown promising results in selectively oxidizing propane to propylene with minimal CO2 formation, the high crystallinity of commercial h-BN leads to a limited number of active sites [10.1002/anie.202106713]. To address this limitation and improve catalytic performance, researchers have explored various surface modification techniques, such as plasma treatment.

Plasma treatment, particularly with N2, has been shown to create three-boron center (TBC) N-defects on the h-BN surface, which act as favorable sites for generating active species [10.1002/anie.202106713]. These defects play a crucial role in facilitating propane conversion, leading to remarkable catalytic performance (26.0% propane conversion with 89.4% selectivity toward olefins at 520°C) [10.1002/anie.202106713]. The positive correlation between reaction rates and the intensity of TBC N-defects suggests that aggregated boron sites are more favorable for becoming active sites, implying that creating a higher density of these defects on the h-BN surface can potentially improve catalytic performance.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for ODHP reactions represents a significant advancement in the field of heterogeneous catalysis. The shift from edge-site catalysis to surface-wide BOx active sites opens up new avenues for catalyst design and optimization. Future research efforts may focus on:

1. Developing novel methods to create and control the density of 2-D dispersed active sites on various boron-containing materials.
2. Investigating the influence of different crystal structures and surface modifications on the formation and stability of BOx species.
3. Exploring the potential of hybrid materials that combine the advantages of 2-D dispersed active sites with other catalytic components to further enhance performance.
4. Utilizing advanced characterization techniques to gain deeper insights into the formation mechanism and nature of BOx active sites under reaction conditions.
5. Scaling up the production of optimized boron-based catalysts with 2-D dispersed active sites for industrial applications in ODHP and related reactions.

These research directions could lead to the development of more efficient and selective catalysts for ODHP reactions, potentially revolutionizing the production of valuable olefins from alkanes.

<Chinese>
在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应中，含硼催化剂上二维分散活性位点的开发近年来引起了广泛关注，这是由于其独特的结构特征和潜在的增强催化性能。这些活性位点可以通过两种主要方法开发：（1）在各种宿主材料表面掺杂活性硼氧化物或硼羟基单元，以及（2）利用大块硼化物材料的暴露表面[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。与传统的线性分散边缘位点相比，这种方法在活性位点密度方面具有几个优势。

二维分散活性位点的一个关键优势是与线性分散边缘位点相比，活性位点密度显著提高[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。这种增加的活性位点密度可能导致催化性能的提高，使这些材料在ODHP反应中特别具有吸引力。这些二维活性位点的独特结构特征，包括其改进的密度和在表面上的分布，在该领域引起了相当大的研究兴趣[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。

最近的研究挑战了先前关于边缘位点是ODHP反应中氮化硼催化剂主要活性位点的假设。相反，研究表明，在ODHP反应过程中，各种含硼材料上形成了表面稳定的BOx活性位点[10.1002/cctc.201701140]。这一发现导致了对催化机制理解的范式转变，从边缘位点催化转向含硼ODHP催化剂中的表面广泛BOx活性位点。

研究发现，含硼材料的晶体结构会影响BOx物种的形成。层状硼化物（如NiB、TiB2、hBN）可能呈现出与四方硼化物（如WB、Co2B）不同的硼表面浓度，这可能影响ODH过程中BOx物种的形成[10.1002/cctc.201701140]。这一观察为氧化脱氢反应领域的催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的可能性。

在六方氮化硼（h-BN）的情况下，它在选择性氧化丙烷生成丙烯并最小化CO2生成方面显示出有希望的结果，但商用h-BN的高结晶度导致活性位点数量有限[10.1002/anie.202106713]。为了解决这一限制并提高催化性能，研究人员探索了各种表面改性技术，如等离子体处理。

等离子体处理，特别是N2等离子体处理，已被证明可以在h-BN表面创建三硼中心（TBC）N缺陷，这些缺陷作为生成活性物种的有利位点[10.1002/anie.202106713]。这些缺陷在促进丙烷转化中起着关键作用，导致了显著的催化性能（在520°C下，丙烷转化率为26.0%，烯烃选择性为89.4%）[10.1002/anie.202106713]。反应速率与TBC N缺陷强度之间的正相关性表明，聚集的硼位点更有利于成为活性位点，这意味着在h-BN表面创建更高密度的这些缺陷可能会提高催化性能。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：在ODHP反应中开发含硼催化剂上的二维分散活性位点代表了非均相催化领域的重大进步。从边缘位点催化到表面广泛BOx活性位点的转变为催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的途径。未来的研究工作可能集中在：

1. 开发新方法来创建和控制各种含硼材料上二维分散活性位点的密度。
2. 研究不同晶体结构和表面改性对BOx物种形成和稳定性的影响。
3. 探索将二维分散活性位点的优势与其他催化组分相结合的混合材料的潜力，以进一步提高性能。
4. 利用先进的表征技术，深入了解反应条件下BOx活性位点的形成机制和性质。
5. 扩大具有二维分散活性位点的优化硼基催化剂的生产规模，用于ODHP和相关反应的工业应用。

这些研究方向可能导致ODHP反应更高效和选择性催化剂的开发，有可能革新从烷烃生产有价值烯烃的过程。
