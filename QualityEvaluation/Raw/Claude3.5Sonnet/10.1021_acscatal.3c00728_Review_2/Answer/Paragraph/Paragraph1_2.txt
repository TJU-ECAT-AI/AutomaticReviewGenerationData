<English>
The development of 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for the Oxidative Dehydrogenation of Propane (ODHP) reaction has garnered significant attention in recent years due to their unique structural features and improved catalytic performance. These 2-D dispersed active sites can be developed through two main methods: (1) doping active boron oxide or boron hydroxyl units onto the surface of various hosting materials, and (2) utilizing the exposed surface of bulk boride materials [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]. This approach offers several advantages over traditional linearly dispersed edge sites, particularly in terms of active site density and catalytic performance.

One of the primary advantages of 2-D dispersed active sites is the significant improvement in active site density compared to linearly dispersed edge sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]. This increased density of active sites can potentially lead to enhanced catalytic performance, as more sites are available for the ODHP reaction to occur. The unique structural features of these 2-D active sites, including their improved density and distribution across surfaces, have attracted considerable research interest in the field of ODHP reactions [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2].

Recent studies have challenged previous assumptions about edge sites being the primary active sites for boron nitride catalysts. Instead, research suggests that surface-stabilized BOx active sites form on various boron-containing materials during the ODHP reaction [10.1002/cctc.201701140]. This shift in understanding from edge-site catalysis to surface-wide BOx active sites has opened up new possibilities for catalyst design and optimization in the field of oxidative dehydrogenation reactions.

The crystal structure of boron-based materials has been found to influence the formation of BOx species, with layered borides potentially presenting different boron surface concentrations compared to tetragonal borides [10.1002/cctc.201701140]. This structural difference could affect the density and distribution of active sites, although specific advantages in terms of catalytic performance are not yet fully elucidated.

In the case of hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN), which has shown promise in selectively oxidizing propane to propylene with minimal CO2 formation, the high crystallinity of commercial h-BN leads to a limited amount of active sites [10.1002/anie.202106713]. To address this limitation and improve catalytic performance, researchers have explored various surface modification techniques, such as plasma treatment.

Plasma treatment, particularly with N2, has been shown to create three-boron center (TBC) N-defects on the h-BN surface, which act as favorable sites for generating active species [10.1002/anie.202106713]. These defects play a crucial role in facilitating propane conversion, leading to remarkable catalytic performance (26.0% propane conversion with 89.4% selectivity toward olefins at 520°C) [10.1002/anie.202106713]. The positive correlation between reaction rates and the intensity of TBC N-defects suggests that aggregated boron sites are more favorable for becoming active sites, implying that creating a higher density of these defects on the h-BN surface can potentially improve catalytic performance [10.1002/anie.202106713].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for ODHP reactions represents a significant advancement in the field of heterogeneous catalysis. The shift from edge-site catalysis to surface-wide active sites, coupled with the ability to modify surface properties through techniques such as plasma treatment, opens up new avenues for catalyst design and optimization.

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Exploring novel methods to further increase the density of 2-D dispersed active sites on various boron-based materials, potentially leading to even higher catalytic performance.

2. Investigating the synergistic effects between different types of surface defects and their impact on catalytic activity and selectivity.

3. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the formation and evolution of BOx species under reaction conditions, which could provide valuable insights for rational catalyst design.

4. Exploring the potential of 2-D dispersed active sites in other related reactions, such as the oxidative dehydrogenation of other light alkanes or the direct conversion of methane to higher hydrocarbons.

5. Investigating the long-term stability and regeneration strategies for these 2-D dispersed active site catalysts under industrial conditions.

By addressing these research areas, scientists and engineers may be able to develop more efficient and selective catalysts for ODHP and related reactions, potentially leading to more sustainable and economically viable processes for olefin production.

<Chinese>
在丙烷氧化脱氢(ODHP)反应中，基于硼的催化剂中2-D分散活性位点的发展近年来引起了广泛关注，这是由于其独特的结构特征和改进的催化性能。这些2-D分散活性位点可以通过两种主要方法开发：(1)将活性硼氧化物或硼羟基单元掺杂到各种宿主材料的表面上，(2)利用大体积硼化物材料的暴露表面[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。与传统的线性分散边缘位点相比，这种方法在活性位点密度和催化性能方面具有多项优势。

2-D分散活性位点的主要优势之一是与线性分散边缘位点相比，活性位点密度显著提高[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。这种增加的活性位点密度可能导致催化性能的提高，因为有更多的位点可用于ODHP反应。这些2-D活性位点的独特结构特征，包括其改进的密度和在表面上的分布，在ODHP反应领域引起了相当大的研究兴趣[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。

最近的研究挑战了之前关于边缘位点是氮化硼催化剂主要活性位点的假设。相反，研究表明，在ODHP反应过程中，各种含硼材料上形成了表面稳定的BOx活性位点[10.1002/cctc.201701140]。这种从边缘位点催化到表面广泛BOx活性位点的认识转变，为氧化脱氢反应领域的催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的可能性。

研究发现，硼基材料的晶体结构会影响BOx物种的形成，层状硼化物可能比四方硼化物呈现不同的硼表面浓度[10.1002/cctc.201701140]。这种结构差异可能影响活性位点的密度和分布，尽管在催化性能方面的具体优势尚未完全阐明。

对于六方氮化硼(h-BN)，它在将丙烷选择性氧化为丙烯的同时产生极少量的CO2，显示出很大的潜力，但商业h-BN的高结晶度导致活性位点数量有限[10.1002/anie.202106713]。为了解决这个限制并提高催化性能，研究人员探索了各种表面改性技术，如等离子体处理。

等离子体处理，特别是N2处理，已被证明可在h-BN表面创建三硼中心(TBC)N缺陷，这些缺陷作为生成活性物种的有利位点[10.1002/anie.202106713]。这些缺陷在促进丙烷转化方面起着关键作用，导致了显著的催化性能（在520°C下，丙烷转化率为26.0%，烯烃选择性为89.4%）[10.1002/anie.202106713]。反应速率与TBC N缺陷强度之间的正相关性表明，聚集的硼位点更有利于成为活性位点，这意味着在h-BN表面创建更高密度的这些缺陷可能会提高催化性能[10.1002/anie.202106713]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：在ODHP反应中，基于硼的催化剂中2-D分散活性位点的开发代表了多相催化领域的重大进展。从边缘位点催化到表面广泛活性位点的转变，加上通过等离子体处理等技术修改表面性质的能力，为催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的途径。

未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 探索新型方法，进一步增加各种硼基材料上2-D分散活性位点的密度，可能导致更高的催化性能。

2. 研究不同类型表面缺陷之间的协同效应及其对催化活性和选择性的影响。

3. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解BOx物种在反应条件下的形成和演变，这可能为理性催化剂设计提供宝贵的见解。

4. 探索2-D分散活性位点在其他相关反应中的潜力，如其他轻烷烃的氧化脱氢或甲烷直接转化为高级碳氢化合物。

5. 研究这些2-D分散活性位点催化剂在工业条件下的长期稳定性和再生策略。

通过解决这些研究领域，科学家和工程师可能能够开发出更高效、更选择性的ODHP和相关反应催化剂，可能导致更可持续和经济可行的烯烃生产过程。
