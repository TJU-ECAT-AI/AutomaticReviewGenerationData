<English>
The development of 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for the Oxidative Dehydrogenation of Propane (ODHP) reaction has attracted significant research interest due to their unique structural features and potential for improved catalytic performance. These active sites can be developed through two main methods: (1) doping active boron oxide or boron hydroxyl units onto the surface of various hosting materials, and (2) utilizing the exposed surface of bulk boride materials [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2].

Compared to linearly dispersed edge sites, 2-D dispersed active sites offer a significant advantage in terms of active site density. The density of active sites can be substantially improved, potentially leading to enhanced catalytic performance [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]. This increased density and distribution of active sites across surfaces have drawn extensive interest from researchers in the field of ODHP reactions.

Recent studies have challenged previous assumptions about edge sites being the primary active sites for boron nitride catalysts. Instead, research suggests that surface-stabilized BOx active sites form on various boron-containing materials during the ODHP reaction [10.1002/cctc.201701140]. This shift in understanding from edge-site catalysis to surface-wide BOx active sites has opened up new possibilities for catalyst design and optimization in the field of oxidative dehydrogenation reactions.

The crystal structure of boron-based materials may influence the formation of BOx species, with layered borides potentially presenting different boron surface concentrations compared to tetragonal borides [10.1002/cctc.201701140]. This variation in surface concentration could affect the density and distribution of active sites, although specific advantages in terms of catalytic performance are not yet fully elucidated.

Research on hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN) has shown promising results in selectively oxidizing propane to propylene with minimal CO2 formation [10.1002/anie.202106713]. However, the high crystallinity of commercial h-BN leads to a limited number of active sites, necessitating methods to increase their density and improve catalytic performance. Plasma treatment, particularly using N2 plasma, has been demonstrated as an effective method to create three-boron center (TBC) N-defects on the h-BN surface, which act as favorable sites for generating active species [10.1002/anie.202106713].

These N-defects play a crucial role in facilitating propane conversion, leading to remarkable catalytic performance (26.0% propane conversion with 89.4% selectivity toward olefins at 520°C) [10.1002/anie.202106713]. The research shows a positive correlation between reaction rates and the intensity of TBC N-defects, suggesting that aggregated boron sites are more favorable for becoming active sites. This implies that creating a higher density of these defects on the h-BN surface can potentially improve catalytic performance.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of 2-D dispersed active sites in boron-based catalysts for ODHP reactions represents a significant advancement in the field of heterogeneous catalysis. The shift from edge-site catalysis to surface-wide active sites opens up new avenues for catalyst design and optimization. Future research efforts may focus on:

1. Developing novel methods to create and control the density of 2-D dispersed active sites on various boron-based materials.
2. Investigating the relationship between the crystal structure of boron-based materials and the formation of BOx species to optimize catalytic performance.
3. Exploring the potential of plasma treatment and other surface modification techniques to create tailored defects and active sites on boron-based catalysts.
4. Conducting in-depth mechanistic studies to better understand the role of 2-D dispersed active sites in the ODHP reaction, potentially leading to the design of more efficient and selective catalysts.
5. Investigating the scalability and long-term stability of these 2-D dispersed active site catalysts for potential industrial applications.

These research directions could lead to the development of more efficient and selective catalysts for ODHP reactions, potentially revolutionizing the production of propylene and other valuable olefins in the chemical industry.

<Chinese>
在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应中，基于硼的催化剂中二维分散活性位点的开发因其独特的结构特征和提高催化性能的潜力而引起了广泛的研究兴趣。这些活性位点可以通过两种主要方法开发：（1）将活性硼氧化物或硼羟基单元掺杂到各种宿主材料的表面上，以及（2）利用块状硼化物材料的暴露表面[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。

与线性分散的边缘位点相比，二维分散活性位点在活性位点密度方面具有显著优势。活性位点的密度可以大大提高，可能导致催化性能的增强[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_2]。这种活性位点在表面上密度和分布的增加吸引了ODHP反应领域研究人员的广泛兴趣。

最近的研究挑战了之前关于边缘位点是硼氮化物催化剂主要活性位点的假设。相反，研究表明在ODHP反应过程中，各种含硼材料上形成了表面稳定的BOx活性位点[10.1002/cctc.201701140]。这种从边缘位点催化到表面宽BOx活性位点的理解转变为氧化脱氢反应领域的催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的可能性。

硼基材料的晶体结构可能会影响BOx物种的形成，层状硼化物可能呈现出与四方硼化物不同的硼表面浓度[10.1002/cctc.201701140]。这种表面浓度的变化可能会影响活性位点的密度和分布，尽管在催化性能方面的具体优势尚未完全阐明。

对六方氮化硼（h-BN）的研究显示了在将丙烷选择性氧化为丙烯的同时最小化CO2生成的有前景的结果[10.1002/anie.202106713]。然而，商业h-BN的高结晶度导致活性位点数量有限，需要方法来增加其密度并提高催化性能。等离子体处理，特别是使用N2等离子体，已被证明是在h-BN表面创建三硼中心（TBC）N缺陷的有效方法，这些缺陷作为生成活性物种的有利位点[10.1002/anie.202106713]。

这些N缺陷在促进丙烷转化中起着关键作用，导致了显著的催化性能（在520°C下，丙烷转化率为26.0%，烯烃选择性为89.4%）[10.1002/anie.202106713]。研究表明反应速率与TBC N缺陷强度之间存在正相关关系，这表明聚集的硼位点更有利于成为活性位点。这意味着在h-BN表面创建更高密度的这些缺陷可能会提高催化性能。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：在ODHP反应中开发基于硼的催化剂的二维分散活性位点代表了多相催化领域的重大进展。从边缘位点催化到表面宽活性位点的转变为催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的途径。未来的研究努力可能会集中在：

1. 开发新方法来创建和控制各种硼基材料上二维分散活性位点的密度。
2. 研究硼基材料的晶体结构与BOx物种形成之间的关系，以优化催化性能。
3. 探索等离子体处理和其他表面改性技术的潜力，以在硼基催化剂上创建定制的缺陷和活性位点。
4. 进行深入的机理研究，以更好地理解二维分散活性位点在ODHP反应中的作用，可能导致设计更高效和选择性的催化剂。
5. 研究这些二维分散活性位点催化剂的可扩展性和长期稳定性，以用于潜在的工业应用。

这些研究方向可能导致开发出更高效和选择性的ODHP反应催化剂，可能彻底改变化学工业中丙烯和其他有价值烯烃的生产。
