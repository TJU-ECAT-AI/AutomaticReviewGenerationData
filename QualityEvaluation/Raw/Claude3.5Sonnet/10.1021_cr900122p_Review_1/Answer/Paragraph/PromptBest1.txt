The main challenge lies in achieving high selectivity and conversion simultaneously, as the oxidation of alkene products is often faster than that of the starting alkane, necessitating operation at low conversions to maintain high selectivities [10.1021/cr900122p_1].

Catalysts for ODH include both bulk metal oxides and supported systems. The reaction mechanism typically follows a Mars-van Krevelen redox cycle, involving the following steps [10.1021/jp002100x]:
1. Weak adsorption of the alkane on lattice oxygen
2. C-H bond activation through hydrogen abstraction
3. Formation of alkyl intermediates
4. Desorption of alkene product
5. Recombination of OH groups to form water and reduced metal centers
6. Reoxidation of the catalyst by O2

The efficiency of the ODH process is collectively influenced by several factors:
