The literature discusses several types of catalysts, including vanadium silicalite, supported V2O5 on silica, and bulk V2O5. These catalysts show different levels of efficiency in terms of selectivity and conversion.

Vanadium silicalite is described as one of the most efficient catalysts for propane ODH. The V2O5·6SiO2 xerogel catalyst prepared in the study showed high initial selectivity (86.6% at 400°C) but demonstrated declining selectivity with increasing temperature and conversion. This behavior is similar to that of bulk V2O5, which exhibits a selectivity of about 80% but also shows rapidly declining selectivity with increasing conversions.

The challenge in achieving high selectivity and conversion simultaneously is evident from the data presented. As the reaction temperature increases, the activity (conversion) increases, but the selectivity decreases. This trade-off between selectivity and conversion is a common challenge in ODH processes.

The study also compares the performance of their catalyst to a supported V2O5 on silica system prepared by incipient wetness impregnation. The latter showed lower selectivity but similar activity, highlighting the importance of catalyst preparation methods in determining performance.

In conclusion, the ODH process efficiency is influenced by the type of catalyst (bulk vs. supported), catalyst preparation method, reaction temperature, and conversion level. Balancing these factors to achieve high selectivity and conversion remains a key challenge in ODH catalysis.
