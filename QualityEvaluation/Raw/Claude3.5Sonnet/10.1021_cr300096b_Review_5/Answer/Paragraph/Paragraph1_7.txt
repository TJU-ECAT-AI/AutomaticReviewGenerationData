<English>
Thermodynamic preferences and reaction conditions significantly influence the stability, surface segregation, and structural configuration of bimetallic catalysts, particularly in Pt-based systems such as Pt-3d alloys. This review focuses on propane dehydrogenation catalysts, emphasizing the thermodynamic stability, structural dynamics, and catalytic implications of bimetallic surface structures under varying reaction conditions.

Key factors affecting the stability and structure of bimetallic catalysts include:

1. Surface energy: Elements with lower surface energy tend to segregate to the surface [10.1016/j.susc.2007.09.052].

2. Atomic size: Larger atoms are more likely to segregate to the surface to relieve strain [10.1016/j.apsusc.2009.03.086].

3. Reaction environment: The presence of adsorbates can dramatically alter surface composition and segregation trends [10.1021/cr300096b_5].

4. Temperature: Higher temperatures promote stronger metal-metal interactions and can induce surface restructuring [10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1].

5. Crystal facet: The stability of specific configurations varies with crystal facets [10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2].

These factors lead to dynamic changes in catalyst structure and composition, which have significant implications for catalytic properties and performance:

1. Surface composition control: The ability to manipulate surface composition through temperature and gas environment allows for tuning of catalytic properties [10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1].

2. Electronic effects: Subsurface alloying elements can modify the electronic properties of surface atoms, potentially enhancing catalytic activity for certain reactions [10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1].

3. Adsorbate-induced restructuring: CO adsorption, for example, can promote the diffusion of certain metals to the surface, altering the catalytic properties [10.1021/ja8089087].

4. Oxidation state changes: In oxidizing environments, some alloying elements may form oxide layers, affecting catalyst stability and activity [10.1016/j.cattod.2010.10.069].

5. Stability considerations: The tendency for surface restructuring under different conditions highlights the importance of considering catalyst stability under reaction environments [10.1039/c0ee00762e].

For propane dehydrogenation catalysts specifically, these factors can significantly impact performance:

1. Coke resistance: Bimetallic catalysts, such as PtSn, show improved resistance to coking compared to monometallic Pt catalysts [10.1016/j.susc.2004.03.030].

2. Selectivity enhancement: The formation of specific surface structures can lead to increased selectivity towards desired products [10.1016/j.susc.2004.03.030].

3. Activity modulation: The dynamic nature of the catalyst surface under reaction conditions can lead to in-situ changes in activity and selectivity [10.1002/cphc.200900053].

4. Stability improvements: Proper design of bimetallic catalysts can lead to enhanced stability under the harsh conditions of propane dehydrogenation [10.1039/c0ee00762e].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The dynamic nature of bimetallic catalysts under reaction conditions presents both challenges and opportunities for catalyst design and optimization. Future research efforts could focus on:

1. In-situ characterization techniques: Developing advanced methods to study catalyst structure and composition under realistic reaction conditions to better understand the dynamic changes occurring during propane dehydrogenation.

2. Rational design strategies: Utilizing the knowledge of thermodynamic preferences and reaction-induced changes to design catalysts that maintain optimal surface structures under reaction conditions.

3. Novel alloy combinations: Exploring new bimetallic and multi-metallic systems that can offer improved stability, activity, and selectivity for propane dehydrogenation.

4. Computational modeling: Enhancing predictive capabilities to simulate catalyst behavior under various conditions, guiding experimental efforts in catalyst development.

5. Smart catalysts: Designing catalysts that can adapt their surface structure in response to reaction conditions, optimizing performance throughout the catalyst lifetime.

By addressing these areas, researchers can work towards developing more efficient and stable catalysts for propane dehydrogenation and other important industrial processes.

<Chinese>
热力学偏好和反应条件显著影响双金属催化剂的稳定性、表面偏析和结构构型，尤其是在基于Pt的系统（如Pt-3d合金）中。本综述聚焦于丙烷脱氢催化剂，强调了在不同反应条件下双金属表面结构的热力学稳定性、结构动态性及其对催化性能的影响。

影响双金属催化剂稳定性和结构的关键因素包括：

1. 表面能：具有较低表面能的元素倾向于偏析到表面 [10.1016/j.susc.2007.09.052]。

2. 原子尺寸：较大的原子更可能偏析到表面以缓解应变 [10.1016/j.apsusc.2009.03.086]。

3. 反应环境：吸附质的存在可以显著改变表面组成和偏析趋势 [10.1021/cr300096b_5]。

4. 温度：较高的温度促进更强的金属-金属相互作用，并可能引起表面重构 [10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1]。

5. 晶面：特定构型的稳定性随晶面而变化 [10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2]。

这些因素导致催化剂结构和组成的动态变化，对催化性能有重要影响：

1. 表面组成控制：通过温度和气体环境操纵表面组成的能力允许调节催化性能 [10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1]。

2. 电子效应：亚表面合金元素可以修改表面原子的电子性质，可能增强某些反应的催化活性 [10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1]。

3. 吸附诱导重构：例如，CO吸附可以促进某些金属向表面扩散，改变催化性能 [10.1021/ja8089087]。

4. 氧化态变化：在氧化环境中，一些合金元素可能形成氧化物层，影响催化剂稳定性和活性 [10.1016/j.cattod.2010.10.069]。

5. 稳定性考虑：在不同条件下表面重构的倾向强调了考虑催化剂在反应环境中稳定性的重要性 [10.1039/c0ee00762e]。

对于丙烷脱氢催化剂，这些因素可以显著影响性能：

1. 抗积碳性：双金属催化剂，如PtSn，相比单金属Pt催化剂表现出更好的抗积碳性能 [10.1016/j.susc.2004.03.030]。

2. 选择性增强：特定表面结构的形成可以导致对目标产品的选择性增加 [10.1016/j.susc.2004.03.030]。

3. 活性调节：催化剂表面在反应条件下的动态特性可能导致活性和选择性的原位变化 [10.1002/cphc.200900053]。

4. 稳定性改善：适当设计的双金属催化剂可以在丙烷脱氢的苛刻条件下表现出增强的稳定性 [10.1039/c0ee00762e]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：双金属催化剂在反应条件下的动态特性为催化剂设计和优化提供了挑战和机遇。未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 原位表征技术：开发先进方法，在实际反应条件下研究催化剂结构和组成，以更好地理解丙烷脱氢过程中发生的动态变化。

2. 理性设计策略：利用热力学偏好和反应诱导变化的知识，设计能在反应条件下保持最佳表面结构的催化剂。

3. 新型合金组合：探索新的双金属和多金属系统，为丙烷脱氢提供改进的稳定性、活性和选择性。

4. 计算机模拟：增强预测能力，模拟各种条件下的催化剂行为，指导催化剂开发的实验努力。

5. 智能催化剂：设计能够根据反应条件调整其表面结构的催化剂，在整个催化剂寿命期间优化性能。

通过解决这些领域的问题，研究人员可以致力于为丙烷脱氢和其他重要的工业过程开发更高效、更稳定的催化剂。
