<English>
Thermodynamic preferences and reaction conditions significantly influence the stability, surface segregation, and structural configuration of bimetallic catalysts, particularly in Pt-based systems such as Pt-3d alloys. This review focuses on propane dehydrogenation catalysts, emphasizing the thermodynamic stability, structural dynamics, and catalytic implications of bimetallic surface structures under varying reaction conditions.

Key findings include:

1. Surface Segregation: In vacuum or reducing conditions, Pt tends to segregate to the surface due to its larger atomic radius and lower surface energy compared to 3d metals [10.1016/j.susc.2007.09.052]. However, under oxidizing conditions, 3d metals often segregate to the surface due to their strong affinity for oxygen [10.1016/j.cattod.2010.10.069].

2. Adsorbate-Induced Restructuring: The presence of adsorbates can dramatically alter surface composition and structure. For example, CO adsorption can induce surface segregation of less reactive elements like Cu in CuPt alloys [10.1021/ja8089087], while oxygen can promote 3d metal segregation in Pt-3d alloys [10.1063/1.2900962].

3. Crystal Facet Effects: The stability of specific configurations varies with crystal facets. For instance, the subsurface PtNiPt configuration is more stable on the (100) crystal facet than on the (111) facet [10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2].

4. Electronic Structure Modification: The formation of bimetallic surfaces alters the electronic properties of surface atoms, influencing reactivity and adsorption characteristics [10.1016/j.susc.2008.09.019].

5. Dynamic Nature: Bimetallic catalysts exhibit dynamic behavior under reaction conditions, with surface composition and structure changing in response to the environment [10.1002/cphc.200900053].

These factors have significant implications for catalytic properties and performance:

1. Enhanced Activity: Certain bimetallic configurations, such as Pt-skin structures with subsurface 3d metals, show increased activity for reactions like oxygen reduction [10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1].

2. Tunable Selectivity: The ability to control surface composition through segregation phenomena allows for tuning catalyst selectivity for specific reactions [10.1002/cphc.201000391].

3. Stability Considerations: While surface segregation can enhance activity, it may also affect long-term stability. Understanding and controlling these processes is crucial for developing durable catalysts [10.1039/c0ee00762e].

4. Reaction-Specific Optimization: The dynamic nature of bimetallic surfaces under reaction conditions necessitates considering the specific reaction environment when designing catalysts [10.1021/jp108939h].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The complex interplay between thermodynamic preferences, reaction conditions, and surface structure in bimetallic catalysts offers both challenges and opportunities for catalyst design. Future research efforts might focus on:

1. In-situ characterization techniques to better understand dynamic surface changes under reaction conditions.
2. Development of predictive models that incorporate both thermodynamic and kinetic factors to guide catalyst design.
3. Exploration of novel bimetallic combinations and preparation methods to achieve desired surface structures and properties.
4. Investigation of the potential for "smart" catalysts that can adapt their surface structure in response to changing reaction conditions.
5. Integration of machine learning approaches to accelerate the discovery and optimization of bimetallic catalysts for specific applications.

By leveraging these insights and emerging methodologies, researchers can work towards designing more efficient, stable, and selective bimetallic catalysts for propane dehydrogenation and other important chemical processes.

<Chinese>
热力学偏好和反应条件显著影响双金属催化剂的稳定性、表面偏析和结构构型，特别是在基于Pt的系统如Pt-3d合金中。本综述着重关注丙烷脱氢催化剂，强调了在不同反应条件下双金属表面结构的热力学稳定性、结构动态性及其对催化性能的影响。

主要发现包括：

1. 表面偏析：在真空或还原条件下，由于Pt的原子半径较大且表面能较低，Pt倾向于偏析到表面 [10.1016/j.susc.2007.09.052]。然而，在氧化条件下，3d金属由于与氧的强亲和力往往偏析到表面 [10.1016/j.cattod.2010.10.069]。

2. 吸附诱导重构：吸附物的存在可以显著改变表面组成和结构。例如，CO吸附可以诱导CuPt合金中较不活泼的Cu元素向表面偏析 [10.1021/ja8089087]，而氧气可以促进Pt-3d合金中3d金属的表面偏析 [10.1063/1.2900962]。

3. 晶面效应：特定构型的稳定性随晶面而变。例如，亚表面PtNiPt构型在(100)晶面上比在(111)晶面上更稳定 [10.1007/s11244-010-9447-2]。

4. 电子结构修饰：双金属表面的形成改变了表面原子的电子性质，影响反应性和吸附特性 [10.1016/j.susc.2008.09.019]。

5. 动态特性：双金属催化剂在反应条件下表现出动态行为，表面组成和结构会随环境变化而改变 [10.1002/cphc.200900053]。

这些因素对催化性能有重要影响：

1. 活性增强：某些双金属构型，如亚表面含3d金属的Pt-皮层结构，对氧还原等反应表现出增强的活性 [10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1]。

2. 可调选择性：通过控制偏析现象来调控表面组成，可以调整催化剂对特定反应的选择性 [10.1002/cphc.201000391]。

3. 稳定性考虑：虽然表面偏析可以提高活性，但也可能影响长期稳定性。理解和控制这些过程对开发耐久催化剂至关重要 [10.1039/c0ee00762e]。

4. 反应特异性优化：双金属表面在反应条件下的动态特性要求在设计催化剂时考虑特定的反应环境 [10.1021/jp108939h]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：双金属催化剂中热力学偏好、反应条件和表面结构之间的复杂相互作用为催化剂设计提供了挑战和机遇。未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解反应条件下的动态表面变化。
2. 建立综合考虑热力学和动力学因素的预测模型，指导催化剂设计。
3. 探索新型双金属组合和制备方法，以实现理想的表面结构和性能。
4. 研究能够根据变化的反应条件自适应调整表面结构的"智能"催化剂。
5. 整合机器学习方法，加速特定应用的双金属催化剂的发现和优化。

通过利用这些见解和新兴方法，研究人员可以朝着为丙烷脱氢和其他重要化学过程设计更高效、更稳定和更具选择性的双金属催化剂的方向努力。
