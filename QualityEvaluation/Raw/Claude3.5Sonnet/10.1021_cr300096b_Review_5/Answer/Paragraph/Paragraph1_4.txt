<English>
Thermodynamic preferences and reaction conditions significantly influence the stability, surface segregation, and structural configuration of bimetallic catalysts, particularly in Pt-based systems such as Pt-3d alloys. This review focuses on propane dehydrogenation catalysts, emphasizing the thermodynamic stability, structural dynamics, and catalytic implications of bimetallic surface structures under varying reaction conditions.

Key factors influencing bimetallic catalyst behavior include:

1. Surface energy: Elements with lower surface energy tend to segregate to the surface. For instance, in Pt-Au alloys, Au shows strong surface segregation due to its lower surface energy [10.1021/ct8004062].

2. Atomic size: Larger atoms are more likely to segregate to the surface to relieve strain. Pt's larger atomic radius (1.39 Å) compared to 3d metals like Co (1.25 Å), Ni (1.24 Å), and Cu (1.28 Å) contributes to its surface segregation in many alloys [10.1016/j.apsusc.2009.03.086].

3. Reaction environment: The presence of adsorbates can dramatically alter surface composition and segregation trends. For example, oxygen adsorption can decrease the stability of Pt-segregated surfaces and even reverse segregation trends in Pt3M alloys [10.1016/j.susc.2008.11.036].

4. Temperature: Higher temperatures promote stronger metal-metal interactions and can induce surface segregation or alloying. For instance, in Pt-Fe systems, annealing at different temperatures leads to various structural configurations [10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1].

These factors have significant implications for catalytic properties and performance:

1. Dynamic surface structures: The composition and structure of catalyst surfaces can change dynamically during reactions, affecting activity and selectivity. In Pt-Fe catalysts, the surface can switch between a Pt-skin structure and an iron oxide-rich surface depending on the reaction environment [10.1002/cphc.200900053].

2. Electronic effects: Subsurface alloying elements can modify the electronic structure of surface Pt atoms, influencing reactivity. For example, Pt-skin structures with subsurface Fe show enhanced activity for oxygen reduction reactions (ORR) compared to pure Pt [10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1].

3. Stability considerations: Surface segregation and restructuring can affect long-term catalyst stability. Understanding these phenomena is crucial for designing catalysts that maintain their performance under reaction conditions [10.1039/c0ee00762e].

4. Tunable catalytic properties: By controlling composition, preparation methods, and reaction conditions, it's possible to tailor surface structures for specific applications. For instance, Pt-Sn catalysts show improved resistance to coking and increased selectivity towards aromatics in reforming reactions compared to pure Pt catalysts [10.1016/j.susc.2004.03.030].

5. Adsorbate-induced effects: Reaction intermediates can induce surface restructuring, potentially creating more active catalytic sites. CO adsorption on Pt-Cu alloys, for example, can pull Cu to the surface, creating a novel surface structure with unique catalytic properties [10.1021/ja8089087].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The complex interplay between thermodynamic preferences and reaction conditions in bimetallic catalysts opens up new avenues for catalyst design and optimization. Future research might focus on:

1. In-situ characterization techniques: Developing advanced methods to study catalyst structures under realistic reaction conditions could provide deeper insights into dynamic surface changes and their impact on catalytic performance.

2. Computational modeling: Enhancing predictive models that incorporate multiple factors influencing surface segregation and restructuring could guide the rational design of more efficient and stable catalysts.

3. Smart catalyst design: Exploring the concept of adaptive catalysts that can dynamically optimize their surface structure in response to changing reaction conditions, potentially leading to more versatile and efficient catalytic systems.

4. Nanoscale engineering: Investigating the effects of particle size, shape, and support interactions on surface segregation and catalytic properties could lead to more precise control over catalyst behavior at the nanoscale.

5. Novel bimetallic combinations: Exploring unconventional metal pairings or multi-metallic systems might reveal synergistic effects that could enhance catalytic performance beyond what is currently achievable with traditional bimetallic catalysts.

These research directions could potentially revolutionize catalyst design for propane dehydrogenation and other important industrial processes, leading to more efficient, stable, and selective catalytic systems.

热力学偏好和反应条件显著影响双金属催化剂的稳定性、表面偏析和结构构型，特别是在基于Pt的系统（如Pt-3d合金）中。本综述重点关注丙烷脱氢催化剂，强调了在不同反应条件下双金属表面结构的热力学稳定性、结构动力学及其对催化性能的影响。

影响双金属催化剂行为的关键因素包括：

1. 表面能：具有较低表面能的元素倾向于偏析到表面。例如，在Pt-Au合金中，由于Au的表面能较低，表现出强烈的表面偏析 [10.1021/ct8004062]。

2. 原子尺寸：较大的原子更可能偏析到表面以释放应力。Pt的较大原子半径（1.39 Å）相比于3d金属如Co（1.25 Å）、Ni（1.24 Å）和Cu（1.28 Å），促进了其在许多合金中的表面偏析 [10.1016/j.apsusc.2009.03.086]。

3. 反应环境：吸附物的存在可以显著改变表面组成和偏析趋势。例如，氧气吸附可以降低Pt偏析表面的稳定性，甚至在Pt3M合金中逆转偏析趋势 [10.1016/j.susc.2008.11.036]。

4. 温度：较高的温度促进更强的金属-金属相互作用，可以诱导表面偏析或合金化。例如，在Pt-Fe系统中，在不同温度下退火导致各种结构构型 [10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1]。

这些因素对催化性能有重要影响：

1. 动态表面结构：催化剂表面的组成和结构可以在反应过程中动态变化，影响活性和选择性。在Pt-Fe催化剂中，表面可以根据反应环境在Pt-皮层结构和富铁氧化物表面之间切换 [10.1002/cphc.200900053]。

2. 电子效应：亚表面合金元素可以修改表面Pt原子的电子结构，影响反应性。例如，具有亚表面Fe的Pt-皮层结构显示出比纯Pt更高的氧还原反应（ORR）活性 [10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60037-1]。

3. 稳定性考虑：表面偏析和重构可能影响催化剂的长期稳定性。理解这些现象对设计能在反应条件下保持性能的催化剂至关重要 [10.1039/c0ee00762e]。

4. 可调催化性能：通过控制组成、制备方法和反应条件，可以为特定应用定制表面结构。例如，Pt-Sn催化剂在重整反应中表现出比纯Pt催化剂更好的抗结焦性能和更高的芳烃选择性 [10.1016/j.susc.2004.03.030]。

5. 吸附物诱导效应：反应中间体可以诱导表面重构，可能创造更活跃的催化位点。例如，CO在Pt-Cu合金上的吸附可以将Cu拉到表面，创造具有独特催化性能的新型表面结构 [10.1021/ja8089087]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：双金属催化剂中热力学偏好和反应条件的复杂相互作用为催化剂设计和优化开辟了新途径。未来研究可能集中在：

1. 原位表征技术：开发先进方法来研究实际反应条件下的催化剂结构，可以深入了解动态表面变化及其对催化性能的影响。

2. 计算模型：增强包含影响表面偏析和重构的多个因素的预测模型，可以指导更高效和稳定的催化剂的理性设计。

3. 智能催化剂设计：探索能够根据变化的反应条件动态优化其表面结构的自适应催化剂概念，可能导致更多功能和高效的催化系统。

4. 纳米尺度工程：研究粒子大小、形状和载体相互作用对表面偏析和催化性能的影响，可能导致在纳米尺度上更精确地控制催化剂行为。

5. 新型双金属组合：探索非常规金属配对或多金属系统可能揭示协同效应，可以增强催化性能超越目前传统双金属催化剂的水平。

这些研究方向可能彻底改变丙烷脱氢和其他重要工业过程的催化剂设计，导致更高效、稳定和选择性的催化系统。
