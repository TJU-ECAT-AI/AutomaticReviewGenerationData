Comparative analysis of FexNiy/CeO2 and vanadium oxide catalysts in CO2-ODH reactions: Performance, selectivity, and the influence of supports and composition on reaction mechanisms and product formation

The development of efficient catalysts for CO2-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation (CO2-ODH) reactions has gained significant attention due to its potential in valorizing CO2 and producing valuable alkenes. This review focuses on comparing the performance of FexNiy/CeO2 catalysts, particularly Fe3Ni1/CeO2, with vanadium oxide catalysts in CO2-ODH reactions.

1. Activity and Selectivity:
FexNiy/CeO2 catalysts, especially Fe3Ni1/CeO2, have demonstrated impressive performance in CO2-ODH reactions. Flow reactor results indicate that Fe3Ni1/CeO2 exhibits the highest C2H4 selectivity (>75%) for the conversion of C2H6 and CO2 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]. In contrast, vanadium oxide catalysts, such as V2O5 dispersed in mesoporous SiO2 nanospheres, can achieve up to 90% propylene selectivity during 8-h cycles. However, V2O5 catalysts suffer from declining conversion over time, decreasing from 60% to 20% within one cycle [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1].

2. Reaction Mechanisms:
The reaction mechanisms of these catalysts differ significantly. V-based catalysts typically operate through a Mars-van Krevelen mechanism, where oxygen atoms from the support play a crucial role in improving alkene selectivity [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]. On the other hand, Fe3Ni1/CeO2 catalysts operate through selective C-H cleavage. DFT calculations indicate that on the mixed FeNi(111) surface, CH bond cleavage is energetically more favorable than CC bond cleavage, leading to preferential dehydrogenation of ethane to form ethylene [10.1016/j.jcat.2016.02.004].

3. Support Materials:
For both catalyst types, support materials play a significant role in determining their performance. In V2O5 catalysts, the support affects reducibility, acid-base properties, and dispersion [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]. For FexNiy/CeO2 catalysts, CeO2 as a support is crucial for CO2 activation and helps prevent coking due to its ability to release and recover oxygen atoms [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]. The reducible CeO2 oxide is known for its ability to promote active metal dispersion and to shift easily between reduced and oxidized states, which helps accommodate CO2 dissociation [10.1016/j.jcat.2016.02.004].

4. Composition and Structure:
The composition and structure of these catalysts significantly influence their performance. The Fe:Ni ratio in FexNiy/CeO2 catalysts dramatically affects product selectivity, with Fe3Ni1/CeO2 favoring CO2-ODH and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 favoring dry reforming [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]. For V2O5 catalysts, better dispersion on the support leads to improved performance compared to bulk V2O5 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1].

5. Challenges and Potential for Improvement:
V2O5 catalysts face challenges from acidic sites promoting alkene cracking and health concerns related to V2O5 dust [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]. FexNiy/CeO2 catalysts show promise in overcoming some of these limitations, offering better stability and resistance to coking. The abundance of reactive lattice O atoms in FexNiy/CeO2 catalysts protects the material by readily removing any coke deposits that form through oxidation [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The comparative analysis of FexNiy/CeO2 and vanadium oxide catalysts in CO2-ODH reactions reveals distinct advantages and challenges for each system. While V2O5 catalysts have shown high selectivity, they face issues with stability and potential health concerns. FexNiy/CeO2 catalysts, particularly Fe3Ni1/CeO2, demonstrate promising performance and stability, offering a potential alternative to V2O5-based systems.

Future research directions may focus on:
1. Optimizing the composition and structure of FexNiy/CeO2 catalysts to further enhance selectivity and stability.
2. Investigating the long-term performance of FexNiy/CeO2 catalysts under industrial conditions.
3. Exploring novel support materials or promoters to improve the activity and selectivity of both catalyst systems.
4. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the reaction mechanisms and active sites in FexNiy/CeO2 catalysts.
5. Investigating the potential of these catalysts for other CO2 utilization reactions, such as dry reforming or CO2 hydrogenation.

By addressing these research areas, we can work towards developing more efficient and sustainable catalysts for CO2-ODH reactions, contributing to the broader goal of CO2 valorization and green chemistry.

FexNiy/CeO2和钒氧化物催化剂在CO2-ODH反应中的比较分析：性能、选择性以及支持材料和组成对反应机理和产物形成的影响

CO2辅助氧化脱氢（CO2-ODH）反应的高效催化剂开发已引起广泛关注，因为它在CO2增值和生产有价值烯烃方面具有潜力。本综述重点比较了FexNiy/CeO2催化剂（特别是Fe3Ni1/CeO2）与钒氧化物催化剂在CO2-ODH反应中的性能。

1. 活性和选择性：
FexNiy/CeO2催化剂，尤其是Fe3Ni1/CeO2，在CO2-ODH反应中表现出令人印象深刻的性能。流动反应器结果表明，Fe3Ni1/CeO2在C2H6和CO2转化中表现出最高的C2H4选择性（>75%）[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]。相比之下，钒氧化物催化剂，如分散在介孔SiO2纳米球中的V2O5，在8小时循环中可以达到90%的丙烯选择性。然而，V2O5催化剂随时间推移转化率下降，在一个循环内从60%降至20%[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]。

2. 反应机理：
这些催化剂的反应机理有显著差异。基于V的催化剂通常通过Mars-van Krevelen机理运作，其中来自支持材料的氧原子在提高烯烃选择性方面起着关键作用[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]。另一方面，Fe3Ni1/CeO2催化剂通过选择性C-H键断裂进行反应。DFT计算表明，在混合FeNi(111)表面上，CH键断裂在能量上比CC键断裂更有利，导致乙烷优先脱氢形成乙烯[10.1016/j.jcat.2016.02.004]。

3. 支持材料：
对于这两种类型的催化剂，支持材料在决定其性能方面起着重要作用。在V2O5催化剂中，支持材料影响还原性、酸碱性质和分散性[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]。对于FexNiy/CeO2催化剂，CeO2作为支持材料对CO2活化至关重要，并且由于其释放和恢复氧原子的能力，有助于防止结焦[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]。可还原的CeO2氧化物以其促进活性金属分散和在还原和氧化状态之间容易转换的能力而闻名，这有助于容纳CO2解离[10.1016/j.jcat.2016.02.004]。

4. 组成和结构：
这些催化剂的组成和结构显著影响其性能。FexNiy/CeO2催化剂中的Fe:Ni比例显著影响产物选择性，Fe3Ni1/CeO2有利于CO2-ODH，而Fe1Ni3/CeO2有利于干重整[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]。对于V2O5催化剂，在支持材料上的更好分散导致比体相V2O5更好的性能[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]。

5. 挑战和改进潜力：
V2O5催化剂面临酸性位点促进烯烃裂解和V2O5粉尘相关健康问题的挑战[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]。FexNiy/CeO2催化剂在克服这些限制方面显示出promise，提供更好的稳定性和抗结焦能力。FexNiy/CeO2催化剂中反应性晶格O原子的丰富性通过氧化作用可以轻易去除形成的任何焦炭沉积物，从而保护材料[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：
对FexNiy/CeO2和钒氧化物催化剂在CO2-ODH反应中的比较分析揭示了每个系统的独特优势和挑战。虽然V2O5催化剂表现出高选择性，但它们面临稳定性和潜在健康问题的挑战。FexNiy/CeO2催化剂，尤其是Fe3Ni1/CeO2，展示了promising的性能和稳定性，为V2O5基系统提供了潜在的替代方案。

未来研究方向可能集中在：
1. 优化FexNiy/CeO2催化剂的组成和结构，以进一步提高选择性和稳定性。
2. 在工业条件下研究FexNiy/CeO2催化剂的长期性能。
3. 探索新型支持材料或促进剂，以提高两种催化剂系统的活性和选择性。
4. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解FexNiy/CeO2催化剂中的反应机理和活性位点。
5. 研究这些催化剂在其他CO2利用反应中的潜力，如干重整或CO2加氢。

通过解决这些研究领域，我们可以致力于开发更高效、更可持续的CO2-ODH反应催化剂，为CO2增值和绿色化学的更广泛目标做出贡献。
