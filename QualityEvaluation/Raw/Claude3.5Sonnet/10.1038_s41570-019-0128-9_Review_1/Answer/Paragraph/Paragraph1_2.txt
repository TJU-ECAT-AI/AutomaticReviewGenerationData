<English>
Comparative analysis of FexNiy/CeO2 and vanadium oxide catalysts in CO2-ODH reactions reveals significant differences in performance, selectivity, and the influence of supports and composition on reaction mechanisms and product formation.

Activity and Selectivity:
Fe3Ni1/CeO2 demonstrates high C2H4 selectivity (>75%) in CO2-ODH of ethane [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]. In contrast, V2O5 catalysts can achieve up to 90% propylene selectivity but suffer from declining conversion over time, decreasing from 60% to 20% within one 8-hour cycle [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]. FeNi/CeO2 catalysts show promising selectivity for oxidative dehydrogenation of ethane (ODEC), with ethylene selectivity up to 30% [10.1016/j.jcat.2016.02.004].

Reaction Mechanisms:
V-based catalysts typically operate through a Mars-van Krevelen mechanism, where oxygen atoms from the support play a crucial role in improving alkene selectivity [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]. Fe3Ni1/CeO2 catalysts, on the other hand, operate through selective C-H cleavage, with the interaction between Fe and Ni creating distinct active sites that contribute to high selectivity [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]. DFT calculations indicate that on the mixed FeNi(111) surface, CH bond cleavage is energetically more favorable than CC bond cleavage, leading to preferential dehydrogenation of ethane to form ethylene [10.1016/j.jcat.2016.02.004].

Support Materials:
For both catalyst types, support materials play a significant role. In V2O5 catalysts, the support affects reducibility, acid-base properties, and dispersion [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]. For FexNiy/CeO2 catalysts, CeO2 as a support is crucial for CO2 activation and helps prevent coking due to its ability to release and recover oxygen atoms [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]. CeO2 is known for its ability to promote active metal dispersion and to shift easily between reduced and oxidized states, which helps accommodate CO2 dissociation [10.1016/j.jcat.2016.02.004].

Composition and Structure:
The Fe:Ni ratio in FexNiy/CeO2 catalysts dramatically affects product selectivity, with Fe3Ni1/CeO2 favoring CO2-ODH and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 favoring dry reforming [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]. For V2O5 catalysts, better dispersion on the support leads to improved performance compared to bulk V2O5 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]. The mixed FeNi surface is found to be more favorable for the ODEC pathway compared to Ni-terminated surfaces, suggesting that the composition and structure of the catalyst play a crucial role in determining its performance [10.1016/j.jcat.2016.02.004].

Challenges and Potential for Improvement:
V2O5 catalysts face challenges from acidic sites promoting alkene cracking and health concerns related to V2O5 dust [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]. FexNiy/CeO2 catalysts show promise in overcoming some of these limitations, offering better stability and resistance to coking [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]. However, further research is needed to fully understand their potential for improvement and long-term stability.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements and findings discussed in the file, there lies an opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies in CO2-ODH catalysis. Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Optimizing the Fe:Ni ratio in FexNiy/CeO2 catalysts to further enhance selectivity and stability.
2. Investigating the synergistic effects between Fe and Ni in more detail to understand the formation of distinct active sites.
3. Exploring novel support materials or modifications to CeO2 to further improve catalyst performance and longevity.
4. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic behavior of these catalysts under reaction conditions.
5. Investigating the potential of FexNiy/CeO2 catalysts for other dehydrogenation reactions beyond ethane.
6. Addressing the scale-up challenges and economic feasibility of FexNiy/CeO2 catalysts for industrial applications.

These research directions could potentially lead to the development of more efficient and sustainable catalysts for CO2 utilization and alkane valorization, contributing to the broader goals of green chemistry and circular carbon economy.

<Chinese>
FexNiy/CeO2和钒氧化物催化剂在CO2-ODH反应中的比较分析揭示了性能、选择性以及支撑材料和组成对反应机理和产物形成的影响方面的显著差异。

活性和选择性：
Fe3Ni1/CeO2在乙烷的CO2-ODH反应中表现出高C2H4选择性（>75%）[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]。相比之下，V2O5催化剂可以达到高达90%的丙烯选择性，但转化率随时间迅速下降，在8小时循环内从60%降至20%[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]。FeNi/CeO2催化剂在乙烷氧化脱氢（ODEC）方面显示出有前景的选择性，乙烯选择性高达30%[10.1016/j.jcat.2016.02.004]。

反应机理：
基于V的催化剂通常通过Mars-van Krevelen机制运作，其中来自支撑材料的氧原子在提高烯烃选择性方面起着关键作用[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]。另一方面，Fe3Ni1/CeO2催化剂通过选择性C-H键断裂进行反应，Fe和Ni之间的相互作用创造了独特的活性位点，有助于高选择性[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]。DFT计算表明，在混合FeNi(111)表面上，CH键断裂在能量上比CC键断裂更有利，导致乙烷优先脱氢形成乙烯[10.1016/j.jcat.2016.02.004]。

支撑材料：
对于这两种类型的催化剂，支撑材料都起着重要作用。在V2O5催化剂中，支撑材料影响还原性、酸碱性质和分散性[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]。对于FexNiy/CeO2催化剂，CeO2作为支撑材料对CO2活化至关重要，并且由于其释放和回收氧原子的能力，有助于防止结焦[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]。CeO2以其促进活性金属分散和在还原态和氧化态之间容易转换的能力而闻名，这有助于促进CO2解离[10.1016/j.jcat.2016.02.004]。

组成和结构：
FexNiy/CeO2催化剂中的Fe:Ni比例对产物选择性有显著影响，Fe3Ni1/CeO2有利于CO2-ODH反应，而Fe1Ni3/CeO2有利于干重整反应[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]。对于V2O5催化剂，在支撑材料上的更好分散性导致比块状V2O5更好的性能[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]。混合FeNi表面被发现比Ni终止表面更有利于ODEC路径，这表明催化剂的组成和结构在决定其性能方面起着关键作用[10.1016/j.jcat.2016.02.004]。

挑战和改进潜力：
V2O5催化剂面临着酸性位点促进烯烃裂解和V2O5粉尘相关健康问题的挑战[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]。FexNiy/CeO2催化剂在克服这些限制方面显示出前景，提供了更好的稳定性和抗结焦性[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_1]。然而，需要进一步研究以充分了解其改进潜力和长期稳定性。

综合理解与未来展望：考虑到文件中讨论的先进成果和发现，我们有机会探索CO2-ODH催化领域的新兴领域和创新方法。未来的研究努力可能会集中在：

1. 优化FexNiy/CeO2催化剂中的Fe:Ni比例，以进一步提高选择性和稳定性。
2. 更详细地研究Fe和Ni之间的协同效应，以理解独特活性位点的形成。
3. 探索新型支撑材料或对CeO2的改性，以进一步提高催化剂性能和寿命。
4. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解这些催化剂在反应条件下的动态行为。
5. 研究FexNiy/CeO2催化剂在乙烷以外的其他脱氢反应中的潜力。
6. 解决FexNiy/CeO2催化剂工业应用的规模放大挑战和经济可行性。

这些研究方向可能会导致开发出更高效和可持续的催化剂，用于CO2利用和烷烃增值，从而为绿色化学和循环碳经济的更广泛目标做出贡献。
