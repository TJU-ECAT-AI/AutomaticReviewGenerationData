Modification of Surface Chemistry:
1. Acid-Base Properties: Doping alters the Lewis acid-base characteristics of the catalyst surface. For instance, La3+ doping in CeO2 creates electron deficiency, making the surface a stronger Lewis acid, while Ta5+ doping creates electron excess, resulting in a stronger Lewis base [10.1021/jp211693v]. This modification affects the binding sites and behavior of adsorbates, with halogens preferentially binding to oxygen sites on La-doped surfaces and to metal sites on Ta-doped surfaces.

2. Electronic Structure: Dopants can act as electron donors or traps, modifying the electronic properties of the surface. For example, Mo dopants in CaO provide excess charges that can be transferred to adsorbed metals like Au, while Li creates electron traps that interrupt charge flow [10.1021/ja211396].

3. Oxygen Vacancy Formation: Low-valence dopants (LVDs) lower the energy of oxygen vacancy formation, which can enhance the catalyst's oxidation capabilities [10.1021/jp301341].

3. Morphology Control: Doping can influence the growth and shape of supported metal particles, affecting their interaction with the oxide support [10.1021/ja211396].

Implications for Active Centers:
