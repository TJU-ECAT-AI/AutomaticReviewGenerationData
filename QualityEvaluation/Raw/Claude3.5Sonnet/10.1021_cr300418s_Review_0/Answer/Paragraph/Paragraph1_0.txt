<English>
Substitutional cation doping in metal oxide catalysts is a powerful technique for modifying surface chemistry and disrupting chemical bonding, leading to enhanced catalytic performance in various industrially relevant reactions. This review section will analyze the mechanisms, surface chemistry modifications, and implications for active centers in propane dehydrogenation catalysts.

Mechanisms of Surface Chemistry Modification:

1. Lewis Acid-Base Properties: Substitutional doping can alter the Lewis acid-base properties of the catalyst surface. For instance, doping CeO2 with La3+ creates electron deficiency, making the surface a stronger Lewis acid, while Ta5+ doping creates electron excess, making it a stronger Lewis base [10.1021/jp211693v]. This modification affects the binding sites and behavior of adsorbates, with Lewis acidic surfaces favoring oxygen sites for halogen binding and Lewis basic surfaces favoring metal sites [10.1021/jp211693v].

2. Electronic Structure Alteration: Dopants can modify the band structure of the oxide, affecting its ability to exchange charges with adsorbates. For example, Mo doping in CaO acts as an electron donor, providing excess charges that can transfer to adsorbed metals like Au, altering their charge state [10.1021/ja211396].

3. Oxygen Vacancy Formation: Low-valence dopants (LVDs) can lower the energy of oxygen vacancy formation, which is crucial for many catalytic processes [10.1021/jp301341]. This modification can enhance the catalyst's oxidation capabilities, although a balance must be struck to maintain catalyst regeneration ability.

4. Amorphous Phase Creation: Doping can create amorphous metal oxide phases at the surface, which often serve as catalytic active sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019].

Surface Chemistry Modifications and Their Effects:

1. Adsorbate Binding: The modified Lewis acid-base properties lead to stronger interactions with complementary adsorbates. For example, halogen binding is considerably stronger on doped surfaces compared to undoped CeO2 [10.1021/jp211693v].

2. Reaction Energetics: Doping can significantly influence reaction energetics. La-doping of CeO2, for instance, dramatically lowers the energy barrier for methane C-H bond activation [10.1021/jp211693v].

3. Particle Morphology: Doping can control the growth and morphology of supported metal particles. For example, anionic gold shows enhanced adhesion and tends to form 2D islands on doped CaO surfaces [10.1021/ja211396].

4. Kinetics vs. Thermodynamics: Doping primarily affects the kinetic rate constant (krds) of catalytic reactions while leaving the thermodynamic adsorption constant (Kads) relatively unchanged [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019].

Implications for Active Centers:

1. New Active Sites: Doping creates new types of active centers on the oxide surface, often near the dopant sites. These can be either the oxygen atoms near the dopant or the dopant itself [10.1021/cr300418s_0].

2. Site Distribution: The nature and location of active sites shift from bulk to surface amorphous phases [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019].

3. Selective Site Modification: Doping can selectively modify certain types of sites. For instance, chloride doping in Na/CaO catalysts affects both highly active and less active oxygen-containing sites, changing the nature and distribution of active centers [10.1016/j.cej.2007.05.038].

4. Enhanced Metal-Support Interactions: Doping can improve metal-support interactions, leading to more stable catalysts and influencing reactivity at the metal-oxide interface [10.1021/ja211396].

Potential Improvements in Industrially Relevant Reactions:

For propane dehydrogenation catalysts, substitutional cation doping could potentially lead to the following improvements:

1. Enhanced C-H Bond Activation: By lowering activation barriers, doped catalysts could facilitate easier propane activation [10.1021/jp211693v].

2. Improved Selectivity: Tailored surface properties could enhance selectivity towards propylene formation by suppressing undesired side reactions [10.1016/j.cej.2007.05.038].

3. Increased Stability: Proper doping could improve catalyst stability by modifying sintering behavior and enhancing metal-support interactions [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019].

4. Optimized Redox Properties: Doping can tune the redox properties of the catalyst, potentially improving its performance in the dehydrogenation cycle [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of substitutional cation doping in metal oxide catalysts for propane dehydrogenation offers exciting opportunities for catalyst design and optimization. Future research directions may include:

1. Precise Control of Dopant Distribution: Developing methods to control the spatial distribution of dopants could lead to more efficient and selective catalysts.

2. Multi-Dopant Systems: Exploring the synergistic effects of multiple dopants could unlock new catalytic properties and enhance performance.

3. In-situ Characterization: Advanced in-situ characterization techniques could provide deeper insights into the dynamic behavior of doped catalysts under reaction conditions.

4. Machine Learning Approaches: Utilizing machine learning algorithms to predict optimal dopant-oxide combinations could accelerate catalyst discovery and optimization.

5. Scale-up and Industrial Implementation: Addressing challenges in scaling up the production of doped catalysts and implementing them in industrial processes will be crucial for realizing their potential benefits.

By pursuing these research directions, we can expect significant advancements in the design of highly efficient and selective propane dehydrogenation catalysts, potentially revolutionizing this important industrial process.

金属氧化物催化剂中的取代阳离子掺杂是一种强大的技术，可以修改表面化学性质并破坏化学键，从而在各种工业相关反应中提高催化性能。本综述部分将分析丙烷脱氢催化剂中的机理、表面化学修饰以及对活性中心的影响。

表面化学修饰机理：

1. 路易斯酸碱性质：取代掺杂可以改变催化剂表面的路易斯酸碱性质。例如，用La3+掺杂CeO2会产生电子缺陷，使表面成为更强的路易斯酸，而Ta5+掺杂会产生电子过剩，使其成为更强的路易斯碱[10.1021/jp211693v]。这种修饰影响吸附物的结合位点和行为，路易斯酸性表面有利于卤素在氧位点结合，而路易斯碱性表面则有利于卤素在金属位点结合[10.1021/jp211693v]。

2. 电子结构改变：掺杂剂可以修改氧化物的能带结构，影响其与吸附物交换电荷的能力。例如，CaO中的Mo掺杂作为电子供体，提供多余的电荷可以转移到吸附的金属（如Au）上，改变其电荷状态[10.1021/ja211396]。

3. 氧空位形成：低价掺杂剂（LVDs）可以降低氧空位形成的能量，这对许多催化过程至关重要[10.1021/jp301341]。这种修饰可以增强催化剂的氧化能力，但必须保持平衡以维持催化剂的再生能力。

4. 非晶相创建：掺杂可以在表面创建非晶金属氧化物相，这些相通常作为催化活性位点[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019]。

表面化学修饰及其影响：

1. 吸附物结合：修改后的路易斯酸碱性质导致与互补吸附物的相互作用更强。例如，与未掺杂的CeO2相比，卤素在掺杂表面上的结合强度明显增强[10.1021/jp211693v]。

2. 反应能量学：掺杂可以显著影响反应能量学。例如，CeO2的La掺杂大大降低了甲烷C-H键活化的能量障碍[10.1021/jp211693v]。

3. 粒子形态：掺杂可以控制负载金属粒子的生长和形态。例如，在掺杂的CaO表面上，阴离子金表现出增强的粘附性，倾向于形成二维岛状结构[10.1021/ja211396]。

4. 动力学与热力学：掺杂主要影响催化反应的动力学速率常数（krds），而热力学吸附常数（Kads）相对保持不变[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019]。

对活性中心的影响：

1. 新的活性位点：掺杂在氧化物表面创造了新类型的活性中心，通常位于掺杂剂附近。这些可以是掺杂剂附近的氧原子或掺杂剂本身[10.1021/cr300418s_0]。

2. 位点分布：活性位点的性质和位置从体相转移到表面非晶相[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019]。

3. 选择性位点修饰：掺杂可以选择性地修饰某些类型的位点。例如，Na/CaO催化剂中的氯化物掺杂影响高活性和低活性的含氧位点，改变活性中心的性质和分布[10.1016/j.cej.2007.05.038]。

4. 增强金属-载体相互作用：掺杂可以改善金属-载体相互作用，导致更稳定的催化剂，并影响金属-氧化物界面的反应性[10.1021/ja211396]。

工业相关反应的潜在改进：

对于丙烷脱氢催化剂，取代阳离子掺杂可能带来以下改进：

1. 增强C-H键活化：通过降低活化能障，掺杂催化剂可以促进丙烷的更容易活化[10.1021/jp211693v]。

2. 提高选择性：定制的表面性质可以通过抑制不需要的副反应来提高对丙烯形成的选择性[10.1016/j.cej.2007.05.038]。

3. 增加稳定性：适当的掺杂可以通过修改烧结行为和增强金属-载体相互作用来改善催化剂的稳定性[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019]。

4. 优化氧化还原性质：掺杂可以调节催化剂的氧化还原性质，可能改善其在脱氢循环中的性能[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019]。

综合理解与未来展望：丙烷脱氢金属氧化物催化剂中的取代阳离子掺杂领域为催化剂设计和优化提供了令人兴奋的机会。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 精确控制掺杂剂分布：开发控制掺杂剂空间分布的方法可能导致更高效和选择性的催化剂。

2. 多掺杂剂系统：探索多种掺杂剂的协同效应可能解锁新的催化性质并提高性能。

3. 原位表征：先进的原位表征技术可以提供对掺杂催化剂在反应条件下动态行为的更深入了解。

4. 机器学习方法：利用机器学习算法预测最佳掺杂剂-氧化物组合可以加速催化剂的发现和优化。

5. 规模扩大和工业实施：解决掺杂催化剂生产规模扩大和在工业过程中实施的挑战将对实现其潜在利益至关重要。

通过追求这些研究方向，我们可以期待在高效和选择性丙烷脱氢催化剂设计方面取得重大进展，可能彻底改变这一重要的工业过程。
