Substitutional cation doping in metal oxide catalysts modifies surface chemistry and disrupts chemical bonding through various mechanisms, significantly impacting catalytic activity and selectivity. This review section focuses on the effects of such doping on propane dehydrogenation catalysts and their implications for industrial applications.

Doping metal oxides with cations of different valences alters the Lewis acid-base properties of the catalyst surface. Low-valence dopants (LVDs) create electron deficiency, turning the surface into a stronger Lewis acid, while high-valence dopants introduce excess electrons, making the surface more basic [10.1021/jp301341]. This modification of acid-base properties has profound effects on the catalytic behavior:

1. Oxygen vacancy formation: LVDs lower the energy required for oxygen vacancy formation, which can enhance the catalyst's redox properties [10.1021/jp301341].

2. Adsorbate binding: The altered Lewis acid-base properties affect the binding strength and sites of adsorbates. For instance, on La-doped CeO2 (Lewis acidic), halogens bind preferentially to oxygen sites, while on Ta-doped CeO2 (Lewis basic), they bind to metal sites [10.1021/jp211693v].

3. Reaction energetics: Doping can significantly lower activation barriers for key steps in catalytic reactions. For example, La-doping of CeO2 dramatically reduces the energy barrier for methane C-H bond activation [10.1021/jp211693v].

The nature and location of active centers are also modified by substitutional doping:

1. Surface amorphous phases: Doping often creates amorphous metal oxide phases at the surface, which can serve as catalytic active sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019].

2. Electronic properties: Less electronegative support cations increase electron density on bridging bonds, enhancing redox activity [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019].

3. New functionalities: Dopants can introduce new catalytic functions without necessarily altering existing ones. For instance, adding acidic promoters to vanadia catalysts provides new acid sites without affecting vanadia's redox activity [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019].

These modifications have significant implications for enhancing catalytic performance in industrially relevant reactions, such as propane dehydrogenation:

1. Improved activity and selectivity: By tuning the surface acid-base properties, catalysts can be optimized for specific reactions. For instance, chloride-modified CeO2 catalysts show enhanced ethane conversion and ethylene selectivity in oxidative dehydrogenation [10.1007/bf00810677].

2. Stability enhancement: Appropriate dopant selection can improve catalyst stability by retarding sintering. Dopants with high Tammann temperatures tend to stabilize the oxide support [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019].

3. Reaction pathway control: Doping allows for manipulation of reaction pathways by altering the electronic structure of active sites. This can lead to improved product selectivity in complex reactions [10.1021/ja211396].

4. Optimized oxidation catalysts: The "moderation principle" suggests that optimal doping levels should balance the ease of oxygen removal without making it too facile, which is crucial for oxidation catalysts following the Mars-van Krevelen mechanism [10.1021/jp301341].

However, it's important to note that the effects of doping are complex and can sometimes lead to compensating effects. The performance of doped catalysts depends strongly on preparation methods, and it can be challenging to provide convincing proof of successful doping [10.1021/cr300418s_0].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of substitutional cation doping in metal oxide catalysts for propane dehydrogenation and related reactions shows great promise for enhancing catalytic performance. The ability to fine-tune surface properties at the atomic level opens up new possibilities for catalyst design.

Future research directions may include:

1. Developing more precise doping techniques to achieve better control over dopant distribution and concentration.
2. Exploring multi-element doping strategies to create synergistic effects and further optimize catalyst performance.
3. Integrating advanced characterization techniques with computational modeling to gain deeper insights into the mechanisms of doping-induced enhancements.
4. Investigating the long-term stability and regeneration of doped catalysts under industrial conditions.
5. Exploring the potential of doped metal oxide catalysts in emerging fields such as biomass conversion and CO2 utilization.

By addressing these challenges and opportunities, researchers can work towards developing next-generation catalysts with improved activity, selectivity, and stability for propane dehydrogenation and other industrially relevant reactions.

金属氧化物催化剂中的置换阳离子掺杂通过各种机制修改表面化学性质并破坏化学键，显著影响催化活性和选择性。本综述部分重点关注这种掺杂对丙烷脱氢催化剂的影响及其在工业应用中的意义。

用不同价态的阳离子掺杂金属氧化物会改变催化剂表面的路易斯酸碱性质。低价掺杂剂（LVDs）造成电子缺乏，使表面成为更强的路易斯酸，而高价掺杂剂引入过量电子，使表面更碱性[10.1021/jp301341]。这种酸碱性质的修改对催化行为有深远影响：

1. 氧空位形成：LVDs降低了氧空位形成所需的能量，这可以增强催化剂的氧化还原性能[10.1021/jp301341]。

2. 吸附物结合：改变的路易斯酸碱性质影响吸附物的结合强度和位点。例如，在La掺杂的CeO2（路易斯酸性）上，卤素优先结合到氧位点，而在Ta掺杂的CeO2（路易斯碱性）上，它们结合到金属位点[10.1021/jp211693v]。

3. 反应能量学：掺杂可以显著降低催化反应关键步骤的活化能垒。例如，CeO2的La掺杂大大降低了甲烷C-H键活化的能量障碍[10.1021/jp211693v]。

活性中心的性质和位置也被置换掺杂所修改：

1. 表面非晶相：掺杂常常在表面创造非晶金属氧化物相，可以作为催化活性位点[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019]。

2. 电子性质：电负性较低的支持阳离子增加桥键上的电子密度，增强氧化还原活性[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019]。

3. 新功能：掺杂剂可以引入新的催化功能，而不一定改变现有功能。例如，向钒氧化物催化剂添加酸性助剂提供了新的酸性位点，而不影响钒氧化物的氧化还原活性[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019]。

这些修改对提高工业相关反应（如丙烷脱氢）的催化性能有重要意义：

1. 提高活性和选择性：通过调节表面酸碱性质，可以针对特定反应优化催化剂。例如，氯化物改性的CeO2催化剂在乙烷氧化脱氢中表现出增强的乙烷转化率和乙烯选择性[10.1007/bf00810677]。

2. 稳定性增强：适当的掺杂剂选择可以通过抑制烧结来改善催化剂稳定性。具有高塔曼温度的掺杂剂倾向于稳定氧化物支持体[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019]。

3. 反应路径控制：掺杂允许通过改变活性位点的电子结构来操纵反应路径。这可以在复杂反应中导致改进的产物选择性[10.1021/ja211396]。

4. 优化氧化催化剂："适度原则"表明，最佳掺杂水平应平衡氧去除的容易程度，而不使其过于容易，这对于遵循Mars-van Krevelen机制的氧化催化剂至关重要[10.1021/jp301341]。

然而，需要注意的是，掺杂的影响是复杂的，有时可能导致补偿效应。掺杂催化剂的性能强烈依赖于制备方法，并且很难提供成功掺杂的令人信服的证据[10.1021/cr300418s_0]。

综合理解与未来展望：金属氧化物催化剂中的置换阳离子掺杂在丙烷脱氢和相关反应中显示出提高催化性能的巨大潜力。在原子水平上精细调节表面性质的能力为催化剂设计开辟了新的可能性。

未来研究方向可能包括：

1. 开发更精确的掺杂技术，以实现对掺杂剂分布和浓度的更好控制。
2. 探索多元素掺杂策略，创造协同效应并进一步优化催化剂性能。
3. 将先进表征技术与计算模拟相结合，深入了解掺杂引起增强的机制。
4. 研究掺杂催化剂在工业条件下的长期稳定性和再生。
5. 探索掺杂金属氧化物催化剂在生物质转化和CO2利用等新兴领域的潜力。

通过解决这些挑战和机遇，研究人员可以致力于开发具有改进的活性、选择性和稳定性的新一代催化剂，用于丙烷脱氢和其他工业相关反应。
