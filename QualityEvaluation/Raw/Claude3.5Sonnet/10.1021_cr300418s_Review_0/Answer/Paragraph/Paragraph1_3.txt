<English>
Substitutional cation doping in metal oxide catalysts is a powerful technique for modifying surface chemistry and disrupting chemical bonding, with significant implications for enhancing catalytic activity, altering the nature and location of active centers, and improving industrially relevant reactions. This review section focuses on the mechanisms, surface chemistry modifications, and implications of this technique for propane dehydrogenation catalysts.

Mechanisms of Surface Chemistry Modification:

1. Electronic Structure Alteration: Substitutional doping with cations of different valences can significantly alter the electronic properties of the catalyst surface. For instance, doping CeO2 with La3+ creates electron deficiency, turning the surface into a stronger Lewis acid, while Ta5+ doping creates electron excess, making the surface a stronger Lewis base [10.1021/jp211693v]. This electronic modification affects the catalyst's ability to interact with reactants and intermediates.

2. Creation of Amorphous Phases: Doping can lead to the formation of amorphous metal oxide phases at the surface, which often serve as catalytic active sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019]. These amorphous regions can exhibit different reactivity compared to the crystalline bulk.

3. Charge Transfer: Dopants can act as electron donors or acceptors, facilitating charge transfer between the catalyst and adsorbed species. For example, Mo dopants in CaO can provide excess charges to be transferred to adsorbed gold particles, while Li dopants can create electron traps that interrupt this charge flow [10.1021/ja211396].

4. Oxygen Vacancy Formation: Low-valence dopants can lower the energy of oxygen vacancy formation, which can be crucial for oxidation reactions following the Mars-van Krevelen mechanism [10.1021/jp301341].

Surface Chemistry Modifications:

1. Acid-Base Properties: Doping can dramatically alter the Lewis acid-base properties of the catalyst surface. This affects the binding strength and preferred binding sites of adsorbates, potentially leading to enhanced catalytic activity [10.1021/jp211693v].

2. Adsorbate Binding: The modified surface properties can lead to changes in adsorbate binding strengths and configurations. For instance, halogen binding is considerably stronger on doped CeO2 surfaces compared to undoped ones [10.1021/jp211693v].

3. Morphology Control: Doping can influence the growth and morphology of supported metal particles. For example, anionic gold on doped CaO tends to form 2D islands due to enhanced interface adhesion [10.1021/ja211396].

4. Selective Site Poisoning: In some cases, dopants (like chloride ions) can selectively poison certain active sites, leading to changes in reactivity and selectivity [10.1016/j.cej.2007.05.038].

Implications for Catalytic Activity Enhancement:

1. Lowered Activation Barriers: Doping can lower activation barriers for rate-limiting steps in catalytic reactions. For example, La-doping of CeO2 significantly lowers the energy barrier for methane C-H bond activation [10.1021/jp211693v].

2. Improved Selectivity: By modifying surface properties, doping can enhance selectivity towards desired products. For instance, chloride-doped catalysts show improved selectivity in the oxidative coupling of methane [10.1016/j.cej.2007.05.038].

3. Stability Enhancement: Appropriate dopant selection can improve catalyst stability by retarding sintering processes [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019].

4. New Reaction Pathways: The creation of new types of active sites through doping can open up alternative reaction pathways, potentially leading to improved catalytic performance [10.1021/jp301341].

Implications for Propane Dehydrogenation Catalysts:

While the literature reviewed does not specifically address propane dehydrogenation, the principles of substitutional cation doping can be applied to this industrially relevant reaction:

1. Enhanced C-H Bond Activation: The ability of doped catalysts to facilitate C-H bond breaking, as demonstrated in methane activation studies, could be beneficial for propane dehydrogenation [10.1021/jp211693v].

2. Improved Selectivity: The selective site poisoning observed with chloride doping could potentially be utilized to enhance propylene selectivity by suppressing undesired side reactions [10.1016/j.cej.2007.05.038].

3. Tailored Redox Properties: By carefully selecting dopants, the redox properties of the catalyst can be optimized to balance the dehydrogenation reaction and catalyst regeneration steps [10.1021/jp301341].

4. Catalyst Stability: The enhanced thermal stability observed in some doped catalysts could lead to improved longevity in the high-temperature conditions typically used for propane dehydrogenation [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of substitutional cation doping in metal oxide catalysts offers exciting possibilities for enhancing catalytic performance in propane dehydrogenation and other industrially relevant reactions. Future research directions may include:

1. High-throughput screening of dopant combinations to identify optimal formulations for specific reactions.
2. In-depth mechanistic studies to elucidate the precise role of dopants in modifying reaction pathways and energetics.
3. Development of advanced characterization techniques to better understand the nature and distribution of active sites in doped catalysts.
4. Exploration of novel preparation methods to achieve more precise control over dopant incorporation and distribution.
5. Integration of computational modeling with experimental studies to predict and rationalize dopant effects on catalytic performance.

These advancements could lead to the design of next-generation catalysts with unprecedented activity, selectivity, and stability for propane dehydrogenation and other challenging industrial processes.

金属氧化物催化剂中的取代阳离子掺杂是一种强大的技术，用于修改表面化学性质和破坏化学键，对增强催化活性、改变活性中心的性质和位置以及改进工业相关反应具有重要意义。本综述部分着重于这种技术对丙烷脱氢催化剂的机理、表面化学修饰和影响。

表面化学修饰机理：

1. 电子结构改变：用不同价态的阳离子进行取代掺杂可以显著改变催化剂表面的电子性质。例如，用La3+掺杂CeO2会产生电子缺陷，使表面成为更强的路易斯酸，而Ta5+掺杂则产生电子过剩，使表面成为更强的路易斯碱[10.1021/jp211693v]。这种电子修饰影响了催化剂与反应物和中间体相互作用的能力。

2. 非晶相的形成：掺杂可导致表面形成非晶态金属氧化物相，这些相通常作为催化活性位点[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019]。这些非晶区域可能表现出与晶体本体不同的反应活性。

3. 电荷转移：掺杂剂可以作为电子供体或受体，促进催化剂和吸附物种之间的电荷转移。例如，CaO中的Mo掺杂剂可以提供过量电荷转移到吸附的金粒子，而Li掺杂剂可以创造电子陷阱，中断这种电荷流动[10.1021/ja211396]。

4. 氧空位形成：低价掺杂剂可以降低氧空位形成的能量，这对遵循Mars-van Krevelen机理的氧化反应至关重要[10.1021/jp301341]。

表面化学修饰：

1. 酸碱性质：掺杂可以显著改变催化剂表面的路易斯酸碱性质。这影响了吸附物的结合强度和优先结合位点，可能导致催化活性的增强[10.1021/jp211693v]。

2. 吸附物结合：修改后的表面性质可导致吸附物结合强度和构型的变化。例如，在掺杂的CeO2表面上，卤素的结合比未掺杂的表面强得多[10.1021/jp211693v]。

3. 形态控制：掺杂可以影响负载金属粒子的生长和形态。例如，掺杂CaO上的阴离子金倾向于形成2D岛状结构，这是由于界面粘附增强[10.1021/ja211396]。

4. 选择性位点中毒：在某些情况下，掺杂剂（如氯离子）可以选择性地毒化某些活性位点，导致反应活性和选择性的变化[10.1016/j.cej.2007.05.038]。

催化活性增强的影响：

1. 降低活化能垒：掺杂可以降低催化反应中限速步骤的活化能垒。例如，La掺杂CeO2显著降低了甲烷C-H键活化的能量障碍[10.1021/jp211693v]。

2. 提高选择性：通过修改表面性质，掺杂可以增强对目标产物的选择性。例如，氯化物掺杂的催化剂在甲烷氧化偶联反应中表现出更好的选择性[10.1016/j.cej.2007.05.038]。

3. 稳定性增强：适当的掺杂剂选择可以通过抑制烧结过程来提高催化剂的稳定性[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019]。

4. 新反应途径：通过掺杂创造新型活性位点可以开辟替代反应途径，可能导致催化性能的改善[10.1021/jp301341]。

对丙烷脱氢催化剂的影响：

虽然所回顾的文献并未特别针对丙烷脱氢，但取代阳离子掺杂的原理可以应用于这个工业相关反应：

1. 增强C-H键活化：掺杂催化剂促进C-H键断裂的能力，如在甲烷活化研究中所证实的，可能对丙烷脱氢有益[10.1021/jp211693v]。

2. 提高选择性：氯化物掺杂观察到的选择性位点中毒可能被用来增强丙烯选择性，通过抑制不需要的副反应[10.1016/j.cej.2007.05.038]。

3. 定制氧化还原性质：通过仔细选择掺杂剂，可以优化催化剂的氧化还原性质，以平衡脱氢反应和催化剂再生步骤[10.1021/jp301341]。

4. 催化剂稳定性：某些掺杂催化剂观察到的增强热稳定性可能导致在丙烷脱氢典型使用的高温条件下寿命延长[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：金属氧化物催化剂中的取代阳离子掺杂领域为增强丙烷脱氢和其他工业相关反应的催化性能提供了令人兴奋的可能性。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 高通量筛选掺杂剂组合，以确定特定反应的最佳配方。
2. 深入的机理研究，阐明掺杂剂在修改反应途径和能量学方面的确切作用。
3. 开发先进的表征技术，以更好地理解掺杂催化剂中活性位点的性质和分布。
4. 探索新型制备方法，以实现对掺杂剂掺入和分布的更精确控制。
5. 将计算模拟与实验研究相结合，预测和解释掺杂剂对催化性能的影响。

这些进展可能会导致设计出具有前所未有的活性、选择性和稳定性的下一代催化剂，用于丙烷脱氢和其他具有挑战性的工业过程。
