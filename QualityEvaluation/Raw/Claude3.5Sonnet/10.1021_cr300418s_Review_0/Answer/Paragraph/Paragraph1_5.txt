<English>
Substitutional cation doping in metal oxide catalysts significantly modifies surface chemistry and disrupts chemical bonding, leading to profound implications for catalytic activity enhancement, the nature and location of active centers, and potential improvements in industrially relevant reactions. This review section will analyze these effects in the context of propane dehydrogenation catalysts.

Modification of Surface Chemistry:
1. Acid-Base Properties: Doping alters the Lewis acid-base characteristics of the catalyst surface. For instance, La3+ doping in CeO2 creates electron deficiency, making the surface a stronger Lewis acid, while Ta5+ doping creates electron excess, resulting in a stronger Lewis base [10.1021/jp211693v]. This modification affects the binding sites and behavior of adsorbates, with halogens preferentially binding to oxygen sites on La-doped surfaces and to metal sites on Ta-doped surfaces.

2. Electronic Structure: Dopants can act as electron donors or traps, modifying the electronic properties of the surface. For example, Mo dopants in CaO provide excess charges that can be transferred to adsorbed metals like Au, while Li creates electron traps that interrupt charge flow [10.1021/ja211396].

3. Oxygen Vacancy Formation: Low-valence dopants (LVDs) lower the energy of oxygen vacancy formation, which can enhance the catalyst's oxidation capabilities [10.1021/jp301341].

Disruption of Chemical Bonding:
1. Surface Amorphous Phases: Doping creates amorphous metal oxide phases at the surface, which often serve as catalytic active sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019].

2. Bond Strength Modification: The altered Lewis acid-base properties lead to stronger interactions with complementary adsorbates. For instance, halogen binding is considerably stronger on doped surfaces compared to undoped CeO2 [10.1021/jp211693v].

3. Morphology Control: Doping can influence the growth and shape of supported metal particles, affecting their interaction with the oxide support [10.1021/ja211396].

Implications for Catalytic Activity Enhancement:
1. Lowered Activation Barriers: Doping can significantly reduce energy barriers for key steps in catalytic reactions. For example, La-doping in CeO2 lowers the energy barrier for methane C-H bond activation [10.1021/jp211693v].

2. Improved Selectivity: By modifying surface properties, doping can enhance selectivity for desired products. This is exemplified in the case of chloride-doped catalysts, which show improved selectivity for C2+ hydrocarbons in oxidative coupling of methane [10.1016/j.cej.2007.05.038].

3. Enhanced Redox Properties: Less electronegative support cations increase electron density on bridging bonds, enhancing redox activity [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019].

Nature and Location of Active Centers:
1. New Active Sites: Doping creates new types of active centers, often near the dopant sites or in amorphous surface phases [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019].

2. Modified Existing Sites: The electronic and structural changes induced by doping can alter the properties of existing active sites, potentially enhancing their activity or changing their selectivity.

3. Surface-Dependent Activity: The active centers are often surface-dependent, with the doped surface playing a crucial role in catalytic performance [10.1007/bf00810677].

Potential Improvements in Industrially Relevant Reactions:
1. Oxidative Dehydrogenation (ODH): Doping has shown promise in improving catalysts for ODH of ethylbenzene to styrene and ODH of ethane [10.1021/cr300418s_0].

2. Alkane Activation: The enhanced ability to break C-H bonds in methane suggests potential improvements in alkane activation and conversion processes [10.1021/jp211693v].

3. Selective Oxidation: The ability to tune oxygen binding and vacancy formation through doping offers opportunities to improve selective oxidation catalysts [10.1021/jp301341].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of substitutional cation doping in metal oxide catalysts for propane dehydrogenation offers exciting possibilities for catalyst design and optimization. The ability to fine-tune surface properties, electronic structure, and active site characteristics through strategic doping provides a powerful toolbox for enhancing catalytic performance.

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Rational design of multi-component dopant systems to synergistically enhance catalytic properties.
2. In-depth mechanistic studies to elucidate the precise role of dopants in each step of the catalytic cycle.
3. Development of advanced characterization techniques to better understand the nature and distribution of active sites in doped catalysts.
4. Exploration of novel dopant-oxide combinations guided by computational screening and machine learning approaches.
5. Investigation of the long-term stability and regeneration of doped catalysts under industrial conditions.

These advancements could lead to more efficient and selective propane dehydrogenation catalysts, potentially revolutionizing the production of propylene and other valuable chemical intermediates.

在金属氧化物催化剂中进行替代性阳离子掺杂显著改变了表面化学性质并破坏了化学键，这对催化活性增强、活性中心的性质和位置以及潜在改进工业相关反应具有深远的影响。本综述部分将在丙烷脱氢催化剂的背景下分析这些效应。

表面化学的修饰：
1. 酸碱性质：掺杂改变了催化剂表面的Lewis酸碱特性。例如，CeO2中的La3+掺杂会造成电子缺陷，使表面成为更强的Lewis酸，而Ta5+掺杂会产生电子过剩，导致更强的Lewis碱[10.1021/jp211693v]。这种修饰影响了吸附质的结合位点和行为，卤素优先在La掺杂表面与氧位点结合，而在Ta掺杂表面与金属位点结合。

2. 电子结构：掺杂剂可以作为电子供体或陷阱，改变表面的电子性质。例如，CaO中的Mo掺杂剂提供了可以转移到吸附金属（如Au）的过剩电荷，而Li则创造了中断电荷流动的电子陷阱[10.1021/ja211396]。

3. 氧空位形成：低价掺杂剂（LVDs）降低了氧空位形成的能量，这可以增强催化剂的氧化能力[10.1021/jp301341]。

化学键的破坏：
1. 表面非晶相：掺杂在表面创造了非晶金属氧化物相，这些相通常作为催化活性位点[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019]。

2. 键强度修饰：改变的Lewis酸碱性质导致与互补吸附质的相互作用更强。例如，相比未掺杂的CeO2，卤素在掺杂表面的结合强度显著增强[10.1021/jp211693v]。

3. 形态控制：掺杂可以影响负载金属粒子的生长和形状，影响其与氧化物支撑体的相互作用[10.1021/ja211396]。

对催化活性增强的影响：
1. 降低活化能垒：掺杂可以显著降低催化反应关键步骤的能量障碍。例如，CeO2中的La掺杂降低了甲烷C-H键活化的能量障碍[10.1021/jp211693v]。

2. 提高选择性：通过修改表面性质，掺杂可以提高对目标产物的选择性。这在氯化物掺杂催化剂中得到了体现，在甲烷氧化偶联反应中表现出对C2+烃类的选择性提高[10.1016/j.cej.2007.05.038]。

3. 增强氧化还原性质：电负性较低的支撑阳离子增加了桥键上的电子密度，增强了氧化还原活性[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019]。

活性中心的性质和位置：
1. 新的活性位点：掺杂创造了新型活性中心，通常位于掺杂剂位点附近或非晶表面相中[10.1016/j.cattod.2004.12.019]。

2. 修饰现有位点：掺杂引起的电子和结构变化可以改变现有活性位点的性质，可能增强其活性或改变其选择性。

3. 表面依赖性活性：活性中心通常是表面依赖的，掺杂表面在催化性能中起着关键作用[10.1007/bf00810677]。

工业相关反应的潜在改进：
1. 氧化脱氢（ODH）：掺杂在改进乙苯到苯乙烯和乙烷的ODH催化剂方面显示出了潜力[10.1021/cr300418s_0]。

2. 烷烃活化：增强断裂甲烷C-H键的能力表明在烷烃活化和转化过程中可能有所改进[10.1021/jp211693v]。

3. 选择性氧化：通过掺杂调节氧结合和空位形成的能力为改进选择性氧化催化剂提供了机会[10.1021/jp301341]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：丙烷脱氢金属氧化物催化剂中的替代性阳离子掺杂领域为催化剂设计和优化提供了令人兴奋的可能性。通过战略性掺杂来精细调节表面性质、电子结构和活性位点特性的能力为增强催化性能提供了强大的工具箱。

未来研究方向可能集中在：

1. 设计多组分掺杂系统，以协同增强催化性能。
2. 深入的机理研究，阐明掺杂剂在催化循环每个步骤中的精确作用。
3. 开发先进的表征技术，以更好地理解掺杂催化剂中活性位点的性质和分布。
4. 在计算筛选和机器学习方法指导下探索新型掺杂剂-氧化物组合。
5. 研究掺杂催化剂在工业条件下的长期稳定性和再生。

这些进展可能导致更高效和选择性的丙烷脱氢催化剂，潜在地革新丙烯和其他有价值化学中间体的生产。
