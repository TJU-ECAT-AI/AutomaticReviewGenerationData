<English>
Coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts remains a crucial challenge for commercial applications, particularly in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) processes. Our understanding of this phenomenon has evolved significantly, revealing the complexity of coke formation mechanisms and the need for innovative strategies to mitigate its detrimental effects [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

The primary challenges associated with coke deposition include:

1. Complexity of coke formation: The structure, composition, and location of cokes are intricately interrelated, with many details still under debate [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

2. Lack of molecular-level understanding: There is a scarcity of investigations into coke kinetics at the molecular level and transformations between disordered and graphitic coke, hindering the development of effective suppression methods [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

3. Inevitable nature of coke formation: Despite various prevention methods, complete elimination of coke formation in PDH processes remains elusive [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

To address these challenges, researchers have developed several strategies:

1. Prevention methods:
   - Cofeeding gas (particularly hydrogen)
   - Metal promoters
   - Support modification
These approaches have shown effectiveness in alleviating coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

2. Novel catalyst designs:
   - Single atom/site strategy: This approach exploits the different size dependence of dehydrogenation and side reactions in PDH [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].
   - Confined subnanosized Pt clusters in zeolites: These catalysts exhibit excellent resistance to coke formation due to unique interactions between the catalyst and support [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].
   - Single-atom alloys (SAAs): Pt/Cu SAAs have demonstrated excellent CO tolerance and selective CH activation while preventing coking. They exhibit intermediate barriers for CH activation, allowing them to remain active but avoid carbon buildup [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].

3. Geometry optimization of active sites: The geometry of the active site has been verified as a crucial factor in controlling coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

4. Promoting desorption: Pt/Cu SAAs can promote the desorption of surface-bonded intermediates like propylene, preventing further dehydrogenation that leads to coke formation. This results in high propylene selectivity (∼90%) [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].

5. Strong metal-support interactions: Pt single atoms anchored on supports like Co3O4 or defective TiO2 through strong electronic metal-support interactions have shown enhanced activity for specific reactions [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].

6. Coke migration: Facilitating coke transfer from active metal to support is a feasible way to maintain catalyst activity [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

Despite these advancements, challenges remain:
1. Optimal promoter selection
2. Understanding and controlling coke migration
3. Developing detailed molecular-level kinetic models
4. Improving long-term stability under industrial conditions
5. Developing scalable synthesis methods for novel catalyst designs

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Taking into consideration the advancements and findings discussed in the file, there lies an opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies. Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques: Developing and applying cutting-edge spectroscopic and microscopic methods to study coke formation and migration in real-time under reaction conditions.

2. Machine learning and artificial intelligence: Utilizing these tools to predict optimal catalyst compositions, geometries, and operating conditions for minimizing coke formation while maintaining high activity and selectivity.

3. Multifunctional catalyst design: Combining multiple strategies, such as SAAs with optimized support materials and promoters, to create synergistic effects for coke resistance and improved catalytic performance.

4. Green regeneration methods: Exploring environmentally friendly and energy-efficient techniques for catalyst regeneration, potentially incorporating renewable energy sources or novel oxidation methods.

5. Molecular-level modeling: Developing comprehensive computational models that can accurately predict coke formation kinetics and transformations between different types of coke at the atomic scale.

6. Tailored coke management: Designing catalysts that can selectively form less detrimental types of coke or promote controlled coke migration to non-critical areas of the catalyst surface.

These research directions aim to address the remaining challenges in understanding and mitigating coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts, ultimately leading to more efficient and sustainable PDH processes.

<Chinese>
铂基催化剂中的积碳沉积仍然是商业应用的一个关键挑战，特别是在丙烷脱氢（PDH）过程中。我们对这一现象的理解已经显著发展，揭示了积碳形成机制的复杂性以及需要创新策略来减轻其有害影响 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

与积碳沉积相关的主要挑战包括：

1. 积碳形成的复杂性：积碳的结构、组成和位置复杂地相互关联，许多细节仍存在争议 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

2. 缺乏分子水平的理解：对分子水平的积碳动力学和无序积碳与石墨化积碳之间的转化研究很少，阻碍了有效抑制方法的开发 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

3. 积碳形成的不可避免性：尽管采取了各种预防方法，在PDH过程中完全消除积碳形成仍然难以实现 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

为了应对这些挑战，研究人员开发了几种策略：

1. 预防方法：
   - 共进料气体（特别是氢气）
   - 金属促进剂
   - 载体改性
这些方法在减轻积碳形成方面显示了有效性 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

2. 新型催化剂设计：
   - 单原子/单位点策略：这种方法利用了PDH中脱氢反应和副反应对尺寸的不同依赖性 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。
   - 沸石中的限域亚纳米铂簇：由于催化剂和载体之间的独特相互作用，这些催化剂表现出优异的抗积碳性能 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。
   - 单原子合金（SAAs）：Pt/Cu SAAs表现出优异的CO耐受性和选择性CH活化，同时防止积碳。它们表现出CH活化的中等能垒，使其保持活性但避免碳积累 [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。

3. 活性位点几何优化：活性位点的几何结构已被证实是控制积碳形成的关键因素 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

4. 促进脱附：Pt/Cu SAAs可以促进表面结合中间体（如丙烯）的脱附，防止进一步脱氢导致积碳形成。这导致了高丙烯选择性（约90%）[10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。

5. 强金属-载体相互作用：通过强电子金属-载体相互作用锚定在Co3O4或缺陷TiO2载体上的Pt单原子对特定反应表现出增强的活性 [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。

6. 积碳迁移：促进积碳从活性金属向载体转移是保持催化剂活性的可行方法 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

尽管取得了这些进展，仍然存在一些挑战：
1. 最佳促进剂的选择
2. 理解和控制积碳迁移
3. 开发详细的分子水平动力学模型
4. 提高工业条件下的长期稳定性
5. 开发新型催化剂设计的可扩展合成方法

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：考虑到文件中讨论的先进成果和发现，我们有机会探索新兴领域和创新方法。未来的研究努力可能会集中在：

1. 先进的原位表征技术：开发和应用先进的光谱和显微技术，在反应条件下实时研究积碳形成和迁移。

2. 机器学习和人工智能：利用这些工具预测最佳催化剂组成、几何结构和操作条件，以最小化积碳形成，同时保持高活性和选择性。

3. 多功能催化剂设计：结合多种策略，如SAAs与优化的载体材料和促进剂，创造协同效应以实现抗积碳和改善催化性能。

4. 绿色再生方法：探索环境友好和能源高效的催化剂再生技术，可能结合可再生能源或新型氧化方法。

5. 分子水平建模：开发全面的计算模型，能够准确预测原子尺度上的积碳形成动力学和不同类型积碳之间的转化。

6. 定制积碳管理：设计能选择性形成危害较小类型积碳或促进积碳向催化剂表面非关键区域控制迁移的催化剂。

这些研究方向旨在解决理解和减轻铂基催化剂中积碳沉积的剩余挑战，最终导致更高效和可持续的PDH过程。
