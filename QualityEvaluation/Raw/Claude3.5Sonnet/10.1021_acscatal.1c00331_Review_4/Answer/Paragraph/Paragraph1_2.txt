<English>
Coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts has been recognized as a critical issue for commercial catalysts, particularly in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) processes. Our understanding of this phenomenon has evolved significantly, revealing the complexity and interrelated nature of coke formation factors, including structure, composition, and location [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. The primary challenges associated with coke deposition include the lack of molecular-level understanding of coke kinetics and transformations between disordered and graphitic coke, as well as the inevitable nature of coke formation in PDH processes [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

To address these challenges, various strategies have been developed and implemented:

1. Prevention methods:
   - Cofeeding gas, particularly hydrogen, has been verified as an effective method to prevent coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].
   - Metal promoters and support modifications have also shown promise in mitigating coke deposition [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

2. Novel catalyst designs:
   - Single atom/site strategy: This approach exploits the different size dependence of dehydrogenation and side reactions in PDH [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].
   - Confined subnanosized Pt clusters in zeolites: These catalysts, deposited at particular positions with unique catalyst-support interactions, exhibit excellent resistance to coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].
   - Geometry optimization of active sites: The geometry of the active site has been verified as a crucial factor in controlling coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

3. Single-atom alloys (SAAs):
   - Pt/Cu SAAs have demonstrated excellent CO tolerance and selective CH activation while preventing coking [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].
   - These SAAs exhibit intermediate barriers for CH activation, allowing them to remain active while avoiding carbon buildup [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].
   - Pt/Cu SAAs promote the desorption of surface-bonded propylene, prohibiting further dehydrogenation and resulting in high propylene selectivity (≈90%) [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].

4. Strong metal-support interactions:
   - Pt single atoms anchored on supports like Co3O4 or defective TiO2 through strong electronic metal-support interactions have shown enhanced activity for specific reactions [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].

5. Coke migration:
   - Facilitating coke transfer from the active metal to the support has been identified as a feasible way to maintain catalyst activity [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

6. Regeneration techniques:
   - While still necessary to prolong catalyst life, efforts are focused on lowering regeneration temperatures [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

Despite the effectiveness of these strategies in alleviating coke formation, several challenges remain:

1. Optimal promoter selection for specific catalytic systems.
2. Understanding and controlling coke migration mechanisms.
3. Developing detailed molecular-level kinetic models for coke formation and transformation.
4. Improving long-term stability of novel catalyst designs under industrial conditions.
5. Developing scalable synthesis methods for advanced catalysts like SAAs.
6. Understanding the fundamental mechanisms of coke resistance in novel catalyst designs.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements and findings discussed, future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to study coke formation and migration at the molecular level during catalytic reactions.
2. Utilizing machine learning and artificial intelligence to predict optimal catalyst compositions and structures for minimizing coke formation while maintaining high activity and selectivity.
3. Exploring the synergistic effects of combining multiple coke mitigation strategies, such as integrating SAAs with zeolite confinement or novel support materials.
4. Investigating the potential of dynamic catalyst systems that can adapt to changing reaction conditions to minimize coke formation throughout the catalytic cycle.
5. Developing novel regeneration techniques specifically tailored for advanced catalyst designs, focusing on maintaining the unique structural and electronic properties of these catalysts.
6. Expanding the application of successful coke mitigation strategies to other catalytic processes beyond propane dehydrogenation, potentially revolutionizing a broader range of industrial chemical processes.

These future directions aim to address the remaining challenges in understanding and mitigating coke deposition, ultimately leading to more efficient and sustainable catalytic processes in the chemical industry.

<Chinese>
铂基催化剂中的积碳沉积被认为是商业催化剂的一个关键问题，特别是在丙烷脱氢（PDH）过程中。我们对这一现象的理解已经显著发展，揭示了积碳形成因素的复杂性和相互关联性，包括结构、成分和位置 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。与积碳沉积相关的主要挑战包括缺乏对积碳动力学和无序积碳与石墨化积碳之间转化的分子水平理解，以及PDH过程中积碳形成的不可避免性 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

为了应对这些挑战，已经开发并实施了各种策略：

1. 预防方法：
   - 共进料气体，特别是氢气，已被证实是防止积碳形成的有效方法 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。
   - 金属助剂和载体改性也在减少积碳沉积方面显示出前景 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

2. 新型催化剂设计：
   - 单原子/位点策略：该方法利用了PDH中脱氢反应和副反应的不同尺寸依赖性 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。
   - 沸石中的限制亚纳米铂簇：这些催化剂在特定位置沉积，具有独特的催化剂-载体相互作用，表现出优异的抗积碳形成能力 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。
   - 活性位点几何优化：活性位点的几何结构已被证实是控制积碳形成的关键因素 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

3. 单原子合金（SAAs）：
   - Pt/Cu SAAs在H2活化中表现出优异的CO耐受性和选择性CH活化，同时防止积碳 [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。
   - 这些SAAs表现出CH活化的中等能垒，使其保持活性同时避免碳积累 [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。
   - Pt/Cu SAAs促进表面结合丙烯的脱附，抑制进一步脱氢，从而获得高丙烯选择性（约90%）[10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。

4. 强金属-载体相互作用：
   - 通过强电子金属-载体相互作用锚定在Co3O4或缺陷TiO2载体上的Pt单原子表现出对特定反应的增强活性 [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。

5. 积碳迁移：
   - 促进积碳从活性金属向载体转移被认为是保持催化剂活性的可行方法 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

6. 再生技术：
   - 虽然仍然需要延长催化剂寿命，但努力集中在降低再生温度上 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

尽管这些策略在减少积碳形成方面很有效，但仍然存在几个挑战：

1. 为特定催化体系选择最佳助剂。
2. 理解和控制积碳迁移机制。
3. 开发详细的分子水平积碳形成和转化动力学模型。
4. 改善新型催化剂设计在工业条件下的长期稳定性。
5. 开发先进催化剂（如SAAs）的可扩展合成方法。
6. 理解新型催化剂设计中抗积碳的基本机制。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：考虑到讨论的先进成果和发现，未来的研究努力可能会集中在：

1. 开发原位表征技术，在催化反应过程中研究分子水平的积碳形成和迁移。
2. 利用机器学习和人工智能预测最佳催化剂成分和结构，以最小化积碳形成，同时保持高活性和选择性。
3. 探索结合多种积碳缓解策略的协同效应，例如将SAAs与沸石限制或新型载体材料相结合。
4. 研究可以适应不断变化的反应条件的动态催化剂系统的潜力，以在整个催化循环中最小化积碳形成。
5. 开发专门针对先进催化剂设计的新型再生技术，重点是保持这些催化剂的独特结构和电子特性。
6. 将成功的积碳缓解策略扩展到丙烷脱氢以外的其他催化过程，有可能革新更广泛的工业化学过程。

这些未来方向旨在解决理解和缓解积碳沉积方面的剩余挑战，最终导致化学工业中更高效和可持续的催化过程。
