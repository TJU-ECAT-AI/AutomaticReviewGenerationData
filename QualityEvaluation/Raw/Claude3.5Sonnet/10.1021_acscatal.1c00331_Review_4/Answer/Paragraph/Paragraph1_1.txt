<English>
Coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) has been recognized as a crucial issue affecting the performance and longevity of commercial catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. The complexity of coke formation, involving interrelated factors such as structure, composition, and location, presents significant challenges in developing effective mitigation strategies [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. Moreover, the lack of molecular-level understanding of coke kinetics and transformations between disordered and graphitic coke hinders the discovery of more efficient methods to suppress coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

To address these challenges, researchers have developed various strategies:

1. Prevention methods:
   Cofeeding gas (particularly hydrogen), metal promoters, and support modification have been verified as effective methods to prevent coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. These approaches aim to alter the reaction environment or catalyst properties to minimize coke precursor formation.

2. Novel catalyst designs:
   The single atom/site strategy has successfully exploited the different size dependence of dehydrogenation and side reactions in PDH [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. Confined subnanosized Pt clusters in zeolites, deposited at particular positions with unique catalyst-support interactions, have exhibited excellent resistance to coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. Additionally, the geometry of active sites has been verified as a crucial factor in controlling coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

   Single-atom alloys (SAAs) have emerged as a promising approach. Pt/Cu SAAs with an atomic ratio of Pt:Cu = 1:1.25 have shown excellent CO tolerance in H2 activation [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]. These SAAs exhibit intermediate barriers for CH activation, maintaining activity while preventing coking [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]. Furthermore, Pt/Cu SAAs promote the desorption of surface-bonded propylene, inhibiting further dehydrogenation and resulting in high propylene selectivity of approximately 90% [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].

3. Strong metal-support interactions:
   Pt single atoms anchored on supports like Co3O4 through strong electronic metal-support interactions have demonstrated enhanced activity for specific reactions, such as the dehydrogenation of ammonia borane [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]. Similarly, single Pt atoms anchored on defective TiO2 support, due to organized PtOTi3+ coordination, exhibited unprecedentedly high photocatalytic hydrogen evolution rates [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].

4. Coke migration:
   Facilitating coke transfer from the active metal to the support has been identified as a feasible way to maintain catalyst activity [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. This approach allows for the continuous operation of the catalyst by preventing the accumulation of coke on active sites.

5. Regeneration techniques:
   Although prevention methods have shown promise in alleviating coke formation, regeneration is still necessary to prolong catalyst life [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. Current efforts focus on lowering regeneration temperatures to minimize thermal stress on the catalyst.

Despite these advancements, several challenges remain:
1. Optimal promoter selection for different catalyst systems and reaction conditions.
2. Understanding and controlling coke migration mechanisms.
3. Developing detailed molecular-level kinetic models to predict and prevent coke formation.
4. Improving the long-term stability of novel catalyst designs under industrial conditions.
5. Developing scalable synthesis methods for advanced catalysts like SAAs.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of our understanding of coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts has led to significant advancements in catalyst design and mitigation strategies. However, there are still numerous opportunities for further research and development in this field.

Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques: Developing and applying cutting-edge spectroscopic and microscopic methods to study coke formation and migration in real-time under reaction conditions. This could provide unprecedented insights into the molecular-level mechanisms of coke formation and transformation.

2. Machine learning and artificial intelligence: Utilizing these tools to analyze large datasets of catalyst performance, composition, and reaction conditions to identify optimal catalyst designs and operating parameters for minimizing coke formation.

3. Multiscale modeling: Integrating molecular simulations with reactor-scale models to predict coke formation and catalyst deactivation over extended periods, enabling the design of more robust and long-lasting catalysts.

4. Hybrid materials: Exploring the synergistic effects of combining different catalyst designs, such as incorporating single-atom sites into zeolite frameworks or developing hierarchical porous structures to optimize mass transport and coke resistance.

5. Green regeneration methods: Investigating environmentally friendly and energy-efficient regeneration techniques, such as plasma-assisted or electrochemical regeneration, to reduce the carbon footprint of catalyst recycling processes.

6. Catalyst design for circular economy: Developing catalysts that not only resist coke formation but also facilitate the conversion of deposited carbon into valuable products, turning a waste product into a resource.

These research directions have the potential to revolutionize the field of propane dehydrogenation catalysis, leading to more efficient, stable, and sustainable processes for the production of valuable chemical intermediates.

<Chinese>
在丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应中，铂基催化剂上的积碳沉积被认为是影响商业催化剂性能和寿命的关键问题[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。积碳形成的复杂性，涉及结构、成分和位置等相互关联的因素，在开发有效缓解策略方面带来了重大挑战[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。此外，缺乏对积碳动力学和无序积碳与石墨化积碳之间转化的分子水平理解，阻碍了发现更有效抑制积碳形成方法的进程[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

为了应对这些挑战，研究人员开发了各种策略：

1. 预防方法：
   共进料气体（特别是氢气）、金属助剂和载体改性已被证实是有效防止积碳形成的方法[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。这些方法旨在改变反应环境或催化剂性质，以最大程度地减少积碳前体的形成。

2. 新型催化剂设计：
   单原子/单位点策略成功地利用了PDH中脱氢和副反应的不同尺寸依赖性[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。在沸石中限制的亚纳米级铂团簇，由于催化剂与载体之间的独特相互作用，可以沉积在特定位置，表现出优异的抗积碳性能[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。此外，活性位点的几何构型已被证实是控制积碳形成的关键因素[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

   单原子合金（SAAs）已成为一种有前景的方法。原子比为Pt:Cu = 1:1.25的Pt/Cu SAAs在H2活化中表现出优异的CO耐受性[10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。这些SAAs表现出CH活化的中等能垒，保持活性的同时防止积碳[10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。此外，Pt/Cu SAAs促进了表面结合丙烯的脱附，抑制了进一步的脱氢，从而获得约90%的高丙烯选择性[10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。

3. 强金属-载体相互作用：
   通过强电子金属-载体相互作用锚定在Co3O4等载体上的Pt单原子表现出增强的活性，例如在氨硼烷脱氢反应中[10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。同样，由于有组织的PtOTi3+配位，锚定在缺陷TiO2载体上的单Pt原子表现出前所未有的高光催化产氢速率[10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。

4. 积碳迁移：
   促进积碳从活性金属向载体转移被认为是保持催化剂活性的可行方法[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。这种方法通过防止活性位点上积碳的积累，允许催化剂持续运行。

5. 再生技术：
   尽管预防方法在减轻积碳形成方面显示出前景，但再生仍然是延长催化剂寿命所必需的[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。目前的努力集中在降低再生温度，以最大程度地减少对催化剂的热应力。

尽管取得了这些进展，仍然存在几个挑战：
1. 为不同催化剂系统和反应条件选择最佳助剂。
2. 理解和控制积碳迁移机制。
3. 开发详细的分子水平动力学模型，以预测和防止积碳形成。
4. 提高新型催化剂设计在工业条件下的长期稳定性。
5. 开发先进催化剂（如SAAs）的可扩展合成方法。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：我们对铂基催化剂积碳沉积的理解演变已经导致了催化剂设计和缓解策略的重大进展。然而，在这个领域仍然存在许多进一步研究和开发的机会。

未来的研究努力可能会集中在：

1. 先进的原位表征技术：开发和应用尖端的光谱和显微技术，在反应条件下实时研究积碳形成和迁移。这可能会为积碳形成和转化的分子水平机制提供前所未有的见解。

2. 机器学习和人工智能：利用这些工具分析催化剂性能、组成和反应条件的大型数据集，以确定最佳催化剂设计和操作参数，以最大程度地减少积碳形成。

3. 多尺度建模：将分子模拟与反应器尺度模型相结合，预测长期积碳形成和催化剂失活，从而设计更加稳健和长寿命的催化剂。

4. 混合材料：探索不同催化剂设计的协同效应，例如将单原子位点incorporation到沸石框架中，或开发分级多孔结构以优化质量传输和抗积碳性能。

5. 绿色再生方法：研究环保和节能的再生技术，如等离子体辅助或电化学再生，以减少催化剂回收过程的碳足迹。

6. 循环经济催化剂设计：开发不仅能抵抗积碳形成，而且能促进沉积碳转化为有价值产品的催化剂，将废物转化为资源。

这些研究方向有潜力彻底改变丙烷脱氢催化领域，导致更高效、更稳定和更可持续的有价值化学中间体生产过程。
