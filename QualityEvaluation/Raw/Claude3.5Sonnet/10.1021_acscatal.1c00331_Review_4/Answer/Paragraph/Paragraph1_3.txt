<English>
Coke deposition remains a crucial challenge for commercial Pt-based catalysts in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) processes, necessitating significant efforts to understand and mitigate its detrimental effects [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. The complexity of coke formation, involving interrelated factors such as structure, composition, and location, presents a formidable obstacle to developing effective prevention strategies. Moreover, the scarcity of molecular-level investigations into coke kinetics and transformations between disordered and graphitic coke further hinders progress in this field [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

To address these challenges, researchers have explored various strategies:

1. Prevention methods: Cofeeding gas (particularly hydrogen), metal promoters, and support modification have been verified as effective methods to prevent coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. These approaches aim to alter the reaction environment or catalyst properties to minimize carbon accumulation.

2. Novel catalyst designs: The single atom/site strategy has wisely exploited the different size dependence of dehydrogenation and side reactions in PDH. Confined subnanosized Pt clusters in zeolites, deposited at particular positions with unique catalyst-support interactions, have exhibited excellent resistance to coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. Additionally, the geometry of the active site has been verified as a crucial factor in controlling coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

3. Single-atom alloys (SAAs): Pt/Cu SAAs have shown promising results in balancing activity and coke resistance. With an atomic ratio of Pt:Cu = 1:1.25, these catalysts exhibit excellent CO tolerance in H2 activation and intermediate barriers for CH activation, allowing them to remain active while preventing coking [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]. Furthermore, Pt/Cu SAAs promote the desorption of surface-bonded propylene, inhibiting further dehydrogenation and resulting in high propylene selectivity (~90%) [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].

4. Strong metal-support interactions: Pt single atoms anchored on supports like Co3O4 or defective TiO2 through strong electronic metal-support interactions have demonstrated enhanced activity for specific reactions, such as the dehydrogenation of ammonia borane and photocatalytic hydrogen evolution [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].

5. Coke migration: Facilitating coke transfer from the active metal to the support has been identified as a feasible way to maintain catalyst activity [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

Despite these advancements, challenges persist. The inevitable nature of coke formation in PDH processes necessitates ongoing research into regeneration techniques, albeit with a focus on lowering regeneration temperatures [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. Additionally, optimal promoter selection, understanding and controlling coke migration, and developing detailed molecular-level kinetic models remain areas requiring further investigation.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements and findings discussed, future research endeavors in the field of coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts for PDH should focus on:

1. Developing advanced in-situ characterization techniques to elucidate the molecular-level mechanisms of coke formation, migration, and transformation between disordered and graphitic forms.

2. Exploring novel combinations of single-atom alloys and support materials to optimize the balance between catalytic activity and coke resistance.

3. Investigating the potential of machine learning algorithms to predict optimal catalyst compositions and geometries for specific reaction conditions.

4. Designing intelligent regeneration strategies that can be integrated into continuous PDH processes, minimizing downtime and extending catalyst lifetime.

5. Exploring the synergistic effects of combining multiple coke mitigation strategies, such as SAAs with tailored support modifications and controlled coke migration pathways.

6. Developing multi-scale modeling approaches that bridge the gap between molecular-level coke formation mechanisms and macroscopic catalyst performance in industrial reactors.

These research directions aim to address the remaining challenges in understanding and mitigating coke deposition, ultimately leading to the development of more efficient and durable Pt-based catalysts for propane dehydrogenation and related processes.

<Chinese>
焦炭沉积仍然是商业铂基催化剂在丙烷脱氢（PDH）过程中的一个关键挑战，需要做出重大努力来理解和减轻其有害影响[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。焦炭形成的复杂性，涉及结构、组成和位置等相互关联的因素，为开发有效的预防策略带来了巨大障碍。此外，分子水平上对焦炭动力学和无序焦炭与石墨焦炭之间转化的研究匮乏，进一步阻碍了该领域的进展[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

为应对这些挑战，研究人员探索了各种策略：

1. 预防方法：共进料气体（特别是氢气）、金属助剂和载体改性已被验证为有效的防止焦炭形成的方法[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。这些方法旨在改变反应环境或催化剂性质以最小化碳积累。

2. 新型催化剂设计：单原子/位点策略巧妙地利用了PDH中脱氢和副反应对尺寸的不同依赖性。在沸石中限制的亚纳米尺寸铂簇，通过催化剂与载体之间的独特相互作用沉积在特定位置，表现出优异的抗焦炭形成能力[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。此外，活性位点的几何结构已被证实是控制焦炭形成的关键因素[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

3. 单原子合金（SAAs）：Pt/Cu SAAs在平衡活性和抗焦炭能力方面显示出了promising结果。原子比为Pt:Cu = 1:1.25的这些催化剂在H2活化中表现出优异的CO耐受性，并在CH活化中表现出中等的能垒，使它们能够保持活性同时防止结焦[10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。此外，Pt/Cu SAAs促进了表面结合丙烯的脱附，抑制了进一步的脱氢，从而导致高丙烯选择性（~90%）[10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。

4. 强金属-载体相互作用：通过强电子金属-载体相互作用锚定在Co3O4或缺陷TiO2等载体上的Pt单原子，已经证明在特定反应（如氨硼烷的脱氢和光催化产氢）中具有增强的活性[10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。

5. 焦炭迁移：促进焦炭从活性金属向载体转移被确定为保持催化剂活性的可行方法[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

尽管取得了这些进展，挑战仍然存在。PDH过程中焦炭形成的不可避免性使得对再生技术的研究仍在继续，尽管重点是降低再生温度[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。此外，最佳助剂选择、理解和控制焦炭迁移以及开发详细的分子水平动力学模型仍然是需要进一步研究的领域。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：考虑到讨论的进展和发现，PDH铂基催化剂焦炭沉积领域的未来研究努力应该集中在：

1. 开发先进的原位表征技术，以阐明焦炭形成、迁移以及无序和石墨形式之间转化的分子水平机制。

2. 探索单原子合金和载体材料的新组合，以优化催化活性和抗焦炭能力之间的平衡。

3. 研究机器学习算法在预测特定反应条件下最佳催化剂成分和几何结构方面的潜力。

4. 设计可以集成到连续PDH过程中的智能再生策略，最小化停机时间并延长催化剂寿命。

5. 探索组合多种焦炭缓解策略的协同效应，例如将SAAs与定制的载体改性和控制的焦炭迁移路径相结合。

6. 开发多尺度建模方法，弥合分子水平焦炭形成机制与工业反应器中宏观催化剂性能之间的差距。

这些研究方向旨在解决理解和缓解焦炭沉积方面的剩余挑战，最终导致开发出更高效、更耐用的丙烷脱氢及相关过程的铂基催化剂。
