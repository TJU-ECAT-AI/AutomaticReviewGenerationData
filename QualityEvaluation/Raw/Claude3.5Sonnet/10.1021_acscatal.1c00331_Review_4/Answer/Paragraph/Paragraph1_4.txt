<English>
Coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts remains a critical challenge in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) processes, significantly impacting catalyst performance and longevity. Our understanding of this phenomenon has evolved substantially, recognizing it as a complex issue with interrelated factors such as structure, composition, and location of coke deposits [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. The primary challenges in addressing coke deposition include its inherent complexity, the lack of molecular-level understanding, and the inevitable nature of coke formation in PDH processes [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

To mitigate these challenges, researchers have developed various strategies:

1. Prevention methods:
   - Cofeeding gas, particularly hydrogen, has been verified as an effective method to prevent coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].
   - Metal promoters and support modifications have shown promise in alleviating coke deposition [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

2. Novel catalyst designs:
   - Single atom/site strategy: This approach exploits the different size dependence of dehydrogenation and side reactions in PDH [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. For instance, Pt/Cu single-atom alloys (SAAs) have demonstrated excellent CO tolerance and selective CH activation while preventing coking [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].
   - Confined subnanosized Pt clusters in zeolites: These catalysts, deposited at particular positions with unique catalyst-support interactions, exhibit excellent resistance to coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].
   - Geometry optimization of active sites: The geometry of the active site has been verified as a crucial factor in controlling coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

3. Regeneration techniques:
   - While still necessary to prolong catalyst life, efforts focus on lowering regeneration temperatures [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

4. Coke migration:
   - Facilitating coke transfer from the active metal to the support has been identified as a feasible way to maintain activity [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

These strategies have shown effectiveness in alleviating coke formation and maintaining high selectivity. For example, Pt/Cu SAAs can promote the desorption of surface-bonded propylene and prohibit its further dehydrogenation, resulting in high propylene selectivity (~90%) [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].

However, several challenges remain:
1. Optimal promoter selection for different reaction conditions.
2. Understanding and controlling coke migration mechanisms.
3. Developing detailed molecular-level kinetic models, particularly for the transformation between disordered and graphitic coke [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of our understanding of coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts has opened up new avenues for research and development. Future efforts may focus on:

1. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques to elucidate the real-time formation and migration of coke deposits at the molecular level.
2. Computational modeling to predict coke formation mechanisms and guide the design of novel catalyst structures.
3. Exploring hybrid approaches that combine multiple strategies, such as SAAs with optimized support materials, to synergistically combat coke deposition.
4. Developing "smart" catalysts that can self-regenerate or actively resist coke formation through dynamic structural changes.
5. Investigating the potential of emerging materials, such as 2D materials or metal-organic frameworks, as supports or co-catalysts to enhance coke resistance.
6. Studying the long-term stability and scalability of novel catalyst designs under industrial conditions to bridge the gap between laboratory research and practical applications.

These research directions could potentially lead to breakthroughs in catalyst design, significantly improving the efficiency and sustainability of propane dehydrogenation processes and other related catalytic applications.

<Chinese>
铂基催化剂中的积碳沉积仍然是丙烷脱氢（PDH）过程中的一个关键挑战，显著影响催化剂的性能和寿命。我们对这一现象的理解已经有了实质性的发展，认识到它是一个复杂的问题，涉及积碳沉积物的结构、成分和位置等相互关联的因素[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。解决积碳沉积问题的主要挑战包括其固有的复杂性、缺乏分子水平的理解，以及在PDH过程中积碳形成的不可避免性[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

为了缓解这些挑战，研究人员开发了各种策略：

1. 预防方法：
   - 共进料气体，特别是氢气，已被证实是防止积碳形成的有效方法[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。
   - 金属助剂和载体改性已显示出减轻积碳沉积的潜力[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

2. 新型催化剂设计：
   - 单原子/位点策略：这种方法利用了PDH中脱氢和副反应的不同尺寸依赖性[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。例如，Pt/Cu单原子合金（SAAs）已经展示了优异的CO耐受性和选择性CH活化，同时防止积碳[10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。
   - 沸石中限制的亚纳米级Pt簇：这些催化剂在特定位置沉积，与载体具有独特的相互作用，表现出优异的抗积碳形成能力[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。
   - 活性位点几何结构优化：活性位点的几何结构已被证实是控制积碳形成的关键因素[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

3. 再生技术：
   - 虽然仍然需要延长催化剂寿命，但努力集中在降低再生温度上[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

4. 积碳迁移：
   - 促进积碳从活性金属向载体转移被认为是保持活性的可行方法[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

这些策略在减轻积碳形成和保持高选择性方面已显示出效果。例如，Pt/Cu SAAs可以促进表面结合丙烯的脱附并抑制其进一步脱氢，从而获得高丙烯选择性（~90%）[10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。

然而，仍然存在几个挑战：
1. 针对不同反应条件选择最佳助剂。
2. 理解和控制积碳迁移机制。
3. 开发详细的分子水平动力学模型，特别是无序积碳和石墨化积碳之间的转化[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：我们对铂基催化剂中积碳沉积的理解的演变为研究和开发开辟了新的途径。未来的努力可能集中在：

1. 先进的原位表征技术，以阐明分子水平上积碳沉积物的实时形成和迁移。
2. 计算模拟以预测积碳形成机制并指导新型催化剂结构的设计。
3. 探索结合多种策略的混合方法，如将SAAs与优化的载体材料结合，以协同对抗积碳沉积。
4. 开发能够通过动态结构变化自我再生或主动抵抗积碳形成的"智能"催化剂。
5. 研究新兴材料（如二维材料或金属有机框架）作为载体或共催化剂以增强抗积碳能力的潜力。
6. 在工业条件下研究新型催化剂设计的长期稳定性和可扩展性，以弥合实验室研究和实际应用之间的差距。

这些研究方向可能会在催化剂设计方面取得突破，显著提高丙烷脱氢过程和其他相关催化应用的效率和可持续性。
