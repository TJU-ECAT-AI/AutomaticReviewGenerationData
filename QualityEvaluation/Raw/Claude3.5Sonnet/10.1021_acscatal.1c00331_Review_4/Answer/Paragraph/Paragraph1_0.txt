Coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts: Understanding mechanisms, developing mitigation strategies, and advancing catalyst design for improved performance and longevity

Coke deposition remains a crucial issue for commercial Pt-based catalysts, particularly in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) processes. Our understanding of this phenomenon has evolved significantly, revealing the complexity of coke formation and its detrimental effects on catalyst performance and longevity [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. The primary challenges associated with coke deposition include:

1. Complexity of coke formation: The structure, composition, and location of cokes are interrelated, with details still under debate [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].
2. Lack of molecular-level understanding: There is a scarcity of investigations into coke kinetics at the molecular level and transformations between disordered and graphitic coke [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].
3. Inevitable nature of coke formation: Despite prevention methods, coke formation cannot be completely eliminated in PDH processes [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

To address these challenges, researchers have developed various strategies:

1. Prevention methods:
   - Cofeeding gas (particularly hydrogen)
   - Metal promoters
   - Support modification
These methods have been verified as effective in preventing coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

2. Novel catalyst designs:
   - Single atom/site strategy: This approach exploits the different size dependence of dehydrogenation and side reactions in PDH [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].
   - Confined subnanosized Pt clusters in zeolites: These exhibit excellent resistance to coke formation due to unique interactions between catalyst and support [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].
   - Geometry optimization of active sites: The geometry of active sites has been verified as a crucial factor in controlling coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].
   - Single-atom alloys (SAAs): Pt/Cu SAAs have shown excellent CO tolerance and selective CH activation while preventing coking [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].

3. Regeneration techniques: Although prevention methods alleviate coke formation, regeneration is still necessary to prolong catalyst life [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

4. Coke migration: Facilitating coke transfer from active metal to support is a feasible way to maintain activity [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

These strategies have shown effectiveness in alleviating coke formation and maintaining high selectivity. For instance, Pt/Cu SAAs have demonstrated high propylene selectivity (~90%) by promoting the desorption of surface-bonded propylene and inhibiting further dehydrogenation [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].

However, challenges remain:
1. Optimal promoter selection
2. Understanding and controlling coke migration
3. Developing detailed molecular-level kinetic models
4. Improving long-term stability under industrial conditions
5. Developing scalable synthesis methods for novel catalyst designs

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The evolution of our understanding of coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts has opened new avenues for research and development. Future efforts should focus on:

1. Advanced characterization techniques: Developing and applying cutting-edge methods to investigate coke formation and transformation at the molecular level, potentially unveiling the mechanisms of disordered to graphitic coke conversion.

2. Computational modeling: Utilizing advanced simulation techniques to predict coke formation, migration, and catalyst deactivation, enabling the design of more effective prevention strategies.

3. Tailored regeneration protocols: Developing regeneration techniques specific to novel catalyst designs, such as SAAs and confined Pt clusters, to optimize their longevity and performance.

4. Synergistic approaches: Combining multiple strategies, such as SAAs with optimized support materials and cofeeding gases, to create next-generation catalysts with enhanced coke resistance and catalytic performance.

5. In situ and operando studies: Conducting real-time investigations of coke formation and catalyst deactivation under realistic reaction conditions to bridge the gap between laboratory findings and industrial applications.

6. Sustainable catalyst design: Exploring environmentally friendly approaches to catalyst synthesis and regeneration, aligning with the growing emphasis on green chemistry and sustainable industrial processes.

By addressing these areas, researchers can potentially develop Pt-based catalysts with unprecedented coke resistance, selectivity, and longevity, revolutionizing propane dehydrogenation and related industrial processes.

铂基催化剂中的积碳沉积：理解机理、开发缓解策略和推进催化剂设计以提高性能和寿命

积碳沉积仍然是商业铂基催化剂面临的一个关键问题，特别是在丙烷脱氢（PDH）过程中。我们对这一现象的理解已经显著发展，揭示了积碳形成的复杂性及其对催化剂性能和寿命的不利影响 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。与积碳沉积相关的主要挑战包括：

1. 积碳形成的复杂性：积碳的结构、组成和位置相互关联，细节仍存在争议 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。
2. 缺乏分子水平的理解：对分子水平的积碳动力学和无序积碳与石墨化积碳之间转化的研究非常匮乏 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。
3. 积碳形成的不可避免性：尽管有预防方法，但在PDH过程中无法完全消除积碳形成 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

为了应对这些挑战，研究人员开发了各种策略：

1. 预防方法：
   - 共进料气体（特别是氢气）
   - 金属助剂
   - 载体改性
这些方法已被证实能有效预防积碳形成 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

2. 新型催化剂设计：
   - 单原子/位点策略：这种方法利用了PDH中脱氢和副反应对尺寸的不同依赖性 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。
   - 沸石中限制的亚纳米铂簇：由于催化剂和载体之间的独特相互作用，这些簇表现出优异的抗积碳形成能力 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。
   - 活性位点几何结构优化：活性位点的几何结构已被证实是控制积碳形成的关键因素 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。
   - 单原子合金（SAAs）：铂/铜SAAs在防止积碳的同时表现出优异的CO耐受性和选择性CH活化 [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。

3. 再生技术：尽管预防方法减轻了积碳形成，但再生仍然是延长催化剂寿命所必需的 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

4. 积碳迁移：促进积碳从活性金属向载体转移是保持活性的可行方法 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

这些策略在减轻积碳形成和保持高选择性方面表现出效果。例如，铂/铜SAAs通过促进表面结合丙烯的脱附并抑制进一步脱氢，表现出高丙烯选择性（约90%）[10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。

然而，仍然存在一些挑战：
1. 最优助剂的选择
2. 理解和控制积碳迁移
3. 开发详细的分子水平动力学模型
4. 改善工业条件下的长期稳定性
5. 开发新型催化剂设计的可扩展合成方法

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：

我们对铂基催化剂中积碳沉积的理解演变为研究和开发开辟了新的道路。未来的努力应该集中在：

1. 先进表征技术：开发和应用尖端方法来研究分子水平的积碳形成和转化，可能揭示无序到石墨化积碳转化的机制。

2. 计算机模拟：利用先进的模拟技术预测积碳形成、迁移和催化剂失活，从而设计更有效的预防策略。

3. 定制再生方案：开发针对新型催化剂设计（如SAAs和限制的铂簇）的特定再生技术，以优化其寿命和性能。

4. 协同方法：结合多种策略，如SAAs与优化的载体材料和共进料气体，创造具有增强抗积碳能力和催化性能的下一代催化剂。

5. 原位和操作中研究：在真实反应条件下进行积碳形成和催化剂失活的实时研究，以弥合实验室发现和工业应用之间的差距。

6. 可持续催化剂设计：探索环境友好的催化剂合成和再生方法，与日益强调的绿色化学和可持续工业过程相一致。

通过解决这些领域的问题，研究人员有可能开发出具有前所未有的抗积碳性、选择性和寿命的铂基催化剂，从而彻底改变丙烷脱氢和相关工业过程。
