<English>
Coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts remains a critical challenge in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) processes, significantly impacting catalyst performance and longevity. Our understanding of this phenomenon has evolved substantially, recognizing its complexity and the need for multifaceted approaches to mitigate its effects [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

The primary challenges associated with coke deposition include:

1. Complexity of coke formation: The interrelated nature of coke structure, composition, and location, with many aspects still under debate [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].
2. Limited molecular-level understanding: A scarcity of investigations into coke kinetics at the molecular level and transformations between disordered and graphitic coke [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].
3. Inevitability of coke formation: Despite prevention methods, complete elimination of coke formation remains challenging in PDH processes [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

To address these challenges, researchers have developed various strategies:

1. Prevention methods:
   - Cofeeding gas (particularly hydrogen)
   - Metal promoters
   - Support modification
These methods have been verified as effective in preventing coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

2. Novel catalyst designs:
   - Single atom/site strategy: This approach exploits the different size dependence of dehydrogenation and side reactions in PDH [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].
   - Confined subnanosized Pt clusters in zeolites: These exhibit excellent resistance to coke formation due to unique interactions between catalyst and support [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].
   - Single-atom alloys (SAAs): Pt/Cu SAAs have shown excellent CO tolerance and selective CH activation while preventing coking [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].
   - Geometry optimization of active sites: The geometry of the active site has been verified as a crucial factor in controlling coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

3. Regeneration techniques: While still necessary to prolong catalyst life, efforts focus on lowering regeneration temperatures [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

4. Coke migration: Facilitating coke transfer from active metal to support has been identified as a feasible way to maintain activity [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

These strategies have shown effectiveness in alleviating coke formation and maintaining high selectivity. For instance, Pt/Cu SAAs have demonstrated high propylene selectivity (~90%) by promoting the desorption of surface-bonded propylene and inhibiting its further dehydrogenation [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].

However, several challenges remain:
1. Optimal promoter selection
2. Understanding and controlling coke migration
3. Developing detailed molecular-level kinetic models
4. Improving long-term stability under industrial conditions
5. Developing scalable synthesis methods for novel catalyst designs

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Taking into consideration the advancements and findings discussed in the file, there lies an opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies. Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques: Developing and applying cutting-edge spectroscopic and microscopic methods to study coke formation and migration in real-time under reaction conditions.

2. Artificial intelligence and machine learning: Utilizing these tools to predict optimal catalyst compositions, geometries, and operating conditions for minimizing coke formation while maintaining high activity and selectivity.

3. Multiscale modeling: Integrating molecular-level simulations with reactor-scale models to better understand and predict coke formation, migration, and its impact on catalyst performance.

4. Novel regeneration strategies: Exploring advanced regeneration techniques, such as plasma-assisted or microwave-induced regeneration, that can efficiently remove coke while minimizing damage to the catalyst structure.

5. Hierarchical catalyst design: Developing catalysts with tailored porosity and active site distributions to optimize mass transfer and minimize coke accumulation.

6. Synergistic promoter combinations: Investigating the combined effects of multiple promoters to achieve superior coke resistance and catalytic performance.

7. Bio-inspired catalyst designs: Drawing inspiration from natural enzymes to create highly selective and coke-resistant catalytic sites for PDH reactions.

These future directions aim to address the remaining challenges in understanding and mitigating coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts, ultimately leading to more efficient and sustainable PDH processes.

<Chinese>
铂基催化剂中的积碳沉积仍然是丙烷脱氢（PDH）过程中的一个关键挑战，显著影响催化剂的性能和寿命。我们对这一现象的理解已经有了实质性的发展，认识到其复杂性以及需要多方面的方法来缓解其影响 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

与积碳沉积相关的主要挑战包括：

1. 积碳形成的复杂性：积碳结构、组成和位置的相互关联性，许多方面仍有争议 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。
2. 分子水平理解的局限性：对分子水平的积碳动力学和无序积碳与石墨化积碳之间转化的研究匮乏 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。
3. 积碳形成的不可避免性：尽管有预防方法，但在PDH过程中完全消除积碳形成仍然具有挑战性 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

为解决这些挑战，研究人员开发了各种策略：

1. 预防方法：
   - 共进料气体（特别是氢气）
   - 金属助剂
   - 载体改性
这些方法已被证实在防止积碳形成方面是有效的 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

2. 新型催化剂设计：
   - 单原子/位点策略：这种方法利用了PDH中脱氢和副反应的不同尺寸依赖性 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。
   - 在沸石中限制的亚纳米铂簇：由于催化剂和载体之间的独特相互作用，这些催化剂表现出优秀的抗积碳性能 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。
   - 单原子合金（SAAs）：Pt/Cu SAAs在防止积碳的同时表现出优异的CO耐受性和选择性CH活化 [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。
   - 活性位点几何优化：活性位点的几何构型已被证实是控制积碳形成的关键因素 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

3. 再生技术：虽然仍然需要延长催化剂寿命，但努力集中在降低再生温度上 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

4. 积碳迁移：促进积碳从活性金属向载体转移被认为是保持活性的可行方法 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

这些策略在缓解积碳形成和保持高选择性方面表现出有效性。例如，Pt/Cu SAAs通过促进表面结合丙烯的脱附并抑制其进一步脱氢，展示了高丙烯选择性（~90%）[10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。

然而，仍然存在几个挑战：
1. 最佳助剂选择
2. 理解和控制积碳迁移
3. 开发详细的分子水平动力学模型
4. 提高工业条件下的长期稳定性
5. 开发新型催化剂设计的可扩展合成方法

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：考虑到文件中讨论的先进成果和发现，我们有机会探索新兴领域和创新方法。未来的研究努力可能会集中在：

1. 先进的原位表征技术：开发和应用尖端的光谱和显微技术，在反应条件下实时研究积碳形成和迁移。

2. 人工智能和机器学习：利用这些工具预测最佳催化剂组成、几何结构和操作条件，以最大程度地减少积碳形成，同时保持高活性和选择性。

3. 多尺度模型：整合分子水平模拟与反应器尺度模型，以更好地理解和预测积碳形成、迁移及其对催化剂性能的影响。

4. 新型再生策略：探索先进的再生技术，如等离子体辅助或微波诱导再生，可以有效去除积碳，同时最大程度地减少对催化剂结构的损害。

5. 分层催化剂设计：开发具有定制孔隙度和活性位点分布的催化剂，以优化质量传递并最小化积碳积累。

6. 协同助剂组合：研究多种助剂的组合效应，以实现卓越的抗积碳性能和催化性能。

7. 生物启发催化剂设计：从天然酶中汲取灵感，为PDH反应创造高选择性和抗积碳的催化位点。

这些未来方向旨在解决理解和缓解铂基催化剂中积碳沉积的剩余挑战，最终导致更高效和可持续的PDH过程。
