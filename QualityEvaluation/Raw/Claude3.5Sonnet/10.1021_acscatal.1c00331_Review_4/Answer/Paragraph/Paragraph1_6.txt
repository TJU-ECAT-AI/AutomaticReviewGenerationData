Here's the analytical and comprehensive review section on propane dehydrogenation catalysts, emphasizing coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts:

<English>
Coke deposition remains a crucial challenge for commercial Pt-based catalysts in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, necessitating extensive research to understand and mitigate its detrimental effects [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. The complexity of coke formation, involving interrelated factors such as structure, composition, and location, presents a significant obstacle in developing effective prevention strategies. Moreover, the scarcity of investigations into coke kinetics at the molecular level and the transformation between disordered and graphitic coke hinders the discovery of efficient suppression methods [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

To address these challenges, various strategies have been developed:

1. Prevention methods:
   Cofeeding gas, particularly hydrogen, metal promoters, and support modification have been verified as effective methods to prevent coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. These approaches aim to alter the reaction environment or catalyst properties to minimize carbon accumulation.

2. Novel catalyst designs:
   The single atom/site strategy has emerged as a promising approach, exploiting the different size dependence of dehydrogenation and side reactions in PDH. Confined subnanosized Pt clusters in zeolites, deposited at particular positions with unique catalyst-support interactions, have exhibited excellent resistance to coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. Furthermore, single-atom alloys (SAAs) have shown remarkable potential in balancing activity and coke resistance. For instance, Pt/Cu SAAs with an atomic ratio of Pt:Cu = 1:1.25 demonstrated excellent CO tolerance in H2 activation and exhibited intermediate barriers for CH activation, maintaining activity while preventing coking [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].

3. Geometry optimization:
   The geometry of the active site has been identified as a crucial factor in controlling coke formation. Advanced designs of active centers focus on optimizing this geometry to enhance coke resistance while maintaining catalytic activity [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

4. Coke migration:
   Facilitating coke transfer from the active metal to the support has been proposed as a feasible way to maintain catalyst activity [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. This approach aims to preserve the accessibility of active sites by redirecting coke deposition.

5. Strong metal-support interactions:
   Pt single atoms anchored on supports like Co3O4 or defective TiO2 through strong electronic metal-support interactions have shown enhanced activity for specific reactions, such as the dehydrogenation of ammonia borane and photocatalytic hydrogen evolution [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].

Despite these advancements, challenges persist. The optimal selection of promoters, understanding and controlling coke migration, and developing detailed molecular-level kinetic models remain areas requiring further research. Additionally, while these methods alleviate coke formation, regeneration is still necessary to prolong catalyst life [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of our understanding of coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts has opened new avenues for catalyst design and optimization. Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques to elucidate the real-time formation and transformation of coke species at the molecular level.
2. Computational modeling to predict coke formation mechanisms and guide the design of novel catalyst structures with enhanced coke resistance.
3. Development of "smart" catalysts capable of self-regeneration or coke mitigation during the reaction process.
4. Exploration of novel support materials or promoters that can synergistically enhance activity while suppressing coke formation.
5. Investigation of the long-term stability and scalability of promising strategies like single-atom alloys and confined subnanosized clusters under industrial conditions.
6. Integration of machine learning algorithms to optimize catalyst compositions and reaction conditions for minimal coke formation.

These future directions aim to bridge the gap between fundamental understanding and practical applications, ultimately leading to the development of more efficient and durable Pt-based catalysts for propane dehydrogenation and related processes.

<Chinese>
焦炭沉积仍然是商业化铂基催化剂在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中的一个关键挑战，需要广泛的研究来理解和缓解其有害影响 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。焦炭形成的复杂性，涉及结构、组成和位置等相互关联的因素，在开发有效预防策略方面构成了重大障碍。此外，分子水平上焦炭动力学和无序焦炭与石墨化焦炭之间转化的研究匮乏，阻碍了有效抑制方法的发现 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

为解决这些挑战，已经开发了各种策略：

1. 预防方法：
   共进料气体（特别是氢气）、金属助剂和载体改性已被证实为有效预防焦炭形成的方法 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。这些方法旨在改变反应环境或催化剂性质以最小化碳积累。

2. 新型催化剂设计：
   单原子/位点策略已成为一种有前途的方法，利用了PDH中脱氢和副反应的不同尺寸依赖性。在沸石中限制的亚纳米级铂簇，通过催化剂-载体之间的独特相互作用沉积在特定位置，表现出优异的抗焦炭形成能力 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。此外，单原子合金（SAAs）在平衡活性和抗焦炭方面显示出显著潜力。例如，Pt:Cu原子比为1:1.25的Pt/Cu SAAs在H2活化中表现出优异的CO耐受性，并且在CH活化方面表现出中等障碍，保持活性的同时防止结焦 [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。

3. 几何优化：
   活性位点的几何结构已被确定为控制焦炭形成的关键因素。先进的活性中心设计专注于优化这种几何结构，以增强抗焦炭能力，同时保持催化活性 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

4. 焦炭迁移：
   促进焦炭从活性金属向载体转移被提出作为保持催化剂活性的可行方法 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。这种方法旨在通过重新定向焦炭沉积来保持活性位点的可达性。

5. 强金属-载体相互作用：
   通过强电子金属-载体相互作用固定在Co3O4或缺陷TiO2等载体上的Pt单原子，已显示出对特定反应（如氨硼烷脱氢和光催化析氢）的增强活性 [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。

尽管取得了这些进展，挑战仍然存在。助剂的最优选择、理解和控制焦炭迁移以及开发详细的分子水平动力学模型仍然是需要进一步研究的领域。此外，虽然这些方法缓解了焦炭形成，但再生仍然是延长催化剂寿命所必需的 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：我们对铂基催化剂中焦炭沉积认知的演变为催化剂设计和优化开辟了新途径。未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 先进的原位表征技术，以阐明分子水平上焦炭物种的实时形成和转化。
2. 计算机模拟预测焦炭形成机制，并指导设计具有增强抗焦炭能力的新型催化剂结构。
3. 开发能够在反应过程中自我再生或缓解焦炭的"智能"催化剂。
4. 探索新型载体材料或助剂，能够协同增强活性同时抑制焦炭形成。
5. 研究有前途的策略（如单原子合金和限制的亚纳米簇）在工业条件下的长期稳定性和可扩展性。
6. 整合机器学习算法，优化催化剂组成和反应条件，以最小化焦炭形成。

这些未来方向旨在弥合基础理解和实际应用之间的差距，最终导致开发出更高效、更耐用的铂基催化剂，用于丙烷脱氢和相关过程。
