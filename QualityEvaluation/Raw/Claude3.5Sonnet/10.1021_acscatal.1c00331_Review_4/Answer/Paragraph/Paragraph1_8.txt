<English>
Coke deposition is a critical challenge for commercial Pt-based catalysts in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) processes, necessitating extensive research to understand and mitigate its detrimental effects [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]. The complexity of coke formation, involving interrelated factors such as structure, composition, and location, presents a significant hurdle in developing effective prevention strategies. Moreover, the scarcity of molecular-level investigations into coke kinetics and transformations between disordered and graphitic coke further complicates our understanding [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

To address these challenges, researchers have explored various strategies:

1. Prevention methods:
   - Cofeeding gas, particularly hydrogen
   - Metal promoters
   - Support modification

These approaches have shown promise in alleviating coke formation, but complete elimination remains elusive [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

2. Novel catalyst designs:
   - Single atom/site strategy: This approach exploits the different size dependence of dehydrogenation and side reactions in PDH [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].
   - Confined subnanosized Pt clusters in zeolites: These exhibit excellent resistance to coke formation due to unique catalyst-support interactions [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].
   - Single-atom alloys (SAAs): Pt/Cu SAAs have demonstrated excellent CO tolerance and selective CH activation while preventing coking. They exhibit intermediate barriers for CH activation, maintaining activity while avoiding carbon buildup [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].

3. Geometry optimization of active sites: The active site geometry has been identified as a crucial factor in controlling coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

4. Coke migration: Facilitating coke transfer from the active metal to the support has emerged as a feasible strategy to maintain catalyst activity [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

5. Promoting desorption: Pt/Cu SAAs can promote the desorption of surface-bonded intermediates like propylene, preventing further dehydrogenation that leads to coke formation. This approach has resulted in high propylene selectivity (~90%) [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].

6. Tuning electronic structure: Alloying Pt with other metals like Cu modifies the electronic structure to balance reactivity and resistance to coking [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].

7. Strong metal-support interactions: Pt single atoms anchored on supports like Co3O4 or defective TiO2 through strong electronic metal-support interactions have shown enhanced activity for specific reactions [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3].

Despite these advancements, challenges persist:
1. Optimal promoter selection
2. Understanding and controlling coke migration
3. Developing detailed molecular-level kinetic models
4. Improving long-term stability under industrial conditions
5. Developing scalable synthesis methods for novel catalyst designs
6. Understanding the fundamental mechanisms of coke resistance in SAAs

Regeneration techniques remain necessary to prolong catalyst life, with ongoing efforts focused on lowering regeneration temperatures [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of our understanding of coke deposition in Pt-based catalysts has led to significant advancements in catalyst design and performance. However, the complexity of the coke formation process and the lack of molecular-level understanding still present considerable challenges. Future research efforts may focus on:

1. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques to elucidate the real-time dynamics of coke formation and migration at the molecular level.
2. Development of multiscale computational models that integrate atomic-scale simulations with reactor-scale kinetics to predict and optimize catalyst performance.
3. Exploration of novel alloy compositions and support materials to further enhance coke resistance while maintaining high catalytic activity.
4. Investigation of the synergistic effects between different prevention strategies, such as combining SAAs with optimized support modifications.
5. Development of intelligent catalyst systems that can self-regulate to minimize coke formation under varying reaction conditions.
6. Integration of machine learning algorithms to accelerate the discovery and optimization of new catalyst formulations with improved coke resistance.

These research directions may pave the way for the next generation of highly efficient and stable Pt-based catalysts for propane dehydrogenation and related processes.

<Chinese>
焦炭沉积是商业铂基催化剂在丙烷脱氢（PDH）过程中的一个关键挑战，需要进行广泛的研究来理解和减轻其有害影响 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。焦炭形成的复杂性，涉及相互关联的因素如结构、成分和位置，在开发有效预防策略方面构成了重大障碍。此外，分子水平上对焦炭动力学和无序焦炭与石墨化焦炭之间转化的研究匮乏，进一步复杂化了我们的理解 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

为解决这些挑战，研究人员探索了各种策略：

1. 预防方法：
   - 共进料气体，特别是氢气
   - 金属助剂
   - 载体改性

这些方法在减轻焦炭形成方面显示出了希望，但完全消除仍然难以实现 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

2. 新型催化剂设计：
   - 单原子/位点策略：这种方法利用了PDH中脱氢和副反应的不同尺寸依赖性 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。
   - 沸石中的受限亚纳米铂簇：由于独特的催化剂-载体相互作用，这些簇表现出优异的抗焦炭形成能力 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。
   - 单原子合金（SAAs）：Pt/Cu SAAs表现出优异的CO耐受性和选择性CH活化，同时防止结焦。它们表现出CH活化的中等势垒，保持活性的同时避免碳积累 [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。

3. 活性位点几何优化：活性位点的几何形状被确定为控制焦炭形成的关键因素 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

4. 焦炭迁移：促进焦炭从活性金属向载体转移已成为保持催化剂活性的可行策略 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

5. 促进脱附：Pt/Cu SAAs可以促进表面结合的中间体（如丙烯）的脱附，防止进一步脱氢导致焦炭形成。这种方法已实现高丙烯选择性（~90%）[10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。

6. 调节电子结构：通过将Pt与其他金属（如Cu）合金化，修改电子结构以平衡反应性和抗焦炭能力 [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。

7. 强金属-载体相互作用：通过强电子金属-载体相互作用锚定在Co3O4或缺陷TiO2等载体上的Pt单原子，对特定反应表现出增强的活性 [10.1007/s12274-020-2755-3]。

尽管取得了这些进展，挑战仍然存在：
1. 最佳助剂的选择
2. 理解和控制焦炭迁移
3. 开发详细的分子水平动力学模型
4. 改善工业条件下的长期稳定性
5. 开发新型催化剂设计的可扩展合成方法
6. 理解SAAs中抗焦炭机理的基本原理

再生技术仍然是延长催化剂寿命所必需的，目前正在努力降低再生温度 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_4]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：我们对铂基催化剂中焦炭沉积的理解演变已导致催化剂设计和性能的显著进步。然而，焦炭形成过程的复杂性和缺乏分子水平的理解仍然构成了相当大的挑战。未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 先进的原位表征技术，以阐明分子水平上焦炭形成和迁移的实时动态。
2. 开发多尺度计算模型，将原子尺度模拟与反应器尺度动力学相结合，以预测和优化催化剂性能。
3. 探索新型合金成分和载体材料，进一步增强抗焦炭能力，同时保持高催化活性。
4. 研究不同预防策略之间的协同效应，例如将SAAs与优化的载体改性相结合。
5. 开发智能催化剂系统，可以在不同反应条件下自我调节以最小化焦炭形成。
6. 整合机器学习算法，加速发现和优化具有改进抗焦炭能力的新催化剂配方。

这些研究方向可能为下一代高效稳定的丙烷脱氢和相关过程的铂基催化剂铺平道路。
