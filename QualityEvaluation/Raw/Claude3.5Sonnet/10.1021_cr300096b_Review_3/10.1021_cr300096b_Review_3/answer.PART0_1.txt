<EnglishVersionAnswer>

The most commonly utilized synthesis methods for supported bimetallic catalysts include wet impregnation, slurry synthesis, organometallic cluster precursors, reductive deposition precipitation, electroless deposition, colloidal synthesis, core-shell synthesis, reverse micelle synthesis, and dendrimer support synthesis.

Wet impregnation is the most popular due to its simplicity in sample preparation. It's suitable for various applications but may not always result in uniform bimetallic particle distribution.

Slurry synthesis is advantageous for high metal loadings and supports with low surface areas. It has been used in hydrogenation of nitroaromatics.

Organometallic cluster precursors offer improved uniformity of bimetallic catalysts compared to conventional methods. This method has shown better performance in ethanol reforming reactions.

Reductive deposition precipitation is useful for producing highly coordinated bimetallic catalysts from monometallic ones. It's been applied in selective hydrogenation of citral.

Electroless deposition allows for targeted placement of the second metal on the first metal's surface. It has shown improved activity in hydrogenation reactions.

Colloidal synthesis provides precise control over size and composition of bimetallic particles. It's been used in CO2 hydrogenation.

Core-shell synthesis creates nanoparticles with a transition metal core covered by another metal shell. These catalysts have shown enhanced activity in CO oxidation and ammonia borane hydrolysis.

Reverse micelle synthesis produces nanoparticles with narrow size distribution and has been effective in 1,3-butadiene hydrogenation.

Dendrimer support synthesis yields monodisperse particles, overcoming size distribution issues of traditional methods. It's been applied in hydrogenation and CO oxidation reactions.

Each method has its unique advantages and is chosen based on the specific requirements of the catalyst and its intended application. The efficacy of these methods varies depending on the metals used, the support material, and the target reaction.

支持的双金属催化剂最常用的合成方法包括湿法浸渍、浆液合成、有机金属簇前驱体、还原沉淀法、无电解沉积、胶体合成、核壳合成、反相胶束合成和树枝状分子支持合成。

湿法浸渍因其样品制备简单而最为常用。它适用于各种应用，但可能无法始终产生均匀的双金属粒子分布。

浆液合成适用于高金属负载和低比表面积的载体。它已被用于硝基芳香化合物的氢化。

有机金属簇前驱体比传统方法能提供更好的双金属催化剂均匀性。这种方法在乙醇重整反应中表现更好。

还原沉淀法对于从单金属催化剂生产高度配位的双金属催化剂很有用。它已被应用于柠檬醛的选择性氢化。

无电解沉积允许将第二种金属有针对性地放置在第一种金属表面上。它在氢化反应中显示出improved的活性。

胶体合成可以精确控制双金属粒子的大小和成分。它已被用于CO2氢化。

核壳合成创造了具有过渡金属核心和另一种金属外壳的纳米粒子。这些催化剂在CO氧化和氨硼烷水解中显示出增强的活性。

反相胶束合成产生粒径分布窄的纳米粒子，在1,3-丁二烯氢化中效果显著。

树枝状分子支持合成产生单分散粒子，克服了传统方法的粒径分布问题。它已被应用于氢化和CO氧化反应。

每种方法都有其独特的优势，根据催化剂的具体要求和预期应用来选择。这些方法的效果因所使用的金属、载体材料和目标反应而异。
