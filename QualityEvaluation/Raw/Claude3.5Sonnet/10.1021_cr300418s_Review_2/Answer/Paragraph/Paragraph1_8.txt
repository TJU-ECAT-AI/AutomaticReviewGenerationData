<English>
Synthesis methods for doped oxide materials play a crucial role in determining the structural characteristics and catalytic performance of the resulting materials. This review focuses on the principles, characterization techniques, and structure-property relationships in catalytic applications, with emphasis on solid-state synthesis and impregnation methods.

Solid-state synthesis involves mechanically mixing solid compounds of the host oxide and dopant, followed by high-temperature heating in oxygen to allow dopant diffusion [10.1021/cr300418s_2]. This method is suitable for thermodynamically stable doped oxides and typically requires higher temperatures and longer processing times. For instance, in the preparation of Sr0.9La0.1Ti1-xTaxO3 ceramics, raw materials are mixed by ball-milling, pressed into pellets, and calcined at 1350°C for 6 hours, followed by sintering at 1460°C for 4 hours [10.1016/j.ceramint.2011.04.004].

In contrast, the impregnation method exposes the host oxide to a liquid containing the dopant precursor, which is then dried and heated in air [10.1021/cr300418s_2]. This approach aims to achieve surface doping and generally uses lower temperatures and shorter processing times. For example, in the preparation of chromium-doped magnetite catalysts, an iron oxide gel is first produced and then impregnated with a chromium nitrate solution [10.1016/j.molcata.2007.07.042].

Key factors influencing the structural characteristics and catalytic performance of doped oxide materials include:

1. Dopant loading: The concentration of dopant significantly affects the material's properties. In copper-doped ceria, loadings below 3% resulted in successful doping without separate phases, while higher loadings led to phase separation [10.1021/cr300418s_2]. Similarly, for Sr0.9La0.1Ti1-xTaxO3, different tantalum doping levels (x = 0.00, 0.01, 0.03, 0.05) were examined, affecting lattice constants and electrical properties [10.1016/j.ceramint.2011.04.004].

2. Calcination temperature: This factor affects dopant diffusion and phase formation. Higher temperatures can promote better dopant diffusion in solid-state synthesis but may lead to increased phase separation in impregnation methods [10.1021/cr300418s_2]. For chromium-doped magnetite catalysts, both precipitation and impregnation methods involved heating to 773 K under nitrogen flow to form the active magnetite phase [10.1016/j.molcata.2007.07.042].

X-ray diffraction (XRD) is a crucial technique for confirming successful doping and characterizing the resulting materials. It can detect changes in crystal structure indicative of doping, reveal the absence of separate crystalline phases at low dopant concentrations, and show lattice constant shifts that indicate successful incorporation of dopant ions [10.1021/cr300418s_2]. For Sr0.9La0.1Ti1-xTaxO3, XRD patterns showed that tantalum was successfully doped into the Sr0.9La0.1TiO3 lattice, forming a single-phase compound [10.1016/j.ceramint.2011.04.004]. Similarly, for chromium-doped magnetite catalysts, XRD showed only magnetite peaks for all samples, indicating chromium incorporation did not form separate phases [10.1016/j.molcata.2007.07.042].

The choice of synthesis method can significantly impact the catalytic performance of doped oxide materials. For example, in chromium-doped magnetite catalysts, the impregnated sample showed higher overall catalytic activity than the precipitated sample in the water gas shift reaction [10.1016/j.molcata.2007.07.042]. In copper-doped ceria catalysts, the doped material was more active for CO oxidation than the undoped oxide [10.1021/cr300418s_2].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The reviewed studies highlight the significant impact of synthesis methods on the structural and catalytic properties of doped oxide materials. Future research endeavors might focus on developing more precise control over dopant distribution and concentration, potentially through advanced techniques such as atomic layer deposition or controlled co-precipitation methods. Additionally, in-situ characterization techniques could provide valuable insights into the dynamic changes occurring during catalyst synthesis and operation. The development of multi-doped systems, where two or more dopants are incorporated to fine-tune catalytic properties, presents an exciting avenue for future exploration. Furthermore, computational modeling and machine learning approaches could be employed to predict optimal dopant-host combinations and synthesis conditions, accelerating the discovery of high-performance catalysts for various applications.

<Chinese>
掺杂氧化物材料的合成方法在决定所得材料的结构特性和催化性能方面起着至关重要的作用。本综述重点关注催化应用中的原理、表征技术和结构-性能关系，特别强调固态合成和浸渍法。

固态合成涉及机械混合主体氧化物和掺杂剂的固体化合物，然后在氧气中高温加热以允许掺杂剂扩散[10.1021/cr300418s_2]。这种方法适用于热力学稳定的掺杂氧化物，通常需要更高的温度和更长的处理时间。例如，在制备Sr0.9La0.1Ti1-xTaxO3陶瓷时，原材料通过球磨混合，压制成片，在1350°C下煅烧6小时，然后在1460°C下烧结4小时[10.1016/j.ceramint.2011.04.004]。

相比之下，浸渍法将主体氧化物暴露在含有掺杂剂前驱体的液体中，然后干燥并在空气中加热[10.1021/cr300418s_2]。这种方法旨在实现表面掺杂，通常使用较低的温度和较短的处理时间。例如，在制备铬掺杂磁铁矿催化剂时，首先生成氧化铁凝胶，然后用硝酸铬溶液浸渍[10.1016/j.molcata.2007.07.042]。

影响掺杂氧化物材料结构特性和催化性能的关键因素包括：

1. 掺杂剂负载量：掺杂剂的浓度显著影响材料的性能。在铜掺杂的氧化铈中，低于3%的负载量导致成功掺杂而没有单独相，而更高的负载量导致相分离[10.1021/cr300418s_2]。类似地，对于Sr0.9La0.1Ti1-xTaxO3，研究了不同的钽掺杂水平（x = 0.00, 0.01, 0.03, 0.05），影响晶格常数和电学性质[10.1016/j.ceramint.2011.04.004]。

2. 煅烧温度：这个因素影响掺杂剂扩散和相形成。更高的温度可以促进固态合成中更好的掺杂剂扩散，但可能导致浸渍法中相分离的增加[10.1021/cr300418s_2]。对于铬掺杂磁铁矿催化剂，沉淀和浸渍方法都涉及在氮气流中加热到773 K以形成活性磁铁矿相[10.1016/j.molcata.2007.07.042]。

X射线衍射（XRD）是确认成功掺杂和表征所得材料的关键技术。它可以检测指示掺杂的晶体结构变化，揭示低掺杂剂浓度下单独晶相的缺失，并显示表明掺杂剂离子成功掺入的晶格常数变化[10.1021/cr300418s_2]。对于Sr0.9La0.1Ti1-xTaxO3，XRD图谱显示钽成功掺入Sr0.9La0.1TiO3晶格，形成单相化合物[10.1016/j.ceramint.2011.04.004]。同样，对于铬掺杂磁铁矿催化剂，XRD仅显示所有样品的磁铁矿峰，表明铬的掺入没有形成单独相[10.1016/j.molcata.2007.07.042]。

合成方法的选择可以显著影响掺杂氧化物材料的催化性能。例如，在铬掺杂磁铁矿催化剂中，浸渍样品在水气变换反应中显示出比沉淀样品更高的整体催化活性[10.1016/j.molcata.2007.07.042]。在铜掺杂的氧化铈催化剂中，掺杂材料在CO氧化方面比未掺杂氧化物更活跃[10.1021/cr300418s_2]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：所回顾的研究强调了合成方法对掺杂氧化物材料的结构和催化性能的重大影响。未来的研究努力可能集中在开发对掺杂剂分布和浓度更精确控制的方法上，可能通过先进技术如原子层沉积或控制共沉淀方法。此外，原位表征技术可以提供关于催化剂合成和操作过程中动态变化的宝贵见解。开发多掺杂系统，即incorporation两种或多种掺杂剂以精细调节催化性能，为未来探索提供了一个令人兴奋的途径。此外，计算机建模和机器学习方法可以用于预测最佳掺杂剂-主体组合和合成条件，加速发现各种应用的高性能催化剂。
