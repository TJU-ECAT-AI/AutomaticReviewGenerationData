<English>
The nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins is an important industrial process, with two main types of catalysts dominating the field: noble metal-based catalysts (particularly platinum) and metal oxide-based catalysts. This review will focus on comparing these two catalyst types in terms of composition, effectiveness, industrial applications, recent developments, and potential advantages.

Composition:
Noble metal-based catalysts typically consist of platinum nanoparticles, often promoted with elements such as tin, indium, gallium, zinc, or germanium. These are usually supported on high surface area materials like alumina, silica, or calcined hydrotalcites (e.g., Mg(Al)O) [10.1021/cr5002436_1]. Metal oxide-based catalysts include chromia-alumina, gallium oxide, vanadium oxide, and more complex mixed oxides like vanadia-silica-zirconia [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

Effectiveness:
Platinum-based catalysts are generally more effective due to their superior activation of paraffinic C-H bonds and low activity for C-C cleavage [10.1021/cr5002436_1]. However, unmodified platinum catalysts suffer from low alkene selectivity and rapid coke formation [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.017]. The addition of promoters like tin significantly improves selectivity and stability [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.09.018]. Metal oxide catalysts vary in effectiveness, with some showing high selectivity for specific reactions. For example, a vanadia-silica-zirconia catalyst demonstrated 81.5% selectivity to propylene at 8% conversion of propane at 550°C [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

Industrial applications:
Platinum-based catalysts are widely used in commercial processes such as UOP's Oleflex and Pacol for light and heavy paraffin dehydrogenation [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]. The Pacol process, using alumina-supported platinum catalysts, has been in use since 1968 for linear olefin production in the detergent industry [10.1021/cr5002436_1]. Metal oxide catalysts, particularly chromia-alumina, have been used in processes like Catofin [10.1016/s0926-860x(00)00842-5].

Recent developments:
For noble metal catalysts, recent developments include the introduction of continuous catalyst regeneration (CCR) technology, allowing for stable activity over several years [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]. Research has focused on optimizing bimetallic compositions and support materials to improve selectivity and stability [10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008]. For metal oxide catalysts, research has explored novel combinations and supports to enhance performance, such as sulfate and sodium treatments of silica supports [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

Potential advantages:
Noble metal catalysts offer high activity, long-term stability with CCR, and flexibility for different feedstocks [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]. They can be finely tuned with promoters and supports to optimize performance [10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008]. Metal oxide catalysts potentially offer lower costs and good selectivity for specific reactions. Some metal oxide catalysts have shown effectiveness in the presence of CO2 as a mild oxidant, which could be advantageous for certain processes [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of light olefin dehydrogenation catalysis is evolving towards more efficient and sustainable processes. While platinum-based catalysts currently dominate industrial applications due to their superior performance, ongoing research into metal oxide catalysts shows promise for developing more cost-effective alternatives. Future research directions may include:

1. Exploring novel bimetallic or multi-metallic compositions to further enhance selectivity and stability of noble metal catalysts.
2. Developing advanced metal oxide formulations that can match or exceed the performance of platinum-based catalysts.
3. Investigating the potential of hybrid catalysts that combine the advantages of both noble metals and metal oxides.
4. Focusing on catalyst designs that minimize coke formation and extend catalyst lifetime.
5. Exploring the integration of catalytic dehydrogenation with membrane technology for in-situ product separation, potentially overcoming thermodynamic limitations.
6. Investigating the use of alternative supports or promoters to enhance catalyst performance and longevity.

These research avenues could lead to more efficient and economical processes for light olefin production, addressing the growing demand for these important chemical feedstocks while minimizing environmental impact.

<Chinese>
轻烯烃的非氧化脱氢是一个重要的工业过程，主要有两种类型的催化剂：贵金属基催化剂（特别是铂）和金属氧化物基催化剂。本综述将从组成、有效性、工业应用、最新发展和潜在优势等方面比较这两种催化剂。

组成：
贵金属基催化剂通常由铂纳米粒子组成，常常添加锡、铟、镓、锌或锗等元素作为助剂。这些通常负载在高比表面积材料上，如氧化铝、二氧化硅或煅烧的水滑石（如Mg(Al)O）[10.1021/cr5002436_1]。金属氧化物基催化剂包括铬铝氧化物、氧化镓、氧化钒和更复杂的混合氧化物，如钒-硅-锆氧化物[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

有效性：
铂基催化剂通常更有效，因为它们对烷烃C-H键的活化能力强，而对C-C键的断裂活性低[10.1021/cr5002436_1]。然而，未经修饰的铂催化剂烯烃选择性低，容易快速积碳[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.017]。添加锡等助剂可显著提高选择性和稳定性[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.09.018]。金属氧化物催化剂的有效性各不相同，有些对特定反应显示出高选择性。例如，一种钒-硅-锆氧化物催化剂在550°C下丙烷转化率为8%时，对丙烯的选择性达到81.5%[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

工业应用：
铂基催化剂广泛用于商业过程，如UOP的Oleflex和Pacol工艺，用于轻质和重质烷烃的脱氢[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。Pacol工艺使用氧化铝负载的铂催化剂，自1968年以来一直用于洗涤剂行业的直链烯烃生产[10.1021/cr5002436_1]。金属氧化物催化剂，特别是铬铝氧化物，已用于Catofin等工艺[10.1016/s0926-860x(00)00842-5]。

最新发展：
对于贵金属催化剂，最新发展包括引入连续催化剂再生（CCR）技术，使催化剂活性可以稳定保持数年[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。研究重点是优化双金属组成和载体材料，以提高选择性和稳定性[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008]。对于金属氧化物催化剂，研究探索了新型组合和载体以提高性能，如硅胶的硫酸盐和钠处理[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

潜在优势：
贵金属催化剂具有高活性、CCR技术下的长期稳定性，以及适用于不同原料的灵活性[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。它们可以通过助剂和载体进行精细调节以优化性能[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008]。金属氧化物催化剂可能提供更低的成本和对特定反应的良好选择性。一些金属氧化物催化剂在CO2存在下表现出效果，这对某些工艺可能是有利的[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：轻烯烃脱氢催化领域正在向更高效和可持续的方向发展。虽然铂基催化剂目前因其优越的性能而主导工业应用，但对金属氧化物催化剂的持续研究显示出开发更具成本效益的替代品的潜力。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 探索新型双金属或多金属组合，以进一步提高贵金属催化剂的选择性和稳定性。
2. 开发能够匹配或超越铂基催化剂性能的先进金属氧化物配方。
3. 研究结合贵金属和金属氧化物优势的混合催化剂的潜力。
4. 专注于最小化积碳形成并延长催化剂寿命的催化剂设计。
5. 探索将催化脱氢与膜技术结合用于原位产品分离，可能克服热力学限制。
6. 研究使用替代载体或助剂以提高催化剂性能和寿命。

这些研究方向可能导致更高效和经济的轻烯烃生产工艺，满足这些重要化工原料不断增长的需求，同时最大限度地减少环境影响。
