<English>
Noble metal-based catalysts (particularly Platinum) and metal oxide-based catalysts are the two main types used for nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins. This review will compare their compositions, effectiveness, industrial applications, recent developments, and potential advantages.

Composition:
Noble metal-based catalysts typically consist of platinum nanoparticles, often promoted with elements such as Sn, Zn, Ge, Ga, or In to form bimetallic alloys [10.1021/cr5002436_1]. These catalysts are usually supported on high surface area materials like alumina, silica, or calcined hydrotalcite-like materials (e.g., Mg(Al)O, Mg(Ga)(Al)O, or Mg(In)(Al)O) [10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008].

Metal oxide-based catalysts include CrOx, Ga2O3, V2O5, TiO2, In2O3, ZnO, and La2O3. Complex mixed oxides like vanadia-silica-zirconia are also used [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

Effectiveness:
Platinum-based catalysts are generally more effective, with high activity and selectivity for dehydrogenation, especially for long-chain paraffins. However, unmodified platinum catalysts may lack selectivity and suffer from rapid coke formation [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.017]. The addition of promoters like Sn or Ga significantly improves alkene selectivity and reduces coke formation [10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008].

Metal oxide catalysts vary in effectiveness, with Ga2O3 showing the highest activity among simple metal oxides. Some complex oxides, like vanadia-silica-zirconia, show high selectivity for specific reactions [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

Industrial applications:
Noble metal catalysts are widely used in commercial processes like UOP's Oleflex and Pacol for light and heavy paraffin dehydrogenation [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]. PtSn supported on alumina is commonly used in reforming and dehydrogenation processes [10.1016/j.molcata.2013.10.007].

Metal oxide catalysts, particularly chromia-alumina, have been used in older processes like Catofin. Cr2O3 supported on alumina or zirconia is also used commercially [10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.022].

Recent developments:
For noble metal catalysts, recent developments include:
1. Introduction of continuous catalyst regeneration (CCR) technology, allowing for stable activity over several years [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].
2. Development of novel bimetallic catalysts like PtGa and PtIn supported on calcined hydrotalcite-like materials, showing improved performance [10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008].
3. Use of non-acidic supports like Mg(Al)O to enhance stability and reduce unwanted side reactions [10.1021/jp074686u].

For metal oxide catalysts, research focuses on novel combinations and supports to enhance performance, such as sulfate and sodium treatments of silica supports, and development of complex mixed oxide systems [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

Potential advantages:
Noble metal catalysts offer high activity, long-term stability with CCR, and flexibility for different feedstocks. They are well-established in industrial processes and can be finely tuned with promoters and supports [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

Metal oxide catalysts potentially offer lower cost, good selectivity for specific reactions, and the ability to be tailored for different feedstocks. In some cases, they show effectiveness in the presence of CO2 as a mild oxidant [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 
The comparison between noble metal-based and metal oxide-based catalysts for nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins reveals a complex landscape where each type of catalyst offers unique advantages. While platinum-based catalysts currently dominate industrial applications due to their superior performance, ongoing research in both fields suggests potential for significant advancements.

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Developing hybrid catalysts that combine the advantages of both noble metals and metal oxides, potentially offering improved performance and cost-effectiveness.

2. Exploring novel support materials and preparation methods to enhance catalyst stability and selectivity, particularly for metal oxide catalysts.

3. Investigating the potential of metal oxide catalysts in oxidative dehydrogenation processes, which could overcome thermodynamic limitations of nonoxidative dehydrogenation.

4. Utilizing advanced characterization techniques and computational modeling to gain deeper insights into catalyst behavior at the molecular level, enabling more rational catalyst design.

5. Exploring the application of these catalysts in emerging fields such as biomass conversion or the production of renewable feedstocks.

As global demand for light olefins continues to grow, the development of more efficient and sustainable catalytic processes for their production remains a critical challenge. The ongoing research in both noble metal-based and metal oxide-based catalysts promises to drive innovation in this field, potentially leading to more economical and environmentally friendly industrial processes.

<Chinese>
贵金属基催化剂（特别是铂）和金属氧化物基催化剂是用于轻烯烃非氧化脱氢的两种主要类型。本综述将比较它们的组成、有效性、工业应用、最新发展和潜在优势。

组成：
贵金属基催化剂通常由铂纳米粒子组成，经常与Sn、Zn、Ge、Ga或In等元素促进形成双金属合金[10.1021/cr5002436_1]。这些催化剂通常负载在高比表面积材料上，如氧化铝、二氧化硅或煅烧的类水滑石材料（如Mg(Al)O、Mg(Ga)(Al)O或Mg(In)(Al)O）[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008]。

金属氧化物基催化剂包括CrOx、Ga2O3、V2O5、TiO2、In2O3、ZnO和La2O3。也使用复杂的混合氧化物，如钒-硅-锆氧化物[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

有效性：
铂基催化剂通常更有效，对脱氢反应具有高活性和选择性，特别是对长链烷烃。然而，未经修饰的铂催化剂可能缺乏选择性并快速形成积碳[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.017]。添加Sn或Ga等促进剂显著提高了烯烃选择性并减少了积碳形成[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008]。

金属氧化物催化剂的有效性各不相同，其中Ga2O3在简单金属氧化物中显示最高活性。一些复杂的氧化物，如钒-硅-锆氧化物，对特定反应显示高选择性[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

工业应用：
贵金属催化剂广泛用于商业过程，如UOP的Oleflex和Pacol工艺，用于轻质和重质烷烃脱氢[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。负载在氧化铝上的PtSn通常用于重整和脱氢过程[10.1016/j.molcata.2013.10.007]。

金属氧化物催化剂，特别是氧化铬-氧化铝，已用于较早的工艺，如Catofin。负载在氧化铝或二氧化锆上的Cr2O3也在商业上使用[10.1016/j.apcata.2010.10.022]。

最新发展：
对于贵金属催化剂，最新发展包括：
1. 引入连续催化剂再生（CCR）技术，使催化剂活性能够在数年内保持稳定[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。
2. 开发新型双金属催化剂，如负载在煅烧类水滑石材料上的PtGa和PtIn，显示出改进的性能[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008]。
3. 使用非酸性载体如Mg(Al)O来增强稳定性并减少不需要的副反应[10.1021/jp074686u]。

对于金属氧化物催化剂，研究重点是新型组合和载体以提高性能，如硅胶的硫酸盐和钠处理，以及复杂混合氧化物系统的开发[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

潜在优势：
贵金属催化剂提供高活性、CCR技术下的长期稳定性，以及对不同原料的灵活性。它们在工业过程中已经得到充分应用，并可通过促进剂和载体进行精细调整[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

金属氧化物催化剂可能提供更低的成本、对特定反应的良好选择性，以及可针对不同原料进行定制的能力。在某些情况下，它们在CO2作为温和氧化剂存在的情况下表现出有效性[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：
比较贵金属基和金属氧化物基催化剂在轻烯烃非氧化脱氢中的应用，揭示了一个复杂的景象，每种类型的催化剂都具有独特的优势。尽管铂基催化剂目前在工业应用中占据主导地位，但两个领域的持续研究都表明有显著进步的潜力。

未来研究方向可能集中在：

1. 开发结合贵金属和金属氧化物优势的混合催化剂，可能提供更好的性能和成本效益。

2. 探索新型载体材料和制备方法，以提高催化剂的稳定性和选择性，特别是对金属氧化物催化剂。

3. 研究金属氧化物催化剂在氧化脱氢过程中的潜力，这可能克服非氧化脱氢的热力学限制。

4. 利用先进的表征技术和计算机模拟，在分子水平上深入了解催化剂行为，实现更合理的催化剂设计。

5. 探索这些催化剂在新兴领域的应用，如生物质转化或可再生原料的生产。

随着全球对轻烯烃需求的持续增长，开发更高效和可持续的催化过程仍然是一个关键挑战。贵金属基和金属氧化物基催化剂的持续研究有望推动该领域的创新，可能导致更经济和环境友好的工业过程。
