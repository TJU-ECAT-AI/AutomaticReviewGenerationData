<English>
Noble metal-based catalysts (particularly platinum) and metal oxide-based catalysts are the two main types used for nonoxidative dehydrogenation of light olefins. This review will compare their compositions, effectiveness, industrial applications, recent developments, and potential advantages.

Composition:
Noble metal-based catalysts typically consist of platinum nanoparticles, often promoted with elements such as Sn, Zn, Ge, Ga, or In to form bimetallic alloys. These are usually supported on high surface area materials like alumina, silica, zeolites, or calcined hydrotalcites (e.g., Mg(Al)O) [10.1021/cr5002436_1]. Metal oxide-based catalysts include CrOx, gallium oxide, vanadium oxide, zinc oxide, and more complex mixed oxides like vanadia-silica-zirconia [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

Effectiveness:
Platinum-based catalysts are generally considered the most effective for alkane dehydrogenation due to their superior activation of paraffinic C-H bonds and low activity for C-C cleavage [10.1021/cr5002436_1]. However, unmodified Pt catalysts suffer from low alkene selectivity and rapid coke formation [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.017]. The addition of promoters like Sn significantly improves selectivity and stability [10.1021/jp074686u].

Metal oxide catalysts vary in effectiveness, with gallium oxide showing the highest activity among simple metal oxides for ethane dehydrogenation. Some complex oxides, like vanadia-silica-zirconia, demonstrate high selectivity for specific reactions [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

Industrial applications:
Platinum-based catalysts are widely used in commercial processes such as UOP's Oleflex and Pacol for light and heavy paraffin dehydrogenation [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]. Metal oxide catalysts, particularly chromia-alumina, have been used in processes like Catofin [10.1016/s0926-860x(00)00842-5].

Recent developments:
For noble metal catalysts, recent developments include the introduction of continuous catalyst regeneration (CCR) technology, allowing for stable activity over several years [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]. Research is also focusing on novel bimetallic formulations, such as PtGa and PtIn supported on calcined hydrotalcite-like materials, which show improved performance in terms of activity, selectivity, and coke resistance [10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008].

For metal oxide catalysts, research is exploring novel combinations and supports to enhance performance, such as sulfate and sodium treatments of silica supports for chromium oxide catalysts [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

Potential advantages:
Noble metal catalysts offer high activity, long-term stability with CCR, and flexibility for different feedstocks. They are well-established in industrial processes and can be finely tuned with promoters and supports [10.1021/cr5002436_1].

Metal oxide catalysts potentially offer lower costs and good selectivity for specific reactions. They can be tailored for different feedstocks and, in some cases, show effectiveness in the presence of CO2 as a mild oxidant [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

In conclusion, while platinum-based catalysts dominate industrial applications due to their superior overall performance, ongoing research into metal oxide catalysts aims to develop more cost-effective and equally efficient alternatives for specific dehydrogenation processes.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of light olefin dehydrogenation catalysis is evolving towards more efficient and sustainable processes. While platinum-based catalysts currently dominate industrial applications, the development of metal oxide catalysts presents opportunities for cost reduction and process optimization. Future research may focus on hybrid catalysts that combine the advantages of both noble metals and metal oxides, potentially leading to synergistic effects in activity, selectivity, and stability. Additionally, the integration of advanced characterization techniques and computational modeling could accelerate the discovery and optimization of new catalyst formulations. As the demand for light olefins continues to grow, there is also potential for exploring alternative feedstocks and process conditions, such as utilizing renewable carbon sources or integrating dehydrogenation with other processes in biorefinery concepts. The ongoing challenge of catalyst deactivation due to coke formation remains a critical area for improvement, and novel approaches to mitigate this issue could significantly enhance the economic viability of dehydrogenation processes.

<Chinese>
贵金属基催化剂（特别是铂）和金属氧化物基催化剂是用于轻烯烃非氧化脱氢的两种主要类型。本综述将比较它们的组成、效能、工业应用、最新发展和潜在优势。

组成：
贵金属基催化剂通常由铂纳米粒子组成，常常添加如Sn、Zn、Ge、Ga或In等元素形成双金属合金。这些通常负载在高比表面积材料上，如氧化铝、二氧化硅、沸石或煅烧的类水滑石（如Mg(Al)O）[10.1021/cr5002436_1]。金属氧化物基催化剂包括CrOx、氧化镓、氧化钒、氧化锌，以及更复杂的混合氧化物如钒-硅-锆氧化物[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

效能：
铂基催化剂通常被认为是烷烃脱氢最有效的催化剂，因为它们对烷烃C-H键的活化能力强，而对C-C键的裂解活性低[10.1021/cr5002436_1]。然而，未经修饰的Pt催化剂存在烯烃选择性低和快速积碳的问题[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.017]。添加Sn等助剂可显著提高选择性和稳定性[10.1021/jp074686u]。

金属氧化物催化剂的效能各不相同，其中氧化镓在简单金属氧化物中对乙烷脱氢显示最高活性。一些复杂的氧化物，如钒-硅-锆氧化物，对特定反应表现出高选择性[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

工业应用：
铂基催化剂广泛用于商业过程，如UOP的Oleflex和Pacol工艺，用于轻质和重质烷烃脱氢[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。金属氧化物催化剂，特别是铬-氧化铝，已用于Catofin等工艺[10.1016/s0926-860x(00)00842-5]。

最新发展：
对于贵金属催化剂，最新发展包括引入连续催化剂再生（CCR）技术，使催化剂活性能够保持稳定数年[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。研究还集中在新型双金属配方上，如负载在煅烧类水滑石材料上的PtGa和PtIn，这些催化剂在活性、选择性和抗积碳性能方面表现出改进[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008]。

对于金属氧化物催化剂，研究正在探索新的组合和载体以提高性能，如对氧化铬催化剂的二氧化硅载体进行硫酸盐和钠处理[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

潜在优势：
贵金属催化剂具有高活性、使用CCR技术后的长期稳定性，以及对不同原料的适应性。它们在工业过程中已经得到充分应用，可以通过助剂和载体进行精细调节[10.1021/cr5002436_1]。

金属氧化物催化剂可能提供更低的成本和对特定反应的良好选择性。它们可以针对不同的原料进行定制，在某些情况下，在CO2存在的情况下作为温和氧化剂时表现出效能[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

总之，虽然铂基催化剂由于其优越的整体性能而主导工业应用，但对金属氧化物催化剂的持续研究旨在为特定脱氢过程开发更具成本效益和同样高效的替代品。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：轻烯烃脱氢催化领域正朝着更高效和可持续的方向发展。尽管铂基催化剂目前主导工业应用，但金属氧化物催化剂的发展为降低成本和优化工艺提供了机会。未来的研究可能会集中在结合贵金属和金属氧化物优势的混合催化剂上，可能在活性、选择性和稳定性方面产生协同效应。此外，先进表征技术和计算建模的整合可能会加速新催化剂配方的发现和优化。随着轻烯烃需求的持续增长，还有可能探索替代原料和工艺条件，如利用可再生碳源或将脱氢与生物炼制概念中的其他工艺整合。由于积碳造成的催化剂失活仍然是一个亟待改进的关键领域，开发新方法来缓解这一问题可能会显著提高脱氢工艺的经济可行性。
