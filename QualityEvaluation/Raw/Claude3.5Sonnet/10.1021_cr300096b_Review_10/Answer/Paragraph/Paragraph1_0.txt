<English>
Selective dehydrogenation reactions play a crucial role in chemical production, fuel synthesis, and hydrogen storage applications. These processes are significant for producing valuable unsaturated hydrocarbons, upgrading fuels, and developing efficient hydrogen storage materials. The importance of these reactions is evident in various industrial applications, such as naphtha reforming, production of light olefins for polymers and other chemicals, and the potential use of cyclic hydrocarbons as organic hydrogen storage materials [10.1021/jp903127b] [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.004] [10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60092-9].

Reactor studies and Ultra-High Vacuum (UHV) studies contribute complementary insights to our understanding of selective dehydrogenation processes:

Reactor studies:
- Provide information on catalyst performance under realistic operating conditions
- Allow investigation of reaction kinetics, selectivity, and yields
- Enable testing of catalyst stability and deactivation over time
- Help optimize reaction parameters such as temperature, pressure, and feed composition

UHV studies:
- Offer precise control over surface composition and structure
- Allow detailed investigation of elementary reaction steps and mechanisms
- Enable use of surface-sensitive spectroscopic techniques for in-depth characterization
- Provide fundamental insights into surface processes and catalyst-reactant interactions at the atomic level

Key research methods and characterization techniques typically used include:
1. Temperature Programmed Desorption (TPD)
2. Auger Electron Spectroscopy (AES)
3. Low Energy Electron Diffraction (LEED)
4. X-ray Photoelectron Spectroscopy (XPS)
5. Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy (FTIR)
6. High-Resolution Transmission Electron Microscopy (HRTEM)
7. X-ray Absorption Spectroscopy (XAS)
8. Density Functional Theory (DFT) calculations

Reaction conditions investigated often include:
- Temperature effects (typically ranging from 300 K to 900 K)
- Pressure effects (from UHV to atmospheric)
- Reactant composition and concentration
- Catalyst composition and structure

These studies inform catalytic process optimization by:
1. Identifying active sites and reaction mechanisms
2. Understanding the role of catalyst composition and structure
3. Elucidating the effects of reaction conditions on selectivity and activity
4. Guiding the design of improved catalysts with enhanced selectivity and stability
5. Developing structure-performance relationships

Specific examples of C-H and N-H bond dehydrogenation over supported catalysts include:
- Propane dehydrogenation to propylene over PtSn catalysts [10.1016/j.apcata.2008.12.022]
- Cyclohexane dehydrogenation to benzene over Ni/ACC catalysts [10.1007/s10562-005-8009-x]
- Ammonia decomposition for hydrogen production over NiPtPt catalysts [10.1038/nchem.626]

These studies have revealed the importance of bimetallic catalysts, such as PtSn, PtZn, and NiPt, in improving selectivity, activity, and stability compared to monometallic catalysts. The addition of a second metal often helps to suppress undesired side reactions, enhance catalyst dispersion, and modify the electronic properties of the active sites [10.1016/j.apcata.2005.06.005] [10.1016/s1381-1169(01)00525-8].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of selective dehydrogenation reactions is rapidly evolving, driven by the need for more efficient and sustainable chemical production processes, cleaner fuels, and advanced hydrogen storage technologies. Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Development of novel multi-metallic catalysts with tailored electronic and geometric properties to further enhance selectivity and stability.
2. Integration of advanced in situ and operando characterization techniques to bridge the gap between UHV studies and realistic reaction conditions.
3. Exploration of novel reactor designs, such as membrane reactors or microreactors, to overcome thermodynamic limitations and improve process efficiency.
4. Investigation of bio-inspired catalysts mimicking enzymatic dehydrogenation processes for milder reaction conditions and improved selectivity.
5. Application of machine learning and artificial intelligence techniques to accelerate catalyst discovery and optimization, leveraging the vast amount of data generated from both experimental and computational studies.
6. Development of more efficient hydrogen storage materials based on reversible dehydrogenation-hydrogenation cycles of organic compounds.

These advancements could lead to more sustainable chemical production processes, improved fuel synthesis technologies, and innovative solutions for the hydrogen economy, ultimately contributing to a cleaner and more energy-efficient future.

<Chinese>
选择性脱氢反应在化学生产、燃料合成和氢存储应用中起着至关重要的作用。这些过程对于生产有价值的不饱和烃、提升燃料品质和开发高效的氢存储材料具有重要意义。这些反应的重要性体现在各种工业应用中，如石脑油重整、用于聚合物和其他化学品的轻烯烃生产，以及环烃作为有机氢存储材料的潜在应用 [10.1021/jp903127b] [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.10.004] [10.1016/s1872-2067(09)60092-9]。

反应器研究和超高真空（UHV）研究为我们理解选择性脱氢过程提供了互补的见解：

反应器研究：
- 提供在实际操作条件下的催化剂性能信息
- 允许研究反应动力学、选择性和产率
- 能够测试催化剂的稳定性和失活情况
- 有助于优化反应参数，如温度、压力和进料组成

UHV研究：
- 提供对表面组成和结构的精确控制
- 允许详细研究基本反应步骤和机理
- 能够使用表面敏感的光谱技术进行深入表征
- 在原子水平上提供表面过程和催化剂-反应物相互作用的基本见解

通常使用的关键研究方法和表征技术包括：
1. 程序升温脱附（TPD）
2. 俄歇电子能谱（AES）
3. 低能电子衍射（LEED）
4. X射线光电子能谱（XPS）
5. 傅里叶变换红外光谱（FTIR）
6. 高分辨率透射电子显微镜（HRTEM）
7. X射线吸收光谱（XAS）
8. 密度泛函理论（DFT）计算

研究的反应条件通常包括：
- 温度效应（通常范围从300 K到900 K）
- 压力效应（从UHV到大气压）
- 反应物组成和浓度
- 催化剂组成和结构

这些研究通过以下方式为催化过程优化提供信息：
1. 识别活性位点和反应机理
2. 理解催化剂组成和结构的作用
3. 阐明反应条件对选择性和活性的影响
4. 指导改进的催化剂设计，以提高选择性和稳定性
5. 发展结构-性能关系

支持催化剂上C-H和N-H键脱氢的具体例子包括：
- PtSn催化剂上的丙烷脱氢生成丙烯 [10.1016/j.apcata.2008.12.022]
- Ni/ACC催化剂上的环己烷脱氢生成苯 [10.1007/s10562-005-8009-x]
- NiPtPt催化剂上的氨分解制氢 [10.1038/nchem.626]

这些研究揭示了双金属催化剂（如PtSn、PtZn和NiPt）在提高选择性、活性和稳定性方面相对于单金属催化剂的重要性。添加第二种金属通常有助于抑制不需要的副反应，增强催化剂分散性，并修改活性位点的电子性质 [10.1016/j.apcata.2005.06.005] [10.1016/s1381-1169(01)00525-8]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：选择性脱氢反应领域正在迅速发展，这主要是由于需要更高效和可持续的化学生产过程、更清洁的燃料以及先进的氢存储技术。未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 开发具有定制电子和几何性质的新型多金属催化剂，以进一步提高选择性和稳定性。
2. 整合先进的原位和操作中表征技术，以弥合UHV研究和实际反应条件之间的差距。
3. 探索新型反应器设计，如膜反应器或微反应器，以克服热力学限制并提高过程效率。
4. 研究模仿酶催化脱氢过程的生物启发催化剂，以实现更温和的反应条件和更高的选择性。
5. 应用机器学习和人工智能技术来加速催化剂的发现和优化，利用实验和计算研究产生的大量数据。
6. 基于有机化合物的可逆脱氢-加氢循环开发更高效的氢存储材料。

这些进展可能会带来更可持续的化学生产过程、改进的燃料合成技术和氢能经济的创新解决方案，最终为更清洁、更节能的未来做出贡献。
