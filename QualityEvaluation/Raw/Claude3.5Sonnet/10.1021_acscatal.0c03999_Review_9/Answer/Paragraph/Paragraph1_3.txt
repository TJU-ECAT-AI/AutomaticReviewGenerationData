<English>
The effectiveness and performance of iron-containing ZSM-5 zeolites as catalysts in N2O-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions are significantly influenced by their synthesis methods. This review analyzes the impact of various preparation techniques on catalytic performance, evaluated through key performance metrics.

Steam Activation:
Steam activation emerges as a crucial method for enhancing catalytic performance. Steam-activated Fe-ZSM-5 catalysts consistently demonstrate superior activity and selectivity compared to other preparation methods [10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035]. The process typically involves calcination followed by treatment with 30 vol.% H2O in N2 flow at 873 K for 5 hours [10.1039/B305568J]. This treatment generates extraframework iron species, which are more active for ODHP than framework iron species [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_9].

Key performance metrics for steam-activated Fe-ZSM-5 include:
1. C3H8 conversion: 45-52%
2. N2O conversion: 86-96%
3. C3H6 selectivity: 45-50%
4. C3H6 yield: 19-25% at 723 K
[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035] [10.1039/B305568J] [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.03.003]

The superior performance of steam-activated catalysts is attributed to the formation of oligonuclear FexOy clusters and the reduction of Brønsted acid sites, which limits undesired propene oligomerization [10.1016/j.jcat.2012.12.010].

Solid-State Ion Exchange:
Solid-state ion exchange methods, using precursors like ferrocene or FeCl3, produce catalysts with various active iron species, including large oxide clusters and small oxygen-containing nanoclusters [10.1016/j.apcata.2012.06.038]. A notable example is Fe-ZSM-5 prepared using ferrocene, which exhibited a maximal initial propylene yield of 30% and maintained stable performance for over 40 hours [10.1016/j.micromeso.2014.07.032].

Liquid Ion Exchange:
Liquid ion exchange methods, particularly reductive solution ion-exchange, introduce extra-framework iron species to the zeolite host [10.1039/c3cy20782j]. These catalysts show good initial activity, with propylene yields of about 14% at 673 K. However, subsequent acid post-treatments can significantly improve performance, increasing propylene yields to about 25% [10.1039/c3cy20782j].

Comparative Performance:
Steam-activated Fe-ZSM-5 generally outperforms catalysts prepared by other methods in terms of initial activity and stability. However, catalysts prepared by solid-state ion exchange using ferrocene have shown comparable or even superior performance in some studies [10.1016/j.micromeso.2014.07.032].

Reaction Conditions and Deactivation:
Optimal performance is typically observed in the temperature range of 673-773 K. All catalysts experience some degree of deactivation due to coke formation, but steam-activated Fe-ZSM-5 generally shows higher resistance to deactivation [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.03.003]. Catalyst regeneration through oxygen treatment at high temperatures can restore initial activity [10.1021/ie0493863].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 
The comprehensive analysis of various synthesis methods for iron-containing ZSM-5 zeolites in N2O-assisted ODHP reactions reveals the critical role of preparation techniques in determining catalytic performance. While steam activation consistently produces highly effective catalysts, recent advancements in solid-state ion exchange methods, particularly using ferrocene, show promising results.

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Optimizing the combination of synthesis methods, such as incorporating steam activation post-treatment for catalysts prepared by other techniques.

2. Investigating the synergistic effects of bimetallic or multimetallic catalysts to further enhance activity and selectivity.

3. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and active sites during the reaction.

4. Exploring the potential of novel zeolite frameworks or hierarchical structures to improve mass transfer and resistance to deactivation.

5. Integrating computational modeling with experimental studies to predict and design catalysts with optimal iron species distribution and acidity for ODHP reactions.

These research avenues could lead to the development of more efficient and stable catalysts for N2O-assisted ODHP, potentially enabling the industrial application of this promising route for propylene production.

<Chinese>
铁含量ZSM-5分子筛作为N2O辅助丙烷氧化脱氢(ODHP)反应的催化剂,其效能和性能显著受到合成方法的影响。本综述分析了各种制备技术对催化性能的影响,并通过关键性能指标进行评估。

蒸汽活化:
蒸汽活化是提高催化性能的关键方法。蒸汽活化的Fe-ZSM-5催化剂在活性和选择性方面持续表现出优于其他制备方法的性能[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035]。该过程通常包括煅烧,随后在873 K下用30体积%的H2O在N2流中处理5小时[10.1039/B305568J]。这种处理产生了比骨架铁物种更活跃的骨架外铁物种[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_9]。

蒸汽活化Fe-ZSM-5的关键性能指标包括:
1. C3H8转化率: 45-52%
2. N2O转化率: 86-96%
3. C3H6选择性: 45-50%
4. C3H6收率: 在723 K时为19-25%
[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035] [10.1039/B305568J] [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.03.003]

蒸汽活化催化剂的优异性能归因于寡核FexOy簇的形成和Brønsted酸位的减少,这限制了不希望的丙烯寡聚反应[10.1016/j.jcat.2012.12.010]。

固态离子交换:
使用二茂铁或FeCl3等前体的固态离子交换方法产生具有各种活性铁物种的催化剂,包括大型氧化物簇和小型含氧纳米簇[10.1016/j.apcata.2012.06.038]。一个显著的例子是使用二茂铁制备的Fe-ZSM-5,它表现出30%的最大初始丙烯收率,并在40多个小时内保持稳定性能[10.1016/j.micromeso.2014.07.032]。

液态离子交换:
液态离子交换方法,特别是还原性溶液离子交换,将骨架外铁物种引入分子筛主体[10.1039/c3cy20782j]。这些催化剂表现出良好的初始活性,在673 K时丙烯收率约为14%。然而,随后的酸后处理可以显著改善性能,将丙烯收率提高到约25%[10.1039/c3cy20782j]。

性能比较:
蒸汽活化的Fe-ZSM-5在初始活性和稳定性方面通常优于其他方法制备的催化剂。然而,在一些研究中,使用二茂铁进行固态离子交换制备的催化剂表现出可比甚至更优的性能[10.1016/j.micromeso.2014.07.032]。

反应条件和失活:
最佳性能通常在673-773 K的温度范围内观察到。所有催化剂都因焦炭形成而经历一定程度的失活,但蒸汽活化的Fe-ZSM-5通常表现出更高的抗失活能力[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.03.003]。通过高温氧气处理可以恢复催化剂的初始活性[10.1021/ie0493863]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望:
对铁含量ZSM-5分子筛在N2O辅助ODHP反应中的各种合成方法的全面分析揭示了制备技术在决定催化性能方面的关键作用。虽然蒸汽活化持续产生高效催化剂,但最近在固态离子交换方法方面的进展,特别是使用二茂铁,显示出很有前景的结果。

未来研究方向可能集中在:

1. 优化合成方法的组合,例如为其他技术制备的催化剂结合蒸汽活化后处理。

2. 研究双金属或多金属催化剂的协同效应,以进一步提高活性和选择性。

3. 开发原位表征技术,以更好地理解反应过程中催化剂结构和活性位点的动态变化。

4. 探索新型分子筛骨架或分级结构的潜力,以改善物质传递和抗失活能力。

5. 将计算机模拟与实验研究相结合,预测和设计具有最佳铁物种分布和酸度的ODHP反应催化剂。

这些研究途径可能导致开发出更高效和稳定的N2O辅助ODHP催化剂,潜在地实现这一有前景的丙烯生产路线的工业应用。
