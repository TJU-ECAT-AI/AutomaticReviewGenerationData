<English>
The effectiveness and performance of iron-containing ZSM-5 zeolites as catalysts in N2O-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions are significantly influenced by their synthesis methods. This review analyzes the impact of various preparation techniques on catalytic performance, evaluating them through key performance metrics.

Steam Activation:
Steam activation has emerged as a crucial method for enhancing catalytic performance in ODHP reactions. Steam-activated Fe-ZSM-5 catalysts consistently demonstrate superior activity and selectivity compared to other preparation methods [10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035]. The process typically involves calcination followed by treatment with 30 vol.% H2O in N2 flow at 873 K for 5 hours [10.1039/B305568J]. This treatment leads to the formation of extraframework iron species, which are more active than framework iron species for N2O-ODHP [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_9].

Key performance metrics for steam-activated Fe-ZSM-5 include:
1. C3H8 conversion: 45-52%
2. N2O conversion: 86-96%
3. C3H6 selectivity: 45-50%
4. C3H6 yield: 19-25% at 723 K
[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035] [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.03.003]

The effectiveness of steam activation is attributed to:
1. Generation of extra-framework iron species
2. Reduction in Brønsted acidity
3. Creation of limited mesoporosity
[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010]

Solid-State Ion Exchange:
Solid-state ion exchange methods, using precursors like ferrocene or FeCl3, have also shown promising results. This method produces various active iron species, including large oxide clusters, small oxygen-containing nanoclusters (Fe4O4, Fe3O4), and isolated iron species [10.1016/j.apcata.2012.06.038].

Performance metrics for solid-state ion exchanged Fe-ZSM-5:
1. C3H8 conversion: 20-60%
2. C3H6 selectivity: 25-70%
3. C3H6 yield: 14-30% (377-527°C)
[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_9]

Liquid Ion Exchange:
Liquid ion exchange methods, particularly reductive solution ion-exchange, have also been investigated. This method introduces extra-framework iron species, resulting in ferric ions with different nuclearity [10.1039/c3cy20782j].

Performance metrics for liquid ion exchanged Fe-ZSM-5:
1. Initial C3H6 yield: ~14% (673 K)
2. C3H6 yield after acid post-treatments: up to 25%
[10.1039/c3cy20782j]

Comparative Analysis:
Among these methods, steam activation consistently demonstrates the best overall performance. Steam-activated Fe-ZSM-5 shows higher resistance to deactivation compared to Fe-ZSM-5 prepared by other methods [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.03.003]. The superior performance is attributed to the formation of oligonuclear FexOy clusters, which are more catalytically active than isolated iron ions [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.03.003].

However, each method has its strengths. Solid-state ion exchange offers better control of metal loading and reproducibility [10.1016/j.apcata.2012.06.038]. Liquid ion exchange, when combined with acid post-treatments, can significantly improve catalyst performance [10.1039/c3cy20782j].

Reaction Conditions:
The performance of these catalysts is heavily influenced by reaction conditions, particularly temperature. Optimal performance is typically observed in the range of 673-773 K, with a balance between conversion and selectivity [10.1039/c3cy20782j].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The synthesis method of iron-containing ZSM-5 zeolites plays a crucial role in determining their effectiveness as catalysts for N2O-assisted ODHP reactions. While steam activation has shown superior results, each method offers unique advantages that could be exploited for specific applications.

Future research directions may include:
1. Hybrid synthesis methods combining the strengths of different techniques.
2. In-depth investigation of the relationship between iron species distribution and catalytic performance.
3. Development of more resistant catalysts to address deactivation issues.
4. Exploration of novel precursors for solid-state and liquid ion exchange methods.
5. Optimization of post-synthesis treatments to enhance catalyst stability and selectivity.

These advancements could lead to more efficient and sustainable processes for propylene production, addressing the growing demand for this crucial chemical feedstock.

<Chinese>
铁含量ZSM-5沸石作为N2O辅助丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应的催化剂的有效性和性能受其合成方法的显著影响。本综述分析了各种制备技术对催化性能的影响，并通过关键性能指标对其进行评估。

蒸汽活化：
蒸汽活化已成为提高ODHP反应催化性能的关键方法。蒸汽活化的Fe-ZSM-5催化剂在活性和选择性方面始终表现出优于其他制备方法的性能[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035]。该过程通常包括煅烧后在873 K下用30体积%的H2O在N2流中处理5小时[10.1039/B305568J]。这种处理导致了骨架外铁物种的形成，这些物种比N2O-ODHP的骨架铁物种更活跃[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_9]。

蒸汽活化Fe-ZSM-5的关键性能指标包括：
1. C3H8转化率：45-52%
2. N2O转化率：86-96%
3. C3H6选择性：45-50%
4. C3H6产率：19-25%（723 K下）
[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035] [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.03.003]

蒸汽活化的有效性归因于：
1. 生成骨架外铁物种
2. 降低Brønsted酸性
3. 创造有限的介孔结构
[10.1016/j.jcat.2007.04.010]

固态离子交换：
使用二茂铁或FeCl3等前体的固态离子交换方法也显示出良好的结果。这种方法产生各种活性铁物种，包括大型氧化物簇、小型含氧纳米簇（Fe4O4、Fe3O4）和孤立的铁物种[10.1016/j.apcata.2012.06.038]。

固态离子交换Fe-ZSM-5的性能指标：
1. C3H8转化率：20-60%
2. C3H6选择性：25-70%
3. C3H6产率：14-30%（377-527°C）
[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_9]

液相离子交换：
液相离子交换方法，特别是还原性溶液离子交换，也已被研究。这种方法引入骨架外铁物种，产生具有不同核数的铁离子[10.1039/c3cy20782j]。

液相离子交换Fe-ZSM-5的性能指标：
1. 初始C3H6产率：~14%（673 K）
2. 酸后处理后的C3H6产率：最高25%
[10.1039/c3cy20782j]

比较分析：
在这些方法中，蒸汽活化始终表现出最佳的整体性能。蒸汽活化的Fe-ZSM-5相比其他方法制备的Fe-ZSM-5表现出更高的抗失活能力[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.03.003]。其优越性能归因于寡核FexOy簇的形成，这些簇比孤立的铁离子具有更高的催化活性[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.03.003]。

然而，每种方法都有其优势。固态离子交换提供了更好的金属负载控制和重现性[10.1016/j.apcata.2012.06.038]。液相离子交换与酸后处理相结合时，可以显著提高催化剂性能[10.1039/c3cy20782j]。

反应条件：
这些催化剂的性能受反应条件的强烈影响，特别是温度。最佳性能通常在673-773 K的范围内观察到，在转化率和选择性之间取得平衡[10.1039/c3cy20782j]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：铁含量ZSM-5沸石的合成方法在决定其作为N2O辅助ODHP反应催化剂的有效性方面起着关键作用。虽然蒸汽活化已显示出优越的结果，但每种方法都提供了独特的优势，可以针对特定应用进行利用。

未来研究方向可能包括：
1. 结合不同技术优势的混合合成方法。
2. 深入研究铁物种分布与催化性能之间的关系。
3. 开发更具抗性的催化剂以解决失活问题。
4. 探索固态和液相离子交换方法的新型前体。
5. 优化后合成处理以提高催化剂的稳定性和选择性。

这些进展可能导致更高效和可持续的丙烯生产过程，满足这一关键化学原料不断增长的需求。
