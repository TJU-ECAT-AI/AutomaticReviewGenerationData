<English>
The effectiveness and performance of iron-containing ZSM-5 zeolites as catalysts in N2O-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions are significantly influenced by their synthesis methods. This review analyzes the impact of various preparation techniques on catalytic performance, evaluated through key performance metrics.

Steam activation emerges as a crucial method for enhancing catalytic performance. Steam-activated Fe-ZSM-5 zeolites consistently demonstrate superior activity and selectivity compared to other preparation methods [10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035]. The steam treatment process, typically conducted at temperatures around 600-873 K, leads to the formation of extraframework iron species, which are more active in the ODHP reaction than framework iron species [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_9]. This method yields catalysts with high initial propylene yields of up to 25% at 723 K [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005].

Solid-state ion exchange is another effective synthesis method. Catalysts prepared via this route, especially using ferrocene as an iron precursor, exhibit high initial propylene yields of up to 30% [10.1016/j.micromeso.2014.07.032]. This method produces various active iron species, including large oxide clusters and small oxygen-containing nanoclusters such as Fe4O4 and Fe3O4 [10.1016/j.apcata.2012.06.038].

Liquid ion exchange methods have also shown promise. Fe-ZSM-5 prepared by this method can achieve propene yields close to 20% [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]. Notably, a reductive solution ion-exchange method, followed by acid post-treatments, can gradually increase the maximal propylene yield to about 25% [10.1039/c3cy20782j].

Key performance metrics used to evaluate these catalysts include:

1. C3H8 conversion: Ranges from 20% to 60% for various synthesis methods [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_9].
2. N2O conversion: Can reach up to 100%, increasing with temperature [10.1039/c3cy20782j].
3. C3H6 selectivity: Typically between 25% and 70% [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_9].
4. C3H6 yield: Ranges from 14% to 30% in the temperature range of 377 to 527°C [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_9].

The reaction conditions, particularly temperature, significantly affect these metrics. The optimal temperature range appears to be 673-798 K, with a volcano-shaped curve for propylene yield peaking around 673-723 K [10.1039/c3cy20782j] [10.1039/B305568J].

While steam activation generally produces the most effective catalysts, each synthesis method has its advantages. Steam-activated catalysts show higher resistance to deactivation by coking [10.1016/j.apcata.2004.03.003], while solid-state ion exchange allows better control of metal loading and reproducibility [10.1016/j.apcata.2012.06.038].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The synthesis method of iron-containing ZSM-5 zeolites plays a crucial role in determining their catalytic performance in N2O-assisted ODHP reactions. Steam activation emerges as a particularly effective method, but combinations of different techniques, such as reductive solution ion-exchange followed by steam treatment or acid post-treatments, show promise for further performance enhancements.

Future research could focus on:
1. Developing hybrid synthesis methods that combine the advantages of different techniques.
2. Investigating the long-term stability and regeneration of these catalysts under industrial conditions.
3. Exploring the potential of novel iron precursors or zeolite frameworks to further improve catalytic performance.
4. Utilizing advanced characterization techniques to gain deeper insights into the nature and distribution of active iron species formed by different synthesis methods.
5. Developing strategies to mitigate catalyst deactivation, a common issue observed across various synthesis methods.

These directions could lead to the development of more efficient and stable catalysts for N2O-assisted ODHP reactions, potentially enabling more widespread industrial application of this process for propylene production.

<Chinese>
铁含量ZSM-5分子筛作为N2O辅助丙烷氧化脱氢(ODHP)反应的催化剂的有效性和性能受其合成方法的显著影响。本综述分析了各种制备技术对催化性能的影响，并通过关键性能指标进行评估。

蒸汽活化作为提高催化性能的关键方法脱颖而出。蒸汽活化的Fe-ZSM-5分子筛在活性和选择性方面始终表现出优于其他制备方法的性能[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035]。通常在600-873 K温度下进行的蒸汽处理过程导致了骨架外铁物种的形成，这些物种在ODHP反应中比骨架铁物种更活跃[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_9]。这种方法制备的催化剂在723 K下的初始丙烯收率高达25%[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]。

固相离子交换是另一种有效的合成方法。通过这种方法制备的催化剂，尤其是使用茂铁作为铁前体，表现出高达30%的初始丙烯收率[10.1016/j.micromeso.2014.07.032]。这种方法产生各种活性铁物种，包括大型氧化物簇和小型含氧纳米簇，如Fe4O4和Fe3O4 [10.1016/j.apcata.2012.06.038]。

液相离子交换方法也显示出潜力。通过这种方法制备的Fe-ZSM-5可以达到接近20%的丙烯收率[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]。值得注意的是，还原性溶液离子交换方法，随后进行酸后处理，可以逐步将最大丙烯收率提高到约25%[10.1039/c3cy20782j]。

用于评估这些催化剂的关键性能指标包括：

1. C3H8转化率：各种合成方法的范围从20%到60% [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_9]。
2. N2O转化率：可达到100%，随温度升高而增加[10.1039/c3cy20782j]。
3. C3H6选择性：通常在25%至70%之间[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_9]。
4. C3H6收率：在377至527°C的温度范围内，从14%到30%不等[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_9]。

反应条件，特别是温度，显著影响这些指标。最佳温度范围似乎是673-798 K，丙烯收率呈火山形曲线，峰值在673-723 K左右[10.1039/c3cy20782j] [10.1039/B305568J]。

虽然蒸汽活化通常产生最有效的催化剂，但每种合成方法都有其优势。蒸汽活化的催化剂表现出更高的抗积碳失活能力[10.1016/j.apcata.2004.03.003]，而固相离子交换允许更好地控制金属负载和重现性[10.1016/j.apcata.2012.06.038]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：铁含量ZSM-5分子筛的合成方法在决定其N2O辅助ODHP反应的催化性能中起着关键作用。蒸汽活化作为一种特别有效的方法脱颖而出，但不同技术的组合，如还原性溶液离子交换随后进行蒸汽处理或酸后处理，显示出进一步提高性能的潜力。

未来研究可以集中在：
1. 开发结合不同技术优势的混合合成方法。
2. 调查这些催化剂在工业条件下的长期稳定性和再生能力。
3. 探索新型铁前体或分子筛骨架，以进一步提高催化性能。
4. 利用先进的表征技术，深入了解不同合成方法形成的活性铁物种的性质和分布。
5. 开发策略以减轻催化剂失活，这是在各种合成方法中观察到的常见问题。

这些方向可能导致开发出更高效、更稳定的N2O辅助ODHP反应催化剂，可能使这一丙烯生产工艺在工业上得到更广泛的应用。
