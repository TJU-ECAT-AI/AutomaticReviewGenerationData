<English>
Halogen-assisted methods for selectively dehydrogenating propane to propene have gained significant attention due to their potential for higher yields and improved selectivity compared to conventional dehydrogenation processes. This review section analyzes various approaches, reaction conditions, and propane's unique reactivity in these processes.

1. Dehydrogenation with molecular halogens (X2):
This method involves the direct reaction of propane with halogens, particularly chlorine and iodine. For instance, propane halo-dehydrogenation using Cl2 over a 0.28% Ru/TiO2 catalyst achieves 50% C3H8 conversion and 95% C3H6 selectivity at 400°C [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. Iodine-mediated dehydrogenation generally gives higher propylene yields compared to conventional routes, but requires higher temperatures (>673 K) [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551].

2. Oxidative dehydrogenation in the presence of halogens (X2 + O2):
This approach combines halogens with oxygen to facilitate the dehydrogenation process. The addition of oxygen shifts the equilibrium towards higher propane conversion and makes the reaction exothermic. For example, gas-phase iodine-mediated oxidative dehydrogenation at 500°C with a propane partial pressure of 0.1 atm yields 64% propylene [10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1].

3. Propane oxyhalogenation via halides (HX + O2):
This method uses hydrogen halides and oxygen to convert propane to propene. The reaction proceeds through the oxidation of hydrogen halide with O2, producing alkyl halide, alkene, and water. Theoretically, it can achieve 100% halogen atom efficiency in the presence of O2, compared to 50% for halo-dehydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11].

4. Molten metal halide salt-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation (LiX + O2):
This approach uses metal iodides like LiI, CdI2, ZnI2, and PbI2 in a molten salt form. The reaction occurs between 450-600°C and can achieve propene yields up to 70% [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. In one study, using a 5 wt.-% LiOH*H2O/LiI salt melt at 600°C with a propane/air mixture of 1:2 resulted in high propene selectivity [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S].

5. Bihalogen-mediated dehydrogenation:
This novel approach uses both Br2 and I2 as mediators. Adding a small amount of bromine to the iodination reaction significantly promotes propane conversion and product selectivity. High yields of propene and propyl halides (up to 80%) can be achieved at moderate temperatures below 800 K [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551].

Reaction conditions for these methods generally involve temperatures between 300-700°C, with specific ranges depending on the approach. The presence of oxygen, halogen species, and specialized catalysts or molten salts are crucial factors in determining the reaction outcomes.

Propane's reactivity in these processes differs significantly from methane and ethane due to its molecular structure. Propane has both primary and secondary carbon atoms, making it more reactive than methane and ethane, which only have primary carbons [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. This allows for easier C-H bond activation and dehydrogenation. Additionally, propane can form more stable intermediate species and undergo dehydrogenation more readily to produce propene [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551].

The presence of reactive allylic hydrogen atoms in propene (the product) can lead to successive oxidation, resulting in lower selectivity compared to ethylene production from ethane [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. Furthermore, at high radical concentrations, propane can undergo coupling reactions to form larger molecules like benzene, which is not possible with methane and less likely with ethane [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The development of halogen-assisted methods for propane dehydrogenation represents a significant advancement in the field of alkane activation. These approaches offer promising alternatives to conventional dehydrogenation processes, potentially addressing challenges such as thermodynamic limitations and catalyst deactivation.

Future research in this area could focus on:

1. Catalyst design: Developing more efficient and stable catalysts that can withstand the corrosive nature of halogen species while maintaining high selectivity towards propene.

2. Process optimization: Integrating these halogen-assisted methods into existing industrial processes, focusing on energy efficiency and cost-effectiveness.

3. Environmental considerations: Investigating ways to minimize halogen emissions and develop closed-loop systems for halogen recycling.

4. Mechanistic studies: Further elucidating the reaction mechanisms, particularly in complex systems like bihalogen-mediated dehydrogenation, to guide rational process design.

5. Application to other alkanes: Exploring the potential of these methods for the activation of longer-chain alkanes or more challenging substrates like methane.

6. Novel reactor designs: Developing specialized reactors that can effectively manage the unique challenges of halogen-assisted processes, such as corrosion resistance and heat management in molten salt systems.

By addressing these areas, researchers can potentially unlock new pathways for more efficient and selective alkane activation, contributing to the broader field of sustainable chemical production and energy systems.

卤素辅助法选择性脱氢丙烷制丙烯因其潜在的高产率和改进的选择性而受到广泛关注，相比传统脱氢工艺具有优势。本综述分析了各种方法、反应条件以及丙烷在这些过程中的独特反应性。

1. 分子卤素脱氢（X2）：
该方法涉及丙烷与卤素（尤其是氯和碘）的直接反应。例如，在400°C下使用0.28% Ru/TiO2催化剂进行Cl2辅助丙烷卤脱氢可达到50%的C3H8转化率和95%的C3H6选择性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。碘介导的脱氢通常比传统路线产生更高的丙烯收率，但需要更高的温度（>673 K）[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551]。

2. 卤素存在下的氧化脱氢（X2 + O2）：
这种方法将卤素与氧气结合以促进脱氢过程。添加氧气可以将平衡向更高的丙烷转化率方向移动，并使反应成为放热反应。例如，在500°C下，丙烷分压为0.1 atm的气相碘介导氧化脱氢可产生64%的丙烯[10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1]。

3. 通过卤化物的丙烷氧卤化（HX + O2）：
该方法使用卤化氢和氧气将丙烷转化为丙烯。反应通过卤化氢与O2的氧化进行，生成烷基卤化物、烯烃和水。理论上，在O2存在的情况下可以达到100%的卤素原子效率，相比之下卤脱氢只有50%[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。

4. 熔融金属卤化物盐辅助氧化脱氢（LiX + O2）：
这种方法使用熔融盐形式的金属碘化物，如LiI、CdI2、ZnI2和PbI2。反应在450-600°C之间进行，可以达到高达70%的丙烯产率[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。在一项研究中，使用5 wt.-% LiOH*H2O/LiI熔盐在600°C下，丙烷/空气混合比为1:2，结果显示高丙烯选择性[10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S]。

5. 双卤素介导脱氢：
这种新方法同时使用Br2和I2作为介质。在碘化反应中添加少量溴可显著促进丙烷转化和产物选择性。在低于800 K的温度下可以达到高达80%的丙烯和丙基卤化物产率[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551]。

这些方法的反应条件通常涉及300-700°C之间的温度，具体范围取决于所采用的方法。氧气的存在、卤素物种以及专门的催化剂或熔融盐是决定反应结果的关键因素。

丙烷在这些过程中的反应性与甲烷和乙烷显著不同，这是由于其分子结构的差异。丙烷既有伯碳原子又有仲碳原子，使其比只有伯碳的甲烷和乙烷更具反应性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。这允许更容易的C-H键活化和脱氢。此外，丙烷可以形成更稳定的中间物种，更容易进行脱氢生成丙烯[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551]。

丙烯（产物）中存在的活泼烯丙基氢原子可能导致连续氧化，与乙烷产生乙烯相比，这导致了较低的选择性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。此外，在高自由基浓度下，丙烷可以进行偶联反应形成较大的分子如苯，这在甲烷中是不可能的，在乙烷中也不太可能发生[10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S]。

综合理解与未来展望：
卤素辅助丙烷脱氢方法的发展代表了烷烃活化领域的重大进步。这些方法为传统脱氢工艺提供了有前途的替代方案，有潜力解决热力学限制和催化剂失活等挑战。

该领域的未来研究可以集中在：

1. 催化剂设计：开发更高效、更稳定的催化剂，能够耐受卤素物种的腐蚀性，同时保持对丙烯的高选择性。

2. 工艺优化：将这些卤素辅助方法整合到现有的工业流程中，着重于能源效率和成本效益。

3. 环境考虑：研究最小化卤素排放的方法，开发卤素循环利用的闭环系统。

4. 机理研究：进一步阐明反应机理，特别是在双卤素介导脱氢等复杂系统中，以指导合理的工艺设计。

5. 应用于其他烷烃：探索这些方法在活化长链烷烃或更具挑战性的底物（如甲烷）方面的潜力。

6. 新型反应器设计：开发专门的反应器，能够有效管理卤素辅助工艺的独特挑战，如耐腐蚀性和熔融盐系统中的热管理。

通过解决这些领域的问题，研究人员可能会开辟新的途径，实现更高效、更选择性的烷烃活化，为可持续化学品生产和能源系统的更广泛领域做出贡献。
