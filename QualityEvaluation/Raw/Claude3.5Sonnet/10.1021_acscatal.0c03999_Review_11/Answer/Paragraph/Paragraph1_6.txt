Halogen-assisted methods for selective propane dehydrogenation: A comparative analysis of various approaches, reaction conditions, and propane's unique reactivity

Halogen-assisted methods for selectively dehydrogenating propane to propene have emerged as promising alternatives to conventional dehydrogenation processes. These methods can be broadly categorized into several approaches, each with its unique reaction conditions and performance characteristics:

1. Dehydrogenation with molecular halogens (X2):
This method involves the direct reaction of propane with halogens, particularly chlorine and iodine. For instance, propane halo-dehydrogenation using Cl2 over a 0.28% Ru/TiO2 catalyst achieves 50% C3H8 conversion and 95% C3H6 selectivity at 400°C [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. Iodine-mediated dehydrogenation generally yields higher propylene yields compared to conventional routes but requires higher temperatures (>673 K) [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551].

2. Oxidative dehydrogenation in the presence of halogens (X2 + O2):
This approach combines halogens with oxygen to facilitate the dehydrogenation process. The addition of oxygen shifts the equilibrium towards higher propane conversion and makes the reaction exothermic [10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1]. For example, using gaseous I2 as a catalyst in the presence of propane and oxygen at 500°C with a propane partial pressure of 0.1 atm yields 64% propylene [10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1].

3. Propane oxyhalogenation via halides (HX + O2):
This method uses hydrogen halides and oxygen to convert propane to propene. The reaction proceeds through the oxidation of hydrogen halide with O2, producing alkyl halide, alkene, and water. Theoretically, it can achieve 100% halogen atom efficiency in the presence of O2, compared to 50% for halo-dehydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11].

4. Molten metal halide salt-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation (LiX + O2):
This approach uses metal iodides like LiI, CdI2, ZnI2, and PbI2 in a molten salt form. The reaction typically occurs between 450-600°C and can achieve propene yields up to 70% [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. A specific example uses a 5 wt.-% LiOH*H2O/LiI salt melt at 600°C, with a propane/air mixture of 1:2 [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S].

5. Bihalogen-mediated dehydrogenation:
This novel approach uses both Br2 and I2 as mediators. Adding a small amount of bromine to the iodination reaction significantly promotes propane conversion and product selectivity. High yields of propene and propyl halides (up to 80%) can be achieved at moderate temperatures below 800 K [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551].

Propane's reactivity in these processes differs significantly from methane and ethane due to its molecular structure. Propane has both primary and secondary carbon atoms, making it more reactive than methane and ethane, which only have primary carbons [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. This allows for easier C-H bond activation and dehydrogenation. Additionally, propane can form more stable intermediate species and undergo dehydrogenation more readily to produce propene [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551].

The presence of reactive allylic hydrogen atoms in propene (the product) can lead to successive oxidation, resulting in lower selectivity compared to ethylene production from ethane [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. At high radical concentrations, propane can undergo coupling reactions to form larger molecules like benzene, which is not possible with methane and less likely with ethane [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S].

The radical nature of the reaction mechanism and the ratio of normal/isopropyl radicals significantly influence the selectivity towards propene. Lower temperatures generally favor higher propene selectivities by suppressing secondary reactions that lead to byproducts like benzene [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The various halogen-assisted methods for propane dehydrogenation offer promising alternatives to conventional processes, each with its own advantages and challenges. Future research efforts might focus on:

1. Catalyst development: Designing novel catalysts that can enhance selectivity and conversion while minimizing side reactions and catalyst deactivation.

2. Process optimization: Fine-tuning reaction conditions, such as temperature, pressure, and reactant ratios, to maximize propene yield and minimize energy consumption.

3. Halogen recycling: Developing more efficient methods for halogen recovery and reuse to improve the economic viability and environmental sustainability of these processes.

4. Reactor design: Investigating new reactor configurations that can better manage heat transfer and product separation, potentially leading to improved process efficiency.

5. Hybrid approaches: Exploring combinations of different halogen-assisted methods or integrating them with conventional dehydrogenation processes to exploit synergies and overcome individual limitations.

6. Mechanistic studies: Conducting in-depth investigations into the reaction mechanisms, particularly the role of radicals and intermediates, to guide the development of more selective and efficient processes.

7. Scale-up and commercialization: Addressing challenges related to scaling up these processes and assessing their feasibility for industrial implementation.

By focusing on these areas, researchers may unlock the full potential of halogen-assisted propane dehydrogenation, potentially revolutionizing propene production in the chemical industry.

卤素辅助的选择性丙烷脱氢方法：各种方法、反应条件和丙烷独特反应性的比较分析

卤素辅助的选择性丙烷脱氢制丙烯方法已成为传统脱氢工艺的有前景的替代方案。这些方法可以大致分为几种方法，每种方法都有其独特的反应条件和性能特征：

1. 分子卤素（X2）脱氢：
这种方法涉及丙烷与卤素（特别是氯和碘）的直接反应。例如，使用0.28% Ru/TiO2催化剂在400°C下进行Cl2辅助丙烷卤脱氢，可实现50%的C3H8转化率和95%的C3H6选择性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。碘介导的脱氢通常比传统路径产生更高的丙烯收率，但需要更高的温度（>673 K）[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551]。

2. 卤素存在下的氧化脱氢（X2 + O2）：
这种方法结合卤素和氧气来促进脱氢过程。添加氧气将平衡向更高的丙烷转化率转移，并使反应变为放热[10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1]。例如，在500°C下使用气相I2作为催化剂，丙烷分压为0.1 atm，可获得64%的丙烯收率[10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1]。

3. 通过卤化物的丙烷氧卤化（HX + O2）：
这种方法使用卤化氢和氧气将丙烷转化为丙烯。反应通过卤化氢与O2的氧化进行，产生烷基卤化物、烯烃和水。理论上，在O2存在的情况下可以实现100%的卤素原子效率，相比之下，卤脱氢的效率仅为50%[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。

4. 熔融金属卤化物盐辅助的氧化脱氢（LiX + O2）：
这种方法使用熔融盐形式的金属碘化物，如LiI、CdI2、ZnI2和PbI2。反应通常在450-600°C之间进行，可以实现高达70%的丙烯收率[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。一个具体的例子是使用5 wt.-% LiOH*H2O/LiI盐熔体在600°C下进行反应，丙烷/空气混合比为1:2 [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S]。

5. 双卤素介导的脱氢：
这种新颖的方法同时使用Br2和I2作为介质。在碘化反应中添加少量溴可显著提高丙烷转化率和产品选择性。在温和的温度（低于800 K）下可以实现高达80%的丙烯和丙基卤化物收率[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551]。

丙烷在这些过程中的反应性与甲烷和乙烷显著不同，这是由于其分子结构的差异。丙烷同时具有伯碳和仲碳原子，使其比只有伯碳的甲烷和乙烷更具反应性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。这使得C-H键活化和脱氢更容易进行。此外，丙烷可以形成更稳定的中间物种，更容易进行脱氢以产生丙烯[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551]。

丙烯（产物）中存在的活性烯丙基氢原子可能导致连续氧化，从而导致选择性低于乙烷生产乙烯的过程[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。在高自由基浓度下，丙烷可以进行偶联反应形成较大的分子如苯，这在甲烷中是不可能的，在乙烷中也不太可能发生[10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S]。

反应机理的自由基性质和正丙基/异丙基自由基的比例显著影响了丙烯的选择性。较低的温度通常有利于更高的丙烯选择性，因为它抑制了导致副产物（如苯）的次级反应[10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S]。

综合理解与未来展望：各种卤素辅助的丙烷脱氢方法为传统工艺提供了有前景的替代方案，每种方法都有其自身的优势和挑战。未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 催化剂开发：设计新型催化剂，可以提高选择性和转化率，同时最小化副反应和催化剂失活。

2. 工艺优化：微调反应条件，如温度、压力和反应物比例，以最大化丙烯产率并最小化能源消耗。

3. 卤素回收：开发更高效的卤素回收和重复使用方法，以提高这些工艺的经济可行性和环境可持续性。

4. 反应器设计：研究新的反应器配置，可以更好地管理热传递和产品分离，潜在地提高工艺效率。

5. 混合方法：探索不同卤素辅助方法的组合，或将它们与传统脱氢工艺整合，以利用协同效应并克服各自的局限性。

6. 机理研究：对反应机理进行深入研究，特别是自由基和中间体的作用，以指导开发更具选择性和效率的工艺。

7. 规模放大和商业化：解决与这些工艺放大相关的挑战，并评估其在工业实施中的可行性。

通过专注于这些领域，研究人员可能会释放卤素辅助丙烷脱氢的全部潜力，可能彻底改变化学工业中的丙烯生产。
