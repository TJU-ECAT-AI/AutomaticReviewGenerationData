<English>
Halogen-assisted methods for selective propane dehydrogenation have emerged as promising approaches to produce propene with high yields and selectivity. This review analyzes various methods, their reaction conditions, and propane's unique reactivity in these processes.

1. Dehydrogenation with molecular halogens (X2):
This method directly uses halogens to remove hydrogen from propane. For example, chlorine-mediated dehydrogenation over a 0.28% Ru/TiO2 catalyst at 400°C achieves 50% propane conversion with 95% propene selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. Iodine-mediated dehydrogenation generally gives higher propylene yields compared to conventional routes, but requires higher temperatures (>673 K) [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551].

2. Oxidative dehydrogenation in the presence of halogens (X2 + O2):
This approach combines halogens with oxygen to facilitate the dehydrogenation process. The addition of oxygen shifts the equilibrium towards higher propane conversion and makes the reaction exothermic [10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1]. For instance, using gaseous I2 as a catalyst at 500°C with a propane partial pressure of 0.1 atm yields 64% propylene [10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1].

3. Propane oxyhalogenation via halides (HX + O2):
This method uses hydrogen halides and oxygen to convert propane to propene. The reaction proceeds through the oxidation of hydrogen halide with O2, producing alkyl halide, alkene, and water. Theoretically, it can achieve 100% halogen atom efficiency in the presence of O2, compared to 50% for halo-dehydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11].

4. Molten metal halide salt-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation (LiX + O2):
This approach uses metal iodides like LiI, CdI2, ZnI2, and PbI2 in a molten salt form. The reaction typically occurs between 450-600°C and can achieve propene yields up to 70% [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. In one study, using a 5 wt.-% LiOH*H2O/LiI salt melt at 600°C with a propane/air mixture of 1:2 achieved 80% propane conversion and 84% selectivity to propylene [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S].

5. Bihalogen-mediated dehydrogenation:
This novel approach uses both Br2 and I2 as mediators. Adding a small amount of bromine to the iodination reaction significantly promotes propane conversion and product selectivity. High yields of propene and propyl halides (up to 80%) can be achieved at moderate temperatures below 800 K [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551].

Propane's reactivity in these processes differs significantly from methane and ethane due to its molecular structure. Propane has both primary and secondary carbon atoms, making it more reactive than methane and ethane, which only have primary carbons [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. This allows for easier C-H bond activation and dehydrogenation. Additionally, propane can form more stable intermediate species and undergo dehydrogenation more readily to produce propene [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551].

The presence of reactive allylic hydrogen atoms in propene (the product) can lead to successive oxidation, resulting in lower selectivity compared to ethylene production from ethane [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. At high radical concentrations, propane can undergo coupling reactions to form larger molecules like benzene, which is not possible with methane and less likely with ethane [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The various halogen-assisted methods for propane dehydrogenation offer promising alternatives to conventional thermal dehydrogenation processes. Each method has its strengths and limitations, with the choice depending on factors such as desired yield, selectivity, and operating conditions.

Future research could focus on:
1. Developing more efficient and cost-effective catalysts for halogen-mediated reactions, particularly for lower temperature operations.
2. Optimizing reactor designs to better integrate the different reaction zones in processes like oxyhalogenation.
3. Exploring novel halogen combinations or ratios in bihalogen-mediated processes to further enhance selectivity and yield.
4. Investigating the potential of other metal halide salts or mixtures for molten salt-assisted dehydrogenation.
5. Addressing challenges related to corrosion and environmental concerns associated with halogen use in industrial-scale processes.
6. Developing in-situ regeneration methods for halogen species to improve overall process efficiency.

The unique reactivity of propane in these processes opens up possibilities for tailored reaction conditions and catalysts that can exploit its structural features. Future work could aim to better understand and control the formation of radical species and intermediate products to maximize propene yield while minimizing unwanted side reactions.

As the demand for propene continues to grow, these halogen-assisted methods have the potential to play a significant role in meeting production needs more efficiently and sustainably than traditional processes.

<Chinese>
卤素辅助法选择性丙烷脱氢已成为一种有前景的方法，可以高产率和高选择性地生产丙烯。本综述分析了各种方法、反应条件以及丙烷在这些过程中的独特反应活性。

1. 分子卤素脱氢 (X2)：
这种方法直接使用卤素从丙烷中除去氢。例如，在400°C下使用0.28% Ru/TiO2催化剂进行氯介导的脱氢可实现50%的丙烷转化率和95%的丙烯选择性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。碘介导的脱氢通常比常规路线产生更高的丙烯产率，但需要更高的温度（>673 K）[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551]。

2. 卤素存在下的氧化脱氢 (X2 + O2)：
这种方法将卤素与氧气结合以促进脱氢过程。添加氧气可以将平衡转向更高的丙烷转化率，并使反应变为放热 [10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1]。例如，在500°C下使用气相I2作为催化剂，丙烷分压为0.1 atm，可产生64%的丙烯 [10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1]。

3. 通过卤化物的丙烷氧卤化 (HX + O2)：
这种方法使用卤化氢和氧气将丙烷转化为丙烯。反应通过卤化氢与O2的氧化进行，产生烷基卤化物、烯烃和水。理论上，在O2存在的情况下可以达到100%的卤素原子效率，相比之下，卤素脱氢的效率仅为50% [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。

4. 熔融金属卤化物盐辅助氧化脱氢 (LiX + O2)：
这种方法使用熔融盐形式的金属碘化物，如LiI、CdI2、ZnI2和PbI2。反应通常在450-600°C之间进行，丙烯产率可达70% [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。在一项研究中，使用5 wt.-% LiOH*H2O/LiI盐熔体在600°C下，丙烷/空气混合比为1:2，实现了80%的丙烷转化率和84%的丙烯选择性 [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S]。

5. 双卤素介导脱氢：
这种新型方法同时使用Br2和I2作为介质。在碘化反应中添加少量溴可显著促进丙烷转化和产物选择性。在800 K以下的中等温度下可以获得高达80%的丙烯和丙基卤化物产率 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551]。

丙烷在这些过程中的反应活性与甲烷和乙烷显著不同，这是由于其分子结构。丙烷同时具有伯碳和仲碳原子，使其比只有伯碳的甲烷和乙烷更具反应性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。这允许更容易的C-H键活化和脱氢。此外，丙烷可以形成更稳定的中间物种，并更容易进行脱氢生成丙烯 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551]。

丙烯（产物）中存在的活泼烯丙基氢原子可能导致连续氧化，与乙烷生成乙烯相比，这导致了较低的选择性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。在高自由基浓度下，丙烷可以进行偶联反应形成苯等较大分子，这在甲烷中是不可能的，在乙烷中也不太可能发生 [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：各种卤素辅助丙烷脱氢方法为传统热脱氢过程提供了有前景的替代方案。每种方法都有其优势和局限性，选择取决于所需的产率、选择性和操作条件等因素。

未来研究可以集中在：
1. 开发更高效和经济的卤素介导反应催化剂，特别是用于低温操作的催化剂。
2. 优化反应器设计，以更好地集成氧卤化等过程中的不同反应区域。
3. 探索新的卤素组合或比例在双卤素介导过程中，以进一步提高选择性和产率。
4. 研究其他金属卤化物盐或混合物在熔融盐辅助脱氢中的潜力。
5. 解决与工业规模过程中使用卤素相关的腐蚀和环境问题。
6. 开发卤素物种的原位再生方法，以提高整体过程效率。

丙烷在这些过程中的独特反应活性为定制反应条件和催化剂开辟了可能性，这些条件和催化剂可以利用其结构特征。未来的工作可以致力于更好地理解和控制自由基物种和中间产物的形成，以最大化丙烯产率，同时最小化不希望的副反应。

随着对丙烯需求的持续增长，这些卤素辅助方法有潜力比传统工艺更高效、更可持续地满足生产需求。
