Halogen-assisted methods for selective propane dehydrogenation: A comparative analysis of various approaches, reaction conditions, and propane's unique reactivity

The selective dehydrogenation of propane to propene using halogen-assisted methods has emerged as a promising alternative to conventional dehydrogenation processes. This review analyzes various approaches, their reaction conditions, and propane's unique reactivity in these processes.

1. Dehydrogenation with molecular halogens (X2):
This method directly uses halogens to remove hydrogen from propane. For instance, propane halo-dehydrogenation using Cl2 over a 0.28% Ru/TiO2 catalyst achieves 50% C3H8 conversion and 95% C3H6 selectivity at 400°C [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. Iodine-mediated dehydrogenation generally yields higher propylene yields compared to conventional routes, but requires high temperatures (>673 K) [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551].

2. Oxidative dehydrogenation in the presence of halogens (X2 + O2):
This approach combines halogens with oxygen to facilitate the dehydrogenation process. The addition of oxygen shifts the equilibrium towards higher propane conversion and makes the reaction exothermic [10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1]. For example, gas-phase iodine-mediated oxidative dehydrogenation at 500°C with a propane partial pressure of 0.1 atm yields 64% propylene [10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1].

3. Propane oxyhalogenation via halides (HX + O2):
This method uses hydrogen halides and oxygen to convert propane to propene. The reaction proceeds through the oxidation of hydrogen halide with O2, producing alkyl halide, alkene, and water. Theoretically, it can achieve 100% halogen atom efficiency in the presence of O2, compared to 50% for halo-dehydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11].

4. Molten metal halide salt-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation (LiX + O2):
This approach uses metal iodides like LiI, CdI2, ZnI2, and PbI2 in a molten salt form. The reaction occurs between 450-600°C and can achieve propene yields up to 70% [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. A study using 5 wt.-% LiOH*H2O/LiI salt melt at 600°C, with a propane/air mixture of 1:2, achieved 80% propane conversion and 84% selectivity to propylene at 500°C [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S] [10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1].

5. Bihalogen-mediated dehydrogenation:
This novel approach uses both Br2 and I2 as mediators. Adding a small amount of bromine to the iodination reaction significantly promotes propane conversion and product selectivity. High yields of propene and propyl halides (up to 80%) can be achieved at moderate temperatures below 800 K [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551] [10.1002/cctc.201200913].

Reaction conditions vary for each method but generally involve temperatures between 300-700°C, with some methods requiring higher temperatures (up to 900K for iodine-based processes) [10.1002/cctc.201200913]. Most reactions occur at or near atmospheric pressure.

Propane's reactivity in these processes differs significantly from methane and ethane due to its molecular structure. Propane has both primary and secondary carbon atoms, making it more reactive than methane and ethane, which only have primary carbons [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. This allows for easier C-H bond activation and dehydrogenation. Additionally, propane can form more stable intermediate species and undergo dehydrogenation more readily to produce propene [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551].

The presence of reactive allylic hydrogen atoms in propene (the product) can lead to successive oxidation, resulting in lower selectivity compared to ethylene production from ethane [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. At high radical concentrations, propane can undergo coupling reactions to form larger molecules like benzene, which is not possible with methane and less likely with ethane [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The comparative analysis of halogen-assisted methods for propane dehydrogenation reveals promising avenues for future research and development. The bihalogen-mediated approach, combining the selectivity of iodine with the reactivity of bromine, emerges as a particularly interesting direction for further investigation. This method addresses the limitations of using single halogens and achieves higher yields at lower temperatures.

Future research efforts might focus on:
1. Optimizing catalyst designs for bihalogen-mediated processes to further improve selectivity and yield.
2. Exploring novel molten salt compositions to enhance heat management and equilibrium shifts in metal halide salt-assisted approaches.
3. Developing more efficient methods for halogen recycling to improve the economic viability and environmental sustainability of these processes.
4. Investigating the potential of combining halogen-assisted methods with other emerging technologies, such as membrane reactors or plasma-assisted catalysis, to achieve synergistic effects.
5. Conducting more comprehensive studies on the reaction mechanisms and kinetics of propane activation in various halogen-assisted processes to gain deeper insights into the unique reactivity of propane compared to other alkanes.

These advancements could lead to more efficient and sustainable propylene production processes, potentially revolutionizing the petrochemical industry and reducing its environmental impact.

卤素辅助的选择性丙烷脱氢方法：各种方法、反应条件和丙烷独特反应性的比较分析

使用卤素辅助方法选择性地将丙烷脱氢生成丙烯已成为传统脱氢工艺的一种有前景的替代方案。本综述分析了各种方法、其反应条件以及丙烷在这些过程中的独特反应性。

1. 分子卤素（X2）脱氢：
这种方法直接使用卤素从丙烷中移除氢。例如，在400°C下使用0.28% Ru/TiO2催化剂的Cl2丙烷卤脱氢可实现50%的C3H8转化率和95%的C3H6选择性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。碘介导的脱氢通常比传统路线产生更高的丙烯收率，但需要高温（>673 K）[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551]。

2. 卤素存在下的氧化脱氢（X2 + O2）：
这种方法将卤素与氧气结合以促进脱氢过程。添加氧气使平衡向更高的丙烷转化率转移，并使反应成为放热反应 [10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1]。例如，在500°C下，丙烷分压为0.1 atm的气相碘介导氧化脱氢可产生64%的丙烯 [10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1]。

3. 通过卤化物的丙烷氧卤化（HX + O2）：
这种方法使用卤化氢和氧气将丙烷转化为丙烯。反应通过卤化氢与O2的氧化进行，产生烷基卤化物、烯烃和水。理论上，在O2存在的情况下可以实现100%的卤素原子效率，相比之下，卤脱氢的效率限制在50% [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。

4. 熔融金属卤化物盐辅助氧化脱氢（LiX + O2）：
这种方法使用LiI、CdI2、ZnI2和PbI2等金属碘化物的熔融盐形式。反应在450-600°C之间进行，可实现高达70%的丙烯收率 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。一项使用5 wt.-% LiOH*H2O/LiI盐熔体在600°C下，丙烷/空气混合比为1:2的研究，在500°C下实现了80%的丙烷转化率和84%的丙烯选择性 [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S] [10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1]。

5. 双卤素介导脱氢：
这种新方法同时使用Br2和I2作为介质。在碘化反应中添加少量溴显著促进了丙烷转化和产品选择性。在800 K以下的中等温度下可以实现高达80%的丙烯和丙基卤化物收率 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551] [10.1002/cctc.201200913]。

各种方法的反应条件不同，但通常涉及300-700°C的温度，某些方法需要更高的温度（碘基过程高达900K）[10.1002/cctc.201200913]。大多数反应在大气压或接近大气压下进行。

由于分子结构的不同，丙烷在这些过程中的反应性与甲烷和乙烷显著不同。丙烷同时具有伯碳和仲碳原子，使其比只有伯碳的甲烷和乙烷更具反应性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。这允许更容易的C-H键活化和脱氢。此外，丙烷可以形成更稳定的中间物种，更容易进行脱氢生成丙烯 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551]。

丙烯（产物）中存在的活性烯丙基氢原子可能导致连续氧化，与乙烷生成乙烯相比，这导致了较低的选择性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。在高自由基浓度下，丙烷可以进行偶联反应形成更大的分子，如苯，这对甲烷来说是不可能的，对乙烷来说也不太可能 [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：
对丙烷脱氢的卤素辅助方法的比较分析揭示了未来研究和发展的有前景的方向。双卤素介导方法，结合了碘的选择性和溴的反应性，成为特别有趣的进一步研究方向。这种方法解决了使用单一卤素的局限性，并在较低温度下实现了更高的收率。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：
1. 优化双卤素介导过程的催化剂设计，以进一步提高选择性和收率。
2. 探索新型熔融盐组成，以增强金属卤化物盐辅助方法中的热管理和平衡转移。
3. 开发更高效的卤素回收方法，以提高这些过程的经济可行性和环境可持续性。
4. 研究将卤素辅助方法与其他新兴技术（如膜反应器或等离子体辅助催化）相结合的潜力，以实现协同效应。
5. 对各种卤素辅助过程中丙烷活化的反应机理和动力学进行更全面的研究，以深入了解丙烷与其他烷烃相比的独特反应性。

这些进展可能导致更高效、更可持续的丙烯生产工艺，有可能革新石化工业并减少其环境影响。
