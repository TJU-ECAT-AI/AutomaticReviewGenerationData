Halogen-assisted methods for selective propane dehydrogenation: A comparative analysis of various approaches, reaction conditions, and propane's unique reactivity

Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) is a crucial process for producing propylene, an essential feedstock for various industrial applications. Several halogen-assisted methods have been developed to enhance the efficiency and selectivity of this process. This review compares these methods, their reaction conditions, and analyzes propane's unique reactivity in these processes.

1. Dehydrogenation with molecular halogens (X2):
This method directly uses halogens to remove hydrogen from propane. For instance, chlorine-mediated dehydrogenation using a 0.28% Ru/TiO2 catalyst at 400°C achieves 50% propane conversion with 95% propene selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. Iodine-mediated dehydrogenation generally yields higher propylene yields compared to conventional routes, but requires higher temperatures (>673 K) [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551].

2. Oxidative dehydrogenation in the presence of halogens (X2 + O2):
This approach combines halogens with oxygen to facilitate the dehydrogenation process. For example, using gaseous I2 as a catalyst in the presence of propane and oxygen at 500°C with a propane partial pressure of 0.1 atm yields 64% propylene [10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1].

3. Propane oxyhalogenation via halides (HX + O2):
This method uses hydrogen halides and oxygen to convert propane to propene. It can theoretically achieve 100% halogen atom efficiency in the presence of O2, compared to 50% for halo-dehydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. Propane oxychlorination using metal phosphate catalysts (MPO, where M = Ti, V, Cr, Mn, Fe, Co, Ni, Cu) at 673-773 K has shown promising results, with CrPO achieving propylene yields up to 50% at 95% selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.9b01315].

4. Molten metal halide salt-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation (LiX + O2):
This approach uses metal iodides like LiI, CdI2, ZnI2, and PbI2 in a molten salt form. The reaction occurs between 450-600°C and can achieve propene yields up to 70% [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. A more specific example using 5 wt.-% LiOH*H2O/LiI salt melt at 600°C, with a propane/air mixture of 1:2, has achieved 80% propane conversion and 84% selectivity to propylene [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S] [10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1].

5. Bihalogen-mediated dehydrogenation:
This novel approach uses both Br2 and I2 as mediators. Adding a small amount of bromine to the iodination reaction significantly promotes propane conversion and product selectivity. High yields of propene and propyl halides (up to 80%) can be achieved at moderate temperatures below 800 K [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551] [10.1002/cctc.201200913].

Propane's reactivity in these processes differs significantly from methane and ethane due to its molecular structure. Propane has both primary and secondary carbon atoms, making it more reactive than methane and ethane, which only have primary carbons [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. This allows for easier C-H bond activation and dehydrogenation. Additionally, propane can form more stable intermediate species and undergo dehydrogenation more readily to produce propene [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551].

The presence of reactive allylic hydrogen atoms in propene (the product) can lead to successive oxidation, resulting in lower selectivity compared to ethylene production from ethane [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. At high radical concentrations, propane can undergo coupling reactions to form larger molecules like benzene, which is not possible with methane and less likely with ethane [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The comparative analysis of various halogen-assisted methods for propane dehydrogenation reveals a complex landscape of reaction pathways and catalytic systems. Each method offers unique advantages and challenges, with the choice depending on factors such as desired yield, selectivity, and process economics.

Future research directions may include:

1. Catalyst development: Exploring novel catalysts or catalyst combinations that can further enhance selectivity and yield while minimizing side reactions and catalyst deactivation.

2. Process intensification: Investigating ways to combine the advantages of different methods, such as the dual halogen approach, to achieve optimal performance at lower temperatures and pressures.

3. Sustainability: Developing more environmentally friendly processes by focusing on halogen recovery and recycling, reducing energy consumption, and minimizing waste generation.

4. Mechanistic studies: Conducting in-depth investigations into the reaction mechanisms of various halogen-assisted methods to better understand and control the formation of byproducts and optimize reaction conditions.

5. Scale-up and industrial implementation: Addressing challenges related to scaling up these processes for industrial use, including reactor design, heat management, and process control strategies.

6. Alternative feedstocks: Exploring the applicability of these halogen-assisted methods to other alkanes or mixed feedstocks, potentially opening new avenues for chemical production.

By addressing these areas, researchers can work towards developing more efficient, selective, and sustainable processes for propane dehydrogenation, ultimately contributing to the advancement of the chemical industry and the production of valuable olefins.

卤素辅助的选择性丙烷脱氢方法：各种方法、反应条件和丙烷独特反应性的比较分析

丙烷脱氢（PDH）是生产丙烯的关键过程，丙烯是各种工业应用的重要原料。为了提高这一过程的效率和选择性，已经开发了几种卤素辅助方法。本综述比较了这些方法、它们的反应条件，并分析了丙烷在这些过程中的独特反应性。

1. 分子卤素脱氢（X2）：
这种方法直接使用卤素从丙烷中去除氢。例如，在400°C下使用0.28% Ru/TiO2催化剂进行氯介导的脱氢可实现50%的丙烷转化率和95%的丙烯选择性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。碘介导的脱氢通常比常规路线产生更高的丙烯收率，但需要更高的温度（>673 K）[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551]。

2. 卤素存在下的氧化脱氢（X2 + O2）：
这种方法将卤素与氧气结合以促进脱氢过程。例如，在500°C下使用气相I2作为催化剂，丙烷分压为0.1 atm，可获得64%的丙烯收率 [10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1]。

3. 通过卤化物的丙烷氧卤化（HX + O2）：
这种方法使用卤化氢和氧气将丙烷转化为丙烯。理论上，在O2存在的情况下可以达到100%的卤素原子效率，相比之下，卤素脱氢的效率为50% [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。使用金属磷酸盐催化剂（MPO，其中M = Ti，V，Cr，Mn，Fe，Co，Ni，Cu）在673-773 K下进行的丙烷氧氯化显示了有前景的结果，CrPO在95%的选择性下实现了高达50%的丙烯收率 [10.1021/acscatal.9b01315]。

4. 熔融金属卤化物盐辅助的氧化脱氢（LiX + O2）：
这种方法使用熔融盐形式的金属碘化物，如LiI、CdI2、ZnI2和PbI2。反应发生在450-600°C之间，可以达到高达70%的丙烯收率 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。一个更具体的例子是在600°C下使用5 wt.-% LiOH*H2O/LiI盐熔体，丙烷/空气混合比为1:2，实现了80%的丙烷转化率和84%的丙烯选择性 [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S] [10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1]。

5. 双卤素介导的脱氢：
这种新方法同时使用Br2和I2作为介质。向碘化反应中添加少量溴显著促进了丙烷转化和产物选择性。在800 K以下的温度下可以达到高达80%的丙烯和丙基卤化物收率 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551] [10.1002/cctc.201200913]。

丙烷在这些过程中的反应性与甲烷和乙烷显著不同，这是由于其分子结构。丙烷具有一级和二级碳原子，使其比只有一级碳的甲烷和乙烷更具反应性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。这允许更容易的C-H键活化和脱氢。此外，丙烷可以形成更稳定的中间物种，更容易进行脱氢生成丙烯 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551]。

丙烯（产物）中存在的活性烯丙基氢原子可能导致连续氧化，从而导致选择性低于乙烷生成乙烯的过程 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。在高自由基浓度下，丙烷可以进行偶联反应形成更大的分子如苯，这在甲烷中是不可能的，在乙烷中也不太可能发生 [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S]。

综合理解与未来展望：
对丙烷脱氢的各种卤素辅助方法的比较分析揭示了反应途径和催化系统的复杂景象。每种方法都提供了独特的优势和挑战，选择取决于所需的产率、选择性和工艺经济性等因素。

未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 催化剂开发：探索新型催化剂或催化剂组合，以进一步提高选择性和产率，同时最小化副反应和催化剂失活。

2. 工艺强化：研究如何结合不同方法的优势，如双卤素方法，以在较低温度和压力下实现最佳性能。

3. 可持续性：通过关注卤素回收和再利用、减少能源消耗和最小化废物生成，开发更环保的工艺。

4. 机理研究：对各种卤素辅助方法的反应机理进行深入研究，以更好地理解和控制副产品的形成，并优化反应条件。

5. 规模扩大和工业实施：解决将这些工艺扩大到工业使用的相关挑战，包括反应器设计、热管理和工艺控制策略。

6. 替代原料：探索这些卤素辅助方法对其他烷烃或混合原料的适用性，潜在地为化学品生产开辟新途径。

通过解决这些领域的问题，研究人员可以致力于开发更高效、更选择性和更可持续的丙烷脱氢工艺，最终为化学工业的进步和有价值烯烃的生产做出贡献。
