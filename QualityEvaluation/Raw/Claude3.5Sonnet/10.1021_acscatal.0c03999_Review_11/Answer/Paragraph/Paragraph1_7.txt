Halogen-assisted methods for selective propane dehydrogenation: A comparative analysis of various approaches, reaction conditions, and propane's unique reactivity

Several halogen-assisted methods have been developed for the selective dehydrogenation of propane to propene, each with distinct advantages and challenges. These methods include dehydrogenation with molecular halogens, oxidative dehydrogenation in the presence of halogens, propane oxyhalogenation, and molten metal halide salt-assisted approaches.

1. Dehydrogenation with molecular halogens (X2):
This method involves directly reacting propane with halogens, particularly chlorine or iodine. For example, propane halo-dehydrogenation using Cl2 over a 0.28% Ru/TiO2 catalyst achieves 50% C3H8 conversion and 95% C3H6 selectivity at 400°C [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. Iodine-mediated dehydrogenation generally gives higher propylene yields compared to conventional routes, but requires high temperatures (>673 K) to overcome thermodynamic limitations [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551].

2. Oxidative dehydrogenation in the presence of halogens (X2 + O2):
This approach combines halogens with oxygen to facilitate the dehydrogenation process. For instance, the addition of tetrachloromethane (TCM) to the feedstream in oxidative dehydrogenation over cerium oxide significantly improves propane conversion and C3H6 selectivity [10.1006/jcat.1999.2700]. Another example is the use of bromine-iodine mixtures with low iodine content (≤20%), which results in up to 80% of C3H6+C3H7X single-pass yield at moderate reaction temperatures (<800K) [10.1002/cctc.201200913].

3. Propane oxyhalogenation via halides (HX + O2):
This method uses hydrogen halides and oxygen to convert propane to propene. The reaction proceeds through the oxidation of hydrogen halide with O2, producing alkyl halide, alkene, and water. Theoretically, it can achieve 100% halogen atom efficiency in the presence of O2, compared to 50% for halo-dehydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. Propane oxychlorination using metal phosphate catalysts (MPO, where M = Ti, V, Cr, Mn, Fe, Co, Ni, Cu) at temperatures between 673-773 K has shown promising results, with the best performing catalyst (CrPO) achieving propylene yields up to 50% at 95% selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.9b01315].

4. Molten metal halide salt-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation (LiX + O2):
This approach uses metal iodides like LiI, CdI2, ZnI2, and PbI2 in a molten salt form. The reaction occurs between 450-600°C and can achieve propene yields up to 70% [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. A specific example is the use of a 5 wt.-% LiOH*H2O/LiI salt melt at 600°C, with a propane/air mixture of 1:2 [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S]. By optimizing this method, researchers have achieved 80% propane conversion and 84% selectivity to propylene at 500°C [10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1].

Propane's reactivity in these processes differs significantly from methane and ethane due to its molecular structure. Propane has both primary and secondary carbon atoms, making it more reactive than methane or ethane, which only have primary carbons [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. This allows for easier C-H bond activation and dehydrogenation. Additionally, propane can form more stable intermediate species and undergo dehydrogenation more readily to produce propene [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551].

However, the increased reactivity of propane also presents challenges. The presence of reactive allylic hydrogen atoms in propene (the product) can lead to successive oxidation, resulting in lower selectivity compared to ethylene production from ethane [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. At high radical concentrations, propane can undergo coupling reactions to form larger molecules like benzene, which is not possible with methane and less likely with ethane [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S].

In conclusion, each halogen-assisted method for propane dehydrogenation offers unique advantages and faces specific challenges. The choice of method depends on factors such as desired yield, selectivity, reaction conditions, and economic considerations. The distinct reactivity of propane compared to other alkanes necessitates careful optimization of reaction conditions and catalyst selection to maximize propene production while minimizing unwanted side reactions.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comparative analysis of various halogen-assisted methods for propane dehydrogenation reveals a complex interplay between reaction conditions, catalyst choice, and molecular structure. Future research in this field could focus on several promising directions:

1. Development of novel catalysts: Given the success of metal phosphates and molten metal halides, there is potential for designing new catalysts that combine the benefits of different approaches. For example, exploring bimetallic or multi-component catalysts that can facilitate both halogenation and dehydrogenation steps more efficiently.

2. Process intensification: Investigating ways to combine different methods, such as integrating molten salt approaches with gas-phase halogen catalysis, could lead to more efficient and selective processes. This could potentially address challenges like overoxidation and unwanted side reactions.

3. In-situ regeneration of halogens: Developing systems that can efficiently regenerate and recycle halogens in-situ could significantly improve the economic viability and sustainability of these processes.

4. Mechanistic studies: Further research into the reaction mechanisms, particularly the formation and behavior of radical intermediates, could provide insights for better controlling selectivity and minimizing unwanted products like benzene.

5. Alternative halogen sources: Exploring the use of more environmentally friendly or cost-effective halogen sources could make these processes more attractive for industrial implementation.

6. Reactor design: Innovative reactor designs that can better manage heat distribution and product separation could enhance the overall efficiency of these processes.

7. Computational modeling: Utilizing advanced computational methods to predict optimal reaction conditions and catalyst compositions could accelerate the development of more efficient propane dehydrogenation processes.

These research directions could lead to more sustainable and economically viable processes for propene production, potentially revolutionizing the petrochemical industry and reducing reliance on traditional, energy-intensive cracking processes.

卤素辅助选择性丙烷脱氢方法：各种方法、反应条件和丙烷独特反应性的比较分析

几种卤素辅助方法已被开发用于丙烷到丙烯的选择性脱氢，每种方法都有其独特的优势和挑战。这些方法包括分子卤素脱氢、卤素存在下的氧化脱氢、丙烷氧卤化和熔融金属卤化物盐辅助方法。

1. 分子卤素脱氢（X2）：
这种方法涉及丙烷直接与卤素反应，特别是氯或碘。例如，在400°C下使用0.28% Ru/TiO2催化剂的Cl2丙烷卤脱氢可实现50%的C3H8转化率和95%的C3H6选择性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。碘介导的脱氢通常比传统路线产生更高的丙烯收率，但需要高温（>673 K）来克服热力学限制[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551]。

2. 卤素存在下的氧化脱氢（X2 + O2）：
这种方法将卤素与氧气结合以促进脱氢过程。例如，在氧化铈上进行氧化脱氢时，向进料流中添加四氯甲烷（TCM）显著提高了丙烷转化率和C3H6选择性[10.1006/jcat.1999.2700]。另一个例子是使用低碘含量（≤20%）的溴-碘混合物，在温和的反应温度下（<800K）可实现高达80%的C3H6+C3H7X单程收率[10.1002/cctc.201200913]。

3. 通过卤化物的丙烷氧卤化（HX + O2）：
这种方法使用氢卤化物和氧气将丙烷转化为丙烯。反应通过氢卤化物与O2的氧化进行，生成烷基卤化物、烯烃和水。理论上，在O2存在的情况下可以实现100%的卤素原子效率，相比之下，卤脱氢的效率限制在50%[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。使用金属磷酸盐催化剂（MPO，其中M = Ti、V、Cr、Mn、Fe、Co、Ni、Cu）在673-773 K温度下进行的丙烷氧氯化显示了有希望的结果，表现最佳的催化剂（CrPO）在95%的选择性下实现了高达50%的丙烯收率[10.1021/acscatal.9b01315]。

4. 熔融金属卤化物盐辅助氧化脱氢（LiX + O2）：
这种方法使用熔融盐形式的金属碘化物，如LiI、CdI2、ZnI2和PbI2。反应发生在450-600°C之间，可以实现高达70%的丙烯收率[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。一个具体的例子是在600°C下使用5 wt.-% LiOH*H2O/LiI盐熔体，丙烷/空气混合比为1:2 [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S]。通过优化这种方法，研究人员在500°C下实现了80%的丙烷转化率和84%的丙烯选择性[10.1007/s10562-016-1701-1]。

丙烷在这些过程中的反应性与甲烷和乙烷显著不同，这是由于其分子结构的差异。丙烷同时具有伯碳和仲碳原子，使其比只有伯碳的甲烷或乙烷更具反应性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。这允许更容易的C-H键活化和脱氢。此外，丙烷可以形成更稳定的中间物种，并更容易进行脱氢生成丙烯[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551]。

然而，丙烷的反应性增加也带来了挑战。丙烯（产物）中存在的活性烯丙基氢原子可能导致连续氧化，导致选择性低于乙烷生成乙烯的选择性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。在高自由基浓度下，丙烷可以进行偶联反应形成更大的分子，如苯，这在甲烷中是不可能的，在乙烷中也不太可能发生[10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S]。

总之，每种卤素辅助丙烷脱氢方法都提供了独特的优势，并面临特定的挑战。方法的选择取决于所需的收率、选择性、反应条件和经济考虑等因素。丙烷与其他烷烃相比的独特反应性需要仔细优化反应条件和催化剂选择，以最大化丙烯产量，同时最小化不需要的副反应。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：对各种卤素辅助丙烷脱氢方法的比较分析揭示了反应条件、催化剂选择和分子结构之间复杂的相互作用。该领域的未来研究可以集中在几个有前景的方向：

1. 开发新型催化剂：鉴于金属磷酸盐和熔融金属卤化物的成功，设计能够结合不同方法优点的新催化剂有很大潜力。例如，探索可以更有效地促进卤化和脱氢步骤的双金属或多组分催化剂。

2. 过程强化：研究如何结合不同的方法，例如将熔融盐方法与气相卤素催化相结合，可能导致更高效和选择性的过程。这可能有助于解决过度氧化和不需要的副反应等挑战。

3. 卤素的原位再生：开发能够高效地原位再生和回收卤素的系统，可以显著提高这些过程的经济可行性和可持续性。

4. 机理研究：进一步研究反应机理，特别是自由基中间体的形成和行为，可以为更好地控制选择性和最小化不需要的产物（如苯）提供见解。

5. 替代卤素来源：探索使用更环保或更具成本效益的卤素来源，可以使这些过程对工业实施更具吸引力。

6. 反应器设计：创新的反应器设计，能够更好地管理热分布和产品分离，可以提高这些过程的整体效率。

7. 计算机模拟：利用先进的计算方法预测最佳反应条件和催化剂组成，可以加速开发更高效的丙烷脱氢过程。

这些研究方向可能导致更可持续和经济可行的丙烯生产过程，潜在地革新石油化工行业，并减少对传统高能耗裂解过程的依赖。
