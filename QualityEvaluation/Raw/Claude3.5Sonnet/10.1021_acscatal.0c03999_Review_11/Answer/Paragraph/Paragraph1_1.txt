<English>
Halogen-assisted methods for selective propane dehydrogenation have emerged as promising approaches to overcome the limitations of conventional dehydrogenation processes. This review analyzes various halogen-assisted methods, their reaction conditions, and propane's unique reactivity compared to other alkanes.

1. Dehydrogenation with molecular halogens (X2):
This method directly uses halogens to remove hydrogen from propane. For example, propane halo-dehydrogenation with Cl2 over a 0.28% Ru/TiO2 catalyst achieves 50% C3H8 conversion and 95% C3H6 selectivity at 400°C [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. Iodine-mediated dehydrogenation generally gives higher propylene yields compared to conventional routes, but requires high temperatures (>673 K) [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551].

2. Oxidative dehydrogenation in the presence of halogens (X2 + O2):
This approach combines halogens with oxygen to facilitate the dehydrogenation process. The addition of tetrachloromethane (TCM) to the feedstream in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane on cerium oxide significantly increases propane conversion and C3H6 selectivity [10.1006/jcat.1999.2700]. Iodine-based processes are highly selective but require very high temperatures (900K) to give good yields of C3H6+C3H7I [10.1002/cctc.201200913].

3. Propane oxyhalogenation via halides (HX + O2):
This method uses hydrogen halides and oxygen to convert propane to propene. Theoretically, it can achieve 100% halogen atom efficiency in the presence of O2, compared to 50% for halo-dehydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. Propane oxybromination over vanadium phosphate catalysts produces mainly ethyl bromide at low temperatures, but propylene selectivity increases rapidly with temperature (typically 750-850 K) [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551].

4. Molten metal halide salt-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation (LiX + O2):
This approach uses metal iodides like LiI, CdI2, ZnI2, and PbI2 in a molten salt form. The reaction occurs between 450-600°C and can achieve propene yields up to 70% [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. Experiments using a 5 wt.-% LiOH*H2O/LiI salt melt at 600°C, with a propane/air mixture of 1:2, have shown promising results [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S].

5. Bihalogen-mediated dehydrogenation:
This novel approach uses both Br2 and I2 as mediators. Adding a small amount of bromine to the iodination reaction significantly promotes propane conversion and product selectivity. High yields of propene and propyl halides (up to 80%) can be achieved at moderate temperatures below 800 K [10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551].

Propane's reactivity in these processes differs significantly from methane and ethane due to its molecular structure. Propane has both primary and secondary carbon atoms, making it more reactive than methane and ethane, which only have primary carbons [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]. The presence of reactive allylic hydrogen atoms in propene (the product) can lead to successive oxidation, resulting in lower selectivity compared to ethylene production from ethane [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11].

The ability to form both normal and isopropyl radicals leads to different reaction pathways and influences selectivity [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S]. At high radical concentrations, propane can undergo coupling reactions to form larger molecules like benzene, which is not possible with methane and less likely with ethane [10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of halogen-assisted methods for selective propane dehydrogenation has opened up new possibilities for more efficient and selective propylene production. The various approaches discussed offer different advantages and challenges, suggesting that future research could focus on optimizing these processes and potentially combining their best features.

One promising direction is the further development of bihalogen-mediated dehydrogenation, which has shown high yields at moderate temperatures. Research could explore different halogen combinations and ratios to fine-tune reactivity and selectivity. Additionally, the integration of molten salt techniques with bihalogen systems could potentially improve heat management and product separation.

The unique reactivity of propane compared to methane and ethane in these processes suggests that tailored catalyst designs could further enhance selectivity. Future studies might focus on developing catalysts that can selectively activate secondary C-H bonds while minimizing overoxidation of the propene product.

Furthermore, the development of in-situ regeneration methods for halogen species could improve the economic viability and sustainability of these processes. This could involve exploring novel reactor designs that allow for continuous regeneration of halogen species while maintaining high propene selectivity.

Lastly, computational modeling and machine learning techniques could be employed to predict optimal reaction conditions and catalyst compositions for different halogen-assisted methods, potentially leading to more efficient and selective processes for propane dehydrogenation.

卤素辅助的选择性丙烷脱氢方法已经成为克服传统脱氢过程局限性的有前景的方法。本综述分析了各种卤素辅助方法、其反应条件以及与其他烷烃相比丙烷的独特反应活性。

1. 分子卤素脱氢（X2）：
这种方法直接使用卤素从丙烷中去除氢。例如，在400°C下使用0.28% Ru/TiO2催化剂进行Cl2辅助的丙烷卤代脱氢可实现50%的C3H8转化率和95%的C3H6选择性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。碘介导的脱氢通常比传统路线提供更高的丙烯收率，但需要高温（>673 K）[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551]。

2. 卤素存在下的氧化脱氢（X2 + O2）：
这种方法将卤素与氧气结合以促进脱氢过程。在氧化铈上进行丙烷氧化脱氢时，向进料流中添加四氯甲烷（TCM）显著提高了丙烷转化率和C3H6选择性[10.1006/jcat.1999.2700]。基于碘的过程具有高选择性，但需要很高的温度（900K）才能获得良好的C3H6+C3H7I收率[10.1002/cctc.201200913]。

3. 通过卤化物进行丙烷氧卤化（HX + O2）：
这种方法使用卤化氢和氧气将丙烷转化为丙烯。理论上，在O2存在的情况下可以达到100%的卤素原子效率，相比之下卤代脱氢的效率仅为50%[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。在钒磷酸盐催化剂上进行丙烷氧溴化在低温下主要产生溴乙烷，但随着温度升高（通常为750-850 K），丙烯选择性迅速增加[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551]。

4. 熔融金属卤化物盐辅助氧化脱氢（LiX + O2）：
这种方法使用熔融盐形式的金属碘化物，如LiI、CdI2、ZnI2和PbI2。反应在450-600°C之间进行，可实现高达70%的丙烯收率[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。使用5 wt.-%LiOH*H2O/LiI盐熔体在600°C下进行实验，丙烷/空气混合比为1:2，显示了令人鼓舞的结果[10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S]。

5. 双卤素介导脱氢：
这种新颖的方法同时使用Br2和I2作为介质。在碘化反应中添加少量溴显著促进了丙烷转化和产品选择性。在800 K以下的中等温度下可以实现高达80%的丙烯和卤代丙基的收率[10.1021/acs.chemrev.6b00551]。

丙烷在这些过程中的反应活性与甲烷和乙烷明显不同，这是由于其分子结构。丙烷既有伯碳原子又有仲碳原子，使其比只有伯碳原子的甲烷和乙烷更具反应性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。产物丙烯中存在的活性烯丙基氢原子可能导致连续氧化，导致选择性低于乙烷生成乙烯的过程[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_11]。

形成正丙基和异丙基自由基的能力导致不同的反应途径并影响选择性[10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S]。在高自由基浓度下，丙烷可以进行偶联反应形成更大的分子如苯，这在甲烷中是不可能的，在乙烷中也不太可能发生[10.1016/0166-9834(91)80033-S]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：卤素辅助选择性丙烷脱氢方法的发展为更高效和选择性的丙烯生产开辟了新的可能性。讨论的各种方法提供了不同的优势和挑战，表明未来的研究可以集中在优化这些过程，并可能结合它们的最佳特征。

一个有前景的方向是进一步发展双卤素介导脱氢，该方法在中等温度下显示出高收率。研究可以探索不同的卤素组合和比例，以微调反应性和选择性。此外，将熔融盐技术与双卤素系统结合可能会改善热管理和产品分离。

与甲烷和乙烷相比，丙烷在这些过程中的独特反应性表明，定制的催化剂设计可以进一步提高选择性。未来的研究可能会集中在开发能够选择性激活仲C-H键同时最小化丙烯产物过度氧化的催化剂。

此外，开发卤素物种的原位再生方法可以提高这些过程的经济可行性和可持续性。这可能涉及探索新型反应器设计，允许卤素物种的连续再生，同时保持高丙烯选择性。

最后，计算机模拟和机器学习技术可用于预测不同卤素辅助方法的最佳反应条件和催化剂组成，可能导致更高效和选择性的丙烷脱氢过程。
