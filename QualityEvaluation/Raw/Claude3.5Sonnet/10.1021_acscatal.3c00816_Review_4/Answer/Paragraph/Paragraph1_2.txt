<English>
The preparation methods, composition, and zeolite topology significantly influence the performance of zeolite-based catalysts in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, particularly for BEA topologic and silicoaluminophosphate zeolites. Key factors contributing to improved propylene selectivity, conversion rates, and catalyst stability include:

1. Zeolite Topology:
BEA-type zeolites feature a three-dimensional structure with 12-ring channel systems and larger pores compared to ZSM-5 zeolites. This structure facilitates better dispersion and stabilization of metal species [10.1002/cctc.201801708]. Silicoaluminophosphate zeolites like SAPO-34 (CHA topology) show superior performance compared to aluminosilicate zeolites, providing weak surface acidity and excellent shape selectivity for propylene [10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008].

2. Preparation Methods:
a) Dealumination: Removing lattice Al atoms from the zeolite framework creates vacant T-atom sites associated with silanol groups, allowing for better accommodation and anchoring of metal species [10.1002/cctc.201801708].
b) Post-synthesis modification: A two-step post-synthesis procedure involving dealumination of H-Beta and incorporation of metal species (e.g., Sn) into the framework is effective for creating highly active catalysts [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019].
c) Ion exchange and impregnation: Sequential metal introduction (e.g., Zn ion-exchange followed by Pt impregnation) results in higher metal dispersion and lower metal loss compared to simultaneous impregnation [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4].

3. Composition:
a) Metal loading: Optimal metal loading varies depending on the specific catalyst system. For example, 10wt% Zn on dealuminated β zeolite [10.1002/cctc.201801708] and 0.5 wt% Pt with 1.6-2.6 wt% Zn on Beta zeolite [10.1016/j.apcata.2006.03.028] showed high performance.
b) Bimetallic systems: Combinations like PtSn and PtZn often show improved performance and stability compared to monometallic catalysts [10.1016/j.apcata.2006.03.028].
c) Promoters: Addition of promoters like Na or K can enhance catalyst performance [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62750-5].

4. Acidity Control:
Dealumination reduces the number of strong acid sites in the zeolite, which effectively minimizes side reactions and enhances propylene selectivity [10.1002/cctc.201801708]. The ratio of Lewis to Brønsted acid sites (L/B ratio) is crucial, with a high L/B ratio being beneficial for PDH [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019].

5. Metal-Support Interactions:
Strong interactions between metal species and the zeolite support contribute to improved catalyst stability and performance. For example, the interaction between Pt and Sn(IV) sites confined in the Si-beta framework leads to high dispersion of Pt species [10.1039/C9CY01907C].

6. Confinement Effect:
The zeolite structure helps confine and stabilize active metal species, inhibiting sintering and coke formation. This effect is particularly pronounced in BEA-type zeolites and SAPO-34 [10.1039/C9CY01907C, 10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008].

These factors collectively contribute to improved propylene selectivity (often exceeding 90%), high conversion rates, and excellent catalyst stability in PDH reactions using BEA topologic and silicoaluminophosphate zeolite-based catalysts.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The research on zeolite-based catalysts for PDH reactions has shown significant progress in recent years, particularly in the development of BEA topologic and silicoaluminophosphate zeolites. The unique properties of these zeolites, combined with advanced preparation methods and optimized metal loading, have resulted in catalysts with superior performance compared to traditional supports.

Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Atomic-scale engineering: Developing methods for precise control over the location and distribution of active metal species within the zeolite framework could further enhance catalyst performance.

2. In-situ characterization: Advanced techniques for real-time observation of catalysts under reaction conditions could provide deeper insights into the mechanisms of catalyst deactivation and regeneration.

3. Machine learning and high-throughput screening: These approaches could accelerate the discovery of optimal zeolite compositions and preparation methods for PDH catalysts.

4. Novel zeolite structures: Exploring new zeolite topologies or hybrid structures that combine the advantages of different zeolite types could lead to breakthroughs in catalyst design.

5. Sustainable catalyst production: Developing more environmentally friendly methods for zeolite synthesis and metal incorporation, potentially using bio-based precursors or low-energy processes.

6. Multifunctional catalysts: Designing zeolite-based catalysts that can perform PDH reactions alongside other valuable transformations could improve process efficiency in industrial applications.

By addressing these areas, researchers may unlock new possibilities for highly efficient, stable, and selective zeolite-based catalysts for propane dehydrogenation, potentially revolutionizing the production of propylene and other light olefins.

<Chinese>
制备方法、组成和沸石拓扑结构显著影响沸石基催化剂在丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应中的性能,特别是对于BEA拓扑结构和硅铝磷酸盐沸石。影响丙烯选择性、转化率和催化剂稳定性的关键因素包括:

1. 沸石拓扑结构:
BEA型沸石具有三维结构,拥有12元环通道系统和比ZSM-5沸石更大的孔径。这种结构有利于金属物种的更好分散和稳定[10.1002/cctc.201801708]。硅铝磷酸盐沸石如SAPO-34(CHA拓扑结构)相比铝硅酸盐沸石表现出更优异的性能,提供弱表面酸性和对丙烯的优异形状选择性[10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008]。

2. 制备方法:
a) 脱铝:从沸石骨架中移除晶格铝原子,创造与硅羟基相关的空缺T原子位点,允许更好地容纳和固定金属物种[10.1002/cctc.201801708]。
b) 后合成修饰:涉及H-Beta脱铝和将金属物种(如Sn)掺入骨架的两步后合成程序,可有效创造高活性催化剂[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019]。
c) 离子交换和浸渍:顺序金属引入(如Zn离子交换后Pt浸渍)与同时浸渍相比,可获得更高的金属分散度和更低的金属损失[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4]。

3. 组成:
a) 金属负载量:最佳金属负载量因特定催化剂体系而异。例如,脱铝β沸石上10wt% Zn [10.1002/cctc.201801708]和Beta沸石上0.5 wt% Pt与1.6-2.6 wt% Zn [10.1016/j.apcata.2006.03.028]表现出高性能。
b) 双金属体系:PtSn和PtZn等组合通常比单金属催化剂表现出更好的性能和稳定性[10.1016/j.apcata.2006.03.028]。
c) 助剂:添加Na或K等助剂可增强催化剂性能[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62750-5]。

4. 酸度控制:
脱铝减少了沸石中强酸位的数量,有效最小化副反应并增强丙烯选择性[10.1002/cctc.201801708]。路易斯酸位与布朗斯特德酸位的比例(L/B比)至关重要,高L/B比有利于PDH反应[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019]。

5. 金属-载体相互作用:
金属物种与沸石载体之间的强相互作用有助于提高催化剂的稳定性和性能。例如,confined在Si-beta骨架中的Pt和Sn(IV)位点之间的相互作用导致Pt物种的高度分散[10.1039/C9CY01907C]。

6. 限域效应:
沸石结构有助于限制和稳定活性金属物种,抑制烧结和积碳形成。这种效应在BEA型沸石和SAPO-34中特别明显[10.1039/C9CY01907C, 10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008]。

这些因素共同促进了BEA拓扑结构和硅铝磷酸盐沸石基催化剂在PDH反应中丙烯选择性的提高(通常超过90%),高转化率和优异的催化剂稳定性。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望: 近年来,关于PDH反应的沸石基催化剂研究取得了显著进展,特别是在BEA拓扑结构和硅铝磷酸盐沸石的开发方面。这些沸石的独特性质,结合先进的制备方法和优化的金属负载,已经产生了比传统载体性能更优异的催化剂。

未来的研究努力可能会集中在:

1. 原子尺度工程:开发精确控制活性金属物种在沸石骨架中位置和分布的方法,可进一步增强催化剂性能。

2. 原位表征:开发在反应条件下实时观察催化剂的先进技术,可以深入了解催化剂失活和再生的机制。

3. 机器学习和高通量筛选:这些方法可以加速发现PDH催化剂的最佳沸石组成和制备方法。

4. 新型沸石结构:探索新的沸石拓扑结构或结合不同沸石类型优势的混合结构,可能会在催化剂设计方面取得突破。

5. 可持续催化剂生产:开发更环保的沸石合成和金属掺入方法,可能使用生物基前体或低能耗过程。

6. 多功能催化剂:设计能够同时进行PDH反应和其他有价值转化的沸石基催化剂,可以提高工业应用中的过程效率。

通过解决这些领域的问题,研究人员可能会为丙烷脱氢开发出高效、稳定和选择性的沸石基催化剂,潜在地革新丙烯和其他轻烯烃的生产。
