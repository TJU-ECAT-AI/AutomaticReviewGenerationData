<English>
The preparation methods, composition, and zeolite topology significantly influence the performance of zeolite-based catalysts in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, particularly for BEA topologic and silicoaluminophosphate zeolites. Key factors contributing to improved propylene selectivity, conversion rates, and catalyst stability include:

1. Zeolite topology:
   - BEA-type zeolites feature a three-dimensional structure with 12-ring channel systems and larger pores compared to ZSM-5, facilitating better dispersion and stabilization of metal species [10.1002/cctc.201801708].
   - SAPO-34, a silicoaluminophosphate zeolite with CHA topology, shows superior performance due to its unique structure, providing weak surface acidity and excellent shape selectivity for propylene [10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008].

2. Preparation methods:
   - Dealumination of Beta zeolites creates T-vacant sites and reduces acidity, which is beneficial for PDH reactions [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037].
   - A two-step post-synthesis procedure involving dealumination of H-Beta and incorporation of metal species (e.g., Sn) into the framework allows for better control of metal incorporation and distribution [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019].
   - Sequential impregnation methods, such as Zn ion-exchange followed by Pt impregnation, result in higher metal dispersion and improved stability [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4].

3. Composition:
   - Optimal metal loadings vary depending on the specific catalyst system. For example, 0.5 wt% Pt and 1.6-2.6 wt% Zn showed good performance for PtZn/Beta catalysts [10.1016/j.apcata.2006.03.028].
   - Bimetallic systems, such as PtSn, show improved performance and stability compared to monometallic catalysts [10.1039/D0CS00814A].
   - The Si/Al ratio in zeolites affects catalytic performance, with intermediate ratios (8.7 ≤ nSi/nAl ≤ 26) often yielding the best results [10.1016/j.apcata.2006.03.028].

4. Acidity control:
   - Reducing Brønsted acidity or controlling its distribution within the zeolite structure can improve catalyst performance by suppressing side reactions and enhancing propylene selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4].
   - The ratio of Lewis to Brønsted acid sites (L/B ratio) is crucial, with a high L/B ratio being beneficial for PDH [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019].

5. Metal-support interactions:
   - Strong metal-support interactions contribute to improved catalyst stability and performance [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022].
   - The confinement effect of metals (e.g., Sn) in the zeolite framework inhibits coke formation and sintering of active metal clusters [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022].

6. Promoters:
   - The addition of promoters, such as Na, K, or La, can enhance catalyst performance by modifying acidity and improving metal dispersion [10.1016/j.cej.2011.11.055].

These factors collectively contribute to improved propylene selectivity (often >90%), high conversion rates, and excellent catalyst stability in PDH reactions. For example, Pt/Sn-Si-Beta catalysts have shown propene selectivity >99% with high formation rates and stability over multiple reaction cycles [10.1039/C9CY01907C].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The extensive research on zeolite-based catalysts for PDH reactions has revealed the complex interplay between preparation methods, composition, and zeolite topology in determining catalyst performance. While significant progress has been made, there are still opportunities for further optimization and exploration:

1. Rational design of hierarchical zeolites: Combining the shape selectivity of micropores with the improved mass transfer of mesopores could lead to more efficient catalysts for PDH reactions.

2. Advanced characterization techniques: Developing in-situ and operando spectroscopic methods to study the dynamic behavior of active sites during PDH reactions could provide deeper insights into the catalyst structure-performance relationships.

3. Computational modeling: Employing advanced computational techniques to predict optimal zeolite structures and metal compositions for PDH catalysts could accelerate the discovery of new, high-performance materials.

4. Sustainable catalyst preparation: Exploring environmentally friendly synthesis methods and utilizing abundant, non-noble metals could lead to more sustainable and cost-effective PDH catalysts.

5. Multifunctional catalysts: Developing zeolite-based catalysts that can perform multiple reactions in one step (e.g., PDH combined with selective oxidation) could improve process efficiency and reduce energy consumption.

6. Catalyst regeneration strategies: Investigating novel approaches to extend catalyst lifetime and improve regeneration efficiency could enhance the economic viability of PDH processes using zeolite-based catalysts.

By addressing these challenges and opportunities, future research in zeolite-based catalysts for PDH reactions could lead to more efficient, stable, and sustainable processes for propylene production.

<Chinese>
制备方法、成分和沸石拓扑结构对沸石基催化剂在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中的性能产生显著影响，特别是对于BEA拓扑结构和硅铝磷酸盐沸石。影响丙烯选择性、转化率和催化剂稳定性的关键因素包括：

1. 沸石拓扑结构：
   - BEA型沸石具有三维结构，带有12元环通道系统，孔径比ZSM-5大，有利于金属物种的更好分散和稳定 [10.1002/cctc.201801708]。
   - SAPO-34是一种具有CHA拓扑结构的硅铝磷酸盐沸石，由于其独特的结构，提供弱表面酸性和优异的丙烯形状选择性，表现出优异的性能 [10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008]。

2. 制备方法：
   - Beta沸石的脱铝创造了T空位并降低了酸性，这对PDH反应有利 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]。
   - 涉及H-Beta脱铝和金属物种（如Sn）掺入骨架的两步后合成程序，能更好地控制金属的掺入和分布 [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019]。
   - 顺序浸渍法，如Zn离子交换后进行Pt浸渍，能实现更高的金属分散度和改善稳定性 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4]。

3. 组成：
   - 最佳金属负载量因具体催化剂系统而异。例如，对于PtZn/Beta催化剂，0.5 wt% Pt和1.6-2.6 wt% Zn表现良好 [10.1016/j.apcata.2006.03.028]。
   - 双金属体系，如PtSn，相比单金属催化剂表现出更好的性能和稳定性 [10.1039/D0CS00814A]。
   - 沸石中的Si/Al比影响催化性能，中等比例（8.7 ≤ nSi/nAl ≤ 26）通常能获得最佳结果 [10.1016/j.apcata.2006.03.028]。

4. 酸性控制：
   - 降低Brønsted酸性或控制其在沸石结构中的分布可以通过抑制副反应和提高丙烯选择性来改善催化剂性能 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_4]。
   - Lewis酸位与Brønsted酸位的比例（L/B比）至关重要，高L/B比有利于PDH反应 [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019]。

5. 金属-载体相互作用：
   - 强金属-载体相互作用有助于提高催化剂的稳定性和性能 [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022]。
   - 金属（如Sn）在沸石骨架中的限制效应抑制了焦炭形成和活性金属簇的烧结 [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022]。

6. 助剂：
   - 添加助剂，如Na、K或La，可以通过改变酸性和改善金属分散来增强催化剂性能 [10.1016/j.cej.2011.11.055]。

这些因素共同作用，提高了PDH反应中的丙烯选择性（通常>90%）、高转化率和优异的催化剂稳定性。例如，Pt/Sn-Si-Beta催化剂在多个反应循环中表现出>99%的丙烯选择性，具有高生成速率和稳定性 [10.1039/C9CY01907C]。

综合理解与未来展望：对PDH反应的沸石基催化剂的广泛研究揭示了制备方法、组成和沸石拓扑结构在决定催化剂性能方面的复杂相互作用。尽管已取得重大进展，但仍有进一步优化和探索的机会：

1. 分级沸石的理性设计：结合微孔的形状选择性和介孔的改善传质性能，可能导致PDH反应更高效的催化剂。

2. 先进表征技术：开发原位和操作中光谱方法来研究PDH反应过程中活性位点的动态行为，可以提供对催化剂结构-性能关系的更深入见解。

3. 计算机模拟：采用先进的计算技术预测PDH催化剂的最佳沸石结构和金属组成，可以加速新型高性能材料的发现。

4. 可持续催化剂制备：探索环境友好的合成方法并利用丰富的非贵金属，可以开发出更可持续和成本效益高的PDH催化剂。

5. 多功能催化剂：开发能在一个步骤中执行多个反应的沸石基催化剂（例如，PDH与选择性氧化结合），可以提高过程效率并减少能源消耗。

6. 催化剂再生策略：研究新颖方法以延长催化剂寿命并提高再生效率，可以增强使用沸石基催化剂的PDH过程的经济可行性。

通过解决这些挑战和机遇，未来在PDH反应沸石基催化剂的研究可能导致更高效、稳定和可持续的丙烯生产工艺。
