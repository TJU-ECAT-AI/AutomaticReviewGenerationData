<English>
The preparation methods, composition, and zeolite topology significantly influence the performance of zeolite-based catalysts in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, particularly for BEA topologic and silicoaluminophosphate zeolites. Key factors contributing to improved propylene selectivity, conversion rates, and catalyst stability include:

1. Zeolite Topology:
BEA-type zeolites feature a three-dimensional structure with 12-ring channel systems and larger pores compared to ZSM-5 zeolites [10.1002/cctc.201801708]. This structure facilitates better dispersion and stabilization of metal species. SAPO-34, a silicoaluminophosphate zeolite with CHA topology, shows superior performance compared to other zeolites due to its unique structure providing weak surface acidity and excellent shape selectivity for propylene [10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008].

2. Preparation Methods:
a) Dealumination: Removing lattice Al atoms from the zeolite framework creates vacant T-atom sites associated with silanol groups, allowing for better accommodation and anchoring of metal species [10.1002/cctc.201801708].
b) Two-step post-synthesis: For Sn-Beta zeolites, a procedure involving dealumination of H-Beta followed by incorporation of Sn species into the framework allows for better control of Sn incorporation and distribution [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019].
c) Sequential impregnation: This method, especially for bimetallic catalysts like PtSn, allows for better control of metal dispersion and interaction with the support [10.1021/ie901465s].

3. Composition:
a) Metal loading: Optimal metal loading varies depending on the specific catalyst system. For example, 10wt% Zn loaded on dealuminated β zeolite showed high performance [10.1002/cctc.201801708], while for Co-based catalysts, a lower loading of 0.5 wt% was optimal [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037].
b) Bimetallic systems: PtSn catalysts often show improved performance and stability compared to monometallic systems [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022].
c) Si/Al ratio: An optimized Si/Al ratio is crucial for catalyst performance. For Sn-Beta zeolites, a Si/Sn ratio of around 30 showed the best results [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019].

4. Acidity Control:
Dealumination reduces the number of strong acid sites in the zeolite, which effectively minimizes side reactions and enhances propylene selectivity [10.1002/cctc.201801708]. The ratio of Lewis to Brønsted acid sites (L/B ratio) is crucial, with a high L/B ratio being beneficial for PDH [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019].

5. Metal-Support Interactions:
Strong metal-support interactions, such as those between Pt and Sn(IV) sites confined in the Si-beta framework, lead to high dispersion of metal species and improved catalyst stability [10.1039/C9CY01907C].

6. Confinement Effect:
The zeolite structure helps in confining and stabilizing active metal species, inhibiting coke formation and sintering [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022].

These factors collectively contribute to improved propylene selectivity (up to 99%), high conversion rates, and excellent catalyst stability in PDH reactions. For example, Pt/Sn-Si-beta catalysts showed propene formation rates of 2.3 mol g⁻¹Pt h⁻¹ at 550°C with >99% selectivity [10.1039/C9CY01907C], while Co in SiBeta zeolite (0.5 wt%) demonstrated propane conversion of about 72% and propylene selectivity of over 92% at 600°C [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of zeolite-based catalysts for propane dehydrogenation has shown significant progress, particularly in the areas of BEA topologic and silicoaluminophosphate zeolites. The synergistic effects of zeolite structure, metal species, and preparation methods have led to catalysts with high activity, selectivity, and stability. Looking forward, several promising research directions emerge:

1. Atomic-scale engineering: Further exploration of single-atom catalysts or precisely controlled metal clusters within zeolite frameworks could lead to even higher catalytic efficiency and selectivity.

2. In-situ characterization: Developing advanced in-situ characterization techniques could provide deeper insights into the dynamic behavior of catalysts under reaction conditions, enabling more targeted catalyst design.

3. Hybrid materials: Combining zeolites with other materials, such as metal-organic frameworks or 2D materials, could create novel catalytic systems with enhanced properties.

4. Computational modeling: Advancing computational methods to predict catalyst performance could accelerate the discovery of optimal catalyst compositions and structures.

5. Green synthesis routes: Developing more environmentally friendly and economical methods for zeolite synthesis and modification could make these catalysts more sustainable and industrially viable.

6. Multifunctional catalysts: Designing catalysts capable of performing multiple reactions in one step (e.g., dehydrogenation coupled with selective oxidation) could lead to more efficient processes.

7. Catalyst regeneration: Improving methods for catalyst regeneration and exploring self-regenerating catalysts could significantly extend catalyst lifetime and reduce operational costs.

These future directions could potentially lead to breakthroughs in propane dehydrogenation technology, making the process more efficient, selective, and economically viable for industrial applications.

制备方法、组成和沸石拓扑结构显著影响了沸石基催化剂在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中的性能，特别是对于BEA拓扑结构和硅铝磷酸盐沸石。影响丙烯选择性、转化率和催化剂稳定性的关键因素包括：

1. 沸石拓扑结构：
BEA型沸石具有三维结构，带有12元环通道系统，孔径比ZSM-5沸石更大[10.1002/cctc.201801708]。这种结构有利于金属物种的更好分散和稳定。SAPO-34是一种具有CHA拓扑结构的硅铝磷酸盐沸石，由于其独特的结构提供弱表面酸性和对丙烯的优异形状选择性，表现出优于其他沸石的性能[10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008]。

2. 制备方法：
a) 脱铝：从沸石骨架中移除格子Al原子，创造与硅醇基相关的空缺T原子位点，从而更好地容纳和锚定金属物种[10.1002/cctc.201801708]。
b) 两步后合成法：对于Sn-Beta沸石，涉及H-Beta脱铝后将Sn物种引入骨架的程序，可以更好地控制Sn的引入和分布[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019]。
c) 序贯浸渍法：这种方法，特别是对于PtSn等双金属催化剂，可以更好地控制金属分散和与载体的相互作用[10.1021/ie901465s]。

3. 组成：
a) 金属负载量：最佳金属负载量因具体催化剂系统而异。例如，10wt%的Zn负载在脱铝的β沸石上表现出高性能[10.1002/cctc.201801708]，而对于Co基催化剂，0.5 wt%的较低负载量是最佳的[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]。
b) 双金属系统：PtSn催化剂通常比单金属系统表现出更好的性能和稳定性[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022]。
c) Si/Al比：优化的Si/Al比对催化剂性能至关重要。对于Sn-Beta沸石，Si/Sn比约为30时显示最佳结果[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019]。

4. 酸性控制：
脱铝减少了沸石中强酸位点的数量，有效地最小化了副反应并提高了丙烯选择性[10.1002/cctc.201801708]。路易斯酸位与布朗斯特酸位的比例（L/B比）至关重要，高L/B比有利于PDH反应[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019]。

5. 金属-载体相互作用：
强金属-载体相互作用，如限制在Si-beta骨架中的Pt和Sn(IV)位点之间的相互作用，导致金属物种的高度分散和催化剂稳定性的提高[10.1039/C9CY01907C]。

6. 限域效应：
沸石结构有助于限制和稳定活性金属物种，抑制积碳形成和烧结[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022]。

这些因素共同促进了PDH反应中丙烯选择性（高达99%）的提高、转化率的提升和催化剂稳定性的显著改善。例如，Pt/Sn-Si-beta催化剂在550°C下显示出2.3 mol g⁻¹Pt h⁻¹的丙烯生成速率，选择性>99%[10.1039/C9CY01907C]，而SiBeta沸石中的Co（0.5 wt%）在600°C下表现出约72%的丙烷转化率和超过92%的丙烯选择性[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]。

综合理解与未来展望：丙烷脱氢沸石基催化剂的发展已经取得了显著进展，特别是在BEA拓扑结构和硅铝磷酸盐沸石领域。沸石结构、金属物种和制备方法的协同效应已经导致了具有高活性、选择性和稳定性的催化剂。展望未来，几个有前景的研究方向浮现：

1. 原子尺度工程：进一步探索沸石骨架内的单原子催化剂或精确控制的金属簇可能导致更高的催化效率和选择性。

2. 原位表征：开发先进的原位表征技术可以提供对催化剂在反应条件下动态行为的更深入洞察，从而实现更有针对性的催化剂设计。

3. 混合材料：将沸石与其他材料（如金属有机骨架或2D材料）结合，可能创造具有增强性能的新型催化系统。

4. 计算建模：推进计算方法以预测催化剂性能可能加速最佳催化剂组成和结构的发现。

5. 绿色合成路线：开发更环保和经济的沸石合成和改性方法可以使这些催化剂更具可持续性和工业可行性。

6. 多功能催化剂：设计能够在一个步骤中执行多个反应的催化剂（如脱氢与选择性氧化耦合）可能导致更高效的过程。

7. 催化剂再生：改进催化剂再生方法并探索自再生催化剂可能显著延长催化剂寿命并降低运营成本。

这些未来方向可能潜在地引领丙烷脱氢技术的突破，使该过程在工业应用中更加高效、选择性更高且更具经济可行性。
