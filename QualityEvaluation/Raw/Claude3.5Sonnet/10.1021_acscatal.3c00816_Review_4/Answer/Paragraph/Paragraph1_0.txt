<English>
The preparation methods, composition, and zeolite topology significantly influence the performance of zeolite-based catalysts in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, particularly for BEA topologic and silicoaluminophosphate zeolites. Key factors contributing to improved propylene selectivity, conversion rates, and catalyst stability include:

1. Zeolite topology:
BEA-type zeolites feature a three-dimensional structure with 12-ring channel systems and larger pores compared to ZSM-5 zeolites [10.1002/cctc.201801708]. This structure facilitates better dispersion and stabilization of metal species. Silicoaluminophosphate zeolites like SAPO-34 (CHA topology) exhibit superior propylene selectivity compared to aluminosilicate zeolites [10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008].

2. Preparation methods:
a) Dealumination: Removing lattice Al atoms from the zeolite framework creates vacant T-atom sites associated with silanol groups, allowing for better accommodation and anchoring of metal species [10.1002/cctc.201801708].
b) Two-step post-synthesis: For Sn-Beta zeolites, a method involving dealumination of H-Beta and incorporation of Sn species into the framework is used [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019].
c) Sequential impregnation: This method is commonly used for introducing multiple metal species, such as Pt and Sn, onto the zeolite support [10.1016/j.apcata.2006.03.028].

3. Composition:
a) Metal loading: Optimal metal loadings vary depending on the specific catalyst system. For example, 0.5 wt% Pt and 1.6-2.6 wt% Zn showed good performance for PtZn-Beta catalysts [10.1016/j.apcata.2006.03.028].
b) Si/Al ratio: Intermediate Si/Al ratios (8.7 ≤ nSi/nAl ≤ 26) generally yield the best results for BEA zeolites [10.1016/j.apcata.2006.03.028].
c) Promoters: Addition of promoters like Na, K, or La can improve catalyst performance [10.1016/j.cej.2011.11.055].

4. Acidity control:
Dealumination reduces the number of strong acid sites in the zeolite, which effectively minimizes side reactions and enhances propylene selectivity [10.1002/cctc.201801708]. For SAPO-34, weak Brønsted acid sites contribute to selective conversion of intermediates to propylene [10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008].

5. Metal-support interactions:
Strong interactions between metal species and the zeolite support contribute to improved dispersion, stability, and catalytic performance. For example, the interaction between Pt and Sn(IV) sites confined in the Si-beta framework leads to high dispersion of Pt species [10.1039/C9CY01907C].

6. Confinement effect:
The zeolite structure helps confine and stabilize active metal species, inhibiting sintering and coke formation. This is particularly evident in Pt@Sn-Beta catalysts [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022].

These factors collectively contribute to improved catalyst performance in PDH reactions. For example, Pt/Sn-Si-beta catalysts show excellent catalytic performance with propene formation rates of 2.3 mol g⁻¹Pt h⁻¹ and propene selectivity >99% at 550°C [10.1039/C9CY01907C]. SAPO-34 supported PtSn catalysts achieve propylene selectivity of 94% and total olefins selectivity greater than 97% [10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The development of zeolite-based catalysts for PDH reactions has shown significant progress, particularly in the areas of BEA topologic and silicoaluminophosphate zeolites. The interplay between preparation methods, composition, and zeolite topology offers numerous opportunities for catalyst optimization.

Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Advanced characterization techniques: Developing and applying in situ and operando spectroscopic methods to gain deeper insights into the dynamic behavior of active sites during PDH reactions.

2. Rational design of multifunctional catalysts: Exploring synergistic effects between different metal species and zeolite frameworks to create catalysts with enhanced activity, selectivity, and stability.

3. Hierarchical zeolite structures: Investigating the potential of hierarchical BEA and SAPO zeolites to improve mass transfer properties and catalyst lifetime.

4. Computational modeling: Utilizing advanced molecular modeling and machine learning techniques to predict optimal catalyst compositions and guide experimental design.

5. Green synthesis methods: Developing more environmentally friendly and cost-effective preparation methods for zeolite-based catalysts, potentially exploring biomass-derived precursors.

6. Process intensification: Integrating catalyst design with novel reactor concepts, such as membrane reactors or structured catalysts, to enhance overall process efficiency.

By addressing these areas, researchers can work towards developing next-generation zeolite-based catalysts for PDH reactions with improved performance, longer lifetimes, and reduced environmental impact.

<Chinese>
制备方法、组成和沸石拓扑结构显著影响了沸石基催化剂在丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中的性能，特别是对于BEA拓扑结构和硅铝磷沸石。以下是影响丙烯选择性、转化率和催化剂稳定性的关键因素：

1. 沸石拓扑结构：
BEA型沸石具有三维结构，带有12元环通道系统，孔径比ZSM-5沸石更大[10.1002/cctc.201801708]。这种结构有利于金属物种的更好分散和稳定。硅铝磷沸石如SAPO-34（CHA拓扑结构）展现出比铝硅酸盐沸石更优异的丙烯选择性[10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008]。

2. 制备方法：
a) 脱铝：从沸石骨架中移除晶格Al原子，创造与硅醇基相关的空缺T-原子位点，有利于金属物种的更好容纳和锚定[10.1002/cctc.201801708]。
b) 两步后合成法：对于Sn-Beta沸石，采用H-Beta脱铝和将Sn物种引入骨架的方法[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019]。
c) 序列浸渍法：这种方法常用于将多种金属物种（如Pt和Sn）引入沸石载体[10.1016/j.apcata.2006.03.028]。

3. 组成：
a) 金属负载量：最佳金属负载量因具体催化剂系统而异。例如，PtZn-Beta催化剂中0.5 wt% Pt和1.6-2.6 wt% Zn表现良好[10.1016/j.apcata.2006.03.028]。
b) Si/Al比：对于BEA沸石，中等Si/Al比（8.7 ≤ nSi/nAl ≤ 26）通常产生最佳结果[10.1016/j.apcata.2006.03.028]。
c) 助剂：添加Na、K或La等助剂可以提高催化剂性能[10.1016/j.cej.2011.11.055]。

4. 酸度控制：
脱铝减少了沸石中强酸位的数量，有效地最小化了副反应并提高了丙烯选择性[10.1002/cctc.201801708]。对于SAPO-34，弱布朗斯特酸位有助于中间体选择性转化为丙烯[10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008]。

5. 金属-载体相互作用：
金属物种与沸石载体之间的强相互作用有助于提高分散性、稳定性和催化性能。例如，Pt与Si-beta骨架中限制的Sn(IV)位点之间的相互作用导致Pt物种的高度分散[10.1039/C9CY01907C]。

6. 限制效应：
沸石结构有助于限制和稳定活性金属物种，抑制烧结和积炭形成。这在Pt@Sn-Beta催化剂中尤为明显[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022]。

这些因素共同促进了PDH反应中催化剂性能的改善。例如，Pt/Sn-Si-beta催化剂在550°C下表现出优异的催化性能，丙烯生成速率为2.3 mol g⁻¹Pt h⁻¹，丙烯选择性>99% [10.1039/C9CY01907C]。SAPO-34负载的PtSn催化剂实现了94%的丙烯选择性和大于97%的总烯烃选择性[10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008]。

综合理解与未来展望：

沸石基催化剂在PDH反应中的发展已经取得了显著进展，特别是在BEA拓扑结构和硅铝磷沸石领域。制备方法、组成和沸石拓扑结构之间的相互作用为催化剂优化提供了众多机会。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 先进表征技术：开发和应用原位和操作中光谱方法，以深入了解PDH反应过程中活性位点的动态行为。

2. 多功能催化剂的理性设计：探索不同金属物种和沸石骨架之间的协同效应，创造具有增强活性、选择性和稳定性的催化剂。

3. 分级沸石结构：研究分级BEA和SAPO沸石的潜力，以改善传质性能和催化剂寿命。

4. 计算机模拟：利用先进的分子建模和机器学习技术预测最佳催化剂组成并指导实验设计。

5. 绿色合成方法：开发更环保、更具成本效益的沸石基催化剂制备方法，可能探索生物质衍生的前体。

6. 过程强化：将催化剂设计与新型反应器概念（如膜反应器或结构化催化剂）相结合，以提高整体过程效率。

通过解决这些领域的问题，研究人员可以致力于开发下一代PDH反应用沸石基催化剂，具有更好的性能、更长的寿命和更低的环境影响。
