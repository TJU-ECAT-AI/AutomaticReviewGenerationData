<English>
The preparation methods, composition, and zeolite topology significantly influence the performance of zeolite-based catalysts in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, particularly for BEA topologic and silicoaluminophosphate zeolites. Key factors contributing to improved propylene selectivity, conversion rates, and catalyst stability include:

1. Zeolite topology:
BEA-type zeolites feature a three-dimensional structure with 12-ring channel systems and larger pores compared to ZSM-5 zeolites [10.1002/cctc.201801708]. This structure facilitates better dispersion and stabilization of metal species. For silicoaluminophosphate zeolites, SAPO-34 (CHA topology) shows superior performance compared to other zeolites, providing weak surface acidity and excellent shape selectivity for propylene [10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008].

2. Preparation methods:
a) Dealumination: Removing lattice Al atoms from the zeolite framework creates vacant T-atom sites associated with silanol groups, allowing for better accommodation and anchoring of metal species [10.1002/cctc.201801708].
b) Two-step post-synthesis: For Sn-Beta zeolites, a procedure involving dealumination of H-Beta followed by incorporation of Sn species into the framework of dealuminated Si-Beta through impregnation is effective [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019].
c) Sequential impregnation: This method is commonly used for preparing bimetallic catalysts, such as PtSn/SAPO-34, allowing for better control of metal dispersion and interaction with the support [10.1021/ie901465s].

3. Composition:
a) Metal loading: Optimal metal loading varies depending on the specific catalyst system. For example, 10wt% Zn loaded on dealuminated β zeolite showed high performance [10.1002/cctc.201801708], while 0.5 wt% Co on SiBeta demonstrated excellent results [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037].
b) Bimetallic systems: PtSn catalysts often show improved performance and stability compared to monometallic catalysts [10.1039/D0CS00814A].
c) Promoters: The addition of promoters like Na or La can enhance catalyst performance [10.1016/j.cej.2011.11.055].

4. Acidity control:
Dealumination reduces the number of strong acid sites in the zeolite, which effectively minimizes side reactions and enhances propylene selectivity [10.1002/cctc.201801708]. The ratio of Lewis to Brønsted acid sites is crucial, with a high L/B ratio being beneficial for PDH [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019].

5. Metal-support interactions:
Strong interactions between metal species and the zeolite support contribute to improved catalyst stability and performance. For example, the interaction between Pt and Sn(IV) sites confined in the Si-beta framework leads to high dispersion of Pt species [10.1039/C9CY01907C].

6. Confinement effect:
The zeolite structure helps confine and stabilize active metal species, inhibiting sintering and coke formation. This effect is particularly pronounced in BEA and SAPO-34 zeolites [10.1039/C9CY01907C, 10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008].

These factors collectively contribute to improved catalyst performance in PDH reactions. For instance, Pt/Sn-Si-beta catalysts prepared via an improved post-synthesis method exhibited propene formation rates of 2.3 mol g⁻¹Pt h⁻¹ with >99% selectivity at 550°C [10.1039/C9CY01907C]. Similarly, PtSn/SAPO-34 catalysts achieved propylene selectivity of 94% and total olefins selectivity greater than 97% [10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The research on zeolite-based catalysts for PDH reactions has shown significant progress, particularly in the development of BEA topologic and silicoaluminophosphate zeolites. The interplay between preparation methods, composition, and zeolite topology offers numerous opportunities for catalyst optimization.

Future research directions may include:
1. Exploring novel synthesis methods to create hierarchical pore structures in BEA and SAPO zeolites, potentially improving mass transfer and catalyst stability.
2. Investigating the use of advanced characterization techniques, such as operando spectroscopy, to gain deeper insights into the dynamic behavior of active sites during PDH reactions.
3. Developing strategies for precise control of metal species distribution and size within zeolite frameworks, possibly through atomic layer deposition or advanced encapsulation techniques.
4. Exploring the potential of zeolite-based catalysts for other dehydrogenation reactions, leveraging the knowledge gained from PDH studies.
5. Investigating the long-term stability and regeneration of zeolite-based catalysts under industrial conditions, focusing on minimizing deactivation and extending catalyst lifetime.

These future directions could lead to the development of more efficient and stable catalysts for PDH reactions, potentially revolutionizing propylene production in the petrochemical industry.

<Chinese>
制备方法、组成和沸石拓扑结构显著影响了沸石基催化剂在丙烷脱氢 (PDH) 反应中的性能，特别是对于 BEA 拓扑结构和硅铝磷酸盐沸石。以下是影响丙烯选择性、转化率和催化剂稳定性的关键因素：

1. 沸石拓扑结构：
BEA 型沸石具有三维结构，有 12 元环通道系统，孔径比 ZSM-5 沸石更大 [10.1002/cctc.201801708]。这种结构有利于金属物种的更好分散和稳定。对于硅铝磷酸盐沸石，SAPO-34（CHA 拓扑结构）表现出优于其他沸石的性能，具有弱表面酸性和对丙烯的优异形状选择性 [10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008]。

2. 制备方法：
a) 脱铝：从沸石骨架中移除晶格 Al 原子会产生与硅醇基相关的空缺 T 原子位，有利于金属物种的更好容纳和锚定 [10.1002/cctc.201801708]。
b) 两步后合成法：对于 Sn-Beta 沸石，先对 H-Beta 进行脱铝，然后通过浸渍法将 Sn 物种引入脱铝的 Si-Beta 骨架的方法是有效的 [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019]。
c) 序列浸渍法：这种方法常用于制备双金属催化剂，如 PtSn/SAPO-34，可以更好地控制金属分散和与载体的相互作用 [10.1021/ie901465s]。

3. 组成：
a) 金属负载量：最佳金属负载量因具体催化剂系统而异。例如，10wt% Zn 负载在脱铝的 β 沸石上表现出高性能 [10.1002/cctc.201801708]，而 0.5 wt% Co 在 SiBeta 上展示了优异的结果 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.12.037]。
b) 双金属系统：PtSn 催化剂通常比单金属催化剂表现出更好的性能和稳定性 [10.1039/D0CS00814A]。
c) 助剂：添加 Na 或 La 等助剂可以增强催化剂性能 [10.1016/j.cej.2011.11.055]。

4. 酸度控制：
脱铝减少了沸石中强酸位的数量，有效地最小化了副反应并增强了丙烯选择性 [10.1002/cctc.201801708]。路易斯酸位与布朗斯特酸位的比例至关重要，高 L/B 比有利于 PDH 反应 [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019]。

5. 金属-载体相互作用：
金属物种与沸石载体之间的强相互作用有助于提高催化剂的稳定性和性能。例如，限制在 Si-beta 骨架中的 Pt 和 Sn(IV) 位点之间的相互作用导致 Pt 物种的高分散 [10.1039/C9CY01907C]。

6. 限域效应：
沸石结构有助于限制和稳定活性金属物种，抑制烧结和积碳形成。这种效应在 BEA 和 SAPO-34 沸石中特别明显 [10.1039/C9CY01907C, 10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008]。

这些因素共同促进了催化剂在 PDH 反应中的性能改善。例如，通过改进的后合成方法制备的 Pt/Sn-Si-beta 催化剂在 550°C 下表现出 2.3 mol g⁻¹Pt h⁻¹ 的丙烯生成速率，选择性 >99% [10.1039/C9CY01907C]。同样，PtSn/SAPO-34 催化剂实现了 94% 的丙烯选择性和大于 97% 的总烯烃选择性 [10.1016/j.catcom.2009.07.008]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：在 PDH 反应的沸石基催化剂研究方面取得了显著进展，特别是在 BEA 拓扑结构和硅铝磷酸盐沸石的开发方面。制备方法、组成和沸石拓扑结构之间的相互作用为催化剂优化提供了众多机会。

未来研究方向可能包括：
1. 探索新型合成方法，在 BEA 和 SAPO 沸石中创建层级孔结构，潜在地改善传质和催化剂稳定性。
2. 研究使用先进表征技术，如原位光谱，以深入了解 PDH 反应过程中活性位点的动态行为。
3. 开发精确控制沸石骨架内金属物种分布和尺寸的策略，可能通过原子层沉积或先进包覆技术实现。
4. 探索沸石基催化剂在其他脱氢反应中的潜力，利用从 PDH 研究中获得的知识。
5. 研究沸石基催化剂在工业条件下的长期稳定性和再生，重点关注最小化失活和延长催化剂寿命。

这些未来方向可能导致开发出更高效、更稳定的 PDH 反应催化剂，有潜力革新石化工业中的丙烯生产。
