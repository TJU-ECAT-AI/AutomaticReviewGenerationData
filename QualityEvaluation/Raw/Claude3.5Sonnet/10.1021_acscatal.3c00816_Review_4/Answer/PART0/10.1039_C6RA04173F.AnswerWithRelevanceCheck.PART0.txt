      <English>
          The study focuses on Ce-modified ZSM-5 zeolites for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, rather than BEA topology or silicoaluminophosphate zeolites. However, several key factors contributing to improved catalyst performance in PDH reactions can be identified:

          1. Preparation method: Hydrothermal synthesis was used to incorporate cerium into the ZSM-5 framework, influencing the zeolite's morphology and textural properties.

          2. Composition: The optimal cerium content was found to be 0.76 wt%, resulting in the highest reaction activity and stability.

          3. Metal dispersion: Ce-modification promoted better dispersion of metallic particles (Pt and Sn) on the support.

          4. Acidity modification: Cerium substitution decreased the strength of weak acid sites.

          5. Metal-support interaction: Ce-modification enhanced the interaction between Sn species and the support.

          6. Hydrogen adsorption: The Ce-modified material improved the catalyst's ability to adsorb hydrogen at low temperatures.

          7. Coke resistance: Cerium facilitated the migration of coke from metal active sites to the support, potentially improving catalyst stability.

          These factors collectively contributed to improved propylene selectivity, conversion rates, and catalyst stability in PDH reactions. While the study doesn't directly address BEA topology or silicoaluminophosphate zeolites, similar principles of metal dispersion, acidity modification, and enhanced metal-support interactions could potentially be applied to other zeolite-based catalysts to improve their performance in PDH reactions.
