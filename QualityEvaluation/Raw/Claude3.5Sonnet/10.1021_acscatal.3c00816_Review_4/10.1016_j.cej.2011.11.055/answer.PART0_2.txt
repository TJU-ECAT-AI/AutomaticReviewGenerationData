The literature provides some insights into how preparation methods, composition, and zeolite topology influence the performance of zeolite-based catalysts in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions, particularly for ZSM-5 topology:

1. Pore geometry: Three-dimensional microporous materials like ZSM-5 were found to be better catalytic supports than mesoporous materials like SBA-15 due to their intrinsic nature.

2. Promoter effects: The addition of La to PtSnNa/ZSM-5 catalysts resulted in higher propane conversion and propene selectivity.

3. Preparation method: Sequential impregnation was used, with La(NO3)3 impregnation followed by calcination at various temperatures, then impregnation with Pt, Sn, and Na precursors.

4. Calcination temperature: The calcination temperature of La significantly affected catalyst performance, with 650°C showing the best activity and stability.

5. Composition: The optimal catalyst composition included Pt, Sn, Na, and La on ZSM-5 support.

Key factors contributing to improved performance include:
- Appropriate pore geometry (3D microporous structure)
- Suitable promoters (La in this case)
- Optimal calcination temperature (650°C for La)
- Proper metal loading and composition
