Silica-supported zirconium hydrides and alumina-supported thorium hydrides are both active catalysts for C-H bond activation reactions, particularly in H/D exchange processes. However, they exhibit distinct characteristics in terms of preparation, structure, and catalytic properties.

Preparation and Structure:
Silica-supported zirconium hydrides are typically prepared by grafting organozirconium compounds (e.g., tetraneopentylzirconium) onto partially dehydroxylated silica, followed by treatment under hydrogen [10.1016/j.molcata.2003.11.028]. This results in a mixture of mono- and bis-hydride species, primarily (≡SiO)3ZrH and (≡SiO)2ZrH2 [10.1007/s11244-005-9259-y]. In contrast, alumina-supported thorium hydrides are generated by treating Th(η5-allyl)4 supported on dehydroxylated γ-alumina with hydrogen [10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8].

Catalytic Properties:
Both systems demonstrate high activity in C-H bond activation and H/D exchange reactions. Silica-supported zirconium hydrides catalyze H/D exchange in H2/D2 or CH4/D2 mixtures via a stepwise insertion of deuterium, with the process being extremely fast, even at temperatures below room temperature [10.1021/cr900122p\_3]. The bis-hydride species (≡SiO)2ZrH2 is reported to be more reactive towards CH4 than the mono-hydride [10.1021/cr900122p\_3].

Alumina-supported thorium hydrides also catalyze H/D exchange reactions in D2/alkane mixtures with high efficiency. The reaction rates are reported to be 800-1200 h^-1, with a slight selectivity for terminal C-H bonds [10.1021/cr900122p\_3].

Mechanisms:
The mechanism for C-H bond activation in zirconium hydride systems involves a σ-bond metathesis pathway. Computational studies suggest a two-step mechanism: first, C-H bond activation via σ-bond metathesis forms a metal-alkyl intermediate, followed by hydrogenolysis, which regenerates the metal hydride and incorporates deuterium into the alkane [10.1021/cr900122p\_3].

For thorium hydrides, the mechanism involves β-H transfer and a chain walking process, which explains the observed cis-trans isomerization of dimethylcyclohexanes during H/D exchange [10.1021/cr900122p\_3].

Selectivity and Reaction Rates:
Zirconium hydrides exhibit extremely fast H2/D2 exchange, with an estimated upper bound for activation energy of about 2 kcal/mol [10.1021/om0007926]. The CH4/D2 exchange shows a low activation energy (7 ± 1 kcal/mol) and a large negative entropy of activation (-27 ± 3 eu), consistent with a σ-bond metathesis pathway [10.1021/om0007926].

Thorium hydrides demonstrate high reaction rates (800-1200 h^-1) in H/D exchange, with slight selectivity for terminal C-H bonds. However, the overall incorporation of deuterium is not selective [10.1021/cr900122p\_3].
