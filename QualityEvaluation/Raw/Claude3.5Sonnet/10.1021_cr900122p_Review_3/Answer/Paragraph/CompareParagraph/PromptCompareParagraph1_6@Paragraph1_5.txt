Silica-supported zirconium hydrides and alumina-supported thorium hydrides are both active catalysts for C-H bond activation reactions, demonstrating unique properties and mechanisms in H/D exchange and alkane activation processes.

Preparation and Structure:
Silica-supported zirconium hydrides are typically prepared by grafting organozirconium compounds (e.g., tetraneopentylzirconium) onto partially dehydroxylated silica, followed by treatment under hydrogen [10.1016/j.molcata.2003.11.028]. This results in a mixture of surface species, primarily (≡SiO)3ZrH and (≡SiO)2ZrH2 [10.1007/s11244-005-9259-y]. In contrast, alumina-supported thorium hydrides are generated by adsorbing Th(η5-allyl)4 on dehydroxylated γ-alumina, followed by treatment under hydrogen [10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8].

Catalytic Properties:
Both catalysts exhibit high activity in C-H bond activation reactions. Zirconium hydrides catalyze H/D exchange in H2/D2 or CH4/D2 mixtures, with the bis-hydride species showing higher reactivity towards CH4 [10.1021/cr900122p\_3]. They also catalyze low-temperature hydrogenolysis of alkanes but cannot cleave the C-C bond of ethane [10.1016/j.molcata.2003.11.028]. Thorium hydrides demonstrate high activity in arene hydrogenation and alkane C-H activation, with benzene reduction occurring at a turnover frequency of ~25,000 h-1 per active site [10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8].

Mechanisms:
For zirconium hydrides, H/D exchange proceeds via a two-step mechanism: (1) C-H bond activation through σ-bond metathesis, forming a metal-alkyl intermediate, followed by (2) hydrogenolysis, regenerating the metal hydride and incorporating deuterium in the alkane [10.1021/cr900122p\_3]. The activation energy for CH4/D2 exchange is low (7 ± 1 kcal/mol) with a large negative entropy of activation (-27 ± 3 eu), consistent with a constrained transition state in σ-bond metathesis [10.1021/om0007926].

Thorium hydrides operate through electrophilic four-center activation processes. The mechanism for alkane activation involves endothermic Th-C bond formation and HD elimination via a four-center, heterolytic σ-bond metathesis pathway, followed by deuterolysis [10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8].

Reaction Rates and Selectivity:
Zirconium hydrides catalyze H2/D2 exchange extremely rapidly, even at liquid nitrogen temperatures, with an estimated upper bound for activation energy of about 2 kcal/mol [10.1021/om0007926]. Thorium hydrides demonstrate high rates for H/D exchange (800-1200 h-1) with slight selectivity for terminal C-H bonds [10.1021/cr900122p\_3].

Zirconium hydride systems generally show low regioselectivity in catalytic processes [10.1007/s11244-005-9259-y]. In contrast, thorium hydrides exhibit substantial selectivity in C-H functionalization, with a preference order of primary > secondary > tertiary C-H bonds, and sterically less hindered > sterically more hindered positions [10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8].

Role of σ-bond metathesis, hydrogenolysis, and chain walking:
Both systems utilize σ-bond metathesis in their catalytic cycles. For zirconium hydrides, this is followed by hydrogenolysis in H/D exchange reactions [10.1021/cr900122p\_3]. Thorium hydrides incorporate chain walking mechanisms, as evidenced by the cis-trans isomerization of dimethylcyclohexanes during H/D exchange, likely via β-H transfer [10.1021/cr900122p\_3].

1. Zirconium hydrides:
   - Catalyze H/D exchange in H2/D2 or CH4/D2 mixtures [10.1021/cr900122p\_3]
   - Active in low-temperature hydrogenolysis of alkanes [10.1016/j.molcata.2003.11.028]
   - Cannot cleave C-C bonds in ethane [10.1016/j.molcata.2003.11.028]

2. Thorium hydrides:
   - Catalyze H/D exchange in D2/alkane mixtures [10.1021/cr900122p\_3]
   - Facilitate arene hydrogenation and alkane activation [10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8]

The mechanisms for C-H bond activation differ between the two catalysts:

1. Zirconium hydrides:
   - H/D exchange proceeds via a two-step mechanism:
     a) C-H bond activation through σ-bond metathesis

   - Low activation energy (7 ± 1 kcal/mol) and large negative entropy of activation (-27 ± 3 eu) for CH4/D2 exchange [10.1021/om0007926]

   - Electrophilic four-center activation processes [10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8]
   - Involves σ-bond metathesis, β-H elimination, and chain walking [10.1021/cr900122p\_3]

Regarding selectivity, zirconium hydrides show low regioselectivity [10.1007/s11244-005-9259-y]. Thorium hydrides display slight selectivity for terminal C-H bonds but overall non-selective deuterium incorporation [10.1021/cr900122p\_3].
