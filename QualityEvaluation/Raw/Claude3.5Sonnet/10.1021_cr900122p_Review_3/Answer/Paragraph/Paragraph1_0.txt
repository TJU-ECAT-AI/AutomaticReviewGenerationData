Catalytic Mechanisms and Reactivity of Surface-Supported Metal Species in C-H Bond Activation, H/D Exchange, and Alkane Metathesis Reactions

Surface-supported metal hydrides, particularly those of early transition metals and actinides, have shown remarkable activity in C-H bond activation, H/D exchange, and alkane metathesis reactions. This review focuses on silica-supported zirconium hydrides and alumina-supported thorium hydrides, comparing their preparation, structure, and catalytic properties.

Preparation and Structure:
Silica-supported zirconium hydrides are typically prepared by grafting organozirconium compounds (e.g., tetraneopentylzirconium) onto partially dehydroxylated silica, followed by treatment under hydrogen [10.1016/j.molcata.2003.11.028]. This process results in a mixture of surface species, primarily (≡SiO)3ZrH and (≡SiO)2ZrH2, with a small amount of Zr(III) species [10.1007/s11244-005-9259-y]. In contrast, alumina-supported thorium hydrides are generated by treating Th(η5-allyl)4 supported on dehydroxylated γ-alumina with hydrogen [10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8].

Catalytic Properties in C-H Bond Activation:
Both zirconium and thorium hydrides demonstrate high activity in C-H bond activation. Silica-supported zirconium hydrides can activate alkanes at 25°C and methane at 120°C, forming zirconium-alkyls [10.1039/c39950000991]. They also catalyze low-temperature hydrogenolysis of various alkanes. Alumina-supported thorium hydrides show high activity for arene hydrogenation and alkane C-H activation at 90°C and 30-180 psi hydrogen pressure [10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8].

H/D Exchange Mechanisms:
Zirconium hydrides catalyze H/D exchange in H2/D2 or CH4/D2 mixtures via a stepwise insertion of deuterium. The reaction is exceptionally fast, even below room temperature. The mechanism involves a two-step process: CH bond activation via σ-bond metathesis, forming a metal-alkyl intermediate, followed by hydrogenolysis, which regenerates the metal hydride and incorporates deuterium in the alkane [10.1021/cr900122p_3]. The activation energy for CH4/D2 exchange is low (7 ± 1 kcal/mol) with a large negative entropy of activation (-27 ± 3 eu), consistent with a σ-bond metathesis pathway [10.1021/om0007926].

Thorium hydrides catalyze H/D exchange in D2/alkane mixtures with fast reaction rates (800-1200 h^-1). The reaction shows slight selectivity for terminal CH bonds. Interestingly, dimethylcyclohexanes undergo cis-trans isomerization during the H/D exchange process, likely via β-H transfer and a chain walking mechanism [10.1021/cr900122p_3].

Alkane Metathesis:
While zirconium hydrides are not reported to catalyze alkane metathesis, silica-supported tantalum hydrides [(≡SiO)2TaH] have been shown to catalyze this reaction. The mechanism may involve a four-centered σ-bond metathesis transition state, an oxidative addition/reductive elimination pathway, or a carbene mechanism [10.1016/j.molcata.2003.11.028].

Reaction Rates and Selectivity:
Zirconium hydrides exhibit extremely fast H2/D2 exchange, even at liquid nitrogen temperatures, with an estimated upper bound for activation energy of about 2 kcal/mol [10.1021/om0007926]. Thorium hydrides show high rates for benzene reduction (turnover frequency ~25,000 h^-1 per active site) and cyclohexane C-H exchange with D2 (~1,300 h^-1 per active site) [10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8].

In terms of selectivity, thorium hydrides show a preference for primary over secondary and tertiary C-H bonds, and for sterically less hindered over more hindered positions [10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8]. Zirconium hydride systems generally show low regioselectivity in catalytic processes [10.1007/s11244-005-9259-y].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The study of surface-supported metal hydrides in C-H bond activation, H/D exchange, and alkane metathesis reactions has revealed the importance of metal-support interactions and the role of different surface species in catalytic activity. Future research could focus on developing more selective catalysts by fine-tuning the metal-support interaction and the distribution of different surface species. The unique properties of early transition metals and actinides in these reactions suggest potential applications in isotope labeling, natural gas conversion, and the development of novel C-H functionalization methodologies. Additionally, the mechanistic insights gained from these studies could be applied to the design of new catalysts for challenging transformations, such as selective alkane functionalization or the activation of traditionally inert C-H bonds. Further investigation into the role of metal oxidation state and ligand environment could lead to the development of more efficient and selective catalysts for these important reactions.

表面支撑金属物种在C-H键活化、H/D交换和烷烃复分解反应中的催化机理和反应性

表面支撑的金属氢化物，特别是早期过渡金属和锕系元素的氢化物，在C-H键活化、H/D交换和烷烃复分解反应中表现出显著的活性。本综述重点关注二氧化硅支撑的锆氢化物和氧化铝支撑的钍氢化物，比较它们的制备、结构和催化性能。

制备和结构：
二氧化硅支撑的锆氢化物通常通过将有机锆化合物（如四新戊基锆）接枝到部分脱羟基的二氧化硅上，然后在氢气下处理来制备[10.1016/j.molcata.2003.11.028]。这个过程产生了表面物种的混合物，主要是(≡SiO)3ZrH和(≡SiO)2ZrH2，还有少量的Zr(III)物种[10.1007/s11244-005-9259-y]。相比之下，氧化铝支撑的钍氢化物是通过在氢气下处理支撑在脱羟基γ-氧化铝上的Th(η5-烯丙基)4生成的[10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8]。

C-H键活化的催化性能：
锆和钍氢化物在C-H键活化中都表现出高活性。二氧化硅支撑的锆氢化物可以在25°C下活化烷烃，在120°C下活化甲烷，形成锆-烷基[10.1039/c39950000991]。它们还可以催化各种烷烃的低温氢解。氧化铝支撑的钍氢化物在90°C和30-180 psi氢压下对芳烃氢化和烷烃C-H活化表现出高活性[10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8]。

H/D交换机理：
锆氢化物通过逐步插入氘来催化H2/D2或CH4/D2混合物中的H/D交换。即使在室温以下，反应也异常迅速。机理涉及两步过程：通过σ-键复分解活化CH键，形成金属-烷基中间体，然后进行氢解，再生金属氢化物并将氘掺入烷烃[10.1021/cr900122p_3]。CH4/D2交换的活化能较低（7 ± 1 kcal/mol），活化熵大幅度为负（-27 ± 3 eu），这与σ-键复分解途径一致[10.1021/om0007926]。

钍氢化物催化D2/烷烃混合物中的H/D交换，反应速率快（800-1200 h^-1）。反应对端基CH键显示出轻微的选择性。有趣的是，二甲基环己烷在H/D交换过程中发生顺反异构化，可能通过β-H转移和链行走机制[10.1021/cr900122p_3]。

烷烃复分解：
虽然没有报道锆氢化物能催化烷烃复分解，但二氧化硅支撑的钽氢化物[(≡SiO)2TaH]已被证明可以催化这种反应。机理可能涉及四中心σ-键复分解过渡态、氧化加成/还原消除途径或卡宾机制[10.1016/j.molcata.2003.11.028]。

反应速率和选择性：
锆氢化物表现出极快的H2/D2交换，即使在液氮温度下，活化能估计上限约为2 kcal/mol [10.1021/om0007926]。钍氢化物在苯还原（每个活性位点的转换频率~25,000 h^-1）和环己烷与D2的C-H交换（~1,300 h^-1每个活性位点）方面显示出高速率[10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8]。

在选择性方面，钍氢化物对一级C-H键比二级和三级C-H键更有偏好，对位阻较小的位置比位阻较大的位置更有偏好[10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8]。锆氢化物系统在催化过程中通常表现出低区域选择性[10.1007/s11244-005-9259-y]。

综合理解与未来展望：
对表面支撑金属氢化物在C-H键活化、H/D交换和烷烃复分解反应中的研究揭示了金属-载体相互作用的重要性，以及不同表面物种在催化活性中的作用。未来的研究可以集中在通过精细调节金属-载体相互作用和不同表面物种的分布来开发更具选择性的催化剂。早期过渡金属和锕系元素在这些反应中的独特性质暗示了它们在同位素标记、天然气转化和发展新型C-H官能化方法学方面的潜在应用。此外，从这些研究中获得的机理洞察可以应用于设计新的催化剂，用于具有挑战性的转化，如选择性烷烃官能化或传统惰性C-H键的活化。对金属氧化态和配体环境作用的进一步研究可能会导致开发出更高效和选择性的催化剂，用于这些重要反应。
