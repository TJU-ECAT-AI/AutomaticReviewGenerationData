Catalytic mechanisms and reactivity of surface-supported metal species in C-H bond activation, H/D exchange, and alkane metathesis reactions

Silica-supported zirconium hydrides and alumina-supported thorium hydrides are both active catalysts for C-H bond activation and H/D exchange reactions, but they exhibit different properties and mechanisms.

Preparation and Structure:
Silica-supported zirconium hydrides are typically prepared by grafting organozirconium compounds (e.g., ZrR4, where R can be allyl, benzyl, or neopentyl) onto partially dehydroxylated silica, followed by treatment under hydrogen at elevated temperatures (e.g., 423 K) [10.1007/s11244-005-9259-y]. This results in a mixture of surface species, including Zr(IV) monohydrides (≡SiO)3ZrH, Zr(IV) dihydrides (≡SiO)2ZrH2, and a small amount (up to 5%) of Zr(III) hydrides (≡SiO)2ZrIIIH [10.1007/s11244-005-9259-y].

Alumina-supported thorium hydrides are generated by adsorbing Th(η5-allyl)4 on dehydroxylated γ-alumina (DA), followed by treatment under hydrogen [10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8]. Only about 8±1% of the Th surface sites are catalytically active, with the active sites being electrophilic and bearing sterically significant, nonhydridic ancillary ligation [10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8].

Catalytic Properties:
Both catalysts are active in C-H bond activation and H/D exchange reactions, but they exhibit different reactivity patterns.

Zirconium hydrides catalyze H/D exchange in H2/D2 or CH4/D2 mixtures via a stepwise insertion of deuterium [10.1021/cr900122p_3]. The reaction is extremely fast, even at temperatures below room temperature, with H2/D2 exchange being instantaneous even at liquid nitrogen temperatures [10.1021/om0007926]. The activation energy for H2/D2 exchange is estimated to be about 2 kcal/mol, while CH4/D2 exchange has an activation energy of 7 ± 1 kcal/mol [10.1021/om0007926].

Thorium hydrides catalyze H/D exchange in D2/alkane mixtures with fast reaction rates of 800-1200 h^-1 [10.1021/cr900122p_3]. They show slight selectivity for terminal C-H bonds, but overall, the incorporation of deuterium is not selective [10.1021/cr900122p_3].

Mechanisms:
The mechanism for H/D exchange on zirconium hydrides involves a two-step process: (1) C-H bond activation via σ-bond metathesis, forming a metal-alkyl intermediate, followed by (2) hydrogenolysis, which regenerates the metal hydride and incorporates deuterium in the alkane [10.1021/cr900122p_3]. This mechanism is supported by the large negative entropy of activation (-27 ± 3 eu) observed for CH4/D2 exchange, consistent with a constrained transition state [10.1021/om0007926].

For thorium hydrides, the mechanism involves electrophilic four-center activation processes [10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8]. The H/D exchange process includes endothermic Th-C bond formation and HD elimination via a four-center, heterolytic σ-bond metathesis pathway, followed by deuterolysis [10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8].

Selectivity and Chain Walking:
Zirconium hydrides show low regioselectivity in catalytic processes [10.1007/s11244-005-9259-y]. In contrast, thorium hydrides exhibit a unique selectivity pattern that does not parallel C-H bond dissociation energies: primary > secondary > tertiary, and sterically less hindered > sterically more hindered [10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8].

Chain walking is observed in the thorium hydride system, particularly during the H/D exchange of dimethylcyclohexanes, which undergo cis-trans isomerization via β-H transfer and a chain walking mechanism [10.1021/cr900122p_3].

Alkane Metathesis:
While both catalyst systems are active in C-H bond activation and H/D exchange, alkane metathesis is primarily observed with silica-supported tantalum hydrides rather than zirconium or thorium hydrides [10.1016/j.molcata.2003.11.028]. The mechanism for alkane metathesis may involve a four-centered σ-bond metathesis transition state, an oxidative addition/reductive elimination pathway, or a carbene mechanism [10.1016/j.molcata.2003.11.028].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The comparison of silica-supported zirconium hydrides and alumina-supported thorium hydrides reveals the complexity and diversity of surface-supported metal species in C-H bond activation and H/D exchange reactions. While both systems demonstrate high activity, they differ in their preparation methods, active site structures, reaction mechanisms, and selectivity patterns.

Future research directions may include:

1. Developing more precise methods for controlling the distribution of surface species (e.g., mono- vs. bis-hydrides) to optimize catalyst performance.

2. Investigating the potential for mixed-metal systems or bimetallic catalysts to combine the advantageous properties of different metals.

3. Exploring the use of advanced characterization techniques, such as in-situ spectroscopy and operando methods, to gain deeper insights into the reaction mechanisms and active site structures during catalysis.

4. Investigating the potential of these catalysts for more complex transformations, such as selective functionalization of hydrocarbons or the activation of strong C-F bonds.

5. Developing computational models that can accurately predict the reactivity and selectivity of surface-supported metal species, enabling rational catalyst design.

6. Exploring the application of these catalysts in industrially relevant processes, such as natural gas upgrading or the valorization of biomass-derived compounds.

By addressing these research directions, we can further our understanding of surface-supported metal species and develop more efficient and selective catalysts for C-H bond activation, H/D exchange, and related reactions.

表面支持金属物种在C-H键活化、H/D交换和烷烃复分解反应中的催化机理和反应活性

二氧化硅负载的锆氢化物和氧化铝负载的钍氢化物都是C-H键活化和H/D交换反应的活性催化剂，但它们表现出不同的性质和机理。

制备和结构：
二氧化硅负载的锆氢化物通常通过将有机锆化合物（如ZrR4，其中R可以是烯丙基、苄基或新戊基）接枝到部分脱羟基的二氧化硅上，然后在高温（如423 K）下进行氢处理来制备[10.1007/s11244-005-9259-y]。这导致了表面物种的混合，包括Zr(IV)单氢化物(≡SiO)3ZrH、Zr(IV)双氢化物(≡SiO)2ZrH2，以及少量（最多5%）的Zr(III)氢化物(≡SiO)2ZrIIIH [10.1007/s11244-005-9259-y]。

氧化铝负载的钍氢化物是通过将Th(η5-烯丙基)4吸附在脱羟基的γ-氧化铝（DA）上，然后进行氢处理生成的[10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8]。只有约8±1%的Th表面位点具有催化活性，活性位点具有亲电性并带有立体显著的非氢化辅助配位[10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8]。

催化性质：
两种催化剂都在C-H键活化和H/D交换反应中表现出活性，但它们表现出不同的反应模式。

锆氢化物通过逐步插入氘来催化H2/D2或CH4/D2混合物中的H/D交换[10.1021/cr900122p_3]。反应极快，即使在室温以下也能进行，H2/D2交换在液氮温度下也是瞬时的[10.1021/om0007926]。H2/D2交换的活化能估计约为2 kcal/mol，而CH4/D2交换的活化能为7 ± 1 kcal/mol [10.1021/om0007926]。

钍氢化物催化D2/烷烃混合物中的H/D交换，反应速率快，为800-1200 h^-1 [10.1021/cr900122p_3]。它们对端基C-H键表现出轻微的选择性，但总体上氘的掺入并不选择性[10.1021/cr900122p_3]。

机理：
锆氢化物上H/D交换的机理涉及两步过程：(1)通过σ-键复分解活化C-H键，形成金属-烷基中间体，然后(2)进行氢解，再生金属氢化物并将氘掺入烷烃[10.1021/cr900122p_3]。这一机理得到了CH4/D2交换观察到的大负活化熵（-27 ± 3 eu）的支持，与受限的过渡态一致[10.1021/om0007926]。

对于钍氢化物，机理涉及亲电的四中心活化过程[10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8]。H/D交换过程包括吸热的Th-C键形成和HD消除，通过四中心的杂裂σ-键复分解途径，然后进行氘解[10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8]。

选择性和链行走：
锆氢化物在催化过程中表现出低区域选择性[10.1007/s11244-005-9259-y]。相比之下，钍氢化物表现出独特的选择性模式，不与C-H键解离能平行：伯位 > 仲位 > 叔位，以及立体位阻小 > 立体位阻大[10.1016/0304-5102(93)e0210-8]。

在钍氢化物系统中观察到链行走，特别是在二甲基环己烷的H/D交换过程中，通过β-H转移和链行走机制发生顺反异构化[10.1021/cr900122p_3]。

烷烃复分解：
虽然两种催化剂系统在C-H键活化和H/D交换中都表现出活性，但烷烃复分解主要在二氧化硅负载的钽氢化物上观察到，而不是锆或钍氢化物[10.1016/j.molcata.2003.11.028]。烷烃复分解的机理可能涉及四中心σ-键复分解过渡态、氧化加成/还原消除途径或卡宾机理[10.1016/j.molcata.2003.11.028]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：
比较二氧化硅负载的锆氢化物和氧化铝负载的钍氢化物揭示了表面支持金属物种在C-H键活化和H/D交换反应中的复杂性和多样性。虽然两个系统都表现出高活性，但它们在制备方法、活性位点结构、反应机理和选择性模式方面存在差异。

未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 开发更精确的方法来控制表面物种的分布（如单氢化物与双氢化物），以优化催化剂性能。

2. 研究混合金属系统或双金属催化剂的潜力，结合不同金属的有利性质。

3. 探索先进表征技术的应用，如原位光谱和操作条件下方法，以深入了解催化过程中的反应机理和活性位点结构。

4. 研究这些催化剂在更复杂转化中的潜力，如碳氢化合物的选择性官能化或强C-F键的活化。

5. 开发能准确预测表面支持金属物种反应活性和选择性的计算模型，实现理性催化剂设计。

6. 探索这些催化剂在工业相关过程中的应用，如天然气升级或生物质衍生化合物的增值。

通过解决这些研究方向，我们可以进一步理解表面支持金属物种，并开发出更高效、更选择性的C-H键活化、H/D交换及相关反应的催化剂。
