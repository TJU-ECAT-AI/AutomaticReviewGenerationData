[1] "Through the selective reaction of homoleptic metal siloxides, hydrocarbyls, alkoxides and aluminates with hydroxyl groups found on the surface of oxides (henceforth described as grafting), it has been possible to generate isolated surface species which can be subsequently thermally treated, under reduced pressure, inert or even reactive conditions (e.g. synthetic air), to generate well-defined surface sites of known oxidation state, geometry and nuclearity"

[2] "This approach has been widely exploited for an array of transition metals (Ti, Zr, Hf, Nb, Ta, Cr, Mo, W, Mn, Fe, Co, Ni) as well as Cu, Zn, main group elements (Al, Ga, etc.) and lanthanides, where the strong MO bonds allow grafting and retention of highly dispersed metal sites."

[3] "The materials generated can be considered surface-doped oxide materials, with effective metal utilization (Msurface = 100%)."

[4] "These uniform materials, as model catalysts, are appealing as reference systems for ab initio modelling studies due to their well-defined nature, and the low nuclearity of the nanoparticles formed (in the case of late transition metals)."

<EnglishVersionAnswer>

The hydroxyl anchoring method facilitates controlled incorporation of active metal compounds in crystal materials through several mechanisms:

1. It enables selective grafting of metal precursors onto surface hydroxyl groups, generating isolated surface species.

2. These grafted species can be thermally treated to produce well-defined surface sites with known oxidation state, geometry, and nuclearity. 

3. It allows incorporation of a wide range of transition metals, main group elements, and lanthanides.

4. The strong metal-oxygen bonds formed enable retention of highly dispersed metal sites on the surface.

This approach has several implications for enhancing metal-support interactions and improving catalyst performance:

1. It produces surface-doped oxide materials with 100% metal utilization at the surface.

2. The uniform, well-defined nature of the materials makes them excellent model systems for computational studies.

3. It enables precise control over the metal sites, allowing systematic study of structure-activity relationships.

4. The highly dispersed nature of the metal sites can lead to improved activity and stability.

5. The ability to selectively incorporate different metals allows for tailoring of catalyst properties for specific reactions.

Overall, this method provides unprecedented molecular-level control over catalyst structure, enabling the development of more active, selective, and stable heterogeneous catalysts for solid-gas reactions.

1. 它能够选择性地将金属前体物接枝到表面羟基上，生成孤立的表面物种。

2. 这些接枝的物种可以通过热处理产生具有已知氧化态、几何构型和核数的明确定义的表面位点。

3. 它允许掺入各种过渡金属、主族元素和镧系元素。

4. 形成的强金属-氧键能够保持表面高度分散的金属位点。

这种方法对增强金属-载体相互作用和改善催化剂性能有几个影响：

1. 它产生表面掺杂的氧化物材料，表面金属利用率达到100%。

2. 材料均匀、结构明确的特性使其成为计算研究的优秀模型系统。

3. 它能够精确控制金属位点，允许系统研究结构-活性关系。

4. 高度分散的金属位点特性可以提高活性和稳定性。

5. 选择性掺入不同金属的能力允许针对特定反应定制催化剂性能。
