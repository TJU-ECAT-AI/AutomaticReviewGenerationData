The implications for enhancing metal-support interactions and improving catalyst performance in solid-gas reactions are significant:

1. Improved dispersion and stability: The method allows for uniform distribution of metal species throughout the support, preventing aggregation and improving catalyst durability [10.1016/j.jechem.2021.04.034].

2. Enhanced activity and selectivity: Highly dispersed, isolated metal sites can lead to improved catalytic performance by providing a high number of accessible active sites and promoting uniform catalytic behavior [10.1016/j.jechem.2021.04.034].

4. Improved stability and regenerability: Strong metal-support interactions prevent sintering and aggregation, leading to improved catalyst longevity and allowing for easy regeneration without significant loss of metal dispersion or activity [10.1016/j.jechem.2021.04.034].
