<English>
The performance of Fe oxide-based catalysts in CO2-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (CO2-ODHP) is influenced by several key factors, including catalyst reducibility, support materials, and reaction mechanisms. Among the various catalysts studied, Fe2O3/AC (iron oxide supported on activated carbon) has shown superior performance in both C3H8 conversion and C3H6 yield [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7].

Catalyst reducibility plays a crucial role in the catalytic activity of Fe oxide-based catalysts. Fe2O3/AC exhibits improved reducibility compared to other Fe oxide-based catalysts, undergoing a complete reduction process from Fe2O3 to Fe3O4 to FeO and finally to Fe [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]. This enhanced reducibility is key to its high catalytic performance in CO2-ODHP reactions.

The choice of support material significantly influences catalyst performance. Activated carbon (AC) has been found to be particularly effective, enhancing the reducibility of Fe2O3 and contributing to its superior activity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]. In contrast, other supports like γ-Al2O3 and MgO can hinder the reducibility of Fe oxide due to strong interactions between the Fe oxide and the support material. Additionally, CeO2 has been identified as a promising support due to its ability to activate CO2 through direct C=O bond scission, enhancing overall catalytic performance [10.1039/D0CY00586J].

The proposed reaction mechanism for CO2-ODHP over Fe oxide-based catalysts involves a redox cycle consisting of two main steps:
1. C3H8 oxidation to C3H6 using lattice oxygen from Fe2O3 or Fe3O4
2. Reoxidation of the reduced Fe oxide by CO2, which acts as a soft oxidant [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]

This redox cycle allows for the continuous regeneration of active sites on the catalyst surface, maintaining its activity throughout the reaction. The ability of Fe oxide to undergo this redox cycle efficiently is crucial for its effectiveness in the CO2-ODHP process.

The presence of oxidized Fe under reaction conditions is key to favoring oxidative C-H bond cleavage in the CO2-ODHP pathway [10.1002/aic.16670]. In bimetallic catalysts, such as Fe-Ni and Fe-Pt, the oxidation state of Fe plays a significant role in determining catalyst performance and stability.

Bimetallic compositions can be used to tune the selectivity towards olefins by adjusting the active sites. For example, Fe-Ni surfaces favor C-H bond scissoring, while Pt-rich surfaces favor C-C cleavage [10.1039/D0CY00586J]. This allows for the optimization of catalyst composition to achieve desired product distributions.

In terms of performance, Fe oxide-based catalysts have shown promising results in CO2-ODHP reactions. Fe2O3/AC outperforms other catalysts in both C3H8 conversion and C3H6 yield [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]. However, the specific performance can vary depending on the catalyst composition and reaction conditions. For instance, a Fe3Pt1 catalyst showed moderate activity with 32% propylene selectivity at steady state [10.1002/aic.16670].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The study of Fe oxide-based catalysts in CO2-ODHP has revealed the complex interplay between catalyst composition, support materials, and reaction conditions. Future research directions could focus on:

1. Optimizing the synergy between Fe oxide and support materials: While AC and CeO2 have shown promise, exploring novel support materials or composite supports could lead to further improvements in catalyst performance and stability.

2. In-depth investigation of the redox mechanism: Advanced in-situ characterization techniques could provide deeper insights into the Fe oxide redox cycle during CO2-ODHP, potentially leading to more efficient catalyst designs.

3. Exploring novel bimetallic and multi-metallic compositions: Building on the success of Fe-Ni and Fe-Pt catalysts, investigating other metal combinations could unveil catalysts with superior activity, selectivity, and stability.

4. Developing strategies to maintain the optimal oxidation state of Fe: As the oxidation state of Fe plays a crucial role in catalyst performance, research into methods to control and maintain the desired oxidation state under reaction conditions could lead to more robust catalysts.

5. Scaling up and process integration: Moving from laboratory-scale studies to pilot-scale testing and exploring the integration of CO2-ODHP processes with existing industrial systems could pave the way for practical applications of these catalysts in sustainable propylene production.

<Chinese>
Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2辅助丙烷氧化脱氢（CO2-ODHP）中的性能受到几个关键因素的影响，包括催化剂的可还原性、载体材料和反应机理。在研究的各种催化剂中，Fe2O3/AC（活性炭负载的氧化铁）在C3H8转化和C3H6产率方面都表现出优异的性能[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。

催化剂的可还原性在Fe氧化物基催化剂的催化活性中起着关键作用。Fe2O3/AC表现出比其他Fe氧化物基催化剂更好的可还原性，经历了从Fe2O3到Fe3O4到FeO最后到Fe的完整还原过程[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。这种增强的可还原性是其在CO2-ODHP反应中表现出高催化性能的关键。

载体材料的选择显著影响催化剂性能。活性炭（AC）被发现特别有效，增强了Fe2O3的可还原性并有助于其优异的活性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。相比之下，其他载体如γ-Al2O3和MgO由于Fe氧化物与载体材料之间的强相互作用，可能会阻碍Fe氧化物的可还原性。此外，CeO2被认为是一种有前景的载体，因为它能够通过直接C=O键断裂激活CO2，从而提高整体催化性能[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。

Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP中的proposed反应机理涉及氧化还原循环，主要包括两个步骤：
1. 利用Fe2O3或Fe3O4的晶格氧将C3H8氧化成C3H6
2. 被还原的Fe氧化物被CO2（作为软氧化剂）再氧化[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]

这个氧化还原循环允许催化剂表面活性位点的持续再生，保持其在整个反应过程中的活性。Fe氧化物高效进行这种氧化还原循环的能力对其在CO2-ODHP过程中的有效性至关重要。

在反应条件下氧化Fe的存在是通过CO2-ODHP途径有利于氧化性C-H键断裂的关键[10.1002/aic.16670]。在双金属催化剂中，如Fe-Ni和Fe-Pt，Fe的氧化状态在决定催化剂性能和稳定性方面起着重要作用。

双金属组成可以通过调整活性位点来调节烯烃的选择性。例如，Fe-Ni表面有利于C-H键断裂，而富Pt表面有利于C-C键断裂[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。这允许优化催化剂组成以获得所需的产物分布。

在性能方面，Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中显示出有前景的结果。Fe2O3/AC在C3H8转化和C3H6产率方面都优于其他催化剂[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。然而，具体性能可能因催化剂组成和反应条件而异。例如，Fe3Pt1催化剂在稳态下显示出中等活性，丙烯选择性为32%[10.1002/aic.16670]。

※※※

综合理解和未来展望：对Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP中的研究揭示了催化剂组成、载体材料和反应条件之间复杂的相互作用。未来的研究方向可以集中在：

1. 优化Fe氧化物与载体材料之间的协同作用：虽然AC和CeO2已经显示出前景，但探索新型载体材料或复合载体可能会进一步提高催化剂性能和稳定性。

2. 深入研究氧化还原机理：先进的原位表征技术可能会提供对CO2-ODHP过程中Fe氧化物氧化还原循环的更深入见解，潜在地导致更高效的催化剂设计。

3. 探索新型双金属和多金属组成：在Fe-Ni和Fe-Pt催化剂成功的基础上，研究其他金属组合可能会发现具有更优异活性、选择性和稳定性的催化剂。

4. 开发维持Fe最佳氧化状态的策略：由于Fe的氧化状态在催化剂性能中起着关键作用，研究在反应条件下控制和维持所需氧化状态的方法可能会导致更稳健的催化剂。

5. 扩大规模和过程集成：从实验室规模研究转向试点规模测试，并探索CO2-ODHP过程与现有工业系统的集成，可能为这些催化剂在可持续丙烯生产中的实际应用铺平道路。
