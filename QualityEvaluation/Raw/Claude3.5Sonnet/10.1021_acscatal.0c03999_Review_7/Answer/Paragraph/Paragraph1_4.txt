<English>
Fe oxide-based catalysts have shown promising performance in CO2-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (CO2-ODHP). The catalytic activity, selectivity, and stability of these catalysts are influenced by several key factors, including catalyst composition, reducibility, and support materials.

Fe2O3/AC (iron oxide supported on activated carbon) has demonstrated superior performance in CO2-ODHP compared to other Fe oxide-based catalysts. This catalyst outperforms others in both C3H8 conversion and C3H6 yield, while maintaining comparable C3H6 selectivity to bare Fe2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]. The enhanced performance of Fe2O3/AC can be attributed to its improved reducibility, which is crucial for the catalytic activity in CO2-ODHP.

The support material plays a significant role in determining the catalyst's performance. Activated carbon (AC) as a support enhances the reducibility of Fe2O3, allowing it to undergo a complete reduction process from Fe2O3 to Fe3O4 to FeO and finally to Fe [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]. This improved reducibility is key to the high catalytic performance of Fe2O3/AC. In contrast, other supports like γ-Al2O3 and MgO can hinder the reducibility of Fe oxide due to strong interactions between the Fe oxide and the support material.

CeO2 has also been identified as a promising support for Fe-based catalysts in CO2-ODHP. Its ability to activate CO2 through direct C=O bond scission enhances the overall catalytic performance [10.1039/D0CY00586J]. The oxygen mobility and oxygen storage capacity (OSC) of ceria-based catalysts contribute to the formation of stable surface active sites.

The proposed reaction mechanism for CO2-ODHP over Fe oxide-based catalysts involves a redox cycle. The process consists of two main steps: (1) C3H8 oxidation to C3H6 using lattice oxygen from Fe2O3 or Fe3O4, and (2) reoxidation of the reduced Fe oxide by CO2, which acts as a soft oxidant [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]. This redox cycle allows for the continuous regeneration of the active sites on the catalyst surface, maintaining its activity throughout the reaction.

Bimetallic compositions have also been investigated for CO2-ODHP. Fe-Ni and Fe-Pt catalysts supported on CeO2 have shown promising results [10.1002/aic.16670]. The presence of oxidized Fe under reaction conditions is crucial for favoring oxidative C-H bond cleavage in the CO2-ODHP pathway. The extent of Fe oxidation plays a significant role in catalyst performance, with higher oxidation of Fe correlating with better activity and stability in CO2-ODHP.

Despite the promising performance of Fe oxide-based catalysts in CO2-ODHP, challenges remain. Catalyst deactivation has been observed in some cases, particularly with Fe-Co bimetallic catalysts [10.1002/aic.16670]. However, the deactivation mechanism appears to differ from traditional coking, as evidenced by the presence of small regions with higher Fe content in spent catalysts [10.1039/D0CY00586J].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of Fe oxide-based catalysts for CO2-ODHP represents a promising direction in the field of heterogeneous catalysis for alkane activation. The synergistic effects between Fe oxide and various support materials, particularly activated carbon and CeO2, offer opportunities for further optimization of catalyst performance. Future research could focus on:

1. Exploring novel support materials or composite supports that can further enhance the reducibility and stability of Fe oxide species.
2. Investigating the potential of trimetallic or multi-component catalysts to fine-tune the balance between C-H activation and C-C cleavage pathways.
3. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation state under reaction conditions.
4. Exploring the potential of Fe oxide-based catalysts for other CO2-assisted reactions, such as the activation of longer-chain alkanes or the valorization of biomass-derived compounds.
5. Investigating the scalability and long-term stability of Fe oxide-based catalysts for potential industrial applications in CO2 utilization and olefin production.

These research directions could lead to more efficient and sustainable processes for olefin production, contributing to the broader goals of CO2 utilization and the development of greener chemical processes.

<Chinese>
在CO2辅助的丙烷氧化脱氢（CO2-ODHP）反应中，Fe氧化物基催化剂表现出了令人瞩目的性能。这些催化剂的催化活性、选择性和稳定性受到多个关键因素的影响，包括催化剂组成、可还原性和载体材料。

Fe2O3/AC（负载在活性炭上的氧化铁）在CO2-ODHP反应中表现出优于其他Fe氧化物基催化剂的性能。该催化剂在C3H8转化率和C3H6产率方面都优于其他催化剂，同时保持了与纯Fe2O3相当的C3H6选择性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。Fe2O3/AC的增强性能可归因于其改善的可还原性，这对CO2-ODHP反应的催化活性至关重要。

载体材料在决定催化剂性能方面起着重要作用。作为载体的活性炭（AC）增强了Fe2O3的可还原性，使其能够经历从Fe2O3到Fe3O4到FeO最后到Fe的完整还原过程 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。这种改善的可还原性是Fe2O3/AC高催化性能的关键。相比之下，其他载体如γ-Al2O3和MgO可能会因Fe氧化物与载体材料之间的强相互作用而阻碍Fe氧化物的可还原性。

CeO2也被确定为Fe基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的一种有前景的载体。其通过直接C=O键断裂激活CO2的能力增强了整体催化性能 [10.1039/D0CY00586J]。基于CeO2的催化剂的氧移动性和氧储存容量（OSC）有助于形成稳定的表面活性位点。

Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的提议反应机理涉及氧化还原循环。该过程主要包括两个步骤：（1）使用来自Fe2O3或Fe3O4的晶格氧将C3H8氧化为C3H6，以及（2）通过CO2（作为软氧化剂）重新氧化还原的Fe氧化物 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。这种氧化还原循环允许催化剂表面活性位点的持续再生，保持其在整个反应过程中的活性。

双金属组成的催化剂也已被研究用于CO2-ODHP反应。负载在CeO2上的Fe-Ni和Fe-Pt催化剂显示出了有前景的结果 [10.1002/aic.16670]。反应条件下氧化Fe的存在对于在CO2-ODHP途径中有利于氧化性C-H键断裂至关重要。Fe氧化程度在催化剂性能中起着重要作用，Fe的高氧化程度与CO2-ODHP中更好的活性和稳定性相关。

尽管Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中表现出令人鼓舞的性能，但仍然存在挑战。在某些情况下观察到催化剂失活，特别是对于Fe-Co双金属催化剂 [10.1002/aic.16670]。然而，失活机制似乎与传统的结焦不同，这从失活催化剂中存在小区域Fe含量较高的证据可以看出 [10.1039/D0CY00586J]。

综合理解和未来展望：Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的发展代表了烷烃活化异相催化领域的一个有前景的方向。Fe氧化物与各种载体材料（特别是活性炭和CeO2）之间的协同效应为进一步优化催化剂性能提供了机会。未来的研究可以集中在：

1. 探索新型载体材料或复合载体，以进一步增强Fe氧化物物种的可还原性和稳定性。
2. 研究三金属或多组分催化剂的潜力，以微调C-H活化和C-C断裂途径之间的平衡。
3. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解反应条件下催化剂结构和氧化态的动态变化。
4. 探索Fe氧化物基催化剂在其他CO2辅助反应中的潜力，如长链烷烃的活化或生物质衍生化合物的增值。
5. 研究Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2利用和烯烃生产的潜在工业应用中的可扩展性和长期稳定性。

这些研究方向可能导致更高效和可持续的烯烃生产过程，为CO2利用和更绿色化学过程的发展的更广泛目标做出贡献。
