<English>
The performance of Fe oxide-based catalysts in CO2-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (CO2-ODHP) is significantly influenced by catalyst reducibility and support materials, particularly in the case of Fe2O3/AC (iron oxide supported on activated carbon). 

Fe2O3/AC demonstrates superior performance in both C3H8 conversion and C3H6 yield compared to other Fe oxide-based catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]. This enhanced performance is attributed to the improved reducibility of Fe2O3 when supported on activated carbon. H2-TPR results show that Fe2O3/AC undergoes a complete reduction process: Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]. This improved reducibility is key to the high catalytic activity of Fe2O3/AC in CO2-ODHP.

The choice of support material significantly influences catalyst performance. Activated carbon (AC) enhances the reducibility of Fe2O3, while other supports like γ-Al2O3 and MgO can hinder reducibility due to strong interactions between Fe oxide and the support material [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]. In a separate study on ethylbenzene dehydrogenation, active carbon with the lowest ash content (D-90) was found to be the best support, outperforming the influence of iron concentration on catalytic activity [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7].

The proposed reaction mechanism for CO2-ODHP over Fe oxide-based catalysts involves a redox cycle, which is crucial for maintaining catalytic activity. The process consists of two main steps:
1. C3H8 oxidation to C3H6 using lattice oxygen from Fe2O3 or Fe3O4
2. Reoxidation of the reduced Fe oxide by CO2, which acts as a soft oxidant [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]

This mechanism is similar to the Mars-van Krevelen type mechanism observed in ethylbenzene dehydrogenation, where the redox cycle can be represented as: MeFemOn + CO2 → MeFemOn+1 + CO [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7].

The oxidation state of Fe plays a significant role in catalyst performance. The presence of oxidized Fe under reaction conditions is crucial for favoring oxidative C-H bond cleavage in the CO2-ODHP pathway [10.1002/aic.16670]. Higher oxidation of Fe correlates with better activity and stability in CO2-ODHP.

In bimetallic catalysts, such as Fe-Ni and Fe-Pt, the composition affects the reaction pathway. Fe-Ni surfaces favor C-H bond scissoring, while Pt-rich surfaces favor C-C cleavage [10.1039/D0CY00586J]. The presence of a second metal can also influence the oxidation state and surface segregation of Fe, impacting catalyst performance [10.1002/aic.16670].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The research on Fe oxide-based catalysts for CO2-ODHP reveals the complex interplay between catalyst composition, support materials, and reaction conditions. Future research directions could focus on:

1. Optimizing the Fe2O3/AC catalyst by fine-tuning the AC properties and Fe loading to further enhance reducibility and catalytic performance.

2. Exploring novel support materials that can maintain the desired oxidation state of Fe while providing high surface area and stability.

3. Investigating the synergistic effects of promoters, such as alkali metal oxides, on Fe2O3/AC to further improve catalytic activity and selectivity.

4. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic changes in Fe oxidation state and surface structure during the CO2-ODHP reaction.

5. Computational studies to elucidate the detailed mechanism of CO2 activation and its role in the Fe oxide redox cycle, potentially leading to the design of more efficient catalysts.

6. Exploring the potential of Fe-based bimetallic catalysts supported on CeO2, leveraging the oxygen storage capacity of ceria and the redox properties of Fe oxide for enhanced CO2-ODHP performance.

These research directions could lead to the development of more efficient and stable Fe oxide-based catalysts for CO2-ODHP, contributing to the broader goal of sustainable propylene production using CO2 as a soft oxidant.

<Chinese>
在CO2辅助的丙烷氧化脱氢（CO2-ODHP）反应中，Fe氧化物基催化剂的性能受到催化剂可还原性和载体材料的显著影响，特别是在Fe2O3/AC（活性炭负载的氧化铁）的情况下。

与其他Fe氧化物基催化剂相比，Fe2O3/AC在C3H8转化和C3H6收率方面表现出优异的性能[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。这种增强的性能归因于Fe2O3在活性炭载体上的可还原性提高。H2-TPR结果显示，Fe2O3/AC经历了完整的还原过程：Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。这种改进的可还原性是Fe2O3/AC在CO2-ODHP中具有高催化活性的关键。

载体材料的选择对催化剂性能有显著影响。活性炭（AC）增强了Fe2O3的可还原性，而其他载体如γ-Al2O3和MgO可能由于Fe氧化物与载体材料之间的强相互作用而抑制可还原性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。在一项关于乙苯脱氢的单独研究中，发现灰分含量最低的活性炭（D-90）是最佳载体，其影响超过了铁浓度对催化活性的影响[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]。

Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP中的提议反应机理涉及一个氧化还原循环，这对维持催化活性至关重要。该过程主要包括两个步骤：
1. 使用来自Fe2O3或Fe3O4的晶格氧将C3H8氧化为C3H6
2. 通过CO2作为软氧化剂重新氧化还原后的Fe氧化物[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]

这种机理类似于在乙苯脱氢中观察到的Mars-van Krevelen型机理，其中氧化还原循环可以表示为：MeFemOn + CO2 → MeFemOn+1 + CO [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]。

Fe的氧化态在催化剂性能中起着重要作用。反应条件下氧化Fe的存在对于促进CO2-ODHP途径中的氧化C-H键断裂至关重要[10.1002/aic.16670]。Fe的高氧化程度与CO2-ODHP中更好的活性和稳定性相关。

在双金属催化剂中，如Fe-Ni和Fe-Pt，组成影响反应途径。Fe-Ni表面有利于C-H键断裂，而富Pt表面有利于C-C键断裂[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。第二种金属的存在还可以影响Fe的氧化态和表面偏析，从而影响催化剂性能[10.1002/aic.16670]。

※※※

综合理解和未来展望：对CO2-ODHP的Fe氧化物基催化剂的研究揭示了催化剂组成、载体材料和反应条件之间的复杂相互作用。未来的研究方向可以集中在：

1. 通过微调AC性质和Fe负载量来优化Fe2O3/AC催化剂，进一步提高可还原性和催化性能。

2. 探索新型载体材料，既可以维持Fe的理想氧化态，又能提供高比表面积和稳定性。

3. 研究助剂（如碱金属氧化物）对Fe2O3/AC的协同效应，进一步提高催化活性和选择性。

4. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解CO2-ODHP反应过程中Fe氧化态和表面结构的动态变化。

5. 进行计算研究，阐明CO2活化和其在Fe氧化物氧化还原循环中的作用的详细机理，可能导致更高效催化剂的设计。

6. 探索CeO2负载的Fe基双金属催化剂的潜力，利用CeO2的储氧能力和Fe氧化物的氧化还原性质，以提高CO2-ODHP性能。

这些研究方向可能导致更高效和稳定的Fe氧化物基催化剂用于CO2-ODHP的开发，为使用CO2作为软氧化剂的可持续丙烯生产的更广泛目标做出贡献。
