Fe oxide-based catalysts have shown promising performance in CO2-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (CO2-ODHP). The catalytic activity, selectivity, and stability of these catalysts are influenced by several key factors, including catalyst reducibility, support materials, and bimetallic composition.

Fe2O3/AC (iron oxide supported on activated carbon) has demonstrated superior performance among Fe oxide-based catalysts in CO2-ODHP, exhibiting high C3H8 conversion and C3H6 yield [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]. The enhanced performance of Fe2O3/AC can be attributed to its improved reducibility, which is crucial for the catalytic activity. H2-TPR results show that Fe2O3/AC undergoes a complete reduction process from Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe, indicating its ability to participate effectively in the redox cycle during the reaction [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7].

The choice of support material significantly influences the catalyst's performance. Activated carbon (AC) has been found to enhance the reducibility of Fe2O3, leading to superior catalytic activity compared to other supports like γ-Al2O3 and MgO [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]. Additionally, CeO2 has been identified as a promising support due to its ability to activate CO2 through direct C=O bond scission, as well as its oxygen mobility and oxygen storage capacity (OSC) [10.1039/D0CY00586J].

The reaction mechanism for CO2-ODHP over Fe oxide-based catalysts involves a redox cycle. The process consists of two main steps: (1) C3H8 oxidation to C3H6 using lattice oxygen from Fe2O3 or Fe3O4, and (2) reoxidation of reduced Fe oxide by CO2, which acts as a soft oxidant [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]. This redox cycle allows for the continuous regeneration of active sites on the catalyst surface, maintaining its activity throughout the reaction.

Bimetallic compositions have also been explored to improve catalyst performance. Fe-Ni and Fe-Pt catalysts supported on CeO2 have shown promising results in CO2-ODHP [10.1002/aic.16670]. The presence of oxidized Fe under reaction conditions is key to favoring oxidative C-H bond cleavage in the CO2-ODHP pathway. Different metal combinations favor different reaction pathways; for example, Fe-Ni surfaces favor C-H bond scissoring, while Pt-rich surfaces favor C-C cleavage [10.1039/D0CY00586J].

The extent of Fe oxidation plays a significant role in catalyst performance, with higher oxidation of Fe correlating with better activity and stability in CO2-ODHP. For instance, Fe3Co1 catalysts showed lower activity and stability compared to Fe3Ni1 due to a lower extent of Fe oxidation [10.1002/aic.16670].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of Fe oxide-based catalysts for CO2-ODHP represents a promising direction in the field of heterogeneous catalysis for sustainable chemical production. The superior performance of Fe2O3/AC and the potential of CeO2-supported catalysts highlight the importance of tailoring both the active metal and support material to optimize catalyst performance.

Future research efforts could focus on:

1. Developing novel support materials or modifying existing ones to further enhance the reducibility and stability of Fe oxide catalysts.
2. Exploring ternary or more complex metal combinations to fine-tune the catalytic properties and optimize the balance between activity, selectivity, and stability.
3. Investigating the long-term stability and regeneration strategies for these catalysts under industrial-like conditions.
4. Utilizing advanced in situ and operando characterization techniques to gain deeper insights into the dynamic changes of the catalyst structure and oxidation state during the reaction.
5. Integrating computational modeling with experimental studies to predict and design more efficient catalyst compositions and structures.

These research directions could lead to the development of highly efficient and stable catalysts for CO2-ODHP, contributing to more sustainable production of propylene and utilization of CO2 as a soft oxidant in industrial processes.

<Chinese>
Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2辅助丙烷氧化脱氢（CO2-ODHP）反应中表现出良好的性能。这些催化剂的催化活性、选择性和稳定性受到几个关键因素的影响，包括催化剂的可还原性、载体材料和双金属组成。

在Fe氧化物基催化剂中，Fe2O3/AC（负载在活性炭上的氧化铁）在CO2-ODHP反应中表现出优异的性能，具有较高的C3H8转化率和C3H6产率[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。Fe2O3/AC的增强性能可归因于其改善的可还原性，这对催化活性至关重要。H2-TPR结果表明，Fe2O3/AC经历了从Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe的完整还原过程，表明其能够在反应过程中有效参与氧化还原循环[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。

载体材料的选择对催化剂的性能有显著影响。研究发现，活性炭（AC）能够增强Fe2O3的可还原性，导致其催化活性优于其他载体如γ-Al2O3和MgO [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。此外，CeO2被确定为一种有前景的载体，因为它能够通过直接C=O键断裂来活化CO2，并具有氧移动性和氧储存能力（OSC）[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。

Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的反应机理涉及氧化还原循环。该过程主要包括两个步骤：（1）使用Fe2O3或Fe3O4的晶格氧将C3H8氧化为C3H6，以及（2）由CO2作为软氧化剂重新氧化还原后的Fe氧化物[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。这种氧化还原循环允许催化剂表面活性位点的持续再生，保持其在整个反应过程中的活性。

双金属组成也被探索用于改善催化剂性能。负载在CeO2上的Fe-Ni和Fe-Pt催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中显示出有前景的结果[10.1002/aic.16670]。在反应条件下氧化Fe的存在对于通过CO2-ODHP途径实现氧化性C-H键断裂至关重要。不同的金属组合有利于不同的反应途径；例如，Fe-Ni表面有利于C-H键断裂，而富Pt表面有利于C-C键断裂[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。

Fe的氧化程度在催化剂性能中起着重要作用，Fe的较高氧化程度与CO2-ODHP反应中更好的活性和稳定性相关。例如，由于Fe氧化程度较低，Fe3Co1催化剂显示出比Fe3Ni1更低的活性和稳定性[10.1002/aic.16670]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的开发代表了异相催化领域中可持续化学品生产的一个有前景的方向。Fe2O3/AC的优异性能和CeO2负载催化剂的潜力突出了同时调整活性金属和载体材料以优化催化剂性能的重要性。

未来的研究努力可以集中在：

1. 开发新型载体材料或改进现有材料，以进一步增强Fe氧化物催化剂的可还原性和稳定性。
2. 探索三元或更复杂的金属组合，以微调催化性能并优化活性、选择性和稳定性之间的平衡。
3. 在类工业条件下研究这些催化剂的长期稳定性和再生策略。
4. 利用先进的原位和operando表征技术，深入了解反应过程中催化剂结构和氧化状态的动态变化。
5. 将计算模拟与实验研究相结合，预测和设计更高效的催化剂组成和结构。

这些研究方向可能导致高效稳定的CO2-ODHP催化剂的开发，为更可持续的丙烯生产和CO2作为工业过程中软氧化剂的利用做出贡献。
