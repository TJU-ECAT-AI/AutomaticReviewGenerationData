<English>
Fe oxide-based catalysts have shown promising performance in CO2-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (CO2-ODHP). The catalytic activity, selectivity, and stability of these catalysts are influenced by several key factors, including catalyst reducibility, support materials, and reaction mechanisms.

Catalyst Performance and Reducibility:
Fe2O3/AC (iron oxide supported on activated carbon) has demonstrated superior performance in both C3H8 conversion and C3H6 yield compared to other Fe oxide-based catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]. The improved reducibility of Fe2O3 on AC is a crucial factor in its high catalytic performance. H2-TPR results show that Fe2O3/AC undergoes a complete reduction process: Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]. This enhanced reducibility is key to the catalyst's effectiveness in the CO2-ODHP process.

Role of Support Materials:
The choice of support material significantly influences catalyst performance. Activated carbon (AC) has been found to be particularly effective, enhancing the reducibility of Fe2O3 and allowing for a complete reduction process [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]. In contrast, other supports like γ-Al2O3 and MgO can hinder Fe oxide reducibility due to strong metal-support interactions.

CeO2 has also been identified as a promising support for Fe-based catalysts in CO2-ODHP. Its ability to activate CO2 through direct C=O bond scission enhances overall catalytic performance [10.1039/D0CY00586J]. The oxygen mobility and oxygen storage capacity (OSC) of ceria-based catalysts contribute to the formation of stable surface active sites, which is crucial for maintaining catalyst activity and stability.

Reaction Mechanism:
The proposed reaction mechanism for CO2-ODHP over Fe oxide-based catalysts involves a redox cycle. This process consists of two main steps:
1. C3H8 oxidation to C3H6 using lattice oxygen from Fe2O3 or Fe3O4
2. Reoxidation of the reduced Fe oxide by CO2, which acts as a soft oxidant [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]

This redox cycle allows for the continuous regeneration of active sites on the catalyst surface, maintaining its activity throughout the reaction. The ability of Fe oxide to undergo this redox cycle efficiently is crucial for its effectiveness in the CO2-ODHP process.

Bimetallic Catalysts:
Bimetallic Fe-based catalysts, such as Fe-Ni and Fe-Pt, have also shown promise in CO2-ODHP. The presence of oxidized Fe under reaction conditions is key to favoring oxidative C-H bond cleavage [10.1002/aic.16670]. Different metal combinations favor different reaction pathways; for example, Fe-Ni surfaces favor C-H bond scissoring, while Pt-rich surfaces favor C-C cleavage [10.1039/D0CY00586J].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The development of Fe oxide-based catalysts for CO2-ODHP represents a significant advancement in the field of catalysis for sustainable chemical production. The superior performance of Fe2O3/AC and the promising results from Fe-doped CeO2 catalysts suggest that further optimization of these systems could lead to even more efficient and stable catalysts for industrial applications.

Future research directions may include:
1. Exploring novel support materials or modifications to existing supports to further enhance Fe oxide reducibility and stability.
2. Investigating the synergistic effects between Fe oxide and other metal oxides or promoters to improve catalyst performance and selectivity.
3. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation state during the CO2-ODHP reaction.
4. Integrating computational modeling with experimental studies to guide the rational design of next-generation Fe oxide-based catalysts with improved activity and stability.
5. Scaling up the most promising catalyst systems for pilot-scale testing and eventual industrial implementation.

These research efforts could pave the way for more efficient and sustainable production of propylene, contributing to the broader goal of reducing the environmental impact of the chemical industry.

<Chinese>
Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2辅助丙烷氧化脱氢（CO2-ODHP）中表现出良好的性能。这些催化剂的催化活性、选择性和稳定性受到几个关键因素的影响，包括催化剂的可还原性、载体材料和反应机理。

催化剂性能和可还原性：
Fe2O3/AC（负载在活性炭上的氧化铁）在C3H8转化率和C3H6产率方面都表现出优于其他Fe氧化物基催化剂的性能[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。Fe2O3在AC上的改善可还原性是其高催化性能的关键因素。H2-TPR结果表明，Fe2O3/AC经历了完整的还原过程：Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。这种增强的可还原性是催化剂在CO2-ODHP过程中有效性的关键。

载体材料的作用：
载体材料的选择显著影响催化剂性能。活性炭（AC）被发现特别有效，能增强Fe2O3的可还原性，并允许完整的还原过程[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。相比之下，其他载体如γ-Al2O3和MgO可能由于强烈的金属-载体相互作用而阻碍Fe氧化物的可还原性。

CeO2也被确定为Fe基催化剂在CO2-ODHP中的一种有前景的载体。其通过直接C=O键断裂激活CO2的能力增强了整体催化性能[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。基于氧化铈的催化剂的氧移动性和氧存储能力（OSC）有助于形成稳定的表面活性位点，这对维持催化剂的活性和稳定性至关重要。

反应机理：
Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP中的提议反应机理涉及一个氧化还原循环。这个过程包括两个主要步骤：
1. 使用来自Fe2O3或Fe3O4的晶格氧将C3H8氧化为C3H6
2. 通过CO2（作为软氧化剂）将还原的Fe氧化物重新氧化[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]

这个氧化还原循环允许催化剂表面活性位点的持续再生，维持其在整个反应过程中的活性。Fe氧化物高效进行这种氧化还原循环的能力对其在CO2-ODHP过程中的有效性至关重要。

双金属催化剂：
双金属Fe基催化剂，如Fe-Ni和Fe-Pt，在CO2-ODHP中也表现出良好的前景。在反应条件下氧化Fe的存在对于促进氧化性C-H键断裂至关重要[10.1002/aic.16670]。不同的金属组合有利于不同的反应途径；例如，Fe-Ni表面有利于C-H键断裂，而富Pt表面有利于C-C键断裂[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：
Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP中的发展代表了可持续化学品生产催化领域的重大进步。Fe2O3/AC的卓越性能和Fe掺杂CeO2催化剂的promising结果表明，进一步优化这些系统可能会导致更高效和稳定的工业应用催化剂。

未来研究方向可能包括：
1. 探索新型载体材料或对现有载体进行改性，以进一步增强Fe氧化物的可还原性和稳定性。
2. 研究Fe氧化物与其他金属氧化物或促进剂之间的协同效应，以提高催化剂性能和选择性。
3. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解CO2-ODHP反应过程中催化剂结构和氧化态的动态变化。
4. 将计算机模拟与实验研究相结合，指导下一代Fe氧化物基催化剂的理性设计，以提高活性和稳定性。
5. 将最有前景的催化剂系统扩大到中试规模测试，最终实现工业化应用。

这些研究努力可能为更高效和可持续的丙烯生产铺平道路，为减少化工行业环境影响的更广泛目标做出贡献。
