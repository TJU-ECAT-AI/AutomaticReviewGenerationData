Fe oxide-based catalysts have shown promising performance in CO2-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (CO2-ODHP). The catalytic activity, selectivity, and stability of these catalysts are influenced by several key factors, including catalyst reducibility, support materials, and the redox cycle of Fe oxide.

Fe2O3/AC (iron oxide supported on activated carbon) has demonstrated superior performance among Fe oxide-based catalysts in CO2-ODHP. This catalyst outperforms others in both C3H8 conversion and C3H6 yield, while maintaining comparable C3H6 selectivity to bare Fe2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]. The enhanced performance of Fe2O3/AC can be attributed to its improved reducibility, which is crucial for catalytic activity in CO2-ODHP.

The support material plays a significant role in determining catalyst performance. Activated carbon (AC) has been found to enhance the reducibility of Fe2O3, allowing it to undergo a complete reduction process from Fe2O3 to Fe3O4 to FeO and finally to Fe [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]. This improved reducibility is key to the high catalytic performance of Fe2O3/AC compared to other tested catalysts. In contrast, other supports like γ-Al2O3 and MgO can hinder the reducibility of Fe oxide due to strong interactions between the Fe oxide and the support material.

CeO2 has also been identified as a promising support for Fe-based catalysts in CO2-ODHP. Its ability to activate CO2 through direct C=O bond scission enhances the overall catalytic performance [10.1039/D0CY00586J]. The oxygen mobility and oxygen storage capacity (OSC) of ceria-based catalysts contribute to the formation of stable surface active sites, which is crucial for maintaining catalyst performance over time.

The reaction mechanism for CO2-ODHP over Fe oxide-based catalysts involves a redox cycle. The process consists of two main steps: (1) C3H8 oxidation to C3H6 using lattice oxygen from Fe2O3 or Fe3O4, and (2) reoxidation of reduced Fe oxide by CO2, which acts as a soft oxidant [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]. This redox cycle allows for the continuous regeneration of active sites on the catalyst surface, maintaining its activity throughout the reaction.

The presence of oxidized Fe under reaction conditions is crucial for favoring oxidative C-H bond cleavage in the CO2-ODHP pathway [10.1002/aic.16670]. The extent of Fe oxidation plays a significant role in catalyst performance, with higher oxidation of Fe correlating with better activity and stability in CO2-ODHP.

Bimetallic compositions, such as Fe-Ni and Fe-Pt, have also been studied for CO2-ODHP. These catalysts show different selectivities towards olefins, which can be tuned by adjusting the bimetallic composition of the active sites [10.1039/D0CY00586J]. For example, Fe-Ni surfaces favor C-H bond scissoring, while Pt-rich surfaces favor C-C cleavage.

In conclusion, the superior performance of Fe2O3/AC in CO2-ODHP can be attributed to its improved reducibility and the synergistic effect between Fe oxide and the AC support. The redox cycle of Fe oxide plays a crucial role in the reaction mechanism, highlighting the importance of maintaining the appropriate oxidation state of Fe throughout the catalytic process.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The research on Fe oxide-based catalysts for CO2-ODHP has provided valuable insights into the factors influencing catalyst performance. However, there are still areas that warrant further investigation. Future research could focus on:

1. Optimizing the Fe2O3/AC catalyst by fine-tuning the Fe loading, particle size, and AC properties to further enhance its performance and stability.

2. Exploring novel support materials or combinations that can further improve the reducibility and stability of Fe oxide catalysts.

3. Investigating the potential of hierarchical or core-shell structures to combine the benefits of different support materials (e.g., CeO2 for CO2 activation and AC for improved reducibility).

4. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation state during the CO2-ODHP reaction.

5. Exploring the potential of Fe-based catalysts for other CO2-assisted reactions, leveraging the knowledge gained from CO2-ODHP studies.

6. Investigating the scalability and long-term stability of Fe2O3/AC catalysts for potential industrial applications in propylene production.

These research directions could lead to the development of more efficient and stable catalysts for CO2-ODHP, contributing to more sustainable propylene production processes.

Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2辅助丙烷氧化脱氢（CO2-ODHP）反应中表现出良好的性能。这些催化剂的催化活性、选择性和稳定性受到几个关键因素的影响，包括催化剂的可还原性、载体材料和Fe氧化物的氧化还原循环。

Fe2O3/AC（负载在活性炭上的氧化铁）在CO2-ODHP反应中表现出优于其他Fe氧化物基催化剂的性能。该催化剂在C3H8转化率和C3H6产率方面都优于其他催化剂，同时保持与纯Fe2O3相当的C3H6选择性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。Fe2O3/AC的增强性能可归因于其改善的可还原性，这对CO2-ODHP反应的催化活性至关重要。

载体材料在决定催化剂性能方面起着重要作用。活性炭（AC）被发现可以增强Fe2O3的可还原性，使其能够经历从Fe2O3到Fe3O4到FeO最后到Fe的完整还原过程[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。这种改善的可还原性是Fe2O3/AC相比于其他测试催化剂具有高催化性能的关键。相比之下，其他载体如γ-Al2O3和MgO可能会由于Fe氧化物与载体材料之间的强相互作用而阻碍Fe氧化物的可还原性。

CeO2也被确定为Fe基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的一种有前景的载体。其通过直接C=O键断裂激活CO2的能力增强了整体催化性能[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。基于氧化铈的催化剂的氧移动性和氧储存容量（OSC）有助于形成稳定的表面活性位点，这对于长期维持催化剂性能至关重要。

Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的反应机理涉及氧化还原循环。该过程主要包括两个步骤：（1）使用来自Fe2O3或Fe3O4的晶格氧将C3H8氧化为C3H6，以及（2）由CO2（作为软氧化剂）重新氧化还原的Fe氧化物[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_7]。这种氧化还原循环允许催化剂表面活性位点的持续再生，维持其在整个反应过程中的活性。

在反应条件下氧化Fe的存在对于促进CO2-ODHP途径中的氧化C-H键断裂至关重要[10.1002/aic.16670]。Fe氧化程度在催化剂性能中起着重要作用，Fe的较高氧化程度与CO2-ODHP中更好的活性和稳定性相关。

双金属组成，如Fe-Ni和Fe-Pt，也被研究用于CO2-ODHP反应。这些催化剂对烯烃表现出不同的选择性，可以通过调整活性位点的双金属组成来调节[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。例如，Fe-Ni表面有利于C-H键断裂，而富Pt表面有利于C-C键断裂。

总之，Fe2O3/AC在CO2-ODHP反应中的优异性能可归因于其改善的可还原性以及Fe氧化物与AC载体之间的协同效应。Fe氧化物的氧化还原循环在反应机理中起着关键作用，突出了在整个催化过程中保持适当的Fe氧化状态的重要性。

综合理解与未来展望：对Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的研究提供了有关影响催化剂性能因素的宝贵见解。然而，仍有一些领域值得进一步研究。未来的研究可以集中在：

1. 通过微调Fe负载量、粒子尺寸和AC性质来优化Fe2O3/AC催化剂，以进一步提高其性能和稳定性。

2. 探索新型载体材料或组合，以进一步改善Fe氧化物催化剂的可还原性和稳定性。

3. 研究分层或核壳结构的潜力，以结合不同载体材料的优势（例如，CeO2用于CO2活化，AC用于改善可还原性）。

4. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解CO2-ODHP反应过程中催化剂结构和氧化状态的动态变化。

5. 探索Fe基催化剂在其他CO2辅助反应中的潜力，利用从CO2-ODHP研究中获得的知识。

6. 研究Fe2O3/AC催化剂在丙烯生产潜在工业应用中的可扩展性和长期稳定性。

这些研究方向可能导致开发出更高效、更稳定的CO2-ODHP催化剂，为更可持续的丙烯生产工艺做出贡献。
