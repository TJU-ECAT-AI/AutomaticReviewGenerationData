3. Catalyst preparation and characterization: Various methods such as impregnation, controlled surface reactions, and microwave-assisted polyol reduction are used to prepare bimetallic catalysts. 4. Reactor studies: Both batch and flow reactor evaluations are performed to assess the performance of supported catalysts under realistic conditions. 5. Mechanistic investigations: Researchers study adsorption modes, reaction intermediates, and the roles of different metal components in the catalytic process to optimize catalyst design [10.1016/j.jcat.2009.05.013].

6. Support effect studies: The influence of different oxide supports (e.g., SiO2, γ-Al2O3, TiO2) on the catalytic properties of bimetallic catalysts is investigated to optimize catalyst performance [10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.023].

7. Optimization of metal ratios and reaction conditions: Studies are conducted to determine the optimal composition of bimetallic catalysts and reaction parameters for maximum selectivity and efficiency [10.1007/s10562-010-0533-7].
