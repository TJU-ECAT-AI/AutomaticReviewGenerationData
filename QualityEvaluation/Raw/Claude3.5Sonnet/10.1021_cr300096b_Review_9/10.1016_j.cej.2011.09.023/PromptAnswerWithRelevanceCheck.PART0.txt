These catalysts offer enhanced hydrogenation activities compared to their monometallic counterparts.

The use of bimetallic catalysts, particularly Pt-Ni systems, allows for improved selectivity and efficiency in the hydrogenation process. The presence of platinum promotes the reduction of nickel oxides through hydrogen spillover, leading to increased catalytic activity. Methodologies employed to study, optimize, and enhance the selectivity and efficiency of this process include:

2. Characterization methods like X-ray diffraction (XRD) and temperature-programmed reduction (TPR)
3. Flow reactor studies to evaluate catalyst performance
4. Optimization of metal loading ratios (e.g., Pt content in Pt-Ni catalysts)
5. Investigation of reaction conditions such as temperature and pressure

These studies span both theoretical and experimental domains, combining catalyst characterization techniques with performance evaluations to develop more efficient and selective catalytic systems for industrial applications.
