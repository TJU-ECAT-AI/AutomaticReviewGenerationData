The primary challenges in elucidating Ga speciation in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts under propane dehydrogenation (PDH) conditions include:

1. The active gallium species are highly dispersed and only present under reaction conditions, making them difficult to detect and characterize.

2. The nature of the active species remains unclear due to their transient nature during catalysis.

In situ and operando characterization techniques, particularly X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS), contribute to overcoming these challenges by:

1. Allowing detection of dispersed and isolated species under reaction conditions.

2. Providing information on the local structure and oxidation state of gallium species during catalysis.

3. Enabling the correlation between structural changes and the degree of reduction of gallium species.

4. Facilitating the study of structure-activity relationships by allowing simultaneous measurement of catalyst structure and catalytic performance.

These advanced characterization techniques have significantly advanced our understanding of the evolution of gallium species during propane dehydrogenation, revealing the formation of isolated Ga+ species as the likely active sites for the reaction.
