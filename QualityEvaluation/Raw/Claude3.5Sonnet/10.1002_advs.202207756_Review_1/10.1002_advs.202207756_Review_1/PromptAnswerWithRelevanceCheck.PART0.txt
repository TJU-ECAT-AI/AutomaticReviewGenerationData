1. The reducing environment created by hydrogen production during PDH.
2. The high temperatures (typically above 500°C) at which the reaction occurs.
3. The short-lived nature of reaction intermediates, making them difficult to detect experimentally.

In situ and operando characterization techniques contribute to overcoming these challenges and advancing our understanding of structure-activity relationships in these catalytic systems by:

1. Allowing the study of Ga speciation under realistic PDH conditions, including high temperatures and reducing environments.
2. Providing real-time information about the catalyst structure and behavior during the reaction.
3. Enabling the detection of short-lived intermediates and structural changes.

Specifically, X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS) and Fourier Transform infrared spectroscopy (FTIR) are two powerful in situ/operando techniques that have contributed significantly to this field. XAS is well-suited for determining Ga speciation under PDH conditions, while FTIR's chemical specificity makes it ideal for identifying reaction intermediates. These techniques have helped researchers gain crucial insights into the structure and behavior of Ga species in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts during PDH, advancing our understanding of structure-activity relationships in these systems.
