The primary challenges in elucidating Ga speciation in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts under propane dehydrogenation (PDH) conditions include:

1. The high mobility and instability of gallium species during reduction and reaction conditions.

2. The complexity of different gallium species that can form, including Ga+, Ga2O, (Ga(OH)2)+, (GaO)+, and Ga2O3.

3. The difficulty in directly observing and distinguishing between these various gallium species under reaction conditions.

In situ and operando characterization techniques contribute to overcoming these challenges by:

1. Allowing observation of catalyst changes in real-time under reaction conditions.

2. Providing spectroscopic evidence for different gallium species and their transformations.

3. Enabling the study of structure-activity relationships by correlating spectroscopic data with catalytic performance.

Specifically, this study used in situ DRIFT spectroscopy with CO as a probe molecule to investigate reduced Ga species. Other studies have employed in situ X-ray absorption spectroscopy to examine the oxidation state and coordination of Ga during propane aromatization. These techniques help elucidate the dynamic nature of Ga species under reaction conditions, contributing to a better understanding of the catalytically active sites and reaction mechanisms in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH reactions.
