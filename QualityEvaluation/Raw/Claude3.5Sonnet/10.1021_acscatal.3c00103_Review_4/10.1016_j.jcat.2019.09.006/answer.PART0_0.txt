The physicochemical properties of catalysts significantly influence their performance in dehydrogenation reactions. The literature discusses Zn-modified zeolites, which exhibit high activity for alkane aromatization. Key properties affecting catalyst performance include:

1. Chemical composition: The presence of Zn species (Zn2+ cations and ZnO particles) in zeolites enhances catalytic activity and selectivity compared to pure acid-form zeolites.

2. Acid strength: Brønsted acid sites (BAS) in zeolites work synergistically with Zn species to activate CH bonds in propane.

3. Metal dispersion: Different Zn species (isolated Zn2+ cations and ZnO particles of various dimensions) are present in the zeolites, affecting their catalytic properties.

4. Preparation method: Ion exchange or impregnation of ZSM-5 zeolite with zinc salts leads to improved catalyst performance.

Support modification strategies to optimize these properties include:

1. Controlled metal loading: Adjusting the amount and type of Zn species introduced into the zeolite.

2. Tailored preparation techniques: Using specific methods like ion exchange or impregnation to achieve desired Zn species distribution.

3. Post-synthesis treatments: Modifying the zeolite structure to optimize the synergy between Zn species and BAS.

4. Balancing metal and acid sites: Optimizing the ratio of Zn species to BAS for enhanced catalytic efficiency and selectivity.

These strategies can lead to dramatic acceleration of reactions, such as H/D exchange of propane with BAS, demonstrating the potential for enhanced catalytic efficiency and selectivity in dehydrogenation reactions.
