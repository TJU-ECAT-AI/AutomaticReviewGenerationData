The physicochemical properties of catalysts, including both chemical qualities and morphological characteristics, significantly influence their performance in dehydrogenation reactions. 

Chemical qualities like acid strength and stability are crucial for catalytic activity. However, the literature does not specifically discuss these in relation to dehydrogenation reactions.

Morphological characteristics play a major role in catalyst performance. The small channels and cavities of microporous zeolites impose diffusion constraints for large organic molecules, limiting their effectiveness in fine chemical and some petrochemical applications. 

To optimize catalytic efficiency and selectivity, support modification strategies focus on creating mesoporous zeolites. These materials have enhanced textural properties compared to conventional micron-sized zeolites. The small particle size and mesoporosity decrease diffusion path length and provide more accessible active sites for large molecules.

Key strategies to optimize catalyst properties include:

1. Increasing surface area
2. Creating intercrystalline mesopores
3. Reducing particle size
4. Improving dispersion of metal active centers in the porous network

These modifications enhance the diffusion of reactants and products, facilitate electron transport in electrochemical processes, and reduce electron transfer resistance. While not specifically mentioned for dehydrogenation reactions, these improvements in catalyst structure and properties are likely to benefit such reactions, especially those involving larger molecules.

形态特征在催化剂性能中起着重要作用。微孔沸石的小通道和空腔对大型有机分子施加扩散限制，限制了它们在精细化工和某些石油化工应用中的效果。

为了优化催化效率和选择性，支撑体修饰策略主要集中在创造介孔沸石上。这些材料与传统的微米级沸石相比具有增强的纹理特性。较小的粒径和介孔结构缩短了扩散路径长度，为大分子提供了更易接近的活性位点。

优化催化剂性能的关键策略包括：

1. 增加表面积
2. 创造晶间介孔
3. 减小粒径
4. 改善金属活性中心在多孔网络中的分散性
