<English>
The physicochemical properties of catalysts, including chemical qualities (such as acid strength and stability) and morphological characteristics, significantly influence their performance in dehydrogenation reactions. Key factors and support modification strategies to optimize these properties for enhanced catalytic efficiency and selectivity include:

1. Acidity and Basicity:
- Proper acidity is crucial for C-H activation and propylene desorption, while excessive acidity can lead to undesired side reactions [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_4].
- A balance between acid and base sites is important to mediate alkane activation and alkene desorption [10.1039/D1GC03700E].
- Support modification strategies:
  a) Introducing alkali or alkaline-earth metals (e.g., Li, Na, K, Ca, Mg) to balance strong acidity and suppress undesired reactions [10.1039/D0CS00814A].
  b) Controlled dealumination to reduce Brønsted acid sites in zeolites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_4].
  c) Alkaline treatment of zeolites to modify both Lewis and Brønsted acid sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.07.002].

2. Morphological Characteristics:
- Pore size, structure, and connectivity significantly affect mass transport and accessibility of active sites [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016].
- Hierarchical structures with both micro and mesoporosity enhance diffusion and catalytic performance [10.1016/j.micromeso.2017.09.030].
- Support modification strategies:
  a) Creating hierarchical zeolites with secondary meso- or macroporosity [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016].
  b) Synthesizing nanosized supports or specific morphologies (e.g., nanosheets, finned structures) to shorten diffusion paths [10.1038/s41563-020-0753-1].
  c) Controlled desilication to create mesopores in zeolites [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020].

3. Metal-Support Interactions:
- Strong metal-support interactions enhance catalyst stability and performance [10.1039/D0CS00814A].
- The nature of the support affects the dispersion and electronic properties of active metal species [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016].
- Support modification strategies:
  a) Incorporating promoters (e.g., Sn, Ga) to enhance metal dispersion and modify surface properties [10.1039/C5SC01227A].
  b) Using mixed oxide supports (e.g., MgAl2O4) to improve selectivity and stability [10.1021/cr5002436].
  c) Tailoring the preparation methods to optimize metal-support interactions [10.1016/j.cej.2020.126656].

4. Thermal and Hydrothermal Stability:
- Catalysts must withstand harsh conditions during reactions and regeneration [10.1021/cr5002436].
- Support modification strategies:
  a) Adding stabilizing elements (e.g., La2O3) to inhibit undesirable phase transformations [10.1039/D0CS00814A].
  b) Developing thermally stable spinel phases through doping (e.g., Zn, Mg) [10.1021/cr5002436].

5. Surface Area and Porosity:
- High surface area and uniform pore size distribution are preferred for optimal dispersion of active species [10.1039/D0CS00814A].
- Support modification strategies:
  a) Synthesizing mesoporous materials with high surface areas [10.1016/j.cattod.2017.08.017].
  b) Creating hierarchical structures to combine high surface area with improved mass transport [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016].

By carefully implementing these support modification strategies, researchers can optimize the physicochemical properties of catalysts to achieve enhanced efficiency, selectivity, and stability in dehydrogenation reactions.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The optimization of catalyst physicochemical properties for dehydrogenation reactions involves a complex interplay of various factors. Future research directions may focus on:

1. Advanced characterization techniques: Developing in-situ and operando methods to better understand the dynamic changes in catalyst properties during reactions.

2. Computational modeling: Utilizing machine learning and AI to predict optimal catalyst compositions and structures based on desired reaction outcomes.

3. Multifunctional catalysts: Designing catalysts that combine multiple functionalities (e.g., dehydrogenation and hydrogen storage) to improve overall process efficiency.

4. Sustainable catalyst design: Exploring eco-friendly synthesis methods and utilizing abundant, non-precious metals to create more sustainable catalytic systems.

5. Tailored hierarchical structures: Developing precise control over pore size distribution and connectivity across multiple length scales to optimize mass transport and active site accessibility.

6. Smart catalysts: Creating responsive catalytic systems that can adapt their properties based on reaction conditions to maintain optimal performance.

These future directions aim to push the boundaries of catalyst design, potentially leading to breakthroughs in dehydrogenation technology and other related fields in heterogeneous catalysis.

<Chinese>
催化剂的物理化学性质，包括化学性质（如酸强度和稳定性）和形态特征，显著影响其在脱氢反应中的性能。优化这些性质以提高催化效率和选择性的关键因素和支持修饰策略包括：

1. 酸碱性：
- 适当的酸度对C-H键活化和丙烯脱附至关重要，而过强的酸度可能导致不良副反应[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_4]。
- 酸碱位点之间的平衡对调节烷烃活化和烯烃脱附很重要[10.1039/D1GC03700E]。
- 支持修饰策略：
  a) 引入碱金属或碱土金属（如Li、Na、K、Ca、Mg）以平衡强酸性并抑制不良反应[10.1039/D0CS00814A]。
  b) 控制脱铝以减少沸石中的布朗斯特酸位[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_4]。
  c) 对沸石进行碱处理以修饰路易斯酸和布朗斯特酸位[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.07.002]。

2. 形态特征：
- 孔径、结构和连通性显著影响质量传输和活性位点的可及性[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]。
- 具有微孔和介孔的分级结构增强扩散和催化性能[10.1016/j.micromeso.2017.09.030]。
- 支持修饰策略：
  a) 创建具有次级介孔或大孔的分级沸石[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]。
  b) 合成纳米尺寸支持物或特定形态（如纳米片、鳍状结构）以缩短扩散路径[10.1038/s41563-020-0753-1]。
  c) 控制脱硅以在沸石中创建介孔[10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]。

3. 金属-载体相互作用：
- 强金属-载体相互作用增强催化剂稳定性和性能[10.1039/D0CS00814A]。
- 载体的性质影响活性金属物种的分散和电子性质[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。
- 支持修饰策略：
  a) 引入助剂（如Sn、Ga）以增强金属分散和修饰表面性质[10.1039/C5SC01227A]。
  b) 使用混合氧化物载体（如MgAl2O4）以提高选择性和稳定性[10.1021/cr5002436]。
  c) 调整制备方法以优化金属-载体相互作用[10.1016/j.cej.2020.126656]。

4. 热稳定性和水热稳定性：
- 催化剂必须能够承受反应和再生过程中的苛刻条件[10.1021/cr5002436]。
- 支持修饰策略：
  a) 添加稳定元素（如La2O3）以抑制不良相变[10.1039/D0CS00814A]。
  b) 通过掺杂（如Zn、Mg）开发热稳定的尖晶石相[10.1021/cr5002436]。

5. 比表面积和孔隙度：
- 高比表面积和均匀的孔径分布有利于活性物种的最佳分散[10.1039/D0CS00814A]。
- 支持修饰策略：
  a) 合成具有高比表面积的介孔材料[10.1016/j.cattod.2017.08.017]。
  b) 创建分级结构，结合高比表面积和改善的质量传输[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]。

通过仔细实施这些支持修饰策略，研究人员可以优化催化剂的物理化学性质，以在脱氢反应中实现提高的效率、选择性和稳定性。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：优化催化剂用于脱氢反应的物理化学性质涉及多种因素的复杂相互作用。未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 先进表征技术：开发原位和操作中方法，以更好地理解反应过程中催化剂性质的动态变化。

2. 计算机模拟：利用机器学习和人工智能预测基于所需反应结果的最佳催化剂组成和结构。

3. 多功能催化剂：设计兼具多种功能（如脱氢和储氢）的催化剂，以提高整体过程效率。

4. 可持续催化剂设计：探索环保合成方法并利用丰富的非贵金属创建更可持续的催化系统。

5. 定制分级结构：在多个尺度上精确控制孔径分布和连通性，以优化质量传输和活性位点可及性。

6. 智能催化剂：创建能够根据反应条件调整其性质的响应性催化系统，以保持最佳性能。

这些未来方向旨在推动催化剂设计的边界，可能导致脱氢技术和其他相关领域的多相催化突破。
