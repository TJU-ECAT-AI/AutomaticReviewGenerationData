<English>
The physicochemical properties of catalysts, including chemical qualities (such as acid strength and stability) and morphological characteristics, significantly influence their performance in dehydrogenation reactions. Key factors and support modification strategies to optimize these properties for enhanced catalytic efficiency and selectivity include:

1. Acidity and Basicity:
   - Proper acidity is crucial for C-H activation and propylene desorption, while excessive acidity can lead to undesired side reactions [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_4].
   - A balance between acid and base sites is important to mediate alkane activation and alkene desorption [10.1039/D1GC03700E].
   - Support modification strategies:
     a) Introducing alkali or alkaline-earth metals (e.g., Li, Na, K, Ca, Mg) to balance strong acidity and suppress coke formation [10.1021/cr5002436].
     b) Controlled dealumination to reduce Brønsted acid sites in zeolites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_4].
     c) Ion-exchange methods to modify acidity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_4].

2. Metal-Support Interactions:
   - Strong metal-support interactions enhance catalyst stability and performance [10.1039/D0CS00814A].
   - Support modification strategies:
     a) Incorporating other metal oxides (e.g., TiO2, CeO2) to enhance metal-support interactions [10.1039/D0CS00814A].
     b) Using mixed oxide supports (e.g., MgAl2O4) to improve selectivity [10.1021/cr5002436].
     c) Optimizing preparation methods to achieve high dopant dispersion [10.1021/cr5002436].

3. Morphology and Porosity:
   - Hierarchical structures with both micro and meso-level porosity enhance mass transport and accessibility of active sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_4].
   - Support modification strategies:
     a) Creating intercrystalline mesopores in zeolites to enhance diffusion [10.1016/j.cattod.2017.08.017].
     b) Synthesizing supports with specific morphologies (e.g., needle-shaped, sheet-shaped structures) [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_4].
     c) Developing nanosized supports to shorten diffusion paths [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_4].

4. Metal Dispersion and Particle Size:
   - Better dispersion of active metal or metal oxide species improves catalytic efficiency [10.1039/D0CS00814A].
   - Support modification strategies:
     a) Optimizing metal loading and impregnation techniques [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016].
     b) Using confinement strategies to prevent sintering of metal clusters [10.1039/D0CS00814A].
     c) Employing promoters to enhance dispersion and modify surface properties [10.1039/D0CS00814A].

5. Thermal and Hydrothermal Stability:
   - Supports must withstand harsh conditions during reaction and regeneration [10.1021/cr5002436].
   - Support modification strategies:
     a) Adding stabilizing elements (e.g., La2O3) to inhibit undesirable phase transformations [10.1039/D0CS00814A].
     b) Doping with Zn or Mg to create more thermally stable spinel phases [10.1021/cr5002436].

6. Electronic Properties:
   - The d-band center of metal catalysts affects dehydrogenation activity [10.1039/C5SC01227A].
   - Support modification strategies:
     a) Alloying active metals (e.g., Pt with Cu or Sn) to modify electronic properties [10.1039/C5SC01227A].
     b) Using electron-donating agents (e.g., Ca2+) to modify the electronic state of active metals [10.1002/anie.202107210].

By carefully implementing these support modification strategies, researchers can tailor the physicochemical properties of catalysts to achieve optimal performance in dehydrogenation reactions, balancing activity, selectivity, and stability.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of advanced catalysts for dehydrogenation reactions requires a multifaceted approach that integrates various support modification strategies. Future research directions may focus on:

1. Rational design of multi-component catalysts: Combining different modification strategies to create synergistic effects between various components, potentially leading to unprecedented catalytic performance.

2. In-situ characterization techniques: Developing advanced methods to study catalyst behavior under reaction conditions, providing deeper insights into the dynamic interplay between physicochemical properties and catalytic performance.

3. Computational modeling and machine learning: Utilizing advanced computational tools to predict optimal catalyst compositions and structures, accelerating the discovery of high-performance catalysts.

4. Sustainable catalyst design: Exploring eco-friendly synthesis methods and abundant, low-cost materials to develop more sustainable catalysts for large-scale industrial applications.

5. Tailored catalysts for specific feedstocks: Designing catalysts optimized for emerging feedstocks, such as bio-derived alkanes or waste plastics, to address the growing need for sustainable chemical production.

These future directions aim to push the boundaries of catalyst design, potentially leading to breakthroughs in dehydrogenation technology and contributing to more efficient and sustainable chemical processes.

催化剂的物理化学性质，包括化学质量（如酸强度和稳定性）和形态特征，显著影响其在脱氢反应中的性能。优化这些性质以提高催化效率和选择性的关键因素和支持修饰策略包括：

1. 酸碱性：
   - 适当的酸性对C-H键活化和丙烯脱附至关重要，而过强的酸性可能导致不良副反应 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_4]。
   - 酸碱位点之间的平衡对烷烃活化和烯烃脱附至关重要 [10.1039/D1GC03700E]。
   - 支持修饰策略：
     a) 引入碱金属或碱土金属（如Li、Na、K、Ca、Mg）以平衡强酸性并抑制积碳形成 [10.1021/cr5002436]。
     b) 控制脱铝以减少沸石中的Brønsted酸位 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_4]。
     c) 离子交换方法修改酸性 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_4]。

2. 金属-载体相互作用：
   - 强金属-载体相互作用增强催化剂稳定性和性能 [10.1039/D0CS00814A]。
   - 支持修饰策略：
     a) 引入其他金属氧化物（如TiO2、CeO2）以增强金属-载体相互作用 [10.1039/D0CS00814A]。
     b) 使用混合氧化物载体（如MgAl2O4）以提高选择性 [10.1021/cr5002436]。
     c) 优化制备方法以实现高掺杂剂分散 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

3. 形态和孔隙度：
   - 具有微孔和介孔级别孔隙度的分层结构增强质量传输和活性位点的可及性 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_4]。
   - 支持修饰策略：
     a) 在沸石中创建晶间介孔以增强扩散 [10.1016/j.cattod.2017.08.017]。
     b) 合成具有特定形态的载体（如针状、片状结构）[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_4]。
     c) 开发纳米尺寸载体以缩短扩散路径 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_4]。

4. 金属分散和粒子尺寸：
   - 活性金属或金属氧化物物种的更好分散提高催化效率 [10.1039/D0CS00814A]。
   - 支持修饰策略：
     a) 优化金属负载和浸渍技术 [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。
     b) 使用限域策略防止金属簇烧结 [10.1039/D0CS00814A]。
     c) 使用助剂增强分散并修改表面性质 [10.1039/D0CS00814A]。

5. 热稳定性和水热稳定性：
   - 载体必须在反应和再生过程中承受苛刻条件 [10.1021/cr5002436]。
   - 支持修饰策略：
     a) 添加稳定元素（如La2O3）以抑制不良相变 [10.1039/D0CS00814A]。
     b) 用Zn或Mg掺杂以创建更热稳定的尖晶石相 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

6. 电子性质：
   - 金属催化剂的d带中心影响脱氢活性 [10.1039/C5SC01227A]。
   - 支持修饰策略：
     a) 将活性金属（如Pt与Cu或Sn）合金化以修改电子性质 [10.1039/C5SC01227A]。
     b) 使用电子供体（如Ca2+）修改活性金属的电子状态 [10.1002/anie.202107210]。

通过仔细实施这些支持修饰策略，研究人员可以调整催化剂的物理化学性质，以在脱氢反应中获得最佳性能，平衡活性、选择性和稳定性。

综合理解和前景展望：开发用于脱氢反应的先进催化剂需要一种结合各种支持修饰策略的多方面方法。未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 多组分催化剂的理性设计：结合不同的修饰策略，在各种组分之间创造协同效应，可能导致前所未有的催化性能。

2. 原位表征技术：开发先进方法在反应条件下研究催化剂行为，深入了解物理化学性质与催化性能之间的动态相互作用。

3. 计算模型和机器学习：利用先进的计算工具预测最佳催化剂成分和结构，加速高性能催化剂的发现。

4. 可持续催化剂设计：探索环保合成方法和丰富、低成本材料，为大规模工业应用开发更可持续的催化剂。

5. 针对特定原料的定制催化剂：设计针对新兴原料（如生物衍生烷烃或废塑料）优化的催化剂，以满足可持续化学品生产的日益增长需求。

这些未来方向旨在推动催化剂设计的边界，可能导致脱氢技术的突破，并为更高效、更可持续的化学过程做出贡献。
