<EnglishVersionAnswer>

The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism, proposed in 1934, is the most widely accepted model for catalytic dehydrogenation reactions. This mechanism follows Langmuir-Hinshelwood kinetics and assumes that all catalyst surface sites are identical. It consists of four main steps:

1. Dissociative adsorption of the paraffin
2. C-H cleavage of a second hydrogen atom
3. Formation of a hydrogen molecule
4. Desorption of both hydrogen and olefin

Despite its widespread use, there is ongoing debate about which step is rate-limiting. Both the C-H cleavage steps and the dissociative adsorption of the paraffin have been suggested as potential rate-limiting steps by various researchers.

The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism remains a topic of scientific interest and controversy for several reasons:

1. Complexity of the reaction: Dehydrogenation is catalyzed by a wide range of materials, from noble metals to transition metal oxides, making it challenging to develop a universally applicable mechanism.

2. Experimental limitations: The high temperatures and pressures involved in dehydrogenation reactions make it difficult to study the mechanism directly, leading to reliance on indirect methods and theoretical calculations.

3. Advances in analytical techniques: New spectroscopic and computational methods continue to provide fresh insights into the reaction mechanism, sometimes challenging existing assumptions.

4. Industrial relevance: The importance of dehydrogenation reactions in industrial processes drives ongoing research to optimize catalysts and reaction conditions, necessitating a deeper understanding of the mechanism.

5. Interdisciplinary nature: The study of catalytic mechanisms involves chemistry, physics, and materials science, leading to diverse approaches and interpretations.

The continued investigation of the Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism reflects the scientific community's commitment to refining our understanding of fundamental catalytic processes, even after nearly a century of study.

1. 烷烃的解离吸附
2. 第二个氢原子的C-H键断裂
3. 氢分子的形成
4. 氢和烯烃的脱附

尽管被广泛使用，但关于哪一步是速率限制步骤仍存在争议。不同研究者提出C-H键断裂步骤和烷烃的解离吸附都可能是潜在的速率限制步骤。

Horiuti-Polanyi机理之所以仍然是科学兴趣和争议的话题，原因如下：

1. 反应的复杂性：脱氢反应可以被从贵金属到过渡金属氧化物等广泛的材料催化，这使得开发一个普遍适用的机理变得具有挑战性。

2. 实验限制：脱氢反应涉及的高温高压条件使直接研究机理变得困难，导致需要依赖间接方法和理论计算。

3. 分析技术的进步：新的光谱和计算方法不断为反应机理提供新的见解，有时会挑战现有的假设。

5. 跨学科性质：催化机理的研究涉及化学、物理和材料科学，导致了多样化的研究方法和解释。

对Horiuti-Polanyi机理的持续研究反映了科学界致力于完善我们对基本催化过程理解的承诺，即使在近一个世纪的研究之后仍然如此。
