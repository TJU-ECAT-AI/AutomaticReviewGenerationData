This mechanism, typically consisting of four main steps, continues to be a subject of intense scientific interest and debate. The four steps are generally described as follows:

1. Adsorption: The reactant molecule (usually an alkane) adsorbs onto the catalyst surface.
2. First C-H bond cleavage: One C-H bond is broken, forming an alkyl intermediate and a surface-bound hydrogen atom.
3. Second C-H bond cleavage: Another C-H bond is broken, forming the alkene product and a second surface-bound hydrogen atom.
4. Desorption: The alkene product and hydrogen molecules desorb from the catalyst surface.

[10.1006/jcat.1998.2312]

4. Desorption of the products
