      <English>
          The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for catalytic dehydrogenation reactions consists of four main steps:

1. Adsorption: The reactant molecule adsorbs onto the catalyst surface.

2. First hydrogen abstraction: One hydrogen atom is removed from the adsorbed molecule, forming an adsorbed alkyl intermediate or half-hydrogenated intermediate species on the surface.

3. Second hydrogen abstraction: A second hydrogen atom is removed, forming the dehydrogenated product still adsorbed to the surface.

4. Desorption: The product molecule desorbs from the catalyst surface.

There is ongoing debate about which step is rate-limiting. Some researchers argue it is the first hydrogen abstraction, while others suggest it could be the second abstraction or even the desorption step in certain cases. The rate-limiting step may depend on factors such as the specific catalyst, reactant, and reaction conditions.

This mechanism remains a topic of interest and controversy despite nearly 100 years of study for several reasons:

1. Complexity of real catalytic systems compared to idealized models.
2. Difficulty in directly observing reaction intermediates and transition states on catalyst surfaces.
3. Variations in behavior across different catalyst materials and reaction conditions.
4. Challenges in isolating individual mechanistic steps experimentally.
5. Advances in surface science, spectroscopic techniques, and computational methods providing new insights.
6. Importance of understanding reaction mechanisms for catalyst design and optimization in industrial processes.
7. Ongoing debates over the role of adsorbate-adsorbate interactions and surface restructuring.

Continued research aims to refine our understanding of this fundamental mechanism and resolve ongoing controversies through advanced experimental and theoretical approaches. The persistent interest and debate highlight the complexity of heterogeneous catalysis and the challenges in fully elucidating reaction mechanisms at the molecular level.

          Horiuti-Polanyi催化脱氢反应机理包括四个主要步骤：

1. 吸附：反应物分子吸附在催化剂表面。

2. 第一次氢原子抽取：从吸附分子中移除一个氢原子，形成吸附的烷基中间体或表面半氢化中间物种。

3. 第二次氢原子抽取：移除第二个氢原子，形成仍吸附在表面的脱氢产物。

4. 脱附：产物分子从催化剂表面脱附。

关于哪一步是速率限制步骤仍存在争议。一些研究人员认为是第一次氢抽取，而其他人则认为可能是第二次抽取，或在某些情况下甚至是脱附步骤。速率限制步骤可能取决于具体的催化剂、反应物和反应条件等因素。

尽管研究了近100年，这个机理仍然是一个备受关注和争议的话题，原因如下：

1. 实际催化系统比理想模型更复杂。
2. 难以直接观察催化剂表面的反应中间体和过渡态。
3. 不同催化剂材料和反应条件下行为的差异。
4. 实验中分离单个机理步骤的挑战。
5. 表面科学、光谱技术和计算方法的进展提供了新的见解。
6. 理解反应机理对工业过程中的催化剂设计和优化很重要。
7. 关于吸附质-吸附质相互作用和表面重构作用的持续争论。

持续的研究旨在通过先进的实验和理论方法完善我们对这一基本机理的理解，并解决持续存在的争议。持续的兴趣和争论突显了多相催化的复杂性，以及在分子水平上完全阐明反应机理的挑战。
