The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for catalytic dehydrogenation reactions consists of four main steps:

1. Adsorption: The alkane molecule adsorbs onto the catalyst surface.

2. First C-H bond cleavage: One C-H bond is broken, forming an alkyl species and a hydrogen atom on the surface.

3. Second C-H bond cleavage: Another C-H bond is broken, forming an alkene species and a second hydrogen atom on the surface.

4. Desorption: The alkene product and hydrogen molecules desorb from the catalyst surface.

There is ongoing debate about which step is rate-limiting. Some researchers argue that the first C-H bond cleavage is rate-limiting, while others suggest the second C-H bond cleavage or even the desorption step could be rate-determining under certain conditions.

This mechanism remains a topic of scientific interest and controversy despite nearly a century of study for several reasons:

1. Complexity: The mechanism involves multiple elementary steps and can be influenced by various factors such as catalyst composition, reaction conditions, and substrate structure.

2. Difficulty in direct observation: Many of the proposed intermediates are short-lived and challenging to detect experimentally.

3. Catalyst dependence: Different catalysts may exhibit different rate-limiting steps or even slightly modified mechanisms.

4. Relevance to industrial processes: Understanding the mechanism is crucial for designing more efficient catalysts for important industrial dehydrogenation reactions.

5. Advances in analytical techniques: New experimental methods and computational tools continue to provide fresh insights into the mechanism, sometimes challenging previous assumptions.

6. Connection to other reactions: The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism serves as a model for understanding other catalytic processes involving C-H bond activation.

The ongoing research and debate surrounding this mechanism highlight the complexity of heterogeneous catalysis and the importance of continued investigation in this field.

1. 吸附：烷烃分子吸附在催化剂表面上。

2. 第一个C-H键断裂：一个C-H键断裂，在表面形成烷基物种和一个氢原子。

3. 第二个C-H键断裂：另一个C-H键断裂，在表面形成烯烃物种和第二个氢原子。

4. 脱附：烯烃产物和氢分子从催化剂表面脱附。

关于哪一步是速率限制步骤，存在持续的争论。一些研究人员认为第一个C-H键断裂是速率限制步骤，而其他人则认为在某些条件下，第二个C-H键断裂甚至脱附步骤可能是速率决定步骤。

尽管已经研究了近一个世纪，但这种机理仍然是科学兴趣和争议的话题，原因如下：

1. 复杂性：该机理涉及多个基本步骤，可能受到各种因素的影响，如催化剂组成、反应条件和底物结构。

2. 直接观察的困难：许多提出的中间体寿命短暂，实验上难以检测。

3. 催化剂依赖性：不同的催化剂可能表现出不同的速率限制步骤，甚至略有修改的机理。

4. 与工业过程的相关性：理解该机理对于设计更高效的催化剂用于重要的工业脱氢反应至关重要。

5. 分析技术的进步：新的实验方法和计算工具不断为该机理提供新的见解，有时会挑战先前的假设。

6. 与其他反应的联系：Horiuti-Polanyi机理作为理解其他涉及C-H键活化的催化过程的模型。

围绕这一机理的持续研究和争论突显了多相催化的复杂性以及在这一领域继续调查的重要性。
