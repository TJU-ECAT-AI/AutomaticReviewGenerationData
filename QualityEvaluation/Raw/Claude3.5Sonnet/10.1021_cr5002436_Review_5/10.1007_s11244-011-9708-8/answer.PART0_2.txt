The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for catalytic dehydrogenation reactions consists of four main steps:

1. Adsorption: The reactant molecule (e.g. an alkane) adsorbs onto the catalyst surface.

2. First dehydrogenation: One hydrogen atom is removed from the adsorbed molecule, forming an alkyl intermediate.

3. Second dehydrogenation: A second hydrogen atom is removed, forming the alkene product.

4. Desorption: The alkene product desorbs from the catalyst surface.

There has been ongoing debate about which step is rate-limiting. Some studies suggest the first or second dehydrogenation step is rate-limiting, while others argue desorption of the product is the slowest step. The rate-limiting step may depend on reaction conditions and catalyst properties.

This mechanism remains controversial despite decades of study for several reasons:

1. Difficulty in directly observing surface intermediates
2. Complexity of real catalyst surfaces compared to idealized models
3. Variations in behavior for different reactants and catalysts
4. Challenges in accurately measuring kinetics of individual steps
5. Evolving analytical techniques providing new insights

Continued research aims to resolve these debates and develop a more complete understanding of dehydrogenation mechanisms to guide catalyst design. The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism provides a valuable conceptual framework, but refining the details remains an active area of catalysis research.

Horiuti-Polanyi催化脱氢反应机理包括四个主要步骤：

1. 吸附：反应分子（如烷烃）吸附在催化剂表面。

2. 第一次脱氢：从吸附分子中移除一个氢原子，形成烷基中间体。

3. 第二次脱氢：移除第二个氢原子，形成烯烃产物。

4. 脱附：烯烃产物从催化剂表面脱附。

关于哪一步是速率限制步骤，一直存在争议。一些研究表明第一次或第二次脱氢步骤是速率限制步骤，而其他研究则认为产物的脱附是最慢的步骤。速率限制步骤可能取决于反应条件和催化剂性质。

尽管经过数十年的研究，这种机理仍然存在争议，原因如下：

1. 难以直接观察表面中间体
2. 实际催化剂表面比理想模型更复杂
3. 不同反应物和催化剂的行为存在差异
4. 准确测量各个步骤动力学的挑战
5. 不断发展的分析技术提供新的见解

持续的研究旨在解决这些争议，并发展对脱氢机理更完整的理解，以指导催化剂设计。Horiuti-Polanyi机理提供了一个有价值的概念框架，但完善细节仍然是催化研究的一个活跃领域。
