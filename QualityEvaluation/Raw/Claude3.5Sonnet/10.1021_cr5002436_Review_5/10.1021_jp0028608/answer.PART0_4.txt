The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for catalytic hydrogenation reactions, not dehydrogenation as stated in the question, consists of four main steps:

1. Adsorption: The reactant molecule and hydrogen gas adsorb onto the catalyst surface.

2. First hydrogen addition: One hydrogen atom is added to the adsorbed reactant molecule, forming a half-hydrogenated intermediate.

3. Second hydrogen addition: Another hydrogen atom is added, completing the hydrogenation.

4. Desorption: The fully hydrogenated product molecule desorbs from the catalyst surface.

There is ongoing debate about which step is rate-limiting. Some studies suggest the first hydrogen addition is rate-limiting, while others point to the second addition or desorption as potential rate-determining steps. The rate-limiting step can vary depending on reaction conditions and the specific system studied.

This mechanism remains a topic of scientific interest and controversy despite nearly a century of study for several reasons:

1. Complexity: The mechanism involves multiple steps and intermediates, making it challenging to study experimentally.

2. Variety of systems: Different catalysts and reactants can lead to variations in the mechanism and rate-limiting step.

3. Advanced techniques: New experimental and computational methods continue to provide fresh insights into the mechanism.

4. Industrial relevance: Understanding and optimizing this mechanism is crucial for many industrial processes.

5. Theoretical implications: The mechanism has broader implications for understanding surface chemistry and catalysis.

The ongoing research and debates surrounding the Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism highlight the complexity of heterogeneous catalysis and the importance of continued study in this field.

2. 第一次氢原子加成：一个氢原子加到吸附的反应分子上，形成半氢化中间体。

3. 第二次氢原子加成：另一个氢原子加成，完成氢化过程。
