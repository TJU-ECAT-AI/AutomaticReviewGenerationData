The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for catalytic dehydrogenation reactions consists of four main steps:

1. Adsorption: The alkane molecule adsorbs onto the catalyst surface.

2. First C-H bond cleavage: One C-H bond breaks, forming an adsorbed alkyl species and a hydrogen atom on the surface. 

3. Second C-H bond cleavage: Another C-H bond breaks, forming the adsorbed alkene.

4. Desorption: The alkene product desorbs from the catalyst surface.

There is ongoing debate about which step is rate-limiting. Some argue it's the initial C-H bond breaking, while others propose alkene desorption as rate-limiting. The mechanism remains controversial because:

1. It's challenging to directly observe surface intermediates.
2. The rate-limiting step may change with reaction conditions.
3. Different catalysts may favor different steps.
4. Side reactions and coking complicate kinetics.

Despite nearly 100 years of study, the Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism is still actively researched to improve catalyst design, optimize reaction conditions, and develop more efficient dehydrogenation processes for industrial applications.

Horiuti-Polanyi催化脱氢反应机理包括四个主要步骤：

1. 吸附：烷烃分子吸附在催化剂表面。

2. 第一个C-H键断裂：一个C-H键断裂，形成吸附的烷基物种和表面上的氢原子。

3. 第二个C-H键断裂：另一个C-H键断裂，形成吸附的烯烃。

4. 脱附：烯烃产物从催化剂表面脱附。

关于哪个步骤是速率限制步骤，仍存在争议。有人认为是初始C-H键断裂，而其他人则提出烯烃脱附是速率限制步骤。该机理仍然存在争议，原因如下：

1. 直接观察表面中间体具有挑战性。
2. 速率限制步骤可能随反应条件而变化。
3. 不同的催化剂可能favors不同的步骤。
4. 副反应和结焦使动力学复杂化。

尽管已经研究了近100年，Horiuti-Polanyi机理仍然是活跃的研究领域，目的是改进催化剂设计、优化反应条件，并为工业应用开发更高效的脱氢过程。
