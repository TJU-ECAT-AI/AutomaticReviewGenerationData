The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for catalytic dehydrogenation reactions, while not directly discussed in the given literature, consists of four main steps:

1. Adsorption: The alkane molecule adsorbs onto the catalyst surface.

2. First dehydrogenation: One hydrogen atom is removed from the alkane, forming an alkyl intermediate.

3. Second dehydrogenation: A second hydrogen atom is removed, forming the alkene product.

4. Desorption: The alkene product desorbs from the catalyst surface.

There is ongoing debate about which step is rate-limiting. Some researchers argue it's the initial C-H bond breaking (step 2), while others suggest it's the second dehydrogenation step (step 3). The adsorption of the alkane (step 1) or desorption of the alkene (step 4) are also sometimes considered rate-limiting under certain conditions.

This mechanism remains a topic of interest and controversy because:

1. It's fundamental to understanding catalytic processes in petrochemical industries.

2. Identifying the rate-limiting step is crucial for catalyst design and optimization.

3. The mechanism can vary depending on the specific catalyst, reactant, and reaction conditions.

4. Advanced spectroscopic and computational techniques continue to provide new insights, sometimes challenging established views.

5. Understanding the mechanism at an atomic level is essential for developing more efficient and selective catalysts.

Despite nearly a century of study, the complexity of real catalytic systems and the challenges in directly observing surface intermediates under reaction conditions continue to fuel scientific debate and research in this area.

1. 吸附：烷烃分子吸附在催化剂表面。

2. 第一次脱氢：从烷烃中移除一个氢原子，形成烷基中间体。

3. 第二次脱氢：移除第二个氢原子，形成烯烃产物。

4. 脱附：烯烃产物从催化剂表面脱附。

关于哪一步是速率限制步骤，仍存在争议。一些研究者认为是初始C-H键断裂（步骤2），而其他人则认为是第二次脱氢步骤（步骤3）。在某些条件下，烷烃的吸附（步骤1）或烯烃的脱附（步骤4）也被认为可能是速率限制步骤。

1. 它对理解石油化工行业的催化过程至关重要。
