The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for catalytic dehydrogenation reactions consists of four main steps:

1. Adsorption: The alkane molecule adsorbs onto the catalyst surface.

2. First C-H bond cleavage: One C-H bond is broken, forming an alkyl intermediate bound to the catalyst. 

3. Second C-H bond cleavage: Another C-H bond is broken, forming the alkene product.

4. Desorption: The alkene product desorbs from the catalyst surface.

There is ongoing debate about which step is rate-limiting. Some argue it is the initial C-H bond breaking, while others propose it is the second C-H cleavage or even desorption of the product. This remains controversial because:

1. It's difficult to directly observe intermediates on catalyst surfaces.

2. The rate-limiting step may vary depending on reaction conditions and catalyst properties.

3. Computational studies sometimes conflict with experimental results.

4. There may be multiple pathways operating simultaneously.

The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism remains a topic of interest nearly 100 years after its proposal because:

1. It provides a framework for understanding and improving catalysts.

2. New analytical techniques allow for more detailed mechanistic studies.

3. It has implications for other catalytic reactions beyond dehydrogenation.

4. A full understanding could enable more efficient industrial processes.

5. It exemplifies fundamental concepts in heterogeneous catalysis.

Overall, while the general steps are agreed upon, the details of the Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism continue to be refined and debated as our understanding of surface chemistry advances.

1. 吸附：烷烃分子吸附在催化剂表面。

3. 第二个C-H键断裂：另一个C-H键断裂，形成烯烃产物。

4. 脱附：烯烃产物从催化剂表面脱附。

关于哪一步是速率限制步骤，仍存在争议。一些人认为是初始C-H键断裂，而其他人则提出是第二个C-H键断裂甚至是产物的脱附。这仍然存在争议，因为：

1. 很难直接观察催化剂表面的中间体。

2. 速率限制步骤可能随反应条件和催化剂性质而变化。

3. 计算研究有时与实验结果相矛盾。

4. 可能同时存在多个反应路径。

尽管提出已近100年，Horiuti-Polanyi机制仍然是一个研究热点，原因如下：

1. 它为理解和改进催化剂提供了框架。

2. 新的分析技术允许进行更详细的机理研究。

3. 它对脱氢反应以外的其他催化反应也有启示。

4. 全面理解可以实现更高效的工业过程。

5. 它体现了多相催化中的基本概念。

总的来说，虽然一般步骤已达成共识，但随着我们对表面化学理解的深入，Horiuti-Polanyi机制的细节仍在不断完善和争论中。
