The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for catalytic dehydrogenation reactions consists of four main steps:

1. Adsorption: The reactant molecule adsorbs onto the catalyst surface.

2. First hydrogen abstraction: One hydrogen atom is removed from the adsorbed molecule, forming a half-hydrogenated intermediate.

3. Second hydrogen abstraction: The second hydrogen atom is removed, forming the dehydrogenated product.

4. Desorption: The product molecule desorbs from the catalyst surface.

There is ongoing debate about which step is rate-limiting. Some researchers argue it is the first hydrogen abstraction, while others claim it is the second. The rate-limiting step may depend on factors like catalyst composition, reaction conditions, and specific reactants.

This mechanism remains controversial despite nearly 100 years of study for several reasons:

1. Difficulty in directly observing reaction intermediates on catalyst surfaces.

2. Complexity of real catalytic systems compared to idealized models.

3. Advances in surface science and computational methods providing new insights.

4. Importance of understanding reaction mechanisms for catalyst design and optimization.

5. Debates over the role of adsorbate-adsorbate interactions and surface restructuring.

Continued research aims to refine our understanding of this fundamental mechanism and resolve ongoing controversies through advanced experimental and theoretical approaches.

Horiuti-Polanyi催化脱氢反应机理包括四个主要步骤：

1. 吸附：反应分子吸附在催化剂表面。

2. 第一次氢抽取：从吸附分子中移除一个氢原子，形成半氢化中间体。

3. 第二次氢抽取：移除第二个氢原子，形成脱氢产物。

4. 解吸：产物分子从催化剂表面解吸。

关于哪一步是速率限制步骤仍存在争议。一些研究人员认为是第一次氢抽取，而其他人则认为是第二次。速率限制步骤可能取决于催化剂组成、反应条件和具体反应物等因素。

尽管研究了近100年，这个机理仍然存在争议，原因如下：

1. 难以直接观察催化剂表面的反应中间体。

2. 实际催化系统比理想模型更复杂。

3. 表面科学和计算方法的进展提供了新的见解。

4. 理解反应机理对催化剂设计和优化很重要。

5. 关于吸附质-吸附质相互作用和表面重构作用的争论。

持续的研究旨在通过先进的实验和理论方法完善我们对这一基本机理的理解，并解决持续存在的争议。
