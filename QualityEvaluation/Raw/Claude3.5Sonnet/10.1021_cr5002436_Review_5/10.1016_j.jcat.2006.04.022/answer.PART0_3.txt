The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism, proposed in 1934, describes the catalytic hydrogenation and dehydrogenation of unsaturated hydrocarbons. It consists of four main steps:

1. Adsorption: The reactant molecule (e.g., an alkene) adsorbs onto the catalyst surface.

2. First hydrogen addition: A hydrogen atom is added to one carbon of the adsorbed molecule, forming a half-hydrogenated intermediate.

4. Desorption: The product molecule desorbs from the catalyst surface.
