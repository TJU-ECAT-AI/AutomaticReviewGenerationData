      <English>
          The Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for catalytic dehydrogenation reactions, proposed in 1934, describes the catalytic hydrogenation and dehydrogenation of unsaturated hydrocarbons. It consists of four main steps:

1. Adsorption: The reactant molecule (e.g., an alkene) adsorbs onto the catalyst surface.
2. First hydrogen addition: A hydrogen atom is added to one carbon of the adsorbed molecule, forming a half-hydrogenated intermediate.
3. Second hydrogen addition or removal: Either a second hydrogen is added (for hydrogenation) or the first hydrogen is removed (for dehydrogenation).
4. Desorption: The product molecule desorbs from the catalyst surface.

The debate regarding which step is rate-limiting has been ongoing for decades. Some researchers argue that the first hydrogen addition is rate-limiting, while others suggest it's the second hydrogen addition/removal, the adsorption, or the desorption step. The rate-limiting step can vary depending on the specific catalyst, substrate, and reaction conditions.

This mechanism remains a topic of scientific interest and controversy despite nearly a century of study for several reasons:

1. It's fundamental to many important industrial processes.
2. Advanced experimental techniques and computational methods continue to provide new insights.
3. The complexity of surface reactions and the difficulty in directly observing intermediates fuel ongoing research.
4. Understanding the mechanism at a molecular level is crucial for designing more efficient catalysts and optimizing industrial processes.
5. The mechanism's applicability to different catalyst systems and reaction conditions is still being explored.

Ongoing research in this area aims to refine our understanding of the mechanism, which could lead to the development of more efficient catalysts and improved industrial processes.

          Horiuti-Polanyi催化脱氢反应机理于1934年提出，描述了不饱和烃类的催化加氢和脱氢过程。它包括四个主要步骤：

1. 吸附：反应物分子（如烯烃）吸附在催化剂表面。
2. 第一个氢原子加成：一个氢原子被加到吸附分子的一个碳原子上，形成半氢化中间体。
3. 第二个氢原子加成或移除：要么加入第二个氢（加氢反应），要么移除第一个氢（脱氢反应）。
4. 脱附：产物分子从催化剂表面脱附。

关于哪一步是速率限制步骤的争论已持续数十年。一些研究人员认为第一个氢原子的加成是速率限制步骤，而其他人则认为是第二个氢原子的加成/移除、吸附或脱附步骤。速率限制步骤可能因特定的催化剂、底物和反应条件而异。

这个机理之所以仍然是科学界感兴趣和存在争议的话题，尽管已经研究了将近一个世纪，原因如下：

1. 它是许多重要工业过程的基础。
2. 先进的实验技术和计算方法不断提供新的见解。
3. 表面反应的复杂性和直接观察中间体的困难推动着持续的研究。
4. 在分子水平上理解该机理对设计更高效的催化剂和优化工业过程至关重要。
5. 该机理在不同催化剂系统和反应条件下的适用性仍在被探索。

这一领域的持续研究旨在完善我们对机理的理解，这可能导致更高效催化剂的开发和改进的工业过程。
