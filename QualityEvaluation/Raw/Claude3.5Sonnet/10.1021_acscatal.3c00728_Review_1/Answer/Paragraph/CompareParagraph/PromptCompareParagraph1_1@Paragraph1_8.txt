The spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in B-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions. The active sites can be classified into three types: one-dimensional (1-D), two-dimensional (2-D), and three-dimensional (3-D) dispersed active sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728\_1].

1-D Edge Active Sites:
1-D active sites, which are linearly dispersed on the edge of 2-D h-BN sheets, have shown superior performance in ODHP reactions. For example, h-BN with 1-D edge active sites exhibited a propene selectivity of 79% at a propane conversion of 14%, outperforming conventional catalysts like V/SiO2 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728\_1]. The edge-hydroxylated boron nitride (BNOH) efficiently catalyzed ODHP, reaching a propylene selectivity of 80.2% with only 0.5% CO2 at a propane conversion of 20.6% [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040].

Reaction Mechanism:
DFT calculations revealed that the reaction starts with hydrogen abstraction from the second carbon of propane, followed by O-O bond cleavage to create B-O and N-O species [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728\_1]. The edge BOH groups may be dehydrogenated by O2, generating BO and hydroperoxyl (HO2) radicals. The BO radical catalyzes hydrogen abstraction of alkanes to produce alkyl radicals, while the HO2 radical may also be involved in the dehydrogenation in the gas phase [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028].

Site Activity:
The zig-B edge was found to be more active than the armchair or zig-N edge sites, with propane dehydrogenation occurring more easily on oxygen-modified edge sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728\_1]. N-terminated edges are particularly effective for oxygen activation, crucial for oxidation reactions [10.1002/smll.201701857].

2-D and 3-D Active Sites:
While 1-D edge sites show promising results, other spatial distributions also play important roles. B2O3/SBA-15 catalysts demonstrated higher activity due to abundant tricoordinated boroxol and hydroxylated linear boron species on the surface, indicating the importance of well-distributed 2-D active sites [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]. The use of honeycomb cordierite structures with straight channels allows for high gas hourly space velocity (GHSV) and improved mass transfer, suggesting the importance of 3-D structural design in catalyst performance [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040].

Performance Improvement Strategies:
Understanding the importance of edge sites has led to strategies for improving catalyst performance, such as:
a) Preoxygen modification of edge sites to reduce the induction period and improve ODHP performance.
b) Increasing the density of edge sites by reducing the size of BN nanosheets and increasing the edge/surface ratio.
c) Creating 1-D shaped materials to increase the number of active sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728\_1].

Challenges and Future Directions:
