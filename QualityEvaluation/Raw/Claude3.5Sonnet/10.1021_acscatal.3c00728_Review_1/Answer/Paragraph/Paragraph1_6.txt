<English>
The spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in B-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions. The active sites can be classified into three types: one-dimensional (1-D), two-dimensional (2-D), and three-dimensional (3-D) dispersed active sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1].

1-D Active Sites:
1-D active sites, typically found on the edges of 2-D h-BN sheets or in boron nitride nanotubes (BNNTs), have shown superior performance in ODHP reactions. For example, h-BN with 1-D edge active sites exhibited a propene selectivity of 79% at a propane conversion of 14%, outperforming conventional catalysts like V/SiO2 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]. The edge-hydroxylated boron nitride (BNOH) efficiently catalyzed ODHP, reaching a propylene selectivity of 80.2% with minimal CO2 formation (0.5%) at a propane conversion of 20.6% [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040].

The unique properties of 1-D edge active sites contribute to their high performance:
1. Stabilization of propyl radicals: Unlike 0D sites (e.g., V/SiO2) that create highly reactive propyl radicals, 1D edge sites in BN catalysts stabilize these radicals, reducing overoxidation [10.1126/science.aaf7885].
2. Selective oxygen activation: DFT calculations revealed that N-terminated edges have moderate dissociative energy for oxygen atoms, enhancing catalytic activity [10.1002/smll.201701857].
3. Dynamic active site formation: Edge BOH groups can be dehydrogenated by O2, generating BO and HO2 radicals that facilitate hydrogen abstraction from alkanes [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028].

2-D Active Sites:
2-D active sites are typically found on the surface of layered boron-containing materials. For instance, B2O3/SBA-15 catalysts demonstrated higher activity due to abundant tricoordinated boroxol and hydroxylated linear boron species on the surface [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]. Layered borosilicate zeolites with 2-D structures have also shown good performance in ODHP reactions [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021].

3-D Active Sites:
3-D active sites are often found in bulk materials or complex structures. For example, the B2O3@BPO4 catalyst features a unique sandwich-like hollow sphere structure, where 1-D B2O3 nanowires cover both the inside and outside of 3-D BPO4 hollow spheres. This arrangement provides a high surface area and increased accessibility of active sites, contributing to excellent performance in ODHP reactions [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028].

Understanding the unique properties and reaction mechanisms of these various active sites, particularly 1-D edge active sites, contributes to the design of more efficient catalysts for commercial applications in several ways:

1. Optimizing edge site exposure: Maximizing the number of accessible edge sites can enhance catalytic performance [10.1002/cctc.202100476].
2. Tailoring edge functionalization: Optimizing hydroxylation or other edge modifications can improve catalyst activity and selectivity [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62786-4].
3. Controlling spatial distribution: Creating porous structures or using supports like cordierite can optimize the distribution of active sites and enhance mass transfer [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028].
4. Balancing surface and gas-phase reactions: Understanding the interplay between surface-catalyzed and gas-phase radical reactions allows for optimizing both pathways [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031].
5. Tuning thermal properties: Leveraging the superior thermal conductivity of materials like h-BN can help avoid hotspots and improve selectivity [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The study of spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in B-based catalysts for ODHP reactions has revealed the critical role of 1-D edge sites, particularly in h-BN materials. These findings open up new avenues for catalyst design and optimization. Future research efforts might focus on:

1. Developing novel synthesis methods to create catalysts with a higher density of optimized edge sites while maintaining stability.
2. Exploring hybrid materials that combine the advantages of different dimensionalities, such as 1-D edge sites on 3-D supports, to maximize both activity and selectivity.
3. Investigating the potential of in-situ modification of active sites during reactions to maintain optimal performance over extended periods.
4. Utilizing advanced characterization techniques and computational modeling to gain deeper insights into the dynamic behavior of active sites under reaction conditions.
5. Exploring the application of these insights to other related reactions, such as the oxidative dehydrogenation of other alkanes or the direct conversion of methane to higher hydrocarbons.

By building on the current understanding of the relationship between active site distribution, dimensionality, and catalytic performance, researchers can potentially develop a new generation of highly efficient and selective catalysts for ODHP and related reactions, bringing us closer to more sustainable and economical processes for olefin production.

<Chinese>
B基催化剂中活性位点的空间分布和维度（1-D、2-D和3-D）显著影响了其在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应中的性能。活性位点可以分为三种类型：一维（1-D）、二维（2-D）和三维（3-D）分散活性位点 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。

1-D活性位点：
1-D活性位点通常位于2-D h-BN片的边缘或氮化硼纳米管（BNNTs）中，在ODHP反应中表现出优异的性能。例如，具有1-D边缘活性位点的h-BN在14%的丙烷转化率下表现出79%的丙烯选择性，优于传统的V/SiO2催化剂 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。边缘羟基化的氮化硼（BNOH）高效催化ODHP，在20.6%的丙烷转化率下达到80.2%的丙烯选择性，且CO2生成量极少（0.5%）[10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]。

1-D边缘活性位点的独特性质贡献了其高性能：
1. 丙基自由基的稳定：与0D位点（如V/SiO2）不同，BN催化剂的1D边缘位点能稳定这些自由基，减少过度氧化 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。
2. 选择性氧气活化：DFT计算表明，N端边缘对氧原子具有适度的解离能，增强了催化活性 [10.1002/smll.201701857]。
3. 动态活性位点形成：边缘BOH基团可被O2脱氢，生成BO和HO2自由基，促进烷烃的氢抽提 [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028]。

2-D活性位点：
2-D活性位点通常位于层状含硼材料的表面。例如，B2O3/SBA-15催化剂由于表面存在大量三配位硼氧环和羟基化线性硼物种而表现出更高的活性 [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]。具有2-D结构的层状硼硅沸石在ODHP反应中也表现出良好的性能 [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021]。

3-D活性位点：
3-D活性位点常见于块状材料或复杂结构中。例如，B2O3@BPO4催化剂具有独特的三明治状空心球结构，1-D B2O3纳米线覆盖在3-D BPO4空心球的内外表面。这种排列提供了高比表面积和增加的活性位点可及性，有助于ODHP反应中的优异性能 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028]。

理解这些不同活性位点的独特性质和反应机理，特别是1-D边缘活性位点，有助于设计更高效的商业应用催化剂：

1. 优化边缘位点暴露：最大化可及的边缘位点数量可以提高催化性能 [10.1002/cctc.202100476]。
2. 定制边缘功能化：优化羟基化或其他边缘修饰可以改善催化剂活性和选择性 [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62786-4]。
3. 控制空间分布：创建多孔结构或使用堇青石等载体可以优化活性位点分布并增强传质 [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028]。
4. 平衡表面和气相反应：理解表面催化和气相自由基反应之间的相互作用，可以优化两种反应途径 [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031]。
5. 调整热性能：利用h-BN等材料的优异导热性可以帮助避免热点并提高选择性 [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：对B基催化剂在ODHP反应中活性位点空间分布和维度的研究揭示了1-D边缘位点的关键作用，特别是在h-BN材料中。这些发现为催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的途径。未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 开发新型合成方法，创造具有更高密度优化边缘位点的催化剂，同时保持稳定性。
2. 探索结合不同维度优势的混合材料，如3-D载体上的1-D边缘位点，以最大化活性和选择性。
3. 研究反应过程中原位修饰活性位点的潜力，以长期保持最佳性能。
4. 利用先进表征技术和计算模拟，深入了解反应条件下活性位点的动态行为。
5. 探索将这些见解应用于其他相关反应，如其他烷烃的氧化脱氢或甲烷直接转化为高级烃。

通过深化对活性位点分布、维度与催化性能之间关系的理解，研究人员有望开发出新一代高效、高选择性的ODHP及相关反应催化剂，使我们更接近可持续和经济的烯烃生产过程。
