<English>
The spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in B-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions. The active sites can be classified into three types: one-dimensional (1-D), two-dimensional (2-D), and three-dimensional (3-D) dispersed active sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1].

1-D active sites:
1-D active sites, which are linearly dispersed on the edge of 2-D h-BN sheets, have shown superior performance in ODHP reactions. For example, h-BN with 1-D edge active sites exhibited a propene selectivity of 79% at a propane conversion of 14%, outperforming conventional catalysts like V/SiO2 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]. The edge-hydroxylated boron nitride (BNOH) efficiently catalyzed ODHP, reaching a propylene selectivity of 80.2% with minimal CO2 formation (0.5%) at a propane conversion of 20.6% [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040].

The unique properties of 1-D edge active sites contribute to their high performance:
1. Stabilization of intermediates: The 1-D nature of edge sites allows for the stabilization of propyl radicals, avoiding the creation of highly reactive species that lead to overoxidation [10.1126/science.aaf7885].
2. Oxygen activation: Edge B atoms are involved in O2 activation, forming BOH and BO active sites that facilitate dehydrogenation without dissociating O2 [10.1002/cctc.201700725].
3. Selective hydrogen abstraction: BO radicals catalyze hydrogen abstraction from alkanes to produce alkyl radicals [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028].

2-D active sites:
2-D active sites are typically found on the surface of materials like B2O3/SBA-15 catalysts. These catalysts demonstrated higher activity due to abundant tricoordinated boroxol and hydroxylated linear boron species on the surface [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]. The 2-D distribution allows for a high density of accessible active sites, contributing to improved catalytic performance.

3-D active sites:
3-D active sites can be found in structures like B2O3@BPO4 composites with sandwich-like hollow sphere structures. At reaction temperatures above 450°C, melted B2O3 supported on the surface of BPO4 hollow spheres acts as the catalytically active species [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028]. This 3-D arrangement provides a high surface area and increased accessibility of active sites.

The spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites influence catalyst performance through several mechanisms:
1. Accessibility: 1-D edge sites and porous 3-D structures provide better accessibility to reactants [10.1002/cctc.202100476].
2. Mass transfer: Well-developed channel structures in 3-D catalysts enhance mass transfer and provide space for gas-phase radical reactions [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028].
3. Thermal management: The distribution of active sites can affect thermal conductivity, helping to avoid hotspots during reactions [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028].
4. Selectivity control: The spatial arrangement of active sites can influence the formation of intermediates and suppress overoxidation reactions [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

Understanding these unique properties and reaction mechanisms contributes to the design of more efficient catalysts for commercial applications by:
1. Optimizing edge site exposure and density in 1-D and 2-D catalysts [10.1002/smll.201701857].
2. Developing synthesis methods to create catalysts with tailored spatial distributions of active sites [10.1002/cctc.202100476].
3. Exploring novel 3-D structures that combine the advantages of high surface area and improved mass transfer [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028].
4. Focusing on the creation and preservation of specific coordination environments (e.g., tricoordinated boron) that are conducive to ODHP reactions [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The research on B-based catalysts for ODHP reactions has revealed the critical role of spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in determining catalyst performance. While 1-D edge sites have shown remarkable selectivity and activity, the integration of 1-D, 2-D, and 3-D active sites in hierarchical structures could potentially lead to synergistic effects, combining the advantages of each dimensionality.

Future research directions may include:
1. Developing advanced characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic behavior of active sites under reaction conditions.
2. Exploring the use of computational modeling to predict optimal spatial distributions and dimensionalities of active sites for specific reaction conditions.
3. Investigating the potential of hybrid catalysts that combine B-based materials with other elements to create multifunctional active sites.
4. Studying the long-term stability and regeneration of active sites in different spatial configurations to improve catalyst longevity for commercial applications.
5. Exploring the application of these insights to other related reactions, such as the oxidative dehydrogenation of other alkanes or the direct conversion of methane to higher hydrocarbons.

By continuing to unravel the complex relationships between active site distribution, reaction mechanisms, and catalyst performance, researchers can pave the way for the next generation of highly efficient and selective catalysts for ODHP and related processes, potentially revolutionizing the production of valuable chemical feedstocks.

<Chinese>
B基催化剂中活性位点的空间分布和维度显著影响其在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应中的性能。活性位点可分为三类：一维（1-D）、二维（2-D）和三维（3-D）分散活性位点 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。

1-D活性位点：
1-D活性位点线性分布在2-D h-BN片的边缘，在ODHP反应中表现出优异的性能。例如，具有1-D边缘活性位点的h-BN在14%的丙烷转化率下表现出79%的丙烯选择性，优于传统的V/SiO2催化剂 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。边缘羟基化的氮化硼（BNOH）高效催化ODHP，在20.6%的丙烷转化率下达到80.2%的丙烯选择性，且CO2生成量极少（0.5%）[10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]。

1-D边缘活性位点的独特性质贡献了其高性能：
1. 中间体稳定：边缘位点的1-D性质允许稳定丙基自由基，避免生成导致过度氧化的高活性物种 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。
2. 氧气活化：边缘B原子参与O2活化，形成BOH和BO活性位点，促进脱氢而不解离O2 [10.1002/cctc.201700725]。
3. 选择性氢抽提：BO自由基催化烷烃的氢抽提，产生烷基自由基 [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028]。

2-D活性位点：
2-D活性位点通常存在于B2O3/SBA-15等材料表面。这些催化剂由于表面丰富的三配位硼氧环和羟基化线性硼物种而表现出更高的活性 [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]。2-D分布允许高密度的可接近活性位点，有助于提高催化性能。

3-D活性位点：
3-D活性位点可以在如B2O3@BPO4复合材料的三明治状空心球结构中找到。在450°C以上的反应温度下，支撑在BPO4空心球表面的熔融B2O3作为催化活性物种 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028]。这种3-D排列提供了高比表面积和增加的活性位点可接近性。

活性位点的空间分布和维度通过几种机制影响催化剂性能：
1. 可接近性：1-D边缘位点和多孔3-D结构为反应物提供更好的可接近性 [10.1002/cctc.202100476]。
2. 质量传递：3-D催化剂中发达的通道结构增强了质量传递，并为气相自由基反应提供空间 [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028]。
3. 热管理：活性位点的分布可以影响热导率，有助于避免反应中的热点 [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028]。
4. 选择性控制：活性位点的空间排列可以影响中间体的形成并抑制过度氧化反应 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

理解这些独特的性质和反应机理有助于设计更高效的商业应用催化剂：
1. 优化1-D和2-D催化剂中的边缘位点暴露和密度 [10.1002/smll.201701857]。
2. 开发合成方法，创造具有定制活性位点空间分布的催化剂 [10.1002/cctc.202100476]。
3. 探索结合高比表面积和改善质量传递优势的新型3-D结构 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028]。
4. 专注于创造和保持有利于ODHP反应的特定配位环境（如三配位硼）[10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：针对ODHP反应的B基催化剂研究揭示了活性位点的空间分布和维度在决定催化剂性能方面的关键作用。虽然1-D边缘位点表现出显著的选择性和活性，但在分层结构中整合1-D、2-D和3-D活性位点可能会产生协同效应，结合每种维度的优势。

未来研究方向可能包括：
1. 开发先进的表征技术，以更好地理解反应条件下活性位点的动态行为。
2. 探索使用计算模型预测特定反应条件下活性位点的最佳空间分布和维度。
3. 研究将B基材料与其他元素结合的混合催化剂潜力，创造多功能活性位点。
4. 研究不同空间配置中活性位点的长期稳定性和再生，以提高商业应用中催化剂的寿命。
5. 探索将这些见解应用于其他相关反应，如其他烷烃的氧化脱氢或甲烷直接转化为高级碳氢化合物。

通过继续揭示活性位点分布、反应机理和催化剂性能之间的复杂关系，研究人员可以为ODHP和相关过程的下一代高效、高选择性催化剂铺平道路，可能彻底改变有价值化学原料的生产。
