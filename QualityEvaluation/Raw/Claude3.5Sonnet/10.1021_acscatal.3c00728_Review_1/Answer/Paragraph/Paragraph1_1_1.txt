<English>
The spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in B-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions. The active sites can be classified into three types: one-dimensional (1-D), two-dimensional (2-D), and three-dimensional (3-D) dispersed active sites, each with unique properties and performance characteristics.

1-D Active Sites:
1-D active sites are primarily located at the edges of 2-D h-BN sheets and boron nitride nanotubes (BNNTs). These edge sites, particularly oxygen-terminated or hydroxylated edges, play a crucial role in ODHP reactions [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]. The 1-D edge structure offers several advantages:

a) High selectivity: h-BN with 1-D edge active sites exhibited a propene selectivity of 79% at a propane conversion of 14%, outperforming conventional catalysts like V/SiO2 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1].

b) Stabilization of intermediates: The 1-D nature of edge sites allows for the stabilization of propyl radicals, preventing overoxidation and improving selectivity [10.126/science.aaf7885].

c) Efficient oxygen activation: Edge B atoms are involved in O2 activation, forming BOH and BO active sites that facilitate the dehydrogenation process [10.1002/cctc.201700725].

2-D Active Sites:
2-D active sites are typically found on the surface of B-based materials, such as B2O3/SBA-15 catalysts. These sites offer:

a) Higher catalytic activity: Abundant tricoordinated boroxol and hydroxylated linear boron species on the surface contribute to improved performance [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040].

b) Enhanced accessibility: The 2-D distribution allows for better accessibility of reactants to active sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021].

3-D Active Sites:
3-D active sites are present in materials like boron carbide (B4C) and metal borides. These sites offer:

a) Structural stability: 3-D structures can provide better thermal stability and resistance to sintering [10.1002/anie.200901826].

b) Improved mass transfer: 3-D structures, such as honeycomb cordierite, allow for high gas hourly space velocity (GHSV) and enhanced mass transfer [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040].

The unique properties and reaction mechanisms of these sites, particularly 1-D edge active sites, contribute to the design of more efficient catalysts for commercial applications in several ways:

1. Optimizing edge site density: Increasing the number of edge sites by reducing the size of BN nanosheets or creating porous structures can enhance catalytic activity [10.1002/smll.201701857].

2. Tailoring edge termination: Oxygen-terminated or hydroxylated edges show superior performance in ODHP reactions [10.1002/cctc.201700004].

3. Balancing surface and edge sites: Combining the benefits of 2-D surface sites with 1-D edge sites can lead to catalysts with improved activity and selectivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028].

4. Controlling reaction pathways: Understanding the interplay between surface-catalyzed and gas-phase radical reactions allows for optimizing both pathways for improved overall activity [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031].

5. Enhancing stability: Developing strategies to improve the stability of 1-D edge sites and prevent sintering in 2-D and 3-D structures is crucial for long-term catalyst performance [10.1002/anie.202106713].

By leveraging these insights, researchers can develop B-based catalysts with tailored spatial distributions and dimensionalities of active sites, leading to more efficient and selective ODHP catalysts for commercial use.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The research on B-based catalysts for ODHP reactions has revealed the critical role of spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in determining catalyst performance. Moving forward, several promising directions emerge:

1. Hierarchical catalyst design: Combining the advantages of 1-D, 2-D, and 3-D structures in a single catalyst system could lead to synergistic effects, potentially improving both activity and selectivity. For instance, developing 3-D frameworks with high densities of 1-D edge sites could optimize both mass transfer and active site accessibility.

2. In-situ characterization techniques: Developing advanced spectroscopic methods to study the dynamic behavior of active sites under reaction conditions could provide deeper insights into the structure-performance relationships, guiding the rational design of next-generation catalysts.

3. Computational modeling: Leveraging machine learning and artificial intelligence to predict optimal spatial distributions and dimensionalities of active sites could accelerate catalyst discovery and optimization processes.

4. Novel synthesis methods: Exploring innovative synthesis techniques, such as atomic layer deposition or 3D printing, to precisely control the spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites at the nanoscale could lead to catalysts with unprecedented performance.

5. Multifunctional catalysts: Integrating B-based active sites with other catalytic functionalities could open up new possibilities for cascade reactions or process intensification in the production of value-added chemicals from propane.

These future directions could potentially revolutionize the field of ODHP catalysis, leading to more efficient, sustainable, and economically viable processes for propylene production.

<Chinese>
B基催化剂中活性位点的空间分布和维度（1-D、2-D和3-D）显著影响其在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应中的性能。活性位点可以分为三类：一维（1-D）、二维（2-D）和三维（3-D）分散活性位点，每种类型都具有独特的性能和特征。

1-D活性位点：
1-D活性位点主要位于2-D h-BN片和氮化硼纳米管（BNNTs）的边缘。这些边缘位点，特别是氧端接或羟基化边缘，在ODHP反应中起着关键作用[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。1-D边缘结构提供了几个优势：

a) 高选择性：具有1-D边缘活性位点的h-BN在14%的丙烷转化率下表现出79%的丙烯选择性，优于传统的V/SiO2催化剂[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。

b) 中间体稳定：边缘位点的1-D性质允许丙基自由基的稳定，防止过度氧化并提高选择性[10.126/science.aaf7885]。

c) 高效的氧气活化：边缘B原子参与O2活化，形成BOH和BO活性位点，促进脱氢过程[10.1002/cctc.201700725]。

2-D活性位点：
2-D活性位点通常位于B基材料的表面，如B2O3/SBA-15催化剂。这些位点提供：

a) 更高的催化活性：表面上丰富的三配位硼氧环和羟基化线性硼物种有助于提高性能[10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]。

b) 增强的可及性：2-D分布允许反应物更好地接触活性位点[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021]。

3-D活性位点：
3-D活性位点存在于碳化硼（B4C）和金属硼化物等材料中。这些位点提供：

a) 结构稳定性：3-D结构可以提供更好的热稳定性和抗烧结性能[10.1002/anie.200901826]。

b) 改善的质量传递：蜂窝状堇青石等3-D结构允许高气体小时空速（GHSV）和增强的质量传递[10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]。

这些位点的独特性质和反应机理，特别是1-D边缘活性位点，为商业应用设计更高效的催化剂做出了贡献，具体表现在以下几个方面：

1. 优化边缘位点密度：通过减小BN纳米片的尺寸或创建多孔结构来增加边缘位点数量，可以增强催化活性[10.1002/smll.201701857]。

2. 调整边缘端接：氧端接或羟基化边缘在ODHP反应中表现出优异的性能[10.1002/cctc.201700004]。

3. 平衡表面和边缘位点：结合2-D表面位点和1-D边缘位点的优势可以导致活性和选择性更高的催化剂[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028]。

4. 控制反应途径：理解表面催化和气相自由基反应之间的相互作用，可以优化这两种途径，提高整体活性[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031]。

5. 提高稳定性：开发策略以提高1-D边缘位点的稳定性并防止2-D和3-D结构的烧结对于催化剂的长期性能至关重要[10.1002/anie.202106713]。

通过利用这些见解，研究人员可以开发具有定制空间分布和维度活性位点的B基催化剂，从而为商业使用提供更高效和选择性的ODHP催化剂。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：对ODHP反应的B基催化剂的研究揭示了活性位点的空间分布和维度在决定催化剂性能方面的关键作用。展望未来，几个有前景的方向浮现出来：

1. 分层催化剂设计：在单一催化剂系统中结合1-D、2-D和3-D结构的优势可能会产生协同效应，潜在地提高活性和选择性。例如，开发具有高密度1-D边缘位点的3-D框架可以同时优化质量传递和活性位点可及性。

2. 原位表征技术：开发先进的光谱方法来研究反应条件下活性位点的动态行为，可以提供关于结构-性能关系的更深入见解，指导下一代催化剂的理性设计。

3. 计算机建模：利用机器学习和人工智能来预测活性位点的最佳空间分布和维度，可以加速催化剂的发现和优化过程。

4. 新型合成方法：探索创新的合成技术，如原子层沉积或3D打印，以在纳米尺度上精确控制活性位点的空间分布和维度，可能会产生具有前所未有性能的催化剂。

5. 多功能催化剂：将B基活性位点与其他催化功能性整合，可能会为丙烷生产高附加值化学品的级联反应或过程强化开辟新的可能性。

这些未来方向可能会彻底革新ODHP催化领域，导致更高效、可持续和经济可行的丙烯生产工艺。
