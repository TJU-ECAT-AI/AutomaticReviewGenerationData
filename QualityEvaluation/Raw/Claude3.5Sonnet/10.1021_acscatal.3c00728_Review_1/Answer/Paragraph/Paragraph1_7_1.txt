<English>
The spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in B-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions. The active sites can be classified into three types: one-dimensional (1-D), two-dimensional (2-D), and three-dimensional (3-D) dispersed active sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1].

1-D Active Sites:
1-D active sites are linearly dispersed on the edges of 2-D h-BN sheets and in boron nitride nanotubes (BNNTs) [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]. These edge sites, particularly oxygen-terminated armchair boron nitride edges, are proposed to be the catalytic active sites for ODHP reactions [10.126/science.aaf7885]. The 1-D edge structure offers unique advantages:

1. Stabilization of propyl radicals: Unlike 0D sites (e.g., V/SiO2) that create highly reactive propyl radicals, 1-D edge sites stabilize these radicals, preventing overoxidation [10.126/science.aaf7885].
2. Suppression of overoxidation: The 1-D nature of BN edges restricts the diffusion of adsorbates, suppressing overoxidation typical of 2D surface catalysts [10.126/science.aaf7885].
3. High selectivity: h-BN with 1-D edge active sites exhibited a propene selectivity of 79% at a propane conversion of 14%, outperforming conventional catalysts like V/SiO2 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1].

The active sites on 1-D edges are primarily BOH groups, which can be dehydrogenated by O2 to generate BO and HO2 radicals. These radicals then facilitate hydrogen abstraction from alkanes, both on the catalyst surface and in the gas phase [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028].

2-D Active Sites:
2-D active sites are found in layered structures such as h-BN nanosheets and layered borosilicate zeolites. These catalysts offer:

1. High surface area: 2-D structures provide a large surface area for catalytic reactions [10.1002/smll.201701857].
2. Improved mass transfer: The 2-D lamellar structure provides good accessibility to active sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021].
3. Thermal conductivity: The 2-D structure of h-BN helps avoid hotspots during the reaction, leading to better control and improved selectivity [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028].

3-D Active Sites:
3-D active sites are found in structures like boron-doped carbon nanofibers and boron oxide clusters. These sites offer:

1. High density of active sites: 3-D structures can provide a higher concentration of active sites [10.1002/cssc.201402363].
2. Improved stability: 3-D structures can offer better thermal stability and resistance to oxidation [10.1002/anie.200901826].

Recent studies have shown that the active sites for ODHP reactions are not isolated BO3 units, but rather require some degree of B-O-B connectivity. This suggests that more complex, multi-dimensional structures are necessary for catalytic activity [10.1002/anie.201914696].

Understanding these unique properties and reaction mechanisms contributes to the design of more efficient catalysts for commercial applications by:

1. Optimizing edge exposure: Maximizing the number of accessible edge sites in BN-based materials [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62786-4].
2. Controlling active site formation: Tailoring synthesis methods to create specific types of active sites, such as BOH groups or B-O-B connected structures [10.1002/anie.201914696].
3. Enhancing mass transfer: Developing structures that optimize both active site exposure and reactant accessibility, such as porous or hierarchical structures [10.1016/j.apsusc.2023.156743].
4. Balancing selectivity and activity: Leveraging the unique properties of 1-D edge sites to achieve high selectivity while optimizing overall catalytic activity [10.126/science.aaf7885].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The research on B-based catalysts for ODHP reactions has revealed the critical role of spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in determining catalytic performance. Moving forward, there are several promising directions for future research and development:

1. Hybrid structures: Combining the advantages of different dimensionalities, such as 1-D edge sites on 2-D nanosheets supported on 3-D structures, could lead to catalysts with optimized performance.

2. In-situ characterization: Developing advanced techniques for studying the dynamic nature of active sites under reaction conditions could provide deeper insights into the catalytic mechanisms and guide catalyst design.

3. Computational modeling: Leveraging machine learning and advanced simulation techniques to predict and design optimal spatial distributions of active sites could accelerate catalyst development.

4. Defect engineering: Exploring methods to create and control specific types of defects, such as N-defects or B-O-B connectivity, could lead to more active and selective catalysts.

5. Scale-up and industrialization: Addressing challenges related to large-scale production and long-term stability of B-based catalysts will be crucial for their commercial implementation in ODHP processes.

By pursuing these research directions, we can expect significant advancements in the field of B-based catalysts for ODHP reactions, potentially leading to more efficient and sustainable propylene production processes.

<Chinese>
B基催化剂中活性位点的空间分布和维度（一维、二维和三维）显著影响其在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应中的性能。活性位点可以分为三种类型：一维（1-D）、二维（2-D）和三维（3-D）分散活性位点[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。

一维活性位点：
一维活性位点线性分布在二维h-BN片的边缘和氮化硼纳米管（BNNTs）中[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。这些边缘位点，特别是氧终止的扶手椅型氮化硼边缘，被认为是ODHP反应的催化活性位点[10.126/science.aaf7885]。一维边缘结构具有独特的优势：

1. 丙基自由基的稳定：与创造高度活性丙基自由基的0D位点（如V/SiO2）不同，一维边缘位点稳定这些自由基，防止过度氧化[10.126/science.aaf7885]。
2. 抑制过度氧化：BN边缘的一维性质限制了吸附物的扩散，抑制了典型的二维表面催化剂的过度氧化[10.126/science.aaf7885]。
3. 高选择性：具有一维边缘活性位点的h-BN在14%的丙烷转化率下表现出79%的丙烯选择性，优于传统的V/SiO2催化剂[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。

一维边缘上的活性位点主要是BOH基团，可以被O2脱氢生成BO和HO2自由基。这些自由基然后促进烷烃的氢抽提，无论是在催化剂表面还是在气相中[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028]。

二维活性位点：
二维活性位点存在于层状结构中，如h-BN纳米片和层状硼硅沸石。这些催化剂提供：

1. 高比表面积：二维结构为催化反应提供了大的表面积[10.1002/smll.201701857]。
2. 改善的质量传递：二维层状结构提供了良好的活性位点可及性[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021]。
3. 热传导性：h-BN的二维结构有助于避免反应过程中的热点，从而实现更好的控制和改善的选择性[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028]。

三维活性位点：
三维活性位点存在于诸如掺硼碳纳米纤维和三氧化二硼团簇等结构中。这些位点提供：

1. 高密度活性位点：三维结构可以提供更高浓度的活性位点[10.1002/cssc.201402363]。
2. 改善的稳定性：三维结构可以提供更好的热稳定性和抗氧化性[10.1002/anie.200901826]。

最近的研究表明，ODHP反应的活性位点不是孤立的BO3单元，而是需要一定程度的B-O-B连接。这表明更复杂的多维结构对于催化活性是必要的[10.1002/anie.201914696]。

理解这些独特的性质和反应机理有助于设计更高效的商业应用催化剂，通过：

1. 优化边缘暴露：最大化BN基材料中可接近的边缘位点数量[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62786-4]。
2. 控制活性位点形成：定制合成方法以创建特定类型的活性位点，如BOH基团或B-O-B连接结构[10.1002/anie.201914696]。
3. 增强质量传递：开发同时优化活性位点暴露和反应物可及性的结构，如多孔或层次结构[10.1016/j.apsusc.2023.156743]。
4. 平衡选择性和活性：利用一维边缘位点的独特性质来实现高选择性，同时优化整体催化活性[10.126/science.aaf7885]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：对ODHP反应B基催化剂的研究揭示了活性位点的空间分布和维度在决定催化性能中的关键作用。展望未来，有几个有前景的研究和开发方向：

1. 混合结构：结合不同维度的优势，如在三维结构上支撑的二维纳米片上的一维边缘位点，可能导致性能优化的催化剂。

2. 原位表征：开发先进技术来研究反应条件下活性位点的动态性质，可以提供对催化机理更深入的见解，并指导催化剂设计。

3. 计算机模拟：利用机器学习和先进的模拟技术来预测和设计最佳的活性位点空间分布，可以加速催化剂开发。

4. 缺陷工程：探索创建和控制特定类型缺陷的方法，如N缺陷或B-O-B连接，可能导致更活跃和选择性的催化剂。

5. 规模扩大和工业化：解决与B基催化剂大规模生产和长期稳定性相关的挑战，对其在ODHP过程中的商业实施至关重要。

通过追求这些研究方向，我们可以期待ODHP反应B基催化剂领域的重大进展，可能导致更高效和可持续的丙烯生产过程。
