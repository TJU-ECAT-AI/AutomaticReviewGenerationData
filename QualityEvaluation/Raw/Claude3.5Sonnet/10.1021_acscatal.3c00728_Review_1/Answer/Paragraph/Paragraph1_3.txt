<English>
The spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in B-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions. The active sites can be classified into three types: one-dimensional (1-D), two-dimensional (2-D), and three-dimensional (3-D) dispersed active sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1].

1-D Active Sites:
1-D active sites, typically found at the edges of 2-D h-BN sheets or in boron nitride nanotubes (BNNTs), have shown superior performance in ODHP reactions. For example, h-BN with 1-D edge active sites exhibited a propene selectivity of 79% at a propane conversion of 14%, outperforming conventional catalysts like V/SiO2 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]. The edge-hydroxylated boron nitride (BNOH) efficiently catalyzed ODHP, reaching a propylene selectivity of 80.2% with minimal CO2 formation (0.5%) at a propane conversion of 20.6% [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040].

The unique properties of 1-D edge active sites include:
1. Stabilization of propyl radicals, preventing overoxidation [10.1126/science.aaf7885].
2. Selective activation of C-H bonds in propane [10.1002/cctc.201700004].
3. Efficient oxygen activation at edge B atoms [10.1002/cctc.201700725].

2-D Active Sites:
2-D active sites are typically found on the surface of h-BN sheets or other layered boron-containing materials. These sites can include defects, such as three-boron center (TBC) N-defects, which play a crucial role in facilitating propane conversion [10.1002/anie.202106713]. B2O3/SBA-15 catalysts demonstrated higher activity due to abundant tricoordinated boroxol and hydroxylated linear boron species on the surface [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040].

3-D Active Sites:
3-D active sites are often found in bulk boron-containing materials or as aggregated boron species. For example, B2O3 clusters formed on carbon nanofiber surfaces at higher calcination temperatures create a 3-D distribution of active sites [10.1002/cssc.201402363]. The aggregated boron species embedded in MWW- and MFI-type zeolites displayed high efficiency in activating propane [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031].

The spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites influence catalyst performance through several mechanisms:

1. Accessibility: 1-D edge sites and 2-D surface sites often provide better accessibility to reactants compared to 3-D bulk sites [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040].
2. Selectivity: The 1-D nature of edge sites helps avoid the creation of highly reactive propyl radicals, leading to higher olefin selectivity [10.1126/science.aaf7885].
3. Stability: 3-D structures and aggregated species can provide better thermal stability and resistance to sintering [10.1002/anie.200901826].
4. Mass transfer: 1-D and 2-D structures often facilitate better mass transfer, allowing for higher catalytic activity at high space velocities [10.1002/cctc.202100476].

Understanding these unique properties and reaction mechanisms contributes to the design of more efficient catalysts for commercial applications by:

1. Optimizing edge site density and accessibility in 2-D materials [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1].
2. Developing synthesis methods to create tailored spatial distributions of active sites [10.1002/smll.201701857].
3. Exploring hybrid structures that combine the advantages of different dimensionalities, such as 1-D B2O3 nanowires on 3-D BPO4 hollow spheres [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028].
4. Focusing on creating and maintaining specific active site structures, such as BOH groups or B-O-B connectivity, rather than isolated BO3 units [10.1002/anie.201914696].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The research on B-based catalysts for ODHP reactions has revealed the critical role of spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in determining catalyst performance. Moving forward, several promising directions emerge:

1. Multiscale catalyst design: Future research could focus on developing catalysts that integrate advantageous features from different dimensionalities. For example, combining 1-D edge sites for high selectivity with 3-D structures for improved stability and heat management.

2. Dynamic active site engineering: Given the observed dynamic nature of some active sites under reaction conditions, future catalyst designs could incorporate features that promote beneficial restructuring during reactions, potentially leading to self-optimizing catalysts.

3. Advanced characterization techniques: Development of in-situ and operando characterization methods to better understand the evolution of active sites during reactions could provide crucial insights for catalyst optimization.

4. Computational screening and machine learning: Leveraging computational methods to predict optimal spatial distributions and compositions of active sites could accelerate the discovery of high-performance catalysts.

5. Sustainable catalyst production: Exploring environmentally friendly synthesis methods for creating tailored B-based catalysts with optimized active site distributions could enhance the commercial viability of ODHP processes.

These future directions could lead to the development of next-generation B-based catalysts with unprecedented performance in ODHP reactions, potentially revolutionizing propylene production technologies.

<Chinese>
B基催化剂中活性位点的空间分布和维度对其在丙烷氧化脱氢(ODHP)反应中的性能有显著影响。活性位点可分为三类：一维(1-D)、二维(2-D)和三维(3-D)分散活性位点[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。

1-D活性位点：
1-D活性位点通常位于2-D h-BN片的边缘或氮化硼纳米管(BNNTs)中，在ODHP反应中表现出优异的性能。例如，具有1-D边缘活性位点的h-BN在丙烷转化率为14%时表现出79%的丙烯选择性，优于传统的V/SiO2催化剂[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。边缘羟基化的氮化硼(BNOH)有效催化ODHP反应，在丙烷转化率为20.6%时达到80.2%的丙烯选择性，且CO2生成量极少(0.5%)[10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]。

1-D边缘活性位点的独特性质包括：
1. 稳定丙基自由基，防止过度氧化[10.1126/science.aaf7885]。
2. 选择性活化丙烷中的C-H键[10.1002/cctc.201700004]。
3. 在边缘B原子处高效活化氧[10.1002/cctc.201700725]。

2-D活性位点：
2-D活性位点通常位于h-BN片或其他层状含硼材料的表面。这些位点可以包括缺陷，如三硼中心(TBC) N缺陷，它们在促进丙烷转化中起着关键作用[10.1002/anie.202106713]。B2O3/SBA-15催化剂由于表面存在大量三配位硼氧环和羟基化线性硼物种而表现出更高的活性[10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]。

3-D活性位点：
3-D活性位点通常存在于体相含硼材料中或作为聚集的硼物种。例如，在较高煅烧温度下，在碳纳米纤维表面形成的B2O3团簇创造了3-D分布的活性位点[10.1002/cssc.201402363]。嵌入MWW和MFI型分子筛中的聚集硼物种在活化丙烷方面表现出高效率[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031]。

活性位点的空间分布和维度通过几种机制影响催化剂性能：

1. 可及性：1-D边缘位点和2-D表面位点通常比3-D体相位点为反应物提供更好的可及性[10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]。
2. 选择性：边缘位点的1-D性质有助于避免产生高活性丙基自由基，从而导致更高的烯烃选择性[10.1126/science.aaf7885]。
3. 稳定性：3-D结构和聚集物种可提供更好的热稳定性和抗烧结性[10.1002/anie.200901826]。
4. 传质：1-D和2-D结构通常促进更好的传质，允许在高空速下实现更高的催化活性[10.1002/cctc.202100476]。

理解这些独特的性质和反应机理有助于设计更高效的商业应用催化剂，方法包括：

1. 优化2-D材料中的边缘位点密度和可及性[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。
2. 开发合成方法以创造定制的活性位点空间分布[10.1002/smll.201701857]。
3. 探索结合不同维度优势的混合结构，如3-D BPO4空心球上的1-D B2O3纳米线[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028]。
4. 注重创建和维持特定的活性位点结构，如BOH基团或B-O-B连接，而不是孤立的BO3单元[10.1002/anie.201914696]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：对ODHP反应的B基催化剂的研究揭示了活性位点的空间分布和维度在决定催化剂性能中的关键作用。展望未来，几个有前景的方向浮现：

1. 多尺度催化剂设计：未来的研究可以集中于开发整合不同维度优势特征的催化剂。例如，结合1-D边缘位点的高选择性与3-D结构的改进稳定性和热管理。

2. 动态活性位点工程：鉴于在反应条件下观察到的一些活性位点的动态性质，未来的催化剂设计可以纳入促进反应过程中有益重构的特征，可能导致自我优化的催化剂。

3. 先进表征技术：开发原位和操作条件下的表征方法，以更好地理解反应过程中活性位点的演变，可为催化剂优化提供关键见解。

4. 计算筛选和机器学习：利用计算方法预测活性位点的最佳空间分布和组成，可以加速高性能催化剂的发现。

5. 可持续催化剂生产：探索环境友好的合成方法，创造具有优化活性位点分布的定制B基催化剂，可以增强ODHP过程的商业可行性。

这些未来方向可能导致开发出在ODHP反应中具有前所未有性能的下一代B基催化剂，潜在地革新丙烯生产技术。
