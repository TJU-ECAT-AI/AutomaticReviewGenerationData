<English>
The spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in B-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions. The active sites can be classified into three types: one-dimensional (1-D), two-dimensional (2-D), and three-dimensional (3-D) dispersed active sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1].

1-D Active Sites:
1-D active sites, typically found on the edges of 2-D h-BN sheets or in boron nitride nanotubes (BNNTs), have shown superior performance in ODHP reactions. For example, h-BN with 1-D edge active sites exhibited a propene selectivity of 79% at a propane conversion of 14%, outperforming conventional catalysts like V/SiO2 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]. The edge hydroxylated boron nitride (BNOH) efficiently catalyzed ODHP, reaching a propylene selectivity of 80.2% with only 0.5% CO2 at a propane conversion of 20.6% [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040].

The unique properties of 1-D edge active sites include:
1. Stabilization of propyl radicals, avoiding the creation of highly reactive species prone to overoxidation [10.1126/science.aaf7885].
2. Efficient activation of O2, with edge B atoms playing a crucial role [10.1002/cctc.201700725].
3. Formation of specific active structures like BOH groups, which selectively break C-H bonds while suppressing propylene overoxidation [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014].

2-D Active Sites:
2-D active sites are typically found on the surface of materials like B2O3/SBA-15 catalysts. These catalysts demonstrate higher activity due to abundant tricoordinated boroxol and hydroxylated linear boron species on the surface [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]. The 2-D distribution of active sites can be effective, as shown by layered borosilicate zeolites with surface-accessible defective trigonal boron species [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021].

3-D Active Sites:
3-D active sites are often found in more complex structures, such as the B2O3@BPO4 composite with a sandwich-like hollow sphere structure. This arrangement provides a high surface area and increased accessibility of active sites, contributing to excellent performance in ODHP reactions [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028].

The performance of these catalysts is influenced by several factors:
1. Edge vs. Surface Sites: Edge sites, particularly in 1-D configurations, often show higher activity and selectivity compared to surface sites [10.1002/cctc.201700725].
2. Defects and N-termination: Defective trigonal boron species and N-terminated edges play crucial roles in catalytic activity [10.1002/anie.202106713].
3. Connectivity: Active boron-based ODH catalysts likely require boron species with some degree of B-O-B connectivity, rather than isolated BO3 units [10.1002/anie.201914696].
4. Dynamic Nature: Under reaction conditions, the active sites can undergo restructuring, forming a highly dynamic oxidized boron active phase [10.1002/anie.201914696].

Understanding these unique properties and reaction mechanisms contributes to the design of more efficient catalysts for commercial applications by:
1. Optimizing edge exposure and density in 1-D and 2-D materials.
2. Tailoring the distribution and connectivity of boron species to enhance activity and selectivity.
3. Developing synthesis methods that create and maintain desired active site structures under reaction conditions.
4. Balancing surface-catalyzed and gas-phase radical reactions for improved overall activity [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The research on B-based catalysts for ODHP reactions has revealed the critical importance of spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites. Moving forward, there are several promising directions for further advancement in this field:

1. Hybrid Structures: Developing catalysts that combine the advantages of different dimensionalities, such as 1-D edge sites on 2-D sheets supported on 3-D structures, could lead to synergistic effects and improved performance.

2. In-situ Characterization: Advanced techniques for studying the dynamic behavior of active sites under reaction conditions could provide deeper insights into the restructuring processes and guide the design of more stable and efficient catalysts.

3. Computational Modeling: Leveraging advanced computational methods to predict and optimize the performance of B-based catalysts with various spatial distributions and dimensionalities could accelerate the discovery of novel, high-performance materials.

4. Scalable Synthesis: Developing methods for large-scale production of catalysts with precisely controlled active site distributions will be crucial for commercial implementation.

5. Multifunctional Catalysts: Exploring the potential of B-based catalysts for other reactions or combining ODHP functionality with other desirable properties could lead to more versatile and economically viable catalytic systems.

By pursuing these research directions, the field of B-based catalysts for ODHP reactions can continue to evolve, potentially leading to breakthroughs in efficiency, selectivity, and commercial viability of propylene production processes.

<Chinese>
B基催化剂中活性位点的空间分布和维度对丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应的性能有显著影响。活性位点可以分为三类：一维（1-D）、二维（2-D）和三维（3-D）分散活性位点 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。

1-D活性位点：
1-D活性位点通常位于2-D h-BN片的边缘或氮化硼纳米管（BNNTs）中，在ODHP反应中表现出优越的性能。例如，具有1-D边缘活性位点的h-BN在14%的丙烷转化率下表现出79%的丙烯选择性，优于传统的V/SiO2催化剂 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。边缘羟基化的氮化硼（BNOH）高效催化ODHP，在20.6%的丙烷转化率下达到80.2%的丙烯选择性，同时CO2生成量仅为0.5% [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]。

1-D边缘活性位点的独特性质包括：
1. 稳定丙基自由基，避免生成容易过度氧化的高活性物种 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。
2. 高效活化O2，边缘B原子在这一过程中起关键作用 [10.1002/cctc.201700725]。
3. 形成特定的活性结构，如BOH基团，可选择性地断裂C-H键，同时抑制丙烯过度氧化 [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014]。

2-D活性位点：
2-D活性位点通常位于B2O3/SBA-15等材料表面。这些催化剂由于表面存在大量的三配位硼氧环和羟基化线性硼物种而表现出更高的活性 [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]。层状硼硅沸石with表面可接触的缺陷三角硼物种展示了2-D活性位点分布的有效性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021]。

3-D活性位点：
3-D活性位点通常存在于更复杂的结构中，如具有三明治状空心球结构的B2O3@BPO4复合材料。这种排列提供了高表面积和增加的活性位点可及性，有助于ODHP反应的优异性能 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028]。

这些催化剂的性能受几个因素影响：
1. 边缘vs表面位点：边缘位点，特别是1-D构型，通常比表面位点表现出更高的活性和选择性 [10.1002/cctc.201700725]。
2. 缺陷和N端接：缺陷三角硼物种和N端接边缘在催化活性中起关键作用 [10.1002/anie.202106713]。
3. 连接性：活性硼基ODH催化剂可能需要具有一定程度B-O-B连接的硼物种，而不是孤立的BO3单元 [10.1002/anie.201914696]。
4. 动态特性：在反应条件下，活性位点可能会重构，形成高度动态的氧化硼活性相 [10.1002/anie.201914696]。

理解这些独特的性质和反应机理有助于设计更高效的商业应用催化剂，通过：
1. 优化1-D和2-D材料中的边缘暴露和密度。
2. 调整硼物种的分布和连接性以提高活性和选择性。
3. 开发能在反应条件下创建和维持所需活性位点结构的合成方法。
4. 平衡表面催化和气相自由基反应，以提高整体活性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：对ODHP反应的B基催化剂的研究揭示了活性位点空间分布和维度的关键重要性。展望未来，该领域有几个有前景的发展方向：

1. 混合结构：开发结合不同维度优势的催化剂，如将1-D边缘位点与2-D片层支撑在3-D结构上，可能产生协同效应并提高性能。

2. 原位表征：开发先进技术以研究反应条件下活性位点的动态行为，可以提供对重构过程更深入的理解，指导设计更稳定和高效的催化剂。

3. 计算模拟：利用先进的计算方法预测和优化具有各种空间分布和维度的B基催化剂的性能，可以加速发现新型高性能材料。

4. 可扩展合成：开发精确控制活性位点分布的大规模催化剂生产方法，对商业实施至关重要。

5. 多功能催化剂：探索B基催化剂在其他反应中的潜力，或将ODHP功能与其他所需性能结合，可能导致更versatile和经济可行的催化系统。

通过追求这些研究方向，ODHP反应的B基催化剂领域可以继续发展，可能在丙烯生产过程的效率、选择性和商业可行性方面取得突破性进展。
