<English>
The spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in B-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions. Based on the extensive research presented, we can categorize the active sites into three main types: one-dimensional (1-D), two-dimensional (2-D), and three-dimensional (3-D) dispersed active sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1].

1-D Edge Active Sites:
The 1-D edge active sites, particularly in hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN) materials, have shown superior performance in ODHP reactions. These sites are linearly dispersed on the edges of 2-D h-BN sheets and in boron nitride nanotubes (BNNTs) [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]. The edge sites, often hydroxylated (BOH groups), play a crucial role in the catalytic process [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62786-4]. 

Key properties of 1-D edge active sites include:
1. High selectivity: h-BN with 1-D edge active sites exhibited a propene selectivity of 79% at a propane conversion of 14%, outperforming conventional catalysts like V/SiO2 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1].
2. Unique reaction mechanism: The reaction starts with hydrogen abstraction from the second carbon of propane, followed by O-O bond cleavage to create B-O and N-O species [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1].
3. Stabilization of intermediates: The 1-D nature of these sites stabilizes propyl radicals, avoiding the creation of highly reactive species that lead to overoxidation [10.126/science.aaf7885].

2-D and 3-D Dispersed Active Sites:
While 1-D edge sites show promising results, research has also explored 2-D and 3-D dispersed active sites:

1. Surface-distributed sites: B2O3/SBA-15 catalysts demonstrated higher activity due to abundant tricoordinated boroxol and hydroxylated linear boron species on the surface [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040].
2. Zeolite-based catalysts: Borosilicate zeolites with homogeneous distribution of boron have shown promise in ODHP reactions [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017].
3. Complex structures: B2O3@BPO4 composites with sandwich-like hollow sphere structures have shown excellent activity and selectivity in ODHP reactions [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028].

Performance Influence and Design Implications:
Understanding the unique properties of these active sites contributes to the design of more efficient catalysts for commercial applications:

1. Maximizing edge exposure: Increasing the density of edge sites by reducing the size of BN nanosheets or creating 1-D shaped materials can enhance catalytic activity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1].
2. Tailoring edge termination: N-terminated edges have shown particular effectiveness for oxygen activation [10.1002/smll.201701857].
3. Optimizing porosity: Creating porous structures with high specific surface areas exposes more active sites, enhancing overall catalytic performance [10.1002/smll.201701857].
4. Balancing surface and gas-phase reactions: Understanding the interplay between surface-catalyzed and gas-phase radical reactions allows for optimizing both pathways [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031].
5. Thermal management: The superior thermal conductivity of h-BN helps avoid hotspots during the reaction, leading to better control and improved selectivity [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The research on B-based catalysts for ODHP reactions has revealed the critical role of active site spatial distribution and dimensionality in determining catalyst performance. The superior performance of 1-D edge active sites, particularly in h-BN materials, opens up new avenues for catalyst design. Future research efforts could focus on:

1. Developing novel synthesis methods to precisely control the density and nature of edge sites in B-based materials.
2. Exploring hybrid structures that combine the advantages of 1-D edge sites with the high surface area of 2-D and 3-D structures.
3. Investigating the potential of doping or functionalization of edge sites to further enhance their catalytic properties.
4. Studying the long-term stability and regeneration of edge active sites under industrial conditions.
5. Applying the insights gained from B-based catalysts to other classes of materials for ODHP and related reactions.

These directions could lead to the development of next-generation catalysts with improved activity, selectivity, and stability for commercial ODHP applications, potentially revolutionizing propylene production technologies.

<Chinese>
B基催化剂中活性位点的空间分布和维度对丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应的性能有显著影响。基于所呈现的广泛研究，我们可以将活性位点分为三种主要类型：一维（1-D）、二维（2-D）和三维（3-D）分散活性位点[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。

1-D边缘活性位点：
六方氮化硼（h-BN）材料中的1-D边缘活性位点在ODHP反应中表现出卓越的性能。这些位点线性分布在2-D h-BN片的边缘和氮化硼纳米管（BNNTs）中[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。边缘位点，通常是羟基化的（BOH基团），在催化过程中起着关键作用[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62786-4]。

1-D边缘活性位点的主要特性包括：
1. 高选择性：具有1-D边缘活性位点的h-BN在14%的丙烷转化率下表现出79%的丙烯选择性，优于传统的V/SiO2催化剂[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。
2. 独特的反应机制：反应从丙烷的第二个碳原子上的氢抽提开始，随后O-O键断裂形成B-O和N-O物种[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。
3. 中间体稳定：这些位点的1-D性质稳定了丙基自由基，避免了产生导致过度氧化的高活性物种[10.126/science.aaf7885]。

2-D和3-D分散活性位点：
虽然1-D边缘位点显示出有前景的结果，但研究也探索了2-D和3-D分散活性位点：

1. 表面分布位点：B2O3/SBA-15催化剂由于表面上丰富的三配位硼氧环和羟基化线性硼物种而表现出更高的活性[10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]。
2. 沸石基催化剂：硼硅沸石中硼的均匀分布在ODHP反应中显示出前景[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017]。
3. 复杂结构：具有三明治状空心球结构的B2O3@BPO4复合材料在ODHP反应中表现出优异的活性和选择性[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028]。

性能影响和设计启示：
理解这些活性位点的独特性质有助于设计更高效的商业应用催化剂：

1. 最大化边缘暴露：通过减小BN纳米片的尺寸或创建1-D形状的材料来增加边缘位点的密度可以提高催化活性[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。
2. 定制边缘终端：N端接边缘对氧气活化特别有效[10.1002/smll.201701857]。
3. 优化孔隙度：创建具有高比表面积的多孔结构可以暴露更多活性位点，提高整体催化性能[10.1002/smll.201701857]。
4. 平衡表面和气相反应：理解表面催化和气相自由基反应之间的相互作用可以优化两种途径[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031]。
5. 热管理：h-BN的优异热导率有助于避免反应过程中的热点，从而实现更好的控制和改善选择性[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：对ODHP反应的B基催化剂的研究揭示了活性位点空间分布和维度在决定催化剂性能方面的关键作用。1-D边缘活性位点，尤其是在h-BN材料中的卓越表现，为催化剂设计开辟了新的途径。未来的研究努力可以集中在：

1. 开发新的合成方法，精确控制B基材料中边缘位点的密度和性质。
2. 探索结合1-D边缘位点优势与2-D和3-D结构高比表面积的混合结构。
3. 研究边缘位点的掺杂或功能化，以进一步增强其催化性能。
4. 研究工业条件下边缘活性位点的长期稳定性和再生。
5. 将从B基催化剂获得的见解应用于ODHP和相关反应的其他类别材料。

这些方向可能导致开发出具有改进的活性、选择性和稳定性的下一代商业ODHP应用催化剂，有可能彻底改变丙烯生产技术。
