<English>
The spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in B-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions. The active sites can be classified into three types: one-dimensional (1-D), two-dimensional (2-D), and three-dimensional (3-D) dispersed active sites, each with unique properties and influences on catalytic performance [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1].

1-D Edge Active Sites:
1-D active sites, typically found on the edges of 2-D h-BN sheets or in boron nitride nanotubes (BNNTs), have shown superior performance in ODHP reactions. These edge sites, particularly when hydroxylated or oxygen-terminated, play a crucial role in catalytic activity [10.1002/cctc.201700004]. For example, h-BN with 1-D edge active sites exhibited a propene selectivity of 79% at a propane conversion of 14%, outperforming conventional catalysts like V/SiO2 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1].

The unique properties of 1-D edge active sites include:
1. Stabilization of propyl radicals, preventing overoxidation [10.1126/science.aaf7885].
2. Efficient activation of O2 molecules [10.1002/cctc.201700725].
3. Formation of dynamic active sites through hydrogen abstraction of BOH groups [10.1002/cctc.201700004].

2-D and 3-D Active Sites:
While 1-D edge sites show promising results, 2-D and 3-D active site distributions also play important roles in catalyst performance. For instance:
1. B2O3/SBA-15 catalysts with 2-D surface-distributed sites showed higher activity due to abundant tricoordinated boroxol and hydroxylated linear boron species [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040].
2. 3-D structures, such as honeycomb cordierite with straight channels, allow for high gas hourly space velocity (GHSV) and improved mass transfer [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040].

The spatial distribution of active sites also influences catalyst performance through:
1. Accessibility of active sites to reactants [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021].
2. Thermal conductivity and hotspot prevention [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028].
3. Synergistic effects between surface-catalyzed and gas-phase radical reactions [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031].

Understanding these unique properties and reaction mechanisms contributes to the design of more efficient catalysts for commercial applications by:
1. Optimizing edge site exposure and density [10.1002/smll.201701857].
2. Tailoring the synthesis methods to create desired active site distributions [10.1002/smll.201701857].
3. Balancing 1-D, 2-D, and 3-D active site distributions for optimal performance [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031].
4. Incorporating structural designs that enhance mass transfer and thermal properties [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The research on B-based catalysts for ODHP reactions has revealed the complex interplay between spatial distribution, dimensionality, and performance of active sites. While 1-D edge active sites have shown promising results, the integration of 1-D, 2-D, and 3-D structures in catalyst design could lead to even more efficient systems. Future research directions may include:

1. Developing hierarchical catalysts that combine the benefits of 1-D edge sites with 2-D and 3-D structures for enhanced mass transfer and stability.
2. Exploring novel synthesis methods to precisely control the spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites.
3. Investigating the dynamic nature of active sites under reaction conditions to design catalysts that maintain optimal performance over time.
4. Utilizing advanced characterization techniques and in-situ studies to gain deeper insights into the structure-activity relationships of B-based catalysts.
5. Exploring the potential of hybrid materials that combine B-based active sites with other elements or supports to achieve synergistic effects in ODHP reactions.

These research directions could lead to the development of next-generation B-based catalysts with improved activity, selectivity, and stability for commercial ODHP applications, potentially revolutionizing propylene production technologies.

<Chinese>
B基催化剂中活性位点的空间分布和维度（一维、二维和三维）显著影响其在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应中的性能。活性位点可以分为三种类型：一维（1-D）、二维（2-D）和三维（3-D）分散活性位点，每种类型都具有独特的性质并对催化性能产生影响[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。

一维边缘活性位点：
一维活性位点，通常位于二维h-BN片的边缘或氮化硼纳米管（BNNTs）中，在ODHP反应中表现出卓越的性能。这些边缘位点，特别是羟基化或氧化终止的边缘位点，在催化活性中起着至关重要的作用[10.1002/cctc.201700004]。例如，具有一维边缘活性位点的h-BN在14%的丙烷转化率下表现出79%的丙烯选择性，优于传统的V/SiO2催化剂[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。

一维边缘活性位点的独特性质包括：
1. 稳定丙基自由基，防止过度氧化[10.1126/science.aaf7885]。
2. 高效活化O2分子[10.1002/cctc.201700725]。
3. 通过BOH基团的氢抽提形成动态活性位点[10.1002/cctc.201700004]。

二维和三维活性位点：
虽然一维边缘位点显示出promising结果，但二维和三维活性位点分布在催化剂性能中也起着重要作用。例如：
1. 具有二维表面分布位点的B2O3/SBA-15催化剂由于丰富的三配位硼氧醇和羟基化线性硼物种而表现出更高的活性[10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]。
2. 三维结构，如具有直通道的蜂窝状堇青石，允许高气体小时空速（GHSV）和改善的质量传递[10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]。

活性位点的空间分布还通过以下方式影响催化剂性能：
1. 反应物对活性位点的可及性[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021]。
2. 热导率和热点预防[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028]。
3. 表面催化和气相自由基反应之间的协同效应[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031]。

理解这些独特的性质和反应机理有助于设计更高效的商业应用催化剂，通过：
1. 优化边缘位点的暴露和密度[10.1002/smll.201701857]。
2. 定制合成方法以创建所需的活性位点分布[10.1002/smll.201701857]。
3. 平衡一维、二维和三维活性位点分布以获得最佳性能[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031]。
4. 纳入增强质量传递和热性能的结构设计[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：关于ODHP反应的B基催化剂的研究揭示了活性位点的空间分布、维度和性能之间的复杂相互作用。虽然一维边缘活性位点显示出promising结果，但在催化剂设计中整合一维、二维和三维结构可能会导致更高效的系统。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 开发结合一维边缘位点与二维和三维结构优势的分级催化剂，以增强质量传递和稳定性。
2. 探索新型合成方法，精确控制活性位点的空间分布和维度。
3. 研究反应条件下活性位点的动态特性，以设计能够长期保持最佳性能的催化剂。
4. 利用先进的表征技术和原位研究，深入了解B基催化剂的结构-活性关系。
5. 探索将B基活性位点与其他元素或载体结合的混合材料的潜力，以在ODHP反应中实现协同效应。

这些研究方向可能导致开发出下一代B基催化剂，具有改进的活性、选择性和稳定性，适用于商业ODHP应用，有潜力革新丙烯生产技术。
