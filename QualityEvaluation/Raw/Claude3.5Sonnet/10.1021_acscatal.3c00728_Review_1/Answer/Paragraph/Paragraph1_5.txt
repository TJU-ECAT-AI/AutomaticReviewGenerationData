<English>
The spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in B-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions. Based on the comprehensive review of various studies, the active sites in B-based catalysts can be classified into three types: one-dimensional (1-D), two-dimensional (2-D), and three-dimensional (3-D) dispersed active sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1].

1-D Edge Active Sites:
The 1-D edge active sites, particularly in hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN) materials, have shown superior performance in ODHP reactions. These sites are linearly dispersed on the edges of 2-D h-BN sheets and boron nitride nanotubes (BNNTs) [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]. The edge sites, often hydroxylated (BOH groups), play a crucial role in the catalytic process [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62786-4]. The unique properties of these 1-D edge active sites contribute to high selectivity and efficiency in ODHP reactions [10.1002/cctc.201700004].

Reaction Mechanism:
The reaction mechanism on 1-D edge sites involves the dehydrogenation of edge BOH groups by O2, generating BO and HO2 radicals. These radicals then facilitate hydrogen abstraction from propane, leading to propylene formation [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028]. DFT calculations have revealed that the reaction starts with hydrogen abstraction from the second carbon of propane, followed by O-O bond cleavage to create B-O and N-O species [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1].

Performance and Selectivity:
h-BN catalysts with 1-D edge active sites have demonstrated remarkable performance, with propene selectivity as high as 79% at a propane conversion of 14%, outperforming conventional catalysts like V/SiO2 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]. Some studies have reported even higher selectivity, with edge hydroxylated boron nitride (BNOH) achieving propylene selectivity of 80.2% with minimal CO2 formation (0.5%) at a propane conversion of 20.6% [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040].

2-D and 3-D Active Sites:
While 1-D edge sites show promising results, 2-D and 3-D active site distributions also play important roles in catalyst performance. For instance, B2O3/SBA-15 catalysts with 2-D surface-distributed sites demonstrated higher activity due to abundant tricoordinated boroxol and hydroxylated linear boron species [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]. 3-D structures, such as honeycomb cordierite with straight channels, allow for high gas hourly space velocity (GHSV) and improved mass transfer [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040].

Challenges and Optimization:
Despite the excellent selectivity of 1-D edge active sites, some challenges exist, including the presence of an induction period, low density of edge active sites in 2-D materials, and stability issues [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]. To address these challenges, researchers have proposed strategies such as preoxygen modification of edge sites, increasing the density of edge sites by reducing the size of BN nanosheets, and creating 1-D shaped materials to increase the number of active sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1].

Future Directions:
Understanding the unique properties and reaction mechanisms of 1-D edge active sites contributes significantly to the design of more efficient catalysts for commercial applications. Future research should focus on optimizing the spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites, maximizing edge exposure, and improving catalyst stability and durability.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 
The comprehensive review of B-based catalysts for ODHP reactions reveals the critical role of spatial distribution and dimensionality of active sites in determining catalyst performance. While 1-D edge active sites, particularly in h-BN materials, have shown promising results, the interplay between 1-D, 2-D, and 3-D active site distributions offers opportunities for further optimization.

Future research directions may include:

1. Hybrid catalyst designs: Developing catalysts that combine the advantages of 1-D edge sites with 2-D and 3-D structures to optimize both selectivity and mass transfer properties.

2. In-situ characterization techniques: Advancing methods to study the dynamic behavior of active sites under reaction conditions, particularly the transformation of edge sites during ODHP reactions.

3. Computational modeling: Utilizing advanced computational techniques to predict and design optimal spatial distributions of active sites for specific ODHP applications.

4. Scalable synthesis methods: Developing industrially viable processes for creating catalysts with controlled dimensionality and high density of active edge sites.

5. Stability enhancement: Investigating novel approaches to improve the long-term stability of edge active sites without compromising their high selectivity and activity.

6. Structure-performance relationships: Conducting systematic studies to establish clear correlations between the spatial distribution of active sites and catalyst performance metrics.

7. Alternative B-based materials: Exploring new boron-containing compounds or composites that could offer unique active site distributions and enhanced catalytic properties.

By pursuing these research directions, the field of B-based catalysts for ODHP reactions could potentially achieve significant breakthroughs, leading to more efficient, selective, and commercially viable processes for propylene production.

<Chinese>
B基催化剂中活性位点的空间分布和维度对丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应的性能有显著影响。根据对各种研究的综合回顾，B基催化剂中的活性位点可以分为三种类型：一维（1-D）、二维（2-D）和三维（3-D）分散活性位点 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。

1-D边缘活性位点：
特别是在六方氮化硼（h-BN）材料中，1-D边缘活性位点在ODHP反应中表现出优异的性能。这些位点线性分布在2-D h-BN片和氮化硼纳米管（BNNTs）的边缘 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。边缘位点，通常是羟基化的（BOH基团），在催化过程中起着关键作用 [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62786-4]。这些1-D边缘活性位点的独特性质有助于ODHP反应中的高选择性和效率 [10.1002/cctc.201700004]。

反应机理：
1-D边缘位点上的反应机理涉及边缘BOH基团被O2脱氢，生成BO和HO2自由基。这些自由基然后促进丙烷的氢抽取，导致丙烯的形成 [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028]。DFT计算揭示，反应始于丙烷第二个碳原子的氢抽取，随后是O-O键断裂，形成B-O和N-O物种 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。

性能和选择性：
具有1-D边缘活性位点的h-BN催化剂表现出卓越的性能，在14%的丙烷转化率下，丙烯选择性高达79%，优于传统的V/SiO2催化剂 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。一些研究报告了更高的选择性，边缘羟基化的氮化硼（BNOH）在20.6%的丙烷转化率下实现了80.2%的丙烯选择性，同时CO2生成量极少（0.5%）[10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]。

2-D和3-D活性位点：
虽然1-D边缘位点显示出有希望的结果，但2-D和3-D活性位点分布在催化剂性能中也起着重要作用。例如，具有2-D表面分布位点的B2O3/SBA-15催化剂由于丰富的三配位硼氧环和羟基化线性硼物种而表现出更高的活性 [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]。3-D结构，如具有直通道的蜂窝状堇青石，允许高气体小时空速（GHSV）和改善的质量传递 [10.1016/j.cjche.2020.07.040]。

挑战和优化：
尽管1-D边缘活性位点具有优异的选择性，但仍存在一些挑战，包括存在诱导期、2-D材料中边缘活性位点密度低以及稳定性问题 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。为了解决这些挑战，研究人员提出了一些策略，如边缘位点的预氧化修饰、通过减小BN纳米片的尺寸来增加边缘位点的密度，以及创建1-D形状的材料以增加活性位点的数量 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_1]。

未来方向：
理解1-D边缘活性位点的独特性质和反应机理对设计更高效的商业应用催化剂有重大贡献。未来的研究应该集中在优化活性位点的空间分布和维度、最大化边缘暴露以及改善催化剂的稳定性和耐久性。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：
对ODHP反应的B基催化剂的综合回顾揭示了活性位点的空间分布和维度在决定催化剂性能方面的关键作用。虽然h-BN材料中的1-D边缘活性位点显示出有希望的结果，但1-D、2-D和3-D活性位点分布之间的相互作用为进一步优化提供了机会。

未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 混合催化剂设计：开发结合1-D边缘位点优势与2-D和3-D结构的催化剂，以优化选择性和质量传递性能。

2. 原位表征技术：改进研究反应条件下活性位点动态行为的方法，特别是ODHP反应过程中边缘位点的转变。

3. 计算模型：利用先进的计算技术预测和设计特定ODHP应用的最佳活性位点空间分布。

4. 可扩展合成方法：开发工业可行的过程，用于创建具有可控维度和高密度活性边缘位点的催化剂。

5. 稳定性增强：研究新的方法来改善边缘活性位点的长期稳定性，同时不影响其高选择性和活性。

6. 结构-性能关系：进行系统研究，建立活性位点空间分布与催化剂性能指标之间的明确相关性。

7. 替代B基材料：探索可能提供独特活性位点分布和增强催化性能的新型含硼化合物或复合材料。

通过追求这些研究方向，ODHP反应的B基催化剂领域可能实现重大突破，导致更高效、更具选择性和商业可行性的丙烯生产过程。
