Among the different polymorphs (α-, β-, γ-, and δ-Ga2O3), β- and γ-Ga2O3 exhibit superior performance in C3H8 conversion and C3H6 selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4]. This superior activity can be attributed to their structural characteristics and surface properties.

Structural Characteristics:
β-Ga2O3 has a monoclinic structure with gallium atoms equally distributed between tetrahedral and octahedral configurations [10.1021/cr5002436]. Both β- and γ-Ga2O3 contain tetrahedral Ga cations in their structure, which form coordinatively unsaturated Ga3+ sites acting as strong Lewis acid sites. In contrast, α- and δ-Ga2O3 are constituted by octahedral Ga3+, which are more coordinated and exhibit weaker acidity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4].

Surface Properties:
The surface acidity density decreases in the order of β > γ > δ > α, with surface basicity following the same trend [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4]. This correlation between surface acidity and catalytic activity highlights the importance of acidic sites in CO2-ODHP reactions. The presence of weak Lewis acid sites, particularly coordinatively unsaturated tetrahedral Ga3+ cations, is crucial for dehydrogenation activity [10.1021/cr5002436].

Lewis Acid Sites:
The population of catalytically relevant coordinatively unsaturated surface (cus) Ga species is a strong function of the surface area of Ga2O3 among different polymorphs [10.1016/j.catcom.2012.11.004]. These sites are essential for hydrocarbon activation in a CO2 atmosphere. The proportion of tetrahedral Ga3+ sites correlates with weak acid sites, implying their essential role in creating surface acidity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4].

Reaction Mechanisms:
The CO2-ODHP reaction over Ga2O3 catalysts proceeds through different mechanisms during the initial and steady-state stages:

1. Initial stage: The reduction of Ga cations (Ga3+ to Ga+) occurs, benefiting the dehydrogenation of propane (DHP) [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4]. The initial activity is dependent on the surface acid density and maximizes on β-Ga2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4].

2. Steady-state: The reaction proceeds via heterolytic dissociation on Ga oxide without a redox cycle [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4]. The presence of acid sites facilitates certain reaction steps, providing an alternative pathway to promote C3H6 formation by bypassing the rate-determining step in C3H8 heterolytic dissociation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4].
