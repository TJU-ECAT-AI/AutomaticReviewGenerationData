The structure-property-performance relationships of Ga2O3 polymorphs in CO2-ODHP catalysis reveal a complex interplay between structural characteristics, surface properties, and reaction mechanisms that significantly influence catalytic activity and selectivity. 

Among the different Ga2O3 polymorphs (α-, β-, γ-, and δ-), β- and γ-Ga2O3 exhibit superior performance in C3H8 conversion and C3H6 selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4]. This superior activity is attributed to their unique structural characteristics and surface properties:

1. Structural Characteristics:
β-Ga2O3 has a monoclinic structure with gallium atoms equally distributed between tetrahedral and octahedral configurations [10.1021/cr5002436]. Both β- and γ-Ga2O3 contain tetrahedral Ga cations in their structure, which form coordinatively unsaturated Ga3+ sites acting as strong Lewis acid sites. In contrast, α- and δ-Ga2O3 are constituted by octahedral Ga3+, which are more coordinated and exhibit weaker acidity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4].

2. Surface Properties:
The surface acidity density decreases in the order of β > γ > δ > α, with surface basicity following the same trend [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4]. This correlates strongly with the catalytic activity trend observed. The presence of coordinatively unsaturated surface (cus) Ga3+ sites is crucial for hydrocarbon activation in a CO2 atmosphere [10.1016/j.catcom.2012.11.004].

3. Lewis Acid Sites:
The concentration and strength of Lewis acid sites, particularly those associated with tetrahedral Ga3+ ions, play a crucial role in determining catalytic performance and stability [10.1039/C5CY02161H]. Weak Lewis acid sites, specifically weak Lewis acid site density on the surface, are crucial for achieving high activity and stability [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.03.021].

4. Reaction Mechanisms:
The proposed mechanism for CO2-ODHP reactions involves two stages:

a) Initial stage: Reduction of Ga cations (Ga3+ to Ga+) occurs, benefiting the dehydrogenation of propane (DHP) [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4].

b) Steady-state: The reaction proceeds via heterolytic dissociation on Ga oxide without a redox cycle [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4].

The reaction mechanism on a Ga2O3 (100) surface involves C-H bond activation when a surface oxygen abstracts a hydrogen from the propane molecule, forming a propyl species that coordinates with a Ga surface site and a hydroxyl group [10.1021/cr5002436].

5. Influence of CO2:
CO2 plays multiple roles in the reaction:
- It improves olefin yield by acting as a mild oxidant and shifting equilibrium towards product formation.
- It assists in decreasing coke deposition through the Boudouart reaction.
- CO2 helps with product desorption, particularly ethylene.
- However, CO2 can also competitively adsorb on basic sites, potentially negatively affecting performance [10.1021/cr5002436].

6. Pore Structure:
Mesoporosity facilitates the dehydrogenation reaction by allowing rapid diffusion of products, which may prevent side reactions and improve selectivity to propene [10.1016/j.catcom.2013.01.019].

Interestingly, weak surface acidity, specifically weak Lewis acid site density, is crucial for achieving high activity and stability, while medium-strong acid sites show a negative correlation with activity [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.03.021].

Reaction Mechanisms:
The CO2-ODHP reaction over Ga2O3 catalysts proceeds through different mechanisms during initial and steady-state stages:

However, CO2 can competitively adsorb on basic sites, potentially affecting performance negatively [10.1021/cr5002436].
