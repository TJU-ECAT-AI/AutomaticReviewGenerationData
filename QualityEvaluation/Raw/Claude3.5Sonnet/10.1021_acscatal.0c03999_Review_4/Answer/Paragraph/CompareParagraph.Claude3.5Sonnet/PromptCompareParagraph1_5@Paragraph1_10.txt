The structure-property-performance relationships of Ga2O3 polymorphs in CO2-ODHP catalysis are significantly influenced by their structural characteristics, surface properties, and Lewis acid sites. The following analysis synthesizes the key findings from the available literature:

1. Structural Characteristics:
β-Ga2O3, with its monoclinic structure, has gallium atoms equally distributed between tetrahedral and octahedral configurations [10.1021/cr5002436]. This unique structure results in a high concentration of weak Lewis acid sites on the surface, which are crucial for dehydrogenation activity. Both β- and γ-Ga2O3 contain tetrahedral Ga cations in their structure, while α- and δ-Ga2O3 are primarily composed of octahedral Ga3+ [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4].

2. Surface Properties:
The surface acidity density decreases in the order of β > γ > δ > α, with surface basicity following the same trend [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4]. This acidity trend correlates strongly with catalytic activity. The presence of weak Lewis acid sites, particularly coordinatively unsaturated surface (cus) Ga3+ sites, is crucial for hydrocarbon activation in a CO2 atmosphere [10.1016/j.catcom.2012.11.004].

3. Lewis Acid Sites:
Coordinatively unsaturated tetrahedral Ga3+ cations act as weak Lewis acid sites and are essential for dehydrogenation activity [10.1021/cr5002436]. The population of these catalytically relevant (cus) Ga species is directly related to the surface area of Ga2O3, which varies among different polymorphs [10.1016/j.catcom.2012.11.004].

4. Catalytic Performance:
β- and γ-Ga2O3 outperform α- and δ-Ga2O3 in C3H8 conversion and C3H6 selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4]. This superior performance is attributed to the abundance of surface medium-strong Lewis acid sites related to unsaturated Ga3+ cations [10.1039/C5CY02161H].

5. Reaction Mechanisms:
The proposed mechanism involves hydrocarbon activation on low-coordinated Ga3+ cations, forming propyl-Ga species, followed by the generation of dehydrogenation products through subsequent decomposition [10.1039/C5CY02161H]. During the initial stage, reduction of Ga cations (Ga3+ to Ga+) occurs, benefiting the dehydrogenation of propane. At steady-state, the reaction proceeds via heterolytic dissociation on Ga oxide without a redox cycle [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4].

6. Influence of CO2:
CO2 plays multiple roles in the reaction. It improves olefin yield by acting as a mild oxidant and shifting equilibrium towards product formation. It also assists in decreasing coke deposition through the Boudouart reaction and helps with product desorption. However, CO2 can competitively adsorb on basic sites, potentially affecting performance negatively [10.1021/cr5002436].

7. Support and Preparation Method:
The interaction between Ga2O3 and support materials (e.g., Al2O3) can enhance catalytic performance. γ-Ga2O3-Al2O3 solid solutions with suitable compositions showed higher activity than bulk γ-Ga2O3 [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.03.021]. The preparation method affects the distribution and strength of acid sites, with coprecipitation methods producing catalysts with a larger amount of surface Ga3+ sites with weak Lewis acidity [10.1039/C5CY02161H].

Additionally, in-situ characterization techniques could provide deeper insights into the dynamic changes of the catalyst surface during the reaction, especially the transition from the initial to the steady-state stage. The development of multifunctional catalysts that combine the advantages of different Ga2O3 polymorphs with other active components could lead to more efficient and stable catalytic systems for propane dehydrogenation.
