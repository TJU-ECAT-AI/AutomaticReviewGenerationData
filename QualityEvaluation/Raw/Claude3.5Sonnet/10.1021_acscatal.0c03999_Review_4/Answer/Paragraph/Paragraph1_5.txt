<English>
The structure-property-performance relationships of Ga2O3 polymorphs in CO2-ODHP catalysis are significantly influenced by their structural characteristics, surface properties, and Lewis acid sites. The following analysis synthesizes the key findings from the available literature:

1. Structural Characteristics:
β-Ga2O3, with its monoclinic structure, has gallium atoms equally distributed between tetrahedral and octahedral configurations [10.1021/cr5002436]. This unique structure results in a high concentration of weak Lewis acid sites on the surface, which are crucial for dehydrogenation activity. Both β- and γ-Ga2O3 contain tetrahedral Ga cations in their structure, while α- and δ-Ga2O3 are primarily composed of octahedral Ga3+ [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4].

2. Surface Properties:
The surface acidity density decreases in the order of β > γ > δ > α, with surface basicity following the same trend [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4]. This acidity trend correlates strongly with catalytic activity. The presence of weak Lewis acid sites, particularly coordinatively unsaturated surface (cus) Ga3+ sites, is crucial for hydrocarbon activation in a CO2 atmosphere [10.1016/j.catcom.2012.11.004].

3. Lewis Acid Sites:
Coordinatively unsaturated tetrahedral Ga3+ cations act as weak Lewis acid sites and are essential for dehydrogenation activity [10.1021/cr5002436]. The population of these catalytically relevant (cus) Ga species is directly related to the surface area of Ga2O3, which varies among different polymorphs [10.1016/j.catcom.2012.11.004].

4. Catalytic Performance:
β- and γ-Ga2O3 outperform α- and δ-Ga2O3 in C3H8 conversion and C3H6 selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4]. This superior performance is attributed to the abundance of surface medium-strong Lewis acid sites related to unsaturated Ga3+ cations [10.1039/C5CY02161H].

5. Reaction Mechanisms:
The proposed mechanism involves hydrocarbon activation on low-coordinated Ga3+ cations, forming propyl-Ga species, followed by the generation of dehydrogenation products through subsequent decomposition [10.1039/C5CY02161H]. During the initial stage, reduction of Ga cations (Ga3+ to Ga+) occurs, benefiting the dehydrogenation of propane. At steady-state, the reaction proceeds via heterolytic dissociation on Ga oxide without a redox cycle [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4].

6. Influence of CO2:
CO2 plays multiple roles in the reaction. It improves olefin yield by acting as a mild oxidant and shifting equilibrium towards product formation. It also assists in decreasing coke deposition through the Boudouart reaction and helps with product desorption. However, CO2 can competitively adsorb on basic sites, potentially affecting performance negatively [10.1021/cr5002436].

7. Support and Preparation Method:
The interaction between Ga2O3 and support materials (e.g., Al2O3) can enhance catalytic performance. γ-Ga2O3-Al2O3 solid solutions with suitable compositions showed higher activity than bulk γ-Ga2O3 [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.03.021]. The preparation method affects the distribution and strength of acid sites, with coprecipitation methods producing catalysts with a larger amount of surface Ga3+ sites with weak Lewis acidity [10.1039/C5CY02161H].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive analysis of Ga2O3 polymorphs in CO2-ODHP catalysis reveals the critical role of structural characteristics, particularly the presence of tetrahedral Ga3+ sites, in determining catalytic performance. Future research should focus on tailoring the synthesis methods to optimize the distribution and strength of Lewis acid sites, potentially through advanced techniques such as atomic layer deposition or precise doping strategies. Additionally, in-situ characterization techniques could provide deeper insights into the dynamic changes of the catalyst surface during the reaction, especially the transition from the initial to the steady-state stage. The development of multifunctional catalysts that combine the advantages of different Ga2O3 polymorphs with other active components could lead to more efficient and stable catalytic systems for propane dehydrogenation. Furthermore, computational studies using advanced DFT methods could help elucidate the detailed reaction mechanisms on different Ga2O3 surfaces, guiding the rational design of next-generation catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions.

<Chinese>
Ga2O3多态体在CO2-ODHP催化中的结构-性能-性能关系显著受到其结构特征、表面性质和Lewis酸位点的影响。以下分析综合了可用文献中的关键发现：

1. 结构特征：
β-Ga2O3具有单斜结构，其中镓原子在四面体和八面体构型之间均匀分布[10.1021/cr5002436]。这种独特的结构在表面形成高浓度的弱Lewis酸位点，这对脱氢活性至关重要。β-和γ-Ga2O3在结构中都包含四面体Ga阳离子，而α-和δ-Ga2O3主要由八面体Ga3+组成[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4]。

2. 表面性质：
表面酸度密度按β > γ > δ > α的顺序降低，表面碱度也遵循相同的趋势[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4]。这种酸度趋势与催化活性强烈相关。弱Lewis酸位点的存在，特别是配位不饱和表面(cus) Ga3+位点，对于CO2气氛中的碳氢化合物活化至关重要[10.1016/j.catcom.2012.11.004]。

3. Lewis酸位点：
配位不饱和的四面体Ga3+阳离子作为弱Lewis酸位点，对脱氢活性至关重要[10.1021/cr5002436]。这些具有催化相关性的(cus) Ga物种的数量与Ga2O3的表面积直接相关，而表面积在不同多态体之间有所不同[10.1016/j.catcom.2012.11.004]。

4. 催化性能：
在C3H8转化和C3H6选择性方面，β-和γ-Ga2O3的性能优于α-和δ-Ga2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4]。这种优越性能归因于与不饱和Ga3+阳离子相关的表面中强Lewis酸位点的丰富性[10.1039/C5CY02161H]。

5. 反应机理：
提出的机理涉及在低配位Ga3+阳离子上活化碳氢化合物，形成丙基-Ga物种，随后通过后续分解生成脱氢产物[10.1039/C5CY02161H]。在初始阶段，Ga阳离子的还原（Ga3+到Ga+）发生，有利于丙烷的脱氢。在稳态下，反应通过Ga氧化物上的异裂解离进行，不涉及氧化还原循环[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4]。

6. CO2的影响：
CO2在反应中扮演多重角色。它通过作为温和的氧化剂并将平衡向产物形成方向移动来改善烯烃产率。它还有助于通过Boudouart反应减少积炭沉积，并帮助产物脱附。然而，CO2可以在碱性位点上竞争性吸附，可能对性能产生负面影响[10.1021/cr5002436]。

7. 载体和制备方法：
Ga2O3与载体材料（如Al2O3）的相互作用可以增强催化性能。具有合适组成的γ-Ga2O3-Al2O3固溶体显示出比纯γ-Ga2O3更高的活性[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.03.021]。制备方法影响酸性位点的分布和强度，共沉淀法产生的催化剂具有更多的弱Lewis酸性表面Ga3+位点[10.1039/C5CY02161H]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：对Ga2O3多态体在CO2-ODHP催化中的全面分析揭示了结构特征，特别是四面体Ga3+位点的存在，在决定催化性能方面的关键作用。未来的研究应关注定制合成方法，以优化Lewis酸位点的分布和强度，可能通过原子层沉积或精确掺杂策略等先进技术实现。此外，原位表征技术可以提供对催化剂表面在反应过程中动态变化的更深入了解，特别是从初始阶段到稳态阶段的转变。开发结合不同Ga2O3多态体优势与其他活性组分的多功能催化剂可能导致更高效和稳定的丙烷脱氢催化系统。此外，使用先进DFT方法的计算研究可以帮助阐明不同Ga2O3表面上的详细反应机理，指导下一代CO2-ODHP反应催化剂的理性设计。
