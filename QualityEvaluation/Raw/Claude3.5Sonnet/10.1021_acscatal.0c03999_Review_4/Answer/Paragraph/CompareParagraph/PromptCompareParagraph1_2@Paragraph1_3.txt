1. Structural Characteristics:
β-Ga2O3, with its monoclinic structure, has gallium atoms equally distributed between tetrahedral and octahedral configurations [10.1021/cr5002436]. This unique structure results in a high concentration of coordinatively unsaturated surface (cus) Ga3+ sites, which are crucial for hydrocarbon activation in a CO2 atmosphere [10.1016/j.catcom.2012.11.004]. Among the different polymorphs, β- and γ-Ga2O3 contain tetrahedral Ga cations, while α- and δ-Ga2O3 are constituted by octahedral Ga3+ [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4].

2. Surface Properties:
The surface acidity density decreases in the order of β > γ > δ > α, with surface basicity following the same trend [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4]. This correlates with the catalytic activity trend, where β- and γ-Ga2O3 outperform α- and δ-Ga2O3 in C3H8 conversion and C3H6 selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4]. The weak acid site density is crucial for achieving high activity and stability, while medium-strong acid sites show a negative correlation with activity [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.03.021].

3. Lewis Acid Sites:
Coordinatively unsaturated tetrahedral Ga3+ cations act as weak Lewis acid sites and are crucial for dehydrogenation activity [10.1021/cr5002436]. The population of catalytically relevant (cus) Ga species is directly related to the surface area of Ga2O3, which varies among different polymorphs [10.1016/j.catcom.2012.11.004]. β- and γ-Ga2O3 contain tetrahedral Ga cations, forming coordinatively unsaturated Ga3+ sites that act as strong Lewis acid sites [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4].

4. Reaction Mechanisms:
The reaction mechanism involves two stages:
a) Initial stage: Reduction of Ga cations (Ga3+ to Ga+) occurs, benefiting the dehydrogenation of propane (DHP) [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_4].
