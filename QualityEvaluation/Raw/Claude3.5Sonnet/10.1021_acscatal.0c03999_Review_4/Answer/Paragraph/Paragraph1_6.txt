<English>
The structure-property-performance relationships of Ga2O3 polymorphs in CO2-ODHP catalysis are significantly influenced by their structural characteristics, surface properties, and Lewis acid sites. Among the different polymorphs (α-, β-, γ-, and δ-Ga2O3), β- and γ-Ga2O3 demonstrate superior catalytic performance in terms of C3H8 conversion and C3H6 selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4].

Structural Characteristics:
1. β-Ga2O3 has a monoclinic structure with gallium atoms equally distributed between tetrahedral and octahedral configurations [10.1021/cr5002436].
2. Both β- and γ-Ga2O3 contain tetrahedral Ga cations, while α- and δ-Ga2O3 are primarily constituted by octahedral Ga3+ [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4].

Surface Properties:
1. Surface acidity and basicity: The surface acidity density decreases in the order of β > γ > δ > α, with surface basicity following the same trend [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4].
2. Weak Lewis acid sites, particularly coordinatively unsaturated tetrahedral Ga3+ cations, are crucial for dehydrogenation activity [10.1021/cr5002436].
3. The weak acid site density for gallia-alumina mixed oxides is approximately linearly related to the tetrahedral Ga3+ population [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.03.021].

Lewis Acid Sites:
1. β- and γ-Ga2O3 contain coordinatively unsaturated Ga3+ sites that act as strong Lewis acid sites [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4].
2. α- and δ-Ga2O3 have octahedral Ga3+ with weaker acidity but may undergo reconstruction to form tetrahedral sites [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4].
3. The proportion of Ga(IV) sites correlates to weak acid sites, implying the essential role of tetrahedral surface Ga3+ sites in creating surface acidity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4].

Catalytic Performance:
1. Initial activity is dependent on surface acid density, maximizing on β-Ga2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4].
2. Surface base site density also correlates with initial activity, demonstrating a promotional effect in the initial reaction period [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4].
3. γ-Ga2O3-Al2O3 solid solutions with suitable compositions can be more active than bulk γ-Ga2O3, despite lower Ga2O3 content [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.03.021].

Reaction Mechanisms:
1. Initial stage: Reduction of Ga cations (Ga3+ to Ga+) occurs, benefiting propane dehydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4].
2. Steady-state: The reaction proceeds via heterolytic dissociation on Ga oxide without a redox cycle [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4].
3. C-H bond activation occurs when a surface oxygen abstracts a hydrogen from the propane molecule, forming a propyl species that coordinates with a Ga surface site and a hydroxyl group [10.1021/cr5002436].

Influence of CO2:
1. CO2 improves olefin yield by acting as a mild oxidant and shifting equilibrium towards product formation [10.1021/cr5002436].
2. It assists in decreasing coke deposition through the Boudouart reaction and helps with product desorption [10.1021/cr5002436].
3. However, CO2 can also competitively adsorb on basic sites, potentially negatively affecting performance [10.1021/cr5002436].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive analysis of Ga2O3 polymorphs in CO2-ODHP catalysis reveals the crucial role of structural characteristics, surface properties, and Lewis acid sites in determining catalytic performance. Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Developing novel synthesis methods to optimize the distribution of tetrahedral and octahedral Ga3+ sites in Ga2O3 catalysts, potentially enhancing their catalytic activity and selectivity.

2. Investigating the synergistic effects between Ga2O3 and other metal oxides or supports to create more efficient and stable catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions.

3. Exploring in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and surface properties during the reaction, particularly during the transition from initial to steady-state stages.

4. Developing advanced computational models to predict the performance of various Ga2O3 polymorphs and guide the design of next-generation catalysts for propane dehydrogenation.

5. Investigating the potential of Ga2O3-based catalysts in other related reactions, such as the valorization of other light alkanes or the utilization of CO2 in different chemical transformations.

<Chinese>
Ga2O3多形体在CO2-ODHP催化中的结构-性质-性能关系受到其结构特征、表面性质和路易斯酸位点的显著影响。在不同的多形体（α-、β-、γ-和δ-Ga2O3）中，β-和γ-Ga2O3在C3H8转化率和C3H6选择性方面表现出优异的催化性能[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4]。

结构特征：
1. β-Ga2O3具有单斜结构，镓原子均匀分布在四面体和八面体构型之间[10.1021/cr5002436]。
2. β-和γ-Ga2O3都含有四面体配位的Ga阳离子，而α-和δ-Ga2O3主要由八面体配位的Ga3+构成[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4]。

表面性质：
1. 表面酸度和碱度：表面酸度密度按β > γ > δ > α的顺序递减，表面碱度也遵循相同的趋势[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4]。
2. 弱路易斯酸位点，特别是配位不饱和的四面体Ga3+阳离子，对脱氢活性至关重要[10.1021/cr5002436]。
3. 镓-氧化铝混合氧化物的弱酸位点密度与四面体Ga3+数量呈近似线性关系[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.03.021]。

路易斯酸位点：
1. β-和γ-Ga2O3含有配位不饱和的Ga3+位点，作为强路易斯酸位点[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4]。
2. α-和δ-Ga2O3具有八面体配位的Ga3+，酸性较弱，但可能会发生重构形成四面体位点[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4]。
3. Ga(IV)位点的比例与弱酸位点相关，表明表面四面体Ga3+位点在创造表面酸性方面起着关键作用[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4]。

催化性能：
1. 初始活性取决于表面酸密度，在β-Ga2O3上达到最大值[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4]。
2. 表面碱位点密度也与初始活性相关，显示出在反应初期的促进作用[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4]。
3. 具有适当组成的γ-Ga2O3-Al2O3固溶体可能比纯γ-Ga2O3更活跃，尽管Ga2O3含量较低[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.03.021]。

反应机理：
1. 初始阶段：发生Ga阳离子的还原（Ga3+到Ga+），有利于丙烷脱氢[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4]。
2. 稳态：反应通过Ga氧化物上的异裂解离进行，没有氧化还原循环[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_4]。
3. C-H键活化发生在表面氧原子从丙烷分子中提取氢原子时，形成与Ga表面位点和羟基配位的丙基物种[10.1021/cr5002436]。

CO2的影响：
1. CO2通过作为温和的氧化剂并将平衡向产物形成方向移动来改善烯烃产率[10.1021/cr5002436]。
2. 它通过布多瓦反应帮助减少积碳沉积，并协助产物脱附[10.1021/cr5002436]。
3. 然而，CO2也可以在催化剂材料的碱性位点上竞争性吸附，可能会对性能产生负面影响[10.1021/cr5002436]。

综合理解与未来展望：对Ga2O3多形体在CO2-ODHP催化中的全面分析揭示了结构特征、表面性质和路易斯酸位点在决定催化性能方面的关键作用。未来的研究努力可能会集中在：

1. 开发新的合成方法，优化Ga2O3催化剂中四面体和八面体Ga3+位点的分布，可能提高其催化活性和选择性。

2. 研究Ga2O3与其他金属氧化物或载体之间的协同效应，以创造更高效和稳定的CO2-ODHP反应催化剂。

3. 探索原位表征技术，以更好地理解反应过程中催化剂结构和表面性质的动态变化，特别是在从初始阶段到稳态阶段的转变过程中。

4. 开发先进的计算模型，预测各种Ga2O3多形体的性能，并指导下一代丙烷脱氢催化剂的设计。

5. 研究Ga2O3基催化剂在其他相关反应中的潜力，如其他轻烷烃的增值或CO2在不同化学转化中的利用。
