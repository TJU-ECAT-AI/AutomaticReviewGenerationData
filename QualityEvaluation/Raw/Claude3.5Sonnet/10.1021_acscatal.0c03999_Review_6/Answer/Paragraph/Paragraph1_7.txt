<English>
The reducibility and support materials of Fe oxide-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in CO2-ODHP reactions. Key findings from the literature review include:

1. Support Material:
Active carbon, particularly low-ash content varieties like D-90, has been found to be the most effective support for Fe oxide-based catalysts in CO2-ODHP reactions [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]. Other promising support materials include CeO2, which has shown excellent properties for CO2 activation and coke suppression due to its oxygen storage capacity and ability to reversibly convert between Ce4+ and Ce3+ [10.1002/aic.16670]. The choice of support material can significantly affect catalyst performance, with some supports like γ-Al2O3 leading to the formation of hardly reducible phases that hinder catalyst activity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6].

2. Reducibility:
The reducibility of Fe oxide catalysts plays a crucial role in their performance. Fe2O3 supported on activated carbon exhibits improved reducibility, following the pathway Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]. However, the relationship between reducibility and catalyst activity is complex. In some cases, maintaining Fe in an oxidized state while keeping other metal components (e.g., Ni) in a metallic form has been found to be beneficial for propylene selectivity [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

3. Promoters and Additives:
The addition of alkali metal oxides (Li, Na, K) and noble metals (Pd, Ag, Pt) can significantly enhance catalyst activity and selectivity [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7] [10.1007/s11144-008-5339-x]. These promoters can improve reducibility, facilitate CO2 activation, and help maintain catalyst surface area. The optimal loading of these additives (e.g., 15 wt% for Pd) results in the best catalytic performance [10.1007/s11144-008-5339-x].

4. Reaction Mechanism:
The proposed reaction mechanism for CO2-ODHP over Fe oxide-based catalysts involves two main steps: (1) C3H8 oxidation to C3H6 using lattice oxygen from Fe2O3 or Fe3O4, and (2) reoxidation of the reduced Fe oxide by CO2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]. The presence of CO2 can also increase propane conversion through the reverse water gas shift reaction [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

5. Catalyst Stability:
Catalyst deactivation mechanisms vary depending on Fe loading and support material. Low Fe catalysts tend to deactivate due to coke deposition, while high Fe loading catalysts may suffer from support sintering and Fe migration [10.1039/D0CY00586J].

To optimize catalytic systems for CO2-ODHP reactions, researchers should focus on:
1. Selecting support materials that enhance Fe oxide reducibility and CO2 activation, such as activated carbon or CeO2.
2. Tailoring the Fe loading and incorporating appropriate promoters to achieve optimal performance.
3. Designing catalysts that facilitate efficient redox cycling of Fe oxide while maintaining the desired oxidation state.
4. Balancing the composition of bimetallic catalysts to leverage the beneficial properties of each component.
5. Enhancing catalyst stability by minimizing coke formation and preventing metal sintering.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 
The complex interplay between Fe oxide, support materials, and promoters in CO2-ODHP reactions presents both challenges and opportunities for future research. While significant progress has been made in understanding the role of reducibility and support materials, there is still room for improvement in catalyst design and performance.

Future research directions may include:
1. Developing novel support materials or composite supports that combine the beneficial properties of multiple materials (e.g., high surface area, oxygen storage capacity, and CO2 activation ability).
2. Exploring advanced synthesis techniques to create more uniform and stable Fe oxide-based catalysts with controlled reducibility and oxidation states.
3. Investigating the potential of other transition metal oxides or mixed metal oxides in combination with Fe oxide to create more efficient and selective catalysts.
4. Utilizing in-situ characterization techniques to gain deeper insights into the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation state during CO2-ODHP reactions.
5. Exploring the use of machine learning and high-throughput screening methods to accelerate the discovery and optimization of new catalyst compositions and structures.
6. Investigating the scalability and long-term stability of promising catalyst systems for potential industrial applications.

By addressing these challenges and exploring new avenues, researchers can work towards developing more efficient, selective, and stable catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions, potentially leading to more sustainable and economically viable processes for propylene production.

<Chinese>
Fe氧化物基催化剂的可还原性和载体材料对其在CO2-ODHP反应中的性能有显著影响。文献综述的主要发现包括：

1. 载体材料：
活性炭，尤其是低灰分含量的D-90等品种，被发现是Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中最有效的载体[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]。其他有前景的载体材料包括CeO2，由于其氧存储能力和在Ce4+和Ce3+之间可逆转换的能力，显示出优秀的CO2活化和焦炭抑制性能[10.1002/aic.16670]。载体材料的选择可以显著影响催化剂性能，一些载体如γ-Al2O3会导致难以还原的相的形成，从而阻碍催化剂活性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]。

2. 可还原性：
Fe氧化物催化剂的可还原性在其性能中起着至关重要的作用。负载在活性炭上的Fe2O3表现出改善的可还原性，遵循Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe的还原路径[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]。然而，可还原性与催化剂活性之间的关系是复杂的。在某些情况下，保持Fe处于氧化态而其他金属组分（如Ni）处于金属态被发现对丙烯选择性有利[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

3. 助剂和添加剂：
添加碱金属氧化物（Li、Na、K）和贵金属（Pd、Ag、Pt）可以显著提高催化剂的活性和选择性[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7] [10.1007/s11144-008-5339-x]。这些助剂可以改善可还原性，促进CO2活化，并有助于维持催化剂的表面积。这些添加剂的最佳负载量（例如，Pd为15 wt%）可以获得最佳的催化性能[10.1007/s11144-008-5339-x]。

4. 反应机理：
Fe氧化物基催化剂上CO2-ODHP的提议反应机理包括两个主要步骤：（1）使用来自Fe2O3或Fe3O4的晶格氧将C3H8氧化为C3H6，（2）CO2重新氧化还原后的Fe氧化物[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]。CO2的存在还可以通过逆水气变换反应增加丙烷转化率[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

5. 催化剂稳定性：
催化剂失活机制因Fe负载量和载体材料而异。低Fe催化剂倾向于由于焦炭沉积而失活，而高Fe负载催化剂可能会遭受载体烧结和Fe迁移的影响[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。

为了优化CO2-ODHP反应的催化系统，研究人员应该关注：
1. 选择能够增强Fe氧化物可还原性和CO2活化的载体材料，如活性炭或CeO2。
2. 调整Fe负载量并引入适当的助剂以达到最佳性能。
3. 设计能够促进Fe氧化物高效氧化还原循环的催化剂，同时保持所需的氧化态。
4. 平衡双金属催化剂的组成，以利用每个组分的有益特性。
5. 通过最小化焦炭形成和防止金属烧结来增强催化剂稳定性。

综合理解与未来展望：
Fe氧化物、载体材料和助剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的复杂相互作用为未来研究带来了挑战和机遇。虽然在理解可还原性和载体材料的作用方面已经取得了重大进展，但在催化剂设计和性能方面仍有改进的空间。

未来的研究方向可能包括：
1. 开发新型载体材料或复合载体，结合多种材料的有益特性（如高比表面积、氧存储能力和CO2活化能力）。
2. 探索先进的合成技术，创造更均匀和稳定的Fe氧化物基催化剂，具有可控的可还原性和氧化态。
3. 研究其他过渡金属氧化物或混合金属氧化物与Fe氧化物结合的潜力，以创造更高效和选择性的催化剂。
4. 利用原位表征技术，深入了解CO2-ODHP反应过程中催化剂结构和氧化态的动态变化。
5. 探索使用机器学习和高通量筛选方法，以加速新催化剂组成和结构的发现和优化。
6. 研究有前景的催化剂系统的可扩展性和长期稳定性，以用于潜在的工业应用。

通过解决这些挑战并探索新的途径，研究人员可以致力于开发更高效、更具选择性和更稳定的CO2-ODHP反应催化剂，有可能导致更可持续和经济可行的丙烯生产工艺。
