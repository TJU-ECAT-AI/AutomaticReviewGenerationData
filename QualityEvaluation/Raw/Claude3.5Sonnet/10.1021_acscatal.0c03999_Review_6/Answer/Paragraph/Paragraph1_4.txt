<English>
The reducibility and support materials of Fe oxide-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in CO2-ODHP (oxidative dehydrogenation of propane) reactions. Key findings from the literature review include:

1. Support Material:
Active carbon, particularly low-ash content varieties like D-90, has been found to be the most effective support for Fe oxide catalysts [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]. CeO2 is also identified as a promising support due to its oxygen storage/release capability, which facilitates CO2 activation and suppresses coke formation [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. The choice of support material can significantly impact catalyst performance, with SiO2-supported catalysts showing unique benefits from CO2 promotion in some cases [10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4].

2. Reducibility:
The reducibility of Fe oxide plays a crucial role in CO2-ODHP reactions. Fe2O3 supported on activated carbon (AC) exhibits improved reducibility, following the pathway Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]. However, the reducibility can be retarded when Fe2O3 is supported on γ-Al2O3 or in hydrotalcite-derived FeMg catalysts due to strong interactions with the support [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6].

3. Active Phase and Oxidation State:
The formation of potassium ferrite on the catalyst surface is proposed as the active phase for some Fe oxide catalysts [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]. In situ XANES measurements revealed that Fe in Fe3Ni catalysts remained in an oxidized state under reaction conditions, while Ni was in a metallic form [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. This oxidized state of Fe appears to be crucial for promoting selective formation of propylene.

4. Promoters and Additives:
The addition of alkali metal oxides (Li, Na, K) significantly enhances catalyst activity, with an optimal alkali metal to Fe atomic ratio of 0.1 [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]. Noble metals (Pd, Ag, Pt) and other elements (Nb) added to Fe-based catalysts enhance their reducibility, which is closely related to improved catalytic activity and selectivity [10.1007/s11144-008-5339-x].

5. Reaction Mechanism:
The proposed reaction mechanism involves two main steps: (1) C3H8 oxidation to C3H6 using lattice oxygen from Fe2O3 or Fe3O4, and (2) reoxidation of the reduced Fe oxide by CO2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]. The presence of CO2 can also increase propane conversion through the reverse water gas shift reaction [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

6. Catalyst Stability and Deactivation:
Deactivation mechanisms vary depending on Fe loading. Low Fe catalysts deactivate primarily due to coke deposition, while high Fe loading catalysts suffer from support sintering and Fe migration to form nanocrystals [10.1039/D0CY00586J].

To optimize catalytic systems for CO2-ODHP reactions, researchers should focus on:
1. Using support materials that enhance Fe oxide reducibility and CO2 activation, such as activated carbon or CeO2.
2. Maintaining an optimal iron content (around 5 wt%) and incorporating appropriate promoters.
3. Controlling the oxidation state of Fe to maintain it in a partially oxidized form during the reaction.
4. Tailoring the surface structure and bimetallic composition to optimize CO2 adsorption and subsequent reaction steps.
5. Enhancing catalyst stability by minimizing coke formation and preventing sintering.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The complex interplay between Fe oxide, support materials, and promoters in CO2-ODHP reactions presents both challenges and opportunities for catalyst design. Future research directions could include:

1. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation state under reaction conditions.
2. Development of novel support materials or composite supports that combine the beneficial properties of multiple materials (e.g., CeO2-AC composites).
3. Exploration of advanced synthesis methods to create highly dispersed and stable Fe oxide species on supports.
4. Investigation of the role of defects and interfaces in CO2 activation and propane dehydrogenation.
5. Computational studies to predict optimal catalyst compositions and structures for enhanced performance.
6. Integration of machine learning approaches to accelerate catalyst discovery and optimization.
7. Exploration of sustainable and economically viable alternatives to noble metal promoters.
8. Scale-up studies to address challenges in translating lab-scale results to industrial applications.

By addressing these areas, researchers can work towards developing more efficient, stable, and selective catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions, potentially enabling more sustainable routes for propylene production.

<Chinese>
Fe氧化物基催化剂的可还原性和载体材料显著影响其在CO2-ODHP（丙烷氧化脱氢）反应中的性能。文献综述的主要发现包括：

1. 载体材料：
活性炭，特别是低灰分的D-90型，被发现是Fe氧化物催化剂最有效的载体[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]。CeO2也被认为是一种有前景的载体，因为它具有氧储存/释放能力，有助于CO2活化和抑制积碳形成[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。载体材料的选择可以显著影响催化剂性能，在某些情况下，SiO2负载的催化剂显示出CO2促进的独特优势[10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4]。

2. 可还原性：
Fe氧化物的可还原性在CO2-ODHP反应中起着至关重要的作用。负载在活性炭（AC）上的Fe2O3表现出改善的可还原性，遵循Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe的还原路径[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]。然而，当Fe2O3负载在γ-Al2O3上或在水滑石衍生的FeMg催化剂中时，由于与载体的强相互作用，可还原性会受到抑制[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]。

3. 活性相和氧化态：
一些Fe氧化物催化剂的活性相被认为是催化剂表面形成的钾铁氧化物[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]。原位XANES测量显示，在反应条件下，Fe3Ni催化剂中的Fe保持在氧化态，而Ni处于金属态[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。Fe的这种氧化态对于促进丙烯的选择性形成至关重要。

4. 促进剂和添加剂：
添加碱金属氧化物（Li、Na、K）显著提高了催化剂活性，最佳碱金属与Fe的原子比为0.1 [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]。添加到Fe基催化剂中的贵金属（Pd、Ag、Pt）和其他元素（Nb）增强了它们的可还原性，这与改善的催化活性和选择性密切相关[10.1007/s11144-008-5339-x]。

5. 反应机理：
提出的反应机理包括两个主要步骤：（1）使用来自Fe2O3或Fe3O4的晶格氧将C3H8氧化为C3H6，（2）CO2重新氧化还原的Fe氧化物[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]。CO2的存在还可以通过逆水气变换反应增加丙烷转化率[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

6. 催化剂稳定性和失活：
失活机制因Fe负载量而异。低Fe催化剂主要由于表面积碳而失活，而高Fe负载催化剂则因载体烧结和Fe迁移形成纳米晶体而失活[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。

为了优化CO2-ODHP反应的催化系统，研究人员应该关注：
1. 使用能够增强Fe氧化物可还原性和CO2活化的载体材料，如活性炭或CeO2。
2. 保持最佳铁含量（约5 wt%）并添加适当的促进剂。
3. 控制Fe的氧化态，使其在反应过程中保持部分氧化状态。
4. 调整表面结构和双金属组成，以优化CO2吸附和后续反应步骤。
5. 通过最小化积碳形成和防止烧结来增强催化剂稳定性。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：
Fe氧化物、载体材料和促进剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的复杂相互作用为催化剂设计带来了挑战和机遇。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 先进的原位表征技术，以更好地理解催化剂在反应条件下结构和氧化态的动态变化。
2. 开发新型载体材料或复合载体，结合多种材料的有益特性（例如，CeO2-AC复合材料）。
3. 探索先进的合成方法，在载体上创建高度分散和稳定的Fe氧化物物种。
4. 研究缺陷和界面在CO2活化和丙烷脱氢中的作用。
5. 计算研究，预测最佳催化剂组成和结构以提高性能。
6. 整合机器学习方法，加速催化剂的发现和优化。
7. 探索可持续和经济可行的贵金属促进剂替代品。
8. 进行放大研究，解决将实验室规模结果转化为工业应用的挑战。

通过解决这些领域的问题，研究人员可以致力于开发更高效、更稳定和更具选择性的CO2-ODHP反应催化剂，可能为丙烯生产开辟更可持续的途径。
