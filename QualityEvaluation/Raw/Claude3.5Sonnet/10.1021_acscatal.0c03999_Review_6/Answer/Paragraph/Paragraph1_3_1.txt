The reducibility and support materials of Fe oxide-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in CO2-ODHP reactions. Key findings include:

1. Support material: Active carbon, particularly low-ash content varieties like D-90, has been found to be the most effective support for Fe oxide-based catalysts [Placeholder_Of_DOI1]. CeO2 is also identified as a promising support due to its oxygen storage/release capability, which may facilitate CO2 activation and suppress coke formation [Placeholder_Of_DOI2].

2. Reducibility: The reducibility of Fe oxide plays a crucial role in catalyst performance. Fe2O3 supported on activated carbon (AC) shows the best performance in terms of C3H8 conversion and C3H6 yield, attributed to its improved reducibility [Placeholder_Of_DOI3]. However, the reducibility of Fe2O3 supported on γ-Al2O3 and hydrotalcite-derived FeMg catalysts is retarded due to strong interactions with the support [Placeholder_Of_DOI3].

3. Oxidation state: In situ XANES measurements revealed that under reaction conditions, Fe in Fe3Ni catalysts remained in an oxidized state, while Ni was in a metallic form [Placeholder_Of_DOI2]. This suggests that maintaining Fe in an oxidized state while keeping Ni metallic may be beneficial for propylene selectivity.

4. Promoters: The addition of alkali metal oxides (Li, Na, K) significantly enhanced catalyst activity, with an optimal alkali metal to Fe atomic ratio of 0.1 [Placeholder_Of_DOI1]. Noble metals (Pd, Ag, Pt) and other elements (Nb) also enhance the reducibility of Fe-based catalysts, improving catalytic activity and selectivity [Placeholder_Of_DOI4].

5. Reaction mechanism: The proposed mechanism involves two main steps: (1) C3H8 oxidation to C3H6 using lattice oxygen from Fe2O3 or Fe3O4, and (2) reoxidation of the reduced Fe oxide by CO2 [Placeholder_Of_DOI3]. The presence of CO2 can also increase propane conversion through the reverse water gas shift reaction [Placeholder_Of_DOI2].

6. Interfacial active sites: DFT calculations on the FeO/Ni(111) interface showed that the oxidative dehydrogenation pathway (CH bond cleavage) was more favorable than the reforming pathway (CC bond cleavage) [Placeholder_Of_DOI2].

To optimize catalytic systems for CO2-ODHP reactions, focus should be placed on:
1. Using high-quality, low-ash content active carbon supports or reducible oxides like CeO2.
2. Enhancing catalyst reducibility through careful selection of additives and support materials.
3. Maintaining an optimal iron content (around 5 wt%) and incorporating appropriate promoters.
4. Designing catalysts that facilitate efficient redox cycling of Fe oxide.
5. Tailoring the surface structure to optimize CO2 adsorption and subsequent reaction steps.
6. Balancing the composition of bimetallic catalysts to leverage the beneficial properties of each component.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The complex interplay between Fe oxide, support materials, and promoters in CO2-ODHP reactions presents opportunities for innovative catalyst design. Future research could focus on developing hierarchical structures that combine the benefits of different support materials, such as CeO2-coated active carbon. This approach could leverage the high surface area and reducibility enhancement of carbon supports while utilizing the oxygen storage capacity and CO2 activation properties of CeO2.

Another promising avenue is the exploration of advanced synthesis techniques to create highly dispersed, single-atom Fe catalysts on optimized supports. This could maximize the number of active sites while maintaining the desired oxidation state of Fe throughout the reaction.

Furthermore, in-operando characterization techniques could be developed to provide real-time insights into the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation state during CO2-ODHP reactions. This information could be crucial for designing more stable and efficient catalysts.

Lastly, computational modeling could be employed to predict optimal catalyst compositions and structures, potentially leading to the discovery of novel, high-performance materials for CO2-ODHP processes.

Fe氧化物基催化剂的可还原性和支撑材料对其在CO2-ODHP反应中的性能有显著影响。主要发现包括：

1. 支撑材料：活性炭，特别是像D-90这样的低灰分品种，被发现是Fe氧化物基催化剂最有效的支撑材料[Placeholder_Of_DOI1]。CeO2也被认为是一种有前景的支撑材料，因为它具有储存/释放氧的能力，可能有助于CO2的活化和抑制积碳的形成[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]。

2. 可还原性：Fe氧化物的可还原性在催化剂性能中起着至关重要的作用。以活性炭（AC）为载体的Fe2O3在C3H8转化和C3H6产率方面表现最佳，这归因于其改善的可还原性[Placeholder_Of_DOI3]。然而，以γ-Al2O3和水滑石衍生的FeMg催化剂为载体的Fe2O3的可还原性由于与载体的强相互作用而受到抑制[Placeholder_Of_DOI3]。

3. 氧化态：原位XANES测量显示，在反应条件下，Fe3Ni催化剂中的Fe保持氧化态，而Ni处于金属态[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]。这表明在保持Ni金属态的同时维持Fe的氧化态可能有利于丙烯的选择性。

4. 促进剂：添加碱金属氧化物（Li、Na、K）显著提高了催化剂活性，最佳碱金属与Fe的原子比为0.1 [Placeholder_Of_DOI1]。贵金属（Pd、Ag、Pt）和其他元素（Nb）也能增强Fe基催化剂的可还原性，提高催化活性和选择性[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]。

5. 反应机理：提出的机理包括两个主要步骤：（1）使用来自Fe2O3或Fe3O4的晶格氧将C3H8氧化为C3H6，（2）CO2重新氧化还原的Fe氧化物[Placeholder_Of_DOI3]。CO2的存在还可以通过逆水气变换反应增加丙烷的转化率[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]。

6. 界面活性位点：对FeO/Ni(111)界面的DFT计算表明，氧化脱氢路径（C-H键断裂）比重整路径（C-C键断裂）更有利[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]。

为了优化CO2-ODHP反应的催化系统，应该关注：
1. 使用高质量、低灰分的活性炭载体或可还原氧化物如CeO2。
2. 通过精心选择添加剂和载体材料来增强催化剂的可还原性。
3. 保持最佳的铁含量（约5 wt%）并添加适当的促进剂。
4. 设计能促进Fe氧化物高效氧化还原循环的催化剂。
5. 调整表面结构以优化CO2吸附和后续反应步骤。
6. 平衡双金属催化剂的组成，以利用每种组分的有益特性。

综合理解与未来展望：Fe氧化物、载体材料和促进剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的复杂相互作用为创新催化剂设计提供了机会。未来的研究可以集中在开发结合不同载体材料优势的分层结构上，例如CeO2涂覆的活性炭。这种方法可以利用碳载体的高比表面积和可还原性增强的特性，同时利用CeO2的氧储存能力和CO2活化特性。

另一个有前景的方向是探索先进的合成技术，在优化的载体上创建高度分散的单原子Fe催化剂。这可以最大化活性位点的数量，同时在整个反应过程中保持Fe所需的氧化态。

此外，可以开发原位表征技术，提供CO2-ODHP反应过程中催化剂结构和氧化态动态变化的实时信息。这些信息对于设计更稳定和高效的催化剂至关重要。

最后，可以使用计算机模拟来预测最佳的催化剂组成和结构，可能导致发现用于CO2-ODHP过程的新型高性能材料。
