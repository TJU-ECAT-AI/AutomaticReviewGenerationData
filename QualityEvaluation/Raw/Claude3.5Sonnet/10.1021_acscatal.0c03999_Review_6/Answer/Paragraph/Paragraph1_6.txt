The reducibility and support materials of Fe oxide-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in CO2-ODHP reactions. Key findings and insights include:

1. Support Material:
Active carbon, particularly low-ash content varieties like D-90, has been found to be the most effective support for Fe oxide catalysts in CO2-ODHP reactions [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]. CeO2 has also been identified as a promising support material due to its oxygen storage/release capability, which facilitates CO2 activation and suppresses coke formation [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. The choice of support material can significantly impact catalyst performance, with SiO2-supported catalysts showing unique benefits from CO2 promotion in some cases [10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4].

2. Reducibility:
The reducibility of Fe oxide plays a crucial role in determining catalyst performance. Fe2O3 supported on activated carbon (AC) exhibits improved reducibility, following the pathway Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe, which contributes to its high catalytic performance [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]. However, the reducibility can be affected by strong interactions with the support material, as observed in Fe2O3/γ-Al2O3 and hydrotalcite-derived FeMg catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6].

3. Active Phase and Oxidation State:
The formation of potassium ferrite on the catalyst surface has been proposed as the active phase for some Fe oxide-based catalysts [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]. In situ XANES measurements have revealed that Fe in Fe3Ni catalysts remains in an oxidized state under reaction conditions, while Ni is in a metallic form [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. This oxidized state of Fe appears to be crucial for favoring oxidative C-H bond cleavage in the CO2-ODHP pathway.

4. Promoters and Additives:
The addition of alkali metal oxides (Li, Na, K) and noble metals (Pd, Ag, Pt) to Fe-based catalysts enhances their reducibility and catalytic performance [10.1007/s11144-008-5339-x]. The optimal loading of these additives (e.g., 15 wt% for Pd) results in the best catalytic performance, attributed to synergistic effects that facilitate the activation of CO2 and propane.

5. Reaction Mechanism:
The proposed reaction mechanism for CO2-ODHP over Fe oxide-based catalysts involves two main steps: (1) C3H8 oxidation to C3H6 using lattice oxygen from Fe2O3 or Fe3O4, and (2) reoxidation of the reduced Fe oxide by CO2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]. The presence of CO2 can also increase propane conversion through the reverse water gas shift reaction [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

6. Catalyst Stability and Deactivation:
Deactivation mechanisms vary depending on Fe loading, with low Fe catalysts deactivating primarily due to coke deposition, while high Fe loading catalysts suffer from support sintering and Fe migration [10.1039/D0CY00586J]. The addition of promoters, such as alkali metal oxides, can help maintain the catalyst's specific surface area during reactions [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7].

To optimize catalytic systems for CO2-ODHP reactions, researchers should focus on:
1. Selecting support materials that enhance Fe oxide reducibility and CO2 activation, such as activated carbon or CeO2.
2. Tailoring the Fe loading and incorporating appropriate promoters to achieve optimal performance and stability.
3. Controlling the oxidation state of Fe to maintain it in a partially oxidized form during the reaction.
4. Designing catalysts that facilitate efficient redox cycling of Fe oxide with CO2 as the oxidant.
5. Balancing the catalyst composition to optimize selectivity towards desired products and minimize deactivation.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The complex interplay between Fe oxide, support materials, and promoters in CO2-ODHP catalysts presents both challenges and opportunities for future research. The findings suggest that the ideal catalyst should maintain Fe in a partially oxidized state while facilitating CO2 activation and efficient redox cycling. Future research directions could include:

1. Developing novel support materials or composite supports that combine the beneficial properties of multiple materials (e.g., CeO2-AC composites) to enhance both reducibility and stability.

2. Investigating the potential of advanced synthesis methods, such as atomic layer deposition or controlled precipitation techniques, to achieve precise control over the Fe oxide dispersion and its interaction with the support.

3. Exploring the use of in-operando characterization techniques to gain deeper insights into the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation state under reaction conditions.

4. Investigating the potential of multicomponent catalysts that combine Fe with other transition metals to create synergistic effects for improved performance and stability.

5. Developing strategies to tune the acid-base properties of the catalyst surface to optimize the balance between CO2 activation, propane activation, and product desorption.

6. Exploring the potential of structured catalysts or membrane reactors to enhance mass transfer and product selectivity in CO2-ODHP reactions.

7. Investigating the scalability and long-term stability of promising catalyst formulations under industrially relevant conditions to bridge the gap between laboratory studies and practical applications.

These research directions could lead to the development of more efficient, stable, and selective catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions, potentially enabling the industrial implementation of this environmentally friendly route to produce valuable olefins.

<Chinese>
Fe氧化物基催化剂的可还原性和支撑材料显著影响其在CO2-ODHP反应中的性能。主要发现和见解包括：

1. 支撑材料：
活性炭，特别是低灰分含量的D-90等品种，被发现是CO2-ODHP反应中Fe氧化物催化剂最有效的支撑材料[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]。CeO2也被确定为有前景的支撑材料，因为它具有氧储存/释放能力，有助于CO2活化和抑制积碳形成[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。支撑材料的选择可以显著影响催化剂性能，在某些情况下，SiO2支撑的催化剂显示出CO2促进的独特优势[10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4]。

2. 可还原性：
Fe氧化物的可还原性在决定催化剂性能方面起着关键作用。负载在活性炭（AC）上的Fe2O3表现出改善的可还原性，遵循Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe的还原路径，这有助于其高催化性能[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]。然而，可还原性可能受到与支撑材料的强相互作用的影响，如在Fe2O3/γ-Al2O3和水滑石衍生的FeMg催化剂中观察到的那样[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]。

3. 活性相和氧化态：
在一些Fe氧化物基催化剂表面形成的钾铁氧化物被提出作为活性相[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]。原位XANES测量揭示，在反应条件下，Fe3Ni催化剂中的Fe保持氧化态，而Ni处于金属态[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。Fe的这种氧化态似乎对于在CO2-ODHP路径中有利于氧化性C-H键断裂至关重要。

4. 助剂和添加剂：
向Fe基催化剂中添加碱金属氧化物（Li，Na，K）和贵金属（Pd，Ag，Pt）可以增强它们的可还原性和催化性能[10.1007/s11144-008-5339-x]。这些添加剂的最佳负载量（例如，Pd为15 wt%）可获得最佳催化性能，这归因于协同效应，有助于CO2和丙烷的活化。

5. 反应机理：
Fe氧化物基催化剂上CO2-ODHP的提议反应机理包括两个主要步骤：（1）使用来自Fe2O3或Fe3O4的晶格氧将C3H8氧化为C3H6，以及（2）CO2重新氧化还原的Fe氧化物[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]。CO2的存在还可以通过逆水气变换反应增加丙烷转化率[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

6. 催化剂稳定性和失活：
失活机制随Fe负载量而变化，低Fe催化剂主要由于积碳沉积而失活，而高Fe负载催化剂则因支撑材料烧结和Fe迁移而失活[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。添加助剂，如碱金属氧化物，可以帮助在反应过程中维持催化剂的比表面积[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]。

为了优化CO2-ODHP反应的催化系统，研究人员应该关注：
1. 选择能增强Fe氧化物可还原性和CO2活化的支撑材料，如活性炭或CeO2。
2. 调整Fe负载量并引入适当的助剂，以实现最佳性能和稳定性。
3. 控制Fe的氧化态，使其在反应过程中保持部分氧化状态。
4. 设计能促进Fe氧化物与CO2作为氧化剂进行有效氧化还原循环的催化剂。
5. 平衡催化剂组成，以优化目标产物的选择性并最小化失活。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：Fe氧化物、支撑材料和助剂在CO2-ODHP催化剂中的复杂相互作用为未来研究带来了挑战和机遇。这些发现表明，理想的催化剂应该保持Fe处于部分氧化状态，同时促进CO2活化和高效的氧化还原循环。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 开发新型支撑材料或复合支撑材料，结合多种材料的有益特性（例如，CeO2-AC复合材料），以增强可还原性和稳定性。

2. 研究先进合成方法的潜力，如原子层沉积或受控沉淀技术，以精确控制Fe氧化物的分散性及其与支撑材料的相互作用。

3. 探索使用原位表征技术，深入了解催化剂在反应条件下结构和氧化态的动态变化。

4. 研究将Fe与其他过渡金属结合的多组分催化剂的潜力，以创造协同效应，提高性能和稳定性。

5. 开发调节催化剂表面酸碱性的策略，以优化CO2活化、丙烷活化和产物脱附之间的平衡。

6. 探索结构化催化剂或膜反应器的潜力，以增强CO2-ODHP反应中的传质和产物选择性。

7. 在工业相关条件下研究有前景的催化剂配方的可扩展性和长期稳定性，以弥合实验室研究和实际应用之间的差距。

这些研究方向可能导致开发出更高效、稳定和选择性的CO2-ODHP反应催化剂，潜在地使这种环境友好的生产有价值烯烃的路线在工业中得以实施。
