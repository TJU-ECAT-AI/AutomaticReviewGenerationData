1. Support Material:
Active carbon, particularly low-ash content varieties like D-90, has been found to be the most effective support for Fe oxide catalysts [Placeholder_Of_DOI1]. CeO2 is also identified as a promising support due to its oxygen storage/release capability, which facilitates CO2 activation and suppresses coke formation [Placeholder_Of_DOI2]. The choice of support material significantly affects catalyst performance, with SiO2-supported catalysts showing unique benefits from CO2 promotion in some cases [Placeholder_Of_DOI3].

2. Reducibility:
The reducibility of Fe oxide plays a crucial role in catalytic performance. Fe2O3 supported on activated carbon (AC) shows improved reducibility, following the pathway Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe, which contributes to its high catalytic activity [Placeholder_Of_DOI4]. However, the reducibility can be retarded when Fe2O3 is supported on γ-Al2O3 or in hydrotalcite-derived FeMg catalysts due to strong interactions with the support [Placeholder_Of_DOI4].

3. Oxidation State:
In situ XANES measurements have revealed that under reaction conditions, Fe in Fe3Ni catalysts remained in an oxidized state, while Ni was in a metallic form [Placeholder_Of_DOI2]. This observation led to further investigation of the FeO/Ni interface as a potential active site for the reaction. The presence of oxidized Fe under reaction conditions is crucial for favoring oxidative C-H bond cleavage in the CO2-ODHP pathway [Placeholder_Of_DOI5].

4. Promoters and Additives:
The addition of alkali metal oxides (Li, Na, K) significantly enhances catalyst activity, with an optimal alkali metal to Fe atomic ratio of 0.1 [Placeholder_Of_DOI1]. Noble metals (Pd, Ag, Pt) and other elements (Nb) added to Fe-based catalysts enhance their reducibility, which is closely related to improved catalytic activity and selectivity [Placeholder_Of_DOI6].

5. Reaction Mechanism:
The proposed reaction mechanism for CO2-ODHP over Fe oxide-based catalysts involves two main steps: (1) C3H8 oxidation to C3H6 using lattice oxygen from Fe2O3 or Fe3O4, and (2) reoxidation of the reduced Fe oxide by CO2 [Placeholder_Of_DOI4]. The presence of CO2 can increase propane conversion through the reverse water gas shift reaction, which consumes H2 [Placeholder_Of_DOI2].

6. Active Sites:
The formation of potassium ferrite on the catalyst surface is proposed as the active phase, supporting an oxidative dehydrogenation mechanism rather than a simple reverse water-gas shift reaction [Placeholder_Of_DOI1]. DFT calculations on the FeO/Ni(111) interface showed that the oxidative dehydrogenation pathway (CH bond cleavage) was more favorable than the reforming pathway (CC bond cleavage) [Placeholder_Of_DOI2].

7. Catalyst Stability:
Deactivation mechanisms vary depending on Fe loading. Low Fe catalysts deactivate primarily due to coke deposition, while high Fe loading catalysts suffer from support sintering and Fe migration to form nanocrystals [Placeholder_Of_DOI5].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 

The complex interplay between Fe oxide, support materials, and promoters in CO2-ODHP reactions presents both challenges and opportunities for catalyst design. Future research directions could focus on:

1. Tailoring support materials: Developing hybrid supports that combine the benefits of different materials (e.g., CeO2-AC composites) to optimize reducibility, oxygen mobility, and CO2 activation simultaneously.

2. Fine-tuning oxidation states: Investigating novel synthesis methods or in-situ treatments to maintain Fe in a partially oxidized state during reactions, potentially enhancing selectivity and stability.

3. Exploring advanced bimetallic systems: Building on the insights from Fe-Ni catalysts, research could expand to other bimetallic combinations, focusing on controlling surface segregation and optimizing the synergy between different metal components.

4. Enhancing CO2 activation: Developing strategies to improve CO2 adsorption and activation on catalyst surfaces, possibly through the introduction of specific defect sites or dopants in support materials.

5. Addressing deactivation mechanisms: Investigating novel approaches to mitigate coke formation and metal sintering, such as core-shell structures or advanced encapsulation techniques.

6. In-situ characterization techniques: Developing and applying advanced in-situ spectroscopic and microscopic techniques to gain deeper insights into the dynamic changes of catalyst structure and oxidation states under reaction conditions.

7. Computational modeling: Utilizing advanced computational methods to predict and design optimal catalyst compositions and structures, focusing on the interplay between different components and their effects on reaction pathways.

These research directions could lead to the development of more efficient, selective, and stable catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions, contributing to the broader goal of utilizing CO2 as a feedstock for valuable chemical production.

Fe氧化物基催化剂的可还原性和载体材料显著影响其在CO2-ODHP反应中的性能。来自各种研究的关键发现为催化剂组分和反应机理之间的复杂相互作用提供了见解：

1. 载体材料：
活性炭，特别是低灰分含量的品种如D-90，被发现是Fe氧化物催化剂最有效的载体[Placeholder_Of_DOI1]。CeO2也被认为是一种有前景的载体，因为它具有储存/释放氧的能力，这有助于CO2的活化和抑制积碳形成[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]。载体材料的选择显著影响催化剂性能，在某些情况下，SiO2支持的催化剂显示出CO2促进的独特优势[Placeholder_Of_DOI3]。

2. 可还原性：
Fe氧化物的可还原性在催化性能中起着至关重要的作用。负载在活性炭（AC）上的Fe2O3显示出改善的可还原性，遵循Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe的还原路径，这有助于其高催化活性[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]。然而，当Fe2O3负载在γ-Al2O3上或在水滑石衍生的FeMg催化剂中时，由于与载体的强相互作用，可还原性可能会受到抑制[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]。

3. 氧化态：
原位XANES测量揭示，在反应条件下，Fe3Ni催化剂中的Fe保持氧化态，而Ni处于金属态[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]。这一观察结果导致进一步研究FeO/Ni界面作为反应的潜在活性位点。在反应条件下氧化Fe的存在对于通过CO2-ODHP途径促进氧化性C-H键断裂至关重要[Placeholder_Of_DOI5]。

4. 促进剂和添加剂：
碱金属氧化物（Li，Na，K）的添加显著提高了催化剂活性，最佳碱金属与Fe的原子比为0.1[Placeholder_Of_DOI1]。添加到Fe基催化剂中的贵金属（Pd，Ag，Pt）和其他元素（Nb）增强了它们的可还原性，这与改善的催化活性和选择性密切相关[Placeholder_Of_DOI6]。

5. 反应机理：
Fe氧化物基催化剂上CO2-ODHP的提议反应机理包括两个主要步骤：（1）使用来自Fe2O3或Fe3O4的晶格氧将C3H8氧化为C3H6，以及（2）由CO2重新氧化还原的Fe氧化物[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]。CO2的存在可以通过消耗H2的逆水煤气变换反应增加丙烷转化率[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]。

6. 活性位点：
在催化剂表面形成的钾铁氧化物被提出作为活性相，支持氧化脱氢机理而不是简单的逆水煤气变换反应[Placeholder_Of_DOI1]。对FeO/Ni(111)界面的DFT计算表明，氧化脱氢途径（CH键断裂）比重整途径（CC键断裂）更有利[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]。

7. 催化剂稳定性：
失活机制随Fe负载量而变化。低Fe催化剂主要由于表面积碳沉积而失活，而高Fe负载催化剂则因载体烧结和Fe迁移形成纳米晶体而失活[Placeholder_Of_DOI5]。

综合理解与未来展望：

Fe氧化物、载体材料和促进剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的复杂相互作用为催化剂设计带来了挑战和机遇。未来的研究方向可以集中在：

1. 定制载体材料：开发结合不同材料优势的混合载体（例如CeO2-AC复合材料），以同时优化可还原性、氧移动性和CO2活化。

2. 精细调节氧化态：研究新型合成方法或原位处理，以在反应过程中保持Fe处于部分氧化状态，可能增强选择性和稳定性。

3. 探索先进的双金属系统：基于Fe-Ni催化剂的见解，研究可以扩展到其他双金属组合，重点控制表面偏析和优化不同金属组分之间的协同作用。

4. 增强CO2活化：开发策略以改善催化剂表面的CO2吸附和活化，可能通过在载体材料中引入特定缺陷位或掺杂剂。

5. 解决失活机制：研究新颖的方法来缓解积碳形成和金属烧结，如核壳结构或先进的包覆技术。

6. 原位表征技术：开发和应用先进的原位光谱和显微技术，以深入了解反应条件下催化剂结构和氧化态的动态变化。

7. 计算模拟：利用先进的计算方法预测和设计最佳催化剂组成和结构，重点关注不同组分之间的相互作用及其对反应路径的影响。

这些研究方向可能导致开发出更高效、更具选择性和更稳定的CO2-ODHP反应催化剂，为更广泛的利用CO2作为有价值化学品生产原料的目标做出贡献。
