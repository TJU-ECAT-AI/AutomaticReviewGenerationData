The reducibility and support materials of Fe oxide-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in CO2-ODHP reactions. Key findings and insights include:

1. Support Material:
Active carbon, particularly low-ash content varieties like D-90, has been found to be the most effective support for Fe oxide catalysts in CO2-ODHP reactions [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]. Other promising support materials include CeO2, which has excellent oxygen storage capacity and can facilitate CO2 activation through direct C=O bond scission [10.1002/aic.16670, 10.1039/D0CY00586J].

2. Reducibility:
The reducibility of Fe oxide plays a crucial role in catalyst performance. Fe2O3 supported on activated carbon (AC) shows improved reducibility, following the pathway Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe, which contributes to its high catalytic activity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]. However, the reducibility of the support material itself may not be the primary factor in determining catalyst performance, as seen in studies with Ru-doped catalysts [10.1039/C6CY00011H].

3. Oxidation State:
Maintaining Fe in an oxidized state during the reaction appears to be beneficial for selectivity towards propylene. In situ XANES measurements revealed that Fe in Fe3Ni catalysts remained in an oxidized form under reaction conditions, while Ni was metallic [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

4. Promoters and Additives:
The addition of alkali metal oxides (Li, Na, K) and noble metals (Pd, Ag, Pt) can significantly enhance catalyst activity and selectivity. These promoters improve reducibility, facilitate CO2 activation, and help maintain catalyst stability [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7, 10.1007/s11144-008-5339-x].

5. Reaction Mechanism:
The proposed mechanism for CO2-ODHP over Fe oxide-based catalysts involves two main steps: (1) C3H8 oxidation to C3H6 using lattice oxygen from Fe2O3 or Fe3O4, and (2) reoxidation of the reduced Fe oxide by CO2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]. The reaction also involves competitive pathways such as reverse water gas shift and alkane dry reforming [10.039/D0CY00586J].

6. Catalyst Deactivation:
Deactivation mechanisms vary depending on Fe loading. Low Fe catalysts deactivate primarily due to coke deposition, while high Fe loading catalysts suffer from support sintering and Fe migration [10.039/D0CY00586J].

To optimize catalytic systems for CO2-ODHP reactions, researchers should focus on:
1. Using high-quality, low-ash content active carbon or CeO2 as support materials.
2. Tailoring the Fe loading and promoter content to achieve optimal reducibility and stability.
3. Designing catalysts that facilitate efficient redox cycling of Fe oxide while maintaining partial oxidation.
4. Incorporating promoters that enhance CO2 activation and suppress coke formation.
5. Optimizing the interaction between Fe oxide and the support material to prevent the formation of inactive phases.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 
The complex interplay between Fe oxide, support materials, and promoters in CO2-ODHP catalysts offers numerous opportunities for further research and development. Future studies could focus on:

1. Developing novel support materials or composites that combine the beneficial properties of active carbon and CeO2, such as high surface area and oxygen storage capacity.
2. Investigating the use of advanced characterization techniques, such as operando spectroscopy, to gain deeper insights into the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation state during reaction conditions.
3. Exploring the potential of MOFs or other porous materials as supports or precursors for Fe oxide catalysts, which could offer unique opportunities for controlling metal dispersion and redox properties.
4. Investigating the use of machine learning algorithms to predict optimal catalyst compositions and synthesis conditions based on the extensive data available from previous studies.
5. Developing strategies for precise control of Fe oxide particle size and morphology to optimize the balance between reducibility and stability.
6. Exploring the potential of bimetallic or multi-metallic systems that combine Fe with other transition metals to create synergistic effects in CO2 activation and propane dehydrogenation.

These research directions could lead to the development of more efficient, stable, and selective catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions, contributing to the broader goal of utilizing CO2 as a feedstock for valuable chemical production.

<Chinese>
Fe氧化物基催化剂的可还原性和载体材料对其在CO2-ODHP反应中的性能有显著影响。主要发现和洞见包括：

1. 载体材料：
活性炭，特别是低灰分含量的D-90等品种，被发现是CO2-ODHP反应中Fe氧化物催化剂最有效的载体 [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]。其他有前景的载体材料包括CeO2，它具有优异的氧储存能力，可以通过直接C=O键断裂促进CO2的活化 [10.1002/aic.16670, 10.1039/D0CY00586J]。

2. 可还原性：
Fe氧化物的可还原性在催化剂性能中起着关键作用。负载在活性炭（AC）上的Fe2O3显示出改善的可还原性，遵循Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe的还原路径，这有助于其高催化活性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]。然而，载体材料本身的可还原性可能不是决定催化剂性能的主要因素，这在Ru掺杂催化剂的研究中得到了证实 [10.1039/C6CY00011H]。

3. 氧化态：
在反应过程中保持Fe的氧化态似乎有利于丙烯的选择性。原位XANES测量揭示，在反应条件下，Fe3Ni催化剂中的Fe保持氧化形式，而Ni为金属态 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

4. 促进剂和添加剂：
添加碱金属氧化物（Li，Na，K）和贵金属（Pd，Ag，Pt）可以显著提高催化剂的活性和选择性。这些促进剂改善了可还原性，促进了CO2的活化，并有助于保持催化剂的稳定性 [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7, 10.1007/s11144-008-5339-x]。

5. 反应机理：
Fe氧化物基催化剂上CO2-ODHP的提议机理包括两个主要步骤：（1）使用来自Fe2O3或Fe3O4的晶格氧将C3H8氧化为C3H6，（2）CO2重新氧化还原的Fe氧化物 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]。反应还涉及竞争性路径，如逆水气变换和烷烃干重整 [10.039/D0CY00586J]。

6. 催化剂失活：
失活机制因Fe负载量而异。低Fe催化剂主要由于焦炭沉积而失活，而高Fe负载催化剂则因载体烧结和Fe迁移而失活 [10.039/D0CY00586J]。

为优化CO2-ODHP反应的催化系统，研究人员应关注：
1. 使用高质量、低灰分含量的活性炭或CeO2作为载体材料。
2. 调整Fe负载量和促进剂含量以实现最佳的可还原性和稳定性。
3. 设计可促进Fe氧化物高效氧化还原循环的同时保持部分氧化的催化剂。
4. 引入能增强CO2活化和抑制焦炭形成的促进剂。
5. 优化Fe氧化物与载体材料之间的相互作用，以防止形成无活性相。

综合理解与未来展望：
CO2-ODHP催化剂中Fe氧化物、载体材料和促进剂之间的复杂相互作用为进一步的研究和发展提供了众多机会。未来的研究可以集中在：

1. 开发新型载体材料或复合材料，结合活性炭和CeO2的有益特性，如高比表面积和氧储存能力。
2. 研究使用先进的表征技术，如原位光谱技术，以深入了解反应条件下催化剂结构和氧化态的动态变化。
3. 探索MOFs或其他多孔材料作为Fe氧化物催化剂的载体或前体的潜力，这可能为控制金属分散和氧化还原性能提供独特的机会。
4. 研究使用机器学习算法，基于先前研究的大量数据预测最佳催化剂组成和合成条件。
5. 开发精确控制Fe氧化物粒径和形貌的策略，以优化可还原性和稳定性之间的平衡。
6. 探索将Fe与其他过渡金属结合的双金属或多金属系统的潜力，以在CO2活化和丙烷脱氢中创造协同效应。

这些研究方向可能导致开发出更高效、更稳定和更具选择性的CO2-ODHP反应催化剂，为更广泛地利用CO2作为有价值化学品生产的原料做出贡献。
