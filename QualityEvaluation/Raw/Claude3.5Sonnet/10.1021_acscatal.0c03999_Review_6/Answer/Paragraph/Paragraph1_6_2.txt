The reducibility and support materials of Fe oxide-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in CO2-ODHP reactions. This comprehensive review synthesizes key findings from multiple studies to provide insights into the performance, reducibility, and reaction mechanism of Fe oxide-based catalysts in CO2-ODHP, emphasizing the role of support materials and the relationship between catalyst properties and catalytic activity.

1. Support Materials:
Active carbon, particularly low-ash content varieties like D-90, has been found to be the most effective support for Fe oxide-based catalysts in CO2-ODHP reactions [Placeholder_Of_DOI1]. CeO2 has also been identified as a promising support material due to its oxygen storage/release capability, which facilitates CO2 activation and suppresses coke formation [Placeholder_Of_DOI2]. The choice of support material significantly affects catalyst performance, with SiO2-supported catalysts showing unique benefits from CO2 promotion in some studies [Placeholder_Of_DOI3].

2. Reducibility and Oxidation State:
The reducibility of Fe oxide plays a crucial role in determining catalyst performance. Fe2O3 supported on activated carbon exhibits improved reducibility, following the pathway Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe [Placeholder_Of_DOI4]. However, the presence of oxidized Fe under reaction conditions is crucial for favoring oxidative C-H bond cleavage in the CO2-ODHP pathway [Placeholder_Of_DOI2]. This suggests that maintaining Fe in a partially oxidized state during the reaction is important for selectivity and activity.

3. Promoters and Additives:
The addition of alkali metal oxides (Li, Na, K) and noble metals (Pd, Ag, Pt) to Fe-based catalysts enhances their reducibility and catalytic performance [Placeholder_Of_DOI1] [Placeholder_Of_DOI5]. These promoters can create synergistic effects with Fe2O3, facilitating the activation of CO2 and propane, and generating more active sites through increased reduction of iron oxide.

4. Reaction Mechanism:
The proposed reaction mechanism for CO2-ODHP over Fe oxide-based catalysts involves two main steps: (1) C3H8 oxidation to C3H6 using lattice oxygen from Fe2O3 or Fe3O4, and (2) reoxidation of the reduced Fe oxide by CO2 [Placeholder_Of_DOI4]. This highlights the crucial role of the redox cycle of Fe oxide in the CO2-ODHP process. The presence of CO2 can also increase propane conversion through the reverse water gas shift reaction [Placeholder_Of_DOI2].

5. Catalyst Deactivation:
Deactivation mechanisms vary depending on Fe loading and support material. Low Fe catalysts tend to deactivate primarily due to coke deposition, while high Fe loading catalysts may suffer from support sintering and Fe migration [Placeholder_Of_DOI6]. The addition of promoters and careful selection of support materials can help mitigate these deactivation processes.

To optimize catalytic systems for CO2-ODHP reactions, researchers should focus on:
1. Selecting support materials that enhance Fe oxide reducibility while maintaining stability.
2. Tailoring the Fe loading and promoter composition to achieve optimal performance and stability.
3. Designing catalysts that facilitate efficient redox cycling of Fe oxide with CO2 as the oxidant.
4. Balancing the oxidation state of Fe to maintain activity and selectivity.
5. Incorporating promoters that enhance CO2 activation and suppress coke formation.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of Fe oxide-based catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions represents a promising avenue for sustainable propylene production. The complex interplay between support materials, Fe oxidation state, and promoters offers numerous opportunities for catalyst optimization. Future research directions may include:

1. Exploring novel support materials or composite supports that combine the beneficial properties of multiple materials (e.g., CeO2-activated carbon composites).
2. Investigating the potential of advanced synthesis techniques, such as atomic layer deposition or controlled precipitation methods, to achieve precise control over Fe oxide dispersion and oxidation state.
3. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation state under reaction conditions.
4. Exploring the potential of bimetallic or multi-metallic catalysts that combine Fe with other transition metals to achieve synergistic effects in CO2 activation and propane dehydrogenation.
5. Investigating the role of catalyst texture and porosity in determining performance and stability, potentially leading to hierarchical catalyst designs that optimize mass transfer and active site accessibility.
6. Exploring the integration of CO2-ODHP catalysts with membrane reactors or other process intensification strategies to enhance overall process efficiency and selectivity.

By addressing these research directions, the scientific community can work towards developing more efficient, stable, and selective Fe oxide-based catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions, contributing to the broader goal of sustainable chemical production and CO2 utilization.

Fe氧化物基催化剂的可还原性和支撑材料显著影响其在CO2-ODHP反应中的性能。这篇综合评述综合了多项研究的关键发现，提供了Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的性能、可还原性和反应机理的见解，强调了支撑材料的作用以及催化剂性质与催化活性之间的关系。

1. 支撑材料：
活性炭，特别是低灰分含量的D-90等品种，被发现是CO2-ODHP反应中Fe氧化物基催化剂最有效的支撑材料[Placeholder_Of_DOI1]。CeO2也被认为是一种有前景的支撑材料，因为它具有氧储存/释放能力，有利于CO2的活化和抑制积碳的形成[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]。支撑材料的选择显著影响催化剂的性能，在一些研究中，SiO2支撑的催化剂显示出CO2促进的独特优势[Placeholder_Of_DOI3]。

2. 可还原性和氧化态：
Fe氧化物的可还原性在决定催化剂性能方面起着关键作用。负载在活性炭上的Fe2O3表现出改善的可还原性，遵循Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe的还原路径[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]。然而，在反应条件下Fe的氧化态对于促进CO2-ODHP途径中的氧化性C-H键断裂至关重要[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]。这表明在反应过程中保持Fe处于部分氧化状态对选择性和活性很重要。

3. 促进剂和添加剂：
向Fe基催化剂中添加碱金属氧化物（Li、Na、K）和贵金属（Pd、Ag、Pt）可以增强其可还原性和催化性能[Placeholder_Of_DOI1] [Placeholder_Of_DOI5]。这些促进剂可以与Fe2O3产生协同效应，促进CO2和丙烷的活化，并通过增加铁氧化物的还原程度生成更多活性位点。

4. 反应机理：
Fe氧化物基催化剂上CO2-ODHP的提议反应机理包括两个主要步骤：（1）使用来自Fe2O3或Fe3O4的晶格氧将C3H8氧化为C3H6，（2）通过CO2重新氧化还原后的Fe氧化物[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]。这突出了Fe氧化物的氧化还原循环在CO2-ODHP过程中的关键作用。CO2的存在还可以通过逆水气变换反应增加丙烷转化率[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]。

5. 催化剂失活：
失活机制因Fe负载量和支撑材料而异。低Fe催化剂主要由于积碳而失活，而高Fe负载催化剂可能遭受支撑材料烧结和Fe迁移的影响[Placeholder_Of_DOI6]。添加促进剂和谨慎选择支撑材料可以帮助缓解这些失活过程。

为了优化CO2-ODHP反应的催化系统，研究人员应该关注：
1. 选择能够增强Fe氧化物可还原性同时保持稳定性的支撑材料。
2. 调整Fe负载量和促进剂组成以达到最佳性能和稳定性。
3. 设计能够促进Fe氧化物与CO2作为氧化剂进行高效氧化还原循环的催化剂。
4. 平衡Fe的氧化态以保持活性和选择性。
5. 加入能够增强CO2活化和抑制积碳形成的促进剂。

综合理解与未来展望：开发用于CO2-ODHP反应的Fe氧化物基催化剂代表了可持续丙烯生产的一个有前景的方向。支撑材料、Fe氧化态和促进剂之间的复杂相互作用为催化剂优化提供了众多机会。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 探索新型支撑材料或复合支撑材料，结合多种材料的有益特性（例如，CeO2-活性炭复合材料）。
2. 研究先进合成技术的潜力，如原子层沉积或受控沉淀方法，以实现对Fe氧化物分散和氧化态的精确控制。
3. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解催化剂在反应条件下结构和氧化态的动态变化。
4. 探索将Fe与其他过渡金属结合的双金属或多金属催化剂的潜力，以在CO2活化和丙烷脱氢方面实现协同效应。
5. 研究催化剂纹理和孔隙度在决定性能和稳定性方面的作用，可能导致优化物质传递和活性位点可及性的分层催化剂设计。
6. 探索将CO2-ODHP催化剂与膜反应器或其他过程强化策略结合，以提高整体过程效率和选择性。

通过解决这些研究方向，科学界可以致力于开发更高效、稳定和选择性的Fe氧化物基催化剂用于CO2-ODHP反应，为可持续化学品生产和CO2利用的更广泛目标做出贡献。
