<English>
The reducibility and support materials of Fe oxide-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in CO2-ODHP reactions. Key findings from the literature include:

1. Support Material: Active carbon, particularly low-ash content varieties like D-90, has been found to be the most effective support for Fe oxide catalysts [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]. However, CeO2 has also been identified as a promising support due to its oxygen storage/release capability, which facilitates CO2 activation and suppresses coke formation [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. The choice of support material can significantly impact catalyst performance, with SiO2-supported catalysts showing unique benefits from CO2 promotion in some cases [10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4].

2. Reducibility: The reducibility of Fe oxide plays a crucial role in catalyst performance. Fe2O3 supported on activated carbon (AC) shows improved reducibility, following the pathway Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe, which correlates with higher catalytic activity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]. However, the reducibility can be retarded when Fe oxide interacts strongly with certain supports, such as γ-Al2O3 or MgO [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6].

3. Oxidation State: Maintaining Fe in a partially oxidized state during the reaction appears to be beneficial for catalyst performance. In situ XANES measurements revealed that Fe in Fe3Ni catalysts remained in an oxidized state under reaction conditions, which may promote selective formation of propylene [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

4. Promoters and Additives: The addition of alkali metal oxides (Li, Na, K) and noble metals (Pd, Ag, Pt) can enhance catalyst reducibility and performance [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7] [10.1007/s11144-008-5339-x]. The optimal loading of these additives (e.g., 15 wt% for Pd) results in the best catalytic performance [10.1007/s11144-008-5339-x].

5. Reaction Mechanism: The proposed mechanism for CO2-ODHP over Fe oxide-based catalysts involves two main steps: (1) C3H8 oxidation to C3H6 using lattice oxygen from Fe2O3 or Fe3O4, and (2) reoxidation of the reduced Fe oxide by CO2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]. The presence of CO2 can also increase propane conversion through the reverse water gas shift reaction [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

6. Catalyst Stability: Fe-doped CeO2 catalysts have shown enhanced stability and promising catalytic performance for propane oxidative dehydrogenation using CO2 as a soft oxidant [10.1039/D0CY00586J]. The Fe/Ce ratio plays a crucial role in determining catalyst behavior, with higher ratios enhancing the propane ODH pathway while suppressing propane cracking [10.1039/D0CY00586J].

To optimize catalytic systems for CO2-ODHP reactions, researchers should focus on:
1. Selecting support materials that enhance Fe oxide reducibility while maintaining the ability to complete the redox cycle with CO2 as the oxidant.
2. Tailoring the bimetallic composition and Fe loading to control the balance between different reaction pathways and minimize deactivation.
3. Incorporating promoters that facilitate the reduction process and stabilize active sites.
4. Designing catalysts that maintain Fe in a partially oxidized state during the reaction.
5. Enhancing CO2 activation through careful selection and design of the support material.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The research on Fe oxide-based catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions reveals a complex interplay between catalyst composition, structure, and performance. Future research directions could focus on:

1. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation state during the reaction.
2. Computational modeling to predict optimal catalyst compositions and structures, potentially leading to the design of novel multi-component catalysts.
3. Exploring the potential of 2D materials or metal-organic frameworks as supports or promoters for Fe oxide catalysts.
4. Investigating the effects of reaction conditions (temperature, pressure, gas composition) on catalyst performance and stability to optimize process parameters.
5. Developing strategies to mitigate catalyst deactivation, such as intelligent promoter combinations or novel regeneration techniques.
6. Exploring the potential of Fe oxide-based catalysts in other CO2 utilization reactions, leveraging the insights gained from CO2-ODHP studies.

These research directions could lead to more efficient and sustainable processes for propylene production, contributing to the broader goal of CO2 utilization in chemical manufacturing.

<Chinese>
Fe氧化物基催化剂的可还原性和支撑材料对其在CO2-ODHP反应中的性能有显著影响。文献中的主要发现包括：

1. 支撑材料：活性炭，特别是低灰分含量的D-90等品种，被发现是Fe氧化物催化剂最有效的支撑材料 [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]。然而，CeO2也被认为是一种有前景的支撑材料，因其具有氧储存/释放能力，有利于CO2活化和抑制积碳形成 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。支撑材料的选择可以显著影响催化剂性能，在某些情况下，SiO2支撑的催化剂显示出CO2促进的独特优势 [10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4]。

2. 可还原性：Fe氧化物的可还原性在催化剂性能中起着关键作用。负载在活性炭（AC）上的Fe2O3表现出改善的可还原性，遵循Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe的还原路径，这与更高的催化活性相关 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]。然而，当Fe氧化物与某些支撑材料（如γ-Al2O3或MgO）强烈相互作用时，其可还原性可能会受到抑制 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]。

3. 氧化态：在反应过程中保持Fe处于部分氧化状态似乎有利于催化剂性能。原位XANES测量显示，在反应条件下，Fe3Ni催化剂中的Fe保持氧化状态，这可能促进丙烯的选择性形成 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

4. 促进剂和添加剂：添加碱金属氧化物（Li、Na、K）和贵金属（Pd、Ag、Pt）可以提高催化剂的可还原性和性能 [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7] [10.1007/s11144-008-5339-x]。这些添加剂的最佳负载量（例如，Pd为15 wt%）可获得最佳催化性能 [10.1007/s11144-008-5339-x]。

5. 反应机理：Fe氧化物基催化剂上CO2-ODHP的提议机理包括两个主要步骤：（1）使用Fe2O3或Fe3O4的晶格氧将C3H8氧化为C3H6，（2）CO2重新氧化还原的Fe氧化物 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]。CO2的存在还可以通过逆水气变换反应增加丙烷转化率 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

6. 催化剂稳定性：Fe掺杂CeO2催化剂在使用CO2作为软氧化剂的丙烷氧化脱氢反应中表现出增强的稳定性和有前景的催化性能 [10.1039/D0CY00586J]。Fe/Ce比例在决定催化剂行为方面起着关键作用，较高的比例可增强丙烷ODH途径，同时抑制丙烷裂解 [10.1039/D0CY00586J]。

为优化CO2-ODHP反应的催化系统，研究人员应关注：
1. 选择能够增强Fe氧化物可还原性的支撑材料，同时保持其以CO2作为氧化剂完成氧化还原循环的能力。
2. 调整双金属组成和Fe负载量，以控制不同反应途径之间的平衡并最小化失活。
3. 引入促进剂，以促进还原过程并稳定活性位点。
4. 设计在反应过程中保持Fe处于部分氧化状态的催化剂。
5. 通过仔细选择和设计支撑材料来增强CO2活化。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：对Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的研究揭示了催化剂组成、结构和性能之间的复杂相互作用。未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 先进的原位表征技术，以更好地理解反应过程中催化剂结构和氧化态的动态变化。
2. 计算机模拟预测最佳催化剂组成和结构，可能导致新型多组分催化剂的设计。
3. 探索2D材料或金属有机框架作为Fe氧化物催化剂的支撑材料或促进剂的潜力。
4. 研究反应条件（温度、压力、气体组成）对催化剂性能和稳定性的影响，以优化工艺参数。
5. 开发减轻催化剂失活的策略，如智能促进剂组合或新型再生技术。
6. 探索Fe氧化物基催化剂在其他CO2利用反应中的潜力，利用从CO2-ODHP研究中获得的见解。

这些研究方向可能导致更高效、更可持续的丙烯生产工艺，为化学制造中CO2利用的更广泛目标做出贡献。
