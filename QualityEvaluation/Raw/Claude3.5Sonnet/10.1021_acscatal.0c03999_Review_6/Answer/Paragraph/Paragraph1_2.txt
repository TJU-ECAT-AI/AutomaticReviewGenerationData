The reducibility and support materials of Fe oxide-based catalysts significantly influence their performance in CO2-ODHP reactions. Key findings and insights include:

1. Support Material:
Active carbon, particularly low-ash content varieties like D-90, has been found to be the most effective support for Fe oxide catalysts in CO2-ODHP reactions [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]. CeO2 has also been identified as a promising support material due to its oxygen storage/release capability, which facilitates CO2 activation and suppresses coke formation [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. The choice of support material can significantly impact catalyst performance, with SiO2-supported catalysts showing unique benefits from CO2 promotion in some cases [10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4].

2. Reducibility:
The reducibility of Fe oxide catalysts plays a crucial role in their performance. Fe2O3 supported on activated carbon (AC) shows improved reducibility, following the reduction pathway Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe, which contributes to its high catalytic performance [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]. However, the reducibility of Fe2O3 supported on γ-Al2O3 and hydrotalcite-derived FeMg catalysts is hindered due to strong interactions with the support, leading to the formation of hardly reducible phases [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6].

3. Oxidation State:
In situ XANES measurements have revealed that under reaction conditions, Fe in Fe3Ni catalysts remains in an oxidized state, while Ni is in a metallic form [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. This oxidized state of Fe appears to be crucial for favoring oxidative C-H bond cleavage in the CO2-ODHP pathway [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

4. Promoters and Additives:
The addition of alkali metal oxides (Li, Na, K) significantly enhances catalyst activity, with an optimal alkali metal to Fe atomic ratio of 0.1 [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]. Noble metals (Pd, Ag, Pt) and other elements (Nb) can also enhance the reducibility of Fe-based catalysts, leading to improved catalytic activity and selectivity [10.1007/s11144-008-5339-x].

5. Reaction Mechanism:
The proposed reaction mechanism for CO2-ODHP over Fe oxide-based catalysts involves two main steps: (1) C3H8 oxidation to C3H6 using lattice oxygen from Fe2O3 or Fe3O4, and (2) reoxidation of the reduced Fe oxide by CO2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]. The presence of CO2 can also increase propane conversion through the reverse water gas shift reaction [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

6. Active Sites:
The formation of potassium ferrite on the catalyst surface is proposed as the active phase for some Fe oxide catalysts [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]. In bimetallic catalysts, the interaction between Fe oxide and other metals (e.g., Ni) at the interface can create active sites for the reaction [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

To optimize catalytic systems for CO2-ODHP reactions, researchers should focus on:
1. Selecting support materials that enhance Fe oxide reducibility while maintaining stability.
2. Controlling the oxidation state of Fe to maintain it in a partially oxidized form during the reaction.
3. Incorporating appropriate promoters and additives to enhance reducibility and catalytic performance.
4. Designing catalyst structures that facilitate efficient redox cycling of Fe oxide.
5. Balancing the composition of bimetallic catalysts to leverage the beneficial properties of each component.
6. Enhancing CO2 activation and coke resistance through careful selection and design of the support material.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 

The extensive research on Fe oxide-based catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions has revealed the complex interplay between catalyst composition, structure, and performance. The findings highlight the importance of tailoring the reducibility and oxidation state of Fe species, as well as the critical role of support materials and promoters in enhancing catalytic activity and stability.

Future research directions may include:

1. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques: Developing and applying more sophisticated in-situ spectroscopic and microscopic methods to gain deeper insights into the dynamic changes of catalyst structure and oxidation states under reaction conditions.

2. Rational design of multi-component catalysts: Exploring novel combinations of Fe with other transition metals and promoters to create synergistic effects that enhance both activity and selectivity in CO2-ODHP reactions.

3. Computational modeling: Utilizing advanced computational techniques, such as machine learning and artificial intelligence, to predict optimal catalyst compositions and structures for CO2-ODHP reactions.

4. Innovative support materials: Investigating novel support materials, such as 2D materials or metal-organic frameworks, that could offer unique properties for stabilizing active Fe species and facilitating CO2 activation.

5. Process intensification: Exploring methods to integrate CO2-ODHP reactions with other processes, such as CO2 capture or utilization, to create more sustainable and efficient chemical production systems.

6. Scalability and industrial application: Focusing on developing catalysts that not only perform well in laboratory conditions but also maintain their activity and stability under industrial-scale operations.

By addressing these challenges and opportunities, researchers can work towards developing more efficient, selective, and sustainable catalytic systems for CO2-ODHP reactions, contributing to the broader goal of utilizing CO2 as a feedstock for valuable chemical production.

<Chinese>
Fe氧化物基催化剂的可还原性和载体材料显著影响其在CO2-ODHP反应中的性能。主要发现和见解包括：

1. 载体材料：
活性炭，特别是低灰分含量的D-90型，被发现是CO2-ODHP反应中Fe氧化物催化剂最有效的载体[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]。CeO2也被确定为一种有前景的载体材料，因为其氧气存储/释放能力可以促进CO2的活化并抑制积炭形成[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。载体材料的选择可以显著影响催化剂性能，在某些情况下，SiO2负载的催化剂显示出CO2促进的独特优势[10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4]。

2. 可还原性：
Fe氧化物催化剂的可还原性在其性能中起着关键作用。负载在活性炭（AC）上的Fe2O3显示出改善的可还原性，遵循Fe2O3 → Fe3O4 → FeO → Fe的还原路径，这有助于其高催化性能[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]。然而，负载在γ-Al2O3和水滑石衍生FeMg催化剂上的Fe2O3的可还原性由于与载体的强相互作用而受到阻碍，导致难还原相的形成[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]。

3. 氧化态：
原位XANES测量揭示，在反应条件下，Fe3Ni催化剂中的Fe保持氧化态，而Ni呈金属态[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。Fe的这种氧化态似乎对于在CO2-ODHP途径中有利于氧化性C-H键断裂至关重要[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

4. 促进剂和添加剂：
碱金属氧化物（Li，Na，K）的添加显著增强了催化剂活性，最佳碱金属与Fe的原子比为0.1 [10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]。贵金属（Pd，Ag，Pt）和其他元素（Nb）也可以增强Fe基催化剂的可还原性，从而提高催化活性和选择性[10.1007/s11144-008-5339-x]。

5. 反应机理：
Fe氧化物基催化剂上CO2-ODHP的提议反应机理包括两个主要步骤：（1）使用来自Fe2O3或Fe3O4的晶格氧将C3H8氧化为C3H6，（2）CO2重新氧化还原的Fe氧化物[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_6]。CO2的存在还可以通过逆水气变换反应增加丙烷转化率[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

6. 活性位点：
钾铁氧化物在催化剂表面的形成被提出作为某些Fe氧化物催化剂的活性相[10.1016/S0926-860X(00)00514-7]。在双金属催化剂中，Fe氧化物与其他金属（如Ni）在界面处的相互作用可以为反应创造活性位点[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

为了优化CO2-ODHP反应的催化系统，研究人员应该专注于：
1. 选择能增强Fe氧化物可还原性同时保持稳定性的载体材料。
2. 控制Fe的氧化态，使其在反应过程中保持部分氧化状态。
3. 纳入适当的促进剂和添加剂以增强可还原性和催化性能。
4. 设计能促进Fe氧化物高效氧化还原循环的催化剂结构。
5. 平衡双金属催化剂的组成，以利用每个组分的有益特性。
6. 通过仔细选择和设计载体材料来增强CO2活化和抗积炭能力。

综合理解与未来展望：

对Fe氧化物基催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的广泛研究揭示了催化剂组成、结构和性能之间的复杂相互作用。这些发现强调了调整Fe物种可还原性和氧化态的重要性，以及载体材料和促进剂在增强催化活性和稳定性方面的关键作用。

未来研究方向可能包括：

1. 先进的原位表征技术：开发和应用更复杂的原位光谱和显微方法，以深入了解催化剂在反应条件下结构和氧化态的动态变化。

2. 多组分催化剂的理性设计：探索Fe与其他过渡金属和促进剂的新组合，以创造在CO2-ODHP反应中同时增强活性和选择性的协同效应。

3. 计算机模拟：利用先进的计算技术，如机器学习和人工智能，预测CO2-ODHP反应的最佳催化剂组成和结构。

4. 创新载体材料：研究新型载体材料，如二维材料或金属有机框架，这些材料可能为稳定活性Fe物种和促进CO2活化提供独特的性质。

5. 过程强化：探索将CO2-ODHP反应与其他过程（如CO2捕获或利用）集成的方法，以创建更可持续和高效的化学生产系统。

6. 可扩展性和工业应用：专注于开发不仅在实验室条件下表现良好，而且在工业规模操作中也能保持其活性和稳定性的催化剂。

通过解决这些挑战和机遇，研究人员可以致力于开发更高效、更选择性和更可持续的CO2-ODHP反应催化系统，为更广泛的利用CO2作为有价值化学品生产原料的目标做出贡献。
