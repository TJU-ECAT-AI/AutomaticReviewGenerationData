<English>
Cyclohexene serves as an ideal probe molecule for studying hydrogenation reactions due to its multiple reaction pathways and relevance to industrial processes. Its versatility allows researchers to gain valuable insights into the influence of bimetallic catalysts on hydrogenation activity and selectivity in industrial applications.

The key reasons for cyclohexene's suitability as a probe molecule include:

1. Multiple reaction pathways: Cyclohexene can undergo hydrogenation, dehydrogenation, decomposition, and disproportionation (self-hydrogenation) [10.1016/j.apcata.2008.01.024]. These diverse pathways enable researchers to study various aspects of catalytic activity on different surfaces.

2. Sensitivity to surface structure: Cyclohexene's adsorption and reaction are highly sensitive to the electronic and geometric properties of catalyst surfaces, making it an excellent tool for comparing different catalysts [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001].

3. Industrial relevance: As a cyclic hydrocarbon, cyclohexene represents important reaction intermediates in refinery and petrochemical processes, as well as building blocks for various chemicals in the industry [10.1021/cr300096b_7].

4. Model compound for C=C bond hydrogenation: Cyclohexene serves as a simple model for studying more complex industrial hydrogenation processes [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.08.016].

The multiple reaction pathways of cyclohexene provide several insights into the influence of bimetallic catalysts on hydrogenation activity and selectivity:

1. Unique bimetallic properties: Cyclohexene reactions reveal novel catalytic properties of bimetallic surfaces that are distinct from their parent metals. For instance, PtNiPt(111) subsurface structures show unique low-temperature hydrogenation activity not observed on pure Pt or Ni surfaces [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001].

2. Surface composition effects: The dominant reaction pathway can change significantly when modifying monometallic surfaces with a second metal. For example, adding Pt to Ni(111) introduces a new low-temperature hydrogenation pathway, producing cyclohexane [10.1016/j.susc.2004.03.032].

3. Electronic structure correlations: Cyclohexene's binding energy and reactivity show a clear correlation with the surface d-band center of bimetallic catalysts, allowing researchers to establish structure-property relationships [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001].

4. Catalyst preparation effects: Cyclohexene reactions can reveal how different catalyst preparation methods, such as impregnation sequence, affect the catalytic activity of supported bimetallic catalysts [10.1016/j.apcata.2008.01.024].

5. Bridging the materials gap: Cyclohexene's behavior on various surfaces helps researchers correlate the performance of model surfaces (e.g., single crystal studies) with supported catalysts used in industrial applications [10.1016/j.jcat.2011.03.007].

These insights can be applied to industrial hydrogenation processes in several ways:

1. Catalyst design optimization: Understanding how different metal combinations affect reaction pathways can guide the development of more efficient and selective catalysts for specific industrial applications [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.05.013].

2. Process improvement: By elucidating the relationship between catalyst structure and performance, researchers can develop strategies to enhance desired reactions and suppress unwanted side reactions in industrial processes [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.05.013].

3. Predictive modeling: The correlations established between electronic structure and catalytic activity can be used to predict the behavior of more complex industrial hydrogenation processes on bimetallic catalysts [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001].

4. Sustainable chemistry: Optimizing catalyst compositions based on cyclohexene studies can lead to improved yields, reduced energy consumption, and more sustainable chemical production methods in industrial hydrogenation processes [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.05.013].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The use of cyclohexene as a probe molecule for studying hydrogenation reactions on bimetallic catalysts has provided valuable insights into the fundamental aspects of catalysis and their applications in industrial processes. Moving forward, researchers could explore the following avenues:

1. In situ and operando studies: Developing advanced spectroscopic techniques to study cyclohexene reactions under realistic industrial conditions could bridge the gap between fundamental surface science and practical applications.

2. Machine learning approaches: Leveraging the extensive data generated from cyclohexene studies to develop predictive models for catalyst performance could accelerate the discovery and optimization of new bimetallic catalysts for industrial hydrogenation processes.

3. Nano-structured catalysts: Investigating the behavior of cyclohexene on precisely controlled nano-structured bimetallic catalysts could provide new insights into the role of catalyst morphology and size effects on hydrogenation activity and selectivity.

4. Green chemistry applications: Exploring the use of bimetallic catalysts optimized through cyclohexene studies for the hydrogenation of bio-derived feedstocks could contribute to the development of more sustainable chemical processes.

5. Multi-functional catalysts: Extending the insights gained from cyclohexene studies to design catalysts capable of performing multiple transformations in one-pot reactions could lead to more efficient and economical industrial processes.

By continuing to leverage cyclohexene as a probe molecule and expanding the scope of these studies, researchers can make significant strides in understanding and improving industrial hydrogenation processes, ultimately contributing to more efficient and sustainable chemical production methods.

<Chinese>
环己烯作为研究氢化反应的理想探针分子，因其多重反应途径和与工业过程的相关性而备受青睐。其多功能性使研究人员能够深入了解双金属催化剂对工业应用中氢化活性和选择性的影响。

环己烯作为探针分子的适用性主要基于以下几个关键原因：

1. 多重反应途径：环己烯可以进行氢化、脱氢、分解和歧化（自氢化）反应[10.1016/j.apcata.2008.01.024]。这些多样的反应途径使研究人员能够研究不同表面上催化活性的各个方面。

2. 对表面结构敏感：环己烯的吸附和反应对催化剂表面的电子和几何性质高度敏感，使其成为比较不同催化剂的优秀工具[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。

3. 工业相关性：作为一种环状烃，环己烯代表了炼油和石油化工过程中的重要反应中间体，以及工业中各种化学品的基本构建块[10.1021/cr300096b_7]。

4. C=C键氢化的模型化合物：环己烯作为研究更复杂的工业氢化过程的简单模型[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.08.016]。

环己烯的多重反应途径为双金属催化剂对氢化活性和选择性的影响提供了几个重要见解：

1. 独特的双金属性质：环己烯反应揭示了双金属表面与其母体金属不同的新型催化性质。例如，PtNiPt(111)亚表面结构显示出在纯Pt或Ni表面上未观察到的独特低温氢化活性[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。

2. 表面组成效应：用第二种金属修饰单金属表面时，主导反应途径可能会发生显著变化。例如，在Ni(111)上添加Pt引入了新的低温氢化途径，产生环己烷[10.1016/j.susc.2004.03.032]。

3. 电子结构相关性：环己烯的结合能和反应性与双金属催化剂表面d带中心显示出明显的相关性，使研究人员能够建立结构-性能关系[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。

4. 催化剂制备效应：环己烯反应可以揭示不同的催化剂制备方法（如浸渍顺序）如何影响负载型双金属催化剂的催化活性[10.1016/j.apcata.2008.01.024]。

5. 跨越材料差距：环己烯在各种表面上的行为有助于研究人员将模型表面（如单晶研究）的性能与工业应用中使用的负载型催化剂相关联[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.03.007]。

这些见解可以应用于工业氢化过程，方式如下：

1. 催化剂设计优化：了解不同金属组合如何影响反应途径可以指导开发更高效、更具选择性的催化剂，用于特定的工业应用[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.05.013]。

2. 工艺改进：通过阐明催化剂结构与性能之间的关系，研究人员可以制定策略，在工业过程中增强所需反应并抑制不需要的副反应[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.05.013]。

3. 预测性建模：在电子结构和催化活性之间建立的相关性可用于预测双金属催化剂上更复杂的工业氢化过程的行为[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。

4. 可持续化学：基于环己烯研究优化催化剂组成可以提高产率，降低能耗，并在工业氢化过程中实现更可持续的化学品生产方法[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.05.013]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：使用环己烯作为探针分子研究双金属催化剂上的氢化反应，为催化基础方面和工业应用提供了宝贵的见解。展望未来，研究人员可以探索以下方向：

1. 原位和操作条件下的研究：开发先进的光谱技术，在真实的工业条件下研究环己烯反应，可以缩小基础表面科学和实际应用之间的差距。

2. 机器学习方法：利用环己烯研究产生的大量数据开发催化剂性能预测模型，可以加速工业氢化过程新型双金属催化剂的发现和优化。

3. 纳米结构催化剂：研究环己烯在精确控制的纳米结构双金属催化剂上的行为，可以为催化剂形态和尺寸效应对氢化活性和选择性的影响提供新的见解。

4. 绿色化学应用：探索通过环己烯研究优化的双金属催化剂在生物衍生原料氢化中的应用，可以促进更可持续化学过程的发展。

5. 多功能催化剂：将从环己烯研究中获得的见解扩展到设计能够在一锅反应中执行多重转化的催化剂，可能导致更高效和经济的工业过程。

通过继续利用环己烯作为探针分子并扩大这些研究的范围，研究人员可以在理解和改进工业氢化过程方面取得重大进展，最终为更高效和可持续的化学品生产方法做出贡献。
