<English>
Cyclohexene serves as an ideal probe molecule for studying hydrogenation reactions due to its multiple reaction pathways and sensitivity to catalyst surface properties. This versatility allows researchers to gain valuable insights into the influence of bimetallic catalysts on hydrogenation activity and selectivity in industrial processes.

The main reasons for using cyclohexene as a probe molecule include:

1. Multiple reaction pathways: Cyclohexene can undergo hydrogenation, dehydrogenation, decomposition, and disproportionation (self-hydrogenation) [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.05.013]. These competitive pathways provide a comprehensive view of catalyst performance and selectivity.

2. Sensitivity to surface structure: The adsorption and reaction of cyclohexene are highly sensitive to the electronic and geometric properties of bimetallic surfaces [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]. This sensitivity allows researchers to study how different metal combinations affect catalytic behavior.

3. Model compound for industrial processes: Cyclohexene represents important reaction intermediates in refinery and petrochemical processes, making it relevant for studying industrial hydrogenation reactions [10.1021/cr300096b_7].

4. Easy monitoring of reaction products: The main reaction products (cyclohexene, cyclohexane, and benzene) can be readily monitored using spectroscopic techniques, enabling quantitative analysis of reaction kinetics and selectivity [10.1016/j.apcata.2008.01.024].

5. Bridging the materials gap: Cyclohexene reactions can be studied on both single crystal and polycrystalline surfaces, as well as supported catalysts, allowing researchers to correlate fundamental surface science studies with practical catalyst systems [10.1016/j.jcat.2011.03.007].

The multiple reaction pathways of cyclohexene provide insights into the influence of bimetallic catalysts on hydrogenation activity and selectivity in industrial processes:

1. Synergistic effects: Bimetallic catalysts often exhibit unique properties not observed in their monometallic counterparts. For example, PtNi bimetallic catalysts show higher hydrogenation activity than supported Ni and Pt monometallic catalysts [10.1016/j.jcat.2011.03.007].

2. Electronic structure effects: The reactivity of cyclohexene correlates with the surface d-band center of bimetallic catalysts, demonstrating a volcano-type relationship between catalytic activity and electronic properties [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001].

3. Surface composition influence: The dominant reaction pathway can change significantly when modifying catalyst surfaces. For instance, adding Pt to Ni(111) introduces a new low-temperature hydrogenation pathway not present on clean Ni(111) [10.1016/j.susc.2004.03.032].

4. Catalyst preparation effects: The impregnation sequence in supported bimetallic catalysts can affect the disproportionation activity of cyclohexene, providing insights into how preparation methods influence catalyst performance [10.1016/j.apcata.2008.01.024].

5. Structure-activity relationships: By comparing the activity and selectivity of different bimetallic surfaces for cyclohexene reactions, researchers can gain insights into how the electronic and geometric properties of catalysts influence their performance [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.05.013].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The use of cyclohexene as a probe molecule has provided valuable insights into the behavior of bimetallic catalysts in hydrogenation reactions. Moving forward, researchers could explore the following avenues:

1. In-situ characterization: Developing advanced in-situ spectroscopic techniques to study cyclohexene reactions on bimetallic catalysts under realistic reaction conditions could bridge the pressure and materials gaps between surface science studies and industrial catalysis.

2. Computational modeling: Combining experimental results with advanced computational modeling could provide a more detailed understanding of the electronic and geometric factors influencing cyclohexene reactions on bimetallic surfaces.

3. Novel bimetallic combinations: Exploring a wider range of bimetallic catalysts, including non-noble metal combinations, could lead to the discovery of more efficient and cost-effective catalysts for industrial hydrogenation processes.

4. Structure-controlled synthesis: Developing methods to precisely control the surface structure and composition of bimetallic catalysts could enable the rational design of catalysts with optimized activity and selectivity for specific hydrogenation reactions.

5. Application to complex feedstocks: Extending the insights gained from cyclohexene studies to more complex industrial feedstocks could help improve the efficiency and selectivity of large-scale hydrogenation processes in the petrochemical and fine chemical industries.

环己烯作为研究氢化反应的理想探针分子，因其具有多重反应途径和对催化剂表面性质的敏感性。这种多功能性使研究人员能够深入了解双金属催化剂对工业过程中氢化活性和选择性的影响。

使用环己烯作为探针分子的主要原因包括：

1. 多重反应途径：环己烯可以经历氢化、脱氢、分解和歧化（自氢化）[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.05.013]。这些竞争性途径提供了对催化剂性能和选择性的全面视角。

2. 对表面结构的敏感性：环己烯的吸附和反应对双金属表面的电子和几何性质高度敏感[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。这种敏感性使研究人员能够研究不同金属组合如何影响催化行为。

3. 工业过程的模型化合物：环己烯代表了炼油和石油化工过程中的重要反应中间体，使其与研究工业氢化反应相关[10.1021/cr300096b_7]。

4. 反应产物易于监测：主要反应产物（环己烯、环己烷和苯）可以通过光谱技术轻松监测，从而实现反应动力学和选择性的定量分析[10.1016/j.apcata.2008.01.024]。

5. 桥接材料差距：环己烯反应可以在单晶和多晶表面以及负载型催化剂上进行研究，使研究人员能够将基础表面科学研究与实际催化剂系统相关联[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.03.007]。

环己烯的多重反应途径为双金属催化剂对工业过程中氢化活性和选择性的影响提供了以下见解：

1. 协同效应：双金属催化剂通常表现出其单金属对应物中未观察到的独特性质。例如，PtNi双金属催化剂表现出比负载型Ni和Pt单金属催化剂更高的氢化活性[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.03.007]。

2. 电子结构效应：环己烯的反应性与双金属催化剂的表面d带中心相关，展示了催化活性与电子性质之间的火山型关系[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。

3. 表面组成影响：修饰催化剂表面可显著改变主导反应途径。例如，向Ni(111)添加Pt引入了在纯Ni(111)上不存在的新的低温氢化途径[10.1016/j.susc.2004.03.032]。

4. 催化剂制备效应：负载型双金属催化剂的浸渍顺序可影响环己烯的歧化活性，提供了制备方法如何影响催化剂性能的见解[10.1016/j.apcata.2008.01.024]。

5. 结构-活性关系：通过比较不同双金属表面对环己烯反应的活性和选择性，研究人员可以深入了解催化剂的电子和几何性质如何影响其性能[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.05.013]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：使用环己烯作为探针分子为了解双金属催化剂在氢化反应中的行为提供了宝贵的见解。展望未来，研究人员可以探索以下方向：

1. 原位表征：开发先进的原位光谱技术，在实际反应条件下研究双金属催化剂上的环己烯反应，可以弥合表面科学研究与工业催化之间的压力和材料差距。

2. 计算机模拟：将实验结果与先进的计算机模拟相结合，可以更详细地理解影响双金属表面环己烯反应的电子和几何因素。

3. 新型双金属组合：探索更广泛的双金属催化剂，包括非贵金属组合，可能会发现更高效、更经济的工业氢化过程催化剂。

4. 结构可控合成：开发精确控制双金属催化剂表面结构和组成的方法，可以实现针对特定氢化反应的催化剂理性设计，优化其活性和选择性。

5. 应用于复杂原料：将从环己烯研究中获得的见解扩展到更复杂的工业原料，可以帮助提高石油化工和精细化工行业大规模氢化过程的效率和选择性。
