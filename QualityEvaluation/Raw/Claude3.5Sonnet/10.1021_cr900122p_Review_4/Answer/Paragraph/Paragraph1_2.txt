Intramolecular C-H bond activation plays a crucial role in the deactivation of supported alkene polymerization and metathesis catalysts, with distinct mechanisms observed for lanthanide/actinide-based and silica-supported Re-based catalysts [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. This review section will delve into the specific deactivation pathways for these catalyst systems and discuss their implications on catalyst performance and longevity.

For lanthanide and actinide-based supported alkene polymerization catalysts, the primary deactivation pathway involves the activation of the allylic C-H bond instead of propene insertion. This process results in the formation of inactive π-allyl surface complexes, effectively deactivating the catalyst [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. The formation of these inactive complexes directly reduces the number of active sites available for polymerization, leading to a decrease in overall catalyst efficiency.

In contrast, the deactivation mechanism for silica-supported Re-based alkene metathesis catalysts is more intricate. The key step in this process involves the activation of the C-H bond in the β-position of metallacyclobutane intermediates, leading to a β-H transfer process [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. This results in the formation of an allyl hydride intermediate, which can then react with ethene to create a highly reactive species. This species can either regenerate the catalyst through α-H abstraction between adjacent alkyl ligands or lead to degrafting via β-H abstraction of the ethyl fragment by the siloxy ligand.

The key steps in these processes can be summarized as follows:
1. For lanthanide/actinide catalysts: Allylic C-H bond activation and formation of π-allyl surface complexes.
2. For Re-based catalysts: β-H transfer in metallacyclobutane intermediates, formation of allyl hydride intermediates, and potential degrafting through β-H abstraction.

These deactivation pathways have significant consequences for catalyst performance and longevity. In the case of Re-based catalysts, the potential for degrafting can lead to a loss of active catalyst from the support surface, reducing the overall catalyst loading and activity over time [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. Additionally, the competing pathways of catalyst regeneration and degrafting in Re-based systems may result in a gradual decline in performance as the reaction progresses.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Understanding these deactivation mechanisms is crucial for developing strategies to improve catalyst stability and longevity. Future research endeavors might focus on modifying ligand structures or support materials to minimize unwanted C-H activation processes. For instance, exploring novel support materials with enhanced stability against degrafting could lead to more robust Re-based catalysts. Additionally, investigating the use of sterically hindered ligands or co-catalysts that can suppress allylic C-H activation in lanthanide/actinide systems may result in improved catalyst performance. 分子内C-H键活化在负载型烯烃聚合和复分解催化剂的失活中起着至关重要的作用，其中镧系/锕系基和硅胶负载的Re基催化剂表现出不同的机理[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。本综述部分将深入探讨这些催化剂系统的具体失活途径，并讨论它们对催化剂性能和寿命的影响。

对于镧系和锕系基负载型烯烃聚合催化剂，主要的失活途径涉及烯丙基C-H键的活化，而不是丙烯的插入。这一过程导致形成无活性的π-烯丙基表面络合物，从而有效地使催化剂失活[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。这些无活性络合物的形成直接减少了可用于聚合的活性位点数量，导致催化剂整体效率下降。

相比之下，硅胶负载的Re基烯烃复分解催化剂的失活机理更为复杂。这个过程的关键步骤涉及金属环丁烷中间体β位C-H键的活化，导致β-H转移过程[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。这导致形成烯丙基氢化物中间体，然后可以与乙烯反应生成高活性物种。这种物种可以通过相邻烷基配体之间的α-H抽提再生催化剂，或通过硅氧基配体对乙基片段的β-H抽提导致脱附。

这些过程的关键步骤可以总结如下：
1. 对于镧系/锕系催化剂：烯丙基C-H键活化和π-烯丙基表面络合物的形成。
2. 对于Re基催化剂：金属环丁烷中间体中的β-H转移，烯丙基氢化物中间体的形成，以及通过β-H抽提可能导致的脱附。

这些失活途径对催化剂性能和寿命有重大影响。对于Re基催化剂，脱附的可能性可能导致活性催化剂从支持表面流失，随时间推移减少整体催化剂负载量和活性[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。此外，Re基系统中催化剂再生和脱附的竞争途径可能导致反应进行过程中性能逐渐下降。

综合理解与未来展望：理解这些失活机理对开发提高催化剂稳定性和寿命的策略至关重要。未来的研究努力可能会集中在修改配体结构或支持材料以最大程度地减少不必要的C-H活化过程。例如，探索具有增强抗脱附稳定性的新型支持材料可能导致更加稳健的Re基催化剂。此外，研究使用空间位阻配体或可以抑制镧系/锕系系统中烯丙基C-H活化的助催化剂可能会提高催化剂性能。另外，开发原位表征技术以实时监测π-烯丙基表面络合物和烯丙基氢化物中间体的形成，可能为这些失活过程的动力学提供宝贵的见解，潜在地导致更有效的催化剂设计和反应条件。
