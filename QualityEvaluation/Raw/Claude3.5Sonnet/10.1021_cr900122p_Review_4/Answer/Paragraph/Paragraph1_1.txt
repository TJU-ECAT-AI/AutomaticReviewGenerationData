Intramolecular C-H bond activation plays a crucial role in the deactivation of supported alkene polymerization and metathesis catalysts, with distinct mechanisms observed for lanthanide/actinide-based and silica-supported Re-based catalysts [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. This review section focuses on the specific deactivation pathways and their implications for catalyst performance and longevity.

In the case of lanthanide and actinide-based supported alkene polymerization catalysts, the deactivation process involves the activation of the allylic C-H bond instead of the desired propene insertion. This unintended reaction leads to the formation of inactive π-allyl surface complexes, effectively rendering the catalyst inactive [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. The formation of these π-allyl complexes directly reduces the number of active sites available for polymerization, resulting in a significant decrease in overall catalyst efficiency.

For silica-supported Re-based alkene metathesis catalysts, the deactivation mechanism is more intricate. The key step in this process involves the activation of the C-H bond in the β-position of metallacyclobutane intermediates, leading to a β-H transfer process [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. This results in the formation of an allyl hydride intermediate, which can subsequently react with ethene to create a highly reactive species. This reactive species can then follow one of two pathways: (1) regenerate the catalyst through α-H abstraction between adjacent alkyl ligands, or (2) lead to degrafting via β-H abstraction of the ethyl fragment by the siloxy ligand [10.1021/cr900122p_4].

The consequences of these deactivation pathways on catalyst performance and longevity are significant. For Re-based catalysts, the potential for degrafting can lead to a gradual loss of active catalyst from the support surface, reducing the overall catalyst loading and activity over time. The competing pathways of catalyst regeneration and degrafting in these systems may result in a progressive decline in performance as the reaction proceeds [10.1021/cr900122p_4].

Understanding these deactivation mechanisms is crucial for developing strategies to improve catalyst stability and longevity. Future research efforts could focus on:

分子内C-H键活化在负载型烯烃聚合和复分解催化剂的失活中起着至关重要的作用，对于镧系/锕系和硅胶负载的Re基催化剂观察到了不同的机理 [10.1021/cr900122p_4]。本综述部分重点关注特定的失活途径及其对催化剂性能和寿命的影响。

对于镧系和锕系基负载型烯烃聚合催化剂，失活过程涉及烯丙基C-H键的活化，而不是所需的丙烯插入。这种意外反应导致形成无活性的π-烯丙基表面络合物，有效地使催化剂失活 [10.1021/cr900122p_4]。这些π-烯丙基络合物的形成直接减少了可用于聚合的活性位点数量，导致催化剂整体效率显著下降。

对于硅胶负载的Re基烯烃复分解催化剂，失活机理更为复杂。这个过程的关键步骤涉及金属杂环丁烷中间体β位C-H键的活化，导致β-H转移过程 [10.1021/cr900122p_4]。这导致形成烯丙基氢化物中间体，随后可与乙烯反应生成高度活性的物种。这种活性物种可以遵循两种途径之一：（1）通过相邻烷基配体之间的α-H抽提再生催化剂，或（2）通过硅氧基配体对乙基片段的β-H抽提导致脱附 [10.1021/cr900122p_4]。

这些失活途径对催化剂性能和寿命的影响是显著的。对于Re基催化剂，脱附的可能性可能导致活性催化剂从支持表面逐渐流失，随时间推移降低整体催化剂负载量和活性。这些系统中催化剂再生和脱附的竞争途径可能导致反应进行过程中性能逐渐下降 [10.1021/cr900122p_4]。
