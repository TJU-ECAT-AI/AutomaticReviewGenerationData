<English>
Intramolecular C-H bond activation plays a crucial role in the deactivation of supported alkene polymerization and metathesis catalysts, with distinct mechanisms observed for lanthanide/actinide-based and silica-supported Re-based catalysts [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. 

For lanthanide and actinide-based supported alkene polymerization catalysts, the deactivation pathway involves the activation of the allylic C-H bond instead of propene insertion. This process results in the formation of inactive π-allyl surface complexes, effectively deactivating the catalyst [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. This mechanism significantly impacts the catalyst's performance by reducing the number of active sites available for polymerization, thereby decreasing overall efficiency.

In contrast, silica-supported Re-based alkene metathesis catalysts exhibit a more complex deactivation mechanism. The key step involves the activation of the C-H bond in the β-position of metallacyclobutane intermediates, leading to a β-H transfer process [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. This results in the formation of an allyl hydride intermediate, which can then react with ethene to create a highly reactive species. This species can either regenerate the catalyst through α-H abstraction between adjacent alkyl ligands or lead to degrafting via β-H abstraction of the ethyl fragment by the siloxy ligand [10.1021/cr900122p_4].

The key steps in these processes can be summarized as follows:
1. For lanthanide/actinide catalysts: Allylic C-H bond activation and formation of π-allyl surface complexes.
2. For Re-based catalysts: β-H transfer in metallacyclobutane intermediates, formation of allyl hydride intermediates, and potential degrafting through β-H abstraction.

These deactivation pathways have significant consequences for catalyst performance and longevity. In the case of Re-based catalysts, the potential for degrafting can lead to a loss of active catalyst from the support surface, reducing the overall catalyst loading and activity over time. Additionally, the competing pathways of catalyst regeneration and degrafting in Re-based systems may result in a gradual decline in performance as the reaction progresses [10.1021/cr900122p_4].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Understanding these deactivation mechanisms is crucial for developing strategies to improve catalyst stability and longevity. Future research endeavors might focus on modifying ligand structures or support materials to minimize unwanted C-H activation processes. Additionally, the development of novel characterization techniques to study these deactivation pathways in situ could provide valuable insights into the kinetics and thermodynamics of these processes. This knowledge could lead to the design of more robust catalysts with enhanced resistance to deactivation, potentially expanding the scope and efficiency of alkene polymerization and metathesis reactions in industrial applications.

分子内C-H键活化在支持的烯烃聚合和复分解催化剂的失活中起着关键作用，其中镧系/锕系基和硅支持的Re基催化剂表现出不同的机理[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。

对于镧系和锕系基支持的烯烃聚合催化剂，失活途径涉及烯丙基C-H键的活化，而不是丙烯的插入。这个过程导致形成无活性的π-烯丙基表面络合物，从而有效地使催化剂失活[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。这种机理通过减少可用于聚合的活性位点数量，显著影响催化剂的性能，从而降低整体效率。

相比之下，硅支持的Re基烯烃复分解催化剂表现出更复杂的失活机理。关键步骤涉及金属环丁烷中间体β位C-H键的活化，导致β-H转移过程[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。这导致形成烯丙基氢化物中间体，然后可以与乙烯反应生成高活性物种。这种物种可以通过相邻烷基配体之间的α-H抽提再生催化剂，或通过硅氧基配体对乙基片段的β-H抽提导致脱附[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。

这些过程的关键步骤可以总结如下：
1. 对于镧系/锕系催化剂：烯丙基C-H键活化和π-烯丙基表面络合物的形成。
2. 对于Re基催化剂：金属环丁烷中间体中的β-H转移，烯丙基氢化物中间体的形成，以及通过β-H抽提可能导致的脱附。

这些失活途径对催化剂性能和寿命有重要影响。对于Re基催化剂，脱附的可能性可能导致活性催化剂从支持表面流失，随时间推移降低整体催化剂负载和活性。此外，Re基系统中催化剂再生和脱附的竞争途径可能导致反应进行过程中性能逐渐下降[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：理解这些失活机理对于开发提高催化剂稳定性和寿命的策略至关重要。未来的研究努力可能会集中在修改配体结构或支持材料以最小化不必要的C-H活化过程。此外，开发新的表征技术来原位研究这些失活途径可能为这些过程的动力学和热力学提供宝贵的见解。这些知识可能导致设计出对失活更具抗性的更稳健的催化剂，潜在地扩大烯烃聚合和复分解反应在工业应用中的范围和效率。
