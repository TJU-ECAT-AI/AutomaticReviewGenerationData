Mechanisms and consequences of intramolecular C-H bond activation in the deactivation of supported alkene polymerization and metathesis catalysts, with emphasis on lanthanide/actinide-based and silica-supported Re-based systems

Intramolecular C-H bond activation plays a crucial role in the deactivation of supported alkene polymerization and metathesis catalysts, with distinct mechanisms observed for lanthanide/actinide-based and silica-supported Re-based catalysts [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. This review focuses on elucidating these mechanisms and their consequences on catalyst performance and longevity.

For lanthanide and actinide-based supported alkene polymerization catalysts, the deactivation pathway primarily involves the activation of the allylic C-H bond instead of the desired propene insertion. This process results in the formation of inactive π-allyl surface complexes, effectively deactivating the catalyst [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. The formation of these complexes directly reduces the number of active sites available for polymerization, leading to a significant decrease in overall catalyst efficiency.

In contrast, the deactivation mechanism for silica-supported Re-based alkene metathesis catalysts is more intricate. The key step in this process involves the activation of the C-H bond in the β-position of metallacyclobutane intermediates, leading to a β-H transfer process [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. This results in the formation of an allyl hydride intermediate, which can subsequently react with ethene to create a highly reactive species. This species can either regenerate the catalyst through α-H abstraction between adjacent alkyl ligands or lead to degrafting via β-H abstraction of the ethyl fragment by the siloxy ligand.

The key steps in these processes can be summarized as follows:
1. For lanthanide/actinide catalysts: Allylic C-H bond activation and formation of π-allyl surface complexes.
2. For Re-based catalysts: β-H transfer in metallacyclobutane intermediates, formation of allyl hydride intermediates, and potential degrafting through β-H abstraction.

These deactivation pathways have significant consequences for catalyst performance and longevity. In the case of Re-based catalysts, the potential for degrafting can lead to a loss of active catalyst from the support surface, reducing the overall catalyst loading and activity over time [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. Additionally, the competing pathways of catalyst regeneration and degrafting in Re-based systems may result in a gradual decline in performance as the reaction progresses.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Understanding these deactivation mechanisms is crucial for developing strategies to improve catalyst stability and longevity. Future research endeavors might focus on modifying ligand structures or support materials to minimize unwanted C-H activation processes. For instance, designing ligands that sterically hinder access to vulnerable C-H bonds could potentially reduce the occurrence of deactivation pathways. Additionally, exploring alternative support materials with different surface chemistries may offer opportunities to mitigate degrafting processes in Re-based systems.

Another promising avenue for future research could involve the development of in situ characterization techniques to monitor the formation of inactive species and degrafting processes in real-time. This would provide valuable insights into the kinetics of deactivation and potentially lead to the design of more robust catalytic systems. Furthermore, computational studies could be employed to predict and model the energetics of various C-H activation pathways, guiding the rational design of next-generation catalysts with enhanced stability and performance.

支持的烯烃聚合和复分解催化剂失活过程中分子内C-H键活化的机理和后果，重点关注镧系/锕系和基于硅胶支持的Re催化剂体系

分子内C-H键活化在支持的烯烃聚合和复分解催化剂的失活过程中起着至关重要的作用，其中镧系/锕系基和基于硅胶支持的Re催化剂观察到了不同的机理[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。本综述重点阐述了这些机理及其对催化剂性能和寿命的影响。

对于基于镧系和锕系的支持烯烃聚合催化剂，失活途径主要涉及烯丙基C-H键的活化，而不是所需的丙烯插入。这个过程导致了无活性的π-烯丙基表面络合物的形成，有效地使催化剂失活[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。这些络合物的形成直接减少了可用于聚合的活性位点数量，导致催化剂整体效率显著降低。

相比之下，基于硅胶支持的Re烯烃复分解催化剂的失活机理更为复杂。这个过程的关键步骤涉及金属环丁烷中间体β位C-H键的活化，导致β-H转移过程[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。这导致了烯丙基氢化物中间体的形成，随后可与乙烯反应生成高活性物种。这种物种可以通过相邻烷基配体之间的α-H抽提再生催化剂，或通过硅氧基配体对乙基片段的β-H抽提导致脱附。

这些过程的关键步骤可总结如下：
1. 对于镧系/锕系催化剂：烯丙基C-H键活化和π-烯丙基表面络合物的形成。
2. 对于Re基催化剂：金属环丁烷中间体中的β-H转移、烯丙基氢化物中间体的形成，以及通过β-H抽提可能导致的脱附。

这些失活途径对催化剂性能和寿命有重大影响。对于Re基催化剂，脱附的可能性可能导致活性催化剂从支持表面流失，随时间推移降低整体催化剂负载和活性[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。此外，Re基系统中催化剂再生和脱附的竞争途径可能导致反应进行过程中性能逐渐下降。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：理解这些失活机理对开发提高催化剂稳定性和寿命的策略至关重要。未来的研究努力可能会集中在修改配体结构或支持材料，以最小化不需要的C-H活化过程。例如，设计能够立体阻碍易受攻击的C-H键的配体可能会减少失活途径的发生。此外，探索具有不同表面化学性质的替代支持材料可能为减轻Re基系统中的脱附过程提供机会。

未来研究的另一个有前景的方向可能涉及开发原位表征技术，以实时监测无活性物种的形成和脱附过程。这将为失活动力学提供宝贵的见解，并可能导致设计出更稳健的催化系统。此外，计算研究可用于预测和模拟各种C-H活化途径的能量学，指导下一代具有增强稳定性和性能的催化剂的理性设计。
