Mechanisms and consequences of intramolecular C-H bond activation in the deactivation of supported alkene polymerization and metathesis catalysts, with emphasis on lanthanide/actinide-based and silica-supported Re-based systems

Intramolecular C-H bond activation plays a crucial role in the deactivation of supported alkene polymerization and metathesis catalysts, with distinct mechanisms observed for lanthanide/actinide-based and silica-supported Re-based catalysts [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. This review focuses on the specific deactivation pathways and their implications for catalyst performance and longevity.

For lanthanide and actinide-based supported alkene polymerization catalysts, the primary deactivation mechanism involves the activation of the allylic C-H bond instead of the desired propene insertion. This process results in the formation of inactive π-allyl surface complexes, effectively rendering the catalyst inoperative [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. The formation of these complexes directly reduces the number of active sites available for polymerization, leading to a decrease in overall catalyst efficiency.

In contrast, silica-supported Re-based alkene metathesis catalysts exhibit a more intricate deactivation mechanism. The key step in this process involves the activation of the C-H bond in the β-position of metallacyclobutane intermediates, resulting in a β-H transfer process [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. This leads to the formation of an allyl hydride intermediate, which can subsequently react with ethene to create a highly reactive species. This species can either regenerate the catalyst through α-H abstraction between adjacent alkyl ligands or lead to degrafting via β-H abstraction of the ethyl fragment by the siloxy ligand.

The consequences of these deactivation pathways are significant for catalyst performance and longevity. In the case of Re-based catalysts, the potential for degrafting can result in a loss of active catalyst from the support surface, reducing the overall catalyst loading and activity over time. Moreover, the competing pathways of catalyst regeneration and degrafting in Re-based systems may lead to a gradual decline in performance as the reaction progresses [10.1021/cr900122p_4].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
Understanding these deactivation mechanisms is crucial for developing strategies to improve catalyst stability and longevity. Future research efforts could focus on several key areas:

1. Ligand design: Developing novel ligand structures that minimize unwanted C-H activation processes while maintaining catalyst activity could significantly enhance catalyst stability.

2. Support material engineering: Investigating alternative support materials or surface modifications that can mitigate degrafting in Re-based systems may lead to more robust catalysts.

3. In-situ characterization techniques: Advancing spectroscopic and analytical methods for real-time monitoring of catalyst deactivation could provide valuable insights into the kinetics and mechanisms of these processes.

4. Computational modeling: Employing advanced computational techniques to predict and understand the energetics of C-H activation pathways could guide the rational design of more stable catalysts.

5. Regeneration strategies: Exploring methods to reverse or mitigate the formation of inactive π-allyl complexes in lanthanide/actinide systems could extend catalyst lifetimes and improve overall process efficiency.

By addressing these areas, researchers may develop next-generation supported alkene polymerization and metathesis catalysts with enhanced stability and performance, potentially opening new avenues for industrial applications and more efficient chemical processes.

支持烯烃聚合和复分解催化剂失活中分子内C-H键活化的机理和后果，重点关注镧系/锕系和基于硅胶的Re催化剂体系

分子内C-H键活化在支持烯烃聚合和复分解催化剂的失活过程中起着至关重要的作用，其中镧系/锕系和基于硅胶的Re催化剂体系表现出不同的机理[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。本综述重点关注特定的失活途径及其对催化剂性能和寿命的影响。

对于基于镧系和锕系的支持烯烃聚合催化剂，主要的失活机理涉及烯丙基C-H键的活化，而不是预期的丙烯插入。这一过程导致形成无活性的π-烯丙基表面络合物，有效地使催化剂失去活性[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。这些络合物的形成直接减少了可用于聚合的活性位点数量，导致催化剂整体效率下降。

相比之下，基于硅胶的Re烯烃复分解催化剂表现出更为复杂的失活机理。这一过程的关键步骤涉及金属环丁烷中间体β位置C-H键的活化，导致β-H转移过程[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。这导致形成烯丙基氢化物中间体，随后可与乙烯反应生成高活性物种。该物种可以通过相邻烷基配体之间的α-H抽提再生催化剂，或通过硅氧基配体对乙基片段的β-H抽提导致脱附。

这些失活途径对催化剂性能和寿命有显著影响。对于Re基催化剂，脱附的可能性可能导致活性催化剂从支持表面流失，随时间推移减少整体催化剂负载和活性。此外，Re基系统中催化剂再生和脱附的竞争途径可能导致反应进行过程中性能逐渐下降[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。

综合理解与未来展望：
理解这些失活机理对开发提高催化剂稳定性和寿命的策略至关重要。未来的研究工作可以集中在以下几个关键领域：

1. 配体设计：开发能够最小化不必要的C-H活化过程同时保持催化剂活性的新型配体结构，可以显著提高催化剂的稳定性。

2. 载体材料工程：研究可以减少Re基系统中脱附的替代载体材料或表面改性，可能会导致更稳定的催化剂。

3. 原位表征技术：发展用于实时监测催化剂失活的光谱和分析方法，可以为这些过程的动力学和机理提供宝贵的见解。

4. 计算模拟：采用先进的计算技术预测和理解C-H活化途径的能量学，可以指导更稳定催化剂的理性设计。

5. 再生策略：探索逆转或减轻镧系/锕系体系中无活性π-烯丙基络合物形成的方法，可以延长催化剂寿命并提高整体过程效率。

通过解决这些领域的问题，研究人员可能开发出具有增强稳定性和性能的下一代支持烯烃聚合和复分解催化剂，可能为工业应用和更高效的化学过程开辟新的途径。
