Intramolecular C-H bond activation plays a crucial role in the deactivation of supported alkene polymerization and metathesis catalysts, with distinct mechanisms observed for lanthanide/actinide-based and silica-supported Re-based catalysts [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. This review section will explore these mechanisms and their consequences on catalyst performance and longevity.

In lanthanide and actinide-based supported alkene polymerization catalysts, the deactivation pathway involves the activation of the allylic C-H bond instead of propene insertion. This process results in the formation of inactive π-allyl surface complexes, effectively deactivating the catalyst [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. The formation of these inactive complexes directly reduces the number of active sites available for polymerization, leading to a decrease in overall catalyst efficiency.

For silica-supported Re-based alkene metathesis catalysts, the deactivation mechanism is more intricate. The key step involves the activation of the C-H bond in the β-position of metallacyclobutane intermediates, leading to a β-H transfer process. This results in the formation of an allyl hydride intermediate, which can then react with ethene to create a highly reactive species [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. This species can either regenerate the catalyst through α-H abstraction between adjacent alkyl ligands or lead to degrafting via β-H abstraction of the ethyl fragment by the siloxy ligand.

The key steps in these processes can be summarized as follows:
1. For lanthanide/actinide catalysts: Allylic C-H bond activation and formation of π-allyl surface complexes.
2. For Re-based catalysts: β-H transfer in metallacyclobutane intermediates, formation of allyl hydride intermediates, and potential degrafting through β-H abstraction.

These deactivation pathways have significant consequences for catalyst performance and longevity. In the case of Re-based catalysts, the potential for degrafting can lead to a loss of active catalyst from the support surface, reducing the overall catalyst loading and activity over time. Additionally, the competing pathways of catalyst regeneration and degrafting in Re-based systems may result in a gradual decline in performance as the reaction progresses [10.1021/cr900122p_4].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Understanding these deactivation mechanisms is crucial for developing strategies to improve catalyst stability and longevity. Future research endeavors might focus on modifying ligand structures or support materials to minimize unwanted C-H activation processes. Additionally, investigating the use of novel support materials or catalyst designs that can mitigate the formation of π-allyl surface complexes in lanthanide/actinide-based catalysts could lead to significant improvements in catalyst performance. For Re-based catalysts, exploring ways to favor the catalyst regeneration pathway over the degrafting process could potentially extend catalyst lifetime. Furthermore, developing in situ characterization techniques to monitor the formation of inactive species and degrafting in real-time could provide valuable insights for optimizing reaction conditions and catalyst designs.

分子内C-H键活化在支持的烯烃聚合和复分解催化剂的失活中起着关键作用，镧系/锕系基和硅胶负载的Re基催化剂观察到不同的机制[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。本综述部分将探讨这些机制及其对催化剂性能和寿命的影响。

对于镧系和锕系基支持的烯烃聚合催化剂，失活途径涉及烯丙基C-H键的活化，而不是丙烯的插入。这个过程导致形成无活性的π-烯丙基表面络合物，有效地使催化剂失活[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。这些无活性络合物的形成直接减少了可用于聚合的活性位点数量，导致整体催化效率下降。

对于硅胶负载的Re基烯烃复分解催化剂，失活机制更为复杂。关键步骤涉及金属环丁烷中间体β位置C-H键的活化，导致β-H转移过程。这导致形成烯丙基氢化物中间体，然后可以与乙烯反应生成高度活性的物种[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。这种物种可以通过相邻烷基配体之间的α-H抽提再生催化剂，或通过硅氧基配体对乙基片段的β-H抽提导致脱离。

这些过程的关键步骤可以总结如下：
1. 对于镧系/锕系催化剂：烯丙基C-H键活化和π-烯丙基表面络合物的形成。
2. 对于Re基催化剂：金属环丁烷中间体中的β-H转移，烯丙基氢化物中间体的形成，以及通过β-H抽提可能的脱离。

这些失活途径对催化剂性能和寿命有重大影响。对于Re基催化剂，脱离的可能性可能导致活性催化剂从支持表面流失，随时间推移降低整体催化剂负载和活性。此外，Re基系统中催化剂再生和脱离的竞争途径可能导致反应进行过程中性能逐渐下降[10.1021/cr900122p_4]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：理解这些失活机制对开发提高催化剂稳定性和寿命的策略至关重要。未来的研究努力可能会集中在修改配体结构或支持材料以最小化不需要的C-H活化过程。此外，研究使用新型支持材料或催化剂设计，以减少镧系/锕系基催化剂中π-烯丙基表面络合物的形成，可能会导致催化剂性能的显著改善。对于Re基催化剂，探索有利于催化剂再生途径而非脱离过程的方法可能会延长催化剂寿命。此外，开发原位表征技术来实时监测无活性物种的形成和脱离过程，可以为优化反应条件和催化剂设计提供宝贵的见解。
