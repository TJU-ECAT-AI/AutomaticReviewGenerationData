Intramolecular C-H bond activation plays a crucial role in the deactivation of supported alkene polymerization and metathesis catalysts, with distinct mechanisms observed for lanthanide/actinide-based and silica-supported Re-based catalysts [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. 

For lanthanide and actinide-based supported alkene polymerization catalysts, the deactivation pathway involves the activation of the allylic C-H bond instead of propene insertion. This process results in the formation of inactive π-allyl surface complexes, effectively deactivating the catalyst [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. This mechanism significantly impacts the catalyst's performance by reducing the number of active sites available for polymerization, thereby decreasing overall catalyst efficiency.

In contrast, silica-supported Re-based alkene metathesis catalysts exhibit a more complex deactivation mechanism. The key step involves the activation of the C-H bond in the β-position of metallacyclobutane intermediates, leading to a β-H transfer process [10.1021/cr900122p_4]. This results in the formation of an allyl hydride intermediate, which can then react with ethene to create a highly reactive species. This species can either regenerate the catalyst through α-H abstraction between adjacent alkyl ligands or lead to degrafting via β-H abstraction of the ethyl fragment by the siloxy ligand [10.1021/cr900122p_4].

The key steps in these processes can be summarized as follows:
1. For lanthanide/actinide catalysts: Allylic C-H bond activation and formation of π-allyl surface complexes.
2. For Re-based catalysts: β-H transfer in metallacyclobutane intermediates, formation of allyl hydride intermediates, and potential degrafting through β-H abstraction.

These deactivation pathways have significant consequences for catalyst performance and longevity. In the case of Re-based catalysts, the potential for degrafting can lead to a loss of active catalyst from the support surface, reducing the overall catalyst loading and activity over time. Additionally, the competing pathways of catalyst regeneration and degrafting in Re-based systems may result in a gradual decline in performance as the reaction progresses [10.1021/cr900122p_4].

分子内C-H键活化在负载型烯烃聚合和复分解催化剂的失活过程中扮演着重要角色，镧系/锕系催化剂和硅胶负载的Re基催化剂表现出不同的机理 [10.1021/cr900122p_4]。

对于镧系和锕系负载型烯烃聚合催化剂，失活途径涉及烯丙基C-H键的活化，而非丙烯的插入。这一过程导致了不活性π-烯丙基表面络合物的形成，从而有效地使催化剂失活 [10.1021/cr900122p_4]。这种机理通过减少可用于聚合的活性位点数量，显著影响了催化剂的性能，从而降低了整体催化效率。

相比之下，硅胶负载的Re基烯烃复分解催化剂表现出更为复杂的失活机理。关键步骤涉及金属环丁烷中间体β位C-H键的活化，导致β-H转移过程 [10.1021/cr900122p_4]。这导致了烯丙基氢化物中间体的形成，该中间体随后可与乙烯反应生成高活性物种。这种物种可以通过相邻烷基配体之间的α-H抽提再生催化剂，或通过硅氧基配体对乙基片段的β-H抽提导致脱附 [10.1021/cr900122p_4]。

这些过程的关键步骤可以总结如下：
1. 对于镧系/锕系催化剂：烯丙基C-H键活化和π-烯丙基表面络合物的形成。
2. 对于Re基催化剂：金属环丁烷中间体中的β-H转移，烯丙基氢化物中间体的形成，以及通过β-H抽提可能导致的脱附。

这些失活途径对催化剂性能和寿命有重大影响。对于Re基催化剂，脱附的可能性可能导致活性催化剂从支持表面流失，随时间推移减少整体催化剂负载量和活性。此外，Re基系统中催化剂再生和脱附的竞争途径可能导致反应进行过程中性能逐渐下降 [10.1021/cr900122p_4]。
