<English>
Sulfur-based oxidants, including H2S, S2, and SO2, have shown promising potential in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) to propylene. This review compares their effectiveness with conventional oxidants, discusses their impact on propylene yield, and outlines specific challenges and areas for future research.

H2S as an oxidant:
H2S has demonstrated significant improvements in propylene yield and selectivity compared to conventional oxidation methods. Studies have shown that H2S can achieve propylene yields of up to 30.4% at 700°C using γ-Al2O3 and V/Al2O3 catalysts [10.1080/17415990412331327909]. In some cases, H2S-promoted ODH over molten salt catalysts at 705°C has resulted in propylene yields as high as 60% [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]. H2S is particularly effective at short residence times, suppressing cracking reactions and reducing the formation of unwanted byproducts such as COx, C2H4, and CH4 [10.1080/17415990412331327909].

S2 as an oxidant:
Elemental sulfur (S2) acts as a soft oxidant, reducing the overoxidation of propane. Studies have shown that S2 can improve propylene yield and selectivity, with one study reporting 73% selectivity at 650°C. However, conversion rates remain relatively low, with only 10% conversion at 650°C [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]. The use of S2 as an oxidant is particularly interesting because it can selectively enhance hydrocarbon dehydrogenation, and catalysts treated with sulfur vapors show considerably increased propylene formation rates [10.1016/S0167-2991(08)60185-8].

SO2 as an oxidant:
SO2 has been extensively studied as an oxidant for ODHP but has shown mixed results. While SO2 can increase the overall rate of propane conversion, it significantly decreases the selectivity to propylene formation. For example, at 855 K, propylene selectivity dropped from 93.1% to 20.6% when SO2 was added to the feed [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]. The main challenge with SO2 is balancing the increased conversion with maintaining high selectivity towards propylene.

Challenges associated with sulfur-based oxidants:
1. Byproduct formation: All sulfur-based oxidants face challenges with the formation of unwanted byproducts. These can include H2S, H2O, CO2, CS2, thiol mercaptans, and elemental sulfur (S8) [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].
2. Selectivity vs. conversion: Balancing high conversion rates with high selectivity towards propylene remains a significant challenge, especially for SO2 [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
3. Catalyst deactivation: Formation of coke and surface sulfides can lead to catalyst deactivation over time [10.1007/BF02071147].
4. High temperature requirements: Some sulfur-based oxidants, particularly S2, require very high temperatures to form reactive species, which can lead to thermal cracking of hydrocarbons [10.1002/cjce.5450480221].

Key areas for future research:
1. Catalyst design: Develop catalysts that can effectively utilize sulfur-based oxidants while maintaining high selectivity towards propylene and minimizing unwanted side reactions [10.1007/BF02071147].
2. Reaction mechanism studies: Further investigate the complex effects of sulfur-based oxidants on the dehydrogenation mechanism to understand and mitigate selectivity issues [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
3. Process optimization: Explore optimal reaction conditions (temperature, pressure, catalyst composition) to balance conversion and selectivity for each sulfur-based oxidant [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
4. Byproduct management: Develop strategies to reduce or utilize byproducts, particularly CO2 and H2S [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
5. Long-term stability: Investigate the long-term stability and regeneration of catalysts under sulfur-rich conditions [10.1080/17415990412331327909].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements and findings discussed in the file, there is significant potential for sulfur-based oxidants in ODHP. While each oxidant (H2S, S2, and SO2) presents unique advantages and challenges, they all offer promising alternatives to conventional oxidants. The ability of these sulfur-based oxidants to act as milder oxidizing agents compared to oxygen could lead to improved selectivity towards propylene, addressing a key challenge in ODHP processes.

Future research endeavors might focus on developing novel catalyst designs that can effectively harness the unique properties of sulfur-based oxidants while mitigating their drawbacks. This could involve exploring new catalyst support materials, investigating the synergistic effects of mixed metal oxides, or developing innovative strategies for in-situ catalyst regeneration. Additionally, the integration of advanced characterization techniques, such as operando spectroscopy, could provide deeper insights into the reaction mechanisms and catalyst behavior under realistic operating conditions.

Another promising avenue for future research is the exploration of hybrid systems that combine sulfur-based oxidants with conventional oxidants or other promoters. This approach could potentially leverage the strengths of different oxidants while minimizing their individual weaknesses. Furthermore, the development of more efficient separation and purification techniques for handling sulfur-containing byproducts could significantly enhance the overall process economics and environmental sustainability of sulfur-based ODHP processes.

As the demand for propylene continues to grow, the successful implementation of sulfur-based oxidants in ODHP could revolutionize propylene production, offering more efficient and environmentally friendly alternatives to current technologies. However, addressing the challenges associated with these oxidants will require continued collaborative efforts between researchers in catalysis, chemical engineering, and materials science.

<Chinese>
硫基氧化剂，包括H2S、S2和SO2，在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）制丙烯方面显示出了很大的潜力。本综述比较了它们与传统氧化剂的有效性，讨论了它们对丙烯产率的影响，并概述了具体的挑战和未来研究方向。

H2S作为氧化剂：
与传统氧化方法相比，H2S在丙烯产率和选择性方面表现出显著的改进。研究表明，在700°C下使用γ-Al2O3和V/Al2O3催化剂，H2S可以实现高达30.4%的丙烯产率[10.1080/17415990412331327909]。在某些情况下，在705°C下使用熔盐催化剂进行H2S促进的ODH可以获得高达60%的丙烯产率[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。H2S在短停留时间内特别有效，抑制了裂解反应并减少了COx、C2H4和CH4等不需要的副产物的形成[10.1080/17415990412331327909]。

S2作为氧化剂：
元素硫（S2）作为一种温和的氧化剂，可以减少丙烷的过度氧化。研究表明，S2可以提高丙烯产率和选择性，一项研究报告在650°C下实现了73%的选择性。然而，转化率仍然相对较低，在650°C下仅有10%的转化率[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。使用S2作为氧化剂特别有趣，因为它可以选择性地增强碳氢化合物的脱氢，经硫蒸气处理的催化剂显示出丙烯形成速率的显著增加[10.1016/S0167-2991(08)60185-8]。

SO2作为氧化剂：
SO2作为ODHP的氧化剂已经被广泛研究，但结果喜忧参半。虽然SO2可以增加丙烷的总体转化率，但它显著降低了丙烯形成的选择性。例如，在855 K下，当向进料中添加SO2时，丙烯选择性从93.1%降至20.6%[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。SO2的主要挑战是在增加转化率的同时保持对丙烯的高选择性。

与硫基氧化剂相关的挑战：
1. 副产物形成：所有硫基氧化剂都面临着不需要的副产物形成的挑战。这些可能包括H2S、H2O、CO2、CS2、硫醇和元素硫（S8）[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。
2. 选择性与转化率：平衡高转化率和高丙烯选择性仍然是一个重大挑战，特别是对于SO2 [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
3. 催化剂失活：焦炭和表面硫化物的形成可能导致催化剂随时间推移而失活[10.1007/BF02071147]。
4. 高温要求：一些硫基氧化剂，特别是S2，需要很高的温度才能形成活性物种，这可能导致碳氢化合物的热裂解[10.1002/cjce.5450480221]。

未来研究的关键领域：
1. 催化剂设计：开发能有效利用硫基氧化剂的催化剂，同时保持对丙烯的高选择性并最小化不需要的副反应[10.1007/BF02071147]。
2. 反应机理研究：进一步研究硫基氧化剂对脱氢机理的复杂影响，以理解和减轻选择性问题[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
3. 工艺优化：探索最佳反应条件（温度、压力、催化剂组成），以平衡每种硫基氧化剂的转化率和选择性[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
4. 副产物管理：开发减少或利用副产物的策略，特别是CO2和H2S [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
5. 长期稳定性：研究在富硫条件下催化剂的长期稳定性和再生[10.1080/17415990412331327909]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：考虑到文件中讨论的进展和发现，硫基氧化剂在ODHP中显示出巨大潜力。虽然每种氧化剂（H2S、S2和SO2）都存在独特的优势和挑战，但它们都为传统氧化剂提供了有前景的替代方案。这些硫基氧化剂作为比氧更温和的氧化剂的能力可能导致对丙烯的选择性提高，解决ODHP过程中的一个关键挑战。

未来的研究努力可能集中在开发新型催化剂设计上，这些设计可以有效利用硫基氧化剂的独特特性，同时减轻其缺点。这可能涉及探索新的催化剂载体材料，研究混合金属氧化物的协同效应，或开发创新的原位催化剂再生策略。此外，集成先进的表征技术，如原位光谱学，可以提供对实际操作条件下反应机理和催化剂行为的更深入了解。

未来研究的另一个有前景的方向是探索将硫基氧化剂与传统氧化剂或其他促进剂结合的混合系统。这种方法可能利用不同氧化剂的优势，同时最小化它们各自的弱点。此外，开发更高效的分离和纯化技术来处理含硫副产物，可能显著提高硫基ODHP过程的整体经济性和环境可持续性。

随着丙烯需求的持续增长，在ODHP中成功应用硫基氧化剂可能彻底改变丙烯生产，为当前技术提供更高效和环保的替代方案。然而，解决与这些氧化剂相关的挑战将需要催化、化学工程和材料科学研究人员之间持续的协作努力。
