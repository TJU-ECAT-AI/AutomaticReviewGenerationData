<English>
The effectiveness of different sulfur-based oxidants (H2S, S2, and SO2) compared to conventional oxidants in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) to propylene varies significantly, with each oxidant presenting unique advantages and challenges.

H2S as an oxidant:
H2S has shown promising results in ODHP, demonstrating improved propylene yields and selectivity compared to conventional oxidation methods. With H2S in the feed mixture, propylene selectivity up to 70% can be achieved with a conversion of 28.1% propane at 700°C [10.1080/17415990412331327909]. The best propylene yield reported was 30.4% using a V2O5/Al2O3 catalyst at 700°C with a short contact time of 0.005 s [10.1080/17415990412331327909]. H2S effectively suppresses the formation of unwanted byproducts such as COx, C2H4, and CH4 [10.1080/17415990412331327909].

S2 as an oxidant:
S2, or elemental sulfur vapor, acts as a soft oxidant and has shown potential in reducing overoxidation of propane. Studies have reported improved propylene yield and selectivity with S2, achieving 73% selectivity at 650°C. However, conversion rates remain low (10% at 650°C), and gas-phase reactions tend to dominate over catalytic reactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]. The use of S2 requires high temperatures (1200-1400°F) to form reactive diradical species, which could lead to thermal cracking of hydrocarbons [10.1002/cjce.5450480221].

SO2 as an oxidant:
SO2 has been extensively studied but has shown mixed results. While it increases propane conversion rates, it significantly decreases selectivity to propylene. For example, at 855 K, propylene selectivity dropped from 93.1% to 20.6% when SO2 was added to the feed [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]. SO2 tends to form COx products over bulk metal oxide catalysts, resulting in low propylene yields [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].

Challenges associated with sulfur-based oxidants:
1. Byproduct formation: All sulfur-based oxidants produce various byproducts, including H2S, H2O, CO2, CS2, and elemental sulfur (S8) [10.1016/S0167-2991(08)60185-8] [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].
2. Selectivity vs. conversion balance: Especially challenging for SO2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].
3. Catalyst deactivation: Formation of surface sulfides and coke can lead to catalyst deactivation over time [10.1007/BF02071147].
4. High temperature requirements: Particularly for S2, which may lead to unwanted side reactions [10.1002/cjce.5450480221].

Key areas for future research:
1. Catalyst design: Develop catalysts that maintain high selectivity while using sulfur-based oxidants, particularly for SO2 [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
2. Reaction mechanism elucidation: Further investigate the complex effects of sulfur-based oxidants on the dehydrogenation mechanism [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
3. Byproduct management: Explore ways to reduce or utilize byproducts, particularly CO2 and H2S [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
4. Process optimization: Investigate optimal reaction conditions (temperature, pressure, catalyst composition) to balance conversion and selectivity [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
5. Long-term stability: Study the long-term performance and regeneration of catalysts under sulfur-rich conditions [10.1080/17415990412331327909].
6. Novel catalyst supports and promoters: Explore new materials to enhance catalyst performance [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].
7. In-situ/operando characterization techniques: Develop methods to better understand the reaction mechanisms and catalyst behavior during ODHP [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The exploration of sulfur-based oxidants in ODHP presents a promising alternative to conventional oxidation methods. While each sulfur-based oxidant (H2S, S2, and SO2) has shown potential in improving certain aspects of the ODHP process, significant challenges remain. The use of H2S appears to be the most promising, with improved propylene yields and selectivity compared to conventional methods. However, the toxicity and handling of H2S pose additional safety concerns that need to be addressed.

Future research should focus on developing multifunctional catalysts that can effectively utilize sulfur-based oxidants while minimizing unwanted side reactions. This may involve exploring novel catalyst compositions, such as bimetallic or core-shell nanoparticles, that can balance the redox properties required for efficient ODHP. Additionally, the integration of in-situ spectroscopic techniques with machine learning algorithms could provide deeper insights into the reaction mechanisms and guide the rational design of catalysts.

Another promising direction is the exploration of hybrid systems that combine sulfur-based oxidants with conventional oxidants or other promoters. This approach could potentially synergize the benefits of different oxidation systems while mitigating their individual drawbacks. Furthermore, the development of membrane reactor technologies specifically tailored for sulfur-based ODHP could help in controlling the reaction environment and improving overall process efficiency.

As environmental concerns grow, future research should also focus on developing closed-loop systems that efficiently recover and recycle sulfur-based oxidants, minimizing waste and improving the sustainability of the ODHP process. This holistic approach to process design and optimization will be crucial in determining the viability of sulfur-based oxidants as a competitive alternative to conventional ODHP methods in industrial applications.

<Chinese>
与传统氧化剂相比，不同的硫基氧化剂（H2S、S2和SO2）在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）制丙烯过程中的效果差异显著，每种氧化剂都具有独特的优势和挑战。

H2S作为氧化剂：
H2S在ODHP中显示出有前景的结果，与传统氧化方法相比，丙烯产率和选择性都有所提高。在700°C下，使用H2S作为进料混合物，丙烯选择性可达70%，丙烷转化率为28.1% [10.1080/17415990412331327909]。使用V2O5/Al2O3催化剂，在700°C和0.005秒的短接触时间下，报告的最佳丙烯产率为30.4% [10.1080/17415990412331327909]。H2S有效抑制了COx、C2H4和CH4等不需要的副产物的形成 [10.1080/17415990412331327909]。

S2作为氧化剂：
S2或元素硫蒸气作为软氧化剂，在减少丙烷过度氧化方面显示出潜力。研究报告显示，使用S2可以提高丙烯产率和选择性，在650°C时达到73%的选择性。然而，转化率仍然较低（在650°C时为10%），气相反应往往主导催化反应 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。使用S2需要高温（1200-1400°F）才能形成活性双自由基物种，这可能导致碳氢化合物的热裂解 [10.1002/cjce.5450480221]。

SO2作为氧化剂：
SO2已被广泛研究，但结果喜忧参半。虽然它增加了丙烷转化率，但显著降低了对丙烯的选择性。例如，在855 K时，当向进料中添加SO2时，丙烯选择性从93.1%降至20.6% [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。SO2在大块金属氧化物催化剂上倾向于形成COx产物，导致丙烯产率低 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。

与硫基氧化剂相关的挑战：
1. 副产物形成：所有硫基氧化剂都会产生各种副产物，包括H2S、H2O、CO2、CS2和元素硫（S8）[10.1016/S0167-2991(08)60185-8] [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。
2. 选择性与转化率的平衡：对SO2来说尤其具有挑战性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。
3. 催化剂失活：表面硫化物和焦炭的形成可能导致催化剂随时间推移而失活 [10.1007/BF02071147]。
4. 高温要求：特别是对于S2，可能导致不需要的副反应 [10.1002/cjce.5450480221]。

未来研究的关键领域：
1. 催化剂设计：开发使用硫基氧化剂时仍能保持高选择性的催化剂，特别是对于SO2 [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
2. 反应机理阐明：进一步研究硫基氧化剂对脱氢机理的复杂影响 [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
3. 副产物管理：探索减少或利用副产物的方法，特别是CO2和H2S [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
4. 工艺优化：研究最佳反应条件（温度、压力、催化剂组成）以平衡转化率和选择性 [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
5. 长期稳定性：研究催化剂在富硫条件下的长期性能和再生 [10.1080/17415990412331327909]。
6. 新型催化剂载体和促进剂：探索新材料以提高催化剂性能 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。
7. 原位/操作过程表征技术：开发方法以更好地理解ODHP过程中的反应机理和催化剂行为 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：在ODHP中探索硫基氧化剂为传统氧化方法提供了一个有前景的替代方案。虽然每种硫基氧化剂（H2S、S2和SO2）都显示出改善ODHP过程某些方面的潜力，但仍然存在重大挑战。H2S的使用似乎最有前景，与传统方法相比，丙烯产率和选择性都有所提高。然而，H2S的毒性和处理带来的额外安全问题需要解决。

未来的研究应该集中在开发多功能催化剂上，这些催化剂可以有效利用硫基氧化剂，同时最大限度地减少不需要的副反应。这可能涉及探索新型催化剂组成，如双金属或核壳纳米粒子，以平衡高效ODHP所需的氧化还原性质。此外，将原位光谱技术与机器学习算法相结合，可能会对反应机理提供更深入的洞察，并指导催化剂的合理设计。

另一个有前景的方向是探索将硫基氧化剂与传统氧化剂或其他促进剂结合的混合系统。这种方法可能会使不同氧化系统的优势协同作用，同时减轻它们各自的缺点。此外，开发专门针对硫基ODHP的膜反应器技术可能有助于控制反应环境并提高整体过程效率。

随着环境问题日益严重，未来的研究还应该集中在开发高效回收和循环利用硫基氧化剂的闭环系统上，最大限度地减少废物并提高ODHP过程的可持续性。这种对过程设计和优化的整体方法将在确定硫基氧化剂作为工业应用中传统ODHP方法的竞争性替代品的可行性方面起到关键作用。
