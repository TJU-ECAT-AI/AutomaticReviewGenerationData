<English>
The use of sulfur-based oxidants (H2S, S2, and SO2) in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) to propylene has shown promising results compared to conventional oxidants. Each sulfur-based oxidant demonstrates unique characteristics in terms of effectiveness, propylene yield, and associated challenges.

H2S as an oxidant:
H2S has shown significant potential in improving propylene yield and selectivity. Studies have reported propylene yields of up to 30.4% at 700°C using γ-Al2O3 and V/Al2O3 catalysts, and even up to 60% yield with H2S-promoted ODH over molten salt catalysts at 705°C [10.1080/17415990412331327909]. The addition of H2S to the feed gas increases propene selectivity while reducing the formation of unwanted byproducts such as COx, C2H4, and CH4 [10.1080/17415990412331327909]. Notably, H2S can transform previously ineffective catalysts, such as V2O5/Al2O3, into effective catalysts for ODHP [10.1080/17415990412331327909].

S2 as an oxidant:
S2 acts as a soft oxidant, reducing the overoxidation of propane. It has been shown to improve propylene yield and selectivity, with one study reporting 73% selectivity at 650°C [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]. However, conversion rates remain low (10% at 650°C), and gas-phase reactions tend to dominate over catalytic reactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]. The use of S2 requires high temperatures (1200-1400°F) to form reactive diradical species, which could lead to thermal cracking of hydrocarbons [10.1002/cjce.5450480221].

SO2 as an oxidant:
SO2 has been extensively studied but has shown mixed results. While it increases propane conversion, it faces challenges in maintaining high propylene selectivity. Studies have reported that the addition of SO2 to the feed increases propane conversion but significantly decreases selectivity to propylene (from 93.1% to 20.6% in one case) [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]. SO2 tends to produce various byproducts, including H2S, H2O, and CO2 [10.1016/0021-9517(77)90228-7].

Challenges associated with sulfur-based oxidants:
1. Byproduct formation: All sulfur-based oxidants produce various byproducts, including sulfur-containing compounds like CS2, thiol mercaptans, and S8 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].
2. Balancing conversion and selectivity: This is particularly challenging for SO2 [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
3. High temperature requirements: Especially for S2, which needs high temperatures to form reactive species [10.1002/cjce.5450480221].
4. Catalyst deactivation: Formation of coke and surface sulfides can deactivate catalysts over time [10.1016/S0167-2991(08)60185-8].

Key areas for future research:
1. Catalyst design: Develop catalysts that can effectively utilize sulfur-based oxidants while minimizing unwanted side reactions and byproduct formation [10.1007/BF02071147].
2. Process optimization: Investigate optimal reaction conditions (temperature, pressure, catalyst composition) to balance conversion and selectivity [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
3. Reaction mechanisms: Further elucidate the role of sulfur species in the reaction mechanisms, particularly for H2S and S2 [10.1080/17415990412331327909].
4. Byproduct management: Explore ways to reduce or utilize byproducts, particularly CO2 and H2S [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
5. Long-term stability: Investigate the long-term stability and regeneration of catalysts under sulfur-rich conditions [10.1080/17415990412331327909].

In conclusion, while sulfur-based oxidants show potential for improving ODHP processes, significant research is still needed to overcome challenges related to propylene yield, selectivity, and process design before they can effectively compete with conventional oxidants.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The exploration of sulfur-based oxidants in ODHP represents a promising avenue for enhancing propylene production. The ability of these oxidants, particularly H2S and S2, to improve selectivity and yield while potentially operating at lower temperatures than conventional oxidants offers exciting possibilities for process intensification and energy efficiency. Future research should focus on developing multi-functional catalysts that can synergistically interact with sulfur species to promote propane activation while inhibiting overoxidation. Additionally, the integration of in-situ spectroscopic techniques to monitor the formation and transformation of active sulfur species during the reaction could provide crucial insights for optimizing catalyst design and reaction conditions. The potential for sulfur-based ODHP to utilize sulfur-containing waste streams as oxidants also presents an opportunity for sustainable process development, aligning with circular economy principles. As research progresses, it will be essential to conduct comprehensive life cycle assessments to fully understand the environmental implications and potential benefits of sulfur-based ODHP compared to conventional methods.

<Chinese>
与传统氧化剂相比，硫基氧化剂（H2S、S2和SO2）在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）制丙烯方面显示出了promising的结果。每种硫基氧化剂在有效性、丙烯产率和相关挑战方面都展现出独特的特性。

H2S作为氧化剂：
H2S在提高丙烯产率和选择性方面表现出显著潜力。研究报告显示，在700°C下使用γ-Al2O3和V/Al2O3催化剂可达到30.4%的丙烯产率，而在705°C下使用H2S促进的ODH熔盐催化剂甚至可达到60%的产率[10.1080/17415990412331327909]。向进料气中添加H2S可提高丙烯选择性，同时减少COx、C2H4和CH4等不需要的副产物的生成[10.1080/17415990412331327909]。值得注意的是，H2S可以将先前无效的催化剂（如V2O5/Al2O3）转变为ODHP的有效催化剂[10.1080/17415990412331327909]。

S2作为氧化剂：
S2作为一种温和的氧化剂，可减少丙烷的过度氧化。它被证明可以提高丙烯产率和选择性，一项研究报告在650°C下实现了73%的选择性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。然而，转化率仍然较低（650°C下为10%），并且气相反应往往主导催化反应[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。使用S2需要高温（1200-1400°F）以形成活性双自由基物种，这可能导致烃类的热裂解[10.1002/cjce.5450480221]。

SO2作为氧化剂：
SO2已被广泛研究，但结果不一。虽然它增加了丙烷转化率，但在维持高丙烯选择性方面面临挑战。研究表明，向进料中添加SO2会增加丙烷转化率，但显著降低丙烯选择性（在一种情况下从93.1%降至20.6%）[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。SO2倾向于产生各种副产物，包括H2S、H2O和CO2 [10.1016/0021-9517(77)90228-7]。

硫基氧化剂相关的挑战：
1. 副产物形成：所有硫基氧化剂都会产生各种副产物，包括含硫化合物如CS2、硫醇和S8 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。
2. 平衡转化率和选择性：这对SO2来说尤其具有挑战性[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
3. 高温要求：特别是对于S2，需要高温形成活性物种[10.1002/cjce.5450480221]。
4. 催化剂失活：焦炭和表面硫化物的形成可能导致催化剂随时间失活[10.1016/S0167-2991(08)60185-8]。

未来研究的关键领域：
1. 催化剂设计：开发能有效利用硫基氧化剂同时最小化不需要的副反应和副产物形成的催化剂[10.1007/BF02071147]。
2. 工艺优化：研究最佳反应条件（温度、压力、催化剂组成）以平衡转化率和选择性[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
3. 反应机理：进一步阐明硫物种在反应机理中的作用，特别是对于H2S和S2 [10.1080/17415990412331327909]。
4. 副产物管理：探索减少或利用副产物的方法，特别是CO2和H2S [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
5. 长期稳定性：研究在富硫条件下催化剂的长期稳定性和再生[10.1080/17415990412331327909]。

总之，虽然硫基氧化剂在改进ODHP工艺方面显示出潜力，但在它们能够有效地与传统氧化剂竞争之前，仍需要进行大量研究以克服与丙烯产率、选择性和工艺设计相关的挑战。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：在ODHP中探索硫基氧化剂代表了提高丙烯生产的一个很有前景的方向。这些氧化剂，特别是H2S和S2，能够提高选择性和产率，同时可能在比传统氧化剂更低的温度下运行，为工艺强化和能源效率提供了令人兴奋的可能性。未来的研究应该集中在开发多功能催化剂上，这些催化剂可以与硫物种协同作用，促进丙烷活化，同时抑制过度氧化。此外，集成原位光谱技术来监测反应过程中活性硫物种的形成和转化，可以为优化催化剂设计和反应条件提供关键见解。硫基ODHP利用含硫废物流作为氧化剂的潜力也为可持续工艺开发提供了机会，符合循环经济原则。随着研究的进展，进行全面的生命周期评估将至关重要，以充分理解硫基ODHP与传统方法相比的环境影响和潜在益处。
