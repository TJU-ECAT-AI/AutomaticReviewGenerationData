<English>
The oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) to propylene using sulfur-based oxidants (H2S, S2, and SO2) has shown promising results compared to conventional oxidants. Each sulfur-based oxidant demonstrates unique characteristics in terms of effectiveness, propylene yield, and associated challenges.

H2S as an oxidant:
H2S has shown significant potential in improving propylene yield and selectivity. When combined with O2, it achieved a propylene yield of 30.4% at 700°C using γ-Al2O3 and V/Al2O3 catalysts [10.1080/17415990412331327909]. In some cases, H2S-promoted ODH over molten salt catalysts at 705°C resulted in propylene yields up to 60% [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]. H2S is particularly effective at short residence times, suppressing cracking reactions and reducing the formation of unwanted byproducts such as COx, C2H4, and CH4 [10.1080/17415990412331327909].

S2 as an oxidant:
S2 acts as a soft oxidant, reducing the overoxidation of propane. It has shown improvements in propylene yield and selectivity, with 73% selectivity reported at 650°C [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]. However, conversion rates remain low (10% at 650°C), and gas-phase reactions tend to dominate over catalytic reactions. The use of S2 requires high temperatures (1200-1400°F) to form reactive species, which could lead to thermal cracking of hydrocarbons [10.1002/cjce.5450480221].

SO2 as an oxidant:
SO2 increases propane conversion but faces challenges in maintaining high propylene selectivity. The addition of SO2 to the feed increases propane conversion; however, the selectivity to propylene decreases significantly. For example, at 855 K, propylene selectivity dropped from 93.1% to 20.6% when SO2 was added [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]. SO2 tends to result in low propylene yield due to the formation of COx products over bulk metal oxide catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].

Challenges associated with sulfur-based oxidants:
1. Formation of sulfur-based byproducts (CS2, thiol mercaptans, S8) [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]
2. Balancing conversion and selectivity, especially for SO2 [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]
3. Kinetic inhibition of byproduct formation, particularly for S2ODHP [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]
4. Optimizing reaction conditions and catalyst compositions for each oxidant [10.1080/17415990412331327909]
5. Catalyst deactivation and regeneration [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]

Key areas for future research:
1. Developing structure-activity relationships for rational catalyst design [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]
2. Designing catalysts to inhibit byproduct formation and enhance selectivity [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]
3. Optimizing catalyst compositions and reaction conditions for each sulfur-based oxidant [10.1080/17415990412331327909]
4. Investigating long-term stability and regeneration of catalysts under sulfur-rich conditions [10.1007/BF02071147]
5. Exploring novel catalyst supports and promoters [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]
6. Developing in-situ/operando characterization techniques [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]
7. Understanding and controlling gas-phase reactions, especially for S2-based ODHP [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]
8. Developing catalysts with moderate redox properties to balance alkane activation and overoxidation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The use of sulfur-based oxidants in ODHP presents a promising alternative to conventional oxidants, offering potential improvements in propylene yield and selectivity. H2S has shown particularly encouraging results, especially when combined with O2, while S2 and SO2 have demonstrated unique advantages and challenges. The future of sulfur-based ODHP lies in addressing the key challenges identified, such as byproduct formation and catalyst stability.

Moving forward, research efforts should focus on developing novel catalyst designs that can effectively utilize sulfur-based oxidants while minimizing unwanted side reactions. This may involve exploring synergistic effects between different sulfur species or combining sulfur-based oxidants with other promoters. Additionally, advanced characterization techniques and computational modeling could provide deeper insights into reaction mechanisms and guide the rational design of more efficient catalysts.

The environmental impact of using sulfur-based oxidants should also be carefully considered, with efforts directed towards developing closed-loop systems that minimize sulfur emissions and maximize resource efficiency. As the chemical industry continues to seek more sustainable processes, the potential of sulfur-based ODHP to utilize waste sulfur compounds (e.g., H2S from natural gas processing) could provide an additional incentive for further development of this technology.

<Chinese>
与传统氧化剂相比，使用硫基氧化剂（H2S、S2和SO2）进行丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）制丙烯已显示出有前景的结果。每种硫基氧化剂在有效性、丙烯产率和相关挑战方面都展现出独特的特性。

H2S作为氧化剂：
H2S在提高丙烯产率和选择性方面显示出显著潜力。当与O2结合时，在700°C下使用γ-Al2O3和V/Al2O3催化剂可达到30.4%的丙烯产率[10.1080/17415990412331327909]。在某些情况下，在705°C下使用H2S促进的ODH熔盐催化剂可获得高达60%的丙烯产率[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。H2S在短停留时间内特别有效，抑制裂解反应并减少COx、C2H4和CH4等不需要的副产物的形成[10.1080/17415990412331327909]。

S2作为氧化剂：
S2作为软氧化剂，可减少丙烷的过度氧化。它在丙烯产率和选择性方面显示出改善，在650°C下报告的选择性为73%[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。然而，转化率仍然较低（650°C下为10%），并且气相反应往往主导催化反应。使用S2需要高温（1200-1400°F）来形成活性物种，这可能导致烃类的热裂解[10.1002/cjce.5450480221]。

SO2作为氧化剂：
SO2增加丙烷转化率，但在维持高丙烯选择性方面面临挑战。向进料中添加SO2会增加丙烷转化率；然而，丙烯的选择性显著降低。例如，在855 K下，当添加SO2时，丙烯选择性从93.1%降至20.6%[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。由于在大块金属氧化物催化剂上形成COx产物，SO2往往导致较低的丙烯产率[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。

硫基氧化剂相关的挑战：
1. 形成含硫副产物（CS2、硫醇、S8）[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]
2. 平衡转化率和选择性，特别是对于SO2 [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]
3. 副产物形成的动力学抑制，特别是对于S2ODHP [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]
4. 优化每种氧化剂的反应条件和催化剂组成 [10.1080/17415990412331327909]
5. 催化剂失活和再生 [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]

未来研究的关键领域：
1. 开发结构-活性关系，用于理性催化剂设计 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]
2. 设计催化剂以抑制副产物形成并提高选择性 [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]
3. 优化每种硫基氧化剂的催化剂组成和反应条件 [10.1080/17415990412331327909]
4. 研究在含硫条件下催化剂的长期稳定性和再生 [10.1007/BF02071147]
5. 探索新型催化剂载体和促进剂 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]
6. 开发原位/操作条件下的表征技术 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]
7. 理解和控制气相反应，特别是对于基于S2的ODHP [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]
8. 开发具有适度氧化还原性能的催化剂，以平衡烷烃活化和过度氧化 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：在ODHP中使用硫基氧化剂为传统氧化剂提供了一个有前景的替代方案，有可能提高丙烯产率和选择性。H2S特别是在与O2结合时显示出令人鼓舞的结果，而S2和SO2则展示了独特的优势和挑战。硫基ODHP的未来在于解决已识别的关键挑战，如副产物形成和催化剂稳定性。

展望未来，研究工作应集中在开发能有效利用硫基氧化剂同时最大限度减少不需要的副反应的新型催化剂设计上。这可能涉及探索不同硫物种之间的协同效应，或将硫基氧化剂与其他促进剂结合。此外，先进的表征技术和计算机模拟可以提供对反应机理更深入的理解，并指导更高效催化剂的理性设计。

使用硫基氧化剂的环境影响也应该被仔细考虑，努力开发封闭循环系统以最小化硫排放并最大化资源效率。随着化工行业不断寻求更可持续的工艺，硫基ODHP利用废硫化合物（如天然气处理中的H2S）的潜力可能为该技术的进一步发展提供额外的动力。
