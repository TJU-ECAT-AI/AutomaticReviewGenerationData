<English>
The oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) to propylene using sulfur-based oxidants has garnered significant attention as an alternative to conventional oxidants. This review focuses on comparing the effectiveness of different sulfur-based oxidants (H2S, S2, and SO2) with conventional oxidants in ODHP.

1. Effectiveness and Propylene Yield:

H2S:
- Significantly improves propylene yield and selectivity compared to conventional oxidation [10.1080/17415990412331327909].
- Achieves up to 70% selectivity to propene with 28.1% propane conversion at 700°C [10.1080/17415990412331327909].
- Best propene yield of 30.4% over V2O5/Al2O3 catalyst at 700°C and 0.005s contact time [10.1080/17415990412331327909].
- H2S-promoted ODH over molten salt catalysts at 705°C can achieve up to 60% propylene yield [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].

S2:
- Acts as a soft oxidant, reducing overoxidation of propane [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].
- Improves propylene yield and selectivity (73% selectivity at 650°C) [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].
- However, conversion rates remain low (10% at 650°C) [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].

SO2:
- Increases overall propane conversion but significantly decreases selectivity to propylene [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
- At 855 K, propylene selectivity dropped from 93.1% to 20.6% when SO2 was added [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
- Results in low propylene yield due to the formation of COx products over bulk metal oxide catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].

2. Challenges:

H2S:
- Potential formation of sulfur-containing byproducts [10.1080/17415990412331327909].
- Need for optimized reaction conditions [10.1080/17415990412331327909].
- Handling of toxic gas, requiring additional safety measures [10.1080/17415990412331327909].

S2:
- High temperature requirements (1200-1400°F) to form reactive species [10.1002/cjce.5450480221].
- Dominance of gas-phase reactions over catalytic reactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].
- Formation of sulfur-based byproducts (CS2, thiol mercaptans, S8) [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].

SO2:
- Significant decrease in propylene selectivity [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
- Formation of undesired byproducts (H2S, H2O, CO2) [10.1016/0021-9517(77)90228-7].
- Catalyst deactivation over time [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].

3. Key Areas for Future Research:

1. Catalyst design:
   - Develop catalysts that maintain high selectivity while using sulfur-based oxidants [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
   - Design catalysts to inhibit byproduct formation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].
   - Explore novel catalyst supports and promoters [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].

2. Reaction mechanisms:
   - Further investigate the complex effect of sulfur-based oxidants on the dehydrogenation mechanism [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
   - Understand and control gas-phase reactions, especially for S2-based ODHP [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].

3. Process optimization:
   - Optimize reaction conditions (temperature, pressure, catalyst composition) to balance conversion and selectivity [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
   - Investigate long-term stability and regeneration of catalysts under sulfur-rich conditions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].

4. Byproduct management:
   - Explore ways to reduce or utilize byproducts, particularly CO2 and H2S [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
   - Develop efficient separation techniques for sulfur-containing byproducts [10.1016/0021-9517(77)90228-7].

5. Comparative studies:
   - Conduct comprehensive research on H2S, S2, and SO2 to compare their effectiveness and challenges with conventional oxidants [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The use of sulfur-based oxidants in ODHP presents a promising alternative to conventional oxidation methods, with each oxidant offering unique advantages and challenges. H2S shows the most promise, significantly improving propylene yield and selectivity, while S2 acts as a softer oxidant but struggles with low conversion rates. SO2, despite increasing conversion, faces selectivity issues.

Future research should focus on developing tailored catalysts for each sulfur-based oxidant, optimizing reaction conditions, and addressing byproduct formation. A deeper understanding of the reaction mechanisms, particularly the role of intermediate sulfur species, could lead to breakthrough improvements in catalyst design. Additionally, exploring synergistic effects between different sulfur-based oxidants or combining them with conventional oxidants may open new avenues for process optimization.

The environmental and economic aspects of using sulfur-based oxidants should also be thoroughly evaluated, considering the potential for sulfur recovery and the mitigation of sulfur emissions from other industrial processes. As the chemical industry moves towards more sustainable practices, the integration of sulfur-based ODHP could potentially offer a dual benefit of improved propylene production and sulfur utilization.

<Chinese>
使用含硫氧化剂进行丙烷氧化脱氢制丙烯(ODHP)作为传统氧化剂的替代方法引起了广泛关注。本综述重点比较了不同含硫氧化剂(H2S、S2和SO2)与传统氧化剂在ODHP中的有效性。

1. 有效性和丙烯产率：

H2S：
- 与传统氧化相比，显著提高了丙烯产率和选择性[10.1080/17415990412331327909]。
- 在700°C下实现了28.1%的丙烷转化率和高达70%的丙烯选择性[10.1080/17415990412331327909]。
- 在700°C和0.005秒接触时间下，使用V2O5/Al2O3催化剂获得了30.4%的最佳丙烯产率[10.1080/17415990412331327909]。
- 在705°C下，使用H2S促进的熔盐催化剂ODH可实现高达60%的丙烯产率[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。

S2：
- 作为软氧化剂，减少了丙烷的过度氧化[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。
- 提高了丙烯产率和选择性（在650°C下选择性为73%）[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。
- 然而，转化率仍然较低（在650°C下为10%）[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。

SO2：
- 增加了总体丙烷转化率，但显著降低了丙烯选择性[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
- 在855 K时，添加SO2后丙烯选择性从93.1%降至20.6%[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
- 由于在大块金属氧化物催化剂上形成COx产物，导致丙烯产率低[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。

2. 挑战：

H2S：
- 可能形成含硫副产物[10.1080/17415990412331327909]。
- 需要优化反应条件[10.1080/17415990412331327909]。
- 处理有毒气体，需要额外的安全措施[10.1080/17415990412331327909]。

S2：
- 需要高温（1200-1400°F）形成活性物种[10.1002/cjce.5450480221]。
- 气相反应占主导地位，而非催化反应[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。
- 形成含硫副产物（CS2、硫醇、S8）[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。

SO2：
- 丙烯选择性显著降低[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
- 形成不需要的副产物（H2S、H2O、CO2）[10.1016/0021-9517(77)90228-7]。
- 催化剂随时间失活[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。

3. 未来研究的关键领域：

1. 催化剂设计：
   - 开发使用含硫氧化剂时保持高选择性的催化剂[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
   - 设计抑制副产物形成的催化剂[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。
   - 探索新型催化剂载体和促进剂[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。

2. 反应机理：
   - 进一步研究含硫氧化剂对脱氢机理的复杂影响[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
   - 理解和控制气相反应，特别是对于基于S2的ODHP[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。

3. 工艺优化：
   - 优化反应条件（温度、压力、催化剂组成）以平衡转化率和选择性[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
   - 研究催化剂在含硫条件下的长期稳定性和再生[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。

4. 副产物管理：
   - 探索减少或利用副产物的方法，特别是CO2和H2S[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
   - 开发含硫副产物的高效分离技术[10.1016/0021-9517(77)90228-7]。

5. 比较研究：
   - 对H2S、S2和SO2进行全面研究，比较它们与传统氧化剂的有效性和挑战[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：在ODHP中使用含硫氧化剂为传统氧化方法提供了一种有前景的替代方案，每种氧化剂都具有独特的优势和挑战。H2S表现最为突出，显著提高了丙烯产率和选择性，而S2作为较温和的氧化剂，但面临转化率低的问题。SO2虽然提高了转化率，但面临选择性问题。

未来研究应集中在为每种含硫氧化剂开发定制催化剂，优化反应条件，并解决副产物形成问题。对反应机理的深入理解，特别是中间硫物种的作用，可能会带来催化剂设计的突破性改进。此外，探索不同含硫氧化剂之间的协同效应或将它们与传统氧化剂结合，可能会为工艺优化开辟新的途径。

还应全面评估使用含硫氧化剂的环境和经济方面，考虑硫回收的潜力和减少其他工业过程中硫排放的可能性。随着化工行业向更可持续的实践发展，基于硫的ODHP的整合可能会带来改善丙烯生产和硫利用的双重好处。
