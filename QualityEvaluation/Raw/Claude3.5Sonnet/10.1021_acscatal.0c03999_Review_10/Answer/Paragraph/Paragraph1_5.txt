<English>
The use of sulfur-based oxidants (H2S, S2, and SO2) in the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) to propylene has shown promising results compared to conventional oxidants. However, each sulfur-based oxidant exhibits different effectiveness, challenges, and impacts on propylene yield and selectivity.

H2S as an oxidant:
H2S has demonstrated significant improvements in propylene yield and selectivity. When combined with O2, it achieved a 30.4% propylene yield at 700°C using γ-Al2O3 and V/Al2O3 catalysts [10.1080/17415990412331327909]. Even higher yields of up to 60% were obtained with H2S-promoted ODH over molten salt catalysts at 705°C [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]. H2S is particularly effective at short residence times, suppressing cracking reactions and reducing the formation of unwanted byproducts such as COx, C2H4, and CH4 [10.1080/17415990412331327909].

S2 as an oxidant:
S2 acts as a soft oxidant, reducing the overoxidation of propane. It has shown improvements in propylene yield and selectivity, with 73% selectivity reported at 650°C [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]. However, conversion rates remain relatively low (10% at 650°C), and gas-phase reactions tend to dominate over catalytic reactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]. The use of S2 requires high temperatures (1200-1400°F) to form reactive species, which could lead to thermal cracking of hydrocarbons [10.1002/cjce.5450480221].

SO2 as an oxidant:
SO2 increases propane conversion but faces challenges in maintaining high propylene selectivity. While it can increase the overall rate of propane conversion, the selectivity to propylene formation decreases significantly [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]. For example, at 855 K, propylene selectivity dropped from 93.1% to 20.6% when SO2 was added [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]. SO2 tends to result in low propylene yields due to the formation of COx products over bulk metal oxide catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].

Challenges associated with sulfur-based oxidants:
1. Byproduct formation: All sulfur-based oxidants face challenges with the formation of various byproducts, including H2S, H2O, CO2, CS2, thiol mercaptans, and elemental sulfur (S8) [10.1016/S0167-2991(08)60185-8] [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].
2. Selectivity vs. conversion balance: Especially for SO2, there is a trade-off between increasing conversion and maintaining high selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].
3. Catalyst deactivation: Formation of coke and surface sulfides can lead to catalyst deactivation over time [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
4. Process design challenges: The presence of sulfur species and formation of sulfur-containing byproducts pose challenges for process design and product separation [10.1016/S0167-2991(08)60185-8].

Key areas for future research:
1. Catalyst design: Develop catalysts that can effectively utilize sulfur-based oxidants while minimizing unwanted side reactions and byproduct formation [10.1007/BF02071147].
2. Reaction mechanisms: Further investigate the complex effects of sulfur-based oxidants on the dehydrogenation mechanism to understand and mitigate selectivity issues [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
3. Process optimization: Optimize reaction conditions (temperature, pressure, catalyst composition) to balance conversion and selectivity for each sulfur-based oxidant [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
4. Byproduct management: Explore ways to reduce or utilize byproducts, particularly CO2 and H2S [10.1002/jctb.5040340407].
5. Long-term stability: Investigate the long-term stability and regeneration of catalysts under sulfur-rich conditions [10.1080/17415990412331327909].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements and findings discussed in the file, there is a significant opportunity to explore the potential of sulfur-based oxidants in ODHP. While each sulfur-based oxidant (H2S, S2, and SO2) has shown promise in different aspects of the reaction, they all face unique challenges that need to be addressed. 

Future research endeavors might focus on developing multi-functional catalysts that can effectively utilize the strengths of different sulfur-based oxidants while mitigating their weaknesses. For instance, combining the high selectivity of H2S with the conversion capabilities of SO2 could lead to a more efficient ODHP process. Additionally, exploring novel reactor designs that can better control the reaction environment and manage byproduct formation could be crucial in optimizing the use of sulfur-based oxidants.

Another promising avenue for research is the development of in-situ characterization techniques that can provide real-time information on the catalyst surface and reaction intermediates during ODHP with sulfur-based oxidants. This could lead to a deeper understanding of the reaction mechanisms and guide the design of more effective catalysts and processes.

Lastly, considering the environmental implications of using sulfur-based oxidants, future studies should also focus on developing closed-loop systems that can efficiently recover and recycle sulfur species, making the process more sustainable and economically viable.

<Chinese>
与传统氧化剂相比，硫基氧化剂（H2S、S2和SO2）在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）生产丙烯方面显示出了promising的结果。然而，每种硫基氧化剂在有效性、挑战性以及对丙烯产率和选择性的影响方面都有所不同。

H2S作为氧化剂：
H2S在提高丙烯产率和选择性方面表现出显著改善。当与O2结合使用时，在700°C下使用γ-Al2O3和V/Al2O3催化剂可达到30.4%的丙烯产率[10.1080/17415990412331327909]。在705°C下使用熔盐催化剂进行H2S促进的ODH甚至可获得高达60%的产率[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。H2S在短停留时间内特别有效，抑制了裂解反应并减少了COx、C2H4和CH4等不需要的副产物的形成[10.1080/17415990412331327909]。

S2作为氧化剂：
S2作为一种软氧化剂，可以减少丙烷的过度氧化。它在丙烯产率和选择性方面显示出改善，在650°C下报告的选择性为73%[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。然而，转化率仍然相对较低（650°C下为10%），并且气相反应往往主导催化反应[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。使用S2需要高温（1200-1400°F）来形成活性物种，这可能导致碳氢化合物的热裂解[10.1002/cjce.5450480221]。

SO2作为氧化剂：
SO2增加了丙烷转化率，但在保持高丙烯选择性方面面临挑战。虽然它可以提高丙烷的整体转化率，但丙烯形成的选择性显著降低[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。例如，在855 K下，当添加SO2时，丙烯选择性从93.1%下降到20.6%[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。由于在块状金属氧化物催化剂上形成COx产物，SO2往往导致低丙烯产率[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。

与硫基氧化剂相关的挑战：
1. 副产物形成：所有硫基氧化剂都面临各种副产物形成的挑战，包括H2S、H2O、CO2、CS2、硫醇和元素硫（S8）[10.1016/S0167-2991(08)60185-8] [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。
2. 选择性与转化率的平衡：特别是对于SO2，在提高转化率和保持高选择性之间存在权衡[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。
3. 催化剂失活：焦炭和表面硫化物的形成可能导致催化剂随时间推移而失活[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
4. 工艺设计挑战：硫物种的存在和含硫副产物的形成对工艺设计和产品分离提出了挑战[10.1016/S0167-2991(08)60185-8]。

未来研究的关键领域：
1. 催化剂设计：开发能够有效利用硫基氧化剂同时最小化不需要的副反应和副产物形成的催化剂[10.1007/BF02071147]。
2. 反应机理：进一步研究硫基氧化剂对脱氢机理的复杂影响，以理解和缓解选择性问题[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
3. 工艺优化：优化反应条件（温度、压力、催化剂组成）以平衡每种硫基氧化剂的转化率和选择性[10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
4. 副产物管理：探索减少或利用副产物的方法，特别是CO2和H2S [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。
5. 长期稳定性：研究在富硫条件下催化剂的长期稳定性和再生[10.1080/17415990412331327909]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：考虑到文件中讨论的先进成果和发现，在ODHP中探索硫基氧化剂的潜力存在重要机会。虽然每种硫基氧化剂（H2S、S2和SO2）在反应的不同方面都显示出前景，但它们都面临需要解决的独特挑战。

未来的研究努力可能会集中在开发多功能催化剂上，这些催化剂能够有效利用不同硫基氧化剂的优势，同时减轻它们的劣势。例如，将H2S的高选择性与SO2的转化能力相结合，可能会导致更高效的ODHP过程。此外，探索能够更好地控制反应环境和管理副产物形成的新型反应器设计，可能对优化硫基氧化剂的使用至关重要。

另一个有前景的研究方向是开发原位表征技术，这些技术可以在使用硫基氧化剂进行ODHP过程中提供关于催化剂表面和反应中间体的实时信息。这可能会导致对反应机理的更深入理解，并指导更有效的催化剂和工艺设计。

最后，考虑到使用硫基氧化剂的环境影响，未来的研究还应该关注开发能够有效回收和循环利用硫物种的闭环系统，使过程更加可持续和经济可行。
