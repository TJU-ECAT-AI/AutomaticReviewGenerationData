<English>
The use of sulfur-based oxidants (H2S, S2, and SO2) for the oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) to propylene has shown promising results compared to conventional oxidants. However, each sulfur-based oxidant presents unique advantages and challenges in terms of propylene yield, selectivity, and process considerations.

H2S as an oxidant:
H2S has demonstrated significant improvements in propylene yield and selectivity. Studies have shown that the addition of H2S to the feed gas can increase propylene selectivity up to 70% with a propane conversion of 28.1% at 700°C [10.1080/17415990412331327909]. The best propylene yield achieved was 30.4% using a V2O5/Al2O3 catalyst in the presence of H2S at 700°C with a short contact time of 0.005 s [10.1080/17415990412331327909]. H2S-promoted ODH over molten salt catalysts has even achieved propylene yields up to 60% at 705°C [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].

H2S effectively suppresses the formation of undesired byproducts such as COx, C2H4, and CH4, and improves catalyst performance. For instance, V2O5/Al2O3 catalyst, which was selective for COx formation without H2S, became effective for propylene production when H2S was added to the feed gas [10.1080/17415990412331327909].

S2 as an oxidant:
S2 acts as a soft oxidant, reducing overoxidation of propane. It has shown improvements in propylene yield and selectivity, with one study reporting 73% selectivity at 650°C. However, conversion rates remain low (10% at 650°C), and gas-phase reactions tend to dominate over catalytic reactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]. The use of S2 requires high temperatures (1200-1400°F) to form reactive diradical species, which could lead to thermal cracking of hydrocarbons [10.1002/cjce.5450480221].

SO2 as an oxidant:
SO2 has been extensively studied but has shown mixed results. While it increases propane conversion, it significantly decreases selectivity to propylene. For example, at 855 K, propylene selectivity dropped from 93.1% to 20.6% when SO2 was added [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]. The main challenge with SO2 is the formation of COx products over bulk metal oxide catalysts, resulting in low propylene yields [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10].

Challenges associated with sulfur-based oxidants:
1. Formation of sulfur-based byproducts (CS2, thiol mercaptans, S8) [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]
2. Balancing conversion and selectivity, especially for SO2
3. High temperature requirements for S2, potentially leading to thermal cracking
4. Catalyst deactivation and coke formation
5. Handling and safety concerns with toxic gases like H2S

Key areas for future research:
1. Developing structure-activity relationships for rational catalyst design [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]
2. Optimizing catalyst compositions to enhance selectivity while maintaining high conversion rates
3. Investigating reaction mechanisms to understand the role of sulfur species in ODHP
4. Exploring synergistic effects between sulfur-based and conventional oxidants
5. Addressing environmental concerns related to sulfur-based oxidants
6. Developing in-situ/operando characterization techniques
7. Optimizing reaction conditions (temperature, pressure, contact time) for each sulfur-based oxidant

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements and findings discussed, sulfur-based oxidants show significant potential for improving ODHP processes. H2S appears to be the most promising among the sulfur-based oxidants, offering high propylene yields and selectivity. However, each oxidant has its unique advantages and challenges. Future research should focus on developing novel catalysts that can effectively utilize these sulfur-based oxidants while minimizing byproduct formation and addressing safety concerns. Additionally, exploring hybrid systems that combine sulfur-based oxidants with conventional oxidants or other promoters could lead to breakthrough improvements in ODHP technology. The development of advanced characterization techniques and computational modeling approaches will be crucial in understanding the complex reaction mechanisms and guiding rational catalyst design. Ultimately, the success of sulfur-based ODHP will depend on achieving a balance between high propylene yields, process efficiency, and environmental sustainability.

<Chinese>
与传统氧化剂相比，使用硫基氧化剂（H2S、S2和SO2）进行丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）制丙烯已显示出promising前景。然而，每种硫基氧化剂在丙烯产率、选择性和工艺考虑方面都具有独特的优势和挑战。

H2S作为氧化剂：
H2S显著提高了丙烯产率和选择性。研究表明，在进料气中添加H2S可以将丙烯选择性提高到70%，在700°C下丙烷转化率为28.1% [10.1080/17415990412331327909]。在H2S存在下，使用V2O5/Al2O3催化剂，在700°C和0.005秒的短接触时间下，达到的最佳丙烯产率为30.4% [10.1080/17415990412331327909]。在705°C下，H2S促进的ODH过程使用熔盐催化剂甚至达到了60%的丙烯产率 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。

H2S有效抑制了COx、C2H4和CH4等不良副产物的形成，并改善了催化剂性能。例如，在没有H2S的情况下选择性生成COx的V2O5/Al2O3催化剂，在进料气中添加H2S后变得有效地生产丙烯 [10.1080/17415990412331327909]。

S2作为氧化剂：
S2作为一种温和的氧化剂，减少了丙烷的过度氧化。它在丙烯产率和选择性方面显示出改进，一项研究报告在650°C下选择性达到73%。然而，转化率仍然较低（650°C下为10%），气相反应往往主导催化反应 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。使用S2需要高温（1200-1400°F）才能形成活性双自由基物种，这可能导致碳氢化合物的热裂解 [10.1002/cjce.5450480221]。

SO2作为氧化剂：
SO2已被广泛研究，但结果喜忧参半。虽然它增加了丙烷转化率，但显著降低了丙烯的选择性。例如，在855 K下，当添加SO2时，丙烯选择性从93.1%下降到20.6% [10.1002/jctb.5040340407]。SO2的主要挑战是在大块金属氧化物催化剂上形成COx产物，导致丙烯产率低 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]。

与硫基氧化剂相关的挑战：
1. 形成含硫副产物（CS2、硫醇、S8）[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]
2. 平衡转化率和选择性，尤其是对于SO2
3. S2需要高温，可能导致热裂解
4. 催化剂失活和积碳形成
5. 处理和安全问题，特别是对于H2S等有毒气体

未来研究的关键领域：
1. 开发结构-活性关系，实现合理的催化剂设计 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_10]
2. 优化催化剂成分，以提高选择性同时保持高转化率
3. 研究反应机理，了解硫物种在ODHP中的作用
4. 探索硫基氧化剂和传统氧化剂之间的协同效应
5. 解决与硫基氧化剂相关的环境问题
6. 开发原位/操作中表征技术
7. 优化每种硫基氧化剂的反应条件（温度、压力、接触时间）

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：考虑到讨论的先进成果和发现，硫基氧化剂在改进ODHP工艺方面显示出重大潜力。H2S似乎是硫基氧化剂中最有前景的，提供了高丙烯产率和选择性。然而，每种氧化剂都有其独特的优势和挑战。未来的研究应该集中在开发能够有效利用这些硫基氧化剂的新型催化剂，同时最大限度地减少副产物的形成并解决安全问题。此外，探索将硫基氧化剂与传统氧化剂或其他促进剂结合的混合系统可能会带来ODHP技术的突破性改进。先进表征技术和计算建模方法的开发将对理解复杂的反应机理和指导合理的催化剂设计至关重要。最终，硫基ODHP的成功将取决于在高丙烯产率、工艺效率和环境可持续性之间实现平衡。
