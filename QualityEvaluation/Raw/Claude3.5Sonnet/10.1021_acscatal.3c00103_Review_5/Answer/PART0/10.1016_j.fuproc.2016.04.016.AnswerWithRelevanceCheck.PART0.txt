      <English>
          The primary challenges in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) for industrial propylene production include:

1. Thermodynamic limitations: PDH is an endothermic process with limited alkene yields due to reaction thermodynamics.

2. High temperature requirements: While higher temperatures increase alkane conversion, they also lead to decreased alkene selectivity and increased side reactions such as dehydrocyclization, aromatization, thermal cracking, and coke formation.

3. Catalyst stability: Catalysts must withstand high temperatures and resist deactivation.

4. Energy input: The highly endothermic nature of the process requires significant energy input.

5. Balancing activity and selectivity: Catalysts must be designed to maximize propylene yield while minimizing unwanted side reactions.

Researchers are addressing these challenges through various approaches:

1. Developing improved catalysts: Current industrial catalysts are platinum or chromium-based. Researchers are working on enhancing their stability, selectivity, and resistance to coking.

   - Platinum-based catalysts: Typically prepared by impregnating support materials (e.g., alumina) with platinum and tin precursors. Tin is used as a promoter to improve performance and stability.
   - Chromium oxide catalysts: Prepared by impregnation of chromate salt (5-15 wt.% Cr) on high surface area support materials.

2. Optimizing catalyst preparation and support materials:
   - Using alumina as a support and titrating its acidic sites with alkali to minimize undesired side reactions.
   - Exploring alternative support materials and preparation methods.

3. Exploring auto-thermal and oxidative dehydrogenation strategies: These approaches can offset some of the energy input required, potentially allowing for lower operating temperatures. However, these technologies are still immature.

4. Investigating alternative reactor designs: Different reactor configurations may help manage heat input and product selectivity more effectively.

5. Studying reaction mechanisms: Better understanding of CH bond activation mechanisms on catalyst surfaces may lead to more rational catalyst design.

Opportunities in PDH research include:

1. Development of novel catalyst materials with improved performance, stability, and selectivity.
2. Optimization of reactor designs for better heat management and product selectivity.
3. Advancement of auto-thermal and oxidative dehydrogenation technologies to industrial viability.
4. Integration of PDH with other processes for better overall efficiency.

          丙烷脱氢(PDH)用于工业丙烯生产的主要挑战包括：

1. 热力学限制：PDH是一个吸热过程，由于反应热力学，烯烃产率有限。

2. 高温要求：虽然更高的温度会增加烷烃转化率，但也会导致烯烃选择性降低和副反应增加，如脱氢环化、芳构化、热裂解和积碳。

3. 催化剂稳定性：催化剂必须能够承受高温并抵抗失活。

4. 能量输入：该过程的高度吸热性质需要大量能量输入。

5. 平衡活性和选择性：催化剂的设计必须在最大化丙烯产率的同时最小化不需要的副反应。

研究人员正通过各种方法应对这些挑战：

1. 开发改进的催化剂：目前的工业催化剂是基于铂或铬的。研究人员正在努力提高它们的稳定性、选择性和抗积碳能力。

   - 铂基催化剂：通常通过将铂和锡前体浸渍到载体材料（如氧化铝）上制备。锡被用作助剂以提高性能和稳定性。
   - 铬氧化物催化剂：通过将铬酸盐（5-15 wt.% Cr）浸渍到高比表面积载体材料上制备。

2. 优化催化剂制备和载体材料：
   - 使用氧化铝作为载体，并用碱滴定其酸性位点以最小化不需要的副反应。
   - 探索替代载体材料和制备方法。

3. 探索自热和氧化脱氢策略：这些方法可以抵消部分所需的能量输入，可能允许在较低的操作温度下进行。然而，这些技术仍处于不成熟阶段。

4. 研究替代反应器设计：不同的反应器配置可能有助于更有效地管理热输入和产品选择性。

5. 研究反应机理：更好地理解催化剂表面上的CH键活化机理可能导致更合理的催化剂设计。

PDH研究的机会包括：

1. 开发具有改进性能、稳定性和选择性的新型催化剂材料。
2. 优化反应器设计，以实现更好的热管理和产品选择性。
3. 推进自热和氧化脱氢技术到工业可行性阶段。
4. 将PDH与其他过程集成，以提高整体效率。
