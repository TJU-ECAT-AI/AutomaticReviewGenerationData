<English>
The development of high-performance catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) in industrial propylene production faces several significant challenges and opportunities. These include addressing high-temperature stability, propylene selectivity, and thermodynamic limitations.

One of the primary challenges in PDH is the thermodynamic limitation of the reaction. As an endothermic process, PDH requires high temperatures to achieve reasonable propane conversion levels [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]. However, these high temperatures lead to decreased alkene selectivity and increased side reactions, such as dehydrocyclization, aromatization, thermal cracking, and coke formation [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016].

Catalyst stability is another major concern. Current industrial PDH catalysts are primarily platinum and chromium-based [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]. While these catalysts show high activity, they suffer from deactivation due to sintering and coke deposition [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5]. This deactivation is particularly problematic at the high temperatures required for the PDH reaction (723-923 K) [10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058].

Researchers are addressing these challenges through various approaches. One strategy involves improving existing catalysts by introducing promoters and optimizing support materials. For instance, platinum is often promoted with tin to enhance its performance and stability [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]. Alumina is a common support material, and researchers are exploring ways to minimize undesired side reactions by titrating its acidic sites with alkali [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016].

Novel catalyst formulations are also being investigated. Bimetallic catalysts, such as Pt-Zn supported on Si-Beta zeolite, have shown promise in achieving high propane conversion and good stability at reasonable reaction temperatures [10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]. Other alternative catalysts, including metal oxides like GaOx, MoOx, and VOx, are being studied as potential replacements for PtSn and CrOx systems [10.1021/cr5002436].

To overcome thermodynamic limitations, researchers are exploring oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) strategies. ODH has the potential to achieve full paraffin conversion by using an oxidant to combust the hydrogen formed during the reaction [10.1021/cr5002436]. Another approach involves the use of solid oxygen carriers, such as CeO2, SbO4, and In2O3, which can selectively oxidize hydrogen through the selective hydrogen combustion (SHC) reaction [10.1021/cr5002436].

Membrane technology is also being investigated as a means to remove hydrogen from the reaction mixture, shifting the equilibrium towards propylene production [10.1021/cr5002436]. This approach could potentially allow for lower operating temperatures while maintaining high propylene yields.

Advanced characterization techniques, such as operando/in situ methods (XAFS, XRD, XPS, and E-TEM), are being employed to gain a deeper understanding of active sites and reaction mechanisms under realistic reaction conditions [10.1039/D0CS00814A]. This knowledge is crucial for the rational design of more effective catalysts.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 

The ongoing research in PDH catalysts reveals a multifaceted approach to addressing the challenges of high-temperature stability, propylene selectivity, and thermodynamic limitations. The integration of novel materials, advanced characterization techniques, and innovative reactor designs presents a promising path forward for industrial propylene production.

Future research efforts may focus on developing hybrid systems that combine the benefits of different approaches. For instance, integrating membrane technology with novel catalyst formulations could potentially overcome thermodynamic limitations while maintaining high selectivity and stability. Additionally, the exploration of nanomaterials and nanostructured catalysts, such as the recently reported hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN) and boron nitride nanotubes (BNNTs) [10.1039/D0CS00814A], may lead to breakthroughs in catalyst performance and stability.

The shift towards more sustainable and energy-efficient processes may drive research into low-temperature PDH catalysts and reactor designs. This could involve the development of multifunctional catalysts that can operate effectively at lower temperatures while resisting deactivation and maintaining high selectivity.

As our understanding of reaction mechanisms at the molecular level improves, there is potential for the design of "smart" catalysts that can adapt to changing reaction conditions, potentially through the use of responsive supports or dynamic nanostructures. This could lead to more robust and efficient PDH processes that can better meet the growing demand for propylene in industrial applications.

<Chinese>
在工业丙烯生产中，开发高性能丙烷脱氢（PDH）催化剂面临着几个重大挑战和机遇。这些包括解决高温稳定性、丙烯选择性和热力学限制等问题。

PDH的主要挑战之一是反应的热力学限制。作为一个吸热过程，PDH需要高温才能达到合理的丙烷转化水平[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。然而，这些高温会导致烯烃选择性降低，并增加副反应，如脱氢环化、芳构化、热裂解和积炭形成[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。

催化剂稳定性是另一个主要关注点。目前工业PDH催化剂主要是基于铂和铬的[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。虽然这些催化剂显示出高活性，但它们因烧结和积炭沉积而导致失活[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5]。这种失活在PDH反应所需的高温（723-923 K）下尤其成问题[10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]。

研究人员正通过各种方法来解决这些挑战。一种策略涉及通过引入助剂和优化载体材料来改进现有催化剂。例如，铂常常与锡一起使用以提高其性能和稳定性[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。氧化铝是一种常见的载体材料，研究人员正在探索通过用碱滴定其酸性位点来最小化不希望的副反应的方法[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。

新型催化剂配方也正在被研究。双金属催化剂，如负载在Si-Beta分子筛上的Pt-Zn，在合理的反应温度下显示出高丙烷转化率和良好稳定性的前景[10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]。其他替代催化剂，包括金属氧化物如GaOx、MoOx和VOx，正被研究作为PtSn和CrOx系统的潜在替代品[10.1021/cr5002436]。

为了克服热力学限制，研究人员正在探索氧化脱氢（ODH）策略。ODH有潜力通过使用氧化剂燃烧反应过程中形成的氢气来实现全烷烃转化[10.1021/cr5002436]。另一种方法涉及使用固体氧载体，如CeO2、SbO4和In2O3，它们可以通过选择性氢燃烧（SHC）反应选择性地氧化氢[10.1021/cr5002436]。

膜技术也正在被研究，作为从反应混合物中去除氢气的手段，将平衡向丙烯产物方向移动[10.1021/cr5002436]。这种方法可能允许在较低的操作温度下保持高丙烯产率。

先进的表征技术，如原位/实时方法（XAFS、XRD、XPS和E-TEM），正被用来在真实反应条件下深入了解活性位点和反应机理[10.1039/D0CS00814A]。这种知识对于理性设计更有效的催化剂至关重要。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：

PDH催化剂的持续研究揭示了一种多方面的方法来解决高温稳定性、丙烯选择性和热力学限制的挑战。新材料、先进表征技术和创新反应器设计的整合为工业丙烯生产提供了一条有前景的前进道路。

未来的研究努力可能集中在开发结合不同方法优势的混合系统上。例如，将膜技术与新型催化剂配方相结合，可能在保持高选择性和稳定性的同时克服热力学限制。此外，对纳米材料和纳米结构催化剂的探索，如最近报道的六方氮化硼（h-BN）和氮化硼纳米管（BNNTs）[10.1039/D0CS00814A]，可能会在催化剂性能和稳定性方面取得突破。

向更可持续和节能过程的转变可能会推动低温PDH催化剂和反应器设计的研究。这可能涉及开发多功能催化剂，能够在较低温度下有效运作，同时抵抗失活并保持高选择性。

随着我们对分子水平反应机理理解的提高，有可能设计出可以适应变化反应条件的"智能"催化剂，可能通过使用响应性载体或动态纳米结构。这可能导致更强大和高效的PDH过程，能够更好地满足工业应用中不断增长的丙烯需求。
