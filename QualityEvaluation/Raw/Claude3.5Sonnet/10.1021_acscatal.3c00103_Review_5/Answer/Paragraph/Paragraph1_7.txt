<English>
The development of high-performance catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) in industrial propylene production faces several significant challenges and opportunities. These include addressing high-temperature stability, propylene selectivity, and resistance to side reactions, while considering thermodynamic limitations.

One of the primary challenges in PDH is the thermodynamic limitation of the reaction. As an endothermic process, PDH requires high temperatures to achieve favorable propane-to-propylene conversion [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]. However, these high temperatures also promote undesired side reactions such as dehydrocyclization, aromatization, thermal cracking, and coke formation, which decrease alkene selectivity [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]. This creates a complex balance between conversion and selectivity that researchers must navigate.

Currently, industrial PDH catalysts are primarily based on platinum or chromium oxide [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]. While these catalysts show high activity, they face significant challenges in terms of stability and deactivation. Catalyst deactivation, mainly due to coke formation and sintering, remains a major hurdle in PDH processes [10.1039/C5SC01227A]. The agglomeration of metal species during reaction or regeneration processes further complicates the development of stable catalysts for industrial applications [10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058].

To address these challenges, researchers are exploring various strategies:

1. Catalyst modification: Introducing promoters, such as tin for platinum-based catalysts, has been shown to improve performance and stability [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]. Other promoters being investigated include late transition metals, main-group metals (e.g., Ga), alkali-metal oxides, and rare-earth metal oxides [10.1039/C5SC01227A].

2. Support optimization: High-surface-area supports with abundant surface hydroxyls, such as Al2O3 or SiO2, are being used to disperse and stabilize active metal species [10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]. Researchers are also exploring the use of zeolites as supports, with Pt-Zn/Si-Beta zeolite showing promise as a robust, low-cost catalyst for industrial PDH processes [10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058].

3. Novel preparation methods: Techniques such as encapsulation are being investigated for their potential in size control, site adjustment, and spatial segmentation of catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5].

4. Alternative catalysts: Researchers are exploring metal oxides such as GaOx, MoOx, and VOx as potential alternatives to PtSn and CrOx systems [10.1021/cr5002436]. However, these alternatives still face challenges with rapid deactivation and incomplete activity recovery after regeneration.

5. Oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH): This approach is being investigated as a way to overcome thermodynamic limitations by using an oxidant to combust the hydrogen formed during the reaction, allowing for full paraffin conversion [10.1021/cr5002436].

6. Membrane technology: The use of membranes to remove hydrogen from the reaction mixture is being studied as a method to shift the equilibrium towards propylene production [10.1021/cr5002436].

Despite these advancements, significant challenges remain. The development of catalysts with lower noble metal loadings, improved stability, and enhanced energy efficiency is still a priority [10.1039/D0CS00814A]. Additionally, a deeper understanding of active sites and reaction mechanisms under reaction conditions is needed, necessitating the use of advanced characterization techniques such as operando/in situ XAFS, XRD, XPS, and environmental TEM [10.1039/D0CS00814A].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of propane dehydrogenation catalysis is at a critical juncture, with numerous opportunities for innovation and improvement. The challenges faced in developing high-performance PDH catalysts highlight the need for a multifaceted approach that combines novel materials science, advanced characterization techniques, and process engineering.

Looking forward, the integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning algorithms could revolutionize catalyst design, potentially uncovering new materials and structures that overcome current limitations. The exploration of nano-structured catalysts, such as single-atom catalysts or core-shell nanoparticles, may offer new avenues for enhancing stability and selectivity while minimizing noble metal usage.

Furthermore, the development of hybrid processes that combine PDH with other technologies, such as membrane separation or in-situ product removal, could lead to more efficient and economical propylene production. As the demand for propylene continues to grow, the successful development of next-generation PDH catalysts will play a crucial role in meeting global needs while improving sustainability in the chemical industry.

<Chinese>
在工业丙烯生产中，开发高性能的丙烷脱氢（PDH）催化剂面临着几个重大挑战和机遇。这些挑战包括解决高温稳定性、丙烯选择性和抗副反应能力，同时考虑热力学限制。

PDH的主要挑战之一是反应的热力学限制。作为一个吸热过程，PDH需要高温才能实现有利的丙烷到丙烯的转化[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。然而，这些高温也促进了不希望发生的副反应，如脱氢环化、芳构化、热裂解和积碳形成，这些反应降低了烯烃的选择性[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。这就造成了转化率和选择性之间的复杂平衡，研究人员必须解决这个问题。

目前，工业PDH催化剂主要基于铂或氧化铬[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。虽然这些催化剂表现出高活性，但在稳定性和失活方面面临重大挑战。催化剂失活，主要是由于积碳形成和烧结，仍然是PDH过程中的一个主要障碍[10.1039/C5SC01227A]。反应或再生过程中金属物种的聚集进一步复杂化了工业应用稳定催化剂的开发[10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]。

为了应对这些挑战，研究人员正在探索各种策略：

1. 催化剂改性：引入助剂，如铂基催化剂的锡，已被证明可以提高性能和稳定性[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。其他正在研究的助剂包括后过渡金属、主族金属（如Ga）、碱金属氧化物和稀土金属氧化物[10.1039/C5SC01227A]。

2. 载体优化：使用具有丰富表面羟基的高比表面积载体，如Al2O3或SiO2，来分散和稳定活性金属物种[10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]。研究人员还在探索使用沸石作为载体，其中Pt-Zn/Si-Beta沸石作为工业PDH过程的稳健、低成本催化剂显示出前景[10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]。

3. 新型制备方法：正在研究包封等技术，以控制催化剂的尺寸、调整活性位点和空间分隔[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5]。

4. 替代催化剂：研究人员正在探索GaOx、MoOx和VOx等金属氧化物作为PtSn和CrOx系统的潜在替代品[10.1021/cr5002436]。然而，这些替代品仍然面临快速失活和再生后活性恢复不完全的挑战。

5. 氧化脱氢（ODH）：这种方法正在被研究作为克服热力学限制的一种方法，通过使用氧化剂燃烧反应过程中形成的氢气，允许完全转化石蜡[10.1021/cr5002436]。

6. 膜技术：正在研究使用膜从反应混合物中去除氢气，作为将平衡转向丙烯生产的一种方法[10.1021/cr5002436]。

尽管取得了这些进展，但仍然存在重大挑战。开发具有更低贵金属负载、改善稳定性和提高能源效率的催化剂仍然是一个优先事项[10.1039/D0CS00814A]。此外，需要对反应条件下的活性位点和反应机理有更深入的理解，这就需要使用先进的表征技术，如原位/同步XAFS、XRD、XPS和环境TEM[10.1039/D0CS00814A]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：丙烷脱氢催化领域正处于关键的转折点，有许多创新和改进的机会。在开发高性能PDH催化剂方面面临的挑战突出了需要采取多方面的方法，结合新型材料科学、先进表征技术和过程工程。

展望未来，人工智能和机器学习算法的整合可能会彻底改变催化剂设计，潜在地发现克服当前限制的新材料和结构。对纳米结构催化剂的探索，如单原子催化剂或核壳纳米粒子，可能会为提高稳定性和选择性同时最小化贵金属使用提供新的途径。

此外，将PDH与其他技术（如膜分离或原位产品去除）相结合的混合工艺的开发可能会导致更高效和经济的丙烯生产。随着丙烯需求的持续增长，下一代PDH催化剂的成功开发将在满足全球需求的同时改善化工行业的可持续性方面发挥关键作用。
