<English>
The development of high-performance catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) in industrial propylene production faces several significant challenges, primarily due to thermodynamic limitations, high-temperature requirements, and catalyst stability issues. These challenges are being addressed through various research approaches, offering opportunities for improving the efficiency and viability of PDH processes.

One of the primary challenges in PDH is the thermodynamic limitation of the reaction. PDH is an endothermic process, with alkene yields limited by reaction thermodynamics [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]. This limitation necessitates high temperatures to achieve reasonable propane conversion levels, typically in the range of 723-923 K [10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]. However, these high temperatures lead to decreased alkene selectivity and increased side reactions, such as dehydrocyclization, aromatization, thermal cracking, and coke formation [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016].

Catalyst stability and deactivation are major concerns in PDH processes. The main causes of catalyst deactivation are sintering (particle agglomeration) and coke deposition on active sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5]. Current industrial PDH catalysts, primarily based on platinum or chromium oxide, face challenges in maintaining long-term stability and activity [10.1039/C5SC01227A]. For instance, supported Pt catalysts show high activity but suffer from deactivation due to coke formation [10.1039/C5SC01227A].

Researchers are addressing these challenges through various approaches:

1. Catalyst Development: Efforts are being made to improve existing Pt-based and CrOx-based catalysts by introducing promoters, such as tin for Pt catalysts, to enhance stability and performance [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]. Novel bimetallic catalysts, like Pt-Zn, are being explored to improve catalytic performance and stability [10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058].

2. Support Material Optimization: High-surface-area supports with abundant surface hydroxyls, such as Al2O3, SiO2, and zeolites, are being used to disperse and stabilize active metal species [10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]. Titration of acidic sites on alumina supports with alkali is employed to minimize undesired acid catalytic cracking and coking side reactions [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016].

3. Alternative Catalysts: Research is ongoing to develop alternative catalysts to conventional metal and metal oxide-based systems. Metal oxides such as GaOx, MoOx, and VOx have received significant attention, although they still face issues with rapid deactivation and incomplete activity recovery after regeneration [10.1021/cr5002436].

4. Novel Materials: Innovative materials like hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN) and boron nitride nanotubes (BNNTs) have shown promising results, exhibiting high selectivity to olefins [10.1039/D0CS00814A].

5. Process Modifications: Researchers are exploring oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) as an alternative to direct dehydrogenation due to its more favorable thermodynamics [10.1039/D0CS00814A]. The use of solid oxygen carriers and membrane technology to remove hydrogen from the reaction mixture is also being investigated to shift the equilibrium towards propylene production [10.1021/cr5002436].

6. Advanced Characterization: Operando/in situ characterization techniques, such as XAFS, XRD, XPS, and environmental TEM (E-TEM), are being employed to better understand active sites and reaction mechanisms under realistic conditions [10.1039/D0CS00814A].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of propane dehydrogenation catalysis is at a critical juncture, with significant opportunities for innovation and improvement. The integration of advanced materials science, nanotechnology, and in-situ characterization techniques offers promising pathways for developing next-generation PDH catalysts. Future research may focus on:

1. Rational design of multi-functional catalysts that can simultaneously address issues of stability, selectivity, and activity.
2. Exploration of novel support materials and nanostructured catalysts that can better withstand high-temperature conditions while maintaining high surface areas and dispersed active sites.
3. Development of hybrid processes that combine the benefits of different approaches, such as membrane reactors with optimized catalysts for continuous hydrogen removal.
4. Utilization of artificial intelligence and machine learning techniques for accelerated catalyst discovery and optimization.
5. Integration of PDH processes with renewable energy sources to address energy efficiency concerns and reduce the carbon footprint of propylene production.

These advancements could potentially lead to more sustainable and economically viable PDH processes, addressing the growing demand for propylene while minimizing environmental impact.

<Chinese>
在工业丙烯生产中，开发高性能丙烷脱氢（PDH）催化剂面临着几个重大挑战，主要是由于热力学限制、高温要求和催化剂稳定性问题。这些挑战正通过各种研究方法得到解决，为提高PDH过程的效率和可行性提供了机会。

PDH的主要挑战之一是反应的热力学限制。PDH是一个吸热过程，烯烃产率受反应热力学限制[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。这一限制需要高温才能达到合理的丙烷转化水平，通常在723-923 K范围内[10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]。然而，这些高温会导致烯烃选择性降低，并增加副反应，如脱氢环化、芳构化、热裂解和积碳形成[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。

催化剂稳定性和失活是PDH过程中的主要问题。催化剂失活的主要原因是烧结（粒子聚集）和活性位点上的积碳[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_5]。目前的工业PDH催化剂，主要基于铂或氧化铬，在保持长期稳定性和活性方面面临挑战[10.1039/C5SC01227A]。例如，负载型Pt催化剂显示出高活性，但由于积碳形成而失活[10.1039/C5SC01227A]。

研究人员正通过各种方法解决这些挑战：

1. 催化剂开发：正在努力改进现有的Pt基和CrOx基催化剂，通过引入助剂（如Pt催化剂的锡）来提高稳定性和性能[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。正在探索新型双金属催化剂，如Pt-Zn，以提高催化性能和稳定性[10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]。

2. 载体材料优化：使用具有丰富表面羟基的高比表面积载体，如Al2O3、SiO2和沸石，来分散和稳定活性金属物种[10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]。对氧化铝载体上的酸性位点进行碱滴定，以最小化不需要的酸催化裂解和积碳副反应[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。

3. 替代催化剂：正在进行研究，以开发传统金属和金属氧化物基系统的替代催化剂。金属氧化物如GaOx、MoOx和VOx已受到重要关注，尽管它们仍面临快速失活和再生后活性不完全恢复的问题[10.1021/cr5002436]。

4. 新型材料：创新材料如六方氮化硼（h-BN）和氮化硼纳米管（BNNTs）已显示出有前景的结果，表现出对烯烃的高选择性[10.1039/D0CS00814A]。

5. 工艺改进：研究人员正在探索氧化脱氢（ODH）作为直接脱氢的替代方案，因为它具有更有利的热力学特性[10.1039/D0CS00814A]。还在研究使用固体氧载体和膜技术从反应混合物中除去氢气，以将平衡向丙烯生产方向移动[10.1021/cr5002436]。

6. 先进表征：正在采用原位/实时表征技术，如XAFS、XRD、XPS和环境TEM（E-TEM），以更好地了解在实际条件下的活性位点和反应机理[10.1039/D0CS00814A]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：丙烷脱氢催化领域正处于关键时刻，有重大的创新和改进机会。先进材料科学、纳米技术和原位表征技术的整合为开发下一代PDH催化剂提供了有前景的途径。未来的研究可能集中在：

1. 理性设计多功能催化剂，同时解决稳定性、选择性和活性问题。
2. 探索新型载体材料和纳米结构催化剂，能更好地承受高温条件，同时保持高比表面积和分散的活性位点。
3. 开发混合工艺，结合不同方法的优势，如带有优化催化剂的膜反应器，用于连续氢气去除。
4. 利用人工智能和机器学习技术加速催化剂发现和优化。
5. 将PDH过程与可再生能源源整合，以解决能源效率问题并减少丙烯生产的碳足迹。

这些进展可能导致更可持续和经济可行的PDH过程，满足不断增长的丙烯需求，同时最大限度地减少环境影响。
