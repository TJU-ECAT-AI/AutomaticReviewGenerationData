<EnglishVersionAnswer>

The primary challenges and opportunities in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) for industrial propylene production include:

1. Improving catalyst stability at high temperatures
2. Increasing propylene selectivity
3. Reducing side reactions
4. Overcoming thermodynamic limitations on conversion

Researchers are addressing these challenges by:

1. Developing catalysts with lower noble metal loadings to improve cost-effectiveness
2. Exploring alternative metal oxide catalysts like GaOx, MoOx, and VOx
3. Investigating ways to increase catalyst stability and reduce deactivation due to coke formation
4. Adding promoters like potassium to reduce coke formation by poisoning acid sites
5. Combining metals like Pt and Ga to achieve high conversions and selectivity
6. Improving energy efficiency of the dehydrogenation process
7. Researching methods to shift equilibrium and increase conversion, such as membrane reactors or oxidative dehydrogenation

However, significant challenges remain, particularly in developing alternatives to PtSn and CrOx systems that can maintain high activity and stability over long periods of industrial use.
