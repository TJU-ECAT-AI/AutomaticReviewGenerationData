The primary causes of metal oxide catalyst deactivation in oxidative dehydrogenation processes, focusing on Cr-based and VOx catalysts, include:

1. Coke formation: This is the main cause of activity loss for both Cr-based and VOx catalysts. The produced alkenes contain allylic hydrogen, which has a lower bond dissociation energy than the starting alkanes, leading to faster hydrogen abstraction and coke precursor formation [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2].

2. Reduction of active metal species: During the reaction, Cr3+ can be reduced to inactive Cr2+, and V5+ can be reduced to lower oxidation states, decreasing catalytic activity [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

3. Aggregation and sintering of active species: The highly dispersed active CrOx or VOx species may agglomerate or transform into less active forms, reducing catalytic activity [10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070].

4. Incorporation of active species into the support: For Cr-based catalysts, the heat released during coke combustion can cause chromia to incorporate into the alumina framework, reducing the amount of catalytically active Cr species [10.1021/cr5002436].

5. Structural rearrangement: High temperatures during reaction or regeneration can cause changes in the oxide structure, potentially leading to the formation of less active species [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.028].

Regarding regeneration methods and their effectiveness:

1. Reoxidation with air or O2:
   - Most effective for removing coke and restoring catalyst activity
   - Can completely restore the activity of CrOx/ZrO2 catalysts after multiple cycles
   - May lead to gradual irreversible deactivation, especially for Cr-based catalysts
   - Typically performed at temperatures around 550-650°C
   [10.1039/C5CY00998G] [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]

2. CO2 addition:
   - Acts as a milder oxidant, helping maintain higher oxidation states of Cr and V species
   - Reduces coke formation through the Boudouard reaction
   - Partially reoxidizes reduced metal species (e.g., V2O3 to V2O4)
   - Can be effective at lower temperatures and during the reaction itself
   - May not fully restore catalyst activity in subsequent cycles
   [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2] [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]

3. O2 + CO2 mixtures:
   - Combines the benefits of both O2 and CO2
   - Improves oxidizing power while potentially mitigating overoxidation
   - Can be effective in reducing coke formation during the reaction
   [10.1002/cctc.201200397]

4. Steam addition:
   - Similar to CO2, helps reduce coke deposition and assists in product desorption
   [10.1021/cr5002436]

Temperature requirements for regeneration are typically in the range of 550-650°C, with lower temperatures preferred to minimize thermal stress and sintering, but high enough to effectively remove coke [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3].

The impact on catalytic performance varies depending on the regeneration method:
- Air regeneration can restore most of the initial activity but may lead to a gradual loss of active sites over multiple cycles.
- CO2 addition can improve catalyst stability and olefin yield, but may not fully prevent deactivation in long-term operation.
- The use of promoters (e.g., Ni, K) can enhance catalyst stability and reduce coke formation [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

For VOx-catalyzed reactions, CO2 addition shows promise in mitigating overoxidation by maintaining a balance between oxidation states and preventing the formation of bulk V2O5, which favors total oxidation [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The complex interplay between catalyst deactivation mechanisms and regeneration strategies highlights the need for tailored approaches in oxidative dehydrogenation processes. Future research directions may focus on:

1. Development of advanced in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation states during reaction and regeneration cycles.

2. Design of novel catalyst formulations that incorporate both active species (Cr, V) and promoters (e.g., Ni, W) to enhance stability and selectivity while minimizing deactivation.

3. Exploration of innovative regeneration protocols that combine multiple oxidants (e.g., CO2, O2, steam) in carefully controlled ratios and sequences to optimize the balance between activity restoration and prevention of overoxidation.

4. Investigation of the potential of mild oxidants like N2O or H2O2 as alternatives to CO2 for selective oxidation and regeneration.

5. Integration of machine learning algorithms to predict optimal regeneration conditions based on real-time monitoring of catalyst performance and deactivation indicators.

6. Development of continuous regeneration strategies that allow for uninterrupted operation while maintaining high catalyst activity and selectivity.

These research directions could lead to significant improvements in the longevity and performance of metal oxide catalysts in oxidative dehydrogenation processes, potentially enabling more efficient and sustainable production of valuable chemical intermediates.

<Chinese>
在氧化脱氢过程中，金属氧化物催化剂失活的主要原因，主要针对基于Cr和VOx的催化剂，包括：

1. 积碳形成：这是Cr基和VOx催化剂活性损失的主要原因。生成的烯烃含有烯丙基氢，其键解离能比起始烷烃低，导致氢更快地被抽取，形成积碳前体 [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2]。

2. 活性金属物种的还原：在反应过程中，Cr3+可被还原为无活性的Cr2+，V5+可被还原为低氧化态，降低催化活性 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

3. 活性物种的聚集和烧结：高度分散的活性CrOx或VOx物种可能聚集或转化为活性较低的形式，降低催化活性 [10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070]。

4. 活性物种纳入载体：对于Cr基催化剂，积碳燃烧释放的热量可能导致氧化铬纳入氧化铝骨架，减少催化活性Cr物种的数量 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

5. 结构重排：反应或再生过程中的高温可能导致氧化物结构发生变化，潜在形成活性较低的物种 [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.028]。

关于再生方法及其有效性：

1. 空气或O2再氧化：
   - 最有效地去除积碳并恢复催化剂活性
   - 可以在多个循环后完全恢复CrOx/ZrO2催化剂的活性
   - 可能导致逐渐不可逆的失活，特别是对于Cr基催化剂
   - 通常在550-650°C左右的温度下进行
   [10.1039/C5CY00998G] [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]

2. CO2添加：
   - 作为较温和的氧化剂，有助于维持Cr和V物种的较高氧化态
   - 通过布道反应减少积碳形成
   - 部分再氧化还原的金属物种（如V2O3到V2O4）
   - 可以在较低温度下和反应过程中有效
   - 可能无法在后续循环中完全恢复催化剂活性
   [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2] [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]

3. O2 + CO2混合：
   - 结合O2和CO2的优点
   - 提高氧化能力，同时可能减轻过度氧化
   - 可以有效减少反应过程中的积碳形成
   [10.1002/cctc.201200397]

4. 蒸汽添加：
   - 类似于CO2，有助于减少积碳沉积并辅助产物脱附
   [10.1021/cr5002436]

再生的温度要求通常在550-650°C范围内，较低温度有助于最小化热应力和烧结，但需要足够高以有效去除积碳 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]。

对催化性能的影响因再生方法而异：
- 空气再生可以恢复大部分初始活性，但可能导致多个循环后活性位点逐渐损失。
- CO2添加可以改善催化剂稳定性和烯烃产率，但可能无法完全防止长期运行中的失活。
- 使用助剂（如Ni、K）可以增强催化剂稳定性并减少积碳形成 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

对于VOx催化的反应，CO2添加显示出在减轻过度氧化方面的前景，通过维持氧化态之间的平衡并防止形成有利于完全氧化的体相V2O5 [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：催化剂失活机制和再生策略之间复杂的相互作用突显了在氧化脱氢过程中需要量身定制方法的必要性。未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 开发先进的原位表征技术，以更好地理解催化剂在反应和再生循环过程中结构和氧化态的动态变化。

2. 设计新型催化剂配方，结合活性物种（Cr、V）和助剂（如Ni、W），以增强稳定性和选择性，同时最小化失活。

3. 探索创新的再生方案，在仔细控制的比例和顺序下结合多种氧化剂（如CO2、O2、蒸汽），以优化活性恢复和防止过度氧化之间的平衡。

4. 研究温和氧化剂如N2O或H2O2作为CO2的替代品，用于选择性氧化和再生的潜力。

5. 整合机器学习算法，基于对催化剂性能和失活指标的实时监测，预测最佳再生条件。

6. 开发连续再生策略，允许在维持高催化剂活性和选择性的同时进行不间断操作。

这些研究方向可能导致氧化脱氢过程中金属氧化物催化剂寿命和性能的显著改善，潜在地实现更高效和可持续的有价值化学中间体生产。
