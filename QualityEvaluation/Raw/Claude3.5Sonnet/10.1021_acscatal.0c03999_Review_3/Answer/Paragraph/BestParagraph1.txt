The primary causes of metal oxide catalyst deactivation in oxidative dehydrogenation processes, focusing on Cr-based and VOx catalysts, include:

1. Coke formation: This is the main cause of activity loss for both Cr-based and VOx catalysts. Coke deposition is accelerated at high temperatures and in the presence of acid sites on the catalyst surface [10.1016/S0926-860X(03)00368-5].

2. Reduction of active metal species: During the reaction, Cr3+ species are reduced to inactive Cr2+ state, while VOx species are reduced from higher to lower oxidation states, decreasing their catalytic activity [10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.028].

3. Aggregation and sintering of active species: The decreased dispersion of Cr and V species due to clustering or sintering leads to a loss of active sites [10.1039/C9CY01990A] [10.1021/cr5002436].

4. Structural changes: High temperatures during reaction or regeneration can cause changes in the oxide structure, potentially leading to the formation of less active species [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.028].

5. Incorporation of active species into the support: For Cr-based catalysts, the heat released during coke combustion can cause chromia to incorporate into the alumina framework, reducing the amount of catalytically active Cr species [10.1021/cr5002436].

Regarding regeneration methods and their effectiveness:

1. Reoxidation with air or O2:
   - Most effective for removing coke deposits and restoring catalyst activity
   - Temperature requirements: Generally around 550-650°C
   - Can lead to overoxidation, especially for VOx catalysts
   - May cause gradual irreversible deactivation over multiple cycles for Cr-based catalysts
   [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3] [10.1039/C5CY00998G]

2. CO2 addition:
   - Acts as a milder oxidant, helping to maintain higher oxidation states of Cr and V species
   - Suppresses coke formation and assists in its removal through the Boudouard reaction
   - Temperature requirements: Similar to reaction temperatures (e.g., 550°C)
   - Less effective than O2 in fully restoring activity but helps maintain selectivity
   - Particularly beneficial for VOx catalysts, oxidizing V2O3 to V2O4 instead of V2O5
   [10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]

3. O2 + CO2 mixtures:
   - Combines the benefits of both oxidants
   - Effective in reducing coke formation during the reaction
   - Maintains Cr in a higher oxidation state (Cr3+)
   - May offer a balance between activity restoration and selectivity maintenance
   [10.1039/C5CY00998G]

4. Steam addition:
   - Similar to CO2, helps reduce coke deposition and assists in product desorption
   - Not as widely studied in the provided literature
   [10.1021/cr5002436]

The impact on catalytic performance varies depending on the regeneration method:

- O2 regeneration is most effective in restoring activity but may lead to overoxidation and gradual loss of active sites over multiple cycles.
- CO2 addition helps maintain catalyst stability and selectivity, particularly for VOx catalysts, by mitigating overoxidation and suppressing coke formation.
- The use of promoters (e.g., Ni, K) in combination with CO2 can enhance catalyst stability and regeneration effectiveness [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
