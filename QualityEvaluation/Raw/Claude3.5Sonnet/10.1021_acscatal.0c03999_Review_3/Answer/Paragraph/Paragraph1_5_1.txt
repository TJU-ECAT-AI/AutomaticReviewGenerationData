The primary causes of metal oxide catalyst deactivation in oxidative dehydrogenation processes, focusing on Cr-based and VOx catalysts, include:

1. Coke formation: This is the most common cause of deactivation for both Cr-based and VOx catalysts. Coke deposition occurs due to the transformation of initially produced olefins, especially at high temperatures and in the presence of acid sites [10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070].

2. Reduction of active metal species: During the reaction, active metal oxides (e.g., Cr3+ to Cr2+, V5+ to lower oxidation states) are reduced, leading to a decline in catalytic activity [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

3. Aggregation or sintering of active species: This leads to a decreased dispersion of active metal oxides, reducing the number of accessible active sites [10.039/C9CY01990A].

4. Structural changes: High temperatures during reaction or regeneration can cause changes in the oxide structure, potentially leading to the formation of less active species [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.028].

5. Incorporation of active species into the support: For Cr-based catalysts, the heat released during coke combustion can cause chromia to incorporate into the alumina framework, reducing the amount of catalytically active Cr species [10.1021/cr5002436].

Regeneration methods and their effectiveness:

1. Reoxidation with air or O2:
   - Most effective for removing coke deposits and restoring catalyst activity
   - Temperature requirements: Typically 550-650°C for 2-6 hours [10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070]
   - Can lead to gradual irreversible deactivation, especially for Cr-based catalysts [10.1021/cr5002436]

2. CO2 addition:
   - Acts as a milder oxidant, helping maintain higher oxidation states of Cr and V species [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2]
   - Effective in suppressing coke formation and partially reoxidizing reduced metal species [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]
   - Temperature requirements: Similar to reaction temperatures, around 500-600°C [10.039/C9CY01990A]
   - Less effective than O2 in fully restoring catalyst activity but helps maintain selectivity [10.1002/cctc.201200397]

3. Combined O2 and CO2:
   - Balances coke removal with selectivity maintenance [10.039/C5CY00998G]
   - Allows for lower regeneration temperatures compared to O2 alone

4. Steam addition:
   - Similar to CO2, helps reduce coke deposition and assists in product desorption [10.1021/cr5002436]

Impact on catalytic performance:

- Air/O2 regeneration can restore most of the initial activity but may lead to a gradual loss of active sites over multiple cycles [10.1021/cr5002436].
- CO2 addition improves catalyst stability and olefin yield, while mitigating overoxidation in VOx-catalyzed reactions [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2].
- The use of promoters (e.g., Ni, K) can enhance catalyst stability and reduce coke formation [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 

The research on metal oxide catalyst deactivation and regeneration in oxidative dehydrogenation processes reveals a complex interplay between various factors affecting catalyst performance. While significant progress has been made in understanding deactivation mechanisms and developing effective regeneration strategies, there are still opportunities for further improvement and innovation.

Future research directions may include:

1. Development of novel catalyst formulations that are inherently more resistant to coke formation and sintering, possibly through the incorporation of advanced nanostructures or composite materials.

2. Exploration of in-situ regeneration techniques that can maintain catalyst activity during continuous operation, reducing the need for offline regeneration cycles.

3. Investigation of alternative mild oxidants or combinations of oxidants that can effectively regenerate catalysts while minimizing overoxidation and structural changes.

4. Utilization of advanced characterization techniques, such as operando spectroscopy, to gain real-time insights into catalyst deactivation and regeneration processes, enabling more precise control over reaction conditions.

5. Integration of machine learning and artificial intelligence approaches to optimize regeneration protocols based on real-time catalyst performance data.

6. Development of sustainable regeneration methods that minimize energy consumption and utilize renewable resources, aligning with broader goals of green chemistry and circular economy principles.

By addressing these challenges and opportunities, future research can lead to more efficient, stable, and selective catalysts for oxidative dehydrogenation processes, potentially revolutionizing the production of valuable chemical intermediates.

在氧化脱氢过程中，以Cr基和VOx催化剂为重点的金属氧化物催化剂失活的主要原因包括：

1. 积碳形成：这是Cr基和VOx催化剂最常见的失活原因。积碳沉积是由于初始生成的烯烃转化而发生的，尤其是在高温和存在酸性位点的情况下 [10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070]。

2. 活性金属物种的还原：在反应过程中，活性金属氧化物（如Cr3+还原为Cr2+，V5+还原为低价态）被还原，导致催化活性下降 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

3. 活性物种的聚集或烧结：这导致活性金属氧化物的分散度降低，减少了可访问的活性位点数量 [10.039/C9CY01990A]。

4. 结构变化：反应或再生过程中的高温可能导致氧化物结构发生变化，可能形成活性较低的物种 [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.028]。

5. 活性物种并入支撑体：对于Cr基催化剂，积碳燃烧释放的热量可能导致铬氧化物并入氧化铝骨架，减少了催化活性Cr物种的数量 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

再生方法及其有效性：

1. 用空气或O2再氧化：
   - 最有效地去除积碳沉积物并恢复催化剂活性
   - 温度要求：通常为550-650°C，持续2-6小时 [10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070]
   - 可能导致逐渐不可逆的失活，尤其是对Cr基催化剂 [10.1021/cr5002436]

2. CO2添加：
   - 作为较温和的氧化剂，有助于维持Cr和V物种的较高氧化态 [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2]
   - 在抑制积碳形成和部分再氧化还原金属物种方面有效 [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]
   - 温度要求：与反应温度相似，约500-600°C [10.039/C9CY01990A]
   - 在完全恢复催化剂活性方面不如O2有效，但有助于维持选择性 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]

3. O2和CO2组合：
   - 平衡积碳去除和选择性维持 [10.039/C5CY00998G]
   - 与单独使用O2相比，允许较低的再生温度

4. 蒸汽添加：
   - 类似于CO2，有助于减少积碳沉积并辅助产物脱附 [10.1021/cr5002436]

对催化性能的影响：

- 空气/O2再生可以恢复大部分初始活性，但可能导致多个循环后活性位点逐渐损失 [10.1021/cr5002436]。
- CO2添加改善了催化剂稳定性和烯烃产率，同时缓解了VOx催化反应中的过度氧化 [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2]。
- 使用助剂（如Ni、K）可以增强催化剂稳定性并减少积碳形成 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

综合理解与未来展望：

氧化脱氢过程中金属氧化物催化剂失活和再生的研究揭示了影响催化剂性能的各种因素之间的复杂相互作用。虽然在理解失活机理和开发有效再生策略方面已取得重大进展，但仍有进一步改进和创新的机会。

未来研究方向可能包括：

1. 开发本质上更能抵抗积碳形成和烧结的新型催化剂配方，可能通过纳入先进的纳米结构或复合材料。

2. 探索可以在连续操作过程中维持催化剂活性的原位再生技术，减少离线再生循环的需求。

3. 研究可以有效再生催化剂的替代温和氧化剂或氧化剂组合，同时最大限度地减少过度氧化和结构变化。

4. 利用先进的表征技术，如原位光谱法，获得催化剂失活和再生过程的实时洞察，从而更精确地控制反应条件。

5. 整合机器学习和人工智能方法，根据实时催化剂性能数据优化再生协议。

6. 开发可持续的再生方法，最大限度地减少能源消耗并利用可再生资源，与绿色化学和循环经济原则的更广泛目标保持一致。

通过解决这些挑战和机遇，未来的研究可以导致更高效、稳定和选择性的氧化脱氢过程催化剂，可能彻底改变有价值化学中间体的生产。
