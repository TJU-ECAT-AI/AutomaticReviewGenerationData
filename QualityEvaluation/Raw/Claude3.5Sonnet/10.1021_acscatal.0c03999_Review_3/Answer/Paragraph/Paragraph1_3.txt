The primary causes of metal oxide catalyst deactivation in oxidative dehydrogenation processes, focusing on Cr-based and VOx catalysts, include:

1. Coke formation: This is a major cause of deactivation for both Cr-based and VOx catalysts. Coke deposits on the catalyst surface, blocking active sites and reducing catalytic activity [10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017].

2. Reduction of active metal species: During the reaction, Cr3+ can be reduced to inactive Cr2+, while VOx species can be reduced from higher to lower oxidation states, decreasing catalytic activity [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

3. Sintering and aggregation: High temperatures during reaction or regeneration can cause metal oxide species to sinter, forming larger crystallites or mixed oxides, which are less active [10.1021/cr5002436].

4. Structural changes: The incorporation of active species into the support material, particularly for Cr-based catalysts on alumina supports, can lead to a decrease in catalytically active species [10.1021/cr5002436].

5. Strong adsorption of reactants: This can block active sites and contribute to initial deactivation [10.1021/cr5002436].

Regarding regeneration methods:

1. Reoxidation with air or O2:
   - Most effective for removing coke deposits and restoring activity
   - Typically performed at high temperatures (550-650°C)
   - Can lead to overoxidation and gradual loss of active sites over multiple cycles
   - For CrOx/ZrO2, regeneration in O2 at 600°C for 5 minutes can completely restore activity for at least 4 cycles [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]

2. CO2 addition:
   - Acts as a milder oxidant, helping to maintain higher oxidation states of Cr and V species
   - Reduces coke formation through the Boudouard reaction
   - Partially reoxidizes reduced species (e.g., V2O3 to V2O4) [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]
   - Can be effective during the reaction itself, suppressing deactivation
   - Less effective than O2 in fully restoring activity, especially over multiple cycles

3. O2 + CO2 mixtures:
   - Combines the benefits of both oxidants
   - Effective in reducing coke formation during the reaction
   - Maintains Cr in a higher oxidation state (Cr3+) [10.1039/C5CY00998G]

Temperature requirements vary but generally range from 500-650°C for effective regeneration. Lower temperatures are preferred to minimize thermal stress and sintering, but must be high enough to remove coke effectively.

Impact on catalytic performance:
- O2 regeneration is most effective in restoring activity but can lead to overoxidation and decreased selectivity
- CO2 addition helps maintain selectivity and suppress deactivation but may not fully restore initial activity
- The addition of promoters (e.g., Ni, K) can enhance the effectiveness of CO2 regeneration and improve overall catalyst stability [10.1002/cctc.201200397]

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The current research highlights the complex interplay between catalyst deactivation mechanisms and regeneration strategies for Cr-based and VOx catalysts in oxidative dehydrogenation processes. Moving forward, there is significant potential for developing more efficient and sustainable catalytic systems. Future research directions may include:

1. Tailored catalyst designs: Developing catalysts with optimized metal oxide dispersion and support interactions to minimize sintering and incorporation into the support material.

2. In-situ regeneration techniques: Exploring methods for continuous or semi-continuous regeneration during the reaction, potentially using CO2 or CO2/O2 mixtures to balance activity restoration and selectivity maintenance.

3. Advanced characterization techniques: Utilizing operando spectroscopy and microscopy methods to better understand the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation state during reaction and regeneration cycles.

4. Machine learning approaches: Employing data-driven models to predict optimal regeneration conditions and catalyst compositions for specific reaction systems.

5. CO2 utilization: Further investigating the potential of CO2 as both a mild oxidant and a C1 feedstock in oxidative dehydrogenation processes, contributing to carbon capture and utilization efforts.

6. Novel promoters and additives: Exploring new materials that can enhance the effectiveness of CO2 regeneration, improve catalyst stability, and mitigate overoxidation issues.

By addressing these areas, researchers can work towards developing more efficient, stable, and environmentally friendly catalytic systems for oxidative dehydrogenation processes, potentially revolutionizing the production of valuable olefins and contributing to the broader goals of sustainable chemistry.

<Chinese>
在氧化脱氢过程中，金属氧化物催化剂失活的主要原因，重点关注基于Cr和VOx的催化剂，包括：

1. 积碳形成：这是Cr基和VOx催化剂失活的主要原因。积碳沉积在催化剂表面，阻塞活性位点并降低催化活性 [10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017]。

2. 活性金属物种还原：在反应过程中，Cr3+可被还原为无活性的Cr2+，而VOx物种可从高氧化态还原为低氧化态，降低催化活性 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

3. 烧结和聚集：反应或再生过程中的高温可导致金属氧化物物种烧结，形成更大的晶体或混合氧化物，活性降低 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

4. 结构变化：活性物种掺入载体材料，特别是Cr基催化剂在氧化铝载体上，可导致催化活性物种减少 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

5. 反应物强吸附：这可以阻塞活性位点并导致初始失活 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

关于再生方法：

1. 空气或O2再氧化：
   - 最有效地去除积碳沉积物并恢复活性
   - 通常在高温下进行（550-650°C）
   - 可能导致过度氧化和多个循环后活性位点的逐渐损失
   - 对于CrOx/ZrO2，在600°C下用O2再生5分钟可以完全恢复活性，至少持续4个循环 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]

2. CO2添加：
   - 作为温和的氧化剂，有助于维持Cr和V物种的较高氧化态
   - 通过Boudouard反应减少积碳形成
   - 部分再氧化还原态物种（例如，V2O3到V2O4）[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]
   - 可在反应过程中有效抑制失活
   - 在完全恢复活性方面不如O2有效，特别是在多个循环中

3. O2 + CO2混合：
   - 结合了两种氧化剂的优点
   - 有效减少反应过程中的积碳形成
   - 保持Cr处于较高的氧化态（Cr3+）[10.1039/C5CY00998G]

温度要求各不相同，但通常在500-650°C范围内进行有效再生。较低的温度有助于最小化热应力和烧结，但必须足够高以有效去除积碳。

对催化性能的影响：
- O2再生最有效地恢复活性，但可能导致过度氧化和选择性降低
- CO2添加有助于维持选择性并抑制失活，但可能无法完全恢复初始活性
- 添加促进剂（如Ni、K）可以增强CO2再生的有效性并提高催化剂的整体稳定性 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]

※※※
综合理解和前景展望：当前的研究突出了氧化脱氢过程中Cr基和VOx催化剂失活机制与再生策略之间的复杂相互作用。展望未来，开发更高效和可持续的催化系统具有重要潜力。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 定制催化剂设计：开发具有优化金属氧化物分散和载体相互作用的催化剂，以最小化烧结和掺入载体材料的问题。

2. 原位再生技术：探索反应过程中连续或半连续再生的方法，可能使用CO2或CO2/O2混合物来平衡活性恢复和选择性维持。

3. 先进表征技术：利用原位光谱和显微技术更好地理解反应和再生循环过程中催化剂结构和氧化态的动态变化。

4. 机器学习方法：采用数据驱动模型预测特定反应系统的最佳再生条件和催化剂组成。

5. CO2利用：进一步研究CO2作为温和氧化剂和C1原料在氧化脱氢过程中的潜力，为碳捕获和利用做出贡献。

6. 新型促进剂和添加剂：探索可以提高CO2再生效果、改善催化剂稳定性和减轻过度氧化问题的新材料。

通过解决这些领域的问题，研究人员可以致力于开发更高效、稳定和环境友好的氧化脱氢过程催化系统，有可能革新宝贵烯烃的生产，并为可持续化学的更广泛目标做出贡献。
