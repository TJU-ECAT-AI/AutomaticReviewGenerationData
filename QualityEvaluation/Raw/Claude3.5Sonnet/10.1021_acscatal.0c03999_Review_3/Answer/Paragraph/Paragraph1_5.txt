The primary causes of metal oxide catalyst deactivation in oxidative dehydrogenation processes, focusing on Cr-based and VOx catalysts, include:

1. Coke formation: This is a major cause of deactivation for both Cr-based and VOx catalysts. Coke deposition occurs due to the transformation of initially produced olefins, especially at high temperatures and in the presence of acid sites [10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070]. The extent of coke formation varies with the oxidant used and catalyst composition [10.1039/C5CY00998G].

2. Reduction of active metal species: During the reaction, active metal oxide species (Cr3+, V5+) are reduced to less active or inactive forms (Cr2+, V3+), leading to a decline in catalytic activity [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

3. Aggregation and sintering of active species: The highly dispersed active CrOx or VOx species may agglomerate or transform into less active forms over time, reducing catalytic activity [10.1039/C9CY01990A].

4. Structural changes: High temperatures during reaction or regeneration can cause changes in the oxide structure, affecting catalyst performance [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.028].

5. Incorporation of active species into the support: For Cr-based catalysts, the heat released during coke combustion can cause chromia to incorporate into the alumina framework, reducing the amount of catalytically active Cr species [10.1021/cr5002436].

Various regeneration methods have been studied to address these deactivation issues:

1. Reoxidation with air or O2: This is the most common and effective method for removing coke deposits and restoring catalyst activity. For CrOx/ZrO2 catalysts, regeneration in O2 at 600°C for 5 minutes can completely restore activity, with this recovery being repeatable for at least 4 cycles [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]. However, high temperatures can cause thermal stress and sintering, potentially leading to gradual irreversible deactivation, especially for Cr-based catalysts [10.1021/cr5002436].

2. CO2 addition: CO2 acts as a milder oxidant and has shown effectiveness in maintaining higher oxidation states of both Cr and V species during the reaction [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2]. It helps reduce coke formation through the Boudouard reaction and assists in product desorption [10.1021/cr5002436]. For VOx catalysts, CO2 can oxidize V2O3 to V2O4, but not fully to V2O5, potentially maintaining better selectivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]. However, CO2 regeneration is less effective than O2 in fully restoring catalyst activity over multiple cycles [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3].

3. Steam addition: Similar to CO2, steam helps reduce coke deposition and assists in product desorption [10.1021/cr5002436].

4. O2 or O2+CO2 mixtures: These are most effective in reducing coke formation during the reaction and maintain Cr in a higher oxidation state (Cr3+) [10.1039/C5CY00998G].

Temperature requirements for regeneration are typically in the range of 550-650°C [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]. Lower temperatures are preferred to minimize thermal stress and sintering, but must be high enough to effectively remove coke.

The impact on catalytic performance varies depending on the regeneration method. While most methods can restore activity and selectivity to some extent, there are trade-offs. CO2 addition can improve catalyst stability and olefin yield, but may not fully restore initial activity [10.1039/C9CY01990A]. Air or O2 regeneration can restore most of the initial activity but may lead to a gradual loss of active sites over multiple cycles [10.1021/cr5002436].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 

The review of various regeneration methods for Cr-based and VOx catalysts in oxidative dehydrogenation processes reveals a complex interplay between catalyst activity, selectivity, and longevity. While traditional regeneration methods using air or O2 are effective in removing coke deposits, they may lead to overoxidation and gradual loss of active sites. The emerging trend of using milder oxidants like CO2 shows promise in maintaining catalyst performance and mitigating some deactivation mechanisms.

Future research directions could focus on:

1. Developing hybrid regeneration methods that combine the benefits of different approaches, such as using CO2 with controlled amounts of O2 to balance coke removal and prevention of overoxidation.

2. Exploring novel catalyst designs that incorporate stabilizers or promoters to enhance resistance to sintering and aggregation during high-temperature regeneration.

3. Investigating in-situ regeneration techniques that allow for continuous operation without the need for offline regeneration cycles.

4. Utilizing advanced characterization techniques to better understand the structural changes occurring during deactivation and regeneration, enabling more targeted regeneration strategies.

5. Exploring the potential of plasma-assisted regeneration or other non-thermal approaches to overcome the limitations of high-temperature treatments.

6. Developing predictive models that can optimize regeneration conditions based on catalyst composition, reaction parameters, and deactivation mechanisms.

By addressing these challenges, future catalysts for oxidative dehydrogenation processes could achieve higher stability, improved selectivity, and longer lifetimes, ultimately leading to more efficient and sustainable chemical production processes.

在氧化脱氢过程中，金属氧化物催化剂失活的主要原因，特别是针对基于Cr和VOx的催化剂，包括：

1. 积碳形成：这是基于Cr和VOx催化剂的主要失活原因。积碳沉积是由于初始生成的烯烃转化而产生的，特别是在高温和酸性位点存在的情况下 [10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070]。积碳形成的程度因所使用的氧化剂和催化剂组成而异 [10.1039/C5CY00998G]。

2. 活性金属物种的还原：在反应过程中，活性金属氧化物物种（Cr3+，V5+）被还原为活性较低或无活性的形式（Cr2+，V3+），导致催化活性下降 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

3. 活性物种的聚集和烧结：高度分散的活性CrOx或VOx物种可能随时间聚集或转化为活性较低的形式，降低催化活性 [10.1039/C9CY01990A]。

4. 结构变化：反应或再生过程中的高温可能导致氧化物结构发生变化，影响催化剂性能 [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.028]。

5. 活性物种进入载体：对于基于Cr的催化剂，积碳燃烧过程中释放的热量可能导致氧化铬进入氧化铝骨架，减少催化活性Cr物种的数量 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

为解决这些失活问题，研究了各种再生方法：

1. 用空气或O2再氧化：这是去除积碳沉积物和恢复催化剂活性最常见和最有效的方法。对于CrOx/ZrO2催化剂，在600°C下用O2再生5分钟可以完全恢复活性，这种恢复在至少4个循环中是可重复的 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]。然而，高温可能导致热应力和烧结，潜在地导致逐渐的不可逆失活，特别是对于基于Cr的催化剂 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

2. CO2添加：CO2作为较温和的氧化剂，在反应过程中显示出保持Cr和V物种较高氧化态的效果 [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2]。它通过布道反应帮助减少积碳形成，并协助产物脱附 [10.1021/cr5002436]。对于VOx催化剂，CO2可以将V2O3氧化到V2O4，但不能完全氧化到V2O5，潜在地保持更好的选择性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。然而，CO2再生在多个循环中完全恢复催化剂活性的效果不如O2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]。

3. 蒸汽添加：类似于CO2，蒸汽有助于减少积碳沉积并协助产物脱附 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

4. O2或O2+CO2混合物：这些方法在反应过程中最有效地减少积碳形成，并将Cr保持在较高的氧化态（Cr3+）[10.1039/C5CY00998G]。

再生的温度要求通常在550-650°C范围内 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]。首选较低温度以最小化热应力和烧结，但必须足够高以有效去除积碳。

对催化性能的影响因再生方法而异。虽然大多数方法可以在一定程度上恢复活性和选择性，但存在权衡。CO2添加可以改善催化剂稳定性和烯烃产率，但可能无法完全恢复初始活性 [10.1039/C9CY01990A]。空气或O2再生可以恢复大部分初始活性，但可能导致多个循环后活性位点逐渐损失 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：

对氧化脱氢过程中Cr基和VOx催化剂各种再生方法的回顾揭示了催化剂活性、选择性和寿命之间复杂的相互作用。虽然使用空气或O2的传统再生方法在去除积碳沉积物方面是有效的，但它们可能导致过度氧化和活性位点的逐渐损失。使用CO2等较温和氧化剂的新兴趋势在保持催化剂性能和缓解某些失活机制方面显示出前景。

未来的研究方向可以集中在：

1. 开发结合不同方法优点的混合再生方法，如使用CO2和受控量的O2来平衡积碳去除和防止过度氧化。

2. 探索新型催化剂设计，纳入稳定剂或促进剂，以增强在高温再生过程中抗烧结和聚集的能力。

3. 研究原位再生技术，允许连续操作而无需离线再生循环。

4. 利用先进的表征技术更好地理解失活和再生过程中发生的结构变化，从而实现更有针对性的再生策略。

5. 探索等离子体辅助再生或其他非热方法的潜力，以克服高温处理的限制。

6. 开发预测模型，基于催化剂组成、反应参数和失活机制优化再生条件。

通过解决这些挑战，未来的氧化脱氢过程催化剂可以实现更高的稳定性、改进的选择性和更长的寿命，最终导致更高效和可持续的化学生产过程。
