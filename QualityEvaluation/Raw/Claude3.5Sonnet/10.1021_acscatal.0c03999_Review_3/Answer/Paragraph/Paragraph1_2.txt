The primary causes of metal oxide catalyst deactivation in oxidative dehydrogenation processes, focusing on Cr-based and VOx catalysts, include:

1. Coke formation: This is the main cause of activity loss for both Cr-based and VOx catalysts. Coke deposition is accelerated at high temperatures and in the presence of acid sites on the catalyst surface [10.1016/S0926-860X(03)00368-5].

2. Reduction of active metal species: During the reaction, Cr3+ species are reduced to inactive Cr2+ state, while VOx species are reduced from their higher oxidation states, decreasing their catalytic activity [10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2].

3. Aggregation and structural changes: The highly dispersed active CrOx or VOx species may agglomerate or transform into less active forms, reducing catalytic activity [10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070] [10.1002/ejic.201701049].

4. Incorporation of active species into the support: For Cr-based catalysts, the heat released during coke combustion can cause chromia to incorporate into the alumina framework, reducing the amount of catalytically active Cr species [10.1021/cr5002436].

Various regeneration methods have been studied to address these issues:

1. Reoxidation with air or O2: This is the most common and effective method for removing coke deposits and restoring catalyst activity. For example, regeneration in O2 at 600°C for 5 minutes can completely restore the activity of CrOx/ZrO2, repeatable for at least 4 cycles [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]. However, this method may lead to gradual irreversible deactivation, especially for Cr-based catalysts, due to the incorporation of chromia into the support [10.1021/cr5002436].

2. CO2 addition: CO2 acts as a milder oxidant, helping to maintain higher oxidation states of both Cr and V species during the reaction. It can eliminate coke deposition and reoxidize the reduced active phase [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. For VOx catalysts, CO2 can oxidize V2O3 to V2O4, but not fully to V2O5, which helps maintain catalyst selectivity by preventing overoxidation [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015].

3. O2 or O2+CO2 mixtures: These are most effective in reducing coke formation during the reaction and maintain Cr in a higher oxidation state (Cr3+) [10.1039/C5CY00998G].

4. Ni promotion with CO2: This enhances CO2 conversion, maintains stable production of propylene, and regenerates reduced Cr2+ species to Cr3+ through reoxidation [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

The effectiveness of these methods varies. O2-based regeneration seems most effective at reversing deactivation but may lead to overoxidation. CO2 addition shows promise in maintaining catalyst performance and potentially improving selectivity, especially for VOx catalysts where overoxidation is a concern.

Temperature requirements for regeneration are typically in the range of 550-650°C [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]. Lower temperatures are preferred to minimize thermal stress and sintering, but must be high enough to effectively remove coke.

The impact on catalytic performance varies depending on the regeneration method. While most methods can restore activity and selectivity, there are trade-offs. For instance, co-feeding O2 during the reaction removes coke but reduces selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of oxidative dehydrogenation catalysis, particularly focusing on Cr-based and VOx catalysts, presents both challenges and opportunities for future research and development. The complex interplay between catalyst deactivation mechanisms and regeneration strategies highlights the need for innovative approaches to catalyst design and process optimization.

Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Rational catalyst design: Developing catalysts with enhanced resistance to coke formation and sintering, possibly through the incorporation of promoters or novel support materials.

2. In-situ regeneration techniques: Exploring methods to continuously regenerate catalysts during the reaction, minimizing downtime and extending catalyst lifetime.

3. Advanced characterization techniques: Utilizing operando spectroscopy and microscopy to better understand the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation state during reaction and regeneration cycles.

4. Computational modeling: Employing molecular simulations and machine learning approaches to predict catalyst behavior and optimize regeneration strategies.

5. CO2 utilization: Further investigating the potential of CO2 as a mild oxidant in regeneration processes, potentially coupling ODH reactions with CO2 conversion for improved sustainability.

6. Hybrid regeneration methods: Developing synergistic combinations of regeneration techniques (e.g., pulsed O2 with continuous CO2 addition) to balance activity recovery with selectivity maintenance.

These research directions could lead to significant advancements in catalyst performance, process efficiency, and sustainability in the field of oxidative dehydrogenation.

在氧化脱氢过程中，以Cr基和VOx催化剂为重点的金属氧化物催化剂失活的主要原因包括：

1. 积炭形成：这是Cr基和VOx催化剂活性损失的主要原因。在高温和催化剂表面存在酸性位点的情况下，积炭沉积会加速[10.1016/S0926-860X(03)00368-5]。

2. 活性金属物种的还原：在反应过程中，Cr3+物种被还原为无活性的Cr2+状态，而VOx物种从其较高的氧化态被还原，降低了它们的催化活性[10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2]。

3. 聚集和结构变化：高度分散的活性CrOx或VOx物种可能会聚集或转化为活性较低的形式，降低催化活性[10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070] [10.1002/ejic.201701049]。

4. 活性物种并入支撑体：对于Cr基催化剂，在积炭燃烧过程中释放的热量可能导致氧化铬并入氧化铝框架，减少了催化活性Cr物种的数量[10.1021/cr5002436]。

已经研究了各种再生方法来解决这些问题：

1. 空气或O2再氧化：这是去除积炭沉积物和恢复催化剂活性最常见和最有效的方法。例如，在600°C下用O2再生5分钟可以完全恢复CrOx/ZrO2的活性，可重复至少4个周期[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]。然而，这种方法可能导致逐渐的不可逆失活，特别是对于Cr基催化剂，这是由于氧化铬并入支撑体中[10.1021/cr5002436]。

2. CO2添加：CO2作为一种较温和的氧化剂，有助于在反应过程中维持Cr和V物种的较高氧化态。它可以消除积炭沉积并重新氧化还原的活性相[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。对于VOx催化剂，CO2可以将V2O3氧化为V2O4，但不能完全氧化为V2O5，这有助于通过防止过度氧化来维持催化剂的选择性[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。

3. O2或O2+CO2混合物：这些方法在反应过程中最有效地减少积炭形成，并将Cr维持在较高的氧化态（Cr3+）[10.1039/C5CY00998G]。

4. Ni促进的CO2：这增强了CO2的转化，维持了稳定的丙烯生产，并通过再氧化将还原的Cr2+物种再生为Cr3+[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

这些方法的有效性各不相同。基于O2的再生似乎最有效地逆转失活，但可能导致过度氧化。CO2添加在维持催化剂性能和潜在改善选择性方面显示出希望，特别是对于过度氧化是一个问题的VOx催化剂。

再生的温度要求通常在550-650°C范围内[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]。较低的温度更受欢迎，以最小化热应力和烧结，但必须足够高以有效去除积炭。

对催化性能的影响因再生方法而异。虽然大多数方法可以恢复活性和选择性，但存在权衡。例如，在反应过程中共进料O2可以去除积炭，但会降低选择性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：氧化脱氢催化领域，特别是关注Cr基和VOx催化剂，为未来的研究和开发提出了挑战和机遇。催化剂失活机制和再生策略之间的复杂相互作用突显了催化剂设计和工艺优化需要创新方法的必要性。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 理性催化剂设计：开发具有增强抗积炭形成和烧结能力的催化剂，可能通过引入促进剂或新型支撑材料。

2. 原位再生技术：探索在反应过程中连续再生催化剂的方法，最小化停机时间并延长催化剂寿命。

3. 先进表征技术：利用原位光谱和显微技术更好地理解催化剂在反应和再生周期中的动态结构和氧化态变化。

4. 计算机模拟：采用分子模拟和机器学习方法预测催化剂行为并优化再生策略。

5. CO2利用：进一步研究CO2作为再生过程中温和氧化剂的潜力，可能将ODH反应与CO2转化耦合，以提高可持续性。

6. 混合再生方法：开发再生技术的协同组合（例如，脉冲O2与连续CO2添加），以平衡活性恢复和选择性维持。

这些研究方向可能会在氧化脱氢领域的催化剂性能、过程效率和可持续性方面带来重大进展。
