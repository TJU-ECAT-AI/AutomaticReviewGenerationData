The primary causes of metal oxide catalyst deactivation in oxidative dehydrogenation processes, focusing on Cr-based and VOx catalysts, include:

1. Coke formation: This is the main cause of activity loss for both Cr-based and VOx catalysts. Coke deposition is accelerated at high temperatures and in the presence of acid sites on the catalyst surface [10.1016/S0926-860X(03)00368-5].

2. Reduction of active metal species: During the reaction, Cr3+ species are reduced to inactive Cr2+ state, while VOx species are reduced from higher to lower oxidation states, decreasing their catalytic activity [10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.028].

3. Aggregation and sintering of active species: The decreased dispersion of Cr and V species due to clustering or sintering leads to a loss of active sites [10.1039/C9CY01990A] [10.1021/cr5002436].

4. Structural changes: High temperatures during reaction or regeneration can cause changes in the oxide structure, potentially leading to the formation of less active species [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.028].

5. Incorporation of active species into the support: For Cr-based catalysts, the heat released during coke combustion can cause chromia to incorporate into the alumina framework, reducing the amount of catalytically active Cr species [10.1021/cr5002436].

Regarding regeneration methods and their effectiveness:

1. Reoxidation with air or O2:
   - Most effective for removing coke deposits and restoring catalyst activity
   - Temperature requirements: Generally around 550-650°C
   - Can lead to overoxidation, especially for VOx catalysts
   - May cause gradual irreversible deactivation over multiple cycles for Cr-based catalysts
   [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3] [10.1039/C5CY00998G]

2. CO2 addition:
   - Acts as a milder oxidant, helping to maintain higher oxidation states of Cr and V species
   - Suppresses coke formation and assists in its removal through the Boudouard reaction
   - Temperature requirements: Similar to reaction temperatures (e.g., 550°C)
   - Less effective than O2 in fully restoring activity but helps maintain selectivity
   - Particularly beneficial for VOx catalysts, oxidizing V2O3 to V2O4 instead of V2O5
   [10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]

3. O2 + CO2 mixtures:
   - Combines the benefits of both oxidants
   - Effective in reducing coke formation during the reaction
   - Maintains Cr in a higher oxidation state (Cr3+)
   - May offer a balance between activity restoration and selectivity maintenance
   [10.1039/C5CY00998G]

4. Steam addition:
   - Similar to CO2, helps reduce coke deposition and assists in product desorption
   - Not as widely studied in the provided literature
   [10.1021/cr5002436]

The impact on catalytic performance varies depending on the regeneration method:

- O2 regeneration is most effective in restoring activity but may lead to overoxidation and gradual loss of active sites over multiple cycles.
- CO2 addition helps maintain catalyst stability and selectivity, particularly for VOx catalysts, by mitigating overoxidation and suppressing coke formation.
- The use of promoters (e.g., Ni, K) in combination with CO2 can enhance catalyst stability and regeneration effectiveness [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of effective regeneration strategies for metal oxide catalysts in oxidative dehydrogenation processes remains a critical challenge. While current methods show promise, there is still room for improvement in balancing activity restoration, selectivity maintenance, and long-term stability. Future research directions may include:

1. Tailored regeneration protocols: Developing catalyst-specific regeneration methods that consider the unique properties of Cr-based and VOx catalysts, potentially combining different oxidants and temperature profiles to optimize performance.

2. In-situ regeneration techniques: Exploring methods for continuous or semi-continuous regeneration during the reaction, possibly using pulsed oxidant addition or cyclic temperature variations to maintain catalyst activity without interrupting the process.

3. Novel promoters and supports: Investigating new materials that can enhance catalyst stability, resist sintering, and facilitate more efficient regeneration, such as advanced mixed metal oxides or nanostructured supports.

4. Mechanistic studies: Conducting detailed investigations into the atomic-level processes occurring during deactivation and regeneration to inform the design of more resilient catalysts and more effective regeneration strategies.

5. Sustainable regeneration approaches: Exploring the use of renewable oxidants or energy sources for catalyst regeneration, aligning with broader sustainability goals in the chemical industry.

By addressing these challenges and opportunities, future research can lead to the development of more efficient, stable, and selective catalysts for oxidative dehydrogenation processes, potentially revolutionizing the production of important chemical intermediates.

<Chinese>
在氧化脱氢过程中，金属氧化物催化剂失活的主要原因，以Cr基和VOx催化剂为重点，包括：

1. 积碳形成：这是Cr基和VOx催化剂活性损失的主要原因。高温和催化剂表面酸性位的存在会加速积碳沉积[10.1016/S0926-860X(03)00368-5]。

2. 活性金属物种的还原：反应过程中，Cr3+物种被还原为无活性的Cr2+状态，而VOx物种从高氧化态被还原为低氧化态，降低其催化活性[10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.028]。

3. 活性物种的聚集和烧结：Cr和V物种由于聚集或烧结导致的分散度下降，导致活性位点的损失[10.1039/C9CY01990A] [10.1021/cr5002436]。

4. 结构变化：反应或再生过程中的高温可能导致氧化物结构发生变化，潜在地形成活性较低的物种[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.028]。

5. 活性物种掺入载体：对于Cr基催化剂，积碳燃烧释放的热量可能导致氧化铬掺入氧化铝框架，减少催化活性Cr物种的数量[10.1021/cr5002436]。

关于再生方法及其有效性：

1. 空气或O2再氧化：
   - 最有效地去除积碳沉积物并恢复催化剂活性
   - 温度要求：一般在550-650°C左右
   - 可能导致过度氧化，特别是对VOx催化剂
   - 对Cr基催化剂可能在多个循环后导致不可逆的活性逐渐下降
   [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3] [10.1039/C5CY00998G]

2. CO2添加：
   - 作为较温和的氧化剂，有助于维持Cr和V物种的较高氧化态
   - 抑制积碳形成，并通过布道反应协助其去除
   - 温度要求：与反应温度相似（如550°C）
   - 在完全恢复活性方面不如O2有效，但有助于维持选择性
   - 对VOx催化剂特别有益，将V2O3氧化为V2O4而不是V2O5
   [10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]

3. O2 + CO2混合物：
   - 结合了两种氧化剂的优点
   - 有效减少反应过程中的积碳形成
   - 维持Cr在较高的氧化态（Cr3+）
   - 可能在活性恢复和选择性维持之间提供平衡
   [10.1039/C5CY00998G]

4. 蒸汽添加：
   - 类似于CO2，有助于减少积碳沉积并协助产物脱附
   - 在提供的文献中研究不够广泛
   [10.1021/cr5002436]

对催化性能的影响因再生方法而异：

- O2再生在恢复活性方面最有效，但可能导致过度氧化和多个循环后活性位点的逐渐损失。
- CO2添加有助于维持催化剂稳定性和选择性，特别是对VOx催化剂，通过缓解过度氧化和抑制积碳形成。
- 使用促进剂（如Ni，K）与CO2结合可以提高催化剂稳定性和再生效果[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：开发氧化脱氢过程中金属氧化物催化剂的有效再生策略仍然是一个关键挑战。虽然当前方法显示出了前景，但在平衡活性恢复、选择性维持和长期稳定性方面仍有改进空间。未来研究方向可能包括：

1. 定制再生协议：开发考虑Cr基和VOx催化剂独特性质的特定催化剂再生方法，可能结合不同氧化剂和温度曲线以优化性能。

2. 原位再生技术：探索反应过程中连续或半连续再生的方法，可能使用脉冲氧化剂添加或循环温度变化来维持催化剂活性，而不中断过程。

3. 新型促进剂和载体：研究可以增强催化剂稳定性、抗烧结和促进更有效再生的新材料，如先进的混合金属氧化物或纳米结构载体。

4. 机理研究：对失活和再生过程中发生的原子级过程进行详细调查，以指导设计更具韧性的催化剂和更有效的再生策略。

5. 可持续再生方法：探索使用可再生氧化剂或能源进行催化剂再生，与化学工业更广泛的可持续发展目标保持一致。

通过解决这些挑战和机遇，未来的研究可以导致开发出更高效、稳定和选择性的氧化脱氢过程催化剂，可能彻底改变重要化学中间体的生产。
