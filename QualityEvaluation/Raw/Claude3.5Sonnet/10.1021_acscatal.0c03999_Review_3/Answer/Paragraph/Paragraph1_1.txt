The primary causes of metal oxide catalyst deactivation in oxidative dehydrogenation processes, focusing on Cr-based and VOx catalysts, include:

1. Coke formation: This is a major cause of deactivation for both Cr-based and VOx catalysts. Coke deposition is influenced by factors such as reaction temperature, presence of acid sites, and the nature of the feed [10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017].

2. Reduction of active metal species: For Cr-based catalysts, this involves the conversion of active Cr3+ to inactive Cr2+ states. In VOx catalysts, reduction from higher to lower oxidation states (e.g., V5+ to V3+) occurs [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

3. Aggregation or sintering of active species: This leads to a decrease in the dispersion of active sites, particularly for Cr-based catalysts [10.1039/C9CY01990A].

4. Structural changes: High temperatures during reaction or regeneration can cause rearrangement of the oxide structure [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.028].

5. Incorporation of active species into the support: For Cr-based catalysts, chromia can be incorporated into the alumina framework during regeneration, reducing the amount of catalytically active Cr species [10.1021/cr5002436].

Regeneration methods and their effectiveness:

1. Reoxidation with air or O2:
   - Most effective for complete removal of coke and restoration of catalyst activity.
   - Temperature range: 550-650°C, typically for 2-6 hours [10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070].
   - Can lead to overoxidation and gradual loss of active sites over multiple cycles [10.1021/cr5002436].

2. CO2 regeneration:
   - Acts as a milder oxidant, helping to maintain catalyst selectivity.
   - For Cr-based catalysts, it helps maintain Cr in a higher oxidation state [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
   - For VOx catalysts, it oxidizes V2O3 to V2O4, but not to V2O5, potentially mitigating overoxidation [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015].
   - Temperature range: Similar to air/O2 regeneration (around 500°C) [10.1039/C9CY01990A].
   - Less effective than O2 in completely restoring activity, especially over multiple cycles [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3].

3. Combined CO2 and O2 regeneration:
   - Balances the benefits of both oxidants.
   - Improves coke removal while potentially reducing overoxidation risks [10.1039/C5CY00998G].

4. In-situ regeneration by co-feeding CO2 or O2:
   - Helps maintain catalyst activity during the reaction.
   - CO2 co-feeding can reduce coke formation and mitigate overoxidation [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2].
   - O2 co-feeding is more effective at preventing coke formation but can decrease selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3].

Impact on catalytic performance:

- Air/O2 regeneration typically restores most of the initial activity but may lead to gradual deactivation over multiple cycles due to irreversible changes in the catalyst structure.
- CO2 regeneration or co-feeding helps maintain selectivity and can extend catalyst lifetime, particularly for VOx catalysts where overoxidation is a concern.
- The effectiveness of regeneration methods often depends on the specific catalyst composition and reaction conditions.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of more effective and sustainable regeneration strategies for metal oxide catalysts in oxidative dehydrogenation processes remains a crucial area for future research. While current methods show promise, there is still room for improvement in balancing activity restoration, selectivity maintenance, and long-term stability.

Future research directions may include:

1. Development of novel, tailored regeneration protocols that combine multiple oxidants or employ cyclic temperature variations to optimize both coke removal and active site preservation.

2. Exploration of alternative mild oxidants or regeneration additives that can effectively remove coke while minimizing structural changes to the catalyst.

3. Design of advanced in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic changes occurring during reaction and regeneration, allowing for more precise control of the catalyst state.

4. Investigation of new catalyst formulations or supports that are more resistant to deactivation or more amenable to regeneration, potentially incorporating elements that facilitate easier coke removal or maintain active site dispersion.

5. Development of predictive models and machine learning approaches to optimize regeneration conditions based on catalyst composition, reaction parameters, and deactivation mechanisms.

By addressing these challenges, future catalytic systems for oxidative dehydrogenation may achieve higher stability, longer lifetimes, and improved overall process efficiency, contributing to more sustainable chemical production processes.

氧化脱氢过程中金属氧化物催化剂失活的主要原因，特别针对基于Cr和VOx的催化剂，包括：

1. 积碳形成：这是Cr基和VOx催化剂失活的主要原因。积碳沉积受反应温度、酸性位点存在和进料性质等因素的影响[10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017]。

2. 活性金属物种的还原：对于Cr基催化剂，这涉及活性Cr3+转化为非活性Cr2+状态。在VOx催化剂中，发生从高氧化态到低氧化态的还原（例如，V5+到V3+）[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

3. 活性物种的聚集或烧结：这导致活性位点分散度降低，特别是对于Cr基催化剂[10.1039/C9CY01990A]。

4. 结构变化：反应或再生过程中的高温可能导致氧化物结构重排[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.028]。

5. 活性物种融入载体：对于Cr基催化剂，在再生过程中铬氧化物可能会融入氧化铝框架，减少了催化活性Cr物种的数量[10.1021/cr5002436]。

再生方法及其有效性：

1. 空气或O2再氧化：
   - 最有效地完全去除积碳并恢复催化剂活性。
   - 温度范围：550-650°C，通常持续2-6小时[10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070]。
   - 可能导致过度氧化和多个循环后活性位点的逐渐损失[10.1021/cr5002436]。

2. CO2再生：
   - 作为温和的氧化剂，有助于保持催化剂选择性。
   - 对于Cr基催化剂，它有助于保持Cr处于较高的氧化态[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
   - 对于VOx催化剂，它将V2O3氧化为V2O4，而不是V2O5，可能缓解过度氧化[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。
   - 温度范围：与空气/O2再生相似（约500°C）[10.1039/C9CY01990A]。
   - 在完全恢复活性方面不如O2有效，特别是在多个循环后[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]。

3. CO2和O2组合再生：
   - 平衡两种氧化剂的优点。
   - 改善积碳去除，同时可能降低过度氧化风险[10.1039/C5CY00998G]。

4. 通过共进料CO2或O2进行原位再生：
   - 有助于在反应过程中维持催化剂活性。
   - CO2共进料可以减少积碳形成并缓解过度氧化[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2]。
   - O2共进料在防止积碳形成方面更有效，但可能降低选择性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]。

对催化性能的影响：

- 空气/O2再生通常可以恢复大部分初始活性，但由于催化剂结构的不可逆变化，可能导致多个循环后逐渐失活。
- CO2再生或共进料有助于保持选择性并可延长催化剂寿命，特别是对于过度氧化是一个问题的VOx催化剂。
- 再生方法的有效性通常取决于特定的催化剂组成和反应条件。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：开发更有效和可持续的金属氧化物催化剂再生策略在氧化脱氢过程中仍然是未来研究的关键领域。虽然当前方法显示出前景，但在平衡活性恢复、选择性维持和长期稳定性方面仍有改进空间。

未来研究方向可能包括：

1. 开发新型、定制的再生协议，结合多种氧化剂或采用循环温度变化，以优化积碳去除和活性位点保存。

2. 探索替代性温和氧化剂或再生添加剂，可以有效去除积碳同时最小化催化剂的结构变化。

3. 设计先进的原位表征技术，以更好地理解反应和再生过程中发生的动态变化，从而更精确地控制催化剂状态。

4. 研究新的催化剂配方或载体，这些配方或载体对失活更具抵抗力或更易于再生，可能包含有助于更容易去除积碳或维持活性位点分散的元素。

5. 开发预测模型和机器学习方法，基于催化剂组成、反应参数和失活机制优化再生条件。

通过解决这些挑战，未来的氧化脱氢催化系统可能实现更高的稳定性、更长的寿命和改进的整体过程效率，为更可持续的化学生产过程做出贡献。
