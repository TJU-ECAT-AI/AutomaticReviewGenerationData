The primary causes of metal oxide catalyst deactivation in oxidative dehydrogenation processes, focusing on Cr-based and VOx catalysts, include:

1. Coke formation: This is a major cause of deactivation for both Cr-based and VOx catalysts. Coke deposition leads to blockage of active sites and pore structures [10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017]. The extent of coke formation varies with the oxidant used and catalyst composition [10.1039/C5CY00998G].

2. Reduction of active metal species: During the reaction, active metal oxide species (e.g., Cr3+ to Cr2+, V5+ to lower oxidation states) are reduced, leading to a decline in catalytic activity [10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.028].

3. Aggregation and sintering of active species: High temperatures during reaction or regeneration can cause metal oxide species to aggregate or sinter, forming larger crystallites or mixed oxides with reduced activity [10.1021/cr5002436].

4. Structural changes: Incorporation of active species into the support material (e.g., chromia into alumina framework) can occur during regeneration, reducing the amount of catalytically active species [10.1021/cr5002436].

5. Strong adsorption of reactants on active sites: This can lead to site blocking and initial deactivation [10.1021/cr5002436].

Regeneration methods and their effectiveness:

1. Reoxidation with air or O2:
   - Most effective for complete removal of coke deposits and restoration of catalyst activity [10.1039/C5CY00998G].
   - Can lead to overoxidation and formation of non-selective oxygen species [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015].
   - Typically performed at high temperatures (550-650°C) [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3].
   - May cause gradual irreversible deactivation over multiple cycles, especially for Cr-based catalysts [10.1021/cr5002436].

2. CO2 addition:
   - Acts as a milder oxidant, helping to maintain higher oxidation states of Cr and V species during reaction [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2].
   - Suppresses coke formation and assists in its removal through the Boudouard reaction [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.03.001].
   - Partially reoxidizes reduced metal species (e.g., V2O3 to V2O4) [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015].
   - Can be effective at lower temperatures and during the reaction itself [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2].
   - Limited in its ability to fully regenerate catalysts over multiple cycles [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3].

3. O2 + CO2 mixture:
   - Combines the benefits of O2's strong oxidizing power with CO2's milder nature [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
   - Can improve catalyst stability and maintain higher oxidation states of active species [10.1039/C5CY00998G].

Temperature requirements:
- Air/O2 regeneration: Typically 550-650°C [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3].
- CO2 regeneration: Can be effective at lower temperatures, but specific requirements vary by catalyst system [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2].

Impact on catalytic performance:
- Air/O2 regeneration can restore most of the initial activity but may lead to gradual loss of active sites over multiple cycles [10.1021/cr5002436].
- CO2 addition can improve catalyst stability and olefin yield, but may not fully recover initial activity [10.1039/C9CY01990A].
- The use of promoters (e.g., Ni, K) can enhance catalyst stability and reduce coke formation [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

CO2 addition for mitigating overoxidation in VOx-catalyzed reactions:
- CO2 acts as a mild oxidant, helping to maintain a balance between oxidation states of vanadium species [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2].
- It can prevent the formation of bulk V2O5, which favors total oxidation [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015].
- CO2 addition allows for lower reaction temperatures, potentially reducing overoxidation risks [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The research on metal oxide catalyst deactivation and regeneration in oxidative dehydrogenation processes reveals a complex interplay between catalyst composition, reaction conditions, and regeneration methods. Future research directions may focus on:

1. Development of novel catalyst formulations that combine the benefits of different metal oxides (e.g., Cr-V mixed oxides) to enhance stability and selectivity.

2. Exploration of advanced in-situ regeneration techniques that can maintain catalyst activity without interrupting the reaction process.

3. Utilization of machine learning and high-throughput screening to optimize catalyst compositions and regeneration protocols for specific reaction conditions.

4. Investigation of alternative mild oxidants or oxidant mixtures that can balance effective regeneration with minimal overoxidation.

5. Design of hierarchical catalyst structures that can better resist coking and sintering while maintaining high dispersion of active sites.

6. Integration of oxidative dehydrogenation processes with CO2 utilization technologies to address both catalyst regeneration and carbon mitigation challenges.

These future directions could lead to more efficient and sustainable oxidative dehydrogenation processes with improved catalyst longevity and selectivity.

在氧化脱氢过程中，金属氧化物催化剂失活的主要原因，重点关注Cr基和VOx催化剂，包括：

1. 积碳形成：这是Cr基和VOx催化剂失活的主要原因。积碳导致活性位点和孔结构堵塞[10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017]。积碳程度随氧化剂和催化剂组成而变化[10.1039/C5CY00998G]。

2. 活性金属物种还原：在反应过程中，活性金属氧化物物种（如Cr3+还原为Cr2+，V5+还原为低价态）被还原，导致催化活性下降[10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.028]。

3. 活性物种聚集和烧结：反应或再生过程中的高温可能导致金属氧化物物种聚集或烧结，形成更大的晶体或活性降低的混合氧化物[10.1021/cr5002436]。

4. 结构变化：再生过程中，活性物种可能会掺入载体材料（如氧化铬掺入氧化铝骨架），减少催化活性物种的数量[10.1021/cr5002436]。

5. 反应物在活性位点上的强吸附：这可能导致位点阻塞和初始失活[10.1021/cr5002436]。

再生方法及其有效性：

1. 空气或O2再氧化：
   - 最有效地完全去除积碳沉积物并恢复催化剂活性[10.1039/C5CY00998G]。
   - 可能导致过度氧化和非选择性氧物种的形成[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。
   - 通常在高温下进行（550-650°C）[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]。
   - 可能导致多个循环后逐渐不可逆失活，特别是对于Cr基催化剂[10.1021/cr5002436]。

2. CO2添加：
   - 作为较温和的氧化剂，有助于在反应过程中维持Cr和V物种的较高氧化态[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2]。
   - 抑制积碳形成，并通过布德瓦反应协助去除积碳[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.03.001]。
   - 部分再氧化还原的金属物种（如V2O3到V2O4）[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。
   - 可在较低温度下和反应过程中有效[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2]。
   - 在多个循环中完全再生催化剂的能力有限[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]。

3. O2 + CO2混合物：
   - 结合了O2强氧化能力和CO2温和性质的优点[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
   - 可以提高催化剂稳定性并维持活性物种的较高氧化态[10.1039/C5CY00998G]。

温度要求：
- 空气/O2再生：通常550-650°C [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]。
- CO2再生：可在较低温度下有效，但具体要求因催化剂系统而异[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2]。

对催化性能的影响：
- 空气/O2再生可恢复大部分初始活性，但可能导致多个循环后活性位点的逐渐损失[10.1021/cr5002436]。
- CO2添加可以提高催化剂稳定性和烯烃产率，但可能无法完全恢复初始活性[10.1039/C9CY01990A]。
- 使用助剂（如Ni、K）可以增强催化剂稳定性并减少积碳形成[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

CO2添加减轻VOx催化反应中过度氧化的作用：
- CO2作为温和的氧化剂，有助于维持钒物种氧化态之间的平衡[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2]。
- 它可以防止有利于完全氧化的块状V2O5的形成[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。
- CO2添加允许在较低的反应温度下进行，可能降低过度氧化的风险[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。

综合理解与未来展望：关于氧化脱氢过程中金属氧化物催化剂失活和再生的研究揭示了催化剂组成、反应条件和再生方法之间的复杂相互作用。未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 开发结合不同金属氧化物优点的新型催化剂配方（如Cr-V混合氧化物），以提高稳定性和选择性。

2. 探索先进的原位再生技术，在不中断反应过程的情况下维持催化剂活性。

3. 利用机器学习和高通量筛选技术，为特定反应条件优化催化剂组成和再生方案。

4. 研究可以平衡有效再生和最小化过度氧化的替代温和氧化剂或氧化剂混合物。

5. 设计能更好地抵抗积碳和烧结，同时保持高度分散活性位点的分层催化剂结构。

6. 将氧化脱氢过程与CO2利用技术相结合，同时解决催化剂再生和碳减排挑战。

这些未来方向可能导致更高效、更可持续的氧化脱氢过程，提高催化剂寿命和选择性。
