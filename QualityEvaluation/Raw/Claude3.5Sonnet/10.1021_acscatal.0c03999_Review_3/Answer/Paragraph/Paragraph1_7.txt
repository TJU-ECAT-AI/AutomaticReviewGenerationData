<English>
The primary causes of metal oxide catalyst deactivation in oxidative dehydrogenation processes, focusing on Cr-based and VOx catalysts, include:

1. Coke formation: This is the main cause of deactivation for both Cr-based and VOx catalysts. Coke deposits on the catalyst surface, blocking active sites and reducing catalytic activity [10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017].

2. Reduction of active metal species: During the reaction, the active metal oxide species (Cr3+ and V5+) can be reduced to less active or inactive forms (Cr2+ and V3+/V4+), leading to a decline in catalytic activity [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

3. Aggregation or sintering of active species: High temperatures during reaction or regeneration can cause metal oxide species to cluster or form larger crystallites, reducing the number of active sites [10.1039/C9CY01990A].

4. Structural changes: The catalyst support or the metal oxide structure itself can undergo changes at high temperatures, affecting the overall catalytic performance [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.028].

5. Strong adsorption of reactants on active sites: This can temporarily block active sites and reduce catalytic activity [10.1021/cr5002436].

Regeneration strategies and their effectiveness:

1. Reoxidation with air or O2:
   - Most effective for complete removal of coke deposits and restoration of the highest oxidation states of Cr and V species.
   - Temperature requirements: Typically 550-650°C for 2-6 hours [10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070].
   - Can lead to overoxidation, especially for VOx catalysts, potentially reducing selectivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015].
   - May cause irreversible changes to the catalyst structure over multiple regeneration cycles [10.1021/cr5002436].

2. CO2 regeneration:
   - Acts as a milder oxidant, helping to maintain higher selectivity.
   - Temperature requirements: Similar to air/O2 regeneration, around 500-600°C [10.1039/C9CY01990A].
   - Less effective in removing coke compared to O2, but helps suppress coke formation during the reaction [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2].
   - Partially reoxidizes reduced metal species (e.g., V2O3 to V2O4), maintaining a balance between activity and selectivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015].
   - Enhances catalyst stability and can improve propylene yield in Cr-based catalysts [10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4].

3. Co-feeding strategies:
   - Adding CO2 or O2 during the reaction can help maintain catalyst activity and reduce coke formation.
   - CO2 co-feeding is particularly effective for VOx catalysts in mitigating overoxidation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3].
   - O2 co-feeding with CO2 can improve coke removal but may decrease selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3].

Performance optimization:

1. Catalyst design:
   - Optimizing metal oxide dispersion and coverage to achieve near-monolayer coverage can improve activity and stability [10.1002/ejic.201701049].
   - Adding promoters like Ni to Cr-based catalysts can enhance CO2 utilization and maintain stable activity [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
   - Incorporating WOx in VOx catalysts can hinder clustering of VOx species and improve stability [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015].

2. Reaction conditions:
   - Balancing oxidant concentration (CO2/O2 ratio) to minimize coke formation while maintaining selectivity.
   - Optimizing temperature to maximize activity while minimizing irreversible structural changes.

3. Regeneration protocols:
   - Implementing cyclic regeneration with carefully controlled oxidant compositions and temperatures to balance coke removal, metal oxide reoxidation, and prevention of irreversible structural changes.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The complex interplay between catalyst composition, reaction conditions, and regeneration methods in oxidative dehydrogenation processes presents both challenges and opportunities for future research. The use of CO2 as a mild oxidant shows promise in balancing activity, selectivity, and stability, particularly for VOx catalysts. Future research directions may include:

1. Development of novel multi-component catalysts that synergistically combine the benefits of different metal oxides (e.g., Cr, V, Mo) with promoters to enhance stability and CO2 utilization.

2. In-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation states during reaction and regeneration, enabling more precise control of these processes.

3. Exploration of alternative regeneration methods, such as plasma-assisted regeneration or electrochemical regeneration, which may offer more controlled and energy-efficient approaches to catalyst reactivation.

4. Integration of machine learning algorithms to optimize reaction conditions and regeneration protocols in real-time, maximizing catalyst lifetime and process efficiency.

5. Investigation of novel support materials or hierarchical catalyst structures that can better resist sintering and maintain high dispersion of active species over multiple reaction-regeneration cycles.

These advancements could lead to more efficient and sustainable oxidative dehydrogenation processes, with improved catalyst lifetimes and better utilization of CO2 as both a reactant and regeneration agent.

<Chinese>
在氧化脱氢过程中，以Cr基和VOx催化剂为重点的金属氧化物催化剂失活的主要原因包括：

1. 积碳形成：这是Cr基和VOx催化剂失活的主要原因。焦炭沉积在催化剂表面，阻塞活性位点并降低催化活性 [10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017]。

2. 活性金属物种的还原：在反应过程中，活性金属氧化物物种（Cr3+和V5+）可能被还原为活性较低或无活性的形式（Cr2+和V3+/V4+），导致催化活性下降 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

3. 活性物种的聚集或烧结：反应或再生过程中的高温可能导致金属氧化物物种聚集或形成更大的晶体，减少活性位点数量 [10.1039/C9CY01990A]。

4. 结构变化：催化剂载体或金属氧化物结构本身在高温下可能发生变化，影响整体催化性能 [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.028]。

5. 反应物在活性位点上的强吸附：这可能暂时阻塞活性位点并降低催化活性 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

再生策略及其有效性：

1. 空气或O2再氧化：
   - 最有效地完全去除焦炭沉积物并恢复Cr和V物种的最高氧化态。
   - 温度要求：通常550-650°C，持续2-6小时 [10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070]。
   - 可能导致过度氧化，特别是对于VOx催化剂，可能降低选择性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。
   - 在多个再生周期后可能导致催化剂结构的不可逆变化 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

2. CO2再生：
   - 作为较温和的氧化剂，有助于保持较高的选择性。
   - 温度要求：与空气/O2再生相似，约500-600°C [10.1039/C9CY01990A]。
   - 与O2相比，去除焦炭的效果较差，但有助于抑制反应过程中的焦炭形成 [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2]。
   - 部分再氧化还原的金属物种（如V2O3到V2O4），在活性和选择性之间保持平衡 [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。
   - 增强催化剂稳定性，可以提高Cr基催化剂的丙烯产率 [10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4]。

3. 共进料策略：
   - 在反应过程中添加CO2或O2可以帮助维持催化剂活性并减少焦炭形成。
   - CO2共进料对VOx催化剂特别有效，可减轻过度氧化 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]。
   - O2与CO2共进料可以改善焦炭去除，但可能降低选择性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_3]。

性能优化：

1. 催化剂设计：
   - 优化金属氧化物的分散和覆盖度，达到近单层覆盖可以提高活性和稳定性 [10.1002/ejic.201701049]。
   - 在Cr基催化剂中添加Ni等促进剂可以增强CO2利用率并保持稳定活性 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
   - 在VOx催化剂中添加WOx可以阻碍VOx物种的聚集并提高稳定性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。

2. 反应条件：
   - 平衡氧化剂浓度（CO2/O2比例）以最小化焦炭形成，同时保持选择性。
   - 优化温度以最大化活性，同时最小化不可逆的结构变化。

3. 再生方案：
   - 实施循环再生，仔细控制氧化剂组成和温度，以平衡焦炭去除、金属氧化物再氧化和防止不可逆结构变化。

※※※

综合理解和前景展望：氧化脱氢过程中催化剂组成、反应条件和再生方法之间的复杂相互作用为未来研究提供了挑战和机遇。使用CO2作为温和氧化剂在平衡活性、选择性和稳定性方面表现出潜力，特别是对于VOx催化剂。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 开发新型多组分催化剂，协同结合不同金属氧化物（如Cr、V、Mo）与促进剂的优势，以增强稳定性和CO2利用率。

2. 采用原位表征技术，更好地理解催化剂在反应和再生过程中结构和氧化态的动态变化，从而实现对这些过程的更精确控制。

3. 探索替代再生方法，如等离子体辅助再生或电化学再生，这可能为催化剂再活化提供更可控和节能的方法。

4. 集成机器学习算法，实时优化反应条件和再生方案，最大化催化剂寿命和过程效率。

5. 研究新型载体材料或分级催化剂结构，在多个反应-再生周期中能更好地抵抗烧结并保持活性物种的高分散性。

这些进展可能会导致更高效和可持续的氧化脱氢过程，延长催化剂寿命，并更好地利用CO2作为反应物和再生剂。
