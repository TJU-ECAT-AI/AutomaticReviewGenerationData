The multidimensional nature of the ODHP reaction mechanism over B-based catalysts, encompassing both surface and gas-phase radical reactions, significantly enhances our understanding of the overall reaction network. This complex interplay between heterogeneous catalysis on the boron-based surface and homogeneous gas-phase chemistry contributes to the exceptional performance of these catalysts in terms of activity and selectivity.

Key aspects of the multidimensional mechanism include:

1. Surface Reactions:
   - BOx and BOH sites on the catalyst surface act as active sites for selective C-H bond activation [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014].
   - These sites facilitate the formation of alkyl radicals, which are then released into the gas phase [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330].
   - Oxygen activation occurs on the catalyst surface, forming selective oxidizing species [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330].

2. Gas-Phase Reactions:
   - Alkyl radicals formed on the surface propagate and react in the gas phase [10.1002/anie.202003695].
   - Gas-phase reactions involve alkyl hydroperoxides and other radical species, contributing to olefin formation [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330].
   - Direct experimental evidence of gas-phase methyl radicals has been observed using SVUV-PIMS [10.1002/anie.202002440].

3. Synergistic Effects:
   - The combination of surface-mediated radical formation and gas-phase propagation explains the remarkable product distribution [10.1002/anie.202003695].
   - Surface reaction rates influence gas-phase free radical processes, and vice versa [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].
   - The effective coupling of surface and gas-phase reactions plays a crucial role in achieving high activity and selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].

4. Reaction Pathways:
   - Propene is mainly formed on the catalyst surface through C-H activation of propane [10.1002/anie.202002440].
   - C1 and C2 products can be formed via both surface-mediated and gas-phase pathways [10.1002/anie.202002440].
   - The reaction occurs not only on 1- and 2-D surface-active sites but also in the 3-D space of the gas phase [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].

5. Catalyst Structure and Performance:
   - The one-dimensional nature of edge sites in BN-based catalysts contributes to high olefin selectivity by stabilizing propyl radicals [10.1126/science.aaf7885].
   - Oxygen-terminated armchair edges (>BOON<) are identified as likely active sites for the ODHP reaction [10.1126/science.aaf7885].
   - The formation and distribution of BOH and BO sites on the catalyst surface are crucial for performance [10.1002/cctc.201700725].

Implications for catalyst performance and design optimization:

1. Selective Site Engineering: Design catalysts with optimized distribution of BOx and BOH sites to balance surface activation and gas-phase reactions [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014].

2. Edge Site Optimization: Focus on creating and exposing more edge sites in BN-based catalysts to enhance selectivity [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

3. Radical Management: Develop strategies to control the formation and propagation of radicals in both surface and gas phases [10.1002/anie.202003695].

4. Oxygen Activation Control: Optimize oxygen activation on the catalyst surface to maintain high selectivity while avoiding over-oxidation [10.1002/cctc.201700725].

5. Reactor Design: Consider both surface catalysis and gas-phase reactions when designing reactors, optimizing factors such as temperature, pressure, and residence time [10.1002/anie.202002440].

6. Kinetic Modeling: Develop comprehensive kinetic models that incorporate both surface and gas-phase reactions for more accurate performance prediction [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].

7. In-situ Characterization: Employ advanced in-situ spectroscopic techniques to better understand the dynamic interplay between surface and gas-phase species during the reaction [10.1002/anie.202002440].

B基催化剂上ODHP反应机理的多维性质，包括表面和气相自由基反应，显著增强了我们对整体反应网络的理解。这种硼基表面上的多相催化作用与均相气相化学之间的复杂相互作用，有助于解释这些催化剂在活性和选择性方面的卓越性能。

多维机理的关键方面包括：

1. 表面反应：
   - 催化剂表面的BOx和BOH位点作为选择性C-H键活化的活性位点 [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014]。
   - 这些位点促进烷基自由基的形成，然后释放到气相中 [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。
   - 氧气在催化剂表面活化，形成选择性氧化物种 [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。

2. 气相反应：
   - 在表面形成的烷基自由基在气相中传播和反应 [10.1002/anie.202003695]。
   - 气相反应涉及烷基过氧化物和其他自由基物种，有助于烯烃的形成 [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。
   - 使用SVUV-PIMS直接观察到气相甲基自由基的实验证据 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。

3. 协同效应：
   - 表面介导的自由基形成和气相传播的组合解释了显著的产物分布 [10.1002/anie.202003695]。
   - 表面反应速率影响气相自由基过程，反之亦然 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4]。
   - 表面和气相反应的有效耦合在实现高活性和选择性方面起着关键作用 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4]。

4. 反应途径：
   - 丙烯主要通过丙烷的C-H活化在催化剂表面形成 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。
   - C1和C2产物可以通过表面介导和气相途径形成 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。
   - 反应不仅发生在1-和2-D表面活性位点上，还发生在气相的3-D空间中 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4]。

5. 催化剂结构和性能：
   - BN基催化剂中边缘位点的一维性质通过稳定丙基自由基有助于高烯烃选择性 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。
   - 氧终止的扶手椅边缘（>BOON<）被确定为ODHP反应的可能活性位点 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。
   - 催化剂表面BOH和BO位点的形成和分布对性能至关重要 [10.1002/cctc.201700725]。

对催化剂性能和设计优化的启示：

1. 选择性位点工程：设计具有优化分布的BOx和BOH位点的催化剂，以平衡表面活化和气相反应 [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014]。

2. 边缘位点优化：专注于在BN基催化剂中创建和暴露更多边缘位点，以提高选择性 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

3. 自由基管理：开发策略来控制表面和气相中自由基的形成和传播 [10.1002/anie.202003695]。

4. 氧气活化控制：优化催化剂表面的氧气活化，以维持高选择性同时避免过度氧化 [10.1002/cctc.201700725]。

5. 反应器设计：在设计反应器时考虑表面催化和气相反应，优化温度、压力和停留时间等因素 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。

6. 动力学建模：开发包含表面和气相反应的综合动力学模型，以更准确地预测性能 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4]。

7. 原位表征：采用先进的原位光谱技术，更好地理解反应过程中表面和气相物种之间的动态相互作用 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。

综合理解与未来展望：

B基催化剂上ODHP反应机理的多维性质为催化剂设计和过程优化开辟了新的途径。未来的研究方向可能包括：
