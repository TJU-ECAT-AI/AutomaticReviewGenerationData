The multidimensional nature of the ODHP reaction mechanism over B-based catalysts, encompassing both surface and gas-phase radical reactions, significantly contributes to our understanding of the overall reaction network. This complex interplay between heterogeneous catalysis on the boron-based surface and homogeneous gas-phase chemistry provides a more comprehensive framework for explaining catalyst performance and guiding optimization efforts.

Key aspects of this multidimensional mechanism include:

1. Surface-mediated reactions:
   - Formation of BOH and BO active sites at the edges of BN, promoted by the presence of alkanes and oxygen [10.1002/cctc.201700725].
   - Selective breaking of C-H bonds while preventing overoxidation to CO2 [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014].
   - Oxygen activation initiated by C-H activation, not by direct oxidation of BN [10.1002/cctc.201700725].

2. Gas-phase reactions:
   - Propagation of radicals formed on the catalyst surface [10.1002/anie.202003695].
   - Formation of alkyl hydroperoxides and other radical species [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330].
   - Direct experimental evidence of gas-phase methyl radicals detected using SVUV-PIMS [10.1002/anie.202002440].

3. Synergistic effects:
   - The interplay between surface and gas-phase reactions contributes to high activity and selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].
   - In-situ formed olefins play a crucial role in promoting alkane conversion and C-C bond cracking [10.1038/s41467-022-35776-3].
   - Oxygen exchange between surface and gas-phase species influences catalyst stability and performance [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014].

4. Reaction pathways:
   - Low-temperature pathway: Direct dehydrogenation via BOH sites [10.1002/cctc.201700725].
   - High-temperature pathway: Hydrogen abstraction by BO sites [10.1002/cctc.201700725].
   - Formation of C1 and C2 products through both surface-mediated and gas-phase pathways [10.1002/anie.202002440].

Implications for catalyst performance and design optimization:

1. Active site engineering: Focus on creating and optimizing BOH and BO sites at catalyst edges [10.1002/cctc.201700725].
2. Selectivity control: Exploit the one-dimensional nature of edge sites to stabilize intermediates and prevent overoxidation [10.1126/science.aaf7885].
3. Temperature management: Control reaction pathways by manipulating temperature [10.1002/cctc.201700725].
4. Oxygen management: Balance oxygen concentration to favor dehydrogenation over C-C bond cleavage [10.1002/anie.202003695].
5. Catalyst configuration: Consider the influence on free radical transport and reactions [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].
6. Holistic design approach: Integrate surface properties and gas-phase chemistry considerations [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031].
7. Advanced characterization: Utilize techniques like SVUV-PIMS for in-situ analysis of gas-phase species [10.1002/anie.202002440].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The multidimensional nature of the ODHP reaction mechanism over B-based catalysts reveals a complex interplay between surface and gas-phase chemistry that opens up new avenues for catalyst design and process optimization. This comprehensive understanding challenges the traditional view of heterogeneous catalysis and highlights the importance of considering both surface and gas-phase reactions in catalyst development.

Future research directions may include:

1. Development of hierarchical catalyst structures that optimize both surface active sites and gas-phase reaction spaces.
2. Exploration of novel in-situ characterization techniques to simultaneously probe surface and gas-phase species under reaction conditions.
3. Integration of machine learning approaches to predict and optimize the synergistic effects of surface and gas-phase reactions.
4. Investigation of the potential for tailoring catalyst bed configurations to enhance the coupling between surface and gas-phase chemistry.
5. Examination of the broader applicability of this multidimensional mechanism to other catalytic systems, particularly those involving redox-poor oxides.

By embracing the complexity of the ODHP reaction mechanism, researchers can potentially develop more efficient and selective catalysts, not only for propane dehydrogenation but also for a wider range of alkane conversion processes. This holistic approach to catalyst design and reaction engineering may lead to significant advancements in the field of heterogeneous catalysis and contribute to the development of more sustainable chemical processes.

<Chinese>
B基催化剂上ODHP反应机理的多维性质，包括表面和气相自由基反应，显著增进了我们对整体反应网络的理解。硼基表面上的非均相催化作用与均相气相化学之间的这种复杂相互作用为解释催化剂性能和指导优化工作提供了更全面的框架。

这种多维机制的关键方面包括：

1. 表面介导反应：
   - 在BN边缘形成BOH和BO活性位点，由烷烃和氧气的存在促进[10.1002/cctc.201700725]。
   - 选择性断裂C-H键，同时防止过度氧化为CO2 [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014]。
   - 氧气活化由C-H活化引发，而不是通过BN的直接氧化[10.1002/cctc.201700725]。

2. 气相反应：
   - 在催化剂表面形成的自由基的传播[10.1002/anie.202003695]。
   - 烷基氢过氧化物和其他自由基物种的形成[10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。
   - 使用SVUV-PIMS检测到气相甲基自由基的直接实验证据[10.1002/anie.202002440]。

3. 协同效应：
   - 表面和气相反应之间的相互作用有助于高活性和选择性[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4]。
   - 原位形成的烯烃在促进烷烃转化和C-C键断裂中起着关键作用[10.1038/s41467-022-35776-3]。
   - 表面和气相物种之间的氧交换影响催化剂的稳定性和性能[10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014]。

4. 反应路径：
   - 低温路径：通过BOH位点的直接脱氢[10.1002/cctc.201700725]。
   - 高温路径：通过BO位点的氢抽取[10.1002/cctc.201700725]。
   - C1和C2产物通过表面介导和气相路径形成[10.1002/anie.202002440]。

对催化剂性能和设计优化的影响：

1. 活性位点工程：专注于在催化剂边缘创建和优化BOH和BO位点[10.1002/cctc.201700725]。
2. 选择性控制：利用边缘位点的一维性质来稳定中间体并防止过度氧化[10.1126/science.aaf7885]。
3. 温度管理：通过操纵温度来控制反应路径[10.1002/cctc.201700725]。
4. 氧气管理：平衡氧气浓度以有利于脱氢而不是C-C键断裂[10.1002/anie.202003695]。
5. 催化剂构型：考虑对自由基传输和反应的影响[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4]。
6. 整体设计方法：整合表面性质和气相化学考虑因素[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031]。
7. 先进表征：利用SVUV-PIMS等技术进行气相物种的原位分析[10.1002/anie.202002440]。

综合理解与未来展望：

B基催化剂上ODHP反应机理的多维性质揭示了表面和气相化学之间复杂的相互作用，为催化剂设计和过程优化开辟了新的途径。这种全面的理解挑战了传统的非均相催化观点，突出了在催化剂开发中同时考虑表面和气相反应的重要性。

未来研究方向可能包括：

1. 开发层次结构催化剂结构，同时优化表面活性位点和气相反应空间。
2. 探索新型原位表征技术，在反应条件下同时探测表面和气相物种。
3. 整合机器学习方法，预测和优化表面和气相反应的协同效应。
4. 研究定制催化剂床构型以增强表面和气相化学之间耦合的潜力。
5. 研究这种多维机制在其他催化系统中的更广泛适用性，特别是涉及氧化还原能力较弱的氧化物的系统。

通过接受ODHP反应机理的复杂性，研究人员可能开发出更高效、更选择性的催化剂，不仅适用于丙烷脱氢，还适用于更广泛的烷烃转化过程。这种对催化剂设计和反应工程的整体方法可能导致非均相催化领域的重大进展，并有助于开发更可持续的化学过程。
