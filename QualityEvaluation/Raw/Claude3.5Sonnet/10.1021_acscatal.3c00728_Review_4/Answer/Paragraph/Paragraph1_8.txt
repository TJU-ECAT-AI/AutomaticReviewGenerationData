<English>
The multidimensional nature of the ODHP reaction mechanism over B-based catalysts, encompassing both surface and gas-phase radical reactions, significantly contributes to our understanding of the overall reaction network. This complex interplay between heterogeneous catalysis on the boron-based surface and homogeneous gas-phase chemistry reveals several key aspects:

1. Surface Reactions:
   - BOH groups on the catalyst surface act as active sites, selectively breaking C-H bonds while preventing overoxidation to CO2 [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014].
   - Oxygen-terminated armchair edges of boron nitride (>BOON<) are identified as likely active sites for the ODHP reaction [10.1126/science.aaf7885].
   - Surface BOx species suppress dissociative adsorption of reactants, contributing to high olefin selectivity [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330].

2. Gas-Phase Reactions:
   - Propyl radicals formed on the catalyst surface continue to react in the gas phase via alkyl hydroperoxides [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330].
   - Direct experimental evidence of gas-phase methyl radicals (CH3•) has been observed using SVUV-PIMS [10.1002/anie.202002440].
   - The formation of ethylene and methyl radicals can occur competitively over either the surface or the gas-phase with similar barriers [10.1002/anie.202002440].

3. Synergistic Effects:
   - The combination of surface-mediated formation of radicals and their sequential propagation in the gas phase explains the remarkable product distribution [10.1002/anie.202003695].
   - The effective coupling of surface and gas-phase reactions plays a crucial role in achieving high activity and high propene selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].
   - In-situ formed olefins, previously thought to be inert, play a crucial role in promoting alkane conversion and C-C bond cracking [10.1038/s41467-022-35776-3].

4. Reaction Pathways:
   - Propene is mainly generated on the catalyst surface from the C-H activation of propane, while C2 and C1 products can be formed via both surface-mediated and gas-phase pathways [10.1002/anie.202002440].
   - Two possible catalytic pathways exist, depending on temperature and conversion rates: a low-temperature pathway (<10% conversion) and a high-temperature pathway (>10% conversion) [10.1002/cctc.201700725].

5. Kinetics and Mechanism:
   - The reaction exhibits a second-order rate dependence with respect to propane concentration, suggesting that two propane molecules are required to generate two water molecules [10.1126/science.aaf7885].
   - Oxygen concentration controls the balance between dehydrogenation and C-C bond cleavage [10.1002/anie.202003695].

Implications for catalyst performance and design optimization:

1. Catalyst Design:
   - Focus on optimizing the distribution and nature of BOH and BO sites [10.1002/cctc.201700725].
   - Develop catalysts with metastable surface sites that favor specific radical formation (e.g., i-propyl radicals) [10.1002/anie.202003695].
   - Design catalysts that generate reactive H-abstracting species favoring secondary C-H bond abstraction in propane [10.1002/anie.202003695].

2. Selectivity Control:
   - Manipulate catalyst properties and reaction conditions to favor desired pathways and suppress unwanted side reactions [10.1002/anie.202002440].
   - Optimize the synergy between surface and gas-phase reactions to improve activity and selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].

3. Process Optimization:
   - Consider both surface properties and gas-phase chemistry when optimizing B-based catalysts for ODHP reactions [10.1038/s41467-022-35776-3].
   - Fine-tune factors such as temperature, pressure, and residence time to favor desired pathways [10.1002/anie.202002440].

4. Reactor Design:
   - Account for both surface catalysis and gas-phase reactions when determining reactor dimensions and catalyst bed configurations [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].

5. Modeling and Prediction:
   - Develop more complex models incorporating both surface and gas-phase kinetics for accurate performance prediction [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The multidimensional nature of the ODHP reaction mechanism over B-based catalysts reveals a complex interplay between surface and gas-phase reactions that offers new opportunities for catalyst design and process optimization. This comprehensive understanding opens up several avenues for future research and development:

1. Advanced in-situ characterization: Developing new techniques for real-time observation of both surface and gas-phase species during the reaction could provide deeper insights into the reaction mechanism and guide catalyst optimization.

2. Computational modeling: Integrating surface science with gas-phase kinetics in computational models could lead to more accurate predictions of catalyst performance and guide the design of novel catalytic materials.

3. Tailored catalyst architectures: Exploring hierarchical structures or composite materials that can optimize both surface reactions and gas-phase radical chemistry may lead to breakthroughs in catalyst performance.

4. Process intensification: Investigating novel reactor designs that can effectively manage the balance between surface and gas-phase reactions could lead to more efficient ODHP processes.

5. Broader applications: The insights gained from B-based ODHP catalysts could be extended to other oxidative dehydrogenation reactions or even to different classes of catalytic processes where surface-gas phase interactions play a crucial role.

6. Sustainable catalyst design: Leveraging the understanding of the multidimensional mechanism to develop catalysts with improved stability, longer lifetimes, and potentially using more abundant and environmentally friendly materials.

7. Machine learning approaches: Utilizing big data and machine learning algorithms to analyze the complex relationships between catalyst properties, reaction conditions, and performance metrics could accelerate the discovery and optimization of new catalytic systems.

These future directions highlight the potential for significant advancements in the field of oxidative dehydrogenation catalysis, driven by the deeper understanding of the multidimensional nature of the reaction mechanism in B-based catalysts.

<Chinese>
B基催化剂上ODHP反应机理的多维性，涵盖了表面和气相自由基反应，显著提高了我们对整体反应网络的理解。这种硼基表面上的多相催化和均相气相化学之间的复杂相互作用揭示了几个关键方面：

1. 表面反应：
   - 催化剂表面的BOH基团作为活性位点，选择性地断裂C-H键，同时防止过度氧化为CO2 [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014]。
   - 硼氮化合物的含氧扶手椅边缘（>BOON<）被确定为ODHP反应的可能活性位点 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。
   - 表面BOx物种抑制反应物的解离吸附，有助于提高烯烃选择性 [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。

2. 气相反应：
   - 在催化剂表面形成的丙基自由基通过烷基过氧化物继续在气相中反应 [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。
   - 使用SVUV-PIMS直接观察到气相甲基自由基（CH3•）的实验证据 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。
   - 乙烯和甲基自由基的形成可以在表面或气相中以相似的能垒竞争性地发生 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。

3. 协同效应：
   - 表面介导的自由基形成与其在气相中的连续传播相结合，解释了显著的产物分布 [10.1002/anie.202003695]。
   - 表面和气相反应的有效耦合在实现高活性和高丙烯选择性方面起着关键作用 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4]。
   - 原本被认为是惰性的原位形成的烯烃，在促进烷烃转化和C-C键裂解方面起着关键作用 [10.1038/s41467-022-35776-3]。

4. 反应途径：
   - 丙烯主要通过丙烷的C-H活化在催化剂表面生成，而C2和C1产物可以通过表面介导和气相途径形成 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。
   - 存在两种可能的催化途径，取决于温度和转化率：低温途径（<10%转化率）和高温途径（>10%转化率）[10.1002/cctc.201700725]。

5. 动力学和机理：
   - 反应对丙烷浓度表现出二阶速率依赖性，表明需要两个丙烷分子生成两个水分子 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。
   - 氧气浓度控制脱氢和C-C键裂解之间的平衡 [10.1002/anie.202003695]。

对催化剂性能和设计优化的影响：

1. 催化剂设计：
   - 专注于优化BOH和BO位点的分布和性质 [10.1002/cctc.201700725]。
   - 开发具有亚稳态表面位点的催化剂，有利于特定自由基的形成（如异丙基自由基）[10.1002/anie.202003695]。
   - 设计能生成有利于丙烷中次级C-H键断裂的反应性H-提取物种的催化剂 [10.1002/anie.202003695]。

2. 选择性控制：
   - 调节催化剂性质和反应条件，以促进所需途径并抑制不必要的副反应 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。
   - 优化表面和气相反应之间的协同作用，以提高活性和选择性 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4]。

3. 过程优化：
   - 在优化B基ODHP催化剂时，同时考虑表面性质和气相化学 [10.1038/s41467-022-35776-3]。
   - 微调温度、压力和停留时间等因素，以促进所需途径 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。

4. 反应器设计：
   - 在确定反应器尺寸和催化剂床构型时，考虑表面催化和气相反应 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4]。

5. 建模和预测：
   - 开发更复杂的模型，包含表面和气相动力学，以准确预测性能 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4]。

综合理解与未来展望：

B基催化剂上ODHP反应机理的多维性揭示了表面和气相反应之间的复杂相互作用，为催化剂设计和过程优化提供了新的机会。这种全面的理解为未来的研究和发展开辟了几个途径：

1. 先进的原位表征：开发新技术，实时观察反应过程中的表面和气相物种，可以提供对反应机理更深入的见解，并指导催化剂优化。

2. 计算模拟：将表面科学与气相动力学整合到计算模型中，可以更准确地预测催化剂性能，并指导新型催化材料的设计。

3. 定制催化剂结构：探索可以同时优化表面反应和气相自由基化学的分层结构或复合材料，可能会带来催化剂性能的突破。

4. 过程强化：研究能够有效管理表面和气相反应平衡的新型反应器设计，可能导致更高效的ODHP过程。

5. 更广泛的应用：从B基ODHP催化剂获得的见解可以扩展到其他氧化脱氢反应，甚至扩展到表面-气相相互作用起关键作用的不同类别的催化过程。

6. 可持续催化剂设计：利用对多维机理的理解，开发稳定性更高、寿命更长，并可能使用更丰富和环境友好材料的催化剂。

7. 机器学习方法：利用大数据和机器学习算法分析催化剂性质、反应条件和性能指标之间的复杂关系，可以加速新催化系统的发现和优化。

这些未来方向凸显了在氧化脱氢催化领域取得重大进展的潜力，这些进展源于对B基催化剂反应机理多维性的深入理解。
