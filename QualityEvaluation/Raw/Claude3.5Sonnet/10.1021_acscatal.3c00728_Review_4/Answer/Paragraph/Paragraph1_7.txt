<English>
The multidimensional nature of the ODHP reaction mechanism over B-based catalysts, encompassing both surface and gas-phase radical reactions, significantly contributes to our understanding of the overall reaction network. This complex interplay between heterogeneous catalysis on the boron-based surface and homogeneous gas-phase chemistry provides a comprehensive framework for explaining the exceptional performance of these catalysts.

Key aspects of this multidimensional mechanism include:

1. Surface-mediated reactions:
   - Formation of BOH active sites at the edges of BN, promoted by alkanes [10.1002/cctc.201700725].
   - Selective C-H bond activation by BOH sites while preventing overoxidation to CO2 [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014].
   - Formation of BOOB intermediates through the reaction of BOH sites with molecular oxygen [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014].
   - Activation of oxygen to form selective oxidizing species [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330].

2. Gas-phase reactions:
   - Propagation of radicals formed on the catalyst surface [10.1002/anie.202003695].
   - Formation of alkyl hydroperoxides and other radical species [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330].
   - Direct experimental evidence of gas-phase methyl radicals (CH3·) detected using SVUV-PIMS [10.1002/anie.202002440].

3. Synergistic effects:
   - The combination of surface and gas-phase processes leads to exceptional product distribution and improved catalytic performance [10.1038/s41467-022-35776-3].
   - Surface reaction rates influence gas-phase free radical processes, and vice versa [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].
   - Oxygen exchange between surface oxygen atoms in BOH groups and gas-phase molecular oxygen [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014].

4. Reaction pathways:
   - Propene is mainly formed on the catalyst surface through C-H activation of propane [10.1002/anie.202002440].
   - C1 and C2 products can be formed via both surface-mediated and gas-phase pathways [10.1002/anie.202002440].
   - The formation of ethylene and methyl radicals can occur competitively over either the surface or the gas-phase [10.1002/anie.202002440].

5. Active site structure and selectivity:
   - The oxygen-terminated armchair edges of boron nitride (>BOON<) are identified as likely active sites [10.1126/science.aaf7885].
   - The one-dimensional nature of edge sites plays a crucial role in achieving high olefin selectivity by stabilizing propyl radicals [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

The implications of this multidimensional understanding for catalyst performance and design optimization are significant:

1. Catalyst design: Development of catalysts with optimized surface properties and metastable sites that favor specific radical formation [10.1002/anie.202003695].
2. Selectivity control: Manipulation of catalyst properties and reaction conditions to favor desired pathways and suppress unwanted side reactions [10.1002/anie.202002440].
3. Process optimization: Fine-tuning of reaction parameters such as temperature, pressure, and residence time to balance surface and gas-phase reactions [10.1002/anie.202002440].
4. Reactor design: Consideration of both surface catalysis and gas-phase reactions in determining reactor dimensions and catalyst bed configurations [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].
5. Predictive modeling: Development of more accurate models incorporating both surface and gas-phase kinetics for performance prediction [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The multidimensional nature of the ODHP reaction mechanism over B-based catalysts opens up new avenues for catalyst design and process optimization. Future research directions may include:

1. Advanced in-situ characterization: Development of techniques to simultaneously probe surface and gas-phase species during reaction conditions, providing real-time insights into the dynamic interplay between these two domains.

2. Computational modeling: Integration of surface chemistry and gas-phase kinetics in a unified model to predict catalyst performance and guide rational design of new materials.

3. Nano-engineered catalysts: Design of catalysts with precisely controlled surface structures and pore architectures to optimize the balance between surface-mediated and gas-phase reactions.

4. Synergistic catalysts: Exploration of multi-component catalysts that can simultaneously promote surface activation and gas-phase radical chemistry, potentially leading to enhanced performance.

5. Process intensification: Development of novel reactor designs that can exploit the multidimensional nature of the reaction, such as membrane reactors or structured catalysts with tailored flow patterns.

6. Broader applications: Extension of the multidimensional mechanism concept to other reactions beyond ODHP, potentially leading to breakthroughs in various catalytic processes.

These future directions hold the promise of developing more efficient and selective catalytic systems for alkane conversion, contributing to the broader goal of sustainable chemical production.

<Chinese>
B基催化剂上ODHP反应机理的多维性，包括表面和气相自由基反应，显著提高了我们对整体反应网络的理解。这种硼基表面上的多相催化作用与均相气相化学之间复杂的相互作用为解释这些催化剂的卓越性能提供了全面的框架。

这种多维机理的关键方面包括：

1. 表面介导反应：
   - 在BN边缘形成BOH活性位点，由烷烃促进[10.1002/cctc.201700725]。
   - BOH位点选择性活化C-H键，同时防止过度氧化为CO2 [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014]。
   - 通过BOH位点与分子氧的反应形成BOOB中间体[10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014]。
   - 活化氧以形成选择性氧化物种[10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。

2. 气相反应：
   - 在催化剂表面形成的自由基在气相中传播[10.1002/anie.202003695]。
   - 形成烷基过氧化物和其他自由基物种[10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。
   - 使用SVUV-PIMS检测到气相甲基自由基（CH3·）的直接实验证据[10.1002/anie.202002440]。

3. 协同效应：
   - 表面和气相过程的结合导致了卓越的产品分布和改善的催化性能[10.1038/s41467-022-35776-3]。
   - 表面反应速率影响气相自由基过程，反之亦然[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4]。
   - BOH基团中的表面氧原子与气相分子氧之间的氧交换[10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014]。

4. 反应途径：
   - 丙烯主要通过丙烷的C-H活化在催化剂表面形成[10.1002/anie.202002440]。
   - C1和C2产物可以通过表面介导和气相途径形成[10.1002/anie.202002440]。
   - 乙烯和甲基自由基的形成可以在表面或气相中竞争性地发生[10.1002/anie.202002440]。

5. 活性位点结构和选择性：
   - 氧化物终止的硼氮化物扶手边缘（>BOON<）被确定为可能的活性位点[10.1126/science.aaf7885]。
   - 边缘位点的一维性质通过稳定丙基自由基在实现高烯烃选择性方面起着至关重要的作用[10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

这种多维理解对催化剂性能和设计优化的影响是显著的：

1. 催化剂设计：开发具有优化表面性质和有利于特定自由基形成的亚稳态位点的催化剂[10.1002/anie.202003695]。
2. 选择性控制：操纵催化剂性质和反应条件以有利于所需途径并抑制不需要的副反应[10.1002/anie.202002440]。
3. 过程优化：微调温度、压力和停留时间等反应参数，以平衡表面和气相反应[10.1002/anie.202002440]。
4. 反应器设计：在确定反应器尺寸和催化剂床构型时考虑表面催化和气相反应[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4]。
5. 预测建模：开发包含表面和气相动力学的更准确模型，用于性能预测[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4]。

综合理解与未来展望：

B基催化剂上ODHP反应机理的多维性为催化剂设计和过程优化开辟了新的途径。未来研究方向可能包括：

1. 先进的原位表征：开发同时探测反应条件下表面和气相物种的技术，提供这两个领域动态相互作用的实时洞察。

2. 计算模拟：在统一模型中集成表面化学和气相动力学，以预测催化剂性能并指导新材料的理性设计。

3. 纳米工程催化剂：设计具有精确控制的表面结构和孔隙结构的催化剂，以优化表面介导反应和气相反应之间的平衡。

4. 协同催化剂：探索能够同时促进表面活化和气相自由基化学的多组分催化剂，可能导致性能增强。

5. 过程强化：开发能够利用反应多维性质的新型反应器设计，如膜反应器或具有定制流型的结构化催化剂。

6. 更广泛的应用：将多维机理概念扩展到ODHP以外的其他反应，可能在各种催化过程中带来突破。

这些未来方向有望开发出更高效、更选择性的烷烃转化催化系统，为可持续化学品生产的更广泛目标做出贡献。
