The multidimensional nature of the ODHP reaction mechanism over B-based catalysts, encompassing both surface and gas-phase radical reactions, significantly contributes to our understanding of the overall reaction network. This complex interplay between heterogeneous catalysis on the boron-based surface and homogeneous gas-phase chemistry has profound implications for catalyst performance and design optimization.

Surface reactions involve the activation of oxygen to form selective oxidizing species, which then interact with alkanes. The formation of BOH and BO active sites at the edges of BN plays a crucial role in the reaction mechanism [10.1002/cctc.201700725]. These sites facilitate the direct dehydrogenation of alkanes with adsorbed O2, producing olefins and H2 without combustion products at low temperatures [10.1002/cctc.201700725]. The BOx species on the catalyst surface suppress dissociative adsorption of reactants, contributing to high olefin selectivity [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330].

The gas-phase component of the reaction involves the continuation of the dehydrogenation process through alkyl hydroperoxides and other radical species [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]. This step is critical for completing the formation of olefins and may contribute to the high selectivity observed in these systems. The direct detection of gas-phase methyl radicals using SVUV-PIMS provides crucial evidence for the existence of gas-phase radical chemistry in this system [10.1002/anie.202002440].

The synergistic effects of surface and gas-phase reactions have significant implications for catalyst performance and design optimization:

1. Selectivity control: The interplay between surface and gas-phase reactions allows for better control of product selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].

2. Reaction pathway manipulation: Understanding the multidimensional nature of the reaction allows for potential manipulation of reaction pathways [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].

3. Catalyst design: Recognition of both surface and gas-phase components provides new avenues for catalyst design, focusing on optimizing the distribution and nature of BOH and BO sites [10.1002/cctc.201700725].

4. Process optimization: A holistic approach to process optimization is required, considering factors such as reactor design, gas flow dynamics, and temperature profiles [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330].

5. Mechanistic insights: The multidimensional nature provides a more complete picture of the ODHP process, allowing for better predictive models and mechanistic understanding [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330].

The one-dimensional nature of the edge sites in B-based catalysts plays a crucial role in achieving high olefin selectivity by stabilizing propyl radicals and preventing overoxidation [10.1126/science.aaf7885]. The desorption of water molecules creates BN edge vacancies, allowing for unique O2 activation, which explains the influence of adsorbed oxygen surface coverage on the rate of propane consumption [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

B基催化剂上ODHP反应机理的多维性，包括表面和气相自由基反应，极大地提高了我们对整个反应网络的理解。硼基表面上的非均相催化作用与均相气相化学之间的这种复杂相互作用对催化剂性能和设计优化具有深远的影响。

表面反应涉及氧的活化，形成选择性氧化物种，然后与烷烃相互作用。BN边缘形成的BOH和BO活性位在反应机理中起着关键作用[10.1002/cctc.201700725]。这些位点在低温下促进烷烃与吸附O2的直接脱氢，生成烯烃和H2，而不产生燃烧产物[10.1002/cctc.201700725]。催化剂表面上的BOx物种抑制了反应物的解离吸附，有助于获得高烯烃选择性[10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。

反应的气相部分涉及通过烷基氢过氧化物和其他自由基物种继续进行脱氢过程[10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。这一步对完成烯烃的形成至关重要，可能会导致这些体系中观察到的高选择性。使用SVUV-PIMS直接检测气相甲基自由基为该系统中存在气相自由基化学反应提供了关键证据[10.1002/anie.202002440]。

表面和气相反应的协同效应对催化剂性能和设计优化具有重要影响：

1. 选择性控制：表面和气相反应之间的相互作用允许更好地控制产物选择性[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4]。

2. 反应途径操纵：了解反应的多维性质使得可能操纵反应途径[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4]。

3. 催化剂设计：认识到表面和气相组分提供了催化剂设计的新途径，重点是优化BOH和BO位点的分布和性质[10.1002/cctc.201700725]。

4. 过程优化：需要采用整体方法进行过程优化，考虑反应器设计、气体流动动力学和温度分布等因素[10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。

5. 机理洞察：多维性提供了ODHP过程更完整的图景，允许更好的预测模型和机理理解[10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。

B基催化剂中边缘位点的一维性质在实现高烯烃选择性方面起着关键作用，通过稳定丙基自由基和防止过度氧化[10.1126/science.aaf7885]。水分子的脱附创造了BN边缘空位，允许独特的O2活化，这解释了吸附氧表面覆盖率对丙烷消耗速率的影响[10.1126/science.aaf7885]。
