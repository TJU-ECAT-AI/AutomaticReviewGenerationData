<English>
The multidimensional nature of the ODHP reaction mechanism over B-based catalysts, encompassing both surface and gas-phase radical reactions, significantly contributes to our understanding of the overall reaction network. This complex interplay between heterogeneous catalysis on the boron-based surface and homogeneous gas-phase chemistry has several key aspects:

1. Surface Reactions:
   - BOH groups act as active sites, selectively breaking C-H bonds while preventing overoxidation to CO2 [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014].
   - Oxygen-terminated armchair edges of boron nitride (>BOON<) are identified as likely active sites for the ODHP reaction [10.1126/science.aaf7885].
   - Surface BOx species suppress dissociative adsorption of reactants, contributing to high olefin selectivity [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330].

2. Gas-Phase Reactions:
   - Propyl radicals formed on the catalyst surface continue to react in the gas phase via alkyl hydroperoxides [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330].
   - Direct experimental evidence of gas-phase methyl radicals (CH3·) in the ODHP reaction has been obtained using SVUV-PIMS [10.1002/anie.202002440].
   - The formation of ethylene and methyl radicals can occur competitively over either the surface or the gas phase [10.1002/anie.202002440].

3. Synergistic Effects:
   - The combination of surface-mediated formation of radicals and their sequential propagation in the gas phase explains the remarkable product distribution [10.1002/anie.202003695].
   - The effective coupling of surface and gas-phase reactions plays a crucial role in achieving high activity and high propene selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].
   - In-situ formed olefins, previously thought to be inert, play a crucial role in promoting alkane conversion and C-C bond cracking [10.1038/s41467-022-35776-3].

4. Reaction Pathways:
   - Propene is mainly formed from surface reactions via the cleavage of C-H bonds of propane, while C1 and C2 products can be formed via both surface-mediated and gas-phase pathways [10.1002/anie.202002440].
   - The reaction mechanism involves a radical rebound process, differing from traditional supported metal catalysts [10.1126/science.aaf7885].
   - The oxygen concentration controls the balance between dehydrogenation and C-C bond cleavage [10.1002/anie.202003695].

Implications for catalyst performance and design optimization:

1. Selectivity Control: The interplay between surface and gas-phase reactions allows for better control of product selectivity by manipulating catalyst properties and reaction conditions [10.1002/anie.202002440].

2. Catalyst Design: Development of catalysts with metastable surface sites that favor specific radical formation (e.g., i-propyl radicals) could enhance ODH performance [10.1002/anie.202003695].

3. Process Optimization: Understanding the multidimensional nature of the reaction allows for optimization of reaction conditions, reactor design, and catalyst bed configurations [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].

4. Performance Prediction: The comprehensive reaction network, including both surface and gas-phase pathways, provides a foundation for developing more accurate predictive models of catalyst performance [10.1002/anie.202002440].

5. Holistic Approach: Both surface properties and gas-phase chemistry must be considered when optimizing B-based catalysts for ODHP reactions [10.1038/s41467-022-35776-3].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The multidimensional nature of the ODHP reaction mechanism over B-based catalysts opens up new avenues for catalyst design and process optimization. Future research directions may include:

1. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques: Developing new methods to simultaneously monitor surface and gas-phase species during the reaction could provide deeper insights into the reaction mechanism and guide catalyst optimization.

2. Computational modeling: Integrating surface and gas-phase reactions in computational models could lead to more accurate predictions of catalyst performance and guide the design of novel catalysts.

3. Tailored catalyst architectures: Exploring hierarchical structures or composite materials that can optimize both surface reactions and gas-phase radical chemistry may lead to breakthroughs in catalyst performance.

4. Reactor design innovations: Developing novel reactor configurations that can effectively manage the interplay between surface and gas-phase reactions could enhance overall process efficiency.

5. Broader applications: Investigating the applicability of this multidimensional understanding to other catalytic systems, particularly those involving redox-poor oxides, could lead to advancements in various chemical processes beyond ODHP.

By addressing these areas, researchers can potentially develop more efficient and selective catalytic systems for alkane conversion, contributing to the broader field of sustainable chemical production.

<Chinese>
在B基催化剂上，ODHP反应机理的多维性质涵盖了表面反应和气相自由基反应，这显著增进了我们对整体反应网络的理解。这种硼基表面上的多相催化作用和均相气相化学之间的复杂相互作用具有几个关键方面：

1. 表面反应：
   - BOH基团作为活性位点，选择性地断裂C-H键，同时防止过度氧化成CO2 [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014]。
   - 氮化硼的含氧扶手边缘（>BOON<）被确定为ODHP反应的可能活性位点 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。
   - 表面BOx物种抑制反应物的解离吸附，有助于提高烯烃选择性 [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。

2. 气相反应：
   - 在催化剂表面形成的丙基自由基通过烷基氢过氧化物在气相中继续反应 [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。
   - 使用SVUV-PIMS直接实验证实了ODHP反应中存在气相甲基自由基（CH3·）[10.1002/anie.202002440]。
   - 乙烯和甲基自由基的形成可以在表面或气相中竞争性地发生 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。

3. 协同效应：
   - 表面介导的自由基形成与其在气相中的连续传播相结合，解释了显著的产物分布 [10.1002/anie.202003695]。
   - 表面反应和气相反应的有效耦合在实现高活性和高丙烯选择性中起着至关重要的作用 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4]。
   - 原本被认为是惰性的原位形成的烯烃，在促进烷烃转化和C-C键断裂中起着关键作用 [10.1038/s41467-022-35776-3]。

4. 反应途径：
   - 丙烯主要通过丙烷C-H键的断裂在表面反应中形成，而C1和C2产物可以通过表面介导和气相途径形成 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。
   - 反应机理涉及自由基反弹过程，这与传统的负载金属催化剂不同 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。
   - 氧气浓度控制着脱氢和C-C键断裂之间的平衡 [10.1002/anie.202003695]。

对催化剂性能和设计优化的影响：

1. 选择性控制：表面和气相反应之间的相互作用允许通过操纵催化剂性质和反应条件来更好地控制产品选择性 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。

2. 催化剂设计：开发具有亚稳态表面位点的催化剂，有利于特定自由基的形成（如i-丙基自由基），可以提高ODH性能 [10.1002/anie.202003695]。

3. 过程优化：理解反应的多维性质允许优化反应条件、反应器设计和催化剂床构型 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4]。

4. 性能预测：包括表面和气相途径在内的综合反应网络为开发更准确的催化剂性能预测模型提供了基础 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。

5. 整体方法：在优化用于ODHP反应的B基催化剂时，必须同时考虑表面性质和气相化学 [10.1038/s41467-022-35776-3]。

综合理解与未来展望：

B基催化剂上ODHP反应机理的多维性质为催化剂设计和过程优化开辟了新的途径。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 先进的原位表征技术：开发新的方法同时监测反应过程中的表面和气相物种，可以提供对反应机理更深入的洞察，并指导催化剂优化。

2. 计算模拟：在计算模型中整合表面和气相反应，可以更准确地预测催化剂性能，并指导新型催化剂的设计。

3. 定制催化剂结构：探索可以同时优化表面反应和气相自由基化学的分层结构或复合材料，可能会导致催化剂性能的突破。

4. 反应器设计创新：开发能有效管理表面和气相反应之间相互作用的新型反应器配置，可以提高整体过程效率。

5. 更广泛的应用：研究这种多维理解在其他催化系统中的适用性，特别是涉及氧化还原能力较弱的氧化物的系统，可能会推动ODHP之外的各种化学过程的进展。

通过解决这些领域的问题，研究人员可能开发出更高效、更选择性的烷烃转化催化系统，为更广泛的可持续化学品生产领域做出贡献。
