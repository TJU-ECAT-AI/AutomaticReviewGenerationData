The multidimensional nature of the ODHP reaction mechanism over B-based catalysts, encompassing both surface and gas-phase radical reactions, significantly enhances our understanding of the overall reaction network. This complex interplay between heterogeneous catalysis on the boron-based surface and homogeneous gas-phase chemistry contributes to the exceptional performance of these catalysts in terms of activity and selectivity.

Key aspects of this multidimensional mechanism include:

1. Surface-mediated reactions:
   - BOH and BO sites on the catalyst surface act as active sites for selective C-H bond activation [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014].
   - Oxygen-terminated armchair edges of boron nitride (>BOON<) are identified as likely active sites for ODHP [10.1126/science.aaf7885].
   - Surface BOx species suppress dissociative adsorption of reactants, contributing to high olefin selectivity [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330].

2. Gas-phase radical reactions:
   - Propyl radicals formed on the catalyst surface continue to react in the gas phase via alkyl hydroperoxides [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330].
   - Direct experimental evidence of gas-phase methyl radicals (CH3·) has been obtained using SVUV-PIMS [10.1002/anie.202002440].
   - Gas-phase reactions contribute significantly to the formation of C1 and C2 products [10.1002/anie.202002440].

3. Synergistic effects:
   - The combination of surface-mediated radical formation and gas-phase propagation explains the remarkable product distribution [10.1002/anie.202003695].
   - Effective coupling of surface and gas-phase reactions plays a crucial role in achieving high activity and propene selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].
   - In-situ formed olefins promote parent alkane conversion and C-C bond cracking [10.1038/s41467-022-35776-3].

4. Reaction pathways:
   - Two main pathways exist depending on temperature and conversion rates: low-temperature (<10% conversion) and high-temperature (>10% conversion) [10.1002/cctc.201700725].
   - Propene is mainly formed on the catalyst surface, while C1 and C2 products can be formed via both surface-mediated and gas-phase pathways [10.1002/anie.202002440].

5. Oxygen activation and management:
   - O2 activation is initiated by C-H activation, not by direct oxidation of BN [10.1002/cctc.201700725].
   - Oxygen concentration controls the balance between dehydrogenation and C-C bond cleavage [10.1002/anie.202003695].

Implications for catalyst performance and design optimization:

1. Catalyst design should focus on optimizing the distribution and nature of BOH and BO sites [10.1002/cctc.201700725].
2. Increasing edge exposure of the catalyst could improve performance [10.1002/cctc.201700725].
3. Catalysts should be designed to generate reactive H-abstracting species that favor secondary C-H bond abstraction in propane [10.1002/anie.202003695].
4. Minimizing strong interactions between catalysts and intermediates/products can prevent rapid consecutive surface oxidation steps [10.1002/anie.202003695].
5. Consideration of both surface properties and void space in catalyst beds is crucial for optimizing performance [10.1002/anie.202003695].
6. The influence of catalyst configuration on free radical transport and reaction must be considered in catalyst design [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The multidimensional nature of the ODHP reaction mechanism over B-based catalysts offers a new paradigm for catalyst design and process optimization. Future research directions may include:

1. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques: Developing methods to simultaneously monitor surface and gas-phase species during the reaction could provide deeper insights into the reaction mechanism and guide catalyst optimization.

2. Computational modeling: Creating comprehensive models that integrate both surface and gas-phase kinetics could lead to more accurate performance predictions and guide rational catalyst design.

3. Tailored catalyst architectures: Exploring novel catalyst structures that optimize the balance between surface-mediated and gas-phase reactions, such as hierarchical porous materials or controlled edge-exposed nanostructures.

4. Intelligent reactor design: Developing reactors that can dynamically adjust conditions to maintain optimal balance between surface and gas-phase reactions throughout the catalyst bed.

5. Multifunctional catalysts: Investigating catalysts that can selectively promote different reaction pathways in different regions of the reactor, potentially leading to improved overall performance.

6. Machine learning applications: Utilizing machine learning algorithms to analyze complex reaction networks and predict optimal catalyst compositions and reactor conditions.

These future directions could lead to significant advancements in ODHP technology, potentially enabling more efficient and selective production of valuable olefins from alkanes.

<Chinese>
B基催化剂上ODHP反应机理的多维性质，包括表面和气相自由基反应，显著增强了我们对整体反应网络的理解。这种硼基表面上的多相催化与均相气相化学之间的复杂相互作用，有助于这些催化剂在活性和选择性方面表现出卓越的性能。

这种多维机理的关键方面包括：

1. 表面介导反应：
   - 催化剂表面的BOH和BO位点作为选择性C-H键活化的活性位点 [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.11.014]。
   - 氧化硼氮化物（h-BN）的氧端接扶手边缘（>BOON<）被确定为ODHP的可能活性位点 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。
   - 表面BOx物种抑制反应物的解离吸附，有助于高烯烃选择性 [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。

2. 气相自由基反应：
   - 在催化剂表面形成的丙基自由基通过烷基氢过氧化物继续在气相中反应 [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。
   - 使用SVUV-PIMS直接实验证明了气相甲基自由基（CH3·）的存在 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。
   - 气相反应对C1和C2产物的形成有显著贡献 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。

3. 协同效应：
   - 表面介导的自由基形成和气相传播的组合解释了显著的产物分布 [10.1002/anie.202003695]。
   - 表面和气相反应的有效耦合在实现高活性和丙烯选择性方面起着至关重要的作用 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4]。
   - 原位形成的烯烃促进母体烷烃转化和C-C键断裂 [10.1038/s41467-022-35776-3]。

4. 反应途径：
   - 存在两种主要途径，取决于温度和转化率：低温（<10%转化率）和高温（>10%转化率）[10.1002/cctc.201700725]。
   - 丙烯主要在催化剂表面形成，而C1和C2产物可以通过表面介导和气相途径形成 [10.1002/anie.202002440]。

5. 氧气活化和管理：
   - O2活化由C-H活化引发，而不是通过BN的直接氧化 [10.1002/cctc.201700725]。
   - 氧气浓度控制脱氢和C-C键断裂之间的平衡 [10.1002/anie.202003695]。

对催化剂性能和设计优化的影响：

1. 催化剂设计应着重于优化BOH和BO位点的分布和性质 [10.1002/cctc.201700725]。
2. 增加催化剂的边缘暴露可以提高性能 [10.1002/cctc.201700725]。
3. 催化剂应设计成能产生有利于丙烷中二级C-H键断裂的活性H-抽取物种 [10.1002/anie.202003695]。
4. 最小化催化剂与中间体和产物之间的强相互作用可以防止快速连续的表面氧化步骤 [10.1002/anie.202003695]。
5. 考虑催化剂床的表面性质和空隙空间对优化性能至关重要 [10.1002/anie.202003695]。
6. 在催化剂设计中必须考虑催化剂构型对自由基传输和反应的影响 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_4]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：B基催化剂上ODHP反应机理的多维性为催化剂设计和过程优化提供了新的范式。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 先进的原位表征技术：开发能同时监测反应过程中表面和气相物种的方法，可以提供对反应机理更深入的理解，并指导催化剂优化。

2. 计算模拟：创建整合表面和气相动力学的综合模型，可以导致更准确的性能预测，并指导理性催化剂设计。

3. 定制催化剂架构：探索优化表面介导和气相反应平衡的新型催化剂结构，如分层多孔材料或控制边缘暴露的纳米结构。

4. 智能反应器设计：开发能动态调整条件以在整个催化剂床中保持表面和气相反应最佳平衡的反应器。

5. 多功能催化剂：研究能在反应器不同区域选择性促进不同反应途径的催化剂，可能导致整体性能的改善。

6. 机器学习应用：利用机器学习算法分析复杂的反应网络，预测最佳催化剂成分和反应器条件。

这些未来方向可能会导致ODHP技术的重大进展，有可能实现更高效和选择性的烷烃到有价值烯烃的生产。
