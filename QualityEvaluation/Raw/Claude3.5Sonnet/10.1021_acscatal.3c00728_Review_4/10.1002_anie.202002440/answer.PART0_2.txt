This complex interplay reveals that while propene is primarily formed on the catalyst surface through CH activation of propane, C1 and C2 products can be generated through both surface-mediated and gas-phase pathways. The direct detection of gas-phase methyl radicals provides crucial evidence for the existence of gas-phase radical chemistry in this system.

The synergistic effects of surface and gas-phase reactions have important implications for catalyst performance and design optimization. First, it suggests that catalyst design should focus not only on optimizing surface properties for propene formation but also on controlling the formation and reactivity of gas-phase radicals to manage the production of C1 and C2 byproducts. This dual focus could lead to more selective and efficient catalysts.
