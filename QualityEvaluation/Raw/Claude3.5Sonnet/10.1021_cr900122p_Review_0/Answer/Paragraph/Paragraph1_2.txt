<English>
Isolated metal centers on oxide materials play a crucial role in facilitating C-H bond activation in heterogeneous catalysis. This review section focuses on the mechanisms involved and their applications in selective alkane oxidation, with particular emphasis on the conversion of butane to maleic anhydride.

The Mars van Krevelen mechanism is one of the primary mechanisms through which isolated metal centers facilitate C-H bond activation. This mechanism involves a series of elementary steps: (i) activation of the substrate on a metallic cation acting as a Lewis acid site, (ii) insertion of oxygen from lattice oxygens, and (iii) a redox process at the metallic site involving electron transfer [10.1021/cr010333u]. The cooperation between acid-base and redox sites on the catalyst surface is key to this mechanism.

H-abstraction is another important mechanism in C-H bond activation. Surface oxygen species abstract hydrogen from the alkane, forming radical intermediates that undergo further transformations [10.1021/cr900122p_0]. This process often occurs in conjunction with the Mars van Krevelen mechanism.

The site isolation theory, developed by Callahan and Grasselli, emphasizes the importance of having a statistically controlled number of surface oxidizing sites to favor selective oxidation reactions over combustion reactions [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00041-3]. This concept is crucial in designing catalysts for selective alkane oxidation.

In industrial applications, these principles are applied in the selective oxidation of alkanes, with the conversion of butane to maleic anhydride being a prime example. This process uses vanadyl pyrophosphate ((VO)2P2O7) as the catalyst in fixed-bed, moving-bed, and fluidized-bed reactors [10.1039/9781847553232-00246]. The catalyst's performance is influenced by factors such as preparation method, phosphorus to vanadium (P/V) ratio, presence of additives, and activation/conditioning procedures.

The mechanism for butane to maleic anhydride conversion involves the initial C-H bond activation of butane as the rate-determining step. The most accepted mechanism involves a concerted abstraction of two hydrogen atoms by V(IV)O species, converting butane into 2-butenes, followed by the reoxidation of the active phase by O2, adhering to the Mars van Krevelen mechanism [10.1021/cr900122p_0].

The vanadyl pyrophosphate catalyst demonstrates remarkable selectivity, achieving up to 97% selectivity at 65% conversion [10.1021/cr900122p_0]. This high selectivity is attributed to the catalyst's ability to efficiently perform multiple oxidation steps once the initial C-H activation occurs.

Recent studies have shown that coupling redox-active metal centers with redox-active polyoxometalates (POMs) can improve the activity and selectivity of metal-catalyzed oxidation processes [10.1021/cr000018s]. Additionally, the basicity of the support in supported metal oxide catalysts has been observed to influence olefin selectivity as alkane conversion increases.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The study of C-H bond activation mechanisms on isolated metal centers has significantly advanced our understanding of selective alkane oxidation processes. However, several areas warrant further investigation. Future research could focus on:

1. Developing more precise models of the catalyst surface at the atomic level, incorporating the effects of defects and dynamic changes during the reaction.

2. Exploring novel combinations of metal centers and oxide supports to enhance selectivity and efficiency in alkane oxidation reactions.

3. Investigating the potential of single-atom catalysts in selective oxidation processes, leveraging the unique properties of isolated metal centers.

4. Applying advanced in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic nature of the catalyst surface during reaction conditions.

5. Exploring the application of machine learning and artificial intelligence in catalyst design and optimization for selective alkane oxidation processes.

These research directions could lead to the development of more efficient and selective catalysts for alkane oxidation, potentially opening new pathways for the valorization of light alkanes in the chemical industry.

<Chinese>
氧化物材料上的孤立金属中心在多相催化中的C-H键活化过程中起着至关重要的作用。本综述部分重点关注涉及的机制及其在选择性烷烃氧化中的应用，特别强调了丁烷到马来酸酐的转化。

Mars van Krevelen机制是孤立金属中心促进C-H键活化的主要机制之一。该机制涉及一系列基本步骤：(i)在作为Lewis酸位点的金属阳离子上活化底物，(ii)从晶格氧插入氧，以及(iii)在金属位点进行涉及电子转移的氧化还原过程[10.1021/cr010333u]。催化剂表面酸碱位点和氧化还原位点的协同作用是该机制的关键。

H-抽提是C-H键活化的另一个重要机制。表面氧物种从烷烃中抽提氢，形成进一步转化的自由基中间体[10.1021/cr900122p_0]。这个过程通常与Mars van Krevelen机制同时发生。

Callahan和Grasselli开发的位点隔离理论强调了控制表面氧化位点数量的统计学重要性，以有利于选择性氧化反应而非燃烧反应[10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00041-3]。这个概念在设计选择性烷烃氧化催化剂时至关重要。

在工业应用中，这些原理被应用于烷烃的选择性氧化，其中丁烷转化为马来酸酐是一个最佳例子。该过程在固定床、移动床和流化床反应器中使用钒焦磷酸盐((VO)2P2O7)作为催化剂[10.1039/9781847553232-00246]。催化剂的性能受到制备方法、磷钒比(P/V)、添加剂的存在以及活化/调节程序等因素的影响。

丁烷到马来酸酐转化的机制涉及丁烷的初始C-H键活化作为速率决定步骤。最被接受的机制包括V(IV)O物种对两个氢原子的协同抽提，将丁烷转化为2-丁烯，随后活性相被O2重新氧化，符合Mars van Krevelen机制[10.1021/cr900122p_0]。

钒焦磷酸盐催化剂表现出显著的选择性，在65%转化率下实现高达97%的选择性[10.1021/cr900122p_0]。这种高选择性归因于催化剂在初始C-H活化发生后能够高效进行多次氧化步骤的能力。

最近的研究表明，将氧化还原活性金属中心与氧化还原活性多金属氧酸盐(POMs)耦合可以提高金属催化氧化过程的活性和选择性[10.1021/cr000018s]。此外，在负载型金属氧化物催化剂中，载体的碱性被观察到会影响烯烃选择性随烷烃转化率增加的变化。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：对孤立金属中心上C-H键活化机制的研究显著提高了我们对选择性烷烃氧化过程的理解。然而，仍有几个领域值得进一步研究。未来的研究可以集中在：

1. 开发更精确的原子级催化剂表面模型，纳入缺陷和反应过程中动态变化的影响。

2. 探索金属中心和氧化物载体的新组合，以提高烷烃氧化反应的选择性和效率。

3. 研究单原子催化剂在选择性氧化过程中的潜力，利用孤立金属中心的独特性质。

4. 应用先进的原位表征技术，更好地理解反应条件下催化剂表面的动态性质。

5. 探索机器学习和人工智能在选择性烷烃氧化过程的催化剂设计和优化中的应用。

这些研究方向可能会导致更高效和选择性的烷烃氧化催化剂的开发，潜在地为化学工业中轻烷烃的增值开辟新的途径。
