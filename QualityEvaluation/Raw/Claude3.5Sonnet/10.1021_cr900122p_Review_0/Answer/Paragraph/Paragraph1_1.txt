<English>
Isolated metal centers on oxide materials play a crucial role in facilitating C-H bond activation in heterogeneous catalysis. This review section focuses on the mechanisms involved and their applications in selective alkane oxidation, with particular emphasis on propane dehydrogenation catalysts.

The activation of C-H bonds on isolated metal centers occurs through several mechanisms:

1. Mars van Krevelen mechanism: This involves the oxidation of the alkane by the metal oxide, followed by the reoxidation of the solid by molecular oxygen [10.1021/cr900122p_0]. It is one of the most general mechanisms for oxidation reactions in metallic oxides, with cooperation between acid-base and redox sites being key for catalysis [10.1021/cr010333u].

2. Metal ion catalysis: In this mechanism, coordinatively unsaturated metal centers, such as Al(O)n sites with n = 3 and 4, insert into C-H bonds, followed by hydrogen migration to an oxygen atom [10.1039/b006774l]. This process challenges previous assumptions about acid-base catalysis being solely responsible for such reactions.

3. H-abstraction: Surface oxygen species abstract hydrogen from the alkane, forming radical intermediates that undergo further transformations [10.1021/cr900122p_0]. This mechanism is often the rate-limiting step in selective oxidation of alkanes.

4. Heterolytic splitting: C-H bonds can be activated through heterolytic splitting on highly reactive metal-oxygen sites, such as GaO-S in gallium oxide [10.1021/cr900122p_0].

The effectiveness of these mechanisms depends on several factors:

1. Site isolation: The density and distribution of active sites on the catalyst surface is crucial for controlling selectivity [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00041-3].

2. Redox properties: The oxidation state of transition metal ions in the catalyst is dynamic and interacts with the gas phase, allowing for controlled oxidation and reduction cycles [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00041-3].

3. Metal-oxygen bond strength: This plays a key role in C-H activation [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00041-3].

4. Structural effects: Defects in the catalyst structure can favor the mobility of ionic species in the bulk, potentially aiding C-H activation [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00041-3].

In the context of propane dehydrogenation, isolated metal centers on oxide materials have shown promising results. For example, Mg3(VO4)2 spread over MgO is considered to be highly active and selective for n-butane oxydehydrogenation due to the presence of isolated VO4 units [10.1016/0920-5861(95)00051-g]. These isolated units prevent over-oxidation by not inserting oxygen into activated hydrocarbons, thus avoiding the formation of oxygenated hydrocarbons that lead to COx.

The industrial application of these principles is exemplified in the conversion of butane to maleic anhydride using vanadyl pyrophosphate ((VO)2P2O7) catalysts [10.1039/9781847553232-00246]. This process achieves high selectivity (up to 97% at 65% conversion) and is commercially viable [10.1021/cr900122p_0]. The reaction is believed to occur specifically on the (1,0,0) crystal face of the catalyst, where the spatial arrangement of atoms facilitates interaction with the C-C and C-H bonds of butane [10.1021/cr010333u].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The study of C-H bond activation mechanisms on isolated metal centers has significantly advanced our understanding of heterogeneous catalysis for selective alkane oxidation. Future research directions may include:

1. Developing more precise methods for controlling the distribution and properties of isolated metal centers on oxide surfaces to further enhance selectivity and activity.

2. Exploring new combinations of metals and oxide supports to create catalysts with improved performance for specific reactions.

3. Investigating the potential of combining isolated metal centers with other catalytic strategies, such as single-atom catalysts or metal-organic frameworks, to create hybrid systems with enhanced properties.

4. Applying advanced in-situ characterization techniques to gain deeper insights into the dynamic behavior of isolated metal centers during catalytic reactions.

5. Developing computational models that can accurately predict the behavior of isolated metal centers in complex reaction environments, potentially leading to the rational design of new catalysts.

These advancements could lead to more efficient and environmentally friendly processes for the production of valuable chemicals from alkanes, contributing to the development of sustainable chemical industries.

<Chinese>
氧化物材料上的孤立金属中心在多相催化中对C-H键的活化起着至关重要的作用。本综述部分聚焦于涉及的机制及其在选择性烷烃氧化中的应用，特别强调了丙烷脱氢催化剂。

在孤立金属中心上C-H键的活化通过几种机制发生：

1. Mars van Krevelen机制：这涉及烷烃被金属氧化物氧化，随后固体被分子氧再氧化 [10.1021/cr900122p_0]。这是金属氧化物中最普遍的氧化反应机制之一，酸碱位点和氧化还原位点的协同作用是催化的关键 [10.1021/cr010333u]。

2. 金属离子催化：在这种机制中，配位不饱和的金属中心，如n=3和4的Al(O)n位点，插入C-H键中，随后氢迁移到氧原子上 [10.1039/b006774l]。这一过程挑战了之前认为仅酸碱催化负责此类反应的假设。

3. H-抽取：表面氧物种从烷烃中抽取氢，形成经历进一步转化的自由基中间体 [10.1021/cr900122p_0]。这种机制通常是烷烃选择性氧化的速率限制步骤。

4. 异裂：C-H键可以通过在高反应性的金属-氧位点上的异裂来活化，例如氧化镓中的GaO-S [10.1021/cr900122p_0]。

这些机制的有效性取决于几个因素：

1. 位点隔离：催化剂表面活性位点的密度和分布对控制选择性至关重要 [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00041-3]。

2. 氧化还原性质：催化剂中过渡金属离子的氧化态是动态的，与气相相互作用，允许控制的氧化还原循环 [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00041-3]。

3. 金属-氧键强度：这在C-H活化中起关键作用 [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00041-3]。

4. 结构效应：催化剂结构中的缺陷可以促进体相中离子物种的移动性，可能有助于C-H活化 [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00041-3]。

在丙烷脱氢的背景下，氧化物材料上的孤立金属中心显示出有前景的结果。例如，由于存在孤立的VO4单元，分散在MgO上的Mg3(VO4)2被认为在正丁烷氧化脱氢中具有高活性和选择性 [10.1016/0920-5861(95)00051-g]。这些孤立单元通过不将氧插入活化的烃中来防止过度氧化，从而避免形成导致COx的含氧烃。

这些原理的工业应用在使用钒焦磷酸盐（(VO)2P2O7）催化剂将丁烷转化为顺丁烯二酸酐的过程中得到了例证 [10.1039/9781847553232-00246]。这个过程实现了高选择性（在65%转化率下高达97%）并且具有商业可行性 [10.1021/cr900122p_0]。据信，反应特定发生在催化剂的(1,0,0)晶面上，其中原子的空间排列有利于与丁烷的C-C和C-H键相互作用 [10.1021/cr010333u]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：对孤立金属中心上C-H键活化机制的研究显著提高了我们对选择性烷烃氧化多相催化的理解。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 开发更精确的方法来控制氧化物表面上孤立金属中心的分布和性质，以进一步提高选择性和活性。

2. 探索金属和氧化物载体的新组合，以创造具有改进性能的特定反应催化剂。

3. 调查将孤立金属中心与其他催化策略（如单原子催化剂或金属有机框架）结合的潜力，以创造具有增强属性的混合系统。

4. 应用先进的原位表征技术，深入了解催化反应过程中孤立金属中心的动态行为。

5. 开发能够准确预测复杂反应环境中孤立金属中心行为的计算模型，可能导致新催化剂的理性设计。

这些进展可能导致从烷烃生产有价值化学品的更高效和环境友好的过程，为可持续化学工业的发展做出贡献。
