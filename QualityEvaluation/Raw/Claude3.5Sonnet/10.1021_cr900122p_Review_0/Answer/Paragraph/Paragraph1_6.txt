<English>
Isolated metal centers on oxide materials play a crucial role in facilitating C-H bond activation in heterogeneous catalysis. This review section will focus on the mechanisms involved, particularly the Mars van Krevelen mechanism and H-abstraction, and their application in industrial selective oxidation of alkanes, with emphasis on the conversion of butane to maleic anhydride.

The Mars van Krevelen mechanism is one of the most general mechanisms for oxidation reactions in metallic oxides, involving cooperation between acid-base and redox sites [10.1021/cr010333u]. This mechanism consists of three key steps:

1. Activation of the substrate on a metal cation acting as a Lewis acid site
2. Insertion of oxygen from the lattice
3. A redox process at the metal site involving electron transfer

The metal centers facilitate oxidation reactions in three general ways:
1. Single electron abstraction from an electron-rich molecule
2. Acting as part of a catalytic center that includes Lewis acid, Lewis base, and redox-active metal sites
3. Forming acid-base adducts with the substrate or oxidizing agent to enhance reactivity [10.1021/cr010333u]

H-abstraction is another important mechanism in C-H bond activation. This process involves the abstraction of hydrogen by reactive oxygen sites on the oxide surface, resulting in the formation of adsorbed alkyl radical intermediates [10.1021/cr900122p_0]. The H-abstraction mechanism is often the rate-limiting step in selective oxidations of alkanes and typically involves surface oxygen species yielding radical intermediates [10.1021/cr900122p_0].

The industrial application of these principles is exemplified in the conversion of butane to maleic anhydride using vanadyl pyrophosphate ((VO)2P2O7) catalysts. This process is the only industrial selective oxidation of a paraffin and achieves high selectivities (up to 97% at 65% conversion) [10.1039/9781847553232-00246]. The reaction is believed to occur specifically on the (1,0,0) crystal face of the catalyst, where the spatial arrangement of atoms facilitates interaction with the C-C and C-H bonds of butane [10.1021/cr010333u].

The mechanism for butane to maleic anhydride conversion involves:
1. C-H bond activation of butane as the rate-determining step
2. Conversion of butane into 2-butenes via concerted abstraction of two hydrogen atoms by V(IV)O species
3. Reoxidation of the active phase by O2, adhering to the Mars van Krevelen mechanism [10.1021/cr900122p_0]

The catalyst's performance is influenced by several factors, including the preparation method, phosphorus to vanadium (P/V) ratio, presence of additives, and activation/conditioning procedures [10.1039/9781847553232-00246]. The redox properties of the metal centers, in terms of reducibility and reoxidizability, and the metal-oxygen bond strength also play crucial roles in the catalytic process [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00041-3].

Recent studies have shown that coupling redox-active metal centers with redox-active polyoxometalates (POMs) can improve the activity and selectivity of metal-catalyzed oxidation processes [10.1021/cr000018s]. Additionally, the basicity of the support in supported metal oxide catalysts has been observed to influence the selectivity of olefin formation as alkane conversion increases [10.1021/cr000018s].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The study of isolated metal centers on oxide materials for C-H bond activation in heterogeneous catalysis has provided valuable insights into the mechanisms of selective oxidation reactions. The Mars van Krevelen and H-abstraction mechanisms have been shown to be key in these processes, particularly in the industrial conversion of butane to maleic anhydride.

Future research in this field could focus on:

1. Developing more precise methods for controlling the distribution and properties of isolated metal centers on oxide surfaces to further enhance selectivity and efficiency.
2. Exploring new combinations of metal centers and oxide supports to expand the range of selective oxidation reactions possible in industrial settings.
3. Investigating the potential of coupling redox-active metal centers with other types of active species, such as metal-organic frameworks or single-atom catalysts, to create hybrid systems with enhanced catalytic properties.
4. Utilizing advanced in situ characterization techniques to gain deeper insights into the dynamic behavior of isolated metal centers during catalytic reactions, potentially leading to the design of more robust and long-lasting catalysts.
5. Applying machine learning and artificial intelligence approaches to predict and optimize catalyst compositions and reaction conditions for selective oxidation processes.

These future directions could lead to the development of more efficient and environmentally friendly processes for the production of valuable chemical intermediates and fine chemicals from abundant alkane feedstocks.

<Chinese>
氧化物材料上的孤立金属中心在非均相催化中的C-H键活化中起着至关重要的作用。本综述部分将集中讨论所涉及的机理，特别是Mars van Krevelen机理和H-抽取机理，以及它们在烷烃工业选择性氧化中的应用，重点是丁烷转化为顺丁烯二酐的过程。

Mars van Krevelen机理是金属氧化物中氧化反应的最普遍机理之一，涉及酸碱位点和氧化还原位点之间的协同作用[10.1021/cr010333u]。这种机理包括三个关键步骤：

1. 在作为Lewis酸位点的金属阳离子上活化底物
2. 从晶格中插入氧
3. 在金属位点进行涉及电子转移的氧化还原过程

金属中心通过三种一般方式促进氧化反应：
1. 从富电子分子中抽取单个电子
2. 作为包括Lewis酸位点、Lewis碱位点和氧化还原活性金属位点的催化中心的一部分
3. 与底物或氧化剂形成酸碱加合物以增强反应性[10.1021/cr010333u]

H-抽取是C-H键活化中另一个重要机理。这个过程涉及氧化物表面上的活性氧位点抽取氢，导致形成吸附的烷基自由基中间体[10.1021/cr900122p_0]。H-抽取机理通常是烷烃选择性氧化的速率限制步骤，通常涉及产生自由基中间体的表面氧物种[10.1021/cr900122p_0]。

这些原理的工业应用在使用焦磷酸钒((VO)2P2O7)催化剂将丁烷转化为顺丁烯二酐的过程中得到了很好的体现。这是唯一的工业选择性氧化石蜡的过程，并且实现了高选择性(在65%转化率下高达97%)[10.1039/9781847553232-00246]。据信，反应特别发生在催化剂的(1,0,0)晶面上，其中原子的空间排列有利于与丁烷的C-C和C-H键相互作用[10.1021/cr010333u]。

丁烷转化为顺丁烯二酐的机理包括：
1. 丁烷的C-H键活化作为速率决定步骤
2. 通过V(IV)O物种协同抽取两个氢原子将丁烷转化为2-丁烯
3. 通过O2重新氧化活性相，遵循Mars van Krevelen机理[10.1021/cr900122p_0]

催化剂的性能受几个因素影响，包括制备方法、磷与钒（P/V）比率、添加剂的存在以及活化/调节程序[10.1039/9781847553232-00246]。金属中心的氧化还原性能，就其可还原性和可再氧化性而言，以及金属-氧键强度也在催化过程中起着关键作用[10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00041-3]。

最近的研究表明，将氧化还原活性金属中心与氧化还原活性多金属氧酸盐（POMs）耦合可以提高金属催化氧化过程的活性和选择性[10.1021/cr000018s]。此外，在负载型金属氧化物催化剂中，载体的碱性已被观察到会影响烯烃形成的选择性，随着烷烃转化率的增加而变化[10.1021/cr000018s]。

综合理解与未来展望：对氧化物材料上孤立金属中心在非均相催化中C-H键活化的研究为选择性氧化反应的机理提供了宝贵的见解。Mars van Krevelen和H-抽取机理被证明是这些过程中的关键，特别是在丁烷转化为顺丁烯二酐的工业过程中。

该领域的未来研究可以集中在：

1. 开发更精确的方法来控制氧化物表面上孤立金属中心的分布和性质，以进一步提高选择性和效率。
2. 探索新的金属中心和氧化物载体的组合，以扩大工业环境中可能的选择性氧化反应范围。
3. 研究将氧化还原活性金属中心与其他类型的活性物种（如金属有机框架或单原子催化剂）耦合的潜力，以创建具有增强催化性能的混合系统。
4. 利用先进的原位表征技术，深入了解催化反应过程中孤立金属中心的动态行为，可能导致设计更稳定和长寿命的催化剂。
5. 应用机器学习和人工智能方法来预测和优化选择性氧化过程的催化剂组成和反应条件。

这些未来方向可能导致开发出更高效、更环保的过程，从丰富的烷烃原料生产有价值的化学中间体和精细化学品。
