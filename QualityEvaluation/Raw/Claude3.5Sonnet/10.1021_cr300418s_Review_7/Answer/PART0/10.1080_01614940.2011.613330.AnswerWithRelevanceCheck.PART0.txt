      <English>
          The Mars-van Krevelen mechanism in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions involves the participation of lattice oxygen from the oxide catalyst. In this mechanism, a reactant molecule is oxidized by removing an oxygen atom from the catalyst surface, creating an oxygen vacancy. This vacancy is then refilled by gas phase oxygen, completing the catalytic cycle.

          The energy required for oxygen vacancy formation (ΔEv) is a key parameter that influences both the catalytic activity and oxidizing ability of the oxide surface:

          1. Catalytic activity: A lower ΔEv generally leads to higher catalytic activity, as it is easier to remove lattice oxygen for the oxidation reaction. However, if ΔEv is too low, the catalyst may become unstable or over-reactive.

          2. Oxidizing ability: ΔEv correlates with the oxide's oxidizing power. A lower ΔEv indicates stronger oxidizing ability, as oxygen can be more readily removed from the lattice to oxidize reactants. Oxides with moderate ΔEv values tend to have optimal oxidizing ability, balancing reactivity and selectivity.

          3. Selectivity: The value of ΔEv can impact reaction selectivity by influencing the strength of oxygen binding to the surface and its reactivity. An optimal intermediate ΔEv is often desired to balance activity and selectivity.

          4. Oxygen mobility: A lower ΔEv typically corresponds to higher oxygen mobility within the lattice, which can enhance the catalyst's ability to transport oxygen to active sites.

          5. Catalyst durability: Very low ΔEv values may lead to excessive oxygen loss and structural instability, potentially causing catalyst deactivation over time.

          ΔEv serves as a descriptor for predicting and understanding catalytic behavior in oxide-catalyzed oxidation reactions, helping to guide the design and selection of catalysts for specific applications. Tuning the ΔEv through catalyst composition and structure is a crucial strategy for optimizing oxide catalysts for specific oxidation reactions, balancing activity, selectivity, and stability.

          Mars-van Krevelen机理在氧化物催化的氧化反应中涉及氧化物催化剂中晶格氧的参与。在这个机理中，反应物分子通过从催化剂表面移除一个氧原子而被氧化，从而产生氧空位。然后这个空位被气相氧重新填充，完成催化循环。

          氧空位形成能(ΔEv)是一个关键参数，影响氧化物表面的催化活性和氧化能力：

          1. 催化活性：较低的ΔEv通常会导致更高的催化活性，因为更容易移除晶格氧进行氧化反应。但是，如果ΔEv太低，催化剂可能会变得不稳定或反应过度。

          2. 氧化能力：ΔEv与氧化物的氧化能力相关。较低的ΔEv表示更强的氧化能力，因为氧更容易从晶格中移除以氧化反应物。具有适中ΔEv值的氧化物往往具有最佳的氧化能力，平衡反应性和选择性。

          3. 选择性：ΔEv的值可能通过影响氧与表面的结合强度及其反应性来影响反应选择性。通常需要一个适中的ΔEv来平衡活性和选择性。

          4. 氧移动性：较低的ΔEv通常对应于晶格内更高的氧移动性，这可以增强催化剂将氧运输到活性位点的能力。

          5. 催化剂耐久性：非常低的ΔEv值可能导致过度的氧损失和结构不稳定性，可能导致催化剂随时间失活。

          ΔEv作为预测和理解氧化物催化氧化反应中催化行为的描述符，有助于指导特定应用的催化剂设计和选择。通过催化剂组成和结构调整ΔEv是优化氧化物催化剂用于特定氧化反应的关键策略，平衡活性、选择性和稳定性。
