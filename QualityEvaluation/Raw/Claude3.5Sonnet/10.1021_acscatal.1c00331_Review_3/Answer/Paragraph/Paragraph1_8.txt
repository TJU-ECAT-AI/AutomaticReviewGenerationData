<English>
The comprehensive influence of catalyst support pore structure characteristics on coke formation, accommodation, and overall catalyst performance in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions is complex and multifaceted. Various studies have provided insights into how different aspects of pore structure affect catalyst behavior and longevity.

Pore size plays a crucial role in determining catalyst performance and coke formation. Smaller pore sizes generally result in higher propane conversion and catalytic activity, with the order of activity decreasing as pore size increases: ZSM-5 > Beta > SBA-15 [10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]. However, the relationship between pore size and coke formation is not straightforward. While smaller pores can limit coke formation, they are also more susceptible to blockage [10.1002/aic.16410]. Studies have shown that coke yield diminishes with decreasing pore size, with catalysts having larger pore sizes correlating to higher coke yields [10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4].

Pore connectivity and uniformity significantly influence catalyst performance and coke formation. Three-dimensional microporous materials (e.g., ZSM-5 and Beta zeolites) outperform one-dimensional mesoporous materials (e.g., SBA-15) in terms of activity, selectivity, and stability [10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]. This suggests that interconnected pore networks facilitate better reactant-catalyst interactions and provide improved accessibility to active sites. Uniformity in pore structure contributes to shape-selectivity effects, which can influence product distribution and coke formation [10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031].

Hierarchical pore arrangements have shown promise in optimizing catalyst performance. The introduction of hierarchical pores in zeolite supports, such as ZSM-5, greatly improves the catalyst's capacity to accommodate coke while maintaining catalytic activity [10.1002/jctb.4686]. These structures typically combine smaller intracrystalline mesopores (around 3 nm) with larger mesopores or macropores (20-80 nm) on the surface, enhancing mass transfer and diffusion kinetics [10.1021/acscatal.0c03286].

The interplay between pore structure and surface chemistry is also crucial. Modifying the pore structure of zeolites by introducing hierarchical pores can decrease support acidity, which inhibits coke formation on the metal and improves the support's capacity for coke accommodation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_3]. This highlights the importance of considering both physical structure and chemical properties when designing catalysts.

Coke formation and accommodation are significantly influenced by pore structure. While coke formation is associated with catalytic events occurring at the metal surface, the resulting carbonaceous species do not necessarily directly block the active sites in well-designed catalyst systems [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]. Supports with more uniform, highly connected, and larger-sized pores can accommodate more coke, potentially extending catalyst lifetime [10.1002/jctb.4686].

The impact of pore structure on metal particle stabilization is another important consideration. Hierarchical pore structures can inhibit the agglomeration of metal particles during reactions, contributing to improved catalyst stability [10.1002/jctb.4686]. This is particularly important for maintaining the activity of subnanometric metal species, which are crucial for optimal catalytic performance in PDH reactions [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7].

These findings have significant implications for designing optimal and effective catalysts for PDH reactions:

1. Balancing pore size to maximize conversion while minimizing coke formation and maintaining accessibility to active sites.
2. Utilizing three-dimensional, interconnected pore networks to enhance reactant-catalyst contact and improve mass transfer.
3. Incorporating hierarchical pore structures that combine the benefits of different pore sizes and connectivities to optimize both reaction kinetics and coke accommodation.
4. Considering the synergy between pore structure and surface chemistry, particularly in tailoring support acidity and metal-support interactions.
5. Designing pore structures that promote uniform coke deposition and facilitate the migration of coke precursors away from active sites.
6. Optimizing pore connectivity to maintain catalyst activity even as some pores become blocked by coke.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive analysis of catalyst support pore structures in PDH reactions reveals the intricate balance required for optimal performance. Future research directions should focus on developing advanced hierarchical pore structures that can dynamically adapt to changing reaction conditions. This could involve the use of stimuli-responsive materials or self-healing supports that can maintain optimal pore characteristics throughout the catalyst lifecycle.

Additionally, the integration of in-situ characterization techniques with machine learning algorithms could provide real-time insights into coke formation and distribution within the pore network. This would enable the development of predictive models for catalyst deactivation and guide the design of next-generation PDH catalysts with enhanced longevity and performance.

Furthermore, exploring the potential of novel materials, such as metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) or covalent organic frameworks (COFs), as catalyst supports could open up new possibilities for precise control over pore structure and metal-support interactions. These materials offer unprecedented tunability in terms of pore size, connectivity, and chemical functionality, which could lead to breakthroughs in catalyst design for PDH reactions and beyond.

<Chinese>
催化剂载体孔结构特性对丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中焦炭形成、容纳以及整体催化剂性能的综合影响是复杂而多方面的。多项研究为不同孔结构方面如何影响催化剂行为和寿命提供了见解。

孔径在决定催化剂性能和焦炭形成方面起着至关重要的作用。较小的孔径通常会导致更高的丙烷转化率和催化活性，活性随孔径增大而降低的顺序为：ZSM-5 > Beta > SBA-15 [10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]。然而，孔径与焦炭形成之间的关系并不简单。虽然较小的孔可以限制焦炭形成，但它们也更容易被堵塞 [10.1002/aic.16410]。研究表明，焦炭产量随孔径减小而减少，具有较大孔径的催化剂与较高的焦炭产量相关 [10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4]。

孔连通性和均匀性显著影响催化剂性能和焦炭形成。三维微孔材料（如ZSM-5和Beta沸石）在活性、选择性和稳定性方面优于一维介孔材料（如SBA-15）[10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]。这表明互联的孔网络有利于反应物-催化剂相互作用，并提供了更好的活性位点可及性。孔结构的均匀性有助于形状选择性效应，这可以影响产物分布和焦炭形成 [10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]。

分层孔结构在优化催化剂性能方面显示出了前景。在沸石载体（如ZSM-5）中引入分层孔大大提高了催化剂容纳焦炭的能力，同时保持催化活性 [10.1002/jctb.4686]。这些结构通常将较小的晶内介孔（约3 nm）与表面上较大的介孔或大孔（20-80 nm）相结合，增强了传质和扩散动力学 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03286]。

孔结构与表面化学之间的相互作用也至关重要。通过引入分层孔来修改沸石的孔结构可以降低载体酸度，这抑制了金属上的焦炭形成，并提高了载体容纳焦炭的能力 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_3]。这突出了在设计催化剂时考虑物理结构和化学性质的重要性。

焦炭形成和容纳受孔结构的显著影响。虽然焦炭形成与金属表面发生的催化事件相关，但在设计良好的催化剂系统中，所产生的碳质物质不一定直接阻塞活性位点 [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]。具有更均匀、高度连通和较大孔径的载体可以容纳更多焦炭，可能延长催化剂寿命 [10.1002/jctb.4686]。

孔结构对金属颗粒稳定性的影响是另一个重要考虑因素。分层孔结构可以抑制反应过程中金属颗粒的团聚，有助于提高催化剂稳定性 [10.1002/jctb.4686]。这对于维持亚纳米金属物种的活性特别重要，这些物种对PDH反应的最佳催化性能至关重要 [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]。

这些发现对设计最佳和有效的PDH反应催化剂有重要意义：

1. 平衡孔径以最大化转化率，同时最小化焦炭形成并保持活性位点的可及性。
2. 利用三维互连孔网络增强反应物-催化剂接触并改善传质。
3. 结合不同孔径和连通性优势的分层孔结构，以优化反应动力学和焦炭容纳。
4. 考虑孔结构与表面化学之间的协同作用，特别是在调整载体酸度和金属-载体相互作用方面。
5. 设计促进均匀焦炭沉积并促进焦炭前体远离活性位点迁移的孔结构。
6. 优化孔连通性，以在某些孔被焦炭堵塞时仍能保持催化剂活性。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：对PDH反应中催化剂载体孔结构的全面分析揭示了实现最佳性能所需的复杂平衡。未来的研究方向应该集中在开发可以动态适应变化反应条件的先进分层孔结构。这可能涉及使用刺激响应材料或自修复载体，以在催化剂整个生命周期内维持最佳孔特性。

此外，将原位表征技术与机器学习算法相结合可以提供孔网络内焦炭形成和分布的实时洞察。这将有助于开发催化剂失活的预测模型，并指导下一代PDH催化剂的设计，以增强其寿命和性能。

此外，探索新型材料（如金属有机框架（MOFs）或共价有机框架（COFs））作为催化剂载体的潜力，可能为精确控制孔结构和金属-载体相互作用开辟新的可能性。这些材料在孔径、连通性和化学功能性方面提供了前所未有的可调性，这可能会导致PDH反应乃至更广泛领域的催化剂设计取得突破性进展。
