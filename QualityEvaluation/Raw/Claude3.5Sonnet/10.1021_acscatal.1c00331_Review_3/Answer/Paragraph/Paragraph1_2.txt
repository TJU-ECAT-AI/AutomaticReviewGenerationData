<English>
The characteristics of catalyst support pore structures significantly influence coke formation, accommodation, and overall catalyst performance in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions. Key aspects include:

1. Pore size: Generally, smaller pores lead to decreased coke formation and improved catalytic performance [10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4]. However, the relationship is complex, as microporous supports (e.g., ZSM-5) can exhibit higher coke formation than some mesoporous supports (e.g., Beta) [10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]. The optimal pore size must balance reactant diffusion, active site accessibility, and coke accommodation.

2. Connectivity: Three-dimensional pore networks, such as those in zeolites, outperform one-dimensional mesoporous materials (e.g., SBA-15) in terms of activity, selectivity, and stability [10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]. Higher pore connectivity can maintain catalyst activity for longer periods by providing alternative pathways when some pores become blocked [10.1002/aic.16410].

3. Uniformity: Uniform pore structures contribute to shape-selectivity effects, influencing product distribution and coke formation [10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]. However, supports with more uniform, highly connected, and larger-sized pores can accommodate more coke, potentially extending catalyst lifetime [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_3].

4. Hierarchical arrangements: Hierarchical pore structures, combining micropores and mesopores, significantly enhance the catalyst's capacity to accommodate coke and improve overall performance [10.1002/jctb.4686]. These structures balance high surface area for active site dispersion with improved mass transfer properties [10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4].

5. Surface area and acidity: Higher surface area generally correlates with improved initial catalytic activity but may also lead to increased coke formation [10.1007/s10562-010-0527-5]. The acidity of the support influences coke formation, with less acidic supports (e.g., SiO2) showing lower coke formation compared to more acidic supports (e.g., Al2O3) [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008].

6. Confinement effects: Well-defined pore structures, such as those in MFI zeolites, can stabilize subnanometric metal species, controlling particle size and distribution, which is crucial for catalyst performance and coke resistance [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7].

7. Coke accommodation: The pore structure influences not only coke formation but also its accommodation. Zeolitic supports with hierarchical pore structures have shown the ability to accommodate substantial coke content while maintaining most of their microporous volume, preserving catalyst performance [10.1002/anie.202003349].

These relationships have significant implications for designing optimal PDH catalysts:

1. Develop supports with hierarchical pore structures to enhance coke accommodation, improve mass transfer, and increase catalyst stability.
2. Optimize pore size distribution to balance metal dispersion, reactant/product diffusion, and coke resistance.
3. Utilize three-dimensional pore networks to enhance reactant-catalyst contact and accessibility to active sites.
4. Consider the interplay between pore structure and surface acidity to achieve desired catalytic performance and coke resistance.
5. Design pore structures that restrict metal particle agglomeration during reactions while facilitating the migration of coke precursors away from active sites.
6. Focus on creating a balance between micropores, mesopores, and macropores to maximize catalytic performance and longevity.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive analysis of catalyst support pore structures in PDH reactions reveals a complex interplay between various structural characteristics and their impact on catalyst performance and coke formation. Moving forward, research efforts should focus on:

1. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic changes in pore structures during PDH reactions and coke formation.
2. Development of predictive models that can simulate the effects of different pore structures on catalyst performance and coke formation, enabling more efficient catalyst design.
3. Exploration of novel synthesis methods to create precisely tailored hierarchical pore structures with optimized connectivity, uniformity, and size distributions.
4. Investigation of the synergistic effects between pore structure and active metal species, potentially leading to the design of highly efficient and stable PDH catalysts.
5. Integration of machine learning algorithms to accelerate the discovery and optimization of catalyst support structures for PDH reactions.

These future directions could lead to breakthroughs in catalyst design, potentially revolutionizing the efficiency and sustainability of propylene production through PDH processes.

<Chinese>
催化剂载体孔结构的特性对丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中的结焦形成、容纳以及整体催化剂性能有显著影响。关键方面包括：

1. 孔径大小：一般来说，较小的孔径导致结焦减少和催化性能提高[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4]。然而，这种关系是复杂的，因为微孔载体（如ZSM-5）可能比某些介孔载体（如Beta）表现出更高的结焦形成[10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]。最佳孔径必须平衡反应物扩散、活性位点可及性和结焦容纳能力。

2. 连通性：三维孔网络（如沸石中的）在活性、选择性和稳定性方面优于一维介孔材料（如SBA-15）[10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]。较高的孔连通性可以通过在一些孔被堵塞时提供替代路径来维持催化剂活性更长时间[10.1002/aic.16410]。

3. 均匀性：均匀的孔结构有助于形状选择性效应，影响产物分布和结焦形成[10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]。然而，具有更均匀、高度连通和较大孔径的载体可以容纳更多结焦，潜在地延长催化剂寿命[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_3]。

4. 层次结构：结合微孔和介孔的层次孔结构显著增强了催化剂容纳结焦的能力，并改善整体性能[10.1002/jctb.4686]。这些结构平衡了活性位点分散的高比表面积和改善的传质性能[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]。

5. 比表面积和酸度：较高的比表面积通常与改善的初始催化活性相关，但也可能导致增加的结焦形成[10.1007/s10562-010-0527-5]。载体的酸度影响结焦形成，酸性较低的载体（如SiO2）比酸性较强的载体（如Al2O3）表现出较低的结焦形成[10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]。

6. 限制效应：明确定义的孔结构，如MFI沸石中的结构，可以稳定亚纳米金属物种，控制粒子大小和分布，这对催化剂性能和抗结焦能力至关重要[10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]。

7. 结焦容纳：孔结构不仅影响结焦形成，还影响其容纳。具有层次孔结构的沸石载体表现出容纳大量结焦的能力，同时保持大部分微孔体积，从而保持催化剂性能[10.1002/anie.202003349]。

这些关系对设计最佳PDH催化剂有重要影响：

1. 开发具有层次孔结构的载体，以增强结焦容纳能力，改善传质，增加催化剂稳定性。
2. 优化孔径分布，以平衡金属分散、反应物/产物扩散和抗结焦能力。
3. 利用三维孔网络增强反应物-催化剂接触和活性位点的可及性。
4. 考虑孔结构和表面酸度之间的相互作用，以实现所需的催化性能和抗结焦能力。
5. 设计能限制反应过程中金属颗粒聚集的孔结构，同时促进结焦前体远离活性位点的迁移。
6. 专注于在微孔、介孔和大孔之间创造平衡，以最大化催化性能和寿命。

※※※
综合理解和未来展望：对PDH反应中催化剂载体孔结构的全面分析揭示了各种结构特性与催化剂性能和结焦形成之间的复杂相互作用。展望未来，研究努力应集中在：

1. 先进的原位表征技术，以更好地理解PDH反应和结焦形成过程中孔结构的动态变化。
2. 开发预测模型，可以模拟不同孔结构对催化剂性能和结焦形成的影响，从而实现更有效的催化剂设计。
3. 探索新型合成方法，创造精确定制的层次孔结构，优化连通性、均匀性和尺寸分布。
4. 研究孔结构和活性金属物种之间的协同效应，可能导致设计高效稳定的PDH催化剂。
5. 集成机器学习算法，加速PDH反应催化剂载体结构的发现和优化。

这些未来方向可能导致催化剂设计的突破，潜在地革新通过PDH过程进行丙烯生产的效率和可持续性。
