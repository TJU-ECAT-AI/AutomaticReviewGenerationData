<English>
The comprehensive influence of catalyst support pore structure characteristics on coke formation, accommodation, and overall catalyst performance in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions is a complex and multifaceted topic. Based on the literature, several key aspects can be identified:

1. Pore Size: Pore size significantly impacts coke formation and catalyst performance. Smaller pores generally lead to higher propane conversion and catalytic activity, with the order of activity decreasing as pore size increases: ZSM-5 > Beta > SBA-15 [10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]. However, the relationship between pore size and coke formation is not straightforward. While smaller pores can limit coke formation, they are also more susceptible to blockage [10.1002/aic.16410]. Studies have shown that coke yield diminishes with decreasing pore size, with catalysts like 3% Pt-SiO2 (1.0 nm pores) showing significantly less coke formation compared to 3% Pt-Al2O3 (7.53 nm pores) [10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4].

2. Connectivity and Accessibility: Three-dimensional microporous materials (e.g., ZSM-5 and Beta zeolites) generally outperform one-dimensional mesoporous materials (e.g., SBA-15) in terms of activity, selectivity, and stability [10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]. This suggests that interconnected pore networks facilitate better reactant-catalyst interactions and provide improved accessibility to active sites. Hierarchical pore structures, combining micropores and mesopores, have shown enhanced mass transfer properties and improved catalyst stability [10.1002/jctb.4686].

3. Uniformity: Uniform pore size distribution, coupled with a large surface area, favors the dispersion of metallic particles, enhancing catalyst performance [10.1002/jctb.4686]. The uniform microporous structure of zeolites contributes to their superior performance compared to materials with larger, less uniform pores [10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031].

4. Hierarchical Arrangements: The introduction of hierarchical pores in zeolites, such as ZSM-5, has shown significant improvements in catalyst performance. These structures typically include smaller intracrystalline mesopores (around 3 nm) and larger mesopores or macropores (20-80 nm) on the surface [10.1002/jctb.4686]. This arrangement enhances the catalyst's capacity to accommodate coke, improves mass transfer, and increases overall stability [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_3].

5. Coke Formation and Accommodation: The pore structure plays a crucial role in coke formation and accommodation. Zeolite-supported catalysts have shown significantly less coke deposition over longer reaction times compared to Al2O3 supports [10.1002/anie.202003349]. Interestingly, even with substantial coke content (14.4%) after extended reaction time, zeolite catalysts maintained most of their microporous volume, suggesting that the pore structure can accommodate coke while preserving catalyst performance [10.1002/anie.202003349].

6. Support Composition: The chemical nature of the support also influences coke formation and catalyst performance. For instance, SiO2 supports showed better propane conversion and propylene selectivity compared to Al2O3, attributed to weaker metal-support interactions allowing for better alloying between Pt and Zn [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008].

These findings have important implications for designing optimal and effective PDH catalysts:

1. Balancing pore size to maximize conversion while minimizing coke formation is crucial. A combination of micropores and mesopores in a hierarchical structure could provide an optimal balance.

2. Developing three-dimensional, interconnected pore networks can enhance reactant-catalyst contact and accessibility to active sites.

3. Incorporating uniform microporous structures with shape-selective properties can improve catalyst selectivity and stability.

4. Exploring hierarchical pore arrangements that combine the benefits of different pore sizes and connectivities could optimize mass transfer and coke accommodation.

5. Considering the synergy between pore structure and active site distribution, particularly in materials with cation exchange capacity, can enhance overall catalyst performance.

6. Tailoring the support's chemical composition to minimize undesired metal-support interactions and promote desired alloy formations can further improve catalyst effectiveness and longevity.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive analysis of catalyst support pore structures in PDH reactions reveals the intricate interplay between various structural characteristics and their impact on catalyst performance. Moving forward, research efforts should focus on developing advanced hierarchical pore structures that optimize the balance between reactant diffusion, active site accessibility, and coke accommodation.

Future directions may include:

1. Exploring novel synthesis methods to create precisely controlled hierarchical pore structures with tailored pore size distributions and connectivity.

2. Investigating the potential of hybrid materials that combine the benefits of different support types, such as zeolite-mesoporous silica composites, to harness the advantages of multiple pore regimes.

3. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to study the dynamic changes in pore structures during PDH reactions, providing real-time insights into coke formation and accommodation processes.

4. Utilizing advanced computational modeling to predict optimal pore structures for specific reaction conditions and desired performance characteristics.

5. Exploring the potential of "smart" catalyst supports that can adapt their pore structures in response to reaction conditions, potentially through the use of stimuli-responsive materials.

6. Investigating the synergistic effects of pore structure optimization with other catalyst design strategies, such as bimetallic nanoparticle engineering and promoter addition, to develop next-generation PDH catalysts with superior performance and longevity.

These research avenues could lead to significant advancements in PDH catalyst design, potentially improving process efficiency, reducing energy consumption, and enhancing the sustainability of propylene production.

催化剂载体孔结构特性对丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中焦炭形成、容纳和整体催化剂性能的全面影响是一个复杂多面的主题。根据文献，可以确定几个关键方面：

1. 孔径：孔径显著影响焦炭形成和催化剂性能。较小的孔径通常会导致更高的丙烷转化率和催化活性，活性随孔径增大而降低：ZSM-5 > Beta > SBA-15 [10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]。然而，孔径与焦炭形成之间的关系并不简单。虽然较小的孔径可以限制焦炭形成，但它们也更容易被堵塞 [10.1002/aic.16410]。研究表明，焦炭产量随孔径减小而减少，例如3% Pt-SiO2（1.0 nm孔）的焦炭形成显著少于3% Pt-Al2O3（7.53 nm孔）[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4]。

2. 连通性和可及性：三维微孔材料（如ZSM-5和Beta沸石）在活性、选择性和稳定性方面通常优于一维介孔材料（如SBA-15）[10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]。这表明相互连接的孔网络可以促进更好的反应物-催化剂相互作用，并提供更好的活性位点可及性。结合微孔和介孔的层级孔结构显示出增强的传质性能和改善的催化剂稳定性 [10.1002/jctb.4686]。

3. 均匀性：均匀的孔径分布与大表面积相结合，有利于金属粒子的分散，从而提高催化剂性能 [10.1002/jctb.4686]。沸石的均匀微孔结构相对于具有较大、不均匀孔的材料，contributes to its superior performance [10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]。

4. 层级排列：在沸石（如ZSM-5）中引入层级孔结构显示出催化剂性能的显著改善。这些结构通常包括较小的晶内介孔（约3 nm）和表面上较大的介孔或大孔（20-80 nm）[10.1002/jctb.4686]。这种排列增强了催化剂容纳焦炭的能力，改善了传质，并增加了整体稳定性 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_3]。

5. 焦炭形成和容纳：孔结构在焦炭形成和容纳中起着关键作用。与Al2O3载体相比，沸石载体的催化剂在较长反应时间内显示出显著较少的焦炭沉积 [10.1002/anie.202003349]。有趣的是，即使在长时间反应后焦炭含量很高（14.4%），沸石催化剂仍保持了大部分微孔体积，这表明孔结构可以在保持催化剂性能的同时容纳焦炭 [10.1002/anie.202003349]。

6. 载体组成：载体的化学性质也影响焦炭形成和催化剂性能。例如，SiO2载体相比Al2O3显示出更好的丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性，这归因于较弱的金属-载体相互作用，允许Pt和Zn之间更好的合金化 [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]。

这些发现对设计最佳和有效的PDH催化剂有重要影响：

1. 平衡孔径以最大化转化率同时最小化焦炭形成至关重要。微孔和介孔在层级结构中的组合可能提供最佳平衡。

2. 开发三维、相互连接的孔网络可以增强反应物-催化剂接触和活性位点的可及性。

3. 引入具有形状选择性的均匀微孔结构可以提高催化剂的选择性和稳定性。

4. 探索结合不同孔径和连通性优势的层级孔排列可以优化传质和焦炭容纳。

5. 考虑孔结构和活性位点分布之间的协同作用，特别是在具有阳离子交换能力的材料中，可以提高整体催化剂性能。

6. 调整载体的化学组成以最小化不期望的金属-载体相互作用并促进所需的合金形成，可以进一步提高催化剂的有效性和寿命。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：对PDH反应中催化剂载体孔结构的全面分析揭示了各种结构特性之间的复杂相互作用及其对催化剂性能的影响。展望未来，研究努力应集中在开发先进的层级孔结构，以优化反应物扩散、活性位点可及性和焦炭容纳之间的平衡。

未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 探索新型合成方法，创造精确控制的层级孔结构，具有量身定制的孔径分布和连通性。

2. 研究结合不同载体类型优势的混合材料，如沸石-介孔二氧化硅复合材料，以利用多个孔径范围的优点。

3. 开发原位表征技术，研究PDH反应过程中孔结构的动态变化，提供焦炭形成和容纳过程的实时洞察。

4. 利用高级计算建模预测特定反应条件和所需性能特征的最佳孔结构。

5. 探索"智能"催化剂载体的潜力，这些载体可以根据反应条件调整其孔结构，可能通过使用刺激响应材料实现。

6. 研究孔结构优化与其他催化剂设计策略（如双金属纳米粒子工程和助剂添加）的协同效应，以开发具有卓越性能和寿命的下一代PDH催化剂。

这些研究方向可能导致PDH催化剂设计的重大进展，潜在地提高过程效率，减少能源消耗，并增强丙烯生产的可持续性。
