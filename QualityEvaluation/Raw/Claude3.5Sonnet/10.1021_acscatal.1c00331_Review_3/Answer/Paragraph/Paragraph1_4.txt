<English>
The characteristics of catalyst support pore structures significantly influence coke formation, accommodation, and overall catalyst performance in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions. This comprehensive review synthesizes findings from multiple studies to elucidate the complex relationships between pore structure and catalyst performance.

Pore Size:
Pore size plays a crucial role in determining catalyst activity, selectivity, and coke formation. Generally, smaller pores lead to higher propane conversion and catalytic activity [10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]. However, the relationship between pore size and coke formation is not linear. While smaller pores can limit coke formation, they are also more susceptible to complete blockage [10.1002/aic.16410]. Studies have shown that coke yield diminishes with decreasing pore size [10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4], but extremely small pores may limit reactant and product diffusion.

Pore Connectivity:
Three-dimensional, interconnected pore networks significantly enhance catalyst performance. Zeolites with three-dimensionally interconnected mesopores have shown improved catalytic performance by facilitating reactant and product diffusion [10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]. Higher pore connectivity can maintain catalyst activity for longer periods by providing alternative pathways when some pores become blocked [10.1002/aic.16410].

Uniformity and Distribution:
Uniform pore size distribution with a relatively large surface area favors the dispersion of metallic particles, enhancing catalyst performance [10.1002/jctb.4686]. Non-uniform pore structures can lead to uneven coke distribution and localized deactivation [10.1002/aic.16410]. However, supports with a wide pore size distribution allow coke to cover the external surface rather than the metallic surface, potentially helping maintain catalyst activity [10.1002/jctb.4686].

Hierarchical Arrangements:
Hierarchical pore structures, combining micropores and mesopores, have shown promising results in PDH catalysts. These structures balance high surface area for active site dispersion with improved mass transfer properties [10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]. The introduction of hierarchical pores in zeolites like ZSM-5 has been shown to reduce support acidity, inhibit coke formation on the metal, and improve the support's capacity for coke accommodation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_3].

Coke Formation and Accommodation:
The pore structure significantly affects the catalyst's ability to form, accommodate, and resist coke. Zeolite supports with ordered microporous structures have demonstrated lower coke formation compared to traditional alumina supports [10.1002/anie.202003349]. Hierarchical pore structures have shown enhanced capacity to accommodate coke while maintaining catalyst performance [10.1002/jctb.4686].

Implications for Catalyst Design:
These findings have important implications for designing optimal PDH catalysts:

1. Balancing pore size to maximize conversion while minimizing coke formation.
2. Utilizing three-dimensional, interconnected pore networks to enhance reactant-catalyst contact and accessibility to active sites.
3. Optimizing pore size distribution to balance metal dispersion, reactant/product diffusion, and coke resistance.
4. Incorporating hierarchical pore structures to combine the benefits of different pore sizes and connectivities.
5. Considering the interplay between pore structure and surface acidity to achieve desired catalytic performance and coke resistance.
6. Designing pore structures that restrict metal particle agglomeration during reactions while facilitating the migration of coke precursors away from active sites.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The comprehensive analysis of catalyst support pore structures in PDH reactions reveals the intricate interplay between various structural characteristics and catalyst performance. Moving forward, research should focus on developing advanced synthesis methods to create tailored hierarchical pore structures with precise control over pore size, connectivity, and distribution.

Future directions may include:

1. Utilizing computational modeling and machine learning to predict optimal pore structures for specific PDH reaction conditions.
2. Exploring novel materials and synthesis techniques to create hybrid supports that combine the benefits of different pore structures.
3. Investigating the potential of dynamic or responsive pore structures that can adapt to changing reaction conditions or coke accumulation.
4. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the evolution of pore structures and coke formation during PDH reactions.
5. Exploring the synergistic effects of pore structure optimization with other catalyst design strategies, such as bimetallic active sites or promoter addition.

By addressing these areas, researchers can work towards developing next-generation PDH catalysts with enhanced activity, selectivity, and longevity, potentially revolutionizing propylene production processes.

<Chinese>
催化剂载体孔结构的特性对丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应中的积碳形成、积碳容纳和整体催化剂性能有显著影响。本综述综合了多项研究的发现，阐明了孔结构与催化剂性能之间的复杂关系。

孔径：
孔径在决定催化剂活性、选择性和积碳形成方面起着关键作用。通常，较小的孔径会导致更高的丙烷转化率和催化活性[10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]。然而，孔径与积碳形成之间的关系并非线性。虽然较小的孔径可以限制积碳形成，但它们也更容易完全堵塞[10.1002/aic.16410]。研究表明，随着孔径的减小，积碳产率降低[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4]，但极小的孔径可能会限制反应物和产物的扩散。

孔连通性：
三维互连的孔网络显著提高了催化剂性能。具有三维互连介孔的沸石已显示出通过促进反应物和产物扩散而提高催化性能[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]。更高的孔连通性可以通过在一些孔被堵塞时提供替代通道来维持催化剂活性更长时间[10.1002/aic.16410]。

均匀性和分布：
具有相对较大比表面积的均匀孔径分布有利于金属颗粒的分散，从而提高催化剂性能[10.1002/jctb.4686]。非均匀的孔结构可能导致不均匀的积碳分布和局部失活[10.1002/aic.16410]。然而，具有宽孔径分布的载体允许积碳覆盖载体的外表面而不是金属表面，这可能有助于维持催化剂活性[10.1002/jctb.4686]。

分级结构：
结合微孔和介孔的分级孔结构在PDH催化剂中显示出良好的效果。这些结构平衡了高比表面积用于活性位点分散和改善的传质性能[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]。在ZSM-5等沸石中引入分级孔结构已被证明可以降低载体酸度，抑制金属上的积碳形成，并提高载体的积碳容纳能力[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_3]。

积碳形成和容纳：
孔结构显著影响催化剂形成、容纳和抵抗积碳的能力。具有有序微孔结构的沸石载体与传统氧化铝载体相比，显示出较低的积碳形成[10.1002/anie.202003349]。分级孔结构显示出增强的积碳容纳能力，同时保持催化剂性能[10.1002/jctb.4686]。

催化剂设计的启示：
这些发现对设计最优PDH催化剂有重要启示：

1. 平衡孔径以最大化转化率，同时最小化积碳形成。
2. 利用三维互连孔网络增强反应物-催化剂接触和活性位点的可及性。
3. 优化孔径分布以平衡金属分散、反应物/产物扩散和抗积碳能力。
4. 纳入分级孔结构，结合不同孔径和连通性的优势。
5. 考虑孔结构与表面酸度之间的相互作用，以实现所需的催化性能和抗积碳能力。
6. 设计可限制反应过程中金属颗粒团聚，同时促进积碳前体远离活性位点迁移的孔结构。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：

对PDH反应中催化剂载体孔结构的全面分析揭示了各种结构特性与催化剂性能之间的复杂相互作用。展望未来，研究应集中于开发先进的合成方法，以创建具有精确控制孔径、连通性和分布的定制分级孔结构。

未来研究方向可能包括：

1. 利用计算机建模和机器学习预测特定PDH反应条件下的最佳孔结构。
2. 探索新型材料和合成技术，创建结合不同孔结构优势的混合载体。
3. 研究可适应变化反应条件或积碳累积的动态或响应性孔结构的潜力。
4. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解PDH反应过程中孔结构和积碳形成的演变。
5. 探索孔结构优化与其他催化剂设计策略（如双金属活性位点或助剂添加）的协同效应。

通过解决这些领域的问题，研究人员可以致力于开发具有增强活性、选择性和寿命的下一代PDH催化剂，有可能革新丙烯生产工艺。
