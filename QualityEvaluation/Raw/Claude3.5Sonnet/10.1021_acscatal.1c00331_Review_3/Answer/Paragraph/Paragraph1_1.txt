<English>
The characteristics of catalyst support pore structures significantly influence coke formation, accommodation, and overall catalyst performance in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions. This review synthesizes findings from multiple studies to elucidate the complex relationships between pore structure and catalyst performance.

Pore size emerges as a crucial factor in determining catalyst activity and coke formation. Generally, smaller pores lead to higher propane conversion and catalytic activity [10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]. However, the relationship between pore size and coke formation is not linear. While smaller pores tend to limit coke yield [10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4], extremely small pores may promote coke formation due to confinement effects [10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]. The optimal pore size balances reactant accessibility, product diffusion, and coke resistance.

Pore connectivity plays a vital role in catalyst performance. Three-dimensional microporous materials, such as zeolites, outperform one-dimensional mesoporous materials in terms of activity, selectivity, and stability [10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]. Interconnected pore networks facilitate better reactant-catalyst interactions and provide improved accessibility to active sites. Higher pore connectivity can potentially maintain catalyst activity for longer periods by providing alternative pathways when some pores become blocked [10.1002/aic.16410].

Uniformity of pore structure contributes to catalyst performance through shape-selectivity effects, influencing product distribution and coke formation [10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]. Non-uniform pore structures can lead to uneven coke distribution, potentially causing localized deactivation [10.1002/aic.16410].

Hierarchical pore arrangements have emerged as a promising strategy to optimize catalyst performance. The introduction of hierarchical pores in zeolites, such as ZSM-5, significantly improves the catalyst's capacity to accommodate coke while maintaining high activity [10.1002/jctb.4686]. These structures typically combine smaller intracrystalline mesopores with larger mesopores or macropores on the surface, enhancing mass transfer and diffusion kinetics [10.1021/acscatal.0c03286].

Coke formation and accommodation are intricately linked to pore structure. While microporous supports generally show better selectivity and stability with lower coke amounts compared to mesoporous supports [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_3], supports with more uniform, highly connected, and larger-sized pores can accommodate more coke, potentially extending catalyst lifetime [10.1002/jctb.4686]. The location of coke deposition is also influenced by pore structure, with most coke found on the outer surfaces of zeolites, suggesting that the internal pore structure helps prevent extensive internal coking [10.1002/anie.202003349].

The interplay between pore structure and metal dispersion is crucial for catalyst performance. Mesoporous structures enable high metal nanoparticle dispersion within the pores, potentially leading to improved catalyst utilization and reduced coke formation [10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]. Hierarchical pore structures can inhibit the agglomeration of metal particles during reactions, contributing to improved catalyst stability [10.1002/jctb.4686].

These findings have important implications for designing optimal PDH catalysts:

1. Develop supports with carefully tailored hierarchical pore structures that balance coke accommodation capacity with catalytic performance.
2. Optimize pore size distribution to achieve an ideal balance between reactant/product diffusion, metal dispersion, and coke resistance.
3. Utilize three-dimensional pore networks to enhance reactant-catalyst contact and accessibility to active sites.
4. Consider the synergy between pore structure and active site distribution, particularly in materials with cation exchange capacity.
5. Design pore structures that restrict metal particle agglomeration during reactions while facilitating the migration of coke precursors away from active sites.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive analysis of catalyst support pore structures in PDH reactions reveals the intricate interplay between various structural characteristics and their impact on catalyst performance. Moving forward, research efforts should focus on developing advanced hierarchical pore structures that can dynamically adapt to changing reaction conditions. This could involve the use of stimuli-responsive materials or in-situ pore modification techniques to optimize catalyst performance throughout its lifecycle.

Future investigations might explore the potential of artificial intelligence and machine learning algorithms to predict optimal pore structures for specific PDH reaction conditions. This could accelerate catalyst design and optimization processes, leading to more efficient and economical PDH technologies.

Additionally, the development of novel characterization techniques that can provide real-time, in-situ information about pore structure changes and coke formation during PDH reactions would be invaluable. Such advancements could lead to a deeper understanding of the dynamic relationships between pore structure, coke formation, and catalyst performance, ultimately guiding the design of next-generation PDH catalysts with unprecedented activity, selectivity, and stability.

<Chinese>
催化剂支撑体孔结构的特性对丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应中的积碳形成、容纳以及催化剂整体性能有显著影响。本综述综合了多项研究的发现，阐明了孔结构与催化剂性能之间的复杂关系。

孔径大小是决定催化剂活性和积碳形成的关键因素。通常，较小的孔径会导致更高的丙烷转化率和催化活性[10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]。然而，孔径大小与积碳形成之间的关系并非线性。虽然较小的孔径往往会限制积碳产量[10.1007/s10562-009-0196-4]，但极小的孔径可能会由于限域效应而促进积碳形成[10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]。最佳孔径应平衡反应物可及性、产物扩散和抗积碳能力。

孔连通性在催化剂性能中起着至关重要的作用。三维微孔材料（如沸石）在活性、选择性和稳定性方面优于一维介孔材料[10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]。相互连接的孔网络有利于反应物与催化剂之间的相互作用，并提供了更好的活性位点可及性。较高的孔连通性可以通过在某些孔被堵塞时提供替代路径，从而可能保持催化剂活性更长时间[10.1002/aic.16410]。

孔结构的均匀性通过形状选择性效应影响产物分布和积碳形成，从而有助于催化剂性能[10.1016/j.micromeso.2009.05.031]。非均匀的孔结构可能导致不均匀的积碳分布，可能造成局部失活[10.1002/aic.16410]。

分级孔结构已成为优化催化剂性能的一种有前景的策略。在沸石（如ZSM-5）中引入分级孔显著提高了催化剂容纳积碳的能力，同时保持高活性[10.1002/jctb.4686]。这些结构通常结合了较小的晶内介孔和表面上较大的介孔或大孔，增强了传质和扩散动力学[10.1021/acscatal.0c03286]。

积碳形成和容纳与孔结构密切相关。虽然微孔支撑体通常与介孔支撑体相比表现出更好的选择性和稳定性，积碳量也更低[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_3]，但具有更均匀、高度连通和较大孔径的支撑体可以容纳更多积碳，可能延长催化剂寿命[10.1002/jctb.4686]。积碳沉积的位置也受孔结构影响，大部分积碳在沸石的外表面上发现，表明内部孔结构有助于防止大量内部积碳[10.1002/anie.202003349]。

孔结构与金属分散之间的相互作用对催化剂性能至关重要。介孔结构能够在孔内实现高金属纳米粒子分散，可能导致催化剂利用率提高和积碳形成减少[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]。分级孔结构可以抑制反应过程中金属粒子的聚集，有助于提高催化剂稳定性[10.1002/jctb.4686]。

这些发现对设计最佳PDH催化剂有重要启示：

1. 开发精心设计的分级孔结构支撑体，平衡积碳容纳能力和催化性能。
2. 优化孔径分布，以实现反应物/产物扩散、金属分散和抗积碳能力之间的理想平衡。
3. 利用三维孔网络增强反应物-催化剂接触和活性位点可及性。
4. 考虑孔结构与活性位点分布之间的协同作用，特别是在具有阳离子交换能力的材料中。
5. 设计孔结构，限制反应过程中金属粒子的聚集，同时促进积碳前体物远离活性位点的迁移。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：对PDH反应中催化剂支撑体孔结构的全面分析揭示了各种结构特性之间的复杂相互作用及其对催化剂性能的影响。展望未来，研究工作应集中于开发可以动态适应变化反应条件的先进分级孔结构。这可能涉及使用刺激响应材料或原位孔修饰技术，以优化催化剂在整个生命周期内的性能。

未来的研究可能会探索人工智能和机器学习算法在预测特定PDH反应条件下最佳孔结构方面的潜力。这可能加速催化剂设计和优化过程，导致更高效和经济的PDH技术。

此外，开发能够提供PDH反应过程中孔结构变化和积碳形成实时、原位信息的新型表征技术将是非常宝贵的。这些进展可能会带来对孔结构、积碳形成和催化剂性能之间动态关系的更深入理解，最终指导设计具有前所未有的活性、选择性和稳定性的下一代PDH催化剂。
