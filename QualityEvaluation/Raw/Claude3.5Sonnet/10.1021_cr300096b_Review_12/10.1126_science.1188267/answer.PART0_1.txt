The literature provides insights into how Pt-Fe bimetallic catalysts influence CO oxidation mechanisms and efficiencies in reactor studies:

1. Pt-Fe catalysts show high activity, selectivity, and robustness for CO oxidation, even under proton-exchange membrane fuel cell (PEMFC) operating conditions.

2. The presence of coordinatively unsaturated ferrous (CUF) sites at the interface between FeO and Pt facilitates O2 activation. O2 can either dissociate directly or adsorb molecularly before dissociation with a low energy barrier.

3. CO oxidation on FeO1-x/Pt(111) surfaces maintains activity even in the presence of H2, as CO poisoning hinders H2 adsorption on Pt.

4. Compared to Pt/SiO2 catalysts, Pt-Fe/SiO2 catalysts demonstrate significantly higher CO conversion and selectivity at lower temperatures.
