The mechanism of improved catalysis involves the creation of coordinatively unsaturated ferrous (CUF) sites at the interface between FeO nanoislands and the Pt substrate. These CUF sites, along with neighboring Pt atoms, activate O2 molecules more effectively than pure Pt surfaces, leading to improved CO oxidation.
