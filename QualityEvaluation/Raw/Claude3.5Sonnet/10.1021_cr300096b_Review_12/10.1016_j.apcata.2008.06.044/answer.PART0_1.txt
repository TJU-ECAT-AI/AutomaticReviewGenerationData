1. Bimetallic catalysts can improve performance by combining functionalities of different metals.

2. They often show synergistic effects beyond the additive contributions of individual metals.

3. For methane partial oxidation (CPO), Ru is a good catalyst but has stability issues and wide hysteresis cycles due to redox reactions.

4. Pt is more stable and resistant to redox reactions while still having CPO activity.

5. Combining Pt and Ru resulted in a bimetallic catalyst with good activity, selectivity, and improved stability compared to monometallic Ru for CPO.
