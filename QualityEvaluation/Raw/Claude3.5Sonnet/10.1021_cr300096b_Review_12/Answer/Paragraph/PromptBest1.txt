Bimetallic Pt-based catalysts significantly influence the mechanisms and efficiencies of CO oxidation, water gas shift reaction, and methane conversions in reactor studies. These catalysts often demonstrate superior performance compared to their monometallic counterparts due to synergistic effects between the metals.

For CO oxidation:
1. Bimetallic catalysts like PtFe, PtNi, and PtCo show improved activity and selectivity compared to monometallic Pt catalysts [10.1126/science.1188267] [10.1016/j.jcat.2009.05.013].
2. The enhanced performance is attributed to a non-competitive dual-site mechanism, where Pt acts as a CO adsorption site and the second metal (e.g., Fe, Ni, Co) serves as an O2 dissociative-adsorption site [10.1007/s10562-008-9513-6].
3. Electronic interactions between Pt and the second metal often lead to weakened CO adsorption on Pt, which enhances the CO oxidation rate [10.1016/j.jcat.2006.08.004].
4. The presence of coordinatively unsaturated ferrous (CUF) sites at the interface between nanostructured ferrous oxides and Pt substrates contributes to high activity and selectivity [10.1126/science.1188267].

For water gas shift reaction (WGS):
1. Pt-based bimetallic catalysts alloyed with Re, Co, or Au show enhanced WGS activity compared to monometallic Pt [10.1021/cr300096b_12].
2. The addition of Re to Pt/CeO2-ZrO2 catalysts enhances the WGS rate, likely due to a bifunctional redox mechanism [10.1016/j.cattod.2004.10.002].
3. PtCu catalysts exhibit higher activity in oxygen-enhanced water gas shift (OWGS) compared to conventional WGS [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.10.014].

For methane conversions:
1. PtNi catalysts show improved activity and stability in dry reforming of methane (DRM) compared to monometallic catalysts [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.05.020].
2. The addition of Pt to Ni catalysts enhances catalyst stability by reducing carbon formation and improving Ni reducibility [10.1016/j.apcata.2010.01.038].
3. PtRu bimetallic catalysts combine Pt's ability to activate methane at low temperatures with Ru's high selectivity towards partial oxidation products [10.1016/j.apcata.2008.06.044].
