B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks exhibit several structural characteristics that enhance catalytic performance in propane dehydrogenation:

1. Porous structure: These materials often feature micro-, meso-, and macroporous structures, providing high surface areas and improved mass transfer properties [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]. For example, ordered macroporous boron phosphate (BPO4) materials show interconnected macropores with average diameters of 200 nm [10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3].

2. Diverse boron species: Multiple types of boron species can be incorporated, including framework trigonal and tetrahedral boron, as well as terminal BOH groups. This diversity contributes to improved catalytic activity and selectivity [10.1039/D0CS01174F].

3. Open coordination: Boron species are often anchored to the support matrix in an open coordination form, allowing for better accessibility of reactants to the active sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017].

4. Defect-rich structures: Incompletely crystallized zeolites with low crystallinity exhibit superior catalytic activity and higher olefin productivity at low temperatures [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017].

5. Uniform dispersion: Active boron species are often uniformly dispersed throughout the support material, ensuring a homogeneous distribution of active sites [10.1126/science.abe7935].

These structural features enhance catalytic performance by:

1. Improving mass transfer and reducing diffusion limitations [10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3].
2. Increasing the number of accessible active sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017].
3. Allowing for dynamic optimization of catalytic activity during the reaction [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017].
4. Suppressing secondary reactions and improving selectivity [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028].

Methods used to incorporate B active components into these structures include:

1. Hydrothermal synthesis: This method is effective for creating borosilicate zeolites with well-dispersed boron species [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021].

2. Incipient wetness impregnation: This technique is used to incorporate boron oxide into carbon nanotubes and other support materials [10.1002/anie.200901826].

3. Chemical vapor deposition (CVD): Used to create thin, uniform layers of hexagonal boron nitride on cordierite supports [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028].

4. Electrospinning: This method creates one-dimensional porous boron-doped silica nanofibers with well-dispersed active sites [10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3].

5. Template-assisted synthesis: Used to create ordered macroporous BPO4 materials with controlled pore structures [10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3].

The effectiveness of these methods varies depending on the specific catalytic application. For propane dehydrogenation, methods that create defect-rich structures or allow for uniform dispersion of boron species tend to be more effective. However, the choice of method should be based on the desired catalyst properties, reaction conditions, and scalability requirements.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The development of B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks represents a significant advance in catalysis for propane dehydrogenation. The ability to control pore structure, active site distribution, and boron species diversity offers unprecedented opportunities for catalyst design and optimization.

Future research directions may include:

1. Exploring novel synthesis methods to create hierarchical pore structures that combine the benefits of micro-, meso-, and macropores for enhanced mass transfer and active site accessibility.

2. Investigating the synergistic effects of different boron species within a single catalyst to optimize both activity and selectivity.

3. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic behavior of boron active sites under reaction conditions, potentially leading to more stable and efficient catalysts.

4. Expanding the application of these materials to other dehydrogenation reactions or related processes, leveraging the unique properties of B-based catalysts.

5. Integrating computational modeling with experimental design to predict and tailor catalyst performance based on structural characteristics and boron species distribution.

These advancements could lead to more efficient and sustainable processes for olefin production, potentially revolutionizing the petrochemical industry and contributing to the development of greener chemical technologies.

具有3-D分散活性位点的B基多孔框架材料在丙烷脱氢方面展现出几种增强催化性能的结构特征：

1. 多孔结构：这些材料通常具有微孔、介孔和大孔结构，提供高比表面积和改善的传质性能[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]。例如，有序大孔硼酸盐（BPO4）材料显示出平均直径为200 nm的互连大孔[10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3]。

2. 多样的硼物种：可以引入多种类型的硼物种，包括骨架三配位和四配位硼，以及端羟基BOH基团。这种多样性有助于提高催化活性和选择性[10.1039/D0CS01174F]。

3. 开放配位：硼物种通常以开放配位形式锚定在支撑基质上，有利于反应物更好地接近活性位点[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017]。

4. 富缺陷结构：结晶度低的不完全结晶沸石表现出优越的催化活性和在低温下更高的烯烃生产率[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017]。

5. 均匀分散：活性硼物种通常均匀分散在整个支撑材料中，确保活性位点的均匀分布[10.1126/science.abe7935]。

这些结构特征通过以下方式增强催化性能：

1. 改善传质并减少扩散限制[10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3]。
2. 增加可接近的活性位点数量[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017]。
3. 允许在反应过程中动态优化催化活性[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017]。
4. 抑制副反应并提高选择性[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028]。

将B活性组分引入这些结构的方法包括：

1. 水热合成：这种方法有效地创建具有良好分散硼物种的硼硅沸石[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021]。

2. 浸渍法：这种技术用于将硼氧化物引入碳纳米管和其他支撑材料[10.1002/anie.200901826]。

3. 化学气相沉积（CVD）：用于在堇青石支撑体上创建薄而均匀的六方氮化硼层[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028]。

4. 电纺丝：这种方法创建具有良好分散活性位点的一维多孔硼掺杂二氧化硅纳米纤维[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3]。

5. 模板辅助合成：用于创建具有可控孔结构的有序大孔BPO4材料[10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3]。

这些方法的有效性取决于具体的催化应用。对于丙烷脱氢，创建富缺陷结构或允许硼物种均匀分散的方法往往更有效。然而，方法的选择应基于所需的催化剂性能、反应条件和可扩展性要求。
