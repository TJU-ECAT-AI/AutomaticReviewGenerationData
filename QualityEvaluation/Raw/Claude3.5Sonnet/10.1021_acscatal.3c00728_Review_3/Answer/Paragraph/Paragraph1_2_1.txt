The structural characteristics of B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks for propane dehydrogenation catalysts include:

1. Hierarchical porous structures: These materials often exhibit a combination of micro-, meso-, and macropores, which enhance mass transfer and accessibility of active sites [10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3].

2. High surface area and pore volume: These features provide abundant sites to accommodate active B species and create confined spaces that influence reaction behavior [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3].

3. Open coordination of boron sites: Boron species are often anchored to the support matrix in an open coordination form, allowing for better accessibility of reactants to the active sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017].

4. Diverse boron species: Multiple types of boron species can be incorporated, including framework trigonal and tetrahedral boron, as well as terminal BOH groups [10.039/D0CS01174F].

5. Uniform dispersion: Boron species are often uniformly dispersed throughout the support matrix, ensuring a homogeneous distribution of active sites [10.126/science.abe7935].

6. Defect-rich structures: Some materials, such as embryonic zeolitic materials, offer abundant SiOH and BOH groups, which contribute to the stabilization of boron species and the formation of active boron centers [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031].

These structural characteristics enhance catalytic performance by:

1. Improving mass transfer and reducing diffusion limitations [10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3].
2. Increasing the number of accessible active sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3].
3. Allowing for dynamic optimization of catalytic activity during the reaction [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017].
4. Providing stability and preventing excessive leaching of boron species [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017].
5. Facilitating the activation of reactants and suppressing undesired side reactions [10.002/cctc.201700004].

Methods used to incorporate B active components into these structures include:

1. Hydrothermal synthesis: This method is effective for creating borosilicate zeolites with well-dispersed boron species [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021].

2. Incipient wetness impregnation: This technique is used to incorporate boron oxide into porous supports like carbon nanotubes [10.002/anie.200901826].

3. Chemical vapor deposition (CVD): This method is effective for creating uniform, thin layers of boron-containing materials on structured supports [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028].

4. Electrospinning: This technique is highly effective for creating porous boron-doped silica nanofibers in a single step [10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3].

5. Template-assisted synthesis: This approach is used to create ordered macroporous boron-containing materials [10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3].

6. One-step template-free synthesis: This method has been used to create unique structures like B2O3@BPO4 sandwich-like hollow spheres [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028].

The effectiveness of these methods varies depending on the specific catalytic application. For propane dehydrogenation, methods that create highly dispersed, accessible boron sites with good thermal stability tend to be most effective. However, the optimal method depends on factors such as desired reaction conditions, selectivity requirements, and scalability considerations.

4. 多样的硼物种：可以包含多种类型的硼物种，包括骨架三配位和四配位硼，以及末端BOH基团 [10.039/D0CS01174F]。

这些结构特征通过以下方式增强催化性能：

1. 改善质量传递并减少扩散限制 [10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3]。
2. 增加可接触的活性位点数量 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]。

4. 提供稳定性并防止硼物种过度流失 [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017]。
5. 促进反应物的活化并抑制不需要的副反应 [10.002/cctc.201700004]。

将B活性组分结合到这些结构中的方法包括：

3. 化学气相沉积（CVD）：这种方法有效地在结构化支撑体上创建均匀、薄层的含硼材料 [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028]。

4. 电纺丝：这种技术在单步中高效地创建多孔硼掺杂二氧化硅纳米纤维 [10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3]。

5. 模板辅助合成：这种方法用于创建有序大孔含硼材料 [10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3]。

6. 一步无模板合成：这种方法已被用于创建独特结构如B2O3@BPO4三明治状空心球 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028]。

这些方法的有效性因具体催化应用而异。对于丙烷脱氢，创建高度分散、易接触且具有良好热稳定性的硼位点的方法往往最有效。然而，最佳方法取决于诸如所需反应条件、选择性要求和可扩展性考虑等因素。
