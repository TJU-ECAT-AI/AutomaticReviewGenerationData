B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks exhibit several structural characteristics that enhance catalytic performance in propane dehydrogenation reactions:

1. High surface area and porous structure: These materials often feature micro-, meso-, and macroporous structures, providing large internal surface areas and facilitating mass transfer [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]. For example, ordered macroporous boron phosphate (BPO4) materials have interconnected macropores with an average diameter of 200 nm, enhancing mass transport and reducing diffusion limitations [10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3].

2. Diverse boron species: Multiple types of boron coordination environments can be present, including trigonal and tetrahedral boron species. This diversity contributes to improved catalytic activity and selectivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021].

3. Open coordination of boron sites: Boron species are often anchored to the framework in open coordination forms, allowing for better accessibility of reactants to active sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017].

4. Defect-rich structures: Incompletely crystallized or embryonic zeolitic materials with defect-rich structures offer abundant SiOH and BOH groups, contributing to the stabilization of boron species and the formation of active boron centers [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031].

5. Uniform dispersion: Boron species can be uniformly dispersed throughout the porous framework, ensuring a homogeneous distribution of active sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017].

These structural features enhance catalytic performance by:

1. Increasing the number of accessible active sites
2. Improving mass transfer of reactants and products
3. Stabilizing boron species and preventing leaching
4. Facilitating the activation of propane and suppressing over-oxidation

Several methods are used to incorporate B active components into these structures:

1. Hydrothermal synthesis: This method is effective for creating borosilicate zeolites with well-dispersed boron species [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021].

2. Electrospinning: This technique creates one-dimensional porous boron-doped silica nanofibers with uniform dispersion of boron species [10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3].

3. Incipient wetness impregnation: This method is used to incorporate boron oxide into carbon nanotubes and other porous supports [10.1002/anie.200901826].

4. Template-assisted synthesis: This approach creates ordered macroporous BPO4 materials with well-dispersed active sites [10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3].

5. Chemical vapor deposition (CVD): This method is effective for creating thin, uniform layers of boron-containing materials on structured supports like cordierite [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028].

The effectiveness of these methods varies depending on the specific catalytic application. For propane dehydrogenation, methods that create defect-rich structures with open coordination boron sites, such as hydrothermal synthesis of embryonic zeolites, have shown promising results [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031]. Electrospinning and template-assisted synthesis have also demonstrated high effectiveness for creating catalysts with improved mass transfer properties and high active site accessibility [10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3] [10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3].

However, the choice of incorporation method should be based on the specific requirements of the catalytic application, considering factors such as desired boron distribution, stability of active sites, and the nature of the support material.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks represents a significant advancement in propane dehydrogenation catalysts. The unique structural characteristics of these materials, such as high surface area, diverse boron species, and defect-rich structures, offer promising opportunities for enhancing catalytic performance.

Looking forward, research efforts could focus on:

1. Optimizing the balance between different types of porosity (micro-, meso-, and macropores) to further improve mass transfer while maintaining high active site density.

2. Developing new synthesis methods or modifying existing ones to create more precise control over the distribution and coordination environment of boron species within the porous framework.

3. Exploring the potential of hybrid materials that combine the advantages of different B-based structures, such as combining zeolitic frameworks with nanofiber or hollow sphere morphologies.

4. Investigating the long-term stability and regeneration strategies for these catalysts under industrial conditions, addressing challenges such as coke formation and thermal degradation.

5. Expanding the application of these B-based materials to other catalytic processes beyond propane dehydrogenation, leveraging their unique structural properties for a wider range of reactions.

By addressing these areas, future research could lead to the development of more efficient, stable, and versatile B-based catalysts, potentially revolutionizing not only propane dehydrogenation but also other important industrial processes.

具有3-D分散活性位点的B基多孔框架材料在丙烷脱氢反应中展现出几种增强催化性能的结构特征：

1. 高比表面积和多孔结构：这些材料通常具有微孔、介孔和大孔结构，提供了大的内部表面积并促进了传质 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]。例如，有序大孔硼酸盐（BPO4）材料具有平均直径为200 nm的互连大孔，增强了传质并减少了扩散限制 [10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3]。

2. 多样的硼物种：可以存在多种硼配位环境，包括三配位和四配位硼物种。这种多样性有助于提高催化活性和选择性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021]。

3. 硼位点的开放配位：硼物种通常以开放配位形式锚定在框架上，使反应物更容易接近活性位点 [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017]。

4. 缺陷丰富的结构：不完全结晶或胚胎沸石材料具有缺陷丰富的结构，提供了大量的SiOH和BOH基团，有助于稳定硼物种并形成活性硼中心 [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031]。

5. 均匀分散：硼物种可以在多孔框架中均匀分散，确保活性位点的同质分布 [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017]。

这些结构特征通过以下方式增强催化性能：

1. 增加可接近的活性位点数量
2. 改善反应物和产物的传质
3. 稳定硼物种并防止流失
4. 促进丙烷的活化并抑制过度氧化

几种方法被用于将B活性组分整合到这些结构中：

1. 水热合成：这种方法对于创建具有良好分散硼物种的硼硅沸石很有效 [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021]。

2. 电纺丝：该技术可创建具有均匀分散硼物种的一维多孔硼掺杂二氧化硅纳米纤维 [10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3]。

3. 浸渍法：这种方法用于将三氧化二硼掺入碳纳米管和其他多孔支撑物中 [10.1002/anie.200901826]。

4. 模板辅助合成：这种方法可创建具有良好分散活性位点的有序大孔BPO4材料 [10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3]。

5. 化学气相沉积（CVD）：这种方法可有效地在堇青石等结构化支撑物上创建均匀的薄层含硼材料 [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028]。

这些方法的有效性因特定的催化应用而异。对于丙烷脱氢，创建具有开放配位硼位点的缺陷丰富结构的方法，如胚胎沸石的水热合成，已显示出有希望的结果 [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.11.031]。电纺丝和模板辅助合成也显示出高效性，可创建具有改善的传质性能和高活性位点可接近性的催化剂 [10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3] [10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3]。

然而，整合方法的选择应基于催化应用的具体要求，考虑诸如所需的硼分布、活性位点的稳定性以及支撑材料的性质等因素。

综合理解与未来展望：具有3-D分散活性位点的B基多孔框架材料的开发代表了丙烷脱氢催化剂的重大进展。这些材料的独特结构特征，如高比表面积、多样的硼物种和缺陷丰富的结构，为提高催化性能提供了有希望的机会。

展望未来，研究努力可以集中在：

1. 优化不同类型孔隙度（微孔、介孔和大孔）之间的平衡，以进一步改善传质，同时保持高活性位点密度。

2. 开发新的合成方法或修改现有方法，以更精确地控制多孔框架中硼物种的分布和配位环境。

3. 探索结合不同B基结构优势的混合材料的潜力，如将沸石框架与纳米纤维或空心球形态结合。

4. 研究这些催化剂在工业条件下的长期稳定性和再生策略，解决焦炭形成和热降解等挑战。

5. 扩大这些B基材料在丙烷脱氢之外的其他催化过程中的应用，利用其独特的结构特性用于更广泛的反应。

通过解决这些领域，未来的研究可能导致更高效、稳定和多用途的B基催化剂的开发，潜在地革新不仅是丙烷脱氢，还包括其他重要的工业过程。
