B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks exhibit several structural characteristics that enhance catalytic performance in propane dehydrogenation:

1. Porous structure: These materials often feature micro-, meso-, and macroporous structures, providing high surface areas and improved mass transfer properties [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]. For instance, ordered macroporous boron phosphate (BPO4) catalysts with interconnected macropores of 200 nm average diameter show excellent performance in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) [10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3].

2. Diverse boron species: Multiple types of boron species can be incorporated, including framework trigonal and tetrahedral boron, as well as terminal BOH groups. This diversity contributes to improved catalytic activity and selectivity [10.1039/D0CS01174F].

3. Open coordination: Boron species are often anchored to the framework in open coordination forms, allowing better accessibility of reactants to active sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017].

4. Defect-rich structures: Incompletely crystallized zeolites with low crystallinity exhibit superior catalytic activity and higher olefin productivity at low temperatures [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017].

5. Uniform dispersion: Active boron species are often uniformly dispersed throughout the support material, ensuring a homogeneous distribution of active sites [10.1126/science.abe7935].

Methods used to incorporate B active components into these structures include:

1. Hydrothermal synthesis: This method is effective for creating borosilicate zeolites with well-dispersed boron species [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021].

2. Incipient wetness impregnation: This technique is used to incorporate boron oxide into carbon nanotubes and other support materials [10.1002/anie.200901826].

3. Chemical vapor deposition (CVD): CVD is effective for creating uniform, thin layers of hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN) on cordierite supports [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028].

4. Electrospinning: This one-step method produces porous boron-doped silica nanofibers with uniform dispersion of boron species [10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3].

5. Template-free synthesis: This approach creates unique structures like B2O3@BPO4 sandwich-like hollow spheres [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028].

The effectiveness of these methods varies depending on the specific catalytic application. For ODHP, methods that create defect-rich structures or allow for uniform dispersion of boron species tend to be more effective. However, the optimal method depends on factors such as desired boron distribution, stability of active sites, and the nature of the support framework.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks represents a significant advancement in propane dehydrogenation catalysts. The ability to tailor pore structures, boron species distribution, and support materials offers unprecedented control over catalyst properties. Future research directions may include:

1. Exploring novel support materials and hierarchical pore structures to further enhance mass transfer and active site accessibility.
2. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic behavior of boron species under reaction conditions.
3. Investigating the synergistic effects between boron and other elements to create multi-functional catalysts with improved performance and stability.
4. Scaling up promising synthesis methods for industrial applications while maintaining the desired structural characteristics and catalytic performance.
5. Applying the insights gained from propane dehydrogenation catalysts to other reactions, potentially expanding the use of B-based materials in various catalytic processes.

These advancements could lead to more efficient and sustainable chemical processes, contributing to the ongoing transition towards greener technologies in the chemical industry.

<Chinese>
具有3-D分散活性位点的B基多孔框架材料在丙烷脱氢中表现出几种提高催化性能的结构特征：

1. 多孔结构：这些材料通常具有微孔、介孔和大孔结构，提供高比表面积和改善的传质性能[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3]。例如，具有200纳米平均直径的互连大孔的有序大孔硼酸盐（BPO4）催化剂在丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）中表现出优异的性能[10.1016/S1872-2067(20)63654-3]。

2. 多样的硼物种：可以引入多种类型的硼物种，包括框架三配位和四配位硼，以及端基BOH基团。这种多样性有助于提高催化活性和选择性[10.1039/D0CS01174F]。

3. 开放配位：硼物种通常以开放配位形式锚定在框架上，允许反应物更好地接近活性位点[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017]。

4. 缺陷丰富结构：结晶度低的不完全结晶沸石表现出优异的催化活性和较低温度下更高的烯烃生产率[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017]。

5. 均匀分散：活性硼物种通常均匀分散在整个支撑材料中，确保活性位点的均匀分布[10.1126/science.abe7935]。

将B活性组分引入这些结构的方法包括：

1. 水热合成：这种方法有效地创建具有良好分散硼物种的硼硅沸石[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021]。

2. 浸渍法：这种技术用于将氧化硼引入碳纳米管和其他支撑材料[10.1002/anie.200901826]。

3. 化学气相沉积（CVD）：CVD有效地在堇青石支撑体上创建均匀、薄层的六方氮化硼（h-BN）[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028]。

4. 电纺丝：这种一步法生产具有均匀分散硼物种的多孔硼掺杂二氧化硅纳米纤维[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3]。

5. 无模板合成：这种方法创建独特的结构，如B2O3@BPO4三明治状中空球[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028]。

这些方法的有效性取决于具体的催化应用。对于ODHP，创建缺陷丰富结构或允许均匀分散硼物种的方法往往更有效。然而，最佳方法取决于诸如所需的硼分布、活性位点的稳定性以及支撑框架的性质等因素。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：具有3-D分散活性位点的B基多孔框架材料的开发代表了丙烷脱氢催化剂的重大进展。能够定制孔结构、硼物种分布和支撑材料，为催化剂性能提供了前所未有的控制。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 探索新型支撑材料和分级孔结构，以进一步增强传质和活性位点的可及性。
2. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解反应条件下硼物种的动态行为。
3. 研究硼与其他元素之间的协同效应，创造具有改进性能和稳定性的多功能催化剂。
4. 扩大有前途的合成方法的工业应用规模，同时保持所需的结构特征和催化性能。
5. 将从丙烷脱氢催化剂获得的见解应用于其他反应，可能扩大B基材料在各种催化过程中的使用。

这些进展可能导致更高效和可持续的化学过程，为化学工业向更绿色技术的持续过渡做出贡献。
