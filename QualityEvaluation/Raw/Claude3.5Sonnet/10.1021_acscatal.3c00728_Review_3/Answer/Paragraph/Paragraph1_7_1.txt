B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks exhibit several structural characteristics that enhance catalytic performance in propane dehydrogenation:

1. Porous structures: These materials often feature hierarchical porosity, including micro-, meso-, and macropores, which improve mass transport and accessibility of active sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_3].

2. High surface area: The porous nature provides large internal surface areas, offering abundant sites for active B species [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021].

3. Uniform dispersion: Boron species are often uniformly distributed throughout the support matrix, ensuring efficient utilization of active sites [10.126/science.abe7935].

4. Open coordination: Boron species can be anchored to the support in open coordination forms, allowing better accessibility to reactants [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017].

5. Defect-rich structures: Incompletely crystallized or defect-rich frameworks can provide superior catalytic activity due to the presence of more active sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017].

6. Thermal stability: Many B-based materials exhibit high thermal stability, crucial for maintaining catalyst performance under reaction conditions [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021].

Methods used to incorporate B active components into these structures include:

1. Hydrothermal synthesis: This method is effective for creating borosilicate zeolites with well-dispersed boron species [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021].

2. Incipient wetness impregnation: Used to incorporate boron oxide onto supports like carbon nanotubes [10.1002/anie.200901826].

3. Chemical vapor deposition (CVD): Effective for creating uniform thin layers of boron nitride on supports like cordierite [10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028].

4. Electrospinning: This technique creates one-dimensional porous boron-doped silica nanofibers with uniformly dispersed boron species [10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3].

5. Template-free synthesis: Used to create unique structures like B2O3@BPO4 sandwich-like hollow spheres [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028].

6. Plasma treatment: While not a direct incorporation method, it can modify the surface of boron nitride to create active defect sites [10.1002/anie.202106713].

The effectiveness of these methods varies depending on the specific catalytic application. For propane dehydrogenation, methods that create defect-rich structures or allow for uniform dispersion of boron species tend to be more effective. For example, incompletely crystallized borosilicate zeolites show superior activity in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017].

However, the relationship between structure and performance is complex. In some cases, isolated boron sites (e.g., in B-MWW zeolites) are found to be inactive for ODHP, suggesting that some degree of B-O-B connectivity may be necessary for catalytic activity [10.1002/anie.201914696].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of B-based materials with 3-D dispersed active sites in porous frameworks represents a significant advance in propane dehydrogenation catalysts. The ability to tailor pore structures, active site distributions, and surface properties offers great potential for optimizing catalytic performance.

Future research directions may include:

1. In-depth investigation of the relationship between boron speciation (e.g., isolated vs. aggregated species) and catalytic activity to guide rational catalyst design.

2. Exploration of novel synthesis methods that combine the advantages of different approaches, such as creating hierarchical pore structures with precisely controlled boron distributions.

3. Development of advanced characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic behavior of boron species under reaction conditions.

4. Integration of computational modeling with experimental studies to predict optimal catalyst structures for specific reactions.

5. Expansion of B-based catalysts to other dehydrogenation reactions or related processes, leveraging the unique properties of these materials.

These advancements could lead to more efficient and selective catalysts for propane dehydrogenation, potentially revolutionizing the production of propylene and other valuable olefins in the petrochemical industry.

3. 均匀分散：硼物种通常均匀分布在整个支撑基质中，确保了活性位点的有效利用[10.126/science.abe7935]。

4. 开放配位：硼物种可以以开放配位形式锚定在支撑体上，允许反应物更好地接近[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017]。

5. 缺陷丰富的结构：不完全结晶或缺陷丰富的框架可以提供优越的催化活性，因为存在更多的活性位点[10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.017]。

6. 热稳定性：许多B基材料表现出高热稳定性，这对于在反应条件下维持催化剂性能至关重要[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021]。

1. 水热合成：这种方法对于创建具有良好分散硼物种的硼硅沸石是有效的[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.03.021]。

2. 浸渍法：用于将氧化硼引入碳纳米管等载体[10.1002/anie.200901826]。

3. 化学气相沉积（CVD）：有效地在堇青石等载体上创建均匀的薄层氮化硼[10.1016/j.cattod.2018.12.028]。

4. 电纺丝：这种技术创建了具有均匀分散硼物种的一维多孔硼掺杂二氧化硅纳米纤维[10.1016/S1872-2067(21)63809-3]。

5. 无模板合成：用于创建独特的结构，如B2O3@BPO4三明治状空心球[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.11.028]。
