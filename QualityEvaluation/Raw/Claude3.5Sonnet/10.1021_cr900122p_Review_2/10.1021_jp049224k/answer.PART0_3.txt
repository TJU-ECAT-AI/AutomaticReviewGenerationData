The literature provides some insights into how metal-based catalysts, specifically zinc-modified zeolites, influence alkane transformations:

1. Composition: The zeolites are modified with Zn2+ ions, either through wet ion exchange or reaction with zinc vapor. This modification changes the catalytic properties of the zeolite.

2. Structure: The zeolites used are ZSM-5 type with particle dimensions of 0.51 mm. The zinc modification replaces acidic protons in the zeolite structure, including those hydrogen-bonded to framework oxygen.

3. Reaction conditions: The studies were conducted at various temperatures and pressures. Room temperature adsorption, heating up to 770 K for modification, and reactions at temperatures between 423 K and 523 K are mentioned.

4. Mechanisms: The zinc-modified zeolites show a unique form of ethane adsorption, characterized by a broad, low-frequency shifted IR band. This indicates strong polarization of the adsorbed ethane by zinc cations. At elevated temperatures, this leads to heterolytic dissociative adsorption and dehydrogenation of ethane.

5. Efficiency: The zinc modification inhibits cracking and increases the rate and selectivity of aromatization compared to unmodified zeolites. The strong adsorption of ethane is relatively stable, requiring prolonged evacuation for removal.

6. Industrial significance: While not directly addressed, the improved selectivity for aromatization and the ability to activate ethane suggest potential industrial applications in alkane transformation processes.
