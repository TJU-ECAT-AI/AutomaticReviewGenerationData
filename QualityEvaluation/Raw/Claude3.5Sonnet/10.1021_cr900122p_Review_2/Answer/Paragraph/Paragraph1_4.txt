<English>
Metal-based catalysts play a crucial role in alkane transformations, with their composition, structure, and reaction conditions significantly influencing the mechanisms and efficiency of these processes. This review focuses on supported vanadium oxides and metal-exchanged zeolites, particularly in oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) and zeolite-catalyzed reactions.

Supported vanadium oxide catalysts are highly effective for alkane ODH due to their unique properties. The composition of these catalysts, particularly the vanadium loading and support material, greatly affects their performance. For V2O5/SiO2 catalysts, optimal activity is observed with vanadium surface coverages below 3 V/nm2, before V2O5 crystallites start to form [10.1021/jp071409e]. The structure of vanadium species changes with loading, with lower loadings (<5 wt%) featuring associated VO4 tetrahedra, while higher loadings have predominant VO6 octahedra [10.1006/jcat.1997.1673].

The reaction mechanism for propane ODH over supported vanadium oxides involves initial hydrogen abstraction by the vanadyl oxygen atom, forming a diradical intermediate (C3H7•·HOVIV(O)3) [10.1021/jp071409e]. This process follows a Mars-van Krevelen redox mechanism involving lattice oxygen in C-H bond activation [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]. The efficiency of these catalysts is highly dependent on reaction conditions, with an optimal temperature window around 500°C [10.1021/cr900122p_2].

Metal-exchanged zeolites, particularly those containing Ga, Zn, or Mo, have shown remarkable activity in alkane aromatization processes. The Cyclar process, using Ga-modified ZSM-5 catalysts, is commercially employed to convert liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) into aromatics [10.1080/01614940701804745]. These catalysts act as multifunctional systems, with metal sites responsible for dehydrogenation and acid sites involved in oligomerization and cyclization [10.1002/cphc.200800569].

The preparation method and activation conditions of metal-exchanged zeolites significantly influence their catalytic performance. For Ga/ZSM-5 catalysts, reduction with hydrogen followed by reoxidation produces a dispersed Ga3+ phase optimal for ethane aromatization [10.1016/0926-860x(92)80336-b]. Similarly, for Mo/HZSM-5 catalysts used in methane dehydroaromatization, an induction period involving the reduction of Mo6+ species to Mo2C is crucial for catalyst activation [10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00210-0].

Reaction conditions play a vital role in determining the efficiency and selectivity of these catalysts. For instance, the presence of water can significantly enhance the activity of Ga/ZSM-5 catalysts in propane dehydrogenation by forming reactive partly hydrolyzed gallium species [10.1002/anie.200702463]. Temperature also greatly affects catalyst performance, with Mo/HZSM-5 catalysts requiring temperatures around 973 K for efficient methane conversion to aromatics [10.1007/bf00767368].

The industrial significance of these metal-based catalysts stems from their ability to efficiently convert low-value alkanes into more valuable products. Supported vanadium oxide catalysts offer high selectivity in ODH processes, while metal-exchanged zeolites enable direct conversion of light alkanes to aromatics. The tunability of these catalysts through composition, structure, and reaction conditions allows for optimization of industrial processes, potentially leading to more economical and sustainable chemical production methods.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The study of metal-based catalysts for alkane transformations has revealed the complex interplay between catalyst composition, structure, and reaction conditions in determining catalytic performance. Future research directions may focus on developing more stable and selective catalysts by exploring novel metal combinations or support materials. The use of advanced characterization techniques and computational modeling could provide deeper insights into reaction mechanisms and guide catalyst design. Additionally, the integration of these catalysts into more environmentally friendly processes, such as utilizing renewable feedstocks or coupling with electrochemical systems, could pave the way for more sustainable chemical production. The potential for in-situ catalyst regeneration and the development of catalysts capable of efficiently activating more recalcitrant alkanes (e.g., methane) under milder conditions remain exciting challenges for future investigations.

<Chinese>
金属基催化剂在烷烃转化中起着至关重要的作用，其组成、结构和反应条件显著影响着这些过程的机理和效率。本综述主要关注负载型钒氧化物和金属交换沸石，特别是在氧化脱氢（ODH）和沸石催化反应中的应用。

负载型钒氧化物催化剂由于其独特的性质，在烷烃ODH反应中表现出高效性。这些催化剂的组成，特别是钒的负载量和载体材料，极大地影响着其性能。对于V2O5/SiO2催化剂，在V2O5晶体开始形成之前，钒表面覆盖度低于3 V/nm2时观察到最佳活性[10.1021/jp071409e]。钒物种的结构随负载量变化，低负载量（<5 wt%）时主要为VO4四面体，而高负载量时则以VO6八面体为主[10.1006/jcat.1997.1673]。

负载型钒氧化物上丙烷ODH的反应机理涉及钒酰氧原子对氢的初始抽提，形成双自由基中间体（C3H7•·HOVIV(O)3）[10.1021/jp071409e]。这个过程遵循Mars-van Krevelen氧化还原机理，涉及晶格氧参与C-H键活化[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。这些催化剂的效率高度依赖于反应条件，最佳温度窗口在500°C左右[10.1021/cr900122p_2]。

金属交换沸石，特别是含有Ga、Zn或Mo的沸石，在烷烃芳构化过程中表现出显著的活性。使用Ga改性ZSM-5催化剂的Cyclar工艺已商业化应用于将液化石油气（LPG）转化为芳烃[10.1080/01614940701804745]。这些催化剂作为多功能系统，金属位点负责脱氢，而酸性位点参与寡聚和环化[10.1002/cphc.200800569]。

金属交换沸石的制备方法和活化条件显著影响其催化性能。对于Ga/ZSM-5催化剂，氢气还原后再氧化可产生分散的Ga3+相，这对乙烷芳构化最为有利[10.1016/0926-860x(92)80336-b]。同样，用于甲烷脱氢芳构化的Mo/HZSM-5催化剂，Mo6+物种还原为Mo2C的诱导期对催化剂活化至关重要[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00210-0]。

反应条件在决定这些催化剂的效率和选择性方面起着至关重要的作用。例如，水的存在可以通过形成反应性部分水解镓物种显著增强Ga/ZSM-5催化剂在丙烷脱氢中的活性[10.1002/anie.200702463]。温度也极大地影响催化剂性能，Mo/HZSM-5催化剂需要约973 K的温度才能有效地将甲烷转化为芳烃[10.1007/bf00767368]。

这些金属基催化剂的工业意义在于它们能够有效地将低价值烷烃转化为更有价值的产品。负载型钒氧化物催化剂在ODH过程中提供高选择性，而金属交换沸石能够直接将轻烷烃转化为芳烃。通过组成、结构和反应条件调节这些催化剂的可调性，使得工业过程的优化成为可能，潜在地导致更经济、更可持续的化学品生产方法。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：对金属基催化剂用于烷烃转化的研究揭示了催化剂组成、结构和反应条件在决定催化性能方面的复杂相互作用。未来的研究方向可能集中在通过探索新型金属组合或载体材料来开发更稳定和选择性的催化剂。使用先进的表征技术和计算建模可能会提供对反应机理更深入的见解，并指导催化剂设计。此外，将这些催化剂整合到更环保的过程中，例如利用可再生原料或与电化学系统耦合，可能为更可持续的化学品生产铺平道路。原位催化剂再生的潜力以及开发能够在更温和条件下有效活化更难处理的烷烃（如甲烷）的催化剂仍然是未来研究的激动人心的挑战。
