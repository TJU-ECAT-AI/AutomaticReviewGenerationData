The composition, structure, and reaction conditions of metal-based catalysts significantly influence the mechanisms and efficiency of alkane transformations:

Composition: Various metal oxides supported on silica, as well as metal-exchanged zeolites like Fe-ZSM-5, are used as catalysts. The Si/Fe ratio in Fe-ZSM-5 affects catalyst performance, with higher iron content generally increasing methane conversion but decreasing selectivity to methanol.

Structure: ZSM-5 zeolites with iron incorporated into the framework are effective catalysts. The form of the zeolite (sodium or hydrogen) impacts activity and selectivity, with Fe-HZSM-5 showing higher methane conversion and Fe-NaZSM-5 demonstrating higher selectivity to methanol.

Reaction conditions: Temperature and contact time significantly affect the reaction. Higher temperatures and longer contact times increase methane conversion but decrease selectivity to methanol. The reaction is typically carried out at atmospheric pressure and temperatures between 350-650°C.

These factors contribute to industrial significance by allowing control over product distribution and reaction efficiency. For example, silica-supported V2O5 and MoO3 are considered most effective for formaldehyde production, while Fe-ZSM-5 can be tuned for methanol or formaldehyde production depending on conditions. The ability to perform reactions at atmospheric pressure and relatively low temperatures also improves the economic viability of these processes.
