The literature discusses molybdenum oxide catalysts supported on silica (MoOx/SiO2) for methane oxidation, rather than vanadium oxides or metal-exchanged zeolites for alkane transformations. However, some insights can be drawn:

1. Composition and structure: Isolated, pentacoordinated molybdate species containing a single MoO bond show the highest activity and selectivity for methane oxidation to formaldehyde.

2. Reaction conditions: The catalyst is operated at high temperatures (873 K) and can be influenced by the presence of water. Low concentrations of H2O enhance catalyst activity.

3. Mechanism: The catalyst is not significantly reduced by methane, suggesting the oxidation mechanism does not involve removal of lattice oxygen. Instead, a peroxide intermediate is proposed.

4. Efficiency: The catalyst shows limited reduction under reaction conditions, with only 50-500 ppm of Mo(VI) reduced to Mo(IV) during steady-state operation.

While this study focuses on a specific catalyst system, it demonstrates how detailed characterization and mechanistic studies can provide insights into the factors influencing catalyst performance for alkane transformations.
