<EnglishVersionAnswer>

The composition, structure, and reaction conditions of metal-based catalysts significantly influence the mechanisms and efficiency of alkane transformations. For zeolite-catalyzed reactions:

1. Metal modification (e.g., with Zn or Ga) enhances the conversion of light alkanes to aromatics with high selectivity.

2. These catalysts have a bifunctional nature: metal species perform dehydrogenation of alkanes, while Brønsted acid sites facilitate oligomerization and cyclization of olefins.

3. The addition of Zn to zeolites affects the activation of C-H bonds in small alkanes:
   - It provides regioselectivity in H/D hydrogen exchange reactions
   - Increases the reaction rate
   - Decreases the activation energy for H/D exchange

4. The mechanism involves the formation of Zn-alkyl species, which then interact with Brønsted acid sites.

These factors contribute to the industrial significance of metal-modified zeolites by improving their catalytic performance in alkane transformation processes. The enhanced activity and selectivity make these catalysts more efficient and potentially more economically viable for industrial applications.

金属基催化剂的成分、结构和反应条件显著影响烷烃转化的机理和效率。对于沸石催化反应：

1. 金属改性（如用Zn或Ga）提高了轻烷烃向芳烃转化的选择性和效率。

2. 这些催化剂具有双功能性：金属物种执行烷烃的脱氢，而布朗斯特酸位点促进烯烃的寡聚和环化。

3. 向沸石中添加Zn影响小烷烃C-H键的活化：
   - 在H/D氢交换反应中提供区域选择性
   - 增加反应速率
   - 降低H/D交换的活化能

4. 其机理涉及Zn-烷基物种的形成，然后与布朗斯特酸位点相互作用。

这些因素通过提高金属改性沸石在烷烃转化过程中的催化性能，提升了它们的工业重要性。增强的活性和选择性使这些催化剂更加高效，在工业应用中可能更具经济可行性。
