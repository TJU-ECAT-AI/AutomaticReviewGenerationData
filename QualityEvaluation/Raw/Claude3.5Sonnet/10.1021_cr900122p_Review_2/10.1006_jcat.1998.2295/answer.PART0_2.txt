<EnglishVersionAnswer>

The composition, structure, and reaction conditions of supported vanadium oxide catalysts significantly influence the mechanisms and efficiency of alkane oxidative dehydrogenation. Key factors include:

1. Support material: The chemical identity and surface area of the support affect the dispersion and structure of VOx species, which in turn impacts catalytic performance.

2. VOx surface density: Higher surface densities lead to the formation of polyvanadate species, which are more active and selective for oxidative dehydrogenation. Lower densities result in exposed support surfaces that promote undesired combustion reactions.

3. Reaction conditions: Kinetic analysis of steady-state reactions in differential flow reactors allows determination of rate constants for primary and secondary reaction pathways.

4. Selectivity challenges: Unselective combustion pathways limit alkene selectivities, especially at high conversions, which is a major factor affecting industrial significance.

The study does not directly address metal-exchanged zeolites or their industrial significance. However, the detailed examination of supported vanadium oxide catalysts provides insights into how catalyst composition and structure influence reaction mechanisms and efficiency in alkane transformations, which could be applicable to other metal-based catalyst systems.

3. 反应条件：在微分流动反应器中对稳态反应进行动力学分析，可以确定主要和次要反应途径的速率常数。

4. 选择性挑战：非选择性燃烧途径限制了烯烃的选择性，特别是在高转化率下，这是影响工业重要性的主要因素。
