2. Metal dispersion: Higher metal dispersion generally leads to improved catalyst performance. The grafting method produces isolated oxo species of molybdenum with large dispersion, which enhances catalytic activity.

3. Oxidation state: The oxidation state of the metal species affects catalyst behavior. In this case, isolated oxo species of molybdenum in oxidation state VI were obtained, which proved beneficial for the reaction.

5. Metal-support interaction: The strength of interaction between the metal species and the support affects catalyst stability and activity. The grafting method creates a strong interaction between the surface oxo-species and the support.

6. Reaction conditions: Factors such as temperature, pressure, and reactant ratios impact catalyst performance and product selectivity.

7. Catalyst structure: The presence of specific structural features, such as tetrahedral dioxomolybdenum species bonded to the support through two oxygen ligands, contributes to catalyst efficiency.

These factors collectively contribute to the industrial significance of metal-based catalysts by improving product yield and selectivity, particularly in valuable processes like the partial oxidation of methane to formaldehyde.
