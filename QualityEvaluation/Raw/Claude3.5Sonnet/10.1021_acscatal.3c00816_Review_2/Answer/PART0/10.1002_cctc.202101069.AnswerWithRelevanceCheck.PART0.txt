      <English>
          The primary synthesis methods for metal-zeolite catalysts used in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions include impregnation and in-situ hydrothermal synthesis. MFI (such as ZSM-5 and silicalite-1) and BEA (Beta) topological zeolites dominate in PDH applications due to several factors:

1. Structural characteristics: MFI and BEA zeolites have three-dimensional pore systems that allow for efficient diffusion of reactants and products while providing shape selectivity.

2. Acidity control: High-silica versions of these zeolites, particularly silicalite-1, offer reduced acidity. This is crucial because strong acidity can lead to undesired side reactions and coke formation. The ability to control acidity by adjusting the Si/Al ratio allows for optimizing catalyst performance.

3. Metal dispersion: These zeolites provide excellent support for dispersing active metal species like ZnO, Cr, and Ga. Their framework structure and surface properties enable the formation of well-dispersed, small metal nanoclusters or isolated metal sites, which are crucial for high activity and selectivity in PDH reactions.

4. Defect sites: The presence of abundant defects, especially silanol nests, in these zeolites acts as anchoring points for metal species, promoting their dispersion and stability.

5. Stability: MFI and BEA zeolites exhibit good thermal and hydrothermal stability, which is essential for maintaining catalyst performance under PDH reaction conditions.

These structural characteristics contribute to the effectiveness of MFI and BEA zeolites in PDH catalysts by providing a balance between metal dispersion, acidity control, and stability. The implications for future developments in PDH catalyst design include:

1. Further optimization of zeolite defect sites to enhance metal-support interactions.
2. Development of synthesis methods to create more uniform and stable metal species.
3. Exploration of other zeolite topologies with similar beneficial characteristics.
4. Investigation of bimetallic or multi-component systems supported on these zeolites.
5. Focus on understanding and enhancing the role of silanol groups in metal dispersion and stability.

          用于丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应的金属-沸石催化剂的主要合成方法包括浸渍法和原位水热合成法。MFI（如ZSM-5和硅铝酸盐-1）和BEA（Beta）拓扑结构的沸石在PDH应用中占主导地位，原因如下：

1. 结构特征：MFI和BEA沸石具有三维孔道系统，可以实现反应物和产物的高效扩散，同时提供形状选择性。

2. 酸度控制：这些沸石的高硅版本，特别是硅铝酸盐-1，提供了较低的酸度。这一点至关重要，因为强酸性可能导致不希望的副反应和积碳形成。通过调整Si/Al比来控制酸度的能力使得可以优化催化剂性能。

3. 金属分散：这些沸石为分散活性金属物种（如ZnO、Cr和Ga）提供了优秀的载体。它们的框架结构和表面特性能够形成分散良好的小金属纳米团簇或孤立金属位点，这对PDH反应的高活性和选择性至关重要。

4. 缺陷位点：这些沸石中存在的大量缺陷，特别是硅醇巢，作为金属物种的锚定点，促进其分散和稳定性。

5. 稳定性：MFI和BEA沸石表现出良好的热稳定性和水热稳定性，这对于在PDH反应条件下维持催化剂性能至关重要。

这些结构特征通过提供金属分散、酸度控制和稳定性之间的平衡，促进了MFI和BEA沸石在PDH催化剂中的有效性。对PDH催化剂设计未来发展的启示包括：

1. 进一步优化沸石缺陷位点，以增强金属-载体相互作用。
2. 开发合成方法，创造更均匀和稳定的金属物种。
3. 探索具有类似有益特性的其他沸石拓扑结构。
4. 研究这些沸石负载的双金属或多组分系统。
5. 重点理解和增强硅醇基在金属分散和稳定性中的作用。
