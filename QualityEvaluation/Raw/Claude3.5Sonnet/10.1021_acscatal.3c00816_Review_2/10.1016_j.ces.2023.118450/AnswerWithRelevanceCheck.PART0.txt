      <English>
          The primary synthesis methods for metal-zeolite catalysts used in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions involve utilizing siliceous zeolites, particularly those with MFI and BEA topologies. These zeolites dominate PDH applications due to their unique structural characteristics:

1. Pore structure: MFI and BEA zeolites have unique pore arrangements that enable better dispersion of metal catalysts and allow for tunable metal-support interactions.

2. High silica content: High-silica versions of these zeolites, such as Beta-557, offer improved thermal and chemical stability under harsh PDH conditions.

3. Silanol nests: These zeolites are rich in silanol groups, which provide strong bonding sites for metal species and help disperse metal clusters inside zeolite channels.

4. Trace aluminum sites: Small amounts of aluminum in the framework help stabilize active metal sites by regulating metal-support interactions.

Synthesis methods include:

1. For MFI zeolites: Creating mesoporous structures rich in silanol nests by eliminating framework elements like Ga from Ga-MFI zeolite.

2. For BEA zeolites: Utilizing high-silica versions to construct stable nanoalloy catalysts.

3. Introducing bimetallic species, such as PtCe, through methods like co-impregnation.

These structural characteristics contribute to the effectiveness of MFI and BEA zeolites in PDH catalysts by:

1. Enabling high dispersion of active metal sites, increasing catalytic activity and stability.
2. Providing a balance between acidity (from trace Al sites) and silanol nests, which helps anchor metal species and prevent sintering.
3. Allowing for the formation of stable bimetallic clusters (e.g., PtCe) within the zeolite framework.

The implications for future developments in PDH catalyst design include:

1. Optimizing the balance between silanol nests and framework Al sites to achieve ideal metal dispersion and stability.
2. Exploring other zeolite topologies with similar beneficial characteristics.
3. Developing synthesis methods to incorporate a wider range of metals, especially rare earth elements, into zeolite frameworks.
4. Further investigating the role of mesoporous structures in enhancing catalyst performance.
5. Continuing research on bimetallic and multimetallic systems supported on these zeolites to improve activity, selectivity, and stability in PDH reactions.

These advancements could lead to more efficient, stable, and selective PDH catalysts, potentially improving the industrial production of propylene.

      <Chinese>
          在丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应中使用的金属-沸石催化剂的主要合成方法涉及利用硅质沸石，特别是具有MFI和BEA拓扑结构的沸石。这些沸石在PDH应用中占主导地位，原因在于它们独特的结构特征：

1. 孔结构：MFI和BEA沸石具有独特的孔道排列，能够更好地分散金属催化剂，并允许可调的金属-载体相互作用。

2. 高硅含量：这些沸石的高硅版本，如Beta-557，在苛刻的PDH条件下提供了改进的热稳定性和化学稳定性。

3. 硅醇巢：这些沸石富含硅醇基团，为金属物种提供了强结合位点，并有助于在沸石通道内分散金属簇。

4. 微量铝位点：骨架中存在少量铝有助于通过调节金属-载体相互作用来稳定活性金属位点。

合成方法包括：

1. 对于MFI沸石：通过消除Ga-MFI沸石中的框架元素（如Ga）来创建富含硅醇巢的介孔结构。

2. 对于BEA沸石：利用高硅版本构建稳定的纳米合金催化剂。

3. 通过共浸渍等方法引入双金属物种，如PtCe。

这些结构特征通过以下方式提高MFI和BEA沸石在PDH催化剂中的有效性：

1. 实现活性金属位点的高度分散，从而提高催化活性和稳定性。
2. 在酸性（来自微量Al位点）和硅醇巢之间提供平衡，有助于锚定金属物种并防止烧结。
3. 允许在沸石骨架内形成稳定的双金属簇（如PtCe）。

对PDH催化剂设计的未来发展的影响包括：

1. 优化硅醇巢和骨架Al位点之间的平衡，以实现理想的金属分散和稳定性。
2. 探索具有类似有益特性的其他沸石拓扑结构。
3. 开发合成方法，将更广泛的金属，特别是稀土元素，纳入沸石框架。
4. 进一步研究介孔结构在提高催化剂性能方面的作用。
5. 继续研究这些沸石上支持的双金属和多金属系统，以提高PDH反应中的活性、选择性和稳定性。

这些进展可能导致更高效、更稳定和更选择性的PDH催化剂，从而可能改善工业丙烯生产。
