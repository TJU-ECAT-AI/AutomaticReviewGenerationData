      <English>
          The primary synthesis method for metal-zeolite catalysts used in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions is the wet impregnation method. This simple technique is used to prepare CrOx supported on high-silica HZSM-5 catalysts.

While the literature does not explicitly state that MFI and BEA topological zeolites dominate in PDH applications, it focuses on the use of HZSM-5 (which has an MFI topology) for this purpose. The effectiveness of HZSM-5 in PDH reactions can be attributed to several structural characteristics and factors:

1. Channel size: ZSM-5 has channels of 0.56 × 0.51 nm, slightly larger than propane molecules (0.47 nm), facilitating propane adsorption and diffusion.

2. High surface area: This provides more active sites for catalytic reactions and metal dispersion.

3. Well-defined channels: The ordered pore structure contributes to uniform distribution of active sites and controlled diffusion of reactants and products.

4. Good thermal stability: Crucial for maintaining catalyst performance under high-temperature PDH conditions.

5. Tunable acidity: The ability to adjust acidity allows for optimization of catalyst performance. Both Lewis and Brønsted acid sites play key roles in catalytic performance.

6. Special environment for metal species: The structure of HZSM-5 provides an ideal environment for the dispersion and stabilization of Cr species, crucial for maintaining high catalytic activity and stability.

These characteristics contribute to the high catalytic activity, good selectivity, and excellent stability observed in CrOx/HZSM-5 catalysts for PDH reactions. The combination of Cr species with Brønsted acid sites in HZSM-5 is particularly responsible for the high catalytic performance, enhancing propane adsorption and C-H bond activation.

Implications for future PDH catalyst design include:

1. Further optimization of zeolite properties (e.g., Si/Al ratio) to balance acidity and stability.
2. Exploration of other zeolite topologies with similar or improved characteristics.
3. Investigation of different metal species or combinations for enhanced PDH performance.
4. Development of synthesis methods to improve metal dispersion and stability within the zeolite structure.
5. Research into minimizing coke formation and extending catalyst lifetime.
6. Deeper studies on metal-zeolite interactions and their impact on catalytic performance.
7. Integration of advanced characterization techniques for better understanding of structure-performance relationships.

          丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应中使用的金属-沸石催化剂的主要合成方法是湿法浸渍法。这种简单的技术用于制备负载在高硅HZSM-5上的CrOx催化剂。

虽然文献没有明确指出MFI和BEA拓扑结构的沸石在PDH应用中占主导地位，但它确实聚焦于使用HZSM-5（具有MFI拓扑结构）用于此目的。HZSM-5在PDH反应中的有效性可归因于几个结构特征和因素：

1. 孔道尺寸：ZSM-5的孔道为0.56 × 0.51 nm，略大于丙烷分子(0.47 nm)，有利于丙烷吸附和扩散。

2. 高比表面积：这为催化反应和金属分散提供了更多的活性位点。

3. 明确定义的孔道：有序的孔结构有助于活性位的均匀分布和反应物及产物的可控扩散。

4. 良好的热稳定性：这对于在PDH高温条件下保持催化剂性能至关重要。

5. 可调酸度：调节酸度的能力允许优化催化剂性能。Lewis酸位和Brønsted酸位在催化性能中都起关键作用。

6. 金属物种的特殊环境：HZSM-5的结构为Cr物种的分散和稳定提供了理想的环境，这对保持高催化活性和稳定性至关重要。

这些特征有助于在PDH反应中观察到的CrOx/HZSM-5催化剂的高催化活性、良好选择性和出色稳定性。HZSM-5中Cr物种与Brønsted酸位的组合特别负责高催化性能，增强了丙烷吸附和C-H键活化。

对PDH催化剂设计未来发展的启示包括：

1. 进一步优化沸石性质（如Si/Al比）以平衡酸度和稳定性。
2. 探索具有类似或改进特性的其他沸石拓扑结构。
3. 研究不同的金属物种或组合以增强PDH性能。
4. 开发合成方法以改善沸石结构中的金属分散和稳定性。
5. 研究最小化积碳形成并延长催化剂寿命的方法。
6. 深入研究金属-沸石相互作用及其对催化性能的影响。
7. 整合先进的表征技术，以更好地理解结构-性能关系。
