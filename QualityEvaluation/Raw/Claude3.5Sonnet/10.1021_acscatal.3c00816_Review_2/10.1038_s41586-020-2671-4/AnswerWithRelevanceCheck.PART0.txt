      <English>
          The primary synthesis method for metal-zeolite catalysts used in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions involves using siliceous MFI zeolites with hierarchical micro-/mesoporous structures. These zeolites are synthesized using multi-ammonium surfactants as structure-directing agents, resulting in ultrathin zeolite frameworks with three-dimensionally interconnected mesopores.

MFI topological zeolites dominate in PDH applications due to their unique structural characteristics:
1. Hierarchical porosity: The combination of micropores and mesopores facilitates reactant and product diffusion.
2. Ultrathin zeolite frameworks: This feature contributes to the overall efficiency of the catalyst.
3. Three-dimensionally interconnected mesopores: This structure enhances catalytic performance by providing both facile reactant and product diffusion and high metal nanoparticle dispersion.
4. High metal dispersion: The mesoporous structure allows for better dispersion of metal nanoparticles.

An important aspect of the synthesis process is the intentional generation of silanol nests. This is achieved by:
1. Synthesizing a mesoporous gallosilicate zeolite
2. Subsequently removing the framework Ga atoms using nitric acid (degallation)

The effectiveness of these zeolites is further enhanced by the formation of intermetallic nanoparticles, such as Pt3La and Pt3Y, on the degallated zeolite surface. These intermetallic compounds significantly improve all aspects of PDH catalytic performance, including activity, selectivity, and durability.

Implications for future developments in PDH catalyst design include:
1. Further optimization of hierarchical pore structures to balance diffusion and metal dispersion.
2. Exploration of other zeolite frameworks that can support intermetallic compound formation.
3. Fine-tuning of silanol nest concentration to optimize catalyst performance.
4. Investigation of other metal combinations that can form effective intermetallic compounds on zeolite supports.
5. Development of more efficient synthesis methods to control zeolite structure and composition precisely.
6. Exploration of the potential of this approach in other catalytic reactions beyond PDH.

These advancements could lead to more active, selective, and durable PDH catalysts, potentially improving the efficiency and economics of propylene production in the petrochemical industry.

      <Chinese>
          用于丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应的金属-沸石催化剂的主要合成方法涉及使用具有分级微孔/介孔结构的硅质MFI沸石。这些沸石使用多铵表面活性剂作为结构导向剂合成，形成超薄沸石骨架和三维互连的介孔。

MFI拓扑结构沸石在PDH应用中占主导地位，这是由于其独特的结构特征：
1. 分级孔隙度：微孔和介孔的组合促进了反应物和产物的扩散。
2. 超薄沸石骨架：这一特征有助于提高催化剂的整体效率。
3. 三维互连的介孔：这种结构通过提供反应物和产物的快速扩散以及高金属纳米粒子分散度来增强催化性能。
4. 高金属分散度：介孔结构允许金属纳米粒子更好地分散。

合成过程的一个重要方面是有意生成硅醇巢。这是通过以下步骤实现的：
1. 合成介孔镓硅沸石
2. 随后用硝酸去除骨架镓原子（脱镓）

这些沸石的效能通过在脱镓沸石表面形成金属间纳米粒子（如Pt3La和Pt3Y）得到进一步提高。这些金属间化合物显著改善了PDH催化性能的各个方面，包括活性、选择性和耐久性。

对PDH催化剂设计未来发展的启示包括：
1. 进一步优化分级孔结构，以平衡扩散和金属分散。
2. 探索可以支持金属间化合物形成的其他沸石骨架。
3. 微调硅醇巢浓度以优化催化剂性能。
4. 研究可以在沸石载体上形成有效金属间化合物的其他金属组合。
5. 开发更有效的合成方法，以精确控制沸石结构和成分。
6. 探索这种方法在PDH以外的其他催化反应中的潜力。

这些进展可能导致更活跃、更选择性和更耐用的PDH催化剂，有可能提高石化工业中丙烯生产的效率和经济性。
