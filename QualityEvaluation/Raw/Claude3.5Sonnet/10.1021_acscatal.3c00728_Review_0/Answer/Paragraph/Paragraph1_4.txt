<English>
Propene's significance in the chemical industry has evolved substantially over recent decades, driven by its versatility as a key building block for numerous valuable products. The increasing demand for propene has outpaced traditional production methods, leading to the adoption of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as a preferred production method.

Propene is widely used in the production of various value-added chemicals and polymer products, including polypropylene, propylene oxide, acrylonitrile, acrylic acid, isopropylbenzene, and butyl alcohol [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_0]. The global demand for propene has been growing at an annual average rate of 5.7% since 1990, with projections indicating continued strong growth [10.1016/j.cattod.2005.07.109]. In 2019, worldwide consumption of propylene reached approximately 115 million tons [10.1039/D0CS01174F].

The increasing demand for propene has driven the adoption of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as a preferred production method due to several factors:

1. Supply gap: Traditional production methods like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) have been unable to meet the growing demand for propene [10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.103].

2. Feedstock availability: The abundance of propane from natural gas and shale gas resources has made PDH an attractive option [10.3390/catal11010128].

3. Targeted production: PDH allows for on-purpose propene production, addressing the supply-demand imbalance more effectively than traditional methods [10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210].

4. Economic viability: PDH is considered more economical compared to traditional methods, especially with increasing oil prices [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.01.018].

5. Environmental considerations: PDH is generally viewed as a more environmentally friendly route compared to conventional processes [10.1039/D0CS01174F].

The economic and environmental advantages of propane dehydrogenation in meeting the growing needs for propene include:

1. Lower energy requirements: Oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH), a variant of PDH, is exothermic and can operate at lower temperatures, potentially reducing energy consumption by up to 45% compared to current production methods [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

2. Improved selectivity: PDH offers higher selectivity towards propylene compared to traditional cracking processes [10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210].

3. Reduced CO2 emissions: PDH typically results in lower CO2 emissions compared to steam cracking [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014].

4. Utilization of CO2: Some PDH processes use CO2 as a co-feed gas, potentially helping to reduce greenhouse gas emissions [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015].

5. Catalyst stability: ODH processes can reduce coking issues, improving catalyst stability and reducing the need for frequent regeneration [10.1039/C8CC04604B].

6. Process simplicity: ODH offers a simpler process compared to traditional methods, potentially reducing capital and operating costs [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.01.018].

However, challenges remain in the widespread adoption of PDH technologies. These include the need for further catalyst development to improve efficiency and selectivity, as well as addressing the endothermic nature of traditional PDH processes [10.1007/s11244-020-01383-z].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of propene's significance in the chemical industry and the adoption of propane dehydrogenation technologies represent a significant shift in how we approach the production of this vital chemical feedstock. Looking forward, several key areas of development and research opportunities emerge:

1. Advanced catalyst design: Future research will likely focus on developing more efficient and stable catalysts for PDH processes, potentially incorporating novel materials or nanotechnology to enhance performance and selectivity.

2. Process intensification: Integrating PDH processes with other chemical production streams or renewable energy sources could lead to more efficient and sustainable production methods.

3. CO2 utilization: Further development of PDH processes that use CO2 as a co-feed could contribute to carbon capture and utilization efforts, aligning with broader sustainability goals.

4. Bioderived feedstocks: Exploring the potential of producing propane or propene from renewable sources could offer a more sustainable long-term solution for meeting propene demand.

5. Artificial intelligence and machine learning: These technologies could be applied to optimize PDH processes, predict catalyst performance, and improve overall efficiency.

6. Modular and distributed production: Developing smaller-scale, modular PDH units could enable more localized production, reducing transportation costs and improving supply chain resilience.

7. Hybrid technologies: Combining PDH with other emerging technologies, such as membrane reactors or microwave-assisted processes, could lead to novel production methods with improved efficiency and selectivity.

As the demand for propene continues to grow and environmental concerns become increasingly pressing, these areas of research and development will be crucial in shaping the future of propene production and its role in the chemical industry.

<Chinese>
近几十年来，丙烯在化工行业的重要性显著提升，这主要是由于其作为众多高价值产品的关键构建块的多功能性。丙烯需求的增长已经超过了传统生产方法的供应能力，导致丙烷脱氢（PDH）成为首选的生产方法。

丙烯广泛用于生产各种高附加值的化学品和聚合物产品，包括聚丙烯、环氧丙烷、丙烯腈、丙烯酸、异丙苯和丁醇[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_0]。自1990年以来，全球丙烯需求以年均5.7%的速度增长，预计将继续保持强劲增长[10.1016/j.cattod.2005.07.109]。2019年，全球丙烯消费量达到约1.15亿吨[10.1039/D0CS01174F]。

丙烯需求的增加推动了丙烷脱氢（PDH）作为首选生产方法的采用，这是由于几个因素：

1. 供应缺口：传统的生产方法如蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解（FCC）无法满足日益增长的丙烯需求[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.103]。

2. 原料可用性：来自天然气和页岩气资源的丙烷丰富，使PDH成为一个有吸引力的选择[10.3390/catal11010128]。

3. 定向生产：PDH允许按需生产丙烯，比传统方法更有效地解决供需不平衡问题[10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210]。

4. 经济可行性：与传统方法相比，PDH被认为更具经济性，特别是在油价上涨的情况下[10.1016/j.cattod.2009.01.018]。

5. 环境考虑：与传统工艺相比，PDH通常被视为更环保的路线[10.1039/D0CS01174F]。

丙烷脱氢在满足日益增长的丙烯需求方面的经济和环境优势包括：

1. 较低的能源需求：氧化脱氢（ODH）是PDH的一种变体，它是放热的，可以在较低的温度下运行，与目前的生产方法相比，可能减少高达45%的能源消耗[10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

2. 提高选择性：与传统裂解工艺相比，PDH对丙烯的选择性更高[10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210]。

3. 减少CO2排放：与蒸汽裂解相比，PDH通常会产生较低的CO2排放[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014]。

4. CO2利用：一些PDH工艺使用CO2作为共进料气，可能有助于减少温室气体排放[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。

5. 催化剂稳定性：ODH工艺可以减少结焦问题，提高催化剂稳定性，减少频繁再生的需求[10.1039/C8CC04604B]。

6. 工艺简单：与传统方法相比，ODH提供了一个更简单的工艺，可能降低资本和运营成本[10.1016/j.cattod.2009.01.018]。

然而，PDH技术的广泛采用仍面临挑战。这些挑战包括需要进一步开发催化剂以提高效率和选择性，以及解决传统PDH工艺的吸热性质[10.1007/s11244-020-01383-z]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：丙烯在化工行业重要性的演变以及丙烷脱氢技术的采用，代表了我们在生产这种重要化学原料方面的重大转变。展望未来，几个关键的发展领域和研究机会浮现：

1. 先进催化剂设计：未来的研究可能会集中在开发更高效、更稳定的PDH工艺催化剂，可能会结合新型材料或纳米技术来提高性能和选择性。

2. 工艺强化：将PDH工艺与其他化学品生产流程或可再生能源源整合，可能会导致更高效和可持续的生产方法。

3. CO2利用：进一步开发使用CO2作为共进料的PDH工艺，可能会为碳捕获和利用努力做出贡献，与更广泛的可持续发展目标保持一致。

4. 生物衍生原料：探索从可再生资源生产丙烷或丙烯的潜力，可能为满足丙烯需求提供更可持续的长期解决方案。

5. 人工智能和机器学习：这些技术可以应用于优化PDH工艺，预测催化剂性能，并提高整体效率。

6. 模块化和分布式生产：开发小型、模块化的PDH装置可以实现更本地化的生产，减少运输成本并提高供应链弹性。

7. 混合技术：将PDH与其他新兴技术结合，如膜反应器或微波辅助工艺，可能会导致效率和选择性更高的新型生产方法。

随着丙烯需求的持续增长和环境问题日益突出，这些研究和开发领域将对塑造丙烯生产的未来及其在化工行业中的角色起到至关重要的作用。
