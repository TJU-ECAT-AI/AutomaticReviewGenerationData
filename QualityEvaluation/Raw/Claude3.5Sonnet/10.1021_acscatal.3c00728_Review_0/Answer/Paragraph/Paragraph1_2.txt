<English>
The significance of propene in the chemical industry has evolved substantially over the past few decades, driven by its increasing demand and versatility as a key building block for numerous products. Propene, also known as propylene, has become one of the most important organic feedstocks in the petrochemical industry, with global production capacity reaching 110-115 million metric tons annually [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330][10.1039/D0CS01174F]. Its applications span a wide range of valuable products, including polypropylene, acrylonitrile, propylene oxide, cumene, phenol, isopropyl alcohol, and many others [10.1016/j.cattod.2005.07.109][10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_0].

The increasing demand for propene has outpaced the supply from traditional sources such as steam crackers and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) units [10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.103]. This supply-demand gap has driven the adoption of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as a preferred production method. PDH, particularly oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH), offers several economic and environmental advantages:

1. Economic viability: PDH is considered more economical compared to traditional methods, especially with rising oil prices [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.01.018]. It allows for the utilization of abundant and cheap propane from natural gas and shale gas resources [10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210].

2. Energy efficiency: ODH is exothermic, requiring lower reaction temperatures and potentially reducing energy consumption by up to 45% compared to current production methods [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

3. Environmental benefits: PDH processes, especially ODH, contribute to minimal environmental pollution due to lower energy demand and high product selectivity [10.1021/acs.iecr.7b00759].

4. Improved catalyst stability: ODH prevents coke deposition on catalyst surfaces, extending catalyst life and reducing regeneration needs [10.1039/C8CC04604B].

5. Higher selectivity: PDH offers relatively higher selectivity towards propylene compared to traditional cracking methods [10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210].

6. Utilization of CO2: Recent developments in using CO2 as a co-feed gas for dehydrogenation offer additional environmental benefits by utilizing this greenhouse gas [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015].

Despite these advantages, challenges remain in the widespread adoption of PDH and ODH technologies. These include optimizing catalyst performance, improving selectivity, and scaling up processes to meet the growing global demand for propene [10.1002/cctc.201700004].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of propene's significance in the chemical industry and the shift towards propane dehydrogenation technologies represent a broader trend in the petrochemical sector towards more sustainable and efficient production methods. As global demand for propene continues to grow, driven by expanding applications in various industries, the need for innovative production technologies becomes increasingly critical.

Future research and development efforts are likely to focus on several key areas:

1. Advanced catalyst design: Developing more efficient and stable catalysts for PDH and ODH processes, potentially incorporating novel materials or nanotechnology to enhance performance and selectivity.

2. Process intensification: Exploring new reactor designs and process configurations to improve energy efficiency and reduce capital costs associated with propene production.

3. Integration with renewable energy sources: Investigating ways to couple PDH processes with renewable energy to further reduce the carbon footprint of propene production.

4. Circular economy approaches: Developing technologies for the recycling and upcycling of propene-based products, creating a more sustainable lifecycle for this versatile olefin.

5. Hybrid technologies: Combining PDH with other emerging technologies, such as membrane separation or electrochemical processes, to create more efficient and flexible production systems.

6. Artificial intelligence and machine learning: Utilizing advanced data analytics and modeling techniques to optimize process conditions and predict catalyst performance in real-time.

As the chemical industry continues to evolve towards more sustainable practices, propene production through advanced dehydrogenation technologies is likely to play a crucial role in meeting the growing global demand while addressing environmental concerns and energy efficiency requirements.

<Chinese>
过去几十年来，丙烯在化工行业的重要性显著提升，这主要由于其需求不断增加以及作为众多产品关键构建块的多功能性。丙烯已成为石油化工行业最重要的有机原料之一，全球年产能达到1.1-1.15亿吨 [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330][10.1039/D0CS01174F]。它的应用范围涵盖了广泛的高价值产品，包括聚丙烯、丙烯腈、环氧丙烷、异丙苯、苯酚、异丙醇等众多产品 [10.1016/j.cattod.2005.07.109][10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_0]。

丙烯需求的增长已经超过了传统来源（如蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂化（FCC）装置）的供应 [10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.103]。这种供需差距推动了丙烷脱氢（PDH）作为首选生产方法的采用。PDH，特别是氧化脱氢（ODH），提供了几个经济和环境优势：

1. 经济可行性：与传统方法相比，PDH被认为更具经济性，特别是在油价上涨的情况下 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.01.018]。它允许利用天然气和页岩气资源中丰富且廉价的丙烷 [10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210]。

2. 能源效率：ODH是放热反应，需要较低的反应温度，与当前生产方法相比，可能减少高达45%的能源消耗 [10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

3. 环境效益：PDH工艺，尤其是ODH，由于能源需求较低和产品选择性高，对环境污染的贡献最小 [10.1021/acs.iecr.7b00759]。

4. 改善催化剂稳定性：ODH防止催化剂表面的积碳沉积，延长催化剂寿命并减少再生需求 [10.1039/C8CC04604B]。

5. 更高的选择性：与传统裂解方法相比，PDH提供了相对更高的丙烯选择性 [10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210]。

6. CO2的利用：最近在使用CO2作为脱氢共进料气方面的发展提供了额外的环境效益，利用了这种温室气体 [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。

尽管存在这些优势，PDH和ODH技术的广泛采用仍面临挑战。这些挑战包括优化催化剂性能、提高选择性以及扩大工艺规模以满足不断增长的全球丙烯需求 [10.1002/cctc.201700004]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：丙烯在化工行业重要性的演变以及向丙烷脱氢技术的转变代表了石油化工领域向更可持续和高效生产方法转变的广泛趋势。随着全球丙烯需求持续增长，受各行业应用扩展的推动，创新生产技术的需求变得越来越关键。

未来的研究和开发努力可能会集中在几个关键领域：

1. 先进催化剂设计：开发更高效和稳定的PDH和ODH工艺催化剂，可能结合新型材料或纳米技术来提高性能和选择性。

2. 工艺强化：探索新的反应器设计和工艺配置，以提高能源效率并降低与丙烯生产相关的资本成本。

3. 与可再生能源的集成：研究将PDH工艺与可再生能源耦合的方法，进一步降低丙烯生产的碳足迹。

4. 循环经济方法：开发基于丙烯产品的回收和升级技术，为这种多功能烯烃创造更可持续的生命周期。

5. 混合技术：将PDH与其他新兴技术（如膜分离或电化学工艺）相结合，创造更高效和灵活的生产系统。

6. 人工智能和机器学习：利用先进的数据分析和建模技术来实时优化工艺条件并预测催化剂性能。

随着化工行业继续向更可持续的实践演变，通过先进脱氢技术生产丙烯可能在满足不断增长的全球需求的同时解决环境问题和能源效率要求方面发挥关键作用。
