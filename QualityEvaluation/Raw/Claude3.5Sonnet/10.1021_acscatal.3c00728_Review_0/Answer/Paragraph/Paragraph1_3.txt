<English>
Propene's significance in the chemical industry has evolved substantially over the past few decades, driven by its versatility as a key building block for numerous valuable products. The demand for propene has been growing rapidly, outpacing even that of ethylene in some regions [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]. This increasing demand has led to a shift in production methods, with propane dehydrogenation (PDH) emerging as a preferred approach to meet the growing needs for this versatile olefin.

The adoption of PDH is driven by several factors:

1. Supply-demand gap: Traditional sources like steam crackers and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) units are unable to meet the growing demand for propene [10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.103].

2. Shale gas revolution: The abundance of propane from shale gas and natural gas liquids has made PDH more economically viable [10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210].

3. On-purpose production: PDH allows for targeted propene production, aligning better with market demands compared to steam cracking, which favors ethylene production [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009].

4. Economic viability: PDH is considered more economically viable compared to traditional methods, especially with increasing oil prices [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.01.018].

5. Environmental considerations: PDH is generally viewed as more environmentally friendly than conventional production methods [10.1039/D0CS01174F].

The economic and environmental advantages of PDH in meeting the growing needs for propene include:

Economic advantages:
1. Lower production costs due to cheaper feedstock, especially in regions with abundant natural gas liquids [10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210].
2. Higher selectivity towards propylene compared to traditional methods [10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210].
3. Potential for lower capital costs due to more focused production [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330].

Environmental advantages:
1. Lower energy requirements compared to steam cracking, potentially reducing carbon emissions [10.1126/science.aaf7885].
2. Utilization of propane from natural gas and refinery off-gases, making the process more sustainable [10.1039/C6CY00078A].
3. Reduced CO2 emissions compared to traditional thermal or catalytic cracking methods [10.1002/anie.201507119].

However, traditional PDH also faces challenges, such as the endothermic nature of the reaction, catalyst deactivation due to coking, and thermodynamic limitations [10.1007/s11244-020-01383-z]. To address these issues, oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) has emerged as a promising alternative, offering additional advantages:

1. Exothermic reaction, reducing energy requirements [10.1039/C8CC04604B].
2. No equilibrium limitations, potentially allowing for higher conversions [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330].
3. Reduced coking, eliminating the need for frequent catalyst regeneration [10.1039/C8CC04604B].

The use of CO2 as a co-feed gas in ODH has also gained attention, offering benefits such as acting as a mild oxidant and utilizing an abundant greenhouse gas [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of propene production methods, particularly the shift towards PDH and ODH, reflects the chemical industry's adaptation to changing market demands, feedstock availability, and environmental concerns. As the demand for propene continues to grow across various applications, future research and development efforts are likely to focus on several key areas:

1. Catalyst optimization: Developing more efficient and stable catalysts for PDH and ODH processes to improve selectivity, reduce energy requirements, and extend catalyst lifespans.

2. Process integration: Exploring innovative ways to integrate PDH and ODH processes with existing petrochemical operations to maximize efficiency and reduce overall environmental impact.

3. CO2 utilization: Further investigating the use of CO2 as a co-feed in dehydrogenation processes, potentially contributing to carbon capture and utilization efforts.

4. Advanced reactor designs: Developing novel reactor configurations to overcome current limitations in PDH and ODH processes, such as improved heat management and catalyst regeneration strategies.

5. Renewable feedstocks: Exploring the potential of bio-based propane sources for dehydrogenation, aligning with the growing trend towards renewable and sustainable chemical production.

6. Techno-economic analysis: Conducting comprehensive studies to assess the long-term viability and competitiveness of PDH and ODH processes in various global markets and under different regulatory scenarios.

7. Life cycle assessment: Performing detailed environmental impact analyses of PDH and ODH processes to ensure their sustainability and identify areas for further improvement.

As the chemical industry continues to evolve, propene production through PDH and ODH is poised to play an increasingly important role in meeting global demand while addressing environmental concerns and resource efficiency.

<Chinese>
丙烯在化学工业中的重要性在过去几十年中发生了重大变化，这主要是由于它作为众多有价值产品的关键构建块的多功能性。丙烯的需求一直在快速增长，在某些地区甚至超过了乙烯[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。这种不断增长的需求导致了生产方法的转变，丙烷脱氢（PDH）成为满足这种多功能烯烃不断增长需求的首选方法。

PDH的采用受到几个因素的推动：

1. 供需缺口：传统来源如蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解（FCC）装置无法满足丙烯不断增长的需求[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.103]。

2. 页岩气革命：来自页岩气和天然气液体的丙烷丰富，使PDH在经济上更加可行[10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210]。

3. 定向生产：与蒸汽裂解相比，PDH允许定向生产丙烯，更好地满足市场需求，而蒸汽裂解更倾向于乙烯生产[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。

4. 经济可行性：特别是随着油价上涨，PDH被认为比传统方法更具经济可行性[10.1016/j.cattod.2009.01.018]。

5. 环境考虑：PDH通常被认为比传统生产方法更环保[10.1039/D0CS01174F]。

PDH在满足丙烯不断增长需求方面的经济和环境优势包括：

经济优势：
1. 由于原料成本较低，特别是在天然气液体丰富的地区，生产成本较低[10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210]。
2. 与传统方法相比，丙烯选择性更高[10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210]。
3. 由于生产更加集中，可能降低资本成本[10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。

环境优势：
1. 与蒸汽裂解相比，能源需求较低，可能减少碳排放[10.1126/science.aaf7885]。
2. 利用来自天然气和炼厂尾气的丙烷，使过程更加可持续[10.1039/C6CY00078A]。
3. 与传统热裂解或催化裂解方法相比，CO2排放量减少[10.1002/anie.201507119]。

然而，传统PDH也面临一些挑战，如反应的吸热性质、由于积碳导致的催化剂失活以及热力学限制[10.1007/s11244-020-01383-z]。为解决这些问题，氧化脱氢（ODH）已成为一种有前景的替代方法，提供了额外的优势：

1. 放热反应，降低能源需求[10.1039/C8CC04604B]。
2. 无平衡限制，可能允许更高的转化率[10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。
3. 减少积碳，消除了频繁催化剂再生的需求[10.1039/C8CC04604B]。

在ODH中使用CO2作为共进料气体也引起了关注，提供了诸如作为温和氧化剂和利用丰富的温室气体等好处[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：丙烯生产方法的演变，特别是向PDH和ODH的转变，反映了化学工业对不断变化的市场需求、原料供应和环境关切的适应。随着丙烯在各种应用中的需求持续增长，未来的研究和开发努力可能会集中在几个关键领域：

1. 催化剂优化：开发更高效和稳定的PDH和ODH过程催化剂，以提高选择性，降低能源需求，并延长催化剂寿命。

2. 过程集成：探索创新方法，将PDH和ODH过程与现有石化业务整合，以最大化效率并减少整体环境影响。

3. CO2利用：进一步研究在脱氢过程中使用CO2作为共进料，可能有助于碳捕获和利用努力。

4. 先进反应器设计：开发新型反应器配置，以克服PDH和ODH过程中的当前限制，如改进热管理和催化剂再生策略。

5. 可再生原料：探索用于脱氢的生物基丙烷来源的潜力，与日益增长的可再生和可持续化学品生产趋势保持一致。

6. 技术经济分析：进行全面研究，评估PDH和ODH过程在不同全球市场和不同监管情景下的长期可行性和竞争力。

7. 生命周期评估：对PDH和ODH过程进行详细的环境影响分析，以确保其可持续性并确定进一步改进的领域。

随着化学工业的不断发展，通过PDH和ODH生产丙烯有望在满足全球需求的同时解决环境问题和资源效率方面发挥越来越重要的作用。
