<English>
Propene's significance in the chemical industry has evolved substantially over the past few decades, driven by its versatility as a key building block for numerous valuable products. The demand for propene has been growing faster than that of ethylene, especially in Western Europe [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]. This increasing demand has led to a shift in production methods, with propane dehydrogenation (PDH) emerging as a preferred approach.

The adoption of PDH, particularly oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH), is driven by several factors:

1. Economic viability: PDH offers a more cost-effective route for propene production, especially with the availability of cheap propane from shale gas and natural gas sources [10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210]. By 2020, it was estimated that approximately 20% of propylene would be produced by on-purpose processes like PDH [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330].

2. Environmental benefits: PDH, especially ODH, is considered more environmentally friendly compared to traditional methods like steam cracking or fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) [10.1039/D0CS01174F]. ODH offers lower energy requirements and reduced CO2 emissions [10.1002/anie.201507119].

3. Process advantages: ODH overcomes limitations of direct dehydrogenation, such as coke formation and thermodynamic constraints. It is exothermic, allowing for lower reaction temperatures and potentially higher conversion rates [10.1039/C8CC04604B].

4. Feedstock flexibility: The abundant availability of propane in natural gas and refinery off-gases makes PDH a sustainable option for propene production [10.1039/C6CY00078A].

5. Market demand: The propylene market is growing faster than the ethylene market, necessitating dedicated production methods like PDH [10.1016/j.cattod.2005.07.109].

However, challenges remain in the widespread adoption of PDH and ODH:

1. Catalyst development: Improving catalyst performance, stability, and selectivity is crucial for enhancing the economic attractiveness of PDH processes [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.01.018].

2. Process optimization: While ODH offers advantages over direct dehydrogenation, issues like overoxidation of propylene to CO2 need to be addressed to improve overall efficiency [10.1002/cctc.201700004].

3. Scale-up: Despite the potential benefits, on-purpose propylene production processes, including PDH, still only cover a small part of the growing propylene demand [10.1016/j.cattod.2005.07.109].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of propene's significance in the chemical industry and the shift towards propane dehydrogenation methods highlight a broader trend in the petrochemical sector towards more sustainable and efficient production processes. As the demand for propene continues to grow, driven by its diverse applications in polymers, oxygenates, and other chemical intermediates, the industry is likely to see further innovations in catalyst design and process engineering for PDH and ODH.

Future research efforts may focus on developing novel catalysts that can further improve selectivity and stability in ODH processes, potentially incorporating advanced materials like metal-organic frameworks or nanostructured composites. Additionally, the integration of PDH processes with renewable energy sources could further enhance their environmental benefits, aligning with global efforts to reduce carbon emissions in industrial processes.

The use of CO2 as a co-feed gas in dehydrogenation processes also presents an intriguing avenue for future development, potentially offering a way to utilize this greenhouse gas productively. This approach could contribute to circular economy initiatives in the chemical industry, turning a waste product into a valuable resource.

As the chemical industry continues to evolve, we may see increased integration of propene production with other chemical processes, creating more efficient and sustainable value chains. This could include the development of modular, small-scale PDH units that can be deployed flexibly to meet local demand, reducing transportation costs and associated emissions.

The ongoing transition towards more sustainable propene production methods also underscores the need for comprehensive life cycle assessments to fully understand the environmental impacts and benefits of these new technologies. This holistic approach will be crucial in guiding future developments and ensuring that innovations in propene production truly contribute to a more sustainable chemical industry.

<Chinese>
在过去的几十年里，丙烯在化学工业中的重要性已经显著提升，这主要是由于它作为众多有价值产品的关键构建块的多功能性。丙烯的需求增长速度快于乙烯，特别是在西欧地区 [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。这种不断增长的需求导致了生产方法的转变，丙烷脱氢（PDH）成为了首选方法。

PDH，特别是氧化脱氢（ODH）的采用，是由几个因素驱动的：

1. 经济可行性：PDH为丙烯生产提供了更具成本效益的途径，尤其是在页岩气和天然气来源的廉价丙烷可用的情况下 [10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210]。据估计，到2020年，约20%的丙烯将由PDH等定向生产工艺生产 [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。

2. 环境效益：与传统的蒸汽裂解或流化催化裂解（FCC）相比，PDH，尤其是ODH被认为更加环保 [10.1039/D0CS01174F]。ODH提供了更低的能源需求和减少的二氧化碳排放 [10.1002/anie.201507119]。

3. 工艺优势：ODH克服了直接脱氢的限制，如焦炭形成和热力学约束。它是放热的，允许较低的反应温度和潜在的更高转化率 [10.1039/C8CC04604B]。

4. 原料灵活性：天然气和炼油厂尾气中丰富的丙烷使PDH成为丙烯生产的可持续选择 [10.1039/C6CY00078A]。

5. 市场需求：丙烯市场的增长速度快于乙烯市场，需要像PDH这样的专门生产方法 [10.1016/j.cattod.2005.07.109]。

然而，PDH和ODH的广泛采用仍面临一些挑战：

1. 催化剂开发：改进催化剂的性能、稳定性和选择性对于提高PDH工艺的经济吸引力至关重要 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.01.018]。

2. 工艺优化：虽然ODH比直接脱氢有优势，但仍需解决丙烯过度氧化成二氧化碳等问题，以提高整体效率 [10.1002/cctc.201700004]。

3. 规模扩大：尽管有潜在的好处，包括PDH在内的定向丙烯生产工艺仍然只能满足不断增长的丙烯需求的一小部分 [10.1016/j.cattod.2005.07.109]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：丙烯在化学工业中重要性的演变以及向丙烷脱氢方法的转变，突出了石油化工行业向更可持续和高效生产工艺转变的更广泛趋势。随着丙烯需求的持续增长，由其在聚合物、含氧化合物和其他化学中间体中的多样化应用推动，该行业可能会在PDH和ODH的催化剂设计和工艺工程方面看到进一步的创新。

未来的研究努力可能会集中在开发新型催化剂上，这些催化剂可以进一步提高ODH工艺的选择性和稳定性，可能会纳入先进材料，如金属有机框架或纳米结构复合材料。此外，将PDH工艺与可再生能源源结合可能会进一步增强其环境效益，与全球减少工业过程碳排放的努力保持一致。

使用二氧化碳作为脱氢过程的共进料气体也为未来的发展提供了一个有趣的途径，可能为生产性地利用这种温室气体提供了一种方法。这种方法可能有助于化学工业的循环经济举措，将废弃物转化为有价值的资源。

随着化学工业的持续发展，我们可能会看到丙烯生产与其他化学过程的更多整合，创造更高效和可持续的价值链。这可能包括开发模块化、小规模的PDH装置，可以灵活部署以满足当地需求，从而减少运输成本和相关排放。

向更可持续的丙烯生产方法的持续过渡也强调了需要全面的生命周期评估，以充分理解这些新技术的环境影响和效益。这种整体方法对于指导未来的发展和确保丙烯生产的创新真正为更可持续的化学工业做出贡献至关重要。
