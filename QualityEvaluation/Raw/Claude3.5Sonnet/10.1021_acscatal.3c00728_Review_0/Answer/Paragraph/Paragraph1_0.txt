<English>
Propene's significance in the chemical industry has evolved substantially over the past few decades, driven by increasing demand across various applications. Initially produced as a byproduct of petroleum refining, propene has become one of the most important building blocks in the petrochemical industry, second only to ethylene [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_0]. Global demand for propene has grown rapidly, with consumption reaching approximately 115 million tons in 2019 [10.1039/D0CS01174F]. This increasing demand is primarily driven by the production of various value-added chemicals and polymer products, including polypropylene, propylene oxide, acrylonitrile, acrylic acid, isopropylbenzene, and butyl alcohol [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_0].

The growing demand for propene, coupled with changes in feedstock availability, has led to a supply gap that traditional production methods such as steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) cannot adequately fill [10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.103]. This shortfall has driven the adoption of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as a preferred production method. PDH offers several economic and environmental advantages:

1. On-purpose production: PDH allows for targeted propene production, aligning better with market demand compared to steam cracking, which favors ethylene production [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009].

2. Feedstock flexibility: PDH can utilize propane from natural gas and shale gas resources, which have become increasingly abundant and cost-effective [10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210].

3. Energy efficiency: While traditional PDH is endothermic, oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) of propane is exothermic, potentially requiring less energy input [10.1039/D0CS01174F].

4. Reduced CO2 emissions: PDH typically results in lower CO2 emissions compared to steam cracking, contributing to a reduced environmental footprint [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009].

5. Improved yield: PDH can offer higher propylene yields compared to FCC, which produces relatively small amounts of propylene [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009].

6. Economic viability: With increasing oil prices, PDH has become more economically attractive, especially when using abundant and low-cost propane feedstock [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.01.018].

However, traditional PDH faces challenges such as coke formation and catalyst deactivation. To address these issues, oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) has emerged as a promising alternative. ODH offers additional advantages, including:

1. Lower reaction temperatures: ODH can proceed at lower temperatures, reducing energy requirements [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_0].

2. Reduced coking: The presence of oxygen in ODH helps prevent coke formation, extending catalyst life [10.1039/C8CC04604B].

3. No equilibrium limitations: This allows for potentially higher conversions [10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330].

4. Potential for CO2 utilization: Using CO2 as a co-feed gas in ODH can act as a mild oxidant, potentially utilizing this greenhouse gas in the process [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015].

The adoption of PDH and ODH for propene production is expected to grow significantly. By 2025, it is forecasted that catalytic dehydrogenation of propane could supply approximately 30% of global propylene production [10.1039/D0CS01174F]. This shift towards more efficient and environmentally friendly production methods reflects the chemical industry's efforts to meet the increasing demand for propene while addressing sustainability concerns.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of propene production methods, particularly the shift towards PDH and ODH, represents a significant adaptation in the chemical industry to meet growing demand while addressing environmental and economic challenges. This trend is likely to continue, with further research and development focusing on improving catalyst performance, process efficiency, and environmental sustainability.

Future research may concentrate on:

1. Advanced catalyst design: Developing catalysts that offer higher selectivity, improved stability, and resistance to coking, potentially incorporating novel materials or nanotechnology.

2. Process intensification: Exploring innovative reactor designs and process configurations to enhance energy efficiency and reduce capital costs.

3. CO2 utilization: Further investigating the use of CO2 as a co-feed in ODH, potentially contributing to carbon capture and utilization efforts.

4. Renewable feedstocks: Exploring the potential for bio-based propane or other renewable sources as feedstocks for PDH/ODH processes.

5. Hybrid technologies: Investigating the integration of PDH/ODH with other processes or renewable energy sources to create more sustainable and efficient production systems.

6. Artificial intelligence and machine learning: Utilizing these technologies for process optimization, predictive maintenance, and catalyst design.

As the demand for propene continues to grow and environmental regulations become more stringent, these research directions could play a crucial role in shaping the future of propene production, potentially leading to more sustainable and economically viable processes in the petrochemical industry.

<Chinese>
过去几十年来，丙烯在化学工业中的重要性显著提升，这主要由其在各种应用领域不断增长的需求所驱动。丙烯最初作为石油精炼的副产品生产，现已成为仅次于乙烯的石油化工行业最重要的基础原料之一[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_0]。全球对丙烯的需求迅速增长，2019年消耗量达到约1.15亿吨[10.1039/D0CS01174F]。这种不断增长的需求主要来自于各种高附加值化学品和聚合物产品的生产，包括聚丙烯、环氧丙烷、丙烯腈、丙烯酸、异丙苯和丁醇[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_0]。

丙烯需求的增长，加上原料供应的变化，导致了传统生产方法如蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂化（FCC）无法充分满足的供应缺口[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.103]。这种供应短缺推动了丙烷脱氢（PDH）作为首选生产方法的采用。PDH提供了几个经济和环境优势：

1. 定向生产：PDH允许定向生产丙烯，与蒸汽裂解更倾向于生产乙烯相比，更好地满足市场需求[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。

2. 原料灵活性：PDH可以利用来自天然气和页岩气资源的丙烷，这些资源变得越来越丰富且具有成本效益[10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210]。

3. 能源效率：虽然传统PDH是吸热反应，但丙烷的氧化脱氢（ODH）是放热反应，可能需要较少的能量输入[10.1039/D0CS01174F]。

4. 减少CO2排放：与蒸汽裂解相比，PDH通常产生较少的CO2排放，有助于减少环境足迹[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。

5. 提高产率：与FCC相比，PDH可以提供更高的丙烯产率，而FCC产生的丙烯量相对较少[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。

6. 经济可行性：随着油价上涨，特别是在使用丰富且低成本的丙烷原料时，PDH变得更具经济吸引力[10.1016/j.cattod.2009.01.018]。

然而，传统PDH面临着焦炭形成和催化剂失活等挑战。为解决这些问题，氧化脱氢（ODH）emerged as a promising alternative。ODH提供了额外的优势，包括：

1. 较低的反应温度：ODH可以在较低的温度下进行，减少能源需求[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_0]。

2. 减少焦炭形成：ODH中氧的存在有助于防止焦炭形成，延长催化剂寿命[10.1039/C8CC04604B]。

3. 无平衡限制：这允许潜在的更高转化率[10.1021/acs.accounts.8b00330]。

4. CO2利用潜力：在ODH中使用CO2作为共进料气可以作为温和的氧化剂，有可能在过程中利用这种温室气体[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。

预计PDH和ODH在丙烯生产中的应用将显著增长。到2025年，预计丙烷催化脱氢可能供应全球约30%的丙烯产量[10.1039/D0CS01174F]。这种向更高效、更环保生产方法的转变反映了化学工业在满足不断增长的丙烯需求的同时，也在努力解决可持续性问题。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：丙烯生产方法的演变，特别是向PDH和ODH的转变，代表了化学工业为满足不断增长的需求同时解决环境和经济挑战所做出的重大调整。这一趋势很可能会持续下去，未来的研究和开发将集中在改进催化剂性能、提高工艺效率和增强环境可持续性方面。

未来的研究可能集中在以下几个方面：

1. 先进催化剂设计：开发提供更高选择性、改善稳定性和抗焦炭性能的催化剂，可能涉及新材料或纳米技术的应用。

2. 工艺强化：探索创新的反应器设计和工艺配置，以提高能源效率并降低资本成本。

3. CO2利用：进一步研究在ODH中使用CO2作为共进料，可能有助于碳捕获和利用工作。

4. 可再生原料：探索生物基丙烷或其他可再生来源作为PDH/ODH过程的原料的潜力。

5. 混合技术：研究PDH/ODH与其他工艺或可再生能源的整合，创造更可持续和高效的生产系统。

6. 人工智能和机器学习：利用这些技术进行工艺优化、预测性维护和催化剂设计。

随着丙烯需求的持续增长和环境法规的日益严格，这些研究方向可能在塑造丙烯生产的未来中发挥关键作用，有望在石油化工行业中形成更可持续和经济可行的工艺。
