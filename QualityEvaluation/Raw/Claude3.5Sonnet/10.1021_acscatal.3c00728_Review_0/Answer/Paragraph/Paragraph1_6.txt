<English>
Propene's significance in the chemical industry has evolved substantially over the past few decades, driven by its versatility as a key building block for numerous valuable products. The demand for propene has been growing faster than that of ethylene, especially in Western Europe, with global consumption reaching 115 million tons in 2019 [10.1039/D0CS01174F]. This increasing demand is primarily due to the expanding use of propene-derived products such as polypropylene, propylene oxide, acrylonitrile, acrylic acid, isopropylbenzene, and butyl alcohol [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_0].

The growing demand for propene has driven the adoption of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as a preferred production method. Traditional sources like steam crackers and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) units are no longer sufficient to meet the increasing market needs [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]. PDH has become more attractive due to several economic and environmental advantages:

1. Targeted production: PDH allows for on-purpose propene production, aligning better with market demand compared to steam cracking, which favors ethylene production [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009].

2. Feedstock flexibility: PDH can utilize propane from natural gas and shale gas resources, which have become increasingly abundant and cost-effective [10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210].

3. Energy efficiency: PDH generally requires less energy than steam cracking, as it doesn't need the high temperatures for cracking larger hydrocarbons [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009].

4. Reduced CO2 emissions: PDH typically results in lower CO2 emissions compared to steam cracking, contributing to a reduced environmental footprint [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009].

5. Improved yield: PDH can offer higher propylene yields compared to FCC, which produces relatively small amounts of propylene [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009].

However, traditional PDH faces some challenges, such as being highly endothermic, requiring high temperatures, and suffering from rapid catalyst deactivation due to coke formation [10.1007/s11244-020-01383-z]. To address these issues, oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) has emerged as a promising alternative, offering additional advantages:

1. Exothermic reaction: ODH is exothermic, potentially requiring less energy input [10.1039/D0CS01174F].

2. Lower operating temperatures: ODH can proceed at lower temperatures, reducing energy costs [10.1039/C8CC04604B].

3. Reduced coking: The presence of oxygen in ODH prevents coke formation, extending catalyst life [10.1039/C8CC04604B].

4. No equilibrium limitations: This allows for potentially higher yields [10.1039/C8CC04604B].

5. Faster reaction rates: The oxidation reaction in ODH is orders of magnitude faster than direct dehydrogenation [10.1039/C8CC04604B].

The adoption of PDH and ODH reflects the chemical industry's efforts to meet the increasing demand for propylene while addressing environmental concerns and energy efficiency requirements. It is estimated that by 2025, catalytic dehydrogenation of propane would supply approximately 30% of the global propylene production [10.1039/D0CS01174F].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of propene production methods, particularly the shift towards PDH and ODH, represents a significant paradigm shift in the chemical industry. This transition not only addresses the growing demand for propene but also aligns with broader sustainability goals and energy efficiency requirements.

Looking forward, research efforts are likely to focus on further improving catalyst performance for PDH and ODH processes. This may include developing novel catalysts with higher selectivity, improved stability, and resistance to coking. Additionally, there is potential for exploring hybrid processes that combine the advantages of different production methods.

The use of CO2 as a co-feed gas in dehydrogenation processes [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015] opens up exciting possibilities for carbon utilization and mitigation of greenhouse gas emissions. Future research could explore the integration of CO2 capture technologies with propene production, creating a more circular and sustainable production cycle.

As the demand for propene continues to grow, there may also be increased interest in developing bio-based routes for propene production, further reducing reliance on fossil fuel feedstocks. This could involve the development of new catalytic processes for converting bio-derived compounds into propene or its precursors.

The ongoing evolution of propene production methods highlights the dynamic nature of the chemical industry and its ability to adapt to changing economic and environmental pressures. As we move towards a more sustainable future, the lessons learned from the development of PDH and ODH technologies may inform the development of cleaner, more efficient processes for other important chemical commodities.

<Chinese>
在过去的几十年里，丙烯在化工行业的重要性显著提升，这主要是由于它作为众多高价值产品的关键构建模块的多功能性。丙烯的需求增长速度超过了乙烯，特别是在西欧，2019年全球消费量达到1.15亿吨[10.1039/D0CS01174F]。这种不断增长的需求主要是由于丙烯衍生产品的使用范围扩大，如聚丙烯、环氧丙烷、丙烯腈、丙烯酸、异丙苯和丁醇[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_0]。

丙烯需求的增长推动了丙烷脱氢（PDH）作为首选生产方法的采用。传统的来源如蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂化（FCC）装置已不足以满足不断增长的市场需求[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。PDH变得更具吸引力，主要是由于以下经济和环境优势：

1. 定向生产：PDH允许有目的的丙烯生产，与蒸汽裂解相比更好地符合市场需求，后者更倾向于乙烯生产[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。

2. 原料灵活性：PDH可以利用来自天然气和页岩气资源的丙烷，这些资源变得越来越丰富且具有成本效益[10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210]。

3. 能源效率：PDH通常比蒸汽裂解需要更少的能源，因为它不需要用于裂解大分子烃的高温[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。

4. 减少CO2排放：与蒸汽裂解相比，PDH通常会产生更少的CO2排放，有助于减少环境足迹[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。

5. 提高产率：与FCC相比，PDH可以提供更高的丙烯产率，FCC产生的丙烯量相对较小[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。

然而，传统的PDH面临一些挑战，如高度吸热、需要高温，以及由于焦炭形成导致催化剂快速失活[10.1007/s11244-020-01383-z]。为了解决这些问题，氧化脱氢（ODH）作为一种有前景的替代方法出现，提供了额外的优势：

1. 放热反应：ODH是放热的，可能需要更少的能量输入[10.1039/D0CS01174F]。

2. 较低的操作温度：ODH可以在较低的温度下进行，减少能源成本[10.1039/C8CC04604B]。

3. 减少结焦：ODH中氧气的存在防止了焦炭形成，延长了催化剂寿命[10.1039/C8CC04604B]。

4. 无平衡限制：这允许潜在的更高产率[10.1039/C8CC04604B]。

5. 更快的反应速率：ODH中的氧化反应比直接脱氢快几个数量级[10.1039/C8CC04604B]。

PDH和ODH的采用反映了化工行业在满足不断增长的丙烯需求的同时，也在解决环境问题和能源效率要求。据估计，到2025年，丙烷催化脱氢将供应全球约30%的丙烯生产[10.1039/D0CS01174F]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：丙烯生产方法的演变，特别是向PDH和ODH的转变，代表了化工行业的重大范式转变。这种转变不仅解决了不断增长的丙烯需求，而且还与更广泛的可持续发展目标和能源效率要求相一致。

展望未来，研究努力可能会集中在进一步提高PDH和ODH过程的催化剂性能上。这可能包括开发具有更高选择性、改进稳定性和抗结焦能力的新型催化剂。此外，还有可能探索结合不同生产方法优势的混合工艺。

在脱氢过程中使用CO2作为共进料气[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]为碳利用和减少温室气体排放开辟了令人兴奋的可能性。未来的研究可能会探索将CO2捕获技术与丙烯生产相结合，创造一个更加循环和可持续的生产周期。

随着丙烯需求的持续增长，对开发生物基丙烯生产路线的兴趣可能也会增加，进一步减少对化石燃料原料的依赖。这可能涉及开发新的催化过程，将生物衍生化合物转化为丙烯或其前体。

丙烯生产方法的持续演变突显了化工行业的动态性质及其适应不断变化的经济和环境压力的能力。随着我们向更可持续的未来迈进，从PDH和ODH技术开发中吸取的经验教训可能会为其他重要化学商品的更清洁、更高效的工艺开发提供启示。
