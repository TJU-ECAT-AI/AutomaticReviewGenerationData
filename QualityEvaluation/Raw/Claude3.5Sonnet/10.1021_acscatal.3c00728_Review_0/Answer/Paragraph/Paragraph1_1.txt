<English>
Propene's significance in the chemical industry has evolved substantially over the past few decades, driven by its versatility as a key building block for numerous valuable products. The demand for propene has been growing faster than that of ethylene, especially in Western Europe, with global consumption reaching 115 million tons in 2019 [10.1039/D0CS01174F]. This increasing demand is primarily due to its use in producing a wide range of petrochemicals, including polypropylene, acrylonitrile, propylene oxide, cumene, phenol, acrylic acid, isopropyl alcohol, and many others [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_0].

The growing demand for propene has outpaced the supply from traditional sources such as steam crackers and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) units [10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.103]. This supply-demand gap has driven the adoption of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as a preferred production method. PDH, particularly oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH), offers several economic and environmental advantages:

1. Economic viability: PDH is considered more economical compared to traditional methods, especially with increasing oil prices [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.01.018]. It allows for the utilization of abundant and cheap propane from natural gas and shale gas resources [10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210].

2. Energy efficiency: ODH is exothermic, potentially requiring less energy input compared to endothermic processes like steam cracking [10.1039/C8CC04604B]. It is estimated that the potential energy savings for moving to ODH could be 45% of the energy consumption [10.1126/science.aaf7885].

3. Environmental benefits: PDH, especially ODH, contributes to minimum environmental pollution due to its low energy demand and high product selectivity [10.1021/acs.iecr.7b00759]. It also offers the potential for reduced CO2 emissions compared to traditional methods [10.1002/anie.201507119].

4. Process advantages: ODH overcomes limitations such as coke formation and equilibrium constraints faced by traditional dehydrogenation methods [10.1039/C8CC04604B]. The use of CO2 as a co-feed gas in some ODH processes provides additional benefits, acting as a mild oxidant and utilizing an abundant greenhouse gas [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015].

5. Targeted production: PDH allows for on-purpose propene production, better aligning with market demands compared to steam cracking, which favors ethylene production [10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009].

The adoption of PDH has been growing rapidly, with its share in global propylene production expected to increase from about 10% currently to approximately 30% by 2025 [10.1039/D0CS01174F]. However, challenges remain, including the need for improved catalysts to enhance selectivity and stability [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of propene production methods, particularly the shift towards propane dehydrogenation, reflects the chemical industry's adaptation to changing feedstock availability, increasing demand, and stricter environmental regulations. As we look to the future, several key areas emerge for potential research and development:

1. Advanced catalyst design: Future research could focus on developing more efficient and stable catalysts for PDH and ODH processes, potentially incorporating nanotechnology or novel materials to enhance selectivity and reduce energy requirements.

2. Process intensification: Innovative reactor designs and process integration techniques could further improve the efficiency and economics of propene production, possibly combining PDH with other processes for synergistic benefits.

3. Renewable feedstocks: As the world moves towards more sustainable practices, exploring the potential of bio-based feedstocks for propene production could become increasingly important, aligning with circular economy principles.

4. CO2 utilization: Building on the use of CO2 as a co-feed in ODH, future research could explore more extensive integration of CO2 utilization in propene production, contributing to carbon capture and utilization efforts.

5. Artificial intelligence and machine learning: These technologies could be leveraged to optimize process conditions, predict catalyst performance, and guide the discovery of new materials for propene production.

6. Life cycle assessment: Comprehensive studies on the environmental impact of different propene production methods across their entire life cycle could guide future policy decisions and industry practices.

As the demand for propene continues to grow and diversify, these research directions could pave the way for more sustainable, efficient, and economically viable production methods, ensuring that this versatile olefin remains a cornerstone of the chemical industry in an increasingly environmentally conscious world.

<Chinese>
在过去的几十年里，丙烯在化学工业中的重要性已经发生了实质性的演变，这主要是由于它作为众多有价值产品的关键构建块的多功能性。丙烯的需求增长速度快于乙烯，特别是在西欧，2019年全球消费量达到1.15亿吨[10.1039/D0CS01174F]。这种不断增长的需求主要是由于它在生产多种石化产品中的应用，包括聚丙烯、丙烯腈、环氧丙烷、异丙苯、苯酚、丙烯酸、异丙醇等[10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_0]。

丙烯不断增长的需求已经超过了传统来源（如蒸汽裂解器和流化催化裂化（FCC）装置）的供应[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.103]。这种供需差距推动了丙烷脱氢（PDH）作为首选生产方法的采用。PDH，特别是氧化脱氢（ODH），提供了几个经济和环境优势：

1. 经济可行性：与传统方法相比，PDH被认为更具经济性，特别是在油价上涨的情况下[10.1016/j.cattod.2009.01.018]。它允许利用丰富且廉价的天然气和页岩气资源中的丙烷[10.1016/j.cej.2018.09.210]。

2. 能源效率：ODH是放热的，与蒸汽裂解等吸热过程相比，可能需要较少的能量输入[10.1039/C8CC04604B]。据估计，转向ODH的潜在能源节约可达能源消耗的45%[10.1126/science.aaf7885]。

3. 环境效益：PDH，特别是ODH，由于其低能耗和高产品选择性，对环境污染的贡献最小[10.1021/acs.iecr.7b00759]。与传统方法相比，它还提供了减少CO2排放的潜力[10.1002/anie.201507119]。

4. 工艺优势：ODH克服了传统脱氢方法面临的焦炭形成和平衡限制等问题[10.1039/C8CC04604B]。在某些ODH过程中使用CO2作为共进料气提供了额外的好处，作为温和的氧化剂并利用了丰富的温室气体[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。

5. 定向生产：PDH允许进行定向丙烯生产，与蒸汽裂解（倾向于生产乙烯）相比，更好地符合市场需求[10.1016/j.cattod.2007.05.009]。

PDH的采用正在迅速增长，预计其在全球丙烯生产中的份额将从目前的约10%增加到2025年的约30%[10.1039/D0CS01174F]。然而，挑战仍然存在，包括需要改进催化剂以提高选择性和稳定性[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：丙烯生产方法的演变，特别是向丙烷脱氢的转变，反映了化学工业对原料可用性变化、需求增加和更严格的环境法规的适应。展望未来，几个关键领域可能成为潜在的研究和发展方向：

1. 先进催化剂设计：未来的研究可能集中在开发更高效和稳定的PDH和ODH过程催化剂上，可能结合纳米技术或新型材料来提高选择性和减少能源需求。

2. 过程强化：创新的反应器设计和过程集成技术可能进一步提高丙烯生产的效率和经济性，可能将PDH与其他过程结合以获得协同效益。

3. 可再生原料：随着世界向更可持续的实践发展，探索生物基原料用于丙烯生产的潜力可能变得越来越重要，符合循环经济原则。

4. CO2利用：在ODH中使用CO2作为共进料的基础上，未来的研究可能探索在丙烯生产中更广泛地整合CO2利用，为碳捕获和利用努力做出贡献。

5. 人工智能和机器学习：这些技术可以用来优化工艺条件，预测催化剂性能，并指导丙烯生产新材料的发现。

6. 生命周期评估：对不同丙烯生产方法在其整个生命周期中的环境影响进行全面研究，可以指导未来的政策决策和行业实践。

随着丙烯需求的持续增长和多样化，这些研究方向可能为更可持续、更高效和经济可行的生产方法铺平道路，确保这种多功能烯烃在日益注重环境的世界中继续成为化学工业的基石。
