Catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) is an important method for propene production, addressing the growing demand for propylene that traditional methods like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking struggle to meet. The process involves the conversion of propane to propene and hydrogen using specific catalysts.

Key considerations for PDH include:

1. Reaction conditions: PDH is highly endothermic (ΔH298 = 124.3 kJ/mol) and requires high temperatures (550-750°C) and low pressures to achieve economically viable conversions [Placeholder_Of_DOI1]. These conditions are necessary due to thermodynamic limitations and to shift the equilibrium towards propene formation.

2. Catalyst types: The most common catalysts are Pt-based and CrOx-based. Pt catalysts offer high activity but suffer from selectivity issues and rapid deactivation due to coke formation. CrOx catalysts show good performance but raise environmental concerns due to the toxicity of Cr(VI) species [Placeholder_Of_DOI2]. Recent research has focused on developing alternative catalysts, such as VOx, Ga2O3, and ZrO2-based materials, to address these limitations.

3. Industrial applications: PDH has become increasingly important for on-purpose propylene production, especially with the availability of low-cost propane from shale gas. Commercial processes like Oleflex (UOP) and Catofin (Lummus) have been widely implemented [Placeholder_Of_DOI3].

Coke formation on Pt-based catalysts significantly impacts process efficiency by causing rapid catalyst deactivation, reducing propylene selectivity, and necessitating frequent regeneration cycles. The extent of coke formation depends on various factors, including catalyst particle size, reaction temperature, and gas composition [Placeholder_Of_DOI4].

Strategies to mitigate coke formation and improve catalyst regeneration include:

1. Catalyst modification: Alloying Pt with elements like Sn, Zn, or In to reduce coke formation and improve stability [Placeholder_Of_DOI5].
2. Optimizing particle size: Larger Pt particles (e.g., 21 nm) show lower coking rates compared to smaller ones (3 nm) [Placeholder_Of_DOI6].
3. Support material selection: Using supports with low Brønsted acidity or modifying them to suppress coke formation [Placeholder_Of_DOI7].
4. Reaction condition optimization: Co-feeding H2 or CO2 to reduce coke precursor formation [Placeholder_Of_DOI8].
5. Advanced regeneration techniques: Implementing continuous catalyst regeneration (CCR) or oxychlorination processes to effectively remove coke and re-disperse Pt particles [Placeholder_Of_DOI9].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of PDH technology has made significant strides in addressing the challenges of coke formation and catalyst stability. Future research directions may focus on:

1. Novel catalyst designs: Exploring single-atom catalysts or intermetallic compounds to maximize atom efficiency and minimize coke formation.
2. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques: Developing methods to study coke formation and catalyst deactivation in real-time under reaction conditions.
3. Machine learning and high-throughput screening: Utilizing computational methods to accelerate the discovery of optimal catalyst compositions and reaction conditions.
4. Process intensification: Investigating membrane reactors or microreactor technologies to enhance propene yield and reduce energy consumption.
5. Sustainable catalysts: Developing catalysts based on earth-abundant elements to reduce reliance on precious metals like Pt.

These advancements could lead to more efficient, stable, and environmentally friendly PDH processes, further solidifying its role in meeting the growing global demand for propylene.

PDH的主要考虑因素包括：

1. 反应条件：PDH是一个高度吸热的反应（ΔH298 = 124.3 kJ/mol），需要高温（550-750°C）和低压才能实现经济可行的转化率[Placeholder_Of_DOI1]。这些条件是由于热力学限制和为了将平衡向丙烯形成方向移动而必需的。

2. 催化剂类型：最常见的催化剂是基于Pt和CrOx的催化剂。Pt催化剂具有高活性，但存在选择性问题和由于积碳而导致的快速失活。CrOx催化剂表现良好，但由于Cr(VI)物种的毒性而引起环境问题[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]。最近的研究集中在开发替代催化剂，如基于VOx、Ga2O3和ZrO2的材料，以解决这些限制。

3. 工业应用：PDH在定向丙烯生产中变得越来越重要，特别是随着页岩气提供的低成本丙烷的可用性。商业化工艺如Oleflex（UOP）和Catofin（Lummus）已被广泛应用[Placeholder_Of_DOI3]。

基于Pt的催化剂上的积碳形成显著影响了工艺效率，导致催化剂快速失活、降低丙烯选择性，并需要频繁的再生循环。积碳形成的程度取决于多种因素，包括催化剂粒径、反应温度和气体组成[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]。

减少积碳形成和改善催化剂再生的策略包括：

1. 催化剂改性：将Pt与Sn、Zn或In等元素合金化，以减少积碳形成并提高稳定性[Placeholder_Of_DOI5]。
2. 优化粒径：较大的Pt粒子（如21 nm）相比较小的粒子（3 nm）表现出较低的积碳速率[Placeholder_Of_DOI6]。
3. 选择适当的载体材料：使用具有低Brønsted酸性的载体或对其进行改性以抑制积碳形成[Placeholder_Of_DOI7]。
4. 优化反应条件：共进料H2或CO2以减少积碳前体的形成[Placeholder_Of_DOI8]。
5. 先进的再生技术：实施连续催化剂再生（CCR）或氧氯化工艺，有效去除积碳并重新分散Pt粒子[Placeholder_Of_DOI9]。

1. 新型催化剂设计：探索单原子催化剂或金属间化合物，以最大化原子效率并最小化积碳形成。
2. 先进的原位表征技术：开发在反应条件下实时研究积碳形成和催化剂失活的方法。
