<English>
Catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) is an important method for propene production, driven by increasing global demand for propylene and the availability of low-cost propane from shale gas. The process involves the conversion of propane to propene and hydrogen using specific catalysts. Key considerations for PDH include:

1. Reaction conditions: PDH is a highly endothermic reaction (ΔH298 = 124.3 kJ/mol) requiring high temperatures (typically 550-750°C) and low pressures to achieve economically viable conversions [10.1039/d0cs00814a]. These conditions are necessary due to thermodynamic limitations and to overcome equilibrium constraints [10.1016/j.catcom.2007.08.015].

2. Catalyst types: The main industrial catalysts are Pt-based and CrOx-based, supported on Al2O3. Pt-based catalysts offer high activity but face challenges with selectivity and coke formation, while CrOx catalysts are cheaper but raise environmental concerns due to Cr(VI) toxicity [10.1039/d0cs01262a]. Recent research has focused on developing alternative catalysts, such as Ga2O3, VOx, and ZrO2-based materials, to address these limitations [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.03.001] [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004] [10.1038/s41467-018-06174-5].

3. Industrial applications: PDH has become increasingly important for on-purpose propylene production, with several commercial processes in operation, including Oleflex (UOP) and Catofin (Lummus) [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]. As of 2000, there were multiple PDH units in commercial operation, with an aggregate capacity of 900,000 MTA polymer-grade propylene [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

Coke formation on Pt-based catalysts significantly impacts process efficiency by causing rapid catalyst deactivation, reducing propylene selectivity, and necessitating frequent regeneration cycles [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]. This deactivation occurs in two stages: quick deactivation followed by a steady state [10.1039/d0cs00983k].

Strategies to mitigate coke formation and improve catalyst regeneration include:

1. Catalyst modification: Alloying Pt with elements like Sn, Zn, or In to improve selectivity and reduce coking [10.1016/j.molcata.2006.10.025]. Novel approaches include developing single-atom catalysts (SACs) or intermetallic compounds to isolate Pt atoms and inhibit structure-sensitive side reactions [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008].

2. Optimizing reaction conditions: Co-feeding H2 or CO2 to suppress coke precursor formation [10.1039/d0cs00814a]. Adding water vapor has also shown to significantly reduce coke formation [10.1002/anie.201701115].

3. Support modification: Using supports with low Brønsted acidity or modifying supports to suppress strong Lewis acidic centers [10.1002/cctc.201900924].

4. Regeneration techniques: Implementing efficient regeneration cycles, often using oxidative treatments followed by reduction. For Pt catalysts, regeneration often involves chlorine-containing compounds to redisperse Pt particles [10.1039/d0cs00983k].

5. Continuous Catalyst Regeneration (CCR) technology: Introduced by UOP, this allows for maintaining stable catalyst activity over extended periods without shutting down the reactor [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

These strategies aim to extend catalyst lifetime, improve selectivity, and enhance overall process efficiency in industrial PDH operations.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of catalytic propane dehydrogenation is rapidly evolving, driven by the need for more efficient and sustainable propylene production. While Pt-based catalysts remain the industry standard, the challenges associated with coke formation and catalyst deactivation have spurred research into novel catalyst designs and process improvements.

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Advanced catalyst design: Developing multi-functional catalysts that combine the high activity of Pt with the coke resistance of other materials. This could involve exploring new intermetallic compounds, single-atom catalysts, or core-shell nanostructures.

2. In-situ regeneration techniques: Investigating methods for continuous or semi-continuous catalyst regeneration that can be integrated into existing reactor designs, potentially reducing downtime and improving overall process efficiency.

3. Alternative feedstocks: Exploring the potential of bio-based propane or other C3 precursors as feedstocks for PDH, aligning with the growing interest in renewable chemical production.

4. Process intensification: Developing novel reactor designs or process configurations that can overcome thermodynamic limitations, potentially allowing for lower operating temperatures or higher per-pass conversions.

5. Computational modeling: Utilizing advanced computational techniques to predict catalyst behavior, optimize formulations, and understand coke formation mechanisms at the molecular level.

6. Sustainable catalyst production: Investigating methods to reduce or eliminate the use of precious metals in PDH catalysts, focusing on abundant and environmentally friendly alternatives.

By addressing these challenges and exploring new frontiers, the PDH process can continue to evolve, potentially becoming an even more significant contributor to global propylene production while improving its environmental footprint and economic viability.

<Chinese>
催化直接丙烷脱氢(PDH)是一种重要的丙烯生产方法，由于全球丙烯需求的增加和页岩气带来的低成本丙烷供应而变得越来越重要。该过程涉及使用特定催化剂将丙烷转化为丙烯和氢气。PDH的主要考虑因素包括：

1. 反应条件：PDH是一种高度吸热反应（ΔH298 = 124.3 kJ/mol），需要高温（通常为550-750°C）和低压以达到经济可行的转化率 [10.1039/d0cs00814a]。这些条件是由于热力学限制和克服平衡约束所必需的 [10.1016/j.catcom.2007.08.015]。

2. 催化剂类型：主要的工业催化剂是基于Pt和CrOx的催化剂，负载在Al2O3上。基于Pt的催化剂活性高，但面临选择性和积碳形成的挑战，而CrOx催化剂较便宜，但由于Cr(VI)的毒性而引起环境问题 [10.1039/d0cs01262a]。近期研究集中在开发替代催化剂，如Ga2O3、VOx和基于ZrO2的材料，以解决这些限制 [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.03.001] [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004] [10.1038/s41467-018-06174-5]。

3. 工业应用：PDH在定向丙烯生产中变得越来越重要，目前有几个商业化过程在运行，包括Oleflex（UOP）和Catofin（Lummus）[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。截至2000年，已有多个PDH装置投入商业运营，总产能达90万吨/年聚合级丙烯 [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

基于Pt催化剂的积碳形成显著影响过程效率，导致催化剂快速失活、丙烯选择性降低，并需要频繁再生循环 [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]。这种失活分为两个阶段：快速失活和稳态阶段 [10.1039/d0cs00983k]。

减少积碳形成和改善催化剂再生的策略包括：

1. 催化剂改性：将Pt与Sn、Zn或In等元素合金化，以提高选择性并减少积碳 [10.1016/j.molcata.2006.10.025]。新方法包括开发单原子催化剂（SACs）或金属间化合物，以隔离Pt原子并抑制结构敏感的副反应 [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]。

2. 优化反应条件：共进料H2或CO2以抑制积碳前体的形成 [10.1039/d0cs00814a]。添加水蒸气也显示出显著减少积碳形成的效果 [10.1002/anie.201701115]。

3. 载体改性：使用具有低Brønsted酸性的载体或改性载体以抑制强Lewis酸性中心 [10.1002/cctc.201900924]。

4. 再生技术：实施高效的再生循环，通常使用氧化处理后还原。对于Pt催化剂，再生通常涉及含氯化合物以重新分散Pt颗粒 [10.1039/d0cs00983k]。

5. 连续催化剂再生（CCR）技术：由UOP引入，这允许在不关闭反应器的情况下长期保持稳定的催化剂活性 [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

这些策略旨在延长催化剂寿命，提高选择性，并提高工业PDH操作的整体过程效率。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：催化丙烷脱氢领域正在快速发展，这是由于需要更高效和更可持续的丙烯生产方法。虽然基于Pt的催化剂仍然是行业标准，但与积碳形成和催化剂失活相关的挑战已经刺激了对新型催化剂设计和工艺改进的研究。

未来研究方向可能集中在：

1. 先进催化剂设计：开发多功能催化剂，结合Pt的高活性和其他材料的抗积碳性能。这可能涉及探索新的金属间化合物、单原子催化剂或核壳纳米结构。

2. 原位再生技术：研究可以集成到现有反应器设计中的连续或半连续催化剂再生方法，可能减少停机时间并提高整体过程效率。

3. 替代原料：探索生物基丙烷或其他C3前体作为PDH原料的潜力，与日益增长的可再生化学品生产兴趣保持一致。

4. 过程强化：开发新型反应器设计或工艺配置，以克服热力学限制，可能允许更低的操作温度或更高的单程转化率。

5. 计算机建模：利用先进的计算技术预测催化剂行为，优化配方，并在分子水平上理解积碳形成机制。

6. 可持续催化剂生产：研究减少或消除PDH催化剂中贵金属使用的方法，专注于丰富和环境友好的替代品。

通过解决这些挑战并探索新的前沿领域，PDH过程可以继续发展，可能成为全球丙烯生产的更重要贡献者，同时改善其环境足迹和经济可行性。
