Catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) is an important method for propene production, driven by increasing global demand for propylene and the availability of low-cost propane from shale gas. This process involves the conversion of propane to propene and hydrogen using specific catalysts under high-temperature conditions.

Key considerations for PDH include:

1. Reaction conditions: PDH is a highly endothermic reaction (ΔH298 = 124.3 kJ/mol) that typically operates at temperatures between 550-750°C and low pressures to achieve high propane conversions [Placeholder_Of_DOI1]. These conditions are necessary due to thermodynamic limitations and to obtain economically viable yields.

2. Catalyst types: The main industrial catalysts are Pt-based and CrOx-based, supported on Al2O3. Pt-based catalysts offer high activity and selectivity but are expensive and prone to deactivation. CrOx-based catalysts are cheaper but raise environmental concerns due to the toxicity of Cr(VI) species [Placeholder_Of_DOI2]. Research is ongoing to develop alternative catalysts, such as gallium oxide, vanadium-based, and zirconia-based catalysts, which show promise in terms of activity, selectivity, and stability [Placeholder_Of_DOI3].

3. Industrial applications: PDH has become a significant on-purpose technique for propylene production, with commercial processes such as Oleflex (UOP) and Catofin (Lummus) being widely implemented [Placeholder_Of_DOI4]. As of 2012, PDH accounted for about 5 million metric tons out of the total 80 million metric tons of global propene production [Placeholder_Of_DOI5].

Coke formation on Pt-based catalysts is a critical challenge in PDH, significantly impacting process efficiency by causing rapid catalyst deactivation and reducing propylene selectivity. The extent of coke formation is influenced by factors such as catalyst particle size, reaction temperature, and feed composition [Placeholder_Of_DOI6].

Strategies to mitigate coke formation and improve catalyst regeneration include:

1. Catalyst design: Developing single-atom catalysts (SACs) or intermetallic compounds to isolate active sites and inhibit undesired side reactions [Placeholder_Of_DOI7]. For example, a PtGa-Pb/SiO2 catalyst with single-atom Pt showed 99.6% propylene selectivity at 600°C for 96 hours without performance degradation.

2. Alloying Pt with other metals: Adding elements like Sn, Zn, or In can improve catalytic performance and suppress carbon formation [Placeholder_Of_DOI8]. The optimal In/Pt ratio of 0.48 has shown maximum activity and nearly 100% selectivity to alkenes.

3. Modifying reaction conditions: Co-feeding H2 or CO2 can reduce coke formation by suppressing the formation of deeply dehydrogenated coke precursors [Placeholder_Of_DOI9]. CO2 acts as a soft oxidant, promoting the reverse Boudouard reaction to remove carbon deposits.

4. Optimizing regeneration processes: Implementing continuous catalyst regeneration (CCR) technology or using oxidative regeneration followed by H2 reduction can effectively remove coke and restore catalyst activity [Placeholder_Of_DOI10]. In the Catofin process, the catalyst is typically regenerated after only 12 minutes of dehydrogenation at 575°C.

5. Support modification: Using supports with low Brønsted acidity or introducing promoters like phosphate can help suppress coke formation and improve catalyst stability [Placeholder_Of_DOI11].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The ongoing research in PDH catalysts reveals a trend towards developing more efficient, stable, and environmentally friendly materials. The emergence of single-atom catalysts and intermetallic compounds represents a significant advancement in addressing the challenges of coke formation and catalyst deactivation. Future research efforts might focus on:

1. Exploring novel catalyst compositions that combine the benefits of different elements to achieve optimal performance and stability.
2. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic processes occurring during PDH reactions, enabling more precise catalyst design.
3. Investigating the potential of machine learning and artificial intelligence in predicting catalyst behavior and optimizing reaction conditions.
4. Integrating PDH processes with renewable energy sources to improve overall sustainability and reduce carbon footprint.
5. Exploring the possibilities of plasma-assisted or microwave-assisted PDH to enhance energy efficiency and potentially operate at lower temperatures.

These advancements could lead to more economically viable and environmentally sustainable PDH processes, contributing to the growing demand for propylene in the chemical industry.

催化丙烷直接脱氢（PDH）是生产丙烯的重要方法，这一方法的发展主要由于全球丙烯需求的增加以及页岩气中低成本丙烷的可用性。该过程涉及在高温条件下使用特定催化剂将丙烷转化为丙烯和氢气。

PDH的关键考虑因素包括：

1. 反应条件：PDH是一个高度吸热的反应（ΔH298 = 124.3 kJ/mol），通常在550-750°C的高温和低压下进行，以实现高丙烷转化率[Placeholder_Of_DOI1]。这些条件是由于热力学限制和获得经济可行收率所必需的。

2. 催化剂类型：主要的工业催化剂是基于Pt和CrOx的，通常负载在Al2O3上。基于Pt的催化剂具有高活性和选择性，但价格昂贵且容易失活。基于CrOx的催化剂较便宜，但由于Cr(VI)物种的毒性而引起环境问题[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]。目前正在研究开发替代催化剂，如氧化镓、钒基和氧化锆基催化剂，这些催化剂在活性、选择性和稳定性方面显示出良好前景[Placeholder_Of_DOI3]。

3. 工业应用：PDH已成为丙烯定向生产的重要技术，商业化工艺如Oleflex（UOP）和Catofin（Lummus）被广泛应用[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]。截至2012年，PDH占全球丙烯总产量（8000万公吨）中的约500万公吨[Placeholder_Of_DOI5]。

在基于Pt的催化剂上的积碳形成是PDH中的一个关键挑战，它通过导致催化剂快速失活和降低丙烯选择性而显著影响工艺效率。积碳形成的程度受催化剂粒径、反应温度和进料组成等因素的影响[Placeholder_Of_DOI6]。

减少积碳形成和改善催化剂再生的策略包括：

1. 催化剂设计：开发单原子催化剂（SACs）或金属间化合物以隔离活性位点并抑制不良副反应[Placeholder_Of_DOI7]。例如，一种具有单原子Pt的PtGa-Pb/SiO2催化剂在600°C下运行96小时，丙烯选择性达到99.6%，且性能未降低。

2. 将Pt与其他金属合金化：添加Sn、Zn或In等元素可以提高催化性能并抑制碳形成[Placeholder_Of_DOI8]。最佳In/Pt比率为0.48时，显示出最大活性和接近100%的烯烃选择性。

3. 改变反应条件：共进料H2或CO2可以通过抑制深度脱氢的积碳前体的形成来减少积碳[Placeholder_Of_DOI9]。CO2作为一种温和的氧化剂，促进反布德瓦反应以去除碳沉积。

4. 优化再生过程：实施连续催化剂再生（CCR）技术或使用氧化再生后进行H2还原可以有效地去除积碳并恢复催化剂活性[Placeholder_Of_DOI10]。在Catofin工艺中，催化剂通常在575°C下脱氢仅12分钟后就需要再生。

5. 载体改性：使用低布朗斯特酸性的载体或引入磷酸盐等助剂可以帮助抑制积碳形成并提高催化剂稳定性[Placeholder_Of_DOI11]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：PDH催化剂的持续研究显示出开发更高效、更稳定和更环保材料的趋势。单原子催化剂和金属间化合物的出现代表了解决积碳形成和催化剂失活挑战的重大进展。未来的研究努力可能会集中在：

1. 探索新型催化剂组成，结合不同元素的优点，以实现最佳性能和稳定性。
2. 开发原位表征技术，更好地理解PDH反应过程中发生的动态过程，从而实现更精确的催化剂设计。
3. 研究机器学习和人工智能在预测催化剂行为和优化反应条件方面的潜力。
4. 将PDH工艺与可再生能源源相结合，以提高整体可持续性并减少碳足迹。
5. 探索等离子体辅助或微波辅助PDH的可能性，以提高能源效率并可能在较低温度下操作。

这些进展可能会导致更具经济可行性和环境可持续性的PDH工艺，为化学工业日益增长的丙烯需求做出贡献。
