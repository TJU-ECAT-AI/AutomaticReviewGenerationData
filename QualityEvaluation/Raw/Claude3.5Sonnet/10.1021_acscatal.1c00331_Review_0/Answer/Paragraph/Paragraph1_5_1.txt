The process involves the conversion of propane to propene and hydrogen using specific catalysts. Key considerations for PDH include:

Industrial applications: Several commercial processes exist, including Oleflex (UOP) and Catofin, which use either fixed-bed, moving-bed, or fluidized-bed reactor configurations [Placeholder_Of_DOI3]. Coke formation on Pt-based catalysts significantly impacts process efficiency by causing rapid catalyst deactivation. The strong interaction between Pt and alkenes leads to severe side reactions and coke deposition, necessitating frequent catalyst regeneration in practical applications.

Strategies to mitigate coke formation and improve catalyst regeneration include:

1. Catalyst design: Developing single-atom catalysts (SACs) or intermetallic compounds to isolate Pt atoms, inhibiting structure-sensitive side reactions that lead to coke formation [Placeholder_Of_DOI5].

5. Regeneration techniques: Implementing efficient regeneration cycles, such as oxidative regeneration followed by reduction, to remove coke and restore catalyst activity [Placeholder_Of_DOI8].
