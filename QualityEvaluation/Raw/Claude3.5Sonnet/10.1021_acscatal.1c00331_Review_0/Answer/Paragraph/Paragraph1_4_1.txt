Catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) is an important method for propene production, driven by increasing global demand for propylene and the availability of low-cost propane from shale gas. The process involves the conversion of propane to propene and hydrogen using specific catalysts under high-temperature conditions.

Key considerations for PDH include:

Reaction conditions: PDH is a highly endothermic reaction (ΔH298 = 124.3 kJ/mol) that requires high temperatures (typically 550-750°C) and low pressures to achieve economically viable conversions [Placeholder_Of_DOI1]. These conditions are necessary due to thermodynamic limitations and to shift the equilibrium towards propene formation.

2. Catalyst types: The most commonly used catalysts are Pt-based and CrOx-based materials. Pt catalysts offer high activity but suffer from rapid deactivation due to coke formation and sintering. CrOx catalysts are cheaper but raise environmental concerns due to the toxicity of Cr(VI) species [Placeholder_Of_DOI2]. Recent research has focused on developing alternative catalysts, such as vanadium-based, gallium-based, and zirconium-based materials, to address these issues.

Industrial applications: PDH has become increasingly important for on-purpose propylene production, with several commercial processes in operation, including Oleflex (UOP) and Catofin (Lummus) [Placeholder_Of_DOI3]. These processes use either fixed-bed, moving-bed, or fluidized-bed reactor configurations to optimize catalyst performance and regeneration.

Coke formation on Pt-based catalysts significantly impacts process efficiency by causing rapid catalyst deactivation, reducing propylene selectivity, and necessitating frequent regeneration cycles. Strategies to mitigate coke formation and improve catalyst regeneration include:

3. Support modification: Using supports with low Brønsted acidity or adding promoters to suppress coke formation [Placeholder_Of_DOI6].

4. Reaction condition optimization: Co-feeding H2 or CO2 to reduce coke formation and maintain catalyst stability [Placeholder_Of_DOI7].

5. Advanced regeneration techniques: Implementing continuous catalyst regeneration (CCR) or oxychlorination processes to effectively remove coke and redisperse Pt particles [Placeholder_Of_DOI8].

These strategies aim to extend catalyst lifetime, maintain high propene selectivity, and improve overall process efficiency in industrial PDH operations.

PDH的主要考虑因素包括：

1. 反应条件：PDH是一个高度吸热反应（ΔH298 = 124.3 kJ/mol），需要高温（通常为550-750°C）和低压条件才能达到经济可行的转化率[Placeholder_Of_DOI1]。这些条件是由于热力学限制和需要将平衡移向丙烯形成而必要的。

2. 催化剂类型：最常用的催化剂是基于铂（Pt）和氧化铬（CrOx）的材料。Pt催化剂具有高活性，但由于结焦和烧结而容易快速失活。CrOx催化剂较便宜，但由于Cr(VI)物种的毒性而引起环境关切[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]。近期研究集中在开发替代催化剂，如基于钒、镓和锆的材料，以解决这些问题。

3. 工业应用：PDH已成为定向丙烯生产的重要方法，目前有几个商业化过程在运行，包括Oleflex（UOP）和Catofin（Lummus）[Placeholder_Of_DOI3]。这些过程使用固定床、移动床或流化床反应器配置来优化催化剂性能和再生。

Pt基催化剂上的结焦显著影响过程效率，导致催化剂快速失活、降低丙烯选择性，并需要频繁再生循环。结焦程度取决于多种因素，包括催化剂粒径、载体材料和反应条件[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]。

缓解结焦和改善催化剂再生的策略包括：

1. 催化剂改性：将Pt与其他金属（如Sn、Zn、Ga）合金化，以减少结焦并提高选择性[Placeholder_Of_DOI5]。

2. 优化粒径：较大的Pt粒子（如21 nm）比较小的粒子（如3 nm）表现出更低的结焦速率[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]。

3. 载体改性：使用低Brønsted酸性的载体或添加助剂以抑制结焦[Placeholder_Of_DOI6]。

4. 反应条件优化：共进料H2或CO2以减少结焦并保持催化剂稳定性[Placeholder_Of_DOI7]。
