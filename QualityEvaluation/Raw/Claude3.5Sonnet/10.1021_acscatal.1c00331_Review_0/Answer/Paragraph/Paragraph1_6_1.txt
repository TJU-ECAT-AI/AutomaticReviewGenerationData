Catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) is an important method for propene production, driven by increasing global demand and the availability of low-cost propane from shale gas. The process involves the conversion of propane to propylene and hydrogen using specific catalysts. Key considerations for PDH include:

1. Reaction conditions: PDH is highly endothermic (ΔH298 = 124.3 kJ/mol) and typically operates at high temperatures (550-750°C) and low pressures to achieve favorable equilibrium conversions [Placeholder_Of_DOI1]. These conditions are necessary to obtain economically viable propylene yields but also create challenges for catalyst stability and coke formation.

2. Catalyst types: The main industrial catalysts are Pt-based and CrOx-based, supported on Al2O3. Pt-based catalysts offer high activity but face issues with selectivity and coke formation, while CrOx catalysts are cheaper but raise environmental concerns due to Cr(VI) toxicity [Placeholder_Of_DOI2]. Recent research has focused on developing alternative catalysts, such as vanadium-based, gallium-based, and zirconium-based materials, to address these limitations.

3. Industrial applications: PDH has become increasingly important for on-purpose propylene production, with several commercial processes in operation, including Oleflex (UOP) and Catofin (Lummus) [Placeholder_Of_DOI3]. These processes use either fixed-bed, moving-bed, or fluidized-bed reactor configurations.

Coke formation on Pt-based catalysts significantly impacts process efficiency by causing rapid catalyst deactivation, reducing propylene selectivity, and necessitating frequent regeneration cycles. The extent of coke formation depends on various factors, including catalyst particle size, reaction conditions, and catalyst composition [Placeholder_Of_DOI4].

Strategies to mitigate coke formation and improve catalyst regeneration include:

1. Catalyst modification: Alloying Pt with promoters like Sn, Zn, or Ga to reduce coke formation and improve selectivity [Placeholder_Of_DOI5].

2. Optimizing catalyst structure: Developing single-atom catalysts or intermetallic compounds to isolate active sites and inhibit undesired side reactions [Placeholder_Of_DOI6].

3. Reaction condition control: Co-feeding H2 or CO2 to suppress coke precursor formation and facilitate coke removal [Placeholder_Of_DOI7].

4. Support modification: Using supports with low Brønsted acidity or introducing surface hydroxyl groups to enhance propylene desorption and prevent coke formation [Placeholder_Of_DOI8].

5. Regeneration processes: Implementing efficient oxidative regeneration cycles, often using air or oxychlorination treatments, to remove coke and restore catalyst activity [Placeholder_Of_DOI9].

These strategies aim to extend catalyst lifetime, maintain high propylene selectivity, and improve overall process efficiency in industrial PDH operations.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of PDH catalysis is rapidly evolving, with significant potential for further improvements in catalyst design and process optimization. Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Advanced material design: Developing novel catalyst formulations that combine the high activity of Pt with the stability and coke resistance of other materials, potentially through the use of nanotechnology and precise atom-level engineering.

1. 反应条件：PDH是高度吸热的（ΔH298 = 124.3 kJ/mol），通常在高温（550-750°C）和低压下进行，以获得有利的平衡转化率[Placeholder_Of_DOI1]。这些条件对于获得经济可行的丙烯产率是必要的，但也为催化剂稳定性和焦炭形成带来挑战。

2. 催化剂类型：主要的工业催化剂是基于铂和氧化铬的，负载在Al2O3上。基于铂的催化剂具有高活性，但面临选择性和焦炭形成的问题，而氧化铬催化剂较便宜，但由于Cr(VI)的毒性引起环境问题[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]。最近的研究集中在开发替代催化剂，如基于钒、镓和锆的材料，以解决这些限制。

3. 工业应用：PDH在定向丙烯生产中变得越来越重要，目前有几个商业流程在运行，包括Oleflex（UOP）和Catofin（Lummus）[Placeholder_Of_DOI3]。这些过程使用固定床、移动床或流化床反应器配置。

基于铂的催化剂上的焦炭形成显著影响过程效率，导致催化剂快速失活、降低丙烯选择性，并需要频繁的再生循环。焦炭形成的程度取决于多种因素，包括催化剂粒径、反应条件和催化剂组成[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]。

减少焦炭形成和改善催化剂再生的策略包括：

1. 催化剂改性：将铂与Sn、Zn或Ga等助剂合金化，以减少焦炭形成并提高选择性[Placeholder_Of_DOI5]。

2. 优化催化剂结构：开发单原子催化剂或金属间化合物，以隔离活性位点并抑制不期望的副反应[Placeholder_Of_DOI6]。

3. 反应条件控制：共进料H2或CO2以抑制焦炭前驱体的形成并促进焦炭清除[Placeholder_Of_DOI7]。

4. 载体改性：使用低Brønsted酸性的载体或引入表面羟基，以增强丙烯脱附并防止焦炭形成[Placeholder_Of_DOI8]。

5. 再生过程：实施高效的氧化再生循环，通常使用空气或氧氯化处理，以去除焦炭并恢复催化剂活性[Placeholder_Of_DOI9]。

这些策略旨在延长催化剂寿命，保持高丙烯选择性，并提高工业PDH操作的整体过程效率。

综合理解与未来展望：PDH催化领域正在快速发展，在催化剂设计和过程优化方面还有很大的改进潜力。未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 先进材料设计：开发结合了铂的高活性和其他材料的稳定性和抗焦性的新型催化剂配方，可能通过使用纳米技术和精确的原子级工程来实现。

2. 原位表征技术：利用先进的分析方法实时了解催化剂行为、焦炭形成机理和再生过程，从而实现更有针对性的催化剂和工艺改进。

4. 可持续催化：研究地球丰富和环境友好的催化材料，可以替代贵金属和有毒化合物，与全球可持续发展目标保持一致。

5. 人工智能和机器学习：利用计算工具加速催化剂发现、优化工艺条件和预测长期催化剂性能，可能彻底改变PDH催化剂开发的方法。

这些未来方向可能会导致更高效、更稳定和更可持续的PDH过程，满足不断增长的全球丙烯需求，同时最小化环境影响和资源消耗。
