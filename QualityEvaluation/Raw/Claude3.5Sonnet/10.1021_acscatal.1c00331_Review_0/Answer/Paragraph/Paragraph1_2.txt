<English>
Catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) is an important method for propene production, driven by increasing global demand for propylene and the availability of low-cost propane from shale gas. The process involves the conversion of propane to propene and hydrogen using specific catalysts.

Key considerations for PDH include:

1. Reaction conditions: PDH is a highly endothermic reaction (ΔH298 = 124.3 kJ/mol) requiring high temperatures (typically 550-750°C) and low pressures to achieve economically viable conversions [10.1039/d0cs00814a]. These conditions are necessary due to thermodynamic limitations and to drive the equilibrium towards propene formation [10.1021/cr5002436].

2. Catalyst types: Two main categories of catalysts are used in PDH:
   a) Noble metal-based catalysts: Primarily platinum (Pt) and its alloys, which are highly active but expensive [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].
   b) Metal oxide-based catalysts: Particularly chromium oxide (CrOx), which is less expensive but raises environmental concerns due to toxicity [10.1038/s41467-018-06174-5].

3. Industrial applications: PDH has become increasingly important for on-purpose propylene production, with several commercial processes such as Oleflex (UOP) and Catofin utilizing Pt-based and CrOx-based catalysts, respectively [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.012].

Coke formation on Pt-based catalysts significantly impacts process efficiency by causing rapid catalyst deactivation, reducing propylene selectivity, and necessitating frequent regeneration cycles [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]. This issue is particularly pronounced at the high temperatures required for PDH.

Strategies to mitigate coke formation and improve catalyst regeneration include:

1. Catalyst modification: Alloying Pt with elements like Sn, Zn, or In to reduce coke formation and improve selectivity [10.1016/j.molcata.2006.10.025].

2. Active site isolation: Developing single-atom catalysts (SACs) to inhibit structure-sensitive side reactions leading to coke formation [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008].

3. Support optimization: Using supports with low Brønsted acidity or modifying supports to suppress strong Lewis acidic centers [10.1002/cctc.201900924].

4. Reaction condition optimization: Co-feeding H2 or CO2 to reduce coke precursor formation and promote coke removal via the reverse Boudouard reaction [10.1039/d0cs00814a].

5. Regeneration techniques: Implementing efficient oxidative regeneration processes, such as the use of air streams at reaction temperatures to quickly remove coke deposits [10.1002/anie.201508731].

6. Continuous catalyst regeneration (CCR) technology: Allowing for stable catalyst activity over extended periods without reactor shutdown [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of PDH technology represents a significant advancement in addressing the growing demand for propylene. While current commercial processes primarily rely on Pt- and CrOx-based catalysts, future research is likely to focus on more sustainable and efficient alternatives. The exploration of novel catalyst formulations, such as single-atom catalysts and bimetallic systems, shows promise in overcoming the limitations of traditional catalysts.

Moving forward, the integration of advanced characterization techniques and computational modeling could provide deeper insights into the mechanisms of coke formation and catalyst deactivation. This knowledge could guide the rational design of next-generation catalysts with improved stability and selectivity. Additionally, the development of innovative reactor designs and process intensification strategies may further enhance the efficiency and economics of PDH technology.

As the chemical industry continues to prioritize sustainability, future research may also explore the integration of PDH with renewable energy sources or the utilization of bio-based feedstocks. The combination of PDH with CO2 utilization technologies could potentially address both propylene production and carbon mitigation goals simultaneously.

Ultimately, the future of PDH technology lies in the synergistic advancement of catalyst design, process engineering, and sustainable practices to meet the growing global demand for propylene while minimizing environmental impact.

<Chinese>
催化丙烷直接脱氢（PDH）是一种重要的丙烯生产方法，由于全球丙烯需求的增加和页岩气中低成本丙烷的可用性而受到推动。该过程涉及使用特定催化剂将丙烷转化为丙烯和氢气。

PDH的主要考虑因素包括：

1. 反应条件：PDH是一个高度吸热反应（ΔH298 = 124.3 kJ/mol），需要高温（通常为550-750°C）和低压才能实现经济可行的转化率 [10.1039/d0cs00814a]。这些条件是由于热力学限制和驱动平衡向丙烯形成方向所必需的 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

2. 催化剂类型：PDH中使用两种主要类别的催化剂：
   a) 贵金属基催化剂：主要是铂（Pt）及其合金，活性高但成本昂贵 [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。
   b) 金属氧化物基催化剂：特别是铬氧化物（CrOx），成本较低但由于毒性而引起环境问题 [10.1038/s41467-018-06174-5]。

3. 工业应用：PDH在定向丙烯生产中变得越来越重要，有几个商业化过程，如使用Pt基催化剂的Oleflex（UOP）和使用CrOx基催化剂的Catofin [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.012]。

Pt基催化剂上的焦炭形成显著影响工艺效率，导致催化剂快速失活、降低丙烯选择性，并需要频繁的再生循环 [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]。这个问题在PDH所需的高温下尤为明显。

减少焦炭形成和改善催化剂再生的策略包括：

1. 催化剂改性：将Pt与Sn、Zn或In等元素合金化，以减少焦炭形成并提高选择性 [10.1016/j.molcata.2006.10.025]。

2. 活性位点隔离：开发单原子催化剂（SACs）以抑制导致焦炭形成的结构敏感的副反应 [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]。

3. 载体优化：使用低Brønsted酸性的载体或修饰载体以抑制强Lewis酸性中心 [10.1002/cctc.201900924]。

4. 反应条件优化：共进料H2或CO2以减少焦炭前体的形成，并通过逆Boudouard反应促进焦炭去除 [10.1039/d0cs00814a]。

5. 再生技术：实施高效的氧化再生过程，例如在反应温度下使用空气流快速去除焦炭沉积物 [10.1002/anie.201508731]。

6. 连续催化剂再生（CCR）技术：允许催化剂在长时间内保持稳定活性，无需停止反应器 [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：PDH技术的发展代表了解决日益增长的丙烯需求的重大进步。虽然目前的商业化工艺主要依赖于Pt和CrOx基催化剂，但未来的研究可能会集中在更可持续和高效的替代方案上。对新型催化剂配方的探索，如单原子催化剂和双金属系统，在克服传统催化剂局限性方面显示出了前景。

展望未来，先进表征技术和计算模拟的整合可能会提供对焦炭形成和催化剂失活机制的更深入了解。这些知识可以指导下一代催化剂的合理设计，提高其稳定性和选择性。此外，创新反应器设计和过程强化策略的开发可能会进一步提高PDH技术的效率和经济性。

随着化学工业继续优先考虑可持续性，未来的研究可能还会探索PDH与可再生能源源的整合或生物基原料的利用。PDH与CO2利用技术的结合可能同时解决丙烯生产和碳减缓目标。

最终，PDH技术的未来在于催化剂设计、工艺工程和可持续实践的协同进步，以满足全球不断增长的丙烯需求，同时最大限度地减少环境影响。
