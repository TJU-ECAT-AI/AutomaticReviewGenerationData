Catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) is an important method for propene production, addressing the growing demand for propylene that traditional methods like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking struggle to meet. The process involves the conversion of propane to propene and hydrogen using specific catalysts.

Key considerations for PDH include:

1. Reaction conditions: PDH is a highly endothermic reaction, typically operating at temperatures between 550-750°C and atmospheric pressure. 2. Catalyst types: The most common catalysts are Pt-based and CrOx-based. Pt catalysts offer high activity but suffer from rapid deactivation due to coke formation and sintering. CrOx catalysts are cheaper but raise environmental concerns due to the toxicity of Cr(VI) species [Placeholder_Of_DOI2]. Recent research has focused on developing alternative catalysts, such as vanadium-based or zirconia-based materials, to address these limitations.

3. Industrial applications: PDH has become increasingly important for on-purpose propylene production, especially with the availability of low-cost propane from shale gas. Commercial processes like Oleflex and Catofin have been widely implemented, with global capacity reaching significant levels [Placeholder_Of_DOI3].

Coke formation on Pt-based catalysts is a critical challenge in PDH, significantly impacting process efficiency by causing rapid catalyst deactivation and reducing propylene selectivity. 2. Optimizing reaction conditions: Co-feeding H2 or CO2 can help suppress coke formation by reducing the coverage of deeply dehydrogenated coke precursors on the catalyst surface [Placeholder_Of_DOI4].

3. Support material selection: Using supports with low Brønsted acidity or modifying them to suppress strong Lewis acidic centers can help prevent coke deposition [Placeholder_Of_DOI7].

4. Catalyst regeneration: Implementing efficient regeneration cycles, such as oxidative treatment followed by reduction, can effectively remove coke and restore catalyst activity [Placeholder_Of_DOI8].

5. Reactor design: Continuous catalyst regeneration (CCR) technology, introduced by UOP, allows for maintaining stable catalyst activity over extended periods without reactor shutdown [Placeholder_Of_DOI9].

PDH的主要考虑因素包括：

1. 反应条件：PDH是一个高度吸热的反应，通常在550-750°C和大气压下进行。由于热力学限制，需要这样的高温，ΔH298 = 124.3 kJ/mol [Placeholder_Of_DOI1]。然而，这些条件也促进了副反应和积碳的形成。

2. 催化剂类型：最常见的催化剂是基于Pt和CrOx的。Pt催化剂活性高，但由于积碳和烧结而快速失活。CrOx催化剂较便宜，但由于Cr(VI)物种的毒性而引起环境问题 [Placeholder_Of_DOI2]。最近的研究集中在开发替代催化剂，如基于钒或氧化锆的材料，以解决这些限制。

3. 工业应用：PDH在有目的的丙烯生产中变得越来越重要，特别是随着页岩气提供的低成本丙烷的可用性。Oleflex和Catofin等商业流程已广泛实施，全球产能已达到显著水平 [Placeholder_Of_DOI3]。

Pt基催化剂上的积碳形成是PDH中的一个关键挑战，通过导致催化剂快速失活和降低丙烯选择性，显著影响了过程效率。失活过程分两个阶段：快速的初始失活，随后是稳态期 [Placeholder_Of_DOI4]。

减少积碳形成和改善催化剂再生的策略包括：

1. 催化剂改性：将Pt与Sn、Zn或In等元素合金可以改善催化剂稳定性并减少积碳形成 [Placeholder_Of_DOI5]。单原子催化剂（SACs）在抑制导致积碳的结构敏感性副反应方面显示出前景 [Placeholder_Of_DOI6]。

2. 优化反应条件：共进料H2或CO2可以通过减少催化剂表面深度脱氢的积碳前体物的覆盖来抑制积碳形成 [Placeholder_Of_DOI4]。

3. 选择支撑材料：使用具有低Brønsted酸性的支撑材料或修改它们以抑制强Lewis酸中心可以帮助防止积碳沉积 [Placeholder_Of_DOI7]。

4. 催化剂再生：实施有效的再生循环，如氧化处理后还原，可以有效地去除积碳并恢复催化剂活性 [Placeholder_Of_DOI8]。

5. 反应器设计：UOP引入的连续催化剂再生（CCR）技术允许在不停机的情况下长期保持稳定的催化剂活性 [Placeholder_Of_DOI9]。
