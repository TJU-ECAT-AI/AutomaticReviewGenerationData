The process involves the conversion of propane to propene and hydrogen using specific catalysts. Key considerations for PDH include:

Recent research has focused on developing alternative catalysts, such as vanadium-based, gallium-based, and zirconium-based materials, to address these limitations.

Industrial applications: PDH has become increasingly important for on-purpose propylene production, with several commercial processes in operation, including Oleflex (UOP) and Catofin (Lummus) [Placeholder_Of_DOI3]. These processes typically use fixed-bed, moving-bed, or fluidized-bed reactor configurations.

5. Regeneration techniques: Implementing efficient regeneration cycles, such as oxidative treatments followed by reduction, to remove coke and restore catalyst activity [Placeholder_Of_DOI9].

These strategies aim to extend catalyst lifetime, improve propylene selectivity, and enhance overall process efficiency in industrial PDH operations.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of PDH technology has seen significant advancements in recent years, driven by the need for more efficient and sustainable propylene production. Future research efforts may focus on several key areas:

1. Novel catalyst materials: Exploring non-noble metal catalysts, such as single-atom catalysts or intermetallic compounds, could lead to more cost-effective and stable catalysts for PDH.

2. In-situ regeneration techniques: Developing methods for continuous or semi-continuous catalyst regeneration without process interruption could greatly improve the economic viability of PDH processes.

3. Hybrid processes: Integrating PDH with other technologies, such as membrane separation or oxidative dehydrogenation, may offer synergistic benefits in terms of conversion, selectivity, and energy efficiency.

基于铂的催化剂上的焦炭形成显著影响过程效率，导致催化剂快速失活，降低丙烯选择性，并需要频繁的再生循环。焦炭形成的程度取决于多种因素，包括催化剂粒径、反应温度和进料组成[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]。
