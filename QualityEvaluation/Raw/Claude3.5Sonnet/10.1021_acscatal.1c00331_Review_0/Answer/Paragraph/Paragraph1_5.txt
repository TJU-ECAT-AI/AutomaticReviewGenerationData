<English>
Catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) is an important method for propene production, driven by increasing global demand for propylene and the availability of low-cost propane from shale gas. The process involves the conversion of propane to propene and hydrogen using specific catalysts under high-temperature conditions.

Key considerations for PDH include:

1. Reaction conditions: PDH is highly endothermic (ΔH298 = 124.3 kJ/mol) and requires high temperatures (typically 550-750°C) to achieve economically viable conversions [10.1039/d0cs00814a]. Low pressures and low propane partial pressures are also favorable for high conversions due to thermodynamic limitations [10.1021/cr5002436].

2. Catalyst types: The most common catalysts are Pt-based and CrOx-based materials. Pt catalysts offer high activity but suffer from rapid deactivation due to coke formation and sintering. CrOx catalysts are cheaper but raise environmental concerns due to the toxicity of Cr(VI) species [10.1038/s41467-020-16693-9]. Recent research has focused on developing alternative catalysts, such as V-, Ga-, and Sn-based materials [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004].

3. Industrial applications: PDH has been commercialized in processes like Oleflex (UOP) and Catofin, contributing significantly to global propylene production [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

Coke formation on Pt-based catalysts is a major challenge in PDH, significantly impacting process efficiency by causing catalyst deactivation and reducing propylene selectivity. Strategies to mitigate coke formation and improve catalyst regeneration include:

1. Catalyst modification: Alloying Pt with elements like Sn, Zn, or In to improve stability and reduce coke formation [10.1016/j.molcata.2006.10.025].

2. Single-atom catalysts: Developing catalysts with isolated Pt atoms to inhibit undesired side reactions leading to coke formation [10.1038/s41467-020-16693-9].

3. Support optimization: Using supports with low Brønsted acidity or modifying supports to suppress strong Lewis acidic centers [10.1002/cctc.201900924].

4. Process modifications: Co-feeding H2 or CO2 to reduce coke formation [10.1039/d0cs00814a].

5. Regeneration techniques: Implementing efficient regeneration cycles, often using oxidative treatments followed by reduction [10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008].

6. Catalyst design: Optimizing particle size and geometry to balance activity, selectivity, and coke resistance [10.1016/j.catcom.2007.08.015].

These strategies aim to extend catalyst lifetime, improve selectivity, and enhance overall process efficiency in industrial PDH applications.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) has made significant strides in recent years, addressing the growing demand for propylene production. However, challenges remain, particularly in catalyst design and process optimization. Future research directions may focus on:

1. Advanced materials: Exploring novel catalyst compositions, such as single-atom alloys or intermetallic compounds, to further improve activity, selectivity, and stability.

2. In-situ characterization: Developing and utilizing advanced spectroscopic techniques for real-time monitoring of catalyst behavior and coke formation during PDH reactions.

3. Computational modeling: Employing machine learning and artificial intelligence to predict catalyst performance and optimize reaction conditions.

4. Process intensification: Investigating innovative reactor designs or hybrid processes that combine PDH with other reactions to improve overall efficiency and reduce energy consumption.

5. Sustainable catalysts: Developing catalysts based on earth-abundant elements to reduce reliance on precious metals and address environmental concerns.

6. Alternative feedstocks: Exploring the potential of PDH technology for other alkanes or renewable feedstocks to broaden its applicability in a sustainable chemical industry.

By addressing these areas, future research can potentially overcome the current limitations of PDH technology, leading to more efficient, sustainable, and economically viable processes for propylene production.

<Chinese>
催化丙烷直接脱氢（PDH）是一种重要的丙烯生产方法，这源于全球对丙烯需求的增加以及页岩气中低成本丙烷的可用性。该过程涉及在高温条件下使用特定催化剂将丙烷转化为丙烯和氢气。

PDH的关键考虑因素包括：

1. 反应条件：PDH是高度吸热的（ΔH298 = 124.3 kJ/mol），需要高温（通常为550-750°C）才能达到经济可行的转化率[10.1039/d0cs00814a]。由于热力学限制，低压和低丙烷分压也有利于获得高转化率[10.1021/cr5002436]。

2. 催化剂类型：最常见的催化剂是基于Pt和CrOx的材料。Pt催化剂具有高活性，但由于焦炭形成和烧结而易于快速失活。CrOx催化剂较便宜，但由于Cr(VI)物种的毒性而引起环境问题[10.1038/s41467-020-16693-9]。近期研究集中在开发替代催化剂，如基于V、Ga和Sn的材料[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004]。

3. 工业应用：PDH已在Oleflex（UOP）和Catofin等工艺中商业化，为全球丙烯生产做出了重大贡献[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

焦炭在基于Pt的催化剂上的形成是PDH面临的一个重大挑战，显著影响了工艺效率，导致催化剂失活和丙烯选择性降低。缓解焦炭形成和改善催化剂再生的策略包括：

1. 催化剂改性：将Pt与Sn、Zn或In等元素合金，以提高稳定性并减少焦炭形成[10.1016/j.molcata.2006.10.025]。

2. 单原子催化剂：开发具有孤立Pt原子的催化剂，以抑制导致焦炭形成的不良副反应[10.1038/s41467-020-16693-9]。

3. 载体优化：使用具有低Brønsted酸性的载体或改性载体以抑制强Lewis酸性中心[10.1002/cctc.201900924]。

4. 工艺改进：共进料H2或CO2以减少焦炭形成[10.1039/d0cs00814a]。

5. 再生技术：实施高效的再生循环，通常使用氧化处理后进行还原[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008]。

6. 催化剂设计：优化粒子大小和几何形状，以平衡活性、选择性和抗焦性[10.1016/j.catcom.2007.08.015]。

这些策略旨在延长催化剂寿命，提高选择性，并提高工业PDH应用中的整体工艺效率。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：近年来，催化丙烷直接脱氢（PDH）技术的发展取得了重大进展，满足了日益增长的丙烯生产需求。然而，在催化剂设计和工艺优化方面仍然存在挑战。未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 先进材料：探索新型催化剂组成，如单原子合金或金属间化合物，以进一步提高活性、选择性和稳定性。

2. 原位表征：开发和利用先进的光谱技术，实时监测PDH反应过程中催化剂行为和焦炭形成。

3. 计算机模拟：运用机器学习和人工智能预测催化剂性能并优化反应条件。

4. 过程强化：研究创新的反应器设计或将PDH与其他反应结合的混合工艺，以提高整体效率并减少能耗。

5. 可持续催化剂：开发基于地球丰富元素的催化剂，减少对贵金属的依赖并解决环境问题。

6. 替代原料：探索PDH技术应用于其他烷烃或可再生原料的潜力，扩大其在可持续化学工业中的适用性。

通过关注这些领域，未来的研究有可能克服PDH技术的当前限制，从而实现更高效、更可持续和经济可行的丙烯生产工艺。
