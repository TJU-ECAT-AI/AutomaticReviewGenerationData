<English>
Catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) is an important method for propene production, addressing the growing demand for propylene that traditional methods like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking struggle to meet. The process involves converting propane to propene and hydrogen using specific catalysts under controlled conditions.

Key considerations for PDH include:

1. Reaction conditions: PDH is a highly endothermic reaction (ΔH298 = 124.3 kJ/mol) that typically operates at high temperatures (550-750°C) and low pressures to achieve economically viable conversions [10.1039/d0cs00814a]. These conditions are necessary due to thermodynamic limitations but can lead to catalyst deactivation and undesired side reactions.

2. Catalyst types: The main industrial catalysts are Pt-based and CrOx-based, supported on Al2O3 [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]. Pt-based catalysts offer high activity and selectivity but are expensive and prone to coking. CrOx-based catalysts are cheaper but raise environmental concerns due to the toxicity of Cr(VI) species [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.012]. Recent research has focused on developing alternative catalysts, such as Ga2O3, ZrO2, and V-based catalysts, to address these limitations [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.03.001] [10.1038/s41467-018-06174-5] [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004].

3. Industrial applications: PDH has become increasingly important for on-purpose propylene production, especially with the availability of low-cost propane from shale gas. Commercial processes like Oleflex (UOP) and Catofin use Pt-based and CrOx-based catalysts, respectively [10.1039/d0cs00814a].

Coke formation on Pt-based catalysts significantly impacts process efficiency by causing rapid catalyst deactivation, reducing propylene selectivity, and necessitating frequent regeneration cycles. This deactivation occurs in two stages: quick deactivation followed by a steady state [10.1039/d0cs00983k].

Strategies to mitigate coke formation and improve catalyst regeneration include:

1. Catalyst modification: Alloying Pt with elements like Sn, Zn, or In can improve catalytic performance and suppress carbon formation [10.1016/j.molcata.2006.10.025]. For example, PtSn/CeO2 catalysts show improved stability and regenerability [10.1002/anie.201701115].

2. Optimizing catalyst structure: Controlling Pt particle size and geometry can significantly impact coking behavior. Larger Pt particles (e.g., 21 nm) show lower coking rates compared to smaller particles (3 nm) [10.1016/j.catcom.2007.08.015].

3. Support modification: Using supports with low Brønsted acidity or modifying supports with promoters can reduce coke formation [10.1021/cr5002436].

4. Reaction condition optimization: Co-feeding H2 or CO2 can help suppress coke formation. For instance, increasing H2 partial pressure is a common measure to control coking rates in the Oleflex process [10.1039/d0cs00983k].

5. Advanced regeneration techniques: Implementing oxychlorination regeneration (using air and chlorine) can effectively redisperse sintered Pt particles, as simple coke burning is insufficient and can worsen sintering [10.1039/d0cs00983k].

6. Novel catalyst designs: Developing single-atom catalysts or intermetallic compounds, such as PtGa-Pb/SiO2, can dramatically enhance propylene selectivity and stability by inhibiting undesired side reactions that lead to coke formation [10.1038/s41467-020-16693-9].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of catalytic propane dehydrogenation is rapidly evolving, driven by the need for more efficient and sustainable propylene production. While significant progress has been made in understanding coke formation mechanisms and developing strategies to mitigate its effects, there are still opportunities for further advancement.

Future research directions may include:

1. Rational design of multi-functional catalysts that combine high activity, selectivity, and coke resistance.
2. Exploration of novel support materials, such as metal-organic frameworks or 2D materials, to enhance catalyst stability and regenerability.
3. Development of in-situ characterization techniques to better understand coke formation and catalyst deactivation mechanisms under realistic reaction conditions.
4. Integration of machine learning and high-throughput screening methodologies to accelerate catalyst discovery and optimization.
5. Investigation of alternative reaction engineering approaches, such as membrane reactors or structured catalysts, to overcome thermodynamic limitations and improve process efficiency.

By addressing these challenges and opportunities, researchers can pave the way for next-generation PDH technologies that offer improved performance, sustainability, and economic viability in the production of this crucial chemical feedstock.

<Chinese>
催化直接脱氢法（PDH）是一种重要的丙烯生产方法，可以满足传统方法如蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解难以满足的日益增长的丙烯需求。该过程在受控条件下使用特定催化剂将丙烷转化为丙烯和氢气。

PDH的主要考虑因素包括：

1. 反应条件：PDH是一个高度吸热反应（ΔH298 = 124.3 kJ/mol），通常在高温（550-750°C）和低压下进行，以实现经济可行的转化率 [10.1039/d0cs00814a]。这些条件是由于热力学限制而必需的，但可能导致催化剂失活和不需要的副反应。

2. 催化剂类型：主要的工业催化剂是以Al2O3为载体的Pt基和CrOx基催化剂 [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。Pt基催化剂具有高活性和选择性，但价格昂贵且容易结焦。CrOx基催化剂较便宜，但由于Cr(VI)物种的毒性而引起环境问题 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.012]。最近的研究集中在开发替代催化剂，如Ga2O3、ZrO2和V基催化剂，以解决这些限制 [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.03.001] [10.1038/s41467-018-06174-5] [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004]。

3. 工业应用：PDH在定向丙烯生产中变得越来越重要，尤其是在页岩气提供低成本丙烷的情况下。商业工艺如Oleflex（UOP）和Catofin分别使用Pt基和CrOx基催化剂 [10.1039/d0cs00814a]。

Pt基催化剂上的焦炭形成显著影响工艺效率，导致催化剂快速失活，降低丙烯选择性，并需要频繁的再生循环。这种失活过程分两个阶段：快速失活后接着稳态 [10.1039/d0cs00983k]。

减少焦炭形成和改善催化剂再生的策略包括：

1. 催化剂改性：将Pt与Sn、Zn或In等元素合金化可以改善催化性能并抑制碳形成 [10.1016/j.molcata.2006.10.025]。例如，PtSn/CeO2催化剂表现出改善的稳定性和再生能力 [10.1002/anie.201701115]。

2. 优化催化剂结构：控制Pt粒子大小和几何形状可以显著影响结焦行为。较大的Pt粒子（如21 nm）比较小的粒子（3 nm）表现出较低的结焦速率 [10.1016/j.catcom.2007.08.015]。

3. 载体改性：使用低Brønsted酸性的载体或用促进剂修饰载体可以减少焦炭形成 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

4. 反应条件优化：共进料H2或CO2可以帮助抑制焦炭形成。例如，在Oleflex工艺中，增加H2分压是控制结焦速率的常用措施 [10.1039/d0cs00983k]。

5. 先进的再生技术：实施氯氧化再生（使用空气和氯气）可以有效地重新分散烧结的Pt粒子，因为简单的焦炭燃烧是不够的，还可能加剧烧结 [10.1039/d0cs00983k]。

6. 新型催化剂设计：开发单原子催化剂或金属间化合物，如PtGa-Pb/SiO2，可以通过抑制导致焦炭形成的不需要的副反应，大大提高丙烯选择性和稳定性 [10.1038/s41467-020-16693-9]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：催化丙烷脱氢领域正在快速发展，驱动力来自于对更高效和可持续丙烯生产的需求。虽然在理解焦炭形成机制和开发减缓其影响的策略方面已经取得了重大进展，但仍有进一步发展的机会。

未来研究方向可能包括：

1. 合理设计多功能催化剂，结合高活性、选择性和抗焦性能。
2. 探索新型载体材料，如金属有机框架或二维材料，以提高催化剂的稳定性和再生能力。
3. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解在实际反应条件下的焦炭形成和催化剂失活机制。
4. 整合机器学习和高通量筛选方法，加速催化剂的发现和优化。
5. 研究替代反应工程方法，如膜反应器或结构化催化剂，以克服热力学限制并提高工艺效率。

通过解决这些挑战和机遇，研究人员可以为下一代PDH技术铺平道路，在生产这种关键化学原料时提供更好的性能、可持续性和经济可行性。
