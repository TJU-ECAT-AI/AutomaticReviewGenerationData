<English>
Catalytic direct dehydrogenation of propane (PDH) is an important method for propene production, driven by increasing global demand for propylene and the availability of low-cost propane from shale gas. The process involves the conversion of propane to propene and hydrogen using specific catalysts. Key considerations for PDH include:

1. Reaction conditions: PDH is a highly endothermic reaction (ΔH298 = 124.3 kJ/mol) requiring high temperatures (typically 550-750°C) and low pressures to achieve economically viable conversions [10.1039/d0cs00814a]. These conditions are necessary due to thermodynamic limitations and the endothermic nature of the reaction.

2. Catalyst types: Two main classes of catalysts are used in PDH:
   a) Noble metal-based catalysts, primarily platinum (Pt): These are highly active but face challenges with selectivity and coking [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_0].
   b) Metal oxide-based catalysts, particularly chromium oxide (CrOx): These show good performance but raise environmental concerns due to the toxicity of Cr(VI) species [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.012].

Recent research has focused on developing alternative catalysts, such as vanadium-based [10.1002/anie.201800123], zirconium-based [10.1002/anie.201508731], and single-atom alloy catalysts [10.1038/s41467-018-06967-8], to address the limitations of traditional Pt and CrOx catalysts.

3. Industrial applications: PDH has become increasingly important for on-purpose propylene production, with several commercial processes in operation, including Oleflex (UOP) and Catofin (Lummus) [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]. As of 2000, there were multiple PDH units in commercial operation, with an aggregate capacity of 900,000 MTA polymer-grade propylene [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

Coke formation on Pt-based catalysts significantly impacts process efficiency by causing rapid catalyst deactivation, reducing propylene selectivity, and necessitating frequent regeneration cycles [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]. Two types of coke are typically formed: aliphatic coke on the metal surface and aromatic coke on the support [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8].

Strategies to mitigate coke formation and improve catalyst regeneration include:

1. Catalyst modification: Alloying Pt with elements like Sn, In, or Ga to reduce coke formation and improve selectivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008].

2. Active site isolation: Developing single-atom catalysts (SACs) to inhibit structure-sensitive side reactions that lead to coke formation [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008].

3. Optimizing reaction conditions: Controlling temperature, pressure, and feed composition to minimize coke formation while maintaining high propene yields [10.1021/cr5002436].

4. Co-feeding strategies: Adding H2 or CO2 to the feed to suppress coke precursor formation and facilitate coke removal [10.1039/d0cs00814a].

5. Support modification: Using supports with low Brønsted acidity or poisoning acid sites to reduce coke formation [10.1021/cr5002436].

6. Continuous Catalyst Regeneration (CCR) technology: Implementing systems that allow for ongoing catalyst regeneration without shutting down the reactor [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

7. Oxidative regeneration: Periodically exposing the catalyst to oxidizing conditions (often with chlorine for Pt catalysts) to remove coke and redisperse active metal particles [10.1039/d0cs00983k].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of PDH technology has seen significant advancements in recent years, driven by the need for more efficient and sustainable propylene production. The focus on catalyst design, particularly in addressing the challenges of coke formation and catalyst stability, has led to innovative approaches such as single-atom catalysts and bimetallic formulations. Moving forward, research efforts are likely to concentrate on:

1. Advanced materials science: Developing novel catalyst materials that combine high activity, selectivity, and resistance to coking, potentially through the use of nanotechnology and precise control of catalyst structure at the atomic level.

2. In-situ characterization techniques: Implementing advanced analytical methods to better understand coke formation mechanisms and catalyst deactivation in real-time, leading to more effective mitigation strategies.

3. Process intensification: Exploring new reactor designs and operating conditions that could enhance heat transfer, improve catalyst utilization, and reduce energy consumption in PDH processes.

4. Integration with renewable energy: Investigating the potential for coupling PDH processes with renewable energy sources to address the high energy requirements and improve overall sustainability.

5. Circular economy approaches: Developing methods to valorize coke deposits or integrate PDH processes with other chemical processes to minimize waste and maximize resource efficiency.

These future directions aim to address the current limitations of PDH technology and pave the way for more sustainable and economically viable propylene production in the face of increasing global demand and environmental concerns.

<Chinese>
催化直接丙烷脱氢（PDH）是一种重要的丙烯生产方法，由于全球丙烯需求的增加和页岩气提供的低成本丙烷而变得越来越重要。该过程涉及使用特定催化剂将丙烷转化为丙烯和氢气。PDH的主要考虑因素包括：

1. 反应条件：PDH是一个高度吸热反应（ΔH298 = 124.3 kJ/mol），需要高温（通常为550-750°C）和低压才能达到经济可行的转化率[10.1039/d0cs00814a]。这些条件是由于热力学限制和反应的吸热性质所必需的。

2. 催化剂类型：PDH中使用两类主要催化剂：
   a) 贵金属基催化剂，主要是铂（Pt）：这些催化剂具有高活性，但面临选择性和结焦的挑战[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_0]。
   b) 金属氧化物基催化剂，特别是氧化铬（CrOx）：这些催化剂表现良好，但由于Cr(VI)物种的毒性引起环境问题[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.012]。

近期研究集中在开发替代催化剂，如钒基[10.1002/anie.201800123]、锆基[10.1002/anie.201508731]和单原子合金催化剂[10.1038/s41467-018-06967-8]，以解决传统Pt和CrOx催化剂的局限性。

3. 工业应用：PDH在定向丙烯生产中变得越来越重要，已有几个商业过程在运行，包括Oleflex（UOP）和Catofin（Lummus）[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。截至2000年，已有多个PDH装置投入商业运营，总产能达900,000 MTA聚合级丙烯[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

Pt基催化剂上的焦炭形成显著影响工艺效率，导致催化剂快速失活、降低丙烯选择性，并需要频繁的再生周期[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]。通常形成两种类型的焦炭：金属表面的脂肪族焦炭和载体上的芳香族焦炭[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]。

减少焦炭形成和改善催化剂再生的策略包括：

1. 催化剂改性：将Pt与Sn、In或Ga等元素合金化，以减少焦炭形成并提高选择性[10.1016/j.jcat.2011.06.008]。

2. 活性位点隔离：开发单原子催化剂（SACs）以抑制导致焦炭形成的结构敏感副反应[10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]。

3. 优化反应条件：控制温度、压力和进料组成，以最小化焦炭形成同时保持高丙烯产率[10.1021/cr5002436]。

4. 共进料策略：向进料中添加H2或CO2，以抑制焦炭前体形成并促进焦炭去除[10.1039/d0cs00814a]。

5. 载体改性：使用低布朗斯特酸性的载体或毒化酸性位点以减少焦炭形成[10.1021/cr5002436]。

6. 连续催化剂再生（CCR）技术：实施允许在不停止反应器的情况下持续催化剂再生的系统[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

7. 氧化再生：定期将催化剂暴露在氧化条件下（对于Pt催化剂通常使用氯气）以去除焦炭并重新分散活性金属颗粒[10.1039/d0cs00983k]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：PDH技术的发展在近年来取得了显著进展，这主要是由于更高效和可持续丙烯生产的需求所驱动。催化剂设计的重点，特别是在解决焦炭形成和催化剂稳定性挑战方面，已经导致了创新方法的出现，如单原子催化剂和双金属配方。展望未来，研究努力可能集中在：

1. 先进材料科学：开发结合高活性、选择性和抗焦炭能力的新型催化剂材料，可能通过使用纳米技术和在原子水平上精确控制催化剂结构。

2. 原位表征技术：实施先进的分析方法，以更好地实时了解焦炭形成机制和催化剂失活，从而制定更有效的缓解策略。

3. 过程强化：探索新的反应器设计和操作条件，可以增强热传递，改善催化剂利用率，并减少PDH过程中的能耗。

4. 与可再生能源的集成：研究将PDH过程与可再生能源源结合的潜力，以解决高能耗要求并提高整体可持续性。

5. 循环经济方法：开发方法来增值焦炭沉积物或将PDH过程与其他化学过程集成，以最小化废物并最大化资源效率。

这些未来方向旨在解决PDH技术的当前限制，并为面对不断增长的全球需求和环境问题时，开辟更可持续和经济可行的丙烯生产道路。
