The ultrafast zeolite crystallization strategy developed by Yu et al. represents a significant advancement in addressing the time-scale mismatch challenges encountered in direct synthesis procedures for metal encapsulation within zeolite structures. This innovative approach synchronizes the rapid formation of metal nanoclusters with the expedited crystallization of zeolites, enabling the in situ encapsulation of ultrasmall metal clusters within high-silica ZSM-5 zeolites in just a few minutes [10.1002/anie.202007044].

The strategy employs a combination of precursor age, seed additives, and a fast-heating tubular reactor to achieve complete crystallization of ZSM-5 zeolites within remarkably short timeframes - as little as 5 minutes at 170°C or 2 minutes at 190°C [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\_3]. This ultrafast crystallization rate is crucial for the successful encapsulation of metal clusters, as it prevents their aggregation and ensures their confinement within the zeolite structure.

The formation and structure of PtSn clusters within ZSM-5 zeolites are significantly influenced by this ultrafast encapsulation method. The simultaneous formation of ultrasmall Pt clusters and their confinement within the high-silica ZSM-5 zeolite during crystallization results in well-dispersed nanoclusters with sizes ranging from 1 to 4 nm [10.1002/anie.202007044]. The addition of Sn plays a crucial role in regulating both the size and dispersion of the Pt nanoclusters. By adjusting the Pt/Sn ratio, the cluster size can be optimized to around 1-2 nm, which is ideal for catalytic applications [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\_3].

The catalytic performance of the PtSn clusters encapsulated within ZSM-5 zeolites using this ultrafast strategy is remarkable, particularly in the context of propane dehydrogenation (PDH). The optimized PtSn-ZSM-5 catalyst exhibits excellent activity and selectivity, with initial propane conversion rates as high as 65-70% [10.1002/anie.202007044][10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\_3]. More importantly, the catalyst demonstrates superior stability compared to traditional catalysts, maintaining high conversion rates (45%) even after 24 hours of continuous operation at 600°C [10.1039/D0CS01260B].

The enhanced performance of these catalysts can be attributed to several factors:

1. The ultrasmall size and uniform dispersion of the PtSn clusters within the zeolite structure, which maximizes the active surface area.
2. The confinement effect of the zeolite framework, which prevents sintering and agglomeration of the metal clusters under harsh reaction conditions.
3. The synergistic effect between Pt and Sn, which improves both the activity and selectivity of the catalyst.

It is worth noting that while this strategy has shown excellent results for PtSn clusters in ZSM-5 zeolites, it may have limitations for certain metal ions such as Fe3+ and In3+ [10.1016/j.cej.2022.139794]. The strategy's efficacy in metal site encapsulation, particularly for PtSn clusters in ZSM-5 zeolites, is evident in several key aspects:

The addition of Sn plays a crucial role in regulating both the size and dispersion of Pt nanoclusters [10.1002/anie.202007044]. By adjusting the Pt/Sn ratio, cluster sizes can be fine-tuned from 4 nm to 1 nm, optimizing metal dispersion and surface area [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\_3].

2. Catalyst Performance: The PtSn-ZSM-5 catalysts produced using this method exhibit superior catalytic performance in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions. Initial propane conversion rates of up to 70% have been reported, with sustained conversion of 45% even after 24 hours of operation at 600°C [10.1002/anie.202007044]. The catalysts also demonstrate remarkably high selectivity, maintaining approximately 99% selectivity throughout long reaction periods [10.1002/anie.202007044].

3. Stability: The ultrafast-encapsulated PtSn-ZSM-5 catalysts show exceptional stability compared to conventional catalysts. They maintain their high catalytic performance for multiple reaction-regeneration cycles without significant deactivation [10.1038/s41563-019-0412-6]. This enhanced stability is attributed to the confinement effect of the zeolite structure, which prevents sintering and agglomeration of metal clusters even under harsh industrial conditions [10.1016/j.cej.2022.139794].

4. Versatility: While the strategy has shown particular success with PtSn clusters, it has also been demonstrated effective for other metal combinations, such as Pt-Zn clusters [10.1016/j.cej.2022.139794]. The impact of this ultrafast zeolite crystallization strategy on catalyst structure and performance is significant. 5. Scaling up the ultrafast synthesis process for industrial production while maintaining the exceptional properties of the lab-scale catalysts.
