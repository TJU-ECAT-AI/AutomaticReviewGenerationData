The ultrafast zeolite crystallization strategy developed by Yu et al. represents a significant advancement in addressing the time-scale mismatch challenges associated with direct synthesis procedures for metal encapsulation in zeolites. This innovative approach synchronizes the rapid formation of metal nanoclusters with expedited zeolite crystallization, enabling the efficient encapsulation of PtSn clusters within ZSM-5 zeolites.

The strategy employs a combination of precursor age, seed additives, and a fast-heating tubular reactor to achieve complete crystallization of ZSM-5 zeolites within remarkably short timeframes - as little as 5 minutes at 170°C or 2 minutes at 190°C [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\_3]. This ultrafast crystallization rate allows for the simultaneous formation and confinement of ultrasmall Pt clusters within the high-silica ZSM-5 zeolite matrix [10.1002/anie.202007044].

The effects of this strategy on the formation, structure, and catalytic performance of PtSn clusters within ZSM-5 zeolites are substantial:

1. Formation: The ultrafast encapsulation method enables the in-situ formation of PtSn clusters with sizes around 1 nm inside the high-silica ZSM-5 zeolites, without the need for additional protective agents [10.1039/D0CS01260B]. The process is completed in just a few minutes, overcoming the traditional time-scale mismatch issues [10.1002/anie.202007044].

2. Structure: The addition of Sn plays a crucial role in regulating the size and dispersion of Pt nanoclusters. By fixing the Pt loading at 0.32 wt% and increasing Sn loadings from 0 to 1.2 wt%, the size of PtSn clusters can be fine-tuned from 4 nm to 1 nm [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\_3]. This level of control over cluster size allows for optimizing metal dispersion and surface area, which are critical factors in catalytic performance.

3. Catalytic Performance: In the context of propane dehydrogenation (PDH), the PtSn-ZSM-5 catalysts produced by this method exhibit exceptional activity, selectivity, and stability. The optimized catalyst demonstrates initial propane conversion rates of 65-70%, significantly higher than those of impregnated Pt/Sn-ZSM-5 catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\_3][10.1002/anie.202007044]. Moreover, the catalyst maintains high conversion (45%) even after 24 hours of reaction at 600°C, with remarkably high selectivity (about 99%) throughout the extended reaction period [10.1002/anie.202007044].

The superior performance of these catalysts can be attributed to several factors:

a) The ultrasmall size and uniform dispersion of PtSn clusters within the zeolite structure, which maximize the active surface area and minimize sintering.

b) The confinement effect of the zeolite framework, which enhances the stability of the metal clusters under harsh reaction conditions.

c) The synergistic effect between Pt and Sn, which modifies the electronic properties of the active sites and improves selectivity towards propylene.

While this strategy shows great promise, it is important to note that it may have limitations for certain metal ions, such as Fe3+ and In3+, due to potential precipitation issues during the high-temperature, high-pH crystallization process [10.1016/j.cej.2022.139794].
