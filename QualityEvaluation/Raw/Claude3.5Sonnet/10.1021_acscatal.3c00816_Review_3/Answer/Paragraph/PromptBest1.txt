The ultrafast zeolite crystallization strategy developed by Yu et al. represents a significant advancement in addressing the time-scale mismatch challenges encountered in direct synthesis procedures for metal encapsulation within zeolite structures. This innovative approach synchronizes the rapid formation of metal nanoclusters with the expedited crystallization of zeolites, enabling the in situ encapsulation of ultrasmall metal clusters within high-silica ZSM-5 zeolites in just a few minutes [10.1002/anie.202007044].

The strategy employs a combination of precursor age, seed additives, and a fast-heating tubular reactor to achieve complete crystallization of ZSM-5 zeolites within remarkably short timeframes - as little as 5 minutes at 170°C or 2 minutes at 190°C [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_3]. This ultrafast crystallization rate is crucial for the successful encapsulation of metal clusters, as it prevents their aggregation and ensures their confinement within the zeolite structure.

The formation and structure of PtSn clusters within ZSM-5 zeolites are significantly influenced by this ultrafast encapsulation method. The simultaneous formation of ultrasmall Pt clusters and their confinement within the high-silica ZSM-5 zeolite during crystallization results in well-dispersed nanoclusters with sizes ranging from 1 to 4 nm [10.1002/anie.202007044]. The addition of Sn plays a crucial role in regulating both the size and dispersion of the Pt nanoclusters. By adjusting the Pt/Sn ratio, the cluster size can be optimized to around 1-2 nm, which is ideal for catalytic applications [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_3].

The catalytic performance of the PtSn clusters encapsulated within ZSM-5 zeolites using this ultrafast strategy is remarkable, particularly in the context of propane dehydrogenation (PDH). The enhanced performance of these catalysts can be attributed to several factors:

1. The ultrasmall size and uniform dispersion of the PtSn clusters within the zeolite structure, which maximizes the active surface area.
