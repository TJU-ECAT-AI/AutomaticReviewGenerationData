The ultrafast zeolite crystallization strategy developed by Yu et al. addresses the time-scale mismatch challenges in direct synthesis procedures for metal encapsulation by significantly reducing the crystallization time of ZSM-5 zeolites. This strategy combines precursor age, seed additives, and a fast-heating tubular reactor to achieve complete crystallization of ZSM-5 zeolites within 5 minutes at 170°C, or even 2 minutes at 190°C.

This ultrafast crystallization rate allows for the full encapsulation of PtSn clusters within the ZSM-5 zeolites. The formation of Pt nanoclusters occurs simultaneously with the rapid zeolite crystallization, as evidenced by the increase in Pt loadings in the silica matrix correlating with increasing zeolite crystallinity.

The strategy affects the structure of the encapsulated PtSn clusters by enabling better control over their size. By fixing the Pt loading at 0.32 wt% and increasing Sn loadings from 0 to 1.2 wt%, the size of PtSn clusters can be reduced from 4 nm to 1 nm. This ability to fine-tune the cluster size allows for optimizing the metal dispersion and surface area, which are crucial factors in catalytic performance.

In terms of catalytic performance, the optimized PtSn-ZSM-5 catalyst produced using this method exhibits significantly higher initial propane conversion (65%) compared to the impregnated Pt/Sn-ZSM-5 catalyst. This improved performance can be attributed to the better encapsulation and dispersion of PtSn clusters within the zeolite structure, which is made possible by the ultrafast crystallization strategy.
