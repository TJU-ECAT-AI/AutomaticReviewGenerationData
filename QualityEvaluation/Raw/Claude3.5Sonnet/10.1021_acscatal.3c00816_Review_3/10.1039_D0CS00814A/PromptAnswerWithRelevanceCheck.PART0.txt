The ultrafast zeolite crystallization strategy developed by Yu et al. addresses the time-scale mismatch challenges in direct synthesis procedures for metal encapsulation by fixing metal species within the zeolite framework during its formation. This approach allows for the generation of highly stable Pt nanoparticles and single Pt atoms within the zeolite structure.

The effects on formation, structure, and catalytic performance of PtSn clusters within ZSM-5 zeolites include:

1. Improved thermal stability of metal species due to encapsulation within the stiff zeolite framework.

2. Formation of both nanoparticles and single atoms of Pt within the zeolite structure.

3. Ability to incorporate a second metal (such as Sn) alongside Pt using the same synthesis procedure.

4. Prevention of metal agglomeration, particularly when potassium is present (KPtSn@MFI), leading to atomically dispersed Pt species even after calcination.

5. Enhanced catalytic performance in propane conversion due to the stabilization of active metal species within the zeolite structure.

This strategy effectively overcomes the challenge of metal sintering and allows for precise control over the metal species' dispersion and stability within the zeolite catalyst, which is crucial for maintaining high catalytic activity and selectivity in propane conversion reactions.
