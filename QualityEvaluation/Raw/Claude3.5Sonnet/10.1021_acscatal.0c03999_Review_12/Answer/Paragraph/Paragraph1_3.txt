<English>
The mechanisms of C-H bond activation and catalyst regeneration in oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) of alkanes differ significantly between redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts. This review focuses on these mechanisms, emphasizing the role of traditional oxidants and CO2, and the effectiveness of various catalysts in activating CO2 for propane ODH.

Redox-active catalysts, such as V2O5, VOx/SiO2, and Cr2O3, typically operate via a Mars-van Krevelen (MvK) mechanism [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.09.012]. In this process, lattice oxygen participates in C-H bond activation, forming alkenes and reducing the metal center. The catalyst is then regenerated by reoxidation with an oxidant, filling the oxygen vacancy [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9]. The rate-limiting step is usually the breaking of the weakest C-H bond in the alkane molecule [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.09.012].

In contrast, redox-inactive catalysts like Ga2O3 and h-BN operate through heterolytic dissociation of alkanes and separate CO2 activation, without changes in metal oxidation state [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9] [10.1002/anie.202002440]. These catalysts typically show lower selectivity to carbon oxides and higher selectivity to desired olefins compared to redox-active catalysts [10.1002/anie.202002440].

Traditional oxidants like O2 can fully reoxidize redox-active catalysts, but often lead to over-oxidation and lower selectivity. CO2, as a milder oxidant, offers several advantages:
1. Suppression of over-oxidation, minimizing carbon oxide production [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].
2. Participation in the reverse water-gas shift reaction, enhancing alkane conversion [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].
3. Removal of coke deposits through the reverse Boudouard reaction [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2].

The effectiveness of different catalysts in activating CO2 for propane ODH varies:

1. V2O5 and VOx/SiO2: These catalysts show moderate effectiveness. CO2 can oxidize V2O3 to V2O4, but not to V2O5, leading to higher propylene selectivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]. The energy barrier for CO2 reoxidation of VOx/SiO2 is 1.76 eV, which is significant but lower than for pure V2O5 (3.16 eV) [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12].

2. CeO2-based catalysts: These are highly effective due to their ability to store and release oxygen, facilitating CO2 dissociation [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. The energy barrier for CO2 dissociation on CeO2 (110) surface with oxygen vacancies is 1.36 eV [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12].

3. Transition metal clusters: Some show excellent CO2 activation properties. For example, Ni4/MgO has a low CO2 dissociation barrier of 0.35 eV [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12].

4. Bimetallic catalysts on CeO2: Fe-Ni/CeO2 favors C-H bond activation for the ODH pathway, while Pt-containing catalysts tend to promote C-C bond cleavage [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w] [10.1039/D0CY00586J].

The proposed mechanisms for CO2 participation in ODH include direct CO2 dissociation to CO and O, and CO2 hydrogenation to HOCO or COOH before forming CO [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]. The effectiveness of these pathways depends on the catalyst's ability to lower energy barriers for CO2 dissociation and subsequent ODH steps.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of efficient catalysts for CO2-assisted ODH of propane represents a significant opportunity in the field of sustainable chemistry. The use of CO2 as a mild oxidant not only addresses environmental concerns but also offers improved selectivity in alkane activation. Future research directions may include:

1. Designing multi-functional catalysts that combine the CO2 activation properties of CeO2 with the selective C-H bond activation capabilities of transition metals.
2. Exploring novel redox-inactive materials that can facilitate CO2 activation without promoting over-oxidation.
3. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic surface processes during CO2-assisted ODH.
4. Investigating the potential of metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) or single-atom catalysts for precise control over active sites in CO2-assisted ODH reactions.
5. Exploring the use of renewable energy sources to drive the endothermic CO2 activation process, further enhancing the sustainability of the ODH process.

These research avenues could lead to more efficient and environmentally friendly processes for olefin production, contributing to the broader goals of carbon utilization and sustainable chemical manufacturing.

<Chinese>
在烷烃的氧化脱氢（ODH）反应中，C-H键活化和催化剂再生的机理在氧化还原活性和非氧化还原活性的金属氧化物催化剂之间存在显著差异。本综述着重讨论了这些机理，强调了传统氧化剂和CO2的作用，以及各种催化剂在活化CO2用于丙烷ODH方面的有效性。

氧化还原活性催化剂，如V2O5、VOx/SiO2和Cr2O3，通常通过Mars-van Krevelen（MvK）机理运作[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.09.012]。在这个过程中，晶格氧参与C-H键活化，形成烯烃并还原金属中心。然后，催化剂通过与氧化剂再氧化来再生，填补氧空位[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9]。限速步骤通常是烷烃分子中最弱C-H键的断裂[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.09.012]。

相比之下，非氧化还原活性催化剂如Ga2O3和h-BN通过烷烃的异裂解离和单独的CO2活化来运作，不涉及金属氧化态的变化[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9] [10.1002/anie.202002440]。这些催化剂通常表现出对碳氧化物的较低选择性和对目标烯烃的较高选择性，相比于氧化还原活性催化剂[10.1002/anie.202002440]。

传统氧化剂如O2可以完全再氧化氧化还原活性催化剂，但常常导致过度氧化和较低的选择性。CO2作为一种温和的氧化剂，提供了几个优势：
1. 抑制过度氧化，最小化碳氧化物的产生[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。
2. 参与逆水气变换反应，增强烷烃转化[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。
3. 通过逆布多瓦反应去除积碳沉积[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2]。

不同催化剂在活化CO2用于丙烷ODH方面的有效性各不相同：

1. V2O5和VOx/SiO2：这些催化剂显示中等有效性。CO2可以将V2O3氧化成V2O4，但不能氧化成V2O5，导致更高的丙烯选择性[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。CO2再氧化VOx/SiO2的能量障碍为1.76 eV，这是显著的，但低于纯V2O5（3.16 eV）[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。

2. 基于CeO2的催化剂：由于其储存和释放氧的能力，这些催化剂高度有效，有助于CO2解离[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。在含有氧空位的CeO2（110）表面上，CO2解离的能量障碍为1.36 eV [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。

3. 过渡金属簇：一些表现出优异的CO2活化性能。例如，Ni4/MgO的CO2解离障碍很低，仅为0.35 eV [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。

4. CeO2上的双金属催化剂：Fe-Ni/CeO2有利于ODH途径的C-H键断裂，而含Pt的催化剂倾向于促进C-C键断裂[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w] [10.1039/D0CY00586J]。

CO2参与ODH的提议机理包括CO2直接解离成CO和O，以及CO2氢化成HOCO或COOH后形成CO [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。这些途径的有效性取决于催化剂降低CO2解离和后续ODH步骤能量障碍的能力。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：开发高效的CO2辅助丙烷ODH催化剂代表了可持续化学领域的重要机遇。使用CO2作为温和氧化剂不仅解决了环境问题，还提供了烷烃活化的改进选择性。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 设计多功能催化剂，结合CeO2的CO2活化性能和过渡金属的选择性C-H键活化能力。
2. 探索新型的非氧化还原活性材料，能够促进CO2活化而不促进过度氧化。
3. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解CO2辅助ODH过程中的动态表面过程。
4. 研究金属有机框架（MOFs）或单原子催化剂在CO2辅助ODH反应中精确控制活性位点的潜力。
5. 探索使用可再生能源驱动吸热的CO2活化过程，进一步增强ODH过程的可持续性。

这些研究方向可能导致更高效和环境友好的烯烃生产过程，为碳利用和可持续化学制造的更广泛目标做出贡献。
