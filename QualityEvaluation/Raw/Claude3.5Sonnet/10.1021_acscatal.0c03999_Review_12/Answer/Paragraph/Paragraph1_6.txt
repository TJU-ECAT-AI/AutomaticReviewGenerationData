<English>
The mechanisms of C-H bond activation and catalyst regeneration in oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) of alkanes differ significantly between redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts. This review focuses on these mechanisms, the role of traditional oxidants and CO2, and the effectiveness of various catalysts in activating CO2 for propane ODH.

Redox-active catalysts, such as V2O5, VOx/SiO2, and Cr2O3, typically operate via a Mars-van Krevelen (MvK) mechanism [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2]. In this process, lattice oxygen participates in C-H bond activation, forming alkenes and reducing the metal center. The catalyst is then regenerated by re-oxidation with gas-phase oxidants [10.1021/jp810664u]. For vanadium-based catalysts, the breaking of the weakest C-H bond in the alkane molecule is the rate-limiting step, with reactivity increasing as C-H bond strength decreases: C2H6 < C3H8 < n-C4H10 < iso-C4H10 [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.09.012].

Redox-inactive catalysts, such as Ga2O3 and h-BN, operate through different mechanisms. They typically activate C-H bonds through heterolytic dissociation over acid-base pairs and do not involve changes in metal oxidation states [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9]. These catalysts show lower selectivity towards carbon oxides and higher selectivity to desired alkenes compared to redox-active catalysts [10.1002/anie.202002440].

The choice of oxidant significantly impacts catalyst performance and selectivity. Traditional oxidants like O2 can fully re-oxidize redox-active catalysts but may lead to over-oxidation and lower selectivity. CO2, as a milder oxidant, offers several advantages:

1. Suppression of over-oxidation, minimizing carbon oxide production [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].
2. Enhanced propane conversion through the reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) reaction [10.1039/D0CY00586J].
3. Reduced coke formation on the catalyst surface [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015].

The effectiveness of different catalysts in activating CO2 for propane ODH varies:

1. V2O5 and VOx/SiO2: Moderately effective, with CO2 re-oxidizing partially reduced metal oxides. VOx/SiO2 shows a lower barrier (1.76 eV) for CO2 activation compared to pure V2O5 (3.16 eV) [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12].

2. CeO2-based catalysts: Highly effective due to their ability to activate CO2 via direct C=O bond scission. CeO2 (110) surfaces with oxygen vacancies show a lower barrier (1.36 eV) for CO2 dissociation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]. CeO2 supports also enhance CO2 activation when combined with transition metals [10.1002/anie.201508128].

3. Bimetallic catalysts on CeO2: Fe-Ni systems favor C-H bond activation, while Pt-containing catalysts promote C-C bond cleavage [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

4. Transition metal clusters: Ni4/MgO shows a remarkably low barrier (0.35 eV) for CO2 dissociation, making it highly effective for CO2 activation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12].

The effectiveness of these catalysts in activating CO2 follows the order: Ni4/MgO > CeO2 > VOx/SiO2 > V2O5. This trend is based on the energy barriers for CO2 dissociation or activation, which is the proposed mechanism for CO2 utilization in these systems.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The research on CO2-assisted ODH of alkanes has revealed promising pathways for more sustainable and selective olefin production. The use of CO2 as a mild oxidant offers a unique opportunity to address multiple challenges in the field of catalysis and petrochemical industry.

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Developing novel multi-component catalysts that combine the CO2 activation ability of CeO2 with the selective C-H bond activation properties of transition metals.

2. Exploring the potential of other redox-inactive supports or 2D materials that can facilitate CO2 activation while maintaining high selectivity towards desired olefins.

3. Investigating the long-term stability and regeneration strategies for CO2-ODH catalysts, particularly addressing issues related to coke formation and metal sintering.

4. Integrating CO2-ODH processes with CO2 capture technologies to create a more sustainable and circular approach to olefin production.

5. Conducting detailed mechanistic studies using advanced in-situ and operando spectroscopic techniques to better understand the interplay between CO2 activation, C-H bond cleavage, and catalyst regeneration.

6. Exploring the potential of CO2-ODH for activating more challenging substrates, such as methane or higher alkanes, to expand the scope of this technology.

By addressing these research directions, the field of CO2-assisted ODH has the potential to revolutionize the production of valuable chemical feedstocks while simultaneously contributing to CO2 utilization and mitigation strategies.

<Chinese>
氧化脱氢（ODH）反应中，碳氢键活化和催化剂再生的机制在氧化还原活性和非氧化还原活性的金属氧化物催化剂之间存在显著差异。本综述聚焦于这些机制，传统氧化剂和CO2的作用，以及各种催化剂在活化CO2用于丙烷ODH方面的有效性。

氧化还原活性催化剂，如V2O5、VOx/SiO2和Cr2O3，通常通过Mars-van Krevelen（MvK）机制进行反应[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2]。在这个过程中，晶格氧参与C-H键活化，形成烯烃并还原金属中心。然后，催化剂通过气相氧化剂再氧化而再生[10.1021/jp810664u]。对于钒基催化剂，烷烃分子中最弱C-H键的断裂是反应的限速步骤，反应活性随C-H键强度的降低而增加：C2H6 < C3H8 < n-C4H10 < iso-C4H10 [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.09.012]。

非氧化还原活性催化剂，如Ga2O3和h-BN，通过不同的机制进行反应。它们通常通过酸碱对上的异裂解离来活化C-H键，不涉及金属氧化态的变化[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9]。与氧化还原活性催化剂相比，这些催化剂对碳氧化物的选择性较低，对目标烯烃的选择性较高[10.1002/anie.202002440]。

氧化剂的选择显著影响催化剂的性能和选择性。传统的氧化剂如O2可以完全再氧化氧化还原活性催化剂，但可能导致过度氧化和较低的选择性。CO2作为一种温和的氧化剂，提供了几个优势：

1. 抑制过度氧化，最大限度地减少碳氧化物的产生[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。
2. 通过逆水气变换（RWGS）反应增强丙烷转化[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。
3. 减少催化剂表面的积碳形成[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。

不同催化剂在活化CO2用于丙烷ODH方面的有效性各不相同：

1. V2O5和VOx/SiO2：中等有效，CO2可以再氧化部分还原的金属氧化物。与纯V2O5（3.16 eV）相比，VOx/SiO2显示出较低的CO2活化能垒（1.76 eV）[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。

2. 基于CeO2的催化剂：由于其通过直接C=O键断裂活化CO2的能力而具有高效性。具有氧空位的CeO2(110)表面显示出较低的CO2解离能垒（1.36 eV）[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。当与过渡金属结合时，CeO2载体还可以增强CO2的活化[10.1002/anie.201508128]。

3. CeO2上的双金属催化剂：Fe-Ni系统有利于C-H键断裂，而含Pt的催化剂促进C-C键断裂[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

4. 过渡金属簇：Ni4/MgO显示出remarkably低的CO2解离能垒（0.35 eV），使其在CO2活化方面高度有效[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。

这些催化剂在活化CO2方面的有效性顺序为：Ni4/MgO > CeO2 > VOx/SiO2 > V2O5。这一趋势基于CO2解离或活化的能垒，这是这些系统中CO2利用的proposed机制。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：CO2辅助烷烃ODH的研究揭示了更可持续和选择性烯烃生产的promising途径。使用CO2作为温和氧化剂为解决催化和石油化工行业的多重挑战提供了独特机会。

未来研究方向可能集中在：

1. 开发新型多组分催化剂，结合CeO2的CO2活化能力和过渡金属的选择性C-H键活化特性。

2. 探索其他非氧化还原活性载体或二维材料的潜力，这些材料可以促进CO2活化，同时保持对目标烯烃的高选择性。

3. 研究CO2-ODH催化剂的长期稳定性和再生策略，特别是解决与积碳形成和金属烧结相关的问题。

4. 将CO2-ODH过程与CO2捕获技术结合，创造一种更可持续和循环的烯烃生产方法。

5. 使用先进的原位和操作中光谱技术进行详细的机理研究，以更好地理解CO2活化、C-H键断裂和催化剂再生之间的相互作用。

6. 探索CO2-ODH在活化更具挑战性的底物（如甲烷或更高级烷烃）方面的潜力，以扩大这项技术的应用范围。

通过解决这些研究方向，CO2辅助ODH领域有潜力彻底改变有价值化学原料的生产，同时为CO2利用和减缓策略做出贡献。
