<English>
The mechanisms of C-H bond activation and catalyst regeneration in oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) of alkanes differ significantly between redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts. This review section will explore these mechanisms, emphasizing the role of different oxidants, particularly CO2, and the effectiveness of various catalysts in activating CO2 for propane ODH.

Redox-active catalysts, such as V2O5, VOx/SiO2, and Cr2O3, typically operate via a Mars-van Krevelen (MvK) mechanism [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025]. In this process, lattice oxygen participates in C-H bond activation, forming alkenes and reducing the metal center. The catalyst is then regenerated by re-oxidation with gas-phase oxidants [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.09.012]. The breaking of the weakest C-H bond in the alkane molecule is generally considered the rate-limiting step, with reactivity increasing as bond strength decreases in the order C2H6 < C3H8 < n-C4H10 < iso-C4H10 [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.09.012].

In contrast, redox-inactive catalysts like Ga2O3 or CeO2 operate through different mechanisms. These catalysts activate C-H bonds through heterolytic dissociation over acid-base pairs or via surface oxygen species, without involving changes in metal oxidation states [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9]. The catalyst regeneration occurs through direct reduction of the oxidant on the surface rather than through redox cycling of the metal [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12].

The choice of oxidant significantly impacts catalyst performance and selectivity. Traditional oxidants like O2 can fully re-oxidize redox-active catalysts but may lead to over-oxidation and lower selectivity. CO2, as a milder oxidant, offers several advantages:

1. Suppression of over-oxidation, minimizing carbon oxide production [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].
2. Enhanced propane conversion through the reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) reaction [10.1039/D0CY00586J].
3. Reduced coke formation on the catalyst surface [10.1039/D0CY00586J].

For redox-active catalysts, CO2 can partially re-oxidize reduced metal centers. For example, CO2 can oxidize V2O3 to V2O4 in vanadium-based catalysts, but not to V2O5, leading to higher propylene selectivity compared to O2 [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015].

The effectiveness of different catalysts in activating CO2 for propane ODH varies:

1. V2O5 and VOx/SiO2: These catalysts show moderate activity, with CO2 activation barriers of 3.16 eV and 1.76 eV, respectively [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12].
2. CeO2-based catalysts: These are particularly promising due to their ability to facilitate CO2 dissociation, with a lower activation barrier of 1.36 eV on oxygen vacancy sites [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12].
3. Bimetallic catalysts on CeO2 support: Fe-Ni/CeO2 favors C-H bond activation, while Pt-containing catalysts promote C-C bond cleavage [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].
4. Transition metal clusters: Ni4/MgO shows a remarkably low CO2 dissociation barrier of 0.35 eV, making it highly effective for CO2 activation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12].

The effectiveness of these catalysts in activating CO2 generally follows the order: Ni4/MgO > CeO2 > VOx/SiO2 > V2O5 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of CO2-assisted ODH of alkanes presents exciting opportunities for developing more efficient and sustainable catalytic processes. The use of CO2 as a mild oxidant offers a promising route to enhance selectivity and reduce carbon footprint simultaneously. Future research directions may include:

1. Rational design of multi-functional catalysts: Combining the CO2 activation ability of CeO2 with the selective C-H bond activation properties of transition metals could lead to highly efficient catalysts for CO2-assisted ODH.

2. In-depth mechanistic studies: Further investigation into the precise mechanisms of CO2 activation and its role in the ODH process could provide insights for optimizing catalyst compositions and reaction conditions.

3. Exploration of novel catalyst supports: Developing supports with tailored oxygen mobility and storage capacity could enhance CO2 activation and improve catalyst stability.

4. Process intensification: Integrating CO2-assisted ODH with other reactions, such as the reverse water-gas shift, could lead to more efficient utilization of CO2 and improved overall process economics.

5. Computational screening: Leveraging high-throughput computational methods to predict catalyst performance and guide experimental efforts could accelerate the discovery of novel, high-performance catalysts for CO2-assisted ODH.

These research avenues could pave the way for more sustainable and economically viable processes for olefin production, contributing to the broader goals of CO2 utilization and green chemistry.

<Chinese>
在烷烃氧化脱氢（ODH）反应中，C-H键活化和催化剂再生的机制在氧化还原活性和氧化还原惰性金属氧化物催化剂之间存在显著差异。本综述部分将探讨这些机制，重点关注不同氧化剂（尤其是CO2）的作用，以及各种催化剂在活化CO2用于丙烷ODH方面的有效性。

氧化还原活性催化剂，如V2O5、VOx/SiO2和Cr2O3，通常通过Mars-van Krevelen（MvK）机制运作[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025]。在这个过程中，晶格氧参与C-H键活化，形成烯烃并还原金属中心。然后催化剂通过气相氧化剂再氧化而再生[10.1016/j.cattod.2008.09.012]。烷烃分子中最弱C-H键的断裂通常被认为是限速步骤，反应活性随着键强度的降低而增加，顺序为C2H6 < C3H8 < n-C4H10 < iso-C4H10 [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.09.012]。

相比之下，氧化还原惰性催化剂如Ga2O3或CeO2通过不同的机制运作。这些催化剂通过酸碱对上的异裂解离或通过表面氧物种活化C-H键，而不涉及金属氧化态的变化[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9]。催化剂再生通过氧化剂在表面上的直接还原进行，而不是通过金属的氧化还原循环[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。

氧化剂的选择显著影响催化剂性能和选择性。传统氧化剂如O2可以完全再氧化氧化还原活性催化剂，但可能导致过度氧化和较低的选择性。CO2作为一种较温和的氧化剂，提供了几个优势：

1. 抑制过度氧化，最小化一氧化碳和二氧化碳的产生[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。
2. 通过逆水气变换（RWGS）反应增强丙烷转化[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。
3. 减少催化剂表面的积碳形成[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。

对于氧化还原活性催化剂，CO2可以部分再氧化还原的金属中心。例如，在钒基催化剂中，CO2可以将V2O3氧化为V2O4，但不能氧化为V2O5，与O2相比，这导致更高的丙烯选择性[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。

不同催化剂在活化CO2用于丙烷ODH方面的有效性各不相同：

1. V2O5和VOx/SiO2：这些催化剂显示中等活性，CO2活化能垒分别为3.16 eV和1.76 eV [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。
2. 基于CeO2的催化剂：由于它们能够促进CO2解离，在氧空位位点上具有较低的活化能垒1.36 eV，因此特别有前景[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。
3. CeO2支撑的双金属催化剂：Fe-Ni/CeO2有利于C-H键断裂，而含Pt的催化剂促进C-C键断裂[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。
4. 过渡金属簇：Ni4/MgO显示出remarkably低的CO2解离能垒0.35 eV，使其在CO2活化方面高度有效[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。

这些催化剂在活化CO2方面的有效性通常遵循以下顺序：Ni4/MgO > CeO2 > VOx/SiO2 > V2O5 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：CO2辅助烷烃ODH领域为开发更高效和可持续的催化过程提供了令人兴奋的机会。使用CO2作为温和氧化剂提供了一种有前景的路径，可以同时提高选择性并减少碳足迹。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 多功能催化剂的理性设计：将CeO2的CO2活化能力与过渡金属的选择性C-H键活化性能相结合，可能会产生高效的CO2辅助ODH催化剂。

2. 深入的机理研究：对CO2活化的精确机制及其在ODH过程中的作用进行进一步研究，可以为优化催化剂组成和反应条件提供见解。

3. 新型催化剂载体的探索：开发具有定制氧移动性和储存能力的载体，可以增强CO2活化并提高催化剂稳定性。

4. 过程强化：将CO2辅助ODH与其他反应（如逆水气变换）集成，可能会导致CO2的更有效利用和改善整体过程经济性。

5. 计算筛选：利用高通量计算方法预测催化剂性能并指导实验努力，可以加速发现用于CO2辅助ODH的新型高性能催化剂。

这些研究方向可能为烯烃生产提供更可持续和经济可行的过程，为CO2利用和绿色化学的更广泛目标做出贡献。
