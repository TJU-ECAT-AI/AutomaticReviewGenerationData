<English>
The mechanisms of C-H bond activation and catalyst regeneration in oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) of alkanes differ significantly between redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts. This review will focus on these mechanisms, the role of traditional oxidants and CO2, and the effectiveness of different catalysts in activating CO2 for ODH of propane.

Redox-active catalysts, such as V2O5, VOx/SiO2, and Cr2O3, typically operate via a Mars-van Krevelen (MvK) mechanism [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.09.012]. In this process, lattice oxygen from the metal oxide participates in C-H bond activation, forming the desired alkene and reducing the metal center. The catalyst is then regenerated by re-oxidation with a gas-phase oxidant [10.1021/jp810664u]. The rate-limiting step in this mechanism is usually the breaking of the weakest C-H bond in the alkane molecule [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.09.012].

In contrast, redox-inactive catalysts like Ga2O3 and h-BN operate through different mechanisms. These catalysts typically activate C-H bonds through heterolytic dissociation over acid-base pairs and do not involve changes in metal oxidation states [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9] [10.1002/anie.202002440]. The catalyst surface brings together hydrogen and the oxidant, which is reduced directly without forming oxygen vacancies [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12].

Traditional oxidants like O2 can fully re-oxidize redox-active catalysts, but they often lead to over-oxidation and lower selectivity. CO2, as a milder oxidant, offers several advantages:

1. It suppresses over-oxidation and minimizes carbon oxide production [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].
2. It can increase propane conversion by consuming H2 through the reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) reaction [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].
3. It helps maintain higher oxidation states of metal species, particularly for V2O5 and Cr2O3 [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2].
4. It assists in coke removal and participates in the Boudouard reaction [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015].

The effectiveness of different catalysts in activating CO2 for ODH of propane varies:

1. V2O5 and VOx/SiO2: These catalysts show moderate activity. CO2 can re-oxidize V2O3 to V2O4, but not to V2O5, leading to higher propylene selectivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]. However, the energy barrier for CO2 activation is still significant (1.76 eV for VOx/SiO2) [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12].

2. CeO2-based catalysts: These are considered highly promising due to their ability to activate CO2 via direct C=O bond scission [10.1039/D0CY00586J]. CeO2 can store and release oxygen, facilitating CO2 dissociation with a lower energy barrier (1.36 eV) [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12].

3. Bimetallic catalysts on CeO2: Fe-Ni/CeO2 favors C-H bond activation for the ODH pathway, while Pt-containing catalysts tend to promote C-C bond cleavage [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

4. Transition metal clusters: Some studies have shown promising results, such as Ni4/MgO with a low CO2 dissociation barrier of 0.35 eV [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12].

The effectiveness of these catalysts in activating CO2 generally follows the order: Transition metal clusters > CeO2-based catalysts > VOx/SiO2 > V2O5 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of efficient catalysts for CO2-assisted ODH of alkanes represents a promising direction for sustainable chemical production. The use of CO2 as a mild oxidant offers multiple benefits, including improved selectivity, reduced carbon formation, and potential CO2 utilization. Future research should focus on:

1. Designing multi-functional catalysts that combine the CO2 activation ability of CeO2 with the selective C-H bond activation properties of transition metals.
2. Exploring novel bimetallic compositions to optimize the balance between ODH, RWGS, and dry reforming pathways.
3. Investigating the role of support materials in enhancing CO2 activation and improving catalyst stability.
4. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation states during CO2-assisted ODH reactions.
5. Exploring the potential of artificial intelligence and machine learning in predicting and designing more effective catalyst compositions for CO2-assisted ODH.

These research directions could lead to more efficient and sustainable processes for alkane conversion, contributing to the broader goals of carbon dioxide utilization and green chemistry.

<Chinese>
烷烃氧化脱氢（ODH）反应中，C-H 键活化和催化剂再生的机理在氧化还原活性和非氧化还原活性金属氧化物催化剂之间存在显著差异。本综述将重点关注这些机理、传统氧化剂和 CO2 的作用，以及不同催化剂在活化 CO2 用于丙烷 ODH 方面的有效性。

氧化还原活性催化剂，如 V2O5、VOx/SiO2 和 Cr2O3，通常通过 Mars-van Krevelen（MvK）机理运作 [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.09.012]。在这个过程中，金属氧化物的晶格氧参与 C-H 键活化，形成所需的烯烃并还原金属中心。然后，催化剂通过气相氧化剂再氧化而再生 [10.1021/jp810664u]。这种机理中的限速步骤通常是烷烃分子中最弱 C-H 键的断裂 [10.1016/j.cattod.2008.09.012]。

相比之下，非氧化还原活性催化剂如 Ga2O3 和 h-BN 通过不同的机理运作。这些催化剂通常通过酸碱对上的异裂解离活化 C-H 键，不涉及金属氧化态的变化 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9] [10.1002/anie.202002440]。催化剂表面将氢和氧化剂结合在一起，氧化剂直接被还原，而不形成氧空位 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。

传统氧化剂如 O2 可以完全再氧化氧化还原活性催化剂，但它们常常导致过度氧化和较低的选择性。CO2 作为一种温和的氧化剂，提供了几个优势：

1. 它抑制过度氧化并最小化一氧化碳的产生 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。
2. 它可以通过逆水气变换（RWGS）反应消耗 H2，从而增加丙烷转化率 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。
3. 它有助于维持金属物种的较高氧化态，特别是对于 V2O5 和 Cr2O3 [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00268-2]。
4. 它协助焦炭去除并参与布多瓦反应 [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。

不同催化剂在活化 CO2 用于丙烷 ODH 方面的有效性各不相同：

1. V2O5 和 VOx/SiO2：这些催化剂显示出中等活性。CO2 可以将 V2O3 再氧化为 V2O4，但不能氧化为 V2O5，从而导致更高的丙烯选择性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。然而，CO2 活化的能垒仍然很高（VOx/SiO2 为 1.76 eV）[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。

2. 基于 CeO2 的催化剂：这些被认为是非常有前景的，因为它们能够通过直接 C=O 键断裂来活化 CO2 [10.1039/D0CY00586J]。CeO2 可以储存和释放氧，促进 CO2 解离，能垒较低（1.36 eV）[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。

3. CeO2 上的双金属催化剂：Fe-Ni/CeO2 有利于 ODH 途径的 C-H 键断裂，而含 Pt 的催化剂倾向于促进 C-C 键断裂 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

4. 过渡金属簇：一些研究显示了有希望的结果，例如 Ni4/MgO 的 CO2 解离能垒低至 0.35 eV [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。

这些催化剂在活化 CO2 方面的有效性通常遵循以下顺序：过渡金属簇 > 基于 CeO2 的催化剂 > VOx/SiO2 > V2O5 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：开发高效的 CO2 辅助烷烃 ODH 催化剂代表了可持续化学生产的一个有希望的方向。使用 CO2 作为温和的氧化剂提供了多重益处，包括改善选择性、减少碳形成和潜在的 CO2 利用。未来的研究应该集中在：

1. 设计多功能催化剂，结合 CeO2 的 CO2 活化能力和过渡金属的选择性 C-H 键活化特性。
2. 探索新型双金属组成，以优化 ODH、RWGS 和干重整途径之间的平衡。
3. 研究载体材料在增强 CO2 活化和提高催化剂稳定性方面的作用。
4. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解 CO2 辅助 ODH 反应过程中催化剂结构和氧化态的动态变化。
5. 探索人工智能和机器学习在预测和设计更有效的 CO2 辅助 ODH 催化剂组成方面的潜力。

这些研究方向可能会导致更高效和可持续的烷烃转化过程，为二氧化碳利用和绿色化学的更广泛目标做出贡献。
