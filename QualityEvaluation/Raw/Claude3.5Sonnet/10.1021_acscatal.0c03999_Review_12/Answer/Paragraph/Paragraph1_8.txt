<English>
The mechanisms of C-H bond activation and catalyst regeneration in oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) of alkanes differ significantly between redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts. This review section will focus on these mechanisms, emphasizing the role of traditional oxidants and CO2, and discuss the effectiveness of various catalysts in activating CO2 for ODH of propane.

Redox-active catalysts, such as V2O5, VOx/SiO2, and Cr2O3, typically operate via a Mars-van Krevelen (MvK) mechanism [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025]. In this process, lattice oxygen participates in C-H bond activation, forming alkenes and reduced metal sites. The catalyst is then regenerated through re-oxidation by gas-phase oxidants [10.1021/jp810664u]. For traditional oxidants like O2, this re-oxidation is rapid and efficient, often keeping the catalyst in a fully oxidized state [10.1021/jp810664u].

When CO2 is used as an oxidant, the mechanism becomes more complex. CO2 acts as a milder oxidant compared to O2, suppressing over-oxidation and minimizing carbon oxide production [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. For vanadium-based catalysts, CO2 can oxidize V2O3 to V2O4, but not to V2O5, resulting in a partially reduced catalyst state that favors higher propylene selectivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]. The ODH reaction with CO2 is proposed to occur in two steps: first, the catalyst dehydrogenates the alkane, producing H2; then, H2 reduces CO2 to CO through the reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) reaction [10.1002/anie.201508128].

Redox-inactive catalysts, such as Ga2O3 or CeO2, operate through different mechanisms. These catalysts typically activate C-H bonds through heterolytic dissociation over acid-base pairs, without involving changes in metal oxidation states [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9]. CO2 activation on these catalysts often occurs through direct C=O bond scission, particularly on CeO2 surfaces with oxygen vacancies [10.1039/D0CY00586J].

The effectiveness of different catalysts in activating CO2 for ODH of propane varies significantly:

1. V2O5 and VOx/SiO2: These catalysts show moderate effectiveness. The barrier for CO2 to re-oxidize VOx/SiO2 is 1.76 eV, which is lower than pure V2O5 but still significant [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12].

2. CeO2-based catalysts: These are highly effective in CO2 activation due to their oxygen storage capacity and ability to form oxygen vacancies. CO2 dissociation on CeO2 (110) surfaces has a rate-limiting barrier of 1.36 eV [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12].

3. Bimetallic catalysts on CeO2: Fe-Ni/CeO2 catalysts favor C-H bond activation, while Pt-containing catalysts tend to promote C-C bond cleavage [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

4. Transition metal clusters: Some, like Ni4/MgO, show exceptionally low barriers for CO2 dissociation (0.35 eV), making them highly effective for CO2 activation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12].

The effectiveness of these catalysts in activating CO2 generally follows the order: Transition metal clusters > CeO2-based catalysts > VOx/SiO2 > V2O5. This trend is based on the energy barriers for CO2 dissociation or activation, which is crucial for CO2 utilization in ODH reactions.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of CO2-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of alkanes presents exciting opportunities for sustainable chemical production. The use of CO2 as a mild oxidant offers advantages in selectivity and coke suppression, addressing key challenges in traditional ODH processes. Future research directions may focus on:

1. Developing novel bimetallic catalysts that combine the CO2 activation properties of CeO2 with the selective C-H activation abilities of transition metals.

2. Exploring the potential of single-atom catalysts or metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) for precise control over active sites and enhanced CO2 activation.

3. Investigating the role of catalyst support interactions in tuning the balance between ODH, RWGS, and dry reforming pathways.

4. Utilizing advanced in-situ characterization techniques and machine learning approaches to gain deeper insights into the complex mechanisms involved in CO2-assisted ODH.

5. Exploring the potential of plasma-assisted or photo-catalytic processes to further enhance CO2 activation and improve energy efficiency in ODH reactions.

These research avenues could lead to more efficient and sustainable processes for olefin production, contributing to the broader goal of CO2 utilization in the chemical industry.

在烷烃的氧化脱氢（ODH）反应中，C-H键活化和催化剂再生的机理在氧化还原活性和氧化还原惰性金属氧化物催化剂之间存在显著差异。本综述部分将重点关注这些机理，强调传统氧化剂和CO2的作用，并讨论各种催化剂在活化CO2用于丙烷ODH方面的有效性。

氧化还原活性催化剂，如V2O5、VOx/SiO2和Cr2O3，通常通过Mars-van Krevelen（MvK）机理运作[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.05.025]。在这个过程中，晶格氧参与C-H键活化，形成烯烃和还原态金属位点。然后，催化剂通过气相氧化剂的再氧化而再生[10.1021/jp810664u]。对于传统氧化剂如O2，这种再氧化过程迅速且高效，通常使催化剂保持在完全氧化状态[10.1021/jp810664u]。

当使用CO2作为氧化剂时，机理变得更加复杂。与O2相比，CO2作为一种更温和的氧化剂，抑制过度氧化并最小化碳氧化物的产生[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。对于钒基催化剂，CO2可以将V2O3氧化成V2O4，但不能氧化成V2O5，导致催化剂处于部分还原状态，有利于提高丙烯选择性[10.1016/j.jcat.2015.12.015]。使用CO2的ODH反应被提出通过两个步骤进行：首先，催化剂使烷烃脱氢，产生H2；然后，H2通过逆水气变换（RWGS）反应将CO2还原为CO [10.1002/anie.201508128]。

氧化还原惰性催化剂，如Ga2O3或CeO2，通过不同的机理运作。这些催化剂通常通过酸碱对上的异裂解离活化C-H键，而不涉及金属氧化态的变化[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9]。在这些催化剂上，CO2的活化通常通过直接C=O键断裂进行，特别是在具有氧空位的CeO2表面上[10.1039/D0CY00586J]。

不同催化剂在活化CO2用于丙烷ODH方面的有效性差异显著：

1. V2O5和VOx/SiO2：这些催化剂显示出中等有效性。CO2再氧化VOx/SiO2的能垒为1.76 eV，低于纯V2O5但仍然显著[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。

2. 基于CeO2的催化剂：由于其氧存储能力和形成氧空位的能力，这些催化剂在CO2活化方面非常有效。CO2在CeO2（110）表面上的解离有一个1.36 eV的速率限制能垒[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。

3. CeO2上的双金属催化剂：Fe-Ni/CeO2催化剂有利于C-H键活化，而含Pt的催化剂倾向于促进C-C键断裂[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

4. 过渡金属簇：一些如Ni4/MgO的催化剂显示出异常低的CO2解离能垒（0.35 eV），使它们在CO2活化方面非常有效[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_12]。

这些催化剂在活化CO2方面的有效性通常遵循以下顺序：过渡金属簇 > 基于CeO2的催化剂 > VOx/SiO2 > V2O5。这一趋势基于CO2解离或活化的能垒，这对于ODH反应中的CO2利用至关重要。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：CO2辅助烷烃氧化脱氢领域为可持续化学品生产提供了令人兴奋的机会。使用CO2作为温和氧化剂在选择性和焦炭抑制方面提供了优势，解决了传统ODH过程中的关键挑战。未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 开发新型双金属催化剂，结合CeO2的CO2活化特性和过渡金属的选择性C-H活化能力。

2. 探索单原子催化剂或金属有机框架（MOFs）的潜力，以精确控制活性位点并增强CO2活化。

3. 研究催化剂载体相互作用在调节ODH、RWGS和干重整途径平衡方面的作用。

4. 利用先进的原位表征技术和机器学习方法，深入了解CO2辅助ODH中涉及的复杂机理。

5. 探索等离子体辅助或光催化过程的潜力，以进一步增强CO2活化并提高ODH反应的能量效率。

这些研究方向可能导致更高效和可持续的烯烃生产过程，为化工行业更广泛的CO2利用目标做出贡献。
