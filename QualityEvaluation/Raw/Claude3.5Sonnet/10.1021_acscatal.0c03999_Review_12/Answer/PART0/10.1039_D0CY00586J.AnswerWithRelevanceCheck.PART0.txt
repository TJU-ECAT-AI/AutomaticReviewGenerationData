      <English>
          The mechanisms of C-H bond activation and catalyst regeneration differ between redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts in oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) of alkanes.

For redox-active catalysts like V2O5 or VOx/SiO2, the Mars-van Krevelen mechanism typically operates. This involves lattice oxygen participating in C-H bond activation, followed by catalyst re-oxidation by gas-phase oxygen. The catalyst undergoes redox cycles during the reaction.

In contrast, redox-inactive catalysts like CeO2 cannot easily change oxidation states. They likely activate C-H bonds through surface oxygen species or defect sites. Regeneration occurs through gas-phase oxygen replenishing surface oxygen rather than redox cycling of the metal.

When using CO2 instead of O2 as the oxidant, the mechanisms change. CO2 acts as a softer oxidant that can avoid deep oxidation of products. It participates in the reverse water-gas shift reaction, which favors alkane conversion.

Different catalysts show varying effectiveness in activating CO2 for propane ODH:

- CeO2 is considered highly promising due to its ability to directly cleave the C=O bond in CO2.
- Fe-Ni bimetallic catalysts on CeO2 favor C-H bond activation.
- Pt-containing bimetallic catalysts tend to promote C-C cleavage instead.

The effectiveness depends on the catalyst's ability to lower energy barriers for CO2 dissociation and subsequent ODH steps. CeO2-based catalysts, especially when doped with transition metals like Fe, show good potential for CO2 activation and selective ODH of propane.

The bimetallic composition can be tuned to balance competitive pathways like ODH, reverse water-gas shift, and dry reforming to optimize olefin selectivity. Overall, CO2-assisted ODH over metal-doped CeO2 catalysts offers a promising approach by combining the benefits of CO2 as a soft oxidant with tunable C-H activation properties of transition metals and the CO2 activation ability of CeO2.

      <Chinese>
          在烷烃的氧化脱氢(ODH)反应中，氧化还原活性和非氧化还原活性金属氧化物催化剂的C-H键活化和催化剂再生机理存在差异。

对于V2O5或VOx/SiO2等氧化还原活性催化剂，通常遵循Mars-van Krevelen机理。这涉及晶格氧参与C-H键活化，随后由气相氧重新氧化催化剂。催化剂在反应过程中经历氧化还原循环。

相比之下，像CeO2这样的非氧化还原活性催化剂不能轻易改变氧化态。它们可能通过表面氧物种或缺陷位点活化C-H键。再生是通过气相氧补充表面氧来实现的，而不是金属的氧化还原循环。

当使用CO2而不是O2作为氧化剂时，机理发生变化。CO2作为一种较弱的氧化剂，可以避免产物的深度氧化。它参与逆水煤气反应，有利于烷烃转化。

不同的催化剂在活化CO2用于丙烷ODH方面显示出不同的效果:

- CeO2由于其直接裂解CO2中C=O键的能力而被认为是非常有前途的。
- CeO2上的Fe-Ni双金属催化剂有利于C-H键活化。
- 含Pt的双金属催化剂倾向于促进C-C键裂解。

效果取决于催化剂降低CO2解离和随后ODH步骤能量障碍的能力。基于CeO2的催化剂，尤其是掺杂Fe等过渡金属时，在CO2活化和选择性丙烷ODH方面表现出良好的潜力。

可以调节双金属组成以平衡ODH、逆水煤气反应和干重整等竞争性反应途径，以优化烯烃选择性。总的来说，在金属掺杂CeO2催化剂上进行CO2辅助ODH提供了一种有前景的方法，它结合了CO2作为温和氧化剂的优势，过渡金属可调的C-H活化特性，以及CeO2的CO2活化能力。
