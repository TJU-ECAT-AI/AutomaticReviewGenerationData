The mechanisms of C-H bond activation and catalyst regeneration in oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) of alkanes differ between redox-active and redox-inactive metal oxide catalysts:

For redox-active metal oxides like Cr, V, and Co oxides, the ODH process typically follows a Mars-van Krevelen mechanism. In this mechanism, the metal oxide catalyst provides lattice oxygen for C-H bond activation, forming the alkene and leaving an oxygen vacancy. The catalyst is then regenerated by oxidation with CO2, which fills the lattice oxygen vacancy.

In contrast, redox-inactive metal oxides like Ga oxides operate through a different mechanism involving heterolytic dissociation of the alkane and activation of CO2. This process does not rely on the redox properties of the catalyst.

Traditional oxidants like O2 can lead to over-oxidation and formation of CO2, while CO2 acts as a milder oxidant in ODH reactions. CO2 can modify catalyst surfaces, influencing activity and selectivity.

Different catalysts show varying effectiveness in activating CO2 for ODH of propane:

1. V2O5 and supported VOx catalysts: These can follow either a one-step ODH pathway or a two-step pathway involving the reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) reaction.

2. CeO2: While not explicitly discussed for propane ODH, CeO2 is known for its oxygen storage capacity and ability to activate CO2, which could be beneficial for ODH reactions.

3. Transition metal clusters: Bimetallic catalysts like Fe-Ni supported on CeO2 have shown promise in CO2 activation and selective C-H bond cleavage.

4. Mo2C: DFT calculations suggest that Mo2C has a high affinity for atomic oxygen adsorption and favors C-H bond cleavage over C-C bond scission in ethane ODH with CO2.

The effectiveness of these catalysts in CO2 activation and ODH depends on factors such as metal-support interactions, redox properties, and the ability to selectively cleave C-H bonds while preserving C-C bonds. The energy barriers for CO2 dissociation vary among catalysts, with some materials like CeO2 and certain transition metal clusters showing particular promise in facilitating CO2 activation.

烷烃氧化脱氢（ODH）反应中C-H键活化和催化剂再生的机制在氧化还原活性和非氧化还原活性金属氧化物催化剂之间存在差异：

对于Cr、V和Co等氧化还原活性金属氧化物，ODH过程通常遵循Mars-van Krevelen机制。在这种机制中，金属氧化物催化剂提供晶格氧用于C-H键活化，形成烯烃并留下氧空位。然后，催化剂通过与CO2的氧化作用再生，填补晶格氧空位。

相比之下，像Ga氧化物这样的非氧化还原活性金属氧化物通过不同的机制运作，包括烷烃的异裂解离和CO2的活化。这个过程不依赖于催化剂的氧化还原性质。

传统氧化剂如O2可能导致过度氧化和CO2的形成，而CO2在ODH反应中作为一种较温和的氧化剂。CO2可以修饰催化剂表面，影响活性和选择性。

不同的催化剂在活化CO2用于丙烷ODH方面表现出不同的效果：

1. V2O5和负载型VOx催化剂：这些可以遵循单步ODH途径或涉及逆水气变换（RWGS）反应的两步途径。

2. CeO2：虽然没有明确讨论用于丙烷ODH，但CeO2以其氧储存能力和活化CO2的能力而闻名，这可能对ODH反应有利。

3. 过渡金属簇：像负载在CeO2上的Fe-Ni这样的双金属催化剂在CO2活化和选择性C-H键断裂方面表现出前景。

4. Mo2C：DFT计算表明，Mo2C对原子氧吸附具有高亲和力，并且在CO2存在下的乙烷ODH中倾向于C-H键断裂而非C-C键断裂。

这些催化剂在CO2活化和ODH中的效果取决于金属-载体相互作用、氧化还原性质以及选择性断裂C-H键同时保留C-C键的能力等因素。CO2解离的能量障碍在不同催化剂之间有所不同，其中一些材料如CeO2和某些过渡金属簇在促进CO2活化方面表现出特别的前景。
