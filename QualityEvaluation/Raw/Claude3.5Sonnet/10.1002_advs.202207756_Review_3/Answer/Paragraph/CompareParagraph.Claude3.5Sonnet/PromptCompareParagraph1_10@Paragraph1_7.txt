The Ga/Al ratio significantly affects the formation and distribution of active gallium species. At low Ga loadings, the intensity of GaHx species increases linearly, reaching a maximum at a Ga/Al ratio of approximately 0.1 [10.1002/advs.202207756\_3]. Further increases in the Ga/Al ratio lead to a decline in GaHx intensity, suggesting an optimal Ga loading for the formation of these active species [10.1002/advs.202207756\_3]. The turnover frequency (TOF) for PDH reactions also shows a complex dependence on the Ga/Al ratio, with an initial rapid increase at low Ga loadings, followed by a slower increase, and eventually a decline at higher Ga/Al ratios [10.1002/advs.202207756\_3].

2. Si/Al Ratio:
The Si/Al ratio of the zeolite framework plays a crucial role in determining the density of framework aluminum pairs, which in turn influences the formation of active gallium species. Lower Si/Al ratios generally result in higher densities of paired Br√∏nsted acid sites (BAS), which favor the formation of Ga+H+ pair sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.11.004]. This distribution of gallium species directly impacts the catalyst's performance in PDH reactions.

3. Framework Aluminum Pair Density:
The density of framework aluminum pairs is crucial for the formation and stabilization of active gallium species. A linear correlation has been observed between the framework Al pair density and the amount of GaHx formed [10.1002/advs.202207756\_3]. Framework Al pairs are essential for stabilizing the precursor of GaHx, which is likely a divalent cationic species [10.1002/advs.202207756\_3]. This relationship underscores the importance of controlling the zeolite's composition to optimize the formation of active gallium sites.

4. Nature of Active Gallium Species:
Several types of gallium species have been identified in Ga-modified zeolite catalysts, including Ga2O22+, Ga+, and unreduced Ga(III)xOy oligomers [10.1002/advs.202207756\_3]. Among these, Ga2O22+ has been identified as the most active species for PDH reactions [10.1002/advs.202207756\_3]. The formation of these species is influenced by both the Ga/Al and Si/Al ratios, as well as the framework aluminum pair density.

5. Gallium Hydrides (GaHx):
The formation of gallium hydrides (GaHx) is a key aspect of the catalyst's activity. GaHx species can only form on Ga+H+ pair sites, rather than isolated Ga+ sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.11.004]. The ability to form these hydrides is closely linked to the catalyst's performance in PDH reactions.
