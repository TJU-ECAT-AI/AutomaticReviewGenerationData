2. Si/Al Ratio:
The Si/Al ratio of the zeolite framework significantly influences the distribution of aluminum pairs and, consequently, the formation of active gallium species. Lower Si/Al ratios (higher Al content) result in higher densities of paired Brønsted acid sites (BAS), which favor the formation of Ga+H+ pair sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.11.004]. The density of framework aluminum pairs is directly correlated with the formation of GaHx species [10.1002/advs.202207756\_3]. The ability to form these GaHx species is exclusive to Ga+H+ pair sites and may play a crucial role in the catalyst's activity and selectivity for PDH reactions [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.11.004].

The formation and stability of these species are influenced by both the Ga/Al and Si/Al ratios, as well as the framework aluminum pair density.

Ga3+ ions exchanged into H-ZSM5 are reduced during hydrogen pretreatment and propane reactions, forming the active sites for hydrogen desorption [10.1016/s0167-2991(08)64298-6]. This reduction process is crucial for the formation of catalytically active gallium species.

6. Location of Gallium Species:
The location of gallium species within the zeolite structure also affects their catalytic activity. Non-framework gallium species act as Lewis acids, stabilizing H- species and enabling H+-H- recombination and heterolytic H2 dissociation [10.1016/s0167-2991(08)64298-6]. In contrast, Ga3+ species in tetrahedral framework positions do not catalyze H-desorption or propane aromatization reactions [10.1016/s0167-2991(08)64298-6].
