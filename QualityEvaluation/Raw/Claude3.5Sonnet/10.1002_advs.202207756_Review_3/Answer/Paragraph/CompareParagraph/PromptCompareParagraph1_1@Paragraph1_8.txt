The Ga/Al ratio significantly affects the formation and distribution of active gallium species. At low Ga loadings, the intensity of GaHx bands increases linearly, reaching a maximum at a Ga/Al ratio of about 0.1, before declining at higher ratios [10.1002/advs.202207756\_3]. This suggests an optimal Ga loading for the formation of active gallium hydride species. The turnover frequency (TOF) also shows a complex dependence on the Ga/Al ratio, initially increasing rapidly at low Ga loadings, then more slowly, and finally declining at higher Ga/Al ratios [10.1002/advs.202207756\_3].

The Si/Al ratio of the zeolite directly influences the density of framework aluminum pairs, which in turn affects the formation and stability of active gallium species. Lower Si/Al ratios (higher Al content) result in higher densities of paired Brønsted acid sites (BAS), which favor the formation of Ga+H+ pair sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.11.004]. These Ga+H+ pair sites are capable of forming stable GaHx species, which are crucial for PDH activity [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.11.004]. A linear correlation has been observed between the framework Al pair density and the amount of GaHx formed [10.1002/advs.202207756\_3].

3. Nature of Active Gallium Species:
Three main types of gallium species have been identified in reduced Ga-modified zeolite catalysts: Ga2O22+, Ga+, and unreduced Ga(III)xOy oligomers [10.1002/advs.202207756\_3]. Among these, Ga2O22+ is considered the most active species for PDH reactions [10.1002/advs.202207756\_3]. The formation of these species is influenced by both the Ga/Al and Si/Al ratios. Ga+H+ pair sites, formed by exchanging Ga+ with one of the paired BAS, are capable of forming GaHx species, while isolated Ga+ sites cannot form stable GaHx species at high temperatures [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.11.004].

4. Framework Aluminum Distribution:
The distribution of framework aluminum atoms influences the stability and configuration of gallium species. Different Al distributions result in varying stabilities of gallium oxide structures [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.027]. The presence of framework Al pairs is crucial for stabilizing divalent cationic species, which are likely precursors to GaHx formation [10.1002/advs.202207756\_3].

5. Reduction and Activation of Gallium Species:
Ga3+ species initially form extrazeolitic Ga2O3 crystals and undergo ion exchange during subsequent contact with propane or hydrogen at high temperatures (700-800 K) via vapor phase exchange of volatile Ga1+ species [10.1016/s0920-5861(96)00028-4]. These reduced gallium species act as Lewis acids, stabilizing H- species and enabling H+-H- recombination and heterolytic H2 dissociation [10.1016/s0167-2991(08)64298-6].

The complex interplay between these factors determines the overall catalytic performance of Ga-modified zeolite catalysts in PDH reactions.
