[1] "The reactivity of the transition and noble metals (Cu, Ag and Au) is determined by the surface properties that determine the ability of metal atoms to make and break bonds"

[2] "The d-band model assumes that the variations between adsorbates and surface metal atoms are largely due to the variations in coupling of the metal d-states and adsorbate orbitals. The variation in coupling is due to the difference in the average energy (d-band centre) of the d-bands, which is sensitive to many factors including strain and ligand effects."

[3] "Given the importance of the d-band characteristics in providing explanations for the reactivity of metal surfaces, it is desirable to study and understand the modifications of these electronic properties with respect to different electronic and geometric effects."

[4] "The modifications in d-band characteristics can be estimated through a correlation between the width of the surface d-band for transition metals and the interatomic matrix element (Vdd) that describes bonding interactions between an atom and its environment"

[5] "Here, the ligand effect is incorporated into the matrix element in the numerator through the rd of each metal, whereas the strain is included in the denominator through the distance between atoms."

<EnglishVersionAnswer>

Geometric and electronic effects in transition-metal alloys synergistically influence catalytic performance through several mechanisms:

1. Surface reactivity is determined by the ability of metal atoms to form and break bonds, which is influenced by both geometric arrangement and electronic structure.

2. The d-band model explains variations in reactivity based on coupling between metal d-states and adsorbate orbitals. This coupling is sensitive to both strain (geometric) and ligand (electronic) effects.

3. Modifications to d-band characteristics, which explain reactivity, arise from both electronic and geometric contributions.

4. The interplay between geometric and electronic effects can be quantified through correlations between d-band width and interatomic matrix elements.

5. In alloys, ligand effects arise from the electronic properties of different metals, while strain effects come from changes in interatomic distances.

To exploit this interplay in designing better catalysts:

1. Use computational modeling to rapidly screen alloy combinations, optimizing both composition and structure.

2. Design core-shell nanoparticles with tuned strain and ligand effects.

3. Create surface alloys with controlled distributions of atoms to fine-tune local electronic environments.

4. Utilize high-entropy alloys to access complex electronic structures.

5. Develop adaptive catalysts that can dynamically modify their geometric and electronic structure under reaction conditions.

These strategies can lead to catalysts with enhanced activity, selectivity, and stability compared to traditional monometallic systems by precisely tuning the synergistic interactions between geometric and electronic effects.

过渡金属合金中的几何和电子效应通过以下几种机制协同影响催化性能：

1. 表面反应性由金属原子形成和断裂键的能力决定，这受到几何排列和电子结构的影响。

2. d带模型基于金属d态与吸附质轨道之间的耦合解释反应性的变化。这种耦合对应变（几何）和配体（电子）效应都很敏感。

3. d带特性的修饰，解释了反应性，来自电子和几何贡献。

4. 几何和电子效应之间的相互作用可以通过d带宽度和原子间矩阵元素之间的相关性来量化。

5. 在合金中，配体效应来自不同金属的电子性质，而应变效应来自原子间距离的变化。

为了利用这种相互作用设计更好的催化剂：

1. 使用计算模型快速筛选合金组合，同时优化成分和结构。

2. 设计具有调谐应变和配体效应的核壳纳米粒子。

3. 创建具有受控原子分布的表面合金，以精细调节局部电子环境。

4. 利用高熵合金获得复杂的电子结构。

5. 开发可以在反应条件下动态修改其几何和电子结构的自适应催化剂。

这些策略可以通过精确调节几何和电子效应之间的协同作用，导致与传统单金属系统相比具有增强的活性、选择性和稳定性的催化剂。
