<English>
The comprehensive analysis of Pt-3d catalysts for C═C bond hydrogenation reveals a strong correlation between DFT calculations of surface d-band center and adsorbate binding energies with experimental observations from techniques such as TPD measurements. This integrated theoretical and experimental approach provides valuable insights into catalyst design and performance prediction.

DFT calculations consistently show that modifying Pt(111) surfaces with subsurface 3d transition metals alters the average energy of the surface Pt d-band center [10.1016/j.apcata.2008.01.024]. This modification leads to weaker binding of hydrogen and unsaturated hydrocarbons on bimetallic surfaces compared to monometallic Pt surfaces [10.1039/c2gc16544a]. The weaker binding correlates with higher hydrogenation activity observed experimentally for various C═C bond-containing molecules, including linear alkenes, cyclic hydrocarbons, and aromatics [10.1016/j.cattod.2010.05.014].

For instance, PtNiPt(111) and PtCoPt(111) structures consistently demonstrate lower binding energies for molecules like cyclohexene, 1,3-butadiene, and benzene compared to Pt(111) [10.1016/j.susc.2008.05.035]. These theoretical predictions align well with experimental observations of enhanced hydrogenation activity for PtNi and PtCo catalysts in reactions involving 1,3-butadiene, 1,4-cyclohexadiene, and benzene [10.1039/c2gc16544a].

The correlation between d-band center position and binding energy serves as a useful descriptor for predicting hydrogenation activity [10.1021/cr300096b_8]. Surfaces with d-band centers shifted further from the Fermi level generally exhibit weaker adsorbate binding and higher hydrogenation activity [10.1016/j.cattod.2010.05.014]. This trend has been observed for various molecules, including linear alkenes (1-butene, 1-pentene, 1-hexene), cyclic hydrocarbons (cyclohexene, 1,3-cyclohexadiene, 1,4-cyclohexadiene), and aromatics (benzene) [10.1016/j.susc.2008.05.035].

However, it's important to note that the correlation between binding energy and hydrogenation activity is not always straightforward, particularly for partial hydrogenation reactions [10.1002/cctc.201000082]. For example, PtCu catalysts showed unexpected behavior in the partial hydrogenation of 1,4-cyclohexadiene to cyclohexene, highlighting the need for more detailed calculations involving reaction networks and activation barriers in complex reaction systems.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The integration of DFT calculations with experimental techniques like TPD measurements has proven to be a powerful approach for understanding and predicting the performance of Pt-3d catalysts in C═C bond hydrogenation reactions. This methodology allows for more efficient catalyst design by enabling computational screening of potential catalyst compositions before experimental synthesis and testing.

Looking forward, several promising research directions emerge:

1. Expanding the scope of molecules studied: While current research has focused on specific unsaturated hydrocarbons, future studies could extend this approach to a broader range of industrially relevant molecules, including more complex alkenes, dienes, and functionalized aromatics.

2. Machine learning integration: The growing database of DFT calculations and experimental results presents an opportunity to employ machine learning algorithms for identifying subtle trends and relationships that may not be immediately apparent through traditional analysis methods.

3. In-situ and operando studies: Combining the theoretical predictions with advanced characterization techniques under reaction conditions could provide deeper insights into the dynamic behavior of catalysts during hydrogenation reactions.

4. Multiscale modeling: Integrating DFT calculations with kinetic Monte Carlo simulations or microkinetic modeling could bridge the gap between atomic-scale interactions and macroscopic reaction rates, leading to more accurate predictions of catalyst performance.

5. Catalyst stability and deactivation: Future research could focus on using this integrated approach to predict and mitigate catalyst deactivation mechanisms, thereby improving the long-term stability and efficiency of Pt-3d catalysts.

By pursuing these directions, researchers can further refine the design principles for Pt-3d catalysts, potentially leading to the development of highly efficient and selective catalysts for a wide range of hydrogenation processes in the chemical industry.

<Chinese>
对Pt-3d催化剂在C═C键氢化反应中的综合分析揭示了表面d带中心的DFT计算和吸附物结合能与实验观察（如TPD测量）之间存在强烈的相关性。这种集成的理论和实验方法为催化剂设计和性能预测提供了宝贵的见解。

DFT计算一致表明，用亚表面3d过渡金属修饰Pt(111)表面会改变表面Pt d带中心的平均能量[10.1016/j.apcata.2008.01.024]。这种修饰导致双金属表面上氢和不饱和烃的结合能力比单金属Pt表面更弱[10.1039/c2gc16544a]。较弱的结合与实验观察到的各种C═C键含分子（包括直链烯烃、环烃和芳香族化合物）的较高氢化活性相关[10.1016/j.cattod.2010.05.014]。

例如，PtNiPt(111)和PtCoPt(111)结构对环己烯、1,3-丁二烯和苯等分子的结合能力始终低于Pt(111) [10.1016/j.susc.2008.05.035]。这些理论预测与PtNi和PtCo催化剂在涉及1,3-丁二烯、1,4-环己二烯和苯的反应中表现出增强的氢化活性的实验观察结果很好地吻合[10.1039/c2gc16544a]。

d带中心位置和结合能之间的相关性成为预测氢化活性的有用描述符[10.1021/cr300096b_8]。d带中心远离费米能级的表面通常表现出较弱的吸附物结合和较高的氢化活性[10.1016/j.cattod.2010.05.014]。这种趋势已在各种分子中观察到，包括直链烯烃（1-丁烯、1-戊烯、1-己烯）、环烃（环己烯、1,3-环己二烯、1,4-环己二烯）和芳香族化合物（苯）[10.1016/j.susc.2008.05.035]。

然而，需要注意的是，结合能与氢化活性之间的相关性并不总是直接的，特别是对于部分氢化反应[10.1002/cctc.201000082]。例如，PtCu催化剂在1,4-环己二烯部分氢化为环己烯的反应中表现出意外行为，这突显了在复杂反应体系中需要进行更详细的反应网络和活化能障计算的必要性。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：DFT计算与实验技术（如TPD测量）的结合已被证明是理解和预测Pt-3d催化剂在C═C键氢化反应中性能的强大方法。这种方法通过在实验合成和测试之前进行计算筛选潜在的催化剂组成，从而实现更高效的催化剂设计。

展望未来，几个有前景的研究方向浮现出来：

1. 扩大研究分子的范围：虽然目前的研究集中在特定的不饱和烃上，但未来的研究可以将这种方法扩展到更广泛的工业相关分子，包括更复杂的烯烃、二烯烃和官能化芳香族化合物。

2. 机器学习集成：不断增长的DFT计算和实验结果数据库为使用机器学习算法识别传统分析方法可能无法立即发现的微妙趋势和关系提供了机会。

3. 原位和操作条件下的研究：将理论预测与反应条件下的先进表征技术相结合，可以深入了解催化剂在氢化反应过程中的动态行为。

4. 多尺度模型：将DFT计算与动力学蒙特卡罗模拟或微观动力学建模相结合，可以弥合原子尺度相互作用和宏观反应速率之间的差距，从而更准确地预测催化剂性能。

5. 催化剂稳定性和失活：未来的研究可以专注于使用这种集成方法来预测和减轻催化剂失活机制，从而提高Pt-3d催化剂的长期稳定性和效率。

通过追求这些方向，研究人员可以进一步完善Pt-3d催化剂的设计原则，有可能开发出高效和选择性的催化剂，用于化学工业中广泛的氢化过程。
