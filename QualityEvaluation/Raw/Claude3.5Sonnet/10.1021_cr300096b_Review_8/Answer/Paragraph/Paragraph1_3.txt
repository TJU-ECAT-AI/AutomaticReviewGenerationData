Comprehensive analysis of Pt-3d catalysts for C═C bond hydrogenation: Integration of DFT calculations, experimental validation, and structure-activity relationships for catalyst design and performance prediction

DFT calculations of surface d-band center and adsorbate binding energies for various C═C bond-containing molecules on Pt-3d catalysts show strong correlations with experimental observations from techniques such as TPD measurements and reactor studies. This integrated theoretical and experimental approach has provided valuable insights into the structure-activity relationships of Pt-3d catalysts for hydrogenation reactions.

Key findings from the literature include:

1. Modification of Pt(111) surfaces with subsurface 3d transition metals significantly alters the average energy of the surface Pt d-band (d-band center) [10.1016/j.apcata.2008.01.024].

2. Pt-terminated bimetallic surfaces, such as PtNiPt(111) and PtCoPt(111), exhibit weaker binding of hydrogen and hydrocarbon adsorbates compared to monometallic Pt surfaces, leading to higher hydrogenation rates [10.1039/c2gc16544a].

3. The binding energies of various C═C bond-containing molecules (e.g., linear alkenes, cyclic hydrocarbons, and aromatics) decrease as the surface d-band center moves away from the Fermi level [10.1016/j.susc.2008.05.035].

4. Bimetallic catalysts like PtNi and PtCo show enhanced hydrogenation activity for molecules such as 1,3-butadiene, 1,4-cyclohexadiene, and benzene compared to monometallic Pt catalysts [10.1039/c2gc16544a].

5. The correlation between binding energy and hydrogenation activity is generally valid for complete hydrogenation reactions but may not always hold for partial hydrogenation reactions [10.1002/cctc.201000082].

This integrated approach can be utilized to predict hydrogenation activity and inform the design of more efficient Pt-3d catalytic systems through the following strategies:

1. Using DFT calculations to screen potential Pt-3d catalyst compositions and structures for optimal binding energies and d-band center positions [10.1021/cr300096b_8].

2. Correlating calculated binding energies with experimental hydrogenation rates obtained from techniques like TPD measurements [10.1016/j.cattod.2010.05.014].

3. Identifying optimal binding energy ranges that correspond to enhanced hydrogenation activity [10.1039/c2gc16544a].

4. Tailoring surface electronic structure by modifying composition to achieve desired binding strengths [10.1016/j.susc.2008.05.035].

5. Developing structure-property relationships to guide rational design of Pt-3d catalytic systems for specific hydrogenation reactions [10.1016/j.susc.2008.05.035].

6. Combining DFT calculations with experimental techniques like TPD to validate theoretical predictions of binding energies [10.1002/cctc.201000082].

7. Extending the approach to study a wider range of C═C bond-containing molecules on various Pt-3d catalyst surfaces to develop a more comprehensive understanding of structure-activity relationships [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.01.019].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The integration of DFT calculations and experimental techniques has significantly advanced our understanding of Pt-3d catalysts for C═C bond hydrogenation. This approach has revealed the importance of surface electronic structure, particularly the d-band center, in determining catalytic activity. Looking forward, several promising research directions emerge:

1. Machine learning approaches: By analyzing the growing body of theoretical and experimental data, machine learning algorithms could potentially uncover new trends and design principles for Pt-3d catalysts, accelerating the discovery of optimal catalyst compositions.

2. In-situ characterization: Developing advanced in-situ spectroscopic techniques could provide real-time information on surface species and reaction intermediates during hydrogenation reactions, offering deeper insights into reaction mechanisms and catalyst behavior under operating conditions.

3. Multiscale modeling: Combining DFT calculations with kinetic Monte Carlo simulations and microkinetic modeling could bridge the gap between atomic-scale phenomena and macroscopic catalyst performance, enabling more accurate predictions of catalyst behavior in real-world applications.

4. Catalyst stability and deactivation: Future research should focus on understanding and predicting the long-term stability of Pt-3d catalysts, incorporating factors such as surface reconstruction, sintering, and poisoning into the integrated theoretical-experimental approach.

5. Extension to other reaction classes: The successful application of this integrated approach to hydrogenation reactions suggests it could be adapted to study other important catalytic processes, such as oxidation reactions or C-C bond formation, potentially leading to breakthroughs in catalyst design for a wide range of industrial applications.

By pursuing these research directions, the field of Pt-3d catalysis for C═C bond hydrogenation is poised to make significant advances in catalyst design, optimization, and performance prediction, ultimately leading to more efficient and sustainable chemical processes.

Pt-3d催化剂C═C键氢化的综合分析：整合DFT计算、实验验证和结构-活性关系，用于催化剂设计和性能预测

DFT计算的Pt-3d催化剂表面d带中心和各种C═C键含分子的吸附结合能与TPD测量和反应器研究等实验观察结果显示出强烈的相关性。这种理论和实验方法的整合为Pt-3d催化剂在氢化反应中的结构-活性关系提供了宝贵的见解。

文献中的主要发现包括：

1. 用亚表面3d过渡金属修饰Pt(111)表面显著改变了表面Pt d带的平均能量（d带中心）[10.1016/j.apcata.2008.01.024]。

2. Pt终端的双金属表面，如PtNiPt(111)和PtCoPt(111)，与单金属Pt表面相比，对氢和碳氢化合物吸附物的结合更弱，从而导致更高的氢化速率[10.1039/c2gc16544a]。

3. 各种C═C键含分子（如直链烯烃、环烃和芳香烃）的结合能随着表面d带中心远离费米能级而降低[10.1016/j.susc.2008.05.035]。

4. 双金属催化剂如PtNi和PtCo对1,3-丁二烯、1,4-环己二烯和苯等分子的氢化活性比单金属Pt催化剂更高[10.1039/c2gc16544a]。

5. 结合能与氢化活性之间的相关性通常适用于完全氢化反应，但对部分氢化反应可能并不总是成立[10.1002/cctc.201000082]。

这种综合方法可以通过以下策略来预测氢化活性并指导更高效的Pt-3d催化系统的设计：

1. 使用DFT计算筛选潜在的Pt-3d催化剂组成和结构，以获得最佳结合能和d带中心位置[10.1021/cr300096b_8]。

2. 将计算得出的结合能与TPD测量等技术获得的实验氢化速率相关联[10.1016/j.cattod.2010.05.014]。

3. 确定对应于增强氢化活性的最佳结合能范围[10.1039/c2gc16544a]。

4. 通过调整组成来定制表面电子结构，以达到所需的结合强度[10.1016/j.susc.2008.05.035]。

5. 开发结构-性能关系，指导特定氢化反应的Pt-3d催化系统的理性设计[10.1016/j.susc.2008.05.035]。

6. 将DFT计算与TPD等实验技术相结合，验证结合能的理论预测[10.1002/cctc.201000082]。

7. 将该方法扩展到研究更广泛的C═C键含分子在各种Pt-3d催化剂表面上的行为，以发展更全面的结构-活性关系理解[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.01.019]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：DFT计算和实验技术的整合显著提高了我们对Pt-3d催化剂C═C键氢化的理解。这种方法揭示了表面电子结构，特别是d带中心，在决定催化活性中的重要性。展望未来，几个有前景的研究方向浮现出来：

1. 机器学习方法：通过分析不断增长的理论和实验数据，机器学习算法可能发现Pt-3d催化剂的新趋势和设计原则，加速最优催化剂组成的发现。

2. 原位表征：开发先进的原位光谱技术可以提供氢化反应过程中表面物种和反应中间体的实时信息，为反应机理和催化剂在工作条件下的行为提供更深入的见解。

3. 多尺度建模：将DFT计算与动力学蒙特卡罗模拟和微观动力学建模相结合，可以弥合原子尺度现象和宏观催化剂性能之间的差距，从而更准确地预测催化剂在实际应用中的行为。

4. 催化剂稳定性和失活：未来的研究应关注Pt-3d催化剂的长期稳定性理解和预测，将表面重构、烧结和中毒等因素纳入综合理论-实验方法中。

5. 扩展到其他反应类别：这种综合方法在氢化反应中的成功应用表明，它可以被改编用于研究其他重要的催化过程，如氧化反应或C-C键形成，可能会导致广泛工业应用的催化剂设计突破。

通过追求这些研究方向，Pt-3d催化C═C键氢化的领域有望在催化剂设计、优化和性能预测方面取得重大进展，最终导致更高效和可持续的化学过程。
