Comprehensive analysis of Pt-3d catalysts for C═C bond hydrogenation reveals a strong correlation between DFT calculations of surface d-band center and adsorbate binding energies with experimental observations from techniques such as TPD measurements. This integrated theoretical and experimental approach provides valuable insights for catalyst design and performance prediction.

DFT calculations consistently show that modifying Pt(111) surfaces with subsurface 3d transition metals significantly alters the Pt d-band center, shifting it away from the Fermi level [10.1016/j.apcata.2008.01.024]. This electronic modification leads to weaker binding of hydrogen and olefins on bimetallic surfaces compared to monometallic Pt surfaces [10.1016/j.apcatb.2009.06.036]. The weaker binding correlates with enhanced hydrogenation activity observed experimentally for various C═C bond-containing molecules, including linear alkenes, cyclic hydrocarbons, and aromatics [10.1016/j.cattod.2010.05.014].

For instance, DFT calculations predict weaker binding of 1-butene, 1-hexene, cyclohexene, 1,3-butadiene, 1,3-cyclohexadiene, 1,4-cyclohexadiene, and benzene on subsurface Pt3dPt(111) structures compared to Pt(111) and 3dPtPt(111) surfaces [10.1021/cr300096b_8]. These predictions have been experimentally verified using TPD measurements and reactor studies, demonstrating facile low-temperature hydrogenation on PtNiPt(111) surfaces [10.1016/j.susc.2005.07.009].

The correlation between d-band center position and binding energies allows for the prediction of hydrogenation activity trends among different bimetallic systems. For example, PtCoPt(111) and PtNiPt(111) structures are predicted to be good hydrogenation catalysts due to their weak binding of both hydrogen and unsaturated hydrocarbons [10.1016/j.cattod.2010.05.014]. These predictions have been confirmed in experimental studies, showing that PtNi and PtCo bimetallic catalysts outperform monometallic Pt in hydrogenating various unsaturated hydrocarbons [10.1039/c2gc16544a].

However, it's important to note that the correlation between binding energy and hydrogenation activity is not always straightforward, particularly for partial hydrogenation reactions. For instance, PtCu catalysts showed unexpected behavior in the partial hydrogenation of 1,4-cyclohexadiene to cyclohexene, highlighting the need for more detailed calculations involving reaction networks and activation barriers in complex reaction systems [10.1002/cctc.201000082].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The integration of DFT calculations with experimental techniques has significantly advanced our understanding of structure-activity relationships in Pt-3d catalysts for C═C bond hydrogenation. This approach enables more efficient and targeted catalyst development by providing fundamental insights into the electronic factors governing reactivity.

Looking forward, several opportunities for further advancement in this field emerge:

1. Expanding the scope: While current studies have focused on specific molecules, future research could extend this approach to a broader range of C═C bond-containing compounds, including more complex and industrially relevant substrates.

2. Machine learning integration: The growing body of theoretical and experimental data presents an opportunity to apply machine learning techniques. This could uncover new trends and design principles for Pt-3d catalysts that may not be immediately apparent through traditional analysis.

3. In-situ and operando studies: Incorporating advanced characterization techniques that can probe catalyst structure and electronic properties under reaction conditions could provide deeper insights into the dynamic behavior of these catalysts during hydrogenation reactions.

4. Multiscale modeling: Developing multiscale models that bridge the gap between atomic-scale DFT calculations and reactor-scale performance could enhance the predictive power of this integrated approach and its applicability to industrial catalyst design.

5. Selectivity optimization: Future research could focus on leveraging the understanding of electronic structure-activity relationships to design catalysts with improved selectivity, particularly for partial hydrogenation reactions or in the presence of multiple functional groups.

By pursuing these directions, researchers can further refine the integrated theoretical and experimental approach, potentially leading to the discovery of novel, highly efficient Pt-3d catalysts for a wide range of hydrogenation applications in the chemical and pharmaceutical industries.

<Chinese>
对Pt-3d催化剂C═C键氢化的全面分析揭示了表面d带中心的DFT计算和吸附质结合能与实验观察（如TPD测量）之间存在强相关性。这种集成的理论和实验方法为催化剂设计和性能预测提供了宝贵的见解。

DFT计算一致表明，用亚表面3d过渡金属修饰Pt(111)表面会显著改变Pt的d带中心，使其远离费米能级[10.1016/j.apcata.2008.01.024]。这种电子修饰导致双金属表面对氢和烯烃的结合比单金属Pt表面更弱[10.1016/j.apcatb.2009.06.036]。这种较弱的结合与实验观察到的各种含C═C键分子（包括直链烯烃、环烷烃和芳香族）的增强氢化活性相关[10.1016/j.cattod.2010.05.014]。

例如，DFT计算预测，1-丁烯、1-己烯、环己烯、1,3-丁二烯、1,3-环己二烯、1,4-环己二烯和苯在亚表面Pt3dPt(111)结构上的结合比在Pt(111)和3dPtPt(111)表面上更弱[10.1021/cr300096b_8]。这些预测已通过TPD测量和反应器研究得到实验验证，证明了PtNiPt(111)表面上的低温氢化反应[10.1016/j.susc.2005.07.009]。

d带中心位置与结合能之间的相关性允许预测不同双金属系统之间的氢化活性趋势。例如，由于PtCoPt(111)和PtNiPt(111)结构对氢和不饱和碳氢化合物的结合较弱，预测它们是良好的氢化催化剂[10.1016/j.cattod.2010.05.014]。这些预测在实验研究中得到了证实，表明PtNi和PtCo双金属催化剂在各种不饱和碳氢化合物的氢化方面优于单金属Pt[10.1039/c2gc16544a]。

然而，需要注意的是，结合能与氢化活性之间的相关性并不总是直接的，特别是对于部分氢化反应。例如，PtCu催化剂在1,4-环己二烯部分氢化为环己烯的反应中表现出意外行为，这突显了在复杂反应系统中需要更详细的计算，包括反应网络和活化能障[10.1002/cctc.201000082]。

综合理解与未来展望：DFT计算与实验技术的集成显著提高了我们对Pt-3d催化剂在C═C键氢化中的结构-活性关系的理解。这种方法通过提供控制反应性的电子因素的基本见解，能够更有效和有针对性地开发催化剂。

展望未来，该领域的进一步发展机会包括：

1. 扩大研究范围：虽然目前的研究集中在特定分子上，但未来的研究可以将这种方法扩展到更广泛的含C═C键化合物，包括更复杂和工业相关的底物。

2. 机器学习集成：不断增长的理论和实验数据为应用机器学习技术提供了机会。这可能会发现通过传统分析不易察觉的Pt-3d催化剂的新趋势和设计原则。

3. 原位和操作条件下的研究：纳入可以在反应条件下探测催化剂结构和电子性质的先进表征技术，可以提供对这些催化剂在氢化反应过程中动态行为的更深入见解。

4. 多尺度建模：开发连接原子尺度DFT计算和反应器尺度性能之间差距的多尺度模型，可以增强这种集成方法的预测能力及其在工业催化剂设计中的适用性。

5. 选择性优化：未来的研究可以集中于利用对电子结构-活性关系的理解来设计具有改进选择性的催化剂，特别是对于部分氢化反应或存在多个官能团的情况。

通过追求这些方向，研究人员可以进一步完善集成的理论和实验方法，可能导致发现新型、高效的Pt-3d催化剂，用于化学和制药工业中广泛的氢化应用。
