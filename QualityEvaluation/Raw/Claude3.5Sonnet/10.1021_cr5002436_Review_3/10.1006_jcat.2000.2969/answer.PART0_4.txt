The paper focuses on studying an alumina-supported vanadium oxide catalyst for butane dehydrogenation, but does not compare it to Pt or CrOx catalysts or discuss historical trends.

The paper does touch on how some variables influence vanadium species behavior:

- Support: Alumina support was used, which stabilizes V4+ species during reduction.
- Metal loading: 5.2 wt% vanadium was used, chosen to be near optimal activity based on previous work.
- Surface area: The catalyst had a surface area of 165 m2/g. 

The paper explores how reduction treatments affect the oxidation state and activity of the vanadium species, finding that reduced V4+ and V3+ states are active for dehydrogenation. Variables like reduction method and water treatment were found to influence catalyst activity.
