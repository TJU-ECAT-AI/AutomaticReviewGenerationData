The reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) consists of three main steps:

1. Dissociative adsorption: Propane adsorbs onto the catalyst surface and undergoes C-H bond cleavage, forming adsorbed propyl and hydrogen species.

2. β-H extraction: A hydrogen atom is abstracted from the β-carbon of the adsorbed propyl species, resulting in the formation of an adsorbed propylene intermediate.

3. Desorption: The adsorbed propylene desorbs from the catalyst surface, releasing the product propylene gas.

There is an ongoing debate in the scientific community regarding which step is rate-controlling in the PDH process. Some researchers argue that the dissociative adsorption of propane is the rate-limiting step, while others propose that the β-H extraction is the slowest and therefore rate-determining step.

This uncertainty impacts our understanding of the PDH process in several ways:

1. Catalyst design: If dissociative adsorption is rate-limiting, catalysts should be optimized for C-H bond activation. If β-H extraction is rate-limiting, the focus should be on facilitating hydrogen abstraction.

2. Reaction conditions: The optimal temperature, pressure, and reactant concentrations may differ depending on which step is rate-controlling.

3. Kinetic modeling: Accurate kinetic models require correct identification of the rate-determining step.

4. Mechanistic studies: The focus of mechanistic investigations may shift depending on which step is believed to be rate-limiting.

5. Catalyst deactivation: Understanding the rate-determining step can provide insights into potential deactivation mechanisms and guide the development of more stable catalysts.

This ongoing debate highlights the complexity of the PDH process and emphasizes the need for further research to elucidate the precise reaction mechanism and kinetics. Resolving this uncertainty would significantly enhance our ability to design more efficient and stable PDH catalysts.

丙烷脱氢(PDH)的反向Horiuti-Polanyi机制包括三个主要步骤：

1. 解离吸附：丙烷在催化剂表面吸附并发生C-H键断裂，形成吸附的丙基和氢物种。

2. β-H提取：从吸附丙基物种的β碳上提取一个氢原子，形成吸附的丙烯中间体。

3. 脱附：吸附的丙烯从催化剂表面脱附，释放出丙烯气体产物。

科学界对PDH过程中哪一步是速率控制步骤存在争议。一些研究人员认为丙烷的解离吸附是速率限制步骤，而另一些人则提出β-H提取是最慢的，因此是速率决定步骤。

这种不确定性对我们理解PDH过程有以下几个方面的影响：

1. 催化剂设计：如果解离吸附是速率限制步骤，催化剂应该针对C-H键活化进行优化。如果β-H提取是速率限制步骤，重点应该放在促进氢提取上。

2. 反应条件：最佳温度、压力和反应物浓度可能会因速率控制步骤的不同而有所不同。

3. 动力学建模：准确的动力学模型需要正确识别速率决定步骤。

4. 机理研究：机理调查的重点可能会根据认为哪一步是速率限制步骤而发生转移。

5. 催化剂失活：了解速率决定步骤可以为潜在的失活机制提供洞见，并指导开发更稳定的催化剂。

这一持续的争论凸显了PDH过程的复杂性，并强调了需要进一步研究以阐明精确的反应机理和动力学。解决这一不确定性将显著增强我们设计更高效、更稳定的PDH催化剂的能力。
