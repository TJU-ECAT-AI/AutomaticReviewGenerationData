The reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) consists of three main steps:

1. Dissociative adsorption: Propane adsorbs onto the catalyst surface and dissociates, forming adsorbed propyl and hydrogen species.

2. β-H extraction: A hydrogen atom is extracted from the β-carbon of the adsorbed propyl species, forming an adsorbed propylene molecule and a second adsorbed hydrogen atom.

3. Desorption: The adsorbed propylene and hydrogen molecules desorb from the catalyst surface.

There is ongoing debate in the scientific community regarding which step is rate-controlling. Some researchers argue that the dissociative adsorption is rate-limiting, while others believe β-H extraction is the slowest step. This uncertainty impacts our understanding of PDH in several ways:

1. Catalyst design: If dissociative adsorption is rate-limiting, catalysts should be designed to facilitate propane adsorption. If β-H extraction is rate-limiting, the focus should be on optimizing the catalyst's ability to extract hydrogen.
