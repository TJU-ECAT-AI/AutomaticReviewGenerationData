The reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) consists of three main steps:

1. Dissociative adsorption: Propane is physically adsorbed on the catalyst surface and then dissociates into adsorbed propyl and hydrogen species.

2. β-H extraction: A β-hydrogen is extracted from the adsorbed propyl species, forming an adsorbed propene molecule.

3. Desorption: The adsorbed propene molecule desorbs from the catalyst surface.

There is an ongoing debate in the scientific community regarding which step is the rate-controlling step in the PDH reaction. Some studies, such as those by Jackson et al. and Biloen et al., identified the β-hydrogen elimination as the rate-determining step. However, Suzuki and Kaneko proposed that the dehydrogenation of adsorbed propane (which could be interpreted as part of the dissociative adsorption step) was rate-determining.

Recent density functional theory studies suggest that propane is only physically adsorbed on Pt surfaces, which could imply that dissociative adsorption might be rate-limiting.

2. β-H extraction: A hydrogen atom is removed from the β-carbon of the adsorbed propyl species, forming an adsorbed propylene intermediate.

3. Desorption: The adsorbed propylene molecule desorbs from the catalyst surface, releasing the product.
