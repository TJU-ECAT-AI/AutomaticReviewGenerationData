<English>
The reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) consists of three main steps:

1. Dissociative adsorption: Propane adsorbs onto the catalyst surface and dissociates into adsorbed propyl and hydrogen species.

2. β-H extraction: A hydrogen atom is extracted from the β-carbon of the adsorbed propyl species, forming an adsorbed propene molecule.

3. Desorption: The adsorbed propene and hydrogen desorb from the catalyst surface.

There is ongoing debate in the scientific community regarding which step is the rate-controlling step in the PDH reaction. Some studies suggest that the dissociative adsorption process is rate-limiting, while others indicate that the β-H extraction step could be the slowest and therefore rate-determining [10.1016/j.apcata.2011.01.039]. This uncertainty depends on various factors such as catalyst surface, cluster size, and reaction conditions [10.1039/D0CS01262A].

For example, on Pt(211) and Pt(100) surfaces with smaller Pt cluster sizes, the first C-H bond activation (dissociative adsorption) is proposed as the rate-determining step (RDS). However, on Pt(111) surfaces, some studies suggest the second C-H bond activation (β-H extraction) is the RDS, while others identify the first C-H activation as rate-limiting [10.1039/D0CS01262A].

This uncertainty impacts our understanding of the PDH process in several ways:

1. Catalyst design: Different rate-limiting steps would require different catalyst optimization strategies. If dissociative adsorption is rate-limiting, catalyst development should focus on improving the initial C-H bond activation and propane adsorption. If β-H extraction is rate-limiting, efforts should concentrate on facilitating hydrogen removal from the adsorbed alkyl species [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016].

2. Reaction conditions: The optimal temperature, pressure, and reactant concentrations may vary depending on which step is rate-limiting [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016].

3. Kinetic modeling: Accurate kinetic models are crucial for process design and optimization, and they depend on correctly identifying the rate-determining step [10.1016/j.apcata.2011.01.039].

4. Mechanistic understanding: The debate highlights gaps in our fundamental understanding of the reaction mechanism on different catalyst surfaces [10.1016/j.apcata.2011.01.039].

5. Catalyst comparison: Evaluating the performance of different catalysts becomes more challenging when the RDS may vary across catalyst types and surface structures [10.1039/D0CS01262A].

6. Catalyst deactivation: Understanding the rate-limiting step could provide insights into the primary causes of catalyst deactivation, affecting strategies for catalyst regeneration and longevity [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016].

This ongoing debate emphasizes the complexity of the PDH process and highlights the need for further research combining experimental and computational approaches to gain a more comprehensive understanding of the reaction mechanism and resolve the uncertainty surrounding the rate-determining step [10.1039/D0CS01262A].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The ongoing debate surrounding the rate-controlling step in the reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for propane dehydrogenation underscores the complexity of heterogeneous catalysis and the need for advanced research methodologies. Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. In-situ spectroscopic techniques: Developing and applying advanced in-situ characterization methods to observe the reaction intermediates and transition states under realistic reaction conditions.

2. Computational modeling: Enhancing the accuracy and scope of density functional theory (DFT) calculations to better predict the energetics of each step across various catalyst surfaces and compositions.

3. Machine learning approaches: Utilizing machine learning algorithms to analyze large datasets of experimental and computational results to identify patterns and predict optimal catalyst designs.

4. Operando studies: Conducting operando experiments that combine spectroscopic techniques with kinetic measurements to correlate surface species with reaction rates in real-time.

5. Single-atom catalysts: Exploring the potential of single-atom catalysts to provide more uniform active sites and potentially simplify the mechanistic understanding of PDH.

6. Microkinetic modeling: Developing more sophisticated microkinetic models that incorporate the effects of catalyst structure, promoters, and reaction conditions on the rate-determining step.

7. Isotope labeling studies: Conducting more detailed isotope labeling experiments to elucidate the reaction pathway and identify rate-limiting steps under various conditions.

By pursuing these research directions, we may not only resolve the debate regarding the rate-controlling step in PDH but also gain deeper insights into the fundamental principles governing catalytic processes in general. This knowledge could lead to the development of more efficient and selective catalysts for PDH and other industrially important reactions, ultimately contributing to more sustainable chemical production processes.

<Chinese>
反向Horiuti-Polanyi机理在丙烷脱氢（PDH）过程中包括三个主要步骤：

1. 解离吸附：丙烷在催化剂表面吸附并解离成吸附的丙基和氢物种。

2. β-H提取：从吸附的丙基物种的β-碳上提取一个氢原子，形成吸附的丙烯分子。

3. 脱附：吸附的丙烯和氢从催化剂表面脱附。

科学界对PDH反应中哪个步骤是速率控制步骤存在争议。一些研究表明，解离吸附过程是速率限制步骤，而其他研究则表明β-H提取步骤可能是最慢的，因此是速率决定步骤[10.1016/j.apcata.2011.01.039]。这种不确定性取决于催化剂表面、簇大小和反应条件等各种因素[10.1039/D0CS01262A]。

例如，在较小Pt簇大小的Pt(211)和Pt(100)表面上，第一个C-H键活化（解离吸附）被提出为速率决定步骤（RDS）。然而，在Pt(111)表面上，一些研究表明第二个C-H键活化（β-H提取）是RDS，而其他研究则确定第一个C-H活化为速率限制步骤[10.1039/D0CS01262A]。

这种不确定性对我们理解PDH过程有几个方面的影响：

1. 催化剂设计：不同的速率限制步骤需要不同的催化剂优化策略。如果解离吸附是速率限制步骤，催化剂开发应集中于改善初始C-H键活化和丙烷吸附。如果β-H提取是速率限制步骤，努力应集中于促进吸附烷基物种的氢去除[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。

2. 反应条件：最佳温度、压力和反应物浓度可能因速率限制步骤的不同而变化[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。

3. 动力学建模：准确的动力学模型对过程设计和优化至关重要，它们依赖于正确识别速率决定步骤[10.1016/j.apcata.2011.01.039]。

4. 机理理解：这场辩论凸显了我们对不同催化剂表面上反应机理的基本理解存在差距[10.1016/j.apcata.2011.01.039]。

5. 催化剂比较：当RDS可能在不同催化剂类型和表面结构之间变化时，评估不同催化剂的性能变得更具挑战性[10.1039/D0CS01262A]。

6. 催化剂失活：了解速率限制步骤可能为催化剂失活的主要原因提供洞见，影响催化剂再生和寿命策略[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。

这场持续的辩论强调了PDH过程的复杂性，并突出了需要进一步结合实验和计算方法的研究，以获得对反应机理更全面的理解，并解决围绕速率决定步骤的不确定性[10.1039/D0CS01262A]。

综合理解与未来展望：围绕丙烷脱氢反向Horiuti-Polanyi机理中速率控制步骤的持续争论凸显了多相催化的复杂性，以及对先进研究方法的需求。未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 原位光谱技术：开发和应用先进的原位表征方法，在实际反应条件下观察反应中间体和过渡态。

2. 计算建模：提高密度泛函理论（DFT）计算的准确性和范围，以更好地预测各种催化剂表面和组成中每个步骤的能量学。

3. 机器学习方法：利用机器学习算法分析大量实验和计算结果数据集，识别模式并预测最优催化剂设计。

4. 操作中研究：进行结合光谱技术和动力学测量的操作中实验，实时关联表面物种与反应速率。

5. 单原子催化剂：探索单原子催化剂的潜力，提供更均匀的活性位点，可能简化PDH的机理理解。

6. 微观动力学建模：开发更复杂的微观动力学模型，纳入催化剂结构、助剂和反应条件对速率决定步骤的影响。

7. 同位素标记研究：进行更详细的同位素标记实验，阐明在各种条件下的反应路径并识别速率限制步骤。

通过追求这些研究方向，我们不仅可能解决关于PDH速率控制步骤的争论，还可能对控制催化过程的基本原理获得更深入的洞察。这些知识可能导致开发更高效和选择性的PDH催化剂以及其他工业重要反应的催化剂，最终为更可持续的化学生产过程做出贡献。
