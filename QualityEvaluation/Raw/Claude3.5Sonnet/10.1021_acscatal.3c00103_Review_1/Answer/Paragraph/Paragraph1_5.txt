<English>
The reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) consists of three main steps:

1. Dissociative adsorption: Propane adsorbs onto the catalyst surface and dissociates into adsorbed propyl and hydrogen species.

2. β-H extraction: A hydrogen atom is extracted from the β-carbon of the adsorbed propyl species, forming an adsorbed propylene intermediate.

3. Desorption: The adsorbed propylene and hydrogen desorb from the catalyst surface as products.

There is an ongoing debate in the scientific community regarding which step is the rate-controlling step in the PDH reaction. Some studies suggest that the dissociative adsorption of propane is the rate-limiting step [10.1016/j.apcata.2011.01.039], while others argue that β-H extraction is the slowest and thus rate-determining step [10.1016/0021-9517(77)90010-0]. This uncertainty depends on various factors such as catalyst surface, cluster size, and reaction conditions [10.1039/D0CS01262A].

The debate impacts our understanding of the PDH process in several ways:

1. Catalyst design: Different rate-limiting steps would require different catalyst optimization strategies. If dissociative adsorption is rate-limiting, efforts should focus on improving C-H bond activation, while if β-H extraction is rate-limiting, the focus should be on facilitating hydrogen removal [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016].

2. Reaction conditions: The optimal temperature, pressure, and reactant concentrations may vary depending on which step is rate-limiting [10.1039/D0CS01260B].

3. Kinetic modeling: Accurate kinetic models are crucial for process design and optimization, and they depend on correctly identifying the rate-determining step [10.1039/D2QI00653G].

4. Mechanistic understanding: The debate highlights gaps in our fundamental understanding of the reaction mechanism on different catalyst surfaces [10.1016/j.apcata.2011.01.039].

5. Catalyst characterization: Different rate-limiting steps may require different characterization techniques to probe the relevant surface species and intermediates [10.1039/D0CS01260B].

6. Process optimization: Industrial PDH processes could be more effectively optimized if the rate-determining step was definitively known [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016].

The literature suggests that the rate-determining step may be dependent on the catalyst structure, including particle size and surface orientation [10.1016/j.apcata.2011.01.039]. For example, on Pt(211) and Pt(100) surfaces with smaller Pt cluster sizes, the first C-H bond activation is proposed as the rate-determining step, while on Pt(111) surfaces, some studies suggest the second C-H bond activation is rate-limiting [10.1039/D0CS01262A].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The ongoing debate surrounding the rate-controlling step in propane dehydrogenation highlights the complexity of heterogeneous catalysis and emphasizes the need for advanced characterization techniques and theoretical studies. Future research directions may include:

1. In-situ spectroscopic studies: Developing and applying advanced in-situ spectroscopic techniques to observe reaction intermediates and surface species under realistic reaction conditions.

2. Computational modeling: Employing more sophisticated computational methods to model the entire reaction pathway, including transition states and intermediates, across various catalyst surfaces and compositions.

3. Microkinetic modeling: Developing comprehensive microkinetic models that incorporate the effects of catalyst structure, reaction conditions, and surface coverage to predict the rate-determining step under different scenarios.

4. Single-atom catalysts: Investigating the behavior of single-atom catalysts in PDH to potentially simplify the reaction mechanism and provide clearer insights into the rate-determining step.

5. Operando studies: Combining spectroscopic techniques with catalytic testing to correlate structural changes and catalytic performance in real-time.

6. Machine learning approaches: Utilizing machine learning algorithms to analyze large datasets of experimental and computational results to identify patterns and predict rate-determining steps for different catalyst systems.

7. Isotope labeling studies: Conducting more detailed isotope labeling experiments to trace the reaction pathway and elucidate the rate-determining step under various conditions.

By pursuing these research directions, we can work towards resolving the debate surrounding the rate-controlling step in PDH and develop a more comprehensive understanding of the reaction mechanism. This knowledge will be crucial for designing more efficient and selective catalysts, optimizing industrial processes, and ultimately improving the sustainability and economic viability of propylene production through PDH.

<Chinese>
反向Horiuti-Polanyi机理描述了丙烷脱氢（PDH）过程，包括三个主要步骤：

1. 解离吸附：丙烷在催化剂表面吸附并解离成吸附的丙基和氢物种。

2. β-H提取：从吸附的丙基物种的β-碳上提取一个氢原子，形成吸附的丙烯中间体。

3. 脱附：吸附的丙烯和氢从催化剂表面脱附成为产物。

科学界对PDH反应中哪一步是速率控制步骤存在争议。一些研究表明丙烷的解离吸附是速率限制步骤[10.1016/j.apcata.2011.01.039]，而其他研究则认为β-H提取是最慢的，因此是速率决定步骤[10.1016/0021-9517(77)90010-0]。这种不确定性取决于诸如催化剂表面、簇大小和反应条件等多种因素[10.1039/D0CS01262A]。

这一争议影响了我们对PDH过程的理解，主要体现在以下几个方面：

1. 催化剂设计：不同的速率限制步骤需要不同的催化剂优化策略。如果解离吸附是速率限制步骤，则应关注改善C-H键活化；如果β-H提取是速率限制步骤，则应关注促进氢的去除[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。

2. 反应条件：最佳温度、压力和反应物浓度可能因速率限制步骤的不同而有所不同[10.1039/D0CS01260B]。

3. 动力学建模：准确的动力学模型对过程设计和优化至关重要，而这些模型依赖于正确识别速率决定步骤[10.1039/D2QI00653G]。

4. 机理理解：这一争议突显了我们对不同催化剂表面上反应机理的基本理解存在差距[10.1016/j.apcata.2011.01.039]。

5. 催化剂表征：不同的速率限制步骤可能需要不同的表征技术来探测相关的表面物种和中间体[10.1039/D0CS01260B]。

6. 过程优化：如果能够明确速率决定步骤，工业PDH过程可以更有效地进行优化[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。

文献表明，速率决定步骤可能取决于催化剂结构，包括粒子大小和表面取向[10.1016/j.apcata.2011.01.039]。例如，在具有较小Pt簇大小的Pt(211)和Pt(100)表面上，第一个C-H键活化被认为是速率决定步骤，而在Pt(111)表面上，一些研究表明第二个C-H键活化是速率限制步骤[10.1039/D0CS01262A]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：围绕丙烷脱氢中速率控制步骤的持续争议凸显了多相催化的复杂性，并强调了需要先进表征技术和理论研究的重要性。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 原位光谱研究：开发和应用先进的原位光谱技术，在真实反应条件下观察反应中间体和表面物种。

2. 计算模拟：采用更复杂的计算方法，在各种催化剂表面和成分上模拟整个反应路径，包括过渡态和中间体。

3. 微观动力学建模：开发综合的微观动力学模型，将催化剂结构、反应条件和表面覆盖度的影响纳入考虑，以预测不同情况下的速率决定步骤。

4. 单原子催化剂：研究单原子催化剂在PDH中的行为，可能简化反应机理并提供对速率决定步骤更清晰的见解。

5. 操作中研究：将光谱技术与催化测试相结合，实时关联结构变化和催化性能。

6. 机器学习方法：利用机器学习算法分析大量实验和计算结果数据集，识别模式并预测不同催化剂系统的速率决定步骤。

7. 同位素标记研究：进行更详细的同位素标记实验，在各种条件下追踪反应路径并阐明速率决定步骤。

通过追求这些研究方向，我们可以努力解决PDH中速率控制步骤的争议，并发展对反应机理更全面的理解。这些知识对设计更高效和选择性的催化剂、优化工业过程以及最终提高通过PDH生产丙烯的可持续性和经济可行性至关重要。
