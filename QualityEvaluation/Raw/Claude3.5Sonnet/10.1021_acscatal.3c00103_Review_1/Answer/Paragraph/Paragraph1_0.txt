<English>
The reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) consists of three main steps:

1. Dissociative adsorption: Propane molecules adsorb onto the catalyst surface and dissociate into adsorbed propyl and hydrogen species.

2. β-H extraction: A hydrogen atom is extracted from the β-carbon of the adsorbed propyl species, forming an adsorbed propylene intermediate.

3. Desorption: The adsorbed propylene and hydrogen desorb from the catalyst surface as products.

There is an ongoing debate in the scientific community regarding which step is the rate-controlling step in the PDH reaction. Some studies suggest that the dissociative adsorption of propane is the rate-limiting step [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016], while others argue that the β-H extraction step is rate-determining [10.1016/0021-9517(77)90010-0]. This uncertainty is further complicated by the fact that the rate-determining step may depend on various factors such as catalyst surface, cluster size, and reaction conditions [10.1039/D0CS01262A].

The ongoing debate impacts our understanding of the PDH process in several ways:

1. Catalyst design: If dissociative adsorption is rate-limiting, catalyst development should focus on improving the initial C-H bond activation and propane adsorption. If β-H extraction is rate-limiting, efforts should concentrate on facilitating hydrogen removal from the adsorbed alkyl species [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016].

2. Reaction conditions: The optimal temperature, pressure, and reactant concentrations may differ depending on which step controls the rate [10.1039/D0CS01262A].

3. Kinetic modeling: Accurate kinetic models are crucial for process optimization and scale-up. Uncertainty about the rate-limiting step complicates the development of reliable models [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016].

4. Mechanistic understanding: The ambiguity in the rate-determining step hinders our ability to fully elucidate the reaction mechanism and identify potential intermediates or transition states [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_1].

5. Process optimization: Industrial PDH processes could be more effectively optimized if the rate-determining step was definitively known [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016].

6. Catalyst deactivation: Understanding the rate-limiting step could provide insights into the primary causes of catalyst deactivation, affecting strategies for catalyst regeneration and longevity [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016].

Recent studies have added complexity to the debate. For instance, density functional theory (DFT) calculations have shown that propane is only physically adsorbed on Pt surfaces, which could imply that dissociative adsorption might be rate-limiting [10.1016/j.apcata.2011.01.039]. Additionally, the rate-determining step may vary depending on the catalyst structure, including particle size and surface orientation [10.1016/j.apcata.2011.01.039].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The ongoing debate surrounding the rate-controlling step in the reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for PDH highlights the complexity of heterogeneous catalysis and emphasizes the need for advanced characterization techniques and theoretical studies. Future research directions may include:

1. In-situ spectroscopic studies: Developing and utilizing advanced in-situ spectroscopic techniques to observe the reaction intermediates and transition states in real-time under realistic reaction conditions.

2. Computational modeling: Enhancing computational models to accurately predict the energetics and kinetics of each step in the PDH mechanism across various catalyst compositions and structures.

3. Machine learning approaches: Employing machine learning algorithms to analyze large datasets of experimental and computational results to identify patterns and predict optimal catalyst designs and reaction conditions.

4. Single-atom catalysts: Investigating the potential of single-atom catalysts to provide more uniform active sites and potentially simplify the mechanistic understanding of PDH.

5. Operando studies: Conducting operando studies that combine spectroscopic techniques with kinetic measurements to correlate catalyst structure and performance under reaction conditions.

6. Microkinetic modeling: Developing more sophisticated microkinetic models that can account for the complexity of the PDH mechanism and the potential variations in rate-determining steps across different catalyst systems.

By pursuing these research directions, we may gain a more comprehensive understanding of the PDH process, leading to the development of more efficient and selective catalysts, improved process designs, and ultimately, more sustainable propylene production.

<Chinese>
丙烷脱氢(PDH)的反向Horiuti-Polanyi机理包括三个主要步骤：

1. 解离吸附：丙烷分子吸附在催化剂表面上并解离成吸附的丙基和氢物种。

2. β-H提取：从吸附的丙基物种的β碳上提取一个氢原子，形成吸附的丙烯中间体。

3. 脱附：吸附的丙烯和氢从催化剂表面脱附成为产物。

科学界对PDH反应中哪个步骤是速率控制步骤存在持续争论。一些研究表明，丙烷的解离吸附是速率限制步骤[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]，而其他研究则认为β-H提取步骤是速率决定步骤[10.1016/0021-9517(77)90010-0]。这种不确定性进一步因各种因素而变得复杂，如催化剂表面、簇大小和反应条件等[10.1039/D0CS01262A]。

这种持续的争论影响了我们对PDH过程的理解，主要体现在以下几个方面：

1. 催化剂设计：如果解离吸附是速率限制步骤，催化剂开发应该集中于改善初始C-H键活化和丙烷吸附。如果β-H提取是速率限制步骤，则应该着重于促进吸附烷基物种的氢移除[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。

2. 反应条件：最佳温度、压力和反应物浓度可能取决于哪个步骤控制反应速率[10.1039/D0CS01262A]。

3. 动力学建模：准确的动力学模型对于过程优化和放大至关重要。对速率限制步骤的不确定性使得开发可靠模型变得复杂[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。

4. 机理理解：速率决定步骤的模糊性阻碍了我们充分阐明反应机理和识别潜在中间体或过渡态的能力[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_1]。

5. 过程优化：如果能明确速率决定步骤，工业PDH过程可以更有效地优化[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。

6. 催化剂失活：了解速率限制步骤可以为催化剂失活的主要原因提供见解，影响催化剂再生和寿命策略[10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。

最近的研究为这场争论增添了复杂性。例如，密度泛函理论(DFT)计算表明，丙烷仅在Pt表面上物理吸附，这可能意味着解离吸附是速率限制步骤[10.1016/j.apcata.2011.01.039]。此外，速率决定步骤可能因催化剂结构而异，包括粒子大小和表面取向[10.1016/j.apcata.2011.01.039]。

综合理解与未来展望：围绕PDH反向Horiuti-Polanyi机理中速率控制步骤的持续争论突显了多相催化的复杂性，并强调了需要先进表征技术和理论研究。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 原位光谱研究：开发和利用先进的原位光谱技术，在实际反应条件下实时观察反应中间体和过渡态。

2. 计算机建模：增强计算模型，以准确预测PDH机理中各步骤在不同催化剂组成和结构下的能量学和动力学。

3. 机器学习方法：运用机器学习算法分析大量实验和计算结果数据集，以识别模式并预测最佳催化剂设计和反应条件。

4. 单原子催化剂：研究单原子催化剂的潜力，以提供更均匀的活性位点，可能简化PDH的机理理解。

5. 操作条件下研究：进行结合光谱技术和动力学测量的操作条件下研究，以在反应条件下关联催化剂结构和性能。

6. 微观动力学建模：开发更复杂的微观动力学模型，能够考虑PDH机理的复杂性以及不同催化剂系统中速率决定步骤的潜在变化。

通过追求这些研究方向，我们可能获得对PDH过程更全面的理解，从而开发出更高效、更选择性的催化剂，改进工艺设计，最终实现更可持续的丙烯生产。
