The reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) consists of three steps:

1. Dissociative adsorption: Propane adsorbs onto the catalyst surface and dissociates into adsorbed propyl and hydrogen species.

2. β-H extraction: A hydrogen atom is removed from the β-carbon of the adsorbed propyl species, forming adsorbed propylene.

3. Desorption: The adsorbed propylene desorbs from the catalyst surface as the product.

There is ongoing debate about whether the dissociative adsorption or the β-H extraction is the rate-controlling step in PDH. 2. Reaction conditions: The rate-controlling step may influence the optimal temperature, pressure, and other reaction conditions for maximizing propylene yield.
