The reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) involves three main steps:

1. Dissociative adsorption: Propane adsorbs onto the catalyst surface and dissociates, forming adsorbed propyl and hydrogen species.

2. β-H extraction: A hydrogen atom is removed from the β-carbon of the adsorbed propyl species, forming an adsorbed propylene intermediate.

3. Desorption: The adsorbed propylene desorbs from the catalyst surface, releasing the product propylene gas.

There is an ongoing debate in the scientific community regarding which step is rate-controlling in the PDH process. Some researchers argue that the dissociative adsorption of propane is the rate-limiting step, while others contend that the β-H extraction is the slowest and therefore rate-determining step.

This uncertainty impacts our understanding of the PDH process in several ways:

1. Catalyst design: If dissociative adsorption is rate-limiting, catalysts should be optimized for propane activation. If β-H extraction is rate-limiting, catalysts should be designed to facilitate hydrogen removal.

2. Reaction conditions: The optimal temperature, pressure, and reactant concentrations may differ depending on which step is rate-controlling.

3. Kinetic modeling: Accurate kinetic models require correct identification of the rate-determining step.

4. Mechanistic studies: The focus of mechanistic investigations may shift depending on which step is believed to be rate-controlling.

5. Process optimization: Industrial PDH processes may be optimized differently based on the understanding of the rate-determining step.

Resolving this debate is crucial for advancing PDH technology and developing more efficient catalysts and processes. Further research, including in-situ spectroscopic studies and advanced computational modeling, is needed to definitively determine the rate-controlling step in the reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism for PDH.

1. 解离吸附：丙烷吸附在催化剂表面并解离，形成吸附的丙基和氢物种。

2. β-H提取：从吸附丙基物种的β-碳上移除一个氢原子，形成吸附的丙烯中间体。

3. 脱附：吸附的丙烯从催化剂表面脱附，释放出丙烯气体产物。

1. 催化剂设计：如果解离吸附是速率限制步骤，催化剂应该针对丙烷活化进行优化。如果β-H提取是速率限制步骤，催化剂应该设计成有利于氢的移除。

2. 反应条件：最佳温度、压力和反应物浓度可能会因速率控制步骤的不同而有所不同。

3. 动力学建模：准确的动力学模型需要正确识别速率决定步骤。

4. 机理研究：机理研究的重点可能会根据被认为是速率控制步骤的不同而转移。

5. 过程优化：工业PDH过程的优化可能会根据对速率决定步骤的理解而有所不同。
