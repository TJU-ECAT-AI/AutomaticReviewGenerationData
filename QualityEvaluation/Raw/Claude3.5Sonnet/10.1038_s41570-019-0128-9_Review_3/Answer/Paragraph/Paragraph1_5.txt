<English>
The bimetallic catalysts Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 have shown promising performance in CO2-ODH (oxidative dehydrogenation) and DRP (dry reforming of propane) reactions with propane, respectively. These catalysts differ significantly in their composition, selectivity, stability, and reaction mechanisms, offering unique insights into the development of efficient catalytic systems for propane conversion and CO2 utilization.

Composition and Structure:
Fe3Ni1/CeO2 consists of oxidized Fe and metallic Ni, while Ni3Pt1/CeO2 contains metallic Ni and Pt, both supported on CeO2 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. In situ XANES measurements confirmed that under reaction conditions, Fe in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 exists in oxidized forms, while Ni in both catalysts and Pt in Ni3Pt1/CeO2 remain in their metallic states [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3].

Selectivity and Activity:
Fe3Ni1/CeO2 exhibits high selectivity (58.2%) towards propylene production via the CO2-ODHP pathway, with good stability [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. In contrast, Ni3Pt1/CeO2 primarily performs DRP with high selectivity (88-96%) towards CO production and demonstrates higher activity and stability compared to monometallic catalysts [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w] [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3].

Reaction Mechanisms:
DFT studies reveal that Fe3Ni1/CeO2 favors CH bond cleavage, promoting the CO2-ODHP pathway, while Ni3Pt1/CeO2 prefers CC bond cleavage, facilitating the DRP pathway [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. The presence of oxidized Fe under reaction conditions is key to favoring oxidative C-H bond cleavage in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 [10.1002/aic.16670].

Synergistic Effects:
The bimetallic Fe3Ni1/CeO2 catalyst shows a synergistic effect, with higher propylene yields (1.6%) compared to monometallic counterparts (Ni: 0.4%, Fe: 0.2%) [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. This synergy is attributed to the unique composition and structure of the bimetallic catalyst.

Theoretical Insights:
DFT calculations revealed that in the presence of oxygen (from CO2 dissociation), Fe segregates to the surface of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 due to stronger Fe-O bonding compared to Ni-O, promoting CO2-ODHP [10.1002/aic.16670]. For Ni3Pt1/CeO2, the higher Ni content likely promotes metallic Ni particle formation, favoring the DRP reaction.

Environmental Potential:
Both catalysts offer promising routes for CO2 utilization, either through CO2-ODHP (Fe3Ni1/CeO2) or DRP (Ni3Pt1/CeO2), addressing global concerns of alkene production and CO2 emissions reduction [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comparative analysis of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 catalysts provides valuable insights into the design of efficient bimetallic systems for propane conversion and CO2 utilization. The synergistic effects observed in these catalysts, particularly in Fe3Ni1/CeO2, suggest that further exploration of bimetallic compositions could lead to even more efficient catalytic systems. Future research efforts might focus on:

1. Investigating other bimetallic combinations, potentially incorporating earth-abundant metals, to optimize catalytic performance and reduce reliance on precious metals.

2. Developing in-depth understanding of the surface dynamics and metal-support interactions under reaction conditions, which could guide the design of more stable and selective catalysts.

3. Exploring the potential for scaling up these catalytic systems for industrial applications, addressing challenges related to catalyst stability, regeneration, and long-term performance.

4. Integrating these catalysts into broader CO2 utilization strategies, potentially coupling propane conversion with other CO2 reduction processes to maximize environmental benefits.

5. Utilizing advanced characterization techniques and computational modeling to further elucidate the atomic-scale mechanisms governing catalyst performance, enabling more precise catalyst design and optimization.

These future directions could contribute significantly to the development of more sustainable and efficient processes for alkene production and CO2 emissions reduction, addressing critical challenges in the chemical industry and environmental protection.

<Chinese>
Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2双金属催化剂分别在丙烷的CO2氧化脱氢(CO2-ODH)和干重整(DRP)反应中表现出良好的性能。这些催化剂在成分、选择性、稳定性和反应机理方面存在显著差异，为开发高效的丙烷转化和CO2利用催化系统提供了独特的见解。

成分和结构：
Fe3Ni1/CeO2由氧化态Fe和金属态Ni组成，而Ni3Pt1/CeO2含有金属态Ni和Pt，两者均以CeO2为载体 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。原位XANES测量证实，在反应条件下，Fe3Ni1/CeO2中的Fe以氧化态存在，而两种催化剂中的Ni和Ni3Pt1/CeO2中的Pt保持金属态 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。

选择性和活性：
Fe3Ni1/CeO2通过CO2-ODHP途径表现出对丙烯生成的高选择性(58.2%)，并具有良好的稳定性 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。相比之下，Ni3Pt1/CeO2主要进行DRP反应，对CO生成具有高选择性(88-96%)，并且与单金属催化剂相比，表现出更高的活性和稳定性 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w] [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。

反应机理：
DFT研究表明，Fe3Ni1/CeO2倾向于C-H键断裂，促进CO2-ODHP途径，而Ni3Pt1/CeO2倾向于C-C键断裂，有利于DRP途径 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。在Fe3Ni1/CeO2中，反应条件下氧化态Fe的存在是促进氧化性C-H键断裂的关键 [10.1002/aic.16670]。

协同效应：
双金属Fe3Ni1/CeO2催化剂表现出协同效应，其丙烯产率(1.6%)高于单金属对应物(Ni: 0.4%, Fe: 0.2%) [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。这种协同效应归因于双金属催化剂的独特成分和结构。

理论洞察：
DFT计算揭示，在存在氧气（来自CO2解离）的情况下，由于Fe-O键比Ni-O键更强，Fe会偏析到Fe3Ni1/CeO2表面，促进CO2-ODHP反应 [10.1002/aic.16670]。对于Ni3Pt1/CeO2，较高的Ni含量可能促进金属Ni粒子的形成，有利于DRP反应。

环境潜力：
这两种催化剂都为CO2利用提供了有前景的途径，无论是通过CO2-ODHP (Fe3Ni1/CeO2)还是DRP (Ni3Pt1/CeO2)，都能解决全球烯烃生产和CO2减排的关切 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2催化剂的比较分析为设计高效的双金属系统用于丙烷转化和CO2利用提供了宝贵的见解。这些催化剂中观察到的协同效应，特别是在Fe3Ni1/CeO2中，表明进一步探索双金属组成可能会导致更高效的催化系统。未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 研究其他双金属组合，可能incorpor地球丰富金属，以优化催化性能并减少对贵金属的依赖。

2. 深入了解反应条件下的表面动力学和金属-载体相互作用，这可以指导设计更稳定和选择性的催化剂。

3. 探索这些催化系统在工业应用中的扩大潜力，解决与催化剂稳定性、再生和长期性能相关的挑战。

4. 将这些催化剂整合到更广泛的CO2利用策略中，可能将丙烷转化与其他CO2还原过程结合，以最大化环境效益。

5. 利用先进的表征技术和计算模型进一步阐明控制催化剂性能的原子尺度机制，实现更精确的催化剂设计和优化。

这些未来方向可能会对开发更可持续和高效的烯烃生产和CO2减排过程做出重大贡献，解决化工行业和环境保护中的关键挑战。
