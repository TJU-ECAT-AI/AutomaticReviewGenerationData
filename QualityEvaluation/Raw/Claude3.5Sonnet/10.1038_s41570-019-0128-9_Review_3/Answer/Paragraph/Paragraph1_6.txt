<English>
The comparative analysis of bimetallic catalysts Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane reveals significant differences in their composition, selectivity, stability, and reaction mechanisms, as well as their potential for environmentally benign catalysis.

Composition and Structure:
Fe3Ni1/CeO2 consists of oxidized Fe and metallic Ni, while Ni3Pt1/CeO2 contains metallic Ni and Pt, both supported on CeO2 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. In situ XANES measurements confirmed that under reaction conditions, Fe in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 exists in oxidized forms, while both catalysts contain metallic Ni (Ni0), and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 features metallic Pt (Pt0) [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3].

Selectivity and Performance:
Fe3Ni1/CeO2 exhibits promising selectivity (58.2%) towards propylene production via the CO2-ODHP pathway, with good stability [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. In contrast, Ni3Pt1/CeO2 primarily performs the DRP reaction with high selectivity (88-96%) towards CO production [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3, 10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

Stability:
Ni3Pt1/CeO2 demonstrates higher activity and stability compared to Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and its monometallic counterparts [10.1002/aic.16670]. The stability of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 is not explicitly mentioned but appears to maintain steady-state performance [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

Reaction Mechanisms:
DFT studies reveal that Fe3Ni1/CeO2 favors CH bond cleavage, promoting the CO2-ODHP pathway, while Ni3Pt1/CeO2 prefers CC bond cleavage, facilitating the DRP pathway [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. The presence of oxidized Fe under reaction conditions is key to favoring oxidative C-H bond cleavage in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 [10.1002/aic.16670].

Synergistic Effects:
The bimetallic Fe3Ni1/CeO2 catalyst shows a synergistic effect, with higher propylene yields (1.6%) compared to monometallic counterparts (Ni: 0.4%, Fe: 0.2%) [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. This synergy is attributed to the formation of the bimetallic catalyst.

Theoretical Insights:
DFT calculations indicate that in Fe3Ni1/CeO2, Fe segregates to the surface due to stronger Fe-O bonding, promoting CO2-ODHP. In Ni3Pt1/CeO2, the higher Ni content likely promotes metallic Ni particle formation, favoring DRP [10.1002/aic.16670].

Environmental Potential:
Both catalysts offer promising routes for CO2 utilization, either through CO2-ODHP (Fe3Ni1/CeO2) or DRP (Ni3Pt1/CeO2), addressing global concerns of alkene production and CO2 emissions reduction [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comparative analysis of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 catalysts provides valuable insights into the design of efficient bimetallic systems for CO2 utilization in propane conversion. The distinct performance of these catalysts in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions highlights the importance of tailoring catalyst composition and structure to achieve desired selectivity and stability.

Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Optimizing the ratio of metal components in bimetallic catalysts to enhance synergistic effects and improve catalytic performance.

2. Investigating the role of the support material (CeO2) in stabilizing the active metal species and its potential participation in the reaction mechanism.

3. Exploring the possibility of designing multi-functional catalysts that can perform both CO2-ODH and DRP reactions efficiently, allowing for tunable product distribution based on reaction conditions.

4. Developing in-operando characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation states during the reaction.

5. Scaling up the production of these catalysts and evaluating their performance in industrial-scale reactors to assess their viability for commercial applications.

6. Investigating the long-term stability and deactivation mechanisms of these catalysts under extended reaction times and multiple regeneration cycles.

7. Exploring the potential of these catalysts in other related reactions, such as CO2 hydrogenation or dry reforming of other hydrocarbons, to expand their applicability in CO2 utilization processes.

By addressing these research directions, we can further advance the development of efficient and sustainable catalytic systems for CO2 utilization, contributing to the global efforts in reducing greenhouse gas emissions and producing valuable chemicals from waste carbon dioxide.

<Chinese>
Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2双金属催化剂在CO2-ODH和DRP与丙烷反应中的比较分析揭示了它们在组成、选择性、稳定性和反应机理方面的显著差异，以及它们在环境友好催化方面的潜力。

组成和结构：
Fe3Ni1/CeO2由氧化铁和金属镍组成，而Ni3Pt1/CeO2包含金属镍和铂，两者均以CeO2为载体 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。原位XANES测量证实，在反应条件下，Fe3Ni1/CeO2中的Fe以氧化态存在，而两种催化剂都含有金属Ni（Ni0），Ni3Pt1/CeO2中还有金属Pt（Pt0）[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。

选择性和性能：
Fe3Ni1/CeO2通过CO2-ODHP途径表现出对丙烯生产的良好选择性（58.2%），并具有良好的稳定性 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。相比之下，Ni3Pt1/CeO2主要进行DRP反应，对CO生产具有高选择性（88-96%）[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3, 10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

稳定性：
与Fe3Ni1/CeO2及其单金属对应物相比，Ni3Pt1/CeO2表现出更高的活性和稳定性 [10.1002/aic.16670]。Fe3Ni1/CeO2的稳定性虽未明确提及，但似乎能保持稳态性能 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

反应机理：
DFT研究表明，Fe3Ni1/CeO2倾向于CH键断裂，促进CO2-ODHP途径，而Ni3Pt1/CeO2更倾向于CC键断裂，有利于DRP途径 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。在反应条件下，氧化铁的存在是Fe3Ni1/CeO2催化剂有利于氧化性C-H键断裂的关键 [10.1002/aic.16670]。

协同效应：
双金属Fe3Ni1/CeO2催化剂表现出协同效应，其丙烯产率（1.6%）高于单金属对应物（Ni：0.4%，Fe：0.2%）[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。这种协同作用归因于双金属催化剂的形成。

理论洞见：
DFT计算表明，在Fe3Ni1/CeO2中，由于Fe-O键更强，Fe会偏析到表面，促进CO2-ODHP。在Ni3Pt1/CeO2中，较高的Ni含量可能促进金属Ni颗粒的形成，有利于DRP [10.1002/aic.16670]。

环境潜力：
这两种催化剂都为CO2利用提供了有前景的途径，无论是通过CO2-ODHP（Fe3Ni1/CeO2）还是DRP（Ni3Pt1/CeO2），都能解决全球烯烃生产和CO2减排的问题 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2催化剂的比较分析为设计高效的双金属系统以用于丙烷转化中的CO2利用提供了宝贵的见解。这些催化剂在CO2-ODH和DRP反应中的不同表现突出了调整催化剂组成和结构以达到所需选择性和稳定性的重要性。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 优化双金属催化剂中金属组分的比例，以增强协同效应并提高催化性能。

2. 研究载体材料（CeO2）在稳定活性金属物种中的作用及其在反应机理中的潜在参与。

3. 探索设计多功能催化剂的可能性，使其能够高效进行CO2-ODH和DRP反应，根据反应条件实现可调的产品分布。

4. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解反应过程中催化剂结构和氧化态的动态变化。

5. 扩大这些催化剂的生产规模，并评估其在工业规模反应器中的性能，以评估其商业应用的可行性。

6. 研究这些催化剂在长期反应时间和多次再生循环下的长期稳定性和失活机理。

7. 探索这些催化剂在其他相关反应中的潜力，如CO2加氢或其他烃类的干重整，以扩大它们在CO2利用过程中的适用性。

通过解决这些研究方向，我们可以进一步推进高效和可持续催化系统的开发，用于CO2利用，为全球减少温室气体排放和从废弃二氧化碳生产有价值化学品的努力做出贡献。
