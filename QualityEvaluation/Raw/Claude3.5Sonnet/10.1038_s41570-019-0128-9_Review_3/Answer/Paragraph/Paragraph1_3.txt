Comparative analysis of bimetallic catalysts' performance, mechanisms, and characteristics in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane: An experimental and theoretical study focusing on Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2

The bimetallic catalysts Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 have emerged as promising systems for CO2-ODH (oxidative dehydrogenation) and DRP (dry reforming) of propane, respectively. These catalysts exhibit distinct compositions, selectivities, stabilities, and reaction mechanisms, which have been elucidated through a combination of experimental and theoretical studies.

Composition and Structure:
Fe3Ni1/CeO2 consists of oxidized Fe and metallic Ni, while Ni3Pt1/CeO2 contains metallic Ni and Pt, both supported on CeO2 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. In situ XANES measurements have confirmed that under reaction conditions, Fe in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 exists in oxidized forms, while Ni in both catalysts and Pt in Ni3Pt1/CeO2 remain in their metallic states [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]. This difference in metal oxidation states plays a crucial role in determining the catalytic behavior of these systems.

Selectivity and Activity:
Fe3Ni1/CeO2 exhibits promising selectivity towards propylene via the CO2-ODHP pathway, with 58.2% C3H6 selectivity [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. In contrast, Ni3Pt1/CeO2 primarily performs the DRP reaction with high selectivity towards CO production, reaching 88% at comparable reactant conversions [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. The Ni3Pt1/CeO2 catalyst also demonstrates higher CO2 conversion (39%) compared to Fe3Ni1/CeO2 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

Stability:
Ni3Pt1/CeO2 shows superior stability compared to monometallic Ni3 catalysts and other precious metal bimetallic catalysts with Fe3 and Co3 [10.1002/aic.16670]. While the stability of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 is not explicitly compared, it maintains steady-state performance during CO2-ODHP reactions [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

Reaction Mechanisms:
DFT calculations have provided insights into the reaction mechanisms of these catalysts. For Fe3Ni1/CeO2, the pathway for oxidative CH bond cleavage is energetically favored over CC bond scission, promoting the CO2-ODHP reaction [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. The presence of oxidized Fe under reaction conditions is key to favoring this oxidative C-H bond cleavage [10.1002/aic.16670]. Conversely, on Pt-terminated Ni3Pt(111), the CC bond cleavage pathway is energetically preferred, facilitating the DRP reaction [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

Synergistic Effects:
Experimental studies have revealed synergistic effects in the Fe3Ni1/CeO2 catalyst. The bimetallic system shows higher propylene yields (1.6%) compared to the respective monometallic catalysts (0.4% for Ni and 0.2% for Fe) [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. This synergy is attributed to the unique interaction between Fe and Ni in the bimetallic structure.

Theoretical Insights:
DFT calculations have provided further understanding of the catalysts' behavior. In Fe3Ni1/CeO2, Fe tends to segregate to the surface in the presence of oxygen (from CO2 dissociation) due to stronger Fe-O bonding compared to Ni-O [10.1002/aic.16670]. This surface segregation of oxidized Fe promotes the CO2-ODHP pathway. For Ni3Pt1/CeO2, the higher Ni content likely promotes the formation of metallic Ni particles, which may contribute to its DRP activity [10.1002/aic.16670].

Environmental Implications:
Both Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 catalysts offer promising routes for CO2 utilization, addressing global concerns of alkene production and CO2 emissions reduction [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]. Fe3Ni1/CeO2, in particular, demonstrates potential for CO2-ODH reactions, which could contribute to the production of valuable alkenes while consuming CO2. The use of non-precious metals like Fe and Ni in these catalysts aligns with the goal of developing more sustainable and cost-effective catalytic systems for environmentally benign processes.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The comparative analysis of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 catalysts reveals the intricate relationships between catalyst composition, structure, and performance in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions. These findings open up new avenues for catalyst design and optimization in the field of CO2 utilization and alkene production.

Future research directions may include:

1. Exploring the potential of other bimetallic combinations, particularly those incorporating earth-abundant elements, to further enhance catalytic performance and sustainability.

2. Investigating the long-term stability and deactivation mechanisms of these catalysts under industrial-like conditions to address scalability challenges.

3. Developing advanced in situ and operando characterization techniques to gain deeper insights into the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and composition during reactions.

4. Integrating machine learning approaches with DFT calculations to accelerate the discovery and optimization of new catalyst formulations for CO2-ODH and DRP reactions.

5. Exploring the potential of these catalysts in other CO2 utilization reactions, such as CO2 hydrogenation or CO2-assisted alkane activation, to broaden their applicability in green chemistry processes.

By building upon the current understanding of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 catalysts, researchers can work towards developing more efficient, selective, and environmentally friendly catalytic systems for propane conversion and CO2 utilization, ultimately contributing to the global efforts in sustainable chemical production and greenhouse gas mitigation.

<Chinese>
双金属催化剂在CO2-ODH和DRP反应中与丙烷的性能、机理和特性的比较分析：以Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2为重点的实验和理论研究

双金属催化剂Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2分别在丙烷的CO2-ODH（氧化脱氢）和DRP（干重整）反应中表现出良好的前景。这些催化剂具有不同的组成、选择性、稳定性和反应机理，通过实验和理论研究的结合已得到阐明。

组成和结构：
Fe3Ni1/CeO2由氧化铁和金属镍组成，而Ni3Pt1/CeO2含有金属镍和铂，两者均以CeO2为载体[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。原位XANES测量证实，在反应条件下，Fe3Ni1/CeO2中的Fe以氧化态存在，而两种催化剂中的Ni和Ni3Pt1/CeO2中的Pt保持金属态[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。这种金属氧化态的差异在决定这些系统的催化行为中起着关键作用。

选择性和活性：
Fe3Ni1/CeO2通过CO2-ODHP途径对丙烯表现出良好的选择性，C3H6选择性达到58.2%[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。相比之下，Ni3Pt1/CeO2主要进行DRP反应，对CO的选择性很高，在相当的反应物转化率下达到88%[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。Ni3Pt1/CeO2催化剂还表现出更高的CO2转化率（39%），相比Fe3Ni1/CeO2[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

稳定性：
Ni3Pt1/CeO2相比单金属Ni3催化剂和其他含Fe3和Co3的贵金属双金属催化剂表现出更优越的稳定性[10.1002/aic.16670]。虽然没有明确比较Fe3Ni1/CeO2的稳定性，但它在CO2-ODHP反应中保持稳态性能[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

反应机理：
DFT计算为这些催化剂的反应机理提供了洞见。对于Fe3Ni1/CeO2，氧化性CH键断裂的路径在能量上优于CC键断裂，促进了CO2-ODHP反应[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。反应条件下氧化Fe的存在是有利于这种氧化性C-H键断裂的关键[10.1002/aic.16670]。相反，在Pt终止的Ni3Pt(111)上，CC键断裂路径在能量上更有利，促进了DRP反应[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

协同效应：
实验研究揭示了Fe3Ni1/CeO2催化剂中的协同效应。双金属系统显示出更高的丙烯产率（1.6%），相比各自的单金属催化剂（Ni为0.4%，Fe为0.2%）[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。这种协同作用归因于双金属结构中Fe和Ni之间的独特相互作用。

理论洞见：
DFT计算进一步理解了催化剂的行为。在Fe3Ni1/CeO2中，由于Fe-O键比Ni-O键更强，Fe倾向于在存在氧气（来自CO2解离）的情况下偏析到表面[10.1002/aic.16670]。这种氧化Fe的表面偏析促进了CO2-ODHP途径。对于Ni3Pt1/CeO2，较高的Ni含量可能促进金属Ni颗粒的形成，这可能有助于其DRP活性[10.1002/aic.16670]。

环境影响：
Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2催化剂都为CO2利用提供了有前景的途径，解决了全球烯烃生产和CO2减排的问题[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。特别是Fe3Ni1/CeO2在CO2-ODH反应中表现出潜力，可以在消耗CO2的同时生产有价值的烯烃。这些催化剂中使用Fe和Ni等非贵金属符合开发更可持续和成本效益的催化系统的目标，用于环境友好的过程。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：
对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2催化剂的比较分析揭示了催化剂组成、结构与CO2-ODH和DRP反应性能之间的复杂关系。这些发现为CO2利用和烯烃生产领域的催化剂设计和优化开辟了新途径。

未来研究方向可能包括：

1. 探索其他双金属组合的潜力，特别是那些包含地球丰富元素的组合，以进一步提高催化性能和可持续性。

2. 在类工业条件下研究这些催化剂的长期稳定性和失活机制，以解决可扩展性挑战。

3. 开发先进的原位和操作表征技术，以深入了解反应过程中催化剂结构和组成的动态变化。

4. 将机器学习方法与DFT计算相结合，加速发现和优化用于CO2-ODH和DRP反应的新催化剂配方。

5. 探索这些催化剂在其他CO2利用反应中的潜力，如CO2加氢或CO2辅助烷烃活化，以扩大它们在绿色化学过程中的应用范围。

通过在当前对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2催化剂理解的基础上，研究人员可以致力于开发更高效、选择性更好、更环保的丙烷转化和CO2利用催化系统，最终为可持续化学品生产和温室气体减排的全球努力做出贡献。
