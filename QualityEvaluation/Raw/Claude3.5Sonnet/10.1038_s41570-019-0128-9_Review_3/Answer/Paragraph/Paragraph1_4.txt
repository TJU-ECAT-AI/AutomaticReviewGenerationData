Comparative analysis of bimetallic catalysts' performance, mechanisms, and characteristics in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane: An experimental and theoretical study focusing on Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2

The bimetallic catalysts Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 have shown promising performance in CO2-ODH (oxidative dehydrogenation) and DRP (dry reforming of propane) reactions with propane, respectively [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]. These catalysts differ significantly in their composition, selectivity, stability, and reaction mechanisms, offering unique insights into the design of efficient catalysts for propane conversion and CO2 utilization.

Composition and Structure:
Fe3Ni1/CeO2 consists of oxidized Fe and metallic Ni, while Ni3Pt1/CeO2 contains metallic Pt and Ni, both supported on CeO2 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. In-situ XANES measurements confirmed that under reaction conditions, Fe in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 exists in oxidized forms, while Ni remains metallic. For Ni3Pt1/CeO2, both Ni and Pt are present in their metallic states [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3].

Selectivity and Activity:
Fe3Ni1/CeO2 exhibits high selectivity (58.2%) towards propylene production via the CO2-ODHP pathway [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. In contrast, Ni3Pt1/CeO2 primarily performs DRP with high selectivity (88%) towards CO production [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. The Fe3Ni1/CeO2 catalyst demonstrates a C3H6 yield of 1.6%, significantly higher than its monometallic counterparts (0.4% for Ni and 0.2% for Fe), indicating a synergistic effect between Fe and Ni [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3].

Stability:
Ni3Pt1/CeO2 demonstrates better stability compared to monometallic Ni3 catalysts [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. The stability of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 is not explicitly mentioned, but it appears to maintain steady-state performance [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

Reaction Mechanisms:
DFT studies reveal that Fe3Ni1/CeO2 favors CH bond cleavage, promoting the CO2-ODHP pathway. On the other hand, Ni3Pt1/CeO2 prefers CC bond cleavage, facilitating the DRP pathway [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. The presence of oxidized Fe under reaction conditions is key to favoring oxidative C-H bond cleavage via the CO2-ODHP pathway in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 [10.1002/aic.16670].

Theoretical Insights:
DFT calculations show that in the presence of oxygen (from CO2 dissociation), Fe segregates to the surface of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 due to stronger Fe-O bonding compared to Ni-O. However, on surfaces with higher Ni content, metallic Ni particles are more pronounced [10.1002/aic.16670]. This surface segregation phenomenon plays a crucial role in determining the catalytic behavior of these bimetallic systems.

Environmental Potential:
Both catalysts offer promising routes for CO2 utilization, either through CO2-ODHP (Fe3Ni1/CeO2) or DRP (Ni3Pt1/CeO2), addressing global concerns of alkene production and CO2 emissions reduction [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. The use of non-precious metals like iron and nickel in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 aligns with the goal of developing more sustainable and cost-effective catalytic systems [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The comparative study of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 catalysts provides valuable insights into the design principles for efficient bimetallic catalysts in CO2 utilization reactions. The synergistic effects observed in these systems, particularly in Fe3Ni1/CeO2, open up new avenues for catalyst design. Future research efforts could focus on:

1. Exploring other bimetallic combinations, potentially incorporating earth-abundant metals, to further enhance catalytic performance and sustainability.
2. Investigating the role of the support material (CeO2) in these reactions and exploring alternative supports to optimize catalyst performance.
3. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and composition under reaction conditions.
4. Scaling up these catalytic systems for potential industrial applications, addressing challenges in catalyst stability and regeneration.
5. Integrating computational modeling with high-throughput experimental screening to accelerate the discovery of novel, high-performance catalysts for CO2 utilization.

These research directions could lead to the development of more efficient and environmentally friendly catalytic processes for propane conversion and CO2 utilization, contributing to the broader goals of sustainable chemical production and greenhouse gas mitigation.

<Chinese>
丙烷脱氢催化剂的比较分析：双金属催化剂Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2在CO2-ODH和DRP反应中的性能、机理和特性的实验和理论研究

双金属催化剂Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2分别在丙烷的CO2-ODH（氧化脱氢）和DRP（干重整）反应中表现出良好的性能[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。这些催化剂在组成、选择性、稳定性和反应机理方面存在显著差异，为设计高效的丙烷转化和CO2利用催化剂提供了独特的见解。

组成和结构：
Fe3Ni1/CeO2由氧化态Fe和金属态Ni组成，而Ni3Pt1/CeO2含有金属态Pt和Ni，两者均负载在CeO2上[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。原位XANES测量证实，在反应条件下，Fe3Ni1/CeO2中的Fe以氧化态存在，而Ni保持金属态。对于Ni3Pt1/CeO2，Ni和Pt均以金属态存在[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。

选择性和活性：
Fe3Ni1/CeO2通过CO2-ODHP途径对丙烯生产表现出高选择性（58.2%）[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。相比之下，Ni3Pt1/CeO2主要进行DRP反应，对CO生产具有高选择性（88%）[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。Fe3Ni1/CeO2催化剂的C3H6产率为1.6%，显著高于其单金属对应物（Ni为0.4%，Fe为0.2%），表明Fe和Ni之间存在协同效应[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。

稳定性：
与单金属Ni3催化剂相比，Ni3Pt1/CeO2表现出更好的稳定性[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。Fe3Ni1/CeO2的稳定性没有明确提及，但似乎能维持稳态性能[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

反应机理：
DFT研究表明，Fe3Ni1/CeO2倾向于CH键断裂，促进CO2-ODHP途径。另一方面，Ni3Pt1/CeO2更倾向于CC键断裂，有利于DRP途径[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。在Fe3Ni1/CeO2中，反应条件下氧化态Fe的存在对于通过CO2-ODHP途径促进氧化性C-H键断裂起关键作用[10.1002/aic.16670]。

理论见解：
DFT计算表明，在存在氧（来自CO2解离）的情况下，由于Fe-O键比Ni-O键更强，Fe会偏析到Fe3Ni1/CeO2的表面。然而，在Ni含量较高的表面上，金属Ni颗粒更为显著[10.1002/aic.16670]。这种表面偏析现象在决定这些双金属系统的催化行为中起着至关重要的作用。

环境潜力：
两种催化剂都为CO2利用提供了有前景的途径，无论是通过CO2-ODHP（Fe3Ni1/CeO2）还是DRP（Ni3Pt1/CeO2），都有助于解决全球烯烃生产和CO2减排的问题[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。在Fe3Ni1/CeO2中使用铁和镍等非贵金属符合开发更可持续和成本效益催化系统的目标[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：
对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2催化剂的比较研究为设计CO2利用反应中高效双金属催化剂提供了宝贵的见解。这些系统中观察到的协同效应，特别是在Fe3Ni1/CeO2中，为催化剂设计开辟了新的途径。未来的研究努力可以集中在：

1. 探索其他双金属组合，可能纳入地球丰富金属，以进一步提高催化性能和可持续性。
2. 研究载体材料（CeO2）在这些反应中的作用，并探索替代载体以优化催化剂性能。
3. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解催化剂在反应条件下的结构和组成的动态变化。
4. 扩大这些催化系统的规模以应用于潜在的工业应用，解决催化剂稳定性和再生的挑战。
5. 将计算机建模与高通量实验筛选相结合，加速发现用于CO2利用的新型高性能催化剂。

这些研究方向可能导致开发出更高效、更环保的丙烷转化和CO2利用催化过程，为可持续化学品生产和温室气体减排的更广泛目标做出贡献。
