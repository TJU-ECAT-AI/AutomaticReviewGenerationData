<English>
The bimetallic catalysts Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 have shown promising performance in CO2-ODH (oxidative dehydrogenation) and DRP (dry reforming) reactions with propane, respectively. These catalysts differ significantly in their composition, selectivity, stability, and reaction mechanisms.

Composition and Structure:
Fe3Ni1/CeO2 consists of oxidized Fe and metallic Ni, while Ni3Pt1/CeO2 contains metallic Ni and Pt, both supported on CeO2 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. In situ XANES measurements confirmed that under reaction conditions, Fe in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 exists in oxidized forms, while Ni is present as Ni0 in both catalysts. Ni3Pt1/CeO2 features metallic Pt (Pt0) [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3].

Selectivity and Activity:
Fe3Ni1/CeO2 exhibits high selectivity (58.2%) towards propylene production via the CO2-ODHP pathway, with good stability [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. In contrast, Ni3Pt1/CeO2 primarily performs DRP with high selectivity (88-96%) towards CO production and demonstrates 39% CO2 conversion [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w] [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3].

Stability:
Ni3Pt1/CeO2 demonstrates better stability compared to monometallic Ni3 and other precious metal catalysts with Fe3 and Co3 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w] [10.1002/aic.16670]. The stability of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 is not explicitly mentioned but appears to maintain steady-state performance.

Reaction Mechanisms:
DFT studies reveal that Fe3Ni1/CeO2 favors CH bond cleavage, promoting the CO2-ODHP pathway. On bulk-terminated-Fe3Ni(111), the pathway for oxidative CH bond cleavage lies lower in energy than that for CC bond scission. Conversely, Ni3Pt1/CeO2 prefers CC bond cleavage, facilitating the DRP pathway. On Pt-terminated-Ni3Pt(111), the pathway for CC bond cleavage lies lower in energy than that for CH bond breaking [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

Synergistic Effects:
The bimetallic Fe3Ni1/CeO2 catalyst shows a synergistic effect, with higher propylene yields (1.6%) compared to monometallic counterparts (Ni: 0.4%, Fe: 0.2%) [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w] [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]. This indicates that the two elements operate synergistically in the bimetallic catalyst.

Structure-Function Relationship:
The oxidation states of metals play a crucial role in determining catalytic performance. The presence of oxidized Fe under reaction conditions is key to favoring oxidative C-H bond cleavage via the CO2-ODHP pathway in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 [10.1002/aic.16670]. DFT calculations show that in the presence of oxygen (from CO2 dissociation), Fe segregates to the surface due to stronger Fe-O bonding compared to Ni-O, promoting CO2-ODHP [10.1002/aic.16670].

Environmental Potential:
Both catalysts offer promising routes for CO2 utilization, either through CO2-ODHP (Fe3Ni1/CeO2) or DRP (Ni3Pt1/CeO2), addressing global concerns of alkene production and CO2 emissions reduction [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]. The use of non-precious metals like iron and nickel in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 aligns with the goal of developing more sustainable and cost-effective catalytic systems.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The comparative analysis of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 catalysts provides valuable insights into the design of efficient bimetallic catalysts for CO2 utilization in propane conversion. The synergistic effects observed in these catalysts, particularly in Fe3Ni1/CeO2, suggest that further exploration of bimetallic combinations could lead to even more effective catalysts. Future research might focus on:

1. Optimizing metal ratios and support materials to enhance selectivity and stability.
2. Investigating the potential of other transition metal combinations to achieve similar or superior performance without relying on precious metals.
3. Developing in-operando characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic behavior of these catalysts under reaction conditions.
4. Exploring the scalability and long-term stability of these catalysts for industrial applications.
5. Investigating the potential of these catalysts for other alkane feedstocks or related reactions.

By building on these findings, researchers can work towards developing more efficient and sustainable catalytic systems for CO2 utilization, potentially addressing both the global propylene production gap and greenhouse gas emissions reduction simultaneously.

<Chinese>
双金属催化剂Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2在丙烷的CO2-ODH（氧化脱氢）和DRP（干重整）反应中分别表现出良好的性能。这些催化剂在组成、选择性、稳定性和反应机理方面存在显著差异。

组成和结构：
Fe3Ni1/CeO2由氧化态Fe和金属态Ni组成，而Ni3Pt1/CeO2包含金属态Ni和Pt，两者均以CeO2为载体 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。原位XANES测量证实，在反应条件下，Fe3Ni1/CeO2中的Fe以氧化态存在，而两种催化剂中的Ni均以Ni0形式存在。Ni3Pt1/CeO2中的Pt呈金属态（Pt0）[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。

选择性和活性：
Fe3Ni1/CeO2通过CO2-ODHP途径对丙烯生成表现出高选择性（58.2%），并具有良好的稳定性 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。相比之下，Ni3Pt1/CeO2主要进行DRP反应，对CO生成具有高选择性（88-96%），并展现出39%的CO2转化率 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w] [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。

稳定性：
与单金属Ni3以及其他含Fe3和Co3的贵金属催化剂相比，Ni3Pt1/CeO2表现出更好的稳定性 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w] [10.1002/aic.16670]。Fe3Ni1/CeO2的稳定性未被明确提及，但似乎能保持稳态性能。

反应机理：
DFT研究表明，Fe3Ni1/CeO2倾向于CH键断裂，促进CO2-ODHP途径。在体相端接的Fe3Ni(111)表面上，氧化性CH键断裂的能量路径低于CC键断裂。相反，Ni3Pt1/CeO2倾向于CC键断裂，有利于DRP途径。在Pt端接的Ni3Pt(111)表面上，CC键断裂的能量路径低于CH键断裂 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

协同效应：
双金属Fe3Ni1/CeO2催化剂表现出协同效应，其丙烯产率（1.6%）高于单金属对应物（Ni：0.4%，Fe：0.2%）[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w] [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。这表明在双金属催化剂中两种元素存在协同作用。

结构-功能关系：
金属的氧化态在决定催化性能方面起着关键作用。在反应条件下，氧化态Fe的存在是通过CO2-ODHP途径促进氧化性C-H键断裂的关键因素 [10.1002/aic.16670]。DFT计算表明，在存在氧气（来自CO2解离）的情况下，由于Fe-O键比Ni-O键更强，Fe会偏析到表面，促进CO2-ODHP [10.1002/aic.16670]。

环境潜力：
这两种催化剂都为CO2利用提供了有前景的途径，无论是通过CO2-ODHP（Fe3Ni1/CeO2）还是DRP（Ni3Pt1/CeO2），都能解决全球烯烃生产和CO2减排的问题 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。Fe3Ni1/CeO2中使用铁和镍等非贵金属符合开发更可持续和经济高效的催化系统的目标。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：
对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2催化剂的比较分析为设计高效的双金属催化剂用于丙烷转化中的CO2利用提供了宝贵见解。这些催化剂中观察到的协同效应，特别是在Fe3Ni1/CeO2中，表明进一步探索双金属组合可能导致更有效的催化剂。未来的研究可能集中在：

1. 优化金属比例和载体材料，以提高选择性和稳定性。
2. 研究其他过渡金属组合的潜力，以实现类似或更优的性能，而无需依赖贵金属。
3. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解这些催化剂在反应条件下的动态行为。
4. 探索这些催化剂在工业应用中的可扩展性和长期稳定性。
5. 研究这些催化剂对其他烷烃原料或相关反应的潜在应用。

通过这些发现，研究人员可以致力于开发更高效、更可持续的CO2利用催化系统，有可能同时解决全球丙烯生产缺口和温室气体减排问题。
