Comparative analysis of bimetallic catalysts' performance, mechanisms, and characteristics in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane: An experimental and theoretical study focusing on Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2

The bimetallic catalysts Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 have shown promising performance in CO2-ODH (oxidative dehydrogenation) and DRP (dry reforming of propane) reactions with propane, respectively [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]. These catalysts differ significantly in their composition, selectivity, stability, and reaction mechanisms, offering unique insights into the design of efficient catalysts for propane conversion and CO2 utilization.

Composition and Structure:
Fe3Ni1/CeO2 consists of oxidized Fe and metallic Ni, while Ni3Pt1/CeO2 contains metallic Pt and Ni, both supported on CeO2 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. In situ XANES measurements have confirmed these oxidation states under reaction conditions, revealing that Fe in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 exists in oxidized forms, while both catalysts contain metallic Ni (Ni0) [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]. The presence of oxidized Fe in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 plays a crucial role in its catalytic activity for CO2-ODH reactions [10.1002/aic.16670].

Selectivity and Activity:
Fe3Ni1/CeO2 exhibits high selectivity (58.2%) towards propylene production via the CO2-ODHP pathway, demonstrating good stability [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. In contrast, Ni3Pt1/CeO2 primarily performs DRP reactions with high selectivity (88-96%) towards CO production [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3] [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. Ni3Pt1/CeO2 also shows higher CO2 conversion (39%) compared to Fe3Ni1/CeO2 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

Stability:
Ni3Pt1/CeO2 demonstrates better stability compared to monometallic Ni3 catalysts [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. The stability of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 is not explicitly mentioned but appears to maintain steady-state performance [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w].

Reaction Mechanisms:
DFT studies reveal that Fe3Ni1/CeO2 favors CH bond cleavage, promoting the CO2-ODHP pathway, while Ni3Pt1/CeO2 prefers CC bond cleavage, facilitating the DRP pathway [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. The presence of oxidized Fe under reaction conditions is key to favoring oxidative C-H bond cleavage in the CO2-ODHP pathway for Fe3Ni1/CeO2 [10.1002/aic.16670].

Synergistic Effects:
The bimetallic Fe3Ni1/CeO2 catalyst shows a synergistic effect, with higher propylene yields (1.6%) compared to monometallic counterparts (Ni: 0.4%, Fe: 0.2%) [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. This synergy is attributed to the unique interaction between Fe and Ni in the bimetallic system.

Theoretical Insights:
DFT calculations have provided valuable insights into the surface chemistry of these catalysts. For Fe3Ni1/CeO2, Fe segregates to the surface due to stronger Fe-O bonding in the presence of oxygen from CO2 dissociation, promoting CO2-ODHP [10.1002/aic.16670]. In Ni3Pt1/CeO2, the higher Ni content likely promotes metallic Ni particle formation, favoring DRP reactions [10.1002/aic.16670].

Environmental Potential:
Both catalysts offer promising routes for CO2 utilization, addressing global concerns of alkene production and CO2 emissions reduction [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. Fe3Ni1/CeO2, in particular, demonstrates potential for CO2-ODH reactions, which could contribute to the production of valuable alkenes while consuming CO2 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The comparative analysis of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 catalysts reveals the intricate relationship between catalyst composition, structure, and performance in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions. These findings pave the way for future research directions in the field of propane dehydrogenation and CO2 utilization:

1. Catalyst design optimization: Further exploration of bimetallic compositions and ratios could lead to catalysts with enhanced selectivity, activity, and stability. The synergistic effects observed in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 suggest that other bimetallic combinations might offer improved performance.

2. In-depth mechanistic studies: Advanced in situ characterization techniques, coupled with computational modeling, could provide deeper insights into the reaction mechanisms and the role of each metal in the catalytic process. This understanding could guide the rational design of next-generation catalysts.

3. Scalability and industrial application: Future research should focus on scaling up these catalytic systems and evaluating their performance under industrial conditions. This includes addressing challenges related to catalyst lifetime, regeneration, and process integration.

4. Sustainable catalyst development: The use of non-precious metals in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 aligns with the goal of developing more sustainable catalytic systems. Future efforts could explore other earth-abundant elements to create environmentally friendly and economically viable catalysts.

5. Integrated CO2 utilization processes: Developing integrated systems that combine CO2 capture with catalytic conversion could maximize the environmental benefits of these catalysts. This approach could contribute to closing the carbon cycle in industrial processes.

By addressing these research directions, the scientific community can work towards more efficient and sustainable solutions for propane conversion and CO2 utilization, potentially revolutionizing the chemical industry and contributing to global efforts in reducing greenhouse gas emissions.

比较分析双金属催化剂在丙烷CO2-ODH和DRP反应中的性能、机理和特性：以Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2为重点的实验和理论研究

双金属催化剂Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2分别在丙烷的CO2-ODH（氧化脱氢）和DRP（干重整）反应中表现出了良好的性能[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。这些催化剂在成分、选择性、稳定性和反应机理方面存在显著差异，为丙烷转化和CO2利用的高效催化剂设计提供了独特的见解。

成分和结构：
Fe3Ni1/CeO2由氧化态Fe和金属Ni组成，而Ni3Pt1/CeO2含有金属Pt和Ni，两者均以CeO2为载体[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。原位XANES测量确认了这些反应条件下的氧化态，揭示Fe3Ni1/CeO2中的Fe以氧化态存在，而两种催化剂都含有金属Ni（Ni0）[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。Fe3Ni1/CeO2中氧化态Fe的存在对其CO2-ODH反应的催化活性起着关键作用[10.1002/aic.16670]。

选择性和活性：
Fe3Ni1/CeO2通过CO2-ODHP途径表现出对丙烯生产的高选择性（58.2%），并具有良好的稳定性[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。相比之下，Ni3Pt1/CeO2主要进行DRP反应，对CO生产具有高选择性（88-96%）[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3] [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。Ni3Pt1/CeO2还表现出较高的CO2转化率（39%），相比Fe3Ni1/CeO2更高[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

稳定性：
与单金属Ni3催化剂相比，Ni3Pt1/CeO2表现出更好的稳定性[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。Fe3Ni1/CeO2的稳定性未被明确提及，但似乎保持了稳态性能[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。

反应机理：
DFT研究揭示，Fe3Ni1/CeO2倾向于C-H键断裂，促进CO2-ODHP途径，而Ni3Pt1/CeO2倾向于C-C键断裂，有利于DRP途径[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。反应条件下氧化态Fe的存在是Fe3Ni1/CeO2在CO2-ODHP途径中有利于氧化性C-H键断裂的关键[10.1002/aic.16670]。

协同效应：
双金属Fe3Ni1/CeO2催化剂表现出协同效应，丙烯产率（1.6%）高于单金属对应物（Ni：0.4%，Fe：0.2%）[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。这种协同作用归因于双金属体系中Fe和Ni之间的独特相互作用。

理论洞察：
DFT计算为这些催化剂的表面化学提供了宝贵的见解。对于Fe3Ni1/CeO2，由于Fe-O键较强，在CO2解离产生的氧存在的情况下，Fe会偏析到表面，促进CO2-ODHP[10.1002/aic.16670]。在Ni3Pt1/CeO2中，较高的Ni含量可能促进金属Ni颗粒的形成，有利于DRP反应[10.1002/aic.16670]。

环境潜力：
这两种催化剂为CO2利用提供了有前景的途径，解决了全球烯烃生产和CO2减排的问题[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。特别是Fe3Ni1/CeO2在CO2-ODH反应中表现出潜力，可以在消耗CO2的同时生产有价值的烯烃[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。

综合理解与未来展望：
对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2催化剂的比较分析揭示了催化剂成分、结构与CO2-ODH和DRP反应性能之间的复杂关系。这些发现为丙烷脱氢和CO2利用领域的未来研究方向铺平了道路：

1. 催化剂设计优化：进一步探索双金属组成和比例可能导致选择性、活性和稳定性增强的催化剂。Fe3Ni1/CeO2中观察到的协同效应表明，其他双金属组合可能提供更好的性能。

2. 深入的机理研究：先进的原位表征技术，结合计算模拟，可以为反应机理和每种金属在催化过程中的作用提供更深入的见解。这种理解可以指导下一代催化剂的合理设计。

3. 可扩展性和工业应用：未来的研究应关注这些催化体系的放大和在工业条件下的性能评估。这包括解决与催化剂寿命、再生和过程集成相关的挑战。

4. 可持续催化剂开发：Fe3Ni1/CeO2中使用非贵金属符合开发更可持续催化系统的目标。未来的努力可以探索其他地球丰富元素，创造环境友好和经济可行的催化剂。

5. 集成CO2利用过程：开发将CO2捕获与催化转化相结合的集成系统，可以最大化这些催化剂的环境效益。这种方法可以为工业过程中的碳循环闭合做出贡献。
