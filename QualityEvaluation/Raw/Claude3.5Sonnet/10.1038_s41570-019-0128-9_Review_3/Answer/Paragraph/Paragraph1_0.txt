Comparative analysis of bimetallic catalysts' performance, mechanisms, and characteristics in CO2-ODH and DRP reactions with propane: An experimental and theoretical study focusing on Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2

Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 are promising bimetallic catalysts for CO2-ODH (oxidative dehydrogenation) and DRP (dry reforming) of propane, respectively. These catalysts exhibit distinct compositions, selectivities, stabilities, and reaction mechanisms, which have been elucidated through experimental and theoretical studies.

Composition and Structure:
Fe3Ni1/CeO2 consists of oxidized Fe and metallic Ni, while Ni3Pt1/CeO2 contains metallic Ni and Pt, both supported on CeO2 [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. In situ XANES measurements confirmed that under reaction conditions, Fe in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 exists in oxidized forms, while Ni in both catalysts and Pt in Ni3Pt1/CeO2 remain in their metallic states [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3].

Selectivity and Activity:
Fe3Ni1/CeO2 demonstrates promising selectivity (58.2%) towards propylene production via the CO2-ODHP pathway [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. In contrast, Ni3Pt1/CeO2 primarily performs the DRP reaction with high selectivity (88-96%) towards CO production [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w] [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3].

Stability:
Ni3Pt1/CeO2 exhibits higher activity and stability compared to monometallic Ni3 and other precious metal counterparts with Fe3 and Co3 [10.1002/aic.16670]. Fe3Ni1/CeO2 also demonstrates good stability, maintaining steady-state performance [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3].

Reaction Mechanisms:
DFT studies reveal that Fe3Ni1/CeO2 favors CH bond cleavage, promoting the CO2-ODHP pathway, while Ni3Pt1/CeO2 prefers CC bond cleavage, facilitating the DRP pathway [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. The presence of oxidized Fe under reaction conditions is key to favoring oxidative C-H bond cleavage in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 [10.1002/aic.16670].

Synergistic Effects:
The bimetallic Fe3Ni1/CeO2 catalyst demonstrates a synergistic effect, with higher propylene yields (1.6%) compared to monometallic counterparts (Ni: 0.4%, Fe: 0.2%) [10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]. This synergy is attributed to the unique combination of metallic Ni and oxidized Fe in the catalyst.

Theoretical Insights:
DFT calculations show that in the presence of oxygen (from CO2 dissociation), Fe segregates to the surface of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 due to stronger Fe-O bonding compared to Ni-O [10.1002/aic.16670]. This surface segregation plays a crucial role in promoting the CO2-ODHP pathway. For Ni3Pt1/CeO2, the higher Ni content likely promotes metallic Ni particle formation, favoring the DRP reaction.

Environmental Implications:
Both catalysts offer promising routes for CO2 utilization, addressing global concerns of alkene production and CO2 emissions reduction [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]. Fe3Ni1/CeO2, in particular, demonstrates potential for CO2-ODH reactions, which could contribute to the production of valuable alkenes while consuming CO2.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comparative analysis of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Ni3Pt1/CeO2 catalysts reveals the critical role of composition, structure, and reaction conditions in determining catalyst performance for CO2 utilization reactions. Future research directions may include:

1. Catalyst design optimization: Fine-tuning the metal ratios and oxidation states to enhance selectivity and stability.
2. In-depth mechanistic studies: Utilizing advanced in situ characterization techniques to further elucidate the reaction pathways and active sites.
3. Scalability and industrial application: Investigating the feasibility of scaling up these catalytic systems for industrial propylene production and CO2 mitigation.
4. Novel bimetallic combinations: Exploring other transition metal combinations to develop more efficient and cost-effective catalysts for CO2-ODH and DRP reactions.
5. Computational screening: Employing machine learning and high-throughput computational methods to predict and design new bimetallic catalysts with improved performance.

These advancements could pave the way for more sustainable and economically viable processes in the chemical industry, addressing both the growing demand for alkenes and the urgent need for CO2 emissions reduction.

双金属催化剂在丙烷CO2-ODH和DRP反应中的性能、机理和特性比较分析：以Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2为重点的实验和理论研究

Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2分别是丙烷CO2-ODH（氧化脱氢）和DRP（干重整）反应中有前景的双金属催化剂。这些催化剂具有不同的组成、选择性、稳定性和反应机理，通过实验和理论研究已经得到阐明。

组成和结构：
Fe3Ni1/CeO2由氧化态Fe和金属态Ni组成，而Ni3Pt1/CeO2含有金属态Ni和Pt，两者均负载在CeO2上[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。原位XANES测量证实，在反应条件下，Fe3Ni1/CeO2中的Fe以氧化态存在，而两种催化剂中的Ni和Ni3Pt1/CeO2中的Pt保持金属态[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。

选择性和活性：
Fe3Ni1/CeO2通过CO2-ODHP途径展现出对丙烯生成的高选择性（58.2%）[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。相比之下，Ni3Pt1/CeO2主要进行DRP反应，对CO的生成具有高选择性（88-96%）[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w] [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。

稳定性：
Ni3Pt1/CeO2相比单金属Ni3和其他含Fe3和Co3的贵金属对应物表现出更高的活性和稳定性[10.1002/aic.16670]。Fe3Ni1/CeO2也表现出良好的稳定性，能够维持稳态性能[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。

反应机理：
DFT研究表明，Fe3Ni1/CeO2倾向于C-H键断裂，促进CO2-ODHP途径，而Ni3Pt1/CeO2更倾向于C-C键断裂，有利于DRP途径[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。在反应条件下，Fe3Ni1/CeO2中氧化态Fe的存在是促进氧化性C-H键断裂的关键[10.1002/aic.16670]。

协同效应：
双金属Fe3Ni1/CeO2催化剂表现出协同效应，其丙烯产率（1.6%）高于单金属对应物（Ni：0.4%，Fe：0.2%）[10.1038/s41467-018-03793-w]。这种协同作用归因于催化剂中金属态Ni和氧化态Fe的独特组合。

理论见解：
DFT计算表明，在存在氧（来自CO2解离）的情况下，由于Fe-O键比Ni-O键更强，Fe会偏析到Fe3Ni1/CeO2表面[10.1002/aic.16670]。这种表面偏析在促进CO2-ODHP途径中起着关键作用。对于Ni3Pt1/CeO2，较高的Ni含量可能促进金属Ni颗粒的形成，有利于DRP反应。

环境影响：
两种催化剂都为CO2利用提供了有前景的途径，解决了全球烯烃生产和CO2减排的问题[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_3]。特别是Fe3Ni1/CeO2在CO2-ODH反应中表现出潜力，可能有助于在消耗CO2的同时生产有价值的烯烃。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Ni3Pt1/CeO2催化剂的比较分析揭示了组成、结构和反应条件在决定CO2利用反应的催化剂性能中的关键作用。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 催化剂设计优化：细调金属比例和氧化态，以提高选择性和稳定性。
2. 深入的机理研究：利用先进的原位表征技术进一步阐明反应途径和活性位点。
3. 可扩展性和工业应用：研究这些催化体系在工业丙烯生产和CO2减排中的扩大应用可行性。
4. 新型双金属组合：探索其他过渡金属组合，开发更高效、更具成本效益的CO2-ODH和DRP反应催化剂。
5. 计算筛选：采用机器学习和高通量计算方法预测和设计性能更优的新型双金属催化剂。

这些进展可能为化工行业开辟更可持续和经济可行的工艺路线，同时解决烯烃需求增长和CO2减排的迫切需求。
