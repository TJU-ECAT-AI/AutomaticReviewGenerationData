<English>
Zeolites are promising support materials for CO2-ODHP catalysts due to their unique properties, including tunable acidity, high surface area, thermal stability, and diverse pore structures. These characteristics make zeolites highly versatile and adaptable for catalytic applications in propane dehydrogenation reactions.

Key properties that make zeolites suitable for CO2-ODHP catalysts include:

1. Adjustable Si/Al ratio: This allows for fine-tuning of the catalyst's acidity, which is crucial for optimizing catalytic performance [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
2. Three-dimensional micropore structure: Zeolites like ZSM-5 have a tri-dimensional micropore structure that provides a stable framework for active metal species and allows efficient mass transfer of reactants and products [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].
3. High thermal and hydrothermal stability: This property ensures catalyst longevity under harsh reaction conditions [10.1007/s10562-011-0566-6].
4. Large specific surface area: This provides ample sites for dispersing active metal oxides like Ga2O3 or ZnO [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].
5. Medium pore size and channel structure: These features make zeolites resistant to coking, a common issue in dehydrogenation reactions [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].

Acidity modification techniques play a critical role in optimizing the catalytic performance of zeolite-supported catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions. The main strategies include:

1. Dealumination: This process removes aluminum from the zeolite framework, reducing Brønsted acidity. Methods include:
   a. Steaming treatment: Exposing the zeolite to high-temperature steam (600-800°C) [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
   b. Acid washing: Treatment with mineral acids like HNO3 to further remove aluminum [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].

2. Si/Al ratio modification: Increasing the Si/Al ratio of the zeolite support effectively reduces both Brønsted and Lewis acid sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

3. Incorporation of other elements: For example, introducing phosphorus into HZSM-5 can displace strong Brønsted acid sites with weaker ones and improve hydrothermal stability [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5].

These modifications contribute to optimizing catalytic performance by:

1. Suppressing side reactions: Reducing the number and strength of acid sites inhibits unwanted reactions like oligomerization, cyclization, and cracking, leading to improved propene selectivity [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].

2. Enhancing stability: Decreased acidity leads to reduced coke formation, resulting in superior long-term stability of the catalyst [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

3. Balancing activity and selectivity: While reducing acidity generally decreases initial catalytic activity, it often improves selectivity and long-term stability [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].

4. Optimizing metal-support interactions: Acidity modifications can affect the dispersion and interaction of active metal species with the zeolite support [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5].

Key considerations in selecting and implementing these strategies include:

1. Zeolite type: Different zeolites (e.g., ZSM-5, ZSM-48, Beta) respond differently to modifications and exhibit varied catalytic behaviors [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].

2. Optimal acidity level: Finding the right balance of acid site number and strength is crucial for maximizing performance. For example, ZnO/HZSM-5 with a Si/Al ratio of 160 showed optimal performance in one study [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

3. Characterization techniques: Employing methods like NH3-TPD and FT-IR spectroscopy of adsorbed pyridine is essential for understanding the acidic properties of modified zeolites [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

4. Preservation of zeolite structure: Modifications should maintain the beneficial properties of the zeolite, such as its pore structure and surface area [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

5. Reaction conditions: The chosen modification strategy should produce a catalyst stable under the intended reaction environment, including the presence of CO2 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The optimization of zeolite-supported catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions represents a complex interplay between acidity modification, metal-support interactions, and reaction conditions. Future research directions may focus on:

1. Advanced characterization techniques: Developing in-situ and operando methods to better understand the dynamic changes in catalyst acidity and structure during CO2-ODHP reactions.

2. Rational design of hierarchical zeolites: Combining micro-, meso-, and macropores to enhance mass transfer while maintaining the beneficial acidity properties of zeolites.

3. Novel acidity modification strategies: Exploring new methods to fine-tune zeolite acidity, such as controlled incorporation of heteroatoms or post-synthetic treatments.

4. Synergistic effects of multiple modifications: Investigating the combined impact of acidity modification, metal loading, and promoter addition to develop highly efficient and stable CO2-ODHP catalysts.

5. Computational modeling: Utilizing advanced simulation techniques to predict optimal zeolite structures and acidity profiles for specific CO2-ODHP reaction conditions.

6. Green synthesis routes: Developing environmentally friendly methods for zeolite synthesis and modification to align with sustainability goals in catalyst production.

7. Integration with process intensification: Exploring how optimized zeolite-supported catalysts can be incorporated into advanced reactor designs or membrane systems for enhanced CO2-ODHP performance.

By addressing these research directions, the scientific community can work towards developing next-generation zeolite-supported catalysts that offer superior activity, selectivity, and stability for CO2-ODHP reactions, potentially revolutionizing propylene production processes.

<Chinese>
沸石作为CO2-ODHP催化剂的支撑材料具有很大的潜力，这源于其独特的性质，包括可调控的酸性、高比表面积、热稳定性和多样化的孔结构。这些特性使得沸石在丙烷脱氢反应的催化应用中具有高度的通用性和适应性。

使沸石适合CO2-ODHP催化剂的关键特性包括：

1. 可调节的Si/Al比：这允许对催化剂的酸性进行精细调节，这对优化催化性能至关重要[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
2. 三维微孔结构：像ZSM-5这样的沸石具有三维微孔结构，为活性金属物种提供稳定的框架，并允许反应物和产物的有效传质[10.1002/cjoc.201100379]。
3. 高热稳定性和水热稳定性：这一特性确保催化剂在苛刻的反应条件下具有长寿命[10.1007/s10562-011-0566-6]。
4. 大比表面积：这为分散Ga2O3或ZnO等活性金属氧化物提供了充足的位点[10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。
5. 中等孔径和通道结构：这些特征使沸石具有抗结焦性，这是脱氢反应中常见的问题[10.1002/cjoc.201100379]。

酸性修饰技术在优化CO2-ODHP反应的沸石支撑催化剂的催化性能方面起着关键作用。主要策略包括：

1. 脱铝：这一过程从沸石框架中去除铝，降低Brønsted酸性。方法包括：
   a. 蒸汽处理：将沸石暴露在高温蒸汽中（600-800°C）[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
   b. 酸洗：用HNO3等无机酸处理，进一步去除铝[10.1002/cjoc.201100379]。

2. Si/Al比修饰：增加沸石支撑体的Si/Al比可有效减少Brønsted和Lewis酸位[10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

3. 其他元素的引入：例如，将磷引入HZSM-5可以用较弱的Brønsted酸位取代强Brønsted酸位，并提高水热稳定性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5]。

这些修饰通过以下方式优化催化性能：

1. 抑制副反应：减少酸位数量和强度可抑制不需要的反应，如齐聚、环化和裂解，从而提高丙烯选择性[10.1002/cjoc.201100379]。

2. 提高稳定性：酸性降低导致结焦减少，从而提高催化剂的长期稳定性[10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

3. 平衡活性和选择性：虽然降低酸性通常会降低初始催化活性，但往往会提高选择性和长期稳定性[10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134]。

4. 优化金属-载体相互作用：酸性修饰可影响活性金属物种与沸石载体的分散和相互作用[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5]。

选择和实施这些策略时的关键考虑因素包括：

1. 沸石类型：不同的沸石（如ZSM-5、ZSM-48、Beta）对修饰的反应不同，表现出不同的催化行为[10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134]。

2. 最佳酸性水平：找到酸位数量和强度的正确平衡对于最大化性能至关重要。例如，在一项研究中，Si/Al比为160的ZnO/HZSM-5显示出最佳性能[10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

3. 表征技术：采用NH3-TPD和吸附吡啶的FT-IR光谱等方法对于理解改性沸石的酸性特性至关重要[10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

4. 保持沸石结构：修饰应保持沸石的有益特性，如其孔结构和表面积[10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

5. 反应条件：选择的修饰策略应产生在预期反应环境下稳定的催化剂，包括CO2的存在[10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

综合理解与未来展望：CO2-ODHP反应的沸石支撑催化剂优化代表了酸性修饰、金属-载体相互作用和反应条件之间的复杂相互作用。未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 先进表征技术：开发原位和操作中方法，以更好地理解CO2-ODHP反应过程中催化剂酸性和结构的动态变化。

2. 分级沸石的理性设计：结合微孔、介孔和大孔，以增强传质同时保持沸石的有益酸性特性。

3. 新型酸性修饰策略：探索新方法来精细调节沸石酸性，如杂原子的可控引入或后合成处理。

4. 多重修饰的协同效应：研究酸性修饰、金属负载和促进剂添加的综合影响，以开发高效稳定的CO2-ODHP催化剂。

5. 计算机模拟：利用先进的模拟技术预测特定CO2-ODHP反应条件下的最佳沸石结构和酸性分布。

6. 绿色合成路线：开发环境友好的沸石合成和修饰方法，以符合催化剂生产的可持续性目标。

7. 与过程强化的结合：探索如何将优化的沸石支撑催化剂整合到先进的反应器设计或膜系统中，以增强CO2-ODHP性能。

通过解决这些研究方向，科学界可以致力于开发下一代沸石支撑催化剂，为CO2-ODHP反应提供更高的活性、选择性和稳定性，有可能彻底改变丙烯生产过程。
