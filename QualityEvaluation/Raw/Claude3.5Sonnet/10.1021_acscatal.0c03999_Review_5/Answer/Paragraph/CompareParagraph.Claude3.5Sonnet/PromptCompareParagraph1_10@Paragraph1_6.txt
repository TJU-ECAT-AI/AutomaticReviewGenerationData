Zeolites have emerged as promising support materials for CO2-ODHP catalysts due to their unique properties and tunable characteristics. The key attributes that make zeolites suitable for this application include:

1. Adjustable acidity: The Si/Al ratio can be modified to control the number and strength of acid sites, crucial for optimizing catalytic performance [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
2. High thermal and hydrothermal stability: This enables zeolites to withstand harsh reaction conditions and modification processes [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
3. Large surface area and microporosity: These features provide ample sites for dispersing active metal oxides and facilitate efficient mass transfer [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_5].
4. Diverse framework structures: With over 190 different topologies available, zeolites offer a wide range of options for tailoring catalyst properties [10.1007/s10562-011-0566-6].

Acidity modification techniques play a critical role in optimizing the performance of zeolite-supported CO2-ODHP catalysts. The main strategies include:

1. Dealumination: This process removes aluminum from the zeolite framework, reducing Brønsted acidity. Methods include:
   a. Steaming treatment: Exposing zeolites to high-temperature steam (600-800°C) followed by acid washing [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
   b. Acid treatment: Using mineral acids like HNO3 to further remove aluminum [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].

2. Weakening methods:
   a. Increasing Si/Al ratios: This reduces both Brønsted and Lewis acid sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].
   b. Incorporating other elements: For example, introducing phosphorus into HZSM-5 to displace strong Brønsted acid sites with weaker ones [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_5].

These modifications contribute to optimizing catalytic performance by:

1. Enhancing stability: Reducing excess acidity decreases coke formation, leading to improved catalyst longevity [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
2. Improving selectivity: Decreasing acidity suppresses side reactions like oligomerization, cyclization, and cracking, favoring propene formation [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].
3. Balancing activity and selectivity: While higher Si/Al ratios may decrease initial catalytic activity, they often improve long-term stability and overall propene yield [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

Key considerations in selecting and implementing these strategies include:

1. Zeolite type: Different zeolites (e.g., ZSM-5, ZSM-48, Beta) respond differently to modifications and exhibit varied catalytic behaviors [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].
2. Optimal acidity level: Finding the right balance between activity and selectivity is crucial. For example, HZSM-5 with a Si/Al ratio of 160 provided the best performance in one study [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].
3. Preservation of zeolite structure: Modifications should maintain the beneficial properties of the zeolite, such as pore structure and surface area [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].
4. Metal-support interactions: The acidity modification should promote favorable interactions between the active metal species and the zeolite support [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].
5. Reaction conditions: The chosen modification strategy should produce a catalyst stable under the intended reaction environment, including the presence of CO2 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].
