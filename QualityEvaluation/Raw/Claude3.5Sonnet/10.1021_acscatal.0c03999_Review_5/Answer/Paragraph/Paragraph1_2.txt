<English>
Zeolites are promising support materials for CO2-ODHP catalysts due to their unique properties, including tunable acidity, high thermal and hydrothermal stability, large surface area, and diverse pore structures. These characteristics make zeolites highly versatile and adaptable for catalytic applications in propane dehydrogenation reactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5].

Key properties that make zeolites suitable for CO2-ODHP catalysts include:

1. Adjustable Si/Al ratio: This allows for fine-tuning of the catalyst's acidity, which is crucial for optimizing catalytic performance [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
2. Three-dimensional micropore structure: This provides ample sites for dispersing active metal oxides and allows for efficient mass transfer of reactants and products [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].
3. Medium pore size and channel structure: These features contribute to coking resistance, which is essential for long-term catalyst stability [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].

Acidity modification techniques play a critical role in optimizing the catalytic performance of zeolite-supported CO2-ODHP catalysts. The main strategies include:

1. Dealumination: This process involves removing aluminum from the zeolite framework, reducing the number and strength of Brønsted acid sites. Methods include:
   a. Steaming treatment: Exposing the zeolite to high-temperature steam (600-800°C) [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
   b. Acid washing: Treatment with mineral acids like HNO3 to further remove aluminum [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].

2. Weakening methods:
   a. Increasing Si/Al ratios: This reduces both Brønsted and Lewis acid sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].
   b. Incorporating other elements: For example, introducing phosphorus into HZSM-5 to displace strong Brønsted acid sites with weaker ones [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5].

These modifications contribute to optimizing catalytic performance by:

1. Enhancing stability: Reducing excess acidity decreases coke formation, leading to improved catalyst longevity [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
2. Improving selectivity: Decreasing acidity suppresses side reactions like oligomerization, cyclization, and cracking, leading to higher propene selectivity [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].
3. Balancing activity and selectivity: While reducing acidity may decrease initial catalytic activity, it often improves long-term stability and overall propene yield [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

Key considerations in selecting and implementing these strategies include:

1. Zeolite type: Different zeolites (e.g., ZSM-5, ZSM-48, Beta) respond differently to modifications and exhibit varied catalytic behaviors [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].
2. Optimal Si/Al ratio: Finding the right balance is crucial for maximizing performance. For example, ZnO/HZSM-5(160) showed the best performance in one study [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].
3. Characterization techniques: Methods like NH3-TPD and FT-IR spectroscopy of adsorbed pyridine are essential for understanding the acidic properties of modified zeolites [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].
4. Active metal interaction: The acidity modification technique should be compatible with the active metal component (e.g., Ga2O3, ZnO) and enhance its dispersion and activity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5].
5. Reaction conditions: The modified zeolite should be suitable for the specific reaction conditions of CO2-ODHP, including the presence of CO2 in the reaction mixture [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements and findings discussed, there are several promising directions for future research in zeolite-supported CO2-ODHP catalysts:

1. Hierarchical zeolites: Developing zeolites with both micro- and mesopores could further enhance mass transfer and catalyst stability while maintaining the beneficial acidity properties.

2. Novel acidity modification techniques: Exploring new methods to fine-tune acidity, such as controlled incorporation of heteroatoms or post-synthesis treatments, could lead to more precise control over catalyst properties.

3. In-situ characterization: Developing advanced techniques for real-time monitoring of acidity changes during the reaction could provide valuable insights into catalyst behavior and deactivation mechanisms.

4. Computational modeling: Utilizing molecular simulations to predict the impact of acidity modifications on catalyst performance could accelerate the development of optimized catalysts.

5. Synergistic effects: Investigating the combined effects of acidity modification and active metal selection could lead to the discovery of highly efficient and stable catalyst systems.

6. Green synthesis methods: Developing environmentally friendly approaches for zeolite modification that reduce the use of harsh chemicals and energy-intensive processes.

7. Multifunctional catalysts: Exploring the integration of CO2 activation sites alongside optimized acidity could enhance the overall efficiency of the CO2-ODHP process.

These research directions have the potential to significantly advance the field of zeolite-supported CO2-ODHP catalysts, leading to more efficient and sustainable propylene production processes.

由于沸石具有可调节的酸性、高热稳定性和水热稳定性、大比表面积以及多样化的孔结构等独特性质，使其成为CO2-ODHP催化剂的理想载体材料。这些特性使沸石在丙烷脱氢反应的催化应用中具有高度的多功能性和适应性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5]。

使沸石适用于CO2-ODHP催化剂的关键性质包括：

1. 可调节的Si/Al比：这允许对催化剂的酸性进行精细调节，这对优化催化性能至关重要 [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
2. 三维微孔结构：这为分散活性金属氧化物提供了充足的位点，并允许反应物和产物的高效传质 [10.1002/cjoc.201100379]。
3. 中等孔径和通道结构：这些特征有助于抗结焦，这对催化剂的长期稳定性至关重要 [10.1002/cjoc.201100379]。

酸性改性技术在优化沸石负载的CO2-ODHP催化剂的催化性能中起着关键作用。主要策略包括：

1. 脱铝：这个过程涉及从沸石骨架中去除铝，减少Brønsted酸位的数量和强度。方法包括：
   a. 蒸汽处理：将沸石暴露在高温蒸汽中（600-800°C）[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
   b. 酸洗：用硝酸等矿酸处理，进一步去除铝 [10.1002/cjoc.201100379]。

2. 弱化方法：
   a. 增加Si/Al比：这减少了Brønsted和Lewis酸位 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。
   b. 掺入其他元素：例如，将磷引入HZSM-5以用较弱的Brønsted酸位替代强Brønsted酸位 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5]。

这些改性有助于优化催化性能：

1. 提高稳定性：减少过量酸性降低了结焦形成，从而提高催化剂的寿命 [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
2. 提高选择性：降低酸性抑制了寡聚、环化和裂解等副反应，导致更高的丙烯选择性 [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134]。
3. 平衡活性和选择性：虽然减少酸性可能降低初始催化活性，但通常会改善长期稳定性和整体丙烯产率 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

选择和实施这些策略时的关键考虑因素包括：

1. 沸石类型：不同的沸石（如ZSM-5、ZSM-48、Beta）对改性的响应不同，表现出不同的催化行为 [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134]。
2. 最佳Si/Al比：找到合适的平衡对最大化性能至关重要。例如，在一项研究中，ZnO/HZSM-5(160)表现最佳 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。
3. 表征技术：NH3-TPD和吸附吡啶的FT-IR光谱等方法对理解改性沸石的酸性质至关重要 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。
4. 活性金属相互作用：酸性改性技术应与活性金属组分（如Ga2O3、ZnO）兼容，并增强其分散性和活性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5]。
5. 反应条件：改性后的沸石应适用于CO2-ODHP的特定反应条件，包括反应混合物中CO2的存在 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

综合理解与未来展望：考虑到讨论的进展和发现，沸石负载的CO2-ODHP催化剂研究有几个有前景的方向：

1. 分级沸石：开发同时具有微孔和介孔的沸石可以进一步增强传质和催化剂稳定性，同时保持有益的酸性质。

2. 新型酸性改性技术：探索新方法来精确调节酸性，如控制掺杂异质原子或后合成处理，可能导致对催化剂性质更精确的控制。

3. 原位表征：开发用于实时监测反应过程中酸性变化的先进技术，可以为催化剂行为和失活机理提供宝贵的见解。

4. 计算机模拟：利用分子模拟来预测酸性改性对催化剂性能的影响，可以加速优化催化剂的开发。

5. 协同效应：研究酸性改性和活性金属选择的组合效应，可能导致发现高效和稳定的催化剂系统。

6. 绿色合成方法：开发环境友好的沸石改性方法，减少使用有害化学品和能源密集型过程。

7. 多功能催化剂：探索将CO2活化位点与优化的酸性相结合，可能提高CO2-ODHP过程的整体效率。

这些研究方向有潜力显著推进沸石负载的CO2-ODHP催化剂领域，导致更高效和可持续的丙烯生产过程。
