<English>
Zeolites have emerged as promising support materials for CO2-ODHP (Oxidative Dehydrogenation of Propane) catalysts due to their unique properties and tunable characteristics. This review section analyzes the properties that make zeolites suitable for this application and critically evaluates various acidity modification techniques used to optimize their catalytic performance.

Properties of Zeolites as Support Materials:

1. Tunable acidity: The Si/Al ratio can be adjusted to control the number and strength of acid sites, which is crucial for optimizing catalytic performance [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5].

2. Microporosity and high surface area: Zeolites provide large surface areas and unique micropore environments, which are ideal for dispersing active metal species and stabilizing transition metal complexes [10.1007/s10562-011-0566-6].

3. Thermal and hydrothermal stability: Zeolites exhibit excellent stability under harsh reaction conditions, making them suitable for high-temperature processes [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80992-X].

4. Diverse topologies: With over 190 different structures available, zeolites offer a wide range of framework architectures that can be tailored to specific catalytic needs [10.1007/s10562-011-0566-6].

5. Shape selectivity: The pore structure of zeolites can influence product selectivity and reduce unwanted side reactions [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].

Acidity Modification Techniques:

1. Dealumination:
   - Steaming treatment: Exposing zeolites to high-temperature steam (600-800°C) followed by acid washing removes framework aluminum, reducing the number and strength of Brønsted acid sites [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
   - Acid treatment: Washing with mineral acids like HNO3 further removes aluminum from the zeolite framework [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].

2. Increasing Si/Al ratio:
   - This method effectively reduces both Brønsted and Lewis acid sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].
   - Higher Si/Al ratios generally lead to improved stability and selectivity but may decrease initial catalytic activity [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].

3. Incorporation of other elements:
   - Introducing elements like phosphorus or boron into the zeolite framework can displace strong Brønsted acid sites with weaker ones [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5].
   - Borosilicate supports have been effective for preparing vanadium-loaded catalysts for ODH of propane [10.1007/s10562-011-0566-6].

Impact on Catalytic Performance:

1. Enhanced stability: Reducing excess acidity decreases coke formation and improves catalyst longevity [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].

2. Improved selectivity: Decreasing acidity suppresses side reactions such as cracking, oligomerization, and aromatization, leading to higher propene selectivity [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

3. Balanced activity and selectivity: While reducing acidity may decrease initial activity, it often improves long-term stability and overall propene yield [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].

4. Optimized metal-support interactions: Acidity modifications can affect the dispersion and activity of supported metal species, such as Ga2O3 or Cr [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

Key Considerations for Implementation:

1. Zeolite type: Different zeolites (e.g., ZSM-5, ZSM-48, Beta) respond differently to modifications and exhibit varied catalytic behaviors [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].

2. Initial Si/Al ratio: The starting composition of the zeolite determines the extent of modification needed [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].

3. Desired acidity level: The optimal acidity depends on the specific reaction and active metal used [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

4. Preservation of structural integrity: Modifications should maintain the zeolite's overall structure, pore system, and beneficial properties [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].

5. Characterization techniques: Proper analysis using methods like NH3-TPD and FT-IR spectroscopy is crucial for understanding the modified acidic properties [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

6. Reaction conditions: The chosen modification strategy should produce a catalyst stable under the intended reaction environment, considering factors such as the presence of CO2 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The optimization of zeolite-supported catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions represents a complex interplay between various factors, including acidity, pore structure, and metal-support interactions. While significant progress has been made in understanding the role of acidity modification in enhancing catalyst performance, several avenues for future research and development emerge:

1. Advanced characterization techniques: Developing and applying more sophisticated in-situ and operando spectroscopic methods could provide deeper insights into the dynamic changes in catalyst acidity and structure during CO2-ODHP reactions.

2. Rational design of hierarchical zeolites: Combining micropores with meso- and macropores could potentially enhance mass transfer and accessibility of active sites while maintaining the beneficial acidity control of traditional zeolites.

3. Synergistic multi-element doping: Exploring the simultaneous incorporation of multiple elements (e.g., B, P, and transition metals) into zeolite frameworks could lead to novel acidity profiles and improved catalytic properties.

4. Computational modeling and machine learning: Leveraging advanced computational techniques to predict optimal zeolite compositions and modification strategies could accelerate the discovery of high-performance CO2-ODHP catalysts.

5. Green synthesis methods: Developing more environmentally friendly approaches to zeolite synthesis and modification, such as solvent-free or low-temperature methods, could enhance the sustainability of catalyst production.

6. Integration with process intensification: Investigating the performance of optimized zeolite catalysts in novel reactor designs or membrane systems could lead to more efficient and economical CO2-ODHP processes.

7. Long-term stability studies: Conducting extended time-on-stream experiments under industrially relevant conditions will be crucial for assessing the true potential of modified zeolite catalysts for commercial applications.

By addressing these research directions, the scientific community can work towards developing next-generation zeolite-supported catalysts that offer superior performance, stability, and sustainability for CO2-ODHP reactions and beyond.

<Chinese>
沸石作为CO2-ODHP（丙烷氧化脱氢）催化剂的支撑材料展现出了巨大的潜力，这主要归功于其独特的性质和可调节的特性。本综述部分分析了使沸石适合这一应用的性质，并对用于优化其催化性能的各种酸性改性技术进行了批判性评价。

沸石作为支撑材料的特性：

1. 可调节的酸性：Si/Al比可以调整以控制酸性位点的数量和强度，这对于优化催化性能至关重要 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5]。

2. 微孔结构和高比表面积：沸石提供了大的表面积和独特的微孔环境，这对于分散活性金属物种和稳定过渡金属配合物是理想的 [10.1007/s10562-011-0566-6]。

3. 热稳定性和水热稳定性：沸石在苛刻的反应条件下表现出优异的稳定性，使其适用于高温过程 [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80992-X]。

4. 多样的拓扑结构：有超过190种不同的结构可供选择，沸石提供了广泛的框架结构，可以针对特定的催化需求进行定制 [10.1007/s10562-011-0566-6]。

5. 形状选择性：沸石的孔结构可以影响产物选择性并减少不需要的副反应 [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134]。

酸性改性技术：

1. 脱铝：
   - 蒸汽处理：将沸石暴露在高温蒸汽（600-800°C）中，然后进行酸洗，可以去除骨架铝，减少Brønsted酸性位点的数量和强度 [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
   - 酸处理：用硝酸等无机酸洗涤可进一步去除沸石骨架中的铝 [10.1002/cjoc.201100379]。

2. 增加Si/Al比：
   - 这种方法可以有效减少Brønsted酸性位点和Lewis酸性位点 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。
   - 较高的Si/Al比通常会导致稳定性和选择性的提高，但可能会降低初始催化活性 [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134]。

3. 引入其他元素：
   - 将磷或硼等元素引入沸石骨架可以用较弱的Brønsted酸性位点替代强Brønsted酸性位点 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5]。
   - 硼硅酸盐支撑体在制备用于丙烷ODH的钒负载催化剂方面表现出效果 [10.1007/s10562-011-0566-6]。

对催化性能的影响：

1. 提高稳定性：减少过量酸性可降低积碳形成并提高催化剂寿命 [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。

2. 改善选择性：降低酸性可抑制裂解、寡聚和芳构化等副反应，从而提高丙烯选择性 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

3. 平衡活性和选择性：虽然降低酸性可能会减少初始活性，但通常会改善长期稳定性和整体丙烯产率 [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134]。

4. 优化金属-载体相互作用：酸性改性可以影响负载金属物种（如Ga2O3或Cr）的分散和活性 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

实施的关键考虑因素：

1. 沸石类型：不同的沸石（如ZSM-5、ZSM-48、Beta）对改性的响应不同，表现出不同的催化行为 [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134]。

2. 初始Si/Al比：沸石的起始组成决定了所需改性的程度 [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。

3. 所需酸性水平：最佳酸性取决于特定反应和所使用的活性金属 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

4. 保持结构完整性：改性应保持沸石的整体结构、孔系统和有益特性 [10.1002/cjoc.201100379]。

5. 表征技术：使用NH3-TPD和FT-IR光谱等方法进行适当分析对于理解改性后的酸性特性至关重要 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

6. 反应条件：所选择的改性策略应产生在预期反应环境下稳定的催化剂，考虑诸如CO2存在等因素 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

综合理解与未来展望：

优化CO2-ODHP反应的沸石负载催化剂涉及酸性、孔结构和金属-载体相互作用等多个因素之间的复杂相互作用。虽然在理解酸性改性对提高催化剂性能的作用方面已取得重大进展，但仍然存在几个未来研究和发展的方向：

1. 先进表征技术：开发和应用更复杂的原位和操作条件下的光谱方法，可以深入了解CO2-ODHP反应过程中催化剂酸性和结构的动态变化。

2. 分级沸石的合理设计：将微孔与介孔和大孔相结合，可能会增强质量传递和活性位点的可及性，同时保持传统沸石的有益酸性控制。

3. 协同多元素掺杂：探索同时将多种元素（如B、P和过渡金属）引入沸石骨架，可能会产生新颖的酸性分布和改善的催化性能。

4. 计算机建模和机器学习：利用先进的计算技术预测最佳沸石组成和改性策略，可能会加速高性能CO2-ODHP催化剂的发现。

5. 绿色合成方法：开发更环保的沸石合成和改性方法，如无溶剂或低温方法，可以提高催化剂生产的可持续性。

6. 与过程强化集成：研究优化沸石催化剂在新型反应器设计或膜系统中的性能，可能会导致更高效和经济的CO2-ODHP过程。

7. 长期稳定性研究：在工业相关条件下进行长时间在线实验，对于评估改性沸石催化剂在商业应用中的真正潜力至关重要。

通过解决这些研究方向，科学界可以致力于开发下一代沸石负载催化剂，为CO2-ODHP反应及其他应用提供更卓越的性能、稳定性和可持续性。
