<English>
Zeolites are promising support materials for CO2-ODHP catalysts due to their unique properties, including tunable acidity, high thermal and hydrothermal stability, large surface area, and diverse pore structures. These characteristics make zeolites highly versatile and adaptable for catalytic applications in propane dehydrogenation reactions.

Key properties that make zeolites suitable for CO2-ODHP catalysts include:

1. Adjustable Si/Al ratio: This allows for fine-tuning of the catalyst's acidity, which is crucial for optimizing catalytic performance [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
2. Pore structure: Medium pore size zeolites like ZSM-5 and ZSM-48 can influence product selectivity and reduce side reactions such as aromatization [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].
3. High surface area: This provides ample sites for dispersing active metal oxides like Ga2O3 or ZnO [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
4. Strong hydrothermal stability: This property ensures catalyst longevity under harsh reaction conditions [10.1007/s10562-011-0566-6].

Acidity modification techniques play a critical role in optimizing catalytic performance for CO2-ODHP reactions. The main strategies include:

1. Dealumination: This process removes aluminum from the zeolite framework, reducing Brønsted acidity. Methods include:
   a. Steaming treatment: Exposing the zeolite to high-temperature steam (600-800°C) [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
   b. Acid washing: Treatment with mineral acids like HNO3 further removes aluminum from the zeolite framework [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].

2. Weakening methods:
   a. Increasing Si/Al ratios: This reduces both Brønsted and Lewis acid sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].
   b. Incorporating other elements: For example, introducing phosphorus into HZSM-5 can displace strong Brønsted acid sites with weaker ones [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5].

These modifications contribute to optimizing catalytic performance by:

1. Enhancing stability: Reducing excess acidity decreases coke formation, leading to improved catalyst longevity [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
2. Improving selectivity: Decreasing acidity suppresses side reactions like oligomerization, cyclization, and cracking, leading to higher propene selectivity [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].
3. Balancing activity and selectivity: While reducing acidity generally decreases activity, it often improves selectivity. Finding the optimal balance is key [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

Key considerations in selecting and implementing these strategies include:

1. Zeolite type: Different zeolites (e.g., ZSM-5, ZSM-48, Beta) respond differently to modifications and exhibit varied catalytic behaviors [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].
2. Desired catalytic properties: The modification strategy should align with the target reaction. For propane dehydrogenation, weak to medium acidity is generally preferred [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].
3. Initial Si/Al ratio: The starting composition of the zeolite determines the extent of modification needed [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
4. Characterization techniques: Methods like NH3-TPD and FT-IR spectroscopy of adsorbed pyridine are crucial for understanding the acidic properties of modified zeolites [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].
5. Active metal interaction: The acidity modification technique should be compatible with the active metal component (e.g., Ga2O3, ZnO) and enhance its dispersion and activity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The optimization of zeolite-supported catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions represents a complex interplay between various factors, including zeolite structure, acidity, and metal-support interactions. Future research directions may focus on:

1. Advanced characterization techniques: Developing in-situ and operando methods to better understand the dynamic changes in zeolite acidity and metal-support interactions during CO2-ODHP reactions.

2. Tailored zeolite synthesis: Designing zeolites with specific pore architectures and acidity distributions optimized for CO2-ODHP, potentially through the use of novel structure-directing agents or post-synthetic modification techniques.

3. Multifunctional catalysts: Exploring the synergistic effects of combining multiple active components (e.g., bimetallic systems) with optimized zeolite supports to enhance both activity and stability.

4. Computational modeling: Utilizing advanced simulation techniques to predict optimal zeolite properties and guide experimental design, potentially accelerating the discovery of high-performance CO2-ODHP catalysts.

5. Scale-up and process integration: Investigating the challenges associated with scaling up optimized zeolite-supported catalysts for industrial applications, including considerations of long-term stability, regeneration strategies, and integration with existing propylene production processes.

By addressing these areas, researchers can continue to push the boundaries of zeolite-supported catalyst design, potentially unlocking new levels of performance in CO2-ODHP reactions and contributing to more sustainable propylene production methods.

<Chinese>
沸石是CO2-ODHP催化剂的有前景的载体材料，这是由于它们独特的性质，包括可调的酸度、高热稳定性和水热稳定性、大比表面积以及多样化的孔结构。这些特性使沸石在丙烷脱氢反应的催化应用中具有高度的多功能性和适应性。

使沸石适合作为CO2-ODHP催化剂的关键性质包括：

1. 可调的Si/Al比：这允许对催化剂的酸度进行精细调节，这对优化催化性能至关重要 [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
2. 孔结构：像ZSM-5和ZSM-48这样的中孔沸石可以影响产物选择性并减少副反应，如芳构化 [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134]。
3. 大比表面积：这为分散活性金属氧化物（如Ga2O3或ZnO）提供了充足的位点 [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
4. 强水热稳定性：这一性质确保了催化剂在苛刻反应条件下的长期稳定性 [10.1007/s10562-011-0566-6]。

酸度修饰技术在优化CO2-ODHP反应的催化性能方面起着关键作用。主要策略包括：

1. 脱铝：这个过程从沸石骨架中移除铝，减少Brønsted酸性。方法包括：
   a. 蒸汽处理：将沸石暴露在高温蒸汽中（600-800°C）[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
   b. 酸洗：用硝酸等无机酸处理，进一步从沸石骨架中移除铝 [10.1002/cjoc.201100379]。

2. 减弱方法：
   a. 增加Si/Al比：这减少了Brønsted和Lewis酸位 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。
   b. 引入其他元素：例如，将磷引入HZSM-5可以用较弱的Brønsted酸位取代强Brønsted酸位 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5]。

这些修饰通过以下方式优化催化性能：

1. 提高稳定性：减少过量酸性降低了积碳形成，从而改善催化剂的寿命 [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
2. 提高选择性：降低酸性抑制了如聚合、环化和裂解等副反应，导致更高的丙烯选择性 [10.1002/cjoc.201100379]。
3. 平衡活性和选择性：虽然减少酸性通常会降低活性，但往往会提高选择性。找到最佳平衡是关键 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

选择和实施这些策略的关键考虑因素包括：

1. 沸石类型：不同的沸石（如ZSM-5、ZSM-48、Beta）对修饰的响应不同，表现出不同的催化行为 [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134]。
2. 所需的催化性能：修饰策略应与目标反应相一致。对于丙烷脱氢，通常首选弱到中等酸性 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。
3. 初始Si/Al比：沸石的起始组成决定了所需修饰的程度 [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
4. 表征技术：NH3-TPD和吸附吡啶的FT-IR光谱等方法对于理解修饰沸石的酸性质至关重要 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。
5. 活性金属相互作用：酸度修饰技术应与活性金属组分（如Ga2O3、ZnO）兼容，并增强其分散性和活性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5]。

综合理解与未来展望：CO2-ODHP反应中沸石载体催化剂的优化代表了各种因素之间的复杂相互作用，包括沸石结构、酸度和金属-载体相互作用。未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 先进表征技术：开发原位和操作条件下的方法，以更好地理解CO2-ODHP反应过程中沸石酸度和金属-载体相互作用的动态变化。

2. 定制沸石合成：设计具有特定孔道结构和酸度分布的沸石，专门针对CO2-ODHP反应优化，可能通过使用新型结构导向剂或后合成修饰技术。

3. 多功能催化剂：探索将多个活性组分（如双金属系统）与优化的沸石载体结合的协同效应，以提高活性和稳定性。

4. 计算机模拟：利用先进的模拟技术预测最佳沸石性质并指导实验设计，可能加速高性能CO2-ODHP催化剂的发现。

5. 放大和过程集成：研究将优化的沸石载体催化剂扩大到工业应用的挑战，包括考虑长期稳定性、再生策略以及与现有丙烯生产工艺的集成。

通过解决这些领域的问题，研究人员可以继续推动沸石载体催化剂设计的边界，可能在CO2-ODHP反应中实现新的性能水平，并为更可持续的丙烯生产方法做出贡献。
