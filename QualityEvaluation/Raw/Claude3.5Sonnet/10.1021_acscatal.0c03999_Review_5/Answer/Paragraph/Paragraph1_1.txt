<English>
Zeolites are promising support materials for CO2-ODHP catalysts due to their unique properties, including tunable acidity, high surface area, thermal stability, and diverse pore structures. These characteristics make zeolites highly versatile and adaptable for catalytic applications in propane dehydrogenation reactions.

Key properties that make zeolites suitable for CO2-ODHP catalysts include:

1. Adjustable Si/Al ratio: This allows for fine-tuning of the catalyst's acidity, which is crucial for optimizing catalytic performance [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
2. Thermal and hydrothermal stability: Zeolites can withstand high temperatures required for dealumination and reaction conditions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5].
3. High surface area: This provides ample sites for dispersing active metal oxides like Ga2O3 or ZnO [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
4. Diverse pore structures: Different zeolites offer various pore sizes and channel systems, influencing product selectivity and diffusion [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].

Acidity modification techniques play a critical role in optimizing catalytic performance for CO2-ODHP reactions. The main strategies include:

1. Dealumination: This process removes aluminum from the zeolite framework, reducing Brønsted acidity. Methods include:
   a. Steaming treatment: Exposing the zeolite to high-temperature steam (600-800°C) [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
   b. Acid washing: Treatment with mineral acids like HNO3 to further remove aluminum [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].

2. Increasing Si/Al ratio: This method effectively reduces both Brønsted and Lewis acid sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

3. Weakening acidity: Achieved by incorporating other elements (e.g., phosphorus or boron) into the zeolite framework to displace strong Brønsted acid sites with weaker ones [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5] [10.1007/s10562-011-0566-6].

These modifications contribute to optimizing catalytic performance by:

1. Enhancing stability: Reducing excess acidity decreases coke formation, leading to improved catalyst longevity [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
2. Improving selectivity: Lower acidity suppresses side reactions like oligomerization, cyclization, and cracking, leading to higher propene selectivity [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].
3. Balancing activity and selectivity: While reducing acidity generally decreases activity, it often improves selectivity. Finding the optimal balance is key [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].
4. Optimizing metal-support interactions: Acidity modifications can affect the dispersion and interaction of active metal species with the support [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

Key considerations in selecting and implementing these strategies include:

1. Zeolite type: Different zeolites (e.g., ZSM-5, ZSM-48, Beta) respond differently to modifications and exhibit varied catalytic behaviors [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].
2. Initial Si/Al ratio: The starting composition of the zeolite determines the extent of modification needed [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
3. Desired acidity level: The optimal acidity depends on the specific reaction and active metal used [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].
4. Stability requirements: More severe dealumination may be necessary for reactions requiring extended catalyst lifetime [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
5. Reaction conditions: The chosen modification strategy should produce a catalyst stable under the intended reaction environment, including the presence of CO2 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The optimization of zeolite-supported catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions represents a complex interplay between various factors, including zeolite structure, acidity, and metal-support interactions. Future research directions may focus on:

1. Advanced characterization techniques: Developing more precise methods to quantify and map acidity distribution within zeolite structures could lead to more targeted modification strategies.

2. Novel modification approaches: Exploring new techniques for acidity modification, such as controlled dealumination-realumination processes or the incorporation of multiple heteroatoms, may offer finer control over acid site distribution and strength.

3. Hierarchical zeolites: Investigating the potential of hierarchical zeolite structures, combining micropores and mesopores, could enhance mass transfer properties while maintaining the advantages of traditional zeolites.

4. In-situ modification: Developing methods for dynamic acidity adjustment during the reaction process could allow for real-time optimization of catalyst performance.

5. Computational modeling: Utilizing advanced modeling techniques to predict the optimal acidity profiles for specific reaction conditions and metal-support combinations could accelerate catalyst design and optimization.

6. Synergistic effects: Exploring the combined effects of acidity modification and metal nanoparticle engineering could lead to highly efficient and stable CO2-ODHP catalysts.

7. Sustainable synthesis: Investigating eco-friendly methods for zeolite synthesis and modification, aligning with green chemistry principles, could enhance the overall sustainability of the CO2-ODHP process.

These future directions aim to push the boundaries of zeolite-supported catalyst design, potentially leading to more efficient, stable, and selective catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions and other related processes in the petrochemical industry.

<Chinese>
沸石是CO2-ODHP催化剂的有前景的载体材料，这是由于它们独特的性质，包括可调节的酸度、高比表面积、热稳定性和多样的孔结构。这些特性使沸石在丙烷脱氢反应中的催化应用中具有高度的多功能性和适应性。

使沸石适合CO2-ODHP催化剂的关键性质包括：

1. 可调节的Si/Al比：这允许对催化剂的酸度进行精细调节，这对于优化催化性能至关重要[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
2. 热稳定性和水热稳定性：沸石能够承受脱铝和反应条件所需的高温[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5]。
3. 高比表面积：这为分散活性金属氧化物（如Ga2O3或ZnO）提供了充足的位点[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
4. 多样的孔结构：不同的沸石提供各种孔径和通道系统，影响产品选择性和扩散[10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134]。

酸度修饰技术在优化CO2-ODHP反应的催化性能方面起着关键作用。主要策略包括：

1. 脱铝：这个过程从沸石骨架中移除铝，减少Brønsted酸性。方法包括：
   a. 蒸汽处理：将沸石暴露在高温蒸汽中（600-800°C）[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
   b. 酸洗：用硝酸等无机酸处理，进一步去除铝[10.1002/cjoc.201100379]。

2. 增加Si/Al比：这种方法有效地减少了Brønsted和Lewis酸位[10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

3. 弱化酸度：通过将其他元素（如磷或硼）引入沸石骨架，用较弱的Brønsted酸位取代强Brønsted酸位来实现[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5] [10.1007/s10562-011-0566-6]。

这些修饰通过以下方式优化催化性能：

1. 提高稳定性：减少过量酸度降低了焦炭形成，从而提高了催化剂的寿命[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
2. 改善选择性：较低的酸度抑制了如齐聚、环化和裂解等副反应，导致更高的丙烯选择性[10.1002/cjoc.201100379]。
3. 平衡活性和选择性：虽然减少酸度通常会降低活性，但常常会提高选择性。找到最佳平衡是关键[10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134]。
4. 优化金属-载体相互作用：酸度修饰可以影响活性金属物种与载体的分散和相互作用[10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

选择和实施这些策略时的关键考虑因素包括：

1. 沸石类型：不同的沸石（如ZSM-5、ZSM-48、Beta）对修饰的反应不同，表现出不同的催化行为[10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134]。
2. 初始Si/Al比：沸石的起始组成决定了所需的修饰程度[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
3. 所需酸度水平：最佳酸度取决于特定反应和所用的活性金属[10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。
4. 稳定性要求：对于需要延长催化剂寿命的反应，可能需要更严格的脱铝[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
5. 反应条件：所选择的修饰策略应产生在预期反应环境（包括CO2的存在）下稳定的催化剂[10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

综合理解与未来展望：CO2-ODHP反应中沸石支持催化剂的优化代表了沸石结构、酸度和金属-载体相互作用等各种因素之间的复杂相互作用。未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 先进的表征技术：开发更精确的方法来量化和绘制沸石结构内的酸度分布，可能导致更有针对性的修饰策略。

2. 新型修饰方法：探索酸度修饰的新技术，如控制性脱铝-再铝化过程或多种杂原子的引入，可能提供对酸位分布和强度更精细的控制。

3. 分级沸石：研究结合微孔和介孔的分级沸石结构的潜力，可能在保持传统沸石优势的同时增强质量传递性能。

4. 原位修饰：开发在反应过程中动态调整酸度的方法，可能允许实时优化催化剂性能。

5. 计算机模拟：利用先进的建模技术预测特定反应条件和金属-载体组合的最佳酸度分布，可能加速催化剂设计和优化。

6. 协同效应：探索酸度修饰和金属纳米粒子工程的综合效应，可能导致高效稳定的CO2-ODHP催化剂。

7. 可持续合成：研究沸石合成和修饰的环保方法，符合绿色化学原则，可能提高CO2-ODHP过程的整体可持续性。

这些未来方向旨在推动沸石支持催化剂设计的边界，可能导致更高效、更稳定、更选择性的CO2-ODHP反应催化剂和石油化工行业其他相关过程的催化剂。
