<English>
Zeolites have emerged as promising support materials for CO2-ODHP (CO2-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane) catalysts due to their unique properties and tunable acidity. This review focuses on the optimization of zeolite-supported catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions, emphasizing properties, acidity modification techniques, and performance enhancement.

Properties of Zeolites as Catalyst Supports:

1. Tunable acidity: The Si/Al ratio can be adjusted to control the number and strength of acid sites, crucial for optimizing catalytic performance [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

2. Three-dimensional micropore structure: This provides a stable framework for active metal species and allows efficient mass transfer of reactants and products [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].

3. High thermal and hydrothermal stability: Zeolites can withstand harsh reaction conditions, ensuring catalyst longevity [10.1007/s10562-011-0566-6].

4. Large specific surface area: This facilitates high dispersion of active metal species like Ga2O3 or Cr [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

5. Shape selectivity: The pore structure of zeolites can influence product selectivity and reduce side reactions [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80992-X].

Acidity Modification Techniques:

1. Dealumination: This process removes aluminum from the zeolite framework, reducing Brønsted acidity. Methods include:
   a. Steaming treatment: Exposing the zeolite to high-temperature steam (600-800°C) [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
   b. Acid washing: Treatment with mineral acids like HNO3 further removes aluminum [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].

2. Increasing Si/Al ratio: This method effectively reduces both Brønsted and Lewis acid sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

3. Incorporation of other elements: For example, introducing phosphorus into HZSM-5 can displace strong Brønsted acid sites with weaker ones [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5].

4. Synthesis of high-silica zeolites: Directly preparing zeolites with fewer acid sites, such as ZSM-48 [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].

Performance Enhancement:

1. Improved selectivity: Reducing strong acid sites suppresses side reactions like oligomerization, cyclization, and cracking, leading to higher propene selectivity [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].

2. Enhanced stability: Fewer strong acid sites can reduce coke formation, improving catalyst longevity [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].

3. Balanced activity and selectivity: While reducing acidity generally decreases activity, it often improves selectivity. Finding the optimal balance is key [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

4. Optimized metal-support interactions: Acidity modifications can affect the dispersion and activity of supported metal species [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5].

Key Considerations:

1. Zeolite type: Different zeolites (e.g., ZSM-5, ZSM-48, Beta) respond differently to modifications and exhibit varied catalytic behaviors [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].

2. Initial Si/Al ratio: The starting composition of the zeolite determines the extent of modification needed [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].

3. Desired catalytic properties: The modification strategy should align with the target reaction. For CO2-ODHP, weak to medium acidity is generally preferred [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].

4. Preservation of zeolite structure: Modifications should maintain the beneficial properties of the zeolite, such as its pore structure and surface area [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].

5. Reaction conditions: The chosen modification strategy should produce a catalyst stable under the intended reaction environment, considering factors like CO2 presence [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The optimization of zeolite-supported catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions has shown significant progress through careful acidity modification techniques. However, there are still areas for future research and development:

1. Advanced characterization techniques: Developing more precise methods to quantify and map acid site distribution within zeolites could lead to more targeted modifications.

2. Hierarchical zeolites: Exploring the potential of hierarchical zeolites with both micro- and mesopores could enhance mass transfer while maintaining the benefits of zeolite acidity.

3. Novel zeolite structures: Investigating newly discovered zeolite frameworks or designing custom zeolites specifically for CO2-ODHP reactions could yield superior catalysts.

4. In-situ modification: Developing methods for dynamic acidity control during the reaction could allow for real-time optimization of catalyst performance.

5. Multifunctional catalysts: Combining acidity modifications with other strategies, such as incorporating promoters or creating core-shell structures, could lead to synergistic effects in catalyst performance.

6. Computational modeling: Utilizing advanced simulation techniques to predict optimal zeolite properties and modification strategies could accelerate catalyst development.

7. Sustainability considerations: Focusing on environmentally friendly modification techniques and exploring the use of renewable resources in zeolite synthesis could enhance the overall sustainability of the CO2-ODHP process.

These future directions could pave the way for more efficient, stable, and selective zeolite-supported catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions, contributing to the broader goal of sustainable chemical production.

<Chinese>
沸石作为CO2-ODHP（CO2辅助丙烷氧化脱氢）催化剂的支撑材料因其独特的性质和可调酸度而备受关注。本综述着重于CO2-ODHP反应中沸石支撑催化剂的优化，强调其性质、酸度改性技术和性能提升。

沸石作为催化剂支撑材料的特性：

1. 可调酸度：可以通过调整Si/Al比来控制酸性位点的数量和强度，这对优化催化性能至关重要 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

2. 三维微孔结构：为活性金属物种提供稳定的框架，并允许反应物和产物的高效传质 [10.1002/cjoc.201100379]。

3. 高热稳定性和水热稳定性：沸石能够承受严苛的反应条件，确保催化剂的长期使用 [10.1007/s10562-011-0566-6]。

4. 大比表面积：有利于活性金属物种如Ga2O3或Cr的高度分散 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

5. 形状选择性：沸石的孔道结构可以影响产物选择性并减少副反应 [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80992-X]。

酸度改性技术：

1. 脱铝：这一过程从沸石骨架中移除铝，减少Brønsted酸性。方法包括：
   a. 蒸汽处理：将沸石暴露在高温蒸汽中（600-800°C）[10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
   b. 酸洗：用HNO3等无机酸进一步移除铝 [10.1002/cjoc.201100379]。

2. 提高Si/Al比：这种方法可以有效减少Brønsted和Lewis酸性位点 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

3. 引入其他元素：例如，将磷引入HZSM-5可以用较弱的Brønsted酸性位点取代强Brønsted酸性位点 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5]。

4. 合成高硅沸石：直接制备含有较少酸性位点的沸石，如ZSM-48 [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134]。

性能提升：

1. 提高选择性：减少强酸性位点可抑制寡聚、环化和裂解等副反应，提高丙烯选择性 [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134]。

2. 增强稳定性：减少强酸性位点可以减少积炭，提高催化剂寿命 [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。

3. 平衡活性和选择性：虽然降低酸度通常会降低活性，但往往会提高选择性。找到最佳平衡点至关重要 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

4. 优化金属-载体相互作用：酸度改性可以影响负载金属物种的分散性和活性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5]。

关键考虑因素：

1. 沸石类型：不同的沸石（如ZSM-5、ZSM-48、Beta）对改性的响应不同，表现出不同的催化行为 [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134]。

2. 初始Si/Al比：沸石的起始组成决定了所需改性的程度 [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。

3. 目标催化性能：改性策略应与目标反应相匹配。对于CO2-ODHP，通常需要弱到中等的酸度 [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134]。

4. 保持沸石结构：改性应保持沸石的有益特性，如其孔道结构和比表面积 [10.1002/cjoc.201100379]。

5. 反应条件：选择的改性策略应产生在预期反应环境下稳定的催化剂，考虑CO2存在等因素 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：通过精心设计的酸度改性技术，CO2-ODHP反应的沸石支撑催化剂优化取得了显著进展。然而，仍有一些领域需要进行未来的研究和开发：

1. 先进表征技术：开发更精确的方法来量化和绘制沸石内酸性位点的分布，可能导致更有针对性的改性。

2. 分级孔沸石：探索同时具有微孔和介孔的分级孔沸石的潜力，可以在保持沸石酸度优势的同时提高传质效率。

3. 新型沸石结构：研究新发现的沸石骨架或专门为CO2-ODHP反应设计定制沸石，可能产生性能更优越的催化剂。

4. 原位改性：开发反应过程中动态控制酸度的方法，可以实现催化剂性能的实时优化。

5. 多功能催化剂：将酸度改性与其他策略结合，如引入助催化剂或创建核壳结构，可能在催化剂性能方面产生协同效应。

6. 计算机模拟：利用先进的模拟技术预测最佳沸石性质和改性策略，可以加速催化剂开发。

7. 可持续性考虑：关注环境友好的改性技术，探索可再生资源在沸石合成中的应用，可以提高CO2-ODHP过程的整体可持续性。

这些未来方向可能为CO2-ODHP反应开发出更高效、更稳定、更具选择性的沸石支撑催化剂铺平道路，为可持续化学品生产的更广泛目标做出贡献。
