<English>
Zeolites have emerged as promising support materials for CO2-ODHP catalysts due to their unique properties and tunable characteristics. The key attributes that make zeolites suitable for this application include:

1. Adjustable acidity: The Si/Al ratio can be modified to control the number and strength of acid sites, crucial for optimizing catalytic performance [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
2. High thermal and hydrothermal stability: This enables zeolites to withstand harsh reaction conditions and modification processes [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
3. Large surface area and microporosity: These features provide ample sites for dispersing active metal oxides and facilitate efficient mass transfer [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5].
4. Diverse framework structures: With over 190 different topologies available, zeolites offer a wide range of options for tailoring catalyst properties [10.1007/s10562-011-0566-6].

Acidity modification techniques play a critical role in optimizing the performance of zeolite-supported CO2-ODHP catalysts. The main strategies include:

1. Dealumination: This process removes aluminum from the zeolite framework, reducing Brønsted acidity. Methods include:
   a. Steaming treatment: Exposing zeolites to high-temperature steam (600-800°C) followed by acid washing [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
   b. Acid treatment: Using mineral acids like HNO3 to further remove aluminum [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].

2. Weakening methods:
   a. Increasing Si/Al ratios: This reduces both Brønsted and Lewis acid sites [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].
   b. Incorporating other elements: For example, introducing phosphorus into HZSM-5 to displace strong Brønsted acid sites with weaker ones [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5].

These modifications contribute to optimizing catalytic performance by:

1. Enhancing stability: Reducing excess acidity decreases coke formation, leading to improved catalyst longevity [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1].
2. Improving selectivity: Decreasing acidity suppresses side reactions like oligomerization, cyclization, and cracking, favoring propene formation [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].
3. Balancing activity and selectivity: While higher Si/Al ratios may decrease initial catalytic activity, they often improve long-term stability and overall propene yield [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

Key considerations in selecting and implementing these strategies include:

1. Zeolite type: Different zeolites (e.g., ZSM-5, ZSM-48, Beta) respond differently to modifications and exhibit varied catalytic behaviors [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134].
2. Optimal acidity level: Finding the right balance between activity and selectivity is crucial. For example, HZSM-5 with a Si/Al ratio of 160 provided the best performance in one study [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].
3. Preservation of zeolite structure: Modifications should maintain the beneficial properties of the zeolite, such as pore structure and surface area [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].
4. Metal-support interactions: The acidity modification should promote favorable interactions between the active metal species and the zeolite support [10.1002/cjoc.201100379].
5. Reaction conditions: The chosen modification strategy should produce a catalyst stable under the intended reaction environment, including the presence of CO2 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The optimization of zeolite-supported catalysts for CO2-ODHP reactions represents a promising avenue for improving the efficiency and sustainability of propene production. The ability to fine-tune zeolite properties through acidity modification techniques offers unprecedented control over catalyst performance. Future research directions may focus on:

1. Advanced characterization techniques: Developing more sophisticated methods to analyze the distribution and nature of acid sites in modified zeolites could lead to more precise catalyst design.

2. Computational modeling: Utilizing molecular simulations to predict the impact of various modification strategies on zeolite properties and catalytic performance could accelerate the discovery of optimal catalyst formulations.

3. Hierarchical zeolites: Exploring the potential of zeolites with both micro- and mesoporous structures to enhance mass transfer while maintaining shape selectivity.

4. Novel modification techniques: Investigating alternative methods for acidity control, such as the use of atomic layer deposition or post-synthetic grafting of functional groups.

5. In-situ regeneration: Developing strategies for continuous catalyst regeneration during the CO2-ODHP process to further improve long-term stability and industrial viability.

6. Synergistic effects: Exploring combinations of different acidity modification techniques to achieve optimal balance between activity, selectivity, and stability.

7. Green synthesis routes: Investigating more environmentally friendly methods for zeolite synthesis and modification to align with sustainable chemistry principles.

By addressing these research areas, the scientific community can work towards developing next-generation zeolite-supported catalysts that offer superior performance in CO2-ODHP reactions, potentially revolutionizing propene production and contributing to more sustainable industrial processes.

<Chinese>
沸石作为CO2-ODHP催化剂的支撑材料展现出了巨大的潜力，这主要归功于其独特的性质和可调节的特性。使沸石适合这种应用的关键属性包括：

1. 可调节的酸性：Si/Al比可以被修改以控制酸性位点的数量和强度，这对优化催化性能至关重要 [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
2. 高热稳定性和水热稳定性：这使得沸石能够承受苛刻的反应条件和改性过程 [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
3. 大比表面积和微孔结构：这些特征为分散活性金属氧化物提供了充足的位点，并促进了高效的质量传递 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5]。
4. 多样化的骨架结构：有超过190种不同的拓扑结构可供选择，沸石为定制催化剂性能提供了广泛的选择 [10.1007/s10562-011-0566-6]。

酸性改性技术在优化沸石支撑的CO2-ODHP催化剂性能方面发挥着关键作用。主要策略包括：

1. 脱铝：这个过程从沸石骨架中移除铝，降低Brønsted酸性。方法包括：
   a. 蒸汽处理：将沸石暴露在高温蒸汽中（600-800°C），然后进行酸洗 [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
   b. 酸处理：使用如HNO3等无机酸进一步去除铝 [10.1002/cjoc.201100379]。

2. 弱化方法：
   a. 增加Si/Al比：这可以减少Brønsted和Lewis酸性位点 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。
   b. 引入其他元素：例如，将磷引入HZSM-5以用较弱的Brønsted酸性位点取代强的Brønsted酸性位点 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_5]。

这些改性有助于优化催化性能，通过：

1. 提高稳定性：减少过多的酸性降低了积碳形成，从而提高催化剂的寿命 [10.1016/S0167-2991(07)80962-1]。
2. 改善选择性：降低酸性抑制了如寡聚、环化和裂解等副反应，有利于丙烯的形成 [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134]。
3. 平衡活性和选择性：虽然较高的Si/Al比可能降低初始催化活性，但通常会改善长期稳定性和整体丙烯产率 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

选择和实施这些策略时的关键考虑因素包括：

1. 沸石类型：不同的沸石（如ZSM-5、ZSM-48、Beta）对改性的响应不同，表现出不同的催化行为 [10.1016/j.jiec.2011.11.134]。
2. 最佳酸性水平：找到活性和选择性之间的正确平衡至关重要。例如，在一项研究中，Si/Al比为160的HZSM-5表现最佳 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。
3. 保持沸石结构：改性应保持沸石的有益特性，如孔结构和比表面积 [10.1002/cjoc.201100379]。
4. 金属-支撑材料相互作用：酸性改性应促进活性金属物种与沸石支撑材料之间的有利相互作用 [10.1002/cjoc.201100379]。
5. 反应条件：所选择的改性策略应产生在预期反应环境下稳定的催化剂，包括CO2的存在 [10.1016/j.cattod.2009.05.011]。

综合理解与未来展望：优化沸石支撑的CO2-ODHP反应催化剂代表了提高丙烯生产效率和可持续性的一个有前景的方向。通过酸性改性技术对沸石性能进行精细调节的能力为催化剂性能的控制提供了前所未有的可能。未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 先进表征技术：开发更复杂的方法来分析改性沸石中酸性位点的分布和性质，这可能导致更精确的催化剂设计。

2. 计算机模拟：利用分子模拟来预测各种改性策略对沸石性质和催化性能的影响，可能加速发现最佳催化剂配方。

3. 层次结构沸石：探索同时具有微孔和介孔结构的沸石的潜力，以增强质量传递同时保持形状选择性。

4. 新型改性技术：研究酸性控制的替代方法，如原子层沉积或后合成功能基团接枝。

5. 原位再生：开发CO2-ODHP过程中的连续催化剂再生策略，进一步提高长期稳定性和工业可行性。

6. 协同效应：探索不同酸性改性技术的组合，以达到活性、选择性和稳定性之间的最佳平衡。

7. 绿色合成路线：研究更环保的沸石合成和改性方法，以符合可持续化学原则。

通过解决这些研究领域，科学界可以朝着开发下一代沸石支撑催化剂的方向努力，这些催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中提供卓越的性能，有可能彻底改变丙烯生产并为更可持续的工业过程做出贡献。
