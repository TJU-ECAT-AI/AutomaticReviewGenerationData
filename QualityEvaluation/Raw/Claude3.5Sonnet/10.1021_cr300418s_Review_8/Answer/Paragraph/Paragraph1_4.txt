The complex effects of dopants on the catalytic properties of metal oxides: A comprehensive analysis of same-valence and high-valence dopants in various host systems

Same-valence dopants (SVDs) and high-valence dopants (HVDs) significantly influence the catalytic properties of metal oxides, with their effects varying depending on the nature of the host oxide and the specific dopant-oxide combination. This review synthesizes insights from computational and experimental studies to elucidate the complex interplay between dopants and host oxides in catalytic applications.

1. Effect on Oxygen Vacancy Formation Energy (OVFE):
SVDs and HVDs affect the OVFE differently, which is a crucial factor in determining catalytic activity:

a) In irreducible oxides (e.g., MgO, ZnO):
- Low-valence dopants (LVDs) generally decrease OVFE, potentially enhancing catalytic activity [10.1021/jp205432].
- HVDs typically increase OVFE, with the effect being more pronounced in less stable oxides like ZnO [10.1021/jp071671n].

b) In reducible oxides (e.g., CeO2, TiO2):
- Both SVDs and HVDs can lower OVFE, but the effects are more complex and less predictable [10.1021/jp205432].
- Some HVDs (e.g., Nb, Ta) in CeO2 can reduce Ce4+ to Ce3+, affecting the oxide's redox properties [10.1016/j.cplett.2010.04.035].

2. Mechanisms of Dopant Effects:
The influence of dopants on catalytic properties involves multiple mechanisms:

a) Electronic effects:
- In reducible oxides, HVDs can donate electrons to reduce host cations, altering the oxide's electronic structure [10.1021/jp205432].
- In irreducible oxides, HVDs may retain extra electrons, becoming strong Lewis bases [10.1021/jp301341].

b) Structural effects:
- Dopants can alter bond lengths and coordination environments, affecting oxygen binding strength [10.1021/jp205432].
- The size mismatch between dopants and host cations can create local stress, influencing catalytic activity [10.1007/s10562-010-0446-5].

3. Catalytic Performance:
The impact of doping on catalytic activity varies depending on the specific reaction and oxide-dopant combination:

a) Oxidation reactions:
- SVDs in CeO2 (e.g., Zr) can enhance CO oxidation by weakening Ce-O bonds and increasing oxygen mobility [10.1007/s10562-010-0446-5].
- HVDs in ZnO can create new reaction pathways for CO oxidation, different from the traditional Mars-van Krevelen mechanism [10.1016/j.jcat.2009.05.011].

b) Reduction reactions:
- HVDs like Nb and Ta in CeO2 can activate the surface for NO2 reduction [10.1016/j.cplett.2010.04.035].

4. Challenges in Predicting Dopant Behavior:
Several factors complicate the formulation of general predictive rules for dopant effects:

a) Host oxide nature:
- The reducibility of the host oxide significantly influences dopant behavior [10.1021/jp301341].
- The stability of the host oxide affects the magnitude of dopant-induced changes [10.1021/jp205432].

b) Dopant-oxide interactions:
- Lewis acid-base interactions between dopants, the oxide, and adsorbates play a crucial role [10.1021/jp301341].
- The formation of dopant-adsorbate complexes can alter the dopant's effect on the host oxide [10.1021/jp301341].

c) Computational limitations:
- Current density functional theory (DFT) methods may not accurately describe the electronic structure of some transition metal and rare-earth oxides [10.1007/s10562-005-0014-6].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The complex interplay between dopants and host oxides in catalytic systems presents both challenges and opportunities for future research. While significant progress has been made in understanding the effects of SVDs and HVDs on various metal oxides, several areas warrant further investigation:

1. Comprehensive dopant screening: Systematic studies across a wider range of dopants and host oxides could help establish more general predictive rules for dopant behavior.

2. Advanced computational methods: Developing and applying more accurate computational techniques, such as hybrid functionals or many-body perturbation theory, could improve the prediction of dopant effects on electronic structures and catalytic properties.

3. In situ characterization: Advanced experimental techniques for real-time observation of dopant behavior under reaction conditions could provide valuable insights into the dynamic nature of doped catalysts.

4. Machine learning approaches: Leveraging machine learning algorithms to analyze large datasets of dopant-oxide combinations and their catalytic properties could uncover hidden patterns and guide the design of more efficient catalysts.

5. Multifunctional doping strategies: Exploring co-doping or hierarchical doping approaches could lead to synergistic effects and enhanced catalytic performance across a broader range of reactions.

6. Integration with other catalytic strategies: Combining doping with other catalyst design approaches, such as nanostructuring or support engineering, could yield more versatile and efficient catalytic systems.

By addressing these research directions, the field can move towards a more comprehensive understanding of dopant effects in metal oxide catalysts, ultimately enabling the rational design of high-performance catalytic materials for various applications.

金属氧化物催化性能的复杂掺杂效应：同价和高价掺杂剂在各种宿主体系中的综合分析

同价掺杂剂（SVDs）和高价掺杂剂（HVDs）显著影响金属氧化物的催化性能，其效果因宿主氧化物的性质和特定的掺杂剂-氧化物组合而异。本综述综合了计算和实验研究的见解，阐明了掺杂剂和宿主氧化物在催化应用中的复杂相互作用。

1. 对氧空位形成能（OVFE）的影响：
SVDs和HVDs对OVFE的影响不同，这是决定催化活性的关键因素：

a) 在不可还原氧化物中（如MgO、ZnO）：
- 低价掺杂剂（LVDs）通常降低OVFE，可能增强催化活性[10.1021/jp205432]。
- HVDs通常增加OVFE，在像ZnO这样不太稳定的氧化物中效果更为显著[10.1021/jp071671n]。

b) 在可还原氧化物中（如CeO2、TiO2）：
- SVDs和HVDs都可以降低OVFE，但效果更复杂，更难预测[10.1021/jp205432]。
- 一些HVDs（如Nb、Ta）在CeO2中可以将Ce4+还原为Ce3+，影响氧化物的氧化还原性质[10.1016/j.cplett.2010.04.035]。

2. 掺杂剂效应的机制：
掺杂剂对催化性能的影响涉及多种机制：

a) 电子效应：
- 在可还原氧化物中，HVDs可以捐赠电子以还原宿主阳离子，改变氧化物的电子结构[10.1021/jp205432]。
- 在不可还原氧化物中，HVDs可能保留额外电子，成为强Lewis碱[10.1021/jp301341]。

b) 结构效应：
- 掺杂剂可以改变键长和配位环境，影响氧结合强度[10.1021/jp205432]。
- 掺杂剂与宿主阳离子之间的尺寸不匹配可以产生局部应力，影响催化活性[10.1007/s10562-010-0446-5]。

3. 催化性能：
掺杂对催化活性的影响因特定反应和氧化物-掺杂剂组合而异：

a) 氧化反应：
- CeO2中的SVDs（如Zr）可以通过弱化Ce-O键和增加氧移动性来增强CO氧化[10.1007/s10562-010-0446-5]。
- ZnO中的HVDs可以为CO氧化创造新的反应途径，不同于传统的Mars-van Krevelen机制[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.05.011]。

b) 还原反应：
- CeO2中的HVDs（如Nb和Ta）可以激活表面进行NO2还原[10.1016/j.cplett.2010.04.035]。

4. 预测掺杂剂行为的挑战：
几个因素使得制定掺杂剂效应的一般预测规则变得复杂：

a) 宿主氧化物的性质：
- 宿主氧化物的可还原性显著影响掺杂剂的行为[10.1021/jp301341]。
- 宿主氧化物的稳定性影响掺杂剂引起变化的幅度[10.1021/jp205432]。

b) 掺杂剂-氧化物相互作用：
- 掺杂剂、氧化物和吸附物之间的Lewis酸碱相互作用起着关键作用[10.1021/jp301341]。
- 掺杂剂-吸附物复合物的形成可以改变掺杂剂对宿主氧化物的影响[10.1021/jp301341]。

c) 计算限制：
- 目前的密度泛函理论（DFT）方法可能无法准确描述某些过渡金属和稀土氧化物的电子结构[10.1007/s10562-005-0014-6]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：

催化系统中掺杂剂和宿主氧化物之间的复杂相互作用为未来研究提供了挑战和机遇。虽然在理解SVDs和HVDs对各种金属氧化物的影响方面已取得重大进展，但仍有几个领域值得进一步研究：

1. 全面的掺杂剂筛选：对更广泛的掺杂剂和宿主氧化物进行系统研究，可以帮助建立更一般的掺杂剂行为预测规则。

2. 先进的计算方法：开发和应用更精确的计算技术，如杂化泛函或多体微扰理论，可以改进对掺杂剂效应的电子结构和催化性能的预测。

3. 原位表征：用于实时观察反应条件下掺杂剂行为的先进实验技术可以提供关于掺杂催化剂动态性质的宝贵见解。

4. 机器学习方法：利用机器学习算法分析大量掺杂剂-氧化物组合及其催化性能的数据集，可以揭示隐藏的模式并指导更高效催化剂的设计。

5. 多功能掺杂策略：探索共掺杂或分层掺杂方法可能导致协同效应，并在更广泛的反应范围内增强催化性能。

6. 与其他催化策略的整合：将掺杂与其他催化剂设计方法（如纳米结构化或载体工程）相结合，可能产生更多功能和更高效的催化系统。

通过解决这些研究方向，该领域可以朝着更全面理解金属氧化物催化剂中掺杂剂效应的方向发展，最终实现各种应用的高性能催化材料的理性设计。
