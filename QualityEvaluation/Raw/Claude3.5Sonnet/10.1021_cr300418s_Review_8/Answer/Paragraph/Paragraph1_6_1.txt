The complex effects of dopants on the catalytic properties of metal oxides: A comprehensive analysis of same-valence and high-valence dopants in various host systems

Same-valence dopants (SVDs) and high-valence dopants (HVDs) significantly influence the catalytic properties of metal oxides, with their effects varying depending on the nature of the host oxide and the specific dopant-oxide combination. Computational and experimental studies have provided valuable insights into these complex interactions, revealing both general trends and system-specific behaviors.

In reducible oxides like CeO2, SVDs such as Zr4+ have been shown to enhance redox performance and catalytic activity. Zr doping weakens Ce-O bonds, facilitating CO oxidation with lattice oxygen and increasing oxygen mobility [Placeholder_Of_DOI1]. This effect is attributed to the smaller size of Zr4+ compared to Ce4+, which releases stress and allows for easier formation of Ce3+ ions. Similarly, other SVDs like Ti4+ and Hf4+ in ceria have been found to lower the oxygen vacancy formation energy, enhancing CO adsorption on doped surfaces [Placeholder_Of_DOI2].

HVDs in reducible oxides exhibit more complex behavior. For instance, pentavalent dopants like Nb5+ and Ta5+ in CeO2 lose an electron to reduce a Ce4+ ion to Ce3+, affecting the oxide's redox properties [Placeholder_Of_DOI3]. This mechanism provides a controlled means of generating Ce3+ ions, which are crucial for catalytic activity, particularly in NO2 reduction [Placeholder_Of_DOI4].

In contrast, the effects of dopants on irreducible oxides like ZnO and MgO differ significantly. HVDs in ZnO, such as Sc, Ti, V, Cr, Mn, Y, Zr, Nb, Mo, Hf, Ta, and W, substantially increase the energy of oxygen vacancy formation [Placeholder_Of_DOI5]. This suggests that HVDs strengthen the oxygen-metal bond in irreducible oxides, potentially decreasing their oxidative power. However, these HVDs can also promote the adsorption of O2 on the dopant site, creating new reaction pathways for CO oxidation that differ from the traditional Mars-van Krevelen mechanism [Placeholder_Of_DOI6].

Low-valence dopants (LVDs) generally act as Lewis acids in both reducible and irreducible oxides, lowering the energy of oxygen vacancy formation and increasing the binding energy of bases to the surface [Placeholder_Of_DOI7]. This behavior can enhance catalytic activity for certain reactions, such as CO oxidation on doped TiO2 surfaces [Placeholder_Of_DOI8].

The complexity of dopant effects is further illustrated by the observation that some dopants can have opposite effects in different host oxides. For example, while HVDs increase oxygen vacancy formation energy in ZnO, they lower it in TiO2 [Placeholder_Of_DOI9]. This highlights the importance of considering the specific dopant-oxide pair when predicting catalytic behavior.

Challenges in formulating general predictive rules for dopant behavior persist due to various factors:
1. The complex interplay between electronic effects and structural changes induced by dopants.
2. The different behaviors observed in reducible vs. irreducible oxides.
3. The formation of dopant-adsorbate complexes and their influence on catalytic properties.
4. The need to consider both local and long-range effects of dopants.
5. The limitations of current computational methods in accurately describing the electronic structures of these complex systems.

Despite these challenges, some general trends have emerged. An approximate linear relationship between the energy of oxygen vacancy formation and the enthalpy of formation of the dopant's oxide has been observed, providing a potential predictive tool [Placeholder_Of_DOI10]. However, the generality of this relationship requires further investigation across a wider range of systems.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The complex effects of dopants on metal oxide catalysts highlight the need for a multifaceted approach to catalyst design and optimization. Future research directions should focus on:

1. Developing more accurate computational models that can better capture the electronic and structural complexities of doped metal oxides, particularly for systems involving charge transfer and localization.

2. Expanding experimental studies to systematically investigate a broader range of dopant-oxide combinations, including mixed-metal oxide systems and co-doping strategies.

3. Exploring the potential of machine learning algorithms to identify patterns and predict dopant effects across diverse metal oxide systems, potentially uncovering new design principles for catalyst optimization.

4. Investigating the dynamic behavior of doped catalysts under realistic reaction conditions, including the effects of gas-phase species on dopant properties and catalyst stability.

5. Developing in situ and operando characterization techniques to directly observe the local environment and electronic structure of dopants during catalytic reactions.

6. Exploring novel synthesis methods to precisely control dopant distribution and concentration in metal oxide catalysts, potentially enabling the creation of catalysts with tailored active sites.

7. Investigating the synergistic effects of multiple dopants and the potential for creating multifunctional catalysts capable of performing cascade reactions.

By addressing these research directions, we can work towards a more comprehensive understanding of dopant effects in metal oxide catalysts, ultimately enabling the rational design of more efficient and selective catalysts for a wide range of applications.

掺杂剂对金属氧化物催化性能的复杂影响：对各种宿主系统中同价和高价掺杂剂的综合分析

同价掺杂剂（SVDs）和高价掺杂剂（HVDs）显著影响金属氧化物的催化性能，其影响效果取决于宿主氧化物的性质和特定的掺杂剂-氧化物组合。计算和实验研究为这些复杂的相互作用提供了宝贵的见解，揭示了一般趋势和特定系统的行为。

在可还原氧化物如CeO2中，SVDs如Zr4+已被证明可以增强氧化还原性能和催化活性。Zr掺杂削弱了Ce-O键，促进了晶格氧与CO的氧化，并增加了氧的流动性[Placeholder_Of_DOI1]。这种效果归因于Zr4+比Ce4+更小的尺寸，释放了应力并允许更容易形成Ce3+离子。类似地，其他SVDs如Ti4+和Hf4+在氧化铈中也被发现可以降低氧空位形成能，增强掺杂表面上CO的吸附[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]。

HVDs在可还原氧化物中表现出更复杂的行为。例如，CeO2中的五价掺杂剂如Nb5+和Ta5+会失去一个电子以将Ce4+离子还原为Ce3+，影响氧化物的氧化还原性质[Placeholder_Of_DOI3]。这种机制提供了一种受控的方式来生成Ce3+离子，这对催化活性至关重要，特别是在NO2还原中[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]。

相比之下，掺杂剂对不可还原氧化物如ZnO和MgO的影响显著不同。ZnO中的HVDs，如Sc、Ti、V、Cr、Mn、Y、Zr、Nb、Mo、Hf、Ta和W，大大增加了氧空位形成能[Placeholder_Of_DOI5]。这表明HVDs增强了不可还原氧化物中的氧-金属键，可能降低了它们的氧化能力。然而，这些HVDs也可以促进O2在掺杂剂位点的吸附，为CO氧化创造了不同于传统Mars-van Krevelen机制的新反应途径[Placeholder_Of_DOI6]。

低价掺杂剂（LVDs）通常在可还原和不可还原氧化物中都表现为Lewis酸，降低氧空位形成能并增加碱性物质与表面的结合能[Placeholder_Of_DOI7]。这种行为可以增强某些反应的催化活性，例如掺杂TiO2表面上的CO氧化[Placeholder_Of_DOI8]。

掺杂剂效应的复杂性进一步由一些掺杂剂在不同宿主氧化物中可能产生相反效果的观察结果所说明。例如，虽然HVDs增加了ZnO中的氧空位形成能，但它们在TiO2中降低了这一能量[Placeholder_Of_DOI9]。这突出了在预测催化行为时考虑特定掺杂剂-氧化物对的重要性。

制定掺杂剂行为的一般预测规则仍面临诸多挑战，这些挑战源于各种因素：
1. 掺杂剂引起的电子效应和结构变化之间的复杂相互作用。
2. 可还原与不可还原氧化物中观察到的不同行为。
3. 掺杂剂-吸附质复合物的形成及其对催化性能的影响。
4. 需要同时考虑掺杂剂的局部和长程效应。
5. 当前计算方法在准确描述这些复杂系统的电子结构方面的局限性。

尽管存在这些挑战，但一些一般趋势已经显现。已观察到氧空位形成能与掺杂剂氧化物的生成焓之间存在近似线性关系，这为预测提供了潜在的工具[Placeholder_Of_DOI10]。然而，这种关系的普遍性需要在更广泛的系统中进行进一步研究。

1. 开发更精确的计算模型，能更好地捕捉掺杂金属氧化物的电子和结构复杂性，特别是涉及电荷转移和局域化的系统。

3. 探索机器学习算法在识别不同金属氧化物系统中掺杂剂效应模式和预测方面的潜力，可能发现催化剂优化的新设计原则。

4. 研究掺杂催化剂在实际反应条件下的动态行为，包括气相物种对掺杂剂性质和催化剂稳定性的影响。

5. 开发原位和操作中表征技术，直接观察催化反应过程中掺杂剂的局部环境和电子结构。

6. 探索新的合成方法，精确控制金属氧化物催化剂中掺杂剂的分布和浓度，可能实现创造具有定制活性位点的催化剂。

7. 研究多种掺杂剂的协同效应，以及创造能够执行级联反应的多功能催化剂的潜力。

通过解决这些研究方向，我们可以朝着更全面理解金属氧化物催化剂中掺杂剂效应的目标迈进，最终实现针对广泛应用的更高效、更选择性催化剂的理性设计。
