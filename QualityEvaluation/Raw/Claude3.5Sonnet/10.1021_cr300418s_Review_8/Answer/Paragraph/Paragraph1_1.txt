The complex effects of dopants on the catalytic properties of metal oxides: A comprehensive analysis of same-valence and high-valence dopants in various host systems

Same-valence dopants (SVDs) and high-valence dopants (HVDs) significantly influence the catalytic properties of metal oxides, with effects varying depending on the nature of the host oxide and the specific dopant-oxide combination. This review synthesizes findings from computational and experimental studies to elucidate the complex relationships between dopants and catalytic performance.

1. Effects on Oxygen Vacancy Formation Energy (OVFE):
SVDs and HVDs affect the OVFE differently depending on the host oxide:

a) In irreducible oxides (e.g., ZnO, MgO):
- SVDs generally increase OVFE, potentially reducing catalytic activity for oxidation reactions [10.1021/jp071671n].
- HVDs substantially increase OVFE, suggesting they strengthen the oxygen-metal bond [10.1021/jp071671n].

b) In reducible oxides (e.g., CeO2, TiO2):
- Both SVDs and HVDs often lower OVFE, enhancing catalytic activity [10.1021/jp205432].
- Some HVDs (e.g., W, Mo, Ta, Nb) in CeO2 donate electrons to reduce Ce atoms, affecting redox properties [10.1021/jp205432].

2. Influence on Catalytic Activity:
Dopants can enhance or inhibit catalytic activity through various mechanisms:

a) Oxidation reactions:
- SVDs in CeO2 (e.g., Zr) facilitate CO oxidation by weakening Ce-O bonds and increasing oxygen mobility [10.1007/s10562-010-0446-5].
- HVDs in ZnO can create new reaction pathways for CO oxidation, differing from the traditional Mars-van Krevelen mechanism [10.1016/j.jcat.2009.05.011].

b) Reduction reactions:
- HVDs (e.g., Ta, Nb) in CeO2 activate the surface for NO2 reduction by generating Ce3+ ions [10.1016/j.cplett.2010.04.035].

3. Lewis Acid-Base Interactions:
Dopants influence the Lewis acid-base properties of metal oxides:

- HVDs in irreducible oxides act as Lewis bases, strongly adsorbing Lewis acids like O2 [10.1021/jp301341].
- Low-valence dopants generally act as Lewis acids, lowering OVFE and increasing binding energy of bases to the surface [10.1021/jp301341].

4. Challenges in Formulating General Predictive Rules:
Several factors complicate the development of universal rules for dopant behavior:

a) Host oxide nature (reducible vs. irreducible) significantly affects dopant behavior [10.1021/cr300418s_8].
b) Complex interplay between electronic and structural effects induced by dopants [10.1021/jp205432].
c) Formation of dopant-adsorbate complexes can alter dopant behavior [10.1021/jp301341].
d) Lack of clear correlations between OVFE and various dopant properties (e.g., ionization energy, electronegativity) [10.1021/jp071671n].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The complex effects of dopants on metal oxide catalysts highlight the need for a more comprehensive and nuanced approach to catalyst design. Future research directions may include:

1. Development of machine learning models to predict dopant effects across a wide range of metal oxides and dopant combinations, incorporating factors such as host oxide reducibility, Lewis acid-base interactions, and dopant-adsorbate complexes.

2. Investigation of co-doping strategies to optimize catalytic performance by combining the beneficial effects of different dopant types (e.g., SVDs for thermal stability and HVDs for enhanced redox properties).

3. Exploration of novel in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic behavior of dopants under reaction conditions, including their interactions with adsorbates and changes in oxidation state.

4. Systematic studies on the long-term stability and deactivation mechanisms of doped metal oxide catalysts, focusing on the role of dopants in preventing sintering and maintaining catalytic activity over extended periods.

5. Integration of computational and experimental approaches to develop more accurate models for predicting dopant behavior, addressing the current limitations of density functional theory in describing complex oxide systems.

By pursuing these research directions, we can work towards developing more efficient and selective catalysts for a wide range of applications, from environmental remediation to sustainable energy production.

掺杂剂对金属氧化物催化性能的复杂影响：同价和高价掺杂剂在各种宿主体系中的全面分析

同价掺杂剂（SVDs）和高价掺杂剂（HVDs）显著影响金属氧化物的催化性能，其效果因宿主氧化物的性质和特定的掺杂剂-氧化物组合而异。本综述综合了计算和实验研究的结果，阐明了掺杂剂与催化性能之间的复杂关系。

1. 对氧空位形成能（OVFE）的影响：
SVDs和HVDs对OVFE的影响因宿主氧化物而异：

a) 在不可还原氧化物中（如ZnO、MgO）：
- SVDs通常会增加OVFE，可能会降低氧化反应的催化活性[10.1021/jp071671n]。
- HVDs显著增加OVFE，表明它们加强了氧-金属键[10.1021/jp071671n]。

b) 在可还原氧化物中（如CeO2、TiO2）：
- SVDs和HVDs通常都会降低OVFE，增强催化活性[10.1021/jp205432]。
- 一些HVDs（如W、Mo、Ta、Nb）在CeO2中会捐赠电子以还原Ce原子，影响氧化还原性质[10.1021/jp205432]。

2. 对催化活性的影响：
掺杂剂可以通过多种机制增强或抑制催化活性：

a) 氧化反应：
- CeO2中的SVDs（如Zr）通过削弱Ce-O键和增加氧移动性来促进CO氧化[10.1007/s10562-010-0446-5]。
- ZnO中的HVDs可以为CO氧化创造新的反应途径，不同于传统的Mars-van Krevelen机制[10.1016/j.jcat.2009.05.011]。

b) 还原反应：
- CeO2中的HVDs（如Ta、Nb）通过生成Ce3+离子激活表面进行NO2还原[10.1016/j.cplett.2010.04.035]。

3. 路易斯酸碱相互作用：
掺杂剂影响金属氧化物的路易斯酸碱性质：

- 不可还原氧化物中的HVDs作为路易斯碱，强烈吸附O2等路易斯酸[10.1021/jp301341]。
- 低价掺杂剂通常作为路易斯酸，降低OVFE并增加碱与表面的结合能[10.1021/jp301341]。

4. 制定一般预测规则的挑战：
几个因素使得开发掺杂剂行为的通用规则变得复杂：

a) 宿主氧化物的性质（可还原vs.不可还原）显著影响掺杂剂行为[10.1021/cr300418s_8]。
b) 掺杂剂引起的电子和结构效应之间的复杂相互作用[10.1021/jp205432]。
c) 掺杂剂-吸附物复合物的形成可以改变掺杂剂行为[10.1021/jp301341]。
d) OVFE与各种掺杂剂性质（如电离能、电负性）之间缺乏明确的相关性[10.1021/jp071671n]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：

掺杂剂对金属氧化物催化剂的复杂影响凸显了需要采用更全面和细致的方法来设计催化剂。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 开发机器学习模型，预测各种金属氧化物和掺杂剂组合中的掺杂剂效应，考虑宿主氧化物的可还原性、路易斯酸碱相互作用和掺杂剂-吸附物复合物等因素。

2. 研究共掺杂策略，通过结合不同类型掺杂剂的有益效果（如用于热稳定性的SVDs和用于增强氧化还原性质的HVDs）来优化催化性能。

3. 探索新型原位表征技术，以更好地理解掺杂剂在反应条件下的动态行为，包括它们与吸附物的相互作用和氧化态的变化。

4. 系统研究掺杂金属氧化物催化剂的长期稳定性和失活机制，重点关注掺杂剂在防止烧结和维持长期催化活性中的作用。

5. 整合计算和实验方法，开发更准确的掺杂剂行为预测模型，解决密度泛函理论在描述复杂氧化物系统方面的当前局限性。

通过追求这些研究方向，我们可以朝着为广泛的应用开发更高效、更选择性的催化剂迈进，从环境修复到可持续能源生产。
