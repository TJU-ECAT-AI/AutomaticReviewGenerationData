The comprehensive evaluation of metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP (oxidative dehydrogenation of propane with CO2) reveals that chromium-based catalysts are among the most promising due to their high activity and selectivity. Key findings from the literature include:

1. Catalyst Composition:
   - Chromium oxide catalysts, particularly when supported on silica-based materials (SiO2, MCM-41, SBA-15), show excellent performance [10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040].
   - Gallium oxide catalysts also demonstrate high activity, but through a different mechanism compared to chromium catalysts [10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040].

2. Support Material:
   - Silica-based supports (SiO2, MCM-41, SBA-15) generally lead to better performance for chromium catalysts [10.1016/j.cattod.2011.10.028].
   - ZrO2 supports can increase activity due to higher surface Cr5+ formation, but may lead to faster deactivation [10.1021/cr5002436].
   - Al2O3 supports often show decreased activity in the presence of CO2 [10.1016/j.cattod.2011.10.028].

3. Chromium Loading and Species Evolution:
   - Optimal chromium loading is typically in the range of 5-7 wt%, beyond which activity may decrease [10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017].
   - As loading increases, chromium species evolve from isolated Cr6+ to oligomeric species, and finally to crystalline α-Cr2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_1].
   - The presence of highly dispersed Cr6+ species is crucial for high initial activity [10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3].

4. Active Sites and Reaction Mechanism:
   - Coordinatively unsaturated Cr3+ species, formed from the reduction of Cr6+, are believed to be the main active sites [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
   - A redox cycle between Cr6+ and Cr3+ species plays a crucial role in the catalytic process [10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4].

5. CO2 Effect:
   - CO2 acts as an oxidant, enhancing propene yield and suppressing catalyst deactivation for silica-supported chromium catalysts [10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4].
   - The promoting effect of CO2 is strongly influenced by the nature of the support and chromium loading [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

6. Catalyst Deactivation and Regeneration:
   - Deactivation occurs due to coke formation and reduction of active Cr3+ to less active Cr2+ [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
   - Catalyst regeneration can be achieved using oxygen or CO2, which reoxidizes Cr3+ species to Cr6+ [10.1016/j.molcata.2004.12.006].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 
The comprehensive evaluation of metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP, particularly focusing on chromium-based catalysts, reveals a complex interplay between catalyst composition, support properties, and reaction conditions. Future research directions may include:

1. Rational design of multi-component catalysts: Combining chromium with other active metals (e.g., Ga, In) or promoters to enhance activity and stability.

2. Advanced characterization techniques: Developing in-situ and operando methods to better understand the dynamic evolution of chromium species during the reaction.

3. Support engineering: Exploring novel support materials or hierarchical structures to optimize chromium dispersion and stability.

4. Mechanistic studies: Investigating the role of CO2 in the reaction mechanism and its interaction with different chromium species.

5. Sustainable catalyst preparation: Developing environmentally friendly methods for catalyst synthesis and exploring the use of abundant, non-toxic materials as alternatives to chromium.

6. Process intensification: Integrating CO2-ODHP with other reactions or separation processes to improve overall efficiency and reduce energy consumption.

These research directions could lead to the development of more efficient and stable catalysts for CO2-ODHP, potentially enabling the industrial application of this promising technology for the valorization of CO2 and the production of valuable olefins.

<Chinese>
对CO2-ODHP（用CO2进行丙烷氧化脱氢）金属氧化物催化剂的全面评估表明，铬基催化剂由于其高活性和选择性而最具前景。文献中的主要发现包括：

1. 催化剂组成：
   - 铬氧化物催化剂，特别是当负载在基于硅的材料（SiO2, MCM-41, SBA-15）上时，表现出优异的性能[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040]。
   - 氧化镓催化剂也表现出高活性，但与铬催化剂相比机理不同[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040]。

2. 载体材料：
   - 基于硅的载体（SiO2, MCM-41, SBA-15）通常会使铬催化剂表现更好[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.10.028]。
   - ZrO2载体可以由于更高的表面Cr5+形成而增加活性，但可能导致更快的失活[10.1021/cr5002436]。
   - Al2O3载体在CO2存在下通常表现出活性降低[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.10.028]。

3. 铬负载量和物种演变：
   - 最佳铬负载量通常在5-7 wt%范围内，超过这个范围活性可能会下降[10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017]。
   - 随着负载量的增加，铬物种从孤立的Cr6+演变为寡聚物种，最后形成结晶α-Cr2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_1]。
   - 高度分散的Cr6+物种的存在对于高初始活性至关重要[10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3]。

4. 活性位点和反应机理：
   - 配位不饱和的Cr3+物种，由Cr6+还原形成，被认为是主要的活性位点[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
   - Cr6+和Cr3+物种之间的氧化还原循环在催化过程中起着关键作用[10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4]。

5. CO2效应：
   - 对于硅载体铬催化剂，CO2作为氧化剂，增强丙烯产率并抑制催化剂失活[10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4]。
   - CO2的促进作用受载体性质和铬负载量的强烈影响[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

6. 催化剂失活和再生：
   - 失活是由于焦炭形成和活性Cr3+还原为不太活性的Cr2+造成的[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
   - 催化剂再生可以使用氧气或CO2实现，这会将Cr3+物种重新氧化为Cr6+ [10.1016/j.molcata.2004.12.006]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：
对CO2-ODHP金属氧化物催化剂的全面评估，特别是对铬基催化剂的关注，揭示了催化剂组成、载体性质和反应条件之间的复杂相互作用。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 多组分催化剂的理性设计：将铬与其他活性金属（如Ga, In）或促进剂结合，以提高活性和稳定性。

2. 先进表征技术：开发原位和操作条件下的方法，以更好地理解反应过程中铬物种的动态演变。

3. 载体工程：探索新型载体材料或分级结构，以优化铬的分散性和稳定性。

4. 机理研究：研究CO2在反应机理中的作用及其与不同铬物种的相互作用。

5. 可持续催化剂制备：开发环境友好的催化剂合成方法，并探索使用丰富、无毒材料作为铬的替代品。

6. 过程强化：将CO2-ODHP与其他反应或分离过程集成，以提高整体效率并减少能源消耗。

这些研究方向可能会导致更高效、更稳定的CO2-ODHP催化剂的开发，潜在地使这种有前景的CO2增值和有价值烯烃生产技术实现工业应用。
