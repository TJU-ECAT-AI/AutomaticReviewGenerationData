The comprehensive evaluation of metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP (oxidative dehydrogenation of propane with CO2) reveals that chromium-based catalysts are among the most promising due to their high activity and selectivity. Key findings from the literature include:

1. Catalyst Composition:
   - Chromium oxide catalysts, particularly when supported on silica-based materials (SiO2, MCM-41, SBA-15), show excellent performance [10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040].
   - Gallium oxide catalysts also demonstrate high activity, but through a different mechanism compared to chromium catalysts [10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040].

2. Support Material:
   - Silica-based supports (SiO2, MCM-41, SBA-15) generally lead to better performance for chromium catalysts [10.1016/j.cattod.2011.10.028].
   - ZrO2 supports can increase activity due to higher surface Cr5+ formation, but may lead to faster deactivation [10.1021/cr5002436].
   - Al2O3 supports often show decreased activity in the presence of CO2 [10.1016/j.cattod.2011.10.028].

3. Chromium Loading and Species Evolution:
   - Optimal chromium loading is typically in the range of 5-7 wt%, beyond which activity may decrease [10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017].
   - As loading increases, chromium species evolve from isolated Cr6+ to oligomeric species, and finally to crystalline Î±-Cr2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999\_1].
   - The presence of highly dispersed Cr6+ species is crucial for high initial activity [10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3].

4. Active Sites and Reaction Mechanism:
   - Coordinatively unsaturated Cr3+ species, formed from the reduction of Cr6+, are believed to be the main active sites [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
   - A redox cycle between Cr6+ and Cr3+ species plays a crucial role in the catalytic process [10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4].

5. CO2 Effect:
   - CO2 acts as an oxidant, enhancing propene yield and suppressing catalyst deactivation for silica-supported chromium catalysts [10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4].
   - The promoting effect of CO2 is strongly influenced by the nature of the support and chromium loading [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

6. Catalyst Deactivation and Regeneration:
   - Deactivation occurs due to coke formation and reduction of active Cr3+ to less active Cr2+ [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
   - Catalyst regeneration can be achieved using oxygen or CO2, which reoxidizes Cr3+ species to Cr6+ [10.1016/j.molcata.2004.12.006].
