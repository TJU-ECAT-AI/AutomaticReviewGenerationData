<English>
The comprehensive evaluation of metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP (Carbon Dioxide-assisted Oxidative Dehydrogenation of Propane) reveals a complex interplay of factors influencing catalytic performance. Among the various catalysts studied, chromium oxide-based materials have emerged as particularly promising due to their high activity and selectivity [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

Initial screening of metal oxide catalysts showed that Cr, Ga, V, Fe, Zr, and Zn oxides exhibit activity for CO2-ODHP, with the C3H6 yield decreasing in the sequence Ga2O3 > Fe2O3 ≈ Cr2O3 ≫ Al2O3 > SiO2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_1]. However, deeper analysis revealed that chromium oxide-based catalysts, especially when supported on silica, consistently demonstrated superior performance.

Key factors influencing the catalytic performance of chromium-based catalysts include:

1. Chromium loading: The optimal loading typically ranges from 5-8 wt%, with the best results often achieved around 6-7 wt% [10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070] [10.1016/j.micromeso.2012.05.011].

2. Oxidation state and species evolution: Initially, Cr6+ species in the form of mono- and polychromates are present. During the reaction, these are reduced to Cr3+ and Cr2+ species, which are believed to be the catalytically active sites [10.1016/j.molcata.2011.08.019].

3. Dispersion: Highly dispersed chromium species show better performance than crystalline α-Cr2O3 [10.1016/j.molcata.2011.08.019].

4. Support material: SiO2-based supports, especially mesoporous materials like MCM-41, SBA-15, and MSU-x, provide better dispersion and activity compared to Al2O3 or ZrO2 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

5. Metal-support interaction: Weaker interactions, such as with SiO2, appear to benefit C3H6 formation [10.1021/cr5002436].

The evolution of chromium species as a function of loading and support properties follows a general pattern:
- At low loadings: Isolated Cr6+ species predominate
- As loading increases: Formation of oligomeric Cr6+ species
- At higher loadings: Formation of Cr2O3 crystallites, which can decrease activity [10.1021/cr5002436]

The redox cycle between Cr6+ and Cr3+ species plays a crucial role in the catalytic mechanism. CO2 acts not only as a diluent but also as an oxidant, helping to maintain the active Cr3+ state and suppress catalyst deactivation [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

Structure-activity relationships indicate that coordinatively unsaturated Cr3+ species, formed from the reduction of polymeric Cr6+, are more active than those from isolated Cr6+ [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]. The balance between different chromium species and their dispersion on the support surface is critical for optimizing catalyst performance.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive evaluation of metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP, particularly the in-depth analysis of chromium-based catalysts, provides valuable insights for future catalyst design and optimization. The complex interplay between chromium loading, oxidation state, dispersion, and support properties offers multiple avenues for further research and development.

Future directions may include:

1. Exploring novel support materials or modifications to existing supports to enhance chromium dispersion and stabilize active species.
2. Investigating the potential of bimetallic or multi-component catalysts to combine the beneficial properties of different metal oxides.
3. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic evolution of chromium species during the reaction.
4. Exploring the use of promoters or dopants to fine-tune the redox properties and stability of chromium species.
5. Investigating the potential of atomically dispersed chromium catalysts to maximize the number of active sites.
6. Developing strategies to mitigate catalyst deactivation, possibly through the design of self-regenerating catalysts.
7. Exploring the potential of machine learning and high-throughput experimentation to accelerate catalyst discovery and optimization.

These research directions could lead to the development of more efficient and stable catalysts for CO2-ODHP, contributing to the broader goal of utilizing CO2 in valuable chemical processes.

<Chinese>
对CO2-ODHP（二氧化碳辅助丙烷氧化脱氢）金属氧化物催化剂的全面评估揭示了影响催化性能的因素之间存在复杂的相互作用。在研究的各种催化剂中，基于铬氧化物的材料因其高活性和选择性而显得特别有前景 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

金属氧化物催化剂的初步筛选表明，Cr、Ga、V、Fe、Zr和Zn氧化物对CO2-ODHP表现出活性，C3H6产率按Ga2O3 > Fe2O3 ≈ Cr2O3 ≫ Al2O3 > SiO2的顺序递减 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_1]。然而，深入分析显示，铬氧化物基催化剂，特别是负载在二氧化硅上的催化剂，始终表现出优越的性能。

影响铬基催化剂催化性能的关键因素包括：

1. 铬负载量：最佳负载量通常在5-8 wt%范围内，最佳结果通常在6-7 wt%左右 [10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070] [10.1016/j.micromeso.2012.05.011]。

2. 氧化态和物种演变：最初以单铬酸盐和多铬酸盐形式存在Cr6+物种。在反应过程中，这些物种被还原为Cr3+和Cr2+物种，被认为是催化活性位点 [10.1016/j.molcata.2011.08.019]。

3. 分散度：高度分散的铬物种表现出比结晶α-Cr2O3更好的性能 [10.1016/j.molcata.2011.08.019]。

4. 载体材料：基于SiO2的载体，特别是MCM-41、SBA-15和MSU-x等介孔材料，与Al2O3或ZrO2相比提供更好的分散性和活性 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

5. 金属-载体相互作用：较弱的相互作用，如与SiO2的相互作用，似乎有利于C3H6的形成 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

铬物种随负载量和载体性质的演变遵循一般模式：
- 低负载量：以孤立的Cr6+物种为主
- 随着负载量增加：形成寡聚Cr6+物种
- 高负载量：形成Cr2O3晶体，可能降低活性 [10.1021/cr5002436]

Cr6+和Cr3+物种之间的氧化还原循环在催化机理中起着至关重要的作用。CO2不仅作为稀释剂，还作为氧化剂，有助于维持活性Cr3+状态并抑制催化剂失活 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

结构-活性关系表明，由多聚Cr6+还原形成的配位不饱和Cr3+物种比由孤立Cr6+形成的更活跃 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。不同铬物种之间的平衡及其在载体表面的分散对于优化催化剂性能至关重要。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：对CO2-ODHP金属氧化物催化剂的全面评估，特别是对铬基催化剂的深入分析，为未来催化剂设计和优化提供了宝贵的见解。铬负载量、氧化态、分散度和载体性质之间的复杂相互作用为进一步研究和开发提供了多个途径。

未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 探索新型载体材料或对现有载体进行改性，以增强铬的分散度并稳定活性物种。
2. 研究双金属或多组分催化剂的潜力，结合不同金属氧化物的有益特性。
3. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解反应过程中铬物种的动态演变。
4. 探索使用助剂或掺杂剂来微调铬物种的氧化还原性质和稳定性。
5. 研究原子分散铬催化剂的潜力，以最大化活性位点数量。
6. 开发减轻催化剂失活的策略，可能通过设计自再生催化剂。
7. 探索机器学习和高通量实验在加速催化剂发现和优化方面的潜力。

这些研究方向可能导致开发出更高效、更稳定的CO2-ODHP催化剂，为在有价值的化学过程中利用CO2的更广泛目标做出贡献。
