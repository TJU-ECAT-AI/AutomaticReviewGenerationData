<English>
Metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP (oxidative dehydrogenation of propane with CO2) have been extensively studied, with chromium and gallium oxide-based catalysts emerging as the most promising due to their high activity, selectivity, and stability [10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040]. Among these, chromium oxide-based catalysts supported on silica have shown particularly high performance [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

Key factors influencing catalytic performance of chromium-based catalysts include:

1. Chromium loading: An optimal loading range exists, typically between 5-7 wt%, beyond which activity may decrease [10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017] [10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070].

2. Oxidation state and evolution of chromium species: Initially, Cr6+ species are crucial for high catalytic activity. During the reaction, these are reduced to Cr3+ and Cr2+ species, which are the main active sites [10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3]. The redox cycle between Cr6+ and Cr3+ plays a vital role in maintaining catalyst activity [10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4].

3. Dispersion of chromium species: Highly dispersed chromium species lead to better catalytic performance. As loading increases, the evolution progresses from isolated species to oligomeric forms, and finally to crystalline α-Cr2O3, which is less active [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_1].

4. Support properties: Silica-based supports, particularly mesoporous materials like MCM-41, SBA-15, and MSU-x, provide high surface areas for better chromium dispersion [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]. The nature of the support significantly affects the catalyst's interaction with CO2, with SiO2 supports showing a promoting effect while Al2O3 supports exhibit an inhibiting effect [10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4].

5. Preparation method: Techniques like metal vapor synthesis (MVS) can produce catalysts with higher Cr dispersion, optimizing activity per Cr atom [10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070].

6. Presence of CO2: CO2 plays multiple roles, including suppressing coke formation, enhancing propylene yield by removing H2, and acting as an oxidant to maintain the active state of chromium species [10.1007/s10562-006-0077-z].

The evolution of chromium species in supported Cr2O3 catalysts as a function of loading typically follows this sequence: grafted Cr6+ → dispersed Cr6+ oxide → microcrystalline Cr3+ → crystalline α-Cr2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_1]. This evolution significantly impacts the catalyst's activity and selectivity in CO2-ODHP reactions.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive evaluation of metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP, particularly chromium-based catalysts, reveals the complex interplay between various factors affecting catalytic performance. Future research directions may focus on:

1. Developing novel support materials or modification techniques to enhance chromium dispersion and stability, potentially exploring hybrid or composite supports that combine the advantages of different materials.

2. Investigating the use of promoters or dopants to fine-tune the redox properties of chromium species and enhance their resistance to deactivation.

3. Exploring in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic evolution of chromium species under reaction conditions, which could lead to more precise catalyst design.

4. Investigating the potential of bimetallic or multi-component catalysts that combine the advantages of different active metals, such as chromium-gallium systems, to achieve synergistic effects in activity and selectivity.

5. Developing strategies for catalyst regeneration and long-term stability, which are crucial for industrial applications of CO2-ODHP processes.

6. Exploring the integration of CO2-ODHP with other processes, such as CO2 capture or utilization, to create more sustainable and economically viable technologies for propylene production.

These research directions could lead to the development of more efficient and stable catalysts for CO2-ODHP, contributing to the broader goal of sustainable chemical production and CO2 utilization.

<Chinese>
对于CO2-ODHP（与CO2共同进行的丙烷氧化脱氢）反应，金属氧化物催化剂已经被广泛研究，其中铬和镓氧化物基催化剂因其高活性、选择性和稳定性而被认为是最有前景的 [10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040]。在这些催化剂中，以二氧化硅为载体的铬氧化物基催化剂表现出特别高的性能 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

影响铬基催化剂催化性能的关键因素包括：

1. 铬负载量：存在一个最佳负载范围，通常在5-7 wt%之间，超过这个范围活性可能会降低 [10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017] [10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070]。

2. 铬物种的氧化态和演变：初始时，Cr6+物种对高催化活性至关重要。在反应过程中，这些物种被还原为Cr3+和Cr2+物种，成为主要的活性位点 [10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3]。Cr6+和Cr3+之间的氧化还原循环在维持催化剂活性方面起着至关重要的作用 [10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4]。

3. 铬物种的分散度：高度分散的铬物种导致更好的催化性能。随着负载量的增加，铬物种的演变从孤立物种到寡聚体形式，最后到结晶的α-Cr2O3，后者活性较低 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_1]。

4. 载体性质：以二氧化硅为基础的载体，特别是MCM-41、SBA-15和MSU-x等介孔材料，提供了更大的比表面积，有利于铬的更好分散 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。载体的性质显著影响催化剂与CO2的相互作用，SiO2载体显示促进效应，而Al2O3载体则表现出抑制效应 [10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4]。

5. 制备方法：诸如金属蒸气合成（MVS）等技术可以生产具有更高铬分散度的催化剂，优化每个铬原子的活性 [10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070]。

6. CO2的存在：CO2起着多重作用，包括抑制焦炭形成，通过去除H2来增强丙烯产率，并作为氧化剂维持铬物种的活性状态 [10.1007/s10562-006-0077-z]。

负载型Cr2O3催化剂中铬物种随负载量的演变通常遵循以下顺序：接枝Cr6+ → 分散Cr6+氧化物 → 微晶Cr3+ → 结晶α-Cr2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_1]。这种演变显著影响了催化剂在CO2-ODHP反应中的活性和选择性。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：对CO2-ODHP金属氧化物催化剂，特别是铬基催化剂的全面评估揭示了影响催化性能的各种因素之间的复杂相互作用。未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 开发新型载体材料或改性技术，以提高铬的分散度和稳定性，可能探索结合不同材料优势的混合或复合载体。

2. 研究使用助催化剂或掺杂剂来微调铬物种的氧化还原性质，并增强其抗失活能力。

3. 探索原位表征技术，以更好地理解反应条件下铬物种的动态演变，这可能导致更精确的催化剂设计。

4. 研究双金属或多组分催化剂的潜力，如铬-镓系统，结合不同活性金属的优势，以实现活性和选择性的协同效应。

5. 开发催化剂再生和长期稳定性策略，这对CO2-ODHP过程的工业应用至关重要。

6. 探索将CO2-ODHP与其他过程（如CO2捕获或利用）集成，以创造更可持续和经济可行的丙烯生产技术。

这些研究方向可能导致开发出更高效、更稳定的CO2-ODHP催化剂，为可持续化学品生产和CO2利用的更广泛目标做出贡献。
