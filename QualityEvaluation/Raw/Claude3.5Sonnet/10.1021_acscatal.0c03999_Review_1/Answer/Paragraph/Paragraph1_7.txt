The comprehensive evaluation of metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP reveals a complex interplay of factors influencing catalytic performance, with particular emphasis on chromium-based catalysts. Among the various metal oxides studied, including Ga, Cr, V, Fe, Zr, and Zn, chromium oxide-based catalysts have consistently shown superior activity and selectivity [10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040]. The key factors influencing catalytic performance include:

1. Support material: SiO2-supported Cr2O3 catalysts demonstrate enhanced performance with CO2, while Al2O3-supported catalysts experience reduced activity [10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4]. Mesoporous supports like MCM-41, SBA-15, and MSU-x provide high surface areas for better chromium dispersion [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

2. Chromium loading: An optimal loading range, typically between 5-7 wt%, maximizes catalytic activity [10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017]. Beyond this range, activity decreases due to the formation of less active crystalline α-Cr2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_1].

3. Oxidation state and species evolution: Initially, Cr6+ species in the form of mono- and polychromates are present. During the reaction, these are reduced to Cr3+ and Cr2+ species, which are believed to be the active sites for dehydrogenation [10.1016/j.molcata.2011.08.019]. The evolution follows the sequence: grafted Cr6+ → dispersed Cr6+ oxide → microcrystalline Cr3+ → crystalline α-Cr2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_1].

4. Dispersion: Highly dispersed chromium species show better catalytic performance than bulk crystalline phases [10.1016/j.apcata.2008.05.037].

5. Redox properties: The ability of chromium to undergo redox cycling between different oxidation states is crucial for maintaining catalytic activity [10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4].

6. CO2 interaction: CO2 plays multiple roles, including suppressing coke formation, enhancing propene yield by removing H2, and acting as an oxidant to maintain the active chromium oxidation state [10.1007/s10562-006-0077-z].

The evolution of chromium species in supported Cr2O3 catalysts is heavily influenced by loading and support properties. At low loadings, highly dispersed Cr6+ species dominate, while higher loadings lead to the formation of less active crystalline α-Cr2O3 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. The support material significantly affects this evolution, with SiO2 supports generally allowing for better dispersion and stability of active chromium species compared to Al2O3 [10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive analysis of metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP, particularly chromium-based systems, reveals the intricate balance required for optimal performance. Future research directions may focus on:

1. Developing novel support materials or modifying existing ones to enhance chromium dispersion and stability, potentially exploring hybrid or composite supports that combine the advantages of different materials.

2. Investigating the use of promoters or dopants to stabilize specific chromium oxidation states or enhance the redox cycling capabilities of the catalyst.

3. Exploring in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic evolution of chromium species under reaction conditions, potentially leading to the design of catalysts with improved stability and longevity.

4. Investigating the potential of bimetallic or multi-metallic catalysts that combine the advantages of different metal oxides, such as the high activity of chromium with the stability of other metals.

5. Developing strategies for controlled synthesis of specific chromium species or nanostructures that maximize the concentration of active sites while minimizing the formation of less active bulk phases.

6. Exploring the potential of advanced preparation methods, such as atomic layer deposition or precise ion implantation, to achieve better control over chromium species distribution and evolution.

These research directions could lead to the development of more efficient and stable catalysts for CO2-ODHP, potentially expanding the application of this process in industrial settings and contributing to more sustainable chemical production methods.

对CO2-ODHP金属氧化物催化剂的全面评估揭示了影响催化性能的因素之间复杂的相互作用，特别强调了基于铬的催化剂。在研究的各种金属氧化物中，包括Ga、Cr、V、Fe、Zr和Zn，铬氧化物基催化剂一直表现出优越的活性和选择性[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040]。影响催化性能的关键因素包括：

1. 载体材料：SiO2负载的Cr2O3催化剂在CO2存在下表现出增强的性能，而Al2O3负载的催化剂活性降低[10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4]。MCM-41、SBA-15和MSU-x等介孔载体提供了高比表面积，有利于铬的更好分散[10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

2. 铬负载量：通常在5-7 wt%之间的最佳负载范围可以最大化催化活性[10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017]。超过这个范围，由于形成活性较低的结晶α-Cr2O3，活性会下降[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_1]。

3. 氧化态和物种演变：最初，以单铬酸盐和多铬酸盐形式存在Cr6+物种。在反应过程中，这些物种被还原为Cr3+和Cr2+物种，被认为是脱氢反应的活性位点[10.1016/j.molcata.2011.08.019]。演变遵循以下顺序：接枝Cr6+ → 分散Cr6+氧化物 → 微晶Cr3+ → 结晶α-Cr2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_1]。

4. 分散度：高度分散的铬物种比体相结晶相表现出更好的催化性能[10.1016/j.apcata.2008.05.037]。

5. 氧化还原性质：铬在不同氧化态之间进行氧化还原循环的能力对维持催化活性至关重要[10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4]。

6. CO2相互作用：CO2扮演多重角色，包括抑制积碳形成，通过去除H2来增强丙烯产率，并作为氧化剂维持活性铬的氧化态[10.1007/s10562-006-0077-z]。

负载Cr2O3催化剂中铬物种的演变受到负载量和载体性质的严重影响。在低负载量下，高度分散的Cr6+物种占主导地位，而高负载量导致活性较低的结晶α-Cr2O3的形成[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。载体材料显著影响这种演变，SiO2载体通常比Al2O3允许更好的分散和活性铬物种的稳定性[10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：对CO2-ODHP金属氧化物催化剂，特别是基于铬的体系的全面分析，揭示了实现最佳性能所需的精细平衡。未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 开发新型载体材料或改进现有载体，以增强铬的分散和稳定性，可能探索结合不同材料优势的混合或复合载体。

2. 研究使用促进剂或掺杂剂来稳定特定铬氧化态或增强催化剂的氧化还原循环能力。

3. 探索原位表征技术，以更好地理解反应条件下铬物种的动态演变，可能导致设计出稳定性和寿命更长的催化剂。

4. 研究双金属或多金属催化剂的潜力，结合不同金属氧化物的优势，如铬的高活性与其他金属的稳定性。

5. 开发控制合成特定铬物种或纳米结构的策略，最大化活性位点浓度，同时最小化不活性体相的形成。

6. 探索先进制备方法的潜力，如原子层沉积或精确离子注入，以实现对铬物种分布和演变的更好控制。

这些研究方向可能导致开发出更高效、更稳定的CO2-ODHP催化剂，潜在地扩大这一过程在工业环境中的应用，并为更可持续的化学生产方法做出贡献。
