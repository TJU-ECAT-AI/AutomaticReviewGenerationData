The comprehensive evaluation of metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP reveals a complex interplay of factors influencing catalytic performance, with particular emphasis on chromium-based catalysts. Among various metal oxides tested, including Ga, V, Fe, Zr, and Zn, chromium oxide-based catalysts have consistently demonstrated superior activity and selectivity for CO2-ODHP [10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040]. The performance of these catalysts is heavily influenced by several key factors:

1. Support material: The choice of support significantly affects catalyst performance. SiO2-supported Cr2O3 catalysts show enhanced activity with CO2, while Al2O3-supported catalysts experience reduced activity [10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4]. Mesoporous silica materials like MCM-41, SBA-15, and MSU-x provide high surface areas, allowing for better chromium dispersion [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

2. Chromium loading: Optimal loading varies depending on the support, but generally falls in the range of 1.7-8.0 wt% [10.1016/j.apcata.2008.05.037]. Activity typically increases with Cr content up to a certain point, after which it plateaus or decreases due to the formation of less active crystalline Cr2O3 [10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070].

3. Oxidation state and speciation of chromium: Initially, Cr6+ species in the form of mono- and polychromates are believed to be precursors to the active sites. During the reaction, these are reduced to Cr3+ and Cr2+ species, which are considered the main catalytically active species [10.1016/j.molcata.2011.08.019].

4. Dispersion of chromium species: Higher dispersion of chromium species generally leads to better catalytic performance. The evolution of chromium species as a function of loading typically follows the sequence: grafted Cr6+ → dispersed Cr6+ oxide → microcrystalline Cr3+ → crystalline α-Cr2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_1].

5. Redox properties: The ability of chromium species to undergo redox cycling between different oxidation states (e.g., Cr6+ ↔ Cr3+) is crucial for maintaining catalyst activity [10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4].

6. Presence of CO2: CO2 plays multiple roles, acting as an oxidant, suppressing coke formation, and enhancing propene yield by removing H2 formed during dehydrogenation [10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3].

The evolution of chromium species in supported Cr2O3 catalysts is a key factor in determining their performance. At low loadings, highly dispersed Cr6+ species dominate, while at higher loadings, less active polymeric species and crystalline Cr2O3 form. The optimal catalyst typically maintains a high dispersion of active Cr species without the formation of bulk Cr2O3 phases [10.1016/j.apcata.2008.07.008].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The comprehensive analysis of metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP, particularly chromium-based systems, reveals the intricate relationship between catalyst composition, structure, and performance. This understanding opens up several avenues for future research and development:

1. Tailored support design: Given the critical role of support materials in determining catalyst performance, future research could focus on developing novel support materials with optimized porosity, surface chemistry, and stability to enhance chromium dispersion and activity.

2. Bimetallic and multi-component catalysts: While chromium-based catalysts show promising results, exploring synergistic effects between chromium and other metals (e.g., Ga, V, or noble metals) could lead to improved catalytic systems with enhanced activity, selectivity, and stability.

3. In-situ characterization techniques: Developing and applying advanced in-situ spectroscopic and microscopic techniques could provide deeper insights into the dynamic evolution of chromium species under reaction conditions, guiding the design of more efficient catalysts.

4. Computational modeling: Leveraging density functional theory (DFT) and other computational methods could help predict optimal catalyst compositions and structures, accelerating the discovery of high-performance catalysts for CO2-ODHP.

5. Process intensification: Integrating CO2-ODHP with other processes, such as CO2 capture or utilization, could lead to more efficient and sustainable industrial applications, addressing both propene production and CO2 mitigation simultaneously.

6. Alternative feedstocks: Extending the application of developed catalysts to other alkane feedstocks or exploring the potential for bio-derived alkanes could broaden the scope and sustainability of the CO2-ODHP process.

These research directions hold the potential to further optimize CO2-ODHP catalysts, paving the way for more efficient and sustainable production of valuable olefins while simultaneously addressing CO2 utilization challenges.

对CO2-ODHP金属氧化物催化剂的全面评估揭示了影响催化性能的因素之间复杂的相互作用，特别强调了基于铬的催化剂。在测试的各种金属氧化物中，包括Ga、V、Fe、Zr和Zn，铬氧化物基催化剂一直表现出CO2-ODHP反应的卓越活性和选择性[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040]。这些催化剂的性能受几个关键因素的强烈影响：

1. 载体材料：载体的选择显著影响催化剂性能。SiO2负载的Cr2O3催化剂在CO2存在下表现出增强的活性，而Al2O3负载的催化剂活性降低[10.1016/S0920-5861(98)00245-4]。MCM-41、SBA-15和MSU-x等介孔二氧化硅材料提供了高比表面积，允许更好的铬分散[10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

2. 铬负载量：最佳负载量因载体而异，但通常在1.7-8.0 wt%范围内[10.1016/j.apcata.2008.05.037]。活性通常随Cr含量增加到某个点，之后由于形成较不活跃的结晶Cr2O3而趋于平稳或下降[10.1016/j.cej.2010.11.070]。

3. 铬的氧化态和物种：最初，以单铬酸盐和多铬酸盐形式存在的Cr6+物种被认为是活性位点的前体。在反应过程中，这些物种被还原为Cr3+和Cr2+物种，被认为是主要的催化活性物种[10.1016/j.molcata.2011.08.019]。

4. 铬物种的分散度：铬物种的高度分散通常导致更好的催化性能。铬物种随负载量的演变通常遵循以下顺序：接枝Cr6+ → 分散Cr6+氧化物 → 微晶Cr3+ → 结晶α-Cr2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_1]。

5. 氧化还原性质：铬物种在不同氧化态之间进行氧化还原循环（例如，Cr6+ ↔ Cr3+）的能力对维持催化剂活性至关重要[10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4]。

6. CO2的存在：CO2扮演多重角色，作为氧化剂，抑制积碳形成，并通过去除脱氢过程中形成的H2来增强丙烯产率[10.1007/s10562-017-2282-3]。

负载型Cr2O3催化剂中铬物种的演变是决定其性能的关键因素。在低负载量时，高度分散的Cr6+物种占主导地位，而在高负载量时，形成较不活跃的聚合物种和结晶Cr2O3。最佳催化剂通常保持高度分散的活性Cr物种，而不形成体相Cr2O3 [10.1016/j.apcata.2008.07.008]。

综合理解与未来展望：

对CO2-ODHP金属氧化物催化剂，特别是基于铬的系统的全面分析，揭示了催化剂组成、结构和性能之间的复杂关系。这种理解为未来的研究和发展开辟了几个途径：

1. 定制载体设计：鉴于载体材料在决定催化剂性能方面的关键作用，未来的研究可以集中在开发具有优化孔隙度、表面化学性质和稳定性的新型载体材料，以增强铬的分散和活性。

2. 双金属和多组分催化剂：虽然基于铬的催化剂显示出有希望的结果，但探索铬与其他金属（如Ga、V或贵金属）之间的协同效应可能导致改进的催化系统，具有增强的活性、选择性和稳定性。

3. 原位表征技术：开发和应用先进的原位光谱和显微技术可能为反应条件下铬物种的动态演变提供更深入的见解，指导更高效催化剂的设计。

4. 计算模拟：利用密度泛函理论（DFT）和其他计算方法可以帮助预测最佳催化剂组成和结构，加速发现高性能CO2-ODHP催化剂。

5. 过程强化：将CO2-ODHP与其他过程（如CO2捕获或利用）结合，可能导致更高效和可持续的工业应用，同时解决丙烯生产和CO2减排问题。

6. 替代原料：将开发的催化剂扩展到其他烷烃原料，或探索生物衍生烷烃的潜力，可以拓宽CO2-ODHP过程的范围和可持续性。

这些研究方向有潜力进一步优化CO2-ODHP催化剂，为更高效和可持续地生产有价值的烯烃铺平道路，同时解决CO2利用挑战。
