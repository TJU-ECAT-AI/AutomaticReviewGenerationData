Metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP (oxidative dehydrogenation of propane) have been extensively studied, with chromium and gallium oxide-based catalysts emerging as the most promising due to their high activity, selectivity, and stability [10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040]. Among these, chromium oxide-based catalysts, particularly when supported on silica (SiO2), have shown exceptional performance.

Key factors influencing the catalytic performance of chromium-based catalysts include:

1. Chromium loading: An optimal loading range typically exists, often around 5-7 wt%, where catalytic activity reaches its peak [10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017]. For instance, Cr/MSU-x catalysts showed maximum propane conversion and propylene yield between 5-12 wt% Cr2O3 loading [10.1016/j.catcom.2006.08.005].

2. Oxidation state and speciation of chromium: Initially, Cr6+ species, present as mono- and polychromates, are believed to be precursors to the active sites. During the reaction, these are rapidly reduced to Cr3+ and Cr2+ species, which exhibit high catalytic performance [10.1016/j.molcata.2011.08.019]. The redox cycle between Cr(VI)O4 tetrahedra and Cr(III)O6 octahedra plays a crucial role in the dehydrogenation process [10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4].

3. Dispersion of chromium species: Higher dispersion generally leads to better catalytic activity. Isolated and oligomeric Cr3+ species that are coordinatively unsaturated are considered the most active sites [10.1021/cr5002436].

4. Support properties: The nature of the support significantly affects catalyst performance. SiO2-supported chromium oxide catalysts show enhanced propene yield with CO2, while Al2O3-supported catalysts experience decreased yield and selectivity [10.1007/s10562-004-3749-6]. Mesoporous supports like MCM-41, SBA-15, and MSU-x provide high surface areas for better chromium dispersion [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

5. Presence of CO2: CO2 plays multiple roles, including suppressing coke formation, enhancing propylene yield by removing H2, and acting as an oxidant to maintain the active Cr3+ oxidation state [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

The evolution of chromium species in supported Cr2O3 catalysts as a function of loading typically follows this sequence: grafted Cr6+ → dispersed Cr6+ oxide → microcrystalline Cr3+ → crystalline α-Cr2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_1]. This evolution significantly impacts catalytic performance, with the optimal catalyst having a high dispersion of Cr3+ species without the formation of bulk Cr2O3.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive evaluation of metal oxide catalysts for CO2-ODHP reveals the complex interplay between catalyst composition, support properties, and reaction conditions. While chromium-based catalysts have shown promising results, there is still room for improvement and exploration of new catalyst systems.

Future research directions may include:

1. Development of novel support materials: Exploring advanced porous materials or composite supports that can enhance chromium dispersion and stability while resisting coke formation.

2. Fine-tuning of chromium speciation: Investigating methods to control the distribution of chromium species and their oxidation states, potentially through precise synthesis techniques or the use of promoters.

3. In-situ characterization studies: Utilizing advanced spectroscopic techniques to gain real-time insights into the evolution of chromium species during the reaction, which could lead to better catalyst design.

4. Exploration of bimetallic systems: Combining chromium with other metals to create synergistic effects that enhance activity, selectivity, or stability.

5. Computational modeling: Employing density functional theory (DFT) and other computational methods to predict catalyst behavior and guide experimental design.

6. Process intensification: Investigating novel reactor designs or operating conditions that can maximize the benefits of CO2 in the reaction while minimizing its potential drawbacks.

7. Sustainability considerations: Exploring more environmentally friendly alternatives to chromium-based catalysts or developing efficient recycling methods for spent catalysts.

These research directions could lead to the development of next-generation catalysts for CO2-ODHP with improved performance, longevity, and sustainability, potentially enabling more efficient utilization of propane resources and CO2 mitigation.

<Chinese>
对于CO2-ODHP（丙烷氧化脱氢）的金属氧化物催化剂已经进行了广泛的研究，其中铬和镓基氧化物催化剂因其高活性、选择性和稳定性而成为最有前景的选择[10.1016/j.apcata.2009.11.040]。在这些催化剂中，铬基氧化物催化剂，特别是负载在二氧化硅（SiO2）上的催化剂，表现出卓越的性能。

影响铬基催化剂催化性能的关键因素包括：

1. 铬负载量：通常存在一个最佳负载范围，通常在5-7 wt%左右，此时催化活性达到峰值[10.1016/j.tca.2008.02.017]。例如，Cr/MSU-x催化剂在5-12 wt% Cr2O3负载量之间显示出最大的丙烷转化率和丙烯产率[10.1016/j.catcom.2006.08.005]。

2. 铬的氧化态和物种：初始时，以单铬酸盐和多铬酸盐形式存在的Cr6+物种被认为是活性位点的前体。在反应过程中，这些物种迅速还原为Cr3+和Cr2+物种，表现出高催化性能[10.1016/j.molcata.2011.08.019]。Cr(VI)O4四面体和Cr(III)O6八面体之间的氧化还原循环在脱氢过程中起着关键作用[10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4]。

3. 铬物种的分散度：更高的分散度通常会带来更好的催化活性。配位不饱和的孤立和寡聚Cr3+物种被认为是最活跃的位点[10.1021/cr5002436]。

4. 载体性质：载体的性质显著影响催化剂性能。SiO2负载的铬氧化物催化剂在CO2存在下表现出增强的丙烯产率，而Al2O3负载的催化剂则表现出产率和选择性下降[10.1007/s10562-004-3749-6]。MCM-41、SBA-15和MSU-x等介孔载体提供了高比表面积，有利于铬的更好分散[10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

5. CO2的存在：CO2发挥多重作用，包括抑制积碳形成，通过除去H2来增加丙烯产率，并作为氧化剂维持活性Cr3+氧化态[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

负载型Cr2O3催化剂中铬物种随负载量的演变通常遵循以下顺序：接枝Cr6+ → 分散Cr6+氧化物 → 微晶Cr3+ → 结晶α-Cr2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_1]。这种演变显著影响催化性能，最佳催化剂具有高度分散的Cr3+物种，而不形成体相Cr2O3。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：对CO2-ODHP金属氧化物催化剂的全面评估揭示了催化剂组成、载体性质和反应条件之间的复杂相互作用。虽然铬基催化剂已经显示出很有前景的结果，但仍有改进空间，并可探索新的催化剂系统。

未来研究方向可能包括：

1. 开发新型载体材料：探索先进的多孔材料或复合载体，以增强铬的分散性和稳定性，同时抵抗积碳形成。

2. 精细调控铬物种：研究控制铬物种分布及其氧化态的方法，可能通过精确的合成技术或使用助剂来实现。

3. 原位表征研究：利用先进的光谱技术实时了解反应过程中铬物种的演变，这可能导致更好的催化剂设计。

4. 探索双金属体系：将铬与其他金属结合，创造协同效应，以提高活性、选择性或稳定性。

5. 计算机模拟：采用密度泛函理论（DFT）和其他计算方法预测催化剂行为，指导实验设计。

6. 过程强化：研究新型反应器设计或操作条件，以最大化CO2在反应中的益处，同时最小化其潜在缺点。

7. 可持续性考虑：探索更环保的铬基催化剂替代品，或开发废旧催化剂的高效回收方法。

这些研究方向可能导致开发出具有改进性能、寿命和可持续性的下一代CO2-ODHP催化剂，潜在地实现丙烷资源的更高效利用和CO2减排。
