Enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems: Challenges, strategies, and the role of promoters in improving performance and selectivity

Carbon dioxide (CO2) has emerged as a promising alternative oxidant for the catalytic oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes to olefins, particularly in chromium-based catalytic systems. However, CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness present significant challenges that need to be addressed to achieve optimal catalytic performance. This review explores the strategies employed to overcome these limitations, with a focus on the role of promoters in enhancing CO2 activation, improving oxidizing ability, and achieving selective and stable catalytic performance.

Challenges of CO2 as an Oxidant:
CO2 has limited oxidizing ability due to its thermodynamically stable properties, making it challenging to reoxidize reduced CrOx species [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. The efficiency of reoxidation by CO2 is much lower than that by oxygen [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]. Additionally, CO2 is quite inert and not easily activated by CrOx [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

Strategies to Enhance CO2 Activation and Oxidizing Ability:

1. Redox Cycle Mechanism:
In CrOx/MCM-41 catalysts, a redox cycle mechanism has been proposed where hydrogen formed during propane dehydrogenation reduces Cr3+ to Cr2+, which is then reoxidized by CO2 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]. This cycle allows for indirect activation of CO2 and maintains the active state of the catalyst.

2. Support Effect:
Using supports like silica that promote deep reduction of chromium to Cr2+, especially at low chromium loadings, allows CO2 to more easily reoxidize the chromium [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]. The boundaries between Cr2O3 and SiO2 particles play an important role in the promoting effect of CO2 [10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4].

3. Low Chromium Content:
Catalysts with low chromium content promote deeper reduction to Cr2+ species, which are more easily reoxidized by CO2 than Cr3+ [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]. This is because low Cr content prevents the formation of stable Cr2O3 clusters resistant to further reduction/oxidation.

4. Introduction of Promoters:
Promoters play a crucial role in addressing the limitations of CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness. Several types of promoters have been investigated:

a) Nickel (Ni):
Ni has an excellent ability to activate CO2, even under harsh reaction conditions [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. It dissociates CO2 into CO and activated oxygen (O*ads), which can then regenerate reduced chromium species. Ni enhances the oxidizing ability of CO2, allowing it to reoxidize reduced Cr2+ to the active Cr3+ state and further to Cr6+ [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

b) Alkali Metal Oxides:
Due to their basic properties, alkali metal oxides promote CO2 adsorption and activation in CO2 hydrogenation reactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]. Potassium (K) has been identified as an effective promoter for increasing catalyst stability and selectivity [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

c) Ruthenium (Ru):
Ru is a potential promoter due to its high activity in CO2 hydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

Advantages of CO2 with Promoters over O2 Co-feeding:

1. Higher Selectivity:
CO2, being a milder oxidant than O2, shifts the oxidative dehydrogenation reaction toward more olefin production by preventing deep oxidation of reactive olefins [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]. This results in higher selectivity compared to O2 co-feeding, which can significantly decrease selectivity for propylene [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

2. Improved Stability:
Promoters like Ni and K help maintain stable catalytic activity and improve catalyst stability over longer periods [10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

3. Reduced Deactivation:
CO2 with promoters can suppress catalyst deactivation by maintaining the Cr2O3 surface in a partially oxidized state during the reaction [10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4]. This leads to more stable catalytic activity over time compared to reactions without CO2.

4. Coke Management:
While CO2 co-feeding may have both positive and negative effects on coke formation, promoters can help manage coke deposition. CO2 can inhibit coke formation and, in some cases, gasify existing coke through the reverse Boudouard reaction [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].

5. Enhanced CO2 Conversion:
Promoters like Ni demonstrate higher CO2 conversion rates, indicating more efficient use of the CO2 reactant [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

6. Stable Propylene Production:
The combination of higher CO2 conversion and maintained selectivity results in stable propylene production, which is a key goal in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) reactions [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

In conclusion, the use of promoters in conjunction with CO2 as an oxidant offers a promising approach to enhance the performance of chromium-based catalytic systems for propane dehydrogenation. By addressing the limitations of CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness, promoters enable more selective and stable catalytic performance compared to O2 co-feeding strategies.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The development of promoters for enhancing CO2 activation in chromium-based catalytic systems represents a significant advancement in the field of oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes. This approach not only addresses the challenges associated with CO2's weak oxidation potential but also offers environmental benefits by utilizing a greenhouse gas as a reactant.

Looking forward, several promising research directions emerge:

1. Synergistic Promoter Combinations: Future studies could explore the synergistic effects of combining different types of promoters (e.g., Ni with alkali metals) to further enhance CO2 activation and catalyst stability.

2. In-situ Characterization Techniques: Developing advanced in-situ characterization methods could provide deeper insights into the mechanisms of CO2 activation and the role of promoters under reaction conditions.

3. Computational Modeling: Utilizing computational modeling and machine learning approaches could help predict optimal promoter compositions and catalyst structures for specific reaction conditions.

4. Novel Support Materials: Investigating new support materials or modifications to existing supports could further enhance the interaction between CO2, promoters, and chromium active sites.

5. Process Intensification: Exploring innovative reactor designs or process configurations that maximize the benefits of CO2 with promoters could lead to more efficient and sustainable industrial processes.

6. Broadening Application Scope: Extending the use of CO2 with promoters to other dehydrogenation reactions or related processes could open up new avenues for CO2 utilization in the chemical industry.

These research directions hold the potential to not only improve the performance of chromium-based catalytic systems but also contribute to the broader goal of developing more sustainable chemical processes that effectively utilize CO2 as a valuable resource.

提高铬基催化体系中CO2的活化和氧化能力：挑战、策略以及促进剂在提高性能和选择性方面的作用

二氧化碳（CO2）已成为轻烷烃催化氧化脱氢制烯烃的一种有前景的替代氧化剂，特别是在铬基催化体系中。然而，CO2的弱氧化能力和惰性给实现最佳催化性能带来了重大挑战。本综述探讨了克服这些局限性的策略，重点关注了促进剂在增强CO2活化、改善氧化能力以及实现选择性和稳定催化性能方面的作用。

CO2作为氧化剂的挑战：
由于其热力学稳定性，CO2的氧化能力有限，使得还原态CrOx物种的再氧化变得困难 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。CO2的再氧化效率远低于氧气 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。此外，CO2相当惰性，不易被CrOx活化 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

增强CO2活化和氧化能力的策略：

1. 氧化还原循环机制：
在CrOx/MCM-41催化剂中，提出了一种氧化还原循环机制，其中丙烷脱氢过程中形成的氢气将Cr3+还原为Cr2+，然后被CO2再氧化 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。这个循环允许CO2的间接活化，并维持催化剂的活性状态。

2. 载体效应：
使用能促进铬深度还原为Cr2+的载体（如二氧化硅），特别是在低铬含量时，使CO2更容易再氧化铬 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。Cr2O3和SiO2颗粒之间的界面在CO2的促进作用中起着重要作用 [10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4]。

3. 低铬含量：
低铬含量的催化剂促进更深度的还原至Cr2+物种，这些物种比Cr3+更容易被CO2再氧化 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。这是因为低Cr含量防止了形成稳定的、抗进一步还原/氧化的Cr2O3簇。

4. 引入促进剂：
促进剂在解决CO2弱氧化能力和惰性的局限性方面起着关键作用。已研究了几种类型的促进剂：

a) 镍（Ni）：
Ni具有出色的CO2活化能力，即使在苛刻的反应条件下也是如此 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。它将CO2解离成CO和活性氧（O*ads），然后可以再生还原态铬物种。Ni增强了CO2的氧化能力，使其能够将还原态Cr2+再氧化为活性Cr3+状态，甚至进一步氧化为Cr6+ [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

b) 碱金属氧化物：
由于其碱性特性，碱金属氧化物促进了CO2在CO2加氢反应中的吸附和活化 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。钾（K）被确定为增加催化剂稳定性和选择性的有效促进剂 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

c) 钌（Ru）：
由于Ru在CO2加氢反应中具有高活性，它是一种潜在的促进剂 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

CO2与促进剂相比O2共进料的优势：

1. 更高的选择性：
作为比O2更温和的氧化剂，CO2通过防止反应性烯烃的深度氧化，将氧化脱氢反应转向更多的烯烃生成 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。这导致比O2共进料更高的选择性，后者可能显著降低丙烯的选择性 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

2. 改善的稳定性：
像Ni和K这样的促进剂有助于保持稳定的催化活性，并在更长时间内改善催化剂稳定性 [10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

3. 减少失活：
CO2与促进剂可以通过在反应过程中保持Cr2O3表面部分氧化状态来抑制催化剂失活 [10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4]。这导致与无CO2反应相比，催化活性随时间更加稳定。

4. 积碳管理：
虽然CO2共进料可能对积碳形成有正面和负面影响，但促进剂可以帮助管理积碳沉积。CO2可以抑制积碳形成，在某些情况下，通过逆布德瓦反应气化现有积碳 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。

5. 增强CO2转化：
像Ni这样的促进剂表现出更高的CO2转化率，表明CO2反应物的使用更加高效 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

6. 稳定的丙烯生产：
更高的CO2转化率和保持的选择性相结合，导致稳定的丙烯生产，这是丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）反应的一个关键目标 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

总之，将促进剂与CO2作为氧化剂结合使用为提高铬基催化体系在丙烷脱氢中的性能提供了一种有前景的方法。通过解决CO2弱氧化能力和惰性的局限性，促进剂实现了比O2共进料策略更具选择性和稳定性的催化性能。

综合理解与未来展望：

开发促进剂以增强铬基催化体系中CO2的活化代表了轻烷烃氧化脱氢领域的重大进展。这种方法不仅解决了与CO2弱氧化能力相关的挑战，还通过利用温室气体作为反应物提供了环境效益。

展望未来，几个有前景的研究方向浮现出来：

1. 协同促进剂组合：未来的研究可以探索不同类型促进剂（如Ni与碱金属）的协同效应，以进一步增强CO2活化和催化剂稳定性。

2. 原位表征技术：开发先进的原位表征方法可以在反应条件下深入了解CO2活化机理和促进剂的作用。

3. 计算模拟：利用计算模拟和机器学习方法可以帮助预测特定反应条件下的最佳促进剂组成和催化剂结构。

4. 新型载体材料：研究新的载体材料或对现有载体的改性可以进一步增强CO2、促进剂和铬活性位点之间的相互作用。
