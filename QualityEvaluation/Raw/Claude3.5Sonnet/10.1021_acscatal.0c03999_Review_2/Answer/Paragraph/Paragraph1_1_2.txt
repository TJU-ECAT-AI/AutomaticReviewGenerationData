Enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems: Challenges, strategies, and the role of promoters in improving performance and selectivity

Chromium-based catalysts have shown promise in the oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes, particularly when using CO2 as an oxidant. However, CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness pose significant challenges. This review section explores the strategies employed to overcome these limitations, with a focus on the role of promoters in enhancing CO2 activation, improving oxidizing ability, and achieving selective and stable catalytic performance.

1. Challenges of CO2 as an oxidant in chromium-based systems

CO2 has limited oxidizing ability due to its thermodynamically stable properties, making it challenging to reoxidize reduced CrOx species [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. While CO2 can participate in redox cycles of Cr2+ ⇌ Cr3+ or Cr3+ ⇌ Cr6+, its oxidation potential is rather weak, and it is not easily activated by CrOx [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

2. Strategies to enhance CO2 activation and oxidizing ability

2.1 Promoter addition

The introduction of promoters is a key strategy to facilitate CO2 activation on Cr2O3 catalysts and improve its oxidizing ability for selective and stable performance [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]. Several types of promoters have been investigated:

a) Alkali metal oxides: Due to their basic properties, alkali metal oxides promote CO2 adsorption and activation in CO2 hydrogenation reactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]. Potassium (K) has been identified as an effective promoter, with optimal concentrations in CrOx/SiO2 catalysts leading to 100% selectivity for propene formation at 22% propane conversion [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

b) Nickel (Ni): Ni-based promoters have shown excellent ability to activate CO2, even under harsh reaction conditions [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. Ni enhances the oxidizing ability of CO2 to reoxidize reduced Cr2+ to active Cr3+ and Cr6+ states [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

c) Ruthenium (Ru): Ru is a potential promoter due to its high activity in CO2 hydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

2.2 Support effects

The choice of support material can significantly impact CO2 activation and oxidizing ability:

a) Silica support: On silica surfaces, deep reduction of chromium species to Cr2+ is promoted, especially at low Cr content. This allows CO2 to more easily reoxidize the chromium [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

b) Mesoporous supports: Cr/MSU-x catalysts have shown excellent activity in the dehydrogenation of propane with CO2 [10.1016/j.catcom.2006.08.005].

3. Advantages of promoter-enhanced CO2 activation over O2 co-feeding

While O2 co-feeding has been proposed to overcome CO2's limitations, it often leads to decreased selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]. Promoter-enhanced CO2 activation offers several advantages:

a) Improved selectivity: CO2, being a milder oxidant, shifts the reaction towards more olefin production by preventing deep oxidation of reactive olefins [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].

b) Enhanced stability: Promoters like K increase the stability of chromium oxide catalysts, allowing for longer catalyst lifetimes [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

c) Balanced reactivity: Proper amounts of promoters can optimize activity and selectivity by balancing reactions such as reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) and propene reforming [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

d) Reduced coke formation: CO2, along with promoters, helps decrease coke deposition on the catalyst surface [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

e) Environmental benefits: CO2 utilization in this process can be environmentally beneficial, especially when using CO2 from waste gases [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

4. Mechanisms of promoter-enhanced CO2 activation

The effectiveness of promoters in enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability can be attributed to several mechanisms:

a) Redox cycle facilitation: Promoters like Ni facilitate CO2 dissociation to CO and adsorbed O*, which can subsequently regenerate Cr3+ via reoxidation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

b) Surface oxidation state control: CO2, in conjunction with promoters, helps maintain the Cr2O3 surface in a partially oxidized state during the reaction, which appears beneficial for catalyst activity and stability [10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4].

c) Enhanced CO2 adsorption: Alkali metal oxides promote CO2 adsorption on catalyst surfaces, facilitating its activation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

d) Hydrogen consumption: Promoters enhance CO2's ability to consume hydrogen species through the RWGS reaction, which is beneficial for enhancing the equilibrium of light alkane conversion [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].

In conclusion, the use of promoters in chromium-based catalytic systems offers a promising approach to enhance CO2 activation and oxidizing ability while maintaining selectivity and stability. This strategy provides a more balanced and environmentally friendly alternative to O2 co-feeding for oxidative dehydrogenation reactions.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 

The development of promoter-enhanced CO2 activation in chromium-based catalytic systems represents a significant advancement in the field of oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes. This approach not only addresses the limitations of CO2 as an oxidant but also offers potential environmental benefits through CO2 utilization.

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Rational design of multi-component promoters: Combining different types of promoters (e.g., alkali metals with transition metals) could potentially lead to synergistic effects, further enhancing CO2 activation and catalyst performance.

2. In-depth mechanistic studies: Advanced characterization techniques could be employed to elucidate the precise mechanisms by which promoters enhance CO2 activation at the molecular level, guiding future catalyst design.

3. Optimization of support materials: Exploring novel support materials or hierarchical structures could further improve the interaction between promoters, active sites, and CO2.

4. Process intensification: Integrating CO2-based oxidative dehydrogenation with other processes, such as CO2 capture or utilization, could enhance overall process efficiency and sustainability.

5. Scalability and industrial implementation: Addressing challenges related to catalyst stability, regeneration, and large-scale production will be crucial for transitioning this technology from lab-scale to industrial applications.

By pursuing these research directions, the scientific community can work towards developing more efficient and sustainable processes for light alkane conversion, contributing to the broader goals of CO2 utilization and green chemistry.

增强铬基催化体系中CO2的活化和氧化能力：挑战、策略和促进剂在提高性能和选择性方面的作用

铬基催化剂在轻烷烃的氧化脱氢反应中表现出良好的前景，特别是在使用CO2作为氧化剂时。然而，CO2的弱氧化能力和惰性特性带来了重大挑战。本综述部分探讨了克服这些限制的策略，重点关注促进剂在增强CO2活化、改善氧化能力以及实现选择性和稳定催化性能方面的作用。

1. CO2作为铬基体系中氧化剂的挑战

由于CO2的热力学稳定性，其氧化能力有限，使得还原态CrOx物种的再氧化变得困难[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。虽然CO2可以参与Cr2+ ⇌ Cr3+或Cr3+ ⇌ Cr6+的氧化还原循环，但其氧化能力较弱，且不易被CrOx活化[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

2. 增强CO2活化和氧化能力的策略

2.1 添加促进剂

引入促进剂是促进Cr2O3催化剂上CO2活化并改善其氧化能力以实现选择性和稳定性能的关键策略[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。已研究了几种类型的促进剂：

a) 碱金属氧化物：由于其碱性特性，碱金属氧化物促进了CO2加氢反应中的CO2吸附和活化[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。钾（K）被确定为有效的促进剂，在CrOx/SiO2催化剂中的最佳浓度可导致22%丙烷转化率下100%的丙烯选择性[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

b) 镍（Ni）：镍基促进剂表现出优异的CO2活化能力，即使在苛刻的反应条件下也是如此[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。Ni增强了CO2的氧化能力，可将还原态Cr2+再氧化为活性Cr3+和Cr6+状态[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

c) 钌（Ru）：由于其在CO2加氢反应中的高活性，Ru是潜在的促进剂[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

2.2 载体效应

载体材料的选择可以显著影响CO2的活化和氧化能力：

a) 二氧化硅载体：在二氧化硅表面上，特别是在低Cr含量时，铬物种可深度还原为Cr2+。这使得CO2更容易再氧化铬[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

b) 介孔载体：Cr/MSU-x催化剂在CO2存在下的丙烷脱氢反应中表现出优异的活性[10.1016/j.catcom.2006.08.005]。

3. 促进剂增强CO2活化相对于O2共进料的优势

虽然O2共进料被提出用于克服CO2的局限性，但它常常导致选择性下降[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。促进剂增强的CO2活化提供了几个优势：

a) 提高选择性：作为较温和的氧化剂，CO2将反应转向更多烯烃产物，防止反应性烯烃深度氧化[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。

b) 增强稳定性：像K这样的促进剂可以增加铬氧化物催化剂的稳定性，延长催化剂寿命[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

c) 平衡反应性：适量的促进剂可以通过平衡逆水气变换（RWGS）和丙烯重整等反应来优化活性和选择性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

d) 减少积碳：CO2与促进剂一起有助于减少催化剂表面的积碳沉积[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

e) 环境效益：在这一过程中利用CO2可带来环境效益，特别是当使用废气中的CO2时[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

4. 促进剂增强CO2活化的机理

促进剂在增强CO2活化和氧化能力方面的有效性可归因于几个机制：

a) 促进氧化还原循环：像Ni这样的促进剂促进CO2解离为CO和吸附O*，后者可以通过再氧化重新生成Cr3+[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

b) 表面氧化态控制：CO2与促进剂一起有助于在反应过程中保持Cr2O3表面处于部分氧化状态，这似乎有利于催化剂的活性和稳定性[10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4]。

c) 增强CO2吸附：碱金属氧化物促进催化剂表面的CO2吸附，促进其活化[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

d) 氢气消耗：促进剂增强了CO2通过RWGS反应消耗氢物种的能力，这有利于提高轻烷烃转化的平衡[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。

总之，在铬基催化体系中使用促进剂提供了一种有前景的方法来增强CO2的活化和氧化能力，同时保持选择性和稳定性。这一策略为氧化脱氢反应提供了一种比O2共进料更平衡和环境友好的替代方案。

综合理解与未来展望：

在铬基催化体系中开发促进剂增强的CO2活化代表了轻烷烃氧化脱氢领域的重大进展。这种方法不仅解决了CO2作为氧化剂的局限性，而且通过CO2利用提供了潜在的环境效益。

未来研究方向可能集中在：

1. 多组分促进剂的理性设计：结合不同类型的促进剂（如碱金属与过渡金属）可能会产生协同效应，进一步增强CO2活化和催化剂性能。

2. 深入的机理研究：可以采用先进的表征技术来阐明促进剂在分子水平上增强CO2活化的精确机制，指导未来的催化剂设计。

3. 载体材料的优化：探索新型载体材料或层次结构可能进一步改善促进剂、活性位点和CO2之间的相互作用。

4. 过程强化：将基于CO2的氧化脱氢与其他过程（如CO2捕获或利用）相结合，可以提高整体过程效率和可持续性。

5. 可扩展性和工业实施：解决与催化剂稳定性、再生和大规模生产相关的挑战将对将该技术从实验室规模转变为工业应用至关重要。

通过追求这些研究方向，科学界可以致力于开发更高效、更可持续的轻烷烃转化过程，为CO2利用和绿色化学的更广泛目标做出贡献。
