Enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems: Challenges, strategies, and the role of promoters in improving performance and selectivity

Carbon dioxide (CO2) has emerged as a promising alternative oxidant for the catalytic oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes to olefins, particularly in chromium-based catalytic systems. However, CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness present significant challenges that researchers have sought to address through various strategies, including the use of promoters.

The primary limitations of CO2 in chromium-based catalytic systems are:

1. Weak oxidizing ability: CO2 has limited oxidizing ability in terms of reoxidation of reduced CrOx species, due to its thermodynamically stable properties [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

2. Inertness: CO2 is quite inert and not easily activated by CrOx [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

To address these limitations, several strategies have been employed:

1. Promoter addition: Introducing promoters to facilitate CO2 activation on Cr2O3 catalysts and improve its oxidizing ability for selective and stable performance [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

2. Support effects: Using supports like silica that promote deep reduction of chromium to Cr2+, especially at low chromium loadings, allows CO2 to more easily reoxidize the chromium [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

3. Redox cycle mechanisms: In some catalysts, like CrOx/MCM-41, the reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) reaction proceeds via a redox cycle, allowing CO2 to be activated indirectly [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

Among these strategies, the use of promoters has shown particular promise. Various promoters have been investigated, including:

1. Nickel (Ni): Ni-promoted CrOx/SBA-15 catalysts have demonstrated excellent ability to activate CO2, even under harsh reaction conditions [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. Ni enhances the oxidizing ability of CO2 to reoxidize reduced Cr2+ to the active Cr3+ state and further to Cr6+ [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

2. Alkali metal oxides: Due to their basic properties, alkali metal oxides have been widely used as promoters for CO2 adsorption and activation in CO2 hydrogenation reactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

3. Ruthenium (Ru): Ru is a potential promoter due to its high activity in CO2 hydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

4. Potassium (K): K has been shown to be an effective promoter for increasing catalyst stability and selectivity. Optimum potassium content in CrOx/SiO2 catalysts resulted in 100% selectivity for propene formation at 22% propane conversion [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

These promoters offer several advantages over alternative strategies like O2 co-feeding:

1. Improved selectivity: CO2 with promoters allows for higher selectivity compared to using O2 as an oxidizing agent [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

2. Enhanced stability: Promoters, especially alkaline metals like potassium, have been shown to increase the stability of chromium oxide catalysts [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

3. Controlled oxidation: CO2 provides a milder oxidizing environment compared to O2, allowing for better control of the reaction when combined with promoters [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

4. Reduced coke formation: The introduction of CO2 as an oxidizing agent, coupled with promoters, helps decrease coke deposition on the catalyst surface [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

5. Environmental benefits: CO2 utilization in this process can be environmentally beneficial, especially when using CO2 from waste gases [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

In conclusion, the use of promoters in chromium-based catalytic systems offers a promising approach to enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability while maintaining selectivity and stability. This strategy provides a more balanced and controlled catalytic performance compared to O2 co-feeding, addressing the limitations of CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of promoters for enhancing CO2 activation in chromium-based catalytic systems represents a significant advancement in the field of oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes. Future research efforts might focus on:

1. Synergistic promoter combinations: Investigating the potential of combining multiple promoters (e.g., Ni with K or Ru) to further enhance CO2 activation and catalyst stability.

2. In-depth mechanistic studies: Conducting detailed spectroscopic and computational studies to elucidate the precise mechanisms by which different promoters enhance CO2 activation and influence the catalyst's redox properties.

3. Novel support materials: Exploring advanced support materials, such as metal-organic frameworks or 2D materials, that could work synergistically with promoters to enhance CO2 activation and catalyst performance.

4. Process intensification: Developing innovative reactor designs or process configurations that could maximize the benefits of CO2 activation by promoters, potentially leading to more efficient and sustainable industrial processes.

5. Broader applications: Investigating the potential of these promoted catalysts in other CO2 utilization reactions, such as dry reforming of methane or CO2 hydrogenation to valuable chemicals.

These future directions could lead to more efficient and environmentally friendly processes for olefin production, contributing to the broader goals of carbon utilization and sustainable chemical manufacturing.

增强铬基催化体系中CO2的活化和氧化能力：挑战、策略和促进剂在提高性能和选择性方面的作用

二氧化碳（CO2）已成为轻烷烃催化氧化脱氢制烯烃的一种有前景的替代氧化剂，特别是在铬基催化体系中。然而，CO2的弱氧化能力和惰性带来了重大挑战，研究人员通过各种策略（包括使用促进剂）寻求解决这些问题。

CO2在铬基催化体系中的主要限制是：

1. 氧化能力弱：由于其热力学稳定性，CO2在还原型CrOx物种的再氧化方面氧化能力有限[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

2. 惰性：CO2相当惰性，不易被CrOx活化[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

为解决这些限制，采用了几种策略：

1. 添加促进剂：引入促进剂以促进Cr2O3催化剂上的CO2活化，并提高其氧化能力，以获得选择性和稳定性能[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

2. 载体效应：使用如二氧化硅等促进铬深度还原至Cr2+的载体，特别是在低铬含量时，允许CO2更容易地再氧化铬[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

3. 氧化还原循环机制：在某些催化剂中，如CrOx/MCM-41，逆水气变换（RWGS）反应通过氧化还原循环进行，允许CO2间接活化[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

在这些策略中，使用促进剂显示出特别的前景。研究了各种促进剂，包括：

1. 镍（Ni）：Ni促进的CrOx/SBA-15催化剂已证明具有出色的CO2活化能力，即使在苛刻的反应条件下也是如此[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。Ni增强了CO2的氧化能力，可以将还原的Cr2+再氧化为活性Cr3+状态，并进一步氧化为Cr6+[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

2. 碱金属氧化物：由于其碱性特性，碱金属氧化物已被广泛用作CO2吸附和活化的促进剂，用于CO2加氢反应[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

3. 钌（Ru）：由于Ru在CO2加氢中具有高活性，因此是潜在的促进剂[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

4. 钾（K）：K已被证明是提高催化剂稳定性和选择性的有效促进剂。在CrOx/SiO2催化剂中，最佳钾含量在22%丙烷转化率下实现了100%的丙烯形成选择性[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

这些促进剂相比O2共进料等替代策略具有几个优势：

1. 提高选择性：与使用O2作为氧化剂相比，CO2与促进剂的组合可以实现更高的选择性[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

2. 增强稳定性：促进剂，特别是像钾这样的碱金属，已被证明可以增加铬氧化物催化剂的稳定性[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

3. 可控氧化：与O2相比，CO2提供了更温和的氧化环境，当与促进剂结合时，可以更好地控制反应[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

4. 降低积炭：将CO2作为氧化剂引入，再加上促进剂，有助于减少催化剂表面的积炭沉积[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

5. 环境效益：在这个过程中利用CO2可以带来环境效益，特别是当使用废气中的CO2时[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

总之，在铬基催化体系中使用促进剂为增强CO2的活化和氧化能力提供了一种有前景的方法，同时保持选择性和稳定性。与O2共进料相比，这种策略提供了更平衡和可控的催化性能，解决了CO2弱氧化能力和惰性的限制。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：开发促进剂以增强铬基催化体系中CO2的活化代表了轻烷烃氧化脱氢领域的重大进展。未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 协同促进剂组合：研究组合多种促进剂（如Ni与K或Ru）的潜力，以进一步增强CO2的活化和催化剂稳定性。

2. 深入的机理研究：进行详细的光谱和计算研究，以阐明不同促进剂增强CO2活化和影响催化剂氧化还原性质的精确机制。

3. 新型载体材料：探索先进的载体材料，如金属有机框架或二维材料，这些材料可能与促进剂协同工作，增强CO2的活化和催化剂性能。

4. 过程强化：开发创新的反应器设计或过程配置，以最大化促进剂CO2活化的益处，可能导致更高效和可持续的工业过程。

5. 更广泛的应用：研究这些促进的催化剂在其他CO2利用反应中的潜力，如甲烷干重整或CO2加氢制有价值化学品。

这些未来方向可能导致更高效和环境友好的烯烃生产过程，为碳利用和可持续化学品制造的更广泛目标做出贡献。
