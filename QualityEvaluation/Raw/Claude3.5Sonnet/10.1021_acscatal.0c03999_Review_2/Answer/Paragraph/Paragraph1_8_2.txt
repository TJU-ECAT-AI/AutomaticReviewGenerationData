Enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems: Challenges, strategies, and the role of promoters in improving performance and selectivity

Carbon dioxide (CO2) has emerged as a promising alternative oxidant for catalytic oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes to olefins, offering several advantages over conventional oxidants like O2. However, CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness present challenges in chromium-based catalytic systems. To address these limitations, researchers have explored various strategies, including the use of promoters, to enhance CO2 activation and improve its oxidizing ability.

Challenges of CO2 as an oxidant:
1. Weak oxidation potential: CO2 has limited oxidizing ability in terms of reoxidation of reduced CrOx species, due to its thermodynamically stable properties [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
2. Inertness: CO2 is quite inert and not easily activated by CrOx [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].
3. Competitive adsorption: CO2 can competitively adsorb on the surface, specifically on the basic sites of catalyst materials, potentially negatively affecting their performance [10.1021/cr5002436].

Strategies to enhance CO2 activation and oxidizing ability:
1. Promoter addition: Introducing promoters like Ni, K, and Ru to facilitate CO2 activation and improve its oxidizing ability [10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].
2. Support effects: Using supports like silica that promote deep reduction of chromium to Cr2+, especially at low chromium loadings, allows CO2 to more easily reoxidize the chromium [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].
3. Redox cycle mechanisms: Utilizing redox cycles, such as the reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) reaction, to indirectly activate CO2 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].
4. Low chromium content: Catalysts with low chromium content promote deeper reduction to Cr2+ species, which are more easily reoxidized by CO2 than Cr3+ [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

Role of promoters in improving performance and selectivity:
1. Enhanced CO2 activation: Promoters like Ni have an excellent ability to activate CO2, even under harsh reaction conditions. Ni dissociates CO2 into CO and activated oxygen (O*ads), which can then regenerate reduced chromium species [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
2. Improved oxidizing ability: Promoters enhance the oxidizing ability of CO2, allowing it to reoxidize reduced Cr2+ to the active Cr3+ state and further to Cr6+. This helps maintain the active sites of the catalyst [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
3. Stable selectivity: Unlike O2 co-feeding, which can significantly decrease selectivity for propylene, promoters like K maintain stable selectivity while improving catalytic performance [10.3103/S0361521912010089].
4. Reduced deactivation: Promoted catalysts show decreased deactivation rates compared to unpromoted catalysts, leading to more stable catalytic activity over time [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
5. Higher CO2 conversion: Promoted catalysts demonstrate higher CO2 conversion rates, indicating more efficient use of the CO2 reactant [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
6. Regeneration of active sites: Promoters enable the regeneration of active Cr3+ sites from inactive Cr2+, addressing a key deactivation mechanism [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

Advantages of CO2 with promoters over O2 co-feeding:
1. Selective oxidation: CO2 has lower oxidizing activity than O2, which shifts the reaction towards more olefin production by preventing deep oxidation of reactive olefins [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].
2. Coke inhibition: CO2 can inhibit coke formation and stabilize the catalyst through the reverse Boudouard reaction [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].
3. Hydrogen consumption: CO2 consumes hydrogen species through the RWGS reaction, enhancing the equilibrium of light alkane conversion [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].
4. Prevention of over-reduction: CO2 prevents the over-reduction of active oxidative chromium species by consuming excess hydrogen [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].
5. Improved catalyst stability: Promoters help extend catalyst life compared to O2 co-feeding, which can lead to faster deactivation [10.3103/S0361521912010089].
6. Environmental benefits: CO2 utilization in this process can be environmentally beneficial, especially when using CO2 from waste gases [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

In conclusion, the use of promoters in combination with CO2 as an oxidant offers a promising approach to address the limitations of CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness in chromium-based catalytic systems for propane dehydrogenation. This strategy provides a more selective, stable, and controlled catalytic performance compared to O2 co-feeding, while still addressing coke formation issues and shifting equilibrium favorably towards olefin production.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of promoters for enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems represents a significant step towards more efficient and sustainable propane dehydrogenation processes. The synergistic effects between promoters and CO2 not only address the inherent limitations of CO2 as an oxidant but also offer unique advantages over conventional O2 co-feeding strategies.

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Rational design of multi-functional promoters: Combining the benefits of different promoters (e.g., Ni for CO2 activation, K for selectivity, and Ru for hydrogenation) to create more efficient and versatile catalytic systems.

2. In-depth mechanistic studies: Utilizing advanced characterization techniques to elucidate the precise mechanisms of CO2 activation and oxidation on promoted chromium catalysts, enabling more targeted catalyst design.

3. Optimizing promoter-support interactions: Exploring novel support materials and promoter combinations to maximize CO2 activation and catalyst stability while minimizing unwanted side reactions.

4. Investigating the potential of bimetallic or trimetallic systems: Combining chromium with other catalytically active metals and promoters to create synergistic effects for improved CO2 utilization and olefin selectivity.

5. Developing promoters for other CO2-based reactions: Applying the knowledge gained from chromium-based systems to other catalytic processes that utilize CO2, such as dry reforming or CO2 hydrogenation.

6. Exploring the potential of MOFs and other advanced materials: Investigating the use of metal-organic frameworks or other porous materials as supports or promoters to enhance CO2 activation and catalyst performance.

7. Integrating computational modeling: Utilizing advanced computational methods to predict optimal promoter-catalyst combinations and guide experimental design, accelerating the discovery of more efficient catalytic systems.

8. Scaling up and process intensification: Developing strategies to translate the improved performance of promoted catalysts from laboratory scale to industrial applications, addressing challenges related to heat management, mass transfer, and long-term stability.

By pursuing these research directions, the field can continue to advance towards more sustainable and efficient utilization of CO2 in catalytic processes, contributing to the broader goals of carbon capture and utilization in the chemical industry.

增强铬基催化体系中CO2的活化和氧化能力：挑战、策略及促进剂在提高性能和选择性方面的作用

二氧化碳（CO2）已成为催化氧化脱氢轻烷烃生成烯烃的一种有前景的替代氧化剂，相比传统的氧化剂如O2具有多项优势。然而，CO2的弱氧化潜能和惰性在铬基催化体系中带来了挑战。为解决这些限制，研究人员探索了各种策略，包括使用促进剂，以增强CO2的活化和提高其氧化能力。

CO2作为氧化剂的挑战：
1. 弱氧化潜能：由于其热力学稳定性，CO2在还原CrOx物种的再氧化方面的氧化能力有限[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
2. 惰性：CO2相当惰性，不易被CrOx活化[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。
3. 竞争性吸附：CO2可以在表面上竞争性吸附，特别是在催化剂材料的碱性位点上，可能会对其性能产生负面影响[10.1021/cr5002436]。

增强CO2活化和氧化能力的策略：
1. 添加促进剂：引入如Ni、K和Ru等促进剂，以促进CO2的活化并提高其氧化能力[10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。
2. 载体效应：使用如二氧化硅等促进铬深度还原至Cr2+的载体，特别是在低铬含量时，使CO2更容易再氧化铬[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。
3. 氧化还原循环机制：利用如逆水煤气反应（RWGS）等氧化还原循环来间接活化CO2[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。
4. 低铬含量：低铬含量的催化剂促进更深度还原至Cr2+物种，这些物种比Cr3+更容易被CO2再氧化[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

促进剂在提高性能和选择性方面的作用：
1. 增强CO2活化：像Ni这样的促进剂具有出色的CO2活化能力，即使在苛刻的反应条件下也能发挥作用。Ni将CO2解离成CO和活性氧（O*ads），然后可以再生还原的铬物种[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
2. 提高氧化能力：促进剂增强了CO2的氧化能力，使其能够将还原的Cr2+再氧化为活性Cr3+状态，甚至进一步氧化为Cr6+。这有助于维持催化剂的活性位点[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
3. 稳定选择性：与可能显著降低丙烯选择性的O2共进料不同，像K这样的促进剂在提高催化性能的同时保持稳定的选择性[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。
4. 减少失活：与未促进的催化剂相比，促进的催化剂显示出较低的失活速率，导致更稳定的催化活性[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
5. 更高的CO2转化率：促进的催化剂表现出更高的CO2转化率，表明CO2反应物的利用更加有效[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
6. 活性位点的再生：促进剂使得不活性的Cr2+能够再生为活性Cr3+位点，解决了关键的失活机制[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

CO2与促进剂相比O2共进料的优势：
1. 选择性氧化：CO2的氧化活性低于O2，这使反应朝着更多烯烃生产的方向转移，通过防止反应性烯烃的深度氧化[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。
2. 抑制结焦：CO2可以通过逆布道反应抑制结焦形成并稳定催化剂[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。
3. 氢消耗：CO2通过RWGS反应消耗氢物种，增强轻烷烃转化的平衡[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。
4. 防止过度还原：CO2通过消耗过量氢防止活性氧化铬物种的过度还原[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。
5. 改善催化剂稳定性：促进剂有助于延长催化剂寿命，相比之下O2共进料可能导致更快的失活[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。
6. 环境效益：在这个过程中利用CO2可以带来环境效益，特别是当使用废气中的CO2时[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

总之，在丙烷脱氢的铬基催化体系中，结合使用促进剂和CO2作为氧化剂提供了一种有前景的方法来解决CO2弱氧化潜能和惰性的限制。与O2共进料相比，这种策略提供了更具选择性、更稳定和更可控的催化性能，同时还解决了结焦问题并有利于向烯烃生产方向移动平衡。
