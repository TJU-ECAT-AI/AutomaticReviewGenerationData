Enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems: Challenges, strategies, and the role of promoters in improving performance and selectivity

Carbon dioxide (CO2) has emerged as a promising alternative oxidant for catalytic oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes to olefins, particularly in chromium-based catalytic systems. However, CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness present significant challenges that need to be addressed to achieve selective and stable catalytic performance. This review focuses on the strategies employed to overcome these limitations, with particular emphasis on the role of promoters in enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability.

Challenges of using CO2 as an oxidant:
1. Weak oxidation potential: CO2 has limited oxidizing ability in terms of reoxidation of reduced CrOx species due to its thermodynamically stable properties [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
2. Inertness: CO2 is quite inert and not easily activated by CrOx [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].
3. Competitive adsorption: CO2 can competitively adsorb on the catalyst surface, particularly on basic sites, potentially negatively affecting performance [10.1021/cr5002436].

Strategies to enhance CO2 activation and oxidizing ability:
1. Promoters: The introduction of promoters is a key strategy to facilitate CO2 activation on Cr2O3 catalysts and improve its oxidizing ability for selective and stable performance [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

2. Redox cycle mechanisms: In CrOx/MCM-41 catalysts, the reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) reaction proceeds via a redox cycle, where hydrogen from propane dehydrogenation reduces Cr3+ to Cr2+, which is then reoxidized by CO2 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

3. Support effects: Using supports like silica that promote deep reduction of chromium to Cr2+, especially at low chromium loadings, allows CO2 to more easily reoxidize the chromium [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

4. Low chromium content: Catalysts with low chromium content promote deeper reduction to Cr2+ species, which are more easily reoxidized by CO2 than Cr3+ [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

Role of promoters in improving performance and selectivity:
1. Ni-based promoters:
   a. Enhanced CO2 activation: Ni has an excellent ability to activate CO2, even under harsh reaction conditions [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
   b. Improved oxidizing ability: Ni enhances the oxidizing ability of CO2, allowing it to reoxidize reduced Cr2+ to the active Cr3+ state and further to Cr6+ [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
   c. CO2 dissociation: Ni facilitates CO2 dissociation to CO and an adsorbed O* next to the Ni site, which can subsequently regenerate Cr3+ via reoxidation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].
   d. Stable selectivity and activity: Ni-promoted catalysts maintain stable selectivity and catalytic activity while improving performance [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

2. Alkali metal oxide promoters:
   a. CO2 adsorption and activation: Due to their basic properties, alkali metal oxides promote CO2 adsorption and activation in CO2 hydrogenation reactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].
   b. Increased stability and selectivity: Potassium, in particular, has been shown to increase the stability and selectivity of chromium oxide catalysts [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

3. Ru promoters:
   Ru serves as a potential promoter due to its high activity in CO2 hydrogenation, which can enhance the overall reaction process [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

Advantages of promoter-enhanced CO2 activation over O2 co-feeding:
1. Higher selectivity: CO2 with promoters allows for higher selectivity compared to using O2 as an oxidizing agent [10.3103/S0361521912010089].
2. Improved stability: Promoted catalysts show improved stability over longer periods compared to O2 co-feeding [10.3103/S0361521912010089].
3. More controlled oxidation: CO2 provides a milder oxidizing environment compared to O2, allowing for better control of the reaction when combined with promoters [10.3103/S0361521912010089].
4. Prevention of deep oxidation: The lower oxidizing activity of CO2 shifts the ODH reaction toward more olefin production by preventing deep oxidation of reactive olefins [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].
5. Coke inhibition: CO2 can inhibit coke formation and stabilize the catalyst through the reverse Boudouard reaction [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].

In conclusion, the use of promoters in chromium-based catalytic systems offers a promising approach to enhance CO2 activation and oxidizing ability while maintaining selectivity and stability. By carefully selecting and optimizing promoters, researchers can overcome the limitations of CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness, leading to more efficient and sustainable catalytic processes for the oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of promoters for enhancing CO2 activation in chromium-based catalytic systems represents a significant step towards more sustainable and efficient catalytic processes. While current research has focused primarily on Ni, alkali metal oxides, and Ru as promoters, future investigations could explore novel combinations of promoters or entirely new classes of materials to further improve CO2 activation and oxidizing ability.

One promising direction for future research could be the development of multi-functional promoters that can simultaneously enhance CO2 activation, improve oxidizing ability, and resist coke formation. This could potentially be achieved through the design of nanostructured promoters with tailored electronic and structural properties.

Another avenue for exploration could be the integration of advanced characterization techniques, such as operando spectroscopy and in situ electron microscopy, to gain deeper insights into the mechanisms of CO2 activation and the role of promoters under reaction conditions. This could lead to the rational design of more effective catalytic systems.

Furthermore, the concept of using CO2 as a mild oxidant with promoters could be extended to other catalytic systems beyond chromium-based catalysts, potentially opening up new possibilities for CO2 utilization in various chemical processes. This could contribute to the broader goal of developing a more circular carbon economy.

Lastly, future research could focus on scaling up these promoted catalytic systems for industrial applications, addressing challenges related to long-term stability, regeneration strategies, and process integration. This would be crucial for realizing the full potential of CO2 as an alternative oxidant in large-scale chemical production.

增强铬基催化体系中CO2的活化和氧化能力：挑战、策略及促进剂在提高性能和选择性方面的作用

二氧化碳（CO2）作为轻烷烃催化氧化脱氢制烯烃的一种有前景的替代氧化剂而备受关注，特别是在铬基催化体系中。然而，CO2的弱氧化能力和惰性特性带来了重大挑战，需要加以解决以实现选择性和稳定的催化性能。本综述重点关注克服这些限制的策略，特别强调了促进剂在增强CO2活化和氧化能力方面的作用。

使用CO2作为氧化剂的挑战：
1. 弱氧化能力：由于其热力学稳定性，CO2在还原态CrOx物种的再氧化方面氧化能力有限[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
2. 惰性：CO2相当惰性，不易被CrOx活化[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。
3. 竞争性吸附：CO2可以在催化剂表面竞争性吸附，尤其是在碱性位点上，可能会对性能产生负面影响[10.1021/cr5002436]。

增强CO2活化和氧化能力的策略：
1. 促进剂：引入促进剂是促进Cr2O3催化剂上CO2活化并提高其氧化能力以实现选择性和稳定性能的关键策略[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

2. 氧化还原循环机制：在CrOx/MCM-41催化剂中，逆水煤气变换（RWGS）反应通过氧化还原循环进行，其中丙烷脱氢产生的氢将Cr3+还原为Cr2+，然后被CO2再氧化[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

3. 载体效应：使用如硅胶等促进铬深度还原为Cr2+的载体，特别是在低铬含量时，可以使CO2更容易地再氧化铬[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

4. 低铬含量：低铬含量的催化剂促进更深度的还原至Cr2+物种，这比Cr3+更容易被CO2再氧化[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

促进剂在提高性能和选择性方面的作用：
1. 镍基促进剂：
   a. 增强CO2活化：镍具有出色的CO2活化能力，即使在苛刻的反应条件下也能发挥作用[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
   b. 提高氧化能力：镍增强了CO2的氧化能力，使其能够将还原态Cr2+再氧化为活性Cr3+状态，并进一步氧化为Cr6+[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
   c. CO2解离：镍促进CO2解离为CO和吸附在镍位点附近的O*，随后可以通过再氧化再生Cr3+[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。
   d. 稳定的选择性和活性：镍促进的催化剂在提高性能的同时保持稳定的选择性和催化活性[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

2. 碱金属氧化物促进剂：
   a. CO2吸附和活化：由于其碱性特性，碱金属氧化物在CO2加氢反应中促进CO2的吸附和活化[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。
   b. 提高稳定性和选择性：特别是钾已被证明可以提高铬氧化物催化剂的稳定性和选择性[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

3. 钌促进剂：
   钌由于其在CO2加氢反应中的高活性而成为潜在的促进剂，可以增强整体反应过程[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

促进剂增强CO2活化相比O2共进料的优势：
1. 更高的选择性：与使用O2作为氧化剂相比，CO2与促进剂的组合可以实现更高的选择性[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。
2. 改善稳定性：促进的催化剂相比O2共进料表现出更长时间的改善稳定性[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。
3. 更可控的氧化：与O2相比，CO2提供了更温和的氧化环境，当与促进剂结合时，可以更好地控制反应[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。
4. 防止深度氧化：CO2较低的氧化活性使ODH反应向更多烯烃生产倾斜，防止了活性烯烃的深度氧化[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。
5. 抑制积碳：CO2可以通过逆布多瓦反应抑制积碳形成并稳定催化剂[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。

总之，在铬基催化体系中使用促进剂提供了一种有前景的方法来增强CO2的活化和氧化能力，同时保持选择性和稳定性。通过仔细选择和优化促进剂，研究人员可以克服CO2弱氧化能力和惰性的限制，为轻烷烃氧化脱氢开发更高效和可持续的催化过程。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：开发促进剂以增强铬基催化体系中CO2的活化代表了向更可持续和高效催化过程迈出的重要一步。虽然当前研究主要集中在镍、碱金属氧化物和钌作为促进剂，但未来的研究可以探索新型促进剂组合或全新类别的材料，以进一步提高CO2的活化和氧化能力。

未来研究的一个有前景的方向可能是开发多功能促进剂，这些促进剂可以同时增强CO2活化、改善氧化能力并抵抗积碳形成。这可能通过设计具有定制电子和结构特性的纳米结构促进剂来实现。

另一个探索方向可能是集成先进表征技术，如原位光谱和原位电子显微镜，以深入了解反应条件下CO2活化机理和促进剂的作用。这可能导致更有效催化体系的理性设计。

此外，使用CO2作为温和氧化剂并结合促进剂的概念可以扩展到铬基催化剂以外的其他催化体系，可能为各种化学过程中CO2的利用开辟新的可能性。这可能有助于发展更加循环的碳经济。

最后，未来研究可以focus在将这些促进的催化体系扩大到工业应用，解决与长期稳定性、再生策略和过程集成相关的挑战。这对于实现CO2作为大规模化学生产中替代氧化剂的全部潜力至关重要。
