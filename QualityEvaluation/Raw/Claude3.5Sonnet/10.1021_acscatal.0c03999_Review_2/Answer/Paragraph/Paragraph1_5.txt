Enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems: Challenges, strategies, and the role of promoters in improving performance and selectivity

Carbon dioxide (CO2) has emerged as a promising alternative oxidant for catalytic oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes to olefins, offering several advantages over conventional oxygen-based systems. However, CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness present significant challenges in chromium-based catalytic systems. This review explores the strategies employed to address these limitations, with a focus on the role of promoters in enhancing CO2 activation, improving oxidizing ability, and achieving selective and stable catalytic performance.

Challenges of CO2 as an Oxidant:
CO2 has limited oxidizing ability in terms of reoxidation of reduced CrOx species due to its thermodynamically stable properties [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. Its oxidation potential is rather weak, and it is not easily activated by CrOx [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]. The efficiency of reoxidation by CO2 is much lower than that by oxygen [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

Strategies to Overcome CO2 Limitations:
1. Redox Cycle Mechanism: In CrOx/MCM-41 catalysts, the reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) reaction proceeds via a redox cycle. Hydrogen from propane dehydrogenation reduces Cr3+ to Cr2+, which is then reoxidized by CO2 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

2. Support Effect: Using supports like silica that promote deep reduction of chromium to Cr2+, especially at low chromium loadings, allows CO2 to more easily reoxidize the chromium [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

3. Low Chromium Content: Catalysts with low chromium content promote deeper reduction to Cr2+ species, which are more easily reoxidized by CO2 than Cr3+ [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

4. Promoter Introduction: The development of suitable promoters to activate CO2, enhance its oxidizing ability, and maintain stable selectivity for propylene is highly desirable [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

Role of Promoters:
Various promoters have been investigated to enhance CO2 activation and improve its oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems:

1. Nickel (Ni):
Ni-based promoters offer several advantages:
- Enhanced CO2 activation: Ni has an excellent ability to activate CO2, even under harsh reaction conditions [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
- Improved oxidizing ability: Ni enhances the oxidizing ability of CO2 to reoxidize reduced Cr2+ to the active Cr3+ state and further to Cr6+ [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
- CO2 dissociation: Ni dissociates CO2 to CO and O*ads, which regenerates the reduced Cr species Cr2+ to Cr3+ through reoxidation [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
- Stable catalytic activity: Ni-promoted catalysts show decreased deactivation rates compared to unpromoted catalysts [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

2. Alkali Metal Oxides:
- CO2 adsorption and activation: Due to their basic properties, alkali metal oxides promote CO2 adsorption and activation in CO2 hydrogenation reactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].
- Improved stability: Potassium (K) has been shown to increase the stability of chromium oxide catalysts [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

3. Ruthenium (Ru):
- Enhanced CO2 hydrogenation: Ru serves as a potential promoter due to its high activity in CO2 hydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

Advantages over O2 Co-feeding:
1. Selective oxidation: CO2 has lower oxidizing activity than O2, which shifts the reaction towards more olefin production by preventing deep oxidation of reactive olefins [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].
2. Stable selectivity: Unlike O2 co-feeding, which can significantly decrease selectivity for propylene, promoted CO2 systems maintain stable selectivity while improving catalytic performance [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
3. Coke inhibition: CO2 inhibits coke formation rather than eliminating existing coke, which can be beneficial for catalyst stability [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].
4. Equilibrium enhancement: CO2 consumes hydrogen species through the reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) reaction, enhancing the equilibrium of light alkane conversion [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].
5. Prevention of over-reduction: CO2 prevents the over-reduction of active oxidative chromium species by consuming excess hydrogen [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The development of promoters for enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems represents a significant advancement in the field of oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes. By addressing the inherent limitations of CO2 as an oxidant, these promoters offer a more balanced approach to catalyst design, potentially leading to more efficient and environmentally friendly processes.

Future research directions may include:

1. Exploration of novel promoter combinations: Investigating synergistic effects between different promoters (e.g., Ni-K or Ru-Ni combinations) could lead to further improvements in catalytic performance and stability.

2. In-depth mechanistic studies: Advanced spectroscopic techniques could be employed to elucidate the precise mechanisms by which promoters enhance CO2 activation and oxidation, providing insights for rational catalyst design.

3. Catalyst support engineering: Developing new support materials or modifying existing ones to work synergistically with promoters could further enhance CO2 activation and catalyst stability.

4. Process optimization: Integrating promoted catalysts into innovative reactor designs or exploring alternative process conditions could maximize the benefits of enhanced CO2 activation.

5. Sustainability assessment: Comprehensive life cycle analyses of promoted catalyst systems could quantify their environmental benefits compared to traditional O2-based processes.

6. Scale-up studies: Investigating the performance of promoted catalysts at pilot and industrial scales will be crucial for their practical implementation.

By pursuing these research directions, the scientific community can work towards developing more efficient, selective, and sustainable catalytic systems for the valorization of CO2 in chemical production processes.

提高铬基催化剂系统中CO2的活化和氧化能力：挑战、策略以及促进剂在改善性能和选择性方面的作用

二氧化碳（CO2）已经成为轻烷烃催化氧化脱氢制烯烃的一种有前景的非常规氧化剂，与传统的氧气系统相比具有多项优势。然而，CO2的弱氧化能力和惰性在铬基催化剂系统中带来了重大挑战。本综述探讨了解决这些限制的策略，重点关注促进剂在增强CO2活化、提高氧化能力以及实现选择性和稳定催化性能方面的作用。

CO2作为氧化剂的挑战：
由于其热力学稳定性，CO2在还原态CrOx物种的再氧化方面具有有限的氧化能力[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。其氧化能力相当弱，而且不容易被CrOx活化[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。CO2的再氧化效率远低于氧气[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

克服CO2限制的策略：
1. 氧化还原循环机制：在CrOx/MCM-41催化剂中，逆水气变换（RWGS）反应通过氧化还原循环进行。丙烷脱氢产生的氢气将Cr3+还原为Cr2+，然后被CO2再氧化[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

2. 载体效应：使用促进铬深度还原为Cr2+的载体（如二氧化硅），特别是在低铬含量时，使CO2更容易再氧化铬[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

3. 低铬含量：低铬含量的催化剂促进更深度的还原为Cr2+物种，这比Cr3+更容易被CO2再氧化[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

4. 引入促进剂：开发合适的促进剂来活化CO2，增强其氧化能力，并保持丙烯的稳定选择性是非常理想的[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

促进剂的作用：
研究了各种促进剂以增强CO2活化并提高其在铬基催化系统中的氧化能力：

1. 镍（Ni）：
Ni基促进剂提供了几个优势：
- 增强CO2活化：Ni具有出色的CO2活化能力，即使在严苛的反应条件下也是如此[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
- 改善氧化能力：Ni增强了CO2的氧化能力，可以将还原态Cr2+再氧化为活性Cr3+状态，甚至进一步氧化为Cr6+[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
- CO2解离：Ni将CO2解离为CO和O*ads，后者通过再氧化将还原态Cr2+物种再生为Cr3+[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
- 稳定的催化活性：与未促进的催化剂相比，Ni促进的催化剂显示出较低的失活率[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

2. 碱金属氧化物：
- CO2吸附和活化：由于其碱性特性，碱金属氧化物促进CO2在CO2加氢反应中的吸附和活化[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。
- 提高稳定性：钾（K）已被证明可以提高铬氧化物催化剂的稳定性[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

3. 钌（Ru）：
- 增强CO2加氢：由于Ru在CO2加氢反应中具有高活性，因此是潜在的促进剂[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

相对于O2共进料的优势：
1. 选择性氧化：CO2的氧化活性低于O2，这使反应向更多烯烃产物方向转移，防止反应性烯烃的深度氧化[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。
2. 稳定的选择性：与O2共进料可能显著降低丙烯选择性不同，促进的CO2系统在改善催化性能的同时保持稳定的选择性[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
3. 抑制积碳：CO2抑制积碳形成而不是消除现有积碳，这对催化剂稳定性有利[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。
4. 增强平衡：CO2通过逆水气变换（RWGS）反应消耗氢物种，增强了轻烷烃转化的平衡[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。
5. 防止过度还原：CO2通过消耗过量氢气防止活性氧化铬物种的过度还原[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。

综合理解与未来展望：
开发促进剂以增强铬基催化系统中CO2的活化和氧化能力代表了轻烷烃氧化脱氢领域的重大进展。通过解决CO2作为氧化剂的固有限制，这些促进剂为催化剂设计提供了更平衡的方法，有可能导致更高效和环境友好的工艺。

未来研究方向可能包括：

1. 探索新型促进剂组合：研究不同促进剂之间的协同效应（例如，Ni-K或Ru-Ni组合）可能会进一步改善催化性能和稳定性。

2. 深入的机理研究：采用先进的光谱技术可以阐明促进剂增强CO2活化和氧化的精确机制，为理性催化剂设计提供见解。

3. 催化剂载体工程：开发新的载体材料或修改现有载体，使其与促进剂协同工作，可能进一步增强CO2活化和催化剂稳定性。

4. 工艺优化：将促进的催化剂整合到创新的反应器设计中或探索替代工艺条件，可以最大化增强CO2活化的益处。

5. 可持续性评估：对促进的催化剂系统进行全面的生命周期分析，可以量化其与传统O2基工艺相比的环境效益。

6. 放大研究：研究促进的催化剂在试点和工业规模上的性能对其实际应用至关重要。

通过追求这些研究方向，科学界可以致力于开发更高效、选择性和可持续的催化系统，用于化学生产过程中CO2的增值利用。
