Enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems: Challenges, strategies, and the role of promoters in improving performance and selectivity

Carbon dioxide (CO2) has emerged as a promising alternative oxidant for catalytic oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes to olefins, offering several advantages over conventional oxygen-based systems. However, CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness pose significant challenges in chromium-based catalytic systems. This review focuses on the strategies employed to overcome these limitations, with particular emphasis on the role of promoters in enhancing CO2 activation, improving oxidizing ability, and achieving selective and stable catalytic performance.

Challenges of CO2 as an oxidant:
CO2 has limited oxidizing ability in terms of reoxidation of reduced CrOx species due to its thermodynamically stable properties [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. It is quite inert and not easily activated by CrOx [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]. The efficiency of reoxidation by CO2 is much lower than that by oxygen [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

Strategies to enhance CO2 activation and oxidizing ability:

1. Promoter addition:
Various promoters have been investigated to facilitate CO2 activation and improve its oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalysts:

a) Nickel (Ni): Ni-based promoters have shown excellent ability to activate CO2, even under harsh reaction conditions [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. Ni enhances the oxidizing ability of CO2 to reoxidize reduced Cr2+ to the active Cr3+ state and further to Cr6+ [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. It facilitates CO2 dissociation to CO and an adsorbed O*, which can subsequently regenerate Cr3+ via reoxidation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

b) Alkali metal oxides: Due to their basic properties, alkali metal oxides promote CO2 adsorption and activation in CO2 hydrogenation reactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]. Potassium (K) has been identified as an effective promoter, with optimum content ranging from 0.2-0.4% in 5% CrOx/SiO2 catalysts [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

c) Ruthenium (Ru): Ru is a potential promoter due to its high activity in CO2 hydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

2. Support effects:
The choice of support plays a crucial role in enhancing CO2 activation:

a) Silica support: CO2 exerts promoting effects only on SiO2-supported Cr2O3 catalysts [10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4]. The boundaries between Cr2O3 and SiO2 particles have an important role in the promoting effect of CO2 [10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4].

b) Mesoporous supports: Cr/MSU-x catalysts exhibit excellent activity in the dehydrogenation of propane with CO2 [10.1016/j.catcom.2006.08.005].

3. Redox cycle mechanisms:
Leveraging redox cycles can improve CO2 activation and oxidizing ability:

a) Cr6+/Cr3+ cycle: During the reaction, C3H8 is dehydrogenated to C3H6 by Cr6+O4 tetrahedra, which is simultaneously reduced to Cr3+O6 octahedra. The reduced Cr3+O6 octahedra are then reoxidized to Cr6+O4 tetrahedra by CO2 [10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4].

b) Cr3+/Cr2+ cycle: Hydrogen formed in the dehydrogenation of propane reduces Cr3+ to Cr2+ species, which are then reoxidized by CO2 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

Advantages of CO2 with promoters over O2 co-feeding:

1. Improved selectivity: CO2, being a milder oxidant, shifts the reaction towards more olefin production by preventing deep oxidation of reactive olefins [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]. This is in contrast to O2 co-feeding, which can significantly decrease selectivity for propylene [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

2. Enhanced stability: Promoters like K and Ni have been shown to increase the stability of chromium oxide catalysts, allowing for longer catalyst lifetimes and sustained performance over extended periods [10.3103/S0361521912010089] [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

3. Coke inhibition: CO2 primarily inhibits coke formation rather than eliminating existing coke [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]. This is advantageous over O2 co-feeding, which can lead to faster deactivation due to coke formation [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

4. Controlled oxidation: CO2 provides a milder oxidizing environment compared to O2, allowing for better control of the reaction when combined with promoters [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

5. Environmental benefits: CO2 utilization in this process can be environmentally beneficial, especially when using CO2 from waste gases [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The development of promoters for enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems has shown significant promise in addressing the limitations of CO2 as an oxidant. The synergistic effects of promoters, support materials, and redox cycle mechanisms have demonstrated the potential to achieve selective and stable catalytic performance in propane dehydrogenation reactions.

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Rational design of multi-functional promoters: Combining the strengths of different promoters (e.g., Ni for CO2 activation, K for stability) could lead to more efficient catalytic systems.

2. In-depth mechanistic studies: Advanced in-situ characterization techniques could provide deeper insights into the role of promoters in CO2 activation and the redox cycles of chromium species.

3. Novel support materials: Exploration of new support materials or hierarchical structures could further enhance CO2 activation and catalyst stability.

4. Process intensification: Integration of membrane technology or microreactor systems could optimize CO2 utilization and product separation.

5. Computational modeling: Advanced modeling techniques could guide the design of more effective promoter-catalyst combinations and predict their performance under various reaction conditions.

6. Sustainable catalyst design: Developing catalysts with lower chromium content or exploring alternative active metals could address environmental concerns while maintaining high performance.

By addressing these research directions, the field of CO2-mediated propane dehydrogenation could move towards more efficient, selective, and environmentally friendly processes, potentially revolutionizing the production of light olefins.

提高铬基催化体系中CO2的活化和氧化能力：挑战、策略以及促进剂在改善性能和选择性方面的作用

二氧化碳（CO2）已成为轻烷烃催化氧化脱氢制烯烃的一种有前景的替代氧化剂，相比传统的氧气体系具有多项优势。然而，CO2的弱氧化能力和惰性在铬基催化体系中带来了重大挑战。本综述着重于克服这些局限性的策略，特别强调了促进剂在增强CO2活化、提高氧化能力以及实现选择性和稳定催化性能方面的作用。

CO2作为氧化剂的挑战：
由于其热力学稳定性，CO2在还原CrOx物种的再氧化方面的氧化能力有限 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。它相当惰性，不易被CrOx活化 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。CO2的再氧化效率远低于氧气 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

增强CO2活化和氧化能力的策略：

1. 添加促进剂：
研究了各种促进剂以促进CO2活化并提高其在铬基催化剂中的氧化能力：

a) 镍（Ni）：基于镍的促进剂表现出优异的CO2活化能力，即使在苛刻的反应条件下也是如此 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。Ni增强了CO2的氧化能力，可以将还原的Cr2+再氧化为活性Cr3+状态，并进一步氧化为Cr6+ [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。它促进CO2解离为CO和吸附的O*，后者可以通过再氧化重新生成Cr3+ [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

b) 碱金属氧化物：由于其碱性特性，碱金属氧化物促进CO2在CO2加氢反应中的吸附和活化 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。钾（K）被认为是一种有效的促进剂，在5% CrOx/SiO2催化剂中的最佳含量范围为0.2-0.4% [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

c) 钌（Ru）：由于其在CO2加氢反应中的高活性，Ru是一种潜在的促进剂 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

2. 载体效应：
载体的选择在增强CO2活化方面起着关键作用：

a) 二氧化硅载体：CO2只对SiO2负载的Cr2O3催化剂产生促进作用 [10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4]。Cr2O3和SiO2颗粒之间的界面在CO2的促进作用中起重要作用 [10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4]。

b) 介孔载体：Cr/MSU-x催化剂在CO2存在下的丙烷脱氢反应中表现出优异的活性 [10.1016/j.catcom.2006.08.005]。

3. 氧化还原循环机制：
利用氧化还原循环可以改善CO2的活化和氧化能力：

a) Cr6+/Cr3+循环：在反应过程中，C3H8被Cr6+O4四面体脱氢为C3H6，同时Cr6+O4被还原为Cr3+O6八面体。还原的Cr3+O6八面体随后被CO2再氧化为Cr6+O4四面体 [10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4]。

b) Cr3+/Cr2+循环：丙烷脱氢产生的氢气将Cr3+还原为Cr2+物种，后者再被CO2氧化 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

CO2与促进剂相比O2共进料的优势：

1. 提高选择性：CO2作为一种较温和的氧化剂，通过防止活性烯烃深度氧化，将反应转向更多的烯烃产物 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。这与O2共进料形成对比，后者可能显著降低丙烯的选择性 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

2. 增强稳定性：K和Ni等促进剂已被证明可以提高铬氧化物催化剂的稳定性，延长催化剂寿命，并在长时间内保持性能 [10.3103/S0361521912010089] [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

3. 抑制积碳：CO2主要抑制积碳的形成，而不是消除现有的积碳 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。这比O2共进料更有优势，因为后者可能由于积碳形成导致催化剂更快失活 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

4. 可控氧化：与O2相比，CO2提供了一个更温和的氧化环境，当与促进剂结合时，可以更好地控制反应 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

5. 环境效益：在这个过程中利用CO2可以带来环境效益，特别是当使用废气中的CO2时 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

综合理解与未来展望：

在铬基催化体系中开发促进剂以增强CO2活化和氧化能力已显示出显著的潜力，可以解决CO2作为氧化剂的局限性。促进剂、载体材料和氧化还原循环机制的协同作用已经证明了在丙烷脱氢反应中实现选择性和稳定催化性能的潜力。

未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 多功能促进剂的理性设计：结合不同促进剂的优势（例如，Ni用于CO2活化，K用于稳定性），可能导致更高效的催化系统。

2. 深入的机理研究：先进的原位表征技术可以提供关于促进剂在CO2活化和铬物种氧化还原循环中作用的更深入见解。

3. 新型载体材料：探索新的载体材料或层次结构可能进一步增强CO2活化和催化剂稳定性。

4. 过程强化：膜技术或微反应器系统的集成可以优化CO2利用和产物分离。

5. 计算机模拟：先进的建模技术可以指导更有效的促进剂-催化剂组合的设计，并预测它们在各种反应条件下的性能。

6. 可持续催化剂设计：开发含铬量更低的催化剂或探索替代活性金属，可以解决环境问题，同时保持高性能。

通过解决这些研究方向，CO2介导的丙烷脱氢领域可能朝着更高效、更选择性和更环保的过程发展，潜在地revolutionizing轻烯烃的生产。
