Enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems: Challenges, strategies, and the role of promoters in improving performance and selectivity

Carbon dioxide (CO2) has emerged as a promising alternative oxidant for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions in chromium-based catalytic systems. However, CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness present significant challenges. This review discusses strategies to overcome these limitations, focusing on the role of promoters in enhancing CO2 activation, improving oxidizing ability, and achieving selective and stable catalytic performance.

Challenges of CO2 as an oxidant:
1. Weak oxidation potential: CO2 has limited oxidizing ability for reoxidizing reduced CrOx species due to its thermodynamically stable properties [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
2. Inertness: CO2 is not easily activated by CrOx, making it challenging to utilize effectively in catalytic systems [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

Strategies to enhance CO2 activation and oxidizing ability:

1. Promoter addition:
   a. Nickel (Ni): Ni-promoted CrOx/SBA-15 catalysts have shown excellent ability to activate CO2, even under harsh reaction conditions [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. Ni enhances the oxidizing ability of CO2 to reoxidize reduced Cr2+ to active Cr3+ and Cr6+ states [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
   
   b. Alkali metal oxides: Due to their basic properties, alkali metal oxides promote CO2 adsorption and activation in CO2 hydrogenation reactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].
   
   c. Ruthenium (Ru): Ru is a potential promoter due to its high activity in CO2 hydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].
   
   d. Potassium (K): K-promoted chromium oxide catalysts have shown increased stability and selectivity. Optimum K content in CrOx/SiO2 catalysts resulted in 100% selectivity for propene formation at 22% propane conversion [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

2. Support effects:
   Silica supports, especially at low chromium loadings, promote deep reduction of chromium to Cr2+, which is more easily reoxidized by CO2 than Cr3+ [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

3. Redox cycle mechanism:
   In CrOx/MCM-41 catalysts, the reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) reaction proceeds via a redox cycle. Hydrogen from propane dehydrogenation reduces Cr3+ to Cr2+, which is then reoxidized by CO2 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

Advantages of CO2 with promoters over O2 co-feeding:

1. Improved selectivity: CO2 is less likely to cause deep oxidation of paraffins and olefins compared to O2 [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

2. Enhanced stability: Promoters like K and Ni help increase catalyst stability and reduce deactivation rates [10.3103/S0361521912010089] [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

3. Controlled oxidation: CO2 provides a milder oxidizing environment compared to O2, allowing for better control of the reaction when combined with promoters [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

4. Improved catalyst lifetime: By reducing coke formation, promoters help extend catalyst life compared to O2 co-feeding, which can lead to faster deactivation [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

5. Environmental benefits: CO2 utilization in this process can be environmentally beneficial, especially when using CO2 from waste gases [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

In conclusion, the use of promoters with CO2 in chromium-based catalytic systems for propane dehydrogenation offers a more selective, stable, and controlled catalytic performance compared to O2 co-feeding. These strategies effectively address the limitations of CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness while maintaining high selectivity and stability.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of promoters for enhancing CO2 activation in chromium-based catalytic systems represents a significant step towards more efficient and environmentally friendly propane dehydrogenation processes. The synergistic effects observed between promoters like Ni, K, and Ru with CO2 open up new possibilities for catalyst design and optimization.

Future research directions may include:

1. Exploring novel multi-component promoter systems that combine the benefits of different promoters (e.g., Ni-K or Ru-K combinations) to further enhance CO2 activation and catalyst stability.

2. Investigating the atomic-level mechanisms of CO2 activation on promoted chromium catalysts using advanced characterization techniques and computational modeling to guide rational catalyst design.

3. Developing strategies to tailor the support material properties to work in concert with promoters for improved CO2 activation and catalyst longevity.

4. Exploring the potential of promoter-enhanced CO2 activation in other dehydrogenation reactions or related processes, such as CO2 reforming of methane or ethane dehydrogenation.

5. Investigating the scalability and long-term stability of promoted chromium catalysts under industrial conditions to bridge the gap between laboratory research and practical applications.

6. Exploring the integration of these catalytic systems with CO2 capture technologies to create a closed-loop process that effectively utilizes waste CO2 streams.

By addressing these research directions, we can expect significant advancements in the field of CO2-mediated dehydrogenation reactions, potentially leading to more sustainable and efficient processes for olefin production.

提高铬基催化系统中CO2的活化和氧化能力：挑战、策略和促进剂在提高性能和选择性方面的作用

二氧化碳（CO2）已成为铬基催化系统中丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应的一种有前景的替代氧化剂。然而，CO2的弱氧化能力和惰性带来了重大挑战。本综述讨论了克服这些限制的策略，重点关注促进剂在增强CO2活化、改善氧化能力以及实现选择性和稳定催化性能方面的作用。

CO2作为氧化剂的挑战：
1. 弱氧化能力：由于其热力学稳定性，CO2对还原态CrOx物种的再氧化能力有限[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
2. 惰性：CO2不易被CrOx活化，使其难以在催化系统中有效利用[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

提高CO2活化和氧化能力的策略：

1. 添加促进剂：
   a. 镍（Ni）：Ni促进的CrOx/SBA-15催化剂显示出优异的CO2活化能力，即使在苛刻的反应条件下也是如此[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。Ni增强了CO2的氧化能力，可以将还原态Cr2+再氧化为活性Cr3+和Cr6+态[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
   
   b. 碱金属氧化物：由于其碱性特性，碱金属氧化物促进了CO2在CO2加氢反应中的吸附和活化[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。
   
   c. 钌（Ru）：Ru是一种潜在的促进剂，因为它在CO2加氢中具有高活性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。
   
   d. 钾（K）：K促进的氧化铬催化剂显示出增加的稳定性和选择性。在CrOx/SiO2催化剂中，最佳K含量在22%丙烷转化率下实现了100%的丙烯形成选择性[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

2. 载体效应：
   二氧化硅载体，特别是在低铬含量时，促进铬深度还原为Cr2+，这比Cr3+更容易被CO2再氧化[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

3. 氧化还原循环机制：
   在CrOx/MCM-41催化剂中，逆水气变换（RWGS）反应通过氧化还原循环进行。丙烷脱氢产生的氢将Cr3+还原为Cr2+，然后被CO2再氧化[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

CO2与促进剂相比O2共进料的优势：

1. 提高选择性：与O2相比，CO2不太可能导致烷烃和烯烃的深度氧化[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

2. 增强稳定性：K和Ni等促进剂有助于提高催化剂稳定性并降低失活率[10.3103/S0361521912010089] [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

3. 可控氧化：与O2相比，CO2提供了更温和的氧化环境，当与促进剂结合时，可以更好地控制反应[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

4. 改善催化剂寿命：通过减少积碳形成，促进剂有助于延长催化剂寿命，而O2共进料可能导致更快的失活[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

5. 环境效益：在这个过程中利用CO2可以带来环境效益，特别是当使用废气中的CO2时[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

总之，在铬基催化系统中将促进剂与CO2结合用于丙烷脱氢反应，提供了比O2共进料更具选择性、稳定性和可控性的催化性能。这些策略有效地解决了CO2弱氧化能力和惰性的限制，同时保持了高选择性和稳定性。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：开发促进剂以增强铬基催化系统中CO2的活化，代表了朝着更高效和环境友好的丙烷脱氢过程迈出的重要一步。在Ni、K和Ru等促进剂与CO2之间观察到的协同效应为催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的可能性。

未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 探索新型多组分促进剂系统，结合不同促进剂（如Ni-K或Ru-K组合）的优势，进一步增强CO2活化和催化剂稳定性。

2. 使用先进的表征技术和计算机建模研究促进铬催化剂上CO2活化的原子级机制，以指导理性催化剂设计。

3. 开发策略，调整载体材料性能，与促进剂协同工作，以改善CO2活化和催化剂寿命。

4. 探索促进剂增强CO2活化在其他脱氢反应或相关过程中的潜力，如CO2重整甲烷或乙烷脱氢。

5. 研究促进铬催化剂在工业条件下的可扩展性和长期稳定性，以弥合实验室研究与实际应用之间的差距。

6. 探索将这些催化系统与CO2捕获技术结合，创建一个有效利用废CO2流的闭环过程。

通过解决这些研究方向，我们可以期待CO2介导的脱氢反应领域取得重大进展，潜在地导致更可持续和高效的烯烃生产过程。
