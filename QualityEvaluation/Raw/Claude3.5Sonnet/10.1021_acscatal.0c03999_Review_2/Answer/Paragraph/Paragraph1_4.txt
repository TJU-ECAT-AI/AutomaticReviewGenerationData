Enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems: Challenges, strategies, and the role of promoters in improving performance and selectivity

Carbon dioxide (CO2) has emerged as a promising alternative oxidant for catalytic oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes to olefins, particularly in chromium-based catalytic systems. However, CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness present significant challenges that need to be addressed to achieve optimal catalytic performance. This review focuses on the strategies employed to overcome these limitations, with particular emphasis on the role of promoters in enhancing CO2 activation, improving oxidizing ability, and achieving selective and stable catalytic performance.

Challenges of CO2 as an Oxidant:
CO2 has limited oxidizing ability due to its thermodynamically stable properties, making it difficult to reoxidize reduced CrOx species [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. Additionally, CO2 is quite inert and not easily activated by CrOx [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]. These limitations result in lower efficiency of reoxidation compared to oxygen [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

Strategies to Enhance CO2 Activation and Oxidizing Ability:

1. Promoter Addition:
The introduction of promoters is a key strategy to facilitate CO2 activation and improve its oxidizing ability [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]. Several types of promoters have been investigated:

a) Nickel (Ni):
Ni has shown excellent ability to activate CO2, even under harsh reaction conditions [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. It enhances the oxidizing ability of CO2 by facilitating its dissociation to CO and adsorbed O*, which can then regenerate reduced chromium species [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. Ni-promoted catalysts demonstrate higher CO2 conversion rates and stable propylene production [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

b) Alkali Metal Oxides:
Due to their basic properties, alkali metal oxides promote CO2 adsorption and activation in CO2 hydrogenation reactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]. Potassium (K) has been identified as an effective promoter, enhancing catalyst stability and selectivity [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

c) Ruthenium (Ru):
Ru is a potential promoter due to its high activity in CO2 hydrogenation, which can enhance the overall reaction process [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

2. Support Effects:
The choice of support material plays a crucial role in enhancing CO2 activation. Silica supports, especially at low chromium loadings, promote deep reduction of chromium to Cr2+, which is more easily reoxidized by CO2 than Cr3+ [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

3. Redox Cycle Mechanism:
In some chromium-based catalysts, such as CrOx/MCM-41, the reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) reaction proceeds via a redox cycle. This cycle involves the reduction of Cr3+ to Cr2+ by hydrogen from propane dehydrogenation, followed by reoxidation of Cr2+ by CO2 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

Advantages over O2 Co-feeding:

1. Improved Selectivity:
CO2, being a milder oxidant compared to O2, shifts the oxidative dehydrogenation reaction towards more olefin production by preventing deep oxidation of reactive olefins [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]. This results in higher selectivity for the desired products.

2. Stable Catalytic Performance:
Promoters like Ni and K help maintain stable catalytic activity and selectivity over extended periods, unlike O2 co-feeding which can lead to significant selectivity drops [10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

3. Reduced Coke Formation:
CO2 can inhibit coke formation and, in some cases, gasify existing coke through the reverse Boudouard reaction [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]. This helps in maintaining catalyst stability and prolonging catalyst lifetime.

4. Thermodynamic Equilibrium Shift:
CO2 consumes hydrogen species through the RWGS reaction, which enhances the equilibrium of light alkane conversion [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].

5. Prevention of Over-oxidation:
CO2 prevents the over-reduction of active oxidative chromium species by consuming excess hydrogen, maintaining the catalyst in its active state [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The development of promoters to enhance CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems represents a significant advancement in the field of oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes. The use of CO2 as an oxidant, coupled with carefully selected promoters, offers a more sustainable and selective approach compared to traditional O2-based systems.

Future research directions may include:

1. Exploration of novel promoter combinations: Investigating synergistic effects between different promoters (e.g., Ni-K or Ru-Ni combinations) could lead to further improvements in catalyst performance and stability.

2. In-depth mechanistic studies: Advanced characterization techniques could be employed to elucidate the precise mechanisms by which promoters enhance CO2 activation and influence the catalyst's redox properties.

3. Optimization of support materials: Development of new support materials or modification of existing ones to further enhance CO2 activation and promote the desired redox cycles.

4. Process intensification: Integration of membrane technology or other separation techniques to continuously remove products and shift equilibrium, potentially leading to higher conversions and yields.

5. Computational modeling: Utilizing density functional theory (DFT) and other computational methods to predict and design optimal promoter-catalyst combinations for specific reactions.

6. Scale-up studies: Investigating the performance of promoted catalysts under industrially relevant conditions to address potential challenges in large-scale applications.

7. Life cycle assessment: Conducting comprehensive environmental impact studies to quantify the potential benefits of CO2-based oxidative dehydrogenation processes compared to conventional methods.

By pursuing these research directions, it may be possible to develop even more efficient and sustainable catalytic systems for the production of valuable olefins from light alkanes, while simultaneously contributing to CO2 utilization efforts.

提高铬基催化体系中CO2的活化和氧化能力：挑战、策略和促进剂在改善性能和选择性方面的作用

二氧化碳（CO2）作为轻烷烃催化氧化脱氢制烯烃的潜在替代氧化剂，尤其是在铬基催化体系中，引起了广泛关注。然而，CO2的弱氧化还原电位和惰性特性带来了重大挑战，需要解决这些问题才能实现最佳的催化性能。本综述重点关注克服这些局限性的策略，特别强调了促进剂在增强CO2活化、提高氧化能力以及实现选择性和稳定催化性能方面的作用。

CO2作为氧化剂的挑战：
由于CO2的热力学稳定性，其氧化能力有限，难以使还原的CrOx物种重新氧化 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。此外，CO2相当惰性，不易被CrOx活化 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。这些限制导致与氧气相比，重新氧化的效率较低 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

增强CO2活化和氧化能力的策略：

1. 添加促进剂：
引入促进剂是促进CO2活化和提高其氧化能力的关键策略 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。已研究了几种类型的促进剂：

a) 镍（Ni）：
Ni表现出优异的CO2活化能力，即使在苛刻的反应条件下也是如此 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。它通过促进CO2解离为CO和吸附的O*来增强CO2的氧化能力，这些物种随后可以使还原的铬物种重新氧化 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。Ni促进的催化剂表现出更高的CO2转化率和稳定的丙烯产量 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

b) 碱金属氧化物：
由于其碱性特性，碱金属氧化物促进CO2在CO2加氢反应中的吸附和活化 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。钾（K）被认为是一种有效的促进剂，可以增强催化剂的稳定性和选择性 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

c) 钌（Ru）：
由于Ru在CO2加氢反应中具有高活性，可以增强整个反应过程，因此是一种潜在的促进剂 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

2. 载体效应：
载体材料的选择在增强CO2活化方面起着至关重要的作用。二氧化硅载体，尤其是在低铬负载量时，促进铬深度还原至Cr2+，这比Cr3+更容易被CO2重新氧化 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

3. 氧化还原循环机制：
在某些铬基催化剂中，如CrOx/MCM-41，逆水气变换（RWGS）反应通过氧化还原循环进行。这个循环涉及丙烷脱氢产生的氢将Cr3+还原为Cr2+，随后CO2将Cr2+重新氧化 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

相比O2共进料的优势：

1. 提高选择性：
与O2相比，CO2是一种较温和的氧化剂，它将氧化脱氢反应转向更多的烯烃产物，防止活性烯烃深度氧化 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。这导致了更高的目标产物选择性。

2. 稳定的催化性能：
像Ni和K这样的促进剂有助于在长时间内保持稳定的催化活性和选择性，而O2共进料可能导致选择性显著下降 [10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

3. 减少积碳形成：
CO2可以抑制积碳形成，在某些情况下，通过逆布道反应气化现有积碳 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。这有助于保持催化剂的稳定性并延长催化剂寿命。

4. 热力学平衡移动：
CO2通过RWGS反应消耗氢物种，这有利于增强轻烷烃转化的平衡 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。

5. 防止过度氧化：
CO2通过消耗过量氢气防止活性氧化铬物种的过度还原，使催化剂保持在活性状态 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。

综合理解与未来展望：
开发促进剂以增强铬基催化体系中CO2的活化和氧化能力代表了轻烷烃氧化脱氢领域的重大进展。使用CO2作为氧化剂，结合精心选择的促进剂，与传统的O2基体系相比，提供了一种更可持续和选择性的方法。

未来研究方向可能包括：

1. 探索新型促进剂组合：研究不同促进剂之间的协同效应（例如，Ni-K或Ru-Ni组合）可能导致催化剂性能和稳定性的进一步提高。

2. 深入的机理研究：采用先进的表征技术阐明促进剂增强CO2活化和影响催化剂氧化还原性能的精确机制。

3. 优化载体材料：开发新的载体材料或修改现有载体，以进一步增强CO2活化并促进所需的氧化还原循环。

4. 过程强化：集成膜技术或其他分离技术，持续去除产品并移动平衡，可能导致更高的转化率和产率。

5. 计算机模拟：利用密度泛函理论（DFT）和其他计算方法预测和设计特定反应的最佳促进剂-催化剂组合。

6. 放大研究：在工业相关条件下研究促进催化剂的性能，以解决大规模应用中的潜在挑战。

7. 生命周期评估：进行全面的环境影响研究，量化基于CO2的氧化脱氢过程与传统方法相比的潜在益处。

通过追求这些研究方向，可能开发出更高效、更可持续的催化体系，用于从轻烷烃生产有价值的烯烃，同时为CO2利用努力做出贡献。
