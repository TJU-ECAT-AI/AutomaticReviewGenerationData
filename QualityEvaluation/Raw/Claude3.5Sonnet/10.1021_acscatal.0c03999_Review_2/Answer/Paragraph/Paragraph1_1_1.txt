Enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems: Challenges, strategies, and the role of promoters in improving performance and selectivity

Carbon dioxide (CO2) has emerged as a promising alternative oxidant for catalytic oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes to olefins, offering several advantages over conventional oxygen-based systems. However, CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness pose significant challenges in chromium-based catalytic systems. This review examines the strategies employed to address these limitations, with a focus on the role of promoters in enhancing CO2 activation, improving oxidizing ability, and achieving selective and stable catalytic performance.

1. Challenges of CO2 as an oxidant:
CO2 has limited oxidizing ability due to its thermodynamically stable properties, making it challenging to reoxidize reduced CrOx species [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. Its weak oxidation potential results in lower efficiency of reoxidation compared to oxygen [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]. Additionally, CO2 is quite inert and not easily activated by CrOx [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

2. Advantages of CO2 over O2:
Despite these challenges, CO2 offers several benefits as an oxidant:
a) Lower oxidizing activity, which shifts the reaction towards more olefin production by preventing deep oxidation [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].
b) Ability to consume hydrogen through the reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) reaction, enhancing the equilibrium of light alkane conversion [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].
c) Potential to oxidize active sites and stabilize the catalyst by eliminating coke through the reverse Boudouard reaction [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].
d) Inhibition of coke formation rather than elimination of existing coke [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].

3. Strategies to enhance CO2 activation and oxidizing ability:
Several approaches have been developed to address CO2's limitations in chromium-based catalytic systems:

a) Promoters:
- Nickel (Ni): Ni-promoted CrOx/SBA-15 catalysts show excellent ability to activate CO2, even under harsh reaction conditions. Ni enhances the oxidizing ability of CO2 to reoxidize reduced Cr2+ to active Cr3+ and Cr6+ states [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
- Alkali metal oxides: Due to their basic properties, these promoters facilitate CO2 adsorption and activation in CO2 hydrogenation reactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].
- Ruthenium (Ru): Ru is a potential promoter due to its high activity in CO2 hydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].
- Potassium (K): K-promoted chromium oxide catalysts show increased stability and selectivity [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

b) Support effects:
- Using supports like silica that promote deep reduction of chromium to Cr2+, especially at low chromium loadings, allows CO2 to more easily reoxidize the chromium [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].
- The boundaries between Cr2O3 and SiO2 particles play an important role in the promoting effect of CO2 [10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4].

c) Redox cycle mechanisms:
- In CrOx/MCM-41 catalysts, the RWGS reaction proceeds via a redox cycle. Hydrogen from propane dehydrogenation reduces Cr3+ to Cr2+, which is then reoxidized by CO2 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].
- CO2 regenerates Cr6+O4 tetrahedra from reduced Cr3+O6 octahedra, creating a reduction-oxidation cycle crucial for the dehydrogenation reaction [10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4].

4. Advantages of promoters over O2 co-feeding:
While O2 co-feeding can improve oxidizing power, it often leads to decreased selectivity [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. Promoters offer several advantages:
a) Maintained or improved selectivity while enhancing catalytic performance [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
b) Reduced deactivation rates and more stable catalytic activity over time [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
c) Higher CO2 conversion rates, indicating more efficient use of the CO2 reactant [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
d) Stable propylene production due to the combination of higher CO2 conversion and maintained selectivity [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
e) No trade-off between activity and selectivity, unlike O2 co-feeding which improves activity at the cost of selectivity [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

In conclusion, the use of promoters in chromium-based catalytic systems offers a promising approach to enhance CO2 activation and oxidizing ability while maintaining selectivity and stability. This strategy provides a more balanced and efficient solution compared to O2 co-feeding, addressing the limitations of CO2 as an oxidant in propane dehydrogenation reactions.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The development of promoters for CO2 activation in chromium-based catalytic systems represents a significant step towards more sustainable and efficient propane dehydrogenation processes. The ability to utilize CO2 as a mild oxidant not only addresses environmental concerns but also offers unique advantages in terms of selectivity and stability.

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Synergistic effects of multiple promoters: Investigating combinations of promoters (e.g., Ni with alkali metals) could lead to catalysts with enhanced performance across multiple parameters.

2. Advanced characterization techniques: Employing in-situ and operando spectroscopic methods to gain deeper insights into the mechanisms of CO2 activation and the role of promoters under reaction conditions.

3. Computational modeling: Utilizing density functional theory (DFT) and machine learning approaches to predict and design optimal promoter-catalyst combinations for specific reaction conditions.

4. Novel support materials: Exploring advanced support materials, such as metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) or 2D materials, to further enhance CO2 activation and catalyst stability.

5. Process intensification: Investigating the potential of membrane reactors or other process intensification techniques to continuously remove products and shift equilibrium, potentially enhancing the effectiveness of CO2 as an oxidant.

6. Broader application: Extending the insights gained from propane dehydrogenation to other reactions where CO2 could serve as a mild oxidant, such as the oxidative coupling of methane or other alkane activation processes.

By pursuing these research directions, we can expect to develop more efficient and sustainable catalytic systems that not only improve propane dehydrogenation processes but also contribute to the broader goal of CO2 utilization in chemical transformations.

提高铬基催化体系中CO2的活化和氧化能力：挑战、策略和促进剂在改善性能和选择性方面的作用

二氧化碳（CO2）已成为轻烷烃到烯烃催化氧化脱氢的一种有前景的替代氧化剂，相比传统的氧气体系具有几个优势。然而，CO2的弱氧化能力和惰性在铬基催化体系中带来了重大挑战。本综述考察了解决这些限制的策略，重点关注促进剂在增强CO2活化、改善氧化能力以及实现选择性和稳定催化性能方面的作用。

1. CO2作为氧化剂的挑战：
由于其热力学稳定性，CO2的氧化能力有限，使得还原态CrOx物种的再氧化变得具有挑战性[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。其弱氧化能力导致再氧化效率低于氧气[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。此外，CO2相当惰性，不易被CrOx活化[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

2. CO2相对于O2的优势：
尽管存在这些挑战，CO2作为氧化剂仍具有几个优点：
a) 较低的氧化活性，这使反应向更多烯烃产物方向偏移，防止深度氧化[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。
b) 通过逆水气变换（RWGS）反应消耗氢气，增强轻烷烃转化的平衡[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。
c) 通过逆布道反应氧化活性位点并稳定催化剂，从而消除积碳的潜力[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。
d) 抑制积碳形成而不是消除现有积碳[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。

3. 增强CO2活化和氧化能力的策略：
已开发了几种方法来解决铬基催化体系中CO2的局限性：

a) 促进剂：
- 镍（Ni）：Ni促进的CrOx/SBA-15催化剂表现出优异的CO2活化能力，即使在苛刻的反应条件下也是如此。Ni增强了CO2的氧化能力，可以将还原态Cr2+再氧化为活性Cr3+和Cr6+态[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
- 碱金属氧化物：由于其碱性特性，这些促进剂促进CO2在CO2加氢反应中的吸附和活化[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。
- 钌（Ru）：由于其在CO2加氢中的高活性，Ru是一种潜在的促进剂[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。
- 钾（K）：K促进的铬氧化物催化剂显示出增加的稳定性和选择性[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

b) 载体效应：
- 使用如二氧化硅这样的载体，特别是在低铬负载量时，促进铬深度还原为Cr2+，使CO2更容易再氧化铬[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。
- Cr2O3和SiO2颗粒之间的界面在CO2的促进效应中起重要作用[10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4]。

c) 氧化还原循环机制：
- 在CrOx/MCM-41催化剂中，RWGS反应通过氧化还原循环进行。丙烷脱氢产生的氢气将Cr3+还原为Cr2+，然后被CO2再氧化[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。
- CO2将还原态的Cr3+O6八面体再生为Cr6+O4四面体，形成对脱氢反应至关重要的氧化还原循环[10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4]。

4. 促进剂相对于O2共进料的优势：
虽然O2共进料可以提高氧化能力，但往往导致选择性降低[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。促进剂提供了几个优势：
a) 在提高催化性能的同时保持或改善选择性[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
b) 降低失活速率，长期内催化活性更稳定[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
c) 更高的CO2转化率，表明CO2反应物的利用更有效[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
d) 由于CO2转化率提高和选择性保持，丙烯产量稳定[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
e) 活性和选择性之间没有权衡，不像O2共进料那样以选择性为代价提高活性[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

总之，在铬基催化体系中使用促进剂提供了一种有前景的方法，可以增强CO2活化和氧化能力，同时保持选择性和稳定性。与O2共进料相比，这种策略提供了一个更平衡、更有效的解决方案，解决了CO2作为丙烷脱氢反应中氧化剂的局限性。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：
开发铬基催化体系中CO2活化的促进剂代表着向更可持续和高效的丙烷脱氢过程迈出的重要一步。利用CO2作为温和氧化剂的能力不仅解决了环境问题，还在选择性和稳定性方面提供了独特的优势。

未来研究方向可能集中在：

1. 多种促进剂的协同效应：研究促进剂的组合（如镍与碱金属）可能会导致在多个参数上性能增强的催化剂。

2. 先进表征技术：采用原位和操作中光谱方法，以深入了解反应条件下CO2活化机理和促进剂的作用。

3. 计算模拟：利用密度泛函理论（DFT）和机器学习方法预测和设计特定反应条件下最佳的促进剂-催化剂组合。

4. 新型载体材料：探索先进载体材料，如金属有机骨架（MOFs）或二维材料，以进一步增强CO2活化和催化剂稳定性。

5. 过程强化：研究膜反应器或其他过程强化技术的潜力，以持续去除产物并移动平衡，可能增强CO2作为氧化剂的有效性。

6. 更广泛的应用：将从丙烷脱氢获得的见解扩展到其他可能使用CO2作为温和氧化剂的反应，如甲烷氧化偶联或其他烷烃活化过程。

通过追求这些研究方向，我们可以期待开发出更高效、更可持续的催化体系，不仅改进丙烷脱氢过程，还为更广泛的CO2在化学转化中的利用目标做出贡献。
