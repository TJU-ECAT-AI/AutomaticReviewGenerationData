Enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems: Challenges, strategies, and the role of promoters in improving performance and selectivity

Carbon dioxide (CO2) has emerged as a promising alternative oxidant for catalytic oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes to olefins, offering several advantages over conventional oxidants like molecular oxygen (O2). However, CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness present significant challenges in chromium-based catalytic systems. This review examines the strategies employed to overcome these limitations, with a particular focus on the role of promoters in enhancing CO2 activation, improving oxidizing ability, and achieving selective and stable catalytic performance.

Challenges of CO2 as an oxidant:
1. Weak oxidation potential: CO2 has limited oxidizing ability in terms of reoxidation of reduced CrOx species, due to its thermodynamically stable properties [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
2. Inertness: CO2 is quite inert and not easily activated by CrOx [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

Strategies to enhance CO2 activation and oxidizing ability:

1. Promoter addition:
a) Nickel (Ni): Ni-promoted CrOx/SBA-15 catalysts have shown excellent ability to activate CO2, even under harsh reaction conditions [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. Ni enhances the oxidizing ability of CO2 to reoxidize reduced Cr2+ to active Cr3+ and further to Cr6+ [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

b) Alkali metal oxides: Due to their basic properties, alkali metal oxides like potassium (K) promote CO2 adsorption and activation in CO2 hydrogenation reactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]. The optimum potassium content in 5% CrOx/SiO2 catalysts (0.2-0.4%) resulted in 100% selectivity for propene formation at 22% propane conversion [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

c) Ruthenium (Ru): Ru is a potential promoter due to its high activity in CO2 hydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

2. Support effects:
Silica supports, especially at low chromium loadings, promote deep reduction of chromium to Cr2+, which is more easily reoxidized by CO2 than Cr3+ [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

3. Redox cycle mechanisms:
In CrOx/MCM-41 catalysts, the reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) reaction proceeds via a redox cycle. Hydrogen from propane dehydrogenation reduces Cr3+ to Cr2+, which is then reoxidized by CO2 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

Advantages of CO2 with promoters over O2 co-feeding:

1. Improved selectivity: CO2's lower oxidizing activity compared to O2 shifts the reaction towards more olefin production by preventing deep oxidation of reactive olefins [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]. Promoters like Ni maintain stable selectivity while improving catalytic performance [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

2. Enhanced stability: Promoters, especially potassium, increase the stability of chromium oxide catalysts, allowing for longer catalyst lifetimes and sustained performance [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

3. Controlled oxidation: CO2 provides a milder oxidizing environment compared to O2, allowing for better control of the reaction when combined with promoters [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

4. Reduced coke formation: CO2 can inhibit coke formation and, in some cases, eliminate coke through the reverse Boudouard reaction [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].

5. Improved catalyst lifetime: By reducing coke formation and maintaining active sites, promoters help extend catalyst life compared to O2 co-feeding, which can lead to faster deactivation [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

6. Environmental benefits: CO2 utilization in this process can be environmentally beneficial, especially when using CO2 from waste gases [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

In conclusion, the use of promoters with CO2 in chromium-based catalytic systems for propane dehydrogenation offers a more selective, stable, and controlled catalytic performance compared to O2 co-feeding. These strategies effectively address the limitations of CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness while maintaining high selectivity and stability in the catalytic process.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of promoters for enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems represents a significant advance in the field of oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes. This approach not only addresses the challenges associated with CO2 as an oxidant but also offers potential environmental benefits through CO2 utilization.

Future research directions may include:

1. Exploring synergistic effects of multiple promoters: Combining promoters with complementary functions (e.g., Ni for CO2 activation and K for selectivity enhancement) could lead to further improvements in catalyst performance.

2. Investigating novel support materials: Development of supports that can further enhance CO2 activation and promote the desired redox cycles could lead to more efficient catalytic systems.

3. In-situ characterization studies: Advanced spectroscopic techniques could provide deeper insights into the mechanisms of CO2 activation and the role of promoters under reaction conditions.

4. Computational modeling: Theoretical studies could help predict optimal promoter combinations and guide catalyst design.

5. Scale-up and process integration: Evaluating the performance of promoted catalysts in pilot-scale reactors and integrating CO2 capture technologies could pave the way for industrial implementation.

6. Life cycle assessment: Comprehensive studies on the environmental impact of using CO2 as an oxidant in these processes could quantify the potential benefits and identify areas for improvement.

These research directions could lead to the development of more efficient and sustainable catalytic systems for the production of valuable olefins while simultaneously addressing the global challenge of CO2 utilization.

提高铬基催化体系中CO2的活化和氧化能力：挑战、策略和促进剂在提高性能和选择性方面的作用

二氧化碳（CO2）作为轻烷烃催化氧化脱氢制烯烃的一种有前景的非常规氧化剂，相比分子氧（O2）等传统氧化剂具有多种优势。然而，CO2的弱氧化潜能和惰性在铬基催化体系中提出了重大挑战。本综述考察了克服这些局限性的策略，特别关注促进剂在增强CO2活化、提高氧化能力以及实现选择性和稳定催化性能方面的作用。

CO2作为氧化剂的挑战：
1. 弱氧化潜能：由于其热力学稳定性，CO2在还原CrOx物种的再氧化方面的氧化能力有限 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
2. 惰性：CO2相当惰性，不易被CrOx活化 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

增强CO2活化和氧化能力的策略：

1. 添加促进剂：
a) 镍（Ni）：Ni促进的CrOx/SBA-15催化剂即使在苛刻的反应条件下也表现出优异的CO2活化能力 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。Ni增强了CO2的氧化能力，可以将还原态的Cr2+再氧化为活性Cr3+，并进一步氧化为Cr6+ [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

b) 碱金属氧化物：由于其碱性特性，钾（K）等碱金属氧化物促进了CO2在CO2加氢反应中的吸附和活化 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。在5% CrOx/SiO2催化剂中，最佳钾含量（0.2-0.4%）在22%丙烷转化率下实现了100%的丙烯选择性 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

c) 钌（Ru）：由于在CO2加氢反应中具有高活性，Ru是一种潜在的促进剂 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

2. 载体效应：
硅胶载体，特别是在低铬负载量时，促进铬深度还原为Cr2+，这比Cr3+更容易被CO2再氧化 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

3. 氧化还原循环机制：
在CrOx/MCM-41催化剂中，逆水气变换（RWGS）反应通过氧化还原循环进行。丙烷脱氢产生的氢将Cr3+还原为Cr2+，然后被CO2再氧化 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

CO2与促进剂相比O2共进料的优势：

1. 提高选择性：与O2相比，CO2的氧化活性较低，通过防止活性烯烃的深度氧化，使反应向更多烯烃生成方向转移 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。像Ni这样的促进剂在提高催化性能的同时保持稳定的选择性 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

2. 增强稳定性：促进剂，特别是钾，增加了铬氧化物催化剂的稳定性，允许更长的催化剂寿命和持续的性能 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

3. 控制氧化：与O2相比，CO2提供了更温和的氧化环境，与促进剂结合时能更好地控制反应 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

4. 减少积碳形成：CO2可以抑制积碳形成，在某些情况下，通过逆布道反应消除积碳 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。

5. 提高催化剂寿命：通过减少积碳形成和维持活性位，促进剂有助于延长催化剂寿命，相比之下，O2共进料可能导致更快的失活 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

6. 环境效益：在这个过程中利用CO2可以带来环境效益，特别是当使用废气中的CO2时 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

综上所述，在铬基催化体系中使用促进剂和CO2进行丙烷脱氢，相比O2共进料提供了更具选择性、稳定性和可控性的催化性能。这些策略有效地解决了CO2弱氧化潜能和惰性的限制，同时在催化过程中保持高选择性和稳定性。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：开发促进剂以增强CO2在铬基催化体系中的活化和氧化能力，代表了轻烷烃氧化脱氢领域的重大进展。这种方法不仅解决了CO2作为氧化剂的挑战，还通过CO2利用提供了潜在的环境效益。

未来研究方向可能包括：

1. 探索多种促进剂的协同效应：结合具有互补功能的促进剂（例如，用于CO2活化的Ni和用于提高选择性的K）可能会进一步改善催化剂性能。

2. 研究新型载体材料：开发能进一步增强CO2活化和促进所需氧化还原循环的载体，可能导致更高效的催化体系。

3. 原位表征研究：先进的光谱技术可以在反应条件下对CO2活化机理和促进剂作用提供更深入的见解。

4. 计算机模拟：理论研究可以帮助预测最佳促进剂组合并指导催化剂设计。

5. 放大和过程集成：评估促进催化剂在中试规模反应器中的性能，并整合CO2捕获技术，可为工业实施铺平道路。

6. 生命周期评估：对这些过程中使用CO2作为氧化剂的环境影响进行全面研究，可以量化潜在效益并确定改进领域。

这些研究方向可能导致开发出更高效、更可持续的催化体系，用于生产有价值的烯烃，同时解决CO2利用的全球挑战。
