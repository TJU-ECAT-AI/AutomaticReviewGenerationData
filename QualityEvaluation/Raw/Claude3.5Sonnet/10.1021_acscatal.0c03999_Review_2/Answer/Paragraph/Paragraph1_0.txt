Promoters play a crucial role in addressing the limitations of CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness in chromium-based catalytic systems for propane dehydrogenation. They enhance CO2 activation and improve its oxidizing ability, offering several advantages over alternative strategies like O2 co-feeding:

1. Enhanced CO2 activation: Promoters like Ni and Ru have excellent ability to activate CO2, even under harsh reaction conditions. Ni, for instance, dissociates CO2 into CO and activated oxygen (O*ads), which can then regenerate reduced chromium species [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. This overcomes CO2's inherent inertness and low reactivity with CrOx [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

2. Improved oxidizing ability: Promoters enhance the oxidizing ability of CO2, allowing it to reoxidize reduced Cr2+ to the active Cr3+ state and further to Cr6+ [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. This helps maintain the active sites of the catalyst and addresses the limitation of CO2's weak oxidation potential.

3. Stable selectivity: Unlike O2 co-feeding, which can significantly decrease selectivity for propylene, promoter-enhanced CO2 systems maintain stable selectivity while improving catalytic performance [10.3103/S0361521912010089]. For example, optimum potassium content in CrOx/SiO2 catalysts resulted in 100% selectivity for propene formation at 22% propane conversion.

4. Reduced deactivation: Promoter-enhanced catalysts show decreased deactivation rates compared to unpromoted catalysts, leading to more stable catalytic activity over time [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. This is particularly important for maintaining long-term catalyst performance.

5. Higher CO2 conversion: Promoted catalysts demonstrate higher CO2 conversion rates, indicating more efficient use of the CO2 reactant [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

6. Coke suppression: The introduction of CO2 as an oxidizing agent, coupled with promoters, helps decrease coke deposition on the catalyst surface [10.3103/S0361521912010089]. This is crucial for maintaining catalyst activity and stability.

7. Thermodynamic equilibrium shift: Promoters help shift the thermodynamic equilibrium of the dehydrogenation reaction towards olefin formation by facilitating CO2's interaction with hydrogen [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

8. Tuning of catalyst properties: Promoters can modify the surface properties of the catalyst, such as redox potential and surface acidity, allowing for fine-tuning of catalytic performance [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

Compared to O2 co-feeding, CO2 with promoters offers several key advantages:

1. Higher selectivity: CO2 is less likely to cause deep oxidation of paraffins and olefins compared to O2 [10.1016/j.catcom.2006.08.005].

2. Better stability: Catalysts promoted with alkali metals like potassium show improved stability over longer periods [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

3. More controlled oxidation: CO2 provides a milder oxidizing environment compared to O2, allowing for better control of the reaction when combined with promoters [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].

4. Improved catalyst lifetime: By reducing coke formation, promoters help extend catalyst life compared to O2 co-feeding, which can lead to faster deactivation [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

5. Environmental benefits: CO2 utilization in this process can be environmentally beneficial, especially when using CO2 from waste gases [10.1016/j.catcom.2006.08.005].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The use of promoters in CO2-based chromium catalytic systems for propane dehydrogenation represents a significant advancement in addressing the limitations of CO2 as an oxidant. This approach not only enhances the catalytic performance but also offers environmental benefits through CO2 utilization. 

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Developing novel multi-functional promoters that can simultaneously enhance CO2 activation, improve oxidizing ability, and suppress coke formation.

2. Investigating the synergistic effects of combining different types of promoters (e.g., alkali metals with transition metals) to further optimize catalyst performance.

3. Exploring the potential of advanced characterization techniques to gain deeper insights into the mechanistic role of promoters in CO2 activation and chromium redox cycles.

4. Studying the long-term stability and regeneration of promoted catalysts under industrial-like conditions to assess their viability for large-scale applications.

5. Investigating the potential of promoter-enhanced CO2-based systems for other dehydrogenation reactions or related processes, expanding the scope of this approach beyond propane dehydrogenation.

6. Developing strategies to integrate these catalytic systems with CO2 capture technologies, creating a more sustainable and circular approach to olefin production.

These research directions could lead to the development of more efficient and environmentally friendly catalytic processes for olefin production, contributing to the broader goals of CO2 utilization and sustainable chemical manufacturing.

促进剂在解决基于铬的催化系统中CO2氧化能力弱和惰性的问题上起着关键作用。它们增强了CO2的活化能力，改善了其氧化能力，与O2共进料等替代策略相比具有几个优势：

1. 增强CO2活化：Ni和Ru等促进剂具有出色的CO2活化能力，即使在苛刻的反应条件下也是如此。例如，Ni将CO2解离成CO和活化氧（O*ads），然后可以再生还原的铬物种 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。这克服了CO2固有的惰性和与CrOx的低反应性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

2. 改善氧化能力：促进剂增强了CO2的氧化能力，使其能够将还原的Cr2+重新氧化为活性Cr3+状态，甚至进一步氧化为Cr6+ [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。这有助于维持催化剂的活性位点，并解决了CO2氧化能力弱的限制。

3. 稳定的选择性：与可能显著降低丙烯选择性的O2共进料不同，促进剂增强的CO2系统在改善催化性能的同时保持稳定的选择性 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。例如，在CrOx/SiO2催化剂中的最佳钾含量下，在22%的丙烷转化率下实现了100%的丙烯形成选择性。

4. 降低失活：与未促进的催化剂相比，促进剂增强的催化剂显示出较低的失活率，导致更稳定的长期催化活性 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。这对于维持催化剂的长期性能特别重要。

5. 更高的CO2转化率：促进的催化剂表现出更高的CO2转化率，表明CO2反应物的利用更加高效 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

6. 抑制积碳：将CO2作为氧化剂引入，再加上促进剂，有助于减少催化剂表面的积碳沉积 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。这对于维持催化剂活性和稳定性至关重要。

7. 热力学平衡移动：促进剂通过促进CO2与氢的相互作用，帮助将脱氢反应的热力学平衡向烯烃形成方向移动 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

8. 调节催化剂性质：促进剂可以修改催化剂的表面性质，如氧化还原电位和表面酸度，从而允许对催化性能进行微调 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

与O2共进料相比，CO2与促进剂的组合提供了几个关键优势：

1. 更高的选择性：与O2相比，CO2不太可能导致烷烃和烯烃的深度氧化 [10.1016/j.catcom.2006.08.005]。

2. 更好的稳定性：用钾等碱金属促进的催化剂显示出更长时间的改善稳定性 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

3. 更可控的氧化：与O2相比，CO2提供了更温和的氧化环境，当与促进剂结合时，可以更好地控制反应 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。

4. 改善的催化剂寿命：通过减少积碳形成，促进剂有助于延长催化剂寿命，相比之下，O2共进料可能导致更快的失活 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

5. 环境效益：在这个过程中利用CO2可以带来环境效益，特别是当使用废气中的CO2时 [10.1016/j.catcom.2006.08.005]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：在基于CO2的铬催化系统中使用促进剂进行丙烷脱氢反应代表了解决CO2作为氧化剂局限性的重大进展。这种方法不仅提高了催化性能，还通过CO2利用提供了环境效益。

未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 开发新型多功能促进剂，可以同时增强CO2活化、改善氧化能力和抑制积碳形成。

2. 研究不同类型促进剂（如碱金属与过渡金属）组合的协同效应，以进一步优化催化剂性能。

3. 探索先进表征技术的潜力，以深入了解促进剂在CO2活化和铬氧化还原循环中的机理作用。

4. 在类工业条件下研究促进的催化剂的长期稳定性和再生能力，以评估其大规模应用的可行性。

5. 研究促进剂增强的基于CO2系统在其他脱氢反应或相关过程中的潜力，扩大这种方法在丙烷脱氢之外的应用范围。

6. 开发将这些催化系统与CO2捕获技术相结合的策略，创造一种更可持续和循环的烯烃生产方法。

这些研究方向可能导致开发出更高效和环境友好的烯烃生产催化过程，为CO2利用和可持续化学品制造的更广泛目标做出贡献。
