Enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems: Challenges, strategies, and the role of promoters in improving performance and selectivity

Carbon dioxide (CO2) has emerged as a promising alternative oxidant for catalytic oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes to olefins, offering several advantages over conventional oxygen (O2) co-feeding. However, CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness pose significant challenges in chromium-based catalytic systems. To address these limitations, researchers have explored various strategies, including the use of promoters, to enhance CO2 activation and improve its oxidizing ability while maintaining selectivity and stability.

CO2 as an oxidant in chromium-based systems:
CO2 offers several advantages over O2 in chromium-based catalytic systems for propane dehydrogenation:

1. Milder oxidation: CO2's lower oxidizing activity shifts the reaction towards more olefin production by preventing deep oxidation of reactive olefins [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].

2. Improved selectivity: The use of CO2 increases process selectivity compared to O2 [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

3. Enhanced thermodynamics: CO2 helps overcome thermodynamic equilibrium limitations by removing hydrogen through the reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) reaction, improving propylene yield [10.1016/j.catcom.2006.08.005].

4. Coke mitigation: CO2 can inhibit coke formation and stabilize the catalyst through the reverse Boudouard reaction [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].

5. Active site preservation: CO2 can prevent the over-reduction of active oxidative chromium species by consuming excess hydrogen [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].

Challenges of using CO2:
Despite these advantages, CO2 presents several challenges in chromium-based catalytic systems:

1. Weak oxidation potential: CO2 has limited oxidizing ability for reoxidation of reduced CrOx species due to its thermodynamically stable properties [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

2. Inertness: CO2 is not easily activated by CrOx, limiting its effectiveness as an oxidant [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

3. Competitive adsorption: CO2 can competitively adsorb on catalyst basic sites, potentially negatively affecting performance [10.1021/cr5002436].

4. Potential coke formation: CO2 co-feeding may result in additional coke deposition due to increased CO and decreased H2 partial pressures from the RWGS reaction [10.1021/cr5002436].

Strategies to enhance CO2 activation and oxidizing ability:
To address these challenges, several strategies have been developed:

1. Promoter addition: Introducing promoters such as Ni, K, and Ru can facilitate CO2 activation and improve its oxidizing ability [10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

2. Support optimization: Using supports like silica that promote deep reduction of chromium to Cr2+, especially at low chromium loadings, allows CO2 to more easily reoxidize the chromium [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

3. Redox cycle exploitation: Utilizing the redox cycle between Cr6+ and Cr3+ species, where CO2 regenerates the active Cr6+O4 tetrahedra from reduced Cr3+O6 octahedra [10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4].

4. Low chromium content: Catalysts with low chromium content promote deeper reduction to Cr2+ species, which are more easily reoxidized by CO2 than Cr3+ [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

Role of promoters in improving performance and selectivity:
Promoters play a crucial role in addressing the limitations of CO2 in chromium-based catalytic systems:

1. Enhanced CO2 activation: Promoters like Ni and Ru facilitate CO2 dissociation and activation, even under harsh reaction conditions [10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

2. Improved oxidizing ability: Promoters enhance CO2's ability to reoxidize reduced chromium species, maintaining the active sites of the catalyst [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

3. Stable selectivity: Unlike O2 co-feeding, which can significantly decrease selectivity for propylene, promoter-enhanced CO2 systems maintain stable selectivity while improving catalytic performance [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

4. Reduced deactivation: Promoted catalysts show decreased deactivation rates compared to unpromoted catalysts, leading to more stable catalytic activity over time [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

5. Higher CO2 conversion: Promoted catalysts demonstrate higher CO2 conversion rates, indicating more efficient use of the CO2 reactant [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

6. Optimized surface properties: Promoters can modify the surface properties of the catalyst, such as redox potential and surface acidity, allowing for fine-tuning of catalytic performance [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

In conclusion, the use of promoters in CO2-based chromium catalytic systems offers a more balanced approach to enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability while maintaining selectivity and stability. This strategy provides significant advantages over O2 co-feeding in terms of reaction control, product selectivity, and catalyst longevity.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The development of promoter-enhanced CO2 activation in chromium-based catalytic systems represents a significant advancement in the field of oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes. This approach not only addresses the limitations of CO2 as an oxidant but also offers a more environmentally friendly alternative to conventional O2-based processes.

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Rational design of multi-functional promoters: Combining the beneficial properties of different promoters (e.g., Ni for CO2 activation and K for selectivity enhancement) could lead to synergistic effects and further improvements in catalyst performance.

2. In-depth mechanistic studies: Advanced spectroscopic techniques and computational modeling could provide deeper insights into the precise roles of promoters in CO2 activation and chromium redox cycles, guiding the design of more efficient catalytic systems.

3. Novel support materials: Exploration of advanced support materials, such as metal-organic frameworks or 2D materials, could offer unique properties for enhancing CO2 activation and controlling chromium species distribution.

4. Process intensification: Integration of membrane technology or microreactor systems with promoted chromium catalysts could lead to more efficient CO2 utilization and improved product yields.

5. Catalyst regeneration strategies: Development of in-situ regeneration techniques specific to CO2-based systems could further extend catalyst lifetime and reduce operational costs.

6. Broadening substrate scope: Applying the lessons learned from propane dehydrogenation to other light alkanes or even more complex hydrocarbons could expand the applicability of this technology.

7. Industrial scale-up studies: Investigating the challenges and opportunities of scaling up these promoted catalytic systems will be crucial for their eventual commercial implementation.

By pursuing these research directions, the scientific community can work towards more sustainable and efficient processes for olefin production, contributing to the broader goals of carbon utilization and green chemistry.

提高铬基催化体系中CO2的活化和氧化能力：挑战、策略和促进剂在提高性能和选择性方面的作用

二氧化碳（CO2）已成为催化氧化脱氢轻烷烃制烯烃的一种有前景的替代氧化剂，相比传统的氧气（O2）共馈，具有多项优势。然而，CO2的弱氧化潜能和惰性在铬基催化体系中构成了重大挑战。为了解决这些限制，研究人员探索了各种策略，包括使用促进剂，以增强CO2的活化和改善其氧化能力，同时保持选择性和稳定性。

铬基体系中CO2作为氧化剂：
在铬基催化体系中，CO2用于丙烷脱氢反应具有以下优势：

1. 更温和的氧化：CO2较低的氧化活性使反应向更多烯烃产物方向转移，防止活性烯烃深度氧化[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。

2. 提高选择性：与O2相比，使用CO2可提高反应的选择性[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

3. 改善热力学：CO2通过逆水气变换（RWGS）反应消耗氢气，有助于克服热力学平衡限制，提高丙烯产率[10.1016/j.catcom.2006.08.005]。

4. 抑制积碳：CO2可以通过逆布多瓦反应抑制积碳形成并稳定催化剂[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。

5. 保护活性位点：CO2可以通过消耗过量氢气防止活性氧化铬物种过度还原[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。

使用CO2的挑战：
尽管具有这些优势，CO2在铬基催化体系中仍然面临几个挑战：

1. 弱氧化潜能：由于其热力学稳定性，CO2对还原态CrOx物种的再氧化能力有限[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

2. 惰性：CO2不易被CrOx活化，限制了其作为氧化剂的有效性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

3. 竞争性吸附：CO2可能会在催化剂的碱性位点上竞争性吸附，可能对性能产生负面影响[10.1021/cr5002436]。

4. 潜在的积碳形成：由于RWGS反应导致CO增加和H2分压降低，CO2共馈可能会导致额外的积碳沉积[10.1021/cr5002436]。

增强CO2活化和氧化能力的策略：
为了应对这些挑战，已开发了几种策略：

1. 添加促进剂：引入Ni、K和Ru等促进剂可以促进CO2的活化并改善其氧化能力[10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

2. 优化载体：使用促进铬深度还原到Cr2+的载体（如二氧化硅），特别是在低铬含量时，使CO2更容易再氧化铬[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

3. 利用氧化还原循环：利用Cr6+和Cr3+物种之间的氧化还原循环，其中CO2将还原态的Cr3+O6八面体重新生成活性Cr6+O4四面体[10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4]。

4. 低铬含量：低铬含量的催化剂促进更深度还原到Cr2+物种，这些物种比Cr3+更容易被CO2再氧化[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

促进剂在提高性能和选择性方面的作用：
促进剂在解决铬基催化体系中CO2的局限性方面起着关键作用：

1. 增强CO2活化：Ni和Ru等促进剂即使在苛刻的反应条件下也能促进CO2的解离和活化[10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

2. 改善氧化能力：促进剂增强了CO2再氧化还原态铬物种的能力，维持催化剂的活性位点[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

3. 稳定选择性：与可能显著降低丙烯选择性的O2共馈不同，促进剂增强的CO2体系在改善催化性能的同时保持稳定的选择性[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

4. 减少失活：与未促进的催化剂相比，促进的催化剂显示出更低的失活率，导致更稳定的催化活性[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

5. 更高的CO2转化率：促进的催化剂表现出更高的CO2转化率，表明CO2反应物的利用更有效[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

6. 优化表面性质：促进剂可以修改催化剂的表面性质，如氧化还原电位和表面酸度，从而实现催化性能的精细调节[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

总之，在基于CO2的铬催化体系中使用促进剂提供了一种更平衡的方法来增强CO2活化和氧化能力，同时保持选择性和稳定性。这种策略在反应控制、产品选择性和催化剂寿命方面比O2共馈具有显著优势。
