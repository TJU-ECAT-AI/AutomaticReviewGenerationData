Enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems: Challenges, strategies, and the role of promoters in improving performance and selectivity

Carbon dioxide (CO2) has emerged as a promising alternative oxidant for catalytic oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes to olefins, offering several advantages over conventional oxygen-based systems. However, CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness present significant challenges in chromium-based catalytic systems. This review explores the strategies employed to address these limitations, with a focus on the role of promoters in enhancing CO2 activation, improving oxidizing ability, and achieving selective and stable catalytic performance.

Challenges of CO2 as an oxidant:
1. Weak oxidation potential: CO2 has limited oxidizing ability in reoxidizing reduced CrOx species due to its thermodynamically stable properties [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
2. Inertness: CO2 is not easily activated by CrOx, making it challenging to utilize effectively in the catalytic process [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

Strategies to enhance CO2 activation and oxidizing ability:

1. Promoter addition:
   a. Nickel (Ni): Ni-promoted CrOx/SBA-15 catalysts have shown excellent ability to activate CO2, even under harsh reaction conditions [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. Ni enhances the oxidizing ability of CO2 to reoxidize reduced Cr2+ to active Cr3+ and Cr6+ states [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
   b. Alkali metal oxides: Due to their basic properties, these promoters facilitate CO2 adsorption and activation in CO2 hydrogenation reactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].
   c. Ruthenium (Ru): Ru is a potential promoter due to its high activity in CO2 hydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].
   d. Potassium (K): K-promoted chromium oxide catalysts have shown improved stability and selectivity [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

2. Support effects:
   a. Silica support: The boundaries between Cr2O3 and SiO2 particles play an important role in the promoting effect of CO2 [10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4].
   b. MCM-41 support: Cr-MCM-41 catalysts demonstrate a redox cycle mechanism that facilitates CO2 activation [10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4].

3. Catalyst design:
   a. Low chromium content: Catalysts with low Cr content promote deeper reduction to Cr2+ species, which are more easily reoxidized by CO2 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].
   b. Bi-functional catalysts: Systems active for both dehydrogenation and reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) reactions show improved performance with CO2 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

Advantages of CO2 with promoters over O2 co-feeding:

1. Improved selectivity: CO2 is less likely to cause deep oxidation of olefins compared to O2, leading to higher selectivity [10.3103/S0361521912010089].
2. Enhanced stability: Promoted catalysts show improved stability and longer lifetimes compared to unpromoted systems or those using O2 co-feeding [10.3103/S0361521912010089].
3. Controlled oxidation: CO2 provides a milder oxidizing environment, allowing for better reaction control when combined with promoters [10.3103/S0361521912010089].
4. Reduced coke formation: CO2, along with promoters, helps decrease coke deposition on the catalyst surface [10.3103/S0361521912010089].
5. Environmental benefits: Utilization of CO2 in this process can be environmentally advantageous, especially when using CO2 from waste gases [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

Mechanism of CO2 activation and oxidation in promoted systems:

1. CO2 dissociation: Promoters like Ni facilitate CO2 dissociation to CO and adsorbed O*, which can then regenerate Cr3+ via reoxidation [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
2. Redox cycle: CO2 participates in redox cycles between Cr2+/Cr3+ and Cr3+/Cr6+, maintaining the active state of the catalyst [10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4].
3. RWGS reaction: CO2 consumes hydrogen species through the RWGS reaction, enhancing the equilibrium of light alkane conversion [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].
4. Coke inhibition: CO2 primarily inhibits coke formation rather than eliminating existing coke [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The development of promoters for enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems has shown significant promise in addressing the limitations of CO2 as an oxidant. The synergistic effects between promoters, CO2, and chromium active sites offer a pathway to achieve both high selectivity and stability in oxidative dehydrogenation reactions.

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Rational design of multi-component promoter systems that can further enhance CO2 activation while maintaining high selectivity.
2. In-depth mechanistic studies to elucidate the precise role of different promoters in CO2 activation and their interaction with chromium species.
3. Development of novel support materials that can facilitate CO2 activation and improve catalyst stability.
4. Exploration of the potential of other abundant and environmentally friendly oxidants that can complement or replace CO2 in these systems.
5. Integration of computational modeling and machine learning approaches to predict and optimize promoter-catalyst combinations for enhanced performance.
6. Investigation of the long-term stability and regeneration strategies for promoted chromium-based catalysts under industrial-like conditions.
7. Exploration of the potential for coupling CO2-based oxidative dehydrogenation with other CO2 utilization processes to create more sustainable and integrated chemical production systems.

By addressing these research directions, the field can move towards more efficient and sustainable catalytic systems for light alkane conversion, contributing to the broader goals of CO2 utilization and green chemistry.

提高铬基催化体系中CO2的活化和氧化能力：挑战、策略及促进剂在改善性能和选择性方面的作用

二氧化碳（CO2）已成为轻烷烃催化氧化脱氢制烯烃的一种有前途的替代氧化剂，相比传统的氧气基体系具有多种优势。然而，CO2的弱氧化能力和惰性在铬基催化体系中带来了重大挑战。本综述探讨了解决这些局限性的策略，重点关注促进剂在增强CO2活化、改善氧化能力以及实现选择性和稳定催化性能方面的作用。

CO2作为氧化剂的挑战：
1. 弱氧化能力：由于其热力学稳定性，CO2在重新氧化还原态CrOx物种方面的氧化能力有限 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
2. 惰性：CO2不易被CrOx活化，使其难以在催化过程中有效利用 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

增强CO2活化和氧化能力的策略：

1. 添加促进剂：
   a. 镍（Ni）：Ni促进的CrOx/SBA-15催化剂表现出优异的CO2活化能力，即使在苛刻的反应条件下也是如此 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。Ni增强了CO2的氧化能力，可以将还原态Cr2+重新氧化为活性Cr3+和Cr6+状态 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
   b. 碱金属氧化物：由于其碱性特性，这些促进剂有助于CO2在CO2加氢反应中的吸附和活化 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。
   c. 钌（Ru）：由于其在CO2加氢反应中的高活性，Ru是一种潜在的促进剂 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。
   d. 钾（K）：K促进的氧化铬催化剂表现出改善的稳定性和选择性 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

2. 载体效应：
   a. 二氧化硅载体：Cr2O3和SiO2颗粒之间的界面在CO2的促进作用中起着重要作用 [10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4]。
   b. MCM-41载体：Cr-MCM-41催化剂展示了有助于CO2活化的氧化还原循环机制 [10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4]。

3. 催化剂设计：
   a. 低铬含量：低Cr含量的催化剂促进更深度的还原至Cr2+物种，这些物种更易被CO2重新氧化 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。
   b. 双功能催化剂：同时活跃于脱氢和逆水气变换（RWGS）反应的体系在使用CO2时表现出改善的性能 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

CO2与促进剂相比O2共进料的优势：

1. 提高选择性：与O2相比，CO2不太可能导致烯烃的深度氧化，从而提高选择性 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。
2. 增强稳定性：促进的催化剂相比未促进的体系或使用O2共进料的体系表现出更好的稳定性和更长的寿命 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。
3. 可控氧化：CO2提供更温和的氧化环境，当与促进剂结合时允许更好的反应控制 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。
4. 减少积碳形成：CO2与促进剂一起有助于减少催化剂表面的积碳沉积 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。
5. 环境效益：在这一过程中利用CO2可能带来环境优势，特别是当使用废气中的CO2时 [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

促进体系中CO2活化和氧化的机理：

1. CO2解离：像Ni这样的促进剂促进CO2解离为CO和吸附的O*，后者可以通过重新氧化再生Cr3+ [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
2. 氧化还原循环：CO2参与Cr2+/Cr3+和Cr3+/Cr6+之间的氧化还原循环，维持催化剂的活性状态 [10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4]。
3. RWGS反应：CO2通过RWGS反应消耗氢物种，增强轻烷烃转化的平衡 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。
4. 抑制积碳：CO2主要抑制积碳形成，而不是消除现有积碳 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。

综合理解与未来展望：

开发促进剂以增强铬基催化体系中CO2的活化和氧化能力在解决CO2作为氧化剂的局限性方面显示出显著的前景。促进剂、CO2和铬活性位点之间的协同效应为在氧化脱氢反应中同时实现高选择性和稳定性提供了途径。

未来研究方向可能集中在：

1. 理性设计多组分促进剂体系，进一步增强CO2活化的同时保持高选择性。
2. 深入的机理研究，阐明不同促进剂在CO2活化中的精确作用及其与铬物种的相互作用。
3. 开发新型载体材料，促进CO2活化并提高催化剂稳定性。
4. 探索其他丰富和环境友好的氧化剂的潜力，这些氧化剂可以补充或替代这些体系中的CO2。
5. 整合计算模拟和机器学习方法，预测和优化促进剂-催化剂组合以提高性能。
6. 在类工业条件下研究促进的铬基催化剂的长期稳定性和再生策略。
7. 探索将基于CO2的氧化脱氢与其他CO2利用过程相结合的潜力，创建更可持续和集成的化学生产体系。

通过解决这些研究方向，该领域可以朝着更高效和可持续的轻烷烃转化催化体系迈进，为CO2利用和绿色化学的更广泛目标做出贡献。
