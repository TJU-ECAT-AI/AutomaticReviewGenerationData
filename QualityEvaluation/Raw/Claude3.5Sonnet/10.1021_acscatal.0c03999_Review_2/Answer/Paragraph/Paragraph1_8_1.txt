Enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems: Challenges, strategies, and the role of promoters in improving performance and selectivity

Carbon dioxide (CO2) has emerged as a promising alternative oxidant for catalytic oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes to olefins, offering several advantages over conventional oxygen-based systems. However, CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness pose significant challenges in chromium-based catalytic systems. This review explores the strategies employed to overcome these limitations, with a focus on the role of promoters in enhancing CO2 activation, improving oxidizing ability, and achieving selective and stable catalytic performance.

1. Challenges of CO2 as an oxidant:
CO2 has limited oxidizing ability due to its thermodynamically stable properties, making it difficult to reoxidize reduced CrOx species [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. While CO2 can participate in the redox cycles of Cr2+ ⇌ Cr3+ or Cr3+ ⇌ Cr6+, its oxidation potential is rather weak, and it is not easily activated by CrOx [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

2. Strategies to enhance CO2 activation and oxidizing ability:

2.1 Promoters:
The introduction of promoters is a key strategy to facilitate CO2 activation on Cr2O3 catalysts and improve its oxidizing ability for selective and stable performance [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

a) Nickel (Ni) promotion:
Ni-based promoters have shown excellent ability to activate CO2, even under harsh reaction conditions [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. Ni enhances the oxidizing ability of CO2 to reoxidize reduced Cr2+ to the active Cr3+ state and further to Cr6+ [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. The mechanism involves Ni dissociating CO2 to CO and O*ads, which regenerates the reduced Cr species through reoxidation [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

b) Alkali metal oxides:
Due to their basic properties, alkali metal oxides have been widely used as promoters for CO2 adsorption and activation in CO2 hydrogenation reactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]. Potassium (K) has been identified as an effective promoter, with optimum content ranging from 0.2-0.4% in 5% CrOx/SiO2 catalysts, resulting in 100% selectivity for propene formation at 22% propane conversion [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

c) Ruthenium (Ru):
Ru is a potential promoter due to its high activity in CO2 hydrogenation, which can enhance the overall reaction process [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

2.2 Support effects:
The choice of support material plays a crucial role in enhancing CO2 activation. Silica supports, especially at low chromium loadings, promote deep reduction of chromium to Cr2+, which is more easily reoxidized by CO2 than Cr3+ [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

2.3 Redox cycle mechanisms:
In systems like CrOx/MCM-41, the reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) reaction proceeds via a redox cycle, where hydrogen from propane dehydrogenation reduces Cr3+ to Cr2+, which is then reoxidized by CO2 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

3. Advantages of CO2 with promoters over O2 co-feeding:

3.1 Selectivity:
CO2, being a milder oxidant than O2, shifts the oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) reaction toward more olefin production by preventing deep oxidation of reactive olefins [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]. Promoters like K can further enhance selectivity, with some systems achieving 100% selectivity for propene formation [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

3.2 Stability:
Promoters, especially Ni and K, have been shown to increase the stability of chromium oxide catalysts, allowing for longer catalyst lifetimes and sustained performance over extended periods [10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

3.3 Coke suppression:
CO2 can inhibit coke formation and, in some cases, eliminate existing coke through the reverse Boudouard reaction [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]. The presence of CO2 prevents the formation of coke bands in Raman spectra, which appear when CO2 is removed and H2 concentration is increased [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026].

3.4 Thermodynamic advantages:
CO2 consumes hydrogen species through the RWGS reaction, which enhances the equilibrium of light alkane conversion [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]. This helps overcome thermodynamic limitations and improves olefin yield [10.1016/j.catcom.2006.08.005].

3.5 Balanced reactivity:
CO2 with promoters provides a more controlled oxidation environment compared to O2, allowing for better tuning of catalyst properties and optimization of activity and selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The use of promoters in CO2-assisted chromium-based catalytic systems for alkane dehydrogenation represents a promising approach to address the limitations of CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness. By enhancing CO2 activation and improving its oxidizing ability, promoters offer a more balanced and selective catalytic performance compared to O2 co-feeding strategies.

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Developing novel multi-functional promoters that can simultaneously enhance CO2 activation, improve selectivity, and suppress coke formation.

2. Investigating the synergistic effects of different promoters (e.g., combinations of Ni, K, and Ru) to optimize catalyst performance.

3. Exploring advanced characterization techniques to gain deeper insights into the mechanisms of CO2 activation and the role of promoters at the molecular level.

4. Designing catalysts with tailored pore structures and surface properties to maximize the interaction between CO2, promoters, and active chromium sites.

5. Integrating computational modeling with experimental studies to predict and optimize promoter effects on CO2 activation and catalyst performance.

6. Investigating the potential of other mild oxidants or co-feeds that can work synergistically with CO2 and promoters to further enhance catalytic performance.

7. Exploring the scalability and long-term stability of promoted catalysts under industrial conditions to bridge the gap between laboratory research and practical applications.

By addressing these research areas, the scientific community can work towards developing more efficient and sustainable catalytic systems for alkane dehydrogenation, contributing to the broader goals of CO2 utilization and green chemistry.

提高铬基催化系统中CO2的活化和氧化能力：挑战、策略及促进剂在改善性能和选择性方面的作用

二氧化碳（CO2）已成为轻烷烃催化氧化脱氢制烯烃的一种有前景的替代氧化剂，相比传统的氧气系统具有多项优势。然而，CO2的弱氧化能力和惰性在铬基催化系统中带来了重大挑战。本综述探讨了克服这些局限性的策略，重点关注促进剂在增强CO2活化、提高氧化能力以及实现选择性和稳定催化性能方面的作用。

1. CO2作为氧化剂的挑战：
由于CO2的热力学稳定性，其氧化能力有限，难以重新氧化还原态的CrOx物种[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。尽管CO2可以参与Cr2+ ⇌ Cr3+或Cr3+ ⇌ Cr6+的氧化还原循环，但其氧化能力较弱，不易被CrOx活化[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

2. 增强CO2活化和氧化能力的策略：

2.1 促进剂：
引入促进剂是促进Cr2O3催化剂上CO2活化并提高其氧化能力以实现选择性和稳定性能的关键策略[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

a) 镍（Ni）促进：
镍基促进剂表现出优异的CO2活化能力，即使在苛刻的反应条件下也能发挥作用[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。Ni增强了CO2的氧化能力，可以将还原态的Cr2+重新氧化为活性Cr3+态，并进一步氧化为Cr6+[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。其机理涉及Ni将CO2解离成CO和吸附氧（O*ads），后者通过再氧化重新生成还原态的Cr物种[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

b) 碱金属氧化物：
由于其碱性特征，碱金属氧化物被广泛用作CO2加氢反应中CO2吸附和活化的促进剂[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。钾（K）被确定为有效的促进剂，在5% CrOx/SiO2催化剂中的最佳含量范围为0.2-0.4%，在22%丙烷转化率下实现100%丙烯选择性[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

c) 钌（Ru）：
由于Ru在CO2加氢反应中表现出高活性，可以增强整体反应过程，因此被视为潜在的促进剂[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

2.2 载体效应：
载体材料的选择在增强CO2活化方面起着关键作用。硅胶载体，特别是在低铬负载量时，促进铬的深度还原至Cr2+，这比Cr3+更容易被CO2重新氧化[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

2.3 氧化还原循环机制：
在CrOx/MCM-41等系统中，逆水气变换（RWGS）反应通过氧化还原循环进行，其中丙烷脱氢产生的氢将Cr3+还原为Cr2+，然后被CO2重新氧化[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

3. CO2与促进剂相比O2共进料的优势：

3.1 选择性：
作为比O2更温和的氧化剂，CO2将氧化脱氢（ODH）反应转向更多烯烃产物，防止活性烯烃深度氧化[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。K等促进剂可以进一步提高选择性，某些系统可实现100%的丙烯选择性[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

3.2 稳定性：
特别是Ni和K等促进剂已被证明可以提高铬氧化物催化剂的稳定性，延长催化剂寿命，并在长时间内保持稳定性能[10.1002/cctc.201200397] [10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

3.3 抑制积碳：
CO2可以抑制积碳形成，在某些情况下，通过逆Boudouard反应消除现有积碳[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。CO2的存在可以防止拉曼光谱中积碳带的形成，这些积碳带在移除CO2并增加H2浓度时出现[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。

3.4 热力学优势：
CO2通过RWGS反应消耗氢物种，这有利于提高轻烷烃转化的平衡[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。这有助于克服热力学限制并提高烯烃产率[10.1016/j.catcom.2006.08.005]。

3.5 平衡反应性：
与O2相比，CO2与促进剂提供了更可控的氧化环境，允许更好地调节催化剂性质并优化活性和选择性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：

在CO2辅助的铬基催化系统中使用促进剂进行烷烃脱氢代表了一种有前景的方法，可以解决CO2弱氧化能力和惰性的局限性。通过增强CO2活化和提高其氧化能力，促进剂提供了比O2共进料策略更平衡和选择性的催化性能。

未来研究方向可能集中在：

1. 开发新型多功能促进剂，可同时增强CO2活化、提高选择性和抑制积碳形成。

2. 研究不同促进剂（如Ni、K和Ru的组合）的协同效应，以优化催化剂性能。

3. 探索先进的表征技术，以深入了解CO2活化机理和促进剂在分子水平上的作用。

4. 设计具有定制孔结构和表面性质的催化剂，以最大化CO2、促进剂和活性铬位点之间的相互作用。

5. 将计算机建模与实验研究相结合，以预测和优化促进剂对CO2活化和催化剂性能的影响。

6. 研究其他温和氧化剂或共进料的潜力，这些氧化剂可与CO2和促进剂协同工作，进一步提高催化性能。

7. 探索在工业条件下促进型催化剂的可扩展性和长期稳定性，以弥合实验室研究与实际应用之间的差距。

通过解决这些研究领域，科学界可以致力于开发更高效、更可持续的烷烃脱氢催化系统，为CO2利用和绿色化学的更广泛目标做出贡献。
