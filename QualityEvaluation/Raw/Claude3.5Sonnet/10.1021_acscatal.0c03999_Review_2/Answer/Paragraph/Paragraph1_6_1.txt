Enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalytic systems: Challenges, strategies, and the role of promoters in improving performance and selectivity

Carbon dioxide (CO2) has emerged as a promising alternative oxidant for catalytic oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes to olefins, particularly in chromium-based catalytic systems. However, CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness present significant challenges that need to be addressed to improve its effectiveness. This review focuses on the strategies employed to enhance CO2 activation and oxidizing ability in chromium-based catalysts, with particular emphasis on the role of promoters in improving performance and selectivity.

Challenges of CO2 as an Oxidant:
CO2 has limited oxidizing ability due to its thermodynamically stable properties [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. It is quite inert and not easily activated by CrOx [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]. The efficiency of reoxidation by CO2 is much lower than that by oxygen [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]. These limitations can lead to reduced catalyst performance and stability.

Strategies to Enhance CO2 Activation and Oxidizing Ability:

1. Redox Cycle Mechanism:
In CrOx/MCM-41 catalysts, the reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) reaction proceeds via a redox cycle. Hydrogen formed in the dehydrogenation of propane reduces Cr3+ to Cr2+ species, which are then reoxidized by CO2 [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]. This mechanism allows for indirect activation of CO2.

2. Support Effects:
Using supports like silica that promote deep reduction of chromium to Cr2+, especially at low chromium loadings, allows CO2 to more easily reoxidize the chromium [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]. The boundaries between Cr2O3 and SiO2 particles play an important role in the promoting effect of CO2 [10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4].

3. Low Chromium Content:
Catalysts with low chromium content promote deeper reduction to Cr2+ species, which are more easily reoxidized by CO2 than Cr3+. This is because low Cr content prevents the formation of stable Cr2O3 clusters resistant to further reduction/oxidation [10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1].

4. Promoters:
The introduction of promoters is a key strategy to facilitate CO2 activation on Cr2O3 catalysts and improve its oxidizing ability for selective and stable performance [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]. Several types of promoters have been investigated:

a) Alkali Metal Oxides:
Due to their basic properties, alkali metal oxides have been widely used as promoters for CO2 adsorption and activation in CO2 hydrogenation reactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]. Potassium (K) has been identified as an effective promoter, with optimum content ranging from 0.2-0.4% in 5% CrOx/SiO2 catalysts. K-promoted catalysts showed 100% selectivity for propene formation at 22% propane conversion [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

b) Nickel (Ni):
Ni-based promoters have shown excellent ability to activate CO2, even under harsh reaction conditions [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. Ni enhances the oxidizing ability of CO2 to reoxidize reduced Cr2+ to the active Cr3+ state and further to Cr6+ [10.1002/cctc.201200397]. Ni facilitates CO2 dissociation to CO and an adsorbed O* next to the Ni site, which can subsequently regenerate Cr3+ via reoxidation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

c) Ruthenium (Ru):
Ru is a potential promoter due to its high activity in CO2 hydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2].

Advantages of CO2 with Promoters over O2 Co-feeding:

1. Improved Selectivity:
CO2, being a milder oxidant than O2, shifts the oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) reaction toward more olefin production by preventing deep oxidation of reactive olefins [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]. This results in higher selectivity compared to O2 co-feeding, which can significantly decrease selectivity for propylene [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

2. Enhanced Stability:
Promoters, especially potassium, have been shown to increase the stability of chromium oxide catalysts, allowing for longer catalyst lifetimes and sustained performance over extended periods [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

3. Reduced Coke Formation:
CO2 can inhibit coke formation and, in some cases, eliminate coke through the reverse Boudouard reaction [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]. The addition of promoters further enhances this effect, leading to improved catalyst stability.

4. Thermodynamic Advantages:
CO2 consumes hydrogen species through the RWGS reaction, which is beneficial for enhancing the equilibrium of light alkane conversion [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]. This helps overcome thermodynamic limitations and improves olefin yield [10.1016/j.catcom.2006.08.005].

5. Balanced Reactivity:
Proper amounts of promoters can optimize activity and selectivity by balancing reactions such as RWGS and C3H6 reforming [10.3103/S0361521912010089].

In conclusion, the use of promoters in CO2-based oxidative dehydrogenation of light alkanes over chromium catalysts offers a more balanced approach to enhancing CO2 activation and oxidizing ability while maintaining selectivity and stability. This strategy addresses the limitations of CO2's weak oxidation potential and inertness, providing advantages over O2 co-feeding in terms of selectivity, stability, and overall catalytic performance.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The development of promoters for enhancing CO2 activation in chromium-based catalytic systems represents a significant step towards more efficient and sustainable catalytic processes. By addressing the inherent limitations of CO2 as an oxidant, these strategies open up new possibilities for CO2 utilization in chemical transformations.

Looking forward, several research directions emerge as particularly promising:

1. Multi-functional promoters: Future research could focus on developing promoters that can simultaneously enhance CO2 activation, improve selectivity, and increase catalyst stability. This might involve designing novel bimetallic or multi-component promoters that synergistically address multiple aspects of catalyst performance.

2. In-situ characterization studies: Advanced in-situ spectroscopic techniques could be employed to gain deeper insights into the mechanisms of CO2 activation and the role of promoters under reaction conditions. This could lead to more rational catalyst design strategies.

3. Computational modeling: Leveraging density functional theory (DFT) and other computational methods could help predict optimal promoter compositions and elucidate the electronic and structural factors governing CO2 activation on chromium-based catalysts.

4. Exploring novel support materials: Given the importance of support effects in CO2 activation, investigating advanced support materials such as metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) or 2D materials could lead to breakthroughs in catalyst performance.

5. Process intensification: Integrating CO2-based oxidative dehydrogenation with other processes, such as membrane separation or in-situ product removal, could further enhance overall process efficiency and economics.

6. Expanding to other reaction systems: The insights gained from enhancing CO2 activation in propane dehydrogenation could be applied to other important reactions, such as CO2 reforming of methane or CO2 hydrogenation to valuable chemicals.

By pursuing these research directions, the scientific community can work towards developing more efficient and sustainable catalytic processes that not only improve chemical production but also contribute to CO2 utilization and mitigation strategies.

增强铬基催化体系中CO2的活化和氧化能力：挑战、策略和促进剂在改善性能和选择性方面的作用

二氧化碳（CO2）已成为轻烷烃催化氧化脱氢制烯烃的一种有前景的替代氧化剂，特别是在铬基催化体系中。然而，CO2的弱氧化能力和惰性给其使用带来了重大挑战，需要加以解决以提高其效率。本综述重点关注在铬基催化剂中增强CO2活化和氧化能力的策略，特别强调了促进剂在改善性能和选择性方面的作用。

CO2作为氧化剂的挑战：
由于其热力学稳定性，CO2的氧化能力有限[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。它相当惰性，不易被CrOx活化[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。CO2的再氧化效率远低于氧气[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。这些限制可能导致催化剂性能和稳定性下降。

增强CO2活化和氧化能力的策略：

1. 氧化还原循环机理：
在CrOx/MCM-41催化剂中，逆水煤气反应（RWGS）通过氧化还原循环进行。丙烷脱氢产生的氢将Cr3+还原为Cr2+物种，然后被CO2再氧化[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。这种机制允许CO2间接活化。

2. 载体效应：
使用如二氧化硅等促进铬深度还原为Cr2+的载体，特别是在低铬含量时，可以使CO2更容易再氧化铬[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。Cr2O3和SiO2颗粒之间的界面在CO2的促进作用中起重要作用[10.1016/S0167-2991(98)80785-4]。

3. 低铬含量：
低铬含量的催化剂促进更深度还原为Cr2+物种，这比Cr3+更容易被CO2再氧化。这是因为低Cr含量防止了抗进一步还原/氧化的稳定Cr2O3簇的形成[10.1007/s11144-007-5089-1]。

4. 促进剂：
引入促进剂是促进Cr2O3催化剂上CO2活化并改善其氧化能力以获得选择性和稳定性能的关键策略[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。已研究了几种类型的促进剂：

a) 碱金属氧化物：
由于其碱性特性，碱金属氧化物被广泛用作CO2吸附和活化的促进剂，用于CO2加氢反应[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。钾（K）被确定为有效的促进剂，在5% CrOx/SiO2催化剂中的最佳含量范围为0.2-0.4%。K促进的催化剂在22%的丙烷转化率下显示出100%的丙烯选择性[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

b) 镍（Ni）：
基于镍的促进剂即使在严苛的反应条件下也表现出优异的CO2活化能力[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。Ni增强了CO2的氧化能力，可以将还原的Cr2+再氧化为活性Cr3+状态，并进一步氧化为Cr6+[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。Ni促进CO2解离为CO和吸附的O*，后者可以随后通过再氧化再生Cr3+[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

c) 钌（Ru）：
由于Ru在CO2加氢反应中具有高活性，因此是潜在的促进剂[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_2]。

CO2与促进剂相比O2共进料的优势：

1. 改善选择性：
作为比O2更温和的氧化剂，CO2将氧化脱氢（ODH）反应转向更多烯烃生产，防止反应性烯烃的深度氧化[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。这导致比O2共进料更高的选择性，后者可能显著降低丙烯的选择性[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

2. 增强稳定性：
促进剂，特别是钾，已被证明可以增加铬氧化物催化剂的稳定性，允许更长的催化剂寿命和长期持续的性能[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

3. 减少积碳形成：
CO2可以抑制积碳形成，在某些情况下，通过逆布多瓦反应消除积碳[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。添加促进剂进一步增强了这种效果，导致催化剂稳定性提高。

4. 热力学优势：
CO2通过RWGS反应消耗氢物种，这有利于提高轻烷烃转化的平衡[10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.026]。这有助于克服热力学限制并提高烯烃产率[10.1016/j.catcom.2006.08.005]。

5. 平衡反应性：
适当数量的促进剂可以通过平衡RWGS和C3H6重整等反应来优化活性和选择性[10.3103/S0361521912010089]。

总之，在铬催化剂上使用促进剂进行基于CO2的轻烷烃氧化脱氢提供了一种更平衡的方法来增强CO2活化和氧化能力，同时保持选择性和稳定性。这种策略解决了CO2弱氧化能力和惰性的限制，在选择性、稳定性和整体催化性能方面提供了比O2共进料更多的优势。

综合理解与未来展望：

开发促进剂以增强铬基催化体系中CO2的活化代表了朝着更高效和可持续催化过程迈出的重要一步。通过解决CO2作为氧化剂的固有限制，这些策略为CO2在化学转化中的利用开辟了新的可能性。

展望未来，几个研究方向显得特别有前景：

1. 多功能促进剂：未来的研究可以集中在开发能同时增强CO2活化、改善选择性和增加催化剂稳定性的促进剂上。这可能涉及设计新型的双金属或多组分促进剂，以协同解决催化剂性能的多个方面。
