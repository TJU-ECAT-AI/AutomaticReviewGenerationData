<English>
Hydrogen cofeeding in the propane dehydrogenation (PDH) process significantly affects coke formation, catalyst stability, and reaction mechanisms. This comprehensive review analyzes these effects and their implications for process efficiency and performance.

1. Coke Formation:
Hydrogen cofeeding substantially reduces coke formation on PDH catalysts [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]. The coke prevention effect is more pronounced than the inhibition of the reaction rate, leading to decreased coke selectivity [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]. The mechanism involves hydrogenation of coke precursor species, which is crucial in reducing coke formation [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]. Interestingly, while overall coke formation is reduced, the graphitization of coke increases with hydrogen partial pressure, making it harder to remove [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2].

2. Catalyst Stability:
Hydrogen cofeeding significantly enhances catalyst stability by reducing coke formation and deactivation rates [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]. This effect is particularly noticeable for catalysts like Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O, which exhibit lower deactivation compared to conventional bimetallic catalysts [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]. The improved stability leads to longer catalyst lifetimes and extended operation periods before regeneration is required [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361].

3. Reaction Mechanisms:
Hydrogen cofeeding alters reaction mechanisms in several ways:
a) It enhances the rate of propene formation through hydrogen-assisted dehydrogenation of surface alkyl species [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016].
b) The presence of adsorbed hydrogen atoms increases the energy barrier for the third (undesired) C-H cleavage step, improving selectivity towards alkene formation [10.1039/c3cp53796j].
c) Hydrogen promotes alkene desorption by weakening its chemisorption on the catalyst surface [10.1039/c0cp00341g].

4. Energy Barriers:
While not explicitly stated in all studies, the enhanced dehydrogenation rate with hydrogen addition suggests a lowering of energy barriers for the dehydrogenation reaction [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]. However, hydrogen cofeeding also increases activation energies for certain steps in the reaction mechanism, particularly the undesired further dehydrogenation of alkenes [10.1039/c5cy00230c].

5. Coke Accumulation Patterns:
Hydrogen cofeeding modifies coke accumulation patterns. It promotes the formation of more graphitic coke structures, mainly on the catalyst support rather than on active metal sites [10.1039/c3cp50646k]. This change in coke distribution and structure affects catalyst performance and regeneration strategies.

6. Acid Site Conversions:
In a hydrogen atmosphere, the acidity of the catalyst support is weakened by converting Brønsted acid sites to Lewis acid sites, which further reduces the coke formation rate on the support [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]. This conversion of acid sites plays a crucial role in reducing undesirable side reactions and improving propylene selectivity.

7. Process Efficiency and Performance:
The effects of hydrogen cofeeding on PDH process efficiency and performance are multifaceted:
a) Improved catalyst longevity and reduced regeneration frequency lead to economic benefits from reduced downtime and catalyst replacement costs [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361].
b) Higher propene yields are achieved, especially after initial cycles [10.1039/c3cp50646k].
c) Selectivity towards propene is enhanced due to suppression of side reactions [10.1039/c5cy00230c].
d) However, hydrogen cofeeding can decrease equilibrium conversion and increase compression energy consumption for downstream separation processes [10.1039/c5cy00230c].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The comprehensive effects of hydrogen cofeeding in PDH processes reveal a complex interplay between coke formation, catalyst stability, and reaction mechanisms. While hydrogen significantly improves catalyst longevity and selectivity, it also introduces challenges such as increased coke graphitization and potential reductions in equilibrium conversion.

Future research directions could focus on:
1. Optimizing hydrogen partial pressure to balance coke prevention, selectivity enhancement, and conversion rates.
2. Developing novel catalyst formulations that synergize with hydrogen cofeeding to further improve stability and selectivity.
3. Investigating the long-term effects of hydrogen cofeeding on catalyst microstructure and performance.
4. Exploring alternative cofeeds or combinations that could provide similar benefits to hydrogen without its drawbacks.
5. Utilizing advanced in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the real-time effects of hydrogen on catalyst surfaces during PDH reactions.

By addressing these areas, researchers and industry professionals can work towards developing more efficient and sustainable PDH processes that leverage the benefits of hydrogen cofeeding while mitigating its limitations.

<Chinese>
氢气共进料在丙烷脱氢（PDH）过程中显著影响焦炭形成、催化剂稳定性和反应机理。本综述分析了这些影响及其对工艺效率和性能的影响。

1. 焦炭形成：
氢气共进料大大减少了PDH催化剂上的焦炭形成 [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]。焦炭预防效果比反应速率抑制更为显著，导致焦炭选择性降低 [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]。其机理涉及焦炭前体物种的氢化，这在减少焦炭形成中起关键作用 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。有趣的是，虽然总体焦炭形成减少，但焦炭的石墨化程度随氢分压的增加而增加，使其更难去除 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]。

2. 催化剂稳定性：
氢气共进料通过减少焦炭形成和失活速率显著提高了催化剂稳定性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。这种效果在如Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O等催化剂上特别明显，它们相比传统双金属催化剂表现出较低的失活 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。稳定性的提高导致催化剂寿命延长，在需要再生之前可以延长操作周期 [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]。

3. 反应机理：
氢气共进料以多种方式改变反应机理：
a) 它通过氢辅助表面烷基物种脱氢增强了丙烯形成速率 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。
b) 吸附氢原子的存在增加了第三步（不期望的）C-H键断裂的能量障碍，提高了烯烃形成的选择性 [10.1039/c3cp53796j]。
c) 氢促进烯烃脱附，减弱其在催化剂表面的化学吸附 [10.1039/c0cp00341g]。

4. 能量障碍：
虽然并非所有研究都明确指出，但氢气添加后脱氢速率的提高暗示了脱氢反应能量障碍的降低 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。然而，氢气共进料也增加了反应机理中某些步骤的活化能，特别是不期望的烯烃进一步脱氢 [10.1039/c5cy00230c]。

5. 焦炭积累模式：
氢气共进料改变了焦炭积累模式。它促进了更多石墨化焦炭结构的形成，主要在催化剂载体上而不是活性金属位点上 [10.1039/c3cp50646k]。焦炭分布和结构的这种变化影响了催化剂性能和再生策略。

6. 酸性位转换：
在氢气氛围中，催化剂载体的酸度通过将Brønsted酸位转换为Lewis酸位而减弱，这进一步降低了载体上的焦炭形成速率 [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]。酸性位的这种转换在减少不期望的副反应和提高丙烯选择性方面起着关键作用。

7. 工艺效率和性能：
氢气共进料对PDH工艺效率和性能的影响是多方面的：
a) 催化剂寿命延长和再生频率降低带来的经济效益，包括停机时间减少和催化剂替换成本降低 [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]。
b) 特别是在初始循环后，获得了更高的丙烯产率 [10.1039/c3cp50646k]。
c) 由于抑制了副反应，丙烯的选择性得到提高 [10.1039/c5cy00230c]。
d) 然而，氢气共进料可能会降低平衡转化率，并增加下游分离过程的压缩能耗 [10.1039/c5cy00230c]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：

PDH过程中氢气共进料的综合效应揭示了焦炭形成、催化剂稳定性和反应机理之间复杂的相互作用。虽然氢气显著改善了催化剂寿命和选择性，但也带来了焦炭石墨化增加和潜在平衡转化率降低等挑战。

未来研究方向可以集中在：
1. 优化氢分压，以平衡焦炭预防、选择性提高和转化率。
2. 开发与氢气共进料协同的新型催化剂配方，进一步提高稳定性和选择性。
3. 研究氢气共进料对催化剂微观结构和性能的长期影响。
4. 探索可以提供类似氢气效益但没有其缺点的替代共进料或组合。
5. 利用先进的原位表征技术，更好地了解氢气在PDH反应过程中对催化剂表面的实时影响。

通过解决这些领域的问题，研究人员和行业专业人士可以致力于开发更高效、更可持续的PDH过程，充分利用氢气共进料的优势，同时缓解其局限性。
