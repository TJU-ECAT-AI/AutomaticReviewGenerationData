<English>
Hydrogen cofeeding in the propane dehydrogenation (PDH) process significantly affects coke formation, catalyst stability, and reaction mechanisms. This comprehensive review synthesizes findings from multiple studies to elucidate the complex interplay between hydrogen and the PDH reaction system.

Coke Formation:
Hydrogen cofeeding substantially reduces coke formation on PDH catalysts [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]. The extent of coke suppression is directly related to hydrogen partial pressure, with higher hydrogen content leading to less coke accumulation [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]. This reduction occurs primarily through the hydrogenation of coke precursor species [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]. Interestingly, while hydrogen reduces overall coke formation, it also promotes the formation of more ordered, graphitic coke structures [10.1039/c3cp50646k]. This graphitic coke tends to accumulate mainly on the catalyst support rather than on active metal sites [10.1039/c3cp50646k].

Catalyst Stability:
By mitigating coke formation, hydrogen cofeeding significantly enhances catalyst stability and longevity [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]. Catalysts exposed to hydrogen-rich feeds exhibit slower deactivation rates and extended lifetimes [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]. This improved stability is particularly pronounced for certain catalyst formulations, such as Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O, which show lower deactivation rates compared to conventional bimetallic catalysts [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016].

Reaction Mechanisms:
Hydrogen cofeeding alters reaction pathways in several ways. It enhances the rate of propene formation through hydrogen-assisted dehydrogenation of surface alkyl species [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]. This mechanism involves the dissociative adsorption of propane, followed by H-atom-assisted removal of hydrogen from the adsorbed alkyl species. However, excessive hydrogen can lead to the hydrogenation of nascent alkenes, reducing overall yield [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016].

Energy Barriers:
The presence of hydrogen increases the activation energy for propylene dehydrogenation, which helps prevent over-dehydrogenation and subsequent coke formation [10.1039/c5cy00230c]. This increase in energy barriers is more pronounced for the undesired third C-H cleavage step compared to the initial dehydrogenation steps, thereby improving selectivity towards propene [10.1039/c3cp53796j].

Acid Site Conversions:
Hydrogen cofeeding affects the acidity of catalyst supports by converting Brønsted acid sites to Lewis acid sites [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]. This conversion reduces the rate of coking on the support and alters the catalyst's selectivity profile.

Graphitization:
While hydrogen reduces overall coke formation, it promotes the formation of more graphitic coke structures [10.1039/c3cp50646k]. The degree of graphitization increases with hydrogen partial pressure, resulting in coke that is harder to remove during regeneration [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2].

Process Efficiency and Performance:
The effects of hydrogen cofeeding on PDH process efficiency are multifaceted. While it improves catalyst stability and reduces coking, potentially allowing for longer operation times between regenerations, it also comes with trade-offs. Higher hydrogen partial pressures can decrease equilibrium conversion and increase energy requirements for downstream separation processes [10.1039/c5cy00230c]. However, the overall propane conversion integrated over time can increase with hydrogen cofeeding, especially at higher temperatures [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive effects of hydrogen cofeeding on the PDH process reveal a complex system with numerous interacting factors. While hydrogen generally improves catalyst stability and reduces coking, its impact on reaction kinetics, selectivity, and overall process efficiency depends on careful optimization of operating conditions.

Future research directions could focus on:
1. Developing advanced catalyst formulations that synergize with hydrogen cofeeding to further enhance stability and selectivity.
2. Exploring the use of membrane reactors or other innovative reactor designs that could optimize hydrogen distribution and utilization within the reaction system.
3. Investigating the potential of using renewable hydrogen sources for cofeeding, aligning PDH processes with sustainability goals.
4. Conducting detailed mechanistic studies to better understand the molecular-level interactions between hydrogen, hydrocarbons, and catalyst surfaces under realistic PDH conditions.
5. Developing predictive models that incorporate the multiple effects of hydrogen cofeeding to optimize process conditions and catalyst design simultaneously.

These research avenues could lead to more efficient, stable, and selective PDH processes, potentially revolutionizing propylene production in the petrochemical industry.

<Chinese>
在丙烷脱氢（PDH）过程中，氢气共进料对积碳形成、催化剂稳定性和反应机理产生显著影响。本综述综合了多项研究的发现，阐明了氢气与PDH反应系统之间的复杂相互作用。

积碳形成：
氢气共进料大大减少了PDH催化剂上的积碳形成[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]。积碳抑制程度与氢气分压直接相关，较高的氢气含量导致较少的积碳累积[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]。这种减少主要通过氢化积碳前体物种实现[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。有趣的是，虽然氢气减少了总体积碳形成，但它也促进了更有序的石墨状积碳结构的形成[10.1039/c3cp50646k]。这种石墨状积碳倾向于主要累积在催化剂载体上，而不是活性金属位点上[10.1039/c3cp50646k]。

催化剂稳定性：
通过减轻积碳形成，氢气共进料显著提高了催化剂的稳定性和寿命[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。暴露在富氢进料中的催化剂表现出较慢的失活速率和延长的寿命[10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]。这种改进的稳定性在某些催化剂配方中尤为明显，例如Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O催化剂与传统的双金属催化剂相比，显示出较低的失活速率[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。

反应机理：
氢气共进料以多种方式改变了反应路径。它通过氢辅助表面烷基物种脱氢来增强丙烯的生成速率[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。这种机制涉及丙烷的解离吸附，随后是吸附烷基物种的H原子辅助氢去除。然而，过量的氢气可能导致新生烯烃的氢化，从而降低总体产率[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。

能量障碍：
氢气的存在增加了丙烯脱氢的活化能，这有助于防止过度脱氢和随后的积碳形成[10.1039/c5cy00230c]。这种能量障碍的增加对不期望的第三个C-H断裂步骤比初始脱氢步骤更为显著，从而提高了丙烯的选择性[10.1039/c3cp53796j]。

酸性位点转化：
氢气共进料通过将Brønsted酸性位点转化为Lewis酸性位点来影响催化剂载体的酸性[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]。这种转化降低了载体上的积碳速率，并改变了催化剂的选择性概况。

石墨化：
虽然氢气减少了总体积碳形成，但它促进了更石墨状积碳结构的形成[10.1039/c3cp50646k]。石墨化程度随氢气分压的增加而增加，导致再生过程中更难去除的积碳[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]。

过程效率和性能：
氢气共进料对PDH过程效率的影响是多方面的。虽然它提高了催化剂稳定性并减少了积碳，可能允许再生之间的更长操作时间，但也带来了权衡。较高的氢气分压可能降低平衡转化率并增加下游分离过程的能量需求[10.1039/c5cy00230c]。然而，随时间积分的总体丙烷转化率可能随着氢气共进料而增加，尤其是在较高温度下[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：氢气共进料对PDH过程的全面影响揭示了一个具有众多相互作用因素的复杂系统。虽然氢气通常改善了催化剂稳定性并减少了积碳，但其对反应动力学、选择性和整体过程效率的影响取决于操作条件的仔细优化。

未来研究方向可以集中在：
1. 开发与氢气共进料协同的先进催化剂配方，以进一步提高稳定性和选择性。
2. 探索使用膜反应器或其他创新反应器设计，以优化反应系统内的氢气分布和利用。
3. 研究使用可再生氢源进行共进料的潜力，使PDH过程与可持续发展目标保持一致。
4. 进行详细的机理研究，以更好地理解在实际PDH条件下氢气、烃类和催化剂表面之间的分子水平相互作用。
5. 开发包含氢气共进料多重效应的预测模型，以同时优化过程条件和催化剂设计。

这些研究途径可能导致更高效、更稳定和更具选择性的PDH过程，有潜力彻底改变石油化工行业的丙烯生产。
