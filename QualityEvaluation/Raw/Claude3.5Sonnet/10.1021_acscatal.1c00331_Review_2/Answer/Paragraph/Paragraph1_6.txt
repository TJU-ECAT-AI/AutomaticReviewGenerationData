<English>
Hydrogen cofeeding in the propane dehydrogenation (PDH) process significantly affects coke formation, catalyst stability, and reaction mechanisms. This comprehensive review synthesizes the current understanding of these effects and their implications for process efficiency and performance.

1. Coke Formation:
Hydrogen cofeeding substantially reduces coke formation on catalysts [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]. The addition of hydrogen to the feed suppresses coke formation, with higher hydrogen content leading to less coke accumulation [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]. This reduction is primarily due to the hydrogenation of coke precursor species [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]. The effect is more pronounced on the catalyst support than on the metal component [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2].

2. Catalyst Stability:
Hydrogen cofeeding improves catalyst stability by reducing coke formation and deactivation rates [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]. It helps maintain catalyst activity over time, leading to longer operational periods before regeneration is required [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]. However, while hydrogen is effective at preventing coke formation, it cannot remove coke that has already formed on the catalyst [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361].

3. Reaction Mechanisms:
Hydrogen enhances the rate of propene formation through hydrogen-assisted dehydrogenation of surface alkyl species [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]. It also promotes alkene desorption by weakening its chemisorption on the catalyst surface [10.1039/c0cp00341g]. The presence of hydrogen shifts the third C-H cleavage step to a higher energy value, making it less competitive compared to the preferred alkene desorption step [10.1039/c5cy00230c].

4. Energy Barriers:
Hydrogen cofeeding increases the activation energy for propylene dehydrogenation [10.1039/c5cy00230c]. This increase in energy barriers for further dehydrogenation steps helps improve selectivity towards the desired propylene product [10.1039/c3cp53796j].

5. Coke Accumulation Patterns:
Hydrogen changes the pattern of coke accumulation, promoting the formation of graphitic coke on the catalyst support rather than disordered coke on active metal sites [10.1039/c3cp50646k]. At higher hydrogen partial pressures, the coke becomes less dense and compact [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8].

6. Acid Site Conversions:
In a hydrogen atmosphere, the acidity of the support is weakened by the conversion of Lewis acid sites to Brønsted acid sites [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]. This conversion helps reduce the rate of coking on the support.

7. Graphitization:
The graphitization of coke is positively correlated with hydrogen partial pressure [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]. This means that while hydrogen reduces overall coke formation, it also makes the formed coke harder to remove due to its more ordered graphitic structure.

These effects have significant implications for process efficiency and performance:

1. Improved catalyst longevity due to reduced coking and deactivation [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016].
2. Higher propene yields, especially after initial cycles [10.1039/c3cp50646k].
3. Potential for longer operation times between regeneration cycles [10.1039/c3cp50646k].
4. More efficient use of the catalyst surface, as hydrogen helps keep active metal sites free from coke [10.1039/c3cp50646k].
5. Need for optimization to balance coke prevention with reaction rate [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361].
6. Trade-off between improved stability and reduced equilibrium conversion [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive effects of hydrogen cofeeding on the PDH process reveal a complex interplay between reaction kinetics, catalyst stability, and coke formation. While hydrogen cofeeding offers significant benefits in terms of reduced coking and improved catalyst longevity, it also introduces challenges that require careful process optimization.

Future research directions may include:

1. Development of advanced catalysts that can maximize the benefits of hydrogen cofeeding while minimizing its drawbacks, such as reduced equilibrium conversion.
2. Investigation of synergistic effects between hydrogen cofeeding and other process modifications, such as the use of promoters or novel support materials.
3. In-depth studies on the molecular-level interactions between hydrogen, hydrocarbons, and catalyst surfaces to better understand and control reaction pathways.
4. Exploration of pulsed or cyclic hydrogen cofeeding strategies to optimize the balance between coke prevention and reaction rates.
5. Integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning techniques for real-time optimization of hydrogen cofeeding in industrial PDH processes.
6. Development of novel regeneration techniques that can effectively remove the more graphitic coke formed under hydrogen-rich conditions.

These research avenues could lead to more efficient and sustainable PDH processes, contributing to the broader goal of optimizing olefin production in the petrochemical industry.

<Chinese>
在丙烷脱氢(PDH)过程中，氢气共进料对焦炭形成、催化剂稳定性和反应机理产生显著影响。本综述综合了对这些影响及其对过程效率和性能影响的当前理解。

1. 焦炭形成：
氢气共进料大大减少了催化剂上的焦炭形成 [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]。向进料中添加氢气可抑制焦炭形成，氢含量越高，焦炭积累越少 [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]。这种减少主要是由于焦炭前体物种的氢化 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。这种效果在催化剂载体上比在金属组分上更显著 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]。

2. 催化剂稳定性：
氢气共进料通过减少焦炭形成和失活速率来提高催化剂稳定性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。它有助于长时间维持催化剂活性，从而延长再生前的操作周期 [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]。然而，虽然氢气能有效防止焦炭形成，但无法去除已经在催化剂上形成的焦炭 [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]。

3. 反应机理：
氢气通过氢辅助表面烷基物种脱氢增强丙烯形成速率 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。它还通过减弱烯烃在催化剂表面的化学吸附来促进烯烃脱附 [10.1039/c0cp00341g]。氢的存在使第三个C-H键断裂步骤的能量增加，使其与优先的烯烃脱附步骤相比竞争力降低 [10.1039/c5cy00230c]。

4. 能量障碍：
氢气共进料增加了丙烯脱氢的活化能 [10.1039/c5cy00230c]。进一步脱氢步骤能量障碍的增加有助于提高对目标产物丙烯的选择性 [10.1039/c3cp53796j]。

5. 焦炭积累模式：
氢改变了焦炭积累的模式，促进在催化剂载体上形成石墨化焦炭，而不是在活性金属位点上形成无序焦炭 [10.1039/c3cp50646k]。在较高的氢分压下，焦炭变得不那么致密和紧实 [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]。

6. 酸性位点转化：
在氢气氛围中，载体的酸性通过Lewis酸性位点向Brønsted酸性位点的转化而减弱 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]。这种转化有助于减少载体上的焦炭形成速率。

7. 石墨化：
焦炭的石墨化程度与氢分压呈正相关 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]。这意味着虽然氢减少了总体焦炭形成，但也使形成的焦炭由于其更有序的石墨结构而更难去除。

这些影响对过程效率和性能有重要意义：

1. 由于焦炭减少和失活减慢，催化剂寿命延长 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。
2. 特别是在初始循环后，丙烯产率更高 [10.1039/c3cp50646k]。
3. 再生循环之间可能有更长的操作时间 [10.1039/c3cp50646k]。
4. 催化剂表面利用更高效，因为氢有助于保持活性金属位点不被焦炭覆盖 [10.1039/c3cp50646k]。
5. 需要优化以平衡焦炭防止和反应速率 [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]。
6. 改善稳定性和降低平衡转化率之间的权衡 [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：氢气共进料对PDH过程的全面影响揭示了反应动力学、催化剂稳定性和焦炭形成之间复杂的相互作用。虽然氢气共进料在减少焦炭形成和提高催化剂寿命方面提供了显著的益处，但也带来了需要仔细进行过程优化的挑战。

未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 开发先进催化剂，可以最大化氢气共进料的优势，同时最小化其缺点，如降低平衡转化率。
2. 研究氢气共进料与其他过程改进（如使用助剂或新型载体材料）之间的协同效应。
3. 深入研究氢气、烃类和催化剂表面之间的分子水平相互作用，以更好地理解和控制反应路径。
4. 探索脉冲或循环氢气共进料策略，以优化焦炭防止和反应速率之间的平衡。
5. 将人工智能和机器学习技术集成到工业PDH过程中，实现氢气共进料的实时优化。
6. 开发新型再生技术，可以有效去除在富氢条件下形成的更石墨化的焦炭。

这些研究方向可能导致更高效、更可持续的PDH过程，为石化行业烯烃生产的优化做出贡献。
