<English>
Hydrogen cofeeding in the propane dehydrogenation (PDH) process significantly affects coke formation, catalyst stability, and reaction mechanisms. This comprehensive review synthesizes the key findings from multiple studies to elucidate the complex interplay between hydrogen and the PDH process.

1. Coke Formation:
Hydrogen cofeeding generally suppresses coke formation on catalysts. As hydrogen content in the feed increases, the total amount of coke decreases significantly [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]. This reduction is attributed to hydrogen's ability to remove coke precursors and hydrogenate coke-forming species [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]. The effect is more pronounced on the catalyst support than on the metal components [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2].

However, the relationship between hydrogen and coke formation is not straightforward. While hydrogen reduces overall coke formation, it also promotes the formation of more graphitic coke, which is harder to remove due to its higher combustion temperature [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]. This graphitic coke tends to accumulate mainly on the catalyst support rather than on active metal sites [10.1039/c3cp50646k].

2. Catalyst Stability:
Hydrogen cofeeding significantly enhances catalyst stability by reducing coke formation and maintaining catalyst activity over time [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]. It helps keep the surface of metal nanoparticles free from disordered coke species, which are more detrimental to catalyst performance [10.1039/c3cp50646k]. This results in slower deactivation rates and longer catalyst lifetimes [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7].

The improved stability is particularly notable for certain catalyst compositions. For instance, Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O catalysts exhibit lower deactivation and coke formation rates compared to conventional bimetallic catalysts when hydrogen is cofed [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016].

3. Reaction Mechanisms:
Hydrogen cofeeding alters reaction pathways in the PDH process. It enhances the rate of propene formation through hydrogen-assisted dehydrogenation of surface alkyl species [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]. This mechanism involves the dissociative adsorption of propane, followed by H-atom-assisted removal of hydrogen from the adsorbed alkyl species.

Interestingly, hydrogen affects the energy barriers of different reaction steps. It increases the activation energy for propylene dehydrogenation, making it less competitive compared to propylene desorption [10.1039/c5cy00230c]. This shift in energy barriers improves selectivity towards propylene by preventing over-dehydrogenation and subsequent coke formation.

4. Acid Site Conversions:
Hydrogen cofeeding influences the acid sites on the catalyst support. In a hydrogen atmosphere, the acidity of the support is weakened by converting Brønsted acid sites to Lewis acid sites [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]. This conversion helps reduce the rate of coking on the support and alters the overall reaction dynamics.

5. Process Efficiency and Performance:
The effects of hydrogen cofeeding on overall process efficiency and performance are complex and sometimes contradictory. While it improves catalyst stability and longevity, potentially allowing for longer operation times between regenerations, it also comes with some drawbacks.

Hydrogen cofeeding can decrease the equilibrium conversion of propane and increase compression energy consumption for downstream separation processes [10.1039/c5cy00230c]. However, the conversion integrated over extended periods may actually increase with hydrogen cofeeding, especially at higher temperatures [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361].

The optimal hydrogen to propane ratio appears to be critical. For instance, propene formation rate increases up to a H2/C3H8 ratio of 1.25, beyond which excessive hydrogen can lead to alkene hydrogenation, reducing the net dehydrogenation rate [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 
The comprehensive effects of hydrogen cofeeding in PDH reveal a complex system with multiple trade-offs. While hydrogen significantly improves catalyst stability and reduces overall coke formation, it also introduces challenges such as increased graphitization of coke and potential reductions in equilibrium conversion.

Future research directions could focus on:

1. Optimizing hydrogen partial pressures to balance coke suppression, catalyst stability, and conversion rates.
2. Developing novel catalyst designs that maximize the benefits of hydrogen cofeeding while minimizing its drawbacks.
3. Investigating the long-term effects of hydrogen cofeeding on catalyst performance and regeneration cycles.
4. Exploring the synergistic effects of hydrogen cofeeding with other process modifications or additives to further enhance PDH efficiency.
5. Conducting in-depth studies on the molecular-level interactions between hydrogen and various catalyst components to better understand and control reaction mechanisms.

By addressing these areas, researchers and industry professionals can work towards developing more efficient, stable, and sustainable PDH processes that leverage the benefits of hydrogen cofeeding while mitigating its challenges.

<Chinese>
在丙烷脱氢（PDH）过程中，氢气共进料对积碳形成、催化剂稳定性和反应机理产生显著影响。本综合评述综合了多项研究的关键发现，阐明了氢气与PDH过程之间的复杂相互作用。

1. 积碳形成：
氢气共进料通常抑制催化剂上的积碳形成。随着进料中氢气含量的增加，积碳总量显著减少 [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]。这种减少归因于氢气能够去除积碳前体物并氢化积碳形成物种 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。这种效果在催化剂载体上比在金属组分上更为明显 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]。

然而，氢气与积碳形成之间的关系并不简单。虽然氢气减少了总体积碳形成，但它也促进了更石墨化积碳的形成，这种积碳由于其较高的燃烧温度而更难去除 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]。这种石墨化积碳倾向于主要积累在催化剂载体上，而不是活性金属位点上 [10.1039/c3cp50646k]。

2. 催化剂稳定性：
氢气共进料通过减少积碳形成和维持催化剂活性，显著提高了催化剂的稳定性 [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]。它有助于保持金属纳米粒子表面不被无序积碳物种覆盖，这些无序积碳物种对催化剂性能更为有害 [10.1039/c3cp50646k]。这导致了更慢的失活速率和更长的催化剂寿命 [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]。

对某些催化剂组成而言，这种稳定性的改善尤为显著。例如，当共进料氢气时，Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O催化剂相比传统的双金属催化剂表现出更低的失活和积碳形成速率 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。

3. 反应机理：
氢气共进料改变了PDH过程中的反应路径。它通过氢辅助表面烷基物种脱氢增强了丙烯的形成速率 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。这种机制涉及丙烷的解离吸附，随后是吸附烷基物种的H原子辅助氢去除。

有趣的是，氢气影响了不同反应步骤的能量障碍。它增加了丙烯脱氢的活化能，使其相比丙烯脱附更不具竞争力 [10.1039/c5cy00230c]。这种能量障碍的转变通过防止过度脱氢和随后的积碳形成，提高了对丙烯的选择性。

4. 酸性位点转化：
氢气共进料影响了催化剂载体上的酸性位点。在氢气氛围中，载体的酸性通过将Brønsted酸性位点转化为Lewis酸性位点而减弱 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]。这种转化有助于减少载体上的积碳速率，并改变了整体反应动力学。

5. 过程效率和性能：
氢气共进料对整体过程效率和性能的影响是复杂的，有时甚至是矛盾的。虽然它改善了催化剂的稳定性和寿命，可能允许在再生之间有更长的运行时间，但它也带来了一些缺点。

氢气共进料可能降低丙烷的平衡转化率，并增加下游分离过程的压缩能耗 [10.1039/c5cy00230c]。然而，在较长时间内积分的转化率实际上可能随着氢气共进料而增加，特别是在较高温度下 [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]。

最佳的氢气与丙烷比例似乎至关重要。例如，丙烯形成速率在H2/C3H8比例达到1.25时增加到最大值，超过这个比例，过量的氢气可能导致烯烃氢化，降低净脱氢速率 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：
PDH中氢气共进料的综合效应揭示了一个具有多重权衡的复杂系统。虽然氢气显著改善了催化剂稳定性并减少了总体积碳形成，但它也带来了诸如积碳石墨化程度增加和潜在的平衡转化率降低等挑战。

未来的研究方向可以集中在：

1. 优化氢气分压，以平衡积碳抑制、催化剂稳定性和转化率。
2. 开发新型催化剂设计，最大化氢气共进料的好处，同时最小化其缺点。
3. 研究氢气共进料对催化剂长期性能和再生周期的影响。
4. 探索氢气共进料与其他过程修改或添加剂的协同效应，以进一步提高PDH效率。
5. 对氢气与各种催化剂组分之间的分子水平相互作用进行深入研究，以更好地理解和控制反应机理。

通过解决这些领域的问题，研究人员和行业专业人士可以致力于开发更高效、更稳定和更可持续的PDH过程，充分利用氢气共进料的优势，同时缓解其挑战。
