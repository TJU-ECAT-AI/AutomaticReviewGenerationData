<English>
The comprehensive effects and mechanisms of hydrogen cofeeding on coke formation, catalyst stability, and reaction dynamics in the propane dehydrogenation (PDH) process are multifaceted and significant. Hydrogen cofeeding has been shown to play a crucial role in influencing various aspects of the PDH process:

1. Coke Formation:
Hydrogen cofeeding significantly reduces coke formation on catalysts. This effect is achieved through several mechanisms:
a) Suppression of coke precursor formation [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361].
b) Hydrogenation of coke precursor species [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016].
c) Weakening of support acidity by converting Brønsted acid sites to Lewis acid sites, which reduces coke formation rate on the support [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8].
d) Inhibition of aromatic coke formation, which is typically harder to remove [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8].

However, it's important to note that while hydrogen reduces overall coke formation, it also leads to more graphitic coke structures, which can be harder to remove during regeneration [10.1039/c3cp50646k].

2. Catalyst Stability:
Hydrogen cofeeding enhances catalyst stability through several mechanisms:
a) Reduced coke formation leads to slower deactivation rates and longer catalyst lifetimes [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7].
b) Hydrogen helps maintain catalyst activity over time [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361].
c) It keeps the surface of Pt nanoparticles free of disordered coke species [10.1039/c3cp50646k].

3. Reaction Mechanisms and Selectivity:
Hydrogen influences reaction pathways and product selectivity:
a) It enhances the rate of propene formation through hydrogen-assisted dehydrogenation of surface alkyl species [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016].
b) Hydrogen increases the energy barrier for propylene dehydrogenation, preventing deep reactions that lead to coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2].
c) It promotes alkene desorption relative to further dehydrogenation, improving selectivity [10.1039/c3cp53796j].

4. Energy Barriers:
While not always explicitly stated, hydrogen cofeeding affects energy barriers in the reaction:
a) It increases the activation energy for propylene dehydrogenation [10.1039/c5cy00230c].
b) The energy barrier for the third (undesired) C-H cleavage step is raised more significantly than for the first two steps, enhancing selectivity [10.1039/c3cp53796j].

5. Coke Accumulation Patterns:
Hydrogen alters coke accumulation patterns on the catalyst:
a) It promotes the formation of graphitic coke on the catalyst support rather than disordered coke on active Pt sites [10.1039/c3cp50646k].
b) The coke formed under hydrogen cofeeding is less dense and compact [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8].

6. Process Efficiency and Performance:
The overall impact on process efficiency is generally positive:
a) Improved catalyst longevity leads to longer operational periods between regenerations [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016].
b) Higher propene yields are achieved, especially after initial cycles [10.1039/c3cp50646k].
c) However, there's a trade-off as hydrogen cofeeding can decrease equilibrium conversion [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The extensive research on hydrogen cofeeding in PDH processes has revealed its complex role in enhancing catalyst performance and process efficiency. Moving forward, several key areas warrant further investigation:

1. Optimizing H2/C3H8 ratios: Finding the ideal balance between coke suppression and conversion rates for different catalyst systems could significantly improve process economics.

2. Novel catalyst designs: Developing catalysts that can maximize the benefits of hydrogen cofeeding while minimizing its drawbacks could lead to breakthroughs in PDH technology.

3. In-situ characterization techniques: Advanced methods to study the real-time effects of hydrogen on catalyst surfaces and coke formation could provide deeper insights into reaction mechanisms.

4. Hybrid processes: Exploring combinations of hydrogen cofeeding with other process enhancements, such as membrane reactors or oxidative dehydrogenation, might yield synergistic benefits.

5. Computational modeling: Leveraging advanced simulations to predict and optimize hydrogen's effects on different catalyst compositions could accelerate catalyst development.

6. Sustainable hydrogen sources: Investigating the use of green hydrogen in PDH processes could improve the overall environmental footprint of propylene production.

These research directions could pave the way for more efficient, stable, and sustainable PDH processes, addressing the growing global demand for propylene while minimizing environmental impact.

<Chinese>
氢气共进料对丙烷脱氢（PDH）过程中焦炭形成、催化剂稳定性和反应动力学的全面影响和机理是多方面的，且意义重大。氢气共进料被证明在影响PDH过程的各个方面起着关键作用：

1. 焦炭形成：
氢气共进料显著减少了催化剂上的焦炭形成。这种效果通过几种机制实现：
a) 抑制焦炭前体物的形成 [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]。
b) 氢化焦炭前体物种 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。
c) 通过将布朗斯特酸位转化为路易斯酸位来削弱载体酸度，从而降低载体上的焦炭形成速率 [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]。
d) 抑制芳香族焦炭的形成，这种焦炭通常更难去除 [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]。

然而，需要注意的是，虽然氢气减少了整体焦炭形成，但它也导致了更多石墨化焦炭结构的形成，这在再生过程中可能更难去除 [10.1039/c3cp50646k]。

2. 催化剂稳定性：
氢气共进料通过几种机制增强了催化剂稳定性：
a) 减少焦炭形成导致失活速率降低和催化剂寿命延长 [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]。
b) 氢气有助于长期维持催化剂活性 [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]。
c) 它保持Pt纳米颗粒表面免于无序焦炭物种的覆盖 [10.1039/c3cp50646k]。

3. 反应机理和选择性：
氢气影响反应路径和产物选择性：
a) 它通过氢辅助表面烷基物种脱氢增强了丙烯形成速率 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。
b) 氢气增加了丙烯脱氢的能量障碍，防止导致焦炭形成的深度反应 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]。
c) 它促进烯烃脱附相对于进一步脱氢，提高了选择性 [10.1039/c3cp53796j]。

4. 能量障碍：
虽然并非总是明确表述，但氢气共进料影响反应中的能量障碍：
a) 它增加了丙烯脱氢的活化能 [10.1039/c5cy00230c]。
b) 第三步（不期望的）C-H断裂的能量障碍比前两步提高得更显著，增强了选择性 [10.1039/c3cp53796j]。

5. 焦炭积累模式：
氢气改变了催化剂上的焦炭积累模式：
a) 它促进了催化剂载体上石墨化焦炭的形成，而不是活性Pt位点上的无序焦炭 [10.1039/c3cp50646k]。
b) 在氢气共进料下形成的焦炭密度较低，结构较为松散 [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]。

6. 过程效率和性能：
对过程效率的整体影响通常是积极的：
a) 催化剂寿命延长导致再生周期之间的操作时间更长 [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。
b) 特别是在初始循环之后，获得更高的丙烯产率 [10.1039/c3cp50646k]。
c) 然而，存在权衡，因为氢气共进料可能降低平衡转化率 [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：对PDH过程中氢气共进料的广泛研究揭示了其在增强催化剂性能和过程效率方面的复杂作用。展望未来，几个关键领域值得进一步研究：

1. 优化H2/C3H8比例：为不同的催化剂系统找到焦炭抑制和转化率之间的理想平衡，可能显著改善过程经济性。

2. 新型催化剂设计：开发能够最大化氢气共进料优势同时最小化其缺点的催化剂，可能导致PDH技术的突破。

3. 原位表征技术：开发先进方法来研究氢气对催化剂表面和焦炭形成的实时影响，可能提供对反应机理更深入的认识。

4. 混合过程：探索将氢气共进料与其他过程强化手段（如膜反应器或氧化脱氢）相结合，可能产生协同效应。

5. 计算机模拟：利用先进模拟来预测和优化氢气对不同催化剂组成的影响，可能加速催化剂开发。

6. 可持续氢源：研究在PDH过程中使用绿色氢气，可能改善丙烯生产的整体环境足迹。

这些研究方向可能为更高效、更稳定和更可持续的PDH过程铺平道路，满足不断增长的全球丙烯需求，同时最大限度地减少环境影响。
