<English>
Hydrogen cofeeding in the propane dehydrogenation (PDH) process significantly affects coke formation, catalyst stability, and reaction mechanisms. This review will analyze these effects and their implications for process efficiency and performance.

1. Coke Formation:
Hydrogen cofeeding substantially reduces coke formation on catalysts. At higher hydrogen partial pressures, the total amount of coke decreases significantly [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]. This reduction is attributed to hydrogen's ability to remove coke precursors and suppress their formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]. The adsorption of H2 is crucial in reducing coke formation, particularly during propane dehydrogenation compared to ethane dehydrogenation [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016].

2. Catalyst Stability:
Hydrogen cofeeding enhances catalyst stability by reducing coke formation and deactivation rates. Catalysts like Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O exhibit lower deactivation rates compared to conventional bimetallic catalysts in the presence of hydrogen [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]. The improved stability leads to longer catalyst lifetimes and extended periods between regeneration cycles [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7].

3. Reaction Mechanisms:
Hydrogen affects reaction mechanisms in several ways:
a) It enhances propene formation through hydrogen-assisted dehydrogenation of surface alkyl species [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016].
b) The presence of adsorbed H atoms increases the activation energy for propylene dehydrogenation, making it less competitive compared to alkene desorption [10.1039/c5cy00230c].
c) Hydrogen promotes alkene desorption by weakening its chemisorption on the catalyst surface [10.1039/c0cp00341g].

4. Energy Barriers:
While not explicitly stated in all studies, the enhanced dehydrogenation rate with hydrogen addition suggests a lowering of energy barriers for the dehydrogenation reaction [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]. However, hydrogen cofeeding also increases activation energies for certain steps in the reaction mechanism, which can improve selectivity towards alkene formation [10.1039/c3cp53796j].

5. Coke Accumulation Patterns:
Hydrogen cofeeding alters coke accumulation patterns. It promotes the formation of graphitic coke on the catalyst support rather than disordered coke on active metal sites [10.1039/c3cp50646k]. The coke formed under hydrogen cofeeding is less dense and compact, making it easier to remove during regeneration [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8].

6. Acid Site Conversions:
In a hydrogen atmosphere, the acidity of the support is weakened by converting Brønsted acid sites to Lewis acid sites, which helps reduce the rate of coking on the support [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2].

7. Graphitization:
Hydrogen cofeeding promotes the formation of more graphitic coke structures, as evidenced by changes in the Raman spectra of coked catalysts [10.1039/c3cp50646k]. The graphitization of coke is positively correlated with hydrogen partial pressure, which can make coke removal more challenging during regeneration [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2].

Implications for Process Efficiency and Performance:
1. Improved catalyst longevity due to reduced coking and deactivation rates.
2. Higher propene yields, especially after initial cycles [10.1039/c3cp50646k].
3. Potential for longer operation times between regeneration cycles.
4. More efficient use of catalyst surface area, as hydrogen helps keep active sites free from coke.
5. Enhanced selectivity towards desired products by suppressing side reactions.
6. Reduced regeneration frequency, leading to improved process economics and reduced downtime.
7. Potential for lower energy requirements during regeneration due to softer, less dense coke formation.

However, there are some trade-offs to consider:
1. Excessive hydrogen can lead to alkene hydrogenation, reducing the net dehydrogenation rate [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016].
2. Hydrogen cofeeding may decrease equilibrium conversion, potentially reducing overall yield [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7].
3. The increased graphitization of coke may require more energy-intensive regeneration processes [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The comprehensive effects of hydrogen cofeeding in PDH processes reveal a complex interplay between coke formation, catalyst stability, and reaction mechanisms. While hydrogen significantly improves catalyst longevity and reduces coke formation, it also introduces new challenges in process optimization.

Future research directions may include:
1. Developing advanced catalyst designs that leverage the benefits of hydrogen cofeeding while mitigating its drawbacks, such as potential yield reduction.
2. Investigating the synergistic effects of hydrogen cofeeding with other process innovations, like membrane reactors or novel heat management systems, to further enhance PDH efficiency.
3. Exploring the use of renewable hydrogen sources for cofeeding to improve the sustainability of PDH processes.
4. Studying the long-term effects of hydrogen cofeeding on catalyst performance and developing strategies for optimal catalyst regeneration under these conditions.
5. Investigating the potential of dynamic hydrogen feeding strategies that adjust hydrogen concentrations in real-time based on reaction conditions and catalyst state.

These research avenues could lead to more efficient, stable, and sustainable PDH processes, potentially revolutionizing propylene production in the petrochemical industry.

<Chinese>
在丙烷脱氢（PDH）过程中，氢气共进料对积碳形成、催化剂稳定性和反应机理产生显著影响。本综述将分析这些影响及其对过程效率和性能的影响。

1. 积碳形成：
氢气共进料大大减少了催化剂上的积碳形成。在较高的氢分压下，总积碳量显著降低[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]。这种减少归因于氢气能够去除积碳前体物并抑制其形成[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]。H2的吸附对于减少积碳形成至关重要，特别是在丙烷脱氢过程中，相比乙烷脱氢更为明显[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。

2. 催化剂稳定性：
氢气共进料通过减少积碳形成和降低失活率来增强催化剂稳定性。在氢气存在的情况下，像Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O这样的催化剂比传统的双金属催化剂表现出更低的失活率[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。提高的稳定性导致催化剂寿命延长和再生周期延长[10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]。

3. 反应机理：
氢气以多种方式影响反应机理：
a) 它通过氢辅助表面烷基物种脱氢来增强丙烯的形成[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。
b) 吸附的H原子存在会增加丙烯脱氢的活化能，使其与烯烃脱附相比不那么有竞争力[10.1039/c5cy00230c]。
c) 氢气通过削弱烯烃在催化剂表面的化学吸附来促进烯烃脱附[10.1039/c0cp00341g]。

4. 能量障碍：
虽然并非所有研究都明确说明，但氢气添加后脱氢率的提高表明脱氢反应的能量障碍降低[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。然而，氢气共进料也会增加反应机理中某些步骤的活化能，这可以提高烯烃形成的选择性[10.1039/c3cp53796j]。

5. 积碳累积模式：
氢气共进料改变了积碳累积模式。它促进了在催化剂载体上形成石墨化积碳，而不是在活性金属位点上形成无序积碳[10.1039/c3cp50646k]。在氢气共进料下形成的积碳密度较低，结构较松散，使其在再生过程中更容易去除[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]。

6. 酸性位点转化：
在氢气氛围中，载体的酸性通过将Brønsted酸性位点转化为Lewis酸性位点而减弱，这有助于减少载体上的积碳速率[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]。

7. 石墨化：
氢气共进料促进了更多石墨化积碳结构的形成，这从积碳催化剂的拉曼光谱变化中可以看出[10.1039/c3cp50646k]。积碳的石墨化程度与氢分压呈正相关，这可能使再生过程中的积碳去除更具挑战性[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]。

对过程效率和性能的影响：
1. 由于积碳和失活率降低，催化剂寿命延长。
2. 特别是在初始循环后，丙烯产率更高[10.1039/c3cp50646k]。
3. 再生周期之间可能有更长的运行时间。
4. 催化剂表面积利用更有效，因为氢气有助于保持活性位点不被积碳覆盖。
5. 通过抑制副反应，提高了对目标产物的选择性。
6. 再生频率降低，导致过程经济性提高和停机时间减少。
7. 由于形成的积碳较软、密度较低，再生过程中可能需要较低的能量需求。

然而，需要考虑一些权衡：
1. 过量的氢气可能导致烯烃加氢，降低净脱氢速率[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。
2. 氢气共进料可能降低平衡转化率，可能减少总体产率[10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]。
3. 积碳石墨化程度的增加可能需要更高能耗的再生过程[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：
PDH过程中氢气共进料的综合影响揭示了积碳形成、催化剂稳定性和反应机理之间复杂的相互作用。虽然氢气显著提高了催化剂寿命并减少了积碳形成，但它也在过程优化中引入了新的挑战。

未来的研究方向可能包括：
1. 开发先进的催化剂设计，充分利用氢气共进料的优势，同时减轻其缺点，如潜在的产率降低。
2. 研究氢气共进料与其他过程创新（如膜反应器或新型热管理系统）的协同效应，以进一步提高PDH效率。
3. 探索可再生氢源的使用，以提高PDH过程的可持续性。
4. 研究氢气共进料对催化剂长期性能的影响，并在这些条件下开发最佳催化剂再生策略。
5. 研究动态氢气进料策略的潜力，根据反应条件和催化剂状态实时调整氢气浓度。

这些研究方向可能导致更高效、更稳定和更可持续的PDH过程，有可能革新石化工业中的丙烯生产。
