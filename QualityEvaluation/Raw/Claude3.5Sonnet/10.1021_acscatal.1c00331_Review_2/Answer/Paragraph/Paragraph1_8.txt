Hydrogen cofeeding in the propane dehydrogenation (PDH) process significantly affects coke formation, catalyst stability, and reaction mechanisms. This review synthesizes the comprehensive effects and mechanisms of hydrogen cofeeding on these aspects:

1. Coke Formation:
Hydrogen cofeeding substantially reduces coke formation on PDH catalysts [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]. The mechanism involves hydrogenation of coke precursor species, which is crucial in reducing the amount of coke formed on the catalysts [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]. The effect is more pronounced on the catalyst support than on the metal component [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]. Increasing hydrogen partial pressure leads to less dense and compact coke, which can be further softened through hydrogenation [10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8].

2. Catalyst Stability:
Hydrogen cofeeding improves catalyst stability by reducing coke formation and deactivation rates [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]. It helps maintain catalyst activity over time, leading to improved catalyst stability and longer operational periods before regeneration is required [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]. The Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O catalysts exhibit lower deactivation and coke formation than conventional bimetallic catalysts in the presence of hydrogen [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016].

3. Reaction Mechanisms:
Hydrogen enhances the rate of propene formation through hydrogen-assisted dehydrogenation of surface alkyl species [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]. The presence of surface hydrogen atoms shifts the third C-H cleavage step to a higher energy value, making it less competitive compared to the preferred alkene desorption step [10.1039/c5cy00230c]. This improves dehydrogenation selectivity but elevates activation energies [10.1039/c5cy00230c].

4. Energy Barriers:
Hydrogen adsorption on the Pt surface increases the energy barrier for propylene dehydrogenation, which prevents deep reactions that can lead to coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]. The enhanced dehydrogenation rate with hydrogen addition suggests a lowering of energy barriers for the dehydrogenation reaction [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016].

5. Coke Accumulation Patterns:
Hydrogen changes the pattern of coke accumulation, promoting the formation of graphitic coke on the catalyst support rather than disordered coke on active Pt sites [10.1039/c3cp50646k]. The graphitization of coke is positively correlated with hydrogen partial pressure [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2].

6. Acid Site Conversions:
In a hydrogen atmosphere, the acidity of the support is weakened by the conversion of Lewis acid sites to Brønsted acid sites, which helps reduce the rate of coking on the support [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2].

7. Process Efficiency and Performance:
Hydrogen cofeeding improves overall process efficiency by maintaining catalyst activity and reducing coke formation [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]. It allows for longer catalyst lifetimes and more stable operation [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]. However, there is a trade-off, as excessive hydrogen can lead to alkene hydrogenation, reducing the net dehydrogenation rate [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 

The comprehensive effects of hydrogen cofeeding in PDH processes reveal a complex interplay between coke suppression, catalyst stability, and reaction selectivity. While hydrogen cofeeding offers significant benefits in terms of reduced coke formation and improved catalyst longevity, it also introduces challenges that require careful process optimization.

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Developing advanced catalysts that can maximize the benefits of hydrogen cofeeding while minimizing its drawbacks, such as reduced equilibrium conversion.

2. Investigating the optimal hydrogen partial pressure for different catalyst systems to balance coke suppression with desired product yield.

3. Exploring novel reactor designs that can better utilize the benefits of hydrogen cofeeding, such as membrane reactors that can selectively remove hydrogen to shift equilibrium.

4. Studying the long-term effects of hydrogen cofeeding on catalyst performance and developing strategies for efficient catalyst regeneration under these conditions.

5. Integrating hydrogen cofeeding with other process intensification techniques to further enhance PDH efficiency and sustainability.

6. Investigating the potential of using renewable hydrogen sources for cofeeding to improve the overall environmental footprint of PDH processes.

These research directions could lead to more efficient, stable, and sustainable PDH processes, potentially revolutionizing propylene production in the petrochemical industry.

氢气共进料在丙烷脱氢(PDH)过程中显著影响焦炭形成、催化剂稳定性和反应机理。本综述总结了氢气共进料对这些方面的综合影响和机理：

1. 焦炭形成：
氢气共进料大大减少了PDH催化剂上的焦炭形成[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。其机理涉及焦炭前体物种的氢化，这对减少催化剂上形成的焦炭量至关重要[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。这种效果在催化剂载体上比在金属组分上更明显[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]。增加氢分压会导致焦炭变得不那么致密和紧实，并且可以通过进一步氢化而软化[10.1007/s11244-011-9708-8]。

2. 催化剂稳定性：
氢气共进料通过减少焦炭形成和失活速率来改善催化剂稳定性[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。它有助于长期维持催化剂活性，从而改善催化剂稳定性并延长再生前的运行周期[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]。在氢气存在的情况下，Pt/Mg(Ga)(Al)O催化剂表现出比传统双金属催化剂更低的失活和焦炭形成[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。

3. 反应机理：
氢气通过氢辅助表面烷基物种脱氢来增强丙烯的生成速率[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。表面氢原子的存在将第三个C-H断裂步骤转移到更高的能量值，使其与优先的烯烃脱附步骤相比不那么具有竞争力[10.1039/c5cy00230c]。这改善了脱氢选择性，但提高了活化能[10.1039/c5cy00230c]。

4. 能量障碍：
氢在Pt表面的吸附增加了丙烯脱氢的能量障碍，这防止了可能导致焦炭形成的深度反应[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]。氢气添加后脱氢速率的提高表明脱氢反应的能量障碍降低[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。

5. 焦炭积累模式：
氢改变了焦炭积累的模式，促进在催化剂载体上形成石墨化焦炭，而不是在活性Pt位点上形成无序焦炭[10.1039/c3cp50646k]。焦炭的石墨化程度与氢分压呈正相关[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]。

6. 酸性位转换：
在氢气氛围中，载体的酸性通过Lewis酸性位向Brønsted酸性位的转换而减弱，这有助于减少载体上的焦炭形成速率[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331_2]。

7. 工艺效率和性能：
氢气共进料通过维持催化剂活性和减少焦炭形成来提高整体工艺效率[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]。它允许更长的催化剂寿命和更稳定的运行[10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]。然而，存在一个权衡，因为过量的氢气可能导致烯烃氢化，从而降低净脱氢速率[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.016]。

综合理解与未来展望：

PDH过程中氢气共进料的综合效应揭示了焦炭抑制、催化剂稳定性和反应选择性之间复杂的相互作用。虽然氢气共进料在减少焦炭形成和改善催化剂寿命方面提供了显著的益处，但它也带来了需要仔细优化过程的挑战。

未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 开发先进的催化剂，可以最大化氢气共进料的益处，同时最小化其缺点，如平衡转化率的降低。

2. 研究不同催化剂系统的最佳氢分压，以平衡焦炭抑制和所需产品产率。

3. 探索能更好地利用氢气共进料优势的新型反应器设计，如可以选择性去除氢气以移动平衡的膜反应器。

4. 研究氢气共进料对催化剂长期性能的影响，并在这些条件下开发高效的催化剂再生策略。

5. 将氢气共进料与其他过程强化技术相结合，以进一步提高PDH效率和可持续性。

6. 研究使用可再生氢源进行共进料的潜力，以改善PDH过程的整体环境足迹。

这些研究方向可能导致更高效、更稳定和更可持续的PDH过程，有可能彻底改变石化工业中的丙烯生产。
