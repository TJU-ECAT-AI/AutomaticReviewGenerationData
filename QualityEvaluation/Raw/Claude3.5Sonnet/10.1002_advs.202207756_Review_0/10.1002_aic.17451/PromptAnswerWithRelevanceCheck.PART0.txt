<quotes>

[1] "Propylene is one of the most important building blocks for producing a wide variety of chemicals such as propylene oxide, acrylonitrile, and so on. With the consumption of fossil energy, the conventional propylene production process such as naphtha steam cracking can no longer meet the growing demands. The direct propane dehydrogenation (PDH) to produce propylene is thus attracting much attention due to the relatively abundant feedstock and less by-products."

[2] "To date, the Oleflex process with PtSn/Al2O3 catalyst and the Catofin process with Cr/Al2O3 catalyst are the most widely used technologies for the industrial PDH reaction. However, the high cost of Pt and serious pollution issues of Cr(VI) greatly restrict the further development."

[3] "Among developed alternatives, cobalt-based catalysts have attracted much attention due to its efficient CH activation ability and inexpensive, nontoxic characters."

<EnglishVersionAnswer>

The growing demand for propylene has led to the development of new production methods, particularly propane dehydrogenation (PDH). This shift is driven by the fact that conventional methods like naphtha steam cracking can no longer meet the increasing demand. PDH has gained attention due to its use of relatively abundant feedstock and production of fewer by-products.

Currently, the most widely used industrial PDH technologies are the Oleflex process using PtSn/Al2O3 catalysts and the Catofin process using Cr/Al2O3 catalysts. However, these processes face limitations due to the high cost of platinum and pollution issues associated with chromium.

As a result, there has been increased interest in developing alternative catalysts. Cobalt-based catalysts have emerged as promising candidates due to their efficient C-H activation ability, low cost, and non-toxic nature.
