      <English>
          The growing demand for propylene, coupled with the availability of low-cost propane from shale gas, has renewed interest in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as a production method. This has led to increased research and development of various catalysts for PDH, including those based on Ga, Zn, Co, Fe, V, Zr, and nanocarbon materials.

Among these, Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts have shown particularly promising results, exhibiting high stability and propylene selectivity (90%) for propane dehydrogenation. However, the understanding of these catalysts has evolved over time, with ongoing debates about the nature of the active Ga species.

Some researchers, like Bell and coworkers, propose that single [GaH]2+ sites are the active species. In contrast, Schreiber et al. suggest that Ga+H+ pair sites are the stable active species. This latter model has been supported by results from other laboratories.

Recent research by Phadke et al. has contributed to our current knowledge by demonstrating that Ga-exchanged species associated with Al-pairs in Ga/H-MFI catalyze both propane and butane dehydrogenation, while isolated Ga species did not show this activity. This finding highlights the importance of the specific arrangement and interaction of Ga species within the zeolite framework for catalytic activity.

These ongoing debates and research findings demonstrate the complexity of understanding the exact nature of active sites in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH. The evolving knowledge in this area continues to drive improvements in catalyst design and performance for propylene production, addressing the growing demand for this important chemical feedstock.
