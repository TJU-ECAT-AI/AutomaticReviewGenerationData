<English>
The growing demand for propylene has significantly influenced the development of new production methods, particularly propane dehydrogenation (PDH). Global propylene consumption reached 90 million tons in 2015 and is expected to increase to 191 megatons by 2030, creating a substantial gap between supply and demand [10.1002/cctc.201500916]. This gap, coupled with the increased availability of propane from shale gas production, has led to a surge in interest in PDH technology, which now accounts for 10% of global propylene production [10.1002/advs.202207756_0].

The understanding of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH has evolved considerably over time, but there remains an ongoing debate about the nature of active Ga species. Various Ga species have been proposed as the active center for PDH, including Ga+, [GaO]+, [Ga(OH)2]+, [GaOH]2+, [GaH]2+, [GaH2]+, and Ga+H+ pair [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]. This diversity of proposed active species reflects the complexity of the catalyst system and the challenges in definitively identifying the active sites.

Recent research has contributed significantly to our current knowledge of these catalysts. Lercher's group found that in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, there is a very low enthalpy barrier of 56 kJ·mol^-1 for heterolytically activating the propane C-H bond. This activation occurs on [GaH]2+ species in conjunction with a basic framework oxygen of the Brønsted-acid site [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]. 

However, other researchers have proposed alternative active species. For instance, Maeno et al. investigated In-CHA catalysts and suggested that [InH2]+ ions are the catalytic active sites for PDH reactions, showing better performance than [GaH]2+ sites [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012].

Studies combining in situ transmission Fourier-transform infrared (FTIR) spectroscopy, pulse titration studies, and PDH kinetics have demonstrated that both Ga speciation and PDH performance are dependent on the Ga/Al ratios and Al distributions within the zeolites [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]. This finding highlights the importance of considering not just the presence of Ga species, but also their interaction with the zeolite structure and composition.

The role of Brønsted acid sites has been further investigated, as they are considered to catalyze undesired reactions such as cracking, dehydrocyclization, and coking. To address this issue, researchers have synthesized aluminum-free gallosilicate MFI catalysts without Ga introduction to modify the acidity caused by the presence of aluminum [10.1039/d0cs00983k].

Despite these advancements, the precise nature of the active sites under reaction conditions and the roles of Brønsted and Lewis acid sites in Ga-containing MFI catalysts remain subjects of ongoing research and debate in the scientific community [10.1039/d0cs00983k].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolving landscape of propylene production, particularly through PDH using Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, presents both challenges and opportunities for future research. The ongoing debate about the nature of active Ga species highlights the complexity of these catalytic systems and underscores the need for advanced in-situ characterization techniques to elucidate the true nature of active sites under reaction conditions.

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Developing novel in-situ characterization methods to directly observe the formation and behavior of active Ga species during PDH reactions.
2. Exploring the synergistic effects between different Ga species and their interaction with the zeolite framework to optimize catalyst performance.
3. Investigating the potential of bimetallic or multi-component catalysts that combine the benefits of Ga with other elements to enhance activity, selectivity, and stability.
4. Studying the impact of zeolite framework topology and composition on the distribution and activity of Ga species to design more efficient catalysts.
5. Developing strategies to mitigate coke formation and catalyst deactivation, potentially through the careful balancing of acid-base properties or the introduction of promoters.

These research avenues could lead to the development of more efficient and stable PDH catalysts, ultimately contributing to meeting the growing global demand for propylene in a more sustainable manner.

<Chinese>
日益增长的丙烯需求显著影响了新生产方法的发展，特别是丙烷脱氢（PDH）技术。全球丙烯消费量在2015年达到9000万吨，预计到2030年将增加到1.91亿吨，这在供需之间造成了巨大的差距[10.1002/cctc.201500916]。这种差距，加上页岩气生产使丙烷供应增加，导致PDH技术受到广泛关注，目前占全球丙烯产量的10%[10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。

对Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂在PDH中的理解随时间不断发展，但关于活性Ga物种的本质仍存在持续争论。多种Ga物种被提出作为PDH的活性中心，包括Ga+, [GaO]+, [Ga(OH)2]+, [GaOH]2+, [GaH]2+, [GaH2]+, 和Ga+H+对[10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。这种多样性反映了催化剂系统的复杂性和明确识别活性位点的挑战。

最近的研究显著增进了我们对这些催化剂的认知。Lercher小组发现，在Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂中，异裂活化丙烷C-H键的焓垒非常低，仅为56 kJ·mol^-1。这种活化发生在[GaH]2+物种上，并与Brønsted酸位点的基本骨架氧配合[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

然而，其他研究者提出了替代的活性物种。例如，Maeno等人研究了In-CHA催化剂，并建议[InH2]+离子是PDH反应的催化活性位点，表现出比[GaH]2+位点更好的性能[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

结合原位傅里叶变换红外（FTIR）光谱、脉冲滴定研究和PDH动力学的研究表明，Ga物种形态和PDH性能都依赖于Ga/Al比例和沸石中Al的分布[10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。这一发现强调了不仅要考虑Ga物种的存在，还要考虑它们与沸石结构和成分的相互作用的重要性。

Brønsted酸位点的作用也被进一步研究，因为它们被认为会催化不需要的反应，如裂解、脱氢环化和积碳。为解决这个问题，研究人员合成了不含Al的镓硅MFI催化剂，以改变由Al存在引起的酸性[10.1039/d0cs00983k]。

尽管取得了这些进展，但在反应条件下活性位点的确切性质以及Ga含MFI催化剂中Brønsted和Lewis酸位点的作用仍然是科学界持续研究和争论的主题[10.1039/d0cs00983k]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：丙烯生产领域的演变，特别是使用Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂的PDH技术，为未来研究提供了挑战和机遇。关于活性Ga物种本质的持续争论突显了这些催化系统的复杂性，并强调了需要先进的原位表征技术来阐明反应条件下真正活性位点的本质。

未来研究方向可能集中在：

1. 开发新型原位表征方法，直接观察PDH反应过程中活性Ga物种的形成和行为。
2. 探索不同Ga物种之间的协同效应及其与沸石骨架的相互作用，以优化催化剂性能。
3. 研究结合Ga与其他元素优势的双金属或多组分催化剂的潜力，以提高活性、选择性和稳定性。
4. 研究沸石骨架拓扑结构和成分对Ga物种分布和活性的影响，以设计更高效的催化剂。
5. 开发减少积碳和催化剂失活的策略，可能通过仔细平衡酸碱性质或引入助剂来实现。

这些研究方向可能导致开发出更高效和稳定的PDH催化剂，最终以更可持续的方式满足全球日益增长的丙烯需求。
