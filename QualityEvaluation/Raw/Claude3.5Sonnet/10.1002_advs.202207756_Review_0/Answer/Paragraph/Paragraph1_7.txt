<English>
The growing demand for propylene has significantly influenced the development of new production methods, particularly propane dehydrogenation (PDH). Global propylene consumption reached 90 million tons in 2015 and is expected to increase to 191 megatons by 2030, creating a substantial gap between supply and demand [10.1002/cctc.201500916]. This gap, coupled with the increased availability of propane from shale gas production, has led to a surge in interest in PDH technology, which now accounts for 10% of global propylene production [10.1002/advs.202207756_0].

The understanding of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH has evolved considerably over time, but there remains an ongoing debate about the nature of active Ga species. Various Ga species have been proposed as the active center for PDH, including Ga+, [GaO]+, [Ga(OH)2]+, [GaOH]2+, [GaH]2+, [GaH2]+, and Ga+H+ pair [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]. This diversity of proposed active species reflects the complexity of the catalyst system and the challenges in definitively identifying the active sites.

Recent research has contributed significantly to our current knowledge of these catalysts. Lercher's group found that in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, there is a very low enthalpy barrier of 56 kJ·mol^-1 for heterolytically activating the propane C-H bond. This activation occurs on [GaH]2+ species in conjunction with a basic framework oxygen of the Brønsted-acid site [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]. 

Studies combining in situ transmission Fourier-transform infrared (FTIR) spectroscopy, pulse titration studies, and PDH kinetics have demonstrated that both Ga speciation and PDH performance are dependent on the Ga/Al ratios and Al distributions within the zeolites [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]. This finding highlights the importance of considering not just the presence of Ga species, but also their interaction with the zeolite structure and composition.

The role of Brønsted acid sites has been further investigated, as they are considered to catalyze undesired reactions such as cracking, dehydrocyclization, and coking. To address this issue, researchers have synthesized aluminum-free gallosilicate MFI catalysts without Ga introduction to modify the acidity caused by the presence of aluminum [10.1039/d0cs00983k].

Despite these advancements, the precise nature of the active sites under reaction conditions and the roles of Brønsted and Lewis acid sites in Ga-containing MFI catalysts remain subjects of ongoing research and debate in the scientific community [10.1039/d0cs00983k].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolving landscape of propylene production through PDH, particularly using Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, presents both challenges and opportunities for future research. The ongoing debate about the nature of active Ga species suggests that there is still much to be discovered about the fundamental mechanisms of these catalysts.

Future research directions may include:

1. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques: Developing and applying more sophisticated in-situ spectroscopic methods could provide real-time insights into the formation and transformation of active Ga species under reaction conditions.

2. Computational modeling: Enhancing computational models to better predict the behavior of Ga species in different zeolite environments could guide the design of more efficient catalysts.

3. Tailored zeolite synthesis: Exploring new methods to control the distribution and interaction of Ga species within the zeolite framework could lead to catalysts with improved activity and selectivity.

4. Hybrid catalysts: Investigating the potential of combining Ga with other elements or incorporating Ga species into novel support materials might result in synergistic effects and enhanced catalytic performance.

5. Operando studies: Conducting more operando studies to bridge the gap between idealized laboratory conditions and industrial reactor environments could provide valuable insights for scaling up these catalysts.

These research directions could potentially lead to breakthroughs in catalyst design, ultimately contributing to more efficient and sustainable propylene production to meet the growing global demand.

<Chinese>
日益增长的丙烯需求显著影响了新生产方法的发展，尤其是丙烷脱氢（PDH）技术。2015年全球丙烯消费量达到9000万吨，预计到2030年将增至1.91亿吨，这在供需之间造成了巨大的差距[10.1002/cctc.201500916]。这一差距，加上页岩气生产带来的丙烷供应增加，导致PDH技术受到广泛关注，目前已占全球丙烯产量的10％[10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。

对Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂在PDH中的理解随时间不断发展，但关于活性Ga物种的本质仍存在争议。各种Ga物种被提议作为PDH的活性中心，包括Ga+、[GaO]+、[Ga(OH)2]+、[GaOH]2+、[GaH]2+、[GaH]2+和Ga+H+对[10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。这些提议的活性物种的多样性反映了催化剂系统的复杂性以及确定活性位点的挑战。

近期研究为我们对这些催化剂的认知做出了重大贡献。Lercher小组发现，在Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂中，异裂活化丙烷C-H键的焓垒很低，仅为56 kJ·mol^-1。这种活化发生在[GaH]2+物种上，并与Brønsted酸位点的碱性骨架氧共同作用[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

结合原位傅里叶变换红外（FTIR）光谱、脉冲滴定研究和PDH动力学的研究表明，Ga物种的形态和PDH性能都依赖于沸石中的Ga/Al比例和Al分布[10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。这一发现强调了不仅要考虑Ga物种的存在，还要考虑它们与沸石结构和组成的相互作用。

对Brønsted酸位点的作用进行了进一步研究，因为它们被认为会催化不希望发生的反应，如裂解、脱氢环化和结焦。为解决这个问题，研究人员合成了不含铝的镓硅MFI催化剂，以修改由铝存在引起的酸性[10.1039/d0cs00983k]。

尽管取得了这些进展，但在反应条件下活性位点的精确本质以及Brønsted和Lewis酸位点在含Ga的MFI催化剂中的作用仍然是科学界ongoing研究和讨论的主题[10.1039/d0cs00983k]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：通过PDH生产丙烯的不断发展，尤其是使用Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂，为未来研究带来了挑战和机遇。关于活性Ga物种本质的持续争论表明，我们对这些催化剂的基本机理还有很多需要探索的地方。

未来研究方向可能包括：

1. 先进的原位表征技术：开发和应用更复杂的原位光谱方法，可以提供反应条件下活性Ga物种形成和转化的实时洞察。

2. 计算机建模：增强计算模型以更好地预测不同沸石环境中Ga物种的行为，可以指导更高效催化剂的设计。

3. 定制沸石合成：探索新方法来控制沸石骨架中Ga物种的分布和相互作用，可能导致活性和选择性更高的催化剂。

4. 混合催化剂：研究将Ga与其他元素结合或将Ga物种结合到新型载体材料中的潜力，可能产生协同效应并提高催化性能。

5. 操作中研究：进行更多操作中研究，以弥合理想实验室条件和工业反应器环境之间的差距，可以为这些催化剂的放大提供宝贵的见解。

这些研究方向可能导致催化剂设计的突破，最终为更高效和可持续的丙烯生产做出贡献，以满足不断增长的全球需求。
