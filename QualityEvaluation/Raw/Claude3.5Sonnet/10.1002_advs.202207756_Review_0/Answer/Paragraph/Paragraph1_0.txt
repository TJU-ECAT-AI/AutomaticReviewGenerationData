<English>
The growing demand for propylene has significantly influenced the development of new production methods, particularly propane dehydrogenation (PDH). Global propylene consumption reached 90 million tons in 2015 and is expected to increase to 191 megatons by 2030, creating a substantial gap between supply and demand [10.1002/cctc.201500916]. This gap, coupled with the increased availability of propane from shale gas production, has led to a surge in interest in PDH technology, which now accounts for 10% of global propylene production [10.1002/advs.202207756_0].

The understanding of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH has evolved considerably over time, but there remains an ongoing debate about the nature of active Ga species. Various Ga species have been proposed as the active center for PDH, including Ga+, [GaO]+, [Ga(OH)2]+, [GaOH]2+, [GaH]2+, [GaH2]+, and Ga+H+ pair [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]. This diversity of proposed active species reflects the complexity of the catalyst system and the challenges in definitively identifying the active sites.

Recent research has contributed significantly to our current knowledge of these catalysts. Notably, studies combining in situ transmission Fourier-transform infrared (FTIR) spectroscopy, pulse titration studies, and PDH kinetics have demonstrated that both Ga speciation and PDH performance are dependent on the Ga/Al ratios and Al distributions within the zeolites [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]. This finding highlights the importance of considering not just the presence of Ga species, but also their interaction with the zeolite structure and composition.

Lercher's group found that in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, there is a very low enthalpy barrier of 56 kJ·mol^-1 for heterolytically activating the propane C-H bond. This activation occurs on [GaH]2+ species in conjunction with a basic framework oxygen of the Brønsted-acid site [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]. However, other researchers have proposed alternative active species. For instance, Maeno et al. investigated In-CHA catalysts and suggested that [InH2]+ ions are the catalytic active sites for PDH reactions, showing better performance than [GaH]2+ sites [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012].

The role of Brønsted acid sites has been further investigated, as they are considered to catalyze undesired reactions such as cracking, dehydrocyclization, and coking. To address this issue, researchers have synthesized aluminum-free gallosilicate MFI catalysts without Ga introduction to modify the acidity caused by the presence of aluminum [10.1039/d0cs00983k].

Despite these advancements, the precise nature of the active sites under reaction conditions and the roles of Brønsted and Lewis acid sites in Ga-containing MFI catalysts remain subjects of ongoing research and debate in the scientific community [10.1039/d0cs00983k].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolving landscape of propylene production, particularly through PDH, reflects the dynamic nature of the petrochemical industry and its response to changing market demands and resource availability. The ongoing research into Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH demonstrates the complexity of heterogeneous catalysis and the challenges in developing efficient, stable, and selective catalysts.

Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic nature of active sites under reaction conditions.
2. Computational modeling to predict and design optimal catalyst compositions and structures.
3. Exploration of novel support materials or promoters to enhance the stability and selectivity of Ga-based catalysts.
4. Development of multifunctional catalysts that can combine PDH with other value-adding reactions in a single process.
5. Investigation of the potential of other metal species (e.g., In, Zn) in zeolite frameworks for PDH, building on the insights gained from Ga/H-ZSM-5 studies.

These research directions could lead to more efficient and sustainable propylene production methods, addressing the growing demand while minimizing environmental impact. The continued evolution of our understanding of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts may also have broader implications for the design of catalysts for other important industrial processes.

<Chinese>
丙烯需求的增长显著影响了新生产方法的发展，特别是丙烷脱氢（PDH）。全球丙烯消费量在2015年达到9000万吨，预计到2030年将增至1.91亿吨，这造成了供需之间的巨大差距[10.1002/cctc.201500916]。这一差距，加上页岩气生产带来的丙烷供应增加，导致PDH技术受到极大关注，目前占全球丙烯产量的10%[10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。

Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂在PDH中的理解随时间不断发展，但关于活性Ga物种的本质仍存在争议。各种Ga物种被提出可能是PDH的活性中心，包括Ga+、[GaO]+、[Ga(OH)2]+、[GaOH]2+、[GaH]2+、[GaH2]+和Ga+H+对[10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。这些提议的活性物种的多样性反映了催化剂系统的复杂性和明确识别活性位点的挑战。

近期研究对我们当前对这些催化剂的了解做出了重大贡献。值得注意的是，结合原位傅里叶变换红外（FTIR）光谱、脉冲滴定研究和PDH动力学的研究表明，Ga物种和PDH性能都依赖于Ga/Al比率和沸石中Al的分布[10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。这一发现强调了不仅要考虑Ga物种的存在，还要考虑它们与沸石结构和组成的相互作用的重要性。

Lercher小组发现，在Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂中，丙烷C-H键的异裂活化只需很低的焓垒（56 kJ·mol^-1）。这种活化发生在[GaH]2+物种上，并与Brønsted酸位点的碱性骨架氧相结合[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。然而，其他研究人员提出了替代的活性物种。例如，Maeno等人研究了In-CHA催化剂，并建议[InH2]+离子是PDH反应的催化活性位点，表现优于[GaH]2+位点[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

Brønsted酸位点的作用也得到了进一步研究，因为它们被认为会催化不需要的反应，如裂解、脱氢环化和积炭。为解决这个问题，研究人员合成了不含铝的镓硅酸盐MFI催化剂，以改变由铝存在引起的酸度[10.1039/d0cs00983k]。

尽管取得了这些进展，但在反应条件下活性位点的精确本质以及Ga含MFI催化剂中Brønsted和Lewis酸位点的作用仍然是科学界持续研究和讨论的主题[10.1039/d0cs00983k]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：丙烯生产格局的演变，特别是通过PDH，反映了石油化工行业的动态性质及其对不断变化的市场需求和资源可用性的响应。对Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂在PDH中的持续研究表明了多相催化的复杂性，以及在开发高效、稳定和选择性催化剂方面的挑战。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 先进的原位表征技术，以更好地理解反应条件下活性位点的动态本质。
2. 计算机建模，以预测和设计最佳的催化剂组成和结构。
3. 探索新型载体材料或助剂，以提高Ga基催化剂的稳定性和选择性。
4. 开发多功能催化剂，可在单一过程中结合PDH和其他增值反应。
5. 基于从Ga/H-ZSM-5研究中获得的见解，研究其他金属物种（如In、Zn）在沸石骨架中用于PDH的潜力。

这些研究方向可能导致更高效和可持续的丙烯生产方法，满足不断增长的需求，同时最大限度地减少环境影响。我们对Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂理解的持续演变可能对其他重要工业过程的催化剂设计也有更广泛的影响。
