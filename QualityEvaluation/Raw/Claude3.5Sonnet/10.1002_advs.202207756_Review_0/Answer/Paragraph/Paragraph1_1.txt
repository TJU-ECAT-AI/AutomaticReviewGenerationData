<English>
The growing demand for propylene has significantly influenced the development of new production methods, particularly propane dehydrogenation (PDH). Global propylene demand is expected to increase from 130 megatonnes in 2019 to 191 megatonnes by 2030, creating a substantial gap between supply and demand [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]. This gap, coupled with the increased availability of propane from shale gas production, has led to a surge in interest in PDH technology, which now accounts for 10% of global propylene production [10.1002/advs.202207756_0].

The understanding of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH has evolved considerably over time, but there remains an ongoing debate about the nature of active Ga species. Various Ga species have been proposed as the active center for PDH, including Ga+, [GaO]+, [Ga(OH)2]+, [GaOH]2+, [GaH]2+, [GaH2]+, and Ga+H+ pair [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]. This diversity of proposed active species reflects the complexity of the catalyst system and the challenges in definitively identifying the active sites.

Recent research has contributed significantly to our current knowledge of these catalysts. Lercher's group found that in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, there is a very low enthalpy barrier of 56 kJ·mol^-1 for heterolytically activating the propane C-H bond. This activation occurs on [GaH]2+ species in conjunction with a basic framework oxygen of the Brønsted-acid site [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]. 

Other researchers have proposed alternative active species. For instance, Maeno et al. investigated In-CHA catalysts and suggested that [InH2]+ ions are the catalytic active sites for PDH reactions, showing better performance than [GaH]2+ sites [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012].

Studies combining in situ transmission Fourier-transform infrared (FTIR) spectroscopy, pulse titration studies, and PDH kinetics have demonstrated that both Ga speciation and PDH performance are dependent on the Ga/Al ratios and Al distributions within the zeolites [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]. This finding highlights the importance of considering not just the presence of Ga species, but also their interaction with the zeolite structure and composition.

The role of Brønsted acid sites has been further investigated, as they are considered to catalyze undesired reactions such as cracking, dehydrocyclization, and coking. To address this issue, researchers have synthesized aluminum-free gallosilicate MFI catalysts without Ga introduction to modify the acidity caused by the presence of aluminum [10.1039/d0cs00983k].

Despite these advancements, the precise nature of the active sites under reaction conditions and the roles of Brønsted and Lewis acid sites in Ga-containing MFI catalysts remain subjects of ongoing research and debate in the scientific community [10.1039/d0cs00983k].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolving landscape of propylene production, particularly through PDH, reflects the dynamic nature of the petrochemical industry and its response to changing market demands and resource availability. The ongoing debate about the nature of active Ga species in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts highlights the complexity of heterogeneous catalysis and the challenges in developing highly efficient and stable catalysts for PDH.

Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic behavior of Ga species under reaction conditions.
2. Computational studies to elucidate the reaction mechanisms and energetics of different Ga species in the zeolite framework.
3. Development of novel synthesis methods to control the distribution and nature of Ga species in the catalyst.
4. Exploration of bimetallic or multi-component catalysts that combine the advantages of different active species.
5. Investigation of the long-term stability and regeneration of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts under industrial conditions.

These research directions could lead to the development of more efficient, stable, and selective catalysts for PDH, ultimately contributing to meeting the growing global demand for propylene in a more sustainable manner.

<Chinese>
由于丙烯需求的不断增长，推动了新生产方法的发展，尤其是丙烷脱氢（PDH）技术。全球丙烯需求预计将从2019年的1.3亿吨增加到2030年的1.91亿吨，造成了供需之间的巨大差距 [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。这一差距，加上页岩气生产使丙烷供应量增加，导致PDH技术受到广泛关注，目前已占全球丙烯生产的10% [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。

对Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂在PDH中的理解随时间不断发展，但关于活性Ga物种的性质仍存在争议。多种Ga物种被提议作为PDH的活性中心，包括Ga+、[GaO]+、[Ga(OH)2]+、[GaOH]2+、[GaH]2+、[GaH2]+和Ga+H+对 [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。这些提议的活性物种的多样性反映了催化剂系统的复杂性和明确识别活性位点的挑战。

近期研究对我们当前对这些催化剂的认知做出了重大贡献。Lercher小组发现，在Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂中，丙烷C-H键的异裂活化存在非常低的焓垒（56 kJ·mol^-1）。这种活化发生在[GaH]2+物种上，并与Brønsted酸位点的碱性骨架氧协同作用 [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

其他研究人员提出了替代的活性物种。例如，Maeno等人研究了In-CHA催化剂，并建议[InH2]+离子是PDH反应的催化活性位点，表现优于[GaH]2+位点 [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

结合原位傅里叶变换红外（FTIR）光谱、脉冲滴定研究和PDH动力学的研究表明，Ga物种和PDH性能都依赖于Ga/Al比率和沸石中Al的分布 [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。这一发现强调了不仅要考虑Ga物种的存在，还要考虑它们与沸石结构和组成的相互作用的重要性。

对Brønsted酸位点的作用进行了进一步研究，因为它们被认为会催化不希望发生的反应，如裂解、脱氢环化和积碳。为解决这个问题，研究人员合成了不含铝的镓硅MFI催化剂，以改变由铝存在引起的酸性 [10.1039/d0cs00983k]。

尽管取得了这些进展，但在反应条件下活性位点的精确性质以及Ga含MFI催化剂中Brønsted和Lewis酸位点的作用仍然是科学界正在进行的研究和争论的主题 [10.1039/d0cs00983k]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：丙烯生产格局的演变，特别是通过PDH技术，反映了石油化工行业的动态性质以及其对不断变化的市场需求和资源可用性的响应。关于Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂中活性Ga物种性质的持续争论突显了多相催化的复杂性，以及开发高效稳定的PDH催化剂的挑战。

未来的研究努力可能会集中在：

1. 先进的原位表征技术，以更好地理解Ga物种在反应条件下的动态行为。
2. 计算研究，阐明沸石骨架中不同Ga物种的反应机理和能量学。
3. 开发新型合成方法，控制催化剂中Ga物种的分布和性质。
4. 探索双金属或多组分催化剂，结合不同活性物种的优势。
5. 研究Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂在工业条件下的长期稳定性和再生。

这些研究方向可能导致开发出更高效、稳定和选择性的PDH催化剂，最终以更可持续的方式为满足不断增长的全球丙烯需求做出贡献。
