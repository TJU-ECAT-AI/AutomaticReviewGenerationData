The growing demand for propylene has significantly influenced the development of new production methods, particularly propane dehydrogenation (PDH). This shift has been driven by several factors:

1. Increasing global demand: Propylene consumption reached 90 million tons in 2015 and is expected to grow to 191 megatons by 2030 [10.1002/cctc.201500916] [10.1002/advs.202207756_0].

2. Changes in feedstock availability: The shale gas boom has led to a shift from naphtha to propane as a feedstock, creating a gap in propylene supply [10.1039/d0cs00983k].

3. Limitations of traditional methods: Conventional processes like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) can no longer meet the growing demand [10.1039/d0cs00814a].

PDH has emerged as a promising alternative, currently accounting for about 10% of global propylene production [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]. The development of efficient catalysts for PDH has been a key focus of research, with Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts gaining significant attention.

The understanding of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH has evolved considerably over time, but there remains an ongoing debate about the nature of active Ga species. Various Ga species have been proposed as active centers for PDH, including:

1. Ga+, [GaO]+, [Ga(OH)2]+, [GaOH]2+, [GaH]2+, [GaH2]+, and Ga+H+ pair [10.1002/advs.202207756_0].
2. Tetrahedrally coordinated Ga3+ species [10.1039/d1gc03700e].
3. Lewis(Ga+)-Brønsted acid pairs [10.1039/d0cs00983k].

Recent research has contributed significantly to our current knowledge of these catalysts:

1. Lercher's group found that in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, there is a very low enthalpy barrier of 56 kJ·mol^-1 for heterolytically activating the propane C-H bond on [GaH]2+ species in conjunction with a basic framework oxygen of the Brønsted-acid site [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012].

2. Studies have shown that both Ga speciation and PDH performance are dependent on the Ga/Al ratios and Al distributions within the zeolites [10.1002/advs.202207756_0].

3. The addition of 3-mercaptopropyl-trimethoxysilane (MPS) to Ga/MFI catalysts reduces Brønsted acid sites and increases Lewis acid sites, resulting in improved performance [10.1039/d1gc03700e].

4. Phadke et al. demonstrated that Ga-exchanged species associated with Al-pairs in Ga/H-MFI catalyze both propane and butane dehydrogenation, while isolated Ga species did not [10.1039/d1sc05866e].

These findings highlight the complexity of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts and the importance of the interplay between different species (Ga, H, and framework oxygen) in creating active sites for PDH. The ongoing debates and research findings demonstrate the need for further investigation to fully understand and optimize these catalysts for industrial applications.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolving landscape of propylene production, particularly through PDH using Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, presents both challenges and opportunities for future research. The complex nature of the active Ga species and their interactions with the zeolite framework suggests that a multifaceted approach combining advanced characterization techniques, computational modeling, and in-situ studies will be crucial for further progress.

Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Developing more precise methods for controlling Ga speciation during catalyst preparation to optimize catalytic performance.
2. Investigating the dynamic behavior of Ga species under reaction conditions to better understand catalyst deactivation and regeneration processes.
3. Exploring novel zeolite frameworks or hierarchical structures that could enhance the stability and accessibility of active Ga species.
4. Integrating machine learning algorithms to predict optimal catalyst compositions and reaction conditions based on the growing body of experimental data.

These advancements could lead to more efficient and sustainable propylene production processes, helping to bridge the growing gap between supply and demand while minimizing environmental impact.

日益增长的丙烯需求显著影响了新生产方法的发展，尤其是丙烷脱氢（PDH）。这种转变由几个因素推动：

1. 全球需求增加：丙烯消耗量在2015年达到9000万吨，预计到2030年将增长到1.91亿吨 [10.1002/cctc.201500916] [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。

2. 原料可用性变化：页岩气繁荣导致原料从石脑油转向丙烷，造成丙烯供应缺口 [10.1039/d0cs00983k]。

3. 传统方法的局限性：蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂化（FCC）等常规工艺已无法满足不断增长的需求 [10.1039/d0cs00814a]。

PDH已成为一种有前景的替代方法，目前占全球丙烯产量的约10% [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。开发高效的PDH催化剂一直是研究的重点，其中Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂受到了广泛关注。

对Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂在PDH中的理解随时间不断发展，但关于活性Ga物种的本质仍存在争议。已经提出了多种Ga物种作为PDH的活性中心，包括：

1. Ga+、[GaO]+、[Ga(OH)2]+、[GaOH]2+、[GaH]2+、[GaH]2+和Ga+H+对 [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。
2. 四面体配位的Ga3+物种 [10.1039/d1gc03700e]。
3. Lewis(Ga+)-Brønsted酸对 [10.1039/d0cs00983k]。

最近的研究对我们当前对这些催化剂的认知做出了重大贡献：

1. Lercher小组发现，在Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂中，[GaH]2+物种与Brønsted酸位的碱性骨架氧共同作用，使丙烷C-H键的杂裂活化能垒降低到56 kJ·mol^-1 [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

2. 研究表明，Ga物种分布和PDH性能都依赖于沸石中的Ga/Al比例和Al分布 [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。

3. 向Ga/MFI催化剂中添加3-巯丙基三甲氧基硅烷（MPS）可减少Brønsted酸位并增加Lewis酸位，从而提高性能 [10.1039/d1gc03700e]。

4. Phadke等人证明，与Al对相关的Ga交换物种在Ga/H-MFI中可以催化丙烷和丁烷脱氢，而孤立的Ga物种则不能 [10.1039/d1sc05866e]。

这些发现凸显了Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂的复杂性，以及不同物种（Ga、H和骨架氧）之间相互作用在形成PDH活性位点中的重要性。持续的争论和研究发现表明，需要进一步调查以充分理解和优化这些催化剂以应用于工业生产。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：丙烯生产领域的发展，特别是使用Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂的PDH技术，为未来研究提供了挑战和机遇。活性Ga物种的复杂性及其与沸石骨架的相互作用表明，结合先进表征技术、计算模拟和原位研究的多方面方法对于取得进一步进展至关重要。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 开发更精确的方法来控制催化剂制备过程中的Ga物种分布，以优化催化性能。
2. 研究反应条件下Ga物种的动态行为，以更好地理解催化剂失活和再生过程。
3. 探索新型沸石骨架或分级结构，可能增强活性Ga物种的稳定性和可及性。
4. 整合机器学习算法，基于不断增长的实验数据预测最佳催化剂组成和反应条件。

这些进展可能导致更高效和可持续的丙烯生产工艺，有助于弥合供需不断扩大的差距，同时最大限度地减少环境影响。
