Recent research has contributed significantly to our current knowledge of these catalysts. Lercher's group found that in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, there is a very low enthalpy barrier of 56 kJ·mol^-1 for heterolytically activating the propane C-H bond. This activation occurs on [GaH]2+ species in conjunction with a basic framework oxygen of the Brønsted-acid site [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]. 

Other researchers have proposed alternative active species. For instance, Maeno et al. investigated In-CHA catalysts and suggested that [InH2]+ ions are the catalytic active sites for PDH reactions, showing better performance than [GaH]2+ sites [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012].

Studies combining in situ transmission Fourier-transform infrared (FTIR) spectroscopy, pulse titration studies, and PDH kinetics have demonstrated that both Ga speciation and PDH performance are dependent on the Ga/Al ratios and Al distributions within the zeolites [10.1002/advs.202207756\_0]. This finding highlights the importance of considering not just the presence of Ga species, but also their interaction with the zeolite structure and composition.

The role of Brønsted acid sites has been further investigated, as they are considered to catalyze undesired reactions such as cracking, dehydrocyclization, and coking. To address this issue, researchers have synthesized aluminum-free gallosilicate MFI catalysts without Ga introduction to modify the acidity caused by the presence of aluminum [10.1039/d0cs00983k].

Despite these advancements, the precise nature of the active sites under reaction conditions and the roles of Brønsted and Lewis acid sites in Ga-containing MFI catalysts remain subjects of ongoing research and debate in the scientific community [10.1039/d0cs00983k].

3. Development of novel synthesis methods to control the distribution and nature of Ga species in the catalyst.
4. Exploration of bimetallic or multi-component catalysts that combine the advantages of different active species.
5. Investigation of the long-term stability and regeneration of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts under industrial conditions.
