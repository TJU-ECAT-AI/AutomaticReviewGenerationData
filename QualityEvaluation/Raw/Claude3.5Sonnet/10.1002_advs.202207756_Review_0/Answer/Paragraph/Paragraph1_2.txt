<English>
The growing demand for propylene has significantly influenced the development of new production methods, particularly propane dehydrogenation (PDH). Global propylene consumption reached 90 million tons in 2015 and is expected to increase to 191 megatons by 2030, creating a substantial gap between supply and demand [10.1002/cctc.201500916]. This gap, coupled with the increased availability of propane from shale gas production, has led to a surge in interest in PDH technology, which now accounts for 10% of global propylene production [10.1002/advs.202207756_0].

The understanding of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH has evolved considerably over time, but there remains an ongoing debate about the nature of active Ga species. Various Ga species have been proposed as the active center for PDH, including Ga+, [GaO]+, [Ga(OH)2]+, [GaOH]2+, [GaH]2+, [GaH2]+, and Ga+H+ pair [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]. This diversity of proposed active species reflects the complexity of the catalyst system and the challenges in definitively identifying the active sites.

Recent research has contributed significantly to our current knowledge of these catalysts. Lercher's group found that in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, there is a very low enthalpy barrier of 56 kJ·mol^-1 for heterolytically activating the propane C-H bond. This activation occurs on [GaH]2+ species in conjunction with a basic framework oxygen of the Brønsted-acid site [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]. However, other researchers have proposed alternative active species. For instance, Maeno et al. investigated In-CHA catalysts and suggested that [InH2]+ ions are the catalytic active sites for PDH reactions, showing better performance than [GaH]2+ sites [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012].

Studies combining in situ transmission Fourier-transform infrared (FTIR) spectroscopy, pulse titration studies, and PDH kinetics have demonstrated that both Ga speciation and PDH performance are dependent on the Ga/Al ratios and Al distributions within the zeolites [10.1002/advs.202207756_0]. This finding highlights the importance of considering not just the presence of Ga species, but also their interaction with the zeolite structure and composition.

The role of Lewis and Brønsted acid sites has been further investigated. Lewis(Ga+)-Brønsted acid pairs have been identified as the active sites for propane dehydrogenation over Ga/H-ZSM-5. The increased Lewis acidity facilitates heterolytic C-H bond activation, while Brønsted acidity reduces the activation barrier for the elimination of hydrogen [10.1039/d0cs00983k].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolving landscape of propylene production, particularly through PDH technologies, reflects the dynamic nature of the petrochemical industry. The ongoing debate about the nature of active Ga species in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts highlights the complexity of these systems and the need for advanced characterization techniques.

Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Developing in-situ characterization methods to better understand the dynamic nature of active sites under reaction conditions.
2. Exploring the synergistic effects between different Ga species and the zeolite framework to optimize catalyst performance.
3. Investigating the potential of bimetallic or multi-component catalysts that combine the benefits of Ga with other elements to enhance activity and stability.
4. Utilizing advanced computational modeling techniques to predict and design more efficient catalyst structures.
5. Exploring the potential of novel zeolite frameworks or hierarchical structures to improve mass transfer and catalyst stability in PDH reactions.

These research directions could lead to the development of more efficient and stable catalysts for PDH, helping to bridge the growing gap between propylene supply and demand while addressing the environmental and economic challenges faced by the industry.

<Chinese>
日益增长的丙烯需求显著影响了新生产方法的发展，特别是丙烷脱氢(PDH)。全球丙烯消费量在2015年达到9000万吨，预计到2030年将增至1.91亿吨，造成供需之间的巨大缺口[10.1002/cctc.201500916]。这一缺口，加上页岩气生产带来的丙烷供应增加，导致PDH技术受到广泛关注，目前占全球丙烯生产的10%[10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。

对Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂在PDH中的理解随时间不断发展，但关于活性Ga物种的本质仍存在争议。各种Ga物种被提出作为PDH的活性中心，包括Ga+、[GaO]+、[Ga(OH)2]+、[GaOH]2+、[GaH]2+、[GaH2]+和Ga+H+对[10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。这些提议的活性物种的多样性反映了催化剂系统的复杂性和明确识别活性位点的挑战。

近期研究对我们当前对这些催化剂的认知做出了重大贡献。Lercher小组发现，在Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂中，异裂活化丙烷C-H键的焓垒很低，仅为56 kJ·mol^-1。这种活化发生在[GaH]2+物种上，并与Brønsted酸位点的碱性骨架氧配合[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。然而，其他研究人员提出了替代的活性物种。例如，Maeno等人研究了In-CHA催化剂，并建议[InH2]+离子是PDH反应的催化活性位点，表现优于[GaH]2+位点[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

结合原位透射傅里叶变换红外(FTIR)光谱、脉冲滴定研究和PDH动力学的研究表明，Ga物种形态和PDH性能都依赖于沸石中的Ga/Al比例和Al分布[10.1002/advs.202207756_0]。这一发现强调了不仅要考虑Ga物种的存在，还要考虑它们与沸石结构和成分的相互作用的重要性。

Lewis和Brønsted酸位点的作用也得到了进一步研究。Lewis(Ga+)-Brønsted酸对被确定为Ga/H-ZSM-5上丙烷脱氢的活性位点。增加的Lewis酸性有利于异裂C-H键活化，而Brønsted酸性降低了氢消除的活化能障[10.1039/d0cs00983k]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：丙烯生产格局的演变，尤其是通过PDH技术，反映了石油化工行业的动态性质。关于Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂中活性Ga物种本质的持续争论凸显了这些系统的复杂性，以及对先进表征技术的需求。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 开发原位表征方法，以更好地理解反应条件下活性位点的动态性质。
2. 探索不同Ga物种与沸石骨架之间的协同效应，以优化催化剂性能。
3. 研究将Ga与其他元素结合的双金属或多组分催化剂的潜力，以提高活性和稳定性。
4. 利用先进的计算模拟技术预测和设计更高效的催化剂结构。
5. 探索新型沸石骨架或分层结构的潜力，以改善PDH反应中的质量传递和催化剂稳定性。

这些研究方向可能导致更高效、更稳定的PDH催化剂的开发，有助于弥合不断扩大的丙烯供需缺口，同时解决行业面临的环境和经济挑战。
