The growing demand for propylene has led to the development of new production methods, particularly propane dehydrogenation (PDH). This trend is driven by the "propylene gap" - the increasing demand for propylene that traditional production methods struggle to meet. PDH has emerged as a promising alternative to produce propylene directly from propane.

The understanding of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts for PDH has evolved over time. There has been an ongoing debate about the nature of active Ga species in these catalysts. Recent research has focused on the support effect in propane dehydrogenation over supported gallium oxide catalysts. Studies have shown that the choice of support can significantly influence the catalytic performance of Ga-based catalysts.

While the literature provided does not give extensive details on the current knowledge of Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts, it suggests that research is ongoing to better understand the role of support materials and the nature of active Ga species in propane dehydrogenation reactions.
