The literature mentions that gallium dispersed in acid materials like ZSM-5 can be used to dehydrogenate alkanes, converting light paraffins into aromatic hydrocarbons.

There are multiple proposals for the role of gallium in the reaction mechanism, including:
1. Acting as a dehydrogenation function in a pure bifunctional mechanism
2. Participating in a concerted mechanism
3. Serving as a promoter for recombinative hydrogen removal during alkane dehydrogenation on Brønsted acid sites
