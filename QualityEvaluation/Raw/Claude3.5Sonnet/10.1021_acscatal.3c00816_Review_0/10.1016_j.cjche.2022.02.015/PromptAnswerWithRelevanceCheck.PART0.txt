<EnglishVersionAnswer>

Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) technology offers several key advantages that make it the most promising method for propylene production:

1. Feedstock availability: PDH directly utilizes propane, which is increasingly available from shale gas resources. This addresses the challenge of feedstock supply faced by conventional methods.

2. Process efficiency: As a direct conversion process, PDH is more efficient compared to traditional methods that involve multiple steps or complex separations.

3. Product selectivity: PDH can achieve high propylene selectivity, allowing for more targeted production of the desired product.

4. Environmental considerations: PDH represents a cleaner utilization of fossil energy resources, contributing to carbon neutrality goals.

5. Economic benefits: PDH reduces reliance on expensive or environmentally problematic catalysts like platinum and chromium used in some conventional methods.

These advantages allow PDH to effectively address challenges in feedstock availability, process efficiency, and product selectivity faced by conventional propylene production methods, making it a promising technology for meeting growing global propylene demand.
