<English>
Propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology has emerged as the most promising method for propylene production due to several key advantages that address the challenges faced by conventional production methods:

1. Feedstock availability: PDH utilizes propane, which is abundantly available from shale gas and natural gas resources. This addresses the challenge of feedstock scarcity faced by conventional methods that rely on dwindling petroleum reserves [10.1039/D1GC03700E]. The recent boom in shale gas production has made propane feedstock for PDH extremely cheap and plentiful [10.1039/D2QI00653G].

2. Process efficiency: PDH is a direct, one-step process for converting propane to propylene, offering higher efficiency compared to traditional multi-step methods [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]. As an on-purpose technique, PDH directly produces propylene instead of a mixture of products, improving overall process efficiency [10.1021/cr5002436].

3. Product selectivity: PDH technology can achieve high propylene selectivity, typically over 90% [10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]. This high selectivity reduces the need for extensive separation and purification steps, thereby improving the overall economics of the process [10.1021/cr5002436].

4. Economic viability: The profitability of PDH is largely determined by the price difference between propane and propylene. Current market conditions, with low-cost propane from shale gas, make PDH highly favorable economically [10.1021/cr5002436].

5. Meeting growing demand: PDH helps address the ever-increasing global demand for propylene that conventional methods struggle to satisfy [10.1016/j.cej.2019.122423]. The Dow Chemical Company forecasts that propylene demand will grow at an average annual rate of 2% to 3% by 2035, which is expected to exceed production capacity [10.1039/D0CS00814A].

6. Environmental considerations: PDH generally has a lower carbon footprint compared to naphtha cracking, as it requires less energy input and produces fewer CO2 emissions [10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345].

7. Flexibility and scalability: PDH plants can be operated independently of refinery or petrochemical complexes, providing greater flexibility in terms of location and scale of production [10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345].

However, PDH technology also faces some challenges:

1. High energy requirements: The process is highly endothermic, requiring high temperatures (generally above 500°C) to achieve satisfactory propylene yields [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012].

2. Catalyst deactivation: Issues like coking, sintering, and other side reactions can affect catalyst longevity and performance [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012].

3. Cost considerations: Some effective catalysts, like Pt-based ones, are expensive, driving research into more cost-effective alternatives [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012].

Despite these challenges, ongoing research in catalyst development and process optimization continues to improve PDH technology, further cementing its position as the most promising method for propylene production.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The emergence of PDH technology represents a significant shift in the propylene production landscape. As the global demand for propylene continues to grow, PDH is well-positioned to become the dominant production method due to its advantages in feedstock availability, process efficiency, and product selectivity.

Looking forward, we can expect several key developments in PDH technology:

1. Advanced catalyst design: Research will likely focus on developing more stable, selective, and cost-effective catalysts. This may involve novel materials, such as single-atom catalysts or advanced support structures, to enhance performance and longevity.

2. Process intensification: Future PDH plants may incorporate innovative reactor designs and heat integration strategies to improve energy efficiency and reduce operating costs.

3. Integration with renewable energy: As the world moves towards carbon neutrality, PDH processes may be coupled with renewable energy sources to reduce their carbon footprint further.

4. Bio-based feedstocks: While current PDH technology relies on fossil-derived propane, future developments may explore the use of bio-based propane sources, aligning with sustainability goals.

5. Hybrid processes: We may see the development of hybrid technologies that combine PDH with other processes, such as oxidative dehydrogenation or membrane separation, to overcome thermodynamic limitations and improve overall efficiency.

These advancements will likely solidify PDH's position as the preferred method for propylene production, addressing the growing global demand while meeting increasingly stringent environmental and economic requirements.

<Chinese>
由于丙烷直接脱氢（PDH）技术具有多项关键优势，能够解决传统生产方法所面临的挑战，因此已成为丙烯生产最有前景的方法：

1. 原料可用性：PDH利用页岩气和天然气资源中丰富的丙烷。这解决了依赖日益枯竭的石油储备的传统方法所面临的原料稀缺问题[10.1039/D1GC03700E]。最近页岩气生产的繁荣使PDH的丙烷原料变得极其便宜和充足[10.1039/D2QI00653G]。

2. 工艺效率：PDH是将丙烷直接转化为丙烯的一步法过程，与传统的多步骤方法相比，效率更高[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。作为一种特定用途的技术，PDH直接生产丙烯而非产品混合物，提高了整体工艺效率[10.1021/cr5002436]。

3. 产品选择性：PDH技术可以实现高达90%以上的丙烯选择性[10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。这种高选择性减少了广泛的分离和纯化步骤的需求，从而提高了整个过程的经济性[10.1021/cr5002436]。

4. 经济可行性：PDH的盈利能力在很大程度上取决于丙烷和丙烯之间的价格差异。目前，由于页岩气带来的低成本丙烷，市场条件使PDH在经济上极具优势[10.1021/cr5002436]。

5. 满足日益增长的需求：PDH有助于满足传统方法难以满足的不断增长的全球丙烯需求[10.1016/j.cej.2019.122423]。陶氏化学公司预测，到2035年，丙烯需求将以平均每年2%至3%的速度增长，预计将超过生产能力[10.1039/D0CS00814A]。

6. 环境考虑：与石脑油裂解相比，PDH通常具有较低的碳足迹，因为它需要较少的能量输入并产生较少的二氧化碳排放[10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。

7. 灵活性和可扩展性：PDH装置可以独立于炼油厂或石化综合体运营，在位置和生产规模方面提供更大的灵活性[10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。

然而，PDH技术也面临一些挑战：

1. 高能耗要求：该过程是高度吸热的，需要高温（通常高于500°C）才能获得令人满意的丙烯产率[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

2. 催化剂失活：焦化、烧结和其他副反应等问题可能影响催化剂的寿命和性能[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

3. 成本考虑：一些有效的催化剂，如基于铂的催化剂，价格昂贵，推动了对更具成本效益的替代品的研究[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

尽管存在这些挑战，但催化剂开发和工艺优化方面的持续研究不断改进PDH技术，进一步巩固了其作为丙烯生产最有前景方法的地位。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：PDH技术的出现代表了丙烯生产格局的重大转变。随着全球丙烯需求持续增长，PDH由于其在原料可用性、工艺效率和产品选择性方面的优势，有望成为主导的生产方法。

展望未来，我们可以预期PDH技术将出现几个关键发展：

1. 先进催化剂设计：研究可能会集中在开发更稳定、更高选择性和更具成本效益的催化剂上。这可能涉及新型材料，如单原子催化剂或先进的支撑结构，以提高性能和寿命。

2. 工艺强化：未来的PDH工厂可能会采用创新的反应器设计和热集成策略，以提高能源效率并降低运营成本。

3. 与可再生能源的整合：随着世界朝着碳中和迈进，PDH过程可能会与可再生能源源相结合，以进一步减少其碳足迹。

4. 生物基原料：虽然当前的PDH技术依赖于化石衍生的丙烷，但未来的发展可能会探索使用生物基丙烷源，以符合可持续发展目标。

5. 混合工艺：我们可能会看到将PDH与其他工艺（如氧化脱氢或膜分离）相结合的混合技术的发展，以克服热力学限制并提高整体效率。

这些进展可能会巩固PDH作为首选丙烯生产方法的地位，满足不断增长的全球需求，同时满足日益严格的环境和经济要求。
