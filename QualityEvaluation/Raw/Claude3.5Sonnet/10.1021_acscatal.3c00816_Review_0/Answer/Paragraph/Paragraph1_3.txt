<English>
Propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology has emerged as the most promising method for propylene production due to several key advantages that address the challenges faced by conventional production methods:

1. Feedstock availability: PDH utilizes propane, which is abundantly available from shale gas and natural gas resources. This addresses the challenge of feedstock scarcity faced by conventional methods relying on dwindling petroleum reserves [10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081]. The recent boom in shale gas production has made propane feedstock extremely cheap and plentiful [10.1039/D2QI00653G].

2. Process efficiency: PDH is a direct, one-step process to convert propane to propylene, offering improved efficiency compared to multi-step conventional methods [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]. It allows for higher propylene yields and fewer byproducts, improving overall process efficiency [10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345].

3. Product selectivity: PDH technology offers high selectivity towards propylene, typically achieving over 90% selectivity [10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]. This high selectivity reduces the need for extensive separation and purification steps, thereby improving the overall economics of the process [10.1039/D2QI00653G].

4. Economic viability: The profitability of PDH is largely determined by the price difference between propane and propylene. Current market conditions, with low-cost propane from shale gas, make PDH highly favorable economically [10.1021/cr5002436].

5. Meeting growing demand: PDH technology provides a scalable solution to meet the ever-increasing global demand for propylene, which conventional methods struggle to satisfy [10.1016/j.cej.2019.122423].

6. Environmental considerations: PDH generally has a lower carbon footprint compared to naphtha cracking, as it requires less energy input and produces fewer CO2 emissions [10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345].

However, PDH technology also faces some challenges:

1. High energy requirements: The process is highly endothermic, requiring high temperatures (generally above 500°C) [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012].

2. Catalyst deactivation: Issues like coking, sintering, and other side reactions can affect catalyst longevity and performance [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012].

3. Cost considerations: Some effective catalysts, like Pt-based ones, are expensive, driving research into more cost-effective alternatives [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012].

Despite these challenges, ongoing research in catalyst development and process optimization continues to enhance the efficiency and viability of PDH technology, making it an increasingly attractive option for meeting the growing global demand for propylene.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of PDH technology represents a significant shift in propylene production, driven by changing feedstock availability and increasing demand. As the petrochemical industry continues to evolve, PDH is likely to play an increasingly important role in propylene production.

Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Advanced catalyst design: Developing novel catalysts that combine high activity, selectivity, and stability while being cost-effective and environmentally friendly. This could involve exploring new materials or optimizing existing ones.

2. Process intensification: Investigating innovative reactor designs or process configurations that can overcome the thermodynamic limitations of PDH, potentially allowing for lower operating temperatures or improved conversion rates.

3. Integration with renewable energy: Exploring ways to couple PDH processes with renewable energy sources to address the high energy requirements and further reduce the carbon footprint.

4. Circular economy approaches: Investigating the potential for integrating PDH technology into circular economy models, possibly by utilizing propane derived from renewable sources or waste streams.

5. Hybrid technologies: Exploring synergies between PDH and other emerging technologies, such as membrane reactors or chemical looping, to enhance overall process efficiency and sustainability.

These research directions could further cement PDH's position as a key technology in the future of propylene production, addressing both economic and environmental concerns in the petrochemical industry.

<Chinese>
丙烷直接脱氢（PDH）技术已成为最有前景的丙烯生产方法，这得益于其几个关键优势，解决了传统生产方法面临的挑战：

1. 原料可用性：PDH利用丙烷作为原料，这种原料从页岩气和天然气资源中丰富可得。这解决了传统方法依赖日益减少的石油储备所面临的原料短缺挑战 [10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081]。最近页岩气生产的蓬勃发展使丙烷原料变得极其便宜和丰富 [10.1039/D2QI00653G]。

2. 工艺效率：PDH是一个直接的单步工艺，将丙烷转化为丙烯，与传统多步骤方法相比，效率更高 [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。它能够提供更高的丙烯产率和更少的副产品，提高整体工艺效率 [10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。

3. 产品选择性：PDH技术提供了高度的丙烯选择性，通常能达到90%以上 [10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。这种高选择性减少了对广泛分离和纯化步骤的需求，从而提高了整个过程的经济性 [10.1039/D2QI00653G]。

4. 经济可行性：PDH的盈利能力在很大程度上取决于丙烷和丙烯之间的价格差异。目前的市场条件下，由于页岩气带来的低成本丙烷，使PDH在经济上非常有利 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

5. 满足不断增长的需求：PDH技术提供了一个可扩展的解决方案，以满足不断增长的全球丙烯需求，而传统方法难以满足这一需求 [10.1016/j.cej.2019.122423]。

6. 环境考虑：与石脑油裂解相比，PDH通常具有较低的碳足迹，因为它需要较少的能量输入并产生较少的CO2排放 [10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。

然而，PDH技术也面临一些挑战：

1. 高能耗要求：该过程是高度吸热的，需要高温（通常高于500°C）[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

2. 催化剂失活：焦炭沉积、烧结和其他副反应等问题可能影响催化剂的寿命和性能 [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

3. 成本考虑：一些有效的催化剂，如基于Pt的催化剂，价格昂贵，推动了对更具成本效益的替代品的研究 [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

尽管存在这些挑战，但在催化剂开发和工艺优化方面的持续研究不断提高PDH技术的效率和可行性，使其成为满足不断增长的全球丙烯需求的越来越有吸引力的选择。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：PDH技术的发展代表了丙烯生产的重大转变，这一转变由不断变化的原料可用性和不断增长的需求驱动。随着石化行业的持续发展，PDH很可能在丙烯生产中扮演越来越重要的角色。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 先进催化剂设计：开发兼具高活性、选择性和稳定性，同时具有成本效益和环境友好性的新型催化剂。这可能涉及探索新材料或优化现有材料。

2. 工艺强化：研究创新的反应器设计或工艺配置，以克服PDH的热力学限制，可能允许更低的操作温度或提高转化率。

3. 与可再生能源的集成：探索将PDH工艺与可再生能源源结合的方法，以解决高能耗要求并进一步减少碳足迹。

4. 循环经济方法：研究将PDH技术整合到循环经济模型中的潜力，可能通过利用来自可再生源或废物流的丙烷。

5. 混合技术：探索PDH与其他新兴技术（如膜反应器或化学循环）之间的协同作用，以提高整体工艺效率和可持续性。

这些研究方向可能进一步巩固PDH作为未来丙烯生产的关键技术的地位，同时解决石化行业的经济和环境问题。
