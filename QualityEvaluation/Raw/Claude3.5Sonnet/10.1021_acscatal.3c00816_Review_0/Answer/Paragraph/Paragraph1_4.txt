<English>
Propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology has emerged as the most promising method for propylene production due to several key advantages that address the challenges faced by conventional production methods:

1. Feedstock availability: PDH utilizes propane, which has become increasingly abundant due to the shale gas revolution and natural gas production. This addresses the challenge of feedstock scarcity faced by conventional methods that rely on naphtha or heavy oil fractions [10.1016/j.cej.2019.122423]. The low cost and wide availability of propane make PDH economically attractive and provide a stable feedstock supply [10.1039/D2QI00653G].

2. Process efficiency: PDH is a direct, one-step conversion process from propane to propylene, offering improved efficiency compared to multi-step conventional methods [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022]. The process can achieve high propane conversion rates, with some catalysts demonstrating conversion up to 66.7% [10.1002/cctc.202101069].

3. Product selectivity: PDH technology offers high selectivity towards propylene production, typically achieving over 90% selectivity [10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]. This high selectivity reduces the need for extensive separation and purification steps, improving overall process economics [10.1039/D2QI00653G].

4. Meeting growing demand: PDH directly addresses the increasing global demand for propylene, which conventional methods like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking struggle to satisfy [10.1016/j.cej.2019.122423]. The Dow Chemical Company forecasted propylene demand to grow at an average annual rate of 2% to 3% by 2035, exceeding production capacity [10.1039/D0CS00814A].

5. Economic viability: The price difference between propane and propylene, combined with the growing propylene market, makes PDH extraordinarily profitable [10.1039/D2QI00653G]. The low cost of propane feedstock from shale gas further enhances the economic attractiveness of PDH [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022].

6. Environmental benefits: PDH generally has a lower carbon footprint compared to naphtha cracking, as it requires less energy input and produces fewer CO2 emissions [10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]. Some catalysts, like Pt-based ones, are more environmentally friendly compared to traditional CrOx catalysts [10.1039/C9CY01907C].

7. Flexibility and scalability: PDH plants can be operated independently of refinery or petrochemical complexes, providing greater flexibility in terms of location and scale of production [10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]. This allows for better adaptation to market demands and optimization of supply chains.

Despite these advantages, PDH technology faces some challenges, particularly in terms of catalyst stability and energy requirements. The process is highly endothermic, requiring high temperatures (typically above 550°C) to achieve satisfactory propylene yields [10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5]. This can lead to catalyst deactivation through coking and sintering, necessitating frequent regeneration [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022].

However, ongoing research is addressing these challenges through the development of more stable and efficient catalysts. For example, recent advancements in catalyst design, such as the use of single atom alloys (SAAs), have shown promise in simultaneously enhancing propylene selectivity and propane conversion while improving catalyst stability [10.1038/s41467-018-06967-8].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advantages of PDH technology position it as a transformative solution for the propylene industry, addressing key challenges in feedstock availability, process efficiency, and product selectivity. As global demand for propylene continues to grow, PDH is likely to play an increasingly important role in meeting this demand sustainably and economically.

Future research efforts may focus on further improving catalyst performance, particularly in terms of stability and resistance to coking. This could involve exploring novel support materials, optimizing catalyst compositions, or developing innovative regeneration techniques. Additionally, process intensification strategies, such as membrane reactors or advanced reactor designs, could be investigated to enhance overall efficiency and reduce energy consumption.

The integration of PDH technology with renewable energy sources could also be an area of interest, potentially leading to more sustainable propylene production. Furthermore, the development of bio-based propane sources could open up new avenues for green propylene production, aligning with global efforts towards carbon neutrality.

As PDH technology continues to evolve, it may drive significant changes in the petrochemical industry landscape, potentially reshaping supply chains and influencing investment decisions in propylene production capacity worldwide.

<Chinese>
丙烷直接脱氢（PDH）技术已成为最有前景的丙烯生产方法，因为它具有几个关键优势，能够解决传统生产方法面临的挑战：

1. 原料可用性：PDH利用丙烷作为原料，由于页岩气革命和天然气生产的增加，丙烷变得越来越丰富。这解决了传统方法依赖石脑油或重油馏分面临的原料稀缺问题[10.1016/j.cej.2019.122423]。丙烷的低成本和广泛可用性使PDH在经济上具有吸引力，并提供了稳定的原料供应[10.1039/D2QI00653G]。

2. 工艺效率：PDH是一个直接的、单步的丙烷到丙烯的转化过程，与传统的多步方法相比，效率更高[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022]。该工艺可以实现高丙烷转化率，一些催化剂显示出高达66.7%的转化率[10.1002/cctc.202101069]。

3. 产品选择性：PDH技术对丙烯生产具有高选择性，通常可以达到90%以上的选择性[10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。这种高选择性减少了对广泛分离和纯化步骤的需求，提高了整体工艺经济性[10.1039/D2QI00653G]。

4. 满足不断增长的需求：PDH直接解决了全球不断增长的丙烯需求，而传统方法如蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解难以满足这种需求[10.1016/j.cej.2019.122423]。陶氏化学公司预测到2035年丙烯需求将以平均每年2%至3%的速度增长，超过生产能力[10.1039/D0CS00814A]。

5. 经济可行性：丙烷和丙烯之间的价格差异，加上不断增长的丙烯市场，使PDH极其有利可图[10.1039/D2QI00653G]。来自页岩气的低成本丙烷原料进一步提高了PDH的经济吸引力[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022]。

6. 环境效益：与石脑油裂解相比，PDH通常具有较低的碳足迹，因为它需要较少的能量输入并产生较少的二氧化碳排放[10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。一些催化剂，如基于Pt的催化剂，比传统的CrOx催化剂更环保[10.1039/C9CY01907C]。

7. 灵活性和可扩展性：PDH装置可以独立于炼油厂或石化综合体运行，在位置和生产规模方面提供更大的灵活性[10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。这允许更好地适应市场需求和优化供应链。

尽管有这些优势，PDH技术仍面临一些挑战，特别是在催化剂稳定性和能量需求方面。该过程是高度吸热的，需要高温（通常高于550°C）才能获得令人满意的丙烯产率[10.1016/S1872-2067(17)62805-5]。这可能导致催化剂通过结焦和烧结而失活，需要频繁再生[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022]。

然而，正在进行的研究通过开发更稳定和高效的催化剂来解决这些挑战。例如，催化剂设计的最新进展，如使用单原子合金（SAAs），在同时提高丙烯选择性和丙烷转化率的同时，还显示出改善催化剂稳定性的前景[10.1038/s41467-018-06967-8]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：PDH技术的优势使其成为丙烯行业的变革性解决方案，解决了原料可用性、工艺效率和产品选择性方面的关键挑战。随着全球对丙烯的需求持续增长，PDH可能在可持续和经济地满足这一需求方面发挥越来越重要的作用。

未来的研究努力可能集中在进一步提高催化剂性能，特别是在稳定性和抗结焦性方面。这可能涉及探索新型支撑材料、优化催化剂组成或开发创新的再生技术。此外，还可以研究过程强化策略，如膜反应器或先进的反应器设计，以提高整体效率并减少能耗。

将PDH技术与可再生能源源结合也可能是一个感兴趣的领域，潜在地导致更可持续的丙烯生产。此外，生物基丙烷源的开发可能为绿色丙烯生产开辟新途径，与全球实现碳中和的努力相一致。

随着PDH技术继续发展，它可能推动石化行业格局的重大变化，潜在地重塑供应链并影响全球丙烯生产能力的投资决策。
