<English>
Propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology has emerged as the most promising method for propylene production due to several key advantages that address the challenges faced by conventional production methods:

1. Feedstock availability: PDH utilizes propane, which has become abundantly available due to the large-scale exploitation of shale gas and natural gas resources. This addresses the challenge of feedstock availability faced by conventional naphtha-based production methods [10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081]. The recent boom in shale gas production has made propane feedstock for PDH extremely cheap and plentiful [10.1039/D2QI00653G].

2. On-purpose production: PDH is specifically designed for propylene production, allowing for more targeted and efficient production compared to traditional methods where propylene is often a byproduct [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]. This on-purpose nature of PDH allows producers to adjust output based on market demand more easily than integrated processes [10.1016/j.jcat.2016.11.017].

3. Process efficiency: As a direct conversion process, PDH offers improved efficiency and greater flexibility in production capacity and location compared to more complex conventional routes [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]. PDH is more energy-efficient compared to traditional steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking processes, which are highly energy-consuming [10.1016/j.fuel.2021.121790].

4. Product selectivity: PDH technology can achieve high propylene selectivity, typically over 90% [10.1021/cr5002436]. This high selectivity reduces the need for extensive separation and purification steps, thereby improving the overall economics of the process [10.146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345].

5. Economic viability: With the increased availability of low-cost propane, especially from shale gas in North America, PDH has become economically attractive [10.146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]. The price difference between propylene and propane, combined with the growing propylene market, makes PDH extraordinarily profitable [10.1039/D2QI00653G].

6. Environmental considerations: PDH generally has a lower carbon footprint compared to naphtha cracking, as it requires less energy input and produces fewer CO2 emissions [10.146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]. Some catalysts used in PDH, like Pt-based ones, are more environmentally friendly compared to toxic Cr-based catalysts used in some conventional methods [10.1039/C8SC00802G].

Despite these advantages, PDH technology also faces some challenges:

1. High energy requirements: The process is highly endothermic, requiring high temperatures (generally above 500°C) [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012].

2. Catalyst deactivation: Issues like coking, sintering, and other side reactions can affect catalyst longevity and performance [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012].

3. Cost considerations: Some effective catalysts, like Pt-based ones, are expensive, driving research into more cost-effective alternatives [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012].

Ongoing research focuses on developing more efficient catalysts that can operate at lower temperatures, improve propylene selectivity, and resist deactivation. The development of single-site catalysts is one approach to tackle these issues by inhibiting structure-sensitive side reactions and improving stability [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The emergence of PDH technology as a promising method for propylene production represents a significant shift in the petrochemical industry. This shift is driven by the changing landscape of feedstock availability, particularly the abundance of propane from shale gas, and the growing demand for propylene that conventional methods struggle to meet.

Looking forward, the continued development of PDH technology is likely to focus on several key areas:

1. Advanced catalyst design: Future research will likely concentrate on developing catalysts that can operate at lower temperatures while maintaining high selectivity and stability. This could involve exploring novel materials, such as single-atom catalysts or advanced bimetallic formulations.

2. Process intensification: Innovative reactor designs and process configurations could help to overcome some of the current limitations of PDH, such as the high energy requirements and equilibrium limitations.

3. Integration with renewable energy: As the world moves towards more sustainable energy sources, there may be opportunities to couple PDH processes with renewable energy sources to reduce their carbon footprint further.

4. Circular economy approaches: Future developments might explore ways to integrate PDH technology into circular economy models, possibly by utilizing propane derived from renewable sources or by finding innovative uses for the hydrogen byproduct.

5. Hybrid technologies: Combining PDH with other technologies, such as membrane separation or oxidative dehydrogenation, could lead to more efficient and flexible propylene production processes.

These advancements could potentially address the remaining challenges of PDH technology and further cement its position as a leading method for propylene production in the coming decades.

<Chinese>
由于丙烷直接脱氢（PDH）技术具有几个关键优势，可以解决传统生产方法面临的挑战，因此它已成为最有前景的丙烯生产方法：

1. 原料可用性：PDH利用丙烷，由于页岩气和天然气资源的大规模开发，丙烷变得非常丰富。这解决了传统石脑油基生产方法面临的原料可用性挑战[10.1016/j.mcat.2021.112081]。最近页岩气生产的蓬勃发展使PDH的丙烷原料变得极其便宜且丰富[10.1039/D2QI00653G]。

2. 定向生产：PDH专门用于丙烯生产，与传统方法中丙烯常常作为副产品相比，允许更有针对性和更高效的生产[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。PDH的这种定向性质使生产商能够比综合流程更容易地根据市场需求调整产量[10.1016/j.jcat.2016.11.017]。

3. 工艺效率：作为直接转化过程，与更复杂的传统路线相比，PDH提供了改进的效率和更大的生产能力和位置灵活性[10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]。与高能耗的传统蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解工艺相比，PDH更加节能[10.1016/j.fuel.2021.121790]。

4. 产品选择性：PDH技术可以实现高丙烯选择性，通常超过90%[10.1021/cr5002436]。这种高选择性减少了广泛分离和纯化步骤的需求，从而提高了整个过程的经济性[10.146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。

5. 经济可行性：由于低成本丙烷的可用性增加，特别是北美的页岩气，PDH变得经济有吸引力[10.146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。丙烯和丙烷之间的价格差异，加上不断增长的丙烯市场，使PDH变得非常有利可图[10.1039/D2QI00653G]。

6. 环境考虑：与石脑油裂解相比，PDH通常具有较低的碳足迹，因为它需要较少的能量输入并产生较少的CO2排放[10.146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。PDH中使用的一些催化剂，如基于Pt的催化剂，比一些传统方法中使用的有毒的基于Cr的催化剂更环保[10.1039/C8SC00802G]。

尽管有这些优势，PDH技术仍面临一些挑战：

1. 高能量需求：该过程是高度吸热的，需要高温（通常高于500°C）[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

2. 催化剂失活：结焦、烧结和其他副反应等问题可能影响催化剂的寿命和性能[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

3. 成本考虑：一些有效的催化剂，如基于Pt的催化剂，价格昂贵，推动了对更具成本效益的替代品的研究[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

正在进行的研究集中于开发可在较低温度下操作、提高丙烯选择性并抵抗失活的更高效催化剂。开发单原子催化剂是通过抑制结构敏感的副反应和改善稳定性来解决这些问题的一种方法[10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：PDH技术作为丙烯生产的一种有前景方法的出现，代表了石油化工行业的重大转变。这种转变是由原料可用性格局的变化，特别是页岩气中丙烷的丰富，以及传统方法难以满足的日益增长的丙烯需求推动的。

展望未来，PDH技术的持续发展可能会集中在几个关键领域：

1. 先进催化剂设计：未来的研究可能会集中在开发能够在较低温度下运行同时保持高选择性和稳定性的催化剂。这可能涉及探索新型材料，如单原子催化剂或先进的双金属配方。

2. 过程强化：创新的反应器设计和过程配置可能有助于克服PDH当前的一些限制，如高能耗和平衡限制。

3. 与可再生能源的集成：随着世界向更可持续的能源源转移，可能会出现将PDH过程与可再生能源源结合的机会，以进一步减少其碳足迹。

4. 循环经济方法：未来的发展可能探索将PDH技术集成到循环经济模型中的方法，可能通过利用来自可再生源的丙烷或找到氢气副产品的创新用途。

5. 混合技术：将PDH与其他技术结合，如膜分离或氧化脱氢，可能会导致更高效和灵活的丙烯生产过程。

这些进展可能有助于解决PDH技术剩余的挑战，并进一步巩固其在未来几十年作为丙烯生产的主要方法的地位。
