<English>
Propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology has emerged as the most promising method for propylene production due to several key advantages that address the challenges faced by conventional production methods:

1. Feedstock availability: PDH utilizes propane, which is abundantly available due to the large-scale exploitation of shale gas and natural gas resources. This addresses the challenge of feedstock limitations faced by conventional naphtha-based production methods [10.1039/D0CS01260B]. The increased availability of low-cost propane, especially from shale gas in North America, has made PDH economically attractive [10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345].

2. Process efficiency: PDH is a direct, one-step process to convert propane to propylene, offering improved efficiency compared to multi-step processes like methanol-to-olefins (MTO) or Fischer-Tropsch to olefins (FTO) [10.1039/D0CS01260B]. The direct conversion of propane to propylene results in fewer unwanted byproducts, leading to improved overall process efficiency [10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345].

3. Product selectivity: PDH technology offers high selectivity towards propylene, typically achieving over 90% selectivity [10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]. This high selectivity reduces the need for extensive separation and purification steps, thereby improving the overall economics of the process [10.1021/cr5002436].

4. Economic viability: The profitability of PDH is largely determined by the price difference between propane and propylene. Current market conditions, with low-cost propane from shale gas, make PDH highly favorable economically [10.1021/cr5002436].

5. On-purpose production: PDH allows for dedicated, on-purpose propylene production, which helps to address the growing demand-supply gap for propylene that cannot be met by conventional production methods alone [10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345].

6. Environmental considerations: PDH generally has a lower carbon footprint compared to naphtha cracking, as it requires less energy input and produces fewer CO2 emissions [10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345].

7. Operational flexibility: PDH plants can be operated independently of refinery or petrochemical complexes, providing greater flexibility in terms of location and scale of production [10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345].

However, PDH technology also faces challenges:

1. High energy requirements: The process is highly endothermic, requiring high temperatures (generally above 500°C), which can limit catalyst development and increase operational costs [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012].

2. Catalyst deactivation: Issues like coking, sintering, and other side reactions can affect catalyst longevity and performance [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012].

3. Thermodynamic limitations: The equilibrium conversion is limited at high temperatures, requiring innovative reactor designs or process modifications to overcome this constraint [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012].

Despite these challenges, ongoing research in catalyst development and process optimization continues to improve the efficiency and sustainability of PDH technology, making it an increasingly attractive option for meeting the growing global demand for propylene.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The emergence of PDH technology as a promising method for propylene production represents a significant shift in the petrochemical industry. As conventional methods struggle to meet growing demand and face feedstock limitations, PDH offers a more targeted, efficient, and potentially more sustainable approach. The abundance of propane from shale gas has created a favorable economic environment for PDH, particularly in regions with access to these resources.

Looking forward, several areas of development could further enhance PDH technology:

1. Advanced catalyst design: Developing catalysts that can operate at lower temperatures while maintaining high selectivity and stability could address the energy intensity and deactivation issues. Research into single-atom catalysts or novel supports like CeO2 shows promise in this direction [10.1002/ange.201701115].

2. Process intensification: Integrating PDH with other processes, such as oxidative dehydrogenation or membrane reactors, could help overcome thermodynamic limitations and improve overall efficiency.

3. Renewable propane sources: As the focus on sustainability increases, exploring ways to produce propane from renewable sources could make PDH an even more attractive option in a low-carbon future.

4. Digitalization and AI: Implementing advanced process control and optimization techniques using artificial intelligence could further improve the efficiency and reliability of PDH plants.

5. Circular economy integration: Developing technologies to recycle or upcycle the hydrogen produced as a byproduct of PDH could enhance the overall sustainability and economics of the process.

As these developments progress, PDH is likely to play an increasingly important role in meeting the global demand for propylene, potentially reshaping the petrochemical industry landscape in the coming decades.

<Chinese>
由于丙烷直接脱氢（PDH）技术具有几个关键优势，它已成为丙烯生产最有前景的方法，解决了传统生产方法面临的挑战：

1. 原料可用性：PDH利用页岩气和天然气资源大规模开采所产生的丰富丙烷。这解决了传统石脑油基生产方法面临的原料限制问题[10.1039/D0CS01260B]。特别是在北美，由页岩气产生的低成本丙烷的增加，使PDH在经济上变得更具吸引力[10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。

2. 工艺效率：PDH是一个直接的单步过程，将丙烷转化为丙烯，相比甲醇制烯烃（MTO）或费托制烯烃（FTO）等多步骤工艺，效率更高[10.1039/D0CS01260B]。丙烷直接转化为丙烯产生的副产品更少，从而提高了整体工艺效率[10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。

3. 产品选择性：PDH技术对丙烯的选择性很高，通常可达90%以上[10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。这种高选择性减少了对广泛分离和纯化步骤的需求，从而提高了整个过程的经济性[10.1021/cr5002436]。

4. 经济可行性：PDH的盈利能力在很大程度上取决于丙烷和丙烯之间的价格差异。目前，由于页岩气提供了低成本丙烷，市场条件使PDH在经济上非常有利[10.1021/cr5002436]。

5. 定向生产：PDH允许专门的、定向的丙烯生产，这有助于解决传统生产方法无法满足的丙烯供需缺口[10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。

6. 环境考虑：与石脑油裂解相比，PDH通常具有较低的碳足迹，因为它需要较少的能量投入并产生较少的CO2排放[10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。

7. 运营灵活性：PDH工厂可以独立于炼油厂或石化综合体运营，在生产位置和规模方面提供更大的灵活性[10.1146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。

然而，PDH技术也面临一些挑战：

1. 高能耗需求：该过程是高度吸热的，需要高温（通常在500°C以上），这可能限制催化剂的开发并增加运营成本[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

2. 催化剂失活：焦炭沉积、烧结和其他副反应等问题可能影响催化剂的寿命和性能[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

3. 热力学限制：在高温下，平衡转化率受到限制，需要创新的反应器设计或工艺改进来克服这一限制[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]。

尽管存在这些挑战，但在催化剂开发和工艺优化方面的持续研究不断提高PDH技术的效率和可持续性，使其成为满足全球日益增长的丙烯需求的越来越具吸引力的选择。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：PDH技术作为丙烯生产的一种有前景的方法的出现，代表了石化行业的重大转变。随着传统方法难以满足日益增长的需求并面临原料限制，PDH提供了一种更有针对性、更高效且可能更可持续的方法。页岩气提供的丰富丙烷为PDH创造了有利的经济环境，特别是在能够获得这些资源的地区。

展望未来，以下几个发展领域可能进一步增强PDH技术：

1. 先进催化剂设计：开发能在较低温度下运行同时保持高选择性和稳定性的催化剂，可以解决能源强度和失活问题。单原子催化剂或新型载体如CeO2的研究在这方面显示出前景[10.1002/ange.201701115]。

2. 工艺强化：将PDH与其他工艺（如氧化脱氢或膜反应器）集成，可以帮助克服热力学限制并提高整体效率。

3. 可再生丙烷来源：随着对可持续性关注的增加，探索从可再生资源生产丙烷的方法可以使PDH在低碳未来中成为更具吸引力的选择。

4. 数字化和人工智能：使用人工智能实施先进的过程控制和优化技术，可以进一步提高PDH工厂的效率和可靠性。

5. 循环经济整合：开发技术来回收或升级PDH副产品氢气，可以提高整个过程的可持续性和经济性。

随着这些发展的进展，PDH可能在满足全球丙烯需求方面发挥越来越重要的作用，潜在地重塑未来几十年石化行业的格局。
