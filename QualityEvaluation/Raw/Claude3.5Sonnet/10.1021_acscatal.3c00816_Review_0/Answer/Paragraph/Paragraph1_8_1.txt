<English>
Propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology has emerged as the most promising method for propylene production due to several key advantages that address the challenges faced by conventional production methods:

1. Feedstock availability: PDH capitalizes on the abundant and cheap propane from shale gas and natural gas liquids, addressing the issue of dwindling petroleum reserves and rising oil prices faced by conventional naphtha-based methods [10.1021/cr5002436]. This shift is particularly significant in the United States, where the shale gas boom has led to a surge in propane availability [10.1021/cr5002436].

2. Process efficiency: Unlike conventional methods that are energy-intensive and have low selectivity, PDH is an on-purpose technique that directly converts propane to propylene, resulting in higher efficiency and lower energy demands [10.1021/cr5002436]. PDH offers a more direct and efficient route to produce propylene compared to traditional methods like steam cracking or fluid catalytic cracking [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022].

3. Product selectivity: PDH technology produces polymer-grade propylene with high purity, without the mixture of other products typically obtained from traditional cracking processes [10.1021/cr5002436]. This high selectivity is particularly advantageous in the current market conditions where propylene demand is outpacing that of other olefins [10.1021/cr5002436].

4. Economic viability: The profitability of PDH is largely determined by the price difference between propane and propylene. Current market conditions, with low-cost propane from shale gas, make PDH highly favorable economically [10.1021/cr5002436]. The recent boom in shale gas production has turned the United States into the lowest cost chemical producer outside the Middle East [10.1021/cr5002436].

5. Flexibility and scalability: As an on-purpose technique, PDH allows producers to adjust propylene production independently of other petrochemical processes, providing greater flexibility in meeting market demands [10.1021/cr5002436]. The PDH industry is experiencing rapid growth, with numerous new installations announced worldwide, demonstrating its ability to address the increasing global demand for propylene [10.1021/cr5002436].

6. Environmental considerations: PDH generally has a lower carbon footprint compared to naphtha cracking, as it requires less energy input and produces fewer CO2 emissions [10.146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]. The more efficient conversion of propane to propylene in PDH processes can lead to lower CO2 emissions compared to traditional production methods [10.146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345].

These advantages make PDH technology well-positioned to address the challenges of feedstock availability, process efficiency, and product selectivity faced by conventional propylene production methods, while meeting the growing global demand for this crucial chemical building block.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The emergence of PDH technology as a promising solution for propylene production represents a significant shift in the petrochemical industry. This shift is driven by the convergence of several factors, including the shale gas revolution, increasing demand for propylene, and the need for more efficient and environmentally friendly production methods.

Looking forward, several areas of development could further enhance the potential of PDH technology:

1. Catalyst innovation: While current PDH processes rely primarily on Pt- and CrOx-based catalysts, there is significant potential for developing more efficient, stable, and environmentally friendly catalysts. Research into single-atom alloy catalysts, for instance, could lead to breakthroughs in catalyst performance and longevity [10.1038/s41467-018-06967-8].

2. Process intensification: Future research could focus on developing more compact and efficient PDH reactors, potentially incorporating membrane technology or novel heat management systems to overcome the endothermic nature of the reaction.

3. Integration with renewable energy: As the world moves towards decarbonization, there may be opportunities to integrate PDH processes with renewable energy sources, particularly for providing the high temperatures required for the reaction.

4. Circular economy approaches: Future developments might explore ways to utilize CO2 in the PDH process, potentially through chemical looping oxidative dehydrogenation (CL-ODH) [10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012], which could contribute to carbon neutrality goals.

5. Bio-based propane sources: While current PDH processes rely on fossil-derived propane, future research could explore the use of bio-based propane sources, further enhancing the sustainability of the process.

These potential developments could not only further improve the efficiency and sustainability of PDH technology but also cement its position as a key player in the future of propylene production and the broader transition towards a more sustainable chemical industry.

<Chinese>
由于丙烷直接脱氢（PDH）技术具有几个关键优势，使其成为最有前景的丙烯生产方法，解决了传统生产方法面临的挑战：

1. 原料可用性：PDH利用来自页岩气和天然气液体的丰富且廉价的丙烷，解决了传统石脑油基方法面临的石油储量减少和油价上涨的问题[10.1021/cr5002436]。这种转变在美国尤其显著，那里的页岩气繁荣导致丙烷供应激增[10.1021/cr5002436]。

2. 工艺效率：与能源密集且选择性低的传统方法不同，PDH是一种直接将丙烷转化为丙烯的定向技术，从而提高效率并降低能源需求[10.1021/cr5002436]。与传统的蒸汽裂解或流化催化裂解方法相比，PDH提供了一种更直接、更高效的丙烯生产路线[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.03.022]。

3. 产品选择性：PDH技术生产高纯度的聚合级丙烯，不含传统裂解工艺通常产生的其他产品混合物[10.1021/cr5002436]。这种高选择性在当前丙烯需求超过其他烯烃的市场条件下特别有优势[10.1021/cr5002436]。

4. 经济可行性：PDH的盈利能力在很大程度上取决于丙烷和丙烯之间的价格差异。目前的市场条件，加上页岩气带来的低成本丙烷，使PDH在经济上非常有利[10.1021/cr5002436]。最近的页岩气生产繁荣使美国成为中东以外生产成本最低的化学品生产国[10.1021/cr5002436]。

5. 灵活性和可扩展性：作为一种定向技术，PDH允许生产商独立于其他石化工艺调整丙烯生产，在满足市场需求方面提供更大的灵活性[10.1021/cr5002436]。PDH行业正在经历快速增长，全球宣布了众多新装置，展示了其满足不断增长的全球丙烯需求的能力[10.1021/cr5002436]。

6. 环境考虑：与石脑油裂解相比，PDH通常具有较低的碳足迹，因为它需要较少的能源输入并产生较少的CO2排放[10.146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。PDH工艺中更高效的丙烷到丙烯转化可以导致比传统生产方法更低的CO2排放[10.146/annurev-chembioeng-060817-084345]。

这些优势使PDH技术能够很好地解决传统丙烯生产方法在原料可用性、工艺效率和产品选择性方面面临的挑战，同时满足这一关键化学建筑块不断增长的全球需求。

综合理解与未来展望：

PDH技术作为丙烯生产的一种有前景的解决方案的出现，代表了石化行业的重大转变。这种转变是由几个因素的融合驱动的，包括页岩气革命、丙烯需求增加以及对更高效和环境友好生产方法的需求。

展望未来，以下几个发展领域可能进一步提升PDH技术的潜力：

1. 催化剂创新：虽然当前的PDH工艺主要依赖于Pt和CrOx基催化剂，但在开发更高效、稳定和环境友好的催化剂方面存在巨大潜力。例如，对单原子合金催化剂的研究可能会在催化剂性能和寿命方面取得突破[10.1038/s41467-018-06967-8]。

2. 工艺强化：未来的研究可能会集中在开发更紧凑和高效的PDH反应器上，可能会结合膜技术或新型热管理系统来克服反应的吸热性质。

3. 与可再生能源的整合：随着世界向去碳化迈进，可能会有机会将PDH工艺与可再生能源源整合，特别是为反应提供所需的高温。

4. 循环经济方法：未来的发展可能会探索在PDH工艺中利用CO2的方法，可能通过化学循环氧化脱氢（CL-ODH）[10.1021/accountsmr.0c00012]，这可能有助于实现碳中和目标。

5. 生物基丙烷来源：虽然目前的PDH工艺依赖于化石衍生的丙烷，但未来的研究可能会探索使用生物基丙烷来源，进一步提高工艺的可持续性。

这些潜在的发展不仅可能进一步提高PDH技术的效率和可持续性，还可能巩固其作为未来丙烯生产和更广泛的向更可持续化学工业过渡中的关键角色。
