2. The ability to operate at high temperatures (>500°C) allows PDH to achieve high propane conversion rates, overcoming thermodynamic limitations.

3. Product selectivity: While conventional Pt-based catalysts face challenges with propylene selectivity, ongoing research and advancements in catalyst design (as described in the study) are leading to improved selectivity and addressing the issue of unwanted by-products.

4. Economic viability: With the increasing availability of propane from shale gas, PDH becomes more economically attractive compared to other propylene production methods, potentially offering cost advantages.

5. Flexibility and adaptability: PDH provides a dedicated route for propylene production, allowing manufacturers to adjust their output based on market demand without being tied to the production of other petrochemicals. This adaptability makes it more responsive to market needs.

6. Meeting growing demand: PDH technology provides a scalable solution to meet the ever-increasing global demand for propylene, which is crucial for various industries.

However, challenges such as catalyst stability and selectivity are being addressed through ongoing research, as evidenced by the development of new catalysts described in the study.
