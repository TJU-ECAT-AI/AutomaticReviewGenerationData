          [2] "PDH technologies, i.e., the Catofin process using a CrOx/Al2O3 catalyst, and the Oleflex process using a supported PtSn catalyst, have drawbacks, such as the high cost and rapid deactivation of Pt and the potential environmental impact caused by Cr."

      <English>
          Propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology has emerged as the most promising method for propylene production due to several key advantages:

          1. Feedstock availability: PDH capitalizes on the current low cost of propane derived from shale gas, addressing the challenge of feedstock availability.

          2. Process efficiency: PDH is a direct conversion process, potentially offering higher efficiency compared to conventional methods that may involve multiple steps.

          3. Product selectivity: PDH can achieve high propylene selectivity, as demonstrated by the 85% selectivity achieved with In-CHA catalysts mentioned in one of the answers.

          4. Economic viability: The low cost of propane feedstock and increasing global demand for propylene make PDH economically attractive compared to other production methods.

          5. Flexibility: PDH allows for on-purpose propylene production, enabling producers to adjust output based on market demand.

          PDH technology addresses challenges faced by conventional methods in several ways:

          1. It utilizes abundant and low-cost propane from shale gas, solving feedstock availability issues.
          2. It directly converts propane to propylene in a single step, improving process efficiency.
          3. It can achieve high propylene selectivity compared to other processes.

          However, current PDH technologies still face some challenges, such as catalyst deactivation and environmental concerns with certain catalysts. The development of new catalysts, like the In-CHA catalyst described in some answers, aims to overcome these limitations by offering better stability, selectivity, and environmental compatibility.

          5. 灵活性：PDH允许定向生产丙烯，使生产商能够根据市场需求调整产量。

          1. 利用丰富且低成本的页岩气丙烷，解决了原料可用性问题。
