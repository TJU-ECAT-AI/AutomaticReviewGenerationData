Propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology offers several key advantages for propylene production:

1. Higher selectivity: PDH provides better selectivity compared to other methods, allowing for more efficient and targeted production of propylene.

2. Sustainability: PDH is considered a sustainable approach, which is crucial in addressing environmental concerns associated with conventional production methods.

3. Excellent catalytic performance: Pt-based catalysts used in PDH processes demonstrate superior catalytic performance, enhancing overall process efficiency.

4. Environment-friendly properties: The catalysts employed in PDH are more environmentally friendly compared to those used in conventional methods.

5. Direct conversion: PDH allows for the direct conversion of propane to propylene, potentially simplifying the production process and reducing the need for complex feedstock preparations.

However, PDH technology also faces challenges, particularly in terms of catalyst stability. The main issues include coke deposition and metal sintering, which can lead to catalyst deactivation. Addressing these challenges is crucial for improving the long-term efficiency and economic viability of PDH processes.
