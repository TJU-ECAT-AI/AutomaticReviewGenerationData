<EnglishVersionAnswer>

The key advantages of propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology that make it the most promising method for propylene production include:

1. Increased commercial attractiveness due to growing propylene demand and shale gas revolution
2. Significant environmental and energy benefits compared to conventional methods
3. Potential for better reactivity with newer catalysts like cobalt-based ones
4. Ability to address feedstock availability issues by utilizing propane from shale gas
5. Improved process efficiency and product selectivity over conventional PtSn/Al2O3 and Cr/Al2O3 catalysts

PDH specifically addresses challenges faced by conventional production methods by:

1. Utilizing more readily available propane feedstock from shale gas
2. Offering a more environmentally friendly process compared to toxic chromium catalysts
3. Potentially lowering costs compared to expensive platinum-based catalysts
4. Improving selectivity towards propylene with newer catalyst formulations
5. Providing a more efficient on-purpose route for propylene production to meet growing demand
