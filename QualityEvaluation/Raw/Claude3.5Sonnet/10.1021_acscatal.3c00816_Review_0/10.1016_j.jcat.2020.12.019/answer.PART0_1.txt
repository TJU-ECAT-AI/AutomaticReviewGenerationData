Propane direct dehydrogenation (PDH) technology offers several key advantages that make it the most promising method for propylene production:

1. Feedstock availability: PDH utilizes cheap and abundant propane, which is widely available from shale gas resources. This addresses the challenge of feedstock availability faced by conventional production methods.

2. Process efficiency: PDH is a direct and efficient process for converting propane to propylene. It does not require complex intermediate steps or additional feedstocks, making it more streamlined compared to other production methods.

3. Product selectivity: PDH can achieve high selectivity to propylene, which is crucial for maximizing yield and reducing unwanted byproducts. This high selectivity addresses the challenge of product purity faced by some conventional methods.

4. Valuable byproduct: The PDH process produces hydrogen as a valuable byproduct, which can be used in other industrial processes or as a clean energy source. This adds economic value to the overall process.

5. Addressing supply-demand gap: With the growing demand for propylene and its downstream products, PDH technology helps bridge the widening gap between supply and demand that conventional methods struggle to address.

6. Eco-friendly potential: The development of eco-friendly catalysts for PDH addresses environmental concerns associated with some conventional production methods, making it a more sustainable option for propylene production.

These advantages make PDH technology particularly well-suited to address the challenges of increasing propylene demand, feedstock availability, and process efficiency in the current industrial context.

3. 产品选择性：PDH可以实现高选择性的丙烯生产，这对于最大化产量和减少不需要的副产品至关重要。这种高选择性解决了一些传统方法面临的产品纯度挑战。

4. 有价值的副产品：PDH工艺产生氢气作为有价值的副产品，可用于其他工业过程或作为清洁能源来源。这为整个工艺增加了经济价值。
