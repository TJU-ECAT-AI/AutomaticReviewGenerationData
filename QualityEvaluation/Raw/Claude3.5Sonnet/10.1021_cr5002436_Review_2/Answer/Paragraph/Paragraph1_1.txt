<English>
Chromium oxide-based catalysts have evolved significantly since their discovery in 1933, becoming crucial in various industrial dehydrogenation processes. The evolution of these catalysts has been marked by continuous improvements in their performance, efficiency, and understanding of their fundamental properties.

Initially developed for n-butane dehydrogenation to produce butenes and butadiene, chromium oxide catalysts gained widespread industrial application during World War II for producing high-octane aviation fuel [10.1002/9783527626113.ch15]. The first UOP-designed plant using chromia-alumina catalysts began operation in England in 1940, followed by two more units in 1941 [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

Over the decades, the applications of these catalysts have expanded significantly. Currently, they are widely used in the production of lower alkenes such as ethene, propene, and isobutene through dehydrogenation of corresponding alkanes [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014]. The CATOFIN process, which employs chromium oxide catalysts, leads the world in producing propene and isobutene, with an installed capacity of 600,000 and 2,600,000 metric tons per annum, respectively [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4].

Research focus areas that have contributed to the significance of chromium oxide-based catalysts in dehydrogenation processes include:

1. Structure and active sites: Studies have identified various chromium species (Cr6+, Cr5+, Cr3+, Cr2+) present on catalyst surfaces [10.1021/cr5002436_2]. The nature of active sites has been a subject of ongoing debate, with recent research suggesting that Cr3+ species, either redox or exposed non-redox, serve as the active sites for dehydrogenation reactions [10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5].

2. Support materials: While alumina has been the primary support material, research has explored other supports such as silica, zirconia, and mesoporous materials like MCM-41 [10.1016/j.micromeso.2012.05.011]. These advanced support materials offer higher surface areas and better dispersion of chromium species, leading to improved catalytic performance.

3. Preparation methods: Various techniques have been developed to synthesize chromium oxide catalysts, including impregnation, atomic layer epitaxy (ALE), co-precipitation, and evaporation-induced self-assembly (EISA) [10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018]. These methods aim to achieve optimal dispersion and structure of chromium species on the support surface.

4. Reaction mechanisms: Research has focused on understanding the redox behavior of supported chromium oxides and their reactivity during dehydrogenation processes. Studies have revealed a redox cycle involving Cr6+ and Cr3+ species, which is crucial for maintaining catalyst activity [10.1007/s10562-008-9569-3].

5. Promoters: The addition of alkali metals, particularly potassium, has been found to enhance olefin selectivity and improve catalyst stability against deactivation [10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018].

6. Characterization techniques: Advanced methods such as in-situ Raman spectroscopy, XPS, UV-Vis DRS, and XAFS have been employed to study catalyst structure, oxidation states, and behavior under reaction conditions [10.1021/jp960543].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of chromium oxide-based catalysts since 1933 has been driven by both industrial needs and academic research, leading to significant improvements in their performance and understanding. Looking forward, several key areas present opportunities for further advancement:

1. Nano-engineered catalysts: Developing catalysts with precisely controlled nanostructures could lead to enhanced activity and selectivity. This may involve novel synthesis methods or the use of advanced support materials with tailored pore structures.

2. In-situ characterization: Further development of in-situ and operando characterization techniques could provide deeper insights into the dynamic behavior of catalysts under reaction conditions, potentially leading to the design of more stable and efficient catalysts.

3. Computational modeling: Integrating experimental studies with advanced computational modeling could accelerate catalyst design and optimization, potentially predicting new formulations with enhanced performance.

4. Sustainable processes: As environmental concerns grow, research may focus on integrating CO2 utilization into dehydrogenation processes or developing catalysts that operate efficiently at lower temperatures to reduce energy consumption.

5. Alternative feedstocks: With the changing landscape of petrochemical feedstocks due to shale gas and renewable resources, research may explore the adaptation of chromium oxide catalysts for new feedstock compositions.

These future directions could lead to more efficient, stable, and environmentally friendly chromium oxide-based catalysts, further enhancing their industrial significance in dehydrogenation processes.

<Chinese>
自1933年发现以来，氧化铬基催化剂在各种工业脱氢过程中发挥了重要作用，其性能、效率和基本性质的认识不断提高。

最初开发用于正丁烷脱氢生产丁烯和丁二烯的氧化铬催化剂，在第二次世界大战期间广泛应用于生产高辛烷值航空燃料[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15]。1940年，第一座使用氧化铬-氧化铝催化剂的UOP设计工厂在英国投入运营，随后1941年又有两个装置投产[10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

几十年来，这些催化剂的应用范围显著扩大。目前，它们被广泛用于通过相应烷烃的脱氢生产乙烯、丙烯和异丁烯等低级烯烃[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014]。采用氧化铬催化剂的CATOFIN工艺在丙烯和异丁烯的生产方面处于世界领先地位，安装产能分别为每年60万和260万公吨[10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4]。

对氧化铬基催化剂在脱氢过程中重要性有贡献的研究重点领域包括：

1. 结构和活性位点：研究已经确定了催化剂表面存在的各种铬物种（Cr6+、Cr5+、Cr3+、Cr2+）[10.1021/cr5002436_2]。活性位点的性质一直是持续争论的主题，最近的研究表明，Cr3+物种（无论是氧化还原型还是暴露的非氧化还原型）都作为脱氢反应的活性位点[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5]。

2. 载体材料：虽然氧化铝一直是主要的载体材料，但研究也探索了其他载体，如二氧化硅、二氧化锆和MCM-41等介孔材料[10.1016/j.micromeso.2012.05.011]。这些先进的载体材料提供了更高的表面积和更好的铬物种分散，从而提高了催化性能。

3. 制备方法：已经开发了各种技术来合成氧化铬催化剂，包括浸渍法、原子层外延法（ALE）、共沉淀法和蒸发诱导自组装（EISA）法[10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018]。这些方法旨在实现铬物种在载体表面的最佳分散和结构。

4. 反应机理：研究重点是了解负载氧化铬的氧化还原行为及其在脱氢过程中的反应性。研究揭示了涉及Cr6+和Cr3+物种的氧化还原循环，这对维持催化剂活性至关重要[10.1007/s10562-008-9569-3]。

5. 助剂：添加碱金属（特别是钾）被发现可以提高烯烃选择性并改善催化剂抗失活稳定性[10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018]。

6. 表征技术：采用了先进的方法，如原位拉曼光谱、XPS、UV-Vis DRS和XAFS，来研究催化剂的结构、氧化态和反应条件下的行为[10.1021/jp960543]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：自1933年以来，氧化铬基催化剂的演变一直受到工业需求和学术研究的双重驱动，导致其性能和理解有了显著改进。展望未来，几个关键领域为进一步发展提供了机会：

1. 纳米工程催化剂：开发具有精确控制纳米结构的催化剂可能会带来更高的活性和选择性。这可能涉及新型合成方法或使用具有定制孔结构的先进载体材料。

2. 原位表征：进一步发展原位和操作过程表征技术可以提供对催化剂在反应条件下动态行为的更深入了解，潜在地导致设计更稳定和高效的催化剂。

3. 计算机建模：将实验研究与先进的计算机建模相结合可能会加速催化剂设计和优化，潜在地预测具有增强性能的新配方。

4. 可持续过程：随着环境问题的增加，研究可能会集中于将CO2利用整合到脱氢过程中，或开发在较低温度下高效运行的催化剂，以减少能耗。

5. 替代原料：由于页岩气和可再生资源导致石化原料格局的变化，研究可能会探索氧化铬催化剂对新原料组成的适应性。

这些未来方向可能会导致更高效、更稳定和更环保的氧化铬基催化剂，进一步增强它们在脱氢过程中的工业重要性。
