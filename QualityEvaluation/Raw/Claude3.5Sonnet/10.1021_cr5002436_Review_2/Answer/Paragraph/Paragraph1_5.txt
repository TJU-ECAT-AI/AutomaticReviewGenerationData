<English>
Chromium oxide-based catalysts have evolved significantly since their discovery in 1933, becoming crucial in various industrial dehydrogenation processes. The evolution of these catalysts has been driven by ongoing research and industrial needs, leading to improvements in their performance, efficiency, and applications.

Key industrial applications of chromium oxide-based catalysts include:

1. Dehydrogenation of light alkanes: The catalysts are widely used for producing propene, isobutene, and other light olefins from their corresponding alkanes [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4]. The CATOFIN process, using these catalysts, has become a world leader in propene and isobutene production [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4].

2. Polymerization: Chromium-on-silica catalysts, known as the Phillips catalyst, are responsible for about one-third of global high-density polyethylene (HDPE) production [10.1039/a801710g].

3. Oxidative dehydrogenation: Recent research has focused on using these catalysts for oxidative dehydrogenation of hydrocarbons in the presence of CO2, including ethane, propane, isobutane, and ethylbenzene [10.1016/j.micromeso.2012.05.011].

Research focus areas that have contributed to the significance of chromium oxide-based catalysts in dehydrogenation processes include:

1. Structure and active sites: Studies have investigated the nature of active chromium species, with research identifying various oxidation states (Cr6+, Cr5+, Cr3+, and Cr2+) and their roles in catalytic activity [10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5]. Recent studies have proposed that Cr3+ species, either redox or exposed non-redox sites, serve as the active sites for dehydrogenation reactions [10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5].

2. Support materials: While alumina has been the traditional support, research has explored alternative materials such as zirconia, silica, and mesoporous molecular sieves like MCM-41 [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014]. These newer supports offer improved surface area and pore structure, allowing for better dispersion of chromium species and enhanced catalytic performance.

3. Preparation methods: Conventional impregnation techniques using Cr(NO3)3 or CrO3 solutions have been complemented by newer methods such as atomic layer epitaxy (ALE) and evaporation-induced self-assembly (EISA) [10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018]. These advanced preparation techniques aim to create well-defined structures of active sites on the support surface.

4. Reaction mechanisms: Research has focused on understanding the redox behavior of supported chromium oxides and their reactivity during dehydrogenation processes [10.1021/jp960543]. Studies have revealed a redox cycle involving Cr6+ and Cr3+ species, which plays a crucial role in maintaining catalyst activity [10.1007/s10562-008-9569-3].

5. Promoters: The addition of alkali metals, particularly potassium, has been found to enhance olefin selectivity and improve catalyst stability against deactivation [10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018].

6. Characterization techniques: Advanced methods such as in situ Raman spectroscopy, XPS, UV-Vis DRS, and XAFS have been employed to probe active sites, understand reaction mechanisms, and analyze the oxidation states of chromium species under reaction conditions [10.1021/cr5002436_2].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of chromium oxide-based catalysts since 1933 has been marked by significant advancements in understanding their structure, active sites, and reaction mechanisms. This progress has led to improved catalyst performance and expanded applications in the petrochemical industry. Looking forward, several promising research directions emerge:

1. Tailored catalyst design: With a deeper understanding of the relationship between catalyst structure and performance, future research could focus on designing catalysts with specific active site configurations to optimize selectivity and activity for targeted reactions.

2. Advanced support materials: The development of novel support materials, such as hierarchical porous structures or 2D materials, could further enhance catalyst performance by providing better dispersion and accessibility of active sites.

3. In situ characterization: Further development of in situ and operando characterization techniques could provide real-time insights into catalyst behavior under reaction conditions, leading to a more comprehensive understanding of reaction mechanisms and deactivation processes.

4. Sustainable catalysis: Given the growing emphasis on environmental sustainability, future research might explore the use of chromium oxide-based catalysts in green chemistry applications, such as CO2 utilization or biomass conversion.

5. Multifunctional catalysts: Combining chromium oxide with other active components could lead to the development of multifunctional catalysts capable of performing sequential or cascade reactions in a single process.

These future directions could potentially lead to more efficient, selective, and sustainable catalytic processes, further expanding the industrial significance of chromium oxide-based catalysts in dehydrogenation and related applications.

<Chinese>
自1933年发现以来，铬氧化物基催化剂已经发展演变，在各种工业脱氢过程中变得至关重要。这些催化剂的演变由持续的研究和工业需求驱动，导致了其性能、效率和应用的改进。

铬氧化物基催化剂的主要工业应用包括：

1. 轻烷烃脱氢：这些催化剂广泛用于从相应的烷烃生产丙烯、异丁烯和其他轻烯烃[10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4]。使用这些催化剂的CATOFIN工艺已成为丙烯和异丁烯生产的世界领导者[10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4]。

2. 聚合：铬-硅催化剂，被称为Phillips催化剂，负责全球约三分之一的高密度聚乙烯（HDPE）生产[10.1039/a801710g]。

3. 氧化脱氢：最近的研究集中在使用这些催化剂在CO2存在下进行烃类的氧化脱氢，包括乙烷、丙烷、异丁烷和乙苯[10.1016/j.micromeso.2012.05.011]。

对铬氧化物基催化剂在脱氢过程中的重要性做出贡献的研究重点领域包括：

1. 结构和活性位点：研究调查了活性铬物种的性质，研究确定了各种氧化态（Cr6+、Cr5+、Cr3+和Cr2+）及其在催化活性中的作用[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5]。最近的研究提出，Cr3+物种，无论是氧化还原还是暴露的非氧化还原位点，都作为脱氢反应的活性位点[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5]。

2. 载体材料：虽然氧化铝一直是传统载体，但研究已探索了替代材料，如氧化锆、二氧化硅和MCM-41等介孔分子筛[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014]。这些新型载体提供了改进的表面积和孔结构，允许更好地分散铬物种并增强催化性能。

3. 制备方法：常规的使用Cr(NO3)3或CrO3溶液的浸渍技术已被更新的方法如原子层外延（ALE）和蒸发诱导自组装（EISA）所补充[10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018]。这些先进的制备技术旨在在载体表面创建明确定义的活性位点结构。

4. 反应机理：研究集中在理解负载的铬氧化物的氧化还原行为及其在脱氢过程中的反应性[10.1021/jp960543]。研究揭示了涉及Cr6+和Cr3+物种的氧化还原循环，这在维持催化剂活性中起着关键作用[10.1007/s10562-008-9569-3]。

5. 促进剂：添加碱金属，特别是钾，被发现可以增强烯烃选择性并改善催化剂抗失活的稳定性[10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018]。

6. 表征技术：先进的方法如原位拉曼光谱、XPS、UV-Vis DRS和XAFS被用来探测活性位点、理解反应机理，并在反应条件下分析铬物种的氧化态[10.1021/cr5002436_2]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：自1933年以来，铬氧化物基催化剂的演变标志着在理解其结构、活性位点和反应机理方面取得了重大进展。这一进展导致了催化剂性能的改善和在石油化工行业应用的扩展。展望未来，几个有前景的研究方向浮现：

1. 定制催化剂设计：随着对催化剂结构与性能关系的深入理解，未来的研究可能集中在设计具有特定活性位点配置的催化剂上，以优化目标反应的选择性和活性。

2. 先进载体材料：开发新型载体材料，如层次孔结构或二维材料，可能进一步提高催化剂性能，提供更好的活性位点分散和可及性。

3. 原位表征：进一步开发原位和操作中表征技术可能提供反应条件下催化剂行为的实时洞察，从而对反应机理和失活过程有更全面的理解。

4. 可持续催化：鉴于对环境可持续性的日益重视，未来的研究可能探索铬氧化物基催化剂在绿色化学应用中的使用，如CO2利用或生物质转化。

5. 多功能催化剂：将铬氧化物与其他活性组分结合可能导致开发能够在单一过程中执行顺序或级联反应的多功能催化剂。

这些未来方向可能导致更高效、更选择性和更可持续的催化过程，进一步扩大铬氧化物基催化剂在脱氢和相关应用中的工业重要性。
