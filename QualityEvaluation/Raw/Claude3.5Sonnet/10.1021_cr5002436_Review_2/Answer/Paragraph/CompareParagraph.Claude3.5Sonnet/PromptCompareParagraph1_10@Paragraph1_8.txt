1. Dehydrogenation of light alkanes (propane, butane, isobutane) to produce olefins [10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5].

3. Oxidative dehydrogenation of hydrocarbons in the presence of CO2 [10.1016/j.micromeso.2012.05.011].

Research Focus Areas:

Structure and Active Sites: Studies have identified various chromium species (Cr6+, Cr5+, Cr3+, Cr2+) on catalyst surfaces, with ongoing debates about the nature of active sites [10.1007/s10562-008-9599-x]. Recent research suggests that redox or exposed non-redox Cr3+ sites in CrOx/Al2O3 serve as active sites for dehydrogenation reactions [10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5].

2. Support Materials: Initially focused on alumina and silica, research has expanded to include zirconia, mesoporous materials, and layered zirconium phosphate [10.1021/la981042s]. 3. Preparation Methods: Evolving from simple impregnation techniques, new methods like atomic layer epitaxy (ALE) and evaporation-induced self-assembly (EISA) have been developed to achieve better control over catalyst structure and properties [10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018].

4. Promoters: Alkali metals, particularly potassium, have been used to enhance olefin selectivity and improve catalyst stability against deactivation [10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018].

5. Reaction Mechanisms: Research has focused on understanding the redox behavior of chromium species during dehydrogenation reactions, including the role of CO2 in oxidative dehydrogenation processes [10.1007/s10562-008-9569-3].

6. Characterization Techniques: Advanced methods such as in situ Raman spectroscopy, XPS, XAFS, and UV-Vis spectroscopy have been employed to study catalyst structure and behavior under reaction conditions [10.1021/jp960543].
