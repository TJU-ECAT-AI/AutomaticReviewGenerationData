<English>
Chromium oxide-based catalysts have evolved significantly since their discovery by Frey and Huppke in 1933, becoming crucial in various industrial processes, particularly in dehydrogenation reactions. Their development has been driven by industrial needs and academic research, leading to substantial improvements in catalyst performance, efficiency, and understanding of their fundamental properties.

Key industrial applications of chromium oxide-based catalysts include:

1. Dehydrogenation of light alkanes: Propane to propene, isobutane to isobutene, and ethane to ethylene [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4]. The CATOFIN process, using these catalysts, leads the world in propene and isobutene production [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4].

2. Polymerization: The Phillips catalyst (Cr/SiO2) is responsible for about one-third of global high-density polyethylene (HDPE) production [10.1039/a801710g].

3. Oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) of hydrocarbons in the presence of CO2 [10.1016/j.micromeso.2012.05.011].

4. Selective catalytic reduction of NOx with ammonia [10.1016/j.micromeso.2012.05.011].

Research focus areas that have contributed to their significance in dehydrogenation processes include:

1. Structure and active sites: Studies have identified various chromium species (Cr6+, Cr5+, Cr3+, Cr2+) and their roles in catalytic activity [10.1002/9783527626113.ch15]. Recent research suggests that Cr3+ or a combination of Cr3+ and Cr2+ are the active sites for dehydrogenation reactions [10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5].

2. Support materials: Research has extensively studied various supports, including alumina, silica, and zirconia [10.1016/s0926-860x(97)00315-3]. Recent attention has focused on mesoporous materials with high surface areas [10.1016/j.molcata.2011.08.019].

3. Promoters: Alkali metals, particularly potassium, have been found to enhance olefin selectivity and improve catalyst stability [10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018].

4. Preparation methods: Techniques have evolved from simple impregnation to more advanced methods like atomic layer epitaxy (ALE) [10.1006/jcat.1996.0276].

5. Reaction mechanisms: Studies have explored both traditional dehydrogenation and oxidative dehydrogenation processes, investigating the role of CO2 as a mild oxidant [10.1016/j.cattod.2011.10.028].

6. Deactivation and regeneration: Research has focused on understanding and mitigating catalyst deactivation, primarily caused by coke formation [10.1039/c2cc38978a].

7. Characterization techniques: Advanced methods such as in-situ Raman spectroscopy, XPS, and XAFS have been employed to study catalyst behavior under reaction conditions [10.1021/jp960543].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of chromium oxide-based catalysts since 1933 has been marked by continuous improvements in performance, selectivity, and stability. Future research directions may focus on:

1. Developing novel support materials with enhanced properties, such as hierarchical pore structures or hybrid organic-inorganic supports, to improve catalyst stability and accessibility of active sites.

2. Exploring new preparation methods, such as atomic-scale design techniques, to create highly dispersed and uniform active sites.

3. Investigating the potential of bimetallic or multi-metallic systems incorporating chromium to enhance catalyst performance and broaden the range of applications.

4. Utilizing advanced in-situ and operando characterization techniques to gain deeper insights into the dynamic behavior of catalysts under reaction conditions.

5. Developing more environmentally friendly processes by integrating CO2 utilization in dehydrogenation reactions, contributing to carbon neutrality goals.

6. Exploring the potential of chromium oxide-based catalysts in emerging fields such as biomass conversion or electrochemical processes.

These research directions could lead to the next generation of chromium oxide-based catalysts with improved efficiency, selectivity, and sustainability, further expanding their industrial applications and significance in the field of catalysis.

<Chinese>
自1933年Frey和Huppke发现以来，铬氧化物催化剂已经发展得非常显著，在多种工业过程中变得至关重要，尤其是在脱氢反应中。它们的发展受到工业需求和学术研究的推动，导致催化剂性能、效率和对其基本性质理解的大幅提升。

铬氧化物催化剂的主要工业应用包括：

1. 轻烷烃脱氢：丙烷制丙烯、异丁烷制异丁烯和乙烷制乙烯[10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4]。使用这些催化剂的CATOFIN工艺在丙烯和异丁烯生产方面领先全球[10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4]。

2. 聚合：菲利普斯催化剂（Cr/SiO2）负责全球约三分之一的高密度聚乙烯（HDPE）生产[10.1039/a801710g]。

3. 在CO2存在下进行烃类的氧化脱氢（ODH）[10.1016/j.micromeso.2012.05.011]。

4. 用氨进行NOx的选择性催化还原[10.1016/j.micromeso.2012.05.011]。

对其在脱氢过程中重要性有贡献的研究重点领域包括：

1. 结构和活性位点：研究已识别出各种铬物种（Cr6+、Cr5+、Cr3+、Cr2+）及其在催化活性中的作用[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15]。最新研究表明，Cr3+或Cr3+和Cr2+的组合是脱氢反应的活性位点[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5]。

2. 载体材料：研究广泛探讨了各种载体，包括氧化铝、二氧化硅和氧化锆[10.1016/s0926-860x(97)00315-3]。最近的关注点集中在具有高比表面积的介孔材料上[10.1016/j.molcata.2011.08.019]。

3. 促进剂：碱金属，特别是钾，被发现可以提高烯烃选择性并改善催化剂稳定性[10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018]。

4. 制备方法：技术已从简单的浸渍法发展到更先进的方法，如原子层外延（ALE）[10.1006/jcat.1996.0276]。

5. 反应机理：研究探索了传统脱氢和氧化脱氢过程，调查了CO2作为温和氧化剂的作用[10.1016/j.cattod.2011.10.028]。

6. 失活和再生：研究重点是理解和减轻催化剂失活，主要由焦炭形成引起[10.1039/c2cc38978a]。

7. 表征技术：采用了先进的方法，如原位拉曼光谱、XPS和XAFS，以研究反应条件下的催化剂行为[10.1021/jp960543]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：自1933年以来，铬氧化物催化剂的演变以性能、选择性和稳定性的持续改进为特征。未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 开发具有增强性能的新型载体材料，如层次孔结构或有机-无机混合载体，以提高催化剂稳定性和活性位点的可及性。

2. 探索新的制备方法，如原子尺度设计技术，以创建高度分散和均匀的活性位点。

3. 调查包含铬的双金属或多金属系统的潜力，以增强催化剂性能并扩大应用范围。

4. 利用先进的原位和操作条件下表征技术，深入了解催化剂在反应条件下的动态行为。

5. 通过将CO2利用集成到脱氢反应中，开发更环保的过程，为碳中和目标做出贡献。

6. 探索铬氧化物催化剂在新兴领域（如生物质转化或电化学过程）中的潜力。

这些研究方向可能会导致下一代铬氧化物催化剂的出现，具有改进的效率、选择性和可持续性，进一步扩大它们在工业应用中的地位和在催化领域的重要性。
