<English>
The evolution of chromium oxide-based catalysts since their discovery in 1933 has been marked by significant developments in industrial applications and research focus areas, contributing to their importance in dehydrogenation processes.

Industrial Applications:
Initially used for butane dehydrogenation during World War II to produce high-octane aviation fuel, chromium oxide-based catalysts have since found widespread use in various industrial processes [10.1002/9783527626113.ch15]. Key applications include:

1. Dehydrogenation of light alkanes (propane, butane, isobutane) to produce olefins [10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5].
2. Ethylene polymerization using the Phillips catalyst (Cr/SiO2) for high-density polyethylene (HDPE) production [10.1039/a801710g].
3. Oxidative dehydrogenation of hydrocarbons in the presence of CO2 [10.1016/j.micromeso.2012.05.011].

The CATOFIN process, developed in the late 1980s, has become a world leader in propene and isobutene production, with an installed capacity of 600,000 and 2,600,000 metric tons per year, respectively [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4].

Research Focus Areas:
1. Structure and Active Sites: Studies have identified various chromium species (Cr6+, Cr5+, Cr3+, Cr2+) on catalyst surfaces, with ongoing debates about the nature of active sites [10.1007/s10562-008-9599-x]. Recent research suggests that redox or exposed non-redox Cr3+ sites in CrOx/Al2O3 serve as active sites for dehydrogenation reactions [10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5].

2. Support Materials: Initially focused on alumina and silica, research has expanded to include zirconia, mesoporous materials, and layered zirconium phosphate [10.1021/la981042s]. High surface area mesoporous supports have gained attention for better dispersion of chromium species [10.1016/j.molcata.2011.08.019].

3. Preparation Methods: Evolving from simple impregnation techniques, new methods like atomic layer epitaxy (ALE) and evaporation-induced self-assembly (EISA) have been developed to achieve better control over catalyst structure and properties [10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018].

4. Promoters: Alkali metals, particularly potassium, have been used to enhance olefin selectivity and improve catalyst stability against deactivation [10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018].

5. Reaction Mechanisms: Research has focused on understanding the redox behavior of chromium species during dehydrogenation reactions, including the role of CO2 in oxidative dehydrogenation processes [10.1007/s10562-008-9569-3].

6. Characterization Techniques: Advanced methods such as in situ Raman spectroscopy, XPS, XAFS, and UV-Vis spectroscopy have been employed to study catalyst structure and behavior under reaction conditions [10.1021/jp960543].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of chromium oxide-based catalysts since 1933 has been driven by the need for more efficient and selective dehydrogenation processes in the petrochemical industry. While significant progress has been made in understanding the structure-activity relationships and optimizing catalyst performance, there are still areas for further research and development.

Future research endeavors might focus on:
1. Developing more environmentally friendly catalysts with reduced chromium content or alternative active species.
2. Exploring novel support materials, such as metal-organic frameworks or 2D materials, to enhance catalyst stability and activity.
3. Investigating the potential of artificial intelligence and machine learning in catalyst design and optimization.
4. Integrating chromium oxide-based catalysts with renewable feedstocks for more sustainable chemical production processes.
5. Developing in operando characterization techniques to better understand catalyst behavior under industrial conditions.

These advancements could lead to more efficient and sustainable dehydrogenation processes, addressing the growing demand for olefins while minimizing environmental impact.

<Chinese>
自1933年发现以来，铬氧化物基催化剂的演变在工业应用和研究重点领域都取得了重大进展，为其在脱氢过程中的重要性做出了贡献。

工业应用：
最初在第二次世界大战期间用于丁烷脱氢生产高辛烷值航空燃料，铬氧化物基催化剂此后在各种工业过程中得到广泛应用[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15]。主要应用包括：

1. 轻烷烃（丙烷、丁烷、异丁烷）脱氢生产烯烃[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5]。
2. 使用菲利普斯催化剂（Cr/SiO2）进行乙烯聚合生产高密度聚乙烯（HDPE）[10.1039/a801710g]。
3. 在CO2存在下进行碳氢化合物的氧化脱氢[10.1016/j.micromeso.2012.05.011]。

20世纪80年代末开发的CATOFIN工艺已成为丙烯和异丁烯生产的世界领导者，安装产能分别达到60万和260万吨/年[10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4]。

研究重点领域：
1. 结构和活性位点：研究已在催化剂表面识别出各种铬物种（Cr6+、Cr5+、Cr3+、Cr2+），关于活性位点性质的讨论仍在继续[10.1007/s10562-008-9599-x]。最新研究表明，CrOx/Al2O3中的氧化还原或暴露的非氧化还原Cr3+位点作为脱氢反应的活性位点[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5]。

2. 载体材料：最初集中在氧化铝和二氧化硅上，研究已扩展到包括氧化锆、介孔材料和层状磷酸锆[10.1021/la981042s]。高比表面积的介孔载体因能更好地分散铬物种而受到关注[10.1016/j.molcata.2011.08.019]。

3. 制备方法：从简单的浸渍技术发展到原子层外延（ALE）和蒸发诱导自组装（EISA）等新方法，以更好地控制催化剂结构和性能[10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018]。

4. 助剂：碱金属，特别是钾，被用来提高烯烃选择性并改善催化剂抗失活稳定性[10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018]。

5. 反应机理：研究集中在理解脱氢反应过程中铬物种的氧化还原行为，包括CO2在氧化脱氢过程中的作用[10.1007/s10562-008-9569-3]。

6. 表征技术：采用了原位拉曼光谱、XPS、XAFS和UV-Vis光谱等先进方法来研究催化剂在反应条件下的结构和行为[10.1021/jp960543]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：自1933年以来，铬氧化物基催化剂的演变一直受到石油化工行业对更高效、更选择性脱氢工艺需求的推动。虽然在理解结构-活性关系和优化催化剂性能方面取得了重大进展，但仍有进一步研究和发展的领域。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：
1. 开发含铬量更低或使用替代活性物种的更环保催化剂。
2. 探索新型载体材料，如金属有机框架或二维材料，以增强催化剂的稳定性和活性。
3. 研究人工智能和机器学习在催化剂设计和优化中的潜力。
4. 将铬氧化物基催化剂与可再生原料结合，实现更可持续的化学品生产过程。
5. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解催化剂在工业条件下的行为。

这些进展可能导致更高效、更可持续的脱氢过程，在满足烯烃不断增长的需求的同时最大限度地减少环境影响。
