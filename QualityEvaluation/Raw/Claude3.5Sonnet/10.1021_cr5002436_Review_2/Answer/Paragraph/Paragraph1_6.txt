<English>
Chromium oxide-based catalysts have evolved significantly since their discovery by Frey and Huppke in 1933, becoming crucial in various industrial processes and the subject of extensive research. This review focuses on their development, industrial significance, and multifaceted research in dehydrogenation processes.

Initially, these catalysts were primarily used for n-butane dehydrogenation to produce butenes and butadiene. During World War II, chromia/alumina catalysts were employed to dehydrogenate butane for high-octane aviation fuel production [10.1002/9783527626113.ch15]. The first UOP-designed plant using chromia-alumina catalysts began operation in England in 1940 [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].

Industrial applications expanded significantly over time. By the late 1980s, Houdry extended the use of chromia-alumina catalysts to propane and isobutane dehydrogenation, introducing the Catofin process [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]. Currently, chromium oxide-based catalysts are widely used for:
1. Dehydrogenation of light alkanes (propane, isobutane) [10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5]
2. Ethylene polymerization (Phillips catalyst) [10.1021/j100120a030]
3. Oxidation of organic compounds [10.1007/s10562-008-9599-x]

The Catofin process has become a world leader in propene and isobutene production, with an installed capacity of 600,000 and 2,600,000 metric tons per year, respectively [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4].

Research focus areas that have contributed to the significance of chromium oxide-based catalysts in dehydrogenation processes include:

1. Structure and active sites: Studies have identified various chromium species (Cr6+, Cr5+, Cr3+, Cr2+) on catalyst surfaces [10.1002/9783527626113.ch15]. Recent research suggests that Cr3+ or a combination of Cr3+ and Cr2+ serve as active sites for dehydrogenation reactions [10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5].

2. Support materials: While alumina remains the primary support for industrial applications, research has explored alternatives such as zirconia, silica, and mesoporous materials [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4]. Mesoporous supports offer larger surface areas and uniform pore structures, allowing better dispersion of chromium species [10.1016/j.molcata.2011.08.019].

3. Promoters: Alkali metals, particularly potassium, have been used to enhance olefin selectivity and improve catalyst stability [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4].

4. Preparation methods: Techniques have evolved from simple impregnation to more advanced methods like atomic layer epitaxy (ALE) [10.1006/jcat.1996.0276].

5. Reaction mechanisms: Recent studies have focused on understanding redox cycles involving different chromium species during dehydrogenation reactions [10.1007/s10562-008-9569-3].

6. Characterization techniques: Advanced methods such as in-situ Raman spectroscopy, XPS, and XAFS have been employed to study catalyst structure and behavior under reaction conditions [10.1021/jp960543].

Despite extensive research, some aspects of chromium oxide-based catalysts remain subjects of debate, such as the exact nature of active sites and reaction mechanisms. Ongoing research aims to optimize catalyst performance, improve stability, and develop more efficient dehydrogenation processes.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of chromium oxide-based catalysts since 1933 demonstrates the synergy between industrial needs and academic research in driving catalyst development. While these catalysts have become crucial in various industrial processes, particularly dehydrogenation, there are still opportunities for improvement and new applications.

Future research may focus on:
1. Developing more environmentally friendly alternatives to chromium-based catalysts, addressing concerns about chromium toxicity.
2. Exploring novel support materials, such as metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) or 2D materials, to enhance catalyst performance and stability.
3. Investigating the potential of chromium oxide-based catalysts in emerging fields like CO2 utilization or renewable energy applications.
4. Applying advanced characterization techniques and computational modeling to gain deeper insights into reaction mechanisms and active site structures.
5. Optimizing catalyst formulations for new feedstocks, such as bio-based alkanes, to support the transition to a more sustainable chemical industry.

As the demand for light olefins continues to grow and environmental regulations become stricter, the development of more efficient and sustainable dehydrogenation catalysts will remain a critical area of research and innovation in the coming years.

<Chinese>
自1933年发现以来，铬氧化物基催化剂已经发展成为各种工业过程中的关键组成部分，并成为广泛研究的主题。本综述着重于它们在脱氢过程中的发展、工业意义和多方面研究。

最初，这些催化剂主要用于正丁烷脱氢以生产丁烯和丁二烯。在第二次世界大战期间，铬/氧化铝催化剂被用于丁烷脱氢以生产高辛烷值航空燃料 [10.1002/9783527626113.ch15]。1940年，第一个使用铬/氧化铝催化剂的UOP设计工厂在英国投入运营 [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。

随着时间的推移，工业应用显著扩大。到20世纪80年代末，Houdry将铬/氧化铝催化剂的应用扩展到丙烷和异丁烷脱氢，引入了Catofin工艺 [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。目前，铬氧化物基催化剂广泛用于：
1. 轻烷烃（丙烷，异丁烷）脱氢 [10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5]
2. 乙烯聚合（Phillips催化剂） [10.1021/j100120a030]
3. 有机化合物的氧化 [10.1007/s10562-008-9599-x]

Catofin工艺已成为丙烯和异丁烯生产的世界领先者，安装产能分别达到60万和260万公吨/年 [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4]。

对铬氧化物基催化剂在脱氢过程中的重要性有贡献的研究重点领域包括：

1. 结构和活性位点：研究已经确定了催化剂表面上存在各种铬物种（Cr6+, Cr5+, Cr3+, Cr2+）[10.1002/9783527626113.ch15]。最新研究表明，Cr3+或Cr3+和Cr2+的组合作为脱氢反应的活性位点 [10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5]。

2. 载体材料：虽然氧化铝仍然是工业应用的主要载体，但研究已经探索了诸如氧化锆、二氧化硅和介孔材料等替代品 [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4]。介孔载体提供更大的表面积和均匀的孔结构，允许更好地分散铬物种 [10.1016/j.molcata.2011.08.019]。

3. 助催化剂：碱金属，特别是钾，已被用于增强烯烃选择性和改善催化剂稳定性 [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4]。

4. 制备方法：技术已经从简单的浸渍法发展到更先进的方法，如原子层外延（ALE）[10.1006/jcat.1996.0276]。

5. 反应机理：最新研究集中在理解脱氢反应过程中不同铬物种之间的氧化还原循环 [10.1007/s10562-008-9569-3]。

6. 表征技术：先进的方法如原位拉曼光谱、XPS和XAFS已被用于研究催化剂在反应条件下的结构和行为 [10.1021/jp960543]。

尽管进行了广泛的研究，铬氧化物基催化剂的某些方面仍然存在争议，例如活性位点的确切性质和反应机理。正在进行的研究旨在优化催化剂性能，提高稳定性，并开发更高效的脱氢工艺。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：自1933年以来铬氧化物基催化剂的发展展示了工业需求和学术研究在推动催化剂发展方面的协同作用。虽然这些催化剂已经在各种工业过程中变得至关重要，特别是在脱氢过程中，但仍然存在改进和新应用的机会。

未来的研究可能集中在：
1. 开发更环保的铬基催化剂替代品，解决铬毒性问题。
2. 探索新型载体材料，如金属有机骨架（MOFs）或二维材料，以提高催化剂性能和稳定性。
3. 研究铬氧化物基催化剂在新兴领域如CO2利用或可再生能源应用中的潜力。
4. 应用先进的表征技术和计算模拟，深入了解反应机理和活性位点结构。
5. 针对新的原料（如生物基烷烃）优化催化剂配方，以支持向更可持续的化学工业过渡。

随着轻烯烃需求的持续增长和环境法规的日益严格，开发更高效和可持续的脱氢催化剂将在未来几年内仍然是研究和创新的关键领域。
