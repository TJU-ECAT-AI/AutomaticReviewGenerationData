<English>
Chromium oxide-based catalysts have undergone significant evolution since their discovery in 1933, becoming crucial in various industrial dehydrogenation processes. Their development has been driven by the growing demand for light olefins and the need for more efficient and selective catalytic systems.

Key industrial applications of chromium oxide-based catalysts include:

1. Dehydrogenation of light alkanes: Primarily used for the production of propene and isobutene from corresponding paraffins [10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5]. The CATOFIN process, utilizing these catalysts, leads the world in propene and isobutene production, with installed capacities of 600,000 and 2,600,000 metric tons per annum, respectively [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4].

2. Ethylene polymerization: The Phillips catalyst (Cr/SiO2) is responsible for about one-third of the world's high-density polyethylene (HDPE) production [10.039/a801710g].

3. Oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) of hydrocarbons: Recent research has focused on using these catalysts for ODH of various hydrocarbons in the presence of CO2, including ethane, propane, isobutane, and ethylbenzene [10.1016/j.micromeso.2012.05.011].

The evolution of these catalysts has been marked by several key research focus areas:

1. Structure and active sites: Studies have investigated the nature of active chromium species, with ongoing debates about the role of different oxidation states (Cr6+, Cr5+, Cr3+, and Cr2+) in catalytic activity [10.1021/cr5002436_2]. Recent research suggests that Cr3+ species, either redox or exposed non-redox sites, serve as the active sites for dehydrogenation reactions [10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5].

2. Support materials: While alumina has been the traditional support, research has explored alternatives such as silica, zirconia, and mesoporous materials. These studies aim to enhance catalyst performance through improved dispersion and stability of chromium species [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014].

3. Promoters: The addition of alkali metals, particularly potassium, has been found to enhance olefin selectivity and improve catalyst stability against deactivation [10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018].

4. Preparation methods: Various techniques have been developed to synthesize chromium oxide-based catalysts, including impregnation, atomic layer epitaxy (ALE), co-precipitation, and evaporation-induced self-assembly (EISA) [10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018].

5. Reaction mechanisms: Research has focused on understanding the redox cycles involving different chromium species during dehydrogenation reactions, particularly in the presence of CO2 as an oxidant [10.1007/s10562-008-9569-3].

6. Deactivation and regeneration: Studies have investigated factors affecting catalyst deactivation, primarily due to coke formation, and methods for regenerating spent catalysts [10.1016/0926-860x(92)80264-d].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of chromium oxide-based catalysts since 1933 has been driven by the interplay between industrial needs and academic research. The ongoing challenge is to develop catalysts with improved activity, selectivity, and stability while addressing environmental concerns.

Future research directions may include:

1. Exploring novel support materials and preparation methods to enhance catalyst performance and longevity.
2. Investigating the potential of bimetallic or multi-component catalysts to synergize the properties of chromium with other active elements.
3. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand catalyst behavior under reaction conditions.
4. Integrating artificial intelligence and machine learning approaches to accelerate catalyst design and optimization.
5. Exploring the use of chromium oxide-based catalysts in emerging fields such as biomass conversion or CO2 utilization, leveraging their dehydrogenation capabilities in new applications.

These advancements could lead to more sustainable and efficient processes in the petrochemical industry, potentially expanding the role of chromium oxide-based catalysts in the production of valuable chemicals and materials.

<Chinese>
自1933年发现以来，氧化铬基催化剂已经历了显著的演变，在各种工业脱氢过程中变得至关重要。它们的发展一直受到对轻烯烃需求的增长以及对更高效、更具选择性的催化系统需求的推动。

氧化铬基催化剂的主要工业应用包括：

1. 轻烷烃脱氢：主要用于从相应的烷烃生产丙烯和异丁烯[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5]。使用这些催化剂的CATOFIN工艺在丙烯和异丁烯生产方面处于世界领先地位，装机容量分别为60万和260万公吨/年[10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4]。

2. 乙烯聚合：菲利普斯催化剂（Cr/SiO2）负责全球约三分之一的高密度聚乙烯（HDPE）生产[10.039/a801710g]。

3. 烃类氧化脱氢（ODH）：近期研究集中在使用这些催化剂在CO2存在下进行各种烃类的ODH，包括乙烷、丙烷、异丁烷和乙苯[10.1016/j.micromeso.2012.05.011]。

这些催化剂的演变主要集中在几个关键研究领域：

1. 结构和活性位点：研究调查了活性铬物种的性质，对不同氧化态（Cr6+、Cr5+、Cr3+和Cr2+）在催化活性中的作用仍存在争议[10.1021/cr5002436_2]。最新研究表明，Cr3+物种，无论是氧化还原型或暴露的非氧化还原型位点，都作为脱氢反应的活性位点[10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5]。

2. 载体材料：虽然氧化铝一直是传统载体，但研究已探索了诸如二氧化硅、二氧化锆和介孔材料等替代品。这些研究旨在通过改善铬物种的分散性和稳定性来提高催化剂性能[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014]。

3. 促进剂：添加碱金属，特别是钾，被发现可以提高烯烃选择性并改善催化剂抗失活性能[10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018]。

4. 制备方法：已开发出各种技术来合成氧化铬基催化剂，包括浸渍法、原子层外延（ALE）、共沉淀法和蒸发诱导自组装（EISA）[10.1016/j.apcata.2013.02.018]。

5. 反应机理：研究集中在理解脱氢反应过程中不同铬物种的氧化还原循环，特别是在CO2作为氧化剂存在的情况下[10.1007/s10562-008-9569-3]。

6. 失活和再生：研究调查了影响催化剂失活的因素，主要是由于焦炭形成，以及再生废催化剂的方法[10.1016/0926-860x(92)80264-d]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：自1933年以来，氧化铬基催化剂的演变一直受到工业需求和学术研究之间相互作用的推动。当前的挑战是开发具有改进的活性、选择性和稳定性的催化剂，同时解决环境问题。

未来研究方向可能包括：

1. 探索新型载体材料和制备方法，以提高催化剂性能和寿命。
2. 研究双金属或多组分催化剂的潜力，以协同铬与其他活性元素的特性。
3. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解反应条件下的催化剂行为。
4. 整合人工智能和机器学习方法，加速催化剂设计和优化。
5. 探索氧化铬基催化剂在新兴领域（如生物质转化或CO2利用）中的应用，利用它们在新应用中的脱氢能力。

这些进展可能导致石油化工行业更可持续和高效的工艺，潜在地扩大氧化铬基催化剂在有价值化学品和材料生产中的作用。
