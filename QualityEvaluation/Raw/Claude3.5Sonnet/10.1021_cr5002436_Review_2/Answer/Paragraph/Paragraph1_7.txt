<English>
Chromium oxide-based catalysts have evolved significantly since their discovery by Frey and Huppke in 1933, becoming crucial in various industrial processes and a subject of extensive research. The evolution of these catalysts has been marked by continuous improvements in their performance, selectivity, and stability for dehydrogenation processes.

Industrial Applications:
Initially used for n-butane dehydrogenation to produce butenes and butadiene, chromium oxide-based catalysts have found widespread applications in the petrochemical industry. Key applications include:
1. Dehydrogenation of light alkanes (propane, butane, isobutane) to produce corresponding olefins [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x].
2. Ethylene polymerization using the Phillips catalyst (Cr/SiO2), responsible for about one-third of global high-density polyethylene (HDPE) production [10.1039/a801710g].
3. Production of propylene and isobutylene, with the CATOFIN process being a world leader, having an installed capacity of 600,000 and 2,600,000 metric tons per year, respectively [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4].

Research Focus Areas:
1. Structure and Active Sites:
Research has focused on understanding the nature of active chromium species. Multiple oxidation states (Cr6+, Cr5+, Cr3+, and Cr2+) have been identified, with their distribution depending on factors such as chromium loading, preparation conditions, and support material [10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5]. Recent studies suggest that Cr3+ species, either redox or exposed non-redox sites, serve as the active sites for dehydrogenation reactions [10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5].

2. Support Materials:
While alumina has been the traditional support, research has explored various alternatives:
- Zirconia has shown promise, with Cr/ZrO2 catalysts exhibiting increased activity compared to Cr/Al2O3 and Cr/SiO2 [10.1016/s0021-9517(03)00141-6].
- Mesoporous materials like MCM-41 have been investigated due to their high surface area and uniform pore structure [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014].
- Ceria has been found to enhance catalytic activity through metal-support interactions [10.1007/s10562-008-9569-3].

3. Preparation Methods:
Conventional impregnation methods using Cr(NO3)3 or CrO3 solutions have been complemented by new techniques:
- Atomic layer epitaxy (ALE) using sequential saturating reactions of Cr(acac)3 vapor and air [10.1006/jcat.1996.0276].
- Thermolytic molecular precursor (TMP) cothermolysis method for creating catalysts with high surface areas and highly dispersed active sites [10.1016/s0021-9517(03)00141-6].

4. Reaction Mechanisms:
Studies have revealed a redox cycle involving Cr6+ and Cr3+ species during oxidative dehydrogenation reactions. CO2 has been found to play a role in reoxidizing Cr3+ to Cr6+, maintaining catalyst activity [10.1007/s10562-008-9569-3].

5. Promoters:
Alkali metals, particularly potassium, have been used to mitigate the acidity of alumina supports, regulate deactivation, and improve selectivity [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4].

6. Characterization Techniques:
Advanced methods such as UV-Vis spectroscopy, Raman spectroscopy, XPS, EPR, and in situ spectroscopic techniques have been developed to study these catalysts under reaction conditions [10.1002/9783527626113.ch15].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The evolution of chromium oxide-based catalysts since 1933 has been driven by the increasing demand for light olefins and the need for more efficient and selective catalytic processes. The multifaceted research approach, combining studies of preparation methods, active sites, support materials, and reaction mechanisms, has led to significant improvements in catalyst performance and stability.

Looking forward, several promising avenues for future research and development emerge:

1. Novel support materials: The exploration of advanced materials, such as 2D materials or metal-organic frameworks, could lead to catalysts with enhanced stability and selectivity.

2. Precise control of active sites: Developing techniques for atomic-level control of chromium species distribution and oxidation states could optimize catalyst performance.

3. In situ and operando characterization: Further development of advanced spectroscopic techniques could provide real-time insights into catalyst behavior under reaction conditions, leading to better understanding and control of deactivation mechanisms.

4. Computational modeling: Integration of experimental data with advanced computational modeling could accelerate catalyst design and optimization.

5. Sustainable catalysis: Exploring the use of chromium oxide-based catalysts in CO2 utilization processes could contribute to more environmentally friendly chemical production routes.

6. Alternative active species: While chromium remains the primary focus, research into other transition metals or mixed-metal systems could lead to new catalytic materials with improved performance or reduced environmental impact.

By addressing these areas, researchers can continue to improve the efficiency, selectivity, and sustainability of dehydrogenation processes, ensuring the ongoing significance of chromium oxide-based catalysts in the chemical industry.

<Chinese>
自1933年被发现以来，铬氧化物基催化剂已经发展显著，成为各种工业过程中的关键组成部分，并成为广泛研究的主题。这些催化剂的演变以其在脱氢过程中性能、选择性和稳定性的持续改进为标志。

工业应用：
最初用于正丁烷脱氢生产丁烯和丁二烯，铬氧化物基催化剂在石油化工行业中得到广泛应用。主要应用包括：
1. 轻烷烃（丙烷、丁烷、异丁烷）脱氢生产相应的烯烃 [10.1016/s0926-860x(01)00816-x]。
2. 使用菲利普斯催化剂（Cr/SiO2）进行乙烯聚合，约占全球高密度聚乙烯（HDPE）产量的三分之一 [10.1039/a801710g]。
3. 生产丙烯和异丁烯，其中CATOFIN工艺是世界领先者，丙烯和异丁烯的安装产能分别为60万和260万吨/年 [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4]。

研究重点领域：
1. 结构和活性位点：
研究集中在理解活性铬物种的性质。已经确定了多种氧化态（Cr6+, Cr5+, Cr3+和Cr2+），它们的分布取决于铬负载量、制备条件和载体材料等因素 [10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5]。最近的研究表明，Cr3+物种，无论是氧化还原型还是暴露的非氧化还原型位点，都作为脱氢反应的活性位点 [10.1016/s0926-860x(99)00310-5]。

2. 载体材料：
虽然氧化铝一直是传统载体，但研究已经探索了各种替代品：
- 氧化锆显示出promise，Cr/ZrO2催化剂表现出比Cr/Al2O3和Cr/SiO2更高的活性 [10.1016/s0021-9517(03)00141-6]。
- 由于其高比表面积和均匀的孔结构，已经研究了介孔材料如MCM-41 [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014]。
- 发现氧化铈通过金属-载体相互作用增强催化活性 [10.1007/s10562-008-9569-3]。

3. 制备方法：
传统的使用Cr(NO3)3或CrO3溶液的浸渍方法已经被新技术补充：
- 使用Cr(acac)3蒸气和空气的序列饱和反应的原子层外延（ALE）[10.1006/jcat.1996.0276]。
- 热解分子前驱体（TMP）共热解方法，用于创建具有高比表面积和高度分散活性位点的催化剂 [10.1016/s0021-9517(03)00141-6]。

4. 反应机理：
研究揭示了在氧化脱氢反应过程中涉及Cr6+和Cr3+物种的氧化还原循环。发现CO2在将Cr3+重新氧化为Cr6+中起作用，维持催化剂活性 [10.1007/s10562-008-9569-3]。

5. 助剂：
碱金属，特别是钾，被用来减轻氧化铝载体的酸性，调节失活，并提高选择性 [10.1016/s0920-5861(99)00047-4]。

6. 表征技术：
开发了先进的方法，如UV-Vis光谱、拉曼光谱、XPS、EPR和原位光谱技术，以在反应条件下研究这些催化剂 [10.1002/9783527626113.ch15]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：
自1933年以来，铬氧化物基催化剂的演变一直由轻烯烃需求的增加和更高效、更选择性催化过程的需求所推动。多方面的研究方法，结合了制备方法、活性位点、载体材料和反应机理的研究，已经导致催化剂性能和稳定性的显著提高。

展望未来，几个有前景的研究和开发方向浮现出来：

1. 新型载体材料：探索先进材料，如2D材料或金属有机框架，可能会导致具有增强稳定性和选择性的催化剂。

2. 精确控制活性位点：开发用于原子级控制铬物种分布和氧化态的技术可以优化催化剂性能。

3. 原位和操作表征：进一步发展先进的光谱技术可以提供反应条件下催化剂行为的实时洞察，从而更好地理解和控制失活机制。

4. 计算机模拟：将实验数据与先进的计算机模拟集成可以加速催化剂设计和优化。

5. 可持续催化：探索在CO2利用过程中使用铬氧化物基催化剂可以贡献于更环保的化学生产路线。

6. 替代活性物种：虽然铬仍然是主要焦点，但对其他过渡金属或混合金属系统的研究可能会导致具有改进性能或减少环境影响的新型催化材料。

通过解决这些领域，研究人员可以继续提高脱氢过程的效率、选择性和可持续性，确保铬氧化物基催化剂在化学工业中持续重要性。
