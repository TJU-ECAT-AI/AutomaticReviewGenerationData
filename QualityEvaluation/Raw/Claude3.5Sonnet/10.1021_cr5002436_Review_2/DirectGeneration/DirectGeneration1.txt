Since their discovery in 1933, chromium oxide-based catalysts have undergone significant evolution and have become industrially crucial in dehydrogenation processes. These catalysts have revolutionized the production of various chemicals, particularly in the petrochemical industry. Over the decades, researchers have extensively studied and refined chromium oxide catalysts, leading to improved efficiency and selectivity in dehydrogenation reactions. Their versatility has been demonstrated across a wide range of applications, from the production of styrene to the conversion of light alkanes into valuable olefins. The multifaceted research surrounding these catalysts has explored various aspects, including their structural properties, reaction mechanisms, and the effects of different supports and promoters. As environmental concerns have grown, recent studies have also focused on developing more sustainable and eco-friendly chromium oxide-based catalysts. The continuous advancement in this field has not only enhanced our understanding of catalytic processes but has also contributed significantly to the growth and innovation in the chemical industry, making chromium oxide-based catalysts an indispensable tool in modern industrial chemistry.
