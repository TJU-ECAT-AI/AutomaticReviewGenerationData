Comparative analysis of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling: thermodynamics, reaction mechanisms, catalytic performance, challenges, and future prospects in contrast to traditional oxygen-based systems

The use of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling presents a novel approach with distinct advantages and challenges compared to traditional oxygen-based systems. This review section analyzes the key aspects of this emerging technology.

Thermodynamics:
Sulfur oxidation demonstrates a significantly lower thermodynamic driving force for overoxidation compared to oxygen. The energy well for sulfur oxidation of methane to CS2 is 3.45 eV, substantially higher than the 13.41 eV for oxygen oxidation to CO2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. This reduced driving force for overoxidation is advantageous for selective alkene production, potentially leading to improved product yields.

Reaction Mechanisms:
Sulfur-based systems involve both surface and gas-phase reactions. On the surface, methane coupling occurs on transition metal sulfides, with gaseous sulfur regenerating vacancies. In the gas phase, sulfur radical species (SH and HS2) participate in hydrogen abstraction, similar to OH and OOH radicals in oxygen-based systems [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. This dual-mechanism approach offers unique opportunities for catalyst design and process optimization.

Catalytic Performance:
The performance of sulfur-based catalysts is currently modest and far from practical consideration. Ethylene selectivity of up to 18% can be achieved on a PdS catalyst, but this requires very high temperatures (1050°C). The high temperatures are necessary due to the thermodynamics of the reaction and the high CH activation barriers on sulfide catalysts, ranging from 1.91 eV on MoS2 to 2.68 eV for TiS2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. These high activation barriers present a significant challenge for industrial application.

Alkene Selectivity:
Sulfur-based systems show promise for improved alkene selectivity. In propane dehydrogenation, propylene formation is favored both kinetically and thermodynamically with propyl-S2 adducts, unlike propyl-O2 adducts which can form various side products such as propanal, methyloxirane, and oxetane [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. This enhanced selectivity is attributed to the relative instability of S-containing hydrocarbon products, potentially leading to higher-purity alkene streams.

Key Challenges:
1. High reaction temperatures required
2. Poor hydrogen abstraction ability of sulfide catalysts compared to metal oxides
3. High CH activation barriers on sulfide catalysts

Future Prospects:
1. Descriptor-based theoretical screening to identify catalysts with higher conversion rates
2. Exploration of MS (metal-sulfur) bond energy as a descriptor for both CH activation and CC coupling reaction barriers
3. Further investigation of gas-phase sulfur radical species in hydrogen abstraction and their role in improving alkene selectivity
4. Development of catalysts that can operate at lower temperatures while maintaining high selectivity

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 
Considering the advancements and findings discussed, the use of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling presents a promising avenue for future research and development. The thermodynamic advantages and improved selectivity offered by sulfur-based systems could potentially revolutionize alkene production processes. However, significant challenges remain, particularly in terms of high reaction temperatures and catalytic performance.

Future research efforts should focus on developing novel catalysts that can leverage the benefits of sulfur oxidation while mitigating its current limitations. This may involve exploring new catalyst compositions, such as bimetallic sulfides or supported nanoparticles, to lower CH activation barriers and improve hydrogen abstraction capabilities. Additionally, the investigation of gas-phase sulfur radical species and their role in the reaction mechanism could lead to innovative reactor designs that optimize both surface and gas-phase reactions.

Another promising direction is the integration of computational methods and machine learning algorithms to accelerate catalyst discovery and optimization. By utilizing descriptor-based theoretical screening and developing predictive models for catalyst performance, researchers may identify new materials with superior activity and selectivity.

Furthermore, the potential synergies between sulfur-based oxidative coupling and other emerging technologies, such as plasma-assisted catalysis or membrane reactors, should be explored. These hybrid approaches could potentially overcome some of the current limitations and pave the way for more efficient and economically viable processes.

In conclusion, while sulfur-based oxidative coupling of methane is still in its early stages, it represents a exciting frontier in catalysis research with the potential to address long-standing challenges in alkene production. As our understanding of the underlying mechanisms deepens and new materials are developed, this approach may emerge as a viable alternative to traditional oxygen-based systems, offering improved selectivity, reduced energy consumption, and enhanced process efficiency.

比较分析硫作为甲烷氧化偶联中的替代氧化剂：热力学、反应机理、催化性能、挑战和未来前景与传统氧气系统的对比

硫作为甲烷氧化偶联中的替代氧化剂的使用呈现出一种新颖的方法，与传统的氧气系统相比具有独特的优势和挑战。本综述部分分析了这种新兴技术的关键方面。

热力学：
硫氧化表现出显著较低的过度氧化热力学驱动力，相比于氧气。甲烷硫氧化为CS2的能量井为3.45 eV，远高于氧气氧化为CO2的13.41 eV [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。这种降低的过度氧化驱动力有利于选择性烯烃生产，可能导致产品收率的提高。

反应机理：
基于硫的系统涉及表面和气相反应。在表面上，甲烷偶联发生在过渡金属硫化物上，气态硫再生空位。在气相中，硫自由基物种（SH和HS2）参与氢抽提，类似于氧气系统中的OH和OOH自由基 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。这种双重机制方法为催化剂设计和过程优化提供了独特的机会。

催化性能：
目前，基于硫的催化剂的性能相对有限，远未达到实际应用的考虑。在PdS催化剂上可以实现高达18%的乙烯选择性，但这需要非常高的温度（1050°C）。高温是由于反应的热力学和硫化物催化剂上较高的CH活化能垒所必需的，这些能垒从MoS2的1.91 eV到TiS2的2.68 eV不等 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。这些高活化能垒对工业应用构成了重大挑战。

烯烃选择性：
基于硫的系统显示出改善烯烃选择性的前景。在丙烷脱氢中，丙烯的形成在动力学和热力学上都有利于丙基-S2加合物，而丙基-O2加合物可能形成各种副产品，如丙醛、甲基环氧丙烷和环氧丁烷 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。这种增强的选择性归因于含硫烃类产物的相对不稳定性，可能导致更高纯度的烯烃流。

主要挑战：
1. 需要高反应温度
2. 与金属氧化物相比，硫化物催化剂的氢抽提能力较差
3. 硫化物催化剂上的CH活化能垒较高

未来前景：
1. 基于描述符的理论筛选，以识别具有更高转化率的催化剂
2. 探索MS（金属-硫）键能作为CH活化和CC偶联反应能垒的描述符
3. 进一步研究气相硫自由基物种在氢抽提中的作用及其在改善烯烃选择性方面的作用
4. 开发可在较低温度下操作同时保持高选择性的催化剂

考虑到讨论的进展和发现，使用硫作为甲烷氧化偶联中的替代氧化剂为未来的研究和发展提供了一个有前景的方向。基于硫的系统所提供的热力学优势和改善的选择性可能彻底改变烯烃生产过程。然而，仍然存在重大挑战，特别是在高反应温度和催化性能方面。

未来的研究努力应集中在开发可以利用硫氧化优势同时缓解其当前限制的新型催化剂。这可能涉及探索新的催化剂组成，如双金属硫化物或负载型纳米粒子，以降低CH活化能垒并提高氢抽提能力。此外，对气相硫自由基物种及其在反应机理中的作用的研究可能导致创新的反应器设计，优化表面和气相反应。
