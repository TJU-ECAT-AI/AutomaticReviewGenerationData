Comparative analysis of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling: thermodynamics, reaction mechanisms, catalytic performance, challenges, and future prospects in contrast to traditional oxygen-based systems.

The use of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling presents a promising avenue for improving selectivity and reducing overoxidation compared to traditional oxygen-based systems. This approach offers several advantages and challenges in various aspects of the reaction process.

Thermodynamics:
Sulfur oxidation exhibits a significantly lower thermodynamic driving force for overoxidation compared to oxygen. The energy well for sulfur oxidation of methane to CS2 is 3.45 eV, which is considerably higher than the 13.41 eV for oxygen oxidation to CO2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. This reduced driving force for overoxidation is advantageous for selective alkene production, as it mitigates the tendency for complete oxidation to carbon dioxide.

Reaction Mechanisms:
Sulfur-based systems involve both surface and gas-phase reactions. On the surface, methane coupling occurs on transition metal sulfides, with gaseous sulfur regenerating vacancies. In the gas phase, sulfur radical species (SH and HS2) participate in hydrogen abstraction, similar to OH and OOH radicals in oxygen-based systems [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. This dual-mechanism approach potentially offers more control over the reaction pathways and product selectivity.

Catalytic Performance:
The performance of sulfur-based catalysts is currently modest and far from practical consideration. Ethylene selectivity of up to 18% can be achieved on a PdS catalyst, but this requires very high temperatures (1050°C). The high temperatures are necessary due to the thermodynamics of the reaction and the high CH activation barriers on sulfide catalysts, which range from 1.91 eV on MoS2 to 2.68 eV for TiS2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. These high activation barriers present a significant challenge for the practical implementation of sulfur-based systems.

Alkene Selectivity:
Sulfur-based systems show promise for improved alkene selectivity. In propane dehydrogenation, propylene formation is favored both kinetically and thermodynamically with propyl-S2 adducts. This is in contrast to propyl-O2 adducts, which can form various side products such as propanal, methyloxirane, and oxetane, all of which are more thermodynamically stable than propylene [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. The relative instability of S-containing hydrocarbon products contributes to this improved selectivity.

Key Challenges:
1. High reaction temperatures required
2. Poor hydrogen abstraction ability of sulfide catalysts compared to metal oxides
3. High CH activation barriers on sulfide catalysts

Opportunities for Improvement:
1. Descriptor-based theoretical screening to identify catalysts with higher conversion rates
2. Exploration of MS (metal-sulfur) bond energy as a descriptor for both CH activation and CC coupling reaction barriers
3. Further investigation of gas-phase sulfur radical species in hydrogen abstraction and their role in improving alkene selectivity
4. Development of catalysts that can operate at lower temperatures while maintaining high selectivity

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The exploration of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling represents a paradigm shift in catalyst design for alkene production. While the current performance of sulfur-based systems falls short of practical application, the underlying principles offer exciting possibilities for future developments.

The lower thermodynamic driving force for overoxidation in sulfur-based systems provides a fundamental advantage that could be exploited through innovative catalyst design. Future research might focus on developing hybrid catalysts that combine the selectivity advantages of sulfur with the superior hydrogen abstraction capabilities of metal oxides. This could potentially address the challenge of high CH activation barriers while maintaining the desired selectivity.

Additionally, the role of gas-phase sulfur radical species in hydrogen abstraction opens up new avenues for reaction engineering. Future studies could explore the controlled generation and utilization of these radicals to enhance reaction rates and selectivity. This might involve the development of novel reactor designs or the use of plasma-assisted catalysis to generate and control these reactive species.

The use of descriptor-based theoretical screening, as suggested in the study, could be expanded to include machine learning approaches for catalyst discovery. By leveraging large datasets of material properties and catalytic performance, researchers might uncover unexpected catalyst compositions or structures that optimize both activity and selectivity.

Lastly, the development of low-temperature sulfur-based catalysts remains a critical challenge. Future research could explore nanoscale engineering of catalyst surfaces to lower activation barriers or investigate the use of co-catalysts or promoters to enhance activity at lower temperatures. These advancements could pave the way for more energy-efficient and economically viable processes for alkene production using sulfur-based oxidative coupling.

比较分析硫作为甲烷氧化偶联替代氧化剂：热力学、反应机理、催化性能、挑战和未来前景与传统氧气系统的对比。

作为甲烷氧化偶联的替代氧化剂，硫的使用为提高选择性和减少过度氧化提供了一条有前景的途径，相比传统的氧气系统。这种方法在反应过程的各个方面都提供了几个优势和挑战。

热力学：
与氧相比，硫氧化表现出显著较低的过度氧化热力学驱动力。甲烷氧化成CS2的硫氧化能量井为3.45 eV，远高于氧氧化成CO2的13.41 eV [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。这种较低的过度氧化驱动力有利于选择性烯烃生产，因为它减缓了完全氧化成二氧化碳的趋势。

反应机理：
基于硫的系统涉及表面和气相反应。在表面上，甲烷偶联发生在过渡金属硫化物上，气态硫再生空位。在气相中，硫自由基物种（SH和HS2）参与氢提取，类似于氧基系统中的OH和OOH自由基 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。这种双机制方法可能为反应途径和产物选择性提供更多控制。

催化性能：
目前，基于硫的催化剂的性能较为一般，远未达到实际应用的考虑。在PdS催化剂上可以达到高达18%的乙烯选择性，但这需要很高的温度（1050°C）。高温是由于反应的热力学和硫化物催化剂上较高的CH活化障碍所必需的，这些障碍从MoS2的1.91 eV到TiS2的2.68 eV不等 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。这些高活化障碍对基于硫系统的实际应用构成了重大挑战。

烯烃选择性：
基于硫的系统在提高烯烃选择性方面显示出前景。在丙烷脱氢中，丙烯的形成在动力学和热力学上都更有利于丙基-S2加合物。这与丙基-O2加合物形成各种副产品如丙醛、甲基环氧乙烷和环氧丁烷的情况形成对比，这些副产品在热力学上比丙烯更稳定 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。含硫烃类产物的相对不稳定性有助于提高这种选择性。

主要挑战：
1. 需要高反应温度
2. 与金属氧化物相比，硫化物催化剂的氢提取能力较差
3. 硫化物催化剂上的CH活化障碍高

改进机会：
1. 基于描述符的理论筛选，以识别具有更高转化率的催化剂
2. 探索MS（金属-硫）键能作为CH活化和CC偶联反应障碍的描述符
3. 进一步研究气相硫自由基物种在氢提取中的作用及其在提高烯烃选择性方面的作用
4. 开发能在较低温度下运行同时保持高选择性的催化剂

综合理解与未来展望：
探索硫作为甲烷氧化偶联的替代氧化剂代表了烯烃生产催化剂设计的范式转变。虽然目前基于硫的系统性能还不足以实际应用，但其基本原理为未来发展提供了令人兴奋的可能性。

硫基系统较低的过度氧化热力学驱动力提供了一个基本优势，可以通过创新的催化剂设计加以利用。未来的研究可能会集中在开发结合硫的选择性优势和金属氧化物优越氢提取能力的混合催化剂上。这可能会解决高CH活化障碍的挑战，同时保持所需的选择性。

此外，气相硫自由基物种在氢提取中的作用为反应工程开辟了新的途径。未来的研究可以探索这些自由基的受控生成和利用，以提高反应速率和选择性。这可能涉及开发新型反应器设计或使用等离子体辅助催化来生成和控制这些活性物种。

研究中提出的基于描述符的理论筛选可以扩展到包括机器学习方法来发现催化剂。通过利用大量材料性质和催化性能数据集，研究人员可能会发现意想不到的催化剂组成或结构，以优化活性和选择性。

最后，开发低温硫基催化剂仍然是一个关键挑战。未来的研究可以探索催化剂表面的纳米尺度工程以降低活化障碍，或研究使用助催化剂或促进剂以在较低温度下增强活性。这些进展可能为使用基于硫的氧化偶联进行烯烃生产开辟更节能和经济可行的道路。
