Comparative analysis of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling: thermodynamics, reaction mechanisms, catalytic performance, challenges, and future prospects in contrast to traditional oxygen-based systems

The use of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling presents a novel approach with distinct advantages and challenges compared to traditional oxygen-based systems. This review examines the key aspects of this approach, including thermodynamics, reaction mechanisms, catalytic performance, and alkene selectivity.

Thermodynamics:
One of the most significant advantages of using sulfur as an oxidant is the reduced thermodynamic driving force for overoxidation. The energy well for sulfur oxidation of methane to CS2 is 3.45 eV, considerably higher than the 13.41 eV for oxygen oxidation to CO2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. This substantial difference in energetics results in a lower propensity for complete oxidation, potentially leading to improved selectivity towards desired alkene products.

Reaction Mechanisms:
The reaction mechanisms in sulfur-based systems involve both surface and gas-phase reactions. On the catalyst surface, methane coupling occurs on transition metal sulfides, with gaseous sulfur regenerating vacancies. In the gas phase, sulfur radical species such as SH and HS2 participate in hydrogen abstraction, analogous to the role of OH and OOH radicals in oxygen-based systems [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. This dual-pathway mechanism offers opportunities for fine-tuning the reaction process to enhance selectivity and conversion.

Catalytic Performance:
Despite the promising thermodynamic advantages, the current catalytic performance of sulfur-based systems remains modest and far from practical consideration. For instance, an ethylene selectivity of up to 18% can be achieved on a PdS catalyst, but this requires extremely high temperatures of 1050°C [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. The need for such high temperatures stems from both the thermodynamics of the reaction and the relatively high CH activation barriers on sulfide catalysts, which range from 1.91 eV on MoS2 to 2.68 eV for TiS2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13].

Alkene Selectivity:
Sulfur-based systems show promise for improved alkene selectivity, particularly in propane dehydrogenation. With propyl-S2 adducts, propylene formation is favored both kinetically and thermodynamically, partly due to the relative instability of S-containing hydrocarbon products. This contrasts with propyl-O2 adducts, which can form various thermodynamically stable side products such as propanal, methyloxirane, and oxetane [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13].

Key Challenges:
1. High reaction temperatures required for sufficient conversion
2. Poor hydrogen abstraction ability of sulfide catalysts compared to metal oxides
3. High CH activation barriers on sulfide catalysts

Future Prospects:
To address these challenges and fully realize the potential of sulfur-based oxidants in methane oxidative coupling, several avenues for improvement can be explored:
1. Descriptor-based theoretical screening to identify catalysts with higher conversion rates at lower temperatures
2. Investigation of MS (metal-sulfur) bond energy as a descriptor for both CH activation and CC coupling reaction barriers
3. Further study of gas-phase sulfur radical species in hydrogen abstraction and their role in improving alkene selectivity
4. Development of novel catalyst formulations that can operate at lower temperatures while maintaining high selectivity

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The exploration of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling represents a paradigm shift in the field of alkane activation. While the current performance metrics fall short of practical application, the underlying thermodynamic advantages and potential for improved selectivity warrant continued research efforts. Future investigations may focus on developing hybrid systems that combine the benefits of both sulfur and oxygen-based oxidants, potentially leading to synergistic effects in terms of conversion and selectivity. Additionally, the insights gained from studying sulfur-based systems could inspire novel approaches in other related fields, such as the direct conversion of methane to value-added chemicals or the development of more efficient desulfurization catalysts for petroleum refining. As our understanding of the fundamental chemistry and catalysis involved in these systems deepens, we may unlock new pathways for sustainable hydrocarbon utilization and contribute to the broader goals of green chemistry and energy efficiency.

硫作为甲烷氧化偶联替代氧化剂的比较分析：热力学、反应机理、催化性能、挑战和未来展望与传统氧气系统的对比

硫作为甲烷氧化偶联的替代氧化剂，相比传统的氧气系统，呈现出一种具有明显优势和挑战的新方法。本综述考察了这种方法的关键方面，包括热力学、反应机理、催化性能和烯烃选择性。

热力学：
使用硫作为氧化剂的最显著优势之一是过度氧化的热力学驱动力减小。硫氧化甲烷生成CS2的能量阱为3.45 eV，远高于氧气氧化生成CO2的13.41 eV [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。这种能量学上的巨大差异导致完全氧化的倾向较低，可能会提高目标烯烃产物的选择性。

反应机理：
硫基系统的反应机理涉及表面和气相反应。在催化剂表面，甲烷偶联发生在过渡金属硫化物上，气相硫再生空位。在气相中，硫自由基物种如SH和HS2参与氢抽提，类似于氧基系统中OH和OOH自由基的作用 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。这种双路径机制为微调反应过程以提高选择性和转化率提供了机会。

催化性能：
尽管热力学优势可观，但目前硫基系统的催化性能仍然较为一般，远未达到实际应用的考虑。例如，在PdS催化剂上可以实现高达18%的乙烯选择性，但这需要极高的温度（1050°C）[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。如此高温的需求源于反应的热力学和硫化物催化剂上较高的CH活化能垒，这些能垒从MoS2的1.91 eV到TiS2的2.68 eV不等 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。

烯烃选择性：
硫基系统在提高烯烃选择性方面显示出潜力，特别是在丙烷脱氢中。对于丙基-S2加合物，丙烯的形成在动力学和热力学上都更有利，部分原因是含硫烃类产物的相对不稳定性。这与丙基-O2加合物形成各种热力学稳定的副产物（如丙醛、甲基环氧乙烷和环氧丁烷）形成对比 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。

主要挑战：
1. 需要高反应温度以实现足够的转化率
2. 与金属氧化物相比，硫化物催化剂的氢抽提能力较差
3. 硫化物催化剂上的CH活化能垒较高

未来展望：
为了应对这些挑战并充分实现硫基氧化剂在甲烷氧化偶联中的潜力，可以探索几个改进途径：
1. 基于描述符的理论筛选，以识别在较低温度下具有更高转化率的催化剂
2. 研究MS（金属-硫）键能作为CH活化和CC偶联反应能垒的描述符
3. 进一步研究气相硫自由基物种在氢抽提中的作用及其在提高烯烃选择性方面的作用
4. 开发能在较低温度下运行同时保持高选择性的新型催化剂配方

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：
探索硫作为甲烷氧化偶联的替代氧化剂代表了烷烃活化领域的范式转变。尽管当前的性能指标未达到实际应用的要求，但其潜在的热力学优势和提高选择性的潜力值得继续研究。未来的研究可能会集中在开发结合硫基和氧基氧化剂优势的混合系统上，可能在转化率和选择性方面产生协同效应。此外，从研究硫基系统获得的见解可能会启发其他相关领域的新方法，如甲烷直接转化为高附加值化学品或开发更高效的石油精炼脱硫催化剂。随着我们对这些系统中基础化学和催化作用的理解不断加深，我们可能会开辟可持续碳氢化合物利用的新途径，并为绿色化学和能源效率的更广泛目标做出贡献。
