Comparative Analysis of Sulfur as an Alternative Oxidant in Methane Oxidative Coupling

The use of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling presents both advantages and challenges when compared to traditional oxygen-based systems. This review focuses on the thermodynamics, reaction mechanisms, catalytic performance, and alkene selectivity of sulfur-based systems, as well as the key challenges and opportunities for future improvements.

Thermodynamics:
One of the primary advantages of using sulfur as an oxidant is the significantly lower thermodynamic driving force for overoxidation compared to oxygen. The energy well for sulfur oxidation of methane to CS2 is 3.45 eV, which is considerably higher than the 13.41 eV for oxygen oxidation to CO2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. This reduced driving force for overoxidation is favorable for selective alkene production, as it decreases the likelihood of complete oxidation to carbon dioxide.

Reaction Mechanisms:
Sulfur-based systems involve both surface and gas-phase reactions. On the surface, methane coupling occurs on transition metal sulfides, with gaseous sulfur regenerating vacancies. In the gas phase, sulfur radical species (SH and HS2) participate in hydrogen abstraction, similar to OH and OOH radicals in oxygen-based systems [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. This dual-pathway mechanism contributes to the overall reaction efficiency and selectivity.

Catalytic Performance:
The performance of sulfur-based catalysts is currently modest and far from practical consideration. Ethylene selectivity of up to 18% can be achieved on a PdS catalyst, but this requires very high temperatures (1050°C) [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. The high temperatures are necessary due to the thermodynamics of the reaction and the high CH activation barriers on sulfide catalysts. These barriers range from 1.91 eV on MoS2 to 2.68 eV for TiS2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13], which are significantly higher than those observed in traditional oxygen-based systems.

Alkene Selectivity:
Sulfur-based systems show promise for improved alkene selectivity, particularly in propane dehydrogenation. With propyl-S2 adducts, propylene formation is favored both kinetically and thermodynamically, in part due to the relative instability of the S-containing hydrocarbon products [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. This is in contrast to propyl-O2 adducts, which can form various side products such as propanal, methyloxirane, and oxetane, all of which are more thermodynamically stable than propylene.

Key Challenges:
1. High reaction temperatures required
2. Poor hydrogen abstraction ability of sulfide catalysts compared to metal oxides
3. High CH activation barriers on sulfide catalysts

Opportunities for Future Improvements:
1. Descriptor-based theoretical screening to identify catalysts with higher conversion rates
2. Exploration of MS (metal-sulfur) bond energy as a descriptor for both CH activation and CC coupling reaction barriers
3. Further investigation of gas-phase sulfur radical species in hydrogen abstraction and their role in improving alkene selectivity
4. Development of catalysts that can operate at lower temperatures while maintaining high selectivity

Integrative Understanding and Prospective Outlook:
The use of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling represents a promising avenue for improving selectivity and reducing overoxidation in alkene production. The thermodynamic advantages of sulfur-based systems, coupled with their potential for enhanced alkene selectivity, suggest that this approach could lead to significant advancements in the field of oxidative coupling reactions.

Future research efforts should focus on addressing the key challenges identified, particularly the high reaction temperatures and CH activation barriers. This may involve the development of novel catalyst materials that can effectively lower these barriers while maintaining the beneficial thermodynamic properties of sulfur-based systems. Additionally, the exploration of metal-sulfur bond energies as descriptors for both CH activation and CC coupling reaction barriers could provide valuable insights for catalyst design and optimization.

The role of gas-phase sulfur radical species in hydrogen abstraction warrants further investigation, as understanding and harnessing these mechanisms could lead to improved process efficiency and selectivity. Computational studies and advanced spectroscopic techniques could be employed to elucidate the precise nature of these gas-phase reactions and their interplay with surface-mediated processes.

Ultimately, the goal should be to develop sulfur-based catalytic systems that can operate at lower temperatures while maintaining high selectivity and conversion rates. This may require a multidisciplinary approach, combining insights from surface science, computational chemistry, and materials engineering. By addressing these challenges and capitalizing on the unique properties of sulfur as an oxidant, researchers may be able to unlock new possibilities in the field of oxidative coupling reactions, potentially leading to more efficient and sustainable processes for alkene production.

硫作为甲烷氧化偶联替代氧化剂的比较分析

与传统的基于氧气的系统相比，使用硫作为甲烷氧化偶联的替代氧化剂既有优势也有挑战。本综述重点关注基于硫的系统的热力学、反应机理、催化性能和烯烃选择性，以及未来改进的主要挑战和机遇。

热力学：
使用硫作为氧化剂的主要优势之一是过氧化的热力学驱动力显著低于氧气。甲烷硫氧化生成CS2的能量阱为3.45 eV，远高于氧气氧化生成CO2的13.41 eV [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。这种降低的过氧化驱动力有利于选择性烯烃生产，因为它降低了完全氧化为二氧化碳的可能性。

反应机理：
基于硫的系统涉及表面和气相反应。在表面上，甲烷偶联发生在过渡金属硫化物上，气态硫再生空位。在气相中，硫自由基物种（SH和HS2）参与氢抽提，类似于基于氧气系统中的OH和OOH自由基 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。这种双路径机制有助于整体反应效率和选择性。

催化性能：
目前，基于硫的催化剂的性能较为一般，远未达到实际应用的考虑。在PdS催化剂上可以实现高达18%的乙烯选择性，但这需要非常高的温度（1050°C）[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。高温是必要的，这是由于反应的热力学和硫化物催化剂上较高的CH活化能垒。这些能垒从MoS2的1.91 eV到TiS2的2.68 eV [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]，明显高于传统基于氧气系统中观察到的能垒。

烯烃选择性：
基于硫的系统在提高烯烃选择性方面显示出前景，特别是在丙烷脱氢反应中。对于丙基-S2加合物，丙烯的形成在动力学和热力学上都更有利，部分原因是含硫碳氢化合物产物的相对不稳定性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。这与丙基-O2加合物形成各种副产品（如丙醛、甲基环氧乙烷和环氧丁烷）形成对比，这些副产品在热力学上比丙烯更稳定。

主要挑战：
1. 需要高反应温度
2. 与金属氧化物相比，硫化物催化剂的氢抽提能力较差
3. 硫化物催化剂上的CH活化能垒高

未来改进的机会：
1. 基于描述符的理论筛选，以识别具有更高转化率的催化剂
2. 探索MS（金属-硫）键能作为CH活化和CC偶联反应能垒的描述符
3. 进一步研究气相硫自由基物种在氢抽提中的作用及其在提高烯烃选择性中的作用
4. 开发能在较低温度下运行同时保持高选择性的催化剂

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：
使用硫作为甲烷氧化偶联的替代氧化剂代表了一种有希望改善烯烃生产中选择性并减少过氧化的途径。基于硫的系统的热力学优势，加上其提高烯烃选择性的潜力，表明这种方法可能会导致氧化偶联反应领域的重大进展。

未来的研究努力应集中于解决已识别的关键挑战，特别是高反应温度和CH活化能垒。这可能涉及开发新型催化剂材料，能够有效降低这些能垒，同时保持基于硫系统的有利热力学性质。此外，探索金属-硫键能作为CH活化和CC偶联反应能垒的描述符可能为催化剂设计和优化提供宝贵的见解。

气相硫自由基物种在氢抽提中的作用值得进一步研究，因为理解和利用这些机制可能会导致过程效率和选择性的提高。可以采用计算研究和先进的光谱技术来阐明这些气相反应的精确性质及其与表面介导过程的相互作用。

最终目标应该是开发能够在较低温度下运行同时保持高选择性和转化率的基于硫的催化系统。这可能需要多学科方法，结合表面科学、计算化学和材料工程的见解。通过解决这些挑战并利用硫作为氧化剂的独特性质，研究人员可能能够在氧化偶联反应领域开辟新的可能性，潜在地导致更高效和可持续的烯烃生产过程。
