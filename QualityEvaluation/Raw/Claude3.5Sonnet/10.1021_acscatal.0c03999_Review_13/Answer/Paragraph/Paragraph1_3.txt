<English>
The use of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling presents a promising avenue for improving selectivity and reducing overoxidation compared to traditional oxygen-based systems. This comparative analysis explores the thermodynamics, reaction mechanisms, catalytic performance, challenges, and future prospects of sulfur-based oxidants in methane oxidative coupling.

Thermodynamics:
Sulfur oxidation exhibits a significantly lower thermodynamic driving force for overoxidation compared to oxygen. The energy well for sulfur oxidation of methane to CS2 is 3.45 eV, which is considerably higher than the 13.41 eV for oxygen oxidation to CO2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. This reduced driving force for overoxidation is advantageous for selective alkene production, as it minimizes the tendency for complete oxidation of methane to carbon dioxide.

Reaction Mechanisms:
Sulfur-based systems involve both surface and gas-phase reactions. On the surface, methane coupling occurs on transition metal sulfides, with gaseous sulfur regenerating vacancies. In the gas phase, sulfur radical species (SH and HS2) participate in hydrogen abstraction, similar to OH and OOH radicals in oxygen-based systems [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. This dual-phase reaction mechanism provides opportunities for tuning catalytic performance and selectivity.

Catalytic Performance:
The performance of sulfur-based catalysts is currently modest and far from practical consideration. Ethylene selectivity of up to 18% can be achieved on a PdS catalyst, but this requires very high temperatures (1050°C) [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. The high temperatures are necessary due to the thermodynamics of the reaction and the high CH activation barriers on sulfide catalysts. These barriers range from 1.91 eV on MoS2 to 2.68 eV for TiS2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. The poor hydrogen abstraction ability of sulfide catalysts compared to metal oxides further contributes to the need for high reaction temperatures.

Alkene Selectivity:
Sulfur-based systems show promise for improved alkene selectivity. In propane dehydrogenation, propylene formation is favored both kinetically and thermodynamically with propyl-S2 adducts, unlike propyl-O2 adducts which can form various side products such as propanal, methyloxirane, and oxetane [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]. This enhanced selectivity is partly due to the relative instability of S-containing hydrocarbon products, making propylene formation more favorable.

Key Challenges:
1. High reaction temperatures required for adequate performance
2. Poor hydrogen abstraction ability of sulfide catalysts compared to metal oxides
3. High CH activation barriers on sulfide catalysts

Future Prospects:
Despite the challenges, several opportunities for improvement exist:
1. Descriptor-based theoretical screening to identify catalysts with higher conversion rates
2. Exploration of MS (metal-sulfur) bond energy as a descriptor for both CH activation and CC coupling reaction barriers
3. Further investigation of gas-phase sulfur radical species in hydrogen abstraction and their role in improving alkene selectivity
4. Development of catalysts that can operate at lower temperatures while maintaining high selectivity

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 
Considering the advancements and findings discussed, the use of sulfur as an alternative oxidant in methane oxidative coupling presents a promising direction for future research. The lower thermodynamic driving force for overoxidation and improved alkene selectivity offer significant advantages over traditional oxygen-based systems. However, to fully realize the potential of this approach, future research efforts should focus on addressing the key challenges identified.

One promising avenue for future exploration is the development of novel catalyst materials that can lower the CH activation barriers while maintaining the beneficial properties of sulfur-based systems. This could involve the use of advanced computational methods to screen a wide range of potential catalysts, focusing on optimizing the metal-sulfur bond energy to balance CH activation and CC coupling.

Additionally, further investigation into the role of gas-phase sulfur radical species could lead to innovative reactor designs that optimize the interplay between surface and gas-phase reactions. This could potentially allow for lower operating temperatures while maintaining high selectivity.

Lastly, the integration of sulfur-based oxidants with other emerging technologies in the field of methane activation, such as plasma-assisted catalysis or microwave-enhanced reactions, could open up new possibilities for efficient and selective methane conversion. These hybrid approaches might offer synergistic effects that overcome the current limitations of sulfur-based systems while capitalizing on their inherent advantages.

<Chinese>
与传统的氧气基系统相比，使用硫作为甲烷氧化偶联的替代氧化剂展现了提高选择性和减少过度氧化的前景。本比较分析探讨了硫基氧化剂在甲烷氧化偶联中的热力学、反应机理、催化性能、挑战和未来前景。

热力学：
与氧气相比，硫氧化表现出显著较低的过度氧化热力学驱动力。甲烷氧化成CS2的能量阱为3.45 eV，远高于氧气氧化成CO2的13.41 eV [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。这种较低的过度氧化驱动力有利于选择性烯烃生产，因为它最大限度地减少了甲烷完全氧化为二氧化碳的趋势。

反应机理：
硫基系统涉及表面和气相反应。在表面上，甲烷偶联发生在过渡金属硫化物上，气态硫再生空位。在气相中，硫自由基物种（SH和HS2）参与氢抽提，类似于氧基系统中的OH和OOH自由基 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。这种双相反应机理为调整催化性能和选择性提供了机会。

催化性能：
目前，硫基催化剂的性能较为一般，距离实际应用还有一定距离。在PdS催化剂上可以达到高达18%的乙烯选择性，但这需要非常高的温度（1050°C）[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。高温是由于反应的热力学和硫化物催化剂上较高的CH活化能垒所必需的。这些能垒从MoS2的1.91 eV到TiS2的2.68 eV不等 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。与金属氧化物相比，硫化物催化剂较差的氢抽提能力进一步导致了对高反应温度的需求。

烯烃选择性：
硫基系统显示出提高烯烃选择性的前景。在丙烷脱氢中，与丙基-O2加合物相比，丙基-S2加合物在动力学和热力学上都更有利于丙烯的形成，而丙基-O2加合物可能形成各种副产品，如丙醛、甲基环氧乙烷和环氧丁烷 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_13]。这种增强的选择性部分是由于含硫烃类产物的相对不稳定性，使得丙烯的形成更为有利。

主要挑战：
1. 需要高反应温度以获得足够的性能
2. 与金属氧化物相比，硫化物催化剂的氢抽提能力较差
3. 硫化物催化剂上的CH活化能垒高

未来前景：
尽管存在挑战，但仍有几个改进的机会：
1. 基于描述符的理论筛选，以识别具有更高转化率的催化剂
2. 探索MS（金属-硫）键能作为CH活化和CC偶联反应能垒的描述符
3. 进一步研究气相硫自由基物种在氢抽提中的作用及其在提高烯烃选择性中的作用
4. 开发能在较低温度下运行同时保持高选择性的催化剂

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：
考虑到讨论的进展和发现，使用硫作为甲烷氧化偶联的替代氧化剂为未来研究提供了一个有前景的方向。较低的过度氧化热力学驱动力和改善的烯烃选择性相对于传统的氧气基系统提供了显著优势。然而，为了充分实现这种方法的潜力，未来的研究努力应该集中在解决已识别的关键挑战上。

未来探索的一个有前景的方向是开发能够降低CH活化能垒同时保持硫基系统有益特性的新型催化剂材料。这可能涉及使用先进的计算方法来筛选广泛的潜在催化剂，重点是优化金属-硫键能以平衡CH活化和CC偶联。

此外，对气相硫自由基物种作用的进一步研究可能导致创新的反应器设计，优化表面和气相反应之间的相互作用。这可能允许在保持高选择性的同时降低操作温度。

最后，将硫基氧化剂与甲烷活化领域的其他新兴技术（如等离子体辅助催化或微波增强反应）结合，可能为高效和选择性甲烷转化开辟新的可能性。这些混合方法可能提供协同效应，克服硫基系统当前的限制，同时利用其固有优势。
