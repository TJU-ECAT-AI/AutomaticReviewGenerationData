<English>
Bimetallic catalysts have evolved significantly in heterogeneous catalysis since their commercial introduction in the 1960s, initially gaining interest for their use in hydrocarbon reforming due to their unique reactivities compared to monometallic catalysts [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]. Over time, their applications have expanded to various catalysis and electrocatalysis processes [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001].

The evolution of bimetallic catalysts has been characterized by several key developments:

1. Unique Properties: Bimetallic catalysts exhibit novel electronic and chemical properties distinct from their parent metals, offering enhanced selectivity, activity, and stability [10.1021/cr300096b_0]. These properties arise from synergistic effects between the two metals, particularly in alloyed and core-shell structures [10.1039/c1jm12020d].

2. Tunable Characteristics: The chemical and physical properties of bimetallic nanoparticles can be finely tuned by adjusting their size, composition, and atomic ordering [10.1039/c1jm12020d]. This tunability allows for precise control over catalytic performance.

3. Advanced Structures: Core-shell nanoparticles represent an advanced form of bimetallic catalysts, with physical and chemical properties strongly dependent on the structure of the core, shell, and interface [10.1039/c1jm12020d].

4. Improved Performance: Bimetallic catalysts have demonstrated significant improvements in performance. For instance, the Pt3Ni(111) surface has shown 10-fold higher activity for oxygen reduction reactions compared to Pt(111) and 90-fold higher activity than state-of-the-art Pt/C catalysts for polymer electrolyte membrane fuel cells [10.1126/science.1135941].

The unique electronic and chemical properties of bimetallic catalysts contribute to bridging the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts in several ways:

1. Model Surfaces: Bimetallic surfaces created by depositing submonolayer to monolayer coverages of admetal on single crystal substrates allow for detailed studies using various chemical, electronic, and physical probes [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]. This provides fundamental insights into their properties.

2. Bridging the Materials Gap: Systematic studies on the formation and chemical properties of equivalent bimetallic structures on polycrystalline substrates and in nanoparticles help translate findings from model surfaces to more industrially relevant supported catalysts [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001].

3. Addressing the Pressure Gap: Research focuses on investigating the stability of surface and subsurface bimetallic structures under reaction conditions [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]. This helps understand catalyst behavior under more realistic conditions.

4. Advanced Characterization Techniques: The use of ex situ and in situ surface-sensitive probes, along with density functional theory (DFT) calculations, allows researchers to study bimetallic catalysts under various conditions, bridging the gap between idealized models and practical applications [10.1126/science.1135941].

5. Computational Screening: The development of computational frameworks combining DFT calculations with microkinetic modeling enables rapid evaluation and screening of complex bimetallic catalysts [10.1038/nchem.626].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of bimetallic catalysts has significantly advanced our understanding of heterogeneous catalysis and opened new avenues for catalyst design. Looking forward, several promising directions emerge:

1. Nanoscale Engineering: Future research could focus on developing precise methods for synthesizing bimetallic nanostructures that mimic the properties of highly active model surfaces, such as the Pt3Ni(111) surface [10.1126/science.1135941].

2. In Situ Characterization: Advancements in operando spectroscopy and microscopy techniques could provide deeper insights into the dynamic behavior of bimetallic catalysts under realistic reaction conditions, further bridging the pressure gap [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001].

3. Machine Learning Integration: Combining the vast data from computational screening and experimental results with machine learning algorithms could accelerate the discovery of novel bimetallic catalysts with optimal properties for specific reactions [10.1038/nchem.626].

4. Multifunctional Catalysts: Future research might explore the development of bimetallic catalysts capable of performing multiple catalytic functions simultaneously, potentially leading to more efficient and compact industrial processes.

5. Sustainable Catalysis: As environmental concerns grow, research could focus on designing bimetallic catalysts that utilize earth-abundant metals while maintaining high activity and selectivity, contributing to more sustainable chemical processes.

These future directions could potentially revolutionize the field of heterogeneous catalysis, leading to more efficient, selective, and environmentally friendly catalytic processes across various industries.

<Chinese>
自20世纪60年代商业引入以来，双金属催化剂在多相催化领域已经发展显著。最初由于其与单金属催化剂相比独特的反应性而在碳氢化合物重整中引起关注[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。随着时间推移，其应用已扩展到各种催化和电催化过程[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。

双金属催化剂的演变特征包括几个关键发展：

1. 独特性质：双金属催化剂表现出与其父金属不同的新型电子和化学性质，提供增强的选择性、活性和稳定性[10.1021/cr300096b_0]。这些性质源于两种金属之间的协同效应，特别是在合金和核壳结构中[10.1039/c1jm12020d]。

2. 可调特性：通过调整双金属纳米粒子的尺寸、组成和原子排列，可以精细调节其化学和物理性质[10.1039/c1jm12020d]。这种可调性允许精确控制催化性能。

3. 先进结构：核壳纳米粒子代表了双金属催化剂的一种先进形式，其物理和化学性质强烈依赖于核、壳和界面的结构[10.1039/c1jm12020d]。

4. 性能改进：双金属催化剂已经展示了显著的性能改进。例如，Pt3Ni(111)表面对氧还原反应的活性比Pt(111)高10倍，比用于聚合物电解质膜燃料电池的最先进Pt/C催化剂高90倍[10.1126/science.1135941]。

双金属催化剂的独特电子和化学性质通过以下几种方式有助于弥合模型表面和工业相关负载催化剂之间的"材料差距"和"压力差距"：

1. 模型表面：通过在单晶基底上沉积亚单层到单层覆盖的外加金属创建的双金属表面允许使用各种化学、电子和物理探针进行详细研究[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。这提供了对其性质的基本洞察。

2. 弥合材料差距：对多晶基底和纳米粒子中等效双金属结构的形成和化学性质进行系统研究，有助于将模型表面的发现转化为更具工业相关性的负载催化剂[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。

3. 解决压力差距：研究重点是在反应条件下调查表面和亚表面双金属结构的稳定性[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。这有助于理解催化剂在更现实条件下的行为。

4. 先进表征技术：使用非原位和原位表面敏感探针，以及密度泛函理论（DFT）计算，允许研究人员在各种条件下研究双金属催化剂，弥合理想模型和实际应用之间的差距[10.1126/science.1135941]。

5. 计算筛选：结合DFT计算和微观动力学建模的计算框架的发展使复杂双金属催化剂的快速评估和筛选成为可能[10.1038/nchem.626]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：双金属催化剂的演变显著提高了我们对多相催化的理解，并为催化剂设计开辟了新的途径。展望未来，几个有前景的方向浮现：

1. 纳米尺度工程：未来研究可能集中于开发精确方法来合成模仿高活性模型表面（如Pt3Ni(111)表面）性质的双金属纳米结构[10.1126/science.1135941]。

2. 原位表征：操作中光谱和显微技术的进步可能提供对双金属催化剂在真实反应条件下动态行为的更深入洞察，进一步弥合压力差距[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。

3. 机器学习整合：将计算筛选和实验结果的大量数据与机器学习算法相结合，可能加速发现具有特定反应最佳性质的新型双金属催化剂[10.1038/nchem.626]。

4. 多功能催化剂：未来研究可能探索开发能够同时执行多种催化功能的双金属催化剂，潜在地导致更高效和紧凑的工业过程。

5. 可持续催化：随着环境关切的增加，研究可能集中于设计利用地球丰富金属的双金属催化剂，同时保持高活性和选择性，为更可持续的化学过程做出贡献。

这些未来方向可能彻底改变多相催化领域，导致各种行业更高效、更具选择性和更环保的催化过程。
