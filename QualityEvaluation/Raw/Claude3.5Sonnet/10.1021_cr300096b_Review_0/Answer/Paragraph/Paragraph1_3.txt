<English>
The evolution of bimetallic catalysts in heterogeneous catalysis since their commercial introduction in the 1960s has been marked by significant advancements in both fundamental understanding and practical applications. These catalysts have gained widespread use, particularly in the petroleum industry, due to their potential for improved selectivity and better activity maintenance compared to single-metal catalysts [10.1002/aic.690310823].

Bimetallic catalysts offer unique electronic and chemical properties that contribute to bridging the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts:

1. Synergistic effects: Bimetallic nanoparticles (NPs) with alloyed and core-shell structures exhibit higher catalytic activities compared to their monometallic counterparts due to synergistic effects between the two metals [10.1039/c1jm12020d].

2. Tunable properties: The chemical and physical properties of alloy NPs can be finely tuned by adjusting their size, composition, and atomic ordering, allowing for precise control over their catalytic performance [10.1039/c1jm12020d].

3. Enhanced binding properties: Alloy materials possess distinct binding properties with reactants compared to monometallic catalysts. The strong metal-metal interactions in bimetallic catalysts modify the bonding between catalyst surfaces and reactants [10.1039/c1jm12020d].

4. Bridging the materials gap: Studies on model bimetallic surfaces provide fundamental insights, while industrial applications use nanoparticles on high-surface-area supports. Research aims to correlate these two forms, bridging the gap between idealized models and practical supported catalysts [10.1021/cr300096b_0].

5. Addressing the pressure gap: Researchers study bimetallic catalysts in both ultrahigh vacuum (UHV) environments and realistic reaction conditions to understand their behavior across different pressure regimes [10.1021/cr300096b_0].

To bridge these gaps, researchers have employed various advanced techniques:

1. Surface characterization: A combination of ex situ and in situ surface-sensitive probes, such as LEED, AES, LEIS, UPS, and SXS, are used to characterize bimetallic surfaces under various conditions [10.1126/science.1135941].

2. Theoretical calculations: Density functional theory (DFT) calculations and microkinetic modeling are used to predict and understand the behavior of bimetallic catalysts [10.1126/science.1135941] [10.1038/nchem.626].

3. Advanced synthesis methods: Techniques like galvanic displacement have been developed to create bimetallic catalysts with specific structures, such as Au clusters on Pt nanoparticles [10.1126/science.1134569].

4. In situ studies: Techniques like in situ x-ray absorption near edge spectroscopy (XANES) are used to study catalyst behavior under reaction conditions [10.1126/science.1134569].

These advancements have led to significant improvements in catalyst performance. For example, the Pt3Ni(111) surface has demonstrated 10-fold higher activity for oxygen reduction reactions compared to Pt(111) and 90-fold higher activity than state-of-the-art Pt/C catalysts used in polymer electrolyte membrane fuel cells (PEMFCs) [10.1126/science.1135941].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of bimetallic catalysts has significantly advanced our understanding of heterogeneous catalysis and has brought us closer to bridging the materials and pressure gaps between model surfaces and industrially relevant catalysts. However, challenges remain in fully translating insights from model surfaces to complex supported catalysts under reaction conditions.

Future research directions may include:

1. Development of more sophisticated in situ and operando characterization techniques to study bimetallic catalysts under realistic reaction conditions.

2. Advanced computational methods that can more accurately predict the behavior of complex bimetallic systems, including effects of support materials and reaction environments.

3. Exploration of novel bimetallic combinations and nanostructures, such as near-surface alloys (NSAs) and core-shell nanoparticles, to further tune catalytic properties.

4. Integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning approaches to accelerate the discovery and optimization of bimetallic catalysts for specific applications.

5. Investigation of the long-term stability and deactivation mechanisms of bimetallic catalysts under industrial conditions to improve their practical applicability.

By continuing to advance our understanding of bimetallic catalysts and developing new tools to study them, we can expect further improvements in catalyst design and performance, leading to more efficient and sustainable chemical processes across various industries.

<Chinese>
自20世纪60年代商业化引入以来，双金属催化剂在多相催化领域的发展已经取得了重大进展，无论是在基础理解还是实际应用方面都有显著提升。这些催化剂因其在选择性和活性维持方面相对于单金属催化剂的潜在优势，已在石油工业等领域得到广泛应用[10.1002/aic.690310823]。

双金属催化剂具有独特的电子和化学性质，有助于弥合模型表面与工业相关负载催化剂之间的"材料差距"和"压力差距"：

1. 协同效应：具有合金和核壳结构的双金属纳米粒子（NPs）由于两种金属之间的协同作用，表现出比单金属对应物更高的催化活性[10.1039/c1jm12020d]。

2. 可调性能：合金NPs的化学和物理性质可以通过调整其尺寸、成分和原子排列进行精细调节，从而精确控制其催化性能[10.1039/c1jm12020d]。

3. 增强的结合性质：合金材料与反应物的结合性质不同于单金属催化剂。双金属催化剂中的强金属-金属相互作用改变了催化剂表面与反应物之间的结合[10.1039/c1jm12020d]。

4. 弥合材料差距：对模型双金属表面的研究提供了基础洞察，而工业应用则使用高比表面积载体上的纳米粒子。研究旨在关联这两种形式，弥合理想化模型和实际负载催化剂之间的差距[10.1021/cr300096b_0]。

5. 解决压力差距：研究人员在超高真空（UHV）环境和真实反应条件下研究双金属催化剂，以了解它们在不同压力范围内的行为[10.1021/cr300096b_0]。

为了弥合这些差距，研究人员采用了各种先进技术：

1. 表面表征：结合使用ex situ和in situ表面敏感探针，如LEED、AES、LEIS、UPS和SXS，在各种条件下表征双金属表面[10.1126/science.1135941]。

2. 理论计算：使用密度泛函理论（DFT）计算和微观动力学建模来预测和理解双金属催化剂的行为[10.1126/science.1135941] [10.1038/nchem.626]。

3. 先进合成方法：开发了诸如电镀置换等技术，以创建具有特定结构的双金属催化剂，例如Pt纳米粒子上的Au簇[10.1126/science.1134569]。

4. 原位研究：使用诸如原位X射线吸收近边结构光谱（XANES）等技术来研究反应条件下催化剂的行为[10.1126/science.1134569]。

这些进展导致了催化剂性能的显著改善。例如，Pt3Ni(111)表面在氧还原反应中表现出比Pt(111)高10倍的活性，比用于聚合物电解质膜燃料电池（PEMFCs）的最先进Pt/C催化剂高90倍的活性[10.1126/science.1135941]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：双金属催化剂的演变显著提高了我们对多相催化的理解，并使我们更接近于弥合模型表面与工业相关催化剂之间的材料和压力差距。然而，在将模型表面的洞察完全转化为反应条件下复杂的负载催化剂方面仍然存在挑战。

未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 开发更复杂的原位和操作中表征技术，以在真实反应条件下研究双金属催化剂。

2. 开发能更准确预测复杂双金属系统行为的先进计算方法，包括载体材料和反应环境的影响。

3. 探索新型双金属组合和纳米结构，如近表面合金（NSAs）和核壳纳米粒子，以进一步调节催化性能。

4. 整合人工智能和机器学习方法，加速特定应用的双金属催化剂的发现和优化。

5. 研究工业条件下双金属催化剂的长期稳定性和失活机制，以提高其实际适用性。

通过继续深化对双金属催化剂的理解并开发新的研究工具，我们可以期待催化剂设计和性能的进一步改进，从而在各种行业中实现更高效、更可持续的化学过程。
