Bimetallic catalysts have undergone significant evolution in heterogeneous catalysis since their commercial introduction in the 1960s for hydrocarbon reforming. Initially, they gained interest due to their unique reactivities compared to monometallic catalysts [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]. Over time, their use has expanded to various catalysis and electrocatalysis applications, offering enhanced selectivity, activity, and stability [10.1021/cr300096b_0].

The unique electronic and chemical properties of bimetallic catalysts contribute to bridging the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts in several ways:

1. Synergistic effects: Bimetallic nanoparticles (NPs) with alloyed and core-shell structures exhibit higher catalytic activities compared to their monometallic counterparts due to synergistic effects between the two metals [10.1039/c1jm12020d].

2. Tunable properties: The chemical and physical properties of alloy NPs can be finely tuned by adjusting their size, composition, and atomic ordering. This allows for precise control over their catalytic performance [10.1039/c1jm12020d].

3. Enhanced binding properties: Alloy materials possess distinct binding properties with reactants compared to monometallic catalysts. The strong metal-metal interactions in bimetallic catalysts modify the bonding between catalyst surfaces and reactants [10.1039/c1jm12020d].

4. Model surfaces: Bimetallic surfaces created by depositing submonolayer to monolayer coverages of admetal on single crystal substrates allow for detailed studies using various chemical, electronic, and physical probes. This provides fundamental insights into their properties [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001].

5. Advanced characterization techniques: Researchers have employed a combination of ex situ and in situ surface-sensitive probes along with DFT calculations to study reactions on bimetallic surfaces. These include LEED, AES, LEIS, UPS, and SXS, which help in understanding surface electronic structure, composition, and reactivity under various conditions [10.1126/science.1135941].

6. Bridging the materials gap: Systematic studies on the formation and chemical properties of equivalent bimetallic structures on polycrystalline substrates and in nanoparticles help translate findings from model surfaces to more industrially relevant supported catalysts [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001].

7. Addressing the pressure gap: The stability of surface and subsurface bimetallic structures is investigated under reaction conditions. While ultra-high vacuum (UHV) studies have shown that surface termination depends on the environment, more research is needed on segregation and diffusion under non-UHV conditions relevant to practical applications [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001].

8. Computational screening: The development of computational frameworks that combine DFT calculations with microkinetic modeling allows for rapid evaluation and screening of complex bimetallic catalysts, bridging fundamental research with practical applications [10.1038/nchem.626].

These advancements have significantly improved our ability to design, predict, and understand the behavior of bimetallic catalysts, bringing model studies closer to practical applications and helping to bridge both the materials and pressure gaps in heterogeneous catalysis research.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of bimetallic catalysts has demonstrated their potential to bridge the gap between fundamental research and industrial applications in heterogeneous catalysis. Looking forward, several key areas emerge for future research and development:

1. Advanced synthesis methods: Further development of techniques like atomic layer deposition and nanoparticle fabrication could lead to more precise control over bimetallic catalyst structures, potentially allowing for the creation of catalysts that mimic highly active model surfaces like Pt3Ni(111) [10.1126/science.1135941].

2. In situ and operando characterization: Continued advancement in characterization techniques that can probe catalyst structure and behavior under realistic reaction conditions will be crucial for fully understanding and optimizing bimetallic catalysts [10.1126/science.1135941].

3. Machine learning and artificial intelligence: Integration of these technologies with existing computational methods could accelerate the discovery and optimization of new bimetallic catalysts, potentially uncovering unexpected combinations with superior properties.

4. Multifunctional catalysts: Exploring bimetallic catalysts that can perform multiple reactions in tandem could lead to more efficient and economical industrial processes.

5. Sustainability focus: Developing bimetallic catalysts that use earth-abundant metals or reduce the use of precious metals while maintaining or improving performance could address resource scarcity concerns.

6. Novel applications: Expanding the use of bimetallic catalysts into emerging fields such as artificial photosynthesis, CO2 reduction, and hydrogen production could contribute to addressing global energy and environmental challenges.

By continuing to bridge the materials and pressure gaps, future research in bimetallic catalysts has the potential to revolutionize industrial catalytic processes, leading to more efficient, sustainable, and economical chemical production.

自从20世纪60年代商业化应用于碳氢化合物重整以来，双金属催化剂在多相催化领域发展显著。它们最初因具有与单金属催化剂不同的独特反应活性而受到关注[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。随着时间推移，它们的应用范围已扩展到各种催化和电催化领域，提供了更高的选择性、活性和稳定性[10.1021/cr300096b_0]。

双金属催化剂独特的电子和化学性质有助于弥合模型表面与工业相关的负载型催化剂之间的"材料差距"和"压力差距"，具体表现在以下几个方面：

1. 协同效应：合金和核壳结构的双金属纳米粒子(NPs)由于两种金属之间的协同效应，表现出比单金属催化剂更高的催化活性[10.1039/c1jm12020d]。

2. 可调性能：合金NPs的化学和物理性质可以通过调整其尺寸、成分和原子排列来精细调节。这允许对其催化性能进行精确控制[10.1039/c1jm12020d]。

3. 增强的结合性质：与单金属催化剂相比，合金材料具有不同的反应物结合性质。双金属催化剂中强烈的金属-金属相互作用改变了催化剂表面与反应物之间的结合[10.1039/c1jm12020d]。

4. 模型表面：通过在单晶基底上沉积亚单层至单层覆盖度的添加金属来创建双金属表面，允许使用各种化学、电子和物理探针进行详细研究。这提供了对其性质的基本洞察[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。

5. 先进表征技术：研究人员结合使用非原位和原位表面敏感探针以及DFT计算来研究双金属表面上的反应。这些技术包括LEED、AES、LEIS、UPS和SXS，有助于了解在各种条件下的表面电子结构、成分和反应活性[10.1126/science.1135941]。

6. 弥合材料差距：对多晶基底和纳米粒子中等效双金属结构的形成和化学性质进行系统研究，有助于将模型表面的发现转化为更具工业相关性的负载型催化剂[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。

7. 解决压力差距：研究反应条件下表面和亚表面双金属结构的稳定性。虽然超高真空(UHV)研究表明表面终止依赖于环境，但需要更多关于非UHV条件下（与实际应用相关）的分离和扩散研究[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。

8. 计算筛选：结合DFT计算和微观动力学建模的计算框架的发展允许对复杂的双金属催化剂进行快速评估和筛选，将基础研究与实际应用联系起来[10.1038/nchem.626]。

这些进展显著提高了我们设计、预测和理解双金属催化剂行为的能力，使模型研究更接近实际应用，并有助于弥合多相催化研究中的材料和压力差距。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：双金属催化剂的演变展示了它们在多相催化领域中连接基础研究和工业应用的潜力。展望未来，几个关键领域为未来的研究和发展提供了方向：

1. 先进合成方法：原子层沉积和纳米粒子制造等技术的进一步发展可能导致对双金属催化剂结构更精确的控制，潜在地允许创建模仿高活性模型表面（如Pt3Ni(111)）的催化剂[10.1126/science.1135941]。

2. 原位和操作中表征：能够在实际反应条件下探测催化剂结构和行为的表征技术的持续进步，对于全面理解和优化双金属催化剂至关重要[10.1126/science.1135941]。

3. 机器学习和人工智能：将这些技术与现有计算方法结合，可能加速新双金属催化剂的发现和优化，潜在地揭示具有卓越性能的意外组合。

4. 多功能催化剂：探索能够串联执行多个反应的双金属催化剂可能导致更高效和经济的工业过程。

5. 可持续性焦点：开发使用地球丰富金属或减少贵金属使用同时保持或改善性能的双金属催化剂，可以解决资源稀缺问题。

6. 新型应用：将双金属催化剂的使用扩展到新兴领域，如人工光合作用、CO2还原和氢气生产，可能有助于解决全球能源和环境挑战。

通过继续弥合材料和压力差距，双金属催化剂的未来研究有潜力彻底改变工业催化过程，导致更高效、可持续和经济的化学品生产。
