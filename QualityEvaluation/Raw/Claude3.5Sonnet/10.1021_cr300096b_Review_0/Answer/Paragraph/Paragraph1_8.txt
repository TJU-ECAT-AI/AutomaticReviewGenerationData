<English>
Bimetallic catalysts have evolved significantly in heterogeneous catalysis since their commercial introduction in the 1960s for hydrocarbon reforming. Initially, they gained interest due to their unique reactivities compared to monometallic catalysts [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]. Over time, their use has expanded to various catalysis and electrocatalysis applications, particularly in the petroleum industry [10.1002/aic.690310823].

The evolution of bimetallic catalysts is characterized by several key developments that contribute to bridging the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts:

1. Unique Properties: Bimetallic catalysts often display novel electronic and chemical properties distinct from their parent metals, offering enhanced selectivity, activity, and stability [10.1021/cr300096b_0]. These properties arise from synergistic effects between the two metals, particularly in alloyed and core-shell nanoparticle structures [10.1039/c1jm12020d].

2. Advanced Characterization Techniques: The development of surface-sensitive probes and in-situ characterization methods has allowed researchers to study bimetallic catalysts under more realistic conditions. Techniques such as EXAFS, LEED, AES, LEIS, and synchrotron-based UPS have been crucial in understanding the structure-property relationships of these catalysts [10.1126/science.1135941].

3. Theoretical Modeling: The integration of density functional theory (DFT) calculations and microkinetic modeling with experimental approaches has become critical for identifying and screening desirable bimetallic systems [10.1038/nchem.626]. This combination of theoretical and experimental methods has greatly enhanced our ability to design and predict catalyst behavior.

4. Bridging the Materials Gap: Research has progressed from studying model single-crystal surfaces to more complex systems like near-surface alloys (NSAs) and supported nanoparticles. This evolution has allowed for a better understanding of how catalyst structure and composition affect performance in real-world applications [10.1038/nmat1223].

5. Addressing the Pressure Gap: Studies now encompass both ultra-high vacuum (UHV) conditions and realistic reaction environments, providing insights into how bimetallic catalysts behave across different pressure regimes [10.1021/cr300096b_0].

6. Tunable Properties: The chemical and physical properties of bimetallic catalysts can be finely tuned by adjusting size, composition, and atomic ordering. This tunability allows for precise control over catalytic performance [10.1039/c1jm12020d].

7. Novel Structures: The development of core-shell nanoparticles and near-surface alloys has opened new avenues for catalyst design, offering unique advantages over traditional monometallic catalysts and alloys [10.1039/c1jm12020d].

These advancements have significantly enhanced our ability to design, predict, and understand the behavior of bimetallic catalysts, bringing model studies closer to practical applications and helping to bridge both the materials and pressure gaps in heterogeneous catalysis research.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of bimetallic catalysts has demonstrated the power of combining advanced characterization techniques, theoretical modeling, and novel synthesis methods to bridge the gap between fundamental research and industrial applications. Looking forward, several promising directions emerge:

1. Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning: The integration of AI and ML techniques with the vast amount of experimental and theoretical data could accelerate the discovery and optimization of bimetallic catalysts for specific applications.

2. In-operando Studies: Further development of in-operando characterization techniques could provide real-time insights into catalyst behavior under realistic operating conditions, helping to better bridge the pressure gap.

3. Atomic-scale Engineering: As synthesis methods become more precise, the ability to control catalyst structure at the atomic level may allow for the creation of bimetallic catalysts with unprecedented selectivity and activity.

4. Multifunctional Catalysts: The principles learned from bimetallic systems could be extended to develop multi-metallic or metal-organic hybrid catalysts capable of performing multiple reactions in a single process.

5. Sustainable Catalysis: Future research may focus on developing bimetallic catalysts for green chemistry applications, such as CO2 conversion or water splitting, leveraging the unique properties of these systems for environmental benefit.

These future directions could further narrow the gaps between model systems and practical catalysts, leading to more efficient and sustainable chemical processes across various industries.

双金属催化剂自20世纪60年代首次在烃类重整中商业化应用以来，在多相催化领域发展显著。它们最初因与单金属催化剂相比具有独特的反应活性而引起关注[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。随着时间的推移，它们的应用已扩展到各种催化和电催化应用中，尤其是在石油工业中[10.1002/aic.690310823]。

双金属催化剂的演变特点是几个关键发展，这些发展有助于弥合模型表面和工业相关支撑催化剂之间的"材料差距"和"压力差距"：

1. 独特性质：双金属催化剂通常显示出与其父金属不同的新型电子和化学性质，提供增强的选择性、活性和稳定性[10.1021/cr300096b_0]。这些性质源于两种金属之间的协同效应，特别是在合金和核壳纳米粒子结构中[10.1039/c1jm12020d]。

2. 先进表征技术：表面敏感探针和原位表征方法的发展使研究人员能够在更接近实际的条件下研究双金属催化剂。诸如EXAFS、LEED、AES、LEIS和同步辐射UPS等技术对理解这些催化剂的结构-性能关系至关重要[10.1126/science.1135941]。

3. 理论建模：密度泛函理论（DFT）计算和微动力学建模与实验方法的结合对于识别和筛选理想的双金属系统变得至关重要[10.1038/nchem.626]。这种理论和实验方法的结合大大增强了我们设计和预测催化剂行为的能力。

4. 弥合材料差距：研究已从研究模型单晶表面发展到更复杂的系统，如近表面合金（NSAs）和支撑纳米颗粒。这种演变使我们能更好地理解催化剂结构和成分如何影响实际应用中的性能[10.1038/nmat1223]。

5. 解决压力差距：研究现在涵盖了超高真空（UHV）条件和真实反应环境，提供了双金属催化剂在不同压力范围内行为的见解[10.1021/cr300096b_0]。

6. 可调性质：双金属催化剂的化学和物理性质可以通过调整大小、成分和原子排列来精细调节。这种可调性允许对催化性能进行精确控制[10.1039/c1jm12020d]。

7. 新型结构：核壳纳米粒子和近表面合金的发展为催化剂设计开辟了新途径，相比传统的单金属催化剂和合金提供了独特的优势[10.1039/c1jm12020d]。

这些进展显著增强了我们设计、预测和理解双金属催化剂行为的能力，使模型研究更接近实际应用，并有助于弥合多相催化研究中的材料和压力差距。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：双金属催化剂的演变展示了将先进表征技术、理论建模和新型合成方法相结合以弥合基础研究和工业应用之间差距的力量。展望未来，几个有前景的方向浮现：

1. 人工智能和机器学习：将AI和ML技术与大量实验和理论数据相结合，可能加速特定应用的双金属催化剂的发现和优化。

2. 原位研究：进一步发展原位表征技术可以提供催化剂在真实操作条件下行为的实时洞察，有助于更好地弥合压力差距。

3. 原子尺度工程：随着合成方法变得更加精确，在原子水平上控制催化剂结构的能力可能允许创建具有前所未有的选择性和活性的双金属催化剂。

4. 多功能催化剂：从双金属系统中学到的原理可以扩展到开发能够在单一过程中执行多个反应的多金属或金属-有机杂化催化剂。

5. 可持续催化：未来研究可能集中于开发用于绿色化学应用的双金属催化剂，如CO2转化或水分解，利用这些系统的独特性质为环境带来益处。

这些未来方向可能进一步缩小模型系统和实际催化剂之间的差距，导致各种行业中更高效和可持续的化学过程。
