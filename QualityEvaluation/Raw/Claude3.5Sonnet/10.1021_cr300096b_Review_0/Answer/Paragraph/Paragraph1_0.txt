<English>
Bimetallic catalysts have evolved significantly in heterogeneous catalysis since their commercial introduction in the 1960s for hydrocarbon reforming. Initially, they gained interest due to their unique reactivities compared to monometallic catalysts [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]. Over time, their use has expanded to various catalysis and electrocatalysis applications, particularly in the petroleum industry [10.1002/aic.690310823].

The evolution of bimetallic catalysts is characterized by:

1. Unique properties: Bimetallic catalysts display novel electronic and chemical properties distinct from their parent metals, offering enhanced selectivity, activity, and stability [10.1021/cr300096b_0]. These properties are often attributed to synergistic effects between the two metals [10.1039/c1jm12020d].

2. Structural diversity: Bimetallic catalysts have evolved from simple alloys to more complex structures, including core-shell nanoparticles and near-surface alloys (NSAs) [10.1038/nmat1223]. These advanced structures allow for precise tuning of catalytic properties.

3. Advanced characterization techniques: The development of sophisticated surface-sensitive probes and in-situ characterization methods has enabled researchers to gain deeper insights into the structure and behavior of bimetallic catalysts under realistic conditions [10.1126/science.1135941].

4. Theoretical modeling: The integration of density functional theory (DFT) calculations and microkinetic modeling has become crucial for identifying and screening desirable bimetallic systems [10.1038/nchem.626].

The unique electronic and chemical properties of bimetallic catalysts contribute to bridging the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts in several ways:

1. Tunable properties: The chemical and physical properties of bimetallic catalysts can be finely tuned by adjusting their size, composition, and atomic ordering [10.1039/c1jm12020d]. This allows researchers to create catalysts that more closely mimic industrially relevant systems while maintaining the well-defined nature of model surfaces.

2. Bridging the materials gap: Studies on model bimetallic surfaces provide fundamental insights, while industrial applications use nanoparticles on high-surface-area supports. Research aims to correlate these two forms, bridging the gap between idealized models and practical supported catalysts [10.1021/cr300096b_0].

3. Addressing the pressure gap: Researchers study bimetallic catalysts in both ultrahigh vacuum (UHV) environments and realistic reaction conditions to understand their behavior across different pressure regimes [10.1021/cr300096b_0]. The stability of surface and subsurface structures under reaction conditions is crucial for bridging this gap [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001].

4. Enhanced performance: Bimetallic catalysts often demonstrate improved activity, selectivity, and stability compared to monometallic catalysts. For example, the Pt3Ni(111) surface has shown 90-fold higher activity for oxygen reduction reactions than state-of-the-art Pt/C catalysts [10.1126/science.1135941].

5. Practical applications: The development of bimetallic catalysts has led to improvements in industrially relevant processes, such as hydrocarbon reforming and fuel cell electrocatalysts [10.1126/science.1134569].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of bimetallic catalysts has significantly advanced our understanding of heterogeneous catalysis and brought us closer to bridging the gaps between model surfaces and industrially relevant catalysts. However, challenges remain in fully translating insights from model systems to complex supported catalysts under reaction conditions.

Future research efforts may focus on:

1. Development of more sophisticated in-situ and operando characterization techniques to study bimetallic catalysts under realistic reaction conditions.

2. Advanced computational methods that can more accurately predict the behavior of complex bimetallic systems, including the effects of support materials and reaction environments.

3. Novel synthesis methods for creating bimetallic catalysts with precisely controlled structures and compositions at the nanoscale.

4. Exploration of new bimetallic combinations and structures, such as multi-metallic catalysts or catalysts with engineered interfaces between metal and support.

5. Integration of machine learning and high-throughput screening techniques to accelerate the discovery and optimization of bimetallic catalysts for specific applications.

These advancements could lead to the development of more efficient and sustainable catalytic processes, with potential applications in energy conversion, environmental remediation, and fine chemical synthesis.

<Chinese>
自1960年代用于烃类重整的商业应用以来，双金属催化剂在多相催化领域已经发展了很长时间。最初，它们因与单金属催化剂相比具有独特的反应活性而受到关注[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。随着时间的推移，它们的应用已扩展到各种催化和电催化应用，尤其是在石油工业中[10.1002/aic.690310823]。

双金属催化剂的演变特点包括：

1. 独特性质：双金属催化剂显示出与其母体金属不同的新颖电子和化学性质，提供了增强的选择性、活性和稳定性[10.1021/cr300096b_0]。这些性质通常归因于两种金属之间的协同效应[10.1039/c1jm12020d]。

2. 结构多样性：双金属催化剂已从简单的合金发展到更复杂的结构，包括核壳纳米粒子和近表面合金（NSAs）[10.1038/nmat1223]。这些先进结构允许精确调节催化性质。

3. 先进表征技术：复杂的表面敏感探针和原位表征方法的发展使研究人员能够在实际条件下深入了解双金属催化剂的结构和行为[10.1126/science.1135941]。

4. 理论建模：密度泛函理论（DFT）计算和微观动力学建模的整合对于识别和筛选理想的双金属系统变得至关重要[10.1038/nchem.626]。

双金属催化剂独特的电子和化学性质有助于弥合模型表面与工业相关支持催化剂之间的"材料差距"和"压力差距"，具体方式如下：

1. 可调性质：通过调整双金属催化剂的尺寸、成分和原子排列，可以精细调节其化学和物理性质[10.1039/c1jm12020d]。这使研究人员能够创造出更接近工业相关系统的催化剂，同时保持模型表面的明确定义性质。

2. 弥合材料差距：对模型双金属表面的研究提供了基础洞察，而工业应用则使用高比表面积支持物上的纳米粒子。研究旨在关联这两种形式，弥合理想化模型和实际支持催化剂之间的差距[10.1021/cr300096b_0]。

3. 解决压力差距：研究人员在超高真空（UHV）环境和实际反应条件下研究双金属催化剂，以了解它们在不同压力范围内的行为[10.1021/cr300096b_0]。表面和亚表面结构在反应条件下的稳定性对于弥合这一差距至关重要[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。

4. 性能提升：与单金属催化剂相比，双金属催化剂通常表现出更好的活性、选择性和稳定性。例如，Pt3Ni(111)表面对氧还原反应的活性比最先进的Pt/C催化剂高90倍[10.1126/science.1135941]。

5. 实际应用：双金属催化剂的发展导致了工业相关过程的改进，如烃类重整和燃料电池电催化剂[10.1126/science.1134569]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：双金属催化剂的演变显著提高了我们对多相催化的理解，并使我们更接近于弥合模型表面和工业相关催化剂之间的差距。然而，在将模型系统的见解完全转化为复杂的支持催化剂在反应条件下的行为方面仍然存在挑战。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 开发更复杂的原位和操作条件表征技术，以在实际反应条件下研究双金属催化剂。

2. 先进的计算方法，可以更准确地预测复杂双金属系统的行为，包括支持材料和反应环境的影响。

3. 用于创造纳米尺度上精确控制结构和成分的双金属催化剂的新合成方法。

4. 探索新的双金属组合和结构，如多金属催化剂或具有工程设计的金属和支持物之间界面的催化剂。

5. 整合机器学习和高通量筛选技术，以加速特定应用的双金属催化剂的发现和优化。

这些进展可能导致更高效和可持续的催化过程的开发，潜在应用于能源转换、环境修复和精细化学品合成。
