<English>
Bimetallic catalysts have evolved significantly in heterogeneous catalysis since their commercial introduction in the 1960s. Initially gaining interest for hydrocarbon reforming due to their unique reactivities compared to monometallic catalysts, bimetallic catalysts have since found wide applications in various catalytic and electrocatalytic processes [10.1021/cr300096b_0]. Their evolution and unique properties have contributed significantly to bridging the "materials gap" and "pressure gap" between model surfaces and industrially relevant supported catalysts.

The unique electronic and chemical properties of bimetallic catalysts that contribute to bridging these gaps include:

1. Synergistic effects: Bimetallic nanoparticles (NPs) with alloyed and core-shell structures exhibit higher catalytic activities compared to their monometallic counterparts due to synergistic effects between the two metals [10.1039/c1jm12020d].

2. Tunable properties: The chemical and physical properties of alloy NPs can be finely tuned by adjusting their size, composition, and atomic ordering, allowing for precise control over catalytic performance [10.1039/c1jm12020d].

3. Enhanced binding properties: Alloy materials possess distinct binding properties with reactants compared to monometallic catalysts. The strong metal-metal interactions in bimetallic catalysts modify the bonding between catalyst surfaces and reactants [10.1039/c1jm12020d].

4. Stabilization of transition states: Bimetallic catalysts offer extra stabilization of transition states compared to monometallic catalysts, leading to improved catalytic performance [10.1039/c1jm12020d].

5. Core-shell structures: Core-shell nanoparticles represent an advanced form of bimetallic catalysts, with physical and chemical properties strongly dependent on the structure of the core, shell, and interface [10.1039/c1jm12020d].

To bridge the materials gap, researchers have developed monolayer bimetallic catalysts, which can represent segregated alloy surfaces or model core-shell nanoparticles. This allows for more accurate modeling of real catalyst systems and the study of more complex and realistic catalyst structures [10.1038/nchem.626]. Additionally, systematic studies of the formation and chemical properties of equivalent bimetallic structures on polycrystalline substrates and in bimetallic nanoparticles are being conducted [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001].

To address the pressure gap, researchers are investigating the stability of surface and subsurface bimetallic structures under reaction conditions. While ultra-high vacuum (UHV) studies have shown that surface termination depends on the environment, more research is needed on segregation and diffusion under non-UHV conditions relevant to practical applications [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001].

Advanced characterization techniques and theoretical calculations have played a crucial role in bridging these gaps. Researchers have employed a combination of ex situ and in situ surface-sensitive probes, along with density functional theory (DFT) calculations, to study reactions on bimetallic surfaces [10.1126/science.1135941]. These methods allow for the identification of surface properties governing reactivity variations and determination of how surface structures, segregation, and inter-metallic bonding affect reaction kinetics.

The integration of theoretical and experimental approaches has become crucial for identifying and screening desirable bimetallic systems, especially when surface properties are not a linear function of the parent metals [10.1038/nchem.626]. Advanced computational screening frameworks combining DFT calculations with microkinetic modeling allow for rapid evaluation of complex bimetallic catalysts, further bridging fundamental research with practical applications [10.1038/nchem.626].

These advancements have led to significant improvements in catalyst performance. For example, the Pt3Ni(111) surface has demonstrated 10-fold higher activity for oxygen reduction reactions compared to Pt(111) and 90-fold higher activity than state-of-the-art Pt/C catalysts used in polymer electrolyte membrane fuel cells [10.1126/science.1135941]. The challenge now is to create nanocatalysts that mimic the properties of these highly active model surfaces, further bridging the gap between fundamental research and industrial applications.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of bimetallic catalysts has demonstrated their potential to bridge the materials and pressure gaps in heterogeneous catalysis. Future research directions may focus on:

1. Developing more sophisticated synthesis methods to create bimetallic nanostructures that closely mimic the properties of highly active model surfaces.

2. Expanding in situ and operando characterization techniques to study bimetallic catalysts under realistic reaction conditions, providing deeper insights into their behavior in industrial settings.

3. Advancing computational models to more accurately predict the properties and performance of complex bimetallic systems, enabling more efficient catalyst design.

4. Exploring new combinations of metals and novel nanostructures to further enhance catalytic performance and stability.

5. Investigating the long-term stability and deactivation mechanisms of bimetallic catalysts under industrial conditions to improve their practical applicability.

These efforts will continue to narrow the gap between fundamental research and industrial applications, potentially leading to breakthroughs in catalyst design for various processes, including energy conversion, environmental remediation, and chemical synthesis.

<Chinese>
自20世纪60年代商业引入以来，双金属催化剂在多相催化领域取得了显著的发展。最初因其在碳氢化合物重整中表现出与单金属催化剂不同的独特反应性而引起关注，双金属催化剂现已在各种催化和电催化过程中得到广泛应用[10.1021/cr300096b_0]。它们的演变和独特性质对于弥合模型表面与工业相关的负载型催化剂之间的"材料差距"和"压力差距"做出了重大贡献。

双金属催化剂的独特电子和化学性质对弥合这些差距的贡献包括：

1. 协同效应：具有合金和核壳结构的双金属纳米粒子（NPs）由于两种金属之间的协同作用，表现出比单金属对应物更高的催化活性[10.1039/c1jm12020d]。

2. 可调性质：合金NPs的化学和物理性质可以通过调整其尺寸、成分和原子排列来精细调节，从而精确控制催化性能[10.1039/c1jm12020d]。

3. 增强的结合性质：与单金属催化剂相比，合金材料具有独特的与反应物的结合性质。双金属催化剂中强烈的金属-金属相互作用改变了催化剂表面与反应物之间的结合[10.1039/c1jm12020d]。

4. 过渡态稳定化：与单金属催化剂相比，双金属催化剂提供了额外的过渡态稳定化，从而提高了催化性能[10.1039/c1jm12020d]。

5. 核壳结构：核壳纳米粒子代表了双金属催化剂的一种先进形式，其物理和化学性质强烈依赖于核、壳和界面的结构[10.1039/c1jm12020d]。

为了弥合材料差距，研究人员开发了单层双金属催化剂，可以代表分离的合金表面或模拟核壳纳米粒子。这允许更准确地模拟真实催化剂系统，并研究更复杂和真实的催化剂结构[10.1038/nchem.626]。此外，还在进行多晶基底和双金属纳米粒子中等效双金属结构的形成和化学性质的系统研究[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。

为了解决压力差距问题，研究人员正在调查双金属结构的表面和亚表面在反应条件下的稳定性。虽然超高真空（UHV）研究表明表面终止取决于环境，但需要更多关于在与实际应用相关的非UHV条件下的分离和扩散的研究[10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001]。

先进的表征技术和理论计算在弥合这些差距方面发挥了关键作用。研究人员采用了原位和非原位表面敏感探针的组合，以及密度泛函理论（DFT）计算，来研究双金属表面上的反应[10.1126/science.1135941]。这些方法允许识别控制反应性变化的表面性质，并确定表面结构、分离和金属间键合如何影响反应动力学。

理论和实验方法的整合对于识别和筛选理想的双金属系统变得至关重要，特别是当表面性质不是母体金属的线性函数时[10.1038/nchem.626]。结合DFT计算和微观动力学建模的高级计算筛选框架允许快速评估复杂的双金属催化剂，进一步将基础研究与实际应用联系起来[10.1038/nchem.626]。

这些进展导致了催化剂性能的显著提高。例如，Pt3Ni(111)表面表现出比Pt(111)高10倍的氧还原反应活性，比用于聚合物电解质膜燃料电池的最先进Pt/C催化剂高90倍[10.1126/science.1135941]。现在的挑战是创造模仿这些高活性模型表面性质的纳米催化剂，进一步缩小基础研究和工业应用之间的差距。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：双金属催化剂的发展表明了它们在弥合多相催化中的材料和压力差距方面的潜力。未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 开发更复杂的合成方法，创造出closely模仿高活性模型表面性质的双金属纳米结构。

2. 扩展原位和操作中表征技术，以在真实反应条件下研究双金属催化剂，提供对其在工业环境中行为的更深入见解。

3. 改进计算模型，以更准确地预测复杂双金属系统的性质和性能，实现更高效的催化剂设计。

4. 探索新的金属组合和新颖的纳米结构，进一步提高催化性能和稳定性。

5. 研究双金属催化剂在工业条件下的长期稳定性和失活机制，以提高其实际适用性。

这些努力将继续缩小基础研究和工业应用之间的差距，可能导致各种过程（包括能源转换、环境修复和化学合成）的催化剂设计取得突破性进展。
