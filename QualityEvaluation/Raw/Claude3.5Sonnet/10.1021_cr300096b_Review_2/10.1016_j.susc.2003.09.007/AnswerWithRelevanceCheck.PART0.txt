      <English>
          The characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces, synthesized by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates, is conducted through a comprehensive set of surface science techniques and computational methods:

1. Physical vapor deposition is used to prepare the surfaces under different conditions (300K and 600K, with and without annealing).

2. Temperature Programmed Desorption (TPD) of H2 is used to study chemical properties and reactivity.

3. Scanning Tunneling Microscopy (STM) is employed to investigate surface structure and morphology.

4. Low Energy Ion Scattering Spectroscopy (LEIS) is used for surface composition analysis.

5. X-ray Photoelectron Spectroscopy (XPS), particularly grazing emission XPS, is utilized to analyze the near-surface composition and chemical states.

6. Auger Electron Spectroscopy (AES) is used to monitor surface composition.

7. Density Functional Theory (DFT) calculations are performed to explore the effects of Ni overlayers, subsurface Ni, and surface/bulk alloying on metal-hydrogen binding energy and electronic structure.

This multi-technique approach is crucial for several reasons:

1. It provides a comprehensive understanding of the structural, compositional, and electronic properties of the bimetallic surfaces.

2. The combination of experimental techniques with computational modeling allows researchers to correlate surface structure and composition with chemical reactivity and electronic properties.

3. It enables accurate structural modeling by providing detailed information on surface morphology, composition, and atomic arrangements.

4. It guides and validates DFT calculations, ensuring that theoretical models accurately represent the real bimetallic surfaces.

5. It allows researchers to understand how the presence of subsurface Ni atoms modifies the electronic structure of surface Pt atoms, leading to changes in chemical bonding and catalytic properties.

By integrating these various techniques and computational methods, researchers can develop a detailed understanding of the relationship between surface structure, composition, and reactivity. This comprehensive approach is essential for advancing surface science and catalysis research, enabling the design of more efficient catalysts and providing fundamental insights into the behavior of bimetallic surfaces at the atomic and molecular level.

          通过在Pt(111)单晶或多晶Pt基底上沉积Ni单层合成的Ni/Pt双金属表面的表征是通过一套综合的表面科学技术和计算方法进行的：

1. 使用物理气相沉积法在不同条件下（300K和600K，有无退火）制备表面。

2. 使用H2的程序升温脱附（TPD）研究化学性质和反应活性。

3. 采用扫描隧道显微镜（STM）研究表面结构和形貌。

4. 使用低能离子散射谱（LEIS）进行表面成分分析。

5. 利用X射线光电子能谱（XPS），特别是掠射发射XPS，分析近表面组成和化学状态。

6. 使用俄歇电子能谱（AES）监测表面组成。

7. 进行密度泛函理论（DFT）计算，探索Ni覆盖层、亚表面Ni以及表面/体相合金化对金属-氢结合能和电子结构的影响。

这种多技术方法至关重要，原因如下：

1. 它提供了对双金属表面的结构、组成和电子性质的全面理解。

2. 实验技术与计算建模的结合使研究人员能够将表面结构和组成与化学反应性和电子性质相关联。

3. 它通过提供表面形态、组成和原子排列的详细信息，实现了准确的结构建模。

4. 它指导和验证DFT计算，确保理论模型准确地表示实际的双金属表面。

5. 它使研究人员能够理解亚表面Ni原子的存在如何修改表面Pt原子的电子结构，从而导致化学键合和催化性质的变化。

通过整合这些不同的技术和计算方法，研究人员可以详细了解表面结构、组成和反应性之间的关系。这种综合方法对于推进表面科学和催化研究至关重要，能够设计更高效的催化剂，并提供双金属表面在原子和分子水平上行为的基本见解。
