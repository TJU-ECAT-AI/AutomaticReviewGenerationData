The characterization process typically involves the following steps:

   c) X-ray Photoelectron Spectroscopy (XPS) and Auger Electron Spectroscopy (AES) are used for elemental analysis and quantification of surface composition [10.1021/cr300096b\_2].

4. Composition Analysis: The surface and near-surface compositions are monitored using:
   a) Low-Energy Ion Scattering (LEIS) spectroscopy, which is highly sensitive to the topmost layer composition [10.1016/j.surfrep.2008.02.001].

5. Electronic Structure Analysis: The electronic properties of the bimetallic surfaces are investigated using:
   a) Density Functional Theory (DFT) calculations to explore the effects of Ni positioning on the electronic structure of surface Pt atoms [10.1016/j.susc.2003.09.007].
