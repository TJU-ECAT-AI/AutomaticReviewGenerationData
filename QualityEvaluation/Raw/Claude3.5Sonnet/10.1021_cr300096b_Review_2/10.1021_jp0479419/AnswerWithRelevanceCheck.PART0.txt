      <English>
          The characterization of Ni/Pt bimetallic surfaces, synthesized by depositing a Ni monolayer on Pt(111) single crystal or polycrystalline Pt substrates, is conducted through a combination of experimental techniques and theoretical calculations. The process includes:

1. Deposition: Ni is deposited as a monolayer on Pt(111) substrates at different temperatures (e.g., 300K and 600K).

2. Surface Analysis Techniques:
   a) Temperature Programmed Desorption (TPD): Used to study hydrogen desorption behavior, providing insights into surface reactivity and electronic properties.
   b) Auger Electron Spectroscopy (AES): Helps determine surface composition, monitor Ni diffusion into subsurface regions, and estimate coverage. It also ensures surface cleanliness (less than 1% impurities).
   c) Low Energy Electron Diffraction (LEED): Used to examine surface structure and ordering.
   d) Scanning Tunneling Microscopy (STM): Provides atomic-scale imaging of the surface structure.

3. Temperature-dependent studies: Annealing experiments are conducted up to 1100K to study diffusion and alloying behavior.

4. Theoretical Modeling: Density Functional Theory (DFT) calculations are employed to understand electronic properties, such as d-band center shifts and binding energies of adsorbates like hydrogen and CO.

This characterization process is crucial because:

1. It reveals the formation of alloy structures with Pt-enriched surfaces and Ni-enriched subsurface layers.
2. It demonstrates the temperature-dependent behavior of Ni diffusion into Pt subsurface regions, which is critical for inducing low-temperature activity.
3. It helps understand the electronic modifications caused by subsurface Ni, which affect the surface Pt d-band and adsorption properties.
4. It allows for comparison with other bimetallic systems (e.g., Co/Pt), providing a broader understanding of bimetallic surface properties.
5. It provides essential data for structural modeling and DFT calculations, enabling a comprehensive understanding of the electronic and chemical properties of these surfaces.
6. It establishes structure-property relationships, crucial for advancing surface science and catalysis research.
7. The insights gained are fundamental to designing improved catalytic materials with tailored properties.

This thorough characterization is essential for structural modeling, DFT calculations, and comprehensive understanding of the electronic and chemical properties of these bimetallic surfaces in the context of advanced surface science and catalysis research.

      <Chinese>
          通过在Pt(111)单晶或多晶Pt基底上沉积Ni单层合成的Ni/Pt双金属表面的表征是通过实验技术和理论计算的结合来进行的。该过程包括：

1. 沉积：在不同温度（如300K和600K）下在Pt(111)基底上沉积Ni单层。

2. 表面分析技术：
   a) 程序升温脱附（TPD）：用于研究氢气脱附行为，提供关于表面反应性和电子性质的见解。
   b) 俄歇电子能谱（AES）：帮助确定表面成分，监测Ni向亚表面区域的扩散，并估算覆盖度。它还确保表面清洁度（杂质少于1%）。
   c) 低能电子衍射（LEED）：用于检查表面结构和有序性。
   d) 扫描隧道显微镜（STM）：提供表面结构的原子尺度成像。

3. 温度依赖性研究：进行高达1100K的退火实验，以研究扩散和合金化行为。

4. 理论建模：使用密度泛函理论（DFT）计算来理解电子性质，如d带中心移动和氢气、CO等吸附质的结合能。

这个表征过程非常重要，因为：

1. 它揭示了形成Pt富集表面和Ni富集亚表面层的合金结构。
2. 它展示了Ni扩散到Pt亚表面区域的温度依赖行为，这对诱导低温活性至关重要。
3. 它有助于理解亚表面Ni引起的电子改性，这些改性影响表面Pt的d带和吸附性质。
4. 它允许与其他双金属系统（如Co/Pt）进行比较，提供对双金属表面性质更广泛的理解。
5. 它为结构建模和DFT计算提供了必要的数据，使得全面理解这些表面的电子和化学性质成为可能。
6. 它建立了结构-性质关系，这对推进表面科学和催化研究至关重要。
7. 获得的见解对于设计具有定制性质的改进催化材料至关重要。

这种彻底的表征对于结构建模、DFT计算以及全面理解这些双金属表面在先进表面科学和催化研究背景下的电子和化学性质至关重要。
