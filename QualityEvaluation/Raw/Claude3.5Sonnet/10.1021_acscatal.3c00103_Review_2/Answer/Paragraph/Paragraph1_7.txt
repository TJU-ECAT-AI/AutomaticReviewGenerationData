<English>
Recent advancements in strategies for enhancing catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes have focused on addressing key challenges in catalyst design, fabrication, and performance optimization. These strategies aim to improve catalyst longevity, selectivity, and efficiency for industrial applications.

1. Bimetallic and Multicomponent Catalysts:
Alloying platinum with other metals such as Sn, Ga, In, Cu, and Zn has proven effective in enhancing catalytic stability and selectivity [10.1039/C8SC00802G]. For instance, the PtGa/Al2O3 catalyst demonstrates high resistance to deactivation and coking [10.1002/anie.201404460]. The combination of metals creates synergistic effects, modifying both geometric and electronic properties of the catalyst surface.

2. Single Atom Alloys (SAA) and Atomic Dispersion:
Developing single atom alloy catalysts, such as Pt/Cu SAA, has shown promise in breaking the traditional PtM alloy scaling relationship. These catalysts exhibit improved propylene selectivity while maintaining good dehydrogenation activity [10.1038/s41467-018-06967-8]. The atomic dispersion of Pt on Cu nanoparticles enhances propylene desorption and inhibits further dehydrogenation.

3. Support Material Optimization:
Novel support materials and modifications have been explored to enhance catalyst stability. For example, using siliceous MFI zeolites with hierarchical micro-/mesoporous structures as a replacement for alumina supports has shown improved performance [10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]. Additionally, ceria (CeO2) supports have gained attention for their ability to trap Pt in an atomically dispersed state [10.1039/D0CS00983K].

4. Promoter Addition and Surface Modification:
The addition of promoters like alkali metals (Li, Na, K) has been shown to poison acid sites on supports, suppressing coke formation [10.1021/cr5002436]. Furthermore, the incorporation of rare-earth elements like La and Y has improved catalyst dispersion and stability through strong metal-support interactions [10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4].

5. Controlled Synthesis Methods:
Advanced synthesis strategies, including Atomic Layer Deposition (ALD) and Surface Organometallic Chemistry (SOMC), have been employed to finely control the structure of active sites [10.1039/D0CS01260B]. These methods allow for precise control over catalyst composition and properties, leading to improved stability and performance.

6. Optimizing Reaction Conditions:
Strategies such as co-feeding steam, hydrogen, or CO2 have been shown to reduce coke formation [10.1021/cr5002436]. Additionally, operating at lower temperatures and optimizing residence times can significantly impact catalyst stability and performance [10.1016/j.jcat.2013.11.017].

7. Novel Regeneration Techniques:
Improved regeneration methods, such as oxychlorination treatment for PtSn/Al2O3 catalysts, have been developed to effectively restore catalyst activity after deactivation [10.3390/catal9050446].

These advancements collectively address the major challenges in catalyst design for direct dehydrogenation processes, including coking resistance, sintering prevention, and maintaining high activity and selectivity over extended periods. By combining multiple strategies, researchers have developed catalysts with significantly improved stability and performance, bringing us closer to more efficient and economical industrial dehydrogenation processes.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:

The recent advancements in catalyst design for direct dehydrogenation processes reflect a trend towards more sophisticated, multifunctional catalytic systems. The integration of various strategies, from atomic-level engineering to macroscopic reactor design, demonstrates the complexity and interdisciplinary nature of modern catalyst development.

Looking forward, several promising directions emerge:

1. Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning: These tools could be leveraged to predict optimal catalyst compositions and structures, accelerating the discovery of novel, high-performance catalysts.

2. In-situ and Operando Characterization: Further development of advanced characterization techniques will provide deeper insights into catalyst behavior under reaction conditions, enabling more targeted design approaches.

3. Sustainable Catalyst Design: Future research may focus on developing catalysts using more abundant, less expensive materials while maintaining or improving performance, addressing economic and environmental concerns.

4. Process Intensification: Integration of membrane technology or microreactor designs with advanced catalysts could lead to more efficient, compact dehydrogenation processes.

5. Multifunctional Catalysts: Development of catalysts capable of performing multiple reactions in one step, such as combined dehydrogenation and selective oxidation, could open new pathways for process optimization.

6. Catalyst Regeneration: Innovations in rapid, efficient regeneration techniques could significantly extend catalyst lifetime and reduce process downtime.

As we move forward, the challenge will be to translate these laboratory-scale advancements into industrially viable solutions, balancing performance, cost, and scalability. The future of catalyst design for direct dehydrogenation processes lies in the synergistic integration of these advanced strategies, potentially revolutionizing the efficiency and sustainability of industrial chemical processes.

<Chinese>
在直接脱氢过程中，支持金属催化剂催化稳定性的增强策略的最新进展主要集中在解决催化剂设计、制造和性能优化方面的关键挑战。这些策略旨在提高催化剂的寿命、选择性和效率，以适应工业应用。

1. 双金属和多组分催化剂：
将铂与其他金属如Sn、Ga、In、Cu和Zn合金化已证明能有效提高催化稳定性和选择性[10.1039/C8SC00802G]。例如，PtGa/Al2O3催化剂表现出对失活和结焦的高度抵抗力[10.1002/anie.201404460]。金属组合产生协同效应，同时修改了催化剂表面的几何和电子性质。

2. 单原子合金（SAA）和原子分散：
开发单原子合金催化剂，如Pt/Cu SAA，在打破传统PtM合金定标关系方面显示出了前景。这些催化剂表现出改善的丙烯选择性，同时保持良好的脱氢活性[10.1038/s41467-018-06967-8]。Pt在Cu纳米粒子上的原子分散增强了丙烯的脱附并抑制了进一步的脱氢。

3. 载体材料优化：
研究人员探索了新型载体材料和修饰方法来增强催化剂稳定性。例如，使用具有分级微/介孔结构的硅质MFI沸石代替氧化铝载体显示出改善的性能[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]。此外，氧化铈（CeO2）载体因其能够将Pt捕获在原子分散状态而受到关注[10.1039/D0CS00983K]。

4. 添加促进剂和表面修饰：
添加如碱金属（Li、Na、K）等促进剂已被证明能毒化载体上的酸性位点，从而抑制结焦形成[10.1021/cr5002436]。此外，掺入稀土元素如La和Y通过强金属-载体相互作用改善了催化剂的分散性和稳定性[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]。

5. 可控合成方法：
采用了先进的合成策略，包括原子层沉积（ALD）和表面有机金属化学（SOMC），以精细控制活性位点的结构[10.1039/D0CS01260B]。这些方法允许精确控制催化剂的组成和性质，从而提高稳定性和性能。

6. 优化反应条件：
共进料蒸汽、氢气或CO2等策略已被证明能减少结焦形成[10.1021/cr5002436]。此外，在较低温度下操作和优化停留时间可以显著影响催化剂的稳定性和性能[10.1016/j.jcat.2013.11.017]。

7. 新型再生技术：
开发了改进的再生方法，如对PtSn/Al2O3催化剂的氧氯化处理，能有效恢复失活后的催化剂活性[10.3390/catal9050446]。

这些进展共同解决了直接脱氢过程催化剂设计中的主要挑战，包括抗结焦、防止烧结以及长期保持高活性和选择性。通过结合多种策略，研究人员开发出了稳定性和性能显著提高的催化剂，使我们更接近更高效和经济的工业脱氢过程。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：

直接脱氢过程催化剂设计的最新进展反映了向更复杂、多功能催化系统发展的趋势。从原子级工程到宏观反应器设计，各种策略的整合展示了现代催化剂开发的复杂性和跨学科性质。

展望未来，几个有前景的方向浮现出来：

1. 人工智能和机器学习：这些工具可以用来预测最佳催化剂组成和结构，加速发现新型高性能催化剂。

2. 原位和操作表征：进一步发展先进表征技术将提供对反应条件下催化剂行为的更深入了解，使更有针对性的设计方法成为可能。

3. 可持续催化剂设计：未来研究可能集中在使用更丰富、更便宜的材料开发催化剂，同时保持或改善性能，解决经济和环境问题。

4. 过程强化：将膜技术或微反应器设计与先进催化剂结合可能导致更高效、更紧凑的脱氢过程。

5. 多功能催化剂：开发能够在一个步骤中执行多个反应的催化剂，如结合脱氢和选择性氧化，可能为过程优化开辟新途径。

6. 催化剂再生：快速、高效再生技术的创新可能显著延长催化剂寿命并减少过程停机时间。

向前迈进时，挑战将是将这些实验室规模的进展转化为工业可行的解决方案，平衡性能、成本和可扩展性。直接脱氢过程催化剂设计的未来在于这些先进策略的协同整合，有可能彻底改革工业化学过程的效率和可持续性。
