<EnglishVersionAnswer>

Recent strategies to enhance catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts for direct dehydrogenation include:

1. Modification of metal oxides with noble metals: This has been shown to improve the reactivity of metal oxides for propane dehydrogenation (PDH).

2. Use of trace amounts of noble metals: Adding small quantities (<0.15 wt%) of noble metals can significantly enhance PDH performance on supports like zirconia.

3. Optimizing metal loading: For example, 0.03 wt% Pt on Ga2O3 showed the best synergistic effect and propylene formation rate.

4. Developing bimetallic systems: The PtGa2O3 system demonstrated synergistic effects and improved stability compared to individual components.

These advancements address challenges in catalyst design and performance by:

1. Improving activity while using minimal amounts of expensive noble metals
2. Enhancing stability through synergistic metal-support interactions  
3. Optimizing metal particle size and dispersion for maximum active sites
4. Tailoring the electronic and chemical properties of the catalyst surface

The research provides guidance for rationally designing metal-metal oxide catalysts with improved activity and stability for industrial dehydrogenation processes.

最近为提高直接脱氢过程中负载金属催化剂的催化稳定性而开发的关键策略和方法包括：

1. 用贵金属修饰金属氧化物：这已被证明可以提高金属氧化物在丙烷脱氢（PDH）中的反应活性。

2. 使用微量贵金属：添加少量（<0.15 wt%）贵金属可以显著提高锆石等载体上的PDH性能。

3. 优化金属负载量：例如，0.03 wt% Pt负载在Ga2O3上显示出最佳的协同效应和丙烯生成率。

4. 开发双金属体系：PtGa2O3体系相比单一组分展现出协同效应和更好的稳定性。

这些进展通过以下方式解决了催化剂设计和性能方面的挑战：

1. 在使用最少量昂贵贵金属的同时提高活性
2. 通过金属-载体之间的协同作用增强稳定性
3. 优化金属粒子尺寸和分散度以最大化活性位点
4. 调整催化剂表面的电子和化学性质

这项研究为理性设计具有更高活性和稳定性的金属-金属氧化物催化剂提供了指导，以用于工业脱氢过程。
