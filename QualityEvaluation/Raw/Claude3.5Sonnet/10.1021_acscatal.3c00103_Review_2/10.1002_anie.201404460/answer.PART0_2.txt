Recent strategies to enhance catalytic stability of supported metal catalysts in direct dehydrogenation processes include:

1. Developing synergistic multi-component catalyst systems, such as PtGa/Al2O3, where different elements work together to improve stability and resistance to deactivation.

2. Incorporating alkali metal dopants like potassium to increase selectivity and reduce coke deposition by poisoning acidic sites on the catalyst surface.

3. Optimizing the interaction between catalyst components, such as using platinum to assist in hydrogen recombination, thereby maintaining active sites for continued dehydrogenation cycles.

4. Designing catalyst materials that are inherently resistant to coking, allowing for prolonged activity without frequent regeneration.

5. Utilizing mixed oxide supports or creating ternary oxide structures to enhance catalyst stability and prevent sintering of active metal components.

These advancements address challenges in catalyst design by focusing on:

1. Improving resistance to common deactivation mechanisms like coking and sintering.
2. Enhancing selectivity towards desired products.
3. Prolonging catalyst lifetime to reduce operational costs and downtime.
4. Optimizing the synergy between different catalyst components to achieve better overall performance.

These strategies aim to create more efficient and economically viable catalysts for industrial applications, potentially leading to improved process economics and reduced environmental impact in large-scale dehydrogenation operations.

1. 提高对常见失活机制（如积碳和烧结）的抵抗能力。
2. 提高对目标产物的选择性。
3. 延长催化剂寿命，以降低运营成本和停机时间。
4. 优化不同催化剂组分之间的协同作用，以实现更好的整体性能。
