<English>
Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) has emerged as a promising alternative for propylene production in response to the increasing industrial demand and limitations of traditional production methods. PDH offers a direct and efficient route to convert propane into propylene, capitalizing on the abundant propane resources from shale gas exploitation. This technique addresses several challenges faced by the petrochemical industry:

1. Increasing demand: The global demand for propylene is expected to reach 191 megatonnes by 2030, which cannot be met by existing steam cracking (SC) and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) processes [10.1039/D1GC03700E].

2. Feedstock availability: The boom in shale gas production has made propane a cheap and plentiful feedstock, making PDH more economically attractive [10.1039/D0CS00814A].

3. Selectivity: PDH offers higher selectivity towards propylene production compared to traditional cracking methods [10.1039/C5SC01227A].

4. Energy efficiency: PDH is environmentally preferred to large-scale sources of propene from steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking, based on energy consumption [10.1039/D1GC03700E].

However, the widespread adoption of PDH technology is hindered by catalyst deactivation issues, primarily due to coke formation and sintering of active compounds at high temperatures. To address these challenges, researchers are developing innovative catalyst design strategies to enhance stability and recyclability:

1. Alternative catalyst materials: Researchers are exploring ZrO2-based catalysts as environmentally friendly and cost-efficient alternatives to traditional CrOx/Al2O3 and Pt/Al2O3 catalysts [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.012]. Another promising approach involves ZnO catalysts supported on high-silica HZSM-5 zeolites, which offer improved selectivity, stability, and reusability [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508].

2. Single atom/site catalysts: This concept achieves good activity and impressive stability by exploiting the size-dependent nature of side reactions, maintaining high activity for propane dehydrogenation while minimizing coke-forming side reactions [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331].

3. Confined metal clusters: Subnanometer PtSn clusters confined in zeolites allow for precise control over size and location of catalytic sites, leading to improved stability and coke resistance [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331].

4. Promoter introduction: Adding late transition metals, main-group metals (e.g., Sn, Ga), alkali-metal oxides, or rare-earth metal oxides can increase robustness against coking [10.1039/C5SC01227A].

5. Support modification: Tuning the acidity and structure of support materials, such as high-silica HZSM-5, can significantly improve propylene selectivity and dehydrogenation stability [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508].

6. Oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH): This approach aims to lower energy requirements by combining endothermic dehydrogenation with exothermic hydrogen combustion [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]. The use of CO2 as a soft oxidant (CO2-PDH) has also gained interest due to environmental advantages [10.1039/D1GC03700E].

7. Chemical looping oxidative dehydrogenation (CL-ODH): This technique, based on redox catalysts, can selectively oxidize hydrogen evolved during the reaction, offering a moderate dehydrogenation technology [10.1039/D0CS00814A].

These innovative strategies aim to create more efficient, stable, and recyclable catalysts for PDH processes, addressing the growing demand for propylene while overcoming the limitations of current technologies.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as a promising alternative for propylene production represents a significant shift in the petrochemical industry. By addressing catalyst deactivation challenges through innovative design strategies, researchers are paving the way for more sustainable and efficient propylene production methods.

Looking forward, the integration of advanced materials science, nanotechnology, and process engineering is likely to yield even more sophisticated catalyst designs. Future research may focus on:

1. Developing "smart" catalysts that can self-regenerate or adapt to changing reaction conditions, potentially using principles from materials science and nanotechnology.

2. Exploring novel reactor designs, such as membrane reactors or microreactors, to complement advanced catalyst formulations and further improve process efficiency.

3. Incorporating artificial intelligence and machine learning to optimize catalyst composition and process parameters in real-time, leading to more robust and adaptable PDH systems.

4. Investigating the potential of bio-based or renewable feedstocks for PDH, aligning with global sustainability goals and circular economy principles.

5. Exploring hybrid processes that combine PDH with other technologies, such as carbon capture and utilization, to create more environmentally friendly and economically viable production routes.

As the global energy landscape continues to evolve, PDH technology, supported by these innovative catalyst design strategies, is poised to play a crucial role in meeting the increasing demand for propylene while addressing environmental and economic challenges in the petrochemical industry.

<Chinese>
面对日益增长的工业需求和传统生产方法的局限性，丙烷脱氢（PDH）技术已成为丙烯生产的一种有前景的替代方法。PDH提供了一种直接高效的方式将丙烷转化为丙烯，利用了页岩气开采带来的丰富丙烷资源。这种技术解决了石油化工行业面临的几个挑战：

1. 需求增长：预计到2030年，全球丙烯需求将达到1.91亿吨，现有的蒸汽裂解（SC）和流化催化裂解（FCC）工艺无法满足这一需求 [10.1039/D1GC03700E]。

2. 原料可用性：页岩气生产的蓬勃发展使丙烷成为廉价且丰富的原料，使PDH在经济上更具吸引力 [10.1039/D0CS00814A]。

3. 选择性：与传统裂解方法相比，PDH在丙烯生产方面具有更高的选择性 [10.1039/C5SC01227A]。

4. 能源效率：从能源消耗的角度来看，PDH比蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解等大规模丙烯生产方法更环保 [10.1039/D1GC03700E]。

然而，PDH技术的广泛应用受到催化剂失活问题的阻碍，主要是由于高温下焦炭形成和活性组分烧结。为了解决这些挑战，研究人员正在开发创新的催化剂设计策略，以提高稳定性和可回收性：

1. 替代催化剂材料：研究人员正在探索基于ZrO2的催化剂，作为传统CrOx/Al2O3和Pt/Al2O3催化剂的环保和成本效益替代品 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.012]。另一种有前景的方法是使用高硅HZSM-5分子筛负载的ZnO催化剂，它具有更好的选择性、稳定性和可重复使用性 [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]。

2. 单原子/位点催化剂：这一概念通过利用副反应的尺寸依赖性，实现了良好的活性和令人印象深刻的稳定性，保持了丙烷脱氢的高活性，同时最大限度地减少了生焦副反应 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331]。

3. 限制金属簇：在分子筛中限制的亚纳米PtSn簇允许精确控制催化位点的尺寸和位置，从而提高稳定性和抗焦性 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331]。

4. 引入助剂：添加后过渡金属、主族金属（如Sn、Ga）、碱金属氧化物或稀土金属氧化物可以增加抗焦性 [10.1039/C5SC01227A]。

5. 载体改性：调整载体材料的酸度和结构，如高硅HZSM-5，可显著提高丙烯选择性和脱氢稳定性 [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]。

6. 氧化脱氢（ODH）：这种方法旨在通过将吸热脱氢与放热氢燃烧相结合来降低能量需求 [10.1016/j.fuproc.2016.04.016]。使用CO2作为软氧化剂（CO2-PDH）也因其环境优势而受到关注 [10.1039/D1GC03700E]。

7. 化学链氧化脱氢（CL-ODH）：这种基于氧化还原催化剂的技术可以选择性地氧化反应过程中产生的氢气，提供了一种适度的脱氢技术 [10.1039/D0CS00814A]。

这些创新策略旨在为PDH工艺创造更高效、更稳定和可回收的催化剂，满足不断增长的丙烯需求，同时克服当前技术的局限性。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：丙烷脱氢（PDH）作为丙烯生产的一种有前景的替代方法的演变，代表了石油化工行业的重大转变。通过创新的设计策略解决催化剂失活的挑战，研究人员正在为更可持续和高效的丙烯生产方法铺平道路。

展望未来，先进材料科学、纳米技术和工艺工程的结合可能会产生更复杂的催化剂设计。未来的研究可能关注：

1. 开发能够自我再生或适应不断变化的反应条件的"智能"催化剂，可能利用材料科学和纳米技术的原理。

2. 探索新型反应器设计，如膜反应器或微反应器，以补充先进的催化剂配方，进一步提高工艺效率。

3. 结合人工智能和机器学习，实时优化催化剂组成和工艺参数，形成更强大和适应性更强的PDH系统。

4. 研究生物基或可再生原料在PDH中的潜力，与全球可持续发展目标和循环经济原则保持一致。

5. 探索将PDH与其他技术（如碳捕获和利用）相结合的混合工艺，创造更环保和经济可行的生产路线。

随着全球能源格局的不断演变，在这些创新催化剂设计策略的支持下，PDH技术有望在满足不断增长的丙烯需求的同时，解决石油化工行业的环境和经济挑战中发挥关键作用。
