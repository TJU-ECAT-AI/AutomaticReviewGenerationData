<English>
Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) has emerged as a promising alternative for propylene production in response to the increasing industrial demand and limitations of traditional production methods. PDH offers several advantages over conventional processes like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking, including higher selectivity, on-purpose production, and utilization of abundant propane resources from shale gas [10.1039/D1GC03700E]. The evolution of PDH technologies has been driven by the need to address key challenges, particularly catalyst deactivation and stability issues.

To overcome these challenges, researchers have developed innovative catalyst design strategies for both oxidative and non-oxidative PDH processes. These strategies aim to enhance catalyst stability, improve recyclability, and increase overall process efficiency:

1. Novel catalyst compositions: Researchers are exploring alternatives to traditional Pt- and Cr-based catalysts, which suffer from high costs, toxicity, and rapid deactivation. New catalysts based on ZnO, V, Ga, and Sn have shown promise in terms of activity, selectivity, and environmental compatibility [10.1038/s41586-021-03923-3] [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004].

2. Support modification: The use of high-silica HZSM-5 zeolites as catalyst supports has demonstrated improved dispersion of active species, enhanced stability, and better recyclability. For example, ZnO catalysts supported on high-silica HZSM-5 have shown excellent propylene selectivity and stability over multiple cycles [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508].

3. Promoter introduction: The addition of promoters such as Sn, Ga, and other transition metals to Pt-based catalysts has been shown to improve catalyst performance and stability by reducing coke formation and enhancing selectivity [10.1039/C5SC01227A].

4. Single atom/site catalysis: This approach aims to maximize catalytic activity while minimizing side reactions and coke formation by precisely controlling the size and distribution of active sites [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331].

5. Oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH): By introducing oxygen or CO2 as co-reactants, ODH processes can overcome thermodynamic limitations and reduce coke formation. However, challenges remain in controlling selectivity and avoiding over-oxidation [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

6. Chemical looping oxidative dehydrogenation (CL-ODH): This emerging technology uses redox catalysts to selectively oxidize hydrogen produced during the reaction, potentially offering a more moderate dehydrogenation approach [10.1039/D0CS00814A].

7. Membrane reactor systems: The introduction of membranes to remove hydrogen from the reaction mixture can shift the equilibrium towards propylene production, potentially improving yields and efficiency [10.1021/cr5002436].

These innovative strategies have led to significant improvements in catalyst performance, stability, and recyclability. For instance, the development of ZnO catalysts supported on high-silica HZSM-5 has resulted in catalysts with high propane conversion (43%) and excellent propylene selectivity (93%) maintained over extended reaction times [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]. Similarly, advances in single-atom catalysis and confined metal clusters have shown promise in reducing coke formation and improving catalyst longevity [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements and findings discussed, the future of PDH catalysts lies in the synergistic integration of multiple design strategies to create highly efficient, stable, and recyclable catalysts. The development of multi-functional catalysts that combine the benefits of novel compositions, optimized supports, and advanced preparation techniques could lead to breakthroughs in addressing the persistent challenges of coke formation and catalyst deactivation.

Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Exploring the potential of artificial intelligence and machine learning in catalyst design to rapidly screen and optimize new catalyst compositions and structures.
2. Developing in-situ regeneration techniques that allow for continuous operation without the need for frequent catalyst replacement or offline regeneration.
3. Investigating the integration of PDH processes with other technologies, such as CO2 utilization or renewable energy sources, to create more sustainable and economically viable propylene production methods.
4. Advancing our understanding of the fundamental mechanisms of catalyst deactivation and propylene formation at the atomic level to guide the rational design of next-generation catalysts.

These future directions could pave the way for more efficient, sustainable, and economically viable PDH processes, addressing the growing global demand for propylene while minimizing environmental impact.

<Chinese>
随着工业对丙烯需求的增加和传统生产方法的局限性，丙烷脱氢（PDH）技术已成为丙烯生产的一种有前途的替代方法。与传统的蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解相比，PDH提供了多项优势，包括更高的选择性、定向生产和利用页岩气中丰富的丙烷资源[10.1039/D1GC03700E]。PDH技术的发展主要是为了解决关键挑战，特别是催化剂失活和稳定性问题。

为了克服这些挑战，研究人员已经为氧化和非氧化PDH过程开发了创新的催化剂设计策略。这些策略旨在提高催化剂稳定性，改善可回收性，并提高整体工艺效率：

1. 新型催化剂组成：研究人员正在探索传统Pt和Cr基催化剂的替代品，这些传统催化剂存在成本高、毒性和快速失活等问题。基于ZnO、V、Ga和Sn的新型催化剂在活性、选择性和环境兼容性方面表现出良好的前景[10.1038/s41586-021-03923-3] [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004]。

2. 载体改性：使用高硅HZSM-5沸石作为催化剂载体已经显示出改善活性物种分散、增强稳定性和提高可回收性的效果。例如，以高硅HZSM-5为载体的ZnO催化剂在多个循环中表现出优异的丙烯选择性和稳定性[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]。

3. 引入助剂：向Pt基催化剂中添加Sn、Ga和其他过渡金属等助剂，已被证明可以通过减少积碳形成和提高选择性来改善催化剂性能和稳定性[10.1039/C5SC01227A]。

4. 单原子/位点催化：这种方法旨在通过精确控制活性位点的大小和分布来最大化催化活性，同时最小化副反应和积碳形成[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331]。

5. 氧化脱氢（ODH）：通过引入氧气或CO2作为共反应物，ODH过程可以克服热力学限制并减少积碳形成。然而，在控制选择性和避免过度氧化方面仍然存在挑战[10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

6. 化学链氧化脱氢（CL-ODH）：这种新兴技术使用氧化还原催化剂选择性氧化反应过程中产生的氢气，可能提供一种更温和的脱氢方法[10.1039/D0CS00814A]。

7. 膜反应器系统：引入膜从反应混合物中除去氢气可以将平衡向丙烯生成方向移动，从而可能提高产率和效率[10.1021/cr5002436]。

这些创新策略已经带来了催化剂性能、稳定性和可回收性的显著改进。例如，以高硅HZSM-5为载体的ZnO催化剂的开发已经产生了具有高丙烷转化率（43%）和优异丙烯选择性（93%）的催化剂，并且在长时间反应中保持稳定[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]。同样，单原子催化和受限金属簇的进展在减少积碳形成和改善催化剂寿命方面也显示出了良好的前景[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：考虑到讨论的先进成果和发现，PDH催化剂的未来在于多种设计策略的协同整合，以创造高效、稳定和可回收的催化剂。开发结合新型组成、优化载体和先进制备技术优势的多功能催化剂可能会在解决持续存在的积碳形成和催化剂失活挑战方面取得突破。

未来的研究努力可能会集中在：

1. 探索人工智能和机器学习在催化剂设计中的潜力，以快速筛选和优化新的催化剂组成和结构。
2. 开发原位再生技术，允许连续操作，无需频繁更换催化剂或离线再生。
3. 研究PDH工艺与其他技术（如CO2利用或可再生能源）的整合，以创造更可持续和经济可行的丙烯生产方法。
4. 深化对催化剂失活和丙烯形成基本机理的原子级理解，以指导下一代催化剂的理性设计。

这些未来方向可能为更高效、更可持续和经济可行的PDH工艺铺平道路，满足不断增长的全球丙烯需求，同时最大限度地减少环境影响。
