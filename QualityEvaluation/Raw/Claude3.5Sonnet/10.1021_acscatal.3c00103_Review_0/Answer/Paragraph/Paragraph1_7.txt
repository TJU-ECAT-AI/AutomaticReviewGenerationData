<English>
The evolution of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as a promising alternative for propylene production has been driven by the increasing industrial demand for propylene and the limitations of traditional production methods. PDH techniques address these challenges by offering a direct and efficient route to produce propylene from propane, capitalizing on the growing availability of propane from shale gas reserves [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]. This approach provides a more targeted and flexible production method compared to traditional steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking processes, which produce propylene as a by-product with low yields [10.1002/cctc.202001640].

The development of PDH processes has focused on both non-oxidative and oxidative dehydrogenation techniques. Non-oxidative PDH has been successfully commercialized and is currently the only industrially operated process [10.1039/D1GC03700E]. However, the catalysts used in these processes, typically based on platinum or chromium oxides, face significant challenges related to rapid deactivation due to coke formation, high costs, and environmental concerns [10.1038/s41586-021-03923-3].

To address these issues, researchers have been developing innovative catalyst design strategies aimed at enhancing stability, improving recyclability, and overcoming deactivation challenges. Some of the key approaches include:

1. Development of alternative catalysts: Researchers are exploring new catalyst formulations, such as ZnO-based catalysts supported on high-silica HZSM-5 zeolites, which offer improved selectivity, stability, and environmental compatibility compared to traditional Pt and Cr-based catalysts [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508].

2. Single atom/site catalysts: This concept has been applied to Pt catalysts, achieving both good activity and impressive stability by exploiting the size-dependent nature of side reactions [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331].

3. Confined metal clusters: Subnanometer PtSn clusters confined in zeolites allow for precise control over size and location of catalytic sites, leading to improved stability and coke resistance [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331].

4. Bimetallic systems: Combining platinum with other metals like Cu or Sn has shown to increase selectivity towards propylene and reduce coke formation [10.1039/C5SC01227A].

5. Support engineering: The use of high-silica HZSM-5 supports has demonstrated enhanced dispersion of active species and improved catalytic activity [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508].

6. Promoter addition: Introduction of promoters such as Sn, Ga, and other elements has been shown to increase robustness against coking and improve overall catalyst performance [10.1039/C5SC01227A].

7. Oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH): This approach aims to overcome thermodynamic limitations and decrease carbon deposition, although it faces challenges related to over-oxidation of propene [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004].

8. CO2-assisted PDH: The use of CO2 as a soft oxidant offers environmental advantages, although current catalysts show lower propene yields compared to traditional PDH methods [10.1039/D1GC03700E].

These strategies collectively aim to create more efficient, stable, and recyclable catalysts for PDH processes. By addressing issues such as coke formation, sintering, and poor regeneration ability, researchers are working towards developing catalysts that can maintain high activity and selectivity over extended periods, reducing the need for frequent regeneration cycles [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advancements in PDH catalyst design reflect a shift towards more sustainable and efficient propylene production methods. The focus on developing catalysts with improved stability, selectivity, and recyclability addresses the key challenges faced by the petrochemical industry in meeting the growing demand for propylene.

Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Advanced characterization techniques: Utilizing cutting-edge analytical methods to gain deeper insights into catalyst behavior under reaction conditions, potentially leading to more rational catalyst design approaches.

2. Hybrid processes: Exploring combinations of different PDH techniques, such as integrating membrane reactors with oxidative dehydrogenation, to leverage the advantages of multiple approaches.

3. Machine learning and computational modeling: Employing these tools to predict catalyst performance and guide the development of novel catalyst formulations.

4. Scale-up and process intensification: Developing strategies to efficiently translate promising lab-scale catalysts to industrial-scale production while optimizing process conditions for maximum efficiency and sustainability.

5. Circular economy integration: Investigating ways to incorporate PDH processes into broader circular economy frameworks, potentially utilizing waste streams as feedstocks or developing closed-loop regeneration processes for spent catalysts.

These future directions could lead to breakthrough technologies that not only meet the increasing demand for propylene but also align with global sustainability goals and changing energy landscapes.

<Chinese>
作为丙烯生产的一种有前景的替代方法，丙烷脱氢（PDH）的发展是由于工业对丙烯的需求不断增加以及传统生产方法的局限性所驱动的。PDH技术通过提供一种直接高效的方法从丙烷生产丙烯来应对这些挑战，利用了页岩气储备中丙烷的不断增加的可用性[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]。与传统的蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解过程相比，这种方法提供了一种更有针对性和灵活的生产方法，后者作为副产品生产丙烯，产率较低[10.1002/cctc.202001640]。

PDH工艺的发展集中在非氧化和氧化脱氢技术上。非氧化PDH已成功商业化，目前是唯一工业化运行的工艺[10.1039/D1GC03700E]。然而，这些工艺中使用的催化剂，通常基于铂或铬氧化物，面临着由于结焦造成的快速失活、高成本和环境问题等重大挑战[10.1038/s41586-021-03923-3]。

为解决这些问题，研究人员一直在开发创新的催化剂设计策略，旨在提高稳定性、改善可回收性并克服失活挑战。一些关键方法包括：

1. 开发替代催化剂：研究人员正在探索新的催化剂配方，如基于高硅HZSM-5沸石负载的ZnO催化剂，与传统的Pt和Cr基催化剂相比，这些催化剂具有更好的选择性、稳定性和环境兼容性[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]。

2. 单原子/位点催化剂：这一概念已应用于Pt催化剂，通过利用副反应的尺寸依赖性，实现了良好的活性和令人印象深刻的稳定性[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331]。

3. 限制金属簇：在沸石中限制的亚纳米PtSn簇允许精确控制催化位点的大小和位置，从而提高稳定性和抗结焦性能[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331]。

4. 双金属体系：将铂与其他金属如Cu或Sn结合，已显示可以提高对丙烯的选择性并减少结焦形成[10.1039/C5SC01227A]。

5. 载体工程：使用高硅HZSM-5载体已显示出增强活性物种的分散性和改善催化活性[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]。

6. 助剂添加：引入Sn、Ga和其他元素作为助剂已被证明可以增加对结焦的抵抗力并改善整体催化剂性能[10.1039/C5SC01227A]。

7. 氧化脱氢（ODH）：这种方法旨在克服热力学限制并减少碳沉积，尽管它面临与丙烯过度氧化相关的挑战[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004]。

8. CO2辅助PDH：使用CO2作为软氧化剂提供了环境优势，尽管目前的催化剂与传统PDH方法相比显示出较低的丙烯产率[10.1039/D1GC03700E]。

这些策略共同旨在为PDH工艺创造更高效、更稳定和可回收的催化剂。通过解决结焦形成、烧结和再生能力差等问题，研究人员正在努力开发能够长期保持高活性和选择性的催化剂，减少频繁再生循环的需求[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：PDH催化剂设计的进展反映了向更可持续和高效的丙烯生产方法的转变。焦点集中在开发具有改进的稳定性、选择性和可回收性的催化剂上，这解决了石油化工行业在满足日益增长的丙烯需求方面面临的关键挑战。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 先进表征技术：利用尖端分析方法深入了解催化剂在反应条件下的行为，可能导致更理性的催化剂设计方法。

2. 混合工艺：探索不同PDH技术的组合，例如将膜反应器与氧化脱氢相结合，以利用多种方法的优势。

3. 机器学习和计算模型：采用这些工具预测催化剂性能并指导新型催化剂配方的开发。

4. 规模扩大和过程强化：开发策略以有效地将有前景的实验室规模催化剂转化为工业规模生产，同时优化工艺条件以实现最大效率和可持续性。

5. 循环经济整合：研究将PDH工艺纳入更广泛的循环经济框架的方法，可能利用废物流作为原料或为用过的催化剂开发闭环再生工艺。

这些未来方向可能导致突破性技术的出现，不仅满足日益增长的丙烯需求，而且与全球可持续发展目标和不断变化的能源格局相一致。
