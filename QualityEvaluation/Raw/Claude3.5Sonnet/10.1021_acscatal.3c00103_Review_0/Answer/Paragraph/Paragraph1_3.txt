<English>
Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) has emerged as a promising alternative for propylene production, addressing the challenges of increasing industrial demand and limitations of traditional production methods. PDH offers a direct, on-purpose route to produce propylene, helping to fill the supply gap that cannot be met by existing steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking processes [10.1039/D1GC03700E]. This technique capitalizes on the growing availability of propane from shale gas reserves, providing a more economical and sustainable feedstock for propylene production [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508].

The evolution of PDH techniques has led to the development of both oxidative and non-oxidative processes, each with its own advantages and challenges. Non-oxidative direct dehydrogenation (DDH) is currently the only commercialized process, using chromium oxide or platinum-tin oxide catalysts [10.1039/D1GC03700E]. However, these catalysts face rapid deactivation due to coking and require frequent regeneration, which has driven research towards innovative catalyst design strategies to enhance stability and recyclability.

One of the primary challenges in PDH processes is catalyst deactivation, primarily due to coke formation and sintering of active compounds at high temperatures [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_0]. To address this issue, researchers are developing several innovative catalyst design strategies:

1. Alternative metal oxide catalysts: Exploring V-, Ga-, and Sn-based catalysts as alternatives to traditional Pt- and Cr-based catalysts. These new catalysts aim to be more cost-effective, environmentally friendly, and resistant to deactivation [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004].

2. Single atom/site catalysts: This approach achieves good activity and impressive stability by exploiting the size-dependent nature of side reactions. Single Pt atoms or sites maintain high activity for propane dehydrogenation while minimizing coke-forming side reactions [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331].

3. Confined metal clusters: Subnanometer PtSn clusters confined in zeolites allow for precise control over size and location of catalytic sites, leading to improved stability and coke resistance [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331].

4. Support engineering: Unique interactions between the catalyst and support, such as those seen in ZnO catalysts supported on high-silica HZSM-5 zeolites, can lead to excellent resistance to coke formation and improved stability [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508].

5. Promoter addition: Elements like Sn are used to modify the catalytic properties of Pt, potentially increasing both activity and stability [10.1039/C5SC01227A].

6. Oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH): This approach aims to overcome thermodynamic limitations and reduce coke formation by introducing oxidants such as O2 or CO2. While ODH with O2 can lead to overoxidation, CO2-assisted ODH offers environmental benefits but currently suffers from low propene yields [10.1039/D1GC03700E].

7. Chemical looping oxidative dehydrogenation (CL-ODH): This technique, based on redox catalysts, can selectively oxidize hydrogen evolved during the reaction, offering a moderate dehydrogenation technology [10.1039/D0CS00814A].

These strategies aim to create more efficient, stable, and recyclable catalysts for PDH processes, addressing the growing demand for propylene while overcoming the limitations of current technologies. By focusing on reducing deep dehydrogenation reactions, improving selectivity, and enhancing catalyst stability, researchers are working towards making PDH a more viable and sustainable option for industrial propylene production.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as a promising alternative for propylene production reflects a broader shift in the chemical industry towards more efficient, sustainable, and adaptable processes. The development of innovative catalyst design strategies not only addresses the immediate challenges of catalyst deactivation and stability but also opens up new possibilities for the future of chemical manufacturing.

Looking forward, the integration of advanced materials science, nanotechnology, and computational modeling could lead to breakthrough catalyst designs that significantly outperform current options. For instance, the use of machine learning algorithms to predict and optimize catalyst performance could accelerate the discovery of novel materials and structures.

Furthermore, the success of PDH technologies could pave the way for similar on-purpose production methods for other valuable chemical intermediates, potentially revolutionizing the petrochemical industry. As the world moves towards a more circular economy, the ability to efficiently produce specific chemicals on-demand from abundant feedstocks will become increasingly important.

The ongoing research in PDH catalysts also aligns with broader sustainability goals. By improving energy efficiency, reducing waste, and potentially utilizing CO2 as a co-reactant, PDH technologies could contribute to reducing the carbon footprint of the chemical industry. This could become particularly significant as regulations around carbon emissions tighten globally.

Lastly, the advancements in PDH catalyst design may have spillover effects in other areas of catalysis research, potentially leading to innovations in related fields such as biomass conversion, fuel cells, and environmental remediation. As such, the continued development of PDH technologies represents not just a solution to a specific industrial challenge, but a stepping stone towards a more sustainable and technologically advanced chemical industry.

<Chinese>
丙烷脱氢（PDH）已成为丙烯生产的一种有前景的替代方法，解决了工业需求增加和传统生产方法限制的挑战。PDH提供了一种直接的、专门的路线来生产丙烯，有助于填补现有蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解过程无法满足的供应缺口[10.1039/D1GC03700E]。这种技术利用了来自页岩气储备的丙烷供应量的增加，为丙烯生产提供了一种更经济、更可持续的原料[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]。

PDH技术的发展导致了氧化和非氧化过程的开发，每种过程都有其自身的优势和挑战。非氧化直接脱氢（DDH）目前是唯一商业化的过程，使用氧化铬或氧化铂锡催化剂[10.1039/D1GC03700E]。然而，这些催化剂面临着由于积炭而导致的快速失活，需要频繁再生，这推动了研究朝着创新催化剂设计策略方向发展，以提高稳定性和可回收性。

PDH过程中的主要挑战之一是催化剂失活，主要是由于在高温下形成积炭和活性化合物烧结[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_0]。为了解决这个问题，研究人员正在开发几种创新的催化剂设计策略：

1. 替代金属氧化物催化剂：探索基于V、Ga和Sn的催化剂作为传统Pt和Cr基催化剂的替代品。这些新催化剂旨在更具成本效益、环境友好性和抗失活性[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004]。

2. 单原子/位点催化剂：这种方法通过利用副反应的尺寸依赖性来实现良好的活性和令人印象深刻的稳定性。单个Pt原子或位点保持了高的丙烷脱氢活性，同时最大限度地减少了形成积炭的副反应[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331]。

3. 限制金属簇：在沸石中限制的亚纳米PtSn簇允许精确控制催化位点的大小和位置，从而提高稳定性和抗积炭能力[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331]。

4. 载体工程：催化剂和载体之间的独特相互作用，如在高硅HZSM-5沸石上负载的ZnO催化剂中所见，可以导致优秀的抗积炭形成能力和提高的稳定性[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]。

5. 助剂添加：使用Sn等元素来修饰Pt的催化性能，可能同时提高活性和稳定性[10.1039/C5SC01227A]。

6. 氧化脱氢（ODH）：这种方法旨在通过引入O2或CO2等氧化剂来克服热力学限制并减少积炭形成。虽然O2辅助的ODH可能导致过度氧化，但CO2辅助的ODH提供了环境效益，但目前丙烯产率较低[10.1039/D1GC03700E]。

7. 化学链氧化脱氢（CL-ODH）：这种基于氧化还原催化剂的技术可以选择性地氧化反应过程中产生的氢气，提供了一种适度的脱氢技术[10.1039/D0CS00814A]。

这些策略旨在为PDH过程创造更高效、更稳定和可回收的催化剂，满足不断增长的丙烯需求，同时克服当前技术的局限性。通过专注于减少深度脱氢反应、提高选择性和增强催化剂稳定性，研究人员正在努力使PDH成为工业丙烯生产的更可行和可持续的选择。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：丙烷脱氢（PDH）作为丙烯生产的一种有前景的替代方法的发展，反映了化学工业向更高效、可持续和适应性强的工艺转变的更广泛趋势。创新催化剂设计策略的开发不仅解决了催化剂失活和稳定性的直接挑战，还为化学制造的未来开辟了新的可能性。

展望未来，先进材料科学、纳米技术和计算机建模的结合可能会导致突破性的催化剂设计，其性能显著优于当前选择。例如，使用机器学习算法来预测和优化催化剂性能可能会加速新材料和结构的发现。

此外，PDH技术的成功可能为其他有价值的化学中间体的类似专门生产方法铺平道路，可能彻底改变石化工业。随着世界向更循环的经济转变，能够从丰富的原料中高效地按需生产特定化学品的能力将变得越来越重要。

PDH催化剂的持续研究也与更广泛的可持续发展目标一致。通过提高能源效率、减少废物，并可能利用CO2作为共反应物，PDH技术可能有助于减少化学工业的碳足迹。随着全球碳排放法规的收紧，这可能变得特别重要。

最后，PDH催化剂设计的进步可能会对其他催化研究领域产生溢出效应，可能导致生物质转化、燃料电池和环境修复等相关领域的创新。因此，PDH技术的持续发展不仅代表了对特定工业挑战的解决方案，而且是迈向更可持续和技术先进的化学工业的一个重要步骤。
