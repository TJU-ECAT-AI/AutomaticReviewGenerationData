<English>
The evolution of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as a promising alternative for propylene production has been driven by the increasing industrial demand for propylene and the limitations of traditional production methods. PDH techniques address these challenges by offering a direct and efficient route to produce propylene from propane, capitalizing on the growing availability of propane from shale gas reserves [10.1039/D1GC03700E]. This approach provides a more targeted and selective production method compared to traditional steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking processes, which produce propylene as a by-product [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508].

The development of PDH technology has been accompanied by significant efforts to overcome catalyst deactivation challenges through innovative design strategies. These strategies aim to enhance stability and improve recyclability in both oxidative and non-oxidative PDH processes. Some key approaches include:

1. Alternative catalyst development: Researchers are exploring new catalysts to replace the traditional Pt- and Cr-based systems, which suffer from high costs, toxicity, and rapid deactivation. For example, ZnO catalysts supported on high-silica HZSM-5 zeolites have shown promising results, offering high selectivity, stability, and reusability [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508].

2. Single atom/site catalysts: This concept has been applied to Pt catalysts, achieving both good activity and impressive stability. By exploiting the size-dependent nature of side reactions, these catalysts maintain high activity for propane dehydrogenation while minimizing coke-forming side reactions [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331].

3. Confined metal clusters: Subnanometer PtSn clusters confined in zeolites allow for precise control over size and location of catalytic sites, leading to improved stability and coke resistance [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331].

4. Promoter introduction: The addition of promoters such as late transition metals, main-group metals (e.g., Sn and Ga), alkali-metal oxides, and rare-earth metal oxides has been shown to increase robustness against coking [10.1039/C5SC01227A].

5. Support modification: Researchers are exploring different support materials and modifying existing ones to enhance catalyst stability and activity. For instance, the use of high-silica HZSM-5 as a support for ZnO catalysts has shown improved dispersion of active species and reduced deactivation [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508].

6. Oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH): This approach aims to overcome thermodynamic limitations and decrease carbon deposition. However, it faces challenges with overoxidation and unwanted by-product formation [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004].

7. CO2-assisted PDH: The use of CO2 as a soft oxidant offers environmental advantages and has shown promise in enhancing propene formation rates [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

These innovative strategies have led to significant improvements in catalyst performance, stability, and recyclability. For example, the ZnO/HZSM-5 catalyst has demonstrated high propane conversion (43%) and excellent propylene selectivity (93%) even after 10 hours of reaction, with good reuse performance over multiple cycles [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of PDH technology represents a paradigm shift in propylene production, driven by the need for more efficient and sustainable processes in the face of changing global energy landscapes. The innovative catalyst design strategies discussed above not only address the immediate challenges of catalyst deactivation and stability but also pave the way for future advancements in the field.

Looking forward, we can anticipate several key areas of development:

1. Advanced materials science: The continued exploration of novel materials, including metal-organic frameworks, 2D materials, and nanostructured composites, could lead to breakthrough catalysts with unprecedented performance and stability.

2. In-situ regeneration techniques: Development of more efficient and less energy-intensive regeneration methods could significantly improve the overall economics and sustainability of PDH processes.

3. Process intensification: Integration of PDH with other processes, such as membrane separation or chemical looping, could lead to more compact and efficient production systems.

4. Artificial intelligence and machine learning: These tools could accelerate catalyst discovery and optimization, potentially uncovering new catalyst compositions and structures that human researchers might overlook.

5. Green hydrogen production: As the hydrogen economy grows, PDH processes could be integrated with hydrogen capture and utilization systems, creating synergies between propylene production and clean energy initiatives.

6. Circular economy approaches: Development of catalysts capable of efficiently converting plastic waste back into propylene could create a closed-loop system for polymer production and recycling.

These future directions highlight the potential for PDH technology to not only meet the growing demand for propylene but also to contribute to broader sustainability goals in the chemical industry. As research continues to advance, we can expect PDH to play an increasingly important role in the evolving landscape of petrochemical production.

<Chinese>
作为一种有前景的丙烯生产替代方法，丙烷脱氢(PDH)技术的发展是由日益增长的丙烯工业需求和传统生产方法的局限性所推动的。PDH技术通过提供一种从丙烷直接高效地生产丙烯的路径来应对这些挑战，充分利用了页岩气储备带来的丙烷供应增长[10.1039/D1GC03700E]。与传统的蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解工艺相比，这种方法提供了一种更有针对性和选择性的生产方法，因为后者将丙烯作为副产品生产[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]。

PDH技术的发展伴随着克服催化剂失活挑战的重大努力，这些努力通过创新的设计策略来实现。这些策略旨在增强稳定性并改善氧化和非氧化PDH过程中的可回收性。一些关键方法包括：

1. 替代催化剂开发：研究人员正在探索新的催化剂来取代传统的Pt和Cr基系统，这些系统存在成本高、毒性大和快速失活的问题。例如，以高硅HZSM-5沸石为载体的ZnO催化剂显示出了有前景的结果，提供了高选择性、稳定性和可重复使用性[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]。

2. 单原子/单位点催化剂：这个概念已应用于Pt催化剂，实现了良好的活性和令人印象深刻的稳定性。通过利用副反应的尺寸依赖性，这些催化剂保持了高丙烷脱氢活性，同时最大限度地减少了形成焦炭的副反应[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331]。

3. 限域金属簇：限制在沸石中的亚纳米PtSn簇允许精确控制催化位点的尺寸和位置，从而提高稳定性和抗焦性[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331]。

4. 引入促进剂：添加后过渡金属、主族金属（如Sn和Ga）、碱金属氧化物和稀土金属氧化物等促进剂已被证明可以增加对焦炭的抵抗能力[10.1039/C5SC01227A]。

5. 载体改性：研究人员正在探索不同的载体材料并改进现有的载体，以增强催化剂的稳定性和活性。例如，使用高硅HZSM-5作为ZnO催化剂的载体已显示出改善了活性物种的分散性并减少了失活[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]。

6. 氧化脱氢（ODH）：这种方法旨在克服热力学限制并减少碳沉积。然而，它面临过度氧化和不需要的副产品形成的挑战[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004]。

7. CO2辅助PDH：使用CO2作为软氧化剂提供了环境优势，并在增强丙烯形成速率方面显示出前景[10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

这些创新策略已经导致催化剂性能、稳定性和可回收性的显著改善。例如，ZnO/HZSM-5催化剂在反应10小时后仍表现出高丙烷转化率（43%）和优异的丙烯选择性（93%），并在多个循环中表现出良好的重复使用性能[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：PDH技术的发展代表了丙烯生产的范式转变，这是由于在全球能源格局变化的背景下，需要更高效和可持续的工艺。上述创新的催化剂设计策略不仅解决了催化剂失活和稳定性的直接挑战，还为该领域的未来进展铺平了道路。

展望未来，我们可以预见几个关键的发展领域：

1. 先进材料科学：持续探索新型材料，包括金属有机框架、二维材料和纳米结构复合材料，可能会带来具有前所未有的性能和稳定性的突破性催化剂。

2. 原位再生技术：开发更高效、能耗更低的再生方法可能会显著改善PDH过程的整体经济性和可持续性。

3. 过程强化：将PDH与其他过程（如膜分离或化学循环）集成可能会导致更紧凑和高效的生产系统。

4. 人工智能和机器学习：这些工具可以加速催化剂的发现和优化，潜在地发现人类研究者可能忽视的新催化剂组成和结构。

5. 绿色氢气生产：随着氢经济的发展，PDH过程可能与氢捕获和利用系统集成，在丙烯生产和清洁能源计划之间创造协同效应。

6. 循环经济方法：开发能够高效地将塑料废物转化回丙烯的催化剂可能会为聚合物生产和回收创造一个闭环系统。

这些未来方向突出了PDH技术不仅能满足日益增长的丙烯需求，而且还能为化工行业更广泛的可持续发展目标做出贡献的潜力。随着研究的不断推进，我们可以期待PDH在石化生产不断发展的格局中发挥越来越重要的作用。
