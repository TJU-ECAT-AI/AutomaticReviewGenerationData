<English>
The evolution of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) as a promising alternative for propylene production has been driven by the increasing industrial demand for propylene and the limitations of traditional production methods. PDH techniques address these challenges by offering a direct and efficient route to produce propylene from propane, capitalizing on the growing availability of propane from shale gas reserves [10.1002/cctc.202001640]. This approach provides a more targeted and flexible method compared to traditional steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking processes, which produce propylene as a by-product with low yields [10.1039/D1GC03700E].

To overcome deactivation issues, enhance stability, and improve recyclability in both oxidative and non-oxidative PDH processes, researchers are developing innovative catalyst design strategies. These strategies aim to address the key challenges of coking, sintering, and high costs associated with current catalysts while improving overall process efficiency and sustainability [10.1039/D0CS00814A].

One of the primary focuses in catalyst development is the creation of alternative catalysts to replace the traditional Pt- and Cr-based catalysts, which suffer from high costs, toxicity, and deactivation issues [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]. Innovative approaches include:

1. Development of ZnO-based catalysts supported on high-silica HZSM-5 zeolites, which offer improved selectivity, stability, and reusability compared to traditional catalysts [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508].

2. Exploration of V-, Ga-, and Sn-based catalysts as cost-effective and environmentally friendly alternatives [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004].

3. Investigation of single atom/site catalysts, particularly for Pt-based systems, to achieve good activity while minimizing coke-forming side reactions [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331].

4. Design of confined metal clusters, such as subnanometer PtSn clusters in zeolites, allowing for precise control over size and location of catalytic sites [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331].

5. Development of bimetallic systems, like single Pt atoms surrounded by inert Cu, to reduce the barrier for propene desorption and diminish coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331].

6. Optimization of support materials and promoters to enhance catalyst stability and reduce precious metal usage [10.1039/D1GC03700E].

7. Exploration of boron-based catalysts for oxidative dehydrogenation with oxygen (ODH-O2), showing promise for commercial application [10.1039/D1GC03700E].

Researchers are also investigating alternative PDH processes to overcome the limitations of traditional non-oxidative PDH. These include:

1. Oxidative dehydrogenation (ODH) using O2 or CO2 as co-reagents to overcome thermodynamic limitations and increase production through hydrogen binding [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

2. Chemical looping oxidative dehydrogenation (CL-ODH) based on redox catalysts, capable of selectively oxidizing the hydrogen evolved during the reaction [10.1039/D0CS00814A].

3. Membrane reactor systems to remove hydrogen and shift the equilibrium towards olefin production [10.1021/cr5002436].

These innovative strategies aim to create more efficient, stable, and recyclable catalysts for PDH processes, addressing the growing demand for propylene while overcoming the limitations of current technologies. By focusing on reducing deep dehydrogenation reactions, improving selectivity, and enhancing catalyst stability, researchers are working towards developing sustainable and economically viable PDH processes for industrial-scale propylene production.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysts represents a significant shift in the approach to propylene production, driven by the need for more efficient and sustainable processes. The development of novel catalysts and innovative reaction systems demonstrates the potential for PDH to become a dominant technology in meeting the growing global demand for propylene.

Looking forward, several key areas emerge as promising directions for future research:

1. Advanced materials science: The application of nanotechnology and materials engineering could lead to the development of more precisely tailored catalysts with enhanced stability and selectivity.

2. In-situ characterization techniques: Advancements in spectroscopic and microscopic methods could provide deeper insights into catalyst behavior under reaction conditions, facilitating the design of more effective catalysts.

3. Artificial intelligence and machine learning: These tools could accelerate catalyst discovery and optimization by predicting performance and guiding experimental design.

4. Process intensification: Integration of PDH with other processes, such as membrane separation or micro-reactor technology, could lead to more compact and efficient production systems.

5. Sustainability focus: Development of catalysts based on abundant, non-toxic materials and processes that minimize energy consumption and waste generation will be crucial for long-term viability.

6. Hybrid systems: Combining different PDH approaches, such as oxidative and non-oxidative methods, could potentially overcome individual limitations and create more robust production processes.

As the field progresses, it is likely that PDH technologies will play an increasingly important role in the chemical industry, potentially reshaping propylene production landscapes and contributing to more sustainable petrochemical processes.

<Chinese>
作为丙烯生产的一种有前景的替代方法，丙烷脱氢（PDH）的发展是由于工业对丙烯需求的增加以及传统生产方法的局限性所驱动的。PDH技术通过提供一种从丙烷直接高效生产丙烯的路径来应对这些挑战，利用了页岩气储备带来的丙烷供应增加 [10.1002/cctc.202001640]。与传统的蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解工艺相比，这种方法提供了一种更有针对性和灵活性的方法，因为传统方法中丙烯只是作为副产品生产，产率较低 [10.1039/D1GC03700E]。

为了克服失活问题，提高稳定性，并改善氧化和非氧化PDH过程中的可回收性，研究人员正在开发创新的催化剂设计策略。这些策略旨在解决目前催化剂面临的结焦、烧结和高成本等关键挑战，同时提高整体工艺效率和可持续性 [10.1039/D0CS00814A]。

催化剂开发的主要焦点之一是创造替代催化剂，以取代传统的Pt和Cr基催化剂，这些催化剂存在高成本、毒性和失活问题 [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]。创新方法包括：

1. 开发以高硅HZSM-5沸石为载体的ZnO基催化剂，与传统催化剂相比，这种催化剂具有更好的选择性、稳定性和可重复使用性 [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]。

2. 探索V、Ga和Sn基催化剂作为具有成本效益和环境友好的替代品 [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004]。

3. 研究单原子/单位点催化剂，特别是对于Pt基系统，以实现良好的活性，同时最大限度地减少形成焦炭的副反应 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331]。

4. 设计受限金属簇，如沸石中的亚纳米PtSn簇，允许精确控制催化位点的大小和位置 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331]。

5. 开发双金属系统，如被惰性Cu包围的单个Pt原子，以降低丙烯脱附的能垒并减少焦炭形成 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331]。

6. 优化载体材料和助剂，以提高催化剂稳定性并减少贵金属的使用 [10.1039/D1GC03700E]。

7. 探索用于氧化脱氢（ODH-O2）的硼基催化剂，显示出商业应用的前景 [10.1039/D1GC03700E]。

研究人员还在调查替代PDH工艺，以克服传统非氧化PDH的局限性。这些包括：

1. 使用O2或CO2作为共反应物的氧化脱氢（ODH），以克服热力学限制并通过氢结合增加产量 [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

2. 基于氧化还原催化剂的化学循环氧化脱氢（CL-ODH），能够选择性地氧化反应过程中产生的氢气 [10.1039/D0CS00814A]。

3. 膜反应器系统，用于去除氢气并将平衡向烯烃产物方向移动 [10.1021/cr5002436]。

这些创新策略旨在为PDH工艺创造更高效、更稳定和可回收的催化剂，满足不断增长的丙烯需求，同时克服当前技术的局限性。通过专注于减少深度脱氢反应、提高选择性和增强催化剂稳定性，研究人员正在努力开发可持续和经济可行的工业规模PDH工艺来生产丙烯。

综合理解与未来展望：丙烷脱氢（PDH）催化剂的演变代表了丙烯生产方法的重大转变，这一转变源于对更高效、更可持续工艺的需求。新型催化剂和创新反应系统的开发展示了PDH技术在满足全球不断增长的丙烯需求方面的潜力。

展望未来，几个关键领域作为未来研究的有前景方向浮现出来：

1. 先进材料科学：纳米技术和材料工程的应用可能导致开发出更精确定制的催化剂，具有增强的稳定性和选择性。

2. 原位表征技术：光谱和显微方法的进步可能提供对催化剂在反应条件下行为的更深入理解，有助于设计更有效的催化剂。

3. 人工智能和机器学习：这些工具可能通过预测性能和指导实验设计来加速催化剂的发现和优化。

4. 过程强化：将PDH与其他过程（如膜分离或微反应器技术）集成可能导致更紧凑和高效的生产系统。

5. 可持续性关注：基于丰富、无毒材料的催化剂开发，以及最小化能耗和废物产生的工艺，对于长期可行性至关重要。

6. 混合系统：结合不同的PDH方法，如氧化和非氧化方法，可能克服各自的局限性，创造更强大的生产工艺。

随着该领域的进展，PDH技术可能在化学工业中发挥越来越重要的作用，潜在地重塑丙烯生产格局，并为更可持续的石化工艺做出贡献。
