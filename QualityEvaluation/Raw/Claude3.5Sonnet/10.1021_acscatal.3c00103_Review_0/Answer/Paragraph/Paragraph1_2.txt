<English>
Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) has emerged as a promising alternative for propylene production, addressing the challenges of increasing industrial demand and limitations of traditional production methods. PDH offers several advantages over conventional processes like steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking, including higher selectivity, on-purpose production, and the ability to utilize abundant propane resources from shale gas [10.1039/D1GC03700E]. The growing demand for propylene, expected to reach 191 megatonnes by 2030, has driven the development of PDH technologies to fill the "propene gap" that cannot be met by existing processes [10.1039/D1GC03700E].

Despite its promise, PDH faces significant challenges related to catalyst deactivation, primarily due to coke formation and sintering of active compounds at high reaction temperatures [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_0]. To address these issues, researchers have been developing innovative catalyst design strategies to enhance stability and improve recyclability in both oxidative and non-oxidative PDH processes.

One major focus area is the development of alternative catalysts to replace the traditionally used Pt- and CrOx-based catalysts, which suffer from high costs, toxicity, and rapid deactivation [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]. Novel catalyst designs include:

1. ZnO-based catalysts supported on high-silica HZSM-5 zeolites, which offer improved selectivity, stability, and recyclability [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508].

2. V-based catalysts, which are cost-effective and environmentally friendly alternatives to Pt- and Cr-based catalysts [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004].

3. Boron-based catalysts for oxidative dehydrogenation with O2 (ODH-O2), showing significant promise for commercial application [10.1039/D1GC03700E].

4. Single atom/site Pt catalysts and confined PtSn clusters in zeolites, which achieve good activity and impressive stability while minimizing coke formation [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331].

Researchers are also exploring various strategies to improve catalyst performance and longevity:

1. Promoter introduction: Adding late transition metals, main-group metals, alkali-metal oxides, and rare-earth metal oxides to increase robustness against coking [10.1039/C5SC01227A].

2. Metal-support interaction enhancement: Optimizing the interaction between active metal species and support materials to improve catalyst stability and activity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_0].

3. Support modification: Developing supports with optimized properties, such as high-silica HZSM-5 zeolites, which provide better dispersion of active species and improved stability [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508].

4. Oxidative dehydrogenation: Exploring ODH-O2 and CO2-assisted PDH (CO2-PDH) to overcome thermodynamic limitations and reduce coke formation [10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018].

5. Chemical looping oxidative dehydrogenation (CL-ODH): Utilizing redox catalysts capable of selectively oxidizing evolved hydrogen, potentially offering a moderate dehydrogenation technology [10.1039/D0CS00814A].

These innovative approaches have led to significant improvements in catalyst performance. For example, ZnO catalysts supported on high-silica HZSM-5 have demonstrated high propane conversion (43%) and excellent propylene selectivity (93%) even after 10 hours of reaction [10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]. Similarly, V-based catalysts have shown remarkable long-term stability over multiple reaction-regeneration cycles [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evolution of propane dehydrogenation catalysts represents a significant step towards meeting the increasing global demand for propylene in a more sustainable and efficient manner. The development of novel catalysts and innovative design strategies has not only addressed the limitations of traditional PDH catalysts but also opened up new possibilities for process optimization and industrial implementation.

Looking forward, the field of PDH catalyst design is likely to continue advancing in several key areas:

1. Further exploration of non-precious metal catalysts: As research progresses, we may see the emergence of new catalytic materials that offer comparable or superior performance to platinum-based catalysts at a fraction of the cost.

2. Integration of multiple strategies: Future catalyst designs may combine various approaches, such as single-atom catalysis, support engineering, and promoter addition, to create synergistic effects that enhance overall performance and stability.

3. In-situ regeneration techniques: Development of advanced regeneration methods that can be seamlessly integrated into PDH processes, minimizing downtime and extending catalyst lifetime.

4. Computational modeling and machine learning: Leveraging these tools to predict catalyst behavior, optimize formulations, and accelerate the discovery of new materials.

5. Scale-up and process intensification: As promising catalysts move from lab-scale to industrial applications, research will focus on overcoming challenges related to large-scale production and process integration.

6. Sustainability considerations: Future catalyst design will likely place greater emphasis on using abundant, non-toxic materials and developing processes with reduced environmental impact.

7. Hybrid technologies: Combining PDH with other emerging technologies, such as membrane reactors or advanced separation techniques, to further improve efficiency and selectivity.

These advancements in PDH catalyst technology will play a crucial role in shaping the future of propylene production, potentially revolutionizing the petrochemical industry and contributing to more sustainable chemical manufacturing processes.

<Chinese>
丙烷脱氢（PDH）已经成为丙烯生产的一种有前景的替代方法，解决了工业需求不断增加和传统生产方法的局限性带来的挑战。与蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解等传统工艺相比，PDH具有多项优势，包括更高的选择性、定向生产能力，以及利用页岩气中丰富的丙烷资源的能力[10.1039/D1GC03700E]。丙烯需求的不断增长（预计到2030年将达到1.91亿吨）推动了PDH技术的发展，以填补现有工艺无法满足的"丙烯缺口"[10.1039/D1GC03700E]。

尽管PDH前景广阔，但仍面临着与催化剂失活相关的重大挑战，主要是由于高反应温度下的积碳形成和活性化合物的烧结[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_0]。为了解决这些问题，研究人员一直在开发创新的催化剂设计策略，以提高氧化和非氧化PDH过程中的稳定性和可循环性。

一个主要的研究重点是开发替代催化剂，以取代传统使用的Pt和CrOx基催化剂，这些催化剂存在成本高、毒性和快速失活等问题[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]。新型催化剂设计包括：

1. 以高硅HZSM-5沸石为载体的ZnO基催化剂，具有更好的选择性、稳定性和可循环性[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]。

2. V基催化剂，是Pt和Cr基催化剂的经济高效和环境友好的替代品[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004]。

3. 用于O2氧化脱氢（ODH-O2）的硼基催化剂，显示出显著的商业应用前景[10.1039/D1GC03700E]。

4. 单原子/位点Pt催化剂和限制在沸石中的PtSn团簇，在实现良好活性和出色稳定性的同时最大限度地减少积碳形成[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331]。

研究人员还在探索各种策略来提高催化剂的性能和寿命：

1. 引入助剂：添加后过渡金属、主族金属、碱金属氧化物和稀土金属氧化物，以提高抗积碳能力[10.1039/C5SC01227A]。

2. 加强金属-载体相互作用：优化活性金属物种与载体材料之间的相互作用，以提高催化剂的稳定性和活性[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103_0]。

3. 载体改性：开发具有优化性能的载体，如高硅HZSM-5沸石，可以更好地分散活性物种并提高稳定性[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]。

4. 氧化脱氢：探索ODH-O2和CO2辅助PDH（CO2-PDH），以克服热力学限制并减少积碳形成[10.1016/j.jcou.2019.09.018]。

5. 化学循环氧化脱氢（CL-ODH）：利用能够选择性氧化产生的氢气的氧化还原催化剂，可能提供一种适度的脱氢技术[10.1039/D0CS00814A]。

这些创新方法已经带来了催化剂性能的显著改善。例如，以高硅HZSM-5为载体的ZnO催化剂在反应10小时后仍表现出高丙烷转化率（43%）和优异的丙烯选择性（93%）[10.1016/j.mcat.2019.110508]。同样，V基催化剂在多个反应-再生循环中表现出显著的长期稳定性[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：丙烷脱氢催化剂的发展代表了朝着更可持续、更高效地满足全球日益增长的丙烯需求迈出的重要一步。新型催化剂和创新设计策略的开发不仅解决了传统PDH催化剂的局限性，还为工艺优化和工业实施开辟了新的可能性。

展望未来，PDH催化剂设计领域可能会在几个关键领域继续取得进展：

1. 进一步探索非贵金属催化剂：随着研究的进展，我们可能会看到新的催化材料的出现，这些材料以较低的成本提供与铂基催化剂相当或更优的性能。

2. 多策略集成：未来的催化剂设计可能会结合多种方法，如单原子催化、载体工程和助剂添加，以创造协同效应，提高整体性能和稳定性。

3. 原位再生技术：开发可无缝集成到PDH工艺中的先进再生方法，最大限度地减少停机时间并延长催化剂寿命。

4. 计算机模拟和机器学习：利用这些工具预测催化剂行为，优化配方，并加速新材料的发现。

5. 规模放大和工艺强化：随着有前景的催化剂从实验室规模向工业应用过渡，研究将关注克服与大规模生产和工艺集成相关的挑战。

6. 可持续性考虑：未来的催化剂设计可能会更加强调使用丰富、无毒的材料，并开发具有较小环境影响的工艺。

7. 混合技术：将PDH与其他新兴技术（如膜反应器或先进分离技术）相结合，以进一步提高效率和选择性。

这些PDH催化剂技术的进步将在塑造丙烯生产的未来中发挥关键作用，有可能彻底改变石油化工行业，并为更可持续的化学品制造工艺做出贡献。
