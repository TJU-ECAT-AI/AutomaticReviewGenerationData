Selective oxygenate reforming involves the conversion of biomass-derived oxygenates, such as ethanol, glycerol, and sorbitol, into hydrogen and syngas. The process typically occurs through catalytic reactions, often utilizing bimetallic catalysts on various supports. Key mechanisms include:
