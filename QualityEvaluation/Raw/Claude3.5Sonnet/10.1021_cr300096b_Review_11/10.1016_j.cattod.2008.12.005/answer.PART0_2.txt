Selective oxygenate reforming contributes to sustainable energy and chemical production in several ways:

1. It produces hydrogen for fuel cells, enabling clean energy generation.

2. When using biomass-derived oxygenates, the process can be CO2 neutral, as the CO2 byproduct is consumed by future biomass growth.

3. The oxygenate reactants have low toxicity and reactivity, making them safer and easier to transport and store compared to other hydrogen sources.

4. It allows for the production of hydrogen from renewable biomass resources rather than fossil fuels.

Key advantages include:

- Potentially carbon-neutral when using biomass feedstocks
- Safer handling of reactants due to low toxicity/reactivity 
- Compatible with existing transportation/storage infrastructure
- Enables production of clean hydrogen fuel from renewable sources

While the text doesn't provide direct comparisons to traditional methods, the ability to produce hydrogen in a potentially carbon-neutral way from renewable biomass, with relatively safe reactants, represents significant environmental and practical advantages over fossil fuel-based hydrogen production methods.

- 能够从可再生资源中生产清洁氢燃料
