The literature provides insights into the influence of support materials (specifically SiO2 and Al2O3) and CO2 partial pressure on chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions:

1. Support material influence:
- Cr/SiO2 showed enhanced activity with increasing CO2 partial pressure.
- Cr/Al2O3 exhibited reduced activity with the addition of even small amounts of CO2.

2. Reaction conditions (CO2 partial pressure):
- Increasing CO2 partial pressure enhanced the activity of Cr/SiO2.
- Even small amounts of CO2 remarkably reduced the activity of Cr/Al2O3.

3. Mechanistic pathways:
- For both supports, the redox cycle between Cr(III)O6 and Cr(VI)O4 played an important role in the dehydrogenation of C3H6.
- CO2 could oxidize Cr(III) species to Cr(VI) species under reaction conditions.
- In the case of Cr/SiO2, both simple dehydrogenation over Cr(III) species and oxidative dehydrogenation over Cr(VI) species occurred.

4. Challenges and trade-offs:
- CO2 adsorption on Al2O3 inhibited C3H8 adsorption, reducing catalyst activity.
- The choice of support material significantly impacts the catalyst's response to CO2, with SiO2 showing benefits and Al2O3 showing drawbacks.

The study does not provide information on other support materials mentioned in the question (MCM-41, H-ZSM-5, ZrO2, and CeO2), nor does it discuss selectivity or stability explicitly. Further research would be needed to fully address all aspects of the question.
