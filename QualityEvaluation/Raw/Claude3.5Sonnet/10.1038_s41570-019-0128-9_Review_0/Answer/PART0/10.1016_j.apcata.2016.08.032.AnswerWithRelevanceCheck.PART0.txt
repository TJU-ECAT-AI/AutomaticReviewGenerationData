1. Support material impact: SiO2-supported Cr2O3 shows superior performance compared to other supports like Al2O3, TiO2, and ZrO2, achieving higher and more stable ethane conversion (around 50%) and ethene selectivity (>90%).

2. Mechanism: The high activity of Cr2O3/SiO2 is attributed to its redox properties and acidity/basicity characteristics.

3. Promoters: Adding acidic and basic oxide promoters to Cr2O3/SiO2 further improves its catalytic activity.

4. Comparison with other catalysts: Cr2O3-based catalysts show superior activity compared to Ga2O3-based catalysts for CO2-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of ethane (ODHE). Ga2O3-based catalysts show lower initial ethane conversion (20-30%) and ethene selectivity (80-90%), which decrease over time.

Key challenges and trade-offs include:
1. Balancing activity and selectivity while ensuring catalyst stability over time.
2. Optimizing support material choice and potential promoters to enhance performance.
3. Maintaining high activity and selectivity while minimizing deactivation.
