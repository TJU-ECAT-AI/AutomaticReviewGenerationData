The literature provides limited information on the influence of various support materials and reaction conditions on chromium oxide catalysts in CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation reactions. The study focused on Cr2O3 catalysts supported on Al2O3, active carbon, and SiO2 for the dehydrogenation of propane to propene.

Among these supports, only SiO2-supported Cr2O3 catalyst showed promoting effects when carbon dioxide was added to the reaction. The addition of CO2 enhanced the yield of propene and suppressed catalyst deactivation for the Cr2O3/SiO2 catalyst.

However, the literature does not provide information on other support materials like MCM-41, H-ZSM-5, ZrO2, and CeO2. It also does not discuss the influence of CO2 partial pressure or other reaction conditions on the catalyst performance.

The study does not provide detailed information on the mechanistic pathways, key challenges, or trade-offs in optimizing the overall performance of these catalysts.
