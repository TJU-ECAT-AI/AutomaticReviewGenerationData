1. Support materials significantly influence catalyst activity. TiO2, Al2O3, and ZrO2 supports led to high activity for Ga2O3 catalysts, while SiO2 and MgO supports were ineffective.

2. CO2 can have both promoting and inhibiting effects depending on the support. It promoted activity for Ga2O3/TiO2 but suppressed activity for Ga2O3/ZrO2 and Ga2O3/Al2O3.

3. CO2 plays dual roles: positively by removing adsorbed H2 through the reverse water-gas shift reaction, and negatively by competing with propane for adsorption sites.

4. The CO2/C3H8 ratio affects performance, with an optimal ratio existing (e.g. 2 for Ga2O3/TiO2) beyond which activity decreases.

5. The paper mentions that silica-supported Cr2O3 shows a promoting effect with CO2, suggesting support and CO2 effects may be similar for chromium oxide catalysts.
