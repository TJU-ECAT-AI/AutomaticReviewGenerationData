The literature provides some insights into how different supports and conditions affect chromium oxide catalysts for CO2 oxidative dehydrogenation, though it does not comprehensively address all aspects of the question:

Support materials: The paper focuses on Cr/TS-1 catalysts with varying Si/Ti ratios, showing that the support composition affects catalyst activity and selectivity. It mentions that Cr/SiO2 catalysts also show excellent performance.

CO2 effects: The presence of CO2 slightly improves catalytic performance compared to inert gas, promoting ethylene yields. CO2 participates in the water-gas shift reaction and may help maintain chromium in higher oxidation states.

Stability and regeneration: The stability of Cr/TS-1 catalysts was investigated, showing some deactivation over time. However, activity could be restored through oxygen treatment.

Key challenges: The trade-off between activity and selectivity is apparent, with more active catalysts showing lower ethylene selectivity at higher temperatures. Catalyst deactivation is also a challenge.
