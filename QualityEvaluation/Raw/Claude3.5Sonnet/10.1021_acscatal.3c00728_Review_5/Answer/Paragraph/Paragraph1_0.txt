Comprehensive analysis of B-based catalysts for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP): Multi-dimensional design of active sites, reaction mechanisms, and implications for future catalyst development

The systematic study of B-based catalysts for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) has significantly enhanced our understanding of catalytic processes, particularly in the realm of propane dehydrogenation. This research has provided crucial insights into the catalytic mechanisms of tricoordinated B species with different dimensional geometric configurations, offering effective guidance for the development of more efficient ODHP catalysts in the future [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_5].

A key aspect of this research is the emphasis on the spatial configuration and dispersion of active sites, as well as the synergism between surface catalytic cycles and gas-phase reactions. These factors are of paramount importance for catalyst design and performance optimization, especially in reactions dominated by radical pathways such as ODHP [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_5]. The multi-dimensional design of active sites has proven to be a critical factor in enhancing catalyst efficiency and selectivity.

Despite significant advancements in constructing different configurations of active sites, several technical challenges persist that hinder the further development of B-based catalysts. These include the conversion-selectivity trade-off, limited radical initiation ability at low temperatures, the role of B-based catalysts in terminating active radicals, insufficient understanding of gas-phase radical reaction mechanisms, and issues with catalyst stability and boric acid leaching [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_5].

The implications of this research for developing more efficient ODHP catalysts are profound. By addressing these challenges and focusing on key aspects of catalyst design, such as the spatial configuration of active sites and the interplay between surface and gas-phase reactions, researchers can potentially improve the catalytic performance of B-based materials. This progress may open up new opportunities for the practical application of oxidative dehydrogenation techniques in the production of low olefins, which is a crucial industrial process [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_5].

These future directions could potentially lead to breakthroughs in ODHP catalyst design, bringing us closer to more efficient and sustainable production of low olefins in industrial applications.

全面分析用于丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）的含硼催化剂：活性位点的多维设计、反应机理及其对未来催化剂开发的启示

对用于丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）的含硼催化剂的系统研究显著提高了我们对催化过程的理解，特别是在丙烷脱氢领域。这项研究为具有不同维度几何构型的三配位硼物种的催化机理提供了重要见解，为未来开发更高效的ODHP催化剂提供了有效指导 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_5]。

这项研究的一个关键方面是强调活性位点的空间构型和分散性，以及表面催化循环和气相反应之间的协同作用。这些因素对催化剂设计和性能优化至关重要，尤其是在以自由基途径为主导的反应中，如ODHP [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_5]。活性位点的多维设计已被证明是提高催化剂效率和选择性的关键因素。

尽管在构建不同活性位点构型方面取得了重大进展，但仍存在几个技术挑战，阻碍了含硼催化剂的进一步发展。这些挑战包括转化率-选择性权衡、低温下有限的自由基引发能力、含硼催化剂在终止活性自由基中的作用、对气相自由基反应机理的理解不足，以及催化剂稳定性和硼酸流失等问题 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_5]。

这项研究对开发更高效的ODHP催化剂具有深远的影响。通过解决这些挑战并关注催化剂设计的关键方面，如活性位点的空间构型和表面与气相反应之间的相互作用，研究人员有可能改善含硼材料的催化性能。这一进展可能为氧化脱氢技术在低烯烃生产中的实际应用开辟新机遇，这是一个关键的工业过程 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00728_5]。
