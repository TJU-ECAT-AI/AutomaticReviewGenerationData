<English>
Hierarchical zeolites are advanced materials that combine micropores and mesopores/macropores to enhance accessibility for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions. These materials offer significant improvements over traditional zeolites by addressing limitations in mass transfer and diffusion while maintaining the intrinsic catalytic properties of microporous structures.

Types and Synthesis Methods:
1. Micro-mesoporous zeolites: Contain micropores and mesopores
2. Micro-macroporous zeolites: Contain micropores and macropores
3. Micro-meso-macroporous zeolites: Contain micropores, mesopores, and macropores [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]

Synthesis methods can be broadly categorized into two approaches:
1. Post-treatment synthetic routes: These involve selective desilication or dealumination processes to create larger meso- or macropores by sacrificing part of the zeolite crystals [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016].
2. In situ synthetic routes: These use organic or inorganic templates during zeolite crystallization [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016].

Specific synthesis methods include:
- Desilication: Treatment of ZSM-5 with NaOH solution under mild conditions [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
- Self-assembly: Enwrapping zeolite crystals with ordered mesoporous silica (OMS) [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
- Use of structure-directing agents: For example, tetra-(n-butyl)phosphonium hydroxide (TBPOH) for hierarchical silicalite-1 nanosheets [10.3390/catal9020174]
- Repetitive branching synthesis strategy [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
- Utilization of long-chain alkyl structure-directing agents [10.1039/D0CS01260B]

Performance Improvements in PDH Reactions:
Hierarchical zeolites significantly enhance PDH reaction performance compared to traditional zeolites:

1. Enhanced accessibility: The combination of micropores and mesopores/macropores improves accessibility to active sites [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016].

2. Improved mass transfer: The hierarchical structure accelerates mass transport and reduces diffusion limitations [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016].

3. Higher conversion and selectivity: For example, PtSn supported on hierarchical ZSM-5 exhibits an initial propane conversion of 50% and propylene selectivity of 95%, compared to 30% conversion and 65% selectivity for parent microporous ZSM-5 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5].

4. Increased catalytic activity: Pt catalysts supported on hierarchical S-1 zeolites show a much higher turnover frequency (TOF) of 1112.2 h⁻¹ compared to 157.6 h⁻¹ for bulk S-1 supports [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5].

5. Better metal dispersion: The mesoporous structure improves the dispersion of metal species due to an increased number of defect sites [10.3390/catal9020174].

6. Enhanced stability: The hierarchical structure helps maintain catalyst stability over longer periods by preventing metal sintering and coking [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020].

7. Tailored acidity: Hierarchical zeolites can be designed with specific acid site distributions, such as lower concentrations of Brønsted acid sites and more Lewis acid sites, leading to improved PDH performance [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5].

8. Confinement effect: Mesopores can confine and stabilize metal particles, allowing them to remain stable at high temperatures during the reaction [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020].

In summary, hierarchical zeolites offer a promising approach to improving PDH reaction performance by combining the benefits of microporous and mesoporous/macroporous structures. These materials address the limitations of traditional zeolites while maintaining their intrinsic catalytic properties, resulting in higher conversion rates, improved selectivity, and enhanced catalyst stability.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of hierarchical zeolites for PDH reactions represents a significant advancement in catalyst design, addressing key challenges in traditional zeolite catalysts. The ability to fine-tune the pore structure, acidity, and metal dispersion opens up new possibilities for optimizing PDH catalysts.

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Developing more precise synthesis methods to control the hierarchical structure and tailor it to specific reaction requirements.

2. Exploring novel combinations of metals and hierarchical zeolites to further enhance catalytic performance and stability.

3. Investigating the long-term stability and regeneration of hierarchical zeolite catalysts under industrial conditions.

4. Applying the principles of hierarchical zeolite design to other important industrial reactions beyond PDH.

5. Integrating advanced characterization techniques to gain deeper insights into the structure-performance relationships of hierarchical zeolites.

6. Exploring the potential of hierarchical zeolites in multifunctional catalysis, where different types of active sites can be incorporated into a single catalyst system.

7. Developing scalable and cost-effective synthesis methods for large-scale production of hierarchical zeolites.

These advancements could lead to more efficient and sustainable chemical processes, not only in propylene production but also in a wide range of industrial applications.

<Chinese>
分级沸石是一种先进材料，它结合了微孔和介孔/大孔，以增强丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应的可及性。与传统沸石相比，这些材料在解决传质和扩散限制的同时保持了微孔结构的固有催化特性，从而提供了显著的改进。

类型和合成方法：
1. 微-介孔沸石：含有微孔和介孔
2. 微-大孔沸石：含有微孔和大孔
3. 微-介-大孔沸石：含有微孔、介孔和大孔 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]

合成方法可以大致分为两种方法：
1. 后处理合成路线：涉及选择性脱硅或脱铝过程，通过牺牲部分沸石晶体来创建更大的介孔或大孔 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]。
2. 原位合成路线：在沸石结晶过程中使用有机或无机模板 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]。

具体的合成方法包括：
- 脱硅：在温和条件下用NaOH溶液处理ZSM-5 [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
- 自组装：用有序介孔二氧化硅（OMS）包裹沸石晶体 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
- 使用结构导向剂：例如，用四-(正丁基)膦hydroxide（TBPOH）合成分级硅铝酸盐-1纳米片 [10.3390/catal9020174]
- 重复分支合成策略 [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
- 利用长链烷基结构导向剂 [10.1039/D0CS01260B]

PDH反应性能改进：
与传统沸石相比，分级沸石显著提高了PDH反应性能：

1. 增强可及性：微孔和介孔/大孔的组合改善了对活性位点的可及性 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]。

2. 改善传质：分级结构加速了传质并减少了扩散限制 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]。

3. 更高的转化率和选择性：例如，负载在分级ZSM-5上的PtSn展现出50%的初始丙烷转化率和95%的丙烯选择性，相比之下，母体微孔ZSM-5的转化率为30%，选择性为65% [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]。

4. 提高催化活性：负载在分级S-1沸石上的Pt催化剂显示出更高的转化频率（TOF），为1112.2 h⁻¹，而块状S-1载体的TOF仅为157.6 h⁻¹ [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]。

5. 更好的金属分散：介孔结构由于缺陷位点的增加而改善了金属物种的分散 [10.3390/catal9020174]。

6. 增强稳定性：分级结构通过防止金属烧结和积碳帮助维持催化剂的长期稳定性 [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]。

7. 定制酸性：分级沸石可以设计具有特定酸位分布，如较低浓度的Brønsted酸位和更多的Lewis酸位，从而改善PDH性能 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]。

8. 限域效应：介孔可以限制和稳定金属颗粒，使其在反应的高温条件下保持稳定 [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]。

总之，分级沸石通过结合微孔和介孔/大孔结构的优势，为改善PDH反应性能提供了一种有前景的方法。这些材料解决了传统沸石的局限性，同时保持其固有的催化特性，从而实现了更高的转化率、改善的选择性和增强的催化剂稳定性。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：分级沸石在PDH反应中的发展代表了催化剂设计的重大进步，解决了传统沸石催化剂的关键挑战。能够精细调节孔结构、酸性和金属分散性为优化PDH催化剂开辟了新的可能性。

未来研究方向可能集中在：

1. 开发更精确的合成方法，以控制分级结构并使其适应特定反应要求。

2. 探索新型金属和分级沸石的组合，以进一步提高催化性能和稳定性。

3. 研究分级沸石催化剂在工业条件下的长期稳定性和再生。

4. 将分级沸石设计原理应用于PDH以外的其他重要工业反应。

5. 整合先进表征技术，以深入了解分级沸石的结构-性能关系。

6. 探索分级沸石在多功能催化中的潜力，其中不同类型的活性位点可以被整合到单一催化剂系统中。

7. 开发可规模化和成本效益的合成方法，以实现分级沸石的大规模生产。

这些进展可能导致更高效和可持续的化学过程，不仅在丙烯生产中，而且在广泛的工业应用中都有潜力。
