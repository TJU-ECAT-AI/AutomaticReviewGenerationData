<English>
Hierarchical zeolites combine micropores and mesopores/macropores to enhance accessibility for PDH reactions by creating a multi-scale porous structure. This structure improves mass transfer, diffusion of reactants and products, and maintains the intrinsic catalytic properties of the zeolite while providing additional benefits.

The main types of hierarchical zeolites include:
1. Micro-mesoporous zeolites
2. Micro-macroporous zeolites
3. Micro-meso-macroporous zeolites
4. Mesoporous zeolites (e.g., hierarchical ZSM-5, silicalite-1 nanosheets)
5. Hierarchical zeolite composites (e.g., TS-1@SBA-16)
6. Dealuminated zeolites (e.g., beta zeolite)

Synthesis methods for hierarchical zeolites include:
1. Post-treatment synthetic routes:
   a. Desilication (e.g., NaOH treatment of ZSM-5) [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
   b. Dealumination
   c. Selective etching with alkaline or fluoride agents [10.1002/adma.201803966]
2. In situ synthetic routes:
   a. Use of structure-directing agents (SDAs) like TBPOH for hierarchical silicalite-1 nanosheets [10.3390/catal9020174]
   b. Use of multi-ammonium surfactants (e.g., C18-6-4) [10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]
   c. Self-assembly and enwrapping of zeolite crystals with ordered mesoporous silica [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
3. Repetitive branching synthesis strategy [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
4. Bottom-up and top-down methods [10.1002/adma.201803966]

Hierarchical zeolites improve PDH reaction performance compared to traditional zeolites in several ways:
1. Enhanced accessibility to active sites and improved mass transfer [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
2. Better dispersion and stability of metal species [10.3390/catal9020174]
3. Reduced diffusion limitations and improved catalytic efficiency [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011]
4. Higher propane conversion and propylene selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
5. Extended catalyst lifetime and improved coke resistance [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
6. Confinement effect for stabilizing metal nanoparticles [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
7. Tailored acid site distributions for improved catalytic performance [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]

For example, PtSn supported on hierarchical ZSM-5 exhibits an initial propane conversion of 50% and propylene selectivity of 95%, compared to 30% conversion and 65% selectivity for conventional ZSM-5 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]. Similarly, hierarchical Ga-MFI zeolite catalysts showed 26 times higher conversion and 10-100% increased propylene selectivity compared to bulk Ga-MFI catalysts [10.1039/D0CS01260B].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of hierarchical zeolites represents a significant advancement in catalysis for PDH reactions. By combining the intrinsic properties of microporous zeolites with the enhanced accessibility of meso- and macropores, these materials address many of the limitations associated with traditional zeolites. 

Future research in this field may focus on:
1. Optimizing the balance between micropores and larger pores to maximize catalytic performance while maintaining stability.
2. Developing more precise synthesis methods to control pore size distribution and connectivity.
3. Exploring new combinations of metals and hierarchical zeolites to further enhance PDH performance.
4. Investigating the long-term stability and regeneration capabilities of hierarchical zeolite catalysts under industrial conditions.
5. Applying machine learning and high-throughput screening techniques to rapidly identify optimal hierarchical zeolite structures for specific PDH applications.
6. Exploring the potential of hierarchical zeolites in other catalytic processes beyond PDH, leveraging their unique properties for a wider range of industrial applications.

As our understanding of structure-property relationships in hierarchical zeolites continues to grow, we can expect to see more tailored catalysts that push the boundaries of PDH performance, potentially revolutionizing propylene production in the coming years.

<Chinese>
分级沸石通过创建多尺度孔结构，将微孔与介孔/大孔相结合，以增强PDH反应的可及性。这种结构改善了物质传递、反应物和产物的扩散，同时保持了沸石的固有催化特性，并提供了额外的优势。

主要的分级沸石类型包括：
1. 微孔-介孔沸石
2. 微孔-大孔沸石
3. 微孔-介孔-大孔沸石
4. 介孔沸石（如分级ZSM-5，硅铝酸盐纳米片）
5. 分级沸石复合材料（如TS-1@SBA-16）
6. 脱铝沸石（如β沸石）

分级沸石的合成方法包括：
1. 后处理合成路线：
   a. 脱硅（如NaOH处理ZSM-5）[10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
   b. 脱铝
   c. 碱性或氟化物试剂选择性刻蚀 [10.1002/adma.201803966]
2. 原位合成路线：
   a. 使用结构导向剂（SDA）如TBPOH合成分级硅铝酸盐纳米片 [10.3390/catal9020174]
   b. 使用多铵表面活性剂（如C18-6-4）[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]
   c. 自组装和包裹沸石晶体与有序介孔二氧化硅 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
3. 重复分支合成策略 [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
4. 自下而上和自上而下的方法 [10.1002/adma.201803966]

与传统沸石相比，分级沸石在PDH反应性能方面的改进包括：
1. 增强活性位点的可及性和改善物质传递 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
2. 更好的金属物种分散和稳定性 [10.3390/catal9020174]
3. 降低扩散限制并提高催化效率 [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011]
4. 更高的丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
5. 延长催化剂寿命和改善抗焦性 [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
6. 稳定金属纳米粒子的限域效应 [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
7. 定制的酸位分布以改善催化性能 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]

例如，负载在分级ZSM-5上的PtSn表现出50%的初始丙烷转化率和95%的丙烯选择性，而传统ZSM-5的转化率为30%，选择性为65% [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]。同样，分级Ga-MFI沸石催化剂与块状Ga-MFI催化剂相比，转化率提高了26倍，丙烯选择性提高了10-100% [10.1039/D0CS01260B]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：分级沸石的发展代表了PDH反应催化领域的重大进展。通过将微孔沸石的固有特性与介孔和大孔的增强可及性相结合，这些材料解决了许多与传统沸石相关的限制。

该领域的未来研究可能集中在：
1. 优化微孔和大孔之间的平衡，以最大化催化性能同时保持稳定性。
2. 开发更精确的合成方法，以控制孔径分布和连通性。
3. 探索新的金属和分级沸石组合，进一步提高PDH性能。
4. 研究分级沸石催化剂在工业条件下的长期稳定性和再生能力。
5. 应用机器学习和高通量筛选技术，快速识别特定PDH应用的最佳分级沸石结构。
6. 探索分级沸石在PDH以外其他催化过程中的潜力，利用其独特性质扩大工业应用范围。

随着我们对分级沸石结构-性能关系理解的不断深入，我们可以期待看到更多量身定制的催化剂，推动PDH性能的边界，有可能在未来几年内彻底改变丙烯生产。
