<English>
Hierarchical zeolites combine micropores and mesopores/macropores to enhance accessibility for PDH reactions by creating a multi-scale porous structure. This structure improves mass transfer, diffusion of reactants and products, and maintains the intrinsic catalytic properties of the zeolite. The combination achieves enhanced micropore accessibility without sacrificing the intrinsic confinement effect of micropores [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5].

The main types of hierarchical zeolites for PDH reactions include:
1. Mesoporous zeolites (e.g., hierarchical ZSM-5, silicalite-1 nanosheets)
2. Micro-macroporous zeolites
3. Micro-meso-macroporous zeolites
4. Hierarchical zeolite composites (e.g., TS-1@SBA-16)
5. Dealuminated zeolites (e.g., beta zeolite)

Common synthesis methods for hierarchical zeolites include:
1. Post-treatment synthetic routes: selective desilication/dealumination processes [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
2. In situ synthetic routes: using organic or inorganic templates during zeolite crystallization [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
3. Repetitive branching synthesis strategy [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
4. Utilization of long chain alkyl structure-directing agents [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
5. Self-assembly methods, such as enwrapping zeolite crystals with ordered mesoporous silica [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
6. Using different structure-directing agents (SDAs) like TBPOH for hierarchical silicalite-1 nanosheets and TPAOH for conventional silicalite-1 [10.3390/catal9020174]

Hierarchical zeolites significantly improve PDH reaction performance compared to traditional zeolites:
1. Enhanced accessibility to active sites and improved diffusion of reactants and products [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
2. Higher conversion rates and selectivity: PtSn supported on hierarchical ZSM-5 exhibits an initial propane conversion of 50% and propylene selectivity of 95%, compared to 30% conversion and 65% selectivity for parent microporous ZSM-5 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
3. Improved catalyst stability and lifetime: The confinement effect of mesopores stabilizes metal particles at high temperatures [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
4. Better dispersion of active metal species: The mesoporous system improves metal dispersion due to an increased number of defect sites [10.3390/catal9020174]
5. Reduced coke formation and improved coke accommodation [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
6. Higher total pore volume while maintaining similar micropore volume, indicating additional meso- and macroporosity [10.3390/catal9020174]
7. Tailored acid site distributions: Lower concentrations of Bronsted acid sites and more Lewis acid sites, leading to improved PDH performance [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of hierarchical zeolites represents a significant advancement in catalysis for PDH reactions. By combining the intrinsic properties of microporous zeolites with the enhanced accessibility of meso- and macropores, these materials address many limitations of traditional zeolites. The ability to fine-tune the pore structure, acid site distribution, and metal dispersion opens up new possibilities for catalyst design.

Future research directions may include:
1. Exploring novel synthesis methods to create more precisely controlled hierarchical structures
2. Investigating the synergistic effects of different metal combinations in hierarchical zeolites for PDH reactions
3. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the behavior of hierarchical zeolites under reaction conditions
4. Optimizing the balance between micropores and larger pores to maximize catalytic performance while maintaining stability
5. Extending the application of hierarchical zeolites to other industrially relevant reactions beyond PDH
6. Studying the long-term stability and regeneration of hierarchical zeolite catalysts in continuous PDH processes

As our understanding of structure-property relationships in hierarchical zeolites grows, we can expect to see further improvements in catalyst design, leading to more efficient and sustainable PDH processes in the future.

<Chinese>
分级沸石通过创建多尺度孔结构，将微孔与介孔/大孔相结合，从而增强PDH反应的可及性。这种结构改善了传质、反应物和产物的扩散，同时保持了沸石的固有催化性质。这种组合实现了微孔可及性的增强，而不牺牲微孔的固有限制效应[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]。

用于PDH反应的主要分级沸石类型包括：
1. 介孔沸石（如分级ZSM-5、硅铝酸盐纳米片）
2. 微孔-大孔沸石
3. 微孔-介孔-大孔沸石
4. 分级沸石复合材料（如TS-1@SBA-16）
5. 脱铝沸石（如beta沸石）

分级沸石的常见合成方法包括：
1. 后处理合成路线：选择性脱硅/脱铝过程[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
2. 原位合成路线：在沸石结晶过程中使用有机或无机模板[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
3. 重复分支合成策略[10.1039/D0CS01260B]
4. 利用长链烷基结构导向剂[10.1039/D0CS01260B]
5. 自组装方法，如用有序介孔二氧化硅包裹沸石晶体[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
6. 使用不同的结构导向剂（SDAs），如用TBPOH合成分级硅铝酸盐纳米片，用TPAOH合成传统硅铝酸盐[10.3390/catal9020174]

分级沸石显著改善了PDH反应性能，相比传统沸石：
1. 增强了活性位点的可及性，改善了反应物和产物的扩散[10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
2. 更高的转化率和选择性：PtSn负载在分级ZSM-5上表现出50%的初始丙烷转化率和95%的丙烯选择性，而母体微孔ZSM-5仅为30%的转化率和65%的选择性[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
3. 改善了催化剂稳定性和寿命：介孔的限域效应在高温下稳定金属颗粒[10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
4. 更好的活性金属物种分散：介孔系统由于缺陷位点数量的增加，改善了金属分散[10.3390/catal9020174]
5. 降低了积碳形成，改善了积碳容纳能力[10.1039/D0CS01260B]
6. 更高的总孔体积，同时保持类似的微孔体积，表明存在额外的介孔和大孔[10.3390/catal9020174]
7. 定制的酸位分布：较低浓度的Bronsted酸位和更多的Lewis酸位，导致PDH性能改善[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：分级沸石的发展代表了PDH反应催化领域的重大进步。通过将微孔沸石的固有特性与介孔和大孔的增强可及性相结合，这些材料解决了传统沸石的许多局限性。能够精细调节孔结构、酸位分布和金属分散为催化剂设计开辟了新的可能性。

未来研究方向可能包括：
1. 探索新型合成方法，创建更精确控制的分级结构
2. 研究分级沸石中不同金属组合对PDH反应的协同效应
3. 开发原位表征技术，更好地理解分级沸石在反应条件下的行为
4. 优化微孔和大孔之间的平衡，以最大化催化性能，同时保持稳定性
5. 将分级沸石的应用扩展到PDH之外的其他工业相关反应
6. 研究连续PDH过程中分级沸石催化剂的长期稳定性和再生

随着我们对分级沸石结构-性能关系理解的加深，我们可以期待在催化剂设计方面取得进一步的改进，从而在未来实现更高效、更可持续的PDH过程。
