<English>
Hierarchical zeolites combine micropores and mesopores/macropores to enhance accessibility for PDH reactions by creating a multi-scale porous structure. This structure improves mass transfer, diffusion of reactants and products, and maintains the intrinsic catalytic properties of the zeolite. The combination of different pore sizes achieves enhanced micropore accessibility without sacrificing the intrinsic confinement effect of micropores [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5].

The main types of hierarchical zeolites include:
1. Micro-mesoporous zeolites
2. Micro-macroporous zeolites
3. Micro-meso-macroporous zeolites [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
4. Hierarchical silicalite-1 nanosheets (Si-MFI-NS) [10.3390/catal9020174]
5. Mesoporous MFI zeolites [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011]
6. Self-pillared mesoporous zeolites with nanosheet layers [10.1002/adma.201803966]
7. Hierarchical ZSM-5 [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
8. Composite materials like TS-1@SBA-16 [10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7]

Synthesis methods for hierarchical zeolites include:
1. Post-treatment synthetic routes: selective desilication/dealumination processes [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
2. In situ synthetic routes: using organic or inorganic templates during zeolite crystallization [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
3. Using different structure-directing agents (SDAs) [10.3390/catal9020174]
4. Post-synthetic degallation [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011]
5. Self-pillaring of zeolite nanosheets [10.1002/adma.201803966]
6. Repetitive branching synthesis strategy [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
7. Utilization of long chain alkyl structure-directing agents [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
8. Enwrapping zeolite crystals with mesoporous materials [10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7]

Hierarchical zeolites significantly improve PDH reaction performance compared to traditional zeolites:
1. Enhanced accessibility to active sites [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
2. Improved diffusion of reactants and products [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
3. Reduced coke formation and improved catalyst stability [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
4. Higher conversion rates and selectivity [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
5. Better dispersion of metal species [10.3390/catal9020174]
6. Confinement effect for stabilizing metal particles [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
7. Increased catalytic activity and selectivity [10.1002/adma.201803966]
8. Extended catalyst lifetime [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]

For example, PtSn supported on hierarchical ZSM-5 exhibits an initial propane conversion of 50% and propylene selectivity of 95%, compared to 30% conversion and 65% selectivity for parent microporous ZSM-5 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]. Hierarchical Ga-MFI zeolite catalysts showed 26 times higher conversion and 10-100% increased propylene selectivity compared to bulk Ga-MFI catalysts [10.1039/D0CS01260B].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of hierarchical zeolites represents a significant advancement in catalyst design for PDH reactions. By combining micropores and mesopores/macropores, these materials address the limitations of traditional zeolites while maintaining their intrinsic catalytic properties. The superior performance of hierarchical zeolites in PDH reactions opens up new possibilities for industrial applications and process intensification.

Future research directions may focus on:
1. Optimizing the balance between micropores and mesopores/macropores to further enhance catalytic performance and stability.
2. Developing novel synthesis methods to create more precise and tailored hierarchical structures.
3. Investigating the synergistic effects between hierarchical zeolites and various metal species to create even more efficient PDH catalysts.
4. Exploring the potential of hierarchical zeolites in other catalytic reactions and industrial processes.
5. Studying the long-term stability and regeneration of hierarchical zeolite catalysts under industrial conditions.
6. Combining hierarchical zeolites with other advanced materials or techniques, such as single-atom catalysts or membrane reactors, to push the boundaries of PDH technology.

These future developments could lead to more sustainable and economically viable propylene production processes, addressing the growing demand for this important chemical feedstock.

<Chinese>
分级沸石通过创建多尺度孔结构来结合微孔和介孔/大孔，以提高PDH反应的可及性。这种结构改善了传质、反应物和产物的扩散，并保持了沸石的固有催化性质。不同孔径的组合实现了微孔可及性的提高，同时不牺牲微孔的固有限域效应 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]。

分级沸石的主要类型包括：
1. 微-介孔沸石
2. 微-大孔沸石
3. 微-介-大孔沸石 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
4. 分级硅铝石纳米片（Si-MFI-NS）[10.3390/catal9020174]
5. 介孔MFI沸石 [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011]
6. 具有纳米片层的自支撑介孔沸石 [10.1002/adma.201803966]
7. 分级ZSM-5 [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
8. 复合材料如TS-1@SBA-16 [10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7]

分级沸石的合成方法包括：
1. 后处理合成路线：选择性脱硅/脱铝过程 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
2. 原位合成路线：在沸石结晶过程中使用有机或无机模板 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
3. 使用不同的结构导向剂（SDAs）[10.3390/catal9020174]
4. 后合成脱镓 [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011]
5. 沸石纳米片的自支撑 [10.1002/adma.201803966]
6. 重复分支合成策略 [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
7. 利用长链烷基结构导向剂 [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
8. 用介孔材料包裹沸石晶体 [10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7]

与传统沸石相比，分级沸石显著改善了PDH反应性能：
1. 提高了活性位点的可及性 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
2. 改善了反应物和产物的扩散 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
3. 减少了积碳形成并提高了催化剂稳定性 [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
4. 更高的转化率和选择性 [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
5. 更好的金属物种分散 [10.3390/catal9020174]
6. 限域效应稳定金属颗粒 [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
7. 提高催化活性和选择性 [10.1002/adma.201803966]
8. 延长催化剂寿命 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]

例如，负载在分级ZSM-5上的PtSn表现出50%的初始丙烷转化率和95%的丙烯选择性，而母体微孔ZSM-5的转化率为30%，选择性为65% [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]。分级Ga-MFI沸石催化剂与体相Ga-MFI催化剂相比，转化率提高了26倍，丙烯选择性增加了10-100% [10.1039/D0CS01260B]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：分级沸石的发展代表了PDH反应催化剂设计的重大进步。通过结合微孔和介孔/大孔，这些材料解决了传统沸石的局限性，同时保持了其固有的催化性质。分级沸石在PDH反应中的优异性能为工业应用和过程强化开辟了新的可能性。

未来研究方向可能集中在：
1. 优化微孔和介孔/大孔之间的平衡，以进一步提高催化性能和稳定性。
2. 开发新的合成方法，创造更精确和定制的分级结构。
3. 研究分级沸石与各种金属物种之间的协同效应，以创造更高效的PDH催化剂。
4. 探索分级沸石在其他催化反应和工业过程中的潜力。
5. 研究分级沸石催化剂在工业条件下的长期稳定性和再生。
6. 将分级沸石与其他先进材料或技术结合，如单原子催化剂或膜反应器，以推动PDH技术的发展。

这些未来的发展可能导致更可持续和经济可行的丙烯生产过程，满足这一重要化学原料不断增长的需求。
