<English>
Hierarchical zeolites combine micropores and mesopores/macropores to enhance accessibility for PDH reactions by creating a multi-scale porous structure. This structure improves mass transfer, diffusion of reactants and products, and maintains the intrinsic catalytic properties of the zeolite while providing additional benefits.

The main types of hierarchical zeolites for PDH reactions include:
1. Mesoporous zeolites (e.g., hierarchical ZSM-5, silicalite-1 nanosheets)
2. Macroporous zeolites
3. Micro-mesoporous zeolites
4. Micro-macroporous zeolites
5. Micro-meso-macroporous zeolites
6. Hierarchical zeolite composites (e.g., TS-1@SBA-16)
7. Dealuminated zeolites (e.g., beta zeolite)

Synthesis methods for hierarchical zeolites include:
1. Post-treatment synthetic routes:
   a. Desilication (e.g., NaOH treatment of ZSM-5) [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
   b. Dealumination
   c. Selective etching with alkaline or fluoride agents [10.1002/adma.201803966]
2. In situ synthetic routes:
   a. Using organic or inorganic templates during zeolite crystallization [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
   b. Repetitive branching synthesis strategy [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
   c. Utilization of long-chain alkyl structure-directing agents [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
   d. Self-assembly methods (e.g., enwrapping zeolite crystals with ordered mesoporous silica) [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
3. Bottom-up methods (e.g., using soft or hard templates) [10.1002/adma.201803966]
4. Top-down methods (e.g., post-synthesis treatments like desilication) [10.1002/adma.201803966]
5. Self-pillaring of zeolite nanosheets [10.1002/adma.201803966]

Hierarchical zeolites improve PDH reaction performance compared to traditional zeolites in several ways:
1. Enhanced accessibility to active sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011]
2. Improved diffusion of reactants and products [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
3. Reduced coke formation and improved catalyst stability [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
4. Higher conversion rates and selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
5. Better dispersion of active metal species [10.3390/catal9020174]
6. Decreased acid content, leading to improved coke accommodation [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
7. Modified migration process of coke from active metal sites to supports [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
8. Confinement effect for stabilizing metal nanoparticles [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
9. Increased surface area and pore volume [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]

These improvements result in significantly higher catalytic activity, selectivity, and stability in PDH reactions. For example:
- PtSn supported on hierarchical ZSM-5 exhibits an initial propane conversion of 50% and propylene selectivity of 95%, compared to 30% conversion and 65% selectivity for parent microporous ZSM-5 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
- Hierarchical Ga-MFI zeolite catalysts showed 26 times higher conversion and 10-100% increased propylene selectivity compared to bulk Ga-MFI catalysts [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
- Pt catalysts supported on hierarchical S-1 zeolites exhibit a much higher turnover frequency (TOF) of 1112.2 h−1 compared to bulk S-1 supports (TOF of 157.6 h−1) [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of hierarchical zeolites for PDH reactions represents a significant advancement in catalyst design, addressing many limitations of traditional zeolites. The combination of micropores and meso/macropores offers a synergistic effect that enhances catalytic performance while maintaining the intrinsic properties of zeolites. 

Future research directions may focus on:
1. Optimizing the balance between micropores and meso/macropores to maximize catalytic performance while maintaining stability.
2. Developing new synthesis methods that allow for precise control over pore size distribution and connectivity.
3. Exploring novel metal-zeolite combinations that take full advantage of the hierarchical structure.
4. Investigating the long-term stability and regeneration capabilities of hierarchical zeolite catalysts under industrial conditions.
5. Applying the principles of hierarchical zeolite design to other important catalytic reactions beyond PDH.
6. Utilizing advanced characterization techniques to gain deeper insights into the structure-property relationships of hierarchical zeolites.
7. Incorporating computational modeling to predict and design optimal hierarchical structures for specific catalytic applications.

These research directions could lead to the development of next-generation catalysts with unprecedented activity, selectivity, and stability for PDH reactions and other important industrial processes.

<Chinese>
分级沸石通过创建多尺度孔结构来结合微孔和介孔/大孔，以增强PDH反应的可及性。这种结构改善了传质、反应物和产物的扩散，并维持了沸石固有的催化性能，同时提供了额外的优势。

用于PDH反应的主要分级沸石类型包括：
1. 介孔沸石（如分级ZSM-5、硅铝酸盐纳米片）
2. 大孔沸石
3. 微-介孔沸石
4. 微-大孔沸石
5. 微-介-大孔沸石
6. 分级沸石复合材料（如TS-1@SBA-16）
7. 脱铝沸石（如beta沸石）

分级沸石的合成方法包括：
1. 后处理合成路线：
   a. 脱硅（如ZSM-5的NaOH处理）[10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
   b. 脱铝
   c. 碱性或氟化物试剂的选择性刻蚀[10.1002/adma.201803966]
2. 原位合成路线：
   a. 在沸石结晶过程中使用有机或无机模板[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
   b. 重复分支合成策略[10.1039/D0CS01260B]
   c. 利用长链烷基结构导向剂[10.1039/D0CS01260B]
   d. 自组装方法（如用有序介孔二氧化硅包裹沸石晶体）[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
3. 自下而上方法（如使用软或硬模板）[10.1002/adma.201803966]
4. 自上而下方法（如脱硅等后合成处理）[10.1002/adma.201803966]
5. 沸石纳米片自支撑[10.1002/adma.201803966]

分级沸石通过以下几种方式改善PDH反应性能，相比传统沸石：
1. 增强活性位点的可及性[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011]
2. 改善反应物和产物的扩散[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
3. 减少积炭形成并提高催化剂稳定性[10.1039/D0CS01260B]
4. 更高的转化率和选择性[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
5. 更好的活性金属物种分散[10.3390/catal9020174]
6. 降低酸含量，导致改善积炭容纳[10.1039/D0CS01260B]
7. 改变积炭从活性金属位点到载体的迁移过程[10.1039/D0CS01260B]
8. 稳定金属纳米粒子的限域效应[10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
9. 增加表面积和孔体积[10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]

这些改进导致PDH反应中显著提高的催化活性、选择性和稳定性。例如：
- 负载在分级ZSM-5上的PtSn表现出50%的初始丙烷转化率和95%的丙烯选择性，相比之下，母体微孔ZSM-5的转化率为30%，选择性为65%[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
- 分级Ga-MFI沸石催化剂显示出比块状Ga-MFI催化剂高26倍的转化率和10-100%增加的丙烯选择性[10.1039/D0CS01260B]
- 负载在分级S-1沸石上的Pt催化剂表现出更高的转换频率（TOF）1112.2 h−1，相比之下，块状S-1载体的TOF为157.6 h−1 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]

综合理解与未来展望：用于PDH反应的分级沸石的开发代表了催化剂设计的重大进步，解决了传统沸石的许多局限性。微孔和介孔/大孔的结合提供了协同效应，在保持沸石固有性质的同时提高了催化性能。

未来的研究方向可能集中在：
1. 优化微孔和介孔/大孔之间的平衡，以最大化催化性能，同时保持稳定性。
2. 开发新的合成方法，允许精确控制孔径分布和连通性。
3. 探索充分利用分级结构的新型金属-沸石组合。
4. 研究分级沸石催化剂在工业条件下的长期稳定性和再生能力。
5. 将分级沸石设计原理应用于PDH以外的其他重要催化反应。
6. 利用先进的表征技术，深入了解分级沸石的结构-性能关系。
7. 结合计算模拟来预测和设计特定催化应用的最佳分级结构。

这些研究方向可能导致开发出具有前所未有的活性、选择性和稳定性的下一代催化剂，用于PDH反应和其他重要的工业过程。
