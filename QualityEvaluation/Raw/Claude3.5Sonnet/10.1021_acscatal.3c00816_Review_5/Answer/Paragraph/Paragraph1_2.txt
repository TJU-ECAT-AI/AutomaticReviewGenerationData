<English>
Hierarchical zeolites combine micropores and mesopores/macropores to enhance accessibility for PDH reactions by creating a multi-scale porous structure. This structure improves mass transfer, diffusion of reactants and products, and maintains the intrinsic catalytic properties of the zeolite while providing additional benefits.

The main types of hierarchical zeolites include:
1. Micro-mesoporous zeolites
2. Micro-macroporous zeolites
3. Micro-meso-macroporous zeolites
4. Mesoporous MFI zeolites
5. Self-pillared mesoporous zeolites with nanosheet layers
6. Zeolites with intracrystalline mesopores
7. Hierarchical silicalite-1 nanosheets
8. Hierarchical ZSM-5
9. Hierarchical zeolite composites (e.g., TS-1@SBA-16)
10. Dealuminated zeolites (e.g., beta zeolite)

Synthesis methods for hierarchical zeolites include:
1. Post-treatment synthetic routes:
   a. Desilication (e.g., NaOH treatment of ZSM-5)
   b. Dealumination
   c. Selective etching with alkaline or fluoride agents
2. In situ synthetic routes:
   a. Using organic or inorganic templates during zeolite crystallization
   b. Self-assembly methods
   c. Repetitive branching synthesis strategy
   d. Utilization of long chain alkyl structure-directing agents
3. Bottom-up methods (e.g., using soft or hard templates)
4. Top-down methods (post-synthesis treatments)
5. Self-pillaring of zeolite nanosheets
6. Enwrapping zeolite crystals with ordered mesoporous silica (OMS)

Hierarchical zeolites significantly improve the performance of PDH reactions compared to traditional zeolites:

1. Enhanced accessibility: The introduction of mesopores and macropores improves access to active sites and reduces diffusion limitations [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016].

2. Improved mass transfer: The hierarchical structure accelerates mass transport, reducing the diffusion path of reactants and products [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016].

3. Higher conversion and selectivity: For example, PtSn supported on hierarchical ZSM-5 exhibits an initial propane conversion of 50% and propylene selectivity of 95%, compared to 30% conversion and 65% selectivity for parent microporous ZSM-5 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5].

4. Increased catalytic activity: Hierarchical Pt/S-1 catalysts show a higher Pt-related turnover frequency (TOF) of 1112.2 h⁻¹ compared to 157.6 h⁻¹ for bulk S-1 supports [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5].

5. Better metal dispersion: The mesoporous structure improves the dispersion of metal species due to an increased number of defect sites [10.3390/catal9020174].

6. Enhanced catalyst stability: The confinement effect of mesopores stabilizes metal particles, preventing sintering at high temperatures [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020].

7. Reduced coke formation: Improved diffusion leads to a decrease in cracking reactions and coking [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020].

8. Tailored acidity: Hierarchical zeolites can be designed with specific acid site distributions, optimizing catalytic performance [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5].

9. Extended catalyst lifetime: The improved mass transfer and reduced coking contribute to longer catalyst lifetimes [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016].

10. Synergistic effects: Composite materials like Pt-SnOx/TS-1@SBA-16 show superior performance due to the combination of excellent mass transfer, suitable acidity, and electronic effects of metal species [10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of hierarchical zeolites represents a significant advancement in catalysis for PDH reactions. By combining the intrinsic properties of microporous zeolites with the enhanced accessibility of meso- and macropores, these materials address many of the limitations associated with traditional zeolite catalysts. 

Future research directions may focus on:

1. Optimizing the balance between micropores and larger pores to maximize catalytic performance while maintaining shape selectivity.

2. Developing new synthesis methods that allow for precise control over pore size distribution and interconnectivity.

3. Exploring novel metal-zeolite combinations to further enhance catalytic activity and stability.

4. Investigating the potential of hierarchical zeolites in other industrially relevant reactions beyond PDH.

5. Studying the long-term stability and regeneration of hierarchical zeolite catalysts under industrial conditions.

6. Developing in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the behavior of hierarchical zeolites during catalytic reactions.

7. Exploring the use of hierarchical zeolites in membrane reactors or other process intensification strategies for PDH.

8. Investigating the potential of hierarchical zeolites in multifunctional catalysis, combining PDH with other reactions in a single catalyst system.

These advancements could lead to more efficient and sustainable propylene production processes, potentially revolutionizing the petrochemical industry.

<Chinese>
分级沸石通过创建多尺度孔结构，将微孔与介孔和/或大孔结合，以增强丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应的可及性。这种结构改善了传质、反应物和产物的扩散，同时保持了沸石的固有催化性质，并提供了额外的优势。

主要的分级沸石类型包括：
1. 微孔-介孔沸石
2. 微孔-大孔沸石
3. 微孔-介孔-大孔沸石
4. 介孔MFI沸石
5. 具有纳米片层的自支撑介孔沸石
6. 具有晶内介孔的沸石
7. 分级硅铝酸盐-1纳米片
8. 分级ZSM-5
9. 分级沸石复合材料（如TS-1@SBA-16）
10. 脱铝沸石（如beta沸石）

分级沸石的合成方法包括：
1. 后处理合成路线：
   a. 脱硅（如用NaOH处理ZSM-5）
   b. 脱铝
   c. 用碱性或氟化物试剂选择性刻蚀
2. 原位合成路线：
   a. 在沸石结晶过程中使用有机或无机模板
   b. 自组装方法
   c. 重复分支合成策略
   d. 利用长链烷基结构导向剂
3. 自下而上方法（如使用软或硬模板）
4. 自上而下方法（后合成处理）
5. 沸石纳米片的自支撑
6. 用有序介孔二氧化硅（OMS）包裹沸石晶体

与传统沸石相比，分级沸石显著改善了PDH反应的性能：

1. 增强可及性：引入介孔和大孔改善了对活性位点的接近，减少了扩散限制[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]。

2. 改善传质：分级结构加速了传质，缩短了反应物和产物的扩散路径[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]。

3. 更高的转化率和选择性：例如，负载在分级ZSM-5上的PtSn表现出50%的初始丙烷转化率和95%的丙烯选择性，相比之下，父体微孔ZSM-5的转化率为30%，选择性为65%[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]。

4. 提高催化活性：分级Pt/S-1催化剂显示出更高的Pt相关转换频率（TOF）1112.2 h⁻¹，而块状S-1载体的TOF为157.6 h⁻¹[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]。

5. 更好的金属分散：介孔结构由于缺陷位点的增加，改善了金属物种的分散[10.3390/catal9020174]。

6. 增强催化剂稳定性：介孔的限域效应稳定了金属颗粒，防止在高温下烧结[10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]。

7. 减少积碳形成：改善的扩散导致裂解反应和积碳减少[10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]。

8. 定制酸度：分级沸石可以设计具有特定酸位分布，优化催化性能[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]。

9. 延长催化剂寿命：改善的传质和减少的积碳有助于延长催化剂寿命[10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]。

10. 协同效应：像Pt-SnOx/TS-1@SBA-16这样的复合材料由于优异的传质、适当的酸度和金属物种的电子效应的组合，表现出优越的性能[10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：分级沸石的开发代表了PDH反应催化领域的重大进步。通过将微孔沸石的固有性质与介孔和大孔的增强可及性相结合，这些材料解决了许多与传统沸石催化剂相关的局限性。

未来的研究方向可能集中在：

1. 优化微孔和大孔之间的平衡，以最大化催化性能，同时保持形状选择性。

2. 开发新的合成方法，允许精确控制孔径分布和互连性。

3. 探索新的金属-沸石组合，以进一步增强催化活性和稳定性。

4. 研究分级沸石在PDH之外的其他工业相关反应中的潜力。

5. 研究分级沸石催化剂在工业条件下的长期稳定性和再生。

6. 开发原位表征技术，以更好地理解分级沸石在催化反应过程中的行为。

7. 探索分级沸石在膜反应器或其他PDH过程强化策略中的应用。

8. 研究分级沸石在多功能催化中的潜力，在单一催化剂系统中结合PDH与其他反应。

这些进展可能导致更高效和可持续的丙烯生产工艺，潜在地革新石油化工行业。
