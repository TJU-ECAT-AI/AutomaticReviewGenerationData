<English>
Hierarchical zeolites combine micropores and mesopores/macropores to enhance accessibility for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) reactions by creating a multi-scale porous structure. This structure improves mass transfer, diffusion of reactants and products, and maintains the intrinsic catalytic properties of the zeolite. The combination of micropores and mesopores/macropores achieves enhanced micropore accessibility without sacrificing the intrinsic confinement effect of micropores [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5].

Main types of hierarchical zeolites:
1. Micro-mesoporous zeolites
2. Micro-macroporous zeolites
3. Micro-meso-macroporous zeolites [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
4. Hierarchical silicalite-1 nanosheets (Si-MFI-NS)
5. Mesoporous MFI zeolites
6. Self-pillared mesoporous zeolites with nanosheet layers
7. Zeolites with intracrystalline mesopores [10.1002/adma.201803966]
8. Hierarchical ZSM-5
9. Dealuminated beta zeolite [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
10. TS-1@SBA-16 composite [10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7]

Synthesis methods:
1. Post-treatment synthetic routes: 
   - Selective desilication/dealumination processes [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
   - Alkaline treatment (e.g., NaOH solution) [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
2. In situ synthetic routes:
   - Using organic or inorganic templates during zeolite crystallization [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
   - Using structure-directing agents (SDAs) like tetra-(n-butyl)phosphonium hydroxide (TBPOH) and tetrapropylammonium hydroxide (TPAOH) [10.3390/catal9020174]
3. Repetitive branching synthesis strategy
4. Utilization of long-chain alkyl structure-directing agents [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
5. Self-assembly method (e.g., enwrapping zeolite crystals with ordered mesoporous silica) [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
6. Post-synthetic degallation of mesoporous MFI gallosilicate zeolite [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011]

Improvements in PDH reaction performance:
1. Enhanced accessibility to active sites
2. Improved diffusion of reactants and products
3. Reduced coke formation and improved catalyst stability
4. Higher conversion rates and selectivity
5. Better dispersion of active metal species
6. Decreased acid content, leading to improved coke accommodation
7. Modified migration process of coke from active metal sites to supports [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
8. Accelerated mass transport
9. Reduced diffusion paths for reactants
10. Extended catalyst lifetime [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]

Specific performance improvements:
- PtSn supported on hierarchical ZSM-5 exhibits an initial propane conversion of 50% and propylene selectivity of 95%, compared to 30% conversion and 65% selectivity for parent microporous ZSM-5 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5].
- Pt catalysts supported on hierarchical S-1 zeolites show a turnover frequency (TOF) of 1112.2 h⁻¹, compared to 157.6 h⁻¹ for bulk S-1 supports [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5].
- Hierarchical Ga-MFI zeolite catalysts demonstrated 26 times higher conversion and 10-100% increased propylene selectivity compared to bulk Ga-MFI catalysts [10.1039/D0CS01260B].
- Pt-SnOx/TS-1@SBA-16 achieved an initial propane conversion of 54.5%, higher than catalysts supported on either TS-1 or SBA-16 alone [10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of hierarchical zeolites represents a significant advancement in catalyst design for PDH reactions. By combining micropores and mesopores/macropores, these materials address the limitations of traditional zeolites while maintaining their beneficial properties. The improved mass transfer, enhanced accessibility to active sites, and reduced diffusion limitations contribute to superior catalytic performance.

Future research directions may focus on:
1. Optimizing the balance between micropores and mesopores/macropores to maximize catalytic activity and stability.
2. Developing novel synthesis methods to create hierarchical structures with more precise control over pore size distribution and interconnectivity.
3. Investigating the long-term stability and regeneration capabilities of hierarchical zeolite catalysts under industrial PDH conditions.
4. Exploring the potential of hierarchical zeolites in other catalytic processes beyond PDH, leveraging their unique structural properties.
5. Combining hierarchical zeolites with advanced metal nanoparticle designs to create synergistic effects and further enhance catalytic performance.
6. Studying the impact of hierarchical structures on the formation and stabilization of specific active sites, such as single-atom catalysts or bimetallic nanoparticles.

These advancements could lead to more efficient and sustainable PDH processes, contributing to the broader goal of improving olefin production technologies.

<Chinese>
分层沸石通过创建多尺度多孔结构，将微孔与介孔/大孔相结合，以增强丙烷脱氢（PDH）反应的可及性。这种结构改善了质量传递、反应物和产物的扩散，同时保持了沸石的固有催化性质。微孔和介孔/大孔的结合实现了增强的微孔可及性，而不牺牲微孔的固有限制效应 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]。

分层沸石的主要类型：
1. 微孔-介孔沸石
2. 微孔-大孔沸石
3. 微孔-介孔-大孔沸石 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
4. 分层硅铝酸盐-1纳米片（Si-MFI-NS）
5. 介孔MFI沸石
6. 具有纳米片层的自支撑介孔沸石
7. 具有晶内介孔的沸石 [10.1002/adma.201803966]
8. 分层ZSM-5
9. 脱铝β沸石 [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
10. TS-1@SBA-16复合材料 [10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7]

合成方法：
1. 后处理合成路线：
   - 选择性脱硅/脱铝过程 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
   - 碱处理（如NaOH溶液） [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
2. 原位合成路线：
   - 在沸石结晶过程中使用有机或无机模板 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
   - 使用结构导向剂（SDAs），如四-(正丁基)膦hydroxide（TBPOH）和四丙基氢氧化铵（TPAOH） [10.3390/catal9020174]
3. 重复分支合成策略
4. 利用长链烷基结构导向剂 [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
5. 自组装方法（如用有序介孔二氧化硅包裹沸石晶体） [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
6. 介孔MFI镓硅酸盐沸石的后合成脱镓 [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.011]

PDH反应性能的改进：
1. 提高活性位点的可及性
2. 改善反应物和产物的扩散
3. 减少积碳形成并提高催化剂稳定性
4. 更高的转化率和选择性
5. 更好的活性金属物种分散
6. 降低酸含量，有利于积碳容纳
7. 改变积碳从活性金属位点到载体的迁移过程 [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
8. 加速质量传输
9. 缩短反应物的扩散路径
10. 延长催化剂寿命 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]

具体性能改进：
- PtSn负载在分层ZSM-5上的催化剂表现出50%的初始丙烷转化率和95%的丙烯选择性，相比之下，母体微孔ZSM-5的转化率为30%，选择性为65% [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]。
- 负载在分层S-1沸石上的Pt催化剂表现出1112.2 h⁻¹的转换频率（TOF），而块状S-1载体的TOF为157.6 h⁻¹ [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]。
- 分层Ga-MFI沸石催化剂与块状Ga-MFI催化剂相比，转化率提高了26倍，丙烯选择性提高了10-100% [10.1039/D0CS01260B]。
- Pt-SnOx/TS-1@SBA-16达到了54.5%的初始丙烷转化率，高于单独在TS-1或SBA-16上负载的催化剂 [10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：分层沸石的发展代表了PDH反应催化剂设计的重大进步。通过结合微孔和介孔/大孔，这些材料解决了传统沸石的局限性，同时保持了其有益特性。改善的质量传递、增强的活性位点可及性和减少的扩散限制共同贡献到卓越的催化性能。

未来研究方向可能集中在：
1. 优化微孔和介孔/大孔之间的平衡，以最大化催化活性和稳定性。
2. 开发新型合成方法，创建具有更精确孔径分布和互连性控制的分层结构。
3. 研究分层沸石催化剂在工业PDH条件下的长期稳定性和再生能力。
4. 探索分层沸石在PDH以外其他催化过程中的潜力，利用其独特的结构特性。
5. 将分层沸石与先进的金属纳米粒子设计相结合，创造协同效应并进一步提高催化性能。
6. 研究分层结构对特定活性位点（如单原子催化剂或双金属纳米粒子）形成和稳定化的影响。

这些进展可能导致更高效和可持续的PDH过程，为改进烯烃生产技术的更广泛目标做出贡献。
