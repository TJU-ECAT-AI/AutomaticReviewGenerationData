<English>
Hierarchical zeolites combine micropores and mesopores/macropores to enhance accessibility for PDH reactions by creating a multi-scale porous structure. This structure improves mass transfer, diffusion of reactants and products, and maintains the intrinsic catalytic properties of the zeolite while providing additional benefits.

The main types of hierarchical zeolites include:
1. Mesoporous zeolites (e.g., hierarchical ZSM-5, TS-1@SBA-16)
2. Macroporous zeolites
3. Micro-mesoporous zeolites
4. Micro-macroporous zeolites
5. Micro-meso-macroporous zeolites
6. 2D/layered zeolites
7. Pillared zeolites
8. Self-pillared mesoporous zeolites with nanosheet layers

Synthesis methods for hierarchical zeolites include:
1. Post-treatment synthetic routes:
   a. Desilication (e.g., NaOH treatment of ZSM-5) [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
   b. Dealumination
   c. Post-synthetic degallation [10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]
2. In situ synthetic routes:
   a. Using organic or inorganic templates during zeolite crystallization
   b. Self-assembly (e.g., enwrapping zeolite crystals with ordered mesoporous silica) [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
3. Repetitive branching synthesis strategy [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
4. Utilization of long chain alkyl structure-directing agents [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
5. Using different structure-directing agents (e.g., TBPOH for hierarchical silicalite-1 nanosheets, TPAOH for conventional silicalite-1) [10.3390/catal9020174]
6. Synthesis using multi-ammonium surfactants (e.g., [C18H37N+(Me)2C6H12N+(Me)2C4H9]Br2) [10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]

Hierarchical zeolites improve PDH reaction performance compared to traditional zeolites in several ways:
1. Enhanced accessibility to active sites [10.1002/adma.201803966]
2. Improved diffusion of reactants and products [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
3. Reduced coke formation and improved catalyst stability [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
4. Higher conversion rates and selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
5. Better dispersion of active metal species [10.3390/catal9020174]
6. Decreased acid content, leading to improved coke accommodation [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
7. Modified migration process of coke from active metal sites to supports [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
8. Confinement effect for stabilizing metal nanoparticles [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
9. Increased surface area and pore volume [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
10. Synergistic effects of hierarchical structure, acid acidity, and electronic properties of metal species [10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7]

For example, PtSn supported on hierarchical ZSM-5 exhibits an initial propane conversion of 50% and propylene selectivity of 95%, compared to 30% conversion and 65% selectivity for parent microporous ZSM-5 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]. Hierarchical Ga-MFI zeolite catalysts showed 26 times higher conversion and 10-100% increased propylene selectivity compared to bulk Ga-MFI catalysts [10.1039/D0CS01260B].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The development of hierarchical zeolites represents a significant advancement in catalyst design for PDH reactions. By combining the intrinsic properties of microporous zeolites with the enhanced accessibility of meso- and macropores, these materials address many of the limitations associated with traditional zeolites. The ability to fine-tune the pore structure, acid site distribution, and metal dispersion opens up new possibilities for optimizing catalyst performance.

Future research in this field may focus on:
1. Developing more precise synthesis methods to control the distribution and connectivity of different pore sizes.
2. Exploring novel combinations of metals and hierarchical zeolites to further enhance catalytic activity and stability.
3. Investigating the long-term stability and regeneration capabilities of hierarchical zeolite catalysts under industrial conditions.
4. Utilizing advanced characterization techniques to gain deeper insights into the structure-property relationships of hierarchical zeolites.
5. Expanding the application of hierarchical zeolites to other industrially relevant reactions beyond PDH.
6. Developing scalable and cost-effective synthesis methods for large-scale production of hierarchical zeolites.
7. Exploring the potential of hierarchical zeolites in multifunctional catalysts for cascade reactions.

These advancements could lead to more efficient and sustainable processes for propylene production, potentially reducing energy consumption and improving the overall economics of the PDH process.

<Chinese>
层级沸石通过创建多尺度孔结构，将微孔与介孔和/或大孔结合起来，以增强丙烷脱氢反应的可及性。这种结构改善了传质效果，提高了反应物和产物的扩散速率，同时保持了沸石的固有催化特性，并提供了额外的优势。

层级沸石的主要类型包括：
1. 介孔沸石（如层级ZSM-5，TS-1@SBA-16）
2. 大孔沸石
3. 微-介孔沸石
4. 微-大孔沸石
5. 微-介-大孔沸石
6. 二维/层状沸石
7. 柱撑沸石
8. 具有纳米片层的自支撑介孔沸石

层级沸石的合成方法包括：
1. 后处理合成路线：
   a. 脱硅（如用NaOH处理ZSM-5）[10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
   b. 脱铝
   c. 后合成脱镓 [10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]
2. 原位合成路线：
   a. 在沸石晶化过程中使用有机或无机模板
   b. 自组装（如用有序介孔二氧化硅包裹沸石晶体）[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
3. 重复分支合成策略 [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
4. 利用长链烷基结构导向剂 [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
5. 使用不同的结构导向剂（如TBPOH用于层级硅铝石纳米片，TPAOH用于常规硅铝石）[10.3390/catal9020174]
6. 使用多铵表面活性剂合成（如[C18H37N+(Me)2C6H12N+(Me)2C4H9]Br2）[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]

与传统沸石相比，层级沸石在几个方面改善了丙烷脱氢反应的性能：
1. 增强了对活性位点的可及性 [10.1002/adma.201803966]
2. 改善了反应物和产物的扩散 [10.1021/acs.chemrev.0c00016]
3. 减少了积碳形成，提高了催化剂稳定性 [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
4. 更高的转化率和选择性 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]
5. 更好的活性金属物种分散 [10.3390/catal9020174]
6. 降低酸性含量，提高积碳容纳能力 [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
7. 改变了积碳从活性金属位点到载体的迁移过程 [10.1039/D0CS01260B]
8. 对金属纳米粒子的限域效应，提高稳定性 [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
9. 增加了表面积和孔体积 [10.1016/j.cjche.2021.11.020]
10. 层级结构、酸性和金属物种电子特性的协同效应 [10.1007/s40242-019-9120-7]

例如，PtSn负载在层级ZSM-5上表现出50%的初始丙烷转化率和95%的丙烯选择性，而母体微孔ZSM-5的转化率为30%，选择性为65% [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816_5]。层级Ga-MFI沸石催化剂与块状Ga-MFI催化剂相比，转化率提高了26倍，丙烯选择性提高了10-100% [10.1039/D0CS01260B]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：层级沸石的发展代表了丙烷脱氢反应催化剂设计的重大进步。通过将微孔沸石的固有特性与介孔和大孔的增强可及性相结合，这些材料解决了与传统沸石相关的许多限制。能够精细调节孔结构、酸位分布和金属分散度，为优化催化剂性能开辟了新的可能性。

该领域未来的研究可能集中在：
1. 开发更精确的合成方法，以控制不同孔径的分布和连通性。
2. 探索金属和层级沸石的新组合，进一步提高催化活性和稳定性。
3. 研究层级沸石催化剂在工业条件下的长期稳定性和再生能力。
4. 利用先进的表征技术，深入了解层级沸石的结构-性能关系。
5. 将层级沸石的应用扩展到丙烷脱氢以外的其他工业相关反应。
6. 开发可规模化和具有成本效益的合成方法，用于层级沸石的大规模生产。
7. 探索层级沸石在多功能催化剂中用于级联反应的潜力。

这些进展可能导致丙烯生产过程更加高效和可持续，潜在地减少能耗并改善丙烷脱氢过程的整体经济性。
