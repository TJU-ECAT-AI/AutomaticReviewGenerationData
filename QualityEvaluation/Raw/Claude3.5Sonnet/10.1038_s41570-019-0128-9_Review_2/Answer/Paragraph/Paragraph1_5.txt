<English>
The comprehensive characterization and comparison of active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques have provided valuable insights into their structural, chemical, and morphological properties at atomic and nanoscale levels. These techniques synergistically contribute to our understanding of the catalysts in several ways:

1. Structural Properties:
In situ X-ray diffraction (XRD) experiments reveal the structural information of both catalysts under reaction conditions, showing the formation of metallic NiFe alloy with fcc structure [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. The XRD patterns also demonstrate the reversible structural evolution of the catalysts under different gas environments and temperatures [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

2. Oxidation States:
In situ X-ray absorption near-edge structure (XANES) spectroscopy determines the valence states of Ni and Fe. In both catalysts, Ni exists as Ni0, while Fe sites exhibit features between FeO and Fe3O4, closer to Fe3O4 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]. XANES spectra at the Fe K-edge indicate that Fe is present as a mixture of Fe0 and Fe2+ species in both catalysts, with a higher proportion of Fe0 in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

3. Local Coordination and Particle Size:
Extended X-ray absorption fine structure (EXAFS) fitting results provide information on the coordination numbers and bond lengths, indicating the formation of NiFe alloy particles with diameters of 2.5-4.0 nm on both catalysts [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. EXAFS analysis at the Fe K-edge shows that Fe-Fe coordination numbers are higher in Fe3Ni1/CeO2, suggesting larger Fe-rich particles [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

4. Spatial Distribution and Morphology:
Ex situ scanning transmission electron microscopy with electron-energy-loss spectroscopy (STEM-EELS) analysis confirms the formation of amorphous FeOx thin layers on CeO2 and provides spatial distribution information of different elements [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. Annular dark-field scanning transmission electron microscopy (ADF-STEM) with EELS mapping demonstrates the spatial distribution and morphology of metal nanoparticles on the ceria support [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2].

Key differences and implications:

1. A 2-nm amorphous FeOx layer is clearly observed on CeO2 particles in Ni1Fe3/CeO2, while such a layer is absent in Ni3Fe1/CeO2 [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. This difference leads to the formation of distinct interfacial sites: NiFeOx in Ni1Fe3/CeO2 and NiCeOx in Ni3Fe1/CeO2.

2. Fe3Ni1/CeO2 has a higher proportion of Fe0 and larger Fe-rich particles compared to Fe1Ni3/CeO2, which could affect catalytic activity and selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

3. The spatial distribution and morphology of Fe and Ni species on the CeO2 support differ between the two catalysts, potentially influencing their performance [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

4. In situ XANES measurements during CO2 hydrogenation show dynamic changes in Fe oxidation state, with partial oxidation occurring under reaction conditions [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

These findings have significant implications for catalyst performance and design, demonstrating that the ratio of Ni to Fe can be used to control the formation of different interfacial sites, thereby tuning the selectivity of the catalyst towards either dehydrogenation or reforming reactions [10.1073/pnas.1806950115].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive characterization of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced analytical techniques has provided a deep understanding of their structure-function relationships. This knowledge opens up new avenues for catalyst design and optimization. Future research endeavors might focus on:

1. Tailoring interfacial sites: By fine-tuning the composition and preparation methods, researchers could create catalysts with optimized interfacial sites for specific reactions, such as CO2-assisted dehydrogenation or dry reforming.

2. In operando studies: Developing more advanced in operando techniques to study the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation states under realistic reaction conditions could provide further insights into the catalytic mechanisms.

3. Rational design of multi-component catalysts: The insights gained from these bimetallic catalysts could be extended to design more complex multi-component catalysts with enhanced performance and stability.

4. Computational modeling: Integrating experimental results with advanced computational modeling could help predict and design catalysts with desired properties and performance.

5. Scale-up and industrial application: Translating the fundamental understanding gained from these studies to the development of industrially relevant catalysts for large-scale applications in CO2 utilization and hydrocarbon conversion processes.

<Chinese>
使用先进的原位和非原位分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂的活性位点进行全面表征和比较，在原子和纳米尺度上提供了关于其结构、化学和形态特性的宝贵见解。这些技术以多种方式协同贡献于我们对催化剂的理解：

1. 结构特性：
原位X射线衍射（XRD）实验揭示了两种催化剂在反应条件下的结构信息，显示了具有面心立方结构的金属NiFe合金的形成[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。XRD图谱还展示了催化剂在不同气体环境和温度下的可逆结构演变[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

2. 氧化态：
原位X射线吸收近边结构（XANES）光谱测定了Ni和Fe的价态。在两种催化剂中，Ni以Ni0形式存在，而Fe位点显示出介于FeO和Fe3O4之间的特征，更接近Fe3O4 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]。Fe K边的XANES光谱表明，在两种催化剂中Fe以Fe0和Fe2+混合物的形式存在，其中Fe3Ni1/CeO2中Fe0的比例更高[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

3. 局部配位和粒径：
扩展X射线吸收精细结构（EXAFS）拟合结果提供了配位数和键长的信息，表明两种催化剂上形成了直径为2.5-4.0 nm的NiFe合金颗粒[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。Fe K边的EXAFS分析显示，Fe3Ni1/CeO2中Fe-Fe配位数更高，表明存在更大的富Fe颗粒[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

4. 空间分布和形态：
非原位扫描透射电子显微镜与电子能量损失谱（STEM-EELS）分析证实了CeO2上非晶态FeOx薄层的形成，并提供了不同元素的空间分布信息[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。环形暗场扫描透射电子显微镜（ADF-STEM）结合EELS映射展示了金属纳米粒子在氧化铈支撑体上的空间分布和形态[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]。

关键差异和影响：

1. 在Ni1Fe3/CeO2的CeO2颗粒上清晰观察到2 nm厚的非晶态FeOx层，而在Ni3Fe1/CeO2中没有这样的层[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。这种差异导致了不同界面位点的形成：Ni1Fe3/CeO2中的NiFeOx和Ni3Fe1/CeO2中的NiCeOx。

2. 与Fe1Ni3/CeO2相比，Fe3Ni1/CeO2具有更高比例的Fe0和更大的富Fe颗粒，这可能影响催化活性和选择性[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

3. 两种催化剂在CeO2支撑体上的Fe和Ni物种的空间分布和形态不同，可能影响它们的性能[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

4. CO2加氢过程中的原位XANES测量显示Fe氧化态发生动态变化，在反应条件下发生部分氧化[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

这些发现对催化剂性能和设计具有重要影响，表明可以通过控制Ni和Fe的比例来形成不同的界面位点，从而调节催化剂对脱氢或重整反应的选择性[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。

综合理解与未来展望：使用先进分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂的全面表征提供了对其结构-功能关系的深入理解。这些知识为催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的途径。未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 定制界面位点：通过微调组成和制备方法，研究人员可以创造出针对特定反应（如CO2辅助脱氢或干重整）优化的界面位点的催化剂。

2. 原位操作研究：开发更先进的原位操作技术，以研究在实际反应条件下催化剂结构和氧化态的动态变化，可以提供关于催化机理的进一步见解。

3. 多组分催化剂的理性设计：从这些双金属催化剂获得的见解可以扩展到设计具有增强性能和稳定性的更复杂的多组分催化剂。

4. 计算机模拟：将实验结果与先进的计算机模拟相结合，可以帮助预测和设计具有所需性能和特性的催化剂。

5. 规模扩大和工业应用：将这些研究获得的基础理解转化为工业相关催化剂的开发，用于CO2利用和碳氢化合物转化过程的大规模应用。
