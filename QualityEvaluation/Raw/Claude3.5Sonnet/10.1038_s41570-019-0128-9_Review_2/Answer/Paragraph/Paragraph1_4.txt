<English>
The comprehensive characterization and comparison of active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques have provided valuable insights into their structural, chemical, and morphological properties at atomic and nanoscale levels. These findings are crucial for understanding the catalytic performance and designing improved catalysts for propane dehydrogenation reactions.

In situ X-ray diffraction (XRD) experiments revealed the structural evolution of both catalysts under reaction conditions, showing the formation of metallic NiFe alloy with fcc structure [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. The reversible structural changes observed under different gas environments and temperatures highlight the dynamic nature of these catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

X-ray absorption near-edge structure (XANES) spectroscopy provided crucial information on the oxidation states of Ni and Fe in both catalysts. Ni was found to exist in the metallic state (Ni0) in both catalysts, while Fe exhibited features between FeO and Fe3O4, closer to Fe3O4 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]. Interestingly, Fe was present as a mixture of Fe0 and Fe2+ species in both catalysts, with a higher proportion of Fe0 in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

Extended X-ray absorption fine structure (EXAFS) analysis provided detailed information on the local coordination environments of Fe and Ni species. The fitting results revealed that metallic NiFe alloy particles with diameters of 2.5-4.0 nm were present in both catalysts [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. Furthermore, EXAFS analysis at the Fe K-edge showed higher Fe-Fe coordination numbers in Fe3Ni1/CeO2, suggesting the presence of larger Fe-rich particles [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

The spatial distribution and morphology of the active sites were elucidated using ex situ annular dark-field scanning transmission electron microscopy (ADF-STEM) coupled with electron energy loss spectroscopy (EELS). This combination of techniques confirmed the formation of amorphous FeOx thin layers on CeO2 in Fe3Ni1/CeO2, while such layers were absent in Fe1Ni3/CeO2 [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. High-resolution micrographs further revealed that the CeO2 particles in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 were in contact with a thin layer of FeOx, which was not observed in Fe1Ni3/CeO2 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2].

The key differences between Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts and their implications for catalyst performance and design are:

1. The presence of a 2-nm amorphous FeOx layer on CeO2 particles in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 leads to the formation of NiFeOx interfacial sites, which are likely active sites for CO2-assisted dehydrogenation of ethane [10.1073/pnas.1806950115].

2. The absence of the FeOx layer in Fe1Ni3/CeO2 results in the dominance of NiCeOx interfacial sites, which are likely active sites for dry reforming of ethane with CO2 [10.1073/pnas.1806950115].

3. Fe3Ni1/CeO2 had a higher proportion of Fe0 and larger Fe-rich particles compared to Fe1Ni3/CeO2, potentially affecting catalytic activity and selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

4. The spatial distribution and morphology of Fe and Ni species on the CeO2 support differed between the two catalysts, influencing their performance [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

These findings demonstrate that the ratio of Ni to Fe can be used to control the formation of different interfacial sites, thereby tuning the selectivity of the catalyst towards either dehydrogenation or reforming reactions [10.1073/pnas.1806950115].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive characterization of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced analytical techniques has provided valuable insights into the structure-function relationships of these materials. This knowledge opens up new avenues for catalyst design and optimization in the field of propane dehydrogenation and related reactions.

Future research efforts might focus on:

1. Developing in operando characterization techniques that combine multiple spectroscopic and microscopic methods to provide real-time information on catalyst structure and performance under reaction conditions.

2. Exploring the use of artificial intelligence and machine learning algorithms to predict optimal catalyst compositions and structures based on the wealth of data generated by these advanced characterization techniques.

3. Investigating the potential of novel synthesis methods, such as atomic layer deposition or controlled precipitation techniques, to fine-tune the spatial distribution and morphology of active sites in bimetallic catalysts.

4. Extending the study to other bimetallic combinations and support materials to develop a broader understanding of the factors influencing catalyst performance in propane dehydrogenation and related reactions.

5. Developing strategies to enhance the stability of the active FeOx layers and prevent sintering of metal nanoparticles during long-term operation, thereby improving catalyst longevity and performance.

These future directions could lead to the development of more efficient and selective catalysts for propane dehydrogenation and related reactions, contributing to the advancement of sustainable chemical processes and energy technologies.

<Chinese>
使用先进的原位和非原位分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂的活性位点进行全面表征和比较，提供了关于其在原子和纳米尺度上的结构、化学和形态特性的宝贵见解。这些发现对于理解催化性能和设计改进的丙烷脱氢催化剂至关重要。

原位X射线衍射（XRD）实验揭示了两种催化剂在反应条件下的结构演变，显示了面心立方结构金属NiFe合金的形成[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。在不同气体环境和温度下观察到的可逆结构变化突出了这些催化剂的动态特性[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

X射线吸收近边结构（XANES）光谱提供了两种催化剂中Ni和Fe氧化态的关键信息。在两种催化剂中，Ni被发现以金属态（Ni0）存在，而Fe表现出介于FeO和Fe3O4之间的特征，更接近Fe3O4 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]。有趣的是，Fe在两种催化剂中都以Fe0和Fe2+物种的混合物存在，其中Fe3Ni1/CeO2中Fe0的比例更高[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

扩展X射线吸收精细结构（EXAFS）分析提供了Fe和Ni物种局部配位环境的详细信息。拟合结果显示，两种催化剂中都存在直径为2.5-4.0 nm的金属NiFe合金颗粒[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。此外，Fe K边的EXAFS分析显示，Fe3Ni1/CeO2中Fe-Fe配位数更高，表明存在较大的富Fe颗粒[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

使用非原位环形暗场扫描透射电子显微镜（ADF-STEM）结合电子能量损失谱（EELS）阐明了活性位点的空间分布和形态。这些技术的组合证实了在Fe3Ni1/CeO2中CeO2上形成了非晶态FeOx薄层，而在Fe1Ni3/CeO2中没有这种层[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。高分辨率显微图进一步揭示，Fe3Ni1/CeO2中的CeO2颗粒与一层薄的FeOx接触，而在Fe1Ni3/CeO2中没有观察到这种现象[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]。

Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂之间的主要差异及其对催化剂性能和设计的影响是：

1. Fe3Ni1/CeO2中CeO2颗粒上存在2 nm非晶态FeOx层，导致形成NiFeOx界面位点，这些位点可能是CO2辅助乙烷脱氢的活性位点[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。

2. Fe1Ni3/CeO2中缺少FeOx层，导致NiCeOx界面位点占主导地位，这些位点可能是CO2干重整乙烷的活性位点[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。

3. 与Fe1Ni3/CeO2相比，Fe3Ni1/CeO2具有更高比例的Fe0和更大的富Fe颗粒，可能影响催化活性和选择性[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

4. 两种催化剂在CeO2载体上Fe和Ni物种的空间分布和形态不同，影响其性能[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

这些发现表明，Ni与Fe的比例可以用来控制不同界面位点的形成，从而调节催化剂对脱氢或重整反应的选择性[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。

综合理解与未来展望：使用先进分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂进行全面表征，为这些材料的结构-功能关系提供了宝贵的见解。这些知识为丙烷脱氢及相关反应领域的催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的途径。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：

1. 开发结合多种光谱和显微方法的原位表征技术，以提供反应条件下催化剂结构和性能的实时信息。

2. 探索使用人工智能和机器学习算法，基于这些先进表征技术产生的大量数据来预测最佳催化剂组成和结构。

3. 研究新型合成方法的潜力，如原子层沉积或受控沉淀技术，以精细调节双金属催化剂中活性位点的空间分布和形态。

4. 将研究扩展到其他双金属组合和载体材料，以更广泛地了解影响丙烷脱氢和相关反应中催化剂性能的因素。

5. 开发策略以增强活性FeOx层的稳定性，防止长期运行过程中金属纳米粒子的烧结，从而提高催化剂的寿命和性能。

这些未来方向可能导致开发出更高效、更选择性的丙烷脱氢和相关反应催化剂，为可持续化学过程和能源技术的发展做出贡献。
