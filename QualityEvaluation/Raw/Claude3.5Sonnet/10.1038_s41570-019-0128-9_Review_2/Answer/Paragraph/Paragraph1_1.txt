<English>
The comprehensive characterization and comparison of active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques have provided crucial insights into their structural, chemical, and morphological properties at atomic and nanoscale levels. These findings have significant implications for catalyst performance and design in propane dehydrogenation reactions.

In situ X-ray diffraction (XRD) experiments revealed the structural information of both catalysts under reaction conditions, demonstrating the formation of metallic NiFe alloy with fcc structure [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. The reversible structural evolution of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts under different gas environments and temperatures was also observed through in situ XRD patterns [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

X-ray absorption near-edge structure (XANES) spectroscopy provided valuable information on the oxidation states of Ni and Fe in both catalysts. In both Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2, Ni exists as Ni0, while Fe sites exhibit features between FeO and Fe3O4, closer to Fe3O4 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]. XANES spectra at the Fe K-edge indicated that Fe was present as a mixture of Fe0 and Fe2+ species in both catalysts, with a higher proportion of Fe0 in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

Extended X-ray absorption fine structure (EXAFS) analysis provided detailed information on the local coordination environments of Fe and Ni species. The coordination numbers of NiNi(Fe) on Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 were 10.4 and 11.3, respectively, indicating that metallic NiFe alloy resided in the form of particles with diameters of 2.5-4.0 nm [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. EXAFS analysis at the Fe K-edge showed that Fe-Fe coordination numbers were higher in Fe3Ni1/CeO2, suggesting larger Fe-rich particles [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

Ex situ scanning transmission electron microscopy with electron-energy-loss spectroscopy (STEM-EELS) analysis confirmed the formation of amorphous FeOx thin layers on CeO2 and provided spatial distribution information of different elements [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. Annular dark-field scanning transmission electron microscopy (ADF-STEM) with EELS mapping demonstrated the spatial distribution and morphology of metal nanoparticles on the ceria support [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2].

Key differences between Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts were observed:

1. A 2-nm amorphous FeOx layer was clearly observed on CeO2 particles in Fe3Ni1/CeO2, while such a layer was absent in Fe1Ni3/CeO2 [10.1073/pnas.1806950115].

2. The presence of the FeOx layer in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 led to the formation of NiFeOx interfacial sites, which were identified as the most likely active sites for CO2-assisted dehydrogenation of ethane [10.1073/pnas.1806950115].

3. The absence of the FeOx layer in Fe1Ni3/CeO2 resulted in the dominance of NiCeOx interfacial sites, which were identified as the most likely active sites for dry reforming of ethane with CO2 [10.1073/pnas.1806950115].

4. Fe3Ni1/CeO2 had a higher proportion of Fe0 and larger Fe-rich particles compared to Fe1Ni3/CeO2 [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

These findings have significant implications for catalyst performance and design. The ratio of Ni to Fe can be used to control the formation of different interfacial sites, thereby tuning the selectivity of the catalyst towards either dehydrogenation or reforming reactions [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. The nanoscale dimensions of the metallic particles (2.5-4.0 nm) highlight the importance of controlling particle size for optimal catalyst performance [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive characterization of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques has provided a deeper understanding of the structure-function relationships in these catalysts. This knowledge enables more targeted and effective catalyst design strategies for specific applications.

Future research directions may include:

1. Exploring the effects of different support materials on the formation and stability of FeOx layers and their impact on catalytic performance.

2. Investigating the dynamic behavior of catalysts under various reaction conditions using in situ and operando techniques to gain insights into the evolution of active sites during catalysis.

3. Developing novel synthesis methods to precisely control the size, composition, and distribution of metal nanoparticles and oxide layers for optimized catalyst performance.

4. Expanding the application of these catalysts to other reactions, such as CO2 hydrogenation or other dehydrogenation processes, leveraging the unique interfacial sites created by different metal ratios.

5. Utilizing advanced computational modeling techniques in conjunction with experimental data to predict and design catalysts with enhanced activity and selectivity for specific reactions.

These future directions could lead to the development of more efficient and selective catalysts for propane dehydrogenation and related reactions, contributing to the advancement of sustainable chemical processes and energy technologies.

<Chinese>
利用先进的原位和非原位分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂活性位点进行全面表征和比较，为我们在原子和纳米尺度上了解其结构、化学和形态特性提供了关键见解。这些发现对丙烷脱氢反应的催化剂性能和设计具有重要意义。

原位X射线衍射（XRD）实验揭示了两种催化剂在反应条件下的结构信息，证明了具有面心立方结构的金属NiFe合金的形成[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。通过原位XRD图谱还观察到Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂在不同气体环境和温度下的可逆结构演变[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

X射线吸收近边结构（XANES）光谱为两种催化剂中Ni和Fe的氧化态提供了宝贵的信息。在Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2中，Ni以Ni0形式存在，而Fe位点表现出介于FeO和Fe3O4之间的特征，更接近Fe3O4 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]。Fe K边的XANES谱图表明，两种催化剂中的Fe以Fe0和Fe2+混合物形式存在，其中Fe3Ni1/CeO2中Fe0的比例更高[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

扩展X射线吸收精细结构（EXAFS）分析提供了Fe和Ni物种局部配位环境的详细信息。Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2上NiNi(Fe)的配位数分别为10.4和11.3，表明金属NiFe合金以直径为2.5-4.0 nm的颗粒形式存在[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。Fe K边的EXAFS分析显示，Fe3Ni1/CeO2中Fe-Fe配位数更高，表明存在更大的富Fe颗粒[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

非原位扫描透射电子显微镜与电子能量损失谱（STEM-EELS）分析证实了在CeO2上形成了无定形FeOx薄层，并提供了不同元素的空间分布信息[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。环形暗场扫描透射电子显微镜（ADF-STEM）与EELS映射展示了金属纳米粒子在氧化铈支撑体上的空间分布和形态[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]。

观察到Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂之间的关键差异：

1. 在Fe3Ni1/CeO2的CeO2颗粒上清晰观察到2 nm厚的无定形FeOx层，而在Fe1Ni3/CeO2中未观察到此层[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。

2. Fe3Ni1/CeO2中FeOx层的存在导致形成NiFeOx界面位点，这被认为是CO2辅助乙烷脱氢的最可能活性位点[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。

3. Fe1Ni3/CeO2中缺少FeOx层导致NiCeOx界面位点占主导地位，这被认为是乙烷与CO2干重整的最可能活性位点[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。

4. 与Fe1Ni3/CeO2相比，Fe3Ni1/CeO2具有更高比例的Fe0和更大的富Fe颗粒[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

这些发现对催化剂性能和设计具有重要意义。Ni与Fe的比例可用于控制不同界面位点的形成，从而调节催化剂对脱氢或重整反应的选择性[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。金属颗粒的纳米尺寸（2.5-4.0 nm）突出了控制颗粒大小对优化催化剂性能的重要性[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：利用先进的原位和非原位分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂进行全面表征，为我们深入了解这些催化剂的结构-功能关系提供了基础。这些知识使我们能够为特定应用开发更有针对性和有效的催化剂设计策略。

未来研究方向可能包括：

1. 探索不同支撑材料对FeOx层形成和稳定性的影响，以及它们对催化性能的影响。

2. 利用原位和操作条件下的技术研究催化剂在各种反应条件下的动态行为，以获得催化过程中活性位点演变的见解。

3. 开发新型合成方法，精确控制金属纳米粒子和氧化物层的尺寸、成分和分布，以优化催化剂性能。

4. 扩大这些催化剂在其他反应中的应用，如CO2加氢或其他脱氢过程，利用不同金属比例创造的独特界面位点。

5. 将先进的计算模拟技术与实验数据结合，预测和设计具有增强活性和选择性的特定反应催化剂。

这些未来方向可能导致开发出更高效、更具选择性的丙烷脱氢及相关反应催化剂，为可持续化学过程和能源技术的进步做出贡献。
