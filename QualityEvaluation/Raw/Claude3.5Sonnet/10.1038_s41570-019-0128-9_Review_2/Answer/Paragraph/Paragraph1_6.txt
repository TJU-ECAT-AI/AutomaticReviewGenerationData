<English>
The comprehensive characterization and comparison of active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques have provided crucial insights into their structural, chemical, and morphological properties at atomic and nanoscale levels. These techniques synergistically contribute to our understanding of the catalysts in several ways:

1. Structural Properties:
In situ X-ray diffraction (XRD) experiments reveal the structural information of both catalysts under reaction conditions, showing the formation of metallic NiFe alloy with fcc structure [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. The XRD patterns also demonstrate reversible structural evolution under different gas environments and temperatures, providing information on the catalysts' dynamic behavior [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

2. Oxidation States:
In situ X-ray absorption near-edge structure (XANES) spectroscopy determines the valence states of Ni and Fe. In both catalysts, Ni exists as Ni0, while Fe sites exhibit features between FeO and Fe3O4, closer to Fe3O4 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]. The XANES spectra at the Fe K-edge indicate that Fe is present as a mixture of Fe0 and Fe2+ species in both catalysts, with a higher proportion of Fe0 in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

3. Local Coordination and Particle Size:
Extended X-ray absorption fine structure (EXAFS) fitting results provide information on the coordination numbers and bond lengths. The analysis reveals that metallic NiFe alloy resides in the form of particles with diameters of 2.5-4.0 nm in both catalysts [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. EXAFS analysis at the Fe K-edge shows higher Fe-Fe coordination numbers in Fe3Ni1/CeO2, suggesting larger Fe-rich particles [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

4. Spatial Distribution and Morphology:
Ex situ scanning transmission electron microscopy with electron-energy-loss spectroscopy (STEM-EELS) analysis confirms the formation of amorphous FeOx thin layers on CeO2 and provides spatial distribution information of different elements [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. Annular dark-field scanning transmission electron microscopy (ADF-STEM) with EELS mapping demonstrates the spatial distribution and morphology of metal nanoparticles on the ceria support [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2].

Key differences and implications for catalyst performance and design:

1. A 2-nm amorphous FeOx layer is clearly observed on CeO2 particles in Ni1Fe3/CeO2, while such a layer is absent in Ni3Fe1/CeO2 [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. This difference leads to the formation of different interfacial sites: NiFeOx in Ni1Fe3/CeO2 and NiCeOx in Ni3Fe1/CeO2, which are responsible for different catalytic behaviors.

2. Fe3Ni1/CeO2 has a higher proportion of Fe0 and larger Fe-rich particles compared to Fe1Ni3/CeO2, which could affect catalytic activity and selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

3. The presence of the FeOx layer in Ni1Fe3/CeO2 leads to the formation of NiFeOx interfacial sites, which are identified as the most likely active sites for CO2-assisted dehydrogenation of ethane (selective C-H bond cleavage to produce ethylene) [10.1073/pnas.1806950115].

4. The absence of the FeOx layer in Ni3Fe1/CeO2 results in the dominance of NiCeOx interfacial sites, which are identified as the most likely active sites for dry reforming of ethane with CO2 (efficient C-C bond scission to produce synthesis gas) [10.1073/pnas.1806950115].

5. In situ XANES measurements during CO2 hydrogenation showed dynamic changes in Fe oxidation state, with partial oxidation occurring under reaction conditions [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]. This highlights the importance of in situ studies for understanding catalyst behavior under operating conditions.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook:
The comprehensive characterization of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques has provided a deep understanding of their structure-function relationships. This knowledge opens up new avenues for catalyst design and optimization in the field of propane dehydrogenation and related reactions.

Future research directions may include:

1. Exploring the tunability of the FeOx layer thickness and its impact on catalyst performance, potentially leading to catalysts with enhanced selectivity and stability.

2. Investigating the dynamic behavior of the catalysts under different reaction conditions to develop more robust and adaptable catalytic systems.

3. Utilizing the insights gained from these studies to design novel multi-component catalysts with tailored interfacial sites for specific reactions.

4. Developing in operando characterization techniques that can provide real-time information on catalyst structure and performance under industrial reaction conditions.

5. Applying similar multi-technique approaches to study other bimetallic catalysts supported on reducible oxides, potentially uncovering new synergistic effects and reaction mechanisms.

These future research directions could lead to the development of more efficient and selective catalysts for propane dehydrogenation and related processes, contributing to the advancement of sustainable chemical production and energy technologies.

<Chinese>
使用先进的原位和非原位分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂的活性位点进行全面表征和比较，为我们提供了关于其在原子和纳米尺度上的结构、化学和形态特性的重要见解。这些技术从以下几个方面协同贡献了对催化剂的理解：

1. 结构特性：
原位X射线衍射（XRD）实验揭示了两种催化剂在反应条件下的结构信息，显示了面心立方结构金属NiFe合金的形成[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。XRD图谱还展示了在不同气体环境和温度下的可逆结构演变，提供了关于催化剂动态行为的信息[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

2. 氧化态：
原位X射线吸收近边结构（XANES）光谱确定了Ni和Fe的价态。在两种催化剂中，Ni以Ni0形式存在，而Fe位点表现出介于FeO和Fe3O4之间的特征，更接近Fe3O4 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]。Fe K边的XANES光谱表明，在两种催化剂中Fe以Fe0和Fe2+混合物的形式存在，其中Fe3Ni1/CeO2中Fe0的比例更高[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

3. 局部配位和粒子尺寸：
扩展X射线吸收精细结构（EXAFS）拟合结果提供了配位数和键长的信息。分析表明，在两种催化剂中，金属NiFe合金以直径为2.5-4.0 nm的颗粒形式存在[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。Fe K边的EXAFS分析显示Fe3Ni1/CeO2中Fe-Fe配位数更高，表明存在更大的富Fe颗粒[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

4. 空间分布和形态：
非原位扫描透射电子显微镜与电子能量损失谱（STEM-EELS）分析证实了在CeO2上形成了无定形FeOx薄层，并提供了不同元素的空间分布信息[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。环形暗场扫描透射电子显微镜（ADF-STEM）与EELS映射展示了金属纳米颗粒在氧化铈载体上的空间分布和形态[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]。

催化剂性能和设计的主要差异和影响：

1. 在Ni1Fe3/CeO2的CeO2颗粒上清晰观察到2 nm的无定形FeOx层，而在Ni3Fe1/CeO2中缺失这样的层[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。这种差异导致形成不同的界面位点：Ni1Fe3/CeO2中的NiFeOx和Ni3Fe1/CeO2中的NiCeOx，这些位点负责不同的催化行为。

2. 与Fe1Ni3/CeO2相比，Fe3Ni1/CeO2具有更高比例的Fe0和更大的富Fe颗粒，这可能影响催化活性和选择性[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

3. Ni1Fe3/CeO2中FeOx层的存在导致形成NiFeOx界面位点，这被认为是CO2辅助乙烷脱氢（选择性C-H键断裂产生乙烯）的最可能活性位点[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。

4. Ni3Fe1/CeO2中FeOx层的缺失导致NiCeOx界面位点占主导地位，这被认为是乙烷与CO2干重整（高效C-C键断裂产生合成气）的最可能活性位点[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。

5. CO2加氢过程中的原位XANES测量显示Fe氧化态发生动态变化，在反应条件下发生部分氧化[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。这突出了原位研究对于理解催化剂在操作条件下行为的重要性。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：
使用先进的原位和非原位分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂进行全面表征，提供了对其结构-功能关系的深入理解。这些知识为丙烷脱氢及相关反应领域的催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的途径。

未来研究方向可能包括：

1. 探索FeOx层厚度的可调性及其对催化剂性能的影响，可能导致具有增强选择性和稳定性的催化剂。

2. 研究催化剂在不同反应条件下的动态行为，以开发更稳健和适应性强的催化系统。

3. 利用这些研究获得的见解设计具有针对特定反应的定制界面位点的新型多组分催化剂。

4. 开发原位表征技术，可以在工业反应条件下提供关于催化剂结构和性能的实时信息。

5. 将类似的多技术方法应用于研究其他以可还原氧化物为载体的双金属催化剂，可能发现新的协同效应和反应机制。

这些未来研究方向可能导致开发出更高效和选择性的丙烷脱氢及相关过程的催化剂，为可持续化学品生产和能源技术的进步做出贡献。
