<English>
The comprehensive characterization and comparison of active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques have provided crucial insights into their structural, chemical, and morphological properties at atomic and nanoscale levels. These techniques synergistically contribute to our understanding of the catalysts in the following ways:

1. Structural Properties:
In situ X-ray diffraction (XRD) experiments reveal the structural information of both catalysts under reaction conditions, showing the formation of metallic NiFe alloy with fcc structure [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. The XRD patterns also demonstrate the reversible structural evolution of the catalysts under different gas environments and temperatures [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

2. Oxidation States:
In situ X-ray absorption near-edge structure (XANES) spectroscopy determines the valence states of Ni and Fe. In both catalysts, Ni exists as Ni0, while Fe sites exhibit features between those of FeO and Fe3O4, closer to Fe3O4 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]. XANES spectra at the Fe K-edge indicate that Fe is present as a mixture of Fe0 and Fe2+ species in both catalysts, with a higher proportion of Fe0 in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

3. Local Coordination and Particle Size:
Extended X-ray absorption fine structure (EXAFS) fitting results provide information on the coordination numbers and bond lengths, indicating the formation of NiFe alloy particles with diameters of 2.5-4.0 nm on both catalysts [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. EXAFS analysis at the Fe K-edge shows that Fe-Fe coordination numbers are higher in Fe3Ni1/CeO2, suggesting larger Fe-rich particles [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

4. Spatial Distribution and Morphology:
Ex situ annular dark-field scanning transmission electron microscopy (ADF-STEM) coupled with electron energy loss spectroscopy (EELS) confirms the formation of amorphous FeOx thin layers on CeO2 and provides spatial distribution information of different elements [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. This technique reveals distinct differences in the morphology of CeO2 particles between Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2].

Key differences and implications:

1. A 2-nm amorphous FeOx layer is clearly observed on CeO2 particles in Fe3Ni1/CeO2, while such a layer is absent in Fe1Ni3/CeO2 [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. This leads to the formation of NiFeOx interfacial sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2, which are identified as the most likely active sites for CO2-assisted dehydrogenation of ethane.

2. The absence of the FeOx layer in Fe1Ni3/CeO2 results in the dominance of NiCeOx interfacial sites, which are identified as the most likely active sites for dry reforming of ethane with CO2 [10.1073/pnas.1806950115].

3. Fe3Ni1/CeO2 has a higher proportion of Fe0 and larger Fe-rich particles compared to Fe1Ni3/CeO2, which could affect catalytic activity and selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

4. In situ XANES measurements during CO2 hydrogenation show dynamic changes in Fe oxidation state, with partial oxidation occurring under reaction conditions [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive characterization of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced analytical techniques has revealed the intricate relationships between catalyst composition, structure, and performance. These findings highlight the importance of precise control over metal ratios and synthesis conditions in tailoring catalyst properties for specific reactions.

Future research directions may include:

1. Exploring the use of in operando techniques to further elucidate the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation states during reactions, providing real-time insights into the active sites and reaction mechanisms.

2. Investigating the effects of different support materials or dopants on the formation and stability of the FeOx layers and interfacial sites, potentially enhancing catalyst performance and longevity.

3. Developing advanced synthesis methods to control the spatial distribution and morphology of metal nanoparticles on the support, optimizing the formation of active interfacial sites.

4. Utilizing machine learning algorithms to analyze and predict catalyst performance based on the comprehensive characterization data, accelerating the discovery and optimization of new catalyst formulations.

5. Extending the application of these characterization techniques to other multi-metallic catalyst systems, broadening our understanding of structure-function relationships in heterogeneous catalysis.

These future directions could lead to the development of more efficient and selective catalysts for a wide range of industrial processes, contributing to the advancement of sustainable chemical production and energy conversion technologies.

<Chinese>
使用先进的原位和非原位分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂活性位点进行全面表征和比较，为我们提供了关于它们在原子和纳米尺度上的结构、化学和形态特性的重要见解。这些技术协同贡献于我们对催化剂的理解，具体表现在以下几个方面：

1. 结构特性：
原位X射线衍射（XRD）实验揭示了两种催化剂在反应条件下的结构信息，显示了面心立方结构的金属NiFe合金的形成 [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。XRD图谱还展示了催化剂在不同气体环境和温度下的可逆结构演变 [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

2. 氧化态：
原位X射线吸收近边结构（XANES）光谱测定了Ni和Fe的价态。在两种催化剂中，Ni以Ni0形式存在，而Fe位点表现出介于FeO和Fe3O4之间的特征，更接近Fe3O4 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]。Fe K边的XANES谱图表明，两种催化剂中的Fe以Fe0和Fe2+物种的混合物形式存在，其中Fe3Ni1/CeO2中Fe0的比例更高 [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

3. 局部配位和粒径：
扩展X射线吸收精细结构（EXAFS）拟合结果提供了配位数和键长信息，表明两种催化剂上形成了直径为2.5-4.0 nm的NiFe合金颗粒 [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。Fe K边的EXAFS分析显示，Fe3Ni1/CeO2中Fe-Fe配位数更高，这表明存在更大的富Fe颗粒 [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

4. 空间分布和形态：
非原位环形暗场扫描透射电子显微镜（ADF-STEM）结合电子能量损失谱（EELS）证实了CeO2上形成了非晶态FeOx薄层，并提供了不同元素的空间分布信息 [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。这种技术揭示了Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2之间CeO2颗粒形态的显著差异 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]。

关键差异和影响：

1. 在Fe3Ni1/CeO2的CeO2颗粒上明显观察到2nm厚的非晶态FeOx层，而在Fe1Ni3/CeO2中没有这样的层 [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。这导致Fe3Ni1/CeO2中形成NiFeOx界面位点，被认为是CO2辅助乙烷脱氢的最可能活性位点。

2. Fe1Ni3/CeO2中缺少FeOx层导致NiCeOx界面位点占主导地位，这些位点被认为是乙烷与CO2干重整的最可能活性位点 [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。

3. 与Fe1Ni3/CeO2相比，Fe3Ni1/CeO2具有更高比例的Fe0和更大的富Fe颗粒，这可能会影响催化活性和选择性 [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

4. 在CO2加氢过程中的原位XANES测量显示Fe氧化态发生动态变化，在反应条件下发生部分氧化 [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

综合理解与未来展望：使用先进分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂的全面表征揭示了催化剂组成、结构和性能之间的复杂关系。这些发现强调了在为特定反应定制催化剂性能时，精确控制金属比例和合成条件的重要性。

未来研究方向可能包括：

1. 探索使用原位操作技术进一步阐明反应过程中催化剂结构和氧化态的动态变化，提供关于活性位点和反应机理的实时洞察。

2. 研究不同支撑材料或掺杂剂对FeOx层和界面位点形成和稳定性的影响，可能提高催化剂性能和寿命。

3. 开发先进的合成方法来控制金属纳米颗粒在支撑体上的空间分布和形态，优化活性界面位点的形成。

4. 利用机器学习算法分析和预测基于综合表征数据的催化剂性能，加速新催化剂配方的发现和优化。

5. 将这些表征技术扩展到其他多金属催化剂系统，拓宽我们对多相催化中结构-功能关系的理解。

这些未来方向可能导致更高效和选择性的催化剂的开发，适用于广泛的工业过程，为可持续化学生产和能源转换技术的进步做出贡献。
