<English>
The comprehensive characterization and comparison of active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques provide crucial insights into their structural, chemical, and morphological properties at atomic and nanoscale levels. These techniques synergistically contribute to our understanding of the catalysts in the following ways:

1. Structural Properties:
In situ X-ray diffraction (XRD) experiments reveal the structural information of both catalysts under reaction conditions, showing the formation of metallic NiFe alloy with fcc structure [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. The XRD patterns also demonstrate the reversible structural evolution of the catalysts under different gas environments and temperatures [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

2. Oxidation States:
In situ X-ray absorption near-edge structure (XANES) spectroscopy determines the valence states of Ni and Fe. In both catalysts, Ni exists as Ni0, while Fe sites exhibit features between FeO and Fe3O4, closer to Fe3O4 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]. XANES spectra at the Fe K-edge indicate that Fe is present as a mixture of Fe0 and Fe2+ species in both catalysts, with a higher proportion of Fe0 in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

3. Local Coordination and Particle Size:
Extended X-ray absorption fine structure (EXAFS) fitting results provide information on the coordination numbers and bond lengths, indicating the formation of NiFe alloy particles with diameters of 2.5-4.0 nm on both catalysts [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. EXAFS analysis at the Fe K-edge shows that Fe-Fe coordination numbers are higher in Fe3Ni1/CeO2, suggesting larger Fe-rich particles [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

4. Spatial Distribution and Morphology:
Ex situ annular dark-field scanning transmission electron microscopy (ADF-STEM) with electron energy loss spectroscopy (EELS) analysis confirms the formation of amorphous FeOx thin layers on CeO2 and provides spatial distribution information of different elements [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. The combination of ADF-STEM and EELS reveals the existence of thin layers of oxidized Fe species on CeO2, supported by the observation of Fe-Ce bonding and the low number of O atoms coordinated to Fe sites [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2].

Key differences and implications:

1. A 2-nm amorphous FeOx layer is clearly observed on CeO2 particles in Ni1Fe3/CeO2, while such a layer is absent in Ni3Fe1/CeO2 [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. This difference leads to the formation of different interfacial sites, with NiFeOx interfacial sites in Ni1Fe3/CeO2 and NiCeOx interfacial sites in Ni3Fe1/CeO2.

2. The presence of the FeOx layer in Ni1Fe3/CeO2 is associated with CO2-assisted dehydrogenation of ethane (selective C-H bond cleavage to produce ethylene), while the absence of this layer in Ni3Fe1/CeO2 is linked to dry reforming of ethane with CO2 (efficient C-C bond scission to produce synthesis gas) [10.1073/pnas.1806950115].

3. Fe3Ni1/CeO2 has a higher proportion of Fe0 and larger Fe-rich particles compared to Fe1Ni3/CeO2, which could affect catalytic activity and selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

4. In situ XANES measurements during CO2 hydrogenation show dynamic changes in Fe oxidation state, with partial oxidation occurring under reaction conditions [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]. This highlights the importance of in situ studies for understanding catalyst behavior under operating conditions.

These findings have significant implications for catalyst performance and design, demonstrating that the ratio of Ni to Fe can be used to control the formation of different interfacial sites, thereby tuning the selectivity of the catalyst towards either dehydrogenation or reforming reactions.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive characterization of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques has provided valuable insights into the structure-function relationships of these materials. Moving forward, this knowledge can be leveraged to design more efficient and selective catalysts for various applications. Future research directions may include:

1. Exploring a wider range of Ni:Fe ratios to fine-tune the catalyst properties and optimize performance for specific reactions.

2. Investigating the effect of different support materials or dopants on the formation and stability of the FeOx layer and its impact on catalytic activity.

3. Developing in situ characterization techniques with higher spatial and temporal resolution to better understand the dynamic changes occurring during catalysis.

4. Applying machine learning algorithms to analyze the complex datasets generated by these advanced characterization techniques, potentially uncovering new structure-property relationships.

5. Extending this multi-technique approach to study other bimetallic catalyst systems, fostering a more comprehensive understanding of heterogeneous catalysis at the atomic and nanoscale levels.

<Chinese>
使用先进的原位和非原位分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂的活性位点进行全面表征和比较，为我们在原子和纳米尺度上了解其结构、化学和形态特性提供了关键见解。这些技术协同对我们理解催化剂有以下贡献：

1. 结构特性：
原位X射线衍射（XRD）实验揭示了两种催化剂在反应条件下的结构信息，显示了面心立方结构的金属NiFe合金的形成[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。XRD图谱还展示了催化剂在不同气体环境和温度下的可逆结构演变[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

2. 氧化态：
原位X射线吸收近边结构（XANES）光谱测定了Ni和Fe的价态。在两种催化剂中，Ni以Ni0形式存在，而Fe位点表现出介于FeO和Fe3O4之间的特征，更接近Fe3O4 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]。Fe K边的XANES谱图表明，两种催化剂中的Fe以Fe0和Fe2+混合物形式存在，其中Fe3Ni1/CeO2中Fe0的比例更高[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

3. 局部配位和粒子尺寸：
扩展X射线吸收精细结构（EXAFS）拟合结果提供了配位数和键长信息，表明两种催化剂上形成了直径为2.5-4.0 nm的NiFe合金粒子[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。Fe K边的EXAFS分析显示，Fe3Ni1/CeO2中Fe-Fe配位数更高，表明存在更大的富Fe粒子[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

4. 空间分布和形态：
非原位环形暗场扫描透射电子显微镜（ADF-STEM）结合电子能量损失谱（EELS）分析证实了CeO2上形成了非晶态FeOx薄层，并提供了不同元素的空间分布信息[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。ADF-STEM和EELS的组合揭示了CeO2上氧化Fe物种薄层的存在，这得到了Fe-Ce键合和Fe位点周围低配位O原子数的观察支持[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]。

主要差异和影响：

1. 在Ni1Fe3/CeO2的CeO2颗粒上清晰观察到2 nm的非晶态FeOx层，而在Ni3Fe1/CeO2中不存在这样的层[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。这种差异导致形成了不同的界面位点，Ni1Fe3/CeO2中形成NiFeOx界面位点，而Ni3Fe1/CeO2中形成NiCeOx界面位点。

2. Ni1Fe3/CeO2中FeOx层的存在与CO2辅助乙烷脱氢（选择性C-H键断裂生成乙烯）有关，而Ni3Fe1/CeO2中该层的缺失与CO2干重整乙烷（高效C-C键断裂生成合成气）有关[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。

3. 与Fe1Ni3/CeO2相比，Fe3Ni1/CeO2具有更高比例的Fe0和更大的富Fe粒子，这可能影响催化活性和选择性[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

4. CO2加氢过程中的原位XANES测量显示Fe氧化态发生动态变化，在反应条件下发生部分氧化[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。这突显了原位研究对于理解催化剂在操作条件下行为的重要性。

这些发现对催化剂性能和设计有重要影响，表明可以通过控制Ni与Fe的比例来控制不同界面位点的形成，从而调节催化剂对脱氢或重整反应的选择性。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：使用先进的原位和非原位分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂进行全面表征，为我们提供了这些材料结构-功能关系的宝贵见解。展望未来，这些知识可以用于设计更高效和选择性的催化剂，应用于各种领域。未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 探索更广泛的Ni:Fe比例范围，以微调催化剂性能并优化特定反应的性能。

2. 研究不同支撑材料或掺杂剂对FeOx层形成和稳定性的影响，以及其对催化活性的影响。

3. 开发具有更高空间和时间分辨率的原位表征技术，以更好地理解催化过程中发生的动态变化。

4. 应用机器学习算法分析这些先进表征技术生成的复杂数据集，可能揭示新的结构-性能关系。

5. 将这种多技术方法扩展到研究其他双金属催化剂系统，促进对原子和纳米尺度上的多相催化的更全面理解。
