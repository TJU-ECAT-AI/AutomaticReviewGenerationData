<English>
The comprehensive characterization and comparison of active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques have provided crucial insights into their structural, chemical, and morphological properties at atomic and nanoscale levels. These techniques synergistically contribute to our understanding of the catalysts in several ways:

1. Structural Properties:
In situ X-ray diffraction (XRD) experiments reveal the structural information of both catalysts under reaction conditions, showing the formation of metallic NiFe alloy with fcc structure [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. The XRD patterns also demonstrate the reversible structural evolution of the catalysts under different gas environments and temperatures [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

2. Oxidation States:
In situ X-ray absorption near-edge structure (XANES) spectroscopy determines the valence states of Ni and Fe. In both catalysts, Ni exists as Ni0, while Fe sites exhibit features between FeO and Fe3O4, closer to Fe3O4 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]. XANES spectra at the Fe K-edge indicate that Fe is present as a mixture of Fe0 and Fe2+ species in both catalysts, with a higher proportion of Fe0 in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

3. Local Coordination and Particle Size:
Extended X-ray absorption fine structure (EXAFS) fitting results provide information on the coordination numbers and bond lengths, indicating the formation of NiFe alloy particles with diameters of 2.5-4.0 nm on both catalysts [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. EXAFS analysis at the Fe K-edge shows that Fe-Fe coordination numbers are higher in Fe3Ni1/CeO2, suggesting larger Fe-rich particles [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

4. Spatial Distribution and Morphology:
Ex situ scanning transmission electron microscopy with electron-energy-loss spectroscopy (STEM-EELS) analysis confirms the formation of amorphous FeOx thin layers on CeO2 and provides spatial distribution information of different elements [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. Annular dark-field scanning transmission electron microscopy (ADF-STEM) with EELS mapping demonstrates the spatial distribution and morphology of metal nanoparticles on the ceria support [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2].

Key differences and implications:

1. A 2-nm amorphous FeOx layer is clearly observed on CeO2 particles in Ni1Fe3/CeO2, while such a layer is absent in Ni3Fe1/CeO2 [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. This difference leads to the formation of different interfacial sites, with NiFeOx interfacial sites in Ni1Fe3/CeO2 and NiCeOx interfacial sites in Ni3Fe1/CeO2.

2. Fe3Ni1/CeO2 has a higher proportion of Fe0 and larger Fe-rich particles compared to Fe1Ni3/CeO2, which could affect catalytic activity and selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

3. The spatial distribution and morphology of Fe and Ni species on the CeO2 support differ between the two catalysts, potentially influencing their performance [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

4. In situ XANES measurements during CO2 hydrogenation reveal dynamic changes in Fe oxidation state, with partial oxidation occurring under reaction conditions [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

These findings have significant implications for catalyst performance and design. The ratio of Ni to Fe can be used to control the formation of different interfacial sites, thereby tuning the selectivity of the catalyst towards either dehydrogenation or reforming reactions [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. The detailed understanding of the catalyst structures and active sites allows for more targeted catalyst design and optimization for specific reactions, such as CO2-ODH or dry reforming of C2H6 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive characterization of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques has provided a deep understanding of their structural, chemical, and morphological properties. This knowledge opens up new avenues for catalyst design and optimization in the field of propane dehydrogenation and related reactions.

Future research directions may include:

1. Exploring a wider range of Fe:Ni ratios to fine-tune the balance between dehydrogenation and reforming activities.

2. Investigating the effect of different support materials on the formation and stability of FeOx layers and their impact on catalytic performance.

3. Developing in operando characterization techniques to monitor the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation states under realistic reaction conditions.

4. Utilizing machine learning algorithms to predict optimal catalyst compositions and structures based on the wealth of data generated by these advanced analytical techniques.

5. Exploring the potential of these bimetallic catalysts in other industrially relevant reactions, such as CO2 utilization or biomass conversion.

By continuing to leverage these advanced characterization techniques and expanding our understanding of structure-function relationships, we can pave the way for the development of more efficient and selective catalysts for sustainable chemical production.

<Chinese>
使用先进的原位和非原位分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂的活性位点进行全面表征和比较，为我们提供了关于其在原子和纳米尺度上的结构、化学和形态特性的重要见解。这些技术以协同方式为我们理解催化剂提供了多方面的信息：

1. 结构特性：
原位X射线衍射（XRD）实验揭示了两种催化剂在反应条件下的结构信息，显示了具有面心立方结构的金属NiFe合金的形成[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。XRD谱图还展示了催化剂在不同气体环境和温度下的可逆结构演变[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

2. 氧化态：
原位X射线吸收近边结构（XANES）光谱测定了Ni和Fe的价态。在两种催化剂中，Ni以Ni0形式存在，而Fe位点的特征介于FeO和Fe3O4之间，更接近Fe3O4 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]。Fe K边的XANES谱图表明，两种催化剂中的Fe以Fe0和Fe2+的混合物形式存在，其中Fe3Ni1/CeO2中Fe0的比例更高[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

3. 局部配位和粒子尺寸：
扩展X射线吸收精细结构（EXAFS）拟合结果提供了配位数和键长的信息，表明两种催化剂上形成了直径为2.5-4.0 nm的NiFe合金粒子[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。Fe K边的EXAFS分析显示，Fe3Ni1/CeO2中Fe-Fe配位数更高，表明存在更大的富Fe粒子[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

4. 空间分布和形态：
非原位扫描透射电子显微镜与电子能量损失谱（STEM-EELS）分析证实了CeO2上非晶FeOx薄层的形成，并提供了不同元素的空间分布信息[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。环形暗场扫描透射电子显微镜（ADF-STEM）与EELS映射展示了金属纳米粒子在氧化铈支持体上的空间分布和形态[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]。

主要差异和影响：

1. 在Ni1Fe3/CeO2的CeO2粒子上清晰观察到2 nm的非晶FeOx层，而在Ni3Fe1/CeO2中该层缺失[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。这种差异导致形成不同的界面位点，Ni1Fe3/CeO2中形成NiFeOx界面位点，而Ni3Fe1/CeO2中形成NiCeOx界面位点。

2. 与Fe1Ni3/CeO2相比，Fe3Ni1/CeO2具有更高比例的Fe0和更大的富Fe粒子，这可能影响催化活性和选择性[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

3. 两种催化剂中Fe和Ni物种在CeO2支持体上的空间分布和形态不同，可能影响其性能[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

4. 在CO2加氢过程中的原位XANES测量显示Fe氧化态发生动态变化，在反应条件下发生部分氧化[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

这些发现对催化剂性能和设计具有重要影响。Ni与Fe的比例可用于控制不同界面位点的形成，从而调节催化剂对脱氢或重整反应的选择性[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。对催化剂结构和活性位点的详细理解允许我们对特定反应（如CO2-ODH或C2H6的干重整）进行更有针对性的催化剂设计和优化[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：利用先进的原位和非原位分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂进行全面表征，为我们提供了对其结构、化学和形态特性的深入理解。这些知识为丙烷脱氢及相关反应领域的催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的途径。

未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 探索更广泛的Fe:Ni比例范围，以精细调节脱氢和重整活性之间的平衡。

2. 研究不同支持材料对FeOx层形成和稳定性的影响，以及它们对催化性能的影响。

3. 开发原位操作表征技术，以监测在实际反应条件下催化剂结构和氧化态的动态变化。

4. 利用机器学习算法，基于这些先进分析技术生成的大量数据，预测最佳催化剂组成和结构。

5. 探索这些双金属催化剂在其他工业相关反应中的潜力，如CO2利用或生物质转化。

通过继续利用这些先进的表征技术并扩展我们对结构-功能关系的理解，我们可以为开发更高效、更选择性的可持续化学品生产催化剂铺平道路。
