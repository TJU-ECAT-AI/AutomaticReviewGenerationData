<English>
The comprehensive characterization and comparison of active sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques have provided valuable insights into their structural, chemical, and morphological properties at atomic and nanoscale levels. These techniques synergistically contribute to our understanding of the catalysts in several ways:

1. Structural Properties:
In situ X-ray diffraction (XRD) experiments reveal the structural information of both catalysts under reaction conditions, demonstrating the formation of metallic NiFe alloy with fcc structure [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. The XRD patterns also show the presence of CeO2 support and metallic Fe and Ni phases in fresh catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]. Furthermore, in situ XRD patterns collected under oxygen and hydrogen environments at different temperatures reveal the reversible structural evolution of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

2. Oxidation States:
In situ X-ray absorption near-edge structure (XANES) spectroscopy determines the valence states of Ni and Fe. In both catalysts, Ni exists as Ni0, while Fe sites exhibit features between those of FeO and Fe3O4, closer to Fe3O4 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]. XANES spectra at the Fe K-edge indicate that Fe is present as a mixture of Fe0 and Fe2+ species in both catalysts, with a higher proportion of Fe0 in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

3. Local Coordination and Particle Size:
Extended X-ray absorption fine structure (EXAFS) fitting results provide information on coordination numbers and bond lengths. The analysis reveals that metallic NiFe alloy resides in the form of particles with diameters of 2.5-4.0 nm in both catalysts [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. EXAFS analysis at the Fe K-edge shows that Fe-Fe coordination numbers are higher in Fe3Ni1/CeO2, suggesting larger Fe-rich particles [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

4. Spatial Distribution and Morphology:
Ex situ scanning transmission electron microscopy with electron-energy-loss spectroscopy (STEM-EELS) analysis confirms the formation of amorphous FeOx thin layers on CeO2 and provides spatial distribution information of different elements [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. Annular dark-field scanning transmission electron microscopy (ADF-STEM) with EELS mapping demonstrates the spatial distribution and morphology of metal nanoparticles on the ceria support [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2].

Key differences and implications:

1. A 2-nm amorphous FeOx layer is clearly observed on CeO2 particles in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 (Ni1Fe3/CeO2), while such a layer is absent in Fe1Ni3/CeO2 (Ni3Fe1/CeO2) [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. This difference leads to the formation of different interfacial sites, with NiFeOx interfacial sites in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and NiCeOx interfacial sites in Fe1Ni3/CeO2.

2. Fe3Ni1/CeO2 has a higher proportion of Fe0 and larger Fe-rich particles compared to Fe1Ni3/CeO2, which could affect catalytic activity and selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

3. The spatial distribution and morphology of Fe and Ni species on the CeO2 support differ between the two catalysts, potentially influencing their performance [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

4. In situ XANES measurements during CO2 hydrogenation show dynamic changes in Fe oxidation state, with partial oxidation occurring under reaction conditions [10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001].

These findings have significant implications for catalyst performance and design. The ratio of Ni to Fe can be used to control the formation of different interfacial sites, thereby tuning the selectivity of the catalyst towards either dehydrogenation or reforming reactions [10.1073/pnas.1806950115]. The detailed understanding of the catalyst structures and active sites allows for more targeted catalyst design and optimization for specific reactions, such as CO2-ODH or dry reforming of C2H6 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 

The comprehensive characterization of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts using advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques has provided a wealth of information about their structural, chemical, and morphological properties. This deep understanding opens up new avenues for catalyst design and optimization in the field of propane dehydrogenation and related reactions.

Future research directions may include:

1. Exploring a wider range of Fe:Ni ratios to fine-tune the balance between dehydrogenation and reforming activities, potentially leading to catalysts with enhanced selectivity and stability.

2. Investigating the effects of different support materials or promoters on the formation and stability of the FeOx layers, which could lead to improved catalyst performance and longevity.

3. Developing in operando characterization techniques that can provide real-time information about the dynamic changes in catalyst structure and oxidation states under reaction conditions, allowing for a better understanding of the reaction mechanisms and catalyst deactivation processes.

4. Utilizing advanced computational modeling techniques, such as density functional theory (DFT) and machine learning, to predict optimal catalyst compositions and structures based on the insights gained from these experimental studies.

5. Exploring the potential of these catalysts in other related reactions, such as the valorization of other light alkanes or the production of value-added chemicals from CO2.

By continuing to leverage these advanced characterization techniques and building upon the fundamental understanding gained from these studies, researchers can work towards developing more efficient, selective, and sustainable catalysts for propane dehydrogenation and related processes, contributing to the broader goals of green chemistry and sustainable energy production.

<Chinese>
使用先进的原位和非原位分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂的活性位点进行全面表征和比较，为我们提供了有关其在原子和纳米尺度上的结构、化学和形态特性的宝贵见解。这些技术以多种方式协同贡献于我们对催化剂的理解：

1. 结构特性：
原位X射线衍射（XRD）实验揭示了两种催化剂在反应条件下的结构信息，证实了具有面心立方结构的金属NiFe合金的形成[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。XRD图谱还显示了新鲜催化剂中CeO2载体和金属Fe和Ni相的存在[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。此外，在不同温度下的氧气和氢气环境中收集的原位XRD图谱揭示了Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂的可逆结构演变[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

2. 氧化态：
原位X射线吸收近边结构（XANES）光谱确定了Ni和Fe的价态。在两种催化剂中，Ni以Ni0形式存在，而Fe位点表现出介于FeO和Fe3O4之间的特征，更接近Fe3O4 [10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]。Fe K边的XANES光谱表明，在两种催化剂中Fe以Fe0和Fe2+的混合物形式存在，其中Fe3Ni1/CeO2中Fe0的比例更高[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

3. 局部配位和粒径：
扩展X射线吸收精细结构（EXAFS）拟合结果提供了配位数和键长信息。分析表明，两种催化剂中的金属NiFe合金以直径为2.5-4.0 nm的颗粒形式存在[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。Fe K边的EXAFS分析显示，Fe3Ni1/CeO2中Fe-Fe配位数更高，表明存在更大的富Fe颗粒[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

4. 空间分布和形态：
非原位扫描透射电子显微镜与电子能量损失谱（STEM-EELS）分析证实了CeO2上非晶态FeOx薄层的形成，并提供了不同元素的空间分布信息[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。环形暗场扫描透射电子显微镜（ADF-STEM）与EELS映射展示了金属纳米颗粒在氧化铈载体上的空间分布和形态[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]。

关键差异和影响：

1. 在Fe3Ni1/CeO2（Ni1Fe3/CeO2）的CeO2颗粒上清晰观察到2 nm厚的非晶态FeOx层，而在Fe1Ni3/CeO2（Ni3Fe1/CeO2）中没有这样的层[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。这种差异导致形成不同的界面位点，Fe3Ni1/CeO2中形成NiFeOx界面位点，而Fe1Ni3/CeO2中形成NiCeOx界面位点。

2. 与Fe1Ni3/CeO2相比，Fe3Ni1/CeO2具有更高比例的Fe0和更大的富Fe颗粒，这可能会影响催化活性和选择性[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

3. Fe和Ni物种在CeO2载体上的空间分布和形态在两种催化剂之间存在差异，可能会影响它们的性能[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

4. CO2加氢过程中的原位XANES测量显示Fe氧化态发生动态变化，在反应条件下发生部分氧化[10.1021/acscatal.6b02991.s001]。

这些发现对催化剂性能和设计有重要影响。Ni与Fe的比例可用于控制不同界面位点的形成，从而调节催化剂对脱氢或重整反应的选择性[10.1073/pnas.1806950115]。对催化剂结构和活性位点的详细了解允许针对特定反应（如CO2-ODH或C2H6的干重整）进行更有针对性的催化剂设计和优化[10.1038/s41570-019-0128-9_2]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望：

使用先进的原位和非原位分析技术对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂进行全面表征，为我们提供了有关其结构、化学和形态特性的丰富信息。这种深入理解为丙烷脱氢及相关反应领域的催化剂设计和优化开辟了新的途径。

未来的研究方向可能包括：

1. 探索更广泛的Fe:Ni比例范围，以微调脱氢和重整活性之间的平衡，可能导致具有增强选择性和稳定性的催化剂。

2. 研究不同载体材料或促进剂对FeOx层形成和稳定性的影响，这可能导致催化剂性能和寿命的改善。

3. 开发能够提供实时信息的原位表征技术，以了解反应条件下催化剂结构和氧化态的动态变化，从而更好地理解反应机理和催化剂失活过程。

4. 利用先进的计算模拟技术，如密度泛函理论（DFT）和机器学习，基于这些实验研究获得的见解预测最佳催化剂组成和结构。

5. 探索这些催化剂在其他相关反应中的潜力，如其他轻烷烃的增值或从CO2生产高附加值化学品。

通过继续利用这些先进的表征技术，并基于这些研究获得的基础理解，研究人员可以致力于开发更高效、更具选择性和更可持续的丙烷脱氢及相关过程的催化剂，为绿色化学和可持续能源生产的更广泛目标做出贡献。
