      <English>
          The advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques synergistically contribute to our understanding of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts in several ways:

1. XRD provides information on the reversible structural evolution of the catalysts under different gas environments and temperatures, as well as identifying crystalline phases in fresh catalysts.

2. XANES and EXAFS analyses offer insights into the oxidation states and local coordination environments of Fe and Ni species during redox treatments. XANES revealed a mixture of Fe0 and Fe2+ species in both catalysts, with a higher proportion of Fe0 in Fe3Ni1/CeO2. EXAFS analysis showed higher Fe-Fe coordination numbers in Fe3Ni1/CeO2, suggesting larger Fe-rich particles.

3. ADF-STEM imaging coupled with EELS mapping demonstrates the spatial distribution and morphology of metal nanoparticles on the ceria support.

4. In situ XANES measurements during CO2 hydrogenation revealed dynamic changes in Fe oxidation state under reaction conditions, showing partial oxidation.

Key differences and implications:

1. Fe3Ni1/CeO2 had a higher proportion of Fe0 and larger Fe-rich particles compared to Fe1Ni3/CeO2, which could affect catalytic activity and selectivity.

2. The spatial distribution and morphology of Fe and Ni species on the CeO2 support differed between the two catalysts, potentially influencing their performance.

3. The observed dynamic changes in Fe oxidation state during reaction conditions highlight the importance of in situ studies for understanding catalyst behavior under operating conditions.

These findings have implications for catalyst design, emphasizing the need to consider both composition and structure in optimizing catalyst performance. The combination of techniques provides a comprehensive understanding of the catalysts' characteristics, allowing researchers to make informed decisions in improving catalyst performance and designing new catalytic systems.

          先进的原位和非原位分析技术协同地为我们理解Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂做出了以下贡献：

1. XRD提供了催化剂在不同气体环境和温度下可逆结构演变的信息，以及识别新鲜催化剂中存在的晶相。

2. XANES和EXAFS分析揭示了Fe和Ni物种在氧化还原处理过程中的氧化态和局部配位环境。XANES显示两种催化剂中Fe0和Fe2+物种的混合，其中Fe3Ni1/CeO2中Fe0的比例更高。EXAFS分析表明Fe3Ni1/CeO2中Fe-Fe配位数更高，暗示存在更大的富Fe颗粒。

3. ADF-STEM成像结合EELS映射展示了金属纳米粒子在氧化铈支撑体上的空间分布和形貌。

4. CO2加氢过程中的原位XANES测量揭示了反应条件下Fe氧化态的动态变化，显示部分氧化发生。

关键差异和影响：

1. 与Fe1Ni3/CeO2相比，Fe3Ni1/CeO2具有更高比例的Fe0和更大的富Fe颗粒，这可能影响催化活性和选择性。

2. 两种催化剂中Fe和Ni物种在CeO2载体上的空间分布和形态不同，可能影响它们的性能。

3. 在反应条件下观察到的Fe氧化态动态变化突出了原位研究对于理解催化剂在操作条件下行为的重要性。

这些发现对催化剂设计有重要影响，强调了在优化催化剂性能时需要同时考虑组成和结构。这些技术的结合提供了对催化剂特性的全面理解，使研究人员能够在改进催化剂性能和设计新的催化系统时做出明智的决定。
