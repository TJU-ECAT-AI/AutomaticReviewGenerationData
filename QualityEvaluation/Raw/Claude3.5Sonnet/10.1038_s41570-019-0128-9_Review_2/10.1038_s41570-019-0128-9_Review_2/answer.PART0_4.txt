The advanced in situ and ex situ analytical techniques synergistically contribute to our understanding of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 catalysts in several ways:

1. Structural properties: In situ synchrotron X-ray diffraction (XRD) provides information on the overall structural properties of both catalysts.

2. Oxidation states: In situ X-ray absorption near edge structure (XANES) reveals the oxidation states of Ni and Fe. Ni exists as Ni0 in both catalysts, while Fe sites show features between FeO and Fe3O4, closer to Fe3O4.

3. Particle size and composition: Extended X-ray absorption fine structure (EXAFS) fitting suggests that metallic Ni or FeNi alloys form particles with diameters of 2.5-4.0 nm.

4. Spatial distribution and morphology: Ex situ annular dark-field scanning transmission electron microscopy (ADF-STEM) with electron energy loss spectroscopy (EELS) corroborates the existence of FeCe bonding and shows that oxidized Fe species exist as thin layers on CeO2.

5. Catalyst-specific differences: High-resolution micrographs and EELS line scan profiles reveal that Fe3Ni1/CeO2 has CeO2 particles in contact with a thin layer of FeOx, which is not observed in Fe1Ni3/CeO2.

These findings have important implications for catalyst performance and design:

1. The presence of Ni0 and partially oxidized Fe species suggests that both metals play crucial roles in the catalytic process, with Ni likely providing active sites for C2H6 activation and Fe contributing to CO2 dissociation.

2. The thin layers of FeOx on CeO2 in Fe3Ni1/CeO2 indicate the formation of unique interfacial sites, which may be responsible for the catalyst's high activity in CO2-ODH of C2H6.

3. The different morphologies of Fe3Ni1/CeO2 and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 explain their distinct catalytic behaviors, with Fe3Ni1/CeO2 favoring CO2-ODH and Fe1Ni3/CeO2 promoting dry reforming.

4. The nanometer-scale particle sizes of Ni or FeNi alloys suggest a high surface area for catalytic activity, which is crucial for efficient catalysis.

5. The identification of specific interfacial sites (NiCeOx and NiFeOx) allows for targeted catalyst design to enhance desired reactions and suppress unwanted side reactions.

These insights enable researchers to fine-tune catalyst composition, morphology, and interfacial structures to optimize performance for specific reactions, such as CO2-ODH or dry reforming of C2H6.

先进的原位和非原位分析技术协同贡献于我们对Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2催化剂的理解，主要体现在以下几个方面：

1. 结构特性：原位同步辐射X射线衍射（XRD）提供了两种催化剂的整体结构特性信息。

2. 氧化态：原位X射线吸收近边结构（XANES）揭示了Ni和Fe的氧化态。两种催化剂中Ni以Ni0形式存在，而Fe位点显示出介于FeO和Fe3O4之间的特征，更接近Fe3O4。

3. 粒子尺寸和组成：扩展X射线吸收精细结构（EXAFS）拟合表明，金属Ni或FeNi合金形成直径为2.5-4.0 nm的粒子。

4. 空间分布和形态：非原位环形暗场扫描透射电子显微镜（ADF-STEM）结合电子能量损失谱（EELS）证实了FeCe键的存在，并显示氧化Fe物种以薄层形式存在于CeO2表面。

5. 催化剂特定差异：高分辨率显微图像和EELS线扫描剖面显示，Fe3Ni1/CeO2中的CeO2粒子与薄层FeOx接触，而Fe1Ni3/CeO2中未观察到这种现象。

这些发现对催化剂性能和设计有重要影响：

1. Ni0和部分氧化Fe物种的存在表明两种金属在催化过程中都起着关键作用，Ni可能提供C2H6活化的活性位点，而Fe有助于CO2解离。

2. Fe3Ni1/CeO2中CeO2表面的FeOx薄层表明形成了独特的界面位点，这可能是该催化剂在C2H6的CO2氧化脱氢反应中表现出高活性的原因。

3. Fe3Ni1/CeO2和Fe1Ni3/CeO2的不同形态解释了它们不同的催化行为，Fe3Ni1/CeO2倾向于CO2氧化脱氢反应，而Fe1Ni3/CeO2则促进干重整反应。

4. Ni或FeNi合金的纳米级粒子尺寸表明催化活性具有高比表面积，这对高效催化至关重要。

5. 特定界面位点（NiCeOx和NiFeOx）的识别允许针对性地设计催化剂，以增强所需反应并抑制不需要的副反应。

这些见解使研究人员能够微调催化剂的组成、形态和界面结构，以优化特定反应（如C2H6的CO2氧化脱氢或干重整）的性能。
