The thermodynamic stability of specific bimetallic structures, particularly in Pt-3d systems with subsurface 3d atoms, plays a crucial role in establishing a strong correlation between model surfaces and supported catalysts in hydrogenation reactions. This stability is observed both in ultra-high vacuum (UHV) environments and under actual hydrogenation reaction conditions, allowing model surfaces to accurately represent the behavior of supported catalysts [10.1021/cr300096b_13].

The desirable bimetallic structures for hydrogenation reactions, featuring subsurface 3d atoms underneath the surface Pt layer, exhibit thermodynamic stability in both UHV environments and under hydrogenation reaction conditions. This stability is the primary reason for the strong correlations observed between model surfaces and supported catalysts [10.1021/cr300096b_13].

However, Pt-based bimetallic catalysts face significant challenges related to the scarcity of Pt-group metals and the stability of surface and subsurface bimetallic structures under reaction conditions [10.1039/c2cs35165j]. At high temperatures, 3d metals deposited on Pt tend to diffuse into the bulk, leading to the destruction of both surface and subsurface bimetallic structures. For instance, Auger Electron Spectroscopy (AES) measurements show that the coverage of 1 monolayer (ML) Ni on Pt(111) decreases upon annealing, reaching less than 0.2 ML at 800 K [10.1039/c2cs35165j].

To address these stability issues, researchers have explored alternative substrates such as tungsten carbide (WC). WC has been found to be a much better diffusion barrier for admetals compared to Pt. In similar annealing experiments, Ni deposited on polycrystalline WC foil showed a less drastic decrease in coverage, maintaining a final coverage of 0.6 ML [10.1039/c2cs35165j]. The similar electronic and chemical properties of Pt and WC suggest that WC can potentially replace Pt in bimetallic systems, producing alternative bimetallic catalysts with lower cost and higher thermal stability [10.1039/c2cs35165j].

The implications of this correlation for designing more effective catalysts using first principles on model surfaces are significant. It demonstrates that insights gained from studying model surfaces can be directly applied to the development of practical supported catalysts. This correlation enables researchers to use computational methods and controlled experiments on model surfaces to predict and optimize the performance of real-world catalysts [10.1021/cr300096b_13].

However, to truly achieve the rational design of catalysts from first principles, it is essential that the model systems closely resemble those under reaction conditions. This is especially critical for bimetallic catalysts due to the potential for adsorbate-induced segregation and diffusion of metal atoms [10.1021/cr300096b_13].

特定双金属结构的热力学稳定性，特别是在表面Pt层下具有亚表面3d原子的Pt-3d系统中，在氢化反应中模型表面和负载催化剂之间的强相关性起着至关重要的作用。这种稳定性在超高真空（UHV）环境和实际氢化反应条件下都能观察到，使模型表面能够准确代表负载催化剂的行为 [10.1021/cr300096b_13]。

对于氢化反应来说，理想的双金属结构（表面Pt层下有亚表面3d原子）在UHV环境和氢化反应条件下都表现出热力学稳定性。这种稳定性是模型表面和负载催化剂之间观察到强相关性的主要原因 [10.1021/cr300096b_13]。

然而，基于Pt的双金属催化剂面临着与Pt族金属稀缺性以及反应条件下表面和亚表面双金属结构稳定性相关的重大挑战 [10.1039/c2cs35165j]。在高温下，沉积在Pt上的3d金属倾向于扩散到体相中，导致表面和亚表面双金属结构的破坏。例如，俄歇电子能谱（AES）测量显示，在Pt(111)上沉积的1个单层（ML）Ni的覆盖率在退火后降低，在800 K时降至不到0.2 ML [10.1039/c2cs35165j]。

为了解决这些稳定性问题，研究人员探索了诸如碳化钨（WC）等替代基底。研究发现，与Pt相比，WC是一种更好的外加金属扩散屏障。在类似的退火实验中，沉积在多晶WC箔上的Ni显示出覆盖率减少不那么剧烈，最终覆盖率保持在0.6 ML [10.1039/c2cs35165j]。Pt和WC相似的电子和化学性质表明，WC可能在双金属系统中替代Pt，产生成本更低、热稳定性更高的替代双金属催化剂 [10.1039/c2cs35165j]。

这种相关性对于使用第一原理在模型表面上设计更有效的催化剂具有重要意义。它表明，从研究模型表面获得的见解可以直接应用于实际负载催化剂的开发。这种相关性使研究人员能够使用计算方法和对模型表面的受控实验来预测和优化实际催化剂的性能 [10.1021/cr300096b_13]。

然而，为了真正实现从第一原理进行催化剂的理性设计，确保模型系统尽可能接近反应条件下的实际情况至关重要。对于双金属催化剂来说，这一点尤为重要，因为存在吸附剂诱导的金属原子分离和扩散的可能性 [10.1021/cr300096b_13]。

综合理解与未来展望：双金属系统，特别是Pt-3d材料，在氢化反应中模型表面和负载催化剂之间的强相关性为催化剂设计开辟了令人兴奋的可能性。未来的研究努力可能集中在：
