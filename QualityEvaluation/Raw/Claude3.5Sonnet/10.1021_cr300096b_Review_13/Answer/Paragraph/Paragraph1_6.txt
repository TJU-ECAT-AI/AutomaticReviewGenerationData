<English>
The thermodynamic stability of specific bimetallic structures, particularly in Pt-3d systems with subsurface 3d atoms, plays a crucial role in establishing a strong correlation between model surfaces and supported catalysts in hydrogenation reactions. This stability is observed both in ultra-high vacuum (UHV) environments and under actual hydrogenation reaction conditions, allowing model surfaces to accurately represent the behavior of supported catalysts [10.1021/cr300096b_13].

The desirable bimetallic structures for hydrogenation reactions, featuring subsurface 3d atoms underneath the surface Pt layer, exhibit thermodynamic stability in both UHV environments and under reaction conditions. This stability is fundamental to the strong correlation between model surfaces and supported catalysts, enabling the application of insights gained from studying model surfaces to the development of practical supported catalysts [10.1021/cr300096b_13].

However, the stability of Pt-3d systems faces challenges at high temperatures. 3d metals tend to diffuse into the bulk Pt, leading to the destruction of both surface and subsurface bimetallic structures. This instability limits the practical application of these model surfaces to real catalytic systems [10.1039/c2cs35165j].

To address this issue, researchers have explored alternative substrates, such as tungsten carbide (WC). WC acts as a much better diffusion barrier for admetals compared to Pt, maintaining a higher coverage of the 3d metal even after high-temperature annealing. This improved stability enhances the applicability of first-principles studies on model surfaces to the design of practical, supported catalysts [10.1039/c2cs35165j].

The implications of this correlation for designing more effective catalysts using first principles on model surfaces are significant. It allows researchers to leverage computational methods and controlled experiments on model surfaces to predict and optimize the performance of real-world catalysts. However, achieving such correlations requires careful consideration of reaction conditions, as the model systems must closely resemble the actual catalysts under reaction conditions to ensure the validity of the predictions [10.1021/cr300096b_13].

The use of WC as a substrate not only improves stability but also provides a pathway to reduce the use of scarce and expensive Pt-group metals while maintaining desirable catalytic properties. This approach opens up possibilities for designing new bimetallic catalysts with unique properties that were previously limited by stability issues [10.1039/c2cs35165j].

对于氢化反应来说，理想的双金属结构（在表面Pt层下具有亚表面3d原子）在UHV环境和反应条件下都表现出热力学稳定性。这种稳定性是模型表面和负载催化剂之间强相关性的基础，使得从研究模型表面获得的见解能够应用于实际负载催化剂的开发 [10.1021/cr300096b_13]。

然而，Pt-3d系统的稳定性在高温下面临挑战。3d金属倾向于扩散到块体Pt中，导致表面和亚表面双金属结构的破坏。这种不稳定性限制了这些模型表面在实际催化系统中的应用 [10.1039/c2cs35165j]。

为解决这个问题，研究人员探索了替代基底，如碳化钨（WC）。与Pt相比，WC作为扩散屏障的效果更好，即使在高温退火后也能保持较高的3d金属覆盖率。这种改进的稳定性增强了第一性原理研究在模型表面上对实际负载催化剂设计的适用性 [10.1039/c2cs35165j]。

这种相关性对于使用第一性原理在模型表面上设计更有效催化剂的影响是重大的。它使研究人员能够利用计算方法和对模型表面的受控实验来预测和优化实际催化剂的性能。然而，实现这种相关性需要仔细考虑反应条件，因为模型系统必须尽可能接近实际反应条件下的催化剂，以确保预测的有效性 [10.1021/cr300096b_13]。

使用WC作为基底不仅提高了稳定性，还提供了一种减少稀有和昂贵的Pt族金属使用的途径，同时保持理想的催化性能。这种方法为设计具有独特性能的新型双金属催化剂开辟了可能性，这些性能在之前受到稳定性问题的限制 [10.1039/c2cs35165j]。
