The thermodynamic stability of specific bimetallic structures, particularly in Pt-3d systems with subsurface 3d atoms, plays a crucial role in establishing a strong correlation between model surfaces and supported catalysts in hydrogenation reactions. This stability is observed both in ultra-high vacuum (UHV) environments and under actual hydrogenation reaction conditions, allowing model surfaces to accurately represent the behavior of supported catalysts [10.1021/cr300096b_13].

The desirable bimetallic structures for hydrogenation reactions, featuring subsurface 3d atoms underneath the surface Pt layer, exhibit thermodynamic stability in both UHV environments and under reaction conditions. This stability is fundamental to the strong correlation between model surfaces and supported catalysts, providing a solid foundation for catalyst design using first principles on model surfaces [10.1021/cr300096b_13].

However, the practical application of Pt-3d systems faces significant challenges due to the scarcity of Pt-group metals and the stability issues of surface and subsurface bimetallic structures under reaction conditions [10.1039/c2cs35165j]. At high temperatures, 3d metals tend to diffuse into the bulk Pt, leading to the destruction of both surface and subsurface bimetallic structures. This instability limits the direct application of these model surfaces to real catalytic systems [10.1039/c2cs35165j].

A promising solution to this stability issue has been found in the use of tungsten carbide (WC) as a substrate. WC acts as a much better diffusion barrier for admetals compared to Pt, maintaining a higher coverage of the 3d metal even after high-temperature annealing. This improved stability significantly enhances the applicability of first-principles studies on model surfaces to the design of practical, supported catalysts [10.1039/c2cs35165j].

The implications for designing more effective catalysts using first principles on model surfaces are significant:

1. Researchers can focus on more stable bimetallic structures, such as those based on WC, to develop model surfaces that more accurately represent the behavior of supported catalysts under real reaction conditions.

2. Both the initial activity and long-term stability of bimetallic structures must be considered in catalyst design.

3. Thermal stability considerations should be incorporated into first-principles studies to better predict the behavior of practical catalysts.

4. The design process should prioritize both the desired catalytic activity and the structural stability of the bimetallic system under reaction conditions.

By addressing these stability issues through careful design of bimetallic structures and choice of substrates, researchers can develop more effective and durable catalysts for hydrogenation reactions and other applications, bridging the gap between model surfaces and practical supported catalysts [10.1039/c2cs35165j].

对于氢化反应来说，理想的双金属结构（表面铂层下具有亚表面3d原子）在UHV环境和反应条件下都表现出热力学稳定性。这种稳定性是模型表面和负载催化剂之间强相关性的基础，为使用第一性原理在模型表面上设计催化剂提供了坚实的基础 [10.1021/cr300096b_13]。

然而，Pt-3d系统的实际应用面临着重大挑战，这是由于铂族金属的稀缺性以及表面和亚表面双金属结构在反应条件下的稳定性问题 [10.1039/c2cs35165j]。在高温下，3d金属倾向于扩散到铂的体相中，导致表面和亚表面双金属结构的破坏。这种不稳定性限制了这些模型表面直接应用于实际催化系统 [10.1039/c2cs35165j]。

使用碳化钨（WC）作为基底材料为解决这一稳定性问题提供了一个有希望的解决方案。与铂相比，WC是一个更好的外加金属扩散屏障，即使在高温退火后也能保持较高的3d金属覆盖率。这种改进的稳定性显著增强了第一性原理研究在模型表面上对实际负载催化剂设计的适用性 [10.1039/c2cs35165j]。

使用第一性原理在模型表面上设计更有效催化剂的影响是显著的：

1. 研究人员可以专注于更稳定的双金属结构，如基于WC的结构，开发更能准确代表实际反应条件下负载催化剂行为的模型表面。

2. 在催化剂设计中必须同时考虑双金属结构的初始活性和长期稳定性。

3. 应将热稳定性考虑纳入第一性原理研究中，以更好地预测实际催化剂的行为。

4. 设计过程应优先考虑双金属系统在反应条件下的所需催化活性和结构稳定性。

通过谨慎设计双金属结构和选择基底材料来解决这些稳定性问题，研究人员可以开发出更有效和耐用的催化剂，用于氢化反应和其他应用，从而缩小模型表面和实际负载催化剂之间的差距 [10.1039/c2cs35165j]。
