The strong correlation between model surfaces and supported catalysts in bimetallic systems, particularly Pt-3d materials, for hydrogenation reactions has significant implications for the design of more effective catalysts using first principles. This correlation is primarily attributed to the thermodynamic stability of specific bimetallic structures, especially those with subsurface 3d atoms underneath the surface Pt layer [10.1021/cr300096b_13].

The stability of these bimetallic structures is crucial in both ultra-high vacuum (UHV) environments and under actual hydrogenation reaction conditions. This stability allows model surfaces to accurately represent the behavior of supported catalysts, enabling researchers to use computational methods and controlled experiments on model surfaces to predict and optimize the performance of real-world catalysts [10.1021/cr300096b_13].

However, the practical application of Pt-3d systems faces challenges due to the scarcity of Pt-group metals and the stability of the surface and subsurface bimetallic structures under reaction conditions [10.1039/c2cs35165j]. At high temperatures, 3d metals tend to diffuse into the bulk Pt, leading to the destruction of both surface and subsurface bimetallic structures. For instance, when 1 monolayer (ML) of Ni is deposited on Pt(111), the Ni coverage decreases upon annealing and reaches a value less than 0.2 ML at 800 K [10.1039/c2cs35165j].

To address these stability issues, researchers have explored alternative substrates such as tungsten carbide (WC). WC has been found to be a much better diffusion barrier for admetals compared to Pt. When Ni is deposited on polycrystalline WC foil, the final coverage after annealing is 0.6 ML, significantly higher than on Pt(111) [10.1039/c2cs35165j]. This improved stability enhances the applicability of first-principles studies on model surfaces to the design of practical, supported catalysts.

The implications for designing more effective catalysts using first principles on model surfaces are significant:

1. Focus on more stable bimetallic structures, such as those based on WC, to develop model surfaces that more accurately represent the behavior of supported catalysts under real reaction conditions.

2. Consider both the initial activity and long-term stability of bimetallic structures when designing catalysts.

3. Incorporate thermal stability considerations in first-principles studies to better predict the behavior of practical catalysts.

4. Prioritize both the desired catalytic activity and the structural stability of the bimetallic system under reaction conditions in catalyst design.

By addressing these stability issues through careful design of bimetallic structures and choice of substrates, researchers can develop more effective and durable catalysts for hydrogenation reactions and other applications, bridging the gap between model surfaces and practical supported catalysts [10.1039/c2cs35165j].

双金属系统，特别是Pt-3d材料，在模型表面和负载催化剂之间的强相关性对于使用第一性原理设计更有效的催化剂具有重要意义。这种相关性主要归因于特定双金属结构的热力学稳定性，尤其是那些表面Pt层下方有3d原子的结构 [10.1021/cr300096b_13]。

这些双金属结构在超高真空（UHV）环境和实际加氢反应条件下的稳定性至关重要。这种稳定性使模型表面能够准确地代表负载催化剂的行为，使研究人员能够使用计算方法和对模型表面的受控实验来预测和优化实际催化剂的性能 [10.1021/cr300096b_13]。

然而，Pt-3d系统的实际应用面临挑战，这是由于Pt族金属的稀缺性以及反应条件下表面和亚表面双金属结构的稳定性问题 [10.1039/c2cs35165j]。在高温下，3d金属倾向于扩散到Pt体相中，导致表面和亚表面双金属结构的破坏。例如，当1个单层（ML）的Ni沉积在Pt(111)上时，Ni覆盖率随退火而降低，在800 K时达到小于0.2 ML的值 [10.1039/c2cs35165j]。

为了解决这些稳定性问题，研究人员探索了诸如碳化钨（WC）等替代基底。WC被发现是比Pt更好的金属扩散屏障。当Ni沉积在多晶WC箔上时，退火后的最终覆盖率为0.6 ML，明显高于Pt(111)上的覆盖率 [10.1039/c2cs35165j]。这种改进的稳定性增强了第一性原理研究在模型表面上对实际负载催化剂设计的适用性。

使用第一性原理在模型表面上设计更有效催化剂的影响是重大的：

1. 关注更稳定的双金属结构，如基于WC的结构，以开发能更准确地代表实际反应条件下负载催化剂行为的模型表面。

2. 在设计催化剂时，考虑双金属结构的初始活性和长期稳定性。

3. 在第一性原理研究中纳入热稳定性考虑，以更好地预测实际催化剂的行为。

4. 在催化剂设计中优先考虑所需的催化活性和反应条件下双金属系统的结构稳定性。

通过仔细设计双金属结构和选择基底来解决这些稳定性问题，研究人员可以开发出更有效和耐用的催化剂，用于加氢反应和其他应用，从而缩小模型表面和实际负载催化剂之间的差距 [10.1039/c2cs35165j]。

综合理解与未来展望：双金属系统，特别是Pt-3d材料，在模型表面和负载催化剂之间的强相关性为未来的研究和催化剂设计开辟了令人兴奋的可能性。使用WC等替代基底不仅解决了稳定性问题，还为减少对稀缺和昂贵的Pt族金属的依赖提供了途径。

未来的研究努力可能集中在：
