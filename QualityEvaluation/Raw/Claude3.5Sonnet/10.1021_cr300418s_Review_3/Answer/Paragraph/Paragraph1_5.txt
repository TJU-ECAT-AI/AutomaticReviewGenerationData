The coprecipitation method is a widely used technique for synthesizing doped ceria catalysts, offering versatility in terms of process variations and dopant selection. This method involves the simultaneous precipitation of both the host cation (cerium) and the desired dopant from a solution of their respective salts. The process typically includes treating the solution with a precipitating agent, followed by washing, drying, and high-temperature calcination of the resulting precipitate [10.1021/cr300418s_3].

Several variations of the coprecipitation method have been developed to optimize the synthesis of doped ceria catalysts. One alternative approach involves precipitating solid salts through solvent evaporation, which can lead to different structural properties in the final catalyst. Another variation utilizes NH4OH as a precipitating agent for treating the aqueous solution of precursors, as demonstrated in the synthesis of Nb-doped ceria [10.1021/cr300418s_3].

The selection of dopants and their precursors plays a crucial role in determining the final properties of the catalyst. Various dopants have been successfully incorporated into ceria using the coprecipitation method, including:

1. Rare earth elements (Gd, Y, La, Sm, Yb, Pr) using nitrate precursors
2. Transition metals (Nb, Ta) using chloride precursors
3. Zirconium (Zr) using oxynitrate precursor

The choice of dopant and its precursor can significantly influence the structural properties and catalytic performance of the resulting doped ceria catalysts [10.1021/cr300418s_3].

The impact of different preparation techniques on the structural properties and catalytic performance of doped ceria catalysts is particularly evident in the case of Nb-doped ceria. Studies have shown that two different preparation methods for Nb-doped ceria resulted in distinct n-butane combustion rates, with the sample prepared using hydroxide coprecipitation exhibiting a 5% higher rate compared to an alternative method. Moreover, the effective activation energies differed significantly, with values of 85 kJ/mol for the sample obtained from coprecipitated hydroxides and 105 kJ/mol for the other sample [10.1021/cr300418s_3].

These observed differences in catalytic performance and activation energies underscore the critical role that specific coprecipitation techniques play in determining the final catalyst's properties and effectiveness, particularly in applications such as n-butane combustion. The ability to fine-tune the synthesis process allows for the optimization of catalysts for specific reactions and operating conditions.

为了优化掺杂氧化铈催化剂的合成，已经开发了几种共沉淀方法的变体。一种替代方法是通过溶剂蒸发沉淀固体盐，这可能导致最终催化剂的结构性质不同。另一种变体是使用NH4OH作为沉淀剂处理前驱体的水溶液，这在Nb掺杂氧化铈的合成中得到了证实[10.1021/cr300418s_3]。

掺杂剂的选择及其前驱体在决定催化剂的最终性质方面起着至关重要的作用。使用共沉淀法已成功将各种掺杂剂掺入氧化铈中，包括：

1. 稀土元素（Gd、Y、La、Sm、Yb、Pr）使用硝酸盐前驱体
2. 过渡金属（Nb、Ta）使用氯化物前驱体
3. 锆（Zr）使用氧硝酸盐前驱体

掺杂剂及其前驱体的选择可以显著影响所得掺杂氧化铈催化剂的结构性质和催化性能[10.1021/cr300418s_3]。

不同制备技术对掺杂氧化铈催化剂的结构性质和催化性能的影响在Nb掺杂氧化铈的案例中特别明显。研究表明，两种不同的Nb掺杂氧化铈制备方法导致了不同的正丁烷燃烧速率，其中使用氢氧化物共沉淀法制备的样品比另一种方法高5%。此外，有效活化能也有显著差异，从共沉淀氢氧化物得到的样品为85 kJ/mol，而另一个样品为105 kJ/mol [10.1021/cr300418s_3]。

这些观察到的催化性能和活化能的差异突显了特定共沉淀技术在决定最终催化剂的性质和有效性方面的关键作用，特别是在正丁烷燃烧等应用中。能够微调合成过程允许针对特定反应和操作条件优化催化剂。
