The coprecipitation method is a widely used technique for synthesizing doped ceria catalysts, offering versatility in process variations and dopant selection. This method involves dissolving salts of both the host cation (cerium) and the desired dopant in a solution, followed by simultaneous precipitation of both cations using a chemical treatment. The resulting precipitate is then washed, dried, and calcined at high temperature to obtain the final catalyst [10.1021/cr300418s_3].

Process variations in the coprecipitation method can significantly influence the structural properties and catalytic performance of the resulting catalysts. One alternative approach involves precipitating solid salts by evaporating the solvent, while another variation utilizes NH4OH to treat the aqueous solution of precursors, causing precipitation [10.1021/cr300418s_3]. These variations in preparation techniques can lead to differences in catalyst properties and effectiveness.

The selection of dopants plays a crucial role in tailoring the properties of ceria catalysts. Various dopants can be incorporated using different precursors:
1. Gd, Y, La, Sm, Yb, and Pr using nitrate precursors
2. Nb and Ta using chloride precursors
3. Zr using oxynitrate precursor (ZrO(NO3)2)

The impact of preparation techniques on catalyst performance is exemplified by the case of Nb-doped ceria catalysts used for n-butane combustion. Two different preparation methods resulted in varying catalytic activities:
1. The sample prepared using hydroxide coprecipitation exhibited a 5% higher n-butane combustion rate compared to the alternative method.
2. The effective activation energies differed significantly: 85 kJ/mol for the sample obtained from coprecipitated hydroxides, and 105 kJ/mol for the other sample [10.1021/cr300418s_3].

These differences in catalytic performance and activation energies underscore the substantial impact that specific coprecipitation techniques can have on the final catalyst's properties and effectiveness, particularly in applications such as n-butane combustion.

共沉淀法是一种广泛使用的合成掺杂氧化铈催化剂的技术，在工艺变化和掺杂剂选择方面具有很大的灵活性。该方法涉及将宿主阳离子（铈）和所需掺杂剂的盐溶解在溶液中，然后使用化学处理同时沉淀两种阳离子。得到的沉淀物随后被洗涤、干燥，并在高温下煅烧以获得最终的催化剂 [10.1021/cr300418s_3]。

共沉淀法的工艺变化可以显著影响所得催化剂的结构特性和催化性能。一种替代方法涉及通过蒸发溶剂来沉淀固体盐，而另一种变体则使用NH4OH处理前驱体的水溶液，导致沉淀 [10.1021/cr300418s_3]。这些制备技术的变化可能导致催化剂性能和有效性的差异。

掺杂剂的选择在调整氧化铈催化剂性能方面起着关键作用。可以使用不同的前驱体掺入各种掺杂剂：
1. 使用硝酸盐前驱体掺入Gd、Y、La、Sm、Yb和Pr
2. 使用氯化物前驱体掺入Nb和Ta
3. 使用氧硝酸盐前驱体（ZrO(NO3)2）掺入Zr

制备技术对催化剂性能的影响可以通过用于正丁烷燃烧的Nb掺杂氧化铈催化剂的案例来说明。两种不同的制备方法导致了不同的催化活性：

这些催化性能和活化能的差异凸显了特定共沉淀技术对最终催化剂的性能和有效性的实质性影响，特别是在正丁烷燃烧等应用中。
