The literature focuses on a specific catalyst system (VOx/SiO2) prepared by flame pyrolysis (FP) for oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP), rather than providing a broad comparison of various catalyst systems. However, some key points can be extracted:

1. The FP-prepared VOx/SiO2 catalysts showed promising selectivity for ODHP under both aerobic and anaerobic conditions.

2. The best performance was achieved with 10 wt% V loading, reaching up to 80% selectivity at 10% propane conversion under anaerobic conditions.

3. Higher dispersion of VOx species in FP-prepared samples contributed to improved performance and reduced coke formation.

4. High V site isolation was beneficial for selectivity, but bulk vanadia segregation at higher loadings decreased catalyst selectivity.

The study does not directly address the use of soft oxidants, corrosiveness issues, or strategies for improving C-H bond activation. However, it highlights the importance of catalyst preparation methods in achieving high dispersion and isolation of active sites for improved ODHP performance.

Future research in this field could focus on:
1. Exploring FP preparation techniques for other metal oxide catalysts.
2. Optimizing catalyst compositions to balance dispersion and loading.
3. Investigating the performance of FP-prepared catalysts under various reaction conditions, including the use of soft oxidants.
4. Developing strategies to maintain high dispersion and site isolation at higher metal loadings.
5. Studying the relationship between catalyst structure and regenerability.

These areas of research could contribute to addressing the persistent challenges in ODHP and lead to more efficient and selective catalytic systems.
