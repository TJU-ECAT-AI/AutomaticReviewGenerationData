<English>
Recent advances in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) using soft oxidants have shown promising results across various catalyst systems and oxidants. Key findings include:

1. Soft Oxidants:
   - CO2: Enhances propane conversion and propene yield at lower temperatures, but decreases selectivity [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2].
   - N2O: Improves propene selectivity significantly, often at the cost of lower conversion rates [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2][10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010].
   - SO2: Acts as an effective oxidizing agent, particularly with carbon-based catalysts [10.1007/BF02071147].
   - Molecular chlorine: Identified as a thermodynamically advantageous oxidizing agent [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018].
   - S2 vapor: Used in sulfur-ODHP (SODHP) systems, showing high selectivity [10.1039/D0CY01039A].

2. Catalyst Systems:
   - Transition metal oxides:
     - CrOx/silica: High activity but suffers from rapid deactivation [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
     - VOx-based catalysts: High selectivity (95-98%) at low conversion rates [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14].
     - Ni-promoted CrOx/SBA-15: Improved stability and performance [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
   - Main group element metal oxides:
     - Ga2O3: Comparable selectivity to CrOx at lower conversion rates [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14].
   - Zeolites:
     - Fe-ZSM-5: Superior performance with N2O, achieving 30% propylene yield [10.1016/j.micromeso.2014.07.032].
   - Supported molten salts:
     - Alkali metal chlorides on SiO2 or ZrO2: High selectivity, especially above melting temperature [10.1007/s11244-008-9102-3].
   - Metal sulfides:
     - S-ZrO2: Achieved 86% propylene selectivity in SODHP [10.1039/D0CY01039A].

3. Performance Metrics:
   - Selectivity: Ranges from 38% (PdS) to 96% (Pt-H3PMo12O40) [10.1039/D0CY01039A][10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018].
   - Conversion: Varies widely, with some catalysts achieving over 50% [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018].
   - Yield: Best catalysts achieve 30-40% propylene yield [10.1016/j.micromeso.2014.07.032][10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018].

Persistent challenges in ODHP include:
1. Balancing selectivity and conversion: High selectivity often comes at the cost of low conversion rates [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00836-5].
2. Catalyst deactivation: Coke formation and reduction of active sites remain issues [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
3. Overoxidation: Formation of CO and CO2 at higher conversion rates [10.1021/jp070747c].
4. Corrosion: Potential issues with halogen-based systems [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018].

Future research directions should focus on:
1. Optimizing catalyst compositions and structures to balance selectivity and conversion.
2. Developing more stable catalysts with improved regenerability.
3. Exploring synergistic effects of multiple soft oxidants.
4. Improving C-H bond activation strategies.
5. Investigating novel catalyst preparation methods, such as the steam activation of zeolites [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of ODHP using soft oxidants has shown significant progress, with various catalyst systems and oxidants demonstrating promising results. The use of soft oxidants like N2O and CO2 has opened new avenues for improving selectivity and yield while addressing environmental concerns. The development of novel catalyst systems, such as supported molten salts and metal sulfides, offers potential for further improvements in ODHP performance.

Future research should focus on understanding the fundamental mechanisms of C-H bond activation and oxidant interactions with catalysts at the molecular level. This could lead to the design of more efficient and stable catalysts. Additionally, exploring the potential of hybrid systems combining different soft oxidants or catalyst types may yield synergistic effects. The integration of in-situ characterization techniques and advanced modeling approaches could provide deeper insights into catalyst behavior under reaction conditions, guiding the development of next-generation ODHP catalysts and processes.

<Chinese>
使用软氧化剂进行丙烷的氧化脱氢(ODHP)的最新进展在各种催化剂系统和氧化剂中显示出了有希望的结果。主要发现包括：

1. 软氧化剂：
   - CO2：在较低温度下提高丙烷转化率和丙烯产率，但降低选择性 [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2]。
   - N2O：显著提高丙烯选择性，但通常以较低的转化率为代价 [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2][10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010]。
   - SO2：作为有效的氧化剂，特别是与碳基催化剂配合使用 [10.1007/BF02071147]。
   - 分子氯：被确定为热力学上最有利的氧化剂 [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018]。
   - S2蒸气：用于硫-ODHP（SODHP）系统，显示出高选择性 [10.1039/D0CY01039A]。

2. 催化剂系统：
   - 过渡金属氧化物：
     - CrOx/二氧化硅：活性高但容易快速失活 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
     - 基于VOx的催化剂：在低转化率下具有高选择性（95-98%）[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14]。
     - Ni促进的CrOx/SBA-15：提高了稳定性和性能 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
   - 主族元素金属氧化物：
     - Ga2O3：在较低转化率下与CrOx具有可比的选择性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14]。
   - 沸石：
     - Fe-ZSM-5：与N2O配合使用时表现出色，丙烯产率达到30% [10.1016/j.micromeso.2014.07.032]。
   - 负载型熔融盐：
     - SiO2或ZrO2上的碱金属氯化物：高选择性，尤其是在熔点以上 [10.1007/s11244-008-9102-3]。
   - 金属硫化物：
     - S-ZrO2：在SODHP中达到86%的丙烯选择性 [10.1039/D0CY01039A]。

3. 性能指标：
   - 选择性：从38%（PdS）到96%（Pt-H3PMo12O40）不等 [10.1039/D0CY01039A][10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018]。
   - 转化率：差异很大，一些催化剂达到50%以上 [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018]。
   - 产率：最好的催化剂达到30-40%的丙烯产率 [10.1016/j.micromeso.2014.07.032][10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018]。

ODHP中持续存在的挑战包括：
1. 平衡选择性和转化率：高选择性往往以低转化率为代价 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00836-5]。
2. 催化剂失活：焦炭形成和活性位点的还原仍然是问题 [10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
3. 过度氧化：在较高转化率下形成CO和CO2 [10.1021/jp070747c]。
4. 腐蚀：卤素基系统可能存在的问题 [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018]。

未来研究方向应关注：
1. 优化催化剂组成和结构以平衡选择性和转化率。
2. 开发具有更好再生能力的更稳定催化剂。
3. 探索多种软氧化剂的协同效应。
4. 改进C-H键活化策略。
5. 研究新型催化剂制备方法，如沸石的蒸汽活化 [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：使用软氧化剂进行ODHP的领域已经取得了显著进展，各种催化剂系统和氧化剂都显示出了有希望的结果。使用N2O和CO2等软氧化剂为提高选择性和产率开辟了新途径，同时解决了环境问题。新型催化剂系统的开发，如负载型熔融盐和金属硫化物，为进一步提高ODHP性能提供了潜力。

未来的研究应该集中在理解分子水平上C-H键活化和氧化剂与催化剂相互作用的基本机制。这可能导致设计更高效和稳定的催化剂。此外，探索结合不同软氧化剂或催化剂类型的混合系统可能会产生协同效应。原位表征技术和先进建模方法的整合可以提供对反应条件下催化剂行为的更深入了解，指导下一代ODHP催化剂和工艺的开发。
