<English>
Recent advances in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) using soft oxidants have shown promising results across various catalyst systems and oxidants. This review focuses on the performance of different catalysts, the role of soft oxidants, and the persistent challenges in the field.

1. Catalyst Systems and Performance:

Transition metal/metal oxides:
- CrOx-based catalysts: High C3H6 selectivity at elevated C3H8 conversion at 550°C [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14]. CrOx/silica shows high activity but suffers from rapid deactivation due to coke deposition and CrOx reduction [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
- VOx-based catalysts: High C3H6 selectivity (95-98%) but at low conversion rates [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14]. VOx supported on mesoporous silica materials (MCM, SBA) show improved performance [10.1021/jp070747c].
- NiMoO4 catalysts: Show promise with CO2 and N2O as promoters [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2].

Main group element metal oxides:
- Ga2O3: Shows comparable selectivity to CrOx below 40% conversion [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14].
- Fe-zeolites: Demonstrate superior performance in N2O-mediated ODHP, with initial propene yields of 24% at 773-798 K [10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035].

Novel catalyst systems:
- Supported alkali metal chloride melts: Show excellent catalytic performance in ODH of ethane and propane [10.1007/s11244-008-9102-3].
- Metal sulfide catalysts: S-ZrO2 shows high propylene selectivity (86%) in sulfur-ODHP (SODHP) [10.1039/D0CY01039A].

2. Soft Oxidants and Their Effects:

- CO2: Enhances propane conversion, maintains catalyst activity by inhibiting coke formation, and promotes the reverse water-gas shift reaction [10.1007/s11144-005-0268-4].
- N2O: Improves propene selectivity by slowing down the reoxidation of reduced catalyst species, resulting in a lower steady-state concentration of active lattice oxygen sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010].
- SO2: Acts as an effective oxidizing agent for hydrocarbon dehydrogenation, with iron oxides supported on activated carbon achieving high propylene yields [10.1007/BF02071147].
- Molecular chlorine: Identified as the most advantageous oxidizing agent based on thermodynamic calculations [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018].

3. Persistent Challenges:

- Balancing selectivity and conversion: High propene selectivity often comes at the cost of low propane conversion, and vice versa [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00836-5].
- Catalyst deactivation: Many catalysts suffer from rapid deactivation due to coke formation or reduction of active species [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
- Carbon oxide formation: Minimizing the formation of COx at high degrees of propane conversion remains a significant challenge [10.1021/jp070747c].
- Catalyst stability and regeneration: Some catalysts show poor regenerability after deactivation [10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4].

4. Future Research Directions:

- Optimizing catalyst composition and structure: Developing catalysts with well-dispersed active species and improved stability [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00836-5].
- Exploring novel soft oxidants and their combinations: Investigating the potential of new oxidants or mixtures to enhance selectivity and conversion [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2].
- Improving C-H bond activation strategies: Developing more efficient methods for selective propane activation.
- Enhancing catalyst regenerability: Focusing on catalyst designs that allow for easy regeneration and longer lifetimes.
- Optimizing reaction conditions: Fine-tuning temperature, pressure, and reactant ratios to maximize performance.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of ODHP using soft oxidants has shown significant progress in recent years, with various catalyst systems and oxidants demonstrating promising results. The use of soft oxidants, particularly N2O and CO2, has emerged as a powerful strategy to enhance propene selectivity and mitigate some of the challenges associated with traditional ODHP processes. However, the persistent trade-off between selectivity and conversion remains a key hurdle.

Moving forward, research efforts should focus on developing multifunctional catalysts that can simultaneously activate propane, stabilize reaction intermediates, and suppress over-oxidation. This may involve exploring novel catalyst architectures, such as core-shell structures or hierarchical porous materials, that can provide spatial control over the reaction pathway. Additionally, the integration of in-situ characterization techniques and advanced computational modeling could provide deeper insights into the reaction mechanisms and guide the rational design of next-generation catalysts.

The use of soft oxidants opens up new possibilities for process intensification and energy efficiency. Future research could explore the potential of coupling ODHP with other reactions, such as CO2 utilization or hydrogen production, to create more sustainable and economically viable processes. Furthermore, the development of membrane reactors or other innovative reactor designs tailored for soft oxidant-mediated ODHP could lead to significant improvements in overall process efficiency.

As the field advances, it will be crucial to consider the scalability and long-term stability of these catalytic systems under industrial conditions. This may necessitate the development of novel synthesis methods for large-scale catalyst production and the implementation of advanced process control strategies to maintain optimal performance over extended periods.

In conclusion, while challenges remain, the continued exploration of soft oxidants and advanced catalyst designs in ODHP holds great promise for revolutionizing propylene production, potentially leading to more sustainable and efficient processes in the petrochemical industry.

<Chinese>
使用软氧化剂进行丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）的最新进展在各种催化剂系统和氧化剂方面都显示出了令人鼓舞的结果。本综述重点关注不同催化剂的性能、软氧化剂的作用以及该领域持续存在的挑战。

1. 催化剂系统及其性能：

过渡金属/金属氧化物：
- 基于CrOx的催化剂：在550°C下，高C3H8转化率时表现出高C3H6选择性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14]。CrOx/二氧化硅显示出高活性，但由于积碳沉积和CrOx还原而导致快速失活[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
- 基于VOx的催化剂：高C3H6选择性（95-98%），但转化率低[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14]。负载在介孔二氧化硅材料（MCM，SBA）上的VOx显示出改进的性能[10.1021/jp070747c]。
- NiMoO4催化剂：使用CO2和N2O作为促进剂时表现出前景[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2]。

主族元素金属氧化物：
- Ga2O3：在40%以下的转化率下，显示出与CrOx相当的选择性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14]。
- Fe-沸石：在N2O介导的ODHP中表现出卓越的性能，在773-798 K下初始丙烯收率达到24%[10.1016/j.ces.2004.07.035]。

新型催化剂系统：
- 负载的碱金属氯化物熔体：在乙烷和丙烷的ODH中显示出优异的催化性能[10.1007/s11244-008-9102-3]。
- 金属硫化物催化剂：在硫-ODHP（SODHP）中，S-ZrO2显示出高丙烯选择性（86%）[10.1039/D0CY01039A]。

2. 软氧化剂及其效果：

- CO2：增强丙烷转化率，通过抑制积碳形成维持催化剂活性，并促进逆水煤气反应[10.1007/s11144-005-0268-4]。
- N2O：通过减缓还原态催化剂物种的再氧化，改善丙烯选择性，导致活性晶格氧位点的稳态浓度降低[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.06.010]。
- SO2：作为碳氢化合物脱氢的有效氧化剂，负载在活性炭上的氧化铁可获得高丙烯收率[10.1007/BF02071147]。
- 分子氯：根据热力学计算，被确定为最有利的氧化剂[10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018]。

3. 持续存在的挑战：

- 平衡选择性和转化率：高丙烯选择性往往以低丙烷转化率为代价，反之亦然[10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00836-5]。
- 催化剂失活：许多催化剂由于积碳形成或活性物种还原而快速失活[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
- 碳氧化物形成：在高丙烷转化率下最小化COx的形成仍然是一个重大挑战[10.1021/jp070747c]。
- 催化剂稳定性和再生：一些催化剂在失活后表现出较差的再生能力[10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4]。

4. 未来研究方向：

- 优化催化剂组成和结构：开发具有良好分散活性物种和改进稳定性的催化剂[10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00836-5]。
- 探索新型软氧化剂及其组合：研究新氧化剂或混合物提高选择性和转化率的潜力[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2]。
- 改进C-H键活化策略：开发更高效的选择性丙烷活化方法。
- 增强催化剂再生能力：专注于允许易于再生和延长寿命的催化剂设计。
- 优化反应条件：调整温度、压力和反应物比例以最大化性能。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：近年来，使用软氧化剂进行ODHP的领域取得了显著进展，各种催化剂系统和氧化剂都显示出了令人鼓舞的结果。使用软氧化剂，尤其是N2O和CO2，已成为提高丙烯选择性和缓解传统ODHP过程中一些挑战的有力策略。然而，选择性和转化率之间持续存在的权衡仍然是一个关键障碍。

展望未来，研究努力应集中在开发能够同时活化丙烷、稳定反应中间体和抑制过度氧化的多功能催化剂。这可能涉及探索新型催化剂结构，如核壳结构或分级多孔材料，以提供对反应路径的空间控制。此外，原位表征技术和先进计算模型的整合可以提供对反应机理更深入的洞察，并指导下一代催化剂的理性设计。

软氧化剂的使用为过程强化和能源效率开辟了新的可能性。未来的研究可以探索将ODHP与其他反应（如CO2利用或氢气生产）耦合的潜力，以创造更可持续和经济可行的过程。此外，开发专门针对软氧化剂介导ODHP的膜反应器或其他创新反应器设计可能会显著提高整体过程效率。

随着该领域的发展，考虑这些催化系统在工业条件下的可扩展性和长期稳定性将至关重要。这可能需要开发用于大规模催化剂生产的新型合成方法，并实施先进的过程控制策略以在长期内保持最佳性能。

总之，尽管仍然存在挑战，但在ODHP中继续探索软氧化剂和先进催化剂设计有望彻底改变丙烯生产，潜在地引领石油化工行业更可持续和高效的过程。
