<English>
Recent advances in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) using soft oxidants have shown promising results across various catalyst systems and oxidants. This review focuses on the performance of different catalysts, the role of soft oxidants, and the challenges and future directions in this field.

Catalyst Systems:
1. Transition Metal/Metal Oxides:
   - CrOx-based catalysts show high C3H6 selectivity at elevated C3H8 conversion [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14].
   - VOx-based catalysts achieve high C3H6 selectivity (95-98%) but at low conversion rates [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14].
   - NiMoO4 catalysts demonstrate improved performance with the addition of soft oxidants [10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2].

2. Main Group Element Metal Oxides:
   - Ga2O3 shows comparable selectivity to CrOx below 40% conversion [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14].
   - Fe-zeolites (MFI and BEA) demonstrate high efficiency with N2O, achieving propylene yields up to 25% at 723 K [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005].

3. Supported Catalysts:
   - Ni-promoted CrOx/SBA-15 shows improved performance in decreasing catalyst deactivation [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
   - VOx supported on mesoporous silica materials (MCM, SBA) exhibit improved performance compared to unsupported catalysts [10.1021/jp070747c].

4. Novel Catalyst Systems:
   - Supported alkali metal chloride melts show excellent catalytic performance in ODH of ethane and propane [10.1007/s11244-008-9102-3].
   - Metal sulfide catalysts in sulfur-ODHP (SODHP) systems demonstrate high propylene selectivity, up to 86% for S-ZrO2 [10.1039/D0CY01039A].

Soft Oxidants:
1. CO2: Enhances propane dehydrogenation by increasing equilibrium conversion, maintaining catalyst activity, and inhibiting coke formation [10.1007/s11144-005-0268-4].

2. N2O: Improves propylene selectivity while decreasing overall activity. It slows down catalyst reoxidation, leading to lower steady-state concentration of active sites [10.1016/S0926-860X(03)00103-0].

3. SO2: Acts as an effective oxidizing agent for hydrocarbon dehydrogenation, with iron oxides supported on activated carbon achieving high propylene yields [10.1007/BF02071147].

4. Molecular Chlorine: Identified as the most advantageous oxidizing agent based on thermodynamic calculations [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018].

Persistent Challenges:
1. Balancing selectivity and conversion: Achieving high propylene selectivity while maintaining high propane conversion remains a challenge [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018].

2. Catalyst deactivation: Many catalysts suffer from rapid deactivation due to coke formation and active site reduction [10.1002/cctc.201200397].

3. Carbon oxide formation: Minimizing the formation of COx, especially at high propane conversion, is still a major challenge [10.1021/jp070747c].

4. Corrosion issues: Potential corrosion problems with halogen-based systems need to be addressed [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018].

Future Research Directions:
1. Optimizing catalyst composition and structure to balance selectivity and conversion.
2. Developing strategies to enhance catalyst regenerability and stability.
3. Exploring novel soft oxidants and their combinations to fine-tune catalyst oxidation states.
4. Improving C-H bond activation strategies for more efficient propane dehydrogenation.
5. Investigating the synergistic effects of multiple promoters or soft oxidants.
6. Developing scalable and economically viable processes for industrial application.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of oxidative dehydrogenation of propane using soft oxidants has shown significant progress in recent years, with various catalyst systems and oxidants demonstrating promising results. The use of soft oxidants, particularly CO2 and N2O, has emerged as a powerful tool to modulate catalyst performance and improve selectivity. However, the persistent challenges of balancing selectivity and conversion, catalyst deactivation, and carbon oxide formation indicate that there is still considerable room for improvement.

Future research efforts should focus on developing more sophisticated catalyst designs that can maintain high activity while suppressing undesired side reactions. This may involve exploring novel catalyst compositions, such as bimetallic systems or hybrid organic-inorganic materials, that can synergistically enhance performance. Additionally, the development of in-situ characterization techniques to better understand the dynamic behavior of catalysts under reaction conditions could provide valuable insights for rational catalyst design.

The use of artificial intelligence and machine learning algorithms in catalyst discovery and optimization could accelerate the development of more efficient ODHP processes. These tools could help identify promising catalyst candidates and predict optimal reaction conditions, reducing the time and resources required for experimental screening.

Furthermore, integrating ODHP processes with other technologies, such as membrane reactors or microwave-assisted heating, could lead to more energy-efficient and selective propylene production. As the demand for propylene continues to grow, the development of sustainable and economically viable ODHP processes using soft oxidants will play a crucial role in meeting future industrial needs while minimizing environmental impact.

<Chinese>
使用软氧化剂进行丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）的最新进展显示了各种催化剂系统和氧化剂的有望结果。本综述重点关注不同催化剂的性能、软氧化剂的作用，以及该领域的挑战和未来方向。

催化剂系统：
1. 过渡金属/金属氧化物：
   - 基于CrOx的催化剂在较高的C3H8转化率下显示出高C3H6选择性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14]。
   - 基于VOx的催化剂实现了高C3H6选择性（95-98%），但转化率较低[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14]。
   - NiMoO4催化剂在添加软氧化剂后表现出改进的性能[10.1016/S0920-5861(03)00120-2]。

2. 主族元素金属氧化物：
   - Ga2O3在40%以下的转化率下显示出与CrOx相当的选择性[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14]。
   - Fe沸石（MFI和BEA）在使用N2O时表现出高效率，在723 K下实现高达25%的丙烯产率[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]。

3. 负载型催化剂：
   - Ni促进的CrOx/SBA-15在减少催化剂失活方面表现出改进的性能[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
   - 负载在介孔二氧化硅材料（MCM，SBA）上的VOx与非负载催化剂相比表现出改进的性能[10.1021/jp070747c]。

4. 新型催化剂系统：
   - 负载的碱金属氯化物熔体在乙烷和丙烷的ODH中显示出优异的催化性能[10.1007/s11244-008-9102-3]。
   - 硫化物催化剂在硫-ODHP（SODHP）系统中表现出高丙烯选择性，S-ZrO2高达86%[10.1039/D0CY01039A]。

软氧化剂：
1. CO2：通过增加平衡转化率、维持催化剂活性和抑制积碳来增强丙烷脱氢[10.1007/s11144-005-0268-4]。

2. N2O：提高丙烯选择性，同时降低整体活性。它减缓催化剂再氧化，导致活性位点的稳态浓度降低[10.1016/S0926-860X(03)00103-0]。

3. SO2：作为碳氢化合物脱氢的有效氧化剂，铁氧化物负载在活性炭上实现高丙烯产率[10.1007/BF02071147]。

4. 分子氯：根据热力学计算被确定为最有利的氧化剂[10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018]。

持续存在的挑战：
1. 平衡选择性和转化率：在保持高丙烷转化率的同时实现高丙烯选择性仍然是一个挑战[10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018]。

2. 催化剂失活：许多催化剂由于积碳形成和活性位点还原而遭受快速失活[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。

3. 碳氧化物形成：特别是在高丙烷转化率下，最小化COx的形成仍然是一个主要挑战[10.1021/jp070747c]。

4. 腐蚀问题：需要解决卤素基系统的潜在腐蚀问题[10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018]。

未来研究方向：
1. 优化催化剂组成和结构以平衡选择性和转化率。
2. 开发策略以增强催化剂的再生能力和稳定性。
3. 探索新型软氧化剂及其组合以精细调节催化剂氧化状态。
4. 改进C-H键活化策略，实现更高效的丙烷脱氢。
5. 研究多种促进剂或软氧化剂的协同效应。
6. 开发可扩展和经济可行的工业应用过程。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：近年来，使用软氧化剂进行丙烷氧化脱氢的领域取得了显著进展，各种催化剂系统和氧化剂展示了令人鼓舞的结果。软氧化剂的使用，特别是CO2和N2O，已成为调节催化剂性能和提高选择性的有力工具。然而，平衡选择性和转化率、催化剂失活以及碳氧化物形成等持续存在的挑战表明，仍有相当大的改进空间。

未来的研究努力应集中在开发更复杂的催化剂设计上，这些设计可以保持高活性，同时抑制不需要的副反应。这可能涉及探索新型催化剂组成，如双金属系统或有机-无机混合材料，它们可以协同增强性能。此外，开发原位表征技术以更好地理解催化剂在反应条件下的动态行为，可以为理性催化剂设计提供宝贵的见解。

在催化剂发现和优化中使用人工智能和机器学习算法可以加速开发更高效的ODHP过程。这些工具可以帮助识别有前途的催化剂候选者并预测最佳反应条件，减少实验筛选所需的时间和资源。

此外，将ODHP过程与其他技术（如膜反应器或微波辅助加热）相结合，可能会导致更节能和选择性的丙烯生产。随着丙烯需求的持续增长，使用软氧化剂开发可持续和经济可行的ODHP过程将在满足未来工业需求的同时最大限度地减少环境影响方面发挥关键作用。
