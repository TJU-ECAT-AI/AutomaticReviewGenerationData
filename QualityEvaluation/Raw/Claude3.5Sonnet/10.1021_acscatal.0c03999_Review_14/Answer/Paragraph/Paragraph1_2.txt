<English>
Recent advances in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) using soft oxidants have shown promising results across various catalyst systems, including transition metal/metal oxides and main group element metal oxides. The use of soft oxidants such as CO2, N2O, S-containing compounds, and halogen/halides has been extensively studied to improve selectivity and conversion rates in ODHP reactions.

Key findings from recent studies include:

1. CO2 as a soft oxidant:
   - Enhances propane dehydrogenation over silica-supported chromium oxide catalysts [10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4].
   - Increases equilibrium conversion, maintains catalyst activity by inhibiting coke formation, and enhances propane conversion through the reverse water-gas shift reaction [10.1007/s11144-005-0268-4].

2. N2O as a soft oxidant:
   - Improves propylene selectivity while decreasing overall activity compared to O2 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00836-5].
   - VOx and Fe-containing zeolites are reported as superior catalysts for N2O-ODHP [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14].
   - Steam-activated Fe-zeolites (MFI and BEA) show high efficiency, achieving propylene yields up to 25% at 723 K [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005].

3. Sulfur-based oxidants:
   - SO2 acts as an effective oxidizing agent for hydrocarbon dehydrogenation [10.1007/BF02071147].
   - Sulfur-ODHP (SODHP) systems using elemental sulfur (S2) as a soft oxidant show promising results, with S-ZrO2 demonstrating propylene selectivity up to 86% [10.1039/D0CY01039A].

4. Halogen-based oxidants:
   - Molecular chlorine has been identified as an advantageous oxidizing agent based on thermodynamic calculations [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018].
   - Ru/TiO2 catalyst with molecular chlorine as an oxidant demonstrated 95% selectivity toward propylene at 50% propane conversion [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018].

Persistent challenges in ODHP include:
1. Balancing high selectivity with high conversion rates.
2. Minimizing the formation of carbon oxides (COx) at high degrees of propane conversion [10.1021/jp070747c].
3. Catalyst deactivation due to coke formation [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005].
4. Managing reaction temperatures and addressing potential corrosion issues with halogen-based systems [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018].

Key areas for future research and development include:
1. Improving C-H bond activation strategies.
2. Enhancing catalyst regenerability to address deactivation issues.
3. Optimizing reaction systems to balance selectivity and conversion.
4. Exploring novel promoters or catalyst compositions to fine-tune catalyst performance.
5. Investigating combinations of soft oxidants to achieve optimal results.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of ODHP using soft oxidants has demonstrated significant potential for improving propylene production efficiency. The diverse range of soft oxidants and catalyst systems explored offers multiple avenues for optimization and innovation. Future research should focus on developing multifunctional catalysts that can effectively utilize combinations of soft oxidants to achieve synergistic effects. Additionally, in-situ characterization techniques and advanced computational modeling could provide deeper insights into reaction mechanisms and catalyst behavior under different conditions. The integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning algorithms may accelerate the discovery of novel catalyst compositions and reaction conditions. Furthermore, exploring the potential of emerging materials such as metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) or 2D materials as catalyst supports or active components could open new possibilities for ODHP processes. Lastly, considering the growing importance of sustainability in chemical processes, future research should also investigate the feasibility of using renewable resources as soft oxidants or incorporating ODHP processes into broader circular economy strategies.

<Chinese>
使用软氧化剂进行丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）的最新进展在各种催化剂体系中显示出了令人鼓舞的结果，包括过渡金属/金属氧化物和主族元素金属氧化物。对CO2、N2O、含硫化合物和卤素/卤化物等软氧化剂的广泛研究旨在提高ODHP反应的选择性和转化率。

最近研究的主要发现包括：

1. CO2作为软氧化剂：
   - 增强了二氧化硅负载的铬氧化物催化剂上的丙烷脱氢 [10.1016/S0167-2991(04)80271-4]。
   - 增加平衡转化率，通过抑制积碳维持催化剂活性，并通过逆水气变换反应增强丙烷转化 [10.1007/s11144-005-0268-4]。

2. N2O作为软氧化剂：
   - 与O2相比，提高了丙烯选择性，但降低了总体活性 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00836-5]。
   - VOx和含铁沸石被报道为N2O-ODHP的优异催化剂 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14]。
   - 蒸汽活化的Fe-沸石（MFI和BEA）显示出高效率，在723 K下实现高达25%的丙烯产率 [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]。

3. 基于硫的氧化剂：
   - SO2作为碳氢化合物脱氢的有效氧化剂 [10.1007/BF02071147]。
   - 使用元素硫（S2）作为软氧化剂的硫-ODHP（SODHP）系统显示出有希望的结果，S-ZrO2表现出高达86%的丙烯选择性 [10.1039/D0CY01039A]。

4. 基于卤素的氧化剂：
   - 根据热力学计算，分子氯被确定为有利的氧化剂 [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018]。
   - 以分子氯为氧化剂的Ru/TiO2催化剂在50%丙烷转化率下展示了95%的丙烯选择性 [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018]。

ODHP中持续存在的挑战包括：
1. 平衡高选择性和高转化率。
2. 在高丙烷转化率下最小化碳氧化物（COx）的形成 [10.1021/jp070747c]。
3. 由于积碳导致的催化剂失活 [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]。
4. 管理反应温度并解决卤素基系统可能带来的腐蚀问题 [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018]。

未来研究和开发的关键领域包括：
1. 改进C-H键活化策略。
2. 提高催化剂的再生能力以解决失活问题。
3. 优化反应系统以平衡选择性和转化率。
4. 探索新型促进剂或催化剂组成以微调催化剂性能。
5. 研究软氧化剂的组合以实现最佳结果。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望：使用软氧化剂进行ODHP的领域已经显示出显著的潜力，可以提高丙烯生产效率。所探索的多种软氧化剂和催化剂系统为优化和创新提供了多个途径。未来的研究应该集中在开发能够有效利用软氧化剂组合以实现协同效应的多功能催化剂。此外，原位表征技术和先进的计算模型可以提供对反应机理和催化剂在不同条件下行为的更深入理解。人工智能和机器学习算法的整合可能加速新型催化剂组成和反应条件的发现。此外，探索新兴材料如金属有机框架（MOFs）或二维材料作为催化剂载体或活性组分的潜力可能为ODHP过程开辟新的可能性。最后，考虑到化学过程中可持续性的日益重要性，未来的研究还应调查使用可再生资源作为软氧化剂或将ODHP过程纳入更广泛的循环经济策略的可行性。
