<English>
Recent advances in oxidative dehydrogenation of propane (ODHP) using soft oxidants have shown promising results with various catalyst systems and oxidants. Key findings include:

1. Soft oxidants:
   - CO2: Enhances propane dehydrogenation, increases equilibrium conversion, and maintains catalyst activity by inhibiting coke formation [10.1007/s11144-005-0268-4].
   - N2O: Improves propylene selectivity but decreases overall activity compared to O2 [10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00836-5].
   - SO2: Effective for hydrocarbon dehydrogenation, with iron oxides supported on activated carbon achieving high propylene yields [10.1007/BF02071147].
   - Elemental sulfur (S2): Enhances selectivity by moderating over-oxidation in sulfur-ODHP (SODHP) systems [10.1039/D0CY01039A].
   - Molecular chlorine: Identified as the most advantageous oxidizing agent based on thermodynamic calculations [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018].

2. Catalyst systems:
   - Transition metal oxides: CrOx/silica shows high activity but suffers from rapid deactivation [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
   - Vanadium-based catalysts: Highly active and selective, especially when supported on mesoporous silica materials [10.1021/jp070747c].
   - Fe-zeolites: Demonstrate superior performance in N2O-mediated ODHP, with initial propylene yields up to 25% at 723 K [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005].
   - Metal sulfides: S-ZrO2 shows high propylene selectivity (86%) in SODHP systems [10.1039/D0CY01039A].
   - Supported alkali metal chlorides: Emerge as a new group of catalysts with high selectivity, especially in molten form [10.1007/s11244-008-9102-3].

3. Performance metrics:
   - Selectivity: Ranges from 38% (PdS) to 96% (Pt-H3PMo12O40) [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018].
   - Conversion: Varies widely, with some catalysts achieving up to 50% propane conversion [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018].
   - Yield: The best catalysts achieve propylene yields of 30-40%, with some exceptional cases reaching up to 55% [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14].

Persistent challenges in ODHP include:
1. Balancing high selectivity with high conversion rates [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018].
2. Minimizing the formation of carbon oxides (COx) at high degrees of propane conversion [10.1021/jp070747c].
3. Catalyst deactivation due to coke formation and reduction of active species [10.1002/cctc.201200397].
4. Managing reaction temperatures and potential corrosion issues with halogen-based systems [10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018].

Future research directions should focus on:
1. Optimizing catalyst composition and structure to enhance selectivity and conversion simultaneously.
2. Developing more efficient soft oxidants or combinations of oxidants to improve reaction kinetics and selectivity.
3. Improving catalyst stability and regenerability to address deactivation issues.
4. Exploring novel catalyst preparation methods to create more active and selective materials.
5. Investigating the potential of supported molten salt catalysts for ODHP applications.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The field of ODHP using soft oxidants has shown significant progress in recent years, with various catalyst systems and oxidants demonstrating promising results. The use of soft oxidants like N2O, CO2, and S2 has opened new avenues for improving selectivity and yield in propylene production. However, the persistent challenge of balancing high selectivity with high conversion rates remains a key focus for future research.

Moving forward, the integration of advanced materials science, nanotechnology, and computational modeling could lead to breakthrough catalyst designs. For instance, the development of multi-functional catalysts that combine the benefits of different active species (e.g., transition metals and main group elements) could potentially overcome current limitations. Additionally, the exploration of novel reactor designs and process intensification techniques may help in optimizing reaction conditions and improving overall process efficiency.

The growing interest in sustainable chemistry also presents an opportunity to align ODHP research with green chemistry principles. This could involve developing bio-inspired catalysts, utilizing renewable feedstocks for catalyst synthesis, or integrating ODHP processes with renewable energy sources. Furthermore, the potential synergies between ODHP and other emerging technologies, such as plasma catalysis or electrochemical processes, could open up entirely new research directions and industrial applications in the field of propylene production.

<Chinese>
使用软氧化剂进行丙烷氧化脱氢（ODHP）的最新进展显示了各种催化剂系统和氧化剂的前景。主要发现包括：

1. 软氧化剂：
   - CO2：增强丙烷脱氢，提高平衡转化率，并通过抑制积碳维持催化剂活性[10.1007/s11144-005-0268-4]。
   - N2O：与O2相比，提高丙烯选择性但降低整体活性[10.1016/S0926-860X(01)00836-5]。
   - SO2：有效进行碳氢化合物脱氢，铁氧化物负载在活性炭上可获得高丙烯产率[10.1007/BF02071147]。
   - 元素硫（S2）：通过调节过度氧化来提高硫-ODHP（SODHP）系统的选择性[10.1039/D0CY01039A]。
   - 分子氯：根据热力学计算，被确定为最有利的氧化剂[10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018]。

2. 催化剂系统：
   - 过渡金属氧化物：CrOx/二氧化硅显示高活性但快速失活[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
   - 钒基催化剂：高活性和选择性，特别是负载在介孔二氧化硅材料上时[10.1021/jp070747c]。
   - Fe-沸石：在N2O介导的ODHP中表现出色，初始丙烯产率在723 K时可达25%[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]。
   - 金属硫化物：在SODHP系统中，S-ZrO2显示高丙烯选择性（86%）[10.1039/D0CY01039A]。
   - 负载型碱金属氯化物：作为新型催化剂组，特别是在熔融状态下显示高选择性[10.1007/s11244-008-9102-3]。

3. 性能指标：
   - 选择性：范围从38%（PdS）到96%（Pt-H3PMo12O40）[10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018]。
   - 转化率：差异很大，部分催化剂达到50%的丙烷转化率[10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018]。
   - 产率：最佳催化剂达到30-40%的丙烯产率，个别情况下可达55%[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999_14]。

ODHP的持续挑战包括：
1. 平衡高选择性和高转化率[10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018]。
2. 在高度丙烷转化条件下最小化一氧化碳和二氧化碳（COx）的生成[10.1021/jp070747c]。
3. 由于积碳形成和活性物种还原导致的催化剂失活[10.1002/cctc.201200397]。
4. 管理反应温度和卤素基系统的潜在腐蚀问题[10.1016/j.apcata.2015.05.018]。

未来研究方向应关注：
1. 优化催化剂组成和结构，同时提高选择性和转化率。
2. 开发更高效的软氧化剂或氧化剂组合，以改善反应动力学和选择性。
3. 提高催化剂稳定性和再生能力，解决失活问题。
4. 探索新型催化剂制备方法，创造更活跃和选择性的材料。
5. 研究负载型熔融盐催化剂在ODHP应用中的潜力。

综合理解与未来展望：近年来，使用软氧化剂进行ODHP的领域取得了显著进展，各种催化剂系统和氧化剂展示了令人鼓舞的结果。使用N2O、CO2和S2等软氧化剂为提高丙烯生产的选择性和产率开辟了新途径。然而，平衡高选择性和高转化率的持续挑战仍然是未来研究的重点。

展望未来，先进材料科学、纳米技术和计算机模拟的融合可能会带来突破性的催化剂设计。例如，开发结合不同活性物种（如过渡金属和主族元素）优势的多功能催化剂可能会克服当前的限制。此外，探索新型反应器设计和过程强化技术可能有助于优化反应条件并提高整体过程效率。

对可持续化学的日益增长的兴趣也为将ODHP研究与绿色化学原则相结合提供了机会。这可能涉及开发生物启发的催化剂，利用可再生原料合成催化剂，或将ODHP过程与可再生能源源相结合。此外，ODHP与其他新兴技术（如等离子体催化或电化学过程）之间的潜在协同作用可能会在丙烯生产领域开辟全新的研究方向和工业应用。
